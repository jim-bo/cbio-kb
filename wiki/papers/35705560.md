**PMID:** 35705560 · **DOI:** 10.1038/s41467-022-30453-x · **Journal:** Nature Communications (2022)

## TL;DR

Nacev et al. used [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) targeted sequencing to prospectively profile 2,138 bone and soft tissue sarcomas across 45 OncoTree-mapped pathological entities from a single institution (MSK), making it the largest comparative sarcoma genomic study to date ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)). The most common alterations were in cell cycle / [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), receptor tyrosine kinase / PI3K / RAS signaling, and epigenetic regulators. Subtype-specific findings included [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) amplification in intimal sarcoma, SWI/SNF complex alterations in uterine adenosarcoma, and recurrent [KDR](../genes/KDR.md)/[FLT4](../genes/FLT4.md)/[EPHA5](../genes/EPHA5.md) mutations in angiosarcoma. Tumor mutational burden was low overall but heterogeneous, and unsupervised clustering revealed that histologically distinct sarcomas can share dominant genetic drivers, motivating genotype-based clinical trial design.

## Cohort & data

- 2,138 bone and soft tissue sarcoma samples from distinct patients, each with paired normal DNA, profiled by [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) ([sarcoma_mskcc_2022](../datasets/sarcoma_mskcc_2022.md)) ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- Median patient age 54 (range <1 to >90); 1,098 (51.4%) female; 790 (36.9%) metastatic site biopsies; 45 distinct OncoTree-mapped pathological entities ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- 22 subtypes had n≥20 samples and formed the core analysis set. Most common: [GIST](../cancer_types/GIST.md) (n=395, 18.5%), [DDLS](../cancer_types/DDLS.md) (n=167), [ULMS](../cancer_types/ULMS.md) (n=165), [UPS](../cancer_types/UPS.md) (n=145), [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) (n=129), [LMS](../cancer_types/LMS.md) (n=125), [ANGS](../cancer_types/ANGS.md) (n=101), [ES](../cancer_types/ES.md) (n=99) ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- Three MSK-IMPACT panel versions used: 341 genes (n=209, 9.8%), [IMPACT410](../methods/IMPACT410.md) (n=573, 26.8%), and [IMPACT468](../methods/IMPACT468.md) (n=1,356, 63.4%); ≥500× median coverage on Illumina HiSeq 2500 ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- Annotation pipeline: OncoKB for oncogenicity/actionability, [FACETS](../methods/facets.md) for allele-specific copy number / WGD calls, [MuSiC](../methods/genome-music.md) and [MutSigCV 1.4](../methods/mutsigcv.md) for recurrent-mutation discovery (q<0.1), [MSIsensor](../methods/msisensor.md) ≥10 threshold for MSI-high, and COSMIC v3 mutational signature extraction for samples with ≥15 SNVs ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- Companion interactive dataset on cBioPortal: `sarcoma_mskcc_2022` ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).

## Key findings

- **TMB is low but heterogeneous.** Median TMB was highest in [ANGS](../cancer_types/ANGS.md) (3.0 mut/Mb), [UPS](../cancer_types/UPS.md) (2.6), and [ULMS](../cancer_types/ULMS.md) (2.6), lowest in [WDLS](../cancer_types/WDLS.md), [EPIS](../cancer_types/EPIS.md), and RCS-other (0.9). TMB ≥5 mut/Mb in 25% of [ANGS](../cancer_types/ANGS.md), 15% of [ULMS](../cancer_types/ULMS.md) and [UPS](../cancer_types/UPS.md), 13% of [ERMS](../cancer_types/ERMS.md); TMB ≥10 mut/Mb in 7.6% of [ANGS](../cancer_types/ANGS.md) and 6.7% of [UPS](../cancer_types/UPS.md) ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- **MSI is rare.** Only 5/1,893 evaluable samples were MSI-high by [MSIsensor](../methods/msisensor.md) (one [UPS](../cancer_types/UPS.md), one [LMS](../cancer_types/LMS.md), three [ULMS](../cancer_types/ULMS.md)); 4 confirmed by PCR ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- **WGD frequency varies and predicts outcome.** Whole-genome doubling occurred in ~50% of [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md), [UPS](../cancer_types/UPS.md), [ERMS](../cancer_types/ERMS.md), and [MPNST](../cancer_types/MPNST.md). WGD was associated with worse overall survival in metastatic disease (p=0.042) and specifically in metastatic UPS (p=0.022), but not in primary cases (p=0.391) or in [MFS](../cancer_types/MFS.md) (p=0.78) ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- **Intimal sarcoma is TERT-amplified.** Oncogenic [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) amplifications were identified in 44% (8/18) of [INTS](../cancer_types/INTS.md), distinct from the classical MDM2/CDK4 amplicon. [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter mutations occurred in 79% (38/48) of [MRLS](../cancer_types/MRLS.md), 46% (24/52) of [SFT](../cancer_types/SFT.md), 35% (5/14) of [DDCHS](../cancer_types/DDCHS.md), and 16% (27/167) of [DDLS](../cancer_types/DDLS.md), almost entirely as amplifications (n=24) ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- **Uterine adenosarcoma is SWI/SNF–driven.** 43% of [UAS](../cancer_types/UAS.md) (n=14) carried oncogenic alterations in SWI/SNF components, predominantly [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) and [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md); 36% had histone-modifying gene alterations ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- **Angiosarcoma has recurrent RTK mutations.** [KDR](../genes/KDR.md) (VEGFR2) altered in 19%, [FLT4](../genes/FLT4.md) (VEGFR3) in 9%, and [EPHA5](../genes/EPHA5.md) in 9% of [ANGS](../cancer_types/ANGS.md) (n=101); [EPHA5](../genes/EPHA5.md) and [FLT4](../genes/FLT4.md) hits were all VUS but clustered in kinase/C-terminal domains. [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) oncogenic alterations in 6% (lower than the previously reported 21%) ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- **[GIST](../cancer_types/GIST.md) harbors a longer-tail driver list.** Beyond [KIT](../genes/KIT.md), [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md), and [SDHA](../genes/SDHA.md), recurrent mutations in [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md) (4%), [MAX](../genes/MAX.md) (4%), and [MGA](../genes/MGA.md) (3%) were identified in [GIST](../cancer_types/GIST.md) ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- **[DDLS](../cancer_types/DDLS.md) vs [WDLS](../cancer_types/WDLS.md) amplification differences.** Beyond shared [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md)/[MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) amplification, [DDLS](../cancer_types/DDLS.md) had higher rates of oncogene amplification than [WDLS](../cancer_types/WDLS.md): [GLI1](../genes/GLI1.md) 25.3% vs 8.5%, [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) 14.4% vs 6.3%, and [JUN](../genes/JUN.md) 13.8% vs 0%; [GLI1](../genes/GLI1.md) and [JUN](../genes/JUN.md) amplifications were mutually exclusive. Epigenetic alterations were also enriched in [DDLS](../cancer_types/DDLS.md) (25%) over [WDLS](../cancer_types/WDLS.md) (8%) ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- **Ewing sarcoma carries [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md).** 10% of [ES](../cancer_types/ES.md) (n=99) had [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md) mutations co-occurring with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) alterations ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- **[ULMS](../cancer_types/ULMS.md) biology.** [MED12](../genes/MED12.md) altered in 16% of [ULMS](../cancer_types/ULMS.md), most frequently missense mutations at glycine 44. [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) or [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md) each mutated in 7% of ULMS; nearly half (41% [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), 47% [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md)) of corpus-wide alterations in those two genes occurred in uterine sarcomas. [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) loss in 21% of ULMS, [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) alterations in ~33% ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- **Pathway alterations.** Cell cycle and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) pathways altered in ≥50% of samples in 8 of 22 core subtypes; PI3K pathway altered in [MRLS](../cancer_types/MRLS.md) (41%), [PECOMA](../cancer_types/PECOMA.md) (40%), ULMS (30%), [PLLS](../cancer_types/PLLS.md) (22%), UPS (20%), [LMS](../cancer_types/LMS.md) (20%); epigenetic pathway altered in [MPNST](../cancer_types/MPNST.md) (64%), ULMS (49%), [PLLS](../cancer_types/PLLS.md) (45%), [CHS](../cancer_types/CHS.md) (43%), UPS (42%), [MFS](../cancer_types/MFS.md) (36%), [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) (32%) ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- **Unsupervised clustering finds 17 genomic clusters that cross histology boundaries.** [WDLS](../cancer_types/WDLS.md), [DDLS](../cancer_types/DDLS.md), and [DSRCT](../cancer_types/DSRCT.md) had the lowest entropy (homogeneous), while [ULMS](../cancer_types/ULMS.md), [UPS](../cancer_types/UPS.md), and [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) had high entropy (multiple distinct genetic variants per histotype) ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md): altered across multiple subtypes; near-universal pathway hit in DDLS/WDLS via CDK4/MDM2 co-amplification (214/215, 99%). Co-occurs with [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md) in [ES](../cancer_types/ES.md) and [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) in [SFT](../cancer_types/SFT.md) ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md): significantly altered across multiple subtypes; pathway-level events in WDLS/DDLS (99%) and elsewhere ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) / [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md): co-amplified in >97% of [WDLS](../cancer_types/WDLS.md) and [DDLS](../cancer_types/DDLS.md); diagnostic and Level 3A actionable ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md): amplifications dominate in [INTS](../cancer_types/INTS.md) (44%) and [DDLS](../cancer_types/DDLS.md) (16%); promoter mutations dominate in [MRLS](../cancer_types/MRLS.md) (79%), [SFT](../cancer_types/SFT.md) (46%), [DDCHS](../cancer_types/DDCHS.md) (35%) ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- [GLI1](../genes/GLI1.md), [JUN](../genes/JUN.md): mutually exclusive amplifications in [DDLS](../cancer_types/DDLS.md); rationale for Shh-pathway inhibition and Jun/cyclin D1 axis study ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- [KIT](../genes/KIT.md) (72% Level 1), [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md) (7%): canonical [GIST](../cancer_types/GIST.md) drivers; mutually exclusive ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- [KDR](../genes/KDR.md), [FLT4](../genes/FLT4.md), [EPHA5](../genes/EPHA5.md): recurrent in [ANGS](../cancer_types/ANGS.md) (19/9/9%); [FLT4](../genes/FLT4.md) VUS cluster in kinase/C-terminus ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- [MED12](../genes/MED12.md): G44 hotspot missense in 16% of [ULMS](../cancer_types/ULMS.md) ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md): mutated in ~10% of [ES](../cancer_types/ES.md), co-occurring with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md), [MAX](../genes/MAX.md), [MGA](../genes/MGA.md): novel recurrent mutations in [GIST](../cancer_types/GIST.md) at 4/4/3% ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md): loss in 14% of [LMS](../cancer_types/LMS.md), 21% of [ULMS](../cancer_types/ULMS.md), 21% of [MRLS](../cancer_types/MRLS.md); 8% of [UPS](../cancer_types/UPS.md). Most often Level 4 actionable ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md): mutated in 25% of [MRLS](../cancer_types/MRLS.md); 6% in [ANGS](../cancer_types/ANGS.md) ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md), [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md): loss-of-function in 30% of [PECOMA](../cancer_types/PECOMA.md); combined TSC1/2 actionable in 37% ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md), [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [FANCA](../genes/FANCA.md), [RAD51](../genes/RAD51.md): DDR pathway altered in 9.6% of all samples; highest in ULMS (24%), [MPNST](../cancer_types/MPNST.md) (16%), PLLS (13%), [PECOMA](../cancer_types/PECOMA.md) (13%), ANGS (13%) ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- [SMARCB1](../genes/SMARCB1.md): loss-of-function deletions/truncations/intragenic fusions in 75% of [EPIS](../cancer_types/EPIS.md); Level 1 deletion in 66% ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md): predominant SWI/SNF alterations in 43% of [UAS](../cancer_types/UAS.md) ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md): loss-of-function in ≥10% of ULMS, PLLS, UPS, [MFS](../cancer_types/MFS.md), PECOMA, [LMS](../cancer_types/LMS.md), ANGS; ULMS rate ~1 in 3. Mutations dominate over deletions; intra-/intergenic fusions also captured. Cluster-defining; raises the possibility of ATR-inhibitor synthetic lethality (NCT03718091, NCT05071209, NCT04807816) ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md), [DICER1](../genes/DICER1.md): mutually exclusive in [ERMS](../cancer_types/ERMS.md); [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md) oncogenic in 16%, [DICER1](../genes/DICER1.md) oncogenic in 12% ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- [NCOR1](../genes/NCOR1.md): amplified in 19% of ULMS, 10% of LMS, 12% of [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) via 17p12-p11.2 cytoband co-amplification with [FLCN](../genes/FLCN.md), [MAP2K4](../genes/MAP2K4.md), [AURKB](../genes/AURKB.md) ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) (MLL4): altered in 16% of [MFS](../cancer_types/MFS.md), more frequently than in the closely related [UPS](../cancer_types/UPS.md) (6%) ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- [NF1](../genes/NF1.md): Level 4 deletions in 32% of [MPNST](../cancer_types/MPNST.md), 14% of UPS, 14% of [ERMS](../cancer_types/ERMS.md), 14% of PLLS ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md): deletions up to 48% in MPNST; broadly distributed actionable hits ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md), [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md): 27% of [CHS](../cancer_types/CHS.md) carry actionable IDH1/2 alterations ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).

## Clinical implications

- **Level 1 (FDA biomarker → FDA drug):** [KIT](../genes/KIT.md)/[PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md) mutations in [GIST](../cancer_types/GIST.md) ([imatinib](../drugs/imatinib.md)); [SMARCB1](../genes/SMARCB1.md) deletion in 66% of [EPIS](../cancer_types/EPIS.md) ([tazemetostat](../drugs/tazemetostat.md)) ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- **Level 3A:** [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md)/[MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) amplifications in [WDLS](../cancer_types/WDLS.md) and [DDLS](../cancer_types/DDLS.md) (>90%) — actionable via [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) inhibitors ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- **Level 3B / 4 highlights:** 37% combined Level-3B TSC1/2 deletions in [PECOMA](../cancer_types/PECOMA.md); 27% IDH1/2 in [CHS](../cancer_types/CHS.md); collective 31% targetable PI3K-pathway hits in [MRLS](../cancer_types/MRLS.md) ([PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md), [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md), [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md)); 21% [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) deletions in MRLS as Level 4; somatic [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) deletions in MPNST (32%), UPS (14%), ERMS (14%), PLLS (14%); [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) deletions up to 48% in MPNST ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- **[EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) inhibition rationale for uterine adenosarcoma.** Given SWI/SNF–[EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) synthetic-lethality precedent in [EPIS](../cancer_types/EPIS.md), the 43% SWI/SNF–altered rate in [UAS](../cancer_types/UAS.md) motivates exploration of [tazemetostat](../drugs/tazemetostat.md) ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- **[ATR](../genes/ATR.md) inhibitor basket trials.** [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) loss-of-function clusters define candidate populations for [ATR](../genes/ATR.md) inhibition; the authors cite NCT03718091, NCT05071209, NCT04807816 as ongoing investigations ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- **Immune checkpoint biomarker caveats.** TMB and MSI distributions are low in sarcoma; PTEN loss in ULMS has been associated with non-response to anti-PD-1 (per cited prior work). The authors argue the 10 mut/Mb pan-cancer TMB threshold may not transfer to mesenchymal tumors and call for subtype-specific correlative analysis on existing immunotherapy trials ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- **Prognostic candidate biomarker.** WGD as an adverse prognostic factor in metastatic UPS (p=0.022) and metastatic sarcoma overall (p=0.042) warrants prospective validation ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- **Genotype-based trial design.** High intra-subtype entropy (notably ULMS, UPS, OS) and shared driver clusters across histologies (e.g., TERT-driven MRLS+SFT) support genotype-defined cohorts over histology-defined cohorts in future sarcoma trials ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Targeted panel limitations.** [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) misses novel rearrangements, unknown drivers, focal CNAs outside captured regions, and certain mutational-signature features (e.g., HRD); whole-genome sequencing is needed for full characterization. Cytoband-level co-amplifications (e.g., MYOD-locus in LMS) cannot be assessed when adjacent genes (e.g., ALOX12B) are off-panel ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- **WGD-call discordance.** WGD estimates from MSK-IMPACT are concordant with WES in general but may differ from other analytical methods/platforms ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- **Sample-set heterogeneity.** Primary and metastatic samples were combined for most analyses; the authors note increased TMB and [FGA](../genes/FGA.md) in metastatic [GIST](../cancer_types/GIST.md) and fewer [JUN](../genes/JUN.md) amplifications in metastatic UPS vs primary. [ANGS](../cancer_types/ANGS.md) cohort has low tumor purity (<50% in majority of cases), which may underestimate event rates ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- **Limited racial diversity** in the single-institution cohort ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- **Functional follow-up needed.** [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) has multiple roles (H3.3 chaperone, ALT, genome integrity); patient-derived models are required to dissect which contribute to sarcomagenesis. Many [ANGS](../cancer_types/ANGS.md) FLT4/EPHA5 hits are VUS that may yet be functionally relevant ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
- **TMB threshold uncertainty in sarcoma.** Whether 10 mut/Mb is the right cutoff for immunotherapy benefit in mesenchymal neoplasms remains open ([PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)).
