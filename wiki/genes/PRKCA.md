# PRKCA

## Overview

PRKCA encodes protein kinase C alpha (PKC-alpha), a calcium-dependent serine/threonine kinase involved in cell signaling, proliferation, differentiation, and apoptosis. It plays roles in cell migration, angiogenesis, and oncogenic transformation. In hepatocellular carcinoma, phosphoproteomic profiling has shown reduced PRKCA kinase activity compared to normal liver, implicating the PKC pathway in [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) biology.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Kinase with decreased activity by KSEA in [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) vs normal liver in a 122-patient [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) multi-omics cohort (WES + RNA-seq + proteome + phosphoproteome) [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)**: Reduced PRKCA kinase activity by KSEA in a 122-patient European HCC cohort with multi-omics profiling [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Decreased PRKCA activity co-occurs with decreased [PRKACA](../genes/PRKACA.md), [PRKACG](../genes/PRKACG.md), [PRKCZ](../genes/PRKCZ.md), and [SGK1](../genes/SGK1.md) activity by KSEA in HCC vs normal liver [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic agents targeting PRKCA reported in this corpus.

## Open questions

- Functional significance of reduced PKC-alpha activity in HCC and its relationship to [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) or [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation subgroups awaits further investigation.
