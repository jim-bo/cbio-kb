# ADGRV1

## Overview

ADGRV1 (Adhesion G Protein-Coupled Receptor V1), also known as VLGR1 or MASS1, encodes the largest known G protein-coupled receptor. It is involved in extracellular matrix (ECM) organization and cell adhesion programs. In cancer, ADGRV1 has been identified as a recurrently mutated gene in combined hepatocellular-intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma (cHCC-ICC), where it co-segregates with ECM/cell-adhesion pathway alterations.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- ADGRV1 recurrently mutated in cHCC-ICC (combined hepatocellular and intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma); co-segregates with ECM and cell adhesion programs alongside [RPS6KA3](../genes/RPS6KA3.md) and [SYNE1](../genes/SYNE1.md) [PMID:31130341](../papers/31130341.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [MIXED](../cancer_types/MIXED.md) (cHCC-ICC): recurrent somatic mutation; ECM/cell-adhesion program involvement [PMID:31130341](../papers/31130341.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-mutated with RPS6KA3 and SYNE1 in a subset of cHCC-ICC tumors reflecting ECM/cell-adhesion pathway dysregulation [PMID:31130341](../papers/31130341.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting of ADGRV1 in cHCC-ICC reported in the corpus.

## Open questions

- Whether ADGRV1 mutations are driver or passenger events in cHCC-ICC awaits functional validation.
