# Automated real-world data integration improves cancer outcome prediction

**PMID:** 39506116 · **DOI:** 10.1038/s41586-024-08167-5 · **Journal:** Nature (2024)

## TL;DR

Jee, Fong, Pichotta et al. combined NLP annotations of free-text clinician, radiology and pathology notes with structured medication, demographic, tumor registry and tumor genomic data from 24,950 MSK patients to build MSK-CHORD, a clinicogenomic harmonized real-world dataset spanning [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) (n=7,809), breast (n=5,368), colorectal (n=5,543), prostate (n=3,211) and pancreatic (n=3,109) cancers. Machine learning models trained on MSK-CHORD that include NLP-derived features (e.g., sites of disease) outperformed models based on genomics or stage alone for overall survival prediction. Annotation of 705,241 radiology reports enabled discovery of genomic predictors of organ-specific metastasis, including a novel association between [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md) mutation and lower metastatic potential and longer immunotherapy response in [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) ([PMID:39506116](../papers/39506116.md)).

## Cohort & data

- 24,950 patients at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center sequenced on the [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) targeted panel, harmonized into the [msk_chord_2024](../datasets/msk_chord_2024.md) dataset.
- Five cancer types: [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) (n=7,809), [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) (n=5,368), [COADREAD](../cancer_types/COADREAD.md) (n=5,543), [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) (n=3,211), [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md) (n=3,109).
- NLP transformer models trained/validated on the Project GENIE BPC (PRISSMM) curation, using MSK-BPC (n=3,202 patients, 38,719 radiology reports) as labeled ground truth.
- 705,241 radiology reports annotated for organ-specific metastasis and progression.

## Key findings

- Integrated multimodal models incorporating NLP-derived clinical features (sites of disease, progression) outperformed models based on genomics or AJCC stage alone at predicting overall survival, validated by cross-validation and an external multi-institution dataset.
- NLP models achieved high per-site classification AUCs (generally 0.85–0.99) for metastases to bone, liver, lung, lymph node, adrenal, pleura and CNS.
- Pathway-level aggregation revealed gene/pathway enrichments associated with specific organ sites of metastasis; [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) oncogenic alterations were enriched in brain and liver metastases.
- Of 5,957 [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) patients, 204 (3%) had [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md) driver mutations, which predicted longer overall survival and lower rates of CNS metastasis.
- [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md) mutation was associated with longer time to next treatment or death following immune checkpoint blockade but not cytotoxic chemotherapy or molecularly targeted therapy; the immunotherapy association held among TMB-low (<10 mut/Mb) patients and replicated in two independent validation cohorts (DFCI and a commercial RWD cohort).
- SETD2 oncogenic alterations were positively associated with [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) and [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) alterations, negatively associated with [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) and [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) alterations and mucinous subtype (q<0.05, Benjamini–Hochberg).
- SETD2 mutation was associated with a small but statistically significant increase in TMB (Mann–Whitney P = 2×10⁻⁹).

## Genes & alterations

- [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md) — oncogenic driver mutations in ~3% of LUAD; biomarker of longer [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md), lower CNS metastasis, and improved immune checkpoint blockade response in [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md).
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — oncogenic alterations enriched in brain and liver metastases.
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) — co-altered with SETD2 in LUAD.
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md), [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) — mutually exclusive with SETD2 in LUAD.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [STK11](../genes/STK11.md), [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md), [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — included in LUAD pathway/metastasis analyses.

## Clinical implications

- SETD2 driver mutation is proposed as an uncommon but promising biomarker of immune checkpoint blockade response in LUAD not explained by TMB, PD-L1, smoking status or histologic subtype; the authors suggest prospective validation.
- Automated NLP extraction from unstructured notes is feasible at scale and materially improves real-world survival prediction over stage- or genomics-only models, supporting integration of NLP-derived features into clinicogenomic decision support.
- MSK-CHORD is released as a public resource for real-world oncologic research.

## Limitations & open questions

- Single-institution training cohort (MSK) with targeted (MSK-IMPACT) rather than exome sequencing; generalizability required external validation.
- NLP annotations, though high AUC, are not perfect ground truth and may propagate curation biases from PRISSMM/BPC.
- Mechanism linking SETD2 loss to improved immunotherapy response in LUAD remains unresolved.
- Only five cancer types included; extension to rarer tumors and non-MSK care settings is an open question.
