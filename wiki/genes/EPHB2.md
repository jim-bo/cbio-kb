# EPHB2

## Overview

EPHB2 (Ephrin type-B receptor 2) is a receptor tyrosine kinase that plays important roles in intestinal epithelial cell organization, stem cell positioning, and WNT/stem-cell signaling programs. It is a WNT target gene whose expression marks intestinal stem and progenitor cells; its over-expression is part of the activated WNT/stem-cell transcriptional program observed in adenoma-derived cancer stem cells and colorectal cancer.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Over-expressed as part of the WNT/stem-cell program in adenoma stem cells (ASCs) and MSS colorectal cancer cells, alongside [LGR5](../genes/LGR5.md), [OLFM4](../genes/OLFM4.md), [ASCL2](../genes/ASCL2.md), and [MYC](../genes/MYC.md); EPHB2 over-expression reflects activated intestinal stem-cell identity in precancerous and cancerous lesions [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Colorectal cancer (MSS subtype): EPHB2 over-expression as part of WNT-driven stem-cell gene program in adenoma-derived stem cells [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-expressed with LGR5, OLFM4, ASCL2, and MYC as part of the WNT/stem-cell transcriptional signature in ASCs and MSS colorectal cancer; this program is driven by [APC](../genes/APC.md) loss and activated WNT signaling [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- EPHB2 over-expression marks the WNT-active stem-cell state in colorectal adenomas; targeting this population may be relevant for chemoprevention in high-risk polyp patients [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Open questions

- Whether EPHB2 expression level alone predicts adenoma-to-carcinoma progression risk independent of other WNT-pathway markers remains to be established [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)
