# MDK

## Overview

MDK (midkine) is a heparin-binding growth factor involved in cell growth, survival, and differentiation. In colorectal cancer biology, MDK has been identified as a fetal-program gene that is re-activated in sessile serrated lesion cells (SSCs) as part of the gastric-type metaplasia program characterizing the serrated polyp-to-MSI-H CRC pathway.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- MDK is a fetal-program gene re-activated in sessile serrated lesion cells (SSCs) as part of the gastric-type metaplasia program observed in serrated polyp progression [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Colorectal cancer (COAD/READ): MDK expression marks the gastric-type metaplastic cell state in SSCs that distinguishes the serrated pathway from conventional adenoma progression [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- MDK co-expressed with [MSLN](../genes/MSLN.md), [AQP5](../genes/AQP5.md), [TACSTD2](../genes/TACSTD2.md) (TROP2), and [TFF2](../genes/TFF2.md) as part of the gastric/endodermal program in SSCs [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting reported in the corpus.

## Open questions

- Whether MDK re-activation is a driver or passenger event in serrated polyp progression remains undefined.
