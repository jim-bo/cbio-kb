# SAE1

## Overview

SAE1 encodes the small subunit of the SUMO-activating enzyme (E1) heterodimer (SAE1/UBA2), which initiates the SUMOylation cascade. SUMOylation is a critical post-translational modification regulating a broad range of cellular processes including DNA repair, transcription, and cell-cycle progression. In the context of cancer, SAE1/UBA2 protein levels have been linked to synthetic lethal vulnerability in microsatellite-instable (MSI) tumors — specifically, to [WRN](../genes/WRN.md) helicase knockdown sensitivity.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- SUMO E1 heterodimer (SAE1/UBA2) abundance predicts WRN knockdown sensitivity in MSI CCLE cell lines; [PCSK7](../genes/PCSK7.md) mutation is uniquely associated with SUMO E1 protein levels, suggesting a connection between endopeptidase activity and SUMOylation capacity [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
