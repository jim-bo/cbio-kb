**PMID:** 38355797 · **DOI:** 10.1097/HC9.0000000000000837 · **Journal:** Hepatology Communications (2025)

## TL;DR

A narrative review synthesizing how dysregulated lipid metabolism drives hepatocellular carcinoma ([[[HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)]]) progression and resistance to standard-of-care therapies (tyrosine kinase inhibitors, immune checkpoint inhibitors, radiotherapy). The authors organize the evidence around six lipid-metabolic signatures of HCC — enhanced lipid uptake, reactivation of de novo lipogenesis (DNL), increased fatty acid oxidation (FAO), suppression of lipid peroxidation, bioactive lipid synthesis, and cholesterol biosynthesis — and argue that targeting these pathways (including via dietary intervention) is a promising strategy to resensitize resistant HCC cells [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Cohort & data

- Review article — no primary cohort. Synthesizes preclinical (mouse, organoid, cell-line) and clinical evidence on lipid metabolism in [[HCC]].
- Clinical context cited: real-world response rates of 26%–28% for [atezolizumab](../drugs/atezolizumab.md) + [bevacizumab](../drugs/bevacizumab.md) and 16%–23% for [lenvatinib](../drugs/lenvatinib.md) in HCC, with median PFS < 12 months; objective response rate of 7% for subsequent TKIs/ICIs after progression on atezolizumab + bevacizumab [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- Epidemiology: MAFLD affects >25% of adults worldwide (>1.26 billion in 2021); ~49% of HCC patients have MAFLD and ~12% have MAFLD as the sole underlying liver disease [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Key findings

- HCC reprograms six lipid-metabolic axes: (1) enhanced lipid uptake ([CD36](../genes/CD36.md), FATPs, FABPs); (2) reactivation of de novo lipogenesis ([FASN](../genes/FASN.md), [SCD](../genes/SCD.md), [ACACA](../genes/ACACA.md) via SREBP1/MYC and PI3K/AKT/mTOR); (3) increased fatty acid oxidation (CPT1, PPARα); (4) suppression of lipid peroxidation/ferroptosis ([SLC7A11](../genes/SLC7A11.md), [GPX4](../genes/GPX4.md)); (5) bioactive lipid synthesis (PGE2, LPA, PA, leukotrienes); (6) cholesterol biosynthesis ([HMGCR](../genes/HMGCR.md), [SQLE](../genes/SQLE.md), [MVK](../genes/MVK.md)) [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- CD36 is upregulated in HCC and drives progression/metastasis via Src/AKT/mTOR and YAP signaling; CD36 upregulation is also observed in irradiation-resistant HCC cells, implicating it in radiotherapy resistance. The CD36-neutralizing antibody PLT012 suppressed HCC progression preclinically, recruited cytotoxic progenitor-exhausted CD8 T cells, suppressed [FOXP3](../genes/FOXP3.md)+ Tregs, and sensitized HCC to ICIs [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- FATP4 ([SLC27A4](../genes/SLC27A4.md)) selectively imports MUFAs that protect HCC cells from lipid peroxidation/ferroptosis, conferring [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md) resistance. FATP5 ([SLC27A5](../genes/SLC27A5.md)) deficiency, conversely, promotes HCC via KEAP1/NRF2, AKT/mTOR, and EMT activation [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- [FABP1](../genes/FABP1.md) helps HCC cells outcompete T cells for long-chain fatty acids (especially linoleic acid), impairing T-cell activation; combined LINC01116 inhibition (an upstream regulator of FABP1) plus anti-PD-1 reduces HCC progression and extends survival in preclinical models. [FABP5](../genes/FABP5.md) protects cells from lipid peroxidation by suppressing FAO; the FABP5 inhibitor SBFI-103 restored FAO, increased CD80/CD86+ macrophages and CD44+CD62L− effector T cells, and suppressed HCC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- DNL is reactivated in HCC via MYC-driven SREBP1 transcription plus PI3K/AKT/mTOR-mediated nuclear translocation; post-transcriptional control includes SIRT3 deacetylation of SREBP1 and KAT8 acetylation of FASN. Cardiolipin synthesis maintains mitochondrial integrity and inhibits cytochrome-c release, driving radiotherapy resistance. Enhanced DNL and lipid-droplet formation are observed in sorafenib-resistant cells; phosphatidic acid generated by DNL activates oncogenic signaling downstream of RTKs and confers lenvatinib resistance [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- FAO is required by Wnt/β-catenin–driven (APC-loss or CTNNB1-mutant) HCC via PPARα/CPT1 activation; [etomoxir](../drugs/etomoxir.md) (CPT1 inhibitor) impairs tumor development. NRas-driven FAO produces ROS and DNA damage that initiates premalignant-to-tumor transition. Conversely, p53-mutation–driven and DEN+high-fat-diet HCC require FAO suppression — etiology and genetic background dictate FAO dependence [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- PD-L1 ([CD274](../genes/CD274.md)) interacts with [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) and [ITGB4](../genes/ITGB4.md) to activate AKT/mTOR and induce lipid accumulation, suggesting a positive feedback loop between DNL and PD-L1 that escapes immune surveillance. Conversely, ACLY-driven DNL lowers the PUFA:SFA ratio and suppresses cGAS–STING, thereby decreasing PD-L1 expression and conferring resistance to anti-PD-L1 therapy [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- Clinical signal: reanalyses of trial data indicate MAFLD-related HCC is less responsive to ICIs, while a small retrospective cohort suggests intratumor steatosis is associated with greater ICI response — the field is unresolved [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Genes & alterations

- [[CD36]] — upregulated; drives Src/AKT/mTOR + YAP signaling and lipid uptake; PLT012 antibody sensitizes HCC to ICIs.
- [[SLC27A4]] (FATP4) — upregulated; MUFA import protects against ferroptosis and confers sorafenib resistance.
- [[SLC27A5]] (FATP5) — loss promotes HCC via KEAP1/NRF2, AKT/mTOR, EMT.
- [[FABP1]], [[[FABP4](../genes/FABP4.md)]], [[FABP5]] — upregulated; FABP1 outcompetes T cells for long-chain fatty acids; FABP5 suppresses FAO to block ferroptosis.
- [[FASN]], [[SCD]], [[ACACA]] — DNL effectors; FASN loss blocks HCC formation; SCD desaturates SFA→MUFA to evade ferroptosis.
- [[[SREBF1](../genes/SREBF1.md)]] (SREBP1) — master DNL transcription factor; activated by [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) and PI3K/AKT/mTOR.
- [[MYC]] — frequently amplified; transcriptionally drives SREBP1.
- [[[ACLY](../genes/ACLY.md)]] — DNL effector; suppresses cGAS–STING/PD-L1, confers anti-PD-L1 resistance.
- [[[PPARA](../genes/PPARA.md)]], [[[PPARG](../genes/PPARG.md)]] — drive DNL/FAO programs.
- [[[CPT1A](../genes/CPT1A.md)]] — rate-limiting FAO enzyme; etomoxir target.
- [[SLC7A11]], [[GPX4]] — suppress lipid peroxidation; targeted by sorafenib/regorafenib-induced ferroptosis.
- [[[KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md)]] / [[[NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md)]] (NRF2) — activated upon FATP5 loss to promote HCC.
- [[[AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md)]], [[[MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md)]] — central nodes activating SREBP1 nuclear translocation and DNL.
- [[[APC](../genes/APC.md)]] / [[[CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md)]] — Wnt/β-catenin-driven HCC depends on FAO via PPARα/CPT1.
- [[[NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md)]] — premalignant FAO activation generates ROS/DNA damage during tumor initiation.
- [[CD274]] (PD-L1) — binds [[EGFR]] and [[ITGB4]] to activate AKT/mTOR and induce lipid accumulation.
- [[[STAT3](../genes/STAT3.md)]] — suppressed by palmitate; JNK/STAT signaling in HCC drives M2-macrophage polarization.
- [[HMGCR]], [[SQLE]], [[MVK]] — cholesterol biosynthesis enzymes implicated in HCC.

## Clinical implications

- Targeting lipid metabolism (CD36, FATP4, FABP1/5, FASN, SCD, CPT1, ACLY, HMGCR) is proposed as a strategy to resensitize HCC to TKIs (sorafenib, lenvatinib, [regorafenib](../drugs/regorafenib.md)), ICIs (atezolizumab + bevacizumab; [nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md) + [ipilimumab](../drugs/ipilimumab.md); [durvalumab](../drugs/durvalumab.md) + [tremelimumab](../drugs/tremelimumab.md); [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md); [camrelizumab](../drugs/camrelizumab.md) + [rivoceranib](../drugs/rivoceranib.md)), and radiotherapy [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- Anti-CD36 (PLT012) and anti-FABP5 (SBFI-103) are highlighted as preclinical leads with ICI-sensitizing or T-cell-activating effects [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- Dietary interventions modulating lipid composition (e.g., ω3-PUFA EPA, low-SFA diets) are proposed as low-cost, low-side-effect adjuncts to resensitize resistant HCC — though clinical evidence is preliminary [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- Etiology matters: MAFLD-related HCC may have distinct ICI responsiveness compared with viral HCC; predictive biomarkers based on lipid-metabolic state are not yet validated [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Limitations & open questions

- Review is mechanistic; no new primary patient cohort or genomic dataset is generated.
- Contradictory evidence on DNL and PD-L1 (DNL both promotes and suppresses PD-L1, depending on the gene targeted and model used) is unresolved [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- Role of FAO in HCC is etiology-dependent (Wnt/β-catenin-driven HCC is FAO-addicted; p53-mutant and DEN+HFD HCC require FAO suppression) — predictive biomarkers for FAO-targeting agents are lacking [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- Clinical signal on intratumor steatosis vs. MAFLD-related HCC for ICI response is inconsistent across small retrospective cohorts and trial reanalyses; prospective validation is needed [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- Most lipid-targeting compounds (PLT012, SBFI-103, etomoxir) are preclinical; translational pharmacology (toxicity, selectivity, hepatotoxicity in cirrhotic livers) is largely open.
