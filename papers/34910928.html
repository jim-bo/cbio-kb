Differential pre-malignant programs and microenvironment chart distinct paths to malignancy in human colorectal polyps

Authors

Bob Chen

Cherie’ R. Scurrah

Eliot T. McKinley

Alan J. Simmons

Marisol A. Ramirez-Solano

Nicholas O. Markham

Cody N. Heiser

Martha J. Shrubsole

Robert J. Coffey

Ken S. Lau

Doi

10.1016/j.cell.2021.11.031

PMID: 34910928 · DOI: 10.1016/j.cell.2021.11.031 · Journal: Cell (2021)

TL;DR

Chen et al. build a multi-omic pre-cancer atlas of human colorectal polyps from the COLON MAP / HTAN cohort, integrating single-cell RNA-seq, whole-exome and targeted DNA sequencing, bulk RNA-seq, and multiplex imaging across 62 polyp/tumor participants (128 scRNA-seq datasets, 142,065 cells). They show that conventional adenomas (TAs, TVAs) arise from APC-truncation-driven WNT-pathway expansion of LGR5+ crypt-base stem cells, while serrated polyps (HPs, SSLs) instead arise from differentiated luminal-surface cells through BRAF-V600E-driven gastric-type metaplasia (MUC5AC+, CDX2-low). Metaplastic serrated lesions are accompanied by a cytotoxic CD8+ T-cell microenvironment that precedes hypermutation, and MSI-H CRCs retain spatially heterogeneous metaplastic vs. stem-like regions in which cytotoxic immune cells are selectively depleted from stem-like compartments. The atlas is deposited as cBioPortal study crc_hta11_htan_2021 and on the HTAN data portal.

Cohort & data

  • Participants / specimens — COLON MAP study at Vanderbilt University Medical Center (first 56 participants with polyps). 62 tumors profiled by scRNA-seq across a discovery set (65 specimens, 30 tumors) and validation set (63 specimens, 32 tumors), bisected for multi-assay analysis; specimens collected ~1 year apart.
  • Cancer types — colorectal pre-malignant polyps and CRC (COAD, COADREAD); AD subtypes (tubular [TA], tubulovillous [TVA]) and serrated subtypes (hyperplastic [HP: goblet-cell-rich GCHP, microvesicular MVHP] and sessile serrated lesions [SSL]).
  • CRC validation cohorts — 7 fresh CRCs (2 MSI-H, 5 MSS) from CHTN Western Division; 60-sample external Broad scRNA-seq CRC cohort (32 MSI-H, 28 MSS) (Pelka et al., 2021, re-analyzed); 26 additional CRC whole-tumor blocks (14 MSI-H, 12 MSS); a CHTN Mid-Atlantic 54-individual CRC progression TMA.
  • Orthogonal bulk cohorts — bulk RNA-seq on 66 polyps and targeted DNA sequencing on 281 polyps from the Tennessee Colorectal Polyp Study (TCPS); 63 TCGA CRC bulk RNA-seq datasets used as external validation; TCGA WES used for non-APC WNT-pathway mutation enrichment in MSI-H CRCs.
  • Assays / methods — scRNA-seq (inDrop / TruDrop platform, dropkick QC); whole-exome-seq on FFPE polyps and CRCs (BWA hg19, GATK4 Mutect2 paired); targeted-dna-seq at 500X on a custom literature-derived panel; bulk RNA-seq; multiplexed immunofluorescence (MxIF) on Cell DIVE; iterative-chromogen MxIHC; CytoTRACE, SCENIC, RNA velocity, DENDRO, p-Creode trajectory inference, and CMS-classifier for consensus molecular subtype scoring.
  • Datasets / portals — cBioPortal study crc_hta11_htan_2021; raw scRNA-seq and imaging on the HTAN data portal (https://data.humantumoratlas.org/); HTAN grant U2CCA233291.

Key findings

  • Two distinct neoplastic cell populations. Regulon-space (SCENIC) clustering of 142,065 single cells identified an AD-specific cell (ASC) population enriched in TA/TVA (p < 1E-4, MWU) and a serrated-specific cell (SSC) population enriched in SSLs/HPs (p < 1E-4, MWU), reproducible between discovery and validation sets.
  • ADs = WNT-driven stem expansion. ASCs over-express the LGR5/OLFM4/ASCL2/AXIN2/RNF43/EPHB2 stem program and possess CytoTRACE-inferred stem potential higher than normal stem cells from the same individuals.
  • SSLs = gastric metaplasia from a committed lineage. SSCs lack WNT/stem signatures, express functional goblet-cell genes (TFF3, MUC2) but lack the master secretory TF ATOH1, express non-colonic gastric/endodermal genes (MUC5AC, AQP5, TACSTD2/TROP2, TFF2, MUC17, MSLN), down-regulate the hindgut TF CDX2, and reactivate a fetal program including MDK.
  • Mutational profile (WES on FFPE polyps). APC mutated in 85% of TAs and both TVAs; KRAS in only 1/13 TAs (8%) vs. both TVAs; BRAF-V600E in 89% of SSLs (8/9), 0/3 GCHPs, 2/3 (67%) MVHPs; KRAS and APC absent from SSLs; BRAF absent from ADs. No SSL was hypermutated; biallelic MMR loss not detected in any polyp.
  • Targeted sequencing of 281 polyps. APC mutations 67% (TA) → 91% (TVA); KRAS 5% (TA) → 42% (TVA); BRAF 1% (TA) vs. 4% (TVA) vs. 67% (SSL). Non-APC WNT-pathway genes (RNF43, ZNRF43) uncommon in SSLs. SSLs again lacked the hypermutation phenotype seen in some TA/TVAs.
  • Top-down vs. bottom-up tumorigenesis. MxIF shows MUC5AC+ neoplastic cells first appear at the luminal crypt surface in GCHPs, extending downward in MVHPs and SSLs; OLFM4/SOX9 (stem markers) decrease, CDX2 declines and is absent in SSLs. p-Creode trajectory inference and RNA-velocity place ASCs downstream of stem cells but place SSCs downstream of absorptive progenitors/colonocytes. DENDRO phylogeny on 34 polyps (24 ADs, 11 SERs) shows ASCs are genetically closer to crypt-base stem cells than SSCs (p < 5E-2, MWU); SSCs cluster with differentiated colonocytes/absorptive progenitors.
  • CRC mutational landscapes. WES of 7 in-house CRCs: MSS CRCs carry APC (100%), KRAS (35%), TP53 (71%); MSI-H CRCs have fewer of these (33%/0%/7%) but more BRAF (53% vs. 0%) and are universally hyper-mutated. MSI-H CRCs accumulate non-APC WNT-pathway activating events: RNF43 (60%), TCF7L2 (53%), ZNRF3 (33%), APC2 (27%), AXIN2 (20%), FAT1 (33%), FAT2 (47%), FAT4 (40%), corroborated by TCGA WES.
  • CRCs retain precursor signatures. MSS CRC cells over-express a regenerative crypt-base stem signature; MSI-H CRC cells retain a metaplastic signature. ASCs and MSS CRC cells score high for CMS2; SSCs and MSI-H CRC cells score low for CMS2 and high for CMS1/CMS3 by the cms-classifier (SSP mode); CMS4 was not enriched in any tumor cell population.
  • MSI-H CRCs gain stemness on top of metaplasia. CytoTRACE inferred stem potential is higher in MSI-H CRC cells than in SSCs (and higher in MSS CRC cells than ASCs); spatial whole-slide MxIF shows none of 17 MSS CRCs stain for MUC5AC but 13/14 MSI-H CRCs do, with variable intratumoral MUC5AC heterogeneity. MUC5AC-low regions of MSI-H CRCs are OLFM4+ stem-like and CDX2+; SOX9 is broadly over-expressed in MSI-H CRCs.
  • Cytotoxic immunity precedes hypermutation. CD8+ T cells, NK cells, and γδT cells are significantly increased in SERs vs. ADs, before hypermutation, and this dichotomy persists into MSI-H vs. MSS CRC. SSCs express a lymphocyte-attracting cytokine signature and significantly higher antigen-processing/presentation signatures than ASCs; ASCs express a monocyte-attracting chemokine signature. MxIF: SERs have more T cells, more CD8+ T cells, and a higher CD8/CD4 ratio than ADs.
  • Intratumoral immune heterogeneity in MSI-H CRC. CD8+ T cells are enriched in MUC5AC+ metaplastic regions and depleted from OLFM4+ stem-like regions of MSI-H CRCs; MSS CRCs are homogeneously OLFM4+ and T-cell-poor.
  • Mouse genetic dissection. Driving identical Apc loss from stem cells (Lrig1-CreERT2) vs. non-stem differentiated cells (Mist1-CreERT2) yields tumors with identical WNT activation but opposite immune microenvironments: Mist1-driven tumors recruit cytotoxic CD8+ T cells, present more class-2 antigen (DQ-OVA assay, I-A/I-E surface staining), and more efficiently stimulate OT-II CD4+ T-cell proliferation. Lrig1-driven tumors instead accrue dysfunctional CD4+ T cells expressing Pdcd1 (PD1), Ctla4, Prdm1, Havcr2 (TIM3), the Foxp3 regulon, and a large myeloid/MDSC infiltrate including PD-L1+ neutrophils.
  • Stemness gradient ↔︎ antigen presentation. Lrig1 tumor-specific cells score higher on ISCI; Mist1 TSCs score higher on ISCII/III and on MHCII machinery. In humans, antigen- presentation machinery expression is inversely proportional to stemness in normal colon epithelium; serrated human tumoroids show reduced stem capacity and increased antigen-presentation signature compared with AD tumoroids. IFN-γ treatment of AD tumoroids up-regulates antigen-presentation machinery.

Genes & alterations

  • APC — truncating loss-of-function initiates AD pathway; 85% of TAs, 100% of TVAs (WES), 67%→91% TA→TVA (targeted); 100% of MSS CRCs vs. 33% of MSI-H CRCs.
  • APC2 — activating WNT-pathway mutations in 27% of MSI-H CRCs (this study’s WES) as a non-APC mechanism of WNT reactivation.
  • KRAS — 1/13 (8%) TAs vs. 2/2 TVAs by WES; 5%→42% TA→TVA in 281 polyps; 35% of MSS CRCs vs. 0% of MSI-H CRCs.
  • BRAF — V600E in 89% of SSLs (8/9) and 67% of MVHPs (2/3) but 0/3 GCHPs and 0% of ADs by WES; 67% of SSLs vs. 1%/4% of TAs/TVAs by targeted sequencing; 53% of MSI-H CRCs vs. 0% of MSS CRCs.
  • TP53 — 71% of MSS CRCs vs. 7% of MSI-H CRCs.
  • MLH1 — protein and gene expression in SSLs comparable to ADs and higher than mean MSI-H CRC level; SSLs in this cohort had not yet acquired biallelic MMR loss / hypermutation.
  • RNF43 — 60% mutated in MSI-H CRCs; activating WNT-pathway events uncommon in SSLs.
  • ZNRF3 — 33% of MSI-H CRCs.
  • TCF7L2 — 53% of MSI-H CRCs.
  • AXIN2 — 20% of MSI-H CRCs; also a WNT-target marker over-expressed in ASCs.
  • FAT1, FAT2, FAT4 — 33%, 47%, 40% of MSI-H CRCs respectively (atypical-cadherin WNT-related mutations).
  • LGR5, OLFM4, ASCL2, EPHB2, MYC — over-expressed WNT/stem-cell program in ASCs and MSS CRC cells.
  • SOX9 — stem marker reduced in HPs/SSLs by MxIF; broadly over-expressed in MSI-H CRC by whole-slide staining.
  • CDX2 — hindgut homeobox TF lost (transcriptionally and by IHC) in SSCs / SSLs and variably reduced in MSI-H CRCs; CDX2-low + MUC5AC+ is the proposed biomarker pair for SSL diagnosis.
  • MUC5AC — gastric-type metaplasia marker; defines SSCs and distinguishes 13/14 MSI-H from 0/17 MSS CRCs.
  • AQP5, TACSTD2 (TROP2), TFF2, MSLN — co-expressed gastric/endodermal program in SSCs.
  • TFF3, MUC2 — goblet-cell genes retained in SSCs despite loss of ATOH1.
  • MDK — fetal-program gene re-activated in SSCs.
  • FOXP3 — regulon activity elevated in AD-derived vs. normal colon CD4+ T cells, supporting Treg-dependent immunosuppression in ADs.
  • PDCD1 (PD1), CTLA4 — expressed on dysfunctional CD4+ T cells in Apc-driven stem-cell mouse tumors, paralleling the immunosuppressive AD microenvironment.

Clinical implications

  • Diagnostic biomarker for SSLs. The authors propose MUC5AC+ / CDX2-low staining as an immunohistochemical readout to confirm SSL diagnosis, which the revised WHO criterion (a single “architecturally distorted serrated crypt”) currently makes difficult in routine practice.
  • Re-thinking the cytotoxic CRC microenvironment. A cytotoxic immune infiltrate is present in serrated polyps before hypermutation, implying neoantigen-independent triggers (epithelial differentiation state / antigen-presentation machinery / microbial exposure). This decouples “cytotoxic-friendly” tumors from MSI-H neoantigen load and motivates interrogating non-hypermutated CRCs with serrated origin or metaplastic features as potential immunotherapy candidates.
  • Intratumoral heterogeneity may shape immunotherapy response in MSI-H CRC. MSI-H CRCs contain spatially distinct metaplastic (CD8-rich) and stem-like (CD8-depleted) compartments; the degree to which a given MSI-H tumor has acquired stem-like properties may modulate immunotherapy response, an explicit hypothesis the authors flag for future testing.
  • Prevention / surveillance. The two divergent precursor pathways and their microenvironmental signatures argue for differentiated surveillance intervals and potentially distinct chemopreventive (or pre/probiotic) strategies for adenomas vs. serrated polyps.

Limitations & open questions

  • Polyps were predominantly small (median ≤5 mm), and FFPE WES yielded usable somatic calls for only ~half of polyps; some mutation-frequency estimates rest on small denominators (e.g., n = 9 SSLs, n = 3 MVHPs, n = 3 GCHPs).
  • No specific enrichment for non-epithelial populations was performed, leaving rare stromal/immune subsets under-sampled.
  • Cross-sectional design — no longitudinal sampling of the same polyp through progression, because lesions were fully removed at colonoscopy.
  • The exact molecular pathway by which differentiated/metaplastic tumor cells maintain antigen-presentation competence, and when the immune system first engages tumor neoantigens, remain undefined.
  • Whether acquisition of stem-like properties in MSI-H CRCs predicts reduced immunotherapy benefit is hypothesized but not tested.
