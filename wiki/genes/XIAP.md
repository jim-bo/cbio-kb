# XIAP

## Overview

XIAP (X-linked inhibitor of apoptosis protein, BIRC4) is a member of the IAP family that suppresses apoptosis by inhibiting caspase-3, -7, and -9. XIAP and related IAP proteins (cIAP1/2, encoded by BIRC2/BIRC3) are targeted by SMAC mimetics/antagonists in NPC.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- XIAP (along with BIRC2/BIRC3) is an inhibitor-of-apoptosis protein targeted by the IAP antagonist tolinapant in combination with radiotherapy in NPC. [PMID:24952746](../papers/24952746.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **NPC** — XIAP is a therapeutic target; tolinapant (IAP antagonist) + radiotherapy is under clinical investigation. [PMID:24952746](../papers/24952746.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- No co-occurrence or mutual exclusivity data reported in the current corpus.

## Therapeutic relevance

- Tolinapant (SMAC mimetic/IAP antagonist targeting XIAP and cIAP1/2) + radiotherapy in NPC. [PMID:24952746](../papers/24952746.md)

## Open questions

- Clinical efficacy and biomarker correlates of IAP inhibition in NPC are not yet established; combination with radiotherapy is investigational.
