# AFF1

## Overview

AFF1 (AF4/FMR2 Family Member 1) encodes a scaffold component of the super elongation complex (SEC) that regulates RNA Pol II pause-release. AFF1 is most commonly implicated in cancer as a fusion partner in [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) (MLL)-rearranged leukemias, where the AFF1-KMT2A fusion drives transcriptional dysregulation and is a dependency for the fusion-bearing cell lines.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- AFF1-KMT2A fusion identified in CCLE cancer cell lines; AFF1 dependence is linked to the fusion event, establishing AFF1 as a fusion-driven dependency in cell line models [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)
- KMT2A fusion partner in therapy-related MDS/AL arising in high-risk neuroblastoma patients; detected by RNA capture at MSKCC in serial bone-marrow surveillance [PMID:35078859](../papers/35078859.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Fusion-driven dependency observed in leukemia cell lines bearing AFF1-KMT2A rearrangements [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- AFF1-KMT2A fusion (analogous to ESR1-CCDC170 → [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) dependence model): fusion drives dependency on the 5' partner gene [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- AFF1 dependence in fusion-bearing lines suggests targeting the SEC complex or KMT2A pathway as a potential therapeutic strategy; no approved drugs directly targeting AFF1 at time of writing.

## Open questions

- Clinical validation of AFF1 dependence in patient-derived models beyond cancer cell lines is pending.
