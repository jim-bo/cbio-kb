# RHEB

## Overview

RHEB (Ras homolog enriched in brain) is a small GTPase that directly activates mTORC1 when bound to GTP. Upstream negative regulation by the TSC1/TSC2 tumor suppressor complex (which acts as a GTPase-activating protein for RHEB) is a key mTOR pathway control node. Loss of TSC1/TSC2 function or activating mutations in RHEB lead to constitutive mTORC1 activation, driving cell growth and proliferation. RHEB hotspot mutations are a mechanism of acquired resistance to mTOR inhibitors.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- **Acquired RHEB Y35S hotspot at [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md) progression:** In serial sequencing of 12 metastatic panNET patients, one patient acquired RHEB Y35S in a progressing liver lesion while on everolimus. The mutant allele fraction rose on continued observation. This patient's tumor also progressed from grade G1 to G3. RHEB Y35S is an established activating mutation that directly activates mTORC1 independently of [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) regulation. [PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)
- Note: RHEB was not included on early versions of the MSK-IMPACT panel used in this study; some early-cohort samples were not interrogated for RHEB, affecting allele-frequency comparisons over time. [PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[PANET](../cancer_types/PANET.md) (pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor):** RHEB Y35S acquired at mTOR inhibitor progression in one patient in a metastatic panNET cohort (80 patients, MSK-IMPACT [panet_msk_2018](../datasets/panet_msk_2018.md)). This represents a direct escape mechanism from everolimus — mTORC1 reactivated by a downstream RHEB mutation despite upstream blockade. [PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- RHEB Y35S was acquired in a patient who also had TSC2 splice mutation acquired at a separate lesion's progression — suggesting parallel resistance evolution in different metastatic sites. Part of the broader mTOR-pathway resistance landscape that includes acquired [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) Q298*, TSC2 splice, and [AKT2](../genes/AKT2.md) G16D in the same cohort. [PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- RHEB hotspot mutations (Y35S) represent a mechanism of acquired resistance to mTOR inhibitors (everolimus) in panNET by constitutively activating mTORC1 downstream of the drug's target (mTORC1 complex). Detection of acquired RHEB mutations in serial tumor biopsies supports the authors' recommendation for real-time NGS at disease progression to characterize resistance. [PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)

## Open questions

- Whether RHEB Y35S also confers cross-resistance to everolimus analogs or combined PI3K/mTOR inhibitors is not addressed. The frequency of acquired RHEB mutations across the broader panNET population on mTOR inhibitors is not established from this small cohort. [PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)
