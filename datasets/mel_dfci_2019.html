DFCI Advanced Melanoma Anti-PD1 ICB Cohort (2019)

Overview

This clinically annotated cohort of 144 patients with advanced melanoma was assembled at Dana-Farber Cancer Institute to identify genomic and transcriptomic predictors of response to anti-PD1 immune-checkpoint blockade. Pre-treatment tumor biopsies were profiled by whole-exome sequencing (matched normal) and bulk RNA-seq. The cohort includes cutaneous (72.9%), occult (13.2%), acral (6.9%), and mucosal (6.9%) melanoma subtypes, with patients treated by nivolumab (n=59) or pembrolizumab (n=85) and stratified by prior ipilimumab exposure (41.7% pretreated). The dataset was used to build parsimonious logistic-regression models of primary resistance stratified by prior ipilimumab status, and was cross-validated against the independent mel_iatlas_riaz_nivolumab_2017 cohort.

Composition

  • Total patients: 144 (after WES QC; 206 initially identified); 121 with RNA-seq
  • Cancer subtypes: cutaneous SKCM n=105 (72.9%), occult n=19 (13.2%), acral n=10 (6.9%), mucosal n=10 (6.9%) — collectively MEL / SKCM
  • Treatment: nivolumab n=59 (41.0%), pembrolizumab n=85 (59.0%)
  • Prior ipilimumab: 41.7% (n=60) pretreated; 58.3% (n=84) naive
  • Stage: 75.0% M1c (n=108); elevated LDH in 49.3%; ECOG 0 in 68.8%
  • Responses (RECIST v1.1): 45% PD, 14% SD, 3% MR, 26% PR, 12% CR; ORR 38%
  • Median follow-up: 29.9 months

Assays / panels (linked)

  • whole-exome-seq — pre-treatment tumor + matched normal; purity/ploidy via absolute
  • bulk-rna-seq — pre-treatment tumor (n=121); immune deconvolution by cibersortx (LM22)
  • mutational-signatures — COSMIC-based decomposition
  • gsea — Hallmark gene-set enrichment analysis for pathway-level response associations

Notable findings derived from this cohort

  • Median nonsynonymous TMB was 6.5 mutations/Mb (IQR 2.0–14.4); BRAF mutated in 39%, NRAS in 30%, NF1 in 17%. PMID:31792460
  • TMB was higher in responders vs progressors (MWW P=0.026), but the association was abolished after adjusting for melanoma subtype (cutaneous/occult vs acral/mucosal; P=0.24 in multivariate regression), arguing against TMB as a generic melanoma biomarker. PMID:31792460
  • TAP2 and MHC-I HLA-A/B/C focal amplifications (chr 6p21) were found exclusively in responders (combined n=8; Fisher’s P=0.001); JAK1 LOH was associated with resistance (OR=0.33, P=0.02). PMID:31792460
  • All 13 MHC-II HLA genes showed higher expression in responders (binomial P=0.0002); an MHC-II ssGSEA score was the strongest single predictor of response in ipilimumab-pretreated tumors and anchored a model (MHC-II + LDH + lymph-node metastasis) with AUC 0.90. PMID:31792460
  • For ipilimumab-naive tumors, tumor heterogeneity + ploidy + purity yielded a complementary model (AUC 0.77); each model performed poorly on the opposite subgroup (interaction P=0.004 and P=0.018). PMID:31792460
  • IFN-γ and IFN-α response pathways were enriched in responders overall and in the ipilimumab-treated subgroup, with validation in mel_iatlas_riaz_nivolumab_2017. PMID:31792460
