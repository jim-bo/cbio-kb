# NHERF1

## Overview

NHERF1 (Na+/H+ exchanger regulatory factor 1, also known as EBP50) is a scaffold protein involved in receptor signaling. It was newly nominated as a significantly mutated gene in papillary renal cell carcinoma (pRCC) in a multisubtype non-clear-cell RCC genomic study.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Newly nominated significantly mutated gene in pRCC in a multisubtype nccRCC WES study [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- pRCC: significantly mutated in discovery cohort; functional characterization not yet established [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-nominated with NF2, SLC5A3, PNKD, CPQ, LRP2, CHD3, SETD2, and CRTC1 as significantly mutated genes in pRCC [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No targeted therapies established; oncogenic versus passenger status unresolved [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)

## Open questions

- NHERF1 lacks functional characterization in pRCC — oncogenic vs passenger status is unestablished [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)
