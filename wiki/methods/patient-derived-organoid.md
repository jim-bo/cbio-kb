# Patient-derived organoid (PDO)

## Overview

Patient-derived organoids (PDOs) are three-dimensional ex vivo tumor cultures grown from patient biopsy or surgical specimens in a defined extracellular matrix. They retain the genetic and histologic features of the original tumor and are used to model drug sensitivity, pathway dependencies, and cancer biology in a patient-specific context.

## Used by

- [PMID:36611031](../papers/36611031.md) — CAC patient-derived organoids used to demonstrate functional Wnt independence; Wnt wild-type and APC-mutant CAC organoids grew without exogenous Wnt supplementation, in contrast to normal colonic organoids; pharmacologic inhibition with the porcupine inhibitor LGK-974 did not suppress CAC organoid growth, indicating transcriptional rewiring away from canonical Wnt signaling [PMID:36611031](../papers/36611031.md).

## Notes

- Wnt independence of APC-mutant PDOs was an unexpected finding counter to the canonical Wnt-driven sporadic CRC model.
