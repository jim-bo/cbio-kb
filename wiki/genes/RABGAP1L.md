# RABGAP1L

## Overview

RABGAP1L (RAB GTPase Activating Protein 1 Like) is a GTPase-activating protein that regulates RAB GTPase activity and vesicular trafficking. It resides on chromosome 1q25. In cancer genomics, it has been identified as a cis-cascade gene whose expression and protein abundance are amplified in concert with chromosome 1q gains observed in ependymoma.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Identified as a CNV-RNA/Protein/Phospho cis-cascade gene at chromosome 1q25 in ependymoma in the CPTAC pediatric brain tumor proteogenomic study (n=218 samples, 7 histologic types); chromosome 1q gain at RABGAP1L locus drives concordant increases at RNA, protein, and phosphosite levels [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[EPM](../cancer_types/EPM.md)**: Diagnosis-specific 1q gain cis-cascade; RABGAP1L is among 515 genes showing CNV-RNA/Protein/Phospho co-amplification specifically in ependymoma [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- 1q gain co-amplifies RABGAP1L (1q25) alongside RAB3GAP2 (1q41) in ependymoma; [FDPS](../genes/FDPS.md) (1q) shows a CNV-RNA/Protein (but not phospho) cis-cascade specifically in HGG [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic implications described in the corpus.

## Open questions

- The functional role of RABGAP1L amplification in ependymoma biology (e.g., effects on vesicular trafficking, signaling) is unexplored in this study [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md).
