# LATS2

## Overview

LATS2 (large tumor suppressor kinase 2) is a serine/threonine kinase and paralog of [LATS1](../genes/LATS1.md) that functions as a core effector of the Hippo tumor suppressor pathway. Together with LATS1, it phosphorylates and inactivates [YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md) and TAZ. Loss of LATS2 derepresses YAP1/TAZ transcriptional activity, which can upregulate [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md) and contribute to CDK4/6 inhibitor resistance in hormone receptor-positive breast cancer.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- LATS2 alterations found in 0.8% of 1,115 HR+/HER2- metastatic breast cancer patients profiled by MSK-IMPACT ([breast_ink4_msk_2021](../datasets/breast_ink4_msk_2021.md)); included in the 17% of patients with CDK6-axis gene alterations (LATS1/LATS2/YAP1 combined ~1.8%) associated with CDK4/6i resistance via Hippo-pathway derepression and CDK6 upregulation. [PMID:34544752](../papers/34544752.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **HR+/HER2- metastatic breast cancer ([BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md))**: LATS2 loss is part of the Hippo-pathway alteration cluster (combined with LATS1 and YAP1, 1.8% combined) implicated in CDK6 upregulation and intrinsic or rapid CDK4/6i resistance in the MSK-IMPACT cohort. [PMID:34544752](../papers/34544752.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- LATS2 alterations co-cluster with LATS1, YAP1, [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), CDK6, and [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) alterations in the broader CDK6-axis alteration group (17% of cohort) in HR+/HER2- metastatic breast cancer. [PMID:34544752](../papers/34544752.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- LATS2 loss is hypothesized to drive CDK6 upregulation via YAP1 activation, predicting resistance to ATP-competitive CDK4/6 inhibitors ([palbociclib](../drugs/palbociclib.md), [ribociclib](../drugs/ribociclib.md), [abemaciclib](../drugs/abemaciclib.md)); CDK4/6 PROTAC degraders (BSJ-05-017, BSJ-03-096) overcome this resistance in cell-line and xenograft models. [PMID:34544752](../papers/34544752.md)

## Open questions

- Individual contribution of LATS2 alterations (0.8%) to CDK6-mediated resistance has not been independently validated in patients; the clinical link between LATS2 alteration alone and CDK4/6i failure remains unproven. [PMID:34544752](../papers/34544752.md)
