# MAPK8

## Overview

MAPK8 (Mitogen-Activated Protein Kinase 8; also known as JNK1) is a stress-activated serine/threonine kinase in the JNK family. In the context of [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md) biology, MAPK8 has been implicated as part of the PEAR1-downstream phagocytic signaling axis, functioning alongside [SYK](../genes/SYK.md) and SRC-family kinases to mediate phagocytic receptor signaling in hematopoietic cells.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Part of the PEAR1-downstream phagocytic signaling axis (with SYK and SRC-family kinases) in AML; MAPK8 (JNK) was co-nominated with [PEAR1](../genes/PEAR1.md) in a prior TCGA-LAML immune prognostic gene signature (Dao et al., 2021); mechanistic background context in a study defining PEAR1 as a single-gene AML prognostic biomarker [PMID:35868306](../papers/35868306.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- AML ([AML](../cancer_types/AML.md)) — mechanistic context as part of PEAR1-associated phagocytic signaling; not directly mutated or altered in the primary study [PMID:35868306](../papers/35868306.md)

## Open questions

- Direct oncogenic or tumor-suppressive role of MAPK8 in AML has not been established; its presence in an immune prognostic signature warrants further investigation.
