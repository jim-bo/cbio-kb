# Low-Grade Endometrial Stromal Sarcoma (LGESS)

## Overview

Low-grade endometrial stromal sarcoma (LGESS) is an indolent uterine mesenchymal malignancy arising from endometrial stroma. In OncoTree it is a child of [Endometrial Stromal Sarcoma (ESS)](../cancer_types/ESS.md), which sits under [Uterine Sarcoma (USARC)](../cancer_types/USARC.md). LGESS is typically hormone receptor positive and characterized by recurrent chromosomal translocations, most commonly JAZF1-SUZ12 (t(7;17)). Its indolent course contrasts sharply with [HGESS](../cancer_types/HGESS.md) and with uterine leiomyosarcoma ([ULMS](../cancer_types/ULMS.md)). Long-term anti-estrogen therapy is the mainstay of treatment for hormone receptor-positive disease, but acquired [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) mutations can drive resistance.

## Cohorts in the corpus

- [usarc_msk_2020](../datasets/usarc_msk_2020.md) — prospective MSK-IMPACT sequencing of 107 uterine sarcoma patients at MSKCC; n=4 LGESS included (small subset, limiting LGESS-specific statistical conclusions). [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)

## Recurrent alterations

- JAZF1-SUZ12 fusion confirmed by MSK-Fusion RNA sequencing in one LGESS patient, demonstrating the value of orthogonal RNA-based fusion detection for diagnostic confirmation in this subtype [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)
- ESR1 Y537S ligand-binding domain mutation identified in a metastatic LGESS patient who had received >20 years of anti-estrogen therapy (long-term aromatase inhibitor); switching to [fulvestrant](../drugs/fulvestrant.md) produced tumor regression, demonstrating ESR1 acquired mutation as a mechanism of aromatase inhibitor resistance [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)

## Subtypes

- No further OncoTree subtypes defined below LGESS. Molecular subcategories under investigation include fusion-positive (JAZF1-SUZ12, PHF1-fusions) vs fusion-negative disease, but these are not formalized in the corpus.

## Therapeutic landscape

- [Fulvestrant](../drugs/fulvestrant.md) — produced tumor regression in an ESR1 Y537S-mutant LGESS patient on >20 years of prior aromatase inhibitor therapy; supports ESR1 mutation testing in patients progressing on long-term anti-estrogen therapy [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)
- Aromatase inhibitors — standard first-line hormonal therapy for hormone receptor-positive LGESS; ESR1 ligand-binding domain mutations (e.g., Y537S) confer resistance.
