**PMID:** 32332851 · **DOI:** 10.1038/s41467-020-15885-7 · **Journal:** Nature Communications (2020)

## TL;DR

Kim, Hu, and colleagues at Memorial Sloan Kettering report the first systematic collection of patient-derived xenograft (PDX) and patient-derived cell line (PDC) models for upper tract urothelial carcinoma ([UTUC](../cancer_types/UTUC.md)). Starting from 34 UTUC tumors collected at radical nephroureterectomy or metastasectomy, they established 17 PDX models (50% take rate) and 6 PDC models (25% take rate), all profiled by MSK-IMPACT and benchmarked against the matched patient tumors. The PDX models recapitulated the genomic landscape, mutational signatures, histology, and chemosensitivity of the source tumors. Using a HER2 S310F-mutant PDX/PDC pair (UCC14), the authors show that HER2-mutant UTUC is largely refractory to the kinase inhibitor [neratinib](../drugs/neratinib.md) but exquisitely sensitive to the antibody–drug conjugate [trastuzumab](../drugs/trastuzumab.md) deruxtecan (DS-8201a), providing a mechanistic rationale for prioritizing ADCs over HER kinase inhibitors in HER2-mutant urothelial cancer.

## Cohort & data

- **Genomic cohort:** 119 UTUC patients sequenced by [msk-impact-panel](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) (37 prospective + 82 retrospective from [Sfakianos 2015](../datasets/utuc_mskcc_2015.md)/Audenet et al.); 118 nephroureterectomies and 1 metastasectomy.
- **Transcriptomic cohort:** [rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) on 80 of the 119 UTUC tumors.
- **Model cohort:** 34 surgical UTUC specimens entered the PDX pipeline; 17 [patient-derived-xenograft](../methods/patient-derived-xenograft.md) models established (50%) and 6 PDC models (25%, 6/24). Three of three distant/lymph-node metastatic specimens (UCC03 abdominal wall, UCC11 liver, UCC32 lymph node) engrafted; the lone endoscopic biopsy (UCC27) did not.
- **Cancer types:** [UTUC](../cancer_types/UTUC.md), with comparative reference to [BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md).
- **Datasets:** Released through cBioPortal as [utuc_msk_2019](../datasets/utuc_msk_2019.md) (patient tumors) and [utuc_pdx_msk_2019](../datasets/utuc_pdx_msk_2019.md) (PDX/PDC). RNA-seq deposited at GEO GSE134292.
- **Assays:** MSK-IMPACT (300/341/410/468-gene versions), [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) on UCC03 and UCC30, [immunohistochemistry](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md) for MMR proteins, [masson-trichrome-staining](../methods/masson-trichrome-staining.md) and Picro Sirius red for ECM characterization.

## Key findings

- **Mutation landscape in 119 UTUC.** Recurrent actionable drivers: [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) 47–48%, [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md) 38%, [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) 26%, [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) 26%, [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) 25%, [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md) 22%, [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) 20%, [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md) 20%, [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) 15% (paper text 12%), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) 14–16%, [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md) 14%, [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) 8–9%, [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) 8%. 39.3% of all somatic mutations identified were variants of unknown functional significance.
- **Molecular subtypes (n=80 RNA-seq).** BASE47 classifier: 70/80 (87.5%) luminal, 10/80 (12.5%) basal. Consensus MIBC classifier: 66/80 (82.5%) luminal–papillary (including all 14 low-grade tumors), 7 LumU (8.75%), 1 LumNS (1.25%), 1 Stroma-rich (1.25%), 5 Ba/Sq (6.25%). No significant subtype difference between high- and low-grade tumors (P=0.5389, chi-square). Somatic [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) mutations occurred only in the luminal subtype.
- **PDX take rate and predictors.** 17/34 PDX (50%) and 6/24 PDC (25%) successfully established. Smoking status differed between engrafters and non-engrafters (P=0.039); muscle-invasive (≥pT2) stage trended toward higher take rate (P=0.166). Three of three distant/nodal metastases engrafted. Transcriptomically, the basal/squamous consensus signature correlated with engraftment (P=0.04); cell-cycle / DNA-replication pathways (SMC1, MCM5, MCM7) were enriched in engrafters and ECM-receptor / focal-adhesion pathways ([FN1](../genes/FN1.md), THBS2, COMP) in non-engrafters.
- **Genomic concordance tumor↔PDX.** 72.7% concordance of known/likely oncogenic mutations across 17 tumor/PDX pairs. PDX-specific deep deletions of [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)/[CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md) appeared in 29% of PDX (selection during engraftment). Only the hypermutated UCC34 PDX dropped below 50% concordance. WES on UCC03 and UCC30 confirmed linear and branched evolution; the AID/APOBEC signature (COSMIC Signatures 2 + 13) was preserved in UCC03 across passages but Signature 2 was lost in the UCC30 PDX.
- **MSI / Lynch enrichment.** 4/17 engrafted tumors were MSI-H (MSIsensor ≥10), including three Lynch cases with germline [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md) (UCC15), [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md) (UCC36, UCC34) and one ([MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md)/[MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md) IHC-negative) without identifiable germline cause (UCC17). All four MSI-H tumors carried TMB 20.3–157.2 mut/Mb; MMR/MSI and aging signatures were stable across passages. The [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) R248C hotspot, previously associated with Lynch-related UTUC, was present in 3/4 MSI-H pairs (UCC17, UCC34, UCC36).
- **Chemosensitivity matches patient.** PDX responses mirrored the source patients in all three tested cases. UCC03 PDX (patient: partial response to gemcitabine/carboplatin) was sensitive to Gem/Carbo (P=0.0177) and [gemcitabine](../drugs/gemcitabine.md) alone (P=0.0065) but not [carboplatin](../drugs/carboplatin.md) alone (P=0.138). UCC19 PDX (patient: durable response, off therapy 15 months) was highly sensitive to Gem/Carbo (P<0.0001) and carried a truncating [RECQL4](../genes/RECQL4.md) F987* mutation. UCC17 (MSI-H, patient: pT3 stable disease after neoadjuvant Gem/Cis, no pathologic response) showed minimal PDX growth inhibition by Gem/Cis (P=0.07); UCC36 (MSI-H Lynch) was similarly resistant.
- **MSI vs neoadjuvant chemotherapy in patients.** In a separate UTUC cohort of 20 patients receiving NAC before RNU (2 MSI-H, 1 MSI-indeterminate, 17 MSS), pathologic response (≤pT1N0) occurred in 10/17 MSS (59%) and in 0/2 MSI-H patients.
- **HER2 epidemiology across 44,183 prospectively sequenced tumors.** Oncogenic/likely-oncogenic [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) mutations: 10.3% of bladder UC (n=1032) and 5.9% of UTUC (n=204), vs 3.2% breast, 2.9% endometrial, 2.6% colorectal, 2.4% [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md). [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) amplification: 6.2% bladder (64/1032) and 4.4% UTUC (9/204).
- **HER2 S310F PDX pharmacology.** UCC14-PDC (HER2 S310F UTUC) IC50 for [neratinib](../drugs/neratinib.md) = 508.3 nM, vs 245.9 nM for ERBB2-WT MGHU3, 56.8 nM for the HER2 S310F cervical line CVX-4, and 0.1 nM for HER2-amplified BT-474. Neratinib suppressed p-ERK but not p-AKT in UCC14-PDC, indicating HER2-independent AKT signaling. In vivo, UCC14-PDX was largely refractory to neratinib (P=0.0034) but strongly inhibited by the HER2 ADC DS-8201a / [trastuzumab-deruxtecan](../drugs/trastuzumab-deruxtecan.md) (P<0.0001), with the ADC significantly outperforming neratinib (P<0.0001).

## Genes & alterations

- [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) — activating hotspots (incl. R248C, S249C) in 47–48% of UTUC and only in luminal-subtype tumors; favorable prognostic marker per prior literature. R248C enriched in Lynch-syndrome UTUC and seen in 3/4 MSI-H PDX pairs.
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) — oncogenic mutations in 8–9% of UTUC and 10.3% of bladder UC; HER2 S310F (UCC14) used to model HER2-mutant UC pharmacology. ADC sensitivity but kinase-inhibitor resistance demonstrated.
- [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — recurrent RTK/RAS/PI3K-pathway drivers consistent with prior UTUC genomics ([Sfakianos 2015](../datasets/utuc_mskcc_2015.md)).
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)/[CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md) — tumor-suppressor losses; CDKN2A/2B showed PDX-specific deep deletions in 29% of models (engraftment selection).
- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter mutations — 53% of PDX models; consistent with UC biology.
- [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md), [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md) — chromatin/cohesin alterations frequent in UTUC; [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md) mutation positively (and ERBB2 negatively) associated with engraftment, though not statistically significant.
- [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md), [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md) — Lynch-syndrome germline/somatic MMR loss; defined the MSI-H UTUC subset (UCC15, UCC17, UCC34, UCC36) with TMB 20.3–157.2 mut/Mb.
- [RECQL4](../genes/RECQL4.md) — truncating F987* in UCC19 patient and PDX; offered as candidate explanation for the durable platinum/gemcitabine response (DNA-repair / homologous recombination role).
- [ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md) — included in the recurrent-mutation panel (4%) and cited as a platinum-response biomarker per prior advanced-UC literature.
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) — [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) cited as a basal-marker gene in the BASE47-defined basal/squamous cluster; [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) low-frequency (2%).

## Clinical implications

- **HER2-mutant urothelial carcinoma.** Antibody–drug conjugates (DS-8201a / [trastuzumab-deruxtecan](../drugs/trastuzumab-deruxtecan.md), and by extension [ado-trastuzumab-emtansine](../drugs/ado-trastuzumab-emtansine.md)) should be prioritized over HER kinase inhibitors such as [neratinib](../drugs/neratinib.md) for HER2-mutant UC. Authors propose this rationalizes the modest activity of HER kinase inhibitors in basket trials.
- **MSI-H UTUC and neoadjuvant chemotherapy.** MSI-H Lynch-associated UTUC may be relatively chemoresistant (0/2 pathologic responses in MSI-H NAC patients vs 10/17 in MSS; both MSI-H PDX models resistant to gemcitabine/cisplatin). Authors suggest MSI status be used to triage MSI-H UTUC patients toward immune checkpoint blockade rather than cytotoxic NAC.
- **[FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) and [erdafitinib](../drugs/erdafitinib.md).** Although [erdafitinib](../drugs/erdafitinib.md) is FDA-approved for FGFR3-altered metastatic bladder cancer, the authors note that targeted therapy impact in UC has been limited to date and frame UTUC PDX as a platform for testing precision strategies.
- **Predictive biomarker hypothesis: DDR alterations and platinum response.** UCC19's exquisite Gem/Carbo response and truncating [RECQL4](../genes/RECQL4.md) mutation are offered (with appropriate caution) as a single-case example consistent with DDR-gene-alteration / platinum-sensitivity associations reported in advanced UC.
- **Resource value.** All genomic data released to cBioPortal as [utuc_msk_2019](../datasets/utuc_msk_2019.md) and [utuc_pdx_msk_2019](../datasets/utuc_pdx_msk_2019.md); models offered to the community as a shared preclinical resource.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Author-acknowledged.** Biological characterization of the variants of unknown significance identified in the PDX collection exceeds the capacity of any one lab; the authors explicitly invite community use.
- **Author-acknowledged.** Models were derived predominantly from large surgical specimens; PDX generation from ureteroscopic biopsies (the pre-RNU diagnostic setting most relevant for guiding neoadjuvant therapy) remains a goal.
- **Statistical power.** Engraftment-predictor analyses (e.g., STAG2/ERBB2 positive/negative engraftment association, basal/squamous correlation) operate on N=17 PDX, so even strong signals are underpowered; STAG2/ERBB2 results were not statistically significant.
- **Cross-paper synthesis.** The MSI-H chemoresistance signal (n=2 MSI-H NAC patients, 2 MSI-H PDX) is hypothesis-generating; whether MSI status alone is sufficient to redirect UTUC patients from cytotoxic NAC to immunotherapy will need confirmation in larger neoadjuvant cohorts.
- **Genomic drift in PDX.** Recurrent CDKN2A/2B deletion specific to PDX (29%) and loss of the AID/APOBEC Signature 2 in the UCC30 PDX flag selection pressure during engraftment that downstream users should account for when interpreting PDX-only findings.
- **HER2 ADC generalizability.** The PDX-vs-ADC efficacy claim rests on a single HER2 S310F model (UCC14) and a single ADC (DS-8201a); generalization across HER2 mutation classes (e.g., kinase-domain mutations, amplifications) is not addressed.
