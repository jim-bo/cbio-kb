# Hypodiploid ALL (St. Jude, Nature Genetics 2013)

## Overview

A St. Jude Children's Research Hospital cohort of 124 pediatric hypodiploid acute lymphoblastic leukemia (ALL) cases profiled by whole-genome sequencing, whole-exome sequencing, SNP microarray copy number profiling, and gene expression arrays. The study defined two genetically and clinically distinct subtypes: near haploid ALL (24–31 chromosomes) and low hypodiploid ALL (32–39 chromosomes). dbGaP accession: phs000341.v1.p1.

## Composition

- 124 pediatric hypodiploid ALL cases: 50 near haploid, 26 low hypodiploid, 18 masked hypodiploid, 22 near diploid, plus 8 masked low hypodiploid.
- Additional adult ALL cohort of 117 cases including 11 low hypodiploid cases.
- Matched remission DNA available for 89 cases.
- Cancer type: [BLL](../cancer_types/BLL.md).

## Assays / panels (linked)

- [Whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) — 20 cases (14 near haploid, 6 low hypodiploid).

- [Sanger sequencing](../methods/sanger-sequencing.md) — validation in 8 genes (all coding exons in 11 genes) across the full cohort.

## Notable findings derived from this cohort

- Near haploid ALL harbors RTK/Ras signaling alterations in 70.6% of cases, including [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) (44.1%), [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md), [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md), and [MAPK1](../genes/MAPK1.md) [PMID:23334668](../papers/23334668.md).
- Low hypodiploid ALL has [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) alterations in 91.2% of pediatric cases; 43.3% of TP53 mutations were inherited, linking this subtype to Li-Fraumeni syndrome [PMID:23334668](../papers/23334668.md).
- [IKZF2](../genes/IKZF2.md) deletions in 52.9% of low hypodiploid ALL; [IKZF3](../genes/IKZF3.md) alterations in 13.2% of near haploid ALL [PMID:23334668](../papers/23334668.md).
- Both subtypes show PI3K pathway dependence and ex vivo sensitivity to GDC-0941 and BEZ235 [PMID:23334668](../papers/23334668.md).
