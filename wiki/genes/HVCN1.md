# HVCN1

## Overview

HVCN1 encodes the voltage-gated proton channel Hv1, which regulates intracellular pH and reactive oxygen species (ROS) levels in B cells. HVCN1 has been identified as a recurrently mutated gene in DLBCL, particularly within the BCR/PI3K signaling axis in the C3/C4 GCB subtypes defined by Chapuy et al.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- HVCN1 identified as a BCR/PI3K signaling modifier recurrently mutated in DLBCL C3/C4 GCB subtypes in a 304-patient WES cohort; co-listed with [GNA13](../genes/GNA13.md), [SGK1](../genes/SGK1.md), and [TNFSF14](../genes/TNFSF14.md) as signaling modifiers in these clusters. [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [DLBCLNOS](../cancer_types/DLBCLNOS.md): HVCN1 mutations observed in the C3/C4 GCB-DLBCL subtypes in the Chapuy et al. genetic classification framework. [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with [GNA13](../genes/GNA13.md), SGK1, and TNFSF14 as BCR/PI3K pathway modifiers in DLBCL C3/C4 subtypes. [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting of HVCN1 described in current corpus. BCR/PI3K pathway addiction in C3/C4 raises hypotheses around PI3K inhibitors.

## Open questions

- Precise functional impact of HVCN1 mutations on B-cell receptor signaling in DLBCL requires experimental validation. [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)
