**PMID:** [22941188](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22941188) · **DOI:** 10.1038/ng.2396 · **Journal:** Nature Genetics (2012)

## TL;DR

This study performed integrative genomic analysis of small-cell lung cancer ([SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md)) by sequencing 29 exomes, 2 whole genomes, and 15 transcriptomes, complemented by SNP array analysis of 63 tumors. The authors found universal inactivation of [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), identified recurrent mutations in histone-modifying genes ([CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md), [EP300](../genes/EP300.md), [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md)), and discovered potentially targetable alterations including [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) amplifications and [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) mutations. Cross-species comparison with mouse SCLC models validated key findings.

## Cohort & data

- 99 surgically resected SCLC specimens from the Clinical Lung Cancer Genome Project (CLCGP) consortium
- Exome sequencing: 27 tumors + 2 cell lines (n=29)
- Whole-genome sequencing: 2 tumors
- Transcriptome sequencing: 15 tumors
- SNP array (Affymetrix 6.0): 63 tumors
- Mouse model validation: 20 SCLC tumors from p53/Rb1 conditional knockout mice
- Validation cohort: 26 additional tumors + 34-45 cell lines (gene-specific)
- Cancer type: [SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md)
- Dataset: [sclc_clcgp](../datasets/sclc_clcgp.md)

## Key findings

- SCLC exhibits an extremely high mutation rate of 7.4 +/- 1 protein-changing mutations per million base pairs, consistent with tobacco carcinogen exposure (elevated C:G>A:T transversions).
- Universal biallelic inactivation of [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) in all 29 sequenced cases, with CNLOH enrichment confirming these as early clonal events.
- [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md)/[EP300](../genes/EP300.md) mutations clustered in the HAT domain at an overall frequency of 18% (point mutations, indels, gene rearrangements); mutations were mutually exclusive, suggesting epistasis.
- Functional validation showed CREBBP HAT domain mutations (Gly1411Arg, Asp1435Tyr, Ser1432Pro) significantly reduced H3K18 acetylation in Crebbp/Ep300 double-knockout MEFs (P<0.05 to P<0.0001, one-way ANOVA with Bonferroni correction).
- [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) (MLL) recurrently mutated at Ile960 (Ile960Met).
- [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) focal amplification in 6% of cases (copy number >= 3.5), validated by FISH in an independent set of 51 tumors (3/51, 6%).
- [MYCL](../genes/MYCL.md) amplification in 5/63 (8%), [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md) in 4/63 (6%), [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) in 1/63; all mutually exclusive (total 16%).
- [SLIT2](../genes/SLIT2.md) mutated in 5/29 exome cases with clustered mutations (2 nonsense, 1 frameshift, 2 missense); overall 10% in extended cohort (n=89).
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) mutations in 3/29 cases with proven/likely loss of phosphatase activity.
- No significant difference in genomic architecture between surgically resected (early-stage) and autopsy (late-stage) specimens.

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — inactivating mutations (universal, 100%); biallelic via mutation + LOH/CNLOH; early clonal event
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — inactivating mutations (universal, 100%); biallelic via mutation + deletion/LOH; early clonal event
- [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md) — inactivating point mutations, indels, and gene fusions in HAT domain (18% overall); haploinsufficient tumor suppressor
- [EP300](../genes/EP300.md) — inactivating mutations in HAT domain; mutually exclusive with CREBBP
- [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) — recurrent missense mutation (Ile960Met); histone methyltransferase
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — inactivating missense mutations (3/29, 10%); loss of phosphatase activity activating PI3K pathway
- [SLIT2](../genes/SLIT2.md) — truncating and missense mutations (10%); candidate novel tumor suppressor
- [EPHA7](../genes/EPHA7.md) — mutations identified; candidate tumor suppressor (previously described in lymphoma)
- [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) — focal amplification (6%); potential therapeutic target
- [MYCL](../genes/MYCL.md) — focal amplification (8%); mutually exclusive with other [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) family members
- [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md) — focal amplification (6%); mutually exclusive with [MYCL](../genes/MYCL.md) and MYC
- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) — focal amplification (rare, 1 case)
- [SOX2](../genes/SOX2.md) — broad 3q gains (less focal than in squamous-cell lung cancer)
- [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md) — focal amplification at 19q12
- [NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) — amplification in mouse SCLC; copy number gain in 3 human samples
- [COBL](../genes/COBL.md) — identified as candidate driver gene
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — no mutations observed (notable negative finding)

## Clinical implications

- FGFR1-amplified SCLC (6%) may benefit from FGFR inhibitors, which were already in clinical testing for squamous-cell lung cancer at the time of publication.
- PTEN-mutant SCLC (10%) harbors PI3K pathway activation, representing a potential therapeutic vulnerability.
- Universal TP53/RB1 inactivation confirms these are obligate events in SCLC pathogenesis and cannot serve as therapeutic targets themselves.
- Loss of RB1 may be mechanistically involved in EGFR-mutant lung adenocarcinoma trans-differentiation to SCLC under therapeutic pressure (one case with shared TP53 mutation but SCLC-restricted RB1 mutation).
- Histone modification (CREBBP/EP300/KMT2A) as a major altered pathway class in SCLC opens potential avenues for epigenetic therapeutic strategies.

## Limitations & open questions

- Limited sample size (29 exomes) for a tumor with extremely high mutational background; statistical power to detect low-frequency drivers is constrained.
- Specimens were surgically resected, representing a minority of SCLC cases (most are treated with chemoradiation without surgery), though the authors show genomic similarity to autopsy specimens.
- No matched normal for copy number in the mouse analysis (array-CGH based).
- Functional validation was limited to CREBBP HAT domain mutations; other candidate drivers ([SLIT2](../genes/SLIT2.md), [EPHA7](../genes/EPHA7.md), [COBL](../genes/COBL.md)) lack functional characterization in this study.
- The study predates immunotherapy and does not assess TMB as a potential biomarker for checkpoint inhibitor response despite the high mutation rate.
- Whether FGFR inhibitors have clinical activity in FGFR1-amplified SCLC remains unproven.
