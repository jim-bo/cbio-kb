**PMID:** 31693904 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.celrep.2019.09.071 · **Journal:** Cell Reports (2019)

## TL;DR

Rokita and colleagues from the Pediatric Preclinical Testing Consortium (PPTC) report a comprehensive genomic characterization of **261 patient-derived xenograft (PDX) models spanning 37 unique pediatric cancer molecular subtypes**. They performed whole-exome sequencing (n=240), RNA-seq (n=244), SNP microarrays (n=252), and STR profiling (n=261), and show that PDX models faithfully recapitulate the mutational, copy-number, and fusion landscapes of their histologies of origin — including relapsed disease. They also derive RNA-expression classifiers for [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) pathway inactivation as candidate response biomarkers. Data are deposited in [pptc_2019](../datasets/pptc_2019.md) and queryable via PedcBioPortal as a public resource for pediatric oncology drug development.

## Cohort & data

- **261 PDX models** from **37 unique pediatric cancer molecular subtypes** (29 unique histologies), drawn from the [Pediatric Preclinical Testing Consortium (PPTC)](../datasets/pptc_2019.md) — successor to the Pediatric Preclinical Testing Program (PPTP). 26 full models and 3 RNA samples were excluded during QC for contamination or misidentification.
- Major histology groups (cancer types linked where canonical OncoTree codes exist):
  - **B-cell precursor [BLL](../cancer_types/BLL.md)** (BCP-ALL), Ph-like / Ph+ [BLL](../cancer_types/BLL.md), MLL-rearranged BLL.
  - **T-cell / ETP [TLL](../cancer_types/TLL.md)** (T-ALL and ETP-ALL).
  - **[NBL](../cancer_types/NBL.md)** (neuroblastoma).
  - **[OS](../cancer_types/OS.md)** (osteosarcoma).
  - **[ES](../cancer_types/ES.md)** (Ewing sarcoma).
  - **[WT](../cancer_types/WT.md)** (Wilms tumor, n=13).
  - **[RMS](../cancer_types/RMS.md)** — [ERMS](../cancer_types/ERMS.md) (Fusion−) and [ARMS](../cancer_types/ARMS.md) (Fusion+, PAX3-FOXO1).
  - **[MBL](../cancer_types/MBL.md)** (medulloblastoma, n=20) spanning [SHH](../genes/SHH.md), WNT, group 3, group 4.
  - **[ATRT](../cancer_types/ATRT.md)** and extracranial **[MRT](../cancer_types/MRT.md)** (rhabdoid).
  - **[EPMT](../cancer_types/EPMT.md)** (ependymomas: ST-EPN-RELA, ST-EPN-YAP, PF-EPN-A/B).
  - **[HGGNOS](../cancer_types/HGGNOS.md)** / glioblastoma / [PXA](../cancer_types/PXA.md) / MYCN-subtype HGG.
  - **[DIFG](../cancer_types/DIFG.md)** / **[DMG](../cancer_types/DMG.md)** including histone H3-wild-type DIPG (n=2).
  - **[ASPS](../cancer_types/ASPS.md)** (alveolar soft part sarcoma) and rare histologies (n=7).
  - **[PPB](../cancer_types/PPB.md)** (pleuropulmonary blastoma).
  - **[ALAL](../cancer_types/ALAL.md)** (ambiguous/mixed-lineage leukemia).
- **Assays / methods**: [patient-derived xenograft](../methods/patient-derived-xenograft.md) generation (subcutaneous flank for solid; intracranial for CNS; intravenous in NOD/SCID or NSG for leukemias); [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) on the HGSC VCRome 2.1 capture (240 models); [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) (strand-specific poly-A+, 244 models); Illumina HumanOmniExpress-24 [SNP arrays](../methods/affymetrix-snp6.md) for segmentation and ethnicity inference (252 models); [STR profiling](../methods/str-profiling.md) for identity verification (all 261 models).
- **Fusion calling** combined four algorithms: [deFuse](../methods/defuse.md), [FusionCatcher](../methods/fusioncatcher.md), [STAR-Fusion](../methods/star-fusion.md), and [SOAPfuse](../methods/soapfuse.md), yielding 925 unique high-confidence fusions and 92 known oncogenic driver fusions.
- **Bioinformatic methods**: hybrid hg19-mm10 competitive mapping for mouse-read subtraction; [MutSigCV](../methods/mutsigcv.md) for SMG filtering; [GSEA](../methods/gsea.md) on Hallmark and TissGDB/TiGER gene sets; [UCSC TumorMap](../methods/tumormap.md) for expression-cluster visualization; previously trained machine-learning classifiers for [TP53 inactivation](../methods/tp53-inactivation-classifier.md) and [NF1 inactivation](../methods/nf1-inactivation-classifier.md) (Knijnenburg et al. 2018; Way et al. 2017, 2018).
- Common germline filtering used a TCGA panel of 809 normal samples; rare germline variants (<0.005 MAF in ExAC / 1000 Genomes / NHLBI ESP) were retained in the "somatic" MAF, so reported TMBs are likely inflated relative to matched-normal cohorts.
- Predicted ancestry inferred from SNP array genotypes: 71% European, 11.5% South Asian/Hispanic, 9.1% African, 5.5% mixed/unknown, 2.4% East Asian.

## Key findings

- **PDX models faithfully recapitulate driver landscapes.**
  - **BCP-ALL ([BLL](../cancer_types/BLL.md))**: 45–48% have canonical homozygous focal deletions of [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) / [CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md) on 9p; RAS-pathway enrichment ([KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) 30%, [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) 18%); JAK-STAT enrichment ([JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md)/[JAK3](../genes/JAK3.md) altered in 15%); [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) altered in 15%; fusion transcripts in 78% (25/33), commonly involving [ETV6](../genes/ETV6.md) (27%, 88% of those partnered with [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md)), [PAX5](../genes/PAX5.md) (18%), and [CRLF2](../genes/CRLF2.md) (6%).
  - **ETP-ALL / T-ALL ([TLL](../cancer_types/TLL.md))**: CDKN2A/B focal deletions in 72–76%; [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) mutations in 68%; [JAK1](../genes/JAK1.md) or [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md) lesions in 24%; [STAT5B](../genes/STAT5B.md) lesions in 4%; oncogenic fusions in ~48% (commonly TRBC2, TRBC1, [ABL1](../genes/ABL1.md), [IGH](../genes/IGH.md), LMAN2, LMO1, LMO2, [ETV6](../genes/ETV6.md)).
  - **Ph-like / Ph+ ALL**: [BCR](../genes/BCR.md)-[ABL1](../genes/ABL1.md) fusion confirmed in all three Ph+ models (ALL-04, ALL-55, ALL-56). 8/19 Ph-like models (42%) carry a canonical [CRLF2](../genes/CRLF2.md) fusion (7 partnered with [P2RY8](../genes/P2RY8.md), 1 with IGHM); [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md) rearrangements in 55% (12/22); [PAX5](../genes/PAX5.md) rearrangements in 23% (5/22); CDKN2A/B deletions in 45% (10/22).
  - **MLL-ALL**: all models carry a canonical [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) fusion and have relatively silent genomes; majority derived from patients <1 year of age.
  - **Neuroblastoma ([NBL](../cancer_types/NBL.md))**: [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md) amplification in 66% (most frequent alteration across the cohort), largely mutually exclusive with 11q deletion; 1p deletion in 77%; 17q gain in 97%; [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) hotspot mutations in 37%; additional alterations in [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (11%), [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) (9%), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) (9%), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) (3%), [CIC](../genes/CIC.md) (3%), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) (3%).
  - **Osteosarcoma ([OS](../cancer_types/OS.md))**: [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) altered in 82% of models; classifier-predicted [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) pathway inactivation in 32/33 RNA-seq models (all except OS-55-SBX); global copy-number complexity consistent with chromothripsis (97% of [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) models with high breakpoint density, 33/34).
  - **Ewing sarcoma ([ES](../cancer_types/ES.md))**: canonical [EWSR1](../genes/EWSR1.md)-[FLI1](../genes/FLI1.md) fusion in all RNA-seq-profiled models; CHLA-258 additionally harbored an FLI1-RP11-9L18.2 fusion; [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations in 7/10 (70%); homozygous CDKN2A/B loss (60%) mutually exclusive with [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md) mutations (20%).
  - **Wilms tumor ([WT](../cancer_types/WT.md), n=13)**: [WT1](../genes/WT1.md) hemizygous deletion in 61%; 11p15.5 LOH (imprinting region) in 69% (9/13); [AMER1](../genes/AMER1.md) (WTX) hemizygous deletions in 15% (2 models); [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) mutations in 15% (KT-6 and NCH-WT-6-S13-1506), mutually exclusive with WTX alterations; KT-9 (bilateral disease) harbored a [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)-[FXR2](../genes/FXR2.md) fusion plus partial homozygous TP53 deletion.
  - **Rhabdomyosarcoma ([RMS](../cancer_types/RMS.md))**: all Fusion+ models ([ARMS](../cancer_types/ARMS.md)) carried [PAX3](../genes/PAX3.md)-[FOXO1](../genes/FOXO1.md); median patient age was 16y for Fusion+ vs 5y for Fusion− ([ERMS](../cancer_types/ERMS.md)); RAS-pathway mutations ([NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md)) observed in 3/6 [ERMS](../cancer_types/ERMS.md) models. [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md) and [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) focal amplifications also observed; [MYOD1](../genes/MYOD1.md) overexpressed in all but IRS-68.
  - **Rhabdoid ([ATRT](../cancer_types/ATRT.md) / [MRT](../cancer_types/MRT.md))**: all carry inactivating alterations of [SMARCB1](../genes/SMARCB1.md) and/or [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md); hedgehog, TNFα, and p53 signaling enriched.
- **Brain-tumor sub-classification.** 20 medulloblastoma models span SHH (n=7), WNT (n=2), group 3 (n=7), group 4 (n=3); [ATRT](../cancer_types/ATRT.md) models include SHH (n=3) and [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) (n=3) subgroups; ETMR models (ICb-S1129MB, ICb-1343ENB, IBs-2373PNET) carry C19MC amplification, [LIN28A](../genes/LIN28A.md) overexpression, and/or TTYH1 fusions; CNS Ewing-like models (ICb-9850PNET and IC-22909PNET-rIII) carry [CIC](../genes/CIC.md)-[DUX4](../genes/DUX4.md) fusions; NCH-MN-1 (originally a rhabdoid meningioma suspected of being ATRT) was reclassified as high-grade glioma based on [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E plus [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)/[CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md) deletion. IC-1621GBM (Lynch / DNA mismatch-repair deficiency syndrome) carried 124 somatic mutations/Mb plus mutations in [PMS1](../genes/PMS1.md) (Q316*), [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md) (G721*), [MSH5](../genes/MSH5.md), and [POLE](../genes/POLE.md).
- **Relapse mutational landscape.** Median TMB across all PDX models = 2.66 somatic Mut/Mb. PDXs derived from relapse tissue had significantly higher TMB than diagnosis-derived (3.08 vs 2.57 Mut/Mb, Wilcoxon p = 2.2e−5); the effect was significant in [NBL](../cancer_types/NBL.md) (p = 0.016) and T-ALL (p = 0.0081), trended in BCP-ALL (p = 0.054), and was absent in [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) (p = 0.42). In paired diagnosis-relapse PDXs, the relapse PDX had higher TMB (median 98.0 vs 27.5 mutations; Wilcoxon p = 0.0083).
- **Chromothripsis / structural complexity.** 25% (64/252) of models had high breakpoint density (HBD) on one or more chromosomes. 97% (33/34) of osteosarcomas had HBD; 30% (10/33) on ≤4 chromosomes (localized chromothripsis) and 70% on ≥5 chromosomes (global rearrangement). Neuroblastoma HBD concentrated on chromosomes 2, 5, 16, 17, 19 — consistent with prior reports linking chromosome 2/5/17 chromothripsis to [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md) amplification, [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) rearrangement, and 17q gain respectively.
- **Expression classifiers for TP53 / [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) pathway inactivation.** Pre-trained TCGA PanCanAtlas classifiers (Knijnenburg 2018; Way 2017/2018) achieved AUROC = 0.89 for [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and AUROC = 0.77 for [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), but only 0.55 for the RAS classifier (suggesting pediatric-specific RAS biology). TP53 classifier scores were significantly higher in TP53-altered (mean 0.790) vs unaltered (mean 0.419) models (Wilcoxon p < 2.2e−16). Models with alterations in [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) and [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) also scored high — phenocopying TP53 loss. Across non-OS models, fusions in TP53 pathway genes produced the highest classifier scores, followed by SNVs and CNVs (Kruskal-Wallis p = 9.8e−11). Copy-number burden (but not TMB) correlated with TP53 classifier score (R = 0.51, p = 1.8e−17), supporting a causal link between TP53 inactivation and chromosomal instability.
- **Fusion catalog.** 50,796 unique fusions called across the cohort were filtered to 925 high-confidence and 92 known oncogenic driver fusions. Five diagnosis-relapse pairs revealed four fusions present at diagnosis but lost at relapse (PAX5-RP11-465M18.1, IGH-[MYC](../genes/MYC.md), [CIC](../genes/CIC.md)-[DUX4](../genes/DUX4.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)-[TNR](../genes/TNR.md)), suggesting these were acquired after an alternative initiating event that was retained.

## Genes & alterations

- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)** — altered in 82% of osteosarcomas, 70% of Ewing sarcomas, 11% of neuroblastomas; loss/inactivation is a recurrent osteosarcoma hallmark with TP53 intron-1 fusion breakpoints, including TP53-[FXR2](../genes/FXR2.md) (Wilms KT-9) and TP53-[TNR](../genes/TNR.md) (diagnostic-only). Inactivation classifier (AUROC 0.89) distinguishes "TP53-pathway-inactive" tumors even among TP53-wild-type models.
- **[NF1](../genes/NF1.md)** — altered in 9% of neuroblastoma models (nonsense/frameshift); inactivation classifier (AUROC 0.77) flags additional pathway-inactive models.
- **[MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md)** — focal amplification in 66% of neuroblastoma models (the cohort's most frequent alteration); also amplified in MYCN-subtype HGG (IC-2664PNET) and in [ARMS](../cancer_types/ARMS.md).
- **[ALK](../genes/ALK.md)** — hotspot mutations in 37% of neuroblastomas; also flagged as a high-expression immunotherapy target.
- **[CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) / [CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md)** — homozygous 9p deletions in 45–48% of BCP-ALL, 72–76% of T-ALL/ETP-ALL, 45% of Ph/Ph-like ALL, 60% of Ewing sarcoma; concurrent with [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E in NCH-MN-1.
- **[SMARCB1](../genes/SMARCB1.md) / [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md)** — inactivating focal deletions, frameshifts, or nonsense mutations in all [ATRT](../cancer_types/ATRT.md) and extracranial [MRT](../cancer_types/MRT.md) models; also three astrocytic tumors carried [SMARCB1](../genes/SMARCB1.md) hemizygous deletions (consistent with 6.7–7.5% reported in pediatric HGG cohorts).
- **[EWSR1](../genes/EWSR1.md)-[FLI1](../genes/FLI1.md)** — canonical fusion in all RNA-seq-profiled Ewing sarcoma models.
- **[PAX3](../genes/PAX3.md)-[FOXO1](../genes/FOXO1.md)** — fusion in all Fusion+ ([ARMS](../cancer_types/ARMS.md)) models.
- **[ASPSCR1](../genes/ASPSCR1.md)-TFE3** — canonical fusion detected in both [ASPS](../cancer_types/ASPS.md) models.
- **[BCR](../genes/BCR.md)-[ABL1](../genes/ABL1.md)** — fusion in all three Ph+ ALL models.
- **[KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md)** — fusions in all MLL-ALL models.
- **[CIC](../genes/CIC.md)-[DUX4](../genes/DUX4.md)** — fusion in CNS Ewing-like model (ICb-9850PNET).
- **[CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md)** — mutations in 15% of Wilms models (mutually exclusive with [AMER1](../genes/AMER1.md)/WTX); also p.D32G in NCH-HEP1 hepatic model.
- **[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md)** — RAS pathway mutations enriched in BCP-ALL and in Fusion− [RMS](../cancer_types/RMS.md) (e.g. Rh-12 [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) T2335fs; NCH-ERMS-1 [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) Q61K; Rh-36 [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) Q61K).
- **[NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md)** — mutated in 68% of T-ALL/ETP-ALL.
- **[WT1](../genes/WT1.md)** — hemizygous deletions in 61% of Wilms models (often with 11p13 LOH).
- **[H3-3A](../genes/H3-3A.md) / [H3-3B](../genes/H3-3B.md)** — both DIPG models (IBs-P1215DIPG, IBs-W0128DIPG) express histone H3.3 at high levels (FPKM > 50) but lack canonical H3.1/H3.3 mutations; IBs-W0128DIPG carries predicted-damaging RNA-derived missense variants in [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) (G13R), [CIC](../genes/CIC.md) (C102Y), and [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) (C988F).
- **[GPC2](../genes/GPC2.md), [ALK](../genes/ALK.md)** — both dramatically overexpressed in neuroblastoma models and in additional pediatric histotypes, flagged as antibody-drug-conjugate and T-cell-engager targets for basket-trial design.
- **[CD274](../genes/CD274.md) (PD-L1)** — intrinsic overexpression in [ASPS](../cancer_types/ASPS.md) xenografts, flagged as a checkpoint-inhibitor target.
- **[AKR1C3](../genes/AKR1C3.md)** — RNA-seq identified [TLL](../cancer_types/TLL.md) (T-ALL) as a target histology for an AKR1C3-activated prodrug.
- **[MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md)** — alterations phenocopy TP53 inactivation per expression classifier.
- **[MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [MSH5](../genes/MSH5.md), [PMS1](../genes/PMS1.md), [POLE](../genes/POLE.md)** — co-occurring mutations in IC-1621GBM (Lynch / constitutional MMR-deficiency); ultrahypermutated at 124 Mut/Mb.

## Clinical implications

- **Resource for rational preclinical trial design.** The authors explicitly frame the [PPTC PDX dataset](../datasets/pptc_2019.md) as a queryable resource (via PedcBioPortal) for selecting models that match a candidate-drug's biomarker. Worked example: querying for [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) loss, [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) mutation, defective homologous recombination, or [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) mutation/ALT identifies PDXs suitable for testing [ATR](../genes/ATR.md) inhibitors.
- **Biomarker-driven basket-trial design.** RNA-seq-defined high expression of immunotherapy targets ([GPC2](../genes/GPC2.md) and [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) in neuroblastoma and other pediatric histotypes; [CD274](../genes/CD274.md)/PD-L1 in [ASPS](../cancer_types/ASPS.md); [AKR1C3](../genes/AKR1C3.md)-activated prodrug in T-ALL) supports preclinical basket design across histologies.
- **TP53 / NF1 pathway-activity scores as candidate response biomarkers.** Authors propose using the expression-based [TP53 inactivation classifier](../methods/tp53-inactivation-classifier.md) and [NF1 inactivation classifier](../methods/nf1-inactivation-classifier.md) — rather than gene-level mutation status alone — to nominate tumors for pathway-targeted therapy. The classifiers identify functionally inactive cases driven by [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), or other phenocopies, and may be future biomarkers for response to TP53-pathway-targeting and NF1-loss-directed agents.
- **Relapse-enriched PDXs match the early-phase pediatric trial population.** 27% of models were derived from relapsed disease and 6% from post-neoadjuvant resections, so the cohort more faithfully recapitulates the post-chemoradiotherapy phase-1 patient population than diagnosis-only sequencing studies.

## Limitations & open questions

- **No paired normal samples.** Rare germline variants could not be removed, so reported TMB values are likely inflated; trends across histology and phase of therapy should remain valid, but [absolute](../methods/absolute.md) Mut/Mb cannot be directly compared with matched-normal cohorts.
- **WES misses pathogenic non-coding lesions** (e.g., the mechanism of [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) inactivation in OS-34-SJ, OS-43-TPMX, and OS-51-CHLX remains unresolved and may require whole-genome sequencing).
- **No DNA methylation profiling** — particularly limiting for pediatric brain tumors where methylation-based classification is now standard.
- **Histology gaps.** No models for acute myelogenous leukemia, juvenile myelomonocytic leukemia, lymphomas, retinoblastoma, melanoma, thyroid malignancies, or histone-mutant midline gliomas. Several rare-histology subtypes have low n.
- **RAS-pathway expression classifier underperforms** (AUROC 0.55), likely reflecting biology differences between pediatric and adult tumors; the [TP53 inactivation classifier](../methods/tp53-inactivation-classifier.md) and [NF1 inactivation classifier](../methods/nf1-inactivation-classifier.md) classifiers also require prospective validation in PDX response studies before clinical translation.
- **PDX cohort is biased toward highly aggressive disease**, reflecting the populations enrolled in pediatric phase-1 trials but not the broader pediatric oncology disease spectrum.
- **Selection / passage effects.** A few alterations (e.g. [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) amplification in Rh-30) are not retained between paired diagnosis and relapse PDXs, complicating direct genotype-phenotype mapping at the model level.
