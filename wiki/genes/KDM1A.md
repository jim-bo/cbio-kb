# KDM1A

## Overview

KDM1A (also known as LSD1) is a lysine-specific histone demethylase that removes mono- and di-methyl marks from H3K4 and H3K9, functioning as a transcriptional co-repressor or co-activator depending on context. In acute myeloid leukemia ([AML](../cancer_types/AML.md)), KDM1A is a validated therapeutic target, particularly in [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) (MLL)-rearranged disease where its demethylase activity supports leukemic stem-cell maintenance. Small-molecule LSD1 inhibitors are in clinical development for AML.

## Cancer types (linked)

- [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md): KDM1A is a recognized therapeutic target in AML, especially KMT2A-rearranged cases; LSD1 inhibitors (e.g., ORY-1001, IMG-7289) under clinical investigation [PMID:30333627](../papers/30333627.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-listed with [TET2](../genes/TET2.md), [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md), [BCL2](../genes/BCL2.md), [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md), and [DOT1L](../genes/DOT1L.md) as epigenetic drug-target genes in the Beat AML landscape [PMID:30333627](../papers/30333627.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- LSD1 inhibitors are a candidate therapeutic class for KMT2A-rearranged and other epigenetically driven AML subtypes, supported by biological rationale from the Beat AML study [PMID:30333627](../papers/30333627.md)

## Open questions

- Clinical efficacy of LSD1 inhibitors in molecularly defined AML subgroups (e.g., KMT2A-rearranged vs. NPM1-mutant) remains to be established in randomized trials.
