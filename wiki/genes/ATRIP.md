# ATRIP

## Overview

ATRIP (ATR-interacting protein) is an essential cofactor of the [ATR](../genes/ATR.md) (ataxia telangiectasia and Rad3-related) serine/threonine kinase. ATRIP recruits ATR to RPA-coated single-stranded DNA at stalled replication forks and DNA damage sites, forming the ATR-ATRIP heterodimeric complex required for checkpoint signaling. In cancer genomics, ATRIP is relevant as a component of the replication-stress response pathway and as a context-dependent vulnerability whose essentiality tracks the abundance of the RPA complex.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Knockdown sensitivity to ATR tracks the ATR/ATRIP and RPA1-containing RPA complexes in CCLE proteomics of 375 cancer cell lines; proteome-level complex abundance is a better predictor of ATR knockdown sensitivity than single transcripts [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

- No cancer-type-specific alteration data for ATRIP in the current corpus.

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- ATRIP functions as an obligate heterodimer partner of ATR; knockdown sensitivity of both genes tracks RPA complex abundance in CCLE cell lines [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- ATRIP-dependent ATR complex abundance may serve as a biomarker for sensitivity to ATR inhibitors; protein-complex-level predictors outperform single-gene expression readouts in CCLE data [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Open questions

- Whether ATRIP protein abundance independently predicts ATR-inhibitor sensitivity in primary tumors (versus cell lines) is untested.
