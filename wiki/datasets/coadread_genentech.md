# Colorectal Adenocarcinoma (Genentech, Nature 2012)

## Overview

74 primary colon tumour-normal pairs collected from commercial sources (fresh-frozen) and characterised by multi-platform genomic profiling at Genentech. Published in Nature 2012. The cohort identified recurrent RSPO2/RSPO3 gene fusions as an alternative mechanism of Wnt pathway activation in colorectal cancer. [PMID:22895193](../papers/22895193.md)

## Composition

- 74 tumour-normal pairs total: 72 by exome sequencing, 68 by RNA-seq, 74 by Illumina 2.5M SNP arrays, 2 by whole-genome sequencing.
- 15 MSI samples (mean 47 mutations/Mb; 764–3,113 coding mutations per sample); 57 MSS samples (mean 2.8 mutations/Mb; 31–149 coding mutations per sample).
- Cancer types: [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md) / [COADREAD](../cancer_types/COADREAD.md).
- Mean exome coverage: 179x (SeqCap EZ v2.0/NimbleGen capture; HiSeq 2000 sequencing). [PMID:22895193](../papers/22895193.md)

## Notable findings derived from this cohort

- [RSPO2](../genes/RSPO2.md) fusions (EIF3E-RSPO2) in 3% (2/68) of tumours and [RSPO3](../genes/RSPO3.md) fusions (PTPRK-RSPO3) in 8% (5/68), combined ~10%; mutually exclusive with [APC](../genes/APC.md) mutations (P = 0.038, Fisher's exact test). [PMID:22895193](../papers/22895193.md)
- 23 significantly mutated genes in MSS samples (FDR ≤ 5%) including [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [APC](../genes/APC.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md), [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), and [ATM](../genes/ATM.md). [PMID:22895193](../papers/22895193.md)
- [IGF2](../genes/IGF2.md) focal amplification and upregulation in 12% (8/68) of samples. [PMID:22895193](../papers/22895193.md)
- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) amplification (8q) in 23% and [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) amplification in 13%. [PMID:22895193](../papers/22895193.md)
- [ERBB3](../genes/ERBB3.md) mutated in 8% with multiple oncogenic hotspot mutations; novel recurrent mutations in [TCF7L2](../genes/TCF7L2.md), [TET2](../genes/TET2.md), and [TET3](../genes/TET3.md). [PMID:22895193](../papers/22895193.md)
