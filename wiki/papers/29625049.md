**PMID:** 29625049 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.cell.2018.03.033 · **Journal:** Cell (2018)

## TL;DR

This is the capstone Perspective of the TCGA PanCancer Atlas, synthesizing analyses across **11,000 tumors from 33 cancer types** into three themes: (1) interactions between somatic drivers and germline pathogenic variants, (2) links between the tumor genome and the methylome/transcriptome/proteome, and (3) interactions between cancer cells and the tumor microenvironment. Using a consensus driver list of **299 genes**, **>3,200 oncogenic mutations**, and **>800 pathogenic/likely-pathogenic germline variants in 99 predisposition genes** (~8% of cases), Ding et al. characterize the joint germline–somatic landscape, somatic–somatic co-occurrence and mutual-exclusivity networks, cis/trans transcriptional effects of driver mutation type, and driver-immune-subtype associations across six immune subtypes (C1–C6).

## Cohort & data

- **11,000 tumors across 33 cancer types** from the TCGA PanCancer Atlas; 9,389 samples used for joint germline/somatic analyses ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- Germline calling on dbGaP-deposited paired tumor/normal data aligned to GRCh37-lite with [bwa](../methods/bwa.md) v0.5.9; germline SNVs via [varscan](../methods/varscan.md) v2.3.8 and GATK HaplotypeCaller; indels via Varscan, GATK, and [pindel](../methods/pindel.md) (≥2-of-3 consensus, or Pindel-unique ≥30× coverage / ≥20% VAF). Germline analysis identified ~1.5 billion calls, refined to **1,461 manually-reviewed pathogenic/likely-pathogenic variants** ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- Somatic calling via the [mc3](../methods/mc3.md) PASS MAF (syn7824274), produced by 7 callers: [mutect](../methods/mutect.md), [varscan](../methods/varscan.md), [indelocator](../methods/indelocator.md), [pindel](../methods/pindel.md), [somatic-sniper](../methods/somatic-sniper.md), [radia](../methods/radia.md), and [muse](../methods/muse.md). 22,485,627 putative variants reduced to **2,907,335 high-confidence calls**; ~3.6 million somatic calls reported across the cohort ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- Tumors and dataset bundles correspond to the TCGA PanCancer Atlas study set including [gbm_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/gbm_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [brca_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/brca_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [coadread_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/coadread_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [ov_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/ov_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [luad_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/luad_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [lusc_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/lusc_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [ucec_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/ucec_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [stad_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/stad_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), and others.
- Assays: TCGA [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) for somatic/germline variants, [rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) (RSEM via [rsem](../methods/rsem.md)) for expression, [dna-methylation-array](../methods/dna-methylation-array.md) for methylation, [rppa](../methods/rppa.md) for proteomics, and CIBERSORT-derived immune fractions ([cibersortx](../methods/cibersortx.md), 22 cell types aggregated to 9). MSI quantified via [msisensor](../methods/msisensor.md) (threshold score >4).

## Key findings

- **Predisposition genes are dominated by genome-integrity pathways.** Genome-integrity genes account for **63% (490/769)** of germline pathogenic variants but only **14% (8,850/75,825)** of somatic driver events (Fisher's exact p = 7e-151) ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- **[OV](../cancer_types/OV.md) is the cancer type most skewed toward genome-integrity disruption** in both germline and somatic compartments, driven by BRCA1/BRCA2 germline variants and high-frequency [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation. [LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md) has **4% germline vs 89% somatic** genome-integrity disruption ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- **Earlier age of onset with germline BRCA1/2.** Mean age at diagnosis was **54.4 ± 13.0 years** for germline-BRCA1/2 carriers vs **62.3 ± 13.4 years** when the BRCA1/2 mutation is somatic (P = 2.07e-10, 95% CI -10.27 to -5.57). Significant per-tumor FDRs: OV 9.12e-6, LUSC 9.23e-3, [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) 1.15e-2 (t-test) ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- **BRCA1/2 has 854 somatic mutations (571 samples) and 153 germline mutations (152 samples)** when considered jointly across the pan-cancer cohort ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- **MMR/MSI:** 250/1,464 samples with non-synonymous somatic MMR mutations and 18/60 samples with germline pathogenic MMR variants reach MSIsensor score >4. 16/18 germline-MMR MSI-high samples also carry somatic MMR mutations, a candidate checkpoint-blockade-responsive population ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- **MSI-high tumors over-express immune effectors** ([GZMA](../genes/GZMA.md), [PRF1](../genes/PRF1.md), [GZMK](../genes/GZMK.md), [GZMH](../genes/GZMH.md)) in [COADREAD](../cancer_types/COADREAD.md), [STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md), and [UCEC](../cancer_types/UCEC.md) (Kolmogorov–Smirnov P < 0.01) ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- **TP53 is the largest co-occurrence/exclusivity hub** of the somatic driver network (Mantel–Haenszel FDR < 0.1): co-occurring with [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md), [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md), [PPP2R1A](../genes/PPP2R1A.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md); mutually exclusive with [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- **Ras-pathway exclusivity is preserved across virtually all 33 tumor types**: [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) mutually exclusive with KRAS/NRAS/HRAS; homologous pairs IDH1/IDH2 and GNAQ/GNA11 show exclusivity (the latter dominating uveal melanoma); PIK3CA–[PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md) show exclusivity within the PI3K complex ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- **Context-dependent driver interactions:** TP53 and [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) are mutually exclusive in [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md), [READ](../cancer_types/READ.md), and [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) but **significantly co-occur in [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md)**, demonstrating that pan-cancer relationships do not always hold tissue-by-tissue ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- **Cis transcriptional effect of mutation type:** Missense mutations tend to up-regulate driver expression; nonsense/frameshift mutations down-regulate it (consistent with nonsense-mediated decay). Tumor suppressors ATRX, [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), RB1 show reduced expression in mutants; oncogenes [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) and [KIT](../genes/KIT.md) show elevated expression in missense mutants (FDR < 0.1). **[GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md) is the notable exception in BRCA**: frameshift/nonsense alleles increase mRNA (FDR = 4.54e-18 Welch's t-test), consistent with reported gain-of-function ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- **Multiplatform integration:** Of 40 driver genes associated with multiplatform iCluster combinations, **TP53, KRAS, and PIK3CA are each enriched in ≥10 multiplatform clusters**, with ARID1A, BRAF, CTNNB1, [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), and [APC](../genes/APC.md) enriched in ≥4 ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- **LGG/GBM split by methylation cluster, not by mRNA cluster:** IDH1-driven LGGs fall into methylation cluster 1 (**330/351 samples are IDH1-mutant**), while EGFR-driven [LGG](../cancer_types/LGG.md) and [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) fall into methylation cluster 16, despite both groups occupying the same mRNA and RPPA clusters ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- **Six pan-cancer immune subtypes** (Wound Healing C1 = 2,416; IFN-γ Dominant C2 = 2,591; Inflammatory C3 = 2,397; Lymphocyte Depleted C4 = 1,157; Immunologically Quiet C5 = 385; TGF-β Dominant C6 = 180), derived by mclust on 5 representative signatures and BIC selection (160-signature ssGSEA panel) ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- **Driver mutations sculpt the immune infiltrate:** Within C3, BRAF-mutant tumors have higher CD8 T-cell fraction than NRAS-mutant tumors (ANOVA P < 2e-5), and a CD8–[CD274](../genes/CD274.md) (PD-L1)–[PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md) (PD-1) signaling loop is recovered, suggesting BRAF + PD-L1 co-targeting may be synergistic. Within C5, ATRX- or TP53-mutant tumors have more macrophages and fewer CD8 T-cells (ANOVA P < 2e-8); IDH1-driven gliomas show lower [STAT1](../genes/STAT1.md) and reduced [CXCL10](../genes/CXCL10.md) ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- **Neoantigen–infiltrate correlations differ by immune subtype:** CD8 fraction rises with neoantigen load in C2 (FDR < 1e-15); [CD4](../genes/CD4.md) and neutrophil fractions rise with neoantigen load in C3 (FDR < 1e-25); macrophage fraction rises with neoantigen load in C5 (FDR < 5e-3) ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- **Splicing & fusions as drivers:** The Splicing AWG identified 1,964 mostly missense/synonymous mutations creating novel splice junctions across 8,656 tumors; the Spliceosome Pathway AWG flagged 119 of >400 surveyed splicing-factor genes as likely drivers; fusion drivers range from disease-defining (e.g., SS18/SSX1 or [SSX2](../genes/SSX2.md) in synovial sarcoma) to small subsets (e.g., ALK/ROS1 fusions in [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md)) ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — Largest hub of somatic–somatic interactions; co-occurs with [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md), [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md), [PPP2R1A](../genes/PPP2R1A.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md); mutually exclusive with [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md). Drives one of two mutually-exclusive UCEC networks (with PPP2R1A). Mutual exclusivity with KRAS in COAD/READ/LUAD but co-occurrence in PAAD ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) / [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) — 854 somatic mutations (571 samples) + 153 germline mutations (152 samples). Germline carriers have **~8 years earlier onset** than somatic-only carriers. In BRCA, somatic BRCA1/2 mutants down-regulate antigen-processing and leukocyte-cytotoxicity programs; germline BRCA1/2 mutants down-regulate mitochondrial respiratory-chain and metabolic programs ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) — Defines methylation cluster 1 in LGG (330/351 samples mutant); IDH1-driven gliomas exhibit lower STAT1, decreasing [CXCL10](../genes/CXCL10.md) and immune infiltrate ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) — Drives methylation cluster 16 in LGG and GBM; missense mutations are associated with elevated EGFR mRNA in the cis-expression analysis ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) / [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) / [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) / [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — Strong mutual exclusivity within Ras signaling across nearly all 33 tumor types; KRAS/NRAS/HRAS and BRAF V600 are among the most frequently predicted neoantigens. BRAF-mutant tumors in C3 have higher CD8 T-cell fractions than NRAS-mutant tumors ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — Among the three most multiplatform-cluster-enriched drivers (with TP53 and KRAS); drives BRCA mRNA/RPPA clusters 23/C6 but spans methylation clusters 6 and 24 ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- [GNA11](../genes/GNA11.md) / [GNAQ](../genes/GNAQ.md) — Activating mutations in uveal melanoma; drive calcium-signaling deregulation in [UVM](../cancer_types/UVM.md) ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- [GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md) — In BRCA, frameshift/nonsense alleles paradoxically **increase** mRNA (FDR = 4.54e-18) and are associated with downregulated microtubule/cytoskeleton programs not seen in missense mutants, consistent with reported gain-of-function ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- [CDH1](../genes/CDH1.md) — In BRCA, nonsense/frameshift mutants upregulate leukocyte-migration genes; missense mutants do not ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- [CASP8](../genes/CASP8.md) — Missense mutants over-express [CASP8](../genes/CASP8.md) (FDR < 0.1) ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- [APC](../genes/APC.md) — APC- and KRAS-driven COAD/READ tumors share mRNA cluster 15 and RPPA C8 but partition into methylation clusters 10 vs 11 ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md), [PPP2R1A](../genes/PPP2R1A.md), [CTCF](../genes/CTCF.md) — Partition UCEC into TP53-PPP2R1A vs CTNNB1-PTEN-CTCF mutually-exclusive networks; ARID1A/PTEN/CTNNB1/PIK3CA-enriched cluster has low TP53, consistent with copy-number-low vs copy-number-high endometrial subtypes ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- DDR predisposition genes [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [BRIP1](../genes/BRIP1.md), [PALB2](../genes/PALB2.md), [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md) — Among the top germline-variant-bearing genes in the core DDR pathway (23 of 36 DDR-affecting predisposition genes belong to Core DDR) ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- MMR genes [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md), [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md), [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md) — Somatic non-synonymous mutations in 250/1,464 samples reach MSI-high; germline variants in MMR (18/60) also reach MSI-high, with 16/18 carrying both germline and somatic hits ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) — Hypermutated UCEC subtype context ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- [CD274](../genes/CD274.md) (PD-L1), [PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md) (PD-1) — Form a CD8–PD-L1–PD-1 signaling loop in the C3 immune subtype, where BRAF-driven tumors are enriched ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- [SS18](../genes/SS18.md) / [SSX1](../genes/SSX1.md) / [SSX2](../genes/SSX2.md) — [SS18](../genes/SS18.md)–SSX1/SSX2 fusion is disease-defining in synovial sarcoma (referenced from the Fusion AWG) ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- [ALK](../genes/ALK.md), [ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md) — Fusion drivers in small subsets of [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) (referenced from the Fusion AWG) ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).

## Clinical implications

- **Germline testing rationale:** ~8% of TCGA cases carry pathogenic germline variants in 99 predisposition genes, dominated by genome-integrity (PARP/HRD) pathways; germline BRCA1/2 carriers present nearly a decade earlier than somatic-only carriers, supporting age-stratified germline screening in OV, BRCA, and LUSC ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- **Checkpoint-blockade biomarker stack:** 16 of 18 germline-MMR carriers also carry somatic MMR mutations and are MSI-high, defining a population with elevated neoantigen load expected to respond to PD-1/PD-L1 blockade. MSI-high tumors in COADREAD/STAD/UCEC over-express GZMA/PRF1/GZMK/GZMH ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- **Combination therapy hypothesis:** In the C3 immune subtype, BRAF-mutant tumors enrich CD8 T-cells and assemble a CD8–PD-L1–PD-1 loop, suggesting synergy between BRAF inhibition and PD-L1 blockade ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- **Functional redundancy as a resistance mechanism:** TP53/PIK3CA and KRAS/BRAF mutual exclusivity in cell-death and MAPK processes (permutation P < 1e-5) implies that single-driver loss-of-function programs can be re-achieved through alternative drivers in heterogeneous tumors — relevant to acquired resistance and metastatic seeding ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- **Subtype-specific pathway therapy:** Calcium-signaling deregulation in [UVM](../cancer_types/UVM.md) (GNA11/GNAQ) and Notch deregulation in [BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md) highlight tumor-type-anchored druggable axes ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- **Bioinformatic care plans:** The Perspective anticipates that multi-omic, network-aware tools will be required to navigate the combinatorial space of drug + immunotherapy + signature-targeted regimens ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Authors highlight:** TCGA samples are overwhelmingly primary tumors; metastatic-driver biology is largely unaddressed and is a stated goal of the Cancer Moonshot / Human Tumor Atlas Network (HTAN) ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- **Authors highlight:** Oncogenic mutations such as BRAF V600E are observed in phenotypically normal tissues, raising the question of which somatic events are clonally tolerated vs. truly transformative ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- **Methodological caveats acknowledged in [STAR](../genes/STAR.md) Methods:** Mutation-burden dichotomization at 500 mutations for co-occurrence/exclusivity testing is heuristic; OV and [LAML](../cancer_types/LAML.md) samples retained `wga` filter flags to preserve sample size, introducing artifact risk; cell-of-origin clustering used private communications with the Cell-of-Origin AWG, complicating reproducibility ([PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)).
- **Open question for cross-paper synthesis:** Many findings are reported as fractions or odds ratios at FDR < 0.1, leaving room for stricter follow-up (e.g., effect-size meta-analysis against later MSK-IMPACT panel cohorts).
- **Coverage gap:** This Perspective references but does not deeply re-analyze the dedicated AWG manuscripts (Aneuploidy, Fusions, Splicing, Driver, Germline, MC3, Cell of Origin, PanImmune, Imaging, DDR, Spliceosome) — those are the primary sources for the underlying numbers.
