# CREBBP

## Overview

CREBBP (CREB-binding protein, CBP) is a transcriptional coactivator and histone acetyltransferase (HAT) that acetylates histones H3 and H4 and acts as a scaffold for transcription factor complexes. Together with its paralog EP300 (p300), CREBBP regulates gene expression programs critical for differentiation, DNA-damage response, and tumor suppression. Loss-of-function alterations in CREBBP are found in hematologic malignancies and some solid tumors. In glioblastoma, CREBBP and EP300 activity are key drivers of H2B N-terminal hyperacetylation marking a classical-like immune-low subtype.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- CREBBP harbors a single putative-driver somatic mutation in retinoblastoma (rbl_mskcc_2020, n=83), previously implicated by whole-exome sequencing studies in this disease [PMID:33466343](../papers/33466343.md).
- CREBBP and EP300 protein/acetylation levels are strongly associated with H2B-K12/K13/K16/K17/K21 acetylation in GBM (gbm_cptac_2021, n=99) by Lasso regression; CREBBP/EP300 activity defines a classical-like, immune-low GBM subset with H2B N-terminal hyperacetylation [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).
- Mutated in 15.7% of CSCC tumors; paralog of EP300; nominated as a driver gene in the 30-gene CSCC catalog from meta-analysis of 88 WES/WGS tumors [PMID:34272401](../papers/34272401.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **Retinoblastoma (RBL):** single putative-driver CREBBP mutation detected; previously implicated by WES in retinoblastoma [PMID:33466343](../papers/33466343.md).
- **Glioblastoma (GBM):** CREBBP protein and acetylation activity are key drivers of H2B N-terminal hyperacetylation, marking the classical-like (nmf3) immune-low GBM subtype; BRD1/BRD3/BRD4 correlate with additional H2B acetylation sites in this subset [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- CREBBP activity co-occurs with EP300 and BRD1/BRD3/BRD4 activity in the H2B-hyperacetylated classical-like GBM subset [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- CREBBP and EP300 inhibitors are nominated as therapeutic candidates for the classical-like, H2B-hyperacetylated GBM subset; hypothesis-generating from proteogenomic data, not clinically tested in this cohort [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Open questions

- Whether targeting CREBBP/EP300 HAT activity is clinically feasible and effective in H2B-hyperacetylated GBM requires preclinical validation [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).
- The functional significance of the single CREBBP mutation in retinoblastoma is unknown [PMID:33466343](../papers/33466343.md).
