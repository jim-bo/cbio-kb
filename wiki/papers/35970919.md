**PMID:** 35970919 · **DOI:** 10.1038/s41591-022-01930-z · **Journal:** Nature Medicine (2022)

## TL;DR

Chatila and colleagues profiled DNA and/or RNA from 738 patients with rectal adenocarcinoma — pooling the ACOSOG Z6041 (n=25), TIMING (n=71), MSK-Research (n=163), and MSK-Clinical (n=479) cohorts — to define genomic and transcriptomic correlates of response to neoadjuvant therapy (NAT) in locally advanced rectal cancer (LARC). They found that [APC](../genes/APC.md) mutations are significantly less frequent in lower-rectum and anal tumors, providing a molecular basis for the more aggressive behavior of distal disease. No somatic alteration was associated with complete response in a treatment-agnostic manner, but [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) mutations predicted faster relapse specifically in patients treated with chemoradiation followed by consolidative chemotherapy (CRT-CNCT). Transcriptomically, overexpression of [IGF2](../genes/IGF2.md) and [L1CAM](../genes/L1CAM.md) marked incomplete responders, and RNA-seq immune deconvolution identified a small subset of pMMR/MSS "immune-hot" tumors with prolonged disease-free survival and overexpression of immune-checkpoint genes — a candidate population for future ICI trials.

## Cohort & data

- **738 rectal adenocarcinoma patients** from four sources: ACOSOG Z6041 (NCT00114231, n=25), TIMING (NCT00335816, n=71), MSK-Research (MSK-R, n=163), MSK-Clinical (MSK-C, n=479) [PMID:35970919](../papers/35970919.md).
- Cancer type: [READ](../cancer_types/READ.md) — rectal adenocarcinoma; cBioPortal study [rectal_msk_2022](../datasets/rectal_msk_2022.md).
- **692 pre-treatment primary tumors** sequenced with the [MSK-IMPACT panel](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) (341–505 cancer-related genes). Stage I=78, II=77, III=375, IV=162. Tumors located in lower (LR, 0–4 cm from anal verge, n=257), middle (MR, 4–8 cm, n=197), and upper rectum (UR, 8–12 cm, n=204); detailed location unknown for 34.
- **MMR status:** 652/692 (94.2%) pMMR/MSS, 36/692 (5.2%) dMMR/MSI, 4/692 (0.6%) [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) hypermutants.
- **[Whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md):** 97 tumors for mutational-signature analysis.
- **[RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md):** 114 pre-treatment endoscopic biopsies; 97 with NAT outcome data; 87 with paired DNA, RNA, and outcomes.
- **Outcome-focused subset:** 346 stage II–III LARC patients (290 pMMR/MSS with MSK-IMPACT data) from TIMING (n=71), MSK-R (n=127), MSK-C (n=148); median follow-up 45.2 months.
- **Comparator cohort:** 178 proximal colon (PCA) + 204 distal colon (DCA) tumors from the published MSK-Colon cohort, sequenced with identical MSK-IMPACT methods.
- **Independent validation cohorts:** Kamran et al. LARC RNA-seq (n=15) for IGF2/L1CAM; TCGA LARC subset (n=42) for immune-cluster replication.

## Key findings

- **Mutational landscape of pMMR/MSS rectal cancer** (n=652): most frequently altered genes were [APC](../genes/APC.md) (81%), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (81%), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) (42%), [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md) (14%), and [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) (12%) [PMID:35970919](../papers/35970919.md). Top altered pathways: WNT (85%), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (82%), RAS (51%).
- **Co-occurrence / mutual exclusivity:** [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) and [AMER1](../genes/AMER1.md) significantly co-occurred (q<0.05); [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) was mutually exclusive with [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) and [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) (q<0.1). C-terminal [APC](../genes/APC.md) mutations co-occurred with [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) and [AMER1](../genes/AMER1.md). Oncogenic [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) mutations were subclonal in ~1/3 of cases where they co-occurred with [APC](../genes/APC.md) or TP53.
- **Clonality / WGD:** 82% of driver mutations were clonal. dMMR/MSI tumors had fewer TP53 mutations (38% vs 81%, p<0.001) and no whole-genome duplication events (0% vs 40.1%, p=0.026). Biallelic TP53 inactivation correlated with higher fraction-genome-altered ([FGA](../genes/FGA.md)).
- **[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) hotspots:** Oncogenic [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) alterations in 274/652 (42%) of pMMR/MSS tumors, majority clonal, predominantly at codons G12 / G13.
- **Actionability per OncoKB:** 28% of pMMR/MSS tumors harbored ≥1 actionable alteration. Level 1: [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E (1%); Level 2: [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) amplification (3%); Level 3A: [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) G12C (2%); Level 3B: [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) (12%), [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) (4%), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) (2%).
- **APC by rectal location:** WNT-pathway alteration frequency declined from upper to lower rectum (UR 92% / MR 90% / LR 77%, p<0.001), driven by [APC](../genes/APC.md). Median tumor location of APC-altered cases was 6.85 cm from the anal verge vs 4 cm for APC-wild type (p<0.001). Replicated in an independent 157-patient MSK-OtherRectal cohort (p<0.001). Anal adenocarcinomas (n=9) had only 1/9 (11%) WNT-altered tumors. Biallelic APC inactivation was also less frequent in LR and anal tumors (p<0.001).
- **Rectal vs colon:** Compared with MSK-Colon (178 PCA + 204 DCA), rectal tumors had higher rates of lung metastasis and lower peritoneal/abdominal metastasis; PCAs had higher TMB among pMMR/MSS tumors, more RAS/PI3K alterations (q<0.001); rectal tumors had more TP53-pathway alterations (q<0.005). 20q amplification was more frequent in DCAs (q<0.05). Rectal and DCA APC mutations were predominantly C-terminal; PCAs predominantly N-terminal.
- **Genomic predictors of NAT response — null in pooled analysis:** Among 290 pMMR/MSS LARC patients, multivariate logistic regression detected no significant clinical or genomic association with complete response (CR). Multivariate Cox proportional-hazards for disease-free survival (DFS) found a borderline association of [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) with shorter DFS (p=0.04).
- **Treatment-stratified KRAS effect:** After stratification, [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) mutations were associated with significantly shorter DFS in CRT-CNCT-treated patients (p=0.004) but not in INCT-CRT-treated patients (p=0.122). KRAS was not associated with different CR rates in CRT-CNCT (25% vs 41%, p=0.277), and showed a borderline-non-significant improved CR in INCT-CRT (33% vs 15%, p=0.056).
- **Transcriptomic predictors:** Differential expression in CR vs incomplete-response (iCR) identified 7 down- and 8 up-regulated genes in iCR; iCR-overexpressed included [IGF2](../genes/IGF2.md) and [L1CAM](../genes/L1CAM.md). Strong [IGF2](../genes/IGF2.md) overexpression was mutually exclusive with PI3K-pathway alterations. High [L1CAM](../genes/L1CAM.md) correlated with CMS4, lower CR, shorter DFS, and higher metastasis rates. Both validated in the independent Kamran et al. cohort (n=15).
- **[CA9](../genes/CA9.md) (hypoxia marker):** Increased in [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)- or [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)-mutant tumors, particularly double-mutants (p<0.001).
- **CMS distribution and outcomes:** CMS4 patients had the worst DFS; CMS2 had slightly better DFS than CMS1/CMS3.
- **Immune clustering (ssGSEA, n=101):** [Hierarchical clustering](../methods/consensus-hierarchical-clustering.md) on pMMR/MSS tumors (n=96) identified IG1 (low, n=52), IG2 (intermediate, n=37), IG3 (immune-hot, n=7); dMMR/MSI tumors formed IG4 (n=5). Immune-hot IG3 (~8% of pMMR/MSS) had higher inflammatory response ssGSEA, higher Th1 cells and regulatory T cells, lower DNA-repair signature, and overexpression of [PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md) (PD-1), [CD274](../genes/CD274.md) (PD-L1), [CTLA4](../genes/CTLA4.md), [HAVCR2](../genes/HAVCR2.md) (TIM3), and [LAG3](../genes/LAG3.md).
- **IG3 + IG4 outcomes (n=87 with DNA/RNA/outcomes):** Better response and DFS than IG1/IG2. [APC](../genes/APC.md) and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations were less frequent in IG3/IG4; [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) mutations less frequent in IG2.
- **CD3/CD4 IHC validation:** CD3 IF correlated with ssGSEA T-cell scores (p<0.001); [CD4](../genes/CD4.md) IF correlated with Th-helper RNA signatures (p<0.01).
- **External replication:** Immune-cluster findings replicated in a 42-sample LARC subset of TCGA.

## Genes & alterations

- [APC](../genes/APC.md) — Altered in 81% of pMMR/MSS rectal tumors. **Frequency decreases toward the anal verge** (UR 92% / MR 90% / LR 77% WNT-pathway, p<0.001); APC-altered cases sit a median 6.85 cm vs 4 cm from the anal verge (p<0.001). Replicated in MSK-OtherRectal (n=157). C-terminal in rectum/DCA; N-terminal in PCA.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — Altered in 81% of pMMR/MSS; only 38% of dMMR/MSI (p<0.001). Biallelic inactivation correlated with higher FGA. Truncating > missense for FGA. Less frequent in immune-hot IG3/IG4 tumors.
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — Oncogenic alterations in 42% (274/652) of pMMR/MSS, mostly clonal at G12/G13. **G12C in 2% (Level 3A).** Borderline shorter DFS overall (p=0.04). **Significantly shorter DFS in CRT-CNCT–treated patients (p=0.004); not significant in INCT-CRT.** Borderline improved CR in INCT-CRT (33% vs 15%, p=0.056).
- [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md) — Altered in 14% of pMMR/MSS rectal tumors.
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — Altered in 12% (Level 3B); mutually exclusive with TP53; subclonal in ~1/3 of co-occurrent cases with APC/TP53.
- [AMER1](../genes/AMER1.md) — Co-occurs significantly with KRAS and with C-terminal APC mutations (q<0.05).
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — V600E in 1% (only Level 1 actionable finding).
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) — Amplification in 3% (Level 2).
- [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) — Mutated in 4% (Level 3B in CRC).
- [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) — Mutated in 2% (Level 3B).
- [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) — Hypermutant in 4/692 (0.6%) tumors.
- [MUTYH](../genes/MUTYH.md) — Strong SBS36 signature observed in one pMMR/MSS patient (single case).
- [IGF2](../genes/IGF2.md) — **Overexpressed in incomplete-responder (iCR) patients.** Strong overexpression mutually exclusive with PI3K-pathway alterations. Validated in Kamran et al. independent cohort.
- [L1CAM](../genes/L1CAM.md) — **Overexpressed in iCR patients; higher levels associated with CMS4, lower CR, shorter DFS, and higher metastasis rates.** IHC confirmed L1CAM-expressing tumor cells pre- and post-NAT. Validated independently.
- [CA9](../genes/CA9.md) — Hypoxia-induced; expression elevated in KRAS-/PIK3CA-mutant tumors, especially double-mutants (p<0.001).
- [PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md) (PD-1), [CD274](../genes/CD274.md) (PD-L1), [CTLA4](../genes/CTLA4.md), [HAVCR2](../genes/HAVCR2.md) (TIM3), [LAG3](../genes/LAG3.md) — Overexpressed in IG3 immune-hot (and to a lesser extent IG4) tumors, nominating these patients for ICI trials.

## Clinical implications

- **Organ preservation candidates:** Rate of organ preservation increased over time (modern era), while overall CR (pathological + clinical) rate remained stable. KRAS status and immune profile may help risk-stratify patients considered for non-operative management.
- **KRAS as treatment-specific biomarker:** [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) mutations predict shorter DFS specifically when patients receive chemoradiation followed by consolidative chemotherapy (CRT-CNCT). The authors interpret KRAS as having treatment-specific predictive value rather than a treatment-agnostic prognostic signal, and call for prospective randomized validation.
- **L1CAM and IGF2 as candidate biomarkers / drug targets:** [L1CAM](../genes/L1CAM.md) overexpression and [IGF2](../genes/IGF2.md) overexpression mark poor responders to NAT in LARC. The authors cite L1CAM-inhibitory molecules and IGF2 inhibitors as candidate therapeutic strategies for non-responders, pending prospective validation.
- **Immune-hot pMMR/MSS subset for ICI trials:** A ~8% subset of pMMR/MSS LARC tumors (IG3) exhibit immune-hot biology with checkpoint-target overexpression — overall trials of ICI in unselected pMMR/MSS LARC have failed, but selecting this subset could re-enable immunotherapy strategies. Consistent with the "Immunoscore" prognostic concept.
- **Anatomic location matters molecularly:** Tumors close to the anal verge harbor less frequent [APC](../genes/APC.md)/WNT activation. This provides a genomic basis for previously anatomy-only explanations of more aggressive distal-rectum behavior and supports recording precise rectal location in future trials.
- **Actionable alterations are rare:** Only 28% of pMMR/MSS rectal tumors harbor any OncoKB-actionable alteration, with Level 1 ([BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E) and Level 2 ([ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) amp) at 1% and 3% respectively.

## Limitations & open questions

- The majority of samples were sequenced with targeted exome panels ([MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md)); broader sequencing platforms ([WES](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md), WGS, methylation) might reveal additional drivers.
- [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) was only available for a small subset (114/738 patients), limiting power for transcriptomic analyses.
- Cohorts are non-overlapping in calendar time, conflating treatment [paradigm](../methods/paradigm.md) shifts (CRT, CRT-CNCT, INCT-CRT) with patient accrual era — KRAS-DFS treatment-specific effects may be confounded by changes in surgical timing, dose, and management.
- Surgical vs non-operative management timing (interval between NAT completion and surgery) may also affect both response and recurrence patterns.
- The borderline KRAS / INCT-CRT improved-CR finding (p=0.056) needs prospective testing — direction is opposite to the CRT-CNCT shorter-DFS finding.
- Authors anticipate that molecular data from OPRA, TNT, and PROSPECT trials will be needed to robustly validate genomic / transcriptomic / immune predictors.
- Only 7 immune-hot IG3 tumors were identified in the n=96 pMMR/MSS RNA cohort — the cluster is robust but small; replication required.
