# LTN1

## Overview

LTN1 (Listerin E3 Ubiquitin Protein Ligase 1) is an E3 ubiquitin ligase involved in ribosome-associated quality control (RQC), targeting stalled polypeptides for proteasomal degradation. In cancer genomics, LTN1 emerged as a newly identified significantly mutated gene (SMG) in the hypermutated subgroup of colon cancer in a CPTAC proteogenomic study, with mutation frequency exceeding 50% in MSI-H/hypermutated samples — a finding not previously reported in the TCGA colorectal study.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Newly identified hypermutated-group SMG in colon adenocarcinoma: mutation frequency >50% in MSI-H/hypermutated samples (n=110 tumors in the CPTAC [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md) proteogenomic cohort); not reported as an SMG in the prior TCGA colorectal study [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)
- LTN1 (RQC E3 ubiquitin ligase targeting nascent dysfunctional proteins) is downregulated in MSI cancer cell lines; its co-complex partner TCF25 is upregulated [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md):** LTN1 is an SMG specific to the hypermutated/MSI-H subgroup in the CPTAC proteogenomic colon cancer cohort [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- LTN1 mutations co-occur with [CASP5](../genes/CASP5.md), [RNF43](../genes/RNF43.md), and [BMPR2](../genes/BMPR2.md) mutations in the hypermutated subgroup of colon cancer, forming a cluster of novel SMGs enriched in MSI-H tumors [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- LTN1-mutant tumors occur exclusively in the MSI-H/hypermutated subgroup of colon cancer, which is associated with sensitivity to immune checkpoint blockade; no direct therapeutic data for LTN1 itself [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Open questions

- Whether LTN1 mutations are driver events or passenger mutations in the hypermutation context is not established; functional validation is lacking [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)
- LTN1 was not identified as an SMG in the TCGA CRC study — whether this reflects cohort composition, the proteogenomic analytical framework, or genuine biology remains unresolved [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)
