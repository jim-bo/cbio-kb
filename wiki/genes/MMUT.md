# MMUT

## Overview

MMUT (Methylmalonyl-CoA Mutase) encodes a mitochondrial enzyme that catalyzes the isomerization of methylmalonyl-CoA to succinyl-CoA in the branched-chain amino acid and odd-chain fatty acid catabolic pathway. In gastric cancer, MMUT is a candidate cis-regulatory target of a recurrent non-coding [CTCF](../genes/CTCF.md) binding site (CBS) hotspot mutation at chromosome 6q25.3 (chr6:50.57 Mb). Expression of MMUT is upregulated in gastric tumors carrying this CBS hotspot mutation, implicating disruption of a CTCF loop boundary in altered local gene regulation.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- MMUT (referred to as "MUT" in the original paper) — upregulated in gastric adenocarcinoma tumors bearing the chr6:50,570,087–50,570,110 CBS hotspot mutation (P=0.0021, adjusted P=0.042); co-regulated target of the same CTCF loop boundary disruption that also upregulates [CENPQ](../genes/CENPQ.md); analysis based on 49/187 samples with paired RNA-seq [PMID:29670109](../papers/29670109.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md) — MMUT is a candidate cis-target of a recurrent non-coding CBS hotspot mutation in gastric adenocarcinoma; its upregulation in mutated tumors is statistically significant (adj P=0.042) and is proposed as part of a mechanism linking CTCF binding site disruption to chromosomal instability via altered mitochondrial metabolic gene expression [PMID:29670109](../papers/29670109.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-upregulated with [CENPQ](../genes/CENPQ.md) at the chr6:50.57 Mb CBS hotspot locus; CENPQ encodes a centromere subunit linked to mitotic progression, providing a potential mechanistic bridge from non-coding mutation to chromosomal instability (CIN) [PMID:29670109](../papers/29670109.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic relevance established. MMUT upregulation at this locus is a candidate mechanistic link to CIN-subtype gastric cancer biology rather than an actionable target.

## Open questions

- The causal direction — whether CBS hotspot mutation leads to MMUT upregulation via disrupted CTCF looping or whether the association reflects confounding by CIN subtype — is not experimentally resolved; no allele-specific expression could be measured due to inter-SNP distance constraints [PMID:29670109](../papers/29670109.md)
- The biological consequence of MMUT upregulation in this context (altered propionate/methylmalonate metabolism in cancer cells) has not been characterized [PMID:29670109](../papers/29670109.md)
