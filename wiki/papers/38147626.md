# High-risk and silent clonal hematopoietic genotypes in patients with nonhematologic cancer

**PMID:** 38147626 · **DOI:** 10.1182/bloodadvances.2023011262 · **Journal:** Blood Advances (2024)

## TL;DR

Stonestrom et al. analyzed matched-normal blood sequencing from 42,714 MSK patients with nonhematologic cancer profiled by MSK-IMPACT to characterize clonal hematopoiesis (CH) genotypes associated with hematologic malignancy risk. They identified [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md), [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md), and [XPO1](../genes/XPO1.md) mutations as high-risk CH genotypes, and showed that "silent" CH (synonymous / noncoding variants), especially when multiple variants were present or VAF was high, also predicted hematologic malignancy. Tracking clones into 26 subsequent hematologic malignancies revealed that [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md) and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) VAFs consistently expanded at disease onset, whereas [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md) and silent CH VAFs mostly decreased [PMID:38147626](../papers/38147626.md).

## Cohort & data

- 42,714 patients with nonhematologic cancer who underwent matched normal peripheral-blood sequencing as the germline comparator for MSK-IMPACT tumor profiling; 39,510 were alive without a hematologic diagnosis within 2 weeks of sequencing and had follow-up beyond that [PMID:38147626](../papers/38147626.md).
- Dataset: [msk_ch_2023](../datasets/msk_ch_2023.md) ("Cancer Therapy and Clonal Hematopoiesis, MSK, Blood Adv 2023"), available on cBioPortal.
- Assay: [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) hybridization-capture targeted sequencing, 341–505 genes depending on panel version [PMID:38147626](../papers/38147626.md).
- Hematologic malignancy events were identified via a hybrid NLP (CEDARS) + manual chart-review pipeline with final diagnoses confirmed by board-certified hematologists/oncologists [PMID:38147626](../papers/38147626.md).

## Key findings

- Any CH was associated with increased risk of subsequent hematologic malignancy (HR = 1.93, 95% CI 1.45–2.57, P < .001) after adjustment for age, sex, and solid cancer type [PMID:38147626](../papers/38147626.md).
- Gene-level hazards identified [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md), [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md), and [XPO1](../genes/XPO1.md) as CH genotypes conferring particularly high hematologic malignancy risk [PMID:38147626](../papers/38147626.md).
- Chronic hematologic diseases were predicted better than acute diseases by baseline CH, consistent with length bias in detection [PMID:38147626](../papers/38147626.md).
- "Silent" CH — synonymous coding and nonsplice noncoding variants — was associated with increased hematologic malignancy risk, especially when multiple silent variants co-occurred or VAF was high, indicating that hematopoietic clonality predicts risk independent of mutational function [PMID:38147626](../papers/38147626.md).
- In 26 patients who developed a hematologic malignancy sequenced on the same platform, [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md) and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) CH VAFs consistently expanded at disease onset, whereas [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md) and silent CH VAFs largely decreased, implying divergent clonal trajectories by driver class [PMID:38147626](../papers/38147626.md).

## Genes & alterations

- [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md) — high-risk CH genotype; VAF expands at onset of hematologic malignancy [PMID:38147626](../papers/38147626.md).
- [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md) — high-risk CH genotype for subsequent hematologic malignancy [PMID:38147626](../papers/38147626.md).
- [XPO1](../genes/XPO1.md) — high-risk CH genotype for subsequent hematologic malignancy [PMID:38147626](../papers/38147626.md).
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — CH VAF consistently expands at hematologic malignancy onset, consistent with preleukemic selection under therapy [PMID:38147626](../papers/38147626.md).
- [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md) — most prevalent CH gene; VAFs mostly decreased at hematologic malignancy onset in this cohort [PMID:38147626](../papers/38147626.md).
- [TET2](../genes/TET2.md), [ASXL1](../genes/ASXL1.md) — prevalent CH drivers used as reference genotypes [PMID:38147626](../papers/38147626.md).

## Clinical implications

- CH interpretation in patients with solid tumors should weight genotype: [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md), [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md), and [XPO1](../genes/XPO1.md) mutations anticipate hematologic malignancy and may warrant closer hematologic surveillance [PMID:38147626](../papers/38147626.md).
- Silent CH (synonymous / noncoding), particularly with high VAF or multiple variants, is itself a signal of elevated risk and should not be dismissed as noise when found on tumor-normal panels [PMID:38147626](../papers/38147626.md).
- Divergent VAF trajectories ([JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md)/[TP53](../genes/TP53.md) expand vs. [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md) contract) argue for genotype-aware longitudinal monitoring in cancer patients receiving genotoxic therapy [PMID:38147626](../papers/38147626.md).

## Limitations & open questions

- Length bias: chronic hematologic diseases are more readily predicted than acute ones, limiting sensitivity for aggressive therapy-related myeloid neoplasms [PMID:38147626](../papers/38147626.md).
- Cohort is single-center (MSK) and defined by nonhematologic cancer patients, so generalizability to healthy populations or other assay platforms is uncertain.
- Only 26 patients had paired post-diagnosis sequencing to track VAF expansion, limiting statistical power of clonal trajectory claims [PMID:38147626](../papers/38147626.md).
- The degree to which specific prior therapies drive expansion of high-risk versus silent clones was not resolved.
