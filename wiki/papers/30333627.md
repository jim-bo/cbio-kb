**PMID:** 30333627 · **DOI:** 10.1038/s41586-018-0623-z · **Journal:** Nature (2018)

## TL;DR

The Beat [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md) program profiled 672 primary tumor specimens from 562 acute myeloid leukemia ([AML](../cancer_types/AML.md)) patients using whole-exome sequencing (622 specimens / 531 patients), RNA-sequencing (451 specimens / 411 patients), and a 122-compound ex vivo drug sensitivity assay (409 specimens / 363 patients). Integrating these layers, the authors identified novel mutational events not previously detected in AML, mapped drug-response patterns to single and combinatorial mutational events (e.g. [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md)-ITD/[NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md) co-mutation sensitizes to [ibrutinib](../drugs/ibrutinib.md) and [entospletinib](../drugs/entospletinib.md); [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md)/[RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md) co-mutation sensitizes to JAK inhibitors), and derived expression signatures predicting response. All data and an interactive Vizome browser (www.vizome.org) were released as the [aml_ohsu_2018](../datasets/aml_ohsu_2018.md) cohort.

## Cohort & data

- **672 tumor specimens from 562 unique AML patients** ([AML](../cancer_types/AML.md)), bone marrow aspirate or peripheral blood, collected across OHSU and 9 partner sites (Utah, UT Southwestern, Stanford, Miami, Colorado, Florida, NIH, Fox Chase, KUMC).
- **Whole-exome sequencing**: 622 specimens / 531 patients ([whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md)) on Illumina Nextera RapidCapture (37 Mb), aligned with [BWA](../methods/bwa.md) MEM, variants from [MuTect](../methods/mutect.md) v1.1.7, [VarScan](../methods/varscan.md) 2 v2.4.1, and [Pindel](../methods/pindel.md) (FLT3-ITD calls); annotated with [VEP](../methods/vep.md) v83 and converted via [vcf2maf](../methods/vcf2maf.md). 11.9-Mb custom-capture validation library applied to 96 specimens (94% SNV / 82% indel validation).
- **RNA-sequencing**: 451 specimens / 411 patients ([rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md)) using Agilent SureSelect Strand-Specific kit on HiSeq 2500.
- **Ex vivo drug sensitivity**: 409 specimens / 363 patients screened against a panel of 122 small-molecule inhibitors ([ex-vivo-drug-sensitivity-screen](../methods/ex-vivo-drug-sensitivity-screen.md)) within 24 hours of sample isolation; AUC summarized per drug.
- **Data release**: dbGaP study ID 30641 / accession phs001657.v1.p1; GDC; interactive Vizome browser. cBioPortal study: [aml_ohsu_2018](../datasets/aml_ohsu_2018.md).
- **Reference genome**: GRCh37 / b37 (Broad bundle 2.8).

## Key findings

- **Mutational landscape comparable to TCGA but with distinct rare events**: median 13 somatic variants per patient (range 1–80). The top 33 most commonly mutated genes overlapped with TCGA AML at similar frequencies, except [SRSF2](../genes/SRSF2.md) which was significantly more frequent in Beat AML (effect preserved in de novo–only comparison). Among low-frequency variants (<2%), 221 mutant genes were shared, 939 were unique to TCGA, ~1,500 were unique to Beat AML; 11 genes mutated in ≥1% of Beat AML patients had not been observed in prior AML sequencing studies (Fig. 1A–C).
- **Co-occurrence patterns**: significant co-segregation of frequent variants suggests biological cooperativity (Fig. 1D).
- **De novo vs. transformed drug response**: of 122 drugs tested, 64 were significantly more sensitive in de novo samples (FDR <0.1); only [panobinostat](../drugs/panobinostat.md) (HDAC inhibitor) was significantly more sensitive in transformed cases (Extended Data Fig. 4).
- **Single-gene drug-response associations** (Fig. 2A, Volcano plot):
  - [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md)-ITD → sensitive to FLT3-pathway inhibitors (proof-of-principle).
  - [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) or [ASXL1](../genes/ASXL1.md) mutation → broad drug resistance, with exceptions: TP53-mutant cases trended sensitive to [elesclomol](../drugs/elesclomol.md); ASXL1-mutant to [panobinostat](../drugs/panobinostat.md).
  - [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) or [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) mutation → broad resistance but predicted sensitivity to MAPK inhibitors; [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) showed stronger MAPK association than [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md).
  - [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) mutation → broad sensitivity; [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) mutation → broad resistance.
  - [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md) mutation → sensitivity to PIK3C/MTOR inhibitors (e.g. [BEZ235](../drugs/bez235.md)) and the VEGFR multi-kinase inhibitor [cediranib](../drugs/cediranib.md).
  - Spliceosome mutations [U2AF1](../genes/U2AF1.md) and [ZRSR2](../genes/ZRSR2.md) → novel sensitivity to multiple drugs.
- **Combinatorial mutation analysis for [ibrutinib](../drugs/ibrutinib.md)** (Fig. 2B): cases with [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md)-ITD alone or any [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md) mutation (including triple FLT3/NPM1/[DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md)) were significantly more sensitive than wild type, whereas [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md) alone or DNMT3A/FLT3 double-mutants were not different from [WT](../cancer_types/WT.md). [Entospletinib](../drugs/entospletinib.md) (selective [SYK](../genes/SYK.md) inhibitor) showed the same FLT3-ITD/NPM1 sensitivity pattern, consistent with prior reports of [SYK](../genes/SYK.md) as an FLT3-ITD interacting target.
- **JAK-inhibitor combinatorial signal** (Fig. 2C): co-mutation of [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md) with [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md) correlated with sensitivity to all four selective JAK inhibitors on the panel ([momelotinib](../drugs/momelotinib.md), [ruxolitinib](../drugs/ruxolitinib.md), [tofacitinib](../drugs/tofacitinib.md), JAK Inhibitor I), whereas [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md) alone or BCOR/[DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md) and BCOR/[SRSF2](../genes/SRSF2.md) co-mutations did not. BCOR-alone cases instead showed sensitivity to the tankyrase/WNT inhibitor XAV-939 and the multi-kinase inhibitor [crizotinib](../drugs/crizotinib.md).
- **Expression signatures of drug response**: 78 of 119 testable drugs (65.5%) had FDR<0.05 expression signatures distinguishing the most- vs. least-sensitive 20% of samples. A 17-gene expression signature stratified [ibrutinib](../drugs/ibrutinib.md) response (Fig. 3).
- **Multivariate WGCNA integration**: 14 co-expression gene clusters were identified across the cohort; lasso regression linked specific mutation × expression-cluster combinations to drug response. For ibrutinib, a 345-gene "turquoise" cluster (enriched for immune pathways) co-occurred with FLT3-ITD and predicted sensitivity (overlap with the 17-gene signature: representation factor 13.6, P<1.734e-04), while a 110-gene "magenta" subnetwork predicted resistance and tracked with adverse ELN 2017 risk.

## Genes & alterations

- [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md) — ITD detected by Pindel and an internal PCR/capillary-electrophoresis assay; FLT3-ITD predicted ibrutinib and [entospletinib](../drugs/entospletinib.md) sensitivity (alone or with [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md)).
- [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md) — 4-bp insertion mutations detected by internal PCR assay; any NPM1 mutation sensitized to ibrutinib/entospletinib; consensus calls derived by combining internal PCR with CLIA/CAP lab tests (Sequenome, GeneTrails, Foundation Medicine, Genoptix, Illumina) and exome data.
- [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md) — frequent co-mutation with FLT3-ITD and NPM1; DNMT3A alone was not sufficient to confer ibrutinib sensitivity.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — broad drug resistance with trend toward elesclomol sensitivity.
- [ASXL1](../genes/ASXL1.md) — broad resistance with trend toward [panobinostat](../drugs/panobinostat.md) sensitivity.
- [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — predicted MAPK-inhibitor sensitivity; NRAS association stronger than KRAS.
- [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md), [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) — opposing patterns: [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) broad resistance, [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) broad sensitivity.
- [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md) — PI3K/mTOR and VEGFR-multikinase sensitivity; co-mutation with BCOR drives JAK-inhibitor sensitivity.
- [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md) — combinatorial effect: alone sensitizes to XAV-939 and [crizotinib](../drugs/crizotinib.md); with [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md) sensitizes to JAK inhibitors.
- [SRSF2](../genes/SRSF2.md) — significantly more frequent in Beat AML than TCGA.
- [U2AF1](../genes/U2AF1.md), [ZRSR2](../genes/ZRSR2.md) — novel drug-sensitivity correlations for spliceosome mutations.
- [CEBPA](../genes/CEBPA.md) — biallelic mutation status (N-terminal + C-terminal) curated as a favorable-risk feature.
- [TET2](../genes/TET2.md), [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md), [BCL2](../genes/BCL2.md), [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md), [KDM1A](../genes/KDM1A.md), [DOT1L](../genes/DOT1L.md) — discussed as known therapeutic-target genes in the AML landscape (hypomethylating agents, [venetoclax](../drugs/venetoclax.md), [DOT1L](../genes/DOT1L.md) inhibitors for KMT2A-rearranged disease, LSD1/EZH2 inhibitors).

## Clinical implications

- **Co-mutation as a therapy-selection biomarker**: FLT3-ITD + NPM1 (regardless of DNMT3A status) defines a subset selectively sensitive to ibrutinib and entospletinib ex vivo, suggesting BTK/SYK pathway dependence in this combinatorial-mutation context.
- **BCOR + RUNX1 co-mutation** marks a subgroup with ex vivo sensitivity to JAK inhibitors ([momelotinib](../drugs/momelotinib.md), [ruxolitinib](../drugs/ruxolitinib.md), [tofacitinib](../drugs/tofacitinib.md)), implicating JAK-pathway dysregulation as a context-specific vulnerability.
- **Adverse-risk subgroup hypotheses**: TP53-mutant AML showed trend sensitivity to elesclomol; ASXL1-mutant to panobinostat — candidate avenues for genotype-matched repurposing in poor-prognosis disease.
- **Risk stratification cross-check**: a 110-gene "magenta" expression subnetwork associated with ibrutinib resistance was significantly enriched in adverse ELN 2017–risk patients, linking transcriptomic and clinical-risk frameworks.
- **Data resource**: the Beat AML cohort (Vizome / dbGaP phs001657 / cBioPortal [aml_ohsu_2018](../datasets/aml_ohsu_2018.md)) provides a public reference for biomarker-driven trial design and hypothesis generation in AML.

## Limitations & open questions

- The drug-response readout is an **ex vivo** AUC assay, not an in vivo or clinical-response endpoint; combinatorial sensitivity signals (e.g. FLT3-ITD/NPM1 → ibrutinib, BCOR/RUNX1 → JAK inhibitors) require clinical validation.
- Mechanisms underlying novel sensitivity correlations ([U2AF1](../genes/U2AF1.md), [ZRSR2](../genes/ZRSR2.md), IDH2, RUNX1, BCOR/RUNX1) "merit further investigation"; the paper does not establish causality.
- [Ibrutinib](../drugs/ibrutinib.md) is described as having "broad off-target effects when maintained in continuous culture with target cells," so single-drug ex vivo response should be interpreted cautiously.
- Comparisons of low-frequency variants (~1,500 unique to Beat AML, 939 unique to TCGA) may reflect cohort composition (de novo vs. transformed/secondary AML), sequencing-pipeline differences, and lift-over from NCBI36→GRCh37, not biology alone.
- 110 patients had multiple specimens (672 specimens / 562 patients), which the analyses do not always disentangle at the patient level.
- Multivariate WGCNA + lasso model is correlative and observational; the 14 co-expression clusters and their drug associations are hypothesis-generating.
