# DOCK7

## Overview

DOCK7 (dedicator of cytokinesis 7) is a guanine nucleotide exchange factor involved in cytoskeletal remodeling and cell migration. In the context of neuroblastoma, DOCK7 is identified as a component of the migratory gene program shared between the novel postnatal human adrenal gland progenitor cluster (hC1) and the undifferentiated, high-risk neuroblastoma cluster (nC3).

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- DOCK7 is part of the progenitor/migratory gene program shared between the hC1 postnatal human adrenal progenitor and the high-risk undifferentiated neuroblastoma nC3 cluster; identified as a significantly upregulated marker of hC1 (FDR <0.01, Welch's t-test) [PMID:34493726](../papers/34493726.md).
- Co-expression of DOCK7 and [CLDN11](../genes/CLDN11.md) in both hC1 and nC3 highlights a migratory/progenitor gene signature distinguishing high-risk tumor identity from low-risk noradrenergic clusters [PMID:34493726](../papers/34493726.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [NBL](../cancer_types/NBL.md) — DOCK7 co-expression with [CLDN11](../genes/CLDN11.md), [ERBB3](../genes/ERBB3.md), [NTRK2](../genes/NTRK2.md), and other progenitor genes marks the high-risk undifferentiated nC3 cluster; correlates with poor-prognosis transcriptional identity [PMID:34493726](../papers/34493726.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-expressed with [BCL11A](../genes/BCL11A.md), [ERBB3](../genes/ERBB3.md), [RTTN](../genes/RTTN.md), [TP63](../genes/TP63.md), [ASXL3](../genes/ASXL3.md), [POU6F2](../genes/POU6F2.md), [SOX6](../genes/SOX6.md), [CLDN11](../genes/CLDN11.md) in the hC1/nC3 progenitor/migratory gene program [PMID:34493726](../papers/34493726.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Not directly targeted in the corpus; DOCK7 expression in the high-risk nC3 cluster is a transcriptional correlate of unfavorable neuroblastoma identity [PMID:34493726](../papers/34493726.md).

## Open questions

- The functional role of DOCK7-mediated cytoskeletal remodeling in neuroblastoma cell migration and invasion has not been experimentally validated [PMID:34493726](../papers/34493726.md).
