# PTPRB

## Overview

PTPRB (protein tyrosine phosphatase receptor type B) encodes a receptor-type phosphatase expressed in vascular endothelium. In angiosarcoma, PTPRB mutations are recurrent and may contribute to oncogenesis through loss of phosphatase-mediated growth suppression.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- PTPRB mutations were identified in RT-AS in a whole-genome sequencing study; among RT-AS, PTPRB mutations were found only in tumors that were either known to be secondary to radiation therapy and/or had [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) amplification (p = 0.005), with a prevalence of 45% in this subgroup [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [ANGS](../cancer_types/ANGS.md) -- PTPRB mutations enriched in RT-AS with [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) amplification or confirmed radiation-associated origin; 45% prevalence in this subgroup [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- PTPRB mutations co-occur with [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) amplification in RT-AS (p = 0.005) and co-occur with [PLCG1](../genes/PLCG1.md) mutations in the RT-AS driver landscape [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No PTPRB-directed therapy is reported in this corpus [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).

## Open questions

- Whether PTPRB mutation status can serve as a biomarker distinguishing radiation-associated from sporadic angiosarcoma warrants further investigation [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).
