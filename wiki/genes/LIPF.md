# LIPF

## Overview

LIPF (Lipase F, Gastric Type) encodes a stomach-lineage-specific gastric lipase responsible for the initial digestion of dietary triglycerides. In the context of cancer genomics, LIPF has been identified as one of three gastric-lineage genes exhibiting broad non-coding indel enrichment in gastric and colorectal cancers. Its high tissue-specific expression (stomach: ≤2 TPM elsewhere) makes it a marker of lineage-specific mutational processes rather than a classical coding driver.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Broad non-coding indel enrichment at the LIPF locus detected in gastric adenocarcinoma whole-genome sequencing (n=212 samples across TCGA, ICGC, and Singapore cohorts); replicates prior reports of GC-specific non-coding indel enrichment; no expression change linked to these indels (Wilcoxon P not significant) [PMID:29670109](../papers/29670109.md)
- LIPF is a stomach-lineage-specific gene (gastric lipase; negligible expression in non-gastric tissues ≤2 TPM) nominated as a top broad-indel-enriched gene in gastric cancer WGS, alongside [PGC](../genes/PGC.md) and [MUC6](../genes/MUC6.md) [PMID:29670109](../papers/29670109.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md) — broad non-coding indel enrichment at the LIPF locus in gastric adenocarcinoma; elevated background indel rate at stomach-lineage genes thought to arise from tissue-specific mutational processes (e.g. replication-associated errors in open chromatin); no prognostic or functional significance established [PMID:29670109](../papers/29670109.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-enriched with [PGC](../genes/PGC.md) and [MUC6](../genes/MUC6.md) as stomach-lineage genes with broad non-coding indel hotspots in gastric cancer; none showed expression changes correlated with mutation status [PMID:29670109](../papers/29670109.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting reported. LIPF indels are not established as functional drivers; they are currently interpreted as a background mutational signature of stomach-lineage tissue rather than a therapeutic target.

## Open questions

- Whether the non-coding indel enrichment at LIPF reflects a genuine driver mechanism or purely elevated background mutation rate in open chromatin of stomach-lineage cells remains unresolved; expression analysis did not detect a cis-effect [PMID:29670109](../papers/29670109.md)
