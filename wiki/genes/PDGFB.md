# PDGFB

## Overview

PDGFB (Platelet-Derived Growth Factor Subunit B) encodes the B-chain of platelet-derived growth factor, a potent mitogen for cells of mesenchymal origin. In cancer, PDGFB is recurrently involved in gene fusions — most notably the COL1A1-PDGFB fusion characteristic of dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans — which constitutively activate PDGF receptor signaling and are targetable by [imatinib](../drugs/imatinib.md).

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- COL1A1-PDGFB fusion (n=1) classified ready-for-routine-use in pediatric solid tumors; targetable by imatinib; identified via comprehensive WES + RNA-seq in the MAPPYACTS relapsed pediatric cohort (n=787) [PMID:35292802](../papers/35292802.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Recurrent COL1A1-PDGFB fusion observed across pediatric solid tumor types in the MAPPYACTS cohort [PMID:35292802](../papers/35292802.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- [COL1A1](../genes/COL1A1.md) is the canonical fusion partner for PDGFB in pediatric and adult sarcomas [PMID:35292802](../papers/35292802.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- COL1A1-PDGFB fusions are targetable by imatinib (PDGFR inhibitor); classified as ready-for-routine-use actionable alteration in the MAPPYACTS pediatric precision-oncology program [PMID:35292802](../papers/35292802.md)

## Open questions

- Broader prevalence of COL1A1-PDGFB fusions across pediatric cancer subtypes beyond the single MAPPYACTS case remains to be characterized.
