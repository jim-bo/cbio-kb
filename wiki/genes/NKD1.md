# NKD1

## Overview

NKD1 (Naked Cuticle Homolog 1) encodes a WNT signaling antagonist that inhibits the pathway by interacting with Dishevelled proteins. It functions as a negative regulator of canonical WNT/β-catenin signaling. In prostate cancer, NKD1 has been identified as a WNT-pathway gene silenced by EZH2-mediated epigenetic repression in neuroendocrine prostate cancer (CRPC-NE).

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- WNT-pathway [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) target significantly down-regulated in CRPC-NE (P = 0.0046) [PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)
- NKD1 identified as part of the Wnt/Hedgehog signaling network activated by [MAML3](../genes/MAML3.md) fusion genes in the Wnt-altered pheochromocytoma/paraganglioma subtype [PMID:28162975](../papers/28162975.md)
- NKD1 mRNA elevated in CTNNB1-mutant HCCs (Wnt target gene upregulation at transcript level) but not differentially expressed at protein level in a European [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) proteogenomics cohort [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **CRPC-NE (castration-resistant prostate cancer — neuroendocrine)**: NKD1 significantly down-regulated vs. CRPC-Adeno (P = 0.0046); repression mediated by [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) [PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- NKD1 down-regulation co-occurs with [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) overexpression and RB1/TP53 loss in CRPC-NE [PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- NKD1 expression restored by [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) inhibitor GSK343 in NCI-H660 neuroendocrine prostate cancer cells [PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)

## Open questions

- Whether NKD1 loss functionally contributes to WNT pathway activation in CRPC-NE, or is purely a bystander effect of global [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) silencing, is not established.
