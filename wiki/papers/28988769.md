**PMID:** 28988769 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.cell.2017.09.007 · **Journal:** Cell (2017)

## TL;DR

Robertson et al. (TCGA bladder analysis working group) report the comprehensive molecular characterization of the full TCGA cohort of 412 chemotherapy-naive muscle-invasive bladder cancers ([BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md)) — an expansion of the prior 131-tumor analysis — integrating [whole-exome](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md), [SNP6 copy-number](../methods/affymetrix-snp6.md), [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md), [miRNA-seq](../methods/mirna-seq.md), [DNA methylation](../methods/hm450-methylation-array.md), and [RPPA](../methods/rppa.md) profiling. They identify 58 significantly mutated genes (34 not seen in the original 131-tumor cohort), confirm that [APOBEC3A](../genes/APOBEC3A.md)/[APOBEC3B](../genes/APOBEC3B.md)-driven mutagenesis dominates the mutational landscape (67% of all SNVs) and is paradoxically associated with the best survival, refine the mRNA expression taxonomy into five subtypes — luminal-papillary, luminal-infiltrated, luminal, basal-squamous, and a newly recognized **neuronal** subtype with the worst prognosis — and propose subtype-stratified therapeutic strategies. The dataset is published as cBioPortal study [blca_tcga_pub_2017](../datasets/blca_tcga_pub_2017.md), superseding the 2014 [blca_tcga_pub](../datasets/blca_tcga_pub.md) cohort.

## Cohort & data

- **412 chemotherapy-naive, invasive, high-grade urothelial tumors** (T1 [n=1], T2–T4a, N0–3, M0–1) from 36 tissue source sites; 4 expert genitourinary pathologists re-reviewed all cases [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **Histology:** 52 (13%) had urothelial carcinoma with variant histology — 42 squamous, 4 small cell/neuroendocrine, 2 micropapillary, 4 plasmacytoid; an additional 5 included pure squamous, non-bladder-origin squamous, and bladder adenocarcinoma cases [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **Clinical state:** Complete clinical data for 406; 35 patients had received prior intravesical BCG; 12 received neoadjuvant chemotherapy after tumor acquisition; at last follow-up 230 alive, 163 recurred, 182 died (≥122/67% cancer-related); median follow-up 20.9 months for the alive [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **Cancer type:** Bladder Urothelial Carcinoma ([BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md)).
- **Dataset (canonical):** [blca_tcga_pub_2017](../datasets/blca_tcga_pub_2017.md) (this 412-tumor cohort); supersedes [blca_tcga_pub](../datasets/blca_tcga_pub.md) (the 131-tumor 2014 subset).
- **Assays/platforms (Table S2.5):** [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) on Illumina HiSeq with Agilent SureSelect Human All Exon 50 Mb (n=412 tumor/normal pairs, mean coverage 85X, 79% target ≥30X); [Affymetrix SNP6.0](../methods/affymetrix-snp6.md) copy-number arrays with [GISTIC 2.0](../methods/gistic.md) and [ABSOLUTE](../methods/absolute.md); [HM450 methylation arrays](../methods/hm450-methylation-array.md); [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) (n=408); [miRNA-seq](../methods/mirna-seq.md); [RPPA](../methods/rppa.md) on 343/412 tumors with 208 antibodies; [whole-genome sequencing](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) on n=136 [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **Analytic methods:** [MuTect](../methods/mutect.md) (SNV calling, identified 128,772 SNVs + 2,888 indels), [Indelocator](../methods/indelocator.md), [MutSig 2CV](../methods/mutsig.md) (significantly mutated genes), [ABSOLUTE](../methods/absolute.md) (purity/ploidy/CCF), [Bayesian NMF](../methods/bayesian-nmf.md) ([mutational-signature](../methods/mutational-signatures.md) discovery), [Polysolver](../methods/polysolver.md) (HLA mutation calling), [Cluster of Cluster Assignments (COCA)](../methods/coca.md) integrative clustering, [VIPER](../methods/viper.md)-style regulon analysis [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).

## Key findings

- **High mutation burden, APOBEC-dominated.** Mean 8.2 / median 5.8 non-synonymous mutations per Mb across 412 tumors; 51% C>T transitions and 27% C>G transversions. Bayesian NMF on the SNV catalogue resolved 5 mutation signatures: APOBEC-a, APOBEC-b, C>T at CpG (5-methylcytosine deamination), [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) (one ultra-mutated case with [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) P286R, >4000 SNVs), and an [ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md) signature. APOBEC-a + APOBEC-b accounted for **67% of all SNVs**; mutation load correlated with [APOBEC3A](../genes/APOBEC3A.md) and [APOBEC3B](../genes/APOBEC3B.md) expression [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **APOBEC mutagenesis is mostly clonal and likely early.** 64% of all mutations, 62% of APOBEC-a-, and 75% of APOBEC-b-signature mutations were clonal (CCF ≥ 0.9), implying APOBEC mutagenic activity acts early in bladder cancer development [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **Mutation-signature clusters predict survival.** Four clusters (MSig1–MSig4) showed distinct outcomes (p = 1.4×10⁻⁴). **MSig1 (high-APOBEC, high-burden) had 75% 5-year survival**; MSig2 (low mutation rate) had the poorest at 22%. MSig4 was enriched in [ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md)-signature mutations and [ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md) mutations (24/39 cases, p = 10⁻¹³); [ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md)-signature mutations were highest in smokers carrying [ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md) mutations (p = 6.9×10⁻¹¹) [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **Neoantigen load tracks mutation burden and predicts survival** independently of age, AJCC stage, and squamous differentiation (p = 8×10⁻⁴; Table S2.12). HLA mutations were detected in 19/412 (4.6%) by [Polysolver](../methods/polysolver.md), enriched in MSig1 (p = 0.039) and in BCG-treated patients (4/35 = 11.4% vs 8/261 = 3.1%, p = 0.04) [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **58 significantly mutated genes (SMGs)** by [MutSig 2CV](../methods/mutsig.md) (q < 0.1; Table S2.9), 34 of which were new vs the 131-tumor cohort. Seven new SMGs mutated in >10% of samples: [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) (18%), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) (14%), [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md) (12%), [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md) (12%), [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) (12%), [SPTAN1](../genes/SPTAN1.md) (12%), [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) (11%) [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **Mutual exclusivity / co-occurrence patterns** (Tables S2.10–11, q < 0.2): mutually exclusive — [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)–[TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)–[RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)–[E2F3](../genes/E2F3.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)–[MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md), [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)–[E2F3](../genes/E2F3.md), [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)–[RB1](../genes/RB1.md); co-occurring — [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)+[RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)+[E2F3](../genes/E2F3.md), [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)+[CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md). [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)+[CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) co-occurred in 27 (7%) tumors, suggesting MIBCs that progressed from non-invasive precursors. 3/4 plasmacytoid tumors had nonsense [CDH1](../genes/CDH1.md) mutations (consistent with [PMID:26901067](../papers/26901067.md)) [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **Recurrent gene fusions (n=784 total).** The most common was an intra-chromosomal **[FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)–[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md) fusion (n=10)**. [PPARG](../genes/PPARG.md) was involved in 4 *TSEN2-PPARG* and 2 *MKRN2-PPARG* fusions; [PPARG](../genes/PPARG.md) was overexpressed in fusion-positive tumors (p = 6×10⁻³); 4/6 fusions yielded predicted proteins retaining both DNA- and ligand-binding domains. Bladder cell-line RNA-seq detected additional *CASC15-PPARG* (5637, 1A6) and *NR2C2-PPARG* (UC9) fusions also retaining functional [PPARG](../genes/PPARG.md) domains [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **Copy-number landscape (Affymetrix SNP6 + GISTIC).** 34 amplified and 32 deleted focal regions (q < 0.1). Recurrent amplifications targeted [AHR](../genes/AHR.md), [BCL2L1](../genes/BCL2L1.md), [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md), [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md), [E2F3](../genes/E2F3.md), [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md), [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md), [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md), [GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md), [MYCL](../genes/MYCL.md), [PPARG](../genes/PPARG.md), [NECTIN4](../genes/NECTIN4.md) (PVRL4), [SOX4](../genes/SOX4.md), [TERT](../genes/TERT.md), [YWHAZ](../genes/YWHAZ.md), [ZNF703](../genes/ZNF703.md). The most common (22%) focal deletion targeted [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) (9p21.3); [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md) (14q24.1) deletions (n=10, 2%) appeared as a new recurrent deletion not detected in the original 131-tumor cohort. 221/412 (54%) tumors had whole-genome doubling [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **Five mRNA expression subtypes** by NMF consensus clustering (n=408): luminal-papillary (35%), luminal-infiltrated (19%), luminal (6%), basal-squamous (35%), neuronal (5%); subtype-associated overall survival p = 4×10⁻⁴ [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
  - **Luminal-papillary** — papillary morphology (58% vs 20%, p < 10⁻¹³); enriched [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) mutations (42/57; p < 10⁻⁹), amplification, overexpression, and *FGFR3-TACC3* fusions (8/10; p = 4×10⁻³); low CIS scores; retained sonic-hedgehog signaling.
  - **Luminal-infiltrated** — lowest purity (median 0.46), strong smooth-muscle and myofibroblast signatures; corresponds to prior TCGA Cluster-II; high [CD274](../genes/CD274.md) (PD-L1) and [PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md) (PD-1).
  - **Luminal** — highest expression of uroplakins ([UPK1A](../genes/UPK1A.md), [UPK2](../genes/UPK2.md)) and umbrella-cell markers ([KRT20](../genes/KRT20.md), [SNX31](../genes/SNX31.md)).
  - **Basal-squamous** — high [CD44](../genes/CD44.md), [KRT5](../genes/KRT5.md), [KRT6A](../genes/KRT6A.md), [KRT14](../genes/KRT14.md); squamous markers; 37/45 squamous-feature tumors (p < 10⁻¹¹); enriched [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations (p = 5×10⁻³); more common in females (33% vs 21%, p = 0.024); strongest immune signature.
  - **Neuronal (new)** — 20 tumors including 3/4 with neuroendocrine histology and 17 *without* histopathologic NE features; 10/20 (50%) had [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)+[RB1](../genes/RB1.md) co-mutation or [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)+[E2F3](../genes/E2F3.md) amplification; 17/20 (85%) had p53/cell-cycle pathway alterations; **poorest survival** of the five subtypes (p = 4×10⁻⁴) [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **DNA methylation hypomethylation cluster 4 = luminal-papillary signature.** A high-purity hypomethylation group with frequent [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) mutation (p = 6×10⁻⁹), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) deletion (p = 4×10⁻¹³), no [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) or [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) mutations, papillary histology, node-negative disease, younger patients (median age 61 vs 69, p < 4×10⁻³), and better survival (p < 0.05) [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **158 epigenetically silenced genes** identified by integrating methylation + expression. [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md), and [CASP8](../genes/CASP8.md) were silenced in some tumors and (mutually exclusively) mutated in others. Top silenced novel hits included [LXN](../genes/LXN.md) (latexin, 27%), [PARP6](../genes/PARP6.md) (26%), [SPATC1L](../genes/SPATC1L.md) (19%), [NAPRT](../genes/NAPRT.md) (13%). No promoter hypermethylation observed for [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md), [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), [NF2](../genes/NF2.md), or [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **Pathway burden.** p53/cell-cycle pathway inactivated in 89% of tumors ([TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation 48%, [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) amplification 6%, [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) overexpression 19%); [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations enriched in genome-doubled tumors (p < 10⁻⁷). DNA repair: [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) 14%, [ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md) 9% (all non-silent [ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md) missense, frequently in/around the helicase domain), [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md) deletion 2%. Oncogene hotspots: [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) S249C/Y373C (more frequent in T2 [21%] than T3/T4 [10%], p = 0.003; better survival, p = 0.04), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) 22% (helical E542/E545 dominant, n=54; kinase M1043/H1047 n=10), [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) S310 in extracellular domain (24/57 = 42% of [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) mutations, also APOBEC-attributable) [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **Chromatin-modifier mutations are pervasive and predominantly inactivating.** 10 of 39 SMGs with mutation frequency > 5% were chromatin-modifying or chromatin-regulatory: histone demethylase [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md); methyltransferases [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md); acetylases [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md), [EP300](../genes/EP300.md), [KANSL1](../genes/KANSL1.md); SWI/SNF member [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md); Polycomb [ASXL1](../genes/ASXL1.md), [ASXL2](../genes/ASXL2.md). 50% frame-shift/nonsense vs 26% in other SMGs (p = 10⁻³⁰). [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md), and [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md) are also targets of focal deletion (4.2%, 14.2%, 4.9% respectively) [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **lncRNA / miRNA expression subtypes refine the mRNA taxonomy.** Four lncRNA consensus clusters and four miRNA consensus clusters were associated with purity, EMT score, CIS gene-set scores, and 5-year survival (lncRNA p = 0.015; miRNA p = 1.7×10⁻³). lncRNA cluster 3 (best-survival luminal-papillary subset) was depleted in [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations and enriched in [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) mutations/fusions; high cancer-associated lncRNAs included [MALAT1](../genes/MALAT1.md), [NEAT1](../genes/NEAT1.md), [UCA1](../genes/UCA1.md), DANCR, GAS5, NORAD (LINC00657). miRNA subtype 3 had the best survival (low EMT, high miR-200) [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **Regulon analysis (VIPER-style on 23 candidate regulators).** Independently confirmed in the Sjödahl et al. 308-sample cohort. Luminal-papillary cases activated 11 regulons consistent with [GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md), [FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md), [PPARG](../genes/PPARG.md) as luminal drivers; basal/squamous cases activated 12 regulons with [TP63](../genes/TP63.md) and [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) as discriminators between miRNA-defined sub-clusters [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **Microbe / virus integration.** RNA-seq + WES + WGS evidence: HPV (n=11, 4 with genomic integration with breakpoints near [BCL2L1](../genes/BCL2L1.md), SLC2A1-AS1, DEC1, SEC16A, CCDC68); HHV4 (n=6, no integration); HHV5 (n=6, no integration); Polyomavirus (n=1, BK integration breakpoints near FIGN and LIMA1). Viral infection contributes to a small percentage of urothelial carcinomas [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **RPPA proteomic clusters.** Five clusters with distinct outcomes (p = 0.019). Cluster 1 (epithelial/papillary) best survival; clusters 1 & 2 had elevated HER2 ([ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md)) suggesting candidates for anti-HER2 (trastuzumab/T-DM1); cluster 3 (proliferative/low signaling) high [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md), candidate for [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) inhibitors; clusters 4 & 5 EMT-high, worst outcomes [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **Multivariate survival model (LASSO Cox).** Variables retained at |β| > 0.1 included AJCC stages III/IV, mRNA neuronal and luminal subtypes, low-mutation MSig2, miRNA subtype 4, and [KLF4](../genes/KLF4.md) regulon activity (all worse survival); luminal-infiltrated, age, [GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md) regulon activity, and MSig4 retained smaller coefficients. Tertile-thresholded score stratified the cohort into three risk groups (p < 0.001) [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).

## Genes & alterations

- **[FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)** — Hotspot S249C/Y373C mutations dominate; enriched in luminal-papillary subtype (42/57; p < 10⁻⁹) and in lower-stage T2 tumors (21% vs 10% T3/T4, p = 0.003); associated with better survival (p = 0.04). Also amplified (5/5 in luminal-papillary) and engaged in recurrent **[FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)–[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md) fusions** (n=10). Identified by the authors as the dominant driver in 44% of luminal-papillary tumors and a candidate target for pan-FGFR tyrosine kinase inhibitors [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md)** — Fusion partner of [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) in the recurrent intra-chromosomal *FGFR3-TACC3* fusion (n=10), an in-frame activating event particularly enriched in the luminal-papillary subtype [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)** — Mutated in 48%; enriched in basal-squamous (p = 5×10⁻³) and neuronal subtypes; co-occurs with [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) and [E2F3](../genes/E2F3.md) amplification, mutually exclusive with [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) amplification and with [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) loss; enriched in genome-doubled tumors (p < 10⁻⁷). 10/20 neuronal tumors carry [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)+[RB1](../genes/RB1.md) co-mutation or [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)+[E2F3](../genes/E2F3.md) [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[RB1](../genes/RB1.md)** — Mostly inactivating mutations (17%), associated with reduced mRNA. Hallmark co-event with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) loss in the neuronal subtype [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md)** — Missense mutations in 9% (n=40), preferentially in/around the helicase domain (suggested dominant-negative effect). Defines the MSig4 mutational-signature cluster (24/39 ERCC2-mutant; p = 10⁻¹³); ERCC2-signature mutations are highest in smokers carrying ERCC2 mutations (p = 6.9×10⁻¹¹) [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[ATM](../genes/ATM.md)** — Mutated in 14% (n=57); now established as a recurrently mutated DNA-repair gene in MIBC [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md)** — 12% mutated (newly significant SMG); recurrent extracellular-domain S310 mutations (24/57 = 42% of ERBB2 mutations) attributable to APOBEC mutagenesis. Also recurrently amplified. RPPA HER2-high clusters 1 and 2 are candidates for [trastuzumab](../drugs/trastuzumab.md) or [ado-trastuzumab-emtansine](../drugs/ado-trastuzumab-emtansine.md) [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)** — Mutated in 22% (n=100); helical-domain E542/E545 (n=54) more common than kinase M1043/H1047 (n=10); APOBEC-attributable [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)** — Recurrently amplified focal SCNA in MIBC [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)** — 7% mutated, 22% homozygous deletion (most common focal deletion); mutually exclusive with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), and [E2F3](../genes/E2F3.md) alterations; co-occurs with [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) (27 tumors, 7%) — possibly MIBCs that progressed from non-invasive papillary precursors [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[CDKN1A](../genes/CDKN1A.md)** — 11% mutations, predominantly inactivating [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md)** — Amplified (CN > 4) in 6% and overexpressed (>2× median) in 19%; mutually exclusive with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[E2F3](../genes/E2F3.md)** — Recurrently amplified; mutually exclusive with [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) loss and [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) mutation; co-occurs with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) loss in the neuronal subtype [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[PPARG](../genes/PPARG.md)** — Six recurrent fusions (4× *TSEN2-PPARG*, 2× *MKRN2-PPARG*) with overexpression (p = 6×10⁻³); 4/6 retained both DNA- and ligand-binding domains. Additional cell-line *CASC15-PPARG* and *NR2C2-PPARG* fusions identified. Implicated as a luminal driver alongside [GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md) and [FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md) [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md)** — Most frequently mutated newly recognized SMG (18%) [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md)** — Newly recognized SMG, mutated in 11% [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md)** — Histone methyltransferase chromatin-modifier SMG, predominantly inactivating mutations [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md)** — Histone demethylase SMG; recurrent inactivating mutations + 4.9% deletions; loss is implicated in dependence on [BRD4](../genes/BRD4.md)–[EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) signaling and sensitivity to [JQ1](../drugs/jq1.md) and [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) inhibition (Ler et al. 2017; Wu et al. 2016, cited) [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md)** — 12% mutated (newly recognized SMG), 14.2% deleted; predominantly inactivating [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[EP300](../genes/EP300.md)** — Histone acetylase chromatin-modifier SMG; predominantly inactivating mutations [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[KANSL1](../genes/KANSL1.md)** — Histone acetylase SMG [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md)** — SWI/SNF chromatin remodeler; recurrent inactivating mutations + 4.2% deletions [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[ASXL1](../genes/ASXL1.md), [ASXL2](../genes/ASXL2.md)** — Polycomb-group chromatin-modifier SMGs with predominantly inactivating mutations [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md)** — Recurrent SMG; one of four MutCN cluster definers (alongside [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) and [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md)) [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md)** — 12% mutated newly recognized SMG; also subject to epigenetic silencing in mutually exclusive subset of tumors [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[SPTAN1](../genes/SPTAN1.md)** — 12% mutated newly recognized SMG [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[CASP8](../genes/CASP8.md)** — Mutated in some tumors, epigenetically silenced (mutually exclusively) in others [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[CDH1](../genes/CDH1.md)** — Nonsense mutations in 3 of 4 plasmacytoid-histology tumors, consistent with prior plasmacytoid-variant findings [PMID:26901067](../papers/26901067.md) [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[POLE](../genes/POLE.md)** — P286R in a single ultra-mutated tumor (>4000 SNVs), defining the [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) mutational-signature subset [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md)** — Recurrent focal deletions (n=10, 2%) at 14q24.1; new finding not seen in the original 131-tumor cohort [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[APOBEC3A](../genes/APOBEC3A.md), [APOBEC3B](../genes/APOBEC3B.md)** — Expression correlates with APOBEC-signature mutation load; the dominant mutagenic source in MIBC accounting for 67% of all SNVs [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md)** — Recurrently amplified; differentially expressed across mRNA subtypes; key luminal-driver transcription factor by regulon analysis; [GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md) regulon activity associated with better survival in the multivariate model [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md)** — Strongly expressed in luminal subtypes; identified as a luminal driver by regulon analysis [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md)** — Recurrently amplified focal SCNA; high protein expression in basal-squamous subtype and RPPA cluster 3 (proliferative/low-signaling), proposed candidates for EGFR inhibitors [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[CD274](../genes/CD274.md) (PD-L1) and [PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md) (PD-1)** — Strongly expressed in luminal-infiltrated subtype, the proposed checkpoint-immunotherapy-responsive subset; expression also high in basal-squamous [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[CTLA4](../genes/CTLA4.md)** — Elevated in luminal-infiltrated and basal-squamous subtypes [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[TP63](../genes/TP63.md)** — Discriminating regulon between miRNA clusters within the basal/squamous mRNA subtype (activated in miR-4, repressed in miR-2) [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[KLF4](../genes/KLF4.md)** — Regulon activity retained in the LASSO Cox model, associated with worse survival [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[BRD4](../genes/BRD4.md), [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md)** — Identified by cited preclinical work (Ler et al. 2017; Wu et al. 2016) as a chromatin-modification growth pathway whose inhibition (e.g. [JQ1](../drugs/jq1.md), EZH2 inhibitors) is therapeutically tractable in [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md)-loss bladder cancer [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[NECTIN4](../genes/NECTIN4.md) (PVRL4)** — Recurrent focal SCNA target; relevant to [enfortumab-vedotin](../drugs/enfortumab-vedotin.md) biology though the paper itself does not test ADCs [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **Epigenetically silenced novel hits — [LXN](../genes/LXN.md), [PARP6](../genes/PARP6.md), [NAPRT](../genes/NAPRT.md), [SPATC1L](../genes/SPATC1L.md)** — silenced in 27%, 26%, 13%, 19% of tumors respectively; candidate functional contributors and potential therapeutic-vulnerability biomarkers [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **lncRNAs [MALAT1](../genes/MALAT1.md), [NEAT1](../genes/NEAT1.md), [UCA1](../genes/UCA1.md)** — Among the cancer-associated lncRNAs differentially abundant across the bladder cancer subtypes; high in lncRNA cluster 3 (best-survival luminal-papillary subset) [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **Uroplakins [UPK1A](../genes/UPK1A.md), [UPK2](../genes/UPK2.md)** and **umbrella-cell markers [KRT20](../genes/KRT20.md), [SNX31](../genes/SNX31.md)** — highest expression in the new luminal subtype, suggesting umbrella-cell-like differentiation [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **Basal/stem markers [CD44](../genes/CD44.md), [KRT5](../genes/KRT5.md), [KRT6A](../genes/KRT6A.md), [KRT14](../genes/KRT14.md)** — defining markers of the basal-squamous subtype [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).

## Clinical implications

The authors propose a five-subtype framework for stratifying treatment in MIBC, explicitly hypothesis-generating and not yet ready for clinical decision-making (Figure 7) [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md):

- **Luminal-papillary (35%)** — Characterized by [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) mutations / *FGFR3-TACC3* fusions / [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) amplification, papillary histology, active [SHH](../genes/SHH.md) signaling, low CIS scores. Low risk of progression; preliminary data suggests **low likelihood of response to cisplatin-based neoadjuvant chemotherapy** ([cisplatin](../drugs/cisplatin.md), Seiler et al. 2017). Pan-FGFR tyrosine kinase inhibitors (e.g. [erdafitinib](../drugs/erdafitinib.md), referenced via Karkera et al. 2017 / Nogova et al. 2017) are proposed as a rational targeted strategy [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **Luminal-infiltrated (19%)** — High EMT/myofibroblast markers, high miR-200, intermediate [CD274](../genes/CD274.md)/[CTLA4](../genes/CTLA4.md). Corresponds to TCGA Cluster-II reported to respond to anti-PD-L1 [atezolizumab](../drugs/atezolizumab.md) (Rosenberg et al. 2016). May be **resistant to cisplatin-based chemotherapy**; trials should validate this subtype as a negative predictive biomarker for chemo response and a positive biomarker for checkpoint blockade [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **Luminal (6%)** — Optimal therapy not defined; future trials may compare neoadjuvant chemotherapy vs mutation-targeted therapy [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **Basal-squamous (35%)** — Squamous differentiation, basal keratins, high [CD274](../genes/CD274.md) and [CTLA4](../genes/CTLA4.md), strong immune infiltrate. Both [cisplatin](../drugs/cisplatin.md)-based neoadjuvant chemotherapy and immune checkpoint therapy (Sharma et al. 2016) are appropriate; head-to-head trials warranted [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **Neuronal (5%)** — Neuroendocrine/neuronal expression program with high cell-cycle signature, often without the histopathologic features of NE tumors. **Worst survival**; identification depends on mRNA-seq or IHC. **[Etoposide](../drugs/etoposide.md)–[cisplatin](../drugs/cisplatin.md) regimens (as for NE tumors elsewhere) are proposed** for neoadjuvant or metastatic settings, pending prospective testing [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).

Additional therapeutic considerations explicitly raised by the authors:

- **MSig1 (high APOBEC, high mutation burden, neoantigen-rich) cancers should be recognized in checkpoint-inhibitor trials as having a much better baseline prognosis** (75% 5-year survival), to avoid attributing host-immune-mediated benefit to therapy [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **p53/Rb-pathway-altered tumors ([CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) loss, retained Rb)** are being targeted with [palbociclib](../drugs/palbociclib.md) (PD-0332991) in NCT02334527 [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **HDAC inhibition with [mocetinostat](../drugs/mocetinostat.md)** has completed Phase 2 accrual in metastatic urothelial carcinoma (NCT02236195); chromatin-modifier mutations are pervasive enough to motivate this class [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **[BRD4](../genes/BRD4.md)–EZH2 axis in [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md)-loss bladder cancer** — preclinical benefit of BET inhibitor [JQ1](../drugs/jq1.md) and EZH2 inhibition (Ler et al. 2017; Wu et al. 2016) suggests a chromatin-modifier-rationale combination strategy [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **HER2-high RPPA clusters 1 and 2** are candidates for [trastuzumab](../drugs/trastuzumab.md) or [ado-trastuzumab-emtansine](../drugs/ado-trastuzumab-emtansine.md) (T-DM1) [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **EGFR-high RPPA cluster 3** is a candidate for EGFR inhibitors [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Limited follow-up.** Median follow-up of 20.9 months for living patients limits long-term survival inference, though event rate (182 deaths) was high enough to be informative for the analyses presented [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **Subtype-stratified treatment recommendations are hypothesis-generating only.** The authors explicitly state these are "not ready to be used for clinical decision making"; subsequent therapy was not included in the integrated analysis [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **APOBEC paradox unexplained mechanistically.** The hypothesis that high APOBEC-driven mutation burden generates a host neo-antigen response that improves outcome is plausible but requires testing in independent cohorts and direct immunological assays [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **Origin and regulation of APOBEC expression in normal bladder is unknown** — preventive strategies would require this knowledge [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **Functional significance of many recurrent fusions (e.g. *ITGB6-LOC100505984*, n=9) is uncertain** [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **The neuronal subtype is defined by expression, not histology**, in 17 of 20 cases; reproducible clinical-grade detection (mRNA assay or IHC panel) needs validation before therapy stratification [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **Many of the 158 epigenetically silenced genes** may reflect background epigenetic noise rather than functional silencing; only a few ([CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md), [CASP8](../genes/CASP8.md)) were validated against the mutually-exclusive-with-mutation criterion [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **No validation cohort for the LASSO Cox multivariate risk model** — internal tertile-stratified survival separation (p < 0.001) is not equivalent to external validation [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **Cross-cohort generalizability.** Regulon analysis was confirmed in the Sjödahl 308-sample cohort, but mRNA subtype prevalences and survival associations should be re-tested in non-TCGA datasets — particularly in non-Western and cisplatin-treated populations [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
- **The 12 patients receiving neoadjuvant chemotherapy after tumor acquisition** are too few to test the proposed subtype-by-treatment interactions internally [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md).
