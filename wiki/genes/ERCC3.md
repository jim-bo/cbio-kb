# ERCC3

## Overview

ERCC3 (XPB) encodes a subunit of the TFIIH transcription/nucleotide excision repair (NER) complex. Germline loss-of-function variants cause xeroderma pigmentosum group B (XP-B) and Cockayne syndrome. Pathogenic germline variants in ERCC3 are cancer-predisposition mutations identified by broader panel sequencing.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Germline pathogenic variant ERCC3 p.Arg452* identified in 1 of 42 retinoblastoma patients tested by MSK-IMPACT germline sequencing; classified as a cancer-predisposition mutation [PMID:33466343](../papers/33466343.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **Retinoblastoma ([RBL](../cancer_types/RBL.md)):** Germline ERCC3 p.Arg452* identified in the germline of a retinoblastoma patient; the carrier presented with intraocular disease only [PMID:33466343](../papers/33466343.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Carrier showed intraocular retinoblastoma; co-occurrence with somatic [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) alterations not separately reported for this individual [PMID:33466343](../papers/33466343.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- ERCC3 encodes a NER component; germline defects in NER pathway genes are associated with hypersensitivity to DNA-damaging agents, though clinical implications for this specific retinoblastoma patient were not elaborated [PMID:33466343](../papers/33466343.md).

## Open questions

- Non-RB1 germline cancer-predisposition variants (including ERCC3) in retinoblastoma patients have unknown influence on tumorigenesis, clinical course, or second-cancer risk; all five carriers in the retinoblastoma cohort showed intraocular disease only, leaving open whether these variants are incidental or causally contributing [PMID:33466343](../papers/33466343.md).
