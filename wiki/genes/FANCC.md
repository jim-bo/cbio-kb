# FANCC

## Overview

FANCC (Fanconi Anemia Complementation Group C) is a component of the Fanconi anemia DNA repair pathway involved in resolving DNA interstrand crosslinks. FANCC mutations are associated with improved immunotherapy survival in metastatic urothelial carcinoma.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- FANCC mutations associated with improved ICI overall survival in metastatic urothelial carcinoma when combined with ATM/RB1 alterations (p = 0.045) [PMID:36333289](../papers/36333289.md)
- Passenger amplification detected in cSCC (cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma) by GISTIC copy-number analysis [PMID:25589618](../papers/25589618.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Urothelial carcinoma (UC): FANCC mutations part of DDR gene set associated with ICI benefit [PMID:36333289](../papers/36333289.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- FANCC mutations co-occur with [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) and [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) mutations as a biomarker cluster for ICI survival benefit in metastatic UC [PMID:36333289](../papers/36333289.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- ATM/FANCC/RB1 mutation cluster associated with improved ICI overall survival in metastatic UC [PMID:36333289](../papers/36333289.md)

## Open questions

- Whether FANCC mutation is independently predictive of ICI benefit or acts only in combination with ATM/RB1 alterations.
