# pyrotinib

## Overview

Pyrotinib (also known as SHR1258) is an irreversible pan-HER (ERBB1/EGFR, ERBB2/HER2, ERBB4/HER4) tyrosine kinase inhibitor developed by Hengrui Medicine (China). It covalently binds the ATP pocket of HER family receptors, blocking downstream RAS-MAPK and PI3K-AKT signaling. Pyrotinib is approved in China for HER2-positive metastatic breast cancer and is under investigation in ERBB2-mutant solid tumors including triple-negative breast cancer (LAR subtype with somatic [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) mutations). It is not FDA- or EMA-approved as of 2026.

## Evidence in the corpus

- In the FUTURE umbrella trial (NCT03805399, refractory metastatic TNBC), pyrotinib combined with [capecitabine](../drugs/capecitabine.md) was used in arm A for LAR-subtype patients with somatic ERBB2 mutations; 2/2 evaluable patients achieved partial response after 2 cycles, though the arm was too small for formal efficacy estimation [PMID:32719455](../papers/32719455.md).

## Resistance mechanisms

- ERBB2 amplification status and concurrent pathway alterations ([PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md)) may modulate response; small arm A sample size in FUTURE trial precluded resistance characterization.
