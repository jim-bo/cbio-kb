**PMID:** 32437664 · **DOI:** 10.1016/S1470-2045(20)30169-8 · **Journal:** Lancet Oncology (2020)

## TL;DR

Janjigian et al. ran a single-arm phase 2 trial (NCT02954536) at Memorial Sloan Kettering testing first-line [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md) added to [trastuzumab](../drugs/trastuzumab.md) plus fluoropyrimidine/platinum chemotherapy in 37 patients with HER2-positive metastatic esophagogastric ([EGC](../cancer_types/EGC.md)) cancer. The study met its primary endpoint with 26 of 37 patients (70%, 95% CI 54–83%) progression-free at 6 months; the overall response rate was 91% and median overall survival was 27.2 months — numerically superior to the 47% ORR and ~16 month median [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) historically reported for [trastuzumab](../drugs/trastuzumab.md) plus chemotherapy alone. Integrated tumor and ctDNA sequencing identified variant allele frequency–adjusted [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) amplification in plasma as a predictor of longer PFS, while [RTK-RAS](../themes/co-mutation.md) pathway co-alterations predicted shorter PFS. These results provided the rationale for the randomized phase 3 KEYNOTE-811 trial (NCT03615326).

## Cohort & data

- **Trial:** Single-center, open-label, non-randomized, single-arm phase 2 study (NCT02954536) at [MSK](../datasets/msk_impact_2017.md).
- **N = 37 patients** enrolled between Nov 11, 2016 and Jan 23, 2019; 41 considered, 4 excluded.
- **Cancer types:** HER2-positive metastatic esophagogastric adenocarcinoma — esophageal ([ESCA](../cancer_types/ESCA.md)) in 14 (38%), gastroesophageal junction ([GEJ](../cancer_types/GEJ.md)) in 12 (32%), and gastric ([STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md)) in 11 (30%). HER2 positivity defined as IHC 3+ or HER2:CEP17 FISH ratio ≥ 2.0.
- **Demographics:** Median age 60 (range 21–84); 78% male; 86% White; ECOG 0–1.
- **Dataset:** [egc_trap_msk_2020](../datasets/egc_trap_msk_2020.md) — clinical and genomic data released on cBioPortal (https://cbioportal.mskcc.org/study/summary?id=egc_trap_msk_2019).
- **Assays:** Tumor + matched-normal targeted sequencing with [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) (468-gene panel) in 32 patients; [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) in 31 (via the TEMPO pipeline; BWA-mem + Strelka2/Mutect2; [FACETS](../methods/facets.md) for copy number; [MSIsensor](../methods/msisensor.md) for MSI; [mutational signatures](../methods/mutational-signatures.md) decomposed for samples with ≥ 5 SNVs). Plasma ctDNA analyzed by [Guardant360](../methods/guardant360.md) in 33 patients. HER2 and PD-L1 measured by [IHC](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md); HER2 amplification by [FISH](../methods/fish.md).
- **Funding:** Investigator-initiated, funded by Merck.

## Key findings

- **Primary endpoint met:** 26 of 37 (70%, 95% CI 54–83%) patients were progression-free at 6 months, exceeding the pre-specified decision rule (≥ 26/37) ([PMID:32437664](../papers/32437664.md)).
- **Response rates:** Overall response rate 91% (32/35 patients with measurable disease, 95% CI 78–97%), including 6 complete responses (17%), 26 partial responses (74%), and 3 stable disease (8%). Disease control rate was 100%.
- **Survival:** Median PFS 13.0 months (95% CI 8.6–NA); Kaplan-Meier 6-month PFS [estimate](../methods/estimate.md) 75% (95% CI 63–91%); median OS 27.2 months (95% CI 18.8–NA); 12-month OS rate 80% (95% CI 68–95%). Median duration of response 9.4 months (95% CI 4.2–NA).
- **Tissue [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) amplification or focal gain** detected in 21 of 32 sequenced tumors (66%). All 20 patients with tumor [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) amplification by NGS had IHC 3+; only 6 of 12 patients without NGS amplification had IHC 3+.
- **Plasma ERBB2 amplification** detected in 18 of 33 (54%) ctDNA-sequenced patients. Tissue vs. plasma concordance for ERBB2 amplification was 27/29 (93%); 16/18 (89%) tissue-amplified tumors had concordant plasma amplification.
- **Plasma ERBB2 status was prognostic:** Patients with tumor-matched ctDNA detectable at baseline and VAF-adjusted ERBB2 amplification (n = 14) had median PFS 16.4 months (95% CI 13.0–NA) versus 6.2 months (n = 12, 95% CI 5.9–NA) for those without; p = 0.013. Tissue-NGS ERBB2 amplification trended in the same direction but was not significant (14.6 vs. 7.8 months; p = 0.12).
- **ctDNA dynamics:** 13 of 16 patients with tumor-matched baseline ctDNA alterations showed a decline in ctDNA after the first dose of [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md) + trastuzumab (induction, prior to chemotherapy); 10 of these 13 were progression-free at 6 months. 9 of 10 remaining-on-study patients achieved durable ctDNA clearance.
- **Induction-only activity:** Of 25 patients who received an induction cycle of pembrolizumab + trastuzumab alone, 2 achieved partial responses and 12 (48%) showed some target-lesion reduction prior to chemotherapy initiation.
- **PD-L1 status (CPS) did not predict outcome:** median PFS 10.3 vs. 14.6 months for PD-L1-positive vs. -negative; p = 0.56.
- **No MSI-H, no EBV-positive tumors** in the cohort. Median tumor mutation burden was 3.3 mut/Mb (range 0.3–7.8), typical for mEG cancer; TMB did not associate with PFS.
- **Pattern of progression:** Non-target escape lesions were the only site of progressive disease in 12 of 22 (54%) patients who progressed.

## Genes & alterations

- **[ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) (HER2) amplification / focal gain** — the primary genomic biomarker. Detected in 21/32 (66%) tumors by targeted/whole-exome sequencing and 18/33 (54%) of plasma ctDNA samples. VAF-adjusted ctDNA amplification (using a normalization derived from the HERACLES trial across 1,630 EG patients) was significantly associated with longer PFS (16.4 vs. 6.2 months; p = 0.013). All 6 patients with HER2 IHC 2+ (equivocal) + FISH-positive amplification were negative for ERBB2 amplification by tissue NGS, attributed to tumor heterogeneity and/or low amplification.
- **[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) / RTK-RAS pathway co-alterations** — patients with tumors harboring co-occurring RTK-RAS pathway alterations had significantly shorter PFS (median 5.9 vs. 14.6 months; p = 0.011), consistent with prior reports that RTK/KRAS and PI3K activation mediates trastuzumab resistance.

## Clinical implications

- **Biomarker:** Plasma-based ctDNA ERBB2 amplification (VAF-adjusted) outperformed tissue NGS as a predictive marker of PFS in this cohort; ctDNA should be explored prospectively as a predictive biomarker. Early on-treatment ctDNA decline after a single pembrolizumab + trastuzumab dose tracked with progression-free status at 6 months.
- **Treatment:** Adding pembrolizumab to trastuzumab + fluoropyrimidine + platinum produced unprecedented 91% ORR and 27.2-month median OS — numerically far exceeding the ToGA-trial benchmark of 47% ORR and 13.8-month median OS for trastuzumab + chemotherapy alone — and motivated the randomized phase 3 KEYNOTE-811 trial (NCT03615326) testing pembrolizumab vs. placebo on top of trastuzumab + chemotherapy. Benefit was observed irrespective of PD-L1 status.
- **Resistance:** RTK-RAS co-alterations identify a HER2-positive subset unlikely to benefit; tumor heterogeneity and selection of ERBB2-non-amplified clones likely drive acquired resistance, with non-target escape lesions accounting for first progression in 54% of relapsed patients.
- **Safety:** Treatment-related adverse events occurred in 36/37 (97%) patients; the most common was neuropathy (97%). Grade 3/4 TRAEs occurred in 21 (67%). 2 patients discontinued treatment for TRAEs and 4 discontinued pembrolizumab for immune-related AEs (interstitial nephritis 3, transaminitis 2, colitis 1). Toxicity was comparable to trastuzumab + chemotherapy or pembrolizumab + chemotherapy combinations reported previously.

## Limitations & open questions

- Single-arm, non-randomized phase 2 design with modest n = 37; the apparent superiority over historical ToGA controls cannot be definitively attributed to pembrolizumab without the randomized KEYNOTE-811 readout.
- Central HER2 confirmation was not required pre-enrollment; 6 of 15 patients with outside HER2 testing could not be centrally confirmed (3 had no tissue, 3 discordant local/central IHC), and none of these 6 had ERBB2 amplification by tissue/plasma NGS. Variability between local and central HER2 testing has been reported in ~23% of mEG patients and historically associates with reduced trastuzumab benefit (median OS 30.7 vs. 7.9 months in the VARIANZ study).
- Baseline tissue for biomarker analysis was insufficient in several patients; PD-L1 CPS was available in only ~70%.
- Mechanism of synergy (immune engagement vs. enhanced trastuzumab/chemotherapy effect) was not established and requires correlative on-treatment sampling.
- No MSI-H or EBV-positive tumors were enrolled, so it is unknown how those two PD-1-responsive subsets contribute to combination benefit.
- The mechanistic basis of acquired resistance — heterogeneity, ERBB2-non-amplified clone selection, or co-occurring resistance mutations — was not directly characterized via paired pre/post-progression sampling in this report.
