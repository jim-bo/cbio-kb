# ICOS

## Overview

ICOS (Inducible T-Cell Co-Stimulator) encodes a co-stimulatory receptor on activated T cells that is structurally related to [CD28](../genes/CD28.md) and CTLA-4. In the cancer immunotherapy context, ICOS is upregulated on-therapy in response to anti-PD-1 treatment in melanoma and has been nominated as a candidate target for combination immunotherapy strategies.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- **Upregulated on-therapy** following [nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md) treatment in advanced [melanoma](../cancer_types/SKCM.md); upregulation observed in all patients (not restricted to responders). Nominated as a candidate combination immunotherapy target. [PMID:29033130](../papers/29033130.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[Skin Cutaneous Melanoma (SKCM)](../cancer_types/SKCM.md):** ICOS was among immune checkpoint / co-stimulatory genes upregulated on-therapy in all patients in the CA209-038 trial of [nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md) (n=68 advanced melanoma). [PMID:29033130](../papers/29033130.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-upregulated on-therapy with [LAG3](../genes/LAG3.md), [TNFRSF9](../genes/TNFRSF9.md) (4-1BB) in all patients; additionally [HAVCR2](../genes/HAVCR2.md) (TIM-3), [TNFRSF4](../genes/TNFRSF4.md) (OX40), [TIGIT](../genes/TIGIT.md), and [VSIR](../genes/VSIR.md) (VISTA) were selectively upregulated in responders. [PMID:29033130](../papers/29033130.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Nominated as a candidate target for combination immunotherapy regimens with anti-PD-1 agents, based on on-therapy upregulation in advanced melanoma. [PMID:29033130](../papers/29033130.md)

## Open questions

- ICOS upregulation was observed in all patients (responders and non-responders), limiting its utility as a predictive biomarker in isolation; combination strategies require prospective testing. [PMID:29033130](../papers/29033130.md)
