# LGALS3BP

## Overview

LGALS3BP (Lectin Galactoside-Binding Soluble 3 Binding Protein, also known as Mac-2-BP or 90K) is a secreted glycoprotein that binds galectin-3 and several extracellular matrix components. It is involved in cell adhesion, tumor-cell interactions with the immune system, and has been reported as upregulated in various carcinomas. In PDAC, it is identified as part of the mucin glycoprotein upregulation signature observed in the CPTAC cohort.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Protein enriched in early-stage PDAC tumors among mucin glycoproteins and extracellular matrix-associated proteins upregulated in the CPTAC PDAC proteogenomic cohort (n=140 treatment-naive tumors); noted as enriched even in early-stage disease as part of the glycoprotein/mucin expression signature. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md):** Upregulated glycoprotein enriched in early-stage PDAC; part of the glycoprotein early-detection signature. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Upregulation co-occurs with [MUC1](../genes/MUC1.md), [MUC5AC](../genes/MUC5AC.md), [MUC13](../genes/MUC13.md), and other mucin-type glycoproteins in the PDAC glycoproteomics landscape. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting described in the corpus. LGALS3BP upregulation in early-stage PDAC may support its development as a diagnostic biomarker.

## Open questions

- Whether LGALS3BP elevations represent a PDAC-specific signature or are shared across upper GI malignancies is not addressed in the corpus.
- The specific glycosylation isoforms contributing to LGALS3BP upregulation in PDAC are not delineated.
