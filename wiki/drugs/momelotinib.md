# momelotinib

## Overview

Momelotinib is an oral inhibitor of [JAK1](../genes/JAK1.md), [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md), and ACVR1 (activin receptor-like kinase 2). It is FDA-approved for myelofibrosis (particularly in anemia-associated disease) and has been investigated in [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md), where JAK-pathway dysregulation driven by specific co-mutations may confer context-specific sensitivity.

## Evidence in the corpus

- In the Beat AML program, co-mutation of [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md) with [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md) correlated with ex vivo sensitivity to all four JAK inhibitors on the 122-compound panel, including momelotinib, [ruxolitinib](../drugs/ruxolitinib.md), [tofacitinib](../drugs/tofacitinib.md), and JAK Inhibitor I; [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md) mutation alone or co-mutation with [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md) or [SRSF2](../genes/SRSF2.md) showed no sensitivity, confirming this is a combinatorial signal [PMID:30333627](../papers/30333627.md).

## Resistance mechanisms

- Absence of [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md) co-mutation in BCOR-mutant AML abrogates JAK-inhibitor sensitivity ex vivo [PMID:30333627](../papers/30333627.md).
