# MAP4K4

## Overview

MAP4K4 (Mitogen-Activated Protein Kinase Kinase Kinase Kinase 4), also known as HGK, is a serine/threonine kinase of the germinal-center kinase (GCK) subfamily that activates JNK/SAPK signaling. It plays roles in cytoskeletal reorganization, cell migration, and apoptosis. In cancer, MAP4K4 has been implicated in invasive behavior and as a potential therapeutic target, particularly in breast cancer.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Identified as a subtype-specific phospho-kinase outlier (elevated phosphorylation, FDR < 0.01) in NMF Basal-I breast cancer by Black-Sheep phosphoproteomic analysis in a CPTAC prospective cohort of 122 treatment-naive primary breast tumors. [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) (Basal-I / TNBC)**: MAP4K4 phosphorylation is enriched in the NMF Basal-I subtype alongside [PRKDC](../genes/PRKDC.md) and [SPEG](../genes/SPEG.md), supporting it as a kinase active in triple-negative/basal breast cancer. [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-elevated with [PRKDC](PRKDC.md) and SPEG as phospho-kinase outliers in NMF Basal-I breast cancer. [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Nominated as a candidate kinase vulnerability in NMF Basal-I (TNBC) breast cancer based on elevated phosphorylation; therapeutic implications require functional validation. [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md)

## Open questions

- Whether elevated MAP4K4 phosphorylation in Basal-I BRCA is a driver of tumor aggression or a downstream consequence of upstream oncogenic events remains unclear. [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md)
