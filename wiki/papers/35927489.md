# Molecular map of chronic lymphocytic leukemia and its impact on outcome

**PMID:** 35927489 · **DOI:** 10.1038/s41588-022-01140-w · **Journal:** Nature Genetics (2022)

## TL;DR

Knisbacher et al. assembled the largest integrated CLL cohort to date (1,148 patients: 1,095 CLL + 54 [MBL](../cancer_types/MBL.md)) and combined WES/WGS (n=1,074), [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) (n=712), and DNA methylation (n=999) to build a "CLL map." They nominate 202 candidate genetic drivers (109 novel), refine molecular profiles of IGHV-mutated (M-CLL) vs IGHV-unmutated (U-CLL) subtypes, discover 8 gene-expression clusters that serve as independent prognostic factors, and integrate genetic, epigenetic, and transcriptomic features into a combined outcome model [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).

## Cohort & data

- 1,148 patients (1,095 CLL, 54 [MBL](../cancer_types/MBL.md)); WES/WGS n=1,074 (984 WES CLL, 177 WGS), [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) n=712 (603 treatment-naive used for clustering), DNA methylation n=999 (450k array n=490, [RRBS](../methods/rrbs.md) n=509).
- Sampling contexts: active surveillance (n=680), post-treatment (n=52), clinical trial enrollment (n=416; 371 treatment-naive, 45 relapsed/refractory) [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).

## Key findings

- 82 putative CLL drivers from recurrent sSNV/indels (q<0.1) across the full cohort, 37 not previously reported as significantly altered in CLL. Six additional drivers (incl. [MAP2K2](../genes/MAP2K2.md), [DIS3](../genes/DIS3.md), [DICER1](../genes/DICER1.md)) found via 3-D clustering with [CLUMPS](../methods/clumps.md) [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- Top known drivers: [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md) 17.5%, [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) 12.3%, [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) 11.2%, [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) 9.1%; IGLV3-21 R110 9.5%; U1 snRNA g.3A>C 3.8%. 59 of 82 drivers mutated in <2% of patients; 24.2% of patients carried at least one novel driver mutation [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- Power analysis: drivers discovered nearly doubled from ~38.8 (n=500) to 74.5 (~n=1,000); also identified 5 novel focal sCNA gains and 30 novel focal losses [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- IGHV-stratified analysis (M-CLL n=512, U-CLL n=459): U-CLL had substantially more drivers than M-CLL (54 vs 25; ratio 2.16, Binomial p=0.0015; treatment-naive-only ratio 2.82, p=5×10^-11). Only 16 drivers were significant in both subtypes, and 10 of those were twice as frequent in U-CLL [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- SV findings (177 WGS): 681 breakpoints in 141 patients (~4.8/patient); [BCL2](../genes/BCL2.md) translocations predominantly in M-CLL (5/88, 5.7%) via aberrant V(D)J; a recurrent 37-Mb chr14 deletion disrupting [ZFP36L1](../genes/ZFP36L1.md) (and [DICER1](../genes/DICER1.md), [TRAF3](../genes/TRAF3.md)) in U-CLL (4/87, 4.6%) via class-switch recombination [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- Mutational signatures across 177 WGS: aging, canonical AID (SBS84) enriched in clustered mutations in U-CLL, non-canonical AID (SBS85) enriched in M-CLL (p=1.6×10^-9), plus SBS18 (reactive oxygen species) [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- Temporal ordering ([PhylogicNDT](../methods/phylogicndt.md)): Trisomy 12 is early; [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)/[NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) intermediate in both subtypes; [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) is early in M-CLL but late in U-CLL (q<0.1); [MYD88](../genes/MYD88.md) early in M-CLL; 20p loss and [FUBP1](../genes/FUBP1.md) potentially initiating in U-CLL [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- Unsupervised clustering of treatment-naive [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) (n=603) identified 8 robust expression clusters that are independent prognostic factors beyond IGHV/epitype [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- Epitype framework (n-CLL, i-CLL, m-CLL) and the epiCMIT mitotic clock extended via [RRBS](../methods/rrbs.md); DNA methylome variation dominated by cell-of-origin and proliferative history [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- Fraction of patients with no identifiable driver dropped from 8.9% in prior work to 3.8%, concentrated in M-CLL (6.6% vs 0.6% in U-CLL; Fisher p=1.04×10^-7) [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).

## Genes & alterations

- **[SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md), [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)** — cardinal known CLL drivers, frequencies 17.5/12.3/11.2/9.1% [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- **[MAP2K2](../genes/MAP2K2.md)** — novel driver via [CLUMPS](../methods/clumps.md); 3 mutations in the kinase domain, activating ERK signaling analogous to [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md) [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- **[DIS3](../genes/DIS3.md)** — novel CLUMPS-nominated driver; 2/4 sites at cancer hotspots D479/D488 in the catalytic domain [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- **[DICER1](../genes/DICER1.md)** — novel driver via CLUMPS; also disrupted by the recurrent chr14 U-CLL deletion [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- **[INO80](../genes/INO80.md)** — novel driver with mutations clustered in the DNA-binding domain [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- **[RFX7](../genes/RFX7.md)** — novel U-CLL driver associated with poor FFS/OS [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- **[NFKB1](../genes/NFKB1.md)** — U-CLL-specific novel driver linked to NFκB signaling and adverse FFS/OS [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- **[RRM1](../genes/RRM1.md)** — U-CLL-specific novel driver; target of [fludarabine](../drugs/fludarabine.md) [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- **[ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md)** — driver enriched in U-CLL, reinforced by 1p36.11 loss (4.4%) [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- **[KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) / [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md)** — epigenetic regulators enriched in M-CLL ([KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) via 7q36.1b loss 2.5%; [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) via SNV), showing pathway convergence [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- **[BCL2](../genes/BCL2.md)** — recurrent Ig translocation partner in M-CLL (5/88 WGS; +9 in WES) [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- **[ZFP36L1](../genes/ZFP36L1.md), [TRAF3](../genes/TRAF3.md)** — disrupted by the recurrent U-CLL chr14 deletion; [ZFP36L1](../genes/ZFP36L1.md) is a [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) negative regulator [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- **[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)** — early acquisition in M-CLL, late in U-CLL [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- **[MYD88](../genes/MYD88.md)** — early in M-CLL [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- **[MSL3](../genes/MSL3.md) / [USP8](../genes/USP8.md)** — predominantly clonal novel drivers in M-CLL and U-CLL respectively [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- **[ZC3H18](../genes/ZC3H18.md)** — novel M-CLL driver associated with short FFS [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- **[ASXL1](../genes/ASXL1.md), [NFKBIE](../genes/NFKBIE.md)** — known lower-frequency drivers reconfirmed as prognostic in U-CLL [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- **IGLV3-21 R110, U1 snRNA g.3A>C** — non-coding / non-standard driver events at 9.5% and 3.8%; IGLV3-21 R110 is a prognostic factor in M-CLL [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- **[MYC](../genes/MYC.md)** — implicated via 8q gain, a rare adverse feature in M-CLL [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md) / 17p** — significantly co-occur; [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation *without* 17p deletion is not associated with adverse outcome in U-CLL, consistent with contemporary [ibrutinib](../drugs/ibrutinib.md)/[venetoclax](../drugs/venetoclax.md) therapy reducing TP53-alone prognostic impact [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).

## Clinical implications

- U-CLL harbors substantially more prognostic genetic events than M-CLL (41 vs 18 linked to FFS/OS), and 18 of those are novel [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- Gene expression clusters are independent prognostic factors beyond IGHV and epitype, motivating integrated genetic + epigenetic + transcriptomic risk stratification [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- In the era of [ibrutinib](../drugs/ibrutinib.md)/[venetoclax](../drugs/venetoclax.md), isolated [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation (without 17p loss) does not confer adverse prognosis in U-CLL, consistent with prior reports the authors cite [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- Novel prognostic markers ([ZC3H18](../genes/ZC3H18.md), 5q32 loss, 15q25.2 loss in M-CLL; [RFX7](../genes/RFX7.md), [NFKB1](../genes/NFKB1.md) in U-CLL) are candidate additions to CLL risk models [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).

## Limitations & open questions

- Many newly nominated drivers are mutated in <2% of patients, so validation in independent cohorts is needed before clinical use [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- SV analysis relied on only 177 WGS; SV landscape of the full cohort is necessarily extrapolated from WES/[IgCaller](../methods/igcaller.md) [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- Treatment heterogeneity across cohort/trial subsets complicates survival inference; authors partially mitigate with treatment-naive subset analyses [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- How expression clusters relate mechanistically to driver genotype and epitype — and whether they are stable over time/treatment — is left open [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- Functional validation of the 109 novel candidate drivers (e.g., [MAP2K2](../genes/MAP2K2.md), [DIS3](../genes/DIS3.md), [INO80](../genes/INO80.md), [RFX7](../genes/RFX7.md)) is deferred to future work [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
