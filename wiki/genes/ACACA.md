# ACACA

## Overview

ACACA (also known as ACC1) encodes acetyl-CoA carboxylase 1, which catalyzes the carboxylation of acetyl-CoA to malonyl-CoA, the rate-limiting step in de novo fatty acid synthesis (DNL). In hepatocellular carcinoma ([HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)), ACACA plays a central role in lipid metabolic reprogramming, and its inhibition is under active investigation as a therapeutic strategy.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- ACC1 inhibitor ND-654 synergizes with [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md) in HCC; liver-specific ACC1 knockout paradoxically promoted DEN-induced HCC by upregulating CD36 and FATP5; Phase IIa trial of ACC1/2 + DGAT2 inhibitor reduced steatosis [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **HCC**: ACACA is part of the de novo lipogenesis axis central to a major subset of HCC; both inhibition and knockout produce context-dependent effects on tumor development [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- ACACA/ACC1 acts downstream of SREBP1 ([SREBF1](../genes/SREBF1.md)) and [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) transcription factors; functionally linked to [FASN](../genes/FASN.md), CD36, and SLC27A5/FATP5 in the DNL/lipid-uptake network [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- ND-654 (ACC1 inhibitor) + sorafenib: synergistic anti-tumor effect in preclinical HCC models [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)
- PF-05221304 (ACC1/2 inhibitor) + DGAT2 inhibitor: Phase IIa trial demonstrated reduction of hepatic steatosis [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Open questions

- Liver-specific ACC1 knockout suppressed DNL but paradoxically promoted DEN-induced HCC via CD36/FATP5 upregulation — the context-dependence of ACACA inhibition in different HCC molecular subtypes requires further investigation [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)
