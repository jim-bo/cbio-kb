# famitinib

## Overview

Famitinib (SHR1020) is an oral multi-target receptor tyrosine kinase inhibitor developed by Hengrui Medicine (China). It inhibits VEGFR2 ([KDR](../genes/KDR.md)), PDGFR-alpha/beta, [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md), c-Kit, and [RET](../genes/RET.md), thereby exerting anti-angiogenic and direct anti-tumor effects. Famitinib is a structural analog of [sunitinib](../drugs/sunitinib.md). It is not FDA- or EMA-approved as of 2026; under investigation in China for multiple solid tumor types including triple-negative breast cancer.

## Evidence in the corpus

- In the FUTURE umbrella trial (NCT03805399, refractory metastatic TNBC), famitinib was used as an alternative to [apatinib](../drugs/apatinib.md) in arm E (BLIS-subtype, BRCA1/2-wildtype, at a dose of 20 mg + VP-16 50 mg after toxicity-driven apatinib dose revision) and as monotherapy in arm F (MES-subtype without PI3K-AKT mutation, ORR 20%; 1/5 evaluable); one BLIS arm-B patient with FGFR1 amplification responded to [anlotinib](../drugs/anlotinib.md) after failing anti-AR + CDK4/6 inhibitor — famitinib was the anti-VEGFR comparator option [PMID:32719455](../papers/32719455.md).

## Resistance mechanisms

- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) loss, [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), and [NOTCH3](../genes/NOTCH3.md) mutations were enriched in PD patients in arm E (apatinib/famitinib arm); high [FOXC1](../genes/FOXC1.md) IHC score trended toward SD/PD [PMID:32719455](../papers/32719455.md).
