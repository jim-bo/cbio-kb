# AGO2

## Overview

AGO2 (Argonaute RISC Catalytic Component 2) encodes the catalytic subunit of the RNA-induced silencing complex (RISC), central to miRNA-mediated gene regulation. In endometrial cancer, AGO2 missense mutations have been observed as private subclonal events restricted to sentinel lymph nodes, representing spatially heterogeneous subclones not detectable in the primary tumor or in baseline cfDNA — a finding with implications for liquid biopsy sensitivity in nodal disease.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Missense mutation restricted to sentinel lymph nodes (private to the nodal compartment) in EC patient CD20 (stage IIIC2 dedifferentiated endometrial carcinoma); mutation was absent from the primary tumor and not detected by MSK-ACCESS in baseline cfDNA, illustrating the limits of liquid biopsy for capturing nodal-private subclones [PMID:36007103](../papers/36007103.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[UCEC](../cancer_types/UCEC.md):** Subclonal AGO2 missense mutation private to sentinel lymph node in 1 of 44 prospectively accrued EC patients profiled by MSK-IMPACT + MSK-ACCESS cfDNA assay [PMID:36007103](../papers/36007103.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- In the same patient (CD20), primary-tumor subclonal mutations in [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), and [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) became clonal in sentinel lymph nodes; AGO2 and [FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md) mutations were exclusive to the nodal compartment — illustrating divergent evolutionary trajectories between primary tumor and metastatic sites [PMID:36007103](../papers/36007103.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No specific therapeutic implication reported; AGO2 mutation was a subclonal, lymph-node-private finding [PMID:36007103](../papers/36007103.md)

## Open questions

- Whether AGO2 mutations contribute to metastatic seeding or lymph node adaptation in endometrial carcinoma, versus being neutral passengers in an already metastatic subclone, is unknown [PMID:36007103](../papers/36007103.md)
