**PMID:** 35982973 · **DOI:** 10.3389/fonc.2022.919118 · **Journal:** Frontiers in Oncology (2022)

## TL;DR

Thind et al. performed whole genome sequencing (WGS) on lymph node metastases and matched blood from 25 patients with regional metastatic cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma ([CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md)) of the head and neck. This is the largest WGS study of metastatic [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md) to date and the first to interrogate non-coding regulatory regions (3′UTRs, lncRNAs, promoters) genome-wide. Beyond confirming [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) as recurrent coding drivers, they identified [C9](../genes/C9.md) (complement component 9) as a novel candidate driver, recurrent 3′UTR alterations in [EVC](../genes/EVC.md), [PPP1R1A](../genes/PPP1R1A.md), and [ABCA4](../genes/ABCA4.md), recurrent mutation of the tumor-suppressor lncRNA [LINC01003](../genes/LINC01003.md) (64% of samples), focal copy-number loss of [PTPRD](../genes/PTPRD.md), and a novel indel-signature profile (ID8/ID9/ID13) distinct from melanoma. The data were deposited in cBioPortal as `cscc_ranson_2022` ([cscc_ranson_2022](../datasets/cscc_ranson_2022.md)).

## Cohort & data

- 25 patients with resectable regional metastatic [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md) of the head and neck (lymph node metastases), recruited 2015–2019 with matched blood DNA.
- Median age 69 years (range 30–87); 24/25 (96%) male; 2/25 immunosuppressed (one on long-term [azathioprine](../drugs/azathioprine.md), one on combination [cyclophosphamide](../drugs/cyclophosphamide.md) and [tacrolimus](../drugs/tacrolimus.md) post solid-organ transplant).
- Nodal stage: predominantly N3b; metastases in neck (n=13) or parotid (n=12). Differentiation: moderately (n=8) or poorly (n=12) differentiated. Extranodal extension in 20/25 (80%).
- 32 samples sequenced; 25 passed QC (>30% tumor cellularity, range 35–95%). Mean tumor coverage 94.56× (range 64–143×); mean blood coverage 41.08×.
- Reference genome GRCh38; alignment with BWA-MEM ([bwa](../methods/bwa.md)); GATK 4.1.2.0 ([gatk](../methods/gatk.md)) HaplotypeCaller (germline) and Mutect2 (somatic) for short variants.
- Cohort deposited in cBioPortal as `cscc_ranson_2022` — see [cscc_ranson_2022](../datasets/cscc_ranson_2022.md).
- Variant call files deposited at the European Genome-Phenome Archive under accession EGAS00001006378.

## Key findings

- **Extreme TMB.** Average tumor mutational burden was 238.7 mutations/Mb (median 166.99, range 32.52–995.66) for SNVs and 2.25 indels/Mb (range 0.63–5.9) at the whole-genome scale — substantially higher than melanoma (~49 mutations/Mb) and much higher than the WES-derived 61.2 mutations/Mb reported by Pickering et al. (the majority of variants fall in non-coding DNA).
- **UV mutational signatures dominate.** Over 80% of SNVs were C>T transitions consistent with UV mutagenesis; COSMIC v3.2 SBS7a and SBS7b dominated, with substantial SBS7c representation. One sample showed SBS32, attributable to [azathioprine](../drugs/azathioprine.md) exposure.
- **Novel indel signatures.** ID8, ID9, and ID13 dominated across all 25 samples. ID13 was previously reported only in melanoma; ID9 — predominant in soft-tissue sarcoma but rare in melanoma (2/104) — was newly identified as a dominant indel signature in CSCC, defining a novel indel pattern for this UV-driven skin cancer (Figure 7).
- **Three-tool consensus driver list.** Filtering genes called significant (p<0.005) by ≥2 of OncodriveFML ([oncodrivefml](../methods/oncodrivefml.md)), MutSigCV ([mutsigcv](../methods/mutsigcv.md)), and dNdScv ([dndscv](../methods/dndscv.md)) yielded 12 coding drivers: [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [C9](../genes/C9.md), C9orf131, [SLC22A6](../genes/SLC22A6.md), [KHDRBS2](../genes/KHDRBS2.md), [COLEC12](../genes/COLEC12.md), [LINGO2](../genes/LINGO2.md), [CDHR5](../genes/CDHR5.md), [ZNF442](../genes/ZNF442.md), [PRLR](../genes/PRLR.md), and [DHRS4](../genes/DHRS4.md). [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), and [C9](../genes/C9.md) were called by all three tools.
- **Non-coding 3′UTR drivers.** OncodriveFML identified significantly altered 3′UTRs (Q-value <0.03) in [EVC](../genes/EVC.md) (48% of samples; Q=0.011), [PPP1R1A](../genes/PPP1R1A.md) (48% of samples; Q=0.022), [ABCA4](../genes/ABCA4.md) (20%), and [LUM](../genes/LUM.md). A unique recurrent [PPP1R1A](../genes/PPP1R1A.md) 3′UTR variant (c.*491C>T; Chr12:54579896 G→A) was observed in 5/25 samples.
- **lncRNA drivers.** Four lncRNAs were significantly biased toward high-impact mutations: [LINC01003](../genes/LINC01003.md) (64% of cohort; Q=0.0158), [LINC01474](../genes/LINC01474.md), RP4-597N16.4, and RP11-61J19.4 (48%).
- **Copy number landscape.** Chromosome arm gains at 7 and 5p and losses at 8p, 18q, and 21q were recurrent across the cohort. [PTPRD](../genes/PTPRD.md) on chr9p was the most frequently deleted gene (24% of samples) and the dominant focus of focal/arm/chromosome-level LOH; [PTPRD](../genes/PTPRD.md) loss and significant [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) mutation co-occurred in 6 samples but [PTPRD](../genes/PTPRD.md) deletion and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) deletion did not co-occur.
- **Rare amplifications.** Recurrent amplifications were uncommon; [CALR](../genes/CALR.md), [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md), and [FGF3](../genes/FGF3.md) were each amplified in only 2/25 samples (8%). [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) and [FGF3](../genes/FGF3.md) co-amplified in 2 cases (CSCC_0132 and CSCC_0134), both with extensive nodal involvement (>50% of lymph nodes harboring tumor). [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) was amplified in only one sample.
- **Structural variation.** Deletions and complex structural variants predominated; despite widespread genomic instability, only two coding–coding gene fusions were detected: [STRN](../genes/STRN.md)–[DLG2](../genes/DLG2.md) (CSCC_0009, the only TP53-wildtype sample) and [HEBP2](../genes/HEBP2.md)–[NTRK2](../genes/NTRK2.md) (CSCC_0011, via unbalanced translocation).
- **The only TP53-wildtype sample (CSCC_0009)** had TMB of 122/Mb (51% of cohort average); the 5 CDKN2A-wildtype samples averaged TMB of 470/Mb (201% of cohort average).
- **Pathway enrichment.** Among 21 candidate altered genes, top enriched BioPlanet terms included [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) network, tumor suppressor ARF, [CTCF](../genes/CTCF.md) pathway, cell cycle G1/S checkpoint, and "TGF-beta regulation of extracellular matrix" — with [LUM](../genes/LUM.md), [CDHR5](../genes/CDHR5.md), [COLEC12](../genes/COLEC12.md), and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) contributing to the latter.
- **cBioPortal cross-cohort comparison.** Using 92 metastatic CSCC and 88 primary CSCC samples already on cBioPortal (drawn from MSK-IMPACT, the Li et al. FFPE cohort, and Pickering et al.), the authors confirmed recurrent mutation of [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [C9](../genes/C9.md), [COLEC12](../genes/COLEC12.md), and [SLC22A6](../genes/SLC22A6.md). [COLEC12](../genes/COLEC12.md) (primary 25%, metastatic 60%) and [SLC22A6](../genes/SLC22A6.md) (primary 16%, metastatic 30%) were enriched in metastatic disease.

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — recurrently mutated; loss-of-function across 24/25 samples (only CSCC_0009 wildtype). Driver by all three tools. Cohort frequency consistent with the high-TMB UV-driven phenotype.
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — recurrently mutated; LOF impacting p16INK4a and ARF, dysregulating cell cycle and apoptotic pathways. Driver by all three tools. Deep deletion observed in 1/25 samples.
- [C9](../genes/C9.md) — novel candidate driver (52% of samples carried SNVs). Encodes complement component 9; identified by all three driver tools. cBioPortal shows [C9](../genes/C9.md) mutated in 31% primary and 10% metastatic CSCC. Mechanistic link proposed via tumor microenvironment and macrophage biology.
- [PTPRD](../genes/PTPRD.md) — most frequently deleted gene (24% of samples) on 9p; tumor suppressor; loss reported to drive [STAT3](../genes/STAT3.md) hyperactivation and resistance to EGFR-targeted therapy in HNSCC.
- [EVC](../genes/EVC.md) — recurrent 3′UTR mutation (48%); regulator of ciliary Hedgehog signaling.
- [PPP1R1A](../genes/PPP1R1A.md) — recurrent 3′UTR mutation (48%); a unique missense variant (c.*491C>T) in 5/25 samples.
- [ABCA4](../genes/ABCA4.md) — recurrent 3′UTR mutation (20%).
- [LUM](../genes/LUM.md) — 3′UTR mutation; keratan sulfate proteoglycan implicated in TGFβ/ECM enrichment.
- [LINC01003](../genes/LINC01003.md) — recurrently mutated tumor-suppressor lncRNA (64% of samples); previously shown to act via miR-33a-5p / [PIM1](../genes/PIM1.md) in multiple myeloma.
- [LINC01474](../genes/LINC01474.md) — recurrent lncRNA mutation.
- [KHDRBS2](../genes/KHDRBS2.md), [COLEC12](../genes/COLEC12.md), [SLC22A6](../genes/SLC22A6.md), [LINGO2](../genes/LINGO2.md), [CDHR5](../genes/CDHR5.md), [ZNF442](../genes/ZNF442.md), [PRLR](../genes/PRLR.md), [DHRS4](../genes/DHRS4.md) — candidate coding drivers called by ≥2 tools; [KHDRBS2](../genes/KHDRBS2.md) altered in 48% of cohort; [COLEC12](../genes/COLEC12.md) and [SLC22A6](../genes/SLC22A6.md) also enriched in metastatic vs primary CSCC on cBioPortal.
- [ZNF730](../genes/ZNF730.md) — OncodriveFML coding driver (Q-value <0.1) but not corroborated by all three tools.
- [CALR](../genes/CALR.md), [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md), [FGF3](../genes/FGF3.md) — focally amplified in 2/25 samples each; [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) and [FGF3](../genes/FGF3.md) co-amplified in CSCC_0132 and CSCC_0134.
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) — amplified in a single sample (CSCC_0012).
- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) — structural amplification in CSCC_0005.
- [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) / [NOTCH2](../genes/NOTCH2.md) — notably absent from significant or recurrent SNVs in this cohort, in contrast to the primary-CSCC NOTCH-prominent landscape reported by Inman et al.
- [GRHL2](../genes/GRHL2.md) — only a single splice variant observed, suggesting a limited role in metastatic disease here.
- Structural variant gene list (LINX/PURPLE): [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) (DEL), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) (DEL), [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) (GAIN), [CRLF2](../genes/CRLF2.md), [PTPRD](../genes/PTPRD.md) (DEL/LOH in multiple samples), [CALR](../genes/CALR.md) (GAIN), [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) (GAIN), [APC](../genes/APC.md) (DEL), [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md) (DEL), [CDKN2C](../genes/CDKN2C.md) (DEL), [PARD6G](../genes/PARD6G.md), [PTPN13](../genes/PTPN13.md) (DEL), [NEGR1](../genes/NEGR1.md) (DEL), [RAF1](../genes/RAF1.md), [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md), [FGF3](../genes/FGF3.md), [MCL1](../genes/MCL1.md), [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md) (DEL).
- Fusions: STRN–DLG2 ([STRN](../genes/STRN.md) exon 1 ENST00000263918 — [DLG2](../genes/DLG2.md) exon 7 ENST00000376104) in CSCC_0009; HEBP2–NTRK2 ([HEBP2](../genes/HEBP2.md) — [NTRK2](../genes/NTRK2.md)) in CSCC_0011 (unbalanced translocation).

## Clinical implications

- **Immune checkpoint blockade.** Recurrent [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) loss-of-function — together with extreme TMB — provides a rationale for the high response rates observed with anti–PD-1 therapy in advanced CSCC (e.g. [cemiplimab](../drugs/cemiplimab.md); Migden et al. 2018, NEJMoa1805131).
- **CDK4/6 inhibition.** [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) loss disables p16INK4a-mediated CDK control; the authors propose CDK4/6 inhibitors such as [palbociclib](../drugs/palbociclib.md) — established in metastatic breast cancer — as a candidate therapeutic strategy for metastatic CSCC.
- **MDM2/MDM4 axis.** The TP53–[MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md)–ARF network (also encoded by [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)) is highlighted as a potential target.
- **TGF-β / ECM axis.** TGFβ-regulation-of-extracellular-matrix was a significantly enriched pathway via [LUM](../genes/LUM.md), [CDHR5](../genes/CDHR5.md), [COLEC12](../genes/COLEC12.md), and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md); TGFβ receptor inhibition with chemotherapy or immunotherapy is proposed as a candidate strategy by analogy to other cancers.
- **miRNA potentiation.** miR-331-5p was predicted to target six driver candidates including [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [C9](../genes/C9.md), suggesting potential as a CSCC-driver–targeting miRNA.
- **Non-coding biomarkers.** Recurrent 3′UTRs in [EVC](../genes/EVC.md), [PPP1R1A](../genes/PPP1R1A.md), [ABCA4](../genes/ABCA4.md) and the lncRNA [LINC01003](../genes/LINC01003.md) are proposed as candidate non-coding biomarkers of metastatic CSCC — and as concrete examples of regions invisible to WES/targeted-panel platforms.
- **Iatrogenic mutagenesis.** SBS32 in one patient is attributable to [azathioprine](../drugs/azathioprine.md) immunosuppression, reinforcing the importance of treatment history in interpreting CSCC mutational landscapes.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Cohort size and sex skew.** Only 25 patients; 24/25 male. Potential sex differences are unaddressed; the authors note that this skew mirrors the disease burden in NSW, Australia but caution it is a limitation.
- **Metastatic disease only.** All samples are nodal metastases; no matched primary tumors. Direct primary-vs-metastasis comparisons rely on external cBioPortal cohorts of differing assay design (WES, targeted NGS) rather than within-patient pairing.
- **5′UTR / promoter regions excluded.** Limitations of current methodology in high-TMB tumors meant 5′UTRs and promoters (other than via prior CCCTC-binding factor analysis) were not analyzed in depth — leaving a sizeable non-coding fraction of the CSCC genome unstudied.
- **Functional validation outstanding.** Novel candidate drivers ([C9](../genes/C9.md), KHDRBS2, COLEC12, SLC22A6, EVC/PPP1R1A 3′UTRs, [LINC01003](../genes/LINC01003.md)) lack experimental validation; biological mechanisms are inferred from cross-cancer literature rather than CSCC-specific functional studies.
- **No matched RNA / methylation.** Effects of UTR and lncRNA variants on gene expression are not measured in this dataset.
- **Comparator panels are heterogeneous.** cBioPortal CSCC cohorts used for cross-validation (Zehir/MSK-IMPACT, Li et al., Pickering et al.) differ in assay (WES vs targeted NGS), tissue preservation (mostly FFPE), and selection — limiting head-to-head frequency comparisons.
