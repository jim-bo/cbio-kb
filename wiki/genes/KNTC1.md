# KNTC1

## Overview

KNTC1 (Kinetochore-Associated Protein 1; also known as ROD) is a component of the RZZ (ROD-Zwilch-ZW10) complex that functions in spindle assembly checkpoint signaling. It is required for proper chromosome congression and mitotic fidelity. Dysregulation of KNTC1 can contribute to chromosomal instability.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- KNTC1 protein levels are down at protein but up at mRNA in TP53-mutant HCCs, identified in a European multi-etiology proteogenomic cohort (n=122). It is classified as a mitotic-spindle regulator exhibiting discordant mRNA/protein regulation in the context of [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)**: KNTC1 is among a group of mitotic-spindle regulators ([CENPF](../genes/CENPF.md), [TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md), KNTC1) showing protein down-regulation despite mRNA up-regulation in TP53-mutant tumors, suggesting post-transcriptional suppression [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- The discordant mRNA/protein pattern is shared with [CENPF](../genes/CENPF.md) and [TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md) in TP53-mutant HCCs [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic implication reported. The discordant regulation in TP53-mutant [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) is noted as a finding requiring further mechanistic characterization [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md).

## Open questions

- The mechanism by which [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation leads to post-transcriptional suppression of KNTC1 and other spindle proteins at the protein level in HCC is unresolved [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md).
