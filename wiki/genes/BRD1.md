# BRD1

## Overview

BRD1 (bromodomain-containing protein 1) is a member of the bromodomain and PHD finger-containing (BRPF) family of chromatin-regulatory proteins. It functions as a scaffolding subunit of the MOZ/MORF histone acetyltransferase complex and binds acetylated histone marks via its bromodomain. In cancer, BRD1 and related BET family members ([BRD3](../genes/BRD3.md), [BRD4](../genes/BRD4.md)) mediate the reading of histone acetylation marks to coordinate transcriptional programs. BRD1 activity has been implicated in histone H2B N-terminal acetylation in glioblastoma.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- In a proteogenomic analysis of 99 treatment-naive GBMs, BRD1, BRD3, and BRD4 were correlated via Lasso regression with H2B N-terminal acetylation sites; their activity marks a classical-like, immune-low [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) subset (nmf3). H2B N-terminal hyperacetylation is negatively correlated with tumor microenvironment enrichment, ferroptosis, mast cells, and ROS pathways, and positively correlated with spliceosome, nuclear-receptor, and SUMOylation pathways. [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md):** BRD1 activity linked to H2B N-terminal acetylation defining a classical-like, immune-low GBM subset; BET inhibitors proposed as therapeutic candidates for this subtype. [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- BRD1 activity (H2B acetylation) co-occurs with BRD3, BRD4, [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md), and [EP300](../genes/EP300.md) activity in the classical-like GBM subtype; H2B hyperacetylation is negatively associated with immune-cell infiltration in the tumor microenvironment. [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Classical-like, immune-low GBMs with high H2B N-terminal acetylation driven by BRD1/BRD3/BRD4 are nominated as candidates for BET inhibitor therapy; CREBBP/EP300 inhibitors are also proposed as co-targeting strategies. No clinical evidence is provided in this corpus. [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Open questions

- The BRD1-H2B acetylation relationship in GBM is inferred from Lasso regression on proteogenomic data and has not been directly validated functionally. Whether BRD1 is essential for this acetylation pattern vs. BRD3/BRD4 requires further study. [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)
