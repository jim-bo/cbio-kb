**PMID:** 35705558 · **DOI:** [10.1038/s41467-022-30496-0](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-022-30496-0) · **Journal:** Nature Communications (2022)

## TL;DR

Gounder et al. report comprehensive genomic profiling of 7,494 soft-tissue and bone sarcomas spanning 44 histologies, profiled by FoundationOne Heme (465-gene DNA panel + 333-gene RNA fusion panel) between 2012 and 2018. Sequencing flagged potential diagnostic refinements/reclassifications in 10.5% of cases; 31.7% of patients harbored at least one OncoKB-defined actionable alteration, 2.6% had kinase gene fusions ([ALK](../genes/ALK.md), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md)–4, [NTRK1](../genes/NTRK1.md)–3, [RET](../genes/RET.md), [ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md)), 3.9% had TMB ≥10 mut/Mb, MSI-H was rare (<0.3%), and 15% had high genome-wide LOH (gLOH ≥19.3%) only partly explained by HRD-gene alterations. A clinically annotated subset of 118 MSK patients demonstrated anecdotal responses to matched targeted and immune therapies. The cohort is registered in cBioPortal as [sarcoma_msk_2022](../datasets/sarcoma_msk_2022.md).

## Cohort & data

- **N = 7,494 patients** with 44 sarcoma histologies; samples profiled from 2012–2018 ([PMID:35705558](../papers/35705558.md)).
- **Tissue composition:** 81.0% soft tissue (n=6,067), 14.7% bone (n=1,105), 4.3% other (n=322).
- **Most common subtypes:** [sarcoma NOS](../cancer_types/SARCNOS.md) 17.2%, [leiomyosarcoma](../cancer_types/LMS.md) 12.7%.
- **Demographics:** median age 53 (range <1–89); 53.4% female; pediatric-adolescent-young-adult (P-AYA, ≤30 years) = 21.8% (1,636/7,494).
- **Categorization:** translocation-associated n=1,724 (23.0%) vs. genomically complex/other n=5,770 (77.0%).
- **Assay:** [FoundationOne Heme](../methods/foundationone.md) targeted panel — hybrid-capture DNA sequencing of 465 genes (median 704× coverage, IQR 515–798×), [select](../methods/select.md) introns of 31 rearrangement genes, plus RNA-seq of 333 commonly rearranged genes. Tumor-only profiling (no matched normal). Reads aligned to hg19 via BWA v0.5.9.
- **Annotation:** Actionability assessed using [OncoKB](../methods/oncokb.md) levels (data cutoff June 8, 2021). Mutational signatures decomposed against COSMIC v2 (30 signatures) and aggregated into APOBEC, aging, [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md), MMR, smoking, UV, [POLE](../genes/POLE.md), and alkylating classes (dominance threshold ≥0.4).
- **Clinical sub-cohort:** 118 adult sarcoma patients at Memorial Sloan Kettering (MSK) with linked outcomes; median age 60 (range 18–89); 60% metastatic; median 4 prior systemic therapies (range 0–12).
- **Dataset:** [sarcoma_msk_2022](../datasets/sarcoma_msk_2022.md).

## Key findings

- **Diagnostic reclassification.** Sequencing prompted potential refinement or reassignment of diagnosis in **10.5% (789/7,494)** of cases, based on presence/absence of pathognomonic alterations or fusions; reclassifications were not re-reviewed by expert sarcoma pathologists ([PMID:35705558](../papers/35705558.md)).
- **Recurrent short-variant / CNV drivers.** [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) 37.1%; [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) 17.2%; [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) 16.7%; [CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md) 12.8%; [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) 10.7%; [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) 10.6%; [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md) 0.7%; [MDM4](../genes/MDM4.md) 0.5%; [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md) 2.6%; [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) 1.0%; [CCND2](../genes/CCND2.md) 1.4%; [CCND3](../genes/CCND3.md) 1.8%.
- **p53 + Rb pathway dominance.** The p53 pathway ([TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md), [MDM4](../genes/MDM4.md)) was altered in 47.8% of sarcomas and the Rb pathway ([RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)/[B](../genes/CDKN2B.md), [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md)/[6](../genes/CDK6.md), [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md)/[2](../genes/CCND2.md)/[3](../genes/CCND3.md), [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md)) in 46.8%; co-occurrence between the two pathways was highly significant (OR=4.2, p<1.3×10⁻¹⁹⁶, FDR-adjusted), while alterations within each pathway were largely mutually exclusive.
- **Complex vs. translocation-associated sarcomas.** p53 pathway 58.2% vs. 12.9%; Rb pathway 56% vs. 15.8%. TMB also higher in complex sarcomas (median 2.4 vs. 1.6 mut/Mb; p<4×10⁻⁶¹).
- **Histology-enriched alterations.** 4q12 amplicon ([KDR](../genes/KDR.md), [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md), [KIT](../genes/KIT.md)) in [osteosarcoma](../cancer_types/OS.md) 10.7%, liposarcoma NOS 8.0%, UPS/MFH 7.3%; 11q13 amplicon ([CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md), [FGF2](../genes/FGF2.md), [FGF3](../genes/FGF3.md), [FGF19](../genes/FGF19.md)) in [UPS](../cancer_types/UPS.md) of bone 4.3%; [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)/[B](../genes/CDKN2B.md) deletion in [MPNST](../cancer_types/MPNST.md) 47.3% and [chordoma](../cancer_types/CHDM.md) 36.8%; [SMARCB1](../genes/SMARCB1.md) in [epithelioid sarcoma](../cancer_types/EPIS.md) 56% and [malignant rhabdoid tumor](../cancer_types/MRT.md) 60%; [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), and AKT E17K in myxoid liposarcoma 36.3%, 17.8%, 3.7%; [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) in [chondrosarcoma](../cancer_types/CHS.md) 22.9%; [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md) in chordoma 15.8%; [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) amplification in embryonal rhabdomyosarcoma 18.4%, clear-cell sarcoma 13.5%, osteosarcoma 15.0%.
- **Kinase fusions actionable in 2.6% (196/7,494).** Recurrent in-frame fusions of [ALK](../genes/ALK.md), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md)–4, [NTRK1](../genes/NTRK1.md)/[2](../genes/NTRK2.md)/[3](../genes/NTRK3.md), [RET](../genes/RET.md), and [ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md). Histology prevalence: [IMT](../cancer_types/IMT.md) 62.1%, [MPNST](../cancer_types/MPNST.md) 4.9%, [UPS](../cancer_types/UPS.md) of bone 4.3%, extraskeletal osteosarcoma 4.0%, UPS 3.6%, sarcoma NOS 3.8%, [LMS](../cancer_types/LMS.md) 1.9%.
- **Novel [TNS1](../genes/TNS1.md)–[ALK](../genes/ALK.md) fusion in [LMS](../cancer_types/LMS.md).** [TNS1](../genes/TNS1.md)–[ALK](../genes/ALK.md) rearrangements (breakpoints in introns 16 and 18; fusions of exons 17–29 / 19–29) detected in 16 cases, primarily uterine and non-uterine leiomyosarcomas — suggesting reclassification of some [LMS](../cancer_types/LMS.md) as kinase-driven.
- **Recurrent fusion-driver gene rearrangements.** [EWSR1](https://www.genenames.org/data/gene-symbol-report/#!/symbol/EWSR1) fusions with [FLI1](../genes/FLI1.md), [WT1](../genes/WT1.md), [NR4A3](../genes/NR4A3.md) and rarer partners; also rearrangements involving [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) 1.5%, [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) 1.4%, [LRP1B](../genes/LRP1B.md) 0.6%, [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) 0.5%, [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md) 0.2%, [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) 0.3%, [NOTCH3](../genes/NOTCH3.md) 0.3%.
- **MMR-D and MSI.** Mismatch-repair deficiency (loss of [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md), [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [MSH3](../genes/MSH3.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md), [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md)) in 2.1% of cases; MSI-H in only 18/6,206 evaluable cases (**0.29%**, most often [uterine endometrial stromal sarcoma](../cancer_types/ESS.md), leiomyosarcoma, sarcoma NOS); median TMB in MSI-H = 25.8 mut/Mb (IQR 19.8–30.1). 14/18 MSI-H cases had corresponding MMR-gene alterations.
- **HRD signal.** Pathogenic HR-pathway alterations ([BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md)/[2](../genes/BRCA2.md), [PALB2](../genes/PALB2.md), [RAD51](https://www.genenames.org/data/gene-symbol-report/#!/symbol/RAD51) and paralogs [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md), [RAD51D](../genes/RAD51D.md), [RAD52](../genes/RAD52.md), [RAD54L](../genes/RAD54L.md)) in **2.5% (184/7,494)**; 72 confirmed biallelic. Biallelic losses most frequent in [uterine LMS](../cancer_types/ULMS.md) 4.8% and chordoma 5.3%.
- **Genome-wide LOH (gLOH).** Median gLOH = 8.6% (IQR 3.0–15.7%) across 4,619 evaluable samples. **15% (697/4,619)** had high gLOH (≥19.3%, 1 SD above mean); only 5.0% of those had HRD-gene alterations and 3.3% biallelic HRD — i.e. high gLOH is largely *not* explained by canonical HRD genes. Liposarcoma NOS: 50.0% high gLOH but only 4.0% with HRD-gene alteration; osteosarcoma 30.6% vs. 1.7%; UPS 26.6% vs. 3.6%. gLOH and high TMB were largely non-overlapping (distinct biology).
- **TMB landscape.** Median TMB = 2.4 mut/Mb (IQR 0.8–4.0). TMB ≥5, ≥10, and ≥15 mut/Mb in 13.0%, **3.9%**, and 2.9% respectively. TMB ≥10 mut/Mb most frequent in angiosarcoma 15.0%, UPS 10.9%, [MPNST](../cancer_types/MPNST.md) 10.8%, ossifying fibromyxoid tumor 7.7%. UV signature dominated cutaneous [angiosarcoma](../cancer_types/ANGS.md) (35/41 evaluable; 22 from skin) and was present in 7/9 evaluable ultra-high-TMB (≥100 mut/Mb) cases.
- **[CD274](../genes/CD274.md) (PD-L1) amplification** detected in **1.0% (75/7,594)** of sarcomas, highest in UPS/MFH 3.6%, myxofibrosarcoma 2.6%, sarcoma NOS 2.2%; CD274-amplified tumors had a median TMB of 3.2 mut/Mb.
- **P-AYA vs. adult differences.** P-AYA patients had significantly lower TMB across most histologies (e.g. alveolar [RMS](../cancer_types/RMS.md) 1.6 vs. 2.4 mut/Mb; [MPNST](../cancer_types/MPNST.md) 2.4 vs. 3.2; [DFSP](../cancer_types/DFSP.md) 0.8 vs. 2.8; all FDR<0.05) and lower median gLOH 3.8% vs. 9.5% (p<4×10⁻⁴³); exception was osteosarcoma where gLOH was higher in P-AYA (16.8% vs. 12.2%; FDR<0.001). In P-AYA osteosarcoma, CNVs more frequent in [CCND3](../genes/CCND3.md), [AURKB](../genes/AURKB.md), [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md), GID4, [MYC](../genes/MYC.md); in adults, more frequent in [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)/[B](../genes/CDKN2B.md), [FRS2](../genes/FRS2.md).
- **Actionability.** **31.7% (2,372/7,494)** harbored ≥1 OncoKB actionable alteration; only 5.9% (n=439) were Level 1 (FDA-recognized for the given sarcoma type), most being [KIT](../genes/KIT.md) mutations in [GIST](../cancer_types/GIST.md) (observed in 64.4% of GISTs). Majority were Level 3B (clinical benefit in other cancers). Resistance markers also captured: SDH loss, [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md) D842V, [KIT](../genes/KIT.md) exon 13 mutations ([imatinib](../drugs/imatinib.md) resistance), [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) mutations (anti-estrogen resistance), and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations (MDM2-inhibitor resistance).
- **MSK clinical sub-cohort (n=118).** 90% (106/118) sequenced successfully; 47% (50/106) had a treatment-linked alteration; **29% (31/106) enrolled in a biomarker-matched trial or off-label FDA-approved drug**; 4% (4/106) had a sequencing-driven diagnostic change; 1% avoided futile treatment.
- **Anecdotal responses (Fig. 6).** Durable partial response to [tazemetostat](../drugs/tazemetostat.md) in sarcoma NOS with [SMARCB1](../genes/SMARCB1.md) deletion; rapid near-complete response to [vemurafenib](../drugs/vemurafenib.md)+[trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md) in [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E sarcoma; >1-year stable disease on PARP-inhibitor + ATR-inhibitor combination in osteosarcoma with [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) exon 57 truncation; near-complete response to [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md) in UPS with TMB 20 mut/Mb; complete response to [nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md)+[ipilimumab](../drugs/ipilimumab.md) in malignant [PEComa](../cancer_types/PECOMA.md) (TMB 7 mut/Mb); complete response to [larotrectinib](../drugs/larotrectinib.md) in [IMT](../cancer_types/IMT.md) with [ETV6](../genes/ETV6.md)–[NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md) fusion.

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md): altered in 37.1% of sarcomas (44.9% complex vs. 11.3% translocation-associated); 79.3% short variants; drives p53-pathway co-occurrence with [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md)/[MDM4](../genes/MDM4.md).
- [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md): 10.7% overall, 13.5% complex vs. 1.6% translocation-associated; >94% amplifications; pathognomonic of well/dedifferentiated liposarcoma; predicts response to [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) inhibitors.
- [MDM4](../genes/MDM4.md): 0.5% overall, 0.6% complex vs. 0.2% translocation-associated.
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md): 17.2% (21.5% complex vs. 2.8% translocation-associated); also rearranged in 1.5% of cases.
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) / [CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md): 16.7% / 12.8% overall; 84.5%/99.0% homozygous deletions; enriched in MPNST (47.3%) and chordoma (36.8%).
- [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) / [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md): 10.6% / 0.7%; typically amplified; targets for CDK4/6 inhibitors.
- [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) / [CCND2](../genes/CCND2.md) / [CCND3](../genes/CCND3.md): 1.0% / 1.4% / 1.8%; co-occur with [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md)/[6](../genes/CDK6.md) and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)/[B](../genes/CDKN2B.md) — unusual within-pathway co-occurrence pattern.
- [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md): 2.6%; typically amplified.
- [KIT](../genes/KIT.md) / [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md) / [KDR](../genes/KDR.md): co-amplified at 4q12 in osteosarcoma 10.7%, liposarcoma NOS 8.0%, UPS/MFH 7.3%. [KIT](../genes/KIT.md) mutations actionable in 64.4% of GISTs ([imatinib](../drugs/imatinib.md)); resistance markers include [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md) D842V and KIT exon 13 substitutions.
- [SMARCB1](../genes/SMARCB1.md): inactivated in 56% of epithelioid sarcomas and 60% of malignant rhabdoid tumors; tazemetostat-responsive.
- [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md): 22.9% of chondrosarcomas.
- [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md): amplified in 20% of granular cell tumors.
- [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md): inactivating alterations in 15.8% of chordomas.
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md): V600E in 0.5% overall — rare but actionable ([vemurafenib](../drugs/vemurafenib.md) + [trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md) response shown). Recurrent kinase fusions also detected.
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) G12X (0.9%), [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) exon 19 deletions (0.01%) and L858R (0.01%) — extremely rare in mesenchymal tumors; may flag sarcomatoid carcinoma.
- [NF1](../genes/NF1.md): co-occurring with UV mutational signature in ultra-high-TMB cases (5/11; may suggest desmoplastic melanoma or radiation-associated MPNST).
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) / [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) / [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md): enriched in myxoid liposarcoma (36.3% / 17.8% / 3.7% AKT E17K).
- [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md) / [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md): PEComa — [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) loss predicted mTOR-inhibitor responsiveness; subclonal [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md) documented in PEComa with response to [nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md)+[ipilimumab](../drugs/ipilimumab.md).
- [CD274](../genes/CD274.md) (PD-L1): amplified in 1.0% of sarcomas; warrants prospective ICI biomarker evaluation.
- Kinase-fusion genes: [ALK](../genes/ALK.md), [ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md), [RET](../genes/RET.md), [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md), [NTRK1](../genes/NTRK1.md), [NTRK2](../genes/NTRK2.md), [NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — 2.6% prevalence; novel [TNS1](../genes/TNS1.md)–[ALK](../genes/ALK.md) fusion in LMS (16 cases).
- Fusion-driver genes (transcription factors): [EWSR1](../genes/EWSR1.md) ([FLI1](../genes/FLI1.md), [WT1](../genes/WT1.md), [NR4A3](../genes/NR4A3.md)); [ETV6](../genes/ETV6.md)–[NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md); [SS18](../genes/SS18.md)–[SSX2](../genes/SSX2.md) (synovial sarcoma reclassification).
- DNA damage repair: [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), [PALB2](../genes/PALB2.md), [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md), [RAD51D](../genes/RAD51D.md), [RAD52](../genes/RAD52.md), [RAD54L](../genes/RAD54L.md) — collectively 2.5% with pathogenic alterations; biallelic in 1.0% (72/7,494).
- MMR genes: [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md), [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md), [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md) — altered in 2.1%; tightly correlated with MSI-H.
- [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md): mutations potentially predict anti-estrogen resistance in endometrial stromal sarcoma (analogous to breast cancer).
- [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md): noted as a prognostic marker in pediatric Ewing sarcoma (citing prior work).
- 11q13 / FGF amplicon: [FGF2](../genes/FGF2.md), [FGF3](../genes/FGF3.md), [FGF19](../genes/FGF19.md) co-amplified with [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) in UPS of bone.

## Clinical implications

- **Diagnosis.** Targeted sequencing identified potential reclassifications in 10.5% of cases by detecting (or excluding) pathognomonic alterations. The authors propose genomic profiling as an *adjunct* to morphologic, immunohistochemical, and cytogenetic (FISH) workup — especially in centers without specialist sarcoma pathologists.
- **Treatment selection — actionable alterations.** 31.7% of sarcomas carry ≥1 OncoKB actionable alteration. Level 1 (5.9%) examples include [KIT](../genes/KIT.md) mutations → [imatinib](../drugs/imatinib.md) in [GIST](../cancer_types/GIST.md). Most actionable findings are Level 3B (other-cancer evidence).
- **Treatment avoidance — resistance markers.** SDH-loss, [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md) D842V, and KIT exon 13 mutations indicate imatinib resistance in [GIST](../cancer_types/GIST.md); [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) mutations predict anti-estrogen resistance in endometrial stromal sarcoma; [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations confer resistance to [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) inhibitors; [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) deletion confers resistance to [palbociclib](../drugs/palbociclib.md) (CDK4/6 inhibition).
- **Immune checkpoint biomarkers.** 3.9% of sarcomas have TMB ≥10 mut/Mb (FDA-relevant threshold for tissue-agnostic [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md) under FoundationOne CDx); MSI-H is rare (0.3%) and routine MSI/IHC testing has low yield. CD274/PD-L1 amplification (1.0%) and gLOH warrant prospective biomarker studies. Anecdotal complete/near-complete ICI responses observed in UPS with TMB 20 mut/Mb ([pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md)) and malignant PEComa with TMB 7 mut/Mb ([nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md) + [ipilimumab](../drugs/ipilimumab.md)).
- **Kinase-fusion oncoprotein targeting.** [ETV6](../genes/ETV6.md)–[NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md) → [larotrectinib](../drugs/larotrectinib.md) durable complete response in [IMT](../cancer_types/IMT.md); novel [TNS1](../genes/TNS1.md)–[ALK](../genes/ALK.md) fusions in LMS may identify ALK-inhibitor candidates. [NTRK](../genes/NTRK1.md) *amplifications* are *not* actionable (negative trial finding cited).
- **Epigenetic targeting.** [SMARCB1](../genes/SMARCB1.md) loss → [tazemetostat](../drugs/tazemetostat.md) ([EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) inhibitor): durable partial response in sarcoma NOS.
- **Combined [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)+MEK.** [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E (rare; 0.5%) → [vemurafenib](../drugs/vemurafenib.md) + [trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md).
- **PARP + [ATR](../genes/ATR.md) combinations.** [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) loss-of-function osteosarcoma with prior [doxorubicin](../drugs/doxorubicin.md)/[cisplatin](../drugs/cisplatin.md) failure → PARP + [ATR](../genes/ATR.md) inhibitor combination (>1-year stable disease).
- **PARP / DNA-damage-repair therapy in HRD/gLOH-high sarcomas.** 15% of sarcomas have high gLOH (≥19.3%), with only a fraction explained by HRD-gene alterations; authors invoke the [ARIEL3](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28916367/) precedent ([rucaparib](../drugs/rucaparib.md) benefit in BRCA-wildtype, high-gLOH ovarian cancer) to motivate gLOH as a candidate biomarker in sarcoma.
- **Prognosis.** In Ewing sarcoma: [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md) alterations carry prognostic weight (citing prior work). Higher TMB is associated with worse outcome in rhabdomyosarcoma (citing Casey et al.).
- **Real-world translation gap.** In the MSK cohort, only 29% of profiled patients were matched to a biomarker-driven trial or off-label therapy; barriers included unavailable trials (3.7%), ineligibility (5.6%), late genomic profiling (5.6%), and loss to follow-up (1.8%) — echoing the NCI-MATCH 5%-match rate.

## Limitations & open questions

- **No expert sarcoma-pathologist re-review.** Reclassifications (10.5%) were not centrally re-confirmed; some may not survive expert review. Cases categorized as "sarcoma, unclassified" were excluded from subtype-specific analyses.
- **Tumor-only profiling.** No matched germline DNA — germline variants may be inadvertently included. Biallelic predictions inferred zygosity computationally rather than by phasing against normal.
- **Selection bias.** Cohort enriched for refractory/advanced disease (since Foundation Medicine sequencing is reimbursed in that setting), which may inflate observed alteration frequencies relative to all sarcomas.
- **Missing clinical metadata.** Stage, tumor grade, treatment outcomes, and whether the WD or DD component of WD/DD-LPS was sequenced were unavailable for the full 7,494-patient cohort; outcomes available only for the 118 MSK patients.
- **Gene-panel scope.** FoundationOne Heme does not cover several known/emerging sarcoma genes — explicitly [MYOD1](../genes/MYOD1.md), [VEGFA](../genes/VEGFA.md), [TERT](../genes/TERT.md), [MTAP](../genes/MTAP.md), POLE/D, [YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md), TAZ (Hippo pathway) — so the catalog of drivers is incomplete.
- **gLOH mechanism unresolved.** High gLOH in 15% of sarcomas is *not* explained by canonical HRD-gene alterations in the majority of cases; aneuploidy and other mechanisms are invoked but not characterized.
- **PD-L1 IHC unavailable.** Authors could not correlate [CD274](../genes/CD274.md) amplification with PD-L1 protein expression; prospective ICI biomarker validation needed.
- **NCT03784014.** A randomized prospective trial in Europe is referenced as the formal test of clinical utility — outcomes pending.
- **Variants with low allele frequency.** Some variants were sub-clonal; impact on clonality and tumor heterogeneity not interrogated in this paper.
- **Source data restrictions.** Raw sequencing data are not deposited; only variant calls in Supplementary Data 1.
