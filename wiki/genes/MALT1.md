# MALT1

## Overview

MALT1 (MALT Lymphoma Translocation Protein 1) is a paracaspase that functions as a scaffold and protease in the CARMA1–[BCL10](../genes/BCL10.md)–MALT1 (CBM) signalosome complex, linking antigen-receptor activation to canonical NF-kB signaling. In B-cell malignancies, MALT1 is recurrently amplified or translocated, and is a key mediator of BCR-pathway addiction in ABC-type diffuse large B-cell lymphoma. MALT1 at 18q21 is part of an amplicon that also harbors [BCL2](../genes/BCL2.md), making the 18q gain in C5 DLBCL simultaneously a BCL2 amplification and MALT1 gain event.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- MALT1 (18q) simultaneous gain by copy number, mutation, and structural variant — identified as a candidate cancer gene (CCG) in the C5 ABC-DLBCL subtype; the 18q gain amplicon covers BCL2 and MALT1 and is near-uniform in C5 tumors in a 304-sample DLBCL WES study [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [DLBCLNOS](../cancer_types/DLBCLNOS.md) — 18q gain encompassing MALT1 is a defining feature of the C5 ABC-DLBCL subtype characterized by [MYD88](../genes/MYD88.md)^L265P^/CD79B co-mutation, BCR/TLR-pathway addiction, and unfavorable prognosis; MALT1 gain co-occurs with BCL2 amplification on the same 18q21 amplicon; C5 represents ~21% of the 304-patient DLBCL WES cohort [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-amplified with [BCL2](../genes/BCL2.md) on the 18q21 locus in C5 DLBCL; this cluster also shows frequent [MYD88](../genes/MYD88.md) L265P and [CD79B](../genes/CD79B.md) mutations [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- BCR/TLR-proximal inhibition strategies proposed for C5 DLBCL (with 18q-BCL2/MALT1 gain): [BTK](../genes/BTK.md) inhibition, IRAK4 inhibition, and BCL2 antagonism (venetoclax-like agents); MALT1 protease inhibitors are an emerging therapeutic class not yet reported in this cohort [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Open questions

- Whether 18q gain drives MALT1 expression specifically (vs. BCL2 being the selected target) has not been directly measured in this cohort; functional distinction between BCL2-amplification and MALT1-amplification contributions to the C5 phenotype remains open [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)
