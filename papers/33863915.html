TERT promoter hotspot mutations and gene amplification in metaplastic breast cancer

PMID: 33863915 · DOI: 10.1038/s41523-021-00250-8 · Journal: npj Breast Cancer (2021)

TL;DR

da Silva and colleagues at Memorial Sloan Kettering characterized TERT somatic alterations across 60 primary metaplastic breast cancers (MBCs), a rare and aggressive breast cancer subtype. Using whole-exome sequencing (n=27), MSK-IMPACT targeted sequencing of 341–468 cancer-related genes (n=17), and Sanger sequencing (n=16), they found TERT promoter hotspot mutations or gene amplification in 17% (10/60) of MBCs — significantly less frequent in chondroid-predominant MBCs (0/25) than in other histologic subtypes (p=0.005). TERT alterations were mutually exclusive with TP53 mutations (p<0.001, CoMEt) and significantly co-occurred with PIK3CA hotspot mutations (p=0.001) PMID:33863915.

Cohort & data

  • Cohort: 60 primary metaplastic breast cancers (MBC) from MSKCC, median age 57 (range 34–85), 92% histologic grade 3, 95% triple-negative phenotype.
  • Predominant histology: squamous 23% (14/60), spindle cell 27% (16/60), osseous 8% (5/60), chondroid 42% (25/60).
  • Datasets: Sequencing data of 19 previously unreported MBCs (5 WES + 14 MSK-IMPACT) released as mbc_msk_2021. Three of the MSK-IMPACT cases were previously reported in msk_impact_2017 (Zehir et al., PMID:28481359 if in corpus).
  • Assays/methods: Whole-exome sequencing (n=27), MSK-IMPACT 341–468-gene targeted panel (n=17), Sanger sequencing of TERT promoter, TP53, PIK3CA, HRAS, BRAF (n=16). Variants called with MuTect, Strelka, VarScan2, Platypus, Lancet, Scalpel; CCFs computed with ABSOLUTE; CNAs/LOH with FACETS; mutational signatures with SigMA and DeconstructSigs; reads aligned with BWA v0.7.15 to GRCh37.

Key findings

  • TERT alteration frequency: 17% (10/60) — TERT promoter C228T in 9/60 (15%) and TERT gene amplification in 1/60 (2%). All TERT-altered MBCs were triple-negative PMID:33863915.
  • Histologic association: TERT alterations were significantly less frequent in chondroid-predominant MBCs (0/25) versus other subtypes (10/35; p=0.005, Fisher’s exact). By subtype: squamous 21% (3/14), spindle 31% (5/16), osseous 40% (2/5), chondroid 0% (0/25) PMID:33863915.
  • Mutual exclusivity with TP53: TP53 mutations were detected in 89% (34/38) of TERT-WT vs 50% (3/6) of TERT-altered MBCs (p=0.04, Fisher). Formal CoMEt mutual-exclusivity analysis on the WES/MSK-IMPACT cohort (n=44) yielded p<0.01; confirmed across the full n=60 cohort at p<0.001 PMID:33863915.
  • Co-occurrence with PIK3CA: TERT-altered MBCs were significantly enriched for clonal PIK3CA hotspot mutations — 5/6 (83%) TERT-altered vs 5/38 (13%) TERT-WT (p=0.001, Fisher) PMID:33863915.
  • Tumor mutation burden: No significant TMB difference between TERT-altered (median 3.1 mut/Mb; range 0.8–6.1) and TERT-WT (median 3.5 mut/Mb; 0.8–8.7) MBCs (p=0.72, Mann–Whitney) PMID:33863915.
  • Fraction of genome altered: TERT-altered MBCs had a significantly lower FGA (median 22%; 9–51%) than TERT-WT MBCs (median 54%; 20–86%; p=0.002, Mann–Whitney) PMID:33863915.
  • Mutational signatures: 60% (23/38) of MBCs displayed dominant COSMIC signatures 3 and 8 (HRD-associated); 34% (13/38) dominant aging signatures 1/5; one case each with APOBEC signature 2 and signature 17. No signature differences between TERT-altered and TERT-WT MBCs (p>0.05) PMID:33863915.
  • Ras pathway mutations co-occurring with TERT: Among 10 TERT-altered MBCs, activating Ras pathway hotspots were found in 3: KRAS A59G (subclonal), NRAS Q61L (clonal), and HRAS Q61R (clonal) — the latter two co-occurring with TP53 mutations and a subclonal BRAF D594N PMID:33863915.
  • Adenomyoepithelioma-like case: One MBC (MBC103T) showed a biphasic epithelial/myoepithelial proliferation (p63+/calponin+ abluminal layer) and harbored TERT C228T plus PIK3CA H1047R without TP53 or HRAS Q61 mutations, suggesting MBC arising in the context of adenomyoepithelioma PMID:33863915.

Genes & alterations

  • TERT — Promoter hotspot C228T (c.-124C>T) in 9/60 MBCs and gene amplification in 1/60; all in triple-negative cases. Mutually exclusive with TP53; co-occur with PIK3CA hotspots PMID:33863915.
  • TP53 — Mutations in 89% of TERT-WT vs 50% of TERT-altered MBCs; mutually exclusive with TERT alterations (CoMEt p<0.001) PMID:33863915.
  • PIK3CA — Clonal hotspot mutations (e.g., H1047R) significantly enriched in TERT-altered MBCs (83% vs 13%; p=0.001) PMID:33863915.
  • HRAS — Q61R/Q61K hotspots in 3/60 MBCs; one Q61R co-occurred with TERT promoter mutation; rare in primary breast cancer overall but recurrent in adenomyoepitheliomas PMID:33863915.
  • KRAS — Subclonal A59G in one TERT-altered MBC PMID:33863915.
  • NRAS — Clonal Q61L in one TERT-altered MBC (MT45), co-occurring with TP53 V173L/E204Vfs*4 PMID:33863915.
  • BRAF — Subclonal D594N hotspot in one TERT-altered MBC; BRAF amplification in 2 TERT-WT MBCs (MTC23, MBC104T) PMID:33863915.
  • PTEN, PIK3R1, ARID1A, KMT2C, RB1, NOTCH1, NOTCH2, BCOR, NCOR1, KDM6A — Mutations identified only in TERT-WT MBCs (though not reaching statistical significance after multiple-testing correction) PMID:33863915.
  • NOTCH3, KMT2D, MED12, CDKN2A — Detected at varying frequencies across both groups PMID:33863915.

Clinical implications

  • PI3K/mTOR therapeutic rationale: The authors note that PIK3CA enrichment in non-chondroid MBCs supports prior phase 1 data showing higher response rates to mTOR inhibition (temsirolimus or everolimus) combined with liposomal doxorubicin and bevacizumab in PI3K-pathway-altered advanced triple-negative MBC (Basho et al. 2017, ref 31) PMID:33863915.
  • Differential diagnosis: TERT promoter hotspot mutations and amplification cannot reliably distinguish MBC from malignant phyllodes tumors of the breast — both can harbor TERT alterations, so this marker should be used with caution in differential diagnosis PMID:33863915.
  • Taxonomic implication: Co-occurrence of TERT promoter mutations with HRAS Q61 and PIK3CA hotspots — alterations classically seen in adenomyoepitheliomas — raises the hypothesis that a subset of MBCs may evolve through pathways similar to or shared with adenomyoepithelial tumors PMID:33863915.

Limitations & open questions

  • Small sample size for a rare tumor type; limited DNA in some cases meant not all 60 MBCs underwent WES or MSK-IMPACT — so the 2% TERT amplification rate is conservative (12 of 16 Sanger-only MBCs could not be assessed for amplification) PMID:33863915.
  • Multi-institutional cohort lacks consistent clinical follow-up; no definitive survival analysis tied to TERT status was possible PMID:33863915.
  • Whether TP53/TERT mutual exclusivity reflects true epistatic interactions or simply differential baseline frequencies across histologic subtypes cannot be resolved without larger cohorts PMID:33863915.
  • Discrepancy with Krings & Chen (2018) regarding TERT presence in chondroid-differentiated MBCs may reflect inclusion of mixed-histology cases in this series versus pure subtypes in the prior study PMID:33863915.
