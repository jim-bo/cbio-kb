**PMID:** 29625055 · **DOI:** [10.1016/j.cell.2018.02.052](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cell.2018.02.052) · **Journal:** Cell (2018)

## TL;DR

Liu et al. systematically curated the clinical and follow-up data files for all 11,160 TCGA patients across 33 cancer types and built the **TCGA Pan-Cancer Clinical Data Resource (TCGA-CDR)**, a standardized table that derives four clinical outcome endpoints — overall survival ([OS](../cancer_types/OS.md)), progression-free interval (PFI), disease-free interval (DFI), and disease-specific survival (DSS) — at the patient level with a uniform time-zero (date of diagnosis). They quality-assessed each endpoint per cancer type and issued tumor-specific endpoint-usage recommendations. PFI is recommended without reservation in 27/33 cancer types; all four endpoints are recommended without reservation in 13/33 ([BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md), [CESC](../cancer_types/CESC.md), [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md), [ESCA](../cancer_types/ESCA.md), [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md), [KIRP](../cancer_types/KIRP.md), [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md), [LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md), [OV](../cancer_types/OV.md), [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md), [SARC](../cancer_types/SARC.md), [STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md), [UCEC](../cancer_types/UCEC.md)). The resource is the canonical survival annotation underlying virtually all subsequent TCGA Pan-Cancer Atlas analyses.

## Cohort & data

- **Patients:** 11,160 across **33** TCGA cancer types (the full PanCanAtlas cohort).
- **Cancer types covered (33):** [ACC](../cancer_types/ACC.md), [BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md), [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md), [CESC](../cancer_types/CESC.md), [CHOL](../cancer_types/CHOL.md), COAD, DLBC (OncoTree [DLBCLNOS](../cancer_types/DLBCLNOS.md)), [ESCA](../cancer_types/ESCA.md), [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md), [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md), [KICH](../cancer_types/KICH.md), [KIRC](../cancer_types/KIRC.md), [KIRP](../cancer_types/KIRP.md), [LAML](../cancer_types/LAML.md), [LGG](../cancer_types/LGG.md), [LIHC](../cancer_types/LIHC.md), [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md), [LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md), [MESO](../cancer_types/MESO.md), [OV](../cancer_types/OV.md), [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md), [PCPG](../cancer_types/PCPG.md), [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md), [READ](../cancer_types/READ.md), [SARC](../cancer_types/SARC.md), [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md), [STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md), [TGCT](../cancer_types/TGCT.md), [THCA](../cancer_types/THCA.md), [THYM](../cancer_types/THYM.md), [UCEC](../cancer_types/UCEC.md), [UCS](../cancer_types/UCS.md), [UVM](../cancer_types/UVM.md).
- **Source data:** 225 TCGA clinical data files from the Genomic Data Commons (GDC) legacy archive; 130 initial enrollment and follow-up files were used (treatment/pharmaceutical files excluded). 33 initial enrollment + 97 follow-up data files were processed.
- **Sample acquisition:** Tumors from 161 tissue source sites (TSSs) worldwide, originally diagnosed 1978–2013 (median 2009). Almost all primary tumors; [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md) is the exception (103 primary, 296 regional lymph-node metastases, 68 distant metastases out of 470).
- **Linked TCGA Pan-Cancer Atlas datasets:** [gbm_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/gbm_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [ov_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/ov_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [lgg_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/lgg_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [brca_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/brca_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [ucec_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/ucec_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [prad_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/prad_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md) (the cBioPortal Pan-Can Atlas study set; TCGA-CDR provides their survival annotations).
- **Endpoints derived:** OS, PFI, DFI, DSS — time-zero = date of diagnosis for all four.
- **Statistical methods:** [Kaplan-Meier](../methods/kaplan-meier.md) survival curves and [Cox proportional hazards](../methods/cox-proportional-hazards.md) regression with proportional-hazards-assumption tests (Grambsch & Therneau 1994); paired Wilcoxon signed-rank tests for endpoint comparisons; Pearson correlation weighted by inverse mean of two standard errors of logHR for cross-endpoint logHR concordance. Analysis performed in R 3.2.2.

## Key findings

- **Overall median follow-up:** 22.1 months across the full 11,160-patient cohort, but follow-up varied widely by cancer type — shortest in [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) and [LAML](../cancer_types/LAML.md) (~12 months), longest in [KICH](../cancer_types/KICH.md) (~48 months) — directly constraining which endpoints are usable per tumor type.
- **Endpoint-usage recommendations (Table 3):**
  - All four endpoints (OS, PFI, DFI, DSS) recommended without reservation in **13/33** cancer types: BLCA, CESC, COAD, ESCA, HNSC, KIRP, LUAD, LUSC, OV, PAAD, SARC, STAD, UCEC.
  - [PCPG](../cancer_types/PCPG.md): **no endpoint** can be recommended.
  - DLBC, LAML, [THYM](../cancer_types/THYM.md): only **one** endpoint recommended.
  - PFI alone is recommended without reservation in **27/33** cancer types; exceptions are LAML (no PFI data available), DLBC and KICH (use with caution), and PCPG (not recommended).
- **GBM validation against literature:** TCGA-derived median OS = **12.6 months** and median PFI = **6.1 months**, both falling between the standard-care arm (OS 12.1 mo, PFI 5.0 mo) and the standard-care-plus-[temozolomide](../drugs/temozolomide.md) arm (OS 14.6 mo, PFI 6.9 mo) of Stupp et al. 2005. TCGA GBM event capture was 82.4% within the 12-month median follow-up.
- **Breast cancer ER+ vs ER- validation (Figures 2A–2D, plots truncated at 10-year follow-up):**
  - PFI: ER+ had better survival than ER- (**p = 0.005**).
  - DFI: ER+ better (**p = 0.001**).
  - DSS (approximated): ER+ better (**p = 0.009**).
  - OS: no significant difference (p = 0.097) — consistent with the paper's recommendation to use PFI/DFI rather than OS for [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md).
- **High-stage (III/IV) vs low-stage (I/II) Cox HRs (Figures 2E–2G):** HR significantly > 1 for most of the 14 cancer types where all of OS, PFI, and DSS were recommended, with the exceptions of [MESO](../cancer_types/MESO.md), PAAD, and [UVM](../cancer_types/UVM.md) (not significantly different by any endpoint).
- **Cross-endpoint logHR correlation (Pearson, weighted by inverse mean of two logHR SEs):**
  - PFI vs OS: **r = 0.96** (95% CI 0.77–0.99).
  - PFI vs DSS: **r = 0.95** (95% CI 0.76–0.99).
  - OS vs DSS: **r = 0.90** (95% CI 0.61–0.98).
  - Paired Wilcoxon signed-rank: PFI vs DSS **p = 0.0008**, PFI vs OS **p = 0.039**, OS vs DSS not significant (p = 0.106) — supports using PFI as an earlier-determined surrogate for OS/DSS.
- **Never-disease-free vs disease-free new-tumor-event (NTE) risk (LUSC example, landmark at 3 months post-diagnosis):** 289 disease-free vs 41 never-disease-free LUSC cases; 21.8% vs 68.2% NTE rate respectively. Cox **HR = 6.68 (95% CI 4.25–10.51, FDR q < 0.05)** for never-disease-free patients. Similar significant differences observed in 21 additional cancer types (29 total tested; Table 4).
- **Data-cleaning scale:** Over 1,000 QA issues resolved; specific edge cases include 7 Stage-0 SKCM cases (all retained because the molecularly profiled samples were subsequent metastases), 483 "died with tumor without defined NTE" cases (excluded from DFI but kept for OS/PFI/DSS), 62 "dead but tumor-free with defined NTE" cases (censored for DSS), and 797 of 3,346 NTEs with no new-tumor-type specified (retained for PFI under the PanCanAtlas Pan-Immune AWG's relaxed PFI definition).
- **Time-zero choice:** Date of diagnosis is used as t=0 for all four endpoints rather than date of sample procurement, because (1) days_to_sample_procurement is not a required TCGA field and (2) for primary tumors the median was small (BRCA mean 34, median 21 days). HIPAA de-identification forces ages ≥ 90 to be coded as exactly "90" — this affected 46 patients.

## Genes & alterations

- [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) / [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) — Cited (via the [LGG](../cancer_types/LGG.md) marker paper, Cancer Genome Atlas Research Network et al. 2015) as a worked example of how TCGA-CDR OS data have already produced practice-changing genomic findings: LGG patients with IDH mutations (with or without 1p/19q codeletion) have substantially longer OS than IDH-wild-type LGG, establishing IDH/1p19q status as a more robust survival predictor than histologic subtype. This contributed evidence to the WHO 2016 diagnostic update for glioma (Louis et al. 2016). The TCGA-CDR paper itself does not perform new IDH analyses but highlights this as the canonical example of TCGA survival-genomic integration.

## Clinical implications

- **Canonical survival annotation for cBioPortal/PanCanAtlas studies.** TCGA-CDR is the source of OS/PFI/DFI/DSS fields downstream cBioPortal Pan-Cancer Atlas 2018 studies expose (e.g. [gbm_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/gbm_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [brca_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/brca_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md)). Any cross-cohort survival analysis built on these studies inherits the endpoint definitions and per-cancer-type quality flags from this paper's Table 3.
- **Endpoint selection guidance per cancer type.** For aggressive cancers with sufficient event capture (GBM, OV, LUAD/LUSC, etc.), OS is reliable. For less aggressive tumors (BRCA, [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md)) the authors specifically advise against using OS/DSS and instead recommend PFI/DFI given the short TCGA follow-up. PFI is the single most generally reliable endpoint and a defensible surrogate for OS/DSS where the latter are underpowered.
- **Surrogate-endpoint justification.** The strong logHR correlation between PFI and OS (r = 0.96) supports clinical-trial practice of using progression-based intermediate endpoints in less-aggressive cancers where mature OS would require decades of follow-up.
- **No treatment-data integration.** TCGA-CDR deliberately omits treatment history; pan-cancer therapeutic-effect inference using TCGA must be done per-cancer-type with awareness of incomplete annotation.
- **SKCM caveat.** Outcome correlations on SKCM should use only the 103 primary tumors (or the regional-nodal stage-III metastases as a separate group); most SKCM molecular profiling was on metastases that lack matching primaries.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Short, non-uniform follow-up.** TCGA did not have a uniform follow-up plan; prospectively enrolled patients could have as little as 1 year of follow-up. This drives nearly all the per-cancer-type endpoint exclusions in Table 3.
- **No treatment data.** Treatment history is not in TCGA-CDR; the authors explicitly note that pan-cancer treatment-effect analyses are out of scope and would require a new AWG.
- **DSS is approximated.** Cause of death was rarely independently verified; DSS values are therefore inferred from existing fields and should be treated as approximations, not gold-standard cancer-specific mortality.
- **TSS heterogeneity.** Comparison of the top two TSSs against all others per cancer type (Table 5) showed measurable site-level differences in outcomes for less-aggressive tumors (e.g. BRCA TSS #1 had worse OS/DSS, no PFI difference, and nominally better DFI based on only 3 events) — site is a confounder that may proxy for unmeasured factors (age, stage/grade, treatment, annotation completeness).
- **Cox proportional-hazards-assumption violations.** Most models passed the PH assumption but the few that failed are not deeply investigated in this paper.
- **Molecular-subgroup integration warning.** Stratifying TCGA-CDR by molecular subtypes shrinks per-arm sample sizes and can erode statistical power (Peduzzi et al. 1995); the authors recommend validating any TCGA-CDR molecular-correlate finding on an independent cohort.
- **Single-section static-snapshot.** TCGA tumors are single primary-tumor sections; the resource cannot capture spatial or temporal intratumor heterogeneity as an outcome variable.
- **Age ceiling at 90.** HIPAA-driven de-identification forces all patients ≥ 90 years old to be reported as exactly 90 (46 patients affected), capping the upper-age covariate.
