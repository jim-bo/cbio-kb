# SOX4

## Overview

SOX4 (SRY-Box Transcription Factor 4) is a transcription factor involved in neuronal, lymphocyte, and cardiac development, and has established roles in cancer progression, EMT, and stem cell maintenance. In glioma, SOX4 is the top-ranked marker of the neural stem/progenitor compartment in oligodendroglioma by single-cell RNA-seq, marking the rare proliferative cells at the apex of the tumor hierarchy.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- SOX4 is the highest-ranking gene (by PC2/PC3 loading) in the stem/progenitor program across 4,347 single cells from 6 untreated grade II IDH-mutant 1p/19q-codeleted oligodendrogliomas (Smart-Seq2). The PC2/PC3 stem/progenitor signature overlaps glioblastoma CSC signatures (hypergeometric P = 1.5×10⁻⁴) and is preferentially expressed in prenatal brain (P = 8×10⁻¹⁸). SOX4 expression was also validated by RNA in situ hybridization on tumor sections. [PMID:27806376](../papers/27806376.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [ODG](../cancer_types/ODG.md) — SOX4 is the defining top marker of the stem/progenitor compartment (1.5–8% of cells per tumor) in grade II oligodendroglioma; nearly all cycling cells are SOX4-high; the stem/progenitor signature correlates with the cell-cycle signature in 69 bulk TCGA LGG oligodendrogliomas. [PMID:27806376](../papers/27806376.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-expressed with [SOX11](../genes/SOX11.md), [SOX2](../genes/SOX2.md), [NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md), [ASCL1](../genes/ASCL1.md), [CHD7](../genes/CHD7.md), [CD24](../genes/CD24.md), [BOC](../genes/BOC.md), [TCF4](../genes/TCF4.md), [CCND2](../genes/CCND2.md) in the stem/progenitor compartment; mutually exclusive with oligodendrocytic ([OLIG1](../genes/OLIG1.md), [OLIG2](../genes/OLIG2.md)) and astrocytic ([SOX9](../genes/SOX9.md), [APOE](../genes/APOE.md)) lineage markers. [PMID:27806376](../papers/27806376.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- SOX4-high stem/progenitor cells represent the proliferative apex of oligodendroglioma; no specific drugs are evaluated in this study.

## Open questions

- Functional stemness was not validated by xenotransplantation; generalizability to other glioma subtypes is not established. [PMID:27806376](../papers/27806376.md)
