# RNF130

## Overview

RNF130 is an E3 ubiquitin ligase. In pilocytic astrocytoma, RNF130 was identified as a novel 5' fusion partner of [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) in 2 tumors, contributing to MAPK pathway activation through an alternative mechanism to the canonical [KIAA1549](../genes/KIAA1549.md):BRAF fusion.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Identified as a novel 5' fusion partner for [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) in 2/96 pilocytic astrocytoma whole-genome sequencing cases; functional characterization not performed in this study. [PMID:23817572](../papers/23817572.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[PAST](../cancer_types/PAST.md) (Pilocytic Astrocytoma)**: RNF130:BRAF fusion is one of four novel BRAF fusion partners (alongside [CLCN6](../genes/CLCN6.md), [MKRN1](../genes/MKRN1.md), [GNAI1](../genes/GNAI1.md)) identified in non-cerebellar pilocytic astrocytoma. [PMID:23817572](../papers/23817572.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- RNF130:BRAF fusions occur in tumors lacking the canonical [KIAA1549](../genes/KIAA1549.md):[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) fusion; expected to be mutually exclusive with other MAPK-activating alterations ([FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) mutations, [NTRK2](../genes/NTRK2.md) fusions). [PMID:23817572](../papers/23817572.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Tumors with RNF130:BRAF fusions would be expected to respond to MEK inhibition; the authors caution against non-V600E-selective BRAF inhibitors in fusion-driven pilocytic astrocytoma due to risk of paradoxical MAPK activation. [PMID:23817572](../papers/23817572.md)

## Open questions

- Functional dissection of the RNF130:BRAF fusion (dimerization domain contribution, ERK activation potency) was not performed by the authors. [PMID:23817572](../papers/23817572.md)
