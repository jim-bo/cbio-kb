**PMID:** 33466343 · **DOI:** [10.3390/cancers13010149](https://doi.org/10.3390/cancers13010149) · **Journal:** Cancers (2021)

## TL;DR

Francis et al. profiled 83 enucleated [retinoblastoma](../cancer_types/RBL.md) specimens from Memorial Sloan Kettering using the [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) targeted NGS panel (468 somatic genes; 88 germline cancer-predisposition genes) to characterize the genomic landscape beyond [RB1](../genes/RB1.md). [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) inactivation was found in 97.5% (79/81) of patients, with copy-neutral LOH dramatically over-represented at the [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) locus relative to other tumor suppressors (OR 19.8) and to [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) in other cancers (OR 44.4). The most common non-[RB1](../genes/RB1.md) somatic alteration was [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md) (22.9%, 19/83), and [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md) mutations were associated with worse metastasis-free survival (nominal p=0.03). Recurrent arm-level CNAs (1q gain, 16q LOH) independently associated with vitreous seeding (BH-corrected p=0.008 and 0.004; OR=12.6 and 26.7). Five of 42 germline-tested patients (12.0%) harbored pathogenic germline variants in non-[RB1](../genes/RB1.md) cancer-predisposition genes ([NTHL1](../genes/NTHL1.md), [ERCC3](../genes/ERCC3.md), [MSH3](../genes/MSH3.md), [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md), [MITF](../genes/MITF.md)).

## Cohort & data

- **N=83** enucleated [retinoblastoma](../cancer_types/RBL.md) ([RBL](../cancer_types/RBL.md)) specimens, accrued May 2006–August 2019 at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center (IRB #17-049).
- Dataset: [rbl_mskcc_2020](../datasets/rbl_mskcc_2020.md) — IMPACT mutation calls deposited at cBioPortal (`https://cbioportal.mskcc.org/study/summary?id=rbl_mskcc_2020`).
- Assay: [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) hybridization capture targeted NGS — 468 genes for somatic analysis ([IMPACT468](../methods/IMPACT468.md)), 88 genes for germline analysis in 42 consented patients. Detects SNVs, indels, gene-level focal CNAs, and [select](../methods/select.md) rearrangements.
- 74 specimens had matched normal tissue; 9 were tumor-only.
- Copy-number and clonality called with [FACETS](../methods/facets.md) v0.5.14; microsatellite instability estimated with [MSIsensor](../methods/msisensor.md); event-free survival modeled with [Kaplan–Meier](../methods/kaplan-meier.md) estimators starting from enucleation date.
- Sample input: 10-µm FFPE microdissected sections, DNeasy extraction, ≥250 ng FFPE DNA per sample.
- 23 specimens additionally had outside commercial [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) testing for methylation and germline status.

## Key findings

- **[RB1](../genes/RB1.md) inactivation is near-universal but mechanistically diverse.** Pathognomonic [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) alterations were detected in 97.5% (79/81) of patients [PMID:33466343](../papers/33466343.md). Biallelic [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) loss was identified in 79.5% (66/83) of specimens. Of these 66, 10 had somatic homozygous deletion of [RB1](../genes/RB1.md); 1 had germline heterozygous loss of exons 7–11 followed by somatic LOH; 38/53 (71.6%) of the remainder had a mutation followed by LOH; 15 had two mutations; 2 had promoter hypermethylation plus LOH. Twelve specimens had only apparent heterozygous alterations detected by MSK-IMPACT, suggesting silencing mechanisms not captured by the panel.
- **Two specimens had no detectable [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) alterations.** One had subclonal mutations in [BIRC3](../genes/BIRC3.md) (p.A385D, VUS) and [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md) (p.G176*, truncating); the other had only a gain of 6p — consistent with the hypothesis that 6p gain may be sufficient for tumorigenesis in wildtype-[RB1](../genes/RB1.md) retinoblastoma.
- **Copy-neutral LOH (CN-LOH) is the dominant second-hit mechanism at [RB1](../genes/RB1.md).** Of 42 matched specimens with LOH at the [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) locus, 31 (73.8%) were CN-LOH. CN-LOH at [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) was significantly enriched vs. other tumor suppressor genes in the same specimens (p<2.2×10⁻¹⁶, OR=19.8) and vs. [RB1](../genes/RB1.md)-mutant tumors in other cancer types from the MSK cohort (~39,846 specimens; p<2.2×10⁻¹⁶, OR=44.4).
- **Intertumoral heterogeneity within bilateral disease.** Two patients with bilateral disease and germline [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) alterations had distinct somatic second hits in each eye (e.g., p.X566_splice vs. p.V654Cfs*4; somatic LOH vs. p.S807*). Three patients had evidence of >2 [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) hits, suggesting multiple clones within a single eye.
- **[BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md) is the most common non-[RB1](../genes/RB1.md) somatic gene alteration.** Found in 19/83 specimens (22.9%). All [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md) mutations were putative loss-of-function. This frequency exceeds previously published series (~13%).
- **[BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md) mutations correlate with worse metastasis-free survival** (Kaplan–Meier, nominal p=0.03), but not with high-risk histopathological features.
- **Other recurrently somatic-mutated genes** (≥3 specimens): [RPTOR](../genes/RPTOR.md) (3/83, 3.6%), [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) (3, 3.6%), [MSH3](../genes/MSH3.md) (3, 3.6%), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) (3, 3.6%), [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md) (3, 3.6%), [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) (2, 2.4%), [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md) (1, 1.2%). Among the ten most-frequent non-[RB1](../genes/RB1.md) alterations, only [TERT](../genes/TERT.md), [MSH3](../genes/MSH3.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), and [HLA-A](../genes/HLA-A.md) had any putative-driver mutations. [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md) and [RPTOR](../genes/RPTOR.md) mutations were all VUS — non-truncating, non-hotspot.
- **Three patients had [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md) amplification** co-occurring with [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) mutation.
- **Tumor mutational burden was uniformly low** (0–3 mutations/Mb).
- **Recurrent arm-level CNAs** (n=69 evaluable eyes): 1q gain 67.0%, 2p gain 32.0%, 6p gain 59.0%, 13q LOH 51.0%, 16q LOH 43.0%.
- **Vitreous seeds independently associate with 1q gain and 16q LOH.** BH-corrected p=0.008 and 0.004; OR=12.6 and 26.7 respectively. 1q gain and 16q loss were not significantly correlated with each other (p=0.37), and a joint model showed both contribute independently (model p=8.2×10⁻⁸; 1q p=1.3×10⁻⁴, 16q p=2.3×10⁻⁴). 6p gain was *not* associated with aggressive histopathology in this enucleated-only cohort, contradicting some prior reports.
- **Germline non-[RB1](../genes/RB1.md) cancer-predisposition mutations in 5/42 (~12%) of germline-tested patients.** Variants: [NTHL1](../genes/NTHL1.md) c.268C>T (p.Gln90*); [ERCC3](../genes/ERCC3.md) c.1354C>T (p.Arg452*); [MSH3](../genes/MSH3.md) c.1341-1G>T (p.X447_splice); [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md) c.470T>C (p.Ile157Thr); [MITF](../genes/MITF.md) c.1255G>A (p.Glu419Lys). Two of these were in autosomal recessive cancer-predisposition genes ([NTHL1](../genes/NTHL1.md), [MSH3](../genes/MSH3.md)); the [MSH3](../genes/MSH3.md) patient showed no microsatellite instability.
- **No two specimens shared an identical genetic profile** across mutations, LOH, or CNVs.

## Genes & alterations

- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — biallelic loss in 66/83 (79.5%); strong preference for copy-neutral LOH as the second-hit mechanism (31/42 LOH events were CN-LOH; OR=19.8 vs. other TSGs; OR=44.4 vs. [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) in other cancers).
- [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md) — most common non-[RB1](../genes/RB1.md) somatic gene; 19/83 (22.9%) putative loss-of-function; significantly associated with worse metastasis-free survival (p=0.03).
- [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md) — amplified in 3 specimens, all in the presence of [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) mutation; no MYCN-only "[RB1](../genes/RB1.md)-wildtype + MYCN-amplified" tumors observed in this cohort (in contrast to ~2% frequency reported elsewhere).
- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md), [MSH3](../genes/MSH3.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [HLA-A](../genes/HLA-A.md) — recurrent at ~3.6% each; only these (among the top ten non-[RB1](../genes/RB1.md) recurrently altered genes) carried putative-driver variants.
- [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md) — single putative-driver mutation; previously implicated by WES studies in retinoblastoma.
- [RPTOR](../genes/RPTOR.md), [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md) — recurrent but all mutations were VUS in this cohort.
- [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) — mutated in 2 specimens; located at 16p13.3 and corresponds with the 16p region commonly altered in retinoblastoma.
- [CDH11](../genes/CDH11.md) — candidate gene previously proposed for 16q-loss-associated vitreous seeding; no [CDH11](../genes/CDH11.md) coding mutations in this cohort, so its role remains unclear here.
- [BIRC3](../genes/BIRC3.md), [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md) — subclonal alterations in a single [RB1](../genes/RB1.md)-wildtype specimen.
- Driver candidates discussed but not recurrently mutated in this cohort: [KIF14](../genes/KIF14.md), [DEK](../genes/DEK.md), [E2F3](../genes/E2F3.md), [RBL2](../genes/RBL2.md), [NGFR](../genes/NGFR.md).
- Germline pathogenic variants (non-[RB1](../genes/RB1.md)) in 5/42 tested patients: [NTHL1](../genes/NTHL1.md) p.Gln90*, [ERCC3](../genes/ERCC3.md) p.Arg452*, [MSH3](../genes/MSH3.md) p.X447_splice, [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md) p.Ile157Thr, [MITF](../genes/MITF.md) p.Glu419Lys.

## Clinical implications

- **[BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md) as a metastasis biomarker.** The authors propose that detection of a [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md) loss-of-function mutation — either in an enucleated specimen or via cell-free DNA (cfDNA) — may inform surveillance strategies or preventative measures for metastatic retinoblastoma, potentially marking minimal residual disease or treatment response.
- **1q gain and 16q LOH as independent biomarkers of vitreous seeding.** The two CNAs do not co-occur statistically (p=0.37) but each independently associates with vitreous seeding (the principal indication for enucleation), suggesting they may be useful jointly as predictive markers.
- **Germline screening implications.** The 12% prevalence of non-[RB1](../genes/RB1.md) germline cancer-predisposition mutations is in line with broader pediatric-cancer germline rates (12–17.5%), and the authors argue this supports broader (non-[RB1](../genes/RB1.md)-only) germline testing in retinoblastoma patients to inform lifetime cancer screening — though to date all five carriers in this cohort have shown intraocular disease only.
- **6p gain in wildtype-[RB1](../genes/RB1.md) retinoblastoma.** One [RB1](../genes/RB1.md)-wildtype tumor had only 6p gain, leading the authors to hypothesize that 6p gain may be sufficient for retinoblastoma tumorigenesis in this setting, though further sensitivity testing for [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) inactivation is needed.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Enucleation-only cohort.** All 83 specimens came from eyes removed primarily or secondarily, biasing the cohort toward more advanced or treatment-refractory disease. This may explain why 6p gain did not correlate with aggressive histopathology here, in contrast to prior reports.
- **MSK-IMPACT does not detect all [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) inactivation mechanisms.** Twelve specimens showed only heterozygous [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) alterations by panel sequencing; promoter hypermethylation and other epigenetic silencing mechanisms are not systematically captured. Not all samples had outside methylation testing.
- **Statistical power for rare alterations.** Only [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md) had high enough frequency to test clinical-feature association; recurrent VUS in [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md) and [RPTOR](../genes/RPTOR.md) could not be functionally assessed.
- **[BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md)–metastasis association is nominal (p=0.03).** Not multiple-testing corrected; needs validation in independent cohorts.
- **Germline cohort is small.** Only 42/83 patients consented to germline MSK-IMPACT testing; the 12% non-[RB1](../genes/RB1.md) germline frequency is based on this subset.
- **Functional role of non-[RB1](../genes/RB1.md) germline variants is unknown.** The authors note that whereas germline cancer-predisposition mutations are associated with metastatic disease in other cancers, all five carriers here have shown intraocular disease only — leaving open whether these variants influence tumorigenesis, clinical course, or second-cancer risk.
- **Future work** suggested by authors: DNA methylation, RNA expression, and cfDNA-based surveillance to extend the genotype-phenotype map.
