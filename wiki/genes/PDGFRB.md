# PDGFRB

## Overview

PDGFRB (Platelet Derived Growth Factor Receptor Beta) encodes a receptor tyrosine kinase activated by PDGF-B and PDGF-D ligands, with established roles in mesenchymal and stromal cell biology. In neuroblastoma, PDGFRB marks the mesenchymal stromal (MSC) compartment and distinguishes it from the undifferentiated tumor cluster.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Expression marker in neuroblastoma: PDGFRB is significantly higher in the mesenchymal stromal (MSC) nC1 cluster than in the undifferentiated nC3 cluster in a single-nuclei RNA-seq study of 11 neuroblastoma tumors (Smart-Seq2), distinguishing stroma from undifferentiated tumor cells. [PMID:34493726](../papers/34493726.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [NBL](../cancer_types/NBL.md) — PDGFRB is specifically elevated in the MSC nC1 stromal cluster of neuroblastoma; its differential expression relative to [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md) helps distinguish stromal (nC1) from undifferentiated tumor (nC3) populations. High-risk neuroblastomas have significantly higher proportions of stromal cells (FDR <0.01, chi-square). [PMID:34493726](../papers/34493726.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- PDGFRB is expressed more highly in MSC nC1 than in nC3; [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md) is expressed in both nC3 and MSC nC1. The two receptors thus have partially distinct compartmental distributions within neuroblastoma tumors. [PMID:34493726](../papers/34493726.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct targeted therapy is reported in the corpus for PDGFRB in neuroblastoma.

## Open questions

- Whether PDGFRB-expressing mesenchymal stromal cells in neuroblastoma actively support tumor growth or are therapeutically targetable is not addressed. [PMID:34493726](../papers/34493726.md)
