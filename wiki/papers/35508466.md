**PMID:** 35508466 · **DOI:** 10.1038/s41467-022-29960-8 · **Journal:** Nature Communications (2022)

## TL;DR

Ng, Dazert, Boldanova and colleagues performed an integrated (phospho)proteogenomic characterization of 122 [hepatocellular carcinoma](../cancer_types/HCC.md) biopsies from 114 systemically-untreated patients across BCLC stages 0–D and across HBV/HCV/alcohol/NAFLD etiologies, combining whole-exome sequencing, RNA-seq, SWATH-MS proteome (n=51), and label-free phosphoproteome (n=51) data with 15/11/10 matched normal-liver controls. The cBioPortal study identifier is [hcc_meric_2021](../datasets/hcc_meric_2021.md). They identified 7 significantly mutated genes (including [GPAM](../genes/GPAM.md), elevated to driver candidacy by 7/9 frameshift mutations and knockdown-induced proliferation), nominated copy-number-driven driver candidates ([NUDCD1](../genes/NUDCD1.md), [UBQLN4](../genes/UBQLN4.md), [BYSL](../genes/BYSL.md), [ATP6V1C1](../genes/ATP6V1C1.md), [RRM2B](../genes/RRM2B.md)) within five recurrent GISTIC2 peaks, and showed that [AURKA](../genes/AURKA.md) and [CDK1](../genes/CDK1.md)/[CDK2](../genes/CDK2.md)/[CDK5](../genes/CDK5.md)/[CDK7](../genes/CDK7.md) are activated kinases by KSEA. [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) mutations were linked to altered phosphorylation of actin-organization proteins (loss of epithelial polarity), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations to altered phosphorylation of lipid-metabolism proteins and increased CDK1/2/5 activity; integrative iCluster analysis defined three clusters with cluster 3 (high cell proliferation, [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation, CDK1/2/6 activity) associated with poor overall survival ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).

## Cohort & data

- **122 [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) biopsies from 114 patients** (University Hospital Basel, 2008–2018), including 6 patients with synchronous multicentric (genetically independent) tumors and 1 with three multicentric tumors. No patient had received systemic or locoregional therapy prior to biopsy ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- Underlying liver disease: alcohol 59%, HCV 26%, NAFLD 17%, HBV 11%, no liver disease 6% (patients may have >1); 72% cirrhotic; BCLC 0/A (early) 53%, BCLC B/C/D (advanced) 47%; Edmondson grade 1/2/3/4: 6%/54%/34%/7%; immunophenotype inflamed 30% / immune-excluded 35% / immune-desert 31% ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- **Normal-liver controls** from 19 patients without [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md): RNA-seq n=15, proteome n=11, phosphoproteome n=10 ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- Dataset released as cBioPortal study [hcc_meric_2021](../datasets/hcc_meric_2021.md).
- Modalities and platforms: [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) (SureSelect XT Clinical Research Exome / SureSelect V6+COSMIC; HiSeq 2500; tumor median 94.3×, normal 49.4×; GRCh37 alignment via BWA; somatic SNVs by MuTect, indels by Strelka; significantly mutated genes by [mutsigcv](../methods/mutsigcv.md) and [oncodrivefml](../methods/oncodrivefml.md)); [rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) (TruSeq Stranded Total RNA with Ribo-Zero Gold, single-end 126 bp, [STAR](../genes/STAR.md) two-pass to GRCh37 + HBV/HCV genomes, RSEM quantification); [swath-ms](../methods/swath-ms.md) data-independent acquisition for the proteome (Q Exactive Plus, 6,167 quantified proteins, library by MaxQuant, peak extraction by Spectronaut X); [label-free-proteomics](../methods/label-free-proteomics.md) (Fe(III)-IMAC enrichment, Q-Exactive HF, 12,205 phosphorylation sites in 4,230 proteins) and processed analogously to [tmt-phosphoproteomics](../methods/tmt-phosphoproteomics.md) workflows ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- Companion analytic methods: allele-specific CNAs by [facets](../methods/facets.md); recurrent focal CNAs by [gistic](../methods/gistic.md) 2.0; single-omics subtypes by consensus [nmf-clustering](../methods/nmf-clustering.md) (transcriptome, proteome, phosphoproteome) and consensus k-means / hierarchical clustering; integrative subtypes by [icluster](../methods/icluster.md) (and SNF as orthogonal validation); kinase activity by Kinase-Substrate Enrichment Analysis ([ksea](../methods/ksea.md)); reference genome GRCh37 ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).

## Key findings

- **Moderate transcriptome-proteome concordance with discordant translational regulation.** Across [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) vs normal liver, mRNA and protein fold-changes correlated at Spearman ρ=0.33 (p<0.0001); quadrant analysis showed 37.7% of genes up-regulated on both RNA and protein, 24.8% down-regulated on both, 20.9% up-mRNA/down-protein, and 16.6% down-mRNA/up-protein. mRNA-splicing/epigenetic-rRNA/translation pathways were up in both; metabolism of amino acids/fatty acids/cofactors was down in both; translational-control genes were up only at mRNA; complement/coagulation up only at protein ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- **Five recurrent GISTIC2 peaks harbor 19 cis-regulated dysregulated genes.** Of 29 cis-copy-number-regulated genes (CNA-mRNA and mRNA-protein Spearman ρ>0.5) inside enriched GISTIC2 regions, 19 were dysregulated at mRNA, 11 at protein, and 9 at both ([ATP6V1C1](../genes/ATP6V1C1.md), [BYSL](../genes/BYSL.md), [CHD1L](../genes/CHD1L.md), [NUDCD1](../genes/NUDCD1.md), [LRRC47](../genes/LRRC47.md), [RRM2B](../genes/RRM2B.md), [UBQLN4](../genes/UBQLN4.md), [XPO5](../genes/XPO5.md), [YWHAZ](../genes/YWHAZ.md)). The five peaks span 1p36.23 (del), 1q21.3 (amp), 6p21.2 (amp), 8q22.2 (amp), 8q24.13 (amp) ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- **Seven significantly mutated genes** by MutSigCV and OncodriveFML: [ALB](../genes/ALB.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [AXIN1](../genes/AXIN1.md), [CDKN1A](../genes/CDKN1A.md), [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), and **[GPAM](../genes/GPAM.md)** (7.4% in this cohort vs 1.8% in a prior meta-analysis, Fisher p=0.001). 7 of 9 [GPAM](../genes/GPAM.md) mutations were frameshift, and knockdown of [GPAM](../genes/GPAM.md) significantly increased proliferation in HepG2 and Huh7 cell lines — together strongly suggestive of a tumor-suppressor role ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- **[CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations are mutually exclusive (Fisher p=0.012).** [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md)-mutant tumors were lower-grade and immune-desert (p=0.039); [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)-mutant tumors were higher-grade (p=0.001) and HBV-associated (p=0.010). Multivariate Cox analysis (independent of BCLC) flagged [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [GPAM](../genes/GPAM.md), [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md), and [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) mutations as associated with poor overall survival ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- **Phosphoproteome reveals activated [AURKA](../genes/AURKA.md), CDKs, ERK1/2, [PLK1](../genes/PLK1.md).** Differential phosphorylation analysis identified 692/648 hyper-/hypo-phosphorylated sites (adjusted p≤0.05, |log2FC|>1) and 302/355 protein-normalized hyper-/hypo sites. KSEA showed increased activity of [AURKA](../genes/AURKA.md), [CDK1](../genes/CDK1.md)/[CDK2](../genes/CDK2.md)/[CDK5](../genes/CDK5.md)/[CDK7](../genes/CDK7.md), ERK1/2 ([MAPK1](../genes/MAPK1.md)/[MAPK3](../genes/MAPK3.md)), and [PLK1](../genes/PLK1.md), and decreased activity of PKACA/G ([PRKACA](../genes/PRKACA.md)/[PRKACG](../genes/PRKACG.md)), PKCA/Z ([PRKCA](../genes/PRKCA.md)/[PRKCZ](../genes/PRKCZ.md)), and [SGK1](../genes/SGK1.md) ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- **CTNNB1-mutant HCCs have altered actin-organization phosphorylation.** Of 23 differentially expressed proteins, 13 also differed at mRNA, including [GLUL](../genes/GLUL.md), [AMACR](../genes/AMACR.md), and [ACSS3](../genes/ACSS3.md); 189 phosphosites differing from CTNNB1-WT (without protein-level difference) were enriched for actin-filament organization. Hyperphosphorylated sites included Par3-α ([PARD3](../genes/PARD3.md)) S1046 and [PDLIM2](../genes/PDLIM2.md) S197; hypophosphorylation at [MAP1B](../genes/MAP1B.md) S541/S937/S1396 and ACF7 ([MACF1](../genes/MACF1.md)) S1752 — consistent with GSK3 inactivation in Wnt signaling. Epithelial markers [CDH1](../genes/CDH1.md) (E-cadherin) and [KRT19](../genes/KRT19.md) were reduced, and KSEA showed increased AMPKα2 and decreased CK II / [CDK5](../genes/CDK5.md) activity ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- **TP53-mutant HCCs show altered lipid-metabolism phosphorylation and CDK1/2/5 activity.** 399 differentially-expressed proteins (238 also at mRNA); [STMN2](../genes/STMN2.md) and [NCOR1](../genes/NCOR1.md) overexpressed at protein but underexpressed at mRNA; 12 proteins (including [CENPF](../genes/CENPF.md), [TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md), [KNTC1](../genes/KNTC1.md)) underexpressed at protein but overexpressed at mRNA. PKR/[EIF2AK2](../genes/EIF2AK2.md) S83 autophosphorylation was elevated, and KSEA showed increased [CDK1](../genes/CDK1.md)/[CDK2](../genes/CDK2.md)/[CDK5](../genes/CDK5.md), [AURKA](../genes/AURKA.md), [TTK](../genes/TTK.md), [NEK2](../genes/NEK2.md), PKR ([EIF2AK2](../genes/EIF2AK2.md)), [PRKD2](../genes/PRKD2.md), p38 delta ([MAPK13](../genes/MAPK13.md)) ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- **Three integrative iCluster subtypes recapitulated by SNF.** Cluster 1: ECM organization, angiogenesis, amino-acid metabolism. Cluster 2: fatty-acid/amino-acid/small-molecule metabolism (low grade, [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) enriched, improved [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md), p=0.015). Cluster 3: cell proliferation, chromatin assembly, RNA processing, CDK1/2/6 activity (high grade, TP53 enriched, macrovascular invasion p=0.01, poor [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) p=0.005). Integrative clusters were associated with mutation, transcriptome and proteome single-omics clusterings (all p≤0.001) but not with CNA or phosphoproteome clusters ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- **Two proteome and two phosphoproteome subclasses.** Proteome subclass 1 (ribonucleoprotein organization, mRNA splicing/processing) was associated with high grade/high stage and poor OS (p=0.022); proteome subclass 2 (metabolism, oxidative phosphorylation) with improved OS. Phosphoproteome subclasses (subclass 1: ribonucleoprotein/mRNA-splicing/chromatin/CK II alpha; subclass 2: telomere maintenance/DNA repair/actin cytoskeleton/PKA C-alpha) did NOT associate with single-omics or integrative clusters, attributed to the dynamic, noisier nature of phosphoproteomic snapshots ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).

## Genes & alterations

- [GPAM](../genes/GPAM.md) — newly nominated significantly mutated gene (7.4% vs 1.8% prior meta-analysis, Fisher p=0.001); 7/9 mutations frameshift; HepG2 and Huh7 knockdown increased proliferation → candidate HCC tumor suppressor; poor-OS association in multivariate Cox independent of BCLC ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) — mutually exclusive with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (p=0.012); lower grade (p=0.017); immune-desert (p=0.039); good prognosis (p=0.025 multivariate). Mutant HCCs show altered phosphorylation of [PARD3](../genes/PARD3.md), [PDLIM2](../genes/PDLIM2.md), [MAP1B](../genes/MAP1B.md), and [MACF1](../genes/MACF1.md), consistent with loss of epithelial polarity and Wnt-driven cytoskeletal remodeling; KSEA shows increased AMPKα2 and decreased CK II / [CDK5](../genes/CDK5.md) activity. Wnt target genes [NKD1](../genes/NKD1.md), [AXIN2](../genes/AXIN2.md), [RNF43](../genes/RNF43.md), [ALDH3A1](../genes/ALDH3A1.md) differ at mRNA but not at protein in CTNNB1 mutants ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — higher grade (p=0.001); HBV-associated (p=0.010); poor prognosis (p=0.034 multivariate, independent of BCLC). Mutant HCCs show altered phosphorylation in lipid- and mRNA-metabolism proteins; increased [CDK1](../genes/CDK1.md)/[CDK2](../genes/CDK2.md)/[CDK5](../genes/CDK5.md), [AURKA](../genes/AURKA.md), [TTK](../genes/TTK.md), [NEK2](../genes/NEK2.md), [EIF2AK2](../genes/EIF2AK2.md), [PRKD2](../genes/PRKD2.md), and p38δ ([MAPK13](../genes/MAPK13.md)) activity by KSEA; PKR ([EIF2AK2](../genes/EIF2AK2.md)) S83 activating autophosphorylation elevated ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [AXIN1](../genes/AXIN1.md), [CDKN1A](../genes/CDKN1A.md), [ALB](../genes/ALB.md) — SMGs identified in this cohort consistent with prior HCC genomic surveys ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md), [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) — non-SMG cancer genes mutated in ≥3 HCCs; associated with poor OS independent of BCLC in multivariate Cox ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) — previously established as the most frequently mutated gene in HCC (promoter); referenced as a known driver but explicitly excluded from this study's SMG analysis ([TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter not captured by WES) ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- [APOB](../genes/APOB.md), [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — non-SMG cancer genes mutated in ≥3 HCCs and analyzed for clinicopathological correlation; [APOB](../genes/APOB.md) and TP53 mutations were associated with lower mRNA and protein expression of the cognate gene ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- [CHD1L](../genes/CHD1L.md) (1q21.3 amplification peak), [YWHAZ](../genes/YWHAZ.md) (14-3-3 zeta, 8q22.2) — known cancer genes among cis-driver candidates ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- [NUDCD1](../genes/NUDCD1.md) (OVA66, 8q22.2) — promotes EMT and apoptosis evasion via PI3K/AKT, ERK1/2-MAPK, IGF-1R-MAPK; cis-driver candidate ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- [UBQLN4](../genes/UBQLN4.md) (1q21.3) — regulates Wnt-β-catenin pathway; associated with genomic instability; reported to repress homologous recombination repair and confer PARP1-inhibitor sensitivity, suggesting a therapeutic angle ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- [BYSL](../genes/BYSL.md) (6p21.1) — direct cMYC target; required for nucleologenesis in HCC proliferation; promotes glioma/glioblastoma via GSK-3β/β-catenin and AKT/mTOR ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- [ATP6V1C1](../genes/ATP6V1C1.md) (8q22.3) — V-ATPase subunit with roles in Wnt/β-catenin, Notch, mTOR signaling and migration; role in HCC unclear but suggested by cis-driver pattern ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- [RRM2B](../genes/RRM2B.md) (p53R2, 8q22.3) — p53 target / DNA damage and replication stress regulator; conflicting reports of tumor-suppressor vs oncogenic roles; nominated as [gemcitabine](../drugs/gemcitabine.md) target in hypoxic conditions ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- [XPO5](../genes/XPO5.md), [LRRC47](../genes/LRRC47.md) — cis-driver candidates dysregulated at both mRNA and protein levels ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- [AURKA](../genes/AURKA.md), [CDK1](../genes/CDK1.md), [CDK2](../genes/CDK2.md), [CDK5](../genes/CDK5.md), [CDK7](../genes/CDK7.md), [PLK1](../genes/PLK1.md), [MAPK1](../genes/MAPK1.md), [MAPK3](../genes/MAPK3.md) — kinases with increased activity by KSEA in HCC vs normal liver; flagged as targetable, with [AURKA](../genes/AURKA.md) and CDK1/2/5 highlighted ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- [PRKACA](../genes/PRKACA.md), [PRKACG](../genes/PRKACG.md), [PRKCA](../genes/PRKCA.md), [PRKCZ](../genes/PRKCZ.md), [SGK1](../genes/SGK1.md) — kinases with decreased activity by KSEA in HCC vs normal liver ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- [PARD3](../genes/PARD3.md), [PDLIM2](../genes/PDLIM2.md), [MAP1B](../genes/MAP1B.md), [MACF1](../genes/MACF1.md) — actin-organization phosphoproteins differentially phosphorylated in CTNNB1-mutant HCCs ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- [GLUL](../genes/GLUL.md), [AMACR](../genes/AMACR.md), [ACSS3](../genes/ACSS3.md) — proteins concordantly up at mRNA and protein in CTNNB1-mutant HCCs ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- [STMN2](../genes/STMN2.md), [NCOR1](../genes/NCOR1.md) — up at protein but down at mRNA in TP53-mutant HCCs ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- [CENPF](../genes/CENPF.md), [TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md), [KNTC1](../genes/KNTC1.md) — mitotic-spindle regulators down at protein but up at mRNA in TP53-mutant HCCs ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- [TNRC6B](../genes/TNRC6B.md) (β-catenin-independent Wnt signaling), [PRKCE](../genes/PRKCE.md) (β-catenin binding partner), [PPIE](../genes/PPIE.md) (spliceosome / FAST lncRNA / β-catenin axis) — among the 10 CTNNB1-mutant differentially-expressed proteins NOT also differential at mRNA, implicating post-transcriptional regulation ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- [EIF2AK2](../genes/EIF2AK2.md) (PKR) — S83 activating autophosphorylation elevated in TP53-mutant HCCs; involved in stress response and HCV/HBV signaling ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- [TTK](../genes/TTK.md), [NEK2](../genes/NEK2.md), [PRKD2](../genes/PRKD2.md), [MAPK13](../genes/MAPK13.md), [GSK3B](../genes/GSK3B.md) — additional kinases with elevated activity in TP53-mutant or overall HCC by KSEA ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- [PYCR2](../genes/PYCR2.md), [ADH1A](../genes/ADH1A.md) — cited from prior HBV-HCC proteogenomics as prognostic biomarkers; framing for the current study ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- [SOAT1](../genes/SOAT1.md) — cited from prior HBV-HCC work where avasimibe-mediated [SOAT1](../genes/SOAT1.md) inhibition reduced PDX tumor size; context for the current study ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).

## Clinical implications

- **Aurora Kinase A and CDK inhibitors as candidate HCC therapeutics.** Authors note preclinical evidence of efficacy in HCC models, potential synergy with [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md)/[regorafenib](../drugs/regorafenib.md) and chemotherapeutic agents, and that CDK1/2/5/7 plus [AURKA](../genes/AURKA.md) emerge as the dominant elevated kinase activities by KSEA ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- **CDK4/6 inhibition for integrative cluster 3.** iCluster 3 was characterized by elevated cell-cycle signatures and CDK1/2/6 activity; CDK4/6 are well-established therapeutic targets, suggesting drug-target stratification in this subset ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- **Wnt signaling as a target in integrative cluster 2.** Cluster 2 was enriched for [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) mutants and improved OS; Wnt-pathway intervention is suggested as a candidate therapeutic axis in this subset ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- **[PARP1](../genes/PARP1.md) inhibition for [UBQLN4](../genes/UBQLN4.md)-overexpressing tumors.** [UBQLN4](../genes/UBQLN4.md) was reported to repress homologous recombination and confer PARP1-inhibitor sensitivity, suggesting a therapeutic angle for UBQLN4-driven HCCs ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- **[gemcitabine](../drugs/gemcitabine.md) for [RRM2B](../genes/RRM2B.md)-elevated tumors.** [RRM2B](../genes/RRM2B.md) encodes a ribonucleotide-reductase subunit that may be targeted by [gemcitabine](../drugs/gemcitabine.md), particularly in hypoxic conditions ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- **Prognostic mutation signatures independent of BCLC.** Multivariate Cox analysis identified [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [GPAM](../genes/GPAM.md), [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md), [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) mutations and TP53-mutation cluster 3 as poor-prognosis markers, and CTNNB1-mutation cluster 2 as good-prognosis — independent of standard BCLC clinical staging ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- **Subtype-specific therapeutic stratification.** Integrative cluster 3 (poor OS, macrovascular invasion, TP53, CDK1/2/6 activity) is the closest analog to the "proliferation" subclass described in prior HBV-HCC proteogenomic studies; cluster 2 (good OS, metabolic, CTNNB1) maps to "metabolic" subclasses — suggesting cross-cohort generalizability of stratified therapy ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- **Avasimibe/[alisertib](../drugs/alisertib.md) precedent.** Authors cite prior HBV-HCC work where [SOAT1](../genes/SOAT1.md) inhibition by avasimibe reduced PDX tumor size in cholesterol-homeostasis-disrupted HCCs; explicitly framed as motivation for proteome-driven target nomination, but avasimibe is not directly tested here ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Phosphoproteome subclasses do not align with mutation/transcriptome/proteome subclasses or integrative clusters.** Authors interpret this as evidence that phosphorylation captures a transient dynamic snapshot, inherently noisier than other molecular layers ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- **Heterogeneous cohort accrual over a decade (2008–2018) during which clinical practice evolved** — overall-survival biomarker interpretation is therefore limited; multivariate adjustment for BCLC is performed but treatment-era differences are not modeled ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- **(Phospho)proteomics performed on 51/122 tumors only**, although the subset was representative of the full cohort across clinicopathological and molecular parameters. Proteome and phosphoproteome data are inherently sparse — not all proteins/phosphosites can be detected ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- **CTNNB1- and TP53-mutation phospho-signatures are correlative.** Mutagenesis experiments would be required to properly attribute the observed phosphorylation changes to those mutations ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- **GPAM frameshift functional dissection incomplete.** The seven frameshift variants and the knockdown-induced proliferation phenotype together suggest tumor-suppressor function, but individual variant impact and the full mechanistic basis remain to be characterized ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- **Contrast with prior HBV-HCC proteogenomics.** Authors did NOT observe the elevated ALDOA S36 phosphorylation reported in HBV-associated CTNNB1-mutant HCCs (FDR=0.98 in this cohort) — possibly reflecting etiology or methodological differences ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- **Cohort is European, single-institution.** Most prior HCC proteogenomic studies were HBV-enriched East Asian cohorts; cross-population generalizability of the integrative subtypes requires external validation ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
- **Reference genome GRCh37**; while standard for WES/RNA-seq at the time, downstream re-integration with GRCh38-based resources may require liftover ([PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)).
