# MAN1A2

## Overview

MAN1A2 (Mannosidase Alpha Class 1A Member 2) encodes a Golgi-resident alpha-1,2-mannosidase involved in N-glycan processing during protein maturation. Fusions involving MAN1A2 have been reported in sarcomas, suggesting it can participate in oncogenic rearrangements that disrupt normal protein glycosylation pathways.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- MAN1A2-ACBD6 fusion — undescribed fusion identified by cWGTS (comprehensive whole-genome and transcriptome sequencing) in an undifferentiated sarcoma patient in a feasibility study of cWGTS in pediatric and young adult cancers (n=29 various tumor types); not detected by standard clinical panel sequencing (MSK-IMPACT/MSK-Fusion) [PMID:35585047](../papers/35585047.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **Undifferentiated sarcoma / pediatric cancers**: MAN1A2 fusion partner in a novel MAN1A2-ACBD6 rearrangement discovered by cWGTS that was missed by standard panels [PMID:35585047](../papers/35585047.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Not reported in this corpus entry [PMID:35585047](../papers/35585047.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic implication reported. The MAN1A2-ACBD6 fusion is a novel finding whose functional significance and therapeutic actionability are not yet established [PMID:35585047](../papers/35585047.md).

## Open questions

- Functional consequence of MAN1A2-ACBD6 fusion on Golgi function and oncogenesis is unknown; whether this fusion is recurrent in undifferentiated sarcoma requires further study [PMID:35585047](../papers/35585047.md).
