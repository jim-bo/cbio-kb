# OSMR

## Overview

OSMR (Oncostatin M Receptor) is the receptor for the IL-6 family cytokine Oncostatin M ([OSM](../genes/OSM.md)). In diffuse glioma, OSMR is expressed by differentiated-like neoplastic cells and mediates [OSM](../genes/OSM.md) signaling from myeloid cells, driving mesenchymal subtype transition. The OSM-OSMR ligand-receptor pair is the top correlate of mesenchymal transition in recurrent IDHwt glioma identified by deconvolution analysis and confirmed by multiplex immunofluorescence in the GLASS longitudinal cohort.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- OSMR expressed by differentiated-like neoplastic cells; together with myeloid-expressed [OSM](../genes/OSM.md), constitutes the top ligand-receptor correlate of mesenchymal subtype transition in recurrent IDHwt diffuse glioma; spatial co-localization confirmed by multiplex immunofluorescence (GLASS cohort, n=304 patients) [PMID:35649412](../papers/35649412.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [DIFG](../cancer_types/DIFG.md) / [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md): OSMR expression on neoplastic cells mediates OSM-driven mesenchymal transition in recurrent IDHwt glioma [PMID:35649412](../papers/35649412.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- OSMR (neoplastic-expressed) co-localizes spatially with OSM (myeloid-expressed) in mesenchymal regions of recurrent IDHwt glioma [PMID:35649412](../papers/35649412.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- OSM/OSMR axis nominated as a candidate therapeutic target for interrupting mesenchymal transition in recurrent IDHwt glioma [PMID:35649412](../papers/35649412.md)

## Open questions

- Directionality of the OSM-OSMR axis (myeloid-driven mesenchymal induction vs. mesenchymal-recruited myeloid response) remains unresolved [PMID:35649412](../papers/35649412.md)
