# Pheochromocytoma and Paraganglioma / PCPG (TCGA)

## Overview

PCPG is the TCGA cohort identifier for pheochromocytoma and paraganglioma. The closest OncoTree equivalents are [PHC](PHC.md) (Pheochromocytoma) and [PGNG](PGNG.md) (Paraganglioma). PCPG tumors have a high rate of hereditary predisposition (SDH subunits, [RET](../genes/RET.md), [VHL](../genes/VHL.md), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), [MAX](../genes/MAX.md)) and frequent fusion-driven oncogenesis.

## Cohorts in the corpus

- TCGA PCPG cohort: included as one of 33 cancer types in the pan-cancer fusion landscape study; subset of the PanCancer Atlas ([pcpg_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/pcpg_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md)).

## Recurrent alterations

- Pan-cancer fusion study (9,624 TCGA samples) found PCPG has a median of 0 fusions per sample; TSG-partner fusion under-expression was highest in PCPG (38% of TSG-partner fusions), suggesting TSG inactivation via fusion is a dominant mechanism in PCPG [PMID:29617662](../papers/29617662.md).

## Subtypes

- SDH-mutant cluster (pseudohypoxia): [SDHB](../genes/SDHB.md), [SDHD](../genes/SDHD.md), [SDHC](../genes/SDHC.md), VHL, [EPAS1](../genes/EPAS1.md) mutations.
- Kinase-signaling cluster: RET, NF1, [TMEM127](../genes/TMEM127.md), MAX mutations.
