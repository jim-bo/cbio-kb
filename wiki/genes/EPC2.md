# EPC2

## Overview

EPC2 (Enhancer of Polycomb Homolog 2) encodes a component of the NuA4/TIP60 histone acetyltransferase complex involved in chromatin remodeling and transcriptional regulation. In the pediatric cancer context, EPC2 has been identified as a novel fusion partner in undifferentiated sarcoma.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- EPC2–AFF3 fusion identified by comprehensive WGS+transcriptome sequencing (cWGTS) in an undifferentiated sarcoma patient; described as an undescribed fusion in a pediatric/rare cancer cohort (n=114, MSK prospective study NCT01775072); detected by integrated WGS+RNA-seq but not by standard panel testing [PMID:35585047](../papers/35585047.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **Undifferentiated sarcoma**: EPC2–AFF3 fusion identified as a novel undescribed fusion event [PMID:35585047](../papers/35585047.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Fusion partner AFF3 is an AF4/FMR2 family transcriptional elongation factor; the EPC2–AFF3 fusion joins a chromatin-remodeling component with a transcriptional elongation regulator [PMID:35585047](../papers/35585047.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No established therapeutic implications for the EPC2–AFF3 fusion; discovery by cWGTS highlights the added value of WGS over panel sequencing for novel fusion detection in rare pediatric sarcomas [PMID:35585047](../papers/35585047.md)

## Open questions

- Functional consequence and oncogenic mechanism of the EPC2–AFF3 fusion in undifferentiated sarcoma remain to be characterized [PMID:35585047](../papers/35585047.md)
