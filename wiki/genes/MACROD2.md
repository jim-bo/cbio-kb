# MACROD2

## Overview

MACROD2 (Mono-ADP-Ribosylhydrolase 2) encodes a hydrolase that removes ADP-ribose modifications from proteins. In cancer genomics, MACROD2 has attracted attention primarily as a recurrently rearranged locus in melanoma whole-genome sequencing studies. Its precise oncogenic role remains under investigation.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- MACROD2 is recurrently rearranged in 5/25 metastatic melanomas by whole-genome sequencing; among the most frequently rearranged loci in the discovery cohort [PMID:22622578](../papers/22622578.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md) (Melanoma):** Structural rearrangements affecting MACROD2 detected in 5/25 WGS discovery tumors [PMID:22622578](../papers/22622578.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

## Therapeutic relevance

- No targeted therapies against MACROD2 are currently described in the corpus.

## Open questions
