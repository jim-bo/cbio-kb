# CDK5RAP2

## Overview

CDK5RAP2 (CDK5 Regulatory Subunit Associated Protein 2) encodes a centrosomal protein involved in mitotic spindle regulation and cell division. In cancer genomics, CDK5RAP2 is notable as a fusion partner for [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), forming the CDK5RAP2-BRAF rearrangement that acts as an oncogenic driver in melanoma. This fusion was identified in the pan-cancer MSK-IMPACT cohort as a novel recurrent event, expanding the landscape of BRAF fusion partners beyond previously characterized genes.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- A novel recurrent [CDK5RAP2](../genes/CDK5RAP2.md)-[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) fusion was identified in two melanoma cases from the [msk_impact_2017](../datasets/msk_impact_2017.md) cohort (10,945 tumors across 62 tumor types profiled by [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md)); the fusion involves BRAF as the 3' partner and is predicted to drive RAS-independent BRAF dimerization [PMID:28481359](../papers/28481359.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[MEL](../cancer_types/MEL.md):** CDK5RAP2-BRAF fusion detected in 2 melanoma cases in the [msk_impact_2017](../datasets/msk_impact_2017.md) dataset; one of 33 BRAF fusions across 11 principal tumor types comprising 18 distinct partner genes (10 novel) [PMID:28481359](../papers/28481359.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- CDK5RAP2-BRAF fusions occurred in melanomas lacking V600E mutations, consistent with acting as alternative BRAF-activating drivers; the broader class of BRAF intragenic deletions and fusions are predicted to drive RAS-independent dimerization [PMID:28481359](../papers/28481359.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- CDK5RAP2-BRAF fusions, like other BRAF fusions predicted to drive RAS-independent dimerization, may benefit from RAF-dimer inhibitors; this connection was noted in the context of 71% radiographic clinical-benefit rate for BRAF-directed therapy in non-melanoma BRAF-altered patients [PMID:28481359](../papers/28481359.md).

## Open questions

- The clinical actionability of CDK5RAP2-BRAF specifically (versus the broader class of BRAF fusions) has not been prospectively tested; only 2 cases identified in the discovery cohort [PMID:28481359](../papers/28481359.md).
