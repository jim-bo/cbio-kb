# MSLN

## Overview

MSLN (mesothelin) is a cell-surface glycoprotein normally expressed in mesothelial cells and over-expressed in several malignancies including mesothelioma, ovarian, and pancreatic cancers. In the context of colorectal precancer, MSLN was identified as a co-expressed component of the gastric/endodermal transcriptional program in sessile serrated lesion cells (SSCs), alongside other markers of the serrated polyp-to-MSI-H CRC pathway.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- MSLN co-expressed with [AQP5](../genes/AQP5.md), [TACSTD2](../genes/TACSTD2.md) (TROP2), and [TFF2](../genes/TFF2.md) as part of the gastric/endodermal program defining sessile serrated lesion cells (SSCs) in the COLON MAP/HTAN multi-omic colorectal polyp atlas [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Colorectal cancer ([COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md)): MSLN expression marks the gastric-type metaplastic SSC state that precedes hypermutation and MSI-H CRC development [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-expressed with [MDK](../genes/MDK.md), AQP5, TACSTD2 (TROP2), TFF2 in SSCs; the broader SSC program also includes [MUC5AC](../genes/MUC5AC.md), CDX2-low, and MUC2/TFF3 goblet-cell retention [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting reported in the corpus for the colorectal serrated pathway context.

## Open questions

- Whether MSLN surface expression in SSCs or SSLs could serve as an IHC marker for the serrated pathway has not been evaluated.
