# MX2

## Overview

MX2 (MX Dynamin Like GTPase 2) is an interferon-gamma ([IFNG](../genes/IFNG.md))-stimulated gene that encodes a dynamin-like GTPase involved in innate antiviral immunity. In cancer biology, MX2 expression is upregulated upon [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) inhibition in urothelial carcinoma cell lines, making it a readout for FGFR3-mediated immune evasion. MX2 belongs to a set of IFNG-response genes that are suppressed by activated FGFR3 signaling, potentially contributing to T-cell exclusion in luminal-papillary urothelial tumors.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- MX2 (along with [BST2](../genes/BST2.md), [IRF9](../genes/IRF9.md), [GBP2](../genes/GBP2.md)) is an IFNG-response gene upregulated upon shRNA knockdown or pharmacologic inhibition of FGFR3 in urothelial carcinoma cell lines; proposed as a mechanistic link between FGFR3 activation and T-cell exclusion in luminal-papillary upper tract urothelial carcinoma ([UTUC](../cancer_types/UTUC.md)). [PMID:31278255](../papers/31278255.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Upper tract urothelial carcinoma (UTUC): MX2 expression is suppressed by FGFR3 signaling in T-cell-depleted luminal-papillary UTUC; restoration of MX2 expression upon FGFR3 inhibition supports a FGFR3-to-immune-exclusion mechanistic axis. [PMID:31278255](../papers/31278255.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- MX2 upregulation is linked to FGFR3 inhibition; in FGFR3-activated luminal-papillary UTUC, MX2 and other IFNG-response genes (BST2, IRF9, GBP2) are concordantly downregulated. [PMID:31278255](../papers/31278255.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Restoration of MX2 and co-regulated IFNG-response gene expression upon FGFR3 inhibition provides mechanistic rationale for combining FGFR3 inhibitors (e.g., [erdafitinib](../drugs/erdafitinib.md)) with immune checkpoint blockade in FGFR3-activated UTUC. [PMID:31278255](../papers/31278255.md)

## Open questions

- Whether MX2 expression levels in pre-treatment UTUC biopsies can serve as a predictive biomarker for FGFR3 inhibitor or combination therapy response has not been tested clinically.
