**PMID:** 27442865 · **DOI:** 10.1001/jamaoncol.2016.1790 · **Journal:** JAMA Oncology (2017)

## TL;DR

Morris et al. report the first large clinical-grade molecular landscape of recurrent and metastatic head and neck cancers, sequencing 151 advanced tumors at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center between January 2014 and July 2015 with the [MSK-IMPACT 410-gene panel](../methods/IMPACT410.md). Next-generation sequencing guided therapy in 21 of 151 patients (14%; 25% of [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md) cases), refined diagnoses in several rare cancer types (notably reclassifying acinic cell carcinomas to [MASC](../cancer_types/MASC.md) via [ETV6](../genes/ETV6.md)-[NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md) fusions), and revealed that recurrent/metastatic [HPV-positive](../themes/hpv-positive.md) [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md) frequently acquires an HPV-negative-like genotype enriched for [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation, whole-genome duplication and 3p deletion. Activating [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) mutations were enriched in metastatic adenoid cystic carcinoma ([ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md)), and [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter mutations were strikingly common in advanced HPV-negative HNSC, [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md), and [BCC](../cancer_types/BCC.md).

## Cohort & data

- **151 patients** (95 men, 56 women; mean age 61.8, range 17–100) with advanced, treatment-resistant head and neck tumors profiled at MSK between January 2014 and July 2015 (IRB protocol NCT01775072; 224 enrolled, 151 with ≥6-month follow-up).
- **Disease distribution:** 53 [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md), 56 salivary carcinomas (36 [ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md) plus 20 other salivary), 27 cutaneous carcinomas (21 [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md), 4 [BCC](../cancer_types/BCC.md), 2 skin adnexal), 8 [NPC](../cancer_types/NPC.md), 3 [HNNE](../cancer_types/HNNE.md), 2 odontogenic carcinomas ([AMBL](../cancer_types/AMBL.md)/[CCOC](../cancer_types/CCOC.md)), and 2 olfactory neuroblastomas ([ONBL](../cancer_types/ONBL.md)).
- **Site distribution:** 66 patients with locoregional recurrence, 106 with distant metastases, 6 with locally advanced surgically unresectable tumors.
- **Assay:** [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) targeted NGS of 410 cancer-relevant genes ([IMPACT410](../methods/IMPACT410.md)) plus genome-wide copy number, with matched normal DNA; median coverage 600× (range 82×–1165×); CLIA-approved.
- **Dataset:** publicly released as [hnc_mskcc_2016](../datasets/hnc_mskcc_2016.md) (cBioPortal study).
- **Analyses:** [FACETS](../methods/facets.md) for allele-specific copy number and clonality; an SVM classifier trained on TCGA HNSC for HPV-status genotyping; [mutational signature](../methods/mutational-signatures.md) decomposition (APOBEC, tobacco, UV light); enrichment comparison versus the [TCGA HNSC primary cohort](../datasets/hnsc_tcga_pub.md) and primary [ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md) sequencing from Ho et al. 2013.

## Key findings

- **Therapy guidance.** NGS guided therapy in **21/151 patients (14%)** overall and **13/53 (25%)** with [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md) ([PMID:27442865](../papers/27442865.md)).
- **Actionable alterations.** **28/135 patients (21%)** harbored alterations with potential investigational implications: 7 (5%) with an FDA-approved biomarker–drug pair in another indication, 21 (16%) with clinical evidence linking the alteration to drug response.
- **HNSC landscape.** Whole-genome duplication in **12/48 cases (25%)** with informative copy number; mismatch-repair gene mutations in 4 tumors ([MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md) truncating, [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md)/[MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md)/[POLD1](../genes/POLD1.md) missense) carried significantly elevated mutation counts (17.3 vs 4.5; P < .001).
- **[TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter enrichment in advanced HPV-negative HNSC.** Mutations in **16/30 (53%)** HPV-negative tumors versus 12/70 (17%) primary HPV-negative tumors (OR 5.5; P < .001); strikingly **10/11 (91%)** HPV-negative tongue SCCs harbored [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter mutations. **Zero** TERT promoter mutations in HPV-positive HNSC, consistent with E6-driven telomerase activation as a parallel mechanism.
- **HPV+ versus HPV− differences attenuated in advanced disease.** No difference in mutation counts (4.4 vs 5.9; P = .42) or fraction of genome copy-number-altered (0.19 vs 0.18; P = .78) between HPV-positive (n = 21) and HPV-negative (n = 30) recurrent/metastatic HNSC, in contrast to primary tumors.
- **Genes enriched in HPV-negative recurrent/metastatic HNSC versus HPV-positive:** [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (72% vs 15%; P < .001), [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter (55% vs 0%; P = 9 × 10⁻⁵), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) mutation/deletion (37% vs 5%; P = .016).
- **"HPV-negative-like" HPV-positive recurrent/metastatic HNSC.** An SVM classifier trained on TCGA correctly categorized 29/31 (91%) HPV-negative MSK-IMPACT cases but only 12/21 (57%) HPV-positive cases (P = .007); 43% of advanced HPV-positive tumors had genotypes resembling primary HPV-negative tumors and trended toward worse survival (HR 2.3; P = .19).
- **Alterations enriched in recurrent/metastatic HPV-positive HNSC** versus TCGA primary HPV-positive: whole-genome duplication 6.7-fold (P = .024), 3p deletion 9.1-fold (P = .002), 3p deletion + [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) co-occurrence 7.6-fold (P = .03); higher [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation rate (15% vs 3%; enrichment 4.6, P = .11) and lower [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) mutation (10% vs 36%; enrichment 0.21, P = .06).
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md) in HPV-positive tumors is often subclonal:** 3 of 4 (75%) HPV-positive TP53 mutations were subclonal versus 4 of 27 (15%) HPV-negative (P = .03); all TP53 mutations in HPV-positive tumors were truncating.
- **Confirmation in cervical cancer.** TP53 mutation frequency markedly higher in MSK-IMPACT recurrent/metastatic [CESC](../cancer_types/CESC.md) (4/16, 25%) than primary TCGA cervix (5/170, 3%; OR 8.3; P = .008).
- **Chromosomal instability:** [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)/[RB1](../genes/RB1.md)-mutated HPV-positive tumors had significantly more copy-number-altered genome than wildtype tumors (P = .009).
- **[NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) enrichment in metastatic [ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md).** [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) altered in 12/33 (33%) of recurrent/metastatic [ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md); activating mutations or amplifications in 8/36 (22.2%) versus 2/60 (3.3%) in primary ACYC (OR 8.3; P = .005); three samples had recurrent truncating alterations at serine 2467, mirroring leukemia activating mutations.
- **TERT promoter mutations identified in 5 [ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md) (14%)** — the first report of TERT promoter mutations in salivary cancers.
- **Salivary duct carcinoma ([SDCA](../cancer_types/SDCA.md)) actionable findings:** [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md), and [AR](../genes/AR.md) alterations plus structural variants in [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) and [ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md).
- **MASC reclassification by [ETV6](../genes/ETV6.md)-[NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md) fusion** changed the diagnosis of 2 cases originally called acinic cell carcinoma; both subsequently enrolled on a TRK inhibitor basket study with major / near-complete responses.
- **Cutaneous carcinomas.** UV-light mutational signature in 18/21 (86%) [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md), associated with higher mutation counts (37.9 vs 4.3; P = .008); 3p deletion + [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) co-occurrence in 6/19 [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md) (32%) — the first report in cutaneous SCC. Three of 4 [BCC](../cancer_types/BCC.md) had UV signature; all 3 hedgehog-pathway-inhibitor-responsive BCCs carried [PTCH1](../genes/PTCH1.md) mutations.
- **Odontogenic carcinomas.** Both profiled cases had [EWSR1](../genes/EWSR1.md) gene fusions: an [EWSR1](../genes/EWSR1.md)-[FLI1](../genes/FLI1.md) fusion in an "ameloblastic carcinoma" reclassified as Ewing sarcoma with epithelial differentiation, and an [EWSR1](../genes/EWSR1.md)-[ATF1](../genes/ATF1.md) fusion in a clear cell odontogenic carcinoma ([CCOC](../cancer_types/CCOC.md)) — not previously reported in odontogenic tumors.
- **Germline finding.** One HNSC patient harbored a germline heterozygous [FANCA](../genes/FANCA.md) deletion plus a somatic [FANCA](../genes/FANCA.md) stopgain — a "double hit" with potential [cisplatin](../drugs/cisplatin.md) sensitivity.

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — mutated in 21/29 (72%) HPV-negative versus 3/20 (15%) HPV-positive recurrent/metastatic HNSC; all HPV-positive TP53 mutations truncating and predominantly subclonal; co-occurrence with 3p deletion enriched 7.6-fold in advanced HPV-positive HNSC; 25% of recurrent/metastatic [CESC](../cancer_types/CESC.md) (versus 3% TCGA primary).
- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) — promoter mutations in 53% of HPV-negative HNSC (91% of HPV-negative tongue SCC), 52% of [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md), 75% of [BCC](../cancer_types/BCC.md), and 14% of [ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md) (first report in salivary cancers); zero in HPV-positive HNSC.
- [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) — altered in 33% of [ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md) with activating mutations/amplifications in 22.2% (8.3-fold enriched versus primary ACYC); recurrent S2467 truncating events; nominates γ-secretase inhibitors.
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — mutated in 10% of recurrent/metastatic HPV-positive HNSC (down from 36% in primary HPV+); 4 HNSC and 1 [NPC](../cancer_types/NPC.md) and 1 [ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md) patient enrolled on PI3K-inhibitor trials; also altered in [SDCA](../cancer_types/SDCA.md).
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — alteration prompted PI3K-inhibitor trial enrollment.
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — mutation/deletion in 37% of HPV-negative versus 5% of HPV-positive recurrent/metastatic HNSC.
- [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) — mutated in 1 HNSC (treated on farnesyl-transferase-inhibitor trial); G13V in a mucoepidermoid carcinoma ([MUCC](../cancer_types/MUCC.md)) treated similarly; recurrent in [SDCA](../cancer_types/SDCA.md).
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — alterations in 3/20 (15%) recurrent/metastatic HPV-positive HNSC; co-mutation with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) associated with elevated chromosomal instability.
- [MAPK1](../genes/MAPK1.md) — mutation guided treatment with an ERK inhibitor on single-patient IND.
- [SMARCB1](../genes/SMARCB1.md) — deletion led to enrollment on an [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) inhibitor trial.
- [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) — mutation in 1 [ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md) patient treated with an FGFR inhibitor.
- [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md) — mutation in 1 [ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md) patient treated with PI3K inhibitor.
- [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) — amplification in 1 [ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md) patient treated with [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) inhibitor.
- [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md), [KDR](../genes/KDR.md), [KIT](../genes/KIT.md) — co-amplification in 4 [ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md) patients treated with [regorafenib](../drugs/regorafenib.md).
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md), [AR](../genes/AR.md) — actionable alterations in [SDCA](../cancer_types/SDCA.md).
- [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), [ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md) — interesting structural variants in [SDCA](../cancer_types/SDCA.md).
- [PTCH1](../genes/PTCH1.md) — mutations in 3 of 4 [BCC](../cancer_types/BCC.md), all responsive to hedgehog-pathway inhibitors before NGS.
- [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md), [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md), [POLD1](../genes/POLD1.md) — mismatch repair / proofreading defects in 4 hypermutated HNSC tumors.
- [ETV6](../genes/ETV6.md)-[NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md) — fusion identified in 2 tumors initially diagnosed as acinic cell carcinoma, reclassifying them as MASC; both responded to TRK inhibitor on basket trial.
- [EWSR1](../genes/EWSR1.md)-[FLI1](../genes/FLI1.md) — fusion in an odontogenic case reclassified as [ES](../cancer_types/ES.md) with epithelial differentiation.
- [EWSR1](../genes/EWSR1.md)-[ATF1](../genes/ATF1.md) — fusion in a clear cell odontogenic carcinoma ([CCOC](../cancer_types/CCOC.md)); novel in odontogenic tumors.
- [FANCA](../genes/FANCA.md) — germline heterozygous deletion + somatic stopgain in one HNSC patient (potential cisplatin-sensitivity biomarker).

## Clinical implications

- **Sequencing recurrent/metastatic disease matters:** the genetic profile of advanced head and neck tumors is distinct from primary disease (notably for [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter, [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) enrichment, and HPV-positive tumors drifting toward an HPV-negative-like genotype).
- **Basket studies are the principal vehicle** for therapeutic gain in this rare-cancer-rich population — basket trials accounted for half of clinical-trial enrollments in this cohort. Notable responses include MASC patients on TRK inhibitors and ACYC patients matched to PI3K, FGFR, MDM2, and γ-secretase inhibitors based on actionable alterations.
- **Diagnosis refinement:** NGS reclassified 2 acinic cell carcinomas as MASC (via [ETV6](../genes/ETV6.md)-[NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md)) and 1 ameloblastic carcinoma as [ES](../cancer_types/ES.md) (via [EWSR1](../genes/EWSR1.md)-[FLI1](../genes/FLI1.md)), enabling appropriate targeted/cytotoxic therapy choice.
- **Negative biomarker → immunotherapy:** in 6/53 (12%) HNSC patients, the lack of actionable alterations on MSK-IMPACT prompted enrollment on immunotherapy trials; one hypermutated [BCC](../cancer_types/BCC.md) (122 mutations) was directed to immunotherapy on the basis of mutation load.
- **Prognostic signal:** "HPV-negative-like" HPV-positive HNSC trended toward worse survival (HR 2.3; P = .19) — relevant to ongoing chemoradiotherapy de-escalation strategies in HPV-positive disease.
- **Hypothesis-generating predictive biomarkers** for immune checkpoint inhibitors enumerated by the authors: intratumor heterogeneity (low ITH), high mutational load, and tobacco/UV-light mutational signatures.
- **HNSC therapeutic landscape context:** the authors note that no new targeted therapies have been approved for HNSC since [cetuximab](../drugs/cetuximab.md) in 2006 and there are no validated predictive biomarkers — this work motivates routine NGS to expand options.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Single-institution cohort with modest sample sizes** for individual rare-cancer subtypes (e.g., 4 BCCs, 2 odontogenic carcinomas, 2 olfactory neuroblastomas), limiting broad conclusions; the authors call for multi-institutional pooling.
- **Targeted 410-gene panel** does not capture exome-wide mutational load with the resolution of WES; deep sequencing may artifactually inflate sensitivity for low-allele-fraction variants like [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter mutations.
- **Cross-cohort comparisons (MSK-IMPACT versus TCGA)** are confounded by differences in sequencing platform, depth, analytic pipeline, and patient selection — enrichment estimates require independent confirmation.
- **The "HPV-negative-like" subset of advanced HPV-positive HNSC (43%)** has only a trend toward poorer survival (P = .19); larger cohorts are needed to confirm prognostic significance and to test whether de-intensified therapy is appropriate.
- **TP53 mutations in HPV-positive tumors are often subclonal**, raising the question of whether they are late events driven by tobacco exposure or selected under treatment pressure — biology and timing are unresolved.
- **No standard-of-care biomarkers** exist for head and neck cancer; only 21% of cases harbored potentially actionable alterations, leaving the majority of patients without molecularly matched options.
- **HPV status was not available for all 53 HNSC cases** (51 with known HPV status); some downstream comparisons are limited by sample dropout.
- **Biological role of TERT promoter mutations in [ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md)** is undefined — first description in salivary cancer; clinical consequences unknown.
- **Generalizability of activating [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) mutations in [ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md)** as a predictive biomarker for γ-secretase inhibitors awaits prospective testing.
