# PRDM10

## Overview

PRDM10 (PR/SET Domain 10) encodes a transcriptional regulator of the PRDM family. In cancer genomics, it has been identified as a novel [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) (MLL1) fusion partner in therapy-related myeloid neoplasms arising after topoisomerase-II-targeted chemotherapy in pediatric solid tumor patients.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Identified as a novel KMT2A fusion partner (KMT2A-PRDM10) in a pediatric neuroblastoma patient who developed therapy-related [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md); associated with topoisomerase-II exposure and earlier leukemic transformation [PMID:35078859](../papers/35078859.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [NBL](../cancer_types/NBL.md) — KMT2A-PRDM10 fusion detected in a pediatric neuroblastoma patient undergoing surveillance who subsequently developed therapy-related AML; identified as part of a serial bone-marrow molecular surveillance study [PMID:35078859](../papers/35078859.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- KMT2A-PRDM10 fusion co-occurs with topoisomerase-II exposure history; KMT2A canonical-breakpoint rearrangements (including this novel partner) occurred in 6/17 transformation patients, associated with earlier transformation than TP53-driven cases [PMID:35078859](../papers/35078859.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- KMT2A-PRDM10 fusion-bearing therapy-related AML is detectable by RNA fusion capture prior to overt leukemia diagnosis, suggesting molecular surveillance utility in high-risk pediatric patients treated with topoisomerase-II inhibitors [PMID:35078859](../papers/35078859.md)

## Open questions

- The functional biology of the KMT2A-PRDM10 fusion and whether it confers distinct therapeutic sensitivity compared to other KMT2A fusion partners is unknown [PMID:35078859](../papers/35078859.md)
