# Myeloid Neoplasm (MNM)

## Overview

Myeloid Neoplasm (MNM) is a broad OncoTree category (level 2, parent: MYELOID) encompassing [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md), [MDS](../cancer_types/MDS.md), [CMML](../cancer_types/CMML.md), and related myeloid disorders. In the corpus it is used specifically to group the spectrum of hematologic malignancies that arise concomitantly with primary mediastinal non-seminomatous germ cell tumor (PM [NSGCT](../cancer_types/NSGCT.md)), including AML (53%, nearly half [AMKL](../cancer_types/AMKL.md) M7), MDS, CMML, and histiocytic sarcoma ([HS](../cancer_types/HS.md)).

## Cohorts in the corpus

- [gct_msk_2020](../datasets/gct_msk_2020.md) — 15 PM NSGCT patients with concomitant myeloid neoplasm, sequenced with MSK-IMPACT and WES on a 5-patient subset at MSKCC (1998–2019). [PMID:32897884](../papers/32897884.md)

## Recurrent alterations

- Hematologic malignancies (MNM) arising in PM NSGCT patients included AML (53%, nearly half AMKL M7), MDS, CMML, and histiocytic sarcoma (HS); one-third of patients developed multiple distinct hematologic subtypes. [PMID:32897884](../papers/32897884.md)
- All MNM in PM NSGCT patients shared [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and/or RAS-pathway ([KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md), [RRAS2](../genes/RRAS2.md)) truncal mutations plus i(12p) with the paired [GCT](../cancer_types/GCT.md), defining a germ-cell-lineage origin. Canonical myeloid drivers ([FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md), [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md), [TET2](../genes/TET2.md), [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md)) were absent in all cases. [PMID:32897884](../papers/32897884.md)
- Median survival of PM NSGCT patients with concomitant MNM was 6.3 months (95% CI 4.6–25.2); only 1 of 15 patients was alive at analysis. [PMID:32897884](../papers/32897884.md)

## Subtypes

- **GCT-associated myeloid neoplasm** — AML, MDS, CMML, and HS arising from a shared germ-cell precursor with PM NSGCT; carries i(12p) and truncal TP53/RAS drivers; distinct from therapy-related MN and clonal hematopoiesis. [PMID:32897884](../papers/32897884.md)

## Therapeutic landscape

- Standard AML/MDS therapy is largely ineffective due to high TP53 burden and absent targetable drivers; median survival <6 months. Autologous SCT products were not contaminated with clonal hematopoiesis in any of 5 tested PM GCT patients, so relapse MNM is not attributable to mobilized CHIP. [PMID:32897884](../papers/32897884.md)
