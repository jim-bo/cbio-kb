# B4GALT1

## Overview

B4GALT1 (beta-1,4-galactosyltransferase 1) encodes a Golgi-localized glycosyltransferase that transfers galactose in a beta-1,4 linkage to GlcNAc-terminating oligosaccharides, contributing to N-linked and O-linked glycoprotein biosynthesis. In cancer, B4GALT1 is implicated in altered cell-surface glycosylation patterns that promote immune evasion, invasion, and therapeutic resistance. Its upregulation in pancreatic cancer has been highlighted as a candidate therapeutic target via sialyl-/fucosyltransferase inhibition strategies.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Elevated at the protein level in PDAC vs paired normal adjacent tissue (NAT) in CPTAC PDAC proteogenomics (140 treatment-naive tumors); elevation validated by DIA mass spectrometry. Protein-level upregulation was not mirrored at the transcript level, suggesting post-transcriptional or post-translational regulation. Listed alongside [ST6GAL1](../genes/ST6GAL1.md), [ST3GAL1](../genes/ST3GAL1.md), [FUT3](../genes/FUT3.md), [FUT11](../genes/FUT11.md), [B4GALT4](../genes/B4GALT4.md), B3GALT5, and ST6GALNAC1 as dysregulated glycosylation enzymes. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **PDAC ([PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md)):** Protein upregulation observed in CPTAC cohort (n=140); proposed as a target for glycan-capping blockade, particularly sialylation inhibition. Authors caution that systemic sialylation blockade (e.g., 3F-NeuAc) caused liver/kidney toxicity in mouse models, underscoring need for selective inhibitors. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-elevated with FUT3, FUT11, B4GALT4, ST6GAL1, ST3GAL1 in PDAC; FUT3 upregulation enriched in C1 (classical) subtype, suggesting subtype-tailored glycan-capping blockade strategies. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Sialyl-/fucosyltransferase inhibition (targeting B4GALT1 alongside ST6GAL1, ST3GAL1, FUT3, FUT11) proposed as a therapeutic strategy in PDAC based on CPTAC proteogenomic evidence; selectivity challenge remains given on-target toxicity in preclinical models. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Open questions

- Systemic galactosyltransferase inhibition has potential off-target toxicity; selective B4GALT1 inhibitors have not yet been developed.
- Mechanistic basis of protein-only (not transcript-level) upregulation of B4GALT1 in PDAC is unresolved.
