# PPP1R1A

## Overview

PPP1R1A (Protein Phosphatase 1 Regulatory Subunit 1A, also known as DARPP-32 in brain) is a phosphoprotein inhibitor of protein phosphatase 1 (PP1). In cancer genomics it has been identified with recurrent 3′UTR mutations in metastatic cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma, representing a candidate non-coding biomarker.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Recurrent 3′UTR mutation (48%) in metastatic [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md) WGS cohort (n=25); unique missense variant c.*491C>T in 5/25 samples; proposed as candidate non-coding biomarker of metastatic CSCC [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)
- Structural variant (LINX/PURPLE) detected in metastatic CSCC WGS cohort (n=25) [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **CSCC (cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma):** 3′UTR mutations in 48% and structural variant in metastatic nodal WGS cohort [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- 3′UTR co-mutated with [EVC](../genes/EVC.md) and [ABCA4](../genes/ABCA4.md) in metastatic CSCC; all three proposed as candidate non-coding biomarkers [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Proposed as candidate non-coding biomarker for metastatic CSCC; represents a region invisible to WES/targeted-panel platforms [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Open questions

- Functional consequence of 3′UTR variants on PPP1R1A expression in CSCC is unmeasured (no matched RNA in this study) [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)
