# GRB2

## Overview

GRB2 (growth factor receptor-bound protein 2) is an adaptor protein that links activated RTKs to the RAS-MAPK pathway via [SOS1](../genes/SOS1.md). GRB2 forms a complex with [GAB1](../genes/GAB1.md) and [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) (SHP2) downstream of [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) and other RTKs. In glioblastoma, GRB2 protein levels are paradoxically decreased in EGFR-altered tumors despite the activated EGFR signaling context.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- GRB2 protein downregulated in EGFR-altered [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) tumors in a CPTAC proteogenomic cohort (n=99 GBM tumors); identified as a member of the PTPN11-GAB1-GRB2 complex that forms a convergent RTK signaling hub downstream of EGFR [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **Glioblastoma (GBM):** GRB2 protein downregulated in EGFR-altered tumors despite functional involvement in the EGFR-GAB1-GRB2-PTPN11-RAS signaling axis; significance of downregulation is not fully explained [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Functions in complex with [GAB1](../genes/GAB1.md) and [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) as a convergent RTK signaling hub downstream of [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) and [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md) in GBM [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- GRB2 is a component of the RAS activation complex downstream of EGFR; its paradoxical protein downregulation in EGFR-altered GBM raises questions about compensatory signaling rewiring that may influence therapeutic response [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Open questions

- The mechanism of GRB2 protein downregulation in EGFR-altered GBM is unexplained; whether this reflects post-translational degradation, transcriptional repression, or negative feedback is unresolved [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).
