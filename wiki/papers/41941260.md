## TL;DR

This is a white-paper / program review of the NIH/NCI-funded Radiation Oncology–Biology Integration Network (ROBIN), a U54 consortium that embeds mechanistic biology inside prospective radiation-therapy (RT) clinical trials. ROBIN comprises five disease-focused centers — OligoMET (oligometastatic prostate cancer), ImmunoRad (rectal cancer), GenRad (bladder cancer and HNSCC), METEOR (cervical and pancreatic cancer), and KIDSROBIN (pediatric diffuse midline glioma and high-risk neuroblastoma) — each running a Molecular Characterization Trial (MCT) with longitudinal biospecimen collection, single-cell and spatial multi-omics, advanced imaging (CBCT, MRI), and integrative data science. The paper summarizes the consortium's mission, governance (Admin, CTC, DSIA, and CBCT working groups), and preliminary findings across centers; no new primary data are analyzed.

## Cohort & data

- **Design:** consortium white paper / review, not a primary-data study. Data availability statement explicitly notes: "No new data were generated or analyzed for this review."
- **Funded centers (U54 grants):**
  - OligoMET — U54 CA273956 — UMB, Weill Cornell Medicine, Thomas Jefferson University.
  - ImmunoRad — U54 CA274291 — Weill Cornell + 9 collaborating centers in the U.S. and Europe (MSKCC, University of Chicago, Rutgers, Cedars-Sinai, ICR/Royal Marsden, Glasgow, Manchester, Leeds, Gustave Roussy).
  - GenRad — U54 CA274513 — Cleveland Clinic and Emory University.
  - METEOR — U54 CA274318 — Washington University, St. Louis.
  - KIDSROBIN — U54 CA274516 — Harvard University and University of California, San Francisco.
- **Active trials referenced:**
  - TERPS (NCT05223803) — Phase II RCT of metastasis-directed therapy in oligometastatic [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md); 80+ screened, 47 randomized.
  - KNIGHTS (NCT06212583) — first integral-biomarker-designed randomized SABR trial in oligometastatic prostate cancer.
  - ImmunoRad MCT1 (NCT05943210) — preoperative short-course radiotherapy (SCRT) in rectal cancer; 39/50 enrolled, 140+ tissue samples banked.
  - ImmunoRad MCT2 (NCT05024097) — Phase I/II SCRT + etrumadenant + zimberelimab + FOLFOX in rectal cancer; 21 enrolled as of writing.
  - RAD-SG (NCT05833867, GenRad MCT part A) — RT + sacituzumab govitecan in muscle-invasive bladder cancer.
  - GenRad HNSCC MCT (NCT03521570, part 2) — RT + cisplatin vs. RT + immune checkpoint inhibitor in HNSCC; completed.
  - METEOR-CRATR (NCT05975593) — longitudinal sampling in locally advanced cervix and pancreas cancer; 26 cervical + 12 pancreatic patients, 75 tumor samples, 126 DICOM-RT plans archived.
  - PNOC023 and COG ANBL1531 — pediatric cooperative-group trials in DMG and high-risk neuroblastoma; 33 patients accrued to PNOC023 arms A/B; ANBL1531 accrual complete.
- **Assays referenced:** 10x Genomics [[visium-hd|Visium HD]] spatial transcriptomics, 10x [[xenium|Xenium]] single-cell spatial profiling, single-cell RNA-seq, bulk RNA-seq, 16S/ITS microbiome sequencing, ctDNA nucleosome positioning, serum miRNA profiling, flow-cytometry immune profiling, plasma proteomics, T-cell receptor repertoire, digital pathology / multimodal AI, CBCT delta radiomics, MRI (including DBSI), and 123I-/131I-MIBG SPECT/CT dosimetry.

## Key findings

This is a program description; the "findings" below are preliminary results the authors highlight from each center.

- **OligoMET (oligometastatic [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md)):** digital-pathology multimodal AI, plasma proteomics, and T-cell receptor repertoire analyses yielded signatures associated with stereotactic ablative radiation (SABR) benefit. Preclinical dietary interventions modulated radiosensitivity — low-fat and calorie-restricted regimens enhanced radiation response, whereas ketogenic diets unexpectedly promoted radio-resistance. Metabolic signatures linked to SABR-MDT response informed the biomarker-designed KNIGHTS (NCT06212583) trial.
- **ImmunoRad ([READ](../cancer_types/READ.md)):** spatial transcriptomics (Visium HD) on 140+ biopsies identified RT-associated spatial reorganization of lymphocytes and altered macrophage wound-healing signals. scRNA-seq of pre/post-RT PBMCs showed stress responses in B cells, ECM-remodeling/immune-activation in monocytes, and [MYC](../genes/MYC.md)-driven activation in CD8+ T cells — interpreted as RT-induced trained immunity. 16S/ITS sequencing showed post-RT increases in Clostridiales and Trichocomaceae. In MCT2, preliminary CR rate was 1/5 (20%) in Part I and 11/15 stage-1 patients had 82% CR in Part II (SCRT + [etrumadenant](../drugs/etrumadenant.md) + [zimberelimab](../drugs/zimberelimab.md) + [FOLFOX](../drugs/folfox.md)).
- **GenRad ([BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md) and [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md)):** RT + [sacituzumab-govitecan](../drugs/sacituzumab-govitecan.md) (RAD-SG, NCT05833867) for bladder preservation was safe and feasible with only grade-1 toxicities observed at preliminary read-out. In recurrent/second-primary HNSCC (NCT03521570), intensity-modulated re-irradiation + PD-1 inhibition was tolerable and effective; shorter PFS correlated with a 1.5-fold increase in PD1+Ki67+CD4+ T cells from baseline to week 2–4. Analysis of the Javelin HN100 chemoradiation ± [avelumab](../drugs/avelumab.md) expansion cohort identified intratumoral bacteria as a major source of therapy resistance in HNSCC.
- **METEOR ([CESC](../cancer_types/CESC.md) and [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md)):** among 26 cervical and 12 pancreatic patients (75 tumor samples; 22 cervical and 3 pancreatic with serial samples), chemoradiation (CRT) remodeled the TME with consistent myeloid-derived-cell infiltration and upregulation of [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)/[MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) in cervix tumor cells, without significant upregulation of [CD274](../genes/CD274.md)/[PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md) axes. SBRT altered cDC1 infiltration in human and murine pancreatic tumors with impaired antigen-presenting-cell phenotype in draining lymph nodes. HPV genotype and gene expression, rather than histology, drove cervical tumor biology.
- **KIDSROBIN (pediatric [DMG](../cancer_types/DMG.md) and [NBL](../cancer_types/NBL.md)):** 2,039 serial MRI scans from 253 DMG patients and 8,057/7,597 clinical notes (DMG/NBL) secured for NLP and radiomics studies. Xenium single-cell spatial profiling on baseline and post-131I-MIBG second-look neuroblastoma samples identified differentially depleted/enriched cell populations. ctDNA nucleosome positioning and serum miRNA profiling identified candidate response biomarkers. Paired 123I-/131I-MIBG SPECT/CT enabled tumor-absorbed dose estimation per unit activity.
- **Infrastructure:** DSIA WG targets FAIR data via the NCI Cancer Research Data Commons, adopting XNAT for imaging informatics and [pycerr](../methods/pycerr.md) (Python Computational Environment for Radiotherapy Research) with AI auto-segmentation and radiomics. The CBCT WG standardizes Cone-Beam CT acquisition protocols across institutions to enable CBCT delta radiomics (high-temporal-resolution treatment-response imaging).

## Genes & alterations

The paper does not make gene-level variant claims; genes are discussed as functional markers or pathway readouts in preliminary results.

- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) — MYC-driven transcriptional activation observed in CD8+ T cells post-RT in rectal-cancer PBMC scRNA-seq (ImmunoRad).
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) / [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) — p53/MDM2 axis upregulated in human cervix tumor cells after chemoradiation (METEOR).
- [CD274](../genes/CD274.md) (PD-L1) / [PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md) (PD-1) — the PD-1/PD-L1 axis was not significantly upregulated after CRT in the METEOR cervical cohort. In GenRad HNSCC, PD1+Ki67+CD4+ T-cell expansion associated with shorter PFS under re-irradiation + PD-1 blockade.

## Clinical implications

- **Biomarker-driven radiation oncology:** the MCT design — longitudinal tissue, blood, stool, and imaging across pre-, on-, and post-RT timepoints — is framed as a scalable template for generating actionable radiation-response biomarkers. OligoMET's metabolic/imaging signatures already catalyzed the KNIGHTS integral-biomarker RCT (NCT06212583).
- **Radiation + systemic therapy combinations:** consortium data support feasibility and biological activity of RT combined with antibody-drug conjugates ([sacituzumab-govitecan](../drugs/sacituzumab-govitecan.md) in MIBC), adenosine-pathway + PD-1 inhibitors ([etrumadenant](../drugs/etrumadenant.md) + [zimberelimab](../drugs/zimberelimab.md) with [folfox](../drugs/folfox.md) in rectal cancer), and PD-1 blockade in re-irradiated HNSCC.
- **Intratumoral microbiome as a resistance axis:** HNSCC intratumoral bacteria identified in the Javelin HN100 expansion cohort are highlighted as a newly druggable resistance mechanism for chemoradiation-immunotherapy combinations.
- **Disparities research:** OligoMET explicitly investigates molecular drivers of poorer outcomes in African-American prostate-cancer patients with the goal of informing trials beyond prostate cancer.
- **Pediatric translatability:** KIDSROBIN positions single-cell and spatial profiling of genetically "simple" pediatric tumors (low TMB) as a tractable model for intra-tumoral heterogeneity whose mechanistic findings are expected to generalize to adult cancers.

## Limitations & open questions

- **No primary data.** This is a consortium white paper; every quantitative result is labeled preliminary and none has undergone formal peer-reviewed primary publication within this article.
- **Accrual is ongoing for most MCTs** (e.g. ImmunoRad MCT1 at 39/50; OligoMET TERPS at 47 randomized; PNOC023 at 33) — statistical power for the stated biomarker claims is not yet established.
- **Small sample sizes** in several preliminary readouts (e.g. 11/15 patients in MCT2 Part II stage 1; 10 patients contributing spatial profiling from Glasgow) mean effect sizes may be unstable.
- **Assay heterogeneity across sites** — the DSIA and CBCT working groups explicitly flag the need for harmonized ontologies, acquisition protocols, and artifact correction before cross-center integrative analysis is robust.
- **Translation gap for radiation-immunotherapy** — the authors note that despite strong preclinical signal, successful translation of RT-induced immune effects to the clinic is still lacking.
- **Open:** how RT-induced microbial shifts (Clostridiales, Trichocomaceae; intratumoral bacteria in HNSCC) causally shape response vs. resistance — the consortium flags this as a major forward-looking mechanistic question.
- **Open:** whether the "trained immunity" / MYC-driven CD8+ activation signal in PBMCs post-RT is a reproducible systemic-response biomarker across disease sites.
