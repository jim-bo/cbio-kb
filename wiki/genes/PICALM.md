# PICALM

## Overview

PICALM (Phosphatidylinositol Binding Clathrin Assembly Protein) encodes a clathrin assembly protein involved in endocytosis and intracellular trafficking. In hematologic malignancies, PICALM is notable as a fusion partner in the recurrent PICALM–[MLLT10](../genes/MLLT10.md) (CALM–AF10) translocation, which is associated with [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md) and T-ALL. This in-frame fusion disrupts normal hematopoietic differentiation and promotes leukemogenesis.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Involved in recurrent in-frame PICALM–MLLT10 fusion detected by RNA-seq in AML; associated with the favorable-risk fusion landscape [PMID:23634996](../papers/23634996.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **AML:** The PICALM–MLLT10 fusion is one of several recurrent in-frame fusions identified by RNA-seq in the TCGA 200-case AML cohort, alongside other favorable-risk translocations [PMID:23634996](../papers/23634996.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- PICALM–MLLT10 fusion belongs to the favorable-risk transcription-factor fusion category, which is mutually exclusive with [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md) mutations and [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md) mutations in AML [PMID:23634996](../papers/23634996.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Favorable-risk fusions including PICALM–MLLT10 may guide treatment de-escalation strategies; no specific targeted therapies for this fusion are described in the corpus.

## Open questions

- Whether the PICALM–MLLT10 fusion in AML carries the same favorable prognosis as [PML](../genes/PML.md)–[RARA](../genes/RARA.md) or [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md)–[RUNX1T1](../genes/RUNX1T1.md) fusions remains to be established in outcome-annotated analyses.
