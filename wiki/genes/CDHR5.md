# CDHR5

## Overview

CDHR5 (Cadherin-Related Family Member 5) encodes a protocadherin-related cell adhesion molecule expressed in epithelial cells, particularly in intestinal microvilli. In metastatic cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma (cSCC), CDHR5 was identified as a candidate coding driver called by at least two of three independent driver-calling tools in a whole-genome sequencing cohort. CDHR5 is also a contributor to TGF-β/ECM pathway enrichment in this cancer context.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Candidate coding driver called by ≥2 of three driver tools (OncodriveFML, MutSigCV, dNdScv) in metastatic cSCC WGS cohort (n=25); CDHR5 contributes to enrichment of the "TGF-beta regulation of extracellular matrix" pathway in BioPlanet pathway analysis [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)
- TGF-β/ECM pathway enrichment: CDHR5, [LUM](../genes/LUM.md), [COLEC12](../genes/COLEC12.md), and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) jointly drive this pathway as a top enriched term across 21 candidate altered genes [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md):** Candidate driver in metastatic cSCC (head and neck, lymph node metastases); WGS cohort (n=25); part of the multi-gene TGF-β axis enrichment [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [KHDRBS2](../genes/KHDRBS2.md), [COLEC12](../genes/COLEC12.md), and other candidate drivers in the high-TMB UV-driven cSCC background; jointly enriches TGF-β/ECM pathway with [LUM](../genes/LUM.md) and COLEC12 [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- TGFβ receptor inhibition with chemotherapy or immunotherapy is proposed as a candidate strategy for cSCC by analogy to other cancers, given the enrichment of the TGF-β pathway including CDHR5 [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Open questions

- Whether CDHR5 mutations are functionally driver events in cSCC or background passengers in a high-TMB UV-mutagenized genome requires functional validation [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)
