# APOE

## Overview

APOE (apolipoprotein E) is a lipid transport protein highly expressed in astrocytes and microglia in the normal brain. In oligodendroglioma, APOE marks the differentiated astro-like tumor cell compartment and is a component of the lineage hierarchy identified by single-cell transcriptomics.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- APOE (together with [ALDOC](../genes/ALDOC.md), [SOX9](../genes/SOX9.md), and [GFAP](../genes/GFAP.md)) marks the differentiated astro-like compartment in grade II IDH-mutant 1p/19q-codeleted oligodendroglioma; identified from scRNA-seq of 4,347 cells across 6 tumors [PMID:27806376](../papers/27806376.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [ODG](../cancer_types/ODG.md) — APOE expression defines the astro-like differentiated sub-population in oligodendroglioma; these cells are largely non-cycling and distinct from the proliferative stem/progenitor compartment [PMID:27806376](../papers/27806376.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- APOE co-expresses with [ALDOC](../genes/ALDOC.md), [SOX9](../genes/SOX9.md), and [GFAP](../genes/GFAP.md) in astro-like tumor cells; mutually exclusive with the stem/progenitor program ([ASCL1](../genes/ASCL1.md), [CHD7](../genes/CHD7.md), [CCND2](../genes/CCND2.md)) and the oligo-like program [PMID:27806376](../papers/27806376.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Not directly therapeutically targeted in the corpus; the APOE-high astro-like cells are largely non-cycling and considered unlikely to drive long-term tumor growth [PMID:27806376](../papers/27806376.md).

## Open questions

- Whether APOE expression in oligodendroglioma influences tumor microenvironment lipid metabolism or immune cell function is not addressed in the corpus.
