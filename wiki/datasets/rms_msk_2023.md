# Pediatric Rhabdomyosarcomas (MSK, JCO Precis Oncol 2023)

## Overview

Single-center retrospective MSK cohort of patients with extremity rhabdomyosarcoma treated between 2000 and 2021, integrating clinicopathologic features, fusion testing, and MSK-IMPACT targeted sequencing to refine risk stratification [PMID:37315267](../papers/37315267.md).

## Composition

- 61 patients with extremity [RMS](../cancer_types/RMS.md) treated at MSKCC, January 2000–December 2021; median age 8 years; ~two-thirds lower extremity; histology 85% [FOXO1](../genes/FOXO1.md) fusion–positive [ARMS](../cancer_types/ARMS.md), 7 fusion-negative [ERMS](../cancer_types/ERMS.md), 2 [MYOD1](../genes/MYOD1.md)-mutant spindle cell/sclerosing [RMS](../cancer_types/RMS.md) ([SCRMS](../cancer_types/SCRMS.md)) [PMID:37315267](../papers/37315267.md).

## Assays / panels (linked)

- Fusion testing by [Archer FusionPlex](../methods/archer-fusionplex.md) or [FISH](../methods/fish.md) with custom BAC probes flanking [FOXO1](../genes/FOXO1.md), [PAX3](../genes/PAX3.md), and [PAX7](../genes/PAX7.md); targeted DNA sequencing via MSK-IMPACT ([IMPACT410](../methods/IMPACT410.md) / [IMPACT505](../methods/IMPACT505.md)) in 40% of patients [PMID:37315267](../papers/37315267.md).

## Papers using this cohort

- [PMID:37315267](../papers/37315267.md) — de Traux de Wardin et al., Extremity Rhabdomyosarcoma: An Integrated Clinicopathologic and Genomic Study to Improve Risk Stratification.

## Notable findings derived from this cohort

- Metastatic disease, high-risk group, and age ≥10 years significantly affected OS (HR 2.68, P=.004; HR 2.78, P=.010; HR 2.26, P=.034) [PMID:37315267](../papers/37315267.md).
- [PAX3](../genes/PAX3.md)::[FOXO1](../genes/FOXO1.md) [ARMS](../cancer_types/ARMS.md) had worse prognosis and afflicted older children than [PAX7](../genes/PAX7.md)::[FOXO1](../genes/FOXO1.md) (HR 3.45, P=.016) [PMID:37315267](../papers/37315267.md).
- Most common [ARMS](../cancer_types/ARMS.md) genomic events were [MED12](../genes/MED12.md) alterations, [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) amplifications, and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) deletions (each 8%–17%); [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) amplifications and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) deletions were mutually exclusive, enriched in acral and high-risk lesions, and correlated with poor OS (P=.02) [PMID:37315267](../papers/37315267.md).
