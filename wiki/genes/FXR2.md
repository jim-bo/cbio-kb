# FXR2

## Overview

FXR2 (Fragile X Mental Retardation Autosomal Homolog 2) encodes an RNA-binding protein related to the Fragile X protein family. In pediatric cancer, FXR2 has been reported as a structural rearrangement partner for [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) in Wilms tumor, arising as a consequence of genomic instability rather than as a primary oncogenic driver.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- TP53-FXR2 fusion detected in Wilms tumor PDX model KT-9 (bilateral disease) in the PPTC pediatric cancer PDX dataset, accompanied by partial homozygous TP53 deletion; considered a structural consequence of TP53 locus rearrangement rather than a standalone oncogenic event [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [WT](../cancer_types/WT.md) — TP53-FXR2 structural fusion observed in one bilateral Wilms tumor PDX model (KT-9) [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with partial homozygous TP53 deletion in the same Wilms tumor PDX model [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic relevance established; FXR2 involvement is interpreted as a passenger consequence of TP53 locus instability in pediatric Wilms tumor.

## Open questions

- Whether TP53-FXR2 fusions are recurrent in Wilms tumor beyond the single PDX model reported, or represent a model-specific rearrangement, remains unresolved.
