# DNA liquid biopsy-based prediction of cancer-associated venous thromboembolism

**PMID:** [39147831](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39147831) · **DOI:** [10.1038/s41591-024-03195-0](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41591-024-03195-0) · **Journal:** Nature Medicine (2024)

## TL;DR

This study investigated whether circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) detected via liquid biopsy can predict cancer-associated venous thromboembolism (VTE). Across three cohorts totaling 6,030 patients with pan-cancer diagnoses (discovery n = 4,141; prospective validation n = 1,426; international generalizability n = 463), ctDNA detection was independently associated with higher VTE rates (HR = 2.49, 95% CI: 1.99--3.11). A random survival forest model trained on liquid biopsy variables outperformed the Khorana score (c-indices 0.74 vs. 0.57 in discovery, 0.73 vs. 0.61 in validation, 0.67 vs. 0.54 in generalizability). In ctDNA-positive patients, anticoagulation was associated with lower VTE rates (adjusted HR = 0.50, 95% CI: 0.30--0.81), whereas ctDNA-negative patients showed no benefit from anticoagulation.

## Cohort & data

- **Discovery cohort**: 4,141 patients with any cancer type, plasma sequenced with [MSK-ACCESS](../methods/ACCESS129.md) (129 genes) at MSK (June 2019--September 2022). Linked to [msk_ctdna_vte_2024](../datasets/msk_ctdna_vte_2024.md).
- **Prospective validation cohort**: 1,426 patients with any cancer type, plasma sequenced with [MSK-ACCESS](../methods/ACCESS129.md) at MSK (September 2022--September 2023). Same dataset.
- **International generalizability cohort**: 463 patients with advanced [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md), plasma sequenced with [ctDx Lung](../methods/ctdx-lung.md) (21 genes) at MSK and GenesisCare (Sydney, Australia). Linked to [nsclc_ctdx_msk_2022](../datasets/nsclc_ctdx_msk_2022.md).
- Cancer types included: [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) (34%), [breast](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) (13%), [pancreatic](../cancer_types/PAAD.md) (10%), [melanoma](../cancer_types/MEL.md) (7%), [prostate](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) (5%), [bladder](../cancer_types/BLCA.md) (5%), plus 53 other cancer types in the discovery cohort.
- 61% of discovery cohort patients were ctDNA-positive; 11% developed VTE after plasma draw.

## Key findings

- ctDNA detection was associated with higher VTE rates in the discovery cohort (HR = 2.49, 95% CI: 1.99--3.11, P < 0.001) [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).
- A dose-dependent relationship was observed: patients in the highest VAF quartile had the greatest VTE risk [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).
- The ctDNA-VTE association held across most cancer types, with the notable exceptions of [bladder](../cancer_types/BLCA.md), hepatobiliary, and colorectal cancers [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).
- In multivariate analysis, ctDNA detection (HR = 1.66, 95% CI: 1.30--2.11), cfDNA concentration (HR = 1.61, 95% CI: 1.36--1.92), Khorana score, number of disease organ sites, and chemotherapy receipt were all independently associated with VTE [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).
- The association between ctDNA and VTE was largely independent of tumor genomic content: in a model including tissue-confirmed gene-level alterations, ctDNA detection remained significant while individual gene alterations (including [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [STK11](../genes/STK11.md), [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md)) were not [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).
- A random survival forest (LB+ model) using liquid biopsy variables plus cancer type and chemotherapy achieved c-indices of 0.74 (discovery), 0.73 (validation), and 0.67 (generalizability), substantially outperforming Khorana score (0.57, 0.61, 0.54) and the risk assessment model RAM (0.62) [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).
- In ctDNA-positive patients (n = 2,522), anticoagulation was associated with lower VTE rates (adjusted HR = 0.50, 95% CI: 0.30--0.81); in ctDNA-negative patients (n = 1,619), anticoagulation showed no benefit (adjusted HR = 0.89, 95% CI: 0.40--2.0) [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).
- Statin prescription was associated with VTE reduction in ctDNA-positive but not ctDNA-negative patients [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md): highest unadjusted HR for VTE when detected in plasma (HR = 1.85, 95% CI: 1.53--2.23; n = 1,272 patients with alteration) [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md): adjusted HR = 1.65 (95% CI: 1.30--2.09; n = 465). Known thrombogenic association [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md): adjusted HR = 1.62 (95% CI: 1.24--2.10; n = 339) [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).
- [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md): adjusted HR = 2.50 (95% CI: 1.62--3.85; n = 76). Strongest gene-specific association [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).
- [STK11](../genes/STK11.md): adjusted HR = 1.61 (95% CI: 1.06--2.46; n = 107) [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).
- [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md): adjusted HR = 2.70 (95% CI: 1.74--4.18; n = 65) [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md): adjusted HR = 1.72 (95% CI: 1.10--2.68; n = 121) [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md): adjusted HR = 1.47 (95% CI: 1.01--2.13; n = 254) [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md): adjusted HR = 1.84 (95% CI: 1.29--2.63; n = 148) [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).
- However, in multivariate analysis with matched tissue sequencing, individual gene-level alterations were not independently associated with VTE after controlling for ctDNA detection, suggesting the ctDNA-VTE link is largely genomic-content-independent [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).

## Clinical implications

- Liquid biopsy-based VTE risk stratification could be provided as a byproduct of standard-of-care ctDNA sequencing already ordered for therapy selection, adding no additional cost or burden to patients or clinicians [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).
- ctDNA detection may identify patients who benefit from prophylactic anticoagulation (adjusted HR = 0.50 in ctDNA-positive patients), while ctDNA-negative patients may be spared unnecessary anticoagulation [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).
- The LB-based model could shift VTE risk assessment from individual providers/hospital systems to assay distributors, who already produce genomic sequencing reports [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).
- Highest-risk patients identified by the model had cumulative VTE incidence exceeding 25%, approximately three times higher than high-risk Khorana score patients [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).

## Limitations & open questions

- Observational, non-randomized design: anticoagulation analysis is exploratory and subject to confounders (comorbidities, indication bias). Randomized prospective trials are needed before clinical adoption [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).
- Liquid biopsies are not universally deployed and are primarily used for targeted therapy matching in certain cancer types, limiting immediate generalizability [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).
- The ctDx Lung panel lacks matched white blood cell sequencing, so approximately 11% of mutations may be attributable to clonal hematopoiesis, potentially inflating VTE risk estimates [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).
- Sparse minority representation in the cohorts; predominantly advanced-disease patients at MSK [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).
- ctDNA was not associated with VTE in bladder, hepatobiliary, or colorectal cancers -- reasons for cancer-type-specific differences remain unexplained [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).
- Other biomarkers (genetic risk scores, tissue factor-bearing microparticles, proteomics) may provide orthogonal information and could further improve risk models [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).
