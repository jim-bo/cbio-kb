# ESRP2

## Overview

ESRP2 (epithelial splicing regulatory protein 2) is an RNA-binding protein that regulates alternative splicing of transcripts involved in epithelial identity. It promotes epithelial splicing patterns and opposes epithelial-to-mesenchymal transition (EMT). In cancer, ESRP2 has been proposed to act in opposition to [QKI](../genes/QKI.md) in a regulatory axis controlling EMT-associated circular RNA production.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Part of the proposed EMT regulatory axis QKI/ESRP2/ZEB1/ZEB2 in endometrial carcinoma: QKI promotes 35 EMT-associated circRNAs and is positively correlated with ZEB1/ZEB2, while ESRP2 opposes this activity [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Endometrial carcinoma: implicated in an EMT regulatory axis opposing QKI-driven circRNA production [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Functionally opposed to QKI in endometrial carcinoma EMT regulatory context [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic agents reported in this corpus.

## Open questions

- The mechanistic link between ESRP2, QKI, and circRNA-mediated EMT requires functional validation; the observation is based on correlational proteogenomic data.
