# KLHL6

## Overview

KLHL6 (Kelch-Like Family Member 6) encodes a substrate adaptor for the BCR-ABL1 E3 ubiquitin ligase complex involved in B-cell receptor signaling. In cancer genomics, KLHL6 mutations in DLBCL show mutual exclusivity with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations, suggesting non-overlapping functional roles in lymphomagenesis.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Mutually exclusive with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations in DLBCL (1001-patient cohort treated with rituximab-containing regimens); TP53 is CRISPR-enriched (loss increases fitness) while KLHL6 shows a distinct pattern. [PMID:28985567](../papers/28985567.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[DLBCL NOS](../cancer_types/DLBCLNOS.md):** KLHL6 mutations are part of the 150 recurrently mutated driver gene landscape in DLBCL; mutual exclusivity with TP53 is a defining co-mutation pattern in this study. [PMID:28985567](../papers/28985567.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Mutually exclusive with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations in DLBCL. [PMID:28985567](../papers/28985567.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct targeted therapy against KLHL6 is reported in this corpus.

## Open questions

- The mechanistic basis of KLHL6 and TP53 mutual exclusivity in DLBCL pathogenesis is not detailed; whether they represent convergent or divergent oncogenic pathways requires functional investigation. [PMID:28985567](../papers/28985567.md)
