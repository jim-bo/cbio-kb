# CDK4

## Overview

CDK4 encodes a cyclin-dependent kinase central to G1/S cell-cycle progression; focal amplification deregulates the RB pathway and is therapeutically targeted by CDK4/6 inhibitors.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Focal CDK4 amplifications were among the most common genomic events in alveolar rhabdomyosarcoma ([ARMS](../cancer_types/ARMS.md)) in the 61-patient MSKCC extremity [RMS](../cancer_types/RMS.md) cohort, with frequencies in the 8%–17% range alongside [MED12](../genes/MED12.md) alterations and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) deletions [PMID:37315267](../papers/37315267.md).
- CDK4 amplifications and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) deletions were mutually exclusive, enriched in acral and high-risk lesions, and correlated with poor overall survival (P=.02) [PMID:37315267](../papers/37315267.md).
- Focal CDK4 amplifications (along with [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md) and [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md)) were used to define "molecular grade-intermediate" in 1p19q intact IDH-mutant astrocytomas in a 128-patient MSKCC active-surveillance cohort [PMID:37910594](../papers/37910594.md).
- CDK4 amplification detected in 17% of FP-RMS in a multi-platform ctDNA study; often co-amplified with [GLI1](../genes/GLI1.md) (2 tumors) or [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) (1 tumor) at the 12q13-15 locus; correlated with worse [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md); identified as a high-risk secondary event alongside [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md) and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) alterations [PMID:37730754](../papers/37730754.md).
- CDK4 alterations designated as markers warranting shorter ctDNA surveillance intervals during follow-up in FP-RMS [PMID:37730754](../papers/37730754.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- Extremity rhabdomyosarcoma ([ARMS](../cancer_types/ARMS.md)) — CDK4 amplification is a recurrent, prognostically adverse event [PMID:37315267](../papers/37315267.md).
- FP-RMS ([RMS](../cancer_types/RMS.md)/[ARMS](../cancer_types/ARMS.md)) — CDK4 amplification in 17% of FP-RMS; co-amplified with GLI1 or MDM2; adverse prognosis marker warranting ctDNA-based monitoring [PMID:37730754](../papers/37730754.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- CDK4 amplification is mutually exclusive with [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) deletion in extremity [ARMS](../cancer_types/ARMS.md), consistent with convergent cell-cycle/RB-pathway dysregulation [PMID:37315267](../papers/37315267.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- The authors argue CDK4 amplifications should be considered for integration into [RMS](../cancer_types/RMS.md) risk stratification, and raise CDK4/6-directed therapy as a rationale, though efficacy is untested in this cohort [PMID:37315267](../papers/37315267.md).

## Open questions

- Whether CDK4/6 inhibition improves outcomes in CDK4-amplified or CDKN2A-deleted extremity [RMS](../cancer_types/RMS.md) remains untested [PMID:37315267](../papers/37315267.md).
