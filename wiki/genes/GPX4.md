# GPX4

## Overview

GPX4 (glutathione peroxidase 4) is the master ferroptosis suppressor, reducing lipid hydroperoxides to prevent oxidative cell death. In [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md), GPX4 suppresses ferroptosis and contributes to resistance to [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md) and [regorafenib](../drugs/regorafenib.md).

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- GPX4 suppresses lipid peroxidation in HCC alongside [SLC7A11](../genes/SLC7A11.md); targeted by sorafenib/regorafenib-induced ferroptosis; high GPX4 expression confers ferroptosis resistance [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) — GPX4 is the central ferroptosis suppressor; resistance to sorafenib is partly mediated by high GPX4/SLC7A11 expression [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Part of the SLC7A11/GPX4 ferroptosis-suppression axis in HCC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- GPX4 inhibitors (e.g., RSL3, ML162) are proposed to resensitize HCC to sorafenib/regorafenib by inducing ferroptosis [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Open questions

- Clinical-grade GPX4 inhibitors are not yet in clinical use; toxicity in normal hepatocytes is a concern.
