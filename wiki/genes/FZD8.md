# FZD8

## Overview

FZD8 (Frizzled class receptor 8) is a Wnt co-receptor that transduces canonical Wnt/β-catenin signaling. In pheochromocytoma/paraganglioma (PCC/PGL), FZD8 is a target of miR-375, a microRNA whose underexpression in [MAML3](../genes/MAML3.md) fusion-positive tumors is proposed to contribute to Wnt pathway activation within the Wnt-altered molecular subtype.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- FZD8 is a Wnt-pathway member overexpressed in MAML3 fusion-positive PCC/PGL; miR-375 (a negative regulator of FZD8) is underexpressed in these tumors, suggesting a miRNA-mediated mechanism for Wnt pathway activation in the Wnt-altered subtype of PCC/PGL (n=173, TCGA PCPG study) [PMID:28162975](../papers/28162975.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [PHC](../cancer_types/PHC.md) / [PGNG](../cancer_types/PGNG.md) — FZD8 overexpression (via miR-375 underexpression) contributes to the Wnt-pathway activation signature in the MAML3 fusion-positive Wnt-altered subtype [PMID:28162975](../papers/28162975.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- FZD8 overexpression co-occurs with [MAML3](../genes/MAML3.md) fusion positivity, elevated [DVL3](../genes/DVL3.md) and β-catenin, and Wnt/Hedgehog target gene overexpression in PCC/PGL [PMID:28162975](../papers/28162975.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- The Wnt-altered PCC/PGL subtype (defined in part by FZD8 overexpression) may be responsive to downstream β-catenin antagonists or [STAT3](../genes/STAT3.md) inhibitors — therapeutic hypotheses, not tested in this study [PMID:28162975](../papers/28162975.md).

## Open questions

- Whether miR-375 underexpression directly drives FZD8 overexpression and Wnt signaling in PCC/PGL, or whether additional mechanisms contribute, requires experimental validation [PMID:28162975](../papers/28162975.md).
