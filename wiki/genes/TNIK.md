# TNIK

## Overview

TNIK ([TRAF2](../genes/TRAF2.md) and NCK Interacting Kinase) is a serine/threonine kinase that functions as a component of the WNT signaling pathway, required for TCF4-mediated target gene activation. It has been proposed as a synthetic-lethal therapeutic target in tumors with SWI/SNF complex ([ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md)) mutations.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Elevated phospho-TNIK in ARID1A-mutant breast tumors in the CPTAC breast proteogenomic cohort (n=122 primary breast tumors); association detected by tumor-suppressor-loss to targetable-kinase analysis (Black-Sheep, FDR < 0.01); TNIK inhibition proposed as a therapeutic strategy for ARID1A-mutant breast cancer [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md)**: Phospho-TNIK elevation marks ARID1A-mutant breast tumors; TNIK is nominated as a druggable WNT-pathway kinase for ARID1A-mutant [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Elevated phospho-TNIK is specifically associated with ARID1A loss-of-function mutations in breast cancer (WNT pathway activation downstream of SWI/SNF loss) [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- TNIK inhibition is proposed as a targeted strategy for ARID1A-mutant breast cancer; the association is hypothesis-generating from phosphoproteomic data and requires functional and clinical validation [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md).

## Open questions

- Whether TNIK inhibition is effective in ARID1A-mutant BRCA requires preclinical and clinical testing; no validated TNIK inhibitor has been evaluated in this context in the corpus [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md).
