# ESRP1

## Overview

ESRP1 (Epithelial Splicing Regulatory Protein 1) is an RNA-binding protein that regulates alternative splicing to maintain the epithelial cell state. In prostate cancer, recurrent 8q22 genomic duplications target the ESRP1 locus and drive overexpression; high ESRP1 protein levels independently predict shorter time to biochemical recurrence ([BCR](../genes/BCR.md)) and are associated with high Gleason Score, advanced pathological stage, and elevated Ki-67. ESRP1 has also been linked to RAF-fusion formation in prostate cancer, though its precise mechanistic role in aggressive disease remains under investigation.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Recurrent 8q22 duplications involving ESRP1 in 17% of EOPC; associated with >1.5-fold mRNA increase; independent prognostic biomarker for biochemical recurrence (BCR) validated by IHC in ~12,000 TMA cores; correlates with high Gleason Score (GS), pT, Ki-67, PSA; enriched in PEPCI-high tumors (OR=15.7, FDR p<1×10⁻⁵); ERG-independent; most strongly GS-associated RGA in the cohort (p<1×10⁻¹¹) [PMID:30537516](../papers/30537516.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md): Recurrent 8q22 duplications in 17% of early-onset prostate cancer (EOPC); ESRP1 IHC staining tracks with high GS (>4+4), advanced pT3b–pT4 stage, lymph-node metastases, higher PSA, and shorter BCR-free survival; enriched in PEPCI-high molecular subgroup (OR=15.7); ESRP1 gain is an independent BCR predictor in multivariate analysis controlling for four established clinico-pathological parameters and [ERG](../genes/ERG.md) status [PMID:30537516](../papers/30537516.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- ESRP1 8q22 duplications often span the [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) locus (~33 Mb away) but only ESRP1 (not MYC) shows significant expression increase, decoupling the ESRP1 gain from MYC amplification effects [PMID:30537516](../papers/30537516.md)
- ESRP1 gain is enriched in the PEPCI-high, CC7-high molecular subgroup (most aggressive) and in CC7-high tumors with [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) loss (p<0.0001) [PMID:30537516](../papers/30537516.md)
- ERG fusion status does not modify the ESRP1 prognostic signal; ESRP1 IHC is an independent marker of BCR beyond ERG [PMID:30537516](../papers/30537516.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- ESRP1 overexpression defines a high-risk [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) subgroup that overlaps with the >40% of EOPC patients matched to PI3K, mTOR, or PARP inhibitor trials in the same cohort; direct ESRP1 targeting has not been reported in this study [PMID:30537516](../papers/30537516.md)

## Open questions

- The mechanistic basis of ESRP1 dosage gain driving aggressive prostate cancer phenotype is unresolved: accelerated epithelial proliferation (consistent with E-cadherin co-expression) vs. partial EMT cannot be distinguished from current data [PMID:30537516](../papers/30537516.md)
- ESRP1 has been linked to RAF-fusion formation in prostate cancer elsewhere, but no ESRP1 fusion events were detected in the 292-case WGS cohort [PMID:30537516](../papers/30537516.md)
