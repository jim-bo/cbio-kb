TL;DR

Wang et al. report an integrated proteogenomic and metabolomic characterization of 99 treatment-naive adult glioblastoma tumors prospectively collected through the Clinical Proteomic Tumor Analysis Consortium (CPTAC), profiled on ten platforms: whole-genome sequencing, whole-exome sequencing, RNA-seq, miRNA-seq, single-nuclei RNA-seq, DNA methylation arrays, global proteome, phosphoproteome, acetylome, lipidome, and metabolome. Multi-omics NMF clustering recapitulated the three TCGA expression subtypes (proneural-like nmf1, mesenchymal-like nmf2, classical-like nmf3) but reclassified 29% of tumors; a “mixed subtype” (n = 12) with low membership in multiple clusters had significantly worse survival (log-rank p = 1.7e-4). Phospho-signaling analyses nominated PTPN11 (Shp2) Y62 and PLCG1 Y783 as a convergent RTK signaling hub downstream of EGFR and PDGFRA, and identified upregulated DNA-repair phosphosites without protein-level upregulation in TP53-mutant tumors. Four immune subtypes (im1–im4) were defined from xCell deconvolution and validated by snRNA-seq; im4 was uniformly cold (1.3% T cells, 6% TAM). H2B N-terminal hyperacetylation marks a classical-like subset and is associated with CREBBP, EP300, and BRD1/BRD3/BRD4 activity. Lipidomic profiling on 75 tumors quantified 582 lipids and revealed subtype-specific patterns (TG enrichment + PC depletion in mesenchymal-like; VLCFA/PUFA-glycerophospholipids enriched in proneural-like) plus IDH1 R132H–driven global metabolic shifts. Drug-connectivity analysis against LINCS L1000 and P100 nominated kinase inhibitors (broader than EGFR inhibitors) for EGFR-altered tumors and MAPK inhibitors for NF1-altered tumors.

Cohort & data

  • N = 99 treatment-naive adult GBM tumors prospectively collected through CPTAC from 10 tissue source sites in 6 countries. Males n = 55, females n = 44; age range 24–88.
  • 10 unmatched GTEx frontal cortex normal samples used as reference.
  • 6 IDH1 R132H–mutant tumors (median onset 47 vs. 59 years for IDH1-WT, t-test p = 0.055); one additional non-hotspot R222C mutation.
  • All samples cryopulverized and aliquoted to 10 omics platforms (Figure 1A, S1A–C):
    • DNA: PCR-free WGS on HiSeq X (min 15× tumor), WES on HiSeq 4000 (min 150× on-target), Illumina Infinium MethylationEPIC (>850k CpGs).
    • RNA: total RNA-seq on HiSeq 4000 (≥120M reads/sample, hg38), miRNA-seq (≥10M reads), snRNA-seq on 10X Chromium for 18 tumors (7 im1, 5 im2, 1 im3, 5 im4).
    • Protein: TMT-11–plex LC-MS/MS global proteome, IMAC-enriched phosphoproteome, and Ac-K–immunoaffinity-enriched acetylome on Orbitrap Fusion Lumos.
    • Small molecules: label-free GC-MS metabolome and LC-MS lipidome (582 lipids quantified across 75 tumors).
  • Independent validation cohort: 39 high-grade (age 14–39) brain tumors from the Children’s Brain Tumor Tissue Consortium (CBTTC) processed by matched TMT proteome/phosphoproteome.
  • Dataset: gbm_cptac_2021 (reference genome hg38). Raw data: CPTAC Data Portal S048, S057; GDC project CPTAC-3; Proteomic Data Commons.

Key findings

  • Multi-omics NMF clustering reproduces TCGA subtypes but reclassifies 29% of tumors. The three IDH-WT clusters were designated nmf1 (proneural-like, n = 29), nmf2 (mesenchymal-like, n = 37), and nmf3 (classical-like, n = 26); 27 tumors (29%) were reassigned relative to TCGA expression-based labels. Pathway enrichment: nmf1 enriched for synaptic vesicle cycling and neurotransmission; nmf2 enriched for innate immune response, neutrophil degranulation, ECM organization; nmf3 enriched for mRNA splicing and RNA metabolism. Acetylation-pathway enrichment: nmf1 acetylated TCA-cycle and amino-acid-metabolism proteins; nmf2 acetylated innate-immune/glycolysis proteins; nmf3 acetylated chromatin modifiers and DNA-repair proteins.
  • Mixed-subtype tumors have worse survival. 12 tumors with low multi-omics membership scores for ≥2 subtypes had significantly shorter overall survival vs. non-mixed (log-rank p = 1.7e-4), excluding IDH1-mutants.
  • Three survival-associated proteins identified across all tumors: low HIST3H2BB (log-rank p = 0.0034), high MT-CYB (p = 0.03), and high PRODH (p = 0.096).
  • DNA methylation: 6 subtypes with two G-CIMP variants. dm6 is IDH-mutant-specific with chromatin-organization upregulation; dm2 is IDH-WT with transcription/mRNA-splicing upregulation. 38 of 90 tumors (42%) showed MGMT promoter hypermethylation with significant downregulation of MGMT RNA (Welch’s t-test p = 4.9e-11) and protein (p = 2.6e-6).
  • TERT promoter mutations in 74% of primary GBMs (C228T and C250T) with elevated TERT RNA expression. ATRX mutations were mutually exclusive with TERTp hotspot mutations and co-occurred with TP53 and IDH1 R132H; 9 ATRX-mutants had significantly diminished ATRX RNA and protein, confirmed by IHC.
  • Driver alterations leave specific phospho-signatures (95 cis-trans phosphorylation events). Strong cis effects: EGFR S1166 and PDGFRA S1067/S1070. Trans effects of EGFR alteration: elevated CTNNB1 protein (β-catenin) despite decreased mRNA, and increased phosphorylation of PTPN11 Y62 and PLCG1 Y783.
  • TP53–mutant tumors show DNA-repair phosphosite upregulation without protein-level upregulation, indicating phosphosite-specific regulation. TP53 S315 and TP53BP1 S1099/S1106/S1109 phosphorylation correlate with TP53 protein abundance (Pearson r = 0.89 and 0.53). In TP53-mutants: elevated ATR, MAPK3, CDK2, and CDK9 protein/phosphorylation; MDM2 decreased at RNA and protein. A recurrent X126_splice variant yields an in-frame 7-aa truncation in exon 5.
  • RB1-altered samples (12% of cohort) show negative feedback to cell-cycle effectors: downregulated RB1 protein and upregulated MCM2, MCM4, MCM6 protein. NF1–altered tumors upregulate IRF8 (RNA and protein).
  • PTPN11 and PLCG1 are convergent RTK signaling hubs. PTPN11 Y546/Y584 phosphorylation associates with high PDGFRA; PTPN11 Y62 phosphorylation tracks with high EGFR. PLCG1 Y783 phosphorylation is significantly higher in EGFR-altered tumors (Wilcox FDR < 0.01) despite no significant change in PLCG1 protein (FDR = 0.11). GAB1 Y657/Y689 phosphorylation tracks high EGFR expression.
  • 45 of 99 tumors harbor EGFR structural variants, all with copy-number amplification (high concordance SV-CNV). EGFR-mutated + SV tumors had elevated EGFR RNA, protein, and Y1172 phosphorylation. EGFR-altered tumors upregulate PHLDA1/PHLDA3 at protein but not RNA, plus SOX9, CTNND2, CDK6, and CDKN2C (SOX9 and GAB1 IHC-validated). GAB1 protein upregulated and GRB2 downregulated in EGFR-altered tumors.
  • Four immune subtypes (im1–im4) from xCell deconvolution + snRNA-seq validation. im1 = high microglia/macrophage/lymphocyte; im2 = high macrophage, low lymphocyte; im3 = high neuron, IDH-mutant-overrepresented (Fisher p = 1.02e-5); im4 = uniformly cold (1.3% T cells, 6% TAM by snRNA-seq vs. 7% and 19% in im1–3). Mesenchymal subtype enriched in im1 + im2 (Fisher p = 1.65e-15). Methylation dm3 strongly associated with im1 (p = 1.26e-5). im1 TAMs upregulate M2 markers CD163 and MRC1. Subtypes confirmed in TCGA-GBM and validated by IHC for CD3, CD68, CD163, PD-1, PD-L1.
  • im4 tumors contain morphologically large/giant cells. A deep-learning model on H&E tiles revealed a distinct cell population in im4 (inflammatory-cell fraction ~5% vs. ~20% in im1–3).
  • Mesenchymal-tumor EMT signature originates partly in tumor cells. snRNA-seq identified EMT-related genes (CHI3L1, MET, TNC, ITGA3, PDPN) upregulated in tumor cells of nmf2 samples; both tumor cells and infiltrating immune cells contribute to bulk mesenchymal signatures.
  • H2B N-terminal acetylation defines a classical-like, immune-low subset. Lasso regression links CREBBP and EP300 protein/acetylation to H2B-K12/K13/K16/K17/K21 acetylation, and BRD1/BRD3/BRD4 to additional H2B sites. H2B acetylation is negatively correlated with TME enrichment, ferroptosis, mast cells, and ROS pathways, and positively correlated with spliceosome, nuclear-receptor, and SUMOylation pathways. SUMO1 and UBE2I upregulated in high-H2B-acetylation tumors; UBE2I tracks CDK6 (Pearson r = 0.413), a known SUMOylation target.
  • Lipidomics: 582 lipids in 75 tumors, >500 differential across subtypes (Wilcoxon FDR < 0.05). Mesenchymal-like enriched for triacylglycerols (TGs) and depleted in phosphatidylcholines (PCs); proneural-like enriched for VLCFAs and long-chain PUFA glycerophospholipids. 22:6 lipids (likely DHA) and ACSL6 co-regulated; ACSL6 protein drastically diminished in tumor vs. normal, but DHA-containing PGs and TGs increased. Proneural-like ≈ normal brain for DHA metabolism; mesenchymal-like is most distant.
  • Ferroptosis pathway upregulated in mesenchymal-like GBM. ACSL4 and ALOX5 protein upregulated only in mesenchymal-like, suggesting accumulation of oxidized PEs with PUFAs. DG levels correlate with AKT1, PLCG1, and PLCD3 protein.
  • Metabolomics in IDH-mutants: 2-HG is the highest-abundance metabolite in IDH-mutants (median log2 FC = 3.62, FDR < 0.05). Glycolysis intermediates increased; serine and glutamate decreased. GLUD1 upregulated in IDH-mutants (Wilcoxon p < 0.05); GLUD1 protein anti-correlates with glutamate (Pearson r = -0.29, p < 0.01). Validation cohort confirmed GLUD1 elevation (p = 0.07).
  • Pathway-integrated views across RTK/RAS, PI3K/AKT, and p53/cell cycle:
    • Classical tumors → amplified EGFR; proneural and IDH-mutant tumors → amplified PDGFRA; mesenchymal → upregulated MET protein, downregulated NF1 protein.
    • PTEN loss in proneural/mesenchymal/classical activates AKT1/AKT2; AKT3 elevated in IDH-mutant and proneural (developmental adult-brain expression).
    • MDM2 amplification specific to mesenchymal; MDM4 amplification specific to proneural and classical; CDKN2A/CDKN2B differential between IDH-WT and IDH-mutant.
  • Druggable kinase-substrate networks. GSK3B phosphorylation tracks with mTOR substrates (RPTOR, TSC1) and Wnt substrates (CTNNB1, APC). AKT1S1 connects strongly to AKT1/AKT2/AKT3 kinases. EGFR phosphorylates CTNNB1 S33 (N-terminal proteasomal-degradation site). ABL1 interacts with HDAC2 deacetylase (~5% phospho outliers); HDAC2 S422 phosphorylation tracks activity.
  • Drug-connectivity analysis (LINCS L1000 RNA + P100 phospho). For EGFR-altered tumors: phosphoproteomics predicts a broader kinase-inhibitor panel beyond canonical EGFR inhibitors; RNA alone suggested HDAC inhibitors. For NF1-altered tumors: both RNA and phospho signatures converge on MAPK inhibitors as top reversers. For RB1-altered and immune-subtype perturbations, RNA vs. phospho readouts agreed variably.

Genes & alterations

  • EGFR — 45/99 tumors with structural variants (all amplified); mutation, SV, and amplification co-occurred (p < 0.01). Elevated RNA, protein, Y1172 autophosphorylation; trans effects on PTPN11 Y62, PLCG1 Y783, GAB1 Y657/Y689, MAP3K1 S1408, RB1 S795/Y805. EGFR phosphorylates CTNNB1 S33.
  • PDGFRA — Amplified preferentially in proneural and IDH-mutant tumors; elevated phospho at S1067/S1070 and trans-effect on PTPN11 Y546/Y584.
  • MET — Protein upregulated in mesenchymal tumors and identified as a mesenchymal-cell-state marker in snRNA-seq.
  • TP53 — Largely missense mutations in DNA-binding domain (hotspots + rare variants); protein elevated relative to TP53-WT without RNA change. Recurrent X126_splice yields in-frame 7-aa truncation in exon 5. Phosphorylation TP53 S315 and TP53BP1 S1099/S1106/S1109 correlate with TP53 abundance (Pearson r = 0.89 / 0.53). TP53-mutant tumors show upregulated DNA-repair phosphosites without protein-level upregulation; ATR/MAPK3/CDK2/CDK9 elevated; MDM2 decreased.
  • RB1 — Altered in 12% of cohort; RB1 protein downregulated, MCM2/MCM4/MCM6 protein upregulated.
  • NF1 — Loss-of-function alterations track decreased NF1 RNA/protein and increased IRF8 (microglial motility TF). Drug-connectivity analysis nominates MAPK inhibitors.
  • PTEN — Mutations/deletions in proneural, mesenchymal, classical; concordant decrease in RNA/protein.
  • ATRX — 9 mutants with diminished ATRX RNA and protein (IHC-validated); mutually exclusive with TERT promoter hotspot mutations; co-occurs with TP53 and IDH1 R132H. Complex partners’ expression not affected by ATRX loss.
  • TERT — Promoter C228T/C250T mutations in 74% of primary GBMs with elevated TERT RNA.
  • IDH1 — 6 R132H mutants + 1 R222C non-hotspot mutant; earlier disease onset (median 47 vs. 59 years, t-test p = 0.055). 2-HG highest-abundance metabolite (median log2 FC = 3.62, FDR < 0.05). GLUD1 upregulated; glutamate decreased.
  • MGMT — Promoter hypermethylation in 38/90 tumors (42%); MGMT RNA (t-test p = 4.9e-11) and protein (p = 2.6e-6) significantly decreased.
  • PTPN11 (Shp2) — Y62 phosphorylation activated by EGFR; Y546/Y584 by PDGFRA. Protein level unchanged; activity regulated by phosphorylation. Forms complex with GAB1/GRB2 to activate RAS pathway. Identified as a convergent RTK signaling hub.
  • PLCG1 — Y783 phosphorylation activating; significantly elevated in EGFR-altered tumors (Wilcox FDR < 0.01) without change in PLCG1 protein (FDR = 0.11). DG levels correlate with PLCG1 protein.
  • GAB1 — Y657/Y689 phosphorylation tracks EGFR; protein upregulated in EGFR-altered tumors (IHC-validated).
  • GRB2 — Protein downregulated in EGFR-altered tumors.
  • SOX9 — Protein upregulated in EGFR-altered tumors; IHC-validated in 5/6 tested tumors.
  • PHLDA1 / PHLDA3 — Protein upregulated in EGFR-altered tumors without RNA change.
  • CDK6, CDKN2C, CTNND2 — Upregulated in EGFR-altered tumors.
  • CTNNB1 — β-catenin protein elevated despite decreased mRNA in EGFR-altered tumors; EGFR phosphorylates CTNNB1 S33 (proteasomal-degradation site).
  • MDM2 — Amplification specific to mesenchymal subtype.
  • MDM4 — Amplification specific to proneural and classical subtypes.
  • AKT1, AKT2 — Potentially activated downstream of PTEN loss; AKT1 protein correlates with DG levels.
  • AKT3 — Higher expression in IDH-mutant and proneural tumors (developmental adult-brain pattern).
  • CDKN2A / CDKN2B — Differential alteration patterns between IDH-WT and IDH-mutant tumors.
  • ATR, MAPK3, CDK2, CDK9 — Protein/phospho elevated in TP53-mutant tumors.
  • MCM2, MCM4, MCM6 — Protein upregulated in RB1-altered tumors.
  • IRF8 — RNA + protein upregulated in NF1-altered tumors.
  • CREBBP, EP300 — Protein/acetylation levels strongly drive H2B-K12/K13/K16/K17/K21 acetylation (Lasso regression).
  • BRD1, BRD3, BRD4 — Correlate with H2B-N-terminal acetylation sites; bind acetylated histones and mediate transcription.
  • SUMO1, UBE2I — Upregulated in tumors with high H2B acetylation; UBE2I tracks CDK6 (Pearson r = 0.413).
  • ACSL4, ALOX5 — Protein upregulated only in mesenchymal-like tumors; suggests ferroptosis-pathway activation and accumulation of oxidized PUFA-PEs.
  • ACSL6 — Protein severely diminished in tumor vs. normal brain; co-regulated with 22:6 (DHA)-containing lipids.
  • GLUD1 — Protein upregulated in IDH-mutant tumors (Wilcoxon p < 0.05; validation p = 0.07); anti-correlates with glutamate (Pearson r = -0.29, p < 0.01).
  • GSK3B — Phosphorylation positively associated with mTOR substrates (RPTOR, TSC1) and Wnt substrates (CTNNB1, APC).
  • ABL1, HDAC2 — Inferred kinase-deacetylase interaction (~5% phospho outliers); HDAC2 S422 phosphorylation tracks activity.

Clinical implications

  • PTPN11 / PLCG1 as RTK-pathway therapeutic hubs. Convergent activation of PTPN11 Y62 and PLCG1 Y783 phosphosites downstream of both EGFR and PDGFRA suggests a shared phospho-signaling bottleneck that could be targeted across multiple RTK-altered GBM subtypes.
  • EGFR-altered tumors: broader kinase-inhibitor opportunity. Drug-connectivity analysis on LINCS L1000 + P100 indicates that phosphoproteomic signatures of EGFR alteration are more strongly reversed by non-canonical kinase inhibitors (beyond approved EGFR inhibitors) than by HDAC inhibitors predicted from RNA alone.
  • NF1-altered tumors: MAPK-pathway inhibition. Both transcriptomic and phosphoproteomic perturbation responses converge on MAPK inhibitors as top signature-reversing compounds for NF1-altered GBM.
  • TP53-mutant tumors: DNA-repair phosphosite vulnerability. Upregulated DNA-repair phosphosites in TP53-mutant tumors (in the absence of total protein-level upregulation), combined with elevated ATR, CDK2, and CDK9, nominate DNA-damage-response inhibitors (e.g., ATR inhibitors, CDK inhibitors) for TP53-mutant GBM.
  • RB1-altered tumors: MCM2/4/6 as biomarkers / vulnerabilities. RB1-altered tumors (12% of cohort) show coordinated upregulation of MCM2, MCM4, MCM6.
  • Mesenchymal-like GBM: ferroptosis and metabolic vulnerabilities. ALOX5/ACSL4 upregulation, oxidized PE accumulation, TG enrichment, and PC depletion mark a metabolic profile distinct from other subtypes — suggesting ferroptosis induction and lipid-metabolism targeting as hypotheses to test.
  • Classical-like / immune-low GBM: BRD- and CBP/p300-driven H2B acetylation. Therapeutic relevance of BET inhibitors (against BRD1/BRD3/BRD4) and CREBBP/EP300 inhibitors as candidates for this subtype.
  • IDH-mutant tumors: GLUD1 / glutamine-glutamate axis. Significantly elevated GLUD1 protein in IDH-mutants suggests GLUD1 inhibition or glutaminase blockade as metabolic strategies (hypothesis-generating; not tested clinically here).
  • Mixed-subtype tumors are a high-risk class. 12 tumors with mixed multi-omics membership had significantly worse survival (log-rank p = 1.7e-4), arguing that single-modality (e.g., RNA-based) classification under-stratifies prognosis.
  • MGMT-promoter hypermethylation confirmed at protein level. 42% of tumors with MGMT-promoter hypermethylation show concordant protein loss (p = 2.6e-6), reinforcing the rationale for temozolomide response stratification by MGMT methylation (well-established prior literature).

Limitations & open questions

  • Cohort size (n = 99) is large for proteogenomic GBM but limits power for subset analyses: 6 IDH1-mutants, 12 mixed-subtype tumors, 5 im4 snRNA-seq samples, only 1 im3 snRNA-seq sample.
  • snRNA-seq data on 18/99 tumors is unbalanced across immune subtypes (7 im1, 5 im2, 1 im3, 5 im4); im1 microglia/macrophage signal seen in bulk was not reproduced in im1 snRNA-seq, suggesting bulk signal is driven by TAM abundance and TAM-intrinsic expression, but is not orthogonally validated.
  • Acetylated peptides recovered from the IMAC flow-through cannot detect dual-modified phospho+acetyl peptides; the authors acknowledge larger input would be needed to recover these.
  • 10 GTEx normal frontal-cortex samples are unmatched (different patients, different processing) — lipidomic and metabolomic comparisons against “normal” are limited.
  • Functional/mechanistic validation is largely IHC-based; kinase-substrate relationships (e.g., EGFR → CTNNB1 S33, ABL1 → HDAC2) are inferred from outlier/network analyses and not directly tested.
  • Drug-connectivity predictions (LINCS L1000 / P100) are signature-based; no preclinical or clinical confirmation is reported in this paper.
  • The CBTTC validation cohort (n = 39) is pediatric/AYA HGG (age 14–39), not adult GBM, so validation is partial.
  • Treatment outcomes (e.g., temozolomide response) were not analyzed in relation to the new multi-omics subtypes — the cohort is treatment-naive by design.
  • IDH-mutant biology (n = 6) is under-powered for many of the comparative analyses (lipidomic, metabolomic, immune).
