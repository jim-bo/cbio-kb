# CHD1L

## Overview

CHD1L (Chromodomain Helicase DNA Binding Protein 1-Like), located at chromosome 1q21.3, encodes an ATP-dependent chromatin-remodeling enzyme. It is a known cancer gene recurrently amplified in hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) and other solid tumors, where amplification at the 1q21.3 locus is among the most frequent somatic copy-number alterations.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- 1q21.3 amplification peak gene; identified as a known cancer gene among cis-driver candidates in a European multi-omic HCC cohort (n=122 WES, n=51 proteome/phosphoproteome) using integrated genomic, transcriptomic, proteomic, and phosphoproteomic analysis [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **HCC**: 1q21.3 amplification is a recurrent copy-number event; CHD1L at this peak locus is a candidate cis-driver [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-locates with UBQLN4 at the 1q21.3 amplification peak in HCC; UBQLN4 is reported to repress homologous recombination and confer PARP1-inhibitor sensitivity [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic data in the corpus. The 1q21.3 amplification locus harbors UBQLN4 which has been linked to PARP1-inhibitor sensitivity, warranting further investigation of co-amplified genes including CHD1L [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Open questions

- Functional contribution of CHD1L amplification to the 1q21.3 cis-driver effect in HCC remains incompletely characterized relative to co-amplified UBQLN4 and other locus genes [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)
