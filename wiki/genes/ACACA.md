# ACACA

## Overview

ACACA (acetyl-CoA carboxylase alpha) catalyzes the first committed step in de novo lipogenesis, converting acetyl-CoA to malonyl-CoA. In [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md), ACACA is an upregulated DNL effector downstream of SREBP1 and [MYC](../genes/MYC.md).

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- ACACA upregulated in HCC as a DNL effector; part of the FASN/SCD/ACACA DNL axis transcriptionally driven by SREBF1/MYC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) — ACACA upregulation drives de novo lipogenesis [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Part of the FASN/SCD/ACACA DNL axis downstream of [SREBF1](../genes/SREBF1.md) in HCC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- ACACA inhibition is proposed as part of the lipid-targeting strategy to resensitize HCC to TKIs and ICIs [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Open questions

- Selective ACACA inhibitors with acceptable hepatotoxicity are not yet established for HCC.
