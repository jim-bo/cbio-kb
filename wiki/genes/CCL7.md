# CCL7

## Overview

CCL7 (C-C Motif Chemokine Ligand 7; also known as MCP-3) is a monocyte/macrophage chemoattractant chemokine. Together with [CCL2](../genes/CCL2.md), [CCL8](../genes/CCL8.md), and [CCL13](../genes/CCL13.md), elevated CCL7 expression in pretreatment melanoma biopsies marks tumors innately resistant to anti-PD-1 checkpoint immunotherapy, as part of the IPRES (innate anti-PD-1 resistance) transcriptional signature.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- CCL7 was identified as a monocyte/macrophage chemoattractant up-regulated in non-responders to anti-PD-1 therapy in melanoma, part of the IPRES innate resistance transcriptional signature [PMID:26997480](../papers/26997480.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md): CCL7 overexpression in pretreatment melanoma biopsies is enriched in tumors that fail to respond to anti-PD-1 therapy; co-upregulated with CCL2, CCL8, CCL13 [PMID:26997480](../papers/26997480.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-expressed with CCL2, CCL8, CCL13, [VEGFA](../genes/VEGFA.md), [VEGFC](../genes/VEGFC.md), [IL10](../genes/IL10.md), and mesenchymal genes ([AXL](../genes/AXL.md), [ROR2](../genes/ROR2.md), [WNT5A](../genes/WNT5A.md)) in IPRES-positive non-responding melanomas [PMID:26997480](../papers/26997480.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- As a component of the IPRES signature, CCL7 overexpression identifies patients unlikely to respond to anti-PD-1 monotherapy; combination approaches targeting the tumor-associated macrophage microenvironment are a proposed strategy [PMID:26997480](../papers/26997480.md).

## Open questions

- Prospective validation of the IPRES signature (including CCL7) in independent cohorts is required before clinical use as a biomarker [PMID:26997480](../papers/26997480.md).
