Integrative proteogenomic characterization of hepatocellular carcinoma across etiologies and stages

PMID: 35508466 · DOI: 10.1038/s41467-022-29960-8 · Journal: Nature Communications (2022)

TL;DR

Ng, Dazert, Boldanova and colleagues performed an integrated (phospho)proteogenomic characterization of 122 hepatocellular carcinoma biopsies from 114 systemically-untreated patients across BCLC stages 0–D and across HBV/HCV/alcohol/NAFLD etiologies, combining whole-exome sequencing, RNA-seq, SWATH-MS proteome (n=51), and label-free phosphoproteome (n=51) data with 15/11/10 matched normal-liver controls. The cBioPortal study identifier is hcc_meric_2021. They identified 7 significantly mutated genes (including GPAM, elevated to driver candidacy by 7/9 frameshift mutations and knockdown-induced proliferation), nominated copy-number-driven driver candidates (NUDCD1, UBQLN4, BYSL, ATP6V1C1, RRM2B) within five recurrent GISTIC2 peaks, and showed that AURKA and CDK1/CDK2/CDK5/CDK7 are activated kinases by KSEA. CTNNB1 mutations were linked to altered phosphorylation of actin-organization proteins (loss of epithelial polarity), TP53 mutations to altered phosphorylation of lipid-metabolism proteins and increased CDK1/2/5 activity; integrative iCluster analysis defined three clusters with cluster 3 (high cell proliferation, TP53 mutation, CDK1/2/6 activity) associated with poor overall survival (PMID:35508466).

Cohort & data

  • 122 HCC biopsies from 114 patients (University Hospital Basel, 2008–2018), including 6 patients with synchronous multicentric (genetically independent) tumors and 1 with three multicentric tumors. No patient had received systemic or locoregional therapy prior to biopsy (PMID:35508466).
  • Underlying liver disease: alcohol 59%, HCV 26%, NAFLD 17%, HBV 11%, no liver disease 6% (patients may have >1); 72% cirrhotic; BCLC 0/A (early) 53%, BCLC B/C/D (advanced) 47%; Edmondson grade 1/2/3/4: 6%/54%/34%/7%; immunophenotype inflamed 30% / immune-excluded 35% / immune-desert 31% (PMID:35508466).
  • Normal-liver controls from 19 patients without HCC: RNA-seq n=15, proteome n=11, phosphoproteome n=10 (PMID:35508466).
  • Dataset released as cBioPortal study hcc_meric_2021.
  • Modalities and platforms: whole-exome-seq (SureSelect XT Clinical Research Exome / SureSelect V6+COSMIC; HiSeq 2500; tumor median 94.3×, normal 49.4×; GRCh37 alignment via BWA; somatic SNVs by MuTect, indels by Strelka; significantly mutated genes by mutsigcv and oncodrivefml); rna-seq (TruSeq Stranded Total RNA with Ribo-Zero Gold, single-end 126 bp, STAR two-pass to GRCh37 + HBV/HCV genomes, RSEM quantification); swath-ms data-independent acquisition for the proteome (Q Exactive Plus, 6,167 quantified proteins, library by MaxQuant, peak extraction by Spectronaut X); label-free-proteomics (Fe(III)-IMAC enrichment, Q-Exactive HF, 12,205 phosphorylation sites in 4,230 proteins) and processed analogously to tmt-phosphoproteomics workflows (PMID:35508466).
  • Companion analytic methods: allele-specific CNAs by facets; recurrent focal CNAs by gistic 2.0; single-omics subtypes by consensus nmf-clustering (transcriptome, proteome, phosphoproteome) and consensus k-means / hierarchical clustering; integrative subtypes by icluster (and SNF as orthogonal validation); kinase activity by Kinase-Substrate Enrichment Analysis (ksea); reference genome GRCh37 (PMID:35508466).

Key findings

  • Moderate transcriptome-proteome concordance with discordant translational regulation. Across HCC vs normal liver, mRNA and protein fold-changes correlated at Spearman ρ=0.33 (p<0.0001); quadrant analysis showed 37.7% of genes up-regulated on both RNA and protein, 24.8% down-regulated on both, 20.9% up-mRNA/down-protein, and 16.6% down-mRNA/up-protein. mRNA-splicing/epigenetic-rRNA/translation pathways were up in both; metabolism of amino acids/fatty acids/cofactors was down in both; translational-control genes were up only at mRNA; complement/coagulation up only at protein (PMID:35508466).
  • Five recurrent GISTIC2 peaks harbor 19 cis-regulated dysregulated genes. Of 29 cis-copy-number-regulated genes (CNA-mRNA and mRNA-protein Spearman ρ>0.5) inside enriched GISTIC2 regions, 19 were dysregulated at mRNA, 11 at protein, and 9 at both (ATP6V1C1, BYSL, CHD1L, NUDCD1, LRRC47, RRM2B, UBQLN4, XPO5, YWHAZ). The five peaks span 1p36.23 (del), 1q21.3 (amp), 6p21.2 (amp), 8q22.2 (amp), 8q24.13 (amp) (PMID:35508466).
  • Seven significantly mutated genes by MutSigCV and OncodriveFML: ALB, ARID1A, AXIN1, CDKN1A, CTNNB1, TP53, and GPAM (7.4% in this cohort vs 1.8% in a prior meta-analysis, Fisher p=0.001). 7 of 9 GPAM mutations were frameshift, and knockdown of GPAM significantly increased proliferation in HepG2 and Huh7 cell lines — together strongly suggestive of a tumor-suppressor role (PMID:35508466).
  • CTNNB1 and TP53 mutations are mutually exclusive (Fisher p=0.012). CTNNB1-mutant tumors were lower-grade and immune-desert (p=0.039); TP53-mutant tumors were higher-grade (p=0.001) and HBV-associated (p=0.010). Multivariate Cox analysis (independent of BCLC) flagged CDKN2A, GPAM, KEAP1, and TSC2 mutations as associated with poor overall survival (PMID:35508466).
  • Phosphoproteome reveals activated AURKA, CDKs, ERK1/2, PLK1. Differential phosphorylation analysis identified 692/648 hyper-/hypo-phosphorylated sites (adjusted p≤0.05, |log2FC|>1) and 302/355 protein-normalized hyper-/hypo sites. KSEA showed increased activity of AURKA, CDK1/CDK2/CDK5/CDK7, ERK1/2 (MAPK1/MAPK3), and PLK1, and decreased activity of PKACA/G (PRKACA/PRKACG), PKCA/Z (PRKCA/PRKCZ), and SGK1 (PMID:35508466).
  • CTNNB1-mutant HCCs have altered actin-organization phosphorylation. Of 23 differentially expressed proteins, 13 also differed at mRNA, including GLUL, AMACR, and ACSS3; 189 phosphosites differing from CTNNB1-WT (without protein-level difference) were enriched for actin-filament organization. Hyperphosphorylated sites included Par3-α (PARD3) S1046 and PDLIM2 S197; hypophosphorylation at MAP1B S541/S937/S1396 and ACF7 (MACF1) S1752 — consistent with GSK3 inactivation in Wnt signaling. Epithelial markers CDH1 (E-cadherin) and KRT19 were reduced, and KSEA showed increased AMPKα2 and decreased CK II / CDK5 activity (PMID:35508466).
  • TP53-mutant HCCs show altered lipid-metabolism phosphorylation and CDK1/2/5 activity. 399 differentially-expressed proteins (238 also at mRNA); STMN2 and NCOR1 overexpressed at protein but underexpressed at mRNA; 12 proteins (including CENPF, TACC3, KNTC1) underexpressed at protein but overexpressed at mRNA. PKR/EIF2AK2 S83 autophosphorylation was elevated, and KSEA showed increased CDK1/CDK2/CDK5, AURKA, TTK, NEK2, PKR (EIF2AK2), PRKD2, p38 delta (MAPK13) (PMID:35508466).
  • Three integrative iCluster subtypes recapitulated by SNF. Cluster 1: ECM organization, angiogenesis, amino-acid metabolism. Cluster 2: fatty-acid/amino-acid/small-molecule metabolism (low grade, CTNNB1 enriched, improved OS, p=0.015). Cluster 3: cell proliferation, chromatin assembly, RNA processing, CDK1/2/6 activity (high grade, TP53 enriched, macrovascular invasion p=0.01, poor OS p=0.005). Integrative clusters were associated with mutation, transcriptome and proteome single-omics clusterings (all p≤0.001) but not with CNA or phosphoproteome clusters (PMID:35508466).
  • Two proteome and two phosphoproteome subclasses. Proteome subclass 1 (ribonucleoprotein organization, mRNA splicing/processing) was associated with high grade/high stage and poor OS (p=0.022); proteome subclass 2 (metabolism, oxidative phosphorylation) with improved OS. Phosphoproteome subclasses (subclass 1: ribonucleoprotein/mRNA-splicing/chromatin/CK II alpha; subclass 2: telomere maintenance/DNA repair/actin cytoskeleton/PKA C-alpha) did NOT associate with single-omics or integrative clusters, attributed to the dynamic, noisier nature of phosphoproteomic snapshots (PMID:35508466).

Genes & alterations

  • GPAM — newly nominated significantly mutated gene (7.4% vs 1.8% prior meta-analysis, Fisher p=0.001); 7/9 mutations frameshift; HepG2 and Huh7 knockdown increased proliferation → candidate HCC tumor suppressor; poor-OS association in multivariate Cox independent of BCLC (PMID:35508466).
  • CTNNB1 — mutually exclusive with TP53 (p=0.012); lower grade (p=0.017); immune-desert (p=0.039); good prognosis (p=0.025 multivariate). Mutant HCCs show altered phosphorylation of PARD3, PDLIM2, MAP1B, and MACF1, consistent with loss of epithelial polarity and Wnt-driven cytoskeletal remodeling; KSEA shows increased AMPKα2 and decreased CK II / CDK5 activity. Wnt target genes NKD1, AXIN2, RNF43, ALDH3A1 differ at mRNA but not at protein in CTNNB1 mutants (PMID:35508466).
  • TP53 — higher grade (p=0.001); HBV-associated (p=0.010); poor prognosis (p=0.034 multivariate, independent of BCLC). Mutant HCCs show altered phosphorylation in lipid- and mRNA-metabolism proteins; increased CDK1/CDK2/CDK5, AURKA, TTK, NEK2, EIF2AK2, PRKD2, and p38δ (MAPK13) activity by KSEA; PKR (EIF2AK2) S83 activating autophosphorylation elevated (PMID:35508466).
  • ARID1A, AXIN1, CDKN1A, ALB — SMGs identified in this cohort consistent with prior HCC genomic surveys (PMID:35508466).
  • CDKN2A, KEAP1, TSC2 — non-SMG cancer genes mutated in ≥3 HCCs; associated with poor OS independent of BCLC in multivariate Cox (PMID:35508466).
  • TERT — previously established as the most frequently mutated gene in HCC (promoter); referenced as a known driver but explicitly excluded from this study’s SMG analysis (TERT promoter not captured by WES) (PMID:35508466).
  • APOB, ARID2, RB1 — non-SMG cancer genes mutated in ≥3 HCCs and analyzed for clinicopathological correlation; APOB and TP53 mutations were associated with lower mRNA and protein expression of the cognate gene (PMID:35508466).
  • CHD1L (1q21.3 amplification peak), YWHAZ (14-3-3 zeta, 8q22.2) — known cancer genes among cis-driver candidates (PMID:35508466).
  • NUDCD1 (OVA66, 8q22.2) — promotes EMT and apoptosis evasion via PI3K/AKT, ERK1/2-MAPK, IGF-1R-MAPK; cis-driver candidate (PMID:35508466).
  • UBQLN4 (1q21.3) — regulates Wnt-β-catenin pathway; associated with genomic instability; reported to repress homologous recombination repair and confer PARP1-inhibitor sensitivity, suggesting a therapeutic angle (PMID:35508466).
  • BYSL (6p21.1) — direct cMYC target; required for nucleologenesis in HCC proliferation; promotes glioma/glioblastoma via GSK-3β/β-catenin and AKT/mTOR (PMID:35508466).
  • ATP6V1C1 (8q22.3) — V-ATPase subunit with roles in Wnt/β-catenin, Notch, mTOR signaling and migration; role in HCC unclear but suggested by cis-driver pattern (PMID:35508466).
  • RRM2B (p53R2, 8q22.3) — p53 target / DNA damage and replication stress regulator; conflicting reports of tumor-suppressor vs oncogenic roles; nominated as gemcitabine target in hypoxic conditions (PMID:35508466).
  • XPO5, LRRC47 — cis-driver candidates dysregulated at both mRNA and protein levels (PMID:35508466).
  • AURKA, CDK1, CDK2, CDK5, CDK7, PLK1, MAPK1, MAPK3 — kinases with increased activity by KSEA in HCC vs normal liver; flagged as targetable, with AURKA and CDK1/2/5 highlighted (PMID:35508466).
  • PRKACA, PRKACG, PRKCA, PRKCZ, SGK1 — kinases with decreased activity by KSEA in HCC vs normal liver (PMID:35508466).
  • PARD3, PDLIM2, MAP1B, MACF1 — actin-organization phosphoproteins differentially phosphorylated in CTNNB1-mutant HCCs (PMID:35508466).
  • GLUL, AMACR, ACSS3 — proteins concordantly up at mRNA and protein in CTNNB1-mutant HCCs (PMID:35508466).
  • STMN2, NCOR1 — up at protein but down at mRNA in TP53-mutant HCCs (PMID:35508466).
  • CENPF, TACC3, KNTC1 — mitotic-spindle regulators down at protein but up at mRNA in TP53-mutant HCCs (PMID:35508466).
  • TNRC6B (β-catenin-independent Wnt signaling), PRKCE (β-catenin binding partner), PPIE (spliceosome / FAST lncRNA / β-catenin axis) — among the 10 CTNNB1-mutant differentially-expressed proteins NOT also differential at mRNA, implicating post-transcriptional regulation (PMID:35508466).
  • EIF2AK2 (PKR) — S83 activating autophosphorylation elevated in TP53-mutant HCCs; involved in stress response and HCV/HBV signaling (PMID:35508466).
  • TTK, NEK2, PRKD2, MAPK13, GSK3B — additional kinases with elevated activity in TP53-mutant or overall HCC by KSEA (PMID:35508466).
  • PYCR2, ADH1A — cited from prior HBV-HCC proteogenomics as prognostic biomarkers; framing for the current study (PMID:35508466).
  • SOAT1 — cited from prior HBV-HCC work where avasimibe-mediated SOAT1 inhibition reduced PDX tumor size; context for the current study (PMID:35508466).

Clinical implications

  • Aurora Kinase A and CDK inhibitors as candidate HCC therapeutics. Authors note preclinical evidence of efficacy in HCC models, potential synergy with sorafenib/regorafenib and chemotherapeutic agents, and that CDK1/2/5/7 plus AURKA emerge as the dominant elevated kinase activities by KSEA (PMID:35508466).
  • CDK4/6 inhibition for integrative cluster 3. iCluster 3 was characterized by elevated cell-cycle signatures and CDK1/2/6 activity; CDK4/6 are well-established therapeutic targets, suggesting drug-target stratification in this subset (PMID:35508466).
  • Wnt signaling as a target in integrative cluster 2. Cluster 2 was enriched for CTNNB1 mutants and improved OS; Wnt-pathway intervention is suggested as a candidate therapeutic axis in this subset (PMID:35508466).
  • PARP1 inhibition for UBQLN4-overexpressing tumors. UBQLN4 was reported to repress homologous recombination and confer PARP1-inhibitor sensitivity, suggesting a therapeutic angle for UBQLN4-driven HCCs (PMID:35508466).
  • gemcitabine for RRM2B-elevated tumors. RRM2B encodes a ribonucleotide-reductase subunit that may be targeted by gemcitabine, particularly in hypoxic conditions (PMID:35508466).
  • Prognostic mutation signatures independent of BCLC. Multivariate Cox analysis identified CDKN2A, GPAM, KEAP1, TSC2 mutations and TP53-mutation cluster 3 as poor-prognosis markers, and CTNNB1-mutation cluster 2 as good-prognosis — independent of standard BCLC clinical staging (PMID:35508466).
  • Subtype-specific therapeutic stratification. Integrative cluster 3 (poor OS, macrovascular invasion, TP53, CDK1/2/6 activity) is the closest analog to the “proliferation” subclass described in prior HBV-HCC proteogenomic studies; cluster 2 (good OS, metabolic, CTNNB1) maps to “metabolic” subclasses — suggesting cross-cohort generalizability of stratified therapy (PMID:35508466).
  • Avasimibe/alisertib precedent. Authors cite prior HBV-HCC work where SOAT1 inhibition by avasimibe reduced PDX tumor size in cholesterol-homeostasis-disrupted HCCs; explicitly framed as motivation for proteome-driven target nomination, but avasimibe is not directly tested here (PMID:35508466).

Limitations & open questions

  • Phosphoproteome subclasses do not align with mutation/transcriptome/proteome subclasses or integrative clusters. Authors interpret this as evidence that phosphorylation captures a transient dynamic snapshot, inherently noisier than other molecular layers (PMID:35508466).
  • Heterogeneous cohort accrual over a decade (2008–2018) during which clinical practice evolved — overall-survival biomarker interpretation is therefore limited; multivariate adjustment for BCLC is performed but treatment-era differences are not modeled (PMID:35508466).
  • (Phospho)proteomics performed on 51/122 tumors only, although the subset was representative of the full cohort across clinicopathological and molecular parameters. Proteome and phosphoproteome data are inherently sparse — not all proteins/phosphosites can be detected (PMID:35508466).
  • CTNNB1- and TP53-mutation phospho-signatures are correlative. Mutagenesis experiments would be required to properly attribute the observed phosphorylation changes to those mutations (PMID:35508466).
  • GPAM frameshift functional dissection incomplete. The seven frameshift variants and the knockdown-induced proliferation phenotype together suggest tumor-suppressor function, but individual variant impact and the full mechanistic basis remain to be characterized (PMID:35508466).
  • Contrast with prior HBV-HCC proteogenomics. Authors did NOT observe the elevated ALDOA S36 phosphorylation reported in HBV-associated CTNNB1-mutant HCCs (FDR=0.98 in this cohort) — possibly reflecting etiology or methodological differences (PMID:35508466).
  • Cohort is European, single-institution. Most prior HCC proteogenomic studies were HBV-enriched East Asian cohorts; cross-population generalizability of the integrative subtypes requires external validation (PMID:35508466).
  • Reference genome GRCh37; while standard for WES/RNA-seq at the time, downstream re-integration with GRCh38-based resources may require liftover (PMID:35508466).
