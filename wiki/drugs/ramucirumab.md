# ramucirumab

## Overview

Ramucirumab is a human monoclonal antibody targeting VEGFR2 ([KDR](../genes/KDR.md)), blocking VEGF-mediated angiogenic signaling. It is approved as second-line therapy in hepatocellular carcinoma ([HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)) for patients with serum AFP ≥400 ng/mL — the first biomarker-selected approval in HCC.

## Evidence in the corpus

- REACH-2 trial (AFP ≥400 ng/mL HCC): ramucirumab median OS 8.5 vs 7.3 months (HR 0.71, 95% CI 0.53–0.95, P=0.0199); first positive biomarker-enriched phase III trial in HCC; approved second-line for AFP-high patients [PMID:24798001](../papers/24798001.md).
- Ramucirumab + durvalumab combination under evaluation (NCT02572687) as part of anti-VEGF/VEGFR + anti-PD-(L)1 combination strategies [PMID:24798001](../papers/24798001.md).
