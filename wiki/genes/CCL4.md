# CCL4

## Overview

CCL4 (C-C Motif Chemokine Ligand 4, also known as MIP-1beta) is a chemokine that mediates immune cell recruitment, particularly T-cell and NK-cell trafficking to sites of inflammation. In the tumor immune microenvironment, CCL4 contributes to anti-tumor immunity, and its loss is associated with T-cell exclusion phenotypes.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Concordantly downregulated in T-cell-depleted [UTUC](../cancer_types/UTUC.md) cluster (28/32, 87.5% of UTUC tumors); part of the immune-exclusion signature alongside [CD8A](../genes/CD8A.md), [CCL2](../genes/CCL2.md), [CCL3](../genes/CCL3.md), [CCL5](../genes/CCL5.md), and [IFNG](../genes/IFNG.md) response genes in upper-tract urothelial carcinoma [PMID:31278255](../papers/31278255.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Upper-tract urothelial carcinoma (UTUC): CCL4 downregulation is part of the T-cell-depleted immune phenotype seen in 87.5% of UTUC tumors, which is associated with reduced ICI response [PMID:31278255](../papers/31278255.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-downregulated with CD8A, CCL2, CCL3, CCL5, [CXCL9](../genes/CXCL9.md), [CXCL10](../genes/CXCL10.md), and IFNG in T-cell-depleted UTUC; [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) activation is proposed as the upstream driver of this immune-exclusion signature [PMID:31278255](../papers/31278255.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- CCL4 downregulation in FGFR3-activated UTUC provides rationale for combining FGFR3 inhibitors with immune checkpoint blockade to remodel the immune microenvironment [PMID:31278255](../papers/31278255.md)

## Open questions

- Whether FGFR3 inhibition restores CCL4 expression in vivo in UTUC patient tumors has not been directly demonstrated; evidence is from UCB cell line models.
