**PMID:** 25401301 · **DOI:** 10.1038/ng.3146 · **Journal:** Nature Genetics (2015)

## TL;DR

Durinck et al. performed integrated exome, RNA-seq, and SNP copy-number profiling on 167 primary non–clear cell renal cell carcinomas (nccRCC) — 67 [papillary RCC](../cancer_types/PRCC.md), 49 [chromophobe RCC](../cancer_types/CHRCC.md), 35 [renal oncocytoma](../cancer_types/ROCY.md), 6 [translocation RCC](../cancer_types/TRCC.md), 8 unclassified and 2 sarcomatoid — to define the molecular taxonomy of subtypes that are largely excluded from clinical trials. They identified ten significantly mutated genes in pRCC (including [MET](../genes/MET.md) mutated in 15% of samples with previously unreported activating kinase-domain alterations) and six in chRCC ([TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [PDHB](../genes/PDHB.md), [PRKAG2](../genes/PRKAG2.md), among others). A five-gene RNA-seq classifier (ASB1, GLYAT, PDZK1IP1, PLCG2, SDCBP2) separates pRCC, chRCC and renal oncocytoma at 95.3% accuracy. RNA-seq also uncovered novel fusions including a transforming [ACTG1](../genes/ACTG1.md)-[MITF](../genes/MITF.md) fusion in pRCC and CLTC-[TFEB](../genes/TFEB.md) in an unclassified case, defining a broader "MiTF-high" subtype in which the anti-apoptotic gene [BIRC7](../genes/BIRC7.md) is upregulated as a potential drug target.

## Cohort & data

- **167 primary nccRCC tumors** with patient-matched normals from the University of Texas Southwestern Kidney Cancer Program: 67 [[PRCC|pRCC]], 49 [[CHRCC|chRCC]] (36 classic, 12 eosinophilic, 1 mixed), 35 [[ROCY|renal oncocytomas]], 8 unclassified RCCs, 6 [[TRCC|tRCCs]], 2 sarcomatoid.
- **Dataset**: [nccrcc_genentech_2014](../datasets/nccrcc_genentech_2014.md) (cBioPortal study id; raw data deposited at EGA `EGAS00001000926`).
- **Assays**: [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) (Agilent SureSelect All Exon 50Mb, Illumina HiSeq 2000, ~82× mean coverage) on 140 tumor-normal pairs + 5 tumor-only; [targeted SPET capture](../methods/targeted-dna-seq.md) using NuGEN Ovation Cancer Panel (344 genes) on 19 pRCCs; [rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) on 159 tumors (TruSeq, HiSeq 2000, ~68M PE reads/sample); SNP-based copy-number profiling ([Illumina HumanOmni2.5-8](../methods/affymetrix-snp6.md) arrays); orthogonal validation by [sanger-sequencing](../methods/sanger-sequencing.md) (96% indel validation rate), [mass-spectrometry genotyping](../methods/sequenom-genotyping.md) (92% SNV validation rate) and [FISH](../methods/fish.md) for amplifications and translocations.
- Reads aligned with [BWA](../methods/bwa.md) to GRCh37/hg19; mutational signatures derived using the Genentech/Huber pipeline.

## Key findings

- **Mutational burden**: pRCC carried significantly more protein-coding alterations than ccRCC (mean 61 ± 31 vs 45 ± 19; *P* = 3.6 × 10⁻⁷), and substantially more than chRCC and renal oncocytoma.
- **Validation**: 90% (4,280/4,751) of protein-altering mutations were novel relative to COSMIC; mass-spec genotyping validated 92% (164/178) of selected SNVs and Sanger validated 96% (127/132) of indels. RNA-seq confirmed expression of 1,412 somatic variants.
- **Mutational signatures**: Five distinct signatures (S1–S5) identified across nccRCC. pRCC dominated by S1+S3; chRCC and renal oncocytoma dominated by S3+S4. Kidney cancer subtypes co-clustered by signature.
- **Significantly mutated genes in pRCC** (q ≥ 1, FDR ≤ 10%): [MET](../genes/MET.md), [SLC5A3](../genes/SLC5A3.md), [NF2](../genes/NF2.md), [PNKD](../genes/PNKD.md), [CPQ](../genes/CPQ.md), [LRP2](../genes/LRP2.md), [CHD3](../genes/CHD3.md), [NHERF1](../genes/NHERF1.md) (SLC9A3R1), [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md), [CRTC1](../genes/CRTC1.md).
- **Significantly mutated genes in chRCC**: [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [FAAH2](../genes/FAAH2.md), [PDHB](../genes/PDHB.md), [PDXDC1](../genes/PDXDC1.md), [ZNF765](../genes/ZNF765.md).
- **Significantly mutated genes in renal oncocytoma**: [ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md), [C2CD4C](../genes/C2CD4C.md).
- **MET in pRCC**: mutated in 15% (10/65) of pRCC, up from a previously reported 8% (9/121). Nearly all mutations were in the kinase domain. Four newly identified activating alterations (p.Val1088Ala, p.Ile1095Thr, recurrent p.Phe1218Ile, and the extracellular p.Asp153Tyr) were experimentally validated — they conferred elevated MET phosphorylation and anchorage-independent growth of NIH3T3 cells over wild-type MET. MET mutations were mutually exclusive with [NF2](../genes/NF2.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md) and [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md) mutations (low frequency, not statistically significant).
- **PDHB / PRKAG2 in chRCC**: [PDHB](../genes/PDHB.md) (pyruvate dehydrogenase E1β) mutations p.Phe222fs*35 and p.Arg105Leu; [PRKAG2](../genes/PRKAG2.md) p.Ile388Val (in pseudosubstrate sequence) and recurrent p.Arg531Gln (analogous to the GSC-causing mutation), plus an analogous [PRKAG1](../genes/PRKAG1.md) p.Arg299Gln in an independent chRCC dataset — implicating metabolic deregulation (AMPK + PDH) in a subtype of chRCC.
- **TP53 in chRCC**: significantly enriched in the chRCC **classic** subtype (*P* = 2.3 × 10⁻⁵); essentially absent from eosinophilic chRCC.
- **Five-gene RNA-seq classifier**: ASB1, GLYAT, PDZK1IP1, PLCG2 and SDCBP2 distinguish pRCC, chRCC and renal oncocytoma. Leave-one-out k-NN on a held-out 43-sample validation cohort (16 chRCC, 10 renal oncocytoma, 17 pRCC) reached 95.3% accuracy (41/43); replicated on an independent expression cohort (4 ROCY, 4 chRCC, 19 pRCC).
- **Copy-number changes**: pRCC frequently amplified whole chromosomes 3, 7, 12, 16, 17, 20; ~70% had chr7 amplification (containing [MET](../genes/MET.md)). Sample 16864T showed a 22-Mb chr7 focal amplification with the highest MET expression in the cohort. Sample 1216T had a 490-kb chr6 amplification encompassing [TFEB](../genes/TFEB.md) with the highest TFEB expression (re-reviewed as low-grade RCC with papillary/oncocytic features). Classic chRCCs showed frequent loss of chr1, 2, 6, 8, 10, 13, 17, 21; eosinophilic chRCCs were near-diploid; renal oncocytomas showed mostly chr1 loss only.
- **MiTF gene fusions**: known ASPSCR1-[TFE3](../genes/TFE3.md) and PRCC-[TFE3](../genes/TFE3.md) fusions confirmed in tRCCs (validated by FISH). Two tRCCs (14336T, PtS1T) had elevated [TFE3](../genes/TFE3.md) expression *without* TFE3 fusion or amplification; sample 14336T harbored a novel [MIDN](../genes/MIDN.md)-[SBNO2](../genes/SBNO2.md) fusion (chr19p13.3 inversion) placing full-length SBNO2 under the MIDN promoter, with the two highest SBNO2 expression levels seen in these two samples.
- **Novel CLTC-TFEB fusion** identified in an unclassified case (8432T) — encodes an in-frame fusion protein containing the bHLH domain of [TFEB](../genes/TFEB.md); validated by FISH. Together with a PRCC-TFE3 fusion in another unclassified case (20825T1), this led to reclassification of both as tRCC after pathology review.
- **Novel transforming [ACTG1](../genes/ACTG1.md)-[MITF](../genes/MITF.md) fusion** in pRCC sample 159T (with oncocytic/papillary features): replaces the first 118 aa of MITF with the N-terminal 121 aa of ACTG1. The fusion is more stable than wild-type MITF, induces [HIF1A](../genes/HIF1A.md), [MET](../genes/MET.md) and [APEX1](../genes/APEX1.md) target genes (*P* < 0.01) in HEK293T cells, and confers significantly greater anchorage-independent growth in NIH3T3 cells than wild-type MITF.
- **BIRC7 in MiTF-high tumors**: 6/7 samples with MiTF family fusion or amplification overexpressed [BIRC7](../genes/BIRC7.md), an anti-apoptotic MITF target gene — proposed as a diagnostic marker and therapeutic vulnerability for the "MiTF-high" nccRCC subtype.

## Genes & alterations

- **[MET](../genes/MET.md)** — recurrent activating kinase-domain mutations (p.Val1088Ala, p.Ile1095Thr, p.Phe1218Ile) and an extracellular p.Asp153Tyr in 15% of pRCC; chr7 amplification of the MET locus in ~70% of pRCCs; induced as a downstream target of the ACTG1-MITF fusion. Major therapeutic rationale for MET inhibitors in pRCC.
- **[NF2](../genes/NF2.md), [SLC5A3](../genes/SLC5A3.md), [PNKD](../genes/PNKD.md), [CPQ](../genes/CPQ.md), [LRP2](../genes/LRP2.md), [CHD3](../genes/CHD3.md), [NHERF1](../genes/NHERF1.md), [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md), [CRTC1](../genes/CRTC1.md)** — newly nominated significantly mutated genes in pRCC. SLC5A3 (SMIT) shows three frameshift LoF + one predicted-damaging point mutation, suggesting a tumor-suppressor role linked to mitochondrial alterations.
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)** — significantly enriched mutations in the chRCC classic subtype only (*P* = 2.3 × 10⁻⁵).
- **[PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [FAAH2](../genes/FAAH2.md), [PDHB](../genes/PDHB.md), [PDXDC1](../genes/PDXDC1.md), [ZNF765](../genes/ZNF765.md)** — significantly mutated in chRCC.
- **[PRKAG2](../genes/PRKAG2.md)** — p.Ile388Val (pseudosubstrate, CBS2) and p.Arg531Gln (CBS4, AMP-binding) substitutions in chRCC, predicted activating by analogy to glycogen storage cardiomyopathy mutants. Also strongly upregulated in chRCC/oncocytoma vs pRCC (~3.4 log fold; *P* < 5 × 10⁻⁹⁰).
- **[PRKAG1](../genes/PRKAG1.md)** — p.Arg299Gln in an independent chRCC cohort, analogous to PRKAG2 p.Arg531Gln.
- **[TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md), [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md), [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md)** — recurrent mutations in chRCC, suggesting mTORC1 addiction and possible responsiveness to mTORC1 inhibitors.
- **[ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md)** — recurrently mutated in chRCC (not reaching q-score significance).
- **[ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md), [C2CD4C](../genes/C2CD4C.md)** — significantly mutated in renal oncocytoma (low overall mutation rate).
- **[IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md), [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md), [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md)** — among 60 genes with COSMIC hotspot mutations identified as potential drug targets across nccRCC.
- **[ACTG1](../genes/ACTG1.md)-[MITF](../genes/MITF.md) fusion** — novel transforming fusion in pRCC sample 159T; activates HIF1A, MET, APEX1; greater protein stability and transforming activity than wild-type MITF.
- **CLTC-[TFEB](../genes/TFEB.md) fusion** — novel; encodes in-frame fusion preserving the TFEB bHLH domain (sample 8432T, reclassified tRCC).
- **ASPSCR1-[TFE3](../genes/TFE3.md) / PRCC-[TFE3](../genes/TFE3.md) fusions** — recovered as expected in tRCCs.
- **[MIDN](../genes/MIDN.md)-[SBNO2](../genes/SBNO2.md) fusion** — novel chr19p13.3 inversion in a TFE3-fusion-negative tRCC (14336T), driving high SBNO2 expression.
- **[TFEB](../genes/TFEB.md) focal amplification** — 490-kb chr6 amplicon with highest TFEB expression in sample 1216T, expanding the spectrum of TFEB alterations beyond translocation.
- **[BIRC7](../genes/BIRC7.md)** — overexpressed in 6/7 MiTF-fusion/amplification samples; proposed therapeutic target via BIRC7 inhibitors.
- **[VHL](../genes/VHL.md), [FH](../genes/FH.md), [FLCN](../genes/FLCN.md), [SDHB](../genes/SDHB.md), [SDHC](../genes/SDHC.md), [SDHD](../genes/SDHD.md), [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md)** — referenced as known germline kidney-cancer predisposition genes against which nccRCC somatic profiles are contrasted.

## Clinical implications

- **MET-targeted therapy in pRCC**: the authors recommend stratifying pRCC patients by [MET](../genes/MET.md) alterations (mutation, amplification, and/or overexpression) in trials of MET inhibitors, noting prior responsiveness of germline-MET pRCC patients to broad-spectrum TKIs.
- **mTORC1 inhibition in chRCC**: recurrent [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md) / [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) / [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md) mutations in chRCC nominate mTORC1 inhibitors (rapalogs) as a candidate therapy.
- **AMPK / metabolic axis in chRCC**: combined [PRKAG2](../genes/PRKAG2.md) and [PDHB](../genes/PDHB.md) mutations define a chRCC subtype with metabolic deregulation, potentially actionable via AMPK or PDH modulators.
- **Five-gene RNA-seq classifier (ASB1, GLYAT, PDZK1IP1, PLCG2, SDCBP2)**: deployable to distinguish pRCC, chRCC and renal oncocytoma in challenging diagnostic cases (e.g. needle core biopsy where chRCC vs oncocytoma is histologically ambiguous). Requires prospective validation.
- **"MiTF-high" subtype**: tumors with [MITF](../genes/MITF.md), [TFE3](../genes/TFE3.md) or [TFEB](../genes/TFEB.md) fusions, amplifications, or overexpression form a coherent biological group beyond classical tRCC. [BIRC7](../genes/BIRC7.md) expression may serve as a diagnostic biomarker and a therapeutic target via BIRC7-directed apoptosis-sensitizing agents in clinical development.
- **TFEB beyond translocation**: focal TFEB amplification (sample 1216T) is identified as an additional cancer-relevant TFEB lesion to evaluate when classical translocation FISH is negative.

## Limitations & open questions

- Many of the newly nominated significantly mutated genes (e.g., [SLC5A3](../genes/SLC5A3.md), [PNKD](../genes/PNKD.md), [CPQ](../genes/CPQ.md), [FAAH2](../genes/FAAH2.md), [PDHB](../genes/PDHB.md), [PDXDC1](../genes/PDXDC1.md), [ZNF765](../genes/ZNF765.md), [C2CD4C](../genes/C2CD4C.md)) lack functional characterization in cancer — their oncogenic vs passenger status is unestablished.
- Renal oncocytoma had very low mutation rates; the authors note that larger cohorts and functional follow-up are needed to confirm [ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md) and [C2CD4C](../genes/C2CD4C.md) as drivers.
- The five-gene classifier was validated on a 43-sample held-out cohort and an external 27-sample expression dataset, but the authors call for prospective clinical validation before deployment.
- Mechanism of TFE3 upregulation in fusion-negative, amplification-negative tRCCs (e.g. PtS1T) remains unresolved; whether [SBNO2](../genes/SBNO2.md) modulates other MiTF family members is hypothesized but not demonstrated.
- The functional consequence of PDHB p.Arg105Leu — inferred from a homologous germline lactic-acidosis mutation — is not directly validated.
- The cohort is single-institution (UTSW) and excludes patients with HIV/HBV/HCV/TB; generalizability to broader nccRCC populations is untested.
- No drug-response data accompany the genomic findings; therapeutic hypotheses (MET inhibitors, mTORC1 inhibitors, BIRC7 inhibitors) await clinical testing in nccRCC subsets.
