# CRIPAK

## Overview

CRIPAK (Cysteine-Rich Inhibitor of [PAK1](../genes/PAK1.md)) is reported as a recurrently mutated gene in upper tract urothelial carcinoma ([UTUC](../cancer_types/UTUC.md)). However, CRIPAK has since been flagged by the broader research community as a potentially problematic gene model, and results reporting recurrent CRIPAK mutations should be interpreted with caution.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- CRIPAK recurrently mutated in UTUC (P=0.0001) and enriched in muscle-invasive (MI)-UTUC vs TCGA MI-BLCA (P=0.022); paper does not flag the problematic gene model caveat [PMID:33397444](../papers/33397444.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[UTUC](../cancer_types/UTUC.md)**: Reported as a statistically significant recurrently mutated gene (P=0.0001) enriched in MI-UTUC relative to TCGA MI-BLCA (P=0.022) [PMID:33397444](../papers/33397444.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

No co-occurrence or mutual exclusivity data reported for CRIPAK in this corpus.

## Therapeutic relevance

No therapeutic implications reported. Given the uncertainty around the CRIPAK gene model, therapeutic targeting would be premature.

## Open questions

- CRIPAK has been reported as a potentially false-positive gene model in the broader genomics literature; the paper reporting recurrent CRIPAK mutations in UTUC does not address this caveat [PMID:33397444](../papers/33397444.md)
- Independent confirmation of CRIPAK recurrence in UTUC in a separate cohort using updated gene model annotations is needed.
