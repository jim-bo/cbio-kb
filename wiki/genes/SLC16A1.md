# SLC16A1

## Overview

SLC16A1 (also known as MCT1, Monocarboxylate Transporter 1) encodes a proton-linked monocarboxylate transporter that mediates the transport of lactate, pyruvate, and other monocarboxylates across the plasma membrane. In cancer, MCT1 upregulation is associated with the Warburg effect and increased glycolytic flux. It is a rate-limiting transporter for hyperpolarized pyruvate uptake in prostate cancer and has been proposed as both a biomarker for metabolic imaging and a therapeutic target.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Significantly upregulated at the RNA level in [prad_tcga](../datasets/prad_tcga.md) tumors vs. matched normals; IHC-positive in cancer regions across all 12 patients in a hyperpolarized MRI study; identified as the rate-limiting transporter for HP pyruvate uptake and the proximate driver of the lactate signal in vivo in prostate adenocarcinoma [PMID:31564440](../papers/31564440.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md)**: MCT1 (SLC16A1) is significantly upregulated in prostate adenocarcinoma vs. normal tissue; IHC positivity confirmed in cancer regions across all patients studied; HP lactate signal (driven by MCT1) increases with Gleason grade [PMID:31564440](../papers/31564440.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- MCT1 upregulation co-occurs with [LDHA](../genes/LDHA.md) upregulation in prostate adenocarcinoma; both are significantly elevated in prad_tcga tumors vs. normals and together drive the glycolytic phenotype measurable by hyperpolarized MRI [PMID:31564440](../papers/31564440.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- SLC16A1/MCT1 expression is proposed as a companion biomarker for MCT1-targeted therapy; HP-MRI could pre-select patients likely to respond to MCT1 inhibitors and provide an early pharmacodynamic readout [PMID:31564440](../papers/31564440.md).

## Open questions

- Whether MCT1 expression or HP lactate signal can be used to stratify patients for PI3K/AKT/mTOR-targeted regimens without upfront biopsy requires larger prospective validation (current data: n=12 patients) [PMID:31564440](../papers/31564440.md).
- The TMPRSS2-ERG fusion's potential role in modulating lactate export via MCT1 remains an open question based on conflicting findings from lung cancer literature [PMID:31564440](../papers/31564440.md).
