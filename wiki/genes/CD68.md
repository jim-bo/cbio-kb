# CD68

## Overview

CD68 is a transmembrane glycoprotein expressed on macrophages and microglia, widely used as a marker for tumor-associated macrophages (TAMs) in [immunohistochemistry](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md) and multiplex immunofluorescence panels. In glioblastoma, CD68+ macrophage/microglia subpopulations are enriched in PTEN-mutant tumors and associated with an immunosuppressive tumor microenvironment that predicts resistance to anti-PD-1 checkpoint blockade.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- CD68 used as a macrophage/microglia marker in quantitative multiplex immunofluorescence (Opal/Vectra platform, 7-plex: DAPI, CD3, CD8, CD68, HLA-DR, PD-L1, [SOX2](../genes/SOX2.md)) profiling the [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) tumor microenvironment in anti-PD-1-treated patients (n=66); CD68+HLA-DR− subpopulation enriched in PTEN-mutant tumors (p=0.011) [PMID:30742119](../papers/30742119.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md):** CD68+HLA-DR− macrophage/microglia subpopulation significantly enriched in PTEN-mutant recurrent GBM treated with PD-1 inhibitors ([nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md) or [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md)); [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) mutation linked to increased macrophage/microglia and neutrophil infiltration and reduced T-cell infiltration, constituting an immunosuppressive microenvironment [PMID:30742119](../papers/30742119.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- CD68+HLA-DR− enrichment co-occurs with [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) mutation and reduced T-cell (CD3, CD8) infiltration in GBM tumors; the PTEN-loss immunosuppressive phenotype also involves elevated [CD44](../genes/CD44.md)+ tumor-cell immunosuppressive signature and reduced [CD274](../genes/CD274.md) (PD-L1) differential expression [PMID:30742119](../papers/30742119.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- CD68 itself is not a therapeutic target in this context but serves as a biomarker of the immunosuppressive microenvironment driven by PTEN loss; elevated CD68+HLA-DR− infiltration identifies GBM patients less likely to respond to anti-PD-1 monotherapy and potentially candidates for combination immunotherapy approaches targeting the myeloid compartment [PMID:30742119](../papers/30742119.md)

## Open questions

- Whether the CD68+HLA-DR− macrophage phenotype in PTEN-mutant GBM is a direct consequence of PTEN-loss signaling (as described in melanoma) or an indirect stromal effect specific to GBM is unresolved; mechanistic dissection and prospective validation in GBM remain open [PMID:30742119](../papers/30742119.md)
