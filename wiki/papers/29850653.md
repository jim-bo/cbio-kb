**PMID:** 29850653 · **DOI:** [10.1200/PO.17.00073](https://doi.org/10.1200/PO.17.00073) · **Journal:** JCO Precision Oncology (2017)

## TL;DR

Bonneville et al. applied the MANTIS microsatellite-instability (MSI) caller to 11,139 tumor-normal whole-exome pairs spanning 39 cancer types from TCGA, TARGET, and several external cohorts — to their knowledge the largest pan-cancer MSI analysis to date at the time. MSI was detected in 3.8% of all tumors and was present in 27 of the 39 cancer types, with previously underappreciated MSI in adrenocortical carcinoma ([ACC](../cancer_types/ACC.md), 4.3%), cervical cancer ([CESC](../cancer_types/CESC.md), 2.6%), and mesothelioma ([MESO](../cancer_types/MESO.md), 2.4%). MSI-H ACC and CESC tumors carried significantly higher mutational burden than MSS counterparts, supporting an expanded role for clinical MSI testing beyond colorectal/endometrial/gastric cancers as a biomarker for immune-checkpoint therapy.

## Cohort & data

- 11,139 paired tumor-normal whole-exome sequencing samples from 11,080 patients across 39 distinct cancer types.
- 10,415 cases from [The Cancer Genome Atlas (TCGA)](../datasets/gbm_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md) (referenced via the pan-can-atlas-2018 freeze for [ACC](../datasets/acc_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [CESC](../datasets/cesc_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [MESO](../datasets/meso_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), and [GBM](../datasets/gbm_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md) among others).
- 280 cases from the TARGET (Therapeutically Applicable Research to Generate Effective Treatments) pediatric initiative.
- 444 cases from external sources (Landau et al. 2015 CLL, n=338; Choi et al. 2015 CTCL, n=32; Zheng et al. 2016 [NPC](../cancer_types/NPC.md), n=51; Ong et al. 2012 [CHOL](../cancer_types/CHOL.md), n=8; Chan-On et al. 2013 CHOL, n=15).
- Assay: [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md). Reads aligned to hg38 with [BWA-MEM](../methods/bwa.md) (except DLBCL, which used hg19); duplicates removed with SAMtools/Picard; in-house L-MAP pipeline orchestrated downloads and processing on the Ohio Supercomputer Center.
- Assessed cancer types include [ACC](../cancer_types/ACC.md), [CESC](../cancer_types/CESC.md), [MESO](../cancer_types/MESO.md), [UCEC](../cancer_types/UCEC.md), [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md), [READ](../cancer_types/READ.md), [STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md), [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md), [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md), [OV](../cancer_types/OV.md), [BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md), [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md), [LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md), [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md), [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md), [KIRC](../cancer_types/KIRC.md), [KIRP](../cancer_types/KIRP.md), [KICH](../cancer_types/KICH.md), [LIHC](../cancer_types/LIHC.md), [LGG](../cancer_types/LGG.md), [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md), [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md), [THCA](../cancer_types/THCA.md), [THYM](../cancer_types/THYM.md), [UCS](../cancer_types/UCS.md), [UVM](../cancer_types/UVM.md), [ESCA](../cancer_types/ESCA.md), CHOL, [PCPG](../cancer_types/PCPG.md), [SARC](../cancer_types/SARC.md), [TGCT](../cancer_types/TGCT.md), DLBC, [LAML](../cancer_types/LAML.md), [NBL](../cancer_types/NBL.md), NPC, CTCL, CLL, [WT](../cancer_types/WT.md), and pediatric [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md).

## Key findings

- MSI was detected in **3.8% of all tumors assessed** (overall pan-cancer rate) and was present in **27 of 39 cancer types**; no MSI was detected in the remaining 12.
- Disease-specific MSI-H prevalence ranged from **31.4% in endometrial carcinoma ([UCEC](../cancer_types/UCEC.md))** at the high end to **0.25% in glioblastoma multiforme (GBM)** among the cancer types where MSI was detected at all.
- The four highest-MSI cancer types were the classic Lynch syndrome–associated tumors: UCEC, [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md), [STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md), and [READ](../cancer_types/READ.md). Consistent with prior work, colon adenocarcinoma had higher MSI prevalence (**19.7%**) than rectal adenocarcinoma (**5.7%**).
- Previously underappreciated MSI signal was identified in three cancer types: **adrenocortical carcinoma (ACC, 4.3%; 4/92 cases)**, **cervical squamous cell carcinoma and endocervical adenocarcinoma (CESC, 2.6%)**, and **mesothelioma (MESO, 2.4%)**.
- Across ACC, CESC, and MESO (n=480 combined), kernel-density estimation on MANTIS scores produced clearly separable MSI-H vs MSS distributions, supporting the classifications.
- **Mutational burden was significantly elevated in MSI-H vs MSS tumors** within these cancer types: ACC averaged **1,157 mutations (MSI-H) vs 216 (MSS), P = .01**; CESC averaged **5,675 vs 639, P = .003**; MESO averaged **982 vs 142, P = .10** (~7-fold but not statistically significant). P values calculated by Welch two-sample t-test on log-normalized counts.
- Of 9,569 tumors **outside** colorectal, endometrial, or gastric cancer, **77 (0.8%) were MSI-H**, of which only 14 fell within ACC, CESC, or MESO — limiting statistical power for downstream mutational-signature analysis.
- Pooled and per-case mutational-signature analyses with [deconstructSigs](../methods/mutational-signatures.md) using the Alexandrov 2013 signature set did not reveal statistically significant differences (after Benjamini-Hochberg correction) between MSI-H and MSS tumors within ACC, CESC, or MESO.
- Across the 2,530 microsatellite loci tested, the authors identified **22 loci** that — within at least five cohorts — showed an MSI-H vs MSS difference score > 0.75 and ≥50% sample coverage. Only two of the standard Bethesda/Promega MSI-PCR-panel loci were assessed by this study's locus set, and neither fell within the top-22 list, indicating substantial heterogeneity of informative loci across cancer types.
- Among MSI-H cases in ACC/CESC/MESO, **64% carried at least one predicted-deleterious somatic mutation in an MMR or proofreading gene** ([MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md), [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md), [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md), [EXO1](../genes/EXO1.md), [POLD1](../genes/POLD1.md), [POLE](../genes/POLE.md)) vs **7% of MSS cases**; statistical significance could not be formally established due to differing exome captures plus the higher background mutation rate in MSI-H samples.

## Genes & alterations

- [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md), [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md), [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md) — core MMR proteins; somatic deleterious mutations enriched in MSI-H tumors of ACC, CESC, and MESO. The authors explicitly screened these genes (along with [EXO1](../genes/EXO1.md)) for DANN-pathogenic somatic variants. Loss of these proteins (or germline Lynch alterations) is the canonical cause of MSI.
- [EXO1](../genes/EXO1.md) — exonuclease component of the MMR pathway; included in the same MMR-gene somatic-mutation screen.
- [POLD1](../genes/POLD1.md), [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) — DNA proofreading polymerases. Not MMR proteins per se, but somatic mutations in these genes can drive hypermutation and were tallied alongside the MMR genes in MSI-H tumors.

## Clinical implications

- The authors explicitly argue these findings support **expanded clinical MSI testing beyond colorectal and endometrial cancers**, since MSI-H tumors are predicted to benefit from PD-1 blockade and [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md) had recently received an FDA pan-tumor approval (May 2017) for any MSI-H or MMR-deficient unresectable/metastatic solid tumor.
- ACC, CESC, and MESO each had ongoing immune-checkpoint clinical trials at the time of writing (ACC: NCT02673333; CESC: NCT02635360; MESO: NCT02784171, NCT02991482, NCT02707666, NCT02399371); the authors suggest these studies could benefit from retrospective MSI-H biomarker analysis.
- Identification of MSI in ACC reinforces prior anecdotal reports of ACC as a possible extracolonic Lynch syndrome manifestation, which has implications for germline testing referral.
- The 22 cancer-type-spanning informative loci identified could form the basis of a **targeted NGS panel for pan-cancer MSI detection**, with the caveat that standard Bethesda/Promega PCR-panel loci performed poorly outside colorectal/endometrial contexts.

## Limitations & open questions

- Statistical power for mutational-signature differences in ACC/CESC/MESO was limited — only 14 MSI-H tumors across the three cohorts combined.
- Samples were sequenced with potentially different exome capture kits, confounding the somatic-mutation-burden comparison of MMR/proofreading genes between MSI-H and MSS cases.
- The study cannot distinguish whether MSI in ACC, CESC, and MESO is driven by classical MMR deficiency (e.g., [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md) promoter methylation, which the authors did not measure) versus other mechanisms.
- No clinical-outcome correlation was performed; whether MSI-H status is prognostic outside colorectal cancer remains an open question that requires larger MSI-H cohorts with linked clinical data.
- The microsatellite locus set (2,530 loci) was originally designed from exome-flanking regions and is not directly comparable to the Bethesda/Promega PCR-panel loci — a methodological gap for clinical adoption.
- The detection threshold (MANTIS average distance ≥ 0.4) was held constant across all cancer types; cancer-type-specific calibration was not explored.
