# HSPH1

## Overview

HSPH1 (also known as HSP110) encodes a large heat-shock protein in the HSP110/HSP105 family with established roles in protein disaggregation and anti-apoptotic signaling. HSPH1 has a recognized disease role in microsatellite-instable (MSI) cancers, where it is downregulated at the protein level in MSI cell lines.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- HSPH1 (HSP110) is a heat-shock protein downregulated in MSI cancer cell lines (CCLE pan-cancer proteomics, n=375 lines); noted to have an established disease role in MSI cancers [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- MSI-associated cancers: downregulation observed across solid-organ MSI cell lines in the CCLE; established prior disease association in MSI tumors [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-downregulated with [HSPA4L](../genes/HSPA4L.md) and [DNAJB14](../genes/DNAJB14.md) in MSI cell lines [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- HSPH1 downregulation in MSI may contribute to proteostasis vulnerabilities; its established role in MSI disease biology makes it a candidate biomarker for MSI-stratified therapeutic strategies.

## Open questions

- Mechanistic basis for HSPH1 downregulation in MSI — whether it is driven by mismatch-repair loss itself or by associated chromatin/transcriptional changes — is not resolved in the CCLE proteomics data [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md).
