# LMNA

## Overview

LMNA encodes Lamin A/C, a structural component of the nuclear lamina involved in nuclear integrity, chromatin organization, and gene regulation. LMNA has been identified as an upstream fusion partner for [NTRK1](../genes/NTRK1.md) in colorectal cancer, creating an oncogenic TRK fusion protein.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- LMNA-NTRK1 fusion identified in a colorectal cancer cell line used as a functional control for TRK fusion studies; represents a known NTRK1 upstream partner in CRC [PMID:31871300](../papers/31871300.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Colorectal cancer: LMNA-NTRK1 fusion reported as a functional TRK rearrangement; used as a positive control for pan-TRK IHC and TRK inhibitor sensitivity assays [PMID:31871300](../papers/31871300.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- LMNA-NTRK1 fusions in colorectal cancer may co-occur with MSI-H status, consistent with the overall TRK fusion enrichment in mismatch-repair-deficient CRC [PMID:31871300](../papers/31871300.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- LMNA-NTRK1 fusion-positive tumors are predicted to be sensitive to TRK inhibitors ([larotrectinib](../drugs/larotrectinib.md), [entrectinib](../drugs/entrectinib.md)) based on the canonical kinase-domain-preserving structure of the rearrangement [PMID:31871300](../papers/31871300.md)

## Open questions

- Clinical outcomes data for LMNA-NTRK1-positive CRC patients treated with TRK inhibitors are not reported in this corpus.
