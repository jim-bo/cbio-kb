# C2CD4C

## Overview

C2CD4C (C2 calcium-dependent domain containing 4C) is a poorly characterized gene. In non-clear-cell renal cell carcinoma genomics, it was identified as significantly mutated in renal oncocytoma, a subtype with very low overall mutation rates.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Significantly mutated in renal oncocytoma (low overall mutation rate cohort); oncogenic vs passenger status unestablished; requires larger cohorts and functional follow-up [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **ROCY**: C2CD4C is one of two significantly mutated genes (alongside ERCC2) in renal oncocytoma identified by multi-method driver analysis [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with ERCC2 mutations as the predominant significantly mutated genes in renal oncocytoma [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No therapeutic relevance established; functional role in renal oncocytoma tumorigenesis is unknown [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)

## Open questions

- Whether C2CD4C mutations are drivers or passengers in renal oncocytoma is unestablished; authors call for larger cohorts and functional characterization [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)
