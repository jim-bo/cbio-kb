# GAB1

## Overview

GAB1 (GRB2-associated binding protein 1) is a scaffolding/docking protein that recruits signaling complexes downstream of receptor tyrosine kinases (RTKs). GAB1 forms a complex with [GRB2](../genes/GRB2.md) and [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) (SHP2) to activate the RAS pathway. In glioblastoma, GAB1 phosphorylation is a key transducer of [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) signaling.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- GAB1 Y657/Y689 phosphorylation tracks EGFR activation in [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md); GAB1 protein upregulated in EGFR-altered tumors (IHC-validated) in a CPTAC proteogenomic cohort (n=99 GBM tumors) [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).
- Identified as a trans-phosphorylation target of EGFR: EGFR trans-effects on GAB1 Y657/Y689 confirmed; GAB1 functions in the PTPN11-GAB1-GRB2 complex that activates the RAS pathway downstream of EGFR [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **Glioblastoma (GBM):** GAB1 protein upregulated and Y657/Y689 phosphorylated in EGFR-altered GBM tumors; IHC-validated in this cohort [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- GAB1 forms a complex with [GRB2](../genes/GRB2.md) and [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) as a convergent RTK signaling hub downstream of [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) and [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md) in GBM [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- As a central node in the EGFR-GAB1-GRB2-PTPN11-RAS signaling axis, GAB1 may be relevant to the sensitivity of EGFR-altered GBM to MAPK pathway inhibitors; drug-connectivity analysis in this cohort nominates MAPK inhibitors for NF1/RTK-altered tumors [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Open questions

- The functional consequence of GAB1 protein upregulation (as distinct from phosphorylation) in EGFR-altered GBM is not fully characterized; whether GAB1 protein level is transcriptionally or post-transcriptionally regulated in this context requires further study [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).
