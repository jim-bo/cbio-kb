# MUC2

## Overview

MUC2 is a gel-forming secretory mucin that is the predominant mucin of the intestinal goblet cell. It is a canonical marker of intestinal-type goblet cell differentiation in the colon. In colorectal precancer, MUC2 expression is retained in sessile serrated lesion cells (SSCs) despite loss of the secretory-lineage transcription factor ATOH1, indicating partial maintenance of goblet-cell identity alongside acquisition of gastric metaplastic features.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- MUC2 (goblet-cell gene) retained in SSCs despite loss of ATOH1 in the COLON MAP/HTAN multi-omic colorectal polyp atlas, co-occurring with [TFF3](../genes/TFF3.md) retention; this partial goblet-cell program co-exists with acquisition of [MUC5AC](../genes/MUC5AC.md)+ gastric-type metaplasia in the SSC state [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Colorectal cancer ([COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md)): MUC2 expression pattern in SSCs (retained despite ATOH1 loss) distinguishes the sessile serrated pathway from conventional adenoma biology [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- MUC2 and TFF3 retention co-occurs with MUC5AC+ gastric metaplasia and CDX2-low state in SSCs [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting reported in the corpus.

## Open questions

- The mechanism by which goblet-cell genes MUC2 and TFF3 are maintained despite ATOH1 loss in SSCs is unresolved.
