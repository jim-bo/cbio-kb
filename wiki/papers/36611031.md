## TL;DR

Chatila et al. assembled the largest annotated clinical and genomic series of colitis-associated cancers (CAC) to date — 174 patients with inflammatory bowel disease (IBD)–associated bowel cancers — and sequenced 29 synchronous or isolated dysplasia samples. They show that CAC is characterized by clonal [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) alterations (90% of CAC, ~50% of dysplasia, often arising via convergent evolution of independent events), low Wnt pathway activation (in striking contrast to sporadic CRC), and that multiple primary or dysplastic lesions in the same patient generally arise as genetically independent events with no shared "field" of mutations. Functional work in patient-derived organoids and xenografts demonstrates that CAC, including APC-mutant tumors, grows independently of exogenous Wnt supplementation, suggesting transcriptional rewiring away from Wnt early in CAC tumorigenesis.

## Cohort & data

- 174 patients with colitis-associated cancers ([COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md)); 144 in the clinical cohort, 161 in the genomic cohort, 166 CAC tumors sequenced.
- 56% ulcerative colitis (n=93), 44% Crohn's disease (n=73). 84.9% had ≥10 years of IBD at CAC diagnosis.
- Primary tumor sites: small intestine 10.3%, right colon 27.1%, left colon 29.5%, rectum 31.3%, fistula tract 1.8%.
- Dataset: [bowel_colitis_msk_2022](../datasets/bowel_colitis_msk_2022.md).
- Sequencing: targeted exon sequencing on 166 CAC + 9 dysplasia (MSK-IMPACT, [IMPACT468](../methods/IMPACT468.md), with FoundationOne in some cases); whole-exome sequencing (150× depth) on 13 CAC + 20 dysplasia paired with adjacent normal mucosa.
- Germline panel data available for 73/161 patients via MSK-IMPACT germline pipeline.
- Functional models: AOM/DSS mouse model of inflammatory CRC; patient-derived organoids (PDOs) and patient-derived xenografts (PDXs) from CAC samples.

## Key findings

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) is altered in 90% of CAC and ~50% of dysplasia samples, making it the earliest and most recurrent driver. Alterations are predominantly clonal (majority of CAC); 14% subclonal and 12% with no detectable TP53 alteration.
- Other recurrent drivers in CAC: [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) 31%, [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) 20% (focal amplifications more common than in sporadic CRC), [APC](../genes/APC.md) 20%, [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) 13%.
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) and [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) alterations are significantly enriched in patients with Crohn's disease versus ulcerative colitis. [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) mutations are enriched in more differentiated CAC.
- Wnt pathway alterations are infrequent. [APC](../genes/APC.md) is altered in only 20% of CAC (vs. dominant frequency in sporadic CRC). RNA-seq shows lower Wnt ssGSEA score in CAC vs. CRC (p=0.13). Beta-catenin IHC in 14 CAC showed membranous staining in all Wnt-pathway wild-type CAC.
- Compared to sporadic metastatic CRC: [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) and [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) amplifications and [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) mutations occur more frequently in CAC; [APC](../genes/APC.md), KRAS, and [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E mutations occur significantly less frequently. Broad copy-number changes are significantly more common in sporadic CRC (P<0.001); focal CNAs similar.
- Oncogenic alterations in CAC are 25.0% copy-number alterations vs. 16.6% in sporadic CRC.
- Subclonal TP53 group is significantly enriched for Wnt pathway alterations versus the clonal TP53 group (P=0.017, two-sided Fisher's exact).
- Germline pathogenic alterations identified in 10/73 patients (14%): APC I1307K (3 cases, Ashkenazi Jewish low-penetrance variant), [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md), [DICER1](../genes/DICER1.md), [FANCA](../genes/FANCA.md), [FANCC](../genes/FANCC.md). Both PMS2 carriers had microsatellite-stable CAC.
- Multiple primary or synchronous lesions: 32% of 110 colectomy patients had synchronous adenoma (n=7), dysplasia (n=20), or cancer (n=8). In six patients with multifocal tumors and a patient with six synchronous lesions, no shared genetic alterations were detected — lesions developed independently at the genetic level. Convergent TP53 evolution was observed across independent lesions.
- WES of 13 paired dysplasia/CAC: no TP53 alterations in adjacent normal mucosa. Fraction genome altered and WGD significantly increased in CAC vs. dysplasia. Most genomic alterations were private to either the precancerous lesion or the cancer (P=0.00022 for shared vs. private mutations across all mutations; P=0.17 for driver mutations).
- AOM/DSS mouse model: two close-proximity neoplastic lesions per mouse showed few shared events (one mouse shared a [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) mutation; the other had no shared alterations).
- Functional Wnt independence: Wnt wild-type and APC-mutant CAC organoids grew without exogenous Wnt supplementation, in contrast to normal colonic organoids. Pharmacologic inhibition of Wnt ligand signaling (porcupine inhibitor LGK-974) and tankyrase inhibition (G007-LK) in three CAC PDXs did not suppress tumor growth.
- IDH1 R132 mutant CAC exhibited high global methylation and confirmed 2-HG production in a PDX from an ileal CAC; selective IDH1 and FGFR inhibitors suppressed growth in vivo, identifying potentially targetable lesions in CAC.

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — altered in 90% of CAC, ~50% of dysplasia; predominantly clonal; convergent evolution across independent multifocal lesions.
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — 31% of CAC; enriched in more differentiated tumors; KRAS amplifications more frequent than in sporadic CRC; BRAF V600E and KRAS hot-spot mutations less frequent than sporadic CRC.
- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) — 20% of CAC; focal amplifications enriched relative to sporadic CRC.
- [APC](../genes/APC.md) — 20% of CAC (vs. dominant in sporadic CRC); germline I1307K seen in 3/73 sequenced patients (Ashkenazi Jewish low-penetrance variant).
- [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) — 13% of CAC.
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) — enriched in Crohn's disease vs. ulcerative colitis cohort; IDH1 R132 mutants drive high global methylation and 2-HG production; targetable in PDX.
- [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) — observed as a shared alteration between two close lesions in one AOM/DSS mouse.
- [RNF43](../genes/RNF43.md) — Wnt-pathway gene; not selectively enriched in APC wild-type CAC.
- [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md), [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md), [ERBB3](../genes/ERBB3.md), [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [TERT](../genes/TERT.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md), [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md), [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md) — recurrent in oncoprint at lower frequencies.
- Germline DNA-repair genes: [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md), [DICER1](../genes/DICER1.md), [FANCA](../genes/FANCA.md), FANCC.

## Clinical implications

- CAC genomic landscape is distinct from sporadic CRC: lower Wnt pathway dependence and higher rate of MYC/KRAS amplifications and IDH1 mutations argue that "treat the same as sporadic CRC" may miss therapeutic opportunities.
- Functional confirmation of IDH1 and FGFR inhibitor sensitivity in PDX models highlights potentially actionable, CAC-enriched alterations.
- Routine surveillance and clinical IBD quiescence do not eliminate CAC risk: 17% of patients had a documented colonoscopy within one year of CAC diagnosis; 15% had quiescent IBD off-treatment.
- Germline testing is informative — 14% of consented patients had pathogenic germline alterations, comparable to sporadic CRC rates.
- Bone and pelvic metastases are significantly enriched in CAC vs. sporadic CRC.

## Limitations & open questions

- The lack of TP53 mutations in adjacent normal mucosa and the largely independent genetic histories of multifocal lesions argue against a genomic field defect — but suggest non-genomic (epigenetic, transcriptional, microenvironmental) mechanisms underlie the IBD-associated cancer susceptibility, which this study cannot directly test.
- RNA-seq cohort for Wnt analysis is small (5 CAC vs. 6 CRC patients; nine PDXs); the Wnt score difference is suggestive (p=0.13) but not formally significant.
- WES on dysplasia–CAC pairs was limited to 13 patients; broader validation needed.
- Cancer susceptibility from germline ATM, RAD51B, DICER1 alterations remains uncertain.
- Mechanism of transcriptional rewiring away from Wnt — including any role of an Lgr5-low / Wnt-low / L1CAM-high regenerative population — is hypothesized but not directly demonstrated here.
