# PRDM5

## Overview

PRDM5 (PR/SET Domain 5) encodes a transcriptional repressor in the PRDM family of zinc-finger proteins. It is implicated in biliary differentiation and is elevated in biliary-like tumor subtypes. In the context of combined hepatocellular-cholangiocarcinoma (cHCC-ICC), PRDM5 is a biliary marker enriched in the combined-type (ICC-like) subtype.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- PRDM5 is a biliary differentiation marker elevated in the P1 cluster (combined-type cHCC-ICC), alongside [EPCAM](../genes/EPCAM.md) and [KRT19](../genes/KRT19.md); expression distinguishes combined-type (ICC-like) from mixed-type (HCC-like) cHCC-ICC [PMID:31130341](../papers/31130341.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- cHCC-ICC (combined hepatocellular-cholangiocarcinoma): PRDM5 biliary marker elevated in combined-type P1 cluster [PMID:31130341](../papers/31130341.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Elevated with EPCAM and KRT19 (biliary markers) in combined-type cHCC-ICC; contrasts with hepatocyte markers ([GPC3](../genes/GPC3.md), [APOE](../genes/APOE.md), [ALB](../genes/ALB.md), [TBX3](../genes/TBX3.md), [SALL4](../genes/SALL4.md)) elevated in HCC-like clusters P2/P4 [PMID:31130341](../papers/31130341.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Combined-type cHCC-ICC enriched for biliary markers (including PRDM5) is ICC-like and may benefit from ICC-directed therapies [PMID:31130341](../papers/31130341.md)

## Open questions

- Whether PRDM5 expression is driven by epigenetic or mutational mechanisms in cHCC-ICC has not been established.
