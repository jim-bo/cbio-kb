# NOTCH3

## Overview

NOTCH3 encodes a member of the Notch receptor family involved in cell fate determination and differentiation signaling. In cancer genomics, NOTCH3 alterations have been reported in sarcomas, where missense mutations may contribute to oncogenic signaling.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Missense mutations in 14% of RT-UPS vs 4% of sporadic UPS, indicating enrichment in radiation-associated undifferentiated pleomorphic sarcoma [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [MFH](../cancer_types/MFH.md) (UPS) -- NOTCH3 missense mutations enriched in RT-UPS (14%) compared to sporadic UPS (4%) [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Not specifically reported in the corpus for NOTCH3 partners [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No NOTCH3-directed therapy is reported in this corpus [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).

## Open questions

- The functional significance of NOTCH3 missense mutations in RT-UPS is not established [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).
