**PMID:** 35670774 · **DOI:** 10.1158/1078-0432.CCR-21-4272 · **Journal:** Clinical Cancer Research (2022)

## TL;DR

Chakraborty et al. analyze 1,417 prostate cancers profiled with [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) (study [prad_pik3r1_msk_2021](../datasets/prad_pik3r1_msk_2021.md)) and find that [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md) — encoding the PI3K regulatory subunit p85α — is the second most frequently altered PI3K-pathway gene after [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) in metastatic [prostate cancer](../cancer_types/PRAD.md). [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md) driver alterations are enriched in metastatic vs primary disease (5.0% vs 2.7%, p=0.030) and copy-number losses even more so (36% vs 24%, p<0.001). In cell lines and patient-derived organoids, [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md) knockdown or mutation activates AKT, increases proliferation, and confers heightened sensitivity to the AKT inhibitors [MK-2206](../drugs/mk-2206.md) and [ipatasertib](../drugs/ipatasertib.md). Critically, PIK3R1-altered tumors show significantly higher 18F-FDG uptake on [FDG-PET/CT](../methods/fdg-pet-ct.md) (p<0.001), the only driver-pathway alteration with this association — providing a noninvasive imaging biomarker for glycolytic reprogramming and possible AKT-inhibitor eligibility.

## Cohort & data

- **MSK-IMPACT prostate cancer cohort** (study [prad_pik3r1_msk_2021](../datasets/prad_pik3r1_msk_2021.md)): 1,417 patients after FACETS QC (from 2,735 sequenced); 825 (58%) primary, 592 (42%) metastatic; paired tumor–normal capture sequencing with [IMPACT341](../methods/IMPACT341.md) / [IMPACT410](../methods/IMPACT410.md) / [IMPACT468](../methods/IMPACT468.md) panels (340/410/468 genes; all contained PIK3R1). Samples with MSI-sensor >10 or TMB >20 mut/Mb were excluded to avoid passenger PIK3R1 events.
- **TCGA primary prostate cancer** ([prad_tcga](../datasets/prad_tcga.md)) used to bridge DNA copy number to mRNA expression and to test progression-free survival.
- **Taylor et al. primary prostate cancer cohort** used as an independent validation for copy-number→mRNA→outcome associations.
- **MSK-IMPACT breast cancer cohort** ([brca_mskcc_2019](../datasets/brca_mskcc_2019.md)): 1,756 tumors used for PIK3R1 domain-clustering comparison; 1,261 metastatic breast tumors analyzed for survival.
- **METABRIC** (2,509 primary breast tumors) used to assess PIK3R1 mRNA across breast cancer subtypes.
- **MSK-IMPACT FDG-PET sub-cohort**: 313 prostate cancer patients (71 primary, 242 metastases) with hybrid FDG-PET/CT.
- **Functional models**: LAPC4, 22RV1, LNCaP, DU145, PC3, VCaP, E006AA-T, PC3M, MDA-MB-231 cell lines; MSK-PCa1–7 patient-derived [organoid](../methods/organoid-drug-screening.md) lines from mCRPC, including the PIK3R1-mutant MSK-PCa3 (Asn564Asp, likely pathogenic).
- **Genomic methods**: [FACETS](../methods/facets.md) for allelic copy number and clonality, [GISTIC](../methods/gistic.md) for recurrent CNAs, [OncoKB](../methods/oncokb-annotation.md) v2.8 for oncogenicity (driver = predicted oncogenic), Cox proportional hazards and Kaplan–Meier for outcomes.

## Key findings

- **PIK3R1 driver alterations were enriched in metastatic vs primary prostate cancer**: 5.0% vs 2.7%, p=0.030 (Fig. 2D); a total 51/1,417 patients (3.6%) harbored mutations or fusions ([PMID:35670774](../papers/35670774.md)).
- **PIK3R1 copy-number loss was enriched in metastases**: 36% vs 24%, p<0.001 (Fig. 2B). GISTIC confirmed focal 5q13.1/PIK3R1 loss (q<0.001) in metastatic samples; ~31% of losses were focal (<25% of 5q arm).
- **Truncating PIK3R1 mutations in prostate cancer clustered in the c-SH2 domain** (11/19 events, p<0.001) — distinct from the n-SH2/i-SH2 hotspots seen in 1,769 MSS [endometrial cancers](../cancer_types/UCEC.md) (16/120, p=0.22), suggesting tumor-specific functional consequences.
- **PIK3R1 was second only to [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) among PI3K-pathway alterations in mCRPC**; alterations in other pathway members ([PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md), mTOR, etc.) were <2% and not enriched in metastasis. [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) was altered in 4.6% of mets vs 3.0% of primaries (p=0.152, not significant).
- **Clonality**: 21 of 44 PIK3R1 mutations had clonality calls; PIK3R1 events were more often subclonal (~62%) than [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) or PIK3CA events.
- **Co-occurrence**: PIK3R1 alterations co-occurred significantly with [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) alterations; trends (not significant) with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [TMPRSS2](../genes/TMPRSS2.md), and [APC](../genes/APC.md).
- **DNA-mRNA bridge (TCGA, [prad_tcga](../datasets/prad_tcga.md))**: ~20% of TCGA primary prostate tumors had PIK3R1 copy-number loss, significantly associated with reduced mRNA (p=7.43×10⁻¹⁰).
- **Outcomes (primary disease)**: PIK3R1 loss was associated with shorter disease/progression-free survival in TCGA (HR 2.04, 95% CI 1.32–3.15, p=0.001) and the Taylor cohort (HR 4.13, 95% CI 2.01–8.50, p<0.001). Low PIK3R1 mRNA was also associated with recurrence after adjustment for Gleason grade (TCGA HR 1.83, p=0.048).
- **Outcomes (metastasis-free survival in MSK-IMPACT primaries, n=264)**: inconclusive due to event sparsity (driver HR 1.40 [0.18–10.46], p=0.74; loss HR 1.53 [0.69–3.38], p=0.28).
- **Outcomes (mCSPC and mCRPC)**: results inconclusive for [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) in metastatic disease — driver alteration HR 1.67 (0.62–4.42, p=0.30) in mCSPC and HR 0.77 (0.38–1.41, p=0.36) in mCRPC.
- **mRNA expression**: PIK3R1 was uniquely strongly attenuated in metastatic prostate cancer (Grasso dataset: 9-fold decrease, p=2×10⁻¹¹); the only class 1 PI3K subunit (catalytic or regulatory) with such loss.
- **Functional consequences of PIK3R1 knockdown** (LAPC4, 22RV1): increased AKT S473 phosphorylation, including insulin-stimulated AKT activation; reduced PTEN protein; reduced [AR](../genes/AR.md) target gene expression (PSA, PLZF, [TMPRSS2](../genes/TMPRSS2.md), NKX3.1) without affecting [AR](../genes/AR.md) mRNA; increased 2D proliferation and 3D prostatosphere formation; relative resistance to [enzalutamide](../drugs/enzalutamide.md) compared to scrambled controls.
- **AKT-inhibitor sensitivity**: PIK3R1-knockdown LAPC4 and 22RV1 cells were more sensitive to [MK-2206](../drugs/mk-2206.md) (active at 100 nM) than controls. The PIK3R1-mutant organoid MSK-PCa3 (Asn564Asp) showed strong growth reduction with MK-2206 and [ipatasertib](../drugs/ipatasertib.md) at 10 nM, whereas PIK3R1–wild-type MSK-PCa1 (also PTEN-deleted) was only modestly affected. MDA-MB-231 (TNBC) cells with PIK3R1 knockdown also showed enhanced sensitivity to both AKT inhibitors.
- **FDG-PET imaging (n=313 MSK-IMPACT prostate patients)**: among all major oncogenic signaling pathways and recurrent driver mutations in mCRPC, **only PIK3R1 driver alterations were significantly associated with elevated FDG uptake** (n=10, p=0.002, FDR=0.039). Across primary+mCSPC+mCRPC samples, PIK3R1-altered tumors had significantly higher SUVpeak (p<0.001). Notably, PTEN loss was *not* associated with FDG uptake.
- **Pan-cancer (MSK-IMPACT, 15 solid tumors)**: PIK3R1 driver alterations most frequent in endometrial cancer (~25%). Low PIK3R1 mRNA was associated with worse [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) in gastric, liver, and non-small cell lung cancer (KM-plotter datasets).
- **Breast cancer**: 29/1,261 (2.3%) metastatic breast tumors carried PIK3R1 alterations; PIK3R1 and PIK3CA mutations were mutually exclusive (p<0.001). PIK3R1-altered metastatic breast cancer had shorter OS than PIK3CA-altered (HR 2.82, 95% CI 1.56–5.12, p<0.001), attenuated but still trending after adjustment (HR 1.84, p=0.05). In HR+/HER2- metastatic breast cancer, PIK3R1 alteration vs PIK3CA-altered: HR 5.65 (1.66–19.16, p=0.005). PIK3R1 alteration was enriched in TNBC (16/176, 9.1%), and PIK3R1 mRNA was attenuated in basal/TNBC vs other subtypes in METABRIC.

## Genes & alterations

- **[PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md)** — focus of the paper. Driver alterations (somatic mutations and fusions) in 3.6% of 1,417 prostate cancers; copy-number losses in 36% of metastases vs 24% of primaries. Truncating mutations cluster in the c-SH2 domain in prostate cancer (distinct from n-SH2/i-SH2 hotspots in endometrial cancer). Loss/knockdown activates AKT, increases proliferation, drives glycolysis (FDG-PET avidity), and sensitizes cells/organoids to AKT inhibitors.
- **[PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md)** — most common PI3K-pathway alteration in metastatic prostate cancer; significantly enriched in mets vs primary (p<0.001). Co-occurs with PIK3R1 loss. Despite frequent loss, PTEN status was *not* associated with FDG uptake. PIK3R1 knockdown also reduced PTEN protein, consistent with the known PTEN-stabilizing function of p85α.
- **[PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)** — altered in 4.6% of metastatic vs 3.0% of primary prostate cancer (p=0.152, not significantly enriched in mets). Mutually exclusive with PIK3R1 alterations in breast cancer (p<0.001).
- **[AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md)** — referenced as a known AKT-inhibitor target; [ipatasertib](../drugs/ipatasertib.md) monotherapy showed activity in AKT1-mutant PDXs.
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)**, **[TMPRSS2](../genes/TMPRSS2.md)**, **[APC](../genes/APC.md)** — trend toward co-occurrence with PIK3R1 alterations (not statistically significant).
- **[BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md)** and **[RB1](../genes/RB1.md)** — both located on 13q, which shows higher deletion frequency in prostate cancer metastases.
- **[AR](../genes/AR.md)** — PIK3R1 knockdown reduced AR target gene expression (PSA, PLZF, [TMPRSS2](../genes/TMPRSS2.md), NKX3.1) without altering AR transcript/protein, consistent with p85α's known AR-transactivating function. PIK3R1-knockdown LAPC4 cells were relatively resistant to [enzalutamide](../drugs/enzalutamide.md).

## Clinical implications

- **Biomarker for AKT-inhibitor selection**: PIK3R1 alteration may identify a PI3K-dependent prostate cancer subtype that responds to AKT-kinase inhibitors. In the IPATential150 phase 3 trial (NCT03072238) cited by the authors, [ipatasertib](../drugs/ipatasertib.md) + abiraterone/prednisone improved radiographic PFS only in PTEN-loss mCRPC (HR 0.77, 95% CI 0.61–0.98); the authors propose AKT inhibition has not been formally tested in PIK3R1-altered patients despite mechanistic rationale.
- **PIK3R1 + PTEN dual alteration** is a candidate biomarker hypothesis: organoid MSK-PCa3 (PIK3R1-mutant + PTEN-deleted) was AKT-inhibitor-sensitive while PIK3R1–wild-type MSK-PCa1 (PTEN-deleted) was only modestly affected — suggesting PTEN loss alone is insufficient and PIK3R1 status may stratify responders.
- **Noninvasive imaging biomarker**: high [FDG-PET](../methods/fdg-pet-ct.md) avidity in PIK3R1-altered prostate tumors gives a noninvasive screening test for trial eligibility in PI3K/AKT-targeted therapy — particularly relevant because FDG-PET is not routinely used in prostate cancer due to generally low FDG avidity.
- **Castration resistance**: PIK3R1 loss reduces AR target gene output and confers relative [enzalutamide](../drugs/enzalutamide.md) resistance in LAPC4 cells, suggesting PIK3R1-altered tumors may progress on AR-pathway-only therapy and may need combination with AKT inhibition.
- **Pan-cancer extension**: TNBC has the highest PIK3R1 alteration rate among breast subtypes (9.1%) and the lowest PIK3R1 mRNA — supporting evaluation of AKT inhibitors in TNBC stratified by PIK3R1 status.
- **Prognostic in primary prostate cancer**: PIK3R1 mRNA loss is independently associated with biochemical recurrence after adjustment for Gleason group, suggesting utility as a prognostic transcriptomic biomarker in localized disease.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Sample dropout**: only 1,417 of 2,735 MSK-IMPACT prostate patients passed FACETS QC. Excluded samples were primarily "copy-number quiet" with ambiguous purity, raising concern about selection bias and generalizability of the 36%/24% loss frequencies.
- **Outcomes inconclusive in metastatic disease**: while PIK3R1 alterations clearly predict worse outcomes in primary disease (TCGA, Taylor), associations with OS in MSK-IMPACT mCSPC and mCRPC cohorts were not statistically significant. Authors explicitly state the data are not strongly suggestive of worse metastatic prognosis.
- **Clonality coverage incomplete**: only 21 of 44 PIK3R1 mutations had clonality calls; subclonal predominance (~62%) needs validation in larger cohorts.
- **MSK-IMPACT has no transcriptome**, so DNA→mRNA bridging required TCGA — limiting direct integration of expression with the MSK clinical outcomes.
- **Heterozygous copy-number loss biology**: whether monoallelic PIK3R1 loss has clear oncogenic potential remains unsettled; GEMM data support haploinsufficiency but human cohorts need larger biallelic vs monoallelic comparisons.
- **AKT-inhibitor clinical evidence in PIK3R1-altered patients is absent**; the IPATential150 trial enrolled patients by PTEN status and post-hoc PIK3R1 analyses were not reported. A prospective PIK3R1-stratified trial is needed.
- **FDG-PET cohort size for PIK3R1-altered mCRPC** (n=10) is small; the FDR-significant SUV association warrants prospective validation.
- **Mechanism of c-SH2-domain hotspot specificity to prostate cancer** vs n-SH2/i-SH2 in endometrial cancer is hypothesis-generating only; functional dissection of domain-specific PIK3R1 mutations is open.
- **No mention of androgen-receptor signaling–inhibitor combination data** for AKT-inhibitor responders in PIK3R1-altered organoids/PDXs beyond enzalutamide-vs-PIK3R1 knockdown in LAPC4.
