# SEZ6

## Overview

SEZ6 (seizure related 6 homolog) is a complement control protein expressed on neuronal and neuroendocrine cell surfaces. In atypical SCLC (aSCLC), SEZ6 is uniformly highly expressed and has emerged as an emerging immunotherapy and antibody-drug conjugate (ADC) target in small cell lung cancer. High SEZ6 expression in aSCLC parallels that of DLL3 and makes the aSCLC subset potentially targetable by SEZ6-directed agents in clinical development.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Uniformly high SEZ6 expression in atypical SCLC (aSCLC; RB1+/TP53+, n=20); mean H score 240 across 9 evaluated samples; identified as an emerging ADC and immunotherapy target alongside DLL3 [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md):** SEZ6 uniformly highly expressed in aSCLC; mean H score 240 across evaluated samples; both SEZ6 and DLL3 high in this subset, supporting their use as therapeutic targets [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-expressed with [DLL3](../genes/DLL3.md) at high levels in aSCLC; both are established/emerging ADC/immunotherapy targets in SCLC [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Uniformly high SEZ6 expression suggests aSCLC patients may benefit from SEZ6-directed agents under clinical development; therapeutic implication parallel to DLL3-targeting approaches such as tarlatamab [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md)

## Open questions

- Functional role of SEZ6 in aSCLC pathogenesis is not established; high expression may reflect neuroendocrine lineage rather than active oncogenic role [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md)
