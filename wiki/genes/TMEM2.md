# TMEM2

## Overview

TMEM2 is a transmembrane protein-encoding gene that has been implicated in metastasis in multiple tumor types. In adenoid cystic carcinoma ([ACC](../cancer_types/ACC.md)), subclonal TMEM2 mutations were identified in the most divergent metastatic cluster during multiregion sequencing, suggesting a role in late metastatic evolution.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Subclonal TMEM2 mutation identified in a divergent metastatic cluster (metastases 6D, 2B, 4A) during multiregion WES + ultra-deep targeted sequencing of one salivary adenoid cystic carcinoma; found in the same NMF-defined cluster as [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md), [XDH](../genes/XDH.md), and [LTF](../genes/LTF.md) mutations — genes implicated in metastasis in other tumor types [PMID:31483290](../papers/31483290.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md)**: Subclonal TMEM2 mutation observed in a divergent metastatic cluster of a multiregion salivary ACC case; consistent with late metastatic acquisition rather than a primary driver event [PMID:31483290](../papers/31483290.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with SF3B1, XDH, and LTF mutations in the same subclonal metastatic cluster of adenoid cystic carcinoma; these mutations were not detected in primary tumor subregions [PMID:31483290](../papers/31483290.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic relevance established; presence in metastatic subclones implicates TMEM2 in metastatic progression biology in ACC [PMID:31483290](../papers/31483290.md).

## Open questions

- TMEM2's specific functional role in ACC metastasis is not established; observations come from a single multiregion case and require broader validation [PMID:31483290](../papers/31483290.md).
