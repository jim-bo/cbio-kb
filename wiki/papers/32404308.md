**PMID:** 32404308 · **DOI:** 10.1158/2159-8290.CD-19-1390 · **Journal:** Cancer Discovery (2020)

## TL;DR

Wander et al. performed whole-exome sequencing on 59 metastatic biopsies from 58 patients with HR+/HER2- metastatic breast cancer to map the genomic landscape of intrinsic and acquired resistance to CDK4/6 inhibitors (CDK4/6i). Eight categories of alterations — biallelic [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) disruption, activating mutation/amplification of [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md), activating [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)/[HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md)/[NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) mutations, [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) alterations, [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) mutations, [AURKA](../genes/AURKA.md) amplification, [CCNE2](../genes/CCNE2.md) amplification, and loss of ER expression — accounted for 27 of 41 (65.9%) resistant tumors versus 3 of 18 (16.7%) sensitive tumors. [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md), RAS, and [AURKA](../genes/AURKA.md) had not previously been demonstrated as mechanisms of CDK4/6i resistance in patients. In vitro overexpression / CRISPR knockout in T47D and MCF7 cells confirmed each alteration could confer resistance to [palbociclib](../drugs/palbociclib.md) or [abemaciclib](../drugs/abemaciclib.md), and cell lines cultured to resistance independently acquired [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) loss, [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) mutation, AURKA up-regulation, or [CCNE2](../genes/CCNE2.md) up-regulation. A single patient with HR+/HER2- [MBC](../cancer_types/MBC.md) who progressed on first-line palbociclib/letrozole derived ~11 months of disease control on the selective AURKA inhibitor LY3295668 in a phase I trial (NCT03092934).

## Cohort & data

- **Patients:** 58 patients with HR+/HER2- metastatic breast cancer ([BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md)) treated with a CDK4/6 inhibitor +/- anti-estrogen, enrolled on DF/HCC tissue protocol 05-246.
- **Biopsies:** 59 metastatic biopsies — 18 sensitive (baseline, subsequent clinical benefit), 28 intrinsic resistance (progression on first interval restaging or SD <6 months), 13 acquired resistance (biopsy at/after progression after initial benefit).
- **Treatment:** 49/58 patients (84.5%) received [palbociclib](../drugs/palbociclib.md); 55/58 (94.8%) combined an aromatase inhibitor or [fulvestrant](../drugs/fulvestrant.md) with the CDK4/6i. Mean treatment duration 316 days (range 43–1052).
- **Assay:** [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) of tumor + matched germline (PBMC) DNA, aligned to hg19; somatic SNV calling with [MuTect](../methods/mutect.md), structural variants/long deletions with [SvABA](../methods/svaba.md), cancer-cell fractions with [ABSOLUTE](../methods/absolute.md), clonal reconstruction with [PyClone](../methods/pyclone.md), ER status by [immunohistochemistry](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md).
- **Dataset:** [brca_dfci_2020](../datasets/brca_dfci_2020.md) on cBioPortal.

## Key findings

- **Eight resistance categories cover 65.9% of resistant tumors.** 27/41 resistant biopsies harbored at least one of biallelic [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) loss, [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md), RAS-family, [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md), [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md), [AURKA](../genes/AURKA.md), [CCNE2](../genes/CCNE2.md), or ER-loss alterations, versus 3/18 (16.7%) sensitive biopsies [PMID:32404308](../papers/32404308.md).
- **AURKA amplification is the most enriched novel mediator.** AURKA amplification occurred in 11/41 (26.8%) resistant biopsies (7 intrinsic, 4 acquired) and 0/18 sensitive (p=0.0081, Welch test) [PMID:32404308](../papers/32404308.md).
- **Biallelic RB1 disruption is exclusive to resistant samples but rare.** 4/41 (9.8%) resistant biopsies; each combined single-copy loss with a point mutation, splice-site, or frameshift on the second allele. In one patient, two post-progression biopsies showed distinct second-allele lesions — convergent evolution of RB1 loss [PMID:32404308](../papers/32404308.md).
- **AKT1 alterations in 5/41 (12.2%) resistant vs 1/18 (5.6%) sensitive**; included a pathogenic W80R point mutation acquired post-progression and an acquired AKT1 amplification on a baseline L52H VUS background [PMID:32404308](../papers/32404308.md).
- **RAS-pathway activation in 4/41 (9.8%) resistant, 0/18 sensitive:** [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) G12D, KRAS Q61L, [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) K117N, focal [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) amplification [PMID:32404308](../papers/32404308.md).
- **CCNE2 amplification in 6/41 (14.6%) resistant vs 1/18 (5.6%) sensitive** — the first reported association of CCNE2 (as opposed to [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md)) with CDK4/6i resistance [PMID:32404308](../papers/32404308.md).
- **[ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) alterations in 5/41 (12.2%) resistant** (two were VUS — L377M and P1074L); [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) alterations in 3/41 (7.3%, all intrinsic) [PMID:32404308](../papers/32404308.md).
- **ER loss by IHC in 3/41 (7.3%) resistant, 0/18 sensitive**, all in patients exposed to CDK4/6i + anti-estrogen [PMID:32404308](../papers/32404308.md).
- **Co-occurrence is common:** 12/41 (29.3%) resistant biopsies carried two or more of the eight drivers (e.g., concurrent ERBB2 + AURKA in samples 349_T1 and 366_T2; ER loss + AKT1 + RAS in 331_T1) [PMID:32404308](../papers/32404308.md).
- **Background enrichments:** [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutated in 24/41 (58.5%) resistant vs 0/18 sensitive; [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) amplification 8/41 (19.5%) vs 1/18 (5.6%); [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) 11/41 (26.8%) vs 3/18 (16.7%); [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) mutation 14/41 (34.1%) vs 3/18 (16.7%, all on [fulvestrant](../drugs/fulvestrant.md)). [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) mutations were equally frequent in both arms (44.4% sensitive vs 43.9% resistant) — not a resistance marker [PMID:32404308](../papers/32404308.md).
- **In vitro validation:** in T47D cells, lentiviral overexpression of AKT1, KRAS G12D, AURKA, or CCNE2, or CRISPR knockout of RB1, was each sufficient to confer resistance to [palbociclib](../drugs/palbociclib.md) or [abemaciclib](../drugs/abemaciclib.md). Similar results in MCF7 except AURKA, indicating cell-context dependence [PMID:32404308](../papers/32404308.md).
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md) loss alone is insufficient.** CRISPR knockout of TP53 in MCF7 (TP53 [WT](../cancer_types/WT.md)) did not confer CDK4/6i resistance, and T47D (TP53 mutant) is baseline CDK4/6i sensitive [PMID:32404308](../papers/32404308.md).
- **Independent emergence under selection:** cells cultured to resistance (361-AR-1, T47D-AR-1, 361-PR-1, MCF7-PR-1) spontaneously acquired KRAS G12V (ERK activation), RB1 down-regulation + AURKA up, AURKA + CCNE2 up, or CCNE2 up respectively [PMID:32404308](../papers/32404308.md).
- **Synthetic-lethal vulnerabilities in resistant derivatives:** KRAS/ERK-activated 361-AR-1 cells became sensitive to the ERK inhibitor LY3214996; AURKA-high or RB1-low cells (T47D-AR-1, 361-PR-1) to the selective AURKA inhibitor LY3295668; CCNE2-amplified MCF7-PR-1 cells to the [CHEK1](../genes/CHEK1.md) inhibitor [prexasertib](../drugs/prexasertib.md) [PMID:32404308](../papers/32404308.md).
- **Clinical proof-of-concept:** A patient with HR+/HER2-, RB1-protein-high MBC who had progressed after >3 years on first-line [palbociclib](../drugs/palbociclib.md) + [letrozole](../drugs/letrozole.md) achieved ~11 months of stable disease on LY3295668 on phase I trial NCT03092934 [PMID:32404308](../papers/32404308.md).

## Genes & alterations

- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — biallelic disruption (single-copy loss + second-allele point/splice/frameshift event) in 4/41 (9.8%) resistant biopsies; exclusive to resistance; convergent evolution in one patient. CRISPR RB1 knockout confers CDK4/6i resistance in T47D/MCF7 cells.
- [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) — activating mutations (e.g., W80R) and amplifications in 5/41 (12.2%) resistant; lentiviral overexpression confers CDK4/6i and fulvestrant resistance. First demonstration of AKT1 as a CDK4/6i resistance driver in patients.
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — G12D (canonical activating), Q61L pathogenic mutation, plus a spontaneously acquired KRAS G12V in 361-AR-1 cells. KRAS G12D overexpression confers resistance; ERK inhibition (LY3214996) restores sensitivity.
- [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) — K117N activating mutation in one resistant biopsy.
- [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) — focal high-level amplification in one resistant biopsy.
- [AURKA](../genes/AURKA.md) — amplification (mostly low–moderate magnitude) in 11/41 (26.8%) resistant, 0/18 sensitive (p=0.0081). Spontaneous AURKA up-regulation in T47D-AR-1 and 361-PR-1 cells; conferred sensitivity to selective AURKA inhibitor LY3295668. First in-patient evidence for AURKA in CDK4/6i resistance.
- [CCNE2](../genes/CCNE2.md) — amplification in 6/41 (14.6%) resistant vs 1/18 (5.6%) sensitive; overexpression confers resistance; CCNE2-amplified cells are sensitized to the CHEK1 inhibitor [prexasertib](../drugs/prexasertib.md). First report linking CCNE2 (vs CCNE1) to CDK4/6i resistance.
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) — activating mutations and amplifications in 5/41 (12.2%) resistant biopsies; two VUS variants (L377M, P1074L) included. Consistent with prior work from the same group.
- [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) — mutations/amplifications in 3/41 (7.3%) resistant biopsies, all intrinsic resistance.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — mutated in 24/41 (58.5%) resistant vs 0/18 sensitive; enriched but not sufficient in vitro (CRISPR knockout in MCF7 does not produce CDK4/6i resistance).
- [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) — mutations in 14/41 (34.1%) resistant vs 3/18 (16.7%) sensitive; sensitive-tumor [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) mutants occurred exclusively in fulvestrant-treated patients. Consistent with endocrine but not CDK4/6i resistance.
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — mutated at similar frequencies in sensitive (8/18, 44.4%) and resistant (18/41, 43.9%) — not a resistance marker.
- [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) — alterations in 11/41 (26.8%) resistant vs 3/18 (16.7%) sensitive; no consistent association with response.
- [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) — copy-number gains in 4/18 (22.2%) sensitive and 4/41 (9.8%) resistant — not enriched in resistance.
- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) — amplifications in 8/41 (19.5%) resistant vs 1/18 (5.6%) sensitive.
- [IGF1R](../genes/IGF1R.md) — amplification in 4/41 (9.8%) resistant vs 1/18 (5.6%) sensitive; flagged as candidate driver for future study.
- [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) — alterations in 4/41 (9.8%) resistant vs 1/18 (5.6%) sensitive; potentially funnels into MAPK-pathway resistance.
- [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md), [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md), [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — discussed as prior-literature resistance candidates; not enriched in this cohort (centromere-proximal CCNE1 copy calls noted as technically difficult by WES).

## Clinical implications

- **Therapeutic nodes.** The authors argue that 65.9% of resistant tumors could be addressed by targeting four pathway nodes: AURKA (covers RB1 loss + AURKA amplification), MAPK (covers RAS, ERBB2, FGFR2, [NF1](../genes/NF1.md)), AKT/MTOR (covers AKT1), and CCNE/CHEK1 (covers CCNE2).
- **AURKA inhibition** (LY3295668, also called LY3295668) achieved ~11 months of disease control in a single HR+/HER2-, RB1-protein-high patient on phase I trial NCT03092934 after progression on first-line palbociclib + [letrozole](../drugs/letrozole.md); supports a phase I study of LY3295668 in CDK4/6i-progressed HR+ MBC (NCT03955939). [alisertib](../drugs/alisertib.md) + fulvestrant is in randomized phase II (NCT02860000).
- **CHEK1 inhibition** with [prexasertib](../drugs/prexasertib.md) is rationalized for CCNE2-amplified post-CDK4/6i tumors.
- **ERK / MAPK inhibition** (ERK inhibitors, MEK inhibitors, SHP2 inhibitors) is proposed as a unifying strategy for the RAS / ERBB2 / FGFR2 / NF1 subset.
- **No actionable role for [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) status** as a CDK4/6i resistance biomarker in this cohort.
- **Precision over empiric.** The heterogeneity argues against a single non-selective post-CDK4/6i regimen and supports genomically guided trial design at the point of progression.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Sample size.** 41 resistant and 18 sensitive biopsies are too small to power formal tests for rare drivers (e.g., [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md) loss reported elsewhere) or to compare intrinsic vs acquired subgroups for proportional differences.
- **34.1% of resistant tumors (14/41) lack any of the eight defined mechanisms** — non-genomic resistance via epigenetic, miRNA, or post-translational events remains unmeasured.
- **Bulk exome sequencing** cannot resolve whether co-occurring drivers (29.3% of resistant biopsies have ≥2) sit in the same subclone or in parallel clones; the authors flag single-cell RNA-seq as a follow-up.
- **CCNE1 copy-number calls are unreliable by WES** because of centromere proximity — true CCNE1 amplification rates may be under-counted.
- **Matched pre/post pairs available for only 7 acquired-resistance patients** — limits inference about which alterations are truly acquired vs pre-existing minor clones.
- **TP53 enrichment in resistant tumors is unexplained** — possibly reflects heavier pre-treatment, permissiveness for co-drivers, or cooperative interactions; CRISPR knockout alone does not phenocopy resistance.
- **Functional validation used T47D and MCF7 only** — context dependence was already apparent (AURKA overexpression failed to drive resistance in MCF7), so generalizability to all HR+ tumors is uncertain.
- **VUS calls in ERBB2** (L377M, P1074L) are not formally demonstrated to drive resistance.
- **Single-patient clinical example for AURKA inhibition** — the LY3295668 case is hypothesis-generating, not a trial result.
