# CLTC

## Overview

CLTC (clathrin heavy chain) is well established as a fusion partner in various hematologic malignancies. In renal cell carcinoma, a novel CLTC-TFEB fusion has been identified in translocation RCC, expanding the spectrum of TFEB-driven tumors beyond classical chromosomal translocations.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- CLTC-TFEB fusion identified as novel in translocation RCC (sample 8432T, reclassified tRCC); encodes an in-frame fusion preserving the TFEB bHLH domain [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **TRCC**: CLTC-TFEB fusion places this case in the MiTF-high tRCC subgroup; the case was reclassified as tRCC based on molecular findings [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- CLTC-TFEB fusion is part of the broader MiTF-high subtype alongside ASPSCR1-TFE3, ACTG1-MITF fusions and TFEB focal amplification [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- MiTF-high tumors including CLTC-TFEB fusion tRCC may benefit from MET inhibitors (downstream target) and BIRC7-directed therapies [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)

## Open questions

- Whether CLTC-TFEB differs functionally from classical t(6;11) TFEB translocations remains uncharacterized [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)
