# Clinical and Genomic Landscape of FGFR3-Altered Urothelial Carcinoma and Treatment Outcomes with Erdafitinib: A Real-World Experience

**PMID:** 37682528 · **DOI:** 10.1158/1078-0432.CCR-23-1283 · **Journal:** Clinical Cancer Research (2023)

## TL;DR

Guercio et al. integrated MSK-IMPACT tumor sequencing and serial MSK-ACCESS cfDNA profiling on 1,421 patients (1,507 tumors) with urothelial carcinoma to map the landscape of [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md)/3 alterations and characterize real-world outcomes on [erdafitinib](../drugs/erdafitinib.md) in 32 metastatic patients. Oncogenic [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) alterations were common (27.5% overall, enriched in non-muscle-invasive and upper-tract disease), [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) actionable alterations were vanishingly rare (1 fusion), and primary/metastatic [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) status was discordant in 26% (7/27) of paired cases. Erdafitinib produced a 40% response rate but median PFS of only 2.8 months and OS of 6.6 months, with toxicity-driven dose reductions (38%) and interruptions (50%); cfDNA identified putative resistance mutations in [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (n=5), [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) (n=1), and second-site [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) kinase-domain mutations including V553M and N540S ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).

## Cohort & data

- 1,421 patients / 1,507 urothelial carcinoma tumors sequenced at MSK (largest single-institution UC cohort to date); cancer types: [BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md) and [UTUC](../cancer_types/UTUC.md) ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- Disease-state breakdown: 504 NMIBC, 526 MIBC, 187 localized upper-tract, 228 distant metastatic specimens ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- Erdafitinib-treated subcohort: 32 patients with metastatic urothelial carcinoma treated between 8/2019 and 7/2022 ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- Tumor assay: [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) FDA-authorized targeted panel; cfDNA assay: [MSK-ACCESS](../methods/ACCESS129.md) (129 genes, >15,000x, 0.1% VAF threshold); copy number via [FACETS](../methods/facets.md); oncogenicity via [OncoKB](../methods/oncokb.md) ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- Dataset: [bladder_msk_2023](../datasets/bladder_msk_2023.md) on cBioPortal ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).

## Key findings

- Oncogenic [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) alterations frequencies by state: 39% (199/504) NMIBC, 14% (75/526) MIBC, 43% (81/187) localized [UTUC](../cancer_types/UTUC.md), 26% (59/228) metastatic ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- Only 1/1,507 tumors had a potentially actionable [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) alteration (an FGFR2:MARVELD3 fusion of unclear significance) ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- FGFR3 S249C was the most frequent alteration overall; R248C was enriched in upper-tract (22%, 18/81) vs bladder (11%, 37/333; p=0.01) tumors ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- Primary/metastatic FGFR3 discordance in 7/27 (26%) paired cases: 4 metastasis-only, 2 primary-only, 1 with different FGFR3 alterations in each site ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- PI3K pathway co-alterations in FGFR2/3-altered tumors: [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) 28% (115/414), [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md) 13% (52/414), [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) 2.7% (11/414); total 37% ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) co-alteration in 5% (19/414) of FGFR3-altered tumors, ranging 2.5% NMIBC to 11% MIBC ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- FGFR3-altered tumors showed inverse association with [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), and [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) alterations and frequent co-alteration of [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md), and [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md) ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- FGFR3-fusion tumors had lower TMB than FGFR3-mutant tumors (median 5 vs 9 mut/Mb, p=0.0006) ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- Erdafitinib ORR 40% (12/30 evaluable); median PFS 2.8 mo (95% CI 2.6–5.4); median OS 6.6 mo (95% CI 4.6–17.3) ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- Dose reductions in 38% (12/32), interruptions in 50% (16/32); TEAEs: hyperphosphatemia 84%, fatigue 59%, mucositis 47% (grade ≥3 16%), PPE 38% (grade ≥3 9%), dysgeusia 31% ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- No PFS/OS difference on immune checkpoint blockade by FGFR3 status (n=26 altered vs 155 wildtype; p=0.47/0.52) ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- Baseline [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) co-alteration associated with lower ORR (22%, 2/9 vs overall 40%) and enriched among primary progressors (3/5, 60%) ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- Serial cfDNA (n=27) identified on-treatment acquired TP53 (n=5/27), FGFR3 (n=2/27), and [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) (n=1/27) oncogenic mutations as putative resistance drivers ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- Acquired FGFR3 kinase-domain mutations at conserved residues (N540S, V553M, K650M, R669G); in silico modeling supports V553M as a gatekeeper allosterically blocking [erdafitinib](../drugs/erdafitinib.md) binding ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).

## Genes & alterations

- [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) — oncogenic mutations (S249C, R248C, Y373C) and fusions (FGFR3-[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md)); primary actionable biomarker for [erdafitinib](../drugs/erdafitinib.md). Acquired kinase-domain mutations N540S and V553M implicated as gatekeeper resistance alleles ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) — only one actionable alteration in 1,507 tumors (an FGFR2:MARVELD3 fusion of unclear significance) ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — inversely associated with FGFR3 alterations; baseline co-mutation correlated with primary erdafitinib resistance; acquired TP53 mutations seen in 5/27 cfDNA on-treatment series ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) — one case acquired an activating AKT1 mutation on erdafitinib as a putative bypass resistance mechanism ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md) — PI3K-pathway co-alterations common in FGFR3-altered tumors but did not predict response to erdafitinib ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) — inversely associated with FGFR3 alterations overall but co-altered in 11% of FGFR3-altered MIBC ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md), [CDKN1A](../genes/CDKN1A.md), [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md) — frequently co-altered with FGFR3 ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — rare in FGFR3-altered tumors (1.2%, 5/414) and inversely associated with FGFR3 alterations ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).

## Clinical implications

- Archival primary tumor sequencing may misclassify FGFR3 status of metastatic disease in ~26% of patients; authors argue for metastatic-site or cfDNA profiling to guide erdafitinib selection ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- Real-world erdafitinib activity is modest (PFS 2.8 mo) and tolerability-limited, supporting unmet need for FGFR3-isoform-specific inhibitors (e.g., TYRA300, LOXO435) ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- Patients with FGFR3-altered tumors should still be offered immune checkpoint blockade as standard of care, since FGFR3 status did not alter ICB outcomes in this cohort ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- TP53 co-alteration may flag higher risk of primary erdafitinib failure and motivate alternative-therapy selection, pending prospective validation ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- cfDNA detection of FGFR3 V553M/N540S can serve as an early biomarker of evolving erdafitinib resistance ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).

## Limitations & open questions

- Retrospective single-institution cohort subject to sampling bias; erdafitinib-treated subgroup small (n=32) and enriched for adverse prognostic factors relative to BLC2001 ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- cfDNA collection timepoints were inconsistent due to COVID-19-related visit reductions ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- MSK-IMPACT panel does not capture transcriptomic resistance mechanisms (e.g., FGFR3 expression changes) or alterations in off-panel genes ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
- Functional validation of V553M and N540S as bona fide gatekeeper mutations, and of TP53-loss as a resistance driver, remains to be established ([PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md)).
