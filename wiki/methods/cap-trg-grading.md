# CAP Tumor Regression Grading

## Overview

The College of American Pathologists (CAP) tumor regression grading (TRG) system is a standardized pathologic assessment of treatment effect following neoadjuvant therapy, primarily used in esophageal and gastroesophageal junction ([GEJ](../cancer_types/GEJ.md)) adenocarcinoma. Grades are assigned on a 0–3 scale: grade 0 = complete response (no residual tumor), grade 1 = near-complete response (≥90% treatment effect, single cells or small groups of residual cancer cells), grade 2 = partial response (residual cancer with evident tumor regression), and grade 3 = poor/no response. In combined pathologic response criteria, major pathologic response (nT-R) is typically defined as pN0 (absence of nodal disease) AND CAP-TRG 0 or 1.

## Used by

- MSK retrospective study of 237 locally advanced esophageal/GEJ adenocarcinoma patients (2014–2020): major pathologic response (nT-R) defined as pN0 AND CAP-TRG 0 or 1 (≥90% treatment effect); 64 nT-R vs 159 nT-NR in 223 MSI-stable patients [PMID:35377946](../papers/35377946.md).

## Notes

- Grading is performed on the resection specimen after neoadjuvant chemoradiotherapy or chemotherapy.
- The CAP-TRG system is widely used in clinical trials for esophagogastric cancers (e.g., CROSS, FLOT4, CALGB 80803) as a standardized endpoint.
- In the MSK esophageal adenocarcinoma cohort, only 12% of patients achieved pCR (grade 0), while the combined nT-R rate was ~29% (64/223).
