# SREBF2

## Overview

SREBF2 (sterol regulatory element binding transcription factor 2), also known as SREBP2, is the master transcription factor driving cholesterol biosynthesis. In hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC), SREBF2 is stabilized by caspase-3 and unspliced XBP1. Loss of SREBF2 alone is insufficient to prevent hepatocarcinogenesis; combined FASN and SREBF2 loss is required to fully abolish tumor development in Pten-loss + MET-overexpression mouse models, demonstrating cross-pathway compensatory rewiring between DNL and cholesterol biosynthesis.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Master transcription factor of cholesterol biosynthesis in HCC; stabilized by caspase-3 and unspliced XBP1; combined FASN + SREBF2 loss required to fully abolish tumor development in Pten-loss + cMet-overexpression HCC models (single pathway inhibition compensated by the other) [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md):** SREBF2 drives cholesterol biosynthesis; cross-pathway compensation between SREBF2 (cholesterol) and FASN (de novo lipogenesis) is a key mechanism of resistance to single-pathway lipid metabolism inhibition [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Functionally linked with [FASN](../genes/FASN.md): FASN loss activates cholesterol biosynthesis via SREBF2 as compensation; only combined Fasn + Srebf2 loss abolishes tumor development in preclinical models; upstream regulator of [HMGCR](../genes/HMGCR.md), [SQLE](../genes/SQLE.md) [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Single-agent FASN inhibition is insufficient in the Pten/MET HCC context due to SREBF2-mediated cholesterol pathway upregulation; combination targeting of both DNL and cholesterol biosynthesis is proposed [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Open questions

- The clinical relevance of the FASN + SREBF2 synthetic-lethality context in human HCC subtypes remains to be established; the preclinical data derive from engineered mouse models [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)
