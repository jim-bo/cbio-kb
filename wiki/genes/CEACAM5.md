# CEACAM5

## Overview

CEACAM5 (Carcinoembryonic Antigen Related Cell Adhesion Molecule 5), commonly known as CEA, is a well-established clinical biomarker for colorectal cancer (CRC). It encodes a GPI-anchored cell-surface glycoprotein involved in cell adhesion and is broadly overexpressed in carcinomas of the colon, lung, breast, and stomach. CEA is used clinically for CRC diagnosis, staging, and post-operative surveillance. Its detection in plasma makes it a candidate blood-based biomarker.
