# alisertib

## Overview

Alisertib (MLN8237) is an oral selective Aurora kinase A ([AURKA](../genes/AURKA.md)) inhibitor. [AURKA](../genes/AURKA.md) plays a key role in mitotic spindle assembly and centrosome function; its inhibition arrests cells in mitosis and promotes apoptosis. Alisertib has been investigated in clinical trials across multiple solid tumors, including prostate cancer with neuroendocrine differentiation and hematologic malignancies.

## Evidence in the corpus

- In the mCRPC cohort ([prad_su2c_2019](../datasets/prad_su2c_2019.md), n=332), the observed post-ARSI neuroendocrine (NE) feature frequency (10.5%) was lower than the 17% reported in a contemporary study; the authors attributed part of this discrepancy to the inclusion of patients with NE features from an alisertib (Aurora kinase A inhibitor) clinical trial in the comparator cohort, highlighting alisertib trials as a context in which aggressive-variant, NE-enriched mCRPC is selectively enrolled.
