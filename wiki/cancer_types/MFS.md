# Myxofibrosarcoma (MFS)

## Overview

Myxofibrosarcoma (formerly myxoid malignant fibrous histiocytoma) is a soft-tissue sarcoma of fibroblastic differentiation with prominent myxoid stroma, classified under Soft Tissue Sarcoma in OncoTree (parent: [SOFT_TISSUE](../cancer_types/SOFT_TISSUE.md)). It predominantly affects elderly patients, arises in the extremities, and has a high local recurrence rate.

## Cohorts in the corpus

- [sarc_mskcc](../datasets/sarc_mskcc.md): 38 MFS patients (18.4% of 207 high-grade [STS](../cancer_types/STS.md), including pleomorphic [MFH](../cancer_types/MFH.md)), profiled by targeted resequencing (722 genes), 250K SNP-array SCNA, LOH, and expression arrays. [PMID:20601955](../papers/20601955.md)
- [sarcoma_ucla_2024](../datasets/sarcoma_ucla_2024.md): represented in the 24-subtype UCLA sarcoma PDTO biobank (194 specimens from 126 patients, Feb 2018–May 2022). [PMID:39305899](../papers/39305899.md)

## Recurrent alterations

- [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) — mutated in 10.5% of MFS (point mutations + genomic deletions); biallelic inactivation (heterozygous mutation + WT-allele deletion) observed; loss linked to mTOR pathway activation. Two mutations (R304*, Q369*) previously reported as germline [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) alleles. First report of somatic [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) alterations outside MPNST/GIST. [PMID:20601955](../papers/20601955.md)
- [ERBB4](../genes/ERBB4.md) — mutations found on resequencing of MFS (and [PLLS](../cancer_types/PLLS.md)) samples. [PMID:20601955](../papers/20601955.md)
- Complex karyotype: MFS clusters with [DDLS](../cancer_types/DDLS.md), [PLLS](../cancer_types/PLLS.md), and [LMS](../cancer_types/LMS.md) as complex-karyotype subtypes with copy-neutral LOH; MFS and [PLLS](../cancer_types/PLLS.md) are strikingly similar genomically, supporting a unified molecular class. [PMID:20601955](../papers/20601955.md)
- No recurrent driver alterations specifically characterized for MFS in the UCLA PDTO study. Complex karyotype with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) alterations are common in the broader literature.
- MFS (n=17) was molecularly indistinguishable from [UPS](../cancer_types/UPS.md) across platforms; myxoid-stroma gene expression was the primary separator; recurrent [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md) (10%), [VGLL3](../genes/VGLL3.md) (11%), and [YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md) (3%) amplifications implicate the Hippo pathway; high NK-cell and dendritic-cell immune signatures correlated with improved DSS, nominating MFS alongside [UPS](../cancer_types/UPS.md) for checkpoint-inhibitor trials [PMID:29100075](../papers/29100075.md)
- In a 7,494-sarcoma cohort, myxofibrosarcoma (MFS) showed CD274/PD-L1 amplification in 2.6% and was among the genomically complex sarcoma histotypes with elevated p53/Rb pathway alteration rates [PMID:35705558](../papers/35705558.md).
- In the 2,138-sarcoma MSK-IMPACT cohort, MFS showed [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) alteration in 16% (more frequent than related UPS at 6%); [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) loss-of-function in ≥10%; epigenetic pathway alteration in 36%; WGD was not significantly prognostic in MFS (p=0.78) [PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md).

## Subtypes

- Graded I–III by degree of cellularity and mitotic activity (FNCLCC system).

## Therapeutic landscape

- mTOR inhibition proposed for NF1-deficient MFS: [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) loss activates the RAS–mTOR axis. [PMID:20601955](../papers/20601955.md)
- Patient-derived tumor organoid (PDTO) drug screening was applied across all 24 sarcoma subtypes in the UCLA cohort, including MFS. Across the full cohort, 80.4% of specimens screened had at least one significant drug response, and 59% had at least one FDA-approved or NCCN-recommended top-five regimen. [PMID:39305899](../papers/39305899.md)
