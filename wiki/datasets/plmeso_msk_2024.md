# Diffuse Pleural Mesothelioma (MSK, 2024)

## Overview

Single-center cohort of 290 patients with diffuse pleural mesothelioma ([PLMESO](../cancer_types/PLMESO.md)) profiled by [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center (January 2014–January 2021). A subset of 210 patients (72%) with sufficient tumor purity was analyzed for LOH using [FACETS](../methods/facets.md) to identify a genomic near-haploidization (GNH) subset. Data deposited in cBioPortal as `plmeso_msk_2024`. [PMID:38630790](../papers/38630790.md)

## Composition

- 290 patients with DPM profiled by MSK-IMPACT; 210 evaluable for LOH analysis (≥20% tumor purity). [PMID:38630790](../papers/38630790.md)
- 10 GNH cases (4.8%) in the primary cohort; 4 additional in a 105-patient validation cohort. [PMID:38630790](../papers/38630790.md)
- Demographics: majority male (73%), median age 70 years; GNH patients younger (median 58 years). [PMID:38630790](../papers/38630790.md)
- Cancer type: [PLMESO](../cancer_types/PLMESO.md) — diffuse pleural mesothelioma. [PMID:38630790](../papers/38630790.md)

## Assays / panels (linked)

- [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) targeted sequencing (primary cohort). [PMID:38630790](../papers/38630790.md)
- [IMPACT505](../methods/IMPACT505.md) for 105-patient validation cohort. [PMID:38630790](../papers/38630790.md)
- Whole-exome sequencing (n=3). [PMID:38630790](../papers/38630790.md)
- Illumina methylationEPIC/850k methylation profiling. [PMID:38630790](../papers/38630790.md)
- [FACETS](../methods/facets.md) for allele-specific copy-number and LOH analysis. [PMID:38630790](../papers/38630790.md)

## Papers using this cohort

- [PMID:38630790](../papers/38630790.md) — Diffuse pleural mesotheliomas with genomic near-haploidization: a newly recognized subset with distinct clinical, histologic, and molecular features.

## Notable findings derived from this cohort

- GNH prevalence: 4.8% (10/210 evaluable), similar to TCGA-ICGC rate of 3.2%. GNH patients were younger (58 vs. 71 years, P=0.008) and had less occupational asbestos exposure (20% vs. 58.3%, P=0.02). [PMID:38630790](../papers/38630790.md)
- 80% of GNH DPMs were biphasic vs. 15% of non-GNH (P<0.0001). [PMID:38630790](../papers/38630790.md)
