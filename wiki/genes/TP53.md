# TP53

## Overview

TP53 encodes the p53 tumor suppressor protein, a master transcription factor governing DNA damage response, apoptosis, and cell cycle arrest. TP53 loss-of-function is one of the most common somatic alterations across human cancers, with roles established in hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC), gastric adenocarcinoma (STAD), nasopharyngeal carcinoma (NPC), and thymic carcinoma.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Loss-of-function mutation in 28% of HCCs (range 23-31%; WES, n=1,289); defines the TCGA iClust3 subtype associated with chromosomal instability and poor prognosis; enriched in the proliferation class. [PMID:24798001](../papers/24798001.md)
- TP53 somatic mutations recur in FIGC tumors (familial intestinal gastric cancer), uncommon in matched sporadic intestinal GC; common risk-associated TP53 polymorphisms enriched in 26/50 FIGC probands vs. 4/38 (11%) sporadic comparators. [PMID:24816255](../papers/24816255.md)
- TP53 germline mutations define Li-Fraumeni syndrome, which must be excluded in familial gastric cancer workup; included in the mandatory multigene panel. [PMID:24816255](../papers/24816255.md)
- TP53 mutations and reduced immune infiltration reported at higher rates in EBV-negative NPC compared to EBV-positive NPC; EBV-negative tumors have distinct genomic profiles including higher TP53 mutation rates. [PMID:24952746](../papers/24952746.md)
- Recurrent somatic TP53 mutations in thymic carcinomas (aggressive histotype); one of the canonical cancer-gene mutations enriched in thymic carcinoma relative to indolent thymomas (which instead carry the GTF2I L404H hotspot). [PMID:24974848](../papers/24974848.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **HCC** -- TP53 LOF in 28%; trunk driver, not currently druggable; defines the proliferation molecular class with poor outcomes; co-occurs with chromosomal instability. [PMID:24798001](../papers/24798001.md)
- **STAD** -- Somatic TP53 mutations in FIGC; germline TP53 (Li-Fraumeni) must be excluded in familial GC; risk-associated TP53 polymorphisms enriched in familial intestinal GC probands. [PMID:24816255](../papers/24816255.md)
- **NPC** -- TP53 mutations enriched in EBV-negative (non-endemic) NPC; lower immune infiltration in TP53-mutant EBV-negative tumors has implications for ICI trial stratification. [PMID:24952746](../papers/24952746.md)
- **THYC (thymic carcinoma)** -- recurrent somatic TP53 mutations; part of the aggressive molecular profile (alongside CYLD, CDKN2A, BAP1, PBRM1) distinguishing thymic carcinomas from indolent thymomas. [PMID:24974848](../papers/24974848.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- In HCC, TP53 inactivation is enriched in the proliferation class alongside FGF19/CCND1 amplifications and HBV aetiology; mutually enriched in iClust3 with chromosomal instability. [PMID:24798001](../papers/24798001.md)
- In thymic carcinomas, TP53 co-occurs with CYLD, CDKN2A, BAP1, and PBRM1 mutations, defining a high mutation-burden aggressive subtype distinct from GTF2I-mutant indolent thymomas. [PMID:24974848](../papers/24974848.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- TP53 is not directly druggable with currently approved agents in HCC; listed among trunk HCC mutations for which no actionable targeted therapy exists. [PMID:24798001](../papers/24798001.md)
- TP53 mutation status in NPC (enriched in EBV-negative tumors) has implications for ICI trial stratification, as EBV-negative NPC has lower immune infiltration. [PMID:24952746](../papers/24952746.md)

## Open questions

- In HCC, blocking TP53-driven chromosomal instability is an unmet need; no TP53 reactivation strategy has been validated clinically. [PMID:24798001](../papers/24798001.md)
- In NPC, whether TP53 mutation status should stratify patients for ICI vs. other treatment modalities requires prospective validation. [PMID:24952746](../papers/24952746.md)
- In thymic carcinomas, the prognostic and predictive significance of TP53 mutations relative to other co-occurring alterations (CYLD, BAP1, PBRM1) requires further characterization. [PMID:24974848](../papers/24974848.md)
