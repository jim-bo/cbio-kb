# BMPR2

## Overview

BMPR2 (Bone Morphogenetic Protein Receptor Type 2) is a TGF-beta superfamily receptor that functions as a tumor suppressor in multiple cancer contexts. In colon cancer, BMPR2 is one of four newly identified significantly mutated genes (SMGs) in the hypermutated (MSI-H) subgroup, mutated in >50% of hypermutated samples in a prospective proteogenomic cohort — a finding not previously reported in the TCGA colorectal study.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- BMPR2 identified as a newly discovered SMG in the hypermutated (MSI-H) subgroup of a prospective proteogenomic colon cancer cohort (n=110); mutated in >50% of hypermutated samples; not reported as an SMG in the prior TCGA colorectal study [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md):** Hypermutated-group SMG in the CPTAC colon cohort; frameshift/truncating mutations predominate in MSI-H tumors [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with other newly identified hypermutated SMGs [CASP5](../genes/CASP5.md), [RNF43](../genes/RNF43.md), and [LTN1](../genes/LTN1.md) in MSI-H colon tumors; all four were mutated in >50% of hypermutated samples and were not previously reported by TCGA [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting of BMPR2 demonstrated in the corpus; mutations may contribute to MSI-H tumor biology relevant to checkpoint blockade sensitivity [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Open questions

- The functional consequence of BMPR2 mutations in MSI-H colon tumors has not been experimentally validated in this study; whether BMPR2 loss cooperates with mismatch-repair deficiency to drive hypermutation or is a bystander effect is unresolved [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)
