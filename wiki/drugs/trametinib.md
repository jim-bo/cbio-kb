# trametinib

## Overview

Allosteric MEK1/2 inhibitor.

## Evidence in the corpus

- Used as matched targeted therapy for [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md)-mutant histiocytosis patients in the Make-an-IMPACT program; MEK inhibition (trametinib/cobimetinib) contributed to clinical benefit in 17/18 treated histiocytosis patients (mean duration 21.7 months, range 6–40+) [PMID:36862133](../papers/36862133.md).
- A [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md)-mutant histiocytosis patient and an [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md)-mutant case were also treated with MEK inhibitor [PMID:36862133](../papers/36862133.md).
- Trametinib (MEK inhibitor monotherapy) provided durable benefit in pilocytic astrocytoma patients with [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) fusions (median 11 months, all 6 patients >6 months on therapy); combination with [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) inhibitors was less durable (median 1 month) in a tumor-agnostic BRAF fusion cohort (N=241) [PMID:38922339](../papers/38922339.md).
- One patient with acquired BRAF fusion after [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) TKI resistance remained on [erlotinib](../drugs/erlotinib.md) + trametinib for 12 months [PMID:38922339](../papers/38922339.md).
