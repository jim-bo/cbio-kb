# Bladder Squamous Cell Carcinoma (BLSC)

## Overview

Bladder squamous cell carcinoma (BLSC) is a rare variant of bladder cancer arising from squamous differentiation of the urothelium, classified under the BLADDER node in OncoTree. It is histologically distinct from the more common urothelial carcinoma ([BLCA](BLCA.md)) and represents a minority of bladder cancers. BLSC can arise de novo or in association with chronic irritation, infection (e.g., Schistosoma haematobium), or prolonged catheterization.

## Cohorts in the corpus

- Patient-derived bladder organoid biobank (Columbia University Medical Center / Memorial Sloan Kettering): 1 of 18 patients (SCBO-11 line) had squamous-cell-carcinoma features on H&E review, classified as BLSC; sequenced with MSK-IMPACT 468-gene panel and whole-exome sequencing; deposited as cBioPortal study [bladder_columbia_msk_2018](../datasets/bladder_columbia_msk_2018.md) [PMID:29625057](../papers/29625057.md).

## Recurrent alterations

- SCBO-11 organoid line (BLSC) showed squamous-cell-carcinoma histopathologic features concordant with its parental tumor; epigenetic regulators ([ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md)) and [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) alterations are recurrently detected across the broader organoid cohort including the BLSC-histology line [PMID:29625057](../papers/29625057.md).

## Subtypes

- Bladder squamous cell carcinoma may arise with or without association with Schistosoma infection; the corpus does not currently stratify by this etiology.

## Therapeutic landscape

- In the organoid biobank context, drug screening was performed across the cohort including the BLSC-histology line with agents including [trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md), [gemcitabine](../drugs/gemcitabine.md), [erdafitinib](../drugs/erdafitinib.md), [AZD8055](../drugs/azd8055.md), and [sirolimus](../drugs/sirolimus.md) [PMID:29625057](../papers/29625057.md).
