# SQLE

## Overview

SQLE (squalene epoxidase) is a rate-limiting enzyme in cholesterol biosynthesis, catalyzing the conversion of squalene to 2,3-oxidosqualene. In [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md), SQLE is frequently amplified and overexpressed, making it a promising therapeutic target in cholesterol-addicted tumors.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- SQLE is a cholesterol biosynthesis enzyme implicated in HCC; part of the cholesterol pathway alongside [HMGCR](../genes/HMGCR.md) and [MVK](../genes/MVK.md) [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) — SQLE upregulation drives cholesterol biosynthesis and HCC progression [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-expressed with HMGCR and MVK in the cholesterol biosynthesis axis in HCC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- SQLE inhibitors (e.g., terbinafine, NB-598) are proposed as metabolic therapeutic targets in cholesterol-addicted HCC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Open questions

- Clinical evidence for SQLE inhibition in HCC is limited to preclinical studies.
