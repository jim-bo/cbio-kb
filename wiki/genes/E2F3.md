# E2F3

## Overview

E2F3 is a transcription factor that activates genes required for S-phase entry and DNA replication; it is a primary transcriptional target released by RB1 upon CDK-mediated hyperphosphorylation. E2F3 amplification at chromosome 6p22 is a recognized event in retinoblastoma that may contribute to tumorigenesis in the subset of tumors with 6p gain, and has been proposed as a potential driver when RB1 inactivation is incomplete.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- E2F3 is discussed as a driver candidate in retinoblastoma (rbl_mskcc_2020, n=83 enucleated specimens); no recurrent E2F3 coding mutations were identified in this MSK-IMPACT–profiled cohort. E2F3 is listed among previously proposed driver candidates (alongside DEK, RBL2, KIF14, NGFR) whose role is not confirmed at the somatic coding-mutation level in this dataset [PMID:33466343](../papers/33466343.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **Retinoblastoma (RBL):** E2F3 is a previously proposed candidate based on chromosomal location at 6p22 (6p gain is a known recurrent CNA in retinoblastoma); no recurrent somatic coding mutations in 83 MSK-IMPACT specimens [PMID:33466343](../papers/33466343.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- No co-occurrence data from somatic mutations; E2F3 amplification may be linked to the 6p gain CNA observed in RB1-wildtype retinoblastoma cases [PMID:33466343](../papers/33466343.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No therapeutic targeting reported in the corpus.

## Open questions

- Whether E2F3 amplification (via 6p gain) contributes to retinoblastoma tumorigenesis independently of or cooperatively with partial RB1 inactivation requires functional testing [PMID:33466343](../papers/33466343.md).
