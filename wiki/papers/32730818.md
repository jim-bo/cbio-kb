**PMID:** 32730818 · **DOI:** 10.1053/j.gastro.2020.07.041 · **Journal:** Gastroenterology (2020)

## TL;DR

Mondaca et al. analyzed 430 microsatellite-stable (MSS) unresectable metastatic colorectal cancer (mCRC) patients treated at Memorial Sloan Kettering with first-line chemotherapy whose tumors were genomically profiled by [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md). They found that [APC](../genes/APC.md) mutation site (N-terminal vs C-terminal, split at amino acid 1400) was prognostic — N-terminal [APC](../genes/APC.md) mutations were associated with longer overall and progression-free survival, while C-terminal APC mutations co-occurred more frequently with activating [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), and [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) alterations. In contrast, DNA damage response (DDR) pathway alterations were not associated with survival or with response to oxaliplatin-containing chemotherapy, arguing against PARP-inhibitor-type vulnerabilities in mCRC.

## Cohort & data

- **n=430** patients with MSS unresectable mCRC treated at Memorial Sloan Kettering with first-line chemotherapy between 2014 and 2017 ([PMID:32730818](../papers/32730818.md)).
- Cancer type: metastatic [colorectal adenocarcinoma](../cancer_types/COADREAD.md) (includes [colon](../cancer_types/COAD.md) and [rectal](../cancer_types/READ.md) primaries).
- Dataset: [crc_apc_impact_2020](../datasets/crc_apc_impact_2020.md).
- Tumor genomic profiling: [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) targeted sequencing panel.
- Copy-number / LOH analysis: [FACETS](../methods/facets.md) allele-specific copy number algorithm; large state transitions (LST) computed as ≥10 Mb breaks per chromosome arm.
- Survival analysis: [Kaplan-Meier](../methods/kaplan-meier.md) for PFS/OS from first-line treatment start; multivariate adjustment via [Cox proportional hazards](../methods/cox-proportional-hazards.md).

## Key findings

- Among MSS mCRC (n=430), only [APC](../genes/APC.md) and [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) alteration frequencies significantly varied by duration of response and were associated with PFS and [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) ([PMID:32730818](../papers/32730818.md)).
- Multivariate analysis: [APC](../genes/APC.md) mutation was associated with longer PFS (HR 0.68, p<0.001) and OS (HR 0.56, p<0.001) versus tumors with no Wnt pathway alteration or with alterations in other Wnt pathway genes.
- [APC](../genes/APC.md) truncating mutations were split at amino acid 1400 into N-terminal (containing up to two 20R β-catenin-binding repeats) and C-terminal groups. Survival differences first appeared at aa 600 and became significantly shorter in the C-terminal group at aa 1400.
- Patients with N-terminal-side [APC](../genes/APC.md) mutations had significantly longer OS and PFS than those with C-terminal-side or no APC alteration. The difference persisted after adjustment for tumor sidedness.
- Co-mutation patterns by APC side:
  - [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) alterations: 46% in N-terminal vs 72% in C-terminal APC mutant cases (p<0.001, q<0.01).
  - [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) alterations: 2% in N-terminal vs 13% in C-terminal APC mutant cases (p<0.001, q<0.01).
  - [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) alterations: 11% in N-terminal vs 28% in C-terminal APC mutant cases (p<0.01, q<0.1).
  - [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) alterations: 3% in N-terminal vs 12% in C-terminal APC mutant cases (p<0.01, q<0.1).
  - [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations were more frequent in tumors with N-terminal-side APC mutations.
- ~75% of C-terminal-side APC tumors had concurrent activating mitogenic alterations vs ~50% of N-terminal-side APC tumors.
- N-terminal APC mutations were more common in left-sided colon/rectal primaries; C-terminal APC mutations were more common in right-sided colon tumors.
- [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) and [RNF43](../genes/RNF43.md) alterations commonly co-occurred with RAS pathway alterations (63% with [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md), 71% with [RNF43](../genes/RNF43.md)) and identified mCRC with poor prognosis.
- **DDR pathway alterations were not associated with survival across the cohort** and were not associated with PFS in patients receiving [oxaliplatin](../drugs/oxaliplatin.md)-containing chemotherapy (HR 1.1, 95% CI 0.7–1.6, p=0.74).
- DDR alterations were enriched in MSI-H mCRC; median LST scores did not differ between DDR-altered (somatic or germline) and DDR-wildtype tumors. No patient had an LST score >15 (the proposed HRD cutoff).

## Genes & alterations

- [APC](../genes/APC.md) — truncating mutations split at aa 1400 into N-terminal (longer OS/PFS, fewer concurrent mitogenic hits) and C-terminal (shorter OS/PFS, enriched RAS/PI3K co-mutation) groups. Identified as a clinically relevant prognostic marker in mCRC.
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — alteration frequency varied by duration of response and was prognostic in MSS mCRC; enriched in C-terminal APC mutant tumors (13% vs 2%).
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — strongly enriched in C-terminal APC mutant tumors (72% vs 46%); part of co-occurring RAS pathway activation pattern.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — more frequent in N-terminal APC mutant tumors.
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — enriched in C-terminal APC mutant tumors (28% vs 11%).
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — enriched in C-terminal APC mutant tumors (12% vs 3%).
- [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) — highly activating Wnt alteration; co-occurred with RAS pathway alterations in 63% of cases and marked poor-prognosis mCRC.
- [RNF43](../genes/RNF43.md) — Wnt ligand-level alteration; co-occurred with RAS pathway alterations in 71% of cases; poor-prognosis subgroup.
- [AMER1](../genes/AMER1.md) — included as a Wnt pathway gene in the multivariate prognostic model.
- [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) / [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) — referenced as DDR alterations; LST analysis showed no enrichment for homologous recombination deficiency signatures in this cohort.

## Clinical implications

- **Prognosis:** N-terminal-side [APC](../genes/APC.md) truncating mutations (5' of aa 1400) identify a less aggressive subset of MSS mCRC with longer OS and PFS, independent of tumor sidedness. C-terminal APC mutations mark a more aggressive subset enriched for concurrent mitogenic activation.
- **Biomarker:** APC mutation site, not merely APC mutation status, should be considered when stratifying mCRC. CTNNB1 and RNF43 alterations identify additional poor-prognosis subgroups.
- **Therapy:** DDR pathway alterations in mCRC (including germline) do not appear to indicate platinum sensitivity or HRD; the authors argue against pursuing PARP inhibitor strategies in this disease, consistent with prior negative PARP inhibitor trials in mCRC. The paper does not advocate for [olaparib](../drugs/olaparib.md)-class therapy in unselected mCRC.
- **Mechanism:** "Just-right" Wnt activation hypothesis — N-terminal APC mutants retain enough β-catenin regulation that additional mitogenic hits are not required, while distal/C-terminal APC mutants (weaker Wnt activators) experience selective pressure for additional RAS/PI3K activation.

## Limitations & open questions

- Authors acknowledge the **small sample size** of DDR-altered patients and the possibility of **subclonal DDR mutations** that may have been missed.
- Single-institution cohort (MSK), retrospective design; no external validation cohort presented.
- LST-based HRD assessment may miss other markers of homologous recombination deficiency.
- The mechanistic claim that N-terminal APC mutants do not need concurrent mitogenic alterations is biologically inferred from co-mutation frequencies, not functionally validated in this study.
- It remains open whether the aa 1400 cutpoint generalizes to non-MSS or non-MSK cohorts.
