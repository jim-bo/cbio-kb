**PMID:** [28726821](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28726821/) · **DOI:** [10.1038/nature22973](https://doi.org/10.1038/nature22973) · **Journal:** Nature (2017)

## TL;DR

The largest integrated genomic and epigenomic survey of medulloblastoma to date — 491 sequenced tumours plus 1,256 methylation-profiled cases — by the ICGC PedBrain Tumour and MAGIC consortia. Whole-genome sequencing across all four consensus subgroups ([WNT](../genes/CTNNB1.md), [SHH](../genes/PTCH1.md), Group 3, Group 4) confidently assigns driver events to 76% of Group 3 and 82% of Group 4 cases, more than doubling the previously explained fraction. The study nominates new subgroup-specific drivers including hotspot in-frame insertions in [KBTBD4](../genes/KBTBD4.md) and an "enhancer-hijacking" mechanism in which Group 4-restricted [SNCAIP](../genes/SNCAIP.md) tandem duplications activate the neighbouring [PRDM6](../genes/PRDM6.md) gene. DNA methylation t-SNE on Group 3 and Group 4 (n=740) refines these subgroups into eight subtypes, each enriched for distinct driver alterations.

## Cohort & data

- 579 untreated medulloblastoma patients sequenced across four institutions; after QC and de-duplication, **491 diagnostic tumour-normal pairs** were used for somatic analysis.
- Sequencing breakdown: whole genomes n=390 (190 previously published in [PMID:22832583](../papers/22832583.md), [PMID:22832587](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22832587/), [PMID:22722829](../papers/22722829.md) plus 200 unpublished); whole exomes n=101 (from prior PCGP/MAGIC studies).
- **1,256 cases** profiled on Illumina 450k methylation arrays (including 396/491, 80.7%, of the NGS cohort) — see [450k-methylation-array](../methods/450k-methylation-array.md).
- Transcriptomes: RNA-seq n=164 and Affymetrix expression arrays n=392.
- ChIP-seq for H3K27ac and [CTCF](../genes/CTCF.md) on a subset (n=28) — see [chip-seq](../methods/chip-seq.md).
- Patient ages: 1 month to 50 years (median 8 years).
- Cancer type: [MBL](../cancer_types/MBL.md). Dataset: [mbl_dkfz_2017](../datasets/mbl_dkfz_2017.md).
- Methods: [whole-genome-seq](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md), [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md), [rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md), [450k-methylation-array](../methods/450k-methylation-array.md), [chip-seq](../methods/chip-seq.md), [mutational-signatures](../methods/mutational-signatures.md), [tsne](../methods/tsne.md), driver detection by [mutsig](../methods/mutsig.md), [genome-music](../methods/genome-music.md) and IntOGen.

## Key findings

- Driver events were confidently assigned to **76% of Group 3 (n=131)** and **82% of Group 4 (n=193)** patients in this study, compared to ~30% in previous NGS series of n=173 — a more-than-doubling of explanatory power per subgroup.
- All 36 sequenced WNT tumours were explained by at least one driver: 86% had somatic [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) hotspot mutations; three CTNNB1-wild-type WNT tumours carried pathogenic germline [APC](../genes/APC.md) variants (Turcot syndrome). Monosomy 6 was present in 83% of WNT cases — confirming that ~10–15% of bona fide WNT MBs lack both routinely-used clinical features.
- New WNT-associated alterations: [CSNK2B](../genes/CSNK2B.md) (14%), [EPHA7](../genes/EPHA7.md) (8%), and SWI/SNF subunits [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) / [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) / [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md) collectively in 33% of WNT tumours.
- [SHH](../genes/SHH.md) MB: ≥1 driver assigned to >95% of patients. Six [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) R132C mutations identified (5 SHH, 1 WNT); IDH1-mutant SHH tumours were CIMP+. HAT-complex genes ([CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md), [KAT6B](../genes/KAT6B.md), [EP300](../genes/EP300.md)) and HAT-regulators ([BRPF1](../genes/BRPF1.md), [KANSL1](../genes/KANSL1.md)) were recurrently mutated (19% of SHH; q=2.2×10⁻³ for HAT-complex enrichment).
- 24 mutational signatures detected with ≥5% contribution. Signature 3 (BRCA1/2-associated) was unexpectedly seen in most Group 3 and Group 4 patients; signature 18 enriched in Group 3 (P=4.7×10⁻⁵) and signature 5 in Group 4 (P=1.0×10⁻¹¹, age-correlated).
- Two hypermutator outliers explained by mutator-gene hits: ICGC_MB62 (>25,000 mutations) carried a somatic [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) mutation and signature-10 substitutions; ICGC_MB265 carried a somatic [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md) mutation with signature 6 (mismatch-repair deficiency).
- Hotspot in-frame insertions in [KBTBD4](../genes/KBTBD4.md): 18/20 (90%) of somatic [KBTBD4](../genes/KBTBD4.md) variants were in-frame indels clustered in a 6-amino-acid stretch (residues 308–313) of the Kelch domain. Subgroup-specific predominant insertions: Group 3 R313delinsPRR; Group 4 P311delinsPP. KBTBD4 mutants were tightly enriched in methylation subtypes II (21%) and VII (14%) — the **most prevalent driver in those subtypes**.
- "Enhancer-hijacking" activates [PRDM6](../genes/PRDM6.md): Group 4-restricted [SNCAIP](../genes/SNCAIP.md) tandem duplications (~600 kb upstream of [PRDM6](../genes/PRDM6.md)) reposition the [SNCAIP](../genes/SNCAIP.md) super-enhancer to drive PRDM6 (P=6.07×10⁻²⁴ for PRDM6 expression in SV+ vs SV-; ≥20-fold upregulation). PRDM6 is now the **most prevalent driver alteration in Group 4** (17% of n=193).
- t-SNE on the 12,454 most variable methylation probes (s.d.>0.25) across 740 Group 3/4 cases resolves **eight molecular subtypes** (I–VIII), each with distinctive driver-event enrichment: [GFI1B](../genes/GFI1B.md) in subtype I, [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) in subtype II, KBTBD4 in II and VII, [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md) and [ZMYM3](../genes/ZMYM3.md) in subtype VIII. Iterative down-sampling showed subtype structure stabilises at ≥500 samples.
- Group 3 (n=131) recurrent drivers (% of subgroup): [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) amplification 17%, [GFI1B](../genes/GFI1B.md) SVs 11%, [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) 9%, [CTDNEP1](../genes/CTDNEP1.md) / [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) / [KBTBD4](../genes/KBTBD4.md) 5%, [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md) 5%, [GFI1](../genes/GFI1.md) 4%, [OTX2](../genes/OTX2.md) 3%, [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) germline 2%.
- Group 4 (n=193) recurrent drivers: PRDM6 17%, GFI1B 9%, [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md) 7%, [ZMYM3](../genes/ZMYM3.md) 6%, [OTX2](../genes/OTX2.md) 6%, [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) 6%, KBTBD4 6%, [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md) 6%, [ZIC1](../genes/ZIC1.md) 4%, [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md) 4%, [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) 3%, [FLG](../genes/FLG.md) 3%, [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) 3%, [MED12](../genes/MED12.md) 2%, [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) 2%.
- Pathway analysis: significant overrepresentation of Notch and TGFβ signalling alterations in Group 3 (first report of Notch pathway mutations in MB) and chromatin-modifier alterations in Group 4.
- Recurrent fusion transcripts integrating SV breakpoints with RNA-seq targeted [GLI2](../genes/GLI2.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) and [PVT1](../genes/PVT1.md).
- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter hotspots were the only confidently called noncoding driver region across the cohort.

## Genes & alterations

- [KBTBD4](../genes/KBTBD4.md): Recurrent in-frame insertions in the Kelch domain (residues 308–313); 18/20 KBTBD4 variants were in-frame indels. Predominant Group 3 insertion R313delinsPRR; predominant Group 4 insertion P311delinsPP. Highly enriched in methylation subtypes II (21%) and VII (14%); homology modelling places insertions on the substrate-binding interface, suggesting altered substrate recognition rather than loss of fold.
- [PRDM6](../genes/PRDM6.md): SET-domain protein activated by [SNCAIP](../genes/SNCAIP.md)-locus tandem duplications in 17% of Group 4 (most prevalent Group 4 driver). PRDM6 expression upregulated ≥20-fold in SV+ vs SV- cases (P=6.07×10⁻²⁴).
- [SNCAIP](../genes/SNCAIP.md): Group 4-restricted tandem duplications; locus overlaps a Group 4-specific super-enhancer that the duplications reposition into the neighbouring TAD containing PRDM6. SV breakpoints cluster near CTCF sites at the TAD boundary separating SNCAIP and PRDM6.
- [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md): Hotspot mutations in 86% of WNT MBs; sometimes subclonal alongside subclonal [DDX3X](../genes/DDX3X.md) variants.
- [APC](../genes/APC.md): Pathogenic germline variants in 3 CTNNB1-wild-type WNT MBs — Turcot syndrome predisposition.
- [PTCH1](../genes/PTCH1.md), [SUFU](../genes/SUFU.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md): Germline variants predominantly restricted to SHH MB.
- [SMO](../genes/SMO.md): Mutated in subset of SHH MBs.
- [CSNK2B](../genes/CSNK2B.md): Clinically actionable, mutated in 14% of WNT MBs.
- [EPHA7](../genes/EPHA7.md): WNT-associated, 8%.
- [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md): SWI/SNF subunits — 33% of WNT MBs collectively; rationale for PRC2 inhibitors in trial NCT02601937.
- [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md), [KAT6B](../genes/KAT6B.md), [EP300](../genes/EP300.md), [BRPF1](../genes/BRPF1.md), [KANSL1](../genes/KANSL1.md): Recurrent mostly-SHH HAT-complex alterations (19% of SHH).
- [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md): R132C in 6 cases (5 SHH, 1 WNT); IDH1-mutant SHH tumours are CIMP+, mirroring G-CIMP in glioma.
- [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md), [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md): Recurrent histone-methylation-modifier mutations across subgroups; KDM6A and [ZMYM3](../genes/ZMYM3.md) especially enriched in methylation subtype VIII.
- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md): Amplifications restricted to Group 3 (17%).
- [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md): Amplifications in Group 3 (5%) and Group 4 (6%) at comparable frequencies.
- [GFI1](../genes/GFI1.md), [GFI1B](../genes/GFI1B.md): SV-driven aberrant induction (mutually exclusive); distributed in both Group 3 and Group 4. GFI1B was a top CESAM-confirmed enhancer-hijacking target.
- [OTX2](../genes/OTX2.md): Recurrent in Group 3 (3%) and Group 4 (6%).
- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md): Promoter hotspots — only confident noncoding driver region across the cohort.
- [POLE](../genes/POLE.md), [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md): Somatic mutations explaining the two cohort hypermutators (ICGC_MB62 and ICGC_MB265 respectively).
- [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md), [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md), [TCF4](../genes/TCF4.md), [ZIC1](../genes/ZIC1.md), [GSE1](../genes/GSE1.md), [CTDNEP1](../genes/CTDNEP1.md), [CHD7](../genes/CHD7.md), [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md), [MED12](../genes/MED12.md), [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), [FLG](../genes/FLG.md): Additional recurrently altered candidate drivers stratified across subgroups in Figs 2–3 oncoprints.
- Fusion transcripts: recurrent rearrangements targeting [GLI2](../genes/GLI2.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) and [PVT1](../genes/PVT1.md).

## Clinical implications

- The reliance on [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) mutation or chromosome-6 monosomy to clinically assign WNT MB will miss ~10–15% of bona fide WNT cases — [APC](../genes/APC.md) germline testing is warranted when WNT MB is suspected without CTNNB1 mutation (Turcot syndrome).
- SWI/SNF mutations in WNT MB (33% of WNT) provide rationale for PRC2 inhibitors already being evaluated in paediatric SWI/SNF-mutant trials (e.g. NCT02601937).
- HAT-complex alterations across 19% of SHH MBs suggest HAT-pathway-targeted epigenetic therapy as an avenue meriting further study.
- IDH1-mutant SHH MBs (CIMP+) phenocopy IDH-mutant glioma, opening the door to repurposing [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) inhibitors in this small subset.
- KBTBD4 hotspot insertions and PRDM6 enhancer-hijacking are nominated as new actionable targets in Group 3/4 MB, but require functional validation before clinical translation.
- Eight methylation-defined Group 3/4 subtypes provide a substrate for subtype-tailored risk-stratification and trial design.
- The single MLH1-mutated mismatch-repair-deficient case (signature 6) is the kind of MB patient who could be considered for immune-checkpoint blockade; the [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) hypermutator likewise.

## Limitations & open questions

- Functional validation is missing for the central new candidates: KBTBD4 hotspot insertions and PRDM6 enhancer-hijacking are nominated by integrative genomics, but their oncogenic mechanisms (KBTBD4 substrate altered? PRDM6 methyltransferase activity essential?) and "actionability" remain to be experimentally established.
- Sample size, although the largest to date, is still underpowered for the long tail of <5% recurrently mutated genes that the authors call "biologically crucial" but cannot yet statistically distinguish from passengers.
- The eight methylation subtypes within Group 3/4 are defined by the cohort; subtype boundaries and the precise developmental cell-of-origin still require subtype-relevant model systems.
- Whether SWI/SNF mutations confer the same PRC2-inhibitor sensitivity in WNT MB as in other paediatric SWI/SNF-mutant tumours is untested.
- The authors observe that signature 3 (BRCA1/2-associated) is unexpectedly common in Group 3 and Group 4, but the underlying somatic or germline lesion(s) responsible are not resolved.
- Notch and TGFβ signalling enrichment in Group 3 are reported as the first formal genomic evidence — functional studies needed to determine whether they are druggable in MB.
- Cohort skews paediatric (median age 8 years); generalisability to the rare adult MB population is limited despite an age range up to 50 years.
