# FABP1

## Overview

FABP1 (Fatty Acid Binding Protein 1), also known as liver FABP (L-FABP), is an intracellular lipid chaperone primarily expressed in hepatocytes. In hepatocellular carcinoma ([HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)), FABP1 plays a role in lipid uptake and transport within the tumor microenvironment, where it enables HCC cells to compete with and outcompete immune cells for available fatty acids, contributing to T-cell dysfunction and immune evasion.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- FABP1 in HCC allows tumor cells to outcompete T cells for linoleic acid in the tumor microenvironment; LINC01116/FABP1 inhibition combined with anti-PD-1 therapy markedly reduced HCC progression in preclinical models. [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md): FABP1 contributes to immune evasion by enabling HCC cells to monopolize linoleic acid in the TME, suppressing T-cell activity; targeting via LINC01116/FABP1 axis enhances anti-PD-1 efficacy. [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- FABP1-driven lipid sequestration in the TME cooperates with other lipid-metabolism reprogramming nodes ([FASN](../genes/FASN.md), CD36, [FABP5](../genes/FABP5.md)) to collectively drive HCC immune evasion and TKI resistance. [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- LINC01116 (a long non-coding RNA) inhibition combined with anti-PD-1 checkpoint blockade: markedly reduces HCC progression in preclinical models by disrupting the FABP1-mediated linoleic acid sequestration mechanism. [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Open questions

- Whether FABP1 expression level or activity can serve as a predictive biomarker for ICI response in HCC has not been prospectively evaluated.
- The generalizability beyond HCC to other FABP1-expressing cancer types requires investigation.
