# TRAF3

## Overview

TRAF3 is a negative regulator of NF-κB signaling disrupted in U-CLL via a recurrent chr14 deletion.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- TRAF3 is disrupted (together with [ZFP36L1](../genes/ZFP36L1.md) and [DICER1](../genes/DICER1.md)) by a recurrent 37-Mb chr14 deletion in U-CLL (4/87 WGS, 4.6%) arising via class-switch recombination [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [CLLSLL](../cancer_types/CLLSLL.md) — disrupted by the recurrent U-CLL chr14 structural deletion [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-disrupted with [ZFP36L1](../genes/ZFP36L1.md) and [DICER1](../genes/DICER1.md) on the same chr14 deletion [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct targeted therapy reported in the corpus.

## Open questions

- Functional contribution of TRAF3 loss relative to ZFP36L1/DICER1 co-disruption on the same event is unresolved [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
