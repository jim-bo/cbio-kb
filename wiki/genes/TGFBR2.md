# TGFBR2

## Overview

TGFBR2 (Transforming Growth Factor Beta Receptor Type 2) encodes the type II receptor of the TGF-beta signaling pathway, a serine/threonine kinase that binds TGF-beta ligands and transduces growth-inhibitory and pro-apoptotic signals via SMAD2/SMAD3 phosphorylation. In normal epithelial cells, TGF-beta/TGFBR2 signaling functions as a canonical tumor suppressor pathway; loss-of-function alterations in TGFBR2 abrogate this restraint. TGFBR2 is recurrently mutated across gastrointestinal cancers — with particularly high frequency in microsatellite-unstable colorectal cancer due to a poly-A frameshift hotspot — and is also observed as a secondary driver alteration in pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDAC). Co-occurring loss of downstream effector [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) provides an independent route to TGF-beta pathway inactivation in PDAC, underscoring the selective pressure to silence this axis during pancreatic cancer progression.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Missense and truncating mutations in PDAC: TGFBR2 was mutated in 8.7% of early-stage (stage I) versus 2.4% of late-stage (stage II–III) PDAC in a consecutive resected cohort (n=397 sequenced); the enrichment in early-stage disease was nominally significant (p=0.008) but did not survive multiple-testing correction. [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **PAAD** — In resected PDAC, TGFBR2 mutation frequency was 8.7% in stage I versus 2.4% in stage II–III patients (p=0.008, not significant after multiple-testing correction); the early-stage enrichment pattern is shared with [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) mutations (3.9% vs 0.7%, p=0.042) and may reflect a distinct path of tumor suppressor co-mutation in KRAS-driven PDAC. [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) — Both TGFBR2 and SMAD4 are components of the canonical TGF-beta signaling axis. SMAD4 is mutated in 17% of sequenced PDAC in the same cohort; the two alterations represent parallel modes of TGF-beta pathway inactivation. [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md)
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — Co-mutation context in PDAC: TP53 is mutated in 71% of sequenced tumors and was assessed as a tumor suppressor co-mutation together with SMAD4 and CDKN2A for prognostic modeling; TGFBR2 was evaluated independently as an early-stage enriched alteration. [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md)
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — TGFBR2 mutation frequency was not significantly different across KRAS allele subgroups (G12D, G12V, G12R) in the MSK PDAC cohort. [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

No direct TGFBR2-targeted therapies are reported in the current corpus. The broader TGF-beta pathway is a therapeutic target of interest in PDAC given its role in immune exclusion and fibrotic stroma remodeling; agents targeting this axis are in clinical investigation but are not directly linked to TGFBR2 mutation status in the papers processed here.

## Open questions

- The nominal enrichment of TGFBR2 mutations in early-stage PDAC (stage I) relative to late-stage (stage II–III) did not survive multiple-testing correction (n=397 sequenced); whether this reflects a true biological association with locally-confined disease or sampling artifact in a small subgroup remains unresolved. [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md)
- The functional consequences of specific TGFBR2 mutation classes (missense vs truncating) in PDAC — and whether they differ from the frameshift hotspot mutations common in MSI colorectal cancer — are not addressed in the current corpus.
