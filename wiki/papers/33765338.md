**PMID:** 33765338 · **DOI:** 10.1002/hep.31829 · **Journal:** Hepatology (2021)

## TL;DR

In a bi-institutional cohort of 412 patients with intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma (iCCA) profiled by MSK-IMPACT targeted sequencing at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center and Erasmus MC, Boerner et al. show that oncogenic alterations in *[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)*, *[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)*, and *[CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)* are independent predictors of shorter overall survival, even after adjusting for known clinicopathologic risk factors (multifocal liver disease, lymph node involvement, large bile duct type, periductal infiltration). The most actionable alterations — *IDH1/2* mutations and *[FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md)* fusions — were mutually exclusive but neither was prognostic. Patients combining clinical high-risk features with *CDKN2A* deletion had universally poor survival irrespective of resection, suggesting they may not benefit from surgery. Data are deposited as the [ihch_msk_2021](../datasets/ihch_msk_2021.md) cBioPortal study.

## Cohort & data

- **N = 412** patients with histologically confirmed iCCA: 390 from MSKCC (1993–2018 resected; 2008–2019 unresected) and 22 from Erasmus MC (2005–2015 resected); 209 resected, 203 unresected.
- Cancer type: [intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma (IHCH)](../cancer_types/IHCH.md).
- Dataset: [ihch_msk_2021](../datasets/ihch_msk_2021.md) on cBioPortal.
- Assay: [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) targeted hybridization-capture NGS — 40 samples on the [341-gene panel](../methods/IMPACT341.md), 147 on the [410-gene panel](../methods/IMPACT410.md), 225 on the [468-gene panel](../methods/IMPACT468.md); all sequenced against matched normal liver or blood with >60% tumor content.
- Oncogenicity annotation via [OncoKB](../methods/oncokb-annotation.md); 12 canonical TCGA PanCancer Atlas signaling pathways evaluated.
- Statistics: [Cox proportional-hazards regression](../methods/cox-proportional-hazards.md) (univariate and multivariate); [Kaplan-Meier estimates](../methods/kaplan-meier.md); Fisher's exact tests; FDR (Q-value) multiple-testing correction; analyses in R 4.0.0.
- Treatments: resection ± adjuvant chemotherapy (predominantly [gemcitabine](../drugs/gemcitabine.md) ± platinum or [capecitabine](../drugs/capecitabine.md)); palliative chemotherapy in 197/203 unresected ([gemcitabine](../drugs/gemcitabine.md)+platinum in 145/197, 74%); hepatic arterial infusion chemotherapy (HAIC) with [floxuridine](../drugs/floxuridine.md) ± concurrent systemic chemotherapy ([gemcitabine](../drugs/gemcitabine.md) + [oxaliplatin](../drugs/oxaliplatin.md) or [cisplatin](../drugs/cisplatin.md)) in 54/203 unresected.

## Key findings

- 1,551 genetic alterations across 412 tumors. Most frequent oncogenic events: *[IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md)* (20%), *[ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md)* (20%), *TP53* (17%), *CDKN2A* (15%, with deep deletions in 13%), *[BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md)* (15%), *FGFR2* (15%), *[PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md)* (12%), *KRAS* (10%), *[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)* (5%), *[NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md)* (3%), *[RASA1](../genes/RASA1.md)* (3%), *[NF1](../genes/NF1.md)* (1.7%).
- 92 oncogenic fusions in 78/412 (19%) tumors; *FGFR2* fusions in 47/412 (11%), 38 of which were known in-frame fusions.
- Pathway-level alteration rates: epigenetic regulators 60% (246/412), RTK/RAS 48% (196/412), TP53 24% (97/412), cell cycle 21% (85/412).
- Median tumor mutation burden (TMB) = 2.6 mut/Mb (IQR 1.8–3.9); only 2 patients had TMB > 40 (MSI-consistent).
- **Mutual exclusivity**: *IDH1/2* mutations were significantly mutually exclusive with *FGFR2* fusions (τ = −0.19, Q = 0.002), *CDKN2A* deletions (τ = −0.17, Q = 0.008), *TP53* mutations (τ = −0.15, Q = 0.02), and *[TERT](../genes/TERT.md)* mutations (τ = −0.13, Q = 0.04). *IDH1/2* alterations were also mutually exclusive with RTK/RAS (τ = −0.24, Q < 0.001), cell cycle, TP53, and TGF-β pathway alterations.
- **Co-occurrence**: *TERT* and *TP53* (τ = 0.19, Q = 0.002); PI3K (τ = 0.21, Q = 0.001), TGF-β, cell cycle, and RTK/RAS pathways with *TP53* mutations.
- **Whole cohort survival** (univariable): *TP53* mut (P < 0.0001), *KRAS* mut (P = 0.0001), and *CDKN2A* deletion (P < 0.0001) were each associated with shorter [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md). *TERT* mut was also significant on univariate analysis but was omitted from multivariate models due to strong correlation with *TP53* mut. Only deep (homozygous) *CDKN2A* deletions (n = 52) predicted shorter OS; monoallelic *CDKN2A* alterations (n = 9) had little impact.
- *IDH1/2* mutations and *FGFR2* fusions were **not** prognostic in this cohort. *IDH1/2* mutations showed only a trend toward better OS (P = 0.08).
- **Resected cohort (n = 209)**: median RFS 18.4 months (95% CI 13.4–24.2), median OS 46.4 months (95% CI 41.2–61.3); 5-year RFS 22%, 5-year OS 42%. Median OS by genotype: *TP53*mut 25.8 vs *TP53*wt 59.9 months; *KRAS*mut 27.6 vs *KRAS*wt 52.3 months; *CDKN2A*del 14.8 vs *CDKN2A*wt 52.6 months.
- **Multivariate model in resected patients** (adjusted for nodal disease, multifocal liver disease, lymphovascular invasion): *TP53*mut (HR 1.82, 95% CI 1.08–3.06, P = 0.03) and *CDKN2A*del (HR 3.40, 95% CI 1.95–5.94, P < 0.001) independently predicted shorter OS; *KRAS*mut trended (HR 1.69, 95% CI 0.97–2.93, P = 0.06). *CDKN2A*del had the strongest effect overall (HR 2.5, 95% CI 1.7–3.8).
- **Unresectable cohort (n = 203)**: median OS 24.1 months (locally advanced) vs 13.1 months (distant metastases). *TP53*mut, *KRAS*mut, and *CDKN2A*del independently predicted worse OS on multivariate analysis. Locally advanced disease genetic-low-risk vs genetic-high-risk: 26.1 vs 16.8 months; metastatic disease 15.7 vs 9.7 months.
- **Combined clinical + genetic risk stratification**: patients with both high-risk clinical and genetic factors had median OS 18.3 months vs 74.2 months for those with neither (P < 0.001); intermediate groups (high-risk genetic alone 38.6 months; high-risk clinical alone 37.0 months) had similar intermediate outcomes.
- **Clinical-pathology correlates**: *TP53*mut associated with LVI (76% vs 47% wt, Q = 0.02), nodal disease (43% vs 15%, Q < 0.001), large bile-duct type (33% vs 7%, Q = 0.02); *KRAS*mut with perineural invasion (65% vs 27%, Q = 0.04) and large BD type (37% vs 9%, Q = 0.02); *CDKN2A*del with multifocal disease (55% vs 25%, Q = 0.04). *IDH1/2*mut associated with lower CA19-9 (44% vs 67% elevated, P = 0.002).
- **Etiology associations**: in 29 cirrhotic patients, 6/29 (21%) had *TERT* promoter alterations vs 14/374 (4%) cirrhosis-free (Q = 0.008). In 6 patients with primary sclerosing cholangitis (PSC), 5/6 (83%) had *TP53*mut (Q = 0.007).
- **Treatment-outcome interaction with *CDKN2A***: clinical high-risk *CDKN2A*del patients submitted to resection (n = 14) had median OS 12.2 months vs 16.8 months for locally advanced unresected *CDKN2A*del (n = 12) — suggesting no benefit from resection in this subgroup. HAIC in locally advanced disease was an independent predictor of improved survival (HR 0.45, 95% CI 0.27–0.77, P = 0.003).

## Genes & alterations

- [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) — mutations in 20% of tumors; mutually exclusive with *FGFR2* fusions and *CDKN2A* deletions; **not** prognostic for OS (trend toward improvement, P = 0.08); associated with lower CA19-9 levels.
- [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) — analyzed jointly with *IDH1* (IDH1/2mut); not prognostic when analyzed separately.
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) — inactivating mutations in 20% of tumors; high frequency in iCCA consistent with previous reports.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — mutations in 17%; high-risk genetic alteration. Independent predictor of shorter OS in resected (HR 1.82) and unresected disease; associates with LVI, nodal disease, large BD type, and PSC. Co-occurs with *TERT* and PI3K/TGF-β/cell cycle/RTK-RAS pathway alterations.
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — focal alterations in 15%; homozygous deletions in 13%. **Strongest** independent prognostic factor (HR 3.40 in resected cohort multivariate; HR 2.5 in whole cohort). Only deep (homozygous) deletions are prognostic; monoallelic alterations are not. Associated with multifocal disease. Defines a subgroup with no benefit from resection in clinical high-risk patients.
- [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md) — mutations in 15%.
- [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) — alterations in 15%; 47/412 (11%) bore *FGFR2* fusions (38 known in-frame). Mutually exclusive with *IDH1/2* mutations but **not** prognostic.
- [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md) — mutations in 12%.
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — activating mutations in 10%. High-risk genetic alteration. Independent predictor of shorter OS in unresected disease; trend (P = 0.06) in resected. Associated with perineural invasion and large BD type.
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — activating mutations in 5%.
- [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) — activating mutations in 3%.
- [RASA1](../genes/RASA1.md) — alterations in 3%.
- [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) — alterations in 1.7%.
- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) — promoter alterations enriched in cirrhotic patients (21% vs 4%); co-occurs with *TP53*; univariate OS association, but dropped from multivariate models due to *TP53* collinearity.
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) — rare amplifications (n = 7).
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) — rare amplifications (n = 6).
- [MET](../genes/MET.md) — rare amplifications (n = 3).

## Clinical implications

- **Prognostic biomarker panel**: routine pretreatment targeted sequencing should report status of [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) (homozygous deletion) — together they stratify OS independently of stage and treatment.
- **Surgical decision-making**: patients with both clinical high-risk features (multifocal disease, nodal involvement) **and** *CDKN2A* deep deletion had universally poor outcomes whether or not they underwent resection (median OS 12.2 months resected vs 16.8 months locally advanced unresected). The authors suggest these patients may be better served by upfront systemic therapy ± HAIC than resection.
- **Risk stratification refinement**: among clinical low-risk patients (solitary, node-negative), those with high-risk genetic alterations had significantly reduced RFS/OS; conversely, clinical high-risk patients without high-risk genetic alterations had outcomes better than expected.
- **HAIC benefit**: in locally advanced unresectable iCCA on chemotherapy, hepatic arterial infusion of [floxuridine](../drugs/floxuridine.md) was independently associated with improved OS (HR 0.45, P = 0.003), independent of genomic profile.
- **Caveats on targetable alterations**: while [IDH1/2](../genes/IDH1.md) mutations and [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) fusions remain predictive for targeted therapies (e.g., [ivosidenib](../drugs/ivosidenib.md), [pemigatinib](../drugs/pemigatinib.md) — referenced but not administered in this cohort beyond a few MEK inhibitors), neither was prognostic for untreated natural history.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Retrospective, tertiary-center bias**: two large referral centers (MSKCC, Erasmus); findings may not generalize to community settings or non-Western populations with liver-fluke- or HBV-associated iCCA (e.g., Asian cohorts).
- **Cohort overlap**: data from 109 patients (31 resected, 78 unresected) were previously reported (Lowery et al. 2018, *Clin Cancer Res* — not in this corpus).
- **Treatment heterogeneity**: chemotherapy regimens and locoregional treatments varied and were not controlled for in primary statistical models.
- **Staging asymmetry**: resected tumors AJCC-staged pathologically; unresected staged clinically/by imaging — direct between-group statistical comparisons were avoided.
- **VUS interpretation**: relied on OncoKB annotations; many variants of unknown significance are poorly characterized and were excluded.
- **Scope limited to DNA alterations**: epigenetic, transcriptional, and tumor-microenvironment contributions were not assessed.
- **Validation needed**: independent prospective validation of the *TP53*/*KRAS*/*CDKN2A* prognostic panel — particularly the recommendation against resection in clinical high-risk + *CDKN2A*del — is required before clinical adoption.
- **Open question**: is the lack of prognostic signal for *IDH1/2* mutations a true biological null or a consequence of cohort composition (mix of resected/unresected)? Authors note literature heterogeneity may track with surgical proportion.
