# SLC16A3

## Overview

SLC16A3 (also known as MCT4, Monocarboxylate Transporter 4) encodes an alternate proton-linked monocarboxylate transporter that primarily exports lactate from highly glycolytic cells. In prostate cancer, MCT4 is a candidate monocarboxylate transporter alongside MCT1 ([SLC16A1](../genes/SLC16A1.md)), though MCT1 appears to be the dominant driver of hyperpolarized pyruvate-to-lactate conversion in clinical imaging studies.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Discussed as an alternate monocarboxylate transporter candidate in prostate adenocarcinoma, but not the primary driver of HP lactate signal in a 12-patient hyperpolarized MRI cohort; MCT1 (SLC16A1) dominated by IHC [PMID:31564440](../papers/31564440.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md)**: SLC16A3/MCT4 is a candidate transporter for lactate export in prostate adenocarcinoma, but IHC data from a clinical HP-MRI study showed MCT1 (SLC16A1) as the dominant transporter in this cohort (n=12 patients) [PMID:31564440](../papers/31564440.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Expression discussed in the context of SLC16A1 (MCT1) dominance; their relative contributions to HP lactate signal in prostate cancer remain an open question [PMID:31564440](../papers/31564440.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Potential target for metabolic therapy in prostate cancer, though MCT1 inhibitors are more directly supported by the current imaging-biomarker evidence [PMID:31564440](../papers/31564440.md).

## Open questions

- Whether MCT4 (SLC16A3) plays a greater role in specific molecular subtypes of prostate cancer (e.g., TMPRSS2-ERG fusion-positive) requires larger cohort validation [PMID:31564440](../papers/31564440.md).
