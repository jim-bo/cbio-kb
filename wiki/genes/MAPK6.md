# MAPK6

## Overview

MAPK6 (Mitogen-Activated Protein Kinase 6, also known as ERK3) is an atypical MAP kinase that lacks the canonical dual-phosphorylation activation motif. It is involved in cell differentiation, migration, and cytoskeletal organization. In PDAC proteogenomics, MAPK6 is a substrate of [PAK2](../genes/PAK2.md) kinase, placing it in the KRAS-RAC1-PAK signaling network.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- MAPK6 phosphorylation at S189 is a substrate of PAK2 in a kinase-substrate signaling axis identified across 41 PDAC tumor/NAT pairs in the CPTAC proteogenomic cohort (n=140 treatment-naive tumors); PAK2 is elevated in ~90% of tumors. This positions MAPK6 as a downstream effector of the [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)→[RAC1](../genes/RAC1.md)→PAK2 signaling cascade. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md):** Phosphorylated at S189 by PAK2 in PDAC, as part of the KRAS-driven PAK signaling network. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- MAPK6 S189 phosphorylation co-occurs with elevated PAK2, [PAK1](../genes/PAK1.md), [MET](../genes/MET.md), and RAC1 protein levels downstream of KRAS in PDAC. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Combined PAK1/PAK2 inhibition (which would suppress MAPK6 S189 phosphorylation) with MAPK-ERK and PI3K-AKT-mTOR inhibitors is proposed as a therapeutic strategy for KRAS-driven PDAC. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Open questions

- The downstream consequences of MAPK6 S189 phosphorylation in PDAC (e.g., effects on cell migration, invasion, or survival) are not characterized in the corpus.
- Whether MAPK6 is a driver or passenger in PAK2 signaling in PDAC remains to be determined.
