# RHOA

## Overview

RHOA is a small GTPase. In peripheral T-cell lymphoma, hotspot RHOA G17V mutations are a hallmark of angioimmunoblastic T-cell lymphoma ([AITL](../cancer_types/AITL.md)).

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Mutated in 30% (40/132) of adults with nodal [PTCL](../cancer_types/PTCL.md) treated with curative-intent CHOP-based chemotherapy at MSK [PMID:37078708](../papers/37078708.md).
- In [AITL](../cancer_types/AITL.md), 88% of RHOA mutations were the canonical G17V variant [PMID:37078708](../papers/37078708.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- Peripheral T-cell lymphoma ([PTCL](../cancer_types/PTCL.md), [AITL](../cancer_types/AITL.md)) — 30% overall, G17V hotspot in [AITL](../cancer_types/AITL.md) [PMID:37078708](../papers/37078708.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Commonly co-mutates with [TET2](../genes/TET2.md) and [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md) in [PTCL](../cancer_types/PTCL.md) [PMID:37078708](../papers/37078708.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No independent prognostic effect on PFS/OS reported; no direct targeted therapy association in the corpus [PMID:37078708](../papers/37078708.md).

## Open questions

- None specific to RHOA in this cohort.
