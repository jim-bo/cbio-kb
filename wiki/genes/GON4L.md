# GON4L

## Overview

GON4L (Gon-4-Like) encodes a transcriptional regulator involved in cell-cycle control and chromatin organization. It has been identified as an upstream fusion partner in [NTRK1](../genes/NTRK1.md) gene rearrangements, with clinical significance arising from the potential for co-occurring oncogenic drivers to abrogate TRK inhibitor sensitivity.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- GON4L-NTRK1 fusion co-occurred with [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) Q61R in one melanoma; patient had progressive disease on [larotrectinib](../drugs/larotrectinib.md); cell line insensitive to larotrectinib/LOXO-195 but sensitive to [trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md); pan-TRK IHC negative, suggesting the fusion transcript is not translated [PMID:31871300](../papers/31871300.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Melanoma: GON4L-NTRK1 observed as a rare rearrangement co-occurring with canonical MAPK driver (NRAS Q61R); represents a case where the TRK fusion may not be the dominant oncogenic driver [PMID:31871300](../papers/31871300.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with NRAS Q61R in at least one melanoma case; this concurrent canonical MAPK-pathway alteration was associated with lack of TRK inhibitor dependence [PMID:31871300](../papers/31871300.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- When GON4L-NTRK1 co-occurs with NRAS Q61R, the tumor shows resistance to larotrectinib and LOXO-195 but sensitivity to trametinib, suggesting MEK inhibition is the appropriate targeted strategy rather than TRK inhibition [PMID:31871300](../papers/31871300.md)

## Open questions

- Whether GON4L-NTRK1 fusions without concurrent MAPK co-drivers would respond to TRK inhibition is unknown; the single reported case had absent pan-TRK IHC, suggesting this specific fusion may not produce a functional TRK oncoprotein [PMID:31871300](../papers/31871300.md)
