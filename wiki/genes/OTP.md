# OTP

## Overview

OTP (orthopedia homeobox) encodes a transcription factor that serves as a carcinoid-specific marker. In neuroendocrine tumor biology, OTP expression distinguishes pulmonary carcinoids from other lung neuroendocrine tumors. Its presence in a subset of atypical [SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md) (aSCLC, RB1-proficient/TP53-proficient) provides molecular evidence for a histogenetic link between aSCLC and pulmonary carcinoids, supporting the hypothesis that aSCLC may arise from or share a common lineage with carcinoids.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- OTP is a carcinoid-specific marker expressed by IHC and RNA-seq in a subset of aSCLC (RB1-proficient, TP53-proficient SCLC; n=20, MSK-IMPACT/WGS), supporting a histogenetic link between aSCLC and pulmonary carcinoids [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **SCLC (aSCLC subtype)**: OTP expression detected in a subset of aSCLC by IHC and RNA-seq; its presence links aSCLC histogenetically to pulmonary carcinoids [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- OTP carcinoid-marker expression co-occurs with [MEN1](../genes/MEN1.md) deletion and [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) deletion in aSCLC, consistent with the carcinoid-like genomic background of this tumor type [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- OTP expression has not yet been directly exploited therapeutically; its carcinoid-marker status in aSCLC may inform treatment decisions given the known activity of somatostatin analogues and [temozolomide](../drugs/temozolomide.md) in carcinoids [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md)

## Open questions

- Whether OTP-positive aSCLC represents a distinct biological subset with differential treatment response (e.g., to somatostatin analogues) compared to OTP-negative aSCLC remains unresolved.
