# RPA1

## Overview

RPA1 (Replication Protein A1) is the large subunit of the heterotrimeric Replication Protein A (RPA) complex, which is essential for DNA replication, repair, and recombination. RPA1 plays a central role in coordinating the DNA damage response (DDR) by recruiting and activating [ATR](../genes/ATR.md) kinase signaling. In cancer genomics, RPA1 abundance in the RPA complex has been linked to gene-knockdown sensitivity in cell lines, making it relevant as a protein-level biomarker.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- ATR knockdown sensitivity tracks the ATR/ATRIP and RPA1-containing RPA complexes in CCLE cell lines; RPA1 protein abundance within the RPA complex is a predictor of ATR dependency [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
