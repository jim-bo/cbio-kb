**PMID:** 30052636 · **DOI:** [10.1371/journal.pone.0200776](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0200776) · **Journal:** PLOS ONE (2018)

## TL;DR

Zheng and colleagues at Memorial Sloan Kettering performed a pilot study of 11 hepatocellular adenomas (HCA) and 10 hepatocellular carcinomas ([HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)) from patients without underlying viral hepatitis or cirrhosis — explicitly excluding the "carcinogenic field effect" — using small RNA sequencing and the MSK-IMPACT 341-gene targeted DNA panel ([hcc_msk_venturaa_2018](../datasets/hcc_msk_venturaa_2018.md)). They show that HCA miRNA profiles cluster between normal liver and HCC, that six miRNAs (miR-200a, miR-429, miR-490-3p down; miR-452, miR-766, miR-1180 up) are similarly deregulated in both HCA and HCC vs. adjacent liver, and that members of the chromosome 19 miRNA cluster (C19MC: miR-515-5p, -517a, -518b, -520c-3p) are 3–4 fold higher in HCC than in HCA or normal liver. On the DNA side, [HNF1A](../genes/HNF1A.md) was the most frequently mutated gene in HCA (3/9 IMPACT-sequenced), while [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) (7/10), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (3/10), [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) (2/10) and [APC](../genes/APC.md) (2/10) dominated the HCC cohort.

## Cohort & data

- 21 patients total: 11 HCA ([LIAD](../cancer_types/LIAD.md)) + 10 HCC ([HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)) resected at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center 1999–2013.
- Exclusion criteria: no viral hepatitis, no prior liver-directed therapy, no cirrhosis; no HCC fibrolamellar variant.
- HCA subtype distribution ([immunohistochemistry](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md) + mutation-based): inflammatory I-HCA n=6 (54%), steatotic H-HCA n=4 (37%), β-catenin B-HCA n=1 (9%).
- HCA cohort: median age 40 (24–56), 91% female, 64% history of oral contraceptive use.
- HCC cohort: median age 73 (63–81), 40% female; median AFP 15 ng/ml (1.9–92,881).
- Dataset: cBioPortal [hcc_msk_venturaa_2018](../datasets/hcc_msk_venturaa_2018.md).
- Assays: small RNA-seq on Illumina HiSeq (TruSeq Small RNA kit, ~11.5M reads/sample, BWA mapping to miRBase) — [mirna-seq](../methods/mirna-seq.md); targeted DNA-seq via MSK-IMPACT 341-gene panel ([IMPACT341](../methods/IMPACT341.md)) on 9 HCA + 10 HCC matched tumor/normal (HCA1, HCA9 lacked IMPACT); IHC for β-catenin, LFABP, SAA, CRP, glutamine synthetase — [immunohistochemistry](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md). Reads aligned with [BWA](../methods/bwa.md)-MEM to hg19/b37 + GATK realignment.

## Key findings

- **HCA cluster between normal liver and HCC in miRNA space.** Unsupervised multidimensional scaling places all three HCA subtypes intermediate between adjacent normal liver and HCC (S2 Fig).
- **86 miRNAs are significantly dysregulated in HCC vs. adjacent normal liver** (unpaired, FDR-adjusted p ≤ 0.05; S2 Table); **only 10 miRNAs** differ between HCA and adjacent normal (S3 Table), and **57 miRNAs** differ between HCC and HCA (S4 Table).
- **Six "adenoma-carcinoma-sequence" miRNAs** are concordantly deregulated in HCA *and* HCC vs. normal liver (Table 3): miR-200a (log fold-change −2.46 HCC / −2.82 HCA), miR-429 (−2.24 / −3.03), miR-490-3p (−3.98 / −2.83) down; miR-452 (+2.08 / +2.25), miR-766 (+1.36 / +1.07), miR-1180 (+1.59 / +1.52) up. All adjusted p ≤ 0.05.
- **C19MC discriminates HCC from HCA.** miR-515-5p, miR-517a, miR-518b, miR-520c-3p are 3–4 fold higher in HCC than in HCA or normal liver (Fig 3A–D) — proposed as a malignant-transformation marker.
- **HCA mutational landscape (IMPACT, n=9):** 7 distinct mutations identified. Most frequent gene = [HNF1A](../genes/HNF1A.md) (3/9, all H-HCA, all LFABP-negative). Single tumors carried mutations in [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) (the lone B-HCA), [GNAS](../genes/GNAS.md), [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md), [HGF](../genes/HGF.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), and [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) (reported as MLL2; [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) is the HUGO symbol).
- **HCC mutational landscape (IMPACT, n=10):** 41 distinct mutations. Top genes: [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter 7/10, [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) 3/10, [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) 2/10, [APC](../genes/APC.md) 2/10.
- **No [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) mutations in any HCA.** Both HCC patients with [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) mutations also had [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter mutations — consistent with the model that [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) is an early event and TERT a later event in the HCA→HCC sequence.
- **None of 11 HCA patients progressed to HCC** during follow-up; the authors note follow-up time may be insufficient.

## Genes & alterations

- [HNF1A](../genes/HNF1A.md) — biallelic-style inactivating mutations in 3/9 IMPACT-sequenced HCA; defines the steatotic H-HCA subtype with corresponding LFABP loss by IHC.
- [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) — mutation in 1/9 HCA (the B-HCA with aberrant nuclear β-catenin) and 2/10 HCC; both HCC CTNNB1-mutant cases co-carried TERT promoter mutations.
- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) — promoter mutation in 7/10 HCC; absent in HCA. Authors interpret this as TERT being a later step in HCA-to-HCC transformation.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — mutation in 3/10 HCC; not detected in HCA. Frames as a late-stage HCC event consistent with prior literature.
- [APC](../genes/APC.md) — mutation in 2/10 HCC.
- [GNAS](../genes/GNAS.md), [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md), [HGF](../genes/HGF.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) (reported as MLL2) — each mutated in a single inflammatory HCA (Table 2).
- C19MC locus (chr19q13.41) — not covered by the [IMPACT341](../methods/IMPACT341.md) panel, so copy-number drivers of C19MC overexpression in HCC could not be assessed in this cohort (explicitly noted as a limitation).

## Clinical implications

- The six concordantly deregulated miRNAs (miR-200a, miR-429, miR-490-3p, miR-452, miR-766, miR-1180) and the C19MC cluster are proposed as **candidate surrogate biomarkers to predict HCA→HCC transformation**, but the authors explicitly call this a hypothesis-generating pilot — no clinical actionability is claimed.
- The pattern of [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) without [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) (HCA) vs. [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) co-occurring with [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) (HCC) supports prior models in which CTNNB1 is the early oncogenic hit and TERT promoter activation marks malignant transformation, with potential utility for risk-stratifying B-HCA patients.
- No drug-targeting claims are made.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Small sample size (n=11 HCA, n=10 HCC) and only 1 B-HCA** — the subtype most prone to malignant transformation. All three HCA subtypes had to be pooled for statistical power; per-subtype miRNA analysis was performed but underpowered.
- **No HCA in the cohort progressed to HCC** during the available follow-up, so the "adenoma-carcinoma sequence" interpretation is inferred from cross-sectional similarity, not longitudinal observation.
- **IMPACT341 does not cover chr19q13.41**, so the mechanism (amplification vs. hypomethylation) of C19MC overexpression in HCC cannot be distinguished here.
- HCC cohort excludes the dominant viral/cirrhotic etiology; findings may not generalize to hepatitis-B/C- or cirrhosis-associated HCC.
- miR-490-3p direction is consistent with one prior report but at odds with another — discrepancy unresolved.
