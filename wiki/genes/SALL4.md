# SALL4

## Overview

SALL4 (Spalt Like Transcription Factor 4) encodes a zinc-finger transcription factor critical for embryonic stem cell maintenance and early development. In cancer genomics, SALL4 is a hepatocyte lineage marker whose expression is elevated in HCC-like molecular clusters, making it useful for characterizing liver cancer lineage identity and distinguishing hepatocyte-like from biliary-like tumor subtypes.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Identified as a hepatocyte lineage marker elevated in HCC-like clusters (P2/P4) of combined hepatocellular-cholangiocarcinoma (cHCC-ICC, n=133 samples); elevated SALL4 expression in these subtypes is consistent with hepatocyte-lineage identity and supports alignment with HCC-directed therapeutic approaches [PMID:31130341](../papers/31130341.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)**: SALL4 is elevated in HCC-like (P2/P4) molecular clusters of cHCC-ICC, consistent with its role as a hepatocyte lineage marker [PMID:31130341](../papers/31130341.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-elevated with [TBX3](../genes/TBX3.md), [GPC3](../genes/GPC3.md), [APOE](../genes/APOE.md), and [ALB](../genes/ALB.md) as part of the hepatocyte marker signature in HCC-like cHCC-ICC clusters [PMID:31130341](../papers/31130341.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Elevated SALL4 expression in HCC-like cHCC-ICC clusters supports molecular alignment with HCC-directed therapies (e.g., [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md), immunotherapy regimens used in [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)); this is based on molecular similarity, not direct therapeutic evidence from this cohort [PMID:31130341](../papers/31130341.md)

## Open questions

- Whether SALL4 expression level in cHCC-ICC predicts response to HCC-directed versus ICC-directed systemic therapies requires prospective clinical validation.
