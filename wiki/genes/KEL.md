# KEL

## Overview

KEL (Kell Blood Group Metallopeptidase 1) encodes a type II transmembrane zinc endopeptidase that is the major component of the Kell blood group antigen system. In cancer genomics, KEL has been identified as a significantly mutated gene in glioblastoma multiforme by large-scale whole-exome sequencing studies, though its functional role in [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) pathogenesis remains unclear.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Identified as an additional significantly mutated gene (SMG) in GBM by MutSig analysis (q<0.1, ~5% frequency) in the TCGA 2013 primary GBM cohort of 291 whole-exome-sequenced cases [PMID:24120142](../papers/24120142.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md): significantly mutated gene (~5% frequency) in the TCGA 2013 GBM cohort; classified alongside [SPTA1](../genes/SPTA1.md) and [GABRA6](../genes/GABRA6.md) as additional MutSig SMGs.

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- No co-occurrence or mutual exclusivity patterns reported in the corpus for KEL in GBM.

## Therapeutic relevance

- Not targeted in the corpus; functional role in GBM has not been characterized.

## Open questions

- Functional consequence of KEL mutation in GBM and whether it is a bona fide driver or a passenger mutation remains uncharacterized [PMID:24120142](../papers/24120142.md)
