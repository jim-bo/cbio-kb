**PMID:** 27276561 · **DOI:** 10.1056/NEJMoa1516192 · **Journal:** New England Journal of Medicine (2016)

## TL;DR

Papaemmanuil and colleagues sequenced 111 cancer genes plus cytogenetics in 1540 adults with acute myeloid leukemia ([AML](../cancer_types/AML.md)) enrolled in three German–Austrian AMLSG intensive-therapy trials, identifying 5234 driver mutations across 76 genes/regions and partitioning the cohort into 11 mutually exclusive genomic subgroups. Beyond established WHO categories, three new categories emerged — chromatin–spliceosome [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md) (18%), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)–aneuploidy AML (13%), and provisionally [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) R172 AML (1%) — with the chromatin–spliceosome and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)–aneuploidy groups carrying poor prognoses. Co-occurring driver mutations and gene–gene interactions (notably the three-way [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md)–[DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md)–[FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md) ITD interaction) substantially modified outcome, and the classification replicated in the independent TCGA AML cohort ([laml_tcga_pub](../datasets/laml_tcga_pub.md)).

## Cohort & data

- **N = 1540** adults with AML enrolled in three prospective AMLSG intensive-therapy trials: AML-HD98A (ages 18–65, ICE induction), AMLSG-07-04 (ages 18–61, ICE ± all-trans retinoic acid), and AML-HD98B (ages 58–84, ICE ± ATRA). Median follow-up 5.9 years. ClinicalTrials.gov NCT00146120.
- **Cancer type:** [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md) (de novo and secondary).
- **Genomic data:** sequencing of 111 leukemia genes plus cytogenetics ([targeted-dna-seq](../methods/targeted-dna-seq.md)); driver criteria per Lawrence et al. 2013. Sequencing data deposited at EGA accession EGAS00001000275.
- **Validation cohort:** TCGA AML ([laml_tcga_pub](../datasets/laml_tcga_pub.md)) — independent cohort with many older patients used to replicate subgroup structure and prognostic findings.
- **Treatments studied:** [cytarabine](../drugs/cytarabine.md), [idarubicin](../drugs/idarubicin.md), and [etoposide](../drugs/etoposide.md) (ICE induction); [tretinoin](../drugs/tretinoin.md) (ATRA) added in two trial arms; allogeneic stem-cell transplant for high-risk patients.

## Key findings

- **5234 driver mutations across 76 genes or genomic regions** in 1540 patients; ≥1 driver in 1478/1540 (96%) and ≥2 drivers in 86% of patients. Point mutations accounted for 73% (3824/5234) of drivers ([PMID:27276561](../papers/27276561.md)).
- **Eleven mutually exclusive genomic subgroups** were defined by Bayesian Dirichlet-process modeling. 1236/1540 (80%) patients fell unambiguously into one subgroup, 56 (4%) met criteria for ≥2 categories, and 166 (11%) with driver mutations remained unclassified ([PMID:27276561](../papers/27276561.md)).
- **[NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md)-mutated AML was the largest subgroup** (27% of cohort, 418 patients); 73% of [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md)-mutated cases (319/436) carried co-mutations in DNA methylation/hydroxymethylation genes ([DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md), [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md), [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) R140, or [TET2](../genes/TET2.md)).
- **Chromatin–spliceosome subgroup (18%, n=275)** — newly defined; mutations in RNA-splicing ([SRSF2](../genes/SRSF2.md), [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md), [U2AF1](../genes/U2AF1.md), [ZRSR2](../genes/ZRSR2.md)), chromatin ([ASXL1](../genes/ASXL1.md), [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md), [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md), [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) PTD, [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md), [PHF6](../genes/PHF6.md)), or transcription ([RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md)). Patients were older with lower WBC/blast counts, lower induction response, higher relapse, and poor long-term outcomes; 84% (232/275) would be classified intermediate-risk under ELN guidelines despite outcomes resembling adverse-risk groups.
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)–aneuploidy subgroup (13%, n=199)** — newly defined; characterized by [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations, complex karyotype, or chromosomal aneuploidies (−5/5q, −7/7q, −17/17p, +8/8q, etc.). [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation and complex karyotype contributed independently and additively to poor survival.
- **Provisional [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) R172 subgroup (1%, n=18)** — mutually exclusive with [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md) (OR 0.06, P=4×10⁻⁵), unlike [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) R140 which co-occurs strongly with [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md) (OR 3.6, P=5×10⁻¹⁰). Long-term outcomes broadly similar to [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md)-mutated AML.
- **Three-way [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md)–[DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md)–[FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md) ITD interaction**: the deleterious effect of [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md) ITD on survival was strongest only when [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md) and [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md) were also mutated (n=93, 6% of cohort; HR 1.5 [1.2–1.9], q=0.004 for three-way interaction). Independent of [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md) mutant-allele ratio.
- **[NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md)–[DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md)–[NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) G12/13** carried an unexpectedly favorable prognosis (n=45, P=0.0007 three-way), refining prior reports that "NPM1–[NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md)" is favorable.
- **[KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) PTD × [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md) TKD** co-occurrence (n=10) was strongly adverse (q=0.008); **[DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md) × [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) R140** co-occurrence (n=19) was also adverse (q=0.05).
- **Survival concordance ~71%** with the full multivariate model vs. ~64% using only ELN variables. Genomic features explained ~two-thirds of explained variation; gene–gene interactions accounted for 11% of explained variance.
- **[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) mutations** were independently associated with worse prognosis (P=0.009, q=0.06); the authors suggest [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) inhibitors as a candidate therapy in this small subgroup.
- **Clonal-evolution patterns**: epigenetic-modifier mutations ([DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md), [ASXL1](../genes/ASXL1.md), [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md), [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md), [TET2](../genes/TET2.md)) were typically founding/early events; RTK–RAS pathway mutations were typically late and frequently subclonal/recurrent within a patient. [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md) was a late driver, often secondary to [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md), [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md), or [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md).
- **WHO 2008 fusion-defined classes** ([PML](../genes/PML.md)–[RARA](../genes/RARA.md), [CBFB](../genes/CBFB.md)–[MYH11](../genes/MYH11.md), [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md)–[RUNX1T1](../genes/RUNX1T1.md), [DEK](../genes/DEK.md)–[NUP214](../genes/NUP214.md), inv(3)/[MECOM](../genes/MECOM.md), [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) fusions including [MLLT3](../genes/MLLT3.md)–[KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md)) were each ≤5% of the cohort and confirmed; [CEBPA](../genes/CEBPA.md) biallelic was a distinct subgroup (4%, n=66).

## Genes & alterations

- **[NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md)** — mutations in 28% (n=436); class-defining for largest subgroup; favorable as monotherapy but outcome strongly modified by co-occurring [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md), [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md), [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md), [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md), [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md), and chromatin–spliceosome mutations.
- **[FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md)** — ITD in 22% (n=341); deleterious effect strongest in NPM1+/DNMT3A+ context. FLT3 TKD distinct from FLT3 ITD in co-mutation patterns and interactions (e.g. with [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) PTD).
- **[DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md)** — frequent founding-clone driver; modifies effect of FLT3 ITD, NRAS G12/13, [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) R140, and [RAD21](../genes/RAD21.md) mutations.
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)** — 6% (n=98); core of TP53–aneuploidy subgroup; HR 1.7 (1.4–2.2), P=7×10⁻⁶, q=0.0002. Independent and additive with complex karyotype.
- **[IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md)** — R140 (co-occurs with [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md), OR 3.6) vs. R172 (mutually exclusive with [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md), OR 0.06) define functionally distinct hotspots; R172 forms a provisional subgroup with metabolic/methylation profile distinct from other IDH mutants.
- **[IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md)** — early/founding driver in epigenetic axis; co-mutes with [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md).
- **[TET2](../genes/TET2.md)**, **[ASXL1](../genes/ASXL1.md)** — early epigenetic drivers; [ASXL1](../genes/ASXL1.md) part of chromatin–spliceosome group with independent adverse effect.
- **[SRSF2](../genes/SRSF2.md)** — splicing factor in chromatin–spliceosome group; HR 1.4 (1.1–1.7), P=0.003, q=0.03. Additive adverse effect with ASXL1.
- **[SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md)**, **[U2AF1](../genes/U2AF1.md)**, **[ZRSR2](../genes/ZRSR2.md)** — additional splicing factors defining chromatin–spliceosome subgroup.
- **[STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md)**, **[BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md)**, **[KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) PTD**, **[EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md)**, **[PHF6](../genes/PHF6.md)** — chromatin regulators in chromatin–spliceosome subgroup.
- **[RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md)** — transcription regulator in chromatin–spliceosome subgroup.
- **[CEBPA](../genes/CEBPA.md)** — biallelic mutations define favorable subgroup (4%, n=66); HR 0.6 (0.4–0.7), P=4×10⁻⁵.
- **[NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md)** — late driver in RTK–RAS pathway; G12/13 vs. Q61 hotspots have distinct co-mutation patterns; G12/13 in NPM1/DNMT3A context paradoxically favorable.
- **[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)** — 1% (n=9); independently adverse (P=0.009, q=0.06); proposed candidate for [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) inhibitor therapy.
- **[MYC](../genes/MYC.md)** — novel hotspot mutations described in the cohort (Supplementary Results S1).
- **[PML](../genes/PML.md)–[RARA](../genes/RARA.md)** t(15;17) — 4% (n=60); class-defining; HR 0.3 (0.2–0.4).
- **[CBFB](../genes/CBFB.md)–[MYH11](../genes/MYH11.md)** inv(16)/t(16;16) — 5% (n=81); HR 0.3 (0.2–0.4).
- **[RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md)–[RUNX1T1](../genes/RUNX1T1.md)** t(8;21) — 4% (n=60).
- **[DEK](../genes/DEK.md)–[NUP214](../genes/NUP214.md)** t(6;9) — 1% (n=15).
- **inv(3)/[MECOM](../genes/MECOM.md)** — 1% (n=20); among the strongest adverse main effects (HR 2.9 [1.8–4.7], P=9×10⁻⁶, q=0.0003).
- **[KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) fusions** — 3% (n=44); multiple partners; [MLLT3](../genes/MLLT3.md)–[KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) (t(9;11)) appears favorable relative to other KMT2A fusions.

## Clinical implications

- **Disease classification reform**: the authors argue genomic subgroups should supplement WHO morphology-based classification. Three new genomic categories (chromatin–spliceosome, TP53–aneuploidy, provisional IDH2 R172) capture 32% of the cohort that was previously unclassified by WHO 2008 lesions.
- **Prognostic stratification beyond ELN**: the full genomic+clinical model achieves ~71% concordance for overall survival vs. ~64% with ELN variables alone. The authors recommend incorporating [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [SRSF2](../genes/SRSF2.md), [ASXL1](../genes/ASXL1.md), [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md), and [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) into prognostic guidelines, and adding [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md), [ASXL1](../genes/ASXL1.md), and [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) PTD evaluation at diagnosis to identify chromatin–spliceosome patients.
- **Risk reclassification**: 84% of chromatin–spliceosome patients (232/275) currently classified as ELN intermediate-risk would be more appropriately treated as adverse-risk based on observed outcomes.
- **Context-dependent prognosis for [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md)-mutated AML**: outcome depends on co-mutation context — favorable with NPM1+DNMT3A+NRAS G12/13, adverse with NPM1+DNMT3A+FLT3 ITD. Implies allogeneic transplant decisions in [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md) AML should integrate co-mutation profiles.
- **Therapeutic candidates**: [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) inhibitors proposed for [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)-mutant AML (small subgroup but independently adverse); [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md) and RAS-pathway inhibitors expected to alter outcome predictions for those subgroups.
- **Boundary with chronic myeloid disorders**: chromatin–spliceosome AML overlaps mutationally with high-risk myeloproliferative neoplasms and [MDS](../cancer_types/MDS.md), suggesting shared evolutionary trajectories and a possible reorganization of acute/chronic myeloid disease boundaries.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Cohort skewed to younger, intensively treated patients**: trials enrolled adults selected for intensive induction. Older AML patients ineligible for intensive therapy are underrepresented; the TCGA replication cohort partially addresses this.
- **Prospective validation needed**: the authors explicitly state predictions from co-mutation contexts (especially within [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md) AML) require validation in prospective clinical trials.
- **166 unclassified patients (11%) with driver mutations** — may harbor mutations in genes not on the 111-gene panel, or unrecognized class-defining lesions.
- **Provisional [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) R172 subgroup is small (n=18)** and the case for it as an independent class rests on co-mutation structure and prior metabolic/methylation evidence; larger cohorts are needed.
- **Treatment-specific outcomes not modeled**: the prognostic model averages over induction regimen and post-remission therapy choices, limiting ability to define class-specific optimal therapy.
- **Open question for cross-paper synthesis**: how does the chromatin–spliceosome AML class relate genetically and clinically to [MDS](../cancer_types/MDS.md) and high-risk [MPN](../cancer_types/MPN.md) with the same mutated genes? See [MDS](../cancer_types/MDS.md).
