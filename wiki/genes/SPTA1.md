# SPTA1

## Overview

SPTA1 encodes alpha-spectrin 1, a cytoskeletal protein critical for red blood cell membrane integrity. In [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md), SPTA1 was identified as a significantly mutated gene (SMG) by MutSig analysis, though its role as a cancer driver is not yet established.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Somatic mutation in 9% of GBM; identified as a MutSig SMG (q<0.1) in the TCGA GBM comprehensive genomic characterization [PMID:24120142](../papers/24120142.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **GBM** — somatic mutation in 9% of 291 cases; identified by MutSig analysis alongside [GABRA6](../genes/GABRA6.md) (4%) and [KEL](../genes/KEL.md) (5%) as additional significantly mutated genes beyond the core GBM drivers [PMID:24120142](../papers/24120142.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-identified as a MutSig SMG in GBM alongside GABRA6 and KEL [PMID:24120142](../papers/24120142.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting reported in the corpus.

## Open questions

- Whether SPTA1 mutations in GBM represent true driver events or are passenger mutations facilitated by high mutation rate is unresolved; the cytoskeletal biology of SPTA1 does not have an obvious mechanistic connection to gliomagenesis.
