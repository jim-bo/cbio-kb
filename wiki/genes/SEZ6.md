# SEZ6

## Overview

SEZ6 (seizure related 6 homolog) is a complement pathway regulator and surface antigen expressed on neuroendocrine tumor cells. It is an emerging therapeutic target in [SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md) and related neuroendocrine malignancies.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- High IHC expression in all 9 evaluable aSCLC samples (mean H-score 240); aSCLC cohort n=20 (MSKCC); this positions aSCLC as a candidate for SEZ6-targeted therapy [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- aSCLC (atypical SCLC) — universally high SEZ6 IHC expression supports consideration for SEZ6-targeted agents [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-expressed with [DLL3](../genes/DLL3.md) at high levels in aSCLC [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Universal SEZ6 expression in aSCLC provides rationale for emerging SEZ6-targeted agents [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md).

## Open questions

- Clinical-grade SEZ6-targeting agents are preclinical; translational pharmacology data in aSCLC are lacking.
