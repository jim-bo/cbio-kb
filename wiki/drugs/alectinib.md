# alectinib

## Overview

Alectinib is a selective, potent 2nd-generation [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) inhibitor with CNS penetrance, approved for ALK-rearranged non-small cell lung cancer ([NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md)). It demonstrates superior efficacy over [crizotinib](../drugs/crizotinib.md) in previously untreated ALK-positive [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) (ALEX trial). Alectinib inhibits crizotinib-resistant [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) mutations including L1196M and G1269A.

## Evidence in the corpus

- In Chinese pan-cancer cohort (n=10,194), ALK fusions detected in 6.7% of NSCLC; alectinib is listed as an OncoKB-actionable agent alongside crizotinib and [ceritinib](../drugs/ceritinib.md) for ALK-fusion NSCLC [PMID:35871175](../papers/35871175.md)

## Resistance mechanisms

- Secondary ALK mutations (G1202R, I1171T) confer resistance to alectinib; addressed by [lorlatinib](../drugs/lorlatinib.md) (3rd-generation).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) — ALK fusions 6.7% of NSCLC in Chinese cohort
