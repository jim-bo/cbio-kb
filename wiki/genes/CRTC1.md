# CRTC1

## Overview

CRTC1 (CREB-regulated transcriptional coactivator 1) is a coactivator of the CREB transcription factor. In oncology, CRTC1 is best known as a fusion partner in mucoepidermoid carcinoma (CRTC1-MAML2 fusion). In the sarcoma corpus, CRTC1 appears as a rare shared somatic SNV across intratumoral subclones.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- CRTC1 was one of three shared SNVs (alongside [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md) and [MAP3K1](../genes/MAP3K1.md)) detected across all four synchronous metastatic lesions of SARC0075 (epithelioid sarcoma with undifferentiated pleomorphic features) in a UCLA sarcoma patient-derived tumor organoid (PDTO) study; the CNV landscape across the four lesions was substantially divergent, including whole-genome duplications and arm-level events, despite this shared SNV clonal ancestor [PMID:39305899](../papers/39305899.md).
- Newly nominated significantly mutated gene in papillary renal cell carcinoma (pRCC) by multi-method driver analysis [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [EPIS](../cancer_types/EPIS.md) / undifferentiated pleomorphic sarcoma — CRTC1 shared SNV across four metastatic lesions, demonstrating clonal ancestry with divergent copy-number evolution [PMID:39305899](../papers/39305899.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs as a shared ancestral SNV with [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md) and [MAP3K1](../genes/MAP3K1.md) mutations in SARC0075 [PMID:39305899](../papers/39305899.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Not directly therapeutically targeted in the corpus; shared SNV status in heterogeneous metastatic sarcoma illustrates limits of single-biopsy PDTO drug screening [PMID:39305899](../papers/39305899.md).

## Open questions

- Functional significance of CRTC1 point mutations in sarcoma (vs the canonical CRTC1-MAML2 fusion of mucoepidermoid carcinoma) is not established in this corpus.
