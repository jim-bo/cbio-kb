# ST6GAL1

## Overview

ST6GAL1 (ST6 Beta-Galactoside Alpha-2,6-Sialyltransferase 1) encodes the enzyme that transfers sialic acid to N-linked glycoproteins and glycolipids via alpha-2,6 linkages. Aberrant sialylation mediated by ST6GAL1 promotes cancer immune evasion, metastasis, and drug resistance by modifying cell surface glycan structures. ST6GAL1 has been identified as a therapeutic target in pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma through proteogenomic characterization of the sialylation enzyme landscape.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Upregulated sialyl-/fucosyltransferase activity including ST6GAL1 identified in PDAC (CPTAC 2021 cohort, n=140 treatment-naive resected tumors); sialyl-/fucosyltransferase inhibition targeting ST6GAL1, [ST3GAL1](../genes/ST3GAL1.md), [FUT3](../genes/FUT3.md), [FUT11](../genes/FUT11.md), and [B4GALT1](../genes/B4GALT1.md) proposed as a therapeutic strategy in PDAC; FUT3 upregulation in C1 (classical) subtype suggests subtype-tailored glycan-capping blockade [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md): ST6GAL1 sialyl transferase activity elevated in PDAC; candidate therapeutic target; authors caution that systemic sialylation blockade (e.g., 3F-NeuAc) caused liver/kidney toxicity in mouse models [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- ST6GAL1 inhibition proposed in combination with ST3GAL1, FUT3, FUT11, and B4GALT1 as part of a glycosylation-enzyme inhibition strategy in PDAC [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Sialyl-/fucosyltransferase inhibition (targeting ST6GAL1 among others) proposed as a therapeutic strategy in PDAC; on-target toxicity concerns from preclinical mouse models require selective inhibitor development before clinical translation [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Open questions

- Selective inhibitors of ST6GAL1 not yet developed; systemic sialylation blockade (3F-NeuAc) caused liver/kidney toxicity in mouse models, requiring subtype-specific or targeted delivery strategies [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)
