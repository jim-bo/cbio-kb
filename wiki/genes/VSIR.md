# VSIR

## Overview

VSIR (V-Set Immunoregulatory Receptor), also known as C10orf54 and VISTA, encodes an immune checkpoint receptor expressed on T cells and myeloid cells. In the [nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md) pharmacodynamic study (CA209-038), VSIR was among the immune checkpoint genes selectively upregulated on-therapy in responders, nominating it as a candidate target for combination immunotherapy strategies.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- VSIR (C10orf54 / VISTA) is selectively upregulated on-therapy in responders (CR/PR) but not non-responders during nivolumab treatment of advanced melanoma; among 2670 differentially expressed genes distinguishing responder vs non-responder on-therapy dynamics (q < 0.20) [PMID:29033130](../papers/29033130.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md) — responder-selective on-therapy upregulation in the CA209-038 prospective trial (NCT01621490) of nivolumab (3 mg/kg q2w) in 68 advanced melanoma patients; the upregulation is specific to CR/PR patients and absent in SD/PD patients [PMID:29033130](../papers/29033130.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- VSIR upregulation on-therapy co-occurs with upregulation of other checkpoint receptors — [TNFRSF4](../genes/TNFRSF4.md) (OX40), [TIGIT](../genes/TIGIT.md), and [HAVCR2](../genes/HAVCR2.md) (TIM-3) — selectively in responders, suggesting coordinated secondary checkpoint induction following anti-PD-1 therapy [PMID:29033130](../papers/29033130.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Responder-selective on-therapy upregulation of VSIR in melanoma patients responding to nivolumab nominates it as a candidate target for combination immunotherapy; co-blockade of VSIR with PD-1 blockade is proposed as a rational next therapeutic strategy [PMID:29033130](../papers/29033130.md).

## Open questions

- Whether VSIR upregulation is a cause or consequence of the immunoediting response is unresolved; clinical testing of anti-VISTA combination therapy with anti-PD-1 agents is nominated but not yet validated in this corpus [PMID:29033130](../papers/29033130.md).
