# ipatasertib

## Overview

Ipatasertib is an ATP-competitive pan-AKT kinase inhibitor targeting [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md), [AKT2](../genes/AKT2.md), and [AKT3](../genes/AKT3.md). It inhibits the PI3K/AKT signaling axis downstream of [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) loss and [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md) alteration. The IPATential150 phase 3 trial (NCT03072238) evaluated ipatasertib in metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer (mCRPC) in combination with abiraterone/prednisone.

## Evidence in the corpus

- The PIK3R1-mutant patient-derived organoid MSK-PCa3 (Asn564Asp) showed strong growth reduction with ipatasertib at 10 nM, whereas the PIK3R1–wild-type, PTEN-deleted organoid MSK-PCa1 was only modestly affected — supporting PIK3R1 alteration (not PTEN loss alone) as a stratifier for AKT-inhibitor response. MDA-MB-231 (TNBC) cells with PIK3R1 knockdown also showed enhanced sensitivity to ipatasertib [PMID:35670774](../papers/35670774.md).
- In the IPATential150 phase 3 trial (NCT03072238), ipatasertib + abiraterone/prednisone improved radiographic PFS only in PTEN-loss mCRPC (HR 0.77, 95% CI 0.61–0.98); PIK3R1-altered patients were not formally analyzed despite mechanistic rationale for AKT-inhibitor benefit in that subgroup [PMID:35670774](../papers/35670774.md).

## Resistance mechanisms

- PIK3R1–wild-type, PTEN-deleted tumors (e.g., MSK-PCa1 organoid) showed only modest sensitivity to ipatasertib at 10 nM, suggesting PTEN loss alone is insufficient to predict response and PIK3R1 status may further stratify responders [PMID:35670774](../papers/35670774.md).
