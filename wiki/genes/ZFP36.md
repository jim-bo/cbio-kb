# ZFP36

## Overview

ZFP36 (ZFP36 Ring Finger Protein, also known as Tristetraprolin/TTP) is an RNA-binding protein of the CCCH tandem zinc finger family that promotes mRNA decay of AU-rich element-containing transcripts, including many oncogenes and pro-inflammatory cytokines. It is part of the ZFP36 gene family alongside [ZFP36L1](../genes/ZFP36L1.md) and [ZFP36L2](../genes/ZFP36L2.md).

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Deleterious p.P253A mutation (COSMIC-curated) identified in 1 [UTUC](../cancer_types/UTUC.md) case (IGBMC cohort, n=40); contributes to the 26.7% overall ZFP36-family mutation rate in UTUC [PMID:33397444](../papers/33397444.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[UTUC](../cancer_types/UTUC.md)**: Deleterious mutation (COSMIC-curated p.P253A) in upper tract urothelial carcinoma; ZFP36-family mutations collectively affect 26.7% of UTUC cases and are associated with higher mutational load and better pooled-bladder-cohort [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) (P=0.01, n=562) [PMID:33397444](../papers/33397444.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- ZFP36, ZFP36L1, and ZFP36L2 mutations are collectively mutually co-occurring across TCGA histologies (P<0.001 for ZFP36L1/ZFP36L2 pair); their combined mutation rate in UTUC (26.7%) is notably higher than in most other tumor types [PMID:33397444](../papers/33397444.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- ZFP36-family-mutant tumors show higher mutational load, suggesting potential benefit from immune checkpoint therapy; this is a hypothesis-generating observation without prospective testing [PMID:33397444](../papers/33397444.md).

## Open questions

- Whether ZFP36 mutation independently drives UTUC biology or is a passenger in high-TMB tumors is unresolved [PMID:33397444](../papers/33397444.md).
- Functional validation in UTUC-specific models is absent from the corpus [PMID:33397444](../papers/33397444.md).
