# LTF

## Overview

LTF (Lactoferrin) encodes an iron-binding glycoprotein with roles in innate immunity and cellular proliferation. In cancer, LTF mutations have been observed as subclonal events in metastatic lesions, suggesting a potential role in clonal evolution and metastatic progression in adenoid cystic carcinoma ([ACC](../cancer_types/ACC.md)).

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Subclonal mutation identified in the most divergent metastatic cluster (metastases 6D, 2B, 4A) in a multiregion WES case of salivary ACC with 6 primary subregions and 8 lung metastases; co-occurs with subclonal [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md), [XDH](../genes/XDH.md), and [TMEM2](../genes/TMEM2.md) mutations; genes in this cluster have been previously implicated in metastasis in other tumor types [PMID:31483290](../papers/31483290.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **ACC** — Subclonal LTF mutation detected in divergent metastatic regions in a multi-institutional R/M ACC cohort multiregion case; not found in all primary subregions [PMID:31483290](../papers/31483290.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs subclonally with SF3B1, XDH, and TMEM2 mutations in the most divergent metastatic cluster of a multiregion ACC case [PMID:31483290](../papers/31483290.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No established therapeutic relevance in ACC; observed as a subclonal metastasis-associated variant of uncertain significance.

## Open questions

- Whether LTF mutation is a driver or passenger in ACC metastatic clones requires functional validation and broader cohort confirmation.
