# MSK Make-An-IMPACT (MSK, Clin Cancer Res 2023)

## Overview

Make-an-IMPACT is a direct-to-patient outreach program that enrolled rare-cancer patients (histiocytosis, female and male germ cell tumors, pediatric cancers) worldwide via social media and advocacy groups for free clinical tumor genomic profiling via MSK-IMPACT, returning results to patients and their local physicians [PMID:36862133](../papers/36862133.md).

## Composition

- 333 patients consented between March 2016 and October 2020; 288 (86.4%) provided tumor tissue and 250 (86.8% of those) had DNA of sufficient quality for MSK-IMPACT sequencing; 63% from US sites, 37% from 17 countries outside the US [PMID:36862133](../papers/36862133.md).
- Profiled cancer types: histiocytosis (n=84; [LCH](../cancer_types/LCH.md), [ECD](../cancer_types/ECD.md)), female germ cell tumor (n=54; [OGCT](../cancer_types/OGCT.md)), male germ cell tumor (n=54; [MGCT](../cancer_types/MGCT.md)), adenoid cystic carcinoma (n=19; [ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md)) [PMID:36862133](../papers/36862133.md).

## Assays / panels (linked)

- [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/IMPACT505.md) (up to 505 genes) with select targeted [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md); whole-exome recapture performed for 62 ovarian GCT tumor/normal pairs from 59 patients [PMID:36862133](../papers/36862133.md).

## Papers using this cohort

- [PMID:36862133](../papers/36862133.md) — Doe-Tetteh et al., Overcoming barriers to tumor genomic profiling through direct-to-patient outreach.

## Notable findings derived from this cohort

- Histiocytosis driver frequencies: [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) 33%, [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md) 13%, [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) 7%, [CSF1R](../genes/CSF1R.md) 2.4%; actionable fusions included MS4A6A-BRAF, DOCK8-BRAF, HLA-A-BRAF, TFG-ALK, and PRDX1-NTRK1 [PMID:36862133](../papers/36862133.md).
- 47/84 histiocytosis patients had potentially actionable alterations; 17/18 (94%) who received matched targeted therapy had clinical benefit with mean duration 21.7 months [PMID:36862133](../papers/36862133.md).
- WES of 62 female GCT pairs revealed 17% (n=10) with a near-haploid genomic profile mutually exclusive with 12p gain — a phenotype rarely seen in other cancers [PMID:36862133](../papers/36862133.md).
