# MUC6

## Overview

MUC6 (Mucin 6, Oligomeric Mucus/Gel-Forming) encodes a large secreted gel-forming mucin expressed predominantly in the gastric mucosa and to a lesser degree in the pancreas. In cancer genomics, MUC6 exhibits broad non-coding indel enrichment in gastric and colorectal cancers, a pattern shared with other stomach-lineage genes ([LIPF](../genes/LIPF.md), [PGC](../genes/PGC.md)). This enrichment is thought to reflect an elevated background indel rate in tissue-specific open chromatin rather than positive selection for a driver function, as no expression change is associated with the indels.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Broad non-coding indel enrichment at the MUC6 locus detected by genome-wide indel analysis of 212 gastric adenocarcinoma whole genomes (TCGA, ICGC, Singapore cohorts); stomach-lineage specific expression (133 TPM stomach, 38 TPM pancreas, ≤2 TPM elsewhere); no expression change associated with MUC6 indels (Wilcoxon P not significant) [PMID:29670109](../papers/29670109.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md) — MUC6 is among the top broad-indel-enriched loci in gastric adenocarcinoma WGS; the enrichment recapitulates prior reports and is interpreted as a tissue-specific background mutational process in stomach-lineage chromatin rather than a coding driver event [PMID:29670109](../papers/29670109.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-enriched with [LIPF](../genes/LIPF.md) and [PGC](../genes/PGC.md) as the three stomach-lineage genes with genome-wide broad non-coding indel enrichment in gastric cancer; none showed expression changes correlated with mutation status [PMID:29670109](../papers/29670109.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting reported. MUC6 non-coding indels are not established functional drivers and carry no current therapeutic implication.

## Open questions

- Whether the non-coding indel enrichment at MUC6 represents a bona fide driver mechanism or purely an elevated background indel rate in accessible chromatin of stomach-lineage cells remains unresolved; current evidence (no expression correlation) does not support a cis-regulatory driver role [PMID:29670109](../papers/29670109.md)
