# FGFR3

## Overview

FGFR3 is a receptor tyrosine kinase recurrently activated in urothelial carcinoma via hotspot missense mutations (S249C, R248C, Y373C) and FGFR3-[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md) fusions, and is the primary actionable biomarker for the FGFR inhibitor [erdafitinib](../drugs/erdafitinib.md).

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Oncogenic FGFR3 alterations were present in 27.5% of urothelial tumors overall ([bladder_msk_2023](../datasets/bladder_msk_2023.md)), with frequencies of 39% (199/504) in NMIBC, 14% (75/526) in MIBC, 43% (81/187) in localized [UTUC](../cancer_types/UTUC.md), and 26% (59/228) in metastatic disease [PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md).
- S249C was the most frequent alteration overall; R248C was enriched in upper-tract (22%, 18/81) vs bladder (11%, 37/333; p=0.01) tumors [PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md).
- FGFR3-[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md) fusions observed; FGFR3-fusion tumors had lower TMB than FGFR3-mutant tumors (median 5 vs 9 mut/Mb, p=0.0006) [PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md).
- Primary/metastatic FGFR3 discordance in 7/27 (26%) paired cases: 4 metastasis-only, 2 primary-only, 1 with different FGFR3 alterations in each site [PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md).
- Acquired FGFR3 kinase-domain mutations identified in serial cfDNA on [erdafitinib](../drugs/erdafitinib.md) at conserved residues (N540S, V553M, K650M, R669G); in silico modeling supports V553M as a gatekeeper allele allosterically blocking [erdafitinib](../drugs/erdafitinib.md) binding [PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md) — 39% NMIBC, 14% MIBC, 26% metastatic [PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md).
- [UTUC](../cancer_types/UTUC.md) — 43% localized; R248C enriched vs bladder [PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Inversely associated with [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), and [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) alterations [PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md).
- Frequently co-altered with [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md), and [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md) [PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md).
- PI3K-pathway co-alterations in 37% of FGFR2/3-altered tumors: [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) 28%, [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md) 13%, [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) 2.7% [PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Primary actionable biomarker for [erdafitinib](../drugs/erdafitinib.md); real-world ORR 40% (12/30) but median PFS 2.8 mo and OS 6.6 mo in 32 metastatic UC patients [PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md).
- Acquired second-site FGFR3 V553M/N540S kinase-domain mutations are putative resistance drivers detectable in cfDNA [PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md).
- Archival primary-tumor FGFR3 status misclassifies metastatic disease in ~26% of patients, arguing for metastatic-site or cfDNA profiling before erdafitinib selection [PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md).
- FGFR3 status did not alter PFS/OS on immune checkpoint blockade (p=0.47/0.52) [PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md).

## Open questions

- Functional validation of V553M and N540S as bona fide gatekeeper mutations remains to be established [PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md).
- Whether FGFR3-isoform-specific inhibitors (TYRA300, LOXO435) will overcome erdafitinib tolerability limits and resistance is unresolved [PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md).
