CPTAC Colon Adenocarcinoma Proteogenomics (coad_cptac_2019)

Overview

The first proteogenomic study of a prospectively collected human colon cancer cohort, produced by the Clinical Proteomic Tumor Analysis Consortium (CPTAC-2). 110 newly diagnosed, untreated colon adenocarcinoma patients (rectal tumors excluded) underwent primary surgery and contributed paired tumor and normal-adjacent tissue (NAT) profiled by whole-exome sequencing, copy-number inference, RNA-Seq, miRNA-Seq, label-free shotgun proteomics, and TMT-based global and phosphoproteomics. Raw genomics deposited in SRA BioProject PRJNA514017; raw proteomics in CPTAC Data Portal S045; processed matrices available at LinkedOmics (http://linkedomics.org/cptac-colon/).

Composition

  • 110 patients: 65 female, 45 male; mean age 65 (range 40–93); newly diagnosed, untreated; colon-only (rectal excluded).
  • Cancer type: COAD — colon adenocarcinoma only.
  • MSI status: 24 MSI-H tumors / 82 MSS tumors (bimodal MS-INDEL distribution; PCR-based MSI testing on 85 samples — 100% concordance with WXS-based calls).
  • Platforms per omics:
    • 106 tumor/normal-blood pairs by whole-exome sequencing (Illumina Nextera Rapid Capture Exome, HiSeq4000, ≥150× target coverage).
    • 105 tumor/normal pairs for copy-number inference (CopywriteR + GISTIC2).
    • 106 tumors with RNA-Seq and miRNA-Seq.
    • 100 tumors with label-free shotgun proteomics; 96 tumor/NAT pairs by TMT global proteomics and TMT phosphoproteomics.
    • Selected Reaction Monitoring (SRM) for targeted validation.
  • Cross-cohort validation: average mRNA profiles correlated with TCGA colorectal cohort at Pearson r=0.92; average label-free protein at r=0.96.

Assays / panels (linked)

  • Whole-exome sequencing — Illumina Nextera Rapid Capture Exome; ≥150× target coverage.
  • RNA-Seq — tumor transcriptomes; miRNA-Seq also performed.
  • TMT global proteomics — tandem-mass-tag quantitative proteomics on 96 tumor/NAT pairs.
  • TMT phosphoproteomics — phosphosite quantification on 96 tumor/NAT pairs.
  • CMS classifier — applied to assign consensus molecular subtypes (CMS1–4).

Papers using this cohort

  • PMID:31031003 — Vasaikar et al. 2019, Cell: CPTAC-2 proteogenomic characterization of colon cancer; first proteogenomic colon cohort integrating WXS, RNA-seq, and multi-level proteomics.

Notable findings derived from this cohort

  • WXS identified 64,010 somatic SNVs, 7,691 somatic INDELs, and 6,186 somatic microsatellite INDELs across 106 tumors; the MS-INDEL distribution was bimodal, partitioning the cohort into 24 MSI-H and 82 MSS tumors. PMID:31031003
  • Proteogenomic integration reclassified SOX9 as oncogenic: protein is significantly overexpressed in tumors vs matched NATs (p=1.02×10⁻¹⁰) despite frequent truncating mutations, because truncations cluster upstream of the K398 ubiquitin-degradation site, stabilizing the protein. PMID:31031003
  • RB1 is recurrently amplified; Rb protein is overexpressed (p=2.10×10⁻¹⁵) and hyperphosphorylated at E2F-regulating sites (T373/S807/S811/T826; average 1.84-fold higher in tumors vs NATs, p<2.2×10⁻¹⁶); CDK2 activity is the strongest predictor of phospho-Rb change (r=0.47, p=1.8×10⁻⁶), nominating CDK2 inhibition as a therapeutic target, particularly for CIN-subtype tumors. PMID:31031003
  • Four new significantly mutated genes in hypermutated samples — CASP5, RNF43, LTN1, BMPR2 (each in >50% of hypermutated tumors) — not previously reported by TCGA COAD. PMID:31031003
  • Proteomics-supported proteogenomic search recovered 88 putative HLA-I binding neoantigens across 38% of tumors; three recurrent cancer/testis antigens (IGF2BP3 51%, SPAG1 14%, ATAD2 8%) were MSI-status-independent — candidates for shared tumor-antigen vaccines in MSS patients. PMID:31031003
  • MSI-H tumors universally upregulated glycolytic enzymes at the protein level (Warburg effect); glycolytic enzyme abundance negatively correlated with CD8A infiltration (Spearman ρ=−0.61, p=0.02), absent in other subtypes — supporting glycolysis inhibition to overcome checkpoint-blockade resistance in MSI-H CRC. PMID:31031003
  • Network integration of CMS subtypes, proteomic subtypes, and MSI status yielded three unified molecular subtypes (UMS): MSI, CIN, and Mesenchymal; CDK inhibition predicted most effective in CIN (highest RB1 copy number and Rb phosphorylation). PMID:31031003
  • 31 proteins increased >2-fold in tumors vs NATs (cancer-associated proteins); 8 elevated in >95% of pairs: DDX21, S100A11, S100P, PLOD2, SERPINH1, GPRC5A and others; only 7 are in the Cancer Gene Census. PMID:31031003
