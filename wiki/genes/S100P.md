# S100P

## Overview

S100P is a member of the S100 family of calcium-binding proteins, originally identified as a protein highly expressed in placenta. S100P promotes cell survival, proliferation, and motility through interactions with RAGE and other receptors. It is overexpressed in several cancer types including pancreatic, breast, and colorectal cancer.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Among the most uniformly upregulated proteins across tumor-NAT pairs (≥95% of colon tumor samples), identified as a candidate biomarker/therapeutic target in colon cancer proteogenomics ([coad_cptac_2019](../datasets/coad_cptac_2019.md)) [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Colorectal cancer ([COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md)) — S100P protein uniformly upregulated in ≥95% of colon tumors vs normal adjacent tissue; one of 31 cancer-associated proteins with potential diagnostic, outcome, or therapeutic utility identified in CPTAC proteogenomics [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-upregulated with [S100A11](../genes/S100A11.md), [DDX21](../genes/DDX21.md), [PLOD2](../genes/PLOD2.md), [SERPINH1](../genes/SERPINH1.md), and [GPRC5A](../genes/GPRC5A.md) as the most uniformly overexpressed proteins in colon tumors [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Uniform overexpression in ≥95% of colon tumors makes S100P a candidate for biomarker and therapeutic follow-up; 19 of the 31 cancer-associated proteins identified in the same study are detectable in plasma as potential blood biomarkers [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Open questions

- Whether S100P overexpression is a driver or passenger event in colon tumorigenesis requires further functional and clinical validation.
