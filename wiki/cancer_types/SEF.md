# Sclerosing Epithelioid Fibrosarcoma (SEF)

## Overview

Sclerosing Epithelioid Fibrosarcoma is a rare, aggressive fibrosarcoma variant characterized by epithelioid cells embedded in a densely sclerotic stroma. It is frequently associated with [EWSR1](../genes/EWSR1.md)–CREB3L1 or EWSR1–CREB3L2 fusions and has histological overlap with Low-Grade Fibromyxoid Sarcoma. OncoTree classifies it under fibrosarcoma ([FIBS](../cancer_types/FIBS.md)).

## Cohorts in the corpus

- [sarcoma_mskcc_2022](../datasets/sarcoma_mskcc_2022.md): SEF was among the 45 OncoTree-mapped sarcoma entities profiled by MSK-IMPACT in 2,138 sarcomas [PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md).

## Recurrent alterations

- In the 2,138-sarcoma MSK-IMPACT cohort, sclerosing epithelioid fibrosarcoma was among the translocation-associated histotypes; EWSR1–CREB3L1/L2 fusions are defining molecular events; unsupervised clustering found that translocation-defined sarcomas have the lowest intra-subtype genomic entropy [PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md).

## Subtypes

- EWSR1–CREB3L1 fusion-positive vs EWSR1–CREB3L2 fusion-positive variants (the latter with overlap with LGFMS).

## Therapeutic landscape

- No Level 1 targeted therapy established; low TMB and MSI expected based on translocation-associated sarcoma class characteristics.
