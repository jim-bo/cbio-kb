# Meningioma (MNG)

## Overview

Meningioma (MNG) is the most common primary intracranial tumor, arising from the meninges. On OncoTree it is a child of MNGT (Meningeal Tumors). WHO grades range from 1 (benign) to 3 (anaplastic). Recurrence risk varies substantially by molecular features, and the 2021 WHO classification incorporates genomic criteria.

## Cohorts in the corpus

- 121 fresh-frozen meningiomas (discovery) and 80 validation meningiomas from the University Health Network Brain Tumor BioBank (Toronto), enriched for WHO grade 2 and 3 tumors; profiled by WES, EPIC methylation array, mRNA-seq, whole-cell proteomics (6,568 proteins), and single-nuclear RNA-seq (54,393 nuclei). Dataset: [mng_utoronto_2021](../datasets/mng_utoronto_2021.md). [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)

## Recurrent alterations

- [NF2](../genes/NF2.md) — mutations + chromosome 22q loss in 88% of MG1 (immunogenic) vs. 9% of MG2 (benign NF2-wildtype); biallelic inactivation is the dominant driver in aggressive meningiomas. [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)
- [TRAF7](../genes/TRAF7.md) — point mutations exclusive to MG2 (25%). [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)
- [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) — point mutations exclusive to MG2 (13%). [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)
- [KLF4](../genes/KLF4.md) — point mutations exclusive to MG2 (13%). [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)
- [SMO](../genes/SMO.md) — point mutations in copy-number-neutral subset of MG2. [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)
- [POLR2A](../genes/POLR2A.md) — point mutations exclusive to MG2 (6%). [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)
- [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md), [CHD2](../genes/CHD2.md) — novel recurrent driver mutations in chromatin-remodeling genes enriched in MG3/MG4 (3--5% frequency). [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — novel recurrent driver mutations enriched in MG3/MG4. [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md), [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md) — subthreshold recurrent inactivating mutations. [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)

## Subtypes

- Four integrative molecular groups (COCA integration of CNA, methylation, mRNA data): [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)
  - MG1 (immunogenic): NF2-mutant (88%), low CNA burden (median 3.5% genome altered); protein marker [S100B](../genes/S100B.md).
  - MG2 (benign NF2-wildtype): TRAF7/AKT1/KLF4/SMO/POLR2A mutations (Fisher exact P=1.20e-8); median 9.6% genome altered; protein marker [SCGN](../genes/SCGN.md).
  - MG3 (hypermetabolic): KDM6A/CHD2/PTEN enriched; median 16.9% genome altered; protein marker [ACADL](../genes/ACADL.md).
  - MG4 (proliferative): highest mutational burden (P=1.6e-3); median 19.5% genome altered; protein marker [MCM2](../genes/MCM2.md); FOXM1/MYC enrichment.
- MG classification outperformed WHO grade, methylation-based classification, and individual-datatype clustering for predicting time to recurrence (multivariable Cox, log-rank P=5e-15). [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)

## Therapeutic landscape

- Vorinostat (HDAC inhibitor) selectively reduced viability of MG4 (proliferative) patient-derived cell lines and attenuated tumor growth in MG4 intracranial xenografts, warranting further clinical investigation. [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)
- MG2 tumors harboring TRAF7/AKT1/KLF4/SMO mutations may be amenable to targeted therapies (PI3K/AKT, Hedgehog pathway). [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)
- Proteomic IHC markers (S100B, SCGN, ACADL, MCM2) could enable molecular group assignment in conventional neuropathology labs. [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)
