# CD44

## Overview

CD44 is a cell-surface glycoprotein and canonical cancer stem cell (CSC) marker involved in cell adhesion, migration, and signaling. It is frequently overexpressed or amplified in multiple cancer types and serves as a readout of EMT and stem-like cell state.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- EMT/stemness marker upregulated in [GBC](../cancer_types/GBC.md) cells (GBC-SD, NOZ) in response to paracrine [SEMA7A](../genes/SEMA7A.md) from stiff-matrix-activated cancer-associated fibroblasts; elevated in co-injection xenografts, downregulated by SEMA7A knockdown in GFs [PMID:24997986](../papers/24997986.md)
- Focal amplification in the CIN subtype of gastric adenocarcinoma; included among recurrent CIN copy-number gains [PMID:25079317](../papers/25079317.md)
- placeholder
- CD44 marks the tumor subpopulation harboring the FOXP3-like immunosuppressive signature in PTEN-mutant [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) (single-cell topological analysis); CD44+ tumor cells are the source of the regulatory-T-cell-like transcriptomic signature rather than [CD4](../genes/CD4.md)+[FOXP3](../genes/FOXP3.md)+ Tregs [PMID:30742119](../papers/30742119.md)
- Stem cell marker elevated in CRPC-SCL patient tumors and in CRPC-SCL cell lines; co-elevated with [TACSTD2](../genes/TACSTD2.md) (TROP2A); expression reduced upon YAP1/WWTR1 double knockdown ([prad_organoids_msk_2022](../datasets/prad_organoids_msk_2022.md)) [PMID:35617398](../papers/35617398.md)
