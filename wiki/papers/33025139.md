**PMID:** 33025139 · **DOI:** 10.1007/s00401-020-02230-x · **Journal:** Acta Neuropathologica (2021)

## TL;DR

Mansouri et al. assembled the largest multi-platform molecular profile of schwannomatosis-associated schwannomas (SWNTS-SWNs) to date — 165 tumors from 72 patients — and compared them to non-syndromic sporadic schwannomas (NS-SWNs). DNA methylation, whole-exome, whole-genome and RNA sequencing reveal that SWNTS-SWNs split into **four methylation subgroups tied to anatomic location**, carry higher copy-number burden (especially in [LZTR1](../genes/LZTR1.md)-mutant cases), and harbor the [SH3PXD2A](../genes/SH3PXD2A.md)–[HTRA1](../genes/HTRA1.md) fusion in ~14% of cases — predominantly in LZTR1-mutant tumors. Painful tumors show coordinated upregulation of RAS/MAPK, PIGF/VEGF, mTOR, and [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) pathways, nominating MEK inhibitors (e.g., [selumetinib](../drugs/selumetinib.md)) and anti-angiogenics (e.g., [bevacizumab](../drugs/bevacizumab.md)) as therapeutic candidates for tumor and pain management.

## Cohort & data

- **165 SWNs from 72 schwannomatosis patients** (F/M: 31/41), assembled through the Children's Tumor Foundation Synodos for Schwannomatosis Consortium and now released as cBioPortal study [schw_ctf_synodos_2025](../datasets/schw_ctf_synodos_2025.md) ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
- Comparator: non-syndromic sporadic schwannomas (NS-SWNs) and 90 neurofibromas (56 plexiform, 34 cutaneous) for [DNA methylation](../methods/epic-methylation-array.md) profiling ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
- Histology: [schwannoma](../cancer_types/SCHW.md) (peripheral nerve sheath tumor); related context for [MPNST](../cancer_types/MPNST.md) excluded.
- Assays per tumor: Illumina Infinium HumanMethylationEPIC ([epic-methylation-array](../methods/epic-methylation-array.md)); [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) on 29 SWNTS-SWNs + 25 NS-SWNs (Agilent SureSelect All Exon V5+UTRs, ≥250× tumor / 50× normal); [whole-genome sequencing](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) on 22 SWNTS-SWNs (Illumina HiSeqX, 60× tumor / 30× normal); [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) on 24 SWNTS-SWNs + 41 NS-SWNs (TruSeq Stranded Total RNA / Ribo-Zero Gold).
- Variant calling: [MuTect](../methods/mutect.md), [Strelka](../methods/strelka.md), HaplotypeCaller; SVs by [DELLY](../methods/delly.md) v0.8.1 with MAVIS validation; CN/LOH by [Sequenza](../methods/sequenza.md); fusion discovery by [FusionCatcher](../methods/fusioncatcher.md) with RT-PCR confirmation; focal CN peaks by [GISTIC](../methods/gistic.md); pathway analysis by [GSEA](../methods/gsea.md); immune deconvolution by [CIBERSORTx](../methods/cibersortx.md); [NF2](../genes/NF2.md) coding-region screen by [Sanger sequencing](../methods/sanger-sequencing.md) (ABI 3100/310); methylation clustering by ConsensusClusterPlus + Ward linkage + [t-SNE](../methods/tsne.md) on top 10,000 variably methylated CpGs ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
- Reference genome: hg19 (GRCh37) for DNA; hg38 for RNA-seq alignment.

## Key findings

- **DNA methylation separates SWNs from neurofibromas, not from NS-SWNs.** Unsupervised clustering of the top 2,000 most variably methylated CpGs cleanly partitioned SWNs (SWNTS + NS) from neurofibromas, but SWNTS-SWNs and NS-SWNs intermixed — supporting a shared Schwann-cell origin ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md), Fig. 1a–b).
- **Global hypomethylation in SWNTS-SWNs.** 75% of differentially methylated CpGs were hypomethylated in SWNTS-SWNs relative to NS-SWNs, concentrated in CpG islands and promoter regions. LINE-1 repetitive elements were significantly hypomethylated (Student's t, p = 0.0005), with corresponding 5.17-fold upregulation of [L1TD1](../genes/L1TD1.md) (q = 0.039) and higher [TET1](../genes/TET1.md)/[TET2](../genes/TET2.md) expression ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
- **Four methylation subgroups within SWNTS-SWNs**, stable on consensus clustering of N = 88, segregated by **anatomic location** (lower extremity, upper extremity, spinal, truncal, head & neck). Clusters 1–4 showed dominant upregulation of cAMP, [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), MEK/PIGF, and PIGF/VEGF programs respectively; tumors from the same patient at different anatomic sites resolved into different clusters, indicating origin-of-cell heterogeneity (Fig. 1d–f, [PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
- **Tumor mutation burden is low and similar across SWNTS-SWNs and NS-SWNs.** SWNTS-SWN: WES 0.17/Mb (5.92/sample), WGS 0.23/Mb (8.81/sample); NS-SWN: WES 0.22/Mb (6.08/sample). C>T transitions dominate; COSMIC signatures present in ≥2 SWNTS-SWNs include 1A ([MUTYH](../genes/MUTYH.md), 26/29), 6 (MMR, 7/29), 15 (MMR, 4/29), and 2 (APOBEC, 3/29). Signature 2 was absent from NS-SWNs; signature 20 appeared in 3/24 NS-SWNs ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
- **NF2 is the only recurrent driver SNV** but is more frequently hit in syndromic tumors. SWNTS-SWNs vs. NS-SWNs harboring [NF2](../genes/NF2.md) mutation + copy-number loss: **83% vs. 58%** (Chi-square, p < 0.0001). All germline LZTR1-mutant SWNTS-SWNs had either an NF2 mutation or deletion ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md), Fig. 2a).
- **22q deletions are far more common in SWNTS-SWNs**: 80% vs. 30% in NS-SWNs (Chi-square, p < 0.01), with **76% vs. 35%** of SWNTS-SWNs vs. NS-SWNs showing deep deletions across [LZTR1](../genes/LZTR1.md), [SMARCB1](../genes/SMARCB1.md), and [NF2](../genes/NF2.md). Other genes on 22q11.21–q11.23 — [MAPK1](../genes/MAPK1.md), [BCR](../genes/BCR.md), [EWSR1](../genes/EWSR1.md), [PATZ1](../genes/PATZ1.md), [ZNRF3](../genes/ZNRF3.md), [MYH9](../genes/MYH9.md) — were deleted in 77% and their expression significantly reduced ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
- **Three-hit / four-hit progression confirmed**, with 9/29 (31%) of SWNTS-SWN samples showing two hits (22q deletion) without somatic NF2 mutation — a pattern absent from NS-SWNs ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
- **Recurrent non-22q arm-level deletions in chromosomes 4, 16, 19, and 21 were specific to LZTR1-mutant tumors.** LZTR1-mutant SWNTS-SWNs carried ~9-fold higher CNV than LZTR1-wild-type (Student's t, p = 0.0072). Overall CNV in SWNTS-SWNs vs. NS-SWNs: WES 5.97% vs. 0.86%, WGS 2.40% (Student's t, p = 0.005) ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md), Fig. 2c–d).
- **Structural variants:** 1 recurrent inversion, 4 inter-chromosomal translocations, and 4 inter-chromosomal inverted translocations seen in ≥2/22 WGS tumors. Most frequent (3/22): inverted translocation between ZNF708 (Chr 19p) and ZNF138 (Chr 7q); significance unknown ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
- **[SH3PXD2A](../genes/SH3PXD2A.md)–[HTRA1](../genes/HTRA1.md) fusion confirmed in SWNTS-SWNs.** RT-PCR detected the fusion in **14% (≈3/22)** of SWNTS-SWNs, enriched in males (14/22, 63%), and **strongly associated with germline [LZTR1](../genes/LZTR1.md) mutation (15/22, 68%; Chi-square, p = 0.025)** ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)). The fusion was originally described in ~10% of sporadic schwannomas (Agnihotri et al. 2016, Nat Genet — not yet in this wiki corpus).
- **Other fusions:** NAIP–OCLN on Chr 5 was significantly higher in SWNTS-SWNs (67%) vs. NS-SWNs (29%) (Chi-square, p < 0.01). The [KANSL1](../genes/KANSL1.md)–[ARL17A](../genes/ARL17A.md) KANSARL predisposition fusion was present at similar rates in both groups ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
- **Transcriptomic upregulation in SWNTS-SWNs vs. NS-SWNs** of PIGF, VEGF, MEK, ERBB2, and [SHH](../genes/SHH.md) pathways; RB is the top downregulated pathway. Cell cycle, cell division, and DNA repair programs are coordinately upregulated; MMR/DNA-repair genes [MSH3](../genes/MSH3.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md), [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md), and [MLH3](../genes/MLH3.md) are significantly higher (Student's t, q < 0.01), with [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md) and [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md) showing concordant promoter hypomethylation. [LZTR1](../genes/LZTR1.md) and [SMARCB1](../genes/SMARCB1.md) transcripts are significantly reduced in SWNTS-SWNs (q < 0.001) ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md), Fig. 4).
- **Immune contexture differs from NS-SWNs.** SWNTS-SWNs have higher naïve B cells, plasma cells, and activated NK cells but **lower total macrophages and CD8+ T cells** (Student's t, p < 0.05). NF2-mutant SWNTS-SWNs are enriched for M2 macrophages (p < 0.01). HLA-A/-B/-C/-DPB1 expression is reduced; [CD28](../genes/CD28.md) and [CD80](../genes/CD80.md) are upregulated ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md), Fig. 5a–h, j).
- **Genomic correlates of pain.** Painful SWNTS-SWNs are enriched in: lower extremity location (58%, Chi-square p = 0.001), female sex (64%, z-score p < 0.001), germline LZTR1 mutation (57%, Chi-square p = 0.002), and SH3PXD2A-HTRA1 fusion (16%, z-score p = 0.039). They carry **4.4-fold higher CNV** (p = 0.042); TMB is not significantly different. Upregulated pathways in painful tumors: RAS/MAPK, PIGF/VEGF, ERBB2/HER2, RB/RAF, mTOR, MEK. Painful tumors also carry higher total mast-cell infiltrates (Student's t, p < 0.01) ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).

## Genes & alterations

- [LZTR1](../genes/LZTR1.md) — germline LZTR1 mutation defines a SWNTS-SWN subclass with markedly higher CNV (~9-fold), recurrent non-22q arm-level deletions (Chr 4/16/19/21), enrichment for SH3PXD2A-HTRA1 fusion, NF2 co-loss, painful clinical course, and upregulated RAS/MAPK + ERBB2/VEGF programs ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
- [NF2](../genes/NF2.md) — sole recurrent driver SNV across all SWNs; SWNTS-SWNs harbor NF2 mutation/loss in 83% vs. 58% in NS-SWNs (p < 0.0001). NF2-mutant tumors show RAS/MAPK upregulation and M2 macrophage enrichment ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
- [SMARCB1](../genes/SMARCB1.md) — co-deleted on 22q in 76% of SWNTS-SWNs; significantly lower transcript level than in NS-SWNs (q < 0.001) ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
- [SH3PXD2A](../genes/SH3PXD2A.md)–[HTRA1](../genes/HTRA1.md) — recurrent gene fusion in 14% of SWNTS-SWNs (3/22 WGS samples by RT-PCR), enriched in LZTR1-mutant and painful tumors; therapeutic rationale for MEK inhibition by analogy with sporadic schwannomas ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
- 22q neighbors of NF2/SMARCB1 — [MAPK1](../genes/MAPK1.md), [BCR](../genes/BCR.md), [EWSR1](../genes/EWSR1.md), [PATZ1](../genes/PATZ1.md), [ZNRF3](../genes/ZNRF3.md), [MYH9](../genes/MYH9.md): deleted in 77% of cases on 22q11.21–q11.23 with coordinated expression loss; proposed as additional contributors to schwannomatosis pathogenesis ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
- MMR / DNA-repair genes: [MSH3](../genes/MSH3.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md), [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md), [MLH3](../genes/MLH3.md) upregulated transcriptionally; [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md) and [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md) promoter-hypomethylated in SWNTS-SWNs; [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md)/[BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) also up. COSMIC signatures 6/15 are present but **no 22q microsatellite instability** is detected ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
- Demethylation machinery: [TET1](../genes/TET1.md), [TET2](../genes/TET2.md) upregulated; [DNMT1](../genes/DNMT1.md), [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md) unchanged. [L1TD1](../genes/L1TD1.md) (LINE-1-encoded) upregulated 5.17-fold (q = 0.039) ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
- Novel deleterious SNVs validated by Sanger in single cases: [MYPN](../genes/MYPN.md), [CAV3](../genes/CAV3.md), [SMOX](../genes/SMOX.md) — non-recurrent ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
- Other recurrent fusions: [NAIP](../genes/NAIP.md)–[OCLN](../genes/OCLN.md) on Chr 5 (67% SWNTS-SWN vs. 29% NS-SWN, p < 0.01); KANSARL ([KANSL1](../genes/KANSL1.md)–[ARL17A](../genes/ARL17A.md)) at similar rates in both groups ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).

## Clinical implications

- **MEK inhibition (e.g., [selumetinib](../drugs/selumetinib.md))** is nominated as a therapeutic strategy in SWNTS-SWNs, particularly tumors carrying germline LZTR1 mutation, somatic NF2 mutation, the SH3PXD2A-HTRA1 fusion, or anatomic location in extremities — all of which show coordinated RAS/MAPK upregulation. The authors note FDA approval of MEK inhibitors for plexiform neurofibromas in [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) (Gross et al. 2020, NEJM) as supporting precedent ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
- **Anti-angiogenic therapy** ([bevacizumab](../drugs/bevacizumab.md) / Avastin and PIGF-targeting compounds) is proposed for management of painful SWNTS-SWNs given PIGF/VEGF/RAF pathway upregulation in painful tumors. The authors specifically call for clinical trials with pain alleviation and quality-of-life as primary endpoints ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
- **mTOR inhibition** is suggested for chronic pain management, citing established mTOR involvement in pain initiation/maintenance and observed mTOR pathway upregulation in painful SWNTS-SWNs and tumors from extremities ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
- **Methylation-based subclassification (4 clusters by anatomic origin) may guide management.** Each subgroup has a distinct dominant pathway (cAMP / KRAS / MEK-PIGF / PIGF-VEGF) that could inform targeted therapy selection — even when tumors are histologically identical ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
- **Clinical risk stratification:** painful SWNTS-SWNs are statistically enriched in lower-extremity tumors from female patients with germline LZTR1 mutations and SH3PXD2A-HTRA1 fusion — a clinically tractable signature for prioritizing aggressive intervention ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Modest sequencing cohort sizes** despite being the largest schwannomatosis series to date: 29 WES + 22 WGS + 24 RNA-seq SWNTS-SWN samples — statistical power for rare-variant discovery is limited (e.g., MYPN/CAV3/SMOX SNVs were non-recurrent) ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
- **Inferred mechanism for MMR-gene upregulation** (promoter hypomethylation) without direct demonstration; the source of low-level COSMIC signatures 6/15 in MSI-negative tumors is not resolved ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
- **Significance of the recurrent ZNF708–ZNF138 inverted translocation is unknown**; no functional follow-up ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
- **Therapeutic claims are correlative**, not validated in preclinical models or trials within this paper; the MEK / VEGF / mTOR proposals require prospective evaluation ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
- **No NF2-mutant germline cases were enrolled** (intentional, to focus on SWNTS), so the molecular boundary between SWNTS and mosaic NF2 disease remains incompletely separated ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
- **Reference-genome split** (DNA aligned to hg19, RNA aligned to hg38) is documented but introduces potential coordinate-conversion artifacts in multi-omic integration ([PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)).
