# ZNF442

## Overview

ZNF442 encodes a zinc finger protein of the C2H2-type family; its normal transcriptional regulatory function is not well characterized. In cancer genomics, ZNF442 has been identified as a candidate coding driver in metastatic cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma ([CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md)), called by multiple independent driver-gene detection tools in a whole-genome sequencing cohort.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Candidate coding driver in metastatic [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md) (n=25 WGS); called by ≥2 driver tools (MutSigCV, OncodriveFML, dNdScv); identified alongside [KHDRBS2](../genes/KHDRBS2.md), [COLEC12](../genes/COLEC12.md), [SLC22A6](../genes/SLC22A6.md), [LINGO2](../genes/LINGO2.md), [CDHR5](../genes/CDHR5.md), [PRLR](../genes/PRLR.md), and [DHRS4](../genes/DHRS4.md) as recurrent candidates in a high-TMB UV-signature cohort [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **CSCC (cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma):** Recurrently mutated in metastatic disease context characterized by UV-induced high TMB [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-identified with other metastatic CSCC candidate drivers ([KHDRBS2](../genes/KHDRBS2.md), [COLEC12](../genes/COLEC12.md), [SLC22A6](../genes/SLC22A6.md)) in the same WGS cohort; precise co-occurrence patterns not reported at individual-sample resolution [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No established therapeutic relevance; no targeted agents known.

## Open questions

- Functional role in CSCC tumorigenesis is unknown; whether mutations cause loss-of-function, gain-of-function, or are passenger events driven by the high UV-mutational load requires experimental follow-up.
