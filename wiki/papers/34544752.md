**PMID:** 34544752 · **DOI:** 10.1158/2159-8290.CD-20-1726 · **Journal:** Cancer Discovery (2022)

## TL;DR

Li et al. dissect why CDK4/6 inhibitor (CDK4/6i) resistance in ER+ breast cancer often co-occurs with [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md) upregulation despite both kinases being equally bound by abemaciclib/palbociclib in vitro. By coimmunoprecipitation and mass spectrometry on isogenic MCF7 FAT1-knockout (CDK6-high) cells, they identify p15INK4B and p18INK4C as CDK6-selective binding partners that lock CDK6 into a kinase-active but drug-insensitive conformation: MST shows p18 increases the Kd of [abemaciclib](../drugs/abemaciclib.md) for CDK6 four-fold, and structural modeling explains an ~80× greater INK4-induced shrinkage of the inhibitor pocket vs the ATP pocket. In an MSK-IMPACT cohort of 1,366 metastatic HR+/HER2– breast tumors from 1,115 patients, 190/1,115 (~17%) carried alterations in genes ([FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), LATS1/2, [YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), CDK6, [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)) that converge on CDK6/INK4 upregulation. To bypass the INK4-locked pocket, the authors built palbociclib-based bifunctional degraders (PROTACs); lead compounds BSJ-05-017 (VHL-recruiting) and BSJ-03-096 (CRBN-recruiting) degrade both [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) and CDK6, induce senescence in resistant cells, and shrink CDK6-overexpressing and PTEN-loss MCF7 xenografts that grow through [ribociclib](../drugs/ribociclib.md).

## Cohort & data

- **Clinical cohort:** 1,366 metastatic tumors from 1,115 patients with HR+/HER2– metastatic breast cancer ([BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md)) profiled by [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) between April 2014 and March 2020 under MSK IRB protocol NCT01775072; sequencing data deposited as cBioPortal study [breast_ink4_msk_2021](../datasets/breast_ink4_msk_2021.md).
- **Cell line models:** MCF7 (parental and FAT1 CRISPR knockout, "FAT1CR"), T47D, ZR-75-1, CAMA1, EFM19, BT474, plus HEK293T; all ATCC-sourced and STR-authenticated.
- **PDX panel:** 14 ER+ breast cancer patient-derived xenografts (Vall d'Hebron Institute of Oncology / V. Serra) scored by [IHC](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md) for FAT1, CDK6, YAP, p15INK4B, p18INK4C and tested for CDK4/6i sensitivity in vivo.
- **Functional screens:** PRISM cell-line viability screen (~931 barcoded lines, Broad) for [palbociclib](../drugs/palbociclib.md) biomarkers; CDK4/CDK6 co-IP coupled with [LC-MS/MS proteomics](../methods/lc-ms-ms-proteomics.md); ADP-Glo in vitro kinase assays; microscale thermophoresis (MST) for binding affinity; molecular dynamics simulations of PROTAC ternary complexes (Amber14SB, OpenMM 7.4.2; code at github.com/choderalab/CDK_PROTAC).

## Key findings

- **CDK6 in resistant cells is INK4-bound but still active.** ADP-Glo kinase assays on CDK4 and CDK6 IPs from MCF7 parental vs MCF7 FAT1CR show abemaciclib (100 nmol/L) inhibits CDK4 activity by 84% (parental) and 82% (resistant) but reduces CDK6 activity by only 48% in resistant cells (P = 0.0485 vs untreated; P = 0.0003 vs parental) ([PMID:34544752](../papers/34544752.md), Fig. 1B).
- **CDK6, not CDK4, recruits INK4 proteins.** Three-replicate IP-MS in FAT1CR cells identifies seven CDK6-selective interactors; p15INK4B ([CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md), 6 unique peptides) and p18INK4C ([CDKN2C](../genes/CDKN2C.md), 4 unique peptides) are the top two CDK6-only INK4 family members, with zero CDKN2B/CDKN2C peptides in the CDK4 IP (Fig. 1E, F).
- **Disrupting the CDK6–INK4 interface restores drug sensitivity.** Interface mutants CDK6-V16D and CDK6-R31C (designed from PDB 1BI7/1BI8/1G3N) disrupt p15/p18 binding without abolishing kinase activity; cells expressing R31C have abemaciclib IC50 = 32.28 nmol/L vs 174.98 nmol/L for CDK6-WT overexpression (51.76 nmol/L parental) (Fig. 1H–J).
- **Knockout of CDKN2C alone partially restores RB-phosphorylation suppression in FAT1CR cells; double knockout of CDKN2B + CDKN2C almost entirely restores it** (Fig. 1K). Long-term growth: p15+p18 double-KO renders FAT1-loss cells sensitive to 100 nmol/L abemaciclib over 21 days (P < 0.0001 vs FAT1CR alone) (Fig. 1L).
- **INK4 binding deforms CDK6 to selectively shrink the drug pocket.** Computational docking on PDB 2EUF, 1BI7, 1BI8, 1G3N: p18 binding reduces the abemaciclib-binding pocket volume by 87.65% and the palbociclib pocket by 85.03%, but the AMP-PNP (ATP-analog) pocket by only 0.54%. p16 yields −87.44% / −78.70% (abemaciclib/palbociclib) vs −7.64% for AMP-PNP; p19 yields −62.36% vs −32.05% (Fig. 2D, E).
- **MST confirms reduced inhibitor affinity:** abemaciclib Kd to CDK6 alone = 20.2 ± 0.9 nmol/L, rising to 86.4 ± 0.5 nmol/L in the presence of 250 nmol/L p18 (~4× increase) (Fig. 2F).
- **CDK6 upregulation has several upstream genetic drivers.** In 14 ER+ PDX models, 8/14 had high CDK6 by IHC, and 7/8 were CDK4/6i-resistant; high-CDK6 tumors were enriched for low FAT1, high nuclear YAP1, and high p15/p18 (Fig. 3A–C). [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) and [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) knockdown in MCF7 each upregulate CDK6 and confer abemaciclib resistance; shYAP rescues abemaciclib sensitivity in shARID1A cells (Fig. 3F, G, I).
- **Cohort-level prevalence:** 190/1,115 (17%) HR+/HER2– metastatic breast cancer patients in the MSK-IMPACT cohort carry at least one alteration in a CDK6-axis gene — [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md) 0.4%, [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) 4%, [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md) 1.6%, [LATS1](../genes/LATS1.md) 0.5%, [LATS2](../genes/LATS2.md) 0.8%, [YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md) 0.5%, [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) 8%, [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) 8% (Fig. 3J).
- **PROTAC degraders overcome the INK4-locked complex.** BSJ-05-017 (palbociclib–[VHL](../genes/VHL.md)) and BSJ-03-096 (palbociclib–CRBN) degrade both CDK4 and CDK6 at doses ≥10 nmol/L (Fig. 4B). BSJ-05-017 has IC50 of 12.37 nmol/L in parental MCF7 and 7.37 nmol/L in CDK6-high cells (i.e., equipotent across sensitive and resistant), vs palbociclib IC50 19.01 nmol/L (parental) and 252.0 nmol/L (CDK6-high) (Fig. 4D). BSJ-05-017 induces senescence in 68.26% of FAT1CR cells vs 12.54% for palbociclib and 4.35% for DMSO (Fig. 4E).
- **In vivo efficacy in CDK4/6i-resistant xenografts:** in MCF7 CDK6-overexpressing and shPTEN xenografts, BSJ-03-096 (50 mg/kg PO daily) and BSJ-05-017 (50 mg/kg IP daily) suppress tumor volume by 68.9%/64.9% (CDK6-OE) and 54.9%/47.4% (shPTEN) over 25–35 days, while ribociclib (25 mg/kg PO) shows only transient response followed by outgrowth (Fig. 4G). Pharmacodynamic analysis at 25 mg/kg IP BSJ-05-017 for 3 days shows near-complete CDK4 and CDK6 degradation and loss of phospho-RB and [E2F1](../genes/E2F1.md) in resistant tumors (Fig. 4F).

## Genes & alterations

- [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md) — central node. Upregulation (amplification 0.4% of the cohort, or transcriptional induction downstream of FAT1/PTEN/ARID1A loss) drives resistance; INK4-binding interface residues V16, R31, K29 (from PDB-derived analysis) are required for the resistant phenotype; mutations V16D and R31C disrupt INK4 binding and re-sensitize cells.
- [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) — fully inhibited by CDK4/6i in both sensitive and resistant cells; does not bind INK4 in resistant cells (zero peptides in IP-MS). Co-degradation of CDK4 + CDK6 is required for sustained antitumor activity.
- [CDKN2C](../genes/CDKN2C.md) (p18INK4C) and [CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md) (p15INK4B) — the dominant INK4 binders to CDK6 in resistant MCF7 FAT1CR cells; CRISPR knockout (single guides ACGGAGTCAACCGTTTCGGG, CTCCACTAGTCCCCGCGCCG for p15; GAATGACAGCGAAACCAGTT, TTAACATCGAGGATAATGAA for p18) restores abemaciclib response.
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) (p16INK4A) — absent endogenously in MCF7 parental; ectopic p16 overexpression in T47D lowers abemaciclib and palbociclib potency. Also flagged in the PRISM screen: high CDKN2A mRNA correlates with poor palbociclib response.
- [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md) — loss-of-function alterations (1.6% of cohort) drive CDK6 upregulation via Hippo-pathway derepression; CRISPR target sequence CACGGTGACGTTGTACTCGG used for the FAT1CR isogenic model. (Mechanism previously established by Li et al., Cancer Cell 2018, cited as ref. 8.)
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — loss (8% of cohort) upregulates CDK6 via AKT activation; the AKT inhibitor [MK-2206](../drugs/mk-2206.md) (2 µmol/L) reverses CDK6 induction in PTEN-knockdown cells.
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) — loss (8% of cohort) upregulates CDK6 in a YAP1-dependent manner; shYAP1 in shARID1A cells lowers CDK6 expression and re-sensitizes to abemaciclib (IC50 shifts from 135.5 → 9.45 nmol/L).
- [YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md), [LATS1](../genes/LATS1.md), [LATS2](../genes/LATS2.md) — Hippo-pathway alterations (combined 1.8%) implicated in CDK6 upregulation.
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — loss (4% of cohort) is the canonical CDK4/6i-resistance lesion (downstream of CDK4/6) and was also a top hit in the PRISM screen for palbociclib insensitivity.
- [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md) and [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) — cited (refs 44, 46) as additional reported CDK4/6i resistance mechanisms that operate outside the CDK6–INK4 axis tested here.

## Clinical implications

- **Biomarker hypothesis:** the 17% of HR+/HER2– metastatic breast cancer patients in the MSK-IMPACT cohort with alterations in the CDK6/INK4 axis (FAT1, PTEN, ARID1A, LATS1/2, YAP1, CDK6 amp, RB1 loss) may be intrinsically or rapidly resistant to current ATP-competitive CDK4/6i (palbociclib, ribociclib, abemaciclib).
- **Therapeutic strategy:** CDK4 + CDK6 bifunctional degraders (CDK4/6-PROTACs) can re-engage the resistant CDK6–INK4 complex via protein–protein contacts on the E3-ligase surface that bypass the distorted ATP-pocket; BSJ-05-017 and BSJ-03-096 are tool compounds demonstrating proof-of-concept in MCF7 xenografts.
- **PK rationale for clinical translation:** at 20–25 mg/kg in mice, BSJ-05-017 (i.p.) and BSJ-03-096 (PO) achieve Cmax of 2.6 µmol/L and 0.9 µmol/L respectively, with 24-h plasma levels near 100 nmol/L (above the in vitro degradation IC50).
- **Diagnostic implication:** the authors propose that CDK4/6i selection should be informed by tumor CDK6/INK4 status (IHC or genomic markers of FAT1/PTEN/ARID1A loss).

## Limitations & open questions

- The 17% prevalence figure aggregates several genes whose individual contributions to CDK6-mediated resistance have not been independently validated in patients (e.g., LATS1/2 alterations are very rare; YAP1 alteration alone has not been clinically linked to CDK4/6i failure).
- INK4-mediated resistance was demonstrated in ER+ breast cancer cell lines and PDX models; whether the same mechanism explains CDK4/6i failure in HER2+ breast cancer, mantle cell lymphoma, or future CDK4/6i indications is untested.
- The PROTAC compounds were evaluated only in subcutaneous MCF7-derived xenografts; safety and tolerability (especially neutropenia given full CDK4/6 degradation) remain unknown.
- The relative contribution of [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), and [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) alterations (cited but not assayed) to non-CDK6-axis resistance is acknowledged as untested by this work.
- Whether degrading both CDK4 and CDK6 is necessary (vs CDK6-only) for efficacy in CDK6-high tumors — the CDK6-selective degrader BSJ-03-123 alone did not produce long-term growth suppression — is left as an open mechanistic question.
