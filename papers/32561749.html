TL;DR

Pollard et al. describe the Johns Hopkins Comprehensive Neurofibromatosis Center (JHCNC) NF1 biospecimen repository — a clinically and genomically annotated collection of nerve sheath tumors and patient-derived models from patients with neurofibromatosis type 1 (NF1). The first data release comprises whole-exome and/or RNA sequencing of 55 tumor samples from 23 unique patients, including matched germline DNA, derived cell lines, and patient-derived xenografts (PDX). Tumor histologies span cutaneous neurofibromas (cNF), plexiform neurofibromas (pNF), atypical neurofibromatous neoplasms of uncertain biologic potential (ANNUBP), and malignant peripheral nerve sheath tumors (MPNST). The cohort is the cBioPortal study nst_nfosi_ntap and all primary data are released via the NF Data Portal / Synapse (syn4939902).

Cohort & data

  • 23 NF1 patients, 55 tumor samples total — sequencing matrix in the paper’s Table 3 covers normal/germline DNA plus tumor, derived cell line, and PDX channels for each patient where available PMID:32561749.
  • Histologies: cutaneous neurofibroma (cNF), superficial diffuse infiltrating neurofibroma, plexiform neurofibroma (pNF) (NFIB), ANNUBP, and MPNST PMID:32561749.
  • Assays:
    • whole-exome-seq — Agilent SureSelectXT HumanAllExon V6 capture, Illumina HiSeq2500, 125-bp paired-end; SNV calling with BWA-MEM (0.7.15) → GATK v4.0.1.1 VQSR → Annovar annotation, plus DeepVariant 0.8 cross-check; CNA via the GATK copy-ratio pipeline. Reference: 1000 Genomes phase 2 (GRCh37) PMID:32561749.
    • rna-seq — paired-end on flash-frozen tumor; Salmon 0.11.3 quantification against gencode v29; raw counts harmonized with prior pNF Schwann-cell RNA-seq resources. One sample (2-025 Neurofibroma) was dropped for >90% duplication, leaving 28 RNA-seq samples PMID:32561749.
    • patient-derived-xenograft — tumor fragments dipped in Matrigel and implanted subcutaneously / pre-tibial / pre-sacral in NOD SCID gamma (NSG) mice; PDX take rate 50% for MPNST PMID:32561749.
  • Dataset: released as cBioPortal study nst_nfosi_ntap; primary data hosted on Synapse at syn4939902 (project URL http://synapse.org/jhubiobank); code at github.com/Sage-Bionetworks/JHU-biobank with a Zenodo-archived release (DOI 10.5281/zenodo.3726380) PMID:32561749.
  • Reference genome: GRCh37 (hg19) PMID:32561749.
  • A second cohort (51 additional WES + 28 additional RNA-seq samples) was described as being processed at the time of publication PMID:32561749.

Key findings

  • This is a data descriptor, not a hypothesis-testing study; primary deliverable is a paired clinical/genomic resource for the NF research community covering cNF → pNF → ANNUBP → MPNST progression specimens, with derived cell-line and PDX models for ex vivo and in vivo work PMID:32561749.
  • Genetic fidelity of derived models. Mutational profiles of commonly mutated genes in plexiform neurofibroma and MPNST — including NF1, CDKN2A, TP53, EED/SUZ12, and ATRX — were preserved between the original tumor, the patient-derived cell line, and the PDX (Fig. 3a) PMID:32561749.
  • PRC2 inactivation tracks histology. Mutations in PRC2-complex genes (EED, SUZ12) were “notably absent, other than in the single MPNST sample” in this first-release cohort — consistent with prior reports that PRC2 loss is restricted to malignant transformation rather than benign neurofibromas PMID:32561749.
  • Copy-ratio recapitulation in models. For patient 2-031, the MPNST tumor showed a chromosome 17 copy-ratio reduction at the NF1 locus (CR of blood = 1, CR of tumor = 0.5), and this 50% copy loss was preserved in both the derived cell line and the xenograft (Fig. 3c–d) PMID:32561749.
  • RNA-seq quality. Per-sample z-scored gene counts were similarly distributed across all 28 retained RNA-seq samples; PCA grouped samples by tumor type with consistent clustering across tumor tissue, cell line, and xenograft sample shapes (Fig. 4) PMID:32561749.

Genes & alterations

  • NF1 — germline driver of the disease; somatic biallelic inactivation (e.g. 50% copy ratio at the chromosome 17 NF1 locus in patient 2-031 MPNST) is the signature alteration tracked across tumor → cell line → PDX PMID:32561749.
  • CDKN2A — included in the panel of commonly mutated genes plotted across the WES cohort; loss in malignant peripheral nerve sheath tumors (vs neurofibromas) is the historical prior the authors anchor their plot to PMID:32561749.
  • TP53 — among the commonly mutated genes profiled in the cohort’s MPNST/pNF gene set PMID:32561749.
  • EED and SUZ12 — PRC2-complex tumor suppressors; in this first-release cohort, only the single MPNST sample carried a PRC2 alteration, consistent with PRC2 loss being a marker of malignant transformation PMID:32561749.
  • ATRX — included in the panel of commonly mutated genes used to evaluate fidelity of derived models PMID:32561749.

Clinical implications

  • This paper does not claim a biomarker or therapy. Its clinical implication is resource-level: it removes a long-standing bottleneck in NF1 nerve-sheath-tumor research by releasing matched tumor / cell line / PDX models with paired germline + somatic sequencing, enabling downstream biomarker and preclinical-drug studies (especially for plexiform neurofibroma, where surgery has been the only therapeutic option) PMID:32561749.
  • 50% MPNST PDX take rate provides a concrete planning number for future preclinical experiments PMID:32561749.

Limitations & open questions

  • Small cohort. 23 patients / 55 samples; only a single MPNST sample contributes to the malignant arm, so PRC2-loss frequency estimates from this dataset alone are not interpretable — the finding is consistent with prior literature but not powered to refine it PMID:32561749.
  • Sample dropout. One Neurofibroma RNA-seq sample (2-025) was excluded for >90% read duplication, dropping the RNA-seq cohort to 28 samples PMID:32561749.
  • Heterogeneous germline source. When patient blood was unavailable, normal adjacent tissue was used as the germline reference, which may carry occult somatic alterations PMID:32561749.
  • No treatment / outcome arm. As a biospecimen-and-sequencing descriptor, the paper does not link genomic features to therapy response or survival; that work is deferred to downstream users of the resource PMID:32561749.
  • Access model. Data are released via Synapse under a request-for-access workflow rather than open download, which is appropriate for the consent posture but adds friction for re-analysis PMID:32561749.
