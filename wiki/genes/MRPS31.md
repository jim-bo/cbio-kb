# MRPS31

## Overview

MRPS31 (Mitochondrial Ribosomal Protein S31) is a component of the mitochondrial small ribosomal subunit. In cutaneous melanoma, MRPS31 was identified as a significantly mutated gene (SMG) with UV-induced 5′ UTR hot-spots, a novel class of mutations affecting ribosomal protein genes. Its function as a melanoma driver remains to be validated.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- MRPS31 identified as a ribosomal-protein SMG with UV-induced 5′ UTR hot-spots (~5% frequency) in cutaneous melanoma (TCGA 333-sample multi-platform cohort); nominated alongside [RPS27](../genes/RPS27.md) (~9%) as a novel class of melanoma SMGs with 5′ UTR mutations affecting translation. [PMID:26091043](../papers/26091043.md)
- Functional characterisation as a melanoma driver is pending; listed among genes where novel SMG status is not yet functionally validated. [PMID:26091043](../papers/26091043.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md)**: Significantly mutated at ~5% frequency with UV-signature 5′ UTR hot-spots; part of a novel ribosomal-protein SMG class in the TCGA cutaneous melanoma dataset. [PMID:26091043](../papers/26091043.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-identified with [RPS27](../genes/RPS27.md) as a ribosomal-protein SMG; [RPS27](../genes/RPS27.md) 5′ TOP element regulates translation downstream of PI3K/AKT/mTOR. [PMID:26091043](../papers/26091043.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting reported in the corpus. [PMID:26091043](../papers/26091043.md)

## Open questions

- Whether MRPS31 5′ UTR mutations alter translational regulation and contribute oncogenic function in melanoma requires functional validation. [PMID:26091043](../papers/26091043.md)
