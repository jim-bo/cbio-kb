# ACLY

## Overview

ACLY (ATP-citrate lyase) converts citrate to acetyl-CoA, linking the TCA cycle to de novo lipogenesis. In [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md), ACLY suppresses cGAS-STING signaling and PD-L1 expression, conferring resistance to anti-PD-L1 therapy.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- ACLY upregulated in HCC; suppresses cGAS-STING/PD-L1 pathway, conferring anti-PD-L1 resistance; part of the DNL effector axis [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) — ACLY drives DNL and anti-PD-L1 resistance via cGAS-STING suppression [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Part of the DNL axis with [FASN](../genes/FASN.md), [SCD](../genes/SCD.md), [ACACA](../genes/ACACA.md) in HCC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- ACLY inhibition (e.g., bempedoic acid) may restore cGAS-STING/PD-L1 signaling and resensitize HCC to ICIs [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Open questions

- The paradox of DNL both promoting and suppressing PD-L1 (depending on the gene targeted) remains unresolved in the corpus [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
