# ATF1

## Overview

ATF1 (Activating Transcription Factor 1) is a member of the CREB/ATF family of basic leucine zipper transcription factors. In cancer genomics, ATF1 is most notable as a fusion partner with [EWSR1](../genes/EWSR1.md), forming the EWSR1-ATF1 fusion oncoprotein characteristic of clear cell sarcoma and related tumors. The EWSR1-ATF1 fusion disrupts normal transcriptional programs and has diagnostic and therapeutic implications in rare malignancies.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- EWSR1-ATF1 fusion identified in a clear cell odontogenic carcinoma ([CCOC](../cancer_types/CCOC.md)); novel finding in odontogenic tumors enabling histologic reclassification [PMID:27442865](../papers/27442865.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Clear cell odontogenic carcinoma (CCOC): EWSR1-ATF1 fusion reported as a novel finding enabling tumor reclassification [PMID:27442865](../papers/27442865.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- EWSR1 is the canonical fusion partner; EWSR1-ATF1 fusions are diagnostically definitive in clear cell sarcoma and related entities [PMID:27442865](../papers/27442865.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- EWSR1-ATF1 fusion in clear cell odontogenic carcinoma nominated as a diagnostic and potentially therapeutic target; analogous fusions in clear cell sarcoma may respond to targeted approaches under investigation [PMID:27442865](../papers/27442865.md)

## Open questions

- Whether EWSR1-ATF1 fusions in odontogenic tumors share therapeutic vulnerabilities with clear cell sarcoma EWSR1-ATF1 fusions remains to be established.
