# acitretin

## Overview

Acitretin is an aromatic synthetic retinoid and retinoic acid receptor (RAR) agonist used systemically for psoriasis and other keratinization disorders. It is not a cytotoxic agent but modulates keratinocyte proliferation and differentiation. Acitretin is teratogenic and is contraindicated in pregnancy. Its use as prior immunosuppressive or anti-psoriatic therapy is relevant in skin-cancer-risk studies because immunosuppression is itself a risk factor for keratinocyte cancers.

## Evidence in the corpus

- Acitretin was recorded as a prior immunosuppressive/anti-psoriatic exposure in individual patients in the NB-UVB phototherapy mutation-burden cohort (psoriasis patients receiving NB-UVB at a UK dermatology centre); it was not an intervention tested in this study and was not shown to affect the mutation-burden or mutational-signature outcomes.
