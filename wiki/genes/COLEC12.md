# COLEC12

## Overview

COLEC12 (Collectin Subfamily Member 12, also known as CL-P1 or SRCL) encodes a type-II transmembrane scavenger receptor with collagen-like and lectin domains. It is implicated in innate immune recognition and phagocytic clearance via the tumor microenvironment. COLEC12 has emerged as a candidate coding driver in metastatic cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma ([CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md)), where it appears to be enriched in metastatic relative to primary disease, suggesting a potential role in metastatic progression.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Candidate coding driver in metastatic [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md): called by ≥2 of three driver-detection tools (OncodriveFML, MutSigCV, dNdScv) in a WGS study of 25 lymph node metastases; COLEC12 and [SLC22A6](../genes/SLC22A6.md) were enriched in metastatic vs primary CSCC on cBioPortal cross-cohort comparison (primary 25%, metastatic 60%); enrichment in TGF-beta regulation of extracellular matrix pathway [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md): Candidate coding driver; altered in ~60% of metastatic CSCC vs ~25% of primary CSCC samples on cBioPortal; part of TGF-beta/ECM pathway enrichment in metastatic nodal disease [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with [LUM](../genes/LUM.md), [CDHR5](../genes/CDHR5.md), and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) in the TGF-beta regulation of extracellular matrix pathway enrichment in metastatic CSCC [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- TGF-beta receptor inhibition with chemotherapy or immunotherapy is proposed as a candidate strategy based on pathway enrichment including COLEC12 — no direct clinical evidence yet [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Open questions

- Functional validation of COLEC12 as a bona fide CSCC driver is outstanding; mechanism linking COLEC12 mutation to TGF-beta/ECM remodeling and metastatic dissemination is inferred from cross-cancer literature rather than CSCC-specific functional studies [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)
