**PMID:** 27161491 · **DOI:** 10.1038/ncomms11479 · **Journal:** Nature Communications (2016)

## TL;DR

Pereira, Chin, Rueda et al. targeted-sequenced 173 breast-cancer genes in 2,433 primary tumours from the [METABRIC](../datasets/brca_metabric.md) cohort that already had matched copy-number, expression, and long-term clinical follow-up data (median 115 months). Using a [ratiometric driver-discovery scheme](../methods/vogelstein-ratiometric.md), they identified 40 mutation-driver (Mut-driver) genes (22 ER+ only, 3 ER- only, 15 shared), characterised co-mutation/mutual-exclusivity patterns, mapped Mut-driver mutations onto the 10 [Integrative Clusters (IntClusts)](../methods/gistic.md), estimated cancer-cell fractions to score clonality, and computed [MATH](../methods/math-score.md) intra-tumour heterogeneity scores. The headline clinical result is that [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) mutations carry distinct prognostic value depending on the IntClust background — they are associated with shorter breast-cancer-specific survival in ER+ tumours belonging to IntClust1, IntClust2, and IntClust9 (each driven by amplification of 17q23, 11q13–14, or 8q24 respectively) but not in other ER+ IntClusts. High MATH scores were also associated with worse outcome in ER+ disease, except in the highly aggressive IntClust2 (CCND1/PAK1 11q13–14 co-amplification) which paradoxically showed low intra-tumour heterogeneity.

## Cohort & data

- 2,433 primary breast tumours ([BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md)), with 2,319 patients (95%) carrying long-term follow-up data (median 115 months). Source cohorts pooled in [METABRIC](../datasets/brca_metabric.md): METABRIC, NeoTango, Nottingham, and DETECT ([PMID:27161491](../papers/27161491.md)).
- 650 normal samples (523 normal-adjacent breast, 127 peripheral blood); 548 matched tumour/normal pairs; 221 primary tumours sequenced in replicate.
- Targeted capture of 173 genes (~1.2 Mbp) selected from 5 large 2012 breast-cancer sequencing studies plus genes recurrently homozygously deleted in the prior METABRIC CNA analysis. Method: Illumina Nextera Custom Target Enrichment + paired-end 100 bp HiSeq 2000 sequencing — see [metabric-targeted-sequencing](../methods/metabric-targeted-sequencing.md). Mean depth ≥112× in 80% of samples (median 152×).
- ER status assigned by IHC where available, reconciled with bimodal [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) gaussian-mixture expression calls; [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) expression similarly used to corroborate HER2 IHC.
- ER+ n=1,780; ER- n=630 (status available for 2,410 tumours).
- Driver discovery: [Vogelstein 20/20-style ratiometric scheme](../methods/vogelstein-ratiometric.md) requiring ≥5 recurrent or inactivating mutations, with ER-stratified ONC ≥20% (oncogene) and TSG ≥20% with ONC ≥5% (tumour suppressor) cut-offs.
- LOH calls from [ASCAT](../methods/ascat.md); recurrent CNAs from [GISTIC2](../methods/gistic.md); MATH scores per [Mroz & Rocco](../methods/math-score.md); panel-of-normals filtering analogous to [MuTect](../methods/mutect.md).

## Key findings

- **Mutation landscape.** 32,476 somatic mutations called; 13,084 protein-affecting (11,006 SNVs — 10,193 missense, 808 nonsense, 5 read-through; 1,635 indels — 1,315 frameshift, 320 in-frame; plus 443 splice-site variants). Mean 13 mutations per tumour (5 coding); 131 tumours had ≥30 mutations and 38 had no mutations at all (76 had no coding mutations).
- **Top recurrently mutated genes.** Coding mutation frequencies: [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) 40.1%, [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) 35.4%, [MUC16](../genes/MUC16.md) 16.8%, [AHNAK2](../genes/AHNAK2.md) 16.2%, [SYNE1](../genes/SYNE1.md) 12.0%, [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) 11.4%, [GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md) 11.1% ([PMID:27161491](../papers/27161491.md)).
- **Pathogenic germline mutations.** [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) 1.36%, [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) 1.64%, [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md) 2.22%, [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) 0.82% of the cohort.
- **40 Mut-driver genes** identified (22 ER+ only, 3 ER- only, 15 shared; only 6 oncogenes); list spans Akt-signalling ([PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md), [FOXO3](../genes/FOXO3.md)), cell-cycle ([RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [CDKN1B](../genes/CDKN1B.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)), chromatin function ([KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [NCOR1](../genes/NCOR1.md), [CTCF](../genes/CTCF.md), [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md), [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md), [TBL1XR1](../genes/TBL1XR1.md)), DNA damage & apoptosis ([TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md), [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md)), MAPK signalling ([NF1](../genes/NF1.md), [MAP3K1](../genes/MAP3K1.md), [MAP2K4](../genes/MAP2K4.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md)), tissue organisation ([CDH1](../genes/CDH1.md), [CTNNA1](../genes/CTNNA1.md), [AFDN](../genes/AFDN.md)/MLLT4), transcription ([GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md), [TBX3](../genes/TBX3.md), [CBFB](../genes/CBFB.md), [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md), [FOXP1](../genes/FOXP1.md), [FOXO3](../genes/FOXO3.md)), ubiquitination ([USP9X](../genes/USP9X.md)), splicing ([SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md)), and others ([SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md), [GPS2](../genes/GPS2.md), [ZFP36L1](../genes/ZFP36L1.md), [MEN1](../genes/MEN1.md), [AGTR2](../genes/AGTR2.md)) ([PMID:27161491](../papers/27161491.md), Fig. 1a).
- **22.6% of all tumours** harboured a coding mutation in one of seven chromatin-function Mut-drivers ([KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [NCOR1](../genes/NCOR1.md), [CTCF](../genes/CTCF.md), [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md), [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md), [TBL1XR1](../genes/TBL1XR1.md)).
- **Ras-pathway events.** [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) codon 12 mutations in 11 samples (ER+ ONC=89%, ER- ONC=60%); two [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) codon-61 and one codon-12 mutations; one [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E in an ER- tumour. [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), and [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) amplification more common in ER- tumours (3.9%, 2.7%, 1.2%).
- **ER-stratified differences.** [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) had a high TSG score only in ER+ tumours (TSG=35% vs 0% in ER-). [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) mutation frequencies were similar by ER status (ER+ 2.8%, ER- 3.2%) but codon 755 mutations were enriched in ER+ tumours (13/53 ER+ vs 1/22 ER-, P=0.053). [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) helical-domain (codons 542/545) and codon 345 mutations were enriched in ER+ tumours, while kinase-domain codon 1047 mutations were enriched in ER-.
- **Homozygous-deletion-derived tumour suppressors.** Of 40 HD-targeted genes resequenced, 8 were independently confirmed as Mut-driver TSGs ([FOXO3](../genes/FOXO3.md), [CTNNA1](../genes/CTNNA1.md), [FOXP1](../genes/FOXP1.md), [MEN1](../genes/MEN1.md), [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md) in ER+; [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md), [AFDN](../genes/AFDN.md)/MLLT4 in both). [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) HDs in 53/2,087 tumours (most common HD target). [JAK1](../genes/JAK1.md) had 4 HDs + 4 inactivating + 4 missense/LOH events; [NT5E](../genes/NT5E.md) (CD73) had 3 HDs + 1 inactivating + 4 missense/LOH events.
- **Clinical-pathology associations.** ER+ functional mutations in [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) (OR=0.58, 95%CI 0.49–0.69), [GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md) (OR=0.77), [MAP3K1](../genes/MAP3K1.md) (OR=0.52), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) (OR=0.69), and [CBFB](../genes/CBFB.md) (OR=0.56) associated with lower grade. [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations associated with higher grade in both ER+ (OR=3.3, P<0.001) and ER- (OR=3.6, P<0.001). Younger age at diagnosis associated with [GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md) (OR=0.63) and [CBFB](../genes/CBFB.md) (OR=0.48) inactivating mutations; older age with [CDH1](../genes/CDH1.md), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md), and [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md) (OR=4.5).
- **Histology.** [CDH1](../genes/CDH1.md) inactivating mutations in 52.6% of lobular carcinomas vs 3.4% of ductal/NST. Mucinous (8.3%) and medullary (8.8%) carcinomas had significantly fewer functional [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) mutations than ductal (36.9%), mixed (50.0%), or lobular (46.9%).
- **Co-mutation / mutual exclusivity.** Mutual exclusivity between [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) and each of [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) (OR=0.017), [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md) (OR=0.092), and [FOXO3](../genes/FOXO3.md) (OR=0.10), reflecting Akt-pathway redundancy. 45.2% of all tumours had a functional mutation in at least one Akt-pathway member ([PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md), [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [FOXO3](../genes/FOXO3.md)). Mutual exclusivity between [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [CDH1](../genes/CDH1.md) (OR=0.23), [GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md) (OR=0.13), and [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md) (OR=0.049). Co-mutation of [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)+[RB1](../genes/RB1.md) (OR=5.3) characteristic of triple-negative cancers; co-mutation of [CDH1](../genes/CDH1.md) with [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [TBX3](../genes/TBX3.md), [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md), and [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) (OR=5.7) characteristic of lobular biology.
- **Mutation–CNA interactions.** Classic mutation/LOH coupling for tumour suppressors ([PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) + 10q23.1 deletion OR=3.4; [GPS2](../genes/GPS2.md) + 17p13.1 deletion OR=7.1). [CDH1](../genes/CDH1.md) (OR=2) and [CBFB](../genes/CBFB.md) (OR=5) (both at 16q22) associated with the t(1q;16p)-driven 1q-gain/16q-loss pattern. Mutual exclusivity of [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) mutations with [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) 17q12 amplification (OR=0.091).
- **IntClust-specific Mut-driver enrichment.** [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutated in 84.6% of IntClust10, 64.2% of IntClust5, 50.5% of IntClust4-, 44.7% of IntClust9, 40.7% of IntClust6, but only 4.4% of IntClust8, 10.0% of IntClust3, and 14.0% of IntClust7. [GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md) enriched in IntClust1 (20.0%) and IntClust8 (19.5%). [CBFB](../genes/CBFB.md) enriched in IntClust3 (7.8%) and IntClust8 (9.7%). 27/2,021 ER+ tumours mapped to IntClust10 (basal-like-mimicking ER+) had 59.3% [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation rate vs 18.7% in all ER+.
- **Clonality.** Most Mut-driver mutation CCFs centred near 1 (early/clonal). PIK3CA mutations were near-clonal in IntClust9 (median 1.0) and IntClust10 (median 1.0) but more subclonal in IntClust3 (median 0.96, IQR 0.75–1, n=215). 199 tumours (10.4%) carried >1 functional mutation in a single Mut-driver gene; [MAP3K1](../genes/MAP3K1.md) most often multi-hit (53/152 mutants).
- **Survival in ER+ disease.** [MAP3K1](../genes/MAP3K1.md) (HR=0.56, CI 0.38–0.82) and [GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md) (HR=0.58, CI 0.4–0.82) associated with longer breast-cancer-specific survival (BCSS). Inactivating [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) (HR=3.4, CI 1.4–8.3) and [USP9X](../genes/USP9X.md) (HR=3.0, CI 1.2–7.2) mutations associated with worse BCSS. [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations associated with worse outcome in ER+ (HR=1.6, P=0.0001) but not ER- (HR=1.1).
- **Survival in ER- disease.** [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) mutations were prognostic (HR=1.4, CI 1.1–1.9) in both helical and kinase domains; [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) inactivating mutations associated with shorter BCSS (HR=2.7, CI 1.3–5.5).
- **PIK3CA × IntClust interaction (key novel finding).** PIK3CA mutations were associated with poor outcome in ER+ tumours specifically within IntClust1 (P=0.02), IntClust2 (P=0.05), and IntClust9 (P=0.09 trend) — i.e. IntClusts defined by 17q23, 11q13–14, and 8q24 amplifications respectively — but not in IntClusts 3, 4+, 7, or 8.
- **Intra-tumour heterogeneity ([MATH](../methods/math-score.md)).** ER+ tumours had lower MATH scores (median 0.29) than ER- (median 0.41). Higher MATH scores associated with worse BCSS in ER+ (P=0.003) but not ER- (P=0.302). IntClust10 had the highest MATH (median 0.47); IntClust2 had paradoxically *low* MATH (median 0.25) despite poor outcome — likely because the [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md)/[PAK1](../genes/PAK1.md) 11q13–14 co-amplification driving IntClust2 is a single early clonal event.

## Genes & alterations

- **[PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)** — 40.1% coding-mutation frequency; ER+ ONC=94%, ER- ONC=81%. Helical-domain (codons 542/545) and codon 345 hotspots enriched in ER+; kinase-domain codon 1047 enriched in ER-. Mutually exclusive with [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md), [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md), [FOXO3](../genes/FOXO3.md). Prognostic in ER- across both domains (HR=1.4); prognostic in ER+ *only* within IntClusts 1, 2, and 9. Frequently co-occurs with [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) inactivating mutations (15/57 PTEN-mutant tumours also have recurrent PIK3CA mutations).
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)** — 35.4% coding-mutation frequency; ER+ ONC=42% TSG=35%, ER- ONC=45% TSG=40%. 85.4% of TP53-mutant tumours show LOH. Mutated in 84.6% of IntClust10. Mutations in the DNA-binding domain associated with worst outcomes; prognostic in ER+ (HR=1.6) but not ER-.
- **[MUC16](../genes/MUC16.md), [AHNAK2](../genes/AHNAK2.md), [SYNE1](../genes/SYNE1.md)** — high coding-mutation frequencies (16.8%, 16.2%, 12.0%) but flagged as having high background mutation rates; uncertain breast-cancer driver status.
- **[KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) (MLL3)** — 11.4% mutated; ER+ TSG-driver. Often subclonal in IntClust1, more clonal in IntClust8. Lower-grade-associated.
- **[GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md)** — 11.1% mutated; enriched in HER2+/ER+ (8.2%) vs HER2+/ER- (0.5%). Mutations associated with younger age, lower grade, longer survival (HR=0.58 ER+).
- **[CDH1](../genes/CDH1.md)** — Inactivating mutations in 52.6% of lobular carcinomas (hallmark of invasive lobular); 96.0% of mutant tumours show LOH. Co-mutated with [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [TBX3](../genes/TBX3.md), [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md), [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md). Strongly mutually exclusive with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md). HD in 18 ductal/NST and 4 lobular cases.
- **[ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md)** — 2.8% ER+, 3.2% ER- somatic mutation frequency; codon 755 hotspot enriched in ER+; mutations observed in relapsed CDH1-mutant lobular carcinomas; mutually exclusive of [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) mutations within 17q12-amplified context.
- **[MAP3K1](../genes/MAP3K1.md)** — Most common multi-hit gene (53/152 mutants with >1 functional mutation), suggesting biallelic inactivation. ER+ TSG. Mutations protect against worse survival (HR=0.56).
- **[SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md)** — Recurrent K700E in 3.5% of ER+ tumours (ONC=52%); linked to differential splicing in breast tumours.
- **SWI/SNF members [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) and [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md)** — Inactivating mutations identified as Mut-drivers; raises possibility of synthetic-lethal vulnerabilities (e.g. [ARID1B](../genes/ARID1B.md) dependency in ARID1A-deficient context).
- **HD-defined TSGs [FOXO3](../genes/FOXO3.md), [FOXP1](../genes/FOXP1.md), [CTNNA1](../genes/CTNNA1.md), [AFDN](../genes/AFDN.md)/MLLT4, [MEN1](../genes/MEN1.md)** — newly nominated breast-cancer Mut-drivers via combined HD + inactivating-mutation evidence.
- **[NF1](../genes/NF1.md)** — Inactivating mutations associated with shorter BCSS in ER- tumours (HR=2.7).
- **[SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md), [USP9X](../genes/USP9X.md)** — Inactivating mutations associated with worse BCSS in ER+ (HR=3.4 and 3.0 respectively).
- **[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)** — classical hotspot activating mutations observed at low frequencies (KRAS codon 12 in 11 samples; [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) at codons 12/61 in 3 samples; one [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E in ER-) but did not meet Mut-driver criteria as standalone breast-cancer events.
- **[CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) + [PAK1](../genes/PAK1.md)** — co-amplification at 11q13–14 defines IntClust2; single early clonal driver event explains the paradoxically low MATH heterogeneity despite poor outcomes.
- **[ZNF703](../genes/ZNF703.md), [MYC](../genes/MYC.md)** — driver CNAs at 8p11 (IntClust6) and 8q24 (IntClust9) respectively, used in the IntClust copy-number taxonomy referenced here.
- **[JAK1](../genes/JAK1.md), NT5E (CD73)** — newly identified HD-targeted candidates linked to immune evasion / immune modulation.

## Clinical implications

- **PIK3CA prognostic context-dependence in ER+ disease.** The paper explicitly argues that the contradictory literature on [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)'s prognostic value in ER+ breast cancer can be resolved by stratifying on IntClust: PIK3CA mutations are associated with shorter BCSS specifically in IntClust1 (17q23-amp), IntClust2 (11q13–14-amp), and IntClust9 (8q24-amp) ER+ tumours. The authors call this out as a finding that should inform stratification in future PI3K-inhibitor trials ([PMID:27161491](../papers/27161491.md), Discussion).
- **PI3K-inhibitor trial design.** No specific PI3K inhibitor is named or tested, but the authors flag that clinical-trial interpretation should account for IntClust background in addition to PIK3CA mutation status.
- **Cross-cancer drug repurposing hypothesis.** Mut-drivers like [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md), [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md), [MEN1](../genes/MEN1.md), [FOXP1](../genes/FOXP1.md), [USP9X](../genes/USP9X.md), [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md), and [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) — well-known drivers in other cancer types — are detectable subsets of breast cancer where therapies developed elsewhere may be applicable. Specifically suggested: ARID1B-targeting in ARID1A-deficient SWI/SNF tumours.
- **[AGTR2](../genes/AGTR2.md) (angiotensin II receptor type 2).** Recurrent P271L mutations in 6 ER+ tumours; flagged as a possible therapeutic target.
- **HER2 + ER subgroup heterogeneity.** HER2+/ER- tumours had higher TP53 functional mutation rates (67.5%) than HER2+/ER+ (42.6%); HER2+/ER+ had higher [GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md) mutation rates (8.2% vs 0.5%). Implications for resistance to anti-HER2 therapy via PIK3CA mutations are noted (HER2-/ER+ had 46.5% PIK3CA mutation rate vs HER2+/ER+ 29.5%).
- **MATH heterogeneity as a survival biomarker in ER+.** Higher MATH score (upper quartile) associated with significantly worse BCSS in ER+ (P=0.003) but not ER-. The authors caution this should be interpreted in the IntClust context: IntClust2 (CCND1/PAK1-amp) has *low* heterogeneity but very poor outcome, so heterogeneity-based biomarkers must be IntClust-aware.
- **Resistance to neo-adjuvant chemotherapy in IntClust2.** Tumours co-amplifying [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) and [PAK1](../genes/PAK1.md) at 11q13–14 are highlighted as a small but highly aggressive subgroup that has previously been shown to be resistant to neo-adjuvant cytotoxic chemotherapy and warrants development of better strategies.

## Limitations & open questions

- Pan-cancer-frequent genes [MUC16](../genes/MUC16.md), [AHNAK2](../genes/AHNAK2.md), and [SYNE1](../genes/SYNE1.md) appear at high coding-mutation rates (16.8%, 16.2%, 12.0%) but their tumorigenic roles in breast cancer remain uncertain — high background mutation rate confounds interpretation.
- The 173-gene targeted panel excludes whole-genome / whole-exome events; novel drivers outside the panel would not be detected. The panel was assembled from 5 prior 2012 sequencing studies plus HD-targeted genes — recently nominated drivers are necessarily under-represented.
- Several IntClust × PIK3CA interactions reach P=0.02–0.09 in fairly small per-IntClust subgroups (e.g. IntClust1 21/117 PIK3CA-mutant; IntClust2 28/74); the authors explicitly flag the need for external validation.
- MATH score depends on having ≥5 mutations per sample; tumours with very few mutations were excluded from the heterogeneity analysis, limiting analysis of mutation-quiet (e.g. IntClust4-CNA-devoid) tumours.
- Cancer-cell-fraction estimates depend on copy number and purity calls from [ASCAT](../methods/ascat.md); subclonal architecture is only approximated, not directly reconstructed.
- No specific PI3K inhibitor (e.g. [alpelisib](../drugs/alpelisib.md), [buparlisib](../drugs/buparlisib.md)) is tested — the trial-design implications are correlative/observational only.
- Pathogenic germline classification was performed in-cohort; concordance with established germline-variant classifications is not formally benchmarked.
- The "tissue-organisation" cluster of [CDH1](../genes/CDH1.md), [CTNNA1](../genes/CTNNA1.md), [AFDN](../genes/AFDN.md)/MLLT4 is biologically compelling but functional confirmation of [CTNNA1](../genes/CTNNA1.md) and AFDN/MLLT4 as breast-cancer TSGs is left to future work.
