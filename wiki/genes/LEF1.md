# LEF1

## Overview

LEF1 (Lymphoid Enhancer-Binding Factor 1) is a transcription factor in the Wnt/beta-catenin signaling pathway. LEF1 acts downstream of beta-catenin and mediates Wnt target gene activation. It is implicated in cell fate decisions, EMT, and stem cell maintenance, and is dysregulated in multiple cancer types including prostate cancer.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- LEF1 is identified as a top transcription factor in the CRPC-WNT subtype of castration-resistant prostate cancer (CRPC), together with [TCF7L2](../genes/TCF7L2.md), [TCF7](../genes/TCF7.md), and [TCF7L1](../genes/TCF7L1.md), in a chromatin-profiling study of 40 CRPC organoid/PDX models projected onto 266 SU2C and 100 WCM patient cohorts [PMID:35617398](../papers/35617398.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) (CRPC)**: LEF1 is a defining transcription factor of the CRPC-WNT subtype, which is characterized by [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) hotspot mutations (3/4 models) and APC/RSPO2 alterations; LEF1 and other TCF/LEF members drive Wnt target gene expression in this lineage-plastic subtype [PMID:35617398](../papers/35617398.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-expressed with TCF7L2, TCF7, and TCF7L1 as the top TF program of CRPC-WNT. CTNNB1 hotspot mutations (3/4 CRPC-WNT models) drive LEF1 activity [PMID:35617398](../papers/35617398.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- CRPC-WNT is proposed as a therapeutically distinct subtype; [AXIN2](../genes/AXIN2.md) is elevated as a Wnt target and proposed as a candidate IHC/RNA biomarker for CRPC-WNT identification. Direct LEF1 targeting is not reported [PMID:35617398](../papers/35617398.md).

## Open questions

- CRPC-WNT models were few (n=4); the prevalence and clinical behavior of this subtype in larger unselected CRPC cohorts requires validation [PMID:35617398](../papers/35617398.md).
