## TL;DR

Zhang et al. built a B cell–specific double-mutant (DM) mouse model coexpressing the recurrent CLL splicing-factor mutation [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md)-K700E with deletion of the chromosome 13q minimal-deleted region (Mdr, encompassing [DLEU1](../genes/DLEU1.md)/[DLEU2](../genes/DLEU2.md)/[MIR15A](../genes/MIR15A.md)). Coexpression accelerated [chronic lymphocytic leukemia](../cancer_types/CLLSLL.md) penetrance (24% vs 7% in Mdr-only mice over 16+ months) and produced a more transplantable, MYC- and Ki67-high disease in NSG recipients. Integrated RNA-seq and proteomics identified mTORC1 and [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) pathway activation in DM CLL cells, driven by Sf3b1-dependent alternative splicing of [Nfatc1](../genes/NFATC1.md) toward the short isoform 5 (exon 9 skipping). Combined RNA-splicing inhibition (H3B-8800) and mTORC1 inhibition ([temsirolimus](../drugs/temsirolimus.md)) was synergistic in DM CLL cells in vitro and in vivo, and human CLL samples with both *[SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md)* mutation and del(13q) were preferentially sensitive to the combination [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).

## Cohort & data

- **Murine model.** B cell–specific *Cd19-Cre* mice with three genotypes: DM (Sf3b1^fl/+ Mdr^fl/+ or fl/fl, n = 25), Mdr-mutant only (n = 27), and WT controls (n = 30). Monitored every 3 months from 6–24 months of age for B220+[CD5](../genes/CD5.md)+ CLL-like cells in peripheral blood [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- **Engraftment.** Splenocytes (3 × 10^6) from DM or Mdr CLL mice transplanted intravenously into NOD/SCID-γ (NSG) recipients [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- **Human reference cohort.** A separate CLLmap survey of 1,009 CLL patients (https://cllmap.org/) used to confirm *SF3B1* mutation frequency (184/1,009 = 18.2%) and co-occurrence with del(13q) [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- **Human CLL validation.** Patient samples from the CLL Research Consortium stratified by FISH/NGS into four groups (DM, del(13q) alone, *SF3B1* mut alone, double-negative; n = 3 each) for drug sensitivity [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- **Assays.** Bulk [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) (NovaSeq, 150 bp PE; STAR, DESeq2; rMATS + LeafCutter + StringTie splicing pipeline); TMT 10-plex [proteomics](../methods/lc-ms-ms-proteomics.md) (Orbitrap Fusion); isoform-specific qPCR; [IHC](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md) for PAX5, CD5, [MYC](../genes/MYC.md), Ki67; [FISH](../methods/fish.md) + NGS for *SF3B1*/del(13q) status in human samples; [flow cytometry](../methods/multiparameter-flow-cytometry.md). Murine RNA-seq deposited under GEO GSE300699 [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).

## Key findings

- In the CLLmap cohort of 1,009 patients, 51% of *SF3B1* mutations co-occur with del(13q); SF3B1-mut/del(13q) versus null patients have significantly shorter time to first therapy (P < 0.0001) and inferior overall survival (P = 0.0002 vs SF3B1-wt/no-del; P = 0.032 vs SF3B1-mut/no-del(13q)) [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- From 16 months onward, 6/25 (24%) DM mice and 2/27 (7%) Mdr-only mice developed circulating B220+CD5+ CLL-like cells (20–60% burden); no WT mouse did. Two DM CLL mice (but no Mdr-only) had enlarged mesenteric lymph nodes [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- DM CLL cells engrafted into NSG mice produced CLL-like disease in 3–4 weeks versus a longer latency for Mdr-only cells, with higher Ki67 and [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) staining by IHC [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- In young (12-week) DM mice without CLL, coexpression of Sf3b1-K700E and Mdr deletion reduced spleen weight and splenocyte/B-cell counts (P < 0.01) and expanded marginal zone B cells (P < 0.01), opposite of Mdr-only mice [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- Splenic-B-cell RNA-seq: 835 dysregulated genes (658 up) in DM vs other genotypes; GSEA enriched for OXPHOS, mRNA splicing, MYC targets, and mTOR pathway (upregulated) and TNF-α / inflammatory response (downregulated; FDR < 0.1) [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- DM CLL versus DM normal B cells: 1,059 dysregulated genes (457 up) including the CLL markers *Cd5*, *Lef1*, *Zap70*; mRNA–protein concordance r = 0.5265 across TMT proteomics [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- rMATS splicing analysis identified 1,029 DM-CLL splice variants and 376 Sf3b1-mutation splice variants, with 117 overlapping. Seven of these directly interact with MYC/mTORC1 in STRING: *Nfatc1*, *Atf2*, *Hdac6*, *Pbrm1*, *Ptprc*, *Tbl1xr1*, *Hdac10*. *Nfatc1* showed the largest |ΔPSI| and most significant FDR [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- DM CLL cells preferentially express [NFATC1](../genes/NFATC1.md) short isoform 5 (exon 9 skipped, exon 1a–driven, ~78 kDa) while Mdr-only CLL cells express predominantly isoform 2. Isoform 5 overexpression in Ba/F3-MYC cells confers IL-3 independence; in HG3 human CLL cells it activates mTORC1 (p-mTORC1, p-4E-BP1) whereas isoform 2 activates AKT/S6 instead [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- In vitro IC50: DM CLL cells more sensitive to H3B-8800 than Mdr-only (0.00346 μM vs 0.05331 μM); Mdr-only more sensitive to temsirolimus than DM (0.0001055 μM vs 2.148 μM). Combination Chou-Talalay index 3.56 × 10^-6 (DM) vs 1.96 × 10^-4 (Mdr-only) — both synergistic but more so in DM [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- In vivo NSG engraftment: combination H3B-8800 (4 mg/kg, oral, 5 days) + temsirolimus (15 mg/kg, i.p., 5 days) extended median [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) in DM CLL mice from 9 to 27 days (3-fold; P < 0.01 log-rank), versus 61 → 63 days in Mdr-only CLL mice (1.05-fold) [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- Human CLL patient samples (n = 3 per group): DM (SF3B1-mut + del(13q)) cells were significantly more sensitive to temsirolimus + H3B-8800 than the other three groups (P < 0.001, 2-way ANOVA), recapitulating the murine phenotype [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).

## Genes & alterations

- [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md) — recurrent K700E hotspot (>50% of all *SF3B1* mutations). In CLLmap, mutated in 184/1,009 (18.2%) patients; mutation associated with shorter time to first therapy independent of del(13q) and inferior OS when co-occurring with del(13q). Sf3b1-K700E drives alternative splicing of *Nfatc1* (and 375 other genes) and activates mTORC1/MYC pathways in B cells [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- [NFATC1](../genes/NFATC1.md) — alternative splicing of exons 8/9 produces short isoform 5 (exon 9–skipped) preferentially in DM CLL cells. Isoform 5 activates mTORC1 (p-4E-BP1) and upregulates MYC; isoform 2 (full-length) instead activates AKT/S6. Validated in HG3, MEC1 isogenic SF3B1-K700E cell lines and Ba/F3-MYC system [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) — upregulated at mRNA and protein level in DM CLL cells; pathway enrichment confirmed by GSEA in both murine and human CLL with SF3B1-mut/del(13q) [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md) — mTORC1 pathway (p-mTOR, p-4E-BP1 T37/46, p-S6 S235/236) activated in DM CLL cells but not Mdr-only CLL cells; targetable with [temsirolimus](../drugs/temsirolimus.md) [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- [DLEU1](../genes/DLEU1.md), [DLEU2](../genes/DLEU2.md), [MIR15A](../genes/MIR15A.md) — comprise the minimal deleted region (Mdr) of 13q14 modeled as floxed deletion; Mdr loss alone gives low-penetrance CLL but cooperates with SF3B1 mutation for full acceleration [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) — referenced as the previously published SF3B1-Atm CLL murine model from the same group; comparison context only [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- [ZAP70](../genes/ZAP70.md), [CD5](../genes/CD5.md) — CLL marker genes upregulated in DM CLL cells at both mRNA and protein levels [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- [BRD9](../genes/BRD9.md), [PPP2R5A](../genes/PPP2R5A.md), [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) — discussed as comparison targets of SF3B1-driven mis-splicing in other cancers (uveal melanoma, breast cancer, PDAC); not directly altered here but provide cross-disease context [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).

## Clinical implications

- Defines [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md)-mutant / del(13q) co-occurrence as a distinct aggressive [CLL](../cancer_types/CLLSLL.md) subtype with poor OS, suggesting genomic stratification at diagnosis for prognosis [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- Provides mechanistic rationale for combining an SF3B1-directed splicing modulator ([H3B-8800](../drugs/h3b-8800.md)) with an mTORC1 inhibitor ([temsirolimus](../drugs/temsirolimus.md)) in patients carrying both lesions; combination was preferentially active on patient cells with both lesions versus single or neither [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- The authors note that H3B-8800 monotherapy was limited by toxicity in a prior phase I trial, but argue lower-dose combination with temsirolimus may be tractable [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- NFATC1 isoform-specific signaling (isoform 5 → mTOR; isoform 2 → AKT) may be a biomarker of mTOR-pathway dependence in SF3B1-mutant CLL [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).

## Limitations & open questions

- Penetrance even in DM mice is moderate (6/25 = 24% over 24 months) and clinical translation requires validation in larger CLL cohorts [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- The human in vitro drug-sensitivity comparison uses only n = 3 patients per group; no in vivo human or PDX confirmation of the combination [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- The study does not formally test whether NFATC1 isoform 5 is necessary (vs sufficient) for mTOR activation in CLL — e.g. via isoform-specific knockdown or exon-9 forced-inclusion rescue [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- Toxicity and long-term efficacy of an H3B-8800 + temsirolimus regimen in patients remain unknown; the authors explicitly call out the need to assess safety [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
- Whether Sf3b1-K700E uses different splicing targets across cancers ([BRD9](../genes/BRD9.md) in UVM, [PPP2R5A](../genes/PPP2R5A.md) in PDAC, MAP3K7/IRAK4 in [MDS](../cancer_types/MDS.md), Nfatc1 here in CLL) implies disease-context dependence that complicates pan-tumor splicing-modulator strategies [PMID:26200345](../papers/26200345.md).
