# LDHA

## Overview

LDHA (Lactate Dehydrogenase A) encodes the A subunit of the lactate dehydrogenase enzyme, which catalyzes the interconversion of pyruvate and lactate with concomitant NAD+/NADH cycling. In cancer, LDHA supports the Warburg effect (aerobic glycolysis) and is frequently overexpressed in tumor cells. A key genomic-level mechanism linking LDHA to cancer biology is its paralog relationship with [LDHB](../genes/LDHB.md): when LDHA promoter is hypermethylated (silenced), cells become dependent on LDHB, and vice versa — a form of paralog-based synthetic lethality exploitable as a therapeutic vulnerability.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- LDHA and LDHB exhibit reciprocal paralog synthetic lethality gated by promoter methylation in cancer cell lines; when LDHA promoter is hypermethylated, LDHB becomes essential, and vice versa; this relationship was replicated in TCGA primary tumors across multiple cancer types. [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Synthetic lethality between LDHA and LDHB was observed across multiple cancer cell line lineages in the Cancer Cell Line Encyclopedia (CCLE), with validation in TCGA primary tumor methylation data. [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- LDHA and LDHB show reciprocal promoter methylation patterns — hypermethylation of one paralogue predicts dependence on the other; this is a canonical example of paralog-based synthetic lethality identified in the next-generation CCLE characterization. [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- The reciprocal LDHA/LDHB synthetic lethality suggests that tumors with LDHA promoter hypermethylation may be selectively vulnerable to LDHB inhibition, and vice versa; this represents a methylation-gated therapeutic target strategy proposed from the CCLE study. [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)

## Open questions

- Whether LDHA/LDHB methylation status can serve as a reliable clinical biomarker for LDH-inhibitor sensitivity in vivo has not been prospectively tested.
