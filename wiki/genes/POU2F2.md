# POU2F2

## Overview

POU2F2 (also known as OCT2) is a POU-domain transcription factor critical for B-cell development and immunoglobulin gene expression. In cancer genomics, POU2F2 has been identified as a driver-gene cluster member in diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL), grouped within B-cell development and transcription/translation functional groups.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- POU2F2 identified as a driver-gene cluster member across signaling, cell growth, B-cell development, and transcription/translation functional groups in a 1001-patient DLBCL genomic cohort (Duke, 2017) [PMID:28985567](../papers/28985567.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **DLBCL**: Driver-gene cluster member identified via integrated genomic and CRISPR functional screening in 1001 patients [PMID:28985567](../papers/28985567.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Grouped with [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md), [PIM2](../genes/PIM2.md), [BTK](../genes/BTK.md), [CHD8](../genes/CHD8.md), YY1, and [H1-4](../genes/H1-4.md) as driver-gene cluster members across B-cell signaling, growth, B-cell development, and transcription functional groups [PMID:28985567](../papers/28985567.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- As a transcription factor in B-cell development and a DLBCL driver, POU2F2 may be relevant to therapeutic strategies targeting OCT2/BOB1 co-activator signaling.
