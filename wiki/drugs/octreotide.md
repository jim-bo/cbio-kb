# octreotide

## Overview

Octreotide is a synthetic somatostatin analog that binds somatostatin receptors (primarily [SSTR2](../genes/SSTR2.md) and SSTR5), inhibiting hormonal secretion and tumor proliferation. It is used for symptom control and antiproliferative therapy in neuroendocrine tumors (NETs).

## Evidence in the corpus

- High SSTR2 expression (often with SSTR1/3/4/5) identified by WGTA (POG program) supported somatostatin-analog therapy (octreotide/lanreotide) in metastatic NEN patients PN5, PN7, PN10, PN23, PN28; PN23 had a 1972-day duration on octreotide/lanreotide [PMID:40328872](../papers/40328872.md)
- Octreotide was among the systemic therapies used in the panNET cohort (n=80 metastatic patients) profiled by [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md); the study focused on genomic characterization rather than response rates for individual agents [PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)
