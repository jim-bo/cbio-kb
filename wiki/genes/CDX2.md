# CDX2

## Overview

CDX2 (Caudal Type Homeobox 2) is a hindgut-specific homeobox transcription factor essential for intestinal differentiation. Its loss marks a shift from normal intestinal identity toward gastric-type metaplasia. In colorectal pathology, CDX2 loss is a hallmark of the serrated pathway (SSLs) and a proposed biomarker for sessile serrated colon lesion (SSL) diagnosis.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Transcriptional and protein loss (by IHC) in sessile serrated colonocytes (SSCs) and SSLs in the COLON MAP / HTAN pre-cancer atlas ([crc_hta11_htan_2021](../datasets/crc_hta11_htan_2021.md)); variably reduced in MSI-H CRCs; CDX2-low combined with [MUC5AC](../genes/MUC5AC.md)+ is proposed as the diagnostic biomarker pair for SSL classification [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [COADREAD](../cancer_types/COADREAD.md): CDX2 loss is driven by BRAF-V600E and accompanies gastric-type metaplasia in the serrated polyp pathway; reduced expression in MSI-H CRCs relative to MSS CRCs [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- CDX2 loss co-occurs with MUC5AC gain in the serrated/metaplastic program; mutually exclusive with WNT/LGR5+ stem-cell activation characteristic of conventional adenomas [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- CDX2-low + MUC5AC+ proposed as a diagnostic biomarker pair to identify SSLs; MSI-H CRCs with serrated origin may have distinct immunotherapy sensitivity profiles [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Open questions

- Whether CDX2 loss is mechanistically required for serrated progression or is a readout of BRAF-V600E-driven epigenetic reprogramming, and whether CDX2 restoration would reverse the metaplastic phenotype, are unresolved.
