# SUMO1

## Overview

SUMO1 (Small Ubiquitin-Like Modifier 1) encodes a protein that is covalently conjugated to target proteins via the SUMO pathway, modifying their stability, localization, and activity. SUMOylation is implicated in chromatin regulation, DNA repair, and transcriptional control. In glioblastoma, SUMO1 upregulation is associated with high H2B N-terminal acetylation in the classical-like tumor subtype, suggesting a link between the SUMOylation and chromatin modification landscapes.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Protein upregulated in [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) tumors with high H2B N-terminal acetylation (classical-like nmf3 subtype); positively correlated with the SUMOylation pathway enrichment score identified by H2B acetylation-pathway NMF analysis; observed in a CPTAC proteogenomic cohort of 99 treatment-naive GBMs. [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md): SUMO1 protein upregulation in H2B-hyperacetylated classical-like tumors; identified by multi-omics NMF clustering and acetylome profiling. [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- SUMO1 upregulation co-occurs with [UBE2I](../genes/UBE2I.md) (the SUMO E2 conjugating enzyme) in high-H2B-acetylation GBM tumors; both track with [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md) protein levels ([UBE2I](../genes/UBE2I.md)–[CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md) Pearson r=0.413). [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting of SUMO1 in GBM is described in the current corpus; the H2B-acetylation–SUMOylation axis co-segregates with [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md)/[EP300](../genes/EP300.md) and [BRD4](../genes/BRD4.md) activity, suggesting potential relevance of BET inhibitors or CBP/p300 inhibitors in SUMO1-high tumors. [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Open questions

- Whether SUMO1 upregulation in classical-like GBM is a driver or passenger of H2B hyperacetylation, and whether SUMO-pathway inhibition has therapeutic activity, requires functional investigation. [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)
