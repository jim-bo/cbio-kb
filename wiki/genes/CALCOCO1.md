# CALCOCO1

## Overview

CALCOCO1 (Calcium Binding And Coiled-Coil Domain 1) encodes a coiled-coil domain protein. In cancer genomics, CALCOCO1 has been identified as a novel fusion partner of [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) in glioblastoma, representing a rare structural variant with potential therapeutic relevance given the targetability of FGFR3.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Novel fusion partner of FGFR3 (FGFR3–CALCOCO1) identified in a glioblastoma PDX panel of 83 IDH-wildtype tumors; represents a rare, potentially targetable FGFR3 fusion variant alongside FGFR3–[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md) and FGFR3–[TRIM54](../genes/TRIM54.md) [PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Glioblastoma: rare FGFR3 fusion partner in [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) PDX; FGFR3 fusions in GBM are candidates for FGFR inhibitor therapy [PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- FGFR3–CALCOCO1 co-occurs with other FGFR3 fusions (FGFR3–TACC3, FGFR3–TRIM54) in the same GBM PDX panel; all three fusion types flagged as potentially targetable [PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- FGFR3–CALCOCO1 fusion may confer sensitivity to FGFR inhibitors, analogous to FGFR3–TACC3; no direct drug-sensitivity data reported for this specific fusion [PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)

## Open questions

- Whether FGFR3–CALCOCO1 retains oncogenic kinase activity and responds to FGFR inhibitors to the same degree as FGFR3–TACC3 is untested in the available PDX panel [PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)
