# DNAJB14

## Overview

DNAJB14 encodes a co-chaperone of the HSP70 family (DnaJ/Hsp40 domain-containing protein). It participates in protein quality control and folding. In cancer genomics, DNAJB14 has been identified as downregulated in microsatellite-instable (MSI) tumors, grouping with other heat-shock proteins such as [HSPA4L](../genes/HSPA4L.md) and [HSPH1](../genes/HSPH1.md) whose reduced abundance tracks with MSI status across cancer cell lines.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Heat-shock protein downregulated in MSI cancer cell lines in the CCLE pan-cancer proteome (375 lines); HSPH1 (HSP110), the related heat-shock protein, has an established role in MSI disease [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Downregulated in MSI cancer cell lines across multiple lineages in CCLE proteome data [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-downregulated with HSPA4L and HSPH1 in MSI cancer cell lines [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic agents reported in this corpus.

## Open questions

- The mechanism linking MSI status to reduced DNAJB14 protein abundance remains unresolved.
