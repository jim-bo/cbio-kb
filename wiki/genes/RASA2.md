# RASA2

## Overview

RASA2 (RAS p21 protein activator 2) is a RAS GTPase-activating protein that negatively regulates the MAPK signaling pathway by accelerating GTP hydrolysis on RAS family members. Loss-of-function or missense mutations in RASA2 impair RAS-GAP activity and can thereby contribute to sustained MAPK/ERK signaling, analogous to activating RAS mutations. RASA2 mutations have been identified as weak MAPK activators in normal human skin melanocytes.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Somatic MAPK negative-regulator mutations (p.L83I, p.P376S) identified in normal-appearing skin melanocytes from sun-exposed skin; classified as weak activators, consistent with absence of clinically visible lesions [PMID:33029006](../papers/33029006.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Melanoma: RASA2 mutations observed in normal melanocytes as somatic driver-class variants; role as a weak MAPK activator that may prime cells for malignant transformation upon acquisition of additional hits [PMID:33029006](../papers/33029006.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Observed in sun-exposed skin melanocytes alongside other weak MAPK pathway activators ([BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) non-V600, [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md), [CBL](../genes/CBL.md)); individually insufficient to drive clinically visible lesions [PMID:33029006](../papers/33029006.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting established; MEK inhibition may be relevant given the MAPK pathway context.

## Open questions

- Whether RASA2 loss-of-function mutations cooperate with subsequent MAPK drivers (e.g., BRAF V600E) to accelerate melanoma development from pre-existing mutant melanocyte clones.
