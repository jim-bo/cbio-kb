# CDKN2A

## Overview

CDKN2A encodes two distinct tumor suppressors via alternative reading frames: p16/INK4A (inhibits CDK4/6-Cyclin D complexes, blocking RB1 phosphorylation) and p14/ARF (stabilizes p53 by inhibiting MDM2). Homozygous deletion of CDKN2A is one of the most common alterations across human cancers and is a major driver of cell-cycle dysregulation. CDKN2A loss is independently prognostic in multiple cancer types including intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma and esophageal adenocarcinoma.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- CDKN2A alterations associated with worse OS in esophageal/GEJ adenocarcinoma: KM p<0.001; adjusted HR 1.65; multivariable HR 1.50 (95% CI 1.14–1.97, p=0.004) in the egc_mskcc_2020 cohort (n=487). CDKN2A deletions alone were not prognostic on univariable analysis [PMID:33795256](../papers/33795256.md).
- CDKN2A focal alterations in 15% of intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma (iCCA); homozygous deletions in 13%; strongest independent prognostic factor (HR 3.40 in resected cohort multivariate; HR 2.5 in whole cohort). Only deep (homozygous) deletions are prognostic; monoallelic alterations are not. Mutually exclusive with IDH1/2 mutations and FGFR2 fusions [PMID:33765338](../papers/33765338.md).
- CDKN2A/CDKN2B show differential alteration patterns between IDH-WT and IDH-mutant GBM tumors in the CPTAC proteogenomic cohort (gbm_cptac_2021, n=99) [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **Esophageal/gastroesophageal junction adenocarcinoma (EAC):** CDKN2A alterations independently prognostic of worse OS (multivariable HR 1.50, p=0.004); deletions alone not significant on univariable; Cell Cycle pathway enrichment [PMID:33795256](../papers/33795256.md).
- **Intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma (IHCH):** homozygous deletion in 13%; strongest independent prognostic factor (HR 3.40 resected, HR 2.5 whole cohort); defines a subgroup with no benefit from resection in clinical high-risk patients; mutually exclusive with IDH1/2 and FGFR2 [PMID:33765338](../papers/33765338.md).
- **Glioblastoma (GBM):** differential CDKN2A/CDKN2B alteration patterns between IDH-WT and IDH-mutant tumors [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Mutually exclusive with IDH1/2 mutations and FGFR2 fusions in intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma [PMID:33765338](../papers/33765338.md).
- CDKN2A alterations associated with multifocal disease in iCCA [PMID:33765338](../papers/33765338.md).
- Co-occurs with TP53 and KRAS alterations as part of the high-risk genomic profile in iCCA [PMID:33765338](../papers/33765338.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- In iCCA, CDKN2A homozygous deletion (with TP53 and KRAS) should be routinely reported as part of pretreatment targeted sequencing to stratify OS independently of stage and treatment; patients with CDKN2A deep deletion + clinical high-risk features may be better served by upfront systemic therapy ± HAIC than resection [PMID:33765338](../papers/33765338.md).

## Open questions

- In EAC, why CDKN2A deletions alone are not prognostic on univariable analysis while the broader alteration category is, requires further investigation; may reflect reduced sensitivity of panel-based sequencing for deletions at low tumor purity [PMID:33795256](../papers/33795256.md).
- Panel-based sequencing has reduced sensitivity for deletions at low tumor purity, potentially dampening the apparent prognostic effect of CDKN2A deletions [PMID:33795256](../papers/33795256.md).
