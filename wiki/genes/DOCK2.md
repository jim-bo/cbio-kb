# DOCK2

## Overview

DOCK2 (Dedicator of Cytokinesis 2) encodes a [RAC1](../genes/RAC1.md) guanine nucleotide exchange factor (GEF) that dimerizes with [ELMO1](../genes/ELMO1.md) to activate RAC1 signaling. In cancer, DOCK2 mutations disrupt ELMO1/DOCK2/RAC1-mediated cytoskeletal remodeling, promoting invasive behavior.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Significantly mutated in esophageal adenocarcinoma (EAC); two tumors carried two independent DOCK2 mutations each; ELMO1/DOCK2 pathway mutations increase NIH/3T3 cell invasion 2-7-fold in functional assays [PMID:23525077](../papers/23525077.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Esophageal adenocarcinoma: significantly mutated gene; functional assays demonstrate pro-invasive activity [PMID:23525077](../papers/23525077.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- DOCK2 mutations co-occur with ELMO1 mutations in EAC; both act upstream of RAC1 GTPase activation [PMID:23525077](../papers/23525077.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic data in the corpus; RAC1/ELMO1/DOCK2 axis is a candidate therapeutic target in invasive EAC.

## Open questions

- Whether DOCK2 mutations drive invasion in vivo and whether DOCK2 loss-of-function or gain-of-function is the primary mechanism.
