# NUP93

## Overview

NUP93 (Nucleoporin 93) is a structural component of the nuclear pore complex ([NPC](../cancer_types/NPC.md)) that plays a role in nucleocytoplasmic transport. NUP93 has been implicated in cell migration and invasion in cancer contexts. A recurrent E14K hotspot mutation affecting NUP93 has been identified in breast carcinoma arising in the setting of juvenile papillomatosis (JP), suggesting a role in the malignant transformation of JP-associated lesions.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- E14K hotspot mutation identified in the [DCIS](../cancer_types/DCIS.md)+IDC-NST components of juvenile papillomatosis (JP) case JuP3, private to the carcinomatous components (not found in the matched JP); nucleoporin function linked to cell migration. [PMID:33263939](../papers/33263939.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) / [IDC](../cancer_types/IDC.md) / DCIS**: NUP93 E14K hotspot mutation was a carcinoma-specific event acquired alongside [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) truncation, [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) missense, and [PIK3CB](../genes/PIK3CB.md) missense in JuP3 during DCIS and IDC-NST evolution. [PMID:33263939](../papers/33263939.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-acquired with [ARID1A](ARID1A.md) truncating, [KMT2C](KMT2C.md) missense, and [PIK3CB](PIK3CB.md) missense mutations in the DCIS+IDC-NST transition in JP-associated breast carcinoma. [PMID:33263939](../papers/33263939.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic implication reported; the E14K hotspot mutation may disrupt NPC function in a way relevant to cell migration, but no targeted therapy exists. [PMID:33263939](../papers/33263939.md)

## Open questions

- Whether NUP93 E14K is a recurrent driver mutation in breast cancer more broadly (beyond JP-associated cases) is not established; the study comprised only 3 JP cases. [PMID:33263939](../papers/33263939.md)
