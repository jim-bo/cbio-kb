## TL;DR

This narrative review by Pinyol, Nault, Quetglas, Zucman-Rossi, and Llovet surveys the state of molecular profiling in hepatocellular carcinoma ([HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)) circa 2014, arguing that genomic and transcriptomic data should be integrated into clinical decision-making across the disease continuum: risk stratification of cirrhotic patients, diagnosis of small nodules, prognostication after curative resection, and selection of targeted therapies for advanced disease. It compiles recurrently mutated HCC driver genes ([TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter, [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md), [AXIN1](../genes/AXIN1.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md), [RPS6KA3](../genes/RPS6KA3.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [IRF2](../genes/IRF2.md), [NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md), [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md)) identified by next-generation sequencing, catalogs gene-expression signatures of prognosis and stem-cell phenotype, and maps these alterations to ongoing or candidate targeted therapy trials ([sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md), [refametinib](../drugs/refametinib.md), [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md), c-MET inhibitors, FGF-axis inhibitors, HDAC inhibitors).

## Cohort & data

- This is a review article — no primary cohort. It synthesizes published HCC genomic, transcriptomic, miRNA, GWAS, and clinical-trial data through early 2014.
- Cancer types discussed: [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) (hepatocellular carcinoma) and [LIAD](../cancer_types/LIAD.md) (hepatocellular adenoma, HCA).
- Reported population context: in Western countries, ~90% of HCC arises on a cirrhotic background; HCC accounts for ~80% of all primary liver cancers worldwide [PMID:24735922](../papers/24735922.md).

## Key findings

- **TERT promoter mutations** are the most prevalent somatic alteration in HCC (~59–60% of tumors) and are the first recurrent somatic genetic alteration identified in cirrhotic preneoplastic lesions (~25% of preneoplastic nodules), positioning them as an early driver of malignant transformation [PMID:24735922](../papers/24735922.md).
- Among HCC drivers, the review cites prevalence figures of ~30% for CTNNB1 (β-catenin activation) and ~30% for TP53; activating RAS/RAF/MAPK pathway mutations are uncommon (<5%) but the pathway is universally activated in advanced HCC [PMID:24735922](../papers/24735922.md).
- Newly identified HCC driver genes (next-generation sequencing) include TERT, ARID1A, ARID2, RPS6KA3, PIK3CA, IRF2, NFE2L2, and KEAP1, in addition to the previously known TP53, CTNNB1, and AXIN1 [PMID:24735922](../papers/24735922.md).
- HCAs carrying CTNNB1 mutations have a high risk of malignant transformation into HCC and should be surgically resected; TERT promoter mutations are observed in 17% of borderline HCA/HCC lesions and 56% of HCA with overt HCC transformation, but absent in classical HCA — supporting TERT promoter as a biomarker of malignant transformation [PMID:24735922](../papers/24735922.md).
- A 186-gene "poor survival signature" from tumor-adjacent cirrhotic tissue predicts overall survival and late recurrence after resection; it is present in ~20% of universal cirrhotic populations and is predictive of future HCC development in newly diagnosed hepatitis-C cirrhosis [PMID:24735922](../papers/24735922.md).
- A 5-gene tumor score based on [TAF9](../genes/TAF9.md), [RAN](../genes/RAN.md), [RAMP3](../genes/RAMP3.md), [KRT19](../genes/KRT19.md), and [JPT1](../genes/JPT1.md) (formerly HN1) expression predicts early recurrence and survival after resection, externally validated in 748 HCC samples worldwide [PMID:24735922](../papers/24735922.md).
- The G3 transcriptomic subgroup (Boyault classification) — characterized by TP53 mutation, [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) inactivation, and cell-cycle gene overexpression — was the most accurate single signature for predicting post-resection recurrence in a comparison of 18 signatures [PMID:24735922](../papers/24735922.md).
- A 13-gene RT-PCR panel (TERT, [IGF2](../genes/IGF2.md), GJB2, [TEK](../genes/TEK.md), [TIAM1](../genes/TIAM1.md), CXCL12, [TOP2A](../genes/TOP2A.md), A2M, PLG, CDKN2A, [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md), [MKI67](../genes/MKI67.md), THBS1) distinguishes dysplastic cirrhotic nodules from early HCC; a more restricted 3-gene panel ([GPC3](../genes/GPC3.md), LYVE1, survivin/BIRC5) achieves 95% sensitivity and 94% specificity for discriminating HGDN from tumors <2 cm [PMID:24735922](../papers/24735922.md).
- The IHC triplet glypican-3 + HSP70 + glutamine synthetase (GS) discriminates early HCC from high-grade dysplastic nodules with 46–72% sensitivity and 100% specificity and is endorsed by EASL guidelines [PMID:24735922](../papers/24735922.md).
- mTOR signaling is disrupted in 40–50% of HCCs (candidate population for [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md), which failed in unselected second-line trials) [PMID:24735922](../papers/24735922.md).
- c-MET is mutated in ~3% of HCC but activated in ~50% of advanced HCC; high c-MET expression has been used to enrich for response in a [tivantinib](../drugs/tivantinib.md) phase II trial (rationale for phase III) [PMID:24735922](../papers/24735922.md).
- Focal amplifications recurrently identified in HCC: 7q31 ([MET](../genes/MET.md)), 11q13 ([FGF19](../genes/FGF19.md)), 6p21 ([VEGFA](../genes/VEGFA.md)), and 8q ([MYC](../genes/MYC.md)) — VEGFA gains have been associated with benefit from [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md) [PMID:24735922](../papers/24735922.md).
- First recurrent gene fusion reported in HCC: [ABCB11](../genes/ABCB11.md)–[LRP2](../genes/LRP2.md) [PMID:24735922](../papers/24735922.md).

## Genes & alterations

- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) — promoter activating mutations in ~59% of HCC and ~25% of cirrhotic preneoplastic nodules; late-event biomarker for HCA-to-HCC transformation (17% of borderline lesions; 56% of overtly transformed HCA).
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — mutated in ~30% of HCC; co-feature of the G3 proliferative/poor-prognosis subgroup.
- [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) — β-catenin activating mutations in ~30% of HCC and a high-risk feature in HCA for malignant transformation.
- [AXIN1](../genes/AXIN1.md) — Wnt-pathway driver, previously known HCC driver.
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md) — chromatin remodeling drivers identified by NGS in HCC.
- RPS6KA3 — novel HCC driver identified by integrated genomic analysis (no canonical wiki page yet).
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — PI3K/AKT pathway driver in HCC.
- IRF2, [NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md), [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md) — drivers linked to oxidative-stress signaling in HCC.
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — inactivated in the G3 proliferative subgroup.
- [MET](../genes/MET.md) — mutated in ~3% of HCC; located at 7q31 focal amplification; high expression used as enrichment biomarker for [tivantinib](../drugs/tivantinib.md).
- [FGF19](../genes/FGF19.md) — 11q13 focal amplification; therapeutic rationale for FGFR-targeted trials.
- VEGFA — 6p21 focal gain; reported predictor of sorafenib benefit (no canonical wiki page yet).
- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) — 8q chromosomal gain in HCC.
- [IGF2](../genes/IGF2.md), [IGF2R](../genes/IGF2R.md), [IGF1R](../genes/IGF1R.md) — IGF axis: ~10% of HCC overexpress IGF2; ~25% have IGF2R allelic loss; [IGF1R](../genes/IGF1R.md) activated in 21% of HCC.
- [EGF](../genes/EGF.md) (rs4444903 SNP) — candidate-gene variant associated with elevated HCC risk in cirrhotic Caucasians.
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [RAF1](../genes/RAF1.md) — RAS/RAF/MAPK pathway components; mutations <5% in HCC but pathway universally activated in advanced disease.
- [EPCAM](../genes/EPCAM.md), KRT19, GPC3 — stem-cell/progenitor-cell signature markers; prognostic in subsets of HCC.
- [HSPA1A](../genes/HSPA1A.md) (HSP70), [GLUL](../genes/GLUL.md) (glutamine synthetase) — components of the EASL-endorsed diagnostic IHC triplet for early HCC.
- [MICA](../genes/MICA.md), [DEPDC5](../genes/DEPDC5.md), [KIF1B](../genes/KIF1B.md), [UBE4B](../genes/UBE4B.md), [PGD](../genes/PGD.md), [DLC1](../genes/DLC1.md), [STAT4](../genes/STAT4.md) — GWAS susceptibility loci/SNPs associated with HBV- or HCV-related HCC.
- ABCB11–LRP2 — first recurrent gene fusion reported in HCC.
- TAF9, RAN, RAMP3, KRT19, JPT1 (formerly HN1) — 5-gene tumor score predicting HCC recurrence and survival after resection.

## Clinical implications

- **Chemoprevention stratification:** The 186-gene poor-survival cirrhotic-tissue signature (present in ~20% of cirrhotics) is proposed as a tool to enrich chemopreventive trials for patients at highest risk of developing HCC [PMID:24735922](../papers/24735922.md).
- **Diagnosis of small nodules:** The IHC panel (GPC3 + HSP70 + GS) and the qRT-PCR 3-gene set (GPC3, LYVE1, survivin) are clinically usable to discriminate high-grade dysplastic nodules from early HCC where imaging is ambiguous; the IHC panel is endorsed by EASL guidelines [PMID:24735922](../papers/24735922.md).
- **HCA management:** CTNNB1 mutation status in hepatocellular adenoma identifies lesions at high risk of malignant transformation and is an indication for surgical resection; TERT promoter mutation can serve as a biomarker of overt HCA→HCC transformation [PMID:24735922](../papers/24735922.md).
- **Adjuvant trial enrichment:** Combined cirrhotic poor-survival signature plus the 5-gene tumor score (or G3-subgroup classification) can identify post-resection patients at highest recurrence risk who would benefit from adjuvant systemic therapy trials [PMID:24735922](../papers/24735922.md).
- **Targeted therapy enrichment for advanced HCC:** The review explicitly proposes biomarker-defined enrollment strategies — high c-MET expression for [tivantinib](../drugs/tivantinib.md); RAS mutations for the MEK inhibitor refametinib (NCT01915602); FGF pathway alterations for FGFR inhibitor trials (NCT01948297, NCT01004224); VEGFA amplification for [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md) benefit; mTOR-pathway dysregulation (40–50% of HCC) for [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md); HDAC inhibition with [vorinostat](../drugs/vorinostat.md) (NCT01075113) [PMID:24735922](../papers/24735922.md).
- **Resistance biology:** Authors note that acquired resistance — analogous to ALK-fusion lung cancer treated with [crizotinib](../drugs/crizotinib.md) or BRAF-mutant melanoma treated with [vemurafenib](../drugs/vemurafenib.md) — is expected for HCC targeted therapies and motivates combination regimens (driver-targeted plus broad-spectrum agents like sorafenib) and second-generation inhibitors [PMID:24735922](../papers/24735922.md).

## Limitations & open questions

- This is a narrative review, not a systematic one; signature comparisons and prevalence figures are aggregated across heterogeneous cohorts and platforms.
- Authors call out that most SNP-association studies in HCC suffer from poor control selection (other liver disease patients), retrospective single-center designs, underpowering, and lack of cross-ancestry validation [PMID:24735922](../papers/24735922.md).
- Molecular signatures have been derived across heterogeneous microarray and qRT-PCR platforms; cross-platform robustness, frozen→FFPE portability, and external validation remain incomplete for most published signatures [PMID:24735922](../papers/24735922.md).
- Stem-cell signatures (EPCAM, CK19) have been challenged in early-stage non-HBV HCC cohorts — the authors flag that their prognostic value may be restricted to specific etiologies or advanced stages [PMID:24735922](../papers/24735922.md).
- mir26 prognostic/predictive value (response to adjuvant IFN-α) is derived from HBV-related cohorts and not yet validated in Western HCC where IFN-α is not adjuvant standard [PMID:24735922](../papers/24735922.md).
- Open question: whether different tumors within the same patient share driver alterations or differ — has direct implications for biopsy-based molecular classification and for liquid-biopsy strategies anticipated by the authors [PMID:24735922](../papers/24735922.md).
- No targeted therapy has yet exceeded sorafenib in HCC phase III trials in first- or second-line; the review explicitly frames this as the central translational gap [PMID:24735922](../papers/24735922.md).
