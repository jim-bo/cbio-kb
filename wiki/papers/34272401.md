**PMID:** 34272401 · **DOI:** 10.1038/s41525-021-00226-4 · **Journal:** npj Genomic Medicine (2021)

## TL;DR

Chang and Shain performed a meta-analysis of publicly available whole-exome/whole-genome sequencing data from 10 prior studies of cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma ([CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md)), reprocessing 105 tumors and retaining 88 after quality control. Using four cancer-gene discovery tools ([MutSig](../methods/mutsig.md), [LOFsigrank](../methods/lofsigrank.md), [dN/dS](../methods/dndscv.md), [OncodriveFML](../methods/oncodrivefml.md)) plus [cancer hotspots](../methods/cancer-hotspots.md) and focal-CNA analysis, they nominate 30 driver genes that operate in the NOTCH, p53, cell-cycle, SWI/SNF chromatin-remodeling, Hippo, stress-response, and MAPK/PI3K pathways. They highlight four genes mutated in >10% of tumors that prior CSCC studies had missed — [EP300](../genes/EP300.md), [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md), [USP28](../genes/USP28.md), and [CHUK](../genes/CHUK.md) — and deposit their reanalyzed mutation calls in cBioPortal as study [cscc_ucsf_2021](../datasets/cscc_ucsf_2021.md).

## Cohort & data

- **105 tumors** aggregated from **10 published whole-exome / whole-genome sequencing studies** of CSCC with publicly available raw sequencing data (Durinck 2011, Wang 2011, South 2014, Zheng 2014, Cammareri 2016, Chitsazzadeh 2016, Yilmaz 2017, Cho 2018, Inman 2018, Ji 2020). After QC, **88 tumors retained** for downstream analyses; 17 removed for low neoplastic cell content, low reference coverage, sample mismatch, or tumor contamination of reference.
- Cancer type: cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma ([CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md)).
- Dataset: reanalyzed mutation calls deposited at [cscc_ucsf_2021](../datasets/cscc_ucsf_2021.md) on cBioPortal.
- Reference genome: **hg19**. Reads aligned with [BWA-MEM v0.7.13](../methods/bwa.md); deduplicated with [GATK v4.1.2.0](../methods/gatk.md) and Picard v4.1.2.0; somatic SNVs called with [MuTect2 v4.1.2.0](../methods/mutect.md) and annotated with Funcotator v4.1.2.0; indels called with [Pindel v0.2.5](../methods/pindel.md); copy-number alterations called with [CNVkit](../methods/cnvkit.md) in reference mode using per-study panels of normals.
- Tumor cellularity inferred per sample (range **12% – 99%**); mean sequencing coverage **12.4× – 498×**; the [Footprints](../methods/footprints.md) software quantified per-sample "call-able" footprint (the basepair count with sufficient coverage to call somatic mutations). Mean call-able coverage was **91.2%** of target bases (range **52.3% – ~100%**).
- Five clinical/etiologic subtypes were resolved by mutational-signature analysis: **xeroderma pigmentosum (XP)**, sporadic, immunosuppressed without azathioprine, immunosuppressed on azathioprine, and **recessive dystrophic epidermolysis bullosa (RDEB)**.

## Key findings

- **30 cancer genes nominated** in CSCC across the meta-analysis (Fig. 4b). 12 genes were called by the discovery tools ([MutSig](../methods/mutsig.md), [LOFsigrank](../methods/lofsigrank.md), [dN/dS](../methods/dndscv.md), [OncodriveFML](../methods/oncodrivefml.md)), of which 7 were called by ≥2 tools. Additional candidates came from [cancerhotspots.org](../methods/cancer-hotspots.md) overlap and from focal copy-number analysis.
- **NOTCH-pathway** loss-of-function mutations were present in **79.5% of tumors** ([NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) 55.4%, [NOTCH2](../genes/NOTCH2.md) 36.1%). **p53-pathway** loss-of-function in **71.1%** ([TP53](../genes/TP53.md) 66.3%, [USP28](../genes/USP28.md) 12.0%, [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) 2.4%). The authors call these "defining features" of CSCC.
- **Cell-cycle-checkpoint** (Rb pathway) mutations in **38.6%**, primarily [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) (34.9%, including focal homozygous deletions).
- **SWI/SNF chromatin remodeling complex** mutations in **38.6%** ([ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md) 27.7%, [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md) 12%, [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) 2.4%).
- **Hippo-pathway** mutations in **37.3%** ([FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md) 30.1%, [AJUBA](../genes/AJUBA.md) 7.2%, [YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md) 1.2%).
- **Stress-response genes** (grouped [CASP8](../genes/CASP8.md), [CHUK](../genes/CHUK.md), [NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md)) mutated in **32.5%** of tumors collectively.
- **MAPK / PI3K** activating mutations in **31.3%** ([HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) 9.6%, [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) 6.0%, [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) 6.0%, plus [RAP1B](../genes/RAP1B.md), [RAC1](../genes/RAC1.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [RRAS2](../genes/RRAS2.md), [GNA11](../genes/GNA11.md), [GNAS](../genes/GNAS.md), [ERBB3](../genes/ERBB3.md), [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md)).
- **Four newly nominated genes** mutated in >10% of tumors but not previously implicated in CSCC by other studies: [EP300](../genes/EP300.md), [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md), [USP28](../genes/USP28.md), [CHUK](../genes/CHUK.md).
- **Focal copy-number alterations** affected: homozygous deletions of [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) and [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md); heterozygous deletion of [AJUBA](../genes/AJUBA.md) (combined with a point mutation on the other allele in the same tumor); focal amplifications of [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md), [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md), [YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md), and [RAP1B](../genes/RAP1B.md) (the RAP1B-amplified tumor also carried an activating point mutation on the amplified allele).
- **[TERT](../genes/TERT.md) was included** in the final 30-gene list despite the exome-focused study being unable to interrogate the locus, because prior studies report frequent TERT-promoter mutations in CSCC.
- **Subtype-specific selection:** [CASP8](../genes/CASP8.md) mutations were significantly enriched in [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md) from RDEB patients (chronic blistering / inflammation), consistent with CASP8's role in inflammatory-cytokine-induced apoptosis. After multiple-testing correction, no other gene/pathway/subtype enrichment reached significance.
- **Mutational-signature subtypes:** XP tumors (n=2) had high TMB and high C>T at CC dipyrimidines but lacked canonical "signature 7" — attributed to differing trinucleotide contexts in mutant-XPC tumors. Azathioprine-treated immunosuppressed tumors carried high signature 32 burdens. RDEB tumors had relatively low TMB primarily from APOBEC mutagenesis.
- **Genes "noted but not nominated" curated as likely false positives / requiring more evidence:** [COL11A1](../genes/COL11A1.md) (borderline q-value, unusual gene structure with many small exons, low expression in keratinocytes), [DIS3](../genes/DIS3.md), [H3C3](../genes/H3C3.md) (HIST1H3C), [KDR](../genes/KDR.md), [MAP2K4](../genes/MAP2K4.md) (OncoKB classifies their hotspots as unlikely to be oncogenic), and [KNSTRN](../genes/KNSTRN.md) S24F (RNA-seq suggests the mutation lies in the promoter, not coding, mirroring TERT/melanoma promoter-vs-coding annotation errors).
- **[KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md)** — implicated in >1 other CSCC study but not nominated here because the majority of observed KMT2D mutations were silent or predicted-benign missense.

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — mutated in **66.3%** of tumors; defining p53-pathway lesion in CSCC.
- [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) (55.4%) and [NOTCH2](../genes/NOTCH2.md) (36.1%) — NOTCH-pathway loss-of-function, ubiquitous across CSCC.
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — 34.9%, including focal homozygous deletions.
- [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md) — 30.1%, Hippo-pathway tumor suppressor.
- [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md) — 27.7%; [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md) — 12% — both SWI/SNF chromatin-remodeling subunits.
- [CASP8](../genes/CASP8.md) — 22.9%, selectively enriched in RDEB CSCC.
- [EP300](../genes/EP300.md) — 21.7%; loss-of-function mutations including missense mutations clustered in the histone acetyltransferase domain; some have been functionally validated to abolish HAT activity. Highlighted as a novel CSCC driver in this study.
- [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md) — 15.7%; paralog of EP300.
- [USP28](../genes/USP28.md) — 12.0%; high frequency of truncating mutations. Encodes a deubiquitinase that stabilizes DNA-repair proteins and is required for Chk2–p53–PUMA-mediated apoptosis. Novel CSCC driver in this study.
- [CHUK](../genes/CHUK.md) — 10.8%; encodes IKKα. High truncating-mutation frequency with biallelic alterations in most mutated tumors. Knockout mice and humans with defective CHUK fail to differentiate keratinocytes; a Sleeping-Beauty transposon screen previously implicated CHUK in keratinocyte tumorigenesis on a Pten-sensitized background. Novel CSCC driver in this study.
- [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) — 9.6%; canonical hotspot codons (G12D, G13D, G13R, Q61L, Q61K observed).
- [AJUBA](../genes/AJUBA.md) — 7.2%; one tumor had a focal heterozygous deletion plus a point mutation on the other allele.
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) (6.0%) and [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) (6.0%) — PI3K pathway; PTEN focally deleted in some tumors.
- [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) — 6.0%, focal amplifications.
- [RAP1B](../genes/RAP1B.md) — 2.4%; the amplified tumor also carried an activating point mutation analogous to known Ras-activating residues.
- [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) — 2.4%, focal amplification.
- [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) — 2.4%, hotspot Y646N observed.
- [RAC1](../genes/RAC1.md) — 2.4%, P29S/P29L hotspots.
- [YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md) — 1.2%, focal amplification.
- [NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md) — 1.2%.
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [RRAS2](../genes/RRAS2.md), [GNA11](../genes/GNA11.md), [GNAS](../genes/GNAS.md), [ERBB3](../genes/ERBB3.md), [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md) — 1.2% each, hotspot mutations.
- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) — included in the 30-gene set on the basis of prior literature reporting frequent promoter mutations; not directly interrogated here.
- [XPC](../genes/XPC.md) — germline loss in two xeroderma-pigmentosum patients; modifies the trinucleotide context of UV-induced mutations.
- [COL7A1](../genes/COL7A1.md) — germline cause of RDEB; not a somatic CSCC driver, but the RDEB context drives the CASP8 enrichment.
- "Possible false positive" / additional-evidence-needed: [COL11A1](../genes/COL11A1.md), [DIS3](../genes/DIS3.md), [H3C3](../genes/H3C3.md), [KDR](../genes/KDR.md), [MAP2K4](../genes/MAP2K4.md), [KNSTRN](../genes/KNSTRN.md), [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) P551L (a single hotspot call), and [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md) (single hotspot call).
- Nominated in prior CSCC studies but not by this meta-analysis: [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) — flagged by the authors as a candidate that may yet prove to be under selection but for which the present data did not support nomination.

## Clinical implications

- The authors frame CSCC management context: current standard of care is **immune-checkpoint blockade**, to which roughly half of patients do not respond and responses are not always durable; immunosuppressed patients (especially organ-transplant recipients) carry ~100-fold elevated CSCC risk and are typically ineligible for immunotherapy. The driver-gene catalog is presented as a source of "points of therapeutic intervention," but the paper does not test specific drugs or report response/survival outcomes.
- Mechanistic implications: the high prevalence of **NOTCH** and **p53** pathway loss-of-function suggests these pathways are essential constraints whose inactivation is near-obligate in CSCC. The novel **EP300 / PBRM1 / USP28 / CHUK** nominations point at chromatin-remodeling, DNA-damage response, and NFκB signaling as additional vulnerabilities.
- Subtype-specific biology: **CASP8 enrichment in RDEB-CSCC** is interpreted as a selective response to chronic inflammation in blistered skin — a biology-of-context insight rather than a directly actionable biomarker.
- Sample-quality message for downstream cBioPortal users of [cscc_ucsf_2021](../datasets/cscc_ucsf_2021.md): the authors document per-tumor cellularity and call-able-footprint estimates and recommend accounting for these technical variables when comparing mutation burdens or driver-gene frequencies across tumors.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Exome-only for most studies.** Non-coding driver mutations (notably the TERT promoter, included on prior-literature grounds without direct evidence here) and structural variants / viral integrations could not be systematically assessed.
- **Power floor ~15% mutation frequency.** Despite being the largest CSCC cohort to date (88 QC-passed tumors), the discovery tools could only detect cancer genes mutated in ≥15% of tumors; rarer drivers were rescued only through hotspot or focal-CNA overlap. The authors explicitly state CSCC has **not yet reached driver-discovery saturation**.
- **Curation judgment calls.** The "likely false positive" / "additional evidence needed" classifications for [COL11A1](../genes/COL11A1.md), [DIS3](../genes/DIS3.md), [H3C3](../genes/H3C3.md), [KDR](../genes/KDR.md), [MAP2K4](../genes/MAP2K4.md), and [KNSTRN](../genes/KNSTRN.md) S24F rest on gene structure, expression, OncoKB hotspot classification, or annotation correctness — useful filters but not formal tests.
- **No functional validation in this study.** EP300, PBRM1, USP28, and CHUK as CSCC drivers are nominated on statistical/mutational grounds; functional/mechanistic confirmation is left to future work.
- **Heterogeneous source studies.** 10 source cohorts with different sequencing depths, capture kits, neoplastic-cell content, and sample-collection methods. The authors mitigate this with the [Footprints](../methods/footprints.md) call-able-footprint framework but cannot fully eliminate batch effects.
- **No germline-cancer-risk analysis** beyond the XP / RDEB contextual annotations.
