# C9

## Overview

C9 (Complement Component 9) encodes the terminal component of the complement membrane-attack complex. In metastatic cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma (cSCC), C9 was identified as a novel candidate oncogenic driver by three independent computational driver-calling tools. A mechanistic link is proposed via tumor microenvironment modulation and macrophage biology — complement system dysregulation may contribute to immune evasion in this UV-driven malignancy. cBioPortal cross-cohort data corroborate recurrent C9 mutation in both primary and metastatic cSCC.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Novel candidate driver: SNVs in 52% (13/25) of metastatic cSCC samples (WGS cohort); identified as significant driver by all three tools (OncodriveFML, MutSigCV, dNdScv) — one of only three genes (alongside [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)) called by all three tools [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)
- cBioPortal cross-cohort validation: C9 mutated in 31% of primary cSCC and 10% of metastatic cSCC from existing MSK-IMPACT/FFPE cohorts on cBioPortal [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)
- Predicted miR-331-5p target: miR-331-5p was predicted to simultaneously target C9 and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), among six driver candidates, suggesting potential miRNA-mediated co-regulation [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md):** Recurrently mutated in 52% of regional metastatic cSCC (head and neck, lymph node metastases; WGS cohort n=25); confirmed recurrent mutation in external cBioPortal cSCC cohorts (31% primary, 10% metastatic) [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with TP53 (24/25 samples), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), and other candidate drivers including [KHDRBS2](../genes/KHDRBS2.md) and [COLEC12](../genes/COLEC12.md) in the same high-TMB UV-mutational background [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Complement inhibitors (e.g., anti-C5 antibodies) are established in non-oncologic contexts; their relevance in cSCC is not established. The proposed tumor-microenvironment mechanism suggests C9-directed complement modulation as a hypothetical approach but no clinical data exist [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Open questions

- Whether C9 mutations are functionally oncogenic (gain or loss of complement function) or represent UV-associated background noise in a very high-TMB tumor remains unresolved; cohort size (n=25) limits statistical power [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)
- The discrepancy in mutation frequency between the WGS cohort (52%) and existing cBioPortal cSCC data (31% primary, 10% metastatic) warrants prospective validation in larger WGS cohorts [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)
