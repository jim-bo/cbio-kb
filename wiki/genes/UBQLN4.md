# UBQLN4

## Overview

UBQLN4 (ubiquilin 4) encodes a ubiquitin-binding protein that regulates the Wnt/β-catenin pathway and is associated with genomic instability. It has been reported to repress homologous recombination (HR) repair, conferring sensitivity to PARP1 inhibitors — making it a candidate therapeutic target in cancers with UBQLN4 overexpression driven by copy-number amplification.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- UBQLN4 was identified as a cis-copy-number-driven candidate driver within the 1q21.3 GISTIC2 amplification peak in a 122-biopsy HCC proteogenomics cohort (hcc_meric_2021); dysregulated at both mRNA and protein levels co-regulated with copy number; among 9 genes dysregulated at both mRNA and protein across five recurrent focal CNA peaks [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)** — 1q21.3 amplification driving UBQLN4 overexpression at mRNA and protein levels in an integrated proteogenomics study of 114 treatment-naive patients; 1q amplification is one of the most frequent CNAs in HCC [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- UBQLN4 overexpression co-occurs with other 1q21.3 amplification-driven genes (CHD1L) in HCC; no mutual exclusivity data reported in the corpus [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- UBQLN4 has been reported to repress homologous recombination repair and confer PARP1-inhibitor sensitivity, suggesting a therapeutic angle for UBQLN4-overexpressing HCC tumors with 1q21.3 amplification [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Open questions

- The clinical efficacy of PARP inhibitors in UBQLN4-overexpressing HCC has not been prospectively evaluated; the relationship between UBQLN4 expression levels and degree of HR suppression requires quantitative characterization.
