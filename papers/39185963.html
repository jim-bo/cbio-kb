Chromothripsis-Mediated Small Cell Lung Carcinoma

Authors

Sam E. Tischfield

PMID: 39185963 · DOI: 10.1158/2159-8290.CD-24-0286 · Journal: Cancer Discovery (2025)

TL;DR

Rekhtman and colleagues at MSKCC define “atypical SCLC” (aSCLC) — a rare subset of small cell lung carcinoma in never/light smokers that lacks dual RB1 and TP53 inactivation. Using integrated MSK-IMPACT targeted NGS, WGS, RNA-seq, FISH, and IHC across 20 patients and 49 pathologic specimens, they show that 84% of non-MSI aSCLC harbor extensive chromothripsis — predominantly on chromosomes 11 and 12 — producing ecDNA amplification of CCND1 or co-amplification of CCND2/CDK4/MDM2. aSCLC shows histogenetic links to pulmonary carcinoids (carcinoid-to-SCLC plasticity), contrasting with EGFR/APOBEC-driven adenocarcinoma-to-SCLC plasticity in RB1−/TP53− never-smoker SCLC.

Cohort & data

  • Discovery cohort: 20 patients with aSCLC (3% of 600 consecutive de novo SCLC patients sequenced by MSK-IMPACT at MSKCC). All never/light smokers (≤10 pack-years).
  • Comparator cohorts on the same platform: smoking-associated SCLC (sSCLC, n = 206); never-smoker RB1−/TP53− SCLC (nsSCLC-RB1−/TP53−, n = 18); pulmonary carcinoids (LNET, n = 157); atypical carcinoids (n = 50).
  • Specimens: 49 pathologic specimens (1–7 per patient); 31 profiled by MSK-IMPACT tNGS, 12 by WGS (11 patients), 7 by RNA-seq.
  • Dataset: asclc_msk_2024 on cBioPortal; raw WGS/RNA-seq in dbGAP phs003676.v1.p1.
  • Assays/methods: MSK-IMPACT (341/468/505-gene versions); WGS on NovaSeq 6000 (~100× tumor / ~80× normal); FACETS for CN calling; ShatterSeek for chromothripsis; MSIsensor/MiMSI for MSI; OncoKB for driver annotation; FISH (3-color CCND1/CCND2/CDK4/MDM2); MutationTimeR for clonal timing.

Key findings

  • aSCLC is rare and demographically distinct. 20 of 600 (3%) SCLC cases were RB1+/TP53+; all were never or light (≤10 pack-year) smokers. Mean age 53 vs 67 years for other SCLC (P < 0.0001); youngest was 19 years old.
  • Lower-end Ki67 but classic SCLC morphology. Mean Ki67 69% in aSCLC vs 87% in other SCLC (P < 0.0001). 5/20 patients had carcinoid histotype in primary/intrathoracic samples alongside SCLC histotype in metastases.
  • Low TMB, no smoking signature. Most aSCLC had TMB < 2 Mut/Mb; outliers were temozolomide-treated case A07 and the single MSI-H case A20 (somatic MLH1 homozygous deletion + dual MLH1/PMS2 IHC loss). Smoking signature absent; dominant signatures reflected systemic therapy (e.g., temozolomide).
  • Carcinoid-like mutation spectrum. Recurrent SNVs in MEN1 (15%), EIF1AX (10%), ARID1A (25%), and ATM (30%). ATM mutation rate enriched vs carcinoids (8%, P = 0.008) and conventional SCLC (3%, P = 0.0003).
  • Recurrent, mutually exclusive oncogene amplifications. CCND1 amplified in 30%; CCND2/CDK4/±MDM2 co-amplified in 15%; MYCL amplified in 10%. CN often >10 copies, with case A02 harboring 125 copies of CCND1. CDK4 amplification was found in 0% of conventional SCLC (P = 0.0006) and 1% of carcinoids (P = 0.01).
  • Chromothripsis is the hallmark. 16/19 (84%) non-MSI aSCLC showed chromothripsis by combined WGS+tNGS. Chromosome 11 (35% of cases) was most commonly affected, always co-occurring with chromothripsis on chromosome 3; chromosome 12 (20%) and chromosome 1 (15%) followed. Average 565 SVs per case (range up to >1,900).
  • Chromothripsis localizes oncogene amplifications. Chr 11 chromothripsis in 5/6 CCND1-amplified cases; chr 12 in 3/3 CCND2/CDK4/±MDM2 cases; chr 1 in 2/2 MYCL-amplified cases.
  • ecDNA mechanism confirmed by FISH. All 5 FISH-tested cases showed extrachromosomal (double-minute) amplification of CCND1 or CCND2/CDK4/MDM2; one case had both extra- and intrachromosomal amplification.
  • Enhancer hijacking as alternative oncogene activation. Case A17 lacked CCND1 amplification but harbored a chromothripsis-derived rearrangement juxtaposing the active chromatin of SH3RF3 (chr 2) upstream of CCND1 (chr 11), with marked cyclin D1 overexpression by IHC and RNA-seq.
  • Novel tNGS-based chromothripsis detection. Manual detection from oscillating CNA patterns on FACETS hisens plots achieved 100% specificity and 77% sensitivity vs WGS gold standard (minimum 5 consecutive oscillating segments, CCF ≥50%).
  • Chromothripsis is early and clonally stable. MutationTimeR placed all chromothripsis-associated amplifications early in tumorigenesis. Multisample analysis across 14 patients showed conservation of chromothriptic chromosomes and amplifications across primary and metastatic specimens, independent of intervening systemic therapy.
  • Cell-cycle pathway deregulation in 75% of samples. pRb pathway deregulation at DNA, mRNA, or protein level in 75% of samples; aSCLC showed major upregulation of G2M-checkpoint and E2F-target signatures by RNA-seq exceeding carcinoids and approaching sSCLC.
  • Paradoxical p16 expression in chr 12 chromothripsis cases. All chr 12 chromothripsis cases (A07, A08, A09) were p16-high despite wild-type pRb — likely reflecting posttranslational pRb inhibition by ecDNA-amplified CDK4 creating a pRb-null–like state.
  • Acquired subclonal RB1 alterations in some metastases. Two patients (A16, A20) acquired private RB1 mutations in metastatic samples with concurrent pRb IHC loss; one primary (A12) showed a subclonal RB1 mutation (CCF 71%) with regional pRb loss.
  • Histogenetic link to carcinoids. 55% of aSCLC had carcinoid features by histology, OTP IHC/RNA expression, or carcinoid-characteristic mutations (MEN1, EIF1AX). aSCLC was uniformly ASCL1-dominant; YAP1 was absent in aSCLC but enriched in nsSCLC-RB1−/TP53−.
  • Two distinct pathways of plasticity-mediated SCLC pathogenesis in never-smokers. (1) Carcinoid-to-SCLC via chromothripsis in RB1+/TP53+ background (aSCLC), and (2) adenocarcinoma-to-SCLC via EGFR (39%) or KRAS (6%) mutations plus APOBEC mutagenesis in RB1−/TP53− background.
  • Distinct survival. Median overall survival in aSCLC was 58 months — intermediate between sSCLC (16 months), nsSCLC-RB1−/TP53− (22 months), and atypical carcinoids (184 months).
  • Reduced platinum sensitivity. Among 15 evaluable patients, platinum-based therapy yielded 13% CR and 20% PR — well below the historical ~70% response rate for conventional SCLC.
  • Suggested benefit from immunotherapy and TMZ. 3/5 patients treated with immune checkpoint inhibitors remained on therapy 2 to >5 years; 4/6 temozolomide-treated patients had >10 months on treatment (up to 2 years). Long TMZ-responders (A01, A07) had lowest MGMT expression by RNA-seq.

Genes & alterations

  • RB1, TP53 — proficient at baseline in all aSCLC by integrated genomic + IHC analysis; defines the subset. Acquired subclonal RB1 mutations seen in some metastatic samples (A12, A16, A20) without overt proliferation escalation.
  • CCND1 (11q13) — amplified in 30% of aSCLC, typically as ecDNA double-minutes from chr 11 chromothripsis; up to 125 copies. Two cases (A11, A17) overexpressed CCND1 without amplification — A17 via SH3RF3-CCND1 enhancer hijacking.
  • CCND2 (12q13), CDK4 (12q14), MDM2 (12q15) — co-amplified in 15% via chr 12 chromothripsis-derived ecDNA. May functionally phenocopy TP53 + RB1 inactivation.
  • MYCL (1p34) — amplified in 10% via chr 1 chromothripsis.
  • HRAS, ERBB3, KRAS — additional amplifications co-localized to chromothriptic chromosomes (11p15, 12q13, 12q12 respectively).
  • MEN1, EIF1AX, ARID1A — carcinoid-characteristic SNVs/deletions; MEN1 (11q13) and ARID1A (1p36) deletions co-localize with chromothriptic chromosomes 11 and 1.
  • ATM — somatic mutations in 30% (enriched vs carcinoids and SCLC); no pathogenic germline ATM variants identified. Authors hypothesize ATM disabling could be permissive for chromothripsis via micronucleus formation.
  • TGFBR2 (3p24) — deleted in 2/7 chr 3 chromothripsis cases; the only recurrent driver event on chromothriptic chromosome 3.
  • CDKN2A — paradoxically p16-high (overexpression by IHC and RNA-seq) in all chr 12 chromothripsis cases despite wild-type pRb.
  • MLH1/PMS2 — somatic MLH1 homozygous deletion with dual IHC loss in the single MSI-H case (A20), an alternative genomic context to chromothripsis.
  • ASCL1, NEUROD1, POU2F3, YAP1 — all evaluated aSCLC were ASCL1-dominant; YAP1 entirely absent in aSCLC vs enriched in nsSCLC-RB1−/TP53−.
  • DLL3, SEZ6 — uniformly high expression in aSCLC (mean H scores 278 and 240 respectively across 9 evaluated samples); both are emerging immunotherapy and ADC targets in SCLC.
  • OTP — carcinoid-specific marker expressed by IHC and RNA-seq in a subset of aSCLC, supporting histogenetic link to carcinoids.
  • TERT — no TERT overexpression detected; alternative lengthening of telomeres footprints also absent.
  • ALDH1L2 — sole in-frame fusion involving a likely-oncogenic gene (folate regulator) by OncoKB across all RNA-seq cases; not recurrent. Authors conclude fusions are unlikely dominant drivers in aSCLC.
  • SH3RF3 — donor of active chromatin in the case A17 SH3RF3-CCND1 enhancer-hijacking rearrangement.
  • EGFR, KRAS — canonical driver mutations found in 39% and 6% of nsSCLC-RB1−/TP53− respectively, anchoring the adenocarcinoma-to-SCLC pathway; absent in aSCLC.

Clinical implications

  • RB1/TP53 IHC + smoking history as triage for aSCLC. Routine IHC-based assessment of pRb and p53 can flag candidate aSCLC, with potential prognostic and therapeutic stratification implications.
  • Distinct prognosis. aSCLC has substantially longer median OS than smoking-related SCLC (58 vs 16 months), warranting separate clinical consideration.
  • Reduced platinum sensitivity. Only 33% achieved CR/PR on platinum-based chemotherapy (carboplatin/cisplatin + etoposide) — well below the conventional SCLC benchmark of ~70%.
  • Potential benefit from immune checkpoint inhibitors. 3/5 ICI-treated patients (including those receiving atezolizumab) remained on therapy 2 to >5 years.
  • Temozolomide shows promise. 4/6 TMZ-treated patients had >10 months on therapy (up to 2 years); long responders had lowest MGMT expression. Relevant given dual histology link to SCLC and carcinoids — TMZ is active in both.
  • Emerging targets: Uniformly high DLL3 and SEZ6 expression suggests aSCLC patients may benefit from emerging DLL3-directed (e.g., tarlatamab class) and SEZ6-directed agents under clinical development.
  • CDK4 and MDM2 inhibition as rationale-based options. Recurrent CDK4 and MDM2 ecDNA amplification provides genomic rationale for CDK4 and MDM2 inhibitors, plus emerging ecDNA-targeting agents.
  • Method advance for clinical sequencing. First demonstration that chromothripsis can be detected from panel NGS (MSK-IMPACT) using oscillating CNA pattern recognition with 100% specificity and 77% sensitivity vs WGS — enables wider clinical recognition.

Limitations & open questions

  • Small cohort (n = 20). The aSCLC cohort is too small for robust subgroup analyses of treatment outcomes by genomic feature.
  • tNGS sensitivity for chromothripsis is incomplete (77%). Case A06 had CCND1 amplification (23 copies) without detectable chromothripsis on tNGS alone; false negatives cannot be excluded.
  • Trigger of chromothripsis remains unclear. No pathogenic germline variants in 90 cancer-predisposition genes among 17 evaluable patients; no viral DNA detected; no TERT overexpression or alternative telomere lengthening footprints.
  • Mechanism of carcinoid-to-SCLC transition. Matched primary-metastatic samples (A05, A17) shared chromothripsis without acquiring additional drivers in the SCLC component; epigenetic reprogramming is hypothesized but not directly demonstrated.
  • Functional role of acquired subclonal RB1 loss in aSCLC is unclear — does not correlate with proliferation escalation, suggesting a noncanonical role outside E2F-mediated cell cycle control.
  • Specific target gene(s) of chr 3 chromothripsis are not established. Only TGFBR2 deletion was recurrent (2/7); the broader 3p loss observed in >90% of SCLC remains mechanistically opaque.
  • Generalizability beyond never/light smokers. A prior WGS report (George et al.) identified one chromothripsis-associated RB1+/TP53+ SCLC in a 30-pack-year smoker, suggesting tobacco exposure does not strictly preclude this pathway.
  • No prospective treatment data. Therapeutic vulnerability claims (CDK4i, MDM2i, ecDNA-targeting agents, ICI, TMZ) are hypothesis-generating; prospective trials enriched for aSCLC are needed.
