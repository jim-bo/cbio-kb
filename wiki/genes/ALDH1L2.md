# ALDH1L2

## Overview

ALDH1L2 (Aldehyde Dehydrogenase 1 Family Member L2) encodes a mitochondrial aldehyde dehydrogenase involved in the folate/one-carbon metabolic pathway. In melanoma, ALDH1L2 expression is associated with mesenchymal/invasive transcriptional programs that correlate with innate resistance to anti-PD-1 immunotherapy.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Expression associated with the IPRES (innate anti-PD-1 resistance) transcriptional signature in pretreatment metastatic melanoma tumors; upregulated in non-responding tumors as part of a wound-healing/ECM/mesenchymal program [PMID:26997480](../papers/26997480.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md) (melanoma):** ALDH1L2 expression linked to the IPRES innate anti-PD-1 resistance program; elevated in pretreatment non-responding tumors [PMID:26997480](../papers/26997480.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-enriched with other IPRES genes including [AXL](../genes/AXL.md), [ROR2](../genes/ROR2.md), [WNT5A](../genes/WNT5A.md), [LOXL2](../genes/LOXL2.md), [TWIST2](../genes/TWIST2.md) in non-responding melanoma [PMID:26997480](../papers/26997480.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- ALDH1L2 expression as a component of IPRES may be relevant to stratification of patients for anti-PD-1 therapy; no direct therapeutic agent targets this gene [PMID:26997480](../papers/26997480.md)

## Open questions

- Whether pharmacological suppression of the IPRES program (including ALDH1L2) restores anti-PD-1 sensitivity is unresolved and identified as a next step [PMID:26997480](../papers/26997480.md)
