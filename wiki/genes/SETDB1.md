# SETDB1

## Overview

SETDB1 encodes a histone H3K9 methyltransferase involved in transcriptional repression. In the corpus, somatic loss-of-function mutations in SETDB1 are the defining molecular feature of genomic near-haploidization (GNH) in diffuse pleural mesothelioma (DPM), a newly recognized aggressive subset.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Somatic loss-of-function mutations in SETDB1 were identified in 8 of 10 GNH DPM cases; absent in non-GNH DPMs. SETDB1 inactivation in the context of genome-wide loss of heterozygosity may rescue expression of monoallelically expressed genes [PMID:38630790](../papers/38630790.md).
- SETDB1 mutations are highly specific for the GNH DPM subset and may serve as a molecular marker when detected on panels covering this gene (e.g., [IMPACT505](../methods/IMPACT505.md)) [PMID:38630790](../papers/38630790.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [PLMESO](../cancer_types/PLMESO.md) — loss-of-function mutations define the GNH subset (n=10); associated with poor [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) (10.9 months) and possible ICB sensitivity [PMID:38630790](../papers/38630790.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- SETDB1 mutations co-occur with early clonal [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations in GNH DPMs; [NF2](../genes/NF2.md) alterations are frequent but confounded by biphasic histology enrichment in this subset [PMID:38630790](../papers/38630790.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Among 3 GNH patients treated with ipilimumab/nivolumab or [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md), 2 achieved partial response (67%), vs. only 1/44 (2%) in non-GNH patients, suggesting SETDB1-mutant/GNH DPM may be ICB-sensitive [PMID:38630790](../papers/38630790.md).

## Open questions

- Sample size of GNH cases is small (n=10 primary, n=4 validation); larger prospective cohorts are required to confirm ICB response rates and validate SETDB1 as a diagnostic biomarker [PMID:38630790](../papers/38630790.md).
