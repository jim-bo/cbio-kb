**PMID:** 35078859 · **DOI:** 10.1158/1078-0432.CCR-21-2451 · **Journal:** Clinical Cancer Research (2022)

## TL;DR

Spitzer and colleagues at MSKCC profiled 199 serial bone marrow samples from 52 high-risk neuroblastoma patients (17 who developed therapy-related MDS/AL, 14 with transient cytogenetic abnormalities, 21 controls) using HemePACT targeted DNA capture and ArcherDX FusionPlex Pan-Heme RNA sequencing. They detected at least one t-MDS/AL-associated mutation in 13/17 transformation patients a median of 15 months before overt leukemia diagnosis, identified two novel [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) fusion partners ([PRDM10](../genes/PRDM10.md), [DDX6](../genes/DDX6.md)), and showed that — unlike adults — [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) clones in pediatric patients were only detectable after initiation of cytotoxic therapy. In a separate cohort of 657 pediatric solid-tumor patients profiled with MSK-IMPACT, only 1.05% had oncogenic clonal hematopoiesis (CH-PD) in myeloid genes, supporting longitudinal (rather than single-timepoint) surveillance for pediatric survivors.

## Cohort & data

- **Primary neuroblastoma cohort:** 52 patients with [NBL](../cancer_types/NBL.md) treated at MSKCC (diagnosed 1988–2009, enrolled on institutional protocol #00-109 / NCT00588068), contributing 199 serial bone-marrow samples.
  - Transformation cohort (developed [TMN](../cancer_types/TMN.md) / [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md) / [MDS](../cancer_types/MDS.md)): 17 patients, 88 samples.
  - Transient cohort (cytogenetic abnormality without t-MDS/AL): 14 patients, 74 samples.
  - Neuroblastoma-treated control cohort: 21 patients, 37 samples.
- **Extended pediatric solid-tumor cohort:** 657 patients ≤25 years old seen in MSKCC Pediatrics (144 neuroblastoma + 513 other solid tumors), profiled by [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md). Subset deposited in cBioPortal as [msk_ch_ped_2021](../datasets/msk_ch_ped_2021.md).
- **Assays:**
  - [HemePACT v3/v4](../methods/msk-hemepact.md) targeted DNA capture (585 / 576 hematologic-malignancy genes; median coverage 705× on BM samples).
  - [ArcherDX FusionPlex Pan-Heme](../methods/archer-fusionplex.md) RNA panel for recurrent fusions.
  - [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) (341/410/468-gene tumor + matched-blood panel) for CH detection in the extended cohort.
  - [Droplet digital PCR](../methods/droplet-digital-pcr.md) for TP53 hotspot backtracking (C135S, V172F, R175H, R213P, R249S, L257Q, G266R) on 28 additional time points.
- **Reference genome:** NCBI build 37 (BWA v0.7.17); variant calling with CaVEMan, Mutect, Strelka, Pindel.

## Key findings

- At t-MDS/AL diagnosis, every case (17/17) had at least one disease-defining alteration detectable by integrated capture-based sequencing, vs. 13/17 (76.5%) by conventional cytogenetics — 39 putative oncogenic events total (median 2/patient, range 1–4) plus 32 VUS.
- Most frequent drivers at transformation: [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) rearrangements (n=6 patients) and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations (n=9 variants in n=5 patients); these were mutually exclusive (1 exception with a clonal KMT2A translocation and a subclonal TP53 mutation).
- KMT2A fusion partners: canonical [ELL](../genes/ELL.md) and [AFF1](../genes/AFF1.md), less common [FRYL](../genes/FRYL.md) and [GAS7](../genes/GAS7.md), plus two novel partners — [PRDM10](../genes/PRDM10.md) (captured by both DNA and cytogenetics) and [DDX6](../genes/DDX6.md) (RNA-capture only). Both novel partners are involved in histone methyltransferase activity.
- Backtracking identified t-MDS/AL-associated mutations in 13/17 (76%) transformation patients at a median of 15.0 months before diagnosis (range 1.3–32.4) — and 26.9 months after initial neuroblastoma diagnosis. Median VAF at initial detection 0.08 (range 0.03–0.33).
- Cytogenetic surveillance caught only 2/6 KMT2A rearrangements before overt disease; RNA-capture caught 5/6 (median 28.5 months earlier).
- Acquired mutations were rare in the transient (4/14) and control (1/21) cohorts. Relative risk for t-MDS/AL given an oncogenic mutation = **8.8** (95% CI 1.3–57.8, p<0.001) for transformation vs. transient.
- Mean VAF in the transient cohort was 5× lower than in t-MDS/AL diagnostic samples (0.042 vs. 0.22, p<1e-5) and 2.5× lower than in pre-malignant samples from transformation patients (0.10, p<1e-4).
- In all 5 transformation patients with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations, the earliest ddPCR-detectable timepoint was **after** initiation of cancer therapy; the burden expanded preferentially during chemotherapy and/or radiation windows.
- Pediatric solid-tumor MSK-IMPACT cohort: 26/657 (3.9%) had any CH (VAF ≥0.02); only 7/657 (**1.05%**) had a CH-PD variant in a myeloid gene — vs. 17.6% in patients >25 years old in the same dataset. 4 patients later developed t-MDS/AL, none with detectable CH-PD at MSK-IMPACT time (median 17.5 months prior).

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — somatic mutations (hotspots C135S, V172F, R175H, R213P, R249S, L257Q, G266R) in 5/17 transformation patients; acquired post-therapy in pediatric [NBL](../cancer_types/NBL.md) (distinct from adult CH); associated with later transformation and allelic imbalance / LOH at the locus. Co-occurrence with [PPM1D](../genes/PPM1D.md) mutations in 2 patients (118730, 118731) consistent with parallel/convergent DDR-pathway clones.
- [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) — canonical breakpoint-region rearrangements in 6/17 transformation patients; partners include [ELL](../genes/ELL.md), [AFF1](../genes/AFF1.md), [FRYL](../genes/FRYL.md), [GAS7](../genes/GAS7.md), and the novel [PRDM10](../genes/PRDM10.md) and [DDX6](../genes/DDX6.md). Associated with topoisomerase-II exposure and earlier transformation.
- [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md) — clonal hotspot mutation 36 months before transformation in patient 118725 (followed by [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) R132H 12 months later).
- [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) — R132H acquisition in patient 118725's evolution toward t-AML.
- [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) — transient R140Q clone 1 year before transformation in patient 118729 (in context of pre-leukemic KMT2A rearrangement).
- [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md), [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — RAS-pathway mutations among transformation drivers; patient 118728 had multiple RAS-pathway hits.
- [PPM1D](../genes/PPM1D.md) — DDR-pathway clones at transformation and in the transient cohort.
- [CUX1](../genes/CUX1.md), [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md), [WT1](../genes/WT1.md) — myeloid-malignancy driver mutations at transformation.
- [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md) — non-R882 oncogenic variant in the transient cohort.
- [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md) — transient clonal oncogenic mutation 2 years before transformation in patient 118731 (fell below detection at t-MDS); also one control-cohort [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md) clone (patient died of neuroblastoma).
- [TET2](../genes/TET2.md) — independent clone with [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) in patient 118725 (parallel evolution); also among transient-clone genes.
- [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — observed as transient molecular abnormalities in the transformation cohort.
- [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md) — cited as a DDR-pathway gene whose CH clones expand under therapeutic pressure in adult cohorts (context; not a primary finding here).

## Clinical implications

- Integrated DNA + RNA capture-based BM surveillance detects t-MDS/AL-associated alterations a median of 15 months before clinical leukemia diagnosis — substantially earlier than conventional cytogenetics, which misses ~67% of pre-transformation KMT2A rearrangements.
- An oncogenic mutation in a serial BM sample carries an **8.8-fold relative risk** of subsequent t-MDS/AL in this neuroblastoma population, supporting molecular surveillance as a risk-stratification tool for pediatric survivors.
- Unlike adult oncology, pediatric CH at a single time point (e.g. MSK-IMPACT matched-normal) has low sensitivity for predicting t-MDS/AL — only 1.05% of pediatric solid-tumor patients had CH-PD, and none of the 4 patients who later developed t-MDS/AL had detectable CH-PD at MSK-IMPACT time. Recommendation: longitudinal BM (or peripheral-blood) surveillance for high-risk pediatric patients rather than single-timepoint screening.
- Distinct molecular natural histories suggest different intervention windows: KMT2A-driven cases may be detectable years earlier by RNA fusion capture; TP53-driven cases acquire mutations post-therapy and expand preferentially during chemo/radiation windows.
- Authors propose that emerging surveillance protocols should integrate deep molecular profiling and serial sampling to enable risk-adapted interventions before overt t-MDS/AL.

## Limitations & open questions

- Single-institution referral cohort — primary neuroblastoma patients may represent higher-than-average risk; though between-cohort comparisons within the study should be unaffected.
- Small longitudinal sample size (n=17 transformation) limits power to build a fully predictive VAF / VAF-change model for t-MDS/AL progression.
- BM-centric (not [PB](../cancer_types/PB.md)) — though authors note BM and PB are comparable for [MDS](../cancer_types/MDS.md), acute leukemia, and CH evaluation in prior work.
- Mutations were called at clinical-grade VAF ≥0.02; 3 of the 4 transformation patients without pre-leukemia detection at ≥0.02 had variants at VAF 0.0012–0.006 retrospectively, suggesting error-corrected sequencing might shift the detection horizon further but with unclear clinical utility.
- Earlier MSK-IMPACT panel versions lack [PPM1D](../genes/PPM1D.md) and [SRSF2](../genes/SRSF2.md), and never include SRCAP, [BRCC3](../genes/BRCC3.md), ZNF318 — true CH prevalence in the pediatric solid-tumor cohort may be underestimated.
- Whether pre-natal or very-early-life clones contribute to pediatric t-MDS/AL (as suggested for adult [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md) MPNs) cannot be excluded; the cohort lacked pre-therapy samples from all patients.
- Open question: do pediatric CH clones detected post-therapy reflect therapy-induced de novo mutations or selection of pre-existing rare clones below detection? The post-therapy-only emergence of TP53 mutations in this cohort favors the former but doesn't exclude the latter.
