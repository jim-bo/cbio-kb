# FAT4

## Overview

FAT4 is a protocadherin and putative tumor suppressor gene encoding a large transmembrane protein involved in tissue polarity and Hippo pathway regulation. Truncating mutations in FAT4 have been identified in adenoid cystic carcinoma (ACC), with functional knockdown experiments demonstrating a growth-suppressor role.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Truncating mutations identified in adenoid cystic carcinoma (ACC); functional growth-suppressor knockdown phenotype demonstrated [PMID:23685749](../papers/23685749.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **ACC (adenoid cystic carcinoma)** — truncating mutations; classified alongside [FAT3](../genes/FAT3.md) as protocadherin tumor suppressors mutated in this salivary gland malignancy [PMID:23685749](../papers/23685749.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-mutated with [FAT3](../genes/FAT3.md) and other tumor suppressors in ACC; part of a diverse mutational landscape also involving chromatin-remodeling genes ([EP300](../genes/EP300.md), [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md), [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md)) [PMID:23685749](../papers/23685749.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic implication reported [PMID:23685749](../papers/23685749.md)

## Open questions

- Mutation frequency not precisely quantified in this cohort; functional mechanism linking FAT4 loss to ACC pathogenesis remains to be elucidated [PMID:23685749](../papers/23685749.md)
