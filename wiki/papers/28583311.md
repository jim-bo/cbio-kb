**PMID:** 28583311 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.eururo.2017.05.032 · **Journal:** European Urology (2017)

## TL;DR

Pietzak et al. performed targeted exon capture sequencing (MSK-IMPACT 341- or 410-gene panels) on pretreatment index tumors and matched germline DNA from 105 patients with nonmuscle invasive bladder cancer (NMIBC) at Memorial Sloan Kettering — the largest NGS effort focused on NMIBC to date. They found [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter mutations (73%) and chromatin-modifying gene alterations (69%) at high prevalence across all stages and grades, suggesting these are early events in urothelial carcinogenesis. [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) and [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) alterations occurred mutually exclusively in 57% of high-grade tumors. DNA damage repair (DDR) gene alterations were found in 30% of high-grade NMIBC — a rate similar to muscle-invasive disease — and were associated with significantly higher mutational burden (p < 0.001). Most importantly, [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) mutations were associated with a 3.14-fold increased risk of recurrence after intravesical bacillus Calmette-Guérin (BCG) therapy (95% CI 1.51–6.51, p = 0.002) [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).

## Cohort & data

- **Primary cohort:** 105 patients with NMIBC, all treatment-naive index tumors with matched germline DNA, profiled on the [MSK-IMPACT panel](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) using the [341-gene](../methods/IMPACT341.md) or updated [410-gene](../methods/IMPACT410.md) version under an IRB-approved protocol at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).
- **Cohort composition:** Low-grade Ta (LGTa, n = 23), high-grade Tis (HGTis, n = 12), high-grade Ta (HGTa, n = 32), and high-grade T1 (HGT1, n = 38). All HGT1 tumors had restaging TUR with confirmation of uninvolved detrusor muscle [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).
- **BCG sub-cohort:** 62 of the 82 high-grade NMIBC patients received a 6-week induction course of BCG without maintenance therapy and were used for genomic correlates of recurrence [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).
- **Comparator MIBC cohorts:** 40 pretreatment index muscle-invasive bladder cancer (MIBC) tumors from MSK ("MSK-MIBC") sequenced on the same MSK-IMPACT protocol, plus 98 MIBC specimens from the [TCGA bladder cohort](../datasets/blca_tcga_pub.md) with no prior history of NMIBC [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).
- **Cancer type:** Nonmuscle invasive bladder urothelial carcinoma — [BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md) (note: OncoTree does not have a separate code distinguishing NMIBC from MIBC).
- **Dataset:** [blca_nmibc_2017](../datasets/blca_nmibc_2017.md) — data made publicly available through the cBioPortal for Cancer Genomics [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).
- **Median follow-up:** 24.4 months for the 100 patients managed by TUR ± adjuvant intravesical therapy; recurrences occurred in 46 of 100 [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).

## Key findings

- **[TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter dominates the mutational landscape.** [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter mutations were the single most frequent alteration at 73% of NMIBC tumors, present at similar frequency across stage (p = 0.2) and grade (p = 0.15): 61% (14/23) of LGTa, 88% (28/32) of HGTa, 79% (30/38) of HGT1, and 85% (34/40) of MSK-MIBC. The TCGA whole-exome data did not interrogate the TERT promoter region [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).
- **Chromatin-modifying gene alterations are nearly universal.** Alterations in chromatin-modifying genes occurred in 69% (72/105) of NMIBC tumors. [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md) was altered in 38% overall (52% LGTa, 38% HGTa, 32% HGT1) and [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) in 21% overall. Neither showed a statistically significant association with grade or stage [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).
- **Mutually exclusive ERBB2/FGFR3 alteration pattern.** 57% (47/82) of high-grade NMIBC tumors harbored alterations in either [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) or [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md), and the alterations were mutually exclusive (Supplementary Table 7). [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) alterations decreased with grade/stage (LGTa 83%; HGTa 59%; HGT1 34%; MSK-MIBC 8%; TCGA-MIBC 16%); [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) alterations were almost exclusively in high-grade tumors, with hotspot S310F mutations and amplification observed especially in HGTis (6/12 Tis tumors had [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) alterations) [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).
- **[FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) fusions identified in NMIBC.** Four [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) fusions were identified, including a [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)-[TNIP2](../genes/TNIP2.md) fusion (predicted activating; in-frame FGFR3 kinase domain) and an [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)-[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md) fusion in an LGTa tumor [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).
- **[STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md) mutations enriched in low-grade Ta disease.** [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md) alterations occurred in 23% of NMIBC overall, but truncating STAG2 mutations were significantly enriched in LGTa tumors (39% [9/23] LGTa vs 16% HGTa, 24% HGT1, 5% MSK-MIBC, 15% TCGA-MIBC; p = 0.046) [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).
- **TP53/cell-cycle pathway alterations rise with stage.** [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)/[MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) alteration rates increased stepwise with stage (p < 0.001) and grade (p < 0.001) — 9% LGTa, 19% HGTa, 45% HGT1, 75% MSK-MIBC. Cell-cycle gene alterations ([RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md), [CDKN1A](../genes/CDKN1A.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)) similarly increased with stage (p = 0.028) and grade (p = 0.009) [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).
- **High DDR alteration burden in high-grade NMIBC.** Deleterious DDR gene alterations were identified in 30% (25/82) of high-grade NMIBC versus only 4% (1/23) of low-grade tumors (p = 0.012). The high-grade NMIBC rate was similar to the MSK-MIBC cohort (33%, 13/40). [ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md) missense mutations were the most common DDR alteration (17%, 14/82 high-grade NMIBC; 20% of MSK-MIBC), clustering around the conserved helicase domain. Other affected DDR genes: [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), [ERCC4](../genes/ERCC4.md), [PALB2](../genes/PALB2.md), [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md), [FANCC](../genes/FANCC.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md) [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).
- **DDR alterations correlate with elevated mutational burden.** Tumors with deleterious DDR gene alterations had significantly higher mutational burden than DDR-intact tumors (median 26, IQR 15–36 vs median 8, IQR 5–12 mutations/Mb; p < 0.001). [ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md)-mutated tumors specifically had median 29 (IQR 23–37) vs 8 (IQR 6–14) for [ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md) wild-type (p < 0.001). High-grade NMIBC had a mutational burden similar to MIBC (median 9, IQR 6–21 vs 10, IQR 7–19; p = 0.84) and significantly higher than low-grade NMIBC (7, IQR 5–10; p = 0.032) [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).
- **[ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) is the only gene significantly associated with BCG recurrence.** Among all genes altered in ≥5 tumors of the 62-patient BCG cohort, only [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) mutations were significantly associated with recurrence after BCG (HR = 3.14, 95% CI 1.51–6.51, p = 0.002), surviving multiple-comparison correction (p = 0.04). The association persisted when ARID1A missense VUS were included (HR = 3.08, 95% CI 1.49–6.35, p = 0.002) and in the larger 100-patient TUR cohort (HR = 2.07, 95% CI 1.10–3.88, p = 0.024). 32 of 62 patients (52%) recurred at median 24 mo follow-up [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md) status, ERBB2/FGFR3 status, and mutational count did NOT predict BCG recurrence.** [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) alterations alone or in combination with [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) showed no association with recurrence (p = 0.94 and p = 0.36). Tumors with [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) and [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) alterations had similar recurrence rates as wild-type (p = 0.3). Mutational count on [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) was not associated with BCG recurrence (HR = 0.96, 95% CI 0.83–1.1, p = 0.3) [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).

## Genes & alterations

- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) — Promoter mutations in 73% of NMIBC; uniformly high across grade and stage; supports use as a noninvasive urinary screening/surveillance biomarker.
- [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) — Hotspot mutations in 49% overall, more frequent in lower grade/stage. Four fusions identified, including a novel [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)-[TNIP2](../genes/TNIP2.md) and an [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)-[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md) fusion in a LGTa tumor. Mutually exclusive with ERBB2.
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) — Hotspot S310F missense and amplification, almost exclusively in high-grade NMIBC, including 6/12 Tis specimens. Together with FGFR3 covers 57% of high-grade NMIBC.
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) — Mutations in 21% of NMIBC overall; the only single gene significantly associated with recurrence after BCG (HR = 3.14, p = 0.002).
- [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md) — Most commonly altered chromatin-modifying gene (38% of NMIBC).
- [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md) — 23% overall; truncating mutations enriched in LGTa (p = 0.046), supporting the lower-grade/stage interpretation in the conflicting literature.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) / [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) — Alterations rise stepwise with stage/grade (p < 0.001).
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md), [CDKN1A](../genes/CDKN1A.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — Cell-cycle alterations more common at higher stage/grade.
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — Altered in 26% of NMIBC; part of the broadly altered RTK/PI3K pathway (79% of tumors).
- [ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md) — Most common DDR gene alteration (17% of high-grade NMIBC), missense mutations clustering in conserved helicase domains; associated with markedly elevated mutational burden.
- [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), [ERCC4](../genes/ERCC4.md), [PALB2](../genes/PALB2.md), [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md), [FANCC](../genes/FANCC.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md) — Less frequent DDR alterations contributing to the 30% high-grade NMIBC DDR-altered fraction.
- [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) — One LGTa harbored a truncating H1901Lfs*15 mutation deemed likely non-functional (3' end, no hypermutation phenotype).

## Clinical implications

- **Targeted therapy opportunity:** 57% of high-grade NMIBC harbor alterations in [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) or [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) — a strong rationale for trials of targeted kinase inhibitors as alternatives or adjuncts to BCG, particularly with the development of less toxic, highly selective [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) inhibitors and intravesical delivery [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).
- **ARID1A as a candidate predictive biomarker for BCG response.** [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) mutations associate with significantly worse recurrence-free survival after induction BCG (HR = 3.14, p = 0.002). If confirmed in independent cohorts, ARID1A status could stratify patients for BCG and motivate testing of [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) methyltransferase inhibitors, which exhibit synthetic lethality in ARID1A-mutated cancers [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).
- **High mutational burden + DDR alterations support checkpoint immunotherapy trials.** High-grade NMIBC has a mutational burden comparable to MIBC, and DDR-altered tumors carry markedly elevated burden. Given the established mutational-load → checkpoint-inhibitor response association in metastatic urothelial carcinoma (e.g. [atezolizumab](../drugs/atezolizumab.md)), this profile supports ongoing trials of systemic PD-1/PD-L1 inhibitors in NMIBC patients [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).
- **Noninvasive urinary biomarkers feasible.** The high prevalence and grade/stage independence of [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter mutations and chromatin-modifying gene alterations supports development of urinary screening/surveillance assays that could replace cystoscopy [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).
- **Mutational count alone does not predict BCG response.** On the [MSK-IMPACT panel](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md), no association was observed between mutational count and BCG recurrence (p = 0.3) — discouraging panel-based TMB as a standalone BCG biomarker without further investigation [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).
- **Comparator therapeutic context:** Treatment of the cohort included intravesical [mitomycin-c](../drugs/mitomycin-c.md) (39% of LGTa) and intravesical BCG (13% LGTa, 81% HGT) — patients who received perioperative mitomycin or other adjuvant perioperative therapies were excluded from index sequencing [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Sample size by subgroup is small.** While 105 NMIBC tumors is the largest such NGS cohort, subgroup analyses by stage/grade (e.g., 12 HGTis, 23 LGTa) are limited. Longer follow-up will be needed for robust survival inference [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).
- **Predictive vs prognostic ambiguity for ARID1A.** Whether [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) mutations are a true predictive biomarker of BCG response or simply prognostic for poor outcome in NMIBC overall is not resolved by this single-arm BCG cohort and warrants validation in an independent cohort with a non-BCG comparator arm [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).
- **Tumor heterogeneity caveat.** Both intertumor and intratumor heterogeneity may confound results from the single-region targeted-panel approach used [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).
- **Carcinoma-in-situ (Tis) sequencing is technically difficult.** Nearly two-thirds of attempted Tis specimens had inadequate tumor purity for analysis, biasing the Tis subset and motivating alternative inputs (cytology, urinary cell-free DNA, urinary exosomes, single-cell sequencing) [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).
- **TMB inferred from a 341/410-gene panel.** Use of [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) mutational count as a TMB surrogate may lack the resolution of whole-exome sequencing for the BCG-response question; the authors call out the need for a larger cohort and WES [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).
- **Pathway conflict around STAG2.** This study finds [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md) truncating mutations enriched in LGTa, which is consistent with some prior reports but not others; a unified interpretation across NMIBC and MIBC remains open [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md).
