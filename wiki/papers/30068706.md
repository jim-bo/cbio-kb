## TL;DR

Prospective tumor and matched-normal sequencing of 197 tumors from 189 patients with advanced endometrial cancer at Memorial Sloan Kettering using the [MSK-IMPACT panel](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) ([341](../methods/IMPACT341.md) or [410](../methods/IMPACT410.md) genes). The cohort was enriched for high-grade and metastatic disease relative to [TCGA](../datasets/ucec_tcga.md), yet histology- and grade-matched genomic profiles were broadly similar. Genomic [MSIsensor](../methods/msisensor.md) and mutational-signature decomposition recovered microsatellite instability with high concordance to IHC and even rescued one MMR-proficient case with occult deficiency. Hierarchical clustering of [FACETS](../methods/facets.md)-derived copy-number profiles uncovered a previously underappreciated heterozygous-loss cluster (Cluster C) with significantly worse PFS<sub>1</sub> (median 9.6 vs. 17.0 and 17.4 months, p=0.006). 67% of patients carried at least one potentially actionable alteration; 27% of those were enrolled to matched trials, of whom 47% achieved clinical benefit.

## Cohort & data

- **189 patients / 197 tumors** with advanced endometrial cancer profiled by [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) (n=70 on the 341-gene [IMPACT341](../methods/IMPACT341.md) panel; n=127 on the 410-gene [IMPACT410](../methods/IMPACT410.md) panel), average ~735× coverage [PMID:30068706](../papers/30068706.md).
- **Cancer types**: [endometrioid carcinoma](../cancer_types/UEC.md) (FIGO grades 1–3; 75/189), [serous carcinoma](../cancer_types/USC.md) (46/189), [carcinosarcoma](../cancer_types/UCS.md) (35/189), mixed (15), [clear cell](../cancer_types/UCCC.md) (13), undifferentiated/dedifferentiated (4), neuroendocrine (1) — all grouped under [endometrial carcinoma (UCEC)](../cancer_types/UCEC.md).
- **Disease state**: 95% advanced disease (FIGO III–IV at diagnosis or at recurrence); 51% tumor samples from metastatic sites; 39% sequenced after chemotherapy and 11% after hormonal therapy.
- **Dataset**: [ucec_msk_2018](../datasets/ucec_msk_2018.md) on cBioPortal; compared throughout to the primary-tumor [ucec_tcga](../datasets/ucec_tcga.md) cohort.
- **Assays/analytical methods**: [FACETS v0.5.6](../methods/facets.md) (allele-specific CN, cval=100); [MSIsensor v0.2](../methods/msisensor.md) (MSI scoring, MSI-H ≥10); [mutational signature decomposition](../methods/mutational-signatures.md) for hypermutated samples; [OncoKB](../methods/oncokb-annotation.md) for actionability (annotated 22-Dec-2016); germline calling over 76 cancer-predisposition genes in the MSK-IMPACT panel; MMR [IHC](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md) for MLH1/PMS2/MSH2/MSH6.

## Key findings

- **Cohort vs TCGA outcomes**: MSK cohort median PFS<sub>1</sub> 15.3 months vs. TCGA median not reached at 23-month follow-up. Restricted to the 19% of TCGA patients who recurred, the survival profiles were comparable (median PFS<sub>1</sub> 13.7 vs. 15.3 months, p=0.1) [PMID:30068706](../papers/30068706.md).
- **Grade matters within endometrioid**: high-grade (FIGO grade 3) [endometrioid carcinoma](../cancer_types/UEC.md) had worse median PFS<sub>1</sub> than low-grade (12.7 vs. 21.6 months, p=0.005).
- **Pathway alterations**: PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway altered in 70% of patients; RTK/RAS/β-catenin pathway in 65% — consistent with prior endometrial profiling.
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md) vs [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) cohort skew**: [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations more common in MSK cohort vs TCGA (63% vs. 36%, p=4×10⁻⁵); [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) alterations more common in TCGA (56% vs. 22%, p=8×10⁻⁹) — attributable to histologic composition.
- **High-grade enrichment**: TP53 mutations significantly enriched in high-grade endometrioid (29% vs. 3%, p=0.013); [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) mutations enriched in high-grade tumors overall (p=0.035) and most prevalent in mixed histology.
- **Histology-specific amplifications**: [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) amplification enriched in carcinosarcoma (p=0.007); [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) amplification enriched in serous tumors (p=0.018).
- **Mutational burden**: median 5.7 mutations/Mb (range 0–419.8). 15.9% (30/189) had ≥10 SNVs enabling signature decomposition. Two [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) exonuclease-domain hotspot tumors were ultramutators (V411L at 419.8 mut/Mb; P286R at 152.8 mut/Mb).
- **MMR concordance**: 24/189 (13%) tumors were MMR-D by IHC (vs. 39% in TCGA, p=5×10⁻¹¹); median TMB 31.6 mut/Mb in MMR-D (excluding the POLE-hotspot case) vs. 4.7 mut/Mb in MMR-proficient (p=1.9×10⁻¹⁴). 29/30 MMR-D/presumed-MMR-D tumors had high MSIsensor scores; the discordant case was a POLE-hotspot ultramutator that was MSS by MSIsensor.
- **IHC misses one occult MMR-D case**: one MMR-proficient-by-IHC tumor showed high TMB and high MSIsensor, suggesting genomic methods can rescue cases standard IHC misses.
- **Novel copy-number cluster**: hierarchical clustering of FACETS profiles produced three clusters. Cluster A: TP53-mutant, whole-genome doubled. Cluster B: PTEN-mutant, low-grade endometrioid, gains of 1q/8/10 (TCGA-like clusters 2–3). **Cluster C**: heterozygous losses across the genome, high-grade endometrioid and non-endometrioid — significantly worse PFS<sub>1</sub> (median 9.6 vs. 17.4 and 17.0 months for A and B; p=0.006).
- **Germline findings**: three pathogenic germline [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) mutations (one serous, two carcinosarcomas; two carrying the c.5946delT/6174delT founder allele, one with c.5799_5802delCCAA frameshift); one Lynch-syndrome germline [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md) splice mutation (c.1731+1G>T) with biallelic somatic CN-LOH and MSIsensor=19.19.
- **[ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) de novo mutations**: five endometrioid tumors carried [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) ligand-binding-domain hotspot mutations; only 2/5 were from samples obtained after endocrine therapy, suggesting de novo emergence in endometrial cancer (not previously reported in breast).
- **Primary vs metastatic**: no global differences except [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) mutations more common in primary than metastatic endometrioid carcinomas (67% [16/24] vs. 34% [10/29], p=0.03). In 4 paired primary–metastatic cases, [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md) L2427Q and [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md) E468* hotspots showed heterogeneity.
- **Actionability and matching**: 67% (127/189) carried ≥1 potentially actionable alteration. Most common: [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) mutation (35%, 66/189), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) mutation (29%, 54/189), MSI-H (16%, 30/189), [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) amplification (16%, 16/189). 27% (34/127) of actionable patients enrolled to matched trials; 47% (16/34) achieved clinical benefit. Trial inhibitor classes: PI3K (n=12), dual PI3K/mTOR (n=6), [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) (n=6), AKT (n=4), mTOR (n=2), FGFR (n=2).
- **Hypermutation source attribution**: of high-TMB samples, n=28 attributed to MSI, n=1 to [POLE](../genes/POLE.md), n=1 to MSI+POLE — no other MSS high-TMB cases identified.

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — mutation in 63% of MSK cohort vs. 36% of TCGA (p=4×10⁻⁵); enriched in high-grade endometrioid (29% vs. 3%, p=0.013); defines copy-number Cluster A together with WGD [PMID:30068706](../papers/30068706.md).
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — altered in 22% (MSK) vs. 56% (TCGA, p=8×10⁻⁹); more common in primary than metastatic endometrioid (67% vs. 34%, p=0.03); 29% somatic mutation rate cohort-wide; defines Cluster B alongside endometrioid histology.
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — most common actionable alteration (35%, 66/189); enriched in high-grade (p=0.035) and mixed histology; n=12 patients matched to PI3K-pathway trials.
- [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) — two exonuclease-domain hotspot mutations (V411L and P286R) drove ultramutator phenotypes (419.8 and 152.8 mut/Mb); seven additional non-hotspot POLE variants of uncertain significance lacked ultramutation; neither hotspot case received an ICI and neither recurred at 2–2.5 yrs.
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) — amplification in 16/189 (16% of actionable subset); enriched in [serous carcinoma](../cancer_types/USC.md) (p=0.018); one heavily pretreated serous patient achieved a 14-month ongoing complete response to [ado-trastuzumab-emtansine (T-DM1)](../drugs/ado-trastuzumab-emtansine.md).
- [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) — amplification enriched in [carcinosarcoma](../cancer_types/UCS.md) (p=0.007).
- [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) / [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) — FGFR2/3 fusions noted as therapeutically relevant; n=2 patients enrolled to FGFR-inhibitor trials.
- [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) — ligand-binding-domain hotspot mutations in 5 endometrioid patients; 3/5 arose in apparent absence of endocrine therapy (de novo). One de novo case progressed on [letrozole](../drugs/letrozole.md) then had ongoing 18-month response on [bevacizumab](../drugs/bevacizumab.md) + [fulvestrant](../drugs/fulvestrant.md).
- [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md) — hotspot L2427Q showed primary-metastatic heterogeneity.
- [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md) — hotspot E468* showed primary-metastatic heterogeneity.
- [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) — three pathogenic germline truncating mutations (one serous, two carcinosarcomas); founder c.5946delT in two patients; c.5799_5802delCCAA frameshift in the third. None of the germline-BRCA1/2 patients were enrolled to PARP inhibitor trials.
- [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) — present in the actionable-alteration tally (n=1 patient at OncoKB level).
- [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md) — Lynch-associated germline splice mutation c.1731+1G>T with biallelic CN-LOH, MSIsensor=19.19; also assayed by MMR IHC.
- [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md), [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md) — components of the standard MMR IHC panel used to call MMR-D status.
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md), [KIT](../genes/KIT.md), [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md) — each n=1 OncoKB-level-annotated actionable alterations contributing to the 67% actionable tally.
- [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) — pathway target of n=4 patients enrolled to AKT-inhibitor trials.

## Clinical implications

- **Genomic MSI calling complements IHC.** Combining [MSIsensor](../methods/msisensor.md) with [mutational-signature decomposition](../methods/mutational-signatures.md) recovered an MMR-D case that was scored MMR-proficient by [IHC](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md), suggesting prospective NGS can refine eligibility for [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md) in MSI-high tumors (5/30 MSI-H patients had received ICIs prior to FDA approval; 2/5 had clinical benefit) [PMID:30068706](../papers/30068706.md).
- **Matched targeted therapy is feasible but attrits sharply.** Of 189 sequenced patients, 67% had actionable alterations, but only 27% of those (18% of the full cohort) reached a matched trial; 47% of matched patients (8.5% of all sequenced) achieved clinical benefit. Barriers: lack of matching trial, standard-of-care response, alternative unmatched immunotherapy options, clinical deterioration, patient choice.
- **ERBB2-amplified serous endometrial cancer responds to T-DM1.** One heavily pretreated serous patient achieved an ongoing 14-month complete response to [ado-trastuzumab-emtansine](../drugs/ado-trastuzumab-emtansine.md), supporting [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md)-targeted therapy in this histology.
- **Prognostic copy-number Cluster C.** Heterozygous-loss-dominant copy-number profile defines a subset with PFS<sub>1</sub> ≈ 9.6 months, worse than TP53-mutant/WGD Cluster A — relevant for risk stratification and clinical-trial design.
- **Germline screening matters even off-histology.** Three pathogenic [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) carriers (not historically endometrial-associated) and a Lynch [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md) carrier were identified via universal tumor/normal sequencing — supporting unbiased germline analysis irrespective of phenotype.
- **Hypermutated endometrial cancer is essentially MSI or POLE.** No other MSS-high-TMB mechanism emerged, simplifying the mechanistic interpretation of TMB as an ICI biomarker in this disease.
- **Panel-size trade-off**: authors note 30–50 genes likely covers the currently "actionable genome", though large panels facilitate simultaneous MSI/germline assessment.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Sample size and retrospective design.** Cluster-C prognostic signal needs validation in an additional advanced-endometrial cohort. Re-analysis of TCGA Cluster 4 identified Cluster-A-like and Cluster-C-like subgroups, but heavy censoring (21 progression events / 81 patients) prevented robust outcome comparison.
- **Single-center bias.** Trial matching rates are dependent on institutional study portfolio; results may not generalize to community practice.
- **MSI-indeterminate calls in low-purity samples.** Two MMR-D-by-IHC tumors were MSI-indeterminate on MSIsensor due to low tumor purity (samples with purity <20% excluded from CN-focused analyses, retaining 154/189).
- **Modest matched-therapy yield.** 18% of all sequenced patients enrolled to matched-targeted therapy and 8.5% of all sequenced patients benefited — substantial room to improve trial portfolio breadth.
- **De novo ESR1 mutations** in endometrial cancer (no prior endocrine therapy exposure) are reported here for the first time; mechanism (selection by what? estrogen exposure from obesity?) is unresolved — patients' BMIs were similar to acquired-mutation cases (median 22.9 vs 24.7).
- **No germline-BRCA1/2 patients enrolled to PARP-inhibitor trials**, leaving the question of PARP-inhibitor benefit in germline-mutant advanced endometrial cancer open.
- **Anonymization tradeoff**: irreversible anonymization required for germline analysis prevented further clinical description of germline-positive cases beyond histology and copy-number summaries.
