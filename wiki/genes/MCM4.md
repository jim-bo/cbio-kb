# MCM4

## Overview

MCM4 (Minichromosome Maintenance Complex Component 4) encodes a subunit of the MCM2-7 replicative helicase complex, which is essential for DNA replication initiation and elongation. In cancer genomics, MCM4 protein upregulation is observed as a downstream consequence of cell-cycle deregulation, particularly in tumors with [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) loss-of-function, where its elevated expression reflects unconstrained S-phase entry.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Protein upregulated in RB1-altered [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) tumors (12% of [gbm_cptac_2021](../datasets/gbm_cptac_2021.md) cohort); MCM2/MCM4/MCM6 coordinate upregulation marks RB1-altered tumors and nominates MCM proteins as potential biomarkers or vulnerabilities [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **GBM** — MCM4 protein upregulated in RB1-altered glioblastoma multiforme samples in the CPTAC proteogenomic cohort [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Upregulated coordinately with [MCM2](../genes/MCM2.md) and [MCM6](../genes/MCM6.md) in RB1-altered GBM tumors [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- RB1-altered tumors with MCM2/MCM4/MCM6 upregulation represent a nominally therapeutic subclass in GBM; no specific clinical trial data reported in this corpus [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Open questions

- Whether MCM4 upregulation is a useful biomarker or therapeutic vulnerability in RB1-altered GBM requires prospective validation.
