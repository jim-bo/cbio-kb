## TL;DR

This study reports on the clinical implementation of prospective CSF circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) sequencing using MSK-IMPACT at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center. The authors profiled 1,007 CSF samples from 711 patients with over 90 distinct CNS tumor types (primary and metastatic) collected between November 2018 and November 2022. Genetic alterations were detected in 53% of CSF samples from patients with clinically documented CNS tumors (489/922), while none of 85 samples from patients without CNS tumors had detectable ctDNA (100% specificity). ctDNA positivity was associated with a three-fold increased risk of death (HR 3.23, p < 0.001), and the distribution of clinically actionable alterations was consistent with tumor-type specific patterns seen in the AACR GENIE cohort [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).

## Cohort & data

- **Sample size:** 1,007 CSF samples from 711 patients (adult and pediatric); 150/711 patients (21.1%) had multiple CSF samples (median 2, range 2-12) [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- **Cancer types:** Over 90 distinct tumor types including lung cancer (n=188), breast cancer (n=150), gliomas (n=148), metastatic melanoma, GI cancers, embryonal tumors, and others [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- **Negative controls:** 85/1,007 CSF samples from patients without clinically documented CNS involvement [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- **Dataset:** [csf_msk_2024](../datasets/csf_msk_2024.md) [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- **Assays:** [IMPACT468](../methods/IMPACT468.md) (n=274 samples) and [IMPACT505](../methods/IMPACT505.md) (n=733 samples); a subset compared with [ACCESS129](../methods/ACCESS129.md) plasma ctDNA (n=31 patients) [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- **Collection period:** November 2018 to November 2022; median follow-up 240 days (IQR 112-483 days) [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).

## Key findings

- **ctDNA detection rate:** 53.0% (489/922) of CSF samples from patients with clinically documented CNS tumors harbored at least one somatic genetic alteration [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- **Specificity:** 100% (0/85 positive among patients without CNS tumors) [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- **Total alterations detected:** 7,110 somatic alterations across 489 ctDNA-positive samples (3,944 non-synonymous mutations, 2,980 copy number alterations, 186 structural rearrangements) [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- **Variant allele frequencies:** Median VAF 38.7% (IQR 23.2-51.1%) across ctDNA-positive samples, reflecting the high tumor purity of CSF cfDNA [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- **Clinical actionability:** 50.7% (248/489) of ctDNA-positive samples carried a level 1 OncoKB actionable alteration. Lung carcinomas had the highest level 1 actionability [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- **Survival association:** ctDNA positivity was associated with a three-fold increased risk of death (HR 3.23, 95% CI 2.58-4.05, p < 0.001). Median [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) was 854 days shorter in ctDNA-positive patients (235 days vs. 1,089 days) [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- **Leptomeningeal disease detection (lung cancer):** In [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) patients, leptomeningeal involvement was a strong predictor of ctDNA positivity (OR 20.17, 95% CI 9.65-42.16, p < 0.0001). CSF ctDNA had greater sensitivity than positive cytology for leptomeningeal disease (85.4% vs. 61.7%) and greater negative predictive value (80% vs. 66%) [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- **CSF vs. tumor tissue:** Among 56 tumor/CSF pairs (55 patients), 43% of alterations were shared, 27% private to CSF, 29% private to tumor. CSF VAFs were significantly higher than tumor tissue (median 32% vs. 24%, p < 0.01) [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- **CSF vs. plasma:** Among 31 patients, 54% of alterations were shared between CSF and plasma. CSF VAFs were markedly higher (median 36.4% vs. 2.3%, p < 0.01), demonstrating the superiority of CSF over plasma for CNS tumor monitoring [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- **Pre-analytic factors:** Volumes below 2 mL critically impacted ctDNA positivity (as low as 8.3% for primary CNS tumors). Optimal volume was 5 mL or above. Delays beyond 7 days reduced ctDNA proportion but did not eliminate detection [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- **Mutational signatures:** In samples with TMB >= 13.8 mutations/Mb (n=35), signatures for UV (n=3), APOBEC (n=7), smoking (n=8), and [temozolomide](../drugs/temozolomide.md) (n=2) were identified in melanoma, breast, lung, and glioma samples, respectively [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md): Most frequently altered gene across all tumor types (n=242, 49% of 489 ctDNA-positive samples) [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md): Mutations and high-level amplification in lung cancer; acquired resistance mutations detected in CSF including p.T790M, p.C797S, p.L792H, p.L718Q, p.L718V, p.G724S [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- [MET](../genes/MET.md): Amplification in lung cancer; exon 14 skipping mutations detected; resistance mutation p.Y1230N emerged on [crizotinib](../drugs/crizotinib.md) [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md): Mutations and amplification in lung cancer and breast cancer [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md): Mutations in lung cancer; off-target resistance alteration in EGFR-mutant patients [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md): p.V600E in metastatic melanoma; fusions as off-target resistance in EGFR-mutant lung cancer; [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)::[KIAA1549](../genes/KIAA1549.md) fusions in gliomas [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md): p.E545K common in breast cancers; off-target resistance alteration in EGFR-mutant lung cancer [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- [ALK](../genes/ALK.md): [EML4](../genes/EML4.md)::[ALK](../genes/ALK.md) fusions in lung carcinomas; resistance mutations p.G1202R and p.G1269A detected upon targeted therapy progression [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- [EML4](../genes/EML4.md): Fusion partner in [EML4](../genes/EML4.md)::[ALK](../genes/ALK.md) rearrangements in lung carcinomas [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- [RET](../genes/RET.md): Rearrangements with diverse gene partners in lung carcinomas [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- [ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md): Rearrangements with diverse gene partners in lung carcinomas [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md): p.R132H in IDH-mutant gliomas [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- [STK11](../genes/STK11.md): Mutations in lung cancer [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md): Mutations in lung cancer [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- [H3-3A](../genes/H3-3A.md): p.K28M histone mutation supporting diagnosis of diffuse midline glioma in CSF ctDNA [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- [KIAA1549](../genes/KIAA1549.md): [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)::[KIAA1549](../genes/KIAA1549.md) fusions detected in gliomas [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).

## Clinical implications

- CSF ctDNA sequencing via MSK-IMPACT is feasible and clinically informative in a routine hospital setting, providing actionable cancer genotype information in a large fraction of patients with CNS tumors [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- CSF ctDNA had greater sensitivity than cytology for leptomeningeal disease detection in lung cancer patients (85.4% vs. 61.7%), supporting its use as a complementary or alternative diagnostic modality [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- Serial CSF ctDNA profiling identified clonal evolution and emergence of resistance mechanisms ([EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) gatekeeper mutations, [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) resistance mutations, [MET](../genes/MET.md) resistance mutations), directly informing treatment changes [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- ctDNA detection in CSF was associated with significantly shortened overall survival (HR 3.23), suggesting prognostic utility of CSF ctDNA status [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- CSF liquid biopsy outperformed plasma for CNS cancer monitoring, with significantly higher VAFs (median 36.4% vs. 2.3%) due to blood-brain barrier limiting tumor DNA shedding into peripheral blood [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- Mutational signatures in CSF ctDNA aided in determining or confirming the primary tumor site (e.g., UV signatures for melanoma, smoking signatures for lung adenocarcinoma) [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Sensitivity ceiling:** Only 53% of CSF samples from patients with known CNS disease were ctDNA-positive; the 47% false-negative rate limits clinical utility as a standalone diagnostic test [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- **Volume dependence:** Samples below 2 mL had ctDNA positivity rates as low as 8.3%, creating a practical barrier when CSF volume is limited [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- **Heterogeneous cohort:** The study encompasses over 90 tumor types with variable ctDNA shedding; disease-specific conclusions are limited for rarer subtypes [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- **Lack of treatment outcome data:** While the study describes actionable alterations and resistance mutations, it does not systematically report treatment decisions or outcomes driven by CSF ctDNA findings [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- **Single-institution study:** All samples from MSK; generalizability to settings without equivalent pre-analytic infrastructure and sequencing capabilities is uncertain [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- **No longitudinal resistance analysis at scale:** Clonal evolution and resistance monitoring examples are illustrative case studies rather than systematic longitudinal analyses [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- **CSF vs. plasma comparison limited:** Only 31 patients had contemporaneous CSF and plasma samples, and comparison was restricted to the overlapping genomic regions of MSK-IMPACT and MSK-ACCESS [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
