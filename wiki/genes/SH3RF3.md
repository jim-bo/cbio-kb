# SH3RF3

## Overview

SH3RF3 (SH3 domain containing ring finger 3) is located on chromosome 2. In atypical SCLC (aSCLC), a chromothripsis-derived structural rearrangement juxtaposes the active chromatin of SH3RF3 upstream of the CCND1 locus on chromosome 11, constituting an enhancer-hijacking mechanism that drives marked cyclin D1 overexpression. This represents an alternative pathway to CCND1 amplification for oncogene activation in aSCLC.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Donor of active chromatin in a chromothripsis-derived SH3RF3-CCND1 enhancer-hijacking rearrangement in aSCLC case A17 (chr 2 active chromatin juxtaposed upstream of chr 11 CCND1); marked cyclin D1 overexpression confirmed by IHC and RNA-seq without CCND1 copy-number amplification [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md):** SH3RF3 active chromatin hijacked by chromothripsis-derived rearrangement to activate CCND1 in aSCLC; represents one of two mechanisms of cyclin D1 overexpression in this subset (the other being ecDNA amplification) [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- SH3RF3 enhancer hijacking co-occurs with CCND1 overexpression; mutually exclusive with CCND1 focal amplification in the same aSCLC case (A17) [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- SH3RF3-mediated CCND1 overexpression may sensitize tumors to CDK4/6 inhibitors targeting the downstream pRb pathway; the aSCLC CCND1-overexpression subset is a candidate for CDK4/6 inhibitor trials [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md)

## Open questions

- Whether SH3RF3 itself has oncogenic properties in aSCLC beyond its role as an enhancer donor is unknown; structural rearrangement was observed in a single case (A17) [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md)
