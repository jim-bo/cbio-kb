# TNFSF14

## Overview

TNFSF14 (Tumor Necrosis Factor Superfamily Member 14; also known as LIGHT or CD258) is a [TNF](../genes/TNF.md) superfamily cytokine that signals through HVEM ([TNFRSF14](../genes/TNFRSF14.md)) and LTbetaR receptors to regulate lymphocyte activation, co-stimulation, and germinal center (GC) biology. In diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL), TNFSF14 mutations are recurrent and have been identified as BCR/PI3K signaling modifiers in specific genetic subtypes, where they may impair HVEM-mediated suppression of GC B-cell proliferation.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Recurrent mutation in DLBCL identified as a BCR/PI3K signaling modifier; enriched in genetic clusters C3 and C4 (GCB-like DLBCL) in a 304-tumor whole-genome sequenced cohort; categorized alongside [GNA13](../genes/GNA13.md), [SGK1](../genes/SGK1.md), and [HVCN1](../genes/HVCN1.md) as regulators of the BCR/PI3K axis [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **DLBCL (Diffuse Large B-Cell Lymphoma):** recurrent somatic mutations identified in GCB-type genetic clusters C3/C4; likely to impair suppression of GC B-cell proliferation via loss of HVEM-mediated signaling.

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with GNA13, SGK1, and HVCN1 mutations in C3/C4 GCB-DLBCL clusters; these represent overlapping BCR/PI3K signaling modifiers [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct TNFSF14-targeted therapy reported in the corpus. The BCR/PI3K pathway context links functionally to PI3K-delta inhibitors (e.g., idelalisib) and [BTK](../genes/BTK.md) inhibitors under investigation in GCB-DLBCL.

## Open questions

- The precise functional consequence of TNFSF14 somatic mutations (loss-of-function vs altered signaling) in GC B cells is not experimentally resolved in the corpus papers.
- Whether TNFSF14 mutation status has independent prognostic value within C3/C4 DLBCLs remains to be determined.
