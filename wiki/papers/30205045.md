**PMID:** 30205045 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.ccell.2018.08.008 · **Journal:** Cancer Cell (2018)

## TL;DR

Razavi et al. prospectively sequenced 1,918 breast tumors from 1,756 patients (1,501 hormone receptor-positive) at MSKCC using the MSK-IMPACT panel and linked the resulting genomic profiles to detailed treatment histories. In the 692 tumors collected after exposure to endocrine therapy, activating *[ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md)* mutations and loss-of-function *[NF1](../genes/NF1.md)* mutations were more than twice as common as in untreated tumors; additional MAPK-pathway lesions (*[EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md)* amplification, *[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)*, *[HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md)*, *[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)*, *[MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md)*, *[ERBB3](../genes/ERBB3.md)*) and ER-transcriptional-machinery alterations (*[MYC](../genes/MYC.md)*, *[CTCF](../genes/CTCF.md)*, *[FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md)*, *[TBX3](../genes/TBX3.md)*) collectively affected 22% of post-hormonal-therapy tumors and were mutually exclusive with *[ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md)* hotspot mutations. MAPK-pathway alterations and transcription-factor lesions both predicted significantly shorter progression-free survival on aromatase inhibitors compared with pan-wild-type tumors.

## Cohort & data

- 1,918 breast tumors from 1,756 patients sequenced prospectively (April 2014 – March 2017) at Memorial Sloan Kettering; clinico-genomic dataset published as [breast_msk_2018](../datasets/breast_msk_2018.md) ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- Hormone-receptor-positive (HR+) tumors comprised 1,501 of 1,918 samples; of these, 692 were collected after exposure to endocrine therapy and 809 were endocrine-therapy-naïve ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- 52% of tumors (1,000/1,918) were from metastatic sites; 52% of samples were collected after exposure to systemic therapy in the (neo)adjuvant (44%) and/or metastatic (27%) settings, with a median of three prior systemic regimens in the metastatic setting (range 1–15) ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- Histologic subtypes included invasive ductal carcinoma ([IDC](../cancer_types/IDC.md)), invasive lobular carcinoma ([ILC](../cancer_types/ILC.md)), and mixed IDC/ILC under the broader [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) umbrella ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- Assay: [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) hybrid-capture targeted sequencing, using the 341-gene ([IMPACT341](../methods/IMPACT341.md), n=432), 410-gene ([IMPACT410](../methods/IMPACT410.md), n=968), or 468-gene ([IMPACT468](../methods/IMPACT468.md), n=518) panels; mean coverage 771-fold; previously described in Cheng 2015 and Zehir 2017 ([PMID:28481359](../papers/28481359.md)).
- 30 patients had matched treatment-naïve primary and post-progression tumors sequenced by both [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) (median 358-fold coverage) and high-depth MSK-IMPACT; an additional 44 patients had only MSK-IMPACT matched pairs (74 total matched pairs) ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- One research autopsy patient: 10 metastatic tissues subjected to WES (median 296-fold coverage); selected variants confirmed by [droplet-digital-pcr](../methods/droplet-digital-pcr.md) on a QX200 system ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- Copy-number inference: [FACETS](../methods/facets.md) algorithm for allele-specific copy number and purity ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- Hotspot detection: the algorithm of Chang et al. 2016 ([cancer-hotspots](../methods/cancer-hotspots.md)) applied to a combined cohort of 2,732 breast tumors (this prospective MSK cohort + retrospectively sequenced primaries including TCGA) ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- IRB approval NCT01775072; protocol allowed return of results to patients and physicians ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).

## Key findings

- Across the 2,732-tumor combined cohort, 313 statistically significant hotspot mutations were identified in 72 genes. *[PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)* alone had 35 hotspots affecting 699 tumors (36.4%); 12 previously unknown *PIK3CA* hotspots were identified as significantly mutated, and 5 of these variants of previously unknown significance induced PI3K pathway activation in MCF10A cells greater than wild-type ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- 32 genes were significantly more frequently mutated in metastases versus primary tumors (q < 0.05); 29 of the 32 (91%) arose in HR+HER2− disease. Metastasis-enriched genes included *[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)*, epigenetic regulators (*[ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md)*, *[ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md)*, *[CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md)*), and MAPK effectors (*ERBB2*, *NF1*, *[FGFR4](../genes/FGFR4.md)*) ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- *ERBB2* activating mutations and *NF1* loss-of-function mutations were more than twice as common in specimens acquired after endocrine therapy versus before (p = 3×10⁻⁵ for *ERBB2*; p = 4×10⁻⁴ for *NF1*) ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- *ESR1* ligand-binding-domain mutations occurred in 18% of post-endocrine-therapy HR+HER2− tumors (128/692), consistent with prior reports from Chandarlapaty 2016 ([PMID:27149842](../papers/27149842.md)) and Toy 2013 ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- *ESR1* mutations were mutually exclusive with MAPK-pathway alterations and with transcription-factor lesions (mutual-exclusivity p < 0.0001). The MAPK-pathway alterations and transcription-factor lesions together affected 27% of *ESR1*-wild-type post-hormonal-therapy HR+HER2− tumors (n = 564) and 22% of all 692 post-hormonal-therapy tumors ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- 16% of post-hormonal-therapy HR+HER2− *ESR1*-wild-type tumors (n = 90) carried known or presumed oncogenic alterations in MAPK effectors (*ERBB2*, *NF1*, *EGFR*, *ERBB3*, *KRAS*, *HRAS*, *BRAF*, *MAP2K1*); 12 patients had focal *EGFR* amplifications in metastases (all previously treated with [tamoxifen](../drugs/tamoxifen.md); 8 also previously received an aromatase inhibitor) ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- MAPK-pathway lesions predicted significantly shorter PFS on aromatase inhibitors: median 3.5 vs 15.2 months for pan-wild-type tumors (log-rank p = 1.4×10⁻⁹). Transcription-factor-altered tumors had median PFS 6.4 months (p = 1.3×10⁻⁶ vs pan-wild-type); *ESR1*-mutant tumors had median PFS 4.1 months (p = 3×10⁻⁴) ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- On SERD ([fulvestrant](../drugs/fulvestrant.md)) therapy, MAPK-altered tumors had shorter PFS than pan-wild-type (median 3.7 vs 4.8 months; p = 0.029); *ESR1*-mutant and transcription-factor-altered tumors had PFS similar to pan-wild-type on SERD ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- HER2 status concordance between IHC/FISH and ERBB2 amplification by MSK-IMPACT was 98% (1,778/1,810 samples; κ = 0.9, 95% CI 0.87–0.94) ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- In matched-pair analyses, 60% of post-progression *EGFR* amplifications and 82% of post-progression *ERBB2* mutations were already present in the treatment-naïve primary tumor, whereas oncogenic mutations in downstream MAPK effectors (*NF1*, *KRAS*, *MAP2K1*, *BRAF*) were typically not detected in the primary and were either acquired or selected by endocrine therapy in 74% of affected patients ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- Research autopsy: a single HR+ patient harbored multiple convergent mechanisms across metastatic sites — pleural disease carried *ESR1* Y537, while five spatially distinct lesions harbored independent *ERBB2* driver mutations (S310, L755, D769); ddPCR (sensitivity ~0.03%) confirmed mutual exclusivity of *ESR1* and *ERBB2* across sites ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- Functional validation in MCF7 cells: stable *EGFR* overexpression conferred fulvestrant resistance, reversed by co-treatment with [erlotinib](../drugs/erlotinib.md) or [gefitinib](../drugs/gefitinib.md), or by structurally distinct ERK inhibitors (including SCH772984). CRISPR/Cas9-mediated *NF1* knockout (4 independent guide RNAs) increased phospho-ERK, MEK, and RSK and reduced fulvestrant sensitivity, which was reversed by ERK inhibition ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).

## Genes & alterations

- [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) — ligand-binding-domain hotspot mutations (e.g., Y537) in 18% of post-endocrine-therapy HR+HER2− tumors; mutually exclusive with MAPK and transcription-factor lesions; associated with shorter PFS on AI but not on SERD ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) — activating hotspot mutations (S310, L755, D769) > 2× enriched in post-endocrine specimens (p = 3×10⁻⁵); 82% of post-progression *ERBB2* mutations pre-existed in matched primaries; functional resistance driver ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) — loss-of-function mutations > 2× enriched after endocrine therapy (p = 4×10⁻⁴); typically acquired or selected under therapy (not detected in matched primaries); CRISPR knockout in MCF7 increased MAPK signaling and induced fulvestrant resistance reversible by ERK inhibition ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) — focal amplifications in 12 post-endocrine-therapy patients (all prior tamoxifen-exposed; 8 also prior AI); 60% of post-progression amplifications were already present in the matched primary; MCF7 *EGFR* overexpression confers fulvestrant resistance, reversed by [erlotinib](../drugs/erlotinib.md) or [gefitinib](../drugs/gefitinib.md) ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md), [ERBB3](../genes/ERBB3.md) — hotspot mutations in MAPK effectors enriched in post-endocrine-therapy tumors; *KRAS* G12R highlighted as a subclonal-to-clonal switch under endocrine selection in one matched-pair patient ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) — focal amplifications enriched in post-hormonal-therapy specimens; ~half were subclonal-and-selected or pre-existing in matched primaries ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- [CTCF](../genes/CTCF.md) — H284 and Y226 hotspots arising exclusively in invasive ductal carcinomas (histology-specific); enriched post-hormonal-therapy; associated with shorter PFS on AI ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- [FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md) — Forkhead-domain hotspots with histology-specific allele distribution (lobular tumors on wing 2; ductal tumors on wing 1); transcription-factor resistance lesion ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- [TBX3](../genes/TBX3.md) — N297 hotspot exclusive to lobular carcinomas; transcription-factor resistance lesion ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — 35 statistically significant hotspots; mutated in 36.4% of the combined 2,732-tumor cohort (699 tumors); 12 newly significant hotspots, 5 of which induced elevated PI3K signaling in MCF10A cells when expressed as variants ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — significantly more common in metastases of all receptor subtypes except HR−HER2+; biomarker of poor prognosis ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- [GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md) — loss-of-function mutations associated with lung metastasis (organotropism) ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- [CDH1](../genes/CDH1.md) — mutations associated with ovarian metastasis, reflecting lobular-carcinoma predisposition to ovarian spread ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — recurrently altered in HR+ metastatic cohort with patterns consistent with primary disease ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md) — SWI/SNF-complex loss-of-function more often acquired post-treatment than pre-treatment in matched pairs ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md) — epigenetic regulator enriched in HR+ metastatic context ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- [FGFR4](../genes/FGFR4.md), [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) — *FGFR4* enriched in HR+ metastases; *[FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md)* focal amplification noted as a clonal event in a representative matched-pair patient ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md) — E78K mutations in 3 HR+HER2− patients post-AI therapy, affecting a MATH-domain region distinct from prostate-cancer [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md) hotspots and analogous to ER-stabilizing loss-of-function alleles in endometrial cancer ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- [RHOA](../genes/RHOA.md) — L22R subclonal hotspot present in a treatment-naïve primary but lost at post-progression, illustrating subclone replacement under endocrine therapy ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).

## Clinical implications

- The authors propose a four-class taxonomy of endocrine-resistant breast cancer: (1) *ESR1*-mutant, (2) MAPK-pathway-altered, (3) ER-transcriptional-machinery-altered (*MYC*, *CTCF*, *FOXA1*, *TBX3*), and (4) pan-wild-type tumors with unknown resistance mechanism ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- HR+ tumors carrying ESR1, MAPK, or transcription-factor lesions are unlikely to benefit from continued aromatase-inhibitor therapy and should be considered for alternative regimens ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- HER2 kinase inhibitors are active in *ERBB2*-mutant breast tumors (citing Hyman et al. 2018); [erlotinib](../drugs/erlotinib.md) and [gefitinib](../drugs/gefitinib.md) reverse fulvestrant resistance in EGFR-amplified MCF7 cells, suggesting EGFR inhibitors warrant prospective evaluation in *EGFR*-amplified endocrine-resistant HR+ disease ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- MAPK-pathway inhibitors (selective MEK and ERK inhibitors) are flagged as candidate combination partners with hormonal therapy in MAPK-altered HR+ tumors ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- The autopsy data — convergent independent *ERBB2* driver mutations across metastatic sites, with *ESR1* and *ERBB2* in distinct subclones — argue for sensitive [cfDNA](../themes/) profiling rather than single-site biopsy to capture coexisting resistance mechanisms ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- [Fulvestrant](../drugs/fulvestrant.md) (SERD) outperforms aromatase inhibitors in *ESR1*-mutant and transcription-factor-altered settings (no PFS deficit vs pan-wild-type), but MAPK-altered tumors remain resistant on SERD as well ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).

## Limitations & open questions

- Single-institution dataset with retrospective clinical annotation; the authors call for independent multi-institutional confirmation ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- 87.5% of biopsied metastatic tumors had prior adjuvant or metastatic therapy exposure, making it analytically impossible to fully disentangle metastasis-intrinsic from therapy-selected alterations; the treatment-naïve metastatic subgroup (n = 125) was underpowered for separate enrichment analysis ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- Single biopsies cannot capture the multi-clonal, spatially heterogeneous resistance phenotypes evident from the autopsy case; cfDNA studies suggest *ESR1*-mutation rates in tissue underestimate true prevalence ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- WES sensitivity (~5% CCF) and even higher-depth MSK-IMPACT (~1.3% CCF) cannot exclude rare pre-existing resistance subclones; mutations called "acquired" may simply have been below detection in the primary tumor ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- Pan-wild-type endocrine-resistant tumors (the fourth taxonomy class) remain unexplained; the authors note non-coding transcription-factor mutations and broader profiling as plausible future avenues ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- The SERM PFS analysis showed no significant difference among the resistance subgroups (Figure S4B); whether SERMs are differentially affected by these mechanisms is not resolved ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
- Differential PFS impact of transcription-factor alterations on AI vs SERD therapy is hypothesis-generating and requires mechanistic validation in models ([PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)).
