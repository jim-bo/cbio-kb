**PMID:** 28985567 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.cell.2017.09.027 · **Journal:** Cell (2017)

## TL;DR

Reddy et al. performed integrative whole-exome and transcriptome sequencing of 1001 newly-diagnosed [DLBCL](../cancer_types/DLBCLNOS.md) patients (502 with paired germline DNA) treated uniformly with rituximab-containing regimens, defining a comprehensive landscape of 150 recurrently mutated driver genes. They characterized functional dependencies via a genome-wide CRISPR screen across six lymphoma cell lines and built a multivariate prognostic model combining genetic alterations with cell-of-origin and MYC/BCL2 expression that outperformed the International Prognostic Index (IPI), cell-of-origin classification, and dual MYC/BCL2 expression alone.

## Cohort & data

- 1001 de novo DLBCL tumors with whole-exome sequencing; 400 paired tumor-normal pairs from this study plus 102 previously published pairs (Lohr 2012, Morin 2011, Pasqualucci 2011, Zhang 2013) for a total 502-pair primary discovery set.
- All cases treated with a rituximab-containing standard regimen; complete clinical annotation including IPI, response, overall survival, gender, age, stage, performance status, and extranodal sites.
- RNA-seq performed on 775 cases for which adequate material was available; 625 used in core integrative analysis.
- Cell-of-origin assignment via RNA-seq classifier (Wright 2003): 313 ABC, 331 GCB, remainder unclassified. Cross-validated against Nanostring (n=200, R²=0.87, p<10⁻⁶) and the Hans IHC algorithm (n=654, p<10⁻⁶).
- Cancer types: [DLBCL NOS](../cancer_types/DLBCLNOS.md); one Burkitt lymphoma cell line ([BL](../cancer_types/BL.md), BJAB) included in the CRISPR screen.
- Dataset: [dlbcl_duke_2017](../datasets/dlbcl_duke_2017.md).
- Assays: [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) (Agilent All Exon V5, ~75X coverage on Illumina HiSeq 2500), [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md), [MuTect](../methods/mutect.md) v1.1.4 somatic variant calling, [BWA](../methods/bwa.md) mem alignment to hg19, [EXCAVATOR](../methods/excavator-cnv.md) for CNVs, [ANNOVAR](../methods/annovar.md) annotation, [FISH](../methods/fish.md) for MYC/BCL2 translocations, [IHC](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md) ([IRF4](../genes/IRF4.md), [BCL6](../genes/BCL6.md), CD10), and [Sanger sequencing](../methods/sanger-sequencing.md) for variant validation (1130 events, 61 genes, 90% concordance).
- CRISPR screen: GeCKO v2 genome-wide library (~120,000 sgRNAs, 19,050 protein-coding genes) in three replicates across six cell lines — three ABC DLBCL (LY3, TMD8, HBL1), two GCB DLBCL (SUDHL4, Pfeiffer), one Burkitt-like (BJAB). 14 population doublings between early and late timepoints; analyzed with MAGeCK-VISPR.
- Standard treatment regimen: [rituximab](../drugs/rituximab.md)-containing immunochemotherapy (de facto R-CHOP).

## Key findings

- Identified **150 recurrent driver genes** in DLBCL (mean 7.75 mutations per case), of which 27 (including [SPEN](../genes/SPEN.md), [KLHL14](../genes/KLHL14.md), and [MGA](../genes/MGA.md)) had not been previously implicated in DLBCL.
- Genes [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) and [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), historically described as primarily copy-number altered, were also recurrent targets of function-altering point mutations.
- 20 driver genes were differentially mutated between ABC and GCB subtypes: [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md), [SGK1](../genes/SGK1.md), [GNA13](../genes/GNA13.md), [SOCS1](../genes/SOCS1.md), [STAT6](../genes/STAT6.md), and [TNFRSF14](../genes/TNFRSF14.md) enriched in GCB; [ETV6](../genes/ETV6.md), [MYD88](../genes/MYD88.md), [PIM1](../genes/PIM1.md), and [TBL1XR1](../genes/TBL1XR1.md) enriched in ABC.
- Pairwise overlap analysis using Fisher's test and mutual exclusion (Leiserson 2016) found 61 of 150 driver genes had statistically significant relationships with other drivers (p<0.01). Notable mutually exclusive pairs include [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) (MLL2) vs [MYC](../genes/MYC.md), and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) vs [KLHL6](../genes/KLHL6.md).
- CRISPR screen identified **1956 essential genes** across at least one cell line. Driver genes from WES were disproportionately enriched at the extremes of the CRISPR-score distribution (p=3×10⁻⁵).
- 35 driver genes were functional oncogenes (knockout reduced viability); top oncogenic dependencies across most DLBCLs included [MYC](../genes/MYC.md), [RHOA](../genes/RHOA.md), [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md), [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md), and [BCL2](../genes/BCL2.md). Knockout-enriched (tumor-suppressor) hits included [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [MGA](../genes/MGA.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), and [NCOR1](../genes/NCOR1.md).
- Subtype-specific essentiality: knockout of [EBF1](../genes/EBF1.md), [IRF4](../genes/IRF4.md), [CARD11](../genes/CARD11.md), [MYD88](../genes/MYD88.md), and [IKBKB](../genes/IKBKB.md) was selectively lethal in ABC DLBCL; knockout of [ZBTB7A](../genes/ZBTB7A.md), [XPO1](../genes/XPO1.md), [TGFBR2](../genes/TGFBR2.md), and [PTPN6](../genes/PTPN6.md) was selectively lethal in GCB DLBCL.
- 9 of 35 CRISPR-validated driver genes are direct targets of drugs in clinical trials or already approved for other indications; 36% of DLBCL patients harbor genetic alterations in these 9 drug targets.
- Integrative gene-set analysis (KEGG, Reactome, MSigDB plus lymphoma-specific signatures from Lenz 2008, Monti 2005, Shaffer) reduced ~9500 gene sets to 31 non-redundant clusters via affinity propagation. CRISPR hits were enriched in cancer-related processes (oxidative phosphorylation, DNA replication, cell cycle, RNA processing) and depleted in immune/stromal sets.
- IPI was highly prognostic (p<10⁻⁶); ABC vs GCB cell-of-origin was prognostic (P=0.002 log-rank); MYC/BCL2 dual-high expressors had worse survival; ABC/GCB status did not further stratify dual expressors.
- FISH analysis: [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) translocations associated with [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) mutations and high MYC expression; [BCL2](../genes/BCL2.md) translocations associated with [BCL2](../genes/BCL2.md) mutations and amplifications.
- Mutations associated with poorer survival across all DLBCL: [MYC](../genes/MYC.md), [CD79B](../genes/CD79B.md), [ZFAT](../genes/ZFAT.md). Favorable: [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), [SGK1](../genes/SGK1.md).
- Within ABC DLBCL: [KLHL14](../genes/KLHL14.md), [BTG1](../genes/BTG1.md), [PAX5](../genes/PAX5.md), and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) alterations associated with poorer survival; [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md) alterations with favorable survival.
- Within GCB DLBCL: [NFKBIA](../genes/NFKBIA.md), [NCOR1](../genes/NCOR1.md) alterations associated with poorer survival; [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md), [MYD88](../genes/MYD88.md), and [ARID5B](../genes/ARID5B.md) alterations associated with significantly better survival.
- A multivariate Cox proportional-hazards model combining 150 driver genes with cell-of-origin, MYC, and BCL2 expression — using all combinations of up to 3 markers affecting ≥20 patients — yielded a 3-tier genomic risk classifier validated on an independent 20% test set (p=8×10⁻⁵) and via 5-fold cross-validation × 100 (median log-rank p=8×10⁻⁶).
- The integrative DNA+RNA combinatorial model outperformed DNA-only, RNA-only, and DNA+RNA-without-combinations models, IPI alone, cell-of-origin alone, and MYC/BCL2 dual-expressor models. The genomic risk model retained prognostic value within IPI strata and within complete-response patients.
- The IPI was strongest at predicting early mortality; the genomic risk model retained prognostic value for both early and late (≥5-year) mortality.
- MYC genetic alterations + high MYC expression marked the worst-prognosis subset; GCB DLBCL with [CD70](../genes/CD70.md) alterations marked the best-prognosis subset.

## Genes & alterations

- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) — combined genetic alteration plus high RNA expression defines the worst-prognosis DLBCL subset; CRISPR-essential across most cell lines; mutually exclusive with [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) (MLL2) mutations.
- [BCL2](../genes/BCL2.md) — recurrent oncogenic missense and copy gains; FISH translocations correlate with mutations and amplifications; CRISPR-essential dependency; component of dual-expressor poor-prognosis signature.
- [BCL6](../genes/BCL6.md) — measured by IHC for cell-of-origin (Hans algorithm); recurrent driver.
- [CARD11](../genes/CARD11.md) — oncogenic missense pattern; CRISPR-essential selectively in ABC DLBCL.
- [IRF4](../genes/IRF4.md) — oncogenic missense; CRISPR-essential selectively in ABC DLBCL; used in Hans IHC algorithm.
- [SPEN](../genes/SPEN.md) — newly implicated tumor suppressor (truncating mutations / CN loss).
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — recurrent CN loss plus function-altering point mutations; alterations associated with poorer survival in ABC DLBCL.
- [TNFAIP3](../genes/TNFAIP3.md) — tumor-suppressor pattern.
- [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) — preferentially mutated in GCB; mutations associated with better survival in GCB.
- [SGK1](../genes/SGK1.md) — preferentially mutated in GCB; mutations associated with favorable survival across DLBCL.
- [GNA13](../genes/GNA13.md), [SOCS1](../genes/SOCS1.md), [STAT6](../genes/STAT6.md), [TNFRSF14](../genes/TNFRSF14.md) — preferentially mutated in GCB DLBCL.
- [ETV6](../genes/ETV6.md), [MYD88](../genes/MYD88.md), [PIM1](../genes/PIM1.md), [TBL1XR1](../genes/TBL1XR1.md) — preferentially mutated in ABC DLBCL. [MYD88](../genes/MYD88.md) mutations associated with better survival in GCB; CRISPR-essential in ABC.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — recurrent driver; mutually exclusive with [KLHL6](../genes/KLHL6.md); CRISPR-enriched (loss of [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) increases fitness).
- [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) (formerly MLL2) — recurrent driver, mutually exclusive with MYC mutations.
- [RHOA](../genes/RHOA.md) — CRISPR-essential dependency in DLBCL; associated with proliferation gene-expression signatures.
- [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md), [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md) — top CRISPR-essential dependencies; [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md) mutations are prevalent but those patients tend to have generally good outcomes (especially in GCB).
- [MGA](../genes/MGA.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [NCOR1](../genes/NCOR1.md) — CRISPR-enriched (tumor-suppressor pattern). [NCOR1](../genes/NCOR1.md) alterations associated with poorer survival in GCB.
- [EBF1](../genes/EBF1.md), [IKBKB](../genes/IKBKB.md) — ABC-selective CRISPR essentiality (B-cell-development/NF-κB signaling).
- [ZBTB7A](../genes/ZBTB7A.md), [XPO1](../genes/XPO1.md), [TGFBR2](../genes/TGFBR2.md), [PTPN6](../genes/PTPN6.md) — GCB-selective CRISPR essentiality.
- [CD79B](../genes/CD79B.md), [ZFAT](../genes/ZFAT.md) — mutations associated with poorer overall survival.
- [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) — mutations associated with favorable survival.
- [KLHL14](../genes/KLHL14.md), [BTG1](../genes/BTG1.md), [PAX5](../genes/PAX5.md) — alterations associated with poorer survival in ABC DLBCL.
- [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md) — alterations associated with favorable survival in ABC DLBCL.
- [NFKBIA](../genes/NFKBIA.md), [ARID5B](../genes/ARID5B.md) — survival modifiers in GCB; [ARID5B](../genes/ARID5B.md) associated with better survival.
- [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md), [PIM2](../genes/PIM2.md), [BTK](../genes/BTK.md), [CHD8](../genes/CHD8.md), [POU2F2](../genes/POU2F2.md), [YY1](../genes/YY1.md), [H1-4](../genes/H1-4.md) (HIST1H1E) — driver-gene cluster members across signaling, cell growth, B-cell development, and transcription/translation functional groups.
- [NOTCH2](../genes/NOTCH2.md), [PIK3CD](../genes/PIK3CD.md), [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md) — recurrent drivers but CRISPR knockout did NOT impair growth, suggesting they may act in early pathogenesis or via non-targetable functions in established DLBCL.
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — recurrent point mutations in addition to historically described copy-number losses.
- [CD70](../genes/CD70.md) — alterations within GCB DLBCL define the most favorable-prognosis subset.

## Clinical implications

- **Risk stratification.** A multivariate combinatorial model integrating 150 driver genes with cell-of-origin and MYC/BCL2 expression outperforms IPI, cell-of-origin, and MYC/BCL2 dual-expression scores. The genomic risk model retains prognostic value within complete responders and across IPI strata, identifying high-risk patients who otherwise appear low-risk by clinical criteria.
- **Biomarker deployment.** The authors note the model can be applied clinically using existing assays — RNA-based cell-of-origin, MYC and BCL2 expression measurement, plus targeted sequencing of a panel of DLBCL driver genes. An interactive web tool was released at dlbcl.davelab.org.
- **Therapeutic targeting.** ~36% of DLBCL patients harbor alterations in one of 9 CRISPR-validated essential drivers that are direct targets of approved or trial-stage drugs, suggesting actionable subsets for targeted therapy.
- **Subtype-directed therapy hypotheses.** ABC-selective CRISPR dependencies on [CARD11](../genes/CARD11.md), [MYD88](../genes/MYD88.md), [IKBKB](../genes/IKBKB.md), [IRF4](../genes/IRF4.md), and [EBF1](../genes/EBF1.md) reinforce NF-κB pathway targeting (e.g., [BTK](../genes/BTK.md) inhibitors, IRAK inhibitors) for ABC patients. GCB-selective dependence on [XPO1](../genes/XPO1.md), [PTPN6](../genes/PTPN6.md), [TGFBR2](../genes/TGFBR2.md), and [ZBTB7A](../genes/ZBTB7A.md) suggests distinct opportunities (e.g., [selinexor](../drugs/selinexor.md) for [XPO1](../genes/XPO1.md)).
- **Caution on naive ontology-based targeting.** CRISPR knockout of [NOTCH2](../genes/NOTCH2.md), [PIK3CD](../genes/PIK3CD.md), and [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md) — all recurrently mutated in DLBCL and pharmacologically targetable — did not impair growth, suggesting therapeutic targeting based on mutation alone may fail in DLBCL.
- **Trial-design implication for [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md).** Although MTOR mutations are prevalent (and CRISPR-essential), MTOR-mutant DLBCLs tend to have favorable prognosis (especially in GCB). Trials enrolling relapsed patients may systematically miss the responder population.
- **Treatment baseline.** All 1001 patients received [rituximab](../drugs/rituximab.md)-containing immunochemotherapy as standard of care, providing a uniformly-treated reference cohort for future biomarker discovery.

## Limitations & open questions

- CRISPR functional screen was performed in only six cell lines (3 ABC, 2 GCB, 1 Burkitt-like), which may not capture the full genetic and microenvironmental diversity of the 1001-patient cohort.
- Expression of many signaling pathways (e.g., PI3-kinase) did not reliably correlate with mutations in pathway components ([PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md), [PIK3CD](../genes/PIK3CD.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md)), suggesting bulk RNA expression from non-malignant infiltrating cells confounds these associations.
- Lethal effects of driver gene knockout were not restricted to cell lines harboring mutations in those genes, indicating these drivers represent broadly favored proliferative pathways rather than synthetic-lethal vulnerabilities for mutated subsets.
- The authors note ongoing mortality even among complete responders, implying a substantial fraction of clinically-defined "cured" patients still harbor genomic risk.
- The genomic model was validated only on a 20% held-out subset of the same cohort plus cross-validation; external multi-institutional validation remains a future need.
- No direct comparison with other contemporary classifications (e.g., LymphGen genetic subtypes, Chapuy clusters) is provided in this paper.
- Some recurrently mutated, pharmacologically-targetable genes ([NOTCH2](../genes/NOTCH2.md), [PIK3CD](../genes/PIK3CD.md), [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md)) lack functional dependence in DLBCL cells, leaving open whether they act earlier in lymphomagenesis or whether the CRISPR screen missed context-dependent essentiality.
