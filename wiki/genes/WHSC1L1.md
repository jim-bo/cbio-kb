# WHSC1L1

## Overview

WHSC1L1 (Wolf-Hirschhorn Syndrome Candidate 1-Like 1), now designated [NSD3](../genes/NSD3.md) (Nuclear Receptor Binding SET Domain Protein 3) by HUGO, is a histone H3K36 methyltransferase located at 8p11.23. In prostate cancer, WHSC1L1 is recurrently co-amplified with [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) as part of a focal amplification at 8p11.23.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Co-amplified with [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) at 8p11.23 in ~8% of primary prostate cancer ([PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md)) in the TCGA cohort [PMID:26544944](../papers/26544944.md)
- Focal amplification at FGFR1/WHSC1L1 locus identified as an expanded Ras/Raf/RTK pathway driver in lung adenocarcinoma ([LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md)) in the TCGA Pan-Lung study of 1,144 NSCLC cases; raises the proportion of lung ADCs with a candidate RTK pathway driver [PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md)**: Focal co-amplification with FGFR1 at 8p11.23 in ~8% of primary prostate tumors [PMID:26544944](../papers/26544944.md)
- **[LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md):** FGFR1/WHSC1L1 focal amplification identified as an expanded RTK pathway driver, contributing to the 76% of lung ADCs with a candidate Ras/Raf/RTK driver [PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity
