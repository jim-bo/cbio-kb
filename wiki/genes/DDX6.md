# DDX6

## Overview

DDX6 (DEAD-Box Helicase 6) is an RNA helicase involved in mRNA decapping, translational repression, and P-body formation. It has been identified as a novel [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) fusion partner in therapy-related myeloid malignancy, detectable only by RNA-capture sequencing and not by conventional cytogenetics or DNA-based methods.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Novel KMT2A::DDX6 fusion identified by RNA-capture (ArcherDX FusionPlex Pan-Heme) in a high-risk neuroblastoma patient who developed therapy-related MDS/AL at MSKCC; not detectable by DNA-based capture or conventional cytogenetics — RNA-capture only [PMID:35078859](../papers/35078859.md)
- DDX6, together with [PRDM10](../genes/PRDM10.md), identified as a novel KMT2A partner involved in histone methyltransferase activity [PMID:35078859](../papers/35078859.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [TMN](../cancer_types/TMN.md) / [MDS](../cancer_types/MDS.md) / [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md): KMT2A::DDX6 fusion identified in therapy-related myeloid neoplasm arising after neuroblastoma treatment [PMID:35078859](../papers/35078859.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- KMT2A rearrangements (including KMT2A::DDX6) are mutually exclusive with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations in the pediatric t-MDS/AL cohort (with one exception involving clonal KMT2A + subclonal TP53) [PMID:35078859](../papers/35078859.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- RNA fusion capture (rather than DNA capture or cytogenetics) is required to detect KMT2A::DDX6; RNA-based surveillance detected KMT2A rearrangements a median of 28.5 months earlier than conventional cytogenetics [PMID:35078859](../papers/35078859.md)

## Open questions

- The functional mechanism by which DDX6 fusion to KMT2A contributes to leukemogenesis is not established; DDX6's role in RNA metabolism is distinct from most KMT2A fusion partners, which are typically transcriptional elongation factors.
