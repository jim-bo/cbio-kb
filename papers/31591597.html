A rectal cancer organoid platform to study individual responses to chemoradiation

TL;DR

Ganesh et al. built a biorepository of 65 patient-derived rectal cancer (RC) organoid cultures (“tumoroids”) from 41 Memorial Sloan Kettering patients with primary, metastatic, or recurrent disease, including pre- and post-treatment samples. Tumoroids retained the histopathology and oncogenic mutation profiles of the parental tumors as confirmed by MSK-IMPACT sequencing, and their ex vivo responses to 5-FU, FOLFOX, and ionizing radiation tracked with the matched patients’ clinical responses (Spearman r = 0.86, p = 0.024 for both chemotherapy regimens against PFS). The authors further established a first-of-its-kind endoluminal patient-derived xenograft model in which human RC tumoroids engraft into the NSG mouse rectum, recapitulate invasion, generate site-matched lung/liver metastases, and reproduce patient-specific 5-FU and FOLFOX response patterns.

Cohort & data

  • Tumoroid biorepository: 65 RC tumoroids derived from 41 patients (84 attempts, 77% success rate). 49 of 65 tumoroids were grown from outpatient biopsy-forceps tissue. 22 tumoroids from treatment-naïve patients; 43 from patients on first- or second-line therapy. 17 from sites of recurrence or metastasis. Two patients had mismatch-repair-deficient tumors (5%, consistent with the 5–6% MMR-d rate observed clinically in RC). 62% of tumoroids were RAS mutant.
  • Normal controls: 51 normal rectal organoids derived from matched adjacent mucosa.
  • Sequencing: Genomic DNA from 31 RC tumoroid lines profiled with MSK-IMPACT (custom, FDA-cleared tumor profiling panel — see IMPACT468 for the contemporaneous gene list).
  • Reference cohort: rectal_msk_2019 — a clinical cohort of 287 rectal cancers ([READ](../cancer_types/READ.md)) resected at Memorial Hospital and sequenced by MSK-IMPACT used as the comparator for mutation landscape.
  • Disease setting: rectal adenocarcinoma (READ), a subtype of colorectal cancer (COADREAD); patients reflected tri-modal (neoadjuvant chemoradiation + surgery + 5-FU-based chemotherapy) treatment pathways.
  • Ex vivo assays: 21 tumoroids screened against 5-fluorouracil and FOLFOX (5-FU + leucovorin + oxaliplatin); 19 tumoroids screened against ionizing radiation; KRAS-mutant vs. wild-type tumoroids tested against cetuximab.
  • In vivo model: Endoluminal xenograft in NOD-scid-gamma (NSG) mouse rectum — 200,000 tumoroid cells injected per mouse, eight tumoroid lines tested, both sexes.

Key findings

  • Establishment efficiency: 65 of 84 derivation attempts succeeded (77%), including from heavily pre-treated specimens and from minute (≈2.8 mm) outpatient biopsy forceps samples. Of 19 failures, seven survived ~6 weeks before senescing (PMID:31591597).
  • Mutational fidelity: 92% of likely-oncogenic tumor mutations (range 0.66–1.00) were preserved in matched tumoroids, comparable to the 88% concordance previously reported for colon organoids. 77% of clonal oncogenic mutations in matched tumors remained clonal in the tumoroid.
  • Landscape concordance: The top mutated genes in tumoroids (APC, TP53, KRAS, FBXW7) mirror those in the matched 287-patient clinical MSK rectal-cancer cohort (rectal_msk_2019).
  • Ex vivo chemosensitivity predicts PFS: In seven patients with mature follow-up, ex vivo AUC for both 5-FU and FOLFOX correlated with patient progression-free survival (Spearman r = 0.86, p = 0.024 for both regimens).
  • Ex vivo radiation response matches clinic: Of 19 radiated tumoroids, those at or above the 75th-percentile resistance level came from patients who recurred or had minimal clinical response (RC-MSK-003, RC-MSK-004, RC-MSK-023); those at or below the 25th-percentile resistance came from patients with ≥50% endoscopic tumor reduction or clinical complete response (RC-MSK-001, RC-MSK-025, RC-MSK-031).
  • Targeted-therapy proof of concept: KRAS-mutant tumoroids were resistant to anti-EGFR cetuximab, while KRAS wild-type tumoroids were sensitive, recapitulating the established CRC clinical-trial finding.
  • Endoluminal xenograft model: 100% of NSG mice transplanted with tumoroids showed hEpCAM-positive engraftment, with gross tumor formation in 20–100% per experiment. Tumors progressed to intramucosal adenocarcinoma by 16 weeks and invasive adenocarcinoma by 22–30 weeks, generating site-specific lung (RC-MSK-001) and liver (RC-MSK-002) metastases that matched the originating patient’s metastatic pattern.
  • In vivo chemosensitivity recapitulates clinic: Mice engrafted with the more aggressive RC-MSK-008 tumoroid (donor OS = 1.1 yr) failed to respond to 5-FU or FOLFOX, while RC-MSK-002 (donor OS = 3.3 yr) responded; similarly, RC-MSK-023 (donor PFS = 3.4 mo) did not respond to FOLFOX whereas RC-MSK-001 (donor PFS = 14.2 mo) did.

Genes & alterations

  • APC — most frequent mutation in both the tumoroid panel and the 287-patient MSK RC cohort (rectal_msk_2019); consistent with canonical Wnt-pathway loss in colorectal/rectal adenocarcinoma.
  • TP53 — top co-occurring driver in tumoroids and matched cohort.
  • KRAS — 62% of tumoroids were RAS mutant; KRAS-mutant tumoroids exhibited cell-autonomous resistance to cetuximab ex vivo, mirroring the established clinical-trial finding that KRAS mutation predicts anti-EGFR-antibody resistance in CRC.
  • FBXW7 — among the top recurrently mutated genes in both tumoroids and the 287-patient comparator cohort.
  • EGFR — target of cetuximab; not mutated, but the KRAS↔︎cetuximab axis depends on intact EGFR signalling.

Clinical implications

  • Predictive biomarker for chemoresponse: Ex vivo tumoroid AUC against 5-FU or FOLFOX could prospectively flag rectal-cancer patients at high risk of progression on standard-of-care chemotherapy (Spearman r = 0.86, p = 0.024 vs. PFS). The 6–12-week derivation-to-readout window fits within the ~6-month total-neoadjuvant-therapy timeline, leaving room to inform clinical decisions.
  • Predictive biomarker for radiotherapy response: Ex vivo radiation sensitivity tracked with endoscopic post-radiation tumor regression, raising the possibility of sparing predicted non-responders the toxicity of pelvic radiation if validated prospectively.
  • Targeted-therapy modeling: The KRAS-mutation→cetuximab-resistance recapitulation in tumoroids supports their use as a preclinical filter for new anti-EGFR or KRAS-pathway agents in the RC-specific setting (cetuximab is not standard RC therapy).
  • Metastasis modelling for organ-tropic biology: The endoluminal xenograft reproduces patient-specific lung-vs-liver metastatic tropism, providing a model to dissect the rectum’s iliac-vein-mediated lung-metastasis bias (~69% lung vs ~20% liver vs colon-cancer pattern).
  • Practical feasibility: Tumoroids can be derived from a single 2.8 mm outpatient biopsy without surgical resection — making serial pre/intra/post-treatment modelling feasible in routine clinical practice.

Limitations & open questions

  • Small predictive cohort: Only seven patients had mature PFS for the ex vivo–to-clinic correlation; prospective, larger validation is required before tumoroid sensitivity can be used to triage real patients.
  • Selection bias: Tumoroid derivation success was 77%, but the authors do not characterize whether the 23% failures share clinical or molecular features that could bias the panel toward more aggressive or growth-factor-independent disease.
  • In vivo metastasis evidence: Lung and liver metastases were demonstrated for selected lines, but the cohort engrafted was small (8 lines for tumor formation; 4 lines for in vivo chemosensitivity) and the patient-specific tropism observation needs broader replication.
  • Microenvironment: Tumoroids lack immune and stromal compartments — limiting their utility for immunotherapy modeling and for chemoradiation effects mediated through the tumor microenvironment (e.g., radiation-induced stromal changes).
  • Standard-of-care offset: Cetuximab is not routinely used in rectal cancer; the targeted-therapy proof of concept thus validates the platform’s biological fidelity but does not generate immediately actionable RC treatment data.
  • Generalizability of the endoluminal model: Gross tumor formation ranged 20–100% per experiment, and the protocol depends on the MSK Precision Modeling Center’s specialized rectal-injection technique — adoption elsewhere may face technical barriers.
