# Korea Catholic University Vulvar SCC WES Cohort (2018)

## Overview

First genome-wide characterization of vulvar squamous cell carcinoma, generated by Han et al. at The Catholic University of Korea. The cohort comprises 15 Korean vulvar SCC patients (6 HPV-positive, 9 HPV-negative) with surgically resected primary tumors and matched normal tissue, profiled by whole-exome sequencing and copy-number analysis. The study establishes etiology-stratified genomic landscapes: HPV(−) tumours recapitulate classical drivers ([TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md)) while HPV(+) tumours exhibit novel mutations in [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), and [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md). This dataset is deposited in cBioPortal as `vsc_cuk_2018`. [PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)

## Composition

- **15 Korean vulvar SCC patients** (ages 45–83) with surgically resected primary tumors and matched normal tissue. [PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)
- **HPV status by real-time PCR (Seegene):** 6 HPV(+) (HPV16, 52, or 58 — all high-risk) and 9 HPV(−). [PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)
- **Cancer type:** vulvar squamous cell carcinoma ([VSC](../cancer_types/VSC.md)). [PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)
- **Tissue:** 1 frozen (VSCC2), 14 FFPE; tumor cell purity approximately 70% after microdissection by two pathologists. [PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)
- **No survival or treatment-response data** are linked to the molecular calls in this dataset. [PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)

## Assays / panels (linked)

- [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md): Agilent SureSelect Human All Exome V4; alignment by [BWA v0.7.15](../methods/bwa.md) to hg19; Picard v2.7.1 and Samtools v1.3.1 for deduplication; GATK v3.6 for local realignment and BQSR. [PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)
- **Variant calling:** [MuTect](../methods/mutect.md) for SNVs; [SomaticIndelDetector](../methods/gatk-somatic-indel-detector.md) for indels; [ANNOVAR](../methods/annovar.md) for annotation; 21 mutations across 9 genes validated by digital PCR or Sanger sequencing. [PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)
- **Mutational signatures:** [SomaticSignatures R package](../methods/somatic-signatures.md) using NMF, compared to 30 COSMIC signatures and to TCGA SCCs ([CESC](../cancer_types/CESC.md), [ESCA](../cancer_types/ESCA.md), [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md), [LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md)). [PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)
- **Copy-number alterations:** ngCGH module and RankSegmentation in [Nexus v7.5](../methods/nexus-copy-number.md); LOH events via [Sequenza](../methods/sequenza.md), manually curated using depth ratio and B-allele frequency. [PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)

## Papers using this cohort

- [PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md) — Han et al. 2018, *Experimental & Molecular Medicine* — "Mutational signatures and chromosome alteration profiles of squamous cell carcinomas of the vulva"

## Notable findings derived from this cohort

- HPV(−) vulvar SCCs carried more nonsilent and driver mutations than HPV(+) cases; CNA burden and mutational signatures did not differ between the two etiologic groups. [PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)
- HPV(−) tumours recapitulated classical drivers: [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), and [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md); CNAs included 7p and 8q gains and 2q loss. [PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)
- HPV(+) tumours harboured novel-for-vulvar-SCC mutations in [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), and [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md). [PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) and [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md) were each altered in 40% (6/15) of vulvar SCCs irrespective of HPV status. [PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)
- Kataegis (localized hypermutation, average inter-mutation distance ≤10 kb) detected in 2 HPV(+) tumours. [PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)
- Copy-neutral loss of heterozygosity observed in 4 tumours (1 HPV(+), 3 HPV(−)). [PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)
