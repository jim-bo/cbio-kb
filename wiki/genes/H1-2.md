# H1-2

## Overview

H1-2 (formerly HIST1H1C) encodes a linker histone H1 variant that packages chromatin by binding to nucleosomes and the linker DNA between them. In DLBCL, recurrent mutations in the globular and C-terminal domains of H1 family members (H1-2, [H1-3](../genes/H1-3.md), [H1-4](../genes/H1-4.md), [H1-5](../genes/H1-5.md)) are a defining feature of the C4 GCB subtype; these mutations are thought to reduce chromatin association and locally elevate the somatic mutation rate via altered chromatin accessibility.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- H1-2 (HIST1H1C) linker histone mutations co-define DLBCL C4 GCB subtype in a 304-patient WES cohort; globular/C-terminal domain mutations likely reduce chromatin association and raise local mutation rate; co-occurring with H1-3, H1-4, H1-5, and HIST1H2 core histone mutations. [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [DLBCLNOS](../cancer_types/DLBCLNOS.md): H1-2 mutations define the C4 GCB-DLBCL genetic subtype characterized by a favorable prognosis relative to C3/C5; associated with histone/BCR-mutant biology. [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with H1-3, [H1-4](../genes/H1-4.md), [H1-5](../genes/H1-5.md), and HIST1H2 core histone mutations in the DLBCL C4 subtype. [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- C4 DLBCL (linker histone-mutant) has a relatively favorable prognosis; no specific targeted therapy for H1-2-mutant DLBCL described in current corpus.

## Open questions

- The precise mechanism by which linker histone mutations elevate local somatic mutation rates and contribute to lymphomagenesis requires functional validation. [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)
