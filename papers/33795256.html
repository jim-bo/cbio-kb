Next-Generation Sequencing of 487 Esophageal Adenocarcinomas Reveals Independently Prognostic Genomic Driver Alterations and Pathways

TL;DR

Sihag et al. performed prospective broad-panel next-generation sequencing on 487 lower esophageal and esophagogastric junction adenocarcinomas (EAC) from Memorial Sloan Kettering, using MSK-IMPACT, and linked the resulting genomic profiles to overall survival in patients triaged into curative-intent (N=230) and palliative-intent (N=257) treatment cohorts. They identified 16 recurrent oncogenic drivers (≥5% prevalence), 10 of which were amplifications, and showed that CDKN2A alterations, SMAD4 alterations, and KRAS amplifications are independently associated with worse OS, while ERBB2 amplification is associated with improved OS (largely attributable to trastuzumab therapy). Genome-instability metrics (fraction of genome altered, whole-genome doubling, number of oncogenic drivers, Cell Cycle pathway enrichment) were higher in palliative-intent / advanced-stage patients.

Cohort & data

  • N = 487 patients with lower esophageal and esophagogastric junction adenocarcinoma (EGC / ESCA) sequenced at MSK between 2014 and 2019.
  • Two clinical cohorts: curative-intent therapy (CIT, N=230) — predominantly stage I–III; palliative-intent therapy (PIT, N=257) — overwhelmingly clinical stage IV.
  • Original cohort of 1,029 esophagogastric patients was filtered to exclude gastric adenocarcinomas (N=473), esophageal squamous cell carcinomas (N=53), and other histologies (N=16).
  • Tumor tissue from primary or metastatic sites; for patients with multiple sequenced samples, the highest-tumor-purity sample was retained.
  • Assay: MSK-IMPACT hybridization-capture NGS — 45 patients on v1 (IMPACT341, 341 genes), 104 on v2 (IMPACT410, 410 genes), 338 on v3 (IMPACT468, 468 genes); all 16 recurrently altered driver genes are covered on every panel version.
  • Variant annotation: OncoKB and Cancer Hotspots; copy-number purity/ploidy correction via FACETS; MSI calling via MSI-sensor (cutoff ≥10).
  • Data deposited and freely available on cBioPortal as study egc_mskcc_2020.

Key findings

  • 16 recurrent oncogenic drivers (≥5% prevalence) were identified; 10 were gene amplifications, affecting ERBB2, KRAS, CCNE1, MYC, CCND1, MDM2, VEGFA, EGFR, CDK6, and CCND3.
  • TP53 mutation was present in ~80% of patients in each cohort. The majority of TP53 wild-type tumors harbored alternative drivers, most commonly MDM2 amplification (24% of TP53-WT). TP53 and MDM2 alterations were mutually exclusive (p<0.001, q<0.05). Other drivers in TP53-WT tumors: CDKN2A (23%), ARID1A (17%), ERBB2 amplification (16%).
  • PIT vs. CIT enrichment: ERBB2 amplification 30% (PIT) vs. 13% (CIT), p<0.001, q<0.05; Cell Cycle pathway 56% vs. 40%, p<0.001, q<0.05; RTK-RAS pathway 65% vs. 46%, p<0.001, q<0.05.
  • Genomic-instability metrics higher in PIT cohort: median fraction of genome altered 0.50 (IQR 0.38–0.58) vs. 0.40 (IQR 0.23–0.53), p<0.001; whole-genome doubling rate 46% vs. 29%, p<0.001; number of pathways altered (median 3 vs. 2, p<0.001); number of oncogenic drivers (median 3 vs. 2, p=0.001). All remained significant after adjusting for tumor purity and tissue site.
  • Median tumor mutation burden: 4.5 mutations/Mb overall; did not differ by treatment cohort but was negatively correlated with fraction of genome altered (Spearman ρ=0.113, p=0.017).
  • MSI-high prevalence: 15/487 (3.1%), consistent across cohorts; these 15 patients were excluded from survival analyses because of their known response to immune checkpoint inhibition.
  • Median OS overall: 31.6 months (95% CI 27.9–36.0); CIT cohort 42.5 months (95% CI 35.6–48.4) vs. PIT cohort 22.8 months (95% CI 19.3–27.1), p<0.001. Median follow-up: 39.2 months.
  • Multivariable Cox model (adjusting for age, clinical stage, tumor grade, treatment intent): independent predictors of worse OS were palliative treatment intent (HR 2.63, 95% CI 1.53–4.51; p<0.001), poor tumor differentiation (HR 1.54, 1.18–2.01; p=0.002), KRAS amplification (HR 1.83, 1.31–2.55; p<0.001), SMAD4 alteration (HR 1.61, 1.14–2.28; p=0.007), CDKN2A alteration (HR 1.50, 1.14–1.97; p=0.004), and number of oncogenic drivers (HR 1.11, 1.01–1.22; p=0.030).
  • ERBB2 amplification was independently associated with improved OS (HR 0.65, 95% CI 0.48–0.90; p=0.009), presumably reflecting trastuzumab therapy.
  • Pathway-level multivariable analysis: Cell Cycle pathway (HR 1.30, 1.02–1.66; p=0.036) and TGFβ pathway (HR 1.46, 1.05–2.02; p=0.024) were independently associated with worse OS.
  • Adding genomic alterations to the clinicopathologic model improved prognostic discrimination: Harrell’s C-index rose from 0.68 to 0.71.
  • Neither TP53 alteration status nor pathway-wild-type status was associated with OS.

Genes & alterations

  • TP53 — oncogenic driver mutation in ~80% of cases in both cohorts; not associated with OS in this analysis.
  • ERBB2 — amplification in 108/487 tumors; enriched in PIT (30%) vs. CIT (13%) cohort (p<0.001); 81% of ERBB2-amplified tumors confirmed Her2+ by IHC or FISH; 86/108 received trastuzumab. Associated with longer OS (adjusted HR 0.62, p=0.003 univariable; HR 0.65 multivariable, p=0.009) and well/moderate tumor differentiation (38% vs. 11%, p<0.001). Trended toward mutual exclusivity with MDM2 and other RTK-RAS-PIK3 alterations.
  • KRAS — amplification associated with significantly worse OS (KM p<0.001; adjusted HR 2.05, 1.48–2.85, p<0.001; multivariable HR 1.83, 1.31–2.55, p<0.001). Consistent with prior reports of KRAS amplification as a prognostic marker in a Japanese EAC cohort (Wong et al., Nat Med 2018).
  • CDKN2A — alterations associated with worse OS (KM p<0.001; adjusted HR 1.65; multivariable HR 1.50, 1.14–1.97, p=0.004). CDKN2A deletions alone were not prognostic on univariable analysis.
  • SMAD4 — alterations associated with worse OS (KM p=0.028; multivariable HR 1.61, 1.14–2.28, p=0.007). Consistent with prior reports linking SMAD4 loss to disease recurrence in EAC.
  • MDM2 — amplification in 24% of TP53-wild-type tumors; mutually exclusive with TP53 mutation (p<0.001, q<0.05).
  • ARID1A — alteration in 17% of TP53-wild-type tumors.
  • CCNE1, CCND1, CDKN2B — major contributors to Cell Cycle pathway enrichment in PIT cohort.
  • MYC, VEGFA, EGFR, CDK6, CCND3 — additional amplification-driven recurrent drivers (≥5%) without independently demonstrated OS association in this cohort.

Clinical implications

  • Prognostic biomarkers (CDKN2A, KRAS amplification, SMAD4): the authors propose that these three alterations identify EAC patients at higher risk of death independent of clinical stage, grade, age, and treatment intent. They argue this supports the case for broad-panel NGS at diagnosis regardless of clinical stage, to inform prognosis and treatment selection.
  • ERBB2 amplification as a positive prognostic factor is largely attributable to availability of trastuzumab (FDA-approved in HER2-amplified metastatic EAC). 86 of the 108 ERBB2-amplified patients received trastuzumab (64 first-line, 22 second-line); 79/86 had stage IV disease. The authors note their study could not fully separate the trastuzumab effect from intrinsic ERBB2-amplified biology, and trended mutual exclusivity with MDM2 and RTK-RAS-PIK3 (known mediators of chemo/trastuzumab resistance) may also contribute.
  • Pathway-level signal: the Cell Cycle and TGFβ pathways are independently prognostic of worse OS; the Cell Cycle pathway is also enriched in PIT-treated (advanced) patients, reinforcing a chromosomal-instability/advanced-disease axis.
  • Genome-instability burden (fraction of genome altered, whole-genome doubling, number of oncogenic drivers) tracks with more advanced disease and may contribute to treatment resistance and dismal prognosis in metastatic EAC.
  • MSI-high cases (3.1%) were rare and excluded from survival modeling; the authors note these patients are generally candidates for immune checkpoint inhibition.

Limitations & open questions

  • Single-institution retrospective cohort (MSK), prospective NGS but retrospective survival analysis; lacks external validation cohort — “no other large-scale clinical or genomic cohorts are available for further validation of our results.”
  • Panel-based sequencing has reduced sensitivity for deletions at low tumor purity; the authors specifically note this may dampen the apparent prognostic effect of CDKN2A and SMAD4 deletions (neither was prognostic on univariable analysis when restricted to deletions alone).
  • Trastuzumab effect on ERBB2-amplified survival is confounded with biology: because 86/108 ERBB2-amplified patients received trastuzumab, the survival benefit cannot be cleanly attributed to either the alteration or the therapy. ERBB2 amplification’s association with better tumor differentiation and mutual exclusivity with MDM2/RTK-RAS-PIK3 alterations also complicates interpretation.
  • 191/487 patients had received some prior systemic therapy or radiotherapy before sequencing (84 PIT, 107 CIT); the authors report no significant differences in tumor purity or driver counts between pre-treated and treatment-naive patients, but residual treatment-selection effects on the genomic landscape cannot be excluded.
  • MSI-high cases (N=15) were excluded from survival modeling, leaving the prognostic interpretation specific to chromosomally-unstable EAC.
  • OS was the only modeled outcome; disease-free survival, response to specific regimens, or recurrence dynamics are not addressed.
  • Whole-genome doubling status was unknown for 27 patients (24 CIT, 3 PIT).
