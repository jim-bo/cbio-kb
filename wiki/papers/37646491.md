**PMID:** 37646491 · **DOI:** [10.1002/mp.16704](https://doi.org/10.1002/mp.16704) · **Journal:** Medical Physics (2024)

## TL;DR

Peng et al. develop a conditional denoising diffusion probabilistic model (DDPM) for translating cone-beam CT (CBCT) images into synthetic CT (sCT) images with planning-CT-like Hounsfield-unit (HU) accuracy, with the goal of enabling CBCT-based online adaptive radiotherapy (ART). The model was trained and tested on paired CBCT and deformably registered planning CT (dpCT) slices from 41 brain and 47 head-and-neck (H&N) radiotherapy patients, and outperformed four other diffusion-based image-translation strategies and a Cycle GAN baseline on MAE, PSNR, and NCC. This is a radiotherapy imaging / medical-physics methods paper and contains **no cancer genomics, no patient-level molecular data, and no cBioPortal-relevant entities** — it is included here only because the source PDF was part of the ingested corpus.

## Cohort & data

- **41 brain** radiotherapy patients (30 train / 11 test; 4,682 training slices, 500 random testing slices) and **47 head-and-neck (H&N)** radiotherapy patients (37 train / 10 test; 4,314 training slices, 500 testing slices).
- CT scans acquired on a Siemens SOMATOM Definition AS (120 kVp); CBCT scans acquired on a Varian TrueBeam (100 kVp). Voxels 1.0 × 1.0 × 1.0 mm³ for both modalities.
- Planning CT was deformably registered and resampled to match the corresponding CBCT (Velocity AI 3.2.1); body contours from CBCT and CT were merged and applied to both. Images cropped to 256 × 256 and normalized to [−1, 1].
- No cancer-type, histology, stage, biomarker, or molecular metadata are reported.

## Key findings

- **Brain study**: proposed sCT achieved MAE = 25.99 HU, PSNR = 30.49 dB, NCC = 0.99 vs. CBCT MAE = 40.63 HU, PSNR = 27.87 dB, NCC = 0.98.
- **H&N study**: proposed sCT achieved MAE = 32.56 HU, PSNR = 27.65 dB, NCC = 0.98 vs. CBCT MAE = 38.99 HU, PSNR = 27.00 dB, NCC = 0.98.
- Averaging 10 stochastic reverse-process samples per slice further improved MAE and PSNR (brain: 22.31 HU / 31.71 dB; H&N: 25.99 HU / 28.63 dB in Table 1).
- The proposed conditional DDPM (CBCT concatenated channel-wise with the noisy sample as static guidance at every reverse step) outperformed two unconditionally-trained conditional-sampling variants (Uncond-1 / ILVR, Uncond-2), two adaptively-noised conditional variants (Adap-Cond-1, Adap-Cond-2), and a supervised Cycle GAN baseline on all three metrics, in both anatomical sites.
- Beam-hardening artifacts in the brain study and streaking / metal artifacts in the H&N study were visibly suppressed on sCT while fine structures (e.g., maxillary sinus, contrast agent uptake) were preserved.
- Notably, in extremely metal-artifact-corrupted H&N slices the proposed sCT was **less artifact-corrupted than the dpCT training target**, suggesting the diffusion model is tolerant to inexact paired data — a property the authors flag as relevant to image-translation tasks where exact pairs are impossible.
- Training took ~50 h for 1 × 10⁶ iterations on a 24 [GB](../cancer_types/GB.md) Nvidia RTX 3090; inference took ~2 min per slice.

## Clinical implications

- **Adaptive radiotherapy workflow**: improved HU fidelity on CBCT-derived sCT could enable direct CBCT-based dose calculation and online organ segmentation, removing dependence on operator-driven deformable registration of planning CT.
- The authors caution that the ~2 min per-slice inference time is the major obstacle to clinical online ART, and explicitly defer acceleration (knowledge distillation, DDIM, DPM-solver) and 3D patch-based extensions to future work.
- No prognostic, predictive-biomarker, or drug-response claims are made.

## Limitations & open questions

- 2D slice-based model; no 3D / volumetric coherence guarantees. Authors flag 3D patch-based extension as future work.
- Stochastic sampling means each generation is non-deterministic; the paper mitigates by averaging 10 samples, but does not quantify clinical impact of remaining variance.
- Quantitative metrics use dpCT as ground truth, which is itself inexact relative to the true patient anatomy at CBCT acquisition time — the authors explicitly note that the sCT may in some cases be closer to an artifact-free ground truth than the reference dpCT, so the MAE/PSNR/NCC numbers under-state model quality in those regimes.
- No reader study by physicians to evaluate the clinical impact of potential hallucinations vs. residual artifacts — the authors call this out as needed future work.
- Single-institution data (Emory); no external validation cohort.
- Anatomic sites limited to brain and H&N; pelvis / thorax / abdomen (where CBCT artifacts are typically worst) not evaluated.
