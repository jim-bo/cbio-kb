# Patient-derived xenograft (PDX)

## Overview

Patient-derived xenografts (PDXs) are established by implanting fresh human tumor tissue or cells into immunocompromised mice. PDX models maintain the genomic complexity and histologic heterogeneity of the donor tumor and are used to test drug efficacy, identify biomarkers of response, and study tumor biology in vivo.

## Used by

- [PMID:36611031](../papers/36611031.md) — three CAC PDXs used to test Wnt pathway inhibitors in vivo; pharmacologic inhibition with porcupine inhibitor LGK-974 and tankyrase inhibitor G007-LK did not suppress tumor growth, confirming functional Wnt independence in CAC; a separate [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) R132-mutant ileal CAC PDX confirmed 2-HG production and showed sensitivity to selective IDH1 and FGFR inhibitors in vivo [PMID:36611031](../papers/36611031.md).
