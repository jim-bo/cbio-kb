# MPL

## Overview

MPL encodes the thrombopoietin receptor (TPOR/CD110), a cytokine receptor that activates JAK-STAT signaling in hematopoietic progenitor cells. Activating mutations in MPL — most commonly W515L/K — are found in JAK2-negative myeloproliferative neoplasms (MPNs), particularly essential thrombocythemia ([ET](../cancer_types/ET.md)) and primary myelofibrosis ([MF](../cancer_types/MF.md)). MPL, [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md), and [CALR](../genes/CALR.md) mutations are mutually exclusive, together accounting for the driver in the vast majority of [MPN](../cancer_types/MPN.md) patients.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Activating mutations in 7 MPN patients (all ET or MF) with unmutated JAK2; strictly mutually exclusive with CALR and JAK2 mutations in a large WES cohort of 197 MPN patients [PMID:24325359](../papers/24325359.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Essential thrombocythemia (ET) — activating MPL mutations present in JAK2/CALR-negative cases [PMID:24325359](../papers/24325359.md)
- Primary myelofibrosis (MF) — activating MPL mutations present in JAK2/CALR-negative cases [PMID:24325359](../papers/24325359.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Mutually exclusive with JAK2 (V617F) and CALR (exon 9 indels); combined JAK2+MPL+CALR testing captures a driver in 146/151 (97%) of MPN patients [PMID:24325359](../papers/24325359.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- JAK1/2 inhibitors ([ruxolitinib](../drugs/ruxolitinib.md)) used in MF and ET target the downstream JAK-STAT pathway shared by MPL, JAK2, and CALR-mutant disease.

## Open questions

- The precise distribution of MPL mutation subtypes (W515L vs W515K vs others) was not detailed in the WES cohort [PMID:24325359](../papers/24325359.md)
