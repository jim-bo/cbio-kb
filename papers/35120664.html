TL;DR

Nguyen et al. assembled MSK-MET, a pan-cancer cohort of 25,775 patients (15,632 primary and 10,143 metastatic specimens) spanning 50 tumor types, all profiled with MSK-IMPACT targeted sequencing and linked to 99,419 EHR-derived metastatic events across 21 anatomic sites. By comparing primary vs. metastatic tumors, primary tumors from metastatic vs. non-metastatic patients, and metastases by organ site, they showed that fraction of genome altered (FGA, a proxy for chromosomal instability) correlates with metastatic burden in some lineages (notably PRAD, LUAD, HR+/HER2+ ductal breast) but not in others (COAD, HGSOC), and identified driver-level associations between specific somatic alterations and target-organ metastasis (PMID:35120664).

Cohort & data

  • 25,775 patients profiled by MSK-IMPACT (341/410/468-gene panels: IMPACT341, IMPACT410, IMPACT468) between 2013-11-18 and 2020-01-06; 15,632 (61%) primary and 10,143 (39%) metastatic specimens across 50 tumor types (PMID:35120664).
  • Median sequencing coverage 653x (IQR 525–790); median tumor purity 40% (IQR 20–50%); median sample-to-sequencing interval 62 days (PMID:35120664).
  • Most common metastatic biopsy sites: lymph nodes (n=2,305, 23%), liver (n=2,289, 23%), lung (n=982, 10%), bone (n=726, 7%) (PMID:35120664).
  • 99,419 metastatic events from 21,546 metastatic patients extracted from electronic health records and mapped to 21 reference anatomic locations (PMID:35120664).
  • Median age at sequencing 64y; median follow-up 30 months; five-year survival 40% (range: 90% testicular seminoma to 10% pancreatic adenocarcinoma) (PMID:35120664).
  • Allele-specific copy-number analysis on a subset (n=17,224) used FACETS to derive purity/ploidy-adjusted FGA, whole-genome doubling (WGD) status, and clonality estimates (PMID:35120664).
  • Independent validation cohort: 9,215 MSK-IMPACT samples sequenced 2020-01-07 to 2021-08-18 (PMID:35120664).
  • Dataset publicly available as msk_met_2021 on cBioPortal and Zenodo (DOI 10.5281/zenodo.5801902) (PMID:35120664).

Key findings

  • Metastases are more chromosomally unstable than primaries in 16 of 50 tumor types by FGA; differences confirmed in 11 tumor types using FACETS-adjusted FGA (PMID:35120664).
  • Whole-genome doubling is more frequent in metastases in 7 tumor types; uterine endometrioid showed the most striking shift (WGD 4% primary vs 16% metastatic; CIN 0% vs 14%) (PMID:35120664).
  • TMB is higher in metastases in 10 tumor types; TMB-high (>=10 mut/Mb) rates rose from 19% to 27% in LUAD, 2% to 7% in HR+/HER2- ductal breast cancer, and 5% to 19% in lobular breast cancer (all q<0.001) (PMID:35120664).
  • Clonality: metastases from 12 tumor types had a significantly higher fraction of clonal mutations, consistent with an evolutionary bottleneck (PMID:35120664).
  • 67 statistically significant oncogenic-alteration frequency differences between primaries and metastases across 17 tumor types; 53 enriched in metastases vs only 14 in primaries (PMID:35120664).
  • FGA correlates with metastatic burden pan-cancer and in 11 individual tumor types; the strongest association was prostate adenocarcinoma (rho=0.33, q=7.0E-45). No association in MSS COAD where CIN is already high at low metastatic burden (PMID:35120664).
  • TMB does not correlate with metastatic burden pan-cancer; positive in 3 tumor types, negative in endometrioid and hypermutated uterine cancer (PMID:35120664).
  • 24 oncogenic-alteration and 16 pathway associations with metastatic burden identified across 8 and 7 tumor types respectively; most enriched in high-burden patients (PMID:35120664).
  • Organ-specific metastasis genomics: 57 oncogenic alterations and 48 oncogenic-pathway associations linked to specific organotropisms across 10 and 12 tumor types (PMID:35120664).
  • Higher metastatic burden was associated with shorter overall survival in 39 of 50 (78%) tumor types (PMID:35120664).

Genes & alterations

  • TP53 — mutation enriched in metastases in 7 tumor types (LUAD, PRAD, HR+/HER2- ductal breast, MSS COAD, lobular breast, pancreatic neuroendocrine, endometrioid uterine); inverse in HNSC (62% primary vs 45% metastatic, q=0.01). Positively correlated with metastatic burden in PRAD, LUAD, and HR+/HER2- ductal breast. More frequent in LUAD CNS/brain mets (55% vs 66%, q=0.002) and in PAAD liver mets (63% vs 84%, q<0.001) (PMID:35120664).
  • CDKN2A — deletion enriched in metastases in 5 tumor types; in LUAD more frequent in skin (16% vs 60%, q=0.03) and liver mets (16% vs 27%, q=0.03) but less frequent in lymph node mets (19% vs 12%, q=0.002). Deletion frequency correlated with metastatic burden in bladder urothelial, LUAD, and papillary thyroid cancer. CDKN2A mutations less frequent in primary PAAD of metastatic patients (17% vs 7%, q=0.02) (PMID:35120664).
  • PTEN — mutation and deletion enriched in metastases in 4 tumor types. Mutation more frequent in SKCM brain metastases (7% vs 23%, q=0.01) and in SKCM patients with brain metastasis (7% vs 14%, q=0.04). PTEN deletion more frequent in PRAD bone mets (9% vs 19%, q<0.001); PTEN loss more frequent in PRAD liver mets (11% vs 30%, q<0.001) (PMID:35120664).
  • MYC — amplification enriched in metastases in 4 tumor types; amplification frequency correlates with metastatic burden in LUAD and PRAD. More frequent in primaries of metastatic patients in PRAD (1% vs 4%, q=0.03), MSS COAD (1% vs 4%, q=0.03), and triple-negative ductal breast (3% vs 17%, q=0.03) (PMID:35120664).
  • AR — amplification (1% vs 30%) and mutations (0% vs 6%) dramatically more frequent in PRAD metastases (both q<0.001); AR amplification positively associated with metastatic burden in metastatic PRAD; enriched in bone (5% vs 21%, q<0.001) and brain (14% vs 34%, q<0.001) PRAD metastases. Attributed to androgen-deprivation therapy selection pressure (PMID:35120664).
  • ESR1 — mutations enriched in HR+/HER2- ductal breast (2% vs 19%, q<0.001), lobular breast (2% vs 13%, q<0.001), and endometrioid uterine (3% vs 10%, q=0.002) metastases; correlate with metastatic burden in HR+/HER2- ductal and lobular breast; enriched in liver metastases of HR+/HER2- ductal breast (5% vs 16%, q<0.001 burden-associated; 79% vs 58% organ-specific, q=0.003). Attributed to aromatase-inhibitor selection pressure (PMID:35120664).
  • TERT — promoter mutations more frequent in papillary thyroid metastases (46% vs 69%, q=0.001) and SKCM metastases (70% vs 81%, q=0.02), but more frequent in primary HNSC (41% vs 25%, q=0.02). TERT promoter mutations more frequent in primary tumors of metastatic papillary thyroid patients (20% vs 56%, q=0.004). TERT amplification associated with LUAD CNS/brain metastasis (PMID:35120664).
  • ALK — fusions slightly more frequent in LUAD metastases (3% vs 6%, q<0.001) (PMID:35120664).
  • KRAS — mutations more frequent in pancreatic neuroendocrine metastases (1% vs 10%, q=0.03); 12p gain (encompassing KRAS) more frequent in PAAD metastases (17% vs 4%, q=0.002). MSS COAD patients with lung metastasis had higher KRAS mutation frequency (39% vs 52%, q<0.001) (PMID:35120664).
  • EGFR — mutations enriched in LUAD patients with CNS/brain metastasis (PMID:35120664).
  • RBM10 — mutations less frequent in LUAD CNS/brain metastasis (PMID:35120664).
  • FOXA1 — mutations negatively correlated with metastatic burden in PRAD; enriched in lobular breast cancer liver metastases (3% vs 33%, q=0.02) (PMID:35120664).
  • CBFB — mutations negatively correlated with metastatic burden in HR+/HER2- ductal breast; less frequent in bone metastases (5% vs 1%, q=0.009) (PMID:35120664).
  • CDH1 — mutations negatively correlated with metastatic burden in lobular breast cancer (PMID:35120664).
  • ERCC2 — mutations negatively correlated with metastatic burden in urothelial bladder cancer (PMID:35120664).
  • RHOA — mutations enriched in lobular breast ovarian metastasis (3% vs 36%, q=0.02) (PMID:35120664).
  • ERG — fusions less frequent in PRAD bone metastases (30% vs 15%, q=0.002; secondary analysis 29% vs 24%, q=0.04) (PMID:35120664).
  • NF1 — mutations more frequent in SKCM lung metastases (20% vs 38%, q=0.003) (PMID:35120664).
  • FGFR3 — mutations more frequent in bladder urothelial lung metastases (11% vs 39%, q=0.005) (PMID:35120664).
  • SPOP — mutations less frequent in primary PRAD from metastatic patients (18% vs 12%, q=0.03); previously reported pattern of decreased metastatic potential (PMID:35120664).
  • PIK3CA — mutations less frequent in primaries of HR+/HER2- ductal breast metastatic patients (49% vs 38%, q=0.003); PI3K pathway alterations enriched in HR+/HER2- ductal breast bone metastases (44% vs 56%, q=0.003) (PMID:35120664).
  • RB1 — loss more frequent in PRAD liver metastases (3% vs 10%, q<0.001) (PMID:35120664).
  • APC — mutations more frequent in PRAD liver (5% vs 11%, q=0.001) and lung (5% vs 12%, q<0.001) metastases (PMID:35120664).
  • CTNNB1 — mutations more frequent in PRAD lung metastases (3% vs 8%, q=0.007); consistent with WNT pathway role in PRAD lung tropism (WNT pathway alterations in 26% of lung-mets PRAD vs 13%, q<0.001) (PMID:35120664).
  • BRAF — mutations less frequent in papillary thyroid bone metastasis patients (73% vs 53%, q=0.02) (PMID:35120664).
  • ERBB2 — amplification more frequent in esophageal cancer patients with lung metastasis (16% vs 37%, q<0.001) (PMID:35120664).
  • MAP3K1 — mutations less frequent in HR+/HER2- ductal breast brain-metastasis patients (9% vs 3%, q=0.02); proposed surrogate for less-aggressive luminal A subtype (PMID:35120664).
  • SRC — amplification less frequent in MSS COAD lung metastases (7% vs 3%, q<0.001) (PMID:35120664).

Clinical implications

  • Hormone-therapy resistance signatures are written into metastatic genomes. The strongest primary-vs-metastatic enrichments were AR amplifications/mutations in PRAD and ESR1 mutations in HR+/HER2- breast cancer — both consistent with positive selection under androgen-deprivation and aromatase-inhibitor therapy respectively (PMID:35120664).
  • Chromosomal instability is a candidate therapeutic vulnerability in lineages where it tracks with metastatic burden (PRAD, LUAD, HR+/HER2+ ductal breast). The authors propose drugs targeting CIN as an attractive strategy in these tumor types (PMID:35120664).
  • Organotropism-genotype associations generate biomarker hypotheses: e.g., EGFR/TP53/TERT-amp in LUAD brain mets; WNT pathway/CTNNB1/APC in PRAD lung mets (with tankyrase-inhibition vulnerability cited from Leibold et al. 2020); FGFR3 in bladder lung mets; ERBB2 amp in esophageal lung mets (PMID:35120664).
  • Metastatic burden is itself a prognostic axis: higher burden tracked with shorter overall survival in 78% of tumor types studied (PMID:35120664).
  • TMB-high frequency rises with metastatic progression in LUAD and breast cancer, potentially affecting checkpoint-inhibitor eligibility downstream (PMID:35120664).
  • ALK fusions slightly enriched in LUAD metastases — implication noted for ALK-inhibitor biomarker testing on metastatic biopsies (PMID:35120664).

Limitations & open questions

  • Sample-size imbalance across 50 tumor types prevents robust conclusions in rarer cancers; non-significant differences may reflect lack of statistical power (PMID:35120664).
  • Tumor-type definitions are coarse — e.g., LUAD histologic subtypes are not resolved; refinement would further reduce per-group sample size (PMID:35120664).
  • ICD-billing-code-derived metastatic events likely under-capture events and are subject to inter-physician variability; NLP/ML on EHR text is an obvious next step (PMID:35120664).
  • Targeted-panel limitation: MSK-IMPACT covers 341–468 genes; whole-exome or whole-genome sequencing might surface additional signals (e.g., structural variants, non-coding drivers) (PMID:35120664).
  • Single representative sample per patient — longitudinal/multi-site sampling needed to investigate timing of genomic events and inter-metastasis heterogeneity (PMID:35120664).
  • No integration of treatment history, microenvironment, or systemic factors at the per-event level; precludes causal claims about drug-driven vs lineage-intrinsic enrichment for many alterations (PMID:35120664).
  • Open question on CIN directionality: in tumor types where copy-number patterns appear set early (e.g., COAD, HGSOC), CIN does not stratify metastatic burden — when and how this differs mechanistically from CIN-burden-coupled lineages remains unresolved (PMID:35120664).
