**PMID:** 24265153 · **DOI:** [10.1158/2159-8290.CD-13-0617](https://doi.org/10.1158/2159-8290.CD-13-0617) · **Journal:** Cancer Discovery (2014)

## TL;DR

Van Allen, Wagle, and colleagues performed whole-exome sequencing on FFPE tumors from 45 patients with [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600 metastatic [cutaneous melanoma](../cancer_types/SKCM.md) treated with single-agent [vemurafenib](../drugs/vemurafenib.md) or [dabrafenib](../drugs/dabrafenib.md) (the DeCOG cohort, [skcm_broad_brafresist_2012](../datasets/skcm_broad_brafresist_2012.md)). Genetic alterations in known or putative RAF inhibitor resistance genes were observed in 23 of 45 patients (51%). Beyond previously characterized [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md), [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) amplification, and [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) events, they discovered a "long tail" of new MAPK-pathway alterations — most notably [MAP2K2](../genes/MAP2K2.md) (MEK2) mutations and [MITF](../genes/MITF.md) amplification — that experimentally confer resistance to RAF/MEK inhibition. Three tumors carried multiple co-occurring resistance mechanisms within the same biopsy.

## Cohort & data

- **45 patients** with [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600 metastatic [cutaneous melanoma](../cancer_types/SKCM.md) treated with single-agent [vemurafenib](../drugs/vemurafenib.md) or [dabrafenib](../drugs/dabrafenib.md) from the Dermatologic Cooperative Oncology Group of Germany (DeCOG); cBioPortal dataset [skcm_broad_brafresist_2012](../datasets/skcm_broad_brafresist_2012.md).
- 14/45 (31%) had early resistance (progression in <12 weeks); 31/45 (68.9%) developed acquired resistance.
- FFPE-derived tumor DNA underwent [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) on Illumina HiSeq, with mean target coverage of 200X (tumor) and 92X (germline).
- Somatic SNVs called with [MuTect](../methods/mutect.md), indels with [Indelocator](../methods/indelocator.md), annotation with [Oncotator](../methods/oncotator.md); alignments against hg19 via the Broad Picard/Firehose pipeline.
- For 32 patients matched pre-treatment and post-resistance tumors were available; the rest contributed either pre-treatment (rapid progressors) or resistant biopsies (clinical responders).
- Functional validation used A375, SKMEL19, UACC62, A2058, and WM266.4 [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E melanoma cell lines treated with [dabrafenib](../drugs/dabrafenib.md), [trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md), [selumetinib](../drugs/selumetinib.md), PLX4720 (RAF tool compound), [GDC-0941](../drugs/gdc-0941.md) (PI3K inhibitor), and VRT11E (ERK tool compound).

## Key findings

- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600 mutations were detected in 100% of pre-treatment biopsies; 44/45 missense at codon V600, plus one in-frame deletion (Val600_Lys601delinsGlu in Patient 11) predicted to phenocopy V600E.
- Resistance alterations were enriched in MAPK-pathway genes ([NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) amplification, [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md), [MAP2K2](../genes/MAP2K2.md), [MITF](../genes/MITF.md), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md)), accounting for 44.4% (20/45) of the cohort.
- Recurrent classes by frequency:
  - [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) somatic mutations: 17.8% (seven Q61, one T58); acquired [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) mutations occurred exclusively in patients on therapy >12 weeks (P = 0.04).
  - [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md) mutations: 15.6%; not all preclude clinical benefit.
  - [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) amplification: 8.9%.
- **MEK2 ([MAP2K2](../genes/MAP2K2.md)) is a new resistance gene.** Four [MAP2K2](../genes/MAP2K2.md) mutations (V35M, L46F, C125S, N126D) identified in resistant tumors. When introduced into A375 cells, all four conferred resistance to both [dabrafenib](../drugs/dabrafenib.md) (RAF) and [trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md) (MEK), with MEK2 C125S — homolog of the previously known MEK1 C121S — yielding >100-fold change in GI50; all four mutants remained sensitive to ERK inhibition (VRT11E) and sustained MEK/ERK phosphorylation under RAF-inhibitor treatment.
- **[MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md) mutations span pre-treatment and resistant tumors.** Five MEK1 mutations identified in resistant or rapidly-progressing samples (V60E, G128V, V154I, P124S, P124L) and two (G276W, F53Y) in pre-treatment tumors of patients with clinical benefit. All tested MEK1 mutants, when doxycycline-induced in A375, produced 10–80-fold resistance to [dabrafenib](../drugs/dabrafenib.md) and 3–20-fold resistance to [trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md) versus wild-type MEK1, but no effect on ERK-inhibitor (VRT11E) sensitivity.
- **[MITF](../genes/MITF.md) amplification is a transcriptional resistance mechanism.** Focal relapse-specific [MITF](../genes/MITF.md) amplification was identified in one patient lacking other known resistance lesions. Overexpression of wild-type [MITF](../genes/MITF.md) (but not the DNA-binding-impaired R217Δ mutant) in WM266.4, SKMEL19, and UACC62 cells produced 30–80-fold increases in PLX4720 GI50 and cross-resistance to RAF, MEK, and ERK inhibitors.
- **Intra-tumor heterogeneity of resistance mechanisms.** Three patients had multiple resistance alterations within the same post-progression biopsy:
  - Patient 41: [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) Q61R + [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) T58I (on mutually exclusive reads) plus a [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md) mutation.
  - Patient 08: acquired [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) Q61K + focal [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) amplification.
  - Patient 02: [MAP2K2](../genes/MAP2K2.md) mutation + [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) amplification.
- **PI3K-pathway alterations are common but contextual.** [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) missense mutations (9/11 in the phosphatase domain), a [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) deletion (Patient 36), a [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) H1047R acquired in a resistant tumor (Patient 48), and [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md) variants were observed. PI3K alterations did not always preclude initial response (Patient 1: pre-treatment [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) H93D, 66-week partial response). In vitro, combined PLX4720 + [GDC-0941](../drugs/gdc-0941.md) markedly suppressed proliferation and induced apoptosis only in [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E / [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md)-null A2058 cells, not in A375 ([BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E / [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) WT).
- **[RAC1](../genes/RAC1.md) P29S marks early resistance.** Pre-treatment [RAC1](../genes/RAC1.md) P29S in 3/14 early-resistance patients (Patients 26, 34, 46) and 0 patients with sustained response (P = 0.026).
- **[HOXD8](../genes/HOXD8.md) nonsense mutation** in one early-resistance tumor, consistent with prior RNAi-screen evidence that [HOXD8](../genes/HOXD8.md) suppression promotes RAF-inhibitor resistance.

## Genes & alterations

- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — V600 missense (44/45 pre-treatment), one Val600_Lys601delinsGlu (Patient 11); focal amplification in 8.9% of resistant tumors (e.g., Patients 02, 08).
- [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) — Q61R/K/H, T58I, Q60H mutations in 17.8% of resistant tumors; acquired [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) restricted to >12-week responders (P = 0.04).
- [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md) (MEK1) — V60E, G128V, V154I, P124S, P124L (resistance/early-progression) plus G276W, F53Y (pre-treatment with clinical benefit). All experimentally validated mutants conferred 10–80-fold [dabrafenib](../drugs/dabrafenib.md) and 3–20-fold [trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md) resistance in A375.
- [MAP2K2](../genes/MAP2K2.md) (MEK2) — V35M, L46F, C125S, N126D; novel RAF-inhibitor resistance gene reported by this paper. C125S confers >100-fold cross-resistance to RAF and MEK inhibition.
- [MITF](../genes/MITF.md) — relapse-associated focal amplification (Patient 58 background); overexpression confers pan-MAPK-inhibitor cross-resistance (30–80-fold) in WM266.4/SKMEL19/UACC62.
- [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) — included among MAPK-pathway resistance gene set (44.4% aggregate).
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — missense mutations (11 patients; 9 in phosphatase domain), nonsense R233*, frameshift Y86fs, and a copy-number deletion (Patient 36); combination of RAF + PI3K inhibition synergistic in [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md)-null A2058 cells.
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — acquired H1047R in Patient 48's resistant tumor (oncogenic catalytic-subunit hotspot).
- [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md) — variants of uncertain significance in resistant tumors.
- [RAC1](../genes/RAC1.md) — recurrent P29S in 3/14 pre-treatment tumors of early-resistance patients (P = 0.026); known gain-of-function melanoma oncogene.
- [HOXD8](../genes/HOXD8.md) — nonsense mutation in one early-resistance tumor; candidate transcriptional-effector resistance gene.

## Clinical implications

- Cross-resistance between RAF and MEK inhibitors (e.g., [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md), [MAP2K2](../genes/MAP2K2.md), [MITF](../genes/MITF.md) lesions) implies combined RAF/MEK regimens may have limited durability in this subset; ERK inhibitors remain experimentally effective against MEK1/MEK2-mutant cells in vitro, motivating clinical evaluation of ERK-pathway inhibitors.
- Patients with acquired [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) mutations or [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) amplification may respond to subsequent MEK-inhibitor regimens, though existing data suggest reduced benefit after RAF-inhibitor progression.
- [MITF](../genes/MITF.md)-driven and other transcriptional-effector resistance mechanisms (potentially [HOXD8](../genes/HOXD8.md)) may confer pan-MAPK-pathway resistance and require non-MAPK therapeutic approaches.
- Combined MAPK + PI3K inhibition merits clinical evaluation in [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)-mutant melanomas with concurrent PI3K-pathway lesions, particularly [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) loss; in [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md)-WT contexts the combination's value is unclear.
- Pre-treatment [RAC1](../genes/RAC1.md) P29S genotyping may identify patients at higher risk of early progression on single-agent RAF inhibitors.
- Demonstrates feasibility of FFPE-based whole-exome sequencing for systematic resistance profiling on multicenter clinical cohorts.

## Limitations & open questions

- Whole-exome sequencing cannot detect non-genetic resistance mechanisms ([BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) alternative splicing, COT/MAP3K8 upregulation, RTK/ligand overexpression, [HGF](../genes/HGF.md) secretion), so true intra-tumor resistance heterogeneity and overall resistance prevalence are underestimated.
- 13 patients lacked complete pre-treatment/post-resistance/germline trios; absent pre-treatment samples (e.g., Patients 02, 08, 36) preclude formal assignment of some alterations as acquired vs. pre-existing.
- Single-focus biopsies cannot resolve heterogeneity from pre-existing tumor variability.
- Several candidate resistance alterations ([RAC1](../genes/RAC1.md), [HOXD8](../genes/HOXD8.md), many [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) and [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md) variants) require larger cohort validation and additional functional experiments.
- The cellular contexts in which PI3K-pathway activation drives proliferative versus survival-only resistance remain unresolved; combination MAPK+PI3K therapy may benefit only a subset.
- Cohort size (45 patients) is too small to reach statistical significance for individual rare-tail resistance genes.
