# GZMK

## Overview

GZMK (Granzyme K) is a serine protease expressed primarily by cytotoxic T lymphocytes and NK cells. It plays a role in immune-mediated cytotoxicity and is a marker of T-cell activation in the tumor microenvironment. In cancer genomics, GZMK expression is notably elevated in microsatellite instability-high (MSI-H) tumors, where it reflects a robust cytotoxic immune response and is associated with potential sensitivity to checkpoint immunotherapy.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- GZMK is overexpressed in MSI-high tumors of COAD/READ, [STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md), and [UCEC](../cancer_types/UCEC.md) alongside [GZMA](../genes/GZMA.md), [PRF1](../genes/PRF1.md), and [GZMH](../genes/GZMH.md); this cytotoxic immune gene signature defines a neoantigen-high population with elevated checkpoint-blockade responsiveness in the pan-cancer MSI landscape (250/1,464 samples MSI-high across 33 TCGA tumor types) [PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **COAD/READ, STAD, UCEC:** GZMK overexpression co-occurs with MSI-high status and elevated neoantigen load; germline MMR carriers (16/18 also carrying somatic MMR hits) are MSI-high and over-express GZMK alongside other cytotoxic immune markers [PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- GZMK overexpression co-occurs with GZMA, GZMH, and PRF1 in MSI-high tumors; this expression cluster is linked to the C3 immune subtype characterized by BRAF-mutant enrichment and CD8–PD-L1–PD-1 signaling [PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Elevated GZMK/GZMA/GZMH/PRF1 expression in MSI-high tumors supports the use of PD-1/PD-L1 checkpoint blockade in MSI-H COAD/READ, STAD, and UCEC; 16/18 germline MMR carriers are MSI-high with high neoantigen load [PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)

## Open questions

- Whether GZMK expression level alone (independent of MSI status) is a predictive biomarker for checkpoint immunotherapy response across tumor types remains to be established.
