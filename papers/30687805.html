Real-Time Genomic Characterization of Metastatic Pancreatic Neuroendocrine Tumors Has Prognostic Implications and Identifies Potential Germline Actionability

Authors

Nitya Raj

Ronak Shah

Zsofia Stadler

Semanti Mukherjee

Joanne Chou

Brian Untch

Janet Li

Virginia Kelly

Muyinat Osoba

Leonard B. Saltz

PMID: 30687805 · DOI: 10.1200/PO.17.00267 · Journal: JCO Precision Oncology (2018)

TL;DR

Raj et al. prospectively sequenced 96 tumor samples from 80 patients with metastatic well-differentiated pancreatic neuroendocrine tumors (panNETs) using the MSK-IMPACT matched tumor-normal NGS platform between May 2014 and March 2017. Somatic alterations were identified in 95% of patients, dominated by chromatin-remodeling factors (DAXX, ATRX, ARID1A), histone methyltransferases (MEN1, SETD2), and mTOR-pathway genes (TSC2, PTEN). The authors document grade progression, mTOR-inhibitor resistance mutations on serial biopsy, alkylator-induced hypermutation, and a 16% rate of clinically actionable germline pathogenic variants — motivating reconsideration of universal germline testing for panNET patients (PMID:30687805).

Cohort & data

  • 80 patients / 96 samples with metastatic well-differentiated PANET; 8 patients contributed 2 samples and 4 patients contributed 3 samples for serial sequencing (PMID:30687805).
  • Cohort identifier: panet_msk_2018 (ClinicalTrials.gov NCT01775072); patients accrued between May 2014 and March 2017 at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center.
  • Baseline demographics: median age 54 (range 10–79); 56% female; all patients had metastatic disease (liver 81%, lymph nodes 33%); WHO grade distribution 30% G1, 46% G2, 24% G3; median Ki-67 10% (range 1–80%) (PMID:30687805).
  • Assay: MSK-IMPACT matched tumor-normal hybridization-capture NGS. Panel versions used: pilot 275-gene (2 samples / 2%), version 1/IMPACT341 (not used in this cohort breakdown), IMPACT410 (16 samples / 17%), IMPACT468 (57 samples / 59%), and version 4 (21 samples / 22%). Median sequencing depth 665.5× (range 75×–1255×) (PMID:30687805).
  • Germline panel: 76 cancer-susceptibility genes called from the matched normal .bam with MuTect + GATK HaplotypeCaller; ExAC <1% population-frequency cutoff; ACMG variant interpretation (PMID:30687805).
  • Loss of heterozygosity: quantified via FACETS allele-specific copy-number analysis on 95 of 96 samples.
  • Actionability: annotated with the OncoKB tier scale (levels 1–4).

Key findings

  • Overall somatic detection rate: 76 of 80 patients (95%) harbored at least one somatic alteration (PMID:30687805).
  • Top recurrently altered genes (first sample per patient): MEN1 45/80 (56%), DAXX 32/80 (40%), ATRX 20/80 (25%), TSC2 20/80 (25%), SETD2 14/80 (18%), PTEN 10/80 (13%), TP53 10/80 (13%), ARID1A 9/80 (11%), BRAF 6/80 (8%), KMT2C 7/80 (9%) (PMID:30687805).
  • Pathway frequencies: chromatin remodeling factors 52/80 (65%), histone methyltransferases 53/80 (66%), mTOR pathway 34/80 (43%), cell-cycle signaling 31/80 (39%). All 10 TP53 alterations were classified as likely oncogenic (PMID:30687805).
  • Tumor mutational burden tracks with grade: average mutations/Mb were 2.74 (G1), 4.20 (G2), and 23.64 (G3). Median TMB across the full cohort was 2.95 mut/Mb (range 0–201.76) (PMID:30687805).
  • Loss of heterozygosity is dominant: LOH affecting ≥50% of the genome was identified in 55/95 sequenced samples (58%). LOH was significantly enriched among samples carrying MEN1, DAXX, PTEN, or TSC2 alterations (q < 0.05). Recurrent LOH was observed on chromosomes 1, 2, 3, 6, 8, 10, 11, 15, 16, 21, and 22; presence of LOH trended toward improved overall survival (P = .06) (PMID:30687805).
  • Survival (median follow-up 27.6 months; 14 deaths; median OS 118.9 months, 95% CI 99.6–NR):
    • 1-year OS in MEN1-altered vs WT: 98% (95% CI 84–99%) vs 82% (63–92%), P = .01.
    • 1-year OS in DAXX- or ATRX-altered vs WT: 97% (83–99%) vs 83% (64–93%), P < .01.
    • 1-year OS in BRAF-altered vs WT: 75% (13–96%) vs 95% (87–98%), P < .01.
    • No significant OS difference by mTOR-pathway or TP53 status (PMID:30687805).
  • Novel recurrently altered genes vs prior panNET series: SETD2 (18% — predominantly truncating loss-of-function; 17 mutated samples from 14 patients, 71% G2 and 24% G3) and BRAF (8% — 10 mutated samples from 6 patients including V600E ×2 and non-V600 variants K601E, T599K, T310I, plus a compound G596D + E451K tumor). 50% of BRAF-mutated samples were G3 (PMID:30687805).
  • Serial-sample clonal evolution (12 patients sequenced at multiple time points):
    • Patient 1 (everolimus + alpelisib/BYL719): acquired oncogenic PTEN Q298* at progression; clone was present at low VAF in the pretreatment pancreas; tumor grade progressed G1→G3.
    • Patient 2 (everolimus 8 weeks): acquired RHEB hotspot Y35S in a progressing liver lesion; allele fraction rose on continued observation; G1→G3.
    • Patient 3 (everolimus >9 months): post-progression liver biopsy lost an IGF2 G226S mutation and acquired TSC2 splice (X161_splice) and AKT2 G16D — no mutations shared with the pretreatment pancreas (clinically consistent with single primary; possible unrecognized clonal sweep).
    • Patient 4 (vemurafenib for BRAF V600E): acquired NRAS Q61R at progression (PMID:30687805).
  • Alkylator-induced hypermutation: three patients re-sequenced after cytotoxic chemotherapy acquired >100 new mutations (117, 165, and 267). Two had received alkylators (dacarbazine, temozolomide) and showed a predominant C>T mutational signature and acquired mismatch-repair gene mutations; all three sampled tumors progressed G1→G3 (PMID:30687805).
  • Germline pathogenic / likely pathogenic variants in 14 / 88 patients (16%): high-penetrance — MEN1 (1), VHL (1), TSC2 (1); moderate-penetrance CHEK2 in 4 patients (5%); low-penetrance monoallelic MUTYH (4) and APC I1307K (3); plus recessive FANCC and RECQL4 alleles and a RAD50 R1093* of uncertain actionability (PMID:30687805).
  • Notable germline finding: a 32-year-old with a germline TSC2 R1200W did not meet clinical criteria for tuberous sclerosis but had brain MRI evidence of tubers and radial bands — discovery was incidental to tumor-normal NGS (PMID:30687805).
  • OncoKB-actionable somatic alterations: Level 1/2a 0%; Level 2b 19/80 (24%) — TSC2 ×16, TSC1 ×1, BRAF V600E ×2; Level 3b 4 patients (5%) — NRAS ×2, BRAF K601E, PTCH1 ×2; Level 4 14 patients (18%) — PTEN ×8, KRAS ×3, MLH1 ×3, MSH6 ×2, NRAS, MSH2, FGFR1 (each ×1) (PMID:30687805).
  • Treatment response to everolimus was not predicted by mTOR-pathway mutation status: among 12 patients with mTOR alterations who received everolimus, 17% regression / 50% stable / 8% progression; among 17 mTOR-WT patients on everolimus, 29% regression / 41% stable / 24% progression (PMID:30687805).

Genes & alterations

  • MEN1 — somatic alteration in 56% (45/80); also recurrent germline driver (1 high-penetrance carrier). Associated with significantly improved 1-year OS (98% vs 82%, P = .01).
  • DAXX / ATRX — somatic alterations in 40% and 25% respectively; together define the chromatin-remodeling / ALT axis; both associated with improved 1-year OS (97% vs 83%, P < .01) in this metastatic cohort, contrasting with prior reports in resected disease.
  • TSC2 — somatic alterations in 25% (20/80); dominant mTOR-pathway driver; one high-penetrance germline R1200W carrier presenting incidentally (no clinical TSC phenotype); acquired splice mutation in one patient at everolimus progression.
  • PTEN — somatic in 13%; acquired Q298* in one patient at everolimus + alpelisib progression.
  • RHEB — Y35S hotspot acquired at everolimus progression in one patient; allele fraction rose on continued observation.
  • AKT2 — G16D acquired post-everolimus.
  • SETD2 — 18% of patients; predominantly truncating loss-of-function; enriched in G2/G3 tumors and in post-treatment biopsies; positioned as a panNET tumor suppressor.
  • BRAF — 8% of patients; V600E plus several non-V600 alterations (K601E, T599K, T310I, compound G596D + E451K); enriched in post-treatment samples; significantly worse 1-year OS (75% vs 95%, P < .01); paired patient on vemurafenib acquired NRAS Q61R at progression.
  • TP53 — 13%; all calls classified as likely oncogenic; no independent OS effect.
  • IGF2 — G226S lost in one patient on everolimus, suggesting clonal replacement rather than direct resistance.
  • VHL, CHEK2, MUTYH, APC, FANCC, RECQL4, RAD50 — germline pathogenic / likely pathogenic carriers (high-, moderate-, low-penetrance, recessive, or uncertain classes; see Table 2 of the paper).

Clinical implications

  • Prognostic biomarkers in metastatic panNET: MEN1, DAXX, and ATRX alterations were associated with improved 1-year OS — opposite to reports in resected disease where DAXX/ATRX loss correlates with poor outcome (PMID:30687805). The authors interpret this as a metastatic-stage–specific signal that needs confirmation.
  • BRAF alterations mark aggressive, post-treatment disease with significantly worse 1-year OS (P < .01) and warrant closer surveillance.
  • Universal germline testing for panNET is reasonable: 16% pathogenic/likely-pathogenic carriers across high-, moderate-, and low-penetrance genes — including a TSC2 carrier missed by phenotype-based testing criteria — argues for genetic-risk assessment in all panNET patients (PMID:30687805).
  • Targeted-therapy resistance mechanisms are detectable in real time: acquired PTEN Q298*, RHEB Y35S, TSC2 splice, and AKT2 G16D after everolimus; NRAS Q61R after vemurafenib — supporting serial NGS on disease progression.
  • mTOR mutation status did not predict everolimus benefit (response rates 17% vs 29%); the authors caution against using mTOR-pathway mutations as a single-marker selection criterion.
  • Alkylator chemotherapy (temozolomide, dacarbazine) induces marked hypermutation and MMR-gene mutations in panNET — a previously unreported pattern in this disease that could be relevant to subsequent immunotherapy decisions.
  • OncoKB-actionable alterations affected 47% of patients when including Levels 2b/3b/4, though no Level 1/2a evidence was available at the time of the report (PMID:30687805).

Limitations & open questions

  • Sample size remains too small to clarify genetic predictors of response or resistance to commonly used panNET therapies, the prognostic effect of individual alterations, or the typical patterns of clonal evolution; only 12 patients underwent multi-timepoint sequencing (PMID:30687805).
  • The MSK-IMPACT panel evolved across the accrual window (preclinical pilot 275 → 341 → 410 → 468 genes), so some early-cohort samples were not interrogated for genes (e.g., RHEB) that later became standard — affecting allele-frequency comparisons over time.
  • DAXX/ATRX/MEN1 survival benefit conflicts with prior resected-cohort data showing the opposite direction; needs prospective replication in metastatic disease.
  • mTOR-pathway alteration prevalence (43%) is substantially higher than prior series, but did not translate into a predictive biomarker for everolimus benefit in this cohort.
  • Single-institution, primarily tertiary-referred metastatic cohort — generalizability to earlier-stage or community-treated panNETs is unclear.
  • Future direction: the authors propose noninvasive (presumably ctDNA-based) serial NGS to enable real-time clonal-evolution profiling without repeat biopsy.
