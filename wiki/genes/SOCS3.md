# SOCS3

## Overview

SOCS3 (Suppressor of Cytokine Signaling 3) is a negative regulator of JAK/STAT signaling, particularly in the interferon-gamma pathway. In cancer, amplification or overexpression of SOCS3 can suppress anti-tumor immune signaling, and focal copy-number gains at the SOCS3 locus have been associated with resistance to immune checkpoint blockade.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Amplification of SOCS3 (grouped with [SOCS1](../genes/SOCS1.md), [PIAS1](../genes/PIAS1.md), and [PIAS4](../genes/PIAS4.md) amplifications and interferon-gene deletions) was enriched in progressive-disease patients vs. complete/partial responders across a pooled ICB cohort of 249 MSS tumors (19/123 PD vs. 3/70 CR/PR; p=0.019, Fisher's exact); the analysis covered melanoma, [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md), bladder cancer, and HNSCC [PMID:30150660](../papers/30150660.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md), [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md), [BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md), [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md) — SOCS3 amplification implicated in ICB resistance across these histologies [PMID:30150660](../papers/30150660.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Grouped analytically with [SOCS1](../genes/SOCS1.md), [PIAS1](../genes/PIAS1.md), [PIAS4](../genes/PIAS4.md) amplifications and interferon-gene deletions as a composite "interferon-γ pathway CNA" signature enriched in ICB non-responders [PMID:30150660](../papers/30150660.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct targeted therapy reported in the corpus. SOCS3 amplification is a candidate biomarker for intrinsic ICB resistance; patients with these CNAs may be less likely to benefit from anti-PD-1/L1 or anti-CTLA-4 therapy [PMID:30150660](../papers/30150660.md).

## Open questions

- Whether SOCS3 amplification alone (vs. as part of a composite interferon-pathway CNA panel) is independently predictive of ICB resistance remains unresolved in this dataset due to limited sample size [PMID:30150660](../papers/30150660.md).
