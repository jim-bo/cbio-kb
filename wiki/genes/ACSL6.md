# ACSL6

## Overview

ACSL6 (acyl-CoA synthetase long-chain family member 6) encodes a long-chain fatty acid-CoA ligase highly expressed in the brain and nervous system. It preferentially esterifies docosahexaenoic acid (DHA, 22:6) into membrane glycerophospholipids, making it a key regulator of brain-specific lipid composition. In glioblastoma, ACSL6 expression is dramatically reduced relative to normal brain tissue, uncoupling tumor lipid metabolism from the normal DHA-driven membrane composition of the brain.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- In a proteogenomic analysis of 99 treatment-naive GBMs with lipidomic profiling across 75 tumors, ACSL6 protein was severely diminished in tumor tissue relative to normal frontal cortex; despite this loss, DHA (22:6)-containing phosphatidylglycerols and triacylglycerols were increased, suggesting alternative pathways compensating for ACSL6 loss in DHA-rich lipid species. ACSL6 and 22:6-containing lipids were co-regulated across the [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) cohort; the proneural-like subtype most closely resembled normal brain for DHA metabolism, while mesenchymal-like was most distant. [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md):** ACSL6 protein severely downregulated in tumor vs. normal brain; DHA-containing lipid subtype differences track with GBM molecular subtypes. [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- ACSL6 protein levels and 22:6 (DHA)-containing lipids are co-regulated across GBM subtypes; proneural-like GBM most closely resembles normal brain DHA metabolism compared to mesenchymal-like. [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- The dramatic reduction of ACSL6 in GBM and its role in DHA-containing lipid metabolism suggests potential implications for fatty acid supplementation strategies or lipid-metabolism-targeted therapy, though no direct clinical evidence is provided in this corpus. [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Open questions

- The mechanism by which DHA-containing lipids increase in GBM despite ACSL6 loss is unresolved; alternative ACSL family members or alternative acyltransferases may compensate. The normal-tissue comparison is based on 10 unmatched GTEx frontal cortex samples. [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)
