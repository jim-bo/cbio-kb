# HTRA1

## Overview

HTRA1 (High-Temperature Requirement Serine Peptidase A1) is a secreted serine protease involved in TGF-beta signaling, extracellular matrix remodeling, and angiogenesis regulation. In cancer genomics, HTRA1 has been identified as a fusion partner in schwannomatosis, where the SH3PXD2A-HTRA1 gene fusion marks a clinically aggressive, RAS/MAPK-driven tumor subtype.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- HTRA1 — recurrent SH3PXD2A-HTRA1 gene fusion detected in 14% of SWNTS-SWNs (3/22 WGS samples confirmed by RT-PCR); enriched in LZTR1-mutant and painful tumors; provides therapeutic rationale for MEK inhibition by analogy with sporadic schwannomas [PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)
- SH3PXD2A-HTRA1 pathognomonic fusion detected by cWGTS, establishing a schwannoma ([SCHW](../cancer_types/SCHW.md)) diagnosis in a pediatric patient evaluated for relapsed stage-IV neuroblastoma [PMID:35585047](../papers/35585047.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Schwannomatosis (SWNTS-SWN): SH3PXD2A-HTRA1 fusion observed in 14% of cases, associated with painful tumors and germline [LZTR1](../genes/LZTR1.md) mutations [PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with germline [LZTR1](../genes/LZTR1.md) mutation and somatic [NF2](../genes/NF2.md) loss in SWNTS-SWNs; the SH3PXD2A-HTRA1 fusion is enriched in LZTR1-mutant tumors and tracks with RAS/MAPK transcriptional upregulation [PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- SH3PXD2A-HTRA1 fusion-positive tumors show RAS/MAPK pathway upregulation, providing rationale for MEK inhibition (e.g., [selumetinib](../drugs/selumetinib.md)) analogous to NF1-associated plexiform neurofibromas [PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)

## Open questions

- The functional consequence of the SH3PXD2A-HTRA1 fusion on HTRA1 protease activity and downstream signaling is not characterized in this study; mechanism of RAS/MAPK activation by the fusion remains to be demonstrated [PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)
