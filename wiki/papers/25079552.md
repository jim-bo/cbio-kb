**PMID:** 25079552 · **DOI:** 10.1038/nature13385 · **Journal:** Nature (2014)

## TL;DR

The TCGA Research Network performed comprehensive multi-platform molecular profiling (whole-exome sequencing, RNA-seq, miRNA-seq, methylation, copy-number, and reverse-phase protein arrays) of 230 resected, treatment-naive lung adenocarcinomas. Combined with 182 previously published cases (n=412 for statistical analysis), MutSig2CV identified 18 significantly mutated genes, including newly characterized activating mutations in *[RIT1](../genes/RIT1.md)* and loss-of-function mutations in *[MGA](../genes/MGA.md)* (mutually exclusive with focal *[MYC](../genes/MYC.md)* amplification). The study nominated focal *[MET](../genes/MET.md)* and *[ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md)* amplifications, plus *[NF1](../genes/NF1.md)* and *RIT1* mutations, as drivers in oncogene-negative tumours, raising the fraction of lung adenocarcinomas with a known RTK/RAS/RAF activating event from 62% to 76%. Integration of DNA and RNA sequencing demonstrated *MET* exon 14 skipping (4% of cases) and *[U2AF1](../genes/U2AF1.md)*-associated splicing alterations.

## Cohort & data

- 230 previously untreated lung adenocarcinomas ([LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md)) with matched normal tissue, deposited as the [luad_tcga_pub](../datasets/luad_tcga_pub.md) cohort.
- All major histologic types represented: 5% lepidic, 33% acinar, 9% papillary, 14% micropapillary, 25% solid, 4% invasive mucinous, 0.4% colloid, 8% unclassifiable.
- 81% of patients reported past or present smoking; median follow-up 19 months; 163 alive at last follow-up.
- Assays: [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) (tumour mean coverage 97.6×, germline 95.8×), low-pass [whole-genome sequencing](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) on n=93, [mRNA sequencing](../methods/rna-seq.md), miRNA-seq, [HM450 methylation array](../methods/hm450-methylation-array.md), [Affymetrix SNP 6.0](../methods/affymetrix-snp6.md) copy number, [reverse-phase protein arrays](../methods/rppa.md) (n=181).
- Statistical pool for significantly mutated gene discovery: n=412 (230 TCGA + 182 previously published [PMID:22980975](../papers/22980975.md) when available in corpus).

## Key findings

- Mean somatic mutation rate **8.87 mutations/Mb** (range 0.5–48, median 5.78); non-synonymous rate 6.86/Mb.
- **18 significantly mutated genes** identified by [MutSig2CV](../methods/mutsig.md) across n=412 tumours.
- Most frequently mutated genes in the TCGA cohort (n=230): [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) 46%, [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) 33%, [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) 14%, [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) 17%, [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md) 17%, NF1 11%, [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) 10%, [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md) 9%, [RBM10](../genes/RBM10.md) 8%, MGA 8%, [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) 7%, [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) 7%, MET 7%, [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) 6%, [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) 4%, [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) 4%, U2AF1 3%, RIT1 2%.
- **MGA loss-of-function mutations were mutually exclusive with focal MYC amplification** (Fisher's exact P = 0.04), implicating a previously unappreciated mechanism of MYC pathway activation.
- C>A transversion-high ([TH](../genes/TH.md)) vs transversion-low (TL) classification (n=269 TH, n=144 TL): TH strongly associated with smoking (P < 2.2×10⁻¹⁶); KRAS mutations enriched in TH (P = 2.1×10⁻¹³); EGFR, PIK3CA, RB1 enriched in TL (P < 0.05).
- **Sex-biased mutations**: EGFR enriched in females (P = 0.03); RBM10 loss-of-function enriched in males (P = 0.002); 16/21 RBM10 mutations in the TH subset occurred in males (P = 0.003).
- Significant focal amplifications: *[NKX2-1](../genes/NKX2-1.md)*, *[TERT](../genes/TERT.md)*, *[MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md)*, *KRAS*, *EGFR*, *MET*, *[CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md)*, *[CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md)*, *[TERC](../genes/TERC.md)*, *[MECOM](../genes/MECOM.md)*, 8q24 near *MYC*, and a novel peak containing *[CCND3](../genes/CCND3.md)*. Most significant deletion: *CDKN2A*.
- Low-pass WGS (n=93) revealed an average of 36 gene–gene/gene–inter-gene rearrangements per tumour; chromothripsis in 6/93 (6%).
- **MET exon 14 skipping in 4% of cases** (10/230); in 9 of these, a 5′ or 3′ splice site mutation or deletion was identified; one case had a *MET* Y1003* stop codon mutation predicted to disrupt splicing enhancers.
- **U2AF1 S34F mutations (n=8)** were associated with 129 differentially-utilized splicing events, predominantly cassette exons and alternative 3′ splice sites — including alternative splicing of *[CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md)*.
- Fusions in TL tumours only (P = 1.85×10⁻⁴): *[ALK](../genes/ALK.md)* (3/230), *[ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md)* (4/230), *[RET](../genes/RET.md)* (2/230).
- **Oncogene-negative subset (38%)**: WES identified enrichment of *TP53*, *KEAP1*, *NF1*, and *RIT1* mutations (P < 0.01); [GISTIC](../methods/gistic.md) identified focal *ERBB2* and *MET* amplifications specific to this group. With these added, **76% of [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) have a defined RTK/RAS/RAF activating event**, up from 62%.
- **Three mTOR activation patterns** by RPPA: (1) basal, (2) STK11-inactivating mutation or low STK11/low AMPK, (3) high p-AKT, PIK3CA mutation, or both.
- **Three transcriptional subtypes** (renamed): TRU (terminal respiratory unit; formerly bronchioid), PI (proximal-inflammatory; formerly squamoid), PP (proximal-proliferative; formerly magnoid). PP enriched for KRAS + STK11 inactivation; PI enriched for NF1+TP53 co-mutation; TRU contained most EGFR-mutant and kinase-fusion tumours and was prognostically favourable.
- **CIMP-H vs CIMP-L methylation clusters**: CIMP-H tumours hypermethylated WNT pathway genes (P = 0.0015), CDKN2A, GATA family, HOXA9, [HOXD13](../genes/HOXD13.md), [RASSF1](../genes/RASSF1.md), SFRP1, [SOX17](../genes/SOX17.md), WIF1; MYC overexpression associated with CIMP-H (P = 0.003).
- **iCluster integrative analysis** yielded 6 clusters; SETD2 mutation associated with CDKN2A methylation in cluster 4 (TRU-associated, low ploidy, low mutation rate).

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — mutated in 46% of TCGA cases; cluster 1–3 enrichment; co-mutated with *NF1* in the PI subtype.
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — activating mutations in 33% (n=74 cancer-associated); mutually exclusive with *EGFR*; enriched in TH/smoker cohort and the PP transcriptional subtype.
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) — mutations in 14% (n=26 cancer-associated activating); enriched in females, TL, and TRU subtype; in-frame insertions specifically enriched in TL.
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — mutations in 10% (n=16 cancer-associated activating).
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — mutations in 7%; enriched in TL; drives mTOR pathway activation via p-AKT in subset.
- [MET](../genes/MET.md) — DNA mutations in 7%; exon 14 skipping (alternative splicing) in 4% (10/230); focal amplification enriched in oncogene-negative tumours.
- [RIT1](../genes/RIT1.md) — activating mutations clustered around residue Q79 (homologous to RAS Q61) in 2% of cases; **exclusively in the oncogene-negative subset**; nominated as a novel RTK/RAS/RAF driver.
- [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) — inactivating mutations in 17%; mTOR activation via loss of LKB1/AMPK signalling; defining co-feature of PP subtype.
- [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md) — mutations in 17%; enriched in oncogene-negative tumours (P < 0.01).
- [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) — loss-of-function mutations in 11%; enriched in oncogene-negative subset; co-mutated with *TP53* in PI subtype; nominated as a driver event.
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — mutated in 4%; frameshift indels enriched in TL adenocarcinomas vs [SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md) (P < 0.05).
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — mutated in 4%; most significant deletion peak in copy-number data; hypermethylated in CIMP-H tumours; methylation associated with low ploidy, low mutation rate, and SETD2 mutation.
- [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md) — mutated in 9% (chromatin modifier); associated with *CDKN2A* methylation in iCluster 4.
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) — mutated in 7% (chromatin modifier).
- [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) — mutated in 6% (chromatin modifier).
- [RBM10](../genes/RBM10.md) — RNA-binding protein on X chromosome; LOF mutations in 8%; strongly enriched in males (P = 0.002).
- [U2AF1](../genes/U2AF1.md) — S34F mutations in 3% (8 samples); drive 129 alternative splicing events including *CTNNB1*.
- [MGA](../genes/MGA.md) — newly described loss-of-function (frameshift/nonsense) mutations in 8%; **mutually exclusive with focal *MYC* amplification** (P = 0.04); encodes a Max-interacting protein on the MYC pathway.
- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) — 8q24 amplification peak; overexpression associated with CIMP-H phenotype (P = 0.003).
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) — activating in-frame insertion and point mutations (n=5); focal amplification specific to oncogene-negative tumours.
- [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) — fusions in 3/230; transversion-low only.
- [ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md) — fusions in 4/230; transversion-low only.
- [RET](../genes/RET.md) — fusions in 2/230; transversion-low only.
- [NKX2-1](../genes/NKX2-1.md) — significant focal amplification.
- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md), [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md), [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md), [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md), [CCND3](../genes/CCND3.md), [TERC](../genes/TERC.md), [MECOM](../genes/MECOM.md) — recurrent focal amplifications.
- [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md) (n=2), [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) (n=1), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) (n=1) — additional RTK/RAS/RAF pathway mutations.
- [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) — alternative splicing strongly associated with *U2AF1* S34F mutations.

## Clinical implications

- **Therapeutic targets in oncogene-negative LUAD**: The paper argues for "increased implementation of MET and ERBB2/HER2 inhibitors" in tumours otherwise lacking an activated oncogene, given the enrichment of focal *MET* and *ERBB2* amplifications in this 38% subset.
- *RIT1* mutations transform NIH3T3 cells and activate MAPK and PI(3)K signalling, supporting *RIT1* as a candidate driver and potential therapeutic target.
- *MET* exon 14 skipping is highlighted as a clinically actionable splicing alteration; ~9/10 cases have an identifiable cis-acting splice-site mutation suitable for genomic detection.
- **TRU transcriptional subtype membership was prognostically favourable**, replicating prior reports.
- The discrepancy between genomic alterations and protein-level pathway activity (RPPA) suggests additional, undetected mechanisms of MAPK/mTOR activation — implying that pathway-state biomarkers may complement DNA-based stratification.
- *MGA*–*MYC* mutual exclusivity underscores MYC pathway dysregulation as a recurrent feature, motivating MYC-pathway-directed therapeutic strategies.

## Limitations & open questions

- Many tumours show MAPK or mTOR pathway activation by RPPA without identifiable underlying genomic alterations — mechanisms unexplained.
- The mechanism by which the *MET* Y1003* mutation produces exon 14 skipping remains unknown.
- The oncogenic contribution of *U2AF1* S34F-driven splicing changes (e.g. *CTNNB1*) is not established functionally.
- Median follow-up (19 months) limits durable conclusions on prognostic subtype performance.
- Driver assignment depends on the published list of known driver events (Pao & Hutchinson 2012); some additional uncharacterized *KRAS*, *EGFR*, and *BRAF* variants observed were not classified as drivers and would inflate frequencies if reclassified.
- Cluster 6 of the iCluster analysis is largely defined by lower cellularity and lacks clear molecular features — biological identity unclear.
- Statistical significance for sex-biased *EGFR* enrichment (P = 0.03) is modest; replication needed.
