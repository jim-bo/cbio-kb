# LRMDA

## Overview

LRMDA (Leucine Rich Melanocyte Differentiation Associated) encodes a leucine-rich protein expressed in melanocytes. In cancer genomics, it has been identified as a recurrent translocation partner of [HMGA1](../genes/HMGA1.md) and [HMGA2](../genes/HMGA2.md) in endometrial polyps, contributing to the enhancer-hijacking mechanism that drives stromal overexpression of the HMGA proteins.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Recurrent translocation partner of [HMGA1](../genes/HMGA1.md) and [HMGA2](../genes/HMGA2.md) in endometrial polyps (10q22.3 breakpoints); intragenic and downstream breakpoints observed; not considered a classical driver in this context but rather a structural partner facilitating enhancer-hijacking of the HMGA loci [PMID:28445112](../papers/28445112.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[UCEC](../cancer_types/UCEC.md) / Endometrial Polyps:** Recurrent HMGA1/HMGA2 translocation partner; contributes to the stromal-driven neoplasm mechanism by facilitating chromosomal rearrangements that upregulate HMGA expression [PMID:28445112](../papers/28445112.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs as a structural rearrangement partner alongside [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md) and [TRAF3IP2](../genes/TRAF3IP2.md) as the most common HMGA1/HMGA2 translocation partners in endometrial polyps [PMID:28445112](../papers/28445112.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No therapeutic targeting of LRMDA reported in this corpus.

## Open questions

- Whether LRMDA locus breakpoints have functional consequences beyond facilitating HMGA rearrangements is unknown [PMID:28445112](../papers/28445112.md)
