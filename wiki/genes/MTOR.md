# MTOR

## Overview

MTOR (mechanistic target of rapamycin) is a central kinase in the PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway that coordinates cell growth, metabolism, and proliferation. In [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md), mTOR activation drives SREBP1 nuclear translocation and de novo lipogenesis, contributing to TKI and ICI resistance.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- AKT/MTOR pathway is a central node activating SREBP1 nuclear translocation and de novo lipogenesis in HCC; mTOR inhibition is proposed as a strategy to suppress DNL and resensitize HCC to TKIs and ICIs [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) — mTOR pathway activation is a key driver of lipid metabolic reprogramming and drug resistance [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- mTOR activation is downstream of [CD36](../genes/CD36.md), FATP5 loss, and PD-L1/EGFR/ITGB4 signaling in HCC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- mTOR inhibitors (rapalogs: [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md), [temsirolimus](../drugs/temsirolimus.md)) are approved in some cancers; combination with lipid-targeting agents is proposed for HCC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Open questions

- Clinical evidence for mTOR inhibition combined with lipid-targeting in HCC is preliminary; predictive biomarkers lacking.
