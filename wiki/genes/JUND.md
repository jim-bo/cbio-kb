# JUND

## Overview

JUND (JunD Proto-Oncogene, AP-1 Transcription Factor Subunit) encodes a basic leucine zipper transcription factor of the Jun family that dimerizes with Fos family members to form AP-1 complexes. JUND is distinguished from other Jun members by being a negative regulator of cell proliferation in some contexts and a tumor suppressor for hepatocellular carcinoma. In metastatic CRPC, JUND is part of the AP-1 transcription factor network enriched in the stem cell-like (CRPC-SCL) subtype.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- AP-1 family member including JUND identified as part of the TF network enriched in CRPC-SCL accessible chromatin peaks; CRPC-SCL is the second-most-common CRPC subtype (~28%) among 366 patients across SU2C and WCM cohorts, associated with shorter time on next-generation ARSI [PMID:35617398](../papers/35617398.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) / [PRSCC](../cancer_types/PRSCC.md): AP-1 family (including JUND) upregulated in CRPC-SCL subtype identified by integrative ATAC-seq/RNA-seq profiling [PMID:35617398](../papers/35617398.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-expressed with [FOSL1](../genes/FOSL1.md), [FOS](../genes/FOS.md), [FOSB](../genes/FOSB.md), [FOSL2](../genes/FOSL2.md), [JUN](../genes/JUN.md), [JUNB](../genes/JUNB.md) as part of the broader AP-1 family TF network in CRPC-SCL [PMID:35617398](../papers/35617398.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- T-5224 (c-Fos/AP-1 DNA-binding inhibitor) and [verteporfin](../drugs/verteporfin.md) selectively reduce CRPC-SCL cell growth, nominating AP-1 members including JUND as candidate targets [PMID:35617398](../papers/35617398.md)
