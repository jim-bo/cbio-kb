**PMID:** 30150660 · **DOI:** 10.1038/s41588-018-0200-2 · **Journal:** Nature Genetics (2018)

## TL;DR

Miao, Margolis, Vokes and colleagues pooled whole-exome sequencing from 249 microsatellite-stable tumors (melanoma, [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md), bladder, HNSCC, anal, sarcoma) drawn from seven previously-published immune checkpoint blockade (ICB) cohorts plus 78 newly-sequenced Dana-Farber samples, and uniformly re-analyzed them against clinical response by RECIST 1.1. Beyond confirming that tumor mutational burden (TMB) is associated with complete/partial response (CR/PR) vs progressive disease (PD), they identify clonal driver alterations in *[PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)*, *[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)*, and *[PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md)* enriched in responders, *[EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md)* hotspots enriched in non-responders, biallelic *[PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md)* loss occurring exclusively in PD, and 11q (*[PAK1](../genes/PAK1.md)*/*[YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md)*/*[CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md)*) and 12q (*[MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md)*/*[CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md)*) amplifications enriched in PD. Dominant mutational signature (UV in melanoma, smoking in NSCLC, APOBEC in bladder/HNSCC) is highly correlated with TMB and intratumoral heterogeneity, and in melanoma TMB ceases to predict response after adjusting for dominant signature. Power calculations indicate that detection of common (~10% prevalence) response-associated variants requires ~300 samples and rare variants thousands.

## Cohort & data

- N = 249 tumors with matched germline (from 314 starting samples after QC) across six cancer types:
  - [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md) melanoma — N=151
  - [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) non-small cell lung cancer — N=57
  - [BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md) bladder cancer — N=27
  - [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md) head and neck squamous cell carcinoma — N=12
  - [ANSC](../cancer_types/ANSC.md) anal cancer — N=1
  - [SARCNOS](../cancer_types/SARCNOS.md) sarcoma — N=1
- Treatment classes: anti-PD-1 (N=74), anti-PD-L1 (N=20), anti-CTLA-4 (N=145), combination anti-CTLA-4 + anti-PD-1/L1 (N=10), other combinations (N=7).
- Assay: tumor/normal [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md); average mean target coverage 150x tumor / 119x germline; mean tumor purity 58% (range 10%–97%).
- Dataset: [mixed_allen_2018](../datasets/mixed_allen_2018.md). Raw BAMs at dbGaP phs000694.v3.p2, phs000980.v1.p1, phs001041.v1.p1, phs000452.v2.p1, phs001075.v1.p1, and new accession phs001565.v1.p1.
- Computational pipeline: [mutect](../methods/mutect.md) (SNVs), [strelka](../methods/strelka.md) (indels), [oncotator](../methods/oncotator.md) (annotation), [absolute](../methods/absolute.md) (clonality, purity, ploidy), [polysolver](../methods/polysolver.md) (HLA typing), [netmhcpan](../methods/netmhcpan.md) v3.0 (neoantigen prediction), non-negative matrix factorization for [mutational-signatures](../methods/mutational-signatures.md) deconvolution against COSMIC signatures.

## Key findings

- **TMB as a single variable is weakly predictive.** CR/PR patients had significantly higher TMB than PD across the pooled cohort (p<0.05, Mann-Whitney U), but the distributions overlap and ROC AUC = 0.66 for CR/PR vs PD ([PMID:30150660](../papers/30150660.md), Supplementary Fig. 4).
- **Clonal TMB is stronger than total TMB.** Mutational clonality inferred by [absolute](../methods/absolute.md) showed that clonal nonsynonymous burden predicts CR/PR vs PD across cancer types and response categorizations. Patients with >50% subclonal mutations (high intratumoral heterogeneity) were enriched for PD (p=0.0014, Fisher's exact).
- **Driver gene enrichment.** Clonal driver alterations in [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), and [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md) were enriched in CR/PR; clonal driver mutations in [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) were enriched in PD (p<0.05 for all; FDR q=0.18 for KRAS and PIK3CA). [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) and [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) remained associated with CR/PR after correcting for TMB (p<0.05, logistic regression), though without surviving FDR correction.
- **PIK3CA cancer-type context dependence.** Patients with clonal hotspot [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) and CR/PR were melanoma, HNSCC, anal, or bladder; most PIK3CA-mutant PD patients were lung. [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) hotspots associated with CR/PR across multiple cancer types.
- **Interferon-γ pathway CNAs.** Copy number alterations expected to disrupt interferon-γ signaling (deletions of interferon genes or amplifications of *[SOCS1](../genes/SOCS1.md)*, *[SOCS3](../genes/SOCS3.md)*, *[PIAS1](../genes/PIAS1.md)*, *[PIAS4](../genes/PIAS4.md)*) were enriched in PD vs CR/PR (19/123 vs 3/70; p=0.019, Fisher's exact).
- **Focal CNAs in PD.** Amplifications of [PAK1](../genes/PAK1.md), [YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md), [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) on 11q, and [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md), [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) on 12q were seen predominantly in PD patients.
- **Biallelic PTEN loss is intrinsic-resistance-only.** Homozygous deletion of [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) occurred exclusively in PD (N=4, p=0.30); truncating clonal biallelic loss was also seen in resistant tumors from mixed-response anti-CTLA-4 patients.
- **PBRM1 / SWI/SNF in non-ccRCC settings.** Truncating mutations in [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md) and other PBAF SWI/SNF members were over-represented in responders, with biallelic loss almost exclusive to CR/PR — generalizing prior ccRCC findings to melanoma, lung, bladder, and HNSCC.
- **Mutational signature dominates in melanoma.** After stratifying by dominant signature (UV S7, alkylating S11, other S1/S5), TMB was no longer a significant predictor of CR/PR vs PD in melanoma (p>0.05 for all). The non-UV/non-alkylating melanoma subgroup (mucosal, uveal, acral) had a higher PD rate.
- **APOBEC in bladder and HNSCC.** APOBEC signatures S2/S13 correlated with higher TMB (p=0.002 bladder, p=0.03 HNSCC, Mann-Whitney U) and CR/PR (p=0.019), and may confound the observed [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)–response association.
- **Smoking signature in NSCLC.** High smoking-signature NSCLC tumors had low intratumoral heterogeneity, high TMB, and frequent [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md). [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md)-hotspot tumors had subclonal-skewed mutations (p=0.035), low TMB, were enriched in never-smokers (p=0.00017, two-tailed Fisher), and had poor ICB response. TMB remained a significant predictor of response after controlling for smoking history.
- **Neoantigens are highly collinear with TMB.** On average each nonsynonymous mutation yielded 2.24 predicted neoantigens (R²=0.99, p<0.00001). 871 of the predicted neoantigens arose from driver mutations; 8 of these driver-derived neoantigens recurred in CR/PR but not PD in an HLA-dependent manner, and were clonal in all carrier samples.
- **Power analysis.** With Bonferroni correction over 116 driver genes and assumed 40% CR/PR rate, detection of a common (~10% prevalence) responder-specific variant requires sample sizes ~300; detection of rare (~1%) variants requires thousands.

## Genes & alterations

- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — clonal hotspot mutations enriched in CR/PR (FDR q=0.18); response association is cancer-type dependent and may be confounded with APOBEC signatures.
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — clonal hotspot mutations enriched in CR/PR across cancer types; remains associated after TMB correction (p<0.05, logistic regression) but does not survive FDR.
- [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md) — truncating / biallelic loss-of-function enriched in CR/PR, generalizing prior ccRCC findings to melanoma, lung, bladder, HNSCC. PBAF SWI/SNF family alterations show the same pattern.
- [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md) — PBAF SWI/SNF member; loss implicated alongside [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md) in responder enrichment.
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) — clonal hotspot mutations enriched in PD; tumors are subclonal-skewed, low-TMB, and over-represented in never-smokers.
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — homozygous deletion occurred only in PD patients (N=4); biallelic truncating loss seen in mixed-response anti-CTLA-4 cases.
- [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) / [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) — 12q focal amplifications enriched in PD.
- [PAK1](../genes/PAK1.md) / [YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md) / [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) — 11q focal amplifications enriched in PD.
- Interferon-γ pathway — combined deletions of interferon genes or amplifications of [SOCS1](../genes/SOCS1.md), [SOCS3](../genes/SOCS3.md), [PIAS1](../genes/PIAS1.md), [PIAS4](../genes/PIAS4.md) enriched in PD (p=0.019).
- [B2M](../genes/B2M.md), [JAK1](../genes/JAK1.md), [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md), [STAT1](../genes/STAT1.md) — antigen-presentation and JAK/STAT pathway loss-of-function events were rare in this pre-treatment cohort, possibly reflecting intrinsic- vs acquired-resistance differences.

## Clinical implications

- TMB alone is an imprecise pre-treatment biomarker (AUC=0.66 in this cohort) — integrating clonality, dominant mutational signature, and specific driver/CNA events yields better stratification.
- For [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md) melanoma, the dominant mutational signature is a stronger correlate of response than TMB; TMB may be a proxy for UV-driven biological processes that both raise immunogenicity and accumulate mutations.
- For [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)-mutant smoker-signature tumors are a favorable subgroup for ICB; [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md)-hotspot never-smoker tumors are unfavorable — consistent with prior reports.
- For [BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md) bladder and [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md) head and neck SCC, APOBEC-signature dominance is associated with both high TMB and CR/PR.
- Biallelic [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) loss flags intrinsic resistance to ICB; loss of intact interferon-γ signaling via CNA also flags poor response and may be a candidate exclusion criterion.
- Loss of [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md) / PBAF SWI/SNF components predicts response across multiple histologies, not only ccRCC.
- Drug-class context: cohort spans [ipilimumab](../drugs/ipilimumab.md), [tremelimumab](../drugs/tremelimumab.md), [nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md), [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md), [atezolizumab](../drugs/atezolizumab.md); anti-CTLA-4 use was concentrated in melanoma while anti-PD-1/L1 dominated other histologies, limiting drug-class-by-tumor-type inference.

## Limitations & open questions

- Pooled retrospective cohort with heterogeneous original sources and FFPE-derived sequencing in the Dana-Farber additions; sample size still under-powered for most single-gene tests after FDR correction (e.g. [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)/[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) at q=0.18).
- Anti-CTLA-4 vs anti-PD-1/L1 effects are confounded with cancer type (anti-CTLA-4 predominantly melanoma).
- Computational neoantigen prediction is highly collinear with TMB (R²=0.99); in-silico methods cannot disentangle the two with this cohort.
- Loss-of-function alterations in JAK/STAT and antigen-presentation machinery ([B2M](../genes/B2M.md), [JAK1](../genes/JAK1.md), [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md), [STAT1](../genes/STAT1.md)) were rare pre-treatment; intrinsic vs acquired-resistance mechanisms may differ.
- Patients with stable disease (SD) by RECIST 1.1 are handled separately from the main CR/PR-vs-PD comparison; alternative response definitions stratifying SD by PFS/OS give different classifications.
- Mixed-response patients (tumor shrinkage at some sites, progression at others) are excluded from main analyses but were enriched for [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) truncating events at progressing sites.
- Authors call out that ccRCC, the original setting for [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md) loss as a response biomarker, is not represented in this cohort; the generalization to other histologies needs further validation.
- Power calculations indicate that detection of rare (~1%) response-associated variants is infeasible without sample sizes in the thousands, motivating hypothesis-driven validation from experimental work.
