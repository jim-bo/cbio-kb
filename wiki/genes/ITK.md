# ITK

## Overview

ITK (IL2-inducible T-cell kinase) encodes a tyrosine kinase critical for T-cell receptor signaling and T-cell development. In cancer genomics, ITK has been observed in germline sequencing as a variant of uncertain significance (VOUS) in pediatric lymphoma contexts.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- ITK V175V variant of uncertain significance (VOUS) returned to a family with Hodgkin lymphoma and EBV infection in pediatric comprehensive genomic sequencing [PMID:28007021](../papers/28007021.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- Hodgkin lymphoma: ITK V175V VOUS identified in a pediatric patient with concurrent EBV infection [PMID:28007021](../papers/28007021.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- No co-occurrence data reported in the current corpus.

## Therapeutic relevance

- No therapeutic actionability established for the V175V VOUS; returned under conservative disclosure criteria in a pediatric tumor board setting [PMID:28007021](../papers/28007021.md).

## Open questions

- The functional significance of ITK V175V and its relationship to Hodgkin lymphoma predisposition or EBV susceptibility remains unresolved [PMID:28007021](../papers/28007021.md).
