**PMID:** 35443055 · **DOI:** 10.1158/1078-0432.CCR-21-4183 · **Journal:** Clinical Cancer Research (2022)

## TL;DR

Manning-Geist and colleagues performed MSK-IMPACT targeted sequencing (341–505 cancer genes) on tumors from 119 patients with centrally pathology-reviewed low-grade serous ovarian carcinoma ([LGSOC](../cancer_types/LGSOC.md)) treated at MSK between 2015 and 2021. Sixty percent of tumors harbored a somatic MAPK pathway alteration, dominated by [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) hotspot mutations (33%), [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) hotspots (11%), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) alterations (11%), and [EIF1AX](../genes/EIF1AX.md) (10%). On multivariate analysis, both MAPK pathway alteration (HR 2.5 for absence, p=0.019) and platinum sensitivity (p=0.005) were independently associated with improved overall survival. Among 79 patients with germline testing, only 7 (9%) carried a pathogenic germline mutation and **none had BRCA1/2 mutations** despite 13% of the cohort identifying as Ashkenazi Jewish — sharply distinguishing the germline landscape of LGSC from HGSC.

## Cohort & data

- **n = 119** patients with centrally reviewed LGSC (ovarian, primary peritoneal LGSC, and serous borderline tumors with invasive implants), drawn from 2,063 epithelial ovarian carcinomas sequenced on the [MSK-IMPACT panel](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) between 04/2015 and 02/2021.
- Cancer type: [LGSOC](../cancer_types/LGSOC.md) (low-grade serous ovarian cancer).
- Dataset: [lgsoc_mapk_msk_2022](../datasets/lgsoc_mapk_msk_2022.md).
- Assay: MSK-IMPACT panel at four versions — [IMPACT341](../methods/IMPACT341.md) (n=8), [IMPACT410](../methods/IMPACT410.md) (n=23), [IMPACT468](../methods/IMPACT468.md) (n=83), and [IMPACT505](../methods/IMPACT505.md) (n=5); median sequencing depth 588x (range 94–1,231x).
- Tumor samples: primary (n=81) and recurrent (n=38); matched normal blood for all.
- Germline: 79/119 (66%) underwent germline genetic testing (custom panels of 76–90 genes at MSK plus outside labs); LOH inferred via [FACETS](../methods/facets.md).
- Clinical: 110 (92%) had advanced-stage (FIGO III/IV) disease; median age at diagnosis 48 years (range 20–79); median [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) follow-up 62.7 months.
- 16 patients (13%) self-identified as Ashkenazi Jewish.

## Key findings

- **MAPK pathway alteration prevalence:** 71/119 (60%) of LGSCs carried a somatic MAPK pathway alteration, with [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) (n=13), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) (n=39), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) (n=1), [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) (n=13), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) (n=2), [NF2](../genes/NF2.md) (n=1), [MAP3K1](../genes/MAP3K1.md) (n=1), and [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) (n=5) hits ([PMID:35443055](../papers/35443055.md)).
- **[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) hotspots dominate:** 33% of cases (G12V n=19; G12D n=14; G12C n=3; G12R n=2; G12A n=1).
- **[NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) hotspots:** 11% (Q61R n=9; Q61K n=4). **[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md):** 11% (V600E n=8; D594N n=2; N581I n=1; BRAF–[MKRN1](../genes/MKRN1.md) fusion n=1; BRAF–[AGAP3](../genes/AGAP3.md) fusion n=1).
- **[EIF1AX](../genes/EIF1AX.md) alterations** in 10% (n=12) and **[CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) homozygous deletions** in 8% (n=9).
- **Mutually exclusive RAS/RAF:** KRAS vs NRAS p=0.004 (q=0.46); KRAS vs BRAF p=0.034 (q=1.0); BRAF vs NRAS p=0.204 (q=1.0); only one tumor had co-existing BRAF + KRAS.
- **No driver alterations** in 18% (n=21) of LGSCs despite adequate tumor purity (median 30% vs 40% in altered cases; p=0.12).
- **Low TMB:** median 1.8 mutations/Mb (range 0–17.8); median 1 non-synonymous somatic mutation (range 0–16). All 102 evaluable cases were microsatellite stable (median MSIsensor score 0.12). Median fraction of genome altered 0.16.
- **KRAS association with age and platinum sensitivity:** KRAS-mutant tumors were enriched in patients diagnosed at >50 years (p=0.03) and in platinum-sensitive disease (p=0.04). NRAS and BRAF showed no significant association with either variable.
- **Overall survival (univariate):** MAPK pathway alteration (p=0.005) and platinum sensitivity (p=0.001) were associated with improved [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md); age (p>0.99) and residual disease (p=0.29) were not.
- **Overall survival (multivariate, n=90 landmark cohort):** Both MAPK pathway alteration (HR 2.5 [1.2–5.2] for absence vs presence, p=0.019) and platinum sensitivity (HR 0.4 [0.2–0.7] for sensitive vs resistant, p=0.005) remained independently associated with OS.
- **Crude mortality:** 14/53 (26%) MAPK-altered patients died versus 18/37 (49%) MAPK-wildtype; 12/24 (50%) platinum-resistant died versus 20/66 (30%) platinum-sensitive.
- **Median OS:** 339 months (MAPK-altered) vs 125 months (MAPK-wildtype); 234 months (platinum-sensitive) vs 45 months (platinum-resistant).
- **PFS:** Only residual disease after initial cytoreduction remained significantly associated with PFS on multivariate analysis (p=0.04).
- **Sequential tumor sampling (n=8):** In 2 of 5 patients whose first tested tumor lacked driver alterations, subsequent recurrences harbored drivers (one [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)/B deletion, one [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) G469V), supporting a role for repeat sequencing across the disease course.
- **Germline:** 7/79 tested (9%) carried a pathogenic germline mutation: [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) (n=1), [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md) (n=1), [MUTYH](../genes/MUTYH.md) (n=2; both c.1187G>A p.Gly396Asp), [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md) (n=1), [APC](../genes/APC.md) (n=1), [FANCA](../genes/FANCA.md) (n=1). **No germline BRCA1/2 mutations** were identified.
- **Biallelic germline driver:** LOH at the germline locus was only observed in 1 of the 2 [MUTYH](../genes/MUTYH.md) carriers; the [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md) carrier had a somatic frameshift [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md) but retained BAP1 IHC, suggesting cis configuration on one allele rather than biallelic loss.
- **MEK inhibitor experience:** 4 patients received MEK inhibitor for recurrence (3 MAPK-altered); none progressed on MEK inhibitor, but all discontinued for toxicity at a median 6.7 months (range 0.7–22.6).

## Genes & alterations

- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — hotspot mutations in 33% (39/119); enriched in age >50 (p=0.03) and platinum-sensitive disease (p=0.04). G12V is the most frequent variant (n=19).
- [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) — Q61R/Q61K hotspots in 11% (13/119); not significantly associated with age or platinum sensitivity but with a numerical trend toward platinum sensitivity (90% vs 71%, p=0.28).
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — 11% (13/119): V600E (n=8), D594N (n=2), N581I (n=1), and two fusions (BRAF–[MKRN1](../genes/MKRN1.md), BRAF–[AGAP3](../genes/AGAP3.md)).
- [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), [NF2](../genes/NF2.md), [MAP3K1](../genes/MAP3K1.md) — singletons/rare events contributing to the broader MAPK pathway-altered group.
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) — 5 cases including 2 amplifications and 1 Y772_A775dup hotspot; included in MAPK-altered group.
- [EIF1AX](../genes/EIF1AX.md) — 10% (12/119); recurrent in LGSC but not part of the MAPK pathway gene set used for survival analysis.
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — homozygous deletion in 8% (9/119).
- [USP9X](../genes/USP9X.md) — discussed as a candidate driver reported in up to 27% of LGSCs in prior whole-exome work; not on MSK-IMPACT and so may explain some of the 18% no-alteration cases.
- Negative results: no detected alterations in [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md), [MAP2K2](../genes/MAP2K2.md), [MAP2K4](../genes/MAP2K4.md), [MAP3K13](../genes/MAP3K13.md), MAP3K14, [MAPK1](../genes/MAPK1.md), [MAPK3](../genes/MAPK3.md), MAPKAP1, DUSP4, RRAS, [RRAS2](../genes/RRAS2.md), or [RAF1](../genes/RAF1.md) in this cohort.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — characterized in the introduction as rare in LGSC (consistent with WHO classification); not a recurrent driver in this cohort.
- Germline pathogenic carriers: [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md), [MUTYH](../genes/MUTYH.md) p.Gly396Asp ×2, [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md), [APC](../genes/APC.md), [FANCA](../genes/FANCA.md).
- [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) / [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) — explicitly **no** pathogenic germline mutations identified, contrasting with reported 3.5–5.7% prevalence in prior LGSC literature and the 20% rate in HGSC.

## Clinical implications

- **Prognostic biomarker:** MAPK pathway alteration is an independent favorable prognostic factor for OS in LGSC (multivariate HR 2.5 for absence vs presence, p=0.019), and platinum sensitivity remains independently favorable (HR 0.4, p=0.005). The authors recommend that future LGSC trials stratify or report MAPK alteration distribution.
- **Predictive context:** The result is biologically coherent with the [MILO/ENGOT-ov11](https://doi.org/10.1200/JCO.20.01164) phase III trial showing a PFS advantage for [binimetinib](../drugs/binimetinib.md) in KRAS-mutated vs KRAS-wildtype recurrent LGSC. The current paper extends that signal from PFS-on-MEKi to OS in a real-world cohort treated heterogeneously.
- **Germline testing yield:** Pathogenic germline mutation rate was 9% across an 88-gene panel — higher than the 6% reported by Norquist et al. (likely due to broader panels) but well below HGSC's 20%. The complete absence of germline BRCA1/2 mutations argues against routine HBOC framing of LGSC and supports separate germline counseling pathways.
- **MEK inhibitor tolerability** remains the limiting factor: 4/4 patients on MEKi for recurrence discontinued due to toxicity (median 6.7 months) despite no progression events while on drug.
- **Sequential sequencing:** Re-biopsy and re-sequencing at recurrence may unmask actionable MAPK pathway drivers absent at first sampling (2/5 cases without initial drivers acquired one — [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)/B deletion or [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) G469V).
- **Maintenance therapy:** Bevacizumab and endocrine maintenance regimens were used heterogeneously; no maintenance regimen reached statistical significance for PFS in this cohort (p=0.11 in NED group; p=0.17 in evidence-of-disease group).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Single-center, retrospective design** with referral bias toward advanced-stage disease (92% FIGO III/IV); sensitivity analyses restricted to patients first seen at diagnosis (n=60) lost power to detect OS associations.
- **Panel coverage gaps:** MSK-IMPACT does not cover [USP9X](../genes/USP9X.md), which a prior whole-exome study reported altered in up to 27% of LGSCs — a plausible explanation for some of the 18% of tumors lacking detectable drivers. WES studies of these "negative" cases are needed.
- **Germline ascertainment bias:** Only 66% of patients underwent germline testing, and 28 cases relied on outside-lab reports (Myriad, Ambry, Invitae, etc.) with heterogeneous gene panels. The absence of BRCA1/2 carriers might partly reflect ascertainment, although prior LGSC literature also reports low BRCA1/2 prevalence (3.5–5.7%).
- **Tumor sample heterogeneity:** Both primary (n=81) and recurrent (n=38) tumors were sequenced; molecular alterations may evolve over time as demonstrated in 8 paired-sample analyses.
- **MEK inhibitor cohort is tiny** (n=4) and uninterpretable for efficacy; toxicity-driven discontinuation remains the dominant limitation.
- **Open question:** What drives the 18% of LGSCs without any detectable alteration on MSK-IMPACT, and do they belong to a distinct clinical subtype? Multi-institutional WES studies are warranted.
- **Open question:** Is MAPK pathway alteration the cause of platinum sensitivity, a marker of indolent biology, or both? The independent association of both with OS on multivariate analysis suggests partially distinct biology.
