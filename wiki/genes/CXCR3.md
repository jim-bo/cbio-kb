# CXCR3

## Overview

CXCR3 (C-X-C Motif Chemokine Receptor 3) encodes a G-protein-coupled receptor that binds [CXCL9](../genes/CXCL9.md), [CXCL10](../genes/CXCL10.md), and CXCL11, mediating T-cell trafficking and immune cell recruitment to tumors. CXCR3 and its ligands form a key axis linking interferon-gamma signaling to anti-tumor immune responses, making CXCR3 expression a candidate biomarker for immunotherapy response.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Higher CXCR3 expression in ipilimumab-treated melanoma responders to subsequent anti-PD1 ICB (MWW P=0.02); part of the CXCL9/CXCL10/CXCR3 immune chemokine axis elevated in responders in the ipilimumab-treated subgroup but not in ipilimumab-naive tumors [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Melanoma: elevated CXCR3 expression associated with response to anti-PD1 ICB in patients pretreated with [ipilimumab](../drugs/ipilimumab.md) [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- CXCR3 expression co-elevated with CXCL9 and CXCL10 in anti-PD1 responders among ipilimumab-pretreated melanoma patients; MHC-II HLA gene expression was the strongest single predictor in this subgroup [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- CXCR3/CXCL9/CXCL10 axis expression is a component of an immune infiltrate signature that, combined with MHC-II HLA expression and clinical features (LDH, lymph-node status), yields AUC 0.90 for predicting anti-PD1 response in ipilimumab-pretreated melanoma [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)

## Open questions

- Whether elevated CXCR3 expression reflects tumor-cell expression or tumor-infiltrating immune cells is unresolved by bulk transcriptome analysis; cell-of-origin disambiguation requires single-cell or IHC data [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
