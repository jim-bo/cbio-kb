# GBP2

## Overview

GBP2 (Guanylate Binding Protein 2) is an interferon-gamma ([IFNG](../genes/IFNG.md))-inducible GTPase involved in innate immune defense. In cancer, GBP2 expression is a readout of IFNG signaling activity within the tumor microenvironment. Its suppression by activated oncogenic receptors such as [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) has been linked to the T-cell-depleted immune phenotype observed in luminal-papillary urothelial cancers.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- GBP2 is an IFNG-response gene upregulated upon FGFR3 shRNA knockdown in RT-112 urothelial carcinoma cells (P=0.038), implicating it in FGFR3-mediated T-cell exclusion in upper tract urothelial carcinoma [PMID:31278255](../papers/31278255.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **Upper tract urothelial carcinoma ([UTUC](../cancer_types/UTUC.md)):** GBP2 is concordantly downregulated in the T-cell-depleted cluster (87.5% of UTUC cases), consistent with suppression of IFNG response by activated FGFR3 signaling [PMID:31278255](../papers/31278255.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- GBP2 downregulation co-occurs with high FGFR3 expression in the T-cell-depleted cluster of luminal-papillary UTUC [PMID:31278255](../papers/31278255.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Pharmacologic FGFR3 inhibition with [erdafitinib](../drugs/erdafitinib.md) upregulates [BST2](../genes/BST2.md) (another IFNG-response gene in the same pathway), suggesting that FGFR inhibitors may restore IFNG signaling including GBP2 expression; this provides mechanistic rationale for combining FGFR3 inhibitors with immune checkpoint blockade [PMID:31278255](../papers/31278255.md).

## Open questions

- Whether GBP2 restoration alone is sufficient to reverse T-cell exclusion in FGFR3-activated UTUC, or whether additional IFNG-response genes ([IRF9](../genes/IRF9.md), [MX2](../genes/MX2.md), BST2) must be co-restored, is untested in vivo.
