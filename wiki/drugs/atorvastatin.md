# atorvastatin

## Overview

Atorvastatin is a lipophilic statin that inhibits [HMGCR](../genes/HMGCR.md) (HMG-CoA reductase), the rate-limiting enzyme of cholesterol biosynthesis. Being lipophilic, it penetrates tumor cells more readily than hydrophilic statins (pravastatin, rosuvastatin). Population-based epidemiologic studies associate lipophilic statins with reduced HCC incidence and cancer-related mortality. It is not FDA-approved as an anti-cancer agent.

## Evidence in the corpus

- Population data link lipophilic statins (atorvastatin, [simvastatin](../drugs/simvastatin.md), [lovastatin](../drugs/lovastatin.md)) to a more pronounced [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)-risk reduction than hydrophilic statins ([pravastatin](../drugs/pravastatin.md), rosuvastatin); the lipophilicity difference is proposed to explain why multiple phase 2/3 trials of hydrophilic statins combined with [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md) in advanced HCC failed to improve survival [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Resistance mechanisms

- Not applicable (atorvastatin is evaluated as a sensitizing/preventive agent rather than a primary therapeutic).
