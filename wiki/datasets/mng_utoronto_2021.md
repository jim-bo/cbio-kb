# Meningioma (University of Toronto, Nature Genetics 2021)

## Overview

Multi-platform integrative meningioma cohort from the University Health Network Brain Tumor BioBank (Toronto), enriched for WHO grade 2 and 3 tumors. Used to develop a clinically applicable integrative molecular classification (MG1–MG4). An 80-sample independent validation cohort was assessed with mRNA signatures. Data deposited in cBioPortal as `mng_utoronto_2021`. [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)

## Composition

- 121 fresh-frozen meningiomas from the University Health Network Brain Tumor BioBank, plus 5 healthy meninges controls; enriched for WHO grade 2 and 3 tumors. [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)
- 80 independent meningiomas in validation cohort assessed using mRNA signatures. [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)
- Cancer type: [MNG](../cancer_types/MNG.md) (meningioma). [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)

## Assays / panels (linked)

- Whole-exome sequencing (median 191X). [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)
- Illumina EPIC methylation array. [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)
- mRNA-seq. [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)
- Whole-cell proteomics (6,568 proteins quantified in 96 tumors). [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)
- Single-nuclear RNA-seq (54,393 nuclei from 8 tumors + 2 healthy meninges). [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)

## Papers using this cohort

- [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md) — A Clinically Applicable Integrative Molecular Classification of Meningiomas.

## Notable findings derived from this cohort

- COCA integration of CNA, DNA methylation, and mRNA clusters identified four stable molecular groups (MG1–MG4), independently associated with recurrence-free survival after adjusting for WHO grade (log-rank P=5×10^-15). [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)
- MG classification was superior to WHO grade, methylation-based classification, and individual-datatype clustering for predicting time to recurrence. [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)
- [NF2](../genes/NF2.md) mutated in 88% of MG1 vs. 9% of MG2 tumors (P=5.9×10^-8); non-NF2 drivers ([TRAF7](../genes/TRAF7.md) 25%, [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) 13%, [KLF4](../genes/KLF4.md) 13%, [POLR2A](../genes/POLR2A.md) 6%) exclusive to MG2. [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)
- Novel recurrent driver mutations in [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md), [CHD2](../genes/CHD2.md), and [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) enriched in MG3/MG4 (P=0.002). [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md)
