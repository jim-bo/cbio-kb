# Proteogenomic landscape of breast cancer (CPTAC, Cell 2020)

## Overview

The CPTAC breast cancer proteogenomics cohort (brca_cptac_2020) was generated by Krug et al. (Cell 2020) under the Clinical Proteomic Tumor Analysis Consortium program. It comprises 122 prospectively collected, treatment-naive primary breast tumors from 134 enrolled participants (ages 30–95), each processed under a stringent short-ischemia cryopulverization protocol enabling co-isolation of DNA, RNA, and protein from a single homogeneous aliquot. Genomic data are released to GDC under dbGaP phs000892 (GRCh38 alignment); proteomics are accessible at CPTAC accession S060 / PDC000120. The cBioPortal study uses reference genome hg19.

## Composition

- **Tumor type:** Invasive [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) — predominantly [IDC](../cancer_types/IDC.md) with some [ILC](../cancer_types/ILC.md); stages IIA–IIIC.
- **IHC receptor classification:** ER and/or PR positive = 83; HER2 positive = 13; triple-negative = 16; ER/PR negative with equivocal HER2 = 12.
- **PAM50 distribution:** 14 HER2-E, 29 Basal, 57 LumA, 17 LumB, 5 Normal-like.
- **Data yield:** 29,647 somatic mutations; 23,692 gene-level copy-number events; 23,121 transcripts; 10,107 proteins; 38,968 phosphosites; 9,869 acetylation sites passing QC.

## Notable findings derived from this cohort

- Integrated multi-omics NMF resolved four breast cancer subtypes (LumA-I, LumB-I, HER2-I, Basal-I) that refine PAM50 classification; NMF LumB-I reassigned PAM50 LumA samples with fewer [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) mutations (binomial p=1.50×10⁻³) showed intermediate outcomes in [brca_metabric](../datasets/brca_metabric.md) validation [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md).
- NMF HER2-I is heterogeneous: 2 of 17 ERBB2-amplified cases lacked detectable [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) protein ("pseudo-ERBB2+"), which would undermine [trastuzumab](../drugs/trastuzumab.md) response; confirmed in the earlier [brca_tcga_pub2015](../datasets/brca_tcga_pub2015.md) retrospective cohort [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md).
- APOBEC mutagenesis ([APOBEC3B](../genes/APOBEC3B.md)/[APOBEC3G](../genes/APOBEC3G.md)) and single-strand break repair defects were nominated as biomarkers for immune checkpoint therapy in luminal breast cancer [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md).
- Rb protein/phosphoprotein status — rather than [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) genotype alone — tracked CDK4/6 inhibitor sensitivity in TNBC, supporting use of [palbociclib](../drugs/palbociclib.md) beyond RB1-mutation-based selection [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md).
