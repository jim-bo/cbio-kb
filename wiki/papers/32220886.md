**PMID:** 32220886 · **DOI:** 10.1158/1078-0432.CCR-19-2563 · **Journal:** Clinical Cancer Research (2020)

## TL;DR

Pareja et al. performed whole-exome sequencing (WES; n=56) and MSK-IMPACT targeted sequencing (n=4) on 27 synchronously diagnosed ductal carcinomas in situ ([DCIS](../cancer_types/DCIS.md)) and 26 matched invasive ductal carcinomas of no special type ([IDC](../cancer_types/IDC.md)-NST) from 25 patients, plus 7 pure DCIS that did not progress, all from MSKCC. They show that DCIS displays a repertoire of somatic mutations and copy-number alterations indistinguishable from synchronous IDC-NSTs and from age/receptor-matched TCGA invasive breast cancers; in 92% (23/25) of patients the synchronous DCIS and IDC-NST were clonally related. Clonal decomposition (PyClone) identified clonal selection as the mechanism of DCIS-to-invasive progression in 28% (7/25) of cases — these DCIS had significantly higher intra-lesion genetic heterogeneity. Pure DCIS lacked [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) mutations entirely (0% vs 41%), suggesting they are less genetically advanced than DCIS synchronously diagnosed with invasion ([PMID:32220886](../papers/32220886.md)).

## Cohort & data

- **Patients / samples:** 25 patients with synchronous DCIS (n=27) and IDC-NST (n=26), plus 7 patients with pure DCIS (n=7). 56 samples were sequenced by [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md); 4 samples (Cases 3 and 9) were sequenced with the [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) targeted panel ([IMPACT410](../methods/IMPACT410.md) for Case 3, [IMPACT468](../methods/IMPACT468.md) for Case 9).
- **Tumor types:** Ductal carcinoma in situ ([DCIS](../cancer_types/DCIS.md)) and invasive ductal carcinoma of no special type ([IDC](../cancer_types/IDC.md)-NST), invasive breast carcinoma ([BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md)).
- **Institution / data release:** Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center; cBioPortal study [brca_pareja_msk_2020](../datasets/brca_pareja_msk_2020.md).
- **Sequencing depth:** WES median 186x (range 130–321x) tumor / 126x (range 103–239x) normal; MSK-IMPACT median 596x (range 525–720x) tumor / 549x (range 402–697x) normal.
- **Clinicopathology:** Median age 50 (range 26–76); 41% (14/34) of DCIS intermediate nuclear grade, 59% (20/34) high nuclear grade; 56% of synchronous DCIS and 54% of IDC-NSTs were ER+/HER2−.
- **Comparator cohort:** Age-, menopausal-status-, and ER/HER2-matched IDC-NSTs from [TCGA breast](../datasets/brca_tcga_pub.md) (1:3 ratio).

## Key findings

- **Mutational repertoires of synchronous DCIS and IDC-NST are concordant.** Median 37 non-synonymous somatic mutations per DCIS (range 16–308) versus 44 per IDC-NST (range 13–250). The most frequently mutated cancer genes overlapped: [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) 52% DCIS vs 54% IDC-NST (P>0.05); [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) 41% vs 42% (P>0.05); [GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md) 26% vs 23% (P>0.05). No statistically significant differences in mutational frequency of any cancer gene were observed (Fisher's exact test) ([PMID:32220886](../papers/32220886.md)).
- **PIK3CA hotspot pattern preserved across stages.** 9/12 PIK3CA mutations affected hotspot residues: H1047R/L (DCIS n=9; IDC-NST n=8) and E545D (IDC-NST n=1). 11/12 PIK3CA mutations in DCIS and 10/12 in IDC-NST were predicted to be clonal.
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md) loss is biallelic.** 13/14 DCIS and 12/14 IDC-NST TP53 mutations were inactivating; 12/13 DCIS and 12/14 IDC-NST were accompanied by LOH of the wild-type allele.
- **Mutational signatures.** Among samples with ≥40 SNVs, aging signatures 1/5 dominated in 63% (12/19) of synchronous DCIS and 58% (11/19) of IDC-NSTs; HRD-related signature 3 dominated in 21% (4/19) of DCIS and 26% (5/19) of IDC-NST; APOBEC signatures 2/13 dominated in 16% (3/19) each. Cases 2 ([BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) E23Vfs*17 germline), 19 (BRCA1 R496H germline), and 25 (somatic BRCA1 L1154Mfs*4) all displayed dominant signature 3 with BRCA1 LOH.
- **Genomic landscape is comparable to TCGA invasive cancers.** Synchronous DCIS had a comparable number of non-synonymous mutations to age-/receptor-matched IDC-NSTs from TCGA (n=75 matches; P>0.05, Mann-Whitney). Mutational frequencies of cancer genes were comparable for the full DCIS cohort (n=27) versus matched TCGA IDC-NSTs (n=81), and for ER+ DCIS (n=20) versus luminal B IDC-NSTs (n=60).
- **Pure DCIS lacks PIK3CA mutations entirely.** Pure DCIS (n=7) had numerically lower [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (14% vs 52%; P>0.05) and complete absence of [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) mutations (0% vs 41%; P>0.05) compared to synchronous DCIS (n=27). 5/6 pure DCIS displayed a dominant signature 1 (aging); 1/6 (40DCIS, with BRCA1 E908* germline) displayed signature 3.
- **Grade tracks with mutation burden.** Grade-3 DCIS (n=19) carried significantly more non-synonymous mutations than grade-2 DCIS (n=13; P<0.01). Grade-3 DCIS had 75% TP53 mutation frequency vs 0% in grade-2 (P<0.001).
- **DCIS and synchronous IDC-NST are usually clonally related.** Clonality index (SNV-based) showed 92% (23/25) of WES-profiled synchronous DCIS–IDC-NST pairs were clonally related. The two unrelated pairs (Cases 5 and 24) had their DCIS and IDC-NST located in different breast quadrants — consistent with independent origin.
- **Clonal selection drives progression in 28% of cases.** [PyClone](../methods/pyclone.md) clonal decomposition revealed minor DCIS subclones that became dominant in the matched IDC-NST in 7/25 patients (28%; Cases 6, 7, 14, 16, 18, 22, 28). In the remaining 18 cases there was no evidence of clonal selection; progression in those cases likely followed multiclonal-invasion or microenvironment-driven routes.
- **DCIS clones undergoing selection have higher intra-lesion heterogeneity.** Synchronous DCIS that evolved by clonal selection (n=7) had significantly higher Shannon and Gini-Simpson diversity indices than DCIS without clonal selection (n=18; both indices P<0.05, Mann-Whitney) and than pure DCIS (n=7; both P<0.05).
- **Whole-genome doubling (WGD) occurs early.** WGD was inferred by [FACETS](../methods/facets.md) and [ABSOLUTE](../methods/absolute.md) in 7 cases (7, 13, 17, 18, 22, 25, 26). In 3/7 (43%) WGD was truncal (present in both DCIS and IDC-NST); in 4/7 (57%) WGD was restricted to the IDC-NST component, supporting WGD as a possible progression mechanism in some cases.
- **Private invasive-restricted alterations include canonical drivers.** Mutations restricted to the IDC-NST included [GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md), [CHD4](../genes/CHD4.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md), [SOX2](../genes/SOX2.md), [SPEN](../genes/SPEN.md), [SMO](../genes/SMO.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [NCOA1](../genes/NCOA1.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [NBN](../genes/NBN.md), [GNAQ](../genes/GNAQ.md), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), and [TET2](../genes/TET2.md). IDC-NST–private amplifications included [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) (11q13.3–13.4), [BCL2](../genes/BCL2.md) (18q21.33-q22.1), [JAK1](../genes/JAK1.md) (19p13.11), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) (3q26.32-q26.33), [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) and [RAD21](../genes/RAD21.md) (8q23.3-q24.21), [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) and [NUTM1](../genes/NUTM1.md) (15q11.1-q26.1), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) (11p15.5), [MYCL](../genes/MYCL.md) (1p34.2), and [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md)/[CDK12](../genes/CDK12.md) (17q12).

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — mutated in 52% of synchronous DCIS and 54% of synchronous IDC-NSTs (P>0.05); 13/14 DCIS and 12/14 IDC-NST mutations were inactivating, almost always with LOH of the wild-type allele. 75% mutation frequency in grade-3 vs 0% in grade-2 DCIS (P<0.001). 14% in pure DCIS.
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — mutated in 41% of synchronous DCIS and 42% of IDC-NSTs (P>0.05); 9/12 mutations affected H1047R/L or E545D hotspots; 11/12 DCIS and 10/12 IDC-NST mutations were clonal. **0% in pure DCIS**, marking PIK3CA mutation as one of the few features that distinguishes synchronous from pure DCIS in this cohort.
- [GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md) — mutated in 26% of synchronous DCIS and 23% of IDC-NSTs; 6/7 DCIS and 6/6 IDC-NST mutations were loss-of-function (frame-shift, truncating, splice-site). Restricted to the IDC-NST in some individual cases. Numerically more frequent in ER+/HER2− DCIS (38%) than ER−/HER2− (0%) or HER2+ (14%).
- [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) — germline pathogenic variants E23Vfs*17 (Case 2), R496H (Case 19), and E908* (Case 40, a pure DCIS) plus somatic L1154Mfs*4 (Case 25), all associated with LOH and dominant signature-3 (HRD-related) profiles. Demonstrates that BRCA1-driven HRD is detectable already at the DCIS stage.
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) — recurrent 17q12 amplification in both synchronous DCIS and IDC-NST; significantly enriched in HER2+ DCIS vs ER+/HER2− DCIS (P<0.05). Case 7 carried a truncal [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) S310F hotspot mutation.
- [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) — IDC-NST–private 11q13.3–13.4 amplification in multiple cases, consistent with an invasive-stage-acquired event.
- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) — IDC-NST–private 8q23.3–24.21 amplification in multiple cases.
- [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md), [NUTM1](../genes/NUTM1.md), [MYCL](../genes/MYCL.md), [ERG](../genes/ERG.md) — invasive-restricted focal amplifications observed in individual cases.
- [KDM5C](../genes/KDM5C.md) — E185Q mutation in a 2DCISA subclone became dominant in 2DCISB, illustrating clonal selection within DCIS itself in a multifocal BRCA1-mutant case.
- [GNAS](../genes/GNAS.md), [SOX2](../genes/SOX2.md), [EZR](../genes/EZR.md) — case-specific private mutations defining individual IDC-NST subclones (Case 2 multifocal).

## Clinical implications

- **DCIS biomarkers must account for intra-lesion heterogeneity.** The authors argue that predictors of DCIS-to-invasive progression should incorporate measures of intra-lesion genetic diversity (Shannon / Gini-Simpson) in addition to gene-level alterations, because clonal-selection cases carried significantly higher heterogeneity ([PMID:32220886](../papers/32220886.md)).
- **Pure DCIS may be molecularly less advanced.** The complete absence of [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) mutations in pure DCIS (versus 41% in synchronous DCIS) suggests that PI3K-pathway activation is associated with — and possibly required for — the synchronous invasive phenotype. The authors caution this contrasts with [Lin et al. 2019](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31420779) and warrants larger, grade-stratified studies.
- **Geographic relationship matters for clonal relatedness.** When synchronous DCIS and IDC-NST were located in different breast quadrants (Cases 5B vs 5IDC; Case 24), they were genetically unrelated. This contrasts with LCIS/invasive lobular carcinoma, where clonal relatedness is independent of anatomic location.
- **No direct treatment claims.** The study is observational and does not test therapeutic interventions; no drug efficacy or biomarker-stratified treatment claims are made.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Restricted grade spectrum.** Only intermediate- and high-grade DCIS were included; low-grade DCIS was excluded by design. Findings may not generalize to low-grade lesions where alternative evolutionary pathways could dominate.
- **Small pure-DCIS cohort.** Only 7 pure DCIS cases were analyzed, limiting statistical power for cross-group comparisons (PIK3CA and TP53 differences did not reach significance despite striking effect sizes).
- **Bulk-sequencing inference.** Clonal decomposition relied on bulk WES processed through [PyClone](../methods/pyclone.md), [ABSOLUTE](../methods/absolute.md), [FACETS](../methods/facets.md), and MEDICC. The authors note their bulk-derived phylogenies are consistent with single-cell studies (Casasent 2018, Martelotto 2017) but acknowledge bulk inference's inherent limits.
- **Coding-only scope.** Only protein-coding alterations were considered; non-coding-region drivers of progression are unexplored.
- **Discordance with prior work.** Several prior reports (5q31.1–5q35.3 / 6q25.3–6q26 / 13q losses in DCIS; 1q/8q/11q gain enrichment in synchronous vs pure DCIS) could not be confirmed here, likely reflecting cohort-size and grade-composition differences.
- **Mechanism of "no clonal selection."** In the 72% of cases without clonal selection, it remains unclear whether progression was driven by early acquisition of invasion-competence, multiclonal invasion, or microenvironmental cues — none directly testable in this design.
