**PMID:** 29337640 · **DOI:** [10.1200/JCO.2017.75.3384](https://doi.org/10.1200/JCO.2017.75.3384) · **Journal:** Journal of Clinical Oncology (2018)

## TL;DR

Rizvi et al. retrospectively analyzed 240 patients with advanced [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) treated with anti–PD-(L)1 therapy at MSKCC and profiled by the targeted [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/IMPACT468.md) panel. Tumor mutation burden (TMB) measured on the targeted panel correlated tightly with whole-exome estimates (Spearman r = 0.86, P < .001) and was significantly higher in patients with durable clinical benefit (DCB) than in those with no durable benefit (NDB). High TMB and PD-L1 expression were independent predictors of benefit; combining them further enriched for response (50% DCB when both high, vs 18% when both low). Mutations in [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) and [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) associated with lack of benefit, and high fraction of copy-number–altered genome ([FGA](../genes/FGA.md)) was enriched in non-responders. The work established that targeted NGS panels routinely deployed in the clinic can reliably [estimate](../methods/estimate.md) TMB and serve as a practical immunotherapy biomarker.

## Cohort & data

- **240 patients** with advanced [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) treated with anti–PD-1 or anti–PD-L1 therapy (alone or with anti–CTLA-4) at Memorial Sloan Kettering between April 2011 and January 2017, all profiled by [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/IMPACT468.md) targeted NGS ([PMID:29337640](../papers/29337640.md)).
- **Histology:** 78% adenocarcinoma ([LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md)), 14% squamous ([LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md)), 8% other.
- **Treatment:** 86% PD-(L)1 monotherapy, 14% PD-(L)1 + CTLA-4 combination ([nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md), [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md), [atezolizumab](../drugs/atezolizumab.md), [durvalumab](../drugs/durvalumab.md), [ipilimumab](../drugs/ipilimumab.md)).
- **Panel versions:** [IMPACT341](../methods/IMPACT341.md) (56 patients), [IMPACT410](../methods/IMPACT410.md) (164), [IMPACT468](../methods/IMPACT468.md) (20). Mean coverage 744×.
- **Validation subset:** 49 patients had paired [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-sequencing.md).
- **PD-L1 IHC** available for 84 patients (22C3, 28-8, E1L3N antibodies).
- **Comparison cohort:** 1,836 non–ICI-treated [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) patients sequenced on MSK-IMPACT 2014–2017 ([dataset: nsclc_pd1_msk_2018](../datasets/nsclc_pd1_msk_2018.md)).
- **Endpoints:** RECIST 1.1; durable clinical benefit (DCB) = CR/PR/SD lasting > 6 months; no durable benefit (NDB) = PD or SD ≤ 6 months. 49 (20%) had CR/PR; 69 (29%) had DCB; 158 (66%) NDB; 13 (5%) not evaluable.

## Key findings

- **Targeted NGS reliably quantifies TMB.** TMB by MSK-IMPACT correlated with TMB by WES at Spearman r = 0.86 (P < .001, n = 49) ([PMID:29337640](../papers/29337640.md)).
- **TMB associates with benefit.** Median TMB 8.5 SNVs/Mb in DCB vs 6.6 in NDB (P = .006); 8.5 vs 6.6 vs 6.6 across CR/PR vs SD vs PD (P = .049 / .015).
- **Dose–response with TMB.** DCB rate above the 50th percentile was 38.6% vs 25.1% below (P = .009). PFS HR for above- vs below-median TMB = 1.38, P = .024. Odds ratios of DCB rose stepwise across TMB percentiles: 25th OR 1.75, 50th OR 2.02, 75th OR 2.06, 90th OR 3.24.
- **TMB is predictive, not prognostic.** In the non–ICI-treated comparison cohort, higher TMB was associated with *worse* survival, ruling out a generic prognostic effect.
- **FGA is highest in non-responders.** Fraction of copy-number–altered genome was significantly higher in NDB than in non-ICI NSCLC (median 0.16 vs 0.11; P = .007). FGA and TMB were modestly positively correlated despite their opposite associations with response.
- **TMB and PD-L1 are independent.** No correlation between TMB and PD-L1% (Spearman r = 0.19, P = .08). AUC for predicting DCB: TMB 0.601, PD-L1 0.646. A composite of TMB-high + PD-L1 ≥ 1% gave 50% DCB rate, vs 18% when both were low (n = 22).
- **[EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) mutations underrepresented in DCB** (7% of EGFR-mutant patients had DCB; FDR-adjusted P = .013 vs non-ICI cohort).
- **[STK11](../genes/STK11.md) mutations enriched in NDB** (FDR-adjusted P = .007 vs non-ICI cohort).
- **[B2M](../genes/B2M.md) / JAK pathway hits rare in this cohort.** Likely-deleterious B2M mutation: 1 patient (S40* in trans with Q28L, with loss of B2M expression by IHC) — paradoxically had ongoing PR at 8.9 months. Homozygous deleterious [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md) mutation: 1 patient with PD.
- **MDM2/MDM4 amplification (n = 8) did not show distinct hyperprogression** in this series (HR 1.4, P = .44), in contrast to prior reports.

## Genes & alterations

- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) — Activating mutations in 17 patients (7%). Significantly underrepresented in the DCB group; only 7% of EGFR-mutant patients achieved DCB. Authors attribute this to the link between EGFR mutations, never-smoker status, and consequent low TMB ([PMID:29337640](../papers/29337640.md)).
- [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) — Significantly enriched in NDB vs non-ICI NSCLC (FDR-adjusted P = .007). Consistent with STK11/LKB1-loss murine and human reports of low tumor inflammation.
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — Mutated in 83 patients; DCB rate (36%) similar to overall cohort. [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) mutation alone did not stratify response.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — Altered in 67% of DCB and 54% of NDB tumors (descriptive, no enrichment claim).
- [B2M](../genes/B2M.md) — Truncating mutations rare; one patient with biallelic deleterious B2M (S40* + Q28L) and confirmed loss of B2M IHC nonetheless had a PR ongoing at 8.9 months and TMB of 48 SNVs/Mb.
- [JAK1](../genes/JAK1.md) / [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md) — Few events overall; one patient with homozygous loss-of-function JAK2 splice mutation + LOH had primary resistance (PD), consistent with interferon-gamma signaling defects described in melanoma.
- [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) / [MDM4](../genes/MDM4.md) — Amplifications identified in 8 patients; PFS not significantly different from the overall cohort (HR 1.4, P = .44). Hyperprogression signal previously reported elsewhere was not reproduced here.
- Other actionable drivers reported with low frequency: [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) (1%), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) (2%), [ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md) (3%), [RET](../genes/RET.md) (1%), [MET](../genes/MET.md) (3%), [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) — too few events for response analysis.
- Antigen-presentation / immunology genes profiled in the OncoPrint: [HLA-A](../genes/HLA-A.md), [POLE](../genes/POLE.md), [JAK3](../genes/JAK3.md), [CD274](../genes/CD274.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [ATR](../genes/ATR.md).

## Clinical implications

- **Targeted NGS panels (≥ ~1 Mb of coding sequence) can replace WES for TMB estimation in routine practice** — directly supports clinical adoption of MSK-IMPACT and similar assays as immunotherapy biomarkers ([PMID:29337640](../papers/29337640.md)).
- **TMB and PD-L1 should be combined, not substituted.** Their AUCs for DCB are comparable but the variables are independent; a composite (TMB > median + PD-L1 ≥ 1%) selects a subgroup with 50% DCB.
- **EGFR-mutant and STK11-mutant NSCLC are enriched in non-responders to anti–PD-(L)1 therapy** — supporting use of these alterations as negative selectors when considering single-agent ICI.
- **No universal TMB cut point** is recommended; appropriate thresholds are panel- and assay-specific. The authors caution against transferring numerical cutoffs across platforms.
- **Hyperprogression with MDM2/MDM4 amplification was not reproduced**, leaving the prior signal an open question rather than a confirmed contraindication.

## Limitations & open questions

- Moderate sample size (n = 240) limits power, particularly for subgroup and multivariable analyses.
- Retrospective clinical outcome adjudication for the non-trial subset, though authors argue the mixed real-world + trial composition improves generalizability.
- Single-institution, single-panel design; the [absolute](../methods/absolute.md) TMB cutpoints derived here are not portable across panels of different sizes or informatics pipelines (caution noted for panels < 0.5 Mb).
- Only 85% of MSK-IMPACT samples were collected pre-immunotherapy; the analysis was not designed to study acquired resistance or selection pressure on B2M/JAK genes.
- PD-L1 testing used three different antibodies (22C3, 28-8, E1L3N) — possible inter-assay variability despite reported concordance.
- Targeted panels lack the immunogenomic genes (HLA, antigen-processing machinery beyond a handful) that may be biologically central; authors call for panel expansion and continued WES/WGS discovery work.
- Mechanism of MDM2/MDM4 hyperprogression and the apparent discordance with prior reports remains unresolved.
