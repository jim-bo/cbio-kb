# PCM1

## Overview

PCM1 (Pericentriolar Material 1) encodes a scaffold protein of the pericentriolar material, critical for centriole biogenesis and ciliogenesis. In cancer genomics, PCM1 has been identified as a cancer-associated gene in significant copy-number-loss peaks, suggesting it may act as a tumor suppressor in certain contexts.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- PCM1 is among representative cancer-associated genes inside significant copy-number-loss peaks (GISTIC2.0) in driverless tumor cases within the PCAWG pan-cancer cohort of 2,658 whole genomes spanning 38 tumor types [PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- Pan-cancer: PCM1 copy-number loss identified in driverless tumor cases across PCAWG tumor types; specific cancer type enrichment not detailed [PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- PCM1 co-occurs in the same GISTIC2.0 copy-number-loss peaks as [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) and [FANCA](../genes/FANCA.md) in driverless PCAWG tumors [PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No therapeutic implications reported in the corpus.

## Open questions

- Whether PCM1 copy-number loss drives tumor biology in these driverless cases or is a passenger event has not been resolved.
