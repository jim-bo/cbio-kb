**PMID:** 30290956 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.eururo.2018.09.002 · **Journal:** European Urology (2019)

## TL;DR

Pietzak et al. compared clinical and genomic features of "primary" versus "secondary" muscle-invasive bladder cancer (MIBC) — the latter defined as MIBC arising after prior non–muscle-invasive bladder cancer (NMIBC) treatment. In a clinical cohort of 288 MSKCC patients receiving cisplatin-based neoadjuvant chemotherapy (NAC) followed by radical cystectomy, secondary MIBC patients had markedly lower pathologic response rates (26% vs 45%) and worse recurrence-free, cancer-specific, and overall survival than primary MIBC patients. Genomic analysis of a 385-tumor retrospective discovery cohort (TCGA + MSKCC) and an independent 94-tumor MSK-IMPACT validation cohort showed that likely deleterious [ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md) missense mutations — a known cisplatin-sensitivity biomarker — were significantly enriched in primary versus secondary MIBC (10.9% vs 1.8% in discovery; 15.7% vs 0% in validation). The authors conclude that secondary MIBC patients derive little benefit from standard cisplatin-based NAC and should be considered for upfront cystectomy or clinical trials.

## Cohort & data

- **Clinical cohort:** 288 MSKCC patients with clinical stage cT2–4aN0M0 [bladder urothelial carcinoma (BLCA)](../cancer_types/BLCA.md) treated with three to four cycles of cisplatin-based NAC followed by radical cystectomy between May 2001 and July 2015 — 245 primary MIBC (85%) and 43 secondary MIBC (15%) [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- **Retrospective discovery genomic cohort:** 385 muscle-invasive (≥T2) chemotherapy-naïve pretreatment specimens (330 primary, 55 secondary) drawn from 981 NGS-sequenced tumors — 412 by [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) through the urothelial TCGA and 569 sequenced at MSKCC by WES or targeted exon capture [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- **Prospective validation genomic cohort:** 94 consecutive chemotherapy-naïve MIBC tumors (70 primary, 24 secondary) prospectively sequenced 2014–2015 on the [MSK-IMPACT panel](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) (341- or 410-gene versions) in a CLIA-certified laboratory under MSKCC IRB #12–245 / NCT01775072 [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- **Assay:** [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) targeted exon capture across three panel generations covering 300, 341, or 410 cancer-associated genes — [IMPACT300](../methods/IMPACT300.md), [IMPACT341](../methods/IMPACT341.md), [IMPACT410](../methods/IMPACT410.md). Data on [FANCC](../genes/FANCC.md) were missing for six patients sequenced using the 300-gene panel [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- **Dataset:** [blca_msk_tcga_2020](../datasets/blca_msk_tcga_2020.md) — combined MSK + TCGA bladder cancer cohort with data publicly available at cbioportal.mskcc.org [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).

## Key findings

- **Lower pathologic response in secondary MIBC.** 26% (11/43) of secondary MIBC vs 45% (110/245) of primary MIBC achieved pathologic response (≤pT1N0) following NAC (univariable p=0.02). Multivariable analysis adjusted for age, sex, and T stage gave OR=0.4 (95% CI 0.18–0.84, p=0.02) [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- **Worse survival outcomes in secondary MIBC.** Secondary MIBC was associated with significantly worse recurrence-free survival (RFS) (univariable p<0.001; multivariable HR=2.10, 95% CI 1.23–3.57, p=0.007), cancer-specific survival (CSS) (univariable p=0.004; multivariable p=0.054), and overall survival (univariable p=0.006; multivariable HR=1.66, 95% CI 1.01–2.73, p=0.048) [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- **NAC actively harmful in secondary MIBC.** In multivariable analysis, NAC was associated with improved RFS vs cystectomy alone in primary MIBC (p=0.042) but with *worse* RFS (p=0.007), CSS (p=0.002), and [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) (p=0.013) in secondary MIBC [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- **Delay to cystectomy in secondary MIBC.** Median time from MIBC diagnosis to cystectomy was 5.8 vs 1.7 months (p<0.001), potentially increasing micrometastatic spread in this chemo-refractory population [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- **[ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md) missense mutations enriched in primary MIBC (discovery cohort).** 10.9% (36/330) primary vs 1.8% (1/55) secondary (p=0.044). Among the four pre-specified DDR/NAC-response genes ([ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [FANCC](../genes/FANCC.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md)), only [ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md) reached significance [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- **[ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md) finding replicated in validation cohort.** 15.7% (12/70 — also reported as 17.1% later in text) primary vs 0% (0/24) secondary, p=0.033 in the prospective MSK-IMPACT validation set [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- **Combined cohort confirms enrichment.** Pooling both cohorts: ERCC2 missense in 12% (48/400) primary vs 1.3% (1/79) secondary MIBC (unadjusted p=0.004). Mutations clustered near or within conserved helicase domains [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- **Other gene-level differences (combined, univariable only).** [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) alterations were enriched in primary MIBC (23% vs 10%, p=0.013); inactivating [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md) (7.8% vs 17%, p=0.014) and [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md) (5.5% vs 14%, p=0.007) alterations were enriched in secondary MIBC. None remained significant after Benjamini-Hochberg correction [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- **Actionable alterations in secondary MIBC.** RTK/MAPK pathway alterations in 42% of secondary MIBC, with [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) (18%) and [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) (15%) among the most commonly altered genes. TP53-pathway and cell-cycle regulatory gene alterations in 61% and 65%, respectively, of secondary MIBC [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- **[FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)/[CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) coalteration not significantly different.** 10% (8/79) secondary vs 5.0% (20/400) primary, unadjusted p=0.08. Coalteration of [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) with any cell-cycle regulatory gene ([RB1](../genes/RB1.md), E2F2, [CDKN1A](../genes/CDKN1A.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md), [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md), [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md)) was also not significant (13% vs 6.8%, p=0.07) [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- **[ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md)-mutant tumors carry higher tumor mutation burden.** WES: 14.7 vs 5.5 mutations/Mb (p<0.001); targeted capture: 25.0 vs 8.5 mutations/Mb (p<0.001) for mutant vs wild-type [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- **Primary MIBC has higher TMB than secondary by targeted capture only.** 11.2 vs 7.7 mutations/Mb (p=0.012) by targeted exon capture; not confirmed by WES (6.1 vs 6.1, p=0.8) [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- **No molecular-subtype enrichment.** Unsupervised clustering of TCGA gene expression data using two published luminal/basal classifier sets showed no enrichment of intrinsic molecular subtypes in primary vs secondary MIBC [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).

## Genes & alterations

- [ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md) — Missense mutations clustering near/within conserved helicase domains. Significantly enriched in primary versus secondary MIBC across both cohorts and in combined analysis. Hypothesized mechanism: NER-pathway defect heightens [cisplatin](../drugs/cisplatin.md) sensitivity. Also associated with higher TMB in both WES and targeted capture data [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) — Pre-specified DDR/NAC-response gene; no significant difference between primary and secondary MIBC in the discovery cohort [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- [FANCC](../genes/FANCC.md) — Pre-specified DDR/NAC-response gene; no significant difference. Missing data for 6 patients on the 300-gene MSK-IMPACT panel [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — Pre-specified DDR/NAC-response gene; no significant difference between groups. Included among cell-cycle regulators tested for FGFR3 co-alteration [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) — Alterations enriched in primary MIBC (23% vs 10%, univariable p=0.013); did not survive FDR correction [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md) — Inactivating alterations enriched in secondary MIBC (17% vs 7.8%, univariable p=0.014); did not survive FDR correction [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md) — Inactivating alterations enriched in secondary MIBC (14% vs 5.5%, univariable p=0.007); did not survive FDR correction [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) — Altered in 18% of secondary MIBC; framed as a potentially actionable target for FGFR-axis small-molecule inhibitors in this chemo-refractory subgroup [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) — Altered in 15% of secondary MIBC; highlighted as actionable [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — Tested for co-alteration with FGFR3; no significant difference between primary and secondary MIBC [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) pathway alterations in 61% of secondary MIBC (not formally compared between groups) [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).

## Clinical implications

- **Cisplatin-based NAC may be ineffective or harmful in secondary MIBC.** Secondary MIBC patients had worse CSS following NAC than after cystectomy alone (p=0.002), inverting the standard-of-care rationale [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- **Upfront radical cystectomy or clinical-trial enrollment should be considered for secondary MIBC.** The authors argue that, until a molecular biomarker of NAC sensitivity is validated for routine use, prior NMIBC history may serve as a clinical surrogate to identify patients who could benefit from skipping NAC [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- **[ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md) missense mutations remain a candidate predictive biomarker for cisplatin response in [BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md).** The enrichment of these mutations in primary versus secondary MIBC offers a molecular explanation for the differential chemosensitivity [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- **Secondary MIBC may be a target population for targeted-therapy trials.** [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) (18%) and [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) (15%) alterations and 42% RTK/MAPK pathway activation make secondary MIBC a candidate cohort for FGFR-axis or HER2-directed agents in the neoadjuvant setting [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- **Possible BCG-mediated selective pressure.** The authors speculate that intravesical [bacille Calmette-Guérin (BCG)](../drugs/bcg.md) used in NMIBC may preferentially eradicate ERCC2-mutant subclones (via mutational/neoantigen burden-driven immune activation or oxidative DNA damage), enriching for chemo-refractory ERCC2-wild-type clones at MIBC progression [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Retrospective clinical cohort with selection bias.** Patients were not randomized to NAC vs cystectomy-alone arms; NAC selection within primary vs secondary MIBC was non-protocolized [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- **Outside-institution NMIBC management.** Most secondary MIBC patients received initial NMIBC treatment elsewhere, so the authors could not control for inadequacy or delay of prior intravesical therapy [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- **DDR focus may miss other mechanisms.** Germline, epigenetic, and transcriptomic factors that modulate or substitute for DDR function were not examined; molecular drivers of cisplatin sensitivity in DDR wild-type patients remain undefined [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- **Limited power for molecular-subtype analysis.** Cohort size was insufficient to detect modest enrichments of luminal/basal subtypes between primary and secondary MIBC [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- **No significance after multiple-testing correction for non-ERCC2 genes.** [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md), and [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md) findings remain hypothesis-generating only [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- **Prospective validation needed.** The authors call out SWOG1314 (COXEN) and the Alliance 017301 bladder-sparing trial as venues where these findings could be tested prospectively [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
