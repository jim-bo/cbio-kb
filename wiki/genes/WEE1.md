# WEE1

## Overview

WEE1 is a nuclear kinase that phosphorylates and inactivates [CDK1](../genes/CDK1.md) (and [CDK2](../genes/CDK2.md)) to enforce the G2/M checkpoint, preventing mitotic entry before DNA repair is complete. It is a key regulator of cell-cycle progression and a validated therapeutic target; WEE1 inhibitors (e.g., adavosertib/AZD1775) are in clinical trials for multiple solid tumors. In lung adenocarcinoma, WEE1 has been identified as a hyperphosphorylated outlier kinase specifically in EML4-ALK-fusion tumors, linking it to ALK-driven oncogenesis.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Identified as a hyperphosphorylated outlier kinase (FDR <0.20) specifically in EML4-ALK-fusion [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) tumors (CPTAC proteogenomic cohort, n=110 treatment-naïve tumors), without corresponding copy-number, RNA, or protein-level changes; nominated as a druggable target with existing non-FDA-approved inhibitors in this fusion subtype [PMID:32649874](../papers/32649874.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md): hyperphosphorylated outlier kinase in EML4-ALK-fusion tumors; nominated as a therapeutic vulnerability [PMID:32649874](../papers/32649874.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Outlier phosphorylation co-occurs with EML4-ALK fusion events in [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) [PMID:32649874](../papers/32649874.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- WEE1 inhibition is nominated for EML4-ALK-fusion [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) based on driver-specific outlier phosphorylation in the CPTAC cohort; multiple WEE1 inhibitors exist but none are FDA-approved in this indication [PMID:32649874](../papers/32649874.md)
