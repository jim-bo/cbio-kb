# HDAC1

## Overview

HDAC1 (Histone Deacetylase 1) is a class I histone deacetylase that removes acetyl groups from lysine residues on histone tails, leading to chromatin compaction and transcriptional repression. It is a component of co-repressor complexes (NuRD, Sin3A, CoREST) and is widely implicated in cancer epigenetics. In pancreatic cancer proteogenomics, HDAC1 phosphorylation is regulated by [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) copy-number status.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- HDAC1 phosphorylation at S406/S410/S421 is upregulated as a trans-effect of SMAD4 deletions in the CPTAC PDAC proteogenomic cohort (n=140 treatment-naive tumors; 105 high-purity); this phosphoregulation suggests SMAD4 loss activates HDAC1-mediated chromatin remodeling programs in PDAC. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md):** HDAC1 phosphosites regulated by SMAD4 copy-number status, implicating HDAC1 in SMAD4-loss biology in PDAC. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Phospho-HDAC1 elevation co-occurs with phospho-SMARCA4 (S613) and phospho-CREBBP (S274) as downstream trans-effects of SMAD4 deletion. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- HDAC inhibitors are a broad class of epigenetic therapies; HDAC1 phosphoregulation by SMAD4 status may provide a rationale for HDAC inhibitor use in SMAD4-deleted PDAC subsets, though this is not directly tested in the cited study. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Open questions

- Whether elevated phospho-HDAC1 in SMAD4-deleted tumors is causal to disease progression or a compensatory response is not established.
- Direct testing of HDAC inhibitors in SMAD4-null PDAC models not performed in the corpus.
