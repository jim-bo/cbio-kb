# LDB1

## Overview

LDB1 (LIM Domain Binding 1) encodes a nuclear co-regulator involved in transcriptional complex assembly, including participation in the N-CoR (nuclear receptor co-repressor) complex. In medulloblastoma, LDB1 harbors missense and nonsense mutations in the [SHH](../genes/SHH.md) subgroup with hemizygous loss facilitated by 10q chromosomal loss, implicating N-CoR complex disruption as a driver mechanism in SHH-subtype tumors.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Missense and nonsense mutations in SHH-subgroup medulloblastoma; hemizygous via 10q loss; role in N-CoR complex assembly identified by WES (Broad, 92 tumors) [PMID:22820256](../papers/22820256.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **MB (medulloblastoma):** Mutations restricted to [SHH](../genes/SHH.md) subtype; hemizygous loss due to 10q deletion; implicated in N-CoR co-repressor complex disruption [PMID:22820256](../papers/22820256.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Mutations co-occur with other N-CoR complex members ([GPS2](../genes/GPS2.md), [NCOR2](../genes/NCOR2.md)) in medulloblastoma, suggesting convergent disruption of the N-CoR transcriptional repressor complex [PMID:22820256](../papers/22820256.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- N-CoR complex disruption across [GPS2](../genes/GPS2.md), LDB1, and [NCOR2](../genes/NCOR2.md) in medulloblastoma highlights potential sensitivity to epigenetic therapies targeting transcriptional co-repressor pathways.

## Open questions

- Whether LDB1 mutations are independent drivers in SHH medulloblastoma or require co-occurring PTCH1/SUFU loss requires functional studies.
- The mechanism by which LDB1 loss disrupts N-CoR complex assembly and transcriptional repression in cerebellar development is not fully elucidated.
