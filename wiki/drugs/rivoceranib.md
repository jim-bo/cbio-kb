# rivoceranib

## Overview

Rivoceranib (apatinib) is an oral small-molecule tyrosine kinase inhibitor that selectively targets VEGFR2 ([KDR](../genes/KDR.md)). It is approved in combination with [camrelizumab](../drugs/camrelizumab.md) (CARES-310 regimen) for first-line unresectable [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md).

## Evidence in the corpus

- A narrative review of lipid metabolism in HCC cites camrelizumab + rivoceranib (CARES-310) as one of the approved ICI + anti-angiogenic combinations for HCC; lipid-metabolic reprogramming involving enhanced lipid uptake, de novo lipogenesis, and suppression of ferroptosis is proposed as a cross-resistance mechanism for VEGFR-targeting agents including rivoceranib. [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Resistance mechanisms

- Not specifically characterized in the corpus; cross-resistance via lipid-metabolic reprogramming (FATP4, [SCD](../genes/SCD.md), GPX4/SLC7A11 axes) proposed for VEGFR inhibitors in HCC. [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)
