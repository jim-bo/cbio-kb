# Adenoid Cystic Carcinoma (MGH, Nature Genetics 2016)

## Overview

The acyc_mgh_2016 cohort was generated by Drier et al. at Massachusetts General Hospital and Dana-Farber Cancer Institute (published in *Nature Genetics* 2016). It comprises 20 adenoid cystic carcinomas (ACCs) analyzed by whole-genome sequencing and chromatin profiling to characterize the mechanism of recurrent [MYB](../genes/MYB.md) rearrangements. New sequencing data from this study are deposited at EGA under accession `EGAS00001001457`; 12 primary tumors were drawn from the previously published EGA dataset `EGAS00001000030`. [PMID:26829750](../papers/26829750.md)

## Composition

- 12 primary ACC tumors (from EGA `EGAS00001000030`).
- 2 additional primary ACC tumors sequenced by targeted paired-end sequencing.
- 6 patient-derived primagraft (PDX) models.
- Cancer type: salivary-gland [ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md) (adenoid cystic carcinoma).
- Histologic grades: grade-2 (cribriform/tubular) and grade-3 (solid) represented.
- Immunohistochemistry on 19 grade-2 ACCs and 8 grade-3 ACCs. [PMID:26829750](../papers/26829750.md)

## Assays / panels (linked)

- [whole-genome-seq](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md): Illumina HiSeq 2500, 100 bp paired-end; aligned to hg19 with BWA; rearrangements called with dRanger and BreakPointer.
- [chip-seq](../methods/chip-seq.md): H3K4me3, H3K27ac, [BRD4](../genes/BRD4.md) ChIP-seq in 13 ACCs (5 primary + 8 primagrafts); MYB and [TP63](../genes/TP63.md) ChIP-seq in 3 primagrafts.
- [immunohistochemistry](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md): MYB, TP63, ICN1 (active [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md)), [KIT](../genes/KIT.md), Ki-67 across grade-2 and grade-3 ACC specimens.
- In vivo drug testing (4 primagrafts in nude mice): BET inhibitor [JQ1](../drugs/jq1.md) at 50 mg/kg daily. [PMID:26829750](../papers/26829750.md)

## Papers using this cohort

- [PMID:26829750](../papers/26829750.md) — Drier et al. 2016, *Nature Genetics*: Primary study demonstrating that MYB rearrangements act via super-enhancer hijacking rather than fusion-protein production; BET bromodomain inhibitor JQ1 effective in grade-2 but not grade-3 ACC primagrafts.

## Notable findings derived from this cohort

- 15/20 ACCs (75%) harbored [MYB](../genes/MYB.md) rearrangements: 12/20 via [NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) (6 with MYB 3′UTR loss, 6 with retained 3′UTR), 2/20 via [TGFBR3](../genes/TGFBR3.md), 1/20 via [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md). [PMID:26829750](../papers/26829750.md)
- Rearrangements act by juxtaposing super-enhancers from [NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md), [TGFBR3](../genes/TGFBR3.md), or [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md) to the MYB locus — not by producing a fusion protein — establishing a MYB-driven positive feedback loop confirmed by 3C and ChIP-seq. [PMID:26829750](../papers/26829750.md)
- MYB ChIP-seq (3 primagrafts) identified 13,278 high-confidence MYB binding sites; second-ranked binding motif is TP53/[TP63](../genes/TP63.md)/TP73 (p<10⁻³⁴⁰); 81% of TP63 binding sites co-bound by MYB. [PMID:26829750](../papers/26829750.md)
- [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) activating mutations or [SPEN](../genes/SPEN.md) loss-of-function found in 7/9 grade-3 ACCs but none of the lower-grade tumors; TP63 and ICN1 IHC are mutually exclusive and stratify tumor grade. [PMID:26829750](../papers/26829750.md)
- BET bromodomain inhibitor JQ1 significantly slowed tumor growth in 2 grade-2 ACC primagrafts; both grade-3 (Notch-activated) primagrafts failed to respond — supporting grade-stratified therapeutic strategy. [PMID:26829750](../papers/26829750.md)
