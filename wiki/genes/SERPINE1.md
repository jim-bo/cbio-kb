# SERPINE1

## Overview

SERPINE1 (Serpin Family E Member 1), also known as PAI-1 (plasminogen activator inhibitor-1), encodes a serine protease inhibitor that regulates fibrinolysis and is a downstream transcriptional target of the TGF-β/SMAD4 signaling pathway. In cancer, SERPINE1 expression is linked to tumor progression, invasion, and metastasis, and it is regulated by [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) in pancreatic and other cancers.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- SERPINE1 mRNA and protein are downregulated by SMAD4 in PDAC; SMAD4 deletions (29% of PDAC) are associated with elevated SERPINE1 levels in a proteogenomic study of treatment-naive resected pancreatic adenocarcinoma [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Pancreatic adenocarcinoma ([PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md)): SERPINE1 protein levels regulated downstream of SMAD4 status [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- SERPINE1 expression inversely correlated with SMAD4 alteration status in PDAC [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting data in the current corpus.

## Open questions

- Whether SERPINE1 upregulation in SMAD4-deleted PDAC contributes to the pro-metastatic phenotype and whether it can serve as a therapeutic target or biomarker requires further investigation.
