# Lung Neuroendocrine Tumor (LNET)

## Overview

Lung Neuroendocrine Tumor (LNET) is a well-differentiated neuroendocrine neoplasm of the lung (pulmonary NEN / PulNET) under Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer in OncoTree (parent: LUNG). Large cell neuroendocrine carcinoma ([LUNE](../cancer_types/LUNE.md)) is a high-grade subtype under LNET.

## Cohorts in the corpus

- [pog570_bcgsc_2020](../datasets/pog570_bcgsc_2020.md) — 7 metastatic pulmonary NENs (PulNENs) within 28-patient metastatic NEN WGTA cohort at BC Cancer POG. [PMID:24326773](../papers/24326773.md)

## Recurrent alterations

- Whole-genome and transcriptome analysis of 7 metastatic pulmonary NENs confirmed recurrent [MEN1](../genes/MEN1.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) alterations. MEN1-mutant PulNENs, unlike MEN1-mutant PanNENs, did not show large-scale LOH. PulNENs predominantly clustered in transcriptome Cluster C. Median TMB was 2.19 mut/Mb across the 28-patient NEN cohort. [PMID:24326773](../papers/24326773.md)

## Subtypes

- [LUNE](../cancer_types/LUNE.md) — Large Cell Neuroendocrine Carcinoma (high-grade, parent: LNET)

## Therapeutic landscape

- [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md) inhibitors (via MEN1 loss), somatostatin analogs (via SSTR expression), and FGFR3-directed therapy flagged as actionable in PulNEN cases via WGTA. [PMID:24326773](../papers/24326773.md)
