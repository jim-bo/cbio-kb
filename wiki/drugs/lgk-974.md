# lgk-974

## Overview

LGK-974 is a potent, selective inhibitor of porcupine (PORCN), the O-acyltransferase required for Wnt ligand palmitoylation and secretion. It blocks autocrine and paracrine Wnt signaling at the ligand level, upstream of receptor binding.

## Evidence in the corpus

- In a study of colitis-associated cancers (CAC, n=174 patients), pharmacologic inhibition of Wnt ligand signaling with LGK-974 in three CAC patient-derived xenografts (PDXs) did not suppress tumor growth, providing functional confirmation that CAC — including APC-mutant tumors — has undergone transcriptional rewiring away from Wnt ligand dependence and grows independently of exogenous Wnt supplementation. This is in contrast to sporadic colorectal cancer, which is typically Wnt-addicted. [PMID:36611031](../papers/36611031.md)

## Resistance mechanisms

- Wnt-pathway–independent growth: CAC organoids and PDXs retain growth in the absence of Wnt ligands and are insensitive to porcupine inhibition, suggesting that the Wnt-independence in CAC is intrinsic rather than acquired resistance. [PMID:36611031](../papers/36611031.md)
