**PMID:** 33509808 · **DOI:** 10.1158/1078-0432.CCR-20-4189 · **Journal:** Clinical Cancer Research (2021)

## TL;DR

Shoushtari and colleagues retrospectively analyzed 696 patients with cutaneous or unknown-primary melanoma prospectively sequenced with MSK-IMPACT (341/410/468-gene panels) between January 2014 and April 2019. Oncogenic RTK-RAS-MAPK pathway alterations in one of 28 genes were detected in 670 tumors (96%), and 33% harbored more than one driver. The authors built a 9-group MAPK driver classifier and showed that time to treatment failure (TTF) on frontline PD-1 monotherapy varied sharply by driver group (median 4.2 mo [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) Q61, 7.5 mo [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600, 22 mo [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), not reached for "Other"; p<0.0001, N=181), independent of TMB, ECOG status, and AJCC stage. No such effect was seen for [nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md)+[ipilimumab](../drugs/ipilimumab.md) (N=141). Among 172 BRAF V600 wild-type patients progressing through checkpoint blockade, 27 received genomically matched therapy and 8 (30%) had ≥6 months of benefit, with durable complete responses to [crizotinib](../drugs/crizotinib.md) ([ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md) fusion), [larotrectinib](../drugs/larotrectinib.md) ([NTRK1](../genes/NTRK1.md) fusion), [trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md) (BRAF K601E + [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md) E203K), and PLX8394 (BRAF-AGK fusion).

## Cohort & data

- **Patients:** 696 unique patients with cutaneous melanoma (556, 80%) or melanoma of unknown primary (140, 20%) sequenced at MSKCC and Lehigh Valley between Jan 2014 and Apr 2019; 792 tumors analyzed, 756 (95.4%) sequenced successfully, 696 met inclusion criteria.
- **Cancer types:** [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md), [CUP](../cancer_types/CUP.md).
- **Dataset:** [mel_mskimpact_2020](../datasets/mel_mskimpact_2020.md) — available at `http://cbioportal.org/study?id=mel_mskimpact_2020`.
- **Assay/panel:** [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md), capture-based NGS in 3 versions — [IMPACT341](../methods/IMPACT341.md), [IMPACT410](../methods/IMPACT410.md), [IMPACT468](../methods/IMPACT468.md). Median sequencing depth 709×; samples excluded if tumor purity <20% or depth <50×.
- **Clonality:** [FACETS](../methods/facets.md) allele-specific copy-number tool applied to 428 samples with adequate quality.
- **Oncogenicity calls:** restricted to variants annotated as oncogenic by [OncoKB](../methods/oncokb-annotation.md).
- **Statistics:** [Kaplan-Meier](../methods/kaplan-meier.md) / log-rank for survival, [Cox proportional hazards](../methods/cox-proportional-hazards.md) for multivariable TTF (backward selection of univariately significant covariates), Fisher's exact / Wilcoxon / Kruskal-Wallis for associations. Analysis in R 3.4.4.
- **Treatment cohorts:** 322 patients with samples collected prior to frontline PD-1 monotherapy ([pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md) or [nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md); N=181) or nivolumab + [ipilimumab](../drugs/ipilimumab.md) (N=141). Median follow-up among non-failures: 36 mo (PD-1 mono), 39 mo (nivo+ipi).

## Key findings

- **MAPK-pathway driver detection rate:** 670/696 tumors (96%) harbored an oncogenic alteration in ≥1 of 28 RTK-RAS-MAPK pathway genes. 233 patients (33%) had ≥2 concurrent drivers.
- **Driver frequencies in the 696-tumor cohort:** [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E/K/R 31% (N=216); non-V600 BRAF 97 tumors (55 Class 2, 30 Class 3); [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) 29%; [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) loss-of-function 23%; [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md)/[MAP2K2](../genes/MAP2K2.md) 7%; [KIT](../genes/KIT.md) 4%; [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) 2%; [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) 1.3%; [GNAQ](../genes/GNAQ.md) 1%; [GNA11](../genes/GNA11.md) 0.4%.
- **Co-alteration patterns:** BRAF V600E co-altered with another MAPK driver in only 11.6% vs 37% for V600K/R (p=0.0003); Class 3 BRAF co-altered in 97% vs 38% for Class 2 (p=3.4e-08); NRAS Q61 co-altered in 29% vs 70% for other RAS (p=7.9e-07). NF1 most often co-altered with RTKs, BRAF Class 3, or [CBL](../genes/CBL.md).
- **Clonality:** Of 428 FACETS-evaluable samples, 394 (92%) had only clonal driver alterations, 22 (5%) mixed, 12 (3%) exclusively subclonal.
- **TMB by driver (median, 9-group classifier):** BRAF V600E and "unknown driver" 7–8 mut/Mb; BRAF V600K/non-V600, NRAS Q61, or other known drivers 14–18 mut/Mb; other RAS (non-Q61) 22 mut/Mb; [KIT](../genes/KIT.md) 37 mut/Mb (N=14); NF1 43 mut/Mb (N=155); overall median 16.3 mut/Mb (range 0–243); driver-group effect p=9.5e-56.
- **TMB by anatomic site and demographics:** Head primaries 34.9 mut/Mb vs upper extremity 18.6, lower extremity 11.6, trunk 10.5 (p=3.7e-21). Males 17.6 vs females 14.9 (p=0.024). TMB rose monotonically with decade of diagnosis (4.7 mut/Mb if <20; 25.6 mut/Mb if >80). UV-signature fraction lowest in BRAF V600E / lower extremity (70%, 74%), highest in NF1 / head (88%, 85%; p<1.0e-9).
- **TTF on PD-1 monotherapy (N=181) by 4-group classifier:** BRAF V600 7.5 mo, NRAS Q61 4.2 mo, NF1 22 mo, Other not reached (p<0.0001). TMB (log10) was an independent predictor of TTF (HR 0.43 per 10-fold increase, 95% CI 0.29–0.62, p<0.0001) and of [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) (HR 0.60, 95% CI 0.37–0.97, p=0.039).
- **TTF on nivolumab + ipilimumab (N=141):** No significant variation by driver group or by primary site. TMB still associated with TTF (HR 0.51 per 10-fold, p=0.008) and OS (HR 0.47, p=0.021).
- **Multivariable Cox model for PD-1-monotherapy TTF (Table 2):** log10(TMB) HR 0.41 (0.25–0.67, p<0.001); Other/Unknown vs BRAF V600 HR 0.35 (0.18–0.67, p=0.002); NRAS Q61 HR 1.38 (p=0.20); NF1 HR 1.04 (p>0.9); ECOG 2–3 HR 4.98 (p<0.001); stage M1d HR 1.90 (p=0.025).
- **Targeted therapy in BRAF V600 wild-type, checkpoint-refractory melanoma:** Of 172 patients requiring systemic therapy beyond PD-1 ± CTLA-4 blockade, 27 (16%) received genomically matched therapy (13 MEK/ERK inhibitors for RAS alterations, 6 for BRAF Class 2 alterations, 3 TRK inhibitors for NTRK fusions, others). Median TTF 3.2 mo (range 0.4–43.7), 8 patients ≥6-month benefit (30% of treated).
- **Durable complete responses:** [trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md) for BRAF K601E (Class 2) + MAP2K1 E203K; [crizotinib](../drugs/crizotinib.md) for [ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md) fusion; [larotrectinib](../drugs/larotrectinib.md) for [NTRK1](../genes/NTRK1.md) fusion; [PLX8394](../drugs/plx8394.md) for a BRAF-[AGK](../genes/AGK.md) fusion (Class 2).
- **Number-needed-to-sequence:** 21 BRAF V600 wild-type patients past PD-1/CTLA-4 to find one with ≥6-month benefit from matched therapy (or 99 across all sequenced patients if FDA-approved BRAF V600 therapies are excluded).

## Genes & alterations

- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — V600E/K/R in 31%; Class 2 (e.g., K601E, fusions) in 55/97 non-V600 tumors; Class 3 (e.g., D594N) in 30/97; Class 3 mutants almost always co-altered (97%). Class 2 BRAF fusion (BRAF-AGK) responded durably to PLX8394.
- [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) — Q61 alterations in 29%; usually sole driver (29% co-altered) but shorter PD-1-monotherapy TTF (4.2 mo).
- [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) — Loss-of-function in 23%; highest median TMB (43 mut/Mb); longest TTF on PD-1 monotherapy (22 mo); commonly co-altered with RTKs, BRAF Class 3, or [CBL](../genes/CBL.md).
- [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md) / [MAP2K2](../genes/MAP2K2.md) — 7% combined. MAP2K1 indels (residues 98–113) behave as sole drivers; MAP2K1 missense (e.g., E203K) often co-occurs with another MAPK driver. E203K acts as a RAF-dependent amplifier requiring co-altered BRAF Class 2 mutation.
- [KIT](../genes/KIT.md) — 4%; older patients (median 72 y); median TMB 37 mut/Mb; not targeted on study but historically responds to [imatinib](../drugs/imatinib.md).
- [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) (2%), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) (1.3%), [GNAQ](../genes/GNAQ.md) (1%), [GNA11](../genes/GNA11.md) (0.4%) — rare RAS/G-protein drivers.
- [ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md), [NTRK1](../genes/NTRK1.md), [NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md), [ALK](../genes/ALK.md), BRAF fusions — rare oncogenic fusions; targeted successfully with kinase inhibitors. The BRAF-[AGK](../genes/AGK.md) fusion is explicitly reported.
- [CBL](../genes/CBL.md), [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md), [RASA1](../genes/RASA1.md), [RAC1](../genes/RAC1.md), [SOS1](../genes/SOS1.md), [SPRED1](../genes/SPRED1.md), [ARAF](../genes/ARAF.md), [RAF1](../genes/RAF1.md) — secondary "backseat" RTK-RAS regulators contributing to NF1 / Class 3 BRAF co-alteration patterns.
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md), [ERBB3](../genes/ERBB3.md), [ERBB4](../genes/ERBB4.md), [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md), [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md), [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md) — RTK genes in the 28-gene driver list, more often appearing as co-alterations.

## Clinical implications

- **Driver-stratified prognosis for PD-1 monotherapy:** Mechanism of ERK activation predicts duration of benefit. BRAF V600 and NRAS Q61 tumors have the shortest TTF on single-agent anti-PD-1; NF1 and "Other" tumors fare better. The effect persists after adjusting for TMB, ECOG, and AJCC stage.
- **Nivolumab + ipilimumab erases driver-class differences:** Combination immunotherapy is the more rational choice in BRAF V600 / NRAS Q61 patients, where PD-1 monotherapy is least effective; driver group does not predict TTF for nivo+ipi.
- **TMB remains an independent biomarker** for both regimens (HR ~0.4–0.5 per 10-fold increase) across TTF and OS endpoints in this cohort.
- **Broader-panel NGS in checkpoint-refractory BRAF V600 wild-type melanoma:** Among such patients, 1 in 21 will harbor a targetable driver yielding ≥6 months of benefit; rare kinase fusions (ROS1, NTRK1, BRAF) and unusual co-mutations (BRAF Class 2 + MAP2K1 E203K) can produce durable complete responses. Authors recommend considering broader-panel NGS for all BRAF V600 wild-type melanomas progressing on PD-1-based therapy.
- **Drug-specific responses on study:** [crizotinib](../drugs/crizotinib.md) (ROS1 fusion → complete response), [larotrectinib](../drugs/larotrectinib.md) (NTRK1 fusion → complete response), [trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md) (BRAF K601E + MAP2K1 E203K → complete response), [PLX8394](../drugs/plx8394.md) (BRAF-AGK Class 2 fusion → complete metabolic response).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Underrepresentation of BRAF V600E:** patients with prior BRAF testing positive may not have been referred for MSK-IMPACT, biasing prevalence downward and possibly affecting outcome comparisons.
- **Referral bias:** tertiary-care series with an unusually high proportion (20%) of unknown-primary melanomas.
- **PD-L1 status unknown for most samples**, so its interaction with MAPK driver class and TMB could not be assessed.
- **TTF on PD-1 monotherapy for BRAF V600** may be foreshortened by ready availability of effective second-line BRAF/MEK therapy and thus reflect treatment switching rather than intrinsic resistance.
- **Non-randomized treatment allocation:** patients selected for nivo+ipi likely had higher-risk disease, complicating cross-arm comparisons.
- **Bioinformatic-pipeline sensitivity:** mutation calling and clonality estimates may not generalize to non-MSK-IMPACT NGS platforms; replication on other panels is requested.
- **Mechanism question left open:** how MAPK driver class shapes the tumor microenvironment and T-cell inflammation independently of TMB requires further functional and translational study.
- **Small driver subgroups:** several of the 9 driver classes were too small to evaluate outcomes individually, motivating pooled analyses across centers.
