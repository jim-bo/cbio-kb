# anastrozole

## Overview

Anastrozole is a non-steroidal aromatase inhibitor that blocks CYP19A1 (aromatase), the enzyme responsible for converting androgens to estrogens, thereby suppressing estrogen synthesis in post-menopausal women. It is approved as adjuvant and first-line therapy for hormone receptor-positive breast cancer. Related aromatase inhibitors used in the corpus include [letrozole](letrozole.md).

## Evidence in the corpus

- Cited as contextual background in a phase Ib trial of [buparlisib](buparlisib.md) + [letrozole](letrozole.md) in ER+/HER2- metastatic breast cancer refractory to ≥1 prior endocrine therapy line (including anastrozole); findings on [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)+[MAP3K1](../genes/MAP3K1.md) co-alteration and luminal A PAM50 subtype as predictive biomarkers for PI3K inhibitor + aromatase inhibitor combinations are broadly applicable to anastrozole-based regimens [PMID:31552290](../papers/31552290.md).

## Resistance mechanisms

- [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) mutations and [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) amplification are cited as established endocrine-therapy resistance mechanisms relevant to the aromatase-inhibitor drug class.
