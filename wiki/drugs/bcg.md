# bcg

## Overview

Bacille Calmette-Guerin (BCG) is a live attenuated strain of Mycobacterium bovis used as intravesical immunotherapy for non-muscle-invasive bladder cancer (NMIBC). It is the standard adjuvant treatment after transurethral resection of intermediate- and high-risk NMIBC, reducing recurrence and progression. Its mechanism involves innate and adaptive immune activation within the bladder wall.

## Evidence in the corpus

- In a genomic study comparing "primary" versus "secondary" muscle-invasive bladder cancer ([BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md)) (n=288 clinical / 479 genomic specimens), secondary MIBC — defined as arising after prior NMIBC management including intravesical BCG — had markedly lower pathologic response to subsequent [cisplatin](../drugs/cisplatin.md)-based neoadjuvant chemotherapy (26% vs 45%, OR=0.4, p=0.02) and worse recurrence-free and overall survival [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
- The authors hypothesize that intravesical BCG may preferentially eradicate [ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md)-mutant subclones (which carry higher mutational burden and neoantigens) via immune activation or oxidative DNA damage, thereby enriching for ERCC2-wild-type, cisplatin-refractory clones at MIBC progression — a candidate mechanism for the cisplatin-resistance phenotype observed in secondary MIBC [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md).
