# LBH

## Overview

LBH (Limb-Bud-And-Heart) encodes a transcriptional regulator involved in developmental signaling. In cancer, LBH is known as a fusion partner with [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) in inflammatory myofibroblastic tumors ([IMT](../cancer_types/IMT.md)), where LBH-ALK fusions create constitutively active ALK kinase signaling amenable to ALK inhibitor therapy.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- LBH-ALK fusion identified by MSK-Fusion RNA sequencing in a patient initially classified as uterine leiomyosarcoma (uLMS) by MSK-IMPACT DNA sequencing; the fusion was initially missed by DNA-only IMPACT sequencing because the breakpoint fell in an intron not commonly targeted for ALK fusions. Retrospective RNA-based detection led to reclassification as inflammatory myofibroblastic tumor (IMT); the patient was treated with [crizotinib](../drugs/crizotinib.md) and achieved 30+ months of radiographic disease stabilization [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [IMT](../cancer_types/IMT.md) — LBH-ALK fusion defines the diagnostic reclassification from uLMS to IMT; ALK inhibitor therapy (crizotinib) produced 30+ months disease stabilization [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)
- [USARC](../cancer_types/USARC.md) — LBH-ALK fusion first identified in a cohort of 107 prospectively sequenced uterine sarcomas [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- The LBH-ALK fusion was the sole genomically defining alteration in this case; co-alteration data not reported for this specific case in the uterine sarcoma cohort [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- LBH-ALK fusion cases should prompt diagnostic reclassification to IMT and consideration of ALK-targeted therapy (e.g., crizotinib); the case reported achieved 30+ months disease stabilization on crizotinib [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)
- DNA-based fusion detection blind spots: the LBH-ALK fusion was initially missed by IMPACT DNA sequencing; orthogonal RNA-based assays (MSK-Fusion) remain important for fusion detection in sarcomas [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)

## Open questions

- Prevalence of LBH-ALK fusions across uterine sarcomas beyond this single case is not established; prospective screening with RNA-based panels is needed.
