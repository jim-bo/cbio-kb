# Appendiceal Adenocarcinoma (APAD)

## Overview

Appendiceal Adenocarcinoma (AC) is an Appendiceal Cancer (parent BOWEL). Subtypes in the corpus include mucinous appendiceal adenocarcinoma (MAAP), goblet cell adenocarcinoma (GCA), and colonic-type appendiceal adenocarcinoma (CTAAP) [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).

## Cohorts in the corpus

- [appendiceal_msk_2022](../datasets/appendiceal_msk_2022.md): retrospective MSK cohort sequenced on MSK-IMPACT (April 2015–October 2020); molecular subtype analysis focused on 164 MAAP tumors [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).

## Recurrent alterations

- [GNAS](../genes/GNAS.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)/[NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md), and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) co-mutations define three molecular subtypes of MAAP [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).
- TP53-mut predominant tumors are highly aneuploid; aneuploidy is an independent predictor of poor [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) (P=.001) [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).

## Subtypes

- RAS-mut predominant (RAS-mut/GNAS-wt/TP53-wt, n=24): lowest mutational and chromosomal burden, best overall survival, 50% first-line chemotherapy response rate [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).
- GNAS-mut predominant: chemotherapy-resistant (6% first-line response); intermediate survival [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).
- TP53-mut predominant: highly aneuploid, invasive, worst prognosis [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).
- Subtype-associated clinical behavior conserved across MAAP, GCA, and CTAAP histologies [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).

## Therapeutic landscape

- First-line regimens include FOLFOX/FOLFIRI/FOLFIRINOX ([fluorouracil](../drugs/fluorouracil.md), [leucovorin](../drugs/leucovorin.md), [oxaliplatin](../drugs/oxaliplatin.md), [irinotecan](../drugs/irinotecan.md)) and [capecitabine](../drugs/capecitabine.md); GNAS-mut predominant tumors appear unlikely to benefit from standard colorectal-style regimens [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).
- Findings challenge the default of managing AC with colorectal cancer treatment paradigms [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).
