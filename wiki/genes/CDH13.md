# CDH13

## Overview

CDH13 (Cadherin 13; also T-cadherin) encodes an atypical cadherin lacking a cytoplasmic domain, functioning as a cell-surface adhesion and signaling molecule. Promoter hypermethylation silencing of CDH13 is frequently reported in multiple cancer types. In pancreatic cancer, CDH13 was identified by MutSigCV whole-exome sequencing as a significantly mutated gene not previously highlighted in PDA.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- MutSigCV-significant mutation in pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDA); listed among additional significantly mutated genes not previously highlighted in PDA; functional roles in PDA biology remain to be validated [PMID:25855536](../papers/25855536.md)
- Mentioned in study [PMID:26862087](../papers/26862087.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md)** — recurrently mutated; classified among additional MutSigCV-significant genes in whole-exome sequencing of 109 PDA cases [PMID:25855536](../papers/25855536.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-mutation patterns with canonical PDA drivers not detailed.

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic relevance established for CDH13 alterations in PDA.

## Open questions

- Whether CDH13 mutations drive PDA tumorigenesis or represent passenger events awaits functional validation.
