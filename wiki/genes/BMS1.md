# BMS1

## Overview

BMS1 is a ribosome biogenesis factor required for small-subunit (40S) assembly; it associates with the U3 snoRNP complex during pre-rRNA processing. In cancer, BMS1 has been identified as the 3' partner in a novel recurrent in-frame fusion with [PARG](../genes/PARG.md) (poly(ADP-ribose) glycohydrolase) in non-smoking lung adenocarcinoma, representing a potential driver event distinct from classical EGFR/ALK fusions.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Partner in the novel recurrent PARG-BMS1 in-frame fusion detected in 5 NS-LUAD tumors in the Sherlock-Lung cohort; PARG encodes poly(ADP-ribose) glycohydrolase involved in DNA damage repair [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) — recurrent PARG-BMS1 fusion observed in non-smoking lung adenocarcinoma (n=5 of ~232 tumors in the Sherlock cohort) [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- The PARG-BMS1 fusion partner PARG encodes a DNA-damage-repair protein; the biological consequence of fusing a DNA-repair enzyme to a ribosome biogenesis factor is not yet characterized [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No targeted therapies against BMS1 or the PARG-BMS1 fusion are currently described in the corpus.

## Open questions

- The functional consequence of the PARG-BMS1 fusion (loss of PARG function, BMS1 overexpression, or a novel gain-of-function) remains uncharacterized.
- Whether PARG-BMS1 is enriched in a particular NS-LUAD transcriptomic subtype was not established in the source paper.
