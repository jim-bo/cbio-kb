**PMID:** 26168399 · **DOI:** 10.1038/nature14664 · **Journal:** Nature (2015)

## TL;DR

George et al. performed whole-genome sequencing on 110 small cell lung cancer ([SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md)) tumours — at the time, the first comprehensive somatic genome analysis of this disease — and showed that bi-allelic inactivation of [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) is obligatory in SCLC (often through complex genomic rearrangements). They discovered recurrent somatic rearrangements of [TP73](../genes/TP73.md) producing N-terminally truncated, dominant-negative oncogenic isoforms (TP73Δex2/3), identified inactivating mutations in NOTCH-family genes in 25% of cases, and validated NOTCH as a tumour suppressor and master regulator of neuroendocrine differentiation in a Trp53;Rb1;Rbl2 triple-knockout mouse model. They also found rare but potentially actionable kinase mutations ([BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [KIT](../genes/KIT.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)).

## Cohort & data

- 152 fresh-frozen SCLC clinical specimens collected under IRB approval; 110 (primary lung n=148, metastatic n=4) passed QC for whole-genome sequencing of tumour + matched normal (median tumour content 84%). Most cases were treatment-naive; only 5 were obtained at relapse.
- Cancer type: [SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md) (Small Cell Lung Cancer; OncoTree code).
- Dataset: [sclc_ucologne_2015](../datasets/sclc_ucologne_2015.md) — cBioPortal study ID for this cohort.
- Transcriptome sequencing ([rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md)) on 81 specimens (71 of the 110 WGS cases + 10 additional).
- Affymetrix SNP 6.0 arrays ([affymetrix-snp6](../methods/affymetrix-snp6.md)) on 142 specimens (103 of the WGS cases + 39 additional).
- Independent validation cohort (n=112): exome sequencing of 28 fresh-frozen tumours + 9 SCLC cell lines re-analysed from prior work, plus custom-panel [targeted-dna-seq](../methods/targeted-dna-seq.md) on 8 fresh-frozen + 67 FFPE samples (22-gene custom Agilent SureDesign panel, ≥200× coverage).
- Mouse models: 8 SCLC tumours from Trp53;Rb1 double-knockout and Trp53;Rb1;Rbl2 triple-knockout (TKO) mice analysed by [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) (n=6) or [whole-genome-seq](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) (n=2).
- Assays: WGS via Illumina HiSeq 2000 (TruSeq DNA PCR-free libraries, paired-end 2×100 bp, ≥30× tumour and normal). Pathology review by ≥2 independent expert pathologists with [immunohistochemistry](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md) for chromogranin A, synaptophysin, CD56, Ki67. Reference genome NCBI37/hg19 (mouse: NCBI37/mm9), BWA v0.6.1-r104 alignment.
- Median follow-up 69 months; 31% of patients alive at last follow-up; smoking status known for 88% (median 45 pack-years among smokers).

## Key findings

- **Mutation burden:** SCLC genomes show 8.62 nonsynonymous mutations per Mb, with 28% C:G>A:T transversions on average — a heavy-smoking signature. Mouse SCLC tumours showed far lower burden (~28.5 protein-altering mutations per sample).
- **Universal TP53/RB1 loss:** In the 108 tumours without chromothripsis, [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) had bi-allelic loss in 100% and [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) in 93% of cases. Inactivating events spanned mutations, translocations, homozygous deletions, hemizygous losses, copy-neutral LOH, and LOH at higher ploidy. Many [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) mutations occurred at exon–intron junctions and caused protein-damaging splice events (confirmed by RNA-seq). Authors conclude [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and RB1 follow the classical "two-hit" Knudson tumour-suppressor pattern in SCLC.
- **Chromothripsis as alternative RB1-loss mechanism:** 2 tumours had wild-type RB1 but exhibited massive rearrangements between chromosomes 3 and 11 that retained [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md), producing significant cyclin D1 overexpression (confirmed by IHC). These cases had fewer Ki67-positive cells. Authors propose that since cyclin D1 negatively regulates Rb family proteins, chromothripsis-driven [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) overexpression can substitute for genomic RB1 loss.
- **Significantly mutated genes (q < 0.05):** [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), KIAA1211 (canonical: [CRACDL](../genes/CRACDL.md)), [COL22A1](../genes/COL22A1.md), [RGS7](../genes/RGS7.md), and [FPR1](../genes/FPR1.md). [RGS7](../genes/RGS7.md) and [FPR1](../genes/FPR1.md) are both involved in G-protein-coupled receptor signalling.
- **Mutation clustering (P < 0.05):** Hotspot/clustering analysis flagged the histone acetyltransferases [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md) and [EP300](../genes/EP300.md) (with recurrent inactivating translocations), centrosome genes [ASPM](../genes/ASPM.md), [ALMS1](../genes/ALMS1.md), [PDE4DIP](../genes/PDE4DIP.md), the RNA-regulating gene [XRN1](../genes/XRN1.md), the tetraspanin gene [PTGFRN](../genes/PTGFRN.md), and [TP73](../genes/TP73.md).
- **Damaging mutations (P < 0.01):** TP53, RB1, [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md), [COL22A1](../genes/COL22A1.md), plus [FMN2](../genes/FMN2.md) and [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md). NOTCH-family genes ([NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md), [NOTCH2](../genes/NOTCH2.md), [NOTCH3](../genes/NOTCH3.md), [NOTCH4](../genes/NOTCH4.md)) were recurrently inactivated in 25% of human SCLC; Notch3 was also mutated in a Trp53−/−,Rb1−/−,Rbl2−/− mouse tumour.
- **Mouse-validated accessory tumour suppressors:** [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) (previously known), and [RBL1](../genes/RBL1.md) and [RBL2](../genes/RBL2.md) — both showed inactivating translocations and mutations in human tumours; Trp53/Rb1/Rbl2 triple-knockout mice develop SCLC with shorter latency than Trp53/Rb1 double-knockouts, confirming [RBL2](../genes/RBL2.md) as an accessory tumour suppressor.
- **Mutual exclusivity:** Mutations in [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md), [EP300](../genes/EP300.md), [TP73](../genes/TP73.md), [RBL1](../genes/RBL1.md), [RBL2](../genes/RBL2.md), and NOTCH-family genes were largely mutually exclusive, suggesting overlapping pro-tumorigenic functions.
- **Rare potentially actionable kinase mutations:** Four tumours carried oncogenic driver mutations from other cancers: [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [KIT](../genes/KIT.md), and [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — see Extended Data Fig. 3e.
- **Copy-number landscape:** Focal 3p losses at 3p14.3–3p14.2 ([FHIT](../genes/FHIT.md)) and 3p12.3–3p12.2 ([ROBO1](../genes/ROBO1.md)); [FHIT](../genes/FHIT.md) expression was reduced in cases with focal deletions. Homozygous [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) losses. Recurrent amplifications of MYC-family genes ([MYC](../genes/MYC.md), [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md), [MYCL](../genes/MYCL.md) — paper uses MYCL1 nomenclature), [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md), and [IRS2](../genes/IRS2.md). Focal [IRS2](../genes/IRS2.md) amplifications occurred in 2% of cases.
- **[TP73](../genes/TP73.md) oncogenic rearrangements:** Genomic breakpoints clustered precisely in the TP73 locus in 7% of cases (n=8), with recurrent breaks in introns 1, 2, and 3 producing intragenic fusions that exclude exon 2 (p73Δex2) or exons 2+3 (p73Δex2/3). One tumour had genomic exclusion of exon 10. p73Δex2/3 was found only in cases with genomic rearrangements, confirmed not to be a natural splice variant. Overall TP73 was somatically altered (mutation or rearrangement) in 13% of cases. These N-terminally truncated isoforms have dominant-negative activity on wild-type p73 and p53 and are confirmed oncogenes in vivo.
- **Subclonality:** SCLC showed threefold lower subclonal diversity than lung adenocarcinoma (P = 0.00023), and heterogeneity did not correlate with clinical stage (in contrast to adenocarcinomas).
- **Transcriptional subtypes & NOTCH:** Unsupervised clustering of RNA-seq (n=69) revealed two major groups. The majority (77%, n=53/69) had high neuroendocrine markers [CHGA](../genes/CHGA.md) (chromogranin A) and [GRP](../genes/GRP.md) (gastrin-releasing peptide), high [DLK1](../genes/DLK1.md) (a non-canonical Notch inhibitor), and high [ASCL1](../genes/ASCL1.md) (a neuroendocrine lineage oncogene whose expression is repressed by active Notch). The remaining 23% (n=16/69) expressed [SYP](../genes/SYP.md) and [NCAM1](../genes/NCAM1.md). Major SCLC driver mutations (TP53, RB1, CREBBP) did not differ between transcriptional subtypes.
- **NOTCH activation suppresses SCLC in vivo:** Crossing Rosa26-LSL-N2ICD or Rosa26-LSL-N1ICD into Trp53;Rb1;Rbl2 TKO mice significantly reduced tumour number (P < 0.001) after Ad-Cre induction and extended survival. The recombination efficiency of an innocuous reporter (Rosa26mT/mG) was much higher than that of the N2ICD allele, indicating strong negative selection against active Notch during SCLC development. Ectopic N1ICD also inhibited growth and cell-cycle progression in mouse and human SCLC cell lines, and upregulated [HES1](../genes/HES1.md), Hey1, Hey2 while abrogating neuroendocrine marker expression.
- **Other recurrent rearrangements:** Clustered breakpoints affected [TTC28](../genes/TTC28.md) on chromosome 22 (inactivating translocations), and [CDKAL1](../genes/CDKAL1.md) on chromosome 6. Breakpoints also clustered downstream of the L1HS retrotransposon, supporting a role for this element in cancer.

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — bi-allelic inactivation in 100% of non-chromothriptic tumours; missense mutations cluster in the DNA-binding domain.
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — bi-allelic loss in 93% of non-chromothriptic tumours; many alterations at exon–intron junctions cause protein-damaging splice events; frequent complex translocations.
- [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) — retained and overexpressed in 2 chromothriptic tumours with wild-type RB1; proposed alternative mechanism of Rb-pathway inactivation.
- [TP73](../genes/TP73.md) — recurrent intragenic genomic rearrangements (breakpoints in introns 1–3) producing dominant-negative N-terminally truncated isoforms p73Δex2 and p73Δex2/3 in 7% of cases; total TP73 alteration rate 13%.
- [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md), [NOTCH2](../genes/NOTCH2.md), [NOTCH3](../genes/NOTCH3.md), [NOTCH4](../genes/NOTCH4.md) — inactivating mutations (often in the extracellular domain) in 25% of human SCLC; mouse models confirm Notch activation suppresses SCLC initiation and prolongs survival.
- [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md) and [EP300](../genes/EP300.md) — clustered mutations and recurrent inactivating translocations.
- [RBL1](../genes/RBL1.md), [RBL2](../genes/RBL2.md) — inactivating translocations and mutations; RBL2 validated as accessory tumour suppressor in TKO mice (shorter SCLC latency).
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — confirmed accessory tumour suppressor (previously known role in murine SCLC).
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [KIT](../genes/KIT.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — rare oncogenic kinase mutations with potential targeted-therapy implications (4 tumours total).
- [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md), [MYC](../genes/MYC.md), [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md), [MYCL](../genes/MYCL.md), [IRS2](../genes/IRS2.md) — recurrent focal amplifications.
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [FHIT](../genes/FHIT.md), [ROBO1](../genes/ROBO1.md) — recurrent focal deletions on 3p (FHIT, [ROBO1](../genes/ROBO1.md)) and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) homozygous loss.
- [ASCL1](../genes/ASCL1.md), [DLK1](../genes/DLK1.md), [CHGA](../genes/CHGA.md), [GRP](../genes/GRP.md), [SYP](../genes/SYP.md), [NCAM1](../genes/NCAM1.md) — neuroendocrine and Notch-axis expression markers defining the two SCLC transcriptional subtypes.
- [COL22A1](../genes/COL22A1.md), [RGS7](../genes/RGS7.md), [FPR1](../genes/FPR1.md), [CRACDL](../genes/CRACDL.md) (paper nomenclature: KIAA1211) — significantly mutated genes of unclear function.
- [ASPM](../genes/ASPM.md), [ALMS1](../genes/ALMS1.md), [PDE4DIP](../genes/PDE4DIP.md) — clustered mutations in centrosome-associated genes.
- [XRN1](../genes/XRN1.md), [PTGFRN](../genes/PTGFRN.md), [FMN2](../genes/FMN2.md) — clustered/damaging mutations; [PTGFRN](../genes/PTGFRN.md) also mutated in murine SCLC.
- [TTC28](../genes/TTC28.md), [CDKAL1](../genes/CDKAL1.md) — clustered chromosomal breakpoints (chromosomes 22 and 6 respectively).
- [HES1](../genes/HES1.md) — upregulated upon Notch activation in mouse models, consistent with canonical Notch target activation suppressing neuroendocrine differentiation.

## Clinical implications

- **Universal TP53/RB1 loss as defining feature:** Authors argue complete genomic loss of both [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) function is obligatory in SCLC pathogenesis. Tumours with wild-type RB1 should be examined for chromothripsis-driven [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) overexpression as an alternative Rb-pathway inactivation mechanism.
- **Rare actionable kinase mutations:** Mutations in [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [KIT](../genes/KIT.md), and [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — found in only 4 tumours total — suggest that genotyping SCLC patients may identify rare individuals who could benefit from targeted kinase inhibitor therapy.
- **TP73Δex2/3 as a potential therapeutic target:** Given the frequent occurrence of oncogenic [TP73](../genes/TP73.md) rearrangements (13% of cases), and prior reports that therapeutic options exist to restrict p73-dependent tumour growth in vivo (including in Trp53-deficient tumours), these approaches may be promising in SCLC.
- **NOTCH pathway as a candidate therapeutic axis:** Since NOTCH activation suppresses SCLC initiation and extends survival in TKO mice, and abrogates neuroendocrine marker expression, Notch-pathway agonism (or de-repression) is suggested as a therapeutic strategy — though the paper does not name specific clinical agents.
- **Prognostic null result:** Mutations in CREBBP, [EP300](../genes/EP300.md), TP73, [RBL1](../genes/RBL1.md), RBL2, and NOTCH-family genes were NOT significantly associated with total mutation number, overall survival, or other clinical parameters (Extended Data Fig. 4). Global mutational signatures also did not correlate with these mutations.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Functional uncertainty for novel hits:** The authors explicitly note that the biological role of most newly discovered genes (KIAA1211/[CRACDL](../genes/CRACDL.md), [COL22A1](../genes/COL22A1.md), [ASPM](../genes/ASPM.md), [PDE4DIP](../genes/PDE4DIP.md), [PTGFRN](../genes/PTGFRN.md)) "is much less clear" and that functional experiments will be required.
- **Stage enrichment:** The 110-tumour cohort was enriched for earlier-stage SCLC (most obtained by surgical resection), since SCLC is rarely surgically managed. This may limit generalizability to the typical advanced-stage clinical SCLC population.
- **Few relapsed cases:** Only 5/110 tumours were obtained at relapse, so the genomic landscape of chemo-resistant/recurrent SCLC is not addressed here.
- **Sample exclusions:** 42/152 cases were excluded for insufficient DNA quality/quantity, potentially introducing selection bias.
- **No survival association for most novel drivers:** Although TP73 and NOTCH alterations are mechanistically validated, the lack of overall-survival association leaves their prognostic and predictive value open.
- **Chromothripsis sample size:** Only 2 tumours with wild-type RB1 and chromothripsis-driven CCND1 retention were identified — a striking but very rare alternative mechanism that needs validation in larger cohorts.
- **Notch therapeutic translation:** While mouse models clearly support Notch as a tumour suppressor in SCLC, clinically tractable Notch-activating agents and patient-selection biomarkers remain to be defined.
