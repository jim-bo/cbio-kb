**PMID:** 32888432 · **DOI:** 10.1007/s00432-025-06341-4 · **Journal:** Journal of Cancer Research and Clinical Oncology (2025)

## TL;DR

Liu et al. characterize Cell Growth Regulator with EF-hand domain 1 ([CGREF1](../genes/CGREF1.md)) as a driver of colorectal cancer ([COADREAD](../cancer_types/COADREAD.md)) progression. Using TIMER 2.0 and GEPIA bioinformatic analyses across TCGA data, an 80-patient tissue microarray with 5-year follow-up, and siRNA/shRNA knockdown in SW480 and RKO CRC cell lines plus an orthotopic mouse metastatic model, they show that [CGREF1](../genes/CGREF1.md) is overexpressed in CRC tissue, correlates with aggressive clinicopathological features (advanced stage, lymph node metastasis, venous invasion, high-grade tumor budding) and poor overall survival, and promotes CRC cell migration and liver metastasis by regulating F-actin polymerization and pseudopodia formation — but does not affect cell proliferation [PMID:32888432](../papers/32888432.md).

## Cohort & data

- **TIMER 2.0 pan-cancer expression analysis** across 32 tumor types, including [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md) and [READ](../cancer_types/READ.md). CGREF1 significantly upregulated in 13 tumors.
- **TCGA CRC data** ([coadread_tcga](../datasets/coadread_tcga.md), reference genome GRCh37): 275 CRC samples plus 349 normal colon samples analyzed via GEPIA2; an additional paired analysis used 17 matched tumor/normal pairs.
- **Tissue microarray cohort** (AiFang Biological AF-CocSur2201; Hunan Cancer Hospital, Jan 2018 – Oct 2018; 5-year follow-up to Oct 2023): 80 CRC tissues with paired adjacent normal intestinal epithelial tissues. 44 male / 36 female, median age 61 (range 31–81); 49 right-colon, 31 left-colon; all underwent colectomy without neoadjuvant therapy; MMR status assessed by [IHC](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md). Associated cBioPortal study slug per corpus mapping: [coadread_cass_2020](../datasets/coadread_cass_2020.md).
- **qRT-PCR validation cohort**: 19 paired primary tumor and adjacent normal colorectal specimens.
- **CRC cell lines**: SW480 and RKO (National Collection of Authenticated Cell Cultures, Shanghai).
- **Orthotopic mouse model**: Balb/C athymic nude (nu/nu) mice with surgical cecal-wall implantation of 2 × 10^6 RKO cells (n = 6 per group), euthanized 1.5 months post-surgery for assessment of liver metastasis.
- **Assays**: TIMER 2.0 / GEPIA2 bioinformatics; [IHC](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md) with immunoreactivity score (IRS = intensity × proportion, 0–12); [qRT-PCR](../methods/quantitative-rt-pcr.md) normalized to GAPDH; Western blot; siRNA and shRNA knockdown; CCK-8, EdU, and colony formation proliferation assays; wound-healing and [transwell migration](../methods/transwell-invasion-assay.md) assays; F-actin phalloidin immunofluorescence.

## Key findings

- **Pan-cancer overexpression.** TIMER 2.0 analysis confirmed CGREF1 was significantly upregulated in 13 of 32 tumor types relative to normal tissue, including both [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md) and [READ](../cancer_types/READ.md) [PMID:32888432](../papers/32888432.md).
- **CRC tissue overexpression validated across modalities.** GEPIA2 (275 CRC vs. 349 normal colon, TCGA) showed CGREF1 significantly higher in CRC than normal; matched-pair TCGA analysis (n = 17) confirmed; in-house qRT-PCR on 19 paired specimens showed CGREF1 mRNA elevated in 78.9% (15/19) of CRCs vs. matched normal [PMID:32888432](../papers/32888432.md).
- **IHC: 61.25% of CRCs over-express CGREF1.** On the 80-patient tissue microarray, CGREF1 was over-expressed (IRS ≥ 4, moderate or strong) in 49/80 (61.25%) of CRC tissues, vs. 0/80 normal colorectal tissues showing moderate or strong staining (Table 1; p = 0.00001) [PMID:32888432](../papers/32888432.md).
- **Association with aggressive features.** High CGREF1 expression correlated with advanced stage III–IV (p = 0.006), lymph node–positive disease (p = 0.007), venous invasion (p = 0.043), and high-grade tumor budding Bd3 (p = 0.017). No significant association with sex, histopathological grade, MMR status, pT stage, or M1 distant metastasis on Table 2 [PMID:32888432](../papers/32888432.md).
- **CGREF1 enriched in tumor-budding regions.** CGREF1 expression was markedly elevated in regions of high-grade budding (Bd3) at the invasive front [PMID:32888432](../papers/32888432.md).
- **Survival: independent prognostic factor.** Kaplan–Meier analysis showed high CGREF1 expression associated with significantly shorter overall survival (log-rank p < 0.0001). Univariate Cox analysis identified advanced stage (HR 6.13, 95% CI 2.06–18.1, p = 0.001), lymph node positivity (HR 3.25, 95% CI 1.36–7.77, p = 0.008), venous invasion (HR 7.44, 95% CI 2.50–22.0, p < 0.001), and CGREF1-positive expression (HR 4.70, 95% CI 1.81–12.1, p = 0.001) as significant prognosticators. On multivariate Cox analysis, **CGREF1 expression (HR 3.85, 95% CI 1.32–11.2, p = 0.014)** and venous invasion (HR 7.13, 95% CI 1.16–43.8, p = 0.034) remained independent predictors of disease-free survival; stage, pN, and tumor budding lost independence (Table 3) [PMID:32888432](../papers/32888432.md).
- **CGREF1 knockdown blocks migration, not proliferation.** siRNA knockdown of CGREF1 in [SW480](../genes/CGREF1.md) and RKO cells did **not** alter proliferation (CCK-8, colony formation, EdU assays were all unchanged), but significantly attenuated wound-healing and transwell migration [PMID:32888432](../papers/32888432.md).
- **In vivo metastasis suppressed.** In the orthotopic RKO/shCGREF1 model, primary intestinal tumor growth was unaffected, but the average number of macroscopic liver metastases was dramatically reduced relative to control RKO implants [PMID:32888432](../papers/32888432.md).
- **Mechanism: cytoskeletal remodeling.** Immunofluorescence showed CGREF1 knockdown reduced F-actin polymerization and pseudopodia formation in SW480 cells, implicating CGREF1 in actin-driven migratory protrusions [PMID:32888432](../papers/32888432.md).

## Genes & alterations

- **[CGREF1](../genes/CGREF1.md)** — 301-aa secreted protein with two calcium-binding EF-hand domains; initially identified as a p53-responsive suppressor in rodent cells (Madden et al. 1996, cited within paper). In this study CGREF1 is over-expressed at both mRNA and protein levels in CRC vs. matched normal; high protein expression is an independent predictor of shorter disease-free survival (HR 3.85, p = 0.014). siRNA/shRNA knockdown suppresses migration in vitro and liver metastasis in vivo without affecting proliferation, acting via F-actin / pseudopodia formation [PMID:32888432](../papers/32888432.md).
- **[MCM2](../genes/MCM2.md), [INHBA](../genes/INHBA.md), [KLF9](../genes/KLF9.md)** — discussed in context only: CGREF1 had previously been included in a 4-gene cell-cycle prognostic signature ([MCM2](../genes/MCM2.md), [INHBA](../genes/INHBA.md), CGREF1, [KLF9](../genes/KLF9.md)) for early-onset CRC (Xiang et al. 2023, cited by the authors). The present study is the first to validate CGREF1 as a standalone independent prognostic biomarker [PMID:32888432](../papers/32888432.md).

## Clinical implications

- **Prognostic biomarker.** CGREF1 IHC (IRS scoring on FFPE tissue) is proposed as a stand-alone prognostic marker for risk stratification in CRC, particularly for early-stage patients in whom micrometastases are often undetected [PMID:32888432](../papers/32888432.md).
- **Anti-metastasis target.** Because CGREF1 selectively promotes migration / invasion (and not bulk proliferation), the authors position it as a "potential therapeutic target" specifically for inhibiting metastatic dissemination rather than primary tumor growth — relevant given that over 50% of CRC patients harbor micrometastases prior to surgery [PMID:32888432](../papers/32888432.md).
- **Tumor-budding biology.** Localized CGREF1 over-expression within budding (Bd3) foci offers a candidate molecular handle on the spatially restricted invasive-front phenotype already used clinically as a prognostic feature in CRC [PMID:32888432](../papers/32888432.md).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Single retrospective Chinese cohort** (n = 80, Hunan Cancer Hospital, 2018 enrollment). External validation in independent CRC cohorts and patient-derived organoids was explicitly flagged as needed by the authors [PMID:32888432](../papers/32888432.md).
- **Downstream pathway uncharacterized.** The specific signaling pathway by which CGREF1 controls F-actin polymerization and pseudopodia formation in CRC was not identified. The authors note that in hepatocellular carcinoma CGREF1 acts via EIF3H/Wnt/β-catenin (Gao et al. 2025), but this mechanism was not tested in CRC and may be cancer-type dependent [PMID:32888432](../papers/32888432.md).
- **Proliferation discrepancy with prior CRC work.** Deng et al. 2015 (cited within the paper) reported CGREF1 *suppresses* CRC cell proliferation via AP-1 inhibition; the present study finds no effect on proliferation. The authors attribute the discrepancy to context-specific signaling but do not resolve it [PMID:32888432](../papers/32888432.md).
- **Only two CRC cell lines.** Functional knockdown work used SW480 and RKO only; broader cell-line and patient-derived-organoid validation is called out as future work [PMID:32888432](../papers/32888432.md).
- **Frontmatter-vs-text dataset mismatch (curation note).** The corpus assigns `study_id: coadread_cass_2020` to this PMID, but the paper's bioinformatic analyses are explicitly on TCGA CRC data via TIMER 2.0 / GEPIA2 (= [coadread_tcga](../datasets/coadread_tcga.md)); the 80-patient tissue microarray is an independent in-house cohort (AiFang AF-CocSur2201, Hunan Cancer Hospital) not deposited in cBioPortal as of writing. Treat the `coadread_cass_2020` slug as a corpus-side study-mapping artifact rather than a cohort the authors themselves curated to cBioPortal.
