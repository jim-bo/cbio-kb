# trastuzumab-deruxtecan

## Overview

Trastuzumab deruxtecan (T-DXd) is an anti-HER2 antibody-drug conjugate (ADC) composed of [trastuzumab](../drugs/trastuzumab.md) linked to a topoisomerase I inhibitor payload. It has FDA approvals in HER2-positive and HER2-low breast cancer, HER2-positive gastric/gastroesophageal junction adenocarcinoma, HER2-mutant [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md), and other HER2-expressing solid tumors.

## Evidence in the corpus

- In 177 cervical cancers profiled by MSK-IMPACT, trastuzumab deruxtecan is under investigation (NCT04482309) for HER2-mutant/amplified cervical cancer; [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) is altered in 28% of unusual endocervical adenocarcinoma (UEA) and 12% overall, establishing [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) as a likely important target [PMID:37643132](../papers/37643132.md).
- In endometrial carcinoma, [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) amplification enriched in Black patients (12% vs 3% in White patients) represents a therapeutic opportunity with trastuzumab deruxtecan; activity was noted even in low-HER2-expressing carcinosarcomas [PMID:37651310](../papers/37651310.md).
