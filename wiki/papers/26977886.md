**PMID:** 26977886 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.ccell.2016.02.009 · **Journal:** Cancer Cell (2016)

## TL;DR

Chun et al. produced an integrative genome-wide reference of 40 extra-cranial malignant rhabdoid tumors ([MRT](../cancer_types/MRT.md), mostly pediatric kidney and soft-tissue cases banked through the TARGET initiative and the Children's Oncology Group), combining whole-genome sequencing, whole-genome bisulfite sequencing, RNA-Seq, miRNA-Seq and H3K27me3 / H3K27ac ChIP-Seq. They confirm that [SMARCB1](../genes/SMARCB1.md) loss is the dominant driver in 39/40 cases (the remaining case is driven by [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) loss), and show that despite this near-uniform driver, MRT split into reproducible miRNA, mRNA and methylation sub-groups with distinct developmental and epigenetic signatures, including loss of H3K27me3 and gain of MRT-specific super-enhancers at HOX clusters. miRNA clustering links extra-cranial MRT to neural-crest-derived tissues.

## Cohort & data

- 40 MRT tumor/normal pairs analyzed by whole-genome sequencing (median tumor content 88.0% by APOLLOH); 66 primary MRTs profiled by miRNA-Seq; 40 MRT cases plus 3 hESC lines profiled by RNA-Seq with 4 fetal cerebellum normals; 40 MRT cases, 3 MRT cell lines (G401, KP-MRT-RY, KP-MRT-NS) and 3 hESC lines (H7, H9, H14) plus 4 NPCs profiled by whole-genome bisulfite sequencing; 35 primary MRTs, 3 MRT cell lines, 2 hESC lines, 1 normal kidney, 2 fetal brains and 2 NPCs profiled by H3K27me3 ChIP-Seq; 10 MRT samples + 3 cell lines + 3 hESC + 1 fetal brain by H3K27ac ChIP-Seq ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- 67 primary extra-cranial MRT cases overall (58 kidney, 7 soft tissue, 2 liver) and 40 matched normals (16 adjacent kidney, 24 peripheral blood) banked by the COG Biopathology Center under the National Wilms Tumor Study Group 5 / COG AREN03B2 protocols ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- Cancer types: rhabdoid tumor of the kidney ([MRT](../cancer_types/MRT.md)) and extra-renal / liver MRT ([MRTL](../cancer_types/MRTL.md)).
- Dataset: [mrt_bcgsc_2016](../datasets/mrt_bcgsc_2016.md). Sequencing reads and analyzed files are deposited in NCBI dbGaP (phs000470) with additional matrices on the TARGET data portal ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- Assays: [whole-genome-seq](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md), [whole-genome-bisulfite-seq](../methods/whole-genome-bisulfite-seq.md), [rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md), [mirna-seq](../methods/mirna-seq.md), [chip-seq](../methods/chip-seq.md) (H3K27me3, H3K27ac); sub-group discovery used [consensus-hierarchical-clustering](../methods/consensus-hierarchical-clustering.md) / NMF.

## Key findings

- **[SMARCB1](../genes/SMARCB1.md) is near-universal**: inactivating mutations, copy-number losses or somatic LOH at [SMARCB1](../genes/SMARCB1.md) were detected in 39/40 cases; the single SMARCB1-intact case carried somatic LOH plus a one-base germ-line [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) deletion ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- **Quiet genomes outside the driver**: median 612.5 somatic SNVs per case (0.231 mutations/Mb), with 97.1% of mutations in non-coding regions, consistent with prior MRT exome reports. 204 non-synonymous substitutions in total (median 5/case, range 1–17) ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- **[DSCAM](../genes/DSCAM.md) is the only recurrent non-SMARCB1 NS target**: two cases harbored heterozygous somatic non-synonymous [DSCAM](../genes/DSCAM.md) substitutions (p.Val424Ile, p.Ser1354Thr); flagged as possibly passenger ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- **Recurrent intronic mutations linked to expression**: [SPECC1L](../genes/SPECC1L.md) intron mutations in 6 cases were associated with decreased [SPECC1L](../genes/SPECC1L.md) expression (log2 FC = −1.45; BH-Wilcoxon p = 1.82e-10), and [KCNJ3](../genes/KCNJ3.md) intron mutations in 8 cases were associated with increased [KCNJ3](../genes/KCNJ3.md) expression (log2 FC = 1.20; p = 2.30e-08) ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- **Chr22 dominates structural variation**: 9/15 recurrent CNA loci and 22/26 verified gene fusions involve chromosome 22; 7 of those fusions arise as direct consequences of SMARCB1 deletion, with [SPECC1L](../genes/SPECC1L.md) as fusion partner in 4/7. Two chromosome-22 deletions did not include SMARCB1 and instead focally deleted [NF2](../genes/NF2.md) and [LIF](../genes/LIF.md) ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- **Two mutational signatures stand out**: case PADYZI was dominated by T>G transversions (Pearson correlation to Alexandrov signatures 9 and 17 of 0.607 and 0.962); case PAUEKW matched signatures 1A/1B (correlations 0.877 and 0.925); neither carried DNA-repair gene mutations ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- **miRNA sub-groups link MRT to neural-crest lineages**: unsupervised clustering of 535 miRNAs against TCGA / normal references (11,819 cases, 37 cancer types, 27 normal tissues) split 66 MRTs into a larger group (n=57) clustering with normal cerebellum and TCGA pheochromocytoma/paraganglioma, and a smaller group (n=9) clustering with synovial sarcomas. NMF on miRNA-Seq independently identified 2 sub-groups in which all 5 miR-200 family members (miR-200a/b/c, miR-141, miR-429) were lower in sub-group 1, suggesting more active EMT in sub-group 1 ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- **Putative miRNA targets enriched for neuron-development genes** (VANGL2, ULK1, [NGFR](../genes/NGFR.md)); [SMARCE1](../genes/SMARCE1.md) is a putative miR-200a target ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- **mRNA sub-groups recapitulate AT/RT vs RTK split**: NMF on the top-25% most-variable protein-coding genes (n=3,179) yielded 2 sub-groups (n=40); organ site was associated with sub-group 1 (all 6 extra-renal cases; Fisher's exact p=0.04). Sub-group 1 over-expressed immunoglobulins, [BMP4](../genes/BMP4.md), SOSTDC1, [DLK1](../genes/DLK1.md), [MEOX2](../genes/MEOX2.md); sub-group 2 over-expressed [PCDH18](../genes/PCDH18.md), SMOC2, [WNT5A](../genes/WNT5A.md), HIC1, [TCF21](../genes/TCF21.md), [MEIS1](../genes/MEIS1.md). Comparison with Grupenmacher 2013 markers showed sub-group 1 is more AT/RT-like (10/11 of the AT/RT-up genes shared) and sub-group 2 is more RTK-like (21/21 of the RTK-up shared genes; one-sided Fisher p = 1.193e-13) ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- **Most over- and under-expressed genes vs hESC + cerebellum**: top induced include immune (IGKC, IGKJ5) and developmental (MGP, [LUM](../genes/LUM.md)) genes; top repressed include developmental ([ZIC3](../genes/ZIC3.md), [SOX3](../genes/SOX3.md)) and neuronal (GABRA3, CADPS) genes ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- **Two methylation sub-groups correlate with age**: WGBS-based promoter CGI clustering produced sub-group A (higher CGI methylation; Welch t-test p = 4.629e-16) enriched for patients >1 year (one-sided Fisher p = 0.0013, with the only sub-group-A patient under one year being 354 days old) and sub-group B (clustering with hESC). MRT cell lines clustered with sub-group A. Sub-group A promoters that gained methylation were enriched for homeobox terms; tumor-suppressor promoters ([RASSF1](../genes/RASSF1.md), IRX1, TWIST2, DLEC1, TBX5) preferentially gained methylation versus hESC (one-sided Fisher p = 0.02041) ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- **Loss of H3K27me3 at homeobox promoters**: H3K27me3 ChIP-Seq on 35 MRTs vs normals identified 158 promoters with decreased H3K27me3 strongly enriched for homeobox / HOX / homeodomain terms (FDR 4.17e-44 to 1.67e-37); 42 promoters with increased H3K27me3 had no significant enrichment ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- **MRT-specific super-enhancers blanket HOX and histone clusters**: 136 super-enhancers (present in ≥50% of MRT and absent in fetal brain + hESC) annotate 197 genes enriched for DNA-binding/homeobox terms (FDR < 0.0001), driven by HOXA, HOXB, HOXC, HISTH1 and HISTH2 clusters; super-enhancers also fall on [FLT3LG](../genes/FLT3LG.md) and [STAT3](../genes/STAT3.md). The HOXC super-enhancer covers the [HOTAIR](../genes/HOTAIR.md) lncRNA, paralleling and extending prior reports of [HOTAIR](../genes/HOTAIR.md) over-expression in AT/RT ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).

## Genes & alterations

- [SMARCB1](../genes/SMARCB1.md) — biallelic inactivation in 39/40 extra-cranial MRT (mutations, deletions, somatic LOH); the dominant driver. Loss is associated with downstream loss of H3K27me3 at homeobox promoters and gain of MRT-specific super-enhancers at HOX clusters ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) — sole driver in the one SMARCB1-intact case, via somatic LOH plus a germ-line one-base deletion ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- [DSCAM](../genes/DSCAM.md) — recurrent (2/40) heterozygous somatic missense substitutions (p.Val424Ile, p.Ser1354Thr); only non-SMARCB1 recurrently NS-mutated gene in the cohort ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- [SPECC1L](../genes/SPECC1L.md) — intronic mutations in 6/40 cases associated with reduced expression; partner in 4/7 SMARCB1-deletion-driven fusions; alterations consistent with disrupted neural-crest cell migration ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- [KCNJ3](../genes/KCNJ3.md) — intronic mutations in 8/40 cases associated with increased expression ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- [NF2](../genes/NF2.md), [LIF](../genes/LIF.md) — focally deleted by the only chromosome-22 deletions that spare SMARCB1 ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- [SMARCE1](../genes/SMARCE1.md) — putative miR-200a target, suggesting miRNA-mediated dysregulation of SWI/SNF members ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- [HOTAIR](../genes/HOTAIR.md) — covered by an MRT-specific super-enhancer in the HOXC cluster and over-expressed in MRT vs normals, paralleling AT/RT ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- [BMP4](../genes/BMP4.md), [DLK1](../genes/DLK1.md), [MEOX2](../genes/MEOX2.md) — among the most over-expressed genes in mRNA sub-group 1 (BMP signaling / differentiation) ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- [WNT5A](../genes/WNT5A.md), [PCDH18](../genes/PCDH18.md), [TCF21](../genes/TCF21.md), [MEIS1](../genes/MEIS1.md) — among the most over-expressed genes in mRNA sub-group 2 (cell adhesion/migration, WNT, differentiation) ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- [ZIC3](../genes/ZIC3.md), [SOX3](../genes/SOX3.md) — among the most under-expressed developmental genes vs hESC + fetal cerebellum ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- [FLT3LG](../genes/FLT3LG.md), [STAT3](../genes/STAT3.md) — flagged within MRT-specific super-enhancers; both have prior roles in stem-cell self-renewal and differentiation ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- Tumor-suppressor promoters with sub-group-A hypermethylation: RASSF1, IRX1, TWIST2, DLEC1, TBX5 ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).

## Clinical implications

- **No prospective treatment claims** — this is a descriptive reference landscape, not a therapy or biomarker-validation study. The authors motivate the work by noting that overall 4-year MRT survival is only 23.2% and that more effective treatment options are needed ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- **Heterogeneity despite a single driver**: the data argue against treating extra-cranial MRT as a molecularly uniform disease. miRNA, mRNA and methylation sub-groups exist, and the mRNA sub-groups recapitulate the AT/RT vs RTK distinction reported by Grupenmacher et al. (2013), suggesting sub-group-specific therapeutic hypotheses are warranted ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- **Methylation sub-group A correlates with age >1 year at diagnosis**, raising the possibility that DNA-methylation status could refine MRT risk stratification beyond age and stage alone ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- **Epigenetic vulnerabilities**: the authors highlight loss of H3K27me3 at homeobox promoters and HOX-cluster super-enhancers as candidate epigenetic dependencies in MRT ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).

## Limitations & open questions

- **No drug or treatment outcomes**: the manuscript presents no patient-level survival analyses tied to the molecular sub-groups beyond the age-at-diagnosis correlation; therapeutic actionability of the HOX/super-enhancer / EMT findings is hypothesis-generating only ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- **Cohort skews to renal MRT**: 58/67 primary tumors are kidney, 7 soft tissue, 2 liver — limiting power for site-specific biology, particularly for MRTL/extra-renal MRT ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- **Cell of origin not directly proven**: miRNA clustering with neural-crest-derived tissues (PCPG, normal cerebellum) is suggestive but indirect; the authors explicitly note that the MRT cell of origin remains unknown ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- **Recurrent intronic SPECC1L / KCNJ3 mutations**: associated with expression changes but mechanism (regulatory disruption vs sequencing artifact in introns of frequently rearranged regions) is not resolved here ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- **Inconsistency with prior literature on MRT vs AT/RT overlap**: the authors note the literature is inconsistent (Grupenmacher 2013 vs Pomeroy 2002); their data partially reconcile these by showing that extra-cranial MRT itself is heterogeneous along an AT/RT-like vs RTK-like axis ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
- **Sample-size differences across assays** (40 WGS, 66 miRNA-Seq, 40 RNA-Seq + 40 WGBS, 35 H3K27me3 ChIP-Seq, 10 H3K27ac ChIP-Seq) make integrated multi-omic per-case analyses uneven across the cohort ([PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)).
