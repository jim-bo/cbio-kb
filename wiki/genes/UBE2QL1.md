# UBE2QL1

## Overview

UBE2QL1 (ubiquitin-conjugating enzyme E2 Q-like 1) is a ubiquitin-conjugating enzyme family member. In cancer genomics it has been identified as a structural-variant partner of [ADCY2](../genes/ADCY2.md) in acral lentiginous melanoma, the most rearranged gene in that disease subtype.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- UBE2QL1 identified as a structural-variant partner of [ADCY2](../genes/ADCY2.md) — the most frequently rearranged gene (21% of patients) in a 34-patient acral lentiginous melanoma ([ACRM](../cancer_types/ACRM.md)) whole-genome sequencing cohort; all [ADCY2](../genes/ADCY2.md) rearrangements (including the UBE2QL1 partnership) occurred in [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) wild-type tumors [PMID:28373299](../papers/28373299.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [ACRM](../cancer_types/ACRM.md) (acral lentiginous melanoma): structural-variant partner of ADCY2 in a subset of patients [PMID:28373299](../papers/28373299.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- ADCY2-UBE2QL1 rearrangements occur in BRAF wild-type ALM; relationship to NRAS/NF1 status not specified [PMID:28373299](../papers/28373299.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic relevance established from the corpus.

## Open questions

- Whether UBE2QL1 contributes functionally to the oncogenic activity driven by ADCY2 rearrangements, or is a passive partner, has not been determined [PMID:28373299](../papers/28373299.md).
