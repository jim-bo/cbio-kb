TL;DR

Abida et al. expand a foundational metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer (mCRPC) resource from 150 to 429 patients (444 biopsied tumors) profiled by whole-exome sequencing, RNA-seq, and central histopathologic review, and link the genomic and transcriptomic landscape to longitudinal clinical outcomes — in particular overall survival and time on first-line next-generation androgen receptor signaling inhibitor (ARSI) therapy (abiraterone, enzalutamide, or ARN-509 / apalutamide). In a clinically homogeneous subset of 128 taxane-naive patients initiating first-line ARSI for mCRPC, alterations in RB1, AR, and TP53 were each associated with shorter time on ARSI, but only RB1 alteration was significantly associated with shorter overall survival; on multivariate analysis RB1 was the only molecular feature independently associated with both endpoints (relative risk 3.31 for survival, 6.56 for time on ARSI). The dataset is released as prad_su2c_2019 on cBioPortal and GitHub.

Cohort & data

  • Patients / tumors: 429 patients with metastatic castration-resistant prostate adenocarcinoma enrolled across seven international consortium centers; 444 biopsied tumors profiled (some patients had multiple biopsies).
  • Biopsy sites (n = 444): 37% lymph node, 36% bone, 14% liver, 7% other soft tissue, 3% prostate, 2% lung, 1% unknown.
  • Histopathology (central review, n = 444): 88.8% adenocarcinoma; 3.8% pure small-cell / neuroendocrine prostate carcinoma plus small-cell prostate carcinoma; 7.4% adenocarcinoma with neuroendocrine features; 11.2% (41/366 evaluable) had any neuroendocrine / small-cell features.
  • Assays: whole-exome sequencing on 444 tumors with matched normal blood DNA; RNA-seq on a 332-tumor / 323-patient subset; central IHC histopathology review.
  • Dataset / cohort: prad_su2c_2019 — deposited on cBioPortal and GitHub (cBioPortal/datahub/public/prad_su2c_2019).
  • Treatment exposure at biopsy: 47% ARSI-naive, 46% ARSI-exposed; 63% taxane-naive. Median age at prostate-cancer diagnosis 61 y; median age at profiled biopsy 67 y; median overall survival from biopsy 16 months.
  • Outcome subcohort for genomic–clinical association: 128 taxane-naive patients initiating first-line abiraterone or enzalutamide; 108 of these received the ARSI as monotherapy and were used for time-on-treatment analyses PMID:31061129.

Key findings

  • Mutational landscape recapitulates prior mCRPC cohorts. Most commonly altered genes were AR, ETS family (ERG, ETV1), TP53, PTEN, and RB1. A significant (>20%) subset of patients harbored at least one alteration in a PI3K, cell-cycle, epigenetic, or DNA-repair pathway gene.
  • Oncogenic SNVs concentrated in known drivers. A high fraction of SNVs in AR, TP53, PIK3CA, BRCA2, PTEN, APC, and CDK12 were classified as likely oncogenic; ~60% of ATM SNVs were predicted oncogenic, the remainder missense variants of unknown significance.
  • Co-occurrence / mutual exclusivity. ERG was mutually exclusive with ETV1 and with SPOP / FOXA1; ERG co-occurred with PTEN. TP53 and RB1 alterations co-occurred (consistent with their enrichment in neuroendocrine cancers), and RB1 alterations tended toward mutual exclusivity with AR alterations. CHD1 co-occurred with SPOP. Loss-of-function CDK12 co-occurred with amplification of CCND1 and CDK4, nominating CDK4/6 inhibitor vulnerability in CDK12-mutated tumors.
  • RB1 is the dominant prognostic biomarker. In the n=128 first-line ARSI subset, univariate analysis (Table 1) showed RB1 alteration significantly associated with both overall survival (P = 0.002, concordance probability estimate [CPE] = 0.768) and time on first-line ARSI (P < 0.001, CPE = 0.818); for time on ARSI, RB1 had the strongest discrimination of any tested feature.
  • AR and TP53 alterations shorten ARSI duration but not survival. AR alteration: P = 0.005 (CPE = 0.651) for time on first-line ARSI; P = 0.658 for survival. TP53: P = 0.046 (CPE = 0.609) for time on ARSI; P = 0.072 for survival.
  • No association for PI3K-pathway or BRCA2/BRCA1/ATM alterations with outcome on ARSI in this tumor-tissue cohort. Univariate PI3K-pathway: P = 0.699 (survival) / 0.138 (time on ARSI); BRCA2/BRCA1/ATM composite: P = 0.495 / 0.611. This contrasts with prior cfDNA-based reports (Annala et al., PMID:28950925 — not in corpus) — see Limitations & open questions.
  • AR splice variants in tumor tissue are not prognostic. AR-V7 spliced reads per million (SRPM), AR-V7/AR-promoter-1:2 ratio, and AR-V3 SRPM were not significantly associated with overall survival or time on first-line ARSI, including when an optimized AR-V7 cutpoint was derived (AR-V7 cutpoint 1.92, P = 0.62), contrary to prior CTC-based studies.
  • Aneuploid chromosomal status associated with worse overall survival and shorter time on ARSI vs diploid (SI Appendix Fig. S2).
  • Expression signatures track RB1 loss. RB1-loss expression score (P < 0.001, n = 99) and cell-cycle progression (CCP) score (P = 0.002) were both significantly associated with shorter survival; NEPC expression score and AR signaling score were not.
  • Multivariate model: RB1 stands alone. Of all molecular features tested, only RB1 alteration was independently associated with overall survival (relative risk 3.31) and with time on first-line ARSI (relative risk 6.56) (Table 2) PMID:31061129.
  • Neuroendocrine features enrich post-ARSI. Histopathologic NE features were present in 10.5% of post-ARSI tumors vs 2.3% of ARSI-naive tumors. Integrative clustering of RNA-seq + histology + RB1/TP53 status yielded three groups: AR-high/NE-low (predominantly adenocarcinoma without NE features, 86%), intermediate AR/NE, and AR-low/NE-high (74% with histologic NE features). A small subset (10/332 RNA-seq cases; 3%) was AR-low/NE-low with adenocarcinoma histology — a candidate “double-negative” state.
  • SPOP mutation associated with longer time on first-line ARSI (SI Appendix Fig. S1), consistent with the favorable prognosis of SPOP-mutated tumors reported in localized disease, though this did not translate into a survival benefit in this cohort.

Genes & alterations

  • AR — amplifications and missense mutations; AR alteration frequency is higher post-ARSI than in ARSI-naive tumors and is associated with shorter time on first-line ARSI (P = 0.005, CPE = 0.651) but not overall survival. AR-amplified tumors have higher AR pathway expression score. AR splice variants (AR-V7, AR-V3) detected in tumor RNA were not prognostic in this cohort.
  • TP53 — among the most frequently altered genes; co-occurs with RB1; shorter time on first-line ARSI (P = 0.046, CPE = 0.609); not significant for survival in this cohort (P = 0.072).
  • RB1 — strongest single prognostic marker. Genomic alteration associated with shorter survival (P = 0.002, CPE 0.768; multivariate RR 3.31) and shorter time on ARSI (P < 0.001, CPE 0.818; multivariate RR 6.56). Co-occurs with TP53; tends to be mutually exclusive with AR alteration. The authors note RB1 loss is likely underestimated by homozygous-deletion calls alone (epigenetic loss, structural events, and focal IHC-only loss also occur).
  • PTEN — frequently altered (32% in the PI3K-pathway oncoprint); co-occurs with ERG; not independently prognostic on ARSI in this cohort.
  • PIK3CA (12%), PIK3CB (4%), PIK3R1 (2.8%), AKT1 (0.9%) — define the broader PI3K-pathway alteration set; pathway alteration not associated with survival or time on ARSI.
  • BRCA2 (11%), BRCA1 (1.9%), ATM (7%) — homologous-recombination repair gene alterations; no association with survival or time on ARSI in this tumor cohort (composite P = 0.495 / 0.611), in contrast to prior cfDNA-based findings.
  • CDK12 — loss-of-function alterations co-occur with CCND1 / CDK4 amplification; authors propose CDK4/6-inhibitor sensitivity hypothesis.
  • SPOP — mutations associated with longer time on first-line ARSI in this subset (SI Appendix Fig. S1), but no survival benefit; mutually exclusive with ERG; co-occurs with CHD1.
  • ERG / ETV1 — ETS-family fusions, mutually exclusive with each other and with SPOP / FOXA1.
  • FOXA1, APC, CTNNB1, RNF43, AURKA — recurrently altered but not significantly prognostic in this subset (Table 1 / Fig. 2A).

Clinical implications

  • RB1 as a clinically actionable prognostic biomarker. RB1 alteration identifies a group with markedly worse survival and rapid failure of first-line ARSI — an enriched candidate population for more aggressive or alternative first-line strategies. The authors explicitly call this out as identifying patients “who may benefit from a more aggressive treatment approach.”
  • AR alteration suggests incomplete AR targeting by current ARSIs. Higher AR-amplification frequency post-ARSI plus shorter time on first-line abiraterone / enzalutamide in AR-altered tumors imply that deeper AR pathway suppression may be clinically beneficial in patients who develop resistance to these next-generation AR-targeting agents.
  • AR-V7 RNA detection in tumor tissue has limited clinical utility, in contrast to AR-V7 protein/RNA detection in circulating tumor cells, which has been prognostic in prior studies.
  • PI3K- and DNA-repair–pathway alterations are not, by themselves, prognostic on ARSI in this tumor cohort — relevant context for interpreting cfDNA-based biomarker studies and for designing prospective trials (e.g., PARP-inhibitor and AKT-inhibitor studies referenced by the authors as NCT02952534, NCT02987543, NCT02854436, NCT02525068, NCT03310541) that select on these alterations.
  • CDK12-mutated tumors as a CDK4/6-inhibitor hypothesis. Co-occurrence of CDK12 loss with CCND1/CDK4 amplification motivates testing CDK4/6 inhibitors in this subset.
  • Aggressive-variant / neuroendocrine biology. The integrative analysis supports — but does not perfectly recapitulate — the model in which RB1/TP53 co-loss, high NE expression score, and low AR signaling co-segregate with histologic neuroendocrine differentiation. A small AR-low/NE-low “double-negative” subset is highlighted as a distinct clinical state warranting study.

Limitations & open questions

  • Outcome cohort size. The clinical association analysis was restricted to 128 taxane-naive patients starting first-line ARSI for mCRPC (108 for time-on-treatment monotherapy analyses). Authors note a larger cohort might surface additional smaller-effect associations.
  • Discordance with cfDNA-based biomarker reports for BRCA2/BRCA1/ATM and AR-V7. The authors flag this conflict explicitly and propose mechanistic candidates (tumor vs cfDNA biology; difficulty detecting homozygous loss in cfDNA; selection bias from requiring detectable cfDNA; tissue vs CTC AR-V7 differences). This is recorded here as an open cross-paper conflict for the wiki.
  • Underestimation of RB1 loss. The authors note RB1 loss can occur epigenetically, via tandem-duplication structural events, and focally by IHC — all of which are missed by genomic homozygous-deletion calls, so the magnitude of the RB1 prognostic effect is likely conservative.
  • Histopathologic NE classification is hard. Concordance between histologic NE features, NE expression score, and RB1/TP53 loss is imperfect; a subset of cases with discrepant pathology/transcriptomic classification display atypical nuclear features (pleomorphism, irregular nuclear membranes, high mitotic activity) on consensus re-review — suggesting a morphologic spectrum not captured by the binary NE-vs-adenocarcinoma label.
  • Neuroendocrine frequency lower than some recent reports. 10.5% post-ARSI NE features vs 17% in another contemporary study; the authors attribute this in part to inclusion of patients with NE features from an alisertib (Aurora kinase A inhibitor) trial.
  • First-line ARSI heterogeneity. Abiraterone and enzalutamide are pooled as “ARSI”; differential biomarker effects between agents are not resolved.
