## TL;DR

Razavi et al. prospectively profiled plasma cell-free DNA (cfDNA) and matched white-blood-cell (WBC) DNA from 124 patients with metastatic breast cancer, non-small cell lung cancer, or castration-resistant prostate cancer, plus 47 non-cancer controls, using a high-intensity targeted assay covering 508 genes / ~2 Mb at >60,000X raw depth. With matched tumor biopsies sequenced by [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md), they showed that the assay detects 84% of patients' tumor-derived mutations de novo, but that the majority of "somatic" cfDNA variants — 81.6% in controls and 53.2% in cancer patients — are actually clonal hematopoiesis (CH) events identifiable only with matched WBC sequencing. The work establishes that joint cfDNA + WBC sequencing is necessary to avoid mis-calling CH variants (notably in [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md), [TET2](../genes/TET2.md), and [PPM1D](../genes/PPM1D.md)) as tumor-derived.

## Cohort & data

- 161 enrolled metastatic cancer patients (53 [MBC](../cancer_types/BRCA.md), 53 [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md), 55 [CRPC](../cancer_types/PRAD.md)); 124 evaluable in the concordance subset (39 [MBC](../cancer_types/MBC.md), 41 [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md), 44 CRPC). Enrollment Sept 24, 2015 – Aug 1, 2016 at MSKCC (protocol 12-245, NCT01775072) [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- 50 non-cancer control whole-blood samples from San Diego Blood Bank; 47 evaluable after three cfDNA-sequencing failures [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- Plasma cfDNA and buffy-coat WBC gDNA sequenced with the GRAIL targeted DNA assay ([grail_cfdna_508](../methods/grail_cfdna_508.md)): 508 genes spanning ~2 Mb, UMIs, >60,000X raw / ~4,400X median collapsed target coverage; assay error rate 1×10⁻⁵ to 3×10⁻⁵; 95% LoD ≈0.36% VAF at 30 ng input [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- Matched tumor biopsies sequenced by [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) (410-gene FDA-authorized CLIA assay, mean >900X coverage); comparisons restricted to the 410-gene panel intersection [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- Dataset deposited as [cfdna_msk_2019](../datasets/cfdna_msk_2019.md) in cBioPortal.

## Key findings

- **De novo tumor-mutation detection.** At least one tumor-derived (biopsy-matched) mutation was detected in cfDNA in 104/124 patients (84%, 95% CI 76–90%). Per-cancer detection: MBC 95% (95% CI 83–99%), NSCLC 76% (95% CI 60–80%), CRPC 82% (95% CI 67–92%); MBC > NSCLC, p=0.0258 [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- **Per-mutation recovery.** 530/740 (72%, 95% CI 66–75%) of MSK-IMPACT tumor-biopsy mutations were re-detected de novo in cfDNA, with comparable rates across MBC (73%), NSCLC (71%), and CRPC (71%) [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- **Clonality matters.** Detection in cfDNA correlated strongly with cancer cell fraction in the biopsy; clonal mutations were detected far more often than subclonal ones (p=5.33e-21) [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- **Disease burden drives ctDNA fraction.** ctDNA fraction varied significantly by tumor type (p=0.0046) and correlated with imaging-based disease burden: MBC (n=34, p=1.03e-4) and NSCLC (n=29, p=0.042) by volumetric CT, CRPC (n=39, p=0.023) by automated bone scan index [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- **Most cfDNA "somatic" variants are CH.** 81.6% (297/364) of cfDNA somatic mutations in controls and 53.2% (918/1727) in non-hypermutated cancer patients had supporting reads in matched WBCs (WBC-matched). 89.5% of cancer patients and 83% of controls had evidence of CH in cfDNA; 99.1% of cancer-patient WBCs and 93.6% of control WBCs harbored at least one CH mutation [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- **CH genes recur.** WBC-matched cfDNA variants preferentially affected canonical CH genes — [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md), [TET2](../genes/TET2.md), [PPM1D](../genes/PPM1D.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md); in 41.6% of cancer patients the highest-VAF mutation was in one of 15 canonical CH genes ([DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md), [TET2](../genes/TET2.md), [ASXL1](../genes/ASXL1.md), [PPM1D](../genes/PPM1D.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md), [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md), [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md), [SRSF2](../genes/SRSF2.md), [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md), [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md), [U2AF1](../genes/U2AF1.md), [CBL](../genes/CBL.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md)) [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- **Therapy-related CH in PPM1D.** [PPM1D](../genes/PPM1D.md) mutations — particularly truncating variants enriched in the C-terminal domain — were significantly more common in cancer patients than controls (age-adjusted p=0.0115) and significantly more common in patients with prior chemotherapy and/or radiation than in untreated patients (age- and smoking-adjusted p=0.0008) [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- **Age dependence.** Number of WBC-matched variants strongly correlated with age (smoking-adjusted p=3.86e-27); biopsy-matched / biopsy-subthreshold counts did not. VUSo (variants of unknown source) were only weakly age-associated (p=0.141) [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- **Tumor heterogeneity in hypermutators.** 10 cfDNA samples were called hypermutated (cfDNA TMB > median + 2×IQR); four matched a biopsy TMB call >13.8 mut/Mb, six were detected only by cfDNA. The 10 hypermutators accounted for 75% of biopsy-subthreshold + VUSo variants across the cohort and 77.7% (994/1280) of cancer-patient VUSo. In hypermutated samples, 41.2% of tumor-derived non-WBC-matched variants were subclonal vs 15.3% in non-hypermutated samples (p=1.7e-13) [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- **Mutational signatures recoverable from cfDNA.** All five hypermutated MBCs and one of three hypermutated CRPCs exhibited APOBEC signatures (COSMIC sigs 2 & 13); hypermutated NSCLCs combined smoking signature with APOBEC, HRD, and MMR contributions [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- **MSI detection.** Using a depth-adjusted [MSIsensor](../methods/msisensor.md), one CRPC patient was identified as MSI-H from cfDNA and exhibited a dominant MMR mutational signature; the patient received an anti-PD-L1 inhibitor and showed rapid sustained tumor regression by RECIST v1.1 and PSA [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- **CNV detection.** Concordance of cfDNA-called CNVs with biopsy was good only when ctDNA fraction ≥10%. Among five patients with actionable amplifications (4 [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md)-amplified MBCs, 1 [MET](../genes/MET.md)-amplified NSCLC), three [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) amplifications were detected de novo; the two missed cases had ctDNA fractions of 1.3% and 1.9% [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- **VUSo are largely tumor-derived in hypermutators.** Cancer-patient VUSo affected late-evolution / subclonal cancer genes — [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) in MBC; [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) T790M in NSCLC; [AR](../genes/AR.md) mutations in CRPC — and orthogonal [ddPCR](../methods/droplet-digital-pcr.md) validation showed complete agreement [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).

## Genes & alterations

- [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md), [TET2](../genes/TET2.md) — most frequent CH drivers; the genes whose mutations most often had the highest VAF in cfDNA in both cancer patients and controls [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- [PPM1D](../genes/PPM1D.md) — therapy-related CH gene; truncating C-terminal mutations significantly enriched in cancer patients vs controls (age-adjusted p=0.0115) and in chemotherapy/radiation-exposed patients (age- and smoking-adjusted p=0.0008) [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — recurrent in WBC-matched cfDNA variants; can be mis-interpreted as tumor-derived without matched WBC sequencing [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- [ASXL1](../genes/ASXL1.md), [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md), [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md), [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md), [SRSF2](../genes/SRSF2.md), [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md), [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md), [U2AF1](../genes/U2AF1.md), [CBL](../genes/CBL.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md) — full list of 15 canonical CH genes used in this paper to classify WBC-matched variants [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) — late/subclonal mutations in MBC detected in cfDNA as VUSo not captured by single biopsy [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) T790M — example of subclonal NSCLC resistance mutation detected as VUSo [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- [AR](../genes/AR.md) — recurrent VUSo in CRPC, consistent with subclonal androgen-receptor evolution [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) — actionable amplification detected de novo in 3 of 4 ERBB2-amplified MBC cases by cfDNA CNV calling [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- [MET](../genes/MET.md) — actionable amplification in one NSCLC case; cfDNA CNV detection limited by low ctDNA fraction [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).

## Clinical implications

- **Joint cfDNA + WBC sequencing is required for accurate variant interpretation.** Without matched WBC, CH variants in cancer genes (e.g., TP53) can be misattributed to the tumor, distorting tumor mutational burden, mutational-signature, and actionable-mutation calls [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- **cfDNA-based TMB / signature / MSI inference is feasible.** With sufficient genomic footprint and depth, the assay supports de novo measurement of TMB, MSI, and mutational signatures, including in patients without contemporaneous tissue [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- **MSI-H detection from cfDNA can guide immunotherapy.** A CRPC patient identified as MSI-H solely from cfDNA had a rapid, sustained response to anti-PD-L1 therapy [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- **Therapy history is a confounder.** Prior chemotherapy/radiation enriches for PPM1D-driven CH, so interpretation of plasma PPM1D variants in heavily pre-treated patients must consider therapy-related CH [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- **Hypermutators benefit most from broad-footprint cfDNA.** A single biopsy systematically under-samples subclones in hypermutator tumors; cfDNA can complement biopsy by recovering resistance-associated subclonal mutations (e.g., [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) T790M, [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md), [AR](../genes/AR.md)) [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- **CNV detection requires adequate ctDNA fraction.** De novo cfDNA CNV calling for actionable amplifications (e.g., ERBB2, [MET](../genes/MET.md)) was only reliable when ctDNA fraction ≥10%; low-ctDNA cases need orthogonal tissue confirmation [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).

## Limitations & open questions

- Colorectal carcinoma — a major indication for cfDNA testing — was not included [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- Tumor sequencing relied on a single FFPE biopsy per patient; multiregional sampling was not feasible, so tumor heterogeneity may be under-captured even after the cfDNA add-on [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- Non-cancer controls came from a different source (San Diego Blood Bank) and were processed in different batches than cancer samples, introducing potential batch effects [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- Per-subtype cohorts (~40 each for MBC, NSCLC, CRPC) are small relative to the clinical and genomic heterogeneity of these diseases [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- The hierarchical Bayesian noise model was trained on <50 healthy donors; longitudinal expansion would improve calibration [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- A residual subset of VUSo may still originate from CH not detectable in matched WBC, other forms of somatic mosaicism, occult cancers, or benign neoplasms — these cannot be ruled out even with this assay design [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- The high per-sample cost of >60,000X cfDNA sequencing is currently a barrier to broad clinical adoption [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md).
- Open question: how the prevalence and clinical impact of therapy-related PPM1D CH evolve under successive lines of cytotoxic therapy.
