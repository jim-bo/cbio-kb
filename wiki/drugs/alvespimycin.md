# alvespimycin

## Overview

Alvespimycin (17-DMAG) is a water-soluble geldanamycin analogue that inhibits HSP90. It is the reduced, active metabolite form of [tanespimycin](../drugs/tanespimycin.md) (17-AAG). [NQO1](../genes/NQO1.md) reduces 17-AAG to 17-DMAG in tumors, meaning [NQO1](../genes/NQO1.md) expression modulates sensitivity to both agents. GI50 for alvespimycin is inversely correlated with [NQO1](../genes/NQO1.md) expression in liver cancer cell lines.

## Evidence in the corpus

- HSP90 inhibitors [tanespimycin](../drugs/tanespimycin.md) (17-AAG) and alvespimycin (17-DMAG) nominated as candidate strategies for KEAP1/NFE2L2-mutated [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md): GI50 for 17-AAG/17-DMAG was inversely correlated with [NQO1](../genes/NQO1.md) expression (Pearson r = −0.56, P = 0.0015) across 29 liver cancer cell lines; two of three KEAP1-mutant lines were highly sensitive; the third (MHCC97H) was resistant due to homozygous [NQO1](../genes/NQO1.md) P187S (rs1800566) variant that destabilizes [NQO1](../genes/NQO1.md) protein [PMID:25822088](../papers/25822088.md)

## Resistance mechanisms

- NQO1 P187S (rs1800566) polymorphism destabilizes NQO1 protein and confers resistance to alvespimycin/tanespimycin in KEAP1-mutated [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) cell line MHCC97H [PMID:25822088](../papers/25822088.md)
