TL;DR

Setton et al. analyzed germline data from an MSK-IMPACT–profiled cohort of 18,087 cancer patients (including 2,265 breast and 1,157 ovarian cancers) and showed that loss-of-function germline variants in the RAD51 paralog RAD51B are significantly enriched in patients with breast or ovarian cancer relative to gnomAD non-cancer controls (carrier frequency 0.26% vs 0.09%; OR 2.69, 95% CI 1.4–5.3, p = 0.004). Tumors with biallelic RAD51B inactivation (germline LOF + LOH) showed genomic features of homologous-recombination deficiency (HRD) — dominant Signature 3 and elevated large-scale state transition (LST) scores — and silencing of RAD51B in MCF-10A/MCF-12A breast epithelial lines impaired RAD51 nuclear focus formation and conferred sensitivity to olaparib and mitomycin-c. The authors argue RAD51B should be added to clinical germline panels for hereditary breast and ovarian cancer.

Cohort & data

  • MSK discovery cohort: 18,087 cancer patients consented to paired tumor/germline sequencing under an MSKCC IRB-approved protocol and profiled with the MSK-IMPACT panel (n = 9,287 reported as the IMPACT-sequenced subset in Methods). Breast and ovarian indications: 2,265 BRCA and 1,157 OVARY cases. Dataset slug: mixed_msk_tcga_2021.
  • Index breast/ovarian sub-cohort with germline consent: 3,422 consecutive breast or ovarian cancer patients used for the carrier-frequency comparison; 9 of these carried a RAD51B LOF germline variant.
  • Validation/expansion: 6 additional RAD51B germline-carrier cases were identified in TCGA, with 3 confirmed biallelic. Whole-exome sequencing data (whole-exome-seq) was available for the 5 biallelic cases used for signature/LST analysis (Fig. 1A).
  • Control population: gnomAD non-cancer subset (gnomad-non-cancer-controls) — 138,632 individuals, 136 (0.10%) RAD51B LOF carriers pan-cancer.
  • Functional models: non-transformed MCF-10A and MCF-12A breast epithelial lines (ATCC); U2OS reporter cells for DR-GFP HR assays (hr-nhej-gfp-reporter-assay); doxycycline-inducible shRNA knockdown (shrna-rnai-screen); clonogenic survival to drug (clonogenic-survival-assay).
  • Bioinformatics: FACETS for allele-specific copy-number/LOH calls; SigMA for Signature 3 detection from targeted panels (samples with ≥5 somatic SNVs); LST ≥15 cut-off for HRD-positive structural calls; variant classification framed against ACMG criteria.

Key findings

  • Breast/ovarian carrier enrichment. Among 3,422 breast or ovarian cancer patients consented to germline analysis, 9 (0.26%) carried a RAD51B LOF germline variant vs 136/138,632 (0.10%) in gnomAD non-cancer controls — OR 2.69 (95% CI 1.4–5.3), p = 0.004 (Fisher exact). The pan-cancer MSK carrier frequency (13/18,087 = 0.07%) was indistinguishable from gnomAD (Table 1).
  • Female penetrance signal. 9 of 10 female RAD51B LOF carriers in the MSK cohort had breast or ovarian cancer (5/2,265 breast; 4/1,157 ovarian). 8 of 13 carriers had a 1st- or 2nd-degree relative with breast or ovarian cancer (Table 1; Supplementary Fig. 3).
  • Recurrent founder-like allele. 7/13 MSK carriers and 11/13 carriers overall harbored variants predicted to disrupt one or both Walker motifs of RAD51B; the recurrent c.139C>T (p.Arg47*) allele was carried by 7/13 MSK patients and by 6/6 ovarian carriers in Table 2.
  • Hormone-receptor subtype skew distinct from RAD51C/D. 4/5 RAD51B-carrier breast cancers were ER+/PR+/HER2−; the 5th was ER−/PR−/HER2+. None were triple-negative, in contrast to the triple-negative skew reported for RAD51C/RAD51D carriers in prior series. Mean age at diagnosis: 49 (breast, range 30–61) and 67.3 (ovarian, range 58–79).
  • Biallelic inactivation drives genomic HRD. 6 cases with biallelic RAD51B loss (germline LOF + LOH of the WT allele) consistently displayed HRD features; all 5 biallelic cases with WES data harbored dominant Signature 3 and high LST scores (Fig. 1A,B). Five of these six biallelic cases also had biallelic somatic TP53 variants, paralleling the BRCA1/BRCA2-associated HRD context.
  • LOH selection in tumors. LOH of the WT RAD51B allele was observed at a significantly higher rate among carriers of a LOF germline variant (6/18 cases) than among carriers of a VUS (6/59 cases; p = 0.02), consistent with biallelic selection.
  • Intermediate HRD phenotype. Even after restricting WT-allele comparators to LOH-positive cases (to control for LOH-driven LST signal), biallelic RAD51B loss tumors had significantly higher LST scores than WT-RAD51B LOH controls (p = 0.02). Magnitude was intermediate between WT and biallelic BRCA1/BRCA2 loss (Supplementary Fig. 1).
  • Functional HR defect. Two independent shRNAs against RAD51B significantly impaired ionizing-radiation-induced RAD51 nuclear focus formation in MCF-10A and MCF-12A cells (Fig. 3). Knockdown also increased clonogenic sensitivity to olaparib and mitomycin-c, though to a lesser degree than BRCA1 knockdown (Fig. 4) — concordant with the intermediate LST/Signature 3 phenotype.
  • R47* is HR-deficient. In a U2OS DR-GFP reporter assay, the recurrent c.139C>T (p.Arg47) variant failed to complement HR proficiency beyond empty vector, while WT RAD51B restored HR roughly twofold (Supplementary Fig. 2). This provides functional evidence that R47 — formally a VUS by ACMG criteria — is loss-of-function.
  • Male carriers. 3 male MSK carriers had non-mammary malignancies (renal, neuroendocrine carcinoma of colon/rectum, esophagogastric adenocarcinoma), with no evidence of breast cancer predisposition in men (Table 2).

Genes & alterations

  • RAD51B — germline LOF SNVs/indels (frameshift, truncating, canonical-dinucleotide splice-site). Recurrent alleles: c.139C>T (p.Arg47) in 7/13 MSK carriers; c.85-1G>C, c.1036+2C>T, c.85-2delA (essential splice); c.37C>T (p.Gln13), c.204T>A (p.Tyr68), c.318Arg, c.595Ser, c.952A>T (p.Arg318). Biallelic inactivation (germline + LOH) drives HRD phenotype with dominant Signature 3 and LST-high status. Walker A/B motif disruption in 11/13 carriers.
  • RAD51C, RAD51D — referenced as established cancer predisposition genes; combined carrier frequency 16/3,422 in the same cohort, comparable to the 9/3,422 observed for RAD51B.
  • TP53 — biallelic somatic variants observed in 5/6 biallelic RAD51B tumors, consistent with selection in BRCA-associated HRD cancers.
  • BRCA1, BRCA2, PALB2, BRIP1, ATM — cited as established HR pathway genes; used as biological/comparator context. 3 additional RAD51B germline carriers were excluded from the primary analysis because they co-harbored a deleterious variant in BRCA2, RAD51D, or ATM.
  • XRCC2, XRCC3 — cited as the other classical RAD51 paralogs.

Clinical implications

  • Panel-testing recommendation. The authors argue that RAD51B should be added to clinical multigene germline panels for hereditary breast and ovarian cancer risk prediction, on par with RAD51C and RAD51D.
  • Therapeutic actionability. Biallelic RAD51B loss confers a measurable HRD phenotype (Signature 3, LST-high) and functional sensitivity to olaparib (PARP inhibitor) and mitomycin-c (interstrand crosslinker). Magnitude of sensitivity is intermediate between WT and BRCA1-deficient cells, predicting partial — not necessarily complete — benefit from HRD-targeting therapies.
  • VUS reclassification. The DR-GFP functional data argues that the truncating c.139C>T (p.Arg47*) allele, currently arguable as a VUS by ACMG criteria, should be treated as loss-of-function for HR proficiency.
  • Subtype counseling. Unlike RAD51C/RAD51D (associated with triple-negative breast cancer), RAD51B-associated breast cancers in this cohort were predominantly hormone-receptor positive (4/5 ER+/PR+/HER2−). Authors flag this association as needing confirmation in larger series.

Limitations & open questions

  • Limited statistical power. Only 9/3,422 breast/ovarian carriers; the cohort is too small to robustly assess hormone-receptor subtype association or rule out ethnic/ancestry confounding when benchmarking against the entire gnomAD database. 6/13 MSK carriers were Hispanic and 2/13 Turkish — the authors raise the possibility that RAD51B may contribute disproportionately to BRCA/OV risk in non-Caucasian populations and call for replication in additional populations.
  • Targeted-panel signature analysis. SigMA-based Signature 3 calls on MSK-IMPACT data are dependent on having ≥5 somatic SNVs and are inherently lower-resolution than WGS-based HRDetect; deletion-length and microhomology-score differences trended but did not reach significance (Fig. 2).
  • No structural-variant analysis. WES does not resolve structural variants; prior WGS work (Wang et al. 2017) linked RAD51B inactivation to a foldback-inversion enriched HRD subgroup. This paper cannot test that linkage.
  • No RNA-seq for splice variants. Three carriers had canonical splice-site variants; computational prediction is >95% PPV but no transcript-level confirmation of intron retention / aberrant splicing was performed.
  • Male carriers. No evidence here of male breast cancer predisposition for RAD51B LOF carriers, in contrast to the prior GWAS association of the intron-7 SNP rs1314913 with male breast cancer risk; the relationship between non-coding regulatory variation and coding LOF remains unresolved.
  • Generalizability of cell-line sensitivity. Knockdown phenotypes in MCF-10A/MCF-12A may not fully recapitulate the in vivo tumor response to olaparib — no patient outcome data on PARP-inhibitor therapy is reported.
