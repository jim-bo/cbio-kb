# FABP4

## Overview

FABP4 (Fatty Acid Binding Protein 4), also known as adipocyte FABP (A-FABP) or aP2, is a lipid chaperone primarily expressed in adipocytes and macrophages. In hepatocellular carcinoma ([HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)), FABP4 has been reported to play a tumor-suppressive role, with reduced expression associated with poor prognosis, contrasting with the tumor-promoting roles of other family members such as [FABP1](../genes/FABP1.md) and [FABP5](../genes/FABP5.md).

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- FABP4 reported to have a tumor suppressor role in HCC — suppresses proliferation and invasion; low FABP4 expression is a poor-prognosis marker in HCC. [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md): FABP4 expression loss associated with more aggressive HCC behavior (increased proliferation and invasion); functions as a poor-prognosis biomarker in this context. [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- FABP4's tumor-suppressive role in HCC contrasts with the tumor-promoting activities of FABP1 and FABP5 within the same fatty acid binding protein family, highlighting context-dependent roles of FABP members. [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting of FABP4 in HCC has been validated; its potential as a prognostic biomarker in HCC requires prospective evaluation.

## Open questions

- Mechanism by which FABP4 loss promotes HCC progression (whether via lipid signaling, immune modulation, or other pathways) is not fully characterized.
- Whether FABP4 expression can stratify HCC patients for specific treatment regimens remains an open question.
