**PMID:** 25583476 · **DOI:** 10.1073/pnas.1422640112 · **Journal:** PNAS (2015)

## TL;DR

Chen et al. performed whole-exome sequencing on 78 primary gastric adenocarcinomas (GCs) from a northern Chinese cohort (Tianjin Medical University Cancer Institute and Hospital), whole-genome sequencing on two cases with multiple primary-tumor and lymph-node-metastasis samples each, and targeted resequencing of 103 recurrently mutated [Wnt](../themes/), [ERBB](../themes/), and homologous-recombination pathway genes in a 216-case validation cohort. Clonality analysis (SciClone) separated GC into a high-clonality (HiC, n=9) subtype — older patients, enriched for [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations and C>G transitions, with significantly shorter survival (adjusted HR 4.69, 95% CI 1.62–13.6, P=0.0043) — and a low-clonality (LoC, n=68) subtype — younger, enriched for [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) mutations, longer survival. The targetable [NRG1](../genes/NRG1.md)–[ERBB4](../genes/ERBB4.md) ligand-receptor pair was mutated in 11.6% (34/294) of cases, and [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) mutations (8% of the discovery cohort, validated in [TCGA](../datasets/stad_tcga_pub.md)) marked a subgroup with significantly longer survival (pooled log-rank P=0.03; adjusted HR 0.37, 95% CI 0.13–0.96, P=0.05).

## Cohort & data

- **Discovery cohort:** 78 frozen primary gastric adenocarcinoma samples (treatment-naive, no prior chemo/radiotherapy) from northern Chinese patients with matching germline blood DNA; [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) on Illumina HiSeq 2000 with Agilent SureSelect capture; mean coverage 167× tumor / 170× normal; 85% of exons ≥20×.
- **WGS sub-cohort:** Two patients (Pt1, Pt2) each with three primary-tumor regions plus two matched lymph-node metastases (5 samples per case) by [whole-genome sequencing](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md).
- **Validation cohort:** 216 additional GC samples from the same population by [targeted sequencing](../methods/targeted-dna-seq.md) of 103 recurrent genes on the Ion Torrent PGM platform.
- **Full study population:** 294 patients (after excluding 5 with discrepant pathology). Anatomic location: antrum 64 (21.77%), body 118 (40.14%), cardia 112 (38.10%). Histology: 124 intestinal-type, 152 diffuse-type, 18 mixed. Stage: I 6 (2.05%), II 85 (28.91%), III 97 (32.99%), IV 106 (36.05%).
- **Follow-up:** Median 13.84 months (WES series 25.08 months, 32 deaths/41.03%; targeted-seq series 12.16 months, 61 deaths/28.24%).
- **Cancer type:** Gastric adenocarcinoma ([STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md)).
- **Dataset:** [egc_tmucih_2015](../datasets/egc_tmucih_2015.md); validation comparisons against the Russian TCGA gastric cohort ([stad_tcga_pub](../datasets/stad_tcga_pub.md), n=289).
- **Variant calling:** [MuTect](../methods/mutect.md) and [VarScan 2.2.5](../methods/varscan.md); driver discovery with [MutSigCV](../methods/mutsig.md); clonality with SciClone; multi-region [phylogenetic tree reconstruction](../methods/phylogenetic-tree-reconstruction.md); drug-gene interactions via DGIdb. Sequence data deposited at EGA under accession EGAS00001001056.

## Key findings

- **Mutation burden:** 13,866 somatic mutations across 78 WES tumors (3,421 synonymous, 8,558 missense, 576 nonsense, 241 splice-site, 967 frameshift, 103 non-frameshift indels). Median 82.5 nonsilent mutations per tumor (range 1–1,486); 11,768 protein-coding subclonal SNVs (92.3% of detected mutations, median 106 per sample).
- **HiC vs. LoC subtypes:** SciClone clustering identified 9 HiC (>4 clones) and 68 LoC (≤4 clones) tumors. HiC tumors were associated with older diagnosis age (Pearson r=0.27, P=0.02), significantly higher [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation frequency, and significantly higher [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) frequency in the LoC group (both P=0.003). HiC GCs had shorter survival (log-rank P=0.02); the HiC–survival association remained significant after adjusting for age, sex, stage, Lauren subtype, [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) mutation status (adjusted HR 4.69, 95% CI 1.62–13.6, P=0.0043), establishing clonal subtype as an **independent prognostic factor**.
- **Mutational signatures:** HiC tumors had a smaller fraction of C>T mutations (45% vs. 52% in LoC) and a higher fraction of C>G mutations (25% vs. 9% in LoC, P=0.002), suggesting distinct mutagenic etiologies.
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md) in HiC is subclonal:** All six [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)-mutant HiC tumors carried [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) only in minor clones (mutation frequency <15%), implying that single-agent therapy could select for [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)-wild-type subclones.
- **Driver discovery:** [MutSigCV](../methods/mutsig.md) identified 16 significantly mutated genes (q<0.2); 13 mutated in ≥5% of tumors, including previously reported drivers [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [CDH1](../genes/CDH1.md), [APC](../genes/APC.md), [RHOA](../genes/RHOA.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md), [MYC](../genes/MYC.md), and [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md). Pathway enrichment highlighted [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), Wnt, ERBB, and homologous-recombination pathways.
- **Validation:** 86/103 (83%) recurrent genes selected from Wnt/ERBB/HR pathways were re-identified in the 216-case validation cohort.
- **Anatomic-location associations (WES):** Antrum tumors enriched for mutations in [CSMD3](../genes/CSMD3.md) (30.7%), TSHZ3 (15.4%), PCDHA11 (19.2%), ARHGAP28 (15.4%), DST (23.1%), SORCS1 (19.2%), MUC17 (15.4%), PCDH20 (19.2%), USP9X (19.2%), CACNA2D1 (15.4%); body tumors enriched in FLG2 (15.4%), GRM1 (15.4%), [SYNE2](../genes/SYNE2.md) (15.4%); cardia tumors enriched in [CDH1](../genes/CDH1.md) (23.1%), GFRAL (15.4%), SBNO1 (15.4%), STAB2 (15.4%) (all P<0.05). After validation cohort expansion, 7 of these (underlined in original) remained significant. None of these anatomic-location associations were observed in the [TCGA](../datasets/stad_tcga_pub.md) Russian cohort, suggesting population-specific mutational distribution.
- **Lauren histology:** [CDH1](../genes/CDH1.md) somatic mutations (21/294, 7.1%) were enriched in diffuse GC, with 8 of 21 being loss-of-function (nonsense/frameshift). [RHOA](../genes/RHOA.md) mutations were likewise enriched in diffuse GC (consistent with Wang et al. 2014 and Kakiuchi et al. 2014). Wnt pathway mutations showed moderate enrichment in intestinal GC. [CDH1](../genes/CDH1.md)-mutated GCs had shorter survival while Wnt-mutated GCs had longer survival (log-rank P=0.04); [RHOA](../genes/RHOA.md)-mutated cases were *not* associated with shorter survival, suggesting [RHOA](../genes/RHOA.md)-driven and [CDH1](../genes/CDH1.md)-driven diffuse GCs are clinically distinct.
- **[NRG1](../genes/NRG1.md)–[ERBB4](../genes/ERBB4.md) axis:** [ERBB4](../genes/ERBB4.md) mutated in 7 discovery + 13 validation = 20 cases; [NRG1](../genes/NRG1.md) mutated in 5 discovery + 16 validation = 21 cases. Combined, [NRG1](../genes/NRG1.md) or [ERBB4](../genes/ERBB4.md) was mutated in **34 of 294 GC samples (11.6%)**, with 3 dual-mutated and 31 mutated in only one of the pair. 7/21 (33%) [ERBB4](../genes/ERBB4.md) mutations fell in the kinase domain and 4 (20%) in receptor domains. [ERBB4](../genes/ERBB4.md) p.R50C overlaps with a melanoma hotspot; [TCGA](../datasets/stad_tcga_pub.md) [STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md) also harbored [ERBB4](../genes/ERBB4.md) kinase-domain mutations (p.V744L, p.774N/G) and receptor-domain mutations (p.R106H/C). [NRG1](../genes/NRG1.md) EGF-like-domain mutations (p.A221T, p.A225P, p.E223G, p.R224Q, p.S226P) cluster near the predicted [ERBB4](../genes/ERBB4.md)-binding site. In 1,000 permutations, ERBB-family and NRG-family mutations were mutually exclusive (P=0.02).
- **[BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) and survival:** 5/78 discovery + 12/216 validation = 17 [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md)-mutant cases in Tianjin; no [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) mutations observed. [TCGA](../datasets/stad_tcga_pub.md) [STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md) carried [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) mutations in 28/289 cases. In Tianjin alone, [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) mutation was marginally associated with longer survival (P=0.07, HR=0.29, 95% CI 0.07–1.19); pooling Tianjin + [TCGA](../datasets/stad_tcga_pub.md) (n=569, 45 [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) carriers) yielded log-rank P=0.03. After adjusting for age, stage, and cohort, [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) mutation remained an independent predictor of longer survival (HR=0.37, 95% CI 0.13–0.96, P=0.05).
- **[BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) mutation profile:** Most [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) mutations in GC were missense, contrasting with the predominantly nonsense/frameshift mutations seen in triple-negative breast and ovarian cancer.
- **Clonal evolution (WGS):** Pt1 had 4,082 nonsilent mutations (2,809 missense, 143 nonsense, 132 splice-site, 958 frameshift, 40 in-frame indels); Pt2 had 287 nonsilent mutations. Pt1 carried mismatch-repair and chromatin-remodeling mutations in [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md), [TGFBR2](../genes/TGFBR2.md), [KDM5A](../genes/KDM5A.md), and [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) (reported as MLL4); Pt2 carried [SETBP1](../genes/SETBP1.md) p.S944N together with [AKAP9](../genes/AKAP9.md) and [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md) amplifications. [CACNA1D](../genes/CACNA1D.md) p.V529G was found in both patients. CNA spanned 516.6 Mb (Pt1) and 706.8 Mb (Pt2); breakpoints 3,812 (Pt1) and 3,390 (Pt2). [Phylogenetic tree analysis](../methods/phylogenetic-tree-reconstruction.md) showed substantial divergence among the three primary-tumor regions, with two lymph-node metastases derived from a common clonal ancestor sharing only one primary-tumor region.

## Genes & alterations

- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)** — significantly enriched in HiC subtype; present only as minor (<15% VAF) subclones in all 6 positive HiC cases. Defines a poor-survival, older-onset GC subtype.
- **[ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md)** — significantly enriched in LoC subtype; associated with younger onset and longer survival.
- **[NRG1](../genes/NRG1.md)** — somatic mutations reported here for the first time in GC; 21/294 cases (~7.1%); EGF-like domain hotspot (p.A221T, p.A225P, p.E223G, p.R224Q, p.S226P). Likely activates [ERBB4](../genes/ERBB4.md) signaling via increased ligand availability.
- **[ERBB4](../genes/ERBB4.md)** — mutated in 20/294 cases (~6.8%); kinase-domain (33%) and receptor-domain (20%) mutations; p.R50C shared with melanoma hotspot. With [NRG1](../genes/NRG1.md), defines a candidate lapatinib-targetable subset.
- **[ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md)** — amplification and mutation confirmed in this cohort; standard [trastuzumab](../drugs/trastuzumab.md) target.
- **[BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md)** — mutated in 17/294 Tianjin cases (5.8%) and 28/289 [TCGA](../datasets/stad_tcga_pub.md) cases; independent predictor of longer survival in pooled cohort (HR 0.37, P=0.05). Predominantly missense, unlike breast/ovarian.
- **[BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md)** — no mutations observed in the 78-case discovery cohort.
- **[CDH1](../genes/CDH1.md)** — 21/294 cases (7.1%) somatic mutations, enriched in diffuse-type and cardia tumors; LoF events common; associated with shorter survival.
- **[RHOA](../genes/RHOA.md)** — enriched in diffuse GC (consistent with [PMID:24816253](24816253.md) and [PMID:24816255](24816255.md) if present); not associated with shorter survival in this cohort, distinguishing it clinically from [CDH1](../genes/CDH1.md)-driven diffuse GC.
- **[APC](../genes/APC.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md), [MYC](../genes/MYC.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)** — recurrently mutated drivers consistent with prior GC studies; participate in Wnt, PI3K-ERBB, and TP53 pathways. Pt1 carried [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) p.G13D, [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) p.E542K, and [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) p.E3002D/p.G602fs across separate clones.
- **[MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md), [TGFBR2](../genes/TGFBR2.md), [KDM5A](../genes/KDM5A.md), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md)** — mismatch-repair and chromatin-remodeling defects in the hypermutated Pt1.
- **[SETBP1](../genes/SETBP1.md) p.S944N, [AKAP9](../genes/AKAP9.md), [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md)** — mutation/amplifications in Pt2; [SETBP1](../genes/SETBP1.md) mutation matches the recurrent myeloid-malignancy site.
- **[CACNA1D](../genes/CACNA1D.md) p.V529G** — shared between Pt1 and Pt2; voltage-dependent calcium channel.

## Clinical implications

- **High-clonality GC needs combination therapy.** Subclonal [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) in HiC tumors implies single-agent regimens may select for resistant minor clones; the authors used DGIdb to enumerate drug targets across subclones (e.g., [ALK](../genes/ALK.md), ABL2, [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md), FANCG, [NRG1](../genes/NRG1.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) in PGM71 and PGM32). For Pt1 specifically, combined targeting of PI3K, [CDK7](../genes/CDK7.md), and Notch plus immunotherapy is proposed; for Pt2, combined [AURKC](../genes/AURKC.md) and [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md) inhibitors.
- **[NRG1](../genes/NRG1.md)–ERBB4 as a [lapatinib](../drugs/lapatinib.md) target.** Mutated [ERBB4](../genes/ERBB4.md) activates ERBB4 and PI3K-AKT signaling and is inhibitable by the dual TKI [lapatinib](../drugs/lapatinib.md), currently in a phase III trial in ERBB4-mutated melanoma (NCT01264081). [NRG1](../genes/NRG1.md) overexpression has been shown to confer [lapatinib](../drugs/lapatinib.md) sensitivity in prior work; 11.6% of GCs carry [NRG1](../genes/NRG1.md) and/or [ERBB4](../genes/ERBB4.md) mutations, defining a candidate biomarker-selected population beyond the <15% of GC patients eligible for [trastuzumab](../drugs/trastuzumab.md).
- **BRCA2 as a prognostic and predictive biomarker.** [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) mutation independently predicts longer survival in pooled Tianjin + [TCGA](../datasets/stad_tcga_pub.md) GC (HR 0.37, P=0.05). The authors note that platinum-based chemotherapy is standard front-line for GC, paralleling ovarian cancer where [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) tumors have improved platinum response; this suggests [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) status could guide platinum and PARP-inhibitor decision-making in GC.
- **Anatomic location matters.** Antrum/body/cardia tumors carry distinct mutation enrichments; cardia GCs are enriched for [CDH1](../genes/CDH1.md), connecting to the diffuse-type/poor-prognosis axis.
- **Clonal subtype as an independent prognostic factor** (adjusted HR 4.69, P=0.0043) — clonality analysis from WES could potentially be developed as a prognostic assay.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Single-population bias:** All Tianjin cases are northern Chinese; the authors note that the anatomic-location mutation enrichments seen in their cohort did not replicate in the Russian [TCGA](../datasets/stad_tcga_pub.md) cohort, suggesting population (genetic, dietary, environmental) effects on mutation distribution. Generalizability to other Asian or Western populations is unknown.
- **HiC group is small:** Only 9 HiC tumors define the lethal subtype. The translational significance of [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) as HiC/LoC markers needs prospective validation.
- **BRCA2 functional impact unknown:** Most GC [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) mutations are missense (unlike breast/ovarian), so deleteriousness is presumed but not functionally validated. The mechanism linking [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) status to longer survival in platinum-treated GC is inferred from ovarian cancer biology.
- **NRG1/ERBB4 mutations not functionally tested:** No experimental demonstration in this paper that the GC-specific [NRG1](../genes/NRG1.md) or [ERBB4](../genes/ERBB4.md) mutations are activating or [lapatinib](../drugs/lapatinib.md)-sensitive; functional validation is required before clinical actionability.
- **Follow-up duration is short:** Median follow-up was 13.84 months overall (25.08 months for WES). Long-term survival differences may be under-estimated.
- **Phylogenetic conclusions rest on two patients:** Multi-region WGS was done in only Pt1 and Pt2; broader clonal-evolution claims are extrapolations.
- **Treatment heterogeneity:** Although the authors note no significant treatment differences between HiC and LoC, treatment regimens are not detailed; residual confounding is possible.
