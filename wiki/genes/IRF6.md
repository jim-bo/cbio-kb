# IRF6

## Overview

IRF6 (Interferon Regulatory Factor 6) is a transcription factor involved in squamous epithelial differentiation. It was identified as a significantly mutated gene in head and neck squamous cell carcinoma (HNSCC), implicating disrupted squamous differentiation programs as a driver mechanism in this cancer type.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Recurrently mutated in HNSCC across 74 tumor-normal pairs by whole-exome sequencing; associated with squamous differentiation (q < 0.25, Broad cohort) [PMID:21798893](../papers/21798893.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md)**: Significantly mutated gene identified by MutSigCV analysis; co-occurs with [TP63](../genes/TP63.md) alterations in the squamous differentiation pathway [PMID:21798893](../papers/21798893.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-mutated with [TP63](../genes/TP63.md) in HNSCC, both implicated in disruption of squamous differentiation [PMID:21798893](../papers/21798893.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting reported in the corpus; its role in squamous differentiation may inform differentiation-based therapeutic strategies.

## Open questions

- Whether IRF6 mutations are drivers or passengers in HNSCC requires functional validation.
- Relationship between IRF6 mutation status and HPV status has not been characterized in the corpus.
