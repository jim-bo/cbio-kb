# Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer Brain Metastasis (MSK, Nat Commun 2023)

## Overview

MSK cohort of resected [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) brain metastases (BM) profiled with MSK-IMPACT alongside matched primary tumors and extracranial metastases, used to characterize the genomic correlates of BM development and patterns of intracranial progression [PMID:37591896](../papers/37591896.md).

## Composition

- 233 patients with resected [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) BM: 180 (77%) [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md), 23 (10%) [LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md), 30 (13%) other [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md); median age 67; 57% female; 80% current/former smokers [PMID:37591896](../papers/37591896.md).
- Matched samples: 47 primary tumors and 42 extracranial metastases; LUAD-restricted downstream analyses used 179 BM, 37 PT, 34 EM [PMID:37591896](../papers/37591896.md).

## Assays / panels (linked)

- [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) targeted panel across [IMPACT341](../methods/IMPACT341.md), [IMPACT410](../methods/IMPACT410.md), [IMPACT468](../methods/IMPACT468.md), [IMPACT505](../methods/IMPACT505.md) versions [PMID:37591896](../papers/37591896.md).

## Papers using this cohort

- [PMID:37591896](../papers/37591896.md) — Skakodub et al., genomic analysis and clinical correlations of [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) brain metastasis.

## Notable findings derived from this cohort

- BM samples had higher TMB (median 8.8 vs 5.8; p=0.00766) and higher FGA than extracranial metastases and primary tumors [PMID:37591896](../papers/37591896.md).
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)/[CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md) deep deletions and broader cell-cycle pathway alterations were enriched in BM vs PT (34% vs 13%, p=0.003, q=0.04) [PMID:37591896](../papers/37591896.md).
- Patients who developed leptomeningeal disease had more [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) alterations (45% vs 21%; p=0.044), frequently non-canonical drivers (L861Q, G719A/S, A755G, N771_H773dup) [PMID:37591896](../papers/37591896.md).
- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) amplifications associated with multifocal regional intracranial progression (22% vs 0% in local progressors; p=0.023) [PMID:37591896](../papers/37591896.md).
