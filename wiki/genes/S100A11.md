# S100A11

## Overview

S100A11 (also known as S100C or MLN70) is a member of the S100 family of calcium-binding proteins. It is involved in regulation of cell proliferation, differentiation, and motility. S100A11 is frequently upregulated in multiple cancer types and has been proposed as a diagnostic biomarker and potential therapeutic target.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Among the most uniformly upregulated proteins across tumor-NAT pairs (≥95% of colon tumor samples), identified as a candidate biomarker/therapeutic target in colon cancer proteogenomics ([coad_cptac_2019](../datasets/coad_cptac_2019.md)) [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Colorectal cancer ([COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md)) — S100A11 protein uniformly upregulated in ≥95% of colon tumors vs normal adjacent tissue; one of 31 cancer-associated proteins with potential diagnostic, outcome, or therapeutic utility identified in CPTAC proteogenomics [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-upregulated with [S100P](../genes/S100P.md), [DDX21](../genes/DDX21.md), [PLOD2](../genes/PLOD2.md), [SERPINH1](../genes/SERPINH1.md), and [GPRC5A](../genes/GPRC5A.md) as the most uniformly overexpressed proteins in colon tumors [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Uniform overexpression in ≥95% of colon tumors makes S100A11 a candidate for biomarker and therapeutic follow-up; 19 of the 31 cancer-associated proteins identified in the same study are detectable in plasma as potential blood biomarkers [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Open questions

- Whether S100A11 overexpression is causally driving tumor biology or is a consequence of the tumor microenvironment remains unresolved; in vivo/clinical validation is outside the scope of the CPTAC proteogenomics study.
