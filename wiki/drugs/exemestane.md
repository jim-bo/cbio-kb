# exemestane

## Overview

Exemestane is an irreversible steroidal aromatase inhibitor (type I AI) that irreversibly inactivates CYP19A1 (aromatase), blocking conversion of androgens to estrogens. It is FDA-approved for postmenopausal women with hormone-receptor-positive (HR+) early and advanced/metastatic breast cancer. Unlike non-steroidal AIs ([letrozole](../drugs/letrozole.md), [anastrozole](../drugs/anastrozole.md)), exemestane causes lasting enzyme degradation ("suicide inhibition"). It is used as an endocrine therapy backbone in PI3Kα inhibitor combination trials.

## Evidence in the corpus

- In NCT01870505 ([alpelisib](../drugs/alpelisib.md) + AI, n=51 HR+ HER2-negative [MBC](../cancer_types/MBC.md)), exemestane was the partner AI in arms B (continuous alpelisib + exemestane, n=7; median TTF 8 weeks) and D (intermittent 5-on/2-off alpelisib 350 mg + daily exemestane, n=25; median TTF 17 weeks). Arm D established the MTD schedule (350 mg 5-on/2-off + exemestane) with numerically lower rash toxicity vs continuous arms. Overall CBR across all arms was 52% (23/44) [PMID:32864625](../papers/32864625.md).

## Resistance mechanisms

- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) loss-of-function alterations (25% of paired ctDNA patients) and [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) activating mutations (D538G, Y537S) were identified as recurrent mechanisms of resistance to alpelisib + exemestane/letrozole in NCT01870505; ESR1 VAFs expanded under combination therapy [PMID:32864625](../papers/32864625.md).
