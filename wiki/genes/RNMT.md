# RNMT

## Overview

RNMT encodes RNA guanine-7 methyltransferase, the catalytic subunit of the cap methyltransferase complex that adds the 7-methylguanosine cap to RNA polymerase II transcripts. Proper mRNA capping is essential for transcript stability and translation. RNMT has been implicated in regulating expression of proliferative genes and has emerging roles in cancer biology.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- RNMT missense variant identified in patient P55, a hypermutator prostate cancer brain metastasis (PCBM) with high SBS10b signature; co-altered with a POLR3C missense variant [PMID:35504881](../papers/35504881.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md)**: RNMT missense in a hypermutator PCBM; SBS10b enrichment consistent with replication proofreading defect or polymerase error accumulation [PMID:35504881](../papers/35504881.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-altered with POLR3C missense in a hypermutator prostate cancer brain metastasis (patient P55) [PMID:35504881](../papers/35504881.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Hypermutation context may confer immune checkpoint inhibitor eligibility; no direct therapy data for RNMT-altered tumors reported in this cohort [PMID:35504881](../papers/35504881.md)

## Open questions

- Whether RNMT missense is a driver of the hypermutation phenotype or a passenger variant in the P55 tumor remains unresolved.
