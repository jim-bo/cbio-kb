# FANCG

## Overview

FANCG (Fanconi Anemia Complementation Group G, also known as XRCC9) is a subunit of the FA core complex essential for [FANCD2](../genes/FANCD2.md) monoubiquitination and interstrand crosslink repair. Biallelic germline FANCG mutations cause Fanconi anemia. In cancer, somatic FANCG inactivation results in homologous recombination deficiency and increased sensitivity to DNA-crosslinking agents.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- FANCG is part of the FA pathway gene classifier used in mCRPC; homozygous deleterious FANCG events qualify patients for the DNA-repair-defect group that showed significantly longer time on [carboplatin](../drugs/carboplatin.md) chemotherapy (log-rank P = 0.02; n=20 treated men at rapid autopsy); siRNA knockdown of individual FA genes reduced proliferation in prostate cancer cell lines and increased gamma-H2AX after carboplatin exposure. [PMID:26928463](../papers/26928463.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md)** — FANCG is part of the FA gene set whose homozygous loss classifies mCRPC patients as DNA-repair deficient; these patients had significantly longer carboplatin response in a rapid-autopsy cohort of 63 men. [PMID:26928463](../papers/26928463.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- FANCG alterations co-occur with other FA pathway gene losses and with elevated [E2F1](../genes/E2F1.md) expression downstream of [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) loss in high-CCP mCRPC tumors. [PMID:26928463](../papers/26928463.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Homozygous deleterious FANCG events support classification of mCRPC patients as DNA-repair deficient; such patients had significantly longer responses to [carboplatin](../drugs/carboplatin.md) (log-rank P = 0.02). [PMID:26928463](../papers/26928463.md)

## Open questions

- The carboplatin response analysis is based on 20 treated men using time-on-drug as surrogate; prospective validation of FANCG as a standalone biomarker is needed. [PMID:26928463](../papers/26928463.md)
