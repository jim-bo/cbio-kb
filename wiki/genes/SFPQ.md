# SFPQ

## Overview

SFPQ (Splicing Factor Proline and Glutamine Rich) is an RNA-binding protein involved in transcriptional regulation and RNA processing. In clear cell renal cell carcinoma (ccRCC), SFPQ is a recurrent fusion partner of [TFE3](../genes/TFE3.md), forming in-frame fusions that are exclusively identified in [VHL](../genes/VHL.md) wildtype tumors and are associated with X(p11) translocations.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Recurrent in-frame fusion with [TFE3](../genes/TFE3.md) (SFPQ–TFE3); identified in 5 samples; all VHL wildtype; often accompanied by X(p11) translocation confirmed by FISH. [PMID:23792563](../papers/23792563.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[CCRCC](../cancer_types/CCRCC.md)**: SFPQ–TFE3 fusions define a VHL-wildtype subset of ccRCC, representing a molecularly distinct subgroup. [PMID:23792563](../papers/23792563.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- SFPQ–TFE3 fusions occur exclusively in [VHL](../genes/VHL.md) wildtype ccRCC tumors, suggesting mutual exclusivity with VHL mutation. [PMID:23792563](../papers/23792563.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No specific therapeutic implication reported in this study; the SFPQ–TFE3 fusion subset may represent a distinct molecular entity warranting separate clinical consideration. [PMID:23792563](../papers/23792563.md)

## Open questions

- Functional consequences of the SFPQ–TFE3 fusion in driving ccRCC in the VHL-wildtype background are not characterized in this study. [PMID:23792563](../papers/23792563.md)
