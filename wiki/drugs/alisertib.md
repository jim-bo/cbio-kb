# alisertib

## Overview

Alisertib (MLN8237) is an oral selective Aurora kinase A ([AURKA](../genes/AURKA.md)) inhibitor. [AURKA](../genes/AURKA.md) plays a key role in mitotic spindle assembly and centrosome function; its inhibition arrests cells in mitosis and promotes apoptosis. Alisertib has been investigated in clinical trials across multiple solid tumors, including prostate cancer with neuroendocrine differentiation and hematologic malignancies.

## Evidence in the corpus

- In the mCRPC cohort ([prad_su2c_2019](../datasets/prad_su2c_2019.md), n=332), the observed post-ARSI neuroendocrine (NE) feature frequency (10.5%) was lower than the 17% reported in a contemporary study; the authors attributed part of this discrepancy to the inclusion of patients with NE features from an alisertib (Aurora kinase A inhibitor) clinical trial in the comparator cohort, highlighting alisertib trials as a context in which aggressive-variant, NE-enriched mCRPC is selectively enrolled. [PMID:31061129](../papers/31061129.md)
- Used as an AURKA inhibitor alternative to LY3295668; a randomized phase II trial (NCT02860000) of alisertib + [fulvestrant](../drugs/fulvestrant.md) in CDK4/6i-progressed HR+ metastatic breast cancer was ongoing at time of reporting [PMID:32404308](../papers/32404308.md).
- Prior HBV-HCC proteogenomic work citing SOAT1/avasimibe inhibition is referenced as precedent for proteome-driven target nomination in HCC; the current phosphoproteogenomic study identifies elevated AURKA kinase activity by KSEA, consistent with alisertib's (Aurora-A inhibitor) preclinical rationale in HCC [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)
