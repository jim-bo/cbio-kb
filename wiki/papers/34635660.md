**PMID:** 34635660 · **DOI:** 10.1038/s41523-021-00339-0 · **Journal:** npj Breast Cancer (2021)

## TL;DR

Setton et al. analyzed germline data from an MSK-IMPACT–profiled cohort of 18,087 cancer patients (including 2,265 breast and 1,157 ovarian cancers) and showed that loss-of-function germline variants in the [RAD51](../genes/RAD51.md) paralog [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md) are significantly enriched in patients with breast or ovarian cancer relative to gnomAD non-cancer controls (carrier frequency 0.26% vs 0.09%; OR 2.69, 95% CI 1.4–5.3, p = 0.004). Tumors with biallelic [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md) inactivation (germline LOF + LOH) showed genomic features of homologous-recombination deficiency (HRD) — dominant Signature 3 and elevated large-scale state transition (LST) scores — and silencing of [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md) in MCF-10A/MCF-12A breast epithelial lines impaired RAD51 nuclear focus formation and conferred sensitivity to [olaparib](../drugs/olaparib.md) and [mitomycin-c](../drugs/mitomycin-c.md). The authors argue [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md) should be added to clinical germline panels for hereditary breast and ovarian cancer.

## Cohort & data

- **MSK discovery cohort:** 18,087 cancer patients consented to paired tumor/germline sequencing under an MSKCC IRB-approved protocol and profiled with the [MSK-IMPACT panel](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) (n = 9,287 reported as the IMPACT-sequenced subset in Methods). Breast and ovarian indications: 2,265 [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) and 1,157 [OVARY](../cancer_types/OVARY.md) cases. Dataset slug: [mixed_msk_tcga_2021](../datasets/mixed_msk_tcga_2021.md).
- **Index breast/ovarian sub-cohort with germline consent:** 3,422 consecutive breast or ovarian cancer patients used for the carrier-frequency comparison; 9 of these carried a [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md) LOF germline variant.
- **Validation/expansion:** 6 additional [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md) germline-carrier cases were identified in TCGA, with 3 confirmed biallelic. Whole-exome sequencing data ([whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md)) was available for the 5 biallelic cases used for signature/LST analysis (Fig. 1A).
- **Control population:** gnomAD non-cancer subset ([gnomad-non-cancer-controls](../methods/gnomad-non-cancer-controls.md)) — 138,632 individuals, 136 (0.10%) [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md) LOF carriers pan-cancer.
- **Functional models:** non-transformed MCF-10A and MCF-12A breast epithelial lines (ATCC); U2OS reporter cells for DR-GFP HR assays ([hr-nhej-gfp-reporter-assay](../methods/hr-nhej-gfp-reporter-assay.md)); doxycycline-inducible shRNA knockdown ([shrna-rnai-screen](../methods/shrna-rnai-screen.md)); clonogenic survival to drug ([clonogenic-survival-assay](../methods/clonogenic-survival-assay.md)).
- **Bioinformatics:** [FACETS](../methods/facets.md) for allele-specific copy-number/LOH calls; [SigMA](../methods/sigma-mutational-signatures.md) for Signature 3 detection from targeted panels (samples with ≥5 somatic SNVs); LST ≥15 cut-off for HRD-positive structural calls; variant classification framed against [ACMG criteria](../methods/acmg-variant-classification.md).

## Key findings

- **Breast/ovarian carrier enrichment.** Among 3,422 breast or ovarian cancer patients consented to germline analysis, 9 (0.26%) carried a [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md) LOF germline variant vs 136/138,632 (0.10%) in gnomAD non-cancer controls — OR 2.69 (95% CI 1.4–5.3), p = 0.004 (Fisher exact). The pan-cancer MSK carrier frequency (13/18,087 = 0.07%) was indistinguishable from gnomAD (Table 1).
- **Female penetrance signal.** 9 of 10 female [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md) LOF carriers in the MSK cohort had breast or ovarian cancer (5/2,265 breast; 4/1,157 ovarian). 8 of 13 carriers had a 1st- or 2nd-degree relative with breast or ovarian cancer (Table 1; Supplementary Fig. 3).
- **Recurrent founder-like allele.** 7/13 MSK carriers and 11/13 carriers overall harbored variants predicted to disrupt one or both Walker motifs of [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md); the recurrent c.139C>T (p.Arg47*) allele was carried by 7/13 MSK patients and by 6/6 ovarian carriers in Table 2.
- **Hormone-receptor subtype skew distinct from RAD51C/D.** 4/5 [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md)-carrier breast cancers were ER+/PR+/HER2−; the 5th was ER−/PR−/HER2+. None were triple-negative, in contrast to the triple-negative skew reported for [RAD51C](../genes/RAD51C.md)/[RAD51D](../genes/RAD51D.md) carriers in prior series. Mean age at diagnosis: 49 (breast, range 30–61) and 67.3 (ovarian, range 58–79).
- **Biallelic inactivation drives genomic HRD.** 6 cases with biallelic [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md) loss (germline LOF + LOH of the [WT](../cancer_types/WT.md) allele) consistently displayed HRD features; all 5 biallelic cases with WES data harbored dominant [Signature 3](../themes/) and high LST scores (Fig. 1A,B). Five of these six biallelic cases also had biallelic somatic [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) variants, paralleling the [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md)/[BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md)-associated HRD context.
- **LOH selection in tumors.** LOH of the WT [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md) allele was observed at a significantly higher rate among carriers of a LOF germline variant (6/18 cases) than among carriers of a VUS (6/59 cases; p = 0.02), consistent with biallelic selection.
- **Intermediate HRD phenotype.** Even after restricting WT-allele comparators to LOH-positive cases (to control for LOH-driven LST signal), biallelic [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md) loss tumors had significantly higher LST scores than WT-RAD51B LOH controls (p = 0.02). Magnitude was intermediate between WT and biallelic [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md)/[BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) loss (Supplementary Fig. 1).
- **Functional HR defect.** Two independent shRNAs against [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md) significantly impaired ionizing-radiation-induced RAD51 nuclear focus formation in MCF-10A and MCF-12A cells (Fig. 3). Knockdown also increased clonogenic sensitivity to [olaparib](../drugs/olaparib.md) and [mitomycin-c](../drugs/mitomycin-c.md), though to a lesser degree than [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) knockdown (Fig. 4) — concordant with the intermediate LST/Signature 3 phenotype.
- **R47* is HR-deficient.** In a U2OS DR-GFP reporter assay, the recurrent c.139C>T (p.Arg47*) variant failed to complement HR proficiency beyond empty vector, while WT [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md) restored HR roughly twofold (Supplementary Fig. 2). This provides functional evidence that R47* — formally a VUS by ACMG criteria — is loss-of-function.
- **Male carriers.** 3 male MSK carriers had non-mammary malignancies (renal, neuroendocrine carcinoma of colon/rectum, esophagogastric adenocarcinoma), with no evidence of breast cancer predisposition in men (Table 2).

## Genes & alterations

- **[RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md)** — germline LOF SNVs/indels (frameshift, truncating, canonical-dinucleotide splice-site). Recurrent alleles: c.139C>T (p.Arg47*) in 7/13 MSK carriers; c.85-1G>C, c.1036+2C>T, c.85-2delA (essential splice); c.37C>T (p.Gln13*), c.204T>A (p.Tyr68*), c.318Arg*, c.595Ser*, c.952A>T (p.Arg318*). Biallelic inactivation (germline + LOH) drives HRD phenotype with dominant Signature 3 and LST-high status. Walker A/B motif disruption in 11/13 carriers.
- **[RAD51C](../genes/RAD51C.md), [RAD51D](../genes/RAD51D.md)** — referenced as established cancer predisposition genes; combined carrier frequency 16/3,422 in the same cohort, comparable to the 9/3,422 observed for [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md).
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)** — biallelic somatic variants observed in 5/6 biallelic [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md) tumors, consistent with selection in BRCA-associated HRD cancers.
- **[BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), [PALB2](../genes/PALB2.md), [BRIP1](../genes/BRIP1.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md)** — cited as established HR pathway genes; used as biological/comparator context. 3 additional [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md) germline carriers were excluded from the primary analysis because they co-harbored a deleterious variant in [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), [RAD51D](../genes/RAD51D.md), or [ATM](../genes/ATM.md).
- **[XRCC2](../genes/XRCC2.md), [XRCC3](../genes/XRCC3.md)** — cited as the other classical RAD51 paralogs.

## Clinical implications

- **Panel-testing recommendation.** The authors argue that [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md) should be added to clinical multigene germline panels for hereditary breast and ovarian cancer risk prediction, on par with [RAD51C](../genes/RAD51C.md) and [RAD51D](../genes/RAD51D.md).
- **Therapeutic actionability.** Biallelic [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md) loss confers a measurable HRD phenotype (Signature 3, LST-high) and functional sensitivity to [olaparib](../drugs/olaparib.md) (PARP inhibitor) and [mitomycin-c](../drugs/mitomycin-c.md) (interstrand crosslinker). Magnitude of sensitivity is intermediate between WT and BRCA1-deficient cells, predicting partial — not necessarily complete — benefit from HRD-targeting therapies.
- **VUS reclassification.** The DR-GFP functional data argues that the truncating c.139C>T (p.Arg47*) allele, currently arguable as a VUS by ACMG criteria, should be treated as loss-of-function for HR proficiency.
- **Subtype counseling.** Unlike [RAD51C](../genes/RAD51C.md)/[RAD51D](../genes/RAD51D.md) (associated with triple-negative breast cancer), [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md)-associated breast cancers in this cohort were predominantly hormone-receptor positive (4/5 ER+/PR+/HER2−). Authors flag this association as needing confirmation in larger series.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Limited statistical power.** Only 9/3,422 breast/ovarian carriers; the cohort is too small to robustly assess hormone-receptor subtype association or rule out ethnic/ancestry confounding when benchmarking against the entire gnomAD database. 6/13 MSK carriers were Hispanic and 2/13 Turkish — the authors raise the possibility that [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md) may contribute disproportionately to BRCA/OV risk in non-Caucasian populations and call for replication in additional populations.
- **Targeted-panel signature analysis.** SigMA-based Signature 3 calls on MSK-IMPACT data are dependent on having ≥5 somatic SNVs and are inherently lower-resolution than WGS-based HRDetect; deletion-length and microhomology-score differences trended but did not reach significance (Fig. 2).
- **No structural-variant analysis.** WES does not resolve structural variants; prior WGS work (Wang et al. 2017) linked [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md) inactivation to a foldback-inversion enriched HRD subgroup. This paper cannot test that linkage.
- **No RNA-seq for splice variants.** Three carriers had canonical splice-site variants; computational prediction is >95% PPV but no transcript-level confirmation of intron retention / aberrant splicing was performed.
- **Male carriers.** No evidence here of male breast cancer predisposition for [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md) LOF carriers, in contrast to the prior GWAS association of the intron-7 SNP rs1314913 with male breast cancer risk; the relationship between non-coding regulatory variation and coding LOF remains unresolved.
- **Generalizability of cell-line sensitivity.** Knockdown phenotypes in MCF-10A/MCF-12A may not fully recapitulate the in vivo tumor response to [olaparib](../drugs/olaparib.md) — no patient outcome data on PARP-inhibitor therapy is reported.
