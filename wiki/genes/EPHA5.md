# EPHA5

## Overview

EPHA5 (Ephrin type-A receptor 5) is a member of the Eph receptor tyrosine kinase family involved in cell-cell communication, axon guidance, and tissue boundary formation. In cancer, EPHA5 alterations have been observed recurrently in angiosarcoma, where it co-occurs with [KDR](../genes/KDR.md) and [FLT4](../genes/FLT4.md) alterations, suggesting a vascular/angiogenic signaling context. Many observed alterations are variants of uncertain significance (VUS) that may yet prove functionally relevant.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- EPHA5 recurrently altered in angiosarcoma ([ANGS](../cancer_types/ANGS.md)) at 9% frequency alongside KDR (19%) and FLT4 (9%); many hits are VUS that may yet be functionally relevant per the authors' assessment [PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **Angiosarcoma ([ANGS](../cancer_types/ANGS.md)):** EPHA5 altered in ~9% of cases in the MSK sarcoma IMPACT cohort (n=9,905 samples, 43 subtypes); co-occurs with KDR (19%) and FLT4 (9%) alterations, consistent with a vascular RTK signaling cluster [PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- In angiosarcoma, EPHA5 co-occurs with KDR and FLT4 alterations; this cluster of vascular receptor tyrosine kinase genes supports the endothelial lineage of angiosarcoma [PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- EPHA5 alterations in angiosarcoma are largely VUS; therapeutic actionability is unestablished in this corpus. KDR alterations in the same tumors are potentially targetable via anti-angiogenic agents [PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)

## Open questions

- Functional significance of EPHA5 VUS in angiosarcoma remains unresolved; patient-derived models are needed to assess which alterations are oncogenic drivers vs. passengers [PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md)
