A proteogenomic portrait of lung squamous cell carcinoma

Authors

Shankha Satpathy

Karsten Krug

Pierre M. Jean Beltran

Sara R. Savage

Francesca Petralia

Chandan Kumar-Sinha

Yongchao Dou

Doi

10.1016/j.cell.2021.07.016

PMID: 34358469 · DOI: 10.1016/j.cell.2021.07.016 · Journal: Cell (2021)

TL;DR

The CPTAC consortium performed a deep proteogenomic characterization of 108 treatment-naive primary lung squamous cell carcinomas (LSCC) and 99 paired normal adjacent tissues (NATs), integrating WES, WGS, RNA-seq, miRNA-seq, methylation arrays, and TMT-based global proteomics with phospho-, acetyl-, and (for the first time in LSCC) ubiquitylproteomics (PMID:34358469). NMF-based multi-omic clustering identified five subtypes — Basal-Inclusive (B-I), EMT-Enriched (EMT-E), Classical, Inflamed-Secretory (I-S), and Proliferative-Primitive (P-P) — and prioritized NSD3 rather than FGFR1 as the likely 8p11.23 amplicon driver, nominated BIRC5 (survivin) as a target in ΔNp63-low tumors, supported chromatin modifiers (KDM1A/LSD1, EZH2) for targeting SOX2-overexpressing tumors, and argued for RB1-protein-based stratification before declaring CDK4/6 inhibition unsuccessful in LSCC (PMID:34358469).

Cohort & data

  • 108 treatment-naive primary LUSC tumors + 99 paired NATs, collected prospectively by 13 tissue source sites in 7 countries between May 2016 and August 2018 (median follow-up 2.5 years; age range 40–88; 90 male / 23 female before exclusions; 5 of 113 originally enrolled samples excluded after pathology review) (PMID:34358469).
  • Dataset is released as cBioPortal study lusc_cptac_2021; processed data at LinkedOmics; raw at PDC (PDC000232/233/234/237), GDC (dbGaP phs001287.v10.p5), and TCIA.
  • Nine data modalities: whole-exome-seq, whole-genome-seq, rna-seq, mirna-seq, epic-methylation-array, TMT11-based tmt-proteomics and tmt-phosphoproteomics, acetylproteomics, and ubiquitylproteomics; reference genome GRCh38/hg38.
  • Companion analytic methods include nmf-clustering (multi-omic subtyping), gistic (focal CNAs), xcell and estimate (immune deconvolution), clumps (PTM 3D clustering), and immunohistochemistry (validation).

Key findings

  • NSD3 (WHSC1L1), not FGFR1, is the likely driver of 8p11.23 amplifications in LSCC. Of 5,523 significant (FDR<0.01) cis CNA-mRNA events, 2,154 also correlated with protein; six amplified and 29 deleted “cancer-associated genes” (CAGs) were identified in significant focal events (Q<0.25). Within the recurrent 8p11.23 amplicon, proteomic data implicated NSD3 rather than FGFR1 as the critical oncogene, offering a potential mechanistic explanation for the historical failure of FGFR1-targeted therapy in this population (PMID:34358469).
  • Five multi-omic NMF subtypes. Basal-Inclusive (B-I; basaloid histology, upregulated metabolic/immune/ER signaling), EMT-Enriched (EMT-E; myxoid features, fibroblast infiltration, Wnt regulators DVL3/FN1/FHL2), Classical (KEAP1/CUL3/NFE2L2 mutations, SOX2/TP63 high-level amplification, CIMP-high, OXPHOS-up, immune-down), Inflamed-Secretory (I-S), and Proliferative-Primitive (P-P) (PMID:34358469).
  • “Mixed” cluster-membership samples had significantly worse survival and were enriched for SOX2 amplifications (p=0.0038); other tested variables (grade, mutation burden, stemness) showed no association (PMID:34358469).
  • EMT-E tumors show phosphorylation-driven PDGFRB and ROR2 signaling. Of 9 RTKs with high correlation-based phosphosite enrichment (CBPE) scores, 7 associated with NMF subtype (Kruskal-Wallis p<0.01); PDGFRB and ROR2 scores were markedly elevated in EMT-E, correlated with loss of epithelial junction markers and gain of mesenchymal/ECM-remodeling proteins, and implicated non-canonical Wnt branches as targetable pathways (PMID:34358469).
  • Universal CDK4/6-pathway dysregulation. Across 108 tumors, every sample showed loss of at least one of CDKN2A or RB1 function (homozygous deletion of CDKN2A → loss of both p16/p14; 28 CDKN2A-WT tumors had p16-promoter hypermethylation; the eight CDKN2A-WT tumors retaining high p16 expression all had RB1 mutations/deletions or low Rb protein), consistent with the mutual exclusivity reported pan-cancer (Knudsen et al., 2020). CCND1 amplification correlated with higher mean Rb protein and phospho-Rb but with broad overlap, helping explain heterogeneous response to abemaciclib and palbociclib (PMID:34358469).
  • NRF2 pathway activation extends beyond mutation. 33 tumors carried NFE2L2/KEAP1/CUL3 hits (28 one-hit, 5 two-hit) versus 68 NRF2-WT; an ssGSEA NRF2 pathway score was elevated in mutated tumors, with the five two-hit cases showing dose-dependent up-/down-regulation of NRF2-pathway genes. The score was also elevated in many NRF2-WT Classical-subtype tumors with NFE2L2 phosphorylation, and CDK5 protein and PTM-SEA-derived activity were significantly upregulated in the Classical subtype (FDR<0.05), suggesting CDK5-mediated NFE2L2 S433 phosphorylation as an alternative NRF2 activation mechanism (PMID:34358469).
  • ΔNp63-low LSCC subset overexpresses survivin (BIRC5). 10 histologically-confirmed LSCC samples lacked TP63 RNA/protein elevation; survivin was among the most overexpressed proteins in this group, and TP63-low LSCC cell lines were significantly more vulnerable to the survivin inhibitor YM-155, nominating TP63 status as a biomarker for survivin-directed therapy (PMID:34358469).
  • Chromatin regulators as a path to “undruggable” SOX2. KDM1A (LSD1) protein correlated significantly with SOX2 protein in unbiased correlation analysis; EZH2 dependency was observed in SOX2-amplified LSCC cell lines; KDM3A also surfaced as a known SOX2 regulator (PMID:34358469).
  • TP63 / TP73 / miR-205 axis. miR-205 correlated tightly with TP63 expression, with ΔNp63α a transcriptional regulator of the miRNA; TP73 correlated with miR-205 at transcript and (more strongly) at protein level; miR-205 negatively correlated with EMT activity and positively with DNA replication (PMID:34358469).
  • Two ubiquitylproteome clusters, with HERC5/ISG15 ISGylation linking immune signaling to glycolysis. Consensus clustering on K-GG abundances (cognate-protein-corrected) yielded two clusters associated with country of origin and ethnicity (Fisher p<0.01); 474 Ub/UbL sites correlated positively with HERC5 (FDR<0.01) and were enriched for glycolysis / TCA cycle, including K-GG sites on PKM, PGK1 and ENO1; HERC5 was elevated in the IFN-gamma dominant immune subtype (PMID:34358469).
  • PTM 3D clustering (clumps). Significant ubiquitylation clusters in 33 proteins and acetylation clusters in 17 proteins (p<0.05); top hits enriched for glycolysis, fatty acid metabolism, and OXPHOS. PGK1 and PKM showed reduced acetylation at known inhibitory sites in tumors versus NATs (PMID:34358469).
  • Three immune microenvironment classes (Hot / Warm / Cold) plus an NAT-enriched cluster from xcell signatures. NMF I-S subtype strongly associated with the Hot cluster (Fisher p=6.371×10⁻⁹). Hot tumors upregulated CTLA4, PD-1 (PDCD1), PD-L1 (CD274), IDO1 and FOXP3, plus Rho GTPase signaling (with K135 acetylation of RhoGDIb/ARHGDIB as a hypothesized regulator of immune-cell function), and CSF1R phosphosite activity (PMID:34358469).
  • EGFR biology differs between squamous and adenocarcinoma lung tumors. EGFR protein was significantly upregulated in squamous cancers but not LUAD; EGFR amplification did not correlate with PROGENy-based EGFR pathway activity, which instead tracked mRNA abundance of five EGFR ligands. The authors argue ligand abundance — rather than EGFR amplification — may better predict response to ligand-blocking inhibitors like cetuximab in squamous tumors (PMID:34358469).
  • Biomarker mining vs. TCGA LSCC. Of 502 tumor-vs-NAT differential proteins, 4 highly differential genes associated significantly with poor overall survival and 15 more with poor disease-free survival in the TCGA LSCC cohort; knockdown of these biomarker proteins reduced fitness across 16 LSCC cell lines (DepMap) (PMID:34358469).

Genes & alterations

  • NSD3 (WHSC1L1) — recurrent 8p11.23 focal amplification (co-amplified with FGFR1); proteomic evidence nominates NSD3 as the driver oncogene; independently validated as a key LSCC oncogenic chromatin regulator (Yuan et al., 2021 — cited in this paper) (PMID:34358469).
  • FGFR1 — co-amplified in the 8p11.23 amplicon but protein-level data argues against it as the primary oncogene, consistent with failure of FGFR1-targeted therapy in LSCC (PMID:34358469).
  • SOX2 — recurrent 3q amplification, co-amplified with TP63; considered undruggable but anti-correlated with JAK-STAT signaling and JAK1 protein, and proposed as targetable via chromatin modifiers KDM1A/LSD1 and EZH2 (PMID:34358469).
  • TP63 — 3q amplification; ΔNp63α dominant isoform; high TP63 expression correlated with improved survival in TCGA LSCC; 10 “Np63-low” LSCC tumors lacked TP63 elevation and overexpressed BIRC5 (PMID:34358469).
  • BIRC5 (survivin) — overexpressed specifically in Np63-low LSCC; TP63-low cell lines sensitive to survivin inhibitor YM-155 (PMID:34358469).
  • KDM1A (LSD1) — strongest protein-protein correlation with SOX2 in unbiased screen; clinical trial NCT04350463 (LSD1 + immunotherapy) cited (PMID:34358469).
  • EZH2 — dependency observed in SOX2-amplified LSCC cell lines (PMID:34358469).
  • CDKN2A — homozygous deletion abolishes p16/p14; mutations paradoxically yielded increased RNA; promoter hypermethylation of p16 (not p14) in 28 WT tumors; loss of CDKN2A or RB1 is universal in LSCC (PMID:34358469).
  • RB1 — mutated tumors had upregulated cell-cycle proteins; loss of Rb protein in CDKN2A-WT samples with preserved p16 expression; mutually exclusive with CDKN2A inactivation (PMID:34358469).
  • CCND1 — amplification associated with higher mean Rb protein and phospho-Rb (but overlapping distributions); no significant association of CCND2/CCND3/CDK4/CDK6 amplification with CDKN2A/p16 or Rb protein levels (PMID:34358469).
  • CDK5 — protein and PTM-SEA-derived kinase activity significantly upregulated in the Classical subtype (FDR<0.05); proposed CDK5-mediated phosphorylation of NFE2L2 S433 as a non-mutational driver of NRF2 activation (PMID:34358469).
  • NFE2L2, KEAP1, CUL3 — NRF2 pathway mutations in 33/101 evaluable tumors; concordant differential mRNA/protein/phosphoprotein of NRF2 pathway genes; dosage effect at SQSTM1, NR0B1, AKR1B10, CARD11, FFAR2 in two-hit cases. KEAP1 mutations did NOT reduce KEAP1 protein in LSCC (unlike LUAD), suggesting NRF2-pathway dysregulation differs between NSCLC subtypes (PMID:34358469, contrasted with PMID:32649874).
  • NOTCH1 — mutations associated with elevated GSK3B protein and T433 phosphorylation, decreased inhibitory GSK3A S21, and upregulated NLK; both NLK and GSK3B inhibit NOTCH1, consistent with its tumor-suppressor role in LSCC (PMID:34358469).
  • ARID1A — mutations associated with higher TMB and downregulated ARID1A protein; ARID1A loss linked to worse OS in NSCLC (PMID:34358469).
  • PDGFRB, ROR2 — high CBPE scores in EMT-E subtype; targetable RTKs implicated in non-canonical Wnt signaling and EMT progression (PMID:34358469).
  • EGFR — significantly upregulated in squamous lung and HNSCC tumors but not LUAD; EGFR amplification did NOT correlate with PROGENy EGFR pathway activity, which instead tracked ligand mRNA abundance (PMID:34358469).
  • CSF1R — highest CBPE score among nine RTKs profiled; phospho-activity positively associated with the immune-Hot cluster and macrophage signatures (PMID:34358469).
  • HERC5, ISG15 — HERC5 (ISG15 E3 ligase) correlated with 474 K-GG sites enriched for glycolysis/TCA; proposed bridge between IFN signaling and glycolytic regulation (PMID:34358469).
  • TP73 — positive correlation with miR-205 and TP63 protein; supports a TP63-TP73 stoichiometric model driving miR-205 overexpression (PMID:34358469).
  • PTGES3 — among the 20 genes whose promoter-methylation cis-effects cascaded to RNA, protein, and PTM levels, and that were differentially expressed between tumors and NATs (PMID:34358469).

Clinical implications

  • CDK4/6 inhibition stratification. Despite limited overall efficacy of abemaciclib and palbociclib in LSCC trials, heterogeneous Rb protein and phospho-Rb in CCND1-amplified tumors offers a possible explanation for outlier responses. The authors argue that downstream functional assessment (RB1 protein expression + phospho-Rb) — not CDKN2A status alone — should guide CDK4/6-inhibitor patient selection (PMID:34358469).
  • NSD3 as an FGFR1-amplicon alternative. Failed FGFR1-targeted therapy in 8p11.23-amplified LSCC may reflect that NSD3 is the true driver; NSD3-directed strategies (e.g., chromatin-targeting agents) are nominated for further study (PMID:34358469).
  • Survivin (BIRC5) inhibition in ΔNp63-low LSCC. TP63-status (specifically Np63-low histologically-confirmed LSCC) is proposed as a candidate biomarker for survivin-inhibitor response, despite prior survivin trials in unselected NSCLC showing modest efficacy (PMID:34358469).
  • Chromatin-modifier strategy for SOX2-amplified LSCC. KDM1A/LSD1 (clinical trial NCT04350463, LSD1 + immunotherapy) and EZH2 are nominated as actionable upstream regulators of “undruggable” SOX2 (PMID:34358469).
  • EGFR ligand-abundance as a biomarker for cetuximab-class therapy in squamous lung. Because EGFR amplification fails to predict EGFR pathway activity in LSCC and ligand mRNA abundance does, the authors suggest ligand abundance may better stratify response to ligand-blocking EGFR inhibitors than EGFR amplification (PMID:34358469).
  • Targetable EMT-E RTKs. PDGFRB and ROR2 phospho-activity in the EMT-E subtype is proposed as a therapeutic vulnerability; pharmacologic TGFβ inhibition was predicted to reverse the EMT-E signature by LINCS query (PMID:34358469).
  • Immune-Hot LSCC as candidates for checkpoint therapy. Upregulation of CTLA4, PD-1 (PDCD1), PD-L1 (CD274), IDO1, and FOXP3 in the Hot cluster, plus the strong I-S/Hot association, supports immune-checkpoint targeting in this subset; CSF1R and AKAP13 are noted as additional anti-cancer targets in immune-Hot tumors (PMID:34358469).
  • Cross-cancer triangulation. Comparison with LUAD and HNSCC delineated unique LSCC features (e.g., 4p14/5q deletions of TLR/interleukin loci) and shared squamous features (3q amplification, JAK2 deletion, EGFR protein elevation); informs trial design that leverages or avoids tissue-of-origin assumptions (PMID:34358469).

Limitations & open questions

  • Hypothesis-generating, not validated. Authors emphasize that landscape-style proteogenomic surveys must be followed by targeted functional validation by the broader community before clinical action (PMID:34358469).
  • Bulk-tumor analysis cannot resolve intratumor heterogeneity. Authors call out the need for microdissection-MS, mass cytometry, and single-cell approaches as adjuncts (PMID:34358469).
  • Primary tumors only. No matched metastatic samples; LSCC mortality is driven by metastatic disease, and metastasis-focused proteogenomics is required to understand lethal biology (PMID:34358469).
  • Long-term clinical follow-up was incomplete at time of analysis; survival biomarker validation relied on the external TCGA LSCC cohort (PMID:34358469).
  • Ubiquitylproteomics coverage incomplete — not all 108 tumors had K-GG data; ubiquitylprotein data was excluded from the multi-omic NMF clustering for this reason (PMID:34358469).
  • Ubiquitylproteome clusters confounded by country of origin / ethnicity (Fisher p<0.01) — the biological vs. technical / population-stratification interpretation is unresolved (PMID:34358469).
  • NRF2 kinase identity unclear. CDK5 is nominated as a candidate kinase for NFE2L2 S433 phosphorylation in LSCC based on prior astrocyte work, but this remains hypothetical (PMID:34358469).
  • Site-level functional roles of most acetyl/ubiquitin marks are unknown, and crosstalk-analysis methodology is nascent — interpretations stated as “suitably cautious” (PMID:34358469).
