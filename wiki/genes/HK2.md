# HK2

## Overview

HK2 (Hexokinase 2) catalyzes the first committed step of glycolysis, phosphorylating glucose to glucose-6-phosphate. It is frequently overexpressed in cancers as part of the Warburg effect, and in pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDAC) it is among the proteins upregulated at the proteome level relative to normal tissue.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Protein upregulated >2-fold in PDAC vs normal adjacent tissue and vs normal ductal tissue in the CPTAC proteogenomic cohort (n=140 treatment-naive tumors); elevated even in early-stage tumors. Listed among 21 proteins consistently upregulated across the PDAC cohort and among early-detection biomarker candidates. Validated by orthogonal DIA mass spectrometry. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md):** Consistently upregulated at protein level across treatment-naive PDACs, including early-stage disease; candidate early-detection marker. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Upregulation co-occurs with immune-cold biology in PDAC; immune-cold CPTAC PDAC clusters show elevated glycolysis markers (GAPDH, PGAM1, ENO1 phosphorylation) alongside HK2 upregulation. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- HK2 inhibition is a conceptual target in the Warburg-effect literature; not directly targeted in the corpus. Its upregulation in immune-cold PDAC is contextualized alongside VEGF/hypoxia signaling. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Open questions

- Whether HK2 upregulation is driven transcriptionally or post-translationally in PDAC is not fully resolved by bulk proteomics alone.
- Serum secretion potential and utility as a liquid biopsy marker not assessed.
