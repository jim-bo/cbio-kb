**PMID:** 30373752 · **DOI:** [10.1158/1078-0432.CCR-18-2293](https://doi.org/10.1158/1078-0432.CCR-18-2293) · **Journal:** Clinical Cancer Research (2019)

## TL;DR

Harding et al. prospectively sequenced matched tumor/normal DNA from 127 patients with advanced [hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC)](../cancer_types/HCC.md) at MSKCC using the [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) targeted NGS assay (341/410/468 genes) and correlated genotype with response to contemporary systemic therapy. They confirmed the canonical [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) genomic landscape ([TERT](../genes/TERT.md) 55.6%, [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) 35.7%, [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) 32.5%; WNT and TP53 alterations mutually exclusive). Two clinically actionable findings emerged: oncogenic [PI3K-mTOR](../themes/pi3k-mtor-pathway.md) pathway alterations were associated with shorter PFS/OS on [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md) (n=81), and activating [WNT/β-catenin](../themes/wnt-beta-catenin-pathway.md) alterations were associated with innate resistance to immune checkpoint inhibitors (n=31, 0% vs 53% disease control). 24% of tumors harbored potentially actionable alterations, and 6% of systemically-treated patients were matched to genome-driven therapy.

## Cohort & data

- **N = 127** unique HCC patients (134 specimens), enrolled August 2014 – August 2017 at MSKCC under prospective genotyping protocol NCT01775072.
- Cancer type: conventional [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) (fibrolamellar and biphenotypic HCC-cholangiocarcinoma excluded).
- Dataset: [hcc_mskimpact_2018](../datasets/hcc_mskimpact_2018.md) — all data available via cBioPortal at `study?id=hcc_mskimpact_2018` and variant calls in cBioPortal DataHub.
- Assay: [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) hybridization-capture targeted NGS sequenced on Illumina HiSeq 2500. Panel expanded during study: [IMPACT341](../methods/IMPACT341.md) (21 cases), [IMPACT410](../methods/IMPACT410.md) (70 cases), [IMPACT468](../methods/IMPACT468.md) (36 cases). Standard input 250 ng DNA (min 50 ng); mean coverage 690×, median tumor purity 60%.
- TMB calculated per sample; MSI status via [MSIsensor](../methods/msisensor.md) (MSI-H threshold ≥10).
- Variant filtering for oncogenicity via [OncoKB](../methods/oncokb-annotation.md) levels 1–4.
- Cohort demographics: 79% male, 76% non-Asian; etiology HBV 17%, HCV 35%, nonviral 49%; BCLC stage A 29%, B 40%, C 30%; 61% not eligible for curative resection or transplantation; 16% local recurrence/extrahepatic specimens.
- Treatment subsets: **[sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md) n=81** (75 first-line, 6 second-line); **immune checkpoint inhibitor n=31** (1 anti-CTLA-4 mono, 25 anti-PD-1/PD-L1 mono, 5 anti-PD-1 + anti-CTLA-4/KIR/LAG-3 combos).
- Reference comparator: [TCGA HCC](../datasets/lihc_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md) cohort (N = 196).

## Key findings

- **Mutation landscape (134 specimens):** alterations in 127 (95%). Most frequent genes (≥10%): [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) 55.6%, [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) 35.7%, [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) 32.5%, [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) 12.7%, [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) 10.6%.
- **WNT pathway alterations 45% overall;** additional WNT-pathway members: [AXIN1](../genes/AXIN1.md) 6.3%, [AXIN2](../genes/AXIN2.md) 0.05% (sic), [APC](../genes/APC.md) 5.2%.
- **CNAs:** [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md)/[FGF19](../genes/FGF19.md) amplification 7%, [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) 6.1%, [MET](../genes/MET.md) 1.5%, [VEGFA](../genes/VEGFA.md) <1%; [HLA-B](../genes/HLA-B.md) loss 2.7%.
- **Mutual exclusivity:** TP53 mutations vs. WNT pathway alterations P = 0.003 — defines at least two major genomic subsets.
- **Etiology:** virally mediated HCC enriched for TP53 mutations vs. nonviral (45% vs. 21%, P = 0.001); numerically less WNT pathway alteration (41% vs. 60%, P = 0.7).
- **TMB:** median 4.08; variance low (SD = 2.71, second only to germ cell tumors among solid tumors); no MSI-H or hypermutators observed; only 1/27 immunotherapy-evaluable patients had TMB > 10.
- **Sorafenib (n = 81; 79 evaluable):** median PFS 4.8 mo, median [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) 16.4 mo overall.
  - Oncogenic [PI3K-mTOR](../themes/pi3k-mtor-pathway.md) pathway alterations (n = 12 of 79) → lower DCR (8.3% vs. 40.2%, P = 0.05), shorter median PFS (1.9 vs. 5.3 mo; HR 3.8, 95% CI 2.0–7.5, P < 0.0001) and OS (10.4 vs. 17.9 mo; HR 2.5, 95% CI 1.21–5.31, P = 0.01).
  - WNT, MAPK, TP53, cell-cycle, and chromatin-remodeling pathway alterations had no effect on sorafenib outcomes.
  - [VEGFA](../genes/VEGFA.md) amplification rare (3.9%) and anecdotally not associated with extreme sorafenib response.
- **Immune checkpoint inhibitors (n = 31; 27 evaluable):** ORR 11.1% (1 CR, 2 PR, 10 SD, 14 PD); median PFS 5.4 mo, median OS 12.9 mo.
  - Activating WNT/β-catenin alterations (10 patients) → 0/10 clinical benefit vs. 9/17 (53%) for non-WNT patients (P = 0.009).
  - Shorter median PFS in WNT-activated tumors (2.0 vs. 7.4 mo; HR 9.2, 95% CI 2.9–28.8, P < 0.0001) and numerically shorter median OS (9.1 vs. 15.2 mo; HR 2.6, 95% CI 0.76–8.7, P = 0.11).
  - TMB did **not** stratify ICI response: median TMB 3.8 (responders) vs. 3.9 (non-responders), P = 0.98; PFS for TMB <3.8 vs. ≥3.8 was 3.1 vs. 3.0 months (P = 0.21).
- **Actionability:** 24% of patients harbored ≥1 potentially actionable alteration via OncoKB. No level 1/2A alterations were found. Actionable hits included [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md) <1% / [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) 7% truncating/inactivating, [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) truncating/homdel 3.9%, [FGF19](../genes/FGF19.md) amplifications 6.3%, [MET](../genes/MET.md) amplifications 1.5%, and oncogenic missense in [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) (all <1%).
- **Matched therapy:** 5/87 (5.7%) systemically-treated patients matched to genome-directed therapy. 4/10 TSC1/2-altered patients received [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md) off-label or on-trial; 1 patient with [VEGFA](../genes/VEGFA.md) + [MET](../genes/MET.md) co-amplification received anti-VEGFR2 + anti-MET mAbs on trial. Of these 5, 4 had progressed on first-line sorafenib within 1.5–2.5 mo; all 4 achieved at least stable disease on matched therapy at first scan; 3/4 had ≥5 mo clinical benefit.

## Genes & alterations

- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) — promoter/coding alterations in 55.6% (most frequently altered gene).
- [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) — activating mutations in 35.7%; defines WNT-activated subset; predicts innate resistance to immune checkpoint blockade.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — mutated in 32.5%; mutually exclusive with WNT pathway alterations (P = 0.003); enriched in virally mediated HCC (45% vs. 21%, P = 0.001).
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) — mutated in 12.7%.
- [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) — inactivating/truncating in ~10.6% (7% by actionable-call); together with [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md) these mTOR negative regulators are the rationale for matched [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md). One [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) E95* patient progressed on sorafenib then had disease stabilization with minor tumor shrinkage on [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md) (Fig 4C).
- [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md) — truncating/inactivating <1%.
- [AXIN1](../genes/AXIN1.md) — inactivating in 6.3%; AXIN1-inactivated tumors grouped with CTNNB1-activated tumors as "WNT-activated" for ICI resistance analysis.
- [AXIN2](../genes/AXIN2.md) — reported at 0.05% (as published).
- [APC](../genes/APC.md) — alterations 5.2%.
- [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) / [FGF19](../genes/FGF19.md) — coamplification on 11q13 amplicon in 7% ([FGF19](../genes/FGF19.md) amplification 6.3% by actionable-call); rationale for [FGFR4](../genes/FGFR4.md) inhibitors in HCC.
- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) — amplification 6.1%.
- [MET](../genes/MET.md) — amplification 1.5%; one patient with concurrent 2.1× MET and 3.7× VEGFA amplification received anti-VEGFR2 + anti-MET mAbs.
- [VEGFA](../genes/VEGFA.md) — amplification <1% in overall cohort, 3.9% of sorafenib-evaluable; proposed biomarker for extreme sorafenib response but not confirmed here.
- [HLA-B](../genes/HLA-B.md) — loss 2.7%.
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — truncating/homozygous deletion 3.9%; contributes to actionable PI3K-mTOR pathway alterations.
- [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — oncogenic missense each <1%.
- [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) — missense <1%; possibly defines a cholangiocarcinoma-like HCC subset susceptible to selective [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) inhibitors such as [ivosidenib](../drugs/ivosidenib.md).
- [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md) — discussed as part of mTOR pathway/actionability framework (with TSC1/TSC2 alterations driving everolimus response per prior renal-cell evidence).

## Clinical implications

- **Predictive biomarker — sorafenib:** activating PI3K-mTOR pathway alterations identify a subset (~15% of evaluable sorafenib patients) with markedly worse outcomes (PFS HR 3.8, OS HR 2.5). The authors propose mTOR inhibition as a rational salvage strategy in this group, citing umbrella trials NCI-MATCH (NCT02465060) and ASCO TAPUR (NCT02693535).
- **Predictive biomarker — immune checkpoint blockade:** activating WNT/β-catenin pathway alterations (CTNNB1 gain-of-function or [AXIN1](../genes/AXIN1.md) loss) are associated with innate resistance to anti-PD-1/PD-L1 ± anti-CTLA-4 therapy in HCC. All 10 WNT-altered ICI-treated patients had progressive disease at first scan vs. 53% disease control in non-WNT patients. Authors caution data are preliminary but, if validated, would inform patient selection and trial design.
- **Negative biomarker — TMB / MSI for ICI:** TMB and MSI status are unlikely to be major determinants of ICI response in advanced HCC in this cohort (no MSI-H, TMB-median 3.8 nondiscriminating between responders and non-responders).
- **Genome-matched targeted therapy:** 24% of HCCs have ≥1 OncoKB-actionable alteration; 6% of systemically-treated patients were matched to therapy in this study. Reported matched options: [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md) for TSC1/2-altered HCC; FGFR4 inhibitors for FGF19-amplified HCC (in development); MET inhibitors / [cabozantinib](../drugs/cabozantinib.md) for MET-amplified HCC; selective IDH1 inhibitors for IDH1-mutant HCC.
- **Other HCC standard agents discussed:** [regorafenib](../drugs/regorafenib.md) (second-line TKI), [lenvatinib](../drugs/lenvatinib.md) (first-line TKI non-inferior to sorafenib), [nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md) (CheckMate 040), [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md), [tivantinib](../drugs/tivantinib.md) (failed MET-high HCC trial METIV-HCC).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Targeted panel coverage:** MSK-IMPACT cannot detect alterations in genes outside the 341/410/468 design, epigenetic silencing, or HBV integration events.
- **Tumor heterogeneity:** single-site biopsy cannot fully characterize clonal architecture in multisite metastatic HCC; authors propose ctDNA or single-cell follow-on studies.
- **Small sample size:** confounding by HCC etiology, AFP, extrahepatic spread, vascular invasion not fully controlled — particularly for the n = 31 ICI subset and the n = 12 PI3K-mTOR-altered sorafenib group.
- **Selection bias:** favorable OS (16.4 mo) vs. published sorafenib (12.2 mo, ref 5) likely reflects MSKCC referral bias, access to novel therapies post-progression, and high proportion of preserved hepatic function (Child-Pugh A 96%).
- **Open question — WNT predictive value:** whether the WNT-resistance signal is causal vs. confounded; needs validation in larger retrospective cohorts and prospective ICI studies. HLA-I heterogeneity (Chowell et al.) cited as another possible innate-resistance axis worth integrating.
- **Open question — mTOR inhibitor efficacy in HCC:** matched everolimus produced cytostatic but modest benefit in TSC1/2-altered HCC; durability and the right drug/combination remain unresolved.
- **No level 1/2A alterations:** underscores the gap between actionability annotation and FDA-validated biomarkers in HCC.
