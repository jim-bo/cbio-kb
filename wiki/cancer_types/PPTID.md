# Pineal Parenchymal Tumor of Intermediate Differentiation (PPTID)

## Overview

Pineal Parenchymal Tumor of Intermediate Differentiation (PPTID) is a rare CNS/Brain tumor arising from the pineal parenchyma, classified between pineocytoma (grade II) and pineoblastoma (grade IV) in the WHO CNS tumor classification. It is designated OncoTree code PPTID under the parent category [PINT](../cancer_types/PINT.md) (Pineal Tumor). PPTID accounts for a minority of pineal region tumors and poses diagnostic and therapeutic challenges given its rarity and variable clinical behavior.

## Cohorts in the corpus

- MSK cWGTS pediatric/rare solid tumor cohort (n=114 patients, [mixed_kunga_msk_2022](../datasets/mixed_kunga_msk_2022.md)): one PPTID patient was profiled by clinical whole-genome and transcriptome sequencing. [PMID:35585047](../papers/35585047.md)

## Recurrent alterations

- [KBTBD4](../genes/KBTBD4.md) p.R313_M314insPRR in-frame insertion identified as a disease-defining mutation in one PPTID patient in the MSK cWGTS cohort; this small insertion in the BTB/POZ domain of [KBTBD4](../genes/KBTBD4.md) is a known recurrent driver in PPTID. [PMID:35585047](../papers/35585047.md)

## Subtypes

- No molecular subtypes distinguished in the corpus; the MSK cWGTS cohort profiled a single PPTID case.

## Therapeutic landscape

- No drug-specific therapeutic data for PPTID in the corpus. The MSK cWGTS case was profiled for diagnostic purposes; no specific targeted therapy was reported. [PMID:35585047](../papers/35585047.md)
