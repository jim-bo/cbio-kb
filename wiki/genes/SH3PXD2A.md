# SH3PXD2A

## Overview

SH3PXD2A (SH3 and PX domains 2A), also known as TKS5, encodes a scaffold protein involved in invadopodia formation and extracellular matrix degradation. It is a recurrent gene fusion partner in schwannomatosis, forming the SH3PXD2A-HTRA1 fusion that is enriched in LZTR1-mutant and painful tumors, providing a potential therapeutic rationale for MEK inhibition.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- SH3PXD2A–[HTRA1](../genes/HTRA1.md) recurrent gene fusion detected in 14% of LZTR1-mutant schwannomatosis (SWNTS-SWN) tumors (3/22 WGS samples confirmed by RT-PCR); enriched in LZTR1-mutant and painful tumors; therapeutic rationale for MEK inhibition by analogy with sporadic schwannomas [PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Schwannomatosis (SWNTS-SWN subtype): SH3PXD2A–HTRA1 fusion enriched in LZTR1-mutant tumors with painful clinical course and higher CNV burden [PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Fusion enriched in tumors with germline [LZTR1](../genes/LZTR1.md) mutation, [NF2](../genes/NF2.md) co-loss, and 22q deletions including [SMARCB1](../genes/SMARCB1.md) [PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- SH3PXD2A–HTRA1 fusion provides rationale for MEK inhibition in LZTR1-mutant schwannomatosis by analogy with sporadic schwannoma studies [PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)

## Open questions

- Whether SH3PXD2A–HTRA1 fusion drives RAS/MAPK upregulation independently or requires co-occurring NF2/LZTR1 loss.
