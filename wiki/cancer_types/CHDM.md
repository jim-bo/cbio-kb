# Chordoma (CHDM)

## Overview

Chordoma is a rare, slow-growing malignant bone tumor arising from notochordal remnants, classified under Bone Cancer in OncoTree (parent: [BONE](../cancer_types/BONE.md)). It most commonly occurs in the skull base, spine, and sacrum. Chordomas are generally resistant to standard cytotoxic chemotherapy.

## Cohorts in the corpus

- [sarcoma_ucla_2024](../datasets/sarcoma_ucla_2024.md): 14 specimens from 10 patients in the UCLA sarcoma PDTO biobank (Feb 2018–May 2022). [PMID:39305899](../papers/39305899.md)

## Recurrent alterations

- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md)/[ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md): pathway implicated by KEGG analysis in multiple chordoma samples (SARC0046_2/3, SARC0053_a, SARC0049); chordomas preferentially sensitive to TAK-285 (EGFR/ERBB2 kinase inhibitor, p=0.034). [PMID:39305899](../papers/39305899.md)
- In a 7,494-sarcoma cohort, chordoma showed CDKN2A/B deletion in 36.8%, [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md) inactivating alterations in 15.8%, and biallelic HRD-gene loss in 5.3% — the highest rate among all 44 sarcoma subtypes [PMID:35705558](../papers/35705558.md).

## Subtypes

- Conventional chordoma (notochordal origin); dedifferentiated chordoma variants also recognized.

## Therapeutic landscape

- [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md): chordoma PDTOs were significantly *less* sensitive than other sarcomas (p=0.022); alvocidib (p=0.00046), apitolisib (p=0.0071), and [bortezomib](../drugs/bortezomib.md) (p=1.8×10⁻⁵) also showed relative resistance. [PMID:39305899](../papers/39305899.md)
- TAK-285 (EGFR/ERBB2 inhibitor): chordomas preferentially sensitive (p=0.034); BI-D1870 (RSK inhibitor, p=0.047) and A-769662 (AMPK activator, p=0.0015) also showed preferential sensitivity. [PMID:39305899](../papers/39305899.md)
- Minimal ex vivo proliferation in PDTOs noted, which limited screening throughput for some chordoma samples. [PMID:39305899](../papers/39305899.md)
