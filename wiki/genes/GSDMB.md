# GSDMB

## Overview

GSDMB (Gasdermin B) is a member of the gasdermin protein family, which are pore-forming effectors involved in pyroptosis and inflammatory cell death. In cancer, GSDMB has been reported as upregulated in several epithelial tumor types. Its identification as an early-detection biomarker candidate in pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDAC) represents a newly reported role in this malignancy.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Protein abundantly upregulated >2-fold in PDAC vs normal adjacent tissue and vs normal ductal tissue in the CPTAC proteogenomic cohort (n=140 treatment-naive tumors); elevated even in early-stage tumors. Authors note this is the first report of elevated GSDMB in PDAC. Validated by orthogonal DIA mass spectrometry. Listed among 12 secreted proteins and nominated as a candidate serum/pancreatic-juice early-detection biomarker. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md):** Protein upregulated in treatment-naive PDAC; candidate early-detection biomarker in serum/pancreatic juice. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Upregulation co-occurs with other secreted proteins ([THBS2](../genes/THBS2.md), LAMC2, [LOXL2](../genes/LOXL2.md), [LECT2](../genes/LECT2.md)) in the CPTAC PDAC early-detection candidate panel. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting described in the corpus. Candidate for diagnostic/early-detection assay development given secreted nature and PDAC-specific upregulation. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Open questions

- Mechanism of GSDMB upregulation in PDAC (transcriptional vs post-transcriptional) not delineated.
- Specificity as a serum biomarker relative to other GI malignancies not yet addressed.
