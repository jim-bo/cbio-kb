**PMID:** 34145282 · **DOI:** 10.1038/s41467-021-24109-5 · **Journal:** Nature Communications (2021)

## TL;DR

The MSK pathology and molecular oncology groups built and clinically deployed MSK-ACCESS, a 129-gene hybrid-capture next-generation sequencing assay that pairs ultra-deep (~20,000x raw / ~1,100x duplex consensus) cfDNA sequencing with matched WBC sequencing to call SNVs, indels, copy-number alterations, and structural variants in plasma. Analytical validation on 70 cfDNA samples with 100 orthogonally confirmed variants showed 92% de novo sensitivity at >=0.5% VAF and at least 99.7% specificity. After New York State approval on 31 May 2019, the team prospectively profiled 681 blood samples from 617 patients across 31 tumor types (most commonly non-small-cell lung, prostate, bladder, pancreatic, and biliary cancer); 73% of samples had at least one detectable alteration and 56% of those carried clinically actionable variants. Matched WBC sequencing removed >10,000 germline and clonal-hematopoiesis variants that plasma-only filtering would have missed.

## Cohort & data

- 681 plasma samples from 617 unique patients (99% QC pass from 687 accessioned), enrolled in MSKCC IRB-approved protocol NCT01775072, sequenced prospectively after the assay's NYS-DOH approval on 31 May 2019.
- Top tumor types: [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) (n=349, 51%); plus [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md), [BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md), [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md), and biliary cancer (collectively 28%); 31 distinct tumor types overall.
- Validation cohorts: 47 healthy-donor plasma + matched WBC samples (median age 29, range 21–48) for background-error and specificity assessment; 70 cfDNA samples carrying 100 orthogonally validated SNVs/indels in AKT1, ALK, BRAF, EGFR, ERBB2, ESR1, KRAS, MET, PIK3CA, and TP53 (orthogonal VAF range 0.1–73%) for accuracy.
- Concordance cohort: 383 of 617 patients with matched MSK-IMPACT tumor tissue (520 sequenced tissues; 1206 mutations in overlapping target regions).
- Dataset: [msk_access_2021](../datasets/msk_access_2021.md). Assay/panel: [ACCESS129](../methods/ACCESS129.md) (the 129-gene cfDNA panel) on the [MSK-ACCESS](../methods/msk-access.md) workflow; tissue comparator was [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) using [IMPACT468](../methods/IMPACT468.md). Sequencing on Illumina HiSeq 2500 or NovaSeq 6000, GRCh37 alignment with [BWA](../methods/bwa.md) MEM, ABRA2, [GATK](../methods/gatk.md); UMI-based duplex/simplex consensus generation via Marianas; SNV/indel calling with [MuTect](../methods/mutect.md) and [VarDict](../methods/vardict.md); SVs called with Manta; copy-number calling via the MSK-IMPACT-derived method; tumor purity via [FACETS](../methods/facets.md); clinical actionability annotation via [OncoKB](../methods/oncokb.md); workflow execution in [CWL](../methods/cwl-docker-workflow.md)/toil.

## Key findings

- **Panel design backed by MSK-IMPACT priors.** The 129 genes / 826 exons were chosen so that at least one non-synonymous mutation would be captured in 84% of 25,000 previously MSK-IMPACT-sequenced solid tumors, including 91% of breast cancers and 94% of NSCLC ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **Ultra-deep coverage with consensus collapsing.** Mean raw cfDNA coverage 18,818x in 47 healthy donors yielded mean duplex 1,103x and simplex 658x; clinical samples averaged 18,264x raw plasma / 1,273x WBC, with median duplex consensus plasma coverage 1,497x ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **Background error suppression.** Median error rate dropped from 3.3x10^-4 in standard BAMs to 1.2x10^-5 (simplex) and 1.7x10^-6 (duplex); 85% of targeted positions had zero base mismatches in simplex BAMs and 99% in duplex BAMs ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **Accuracy.** 94% of expected variants detected by genotyping and 82% by de novo calling across 100 orthogonally validated mutations (R^2=0.98); for VAF >=0.5% (n=83), 92% (95% CI 84–96.5%) were called de novo and 99% (95% CI 92.5–99.9%) by genotyping ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **Reproducibility.** Across 7 samples with 152 mutations replicated within and across runs, 99% (n=151, 95% CI 96.4–100%) were detected by genotyping with median coefficient of variation 0.16 (range 0.04–1.2). Limit-of-detection dilution series: all expected mutations called de novo at 0.5% VAF; at 0.1%, 11% (2/19) de novo and 74% (14/19) by genotyping ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **Specificity.** 0 mutations called in 47 healthy donors vs matched WBCs; on 1620 sites from 36 orthogonal NGS samples, 4 potential false positives (TP53 p.R253H VAFs 0.17/0.24%; PIK3CA p.H1047R VAFs 0.05/0.07%) gave >=99.7% specificity by genotyping and 99.9% by de novo calling. PPA 94% / NPA 99.7% (genotyping); PPA 98% / NPA 99.2% (de novo) ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **Clinical detection yield.** 498/681 (73%) of prospective samples had >=1 alteration; non-zero median 3 alterations/patient (range 1–28); 1697 SNVs/indels reported in 486 samples from 435 patients (median VAF 1.9%, range 0.02–99%). 41% (278/681) of samples carried an OncoKB level 1–3B alteration; level-1 alterations were most frequent in bladder (48%), breast (37%), and NSCLC (33%) ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **De novo vs genotyped.** 95% (1606/1697) of reported SNVs/indels were called de novo without prior tissue results; the 91 rescued by genotyping had median observed VAF 0.08% ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **Tissue–plasma concordance.** Of 1206 mutations in overlapping target regions across 383 patients, 706 (59%) were shared, 254 (21%) tissue-only, and 246 (20%) plasma-only. 58 of the 246 plasma-only calls were present at sub-threshold levels in matched tissue. 18% (46/254) of MSK-IMPACT-only mutations were OncoKB level 1–3 actionable; 12% (30/246) of MSK-ACCESS-only mutations were actionable. For patients without MSK-IMPACT (n=234), MSK-ACCESS detected 79 actionable mutations in 26% (61/234) of them ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **Time and clonality drive discordance.** Average ΔDOP (date-of-procedure gap) was 65 weeks; tissue-only and plasma-only discordant calls came from patients with significantly longer ΔDOP (median 491 days, p=3.63x10^-14; median 288 days, p=1.33x10^-9) than concordant patients (median 83 days). Shared mutations were also more clonal in tissue than tissue-only calls (all mutations p=8.10x10^-13; actionable p=6.06x10^-3). Tumor purity did not differ between concordant and discordant groups (p=0.812) ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **Matched-normal removes >10,000 confounders.** Plasma-only variant calling produced 24,561 calls; database/population filtering (in-house plasma normals, gnomAD >=0.5%) removed 14,508; an additional VAF-based germline rule would have removed 721 but at the cost of mis-filtering 70 verified somatic mutations (15 of them actionable). Patient-matched WBC sequencing retained those somatic calls while removing 10,053 remaining germline/CH variants (mean VAF 4.7%, median 0.05%) ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **Fragment-length analysis distinguishes ctDNA from CH/germline.** cfDNA showed the expected bimodal peaks at ~161 bp and ~317 bp; 1,558 confirmed tumor-derived fragments were significantly shorter than wild-type fragments (bootstrap p<0.0001), while WBC-supported variants (CH, germline) had length distributions indistinguishable from wild-type (p=0.94 / p=0.99). This allowed reclassification of variants such as a commercially reported KRAS p.G12S in lung adenocarcinoma (cfDNA 0.44% / WBC 0.31%) as CH rather than tumor ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **Matched WBC reassignment of germline events.** Indel ATM p.E522fs*43 reported as somatic by a commercial cfDNA test was reclassified as germline (~50% VAF in both plasma and WBC); copy-number deletions in ATM and BRCA2, plus RB1 deletions in two retinoblastoma patients, were similarly recognized as germline via matched WBC sequencing ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).

## Genes & alterations

- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)** — most commonly altered gene across the cohort; mutated in 144/248 (58%) of NSCLC samples with detectable alterations and recurrently mutated/deleted in biliary, bladder, breast, prostate, and pancreatic cancers. Two TP53 p.R253H calls (0.17% / 0.24% VAF) were the assay's borderline false positives on the orthogonal-NGS specificity panel ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **[EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md)** — recurrent oncogenic driver mutations and amplifications in NSCLC; EGFR mutations were enriched in cfDNA relative to MSK-IMPACT in lung ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)** — recurrent driver in NSCLC and a "lower in cfDNA than tissue" gene in pancreatic cancer; KRAS p.G12S in a lung adenocarcinoma case was reclassified from somatic to CH using matched WBC + fragment-length evidence ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **[MET](../genes/MET.md)**, **[ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md)**, **[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)** — additional targetable driver mutations / amplifications recurrent in NSCLC ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **[ALK](../genes/ALK.md) / [EML4](../genes/EML4.md)** — EML4-ALK fusions detected de novo and by genotyping in NSCLC ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **[RET](../genes/RET.md) / [KIF5B](../genes/KIF5B.md)** — KIF5B-RET fusions detected in NSCLC ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **[ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md)** — multiple-partner ROS1 rearrangements detected in NSCLC; ROS1 was also among genes where a previously reported somatic variant was reassigned to germline via matched WBC ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **[STK11](../genes/STK11.md), [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md)** — NSCLC samples lacking known mitogenic drivers were enriched for STK11 and KEAP1 mutations ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **[FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) / [BICC1](../genes/BICC1.md)** — FGFR2 mutations and fusions (most commonly FGFR2-BICC1) identified in 8/24 intrahepatic cholangiocarcinomas with detectable ctDNA, including missense mutations in the FGFR2 kinase domain known to confer FGFR-inhibitor resistance ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **[IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)** — targetable alterations also recurrently detected in intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma; PIK3CA p.H1047R at very low VAF (0.05% / 0.07%) was the other borderline false positive on the specificity panel ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **[FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) / [TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md)** — FGFR3 point mutations conferring resistance to FGFR inhibitors detected in bladder cancers with FGFR3-TACC3 fusions; FGFR3 also recurrently altered in bladder cancer ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **[AR](../genes/AR.md)** — recurrent in prostate cancer; AR mutations were enriched in cfDNA vs MSK-IMPACT in prostate cancer ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **[ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md)** — also recurrent in breast cancer ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **[NF1](../genes/NF1.md)** — enriched in cfDNA relative to tissue in breast cancer ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **[BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md)** — used as case studies of germline alterations (mutations and copy-number deletions) recovered via matched WBC sequencing that would have been reported as somatic without paired-normal data; RB1 deletions seen in two retinoblastoma patients ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **[AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md), [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md)** — represented among the 10 genes (AKT1, ALK, BRAF, EGFR, ERBB2, ESR1, KRAS, MET, PIK3CA, TP53) used to assemble the orthogonal-truth accuracy cohort ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **[DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md)** — cited as a commonly mutated CH gene; recurrent CH-origin calls in DNMT3A are among the variants matched WBC sequencing reclassified ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).

## Clinical implications

- **NYS-approved clinical assay.** MSK-ACCESS was approved by the New York State Department of Health on 31 May 2019 and used prospectively to guide patient management; 41% (278/681) of clinical samples had an OncoKB level 1–3B alteration, with level 1 yields of 48% in [BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md), 37% in [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md), and 33% in [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **Complementary to tissue, not replacement.** For patients without MSK-IMPACT (n=234), MSK-ACCESS uncovered 79 actionable mutations in 26% (61/234), while 18% (46/254) of MSK-IMPACT-only calls were also actionable — supporting integrated tissue + plasma profiling rather than substitution ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **Resistance detection.** MSK-ACCESS detected acquired FGFR3 point mutations conferring resistance to FGFR inhibitor therapy in FGFR3-TACC3 bladder cancers ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **Matched WBC prevents false somatic calls.** Without matched normal, >10,000 germline + CH calls would have leaked into clinical reports; documented commercial false positives (ATM p.E522fs*43 indel, KRAS p.G12S CH, germline deletions in ATM/BRCA2/RB1) were avoided ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- **Timing matters.** Tissue–plasma concordance is strongly inversely related to the time between tissue biopsy and blood draw; clinicians interpreting MSK-ACCESS results should consider sample-collection ΔDOP and clonality of the tissue calls ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).

## Limitations & open questions

- The 129-gene panel is uniform and not patient-specific; the authors note MSK-ACCESS will not match the per-patient sensitivity of bespoke whole-exome/whole-genome-derived MRD panels, but argue that bespoke approaches are operationally unattainable for most laboratories ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- Plasma cfDNA cannot replace tissue in all situations — 260 tissue mutations were not seen in matched plasma, plausibly due to low ctDNA shedding, tumor heterogeneity, or anatomical differences in shedding by site; the authors call for further studies to pin down which clinical/analytic factors drive discordance ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- VAF-based germline filtering (35–65% in both plasma and WBC) is insufficient as a substitute for true matched-normal sequencing — it would have mis-removed 70 verified somatic mutations including 15 actionable ones ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- Low-VAF CH variants remain a fundamental challenge for cfDNA assays; matched WBC plus fragment-length analysis substantially mitigates but does not fully eliminate the risk, particularly for CH mutations that arise in less commonly mutated genes outside DNMT3A/TET2/ASXL1 ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
- Sensitivity drops sharply below 0.5% VAF (only 11% of mutations called de novo at 0.1% VAF); MRD-grade sensitivity is not the assay's strength ([PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)).
