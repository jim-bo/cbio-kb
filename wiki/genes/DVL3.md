# DVL3

## Overview

DVL3 (Dishevelled segment polarity protein 3) is a cytoplasmic signal transducer in the canonical and non-canonical Wnt signaling pathways. Elevated DVL3 protein levels in pheochromocytoma and paraganglioma (PCC/PGL) have been reported in association with [MAML3](../genes/MAML3.md) fusion-positive tumors, which define the Wnt-altered molecular subtype — the subtype with the poorest clinical outcome.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Elevated DVL3 protein (by RPPA) detected in MAML3 fusion-positive pheochromocytoma/paraganglioma tumors ([PHC](../cancer_types/PHC.md)/[PGNG](../cancer_types/PGNG.md)); elevated alongside β-catenin and GSK3 in the Wnt-altered subtype in the TCGA PCPG multi-platform study (n=173) [PMID:28162975](../papers/28162975.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [PHC](../cancer_types/PHC.md) / [PGNG](../cancer_types/PGNG.md) — DVL3 protein overexpression marks the Wnt-altered mRNA subtype, which contains all MAML3 fusion-positive tumors and is associated with poor aggressive-disease-free and metastasis-free survival [PMID:28162975](../papers/28162975.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- DVL3 protein elevation co-occurs with elevated β-catenin and GSK3 and with [MAML3](../genes/MAML3.md) fusion positivity in PCC/PGL [PMID:28162975](../papers/28162975.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- The Wnt-altered subtype (defined partly by DVL3 elevation) may be responsive to β-catenin antagonists (e.g., PRI-274) or [STAT3](../genes/STAT3.md) inhibitors (e.g., BB1608), as proposed by the authors — not yet tested [PMID:28162975](../papers/28162975.md).

## Open questions

- Whether DVL3 protein overexpression in MAML3 fusion-positive tumors is a direct transcriptional target of the fusion or a secondary consequence of Wnt pathway activation is not established [PMID:28162975](../papers/28162975.md).
