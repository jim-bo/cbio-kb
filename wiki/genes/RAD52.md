# RAD52

## Overview

RAD52 is a DNA repair gene involved in homologous recombination (HR). It mediates single-strand DNA annealing and supports RAD51-dependent strand invasion. In cancer genomics, RAD52 is catalogued as part of the broader HR-deficiency (HRD) gene panel, with pathogenic alterations conferring genomic instability and potential sensitivity to PARP inhibitors and other DNA-damage-response (DDR)-targeted agents.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- RAD52 part of DNA damage repair gene set (ATM, BRCA1, BRCA2, PALB2, RAD51B, RAD51D, RAD52, RAD54L) with collectively 2.5% pathogenic alterations across 7,494 sarcomas; biallelic in 1.0% of all sarcomas; candidate for PARP/HRD-targeted therapy [PMID:35705558](../papers/35705558.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Sarcoma (various histologies): RAD52 pathogenic alterations catalogued across 44 histologies in a large FoundationOne sequencing cohort; highest DDR burden in ULMS, MPNST, PLLS, PECOMA, and ANGS subtypes [PMID:35705558](../papers/35705558.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with other DDR gene alterations (ATM, BRCA1/2, PALB2, RAD51B, RAD51D, RAD54L) as part of the HRD gene panel in sarcoma [PMID:35705558](../papers/35705558.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- High genomic LOH (gLOH ≥19.3%) in 15% of sarcomas motivates PARP inhibitor evaluation (rucaparib precedent from ovarian cancer); RAD52-altered tumors may be candidates for HRD-targeted therapy [PMID:35705558](../papers/35705558.md)

## Open questions

- Whether biallelic RAD52 loss in sarcoma predicts PARP inhibitor benefit analogous to BRCA1/2 in ovarian cancer requires prospective validation.
- gLOH mechanism in sarcoma is largely unexplained by canonical HRD-gene alterations; RAD52's contribution to this signal is unclear.
