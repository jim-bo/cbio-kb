# CPQ

## Overview

CPQ (carboxypeptidase Q) is a metalloprotease enzyme. It has been newly nominated as a significantly mutated gene in papillary renal cell carcinoma through comprehensive genomic analysis of non-clear-cell RCC.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Newly nominated significantly mutated gene in pRCC by multi-method driver analysis; oncogenic vs passenger status unestablished [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **PRCC**: CPQ identified among new significantly mutated genes in papillary renal cell carcinoma [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Nominated alongside NF2, SLC5A3, PNKD, LRP2, CHD3, NHERF1, SETD2, and CRTC1 as new significantly mutated genes in pRCC [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No therapeutic relevance established; functional role in pRCC tumorigenesis is unknown [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)

## Open questions

- Functional characterization of CPQ mutations in cancer is lacking; the authors flag CPQ alongside other newly nominated genes as requiring validation [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)
