# TWIST1

## Overview

TWIST1 is a basic helix-loop-helix transcription factor with established roles in epithelial-to-mesenchymal transition (EMT) and cancer metastasis. In small cell lung cancer ([SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md)), TWIST1 induction is associated with an acquired chemoresistance expression program, though functional experiments indicate it is a biomarker of resistance rather than a causal driver.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- TWIST1 transcriptionally upregulated in 3/10 SCLC patient-derived xenograft (PDX) models that developed acquired cisplatin/etoposide resistance in vivo; upregulation was accompanied by EMT-like changes including E-cadherin loss [PMID:28196596](../papers/28196596.md).
- TWIST1 induction and [SLFN11](../genes/SLFN11.md) suppression define mutually exclusive resistance programs across 7/10 resistant SCLC PDX models [PMID:28196596](../papers/28196596.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md): upregulated in 3/10 PDX models with acquired chemoresistance; pattern is mutually exclusive with [SLFN11](../genes/SLFN11.md) silencing [PMID:28196596](../papers/28196596.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Mutually exclusive with [SLFN11](../genes/SLFN11.md) downregulation as a resistance mechanism across 7/10 SCLC PDX models [PMID:28196596](../papers/28196596.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Functional gain/loss experiments (doxycycline-inducible [WT](../cancer_types/WT.md) or K145E DNA-binding-mutant TWIST1; shRNA suppression) showed TWIST1 does not change [etoposide](../drugs/etoposide.md) IC50 in murine SCLC cells, nor reverse EMT markers — authors conclude TWIST1 is a biomarker rather than a direct driver of chemoresistance [PMID:28196596](../papers/28196596.md).

## Open questions

- The 3/10 SCLC PDX models showing TWIST1 induction without SLFN11 loss were not fully characterised; the resistance mechanism in the remaining models (neither TWIST1 nor SLFN11-based) is unknown [PMID:28196596](../papers/28196596.md).
