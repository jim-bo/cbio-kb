# MSK-ACCESS (ACCESS129)

## Overview

Targeted (129 genes) sequencing of cfDNA via MSK-ACCESS on Illumina HiSeq sequencers — canonical cBioPortal gene panel `ACCESS129`.

## Used by

- [PMID:36357680](../papers/36357680.md) — at MSK, plasma sequencing with MSK-ACCESS was offered from 10 June 2019 onward in the ctDNA-guided [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) cohort, alongside MSK-IMPACT tissue sequencing; both assays use matched white-blood-cell sequencing to remove clonal hematopoiesis and germline variants [PMID:36357680](../papers/36357680.md).
- [PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md) — used for serial cfDNA profiling (>15,000x coverage, 0.1% VAF threshold) of 27 [erdafitinib](../drugs/erdafitinib.md)-treated urothelial carcinoma ([BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md)/[UTUC](../cancer_types/UTUC.md)) patients, identifying putative on-treatment resistance mutations in [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (n=5), [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) (n=1), and second-site [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) kinase-domain alleles including V553M and N540S [PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md).
- [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md) — MSK-ACCESS plasma ctDNA was compared with CSF ctDNA (MSK-IMPACT) in 31 patients with CNS tumors; 54% of alterations were shared between CSF and plasma, but CSF VAFs were markedly higher (median 36.4% vs. 2.3%, p < 0.01), demonstrating the superiority of CSF over plasma for CNS tumor monitoring [PMID:39289779](../papers/39289779.md).
- [PMID:37769223](../papers/37769223.md) — MSK-ACCESS (129-gene panel, ~200,000x raw coverage with error suppression) used to profile cfDNA from 53 plasma samples of 51 advanced [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) patients; detected genomic alterations in 90.6% of samples; complemented by matched tumor tissue profiled with MSK-IMPACT in 72.5% of patients [PMID:37769223](../papers/37769223.md).
- [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md) — MSK-ACCESS used to sequence plasma from 4,141 (discovery) and 1,426 (validation) patients across all cancer types; ctDNA detection was independently associated with VTE risk (HR 2.49); random survival forest model incorporating ACCESS ctDNA variables achieved c-index 0.74 for VTE prediction [PMID:39147831](../papers/39147831.md).

## Notes

- Sequencing operational metrics in the [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) ctDNA cohort: only 37/1,919 (2%) ctDx Lung or MSK-ACCESS samples failed, vs 13% of tissue sequencing attempts; turnaround from blood draw to report was 11 days (IQR 9–14) for MSK-ACCESS/ctDx Lung vs 33 days for MSK-IMPACT [PMID:36357680](../papers/36357680.md).
