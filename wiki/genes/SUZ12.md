# SUZ12

## Overview

SUZ12 encodes a core component of the Polycomb Repressive Complex 2 (PRC2), which catalyzes histone H3 lysine 27 trimethylation (H3K27me3) to mediate transcriptional silencing. Loss-of-function SUZ12 alterations lead to PRC2 inactivation and are recurrent in malignant peripheral nerve sheath tumors ([MPNST](../cancer_types/MPNST.md)), where they are associated with epigenetic dysregulation.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Inactivating mutations and deletions in 33% of RT-MPNST vs 17% of sporadic MPNST, indicating enrichment in radiation-associated MPNST [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [MPNST](../cancer_types/MPNST.md) -- SUZ12 inactivation enriched in RT-MPNST (33%) compared to sporadic MPNST (17%); PRC2 complex component [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- SUZ12 inactivation in MPNST co-occurs with [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) inactivation (67% in RT-MPNST) and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)/[CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md) deletions (92% in RT-MPNST) [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Investigations on small molecular inhibitors of DNA methyltransferase (DNMT1) are ongoing in sporadic/NF1-related and RT-MPNSTs harboring PRC2-inactivating mutations such as SUZ12 loss [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).

## Open questions

- Whether DNMT1 inhibitor efficacy differs between RT-MPNST and sporadic MPNST with PRC2 inactivation is not yet established [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).
