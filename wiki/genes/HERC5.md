# HERC5

## Overview

HERC5 (HECT And RLD Domain Containing E3 Ubiquitin Protein Ligase 5) is the primary [ISG15](../genes/ISG15.md) E3 ligase, responsible for conjugating ISG15 to substrate proteins (ISGylation) during the interferon response. HERC5 and its substrate ISG15 are interferon-stimulated genes (ISGs). In lung squamous cell carcinoma (LSCC) proteogenomics, HERC5 protein levels correlate with widespread ubiquitination changes enriched in metabolic pathways.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- HERC5 protein levels in LSCC correlated with 474 K-GG (di-glycine) ubiquitination sites enriched for glycolysis and TCA-cycle proteins; proposed as a mechanistic bridge between IFN signaling and regulation of glycolytic metabolism. Co-upregulated with its substrate ISG15 in the CPTAC LSCC cohort (n=108 tumors). [PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[LSCC](../cancer_types/LSCC.md):** HERC5 protein correlated with 474 ubiquitination sites enriched in metabolic pathways; identified in the CPTAC proteogenomic cohort. [PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Functionally coupled with [ISG15](../genes/ISG15.md) (its ubiquitination substrate) in the IFN–glycolysis axis in LSCC. [PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting described in the corpus. The HERC5–ISG15–metabolic axis may be relevant to immunotherapy response in immune-hot LSCC tumors.

## Open questions

- Whether the broad glycolytic ubiquitination program driven by HERC5 is tumor-promoting or tumor-suppressive in LSCC is not established.
- ISGylation targets within the glycolytic pathway (specific substrates among the 474 K-GG sites) are not individually characterized.
