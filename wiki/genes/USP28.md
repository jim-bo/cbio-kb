# USP28

## Overview

USP28 (ubiquitin-specific peptidase 28) is a deubiquitinase that regulates protein stability for several substrates including [MYC](../genes/MYC.md), [JUN](../genes/JUN.md), and 53BP1. It has been identified as a significantly mutated gene in prostate cancer, placing it within the ubiquitin-pathway SMG cluster alongside [USP7](../genes/USP7.md) in the SU2C/PCF 1,013-sample cohort.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Identified as a ubiquitin-pathway SMG in a 1,013-sample prostate cancer cohort (discovery + MSK-IMPACT validation); co-nominated with USP7 as a novel driver in the ubiquitin pathway [PMID:29610475](../papers/29610475.md)
- Mutated in 12.0% of CSCC tumors; high frequency of truncating mutations; encodes a deubiquitinase that stabilizes DNA-repair proteins and is required for Chk2-p53-PUMA-mediated apoptosis; nominated as novel CSCC driver in this meta-analysis [PMID:34272401](../papers/34272401.md)
