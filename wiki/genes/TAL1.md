# TAL1

## Overview

TAL1 (T-cell acute lymphocytic leukemia protein 1; also known as SCL) is a basic helix-loop-helix transcription factor that is a master regulator of hematopoiesis and is among the most frequently activated oncogenes in T-cell acute lymphoblastic leukemia (T-ALL). In the context of cancer genomics, TAL1 is widely cited as a canonical precedent for non-coding driver discovery: insertions of a cryptic regulatory element (an enhancer) upstream of the TAL1 locus activate its expression in T-ALL without altering the coding sequence, demonstrating that positive selection can act on non-coding regulatory mutations.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Cited as a founding precedent for enhancer-insertion–driven non-coding oncogene activation in T-ALL; used to contextualize the nomination of CTCF-binding-site (CBS) hotspot mutations as candidate non-coding drivers in gastric and colorectal cancer [PMID:29670109](../papers/29670109.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **T-cell acute lymphoblastic leukemia (T-ALL):** canonical oncogene; aberrant activation via enhancer insertions upstream of the TAL1 locus is a hallmark alteration.

- No co-occurrence or mutual exclusivity data reported in the corpus papers.

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting reported in the corpus papers. TAL1 activation in T-ALL is an upstream driver without a currently approved targeted agent.

## Open questions

- Whether non-coding regulatory mechanisms analogous to the TAL1 enhancer-insertion model operate at CBS hotspot loci in gastric and colorectal cancer is an open question raised in [PMID:29670109](../papers/29670109.md).
