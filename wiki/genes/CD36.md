# CD36

## Overview

CD36 (cluster of differentiation 36) is a scavenger receptor and fatty acid translocase involved in lipid uptake. In hepatocellular carcinoma, CD36 is frequently upregulated and promotes cancer progression by activating Src/AKT/mTOR and YAP signaling. It also mediates lipid-induced T-cell dysfunction in the tumor microenvironment.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- CD36 upregulated in [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md); drives Src/AKT/mTOR + YAP signaling and lipid uptake; anti-CD36 antibody (PLT012) sensitizes HCC to immune checkpoint inhibitors in preclinical models [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) — CD36 upregulation promotes HCC progression and immune evasion via lipid-mediated T-cell dysfunction; anti-CD36 (PLT012) is a preclinical ICI-sensitizing lead [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- CD36 upregulation is part of a broader lipid metabolic reprogramming axis alongside [FABP1](../genes/FABP1.md), [FABP4](../genes/FABP4.md), [FABP5](../genes/FABP5.md), [SLC27A4](../genes/SLC27A4.md) in HCC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Anti-CD36 antibody PLT012 (preclinical) resensitizes HCC to ICIs; targeting CD36-mediated lipid uptake is proposed as a strategy to overcome ICI and TKI resistance in HCC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Open questions

- PLT012 and other anti-CD36 agents are preclinical; translational pharmacology (toxicity, selectivity in cirrhotic livers) is largely open.
