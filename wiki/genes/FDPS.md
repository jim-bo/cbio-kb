# FDPS

## Overview

FDPS (Farnesyl Diphosphate Synthase) encodes an enzyme in the mevalonate/cholesterol biosynthesis pathway. In pediatric brain tumors, FDPS resides on chromosome 1q gain regions and has been identified as a CNV-RNA-Protein-Phospho cis-cascade gene in high-grade glioma (HGG), where copy number gains drive proportional increases in RNA and protein abundance.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- FDPS resides on chromosome 1q gain regions in pediatric HGG; identified as a CNV-RNA/Protein/Phospho cis-cascade gene in HGG, meaning 1q copy number gain proportionally elevates FDPS RNA, protein, and phosphoprotein abundance [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[HGGNOS](../cancer_types/HGGNOS.md)**: FDPS on chromosome 1q gain region; cis-cascade gene in pediatric HGG (copy number → RNA → protein → phosphoprotein) [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- FDPS co-localizes on chromosome 1q with [RABGAP1L](../genes/RABGAP1L.md); RABGAP1L is a cis-cascade gene in ependymoma ([EPM](../cancer_types/EPM.md)) while FDPS shows cis-cascade in HGG [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

FDPS encodes a key enzyme in the mevalonate pathway; bisphosphonates inhibit FDPS activity. No direct therapeutic application in pediatric brain tumors has been evaluated in this corpus.

## Open questions

- Whether FDPS upregulation (driven by 1q gain) is a therapeutic vulnerability in pediatric HGG via mevalonate pathway inhibition (bisphosphonates, statins) remains unexplored.
