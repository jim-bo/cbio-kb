# pazopanib

## Overview

Pazopanib is an FDA-approved multi-target tyrosine kinase inhibitor targeting VEGFR1/2/3, PDGFRA/B, and [KIT](../genes/KIT.md). It has regulatory approval for advanced soft-tissue sarcoma (excluding liposarcoma) after prior chemotherapy.

## Evidence in the corpus

- [RMS](../cancer_types/RMS.md) PDTOs from the UCLA functional sarcoma screen were significantly more sensitive than the pan-sarcoma average to pazopanib (p=0.049). [PMID:39305899](../papers/39305899.md)
- Specimens from patients who had received ≥3 prior therapy lines showed a trend toward enhanced sensitivity to pazopanib (p=0.0062), consistent with a late-line activity profile in heavily pre-treated sarcomas. [PMID:39305899](../papers/39305899.md)
- High [PIK3R2](../genes/PIK3R2.md) and [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) expression guided pazopanib recommendation in metastatic NEN patients (PN1, PN14, PN17, PN25) in the POG NEN WGTA cohort [PMID:24326773](../papers/24326773.md)
- Nominated for Triple-WT melanoma subtype (14% of 318 TCGA cases) to target co-amplified PDGFRA/KDR at 4q12 alongside [imatinib](../drugs/imatinib.md), [dasatinib](../drugs/dasatinib.md), [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md), [crenolanib](../drugs/crenolanib.md), and [regorafenib](../drugs/regorafenib.md) [PMID:26091043](../papers/26091043.md)
