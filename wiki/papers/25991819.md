**PMID:** 25991819 · **DOI:** 10.1158/1078-0432.CCR-14-2116 · **Journal:** Clinical Cancer Research (2015)

## TL;DR

Braggio et al. performed a comprehensive genomic study of 19 immunocompetent primary central nervous system lymphoma ([PCNSL](../cancer_types/PCNSL.md)) patients using array-CGH, whole exome sequencing, mate-pair WGS, targeted sequencing, and FISH. They uncovered novel PCNSL-specific recurrent biallelic inactivation of **[TOX](../genes/TOX.md)** and **[PRKCD](../genes/PRKCD.md)**, a high prevalence of **[MYD88](../genes/MYD88.md)** mutations (79%) and **[CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)** biallelic loss (60%), plus a set of alterations shared with systemic DLBCL (e.g. **[TNFAIP3](../genes/TNFAIP3.md)**, **[PRDM1](../genes/PRDM1.md)**, **[GNA13](../genes/GNA13.md)**, **[TMEM30A](../genes/TMEM30A.md)**, **[TBL1XR1](../genes/TBL1XR1.md)**, **[B2M](../genes/B2M.md)**, **[CD58](../genes/CD58.md)**, **[CD79B](../genes/CD79B.md)**, **[CARD11](../genes/CARD11.md)**, IGH-BCL6 translocations). BCR/TLR/NF-κB pathway components were altered in >90% of [PCNSL](../cancer_types/PCNSL.md) cases, highlighting potential therapeutic targets.

## Cohort & data

- **N = 19 immunocompetent [PCNSL](../cancer_types/PCNSL.md) patients** (HIV− and EBV−), newly diagnosed and confined to the CNS, retrieved from the Mayo Clinic Tumor Registry (IRB 08-001933) and the University of Virginia (IRB 14225). Cancer type: [PCNSL](../cancer_types/PCNSL.md); compared throughout to systemic [DLBCLNOS](../cancer_types/DLBCLNOS.md).
- Dataset: [pcnsl_mayo_2015](../datasets/pcnsl_mayo_2015.md). aCGH microarray data deposited in GEO under accession **GSE28952**.
- DNA from frozen tissue (7 cases) or FFPE (12 cases); 3 frozen samples required whole-genome amplification due to insufficient yield. 9 of 19 biopsies provided tumor-free tissue for matched-normal validation sequencing.
- Assays/methods applied:
  - [array-cgh-agilent-1m](../methods/array-cgh-agilent-1m.md) — 18 cases, Human Genome 244A + Sureprint G3 (Agilent); CNA calling via Nexus RANK segmentation; CNVs filtered using TCAG plus 10 in-house HapMap controls.
  - [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) — 10 cases, SureSelect 50 Mb capture, 100 bp paired-end on HiSeq2000, mean 65.6M mapped reads in capture, median 80% of exome at ≥30× coverage. SNVs called with SomaticSniper; indels with [gatk-somatic-indel-detector](../methods/gatk-somatic-indel-detector.md); annotated by snpEFF/PolyPhen-2 via the TREAT workflow.
  - [mate-pair-seq](../methods/mate-pair-seq.md), [targeted-dna-seq](../methods/targeted-dna-seq.md) (Ion Torrent PGM, [MYD88](../genes/MYD88.md) coding regions at ~2,005× average depth), [sanger-sequencing](../methods/sanger-sequencing.md) for validation of CARD11/CD79B/PRKCD/TNFAIP3 variants, and interphase [fish](../methods/fish.md) for IGH-BCL6, [PRKCD](../genes/PRKCD.md), and [TOX](../genes/TOX.md) rearrangements.

## Key findings

- **Highly complex karyotype.** Median 21 copy-number abnormalities per patient (range 10–47). Sixteen chromosomal abnormalities were present in >25% of cases (10 deletions, 6 gains).
- **CDKN2A/B (9p21.3) deletion** was the most common CNA: 83% of cases. Homozygous deletion of [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) (and [CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md)) was found in 55% of cases; another 21% had monoallelic loss.
- **Chromosome 6 abnormalities** were prominent: 6p21 (HLA genes) deletion in 9/18 (50%); 6q21 ([PRDM1](../genes/PRDM1.md)) and 6q23 ([TNFAIP3](../genes/TNFAIP3.md)) losses in 10/18 each (55%); 6q14.1 ([TMEM30A](../genes/TMEM30A.md)) deletion in 8/18 (44%).
- **PCNSL-specific biallelic [PRKCD](../genes/PRKCD.md) inactivation.** Two of 10 WES cases had splice-donor mutations (one with an additional missense p.W608S), each co-occurring with monoallelic deletion of *[PRKCD](../genes/PRKCD.md)*; another 3/18 aCGH cases carried focal monoallelic deletions (<1 Mb). No [PRKCD](../genes/PRKCD.md) recurrence has been reported in nodal DLBCL; COSMIC lists only 1/1,058 hematologic samples (p.A163T in ALL, 0.01%).
- **PCNSL-specific [TOX](../genes/TOX.md) biallelic loss.** 11% of cases had homozygous deletion of *[TOX](../genes/TOX.md)* (8q12); 17% additional cases showed focal monoallelic deletion. No [TOX](../genes/TOX.md) homozygous deletions were detected in 754 cell lines (including 127 hematopoietic/lymphoid) in the Cancer Genome Project dataset.
- **[MYD88](../genes/MYD88.md) activating mutations were near-ubiquitous.** Mutated in 7/10 WES cases (70%) and in 11/14 cases (79%) after adding 4 targeted-sequenced cases. Nearly all were the L265P hotspot; two were V217F and M232T. Prevalence is ~2× that reported in nodal ABC-DLBCL.
- **Other recurrently mutated NF-κB pathway genes:** [CD79B](../genes/CD79B.md) 40%, [PIM1](../genes/PIM1.md) 40%, [BTG2](../genes/BTG2.md) 30%, [CARD11](../genes/CARD11.md) 30%, [GNA13](../genes/GNA13.md) 20%, [TNFAIP3](../genes/TNFAIP3.md) 20%, [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) 10%. 3 of 4 CD79B-mutant cases co-mutated [MYD88](../genes/MYD88.md).
- **Recurrent homozygous deletions** in [PCNSL](../cancer_types/PCNSL.md) included HLA genes (4/18, 22%), [PRDM1](../genes/PRDM1.md), [TMEM30A](../genes/TMEM30A.md), [TOX](../genes/TOX.md), and [CD58](../genes/CD58.md) (each in 2/18, 11%); plus individual HDs of [B2M](../genes/B2M.md), [ETV6](../genes/ETV6.md), [TNFAIP3](../genes/TNFAIP3.md), and [TNFRSF10A](../genes/TNFRSF10A.md) (Table 1).
- **High-level amplifications were rare** (4 events total): 1p31.1–p22.2 (including [BCL10](../genes/BCL10.md)), 7p22.3–p22.2 (including [CARD11](../genes/CARD11.md)), 12q14.1–q21.33 (including [BCL7A](../genes/BCL7A.md) region), and 18q21.2 ([TCF4](../genes/TCF4.md)) — each in one sample.
- **Gains** of 3p14 ([FOXP1](../genes/FOXP1.md)) and 3q27 ([BCL6](../genes/BCL6.md)) were noted, with breakpoints inside or 5′ of these genes suggesting their involvement in unbalanced translocations. Other recurrent gains: 7q21–q31 (39%), 11q (28%), 12q (50%), 19q13 (33%); loss of 10p15.3–p14 in 33%.
- **IGH-BCL6 translocations** detected by FISH in 2/11 PCNSL cases analyzed.
- **BCR/TLR/NF-κB pathway alteration in 93% (≈>90%) of PCNSL** when integrating mutation and CNA data. Pathway enrichment also implicated proliferation, immune response, apoptosis regulation, and lymphocyte differentiation.
- **Mutually exclusive copy-number patterns:** gain of 12q was mutually exclusive with deletion of 10p15.3–p14 and with gains of 3p14 ([FOXP1](../genes/FOXP1.md)) and 11q (P = 0.0004); losses of 3p21.31 (PRKCD) and 6q23 ([TNFAIP3](../genes/TNFAIP3.md)) were rare in 12q-gained cases.
- **Survival association:** deletion of 6q21 ([PRDM1](../genes/PRDM1.md)) was associated with shorter overall survival in univariate analysis (log-rank P = 0.001), but the authors caution this is in a small, heterogeneously treated cohort.

## Genes & alterations

- [PRKCD](../genes/PRKCD.md) — biallelic inactivation (splice-donor mutations + p.W608S missense; monoallelic deletions). Novel PCNSL-specific finding; not recurrently mutated in nodal DLBCL.
- [TOX](../genes/TOX.md) — homozygous deletion (11%) and focal monoallelic deletion (17%) at 8q12. Novel PCNSL-specific finding; loss tentatively associated with shorter [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md).
- [MYD88](../genes/MYD88.md) — activating mutations in 79% (11/14), predominantly L265P, plus V217F and M232T. ~2× nodal ABC-DLBCL prevalence.
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) / [CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md) — 9p21.3 deletion in 83%; homozygous in 55%.
- [PRDM1](../genes/PRDM1.md) — 6q21 deletion in 55%; homozygous in 2/18. Deletion associated with shorter [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md).
- [TNFAIP3](../genes/TNFAIP3.md) — 6q23 deletion in 55%; somatic mutations in 20%.
- [TMEM30A](../genes/TMEM30A.md) — 6q14.1 deletion in 44%; homozygous in 2/18.
- [TBL1XR1](../genes/TBL1XR1.md) — 3q26.32 deletion (recurrent).
- [B2M](../genes/B2M.md) — homozygous deletion in individual cases.
- [CD58](../genes/CD58.md) — homozygous deletion in 2/18 (11%).
- [CD79B](../genes/CD79B.md) — activating mutations in 40%; frequently co-occur with [MYD88](../genes/MYD88.md) mutations (3 of 4 CD79B-mutant cases).
- [CARD11](../genes/CARD11.md) — mutations in 30%; one case with high-level amplification at 7p22.
- [BCL6](../genes/BCL6.md) — IGH-BCL6 translocation in 2/11 FISH-screened cases; recurrent 3q27 gains with breakpoints suggesting translocation.
- [FOXP1](../genes/FOXP1.md) — recurrent 3p14 gain with breakpoints suggesting translocation.
- [GNA13](../genes/GNA13.md) — mutations in 20%; recurrent homozygous deletion.
- [PIM1](../genes/PIM1.md) — mutations in 40%.
- [BTG2](../genes/BTG2.md) — mutations in 30%.
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — mutations in 10%.
- [BCL10](../genes/BCL10.md), [BCL7A](../genes/BCL7A.md), [TCF4](../genes/TCF4.md), [ETV6](../genes/ETV6.md), [TNFRSF10A](../genes/TNFRSF10A.md) — each implicated in one or two cases as part of focal amplification or homozygous deletion events.

## Clinical implications

- BCR/TLR/NF-κB pathway components were altered in >90% of PCNSL, supporting targeted therapy directed at this axis (e.g. BTK, IRAK4, PI3K inhibition) as a rational strategy in PCNSL — though the paper does not test any drug.
- The PCNSL-specific MYD88 L265P prevalence (~79%) is higher than reported in nodal ABC-DLBCL and makes MYD88-dependent signaling a particularly attractive target in PCNSL.
- Loss of [PRKCD](../genes/PRKCD.md) expression has previously been linked to radiation resistance in cervical cancer (via MiR-181a); the authors speculate this could extrapolate to PCNSL, suggesting *PRKCD* status as a candidate biomarker for radiation responsiveness.
- Deletion of 6q21 ([PRDM1](../genes/PRDM1.md)) was associated with shorter overall survival (univariate log-rank P = 0.001), nominating it as a candidate prognostic marker pending validation.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Small cohort (N = 19)** with heterogeneous treatment exposure limits power for survival associations; the PRDM1/OS link is explicitly flagged by the authors as requiring confirmation in larger cohorts with robust age, performance status, comorbidity, and treatment annotation.
- **FFPE/amplified DNA** in a subset of samples yielded lower WES coverage than fresh-frozen DNA; some cases had only 18-case aCGH or 10-case WES coverage, so prevalence estimates have wide confidence intervals.
- **No functional validation** of TOX or PRKCD loss in PCNSL models — the pathogenic role is inferred from recurrence and from prior literature in T- and B-cell biology.
- **No matched germline DNA** was available for 10 of 19 cases, which limited somatic-vs-germline calling for those samples.
- **Targetable-pathway claim is not therapeutically tested** in this work — actual drug response in BCR/TLR/NF-κB-altered PCNSL remains an open question.
- **Comparison to systemic DLBCL is qualitative** (literature-based) rather than against a paired internal DLBCL cohort.
