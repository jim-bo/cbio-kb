# TP53

## Overview

TP53 (Tumor Protein P53) encodes the p53 transcription factor, the most frequently mutated gene across all human cancers. p53 acts as a master regulator of the DNA damage response, cell cycle arrest, apoptosis, and senescence. TP53 is inactivated by missense mutation (often with dominant-negative gain-of-function effects), deletion, or MDM2-mediated degradation. Its mutation frequency varies substantially by cancer type, and in the metastatic setting TP53 is often enriched relative to primary tumors. Germline TP53 mutations define Li-Fraumeni syndrome.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Somatic mutation identified in the TCGA PCC/PGL cohort ([pcpg_tcga_pub](../datasets/pcpg_tcga_pub.md), n=173) as part of a cancer-relevant gene scan alongside [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md) and [ARNT](../genes/ARNT.md). [PMID:28162975](../papers/28162975.md)
- Germline TP53 S215N missense identified in a pediatric [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md) patient in the PIPseq program (n=101, Columbia University); explained lack of response to conventional therapy and increased other-cancer risk. [PMID:28007021](../papers/28007021.md)
- Significantly mutated in both [EAC](../cancer_types/EAC.md) and [ESCC](../cancer_types/ESCC.md) in the TCGA esophageal carcinoma cohort ([stes_tcga_pub](../datasets/stes_tcga_pub.md), n=164 esophageal); notably mutated in only 1 of 4 ESCC3 tumors. [PMID:28052061](../papers/28052061.md)
- Mutated in 27% of HR+/HER2− metastatic breast cancer (mBC) vs 20% of HR+/HER2− early breast cancer (eBC) in 216 mBC patients (Fisher p=0.13, not statistically significant); TP53 mutation is not enriched in the metastatic setting for HR+/HER2− subtype. [PMID:28027327](../papers/28027327.md)
- TP53 recurrently altered at baseline in [SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md) PDX models; alterations preserved through acquired cisplatin/etoposide resistance with no evidence of additional acquired TP53 mutations under chemotherapy selection [PMID:28196596](../papers/28196596.md).
- TP53 significantly enriched in the unknown mitogenic driver (UMD) subset of 860 metastatic [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) patients profiled by MSK-IMPACT (p<0.05 vs level 1–4 cohort); elevated in heavy-smoker UMD samples [PMID:28336552](../papers/28336552.md).
- Single TP53 R248W missense event observed in an acral lentiginous melanoma ([ACRM](../cancer_types/ACRM.md)) cohort of 34 patients; MDM2/TP53 (apoptosis/senescence) pathway altered in 17% of ALM patients [PMID:28373299](../papers/28373299.md).

- Fewer alterations in Black prostate cancer patients (19% vs. 30% White), specifically fewer missense mutations; R273 hotspot prevalence is only 2% in White men in MSK-IMPACT cohort (n=1,123). [PMID:34667026](../papers/34667026.md)
- Loss-of-function mutations and CNAs are the defining [HGSOC](../cancer_types/HGSOC.md) alteration; TP53 ranked #1 by positive Δκ<sub>risk</sub> (0.209) in network curvature analysis of 45 ICI-treated [HGSOC](../cancer_types/HGSOC.md) patients, validating the methodology against known HGSOC biology. [PMID:34819508](../papers/34819508.md)
- Similar overall mutation frequency in MSS colorectal tumors (71.7% Nigeria vs 77.5% MSKCC), but skewed away from right-sided tumors in Nigerian CRC (25% right-sided vs 81.6% left-sided) [PMID:34819518](../papers/34819518.md)
- Mutated in 71% of MSS CRCs vs 7% of MSI-H CRCs in the COLON MAP/HTAN colorectal polyp atlas, confirming TP53 as a major driver of the conventional adenoma-to-MSS CRC pathway [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)
- Somatic hotspot mutations (C135S, V172F, R175H, R213P, R249S, L257Q, G266R) in 5/17 pediatric neuroblastoma-to-leukemia transformation patients; acquired post-therapy (distinct from adult CH); co-occurs with [PPM1D](../genes/PPM1D.md) mutations in 2 patients, consistent with parallel DDR-pathway clonal evolution [PMID:35078859](../papers/35078859.md)
- Mutation enriched in metastases in 7 tumor types (LUAD, [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md), HR+/HER2- ductal breast, MSS [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md), lobular breast, pancreatic neuroendocrine, endometrioid uterine); inverse pattern in [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md) (62% primary vs 45% metastatic, q=0.01); positively correlated with metastatic burden in [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md), LUAD, and HR+/HER2- ductal breast [PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)
- TP53 co-mutated in CRC-DD (dual MAPK driver colorectal cancer); TP53 C275Y identified as a truncal event in patient P-0021518's evolutionary tree, preceding both KRAS mutations in the clonal hierarchy [PMID:35235413](../papers/35235413.md).
- TP53 most frequent driver in the AcSé-MAPPYACTS pediatric cohort with 215 deleterious mutations/deletions across 632 relapsed/refractory samples; recommended for [WEE1](../genes/WEE1.md) inhibitor matching based on ovarian-cancer data; recurrent in CNS subgroups (HGG, [MBL](../cancer_types/MBL.md), EPN) [PMID:35292802](../papers/35292802.md).
- Concurrent TP53 mutations in 8 [RET](../genes/RET.md) fusion-positive lung cancer cases linked to significantly shorter PFS (HR=3.5) on [selpercatinib](../drugs/selpercatinib.md) therapy in the MSK clinical cohort [PMID:35304457](../papers/35304457.md).
- TP53 altered in 79% of MSI-stable esophageal adenocarcinoma (EAC) patients in the MSK neoadjuvant CRT cohort (n=237); severity of TP53 dysfunction (TruncBi > Other > [WT](../cancer_types/WT.md)) tracks with worse pathologic treatment response and shorter PFS; TP53 TruncBi mutations (n=37) had 24-month cumulative progression incidence 67% [PMID:35377946](../papers/35377946.md).
- TP53 missense, truncating, splice, and in-frame mutations in 92% (24/26) clinical [SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md) samples and 90% of PDX samples; retained in 16/18 (89%) matched clinical-PDX pairs; TP53 loss is SCLC-specific in MSK773 admixed SCLC/LUAD tumor; 2 PDXs (MSK599, MSK871) lost the TP53 alterations seen in matched clinical samples [PMID:35440124](../papers/35440124.md).
- TP53 mutations are rare in low-grade serous ovarian carcinoma ([LGSOC](../cancer_types/LGSOC.md)) and are characterized in the WHO classification as distinguishing LGSC from HGSC; no recurrent TP53 driver mutations were found in 119 LGSOC cases on MSK-IMPACT [PMID:35443055](../papers/35443055.md)
- TP53 was mutated in 13/51 (25%) prostate cancer brain metastasis (PCBM) cases and served as a truncal alteration in patient P4, illustrating early clonal origin of PCBM [PMID:35504881](../papers/35504881.md)
- TP53 mutations in HCC (122-biopsy proteogenomics cohort) were associated with higher grade (p=0.001), HBV etiology (p=0.010), and poor prognosis (multivariate Cox p=0.034 independent of BCLC); TP53 and [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) mutations were mutually exclusive (Fisher p=0.012); TP53-mutant HCCs showed increased CDK1/CDK2/CDK5, [AURKA](../genes/AURKA.md), [TTK](../genes/TTK.md), [NEK2](../genes/NEK2.md), [EIF2AK2](../genes/EIF2AK2.md), [PRKD2](../genes/PRKD2.md), and p38δ kinase activity by KSEA [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)
- TP53 locus was altered by noncoding structural variants (SVs) in 13/29 osteosarcoma patients and by SNV/indels in 12/29; only 3 of the SV-driven alterations were detected by MSK-IMPACT, demonstrating that panel sequencing misclassifies TP53 SV-altered tumors as wild type — with implications for p53-pathway clinical trial eligibility [PMID:35585047](../papers/35585047.md)
- TP53 was the most frequently altered gene in 40 metastatic CRPC models (organoids, PDXs, cell lines), with putative driver mutations or deep deletions in 23/35 (66%) samples across all four chromatin-defined subtypes; no subtype-specific TP53 enrichment was observed, distinguishing it from RB1 which was enriched in AR-negative/low samples [PMID:35617398](../papers/35617398.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [PHC](../cancer_types/PHC.md) / [PGNG](../cancer_types/PGNG.md): Somatic TP53 mutation identified as a cancer-relevant gene scan finding; not a primary driver in this low-mutation-burden tumor type. [PMID:28162975](../papers/28162975.md)
- [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md): Germline TP53 S215N — a functionally relevant mutation explaining conventional-therapy resistance and conferring elevated risk for secondary cancers; family counseling implications. [PMID:28007021](../papers/28007021.md)
- [ESCC](../cancer_types/ESCC.md) / [ESCA](../cancer_types/ESCA.md): TP53 is a significantly mutated gene in both [ESCC](../cancer_types/ESCC.md) and EAC; low frequency in ESCC3 distinguishes this subtype. [PMID:28052061](../papers/28052061.md)
- [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md): TP53 mutation rate (27%) in HR+/HER2− mBC is slightly above eBC (20%) but not statistically enriched (p=0.13); TP53 is not a metastasis-specific driver in HR+/HER2− breast cancer. [PMID:28027327](../papers/28027327.md)
- [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md): Largely missense mutations in DNA-binding domain; protein elevated without RNA change; DNA-repair phosphosites upregulated without protein-level upregulation in TP53-mutant tumors; nominates ATR/CDK inhibitors. [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)
- [IHCH](../cancer_types/IHCH.md): Mutated in 17% of iCCA; independent predictor of shorter OS (HR 1.82, P=0.03 resected; HR 2.05, P<0.001 unresected on multivariate); associated with LVI, nodal disease, large bile-duct subtype, and PSC. [PMID:33765338](../papers/33765338.md)
- [EGC](../cancer_types/EGC.md) / [ESCA](../cancer_types/ESCA.md): Mutated in ~80% of EAC; not associated with OS; mutually exclusive with MDM2 amplification. [PMID:33795256](../papers/33795256.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- In PCC/PGL, somatic TP53 mutation occurred in a context of otherwise mutually exclusive driver events; driver gene mutual exclusivity was significant (p < 1e-4) across the 21-gene set. [PMID:28162975](../papers/28162975.md)
- In ESCC, TP53 is nearly universally mutated and does not show the same strong mutual exclusivity pattern as in PCC/PGL. [PMID:28052061](../papers/28052061.md)
- In GBM, TP53 mutations co-occur with [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) loss and [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) R132H; mutually exclusive with [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter hotspot mutations. [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)
- In iCCA, TP53 co-occurs with [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter alterations (τ=0.19, Q=0.002) and PI3K/TGF-β/cell cycle/RTK-RAS pathway alterations; mutually exclusive with IDH1/2 mutations (τ=−0.15, Q=0.02). [PMID:33765338](../papers/33765338.md)
- In EAC, TP53 mutations are mutually exclusive with [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) amplification (p<0.001, q<0.05); MDM2 amplification present in 24% of TP53-wild-type tumors. [PMID:33795256](../papers/33795256.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Germline TP53 S215N in pediatric AML explained poor response to conventional therapy; this finding has implications for altered treatment strategies and second-cancer surveillance. [PMID:28007021](../papers/28007021.md)
- In GBM, TP53-mutant tumors show upregulated DNA-repair phosphosites with elevated [ATR](../genes/ATR.md), [CDK2](../genes/CDK2.md), and [CDK9](../genes/CDK9.md), nominating DNA-damage-response inhibitors ([ATR](../genes/ATR.md) inhibitors, CDK inhibitors) as a hypothesis-generating therapeutic strategy for TP53-mutant GBM. [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)
- In iCCA, TP53 mutation is an independent predictor of shorter OS (HR 1.82 resected, P=0.03); proposed as part of a pretreatment prognostic genomic panel alongside KRAS and CDKN2A to inform surgical vs. systemic therapy decisions. [PMID:33765338](../papers/33765338.md)

## Open questions

- The functional significance of somatic TP53 mutation in PCC/PGL (a tumor type with low overall mutation burden) and its relationship to tumor aggressiveness requires further characterization. [PMID:28162975](../papers/28162975.md)
- Whether TP53 mutations in metastatic HR+/HER2− breast cancer influence response to CDK4/6 inhibitors or endocrine therapy requires prospective biomarker studies. [PMID:28027327](../papers/28027327.md)
