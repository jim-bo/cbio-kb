# JAG1

## Overview

JAG1 (Jagged canonical Notch ligand 1) encodes a Notch pathway ligand. In oral squamous cell carcinoma (OSCC), copy gains of JAG1 have been identified alongside [JAG2](../genes/JAG2.md) gains; these ligand gains may either inhibit [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) through cis-inhibition or activate it, and are observed in the context where NOTCH1 functions as a tumor suppressor in OSCC.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Copy gain observed in OSCC; Notch ligand gains may inhibit NOTCH1 via cis-inhibition or activate it, consistent with the tumor-suppressive role inferred for NOTCH1 in OSCC [PMID:23619168](../papers/23619168.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **OSCC (Oral Squamous Cell Carcinoma):** copy number gain; co-occurs with JAG2 gains; mechanistic interpretation (cis-inhibition vs. activation of NOTCH1) is uncertain [PMID:23619168](../papers/23619168.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-gained with JAG2 in OSCC; both are Notch pathway ligands; NOTCH1 is mutated in 9% of OSCC and functions as a tumor suppressor [PMID:23619168](../papers/23619168.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting reported; Notch pathway context in OSCC under investigation [PMID:23619168](../papers/23619168.md)

## Open questions

- Mechanistic role of JAG1 copy gain in OSCC is ambiguous: whether it promotes cis-inhibition of NOTCH1 (tumor-suppressive) or activates Notch signaling (oncogenic) is unresolved [PMID:23619168](../papers/23619168.md)
