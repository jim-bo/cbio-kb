**PMID:** 33577785 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.ccell.2021.01.006 · **Journal:** Cancer Cell (2021)

## TL;DR

Wang et al. report an integrated proteogenomic and metabolomic characterization of 99 treatment-naive adult [glioblastoma](../cancer_types/GBM.md) tumors prospectively collected through the Clinical Proteomic Tumor Analysis Consortium (CPTAC), profiled on ten platforms: whole-genome sequencing, whole-exome sequencing, RNA-seq, miRNA-seq, single-nuclei RNA-seq, DNA methylation arrays, global proteome, phosphoproteome, acetylome, lipidome, and metabolome. Multi-omics NMF clustering recapitulated the three TCGA expression subtypes (proneural-like nmf1, mesenchymal-like nmf2, classical-like nmf3) but reclassified 29% of tumors; a "mixed subtype" (n = 12) with low membership in multiple clusters had significantly worse survival (log-rank p = 1.7e-4). Phospho-signaling analyses nominated [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) (Shp2) Y62 and [PLCG1](../genes/PLCG1.md) Y783 as a convergent RTK signaling hub downstream of [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) and [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md), and identified upregulated DNA-repair phosphosites without protein-level upregulation in [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)-mutant tumors. Four immune subtypes (im1–im4) were defined from xCell deconvolution and validated by snRNA-seq; im4 was uniformly cold (1.3% T cells, 6% TAM). H2B N-terminal hyperacetylation marks a classical-like subset and is associated with [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md), [EP300](../genes/EP300.md), and [BRD1](../genes/BRD1.md)/[BRD3](../genes/BRD3.md)/[BRD4](../genes/BRD4.md) activity. Lipidomic profiling on 75 tumors quantified 582 lipids and revealed subtype-specific patterns (TG enrichment + PC depletion in mesenchymal-like; VLCFA/PUFA-glycerophospholipids enriched in proneural-like) plus [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) R132H–driven global metabolic shifts. Drug-connectivity analysis against [LINCS L1000](../methods/lincs-l1000.md) and P100 nominated kinase inhibitors (broader than [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) inhibitors) for EGFR-altered tumors and MAPK inhibitors for [NF1](../genes/NF1.md)-altered tumors.

## Cohort & data

- **N = 99 treatment-naive adult [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) tumors** prospectively collected through CPTAC from 10 tissue source sites in 6 countries. Males n = 55, females n = 44; age range 24–88.
- 10 unmatched GTEx frontal cortex normal samples used as reference.
- **6 [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) R132H–mutant tumors** (median onset 47 vs. 59 years for IDH1-WT, t-test p = 0.055); one additional non-hotspot R222C mutation.
- All samples cryopulverized and aliquoted to 10 omics platforms (Figure 1A, S1A–C):
  - DNA: PCR-free [WGS](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) on HiSeq X (min 15× tumor), [WES](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) on HiSeq 4000 (min 150× on-target), [Illumina Infinium MethylationEPIC](../methods/epic-methylation-array.md) (>850k CpGs).
  - RNA: total [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) on HiSeq 4000 (≥120M reads/sample, hg38), [miRNA-seq](../methods/mirna-seq.md) (≥10M reads), [snRNA-seq](../methods/snrna-seq.md) on 10X Chromium for 18 tumors (7 im1, 5 im2, 1 im3, 5 im4).
  - Protein: [TMT-11–plex LC-MS/MS](../methods/tmt-proteomics.md) global proteome, [IMAC-enriched phosphoproteome](../methods/tmt-phosphoproteomics.md), and Ac-K–immunoaffinity-enriched acetylome on Orbitrap Fusion Lumos.
  - Small molecules: label-free GC-MS metabolome and LC-MS lipidome (582 lipids quantified across 75 tumors).
- Independent validation cohort: 39 high-grade (age 14–39) brain tumors from the Children's Brain Tumor Tissue Consortium (CBTTC) processed by matched TMT proteome/phosphoproteome.
- Dataset: [gbm_cptac_2021](../datasets/gbm_cptac_2021.md) (reference genome hg38). Raw data: CPTAC Data Portal S048, S057; GDC project CPTAC-3; Proteomic Data Commons.

## Key findings

- **Multi-omics NMF clustering reproduces TCGA subtypes but reclassifies 29% of tumors.** The three IDH-WT clusters were designated nmf1 (proneural-like, n = 29), nmf2 (mesenchymal-like, n = 37), and nmf3 (classical-like, n = 26); 27 tumors (29%) were reassigned relative to TCGA expression-based labels. Pathway enrichment: nmf1 enriched for synaptic vesicle cycling and neurotransmission; nmf2 enriched for innate immune response, neutrophil degranulation, ECM organization; nmf3 enriched for mRNA splicing and RNA metabolism. Acetylation-pathway enrichment: nmf1 acetylated TCA-cycle and amino-acid-metabolism proteins; nmf2 acetylated innate-immune/glycolysis proteins; nmf3 acetylated chromatin modifiers and DNA-repair proteins.
- **Mixed-subtype tumors have worse survival.** 12 tumors with low multi-omics membership scores for ≥2 subtypes had significantly shorter overall survival vs. non-mixed (log-rank p = 1.7e-4), excluding IDH1-mutants.
- **Three survival-associated proteins identified across all tumors:** low [HIST3H2BB](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/?term=HIST3H2BB) (log-rank p = 0.0034), high MT-CYB (p = 0.03), and high PRODH (p = 0.096).
- **DNA methylation: 6 subtypes with two G-CIMP variants.** dm6 is IDH-mutant-specific with chromatin-organization upregulation; dm2 is IDH-WT with transcription/mRNA-splicing upregulation. 38 of 90 tumors (42%) showed [MGMT](../genes/MGMT.md) promoter hypermethylation with significant downregulation of [MGMT](../genes/MGMT.md) RNA (Welch's t-test p = 4.9e-11) and protein (p = 2.6e-6).
- **[TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter mutations in 74% of primary GBMs** (C228T and C250T) with elevated [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) RNA expression. [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) mutations were mutually exclusive with TERTp hotspot mutations and co-occurred with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) R132H; 9 ATRX-mutants had significantly diminished [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) RNA and protein, confirmed by [IHC](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md).
- **Driver alterations leave specific phospho-signatures (95 cis-trans phosphorylation events).** Strong cis effects: [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) S1166 and [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md) S1067/S1070. Trans effects of EGFR alteration: elevated [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) protein (β-catenin) despite decreased mRNA, and increased phosphorylation of [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) Y62 and [PLCG1](../genes/PLCG1.md) Y783.
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)–mutant tumors show DNA-repair phosphosite upregulation without protein-level upregulation,** indicating phosphosite-specific regulation. [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) S315 and [TP53BP1](../genes/TP53BP1.md) S1099/S1106/S1109 phosphorylation correlate with TP53 protein abundance (Pearson r = 0.89 and 0.53). In TP53-mutants: elevated [ATR](../genes/ATR.md), [MAPK3](../genes/MAPK3.md), [CDK2](../genes/CDK2.md), and [CDK9](../genes/CDK9.md) protein/phosphorylation; [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) decreased at RNA and protein. A recurrent X126_splice variant yields an in-frame 7-aa truncation in exon 5.
- **[RB1](../genes/RB1.md)-altered samples (12% of cohort) show negative feedback to cell-cycle effectors:** downregulated [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) protein and upregulated [MCM2](../genes/MCM2.md), [MCM4](../genes/MCM4.md), [MCM6](../genes/MCM6.md) protein. [NF1](../genes/NF1.md)–altered tumors upregulate [IRF8](../genes/IRF8.md) (RNA and protein).
- **[PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) and [PLCG1](../genes/PLCG1.md) are convergent RTK signaling hubs.** [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) Y546/Y584 phosphorylation associates with high [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md); PTPN11 Y62 phosphorylation tracks with high EGFR. [PLCG1](../genes/PLCG1.md) Y783 phosphorylation is significantly higher in EGFR-altered tumors (Wilcox FDR < 0.01) despite no significant change in PLCG1 protein (FDR = 0.11). [GAB1](../genes/GAB1.md) Y657/Y689 phosphorylation tracks high EGFR expression.
- **45 of 99 tumors harbor [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) structural variants, all with copy-number amplification** (high concordance SV-CNV). EGFR-mutated + SV tumors had elevated EGFR RNA, protein, and Y1172 phosphorylation. EGFR-altered tumors upregulate [PHLDA1](../genes/PHLDA1.md)/[PHLDA3](../genes/PHLDA3.md) at protein but not RNA, plus [SOX9](../genes/SOX9.md), [CTNND2](../genes/CTNND2.md), [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md), and [CDKN2C](../genes/CDKN2C.md) ([SOX9](../genes/SOX9.md) and GAB1 IHC-validated). [GAB1](../genes/GAB1.md) protein upregulated and [GRB2](../genes/GRB2.md) downregulated in EGFR-altered tumors.
- **Four immune subtypes (im1–im4) from xCell deconvolution + snRNA-seq validation.** im1 = high microglia/macrophage/lymphocyte; im2 = high macrophage, low lymphocyte; im3 = high neuron, IDH-mutant-overrepresented (Fisher p = 1.02e-5); im4 = uniformly cold (1.3% T cells, 6% TAM by snRNA-seq vs. 7% and 19% in im1–3). Mesenchymal subtype enriched in im1 + im2 (Fisher p = 1.65e-15). Methylation dm3 strongly associated with im1 (p = 1.26e-5). im1 TAMs upregulate M2 markers [CD163](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/?term=CD163) and [MRC1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/?term=MRC1). Subtypes confirmed in TCGA-GBM and validated by IHC for CD3, [CD68](../genes/CD68.md), CD163, PD-1, PD-L1.
- **im4 tumors contain morphologically large/giant cells.** A deep-learning model on H&E tiles revealed a distinct cell population in im4 (inflammatory-cell fraction ~5% vs. ~20% in im1–3).
- **Mesenchymal-tumor EMT signature originates partly in tumor cells.** snRNA-seq identified EMT-related genes ([CHI3L1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/?term=CHI3L1), [MET](../genes/MET.md), TNC, ITGA3, PDPN) upregulated in tumor cells of nmf2 samples; both tumor cells and infiltrating immune cells contribute to bulk mesenchymal signatures.
- **H2B N-terminal acetylation defines a classical-like, immune-low subset.** Lasso regression links [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md) and [EP300](../genes/EP300.md) protein/acetylation to H2B-K12/K13/K16/K17/K21 acetylation, and [BRD1](../genes/BRD1.md)/[BRD3](../genes/BRD3.md)/[BRD4](../genes/BRD4.md) to additional H2B sites. H2B acetylation is negatively correlated with TME enrichment, ferroptosis, mast cells, and ROS pathways, and positively correlated with spliceosome, nuclear-receptor, and SUMOylation pathways. [SUMO1](../genes/SUMO1.md) and [UBE2I](../genes/UBE2I.md) upregulated in high-H2B-acetylation tumors; [UBE2I](../genes/UBE2I.md) tracks [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md) (Pearson r = 0.413), a known SUMOylation target.
- **Lipidomics: 582 lipids in 75 tumors, >500 differential across subtypes (Wilcoxon FDR < 0.05).** Mesenchymal-like enriched for triacylglycerols (TGs) and depleted in phosphatidylcholines (PCs); proneural-like enriched for VLCFAs and long-chain PUFA glycerophospholipids. 22:6 lipids (likely DHA) and [ACSL6](../genes/ACSL6.md) co-regulated; [ACSL6](../genes/ACSL6.md) protein drastically diminished in tumor vs. normal, but DHA-containing PGs and TGs increased. Proneural-like ≈ normal brain for DHA metabolism; mesenchymal-like is most distant.
- **Ferroptosis pathway upregulated in mesenchymal-like [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md).** [ACSL4](../genes/ACSL4.md) and [ALOX5](../genes/ALOX5.md) protein upregulated only in mesenchymal-like, suggesting accumulation of oxidized PEs with PUFAs. DG levels correlate with [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md), [PLCG1](../genes/PLCG1.md), and PLCD3 protein.
- **Metabolomics in IDH-mutants:** 2-HG is the highest-abundance metabolite in IDH-mutants (median log2 FC = 3.62, FDR < 0.05). Glycolysis intermediates increased; serine and glutamate decreased. [GLUD1](../genes/GLUD1.md) upregulated in IDH-mutants (Wilcoxon p < 0.05); [GLUD1](../genes/GLUD1.md) protein anti-correlates with glutamate (Pearson r = -0.29, p < 0.01). Validation cohort confirmed GLUD1 elevation (p = 0.07).
- **Pathway-integrated views across RTK/RAS, PI3K/AKT, and p53/cell cycle:**
  - Classical tumors → amplified [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md); proneural and IDH-mutant tumors → amplified [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md); mesenchymal → upregulated [MET](../genes/MET.md) protein, downregulated [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) protein.
  - [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) loss in proneural/mesenchymal/classical activates [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md)/[AKT2](../genes/AKT2.md); [AKT3](../genes/AKT3.md) elevated in IDH-mutant and proneural (developmental adult-brain expression).
  - [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) amplification specific to mesenchymal; [MDM4](../genes/MDM4.md) amplification specific to proneural and classical; [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)/[CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md) differential between IDH-WT and IDH-mutant.
- **Druggable kinase-substrate networks.** [GSK3B](../genes/GSK3B.md) phosphorylation tracks with mTOR substrates ([RPTOR](../genes/RPTOR.md), [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md)) and Wnt substrates ([CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md), [APC](../genes/APC.md)). AKT1S1 connects strongly to [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md)/[AKT2](../genes/AKT2.md)/[AKT3](../genes/AKT3.md) kinases. [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) phosphorylates [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) S33 (N-terminal proteasomal-degradation site). [ABL1](../genes/ABL1.md) interacts with [HDAC2](../genes/HDAC2.md) deacetylase (~5% phospho outliers); [HDAC2](../genes/HDAC2.md) S422 phosphorylation tracks activity.
- **Drug-connectivity analysis ([LINCS L1000](../methods/lincs-l1000.md) RNA + P100 phospho).** For EGFR-altered tumors: phosphoproteomics predicts a broader kinase-inhibitor panel beyond canonical EGFR inhibitors; RNA alone suggested HDAC inhibitors. For [NF1](../genes/NF1.md)-altered tumors: both RNA and phospho signatures converge on MAPK inhibitors as top reversers. For [RB1](../genes/RB1.md)-altered and immune-subtype perturbations, RNA vs. phospho readouts agreed variably.

## Genes & alterations

- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) — 45/99 tumors with structural variants (all amplified); mutation, SV, and amplification co-occurred (p < 0.01). Elevated RNA, protein, Y1172 autophosphorylation; trans effects on PTPN11 Y62, PLCG1 Y783, GAB1 Y657/Y689, [MAP3K1](../genes/MAP3K1.md) S1408, RB1 S795/Y805. EGFR phosphorylates CTNNB1 S33.
- [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md) — Amplified preferentially in proneural and IDH-mutant tumors; elevated phospho at S1067/S1070 and trans-effect on PTPN11 Y546/Y584.
- [MET](../genes/MET.md) — Protein upregulated in mesenchymal tumors and identified as a mesenchymal-cell-state marker in snRNA-seq.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — Largely missense mutations in DNA-binding domain (hotspots + rare variants); protein elevated relative to TP53-WT without RNA change. Recurrent X126_splice yields in-frame 7-aa truncation in exon 5. Phosphorylation TP53 S315 and TP53BP1 S1099/S1106/S1109 correlate with TP53 abundance (Pearson r = 0.89 / 0.53). TP53-mutant tumors show upregulated DNA-repair phosphosites without protein-level upregulation; ATR/MAPK3/CDK2/CDK9 elevated; [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) decreased.
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — Altered in 12% of cohort; RB1 protein downregulated, [MCM2](../genes/MCM2.md)/[MCM4](../genes/MCM4.md)/[MCM6](../genes/MCM6.md) protein upregulated.
- [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) — Loss-of-function alterations track decreased [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) RNA/protein and increased [IRF8](../genes/IRF8.md) (microglial motility TF). Drug-connectivity analysis nominates MAPK inhibitors.
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — Mutations/deletions in proneural, mesenchymal, classical; concordant decrease in RNA/protein.
- [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) — 9 mutants with diminished ATRX RNA and protein (IHC-validated); mutually exclusive with [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter hotspot mutations; co-occurs with TP53 and [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) R132H. Complex partners' expression not affected by ATRX loss.
- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) — Promoter C228T/C250T mutations in 74% of primary GBMs with elevated TERT RNA.
- [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) — 6 R132H mutants + 1 R222C non-hotspot mutant; earlier disease onset (median 47 vs. 59 years, t-test p = 0.055). 2-HG highest-abundance metabolite (median log2 FC = 3.62, FDR < 0.05). [GLUD1](../genes/GLUD1.md) upregulated; glutamate decreased.
- [MGMT](../genes/MGMT.md) — Promoter hypermethylation in 38/90 tumors (42%); MGMT RNA (t-test p = 4.9e-11) and protein (p = 2.6e-6) significantly decreased.
- [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) (Shp2) — Y62 phosphorylation activated by EGFR; Y546/Y584 by PDGFRA. Protein level unchanged; activity regulated by phosphorylation. Forms complex with GAB1/GRB2 to activate RAS pathway. Identified as a convergent RTK signaling hub.
- [PLCG1](../genes/PLCG1.md) — Y783 phosphorylation activating; significantly elevated in EGFR-altered tumors (Wilcox FDR < 0.01) without change in PLCG1 protein (FDR = 0.11). DG levels correlate with PLCG1 protein.
- [GAB1](../genes/GAB1.md) — Y657/Y689 phosphorylation tracks EGFR; protein upregulated in EGFR-altered tumors (IHC-validated).
- [GRB2](../genes/GRB2.md) — Protein downregulated in EGFR-altered tumors.
- [SOX9](../genes/SOX9.md) — Protein upregulated in EGFR-altered tumors; IHC-validated in 5/6 tested tumors.
- [PHLDA1](../genes/PHLDA1.md) / [PHLDA3](../genes/PHLDA3.md) — Protein upregulated in EGFR-altered tumors without RNA change.
- [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md), [CDKN2C](../genes/CDKN2C.md), [CTNND2](../genes/CTNND2.md) — Upregulated in EGFR-altered tumors.
- [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) — β-catenin protein elevated despite decreased mRNA in EGFR-altered tumors; EGFR phosphorylates CTNNB1 S33 (proteasomal-degradation site).
- [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) — Amplification specific to mesenchymal subtype.
- [MDM4](../genes/MDM4.md) — Amplification specific to proneural and classical subtypes.
- [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md), [AKT2](../genes/AKT2.md) — Potentially activated downstream of PTEN loss; [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) protein correlates with DG levels.
- [AKT3](../genes/AKT3.md) — Higher expression in IDH-mutant and proneural tumors (developmental adult-brain pattern).
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) / [CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md) — Differential alteration patterns between IDH-WT and IDH-mutant tumors.
- [ATR](../genes/ATR.md), [MAPK3](../genes/MAPK3.md), [CDK2](../genes/CDK2.md), [CDK9](../genes/CDK9.md) — Protein/phospho elevated in TP53-mutant tumors.
- [MCM2](../genes/MCM2.md), [MCM4](../genes/MCM4.md), [MCM6](../genes/MCM6.md) — Protein upregulated in RB1-altered tumors.
- [IRF8](../genes/IRF8.md) — RNA + protein upregulated in NF1-altered tumors.
- [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md), [EP300](../genes/EP300.md) — Protein/acetylation levels strongly drive H2B-K12/K13/K16/K17/K21 acetylation (Lasso regression).
- [BRD1](../genes/BRD1.md), [BRD3](../genes/BRD3.md), [BRD4](../genes/BRD4.md) — Correlate with H2B-N-terminal acetylation sites; bind acetylated histones and mediate transcription.
- [SUMO1](../genes/SUMO1.md), [UBE2I](../genes/UBE2I.md) — Upregulated in tumors with high H2B acetylation; UBE2I tracks [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md) (Pearson r = 0.413).
- [ACSL4](../genes/ACSL4.md), [ALOX5](../genes/ALOX5.md) — Protein upregulated only in mesenchymal-like tumors; suggests ferroptosis-pathway activation and accumulation of oxidized PUFA-PEs.
- [ACSL6](../genes/ACSL6.md) — Protein severely diminished in tumor vs. normal brain; co-regulated with 22:6 (DHA)-containing lipids.
- [GLUD1](../genes/GLUD1.md) — Protein upregulated in IDH-mutant tumors (Wilcoxon p < 0.05; validation p = 0.07); anti-correlates with glutamate (Pearson r = -0.29, p < 0.01).
- [GSK3B](../genes/GSK3B.md) — Phosphorylation positively associated with mTOR substrates (RPTOR, TSC1) and Wnt substrates (CTNNB1, APC).
- [ABL1](../genes/ABL1.md), [HDAC2](../genes/HDAC2.md) — Inferred kinase-deacetylase interaction (~5% phospho outliers); HDAC2 S422 phosphorylation tracks activity.

## Clinical implications

- **PTPN11 / PLCG1 as RTK-pathway therapeutic hubs.** Convergent activation of [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) Y62 and [PLCG1](../genes/PLCG1.md) Y783 phosphosites downstream of both [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) and [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md) suggests a shared phospho-signaling bottleneck that could be targeted across multiple RTK-altered GBM subtypes.
- **EGFR-altered tumors: broader kinase-inhibitor opportunity.** Drug-connectivity analysis on [LINCS L1000](../methods/lincs-l1000.md) + P100 indicates that phosphoproteomic signatures of EGFR alteration are more strongly reversed by non-canonical kinase inhibitors (beyond approved EGFR inhibitors) than by HDAC inhibitors predicted from RNA alone.
- **NF1-altered tumors: MAPK-pathway inhibition.** Both transcriptomic and phosphoproteomic perturbation responses converge on MAPK inhibitors as top signature-reversing compounds for [NF1](../genes/NF1.md)-altered GBM.
- **TP53-mutant tumors: DNA-repair phosphosite vulnerability.** Upregulated DNA-repair phosphosites in [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)-mutant tumors (in the absence of total protein-level upregulation), combined with elevated [ATR](../genes/ATR.md), [CDK2](../genes/CDK2.md), and [CDK9](../genes/CDK9.md), nominate DNA-damage-response inhibitors (e.g., [ATR](../genes/ATR.md) inhibitors, CDK inhibitors) for TP53-mutant GBM.
- **RB1-altered tumors: MCM2/4/6 as biomarkers / vulnerabilities.** [RB1](../genes/RB1.md)-altered tumors (12% of cohort) show coordinated upregulation of [MCM2](../genes/MCM2.md), [MCM4](../genes/MCM4.md), [MCM6](../genes/MCM6.md).
- **Mesenchymal-like GBM: ferroptosis and metabolic vulnerabilities.** ALOX5/ACSL4 upregulation, oxidized PE accumulation, TG enrichment, and PC depletion mark a metabolic profile distinct from other subtypes — suggesting ferroptosis induction and lipid-metabolism targeting as hypotheses to test.
- **Classical-like / immune-low GBM: BRD- and CBP/p300-driven H2B acetylation.** Therapeutic relevance of BET inhibitors (against [BRD1](../genes/BRD1.md)/[BRD3](../genes/BRD3.md)/[BRD4](../genes/BRD4.md)) and [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md)/[EP300](../genes/EP300.md) inhibitors as candidates for this subtype.
- **IDH-mutant tumors: GLUD1 / glutamine-glutamate axis.** Significantly elevated [GLUD1](../genes/GLUD1.md) protein in IDH-mutants suggests GLUD1 inhibition or glutaminase blockade as metabolic strategies (hypothesis-generating; not tested clinically here).
- **Mixed-subtype tumors are a high-risk class.** 12 tumors with mixed multi-omics membership had significantly worse survival (log-rank p = 1.7e-4), arguing that single-modality (e.g., RNA-based) classification under-stratifies prognosis.
- **MGMT-promoter hypermethylation confirmed at protein level.** 42% of tumors with MGMT-promoter hypermethylation show concordant protein loss (p = 2.6e-6), reinforcing the rationale for [temozolomide](../drugs/temozolomide.md) response stratification by MGMT methylation (well-established prior literature).

## Limitations & open questions

- Cohort size (n = 99) is large for proteogenomic GBM but limits power for subset analyses: 6 IDH1-mutants, 12 mixed-subtype tumors, 5 im4 snRNA-seq samples, only 1 im3 snRNA-seq sample.
- snRNA-seq data on 18/99 tumors is unbalanced across immune subtypes (7 im1, 5 im2, 1 im3, 5 im4); im1 microglia/macrophage signal seen in bulk was not reproduced in im1 snRNA-seq, suggesting bulk signal is driven by TAM abundance and TAM-intrinsic expression, but is not orthogonally validated.
- Acetylated peptides recovered from the IMAC flow-through cannot detect dual-modified phospho+acetyl peptides; the authors acknowledge larger input would be needed to recover these.
- 10 GTEx normal frontal-cortex samples are unmatched (different patients, different processing) — lipidomic and metabolomic comparisons against "normal" are limited.
- Functional/mechanistic validation is largely IHC-based; kinase-substrate relationships (e.g., EGFR → CTNNB1 S33, [ABL1](../genes/ABL1.md) → HDAC2) are inferred from outlier/network analyses and not directly tested.
- Drug-connectivity predictions (LINCS L1000 / P100) are signature-based; no preclinical or clinical confirmation is reported in this paper.
- The CBTTC validation cohort (n = 39) is pediatric/AYA HGG (age 14–39), not adult GBM, so validation is partial.
- Treatment outcomes (e.g., [temozolomide](../drugs/temozolomide.md) response) were not analyzed in relation to the new multi-omics subtypes — the cohort is treatment-naive by design.
- IDH-mutant biology (n = 6) is under-powered for many of the comparative analyses (lipidomic, metabolomic, immune).
