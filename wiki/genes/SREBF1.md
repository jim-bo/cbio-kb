# SREBF1

## Overview

SREBF1 (sterol regulatory element binding transcription factor 1, SREBP1) is the master transcription factor of de novo lipogenesis. In [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md), SREBP1 is activated downstream of [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) and the PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway and transcriptionally drives the entire DNL program.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) — SREBP1 activation is the central transcriptional driver of de novo lipogenesis in HCC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Activated downstream of MYC amplification and AKT/mTOR pathway activation in HCC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Blocking SREBP1 nuclear translocation (via AKT/mTOR inhibition or statins) is proposed as a strategy to suppress DNL in HCC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Open questions

- Whether SREBP1 inhibition alone is sufficient to overcome TKI/ICI resistance in HCC is not established.
