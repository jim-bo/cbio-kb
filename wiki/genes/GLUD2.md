# GLUD2

## Overview

GLUD2 (Glutamate Dehydrogenase 2) encodes a mitochondrial enzyme that catalyzes the oxidative deamination of glutamate to alpha-ketoglutarate, linking amino acid catabolism to the TCA cycle. In the context of cancer predisposition, GLUD2 has been identified as carrying recurrent rare deleterious germline variants in lung cancers from never smokers.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Identified as carrying recurrent rare deleterious germline variants in lung cancers from never smokers (LCINS; 6 carriers in the 232-patient WGS cohort) [PMID:34493867](../papers/34493867.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) / LCINS**: Recurrent germline variant carrier status observed in the Sherlock-Lung cohort of 232 never-smoker lung cancer patients [PMID:34493867](../papers/34493867.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-identified with [CYP21A2](../genes/CYP21A2.md) as carrying recurrent rare deleterious germline variants in the LCINS cohort [PMID:34493867](../papers/34493867.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No therapeutic agents currently linked to GLUD2 in the corpus.

## Open questions

- Whether GLUD2 germline variants are causally driving lung carcinogenesis in never smokers or are coincidental findings requires additional functional validation and replication in independent LCINS cohorts.
