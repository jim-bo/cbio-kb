**PMID:** 25092538 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.eururo.2014.06.050 · **Journal:** European Urology (2015)

## TL;DR

Kim et al. performed capture-based, massively-parallel sequencing ([MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md)) on 109 high-grade urothelial carcinomas of the bladder ([BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md)) to identify prognostic genomic markers. In the 89-patient subset treated with radical cystectomy, recurrent [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) missense mutations were associated with improved recurrence-free and cancer-specific survival, while [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) alterations independently predicted worse outcomes. [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations (57% of tumors) tracked with locally advanced and node-positive disease but did not retain prognostic significance after adjustment for stage. Chromatin-modifying gene mutations were highly prevalent (83%) but not prognostic.

## Cohort & data

- 109 patients with high-grade urothelial carcinoma of the bladder ([BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md)); 89-patient subset analyzed for prognostic associations were those whose tumors were obtained at radical cystectomy.
- Tumor + matched germline blood sequenced using [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) capture-based [targeted DNA-seq](../methods/targeted-dna-seq.md).
- Mean coverage 579× across targeted exons; average of 10 mutations per tumor (range 0–46).
- Orthogonal sequencing of all coding exons of [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md), [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), and [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) (MLL2) confirmed 99.3% of MSK-IMPACT mutation calls.
- Dataset: [blca_mskcc_solit_2014](../datasets/blca_mskcc_solit_2014.md).

## Key findings

- Mutations were detected in 240 genes total, with 23 genes mutated in ≥5% of cases [PMID:25092538](../papers/25092538.md).
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation was the most common alteration (62/109; 57%); more common in extravesical vs. organ-confined disease (69% vs. 32%, p=0.005) and in lymph node-positive vs. node-negative disease (77% vs. 56%, p=0.025).
- Cell-cycle (S-phase entry) alterations present in 46% of tumors; chromatin-modifying gene mutations present in 83%.
- [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md) mutated in 45/109 (41%), 41/45 truncating.
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) altered in 28% (30/31 truncating); mutually exclusive with [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) alterations.
- [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md) (13%) and [EP300](../genes/EP300.md) (15%) showed mutually exclusive truncating mutation patterns.
- PI3K/AKT pathway alterations identified in 38/109 (35%); [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) recurrent missense mutations in 23/109 (21%), predominantly activating helical-domain mutations. Other PI3K/AKT pathway hits: [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) 6%, [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) 2%, [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md) 6%.
- In the 89-patient radical cystectomy cohort, recurrent [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) mutation was associated with improved recurrence-free survival (RFS; HR=0.35, p=0.014) and cancer-specific survival (CSS; HR=0.35, p=0.040).
- Analyzing PI3K/AKT pathway as a functionally related group: RFS HR=0.34 (p=0.006); CSS HR=0.29 (p=0.01).
- In multivariable analyses controlling for pT stage and nodal involvement: [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) mutation HR=0.39, p=0.032 for RFS; PI3K/AKT pathway alteration HR=0.37, p=0.017 for RFS.
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) alterations independently associated with worse outcomes in multivariable analyses: RFS HR=5.76 (p<0.001); CSS HR=2.94 (p=0.029).
- p53 pathway alterations: univariate RFS HR=1.92 (p=0.048) and CSS HR=2.94 (p=0.013), but not significant after adjustment for pT/pN.
- Chromatin-modifying gene mutations highly prevalent (83%) but not associated with outcomes.
- [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md) mutations not associated with outcomes in this cohort.
- Even among [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)-mutant patients, 5-year disease recurrence rate was 44%.

## Genes & alterations

- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — recurrent activating helical-domain missense mutations in 21% of tumors; associated with favorable RFS/CSS after radical cystectomy.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — mutation in 57% of tumors; enriched in extravesical and node-positive disease; worse univariate outcomes that did not survive stage adjustment.
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — alterations independently associated with worse RFS (HR=5.76) and CSS (HR=2.94).
- [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md) — truncating mutations in 41% of tumors; not associated with outcomes.
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) — truncating alterations in 28%; mutually exclusive with [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md).
- [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md) and [EP300](../genes/EP300.md) — mutually exclusive truncating mutations (13% and 15%).
- [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md) — mutations present, but no outcome association in this cohort (contradicts some prior reports, agrees with others).
- [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) (MLL2) — included as one of four CMGs validated by orthogonal sequencing (99.3% concordance).
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md), [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md) — recurrent mutations in PI3K/AKT pathway (6%, 2%, 6% respectively).

## Clinical implications

- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) mutation / PI3K/AKT pathway alteration status may identify [BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md) patients with favorable post-cystectomy outcomes, providing rationale for adjuvant trials of selective PI3-kinase inhibitors in this genetically defined subset (note: 44% 5-year recurrence rate even in PIK3CA-mutant patients).
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) alterations identify a high-risk genomic subgroup; authors propose rationale for studies of selective [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) inhibitors in this group.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation status reflects locally advanced/node-positive disease but does not provide independent prognostic information beyond pT/pN stage.
- Genomic profiling may aid identification of UCB patients at highest risk following radical cystectomy.

## Limitations & open questions

- Sample size limited given the genomic heterogeneity of bladder cancer.
- Single-site sampling per tumor may underestimate intratumoral genetic heterogeneity.
- Targeted capture panel does not detect less frequently mutated genes, most structural alterations, epigenetic changes, or expression differences.
- Findings pending validation in a larger and more comprehensive cohort.
- Authors note that prior literature on [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md) prognostic significance in UCB is contradictory ([PMID:24121792](../papers/24121792.md) reported worse survival with [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md) alterations; this study found no association).
- Why are chromatin-modifying gene mutations near-universal in UCB (83%) yet not prognostic? Authors speculate they may be necessary early events in pathogenesis.
