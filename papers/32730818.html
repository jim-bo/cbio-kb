Specific Mutations in APC, but Not Alterations in DNA Damage Response, Associate With Outcomes of Patients With Metastatic Colorectal Cancer

Authors

Sebastian Mondaca

Henry Walch

Subhiksha Nandakumar

Walid K. Chatila

Nikolaus Schultz

Rona Yaeger

Doi

10.1053/j.gastro.2020.07.041

PMID: 32730818 · DOI: 10.1053/j.gastro.2020.07.041 · Journal: Gastroenterology (2020)

TL;DR

Mondaca et al. analyzed 430 microsatellite-stable (MSS) unresectable metastatic colorectal cancer (mCRC) patients treated at Memorial Sloan Kettering with first-line chemotherapy whose tumors were genomically profiled by MSK-IMPACT. They found that APC mutation site (N-terminal vs C-terminal, split at amino acid 1400) was prognostic — N-terminal APC mutations were associated with longer overall and progression-free survival, while C-terminal APC mutations co-occurred more frequently with activating KRAS, BRAF, PIK3CA, and PTEN alterations. In contrast, DNA damage response (DDR) pathway alterations were not associated with survival or with response to oxaliplatin-containing chemotherapy, arguing against PARP-inhibitor-type vulnerabilities in mCRC.

Cohort & data

  • n=430 patients with MSS unresectable mCRC treated at Memorial Sloan Kettering with first-line chemotherapy between 2014 and 2017 (PMID:32730818).
  • Cancer type: metastatic colorectal adenocarcinoma (includes colon and rectal primaries).
  • Dataset: crc_apc_impact_2020.
  • Tumor genomic profiling: MSK-IMPACT targeted sequencing panel.
  • Copy-number / LOH analysis: FACETS allele-specific copy number algorithm; large state transitions (LST) computed as ≥10 Mb breaks per chromosome arm.
  • Survival analysis: Kaplan-Meier for PFS/OS from first-line treatment start; multivariate adjustment via Cox proportional hazards.

Key findings

  • Among MSS mCRC (n=430), only APC and BRAF alteration frequencies significantly varied by duration of response and were associated with PFS and OS (PMID:32730818).
  • Multivariate analysis: APC mutation was associated with longer PFS (HR 0.68, p<0.001) and OS (HR 0.56, p<0.001) versus tumors with no Wnt pathway alteration or with alterations in other Wnt pathway genes.
  • APC truncating mutations were split at amino acid 1400 into N-terminal (containing up to two 20R β-catenin-binding repeats) and C-terminal groups. Survival differences first appeared at aa 600 and became significantly shorter in the C-terminal group at aa 1400.
  • Patients with N-terminal-side APC mutations had significantly longer OS and PFS than those with C-terminal-side or no APC alteration. The difference persisted after adjustment for tumor sidedness.
  • Co-mutation patterns by APC side:
    • KRAS alterations: 46% in N-terminal vs 72% in C-terminal APC mutant cases (p<0.001, q<0.01).
    • BRAF alterations: 2% in N-terminal vs 13% in C-terminal APC mutant cases (p<0.001, q<0.01).
    • PIK3CA alterations: 11% in N-terminal vs 28% in C-terminal APC mutant cases (p<0.01, q<0.1).
    • PTEN alterations: 3% in N-terminal vs 12% in C-terminal APC mutant cases (p<0.01, q<0.1).
    • TP53 mutations were more frequent in tumors with N-terminal-side APC mutations.
  • ~75% of C-terminal-side APC tumors had concurrent activating mitogenic alterations vs ~50% of N-terminal-side APC tumors.
  • N-terminal APC mutations were more common in left-sided colon/rectal primaries; C-terminal APC mutations were more common in right-sided colon tumors.
  • CTNNB1 and RNF43 alterations commonly co-occurred with RAS pathway alterations (63% with CTNNB1, 71% with RNF43) and identified mCRC with poor prognosis.
  • DDR pathway alterations were not associated with survival across the cohort and were not associated with PFS in patients receiving oxaliplatin-containing chemotherapy (HR 1.1, 95% CI 0.7–1.6, p=0.74).
  • DDR alterations were enriched in MSI-H mCRC; median LST scores did not differ between DDR-altered (somatic or germline) and DDR-wildtype tumors. No patient had an LST score >15 (the proposed HRD cutoff).

Genes & alterations

  • APC — truncating mutations split at aa 1400 into N-terminal (longer OS/PFS, fewer concurrent mitogenic hits) and C-terminal (shorter OS/PFS, enriched RAS/PI3K co-mutation) groups. Identified as a clinically relevant prognostic marker in mCRC.
  • BRAF — alteration frequency varied by duration of response and was prognostic in MSS mCRC; enriched in C-terminal APC mutant tumors (13% vs 2%).
  • KRAS — strongly enriched in C-terminal APC mutant tumors (72% vs 46%); part of co-occurring RAS pathway activation pattern.
  • TP53 — more frequent in N-terminal APC mutant tumors.
  • PIK3CA — enriched in C-terminal APC mutant tumors (28% vs 11%).
  • PTEN — enriched in C-terminal APC mutant tumors (12% vs 3%).
  • CTNNB1 — highly activating Wnt alteration; co-occurred with RAS pathway alterations in 63% of cases and marked poor-prognosis mCRC.
  • RNF43 — Wnt ligand-level alteration; co-occurred with RAS pathway alterations in 71% of cases; poor-prognosis subgroup.
  • AMER1 — included as a Wnt pathway gene in the multivariate prognostic model.
  • BRCA1 / BRCA2 — referenced as DDR alterations; LST analysis showed no enrichment for homologous recombination deficiency signatures in this cohort.

Clinical implications

  • Prognosis: N-terminal-side APC truncating mutations (5’ of aa 1400) identify a less aggressive subset of MSS mCRC with longer OS and PFS, independent of tumor sidedness. C-terminal APC mutations mark a more aggressive subset enriched for concurrent mitogenic activation.
  • Biomarker: APC mutation site, not merely APC mutation status, should be considered when stratifying mCRC. CTNNB1 and RNF43 alterations identify additional poor-prognosis subgroups.
  • Therapy: DDR pathway alterations in mCRC (including germline) do not appear to indicate platinum sensitivity or HRD; the authors argue against pursuing PARP inhibitor strategies in this disease, consistent with prior negative PARP inhibitor trials in mCRC. The paper does not advocate for olaparib-class therapy in unselected mCRC.
  • Mechanism: “Just-right” Wnt activation hypothesis — N-terminal APC mutants retain enough β-catenin regulation that additional mitogenic hits are not required, while distal/C-terminal APC mutants (weaker Wnt activators) experience selective pressure for additional RAS/PI3K activation.

Limitations & open questions

  • Authors acknowledge the small sample size of DDR-altered patients and the possibility of subclonal DDR mutations that may have been missed.
  • Single-institution cohort (MSK), retrospective design; no external validation cohort presented.
  • LST-based HRD assessment may miss other markers of homologous recombination deficiency.
  • The mechanistic claim that N-terminal APC mutants do not need concurrent mitogenic alterations is biologically inferred from co-mutation frequencies, not functionally validated in this study.
  • It remains open whether the aa 1400 cutpoint generalizes to non-MSS or non-MSK cohorts.
