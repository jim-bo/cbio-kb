# MAP3K4

## Overview

MAP3K4 (mitogen-activated protein kinase kinase kinase 4) encodes a serine/threonine kinase in the MAPKKK tier of the MAPK signaling cascade. It activates the JNK and p38 MAPK pathways and has roles in stress response, apoptosis, and development. In cancer, MAP3K4 is implicated as a downstream effector of [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md), and [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) mutations.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- MAP3K4 is identified as a downstream effector of TP53, CTNNB1, and ARID1A mutations in trans in endometrial cancer proteogenomics (n=95 tumors); grouped with [PLK1](../genes/PLK1.md), [CDK1](../genes/CDK1.md), [CHEK1](../genes/CHEK1.md), [XPO1](../genes/XPO1.md), [TPX2](../genes/TPX2.md), and [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) as trans-regulated targets [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[UCEC](../cancer_types/UCEC.md)** (Endometrial carcinoma): identified as a trans-effector of major EC drivers in multi-omic proteogenomics [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-regulated with PLK1, CDK1, CHEK1, XPO1, TPX2, and KMT2D downstream of TP53/CTNNB1/ARID1A mutations [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting of MAP3K4 reported in this corpus; its downstream position relative to major EC drivers suggests potential as a pathway node in multi-omic drug-target discovery.

## Open questions

- Whether MAP3K4 regulation is a direct transcriptional/proteomic response to TP53/CTNNB1/ARID1A mutations or an indirect secondary effect has not been functionally validated [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md).
