# SMOX

## Overview

SMOX (spermine oxidase) encodes a flavoprotein oxidase that catalyzes the oxidation of spermine to spermidine, generating hydrogen peroxide as a byproduct. Dysregulation of SMOX can contribute to oxidative stress in cancer cells. In schwannomatosis, a novel deleterious SMOX variant was identified as a non-recurrent somatic event.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Novel deleterious SNV validated by Sanger sequencing in a single schwannomatosis case; classified as non-recurrent alongside MYPN and [CAV3](../genes/CAV3.md) variants [PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Schwannomatosis: SMOX identified as a non-recurrent somatic deleterious variant in the epigenomic/genomic landscape study [PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs in tumors with global epigenetic dysregulation ([TET1](../genes/TET1.md), [TET2](../genes/TET2.md) upregulation; NF2/SMARCB1 loss) in schwannomatosis [PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No established direct therapeutic targeting; context of oxidative stress biology may be relevant to schwannomatosis pathogenesis.

## Open questions

- Whether SMOX loss contributes to the polyamine metabolism dysregulation observed in schwannomatosis and if this is a driver or passenger event.
