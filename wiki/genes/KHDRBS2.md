# KHDRBS2

## Overview

KHDRBS2 (KH RNA Binding Domain Containing, Signal Transduction Associated 2) is an RNA-binding protein of the STAR (Signal Transduction and Activation of RNA) family. It was identified as a candidate driver in regional metastatic cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma (CSCC) by a three-tool consensus driver analysis (OncodriveFML, MutSigCV, dNdScv), with high alteration frequency among the 25 WGS samples.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Altered in 48% of 25 regional metastatic [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md) samples (WGS); called as a candidate coding driver by ≥2 of three tools (OncodriveFML, MutSigCV, dNdScv) [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md).
- Also enriched in metastatic vs primary CSCC on cBioPortal cross-cohort comparison (92 metastatic + 88 primary CSCC) [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md):** 48% alteration rate in regional lymph node metastases (WGS); candidate coding driver in the [cscc_ranson_2022](../datasets/cscc_ranson_2022.md) metastatic CSCC cohort [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- No specific co-occurrence or mutual exclusivity patterns reported in this corpus beyond the general UV-signature mutation landscape of metastatic CSCC.

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic implications reported in this corpus.

## Open questions

- Mechanism by which KHDRBS2 alterations contribute to CSCC metastasis is not established; the high frequency (48%) in metastatic disease relative to primary CSCC suggests a role in progression rather than initiation.
