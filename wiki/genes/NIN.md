# NIN

## Overview

NIN (Ninein) encodes a centrosomal protein involved in microtubule anchoring. It was identified as a significantly mutated gene (MutSigCV) in pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma; its functional role as a cancer driver versus passenger in pancreatic cancer remains to be established.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- In pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma ([PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md)), NIN was among 24 significantly mutated genes (MutSigCV q<0.1) identified in a 109-case whole-exome sequencing study; mutated in ~5% of cases. Functional significance as a PDA driver is unvalidated. [PMID:25855536](../papers/25855536.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md): recurrently mutated at ~5% frequency; nominated as a candidate driver pending functional validation. [PMID:25855536](../papers/25855536.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- No specific co-occurrence or mutual exclusivity patterns reported in the corpus.

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic relevance established in the corpus.

## Open questions

- Whether NIN mutations are oncogenic drivers or passengers in PDA awaits orthogonal functional validation. [PMID:25855536](../papers/25855536.md)
