# FAS

## Overview

FAS (also known as CD95 or APO-1) is a death-domain-containing cell-surface receptor belonging to the [TNF](../genes/TNF.md) receptor superfamily that mediates apoptosis upon binding of FAS ligand (FASL). In cancer, FAS loss-of-function mutations or copy number deletions allow tumor cells to evade cytotoxic T-lymphocyte-mediated killing. In diffuse large B-cell lymphoma, FAS alterations are a mechanism of immune escape enriched in specific genetic subtypes.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- FAS (apoptosis mediator) mutations occur in C1 ABC-DLBCL (extrafollicular/marginal-zone-like subtype) alongside [BCL10](../genes/BCL10.md) and [TNFAIP3](../genes/TNFAIP3.md) NF-kB pathway mutations; FAS loss contributes to immune escape in this subtype [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [DLBCLNOS](../cancer_types/DLBCLNOS.md): FAS mutations enriched in C1 ABC-DLBCL, a subtype with marginal-zone-like biology and favorable outcome on R-CHOP; FAS alterations are part of the 74% immune-escape frequency observed across all DLBCLs [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- FAS mutations co-occur with BCL10 and TNFAIP3 NF-kB pathway mutations in C1 ABC-DLBCL; co-occur with [CD70](../genes/CD70.md) and [B2M](../genes/B2M.md) immune-escape alterations in the broader 74% immune-escape landscape [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- FAS loss in C1 DLBCL is proposed as a rationale for immune-checkpoint-targeted approaches, particularly given the co-occurrence of CD274/PD-L1 and PDCD1LG2/PD-L2 SVs in C1 [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Open questions

- Whether FAS mutations/deletions in DLBCL are clonal or subclonal events, and whether they are required for C1 subtype maintenance, has not been fully characterized in this cohort.
