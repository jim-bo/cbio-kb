# PLCG1

## Overview

PLCG1 (phospholipase C gamma 1) encodes a signaling enzyme involved in receptor tyrosine kinase pathways. PLCG1 mutations have been identified in radiation-associated angiosarcoma, where they may contribute to aberrant vascular signaling.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- PLCG1 mutations were identified in RT-AS in a whole-genome sequencing study of radiation-associated secondary malignancies; PLCG1 mutations were found alongside [PTPRB](../genes/PTPRB.md) mutations in RT-AS driver landscapes [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [ANGS](../cancer_types/ANGS.md) -- PLCG1 mutations reported in RT-associated angiosarcoma [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- PLCG1 mutations co-occur with [PTPRB](../genes/PTPRB.md) mutations in RT-AS [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No PLCG1-directed therapy is reported in this corpus [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).

## Open questions

- The prevalence and functional significance of PLCG1 mutations in RT-AS vs sporadic AS has not been fully characterized in the MSK-IMPACT cohort [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).
