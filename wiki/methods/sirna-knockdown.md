# siRNA Knockdown

## Overview

siRNA (small interfering RNA) knockdown is an RNA interference (RNAi) technique used to transiently silence gene expression in cell culture. Double-stranded siRNA oligonucleotides are transfected into cells, where they are loaded into the RISC complex and guide sequence-specific degradation of the target mRNA, reducing protein expression by 70–95% for several days post-transfection.

## Used by

- Used to silence [ZFP36L1](../genes/ZFP36L1.md) in the TCCSUP bladder cancer cell line; knockdown caused loss of E-cadherin, EMT-consistent spindle-shaped morphology, and significantly increased migration on transwell assay with no effect on proliferation or apoptosis, supporting ZFP36L1 as a tumor suppressor driving invasion in urothelial carcinoma [PMID:33397444](../papers/33397444.md)

## Notes

- Corpus-grown slug; not in cBioPortal gene-panel ontology.
- Transient (typically 48–96 h); for stable long-term silencing, shRNA or CRISPR/Cas9 knockout are preferred.
- Knockdown efficiency must be confirmed by Western blot or RT-qPCR.
- Off-target effects are a limitation; rescue experiments or multiple siRNA sequences are used for validation.
