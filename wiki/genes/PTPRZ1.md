# PTPRZ1

## Overview

PTPRZ1 (Protein Tyrosine Phosphatase Receptor Type Z1) is a receptor-type protein tyrosine phosphatase expressed predominantly in the brain. In glioblastoma, PTPRZ1 is a fusion partner for [MET](../genes/MET.md), generating the PTPRZ1-MET fusion kinase that constitutively activates MET signalling. The PTPRZ1-MET fusion has been identified as a potentially targetable alteration in [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) PDX models.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- PTPRZ1-MET fusion detected in GBM PDX models; flagged as a potentially targetable event amenable to MET inhibitor testing; identified alongside the CHTOP-NTRK1 fusion in a panel of 84 glioblastoma PDX [PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md): PTPRZ1-MET fusion in glioblastoma PDX, potentially targetable with MET inhibitors [PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with CHTOP-NTRK1 fusion in the same GBM PDX panel [PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- PTPRZ1-MET fusion provides a model for testing MET inhibitors in glioblastoma [PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)

## Open questions

- Frequency of PTPRZ1-MET fusion in primary patient GBM not well established; PDX panel may not fully represent patient prevalence.
