# MSH5

## Overview

MSH5 encodes a MutS homolog involved in mismatch repair (MMR) and meiotic recombination. Germline or somatic mutations in MSH5 contribute to constitutional MMR deficiency (Lynch syndrome spectrum), which can result in hypermutated tumors. In pediatric cancers, MSH5 co-mutation with other MMR genes defines an ultrahypermutated phenotype.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Co-occurring mutation with [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md) (G721*), [PMS1](../genes/PMS1.md) (Q316*), and [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) in IC-1621GBM (Lynch/constitutional MMR-deficiency) PDX model in the PPTC panel; this model was ultrahypermutated at 124 Mut/Mb across 261 pediatric cancer PDX models spanning 37 histotypes [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[GB](../cancer_types/GB.md)** — Constitutional MMR-deficiency [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) PDX model (IC-1621GBM) in the PPTC panel carried MSH5 mutation alongside MSH2, PMS1, and POLE mutations resulting in ultrahypermutation (124 Mut/Mb) [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with MSH2 (G721*), PMS1 (Q316*), and POLE mutations in a constitutional MMR-deficiency pediatric GBM model [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Ultrahypermutated tumors with constitutional MMR deficiency (including MSH5 co-mutation) represent candidates for checkpoint immunotherapy; the PPTC PDX resource enables preclinical investigation [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md).

## Open questions

- MSH5's specific contribution to the ultrahypermutated phenotype vs. MSH2/PMS1/POLE is not resolved; functional studies are needed to dissect individual gene roles.
