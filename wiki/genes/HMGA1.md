# HMGA1

## Overview

HMGA1 (High Mobility Group AT-Hook 1) encodes an architectural chromatin-binding protein that modulates transcription by altering chromatin structure. It is a well-established oncogene in multiple tumor types and has now been identified as a dominant driver in endometrial polyps via non-fusion enhancer hijacking rather than classic gene fusion mechanisms. HMGA1 rearrangements lead to stromal overexpression of the protein and downstream upregulation of [PLAG1](../genes/PLAG1.md) and other transcriptional targets, repositioning endometrial polyps as stromal-driven neoplasms.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Chromosomal rearrangements upstream (12/14 rearrangements), in the 3'UTR (2/14), or downstream (1/14) of HMGA1 in 14/23 endometrial polyps (WGS discovery cohort); mean rearrangement VAF 0.24 (range 0.06–0.43); recurrent partners include the 7p15.2 enhancer ENSR00001272277, [LRMDA](../genes/LRMDA.md) (10q22.3), [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md) (14q24.1), and [TRAF3IP2](../genes/TRAF3IP2.md) (6q21); one sample also carried a somatic missense c.268C>G p.(Leu90Val); result is HMGA1 mRNA and protein overexpression in the stromal compartment [PMID:28445112](../papers/28445112.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[UCEC](../cancer_types/UCEC.md) (Endometrial Carcinoma / Endometrial Polyps):** HMGA1 rearrangements are the most common alteration in endometrial polyps (61%, 14/23 polyps), establishing stromal enhancer hijacking as the dominant initiating event; co-occurring low-VAF mutations in [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) support a subset of polyps as precursor lesions for endometrial carcinoma [PMID:28445112](../papers/28445112.md)
- **[ULM](../cancer_types/ULM.md) (Uterine Leiomyoma):** HMGA1 alterations parallel those found in uterine leiomyoma biology, suggesting shared HMGA-pathway mechanisms across gynecological stromal neoplasms [PMID:28445112](../papers/28445112.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- HMGA1 and [HMGA2](../genes/HMGA2.md) rearrangements share the same partner repertoire and typically occur in distinct polyps (HMGA2-rearranged in 4/23 polyps); downstream target [PLAG1](../genes/PLAG1.md) is transcriptionally upregulated (log2FC 3.22) in HMGA1-rearranged polyps without DNA-level PLAG1 alterations [PMID:28445112](../papers/28445112.md)
- [ZMAT3](../genes/ZMAT3.md) is the top differentially upregulated gene in HMGA-aberrant polyps [PMID:28445112](../papers/28445112.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No specific drug targeting HMGA1 is proposed for current clinical use; authors suggest HMGA-targeted therapy (e.g., [HMGA2](../genes/HMGA2.md) gene silencing explored in ovarian carcinoma) could in principle be repurposed across HMGA-driven gynecological lesions [PMID:28445112](../papers/28445112.md)

## Open questions

- Some samples showed strong HMGA1 staining without a WGS-detected rearrangement and vice versa, possibly due to cellular composition, FFPE artifact, or short-read SV sensitivity limits [PMID:28445112](../papers/28445112.md)
- The 7p15.2 enhancer ENSR00001272277 has no known target gene in this tissue; the proposed enhancer-hijacking mechanism is plausible but not yet functionally proven [PMID:28445112](../papers/28445112.md)
