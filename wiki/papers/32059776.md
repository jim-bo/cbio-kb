**PMID:** 32059776 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.cell.2020.01.026 · **Journal:** Cell (2020)

## TL;DR

Dou et al. report the CPTAC proteogenomic characterization of 95 prospectively collected endometrial carcinomas (83 endometrioid, 12 serous) integrating whole-exome, whole-genome, total and miRNA RNA-seq, DNA methylation, global proteomics, phosphoproteomics, and acetylproteomics. Tumors were classified into the four TCGA-defined genomic subtypes ([POLE](../genes/POLE.md), MSI, CNV-low, CNV-high). The study uncovers (i) mutation-type-specific cis/trans effects of [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) on the proteome and phosphoproteome, (ii) coupling between Wnt signaling and histone acetylation through BRD3/SIRT1, (iii) a [QKI](../genes/QKI.md)/circRNA/[ESRP2](../genes/ESRP2.md) regulatory axis controlling EMT, and (iv) APM (antigen processing and presentation machinery) deficiency — including microsatellite-indel-derived [JAK1](../genes/JAK1.md) truncation mutations — as a potential immune-evasion mechanism that complements TMB for immunotherapy patient selection in EC.

## Cohort & data

- 95 prospectively collected endometrial carcinoma (EC) tumors and 49 normal tissue samples from the CPTAC project, biospecimens collected April 2016–May 2017.
- Histology: 83 endometrioid and 12 serous tumors. Genomic subtype distribution: 7 [POLE](../genes/POLE.md), 25 MSI, 43 CNV-low (endometrioid-like), 20 CNV-high (serous-like).
- Cancer types: [UCEC](../cancer_types/UCEC.md) (overall), with [UEC](../cancer_types/UEC.md) endometrioid and [USC](../cancer_types/USC.md) serous histotypes. The CNV-high subtype contains all serous samples plus the most aggressive endometrioid tumors.
- Dataset / cohort: [ucec_cptac_2020](../datasets/ucec_cptac_2020.md).
- Assays: [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) (Illumina HiSeq4000, paired 76-cycle, min 150× on-target, hg38), PCR-free [whole-genome-seq](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) (HiSeqX, min 15× coverage, 151 bp paired-end), total and miRNA [rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) ([mirna-seq](../methods/mirna-seq.md)), Illumina Infinium MethylationEPIC BeadChip ([epic-methylation-array](../methods/epic-methylation-array.md), >850,000 CpG sites), isobaric 10-plex TMT-based global [tmt-proteomics](../methods/tmt-proteomics.md) and [tmt-phosphoproteomics](../methods/tmt-phosphoproteomics.md) (IMAC enrichment) plus genome-wide acetylproteomics (PTMScan Acetyl-Lysine Motif IAP), run at the PNNL Proteomic Characterization Center under a 1% protein-level FDR cutoff.
- Data availability: Genomic Data Commons (GDC), CPTAC Data Portal, the `cptac` Python package, and LinkedOmics.

## Key findings

- **Mutation burden by subtype.** Roughly 61% of all somatic mutations were found in the 7 [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) tumors (n = 32,340; 32,188 point mutations and 152 indels), 5 of which harbored the recurrent hotspot mutation P286R in the catalytic subunit of DNA polymerase epsilon. MSI tumors carried 88% of all indels (n = 5,061).
- **Significantly mutated genes in MSI tumors.** Microsatellite-indel focused analysis revealed higher mutation rates than previously reported for [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) (92%), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) (76%), and [RPL22](../genes/RPL22.md) (64%) — all FDR = 0. Three putative SMGs were newly identified in the MSI subtype: [INPPL1](../genes/INPPL1.md) (56%, FDR = 0), [KMT2B](../genes/KMT2B.md) (56%, FDR = 0.001), and [JAK1](../genes/JAK1.md) (44%, FDR = 6.4e-07).
- **CNV landscape.** Genes frequently altered by copy number variation — [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md), [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md), [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md), and [IGF1R](../genes/IGF1R.md) — overlapped substantially with TCGA findings.
- **Driver-mutation proteomic impact.** Across 18 SMGs studied, 7 had cis and 6 had trans effects on protein/phosphoprotein levels (FDR < 0.05, Wilcoxon rank-sum); 143 trans associations spanning 49 interacting proteins were detected. The largest trans signatures were driven by [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), and [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) mutations.
- **[CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) hotspot mutations (23 tumors).** All but two were CNV-low or MSI; none were higher than grade 2 and only three were stage III. HotSpot3D analysis showed exon 3 mutations (centered on S33) clustered in 3D with phosphosites Y30, S33, T40 and recurrent mutations at D32 and G34. Tumors with hotspot mutations had higher Wnt-pathway activity than [WT](../cancer_types/WT.md) MSI/CNV-low counterparts (p = 2.5e-3, Student's t test) and concurrent upregulation of [APC](../genes/APC.md) and [AXIN1](../genes/AXIN1.md) protein levels.
- **APC/CTNNB1 mutual exclusivity.** 11 tumors lacking CTNNB1 hotspot mutations but carrying [APC](../genes/APC.md) mutations showed reduced [APC](../genes/APC.md) and elevated β-catenin protein, supporting two mutually exclusive routes to β-catenin overexpression in EC.
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation-type-specific effects (23 tumors, including all 12 serous).** 11 tumors harbored DNA-binding-domain missense hotspots (including R248 and R273), leading to elevated p53 protein and downstream dysregulation of [AURKA](../genes/AURKA.md) and XRN2 (p = 7.8e-06, t test for XRN2). 7 truncating-mutation tumors did not alter p53 levels (p = 0.082) but showed increased PLK1-T210 phosphorylation and enrichment of mitotic-marker proteins. A third subset including [CDK1](../genes/CDK1.md), [XPO1](../genes/XPO1.md), and [TPX2](../genes/TPX2.md) was dysregulated regardless of TP53 mutation type. These mutation-type-specific effects were not observed in CPTAC ovarian or colon cancer data.
- **Wnt–acetylation coupling.** Upregulation of H2B N-terminal acetylation at K16, K20, K24 in [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) hotspot mutants, and upregulation of H3K27 and H3K36 acetylation in [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md)- and [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)-mutant samples. [BRD3](../genes/BRD3.md) and [SIRT1](../genes/SIRT1.md) protein levels were increased in CTNNB1 hotspot mutants; [BRD3](../genes/BRD3.md) correlated positively with multiple H2B N-terminal acetyl sites, while [SIRT1](../genes/SIRT1.md), [SIRT3](../genes/SIRT3.md), and [BRD4](../genes/BRD4.md) correlated negatively with H3K27 and H3K36 acetylation. 322 tumor-vs-normal acetylation sites differed (216 up, 106 down).
- **DNA methylation.** MSI tumors had elevated genome-wide CpG island methylation; methylation-silenced genes include [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md) and several HOX family members. HOX protein levels were anticorrelated with H2AX phosphorylation, linking methylation to dampened DSB repair.
- **SCNAs and the p53 pathway.** 14% of SCNAs had cis effects on transcript/protein (FDR < 0.01). 1q amplification — present in both MSI and MSS tumors — anticorrelated with p53 pathway activity (p < 0.01, Pearson). [MDM4](../genes/MDM4.md) mRNA increased with 1q copy number (p < 0.01, Spearman); [SETDB1](../genes/SETDB1.md), SDE2, [PARP1](../genes/PARP1.md), and [GATAD2B](../genes/GATAD2B.md) were the top 1q histone-modifier candidates inversely correlated with p53 pathway target proteins [CDKN1A](../genes/CDKN1A.md) and [TNFRSF10B](../genes/TNFRSF10B.md).
- **circRNA/EMT regulator.** 234 recurrent circRNAs identified. [QKI](../genes/QKI.md) protein correlated positively with 35 circRNAs and with EMT score and the EMT activators [ZEB1](../genes/ZEB1.md) and [ZEB2](../genes/ZEB2.md); [ESRP2](../genes/ESRP2.md) correlated negatively with 20 circRNAs and negatively with [QKI](../genes/QKI.md). QKI-correlated circRNAs harbored predicted binding sites for 36 miRNAs (p < 0.02), including miR-200c, miR-221, miR-130a, miR-130b, and miR-183.
- **MSI vs MSS proteomics.** [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md) and [EPM2AIP1](../genes/EPM2AIP1.md) were downregulated in MSI at mRNA and protein levels (shared promoter methylation); [PMS1](../genes/PMS1.md) and [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md) were downregulated only at the protein level. [RPL22L1](../genes/RPL22L1.md) was upregulated in MSI tumors (highest in RPL22-mutant samples; p = 0.002, rank-sum) — consistent with the previously described [RPL22](../genes/RPL22.md)/RPL22L1 synthetic-lethal relationship.
- **Serous vs endometrioid.** Serous tumors had increased [TP53BP1](../genes/TP53BP1.md)-S1763 and [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md)-S163 phosphorylation; both phosphosites correlated with [PLK1](../genes/PLK1.md) protein, which in turn correlated with G2M checkpoint score (R = 0.67, p = 7.9e-14).
- **Tumor immune landscape.** Protein-level evidence for putative neoantigens in 49.3% of samples; [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) > MSI in neoantigen burden. 8 known cancer/testis (CT) antigens — including [IGF2BP3](../genes/IGF2BP3.md), [ATAD2](../genes/ATAD2.md), [PBK](../genes/PBK.md) — observed in >10% of tumors (>3-fold tumor vs normal). 59% of tumors had ≥1 CT antigen; 78% had either a neoantigen or a high-expression CT antigen.
- **APM-deficient TMB-high tumors.** TMB and antigen processing/presentation machinery (APM) efficiency varied independently. TMB-H/APM-L tumors had lower CD8+ T-cell infiltration than TMB-H/APM-H, lower IFNγ and IFNα activity, and were enriched for [JAK1](../genes/JAK1.md) truncation mutations and [STAT1](../genes/STAT1.md) truncation mutations (POLE samples). TMB-H/APM-L tumors without JAK1/STAT1 mutations had lower [TAP1](../genes/TAP1.md), [TAP2](../genes/TAP2.md), and TAP-binding protein levels, plus reduced HLA class I.

## Genes & alterations

- [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) — recurrent catalytic-domain hotspot P286R in 5/7 POLE-subtype tumors; defines the ultramutated subtype (~61% of all somatic mutations).
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — mutations in 23 tumors including all serous carcinomas; missense DNA-binding-domain hotspots (R248, R273) elevate p53 protein and dysregulate [AURKA](../genes/AURKA.md), XRN2; truncating mutations increase PLK1-T210 phosphorylation and mitotic-marker proteins.
- [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) — exon 3 hotspot region centered at S33 (with phosphosites Y30, S33, T40 and mutations D32, G34) found in 23 tumors; increases Wnt-pathway activity, β-catenin protein, and H2B N-terminal acetylation (K16/K20/K24).
- [APC](../genes/APC.md) — loss-of-function mutations in 11 CTNNB1-WT tumors provide an alternative route to β-catenin overexpression; APC and [AXIN1](../genes/AXIN1.md) protein are upregulated when CTNNB1 hotspot mutated.
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — mutated in 92% of MSI tumors (FDR = 0).
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) — mutated in 76% of MSI tumors (FDR = 0); ARID1A-mutant samples show H3K27/H3K36 hyperacetylation; truncating mutations reduce [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) protein.
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — KRAS-mutant samples show upregulation of H3K27 and H3K36 acetylation.
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — missense mutations decrease [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) protein levels in this cohort.
- [RPL22](../genes/RPL22.md) / [RPL22L1](../genes/RPL22L1.md) — [RPL22](../genes/RPL22.md) mutated in 64% of MSI tumors; [RPL22L1](../genes/RPL22L1.md) protein upregulated in MSI, especially in RPL22-mutant tumors (p = 0.002), consistent with their synthetic-lethal pair.
- [KMT2B](../genes/KMT2B.md) — putative SMG in MSI (56%, FDR = 0.001), not reported in TCGA.
- [INPPL1](../genes/INPPL1.md) — putative SMG in MSI (56%, FDR = 0); truncating mutations reduce protein levels.
- [JAK1](../genes/JAK1.md) — putative SMG in MSI (44%, FDR = 6.4e-07); all [JAK1](../genes/JAK1.md) frameshifts were in MSI samples and derived from microsatellite indels; associated with higher tumor grade and enriched in TMB-H/APM-L tumors → candidate immune-evasion driver.
- [STAT1](../genes/STAT1.md) — truncation mutation in a POLE TMB-H/APM-L sample.
- [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md) — silenced by promoter methylation in MSI tumors; protein downregulated.
- [EPM2AIP1](../genes/EPM2AIP1.md) — downregulated at mRNA and protein in MSI tumors via shared promoter methylation with [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md).
- [PMS1](../genes/PMS1.md) / [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md) — downregulated only at the protein level in MSI tumors (loss of MLH1 destabilizes them).
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md), [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md), [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md), [IGF1R](../genes/IGF1R.md) — frequently copy-number altered, consistent with TCGA.
- [MDM4](../genes/MDM4.md) — mRNA increases with 1q copy number in MSI tumors (p < 0.01, Spearman) — candidate p53-pathway suppressor on amplified 1q.
- [SETDB1](../genes/SETDB1.md), SDE2, [PARP1](../genes/PARP1.md), [GATAD2B](../genes/GATAD2B.md) — 1q-encoded histone modifiers anticorrelated with p53-pathway target proteins ([CDKN1A](../genes/CDKN1A.md), [TNFRSF10B](../genes/TNFRSF10B.md)).
- [CDK12](../genes/CDK12.md) — hyperphosphorylated in CNV-high tumors; modulates ovarian-cancer sensitivity to [olaparib](../drugs/olaparib.md) (Bajrami et al., 2014) and could enhance immune-checkpoint response.
- [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) — hyperphosphorylated in CNV-high tumors; targetable by FDA-approved drugs.
- [DNMT1](../genes/DNMT1.md) — hyperphosphorylated in DDR-high endometrioid and serous tumors; pharmacologically inhibited by [azacitidine](../drugs/azacitidine.md) and [decitabine](../drugs/decitabine.md).
- [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) — kinase-activity inference flags [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) activation in CNV-high endometrioid vs CNV-low tumors; multiple FDA-approved inhibitors available.
- [QKI](../genes/QKI.md) / [ESRP2](../genes/ESRP2.md) / [ZEB1](../genes/ZEB1.md) / [ZEB2](../genes/ZEB2.md) — proposed EMT regulatory axis where QKI promotes 35 EMT-associated circRNAs (and is positively correlated with ZEB1/ZEB2), opposed by [ESRP2](../genes/ESRP2.md).
- [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md) — circRNA from [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md) reported (cited from Yang et al., 2018) to encode a protein product reducing c-Myc half-life.
- [FOXA2](../genes/FOXA2.md) — increased K274 acetylation in CNV-low subtype (deacetylation reduces [FOXA2](../genes/FOXA2.md) stability); role in EC remains unclear.
- [ATAD2](../genes/ATAD2.md), [PBK](../genes/PBK.md), [IGF2BP3](../genes/IGF2BP3.md) — CT antigens observed in >10% of tumors; [IGF2BP3](../genes/IGF2BP3.md) highly upregulated in serous and known immunogenic peptide source.
- [TAP1](../genes/TAP1.md), [TAP2](../genes/TAP2.md) — reduced antigen-peptide-transporter protein levels in TMB-H/APM-L tumors lacking JAK1/STAT1 mutations.
- [TP53BP1](../genes/TP53BP1.md) S1763 / [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md) S163 — phosphosites elevated in serous tumors; PLK1-driven inactivation of the DDR and G2M-checkpoint progression.
- [PLK1](../genes/PLK1.md), [CDK1](../genes/CDK1.md), [CHEK1](../genes/CHEK1.md), [XPO1](../genes/XPO1.md), [TPX2](../genes/TPX2.md), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), [MAP3K4](../genes/MAP3K4.md) — downstream of TP53 / CTNNB1 / ARID1A mutations in trans.

## Clinical implications

- **CTNNB1 hotspot mutations** identify a subset of low-grade, low-stage endometrioid tumors with potentially worse prognosis (recapitulating Kurnit et al., 2017; Myers et al., 2014), now mechanistically linked to constitutive Wnt activation and H2B acetylation — refining biomarkers for adjuvant therapy selection.
- **TP53 hotspot missense (gain-of-function) mutations** upregulate the Aurora kinase pathway, providing a theoretical basis for [AURKA](../genes/AURKA.md) inhibitor evaluation in TP53-hotspot-mutant EC.
- **DDR-high tumors**: hyperphosphorylation of [DNMT1](../genes/DNMT1.md) suggests potential utility of FDA-approved DNMT inhibitors [azacitidine](../drugs/azacitidine.md) and [decitabine](../drugs/decitabine.md) — beyond traditional PARP inhibitors — in elevated-DNA-damage EC.
- **CNV-high tumors**: [CDK12](../genes/CDK12.md) hyperphosphorylation could sensitize EC to the PARP inhibitor [olaparib](../drugs/olaparib.md) (analogous to ovarian cancer; Bajrami et al., 2014) and may enhance immune-checkpoint blockade responses. [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) and CDK4 are additional CNV-high candidates with existing FDA-approved drugs.
- **Immunotherapy selection**: TMB alone is insufficient. JAK1/STAT1 truncating mutations and reduced TAP1/TAP2/HLA levels mark APM-deficient tumors that may resist immune checkpoint inhibitors even when TMB is high — and should be considered when selecting EC patients for ICI, in addition to the FDA-approved MSI biomarker.
- **Putative vaccine antigens**: 78% of EC tumors harbor either a neoantigen or a high-expression CT antigen, supporting proteogenomics as a vaccine target-discovery tool in EC.

## Limitations & open questions

- Observational study: most mechanistic links (Wnt–acetylation, QKI/circRNA/EMT, APM deficiency, CDK12/PARP-inhibitor sensitivity) require functional validation.
- Cohort size of 95 tumors limits statistical power, particularly for the 7 [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) and 12 serous subgroups.
- Demographic and outcome data for this prospective cohort were still maturing at publication; associations between molecular features and recurrence/response/survival remain to be tested.
- [MDM4](../genes/MDM4.md) protein was not detected (low abundance), preventing direct protein-level confirmation of the 1q-amp / p53-pathway-repression model.
- Whether the CTNNB1 → histone-acetylation effects are EC-specific or generalize across cancers will require parallel acetylproteomic studies.
- FOXA2 acetylation has been linked to both proliferation (colon) and metastasis inhibition (lung adenocarcinoma); its role in EC is undefined.
- Sequencing-platform differences from the TCGA EC cohort may account for some observed mutation-rate increases.
- Acetylated peptides prepared via IP from the IMAC flow-through likely miss peptides that are both phosphorylated and acetylated; dual-modified PTM stoichiometry was not pursued.
