# Uterine Clear Cell Carcinoma (UCCC)

## Overview

UCCC (Uterine Clear Cell Carcinoma) is a rare, high-grade endometrial carcinoma subtype with clear-cell histology, classified as a child of [UCEC](UCEC.md) in OncoTree. It is histologically distinct from endometrioid ([UEC](../cancer_types/UEC.md)) and serous ([USC](../cancer_types/USC.md)) endometrial carcinomas but overlaps with both molecularly; approximately one-third of CCECs show serous-like profiles (TP53/PPP2R1A co-mutation) and one-fifth show endometrioid-like profiles (PTEN/KRAS/ARID1A mutations). MSI is present in ~11% of cases. The largest exome-scale characterization was the NIH study ([uccc_nih_2017](../datasets/uccc_nih_2017.md), n=63) that nominated [TAF1](../genes/TAF1.md) and [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) as novel candidate drivers.

## Cohorts in the corpus

- [uccc_nih_2017](../datasets/uccc_nih_2017.md) — 63 clinically diagnosed CCEC cases (16 paired exomes, 47 Sanger-validated); NIH, Le Gallo et al. 2017.

## Recurrent alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — 39.7% of CCEC; enriched in serous-like profiles (group 1). [PMID:28485815](../papers/28485815.md)
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — 23.8%; part of the PI3K axis mutated in 34.9% of CCECs. [PMID:28485815](../papers/28485815.md)
- [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md) — 15.9%. [PMID:28485815](../papers/28485815.md)
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) — 15.9%. [PMID:28485815](../papers/28485815.md)
- [PPP2R1A](../genes/PPP2R1A.md) — 15.9%; co-enriched with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) in serous-like profiles. [PMID:28485815](../papers/28485815.md)
- [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md) — 14.3%. [PMID:28485815](../papers/28485815.md)
- [TAF1](../genes/TAF1.md) — 9.5% (6/63); 5 mutations in the putative HAT domain including recurrent Arg843 and Ala850; nominated as a novel CCEC candidate driver. [PMID:28485815](../papers/28485815.md)
- [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md) — 7.9%. [PMID:28485815](../papers/28485815.md)
- [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) (MLL3) — 7.9%; 3 frameshift mutations N-terminal to the SET domain; nominated as a novel CCEC candidate driver. [PMID:28485815](../papers/28485815.md)
- MSI — 11.3% of CCECs; MSI-high tumors carry markedly elevated mutational loads (>400 somatic calls). [PMID:28485815](../papers/28485815.md)
- No [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) exonuclease-domain hotspot mutations identified in CCEC (contrasting with favorable-prognosis EEC subset). [PMID:28485815](../papers/28485815.md)
- 13/189 advanced endometrial tumors were uterine clear cell carcinoma; included in the prospective MSK-IMPACT 341/410-gene panel cohort; grouped under UCEC for pathway and actionability analysis [PMID:30068706](../papers/30068706.md)

## Subtypes

- Serous-like (group 1): TP53 + [PPP2R1A](../genes/PPP2R1A.md) co-mutation; 27.0% of CCECs; resembles USC.
- Mixed (group 2): 19.1%; intermediate molecular features.
- Endometrioid-like (group 3): PTEN/KRAS/ARID1A enrichment; 20.6%; resembles UEC.
- No detectable alteration across the 7-gene + MSI panel: 33.3% — likely harbor drivers outside the surveyed loci.

## Therapeutic landscape

- 11.3% MSI rate supports consideration of anti-PD-1 therapy ([pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md)); two MSI endometrial cancer patients responded to [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md) in a phase 2 trial (Le Gallo et al.). [PMID:28485815](../papers/28485815.md)
- 34.9% PI3K-axis mutation rate (PIK3CA/PIK3R1/PTEN) implies potential vulnerability to PI3K/AKT/mTOR-axis inhibitors. [PMID:28485815](../papers/28485815.md)
