**PMID:** 25855536 · **DOI:** 10.1038/ncomms7744 · **Journal:** Nature Communications (2015)

## TL;DR

Witkiewicz et al. performed whole-exome sequencing on 109 needle-microdissected pancreatic ductal adenocarcinomas ([PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md)) paired with germline normals, with the goal of overcoming the low-tumour-cellularity limitation of prior pancreatic-cancer sequencing studies. Microdissection raised mean tumour cellularity above 50% and lifted the average non-synonymous mutation burden to 67 per case, comparable to other solid tumours. They confirmed the canonical [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) / [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) / [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) / [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) backbone and nominated 24 significantly mutated genes (MutSigCV, q<0.1), including several with prognostic associations: [RBM10](../genes/RBM10.md) mutations (4%) tracked with longer survival despite aggressive histology, while [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) loss (confirmed by IHC in an independent 296-case cohort) tracked with poor outcome. [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) amplification at 8q24.13 was uniquely associated with poor outcome and the adenosquamous ([PAASC](../cancer_types/PAASC.md)) subtype. Within the [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) signalling axis, codon-12 mutations dominated but codon-Q61 alleles showed a remarkably favourable prognosis with reduced pERK staining; [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)^V600E^ mutations (3%) were mutually exclusive with [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), and a patient-derived [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)^V600E^ PDA cell line was as sensitive to [vemurafenib](../drugs/vemurafenib.md) (PLX-4032) as a melanoma control. High-frequency pathway lesions (WNT/β-catenin, chromatin-remodelling SWI/SNF, Hedgehog, DNA repair, RB/cell-cycle) define multiple candidate targeted therapies in PDA.

## Cohort & data

- **109 surgically resected pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma cases** with matched germline (normal tissue n=105 or peripheral blood n=4), all annotated with outcome and etiological features.
- **Histological subtypes**: ductal carcinoma NOS / [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md) (n=94, 86%), adenosquamous / [PAASC](../cancer_types/PAASC.md) (n=11, 10%), colloid / [PAAC](../cancer_types/PAAC.md) (n=4, 4%)). Mostly stage I (n=5) or II (n=97); stage III (n=6) or IV (n=1). Median age 66 (range 29–86); 50% male.
- **Median overall survival**: 21 months. Grade 3 (n=27) was associated with poor outcome; nodal status and adenosquamous subtype trended toward poor outcome.
- **Dataset**: [paad_utsw_2015](../datasets/paad_utsw_2015.md) — cBioPortal study id; raw reads at NCBI SRA BioProject PRJNA278883 ("Whole Exome Sequencing of Microdissected Pancreatic Cancer").
- **Sample prep**: ~7-μm frozen-tissue slides; tumour epithelium manually needle-dissected (14-gauge needle, dissecting microscope); DNA extracted with QIAamp DNA Mini Kit. Tumour cellularity >50% (vs >60% recommended threshold for unselected resections).
- **Assays**: [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) using TruSeq Exome Enrichment (FC-121-1048) or Nextera Exome Enrichment (FC-140-1003) on Illumina HiSeq 2500. Mean depth 51.28× (95% CI 49.5–53.1) covering 99.26% of targets at ≥1×; 91.51% of targets at ≥15× coverage; 21 cases re-sequenced to ~123× depth.
- **Variant calling pipeline**: reads aligned with [bwa](../methods/bwa.md) to UCSC hg19; SNVs by [mutect](../methods/mutect.md) (≥14 tumour reads, ≥8 normal reads); INDELs by [varscan](../methods/varscan.md) (≥8 reference, ≥3 variant reads, indels manually inspected in IGV). Annotation by Broad Institute [oncotator](../methods/oncotator.md); segmentation with ExomeCNV + DNAcopy (circular binary segmentation); copy-number calls by [gistic](../methods/gistic.md) 2.0 (join_segment_size=4, conf=0.95). Significantly mutated genes by [mutsig](../methods/mutsig.md) (MutSigCV, default settings).
- **Validation**: 248 non-silent mutations across 132 genes validated by [sanger-sequencing](../methods/sanger-sequencing.md) in 84 cases — 92% confirmation rate (95% CI 87.6–94.6%). [fish](../methods/fish.md) with the Vysis LSI [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) dual-colour break-apart probe used to confirm 8q24 [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) amplification. [immunohistochemistry](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md) for [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) (Santa Cruz sc-32761), p53, pERK (Cell Signaling 4370) and p63 (Biolegend 619002) on autostainers; [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) IHC scored in an independent cohort of 296 PDA cases.
- **Pathway/subtype clustering**: [APC](../genes/APC.md) affinity-propagation clustering for CNV and pathway analysis; [random-forest-classifier](../methods/random-forest-classifier.md)-based unsupervised clustering of pathway alteration profiles. Combined re-analysis with the 99-case Biankin et al. ICGC cohort ([PMID:23103869](../papers/23103869.md))) yielded a 208-case meta-cohort for additional MutSigCV power.

## Key findings

- **Microdissection raised mutation discovery**: average 67 non-synonymous mutations per case — a substantial increase over the 26/case reported in the prior 99-case bulk-tissue PDA exome series ([PMID:23103869](../papers/23103869.md))) — consistent with stromal contamination masking somatic events in unselected resections.
- **Mutator-phenotype subset**: cases in the top mutation-burden quartile harboured lesions in mismatch-repair genes ([MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md), [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md), [MLH3](../genes/MLH3.md), [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md), [POLE](../genes/POLE.md), [EXO1](../genes/EXO1.md)) and displayed a T→C-at-CTG mutation signature consistent with mismatch-repair deficiency; overall PDA spectra were dominated by C→T (age-associated) with minimal APOBEC TCW signal.
- **Smoking signature**: smokers showed an enrichment of G→T transversions matching the canonical smoking signature, and had ~half the median survival of non-smokers (HR=2.8734, *P*=0.069).
- **[MYC](../genes/MYC.md) amplification (8q24.13)**: the only CNA among GISTIC peaks uniquely associated with poor overall survival (*P*=0.0013). [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) amplification was significantly over-represented in adenosquamous histology and was confirmed by break-apart FISH (no translocation). MYC-amplified cases did not show higher mutation burden or co-occurrence with other PDA-hallmark mutations. [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) amplification was also detectable in PanIN precursor lesions adjacent to invasive disease.
- **[APC](../genes/APC.md) clustering of CNV** partitioned cases into 6 clusters; clusters 5 and 6 (high overall CNV) were enriched for mutations or homozygous deletions in DNA double-strand-break-repair genes ([ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md), [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), [FANCA](../genes/FANCA.md), [FANCD2](../genes/FANCD2.md), [FANCM](../genes/FANCM.md), NBN, RAD51AP2, RAD54B, RAD9A, XRCC4) — but *not* [TP53](../genes/TP53.md). Cluster 6 trended toward worst survival (HR=3.9115, *P*=0.034 vs cluster 1).
- **24 significantly mutated genes (MutSigCV)** occurring in ≥3.5% of cases: [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) 92%, [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) 50%, [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) 19%, [FLG](../genes/FLG.md) 10%, [ATXN1](../genes/ATXN1.md) 7%, [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) 6%, [COL14A1](../genes/COL14A1.md) 6%, [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) 6%, [RNF43](../genes/RNF43.md) 6%, [RP1L1](../genes/RP1L1.md) 6%, [ITGAE](../genes/ITGAE.md) 6%, [GLI3](../genes/GLI3.md) 6%, [GNAS](../genes/GNAS.md) 6%, [SPTA1](../genes/SPTA1.md) 6%, [BCLAF1](../genes/BCLAF1.md) 5%, [AXIN1](../genes/AXIN1.md) 5%, [NIN](../genes/NIN.md) 5%, [RBM10](../genes/RBM10.md) 4%, [IRF6](../genes/IRF6.md) 4%, [HDAC2](../genes/HDAC2.md) 4%, [PABPC1](../genes/PABPC1.md) 4%, [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) 4%, [USP10](../genes/USP10.md) 4%, [CDH13](../genes/CDH13.md) 4%. Combined 208-case meta-MutSigCV with Biankin et al. added lower-frequency cancer genes: [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md), [TGFBR2](../genes/TGFBR2.md), [ACVR1B](../genes/ACVR1B.md).
- **[GNAS](../genes/GNAS.md) hotspots in IPMN-derived PDA**: [GNAS](../genes/GNAS.md) mutations (n=6) were in hotspot codon 201 (R201C, R201H); all four colloid carcinomas plus two conventional PDAs carried [GNAS](../genes/GNAS.md) lesions, and all GNAS-mutant invasive cases were derived from intraductal papillary mucinous neoplasm ([IPMN](../cancer_types/IPMN.md) precursors. Four of six GNAS-mutant cases also carried [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) mutations.
- **[RBM10](../genes/RBM10.md) favourable prognosis**: [RBM10](../genes/RBM10.md) mutations (4 cases) were associated with longer overall survival (log-rank *P*=0.0345) *despite* all RBM10-mutant cases being high grade, pT3, with lymph-node metastasis in 3 of 4 cases. RBM10-mutant cases did not arise from [IPMN](../cancer_types/IPMN.md) and were not associated with [GNAS](../genes/GNAS.md) mutations.
- **[ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) loss confirmed prognostic in independent cohort**: [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) protein deficiency by IHC in an additional 296 PDA cases was associated with poor overall survival (Kaplan–Meier *P*=0.0202). ARID1A-deficient PDA cell lines showed vulnerability to siRNA depletion of [ARID1B](../genes/ARID1B.md), recapitulating the ARID1A-loss synthetic-lethal interaction reported in other cancers.
- **[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) allele heterogeneity drives prognosis**: 92% [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) mutation rate, dominated by codon 12 (G12D/G12V/G12C/G12R/G12S) with codon 13 and codon 61 (Q61H/Q61K/Q61R) at lower frequency (Fig. 4b–c). Codon-12 mutations had uniformly poor prognosis, but **codon-61 cases had a remarkably favourable prognosis** (log-rank *P*=0.01999), despite histological/clinical features otherwise indicative of aggressive disease. By IHC, codon-61 cases showed lower pERK staining than other KRAS-mutant cases, suggesting allele-specific differences in MAPK pathway output.
- **KRAS-wildtype PDA carries RAS-effector lesions**: in the 8% KRAS-wild-type subset, the authors identified [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) activating mutations (4% overall) and [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)^V600E^ (3% overall) — all mutually exclusive with KRAS — plus additional cancer-gene lesions ([STK11](../genes/STK11.md), [GNAS](../genes/GNAS.md), [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md)).
- **[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)^V600E^ PDA is vemurafenib-sensitive in vitro**: a patient-derived cell line (PDA_014, [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)^V600E^) established from one of the BRAF-mutant cases was as sensitive to [vemurafenib](../drugs/vemurafenib.md) (PLX-4032) as the MNT1 [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)^V600E^ melanoma control across ERK phosphorylation, cell-cycle (BrdU) and viability assays; KRAS-mutant PL45 PDA cells were resistant.
- **High-frequency pathway disruption (>20% of cases) beyond KRAS/TP53**: TGF-β (43% [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md), 9% [NOTCH4](../genes/NOTCH4.md), 7% [TGFBR2](../genes/TGFBR2.md), 6% [ACVR1B](../genes/ACVR1B.md), [SMAD3](../genes/SMAD3.md) 3%); NOTCH (10% [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md), 6% [NOTCH2](../genes/NOTCH2.md), 1% [NOTCH3](../genes/NOTCH3.md), [MAML1](../genes/MAML1.md)/[MAML2](../genes/MAML2.md)/[MAML3](../genes/MAML3.md)/[JAG1](../genes/JAG1.md)/[JAG2](../genes/JAG2.md) at low frequency); Hedgehog (8% [GLI3](../genes/GLI3.md), 8% [SMO](../genes/SMO.md), 6% [LRP2](../genes/LRP2.md), 3% [GLI2](../genes/GLI2.md), 2% [PTCH1](../genes/PTCH1.md)); WNT/β-catenin (9% [AXIN1](../genes/AXIN1.md), 7% [RNF43](../genes/RNF43.md), 3% [AXIN2](../genes/AXIN2.md), 2% [APC](../genes/APC.md), 2% [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md), 2% [TCF4](../genes/TCF4.md)); RB pathway (36% [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), 36% [CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md), 9% [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md), 6% [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md), 3% [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), 2% CDKN2D); SWI/SNF (7% [SMARCA2](../genes/SMARCA2.md), 6% [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), 5% [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md), 4% [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md), 4% [ARID1B](../genes/ARID1B.md), [SMARCC1](../genes/SMARCC1.md)/[SMARCC2](../genes/SMARCC2.md) at low frequency); DNA repair (9% [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), 6% [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md), 6% [BCLAF1](../genes/BCLAF1.md), 6% RAD51AP2, 5% [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md), 4% [FANCF](../genes/FANCF.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) 1%, etc.). Pearson correlations between pathways were weak, suggesting largely independent acquisition.
- **Pathway-based subtypes correlate with prognosis**: random-forest + [APC](../genes/APC.md) clustering of pathway alterations identified subtypes; cases with isolated KRAS-pathway alterations (alone or with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)) — clusters 1 & 2 — had poor prognosis, and cases with more complex multi-pathway deregulation trended toward even poorer outcome (HR=0.4369, *P*=0.1009 for clusters 1&2 vs others).

## Genes & alterations

- **[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — mutated in 92% of cases. Codon-12 alleles (G12D/G12V/G12C/G12R/G12S) dominate and confer poor prognosis; codon-13 and codon-61 (Q61H/Q61K/Q61R) alleles are minority. Codon-61 cases have favourable survival (*P*=0.01999) and lower pERK staining despite aggressive pathology, suggesting allele-specific MAPK output. Mutually exclusive with [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) and [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) lesions.
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — mutated in 50%, with strong p53-IHC stabilization concordance.
- **[SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) — mutated/deleted in 19–43% across analyses; backbone of the TGF-β pathway lesions in PDA.
- **[CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) / [CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md) — frequently deleted (36% each in pathway analysis); the dominant RB-pathway lesion. Co-occurs with [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) (9%) and [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) (6%) amplification and [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) loss (3%) in a smaller subset.
- **[MYC](../genes/MYC.md) — focal amplification at 8q24.13 in a subset of cases; uniquely associated with poor overall survival (*P*=0.0013) and adenosquamous histology ([PAASC](../cancer_types/PAASC.md)). Confirmed by FISH; no MYC translocations. Detectable already in PanIN precursor lesions.
- **[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — V600E mutations in 3% of cases; mutually exclusive with KRAS. Patient-derived PDA_014 cell line confirmed [vemurafenib](../drugs/vemurafenib.md)-sensitive.
- **[PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — activating mutations in 4% of cases; mutually exclusive with KRAS; nominate PI3K inhibitor candidates in PDA.
- **[RBM10](../genes/RBM10.md) — 4% of cases; loss-of-function mutations associated with prolonged overall survival despite aggressive histology, mirroring the favourable association seen in lung adenocarcinoma. Independent of [IPMN](../cancer_types/IPMN.md) origin and GNAS status.
- **[ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) — 6% mutated by sequencing; protein loss by IHC in independent 296-case cohort is significantly associated with poor outcome (*P*=0.0202). ARID1A-deficient PDA cell lines are vulnerable to [ARID1B](../genes/ARID1B.md) depletion (synthetic lethal).
- **[RNF43](../genes/RNF43.md) — 6% mutated; WNT-pathway tumour suppressor; predicted sensitivity to porcupine inhibitors (LGK974).
- **[GNAS](../genes/GNAS.md) — 6% mutated, hotspot codon 201 (R201C/R201H); all invasive cases derived from [IPMN](../cancer_types/IPMN.md) precursors; present in all four colloid ([PAAC](../cancer_types/PAAC.md)) and two conventional PDAs; frequent co-mutation with KRAS.
- **[AXIN1](../genes/AXIN1.md) / [AXIN2](../genes/AXIN2.md) / [APC](../genes/APC.md) — recurrently mutated WNT-pathway components nominating tankyrase inhibitors (XAV939) as therapeutic candidates.
- **[ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md), [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), [FANCF](../genes/FANCF.md), [FANCA](../genes/FANCA.md), [FANCD2](../genes/FANCD2.md), [FANCM](../genes/FANCM.md), [BCLAF1](../genes/BCLAF1.md) — DNA double-strand-break and Fanconi-anaemia pathway lesions enriched in high-CNV clusters; nominate [olaparib](../drugs/olaparib.md) (PARP inhibitor) and cross-linking agents like [mitomycin-c](../drugs/mitomycin-c.md) as therapeutic candidates.
- **[NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md)–4, [JAG1](../genes/JAG1.md)/[JAG2](../genes/JAG2.md), [MAML1](../genes/MAML1.md)–3** — NOTCH-pathway alterations totalling 31% of cases (amplification/mutation), nominating γ-secretase inhibitor (MK0752) candidates.
- **[SMO](../genes/SMO.md), [GLI2](../genes/GLI2.md), [GLI3](../genes/GLI3.md), [PTCH1](../genes/PTCH1.md), [LRP2](../genes/LRP2.md) — Hedgehog-pathway alterations.
- **[TGFBR2](../genes/TGFBR2.md), [TGFBR1](../genes/TGFBR1.md), [TGFB1](../genes/TGFB1.md), [ACVR1B](../genes/ACVR1B.md), [ACVR1C](../genes/ACVR1C.md), [SMAD3](../genes/SMAD3.md), [SMAD6](../genes/SMAD6.md) — TGF-β-axis lesions augmenting [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) loss.
- **[SMARCA2](../genes/SMARCA2.md), [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md), [SMARCC1](../genes/SMARCC1.md), [SMARCC2](../genes/SMARCC2.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [ARID1B](../genes/ARID1B.md), [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md) — SWI/SNF chromatin-remodelling lesions in >42% of cases.
- **[STK11](../genes/STK11.md), [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md)** — additional cancer-gene lesions identified in KRAS-wildtype cases.
- **[FLG](../genes/FLG.md), [ATXN1](../genes/ATXN1.md), [COL14A1](../genes/COL14A1.md), [RP1L1](../genes/RP1L1.md), [ITGAE](../genes/ITGAE.md), [SPTA1](../genes/SPTA1.md), [NIN](../genes/NIN.md), [BCLAF1](../genes/BCLAF1.md), [IRF6](../genes/IRF6.md), [HDAC2](../genes/HDAC2.md), [PABPC1](../genes/PABPC1.md), [USP10](../genes/USP10.md), [CDH13](../genes/CDH13.md)** — additional MutSigCV-significant genes not previously highlighted in PDA; functional roles in PDA biology remain to be validated.
- **[TERT](../genes/TERT.md) (5p15.33), [PDGFA](../genes/PDGFA.md) (7p22.3)** — recurrently amplified loci identified by GISTIC.

## Clinical implications

- **BRAF^V600E^ PDA as a [vemurafenib](../drugs/vemurafenib.md) candidate**: the 3% of cases harbouring BRAF V600E (mutually exclusive with KRAS) define a small but pharmacologically tractable subset. The patient-derived PDA_014 cell line is as sensitive to PLX-4032 as MNT1 melanoma cells, supporting BRAF-mutation testing in KRAS-wildtype PDA.
- **PIK3CA-mutant PDA**: 4% of cases carry activating [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) mutations; the authors nominate [buparlisib](../drugs/buparlisib.md) (BKM120) and [gdc-0941](../drugs/gdc-0941.md) as candidate PI3K-pathway therapies.
- **RB-pathway dysregulation as a [palbociclib](../drugs/palbociclib.md) opportunity**: combined [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) loss (>36%), [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) amplification, and [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) amplification (≥9% combined) make PDA a high-prior candidate for CDK4/6 inhibition with [palbociclib](../drugs/palbociclib.md) (PD-0332991) or LEE-11; the authors and others (Franco et al. 2014) had previously shown synergy with pathway-selective agents in PDA models.
- **MYC amplification as a CDK9 / BET-bromodomain target**: MYC amplification (~14% of cases) and association with poor outcome and adenosquamous histology nominate CDK9 (PHA767491) and BET-bromodomain ([jq1](../drugs/jq1.md)) inhibitors.
- **DNA repair deficiency as [olaparib](../drugs/olaparib.md) / [mitomycin-c](../drugs/mitomycin-c.md) opportunity**: 14% Fanconi-anaemia-pathway alterations and 3% BRCA1/2 alterations nominate cross-linking agents (mitomycin C) and PARP inhibitors ([olaparib](../drugs/olaparib.md)). The high-CNV clusters (clusters 5 and 6) enriched for DSB-repair lesions could be a stratification biomarker.
- **WNT/β-catenin therapy in RNF43- or AXIN1/2/APC-loss cases**: ~11% AXIN1/2 or [APC](../genes/APC.md) loss, plus 7% [RNF43](../genes/RNF43.md) loss, nominate porcupine inhibitors (LGK974) and tankyrase inhibitors (XAV939). These remain actionable in the presence of activating KRAS.
- **NOTCH-pathway inhibition** with γ-secretase inhibitors (MK0752) — 31% of cases harbour NOTCH-family amplifications/mutations.
- **Allele-specific KRAS prognosis**: codon-61 KRAS mutations identify a favourable-survival subset within otherwise aggressive PDA; suggests molecular stratification beyond simple KRAS-mutant vs wild-type and may have implications for emerging allele-selective RAS inhibitors.
- **[RBM10](../genes/RBM10.md) as a candidate favourable prognostic biomarker** in resected PDA, independent of histological grade and lymph-node status. ARID1A loss (by IHC) as a candidate unfavourable prognostic biomarker, validated in 296 cases.
- **MYC amplification as a candidate unfavourable prognostic biomarker** for adenosquamous and high-CNV PDA, detectable by routine FISH.

## Limitations & open questions

- Mean exome depth (~51×) was modest; deeper resequencing of 21 cases to ~123× recovered mostly low-AF events, suggesting a residual undercall of subclonal mutations even after microdissection.
- The 109-case cohort is single-institution (UT Southwestern + Thomas Jefferson) and resectable-disease only (mostly stage I–II); generalizability to unresectable/metastatic PDA is untested.
- Functional consequences of many novel MutSigCV genes (e.g., [FLG](../genes/FLG.md), [ATXN1](../genes/ATXN1.md), [COL14A1](../genes/COL14A1.md), [RP1L1](../genes/RP1L1.md), [ITGAE](../genes/ITGAE.md), [SPTA1](../genes/SPTA1.md), [NIN](../genes/NIN.md), [BCLAF1](../genes/BCLAF1.md), [IRF6](../genes/IRF6.md), [HDAC2](../genes/HDAC2.md), [PABPC1](../genes/PABPC1.md), [USP10](../genes/USP10.md), [CDH13](../genes/CDH13.md)) are not established; their oncogenic vs passenger status awaits orthogonal validation.
- The authors did not identify UPF1 mutations in their adenosquamous cases despite Liu et al. (Nat. Med. 2014) reporting frequent UPF1 mutation in pancreatic adenosquamous carcinoma — discordance unresolved.
- KRAS codon-61 favourable-prognosis finding is based on a small subset of the 92% KRAS-mutant cases (relatively rare allele); requires replication in larger cohorts.
- [RBM10](../genes/RBM10.md) favourable-survival association is based on only 4 RBM10-mutant cases — statistically suggestive (*P*=0.0345) but underpowered.
- BRAF V600E sensitivity to [vemurafenib](../drugs/vemurafenib.md) was shown in a single patient-derived line; clinical activity in BRAF-mutant PDA patients remains untested.
- Drivers of chromosomal instability (the high-CNV cluster 5/6 phenotype) beyond DSB-repair lesions are not identified — additional structural-variant or replication-stress mechanisms are not interrogated by exome alone.
- No matched RNA-seq or proteomic data accompany the exomes, limiting interpretation of expression-level consequences of CNAs (e.g., MYC amplification) and pathway flux for the proposed combination strategies.
