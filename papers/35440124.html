TL;DR

Caeser et al. assembled and deeply characterized a resource of 46 small cell lung cancer (SCLC) patient-derived xenograft (PDX) and circulating tumor cell-derived xenograft (CDX) models from 33 patients at Memorial Sloan Kettering, with paired clinical specimens for 18 cases. Multi-omic profiling — MSK-IMPACT targeted DNA sequencing (42 PDX/CDX, 26 clinical), bulk RNA-seq (43 PDXs), and IHC for subtype markers (37 PDXs) — confirmed retention of the canonical SCLC genomic landscape (near-universal TP53 and RB1 loss) and recapitulated the ASCL1/NEUROD1/POU2F3 transcriptional subtype framework in xenografts. Notably, the cohort includes 10 POU2F3-driven PDXs (subtype SCLC-P) derived from a single never-smoker donor — the first reported SCLC-P PDX models — along with evidence that SCLC-P can share a cell of origin with lung adenocarcinoma. The companion dataset is deposited in cBioPortal as lung_pdx_msk_2021.

Cohort & data

  • Models: 46 PDX/CDX models from 33 SCLC patients; 40/46 (87%) tissue-derived PDXs and 6/46 (13%) CDXs.
  • Patient cohort: 55% (18/33) male, 45% female; average age 66 at diagnosis. 27/33 (82%) current or former smokers. 19/33 (58%) extensive-stage at presentation, 14/33 (42%) limited-stage.
  • Engraftment: 45/198 (23.81%) pure SCLC samples and 13/29 (44.83%) mixed-histology samples successfully engrafted; mean engraftment time 112 days (110 days PDX vs 131 days CDX).
  • Assays: MSK-IMPACT targeted NGS on 42 PDX and 26 clinical samples (panel iterations of 341, 410, or 468 genes), bulk RNA-seq on 43 PDXs, and immunohistochemistry on 37 PDX samples and 19 clinical samples.
  • Paired clinical-PDX comparison: 18 cases with both clinical and matched PDX MSK-IMPACT data.
  • Dataset: lung_pdx_msk_2021 in cBioPortal (gene expression deposited at ArrayExpress E-MTAB-11230).

Key findings

  • Canonical SCLC drivers are retained in PDX: TP53 alterations in 24/26 (92%) clinical samples and 90% of PDX samples; RB1 truncating/splice/structural variants/deep deletions in 21/26 (81%) clinical and 76% of PDX samples (PMID:35440124).
  • Paired-pair concordance: 16/18 (89%) clinical-PDX pairs retained genomic alterations in TP53 and RB1; MSK599 and MSK871 PDXs lacked the TP53 alterations seen in matched clinical samples.
  • Two atypical KRAS-amplified clinical samples (P-0039208-T01-IM6 and -T02-IM6) lacked TP53 and RB1 mutations but harbored KRAS amplifications together with MDM2 amplification; the authors speculate MDM2 amplification could phenocopy TP53 inactivation.
  • Four never-smoker samples (P-0022320-T01/2-IM6 and P-0025975-T01/2-IM6) carried oncogenic EGFR mutations consistent with histologic transformation from LUAD.
  • Higher copy-number alteration density in PDXs vs clinical samples, attributed to greater purity after stromal replacement by murine cells.
  • Recurrent NSCLC-associated mutations in a subset of PDXs (MSK1053, MSK511c, MSK773D/E/G/I): SMARCA4, STK11, KEAP1, and PTPRS (PTPRS absent in MSK511c).
  • Subtype recapitulation: IHC + RNA-seq confirmed ASCL1, NEUROD1, POU2F3, and YAP1 subtype structure. YAP1 protein expression was consistently absent or very low across the cohort, questioning YAP1 as a defining IHC marker.
  • POU2F3-driven cluster: A distinct subset of 10 PDXs (MSK773 B-L) from one patient was strongly POU2F3+ and negative for other subtype markers — the first published SCLC-P PDX resource. All 10 expressed MYC at protein and RNA levels.
  • YAP/TAZ downstream activity in SCLC-P: POU2F3-positive PDXs highly expressed YAP1 downstream genes (NOTCH2, NOTCH3) regardless of YAP1 expression itself.
  • SCLC-P transcriptional features: high MYC, high REST and BACH2, low DLL3 and ATOH1; low INSM1 and CHGA NE markers.
  • EMT signature heterogeneous in non-NE SCLC: SCLC-P samples lacked detectable EPCAM but showed variable VIM expression and low SNAI1/SNAI2/ZEB1, consistent with EMT not being a uniform hallmark of non-NE SCLC.
  • MYC-family expression tracks subtype: SCLC-P samples expressed high MYC; the two SCLC-N samples (MSK424, MSK443) had the highest MYCL expression, and MSK-IMPACT detected MYCL amplifications in MSK424, MSK304, MSK40, MSK599b, MSK891, and MSK736.
  • Inflamed signature: 7/43 PDXs (MSK831b, MSK511c, MSK325d, MSK773B, MSK773H, MSK761, MSK891) showed high expression of HLAs and antigen-presentation genes, not segregating cleanly with a single transcription-factor subtype.
  • Shared SCLC-P/LUAD origin (MSK773 case study): A male never-smoker in his 70s with prior Hodgkin’s disease and mantle field radiation, treated with carboplatin/etoposide and then ipilimumab + nivolumab without response, had an initial biopsy showing admixed SCLC and lung adenocarcinoma. Separate MSK-IMPACT on the two histologies showed shared somatic mutations and copy-number changes (TET2, CDKN1B, PTEN, TCF3, MDM2, FGFR1, KRAS) with RB1 and TP53 loss specific to the SCLC component, supporting clonal origin and consistent with type II pneumocyte as a possible cell of origin for SCLC-P.

Genes & alterations

  • TP53 — missense, truncating, splice, in-frame mutations in 92% (24/26) clinical and 90% PDX samples (PMID:35440124).
  • RB1 — truncating, splice, structural variants, deep deletions in 81% (21/26) clinical and 76% PDX samples; lost specifically in SCLC component of MSK773 admixed tumor.
  • KRAS — amplifications in two atypical TP53/RB1-wildtype clinical samples and in the MSK773 SCLC/adenocarcinoma case; ~15% of PDX samples.
  • MDM2 — amplification co-occurring with KRAS amplification in two TP53/RB1-wildtype clinical samples; 100% of MSK773 admixed components.
  • EGFR — oncogenic mutations in four never-smoker samples consistent with histologic transformation from LUAD; 15% PDX samples.
  • MYCL — amplification correlated with high transcript expression in MSK424, MSK304, MSK40, MSK599b, MSK891, MSK736; highest expression in two SCLC-N samples.
  • MYC — uniformly high in POU2F3-driven PDXs and in MSK761/MSK900b outliers; 12% of PDXs.
  • SMARCA4, STK11, KEAP1, PTPRS — NSCLC-typical mutations seen in MSK1053, MSK511c, and MSK773D/E/G/I PDX subset (PTPRS not in MSK511c).
  • PIK3CA, FOXA1 — MSK304B PDX lost matched-clinical amplifications but gained MYCL amplification.
  • CDKN1B, PTEN, TCF3, FGFR1, TET2 — shared between LUAD and SCLC components of MSK773 (clonal).
  • ASCL1, NEUROD1, POU2F3, YAP1 — subtype-defining transcriptional regulators; protein-level YAP1 absent across cohort.
  • NOTCH1/NOTCH2/NOTCH3 — downstream YAP/TAZ pathway upregulated in POU2F3-positive PDXs.
  • INSM1, CHGA, NCAM1 — NE markers high in SCLC-A/N/A-N, low in SCLC-P.
  • REST, BACH2 — high in non-NE SCLC-P; DLL3, ATOH1 — low in SCLC-P.
  • VIM, EPCAM, SNAI1, SNAI2, ZEB1 — variable / non-uniform EMT pattern in SCLC-P.

Clinical implications

  • PDX/CDX as a translational platform: The authors argue that PDX models retain key genomic and proteomic features of donor tumors (89% concordance for TP53/RB1 in paired pairs) and are therefore suitable for testing subtype-targeted SCLC therapies emerging from early-phase trials.
  • Subtype-specific therapy hypotheses: SCLC-P cell lines have previously been reported sensitive to PARP inhibition, and MYC-high SCLC may have selective sensitivity to Aurora kinase inhibition; the 10 MSK773 POU2F3-driven PDXs provide the first SCLC-P in-vivo system for testing such hypotheses.
  • Histologic transformation cases (EGFR-mutant never-smoker samples) support the established phenomenon of LUAD → SCLC transformation as a resistance mechanism to EGFR-targeted therapy.
  • Standard-of-care backdrop: Authors note current first-line standard for extensive-stage SCLC is platinum-based chemoimmunotherapy (atezolizumab or durvalumab with cisplatin/carboplatin + etoposide) and chemoradiotherapy for limited-stage disease.
  • Clinical case (MSK773): patient received carboplatin + etoposide, progressed within 6 months, then received ipilimumab + nivolumab without response — illustrating limited ICB activity in SCLC-P.

Limitations & open questions

  • PDX/CDX models are maintained in immunodeficient NOD.Cg-PrkdcIl2rg/SzJ mice; absence of an intact adaptive immune system and replacement of human stromal elements by murine cells precludes evaluation of immune-targeted therapies and may alter antigen-presentation and immune profiling.
  • All 10 SCLC-P models derive from a single patient (MSK773), limiting generalizability of SCLC-P findings to broader patient populations.
  • Two PDXs (MSK761, MSK900b) showed transcriptional patterns intermediate between non-SCLC-P and SCLC-P clusters, raising open questions about whether these represent additional subtype classes or transitional phenotypes — and whether site of biopsy (liver vs blood CDX in the MSK761/MSK761c pair) contributes to heterogeneity.
  • The authors observed minor differences between matched clinical and PDX genomic spectra (e.g., MSK599, MSK871 lost TP53; MSK304B lost PIK3CA/FOXA1 amplifications, gained MYCL amplification) — significance of these differences (intrinsic clonal heterogeneity vs PDX bottleneck/selection) remains to be elucidated.
  • Larger sample sizes and definition of gene expression signatures correlating with disease progression and drug resistance are needed; current cohort showed no major transcriptional differences between treated and treatment-naïve samples.
