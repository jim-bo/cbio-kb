**PMID:** 30537516 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.ccell.2018.10.016 · **Journal:** Cancer Cell (2018)

## TL;DR

Gerhauser et al. integrated whole-genome, transcriptome, and methylome profiling of 292 prostate cancer (PC) cases — 203 of them early-onset (EOPC, diagnosis ≤ 55 years) — to chart the earliest somatic events in PC and to derive risk markers. They identified recurrent duplications at 8q22 centered on [ESRP1](../genes/ESRP1.md) (a novel prognostic biomarker validated by IHC in ~12,000 TMA cores), showed that [APOBEC3B](../genes/APOBEC3B.md)-driven mutagenesis acts in a clock-like manner from the earliest detectable lesions, defined a methylation-based risk score (PEPCI) and four prognostic molecular subgroups by integrating cell-type composition with co-expression clusters, and developed PRESCIENT, a conditional-probability network model that predicts the next molecular event and event-free survival from a single biopsy.

## Cohort & data

- 292 PC genomes profiled in [prostate_dkfz_2018](../datasets/prostate_dkfz_2018.md), comprising 203 early-onset PC (EOPC, age ≤ 55) and 89 late-onset PC (LOPC) cases; 41 of the WGS samples were re-used from Fraser et al. 2017.
- Cancer type: [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) (prostate adenocarcinoma).
- Assays per cohort:
  - [whole-genome sequencing](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) of tumor + matched blood in 184 EOPC and 85 LOPC patients.
  - [450k methylation array](../methods/450k-methylation-array.md) on 203 EOPC and 45 LOPC tumors.
  - [mRNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) on 96 EOPC samples.
  - Tissue-microarray [immunohistochemistry](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md) for [ESRP1](../genes/ESRP1.md) on 11,954 tumor cores from radical-prostatectomy specimens (1992–2014, University Medical Center Hamburg-Eppendorf; median follow-up 48 months).
  - ESRP1 [FISH](../methods/fish.md) using BAC RP11-267M23 / RP11-22C11 (8q22.1) with centromere 8 reference probe.
- Independent validation in the TCGA PC cohort ([prad_tcga_pub](../datasets/prad_tcga_pub.md), 462 PC samples with RNA-seq).

## Key findings

- **Lower SNV burden in EOPC than LOPC.** Median 0.47 SNVs/Mb in EOPC (IQR 0.49) vs. 0.53 in LOPC; SNV and SV counts both increase significantly with age (p < 0.001).
- **ETS fusions dominate EOPC architecture.** 70% of EOPC tumors carry an SV consistent with an ETS-fusion event, with [TMPRSS2](../genes/TMPRSS2.md)-[ERG](../genes/ERG.md) the canonical case.
- **Top recurrent SV-affected loci in EOPC** were chromosome 8p centered on [NKX3-1](../genes/NKX3-1.md) (37%), 3p14 centered on [FOXP1](../genes/FOXP1.md) (30%), 13q22 centered on [KLF5](../genes/KLF5.md) (27%), and 8q22 centered on [ESRP1](../genes/ESRP1.md) (17%).
- **Biallelic inactivation.** [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) had the highest rate of biallelic inactivation (12 samples), followed by [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (8 samples). [NKX3-1](../genes/NKX3-1.md) and [FOXP1](../genes/FOXP1.md) were rarely biallelically lost despite frequent SV involvement, consistent with haploinsufficient tumor-suppressor roles.
- **EOPC vs. LOPC architecture.** LOPC was enriched for genomic losses at recurrent altered loci; EOPC had a higher rate of balanced breaks (p < 1×10⁻⁷ and p < 1×10⁻⁴, Fisher's exact). EOPC tumors were more monoclonal (66% vs. 53% in LOPC).
- **Chromatin context of SV breakpoints.** EOPC breakpoints are enriched near open chromatin, active enhancers, transcription-factor binding, and actively transcribed regions; SV breakpoint density correlates with Hi-C chromatin-loop counts and H3K27ac peaks (p < 0.0001; Spearman ρ = 0.23 and 0.18, respectively), with weaker correlations in LOPC (ρ = 0.11 and 0.06).
- **[KLF5](../genes/KLF5.md) loss has multi-omic consequences.** 13q22 loss reduces [KLF5](../genes/KLF5.md) mRNA and is coupled to differential methylation of a CpG-island-shore site upstream of *KLF5* (Spearman ρ = −0.523, q = 0.0038). KLF5 mRNA correlates positively with [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md) mRNA in both this cohort and TCGA (p < 1×10⁻⁴, ρ = 0.19) — but independently of [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md) mutation status.
- **ESRP1 duplications drive overexpression and aggressive disease.** Tumors with 8q22 duplications intersecting [ESRP1](../genes/ESRP1.md) (33 Mb away from [MYC](../genes/MYC.md)) show >1.5-fold higher ESRP1 mRNA. Although duplications often span both genes, only ESRP1 (not [MYC](../genes/MYC.md)) showed a significant expression increase. ESRP1 duplications were significantly associated with elevated Gleason Score (GS) — more strongly than any other RGA in the cohort (p < 1×10⁻¹¹, chi-squared).
- **ESRP1 IHC validation in 11,954 TMA cores.** High ESRP1 protein staining tracks with high GS (>4+4), advanced stage (pT3b–pT4), more lymph-node metastases, higher preoperative PSA, higher Ki-67 proliferation index, and significantly shorter time to biochemical recurrence ([BCR](../genes/BCR.md)). ESRP1 is an independent prognostic marker in multivariate analysis controlling for four established clinico-pathological parameters and independent of [ERG](../genes/ERG.md) status; the signal is particularly strong in the biopsy setting where GS is often underestimated.
- **APOBEC3B-driven clock-like mutagenesis.** Six mutational signatures were observed: clock-like signatures 1 and 5, DNA-repair signatures 3 and 6, and APOBEC signatures 2 and 13. Both clock-like signatures and the APOBEC signatures correlate with patient age (the latter at p = 5.2×10⁻³, ρ = 0.17). APOBEC C/G clusters (kataegis events) accumulate with age and are predominantly [APOBEC3B](../genes/APOBEC3B.md)-like. Carriers of a known ~30 kb germline *[APOBEC3B](../genes/APOBEC3B.md)* deletion (complete loss of coding sequence) had significantly fewer signature 2/13 mutations, lower A3B expression, and a shift from A3B-like to [APOBEC3A](../genes/APOBEC3A.md)-like mutagenesis.
- **APOBEC-driven kataegis co-localizes with SV breakpoints.** C/G clusters are strongly enriched at SV breakpoints (1–10 kb co-localization window), and several such breakpoints created driver events including the TMPRSS2-ERG fusion and disruption of [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [FOXP1](../genes/FOXP1.md), and [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md).
- **Germline DDR variants amplify genomic instability.** Germline protein-truncating variants in [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), [PALB2](../genes/PALB2.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), and [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md) were significantly associated with higher somatic SV/SNV counts and elevated APOBEC signature 2 and "BRCAness" signature 3.
- **PEPCI — methylation-based purity-adjusted risk score.** Combining T-luminal cell content and infiltrating-immune cell content (from FACS-sorted reference methylomes) into a Purity-adjusted Epigenetic Prostate Cancer Index (PEPCI) stratifies tumors: high PEPCI is associated with high pT (p < 1×10⁻⁷, Kruskal-Wallis), high GS (p < 1×10⁻¹⁷, Wilcoxon), and BCR (log-rank p < 0.0001). PEPCI predicts GS at AUC = 0.831 and BCR at AUC = 0.702, and stratifies intermediate-risk GS7 (especially GS 4+3) cases — validated in TCGA. PEPCI-high tumors are strongly enriched for [ESRP1](../genes/ESRP1.md) gain (odds ratio = 15.7, FDR p < 1×10⁻⁵).
- **Four molecular subgroups from CLICK co-expression clusters.** Seven co-expression clusters (CC1–7) integrated with cell-type content and PEPCI define four PC subgroups: (1) PEPCI-high tumors with high T-luminal content and CC7 (reactive stroma, ASPN marker) — most aggressive; (2) a smaller PEPCI-high subgroup with high CC1 (immune), CC5 (stroma), and CC7 expression; (3) intermediate-risk, high-CC5; (4) PEPCI-low, basal/luminal-like (CC2/CC4 high) — best prognosis. Subgroup 4 is enriched in EOPC (p = 0.008, adjusted for GS). CC2-low and CC7-high tumors are enriched for [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) loss (p < 0.0001) and [ESRP1](../genes/ESRP1.md) gain (p < 0.0001 and p < 0.0005, respectively). Subgroup membership improves BCR prediction over GS alone within GS7 cases in TCGA (p = 0.015).
- **PRESCIENT temporal-order model.** A conditional-probability network model predicts the next likely somatic event and the associated Probability of Event-Free Survival (PEFS) given preceding alterations. [ERG](../genes/ERG.md) had the highest exclusion score and is most often the initiating event, frequently followed by [FOXP1](../genes/FOXP1.md). Multi-region WGS of seven EOPC tumors was used as orthogonal validation: in tumors with subclonal divergence, the dominant clone matched the branch with the shorter predicted PEFS. PCA041 displayed an early clonal ESRP1 gain that preceded loss of *KLF5*/*[BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md)*/[RB1](../genes/RB1.md) on one branch and parallel biallelic inactivation of [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) on another.
- **Actionable targets.** >40% of EOPC patients could be matched to at least one targeted therapy in pre-clinical or clinical trials for PC; classes included PI3K, mTOR, and PARP inhibitors. *[BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md)*-mutated patients showed high signature-3 ("BRCAness") burden and elevated mutational load, suggesting PARP-inhibitor susceptibility.

## Genes & alterations

- [ESRP1](../genes/ESRP1.md) — recurrent 8q22 duplications (17% of EOPC); duplication associated with >1.5-fold mRNA increase and high protein staining; independent prognostic biomarker for BCR validated in ~12k TMA cores; correlates with high GS, pT, Ki-67, and PSA; also enriched in PEPCI-high tumors (OR = 15.7).
- [KLF5](../genes/KLF5.md) — recurrent 13q22 loss (27% of EOPC); loss decreases KLF5 mRNA and increases global SV/SNV burden; epigenetic regulation via a CpG-island-shore site; co-expression with [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md) mRNA (independent of SPOP mutation status).
- [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md) — direct KLF5 ChIP-seq target; mRNA correlates with KLF5 mRNA; mutation status does not modify the correlation.
- [TMPRSS2](../genes/TMPRSS2.md)–[ERG](../genes/ERG.md) — ETS fusions occur in 70% of EOPC tumors; ERG is the most frequent initiating event in the PRESCIENT model; some TMPRSS2-ERG fusions arise from APOBEC-associated SV breakpoints.
- [FOXP1](../genes/FOXP1.md) — recurrent 3p14 SV (30% of EOPC); rarely biallelically inactivated (haploinsufficient); among the most likely second events after ERG.
- [NKX3-1](../genes/NKX3-1.md) — chr 8p SVs in 37% of EOPC; haploinsufficient tumor-suppressor pattern.
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — highest biallelic-inactivation rate (12 samples); some breakpoints co-localize with APOBEC C/G clusters; loss enriched in CC2-low/CC7-high tumors (p < 0.0001).
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — most frequent nonsynonymous SNV target in EOPC (6%); 8 biallelic inactivation events; parallel inactivation with [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) observed in subclones (PCA041).
- [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), [PALB2](../genes/PALB2.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md) — germline PTVs in these DDR genes drive elevated SV/SNV burden, APOBEC signature 2, and signature 3 (BRCAness); some BRCA2 disruptions in tumor genomes arise from APOBEC-associated SV breakpoints.
- [APOBEC3B](../genes/APOBEC3B.md) — endogenous mutagen behind clock-like APOBEC signatures (2 and 13) and kataegis events; germline *APOBEC3B* deletion shifts mutagenesis toward [APOBEC3A](../genes/APOBEC3A.md)-like patterns.
- [MAP3K7](../genes/MAP3K7.md) — discussed as a known PC SV-associated tumor suppressor (context only; not a primary finding of this paper).
- [AR](../genes/AR.md) — invoked as the source of fusion-driving promoters in ETS fusions and as part of an AR-signaling pathway-level event downstream of ETS initiation in PRESCIENT.
- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) — proximal to (but not the target of) the 8q22 ESRP1 duplications; ESRP1, not MYC, drives the mRNA increase across these duplications.
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — lost on one branch of the aggressive multi-region tumor PCA041.

## Clinical implications

- **ESRP1 as an independent prognostic biomarker.** Strong ESRP1 IHC staining predicts shorter time to BCR independent of ERG status and four established clinico-pathological parameters; particularly useful in the biopsy setting where GS is unreliable.
- **PEPCI methylation score for risk stratification.** Stratifies intermediate-risk GS7 (especially GS 4+3) into prognostically distinct groups; validated in TCGA.
- **Four molecular subgroups improve BCR prediction over GS alone.** Subgroup membership adds significant information over GS in both this cohort (p = 0.003) and TCGA (p = 0.01); subgroup 4 (PEPCI-low, basal/luminal-like) is enriched in EOPC and tracks with better prognosis.
- **PRESCIENT — single-biopsy prediction of next event and event-free survival.** Demonstrates feasibility of using a probabilistic network model on a single sample to forecast clinical trajectory, with multi-region WGS used to validate that the dominant clone is the one with the shorter predicted PEFS.
- **Targeted-therapy matching for EOPC.** >40% of EOPC patients matched a PI3K, mTOR, or PARP inhibitor at pre-clinical/clinical-trial stage; *BRCA2*-mutated patients with high signature-3 burden are candidates for PARP inhibitors.

## Limitations & open questions

- PRESCIENT is currently limited by cohort size; the authors note that incorporating more samples (especially aggressive LOPC and metastatic disease) is needed to improve sensitivity and specificity for event-free-survival prediction and to enable forecasting of secondary, metastasis-associated alterations.
- Whether the four molecular subgroups predict differential therapy response remains untested.
- ESRP1 has been linked to RAF-fusion formation in PC elsewhere, but no ESRP1 fusion events were detected in this cohort — the mechanistic link between ESRP1 dosage gain and aggressive phenotype (EMT vs. accelerated epithelial proliferation in the E-cadherin-coexpressed state) is not fully resolved.
- The APOBEC3B "clock-like" model relies on cross-sectional associations with age plus germline-deletion-carrier comparisons; longitudinal observation of normal prostate would be needed to directly demonstrate that A3B mutagenesis pre-dates transformation.
- Single-biopsy PRESCIENT predictions converge well in the cases shown (e.g., PCA035 T5), but the paper does not quantify per-region disagreement rates across all multi-region tumors.
