# TNR

## Overview

TNR (Tenascin R) is an extracellular matrix glycoprotein-encoding gene. In cancer genomics, TNR has been identified as a fusion partner in [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) structural rearrangements in pediatric tumors. The TP53-TNR fusion has been described as a diagnostic-only finding (present at diagnosis but lost at relapse), indicating it may mark an initiating structural event rather than driving relapse-phase biology.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- TP53-TNR fusion identified as a diagnostic-only structural rearrangement in the PPTC 261-model pediatric cancer PDX genomic study; the fusion was present at diagnosis but lost in the paired relapse PDX, suggesting it arose from a secondary event after an alternative initiating alteration [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[OS](../cancer_types/OS.md)** / **[WT](../cancer_types/WT.md)**: TP53-TNR (and TP53-FXR2) intron-1 fusion breakpoints identified in pediatric solid tumors, consistent with TP53 inactivation as a recurrent osteosarcoma hallmark [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- TP53-TNR fusion co-occurs with TP53 pathway inactivation in pediatric tumors; classified as a TP53 intron-1 structural rearrangement alongside the TP53-FXR2 fusion observed in Wilms tumor model KT-9 [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic relevance established for TNR itself; the TP53-TNR fusion serves as a diagnostic marker of TP53 pathway inactivation in pediatric cancers [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md).

## Open questions

- Whether TP53-TNR fusions are recurrent across larger pediatric sarcoma cohorts beyond the PPTC PDX panel requires further investigation; the fusion's presence at diagnosis and absence at relapse suggests complex clonal dynamics [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md).
