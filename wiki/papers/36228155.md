**PMID:** [36228155](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36228155/) · **DOI:** [10.1158/1078-0432.CCR-22-1954](https://doi.org/10.1158/1078-0432.CCR-22-1954) · **Journal:** Clinical Cancer Research (2022)

## TL;DR

This study provides the largest single-institution genomic characterization of gallbladder carcinoma ([GBC](../cancer_types/GBC.md)) to date, profiling 244 samples from 233 patients using MSK-IMPACT. The most frequent oncogenic alterations were in [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (63%), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) (21%), [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) (19%), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) (18%), and [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) (15%). SMAD4 and [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) mutations independently associated with reduced overall survival in metastatic disease. Clinically actionable alterations (OncoKB levels 1, 3A, or 3B) were identified in 35% of patients, and 18% of metastatic patients received biomarker-directed therapy or enrolled in clinical trials based on molecular findings.

## Cohort & data

- **233 patients / 244 samples** of [GBC](../cancer_types/GBC.md), collected 2014--2021 at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center.
- 57% primary tumors, 43% metastases; 85% adenocarcinomas, 10% carcinomas with squamous differentiation, 5% neuroendocrine carcinomas.
- Median age at collection: 66 years (range 37--90); 63% Caucasian, 12% African American, 12% Asian.
- 67% stage IV, 18% stage III at time of collection.
- Profiled with [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) (341--505 gene panel versions); dataset deposited in cBioPortal as [gbc_mskcc_2022](../datasets/gbc_mskcc_2022.md).
- Median sequencing coverage: 634X (range 72X--1150X).
- Microsatellite instability assessed via [MSIsensor](../methods/msisensor.md), MiMSI, and IDYLLA MSI; variants annotated with [OncoKB](../methods/oncokb.md).

## Key findings

- **Most frequent oncogenic mutations:** [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) 63% (all LOF), [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) 19% (16% LOF), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) 18% (all LOF), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) 9% (all GOF), [ELF3](../genes/ELF3.md) 9% (all LOF), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) 9% (8% LOF), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) 7% (all GOF), [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) 6% (all GOF), [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md) 6% (all LOF), [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) 6%, [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) 6% (all GOF), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) 6% (all LOF), [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter 6% (all GOF), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) 3.5% (all LOF).
- **Most frequent oncogenic CNAs:** [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)/[CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md) deletions (14% each), [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) amplification (11%), [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) amplification (10%), [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md) amplification (9%), [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) amplification (7%), [ERBB3](../genes/ERBB3.md) amplification (5%), [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) amplification (5%), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) amplification (4%).
- **No recurrent structural variants** were identified. Notable oncogenic SVs included an [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)::[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md) in-frame fusion (GOF) and LMNA::[NTRK1](../genes/NTRK1.md) fusion (OncoKB level 1).
- **Oncodrive clustering** identified [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) (p.S45F/P), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) (p.G12A/C/D/R), and [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) (p.S310F/Y) as potential molecular drivers in GBC.
- **Most commonly altered pathways:** TP53 (74%), Cell Cycle (46%), RTK-RAS (46%), Epigenetic (40%), TGF-Beta (28%), NOTCH (9%).
- **Histology--genomics correlations:** [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) LOF enriched in small cell neuroendocrine carcinoma (q<0.001); [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) pathway alterations higher in carcinomas with squamous differentiation (26% vs. 8% in adenocarcinoma, q=0.07).
- No significant differences in molecular landscape between primary and metastatic samples.
- 6 tumors (3%) were MSI-High.
- **Dominant mutational signatures** (in TMB-high cases): APOBEC (n=3) and MMR (n=3).
- **Microbiome:** Metastatic lesions had higher Shannon diversity than primaries. No Helicobacter or Salmonella reads were detected despite their association with GBC in endemic regions.

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) -- LOF mutations in 63% of GBC; most frequently altered gene. Part of the TP53 pathway altered in 74% of samples.
- [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) -- LOF mutations (19%) and deletions (5%); independently associated with reduced [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) in metastatic disease (HR 2.17, 95% CI 1.21--3.89, q=0.10; multivariate HR 2.11, p=0.012).
- [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) -- LOF mutations in 6%; independently associated with reduced OS in metastatic disease (HR 3.94, 95% CI 1.62--9.55, q=0.05; multivariate HR 3.76, p=0.004).
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) -- GOF mutations (6%) and amplifications (10%); amplification is OncoKB level 3B actionable; 10 patients received HER2-directed therapy.
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) -- GOF mutations in 9%; actionable at OncoKB level 3B (e.g., p.E545K, p.H1047R).
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) -- GOF mutations (7%) and amplifications (4%); p.G12C present in 2 patients (OncoKB level 3A).
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)/[CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md) -- Deletions in 14% each; mutations in CDKN2A 9%.
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) -- LOF mutations in 18%.
- [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md) -- LOF mutations in 6%; structural variants in 2 samples.
- [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) -- GOF mutations in 6%; identified as potential driver by oncodrive clustering (p.S45F/P). Higher frequency in primaries without cholelithiasis (22% vs 4.3%).
- [ELF3](../genes/ELF3.md) -- LOF mutations in 9%.
- [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) -- Amplification in 11%.
- [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md) -- Amplification in 9%.
- [ERBB3](../genes/ERBB3.md) -- Amplification in 5%; mutations more frequent in T1/T2 primaries (16% vs 0%).
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) -- LOF mutations in 3.5%; enriched in small cell neuroendocrine carcinoma (q<0.001).
- [NTRK1](../genes/NTRK1.md) -- LMNA::[NTRK1](../genes/NTRK1.md) fusion in 1 patient (OncoKB level 1; FDA-recognized biomarker).
- [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md)/[BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) -- Oncogenic mutations in 3 and 6 patients respectively (level 3B).
- [CDK12](../genes/CDK12.md) -- Structural variants in 3 patients including fusions (level 3B).
- [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) -- [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)::TACC3 in-frame fusion (GOF) in 1 patient.
- [RET](../genes/RET.md) -- Structural variants in 2 patients including [RET](../genes/RET.md) fusions (level 3B).
- [ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md) -- [ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md)::PARK2 fusion (likely GOF) in 1 patient.
- [MET](../genes/MET.md) -- Amplification in 5 patients (level 3B).
- [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) -- Oncogenic mutations in 5 patients (level 3B).

## Clinical implications

- **Actionable alterations:** 35.2% of patients harbored at least one mutation or structural variant considered actionable in GBC or other solid tumors (OncoKB levels 1, 3A, or 3B).
- **NTRK1 fusions** (level 1): FDA-recognized biomarker predictive of response to [entrectinib](../drugs/entrectinib.md) and [larotrectinib](../drugs/larotrectinib.md) across solid tumor types.
- **[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) G12C** (level 3A): Present in 2 patients (1%); promising response rates in biliary tract tumors in phase II trials.
- **HER2-directed therapy:** 10 metastatic patients received [trastuzumab](../drugs/trastuzumab.md)-based regimens targeting [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) amplification/mutation (level 3B).
- **PARP inhibitors:** 1 patient treated with [olaparib](../drugs/olaparib.md) targeting BRCA/ATM alterations.
- **Checkpoint inhibitors:** 12 metastatic patients (8%) received ICIs ([pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md) n=9, [atezolizumab](../drugs/atezolizumab.md) n=1, [nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md) n=1, [ipilimumab](../drugs/ipilimumab.md)/nivolumab n=1); 42% (5/12) showed evidence of response (3 MSI-High, 1 MSS/TMB-high, 1 MSS/TMB-low).
- **Prognostic biomarkers:** [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) and [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) mutations independently predicted reduced OS in metastatic GBC on multivariate analysis.
- **Standard first-line:** 82% of patients received first-line chemotherapy for advanced disease; 143 patients received [gemcitabine](../drugs/gemcitabine.md)/platinum (mostly [cisplatin](../drugs/cisplatin.md)).
- 18% of metastatic patients received biomarker-directed targeted therapy or enrolled in clinical trials based on molecular findings.

## Limitations & open questions

- Single-institution cohort (MSK) with patients primarily from the continental United States, limiting geographic and environmental diversity analysis despite GBC being endemic in parts of South America and Asia.
- Low number of neuroendocrine histology tumors (n=11) limits power for subtype-specific molecular analyses.
- Cholelithiasis data was missing for 49% of primary tumor cases, limiting power for correlations with [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) and [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) alterations.
- The absence of Helicobacter and Salmonella in the microbiome contrasts with reports from endemic regions, but the MSK-IMPACT microbiome pipeline is an opportunistic analysis of off-target reads rather than a dedicated metagenomic assay.
- ERBB2/ERBB3 and TP53 alterations did not associate with clinical outcomes in this cohort, potentially confounded by HER2-directed therapy use.
- No recurrent structural variants were found, possibly reflecting the rarity of targetable fusions in GBC compared to cholangiocarcinoma (which harbors [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) fusions and [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) mutations at higher rates).
- Response to targeted therapy was assessed by treating physician based on serial radiology rather than RECIST criteria.
