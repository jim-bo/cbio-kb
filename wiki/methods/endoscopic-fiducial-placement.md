# Endoscopic Fiducial Placement

## Overview

Endoscopic fiducial placement is a procedure in which radiopaque gold fiducial markers are implanted into or adjacent to a pancreatic tumour under endoscopic ultrasound (EUS) guidance. The fiducials serve as surrogates for the tumour position in real-time X-ray or CBCT image guidance during SBRT delivery, enabling precise tumour tracking and tighter treatment margins.

## Used by

- Endoscopic (EUS-guided) fiducial placement is described as a standard pre-treatment procedure in pancreatic SBRT protocols reviewed across nine prospective trials in locally advanced pancreatic cancer; fiducials enable real-time image-guided tumour tracking during delivery on CyberKnife (Synchrony module) and linac-based platforms. [PMID:27826200](../papers/27826200.md)

## Notes

- Performed under endoscopic ultrasound (EUS) guidance using a 22-gauge needle to deploy gold seed fiducials (typically 0.8 mm × 3 mm or 1.2 mm × 3 mm) into the peritumoral pancreatic parenchyma.
- Required for CyberKnife Synchrony respiratory tracking; also used for CBCT-based IGRT on linac platforms.
- Complication rate is low (<5%) in experienced centres; rare risks include pancreatitis and fiducial migration.
- Fiducial visibility on kV X-ray or CBCT is the basis for real-time tracking adjustments during each SBRT fraction.
