# CDK9

## Overview

CDK9 (Cyclin-Dependent Kinase 9) is a transcriptional CDK that, as part of the P-TEFb complex, phosphorylates RNA polymerase II to promote transcriptional elongation. It is also implicated in DNA-damage response signaling. In glioblastoma, CDK9 protein and phosphorylation are elevated in TP53-mutant tumors, suggesting a role in the DNA-repair-response phenotype of these tumors.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- CDK9 protein and phosphorylation are elevated in TP53-mutant [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) tumors in the CPTAC proteogenomic cohort ([gbm_cptac_2021](../datasets/gbm_cptac_2021.md), n=99); co-elevated with [ATR](../genes/ATR.md) and [CDK2](../genes/CDK2.md) as part of the DNA-damage-response phosphosite upregulation signature specific to TP53-mutant tumors [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **Glioblastoma (GBM):** CDK9 elevated at protein/phospho level in TP53-mutant tumors (n=99 CPTAC cohort); part of DNA-repair-response signaling axis [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- CDK9 elevation co-occurs with ATR and CDK2 kinase upregulation in TP53-mutant GBM [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Elevated CDK9 in TP53-mutant GBM contributes to the nomination of CDK inhibitors as candidates for this subtype; hypothesis-generating from phosphoproteomic data, not clinically validated [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Open questions

- The specific functional role of CDK9 elevation (transcriptional elongation vs. DNA-repair) in TP53-mutant GBM, and whether CDK9 inhibition is a viable therapeutic strategy, remain open [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).
