# XRCC4

## Overview

XRCC4 (X-Ray Repair Cross Complementing 4) encodes a component of the non-homologous end-joining (NHEJ) DNA double-strand break repair complex. In salivary gland adenoid cystic carcinoma (ACC), XRCC4 has been identified as a novel 5'-NFIB fusion partner, implicating NHEJ machinery in the chromosomal rearrangements that drive ACC oncogenesis.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Novel 5'-NFIB fusion partner in t(6;9)-positive/MYB-NFIB-negative ACC tumors; XRCC4 fuses its 5' end (exons 1–2) with [NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) in salivary adenoid cystic carcinoma [PMID:26631609](../papers/26631609.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md)**: Novel gene fusion (XRCC4-NFIB) identified in MYB-NFIB-negative ACC; part of a panel of rare alternative [NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) fusion partners [PMID:26631609](../papers/26631609.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- XRCC4-NFIB fusion occurs in t(6;9)-positive tumors that lack the canonical MYB-NFIB fusion, placing it within the MYB-driven ACC molecular subclass [PMID:26631609](../papers/26631609.md)
