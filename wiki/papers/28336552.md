**PMID:** 28336552 · **DOI:** 10.1158/2159-8290.CD-16-1337 · **Journal:** Cancer Discovery (2017)

## TL;DR

Jordan et al. report the first 860 patients with recurrent or metastatic [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) prospectively profiled by the [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) hybrid-capture NGS assay (versions [IMPACT341](../methods/IMPACT341.md) and [IMPACT410](../methods/IMPACT410.md)) at Memorial Sloan Kettering between January 2014 and March 2016 ([PMID:28336552](../papers/28336552.md), study `lung_msk_2017`). Potentially actionable somatic alterations were identified in 86.9% of patients and stratified into OncoKB levels 1–4 by strength of supporting evidence. Overall 37.1% (319/860) received a molecularly matched therapy; excluding standard-of-care matches (sensitizing [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) mutations, [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) and [ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md) fusions), only 14.4% (69/478) received matched therapy, with 52% of those deriving clinical benefit. Among 103 patients without any level 1–4 driver ("unknown mitogenic driver", UMD), [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) and [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md) were significantly enriched and nominated as candidate targetable mitogenic drivers.

## Cohort & data

- **Patients:** 860 patients with recurrent or metastatic lung adenocarcinoma ([LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md)); 915 tumors profiled.
- **Dataset:** [lung_msk_2017](../datasets/lung_msk_2017.md) — clinical and genomic data deposited in cBioPortal.
- **Assay:** [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) matched tumor:normal hybrid-capture NGS — [IMPACT341](../methods/IMPACT341.md) (341 genes) for 296 samples; [IMPACT410](../methods/IMPACT410.md) (410 genes) for 619 samples. Average coverage 615X; matched normal available for 97% of samples.
- **Demographics (Table 1):** women 58.8% (506); men 41.2% (354); age 51–75 years 71.5%; never smokers 32.2% (277); former heavy smokers (>15 pack-years) 48.8% (420); 8.4% Asian.
- **Tissue origin:** lung 420, lymph node 169, pleura/pleural fluid 110, liver 59, brain 47, bone 29.
- **Sample timing:** median 28 days from collection to genomic analysis (range 0–3274); 89% used tissue ≤1 year old; mean turnaround 17 days from receipt to report.
- **Comparator cohort:** retrospective TCGA [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) ([luad_tcga_pub](../datasets/luad_tcga_pub.md), [PMID:25079552](../papers/25079552.md)) used as reference for treatment-naïve, primary-resection LUAD.
- **Tumor purity in UMD subset:** estimated via [FACETS](../methods/facets.md) allele-specific copy number analysis (range 12–92%, mean 35%).
- **Trial registration:** NCT01775072 (genomic testing protocol); subsequent basket trials NCT02201212, NCT02675829, NCT02352844.

## Key findings

- **Actionability rate:** Potentially actionable OncoKB level 1–4 alterations in 747/860 patients (86.9%) ([PMID:28336552](../papers/28336552.md), Figure 1A).
- **Matched therapy uptake by evidence level:** 92% (level 1), 52% (level 2A), 17% (level 2B), 25% (level 3), 2% (level 4) — strongly correlated with level of evidence (p<0.0001, Cochran-Armitage trend, Figure 2A).
- **Sensitizing [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) mutations:** 95.3% received matched therapy with clinical benefit in 84.8%; [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) fusions 90.9% / 93.3%; [ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md) fusions 59.1% / 84.6%.
- **Level 2A drivers:** [MET](../genes/MET.md) exon 14 alterations matched in 65.4% (76.5% benefit), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E 55.6% (75% benefit), [RET](../genes/RET.md) fusions 53.3% (72.7% benefit), wild-type [MET](../genes/MET.md) amplification 16.7% (50% benefit).
- **Cohort enrichment vs TCGA:** activating [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) alterations 27% vs 11% (p<0.001); [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) T790M 5.5% (47/860) vs 0.4% (1/230) in TCGA (p<0.001), all post-EGFR-TKI. [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) truncating/deletion lower at 2% vs 8.3% (p<0.001); [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) mutations 3.6% vs 7% (p=0.042) — reflecting referral bias and a more clinically aggressive, treatment-experienced cohort.
- **Co-occurring actionable alterations:** 239 patients (27.8%) had ≥2 level 1–4 alterations; 46 had ≥3; 52 had co-occurring level 1–3 mutations (25 with concurrent [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md)+[PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)); no patient was treated on a trial that simultaneously targeted two actionable alterations.
- **[EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md)/[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) mutual exclusivity:** Only 2/214 EGFR-mutant tumors had concurrent [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) mutations (p<0.0001); allele frequencies in both cases suggested subclonal KRAS-mutant populations.
- **PI3K pathway as highest-level driver:** 32/860 (3.7%) — 17 [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), 6 inactivating [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), 3 each [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md)/[TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md), 2 [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) E17K, 1 [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md) S2215Y; 9/17 PIK3CA-driver tumors co-harbored a [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) mutation.
- **Rare actionable drivers:** 2 [ARAF](../genes/ARAF.md) S214Y/S214P (sorafenib-sensitive), 2 [RAF1](../genes/RAF1.md) S257L, 1 [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), 10 [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) (9 Q61, 1 G13), 6 [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md) (E203K/K57N/Q56P/G128V/E102_I103del), 1 [CD74](../genes/CD74.md)-[NRG1](../genes/NRG1.md) fusion, 1 [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)-[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md) fusion, 1 [KIT](../genes/KIT.md) E490Q, 1 [ERBB3](../genes/ERBB3.md) D297H.
- **[EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) allele-specific response:** clinical benefit 87.3% overall in sensitizing [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md), but significantly lower for L861Q (43%; p=0.039 vs L858R; p=0.01 vs exon 19 del) and exon 18 deletions (40%; p=0.02 vs L858R; p=0.005 vs exon 19 del). Both [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md)-kinase domain duplication (EGFR-KDD) patients (treated with [erlotinib](../drugs/erlotinib.md) and [afatinib](../drugs/afatinib.md)) derived clinical benefit.
- **[EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) exon 20 insertions:** 17 patients identified; one treated with [erlotinib](../drugs/erlotinib.md) had no response, consistent with prior data.
- **UMD cohort (n=103, 12.0% of patients):** alterations in [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [STK11](../genes/STK11.md), [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), and [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md) significantly enriched (p<0.05) vs level 1–4 cohort. In never/former-light smokers within UMD: chromatin modifiers ([KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md), [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md), [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md)) and homologous recombination genes ([MRE11](../genes/MRE11.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md)) enriched. Heavy smokers within UMD: [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) elevated. Unlike TCGA, no [RIT1](../genes/RIT1.md) mutations in UMD.
- **[KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md) in UMD:** altered in 35/103 (34%) UMD samples (10 truncations, 25 missense); 3D structural analysis localized missense variants to the Kelch domain that interacts with NFE2L2/Nrf2.
- **Level 3/4 patients and immunotherapy:** Only 7% (26/373) of level 3/4 patients enrolled on a matched-therapy trial, but 19% (70/373) enrolled on a therapeutic trial; 62.9% (44/70) on immunotherapy trials. Inverse trend toward higher mutational load in level 3/4 vs level 1/2 patients (Supplementary Figure S7), driven by smoking history.

## Genes & alterations

- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) — 214 (24.9%) patients with sensitizing alleles; exon 19 del/ins (113) and L858R (70) most common; rarer activating: L861Q (7), E709_T710delinsD (6), G719A (4), exon 18–25 kinase domain duplication (2). 17 exon 20 insertions and 1 H773R were resistance-associated. T790M in 5.5% (47/860), all post-TKI. Allele-specific response data support [afatinib](../drugs/afatinib.md)/[osimertinib](../drugs/osimertinib.md) over first-generation TKIs for L861Q.
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — 218 patients with [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) mutations; only 0.9% (2/218) received matched therapy. Codon 12 dominant; codon 61 in 12/235 (5%) of KRAS-mutated tumors. Mutually exclusive with [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) (p<0.0001).
- [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) and [ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md) — fusions; level 1; high matched-therapy uptake and benefit. Two ROS1-fusion patients died before [crizotinib](../drugs/crizotinib.md) FDA-approval (March 2016) for that indication.
- [RET](../genes/RET.md) — fusions in 1.7–3.8% range; level 2A; 53.3% matched therapy with 72.7% benefit; [cabozantinib](../drugs/cabozantinib.md) cited as matched agent.
- [MET](../genes/MET.md) — exon 14 alterations 65.4% matched (76.5% benefit); wild-type [MET](../genes/MET.md) amplification 16.7% matched (50% benefit).
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — V600E (level 2A) 55.6% matched / 75% benefit. Non-V600E alleles (K601E, D594G, T599 dup, SND1-BRAF fusion): 4/13 received matched therapy (single-agent MEK/ERK inhibitor or [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)+MEK combo for the [SND1](../genes/SND1.md)-BRAF fusion); none derived clinical benefit.
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) — amplification (level 2B) 5/12 (42%) matched; 1 with clinical benefit. [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) mutation (level 3): 50% matched, 40% benefit.
- [ERBB3](../genes/ERBB3.md) — D297H hotspot in 1 patient.
- [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) — truncating/deletion in 16 patients (level 4); enriched in TCGA cohort (8.3% vs 2%, p<0.001).
- [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) — 10 patients (9 Q61, 1 G13).
- [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) — 1 patient.
- [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md) (MEK1) — 6 patients with activating alleles (E203K, K57N, Q56P, G128V, E102_I103del); none received matched therapy as highest-level alteration.
- [ARAF](../genes/ARAF.md) — 2 hotspot S214Y/S214P (sorafenib-sensitive in prior preclinical work).
- [RAF1](../genes/RAF1.md) — 2 S257L hotspot.
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — 17 patients with [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) as highest-level driver; 25 [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md)+PIK3CA co-mutations across the cohort.
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — 6 inactivating.
- [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md), [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) — 3 patients each with truncating alterations (level 2B); none received matched therapy.
- [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) — 2 E17K mutations; 1 of 2 derived 12-month clinical benefit on matched therapy.
- [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md) — 1 S2215Y as highest driver; 1 UMD patient with novel L2383F missense had 1-month no-benefit course on [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md).
- [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) — 3 [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) (0.3%) and 8 [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) (0.9%) likely-inactivating truncating mutations; level 2B based on [olaparib](../drugs/olaparib.md) approval in BRCA-mutant ovarian carcinoma; no patient received matched PARP-inhibitor therapy.
- [KIT](../genes/KIT.md) — 1 exon 9 E490Q (previously described in thymic carcinoma).
- [CD74](../genes/CD74.md)–[NRG1](../genes/NRG1.md) fusion — 1 patient.
- [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)–[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md) fusion — 1 patient.
- [SND1](../genes/SND1.md)–[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) fusion — 1 patient (treated with BRAF+MEK combo, no benefit).
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [STK11](../genes/STK11.md), [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md) — significantly enriched in UMD cohort (p<0.05). [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md) missense variants cluster in the Kelch domain interacting with NFE2L2/Nrf2.
- [NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md) (NRF2) — mutations in 2.9% (3/103) of UMD samples.
- [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md), [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md), [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md) — chromatin modifiers enriched in UMD (especially never/former-light smokers).
- [MRE11](../genes/MRE11.md) — homologous recombination gene enriched in never/former-light smoking UMD subset (paper writes "MRE11A"; current HUGO symbol is [MRE11](../genes/MRE11.md)).
- [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md) — significantly enriched in UMD vs level 1–4.
- [ERBB4](../genes/ERBB4.md) — alterations identified in UMD subset, but none were hotspot or HER-kinase-inhibitor-sensitivity-conferring variants.
- [ERRFI1](../genes/ERRFI1.md) (MIG6) — A143D missense in 1 UMD patient with [erlotinib](../drugs/erlotinib.md) benefit despite no EGFR mutation; [ERRFI1](../genes/ERRFI1.md) mutations identified in 6 (0.7%) total, 4 with co-occurring higher-level alterations (2 EGFR exon 19 del; 2 KRAS G12).
- [KDM5C](../genes/KDM5C.md) — frameshift in 1 UMD patient with 6-month stable disease on off-label [azacitidine](../drugs/azacitidine.md).
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — deletions identified; no patient with [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) deletion as highest actionable alteration received matched therapy.
- [RIT1](../genes/RIT1.md) — notably absent from this UMD cohort despite enrichment in the TCGA UMD set.

## Clinical implications

- **Standard-of-care biomarkers (level 1) deliver:** 93% of patients with sensitizing [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) mutations or [ALK](../genes/ALK.md)/[ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md) fusions received corresponding matched therapy and 85.8% derived clinical benefit, supporting universal upfront genotyping for these lesions in advanced [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md).
- **Broaden screening to level 2A drivers:** Although level 2A matched-therapy uptake was only 52%, clinical benefit when treated was 76% — an argument for routine, early testing for [MET](../genes/MET.md) exon 14, [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E, and [RET](../genes/RET.md) fusions rather than waiting for sequential single-gene assays.
- **EGFR allele matters:** L861Q and exon 18 deletions had significantly lower clinical-benefit rates with first-generation TKIs ([erlotinib](../drugs/erlotinib.md)), supporting clinical evaluation of [afatinib](../drugs/afatinib.md), [osimertinib](../drugs/osimertinib.md), and [dacomitinib](../drugs/dacomitinib.md) for these alleles. EGFR exon 20 insertions remained TKI-resistant.
- **Off-label barriers limit level 2B uptake:** Despite FDA-approval of [olaparib](../drugs/olaparib.md) in BRCA-mutant ovarian and HER2-directed agents and mTOR inhibitors in other indications, no patient with [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md)/[BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) or [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md)/[TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) truncating mutations received matched therapy in this cohort. Authors opened basket trials NCT02201212 and NCT02675829 to address this gap.
- **Immunotherapy is the de facto matched option for level 3/4 patients:** With higher mutational load (driven by smoking) and lacking compelling matched therapy, 62.9% of level 3/4 patients enrolled on therapeutic trials received immunotherapy.
- **UMD nominates [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) and [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md) as targetable drivers:** Both significantly enriched in UMD vs level 1–4 cohort, consistent with TCGA findings ([PMID:25079552](../papers/25079552.md)). Authors flag KEAP1 Kelch-domain-clustering missense variants and propose testing Nrf2 inhibitors (luteolin, brusatol) as chemosensitizers in future trials.
- **No combination matched therapy was administered** despite 27.8% of patients having ≥2 actionable alterations — a recognized gap requiring novel trial designs.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Cohort selection bias:** MSK referral pattern enriches for [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) mutants (27% vs TCGA 11%) and [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) T790M (5.5% vs 0.4%); 32.2% never smokers and 8.4% Asian over-represent specific demographics relative to TCGA.
- **Treatment-experienced cohort:** 37.7% of samples were post at least one prior systemic therapy, potentially biasing detection of resistance alleles and limiting comparison to treatment-naïve cohorts.
- **No combinatorial matched therapy:** No patient received simultaneous matched therapy for two actionable alterations even though 239 (27.8%) had multiple actionable lesions — limits inference on combination strategies.
- **Clinical-benefit definition is imaging+symptom-based:** Stable disease on two consecutive imaging scans ≥30 days apart with symptom improvement counts as benefit; no formal RECIST PFS/OS reported per arm.
- **Variants of uncertain significance:** All UMD [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) mutations were somatic missense VUS; the [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md) L2383F UMD variant was novel and lacked functional validation.
- **Panel evolution within study:** Two MSK-IMPACT versions used in parallel ([IMPACT341](../methods/IMPACT341.md) for 296 samples; [IMPACT410](../methods/IMPACT410.md) for 619 samples) — alterations restricted to v1 panel were not testable in v2-only contexts and vice versa.
- **No fusion/expression analysis beyond panel scope:** Whole exome/genome/transcriptome sequencing might have identified additional actionable fusions or alterations in the 13.1% UMD cases.
- **Rapid clinical deterioration prevented matched therapy:** 11.3% (8/71) of level 2A patients deteriorated before matched therapy could be initiated, suggesting a benefit to upfront (rather than progression-driven) testing.
- **No prospective validation of [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) as predictive biomarker:** Authors note no clinical trials test [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) as a predictive biomarker of mTOR-inhibitor response in lung cancer; NCT02352844 is a phase 2 in patients with TSC1/2, NF1/2, or STK11 mutations across solid tumors but specifically lung-restricted data are pending.
