Molecular subtyping and genomic profiling expand precision medicine in refractory metastatic triple-negative breast cancer: the FUTURE trial

TL;DR

The FUTURE trial (NCT03805399) was a phase Ib/II, single-center, open-label umbrella trial conducted at Fudan University Shanghai Cancer Center that combined immunohistochemistry-based TNBC molecular subtyping (LAR / IM / BLIS / MES) with a 484-gene FUSCC targeted NGS panel to assign 69 refractory metastatic triple-negative breast cancer patients (median 3 prior lines of therapy, range 1–8) to one of seven biomarker-matched treatment arms. The intention-to-treat objective response rate was 29.0% (20/69; 95% CI 18.7%–41.2%); the anti-PD-1 (camrelizumab/SHR-1210) plus nab-paclitaxel arm for the immunomodulatory (IM) subtype achieved an ORR of 52.6% (10/19; 95% CI 28.9%–75.6%), and the anti-VEGFR (apatinib or famitinib) arm for BRCA1/2-wildtype BLIS-subtype patients achieved 26.1% (6/23; 95% CI 10.2%–48.4%) but with substantial high-grade toxicity. Exploratory analyses nominated somatic TOP2A mutation and CD8 IHC score as candidate predictors of immunotherapy response in IM-subtype TNBC.

Cohort & data

  • 69 enrolled patients with refractory metastatic BRCA (triple-negative subset) out of 87 screened, enrolled October 18, 2018 – March 25, 2020.
  • Heavily pretreated: median 3 prior antitumor regimens in the metastatic setting (range 1–8); ≥99% had received taxanes, 86% anthracyclines, 88% platinums, 81% vinorelbine, 75% capecitabine, 72% gemcitabine.
  • 81% had ≥2 metastatic organs; 67% progressed within 6 months of first-line chemotherapy.
  • Cohort linked to cBioPortal study brca_fuscc_2020.
  • Tumor biopsies at recurrence/metastasis profiled with the FUSCC 484-gene NGS panel (somatic and germline calling) plus IHC staining for AR, CD8, and FOXC1 as surrogates for the LAR/IM/BLIS subtypes.
  • Imaging assessed every 1–2 cycles by RECIST v1.1; median follow-up at data cutoff (April 7, 2020) was 9.2 months (IQR 5.3–12.2).

Key findings

  • Overall efficacy (ITT, n=69): ORR 29.0% (20/69; 95% CI 18.7%–41.2%); DCR 42.0% (29/69; 95% CI 30.2%–54.5%). PP population (n=50): ORR 40.0% (95% CI 26.4%–54.8%), DCR 58.0% (95% CI 43.2%–71.8%).
  • Treatment duration vs. prior therapy: in 18 patients with comparable data, median duration of FUTURE-trial therapy was 3.5 months vs. 2.4 months for their most recent prior therapy (P=0.02).
  • Arm C (anti-PD-1 SHR-1210 camrelizumab + nab-paclitaxel for IM subtype, n=19): ORR 52.6% (95% CI 28.9%–75.6%, ITT); 62.5% (10/16; 95% CI 35.4%–84.8%, PP). 10 PRs (62.5% of 16 evaluable patients) at first post-baseline evaluation; median DOR 3.1 months (range 1.0–9.1).
  • Arm E (anti-VEGFR apatinib or famitinib for BLIS without germline BRCA1/2 mutation, n=23): ORR 26.1% (95% CI 10.2%–48.4%, ITT) and 35.3% (95% CI 14.2%–61.7%, PP); 1 CR and 5 PR among 17 evaluable patients; median DOR 4.1 months (range 1.1–9.8).
  • Toxicity (arm E, apatinib 500 mg): grade ≥3 hypertension 22% (5/23), proteinuria 17% (4/23), hand–foot syndrome 17% (4/23); dose reduction in 6/15 apatinib-monotherapy patients. Investigators revised the regimen to apatinib 250 mg or famitinib 20 mg + VP-16 50 mg.
  • Other arms: arm A (pyrotinib + capecitabine, LAR with ERBB2 somatic mutation): 2/2 evaluable PRs after 2 cycles. Arm B (AR inhibitor SHR3680 + CDK4/6 inhibitor SHR6390 dalpiciclib, LAR without ERBB2 mutation): 1/8 SD; 7/8 progressed; all BLIS-arm-B tumors were CDKN2A-neutral, possibly reducing CDK4/6 inhibitor efficacy. Arm D (PARP inhibitor SH3162 fuzuloparib, BLIS with germline BRCA1/2): 3/3 evaluable patients progressed (3 were platinum-refractory). Arms F (anti-VEGFR famitinib, MES without PI3K-AKT mutation) and G (everolimus + nab-paclitaxel, MES with PI3K-AKT mutation): ORR 20% (1/5) and 50% (1/2), respectively.
  • Genomic landscape of refractory metastatic TNBC: most frequently mutated genes were TP53 (72%), PIK3CA (18%), PTEN (10%), KMT2D (9%), TSC2 (9%). Compared with primary TNBC, low-frequency mutated genes PTPRD, TSC2, PLCG1, ARID1B, CREBBP, and FAM47C were enriched in metastatic samples.
  • Race comparison: the FUSCC Chinese metastatic TNBC cohort showed a lower TP53 mutation frequency than the MSKCC American metastatic TNBC cohort (from Zehir et al. — PMID:28481359); no other significant differences were detected.
  • Predictors of immunotherapy response (arm C): TOP2A somatic mutations were detected in 2/6 non-PR patients but in 0 PR patients; the mutations fell in or near the topoisomerase 2-like DNA-binding domain (PTZ00108). Median CD8 IHC score was 30 in non-PD vs 22.5 in PD patients (P=0.25). Some PR patients had higher PD-L1 IHC scores than non-PR patients.
  • Resistance-mutation tendency: higher frequency of drug-resistance–related mutations (PTEN, RB1, NOTCH3) in PD patients of the whole cohort and arm E; trend toward higher FOXC1 IHC score in SD+PD vs CR+PR patients of arm E (P=0.26).
  • Anecdotal arm-B responses: one TNBC with FGFR1 amplification responded to anlotinib after PD on anti-AR + anti-CDK4/6; one AR(+) CD8(+, 25%) patient responded to anti-PD-1.

Genes & alterations

  • TP53 — mutated in 72% of refractory metastatic TNBC in the FUSCC cohort; significantly lower frequency than the MSKCC metastatic TNBC cohort.
  • PIK3CA — mutated in 18%; arm G enrolled MES-subtype TNBCs with PI3K-AKT pathway mutations for everolimus + nab-paclitaxel.
  • PTEN — mutated in 10%; flagged as part of a resistance-mutation tendency in arm E and whole-cohort PD patients.
  • KMT2D — mutated in 9%.
  • TSC2 — mutated in 9%; enriched in metastatic vs primary TNBC.
  • TOP2A — somatic mutations in 2/6 non-PR patients (and 0/10 PR patients) in arm C (anti-PD-1); mutations near the topoisomerase 2-like DNA-binding domain; nominated as a candidate negative predictor of ICI response in IM-subtype TNBC.
  • PTPRD, PLCG1, ARID1B, CREBBP, FAM47C — low-frequency genes enriched in metastatic vs primary TNBC samples.
  • ERBB2 — somatic mutations used as a stratifier for arm A (pyrotinib + capecitabine, LAR subtype).
  • BRCA1 / BRCA2 — germline mutation status stratified BLIS-subtype patients between PARP-inhibitor arm D and anti-VEGFR arm E.
  • CDKN2A — all arm B (anti-AR + CDK4/6i, LAR-without-ERBB2-mutation) tumors were CDKN2A-neutral, possibly reducing CDK4/6 inhibitor efficacy.
  • RB1, NOTCH3 — included among drug-resistance–related mutations more frequent in PD patients.
  • FGFR1 — one arm-B TNBC with FGFR1 amplification responded to anlotinib after progression on anti-AR + anti-CDK4/6.
  • AR, FOXC1 — used as IHC surrogate markers for LAR and BLIS subtypes, respectively; FOXC1 score trended higher in SD+PD vs CR+PR within arm E.

Clinical implications

  • Subtyping-based, biomarker-guided umbrella design is feasible in heavily pretreated metastatic TNBC and produced a 29.0% ITT ORR — favorable versus historical 5–10% ICI-monotherapy ORRs in similar populations.
  • Anti-PD-1 + nab-paclitaxel (camrelizumab + nab-paclitaxel) is a promising regimen for IM-subtype metastatic TNBC: 52.6% ITT ORR despite median 3 prior lines of therapy.
  • IHC-only TNBC subtyping (AR / CD8 / FOXC1) is a practical surrogate for mRNA subtyping and feasible in routine pathology workflow.
  • TOP2A mutation status and CD8 IHC score warrant prospective validation as predictive biomarkers of ICI response in IM-subtype TNBC.
  • Anti-VEGFR therapy (apatinib) in BLIS-subtype, BRCA1/2-wildtype TNBC produced responses but high-grade hypertension, proteinuria, and hand-foot syndrome led investigators to abandon apatinib 500 mg in favor of lower-dose apatinib or famitinib + etoposide combinations.
  • PARP inhibitor monotherapy (fuzuloparib) showed no responses in 3 platinum-refractory BLIS BRCA1/2-mutant patients in arm D, consistent with platinum-PARPi cross-resistance.
  • CDKN2A-neutral status across arm B tumors may explain the negative result of anti-AR + CDK4/6 inhibitor combination in LAR-subtype-without-ERBB2 patients; future LAR-arm designs should select for CDKN2A loss/alteration.

Limitations & open questions

  • Single-center, single-arm, small sample size: 69 patients across 7 arms; some arms (A: 2 evaluable, D: 3, F: 5, G: 2) too small to support strong conclusions.
  • Interim analysis: efficacy estimates may shift with full enrollment.
  • No central blinded review: response assessments were investigator-assessed by RECIST v1.1.
  • Predictive biomarkers (TOP2A mutation, CD8 IHC score) require independent validation; the CD8 IHC score comparison did not reach significance (P=0.25).
  • Functional impact of metastasis-enriched low-frequency genes (PTPRD, TSC2, PLCG1, ARID1B, CREBBP, FAM47C) is largely unknown and not directly tested in this trial.
  • Generalizability: all patients were Chinese, heavily pretreated (median 3 prior lines), and recruited at a single center; the FUSCC vs MSKCC TP53 frequency difference is unexplained.
  • Open question for cross-paper synthesis: does subtype-guided therapy assignment in first- or second-line metastatic TNBC produce comparable benefit to the salvage setting reported here?
