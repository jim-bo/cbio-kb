# mk-2206

## Overview

MK-2206 is an allosteric pan-AKT inhibitor that binds the pleckstrin homology (PH) domain of [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md), [AKT2](../genes/AKT2.md), and [AKT3](../genes/AKT3.md), locking the enzyme in an inactive conformation. Unlike ATP-competitive inhibitors (e.g., [gsk-690693](../drugs/gsk-690693.md)), its mechanism requires an intact PH domain on the target, limiting efficacy against AKT fusion proteins that lack this domain.

## Evidence in the corpus

- MK-2206 failed to inhibit GSK3β phosphorylation driven by the MAGI3-AKT3 fusion kinase in breast cancer, because the fusion protein lacks the PH domain required for allosteric inhibitor binding [PMID:22722202](../papers/22722202.md).
- In contrast, the ATP-competitive AKT inhibitor [gsk-690693](../drugs/gsk-690693.md) was effective against the same fusion, establishing a mechanistic basis for inhibitor class selection in fusion-positive tumors [PMID:22722202](../papers/22722202.md).
- In high-grade urothelial bladder carcinoma (n=97), PIK3CA-mutant and AKT1-mutant cell lines were selectively sensitive to MK-2206 while TSC1-null lines were resistant at the S6/4EBP1 level despite proximal AKT inhibition, mirroring KRAS-driven EGFR-therapy resistance [PMID:23897969](../papers/23897969.md).
- In PTEN-knockdown MCF7 cells (ER+ breast cancer), MK-2206 (2 µmol/L) reverses [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md) upregulation caused by AKT activation, linking PI3K/AKT pathway loss to CDK4/6i resistance; confirms AKT as the mechanistic intermediate upstream of [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md) in PTEN-null context. [PMID:34544752](../papers/34544752.md)
- In NF1-null HER2+ breast cancer cells, MK-2206 (AKT inhibitor) IC50 shifts from 197 nM (control) to 4,475 nM (~23-fold resistance), confirming pathway switch from PI3K/AKT to MEK/ERK as the resistance mechanism in MAPK-altered HER2+ disease. [PMID:34795269](../papers/34795269.md)
- PIK3R1-knockdown LAPC4 and 22RV1 prostate cancer cells showed enhanced sensitivity to MK-2206 at 100 nM; the PIK3R1-mutant organoid MSK-PCa3 (Asn564Asp) showed strong growth reduction at 10 nM, whereas the PIK3R1-wild-type, PTEN-deleted MSK-PCa1 was only modestly affected — supporting [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md) alteration as a biomarker for AKT-inhibitor eligibility [PMID:35670774](../papers/35670774.md).

## Resistance mechanisms

- Activating AKT fusions that delete or disrupt the PH domain (e.g., MAGI3-AKT3) are intrinsically resistant to PH-domain allosteric inhibitors including MK-2206 [PMID:22722202](../papers/22722202.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) — triple-negative subtype with MAGI3-AKT3 fusions (PH-domain loss confers allosteric inhibitor resistance).
