# topotecan

## Overview

Topotecan is a topoisomerase I inhibitor used in relapsed ovarian cancer, small-cell lung cancer, and pediatric solid tumors. In sarcoma it has been evaluated in functional drug screens as a single agent.

## Evidence in the corpus

- [Osteosarcoma](../cancer_types/OS.md) PDTOs from the UCLA functional screen were significantly more sensitive to topotecan than the pan-sarcoma average (p=0.028). [PMID:39305899](../papers/39305899.md)
- [LMS](../cancer_types/LMS.md) PDTOs were significantly less sensitive to topotecan than the pan-sarcoma average (p=0.031), consistent with prior negative clinical trial data in leiomyosarcoma. [PMID:39305899](../papers/39305899.md)
- Included in the CCLE pharmacogenomic screen across 947 cancer cell lines; sensitivity correlated with genomic features via [elastic-net](../methods/elastic-net.md) regression [PMID:22460905](../papers/22460905.md)
- Cross-resistance to topotecan observed in [SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md) chemoresistant PDX models; [SLFN11](../genes/SLFN11.md) re-expression via [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) inhibitor EPZ011989 re-sensitized cell lines to topotecan (Pearson r = 0.916 correlation between [SLFN11](../genes/SLFN11.md) re-expression and sensitization); ectopic SLFN11 was sufficient to sensitize NCI-H82 and NCI-H446 [PMID:28196596](../papers/28196596.md).
- Topotecan was used as a chemotherapy backbone in combination regimens within the MAPPYACTS trial for pediatric/young-adult recurrent malignancies [PMID:35292802](../papers/35292802.md)

## Resistance mechanisms

- Not reported in corpus.
