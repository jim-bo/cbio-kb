# RBL2

## Overview

RBL2 (RB transcriptional corepressor like 2), also known as p130, is a member of the retinoblastoma protein family involved in cell cycle control and differentiation. It represses E2F-dependent gene expression during quiescence. In small cell lung cancer, RBL2 undergoes inactivating translocations and mutations, and has been validated as an accessory tumour suppressor in mouse models (TKO mice show shorter SCLC latency when RBL2 is lost).

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Inactivating translocations and mutations in small cell lung cancer (SCLC); validated as an accessory tumour suppressor in TKO mice, where loss of RBL2 results in shorter SCLC latency. [PMID:26168399](../papers/26168399.md)
- Co-deleted with Rb1/Trp53 in murine triple-knockout (TKO) SCLC models used to corroborate findings on EZH2-SLFN11-mediated chemoresistance [PMID:28196596](../papers/28196596.md)
- Driver candidate discussed but not recurrently mutated in an 83-tumor retinoblastoma MSK-IMPACT cohort [PMID:33466343](../papers/33466343.md)
