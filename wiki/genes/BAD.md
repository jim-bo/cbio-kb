# BAD

## Overview

BAD ([BCL2](../genes/BCL2.md) associated agonist of cell death) is a pro-apoptotic member of the BCL-2 protein family. Its activity is regulated by phosphorylation: phosphorylation at S134 (and other sites) by kinases such as [PAK1](../genes/PAK1.md) sequesters BAD from its apoptotic targets, promoting cell survival. In cancer, BAD phosphorylation is a downstream readout of oncogenic kinase activity and a potential indicator of sensitivity to kinase inhibitor therapy.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- BAD S134 phosphorylation identified as a PAK1 kinase-substrate axis in CPTAC PDAC proteogenomics (140 treatment-naive tumors; 41 tumor/NAT pairs with kinase-substrate analysis); PAK1 was elevated in >70% of PDAC tumors and is a downstream effector of [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) via [RAC1](../genes/RAC1.md). [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **PDAC ([PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md)):** BAD S134 phosphorylation documented as part of the PAK1–BAD kinase-substrate signaling axis in the KRAS-driven PDAC proteogenomic landscape; elevated PAK1 activity in >70% of tumors supports widespread BAD phospho-regulation. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- PAK1–BAD (S134) axis co-activated with [PAK2](../genes/PAK2.md)–[MAPK6](../genes/MAPK6.md) (S189) axis and other FDA-approved drug-target kinase substrates ([CDK7](../genes/CDK7.md)–[MCM2](../genes/MCM2.md), [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md)–[FLNA](../genes/FLNA.md), [SRC](../genes/SRC.md)–[STAT3](../genes/STAT3.md)) in PDAC. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- BAD phosphorylation by PAK1 is a pro-survival signal in PDAC; combined PAK1/PAK2 inhibition with MAPK-ERK and PI3K-AKT-mTOR inhibitors proposed to overcome KRAS-driven survival signaling including the PAK1–BAD axis. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Open questions

- Whether BAD phospho-status (S134) has independent prognostic value in PDAC beyond PAK1 expression level is not established in the current dataset.
- Causal role of PAK1-mediated BAD phosphorylation versus other survival-signaling pathways downstream of KRAS has not been functionally validated.
