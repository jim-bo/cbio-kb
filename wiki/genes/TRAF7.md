# TRAF7

## Overview

TRAF7 is a RING domain E3 ubiquitin ligase that is a recurrent driver in the NF2-wildtype (MG2) molecular group of meningiomas.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- TRAF7 point mutations were identified in 25% of MG2 meningiomas (NF2-wildtype group); exclusive to MG2 and mutually exclusive with [NF2](../genes/NF2.md) mutations [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [MNG](../cancer_types/MNG.md) — point mutations in 25% of the NF2-wildtype MG2 molecular group; part of the TRAF7/AKT1/KLF4/SMO driver set [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Mutually exclusive with NF2 mutations; co-occurs with [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md), [KLF4](../genes/KLF4.md), and [SMO](../genes/SMO.md) mutations in MG2 meningiomas [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- MG2 tumors harboring TRAF7 mutations may be amenable to targeted therapies directed at downstream effectors; TRAF7 drives NF-kB and JNK pathway activation [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md).

## Open questions

- None flagged in the corpus beyond the need for independent validation of the MG2 molecular group classification [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md).
