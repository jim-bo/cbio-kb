# Cutaneous Squamous Cell Carcinoma (UOW, Front Oncol 2022)

## Overview

`cscc_ranson_2022` is the largest whole-genome sequencing (WGS) study of regional metastatic cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma ([CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md)) reported to date. Generated by the Ranson Lab at the University of Wollongong, it profiled 25 lymph node metastases from patients with head-and-neck [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md) alongside matched blood DNA using WGS (GRCh38), enabling genome-wide interrogation of both coding and non-coding driver regions — a capability absent from prior WES or targeted-panel [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md) cohorts. Raw data were deposited at the European Genome-Phenome Archive (EGAS00001006378) and somatic variant data published to cBioPortal.

## Composition

- **25 patients** with resectable regional metastatic [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md) of the head and neck (lymph node metastases) recruited 2015–2019 [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md).
- Median age 69 years (range 30–87); 24/25 (96%) male; 2/25 immunosuppressed.
- Nodal stage predominantly N3b; metastases in neck (n=13) or parotid (n=12); 20/25 (80%) with extranodal extension.
- 32 samples sequenced; 25 passed QC (>30% tumor cellularity; range 35–95%).
- Mean tumor coverage 94.56× (range 64–143×); mean blood coverage 41.08×.
- **Reference genome:** GRCh38; alignment with BWA-MEM; variant calling with GATK 4.1.2.0 HaplotypeCaller (germline) and Mutect2 (somatic).

## Assays / panels (linked)

- [Whole-genome sequencing](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) — tumor + matched blood pairs; GRCh38 reference; enables non-coding driver and structural variant analysis.
- Copy-number and structural variant analysis via PURPLE/LINX pipeline.
- Driver gene calling via ≥2-of-3 consensus: [OncodriveFML](../methods/oncodrivefml.md), [MutSigCV](../methods/mutsigcv.md), [dNdScv](../methods/dndscv.md).

## Papers using this cohort

- [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md) — Thind et al., Front Oncol 2022: primary publication characterizing the WGS landscape of metastatic CSCC, including novel drivers, non-coding alterations, and indel signatures.

## Notable findings derived from this cohort

- Average whole-genome TMB of 238.7 mutations/Mb (median 166.99; range 32.52–995.66 SNVs/Mb) plus 2.25 indels/Mb — substantially exceeding melanoma (~49/Mb) and prior WES-based CSCC estimates; the majority of variants fall in non-coding DNA [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md).
- 12 coding drivers identified by ≥2-of-3 tools, led by [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), and [C9](../genes/C9.md) (called by all three); [C9](../genes/C9.md) (complement component 9) is a novel CSCC candidate driver present in 52% of samples [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md).
- First identification of recurrent non-coding drivers in CSCC: 3′UTR alterations in [EVC](../genes/EVC.md) and [PPP1R1A](../genes/PPP1R1A.md) (each 48% of samples), and recurrent mutation of lncRNA [LINC01003](../genes/LINC01003.md) in 64% of samples [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md).
- Novel indel signature profile (ID8/ID9/ID13) distinct from melanoma; ID9 (predominant in soft-tissue sarcoma, rare in melanoma) newly identified as a dominant indel signature in CSCC [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md).
- [PTPRD](../genes/PTPRD.md) was the most frequently deleted gene (24% of samples) and most frequent site of focal LOH on chr9p; [PTPRD](../genes/PTPRD.md) deletion and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) deletion did not co-occur despite frequent co-mutation [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md).
