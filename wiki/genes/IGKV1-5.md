# IGKV1-5

## Overview

IGKV1-5 (Immunoglobulin Kappa Variable 1-5) encodes a variable region segment of the immunoglobulin kappa light chain. Focal somatic copy-number gains at immunoglobulin loci in tumor genomes are unusual and may reflect genomic instability, antigen-driven selection in the tumor microenvironment, or passenger structural variation. In LCINS [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md), a focal gain at 2p11.2 involving IGKV1-5 was identified as an early evolutionary event.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Focal copy-number gain at 2p11.2 involving IGKV1-5 identified as an early evolutionary event in the piano SCNA subtype of LCINS LUAD (n=232, whole-genome sequencing). This early timing distinguishes it from later SCNA events in mezzo-forte and forte subtypes. [PMID:34493867](../papers/34493867.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md):** Focal 2p11.2 gain involving IGKV1-5 as an early event in piano LCINS LUAD. [PMID:34493867](../papers/34493867.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- The 2p11.2 focal gain was specifically identified in the piano (SCNA-flat) subtype, which includes carcinoids and LCINS adenocarcinomas with rare drivers. [PMID:34493867](../papers/34493867.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No therapeutic relevance described in the corpus.

## Open questions

- The biological driver at 2p11.2 may not be IGKV1-5 itself; the relevant oncogenic target within this focal amplicon has not been identified.
- The functional consequence of immunoglobulin variable gene copy gain in a lung epithelial cell is unclear.
