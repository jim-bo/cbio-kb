# ATAD2

## Overview

ATAD2 (ATPase Family AAA Domain Containing 2) is a chromatin-remodeling protein and cancer/testis (CT) antigen overexpressed in multiple tumor types. In colon cancer, ATAD2 is recurrently elevated at the protein level in tumors compared to matched normal adjacent tissue, making it a candidate for CT-antigen-based immunotherapy, particularly for microsatellite-stable (MSS) tumors that are poor candidates for checkpoint blockade.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- ATAD2 identified as a recurrent cancer/testis antigen elevated >2-fold in tumors vs matched NATs in 8% of tumor-NAT pairs in the CPTAC proteogenomic colon cancer cohort (n=110); MSI-status-independent expression makes it a shared CT-antigen candidate across both MSS and MSI-H tumors [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)
- Cancer-testis antigen observed in >10% of uterine corpus endometrial carcinoma tumors in the CPTAC proteogenomic cohort (n=95); identified as a potential immunogenic peptide source for vaccine antigen discovery [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md):** Detected as a recurrent CT antigen in 8% of colon adenocarcinoma tumor-NAT pairs by proteomics; expression is independent of MSI status, making it relevant for both MSS and MSI-H subtypes [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with other CT antigens [IGF2BP3](../genes/IGF2BP3.md) and [SPAG1](../genes/SPAG1.md) as part of the three most recurrently overexpressed CT antigens in the CPTAC colon cohort; [IGF2BP3](../genes/IGF2BP3.md) was most prevalent (51%), [SPAG1](../genes/SPAG1.md) 14%, ATAD2 8% [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Proposed as a CT-antigen vaccine target for MSS colon tumors that are poor candidates for checkpoint blockade or neoantigen vaccines; MSI-status-independent expression enables patient selection across molecular subtypes [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Open questions

- Therapeutic validation of ATAD2-based CT-antigen vaccines is explicitly outside the scope of the CPTAC proteogenomic study; in vivo efficacy and immunogenicity data in colon cancer are not yet established [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)
