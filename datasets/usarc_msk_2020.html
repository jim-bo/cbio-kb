MSK Uterine Sarcoma (Hensley et al. 2020)

Overview

Prospective single-institution cohort of 107 women with histologically confirmed uterine sarcoma profiled at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center between April 2014 and April 2017 under IRB protocol NCT01775072. Tumors were sequenced by the MSK-IMPACT targeted DNA panel, with MSK-Fusion RNA sequencing in a diagnostic subset. Designed to characterize the genomic landscape of uterine sarcoma subtypes and identify actionable alterations. Released on cBioPortal as usarc_msk_2020. PMID:32299819

Composition

  • 107 women with uterine sarcoma; histologic breakdown: ULMS n=80, high-grade non-LMS n=22 (HG-ESS n=7, undifferentiated n=5, high-grade adenosarcoma with sarcomatous overgrowth n=4, other rare subtypes), LGESS n=4, STUMP n=2.
  • Cancer type OncoTree code: USARC.
  • 57% (n=61) FIGO stage I at diagnosis; 89% (n=95) recurrent or metastatic at time of sequencing; 94.3% (n=101) histologically high grade.
  • Samples: 39% (n=42) primary, 61% (n=66) metastatic.
  • Median survival from diagnosis 5.6 years (range 0.2–23.1). PMID:32299819

Assays / panels (linked)

  • MSK-IMPACT — three panel versions: 341-gene (n=44), 410-gene (n=44), 468-gene (n=20); average ~646x coverage.
  • MSK-Fusion — RNA panel used diagnostically in a subset for fusion detection.
  • MSIsensor v0.2 for MSI scoring; FACETS v0.5.6 for allele-specific copy-number, purity, and ploidy.
  • Germline variant annotation across 76 cancer-predisposition genes via MSK-IMPACT germline pipeline.
  • Actionability annotated by OncoKB (October 2019). PMID:32299819

Papers using this cohort

  • PMID:32299819 — Hensley et al., prospective genomic profiling of 107 uterine sarcomas; TP53/RB1/ATRX as dominant uLMS drivers; BRCA2 homozygous deletions with PARP-inhibitor responses; PDCD1 deletions enriched vs. pan-cancer.

Notable findings derived from this cohort

  • uLMS driver landscape: TP53 56%, RB1 51%, ATRX 31% loss-of-function alterations; significantly more frequent than in high-grade non-LMS (14%, 14%, 5%; p<0.01 each). PMID:32299819
  • BRCA2 homozygous deletions in ~5% of uLMS (exclusively in uLMS); seven total somatic BRCA2 alterations; PARP-inhibitor-containing therapy produced sustained partial responses in BRCA2-altered uLMS patients. PMID:32299819
  • PDCD1 homozygous deletions in uLMS at the highest frequency among all cancer types with ≥25 cases in a concurrent 15,816-patient MSK-IMPACT pan-cancer cohort; IHC confirmed PD-1 protein loss. PMID:32299819
  • Potentially actionable alterations in 45% (48/107); 17% (8/48) received matched therapy, with two radiographic responses. PMID:32299819
  • 50.5% (50/99) of high-grade uterine sarcomas displayed whole-genome duplication; none of the low-grade tumors (p=0.04); WGD not associated with OS. PMID:32299819
  • Two MSI-H hypermutated uLMS cases (MSIsensor ≥10; IHC loss of MSH2 and MSH6 respectively); both remain disease-free at 5.9 and 13 years after multimodality therapy without immune checkpoint inhibitors. PMID:32299819
  • MED12 mutated in only 11% (9/80) of uLMS vs. ~70% in benign leiomyomas, arguing against uLMS evolving from antecedent leiomyomas. PMID:32299819
  • Genomic profiling enabled diagnostic reclassification in 3 cases (2 uLMS to IMT via ALK fusion; 1 uLMS to BCOR-mutated high-grade stromal sarcoma). PMID:32299819
