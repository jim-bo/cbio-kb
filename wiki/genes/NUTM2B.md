# NUTM2B

## Overview

NUTM2B (NUT midline carcinoma family member 2B) encodes a chromatin-associated protein of the NUT family. Like its paralog [NUTM2A](../genes/NUTM2A.md), NUTM2B is best characterized as a fusion partner with [YWHAE](../genes/YWHAE.md), yielding the YWHAE-NUTM2 rearrangement that defines a subset of high-grade endometrial stromal sarcomas and related uterine mesenchymal tumors. NUTM2B has also been observed as an amplified locus in invasive breast cancer. Detection of YWHAE-NUTM2B by molecular profiling carries direct diagnostic implications.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- YWHAE/NUTM2B rearrangement identified by MSK-Fusion in two high-grade, non-pleomorphic uterine stromal sarcomas among 107 uterine sarcoma patients profiled by MSK-IMPACT; used diagnostically to reclassify cases initially categorized as uterine leiomyosarcoma [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)
- Listed among IDC-NST–private amplifications observed in individual synchronous DCIS/invasive breast cancer (IDC-NST) cases (n=25 patients; [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md)); classified as an invasive-stage-acquired event [PMID:32220886](../papers/32220886.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[ULMS](../cancer_types/ULMS.md) / high-grade uterine stromal sarcoma:** YWHAE-NUTM2A/B fusions are defining molecular events; detected in prospective MSK uterine sarcoma sequencing cohort [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)
- **BRCA (invasive breast carcinoma):** focal amplification of NUTM2B locus observed as an IDC-NST–private event in synchronous DCIS/IDC-NST cohort [PMID:32220886](../papers/32220886.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- YWHAE-NUTM2B fusions co-occur with YWHAE-NUTM2A fusions as alternative fusion partners within the same diagnostic category (high-grade uterine stromal sarcoma with YWHAE rearrangement) [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No targeted therapy directly addressing NUTM2B fusions or amplifications reported in this corpus; fusion detection is primarily used for diagnostic reclassification [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)

## Open questions

- Whether YWHAE-NUTM2A vs. YWHAE-NUTM2B fusions have distinct clinical behavior is unresolved due to small case numbers [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)
- The significance of focal NUTM2B amplification at the invasive transition in breast cancer is unknown [PMID:32220886](../papers/32220886.md)
