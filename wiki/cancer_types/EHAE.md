# Epithelioid Hemangioendothelioma (EHAE)

## Overview

Epithelioid Hemangioendothelioma is a rare vascular tumor of intermediate malignancy defined by the [WWTR1](../genes/WWTR1.md)–CAMTA1 fusion (t(1;3)) or, in a subset, [YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md)–[TFE3](../genes/TFE3.md) fusion. It can arise in soft tissue, liver, lung, and bone. OncoTree classifies it under Soft Tissue Sarcoma.

## Cohorts in the corpus

- [sarcoma_msk_2022](../datasets/sarcoma_msk_2022.md): EHAE was among the 44 sarcoma histologies profiled by FoundationOne Heme in 7,494 sarcomas [PMID:35705558](../papers/35705558.md).

## Recurrent alterations

- In a 7,494-sarcoma cohort, epithelioid hemangioendothelioma was included as a translocation-associated histotype; [WWTR1](../genes/WWTR1.md)–CAMTA1 and [YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md)–[TFE3](../genes/TFE3.md) fusions are the defining molecular events; 31.7% of all sarcomas harbored ≥1 OncoKB actionable alteration [PMID:35705558](../papers/35705558.md).

## Subtypes

- [WWTR1](../genes/WWTR1.md)–CAMTA1 fusion-positive (majority) vs [YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md)–[TFE3](../genes/TFE3.md) fusion-positive (minority, more aggressive).

## Therapeutic landscape

- No established targeted therapy at Level 1; [sunitinib](../drugs/sunitinib.md) and [sirolimus](../drugs/sirolimus.md) have shown activity in anecdotal reports. NTRK fusions are absent in EHAE; molecular characterization is used to distinguish from angiosarcoma and epithelioid sarcoma.

## Sources
