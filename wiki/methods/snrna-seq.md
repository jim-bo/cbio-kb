# Single-nuclear RNA sequencing (snRNA-seq)

## Overview

Single-nuclear RNA sequencing (snRNA-seq) is a single-cell transcriptomics method that works on nuclei isolated from frozen tissue, enabling cell type decomposition and transcriptional characterization in samples where intact cells cannot be recovered [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md).

## Used by

- [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md) — droplet-based snRNA-seq was performed on 8 meningioma tumors and 2 healthy meninges (54,393 nuclei total) from the University Health Network Brain Tumor BioBank; identified cell-type-specific transcriptional programs distinguishing meningioma molecular groups (MG1–MG4) and characterized the meningioma tumor microenvironment [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md).

## Notes

- snRNA-seq data contributed to functional interpretation of the COCA-derived molecular groups in meningioma [PMID:34433969](../papers/34433969.md).
