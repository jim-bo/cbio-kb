# FOXC1

## Overview

FOXC1 is a forkhead box transcription factor implicated in the regulation of cellular differentiation and cancer cell invasiveness. In breast cancer, FOXC1 protein expression by [immunohistochemistry](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md) (IHC) is used as a surrogate marker to identify the BLIS (BRCAness-like immune suppressed) subtype of triple-negative breast cancer (TNBC). FOXC1 IHC positivity is associated with a more aggressive tumor phenotype.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- FOXC1 IHC score trended higher in stable-disease and progressive-disease vs complete-response and partial-response patients within arm E (anti-VEGFR, apatinib/famitinib) of the FUTURE trial TNBC cohort (n=69 refractory metastatic TNBC; P=0.26, not significant). [PMID:32719455](../papers/32719455.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) / TNBC:** FOXC1 IHC expression is used as a surrogate marker to identify the BLIS subtype of metastatic TNBC in the FUTURE umbrella trial, alongside [AR](../genes/AR.md) (LAR subtype) and CD8 (IM subtype). High FOXC1 IHC score was associated with non-response in anti-VEGFR-treated BLIS-subtype patients. [PMID:32719455](../papers/32719455.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- FOXC1 positivity by IHC co-occurs with BRCA1/2-wildtype status in the BLIS molecular subtype of TNBC, distinguishing patients routed to anti-VEGFR therapy vs PARP inhibitor therapy in the FUTURE trial. [PMID:32719455](../papers/32719455.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Higher FOXC1 IHC score in arm E (anti-VEGFR: [apatinib](../drugs/apatinib.md) or [famitinib](../drugs/famitinib.md) for BLIS-subtype, BRCA1/2-wildtype TNBC) was associated with non-response (SD+PD), suggesting FOXC1 may be a candidate negative predictive biomarker for anti-VEGFR therapy in BLIS TNBC; the trend did not reach statistical significance (P=0.26) in this small series. [PMID:32719455](../papers/32719455.md)

## Open questions

- The prognostic and predictive significance of FOXC1 IHC score within BLIS-subtype TNBC requires prospective validation in adequately powered cohorts. [PMID:32719455](../papers/32719455.md)
- Whether somatic mutations in FOXC1 (as opposed to protein overexpression) drive TNBC invasiveness is not addressed in this corpus. [PMID:32719455](../papers/32719455.md)
