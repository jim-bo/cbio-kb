**PMID:** [22960745](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22960745/) · **DOI:** 10.1038/nature11404 · **Journal:** Nature (2012)

## TL;DR

The Cancer Genome Atlas comprehensively profiled 178 lung squamous cell carcinomas ([LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md)) using whole-exome sequencing, whole-genome sequencing, RNA-seq, copy number arrays, and methylation arrays. The study identified statistically recurrent mutations in 18 genes including near-universal [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation (81-90%), novel loss-of-function mutations in [HLA-A](../genes/HLA-A.md), and defined key altered pathways: NFE2L2/KEAP1 (34%), squamous differentiation (44%), PI3K/AKT (47%), and CDKN2A/RB1 (72%). A potential therapeutic target was identified in the majority (64%) of tumors.

## Cohort & data

- 178 previously untreated stage I-IV lung squamous cell carcinomas ([LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md))
- Dataset: [lusc_tcga_pub](../datasets/lusc_tcga_pub.md)
- 96% of patients had a history of tobacco use; median follow-up 15.8 months
- Platforms: whole-exome sequencing (178 tumors + matched normals, mean 121X coverage), whole-genome sequencing (19 pairs, mean 54X), RNA-seq (178 samples), miRNA-seq (159 samples), Affymetrix SNP 6.0 copy number arrays, DNA methylation arrays
- Methods: [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md), [whole-genome-seq](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md), [rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md), [affymetrix-snp6](../methods/affymetrix-snp6.md), [GISTIC](../methods/gistic.md), [MutSig](../methods/mutsig.md)

## Key findings

- Mean somatic mutation rate of 8.1 mutations/Mb (median 8.4/Mb), higher than all other TCGA tumor types at the time (p < 2.2e-16).
- Mean of 360 exonic mutations, 323 copy number segments, and 165 genomic rearrangements per tumor.
- 10 significantly mutated genes (FDR q < 0.1): [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) (MLL2), [HLA-A](../genes/HLA-A.md), [NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md), [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md).
- 12 additional significantly mutated genes from COSMIC-restricted analysis: [AMER1](../genes/AMER1.md) (FAM123B/WTX), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md), [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md), [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md), [APC](../genes/APC.md), [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [TNFAIP3](../genes/TNFAIP3.md), [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md).
- NFE2L2/KEAP1/CUL3 pathway altered in 34% of tumors; mutations in [NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md) concentrated in KEAP1-interaction motifs (DLG/ETGE).
- Squamous differentiation pathway altered in 44%: overexpression/amplification of [SOX2](../genes/SOX2.md) and [TP63](../genes/TP63.md), loss-of-function mutations in [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md), [NOTCH2](../genes/NOTCH2.md), and [ASCL4](../genes/ASCL4.md).
- PI3K/AKT pathway altered in 47%: [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), or [AKT3](../genes/AKT3.md).
- CDKN2A/RB1 pathway altered in 72%, through methylation (21%), mutation (18%), exon 1-beta skipping (4%), and homozygous deletion (29%).
- Four gene expression subtypes identified: classical (36%), basal (25%), secretory (24%), primitive (15%).
- Chromosome 3q selectively amplified in LUSC vs. lung adenocarcinoma (p < 1e-15).
- EGFR/KRAS mutations common in lung adenocarcinoma are essentially absent in LUSC.
- 64% of tumors harbored a potentially targetable somatic alteration.
- No recurrent in-frame fusion proteins were identified despite a high rearrangement burden.

## Clinical implications

- 64% of LUSC tumors contain a potentially targetable alteration in a kinase, PI3K subunit, or RTK pathway gene, suggesting opportunity for targeted therapy trials.
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) L861Q mutations (2 cases) confer sensitivity to [erlotinib](../drugs/erlotinib.md) and [gefitinib](../drugs/gefitinib.md), but canonical adenocarcinoma [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) mutations are absent.
- [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) amplification is a candidate therapeutic target; FGFR family members are commonly mutated/amplified.
- Inactivating [HLA-A](../genes/HLA-A.md) mutations suggest a role for genotypic selection of patients for immunotherapy (anti-PD1, anti-CTLA4).
- PI3K pathway alterations (47%) are mutually exclusive with EGFR alterations, pointing to non-overlapping targetable subgroups.
- Expression subtypes correlate with specific pathway alterations, potentially informing subtype-specific therapeutic strategies.

## Limitations & open questions

- Functional dependence of LUSC tumors on the identified alterations was not experimentally validated in this study.
- Median follow-up was only 15.8 months, limiting survival analyses.
- The cohort is predominantly North American with 96% tobacco-use history; generalizability to never-smoker LUSC or non-Western cohorts is uncertain.
- No recurrent actionable gene fusions were found despite high rearrangement burden; the clinical significance of passenger rearrangements remains unclear.
- Whether expression subtypes (classical, basal, secretory, primitive) predict therapeutic response requires prospective validation.
