# VAV2

## Overview

VAV2 is a guanine nucleotide exchange factor (GEF) for RHO-family GTPases, including [RAC1](../genes/RAC1.md) and CDC42, that promotes actin cytoskeletal reorganization, cell migration, and invasion. In esophageal adenocarcinoma (EAC), VAV2 is one of several upstream RAC1-regulatory GEFs recurrently mutated, converging on RAC1 pathway activation.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Recurrently mutated in EAC (145-tumor WES cohort); identified as one of several upstream regulators of RAC1 alongside [TIAM1](../genes/TIAM1.md), [TRIO](../genes/TRIO.md), [ECT2](../genes/ECT2.md), [DOCK2](../genes/DOCK2.md), and [ELMO1](../genes/ELMO1.md) [PMID:23525077](../papers/23525077.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **EAC:** Part of a convergently mutated RAC1-GEF regulatory axis in esophageal adenocarcinoma; downstream effector [PAK1](../genes/PAK1.md) is recurrently amplified at 11q13 [PMID:23525077](../papers/23525077.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-altered with TIAM1, TRIO, ECT2, DOCK2, and ELMO1 as upstream regulators of RAC1 in EAC [PMID:23525077](../papers/23525077.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- The convergent RAC1-pathway alteration pattern in EAC implicates RAC1/PAK1 signaling as a candidate therapeutic axis; no VAV2-specific agents are discussed in the corpus [PMID:23525077](../papers/23525077.md).

## Open questions

- Functional validation of VAV2 mutations in EAC invasion models has not been reported in this cohort [PMID:23525077](../papers/23525077.md).
