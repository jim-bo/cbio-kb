**PMID:** 28825054 · **DOI:** [10.1200/PO.17.00029](https://doi.org/10.1200/PO.17.00029) · **Journal:** JCO Precision Oncology (2017)

## TL;DR

Abida et al. prospectively profiled 504 tumors from 451 patients with prostate cancer spanning the full clinical spectrum (locoregional, metastatic noncastrate, and metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer [mCRPC]) using the [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) hybridization-capture targeted DNA panel, with paired germline analysis in 221 patients ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)). They identified potentially actionable alterations in 36% of patients (per OncoKB annotation), including a 27% combined germline-or-somatic alteration rate in DNA damage repair genes [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), and [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md). Comparative analyses across disease states showed that [APC](../genes/APC.md) alterations are enriched in metastatic disease, [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) alterations in mCRPC, and that [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and somatic [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) alterations arise as early clonal events in patients who later progress to metastasis ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)). The authors argue for routine paired germline + somatic profiling as standard of care in advanced [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md).

## Cohort & data

- **Cohort:** 451 patients with [prostate adenocarcinoma](../cancer_types/PRAD.md), 504 tumors profiled (44 patients had >1 tumor sequenced); enrolled at Memorial Sloan Kettering ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **Disease-state composition (last known):** 348 metastatic (77%), 53 biochemically recurrent (12%), 50 locoregional (11%) ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **Disease-state at tissue acquisition (504 tumors):** 140 locoregional, 51 metastatic noncastrate prostate, 54 metastatic noncastrate metastasis sites, 95 mCRPC prostate, 164 mCRPC metastasis sites ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **Metastatic biopsy sites:** lymph node 45%, bone 22%, liver 14%, lung 5%, other soft tissue 14% ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **Sequencing success rate:** 504/746 (68%); bone and lung samples were the most challenging (42–52% success) ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **Assay:** [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) hybridization-capture targeted DNA panel covering >300 cancer-related genes for SNVs, indels, somatic copy number alterations, and structural rearrangements; matched normal blood for germline filtering ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **Germline analysis:** 221 patients consented to germline analysis of 76 known cancer-predisposing genes (the first 124 also reported in Pritchard et al.) ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **Other tools:** clonality estimated via cancer cell fraction with the [FACETS](../methods/facets.md) algorithm; actionability called using [OncoKB](../methods/oncokb-annotation.md) ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **Dataset:** released as [prad_mskcc_2017](../datasets/prad_mskcc_2017.md) on cBioPortal ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **Comparison cohorts:** the SU2C-PCF mCRPC dataset ([prad_su2c_2015](../datasets/prad_su2c_2015.md), [PMID:26000489](../papers/26000489.md)) and the TCGA primary prostate cancer cohort ([prad_tcga_pub](../datasets/prad_tcga_pub.md), [PMID:26544944](../papers/26544944.md)).

## Key findings

- **Mutation burden rises with castration resistance:** mean nonsynonymous mutations/megabase increased from 1.74 (locoregional) to 4.02 (mCRPC), P < .001; metastatic noncastrate tumors were similar to locoregional (2.08 mut/Mb) ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **PI3K/AKT pathway alterations in 24% of patients,** including [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [PIK3CB](../genes/PIK3CB.md), [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md), [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md), [AKT3](../genes/AKT3.md); most [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md), [AKT3](../genes/AKT3.md) point mutations were known activating hotspots ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **MAPK pathway alterations in 5%,** including hotspot mutations in [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), and [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md) ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **Wnt/β-catenin pathway alterations in 15%,** including [APC](../genes/APC.md), [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md), and [RNF43](../genes/RNF43.md) ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **Somatic homologous recombination (HR) gene alterations in 22%** of the 451 patients — [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) 7%, [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) 5%, [CDK12](../genes/CDK12.md) 7%, [FANCA](../genes/FANCA.md) 3%, [PALB2](../genes/PALB2.md) 2%, [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) 1%, [RAD50](../genes/RAD50.md) 1% ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **Germline pathogenic alterations in 19% of 221 patients consented:** [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) 9%, [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md) 4%, [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) 2%, [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) 1%, [FH](../genes/FH.md) 1%, plus [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md), [NBN](../genes/NBN.md), [PALB2](../genes/PALB2.md), [BRIP1](../genes/BRIP1.md) (<1% each) ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **Combined germline-or-somatic alterations in [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), or [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md) in 27%** of the 221 germline-tested patients; germline-only analysis would have captured ~half of these patients ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **Mismatch repair (MMR) gene alterations in 3%** of patients ([MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md), [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md)); MMR-deficient tumors carried the highest mutation counts and were enriched for MMR / microsatellite instability mutational signatures ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **mCRPC-enriched alterations vs. locoregional:** [AR](../genes/AR.md) (2% → 52%), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (27% → 48%), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) (12% → 29%), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) (2% → 18%), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) (2% → 11%), [APC](../genes/APC.md) (4% → 15%), [FANCA](../genes/FANCA.md) (1% → 7%), [CDK12](../genes/CDK12.md) (4% → 11%) ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **mCRPC-enriched vs. metastatic noncastrate:** [AR](../genes/AR.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) all significantly enriched, implicating them in development of castration resistance — particularly [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), a DDR gene ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **Metastatic-noncastrate-enriched vs. locoregional:** only [APC](../genes/APC.md) and (less strongly) [ARID5B](../genes/ARID5B.md), implicating [APC](../genes/APC.md) in metastasis ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **[SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md) alterations were enriched in earlier disease states** (locoregional 12%, metastatic noncastrate 11%, mCRPC 5%), suggesting better outcomes / increased sensitivity to androgen-deprivation therapy for [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md)-mutant tumors (pending functional validation) ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **Truncal/clonal events in matched samples:** [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and somatic [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) alterations were present early and clonal (cancer cell fraction > 0.9) in primary tumors of patients who later developed metastasis; conversely, [AR](../genes/AR.md) alterations and a recurrent activating [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) E545K mutation were acquired late ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **Phylogenetic analyses** identified truncal alterations in epigenetic regulators [EP300](../genes/EP300.md) and [KDM5A](../genes/KDM5A.md) in metastatic tumors of patient P-0000377, with subclonal [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) alteration in metachronous bone metastases ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **OncoKB-annotated actionability:** 36% of patients carried at least one potentially actionable alteration; this excludes non-BRCA/ATM germline events, VUS missense, and genes of less clear significance such as [CDK12](../genes/CDK12.md) and [FANCA](../genes/FANCA.md), so the true rate may be higher ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).

## Genes & alterations

- **[AR](../genes/AR.md)** — amplification and resistance mutations (e.g., F877L conferring resistance to [enzalutamide](../drugs/enzalutamide.md)/[apalutamide](../drugs/apalutamide.md), and H875Y conferring resistance to [flutamide](../drugs/flutamide.md)) enriched in mCRPC; 4% of metastatic noncastrate tumors already carried [AR](../genes/AR.md) alterations, consistent with subclinical transition to castration resistance ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)** — present at 27% locoregional / 30% metastatic noncastrate / 48% mCRPC; alterations are early clonal events in matched primaries of patients who later progress to metastasis, consistent with prior reports of aggressive behavior of [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)-altered prostate cancer ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **[PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md)** — frequencies rise sharply with castration resistance ([PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) 12→29%, [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) 2→18%) ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **[BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md)** — 9% germline pathogenic in 221 tested; somatic alterations clonal/truncal in matched samples; combined germline+somatic frequency drives PARP-inhibitor / platinum sensitivity rationale (see [olaparib](../drugs/olaparib.md)) ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **[BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md)** — additional germline DDR alterations contributing to the 27% combined HR-deficiency rate; [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) also shows somatic enrichment specifically in mCRPC ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **[CDK12](../genes/CDK12.md), [FANCA](../genes/FANCA.md), [PALB2](../genes/PALB2.md), [RAD50](../genes/RAD50.md)** — additional somatic HR pathway genes; [CDK12](../genes/CDK12.md) loss is hypothesized to confer PARP inhibitor sensitivity (per cited preclinical work) ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **[APC](../genes/APC.md)** — enriched in both metastatic disease states relative to locoregional, implicating it as a driver of metastasis ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **[SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md)** — enriched in earlier disease states (12% locoregional → 5% mCRPC); paired with [FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md), defines a possibly androgen-deprivation-sensitive subset (functional validation pending) ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **[PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md), [AKT3](../genes/AKT3.md), [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md)** — predominantly known activating hotspots; one patient acquired a [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) E545K hotspot ~3 years after prostatectomy, illustrating late-emergent actionable events ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md)** — hotspot MAPK-pathway mutations in ~5% of patients ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **[CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md), [RNF43](../genes/RNF43.md)** — Wnt-pathway co-alterations alongside [APC](../genes/APC.md) ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **[MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md), [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md)** — MMR genes altered in 3%; produce hypermutator phenotype with MMR/MSI signatures, raising potential for immune-checkpoint blockade response ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **[BRIP1](../genes/BRIP1.md), [NBN](../genes/NBN.md), [FH](../genes/FH.md)** — additional germline cancer-predisposition findings (each <1–1%) ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **[EP300](../genes/EP300.md), [KDM5A](../genes/KDM5A.md), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md)** — epigenetic regulators identified as truncal/subclonal events in matched-tumor phylogenetic analysis ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **[FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md), [ARID5B](../genes/ARID5B.md)** — additional context genes in disease-state enrichment analyses ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).

## Clinical implications

- **Routine paired germline + somatic profiling for advanced prostate cancer:** germline-only analysis missed about half of patients with actionable [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md)/[BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md)/[ATM](../genes/ATM.md)/[CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md) alterations, supporting paired testing as standard of care irrespective of family history ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **PARP inhibitor candidacy:** the 22% somatic HR-gene alteration rate (and 27% combined germline+somatic for the four canonical DDR genes) identifies a substantial population of candidates for PARP inhibitors such as [olaparib](../drugs/olaparib.md) and platinum-based therapy, citing prior clinical evidence (TOPARP-A and platinum case series) ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **Immune checkpoint blockade in MMR-deficient prostate cancer:** the 3% MMR-altered subset with hypermutator phenotype may be candidates for PD-1 blockade by analogy to MSI-high colorectal and other malignancies ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **AR-resistance biomarkers:** detection of an [AR](../genes/AR.md) F877L mutation in a patient progressing after 4 years on [apalutamide](../drugs/apalutamide.md) (ARN-509), and an H875Y mutation associated with [flutamide](../drugs/flutamide.md) resistance, illustrate clinical-grade detection of acquired antiandrogen-resistance mechanisms ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md) as an early biomarker of progression risk:** clonal [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) alterations in localized tumors may predict eventual progression to metastasis, potentially informing more aggressive upfront therapy ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **Family-counseling implications:** the high germline pathogenic-variant rate (19% of consented patients) supports referral for cascade testing of relatives ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **Trial enrollment:** authors frame the assay as a feasible mechanism for routing patients into molecularly-guided trials within standard clinical workflow ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Selection bias toward aggressive disease:** locoregional tumors in MSK-IMPACT were enriched for cases that subsequently recurred or metastasized, biasing comparisons against the [TCGA primary cohort](../datasets/prad_tcga_pub.md) (e.g., higher [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md) frequencies than TCGA) ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **Targeted panel scope:** [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) covers >300 cancer-related genes but cannot capture exome-wide signatures, non-coding drivers, or comprehensive structural rearrangements outside the bait set ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **Bone-biopsy success rate:** only 42–52% of bone and lung biopsies yielded sequenceable DNA, leaving the dominant osseous-metastasis pattern of prostate cancer underrepresented; circulating tumor DNA assays are flagged as a possible solution ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **Germline-cohort consent:** only 221/451 patients consented to germline analysis, limiting power for rare-variant frequency estimates ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **Statistical power on metastases-only sub-analyses:** when restricted to metastatic tumors or lymph nodes, several disease-state enrichment trends did not reach significance owing to smaller sample size (Appendix Fig A12) ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **Functional validation pending:** disease-state enrichment of [APC](../genes/APC.md) (metastasis) and [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) (castration resistance), as well as putative ADT-sensitivity of [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md)-mutant tumors, are correlative and require laboratory validation ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **OncoKB actionability undercounts:** the 36% actionability rate excludes non-BRCA/ATM germline alterations, missense VUS, and genes such as [CDK12](../genes/CDK12.md) and [FANCA](../genes/FANCA.md) whose clinical significance is still emerging ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
- **Trial availability:** at the time of publication, the authors note PARP-inhibitor trials in prostate cancer were "due to open shortly," constraining immediate translation of HR-deficiency findings into therapy ([PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)).
