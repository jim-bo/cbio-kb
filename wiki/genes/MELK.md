# MELK

## Overview

MELK (Maternal Embryonic Leucine Zipper Kinase) is a serine/threonine kinase involved in cell cycle regulation, apoptosis, and stem cell renewal. MELK is overexpressed and shows elevated kinase activity in multiple cancer types. In the context of colon cancer, MELK was identified as one of several kinases with elevated activity in tumor tissue relative to normal adjacent tissue in a CPTAC proteogenomic study, positioning it as a candidate therapeutic target.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Elevated kinase activity detected in colon adenocarcinoma tumors relative to normal adjacent tissue (NAT) in a CPTAC proteogenomic cohort (n=110 tumors, 96 with tumor/NAT pairs); identified as a targetable therapeutic candidate alongside [CDK1](../genes/CDK1.md), [CDK2](../genes/CDK2.md), [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md), [CDK7](../genes/CDK7.md), [PFKFB3](../genes/PFKFB3.md), and [PI4KB](../genes/PI4KB.md) [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md):** MELK kinase activity is elevated in colon tumors vs normal adjacent tissue in the CPTAC proteogenomic study; MELK inhibitors are noted as being in clinical development [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- MELK elevated kinase activity co-occurs with CDK1, CDK2, CDK4, CDK7, PFKFB3, and PI4KB elevated activities in colon cancer tumors; the full kinase activation landscape is proposed as a therapeutically actionable set [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- MELK inhibitors are in clinical development and identified as a therapeutic candidate for colon cancer based on elevated tumor kinase activity in the CPTAC proteogenomic dataset [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)
- No therapeutic validation in vivo or clinical data is provided in this study; hypothesis is computationally generated from phosphoproteomic and kinase-activity data

## Open questions

- Clinical efficacy of MELK inhibition in [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md) has not been established in this study; all therapeutic hypotheses are based on omics data without in vivo validation [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)
