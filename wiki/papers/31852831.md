**PMID:** 31852831 · **DOI:** 10.1158/1078-0432.CCR-19-0909 · **Journal:** Clinical Cancer Research (2020)

## TL;DR

Vaubel et al. report the comprehensive genomic and phenotypic characterization of the Mayo Clinic patient-derived xenograft (PDX) panel — 96 glioblastoma PDX lines established from 261 glioma patients surgically resected at Mayo Clinic between 2000 and 2017. Whole-exome sequencing (n=83), RNA-seq (n=68) and genome-wide methylation profiling (n=76) show that the panel recapitulates the canonical genetic drivers of [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) ([TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter mutation, [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md)) at frequencies broadly comparable to TCGA, with one striking exception: [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md) amplification is absent. WES of 24 matched patient–PDX pairs shows preservation of most driver alterations (including [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) amplification in 100% of pairs), although four pairs gained or lost drivers consistent with clonal selection. [MGMT](../genes/MGMT.md) promoter methylation correlated with response to [temozolomide](../drugs/temozolomide.md) in the PDX panel, mirroring the human clinical phenotype.

## Cohort & data

- **Panel size:** 96 [PDX](../methods/patient-derived-xenograft.md) lines from 261 glioma patients (overall engraftment rate 36%; WHO grade IV engraftment 47%; newly diagnosed 50%, recurrent 42%) ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **Histology of viable PDX:** [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md), IDH-wildtype (n=93); [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md), IDH-mutant (n=2); diffuse midline glioma, H3 K27M-mutant ([DMG](../cancer_types/DMG.md), n=1). Primary tumors n=60; recurrent n=34 ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **Dataset:** [gbm_mayo_pdx_sarkaria_2019](../datasets/gbm_mayo_pdx_sarkaria_2019.md) — Mayo Clinic Brain Tumor PDX National Resource ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **Assays:** [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) n=83 (SureSelect / TGen Strexome V2; mouse reads removed with Xenome); [rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) n=68 (TruSeq libraries); [epic-methylation-array](../methods/epic-methylation-array.md) n=76 (Illumina MethylationEPIC at MD Anderson); [sanger-sequencing](../methods/sanger-sequencing.md) for IDH1/2 and TERT promoter; methylation-specific PCR ([qmsp](../methods/qmsp.md)) for MGMT promoter (n=78); [fish](../methods/fish.md) for EGFR/MDM2/N-MYC/PDGFRA validation; [immunohistochemistry](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md) for GFAP/Ki67/EGFR/PDGFRA; [str-profiling](../methods/str-profiling.md) for tissue provenance ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **Reference comparison set:** TCGA glioblastoma cohort ([gbm_tcga](../datasets/gbm_tcga.md)) used as the frequency benchmark for driver alterations ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **Patient–PDX pairs:** matched WES on 24 patient tumors with derivative PDX; germline WES on 55 patients ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).

## Key findings

- **Engraftment is restricted to grade IV astrocytomas.** No viable PDX were established from any of 59 WHO grade II–III glioma samples. Within grade IV, engraftment did not correlate with patient age, sex, or overall survival (newly diagnosed p=0.76; recurrent p=0.11) ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **Driver landscape mirrors TCGA glioblastoma.** TERT promoter mutation 86%, homozygous deletion of [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) 70% (n=59), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation 36% (n=30), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) alteration 48% (n=40), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) alteration 17% (n=14), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) loss/mutation 16% (n=13), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) alteration 4% (n=3) ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **[EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) is the dominant RTK alteration.** 45% of PDX (n=37/83) carried at least one EGFR alteration; 35 had high-level amplification, 14 SNVs were observed across 12 lines (11 of those with concurrent amplification), and 16 PDX harbored exon-deletion variants including EGFRvIII (n=11, Δexon2–7), EGFRvII (n=2, Δexon14–15), c-terminal deletions (n=2, Δexon25–27), and a previously uncharacterized Δexon6–7 (n=1). EGFR protein expression by IHC was strongly correlated with copy number ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **[PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md) amplification is absent from the panel.** No PDX showed [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md) amplification despite a ~15% expected frequency in IDH-wildtype glioblastoma; three PDX carried PDGFRA SNVs (including oncogenic D842V), and 29% of PDX still showed strong PDGFRA protein expression ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **Other RTK pathway alterations are sparser.** [MET](../genes/MET.md) amplification n=2; [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) amplification n=1; p53 pathway also includes [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) amplification (n=8, 10%) and [MDM4](../genes/MDM4.md) amplification (n=2, 2%); Rb pathway also inactivated by [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) amplification (n=7, 8%) or [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md) amplification (n=1, 1%). [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) (n=3) and [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md) (n=2) amplifications are also captured ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **Expression and methylation subtypes are broadly represented.** Of 68 PDX with RNA-seq: classical 46% (n=31), mesenchymal 31% (n=21), proneural 23% (n=16); classical subgroup enriched for EGFR amplification (78% vs 29% mesenchymal / 7% proneural). Methylation classes (n=76): three LGm1 (IDH-mutant), LGm4 46%, LGm5 44%, LGm6 10% ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **Recurrent-glioma-associated alterations are captured.** Four recurrence-derived PDX carry multiple [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md) mutations with high mutation burden (hypermutation phenotype); variants of [LTBP4](../genes/LTBP4.md) (14%), [IGF1R](../genes/IGF1R.md) (7%) and [PRDM2](../genes/PRDM2.md) (7%) were identified in recurrent PDX at frequencies similar to prior reports. GBM154 (recurrence of a low-grade oligodendroglioma 10 years post-diagnosis) lost [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) p.R132H but gained a novel [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) p.F397L while retaining 1p/19q co-deletion, [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md), and TERT promoter mutations ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **Recurrent and novel fusions.** [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)–[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md) fusions in three lines (GBM14, GBM148, GBM232); two non-canonical [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) fusions (FGFR3–[TRIM54](../genes/TRIM54.md), FGFR3–[CALCOCO1](../genes/CALCOCO1.md)); previously reported [PTPRZ1](../genes/PTPRZ1.md)–[MET](../genes/MET.md) and [CHTOP](../genes/CHTOP.md)–[NTRK1](../genes/NTRK1.md) fusions in three PDX, both representing targetable alterations ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **Therapy response mirrors patient response.** Across 37 PDX treated orthotopically, median survival ratio relative to placebo was 1.6 for RT, 2.5 for [temozolomide](../drugs/temozolomide.md) monotherapy, and 2.8 for concurrent RT/TMZ. MGMT promoter methylation was strongly associated with TMZ and RT/TMZ benefit but not RT alone. Single-agent [bevacizumab](../drugs/bevacizumab.md) gave a survival ratio >1.3 in 23% of PDX (7/30). Patient and PDX survival under standard therapy were positively correlated (Spearman ρ=0.53) ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **Patient–PDX concordance is high but not perfect.** Across 24 matched patient–PDX pairs, EGFR amplification was preserved in 100% (also confirmed by FISH in four pairs). Four pairs (GBM168, GBM110, GBM159, GBM181) showed clinically meaningful divergence consistent with clonal selection: e.g., GBM110 acquired [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md) amplification in PDX (FISH: subclonal 0–27% in patient, 100% in PDX); GBM159 lost PDGFRA amplification (45–75% of patient tumor cells with two amplified subclones distinguishable by centromeric probe co-amplification) and gained [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md) amplification in PDX; GBM168 patient and PDX shared EGFR/CDK4/MDM2 amplifications but had different EGFR exon-deletion variants, plus a private [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) alteration in the patient and a private [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) variant in the PDX; GBM181 showed many private SNVs in both directions consistent with MSH6-driven MMR-deficient hypermutation ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **Blood–brain barrier integrity varies between models.** GBM6 intact; GBM108 partial disruption; GBM39 more complete disruption — relevant to drug-delivery design choices ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).

## Genes & alterations

- **[EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md):** amplification (n=35, 42%), 14 SNVs across 12 PDX, exon-deletion variants in 16 PDX (EGFRvIII n=11, EGFRvII n=2, C-terminal deletion n=2, Δexon6–7 n=1). Amplification preserved in 100% of 24 matched patient–PDX pairs; different exon-deletion variants in GBM168 between patient and PDX ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **[PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md):** no amplification observed in any PDX despite ~15% expected frequency in IDH-wildtype GBM; three PDX with SNVs including D842V; PDX-engraftment-associated loss demonstrated in GBM159 (FISH: amplified in 45–75% of patient tumor cells, not in PDX) ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **[TERT](../genes/TERT.md):** promoter mutations in 86% of PDX, the most frequent alteration in the panel ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **[CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md):** homozygous deletion in 70% (n=59); GBM164 and GBM187 showed homozygous loss in PDX vs single-copy loss in matched patient — clonal-selection candidate ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md):** mutation in 36% (n=30); GBM168 PDX carried a private TP53 variant absent from the patient tumor ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **[PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md):** alteration in 48% (n=40); GBM168 patient carried a private PTEN alteration not detected in the PDX ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **[MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) / [MDM4](../genes/MDM4.md):** amplification in 10% and 2% respectively; [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) amplification preserved in 100% of GBM108 patient and PDX cells by FISH ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **[RB1](../genes/RB1.md) / [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) / [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md):** Rb pathway inactivated by [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) loss/mutation (16%), [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) amplification (8%), or CDK6 amplification (1%) ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **[NF1](../genes/NF1.md) / [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md):** MAPK pathway alterations in 17% and 4% of PDX, respectively ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **[MYC](../genes/MYC.md) / [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md):** amplifications in 3 and 2 PDX, respectively; GBM110 PDX acquired N-MYC amplification (subclonal 0–27% in patient by FISH) ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **[MET](../genes/MET.md):** amplification in 2 PDX; copy number gains in both IDH-mutant lines (GBM164, GBM196), consistent with the secondary-glioblastoma literature ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **[FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md):** amplification n=1; canonical FGFR3–[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md) fusion in three PDX; two novel fusions FGFR3–[TRIM54](../genes/TRIM54.md) and FGFR3–[CALCOCO1](../genes/CALCOCO1.md) ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **[MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md):** multiple mutations in four recurrent-tumor-derived PDX, driving MMR-deficient hypermutation phenotype; GBM181 patient–PDX pair shows the pattern of many private SNVs in both directions ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **[IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) / [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) / [H3-3A](../genes/H3-3A.md):** IDH1-mutant PDX (GBM164, GBM196) carried TP53 mutations and homozygous [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) deletion; GBM36 (cerebellar diffuse midline glioma) carried H3F3A ([H3-3A](../genes/H3-3A.md)) p.K27M, [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) and TP53 mutations; GBM154 lost IDH1 p.R132H but acquired IDH1 p.F397L on recurrence ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **[PTPRZ1](../genes/PTPRZ1.md) / [NTRK1](../genes/NTRK1.md) / [CHTOP](../genes/CHTOP.md):** [PTPRZ1](../genes/PTPRZ1.md)–[MET](../genes/MET.md) and [CHTOP](../genes/CHTOP.md)–[NTRK1](../genes/NTRK1.md) fusions in three PDX — flagged as potentially targetable ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **[LTBP4](../genes/LTBP4.md) / [IGF1R](../genes/IGF1R.md) / [PRDM2](../genes/PRDM2.md):** variants identified in recurrent PDX at frequencies similar to prior reports (14%, 7%, 7% of 28 recurrent PDX), though some also seen in primary PDX (possible germline) ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **[CIC](../genes/CIC.md) / [FUBP1](../genes/FUBP1.md) / [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md):** present in the original oligodendroglioma that gave rise to GBM154; only [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md) (and TERT promoter mutation, plus 1p/19q co-deletion) was retained in the high-grade recurrent PDX ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).

## Clinical implications

- **MGMT methylation predicts TMZ benefit, not RT benefit.** In the PDX panel, MGMT promoter methylation was strongly associated with response to [temozolomide](../drugs/temozolomide.md) and concurrent RT/TMZ, but not response to radiation alone — directly mirroring the human clinical phenotype and supporting the use of MGMT status as a stratification biomarker in glioblastoma trials ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **PDX panel suitable for 1×1×1 preclinical trial designs.** The authors explicitly position the Mayo PDX collection as the substrate for population-level "preclinical phase II" trials; they cite the [veliparib](../drugs/veliparib.md) + [temozolomide](../drugs/temozolomide.md) work in 28 of these PDX (efficacy limited to MGMT-methylated tumors) as the biomarker-enrichment rationale for the Alliance [veliparib](../drugs/veliparib.md) trial ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **Targetable fusions present.** FGFR3–[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md), PTPRZ1–MET, and CHTOP–NTRK1 fusions are present in the panel, providing models for testing FGFR, MET, and TRK inhibitors in glioblastoma ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **[bevacizumab](../drugs/bevacizumab.md) sensitivity is rare and unstratified.** Single-agent [bevacizumab](../drugs/bevacizumab.md) gave a survival ratio >1.3 in only 23% of PDX (7/30); the authors do not identify a predictive biomarker, consistent with the modest benefit observed clinically ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **Practical caution on PDGFRA-amplified disease.** Because no PDGFRA-amplified PDX engrafted (and GBM5 lost PDGFRA amplification by passage eight), the Mayo panel is not suitable for studying the ~15% of glioblastoma patients with PDGFRA amplification — investigators studying this subset must look elsewhere (e.g., BTIC or GEMM models) ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **Open-resource availability.** All models and accompanying genomic data are openly available to the neuro-oncology research community via the Mayo PDX National Resource ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Selection bias against PDGFRA-amplified tumors.** The complete absence of PDGFRA amplification in 83 sequenced PDX (vs ~15% expected) is unexplained; authors raise both engraftment failure and serial-passage loss as candidates but cannot adjudicate ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **Drift over serial passage is not directly assessed.** WES was performed at varying passage; the manuscript does not characterize evolution across multiple passages of the same line and only recommends restoring from early-passage frozen stock and re-verifying alterations of interest before downstream studies ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **Orthotopic PDX lack microvascular proliferation and necrosis.** Fewer than 20% of orthotopic PDX showed these diagnostic features of glioblastoma, whereas >80% of flank PDX show palisading necrosis — implying microenvironmental contribution to these phenotypes and potentially limiting fidelity for studies of vascular biology or pseudopalisading necrosis ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **Methylation classification is noisy in PDX.** Principal component analysis did not cleanly separate PDX by methylation class, attributed by the authors to predominance of IDH-wildtype tumors, contaminating mouse stroma, and effects of higher passage number ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **No intact immune system.** As athymic-nude-mouse xenografts, the panel is unsuitable for immunotherapy studies — a limitation the authors flag as motivating complementary GEMM resources ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **Engraftment limited to grade IV.** Zero of 59 grade II–III gliomas engrafted, restricting the panel's reach to the most aggressive end of the glioma spectrum ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
- **Clinical correlation of patient–PDX response is moderate.** Spearman ρ=0.53 between patient and matched-PDX survival is supportive but leaves substantial unexplained variance; the authors do not break this down by treatment arm or MGMT status ([PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)).
