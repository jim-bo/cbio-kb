# APOBEC3G

## Overview

APOBEC3G (Apolipoprotein B mRNA Editing Enzyme Catalytic Subunit 3G) is a cytidine deaminase of the APOBEC family that generates C→U edits in single-stranded DNA, contributing to APOBEC mutational signatures (COSMIC SBS2/SBS13). Unlike [APOBEC3B](../genes/APOBEC3B.md), APOBEC3G protein levels in breast cancer have been linked to immune microenvironment activation and PD-L1 expression.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- APOBEC3G protein levels correlate with PD-L1 ([CD274](../genes/CD274.md)) mRNA and CIBERSORT immune infiltration score in both PAM50 luminal and basal breast cancer (CPTAC proteogenomics, n=122); protein-level APOBEC3G (rather than genetic alteration) serves as a readout of APOBEC mutagenesis and immune activation [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md): APOBEC3G protein correlates with immune infiltration in luminal and basal subtypes; APOBEC-enriched luminal cases had higher mutation loads (Wilcoxon p=0.008) and active immune microenvironments [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- APOBEC3G protein co-varies with APOBEC3B protein levels, COSMIC SBS2/SBS13 signatures, and PD-L1/immune-infiltration markers in breast cancer [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Elevated APOBEC3G protein expression in subsets of luminal A/B [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) with active immune microenvironments supports ICI trials in endocrine-resistant ER+ breast cancer; these tumors display high PD-L1 and immune infiltration [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md)

## Open questions

- Whether APOBEC3G or APOBEC3B is the dominant cytidine deaminase generating APOBEC mutational signatures in luminal breast cancer is not fully resolved by the CPTAC proteomics data [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md)
