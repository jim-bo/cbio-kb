# NUMB

## Overview

NUMB is an endocytic adaptor protein that functions as a Notch pathway inhibitor by promoting NOTCH receptor degradation. NUMB can act as either a Notch inhibitor or modulator depending on cellular context. In oral squamous cell carcinoma (OSCC), NUMB copy gain was identified as part of the Notch pathway alteration signature affecting 66% of tumors.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Copy gain observed in OSCC; categorized as a Notch pathway modulator contributing to the 66% prevalence of Notch pathway alterations when combined with [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) mutations, [TP63](../genes/TP63.md) alterations, and [MAML1](../genes/MAML1.md) losses (40-tumor MD Anderson OSCC cohort) [PMID:23619168](../papers/23619168.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md) (Head and Neck Squamous Cell Carcinoma) / OSCC:** Copy gain detected by SNP array; the impact on Notch signaling (activating or inhibitory) depends on NUMB isoform and cellular context [PMID:23619168](../papers/23619168.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- NUMB copy gain co-occurs with [JAG1](../genes/JAG1.md) and [JAG2](../genes/JAG2.md) gains and [MAML1](../genes/MAML1.md) losses in OSCC as part of the Notch pathway alteration pattern [PMID:23619168](../papers/23619168.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic implications identified in this study; the Notch pathway tumor-suppressor role in OSCC (as evidenced by functional NOTCH1 activation suppressing growth) would suggest that NUMB gains potentially reflect counter-regulatory Notch modulation [PMID:23619168](../papers/23619168.md)

## Open questions

- Whether NUMB copy gain in OSCC activates or inhibits Notch signaling in the specific OSCC context has not been functionally resolved; the interpretation depends on which NUMB isoforms are expressed [PMID:23619168](../papers/23619168.md)
