# SLC2A3

## Overview

SLC2A3 (GLUT3) is a high-affinity facilitative glucose transporter that mediates glucose uptake into cells. It is predominantly expressed in neurons under normal conditions, but is frequently upregulated in cancer cells to support the Warburg effect (aerobic glycolysis). SLC2A3 upregulation in tumors is associated with increased glucose consumption and aggressive phenotypes.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Glucose transporter elevated in MSI colon tumors; central to the MSI glycolytic phenotype and inversely correlated with CD8 infiltration; proposed as target for glycolysis inhibition combined with checkpoint blockade in MSI-H CRC ([coad_cptac_2019](../datasets/coad_cptac_2019.md)) [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Colorectal cancer ([COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md)) — SLC2A3 protein elevated specifically in MSI-H colon tumors; high glycolytic activity (SLC2A3 and PKM2) inversely correlates with CD8+ T-cell infiltration in MSI tumors, suggesting that glycolysis suppresses anti-tumor immunity and may explain checkpoint-inhibitor resistance in a subset of MSI-H CRC [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-elevated with [PKM](../genes/PKM.md) (pyruvate kinase M2 isoform) in MSI colon tumors as part of the MSI glycolytic phenotype; together inversely correlated with [CD8A](../genes/CD8A.md) expression [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- The negative correlation between SLC2A3-driven glycolysis and CD8 infiltration in MSI-H colon tumors supports combining glycolysis inhibitors (SLC2A3-targeting agents, [PFKFB3](../genes/PFKFB3.md) inhibitors) with immune checkpoint blockade as a strategy for MSI-H CRC tumors that fail to respond to checkpoint inhibition [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Open questions

- Whether glycolysis inhibition can restore CD8 T-cell infiltration and synergize with checkpoint blockade in MSI-H CRC requires in vivo/clinical validation; all therapeutic hypotheses in the CPTAC proteogenomics study are computationally generated.
