# E2F3

## Overview

E2F3 encodes a transcription factor of the E2F family that drives cell-cycle progression. In bladder urothelial carcinoma, E2F3 amplification is notably enriched compared to other epithelial tumor types and is proposed as a bladder-lineage-specific driver analogous to [MITF](../genes/MITF.md) in melanoma. High E2F3 amplification correlates with advanced stage and neuroendocrine differentiation.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Amplification in 21% of bladder tumors (vs 4.9% in 1,932 non-urothelial epithelial tumors); associated with advanced stage and high E2F3-target gene signature; enriched in neuroendocrine-differentiated tumors (50% vs 17%; P=0.03); proposed as a bladder-lineage-specific driver [PMID:23897969](../papers/23897969.md)
- Focal amplification in bladder TCC [PMID:24121792](../papers/24121792.md)
- Focal amplification of E2F3 observed in 20% of muscle-invasive bladder cancers (peak region shared with [SOX4](../genes/SOX4.md)) in TCGA urothelial carcinoma comprehensive analysis [PMID:24476821](../papers/24476821.md)
- 6p22.3 amplification is a defining feature of CN Cluster B in urothelial carcinoma (P=0.001 vs other clusters); clusters with [SETDB1](../genes/SETDB1.md) and [MLLT11](../genes/MLLT11.md) 1q21.1 amplification (P=0.0002) [PMID:27749842](../papers/27749842.md)
- Recurrently amplified in MIBC; mutually exclusive with CDKN2A loss and FGFR3 mutation; co-occurs with TP53 loss and RB1 inactivation in the neuronal subtype; 10/20 neuronal tumors carry TP53+RB1 co-mutation or TP53+E2F3 co-alteration [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md): amplification in 21% of high-grade bladder tumors; enriched in neuroendocrine subset and advanced-stage disease [PMID:23897969](../papers/23897969.md) [PMID:24121792](../papers/24121792.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Enriched in tumors with [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) loss and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation in the neuroendocrine-differentiated subset of bladder cancer [PMID:23897969](../papers/23897969.md)
- Amplified alongside [E2F1](../genes/E2F1.md), [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md), [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md), and [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) in bladder TCC [PMID:24121792](../papers/24121792.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- High E2F3-target gene signature in amplified tumors provides a potential transcriptomic biomarker; direct therapeutic strategies not described in the current corpus.

## Open questions

- Whether E2F3 amplification is a bladder-specific vulnerability amenable to targeted therapy (analogous to [MITF](../genes/MITF.md) in melanoma) remains to be established.
