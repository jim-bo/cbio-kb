# Juvenile Papillomatosis and Breast Cancer (MSK, J Pathol 2021)

## Overview

The brca_jup_msk_2020 dataset was generated by D'Alfonso et al. (J Pathol Clin Res, 2021) at MSKCC and Weill Cornell. It contains whole-exome sequencing data from 3 juvenile papillomatosis (JP) lesions of the breast, including one case (JuP3) with synchronously identified [DCIS](../cancer_types/DCIS.md) and invasive IDC-NST. Microdissected hyperplastic epithelial components (and carcinoma components of JuP3) were sequenced against matched benign non-hyperplastic breast parenchyma. All patients were young women aged 31–43. Data are deposited on cBioPortal (hg19/GRCh37).

## Composition

- **Total cases:** 3 JP lesions (JuP1, JuP2, JuP3).
- **Cancer types:** [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) — JuP3 includes coexisting [DCIS](../cancer_types/DCIS.md) and invasive [IDC-NST](../cancer_types/IDC.md).
- **Clinical context:** Young women (ages 31–43); JuP2 and JuP3 had family history of breast carcinoma. JP classified as histologically benign.
- **Mutation burden (JP component):** Median 9 total mutations (range 6–25), 6 nonsynonymous mutations (range 3–18), with scant copy-number alterations.
- **Sequencing:** FFPE MSKCC Integrated Genomics Operation; reads aligned to GRCh37 with BWA v0.7.15.

## Assays / panels (linked)

- [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) — Microdissected FFPE sections (10–15 × 8 µm), Qiagen DNAeasy extraction; [BWA](../methods/bwa.md) + [GATK](../methods/gatk.md) alignment; [MuTect](../methods/mutect.md) for SNVs; [Strelka](../methods/strelka.md) + [VarScan2](../methods/varscan.md) for indels; [FACETS](../methods/facets.md) for CNA/LOH; [ABSOLUTE](../methods/absolute.md) for cancer cell fractions; [SigMA](../methods/sigma-mutational-signatures.md) for mutational signatures.

## Papers using this cohort

- [PMID:33263939](../papers/33263939.md) — D'Alfonso et al. (J Pathol Clin Res 2021): Whole-exome sequencing analysis of juvenile papillomatosis and coexisting breast carcinoma.

## Notable findings derived from this cohort

- Clonal somatic [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) hotspot mutations in 2 of 3 JP cases (H1047R in JuP2; E542K in JuP3), supporting recurrent PI3K pathway activation in this rare benign lesion [PMID:33263939](../papers/33263939.md).
- JP, DCIS, and IDC-NST components of JuP3 are clonally related, sharing the [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) E542K hotspot mutation, demonstrating that JP can constitute a non-obligate substrate for invasive breast carcinoma development [PMID:33263939](../papers/33263939.md).
- The carcinoma components (DCIS + IDC-NST) of JuP3 acquired 29 private mutations including truncating [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) missense, [PIK3CB](../genes/PIK3CB.md) missense, and 1q gain/16q loss (canonical ER-positive breast CNA), absent from the matched JP [PMID:33263939](../papers/33263939.md).
- Mutational signature shifted from aging-related Signature 5 (76% in JP) to APOBEC Signature 2 dominance (55% in DCIS; 64% in IDC-NST) in JuP3, consistent with APOBEC activity driving carcinomatous progression [PMID:33263939](../papers/33263939.md).
