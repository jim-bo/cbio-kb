**PMID:** 39185963 · **DOI:** 10.1158/2159-8290.CD-24-0286 · **Journal:** Cancer Discovery (2025)

## TL;DR

Rekhtman et al. provide the first detailed genomic and clinicopathologic description of "atypical" small cell lung carcinoma (aSCLC) — a rare subset of [[[SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md)]] that retains wild-type [[[RB1](../genes/RB1.md)]] and [[[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)]] and arises almost exclusively in never/light smokers (≤10 pack-years). Among the first 600 consecutive de novo SCLC patients sequenced by [[MSK-IMPACT|[msk-impact-panel](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md)]] at MSKCC, 20 (3%) met criteria for aSCLC. Integrated MSK-IMPACT, whole-genome sequencing, RNA-seq, IHC, and FISH on this 20-patient cohort revealed that 16/19 (84%) non-MSI aSCLC cases harbored extensive chromothripsis — most often on chromosome 11 or 12 — producing extrachromosomal (ecDNA) amplification of [[[CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md)]], or co-amplification of [[[CCND2](../genes/CCND2.md)]]/[[[CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md)]]/[[[MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md)]], plus enrichment for carcinoid-associated mutations ([[[MEN1](../genes/MEN1.md)]], [[[EIF1AX](../genes/EIF1AX.md)]], [[[ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md)]], [[[ATM](../genes/ATM.md)]]). The data argue for a novel pathogenetic pathway in which never-smoker SCLC arises by chromothripsis-driven plasticity from pulmonary carcinoid–like progenitors, distinct from the EGFR/APOBEC-driven adenocarcinoma-to-SCLC transformation pathway seen in RB1−/TP53− never-smoker SCLC.

## Cohort & data

- **20 patients** with aSCLC ([[RB1]]+/[[TP53]]+, never/light smokers ≤10 pack-years) identified from a prospectively profiled cohort of 600 consecutive de novo SCLC ([[[asclc_msk_2024](../datasets/asclc_msk_2024.md)]]; MSKCC).
- **Comparator cohorts** profiled on the same platform: smoking-associated SCLC (sSCLC, n = 206); pulmonary carcinoids (n = 157); never-smoker RB1−/TP53− SCLC (nsSCLC-RB1−/TP53−, n = 18); atypical carcinoids (n = 50 for survival).
- **Assays**: [[MSK-IMPACT|msk-impact-panel]] targeted NGS on all 20 patients (mean 613× coverage); [[[whole-genome-seq](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md)]] on 12 samples from 11 patients; [[[rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md)]] on 7 samples; [[[fish](../methods/fish.md)]] for [[CCND1]] and [[CCND2]]/[[CDK4]]/[[MDM2]] in 5 cases; copy-number segmentation by [[FACETS|[facets](../methods/facets.md)]]; mutational-signature analysis ([[SigMA-style|[sigma-mutational-signatures](../methods/sigma-mutational-signatures.md)]]); IHC for pRb, p53, p16, neuroendocrine markers, OTP, and transcriptional-subtype markers.
- 49 pathology specimens (1–7 per patient) reviewed; serial samples enabled multisample/temporal analyses.

## Key findings

- **aSCLC is rare and distinct.** 20/600 (3%) of de novo SCLC at MSKCC retained both [[RB1]] and [[TP53]]; all 20 were never or light smokers. Compared to other SCLC, aSCLC patients were significantly younger (mean age 53 vs. 67 yr; P < 0.0001), had lower Ki67 (mean 69% vs. 87%; P < 0.0001), and lower pack-years (P < 0.0001).
- **Chromothripsis is the hallmark mechanism.** 16 of 19 non-MSI aSCLC (84%) showed chromothripsis by combined WGS + tNGS; chromosome 11 was affected in 35% (always co-occurring with chromosome 3), chromosome 12 in 20%, and chromosome 1 in 15%. Mean of 565 SVs per WGS case (up to >1,900).
- **Recurrent ecDNA-amplified oncogenes:** [[CCND1]] (30%, up to 125 copies), co-amplification of [[CCND2]]/[[CDK4]]±[[MDM2]] (15%), and [[[MYCL](../genes/MYCL.md)]] (10%; reported as MYCL1). Chromothripsis on chr11 was seen in 5/6 [[CCND1]]-amplified cases, chr12 in 3/3 [[CCND2]]/[[CDK4]]/[[MDM2]] co-amplified cases, and chr1 in 2/2 [[MYCL]]-amplified cases. [[fish]] confirmed extrachromosomal ("double-minute") amplification in all 5 tested cases.
- **Enhancer hijacking in lieu of amplification.** Case A17 lacked [[CCND1]] amplification but had a translocation between [[[SH3RF3](../genes/SH3RF3.md)]] (chr2) and the [[CCND1]] upstream regulatory region, producing strong cyclin D1 overexpression by IHC and RNA-seq.
- **TMB is low and the smoking signature is absent.** Most aSCLC had TMB <2 mut/Mb; the two high-TMB outliers were one post-[[[temozolomide](../drugs/temozolomide.md)]] case (A07) and one MSI-H case (A20, with biallelic [[[MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md)]] loss and concordant [[MLH1]]/[[[PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md)]] IHC loss).
- **Carcinoid-like mutational landscape.** Recurrent mutations in [[MEN1]] (15%), [[EIF1AX]] (10%), [[ARID1A]] (25%), and notably enriched [[ATM]] (30% vs. 8% in carcinoids, P = 0.008; vs. 3% in SCLC, P = 0.0003). Recurrent deletion of [[[TGFBR2](../genes/TGFBR2.md)]] (3p24) on chromothriptic chr3.
- **Co-amplifications phenocopy RB1/TP53 loss.** [[MDM2]] (chr12) co-amplified with [[CDK4]] and [[CCND2]] is interpreted as a functional phenocopy of TP53/RB1 co-inactivation; [[[CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)]] (p16) is paradoxically high in chr12-chromothriptic cases despite wild-type pRb, possibly reflecting extreme post-translational pRb inhibition by ecDNA-amplified [[CDK4]].
- **Histogenetic link to pulmonary carcinoids ([[[LNET](../cancer_types/LNET.md)]]).** 5/20 patients had carcinoid histotype in primary tumor and SCLC histotype in metastases; an additional subset expressed OTP (a carcinoid-specific marker); 55% of aSCLC overall showed at least one line of evidence for a carcinoid relationship.
- **Chromothripsis is early and clonally stable.** Multisample analysis (8 patients via tNGS, 1 via WGS) showed identical chromothripsis patterns and amplifications across primary and metastatic samples and across pre/post-therapy time points. MutationTimeR placed the amplifications early in tumorigenesis.
- **Two pathogenetic pathways for never-smoker SCLC.** aSCLC ([[RB1]]+/[[TP53]]+) is ASCL1-dominant (100%), YAP1-negative, and APOBEC-low; nsSCLC-[[RB1]]−/[[TP53]]− is enriched for [[[EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md)]] mutations (39%), [[[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)]] mutations (6%), [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) histologic components ([[[LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md)]]-to-SCLC transformation), APOBEC mutagenesis, and [YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md) expression.
- **Acquired RB1 loss can occur late.** Two patients (A16, A20) acquired private [[RB1]] mutations in metastatic samples with concordant pRb IHC loss; one primary (A12) had a subclonal [[RB1]] mutation. [[TP53]] remained wild-type across all multisample analyses.

## Genes & alterations

- [[RB1]] — wild-type by definition in aSCLC; private acquired truncating mutations seen in metastases of A16/A20 and a subclonal mutation in A12.
- [[TP53]] — wild-type by definition; uniformly retained across multisample analysis.
- [[CCND1]] — ecDNA amplification on chromothriptic chr11 in 30% of cases (up to 125 copies); enhancer-hijack overexpression in A17 via [[SH3RF3]] translocation.
- [[CCND2]], [[CDK4]], [[MDM2]] — co-amplified on chromothriptic chr12 (12q13–q15) in 15% of cases; ecDNA confirmed by [[fish]].
- [[MYCL]] — focal amplification on chromothriptic chr1 (1p34) in 10% of cases (reported as MYCL1).
- [[ATM]] — somatic mutations in 30% of aSCLC, significantly enriched vs. carcinoids (8%) and SCLC (3%); no pathogenic germline ATM variants detected.
- [[ARID1A]] (25%), [[MEN1]] (15%), [[EIF1AX]] (10%) — carcinoid-associated mutations enriched in aSCLC.
- [[[HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md)]] — amplified on chromothriptic chr11 in some cases.
- [[KRAS]] (12q12), [[[ERBB3](../genes/ERBB3.md)]] (12q13) — amplified on chromothriptic chr12.
- [[TGFBR2]] (3p24) — recurrent deletion on chromothriptic chr3.
- [[MLH1]] — homozygous deletion in the sole MSI-H case (A20), with concordant [[MLH1]]/[[PMS2]] IHC loss.
- [[CDKN2A]] (p16) — paradoxically overexpressed in chr12-chromothriptic, [[CDK4]]-amplified cases.
- [[EGFR]] (39%) and [[KRAS]] (6%) — enriched in the comparator nsSCLC-[[RB1]]−/[[TP53]]− cohort, NOT in aSCLC.
- [[[ASCL1](../genes/ASCL1.md)]] — uniformly high in aSCLC (100% ASCL1-dominant subtype); [[[NEUROD1](../genes/NEUROD1.md)]], [[[POU2F3](../genes/POU2F3.md)]], and [[YAP1]] absent or rare.
- [[[DLL3](../genes/DLL3.md)]], [[[SEZ6](../genes/SEZ6.md)]] — high IHC expression in all 9 evaluable aSCLC samples (mean H-scores 278 and 240, respectively), positioning aSCLC as candidates for DLL3- and SEZ6-targeted therapy.
- [[[TERT](../genes/TERT.md)]] — no overexpression detected; no ALT footprints found.

## Clinical implications

- **Survival**: aSCLC has intermediate survival between sSCLC and atypical carcinoids — median overall survival 58 months for aSCLC vs. 16 months (sSCLC), 22 months (nsSCLC-RB1−/TP53−), and 184 months (atypical carcinoids).
- **Platinum response is below historical SCLC norms.** Among 15 aSCLC patients with [[[cisplatin](../drugs/cisplatin.md)]]/[[[carboplatin](../drugs/carboplatin.md)]] + [[[etoposide](../drugs/etoposide.md)]] response data, complete response 13% and partial response 20% (combined ORR 33%, vs. ~70% historical for conventional SCLC).
- **Possible TMZ sensitivity.** 4 of 6 patients treated with [[temozolomide]] had >10 months on treatment (up to 2 years); the two longest responders (A01, A07) had the lowest [MGMT](../genes/MGMT.md) mRNA expression.
- **Possible immune-checkpoint sensitivity.** 3 of 5 patients treated with checkpoint inhibitors had 2 to >5 years time-on-treatment.
- **Therapeutic biomarkers.** Universally high [[DLL3]] and [[SEZ6]] expression suggests aSCLC patients may be candidates for emerging targeted agents against these markers; chromothripsis-driven [[CDK4]]/[[CCND2]] amplification provides rationale for CDK4/6-pathway investigation.

## Limitations & open questions

- Cohort is small (n = 20) and single-institution (MSKCC), limiting power to associate genomic features with survival or treatment response.
- The driver gene(s) on the always-co-thriptic chromosome 3 remain unclear; only [[TGFBR2]] deletion was recurrent (2 cases).
- Mechanism initiating chromothripsis is not established — no germline cancer-predisposition variants, no viral DNA detected, and no [[TERT]] overexpression or ALT signature; somatic [[ATM]] mutations are a candidate predisposing factor but causality is not demonstrated.
- Whether carcinoid tumors are bona fide progenitors of aSCLC, or whether both arise from a shared precursor, cannot be resolved by this study; the epigenetic events that may permit carcinoid-to-SCLC plasticity were not directly profiled.
- Two CCND1-overexpressing cases (A11, A17) lacked CCND1 amplification, indicating non-amplification mechanisms (e.g., enhancer hijacking) that may be under-detected.
- tNGS sensitivity for chromothripsis was only 77% vs. WGS, so the real-world prevalence of aSCLC chromothripsis under routine clinical sequencing may be underestimated.
- Limited tissue precluded WGS or RNA-seq for many cases; some clinical-outcome inferences (TMZ, ICI sensitivity) are based on very small numbers.
