**PMID:** 31031003 · **DOI:** [10.1016/j.cell.2019.03.030](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cell.2019.03.030) · **Journal:** Cell (2019)

## TL;DR

The CPTAC consortium performed the first proteogenomic study of a prospectively collected human [colon adenocarcinoma](../cancer_types/COAD.md) cohort: 110 patients with paired tumor and normal-adjacent tissue (NAT) profiled by whole-exome sequencing, copy-number array, RNA-Seq, miRNA-Seq, label-free shotgun proteomics, and TMT-based global and phosphoproteomics ([coad_cptac_2019](../datasets/coad_cptac_2019.md)). Proteogenomic integration corrected genomic inferences (re-classifying [SOX9](../genes/SOX9.md) as oncogenic despite truncating mutations), revealed [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) hyperphosphorylation as a colon-cancer driver suggesting [CDK2](../genes/CDK2.md) inhibition as a therapy, identified 88 putative HLA-binding neoantigens and three recurrent cancer/testis antigens ([IGF2BP3](../genes/IGF2BP3.md), [SPAG1](../genes/SPAG1.md), [ATAD2](../genes/ATAD2.md)), and tied increased glycolysis to decreased CD8 T-cell infiltration in MSI-H tumors — proposing glycolysis inhibition as a strategy to overcome checkpoint-blockade resistance in microsatellite-unstable disease.

## Cohort & data

- **Patients:** 110 prospectively collected colon cancer patients (65 female, 45 male; mean age 65, range 40–93). Inclusion criterion: newly diagnosed, untreated patients undergoing primary surgery for colon adenocarcinoma. Rectal cancers excluded (untreated rectal tumors are hard to obtain).
- **Cancer type:** [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md) — colon adenocarcinoma only (the study deliberately excluded rectal tumors).
- **Dataset:** [coad_cptac_2019](../datasets/coad_cptac_2019.md) — CPTAC-2 colon cohort. Raw genomics in SRA BioProject PRJNA514017; raw proteomics in CPTAC Data Portal S045; processed matrices in LinkedOmics (http://linkedomics.org/cptac-colon/).
- **Platforms applied per omics:**
  - 106 tumor/normal-blood pairs profiled by [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) (Illumina Nextera Rapid Capture Exome, HiSeq4000, ≥150× target coverage).
  - 105 tumor/normal pairs with sufficient off-target reads used for [copy-number inference (CopywriteR + GISTIC2)](../methods/copywriter.md).
  - 106 tumors with [RNA-Seq](../methods/rna-seq.md); also miRNA-Seq.
  - Label-free shotgun proteomics on 100 tumors plus 96 tumor/NAT pairs profiled by [TMT global proteomics](../methods/tmt-proteomics.md) and [TMT phosphoproteomics](../methods/tmt-phosphoproteomics.md).
  - [Selected Reaction Monitoring (SRM)](../methods/selected-reaction-monitoring.md) for targeted validation of [CD8A](../genes/CD8A.md), [SLC2A3](../genes/SLC2A3.md), and [PKM](../genes/PKM.md) isoforms.
  - PCR-based MSI testing (Promega MSI Analysis System; 5 mononucleotide + 2 pentanucleotide markers; see [msi-pcr-pentaplex](../methods/msi-pcr-pentaplex.md)) on 85 samples — 100% concordance with WXS-based MSI calls.
- **Cross-cohort comparison:** Average mRNA profiles correlated with the TCGA colorectal cohort at Pearson's r = 0.92; average label-free protein profiles at r = 0.96 — both higher than between different cancer types or between colon tumors and cell lines.

## Key findings

- **Mutation landscape:** WXS identified 64,010 somatic SNVs, 7,691 somatic INDELs, and 6,186 somatic microsatellite INDELs across 106 tumors — 56,592 unique protein-altering events. The MS INDEL distribution was bimodal, partitioning the cohort into 24 MSI-H tumors and 82 MSS tumors. MSI-H tumors had increased A>G/T>C transitions and decreased G>C/C>G transversions and were enriched for mismatch-repair-pathway, [POLE](../genes/POLE.md), and [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) mutations.
- **Significantly mutated genes:** Eight in the non-hypermutated group (all previously reported by TCGA); nine in the hypermutated group, six of which were not reported in TCGA. Four newly identified genes — [CASP5](../genes/CASP5.md), [RNF43](../genes/RNF43.md), [LTN1](../genes/LTN1.md), and [BMPR2](../genes/BMPR2.md) — were mutated in >50% of hypermutated samples.
- **Proteomic readout of mutations:** Tumors with stop-gain/frameshift [APC](../genes/APC.md) mutations had reduced abundance of phosphosite APC-T2451 downstream of the truncation. [TGFBR2](../genes/TGFBR2.md) frameshift mutations reduced TGFBR2-S553 phosphorylation. [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)-S315 phosphorylation was increased in tumors over matched NATs (p=0.001, paired t-test); three tumors with the R273 hotspot mutation had >10-fold increases — corroborated by reanalyzing CPTAC breast and ovarian cancers where R273-mutant tumors had 3.4–83× TP53-S315 phosphorylation above cohort medians.
- **[SOX9](../genes/SOX9.md) re-classification:** Mutated in 19 tumors (6 stop-gain, 8 frameshift, 5 nonsynonymous SNVs). The 20/20 rule classifies it as a tumor suppressor, but [SOX9](../genes/SOX9.md) protein was significantly *overexpressed* in tumors versus matched NATs (p = 1.02×10⁻¹⁰, paired t-test) — even within the subset bearing truncating mutations (p=0.04). Truncations cluster downstream of the HMG-box and upstream of K398, the conserved ubiquitin-target site for 26S proteasomal degradation; loss of K398 likely stabilizes the protein. The proteomic evidence reclassifies SOX9 as oncogenic, consistent with functional CRC assays.
- **Copy-number drivers prioritized by protein:** Arm-level SCNAs matched TCGA (amplifications of 1q, 7p/q, 8p/q, 13q, 20p/q; deletions of 1p, 14q, 15q, 17p/q, 18p/q, 22q). cis-effects of SCNA on protein were stronger than on mRNA. Among focal driver candidates, only 59% showed expected protein-level effects in tumor vs NAT — NAT data substantially refined the driver list. Confirmed amplification drivers included [HNF4A](../genes/HNF4A.md) and [TOMM34](../genes/TOMM34.md) on 20q; confirmed deletion driver [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) on 18q. 17 SMGs + 90 prioritized SCNA genes were enriched for colorectal cancer, proliferation, apoptosis, Hippo signaling, endosome organization, and endocytosis (the latter spanning six genes across multiple focal deletions).
- **Rb phosphorylation as a driver:** [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) was recurrently amplified, and Rb protein was overexpressed in tumors versus NATs (p = 2.10×10⁻¹⁵). Phosphoproteomics quantified four E2F-regulating Rb phosphosites (T373, S807, S811, T826) — average phospho-Rb was 1.84-fold higher in tumors vs NATs (p < 2.2×10⁻¹⁶), significantly exceeding the 1.58-fold increase in total Rb (p=0.01, Wilcoxon). Phospho-Rb change correlated with predicted [E2F1](../genes/E2F1.md) activity (r=0.30, p=3.5×10⁻³), with [CDK2](../genes/CDK2.md) predicted activity (r=0.47, p=1.8×10⁻⁶) more strongly than any other kinase, with histone H3.1 phosphorylation (a proliferation marker; r=0.49, p=2.6×10⁻⁴), and negatively with the apoptosis hallmark gene set (r=−0.28, p=5.3×10⁻³). Conclusion: hyperphosphorylation, not [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) loss, drives proliferation and dampens apoptosis in colon cancer — a vulnerability addressable by [CDK2](../genes/CDK2.md) inhibition.
- **Cancer-associated proteins/phosphosites:** Among 6,422 proteins quantifiable in ≥50% of samples, 2,217 (35%) were significantly increased and 2,527 (39%) significantly decreased in tumors versus paired NATs (adj. p < 0.01). 31 proteins increased >2-fold (defined as "colon-cancer-associated"); 8 were elevated in >95% of pairs ([DDX21](../genes/DDX21.md) 100%, [S100A11](../genes/S100A11.md) 100%, RSL1D1 99%, [S100P](../genes/S100P.md) 97%, RPL36A 97%, [PLOD2](../genes/PLOD2.md) 96%, [SERPINH1](../genes/SERPINH1.md) 95%, [GPRC5A](../genes/GPRC5A.md) 95%). Among 7,295 quantifiable phosphosites, 63 were upregulated >2-fold (mapping to 50 proteins); only four proteins overlapped the protein and phosphosite lists. Only 7 of the combined protein/phosphosite-defined cancer proteins are in the Cancer Gene Census — most are invisible to mutation-based discovery.
- **Druggable kinases:** Activity prediction (kinase-activating site phosphorylation + target-site enrichment) flagged [CDK1](../genes/CDK1.md), [CDK2](../genes/CDK2.md), [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md), [CDK7](../genes/CDK7.md), [MELK](../genes/MELK.md), [PFKFB3](../genes/PFKFB3.md), and [PI4KB](../genes/PI4KB.md) as elevated in tumors. [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) has FDA-approved inhibitors; CDK1/2/7, [MELK](../genes/MELK.md), and [PFKFB3](../genes/PFKFB3.md) are targeted by clinical-trial drugs. [PI4KB](../genes/PI4KB.md) is a novel candidate.
- **Tumor antigens:** Customized-database proteomics search recovered 173 proteomics-supported somatic mutations; 88 mutant 9–11mer peptides had high predicted HLA-I binding affinity (putative neoantigens). One or more neoantigens were identified in 38% of tumors. 16 cancer/testis antigens were detected; three were >2-fold elevated in tumors in >5% of pairs: [IGF2BP3](../genes/IGF2BP3.md) (51%), [SPAG1](../genes/SPAG1.md) (14%), [ATAD2](../genes/ATAD2.md) (8%). Proteomics-supported neoantigens or CT antigens were found in 78% of tumors. Neoantigens were enriched in MSI-H tumors and patient-specific; CT antigens were MSI-status-independent and shared across patients.
- **Unified molecular subtypes (UMS):** Network integration of consensus molecular subtypes (CMS1–4; [cms-classifier](../methods/cms-classifier.md)), the previously published proteomic subtypes (ProS A–E), and MSI status collapsed the heterogeneity into three subtypes — MSI, CIN, and Mesenchymal. 87/110 tumors assigned. CMS3 dissolved (its molecular boundary was vague). Tumors in the CIN subtype had higher chromosome instability and higher [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) copy number; Rb-S811 and S807 were 1.61- and 1.51-fold higher in CIN vs other subtypes (much greater than total Rb at 1.13-fold), suggesting CDK2 inhibition may be most effective in CIN. The Mesenchymal subtype had elevated stromal infiltration and suppressor immune cells (MDSCs, macrophages, Tregs); MSI was enriched in cytotoxic immune cells (NK, activated CD8).
- **Glycolysis–immune-evasion axis in MSI:** Despite cytotoxic-cell enrichment, MSI tumors universally upregulated glycolytic enzymes at the protein level (also at mRNA, broadly) while TCA-cycle enzymes were decreased *only at the protein level* — a protein-level Warburg effect. Almost all glycolytic enzymes correlated negatively with [CD8](../genes/CD8A.md) infiltration in MSI tumors; median glycolytic-enzyme abundance versus CD8 infiltration: Spearman ρ = −0.61, p = 0.02. This anti-correlation was absent in other subtypes or when all tumors were pooled. SRM validation confirmed [SLC2A3](../genes/SLC2A3.md) (1.85-fold) and [PKM](../genes/PKM.md)2 (1.4-fold) elevation in MSI vs other UMS subtypes (p=0.007 and 0.003, t-test), and showed PKM2 (not PKM1) is the predominant isoform measured.

## Genes & alterations

- **[APC](../genes/APC.md):** stop-gain and frameshift mutations; reduced abundance of downstream phosphosite APC-T2451 in mutant tumors.
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md):** R273 hotspot missense mutations associated with >10-fold elevation of TP53-S315 phosphorylation (paired t-test p=0.001 for global tumor-NAT increase); confirmed in CPTAC breast and ovarian cohorts.
- **[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [POLE](../genes/POLE.md):** classic colon-cancer SMGs; [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) and [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) mutations enriched in MSI-H/hypermutated tumors.
- **[SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md):** confirmed deletion driver in 18q focal-deletion region.
- **[HNF4A](../genes/HNF4A.md), [TOMM34](../genes/TOMM34.md):** confirmed 20q amplification drivers via integrated copy-number/mRNA/protein prioritization.
- **[TGFBR2](../genes/TGFBR2.md):** frameshift mutations reduce TGFBR2-S553 phosphosite abundance.
- **[CASP5](../genes/CASP5.md), [RNF43](../genes/RNF43.md), [LTN1](../genes/LTN1.md), [BMPR2](../genes/BMPR2.md):** newly identified hypermutated-group SMGs (>50% mutation frequency in hypermutated samples), not reported in the TCGA colorectal study.
- **[SOX9](../genes/SOX9.md):** 19 mutated tumors (mostly truncating, downstream of HMG box, upstream of K398 ubiquitin site). Despite genomic prediction of tumor-suppressor status (20/20 rule), protein is significantly *overexpressed* in tumors vs NATs (p=1.02×10⁻¹⁰); truncations stabilize the protein. Reclassified as oncogene.
- **[RB1](../genes/RB1.md):** recurrently amplified (CIN subtype enriched); protein overexpressed (p=2.10×10⁻¹⁵); hyperphosphorylation at T373/S807/S811/T826 (E2F-regulating sites) drives proliferation and suppresses apoptosis.
- **[E2F1](../genes/E2F1.md):** activity (inferred) positively correlated with total Rb (r=0.40, p=5.1×10⁻⁵) and phospho-Rb (r=0.30, p=3.5×10⁻³) — supports an [E2F1](../genes/E2F1.md)–cyclin-E–CDK2–phospho-Rb feedback loop.
- **[CDK1](../genes/CDK1.md), [CDK2](../genes/CDK2.md), [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md), [CDK7](../genes/CDK7.md), [MELK](../genes/MELK.md), [PFKFB3](../genes/PFKFB3.md), [PI4KB](../genes/PI4KB.md):** elevated kinase activities in tumors; targetable therapeutic candidates.
- **[CEACAM5](../genes/CEACAM5.md):** confirmed clinical CRC biomarker, recovered among cancer-associated proteins.
- **[IGF2BP3](../genes/IGF2BP3.md), [SPAG1](../genes/SPAG1.md), [ATAD2](../genes/ATAD2.md):** recurrent CT antigens (in 51%, 14%, 8% of tumor-NAT pairs respectively). [IGF2BP3](../genes/IGF2BP3.md) has demonstrated in-vitro/in-vivo CTL induction in esophageal cancer.
- **[SLC2A3](../genes/SLC2A3.md), [PKM](../genes/PKM.md):** glucose transporter and pyruvate kinase elevated in MSI tumors (PKM2 is the predominant isoform); central to the MSI glycolytic phenotype and inversely correlated with CD8 infiltration.
- **[CD8A](../genes/CD8A.md):** marker for CD8 T-cell infiltration; reduced in MSI tumors with high glycolysis.
- **[DDX21](../genes/DDX21.md), [S100A11](../genes/S100A11.md), [S100P](../genes/S100P.md), [PLOD2](../genes/PLOD2.md), [SERPINH1](../genes/SERPINH1.md), [GPRC5A](../genes/GPRC5A.md):** most uniformly upregulated proteins across tumor-NAT pairs (≥95%), candidates for biomarker/therapeutic follow-up.

## Clinical implications

- **CDK2 inhibition for colon cancer:** Phospho-Rb is the principal Rb-pathway lesion in [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md); CDK2 inhibitors should be evaluated, particularly in CIN-subtype tumors where RB1 copy gain and Rb phosphorylation are highest. This opportunity is invisible to RB1 mutation/deletion analysis used in other cancers.
- **Glycolysis inhibition + checkpoint blockade in MSI-H CRC:** The negative correlation between glycolytic activity and CD8 infiltration specifically in MSI tumors supports combining glycolysis inhibitors (e.g., PKM2- or SLC2A3-targeting agents, PFKFB3 inhibitors) with immune checkpoint blockade as a strategy for MSI-H tumors that fail to respond to checkpoint inhibition.
- **Personalized neoantigen vaccines:** Proteomics-supported filtering (vs. mRNA-only filtering) identified high-affinity-HLA-binding neoantigens for 38% of tumors. The authors argue this is a more effective prioritization because neoepitopes are themselves peptides.
- **CT-antigen vaccines for MSS tumors:** IGF2BP3, [SPAG1](../genes/SPAG1.md), and [ATAD2](../genes/ATAD2.md) are MSI-status-independent recurrent CT antigens — potential immunotherapy targets for MSS colon tumors that are poor candidates for checkpoint blockade or neoantigen vaccines.
- **Druggable kinases beyond CDKs:** CDK4 (palbociclib-class inhibitors approved); [CDK1](../genes/CDK1.md), CDK2, [CDK7](../genes/CDK7.md), MELK, and PFKFB3 inhibitors in clinical development. PI4KB is a novel target proposed by this work.
- **Biomarkers:** 15 of the 31 cancer-associated proteins have known clinical utility (diagnostic, outcome, or therapeutic), including [CEACAM5](../genes/CEACAM5.md) (standard-of-care CRC marker). 19 of the 31 are detectable in plasma — candidate blood biomarkers.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Cohort scope:** Colon-only, not colorectal — rectal tumors were excluded because untreated rectal cancers are difficult to obtain. Generalization to rectal cancer requires separate validation.
- **Mechanistic question:** Why is RB1 recurrently *amplified* in colon cancer if the protein's inhibitory activity is then negated by phosphorylation? The authors flag this as mechanistically inefficient and unresolved; the antiapoptotic role of phospho-Rb (negative correlation with apoptosis hallmark, r=−0.28) is a partial answer but motivates further work.
- **MSI subtype heterogeneity:** Three MSI-H tumors with relatively fewer MS INDELs were assigned to the Mesenchymal UMS subtype — boundary cases the unified classification absorbs but does not fully explain.
- **No therapeutic validation:** All therapeutic hypotheses (CDK2 inhibition for COAD, glycolysis inhibition for MSI checkpoint resistance, PI4KB targeting, CT-antigen vaccines) are generated computationally from omics data; in vivo/clinical validation is explicitly outside the scope.
- **Sex-stratified analyses not performed:** Sample size insufficient after stratification (65F/45M).
- **Sample sizes for some readouts:** 96 tumor/NAT pairs for global and phospho-TMT; 100 tumors for label-free proteomics; 105 for SCNA; 106 for RNA-Seq/WXS — minor non-overlap across modalities reduces power on a per-feature basis.
- **SNP array data discarded:** Illumina SNP array data had low dynamic range and were dropped in favor of WXS-derived SCNAs — limits comparability with array-based studies.
