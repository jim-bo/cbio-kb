**PMID:** 31278255 · **DOI:** 10.1038/s41467-019-10873-y · **Journal:** Nature Communications (2019)

## TL;DR

Robinson, Vlachostergios and colleagues performed integrated whole-exome and RNA sequencing of 37 high-grade upper tract urothelial carcinoma ([UTUC](../cancer_types/UTUC.md)) tumors from Weill Cornell Medicine (WCM, n=22) and Baylor College of Medicine / MD Anderson Cancer Center (BCM-MDA, n=15), compared against the TCGA muscle-invasive bladder cancer cohort. They show that the great majority of high-grade sporadic UTUCs are luminal-papillary by UNC/MDACC/TCGA classifiers, are T-cell depleted at the immune-contexture level, harbor [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) mutations in ~30% of cases, and despite reduced MMR protein expression are not microsatellite-unstable and carry a *lower* tumor mutational burden than urothelial carcinoma of the bladder (UCB). They further propose [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) as a cell-intrinsic driver of T-cell exclusion via attenuated [IFNG](../genes/IFNG.md) signaling and provide rationale for combining [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) inhibitors (e.g. [erdafitinib](../drugs/erdafitinib.md)) with immune checkpoint blockade in UTUC.

## Cohort & data

- **Patients:** 37 high-grade UTUC tumor–normal pairs profiled by whole-exome sequencing (WCM UTUC n=22; BCM-MDA UTUC n=15). RNA-seq meta-dataset: 32 UTUC tumors (WCM n=17; BCM-MDA n=15). 64.8% of patients were former/current smokers.
- **Cancer type:** high-grade [upper tract urothelial carcinoma (UTUC)](../cancer_types/UTUC.md), nephroureterectomy or endoscopic biopsy specimens; "conventional" urothelial histology only.
- **Comparator cohort:** TCGA muscle-invasive bladder cancer ([blca_tcga_pub_2017](../datasets/blca_tcga_pub_2017.md), n=124 WES / n=128 RNA-seq); see [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md) for the source TCGA UCB study.
- **Dataset of record:** [utuc_cornell_baylor_mdacc_2019](../datasets/utuc_cornell_baylor_mdacc_2019.md) on cBioPortal (`https://www.cbioportal.org/study?id=utuc_cornell_baylor_mdacc_2019`).
- **Assays / methods:** [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) (Illumina HiSeq 2500, 2×100 bp; ~85× mean coverage for BCM-MDA samples), [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) aligned with [STAR](../methods/star-aligner.md), consensus SNV calling combining [MuTect](../methods/mutect.md) / [Strelka](../methods/strelka.md) / [VarScan](../methods/varscan.md), variant annotation by [Oncotator](../methods/oncotator.md), [MSIsensor](../methods/msisensor.md) for microsatellite instability, [Bayesian NMF](../methods/bayesian-nmf.md) for [mutational signature](../methods/mutational-signatures.md) discovery, [consensus hierarchical clustering](../methods/consensus-hierarchical-clustering.md) for subtype calling (UNC BASE47, MDACC, TCGA classifiers), [GSEA](../methods/gsea.md), and [immunohistochemistry](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md) ([MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md), [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md), [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md) on a Leica Bond III stainer with HALO H-score quantification).
- **Functional validation:** Re-analysis of a published [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) shRNA / doxycycline-inducible knockdown microarray dataset in the RT-112 UCB line; pharmacologic FGFR3 inhibition with [erdafitinib](../drugs/erdafitinib.md) at 1 nM and 5 nM in RT-112, RT-4, and SW780 UCB cell lines, with RT-qPCR readout of [BST2](../genes/BST2.md).

## Key findings

- **FGFR3 mutation enrichment in UTUC.** [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) mutations were detected in **11/37 (29.7%)** UTUC tumors versus **17/124 (13.7%)** in the TCGA UCB comparison (Wilcoxon test, P=0.04).
- **No significant difference in other commonly mutated bladder-cancer drivers.** Mutation prevalence in chromatin modifiers ([KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md)), RTK / PI3K pathway ([PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md)), transcription factors ([RXRA](../genes/RXRA.md), [KLF5](../genes/KLF5.md), [ELF3](../genes/ELF3.md)), and cell-cycle genes ([TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [CDKN1A](../genes/CDKN1A.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)) did not differ significantly between UTUC and TCGA UCB.
- **Dominant mutational signatures in UTUC are APOBEC (COSMIC 2, 13), defective MMR (COSMIC 6, C>T at CpG), and an ERCC2-like signature (COSMIC 5).** Identified by hierarchical clustering of cosine similarity against the 30 COSMIC signatures.
- **UTUC is luminal-papillary.** Across three independent classifiers, the high-grade UTUC tumors clustered overwhelmingly with luminal lineages:
  - UNC BASE47: **27/32 (84.3%)** UTUC luminal vs 59/128 (46.1%) UCB luminal.
  - MDACC classifier: **22/32 (68.7%)** UTUC luminal vs 47/128 (36.7%) UCB luminal.
  - TCGA classifier: **20/32 (62.5%)** UTUC luminal-papillary vs 35/128 (27.3%) UCB luminal-papillary; the majority of remaining UTUC tumors (8/12, 67%) carried other luminal labels.
  - Unsupervised NMF on the top-25%-variable genes (rank k=3) confirmed UTUCs partition with the luminal/CIS-low component.
- **UTUC is T-cell depleted.** A 170-immune-gene classifier built from the top variable genes containing [CD8A](../genes/CD8A.md) split tumors into T-cell-inflamed and T-cell-depleted clusters. **28/32 (87.5%)** UTUC tumors were T-cell depleted versus a roughly even split in UCB (57/128 inflamed, 71/128 depleted); Fisher's exact P=9×10⁻⁵ (UTUC vs UCB) / P=0.0009 reported in the text. T-cell-depleted tumors showed concordant downregulation of [CD8A](../genes/CD8A.md), [CCL2](../genes/CCL2.md), [CCL3](../genes/CCL3.md), [CCL4](../genes/CCL4.md), [CCL5](../genes/CCL5.md), [CXCL9](../genes/CXCL9.md), [CXCL10](../genes/CXCL10.md), and [IFNG](../genes/IFNG.md) response genes.
- **FGFR3 is an outlier-overexpressed gene in UTUC.** Outlier analysis on 74 cancer-related genes flagged [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) high in 14/32 (43.7%) UTUC tumors. Nine activating missense mutations were identified. FGFR3 mRNA was significantly higher in the T-cell-depleted cluster (Wilcoxon P=1.3×10⁻⁶); [PPARG](../genes/PPARG.md) showed the same pattern (P=1.1×10⁻⁵).
- **FGFR3 cell-autonomously suppresses [IFNG](../genes/IFNG.md) response.** In RT-112 UCB cells, doxycycline-induced shRNA knockdown of [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) upregulated IFNG-response genes [BST2](../genes/BST2.md) (P<0.001), [MX2](../genes/MX2.md) (P=0.003), [IRF9](../genes/IRF9.md) (P=0.005), and [GBP2](../genes/GBP2.md) (P=0.038). Pharmacologic inhibition with [erdafitinib](../drugs/erdafitinib.md) at 1 nM and 5 nM significantly upregulated [BST2](../genes/BST2.md) in RT-112, RT-4, and SW780 cells (all three lines harbor activating FGFR3 fusions: RT-112 and RT-4 carry [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)–[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md), SW780 carries [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)–[BAIAP2L1](../genes/BAIAP2L1.md)); no statistically significant difference between 1 nM and 5 nM doses in any line.
- **Somatic MMR transcript and protein loss without MSI or TMB elevation.** 35 canonical DDR genes were differentially expressed between WCM UTUC and TCGA UCB; the MMR transcripts [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md), [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), and [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md) were significantly down in UTUC. Matched IHC on 16 WCM UTUC vs 14 stage-matched WCM UCB showed significantly lower H-scores for MLH1, PMS2, MSH2, and MSH6 (t-test P<0.05; with one MMR-protein composite reported at P=1.9×10⁻⁵). No germline MMR mutations (in MLH1, PMS2, MSH2, MSH6) were detected on germline WES of the UTUC cohorts.
- **UTUC is not microsatellite-unstable.** Mean MSIsensor scores in WCM UTUC and TCGA UCB were both below the MSI-H threshold of 3.5 and indistinguishable from each other.
- **Sporadic UTUC has lower TMB than UCB.** Mean total mutational burden was **2.91 mutations/Mb** in UTUC (WCM+BCM-MDA) vs **5.46 mutations/Mb** in TCGA UCB (Mann–Whitney P=1.9×10⁻⁵).

## Genes & alterations

- **[FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)** — activating missense mutations in 11/37 (29.7%) UTUC tumors, significantly higher than 13.7% in TCGA UCB (P=0.04); outlier-overexpressed in 14/32 UTUC tumors and proposed as a cell-intrinsic driver of the T-cell-depleted phenotype via suppression of [IFNG](../genes/IFNG.md) response genes. Recurrent activating [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)–[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md) and [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)–[BAIAP2L1](../genes/BAIAP2L1.md) fusions are present in the cell-line models used for functional validation.
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md), [CDKN1A](../genes/CDKN1A.md), [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md), [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md), [RXRA](../genes/RXRA.md), [KLF5](../genes/KLF5.md), [ELF3](../genes/ELF3.md), [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md), [RHOB](../genes/RHOB.md), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md)** — recurrently mutated in the UTUC cohort at frequencies not significantly different from TCGA UCB (Fig. 1a–b).
- **[MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md), [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md), [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md)** — significantly reduced transcript (for MLH1/MSH2/MSH6) and protein (all four by IHC) expression in UTUC vs UCB in the absence of germline or somatic mutations; insufficient to cause MSI or elevate TMB.
- **[CD8A](../genes/CD8A.md), [CCL2](../genes/CCL2.md), [CCL3](../genes/CCL3.md), [CCL4](../genes/CCL4.md), [CCL5](../genes/CCL5.md), [CXCL9](../genes/CXCL9.md), [CXCL10](../genes/CXCL10.md), [IFNG](../genes/IFNG.md)** — concordantly downregulated in the T-cell-depleted UTUC cluster.
- **[BST2](../genes/BST2.md), [MX2](../genes/MX2.md), [IRF9](../genes/IRF9.md), [GBP2](../genes/GBP2.md)** — IFNG-response genes upregulated upon shRNA knockdown or pharmacologic inhibition of [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md); proposed mechanistic link to T-cell exclusion in luminal-papillary UTUC.
- **[PPARG](../genes/PPARG.md)** — significantly higher mRNA expression in the T-cell-depleted cluster (P=1.1×10⁻⁵), consistent with a luminal/PPARG-driven phenotype.

## Clinical implications

- **Rationale for FGFR3 inhibition in UTUC.** The combination of (i) enrichment of [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) activating mutations, (ii) outlier FGFR3 overexpression in luminal-papillary UTUC, and (iii) reversal of IFNG-response gene suppression by FGFR3 inhibition supports clinical evaluation of FGFR3 inhibitors in UTUC. The authors invoke the FDA accelerated approval of [erdafitinib](../drugs/erdafitinib.md) in metastatic urothelial carcinoma harboring actionable FGFR alterations (phase 2 response rate 32.2% in 87 patients) as direct precedent.
- **Rationale for combining FGFR3 inhibitors with immune checkpoint blockade.** Because [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) signaling cell-autonomously dampens IFNG response and is enriched in T-cell-depleted UTUC, the authors propose combining FGFR3 inhibitors with PD-1/PD-L1 blockade as a targeted strategy to remodel the immune contexture of UTUC, potentially extendable to other [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)-activated tumors.
- **Mechanistic explanation for blunted ICI response in luminal-papillary UC.** The luminal-papillary, T-cell-depleted, low-TMB profile of sporadic UTUC is consistent with the lower response rates of luminal-papillary UCB to [atezolizumab](../drugs/atezolizumab.md) (Rosenberg et al., IMvigor210; cluster I had the lowest response) and [nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md) (Sharma et al., CheckMate 275; cluster I tumors with low IFNG signature were less responsive).
- **MMR/MSI implications.** Despite reduced MMR transcript and protein expression, sporadic (non-Lynch) UTUC is *not* hypermutated or microsatellite-unstable — arguing against MSI/TMB-based ICI biomarkers as primary stratifiers in sporadic UTUC and aligning with the earlier observation that incomplete MMR loss can preserve microsatellite stability.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Sample size.** 37 UTUC tumors across three institutions remains small given the rarity of UTUC; the authors explicitly call for confirmation in larger cohorts.
- **Primary-only sampling.** Stability of luminal-papillary subtype calls and immune contexture across matched primary and metastatic UTUC is not addressed and is flagged as future work.
- **High-grade only.** The cohort is restricted to high-grade UTUC; low-grade UTUC may differ in subtype distribution (prior unsupervised clustering of mixed high+low grade UTUC produced four molecular subtypes — different framing than the UCB-anchored "continuum" used here).
- **FGFR3 mechanism in vivo.** The IFNG-suppressing effect of [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) is demonstrated in three UCB cell lines (RT-112, RT-4, SW780) under shRNA or short-duration [erdafitinib](../drugs/erdafitinib.md) exposure; in vivo evidence in UTUC models and direct evidence in patient tumors treated with FGFR inhibitors is not provided.
- **Drug-class generalization.** Whether other pan-FGFR inhibitors such as BGJ398 ([infigratinib](../drugs/infigratinib.md)) phenocopy the IFNG-response upregulation seen with [erdafitinib](../drugs/erdafitinib.md) is not tested here, though the authors note encouraging early clinical signals for both agents.
