**PMID:** 25233892 · **DOI:** 10.1038/ncomms6006 · **Journal:** Nature Communications (2014)

## TL;DR

Jones et al. performed whole-exome sequencing on 22 gynaecologic carcinosarcomas (malignant mixed Müllerian tumours: 17 uterine, 5 ovarian) matched to normal tissue. Microsatellite-stable tumours carried an average of 43 non-synonymous mutations each; four mismatch-repair–deficient tumours harboured 904–5,913 somatic alterations. Beyond expected hits in [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (67%), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) (27%), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) (41%), and [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) (41%), they discovered that nearly two-thirds of cases harbour mutations in chromatin-remodelling genes — most prominently [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) (32%), [ARID1B](../genes/ARID1B.md) (14%), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md)/MLL3 (27%), [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md) (14%), and [BAZ1A](../genes/BAZ1A.md) (18%) — and that more than three quarters of cases carry alterations in genes with potential clinical actionability (PI3K pathway, homologous recombination, mismatch repair).

## Cohort & data

- 22 carcinosarcoma patients (17 uterine, 5 ovarian) with matched tumour/normal exomes; one tumour (MM02) was a recurrence at the primary site, others were primary. Samples from OHSU Knight BioLibrary; 11 patients received adjuvant chemotherapy or radiation after surgery.
- Cancer types: [UCS](../cancer_types/UCS.md) (uterine carcinosarcoma, primary) and [MXOV](../cancer_types/MXOV.md) (mixed ovarian carcinoma; five ovarian cases).
- Dataset: [ucs_jhu_2014](../datasets/ucs_jhu_2014.md) — exome data deposited at the European Genome-phenome Archive under accession EGAS00001000941.
- Assay: [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) using Agilent SureSelect 51 Mb (v4) capture + Illumina HiSeq 2000 paired-end 100 bp, average 191× coverage with ≥90% of bases covered ≥10×; aligned to hg19 with Eland/CASAVA 1.7.
- 10 fresh-frozen tumours; 12 formalin-fixed paraffin-embedded tumours; ~85% mean neoplastic cellularity after macrodissection.

## Key findings

- Total burden: 777 somatic mutations in 702 genes across 18 microsatellite-stable tumours; 18,371 alterations across 4 mismatch-repair–deficient tumours (range 904–5,913 in the hypermutators).
- Mismatch-repair deficiency: biallelic truncating mutations in [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md) (MM12T) plus single nonsense/frameshift hits in [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md) (MM04T, MM18T) and [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md) (MM20T) — driving the mutator phenotype. The [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md)-mutant case showed ~50% small indels (vs ~10% in MSS); [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md)-mutant cases showed elevated C:G→A:T transversions (30%) suggestive of unrepaired oxidative damage.
- MSS substitution spectrum was dominated by C:G→T:A transitions at CpG sites (deamination of 5-methylcytosine).
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutated in 67% (16/22) — a higher fraction than previously appreciated for carcinosarcoma. Seven cases had [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation allele frequencies >80% (clonal, homozygous after normal-tissue adjustment).
- PI3K pathway activated in >50% of cases: [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) 41% (9/22), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) 41% (9/22), [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md) 14% (3/22). [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) and [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md) mutations were mutually exclusive; [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) loss co-occurred with other pathway hits. Unlike prior reports, PI3K pathway mutations were not confined to uterine cases — they also occurred in ovarian cases.
- Chromatin-remodelling genes are the most prominently mutated class: [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) 32% (7/22), [ARID1B](../genes/ARID1B.md) 14%, [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md)/MLL3 27% (6/22) plus one [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md)/MLL2 splice-site, [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md) 14% (3/22), [BAZ1A](../genes/BAZ1A.md) 18% (4/22). Three of the [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md)-mutated tumours also carried [ARID1B](../genes/ARID1B.md) frameshift/nonsense alterations. Approximately two-thirds of cases harbour mutations in genes involved in chromatin remodelling or modification.
- Site-specific signature: among MSS tumours, [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) mutations were unique to ovarian-origin carcinosarcomas — 4 of 5 ovarian tumours, none of the MSS uterine tumours.
- Other recurrently mutated genes: [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) hotspots in 27% (5/22; 5 cases for actionability counting); [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) hotspot mutations in two MSS cases; [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md) in 23% (5/22, including the canonical R505C hotspot in MM19, three WD-repeat missense, one frameshift); [PPP2R1A](../genes/PPP2R1A.md) hotspot mutation M180R in one uterine carcinosarcoma; [ERBB3](../genes/ERBB3.md) extracellular-domain hotspot V104M in one tumour.
- Clonality: TP53/known cancer-gene mutations cluster at ~50% (heterozygous) or ~100% (homozygous) allele frequencies after purity adjustment — consistent with monoclonal origin of carcinosarcomas (supporting the conversion theory of histogenesis).
- DNA-repair: somatic frameshift in [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) (1 case), nonsense mutations in [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) (3 cases), splice-site in [FANCM](../genes/FANCM.md) (2 cases). One germline [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) nonsense (K3326X) identified in patient MM08 with family history of cervical and lung cancer.

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — 16/22 (67%); seven cases clonal homozygous (>80% VAF).
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — 5/22 hotspot mutations (27%); flagged as actionable via MEK/BRAF inhibitors.
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — 9/22 (41%), hotspot activating; mutually exclusive with [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md).
- [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md) — 3/22 (14%) in-frame indels and frameshift; first implication in carcinosarcoma.
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — 9/22 (41%) missense/nonsense/frameshift; co-occurs with other PI3K hits.
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) — 7/22 (32%) truncating; ovarian-restricted among MSS cases (4/5 ovarian, 0/uterine MSS).
- [ARID1B](../genes/ARID1B.md) — 3/22 (14%) frameshift/nonsense; co-occurs with [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) in 3 cases.
- [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) (MLL3) — 6/22 (27%) mostly missense, with truncations.
- [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) (MLL2) — 1 splice-site alteration (co-occurring with an MLL3 mutation in the same tumour).
- [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md) — 3/22 (14%) missense in/near the MATH domain (E46K, E78K, M117V).
- [BAZ1A](../genes/BAZ1A.md) — 4/22 (18%); first carcinosarcoma report.
- [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) — 2 MSS cases with hotspot mutations.
- [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md) — 5/22 (23%) including R505C WD-repeat hotspot in MM19.
- [PPP2R1A](../genes/PPP2R1A.md) — 1 uterine case, M180R HEAT-domain hotspot.
- [ERBB3](../genes/ERBB3.md) — 1 case, V104M extracellular-domain hotspot.
- [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) — 1 frameshift.
- [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) — 3 nonsense (somatic) + 1 germline (K3326X in patient MM08).
- [FANCM](../genes/FANCM.md) — 2 splice-site/frameshift.
- [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md) — 1 frameshift (MM20T, hypermutator).
- [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md) — 3 cases (MM04T, MM12T biallelic, MM18T) nonsense/frameshift — drivers of the mutator phenotype.

## Clinical implications

- More than three quarters of cases carry an alteration in a gene with potential clinical actionability — a striking yield given that no targeted therapies are currently approved for carcinosarcoma and prognosis remains poor.
- PI3K pathway hits ([PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md)) in >50% of cases support trials of PI3K/mTOR/AKT/MEK inhibitors — already in Phase I/II testing in endometrial cancer.
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) hotspot mutations (5 cases) flag candidates for MEK/BRAF inhibition.
- [ERBB3](../genes/ERBB3.md) V104M (1 case) is targetable with ERBB-family inhibitor antibodies in Phase II development.
- [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md) loss-of-function (5 cases) may sensitize tumours to HDAC inhibitors (preclinical evidence).
- Homologous-recombination repair defects — somatic [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md)/[BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) (4 cases) and germline [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) (1 case) — suggest sensitivity to platinum agents and PARP inhibitors; [FANCM](../genes/FANCM.md) defects flag mitomycin sensitivity.
- The four mismatch-repair–deficient hypermutators ([MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md)) are candidates for anti–PD-1 immunotherapy (MK-3475/pembrolizumab being evaluated in MSI-unstable tumours at time of publication).
- Mutational profiling has potential diagnostic utility: detection of carcinosarcoma-defining mutations in cell-free DNA or liquid Pap smear specimens could enable early detection.

## Limitations & open questions

- Modest cohort (n=22) with an imbalance between uterine (17) and ovarian (5) primaries, limiting power to fully resolve site-specific differences.
- No transcriptomic, methylation, or copy-number data presented — only exomes; functional consequence of many chromatin-remodelling missense alterations is inferred, not measured.
- The site-specific finding that [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) mutations are restricted to ovarian-origin MSS carcinosarcomas is provocative but rests on 4/5 ovarian vs 0/MSS-uterine — open to replication in larger series.
- Clinical actionability claims are inferred from mutation status; the paper does not test response to any of the suggested targeted agents in this cohort.
- Whether the observed chromatin-remodelling dysregulation drives the biphasic carcinoma+sarcoma histology (the "conversion" hypothesis) or merely co-occurs with it remains untested mechanistically.
- The germline [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) K3326X carrier was identified opportunistically; the paper does not pursue a systematic germline analysis.
