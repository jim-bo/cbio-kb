# SLC27A4

## Overview

SLC27A4 (FATP4, fatty acid transport protein 4) is a long-chain fatty acid transporter. In [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md), upregulation of FATP4 promotes import of monounsaturated fatty acids (MUFAs), protecting tumor cells against ferroptosis and conferring resistance to [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md).

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- SLC27A4 (FATP4) upregulated in HCC; MUFA import protects against ferroptosis and confers sorafenib resistance; part of the lipid-resistance axis targetable to resensitize HCC to TKIs [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) — FATP4 upregulation is a ferroptosis-evasion mechanism and sorafenib resistance driver [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Part of the fatty acid transport upregulation axis with [CD36](../genes/CD36.md) and [SLC27A5](../genes/SLC27A5.md) in HCC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- FATP4 targeting is proposed as a strategy to resensitize HCC to sorafenib by restoring ferroptosis sensitivity [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Open questions

- Selective FATP4 inhibitors with acceptable hepatotoxicity profiles in cirrhotic livers are not yet validated.
