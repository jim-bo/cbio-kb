# PI4KB

## Overview

PI4KB (phosphatidylinositol 4-kinase beta) is a lipid kinase that phosphorylates phosphatidylinositol to generate phosphatidylinositol 4-phosphate (PI4P), a key signaling lipid involved in membrane trafficking, Golgi integrity, and intracellular transport. While established roles exist in viral replication and infection biology, PI4KB has recently emerged as a novel druggable candidate in cancer through phosphoproteomic discovery.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Elevated kinase activity detected by phosphoproteomics in colon cancer tumors versus matched normal adjacent tissue; PI4KB is flagged as a novel candidate with no prior clinical-development inhibitors at the time of reporting, contrasting with co-identified kinases CDK1/2/7, [MELK](../genes/MELK.md), and [PFKFB3](../genes/PFKFB3.md) that already have drugs in clinical trials. [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md) (colon adenocarcinoma):** PI4KB kinase activity is elevated in colon tumors compared to matched normal adjacent tissue in a 106-tumor proteogenomic cohort. It was identified by activity-prediction analysis (kinase-activating-site phosphorylation enrichment plus substrate-site analysis). [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- PI4KB elevated kinase activity co-occurs with [CDK1](../genes/CDK1.md), [CDK2](../genes/CDK2.md), [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md), [CDK7](../genes/CDK7.md), MELK, and PFKFB3 activity elevation in the same colon tumor dataset. [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Identified as a novel therapeutic target candidate in colon cancer. Unlike the co-identified kinases, PI4KB had no established inhibitor in clinical development at the time of the report — positioning it as an emerging drug discovery opportunity. [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Open questions

- No inhibitor targeting PI4KB in cancer was in clinical development as of the report; preclinical validation in colorectal cancer models is absent. Whether PI4KB elevation is driver or passenger activity is unresolved. [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)
