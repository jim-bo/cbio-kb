**PMID:** 32025007 · **DOI:** 10.1038/s41586-020-1969-6 · **Journal:** Nature (2020)

## TL;DR

This is the flagship marker paper of the [ICGC](../datasets/pancan_pcawg_2020.md)/TCGA Pan-Cancer Analysis of Whole Genomes (PCAWG) Consortium. It reports an integrative analysis of 2,658 high-quality matched tumor–normal whole genomes spanning 38 tumor types, uniformly aligned and variant-called across three core pipelines on a globally distributed cloud-computing infrastructure. The cohort yielded ~43.8 million somatic SNVs, ~2.4 million indels, 288,416 SVs, 19,166 retrotransposition events, and 8,185 de novo mtDNA mutations. Cancer genomes contain an average of 4.6 coding+non-coding driver mutations per tumor (2.6 coding); 91% of tumors had ≥1 identified driver, but ~5% had no identifiable driver after accounting for technical and biological causes. The paper anchors a series of 22 companion papers covering chromothripsis, mutational signatures, evolutionary timing, transcriptomic consequences, germline determinants, and telomere maintenance — establishing PCAWG as the canonical pan-cancer WGS reference resource (cBioPortal `studyId`: `pancan_pcawg_2020`).

## Cohort & data

- **2,658 white- and grey-listed donors** with matched tumor/normal WGS, drawn from 2,834 donors after QC exclusion of 176 ([PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md)).
- **38 tumor types** including [breast](../cancer_types/BRCA.md), [prostate](../cancer_types/PRAD.md), ovary, colorectal, [hepatocellular](../cancer_types/HCC.md), [pancreatic](../cancer_types/PAAD.md), [pancreatic neuroendocrine](../cancer_types/PANET.md), [chromophobe RCC](../cancer_types/CHRCC.md), [glioblastoma](../cancer_types/GB.md), [lung squamous](../cancer_types/LUSC.md), [liposarcoma](../cancer_types/LIPO.md), [medulloblastoma](../cancer_types/MBN.md), [MPN](../cancer_types/MPN.md), [thyroid](../cancer_types/THPA.md), bladder, head-and-neck, esophagus, [CLL](../cancer_types/CLLSLL.md), [DLBCL](../cancer_types/DLBCLNOS.md), [cutaneous melanoma](../cancer_types/SKCM.md), and [acral melanoma](../cancer_types/ACRM.md).
- **2,605 primary tumors + 173 metastases/local recurrences**; mean read coverage 39× normal, bimodal 38×/60× tumor.
- **RNA-seq** for 1,222 donors. Mean age 56 (range 1–90); 55% men, 45% women.
- **Pipelines**: three core SNV/indel/SV/CNA callers (Sanger, [DKFZ], Broad [MuTect](../methods/mutect.md)) plus bespoke algorithms for retrotransposition, mtDNA, and telomere length. SVs called with [DELLY](../methods/delly.md)/[SvABA](../methods/svaba.md); CNAs/purity-ploidy by [ABSOLUTE](../methods/absolute.md)-class tools; significantly mutated regions by [GISTIC2.0](../methods/gistic.md); signatures via [SigProfiler](../methods/sigprofiler.md).
- **Benchmarking**: 63 representative tumor–normal pairs validated by deep custom-bait sequencing; consensus SNV calls reached 95% sensitivity (90% CI 88–98%) and 95% precision (90% CI 71–99%); consensus indel sensitivity 60% (34–72%), precision 91% (73–96%); consensus SV sensitivity ~90%, precision ~97.5%.
- **Compute**: alignment and variant calling distributed across 13 data centres on 3 continents; pipelines packaged as Docker containers and published on Dockstore.
- Dataset page: [pancan_pcawg_2020](../datasets/pancan_pcawg_2020.md).

## Key findings

- **Genome-wide mutation burden** across 2,583 white-listed donors: 43,778,859 somatic SNVs, 410,123 MNVs, 2,418,247 indels, 288,416 SVs, 19,166 retrotransposition events, 8,185 mtDNA mutations.
- **Age correlates with somatic burden** across most variant classes: ~190 SNVs/year (P = 0.02), ~22 indels/year (P = 5×10⁻⁵), ~1.5 SVs/year (P < 2×10⁻¹⁶; linear regression).
- **Driver landscape**: 91% of tumors carried ≥1 driver; mean 4.6 drivers per tumor (2.6 coding). Across PCAWG only **13% (785/5,913) of driver point mutations are non-coding**, but 25% of tumors carry ≥1 non-coding driver, one-third of which (237/785) hit the [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter (9% of PCAWG tumors).
- **Tumor-type SV vs SNV mix**: driver SVs dominate breast adenocarcinoma (6.4 ± 3.7 SVs vs 2.2 ± 1.3 point mutations; P < 1×10⁻¹⁶) and ovary adenocarcinoma (5.8 ± 2.6 vs 1.9 ± 1.0; P < 1×10⁻¹⁶), whereas point mutations dominate colorectal adenocarcinoma (2.4 ± 1.4 SVs vs 7.4 ± 7.0 point muts; P = 4×10⁻¹⁰) and mature B-cell lymphomas.
- **Biallelic TSG inactivation** is the rule for tumor-suppressor drivers: of 954 [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)-driver tumors, 736 (77%) had both alleles mutated, 96% via a somatic SNV + somatic deletion of the other allele.
- **Germline contribution**: 17% of patients carried rare germline protein-truncating variants in cancer-predisposition / DDR / somatic-driver genes; biallelic inactivation by germline+somatic combined was 4.5% overall, 81% of which affected [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), or [ATM](../genes/ATM.md).
- **Tumors with no apparent driver**: 181/2,658 (≈6.8%) had no identifiable driver. After triage of technical causes (tumor-in-normal contamination, low coverage at hotspots — e.g. only ~50% of PCAWG tumors had ≥90% power at the two TERT-promoter hotspots due to GC bias) and biological causes, **5.3% of PCAWG tumors still had no driver identified**. [Chromophobe RCC](../cancer_types/CHRCC.md) (44%; 19/43) and [pancreatic neuroendocrine](../cancer_types/PANET.md) (22%; 18/81) tumors were most enriched for missing drivers — both showing recurrent aneuploidy patterns.
- **Bioinformatic failure mode**: PCAWG missed the canonical [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md) V617F driver in 35 myeloproliferative neoplasms because panels-of-normals built from blood samples filtered out the mutation (2–5% of healthy individuals carry occult haematopoietic clones).
- **Novel driver**: recurrent CNA spanning [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md) in driverless medulloblastomas; [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md) loss occurred exclusively in group-4 medulloblastomas (P < 1×10⁻⁴) and significantly decreased SETD2 expression (P = 0.002).
- **Clustered mutational processes** (Fig. 4):
  - **Chromoplexy** in 467/2,658 (17.8%) samples; prominent in [prostate adenocarcinoma](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) and lymphoid malignancies; **unexpectedly enriched in thyroid adenocarcinoma** where ≥31% of fusion/enhancer-hijacking events were caused by chromoplexy, generating [RET](../genes/RET.md) and [NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md) fusions and juxtaposition of [IGF2BP3](../genes/IGF2BP3.md) with regulatory elements.
  - **Kataegis** in 60.5% of cancers, dominated by APOBEC C>N at TpC (81.7% of foci; correlates with [APOBEC3B](../genes/APOBEC3B.md) expression, SV burden, age); 5.7% T>N error-prone polymerase signature; 2.3% alternative GpC/CpC deaminase signature in sarcomas.
  - **Chromothripsis** in 587/2,658 (22.3%) samples; most frequent in sarcoma, glioblastoma, lung squamous, melanoma, and breast. [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) is the strongest associated driver (pan-cancer OR = 3.22, P = 8.3×10⁻³⁵; q < 0.05 in breast lobular OR = 13, colorectal OR = 25, prostate OR = 2.6, hepatocellular OR = 3.9). Chromothripsis coincided with 3.6% of all PCAWG drivers and ~7% of CNA drivers; 58% of coincident events were amplifications, 34% homozygous deletions.
- **Evolutionary timing**: chromothripsis tends to be clonal/early in [liposarcoma](../cancer_types/LIPO.md), [prostate adenocarcinoma](../cancer_types/PRAD.md), and lung squamous. In [acral melanoma](../cancer_types/ACRM.md), chromothripsis-induced co-amplification of [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) + [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) occurred very early (few/no high-VAF SNVs predated amplification), whereas [SOX2](../genes/SOX2.md) amplification in [lung squamous](../cancer_types/LUSC.md) followed many earlier SNVs (late event). [Melanoma](../cancer_types/MEL.md) had 89 chromothripsis events across 66 tumors (61%); 47/89 affected genes recurrently altered in melanoma. Chromosome-11 involvement including [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) was seen in 21 cases (10/86 cutaneous, 11/21 acral/mucosal), nearly always with amplification (19/21).
- **Germline GWAS of mutational signatures**:
  - **22q13.1 locus** (rs12628403) tags a ~30-kb germline deletion fusing [APOBEC3B](../genes/APOBEC3B.md) coding into [APOBEC3A](../genes/APOBEC3A.md) 3' UTR; minor allele protective against APOBEC3B-like mutagenesis (β = −0.43, P = 5.6×10⁻⁹, MAF 8.2%, n = 1,201 EUR donors). The deletion reduces [APOBEC3B](../genes/APOBEC3B.md) mutagenesis in low-APOBEC cancers and increases APOBEC3A-like mutagenesis in high-APOBEC cancers.
  - **Novel 22q13.1 locus** rs2142833 (β = 0.23, P = 1.3×10⁻⁸) associated with APOBEC3B-like mutagenesis, replicated in East Asian cohorts (β = 0.58, P = 8×10⁻¹⁵); cis-eQTL for [APOBEC3B](../genes/APOBEC3B.md) (β = 0.19, P = 2×10⁻⁶).
- **Germline rare-variant associations**:
  - Germline [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) PTVs ↔ excess small tandem duplications (P = 6×10⁻¹³); germline [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) PTVs ↔ excess small SV deletions (P = 1×10⁻⁸). 20/21 BRCA1-deficient tumors with the "templated insertion SV phenotype" had biallelic [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) hits, linking biallelic BRCA1 inactivation to the templated-insertion phenotype.
  - **Germline [MBD4](../genes/MBD4.md) PTVs ↔ elevated somatic CpG > T mutagenesis** (P < 2.5×10⁻⁶), replicated in TCGA exome cohort (n = 8,134; P = 7.1×10⁻⁴). [MBD4](../genes/MBD4.md) encodes a DNA repair gene that removes thymine from T:G mismatches at methylated CpGs.
- **Active germline LINE-1 source elements**: 114 germline L1 source elements capable of somatic retrotransposition, 70 representing insertions vs the human reference. **16 hot-L1 elements account for 67% (2,440/3,669) of all L1-mediated transductions**, split into "Strombolian" (common, frequently active, modest output) and "Plinian" (rare MAF ≤ 2%, aggressive output) classes; only 38% of donors carry ≥1 Plinian element.
- **Telomere maintenance**: 16% of PCAWG tumors have somatic mutations in at least one of [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md), [DAXX](../genes/DAXX.md), or [TERT](../genes/TERT.md). Twelve telomere-sequence features cluster tumors into 4 groups, with ALT-pathway clusters C1 enriched for [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) alterations + [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) SVs (P = 3×10⁻⁵ for [RB1](../genes/RB1.md); P = 6×10⁻¹⁴ for ATRX SVs) and C2 enriched for ATRX/DAXX point mutations (P = 6×10⁻⁵). C3 (predominantly thyroid adenocarcinomas) had frequent [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter mutations (30%; P = 2×10⁻⁶). Telomere maintenance abnormalities inversely correlated with tissue stem-cell division rates (P = 0.01, Poisson regression).

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — driver in 954 tumors; 736 (77%) biallelic, 96% somatic-SNV + somatic-deletion. Pan-cancer associated with chromothripsis (OR = 3.22, P = 8.3×10⁻³⁵) ([PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md)).
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — biallelic inactivation in 475 tumors; co-deleted in chromothripsis events in glioblastoma ([PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md)).
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) — driver in 316 tumors ([PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md)).
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — driver in 287 tumors ([PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md)).
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — driver in 269 tumors ([PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md)).
- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) — driver in 263 tumors; promoter mutations are the dominant non-coding driver (237/785 non-coding drivers; 9% of PCAWG). In acral melanoma, chromothripsis-induced TERT amplification occurs as a very early event; in chromophobe [RCC](../cancer_types/RCC.md), breakpoints immediately adjacent to TERT increase expression 80-fold (P = 0.0004) ([PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md)).
- [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) — driver in 181 tumors ([PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md)).
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — driver in 177 tumors ([PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md)).
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — driver in 167 tumors; enriched in ALT-pathway telomere cluster C1 (P = 3×10⁻⁵); links RB1 loss to ALT activation independent of ATRX/DAXX ([PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md)).
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — driver in 162 tumors ([PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md)).
- [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) — recurrently co-amplified with TERT via chromothripsis in acral melanoma (11/21 acral/mucosal melanomas affected on chr 11) ([PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md)).
- [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) — universally amplified in liposarcoma chromothripsis events ([PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md)).
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) — focally amplified in [glioblastoma](../cancer_types/GB.md) chromothripsis events ([PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md)).
- [SOX2](../genes/SOX2.md) — amplified in lung squamous cell carcinoma chromothripsis, but as a late event (many predating SNVs are amplified) ([PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md)).
- [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md) — novel recurrent CNA-driven loss in driverless group-4 [medulloblastoma](../cancer_types/MBN.md) (P < 1×10⁻⁴); decreased expression (P = 0.002) ([PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md)).
- [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) / [DAXX](../genes/DAXX.md) — 128 tumors with alterations in either; 71 truncating. Cluster differently across telomere phenotype groups (ATRX SVs in C1, ATRX/DAXX point muts in C2) ([PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md)).
- [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) — germline PTVs associated with somatic tandem-duplication burden (P = 6×10⁻¹³) and templated-insertion SV phenotype (P = 4×10⁻¹⁵); 20/21 templated-insertion tumors had biallelic BRCA1 inactivation ([PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md)).
- [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) — germline PTVs associated with somatic small-deletion burden (P = 1×10⁻⁸) ([PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md)).
- [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) — alongside BRCA1/BRCA2, one of the three genes accounting for 81% of germline+somatic biallelic combinations ([PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md)).
- [MBD4](../genes/MBD4.md) — germline PTVs cause elevated somatic CpG > T mutagenesis (P < 2.5×10⁻⁶ in PCAWG; replicated P = 7.1×10⁻⁴ in TCGA n = 8,134) ([PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md)).
- [APOBEC3A](../genes/APOBEC3A.md) / [APOBEC3B](../genes/APOBEC3B.md) — germline ~30-kb [APOBEC3A](../genes/APOBEC3A.md)–APOBEC3B fusion deletion (rs12628403) and independent rs2142833 cis-eQTL modulate APOBEC mutagenesis pan-cancer ([PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md)).
- [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md) — V617F driver missed in 35 MPNs because panels-of-normals from blood samples filtered the mutation; illustrates a systematic bioinformatic failure mode ([PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md)).
- [RET](../genes/RET.md), [NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md), [IGF2BP3](../genes/IGF2BP3.md) — chromoplexy-mediated fusions/enhancer-hijacking events in thyroid adenocarcinoma ([PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md)).
- [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md), [FANCA](../genes/FANCA.md), [PCM1](../genes/PCM1.md) — among representative cancer-associated genes inside significant copy-number-loss peaks (GISTIC2.0) in driverless cases ([PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md)).

## Clinical implications

- The paper is a **methods/resource marker**; it makes no direct treatment recommendations but explicitly frames clinical applications:
  - **Quality-of-call argument for clinical WGS**: PCAWG documents that single-pipeline calls miss ≥10% of true SNVs and have wider precision bounds; the authors argue that "careful and critical appraisal of the whole pipeline — including sample acquisition, genome sequencing, mapping, variant calling and driver annotation, as done here — should be required for laboratories that offer clinical sequencing of cancer genomes" ([PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md)).
  - **Non-coding driver discovery (TERT promoter, others)** demonstrates that exome-only sequencing systematically misses 13% of point-mutation drivers, with implications for biomarker assays in cancers where TERT promoter mutation is therapeutically actionable.
  - **Germline biomarkers**: rare germline [MBD4](../genes/MBD4.md) PTVs and the APOBEC3 22q13.1 deletion are candidate inherited modifiers of somatic mutation landscape that may inform predisposition screening and signature-based therapy stratification.
  - **Knowledge-bank thesis**: authors argue that delivering precision medicine requires tens-of-thousands-scale knowledge banks with matched clinical outcomes (ICGC-ARGO).

## Limitations & open questions

- **5.3% residual driverless tumors** even after technical/biological triage — suggests undiscovered driver elements, especially in [chromophobe RCC](../cancer_types/CHRCC.md) and [pancreatic neuroendocrine](../cancer_types/PANET.md), and the authors speculate that aneuploidy alone may suffice in some lineages.
- **Coverage/GC-bias gaps**: only ~50% of tumors had ≥90% power at the two TERT promoter hotspots — true non-coding driver prevalence may be higher.
- **Panel-of-normals failure** for [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md) V617F in [MPN](../cancer_types/MPN.md) highlights a general risk of filtering recurrent driver mutations that appear in healthy clonal haematopoiesis.
- **Indel sensitivity** of consensus calls is 60% (much lower than SNV); indel-mediated drivers are likely systematically under-counted.
- **Population bias**: germline GWAS was performed in European-ancestry donors with independent replication in East Asians; non-European associations may be missed.
- **Cohort imbalance**: tumor types vary 50-fold in donor count (1–317), limiting per-type discovery power for rare drivers and complicating cross-type comparisons.
- **Sample-collection era**: PCAWG was largely assembled from primary, treatment-naive ICGC/TCGA cases — does not address treated/resistant or metastatic biology except via the 173 metastasis subset.
- **Open question**: how heritable patterns of clustered mutational processes (chromothripsis, kataegis, chromoplexy) map onto specific cell-cycle/repair defects across lineages.
