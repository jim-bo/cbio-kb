# Sequential genomic analysis using a multisample/multiplatform approach to better define rhabdomyosarcoma progression and relapse

**PMID:** [37730754](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37730754/) · **DOI:** [10.1038/s41698-023-00445-1](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41698-023-00445-1) · **Journal:** npj Precision Oncology (2023)

## TL;DR

This study investigated the genomic evolution from primary to relapsed rhabdomyosarcoma ([RMS](../cancer_types/RMS.md)) in 35 paired primary/relapse samples (18 fusion-positive, 17 fusion-negative) from MSKCC and Institut Curie, using MSK-IMPACT, whole-exome sequencing, and whole-genome sequencing. Fusion-positive RMS (FP-RMS) showed stable genomes at relapse, with secondary alterations in CDKN2A/B, [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md), and [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) present at diagnosis and correlating with worse survival. Fusion-negative RMS (FN-RMS) acquired more alterations at relapse, most commonly [SMARCA2](../genes/SMARCA2.md) missense mutations. A complementary ctDNA liquid biopsy analysis in 10 patients demonstrated feasibility of detecting [PAX3](../genes/PAX3.md)::[FOXO1](../genes/FOXO1.md) fusions, SNVs, and CNVs in plasma, with fusion read counts correlating with disease burden.

## Cohort & data

- 35 patients with relapsed/metastatic [RMS](../cancer_types/RMS.md): 18 fusion-positive ([ARMS](../cancer_types/ARMS.md)) and 17 fusion-negative ([ERMS](../cancer_types/ERMS.md)), from MSKCC (n=20) and Institut Curie (n=15) [PMID:37730754](../papers/37730754.md).
- MSK cohort sequenced on [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md); Curie cohort sequenced by [WES](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) or combined targeted DNA (Dragon panel) and WES.
- Dataset: [rms_msk_2023](../datasets/rms_msk_2023.md).
- 10 patients (7 FP-RMS, 3 FN-RMS) had longitudinal ctDNA analysis from 62 plasma samples using a 36-gene custom RMS panel for SNV/fusion detection and shallow [WGS](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) for CNV assessment.
- [Archer FusionPlex](../methods/archer-fusionplex.md) used for FOXO1 fusion evaluation in the MSK FP-RMS cohort.
- Median age 14 years (FP-RMS) and 7.8 years (FN-RMS) in the MSK cohort; 5-year overall survival was 33%.

## Key findings

- **FP-RMS genomic stability at relapse:** FP-RMS had a median of 2 alterations at primary and 3 at relapse (range 0--7). Half of patients acquired additional alterations at relapse (median 1, range 0--5) [PMID:37730754](../papers/37730754.md).
- **Secondary major alterations in FP-RMS:** [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)/[CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md) alterations (5/18, 28%), [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md) alterations (4/18, 22%), and [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) amplifications (3/18, 17%) were the most common recurrent events, mostly present at diagnosis and largely mutually exclusive [PMID:37730754](../papers/37730754.md).
- **Survival impact in FP-RMS:** [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md) alterations (p=0.0012) and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) deletions (p=0.049) each had statistically significant impact on [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md). Combined [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) and MYCN alterations correlated with worse OS (p=0.0017) and PFS (p=0.002) [PMID:37730754](../papers/37730754.md).
- **Two FP-RMS subgroups:** Patients with secondary major alterations at diagnosis acquired zero or very few new alterations at relapse; patients lacking such events developed more alterations at relapse, including de novo [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) deletions and [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md)/[MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md) alterations [PMID:37730754](../papers/37730754.md).
- **FN-RMS heterogeneity:** FN-RMS acquired an average of 4.3 new alterations per patient at relapse (range 0--17), with 111% overall increase in alterations. Average mutations per sample was higher in FN-RMS vs FP-RMS (5.6 vs 3.2, p=0.002) [PMID:37730754](../papers/37730754.md).
- **Recurrent FN-RMS alterations:** [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (7/17, 41%), [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md) (5/17, 30%), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) pathway genes ([HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md); 5/17), [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) (4/17), and [SMARCA2](../genes/SMARCA2.md) (4/17) [PMID:37730754](../papers/37730754.md).
- **SMARCA2 as relapse-specific event:** [SMARCA2](../genes/SMARCA2.md) missense mutations and frameshift deletions (4/17, 24%) were found exclusively at relapse in FN-RMS, with immunohistochemistry showing loss of SMARCA2 protein expression [PMID:37730754](../papers/37730754.md).
- **[BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md) deletions** (2/17, 12%) also found only at relapse in FN-RMS [PMID:37730754](../papers/37730754.md).
- **ctDNA detection rates:** Combined approach detected alterations in 88% at diagnosis (7/8) and 90% at relapse (9/10). PAX3::[FOXO1](../genes/FOXO1.md) fusion detected in 100% at diagnosis (6/6) and 86% at relapse (6/7). Blood plasma showed higher sensitivity than bone marrow (86% vs 61%) [PMID:37730754](../papers/37730754.md).
- **Fusion reads correlate with disease burden:** Higher PAX3::FOXO1 fusion read counts correlated with increased disease burden and relapse in patients with fatal outcomes [PMID:37730754](../papers/37730754.md).

## Genes & alterations

- [PAX3](../genes/PAX3.md)::FOXO1 fusion -- pathognomonic driver in FP-RMS (16/18 patients); detected in ctDNA at diagnosis (100%) and relapse (86%).
- [PAX7](../genes/PAX7.md)::FOXO1 fusion -- found in 2/18 FP-RMS patients, associated with [FOXO1](../genes/FOXO1.md) amplification.
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)/[CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md) -- homozygous deletions and loss-of-function in 28% of FP-RMS; correlated with worse OS (p=0.049) and PFS (p=0.0082).
- [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md) -- amplifications and missense mutations in 22% of FP-RMS; correlated with worse OS (p=0.0012); high MYCN mRNA expression confirmed by RNA-seq.
- [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) -- amplifications in 17% of FP-RMS, often co-amplified with [GLI1](../genes/GLI1.md) (2 tumors) or [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) (1 tumor) at 12q13-15 locus.
- [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) -- deletions acquired at relapse in FP-RMS lacking major secondary events.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) -- loss-of-function in 41% of FN-RMS patients (33% of samples); previously associated with poor outcome.
- [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md) -- alterations in 30% of FN-RMS patients (24% of samples).
- [SMARCA2](../genes/SMARCA2.md) -- missense mutations and frameshift deletions in 24% of FN-RMS at relapse only; 4 different point mutations with no redundancy in protein domain.
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) -- RAS/PIK3CA pathway alterations in 5/17 FN-RMS.
- [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) -- amplifications in 4/17 FN-RMS, detectable in ctDNA.
- [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md) -- deletions in 2/17 FN-RMS, exclusively at relapse.
- [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) -- driver events in a subset of FN-RMS.
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) -- SNV detected in ctDNA of FP-RMS.
- [MYOD1](../genes/MYOD1.md) -- L122R mutation noted as marker for spindle/sclerosing RMS.
- [IGF2](../genes/IGF2.md), [MED12](../genes/MED12.md), [NCOR2](../genes/NCOR2.md) -- recurrent alterations in FP-RMS.
- [GLI1](../genes/GLI1.md) -- co-amplified with CDK4 in 2 FP-RMS tumors.
- [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md) -- SNV detected in ctDNA at relapse in FN-RMS.
- [DMD](../genes/DMD.md) -- 30Kb deletion acquired at relapse in one FN-RMS patient.
- DNA repair genes ([RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md), RAD21, RAD51C, [ATR](../genes/ATR.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [FANCA](../genes/FANCA.md), FANCC) -- exclusively at relapse in FN-RMS with higher mutational counts.

## Clinical implications

- Secondary alterations in [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md), and [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) at diagnosis in FP-RMS identify a high-risk subgroup with worse OS and PFS, supporting their use in risk stratification [PMID:37730754](../papers/37730754.md).
- Liquid biopsy using a combined targeted DNA panel plus shallow WGS provides reliable detection of PAX3::FOXO1 fusions, SNVs, and CNVs in both FP-RMS and FN-RMS subtypes [PMID:37730754](../papers/37730754.md).
- Fusion read number quantification via unique molecular identifiers (UMIs) enables monitoring of disease burden and treatment response over time [PMID:37730754](../papers/37730754.md).
- Patients with FP-RMS harboring CDKN2A, CDK4, and MYCN alterations, and FN-RMS with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md), or RAS pathway alterations, may benefit from shorter intervals between cfDNA collections during follow-up [PMID:37730754](../papers/37730754.md).
- Rationale provided for implementing ctDNA analysis in the prospective FaR-RMS trial (NCT04625907) [PMID:37730754](../papers/37730754.md).

## Limitations & open questions

- Small cohort size (n=35 tumor pairs, n=10 ctDNA cases) limits statistical power; findings require validation in larger prospective studies.
- Retrospective sample collection and lack of centralized blood processing introduced variability; 4 cases excluded due to undetectable ctDNA.
- The prognostic role of MYCN and CDK4 amplifications in FP-RMS remains controversial, with conflicting findings in prior literature.
- The functional significance of relapse-specific SMARCA2 alterations in FN-RMS requires further investigation.
- No correlation found between cfDNA concentration and clinical status, consistent with prior observations that somatic mutation detection is more reliable than cfDNA levels alone.
- The study did not include digital droplet PCR, which may be more sensitive for fusion detection in low-burden settings.
