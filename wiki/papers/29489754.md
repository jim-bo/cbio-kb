**PMID:** 29489754 · **DOI:** 10.1002/ijc.70097 · **Journal:** International Journal of Cancer (2025)

## TL;DR

Daugs et al. performed whole-exome sequencing on a single-center prospective cohort of 372 pediatric cancer patients (Düsseldorf, 2015–2023) and assessed (likely) pathogenic germline variants (LP/PVs) in 25 genes associated with adult-onset hereditary breast and ovarian cancer (HBOC). Twenty-seven of 372 (7.3%) patients carried LP/PVs; 12/27 (44%) had no clinically suspected cancer predisposition syndrome (CPS) prior to genotyping. LP/PV carriers had a markedly elevated risk for second malignant neoplasms (SMNs; 18.5% vs. 3.8%, OR=5.8, p=.0021), with all five SMNs occurring in carriers of [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (n=3), [NBN](../genes/NBN.md) (n=1), or [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) (n=1) variants. Single-cohort burden testing against gnomAD v3.1.1 non-cancer controls flagged monoallelic LP/PVs in [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), and [NBN](../genes/NBN.md) as significantly enriched; a joint analysis with the 1120 patients from Zhang et al. (2015) confirmed strong associations for [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), and [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md). The authors argue for systematic genotype-driven screening because clinical criteria miss ~44% of carriers.

## Cohort & data

- **372 pediatric cancer patients** enrolled prospectively in the "Germline Mutations in Children with Cancer" study at the Department of Pediatric Oncology, Hematology and Clinical Immunology, Heinrich Heine University Düsseldorf (January 2015 – January 2023). Median age at first diagnosis 5.1 years (range 0–22.2); 160 girls (43%) [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **Disease distribution:** 212 (57%) hematologic neoplasms, 71 (19%) brain tumors, 89 (24%) various solid entities. Among LP/PV carriers (n=27): 10 leukemias (37%), 5 lymphomas (18.5%), 3 brain tumors (11.1%), 8 solid tumors (29.6%), 1 other (3.7%) [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **Assay:** trio [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md); variant pathogenicity assessed by standard diagnostic guidelines ([ACMG-style classification](../methods/acmg-variant-classification.md)). Variant positions on GRCh38 (hg38) [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **Candidate gene set:** 25 selected HBOC-related genes, drawn from the literature (Table S1) [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **Statistical comparator:** [gnomAD v3.1.1 non-cancer dataset](../methods/gnomad-non-cancer-controls.md) (n=74,023 healthy adults) for [gene-level burden testing](../methods/germline-burden-test.md) using logistic regression and two-sided Fisher's exact tests; results considered relevant at p<.05 [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **Joint-analysis cohort:** Genotype data from 1120 pediatric cancer patients in Zhang et al. (2015) added to augment statistical power (Table S6, Figure S3) [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **Data availability:** WES data for the 27 LP/PV carriers deposited in EGA under accession EGAD50000001561 [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).

## Key findings

- **7.3% of pediatric cancer patients carried LP/PVs in HBOC genes.** 28 LP/PVs were found across 27/372 patients (7.3%): 13 likely pathogenic and 15 pathogenic, plus 187 (31.5%) variants of uncertain significance among the 593 total variants in 297 patients [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **44% of carriers were clinically unsuspected.** 12 of 27 LP/PV carriers (44.4%) did not meet the German Cancer Society (DKG) CPS criteria prior to genotyping; only 14/135 (10.4%) of clinically suspected CPS cases harbored an HBOC-related LP/PV [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **Carriers had a 5.8-fold increased risk of second malignant neoplasms.** SMN incidence: 5/27 (18.5%) in carriers vs. 13/345 (3.8%) in non-carriers; OR=5.8 (95% CI=1.89–17.75), p=.0021. All five SMNs occurred in carriers of [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (n=3), [NBN](../genes/NBN.md) (n=1), or [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) (n=1) [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **Twelve of the 25 candidate genes harbored at least one LP/PV.** Distribution across 28 LP/PVs: [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) 6 (21.4%), [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md) 5 (17.9%), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) 4 (14.3%), [NBN](../genes/NBN.md) 3, [BRIP1](../genes/BRIP1.md) 2, [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) 2, [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) 1, [BLM](../genes/BLM.md) 1, [FANCC](../genes/FANCC.md) 1, [FANCM](../genes/FANCM.md) 1, [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md) 1, [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) 1 [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **Single-cohort burden testing flagged five HBOC genes as significantly enriched** vs. gnomAD non-cancer controls: [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (OR=32.8, 95% CI 13.8–78.1, p=7.83×10⁻⁸), [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md) (OR=9.6, 95% CI 3.9–23.7, p=2.42×10⁻⁴), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) (OR=3.2, 95% CI 1.2–8.6, p=.040), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) (OR=9.8, 95% CI 2.4–40.5, p=.020), and [NBN](../genes/NBN.md) (OR=9.4, 95% CI 2.3–38.7, p=.021) [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **[STK11](../genes/STK11.md) showed an extreme but wide-CI association** (OR=199.5, 95% CI 12.5–3195.8, p=.010); the single carrier (LPP_27) presented with congenital Peutz-Jeghers syndrome features and a T-cell ALL with maternal-line family history [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **Joint analysis with Zhang et al. 2015 (n=1120) confirmed associations for [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), and [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md) with pediatric cancers**; [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md) became significant only after pooling (OR=7.1, 95% CI 1.6–31.2, p=.0390). [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [NBN](../genes/NBN.md), [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md), and other less-constrained genes did not retain significance in the joint analysis [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **Most LP/PVs were pathogenic loss-of-function (pLoF) variants.** All LP/PVs in [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [NBN](../genes/NBN.md), [BRIP1](../genes/BRIP1.md), [STK11](../genes/STK11.md), [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [FANCM](../genes/FANCM.md), [BLM](../genes/BLM.md), and [FANCC](../genes/FANCC.md) were pLoF. Six missense LP/PVs were found in [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (n=2), [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md) (n=3), and [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) (n=1); one truncating intronic splice-region LP/PV was found in [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **One patient (LPP_12) was homozygous for the [NBN](../genes/NBN.md) c.657_661del p.(Lys219Asnfs*16) (rs587776650) founder mutation** with typical clinical features of Nijmegen breakage syndrome (NBS) and a T-cell lymphoma; he was excluded from monoallelic burden testing. Two additional patients carried this same [NBN](../genes/NBN.md) founder variant in heterozygous form and developed hematologic neoplasms without NBS-related symptoms [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **One patient (LPP_20) carried two distinct heterozygous LP/PVs in different genes:** [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md) c.1165C>T p.(Arg389Cys) and [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) c.4137dup p.(Ala1380Serfs*15). She was an 11.4-year-old girl with Langerhans cell histiocytosis, no neurofibromatosis type 1 features, but a positive family history of late-onset cancers (breast cancer, acute and chronic myeloid leukemias) [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **The recurrent LFS-related TP53 variant c.586C>T p.(Arg196*) was detected in two unrelated patients** (LPP_07, de novo, T-cell ALL with lineage switch to BCP-ALL; LPP_10, who developed adenocarcinoma at 1.8y, BCP-ALL at 4.8y, then [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md) during ALL maintenance) [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **Highly constrained genes drove the strongest pediatric-cancer associations.** Among candidate genes, [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), and [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) are highly constrained (LOEUF<.35); [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) are moderately constrained; [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md) (LOEUF=1.522) and [NBN](../genes/NBN.md) (LOEUF=1.010) are less constrained. Constraint correlated with pLoF tolerance, consistent with prior work (Stoltze et al., Nat Commun 2024) [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).

## Genes & alterations

- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)** — Six LP/PV carriers (21.4% of LP/PV carriers); strongest single-gene burden-test association (OR=32.8, p=7.83×10⁻⁸). Recurrent c.586C>T p.(Arg196*) detected twice (LPP_07 de novo, LPP_10 with LFS). Three of six TP53 carriers (50%) developed SMNs; carriers presented with rhabdomyosarcoma, osteosarcoma, or BCP-ALL. Five of six had a positive family history of cancer; two had relatives who also experienced pediatric cancer [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **[CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md)** — Five LP/PVs (17.9% of LP/PV carriers); burden OR=9.6, p=2.42×10⁻⁴. Four in BCP-ALL patients and one in a Langerhans-cell-histiocytosis patient (LPP_20, with co-occurring NF1 LP/PV). Three of three pLoF (40% of CHEK2 LP/PVs were pLoF) plus three missense [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **[ATM](../genes/ATM.md)** — Four monoallelic LP/PVs (all pLoF, 100%); burden OR=3.2, p=.040 in single-cohort analysis but not significant in joint analysis. Two BCP-ALL patients (LPP_02, LPP_03) and one each with medulloblastoma and hepatoblastoma (LPP_01, LPP_04). One [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) carrier developed a secondary event. None had ataxia-telangiectasia features [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **[NF1](../genes/NF1.md)** — Two LP/PVs (one pLoF, one truncating intronic splice-region variant); burden OR=9.8, p=.020 single-cohort; confirmed in joint analysis. One carrier (LPP_20) co-carried CHEK2 LP/PV and lacked typical NF1 clinical features [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **[NBN](../genes/NBN.md)** — Three LP/PVs in total: one homozygous (LPP_12, classical NBS phenotype + T-cell lymphoma) excluded from monoallelic burden testing; two heterozygous carriers of the same c.657_661del p.(Lys219Asnfs*16) (rs587776650) founder mutation developed hematologic neoplasms without NBS features. Burden OR=9.4, p=.021 single-cohort; one heterozygous carrier developed an SMN [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **[BRIP1](../genes/BRIP1.md)** — Two pLoF LP/PVs; burden test not significant (OR=4.2, p=.085) [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **[STK11](../genes/STK11.md)** — One pLoF LP/PV: c.179dup p.(Tyr60*) in patient LPP_27, a boy with congenital Peutz-Jeghers syndrome and T-cell ALL; mother and maternal grandfather also have PJS. Single-cohort OR=199.5 (95% CI 12.5–3195.8), p=.010 — extreme [estimate](../methods/estimate.md) driven by very low gnomAD baseline (1/74,022 controls) [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **[MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md)** — One pLoF LP/PV; not significant in single cohort but reached significance in joint analysis with Zhang et al. (OR=7.1, p=.0390). Authors highlight MSH2 as the only DNA-repair gene consistently associated with childhood cancer in a prior meta-analysis [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **[BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md)** — One missense LP/PV; burden test non-significant (OR=1.9, p=.406) [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **[BLM](../genes/BLM.md)** — One pLoF LP/PV; non-significant (OR=1.7, p=.450) [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **[FANCC](../genes/FANCC.md)** — One pLoF LP/PV; non-significant (OR=1.3, p=.534) [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **[FANCM](../genes/FANCM.md)** — One pLoF LP/PV; non-significant (OR=2.2, p=.370) [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **[BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md)** — Listed among the 25 candidate HBOC genes; no LP/PVs were detected in this cohort [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).

## Clinical implications

- **Genotype-driven screening identifies clinically unsuspected carriers.** 44% of HBOC-related LP/PV carriers in this cohort failed standard DKG CPS criteria; the authors argue for systematic WES regardless of phenotype-based suspicion to enable risk-adapted therapy and surveillance [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **HBOC LP/PV carriers warrant SMN surveillance.** A 5.8-fold elevated SMN risk concentrated in [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [NBN](../genes/NBN.md), and [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) carriers supports intensified surveillance protocols (analogous to the Toronto Protocol for [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) carriers cited via Villani et al. 2016) and consideration of reduced-toxicity treatment regimens [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **Therapy de-escalation can mitigate genotype-related toxicity.** Patient LPP_10 (LFS, BCP-ALL → AML) received a personalized chemotherapy regimen with reduced doses of [cytarabine](../drugs/cytarabine.md) and gemtuzumab and, after stem-cell rejection from her mother, achieved remission following haploidentical HSCT from her maternal grandmother [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **Severe neurotoxicity in an [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) / TP53-spectrum context can be lethal.** Patient LPP_07 developed muscle atrophy progressing to tetraparesis during HSCT conditioning with [fludarabine](../drugs/fludarabine.md) and died one month later — illustrating chemotoxicity risk in carriers of DNA-damage-response gene LP/PVs [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **Monoallelic variants in autosomal-recessive HBOC genes may also matter.** Four monoallelic ATM carriers (no AT features) and two heterozygous NBN carriers (no NBS features) developed pediatric cancers; the authors hypothesize that single-allele [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) / [NBN](../genes/NBN.md) impairment contributes to risk and merits further investigation [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **Family-history alone underestimates HBOC predisposition in pediatric oncology.** Only 22% of HBOC LP/PV carriers had a positive family history (vs. ~50% for general CPGs in prior literature), suggesting either lower familial penetrance for these genes or underrepresentation in the studied cohort [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **Cascade testing of relatives is implied but not formally evaluated.** Identifying HBOC LP/PVs in pediatric probands may also enable identification of at-risk adult relatives [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Limited candidate-gene set.** Restricting analysis to 25 HBOC-related genes excludes related DNA-repair / mismatch-repair genes (e.g., [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md), [ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)) that prior work has implicated in pediatric cancer predisposition [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **No copy-number variation analysis.** Technical limitations of the WES pipeline excluded CNVs; carrier frequencies in genes susceptible to large deletions ([NF1](../genes/NF1.md), [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md)/2) may be underestimated [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **No second-hit / tumor analysis.** Lack of available tumor material precluded investigation of somatic second hits to corroborate driver status in carriers [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **Variable follow-up time.** Heterogeneous follow-up durations may underestimate SMN incidence, particularly in recently enrolled patients [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **Single-center cohort.** 372 patients from one Düsseldorf institution; the joint analysis with Zhang et al. (2015) only partially mitigates statistical-power constraints. Wide confidence intervals on rare-variant ORs (e.g., [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) OR=199.5, 95% CI 12.5–3195.8) reflect this.
- **Comparator may inflate ORs.** Using gnomAD non-cancer adults as the control population assumes no enrichment of rare HBOC LP/PVs in adults who simply have not yet developed cancer; ascertainment bias is possible [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md).
- **Multiple-testing correction not explicitly applied.** Twelve gene-level burden tests with p<.05 cutoff; only [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md) survive any reasonable Bonferroni correction in the single-cohort analysis.
- **Penetrance and age-at-onset for monoallelic [AR](../genes/AR.md) variants are unknown.** Whether monoallelic [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) or [NBN](../genes/NBN.md) variants act as low-penetrance alleles or modifiers remains an open question that this study highlights but cannot resolve.
- **No prospective evaluation of risk-adapted therapy.** The clinical-utility argument is implicit; the study does not benchmark outcomes for genotype-guided treatment vs. standard care.
