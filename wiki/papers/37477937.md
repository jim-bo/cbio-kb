# Novel Genomic Risk Stratification Model for Primary Gastrointestinal Stromal Tumors (GIST) in the Adjuvant Therapy Era

**PMID:** 37477937 · **DOI:** 10.1158/1078-0432.CCR-23-1184 · **Journal:** Clinical Cancer Research (2023)

## TL;DR

Dermawan et al. profiled 501 [GIST](../cancer_types/GIST.md) patients (592 samples) from MSKCC with MSK-IMPACT targeted sequencing and built an elastic-net penalized Cox machine learning model to create a 3-tier genomic risk stratification for recurrence-free survival in 152 primary localized gastric and 80 small bowel GISTs. The genomic model, incorporating arm-level CNAs (chr1p, chr14q, chr5q, chr1q) and gene alterations ([SDHB](../genes/SDHB.md), [KIT](../genes/KIT.md) exon 11, MAX/MGA/MYC, [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md)), both upgrades and downgrades patients compared to conventional Miettinen/NIH-Fletcher/Joensuu schemes, suggesting the traditional approaches may under- or over-treat subsets of patients in the imatinib/TKI adjuvant era [PMID:37477937](../papers/37477937.md).

## Cohort & data

- 501 patients (592 samples) with [GIST](../cancer_types/GIST.md) diagnosed at MSKCC between 1991 and 2021: 275 gastric (307 samples), 194 small bowel (244 samples), 32 rectal (41 samples; excluded from analyses).
- Primary localized gastric (n=152) and small bowel (n=80) GISTs analyzed for risk stratification; 41% of gastric and 53% of small bowel received adjuvant [imatinib](../drugs/imatinib.md).
- Cancer type: [GIST](../cancer_types/GIST.md).
- Dataset/cohort: [gist_msk_2023](../datasets/gist_msk_2023.md).
- Assay: [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) targeted DNA NGS (341–505 genes), matched tumor-normal.

## Key findings

- A 3-tier genomic risk stratification model was developed separately for gastric and small bowel GISTs using an elastic-net penalized Cox proportional hazards model.
- Gastric GISTs: **high risk** if chr1p deletion or [SDHB](../genes/SDHB.md) loss; **intermediate risk** if chr14q deletion or absence of [KIT](../genes/KIT.md) exon 11 mutation.
- Small bowel GISTs: **high risk** if MAX/MGA/MYC, [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), or [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) alterations; **intermediate risk** if chr1p deletion or chr5q amplification.
- Compared with conventional Miettinen/NIH-Fletcher/Joensuu stratification, the genomic model both upgraded and downgraded patients, indicating conventional models may underestimate risk in some and overtreat others.
- Longitudinal sequencing detected most KIT-independent genomic alterations already at baseline.
- In a subanalysis of 26 SDH-deficient GISTs, presence of [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations or chr1q amplification was associated with worse recurrence-free and disease-free survival.

## Genes & alterations

- [KIT](../genes/KIT.md) — exon 11 mutation status incorporated into gastric risk model; absence flagged as intermediate risk.
- [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md) — co-primary GIST driver; KIT/PDGFRA wild-type cases with SDHB IHC loss classified as SDH-deficient.
- [SDHB](../genes/SDHB.md) — loss of protein expression defines high-risk gastric GIST in the genomic model.
- [SDHA](../genes/SDHA.md) — assessed for germline/somatic mutation in SDH-deficient subset.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — mutations portend worse RFS/DSS in SDH-deficient GIST subset.
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — alterations define high risk in small bowel GIST.
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — alterations define high risk in small bowel GIST.
- [MAX](../genes/MAX.md), [MGA](../genes/MGA.md), [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) — any alteration in this set defines high risk in small bowel GIST.

## Clinical implications

- Genomic risk stratification may refine selection of patients for adjuvant [imatinib](../drugs/imatinib.md) therapy by identifying high-risk patients missed by conventional models and by sparing some low-risk patients from overtreatment.
- SDH-deficient GISTs harboring [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation or chr1q amplification represent a prognostically worse subgroup warranting closer surveillance.
- Authors emphasize the model complements rather than replaces clinicopathologic stratification and requires independent external validation.

## Limitations & open questions

- Single-institution retrospective cohort from MSKCC; requires external validation as explicitly noted by the authors.
- DSS could not be examined in the primary localized cohort due to insufficient events.
- Rectal GISTs were excluded from modeling.
- SDH-deficient subanalysis is small (n=26).
- Generalizability to non–MSK-IMPACT panels and to patients not receiving adjuvant TKI is uncertain.
