# SERPINH1

## Overview

SERPINH1 (also known as HSP47 or heat shock protein 47) is a collagen-specific molecular chaperone belonging to the serpin superfamily. It is required for proper collagen folding and secretion, and is upregulated in fibrotic conditions and multiple cancer types. Its overexpression has been associated with tumor invasion, metastasis, and poor prognosis.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Among the most uniformly upregulated proteins across tumor-NAT pairs (≥95% of colon tumor samples), identified as a candidate biomarker/therapeutic target in colon cancer proteogenomics ([coad_cptac_2019](../datasets/coad_cptac_2019.md)) [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Colorectal cancer ([COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md)) — SERPINH1 protein uniformly upregulated in ≥95% of colon tumors vs normal adjacent tissue; one of 31 cancer-associated proteins with potential diagnostic, outcome, or therapeutic utility identified in CPTAC proteogenomics [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-upregulated with [S100A11](../genes/S100A11.md), [S100P](../genes/S100P.md), [DDX21](../genes/DDX21.md), [PLOD2](../genes/PLOD2.md), and [GPRC5A](../genes/GPRC5A.md) as the most uniformly overexpressed proteins in colon tumors [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Uniform overexpression in ≥95% of colon tumors positions SERPINH1 as a candidate for biomarker and therapeutic follow-up; 19 of the 31 cancer-associated proteins identified in the same study are detectable in plasma as potential blood biomarkers [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Open questions

- Whether SERPINH1 overexpression in colon cancer reflects collagen remodeling in the tumor microenvironment or has direct oncogenic activity remains to be established.
