# ETV1

## Overview

ETV1 (ETS Variant Transcription Factor 1) is an ETS-family transcription factor involved in gene fusions in prostate cancer, analogous to [ERG](../genes/ERG.md). ETV1 fusions (typically with androgen-responsive promoters) occur in a minority of prostate cancers and activate ETS-driven transcriptional programs.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- ETV1 fusions (ETV1-FOXA1, ACSL3-ETV1) detected in prostate cancer PDX models (MDA PCa PDX series); these fusions did not correlate with ETV1 expression changes in this cohort, in contrast to TMPRSS2-ERG and TMPRSS2-ETV4 fusions [PMID:38488813](../papers/38488813.md).
- ETV1 expression is upregulated in cells carrying a subclonal [CIC](../genes/CIC.md) loss-of-function mutation (~30% VAF) in the MGH53 oligodendroglioma, consistent with derepression of [CIC](../genes/CIC.md) targets; mutation spans all three developmental compartments (stem/progenitor, oligodendrocyte-like, astrocyte-like) [PMID:27806376](../papers/27806376.md).
- Complex rearrangements (6 validated, including 4 interchromosomal translocations) and high-level amplification detected in acral melanoma ME032; known melanoma oncogene associated with [MITF](../genes/MITF.md) upregulation [PMID:22622578](../papers/22622578.md)
- ETV1 identified as an ETS family fusion partner in prostate cancer WES cohort (Michigan, 112 tumors); ETV1 rearrangements represent a distinct subset of ETS-driven prostate cancers [PMID:22722839](../papers/22722839.md)
- Alternative ETS fusion partner in prostate cancer detected by sequencing and validated by FISH in chromoplexy study of 57 prostate tumors [PMID:23622249](../papers/23622249.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) — ETV1 fusions (ETV1-FOXA1, ACSL3-ETV1) detected in PDX models; functional significance unresolved [PMID:38488813](../papers/38488813.md).
- [ODG](../cancer_types/ODG.md) — ETV1 derepressed in CIC-mutant oligodendroglioma cells across all developmental compartments [PMID:27806376](../papers/27806376.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- ETV1 derepression co-occurs with [ETV5](../genes/ETV5.md) derepression in CIC-mutant oligodendroglioma cells [PMID:27806376](../papers/27806376.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Not reported in the corpus.

## Open questions

- The biological consequences of ETV1-FOXA1 and ACSL3-ETV1 fusions detected in PDX models remain to be elucidated [PMID:38488813](../papers/38488813.md).
- Whether ETV1 derepression in CIC-mutant oligodendroglioma contributes to the developmental plasticity of tumor cells or represents a passenger effect is unresolved [PMID:27806376](../papers/27806376.md).
