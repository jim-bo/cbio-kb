# PIAS4

## Overview

PIAS4 (Protein Inhibitor of Activated STAT 4) is an E3 SUMO ligase in the PIAS family that can suppress JAK/STAT signaling. In cancer genomics, amplification of PIAS4 has been associated with disruption of interferon-gamma pathway activity, potentially contributing to immune evasion and resistance to checkpoint immunotherapy.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Focal amplification of PIAS4 was enriched in progressive disease (PD) patients in a pooled ICB cohort (193 patients across melanoma, [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md), bladder, HNSCC, and other cancer types); PIAS4 amplification was part of an interferon-gamma pathway CNA signature (together with [PIAS1](../genes/PIAS1.md), [SOCS1](../genes/SOCS1.md), [SOCS3](../genes/SOCS3.md) amplifications and interferon-gene deletions) enriched in PD (19/123 PD vs 3/70 CR/PR; p=0.019, Fisher's exact) [PMID:30150660](../papers/30150660.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Pan-cancer (pooled melanoma, NSCLC, bladder, HNSCC, and others): PIAS4 amplification as a potential ICB resistance marker [PMID:30150660](../papers/30150660.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-amplified with PIAS1, SOCS1, and SOCS3 as part of an interferon-gamma pathway suppression CNA pattern in ICB non-responders [PMID:30150660](../papers/30150660.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- PIAS4 amplification is a candidate genomic predictor of primary resistance to anti-CTLA-4 and anti-PD-1/L1 checkpoint inhibitors; loss of intact interferon-gamma signaling may be a candidate exclusion criterion for ICB [PMID:30150660](../papers/30150660.md)

## Open questions

- Whether PIAS4 amplification is causally sufficient to suppress IFN-gamma signaling in vivo or is a passenger CNA has not been established in this cohort.
