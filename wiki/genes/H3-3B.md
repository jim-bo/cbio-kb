# H3-3B

## Overview

H3-3B (formerly H3F3B) encodes histone H3.3, a replication-independent histone variant whose oncogenic missense mutations at K27 (K27M) and G34 (G34R/V) are canonical drivers of pediatric diffuse intrinsic pontine glioma (DIPG) and diffuse hemispheric glioma. H3-3B is the paralogue of [H3-3A](../genes/H3-3A.md); both encode identical histone H3.3 protein. Canonical K27M mutations arising in either gene are the defining molecular event for diffuse midline glioma (WHO grade 4) and are mutually exclusive between paralogues in most series.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Both DIPG PDX models (IBs-P1215DIPG, IBs-W0128DIPG) in the PPTC dataset express histone H3.3 (encoded by H3-3B and [H3-3A](../genes/H3-3A.md)) at high levels (FPKM > 50) but lack canonical H3.1/H3.3 K27M/G34 mutations; IBs-W0128DIPG carries predicted-damaging RNA-derived missense variants in [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) (G13R), [CIC](../genes/CIC.md) (C102Y), and [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) (C988F) [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [DIFG](../cancer_types/DIFG.md) — DIPG PDX models show high H3.3 expression without canonical hotspot mutations; the H3-3A/H3-3B mutation landscape in DIPG is a defining diagnostic criterion [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md)
- [DMG](../cancer_types/DMG.md) — diffuse midline glioma is defined by H3 K27M mutation (either H3-3A or H3-3B) per WHO 2021 classification [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- H3-3B mutations are typically mutually exclusive with H3-3A mutations in DIPG/DMG (one paralogue carries the K27M hotspot); co-occurrence with NRAS, CIC, and KMT2C somatic variants observed in one H3-wildtype DIPG PDX model [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- H3-3B K27M-mutant DIPG is a therapeutic target for H3K27M-directed therapies; the absence of canonical H3K27M mutations in some DIPG models (as in the PPTC cohort) suggests alternative epigenetic drivers that may require distinct treatment approaches [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md)

## Open questions

- Whether DIPG models lacking canonical H3-3A/H3-3B hotspot mutations represent a molecularly distinct subtype or sequencing/sensitivity artifacts is unresolved; RNA-level missense variants (rather than confirmed genomic mutations) were reported in IBs-W0128DIPG [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md)
