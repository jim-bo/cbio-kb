**PMID:** 33863915 · **DOI:** [10.1038/s41523-021-00250-8](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41523-021-00250-8) · **Journal:** npj Breast Cancer (2021)

## TL;DR

da Silva and colleagues at Memorial Sloan Kettering characterized [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) somatic alterations across 60 primary [metaplastic breast cancers (MBCs)](../cancer_types/MBC.md), a rare and aggressive [breast cancer](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) subtype. Using whole-exome sequencing (n=27), [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) targeted sequencing of 341–468 cancer-related genes (n=17), and Sanger sequencing (n=16), they found TERT promoter hotspot mutations or gene amplification in 17% (10/60) of MBCs — significantly less frequent in chondroid-predominant MBCs (0/25) than in other histologic subtypes (p=0.005). TERT alterations were mutually exclusive with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations (p<0.001, CoMEt) and significantly co-occurred with [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) hotspot mutations (p=0.001) [PMID:33863915](../papers/33863915.md).

## Cohort & data

- **Cohort:** 60 primary [metaplastic breast cancers (MBC)](../cancer_types/MBC.md) from MSKCC, median age 57 (range 34–85), 92% histologic grade 3, 95% triple-negative phenotype.
- **Predominant histology:** squamous 23% (14/60), spindle cell 27% (16/60), osseous 8% (5/60), chondroid 42% (25/60).
- **Datasets:** Sequencing data of 19 previously unreported MBCs (5 WES + 14 MSK-IMPACT) released as [mbc_msk_2021](../datasets/mbc_msk_2021.md). Three of the MSK-IMPACT cases were previously reported in [msk_impact_2017](../datasets/msk_impact_2017.md) (Zehir et al., [PMID:28481359](../papers/28481359.md) if in corpus).
- **Assays/methods:** [Whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) (n=27), [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) 341–468-gene targeted panel (n=17), [Sanger sequencing](../methods/sanger-sequencing.md) of TERT promoter, TP53, PIK3CA, HRAS, BRAF (n=16). Variants called with [MuTect](../methods/mutect.md), [Strelka](../methods/strelka.md), VarScan2, Platypus, Lancet, Scalpel; CCFs computed with [ABSOLUTE](../methods/absolute.md); CNAs/LOH with [FACETS](../methods/facets.md); mutational signatures with [SigMA](../methods/sigma-mutational-signatures.md) and [DeconstructSigs](../methods/deconstructsigs.md); reads aligned with [BWA](../methods/bwa.md) v0.7.15 to GRCh37.

## Key findings

- **TERT alteration frequency:** 17% (10/60) — TERT promoter C228T in 9/60 (15%) and TERT gene amplification in 1/60 (2%). All TERT-altered MBCs were triple-negative [PMID:33863915](../papers/33863915.md).
- **Histologic association:** TERT alterations were significantly less frequent in chondroid-predominant MBCs (0/25) versus other subtypes (10/35; p=0.005, Fisher's exact). By subtype: squamous 21% (3/14), spindle 31% (5/16), osseous 40% (2/5), chondroid 0% (0/25) [PMID:33863915](../papers/33863915.md).
- **Mutual exclusivity with TP53:** TP53 mutations were detected in 89% (34/38) of TERT-WT vs 50% (3/6) of TERT-altered MBCs (p=0.04, Fisher). Formal CoMEt mutual-exclusivity analysis on the WES/MSK-IMPACT cohort (n=44) yielded p<0.01; confirmed across the full n=60 cohort at p<0.001 [PMID:33863915](../papers/33863915.md).
- **Co-occurrence with PIK3CA:** TERT-altered MBCs were significantly enriched for clonal PIK3CA hotspot mutations — 5/6 (83%) TERT-altered vs 5/38 (13%) TERT-WT (p=0.001, Fisher) [PMID:33863915](../papers/33863915.md).
- **Tumor mutation burden:** No significant TMB difference between TERT-altered (median 3.1 mut/Mb; range 0.8–6.1) and TERT-WT (median 3.5 mut/Mb; 0.8–8.7) MBCs (p=0.72, Mann–Whitney) [PMID:33863915](../papers/33863915.md).
- **Fraction of genome altered:** TERT-altered MBCs had a significantly lower FGA (median 22%; 9–51%) than TERT-WT MBCs (median 54%; 20–86%; p=0.002, Mann–Whitney) [PMID:33863915](../papers/33863915.md).
- **Mutational signatures:** 60% (23/38) of MBCs displayed dominant COSMIC signatures 3 and 8 (HRD-associated); 34% (13/38) dominant aging signatures 1/5; one case each with APOBEC signature 2 and signature 17. No signature differences between TERT-altered and TERT-WT MBCs (p>0.05) [PMID:33863915](../papers/33863915.md).
- **Ras pathway mutations co-occurring with TERT:** Among 10 TERT-altered MBCs, activating Ras pathway hotspots were found in 3: [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) A59G (subclonal), [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) Q61L (clonal), and [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) Q61R (clonal) — the latter two co-occurring with TP53 mutations and a subclonal [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) D594N [PMID:33863915](../papers/33863915.md).
- **Adenomyoepithelioma-like case:** One MBC (MBC103T) showed a biphasic epithelial/myoepithelial proliferation (p63+/calponin+ abluminal layer) and harbored TERT C228T plus PIK3CA H1047R without TP53 or HRAS Q61 mutations, suggesting MBC arising in the context of adenomyoepithelioma [PMID:33863915](../papers/33863915.md).

## Genes & alterations

- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) — Promoter hotspot C228T (c.-124C>T) in 9/60 MBCs and gene amplification in 1/60; all in triple-negative cases. Mutually exclusive with TP53; co-occur with PIK3CA hotspots [PMID:33863915](../papers/33863915.md).
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — Mutations in 89% of TERT-WT vs 50% of TERT-altered MBCs; mutually exclusive with TERT alterations (CoMEt p<0.001) [PMID:33863915](../papers/33863915.md).
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — Clonal hotspot mutations (e.g., H1047R) significantly enriched in TERT-altered MBCs (83% vs 13%; p=0.001) [PMID:33863915](../papers/33863915.md).
- [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) — Q61R/Q61K hotspots in 3/60 MBCs; one Q61R co-occurred with TERT promoter mutation; rare in primary breast cancer overall but recurrent in adenomyoepitheliomas [PMID:33863915](../papers/33863915.md).
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — Subclonal A59G in one TERT-altered MBC [PMID:33863915](../papers/33863915.md).
- [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) — Clonal Q61L in one TERT-altered MBC (MT45), co-occurring with TP53 V173L/E204Vfs*4 [PMID:33863915](../papers/33863915.md).
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — Subclonal D594N hotspot in one TERT-altered MBC; BRAF amplification in 2 TERT-WT MBCs (MTC23, MBC104T) [PMID:33863915](../papers/33863915.md).
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md), [NOTCH2](../genes/NOTCH2.md), [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md), [NCOR1](../genes/NCOR1.md), [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md) — Mutations identified only in TERT-WT MBCs (though not reaching statistical significance after multiple-testing correction) [PMID:33863915](../papers/33863915.md).
- [NOTCH3](../genes/NOTCH3.md), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), [MED12](../genes/MED12.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — Detected at varying frequencies across both groups [PMID:33863915](../papers/33863915.md).

## Clinical implications

- **PI3K/mTOR therapeutic rationale:** The authors note that PIK3CA enrichment in non-chondroid MBCs supports prior phase 1 data showing higher response rates to [mTOR inhibition](../drugs/temsirolimus.md) (temsirolimus or [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md)) combined with liposomal [doxorubicin](../drugs/doxorubicin.md) and [bevacizumab](../drugs/bevacizumab.md) in PI3K-pathway-altered advanced triple-negative MBC (Basho et al. 2017, ref 31) [PMID:33863915](../papers/33863915.md).
- **Differential diagnosis:** TERT promoter hotspot mutations and amplification cannot reliably distinguish MBC from malignant phyllodes tumors of the breast — both can harbor TERT alterations, so this marker should be used with caution in differential diagnosis [PMID:33863915](../papers/33863915.md).
- **Taxonomic implication:** Co-occurrence of TERT promoter mutations with HRAS Q61 and PIK3CA hotspots — alterations classically seen in adenomyoepitheliomas — raises the hypothesis that a subset of MBCs may evolve through pathways similar to or shared with adenomyoepithelial tumors [PMID:33863915](../papers/33863915.md).

## Limitations & open questions

- Small sample size for a rare tumor type; limited DNA in some cases meant not all 60 MBCs underwent WES or MSK-IMPACT — so the 2% TERT amplification rate is conservative (12 of 16 Sanger-only MBCs could not be assessed for amplification) [PMID:33863915](../papers/33863915.md).
- Multi-institutional cohort lacks consistent clinical follow-up; no definitive survival analysis tied to TERT status was possible [PMID:33863915](../papers/33863915.md).
- Whether TP53/TERT mutual exclusivity reflects true epistatic interactions or simply differential baseline frequencies across histologic subtypes cannot be resolved without larger cohorts [PMID:33863915](../papers/33863915.md).
- Discrepancy with Krings & Chen (2018) regarding TERT presence in chondroid-differentiated MBCs may reflect inclusion of mixed-histology cases in this series versus pure subtypes in the prior study [PMID:33863915](../papers/33863915.md).
