# CNA-PP (copy-number analysis pipeline)

## Overview

CNA-PP (also written CNApp) is a computational tool for copy-number alteration analysis from sequencing data. It calculates broad and focal copy-number alteration scores and whole-genome doubling (WGD) status from segmented copy-number data, enabling genome-wide CNA burden quantification.

## Used by

- [PMID:36611031](../papers/36611031.md) — used to quantify copy-number alterations in colitis-associated cancer (CAC) and sporadic CRC samples; documented that broad copy-number changes are significantly more common in sporadic CRC than CAC (P<0.001), while focal CNAs were similar; also established that fraction genome altered and WGD are significantly increased in CAC vs. dysplasia in the WES subcohort [PMID:36611031](../papers/36611031.md).
