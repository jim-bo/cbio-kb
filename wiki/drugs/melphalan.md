# melphalan

## Overview

Melphalan (L-phenylalanine mustard) is a bifunctional alkylating agent that cross-links DNA. It is used in high-dose autologous stem-cell transplant conditioning (principally in multiple myeloma) and has been evaluated in reduced-intensity allogeneic conditioning regimens for [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md) and [MDS](../cancer_types/MDS.md).

## Evidence in the corpus

- Referenced as a component of conditioning regimens for allogeneic SCT in the context of the WashU [decitabine](../drugs/decitabine.md) AML/MDS trial (NCT01687400, N=116); allogeneic SCT vs no SCT was the dominant Cox survival predictor (P<0.001), and benefit was not adversely affected by [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) status [PMID:27959731](../papers/27959731.md)

## Resistance mechanisms

- Not specifically reported in the corpus.

## Cancer types (linked)

- [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md) — reduced-intensity or myeloablative conditioning for allo-SCT.
- [MDS](../cancer_types/MDS.md) — conditioning for allo-SCT as consolidation after HMA response.

## Sources

- [PMID:27959731](../papers/27959731.md) — Welch et al. 2016, *NEJM*. WashU AML/MDS [decitabine](../drugs/decitabine.md) trial; melphalan referenced as SCT conditioning context; SCT was the dominant survival predictor.
