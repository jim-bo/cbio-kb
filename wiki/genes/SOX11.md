# SOX11

## Overview

SOX11 (SRY-Box Transcription Factor 11) is a neurodevelopmental transcription factor critical for neural stem and progenitor cell identity. In glioma, SOX11 is one of the top-ranking markers of the cancer stem cell (CSC)/stem-progenitor compartment in oligodendroglioma, supporting a tri-potent neural-progenitor-like rather than oligodendrocyte-progenitor-like apex of the tumor hierarchy.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- SOX11 is among the 20 highest-ranking genes defining the stem/progenitor program (PC2/PC3) in a single-cell RNA-seq study of 4,347 cells from 6 untreated grade II IDH-mutant 1p/19q-codeleted oligodendrogliomas (Smart-Seq2). SOX11 co-defines this compartment alongside [SOX4](../genes/SOX4.md) and [SOX2](../genes/SOX2.md), and overlaps glioblastoma CSC signatures (hypergeometric P = 1.5×10⁻⁴). [PMID:27806376](../papers/27806376.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [ODG](../cancer_types/ODG.md) — SOX11 marks the rare stem/progenitor subpopulation (1.5–8% of cells per tumor) that accounts for nearly all proliferating cancer cells; its expression program is preferentially present in prenatal human brain (Allen Brain Atlas, t-test P = 8×10⁻¹⁸) and fetal human brain single cells (hypergeometric P = 0.006), consistent with a neural-progenitor cell of origin. [PMID:27806376](../papers/27806376.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-expressed with [SOX4](../genes/SOX4.md), [SOX2](../genes/SOX2.md), [NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md), [ASCL1](../genes/ASCL1.md), [CHD7](../genes/CHD7.md), [CD24](../genes/CD24.md), [BOC](../genes/BOC.md), [TCF4](../genes/TCF4.md), [CCND2](../genes/CCND2.md) in the oligodendroglioma stem/progenitor compartment; mutually exclusive with differentiated oligodendrocytic ([OLIG1](../genes/OLIG1.md), [OLIG2](../genes/OLIG2.md), [OMG](../genes/OMG.md)) and astrocytic ([APOE](../genes/APOE.md), [SOX9](../genes/SOX9.md), [GFAP](../genes/GFAP.md)) markers. [PMID:27806376](../papers/27806376.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- The stem/progenitor program (including SOX11) defines the proliferative apex of oligodendroglioma; differentiated cells are largely non-cycling. Targeting the stem/progenitor compartment is proposed as a therapeutic strategy, but no specific drug is evaluated in this study. [PMID:27806376](../papers/27806376.md)

## Open questions

- Stemness of SOX11-high cells was not functionally validated by xenotransplantation (grade II oligodendroglioma cells do not engraft). Generality beyond IDH-mutant 1p/19q-codeleted oligodendroglioma is unestablished. [PMID:27806376](../papers/27806376.md)
