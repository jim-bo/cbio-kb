# PathwayMapper

## Overview

PathwayMapper is an interactive, collaborative web-based tool for visualizing cancer genomic alterations overlaid on biological pathway diagrams. It is integrated with cBioPortal and allows users to create, share, and edit pathway templates that display alteration frequencies (mutations, copy-number changes, expression outliers) from cancer genomic studies. It uses the SBGN (Systems Biology Graphical Notation) standard for pathway representation and is particularly suited for communicating pan-cancer pathway-level alteration patterns.

## Used by

- Used to visualize and share the 10 canonical oncogenic signaling pathway templates (cell cycle, Hippo, Myc, Notch, NRF2, PI3K/Akt, RTK-RAS, TGFβ, p53, WNT/β-catenin) populated with alteration frequencies from 9,125 TCGA PanCanAtlas tumors; all pathway templates accessible via cBioPortal (http://cbioportal.org/pancanatlas) and PathwayMapper [PMID:29625050](../papers/29625050.md).

## Notes

- Integrated with cBioPortal for direct querying of genomic alteration data.
- Supports collaborative editing and sharing of pathway diagrams.
- Part of the TCGA PanCanAtlas data dissemination toolkit alongside the cBioPortal pancanatlas portal.
