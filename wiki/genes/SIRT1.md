# SIRT1

## Overview

SIRT1 is a NAD+-dependent deacetylase (sirtuin family) that regulates a broad array of substrates including histones, transcription factors, and metabolic enzymes. In cancer, SIRT1 modulates chromatin state by deacetylating H3K27 and H3K36 residues, influencing gene expression programs linked to Wnt signaling and epithelial identity. Multi-omic profiling of endometrial carcinoma has revealed SIRT1 as part of a histone acetylation network altered by oncogenic driver mutations.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- SIRT1 protein levels are increased in [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) hotspot-mutant endometrial tumors; SIRT1, [SIRT3](../genes/SIRT3.md), and [BRD4](../genes/BRD4.md) protein levels correlate negatively with H3K27 and H3K36 acetylation in CPTAC endometrial carcinoma, whereas [BRD3](../genes/BRD3.md) correlated positively with multiple H2B N-terminal acetyl sites [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)
- SIRT1 implicated together with mir17HG in dsDNA break repair, linked to parenchymal HRD enrichment in prostate cancer brain metastases (PCBM) [PMID:35504881](../papers/35504881.md)

## Cancer types (linked)
