# IGLL5

## Overview

IGLL5 encodes immunoglobulin lambda-like polypeptide 5, an immunoglobulin-related gene expressed in B cells. In DLBCL, IGLL5 is an aberrant somatic hypermutation (AID) target with a predominant AID mutational signature, recurrently mutated in the C5 ABC-DLBCL subtype alongside [PIM1](../genes/PIM1.md).

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- IGLL5 identified as an aberrant somatic-hypermutation target with predominant AID signatures in DLBCL C5 ABC subtype in a 304-patient WES cohort; co-listed with PIM1 as recurrently mutated AID targets in C5. [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [DLBCLNOS](../cancer_types/DLBCLNOS.md): IGLL5 recurrently mutated by aberrant AID activity in the C5 [MYD88](../genes/MYD88.md)^L265P^/CD79B DLBCL subtype, which is associated with inferior progression-free survival. [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with PIM1 as AID signature-driven mutation targets in DLBCL C5; C5 is defined by MYD88 L265P and [CD79B](../genes/CD79B.md) mutations. [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- C5 DLBCL (MYD88/CD79B-driven) represents an unfavorable subtype where BCR-pathway inhibitors ([BTK](../genes/BTK.md) inhibitors, e.g., [ibrutinib](../drugs/ibrutinib.md)) are under investigation; IGLL5 mutations in this context reflect AID-driven genomic instability rather than a therapeutic target per se. [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Open questions

- Whether IGLL5 mutations contribute functionally to C5 DLBCL biology or are passenger AID off-targets is not established. [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)
