# Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (University of Turin, Lung Cancer 2017)

## Overview

Vavalà et al. performed targeted next-generation sequencing on 41 [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) patient samples at the University of Turin. The cohort was used to characterize the genomic landscape of NSCLC, with a particular focus on [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) hotspot mutations and their co-occurrence with oncogenic driver events in young-onset lung cancer contexts.

## Composition

- **N = 41** NSCLC samples.
- Cancer type: [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) (OncoTree code); predominantly [lung adenocarcinoma](../cancer_types/LUAD.md).
- Institution: University of Turin, Italy.
- Sequencing approach: targeted DNA-seq ([targeted-dna-seq](../methods/targeted-dna-seq.md)); reference genome hg19.

## Assays / panels (linked)

- [targeted-dna-seq](../methods/targeted-dna-seq.md) — targeted next-generation sequencing of a cancer-relevant gene panel.

## Papers using this cohort

- [PMID:27346245](../papers/27346245.md) — Tansir et al., "A profile of lung cancer in the young population with a highlight on the Indian perspective," *Frontiers in Oncology* 2025. (Cites this cohort for TP53 hotspot mutation data in young NSCLC.)

## Notable findings derived from this cohort

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) hotspot residues R248, R273, G245, and R282 were identified in young-onset [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) patients, establishing the mutational profile of TP53 co-alterations in this cohort (Vavalà et al., cited in [PMID:27346245](../papers/27346245.md)).
