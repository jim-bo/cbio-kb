# RPP25

## Overview

RPP25 (Ribonuclease P/MRP Subunit P25) encodes a subunit of the RNase P/MRP RNA processing complex. Promoter hypermethylation and silencing of RPP25 drives dependency on its paralogue [POP7](../genes/POP7.md) through a paralogue synthetic lethality mechanism. This was among the new synthetic lethal relationships identified by integrating CCLE methylation data with CRISPR/shRNA cancer dependency screens.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- RPP25 methylation/silencing drives dependence on its paralogue POP7; also associated with [RPP25L](../genes/RPP25L.md) expression changes; this paralogue synthetic lethality was discovered via multi-omic CCLE profiling (methylation + dependency screens across 1,072 cancer cell lines); replicated in TCGA primary tumor methylation data [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Pan-cancer (cell lines and TCGA primary tumours): RPP25 promoter methylation gating POP7 dependency was replicated in TCGA primary tumor methylation data across multiple lineages [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Synthetic lethal pair with POP7 (paralogue); also linked to RPP25L as a related complex subunit in the same dependency network [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- RPP25 promoter methylation status is a candidate biomarker for POP7 dependency and potential therapeutic vulnerability in tumors where RPP25 is epigenetically silenced [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)

## Open questions

- The cancer types in which RPP25 methylation is most prevalent and clinically actionable have not been systematically characterized in prospective cohorts.
