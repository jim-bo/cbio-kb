# PLK1

## Overview

PLK1 (Polo-Like Kinase 1) is a serine/threonine kinase that plays a central role in mitotic entry, spindle assembly, and cytokinesis. It is frequently overexpressed in cancer and is a therapeutic target under active investigation.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Target oncogene with increased expression in HNSCC tumors driven by deletion/decreased levels of let-7c-5p and miR-100-5p; co-regulated alongside [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md), [E2F1](../genes/E2F1.md), and [HMGA2](../genes/HMGA2.md) via this miRNA network [PMID:25631445](../papers/25631445.md)
- PLK1 activity is downstream of [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations in endometrial carcinoma; truncating [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations increase PLK1-T210 phosphorylation and mitotic-marker proteins, and PLK1 inhibition is a therapeutic candidate in TP53-mutant EC [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)
- PLK1 kinase activity elevated in [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) versus normal liver by KSEA (kinase-substrate enrichment analysis) in a European [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) proteogenomics cohort (n=51 tumors with phosphoproteome); flagged as a targetable kinase alongside [AURKA](../genes/AURKA.md) and CDK1/2/5 [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)
