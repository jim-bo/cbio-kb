# CPT1A

## Overview

CPT1A (carnitine palmitoyltransferase 1A) is the rate-limiting enzyme in mitochondrial fatty acid oxidation (FAO), catalyzing transfer of long-chain acyl groups into mitochondria. In [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md), CPT1A is targeted by [etomoxir](../drugs/etomoxir.md), and its activity is required in Wnt/β-catenin-driven HCC.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- CPT1A is the rate-limiting FAO enzyme in HCC; etomoxir (CPT1 inhibitor) showed efficacy in APC/CTNNB1-mutant (Wnt-driven) HCC models; CPT1A activity is part of the PPARα-driven FAO axis [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) — CPT1A-mediated FAO is a dependency in Wnt/β-catenin-mutant HCC; etomoxir is the prototype inhibitor [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- CPT1A FAO dependency is enriched in APC/CTNNB1-mutant HCC; the role is context-dependent (Wnt vs. p53-mutant HCC have opposing FAO dependencies) [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Etomoxir (CPT1 inhibitor) showed efficacy in Wnt-mutant HCC models; selectivity for Wnt-mutant HCC is proposed as a predictive biomarker strategy [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Open questions

- Etomoxir is a preclinical compound; dose-limiting hepatotoxicity has been a concern in early clinical use. Biomarker strategy (Wnt mutation status) for patient selection is not validated.
