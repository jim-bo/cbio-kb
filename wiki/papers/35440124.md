**PMID:** 35440124 · **DOI:** 10.1038/s41467-022-29794-4 · **Journal:** Nature Communications (2022)

## TL;DR

Caeser et al. assembled and deeply characterized a resource of 46 small cell lung cancer ([SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md)) patient-derived xenograft (PDX) and circulating tumor cell-derived xenograft (CDX) models from 33 patients at Memorial Sloan Kettering, with paired clinical specimens for 18 cases. Multi-omic profiling — [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) targeted DNA sequencing (42 PDX/CDX, 26 clinical), bulk [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) (43 PDXs), and IHC for subtype markers (37 PDXs) — confirmed retention of the canonical SCLC genomic landscape (near-universal [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) loss) and recapitulated the ASCL1/NEUROD1/POU2F3 transcriptional subtype framework in xenografts. Notably, the cohort includes 10 [POU2F3](../genes/POU2F3.md)-driven PDXs (subtype SCLC-P) derived from a single never-smoker donor — the first reported SCLC-P PDX models — along with evidence that SCLC-P can share a cell of origin with lung adenocarcinoma. The companion dataset is deposited in cBioPortal as [lung_pdx_msk_2021](../datasets/lung_pdx_msk_2021.md).

## Cohort & data

- **Models:** 46 PDX/CDX models from 33 [SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md) patients; 40/46 (87%) tissue-derived PDXs and 6/46 (13%) [CDXs](../methods/ctdx-lung.md).
- **Patient cohort:** 55% (18/33) male, 45% female; average age 66 at diagnosis. 27/33 (82%) current or former smokers. 19/33 (58%) extensive-stage at presentation, 14/33 (42%) limited-stage.
- **Engraftment:** 45/198 (23.81%) pure SCLC samples and 13/29 (44.83%) mixed-histology samples successfully engrafted; mean engraftment time 112 days (110 days PDX vs 131 days CDX).
- **Assays:** [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) targeted NGS on 42 PDX and 26 clinical samples (panel iterations of 341, 410, or 468 genes), bulk [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) on 43 PDXs, and [immunohistochemistry](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md) on 37 PDX samples and 19 clinical samples.
- **Paired clinical-PDX comparison:** 18 cases with both clinical and matched PDX MSK-IMPACT data.
- **Dataset:** [lung_pdx_msk_2021](../datasets/lung_pdx_msk_2021.md) in cBioPortal (gene expression deposited at ArrayExpress E-MTAB-11230).

## Key findings

- **Canonical SCLC drivers are retained in PDX:** [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) alterations in 24/26 (92%) clinical samples and 90% of PDX samples; [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) truncating/splice/structural variants/deep deletions in 21/26 (81%) clinical and 76% of PDX samples ([PMID:35440124](../papers/35440124.md)).
- **Paired-pair concordance:** 16/18 (89%) clinical-PDX pairs retained genomic alterations in [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [RB1](../genes/RB1.md); MSK599 and MSK871 PDXs lacked the [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) alterations seen in matched clinical samples.
- **Two atypical KRAS-amplified clinical samples (P-0039208-T01-IM6 and -T02-IM6)** lacked [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) mutations but harbored [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) amplifications together with [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) amplification; the authors speculate [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) amplification could phenocopy [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) inactivation.
- **Four never-smoker samples** (P-0022320-T01/2-IM6 and P-0025975-T01/2-IM6) carried oncogenic [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) mutations consistent with histologic transformation from [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md).
- **Higher copy-number alteration density in PDXs vs clinical samples**, attributed to greater purity after stromal replacement by murine cells.
- **Recurrent NSCLC-associated mutations in a subset of PDXs** (MSK1053, MSK511c, MSK773D/E/G/I): [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md), [STK11](../genes/STK11.md), [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md), and [PTPRS](../genes/PTPRS.md) ([PTPRS](../genes/PTPRS.md) absent in MSK511c).
- **Subtype recapitulation:** IHC + RNA-seq confirmed [ASCL1](../genes/ASCL1.md), [NEUROD1](../genes/NEUROD1.md), [POU2F3](../genes/POU2F3.md), and [YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md) subtype structure. YAP1 protein expression was consistently absent or very low across the cohort, questioning YAP1 as a defining IHC marker.
- **POU2F3-driven cluster:** A distinct subset of 10 PDXs (MSK773 B-L) from one patient was strongly POU2F3+ and negative for other subtype markers — the first published SCLC-P PDX resource. All 10 expressed [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) at protein and RNA levels.
- **YAP/TAZ downstream activity in SCLC-P:** POU2F3-positive PDXs highly expressed YAP1 downstream genes ([NOTCH2](../genes/NOTCH2.md), [NOTCH3](../genes/NOTCH3.md)) regardless of YAP1 expression itself.
- **SCLC-P transcriptional features:** high [MYC](../genes/MYC.md), high [REST](../genes/REST.md) and [BACH2](../genes/BACH2.md), low [DLL3](../genes/DLL3.md) and [ATOH1](../genes/ATOH1.md); low [INSM1](../genes/INSM1.md) and [CHGA](../genes/CHGA.md) NE markers.
- **EMT signature heterogeneous in non-NE SCLC:** SCLC-P samples lacked detectable [EPCAM](../genes/EPCAM.md) but showed variable [VIM](../genes/VIM.md) expression and low [SNAI1](../genes/SNAI1.md)/[SNAI2](../genes/SNAI2.md)/[ZEB1](../genes/ZEB1.md), consistent with EMT not being a uniform hallmark of non-NE SCLC.
- **MYC-family expression tracks subtype:** SCLC-P samples expressed high [MYC](../genes/MYC.md); the two SCLC-N samples (MSK424, MSK443) had the highest [MYCL](../genes/MYCL.md) expression, and MSK-IMPACT detected [MYCL](../genes/MYCL.md) amplifications in MSK424, MSK304, MSK40, MSK599b, MSK891, and MSK736.
- **Inflamed signature:** 7/43 PDXs (MSK831b, MSK511c, MSK325d, MSK773B, MSK773H, MSK761, MSK891) showed high expression of HLAs and antigen-presentation genes, not segregating cleanly with a single transcription-factor subtype.
- **Shared SCLC-P/LUAD origin (MSK773 case study):** A male never-smoker in his 70s with prior Hodgkin's disease and mantle field radiation, treated with carboplatin/etoposide and then [ipilimumab](../drugs/ipilimumab.md) + [nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md) without response, had an initial biopsy showing admixed SCLC and lung adenocarcinoma. Separate MSK-IMPACT on the two histologies showed shared somatic mutations and copy-number changes ([TET2](../genes/TET2.md), [CDKN1B](../genes/CDKN1B.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [TCF3](../genes/TCF3.md), [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md), [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)) with [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) loss specific to the SCLC component, supporting clonal origin and consistent with type II pneumocyte as a possible cell of origin for SCLC-P.

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — missense, truncating, splice, in-frame mutations in 92% (24/26) clinical and 90% PDX samples ([PMID:35440124](../papers/35440124.md)).
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — truncating, splice, structural variants, deep deletions in 81% (21/26) clinical and 76% PDX samples; lost specifically in SCLC component of MSK773 admixed tumor.
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — amplifications in two atypical [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)/[RB1](../genes/RB1.md)-wildtype clinical samples and in the MSK773 SCLC/adenocarcinoma case; ~15% of PDX samples.
- [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) — amplification co-occurring with [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) amplification in two TP53/RB1-wildtype clinical samples; 100% of MSK773 admixed components.
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) — oncogenic mutations in four never-smoker samples consistent with histologic transformation from [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md); 15% PDX samples.
- [MYCL](../genes/MYCL.md) — amplification correlated with high transcript expression in MSK424, MSK304, MSK40, MSK599b, MSK891, MSK736; highest expression in two SCLC-N samples.
- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) — uniformly high in POU2F3-driven PDXs and in MSK761/MSK900b outliers; 12% of PDXs.
- [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md), [STK11](../genes/STK11.md), [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md), [PTPRS](../genes/PTPRS.md) — NSCLC-typical mutations seen in MSK1053, MSK511c, and MSK773D/E/G/I PDX subset (PTPRS not in MSK511c).
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md) — MSK304B PDX lost matched-clinical amplifications but gained [MYCL](../genes/MYCL.md) amplification.
- [CDKN1B](../genes/CDKN1B.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [TCF3](../genes/TCF3.md), [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md), [TET2](../genes/TET2.md) — shared between [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) and SCLC components of MSK773 (clonal).
- [ASCL1](../genes/ASCL1.md), [NEUROD1](../genes/NEUROD1.md), [POU2F3](../genes/POU2F3.md), [YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md) — subtype-defining transcriptional regulators; protein-level YAP1 absent across cohort.
- [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md)/[NOTCH2](../genes/NOTCH2.md)/[NOTCH3](../genes/NOTCH3.md) — downstream YAP/TAZ pathway upregulated in POU2F3-positive PDXs.
- [INSM1](../genes/INSM1.md), [CHGA](../genes/CHGA.md), [NCAM1](../genes/NCAM1.md) — NE markers high in SCLC-A/N/A-N, low in SCLC-P.
- [REST](../genes/REST.md), [BACH2](../genes/BACH2.md) — high in non-NE SCLC-P; [DLL3](../genes/DLL3.md), [ATOH1](../genes/ATOH1.md) — low in SCLC-P.
- [VIM](../genes/VIM.md), [EPCAM](../genes/EPCAM.md), [SNAI1](../genes/SNAI1.md), [SNAI2](../genes/SNAI2.md), [ZEB1](../genes/ZEB1.md) — variable / non-uniform EMT pattern in SCLC-P.

## Clinical implications

- **PDX/CDX as a translational platform:** The authors argue that PDX models retain key genomic and proteomic features of donor tumors (89% concordance for TP53/RB1 in paired pairs) and are therefore suitable for testing subtype-targeted SCLC therapies emerging from early-phase trials.
- **Subtype-specific therapy hypotheses:** SCLC-P cell lines have previously been reported sensitive to PARP inhibition, and MYC-high SCLC may have selective sensitivity to Aurora kinase inhibition; the 10 MSK773 POU2F3-driven PDXs provide the first SCLC-P in-vivo system for testing such hypotheses.
- **Histologic transformation cases (EGFR-mutant never-smoker samples)** support the established phenomenon of [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) → SCLC transformation as a resistance mechanism to EGFR-targeted therapy.
- **Standard-of-care backdrop:** Authors note current first-line standard for extensive-stage SCLC is platinum-based chemoimmunotherapy ([atezolizumab](../drugs/atezolizumab.md) or [durvalumab](../drugs/durvalumab.md) with [cisplatin](../drugs/cisplatin.md)/[carboplatin](../drugs/carboplatin.md) + [etoposide](../drugs/etoposide.md)) and chemoradiotherapy for limited-stage disease.
- **Clinical case (MSK773):** patient received [carboplatin](../drugs/carboplatin.md) + [etoposide](../drugs/etoposide.md), progressed within 6 months, then received [ipilimumab](../drugs/ipilimumab.md) + [nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md) without response — illustrating limited ICB activity in SCLC-P.

## Limitations & open questions

- PDX/CDX models are maintained in immunodeficient NOD.Cg-Prkdc<scid>Il2rg<tm1Wjl>/SzJ mice; absence of an intact adaptive immune system and replacement of human stromal elements by murine cells precludes evaluation of immune-targeted therapies and may alter antigen-presentation and immune profiling.
- All 10 SCLC-P models derive from a single patient (MSK773), limiting generalizability of SCLC-P findings to broader patient populations.
- Two PDXs (MSK761, MSK900b) showed transcriptional patterns intermediate between non-SCLC-P and SCLC-P clusters, raising open questions about whether these represent additional subtype classes or transitional phenotypes — and whether site of biopsy (liver vs blood CDX in the MSK761/MSK761c pair) contributes to heterogeneity.
- The authors observed minor differences between matched clinical and PDX genomic spectra (e.g., MSK599, MSK871 lost TP53; MSK304B lost PIK3CA/FOXA1 amplifications, gained MYCL amplification) — significance of these differences (intrinsic clonal heterogeneity vs PDX bottleneck/selection) remains to be elucidated.
- Larger sample sizes and definition of gene expression signatures correlating with disease progression and drug resistance are needed; current cohort showed no major transcriptional differences between treated and treatment-naïve samples.
