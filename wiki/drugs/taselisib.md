# taselisib

## Overview

Taselisib (GDC-0032) is a beta-sparing PI3K inhibitor that preferentially inhibits the alpha, delta, and gamma isoforms of PI3K while sparing [PIK3CB](../genes/PIK3CB.md) (PI3K-beta). This isoform selectivity was designed to improve the therapeutic index compared to pan-PI3K inhibitors such as [buparlisib](buparlisib.md) and [pictilisib](pictilisib.md). Taselisib was investigated in phase I/II basket trials in PIK3CA-mutant solid tumors (e.g., NCT01296555) and breast cancer.

## Evidence in the corpus

- In a PIK3CA-mutant adenoid cystic carcinoma ([ACC](../cancer_types/ACC.md)) basket trial (NCT01296555), 6 patients with hot-spot [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) mutations received taselisib or [alpelisib](alpelisib.md): 1/6 achieved partial response and 5/6 had stable disease at 2 months; 5/6 had any tumor-volume reduction (mean 18.8%, range 4.8%–30.6%); 4/6 had PR or SD lasting >6 months; no clear genomic differences distinguished responders from non-responders [PMID:31483290](../papers/31483290.md).
