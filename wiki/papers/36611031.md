**PMID:** 36611031 · **DOI:** [10.1038/s41467-022-35592-9](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-022-35592-9) · **Journal:** Nature Communications (2023)

## TL;DR

Chatila et al. assemble the largest integrated clinico-genomic series of colitis-associated cancers (CAC) to date — 174 patients with inflammatory bowel disease (IBD)–associated bowel cancer plus 29 synchronous or isolated dysplasia specimens — and use targeted panel sequencing ([MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md), [FoundationOne](../methods/foundationone.md)) plus whole-exome sequencing, [FACETS](../methods/facets.md) clonality analysis, RNA-seq, methylation profiling, patient-derived organoids and xenografts, and an AOM/DSS mouse model to map the molecular evolution of CAC. They show that CACs are dominated by clonal [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) alterations (90% of cancers; ~50% of dysplasias, frequently via convergent evolution of independent events), are largely independent of Wnt signaling (low Wnt ssGSEA scores, growth independent of exogenous Wnt in PDOs), and arise from genetically independent neoplastic lesions rather than from a shared genomic field effect, supporting an early non-mutational (transcriptional/epigenetic) predisposing event in inflamed bowel.

## Cohort & data

- **Patients:** 174 IBD patients with colitis-associated cancer — 144 in the "clinical cohort," 161 in the "genomic cohort" (131 with full clinical + sequencing, 30 with sequencing only, plus 47 cases re-annotated from the prior MSK series). 56% ulcerative colitis, 44% Crohn's disease.
- **Tumors sequenced:** 166 CAC + 9 dysplasia with targeted panel sequencing (132 MSK-IMPACT 341–505 genes; 34 FoundationOne 315 genes); 13 CAC + 20 dysplasia with whole-exome sequencing at 150× depth.
- **Site distribution:** 10.3% small intestine, 27.1% right colon, 29.5% left colon, 31.3% rectum, 1.8% fistula tract.
- **Stage at diagnosis:** 0 (2.4%), I (16.9%), II (20.5%), III (28.3%), IV (29.5%).
- **Sites of accrual:** Memorial Sloan Kettering (n=125), Weill Cornell Medical Center–NYP (n=29), Sheba Medical Center, Israel (n=7).
- **Germline analysis:** MSK-IMPACT germline panel (76 → 88 genes) in 73 consented patients (45% of the genomic cohort).
- **Dataset:** [bowel_colitis_msk_2022](../datasets/bowel_colitis_msk_2022.md).
- **Cancer types:** [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md), [READ](../cancer_types/READ.md), [SBC](../cancer_types/SBC.md) (small bowel), with overall [COADREAD](../cancer_types/COADREAD.md) framing.
- **Functional models:** patient-derived organoids and patient-derived xenografts ([PDX](../methods/patient-derived-xenograft.md)) from CAC; AOM/DSS mouse model of inflammatory colorectal cancer with mouse MSK-IMPACT sequencing of macrodissected lesions.

## Key findings

- **Persistent risk despite surveillance:** 17% of patients (n=24) had a documented colonoscopy within one year of CAC diagnosis; 15% (n=22) had quiescent IBD off-treatment with no surveillance. 22% (n=32) of cancers were detected at surveillance colonoscopy and all carcinoma-in-situ cases were screen-detected.
- **Metastatic pattern differs from sporadic CRC:** Bone and pelvic metastases were significantly enriched in CAC versus sporadic CRC (Supplementary Fig. 2a–c).
- **Recurrent driver landscape in CAC:** [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) 90%, [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) 31%, [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) 20% (amplification frequent), [APC](../genes/APC.md) 20%, [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) 13%. [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) and [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) alterations were significantly enriched in patients with Crohn's disease versus ulcerative colitis. [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) mutations were enriched in more differentiated CAC.
- **Dysplasia driver landscape (n=29):** [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) altered in ~50% of dysplasia samples. Other oncogenic drivers seen in dysplasia included [APC](../genes/APC.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) — no alteration emerged specifically at the dysplasia-to-carcinoma transition.
- **Convergent evolution of [TP53](../genes/TP53.md):** Using [FACETS](../methods/facets.md), the majority of CAC harbored clonal [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) alterations; 14% had subclonal TP53 and 12% had no detectable TP53 alteration. Paired-sample analysis showed TP53 was always carried in both samples and often became clonal in the second specimen, supporting independent convergent TP53 acquisition rather than a shared ancestral clone.
- **Wnt pathway alterations are infrequent and functionally dispensable:** Wnt alterations were significantly enriched only in subclonal-TP53 CACs (Fisher's exact P=0.017). CAC RNA-seq showed lower Wnt ssGSEA scores than sporadic CRC (P=0.13 in 11 patients) and low Wnt expression in pathway-wild-type CAC; no canonical CMS2 tumors among CAC. Beta-catenin IHC in 14 CAC showed membranous staining in all Wnt pathway wild-type tumors.
- **Functional Wnt-independence:** Wnt wild-type and APC-mutant CAC organoids (P-0043364-PDO, P-0017016-PDO, P-0012110-PDO, P-0027647-PDO) grew without exogenous Wnt supplementation; normal colonic organoids did not (P=0.02 for normal). Porcupine inhibition with LGK-974 and tankyrase inhibition with G007-LK in three CAC PDXs (two Wnt wild-type, one APC-mutant) did not suppress tumor growth.
- **Copy-number profile distinguishes CAC from sporadic CRC:** Broad copy-number alterations were significantly more common in sporadic CRC than CAC (CNapp broad CNA score P=0.000016, Mann–Whitney U-test, CAC n=112 vs CRC n=921 IMPACT cases); focal CNA frequency was similar between groups, but a larger fraction of oncogenic alterations in CAC were CNAs (25.0% in CAC vs 16.6% in CRC). [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) focal amplifications were enriched in CAC.
- **Late ongoing instability in carcinoma:** Fraction genome altered and whole-genome doubling (WGD) were significantly increased in CAC versus paired dysplasia, consistent with copy-number/WGD accruing late in tumorigenesis.
- **Multifocal lesions develop independently:** In one patient with total colectomy yielding six synchronous lesions (low-grade dysplasia through carcinoma), no shared genetic alterations were detected across lesions. Across six additional patients with multiple primaries (two synchronous, four metachronous), multifocal tumors shared no genomic changes despite close anatomic proximity in three cases. AOM/DSS mouse model: two adjacent lesions per mouse — one mouse shared a [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) mutation between intramucosal carcinoma and high-grade dysplasia; the other shared no alterations between two intramucosal carcinomas.
- **Dysplasia/CAC pair sequencing (WES, 13 pairs):** Cell-cycle copy-number alterations were present in three carcinoma samples but not in synchronous or other dysplasia samples (late event). The majority of detected alterations were private to one lesion (P=0.00022 shared vs private for all mutations; driver-mutation comparison P=0.17).
- **No TP53 in non-neoplastic mucosa:** WES of 13 adjacent normal-appearing mucosa samples (matched with blood as normal in 5 patients) detected no TP53 alterations, arguing against a TP53-driven genomic field defect.
- **Germline alterations (n=73 consented):** Pathogenic germline alterations in 10/73 patients (14%) — most common [APC](../genes/APC.md) I1307K (3 cases, Ashkenazi Jewish low-penetrance variant); plus [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md), [DICER1](../genes/DICER1.md), [FANCA](../genes/FANCA.md), [FANCC](../genes/FANCC.md). Both [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md) carriers harbored microsatellite-stable CAC, consistent with incidental Lynch findings. Overall germline alteration frequency similar to sporadic CRC.
- **[IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) mutations are uniquely actionable in CAC:** [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) R132 mutant tumors (six identified) showed high global methylation, clustering with microsatellite-instable tumors on methylation analysis. An IDH1-mutant ileal CAC PDX produced high 2-hydroxyglutarate (2-HG); selective [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) inhibition suppressed PDX growth. One dysplastic lesion harbored an IDH1 mutation without TP53 alteration, suggesting IDH1 mutations do not require prior TP53 events. Both [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) amplification/fusion and IDH1 R132 mutations (rare in sporadic CRC) occur in CAC and have FDA-approved targeted therapies in other inflammation-associated GI cancers (intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma).
- **Dominant mutational signature:** SBS1 (aging-associated) was the most frequent mutational signature in the WES subset.

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — altered in 90% of CAC and ~50% of dysplasia; clonal in the majority of CAC, subclonal in 14%; convergent evolution of independent TP53 events across multifocal lesions; not detected in adjacent normal mucosa. Subclonal-TP53 CACs were enriched for Wnt-pathway alterations (P=0.017).
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — 31% of CAC; enriched in moderately differentiated histology; less frequent in CAC than sporadic CRC.
- [APC](../genes/APC.md) — somatic mutation in only 20% of CAC (vs ~80% in sporadic CRC); APC-mutant CAC organoids remained Wnt-independent for growth; germline I1307K seen in 3 CAC patients (Ashkenazi Jewish low-penetrance allele).
- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) — amplified in 20% of CAC; focal amplifications enriched vs sporadic CRC; also amplified in the AOM/DSS mouse model lesions.
- [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) — altered in 13% of CAC.
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — enriched in Crohn's disease–associated CAC vs ulcerative colitis–associated CAC.
- [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) — R132 mutations identified in CAC (6 cases); associated with high global methylation; 2-HG production confirmed in PDX; pharmacologic IDH1 inhibition suppressed PDX growth; enriched in Crohn's disease–associated CAC.
- [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) — amplification or fusion seen in CAC (rare in sporadic CRC); selective FGFR inhibitor active in PDX.
- [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) — shared mutation between adjacent intramucosal carcinoma and high-grade dysplasia in one of two analyzed AOM/DSS mouse lesions.
- [RNF43](../genes/RNF43.md) — Wnt-pathway alteration not selectively enriched in APC-wild-type CAC; mutated in a subset of dysplasia and CAC.
- [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md) — recurrently altered in CAC (10% in dysplasia comparison oncoprint).
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — V600E mutations occurred significantly less frequently in CAC than sporadic CRC.
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) — recurrent alterations among multi-primary metachronous CAC samples.
- Germline DNA-repair alterations in CAC: [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md), [DICER1](../genes/DICER1.md), [FANCA](../genes/FANCC.md), [FANCC](../genes/FANCC.md) — overall frequency similar to sporadic CRC; both [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md) carriers had microsatellite-stable tumors.

## Clinical implications

- **Surveillance limits:** Surveillance colonoscopy detected all carcinoma-in-situ cases but ~62% of CAC patients still presented with worsening bowel symptoms; cancer risk persists in quiescent IBD off treatment and in any residual mucosa, motivating continued surveillance even outside active flares.
- **Actionable drivers distinct from sporadic CRC:** [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) R132 mutations and [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) amplification/fusion — both with FDA-approved targeted therapies in cholangiocarcinoma ([ivosidenib](../drugs/ivosidenib.md) for IDH1; FGFR inhibitors) — are present in CAC and were functionally validated in PDX models, supporting prospective genomic profiling of CAC for trial assignment beyond standard CRC regimens.
- **Wnt-pathway therapeutics unlikely to benefit CAC:** Pharmacologic Wnt blockade (porcupine inhibitor LGK-974, tankyrase inhibitor G007-LK) did not suppress CAC PDX growth, including in APC-mutant tumors — arguing against repurposing Wnt-axis drugs for CAC.
- **Germline testing yield comparable to sporadic CRC:** 14% pathogenic germline alteration rate supports offering germline panel testing to CAC patients, though PMS2 incidental findings should be interpreted with the patient's MSI status.
- **Multifocal CAC requires independent sampling/treatment decisions:** Because separate primaries lack shared driver alterations, treatment decisions (especially targeted therapy selection) should be informed by sequencing the specific tumor being treated, not extrapolated from a synchronous lesion.

## Limitations & open questions

- Retrospective sample collection; relatively small number of dysplasia samples (29 sequenced, 13 dysplasia/CAC pairs with WES).
- Primarily targeted-panel sequencing with WES in a subset — non-cancer genes possibly involved in CAC development may be missed.
- Patients with Lynch syndrome or microsatellite-instable tumors were excluded to focus on inflammation-driven biology — the findings therefore do not generalize to MMR-deficient CAC.
- Mechanism of the inferred non-mutational (likely epigenetic/transcriptional) early field defect remains unidentified; whether Wnt-independence is itself the predisposing event or a consequence of lineage plasticity is unresolved.
- Only one mouse pair was sequenced from the AOM/DSS model (two mice, two lesions each).
- Wnt ssGSEA comparison between CAC and CRC patients was based on only 11 patients (5 CAC, 6 CRC).
- Substantial unanswered question: which non-genomic event(s) in inflamed mucosa license neoplastic transformation, given the absence of a genomic field effect?
