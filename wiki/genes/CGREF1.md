# CGREF1

## Overview

CGREF1 (Cell Growth Regulator with EF Hand domain 1) encodes a 301-amino-acid secreted protein with two calcium-binding EF-hand domains. It was initially identified as a p53-responsive growth suppressor in rodent cells. In colorectal cancer (CRC), CGREF1 is overexpressed relative to matched normal tissue and functions as a pro-migratory/pro-invasive factor at the tumor invasive front, without affecting bulk proliferation. High CGREF1 protein expression is an independent predictor of shorter disease-free survival. Its overexpression within tumor-budding foci implicates it in the spatially restricted invasive-front phenotype.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- CGREF1 (301-aa secreted protein with EF-hand domains) is over-expressed at mRNA and protein levels in colorectal cancer vs. matched normal; high protein expression is an independent predictor of shorter disease-free survival (HR 3.85, p=0.014, n=80). siRNA/shRNA knockdown suppresses migration in vitro and liver metastasis in vivo via F-actin/pseudopodia disruption without affecting proliferation. [PMID:32888432](../papers/32888432.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **Colorectal cancer (COAD/READ):** Overexpressed in CRC vs. matched normal tissue; high IHC protein expression (IRS scoring on FFPE tissue) is an independent predictor of shorter disease-free survival (HR 3.85, p=0.014). Localized overexpression within tumor-budding (Bd3) foci at the invasive front. Studied in a retrospective cohort of 80 CRC patients (Hunan Cancer Hospital, 2018). [PMID:32888432](../papers/32888432.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- CGREF1 was previously included in a 4-gene cell-cycle prognostic signature ([MCM2](../genes/MCM2.md), [INHBA](../genes/INHBA.md), CGREF1, [KLF9](../genes/KLF9.md)) for early-onset CRC (Xiang et al. 2023, cited within paper). The corpus paper is the first to validate CGREF1 as a standalone independent prognostic biomarker in CRC. [PMID:32888432](../papers/32888432.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- CGREF1 is proposed as a potential therapeutic target specifically for inhibiting metastatic dissemination (via F-actin/pseudopodia signaling) rather than primary tumor growth. The downstream pathway by which CGREF1 controls F-actin polymerization in CRC has not yet been identified. In hepatocellular carcinoma, CGREF1 acts via EIF3H/Wnt/β-catenin, but this mechanism was not tested in CRC. [PMID:32888432](../papers/32888432.md)

## Open questions

- Downstream signaling pathway by which CGREF1 controls F-actin polymerization and pseudopodia formation in CRC remains uncharacterized.
- Deng et al. 2015 reported CGREF1 suppresses CRC cell proliferation via AP-1 inhibition; the present study finds no effect on proliferation — the discrepancy is unresolved. [PMID:32888432](../papers/32888432.md)
- External validation in independent CRC cohorts and patient-derived organoids is needed; functional knockdown work used only two CRC cell lines (SW480 and RKO). [PMID:32888432](../papers/32888432.md)
