# UBA2

## Overview

UBA2 (Ubiquitin Like Modifier Activating Enzyme 2), also known as SAE2, is the catalytic subunit of the SUMO-1 E1 activating enzyme heterodimer (with [SAE1](../genes/SAE1.md)). It initiates the SUMOylation cascade, which modulates nuclear transport, DNA damage response, and transcriptional regulation. In the CCLE proteomics dataset, UBA2 protein abundance was identified as a predictor of [WRN](../genes/WRN.md) synthetic lethality in MSI cancer cell lines.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- SUMO E1 heterodimer subunit (with SAE1); UBA2 protein abundance predicts WRN knockdown sensitivity in MSI cancer cell lines across CCLE [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- MSI cancer cell lines (CCLE): UBA2 levels associated with sensitivity to WRN synthetic lethality [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Functionally linked to SAE1 as the SUMO E1 heterodimer; both predict WRN knockdown sensitivity in MSI lines [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- [PCSK7](../genes/PCSK7.md) mutation uniquely associated with SUMO E1 heterodimer protein levels [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- UBA2 protein abundance as a proteomic biomarker for WRN-directed synthetic lethality therapy in MSI tumors [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Open questions

- Mechanistic link between SUMO E1 activity (UBA2/SAE1) and WRN helicase function in MSI cells remains to be elucidated [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
