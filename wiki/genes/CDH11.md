# CDH11

## Overview

CDH11 (Cadherin-11, OB-cadherin) is a type II classical cadherin involved in cell adhesion and signaling. It is located at 16q22, a chromosomal region frequently lost in retinoblastoma. CDH11 has been proposed as a candidate gene mediating the aggressive vitreous seeding phenotype associated with 16q loss-of-heterozygosity in retinoblastoma, though coding mutations in CDH11 are not observed in the relevant cohorts.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- CDH11 is a candidate gene previously proposed for 16q-loss-associated vitreous seeding in retinoblastoma; no CDH11 coding mutations were identified in the MSK-IMPACT–profiled retinoblastoma cohort ([rbl_mskcc_2020](../datasets/rbl_mskcc_2020.md), n=83 enucleated specimens), leaving its role unclear in this dataset [PMID:33466343](../papers/33466343.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **Retinoblastoma ([RBL](../cancer_types/RBL.md)):** CDH11 proposed as candidate vitreous-seeding mediator via chromosomal 16q loss; no somatic coding mutations detected in 83 enucleated retinoblastoma specimens [PMID:33466343](../papers/33466343.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- No co-occurrence data available for CDH11 in the corpus; association is with the chromosomal-level 16q LOH event rather than point mutations [PMID:33466343](../papers/33466343.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No therapeutic targeting reported in the corpus.

## Open questions

- Whether CDH11 protein loss (via 16q LOH without coding mutation) mediates vitreous seeding in retinoblastoma requires functional and expression-level investigation [PMID:33466343](../papers/33466343.md).
