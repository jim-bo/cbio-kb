# MCM6

## Overview

MCM6 (Minichromosome Maintenance Complex Component 6) encodes a core subunit of the MCM2-7 helicase complex required for replication fork establishment. In cancer, MCM6 protein elevation is a downstream marker of cell-cycle dysregulation. In [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md), MCM6 is coordinately upregulated with [MCM2](../genes/MCM2.md) and [MCM4](../genes/MCM4.md) specifically in RB1-altered tumors, consistent with loss of RB1-mediated G1/S checkpoint repression of E2F transcription targets.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Protein upregulated in RB1-altered GBM tumors (12% of [gbm_cptac_2021](../datasets/gbm_cptac_2021.md) cohort); MCM2/MCM4/MCM6 coordinate upregulation marks RB1-altered tumors and nominates MCM proteins as potential biomarkers or vulnerabilities [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **GBM** — MCM6 protein upregulated in RB1-altered glioblastoma multiforme samples in the CPTAC proteogenomic cohort [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Upregulated coordinately with MCM2 and MCM4 in RB1-altered GBM tumors [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- RB1-altered tumors with MCM2/MCM4/MCM6 upregulation represent a nominally therapeutic subclass in GBM; no specific clinical trial data reported in this corpus [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Open questions

- Whether MCM6 upregulation is a useful biomarker or therapeutic vulnerability in RB1-altered GBM requires prospective validation.
