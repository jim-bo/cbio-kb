# Wilms' Tumor (WT)

## Overview

Wilms' tumor (nephroblastoma) is the most common renal tumor of childhood, typically presenting between ages 3–4. It is a triphasic tumor (blastemal, epithelial, stromal components) and may be associated with [WT1](../genes/WT1.md), [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md), WTX/AMER1, SIX1/SIX2, and DROSHA/DGCR8 mutations. Associated syndromes include WAGR syndrome ([WT1](../genes/WT1.md) deletion), Denys-Drash syndrome ([WT1](../genes/WT1.md) missense), Beckwith-Wiedemann syndrome (11p15.5 imprinting), and Fanconi anemia. Prognosis is generally excellent with multimodal treatment.

## Cohorts in the corpus

- [mixed_pipseq_2017](../datasets/mixed_pipseq_2017.md) — PIPseq pediatric pan-cancer cohort (Columbia University Medical Center), which includes Wilms' tumor / nephroblastomatosis cases among 101 high-risk pediatric patients [PMID:28007021](../papers/28007021.md).

## Recurrent alterations

- PIPseq cohort: [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) N345K identified by WES in a patient with nephroblastomatosis (precursor lesion closely related to WT) — a PI3K/AKT/mTOR-inhibitor target; the same variant also supported the diagnostic interpretation of nephroblastomatosis vs Wilms' tumor [PMID:28007021](../papers/28007021.md).
- Wilms tumor PDX models in PPTC cohort (n=13): [WT1](../genes/WT1.md) hemizygous deletion in 61%; 11p15.5 LOH in 69%; [AMER1](../genes/AMER1.md) hemizygous deletions in 15%; [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) mutations in 15% (mutually exclusive with [AMER1](../genes/AMER1.md)); KT-9 (bilateral disease) harbored TP53-FXR2 fusion plus partial homozygous [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) deletion [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md).
- Wilms tumor (WT) was enrolled in MAPPYACTS pediatric precision-medicine trial (other solid tumors 23% of 787 patients); WES + RNA-seq profiling across 624 patients identified actionable alterations in 69%, with germline cancer-predisposition variants in 7–12% [PMID:35292802](../papers/35292802.md)
- Included in the MSK cWGTS pediatric/rare solid tumor cohort (n=114 patients, [mixed_kunga_msk_2022](../datasets/mixed_kunga_msk_2022.md)); whole-genome + transcriptome sequencing added oncogenic findings beyond MSK-IMPACT in 54% of patients [PMID:35585047](../papers/35585047.md)

## Subtypes

- Favorable histology (90%): predominantly blastemal, epithelial, or stromal patterns.
- Anaplastic histology (10%): diffuse anaplasia (associated with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations) — worse prognosis.
- Blastemal-predominant: high-risk; frequent DROSHA/DGCR8 mutations.

## Therapeutic landscape

- Nephrectomy plus chemotherapy (actinomycin-D, [vincristine](../drugs/vincristine.md); [doxorubicin](../drugs/doxorubicin.md) for high-stage); radiotherapy for stage III/IV or anaplastic disease.
