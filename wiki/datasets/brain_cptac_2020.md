# Pediatric Brain Cancer (CPTAC/CHOP, Cell 2020)

## Overview

The brain_cptac_2020 cohort is the first large-scale integrated proteogenomic dataset for pediatric brain tumors, generated by Petralia et al. (Cell 2020) under the CPTAC framework in collaboration with the Children's Brain Tumor Tissue Consortium (CBTTC) at Children's Hospital of Philadelphia. It comprises 218 fresh-frozen tumor samples from 199 patients spanning 7 histologic types, profiled by whole-genome sequencing, RNA-seq, and TMT-based global proteomics and phosphoproteomics. Processed data are available at http://pbt.cptac-data-view.org/ and cBioPortal (reference genome hg19/hg38).

## Composition

- **Total samples:** 218 tumor specimens from 199 patients.
- **Histology breakdown:**
  - [Low-grade glioma (LGG)](../cancer_types/LGGNOS.md) — n=93 (including [DLGGMAPK](../cancer_types/DLGGMAPK.md) subtype)
  - [Ependymoma](../cancer_types/EPM.md) — n=32
  - [High-grade glioma (HGG)](../cancer_types/HGGNOS.md) — n=25
  - [Medulloblastoma](../cancer_types/MBL.md) — n=22
  - [Ganglioglioma](../cancer_types/GNG.md) — n=18
  - [Craniopharyngioma](../cancer_types/ACPP.md) — n=16
  - [ATRT](../cancer_types/ATRT.md) — n=12
- **Sample collection:** 217 at surgery, 9 at autopsy; 18 patients contributed paired initial/recurrent tumor samples.
- **Proteomic depth:** 4,661–5,731 proteins quantified per sample (median 5,122); 2,155–3,415 phosphosites per sample (median 2,714). Total: 8,802 proteins and 18,235 phosphosites identified; 6,429 proteins and 4,548 phosphosites in ≥50% of samples in at least one diagnosis carried forward.
- **Independent HGG validation cohort:** 23 additional pediatric/young-adult HGG samples plus 18 from the discovery cohort (total n=41) profiled by TMT proteomics/phosphoproteomics.

## Assays / panels (linked)

- [whole-genome-seq](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) — WGS at 60× tumor / 30× germline on Illumina HiSeq; mutations called with [Strelka2](../methods/strelka.md), CNVs with [CNVkit](../methods/cnvkit.md).
- [rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) — Aligned with [STAR](../methods/star-aligner.md) and quantified with [RSEM](../methods/rsem.md); n=188 samples passed QC.
- [tmt-proteomics](../methods/tmt-proteomics.md) — TMT11-plex global proteomics; all 218 samples.
- [tmt-phosphoproteomics](../methods/tmt-phosphoproteomics.md) — Phosphopeptide enrichment; all 218 samples.
- [selected-reaction-monitoring](../methods/selected-reaction-monitoring.md) — Immuno-MRM validation of [select](../methods/select.md) analytes.

## Papers using this cohort

- [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md) — Petralia et al. (Cell 2020): Integrated Proteogenomic Characterization across Major Histological Types of Pediatric Brain Cancer.

## Notable findings derived from this cohort

- Consensus proteomic clustering on 218 samples resolved 8 subgroups (Ependy, Medullo, Aggressive, Cranio/LGG-BRAFV600E, HGG-rich, Ganglio-rich, [LGG](../cancer_types/LGG.md) BRAFWT-rich, LGG BRAFFusion-rich) that frequently cross histologic boundaries [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md).
- A subset of pediatric craniopharyngioma without [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)V600E mutation shared the BRAFV600E LGG proteomic signature (C4 cluster), with MEK/ERK activation at the phosphoproteomic level, nominating MEK inhibitor strategies for this [CP](../genes/CP.md) subset [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md).
- Wild-type [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md)/[IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) protein abundance was positively prognostic for overall survival in H3-wild-type HGG: combined IDH1/IDH2 protein score gave HR=23.58 (95% CI 1.42–384.6) per 50% reduction (discovery n=19) [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md).
- Recurrent medulloblastoma tumors (n=3 pairs) each acquired a new [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation absent in the matched primary, while paired primary/recurrent samples retained only a mean 18% of somatic mutations [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md).
- Multiple key findings — CP proteomic split, PAF1C upregulation in aggressive ependymoma, M1/M2 polarization differences — were detectable only at the protein/phosphoprotein level, not in RNA data [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md).
