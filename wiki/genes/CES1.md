# CES1

## Overview

CES1 (Carboxylesterase 1) encodes a serine hydrolase involved in lipid metabolism and xenobiotic detoxification. In cancer genomics, CES1 mutations have been identified as part of the somatic mutational landscape of breast lesions, with interest in its role as a marker of clonal evolution from benign to malignant breast disease.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- CES1 S12A missense mutation shared by juvenile papillomatosis (JP), [DCIS](../cancer_types/DCIS.md), and IDC-NST components in one patient (JuP3), indicating clonal origin across these lesions [PMID:33263939](../papers/33263939.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) / [IDC](../cancer_types/IDC.md) / [DCIS](../cancer_types/DCIS.md)**: CES1 S12A missense identified as a shared clonal mutation spanning juvenile papillomatosis, DCIS, and IDC-NST in the same patient, supporting clonal relatedness across these breast lesion types [PMID:33263939](../papers/33263939.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- In JuP3, CES1 S12A co-occurs with [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) E542K hotspot mutation (the dominant driver), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) truncating mutation, and [SMG1](../genes/SMG1.md) missense across JP/DCIS/IDC-NST components [PMID:33263939](../papers/33263939.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

No direct therapeutic implications have been reported for CES1 in this corpus.

## Open questions

- Whether CES1 S12A (found in a single case) is a recurrent mutation in JP-associated breast carcinoma or a passenger event requires larger series.
- Functional significance of CES1 S12A in breast carcinogenesis is unknown.
