# tucatinib

## Overview

Tucatinib (brand name Tukysa) is an orally bioavailable, HER2-selective small-molecule tyrosine kinase inhibitor that is highly selective for [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) over [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md), thereby reducing EGFR-associated toxicity (e.g., diarrhea) compared to pan-HER inhibitors. It was FDA-approved in April 2020 (in combination with [trastuzumab](../drugs/trastuzumab.md) and [capecitabine](../drugs/capecitabine.md)) for HER2+ metastatic breast cancer following one or more prior anti-HER2 regimens. Resistance mechanisms include MAPK pathway reactivation (e.g., [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) loss, activating [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) mutations).

## Evidence in the corpus

- NF1-null and ERBB2-mutant (L755S, [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E, [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) G12V) HER2+ breast cancer cells (SKBR3, BT-474, MDA-MB-361, HCC1954) are resistant to tucatinib (100 nM); [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) re-expression restores tucatinib sensitivity. Biallelic NF1 loss (8% of metastatic HER2+ tumors in a 733-patient MSK-IMPACT cohort) is the most common MAPK alteration driving anti-HER2 resistance; patients with MAPK-pathway alterations had median PFS 9.9 vs 21 months on first-line anti-HER2 therapy (HR 2.03, p=0.011 multivariate). [PMID:34795269](../papers/34795269.md)
- HER2-selective TKI; ERBB2-amplified [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) (24% in Chinese pan-cancer cohort n=10,194) actionable with [trastuzumab](../drugs/trastuzumab.md)+tucatinib alongside other HER2-directed agents [PMID:35871175](../papers/35871175.md)

## Resistance mechanisms

- [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) — biallelic loss-of-function (8% of metastatic HER2+ tumors) switches the oncogenic driver from PI3K/AKT to MEK/ERK, conferring tucatinib resistance and sensitizing cells to [trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md). [PMID:34795269](../papers/34795269.md)
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) — activating mutations (e.g., L755S; 7% of metastatic HER2+ tumors) confer tucatinib resistance via MAPK pathway reactivation, independent of HER2 amplification. [PMID:34795269](../papers/34795269.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) — HER2+ metastatic breast cancer; primary indication for tucatinib based on genomic data from 733 ERBB2-amplified tumors. [PMID:34795269](../papers/34795269.md)
