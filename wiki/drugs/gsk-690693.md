# gsk-690693

## Overview

GSK-690693 is an ATP-competitive pan-AKT inhibitor that blocks the kinase domain of [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md), [AKT2](../genes/AKT2.md), and [AKT3](../genes/AKT3.md). Unlike allosteric inhibitors (e.g., [mk-2206](../drugs/mk-2206.md)) that target the pleckstrin homology (PH) domain, GSK-690693 binds the ATP-binding site of the AKT catalytic domain, making it effective against activating fusions that lack an intact PH domain.

## Evidence in the corpus

- GSK-690693 inhibited constitutive GSK3β phosphorylation driven by the MAGI3-AKT3 fusion kinase in breast cancer, whereas the allosteric AKT inhibitor [mk-2206](../drugs/mk-2206.md) did not, because the fusion lacks the PH domain required for allosteric inhibitor binding [PMID:22722202](../papers/22722202.md).
- Findings suggest that triple-negative breast cancer patients with MAGI3-AKT3 fusions (found in ~6.9% of triple-negative cases) should be treated with ATP-competitive rather than allosteric AKT inhibitors [PMID:22722202](../papers/22722202.md).

## Resistance mechanisms

- Allosteric AKT inhibitors (PH-domain targeting) such as [mk-2206](../drugs/mk-2206.md) are predicted to be ineffective against [AKT3](../genes/AKT3.md) fusion proteins that lack an intact PH domain [PMID:22722202](../papers/22722202.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) — triple-negative subtype with MAGI3-AKT3 fusions.
