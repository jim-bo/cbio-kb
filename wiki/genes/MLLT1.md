# MLLT1

## Overview

MLLT1 (MLLT1 Super Elongation Complex Subunit, also known as ENL) encodes a chromatin reader protein that recognizes acetylated histones and is a component of the super elongation complex (SEC). MLLT1 was originally characterized as a recurrent translocation partner with [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) (MLL) in acute leukemia. In solid tumors, it has been identified as a somatically altered gene in breast cancer through genomic profiling.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- MLLT1 detected among the genes altered in whole-exome sequencing of synchronous ductal carcinoma in situ ([DCIS](../cancer_types/DCIS.md)) and invasive ductal carcinomas of no special type (IDC-NST) from 25 patients at MSKCC; found in the somatic alteration repertoire of this cohort [PMID:32220886](../papers/32220886.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) — identified among the genes altered in synchronous DCIS and IDC-NST WES profiling [PMID:32220886](../papers/32220886.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Observed alongside canonical breast cancer drivers ([TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md)) in the DCIS/IDC-NST cohort [PMID:32220886](../papers/32220886.md)

## Open questions

- The specific alteration class (mutation vs. amplification) and individual case details for MLLT1 in this breast cancer cohort are not described in the available narrative text; the role of MLLT1 as a driver vs. passenger in breast cancer progression remains to be established.
