**PMID:** 37119971 · **DOI:** [10.1016/j.mcpro.2023.100561](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.mcpro.2023.100561) · **Journal:** Molecular & Cellular Proteomics (2023)

## TL;DR

A narrative review by Babu and Snyder (Stanford) surveying the state of multi-omics
profiling for precision health as of early 2023. The review walks through the major
omics modalities (genomics, epigenomics, transcriptomics, proteomics, [metabolomics](../methods/metabolomics.md),
[lipidomics](../methods/lipidomics.md)) plus wearables and electronic health records, and reviews their
integrative use across genetic variation, cardio-metabolic disease, cancer,
infectious disease, organ transplantation, pregnancy, longevity, and host–microbiome
/ host–environment interactions. Cancer-specific content is brief but cites
landmark proteogenomic and integrative cancer-subtyping efforts ([HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md), oligodendroglial
tumors, cutaneous melanoma, thyroid cancer, high-grade serous ovarian cancer) and
identifies TCGA and CPTAC as canonical multi-omics repositories. The review is
methodological — it does not introduce a new cohort or analytical pipeline.

## Cohort & data

- **Type of study:** review article (no primary cohort).
- **Cancer-relevant references cited within the review:**
  - 256 hepatocellular carcinoma samples integrated across CNV, DNA methylation, and
    gene expression to derive five subgroups with distinct survival
    (Liu et al. 2016, cited as ref 83) — see [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md).
  - Multi-omics of oligodendroglial tumors identifying three subgroups of 1p/19q
    co-deleted gliomas (Kamoun et al. 2016, ref 84) — see [ODG](../cancer_types/ODG.md).
  - Integrated analysis of clinical variables, CNVs, DNA methylation, and gene
    expression from TCGA cutaneous melanoma data, yielding prognostic models with
    mean C-statistic 0.724 (Jiang et al. 2016, ref 85) — see [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md).
  - Multi-omics profiling of chromatin accessibility, RNA, and protein abundance in
    primary thyroid cancers, metastases, and patient-matched normal tissue,
    identifying gene-body enhancers predictive of correlated RNA/protein expression
    (Sanghi et al. 2021, ref 86).
  - Genome sequencing + proteomics of high-grade serous ovarian carcinoma linking
    gene copy-number variation to proteome and post-translational modification
    levels (Zhang et al. 2016, ref 87) — see [HGSOC](../cancer_types/HGSOC.md).
- **Repositories highlighted as multi-omics resources for cancer:** TCGA (genomic,
  epigenomic, transcriptomic, proteomic, clinical data across 32 cancers) and CPTAC
  (CNV, WGS/WES, DNA methylation, RNA-seq, miRNA, global proteome, phosphoproteome,
  acetylome, ubiquitinome, immune subtyping).

## Key findings

- The review's central thesis: individual omics layers are largely correlative;
  multi-omics integration captures functional, mechanistic, and longitudinal
  signal that no single layer can ([PMID:37119971](../papers/37119971.md)).
- Genome sequencing costs fell from billions of dollars (Human Genome Project,
  2000) to ~$100/genome by 2022 (Ultima Genomics); ultra-rapid nanopore
  sequencing now enables clinical genomes in as few as 5 hours.
- Mass-spectrometry proteomics costs dropped from $3,250/sample (2006) to
  $375/sample (2021), enabling routine clinical proteomic biomarker discovery.
- Longitudinal integrative personal omics profiling (iPOP) on a 54-year-old
  individual across 20 time points / 14 months captured a healthy → insulin-resistant
  transition following viral infection — establishing the template for
  longitudinal multi-omics that the cancer field has since adopted.
- The Pioneer 100 Wellness Project (108 individuals × 9 months) was scaled into the
  100K Wellness Project; deep multi-omics of >67 clinically actionable health
  discoveries was reported in a >8-year T2DM-enriched cohort.
- In cancer, integrative multi-modal analysis improves prognostic model accuracy
  (cutaneous melanoma C-statistic = 0.724 with integrated CNV + methylation +
  expression + clinical, compared with weaker single-modality models, ref 85).
- Chromatin-accessibility multi-omics in thyroid cancer (ref 86) demonstrated that
  gene-body enhancers can predict correlated RNA and protein expression — a
  mechanism not visible from genomics or transcriptomics alone.
- Proteogenomic integration in [HGSOC](../cancer_types/HGSOC.md) (ref 87) tied copy-number variation to
  proteome and PTM-level changes, providing a mechanistic link between CNV
  drivers and downstream protein-level cancer progression events.

## Clinical implications

- The authors argue that multi-omics integration is poised to transform precision
  oncology by enabling subtyping, biomarker discovery, and stratification beyond
  what any single assay can deliver.
- They highlight that proteomics specifically reveals functional consequences of
  genetic variation that RNA analysis misses, citing translation-level regulation
  in lymphoblastoid cell lines (ref 73) and post-translational dynamics in cancer
  (refs 86, 87).
- For cancer, they project that by 2030 multi-omics will be routinely used
  alongside microbiome analysis, real-time exposome monitoring, and continuous
  wearable monitoring for management of chronic disease.
- The cited proteogenomic and CPTAC-style efforts are framed as the template for
  multi-omics clinical translation in oncology (refs 88–90 covered in linked
  cancer-proteogenomics reviews).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Review, not primary data.** No new cohort, no new pipeline, no new validation
  set. All cancer-specific claims are inherited from cited primary literature.
- **Cancer coverage is shallow.** Cancer occupies a single paragraph of the
  applications survey; cardio-metabolic disease, COVID-19, and pregnancy receive
  substantially more depth.
- **Practical barriers acknowledged:** the authors list cost, specialized
  equipment, lack of sample-collection and analysis standardization, batch effects,
  missing data, high dimensionality, scalability beyond ~hundreds of participants
  (though they note one study reaching >4,000), and a shortage of clinicians
  trained to interpret multi-omics results.
- **Ethics and data governance:** privacy, consent, discrimination risk, data
  ownership, and HIPAA/GDPR compliance for cloud-based multi-omics platforms are
  flagged as unresolved.
- **For cross-paper synthesis in cbio-kb:** this review is most useful as a
  citation anchor when papers in the corpus invoke "multi-omics," "proteogenomics,"
  "TCGA-class integration," or "longitudinal molecular profiling" as motivation.
  It does not contribute primary findings to any cBioPortal study page.
