# UACA

## Overview

UACA (uveal autoantigen with coiled-coil domains and ankyrin repeats) is a cytoplasmic protein whose fusion with LTK has been identified as an oncogenic driver in papillary thyroid carcinoma. The UACA–LTK fusion was discovered by comprehensive whole-genome and transcriptome sequencing (cWGTS) in a pediatric/rare-cancer cohort, representing a novel targetable structural variant in thyroid malignancy.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- UACA–LTK gene fusion detected by cWGTS in a metastatic papillary thyroid carcinoma (THPA) patient in a pediatric/rare-cancer cohort (n=114 patients); the fusion was not detectable by panel sequencing and was identified only through whole-genome + RNA-seq integration [PMID:35585047](../papers/35585047.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[THPA](../cancer_types/THPA.md)** — UACA–LTK fusion identified by cWGTS in a metastatic papillary thyroid carcinoma; represents one of 8 novel/incremental fusion findings from the pediatric cWGTS cohort [PMID:35585047](../papers/35585047.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- No co-occurrence or mutual exclusivity data reported for UACA in the corpus.

## Therapeutic relevance

- The UACA–LTK fusion involves LTK (leukocyte tyrosine kinase), a receptor tyrosine kinase; this class of fusions is potentially targetable by tyrosine kinase inhibitors, though no direct clinical data on UACA–LTK-directed therapy were reported [PMID:35585047](../papers/35585047.md)

## Open questions

- The functional oncogenicity of the UACA–LTK fusion and its prevalence in papillary thyroid carcinoma beyond this single case require further characterization.
