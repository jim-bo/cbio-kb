**PMID:** 31130341 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.ccell.2019.04.007 · **Journal:** Cancer Cell (2019)

## TL;DR

Xue et al. performed a pan-Asia, multi-center integrative genomic and transcriptomic study of 133 combined hepatocellular and intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma (cHCC-ICC) cases — a rare primary liver cancer (PLC) showing both hepatocellular and biliary differentiation — using WES (173 tumor samples, 121 cases), WGS (41 tumor samples, 37 cases), RNA-seq (97 tumor samples, 77 cases), and single-nucleus sequencing (SNS) of one mixed-type case. They classify cHCC-ICC by the Allen-Lisa criteria into separate, combined, and mixed subtypes, show that combined-type cHCC-ICC is molecularly ICC-like and mixed-type cHCC-ICC is HCC-like (Hoshida-S2), demonstrate that combined- and mixed-type tumors are monoclonal while separate-type tumors can be either mono- or multiclonal, and identify Nestin ([NES](../genes/NES.md)) as a diagnostic and prognostic biomarker for cHCC-ICC.

## Cohort & data

- **133 cHCC-ICC cases** from a pan-Asia multi-center study (8 hospitals across China, Singapore, and Japan); cohort published in cBioPortal as [hccihch_pku_2019](../datasets/hccihch_pku_2019.md).
- Pathologic subtypes (per Allen & Lisa 1949 criteria, 121 classified cases): **6 separate (Sep)**, **56 combined (Com)**, **59 mixed (Mix)**.
- Etiology: **100 HBV+**, **7 HCV+**, **1 double-positive**, **25 double-negative**.
- Assays: [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) (173 tumors / 121 cases, mean depth 108× tumor / 101× normal), [whole-genome-seq](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) (41 tumors / 37 cases), [rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) (97 tumors / 77 cases), [single-cell-dna-seq](../methods/single-cell-dna-seq.md) on case Mix_19 (74 tumor + 11 normal nuclei).
- Diagnostic validation: [immunohistochemistry](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md) for Nestin on 128 cHCC-ICC, 99 newly recruited [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md), and 86 newly recruited ICC cases.
- Tissue source: laser capture microdissection (LCM) used to separate HCC and ICC components in 3 separate-type and 41 combined-type cases.
- Cancer types: [MIXED](../cancer_types/MIXED.md) (cHCC-ICC index group); reference cohorts of [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) and [IHCH](../cancer_types/IHCH.md) drawn from TCGA-LIHC ([lihc_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/lihc_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md)), RIKEN-LIHC ([lihc_riken](../datasets/lihc_riken.md)), and ICGC cholangiocarcinoma ([chol_icgc_2017](../datasets/chol_icgc_2017.md)).

## Key findings

- **Significantly mutated genes (q < 0.1, dNdScv):** [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [AXIN1](../genes/AXIN1.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md), and [BRD7](../genes/BRD7.md). MutSigCV identified [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) as the only significantly mutated gene at FDR < 0.1.
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation rate 49.2%** in cHCC-ICC, significantly higher than TCGA-HCC (31%, p < 0.001), ICGC-ICC (22%, p < 0.0001), China-HCC (35%, p < 0.05); not significantly different from China-ICC (38%, p = 0.207).
- **[TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter C228T mutation** in 22.9% of cases — lower than TCGA-HCC (46%, p < 0.001) and absent in ICGC-ICC.
- **Epigenetic dysregulation:** [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md), [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md), [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) mutated in 35.6% of cases (histone modification / DNA methylation). SWI/SNF complex ([ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md), [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md), [BRD7](../genes/BRD7.md)) mutated in 19.5%.
- **Cellular energetics disruption:** [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md), [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md), [APOB](../genes/APOB.md), [ALB](../genes/ALB.md) mutated in 21.2% of cases.
- **[AXIN1](../genes/AXIN1.md) enrichment in mixed type:** 20% in Mix vs 2% in Com (p < 0.001); [AXIN1](../genes/AXIN1.md) expression in combined-type cHCC-ICC significantly higher than in mixed-type (p < 0.05, Student's t-test).
- **Strikingly low [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) (6%) and [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) (0%) mutation frequencies** vs HCC and ICC reference cohorts respectively, identified as a unique feature of cHCC-ICC not explained by etiology or ethnicity.
- **HBV integrations** (133 cases): [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) and [KMT2B](../genes/KMT2B.md) most recurrent. Promoter integrations in [LANCL2](../genes/LANCL2.md), PLG, MDGA2, PTPRM correlated with elevated expression.
- **Noncoding & fusion:** [NEAT1](../genes/NEAT1.md) mutated in 29.7% of cases (most frequent intergenic ncRNA); PTMS-AP1G1 fusion in 11.7%; FGFR-related fusions in 6.5%.
- **Focal CNAs:** amplifications/gains in [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) (73%), [MET](../genes/MET.md) (24%), [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md) (24%), [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md) (20%), [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) (19%); deletions/losses in [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (46%), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) (37%), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) (26%), [AXIN1](../genes/AXIN1.md) (25%).
- **Mutational signatures** ([sigprofiler](../methods/sigprofiler.md), [mutational-signatures](../methods/mutational-signatures.md)): three de novo signatures match COSMIC 22 (aristolochic acid, 63.5%), COSMIC 5 (clock-like), and COSMIC 24 (aflatoxin B1, 38.8%). TP53 R249S — an AFB1-induced HCC marker — accounted for 25.8% of TP53 mutations, all in HBV+ patients with signature 24.
- **Transcriptomic clusters (PLC, n = 367):** P1 (n = 103, biliary/ICC-like, enriched for combined-type cHCC-ICC and [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)/[IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) mutations), P2 (n = 99, Hoshida-S2 HCC-like, enriched for mixed-type cHCC-ICC, [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), high AFP), P3 (n = 65, Hoshida-S1, TGF-β-activated), P4 (n = 100, Hoshida-S3, [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md)-enriched, well-differentiated HCC).
- **Combined-type cHCC-ICCs are enriched in P1 (p < 0.001); mixed-type cHCC-ICCs are enriched in P2 (p < 0.001).** EMT is the top GSEA-enriched pathway in P1; xenobiotic and bile acid metabolism in P2.
- **Survival:** P1 and P2 patients have significantly poorer prognosis than P3 and P4 (p < 0.0001, log-rank).
- **Immune microenvironment:** three immune clusters (High, Medium, Low) using [CTLA4](../genes/CTLA4.md), [PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md), [CD274](../genes/CD274.md), etc. — High immune is enriched in mixed-type/P2; Medium in combined-type/P1.
- **Clonal origin (separate type):** 4/6 separate-type cases (Sep_01/03/05/06) shared 46–67% of mutations → monoclonal origin with branched evolution. Sep_02 and Sep_04 shared no mutations → independent (multiclonal) origins. Sep_03 CCF analysis suggests an HCC-component subclone subsequently transdifferentiated into the ICC phenotype.
- **Clonal origin (combined and mixed):** all combined-type H-I pairs (Com cases) are monoclonal; shared mutation fraction ranged 27–95% (median 74%). Mixed-type Mix_19 SNS (74 tumor nuclei) recapitulates one founding clone and two subclones consistent with [pyclone](../methods/pyclone.md) bulk CCF. All mixed-type cases harbored clonal mutations consistent with monoclonal origin.
- **Nestin ([NES](../genes/NES.md)) as biomarker:** RNA expression in cHCC-ICC significantly higher than HCC (p < 0.01), not different from ICC (p = 0.07). 104/128 (81.3%) of cHCC-ICC cases IHC Nestin-positive vs HCC and ICC (p < 0.001 for both). Median [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) Nestin-positive 18.7 mo vs Nestin-negative 46.6 mo across all PLC types (p < 0.0001, log-rank).

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — mutated 49.2% (significantly enriched vs HCC and ICC); R249S AFB1-signature hotspot in 25.8% of TP53-mutant cases (all HBV+). Focal deletion/loss in 46%. Trunk driver in phylogenetic trees.
- [AXIN1](../genes/AXIN1.md) — significantly enriched in mixed type (20% Mix vs 2% Com, p < 0.001). Loss/deletion in 25%.
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — driver gene (q < 0.1); focal loss in 26%; trunk mutation in some separate-type cases.
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md), [BRD7](../genes/BRD7.md) — significantly mutated drivers (dNdScv q < 0.1).
- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) — promoter C228T hotspot in 22.9% of cases; most recurrent HBV-integration site; gain/amplification in 19%.
- [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md), [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md), [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) — histone modification / DNA methylation pathway, jointly mutated in 35.6%.
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md), [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md), [BRD7](../genes/BRD7.md) — SWI/SNF complex, jointly mutated in 19.5%.
- [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md), [APOB](../genes/APOB.md), [ALB](../genes/ALB.md) — cellular energetics group, jointly mutated (with [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md)) in 21.2%.
- [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) — paradoxically low (6%) in cHCC-ICC vs HCC reference cohorts; enriched in P4 cluster.
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — zero mutations in cHCC-ICC; enriched in P1 cluster transcriptionally.
- [KMT2B](../genes/KMT2B.md) — second most recurrent HBV integration target.
- [NEAT1](../genes/NEAT1.md) — most frequently mutated intergenic noncoding RNA (29.7%).
- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md), [MET](../genes/MET.md), [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md), [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md) — focal amplifications in 73%, 24%, 24%, 20% respectively.
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — focal loss in 37%.
- [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) — FGFR-related fusion events in 6.5% of cases.
- [NES](../genes/NES.md) (Nestin) — proposed cHCC-ICC diagnostic and pan-PLC prognostic biomarker; positively stained in 81.3% of cHCC-ICC vs significantly lower rates in HCC/ICC.
- [EPCAM](../genes/EPCAM.md), [KRT19](../genes/KRT19.md), [PRDM5](../genes/PRDM5.md) — biliary markers elevated in P1 (combined-type cHCC-ICC).
- [GPC3](../genes/GPC3.md), [APOE](../genes/APOE.md), [ALB](../genes/ALB.md), [TBX3](../genes/TBX3.md), [SALL4](../genes/SALL4.md) — hepatocyte markers elevated in P2/P4 (HCC-like clusters).
- [RPS6KA3](../genes/RPS6KA3.md), [SYNE1](../genes/SYNE1.md), [ADGRV1](../genes/ADGRV1.md) — recurrently mutated in cHCC-ICC; ECM / cell adhesion programs.

## Clinical implications

- **Allen-Lisa pathologic subtyping has molecular and likely therapeutic weight.** Combined-type cHCC-ICC is ICC-like and may benefit from ICC-directed therapies; mixed-type cHCC-ICC is HCC-like (Hoshida-S2) and may benefit from HCC-directed therapies.
- **Nestin ([NES](../genes/NES.md)) IHC is proposed as a diagnostic biomarker for cHCC-ICC**, outperforming EpCAM because Nestin is positive in both HCC and ICC components of combined-type tumors (EpCAM is only positive in the ICC component). It is also a pan-PLC prognostic marker (median OS 18.7 mo positive vs 46.6 mo negative, p < 0.0001).
- **Differential immune microenvironments** between subtypes (mixed-type/P2 enriched for High immune cluster) suggest combined- and mixed-type cHCC-ICCs may respond differently to immune checkpoint therapies targeting [PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md), [CD274](../genes/CD274.md), [CTLA4](../genes/CTLA4.md).
- **FGFR-related fusions in 6.5%** of cHCC-ICCs may identify a subset targetable by FGFR inhibitors (this paper does not test the hypothesis directly).
- **Mutational-signature etiology** — aristolochic acid (signature 22, 63.5%) and aflatoxin B1 (signature 24, 38.8%) — implicate preventable exposures in Chinese cHCC-ICC; TP53 R249S + signature 24 is a marker for AFB1-driven HBV+ disease.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Subtype imbalance:** only 6 separate-type cases — too few for genomic subtype comparison; separate type excluded from many quantitative comparisons.
- **Reference-cohort heterogeneity:** comparisons to TCGA-HCC, ICGC-ICC, China-HCC, and China-ICC pool studies with differing assays, depths, and ethnic compositions; intermediate TERT-promoter mutation rate (22.9%) in cHCC-ICC explicitly flagged for validation in larger Chinese HCC/ICC cohorts.
- **Geographic / etiologic bias:** 75% HBV+ pan-Asia cohort; generalizability to HCV-driven or non-viral cHCC-ICC in Western populations is untested.
- **Order of phenotypic transition vs metastasis** in separate-type monoclonal cases (e.g., Sep_01, Sep_03) is unresolved — whether transdifferentiation precedes or follows dissemination is left as a future-studies item.
- **Mechanism of Nestin upregulation:** the paper notes the [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)–[NES](../genes/NES.md) repression axis from mouse models (Tschaharganeh et al., 2014) but does not directly demonstrate it in cHCC-ICC.
- **Therapeutic claims are inferential:** the recommendation to apply ICC therapies to combined-type and HCC therapies to mixed-type cHCC-ICC is based on molecular similarity, not on prospective trial evidence.
- **Single-cell coverage is limited** — SNS performed on one mixed-type case (Mix_19, 74 tumor nuclei); other mixed-type cases analyzed only by bulk CCF inference.
