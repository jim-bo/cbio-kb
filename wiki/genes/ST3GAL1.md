# ST3GAL1

## Overview

ST3GAL1 (ST3 Beta-Galactoside Alpha-2,3-Sialyltransferase 1) encodes a sialyl transferase enzyme responsible for adding sialic acid residues to galactose-containing glycoproteins and glycolipids. Aberrant sialylation of cell surface glycoproteins is a hallmark of cancer and contributes to immune evasion, metastasis, and treatment resistance. ST3GAL1 has been nominated as a therapeutic target in pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma through proteogenomic characterization of the sialylation enzyme landscape.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Upregulated sialyl-/fucosyltransferase activity including ST3GAL1 identified in PDAC (CPTAC 2021 cohort, n=140 treatment-naive resected tumors); sialyl-/fucosyltransferase inhibition targeting ST3GAL1, [ST6GAL1](../genes/ST6GAL1.md), [FUT3](../genes/FUT3.md), [FUT11](../genes/FUT11.md), and [B4GALT1](../genes/B4GALT1.md) proposed as therapeutic strategy; FUT3 upregulation in C1 (classical) subtype suggests subtype-tailored glycan-capping blockade [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md): ST3GAL1 sialyl transferase activity elevated in PDAC; candidate therapeutic target; authors caution that systemic sialylation blockade (e.g., 3F-NeuAc) caused liver/kidney toxicity in mouse models [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- ST3GAL1 inhibition proposed in combination with ST6GAL1, FUT3, FUT11, and B4GALT1 as part of a glycosylation-enzyme inhibition strategy in PDAC [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Sialyl-/fucosyltransferase inhibition (targeting ST3GAL1 among others) proposed as a therapeutic strategy in PDAC; on-target toxicity concerns from preclinical mouse models require selective inhibitor development [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Open questions

- Selective inhibitors of ST3GAL1 not yet developed; systemic sialylation blockade (3F-NeuAc) caused liver/kidney toxicity in mouse models, requiring subtype-specific or targeted delivery strategies [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)
