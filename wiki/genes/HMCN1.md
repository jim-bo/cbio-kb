# HMCN1

## Overview

HMCN1 (Hemicentin-1) encodes a large extracellular matrix protein involved in cell-cell adhesion and tissue integrity. Frequent alterations in anaplastic thyroid carcinoma implicate HMCN1 in tumor cell migration and invasion.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- HMCN1 is a cell-adhesion gene with frequent alterations in anaplastic thyroid carcinoma (ATC) in the GATCI study (n=141 WES/WGS); implicated in tumor cell migration and invasion alongside [HSPG2](../genes/HSPG2.md) [PMID:38412093](../papers/38412093.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [THAP](../cancer_types/THAP.md) — recurrent alterations observed in ATC in the multi-site GATCI consortium (329 thyroid cancer regions across 292 patients, 15 institutions) [PMID:38412093](../papers/38412093.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-altered with [HSPG2](../genes/HSPG2.md) in ATC as part of a cell-adhesion gene alteration cluster [PMID:38412093](../papers/38412093.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting reported; cell-adhesion alterations may contribute to metastatic potential in ATC [PMID:38412093](../papers/38412093.md).

## Open questions

- Functional studies are needed to determine which specific HMCN1 mutations drive dedifferentiation or invasion in ATC [PMID:38412093](../papers/38412093.md).
