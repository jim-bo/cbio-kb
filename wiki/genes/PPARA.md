# PPARA

## Overview

PPARA (peroxisome proliferator-activated receptor alpha) is a nuclear receptor that drives fatty acid oxidation (FAO). In [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md), PPARα-driven FAO is exploited by Wnt/β-catenin-mutant tumors for energy production, and its inhibition blocks tumor growth in this subtype.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- PPARA drives DNL/FAO programs in HCC; Wnt/β-catenin-driven ([APC](../genes/APC.md)/[CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md)-mutant) HCC is FAO-addicted via PPARα/CPT1A axis [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) — PPARα-driven FAO is a dependency in Wnt/β-catenin-mutant HCC; [etomoxir](../drugs/etomoxir.md) (CPT1 inhibitor) showed efficacy in these models [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- PPARα FAO dependency is specific to APC/CTNNB1-mutant HCC; p53-mutant and DEN+HFD HCC require FAO suppression (opposing role) [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- PPARα/CPT1A pathway is a therapeutic target in Wnt-mutant HCC; etomoxir targets [CPT1A](../genes/CPT1A.md) downstream of PPARα [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Open questions

- Predictive biomarkers for FAO-targeting agents (PPARα pathway) in HCC are lacking; the etiology-dependent role (Wnt-mutant vs. p53-mutant) complicates patient selection.
