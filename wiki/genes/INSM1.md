# INSM1

## Overview

INSM1 (Insulinoma-Associated Protein 1) is a zinc-finger transcription factor and well-established neuroendocrine (NE) differentiation marker. In cancer genomics, INSM1 is widely used as an immunohistochemical and RNA-level marker of neuroendocrine identity, particularly in small cell lung cancer ([SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md)), where its expression distinguishes NE subtypes (SCLC-A and SCLC-N) from the non-NE SCLC-P subtype driven by [POU2F3](../genes/POU2F3.md).

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- INSM1 NE marker expression is high in SCLC-A and SCLC-N PDX subtypes and low in SCLC-P PDX models; in the MSK SCLC PDX/CDX cohort (n=46 models from 33 patients), INSM1 low expression in POU2F3-driven (SCLC-P) models was used as a defining characteristic to distinguish non-NE from NE subtypes [PMID:35440124](../papers/35440124.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md):** INSM1 protein and transcript expression discriminates NE subtypes (SCLC-A, SCLC-N) from the non-NE SCLC-P subtype; low INSM1 was confirmed in all 10 POU2F3-positive PDX models in the MSK SCLC PDX/CDX resource [PMID:35440124](../papers/35440124.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Low INSM1 and [CHGA](../genes/CHGA.md) (chromogranin A) co-occur with high POU2F3 expression in SCLC-P models; high INSM1 correlates with [ASCL1](../genes/ASCL1.md) and [NEUROD1](../genes/NEUROD1.md) subtype-defining markers in NE SCLC [PMID:35440124](../papers/35440124.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- The INSM1-low, POU2F3-high SCLC-P subtype represents the first PDX resource for testing PARP inhibitor and other subtype-targeted therapeutic hypotheses in non-NE SCLC [PMID:35440124](../papers/35440124.md).

## Open questions

- Whether INSM1 [immunohistochemistry](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md) alone is sufficient to distinguish SCLC-P from SCLC-A/N in clinical diagnostic settings, given the overlap with other NE markers.
