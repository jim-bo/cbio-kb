Clinical sequencing of soft tissue and bone sarcomas delineates diverse genomic landscapes and potential therapeutic targets

TL;DR

Nacev et al. used MSK-IMPACT targeted sequencing to prospectively profile 2,138 bone and soft tissue sarcomas across 45 OncoTree-mapped pathological entities from a single institution (MSK), making it the largest comparative sarcoma genomic study to date (PMID:35705560). The most common alterations were in cell cycle / TP53, receptor tyrosine kinase / PI3K / RAS signaling, and epigenetic regulators. Subtype-specific findings included TERT amplification in intimal sarcoma, SWI/SNF complex alterations in uterine adenosarcoma, and recurrent KDR/FLT4/EPHA5 mutations in angiosarcoma. Tumor mutational burden was low overall but heterogeneous, and unsupervised clustering revealed that histologically distinct sarcomas can share dominant genetic drivers, motivating genotype-based clinical trial design.

Cohort & data

  • 2,138 bone and soft tissue sarcoma samples from distinct patients, each with paired normal DNA, profiled by MSK-IMPACT (sarcoma_mskcc_2022) (PMID:35705560).
  • Median patient age 54 (range <1 to >90); 1,098 (51.4%) female; 790 (36.9%) metastatic site biopsies; 45 distinct OncoTree-mapped pathological entities (PMID:35705560).
  • 22 subtypes had n≥20 samples and formed the core analysis set. Most common: GIST (n=395, 18.5%), DDLS (n=167), ULMS (n=165), UPS (n=145), OS (n=129), LMS (n=125), ANGS (n=101), ES (n=99) (PMID:35705560).
  • Three MSK-IMPACT panel versions used: 341 genes (n=209, 9.8%), IMPACT410 (n=573, 26.8%), and IMPACT468 (n=1,356, 63.4%); ≥500× median coverage on Illumina HiSeq 2500 (PMID:35705560).
  • Annotation pipeline: OncoKB for oncogenicity/actionability, FACETS for allele-specific copy number / WGD calls, MuSiC and MutSigCV 1.4 for recurrent-mutation discovery (q<0.1), MSIsensor ≥10 threshold for MSI-high, and COSMIC v3 mutational signature extraction for samples with ≥15 SNVs (PMID:35705560).
  • Companion interactive dataset on cBioPortal: sarcoma_mskcc_2022 (PMID:35705560).

Key findings

  • TMB is low but heterogeneous. Median TMB was highest in ANGS (3.0 mut/Mb), UPS (2.6), and ULMS (2.6), lowest in WDLS, EPIS, and RCS-other (0.9). TMB ≥5 mut/Mb in 25% of ANGS, 15% of ULMS and UPS, 13% of ERMS; TMB ≥10 mut/Mb in 7.6% of ANGS and 6.7% of UPS (PMID:35705560).
  • MSI is rare. Only 5/1,893 evaluable samples were MSI-high by MSIsensor (one UPS, one LMS, three ULMS); 4 confirmed by PCR (PMID:35705560).
  • WGD frequency varies and predicts outcome. Whole-genome doubling occurred in ~50% of OS, UPS, ERMS, and MPNST. WGD was associated with worse overall survival in metastatic disease (p=0.042) and specifically in metastatic UPS (p=0.022), but not in primary cases (p=0.391) or in MFS (p=0.78) (PMID:35705560).
  • Intimal sarcoma is TERT-amplified. Oncogenic TERT amplifications were identified in 44% (8/18) of INTS, distinct from the classical MDM2/CDK4 amplicon. TERT promoter mutations occurred in 79% (38/48) of MRLS, 46% (24/52) of SFT, 35% (5/14) of DDCHS, and 16% (27/167) of DDLS, almost entirely as amplifications (n=24) (PMID:35705560).
  • Uterine adenosarcoma is SWI/SNF–driven. 43% of UAS (n=14) carried oncogenic alterations in SWI/SNF components, predominantly ARID1A and PBRM1; 36% had histone-modifying gene alterations (PMID:35705560).
  • Angiosarcoma has recurrent RTK mutations. KDR (VEGFR2) altered in 19%, FLT4 (VEGFR3) in 9%, and EPHA5 in 9% of ANGS (n=101); EPHA5 and FLT4 hits were all VUS but clustered in kinase/C-terminal domains. PIK3CA oncogenic alterations in 6% (lower than the previously reported 21%) (PMID:35705560).
  • GIST harbors a longer-tail driver list. Beyond KIT, PDGFRA, and SDHA, recurrent mutations in SETD2 (4%), MAX (4%), and MGA (3%) were identified in GIST (PMID:35705560).
  • DDLS vs WDLS amplification differences. Beyond shared CDK4/MDM2 amplification, DDLS had higher rates of oncogene amplification than WDLS: GLI1 25.3% vs 8.5%, TERT 14.4% vs 6.3%, and JUN 13.8% vs 0%; GLI1 and JUN amplifications were mutually exclusive. Epigenetic alterations were also enriched in DDLS (25%) over WDLS (8%) (PMID:35705560).
  • Ewing sarcoma carries STAG2. 10% of ES (n=99) had STAG2 mutations co-occurring with TP53 alterations (PMID:35705560).
  • ULMS biology. MED12 altered in 16% of ULMS, most frequently missense mutations at glycine 44. BRCA2 or RAD51B each mutated in 7% of ULMS; nearly half (41% BRCA2, 47% RAD51B) of corpus-wide alterations in those two genes occurred in uterine sarcomas. PTEN loss in 21% of ULMS, ATRX alterations in ~33% (PMID:35705560).
  • Pathway alterations. Cell cycle and TP53 pathways altered in ≥50% of samples in 8 of 22 core subtypes; PI3K pathway altered in MRLS (41%), PECOMA (40%), ULMS (30%), PLLS (22%), UPS (20%), LMS (20%); epigenetic pathway altered in MPNST (64%), ULMS (49%), PLLS (45%), CHS (43%), UPS (42%), MFS (36%), OS (32%) (PMID:35705560).
  • Unsupervised clustering finds 17 genomic clusters that cross histology boundaries. WDLS, DDLS, and DSRCT had the lowest entropy (homogeneous), while ULMS, UPS, and OS had high entropy (multiple distinct genetic variants per histotype) (PMID:35705560).

Genes & alterations

  • TP53: altered across multiple subtypes; near-universal pathway hit in DDLS/WDLS via CDK4/MDM2 co-amplification (214/215, 99%). Co-occurs with STAG2 in ES and TERT in SFT (PMID:35705560).
  • RB1: significantly altered across multiple subtypes; pathway-level events in WDLS/DDLS (99%) and elsewhere (PMID:35705560).
  • MDM2 / CDK4: co-amplified in >97% of WDLS and DDLS; diagnostic and Level 3A actionable (PMID:35705560).
  • TERT: amplifications dominate in INTS (44%) and DDLS (16%); promoter mutations dominate in MRLS (79%), SFT (46%), DDCHS (35%) (PMID:35705560).
  • GLI1, JUN: mutually exclusive amplifications in DDLS; rationale for Shh-pathway inhibition and Jun/cyclin D1 axis study (PMID:35705560).
  • KIT (72% Level 1), PDGFRA (7%): canonical GIST drivers; mutually exclusive (PMID:35705560).
  • KDR, FLT4, EPHA5: recurrent in ANGS (19/9/9%); FLT4 VUS cluster in kinase/C-terminus (PMID:35705560).
  • MED12: G44 hotspot missense in 16% of ULMS (PMID:35705560).
  • STAG2: mutated in ~10% of ES, co-occurring with TP53 (PMID:35705560).
  • SETD2, MAX, MGA: novel recurrent mutations in GIST at 4/4/3% (PMID:35705560).
  • PTEN: loss in 14% of LMS, 21% of ULMS, 21% of MRLS; 8% of UPS. Most often Level 4 actionable (PMID:35705560).
  • PIK3CA: mutated in 25% of MRLS; 6% in ANGS (PMID:35705560).
  • TSC2, TSC1: loss-of-function in 30% of PECOMA; combined TSC1/2 actionable in 37% (PMID:35705560).
  • BRCA2, RAD51B, CHEK2, ATM, FANCA, RAD51: DDR pathway altered in 9.6% of all samples; highest in ULMS (24%), MPNST (16%), PLLS (13%), PECOMA (13%), ANGS (13%) (PMID:35705560).
  • SMARCB1: loss-of-function deletions/truncations/intragenic fusions in 75% of EPIS; Level 1 deletion in 66% (PMID:35705560).
  • ARID1A, PBRM1: predominant SWI/SNF alterations in 43% of UAS (PMID:35705560).
  • ATRX: loss-of-function in ≥10% of ULMS, PLLS, UPS, MFS, PECOMA, LMS, ANGS; ULMS rate ~1 in 3. Mutations dominate over deletions; intra-/intergenic fusions also captured. Cluster-defining; raises the possibility of ATR-inhibitor synthetic lethality (NCT03718091, NCT05071209, NCT04807816) (PMID:35705560).
  • BCOR, DICER1: mutually exclusive in ERMS; BCOR oncogenic in 16%, DICER1 oncogenic in 12% (PMID:35705560).
  • NCOR1: amplified in 19% of ULMS, 10% of LMS, 12% of OS via 17p12-p11.2 cytoband co-amplification with FLCN, MAP2K4, AURKB (PMID:35705560).
  • KMT2D (MLL4): altered in 16% of MFS, more frequently than in the closely related UPS (6%) (PMID:35705560).
  • NF1: Level 4 deletions in 32% of MPNST, 14% of UPS, 14% of ERMS, 14% of PLLS (PMID:35705560).
  • CDKN2A: deletions up to 48% in MPNST; broadly distributed actionable hits (PMID:35705560).
  • IDH1, IDH2: 27% of CHS carry actionable IDH1/2 alterations (PMID:35705560).

Clinical implications

  • Level 1 (FDA biomarker → FDA drug): KIT/PDGFRA mutations in GIST (imatinib); SMARCB1 deletion in 66% of EPIS (tazemetostat) (PMID:35705560).
  • Level 3A: CDK4/MDM2 amplifications in WDLS and DDLS (>90%) — actionable via CDK4 inhibitors (PMID:35705560).
  • Level 3B / 4 highlights: 37% combined Level-3B TSC1/2 deletions in PECOMA; 27% IDH1/2 in CHS; collective 31% targetable PI3K-pathway hits in MRLS (PIK3CA, AKT1, MTOR, TSC1); 21% PTEN deletions in MRLS as Level 4; somatic NF1 deletions in MPNST (32%), UPS (14%), ERMS (14%), PLLS (14%); CDKN2A deletions up to 48% in MPNST (PMID:35705560).
  • EZH2 inhibition rationale for uterine adenosarcoma. Given SWI/SNF–EZH2 synthetic-lethality precedent in EPIS, the 43% SWI/SNF–altered rate in UAS motivates exploration of tazemetostat (PMID:35705560).
  • ATR inhibitor basket trials. ATRX loss-of-function clusters define candidate populations for ATR inhibition; the authors cite NCT03718091, NCT05071209, NCT04807816 as ongoing investigations (PMID:35705560).
  • Immune checkpoint biomarker caveats. TMB and MSI distributions are low in sarcoma; PTEN loss in ULMS has been associated with non-response to anti-PD-1 (per cited prior work). The authors argue the 10 mut/Mb pan-cancer TMB threshold may not transfer to mesenchymal tumors and call for subtype-specific correlative analysis on existing immunotherapy trials (PMID:35705560).
  • Prognostic candidate biomarker. WGD as an adverse prognostic factor in metastatic UPS (p=0.022) and metastatic sarcoma overall (p=0.042) warrants prospective validation (PMID:35705560).
  • Genotype-based trial design. High intra-subtype entropy (notably ULMS, UPS, OS) and shared driver clusters across histologies (e.g., TERT-driven MRLS+SFT) support genotype-defined cohorts over histology-defined cohorts in future sarcoma trials (PMID:35705560).

Limitations & open questions

  • Targeted panel limitations. MSK-IMPACT misses novel rearrangements, unknown drivers, focal CNAs outside captured regions, and certain mutational-signature features (e.g., HRD); whole-genome sequencing is needed for full characterization. Cytoband-level co-amplifications (e.g., MYOD-locus in LMS) cannot be assessed when adjacent genes (e.g., ALOX12B) are off-panel (PMID:35705560).
  • WGD-call discordance. WGD estimates from MSK-IMPACT are concordant with WES in general but may differ from other analytical methods/platforms (PMID:35705560).
  • Sample-set heterogeneity. Primary and metastatic samples were combined for most analyses; the authors note increased TMB and FGA in metastatic GIST and fewer JUN amplifications in metastatic UPS vs primary. ANGS cohort has low tumor purity (<50% in majority of cases), which may underestimate event rates (PMID:35705560).
  • Limited racial diversity in the single-institution cohort (PMID:35705560).
  • Functional follow-up needed. ATRX has multiple roles (H3.3 chaperone, ALT, genome integrity); patient-derived models are required to dissect which contribute to sarcomagenesis. Many ANGS FLT4/EPHA5 hits are VUS that may yet be functionally relevant (PMID:35705560).
  • TMB threshold uncertainty in sarcoma. Whether 10 mut/Mb is the right cutoff for immunotherapy benefit in mesenchymal neoplasms remains open (PMID:35705560).
