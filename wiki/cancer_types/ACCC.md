# Acinic Cell Carcinoma (ACCC)

## Overview

Acinic Cell Carcinoma is a low- to intermediate-grade salivary gland malignancy sitting under [SACA](../cancer_types/SACA.md) in OncoTree. A clinically important finding is that a subset of tumors histologically diagnosed as acinic cell carcinoma harbor ETV6-NTRK3 fusions and are better classified as Mammary Analogue Secretory Carcinoma ([MASC](../cancer_types/MASC.md)), with implications for TRK-inhibitor sensitivity.

## Cohorts in the corpus

- [hnc_mskcc_2016](../datasets/hnc_mskcc_2016.md) — included as part of the salivary carcinoma cases in 151 recurrent/metastatic head and neck tumors profiled by MSK-IMPACT 410-gene panel [PMID:27442865](../papers/27442865.md).

## Recurrent alterations

- **[ETV6](../genes/ETV6.md)-[NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md) fusion** — identified in 2 tumors initially diagnosed as acinic cell carcinoma by MSK-IMPACT NGS; these were reclassified as [MASC](../cancer_types/MASC.md) (Mammary Analogue Secretory Carcinoma); both patients subsequently enrolled on a TRK inhibitor basket study with major or near-complete responses [PMID:27442865](../papers/27442865.md).

## Subtypes

- ACCC overlaps histologically with [MASC](../cancer_types/MASC.md); ETV6-NTRK3 fusion testing is warranted in all cases to enable correct classification and TRK-inhibitor access [PMID:27442865](../papers/27442865.md).

## Therapeutic landscape

- **ETV6-NTRK3 fusions warrant TRK inhibitor evaluation** — the reclassification of ETV6-NTRK3-positive acinic cell carcinomas as MASC enabled enrollment on TRK inhibitor basket trials with dramatic responses [PMID:27442865](../papers/27442865.md).

## Sources

- [PMID:27442865](../papers/27442865.md) — Morris et al. 2017 (JAMA Oncol). ETV6-NTRK3 fusions identified in 2 ACCC cases, reclassified as MASC; dramatic TRK inhibitor responses on basket trial.
