# ZFP36L1

## Overview

ZFP36L1 is a [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) negative regulator disrupted in U-CLL via a recurrent chr14 deletion.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- ZFP36L1 is disrupted (together with [DICER1](../genes/DICER1.md) and [TRAF3](../genes/TRAF3.md)) by a recurrent 37-Mb chr14 deletion in U-CLL (4/87 WGS, 4.6%) arising via class-switch recombination [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- ZFP36L1 is a [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) negative regulator, and its loss connects the structural-variant landscape of U-CLL to [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) pathway activation [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [CLLSLL](../cancer_types/CLLSLL.md) — disrupted by the recurrent U-CLL chr14 deletion [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-disrupted with [DICER1](../genes/DICER1.md) and [TRAF3](../genes/TRAF3.md) on the same chr14 deletion [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct targeted therapy reported in the corpus; mechanistically links to [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) pathway [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).

## Open questions

- Relative contribution of ZFP36L1 loss vs co-disrupted DICER1/TRAF3 to U-CLL biology is unresolved [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
