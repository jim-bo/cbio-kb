# ATRX

## Overview

ATRX is a SWI/SNF-family chromatin remodeler whose loss enables the alternative lengthening of telomeres (ALT) phenotype and is a defining alteration of IDH-mutant astrocytomas.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- ATRX mutations observed in 77% of 1p19q intact IDH-mutant astrocytomas in the MSK-IMPACT subcohort (n=73) of a low-grade glioma active-surveillance study [PMID:37910594](../papers/37910594.md).
- Inactivating mutations (frameshift, nonsense, splice site) in 7% of sporadic [ANGS](../cancer_types/ANGS.md) vs only 2% of radiation-associated (RT) angiosarcoma (n=44 RT-AS, n=135 sporadic AS); ATRX alterations were depleted in RT-AS [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).
- ATRX mutations in 10% of soft-tissue [LMS](../cancer_types/LMS.md) (STLMS) and 32% of uterine LMS ([ULMS](../cancer_types/ULMS.md)); prognostically significant in both risk models; co-occurrence with [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) mutations (STLMS) or [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations (ULMS) defines high-risk classification (multi-institutional study, n=~450 LMS) [PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md).
- ATRX/DAXX mutations unique to treatment-refractory corticotroph pituitary neuroendocrine tumors (PitNETs), implicated in alternative lengthening of telomeres (ALT); found in aggressive/treatment-refractory cases not in benign PitNETs (n=23 refractory, n=69 benign) [PMID:38758238](../papers/38758238.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [ASTR](../cancer_types/ASTR.md) / [DIFG](../cancer_types/DIFG.md) — canonical co-alteration in IDH-mutant 1p19q intact astrocytoma [PMID:37910594](../papers/37910594.md).
- [ANGS](../cancer_types/ANGS.md) — inactivating ATRX mutations depleted in RT-AS (2%) relative to sporadic AS (7%) in the MSK RT-sarcoma comparative genomic study [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).
- [LMS](../cancer_types/LMS.md) — ATRX mutations present in 10% of STLMS and 32% of ULMS; high-risk co-occurrence with [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) or [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations defines genomic risk stratification models [PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md).
- Pituitary neuroendocrine tumor (corticotroph subtype) — ATRX/DAXX mutations exclusively in treatment-refractory corticotroph PitNETs; linked to ALT mechanism [PMID:38758238](../papers/38758238.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) in 1p19q intact IDH-mutant astrocytomas [PMID:37910594](../papers/37910594.md).
