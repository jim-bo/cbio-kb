# SGK1

## Overview

SGK1 (Serum/Glucocorticoid Regulated Kinase 1) is a serine/threonine kinase regulated by the PI3K/AKT pathway. In lymphomas, SGK1 functions as a tumor suppressor, particularly in germinal center B-cell (GCB) subtypes, where recurrent inactivating mutations have been identified.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Recurrently mutated (nonsense, missense) in diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL) and follicular lymphoma ([FL](../cancer_types/FL.md)); acts as a tumor suppressor in GCB-subtype lymphomas identified by whole-genome/exome sequencing [PMID:21796119](../papers/21796119.md)
- WES of 55 DLBCL tumors identified recurrent SGK1 mutations in ~4% of cases, implicating PI3K/AKT signaling pathway dysregulation in diffuse large B-cell lymphoma [PMID:22343534](../papers/22343534.md)
- Preferentially mutated in GCB DLBCL; mutations associated with favorable survival across DLBCL subtypes [PMID:28985567](../papers/28985567.md)
- BCR/PI3K signaling modifier in C3/C4 GCB-DLBCL; AID-target of aberrant somatic hypermutation (cAID signature); among 98 CCGs identified in 304 primary DLBCLs by MutSigCV [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)
- SGK1 kinase activity was decreased in [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) vs. normal liver by KSEA in a Chinese [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) proteogenomic cohort (n=159); SGK1 is one of five kinases with globally reduced activity in HCC [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL): inactivating mutations; GCB-restricted pattern [PMID:21796119](../papers/21796119.md)
- Follicular lymphoma ([FL](../cancer_types/FL.md)): inactivating mutations [PMID:21796119](../papers/21796119.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- GCB-restricted mutation pattern suggests co-occurrence with other GCB-enriched alterations such as [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) Y641 and [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) mutations [PMID:21796119](../papers/21796119.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- SGK1 loss may affect PI3K pathway signaling; potential implications for PI3K inhibitor sensitivity in GCB-DLBCL [PMID:21796119](../papers/21796119.md)

## Open questions

- Mechanism by which SGK1 loss promotes lymphomagenesis in GCB B-cells remains to be elucidated.
