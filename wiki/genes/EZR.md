# EZR

## Overview

EZR (Ezrin) encodes a membrane-cytoskeleton linker protein belonging to the ERM (Ezrin-Radixin-Moesin) family. It plays roles in cell morphology, motility, and signal transduction. Somatic EZR alterations have been observed in various cancers, and EZR has been reported as a fusion partner in rare rearrangements.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Case-specific private mutation defining an individual IDC-NST subclone in Case 2 multifocal BRCA1-mutant synchronous DCIS/IDC-NST, identified by WES (n=25 patients, MSKCC) [PMID:32220886](../papers/32220886.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) — identified as a subclonal IDC-NST-private mutation in paired DCIS/IDC-NST profiling [PMID:32220886](../papers/32220886.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Identified in a BRCA1-mutant (E23Vfs*17 germline) multifocal case (Case 2), suggesting co-occurrence with [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) loss in the invasive compartment [PMID:32220886](../papers/32220886.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic data in the corpus.

## Open questions

- Whether EZR mutation is a driver or passenger event in the IDC-NST subclone remains unclear; only a single case in the corpus.
