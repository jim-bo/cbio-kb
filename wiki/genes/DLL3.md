# DLL3

## Overview

DLL3 is a Notch inhibitory ligand expressed on the surface of neuroendocrine tumor cells. High DLL3 expression is a hallmark of [SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md) and related neuroendocrine malignancies, making it a leading therapeutic target for antibody-drug conjugates and bispecific T-cell engagers.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- High IHC expression in all 9 evaluable aSCLC samples (mean H-score 278); aSCLC cohort n=20 (MSKCC); this positions aSCLC as a candidate for DLL3-targeted therapy [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- aSCLC (atypical SCLC) — universally high DLL3 IHC expression supports consideration for DLL3-targeted agents [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-expressed with [SEZ6](../genes/SEZ6.md) at high levels in aSCLC [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Universal DLL3 expression in aSCLC provides rationale for emerging DLL3-targeted agents (e.g., rovalpituzumab tesirine, DLL3 bispecific T-cell engagers) [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md).

## Open questions

- Whether DLL3 expression in aSCLC predicts response to DLL3-targeted therapy to the same degree as in conventional SCLC is not yet established.
