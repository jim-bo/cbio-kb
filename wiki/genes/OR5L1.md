# OR5L1

## Overview

OR5L1 is an olfactory receptor gene identified as one of nine significantly mutated genes (FDR ≤ 0.10) in castration-resistant prostate cancer (CRPC) by whole-exome sequencing. Olfactory receptor genes are frequently mutated in cancers due to their large family size, and their functional significance as cancer drivers remains unclear; OR5L1 has not been functionally validated as a bona fide cancer driver.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Identified as one of nine significantly mutated genes (FDR ≤ 0.10) in prostate cancer WES (Michigan cohort, 112 tumors including 50 CRPCs); functional significance uncharacterised [PMID:22722839](../papers/22722839.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md)**: Statistical significance mutation in CRPC cohort; not functionally validated as a prostate cancer driver [PMID:22722839](../papers/22722839.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-identified as significantly mutated alongside [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [AR](../genes/AR.md), [ZFHX3](../genes/ZFHX3.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [APC](../genes/APC.md), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), and [CDK12](../genes/CDK12.md) in CRPC [PMID:22722839](../papers/22722839.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No known therapeutic relevance; functional validation has not been performed [PMID:22722839](../papers/22722839.md)

## Open questions

- Whether OR5L1 represents a true cancer driver or a passenger mutation amplified by statistical analysis of a large gene family is unresolved [PMID:22722839](../papers/22722839.md)
