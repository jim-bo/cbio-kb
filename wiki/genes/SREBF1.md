# SREBF1

## Overview

SREBF1 (sterol regulatory element binding transcription factor 1), also known as SREBP1, is the master transcription factor driving de novo lipogenesis (DNL) in hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC). SREBF1 is transcriptionally upregulated by MYC (frequently amplified in HCC) and post-transcriptionally activated by the PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway. Once active, SREBF1 upregulates key DNL enzymes including FASN, SCD, and ACACA, fueling enhanced lipid droplet accumulation in TKI-resistant HCC cells. ITGB4 forms a complex with PD-L1 and EGFR that drives SREBP1c-dependent lipid accumulation. Post-translational modifiers SIRT3 and KAT8 regulate SREBF1 deacetylation and activation.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Master transcription factor of de novo lipogenesis in HCC; transcriptionally driven by MYC and post-transcriptionally activated by PI3K/AKT/mTOR; upregulates FASN, SCD, and ACACA; enhanced DNL and lipid droplets observed in sorafenib-resistant HCC cells; phosphatidic acid generated by DNL activates RTK signaling and confers lenvatinib resistance [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md):** SREBF1 drives DNL reprogramming; activated by MYC amplification (frequent in HCC) and PI3K/AKT/mTOR; associated with sorafenib and lenvatinib resistance [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Transcriptionally driven by [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) (frequently amplified in HCC); targets include [FASN](../genes/FASN.md), [SCD](../genes/SCD.md), and [ACACA](../genes/ACACA.md); ITGB4/PD-L1/EGFR complex drives SREBP1c-dependent lipid accumulation; combined FASN + [SREBF2](../genes/SREBF2.md) loss required to abolish tumor development in Pten-loss + MET-overexpression models [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- FASN inhibition (targeting SREBF1 downstream output) does not downregulate PD-L1 and instead synergizes with anti-PD-L1; DNL inhibition combinations proposed as strategy to overcome TKI resistance in the ~75% of HCC patients with primary or acquired resistance [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Open questions

- Bidirectional relationship between ACLY-driven DNL and PD-L1 expression/ICI response in MAFLD-HCC remains unresolved; FASN loss can paradoxically activate cholesterol biosynthesis as compensation [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)
