# azd8055

## Overview

AZD8055 is an ATP-competitive inhibitor of mTOR (mechanistic target of rapamycin) that blocks both mTORC1 and mTORC2 complexes, inhibiting downstream S6K and 4EBP1 phosphorylation. It has been investigated as a senolytic agent to selectively kill senescent cancer cells.

## Evidence in the corpus

- Selectively inhibited S6K and S6 phosphorylation in KRASG12C-amplified colorectal cancer cells that entered oncogene-induced senescence after [sotorasib](../drugs/sotorasib.md) + [cetuximab](../drugs/cetuximab.md) withdrawal; showed enhanced cytotoxicity in the senescent (drug-off) condition compared with continuous drug exposure (P ≤ 0.0001), establishing mTOR inhibition as a candidate senolytic strategy for KRASG12C-amplified resistant tumors [PMID:36355783](../papers/36355783.md)
- SCBO-6 (bladder organoid with [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md) nonsense mutation) was sensitive to AZD8055 (mTOR inhibitor); SCBO-5 (TSC1-wild-type) was resistant; Western blot confirmed p-S6 downregulation in both lines; additive response when combined with FGFR inhibitor erdafitinib in SCBO-6 [PMID:29625057](../papers/29625057.md)
