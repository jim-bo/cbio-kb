# CCL5

## Overview

CCL5 (RANTES) is a CCR5 ligand chemokine that recruits effector T cells and is a core component of tumor immune infiltration signatures.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- CCL5 is one of the 20 genes in the Immunologic Constant of Rejection (ICR) signature applied to the 348-patient Sidra-LUMC AC-ICAM colon cancer cohort, grouped with the CXCR3/CCR5 chemokine ligands [CXCL9](../genes/CXCL9.md) and [CXCL10](../genes/CXCL10.md) [PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- Colon adenocarcinoma — CCL5 contributes to ICR-based consensus clustering that segregates ICR-high, -medium, and -low tumors with distinct overall survival [PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-expressed with the other ICR Th1/cytotoxic module genes ([IFNG](../genes/IFNG.md), [CD8A](../genes/CD8A.md), [CD8B](../genes/CD8B.md), [CXCL9](../genes/CXCL9.md), [CXCL10](../genes/CXCL10.md), GZMA/B, [PRF1](../genes/PRF1.md)) in AC-ICAM [PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct CCL5-targeting therapy is evaluated; the ICR module as a whole is proposed as a prognostic and potentially predictive biomarker [PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md).

## Open questions

- The paper treats CCL5 as a module component rather than a standalone biomarker; independent contribution is not dissected [PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md).
