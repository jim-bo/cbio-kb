# Giant Cell Tumor of Bone (GCTB)

## Overview

Giant Cell Tumor of Bone is a locally aggressive benign-to-borderline bone tumor arising at the epiphysis of long bones, characterized by multinucleated osteoclast-like giant cells. Most cases harbor H3F3A p.G34W mutations and show high RANKL (TNFSF11) expression — the basis for denosumab therapy. OncoTree classifies it under Bone Cancer.

## Cohorts in the corpus

- [sarcoma_msk_2022](../datasets/sarcoma_msk_2022.md): GCTB was among the 44 sarcoma histologies profiled by FoundationOne Heme in 7,494 sarcomas [PMID:35705558](../papers/35705558.md).

## Recurrent alterations

- In a 7,494-sarcoma cohort, giant cell tumor of bone was among the 44 sarcoma histologies profiled; H3F3A p.G34W is the pathognomonic mutation; 31.7% of all sarcomas harbored ≥1 OncoKB actionable alteration but GCTB has limited conventional genomic complexity [PMID:35705558](../papers/35705558.md).

## Subtypes

- Benign GCTB (90%) vs. malignant GCTB (primary malignant or secondary after denosumab/radiation).

## Therapeutic landscape

- Denosumab (anti-RANKL) is the standard targeted therapy for unresectable/recurrent GCTB; response monitoring by H3F3A mutation burden not yet established clinically.
