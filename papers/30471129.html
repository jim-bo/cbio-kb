Learning-based CBCT correction using alternating random forest based on auto-context model

Authors

Yang Lei

Xiangyang Tang

Kristin Higgins

Jolinta Lin

Jiwoong Jeong

Tian Liu

Doi

10.1002/mp.13295

PMID: 30471129 · DOI: 10.1002/mp.13295 · Journal: Medical Physics (2019)

Scope note: This paper is a medical-physics / image-processing contribution about cone-beam CT (CBCT) image correction for image-guided radiation therapy. It does not describe any cancer-genomics cohort, gene alterations, or cBioPortal-style dataset. It is included in the corpus for completeness but contains no genes, cancer-type codes, cBioPortal studies, or drugs to extract. The “cohort” referenced below is a set of patients imaged for radiotherapy planning, not a genomic cohort.

TL;DR

The authors develop a learning-based pipeline that turns low-quality cone-beam CT (CBCT) images — currently usable only for patient setup — into corrected CBCT (CCBCT) images with image quality approaching planning CT. They combine LASSO + fuzzy c-means anatomical feature selection, an alternating random forest (ARF) trained with a joint information gain over both CBCT features and CT targets, and an auto-context model (ACM) that iteratively refines the regressor. Evaluated by leave-one-out cross-validation on 12 brain and 14 pelvis radiotherapy patients, the method achieves mean absolute error (MAE) of 12.81 ± 2.04 HU on brain data and 19.94 ± 5.44 HU on pelvis data, outperforming both a conventional non-uniformity scatter-correction method and a state-of-the-art dictionary-learning (DL) baseline. The corrected images are proposed to enable CBCT-guided adaptive radiotherapy (PMID:30471129).

Cohort & data

  • 12 patients with brain imaging and 14 patients with pelvis imaging acquired for radiotherapy planning at Emory University (PMID:30471129). No cancer-type metadata, no genomic profiling — this is an imaging-only cohort.
  • Planning CT: Siemens SOMATOM Definition AS at 120 kVp; voxel spacing 0.586 × 0.586 × 0.6 mm (brain) and 0.98 × 0.98 × 2.0 mm (pelvis).
  • CBCT: Varian On-Board Imager at 125 kVp; voxel spacing 1.17 × 1.17 × 2.5 mm (brain) and 0.908 × 0.908 × 2.0 mm (pelvis). CBCT was acquired at the first fraction of treatment to maximize alignment with the planning CT (PMID:30471129).
  • Evaluation: Leave-one-out cross-validation against the paired planning CT; metrics MAE, PSNR, NCC, and spatial non-uniformity (SNU) computed over selected ROIs at the image center and periphery. Dose-volume histogram (DVH) metrics computed by replaying the original treatment plan on uncorrected and corrected CBCT images.
  • Assay / panel: Not applicable. Method tags: cone-beam-ct-igrt, random-forest-classifier.

Key findings

  • Brain CCBCT: MAE 12.81 ± 2.04 HU, PSNR 40.22 ± 3.70 dB, NCC 0.98 ± 0.02 (PMID:30471129).
  • Pelvis CCBCT: MAE 19.94 ± 5.44 HU, PSNR 31.31 ± 2.85 dB, NCC 0.95 ± 0.01 (PMID:30471129).
  • Component ablation (brain): plain RF MAE = 18.03 HU; RF + feature selection (RF+FS) MAE = 16.78 HU; RF+FS+JIG MAE = 15.66 HU; ARF+FS+JIG MAE = 14.74 HU; ARF+ACM+FS+JIG (final) MAE = 12.81 HU. JIG and ARF each improve MAE with P < 0.001 versus the prior step; PSNR P < 0.01 (PMID:30471129).
  • vs. dictionary-learning baseline (brain): ARF+ACM+FS+JIG MAE 12.81 vs DL 20.89 (P < 0.001); PSNR 40.22 vs 38.06 dB (P < 0.001); NCC 0.987 vs 0.982 (P < 0.005) (PMID:30471129).
  • vs. conventional non-uniformity scatter correction (pelvis phantom + patient): On pelvis patient data, MAE 12.32 (proposed) vs 38.58 HU (conventional); PSNR 39.77 vs 34.74 dB; NCC 0.973 vs 0.938; SNU 7.56% vs 15.77% (PMID:30471129).
  • Spatial non-uniformity reduction: Brain CBCT SNU reduced from 6.96 ± 4.93% to 2.07 ± 3.36%; pelvic CBCT SNU reduced from 27.24 ± 14.09% to 15.61 ± 7.77% with the proposed correction (PMID:30471129).
  • Dosimetry: On pelvic plans, replaying the original treatment plan on CCBCT instead of uncorrected CBCT reduces the mean dose error for PTV, bladder, and rectum from roughly 1.0% to 0.3% (PMID:30471129).
  • Hyperparameters (default): 20 trees, max tree depth 20 (parameter sweep found 22–24 trees to be the practical optimum), max 5 training samples per leaf, smoothing parameter k = 0.05, balancing parameter c = 0.05, LASSO regularization l = 0.5 (PMID:30471129).

Genes & alterations

  • None. The paper does not study any genes or genetic alterations.

Clinical implications

  • The authors propose that CCBCT — with HU values restored close to planning-CT quality — can be used for CBCT-guided adaptive radiotherapy: online dose recalculation, plan adaptation between fractions, and online tumor delineation. The DVH analysis on pelvic radiotherapy plans (PTV/bladder/rectum mean dose error 1.0% → 0.3%) is offered as proof-of-concept that the corrected images are dosimetrically usable, not just visually improved (PMID:30471129).
  • No biomarker, prognosis, or therapy-selection claim is made.

Limitations & open questions

  • Small cohort: 12 brain and 14 pelvis patients; the authors explicitly note that more patients are needed to evaluate robustness (PMID:30471129).
  • Compute cost: The joint-information-gain ARF splitting procedure is more complex than conventional binary splitting and takes more computation time per training iteration (PMID:30471129).
  • Site-specific training: Models are trained per anatomical site (separate brain and pelvis models). Generalization to other sites (head-and-neck, thorax, abdomen) is not evaluated.
  • Vendor lock-in: Training pairs come from one CT scanner (Siemens SOMATOM Definition AS) and one CBCT system (Varian On-Board Imager); transferability to other vendors / kVp settings is untested.
  • Out-of-scope for cbio-kb cross-paper synthesis: No genes, OncoTree codes, or cBioPortal studies are reported, so this paper cannot participate in the wiki’s gene / cancer-type / dataset cross-links. Suggested triage: keep in corpus as a methodology reference for radiation-oncology imaging, or exclude from future ingest passes if the corpus scope is narrowed to genomics-only.
