**PMID:** 31068700 · **DOI:** 10.1038/s41586-019-1186-3 · **Journal:** Nature (2019)

## TL;DR

Ghandi et al. expand the Cancer Cell Line Encyclopedia (CCLE) with a next-generation multi-omic
characterization of 1,072 cancer cell lines, layering whole-exome (326 lines), whole-genome
(329 lines), deep RNA-seq (1,019 lines), reduced-representation bisulfite methylation (843
lines), histone H3 mass-spec profiling (897 lines), microRNA profiling (954 lines) and RPPA
protein/phospho-protein quantification (899 lines) on top of the original genetic and
expression backbone. Integrating these data with Project Achilles/DRIVE [shRNA](../methods/shrna-rnai-screen.md)
and CRISPR–Cas9 dependency screens reveals new paralogue synthetic lethalities
([RPP25](../genes/RPP25.md)/[RPP25L](../genes/RPP25L.md), [LDHA](../genes/LDHA.md)/[LDHB](../genes/LDHB.md)),
identifies [MDM4](../genes/MDM4.md) exon-6 inclusion (regulated by [RPL22](../genes/RPL22.md)/[RPL22L1](../genes/RPL22L1.md))
as a predictor of sensitivity to [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) inhibitors such as [nutlin-3a](../drugs/nutlin-3a.md),
and nominates phospho-SHP2 (pY542) as a marker of [ponatinib](../drugs/ponatinib.md) sensitivity
in [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md) and [CML](../cancer_types/CML.md).

## Cohort & data

- **1,072 human cancer cell lines** spanning multiple lineages and ethnicities
  ([MIXED](../cancer_types/MIXED.md) pan-cancer cohort, with disease-specific findings in
  [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md), [CML](../cancer_types/CML.md), and [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md)
  melanoma).
- Profiling matrices: [whole-exome seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) (326), [whole-genome seq](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) (329),
  deep [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) (1,019), RainDance targeted sequencing (657, plus 950 in total per Methods),
  Sanger GDSC WES (1,001; 667 overlapping), [RRBS](../methods/rrbs.md) promoter methylation
  (843; 17,182 promoter regions covered), Nanostring [miRNA profiling](../methods/mirna-seq.md)
  of 734 miRNAs (954 lines), mass-spec global chromatin profiling on 42 H3 peptide marks
  (897 lines), and [RPPA](../methods/rppa.md) for 213 antibodies (899 lines).
- Dataset slug: [ccle_broad_2019](../datasets/ccle_broad_2019.md) (this study's release);
  related cell-line resource [cellline_ccle_broad](../datasets/cellline_ccle_broad.md).
- Functional perturbation data integrated from Project Achilles and DRIVE
  ([shRNA / sgRNA](../methods/shrna-rnai-screen.md) dependency screens).
- Companion metabolomic dataset of 225 metabolites across 928 lines reported in parallel work.

## Key findings

- **Cell-line concordance and drift.** Comparison of germline variant calls between CCLE and
  Sanger GDSC for 667 overlapping lines showed high concordance (Pearson r = 0.95 for allelic
  fractions); 3 of 667 (0.4%) overlapping lines had mismatching germline calls suggestive of
  mislabelling. Cancer hotspot somatic variants correlated at r = 0.92, but non-hotspot somatic
  variants only at r = 0.8; 3–10% of lines (at cut-offs 0.60 or 0.75) showed substantial
  somatic-variant differences indicative of genetic drift.
- **[TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter mutations** detected in 16.7% (84/503) of cell lines surveyed by
  [WGS](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) and targeted sequencing — the most common non-coding
  somatic mutation in the panel.
- **Mismatch repair / MSI.** Among 65 inferred-MSI lines, 17 had [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md)
  hypermethylation and 38 had genomic alterations in [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md) or
  [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md). [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md) protein loss tracks with truncating [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md) mutations,
  consistent with MSH2–MSH6 heterodimer co-stability. COSMIC signatures 6, 21, 26, 15 (MSI)
  and 5, 1 (clock-like) correlated with higher genetic drift in cell lines.
- **Fusion-driven dependencies.** [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md)–[CCDC170](../genes/CCDC170.md) and [AFF1](../genes/AFF1.md)–[KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) fusions identified as drivers
  conferring dependence on [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) and [AFF1](../genes/AFF1.md), respectively
  (cross-checked against RNAi loss-of-function data).
- **Lineage methylation → dependency.** Promoter hypomethylation of lineage transcription
  factors [SOX10](../genes/SOX10.md), [PAX8](../genes/PAX8.md), [HNF1B](../genes/HNF1B.md),
  and [HNF4A](../genes/HNF4A.md) correlates with selective dependence on those factors; [SOX10](../genes/SOX10.md)
  hypomethylation and expression are restricted to melanoma lines and predict SOX10
  knockdown sensitivity.
- **Paralogue synthetic lethalities driven by methylation.**
  - [RPP25](../genes/RPP25.md) promoter methylation silences [RPP25](../genes/RPP25.md) expression in bladder,
    ovary, endometrium, and glioma lineages and creates dependence on the paralogue
    [RPP25L](../genes/RPP25L.md); RPP25 silencing also predicts sensitivity to
    [POP7](../genes/POP7.md) knockout (both components of RNase P / RNase MRP complexes).
  - [LDHB](../genes/LDHB.md) promoter methylation marks vulnerability to
    [LDHA](../genes/LDHA.md) knockout, and conversely [LDHA](../genes/LDHA.md) methylation marks
    [LDHB](../genes/LDHB.md) dependence — a reciprocal paralogue synthetic-lethal pair. The same methylation
    patterns are observed in primary tumours (TCGA).
- **[VHL](../genes/VHL.md) inactivation by methylation.** Three renal clear cell lines lack
  [VHL](../genes/VHL.md) mutation/deletion of both alleles but instead carry one VHL deletion and one methylated
  VHL allele, extending the "VHL-null" genotype in renal clear cell.
- **Histone-modification clusters.** Mass-spec H3 profiling separated lines enriched for
  [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) and [NSD2](../genes/NSD2.md) mutations (as previously reported)
  and revealed a novel cluster with elevated H3K18ac/H3K27ac enriched for truncating
  [EP300](../genes/EP300.md) and [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md) mutations downstream of the
  HAT domain — proposed as the first cancer-associated gain-of-function alterations of p300/CBP.
- **[MDM4](../genes/MDM4.md) splicing predicts MDM2-inhibitor response.** Among genes whose
  splicing correlates with dependence, [MDM4](../genes/MDM4.md) dependency tracked only with the MDM4-FL isoform
  (exon 6 retained), not total MDM4 mRNA. In wild-type [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) lines, low MDM4-FL expression
  abrogated MDM4 dependence, and [nutlin-3a](../drugs/nutlin-3a.md) (an [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md)
  inhibitor) was the small molecule most strongly correlated with MDM4 exon-6 inclusion.
  Exon-6 quantitation outperformed total MDM4 mRNA for predicting response.
- **[RPL22](../genes/RPL22.md) loss → [RPL22L1](../genes/RPL22L1.md) → MDM4 splicing.**
  [RPL22L1](../genes/RPL22L1.md) was the top correlate of MDM4 exon-6 inclusion. [RPL22](../genes/RPL22.md) K15fs frameshift mutations
  (common in MSI tumours) were found in 68% (67/99) of inferred-MSI CCLE lines; RPL22
  loss-of-function or deletion was associated with higher RPL22L1 expression and increased
  MDM4 exon-6 inclusion in both CCLE and TCGA. High RPL22L1 expression marks RPL22L1
  dependence — extending paralogue synthetic lethality logic to ribosomal proteins.
- **microRNA / lineage signalling.** mir-215 expression correlated with
  [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) (β-catenin) dependence, especially in stomach and colon
  lineage lines; mir-215 also correlated with WNT-pathway and gastric-cancer gene sets in
  TCGA.
- **Phospho-SHP2 as a [ponatinib](../drugs/ponatinib.md) biomarker.** RPPA dependence
  modelling found pSHP2 (Y542) was a stronger predictor of [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md)
  (SHP2) dependence than [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) mRNA. Among tyrosine-kinase inhibitors, [ponatinib](../drugs/ponatinib.md) was the
  drug whose sensitivity prediction was most improved by adding RPPA data, and SHP2.pY542
  was the top RPPA predictor. In a validation set of [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md) lines
  plus [CML](../cancer_types/CML.md) [BCR](../genes/BCR.md)–ABL positive controls, 4/5 previously untested
  AML lines (CTV1, NKM1, EOL1, MonoMAC1) with high pSHP2 were ponatinib-sensitive; HEL9217
  was the lone exception. In 7/9 ponatinib-sensitive AML lines the authors detected
  alterations in [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md), [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md),
  [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md), or [KIT](../genes/KIT.md). In vivo, AML primagrafts with
  high pSHP2 (CBAM-87679, NVAM-61786 — carrying BCR–ABL and FLT3-ITD respectively) showed
  extended survival on ponatinib relative to a low-pSHP2 primagraft (DFAM-68555).

## Genes & alterations

- [SOX10](../genes/SOX10.md), [PAX8](../genes/PAX8.md), [HNF1B](../genes/HNF1B.md),
  [HNF4A](../genes/HNF4A.md) — promoter hypomethylation drives lineage-restricted dependence.
- [RPP25](../genes/RPP25.md) (methylation/silencing) → [RPP25L](../genes/RPP25L.md) and
  [POP7](../genes/POP7.md) dependence.
- [LDHA](../genes/LDHA.md) ↔ [LDHB](../genes/LDHB.md) — reciprocal paralogue synthetic
  lethality gated by promoter methylation; replicated in TCGA primary tumours.
- [VHL](../genes/VHL.md) — biallelic inactivation in renal clear cell can include one
  deleted + one methylated allele (3 cell lines identified).
- [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md), [NSD2](../genes/NSD2.md) — known histone-mark cluster drivers.
- [EP300](../genes/EP300.md), [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md) — truncating mutations distal to
  the HAT domain define a new H3K18/H3K27 hyperacetylation cluster; nominated as
  gain-of-function.
- [MDM4](../genes/MDM4.md) exon 6 inclusion (MDM4-FL) — predictive biomarker for MDM4
  dependence and for [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) inhibitor [nutlin-3a](../drugs/nutlin-3a.md)
  sensitivity in [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) wild-type lines.
- [RPL22](../genes/RPL22.md) K15fs frameshift (68% of MSI lines) → elevated
  [RPL22L1](../genes/RPL22L1.md) → MDM4 exon-6 inclusion; RPL22L1 over-expression itself
  marks RPL22L1 dependence.
- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter mutations — 16.7% of 503 lines (most common non-coding
  somatic alteration in cancer cell lines).
- [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md) / [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md) — truncating MSH2 mutations cause
  concordant MSH6 protein loss; [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md) hypermethylation in 17/65 MSI lines.
- [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md)–[CCDC170](../genes/CCDC170.md) fusion → ESR1 dependence;
  [AFF1](../genes/AFF1.md)–[KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) fusion → AFF1 dependence.
- [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) — dependence correlates with mir-215 expression in
  stomach/colon lineages.
- [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) (SHP2 pY542 phospho-protein) — dependency biomarker; phospho
  level explains sensitivity to [ponatinib](../drugs/ponatinib.md) in [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md)
  and [CML](../cancer_types/CML.md), including lines with [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md),
  [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md), [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md), [KIT](../genes/KIT.md), or
  [BCR](../genes/BCR.md)–[ABL1](../genes/ABL1.md) alterations.

## Clinical implications

- **[MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) inhibitor patient selection.** MDM4 exon-6 inclusion and RPL22/RPL22L1 status are
  proposed as biomarkers for clinical response to [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) inhibitors
  (e.g. [nutlin-3a](../drugs/nutlin-3a.md) class) *beyond* [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation
  status alone.
- **Phospho-SHP2 as an RTK-activation screening biomarker.** Because clinical detection of
  the full spectrum of RTK fusions and activating mutations remains difficult, pSHP2 (Y542)
  abundance is proposed as a unified screening biomarker for tumours likely to respond to
  RTK inhibitors such as [ponatinib](../drugs/ponatinib.md), with direct relevance to
  [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md), [CML](../cancer_types/CML.md), thyroid, and rhabdoid
  sarcoma lineages identified in the screen.
- **LDH-pathway therapeutics.** Targeting lactate dehydrogenase in cancer should take into
  account paralogue methylation status ([LDHA](../genes/LDHA.md) vs [LDHB](../genes/LDHB.md))
  to [select](../methods/select.md) sensitive tumours.
- **Cell-line reproducibility.** The 3–10% of CCLE lines with substantial somatic drift and
  the 0.4% mislabelling rate detected against GDSC argue for routine identity / drift QC
  when reusing cell-line panels for therapeutic studies.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Cell-line model only.** All molecular and dependency findings derive from cancer cell
  lines (with limited primagraft / xenograft validation for the ponatinib axis); clinical
  validation in patient tumours is left for future work.
- **Patchy assay coverage across lines.** Not every cell line has every data type
  (e.g., 326 WES, 329 WGS, 843 RRBS, 897 chromatin, 899 RPPA, 954 miRNA), which constrains
  multi-omic joint models.
- **Drift among passaged lines.** 3–10% of lines have substantial somatic-variant
  divergence between CCLE and GDSC passages, complicating experimental reproducibility.
- **MDM4 splicing axis is correlative.** The RPL22 → RPL22L1 → MDM4 exon-6 inclusion model
  is supported by orthogonal CCLE and TCGA evidence but lacks direct mechanistic
  perturbation experiments showing causation in human tumours.
- **[EP300](../genes/EP300.md) / [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md) truncating variants as "gain of function"** is a strong claim grounded in
  cluster-level histone-acetylation enrichment; functional validation of individual
  variants is not provided.
- **Drug-sensitivity coverage.** Drug correlations rely on the Sanger GDSC and CCLE pharma
  panels — many clinically relevant agents (e.g., newer MDM2 inhibitors, SHP2 inhibitors
  beyond SHP099) are not directly tested.
