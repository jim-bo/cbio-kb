# MEN1

## Overview

MEN1 encodes menin, a tumor suppressor frequently mutated in multiple endocrine neoplasia type 1 and in sporadic pancreatic and pulmonary neuroendocrine tumors. Mutations in MEN1 are enriched in carcinoid tumors and related neuroendocrine malignancies.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Carcinoid-associated mutations enriched in aSCLC at 15% frequency (alongside [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) and [EIF1AX](../genes/EIF1AX.md)); aSCLC cohort n=20 (MSKCC) [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- aSCLC (atypical [SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md)) — carcinoid-associated MEN1 mutations in 15% of cases [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) and [EIF1AX](../genes/EIF1AX.md) mutations in aSCLC, consistent with a shared carcinoid heritage [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- None flagged in the corpus.

## Open questions

- Whether MEN1 mutation in aSCLC reflects a carcinoid precursor lineage or is independently acquired is unresolved.
