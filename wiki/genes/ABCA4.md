# ABCA4

## Overview

ABCA4 (ATP Binding Cassette Subfamily A Member 4) encodes a retina-specific ABC transporter, but recurrent 3′UTR mutations in ABCA4 have been identified as non-coding driver events in metastatic cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma (cSCC). The 3′UTR alterations were detected by whole-genome sequencing and are invisible to standard WES or targeted-panel approaches, highlighting the importance of non-coding genomic profiling in UV-driven skin cancers.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Recurrent 3′UTR mutation in 20% (5/25) of metastatic cSCC samples (WGS cohort); called as significant non-coding driver by OncodriveFML (Q-value <0.03); proposed as a candidate non-coding biomarker of metastatic cSCC — a region invisible to WES/targeted-panel platforms [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md):** Non-coding 3′UTR driver in 20% of regional metastatic [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md) (head and neck, lymph node metastases); identified in a WGS cohort (n=25) of the largest WGS study of metastatic cSCC to date [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), and [C9](../genes/C9.md) driver mutations in the same high-TMB UV-mutagenized metastatic cSCC background; contributes alongside [LUM](../genes/LUM.md), [CDHR5](../genes/CDHR5.md), and [COLEC12](../genes/COLEC12.md) to TGF-β/ECM pathway enrichment [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic agent targeting ABCA4 in cSCC; 3′UTR alterations proposed as candidate biomarkers and as potential miRNA-targeting sites (miR-331-5p predicted to target multiple [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md) drivers in the same cohort) [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Open questions

- Whether ABCA4 3′UTR mutations functionally drive oncogenesis (e.g., via disruption of miRNA binding sites) or are passenger events in the context of extreme UV-associated TMB remains unresolved [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)
