**PMID:** 37119971 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.mcpro.2023.100561 · **Journal:** Molecular & Cellular Proteomics (2023)

## TL;DR

A narrative review by Babu and Snyder (Stanford) surveying how integrated multi-omics — combining genomics, epigenomics, transcriptomics, proteomics, metabolomics, lipidomics, microbiome, and wearables — enables longitudinal "deep phenotyping" for precision health. The paper traces the technological evolution of each omics layer, then walks through applications across genetic variation, cardio-metabolic disease, cancer, infectious disease, organ transplantation, pregnancy, and longevity/aging, and closes with a survey of large-scale consortium efforts (TCGA, CPTAC, GTEx, UK Biobank, All of Us) and computational integration methods. It is a position/review piece, not a primary cohort study [PMID:37119971](../papers/37119971.md).

## Cohort & data

- No primary cohort. The review cites third-party studies, including the Stanford integrative Personal Omics Profiling (iPOP) effort (one individual followed at 20 timepoints over 14 months, later expanded to >116 individuals), the Pioneer 100 Wellness Project (108 individuals, expanded toward 100K), and a longitudinal multi-omics T2DM-risk cohort followed up to 8 years that yielded >67 clinically actionable findings.
- Cancer-relevant studies summarized: multi-omics of 256 [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) samples (CNV + DNA methylation + expression, five subgroups with distinct survival, Liu et al.); integrative analysis of [ODG](../cancer_types/ODG.md) (oligodendroglial) tumors yielding three 1p/19q co-deleted glioma subgroups (Kamoun et al.); chromatin accessibility + RNA + protein profiling of human thyroid primary tumors, metastases, and matched normals identifying gene-body enhancers predictive of correlated RNA/protein expression; genome sequencing + proteomics of high-grade serous ovarian carcinoma ([HGSOC](../cancer_types/HGSOC.md), Zhang et al.) linking copy-number variations to proteome and PTM changes; a TCGA-based integrative model for [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md) (melanoma) prognosis reaching mean C-statistic 0.724 (Jiang et al.).
- Public resources catalogued in Table 1 include FANTOM, ENCODE, Roadmap Epigenomics, 1000 Genomes, UK Biobank, GTEx/eGTEx, eQTLGen, MoTrPAC, All of Us, COSMIC, TCGA (~20,000 samples, 32 cancers), CPTAC, TARGET, ADNI, CommonMind, PsychENCODE, AMP-PD.
- Methods discussed: Sanger sequencing, NGS, long-read/T2T sequencing, [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md), bisulfite sequencing / EM-seq / methyl-seq / MeDIP, ChIP-seq / ChIP-chip, [LC-MS/MS proteomics](../methods/lc-ms-ms-proteomics.md) with TMT and iTRAQ labeling, DIA / SWATH-MS, targeted and untargeted metabolomics, fluxomics, lipidomics, single-cell multi-omics, and wearable sensors.

## Key findings

- Single-omics is largely correlative; combining layers (e.g., transcriptomics + proteomics) recovers functional context that genomics alone cannot — for example, GWAS hits often fail to identify causal genes, limiting clinical utility.
- Genome sequencing cost has fallen from billions of dollars (first human genome, 2000) to ~$100 (Ultima Genomics, 2022); proteome cost per sample dropped from $3,250 (2006) to $375 (2021); patient genomes are now sequenceable in as few as 5 hours with long-read tech.
- Longitudinal iPOP profiling captured the transition from healthy to insulin-resistant state following viral infection, including extensive dynamic molecular shifts that prompted lifestyle changes.
- A multi-omics weight-perturbation study showed weight gain activates inflammatory and hypertrophic blood signatures; some signatures persisted after weight loss, indicating long-term physiological imprint, and individual-stable biomolecules emerged as candidate "personalized biomarkers."
- A prediabetes-to-T2DM longitudinal study found insulin-resistant vs insulin-sensitive participants mount distinct immune responses to respiratory viral infection and identified pre-onset T2DM molecular signatures (IL-1 receptor agonist, hs-CRP, dysregulated xenobiotic immune signaling).
- Pioneer 100 inter-omics correlation analysis surfaced putative cardiometabolic biomarkers; the project is being scaled to 100,000 participants.
- In COVID-19, longitudinal multi-omics on PBMCs (Bernardes et al.) showed interferon-activated megakaryocytes and increased erythropoiesis tracked with critical illness, with megakaryocyte/erythroid co-expression modules predictive of fatality; an integrated single-cell multi-omics lung study identified >1000 severe-COVID risk genes across 19 cell types, with risk enriched in NK cells, and the RefMap ML algorithm enabled severe-disease prediction in non-elderly patients.
- For cancer, multi-omics integration (CNV + methylation + expression) stratified [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) into five molecular/survival subgroups; analogous integration of TCGA data improved [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md) prognostic modeling to a mean C-statistic of 0.724.
- Smartwatch-derived physiological signals can detect symptomatic and pre-symptomatic COVID-19, predict atrial fibrillation, and approximate clinical readouts of inflammation, infection, and insulin sensitivity; continuous glucose monitoring uncovered personalized "glucotypes."

## Clinical implications

- Argues that integrative, longitudinal multi-omics combined with wearables and EHR is poised to shift care from reactive treatment of late-stage disease toward prediction, prevention, and individualized intervention.
- Highlights concrete clinical use cases already demonstrated: pre-symptomatic infection detection by wearables, glucotype-guided nutrition, multi-omics subtyping of [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md), [ODG](../cancer_types/ODG.md), thyroid cancer, and [HGSOC](../cancer_types/HGSOC.md) for therapy stratification, and proteomic biomarker panels (e.g., a 27-marker MS-based plasma platform that graded COVID-19 severity).
- Calls out proteogenomics as a maturing pillar of precision oncology that complements DNA-only profiling.

## Limitations & open questions

- As a review, conclusions inherit the limitations of cited studies; there is no new primary data, statistical analysis, or independent validation.
- Authors flag clinical-implementation barriers for multi-omics: heterogeneous and high-dimensional data, the need for robust integration algorithms (matrix factorization, Bayesian, kernel, network, deep learning), sparse EHR records from discrete visits, cost of multi-platform profiling, and the challenge of accurately predicting and prioritizing pathogenic variants among the ~10,000 per genome found by WGS/WES.
- Causality between molecular signatures and outcomes (e.g., persistent post-weight-loss signatures, "personalized biomarkers") remains largely associative.
- Cross-paper synthesis caveat: the cancer subsection is brief and many findings ([HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) five-group classifier, melanoma C-statistic 0.724, thyroid enhancer atlas) are reported without independent cohorts cited here, so any downstream wiki claim should be checked against the underlying primary publications rather than this review alone.
