Genomic and evolutionary classification of lung cancer in never smokers

Authors

Tongwu Zhang

Maria Teresa Landi

David C. Wedge

Jian Sang

Bin Zhu

Nathaniel Aleynik-Pellatt

Nuria Lopez-Bigas

Stephen H. Wong

Yongchao Pan

Hua Chen

Thomas Robert

David Strauss

David A. Garcia

Doi

PMID: 34493867 · DOI: 10.1038/s41588-021-00920-0 · Journal: Nature Genetics (2021)

TL;DR

The Sherlock-Lung study performed high-coverage whole-genome sequencing (mean tumor coverage 85×) on 232 treatment-naïve lung cancers from never smokers (LCINS), the largest WGS LCINS cohort to date and the basis of the cBioPortal study lung_nci_2022. Unsupervised clustering of arm-level somatic copy number alterations (SCNAs) identified three subtypes — piano (49.6%, low SCNA burden, stem-cell-like, enriched for UBA1/KRAS/SETD2 and carcinoid histology), mezzo-forte (30.2%, arm-level amplifications, EGFR-driven), and forte (20.2%, whole-genome doubling). No tobacco mutational signature (SBS4) was detectable, even in 62 cases with reported second-hand smoke exposure. Five independent alterations (TP53 mutation, MDM2 amplification, EGFR mutation, CHEK2 LOH, 22q loss, 15q loss) each ~doubled mortality risk, and survival differed sharply across RTK-RAS pathway genes.

Cohort & data

  • 232 LCINS patients with paired tumor + germline WGS (tumor coverage 70.6–141.5×, mean 85×; normal 26.2–57.2×, mean 31.6×). Of 256 enrolled, 20 failed QC and 4 were excluded by mutational-signature analysis.
  • Histology: 189 adenocarcinomas, 36 carcinoids, 5 sarcomatoid/undifferentiated NSCLC, 2 squamous-cell carcinomas.
  • Ancestry: 226 European (97.4%), 4 Asian (1.7%), 2 African (0.9%). Mean age 64.8 (range 21–86); 75.4% female.
  • 27.6% of patients had reported second-hand (“passive”) tobacco smoke exposure; all 232 were never smokers.
  • 35 adenocarcinomas additionally underwent RNA-seq.
  • Cohort = cBioPortal study lung_nci_2022; raw BAMs in dbGaP phs001697.v1.p1; RNA-seq FASTQs in GEO GSE171415. Used pcawg LUAD (n=38) as the smoker comparison.

Key findings

  • Median TMB is 1.1 Mut/Mb (SNV=1.0, indel=0.06), >7-fold lower than LUAD in smokers (P = 7.0e-73). TMB tracks tumor stage, histology, and age, but not purity.
  • Three SCNA subtypes:
    • piano (49.6%, 115/232): largely SCNA-flat despite high purity; includes 33/36 carcinoids and 78 adenocarcinomas; rare drivers; long telomeres; low SBS18.
    • mezzo-forte (30.2%): arm-level gains on 1q, 5p, 7p, 7q (each P<0.001 vs others) and 8q (exclusively this subtype); 51.4% EGFR-mutant.
    • forte (20.2%): dominated by WGD (95.7% of forte tumors vs 41.4% mezzo-forte and 8.7% piano).
  • RTK-RAS pathway alterations: EGFR 30.6%, KRAS 7.3%, ALK 6.0%, MET 4.3%, ERBB2 3.9% (all indels), ROS1 2.6%, RET 1.3% — strong mutual exclusivity; 54.3% of tumors harbor at least one. Driver fusion oncogene rearrangements were found in 24/232 (10.3%) tumors and were mutually exclusive with EGFR mutations (P = 1.1e-4).
  • TP53 / MDM2: TP53 mutations are mutually exclusive with MDM2 amplifications (P = 0.03); combined burden = 25.4% of tumors; this dual axis is enriched for SNVs, SCNAs, SVs, kataegis, WGD, HLA LOH, and BRCA1 LOH. TP53 mutation frequency rises with SCNA burden: 7.0% piano vs 18.6% mezzo-forte vs 31.9% forte (P = 1.2e-3) — still below 53.4% in LUAD smokers.
  • Driver discovery via IntOGen identified 25 recurrently mutated genes, all under positive selection. UBA1 (6/9 cases in piano) is highlighted as a likely new LCINS driver.
  • Mutational signatures (SigProfiler / COSMIC v3): 14 SBS signatures recovered. SBS4 (tobacco smoking) absent in all 232 tumors, including 62 passive smokers; simulation-based detection threshold = 15% of somatic mutations. SBS18 (reactive-oxygen-species damage) in 46% overall, enriched in forte (59.6%) and mezzo-forte (67.1%) vs piano (P = 2.2e-9). SBS8 in 13% of samples, enriched in carcinoids (30.3%, P = 2.7e-4). 6 tumors (4 in piano) carry a 1,8-dinitropyrene (Nitro-PAH/diesel exhaust) signature contributing 18.7% of SNVs on average.
  • APOBEC (SBS2/SBS13): ≥100 SNVs assigned to APOBEC in ~58% of samples; APOBEC-dominant (>80%) in 4 hypermutated tumors (TMB > 8 Mut/Mb). Enriched in TP53-deficient (P = 1.9e-8) and RTK-RAS+ (P = 3.5e-8) tumors; verified with P-MACD.
  • Passive vs non-passive smokers: No significant mutational-pattern differences (Q > 0.05 for all SBS/DBS/ID signatures), even comparing extremes of exposure. Passive-smoker tumors do have shorter telomeres (P = 0.005).
  • Whole-genome doubling: 36.2% of tumors overall.
  • Kataegis: 49.6% of tumors (mean 4 events/sample); rare in piano (29.6% vs 68.1% forte and 70% mezzo-forte; P = 1.3e-9); enriched at MDM2 locus (P = 1.3e-15).
  • Telomere length (TelSeq + TelomereHunter): LCINS LUAD telomeres significantly longer than LUAD-smoker telomeres (6.4 Kb, 95% CI 5.3–7.6, P = 7.1e-11). T/N TL ratio: forte 0.9 (P = 0.01), piano 1.1 (P = 4.7e-3, longer than normal).
  • HRDetect: ~16% (n = 37) have high HRDetect score (>0.7), concentrated in forte and mezzo-forte (P = 1.4e-3 vs piano). Biallelic ATM loss in 1 tumor; monoallelic loss of HRD-associated genes in 42% of tumors.
  • Evolutionary timing: Driver mutations in TP53, RBM10, KRAS, or EGFR generally precede WGD and most SCNAs across all three subtypes. EGFR-mutant tumors: estimated MRCA at age 61, with clinical diagnosis a median of 8 years later. Median latency: piano 9.10 yr vs forte 0.08 yr vs mezzo-forte 0.28 yr (P = 8.3e-4).
  • Survival (Cox PH): TP53 mutation or MDM2 amplification → HR = 2.9 (CI 1.6–5.2, P = 4.5e-4). EGFR mutation, CHEK2 LOH, 22q loss, 15q loss each independently associated with poor OS. A risk score = count of these five alterations gives HR ≈ 1.9 per alteration (CI 1.5–2.4, P = 3.7e-7). ERBB2 mutation → HR = 5.7 (CI 1.6–20.4, P = 7.2e-3, partially confounded with TP53). TP53-deficient + RTK-RAS- tumors (n = 8) → HR = 5.3 (CI 1.8–15.2, P = 2.0e-3). MET-altered tumors had better OS than RTK-RAS-. Piano patients fared better overall (HR = 0.52, CI 0.3–0.9, P = 0.03), particularly carcinoids (HR = 0.24, CI 0.06–1.0, P = 0.05). SETD2+ tumors HR = 0.13 (CI 0.02–1, P = 0.05).

Genes & alterations

  • EGFR — mutated in 30.6% overall (51.4% in mezzo-forte vs 9.0% in LUAD-smokers); independent poor-prognosis marker; mutations occur before the corresponding clonal copy gain; MRCA appears ~8 yr before diagnosis in EGFR-mutant tumors.
  • KRAS — 7.3% overall; 76.5% of KRAS+ tumors fall in piano (P = 0.02); only 1.4% of mezzo-forte vs 34.0% in LUAD-smokers; trend toward worse OS (NS).
  • ALK — fusion-driver alterations in 6.0%; trend toward worse OS (NS).
  • MET — 4.3%; better OS than RTK-RAS- group (unusual within the pathway).
  • ERBB2 — 3.9%, all indels; OS HR = 5.7 (P = 7.2e-3) but partially confounded with TP53 (4/7 ERBB2+ tumors also had TP53 alterations).
  • ROS1 — 2.6%.
  • RET — 1.3%; RET fusions found only in the piano subtype (2.6% of piano).
  • TP53 — mutually exclusive with MDM2 amp; subtype gradient (piano 7.0%, mezzo-forte 18.6%, forte 31.9%); HR = 2.9 jointly with MDM2.
  • MDM2 — focal amplifications less frequent in piano than forte/mezzo-forte (P = 0.001); kataegis hotspot at MDM2 locus (P = 1.3e-15); short-latency tumors when amplified.
  • UBA1 — newly identified candidate driver; 6/9 mutated tumors are in piano; encodes the E1 ubiquitin enzyme central to DNA damage response.
  • SETD2 — 8/10 mutated tumors in piano; positive prognosis (HR = 0.13).
  • ARID1A — enriched in piano carcinoids (P = 7.5e-4); part of mutually-exclusive piano driver set (KRAS, UBA1, RET, ARID1A).
  • NKX2-1 — 4 LUAD mutations, all in piano; regulates RET.
  • CHEK2 — LOH independently associated with poor OS.
  • CDKN2A, STK11 — among the long-latency driver groups (MRCA > a decade pre-diagnosis).
  • BRCA1, BRCA2, ATM, RAD51D — germline variants in HR genes (n = 3, 2, 2 respectively for BRCA1, ATM, RAD51D); BRCA2 LOH enriched in RTK-RAS+ tumors.
  • AR — most frequently mutated cancer-susceptibility germline gene (n = 5, 4 in piano), supporting a hormonal contribution.
  • CYP21A2, GLUD2 — recurrent rare deleterious germline variants (8 and 6 carriers respectively).
  • RBM10, HUWE1, PIK3CA, SFTPB, CDK4, SOS1, MSH6 — driver/timing observations: RBM10 and KRAS mutations precede copy gains; PIK3CA and SFTPB mutations occur after gain events; CDK4 germline variant in forte; SOS1 in mezzo-forte; RET and MSH6 germline variants in piano.
  • TERT — amplified in 11.6% and promoter-mutated in only 0.9%; rare in piano; insufficient to explain TL elongation in piano.
  • MYC, GNAS — SV breakpoint hotspots together with MDM2, TERT, CDKN2A, NKX2-1, and 6p21.
  • APOBEC3A, APOBEC3B — kataegis events show A3A-like and A3B-like signatures.
  • SOX2, SOX9, HMGA2 — basis of the RNA-seq “development score” that is elevated in piano, supporting the stem-cell-like hypothesis.
  • NOTCH1 — discussed as a candidate exit-from-quiescence driver consistent with the piano phenotype.
  • ZNF717 — LOH on 3p12.2 likely targets ZNF717; identified as an early event in mezzo-forte.
  • IGKV1-5 — focal gain at 2p11.2 involving this immunoglobulin gene identified as an early event in piano.

Clinical implications

  • Five-feature genomic risk score (TP53 mut, MDM2 amp, EGFR mut, CHEK2 LOH, 22q loss, 15q loss — five independent alterations) gives ≈ 2× mortality per alteration and is proposed as a stratification tool for LCINS.
  • Subtype-targeted therapy hypotheses:
    • forte/mezzo-forte: existing or trial-stage agents targeting TP53, MDM2-TP53 interaction, EGFR, and ERBB2 are already applicable; ≈ 21% of tumors carry both TP53 deficiency and RTK-RAS mutations, a published combination-therapy target. >15% of tumors have LOH of an HRD-associated gene → PARP inhibitor / HRD-directed therapy candidates. Collateral lethality for genes co-deleted with tumor suppressors on 22q, 15q, and at CHEK2 is explicitly proposed as a new therapeutic angle.
    • piano: scarce known drivers and low TMB plus rare HLA LOH suggest these patients are unlikely to benefit from immune checkpoint inhibitors. The paper proposes KRAS inhibitors, stem-cell-signaling targeting, and stem-cell microenvironment modulation. RET-fusion-only-in-piano is a candidate selpercatinib/pralsetinib indication.
  • Early-detection window: Because piano MRCAs are estimated to appear ~9 yr before clinical diagnosis, the authors argue piano offers an unusually long window for early-detection screening.
  • Survival heterogeneity within RTK-RAS: MET alterations associate with better survival, while ERBB2 (HR = 5.7), EGFR, KRAS, and ALK trend toward worse — suggesting RTK-RAS status alone is not a useful prognostic axis in LCINS.
  • Passive-smoking biology: Absent SBS4 below 15% threshold means passive smoking, if it contributes mutationally in LCINS, does so subtly and/or via non-canonical mechanisms (e.g., telomere shortening).

Limitations & open questions

  • Ancestry skew: 97.4% European; only 4 Asian and 2 African patients, limiting generalizability to Asian LCINS populations where EGFR-mutant LUAD is even more common.
  • SBS4 detection floor of 15% of somatic mutations cannot exclude low-level smoking-induced mutagenesis in passive smokers.
  • ERBB2 prognostic signal (HR = 5.7) is confounded with TP53 co-alteration in 4/7 ERBB2+ tumors; needs validation in ERBB2+/TP53-wt cohorts.
  • Stem-cell-like origin of piano is inferred from bulk WGS + 35-sample RNA-seq; authors explicitly call for single-cell RNA-seq and methylome analyses, especially in piano tumors with no apparent driver.
  • Survival analyses are based on a single cohort; the 5-feature risk score has not been externally validated.
  • Lifetime MRCA timing depends on an assumed 1× tumor acceleration rate (with sensitivity analyses to 20×) and on the COSMIC clock-like CpG>TpG model — model assumptions could shift latency estimates.
  • Carcinoid representation (36/232) is unusual for an LUAD-focused cohort; whether the piano subtype as defined is genuinely the same biological state across adenocarcinomas and carcinoids remains open.
