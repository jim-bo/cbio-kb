# FGF12

## Overview

FGF12 (Fibroblast Growth Factor 12) is a member of the FGF family that acts intracellularly (iFGF) and is not a canonical receptor-activating ligand. It has roles in neuronal function and may influence cancer cell signaling. In breast cancer, FGF12 mutations have been identified as part of somatic mutational landscapes in clonal evolution studies.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- FGF12 missense mutation identified as private to the IDC-NST component in one juvenile papillomatosis (JP) case (JuP3), not shared with the preceding JP or [DCIS](../cancer_types/DCIS.md) lesion, suggesting it was acquired during invasive progression [PMID:33263939](../papers/33263939.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) / [IDC](../cancer_types/IDC.md)**: FGF12 missense mutation private to IDC-NST in a JP-associated invasive breast carcinoma, potentially acquired at the invasive stage [PMID:33263939](../papers/33263939.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- In JuP3, FGF12 missense is an IDC-private mutation that does not co-occur with the JP/DCIS clonal events ([PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) E542K, [CES1](../genes/CES1.md) S12A, [SMG1](../genes/SMG1.md)) — it was acquired later in cancer progression [PMID:33263939](../papers/33263939.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

No direct therapeutic implications reported for FGF12 in breast cancer in this corpus.

## Open questions

- Whether FGF12 mutations are recurrent in invasive breast carcinoma arising from JP or in breast cancer more broadly requires larger series.
- The functional consequence of the FGF12 missense on cancer cell behavior is unknown.
