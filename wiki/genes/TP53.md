# TP53

## Overview

TP53 encodes the p53 tumor suppressor protein, a master transcription factor governing DNA damage response, apoptosis, and cell cycle arrest. TP53 loss-of-function is one of the most common somatic alterations across human cancers, with roles established in hepatocellular carcinoma ([HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)), gastric adenocarcinoma ([STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md)), nasopharyngeal carcinoma ([NPC](../cancer_types/NPC.md)), and thymic carcinoma.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Loss-of-function mutation in 28% of HCCs (range 23-31%; WES, n=1,289); defines the TCGA iClust3 subtype associated with chromosomal instability and poor prognosis; enriched in the proliferation class. [PMID:24798001](../papers/24798001.md)
- TP53 somatic mutations recur in FIGC tumors (familial intestinal gastric cancer), uncommon in matched sporadic intestinal GC; common risk-associated TP53 polymorphisms enriched in 26/50 FIGC probands vs. 4/38 (11%) sporadic comparators. [PMID:24816255](../papers/24816255.md)
- TP53 germline mutations define Li-Fraumeni syndrome, which must be excluded in familial gastric cancer workup; included in the mandatory multigene panel. [PMID:24816255](../papers/24816255.md)
- TP53 mutations and reduced immune infiltration reported at higher rates in EBV-negative [NPC](../cancer_types/NPC.md) compared to EBV-positive [NPC](../cancer_types/NPC.md); EBV-negative tumors have distinct genomic profiles including higher TP53 mutation rates. [PMID:24952746](../papers/24952746.md)
- Recurrent somatic TP53 mutations in thymic carcinomas (aggressive histotype); one of the canonical cancer-gene mutations enriched in thymic carcinoma relative to indolent thymomas (which instead carry the [GTF2I](../genes/GTF2I.md) L404H hotspot). [PMID:24974848](../papers/24974848.md)
- Focal copy number loss (16p) in prostate cancer; not significantly associated with biochemical recurrence in isolation ([prad_mskcc](../datasets/prad_mskcc.md), [prad_mskcc_2014](../datasets/prad_mskcc_2014.md) cohorts). [PMID:25024180](../papers/25024180.md)
- Mutated in 50% of gastric cancers overall; highest in CIN subtype (71%); rare in EBV-positive tumours (TCGA gastric). [PMID:25079317](../papers/25079317.md)
- Mutated in 46% of [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) (TCGA, n=230); enriched in cluster 1–3; co-mutated with [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) in the PI (proximal-inflammatory) transcriptional subtype. [PMID:25079552](../papers/25079552.md)
- Mutated in 57% of high-grade urothelial carcinoma (MSK-IMPACT, n=109); enriched in extravesical vs. organ-confined disease (69% vs. 32%, P=0.005) and node-positive disease (77% vs. 56%, P=0.025); worse univariate outcomes not significant after adjustment for pT/pN stage. [PMID:25092538](../papers/25092538.md)
- Significantly mutated across a 25-sample muscle-invasive urothelial carcinoma cohort (MutSigCV); not differentially enriched between [cisplatin](../drugs/cisplatin.md) responders and non-responders [PMID:25096233](../papers/25096233.md)
- Somatic mutation in 21/66 (32%) chromophobe RCC; MutSig q<0.1; mutations correlated with decreased expression of p53 transcriptional targets [PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)
- Higher prevalence in this metastatic CRC cohort than in TCGA (attributed to metastatic-stage enrichment); parallel evolution case with independent R248Q and Y163* mutations in primary vs metastasis [PMID:25164765](../papers/25164765.md)
- p53-p21-RB1 pathway intact in EWS::FLI1-expressing heMSCs; p53 induction limits long-term in vitro culture, consistent with p53 acting as a barrier to oncogenic transformation in the Ewing sarcoma cell-of-origin model [PMID:25186949](../papers/25186949.md)
- Mutation with complete loss of wild-type allele in 4/7 MSK prostate cancer cell lines; represents a frequent hit in castration-resistant prostate cancer models [PMID:25201530](../papers/25201530.md)
- TP53 missense mutations in 7% of Ewing sarcoma WGS cohort and 5.2% of extended 299-patient cohort; cooperates with [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md) mutation to define worst-prognosis subtype [PMID:25223734](../papers/25223734.md)
- TP53 mutated in 16/22 (67%) uterine/ovarian carcinosarcomas; seven cases clonal homozygous (>80% VAF) [PMID:25233892](../papers/25233892.md)
- TP53 non-synonymous mutation and heterozygous loss in 42% of malignant peripheral nerve sheath tumors ([MPNST](../cancer_types/MPNST.md)) [PMID:25240281](../papers/25240281.md)
- TP53 mutated in 94.9% of aggressive cSCC (37/39 patients); top driver by all four significance methods (MutSig q-value below detection limit) [PMID:25303977](../papers/25303977.md)
- TP53 significantly enriched mutations specifically in chromophobe renal cell carcinoma (chRCC) classic subtype (P = 2.3 x 10^-5) in the UTSW nccRCC study [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)
- TP53 tumor suppressor mutations in 15/402 (3.7%) PTCs alongside [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), [NF2](../genes/NF2.md), and [MEN1](../genes/MEN1.md); part of the tumor suppressor gene alteration cluster in PTC (TCGA cohort). [PMID:25417114](../papers/25417114.md)
- Xenograft-specific deletion event encompassing TP53 detected in breast cancer PDX series SA500, coinciding with retention of a somatic SNV; illustrates PDX-specific structural alterations that diverge from the original tumor's clonal composition. [PMID:25470049](../papers/25470049.md)
- TP53 mutations in 20–27% of intrahepatic CCA (iCCA) and 35–68% of extrahepatic CCA (eCCA); HBsAg-positive iCCA preferentially harbors TP53 mutations; TP53 co-mutations associated with primary resistance to FGFR inhibitors. [PMID:25526346](../papers/25526346.md)
- TP53 significantly enriched in the High-Clonality (HiC) gastric cancer subtype in the Tianjin Chinese cohort (n=294); present only as minor subclones (<15% VAF) in all 6 HiC-positive cases; independently associated with shorter survival and older age; TP53 subclonal status defines the poor-prognosis HiC subtype (adjusted HR 4.69, P=0.0043). [PMID:25583476](../papers/25583476.md)
- Classic CRC driver gene; studied for comparison in AA vs Caucasian CRC cohort (n=103 AA, n=129 Caucasian); no significant difference in mutation frequency between ethnicities [PMID:25583493](../papers/25583493.md)
- Mutated in 23/29 (79%) of metastatic cSCC; dominant event with UV-pattern missense plus truncations; HPV(-) biology marker; TP53 status (wild-type vs mutant) correlates with patient outcome in HNSCC [PMID:25589618](../papers/25589618.md)
- Cited in context of murine KRAS/p53 knockout CCA models; the authors flag these as inadequately recapitulating human cholestatic pathophysiology [PMID:25608663](../papers/25608663.md)
- Mutated in 72% of HNSCC overall (86% in HPV(-), 1/36 in HPV(+)); predominantly loss-of-function; central to classical subtype; TP53 status and 11q13 amplification proposed as candidate prognostic biomarkers beyond HPV [PMID:25631445](../papers/25631445.md)
- Significantly mutated (MutSigCV q<0.05) in [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md); TP53/cell-cycle pathway altered in 49% of cases; HBV-related [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) significantly enriched for TP53 mutation; R249S hotspot is the AFB1 mutational signature (signature 24). [PMID:25822088](../papers/25822088.md)
- Mutated in 50% of pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma cases with strong p53-IHC stabilization concordance; one of the four core driver genes ([KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), TP53, [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)) in PDA. [PMID:25855536](../papers/25855536.md)
- Most selectively mutated gene in metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer vs. primary prostate cancer (q<0.001); mutated in ~53.3% of mCRPC cases. [PMID:26000489](../papers/26000489.md)
- 49 mutations in cutaneous melanoma TCGA cohort; 93.9% (46/49) in UV-signature samples; enriched in BRAF/RAS/NF1 vs Triple-WT subtype. [PMID:26091043](../papers/26091043.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)** -- TP53 LOF in 28%; trunk driver, not currently druggable; defines the proliferation molecular class with poor outcomes; co-occurs with chromosomal instability. [PMID:24798001](../papers/24798001.md)
- **[STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md)** -- Somatic TP53 mutations in FIGC; germline TP53 (Li-Fraumeni) must be excluded in familial GC; risk-associated TP53 polymorphisms enriched in familial intestinal GC probands. [PMID:24816255](../papers/24816255.md)
- **[NPC](../cancer_types/NPC.md)** -- TP53 mutations enriched in EBV-negative (non-endemic) [NPC](../cancer_types/NPC.md); lower immune infiltration in TP53-mutant EBV-negative tumors has implications for ICI trial stratification. [PMID:24952746](../papers/24952746.md)
- **[THYC](../cancer_types/THYC.md) (thymic carcinoma)** -- recurrent somatic TP53 mutations; part of the aggressive molecular profile (alongside [CYLD](../genes/CYLD.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md), [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md)) distinguishing thymic carcinomas from indolent thymomas. [PMID:24974848](../papers/24974848.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- In [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md), TP53 inactivation is enriched in the proliferation class alongside FGF19/CCND1 amplifications and HBV aetiology; mutually enriched in iClust3 with chromosomal instability. [PMID:24798001](../papers/24798001.md)
- In thymic carcinomas, TP53 co-occurs with [CYLD](../genes/CYLD.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md), and [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md) mutations, defining a high mutation-burden aggressive subtype distinct from GTF2I-mutant indolent thymomas. [PMID:24974848](../papers/24974848.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- TP53 is not directly druggable with currently approved agents in [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md); listed among trunk HCC mutations for which no actionable targeted therapy exists. [PMID:24798001](../papers/24798001.md)
- TP53 mutation status in [NPC](../cancer_types/NPC.md) (enriched in EBV-negative tumors) has implications for ICI trial stratification, as EBV-negative NPC has lower immune infiltration. [PMID:24952746](../papers/24952746.md)

## Open questions

- In HCC, blocking TP53-driven chromosomal instability is an unmet need; no TP53 reactivation strategy has been validated clinically. [PMID:24798001](../papers/24798001.md)
- In NPC, whether TP53 mutation status should stratify patients for ICI vs. other treatment modalities requires prospective validation. [PMID:24952746](../papers/24952746.md)
- In thymic carcinomas, the prognostic and predictive significance of TP53 mutations relative to other co-occurring alterations ([CYLD](../genes/CYLD.md), [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md), [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md)) requires further characterization. [PMID:24974848](../papers/24974848.md)
