# Molecular Classification of Appendiceal Adenocarcinoma

**PMID:** 36493333 · **DOI:** 10.1200/JCO.22.01392 · **Journal:** Journal of Clinical Oncology (2022)

## TL;DR

Foote et al. performed [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) targeted sequencing on a retrospective MSK cohort of patients with [appendiceal adenocarcinoma](../cancer_types/APAD.md) (AC) to ask whether co-occurring somatic mutations define clinically meaningful subtypes beyond histology. In 164 mucinous appendiceal adenocarcinomas (MAAP), they define three molecular lineages based on [GNAS](../genes/GNAS.md), RAS, and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations: a RAS-mutant/[GNAS](../genes/GNAS.md)-wt/[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)-wt indolent subtype, a chemotherapy-resistant GNAS-mut predominant subtype, and an aggressive, highly aneuploid TP53-mut predominant subtype. Subtype was associated with overall survival, peritoneal tumor burden, and first-line chemotherapy response independently of histopathologic grade [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).

## Cohort & data

- Retrospective cohort of patients with [appendiceal adenocarcinoma](../cancer_types/APAD.md) sequenced on [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) at Memorial Sloan Kettering between April 2015 and October 2020, released as the [appendiceal_msk_2022](../datasets/appendiceal_msk_2022.md) cBioPortal study [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).
- Molecular subtype analysis focused on 164 mucinous appendiceal adenocarcinoma (MAAP) tumors, with findings then extended to the broader AC series including goblet cell adenocarcinoma (GCA) and colonic-type appendiceal adenocarcinoma (CTAAP) [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).
- Matched tumor/normal hybridization-capture next-generation sequencing via [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md); oncogenic drivers and actionable variants annotated via [OncoKB](../methods/oncokb.md); clonality assessed with [FACETS](../methods/facets.md); aneuploidy scored from segment-length-weighted copy-number deviation from diploid [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).

## Key findings

- Three MAAP molecular subtypes defined by co-occurring mutations in [GNAS](../genes/GNAS.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)/[NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md), and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md): RAS-mut predominant (RAS-mut/[GNAS](../genes/GNAS.md)-wt/[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)-wt, n=24), GNAS-mut predominant, and TP53-mut predominant [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).
- The RAS-mut predominant subtype had significantly fewer mutations and chromosomal alterations than GNAS-mut or TP53-mut predominant tumors; no patient with metastatic RAS-mut predominant MAAP died of cancer during follow-up [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).
- Overall survival was significantly better in RAS-mut predominant patients than in GNAS-mut predominant (P=.05) or TP53-mut predominant (P=.004) subtypes [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).
- TP53-mut predominant tumors were highly aneuploid; increased tumor aneuploidy was independently associated with poor prognosis (P=.001) in multivariable Cox models adjusting for histology, grade, age, CRS number, and first-line chemotherapy type [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).
- RAS-mut predominant metastases showed reduced peritoneal tumor bulk (lower peritoneal cancer index, P=.04) and reduced stromal invasion (P<.001) relative to GNAS-mut or TP53-mut predominant tumors, respectively [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).
- First-line chemotherapy response rate was 50% in RAS-mut predominant MAAP vs 6% in GNAS-mut predominant MAAP (P=.03) [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).
- Subtype-associated clinical behavior was conserved when the analysis was extended to any metastatic AC, irrespective of histopathologic subtype (MAAP, GCA, CTAAP) [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).

## Genes & alterations

- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) / [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) — RAS mutations in the absence of [GNAS](../genes/GNAS.md) and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) alterations mark the clinically indolent MAAP subtype with the lowest mutational and chromosomal burden and the best overall survival [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).
- [GNAS](../genes/GNAS.md) — [GNAS](../genes/GNAS.md)-mut predominant MAAP is associated with chemotherapy resistance (6% first-line response) and intermediate survival [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)-mut predominant tumors are highly aneuploid, invasive, and carry the worst prognosis; aneuploidy is an independent predictor of poor OS [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).

## Clinical implications

- The authors argue that somatic tumor profiling of [appendiceal adenocarcinoma](../cancer_types/APAD.md) has direct clinical utility: molecular subtype may inform whether to offer cytoreductive surgery and/or systemic chemotherapy, independent of histologic grade [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).
- RAS-mut predominant MAAP patients may be candidates for less aggressive management given their indolent course and chemotherapy sensitivity; [GNAS](../genes/GNAS.md)-mut predominant patients appear unlikely to benefit from standard colorectal-style first-line regimens combining [fluorouracil](../drugs/fluorouracil.md), [leucovorin](../drugs/leucovorin.md), [oxaliplatin](../drugs/oxaliplatin.md), and/or [irinotecan](../drugs/irinotecan.md) (FOLFOX/FOLFIRI/FOLFIRINOX), or [capecitabine](../drugs/capecitabine.md) [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).
- Findings challenge the default of managing AC with colorectal cancer treatment paradigms [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).

## Limitations & open questions

- Retrospective, single-institution MSK cohort sequenced on a targeted panel ([MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md)) rather than whole-exome/whole-genome; subtype calls depend on panel coverage [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).
- Sample sizes in some subtypes (e.g., 24 RAS-mut predominant MAAP) are small, and no cancer-specific deaths in that subgroup means OS estimates are imprecise [PMID:36493333](../papers/36493333.md).
- External validation in independent appendiceal cohorts and prospective evaluation of subtype-guided therapy selection are open questions.
- Mechanistic basis for [GNAS](../genes/GNAS.md)-mut-associated chemoresistance and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)-mut-associated aneuploidy in appendiceal tumors remains to be determined.
