# LRRC47

## Overview

LRRC47 (Leucine-Rich Repeat Containing 47) encodes a protein of poorly characterized function; it contains leucine-rich repeat domains that often mediate protein-protein interactions. LRRC47 has been nominated as a candidate cis-driver in hepatocellular carcinoma based on copy-number amplification with concordant mRNA and protein dysregulation.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Cis-driver candidate dysregulated at both mRNA and protein levels in [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) in a European multi-etiology proteogenomic cohort (n=122 tumors); co-nominated alongside [XPO5](../genes/XPO5.md) as a candidate cis-driver in this HCC cohort [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **HCC**: LRRC47 is dysregulated at both mRNA and protein levels, qualifying it as a cis-driver candidate in the integrative proteogenomic analysis of HCC [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-nominated as a cis-driver candidate with XPO5 in HCC [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic implication reported. LRRC47's cis-driver nomination requires functional validation before therapeutic relevance can be established [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md).

## Open questions

- The mechanism by which LRRC47 dysregulation contributes to HCC pathogenesis is unknown; functional validation studies are needed [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md).
