# KIT

## Overview

KIT is a receptor tyrosine kinase and a canonical oncogenic driver in [GIST](../cancer_types/GIST.md), mastocytosis, and germ cell tumors.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Oncogenic KIT mutations observed in a minority of female germ cell tumors profiled by MSK-IMPACT; mostly clonal by WES [PMID:36862133](../papers/36862133.md).
- KIT exon 11 mutation status was incorporated into the gastric [GIST](../cancer_types/GIST.md) elastic-net Cox genomic risk model (n=152 primary localized gastric GISTs); absence of KIT exon 11 mutation flagged intermediate risk for recurrence [PMID:37477937](../papers/37477937.md).
- KIT amplification was detected exclusively in sporadic [ANGS](../cancer_types/ANGS.md) but absent in radiation-associated (RT) angiosarcoma (n=44 RT-AS, n=135 sporadic AS) in the MSK RT-sarcoma comparative genomic study [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [OGCT](../cancer_types/OGCT.md) — oncogenic KIT mutations in a subset of female GCT cases in the Make-an-IMPACT rare-cancer cohort [PMID:36862133](../papers/36862133.md).
- [ANGS](../cancer_types/ANGS.md) — KIT amplification exclusive to sporadic AS, absent in RT-AS [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- None reported.

## Therapeutic relevance

- Not directly targeted in the reported female GCT cases [PMID:36862133](../papers/36862133.md).

## Open questions

- Whether KIT-mutant female GCTs derive benefit from KIT inhibitors is not addressed [PMID:36862133](../papers/36862133.md).
