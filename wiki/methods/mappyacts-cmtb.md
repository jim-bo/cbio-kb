# MAPPYACTS Clinical Molecular Tumor Board

## Overview

The MAPPYACTS Clinical Molecular Tumor Board (CMTB) is the decision-making body within the MAPPYACTS precision-oncology trial (NCT02613962) that translates comprehensive molecular profiling results into actionable treatment recommendations for pediatric and young-adult patients with recurrent/refractory cancers. The process begins with a weekly molecular tumor board (MTB) that reviews WES and RNA-seq findings; results then proceed to a CMTB that includes the treating physician. The CMTB applies an ESCAT-adapted pediatric actionability scale to categorize findings as "ready for routine use," "investigational," "hypothetical," or "resistance." Recommendations are communicated to the treating physician within approximately 28 days of biopsy.

## Used by

- MAPPYACTS trial (NCT02613962): weekly MTB feeding the CMTB applied an ESCAT-adapted pediatric actionability scale to 624 patients; 432/624 (69%) had at least one potentially actionable alteration; 107/356 (30%) of patients with ≥12 months follow-up received ≥1 matched targeted therapy; overall ORR for matched therapy was 17%, rising to 38% for "ready for routine use" alterations [PMID:35292802](../papers/35292802.md).

## Notes

- Four actionability tiers: "ready for routine use" (4% of actionable alterations), "investigational" (80%), "hypothetical" (16%), and "resistance."
- Median 2 treatment recommendations per patient (range 1–7); most recommended drug classes were [WEE1](../genes/WEE1.md) inhibitors (n=150), mTOR (n=123), CDK4/6 (n=105), and MEK (n=95).
- 56% of matched therapies were administered within phase I/II trials, predominantly the AcSe-ESMART platform (NCT02813135).
- 14/122 (11%) matched therapies were "ready for routine use," yielding 38% ORR (5/13 single-agent evaluable); investigational-tier ORR was 14% (12/86).
- CMTB-based actionability classification differs from the INFORM registry in key areas (e.g., [ALK](../genes/ALK.md), HRAS/KRAS not classified as ready-for-routine-use in MAPPYACTS).
