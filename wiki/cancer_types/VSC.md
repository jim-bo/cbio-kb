# Vulvar Squamous Cell Carcinoma (VSC)

## Overview

Vulvar squamous cell carcinoma (VSC) is a squamous malignancy arising from the vulvar epithelium. Two distinct etiologic pathways exist: HPV-associated tumors (HPV-positive, typically younger patients) and HPV-independent tumors arising on a background of lichen sclerosus or differentiated vulvar intraepithelial neoplasia (HPV-negative, older patients). VSC is a rare gynecologic cancer; approximately one-third of patients experience recurrence with limited therapeutic options.

## Cohorts in the corpus

- [vsc_cuk_2018](../datasets/vsc_cuk_2018.md) — 15 Korean vulvar SCC patients (6 HPV+, 9 HPV-) profiled by whole-exome sequencing at The Catholic University of Korea; first genome-wide landscape of this tumor type [PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md).

## Recurrent alterations

- HPV(-) vulvar SCC recurrently harbors [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), and [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) mutations plus 7p and 8q gains and 2q loss; HPV(-) cases carried higher nonsilent and driver mutation loads than HPV(+) cases [PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md).
- HPV(+) vulvar SCC showed novel mutations in [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), and [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md) — none previously reported in this tumor type [PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md).
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) and [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md) were each altered in 40% (6/15) of vulvar SCCs irrespective of HPV status, making them the highest-frequency alterations in this cohort [PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md).
- Kataegis (localized hypermutation, inter-mutation distance ≤10 kb) was detected in 2 HPV(+) tumors; copy-neutral LOH was found in 4 tumors (1 HPV+, 3 HPV-) [PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md).
- Mutational signatures were compared against TCGA SCC cohorts ([CESC](../cancer_types/CESC.md), [ESCA](../cancer_types/ESCA.md), [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md), [LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md)) and published CSCC/oral SCC datasets; CNA burden and signatures did not differ between HPV(+) and HPV(-) VSC subgroups [PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md).

## Subtypes

- **HPV-positive vulvar SCC:** Associated with high-risk HPV types (HPV16, 52, 58 in the Korean WES cohort); carries lower mutation burden than HPV(-) cases; harbors [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), and [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md) mutations as novel genomic features [PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md).
- **HPV-negative vulvar SCC:** Arises independently of HPV; characterized by [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), and [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) mutations plus recurrent 7p/8q gains and 2q loss; higher mutational and driver-mutation load than HPV(+) cases [PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md).

## Therapeutic landscape

- No specific drug or targeted therapy has been established for VSC based on the corpus to date. The 40% prevalence of [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) alterations across both HPV subgroups has been noted as a potential actionable target, though no clinical data from the corpus support a specific recommendation [PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md).
