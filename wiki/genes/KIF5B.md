# KIF5B

## Overview

KIF5B (Kinesin Family Member 5B) encodes a kinesin motor protein involved in intracellular transport. In lung cancer, KIF5B is best known as the 5' partner in the KIF5B-MET fusion gene, an oncogenic fusion detected in lung adenocarcinoma. KIF5B-MET leads to constitutive [MET](../genes/MET.md) kinase activation and is a potential therapeutic target for [MET](../genes/MET.md) inhibitors.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- KIF5B-MET fusion detected in lung ADC (previously reported fusion); identified as part of the novel MET fusion landscape in the TCGA pan-lung cancer cohort alongside a novel MET-CAPZA2 fusion [PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)
- KIF5B is the fusion partner in KIF5B-RET fusions detected in 8 tumors in the Sherlock-Lung non-smoking [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) cohort (n=232) [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md)
- KIF5B-RET fusions detected in [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) by MSK-ACCESS cfDNA assay (129-gene panel, NYS-approved) [PMID:34145282](../papers/34145282.md)
- KIF5B is the most common [RET](../genes/RET.md) fusion partner in RET-driven lung cancers; KIF5B-RET was the predominant fusion in the [selpercatinib](../drugs/selpercatinib.md) resistance study cohort (n=72), with acquired on-target solvent-front and gatekeeper RET mutations emerging at progression [PMID:35304457](../papers/35304457.md).
