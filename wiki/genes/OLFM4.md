# OLFM4

## Overview

OLFM4 (olfactomedin 4) is a secreted glycoprotein highly expressed in intestinal crypt-base columnar cells and [LGR5](../genes/LGR5.md)+ stem cells, where it functions as a marker of the intestinal stem cell niche. In colorectal precancer biology, OLFM4 is part of the WNT/stem-cell transcriptional program that is over-expressed in adenoma stem cells (ASCs) and MSS CRC cells, in contrast to the BRAF-driven serrated pathway.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- OLFM4 over-expressed as part of the WNT/stem-cell program (alongside LGR5, [ASCL2](../genes/ASCL2.md), [EPHB2](../genes/EPHB2.md), and [MYC](../genes/MYC.md)) in adenoma stem cells (ASCs) and MSS CRC cells in the COLON MAP/HTAN multi-omic colorectal polyp atlas (128 scRNA-seq datasets, 142,065 cells) [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Colorectal cancer ([COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md)): OLFM4 marks the APC-truncation-driven WNT-pathway stem cell state in conventional adenomas and MSS CRCs [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- OLFM4 co-expressed with LGR5, ASCL2, EPHB2, and MYC in ASCs; this WNT stem-cell program is elevated in conventional adenomas but not in serrated polyps (HPs, SSLs) [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting reported in the corpus.

## Open questions

- Whether OLFM4 protein expression can distinguish adenoma stem cells from normal crypt-base cells in clinical pathology settings has not been addressed.
