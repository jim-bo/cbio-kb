# MVK

## Overview

MVK (mevalonate kinase) catalyzes phosphorylation of mevalonate in the cholesterol biosynthesis pathway. In [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md), MVK is part of the cholesterol biosynthesis axis implicated in tumor progression.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- MVK is a cholesterol biosynthesis enzyme implicated in HCC; part of the HMGCR/SQLE/MVK axis targeted by statin-based and pathway-specific inhibitor strategies [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) — MVK is part of the cholesterol biosynthesis pathway contributing to HCC progression [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-expressed with [HMGCR](../genes/HMGCR.md) and [SQLE](../genes/SQLE.md) in cholesterol biosynthesis in HCC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- MVK is within the mevalonate pathway targeted by statins and farnesyl transferase inhibitors [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Open questions

- Specific MVK inhibitors for HCC therapy are not yet developed.
