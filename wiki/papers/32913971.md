**PMID:** 32913971 · **DOI:** [10.1200/PO.16.00054](https://doi.org/10.1200/PO.16.00054) · **Journal:** JCO Precision Oncology (2017)

## TL;DR

Catalanotti et al. profiled pretreatment tumors from 66 patients with metastatic [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E/K-mutant [melanoma](../cancer_types/SKCM.md) (55 treated with BRAF-inhibitor monotherapy, 11 with a BRAFi + MEKi combination) using a >250-gene exon-capture sequencing panel ([PMID:32913971](../papers/32913971.md)). Loss-of-function [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) alterations were significantly enriched in poor responders to [BRAFi](../drugs/vemurafenib.md) monotherapy and were associated with shorter PFS (HR 3.46, 95% CI 1.79–6.71; P < .001) and [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) (HR 3.10, 95% CI 1.59–6.05; P < .001). Excellent responders were enriched for elevated BRAF-mutant allele fraction, suggesting that selective amplification of the mutant allele or loss of the wild-type allele sensitizes tumors to BRAFi. The authors propose co-targeting [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) and the PI3K/AKT pathway in patients with concurrent [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) and [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) alterations.

## Cohort & data

- **Cohort:** 66 patients with stage IV or unresectable stage III BRAF V600E/K-mutant metastatic [melanoma (SKCM)](../cancer_types/SKCM.md), collected at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center and Vanderbilt-Ingram Cancer Center before BRAFi-based therapy ([PMID:32913971](../papers/32913971.md)).
- **Treatment split:** 55 patients received BRAFi monotherapy (49 [vemurafenib](../drugs/vemurafenib.md), 5 [dabrafenib](../drugs/dabrafenib.md), 1 [vemurafenib](../drugs/vemurafenib.md) + [cobimetinib](../drugs/cobimetinib.md)*) and 11 received a BRAFi + MEKi combination (3 [vemurafenib](../drugs/vemurafenib.md) + [cobimetinib](../drugs/cobimetinib.md); 8 [dabrafenib](../drugs/dabrafenib.md) + [trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md)). *One BRAFi-monotherapy patient received [cobimetinib](../drugs/cobimetinib.md) later for chronic myelomonocytic leukemia.
- **Dataset:** [skcm_vanderbilt_mskcc_2015](../datasets/skcm_vanderbilt_mskcc_2015.md); data deposited at the MSK cBioPortal for Cancer Genomics. Comparator: 151 BRAF V600E/K-mutant tumors from the [TCGA SKCM cohort (skcm_tcga_pub_2015)](../datasets/skcm_tcga_pub_2015.md).
- **Assay / panel:** custom capture-based DNA sequencing across all coding exons and selected introns of >250 cancer-associated genes ([msk-impact-panel](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) precursor, per Wagle et al. 2012 and Cheng et al. 2015), mean coverage 622×.
- **Variant calling:** [MuTect](../methods/mutect.md) for SNVs; [GATK Somatic Indel Detector](../methods/gatk-somatic-indel-detector.md) for indels; [FACETS](../methods/facets.md) for tumor purity, ploidy, and allele-specific copy number.
- **Response endpoints:** RECIST 1.1 plus a composite "response grade" (excellent: >50% shrinkage for >7 months or any shrinkage for >12 months; poor: tumor growth, new lesions, or <50% shrinkage for <4 months; intermediate: neither).
- **Follow-up:** median 14.6 months (cutoff March 2016).

## Key findings

- **BRAFi monotherapy response (RECIST):** 30/55 (55%) objective responses, 5/55 (9%) complete responses, 25/55 (45%) partial responses, 11/55 (20%) stable disease, 10/55 (18%) primary progressive disease, 4/55 (7%) not evaluable ([PMID:32913971](../papers/32913971.md)).
- **Response-grade distribution (BRAFi monotherapy):** excellent 17/55 (31%), poor 19/55 (35%), intermediate 15/55 (27%), not evaluable 4/55 (7%). RECIST and response-grade classifications were discordant in 8/66 patients (PR by RECIST but poor responders by grade due to short duration).
- **[PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) alterations enriched in poor responders:** 11 PTEN alterations in poor responders vs. 3 in intermediate and 2 in excellent responders (P = .005). Of the 11 PTEN alterations in the poor group, 10 were likely inactivating: deep deletions (n = 5), truncating mutations (n = 4), and one C2-domain missense (P204S); a P38S variant in the phosphatase domain was of unknown significance.
- **Survival impact of PTEN alteration:** patients with [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) alterations had shorter PFS (HR 3.46, 95% CI 1.79–6.71; P < .001) and shorter OS (HR 3.10, 95% CI 1.59–6.05; P < .001). The association persisted after controlling for stage and ECOG performance status (PFS HR 3.30, 95% CI 1.70–6.43, P < .001; OS HR 3.37, 95% CI 1.70–6.70, P < .001).
- **PTEN alterations are not exclusive to poor responders:** 2 excellent and 3 intermediate responders also harbored PTEN deep deletions or recurrent missense variants (R15S, G132C, Y177H), indicating PTEN loss does not preclude BRAFi response.
- **Elevated BRAF-mutant allele fraction marks excellent responders:** significantly enriched in excellent responders (P < .001) versus poor responders (P = .02), with FACETS confirming allelic imbalance in two excellent responders; consistent with selective amplification of mutant BRAF or loss of the wild-type allele as a sensitizing event.
- **[CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) does NOT predict response:** 21/51 (41%) BRAFi-monotherapy patients had CDKN2A alterations (19 putative loss-of-function), but neither mutation nor homozygous deletion correlated with response grade — contradicting an earlier report ([Nathanson et al. 2013, Clin Cancer Res 19:4868](https://doi.org/10.1158/1078-0432.CCR-13-0827)).
- **Cohort vs. TCGA comparison:** [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) (P = .0036) and [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) (P = .045) alterations were enriched in the MSK/Vanderbilt cohort relative to TCGA, likely reflecting the more aggressive clinical profile.
- **MAPK-pathway escape mutations rare in pretreatment tumors:** 0/66 RAS mutations; 1 patient had an [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) splice variant (X2441_splice) and was a poor responder; 2 [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md) mutations (P124S in an intermediate responder on BRAFi monotherapy; Q56P at 0.04 allele frequency in an intermediate responder on BRAFi + MEKi, indicating a subclonal population).
- **PI3K-pathway hits beyond PTEN:** 4 [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) missense mutations (3 hotspots: V344G, E545K, H1047R), present in both excellent and poor responders; one [AKT3](../genes/AKT3.md) E17K hotspot in a poor responder treated with BRAFi + MEKi; no hotspot AKT mutations in BRAFi-monotherapy patients.
- **Other notable alterations:** BRAF amplification in 3 tumors (all intermediate responders) — BRAFi response is not precluded by BRAF amplification, contrasting with its known role in acquired resistance; [MITF](../genes/MITF.md) amplifications (n = 3) seen exclusively in poor/intermediate responders (not statistically significant); 4 [KDR](../genes/KDR.md) mutations (1 hotspot R1032Q, 3 VUS), all in poor responders, with significantly shorter PFS and OS in KDR-mutant patients; [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) + [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) alterations in 13/51 (25%) of BRAFi-monotherapy patients did not correlate with response grade or survival.
- **Co-mutation landscape:** [PTPRT](../genes/PTPRT.md), [PTPRD](../genes/PTPRD.md), and [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) were among the most commonly co-altered genes with BRAF, but most variants were missense VUS, consistent with passenger events given the high mutation burden of melanoma.

## Genes & alterations

- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — V600E/K confirmed in all 66 tumors; amplification in 3 (all intermediate responders); elevated mutant-allele fraction (more than 2× the median variant fraction) in 9 patients and significantly enriched in excellent responders (P < .001 vs. poor).
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — loss-of-function alterations (deep deletions, truncations, C2-domain P204S, plus VUS) are the headline biomarker of intrinsic [BRAFi](../drugs/vemurafenib.md) resistance: enriched in poor responders (P = .005) and associated with shorter PFS (HR 3.46) and OS (HR 3.10).
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — altered in 21/51 (41%) BRAFi-monotherapy patients (19 LOF), but no association with response grade, contradicting prior reports.
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — 4 missense (3 hotspots: V344G, E545K, H1047R); present in excellent and poor responders alike; one excellent responder carried the activating H1047R.
- [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md), [AKT2](../genes/AKT2.md) — no hotspot mutations observed in 51 BRAFi-monotherapy patients.
- [AKT3](../genes/AKT3.md) — one E17K hotspot in a poor responder on BRAFi + MEKi.
- [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md), [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md), [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md) — rare non-recurrent VUS in both excellent and poor responders.
- [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) — 0 RAS mutations across all 66 pretreatment tumors.
- [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) — one splice variant (X2441_splice) in a poor responder, consistent with preclinical models of NF1-loss BRAFi resistance.
- [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md) (MEK1) — P124S (BRAFi monotherapy intermediate responder); Q56P at 0.04 allele frequency (subclonal) in BRAFi + MEKi intermediate responder; both previously linked to context-dependent BRAFi/MEKi resistance.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md), [MDM4](../genes/MDM4.md) — 25% of BRAFi-monotherapy patients harbored functional TP53 or MDM2 events (TP53 in 8; MDM2 amplification in 4 and mutation in 1) but no association with response grade or survival.
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) — alterations or amplifications in 11% of BRAFi-monotherapy patients; no association with response.
- [MITF](../genes/MITF.md) — 3 amplifications, exclusively in poor/intermediate responders (not statistically significant).
- [KDR](../genes/KDR.md) (VEGFR2) — 4 mutations (1 hotspot R1032Q, 3 VUS), all in poor responders, with significantly shorter PFS and OS.
- [PTPRT](../genes/PTPRT.md), [PTPRD](../genes/PTPRD.md), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) — frequently co-mutated with BRAF, but most variants were VUS likely representing passenger events given the high tumor mutational burden of melanoma.

## Clinical implications

- **Pretreatment biomarker:** the authors argue that PTEN loss-of-function alterations identified by capture-based sequencing of pretreatment tumors mark patients with [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)-mutant [melanoma](../cancer_types/SKCM.md) who are unlikely to derive durable benefit from [vemurafenib](../drugs/vemurafenib.md)/[dabrafenib](../drugs/dabrafenib.md) monotherapy ([PMID:32913971](../papers/32913971.md)).
- **Combination strategy:** the results provide a rationale for combining BRAFi (+/- MEKi) with [PI3K/AKT-pathway inhibitors](../drugs/alpelisib.md) in patients with concurrent BRAF and PTEN alterations; the authors further suggest such combinations might extend response depth/duration even in PTEN wild-type patients because complete responses are uncommon.
- **BRAF-mutant allele fraction as a candidate predictive marker:** elevated mutant-allele fraction (selective gain of mutant BRAF or loss of the wild-type allele) tracks with excellent response; the authors call for broader whole-exome/whole-genome sequencing of BRAF-mutant tumors to test this prospectively.
- **BRAF amplification does not preclude response:** in contrast to acquired resistance, pretreatment BRAF amplification (n = 3) did not predict primary BRAFi failure.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Small cohort, low statistical power for rare events:** 66 patients is too few to define associations for genes that are infrequently altered in melanoma.
- **Standard of care has shifted:** since accrual, BRAFi + MEKi combinations are now standard, and PTEN's predictive value in the combination setting is not well characterized here (only 11 patients received the combination, with no formal response-grade analysis powered against PTEN status).
- **Targeted panel misses non-coding and epigenetic events:** the >250-gene capture panel cannot detect PTEN expression changes from epigenetic silencing, structural variants outside captured intervals, or alterations in genes not on the panel.
- **Pretreatment sampling limitations:** variability between sample collection and BRAFi initiation; single-site sampling cannot capture intratumoral or lesion-to-lesion heterogeneity. The authors are exploring cell-free DNA / plasma profiling as a follow-up modality.
- **PTEN-alteration heterogeneity in responders:** because two excellent and three intermediate responders also harbored PTEN deep deletions or recurrent missense variants, PTEN loss is not a deterministic predictor — additional cooperating events must contribute to intrinsic resistance.
- **Open question — allelic imbalance as a predictive marker:** does selective gain of mutant BRAF / loss of wild-type BRAF prospectively identify patients who will derive complete and durable responses? Whole-genome sequencing or zygosity-resolved assays are needed to validate.
