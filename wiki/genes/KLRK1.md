# KLRK1

## Overview

KLRK1 encodes NKG2D, an activating C-type lectin receptor expressed on NK cells, CD8+ T cells, and NKT cells. NKG2D-mediated cytotoxicity is a key mechanism of immune surveillance against stressed or transformed cells. In [HGSOC](../cancer_types/HGSOC.md), declining NKG2D (KLRK1) expression marks progressive NK cell dysfunction.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- KLRK1 (NKG2D) expression declined in STIC.C and invasive cancer stages in HGSOC fallopian tube specimens, indicating progressive NK cell dysfunction; NK cells became nearly undetectable in STIC.I, STIC.C, and cancer (median 0.02% of cells vs 0.1% in normal/p53.I) [PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HGSOC](../cancer_types/HGSOC.md) — KLRK1 expression loss marks progressive NK cell dysfunction from STIC stages through invasive cancer; studied in 44 FFPE fallopian tube specimens from 43 individuals using CyCIF (31-antibody panel) [PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- KLRK1 decline co-occurs with progressive [HLA-E](../genes/HLA-E.md) upregulation in HGSOC precursors, consistent with NKG2A-mediated inhibition of NK cell cytotoxicity overriding NKG2D activation [PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- NK cell reactivation strategies that restore NKG2D-mediated surveillance (e.g., anti-NKG2A antibodies such as [monalizumab](../drugs/monalizumab.md)) could counteract the progressive NK cell dysfunction evidenced by declining KLRK1 expression in HGSOC [PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md).

## Open questions

- Whether the decline in KLRK1 expression reflects loss of NK cells, NK cell exhaustion, or transcriptional downregulation within surviving NK cells in HGSOC precursors is unresolved [PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md).
