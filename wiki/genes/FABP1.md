# FABP1

## Overview

FABP1 (fatty acid binding protein 1, liver type) is highly expressed in hepatocytes and [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md). It competitively sequesters long-chain fatty acids away from tumor-infiltrating T cells, contributing to T-cell dysfunction and immune evasion in HCC.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- FABP1 upregulated in HCC; outcompetes T cells for long-chain fatty acids, impairing T-cell function and contributing to immunosuppression in the tumor microenvironment [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) — FABP1 upregulation drives T-cell lipid deprivation and immune evasion [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Part of the FABP/lipid-transport upregulation axis with [FABP4](../genes/FABP4.md) and [FABP5](../genes/FABP5.md) in HCC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Targeting FABP1 to restore T-cell fatty acid availability is proposed as an immune-sensitization strategy in HCC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Open questions

- Selective FABP1 inhibitors for use in the liver context are not yet validated.
