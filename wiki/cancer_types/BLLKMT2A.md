# B-Lymphoblastic Leukemia/Lymphoma with t(v;11q23.3);KMT2A Rearranged (BLLKMT2A)

## Overview

B-Lymphoblastic Leukemia/Lymphoma with [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) (MLL) rearrangement is a distinct aggressive subtype of ALL, most prevalent in infants where it carries a poor prognosis. It is defined by the presence of a translocation involving the [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) gene at chromosome 11q23.

## Cohorts in the corpus

- **[all_stjude_2015](../datasets/all_stjude_2015.md):** Comprehensive profiling of 47 infant cases and 20 older child cases with MLL-R ALL [PMID:25730765](../papers/25730765.md).

## Recurrent alterations

- **[KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md):** Rearrangements (11q23 translocations) are the defining primary driver [PMID:25730765](../papers/25730765.md).
- **[FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md):** Activating mutations found in 19% of infant MLL-R cases; frequently sub-clonal [PMID:25730765](../papers/25730765.md).
- **[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) / [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md):** RAS pathway mutations found in ~28% of cases combined [PMID:25730765](../papers/25730765.md).

## Subtypes

- **Infant vs. Older Child MLL-R ALL:** Infant cases have an exceedingly low somatic mutation rate (mean 1.3 non-silent mutations), whereas older children have a significantly higher burden (mean 6.5) and frequent epigenetic regulator mutations (45%) [PMID:25730765](../papers/25730765.md).

## Therapeutic landscape

- Signaling pathway mutations (Kinase/PI3K/RAS) are frequently lost at relapse, suggesting that standard treatments may fail to eliminate the founder [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md)-rearranged clone [PMID:25730765](../papers/25730765.md).
