# GLI3

## Overview

GLI3 is a bifunctional transcription factor in the Hedgehog (Hh) signaling pathway, acting as both an activator and a repressor. In PDAC, GLI3 alterations contribute to Hedgehog-pathway dysregulation alongside other pathway members.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Hedgehog-pathway alteration in PDAC (alongside [GLI2](../genes/GLI2.md), [SMO](../genes/SMO.md), [PTCH1](../genes/PTCH1.md), [LRP2](../genes/LRP2.md)) [PMID:25855536](../papers/25855536.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md)**: Hedgehog pathway alteration contributing to the landscape of actionable genomic lesions in PDAC [PMID:25855536](../papers/25855536.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-altered with [SMO](../genes/SMO.md), [GLI2](../genes/GLI2.md), [PTCH1](../genes/PTCH1.md), and [LRP2](../genes/LRP2.md) as part of Hedgehog-pathway lesions in PDAC [PMID:25855536](../papers/25855536.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Hedgehog-pathway alteration nominates [SMO](../genes/SMO.md) inhibitors as therapeutic candidates in PDAC [PMID:25855536](../papers/25855536.md)

## Open questions

- Whether GLI3 alterations in PDAC activate or impair Hh output, and whether they predict response to Hh-pathway inhibitors.
