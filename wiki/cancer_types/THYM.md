# Thymoma (THYM)

## Overview

Thymoma encompasses WHO types A, AB, B1, B2, and B3 thymic epithelial tumors; in OncoTree THYM is a child of TET under THYMUS. Indolent types A/AB are defined by a recurrent GTF2I hotspot mutation (p.Leu404His) present in ~78% of combined type A/AB cases, distinguishing them molecularly from the more aggressive B-type thymomas and thymic carcinomas.

## Cohorts in the corpus

- [tet_nci_2014](../datasets/tet_nci_2014.md) — 286 patients including the major thymoma cohort; referenced in [PMID:24974848](../papers/24974848.md).

## Recurrent alterations

- GTF2I p.Leu404His (chr7:74146970 T>A) in 82% of type A, 74% of type AB, 78% combined A/AB thymomas (n=270 TETs total); associated with 96% 10-year disease-related survival vs 70% for GTF2I wild-type (P<0.001); GTF2I mutation was enriched in early-stage (I–II, 57%) vs advanced (III–IV, 19%) disease [PMID:24974848](../papers/24974848.md).
- Mutation burden is significantly lower in thymomas than thymic carcinomas (mean 18.4 vs 43.5 coding mutations per sample; P=0.001) [PMID:24974848](../papers/24974848.md).

## Subtypes

- Type A/AB: most indolent; GTF2I-mutation dominant; almost no copy-number aberrations.
- Type B1/B2/B3: intermediate; GTF2I prevalence decreases with increasing aggressiveness.

## Therapeutic landscape

- No drug pages currently linked for THYM in this corpus. GTF2I mutation status is proposed as a complement to WHO histological classification for prognostic stratification.
