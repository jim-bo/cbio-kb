# DNM2

## Overview

DNM2 (Dynamin 2) encodes a large GTPase involved in membrane tubulation and vesicle formation. In cancer genomics, DNM2 has been identified as a putative fusion partner in structural rearrangements, and its disruption may affect vesicle trafficking and cytokinesis pathways.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Putative fusion partner with [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) (SMARCA4–DNM2 fusion) detected by whole-genome sequencing in esophageal adenocarcinoma [PMID:23525077](../papers/23525077.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Esophageal adenocarcinoma: detected as a structural fusion partner by WGS [PMID:23525077](../papers/23525077.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- SMARCA4–DNM2 fusion observed in the context of SWI/SNF chromatin-remodeling gene disruption in EAC [PMID:23525077](../papers/23525077.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic data in the corpus.

## Open questions

- Functional significance and frequency of the SMARCA4–DNM2 fusion in EAC remain to be determined.
