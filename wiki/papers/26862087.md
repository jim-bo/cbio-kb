**PMID:** 26862087 · **DOI:** 10.1158/1940-6207.CAPR-15-0316 · **Journal:** Cancer Prevention Research (2016)

## TL;DR

Rettig et al. performed whole-genome sequencing of 25 fresh-frozen salivary-gland adenoid cystic carcinomas (ACCs) with matched normal tissue, plus mRNA expression analysis of 17 of those tumors. Beyond the canonical t(6;9) [MYB](../genes/MYB.md)–[NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) fusion (44%), they uncovered five additional rearrangements involving [NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) (with [MAP3K5](../genes/MAP3K5.md), [MYBL1](../genes/MYBL1.md), [RIMS1](../genes/RIMS1.md), [MYO6](../genes/MYO6.md), and [RPS6KA2](../genes/RPS6KA2.md)), pushing total [NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) translocation prevalence to 60% (15/25). [NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) mRNA was overexpressed in tumors versus normal tissue (p=0.002) regardless of fusion status, suggesting an [MYB](../genes/MYB.md)-independent oncogenic role for [NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) in [ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md). The study also reports recurrent somatic mutations in Rho-family GTPase signaling (44% of tumors, p=3.9×10⁻⁶) and axon-guidance signaling (56%, p=8.3×10⁻⁵) pathways, and corroborates earlier findings of chromatin-regulator and Notch-pathway disruption.

## Cohort & data

- **n=25** fresh-frozen surgically resected ACC tumors with matched blood/normal tissue, accrued under IRB at Johns Hopkins Hospital and the Salivary Gland Tumor Biorepository ([PMID:26862087](../papers/26862087.md)).
- Cancer type: salivary-gland [ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md) (adenoid cystic carcinoma); tumor sites included parotid, submandibular, sublingual, minor salivary glands of palate/maxilla/lip/septum/ear (Table 1).
- Neoplastic cellularity ≥60% (mean) achieved via macro-dissection from frozen-section histology.
- Dataset: [acyc_jhu_2016](../datasets/acyc_jhu_2016.md) (genomic and mRNA data deposition described as "in process" at time of publication).
- Assays: [whole-genome-seq](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) on all 25 tumor/normal pairs (mean coverage 65.2× tumor / 38.1× normal, 98.2% of targets ≥10×); [rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) on 17 tumors (Illumina HiSeq 2000, ≥50M paired 100×100 bp reads/sample).
- Bioinformatics: [variantdx](../methods/variantdx.md) custom somatic-mutation caller (matched tumor/normal, hg19), [tophat-fusion](../methods/tophat-fusion.md) for fusion-transcript discovery, [rsem](../methods/rsem.md) for transcript quantification, snpEff for functional annotation, [ingenuity-pathway-analysis](../methods/ingenuity-pathway-analysis.md) for pathway enrichment.

## Key findings

- **396 somatic mutations in 372 genes** across 25 tumors, median 14 mutations/tumor (range 2–36) ([PMID:26862087](../papers/26862087.md)).
- **253 chromosomal rearrangements** total (median 7/tumor, range 0–42), with breakpoint hotspots on chromosome 6q (28% of breakpoints) and 9p (14%).
- **MYB-NFIB fusion** detected in 11/25 tumors (44%, 95% CI 27–63%) by [whole-genome-seq](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md).
- **Five novel/expanded NFIB fusion partners** identified: [MAP3K5](../genes/MAP3K5.md)–[NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) (2 tumors), [MYBL1](../genes/MYBL1.md)–[NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) (2 tumors), [RPS6KA2](../genes/RPS6KA2.md)–[NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) (1), [MYO6](../genes/MYO6.md)–[NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) (1), [RIMS1](../genes/RIMS1.md)–[NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) (1). Three tumors carried both MYB-NFIB and a non-MYB NFIB fusion. Total NFIB translocation prevalence: **15/25 (60%, 95% CI 41–77%)**.
- **NFIB intrachromosomal rearrangements** in 3 additional tumors (2 deletions, 1 inversion), including HN 324 which lacked any NFIB translocation.
- mRNA confirmation: 9/9 tumors with MYB-NFIB DNA rearrangement and available RNA expressed the fusion transcript; one extra tumor (HN 335 PT) had MYB-NFIB transcript without detected DNA rearrangement; only 2/6 non-MYB NFIB rearrangements (RIMS1-NFIB, MYBL1-NFIB) yielded detectable fusion transcripts.
- **NFIB overexpressed** in tumors vs. normal tissue (p=0.002), with **no difference** between NFIB-fusion-positive and -negative tumors (p=0.91).
- **MYB overexpressed** in tumors vs. normal (p<0.001), and higher in MYB-NFIB-fusion-positive than -negative tumors (p<0.001). The two MYBL1-NFIB tumors (HN 333 PT, HN 320 PT) uniquely lacked MYB expression and trended toward higher MYBL1 expression (p=0.07), consistent with mutually exclusive MYB/MYBL1 alterations.
- **Most-mutated gene:** [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) (4 mutations in 3 tumors; nonsense, missense, frameshift, in-frame deletion). [NOTCH2](../genes/NOTCH2.md) also carried a non-synonymous substitution (Table 2).
- **Chromatin-regulator mutations** in [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) (MLL2; 3 mutations/2 tumors), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) (MLL3), [EP300](../genes/EP300.md), [SMARCA2](../genes/SMARCA2.md), [SMARCC1](../genes/SMARCC1.md), and [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md); truncating mutations were enriched (6 of 11 non-silent) at p=3.8×10⁻⁶ vs. randomization-based null. Individually significant: MLL2 (p=0.008) and EP300 (p=0.01).
- **Rho-family GTPase signaling** disrupted in 11/25 tumors (44%, p=3.9×10⁻⁶ by IPA), including [RHOA](../genes/RHOA.md) (2 tumors with distinct mutations), [CDH2](../genes/CDH2.md), [CDH13](../genes/CDH13.md), [ARHGEF3](../genes/ARHGEF3.md), [ARHGEF5](../genes/ARHGEF5.md), [ARHGEF18](../genes/ARHGEF18.md), [ACTB](../genes/ACTB.md) and others (Figure 4).
- **Axon-guidance signaling** disrupted in 14/25 tumors (56%, p=8.3×10⁻⁵), including [PLXNB1](../genes/PLXNB1.md) (2), [PRKD1](../genes/PRKD1.md) (2), [SLIT1](../genes/SLIT1.md), [PLXNA1](../genes/PLXNA1.md), [SRGAP2](../genes/SRGAP2.md), [SRGAP3](../genes/SRGAP3.md), [ADAM2](../genes/ADAM2.md), [ADAMTS5](../genes/ADAMTS5.md), [ADAMTS13](../genes/ADAMTS13.md), [SEMA3G](../genes/SEMA3G.md) (one each). An identical [SEMA3G](../genes/SEMA3G.md) 474S>P substitution was also observed in the prior Ho et al. cohort ([PMID:23685749](../papers/23685749.md)).
- **41 copy-number variations** in 12/25 tumors (range 0–9). Most common: amplifications at 7p14.1 (5 tumors) and 14q11.2 (4 tumors) — both T-cell-receptor loci, possibly artifactual; deletions at 10q26.3 spanning [DUX4](../genes/DUX4.md)/DUX4L homeobox genes in 3 tumors.
- Pathway-level disruption (mutations + rearrangements) corroborated prior reports for protein-kinase-A signaling (21 tumors), FGF signaling (15), Wnt/β-catenin (13), PI3K/AKT (13), and Notch (9).

## Genes & alterations

- **[NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md)** — translocations in 15/25 (60%) tumors with five distinct partners ([MYB](../genes/MYB.md), [MAP3K5](../genes/MAP3K5.md), [MYBL1](../genes/MYBL1.md), [RIMS1](../genes/RIMS1.md), [MYO6](../genes/MYO6.md), [RPS6KA2](../genes/RPS6KA2.md)); plus intrachromosomal rearrangements in 3 tumors. Overexpressed at the mRNA level vs. normal (p=0.002) **independent of fusion status** (p=0.91), implicating a fusion-independent oncogenic role.
- **[MYB](../genes/MYB.md)** — t(6;9) [MYB](../genes/MYB.md)–[NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) fusion in 11/25 tumors. Overexpressed in tumors vs. normal (p<0.001) and significantly higher in fusion-positive tumors (p<0.001).
- **[MYBL1](../genes/MYBL1.md)** — recurrent [MYBL1](../genes/MYBL1.md)–[NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) fusion in 2 tumors (HN 333 PT, HN 320 PT) which uniquely lacked MYB expression, supporting a mutually exclusive [MYB](../genes/MYB.md)/[MYBL1](../genes/MYBL1.md) alteration model in [ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md).
- **[MAP3K5](../genes/MAP3K5.md)** (ASK1) — recurrent fusion partner with [NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) in 2 tumors; encodes a serine/threonine kinase regulating apoptosis.
- **[RIMS1](../genes/RIMS1.md)**, **[MYO6](../genes/MYO6.md)**, **[RPS6KA2](../genes/RPS6KA2.md)** — single-tumor [NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) fusion partners on chromosome 6q.
- **[NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md)** — most-frequently mutated gene; 4 mutations in 3 tumors (nonsense, missense, frameshift, in-frame deletion) — 12% of cohort.
- **[NOTCH2](../genes/NOTCH2.md)** — non-synonymous substitution in one tumor.
- **[KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) (MLL2)** — 3 mutations in 2 tumors; significant enrichment of truncating mutations (p=0.008).
- **[KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) (MLL3)** — 2 mutations in 2 tumors.
- **[EP300](../genes/EP300.md)** — 2 mutations in 2 tumors; significant enrichment of truncating mutations (p=0.01).
- **[SMARCA2](../genes/SMARCA2.md)**, **[SMARCC1](../genes/SMARCC1.md)**, **[KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md)** — recurrent chromatin-regulator mutations.
- **[RHOA](../genes/RHOA.md)** — two distinct somatic mutations in 2 tumors; first reported in [ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md). (Recurrent RHOA hotspot mutations had previously been reported in angioimmunoblastic T-cell lymphoma; see [AITL](../cancer_types/AITL.md).)
- **[ARHGEF3](../genes/ARHGEF3.md)**, **[ARHGEF5](../genes/ARHGEF5.md)**, **[ARHGEF18](../genes/ARHGEF18.md)**, **[CDH2](../genes/CDH2.md)**, **[CDH13](../genes/CDH13.md)**, **[ACTB](../genes/ACTB.md)** — additional Rho-pathway mutations.
- **[PLXNB1](../genes/PLXNB1.md)**, **[PRKD1](../genes/PRKD1.md)** — recurrent (2 tumors each) axon-guidance mutations.
- **[PLXNA1](../genes/PLXNA1.md)**, **[SLIT1](../genes/SLIT1.md)**, **[SRGAP2](../genes/SRGAP2.md)**, **[SRGAP3](../genes/SRGAP3.md)**, **[SEMA3G](../genes/SEMA3G.md)**, **[ADAM2](../genes/ADAM2.md)**, **[ADAMTS5](../genes/ADAMTS5.md)**, **[ADAMTS13](../genes/ADAMTS13.md)** — single-tumor axon-guidance mutations.
- **[DUX4](../genes/DUX4.md)** — homozygous deletions at 10q26.3 in 3 tumors (interpreted with caution as DUX4/DUX4L are tandemly repeated).

## Clinical implications

- **[NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) as a therapeutic target.** Authors argue that the high prevalence of [NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) translocations (60% of cases) and fusion-independent [NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) overexpression suggest "targeting NFIB alone may also be of high yield," independent of [MYB](../genes/MYB.md). (Statement of hypothesis only; no functional/therapeutic data.)
- **Rho-pathway and axon-guidance pathway inhibition** are flagged as candidate therapeutic strategies for [ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md), leveraging existing precision-anticancer Rho-targeting research and Slit/Robo pathway exploration.
- **Notch pathway** disruption (12% of cases) is highlighted as a potentially targetable signaling axis, given existing Notch-targeting therapeutics in development.
- **Perineural invasion (PNI) link.** The disruption of axon-guidance signaling in 56% of tumors is hypothesis-generating for [ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md)'s well-known propensity for PNI; the same pathway is disrupted in pancreatic adenocarcinoma, another PNI-prone malignancy.
- No predictive biomarker, treatment outcome, or survival analysis is reported in this study (n=25, fresh-frozen discovery cohort).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Small cohort (n=25).** Statistical power for rare events and pathway enrichment is limited; novel single-tumor NFIB fusion partners (RPS6KA2, MYO6, RIMS1) are not corroborated by other ACC sequencing studies.
- **mRNA confirmation incomplete.** Only 2/6 non-MYB NFIB DNA rearrangements yielded detectable fusion transcripts, leaving the functional significance of those novel rearrangements unclear.
- **Functional role of NFIB overexpression unresolved.** NFIB mRNA is elevated in tumors regardless of fusion status (p=0.91 between groups), but the mechanism of dysregulation in fusion-negative tumors is not characterized.
- **T-cell-receptor amplifications interpreted with caution** as potential artifacts of TCR gene rearrangement in tumor-infiltrating lymphocytes.
- **No survival/outcome data**; cohort is descriptive genomic-discovery only.
- **DUX4 deletion calls** at 10q26.3 are flagged by the genomic context (tandem-repeat region) as needing orthogonal validation.
- **Therapeutic implications are speculative.** The paper proposes [NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md), Rho, axon-guidance, and Notch pathways as therapeutic targets but provides no in vitro or in vivo functional data; downstream functional characterization is called out as required.
- **Cross-paper synthesis:** authors corroborate chromatin-regulator and Notch findings from Ho et al. ([PMID:23685749](../papers/23685749.md)), Stephens et al. ([PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md)), and Ross et al. ([PMID:24418857](../papers/24418857.md)); the novel finding here is the pan-NFIB rearrangement landscape and the Rho-pathway involvement.
