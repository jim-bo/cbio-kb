# ELL

## Overview

ELL (elongation factor for RNA polymerase II) is a transcription elongation factor that forms oncogenic fusion proteins with [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) (MLL) through chromosomal translocations. KMT2A-ELL fusions are a well-characterized class of rearrangements observed in acute myeloid leukemia, particularly in therapy-related myeloid neoplasms following topoisomerase II inhibitor exposure.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- KMT2A translocation partner in pediatric neuroblastoma patients who developed therapy-related AML/MDS; KMT2A-ELL rearrangements were among the most common fusion events detected by RNA capture in pre-transformation bone marrow samples [PMID:35078859](../papers/35078859.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Therapy-related AML/MDS in pediatric neuroblastoma survivors: KMT2A-ELL fusion detected as a pre-transformation event via RNA capture-based bone marrow surveillance [PMID:35078859](../papers/35078859.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- KMT2A rearrangements (including ELL fusions) associated with topoisomerase-II exposure; co-occur in the same patient populations as KMT2A-AFF1, KMT2A-FRYL, KMT2A-GAS7, and novel KMT2A-PRDM10/DDX6 fusions [PMID:35078859](../papers/35078859.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- KMT2A-ELL-driven therapy-related myeloid neoplasms detected a median of 15 months before clinical leukemia diagnosis by RNA capture-based surveillance; early detection supports intervention planning [PMID:35078859](../papers/35078859.md)

## Open questions

- Whether targeted intervention at the pre-transformation stage (ELL fusion detectable but clinical leukemia absent) improves outcomes in pediatric neuroblastoma survivors remains to be prospectively studied [PMID:35078859](../papers/35078859.md)
