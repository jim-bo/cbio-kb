# GRHL2

## Overview

GRHL2 (Grainyhead-Like Transcription Factor 2) is a transcription factor with roles in epithelial differentiation, barrier function, and wound healing. In primary cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma (CSCC) it has been proposed as a driver gene; however, in regional metastatic CSCC only a single splice variant was observed in a WGS cohort, suggesting a limited driver role in the metastatic setting.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Single splice variant observed in a WGS cohort of 25 regional metastatic [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md) samples; frequency is not sufficient to call GRHL2 a recurrent metastatic driver, in contrast to its proposed role in primary CSCC [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md):** Limited mutational activity in regional lymph node metastases; one splice variant in 25 WGS samples — suggesting GRHL2's driver role may be confined primarily to the primary tumor setting rather than metastatic progression [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic implications reported in this corpus.

## Open questions

- Whether GRHL2 loss or dysregulation is a prerequisite for primary CSCC oncogenesis but is dispensable in metastatic disease remains unclear from this WGS cohort [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md).
