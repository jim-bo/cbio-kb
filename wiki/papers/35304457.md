**PMID:** 35304457 · **DOI:** 10.1038/s41467-022-28848-x · **Journal:** Nature Communications (2022)

## TL;DR

Rosen et al. characterize molecular determinants of response and resistance to the selective [RET](../genes/RET.md) inhibitor [selpercatinib](../drugs/selpercatinib.md) in 72 patients with *RET* fusion- or mutation-positive lung and thyroid cancers enrolled on the registrational phase 1/2 LIBRETTO-001 trial (NCT03157128) at Memorial Sloan Kettering. Pre-treatment genomic profile did not predict initial response (except for rare RAS-mediated primary resistance), but acquired resistance was driven by MAPK pathway reactivation through two main routes: on-target secondary *RET* solvent-front mutations or *[MET](../genes/MET.md)* amplifications, and off-target selection of *RET*-wild-type subclones harboring alternative mitogenic drivers (*[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)*, *[NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md)*, *[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)*, *[FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md)*). Multiple distinct resistance mechanisms frequently co-existed in the same patient, indicating polyclonal resistance is common in RET-driven cancers.

## Cohort & data

- 72 patients with *RET* activating fusion or mutation treated with selpercatinib on LIBRETTO-001 (NCT03157128) at Memorial Sloan Kettering; 70 included in correlative genomic analyses after two were lost to follow-up.
- Cancer types: lung adenocarcinoma ([LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md)) predominated among fusion-positive cancers (81%), followed by papillary thyroid ([THPA](../cancer_types/THPA.md), 12%); also large-cell neuroendocrine lung ([LCNEC](../cancer_types/LCNEC.md)), lung adenosquamous ([LUAS](../cancer_types/LUAS.md)), high-grade neuroendocrine rectal carcinoma ([HGNEC](../cancer_types/HGNEC.md)), poorly differentiated thyroid, and non-Langerhans cell histiocytosis. All *RET*-mutant cancers were medullary thyroid carcinoma ([THME](../cancer_types/THME.md)).
- Dataset: [mixed_selpercatinib_2020](../datasets/mixed_selpercatinib_2020.md).
- Assays: tumor tissue [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) targeted sequencing (up to 468 genes, n=51 pre-treatment + 9 post-progression matched pairs); [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) on 44 pre-treatment tumors; pre-treatment plasma cfDNA via [MSK-ACCESS](../methods/msk-access.md) (n=68); longitudinal cfDNA on 23 patients with 18 progressing patients sequenced post-progression.
- Data freeze: December 2019. Enrollment between May 2017 and December 2019. Response assessed by RECIST 1.1.

## Key findings

- Selpercatinib was active independent of tissue of origin, alteration class (fusion vs mutation), or prior multikinase-inhibitor exposure; investigator-assessed RECIST 1.1 ORR was 67% in *RET* fusion-positive and 58% in *RET*-mutant cancers, with 82% of responders ongoing at 1 year.
- 19 of 72 patients received selpercatinib as first-line; of 53 previously treated, 29 (55%) had prior multikinase inhibitor (MKI) therapy ([vandetanib](../drugs/vandetanib.md), [cabozantinib](../drugs/cabozantinib.md), or RXDX-105). Prior MKI exposure did not alter outcomes (HR=1.2, 95% CI 0.6–2.6, P=0.6).
- Pre-treatment tumor mutational burden and subclonal heterogeneity were not associated with depth or durability of response (P>0.4 for all comparisons).
- Concurrent *[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)* mutations (n=8, all in fusion-positive cancers) were associated with shorter median PFS on selpercatinib (HR=3.5, 95% CI 1.3–9.7, P=0.016).
- Pre-treatment plasma profiling detected the qualifying *RET* alteration in 95% (18/19) of *RET*-mutant cases and 74% (36/49) of fusion-positive cases. Patients with no or low pre-treatment cfDNA shedding had lower baseline disease burden (P=0.02) and superior PFS on selpercatinib (HR=3.7, 95% CI 1.1–12.4, P=0.013).
- In 23 longitudinally followed patients, the qualifying *RET* mutant allele frequency in plasma decreased by a median of 82.5% upon selpercatinib initiation, supporting cfDNA as a surrogate for target engagement.
- Four MTC patients previously treated with MKIs harbored coincident secondary *RET* mutations (V804M ×2, G601E, K666E, D898Y) in baseline plasma at low frequencies (0.65–7%); all decreased with selpercatinib, indicating these prior-therapy-acquired alleles remained selpercatinib-sensitive.
- Pre-existing PI3K pathway alterations (*[PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md)* loss-of-function in 2 tumor-tissue cases, additional *PTEN*/*[PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)* mutations in 9 plasma cases) did not preclude clinical benefit (CBR 91%).
- Primary resistance: 2 patients had pre-existing *KRAS* G12D and G12V mutations detected only in baseline plasma (not tissue) at low allele frequencies; these *KRAS*-mutant subclones grew out while the *RET* fusion-positive clone responded, indicating inter-tumoral heterogeneity of mitogenic drivers.
- Acquired resistance was identified in 11/18 evaluable progressing patients (61%). Mechanisms:
  - **On-target** (n=3): secondary *RET* mutations including solvent-front G810C, G810S, and compound cis mutants (e.g. M918T/V804M/Y806C triple mutant); 293T expression experiments confirmed the triple cis mutant abrogated selpercatinib inhibition of pRET and pERK.
  - **Off-target / bypass** (n=7): acquired *KRAS* (G12A/R/V, G13D, A59del), *NRAS* (G13D, Q61R), *BRAF* activating mutations, or *MET* / *FGFR1* amplifications.
- One patient with subclonal pre-existing *MET* amplification responded to combination selpercatinib + [crizotinib](../drugs/crizotinib.md) at progression, but an emerging *BRAF* D594N (kinase-dead, RAS- and BRAF-dimer–dependent) clone was detected during combination therapy, reinforcing MAPK dependence of *RET*-altered tumors.
- Polyclonal resistance was common: two patients had co-occurring *RET* solvent-front mutations and *KRAS* G12A/G12R mutations emerging at distinct timepoints in plasma, indicating distinct resistant subclones.
- No evidence of lineage/histology change as a mechanism of acquired resistance was found in three evaluable fusion-positive lung cancers with post-progression biopsies.

## Genes & alterations

- [RET](../genes/RET.md) — activating fusions (KIF5B-RET most common, plus CCDC6-RET, NCOA4-RET, TAF3-RET, ERC1-RET, RBPMS-RET, CLIP1-RET, EML4-RET, KIF13A-RET, KIAA1468-RET, RUFY3-RET) and kinase-domain mutations (germline and somatic; e.g. M918T) define trial-qualifying drivers. Acquired solvent-front G810C/G810S, gatekeeper V804M, Y806C and compound cis mutants (M918T/V804M/Y806C) drive on-target resistance.
- [KIF5B](../genes/KIF5B.md), [CCDC6](../genes/CCDC6.md), [NCOA4](../genes/NCOA4.md) — recurrent *RET* fusion partners retaining full kinase domain.
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — G12D/G12V pre-existing primary resistance (subclonal in plasma); acquired G12A/G12R/G12V, G13D, A59del at progression in fusion-positive lung cancers.
- [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) — acquired G13D, Q61R at progression.
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — acquired activating mutations including kinase-dead D594N (RAS- and BRAF-dimer–dependent) emerging during selpercatinib + crizotinib combination therapy.
- [MET](../genes/MET.md) — focal amplifications mediating bypass resistance; one patient with subclonal pre-treatment *MET* amplification re-responded to selpercatinib + crizotinib.
- [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) — focal amplification mediating MAPK reactivation in an oligo-progressing site.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — concurrent mutations in 8 fusion-positive cases linked to shorter PFS (HR=3.5).
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — PI3K pathway lesions present at baseline did not preclude response; PI3K activating mutations decreased on therapy alongside *RET*, inconsistent with a primary driver of resistance.
- [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md) C121S — observed among acquired MAPK-activating alterations.
- [ALK](../genes/ALK.md), [ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md), [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) — referenced as analogous oncogene-addicted systems where compound on-target mutations and bypass signaling drive resistance to selective inhibitors.

## Clinical implications

- Selpercatinib is broadly active in *RET*-driven cancers across tissue of origin and alteration class; pre-treatment co-mutation profiling has limited predictive value, except *TP53* co-mutations (shorter PFS) and rare *RAS*-mediated primary resistance.
- Plasma cfDNA ([MSK-ACCESS](../methods/msk-access.md)) is informative for both pre-treatment risk stratification (low shedders have better PFS) and longitudinal target engagement (median 82.5% decrease in qualifying *RET* mutant allele frequency on therapy).
- At selpercatinib progression, prospective molecular characterization (tissue + plasma) is warranted because polyclonal resistance is common: ≥1 patient often harbors both on-target *RET* mutations and off-target MAPK reactivation through distinct subclones.
- Sequential single-agent next-generation RET TKI strategy (analogous to [ALK](../genes/ALK.md)) may be insufficient because of MAPK-pathway-driven bypass; combination therapy targeting MET, RAS-MAPK effectors, or solvent-front–competent next-generation RET inhibitors will likely be required.
- Selpercatinib + [crizotinib](../drugs/crizotinib.md) can re-induce response in *MET*-amplified bypass resistance, but new MAPK-activating clones (e.g. *BRAF* D594N) can emerge under combination pressure.
- PI3K pathway co-alterations do not preclude selpercatinib benefit; clinical benefit rate in PI3K-co-altered patients was 91%.

## Limitations & open questions

- Small cohort (72 patients; 18 evaluable progressing patients with paired pre/post-progression material) limits statistical resolution of co-mutation effects beyond *TP53*.
- A genetic mechanism of resistance was identified in 11/18 (61%) of progressing patients with sequencing material — the remaining 7 (39%) lacked an identified discrete genetic driver, leaving non-genetic mechanisms (e.g. transcriptional adaptation, epigenetic) unexplored.
- Lineage plasticity was excluded only in three evaluable fusion-positive lung cancers with post-progression biopsies; broader sampling needed.
- The functional significance of low-allele-frequency *RAS* mutations in baseline plasma (sub-clonal mitogenic drivers in a *RET*-naive context) requires prospective validation.
- Whether next-generation selpercatinib-successor RET inhibitors active against solvent-front G810 mutations can overcome compound cis mutants (M918T/V804M/Y806C) is not addressed clinically.
- Optimal combination strategies (RET + MEK, RET + MET, RET + pan-RAS) at the time of monotherapy progression remain to be tested in trials.
