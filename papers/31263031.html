Genomic correlates of disease progression and treatment response in prospectively characterized gliomas

Authors

Philip Jonsson

Andrew L. Lin

Robert J. Young

Natalie M. DiStefano

David M. Hyman

Bob T. Li

Michael F. Berger

Ahmet Zehir

Marc Ladanyi

David B. Solit

Ingo K. Mellinghoff

Barry S. Taylor

Doi

PMID: 31263031 · DOI: 10.1158/1078-0432.CCR-19-0032 · Journal: Clinical Cancer Research (2019)

TL;DR

Jonsson et al. integrated prospective clinical sequencing of 1,004 tumors (733 primary, 271 recurrent) from 923 adult glioma patients at Memorial Sloan Kettering with curated treatment and outcome data (MSK-IMPACT n=837; FoundationOne n=167) and deposited the cohort as glioma_mskcc_2019. They report (i) a 13% germline pathogenic-variant rate enriched for DNA-repair genes, (ii) cell-cycle alterations (chiefly CDKN2A/B loss) as IDH-status-independent drivers of malignant progression and contrast enhancement in 1p19q-intact astrocytomas, (iii) alkylator-induced hypermutation more common in IDH-mutant and MGMT-methylated tumors with cell-cycle defects likely preceding temozolomide exposure, and (iv) BRAF V600 responses to RAF/MEK inhibition conditioned on clonality, allele class, and co-mutational context.

Cohort & data

  • N = 923 patients / 1,004 tumors prospectively sequenced July 2013–July 2017 at MSKCC; 733 primary and 271 recurrent samples; 57 patients had matched pre/post-treatment tumors.
  • Cancer types: WHO 2016 diffuse glioma (DIFG) spanning IDH-mutant astrocytoma (grades II–IV), IDH-mutant 1p/19q co-deleted oligodendroglioma, IDH-WT and IDH-mutant glioblastoma, and rare/HGGNOS subtypes. 355 IDH-mutant vs. 649 IDH-WT tumors.
  • Assays: MSK-IMPACT paired tumor/normal (n=837) and FoundationOne tumor-only (n=167); both detect SNVs, indels, CNVs, fusions across cancer-associated coding exons.
  • Allele-specific copy number via FACETS v0.3.9 (75% threshold for 1p/19q codeletion, 90% threshold for chr7 gain / chr10 loss); BRAF clonality estimated from MSK-IMPACT purity-corrected CCF.
  • Mutational signatures inferred for samples with ≥5 mutations; alkylator-induced hypermutation called when mutational signatures signature 11 ≥50% and TMB exceeded 1.5 MAD above median (per platform).
  • MGMT promoter methylation available for 636 patients (pyrosequencing or methylation-specific real-time PCR after bisulfite conversion).
  • External reference cohorts: AACR GENIE (498 samples), TCGA (894), Foundation Medicine (739); re-annotated with VEP v88 via vcf2maf.
  • Data deposited in cBioPortal as glioma_mskcc_2019.

Key findings

  • Germline burden. 13% (103/764) of evaluable patients harbored a pathogenic or likely pathogenic germline variant; 28% (29/103) targeted DNA-repair effectors. High-penetrance syndromes were observed in NF1, TP53, PTEN, and RB1. Three patients carried BRCA2 P/LP alleles. Six patients carried germline MMR defects (two MSH2, two PMS1, one MSH6, one PMS2); two had biallelic somatic loss + MSI-pattern hypermutation consistent with Lynch syndrome. No association with age at diagnosis, pathological grade (P=0.3), or IDH status (P=0.7).
  • Novel hotspots. Combining the MSK cohort with retrospectively sequenced gliomas (n=3,130 total) identified 34 previously uncharacterized hotspots in EGFR and PDGFRA, and candidate driver mutations in MAP3K1 and SF3B1 — two genes not previously linked to glioma.
  • Cell-cycle alterations and contrast enhancement. In IDH-mutant tumors, cell-cycle pathway alterations were strongly enriched in MRI-enhancing 1p19q-intact tumors (44% enhancing vs. 8% non-enhancing, Bonferroni-corrected P<0.001) but absent in 1p19q-codeleted tumors (3% vs. 4%). Longitudinal sequencing of 22 IDH-mutant patients showed cell-cycle aberrations co-acquired with contrast enhancement only in astrocytomas (6/11) and not codeleted tumors (0/11, P=0.01).
  • Cell-cycle and progression. Among recurrent IDH-mutant 1p19q-intact grade II/III tumors with enhancement (n=65), cell-cycle alterations were independently associated with shorter progression-free survival (HR=2.6, 95% CI 1.6–5.7, log-rank P=0.02). Spatial sequencing of a multi-region IDH-mutant GBM showed an RB1 deletion confined to the enhancing component.
  • Alkylator-induced hypermutation. 38 hypermutated tumors (signature 11–driven) were identified; therapy-induced hypermutation was more common in IDH-mutant than IDH-WT alkylator-exposed tumors (29% vs. 12%, P=0.004) and in MGMT-methylated patients (30% vs. 10%, P=0.006). 31/38 (82%) harbored mutations in MMR effectors. In 1p19q-intact tumors, all astrocytic relapses with therapy-induced hypermutation (n=22) carried a cell-cycle alteration — 15/26 were focal CDKN2A/B deletions. In three patients with pre-treatment matched specimens, cell-cycle alterations preceded TMZ exposure and remained clonal through hypermutation.
  • Therapeutic actionability. 32% of patients (295/923) harbored a potentially actionable lesion (OncoKB levels 1–3); 20% of IDH-WT and 39% of IDH-mutant tumors carried an investigationally actionable alteration (P<0.001). 32 patients received approved/standard-of-care targeted therapy and 32 additional patients received a targeted agent on a preclinical basis; 60% of targeted-therapy recipients were IDH-WT GBMs.
  • BRAF biology by glioma subtype. Oncogenic BRAF mutations occurred in 29 samples (2% of cohort). BRAF V600 mutations were clonal and nearly exclusive to epithelioid IDH-WT GBMs or high-grade tumors arising from lower-grade lesions (17/17). Conventional IDH-WT GBMs had a mix of subclonal V600 and non-V600 class-3 alleles (G466, G469, L597; 5/10), preferentially associated with +7/–10 (4/6 vs. 2/13, P=0.05).
  • BRAF V600 response. 7 BRAF V600E gliomas received RAF/MEK/ERK or RET-directed therapy: 4 partial/near-complete responses (ongoing at 9–41 months), 2 stable disease. The one patient who progressed immediately had a subclonal V600E (only treated tumor with subclonal BRAF). One responder with 78% overall regression had a spinal metastasis that failed RAF inhibition and carried a CDKN2A homozygous deletion absent from responding lesions — echoing CDKN2A’s role in BRAF-inhibitor resistance in melanoma.

Genes & alterations

  • IDH1 R132 and IDH2 R140/R172 — define the IDH-mutant subgroup (355 tumors); not directly targeted in this study but anchor all subtype analyses. After the data freeze, OncoKB upgraded to level 2B for glioma; held at level 4 here for analytical consistency.
  • CDKN2A / CDKN2B — focal deletions are the dominant cell-cycle alteration driving malignant progression and contrast enhancement in 1p19q-intact gliomas; also tied to BRAF-inhibitor resistance in a treated patient. CDKN2A/B alterations were enriched in higher-grade IDH-WT disease.
  • RB1, CDK4 — alternative cell-cycle alterations; one patient with primary RB1 mutation and one with CDK4 amplification later acquired CDKN2A/B loss at recurrence and died within 11–15 months.
  • BRAF — V600 (clonal, epithelioid/secondary GBMs) vs. non-V600 class-3 (G466, G469, L597; subclonal in conventional GBMs). Allele class and clonality predict MAPK-inhibitor response.
  • EGFR, PDGFRA — 34 newly identified hotspots from a 3,130-sample meta-analysis; rare EGFR kinase-domain-retaining fusions identified.
  • FGFR3–TACC3 — fusions in 16 patients.
  • MGMT — promoter methylation associated with higher rate of alkylator-induced hypermutation (30% vs. 10%, P=0.006).
  • MSH2, MSH6, PMS1, PMS2 — germline MMR alleles; two cases had biallelic loss and Lynch-pattern MSI hypermutation. Somatic MMR mutations were observed in 31/38 (82%) hypermutated recurrences.
  • TP53, PTEN, NF1, BRCA2, RB1 — germline P/LP alleles tied to known heritable syndromes.
  • NF1, PTPN11 — preferentially altered in recurrent lower-grade IDH-WT tumors that started as grade II/III (P<0.05).
  • TERT promoter, CIC, FUBP1, ATRX — canonical glioma subtype-defining lesions; frequencies stable across primary vs. recurrent specimens.
  • MAP3K1, SF3B1 — candidate novel glioma drivers from the 3,130-sample meta-analysis.
  • ATM, DNMT3A, JAK2, SRSF2, STAG2, TET2 — clonal-hematopoiesis filter genes used to exclude CH-attributable variants from germline calls; JAK2 V617F removed wherever observed.
  • ATR, ERCC2, MUTYH — among “Other” DNA-repair germline P/LP alleles annotated in Fig. 1G.

Clinical implications

  • Cell-cycle alterations as a prognostic biomarker. CDKN2A/B (and broader cell-cycle pathway) loss in 1p19q-intact lower-grade gliomas is an IDH-status-independent marker of impending malignant progression and shorter PFS at recurrence (HR=2.6), supporting routine assessment in molecular pathology workflow alongside IDH/1p19q status.
  • Predictive biomarker for therapy-induced hypermutation. Pre-existing cell-cycle defects in 1p19q-intact tumors may flag patients at elevated risk of temozolomide-driven hypermutation upon recurrence — relevant to TMZ-versus-radiation sequencing decisions and to immunotherapy candidacy (hypermutated tumors are MMR-mutant).
  • Germline screening rationale. A 13% pathogenic germline rate (DNA-repair-enriched) argues for broader germline assessment in glioma, including Lynch-related MMR genes, BRCA2, and high-penetrance NF1/TP53/PTEN/RB1.
  • MAPK-pathway therapy in glioma. Clinical responses to RAF/MEK/ERK inhibitors (vemurafenib, dabrafenib, trametinib, cobimetinib) are conditioned by BRAF allele class (V600 vs. non-V600 class-3), mutation clonality, and co-mutational context (CDKN2A loss as a candidate resistance modifier). Subclonal V600E may not be a useful predictive biomarker.
  • IDH-directed therapy. Ivosidenib (approved in AML) is the canonical IDH inhibitor under glioma investigation; this cohort provides the molecular substrate (355 IDH-mutant tumors with longitudinal context) for assessing IDH-inhibitor response.

Limitations & open questions

  • Tumor-only FoundationOne samples (n=167) cannot be used for germline analysis, biasing the germline-prevalence denominator to 764 MSK-IMPACT patients.
  • Targeted-therapy responses are exploratory (n=7 BRAF V600 treated); the case for CDKN2A as a BRAF-inhibitor resistance modifier in glioma is hypothesis-generating and rests on one mixed-response patient.
  • Only three matched pre-/post-TMZ pairs were available to establish that cell-cycle alterations precede alkylator-induced hypermutation; the “biomarker of TMZ-susceptibility” interpretation needs validation in a larger longitudinal series.
  • OncoKB level-4 alterations (including EGFR amplification) were excluded from actionability counts, conservatively under-estimating molecular eligibility for trials.
  • Authors did not report tumor-infiltrating lymphocyte, neoantigen, or immune-checkpoint response data on hypermutated tumors despite the obvious immunotherapy implication.
  • IDH-WT lower-grade astrocytomas were a rare subset; cell-cycle association was demonstrated but underpowered.
  • Whether MGMT methylation interacts with cell-cycle status to gate alkylator-induced hypermutation (versus being marginally additive) is not formally tested.
