# BCL9L

## Overview

BCL9L encodes a WNT signaling co-factor that promotes beta-catenin-mediated transcriptional activation. In colorectal carcinoma, BCL9L is a newly significant driver gene; overexpression promotes intestinal tumor progression in mouse models. Its identification [expands](../methods/expands.md) the known set of WNT pathway drivers in CRC beyond the canonical APC/CTNNB1 axis.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Newly significant CRC driver; WNT co-factor; overexpression promotes intestinal tumor progression in mouse models (Brembeck et al. 2011); identified as significantly mutated in the DFCI CRC cohort (n=619) [PMID:27149842](../papers/27149842.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **COAD/READ (colorectal carcinoma):** Newly significant driver identified in a 619-sample WES cohort; WNT pathway co-factor role [PMID:27149842](../papers/27149842.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Identified alongside other WNT pathway drivers including [TCF7](../genes/TCF7.md) and known CRC drivers [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md), [RNF43](../genes/RNF43.md) in the same cohort [PMID:27149842](../papers/27149842.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- WNT pathway co-factor; potential target for WNT inhibitor strategies in CRC; no approved targeted therapy established for BCL9L specifically.

## Open questions

- Driver vs. passenger status requires further validation; the mechanism by which BCL9L mutations (rather than overexpression) promote CRC remains to be characterised [PMID:27149842](../papers/27149842.md)
