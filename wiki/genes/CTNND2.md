# CTNND2

## Overview

CTNND2 (delta-catenin) is a member of the p120-catenin subfamily that regulates cadherin-mediated cell adhesion and Wnt signaling. It is expressed predominantly in neural tissues and has been linked to cell motility and invasiveness. In glioblastoma, CTNND2 is upregulated specifically in EGFR-altered tumors, suggesting a role in the molecular reprogramming driven by EGFR activation.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- CTNND2 protein is upregulated in EGFR-altered GBM tumors in the CPTAC proteogenomic cohort (gbm_cptac_2021, n=99); co-elevated with CDK6 and CDKN2C specifically in the EGFR-altered subgroup (45/99 tumors with EGFR structural variants + amplification) [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **Glioblastoma (GBM):** CTNND2 upregulated in EGFR-altered tumors; co-occurs with CDK6 and CDKN2C elevation in this subgroup [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with CDK6 and CDKN2C protein upregulation in EGFR-altered GBM [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting reported for CTNND2 in the corpus.

## Open questions

- The functional role of CTNND2 upregulation in EGFR-altered GBM (cell adhesion, invasion, Wnt signaling) requires further investigation [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).
