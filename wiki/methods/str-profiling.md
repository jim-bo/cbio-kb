# STR Profiling

## Overview

Short tandem repeat (STR) profiling is a genotyping method that measures the number of repeated sequence motifs at a set of polymorphic loci to generate a unique genetic fingerprint. In cancer research, STR profiling is used for identity verification of cell lines and patient-derived xenograft (PDX) models, confirming model authenticity and detecting cross-contamination or misidentification. Profiles are compared against parental tumor tissue or matched reference samples.

## Used by

- Applied to all 261 PPTC pediatric PDX models for identity verification; STR profiling confirmed model authenticity and detected cross-contamination, leading to exclusion of 26 full models and 3 RNA samples from downstream analyses [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md).
