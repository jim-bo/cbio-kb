**PMID:** 31061129 · **DOI:** 10.1073/pnas.1902651116 · **Journal:** Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences (2019)

## TL;DR

Abida et al. expand a foundational metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer (mCRPC) resource from 150 to 429 patients (444 biopsied tumors) profiled by whole-exome sequencing, RNA-seq, and central histopathologic review, and link the genomic and transcriptomic landscape to longitudinal clinical outcomes — in particular overall survival and time on first-line next-generation androgen receptor signaling inhibitor (ARSI) therapy ([abiraterone](../drugs/abiraterone.md), [enzalutamide](../drugs/enzalutamide.md), or ARN-509 / [apalutamide](../drugs/apalutamide.md)). In a clinically homogeneous subset of 128 taxane-naive patients initiating first-line ARSI for mCRPC, alterations in [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [AR](../genes/AR.md), and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) were each associated with shorter time on ARSI, but only [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) alteration was significantly associated with shorter overall survival; on multivariate analysis [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) was the only molecular feature independently associated with both endpoints (relative risk 3.31 for survival, 6.56 for time on ARSI). The dataset is released as [prad_su2c_2019](../datasets/prad_su2c_2019.md) on cBioPortal and GitHub.

## Cohort & data

- **Patients / tumors:** 429 patients with [metastatic castration-resistant prostate adenocarcinoma](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) enrolled across seven international consortium centers; 444 biopsied tumors profiled (some patients had multiple biopsies).
- **Biopsy sites (n = 444):** 37% lymph node, 36% bone, 14% liver, 7% other soft tissue, 3% prostate, 2% lung, 1% unknown.
- **Histopathology (central review, n = 444):** 88.8% adenocarcinoma; 3.8% pure small-cell / [neuroendocrine prostate carcinoma](../cancer_types/PRNE.md) plus [small-cell prostate carcinoma](../cancer_types/PRSCC.md); 7.4% adenocarcinoma with neuroendocrine features; 11.2% (41/366 evaluable) had any neuroendocrine / small-cell features.
- **Assays:** [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) on 444 tumors with matched normal blood DNA; [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) on a 332-tumor / 323-patient subset; central [IHC](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md) histopathology review.
- **Dataset / cohort:** [prad_su2c_2019](../datasets/prad_su2c_2019.md) — deposited on cBioPortal and GitHub (`cBioPortal/datahub/public/prad_su2c_2019`).
- **Treatment exposure at biopsy:** 47% [ARSI](../drugs/abiraterone.md)-naive, 46% ARSI-exposed; 63% taxane-naive. Median age at prostate-cancer diagnosis 61 y; median age at profiled biopsy 67 y; median overall survival from biopsy 16 months.
- **Outcome subcohort for genomic–clinical association:** 128 taxane-naive patients initiating first-line [abiraterone](../drugs/abiraterone.md) or [enzalutamide](../drugs/enzalutamide.md); 108 of these received the ARSI as monotherapy and were used for time-on-treatment analyses [PMID:31061129](../papers/31061129.md).

## Key findings

- **Mutational landscape recapitulates prior mCRPC cohorts.** Most commonly altered genes were [AR](../genes/AR.md), ETS family ([ERG](../genes/ERG.md), [ETV1](../genes/ETV1.md)), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), and [RB1](../genes/RB1.md). A significant (>20%) subset of patients harbored at least one alteration in a PI3K, cell-cycle, epigenetic, or DNA-repair pathway gene.
- **Oncogenic SNVs concentrated in known drivers.** A high fraction of SNVs in [AR](../genes/AR.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [APC](../genes/APC.md), and [CDK12](../genes/CDK12.md) were classified as likely oncogenic; ~60% of [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) SNVs were predicted oncogenic, the remainder missense variants of unknown significance.
- **Co-occurrence / mutual exclusivity.** [ERG](../genes/ERG.md) was mutually exclusive with [ETV1](../genes/ETV1.md) and with [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md) / [FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md); [ERG](../genes/ERG.md) co-occurred with [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md). [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) alterations co-occurred (consistent with their enrichment in neuroendocrine cancers), and [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) alterations tended toward mutual exclusivity with [AR](../genes/AR.md) alterations. [CHD1](../genes/CHD1.md) co-occurred with [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md). Loss-of-function [CDK12](../genes/CDK12.md) co-occurred with amplification of [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) and [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md), nominating CDK4/6 inhibitor vulnerability in CDK12-mutated tumors.
- **[RB1](../genes/RB1.md) is the dominant prognostic biomarker.** In the n=128 first-line ARSI subset, univariate analysis (Table 1) showed [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) alteration significantly associated with both overall survival (P = 0.002, concordance probability [estimate](../methods/estimate.md) [CPE] = 0.768) and time on first-line ARSI (P < 0.001, CPE = 0.818); for time on ARSI, RB1 had the strongest discrimination of any tested feature.
- **[AR](../genes/AR.md) and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) alterations shorten ARSI duration but not survival.** [AR](../genes/AR.md) alteration: P = 0.005 (CPE = 0.651) for time on first-line ARSI; P = 0.658 for survival. [TP53](../genes/TP53.md): P = 0.046 (CPE = 0.609) for time on ARSI; P = 0.072 for survival.
- **No association for PI3K-pathway or BRCA2/BRCA1/ATM alterations with outcome on ARSI** in this tumor-tissue cohort. Univariate PI3K-pathway: P = 0.699 (survival) / 0.138 (time on ARSI); BRCA2/BRCA1/ATM composite: P = 0.495 / 0.611. This contrasts with prior cfDNA-based reports (Annala et al., PMID:28950925 — not in corpus) — see *Limitations & open questions*.
- **AR splice variants in tumor tissue are not prognostic.** AR-V7 spliced reads per million (SRPM), AR-V7/AR-promoter-1:2 ratio, and AR-V3 SRPM were not significantly associated with overall survival or time on first-line ARSI, including when an optimized AR-V7 cutpoint was derived (AR-V7 cutpoint 1.92, P = 0.62), contrary to prior CTC-based studies.
- **Aneuploid chromosomal status** associated with worse overall survival and shorter time on ARSI vs diploid (SI Appendix Fig. S2).
- **Expression signatures track RB1 loss.** RB1-loss expression score (P < 0.001, n = 99) and cell-cycle progression (CCP) score (P = 0.002) were both significantly associated with shorter survival; NEPC expression score and AR signaling score were not.
- **Multivariate model: RB1 stands alone.** Of all molecular features tested, only [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) alteration was independently associated with overall survival (relative risk 3.31) and with time on first-line ARSI (relative risk 6.56) (Table 2) [PMID:31061129](../papers/31061129.md).
- **Neuroendocrine features enrich post-ARSI.** Histopathologic NE features were present in 10.5% of post-ARSI tumors vs 2.3% of ARSI-naive tumors. Integrative clustering of RNA-seq + histology + RB1/TP53 status yielded three groups: AR-high/NE-low (predominantly adenocarcinoma without NE features, 86%), intermediate AR/NE, and AR-low/NE-high (74% with histologic NE features). A small subset (10/332 RNA-seq cases; 3%) was AR-low/NE-low with adenocarcinoma histology — a candidate "double-negative" state.
- **[SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md) mutation associated with longer time on first-line ARSI** (SI Appendix Fig. S1), consistent with the favorable prognosis of SPOP-mutated tumors reported in localized disease, though this did not translate into a survival benefit in this cohort.

## Genes & alterations

- [AR](../genes/AR.md) — amplifications and missense mutations; AR alteration frequency is higher post-ARSI than in ARSI-naive tumors and is associated with shorter time on first-line ARSI (P = 0.005, CPE = 0.651) but not overall survival. AR-amplified tumors have higher AR pathway expression score. AR splice variants (AR-V7, AR-V3) detected in tumor RNA were *not* prognostic in this cohort.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — among the most frequently altered genes; co-occurs with [RB1](../genes/RB1.md); shorter time on first-line ARSI (P = 0.046, CPE = 0.609); not significant for survival in this cohort (P = 0.072).
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — strongest single prognostic marker. Genomic alteration associated with shorter survival (P = 0.002, CPE 0.768; multivariate RR 3.31) and shorter time on ARSI (P < 0.001, CPE 0.818; multivariate RR 6.56). Co-occurs with TP53; tends to be mutually exclusive with AR alteration. The authors note RB1 loss is likely underestimated by homozygous-deletion calls alone (epigenetic loss, structural events, and focal IHC-only loss also occur).
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — frequently altered (32% in the PI3K-pathway oncoprint); co-occurs with [ERG](../genes/ERG.md); not independently prognostic on ARSI in this cohort.
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) (12%), [PIK3CB](../genes/PIK3CB.md) (4%), [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md) (2.8%), [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) (0.9%) — define the broader PI3K-pathway alteration set; pathway alteration not associated with survival or time on ARSI.
- [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) (11%), [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) (1.9%), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) (7%) — homologous-recombination repair gene alterations; no association with survival or time on ARSI in this tumor cohort (composite P = 0.495 / 0.611), in contrast to prior cfDNA-based findings.
- [CDK12](../genes/CDK12.md) — loss-of-function alterations co-occur with [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) / [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) amplification; authors propose CDK4/6-inhibitor sensitivity hypothesis.
- [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md) — mutations associated with longer time on first-line ARSI in this subset (SI Appendix Fig. S1), but no survival benefit; mutually exclusive with [ERG](../genes/ERG.md); co-occurs with [CHD1](../genes/CHD1.md).
- [ERG](../genes/ERG.md) / [ETV1](../genes/ETV1.md) — ETS-family fusions, mutually exclusive with each other and with [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md) / [FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md).
- [FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md), [APC](../genes/APC.md), [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md), [RNF43](../genes/RNF43.md), [AURKA](../genes/AURKA.md) — recurrently altered but not significantly prognostic in this subset (Table 1 / Fig. 2A).

## Clinical implications

- **RB1 as a clinically actionable prognostic biomarker.** RB1 alteration identifies a group with markedly worse survival and rapid failure of first-line ARSI — an enriched candidate population for more aggressive or alternative first-line strategies. The authors explicitly call this out as identifying patients "who may benefit from a more aggressive treatment approach."
- **AR alteration suggests incomplete AR targeting by current ARSIs.** Higher AR-amplification frequency post-ARSI plus shorter time on first-line abiraterone / enzalutamide in AR-altered tumors imply that deeper AR pathway suppression may be clinically beneficial in patients who develop resistance to these next-generation AR-targeting agents.
- **AR-V7 RNA detection in tumor tissue has limited clinical utility,** in contrast to AR-V7 protein/RNA detection in circulating tumor cells, which has been prognostic in prior studies.
- **PI3K- and DNA-repair–pathway alterations are not, by themselves, prognostic on ARSI in this tumor cohort** — relevant context for interpreting cfDNA-based biomarker studies and for designing prospective trials (e.g., PARP-inhibitor and AKT-inhibitor studies referenced by the authors as NCT02952534, NCT02987543, NCT02854436, NCT02525068, NCT03310541) that [select](../methods/select.md) on these alterations.
- **CDK12-mutated tumors as a CDK4/6-inhibitor hypothesis.** Co-occurrence of [CDK12](../genes/CDK12.md) loss with CCND1/CDK4 amplification motivates testing CDK4/6 inhibitors in this subset.
- **Aggressive-variant / neuroendocrine biology.** The integrative analysis supports — but does not perfectly recapitulate — the model in which RB1/TP53 co-loss, high NE expression score, and low AR signaling co-segregate with histologic neuroendocrine differentiation. A small AR-low/NE-low "double-negative" subset is highlighted as a distinct clinical state warranting study.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Outcome cohort size.** The clinical association analysis was restricted to 128 taxane-naive patients starting first-line ARSI for mCRPC (108 for time-on-treatment monotherapy analyses). Authors note a larger cohort might surface additional smaller-effect associations.
- **Discordance with cfDNA-based biomarker reports for BRCA2/BRCA1/ATM and AR-V7.** The authors flag this conflict explicitly and propose mechanistic candidates (tumor vs cfDNA biology; difficulty detecting homozygous loss in cfDNA; selection bias from requiring detectable cfDNA; tissue vs CTC AR-V7 differences). This is recorded here as an open cross-paper conflict for the wiki.
- **Underestimation of RB1 loss.** The authors note RB1 loss can occur epigenetically, via tandem-duplication structural events, and focally by IHC — all of which are missed by genomic homozygous-deletion calls, so the magnitude of the RB1 prognostic effect is likely conservative.
- **Histopathologic NE classification is hard.** Concordance between histologic NE features, NE expression score, and RB1/TP53 loss is imperfect; a subset of cases with discrepant pathology/transcriptomic classification display atypical nuclear features (pleomorphism, irregular nuclear membranes, high mitotic activity) on consensus re-review — suggesting a morphologic spectrum not captured by the binary NE-vs-adenocarcinoma label.
- **Neuroendocrine frequency lower than some recent reports.** 10.5% post-ARSI NE features vs 17% in another contemporary study; the authors attribute this in part to inclusion of patients with NE features from an [alisertib](../drugs/alisertib.md) (Aurora kinase A inhibitor) trial.
- **First-line ARSI heterogeneity.** Abiraterone and enzalutamide are pooled as "ARSI"; differential biomarker effects between agents are not resolved.
