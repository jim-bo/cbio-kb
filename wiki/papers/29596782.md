**PMID:** 29596782 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.cels.2018.03.002 · **Journal:** Cell Systems (2018)

## TL;DR

Ellrott et al. describe the Multi-Center Mutation Calling in Multiple Cancers (MC3) project — a coordinated re-call of every TCGA tumor exome using an ensemble of seven somatic variant callers ([MuTect](../methods/mutect.md), [VarScan2](../methods/varscan.md), [Pindel](../methods/pindel.md), [Indelocator](../methods/indelocator.md), [RADIA](../methods/radia.md), [MuSE](../methods/muse.md), [SomaticSniper](../methods/somatic-sniper.md)) plus a battery of eight filters. Operating on ~10,510 tumor/normal pairs from 33 cancer types (~400 TB of raw BAMs, ~1.8 million core-hours on DNAnexus), the project produced a controlled-access MAF of 22,485,627 variants and an open-access "PASS"-filtered MAF of 3,600,963 variants (~3.5 million high-confidence somatic mutations) that became the canonical mutation substrate for all PanCanAtlas analyses [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).

## Cohort & data

- **Samples:** 11,069 tumor/normal pairs covering 10,486 TCGA participants across 33 cancer types after sample whitelisting; the published MAF analysis covers 10,510 tumor/normal pairs [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).
- **Sample selection rules:** exclude redacted samples, non-HG19 alignments, non-Illumina sequencing, FFPE samples (97 removed), and samples with [ContEst](../methods/contest.md) contamination > 4% (12 rules total); prefer Broad-aligned, GATK co-cleaned, native (non-WGA) DNA, with matching genome-build strings for tumor and normal [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).
- **Assay:** TCGA whole-exome sequencing ([whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md)) on Illumina platforms with multiple capture kits; intersection-of-capture-kit BED used as the `bitgt` exonic mask. Orthogonal validation via TCGA targeted deep sequencing on 3,128 samples and [whole-genome sequencing](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) on 1,059 samples (median 126 validation sites/sample) [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).
- **Compute:** Variant calling for Pindel/MuSE/RADIA/VarScan/SomaticSniper run on the DNAnexus cloud (~1.8M core-hours over 4 weeks, 400 TB processed, ~500 [GB](../cancer_types/GB.md) of VCFs produced); MuTect/Indelocator + ContEst + OxoG run on the Broad Firehose; OxoG and validation runs on the ISB Cancer Genomics Cloud and Broad FireCloud; GATK co-cleaning of ~1,600 BAMs at the UCSC NCI cluster. Pipeline distributed as [Common Workflow Language (CWL) + Docker](../methods/cwl-docker-workflow.md) at https://github.com/OpenGenomics/mc3 [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).
- **Outputs:** Three MAFs released — controlled-access (22,485,627 variants: 13,044,511 SNVs + 9,441,116 indels), open-access PASS-only (3,600,963 variants from 10,295 tumors: 3,427,680 SNVs + 173,283 indels), and a validation MAF; data hosted at the NCI GDC (https://gdc.cancer.gov/about-data/publications/mc3-2017) [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).
- **Anchor study in cBioPortal:** the MC3 mutation calls form the somatic-variant backbone of the PanCanAtlas studies in cBioPortal, e.g. [gbm_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/gbm_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [coadread_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/coadread_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), and [cesc_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/cesc_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md).

## Key findings

- **Ensemble + filters > single caller.** Seven callers were applied uniformly across TCGA. Of 22,485,627 putative variants, post-filter retention was 2,907,335 high-confidence calls; the open-access PASS MAF holds 3,600,963 variants from 10,295 tumors [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).
- **MC3 vs. PanCan12 legacy MAF.** On the PanCan12 sample set, MC3 reported 1,079,216 variants vs. 804,571 in the legacy PanCan12 MAF, sharing 717,326 calls — MC3 recovered 89.5% of the original calls while expanding the call set by 25%. Cancer types with > 90% concordance: [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md), [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md), [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md), [BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md), COAD/READ, [UCEC](../cancer_types/UCEC.md). Outliers: [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md) recovered only 33% of original calls (low tumor purity), and [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md) ([LAML](../cancer_types/LAML.md)) recovered 44% because the legacy MAF relied on Sanger-recovered calls not in the TCGA exome data [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).
- **Filter contributions.** ~60% of called variants were dropped by the `NonExonic` and `bitgt` (capture-kit) regional filters; ~30% were dropped by the [Broad Panel of Normals v2](../methods/panel-of-normals-filter.md). Eight named filters were used: `broad_PoN_v2`, Common-in-ExAC ([vcf2maf](../methods/vcf2maf.md) v1.6.11; AC>16 unless ClinVar-pathogenic, AC=16 anchor from [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md) K700E in clonal hematopoiesis), [OxoG](../methods/oxog-filter.md), [ContEst](../methods/contest.md), StrandBias (low-VAF G>T from WashU samples), Normal Depth (≥8 reads non-dbSNP / ≥19 dbSNP), Capture Kit (`bitgt`), and NonExonic [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).
- **Filtering matters for SMG discovery.** Running [MutSig2CV](../methods/mutsig.md) (P < 3.5e-5) and [MuSiC2](../methods/genome-music.md) (P < 1e-7) on [KIRC](../cancer_types/KIRC.md) PASS variants yielded 10 SMGs each; the controlled (unfiltered) MAF inflated the lists to 1,203 (MutSig2CV) and 321 (MuSiC2) — the noise made real signal extremely difficult to find [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).
- **Caller-specific behavior.** MuTect and MuSE detected the largest number of true positives among validated SNVs and showed the highest pair-wise agreement; SomaticSniper made the fewest calls overall but had the lowest false-positive rate. Pindel made the most indel calls, but >130K calls clustered in two samples, indicating sample-specific artifacts. Two-caller intersection effectively eliminates SNV false positives but increases indel false negatives [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).
- **Cancer-type variability in calling consistency.** [THYM](../cancer_types/THYM.md), PAAD, [KICH](../cancer_types/KICH.md), and [UVM](../cancer_types/UVM.md) showed the largest sample-to-sample variability in mutations per sample, attributable to low tumor purity (THYM and PAAD median ABSOLUTE purity 39.0% and 39.7% respectively). SKCM, [LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md), and [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) had the largest median number of SNVs per sample, consistent with tobacco/UV mutagen exposure [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).
- **Liquid-tumor caveat (LAML).** Because LAML "normal" skin biopsies often contain blood enriched with tumor cells, conservative MC3 filtering misclassifies somatic calls as germline and recovers only 44% of the LAML AWG calls (which had used Sanger-based manual recovery) [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).
- **Capture-kit blind spots.** The `bitgt` filter excludes 170 CDS-altering MC3 calls in [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md)'s 410-gene panel that fall outside the Broad capture BED, including [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter hits, truncations in [CIC](../genes/CIC.md), splice alterations in the frequently rearranged [CRLF2](../genes/CRLF2.md), and a cluster of 5'-end events in [FOXP1](../genes/FOXP1.md) [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).
- **Complex indels.** The Pindel-priority merging strategy created 14,241 complex indel sites in the controlled MAF and 3,611 in the open-access MAF — events that combine an indel and a substitution in cis and that other callers cannot represent [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).

## Genes & alterations

- KIRC SMG overlap between MutSig2CV and MuSiC2 on the open-access MAF: [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [VHL](../genes/VHL.md), [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md), [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md), [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md), [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md) [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).
- KIRC SMGs uniquely called by MutSig2CV: [ELOC](../genes/ELOC.md) (paper-era symbol "TCEB1"), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).
- KIRC SMGs uniquely called by MuSiC2 after long-gene filtering: [ERBB4](../genes/ERBB4.md), [SLITRK6](../genes/SLITRK6.md), [KDM5C](../genes/KDM5C.md) [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).
- [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md) K700E used as the empirical AC=16 cutoff for the "Common in ExAC" filter — it is the highest ExAC count observed for a known clonal-hematopoiesis somatic event in the non-TCGA subset of ExAC v0.3.1 [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).
- Capture-kit-excluded events of biological interest: [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter mutations (non-coding, outside Broad BED), [CIC](../genes/CIC.md) truncations, [CRLF2](../genes/CRLF2.md) splice alterations, [FOXP1](../genes/FOXP1.md) 5'-end clusters [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).

## Clinical implications

- This is a methods/data-resource paper and makes no direct clinical claims about prognosis or treatment.
- **Resource for downstream PanCanAtlas analyses.** The MC3 MAF is the somatic-variant input for cBioPortal PanCanAtlas studies (e.g. [gbm_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/gbm_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [coadread_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/coadread_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [cesc_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/cesc_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md)) and for downstream PanCanAtlas papers including the driver-discovery, immune-landscape, and DNA-damage-repair manuscripts [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).
- **Operational guidance for cohort calling.** The authors argue that for driver-gene discovery a high-specificity (low FP) caller is preferable; for heterogeneity / sub-clonal analysis, sensitive callers tolerating low-VAF events are preferable. Annotating rather than removing filtered variants is recommended so analysts can pick a stringency appropriate to their question [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Validation set is non-random.** Targeted-validation sites were selected by individual TCGA AWGs from the most likely SMG candidates, so every validated site was already called by ≥1 method; false-negative rate cannot account for sites missed by all callers [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).
- **Validation technology overlap.** Most validation used the same sequencing chemistry as discovery, so systematic errors that filters target (especially [Panel of Normals](../methods/panel-of-normals-filter.md)) appear as "erroneous filtering events" in the validation comparison [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).
- **Liquid tumors need different strategies.** [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md) (LAML) recovered only 44% of legacy calls because tumor-in-normal contamination breaks the assumption that the matched normal is variant-free; the authors flag this as a general open problem for all liquid tumors [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).
- **Capture-kit-mask side effects.** The `bitgt` filter discards [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter hits and other clinically meaningful events that fall outside the Broad capture BED — a pancan one-size-fits-all mask trades sensitivity for cohort comparability [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).
- **Default parameters are insufficient.** The authors call out that achieving the best per-tool performance often required non-default parameters and direct consultation with tool authors (Table S1) — a reproducibility risk for any group running these tools off-the-shelf [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).
- **Future direction.** Lessons learned should inform a future end-to-end FASTQ-to-filtered-MAF workflow with full containerization in a single cloud and a robust benchmarking effort that scans caller / filter / parameter combinations per tumor type [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md).
