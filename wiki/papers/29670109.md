**PMID:** 29670109 · **DOI:** 10.1038/s41467-018-03828-2 · **Journal:** Nature Communications (2018)

## TL;DR

Guo et al. analyzed 212 gastric adenocarcinoma whole genomes (TCGA, ICGC, a Hong Kong cohort, and 40 newly sequenced Singapore tumors) to identify recurrently mutated non-coding regions. After modelling background mutation rates with epigenetic and sequence-context covariates, they detected 34 significant non-coding SNV hotspots — 11 of which overlap [CTCF](../genes/CTCF.md) binding sites (CBSs) — plus broad indel enrichment at three stomach-lineage-specific genes ([LIPF](../genes/LIPF.md), [PGC](../genes/PGC.md), [MUC6](../genes/MUC6.md)). The CBS hotspots remained significant under a CBS-specific background model, were nominally linked to expression changes in neighboring genes ([CENPQ](../genes/CENPQ.md), [KCNQ5](../genes/KCNQ5.md), SPART/SPG20), were enriched in CIN-subtype tumors, and co-occurred with nearby SCNA breakpoints. CBS hotspot mutations appeared in 25% of gastric and 19% of colorectal tumors but were rare in 16 other cancer types, nominating CBS disruption as a tissue-specific non-coding driver mechanism in gastrointestinal cancers.

## Cohort & data

- **212 gastric adenocarcinoma WGS samples**, pooled from four sources: 40 [STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md) tumor/normal pairs newly sequenced in Singapore ([stad_oncosg_2018](../datasets/stad_oncosg_2018.md)); 40 from TCGA; 32 from ICGC (EGAD00001003132); 100 from the Wang et al. Hong Kong cohort.
- **Discovery cohort after QC: 187 tumors** (20 low-quality and 5 oxidative-damage samples removed); MSI tumors (N=19) further removed for hotspot discovery to homogenize mutational biases, leaving CIN (n=41), GS (n=11), EBV (n=17) as the analysis groups for CBS subtype comparisons.
- **Assay:** [whole-genome sequencing](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) on Illumina HiSeq (101 bp or 151 bp paired-end, 300–400 bp inserts), aligned to hg19 with [BWA](../methods/bwa.md). Somatic SNVs/indels called by four callers ([VarScan](../methods/varscan.md), [MuTect](../methods/mutect.md), VarDict, FreeBayes) and merged via a [random-forest classifier](../methods/random-forest-classifier.md) (SMuRF) with >85% accuracy on a curated test set.
- **Transcriptomes:** [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) on 19 matched tumor-normal pairs (Singapore); normalized RNA-seq counts for 35 TCGA tumors obtained from GDC. Available paired expression data covered only 49/187 samples.
- **Pan-cancer comparison:** 826 non-hypermutated WGS tumors from 18 other cancer types reanalyzed at the 11 CBS hotspots, including [colorectal](../cancer_types/COADREAD.md) (N=21), breast (N=172), liver (N=88), lung adeno (N=63), lung squamous (N=20), bladder (N=20), melanoma (N=11), and others.

## Key findings

- **34 significant non-coding SNV hotspots** identified genome-wide at 21 bp resolution (Bonferroni-adjusted P<0.01); **11 of the 34 overlap CTCF binding sites** [PMID:29670109](../papers/29670109.md).
- **Top indel hotspot lies ~100 kb upstream of [AFDN](../genes/AFDN.md)**; second-highest indel hotspot sits in an intron of [PGC](../genes/PGC.md). Gene-level recurrence analysis flagged three stomach-lineage genes — [LIPF](../genes/LIPF.md), [PGC](../genes/PGC.md), [MUC6](../genes/MUC6.md) — as the top broad-indel-enriched genes, none of which showed expression change with mutation status (Wilcoxon P=0.14–0.51).
- **Genome-wide CBS mutation rate is 3-fold elevated** over 1 kb flanks (11 vs 3.6 mutations/Mb), and is **7.1-fold elevated in CIN** and **4.7-fold in GS** tumors, but flat in MSI and only 1.7-fold in EBV — arguing the CBS bias is not solely a DNA-repair-deficiency artifact.
- **CBS-specific background model** (adding tumor subtype, replication timing, local mutation rate, COSMIC signatures 1 and 17, CBS-loop-boundary status, and CBS-specific sequence context) confirmed **9/11 CBS hotspots at Bonferroni P<0.01**; the other two had adjusted P=0.025 and 0.086. Seven additional CBSs became significant under this restricted-hypothesis test.
- **71% of CBS-hotspot mutations occurred in CIN tumors** (Fisher's exact P=0.012), the most common GC subtype (~50% of GC cases). Substitutions at CBS motifs were predominantly A·T>C·G and A·T>G·C; C>T (a hallmark of all-CBS mutations) was depleted at CBS hotspots (Fisher's P=4.4×10⁻⁷), suggesting a partly distinct selective pressure at hotspot sites.
- **CBS hotspot mutations couple to chromosomal instability.** Distance from a CBS hotspot to the nearest SCNA breakpoint was significantly shorter in mutated than non-mutated tumors (median ~1 Mb vs ~2 Mb; Wilcoxon P=0.0018); non-CBS hotspots showed no such association (P=0.53). Loop-boundary CBSs were closer to SCNA breakpoints than non-boundary CBSs (Wilcoxon P=5.7×10⁻¹⁶).
- **Three of four tested CBS hotspots associate with cis-gene expression changes** (adjusted Wilcoxon two-sided rank-sum, validated in the Singapore RNA-seq cohort):
  - chr6:50,570,087–50,570,110 hotspot (12 mutated samples) → **CENPQ up** (P=0.007, adj 0.026) and **[MMUT](../genes/MMUT.md) up** (P=0.0021, adj 0.042).
  - chr6:73,122,094–73,122,113 hotspot (9 mutated samples) → **KCNQ5 down** (P=0.0059, adj 0.047); chr6:73,122,103 A>G was independently shown by Umer et al. to disrupt CTCF binding via EMSA.
  - chr13:36,552,831–36,552,851 hotspot → **[SPART](../genes/SPART.md) (SPG20) down** ~3-fold (P=0.045, adj 0.65; not significant after multiple testing).
- **Pan-cancer:** 25% of gastric and 19% of colorectal tumors carry a mutation in ≥1 of the 11 CBS hotspots; the same hotspots are rare in melanoma and bladder despite those tumors' high genome-wide CBS mutation loads. In gastric tumors, CBS hotspot mutations form the **second-most-mutated functional region after [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (50% of GC)**.
- **CBS hotspot mutations are clonal early events:** VAFs cluster at clonal frequencies and are statistically indistinguishable from paired non-silent coding mutations in known GC driver genes (paired Wilcoxon P=0.49).
- **No mutation hotspot detected near [TERT](../genes/TERT.md)**, confirming that [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) reactivation is rare in gastric cancer; the authors contrast their findings with non-coding driver discoveries elsewhere (e.g., TERT promoter mutations, [TAL1](../genes/TAL1.md) enhancer insertions in T-ALL).

## Genes & alterations

- [CTCF](../genes/CTCF.md) — not mutated as a gene per se; rather, its binding motifs and flanks are the site of recurrent non-coding SNVs. CBS hotspot substitutions are predominantly A·T>C·G/A·T>G·C and enriched in the 4 bp 5′ flank of the 19 bp CTCF motif. Some hotspot mutations are predicted (DeepBind) or experimentally shown (Umer et al., EMSA) to disrupt CTCF binding.
- [AFDN](../genes/AFDN.md) — top genome-wide indel hotspot located ~100 kb upstream; expression effect not tested (no paired RNA-seq for mutated samples).
- [PGC](../genes/PGC.md) — second-most-significant indel hotspot in an intron; stomach-lineage gene expressed at 11,940 TPM in stomach vs ≤2 TPM elsewhere; indels not associated with PGC expression change.
- [MUC6](../genes/MUC6.md) — broad non-coding indel enrichment; stomach-specific (133 TPM stomach, 38 TPM pancreas, ≤2 TPM elsewhere); no expression change linked to indels.
- [LIPF](../genes/LIPF.md) — replicates prior reports of broad GC-specific non-coding indel enrichment; stomach-lineage lipase; no expression change.
- [CENPQ](../genes/CENPQ.md) — nominated cis-target of the chr6:50.57 Mb CBS hotspot; expression upregulated in mutated tumors (P=0.007, adj 0.026); also upregulated in GC tumors vs normal gastric tissue (Wilcoxon P=0.0028). Encodes a centromere subunit linked to mitotic progression — a plausible mechanistic bridge to CIN.
- [MMUT](../genes/MMUT.md) (paper's "MUT") — co-target of the same chr6:50.57 Mb hotspot; upregulated in mutated tumors (P=0.0021, adj 0.042).
- [KCNQ5](../genes/KCNQ5.md) — cis-target of the chr6:73.12 Mb CBS hotspot ~200 kb downstream; downregulated in mutated tumors (P=0.0059, adj 0.047); downregulated in GC vs normal (Wilcoxon P=3.2×10⁻⁷); CTCF knockdown previously shown to downregulate KCNQ5.
- [SPART](../genes/SPART.md) (paper's "SPG20") — cis-target of chr13:36.55 Mb CBS hotspot; ~3-fold downregulated in mutated tumors (P=0.045, adj 0.65 — not significant after multiple testing); downregulated in GC vs normal (P=0.00082). Previously reported significantly mutated in esophageal exomes; involved in [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) trafficking.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — cited as the only functional region more frequently mutated than CBS hotspots in GC (50% of tumors).
- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) — no hotspot near TERT detected, confirming rarity of TERT promoter mutations in GC.
- [TAL1](../genes/TAL1.md) — cited in the introduction as a precedent (enhancer insertions in T-ALL) for non-coding driver discovery.

## Clinical implications

- The paper does not propose a treatment or biomarker. It nominates **CBS hotspot mutations as candidate non-coding drivers in gastric and colorectal cancer** and proposes a model in which positive selection acts on a subset of mutations arising from the elevated background CBS mutation rate.
- Mechanistically, the **co-occurrence of CBS hotspot mutations with nearby SCNA breakpoints** and the enrichment in the CIN subtype frame CBS disruption as a candidate mechanism linking non-coding regulatory mutation to chromosomal instability — a hypothesis that, if confirmed functionally, could refine subtype classification of GC and CRC.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Only 49/187 samples had paired RNA-seq**, restricting the cis-expression analysis to four of the eleven CBS hotspots and three of those reaching nominal significance; cohort-level statistical power for SPART/SPG20 was insufficient.
- **Allele-specific expression could not be resolved** at any of the three candidate hotspots because the maximum inter-SNP distance between hotspot and candidate target gene was >5 kb in every case.
- **No gastric-tissue Hi–C data** was available; the authors used IMR90 Hi–C and a constitutive CTCF loop set from GM12878, Jurkat, and K562 to define loop boundaries, relying on the assumption that chromatin topology is largely cell-type-invariant.
- **The temporal ordering of CBS mutations versus chromosomal breaks is unresolved.** VAFs suggest CBS hotspot mutations are clonal early events, but the causal direction (CBS disruption → CIN vs CIN → CBS mutation) is not established.
- **Passenger-vs-driver discrimination within hotspot windows** is not possible with the current statistical framework; the model identifies regions of mutational recurrence but does not distinguish individual driver mutations from passengers within those regions.
- **23 of 34 non-CBS hotspots have no clear functional theme** — seven intronic, one immediately downstream of a gene, the rest intergenic, with no overlap with known cancer drivers.
- MSI tumors were excluded from the discovery cohort because their mutation rate covariate structure differs sharply from non-MSI tumors; whether CBS hotspots also operate in MSI gastric cancer remains untested here.
