# MAP3K13

## Overview

MAP3K13 (also known as LZK) is a serine/threonine kinase that functions in the JUN kinase signalling cascade. It phosphorylates and activates MAP2K7, transmitting upstream signals to JNK. In breast cancer, MAP3K13 harbours truncating and non-synonymous mutations, implicating it as a tumour suppressor whose inactivation contributes to abrogation of JUN kinase signalling alongside [MAP3K1](../genes/MAP3K1.md) and [MAP2K4](../genes/MAP2K4.md).

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Truncating and non-synonymous mutations identified in breast cancer WES (100 tumors, Sanger cohort); activates MAP2K7 in the JUN kinase pathway [PMID:22722201](../papers/22722201.md)
- MAP3K13 harbors inactivating mutations in ESCC, contributing to MAPK pathway dysregulation [PMID:24686850](../papers/24686850.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md)**: Truncating and non-synonymous mutations observed; contributes to JUN kinase pathway inactivation in a subset of breast cancers alongside MAP3K1 and MAP2K4 mutations [PMID:22722201](../papers/22722201.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with MAP3K1 and MAP2K4 mutations in a subset of breast cancers; together these genes contribute to JUN kinase pathway inactivation in approximately half of breast cancers [PMID:22722201](../papers/22722201.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- JUN kinase pathway inactivation via MAP3K13 (and MAP3K1/MAP2K4) mutations may represent a therapeutic vulnerability in breast cancer, though specific targeted agents have not yet been clinically validated [PMID:22722201](../papers/22722201.md)

## Open questions

- Functional consequences of specific MAP3K13 mutations in breast cancer remain incompletely characterized.
- The biological downstream effects of JUN kinase pathway inactivation in breast cancer are not yet fully understood [PMID:22722201](../papers/22722201.md)
