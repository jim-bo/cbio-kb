# MUC16

## Overview

MUC16 (Mucin 16, Cell Surface Associated), also known as CA-125, is a large transmembrane mucin widely used as an ovarian cancer biomarker. Somatic mutations in MUC16 have been observed across multiple tumor types, and in adenoid cystic carcinoma it is enriched in the fusion-negative subclass.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Somatic mutations enriched in tumors lacking both [MYB](../genes/MYB.md) and [MYBL1](../genes/MYBL1.md) fusions in adenoid cystic carcinoma (ACC), suggesting preferential involvement in the fusion-negative subclass [PMID:26631609](../papers/26631609.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **Adenoid cystic carcinoma**: somatic mutations enriched in the fusion-negative (MYB/MYBL1-lacking) subclass; NOTCH-pathway context [PMID:26631609](../papers/26631609.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) and [SPEN](../genes/SPEN.md) mutations in the fusion-negative ACC subclass [PMID:26631609](../papers/26631609.md)
