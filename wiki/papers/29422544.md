**PMID:** 29422544 · **DOI:** 10.1038/emm.2017.265 · **Journal:** Experimental & Molecular Medicine (2018)

## TL;DR

Han et al. performed whole-exome sequencing and copy-number profiling on 15 Korean vulvar squamous cell carcinomas (6 HPV-positive, 9 HPV-negative) — the first genome-wide characterization of this tumor type. HPV(−) tumors recapitulated the classical vulvar SCC mutation pattern ([TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md)) plus 7p and 8q gains and 2q loss, while HPV(+) tumors carried novel-for-vulvar-SCC mutations in [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), and [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md). HPV(−) cases had a higher mutational load than HPV(+) cases, but copy-number burden and mutational signatures did not differ between the two etiologic groups. Notably, [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) and [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md) alterations each occurred in 40% (6/15) of cases, kataegis was found in 2 HPV(+) tumors, and copy-neutral loss of heterozygosity in 4 tumors (1 HPV(+), 3 HPV(−)).

## Cohort & data

- **15 Korean vulvar SCC patients** (ages 45–83) with surgically resected primary tumors and matched normal tissue, profiled by [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) and copy-number analysis ([PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)).
- HPV status by real-time PCR (Seegene): **6 HPV(+)** (HPV16, 52, or 58 — all high-risk) and **9 HPV(−)** ([PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)).
- Tissue: 1 frozen (VSCC2), 14 FFPE; tumor cell purity ~70% after microdissection by two pathologists ([PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)).
- Cancer type: vulvar squamous cell carcinoma ([VSC](../cancer_types/VSC.md)).
- Dataset: [vsc_cuk_2018](../datasets/vsc_cuk_2018.md) (The Catholic University of Korea, 2018).
- Library prep: Agilent SureSelect Human All Exome V4; alignment by [BWA v0.7.15](../methods/bwa.md) to hg19; Picard v2.7.1 and Samtools v1.3.1 for de-duplication; GATK v3.6 for local realignment and BQSR ([PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)).
- Variant calling: [MuTect](../methods/mutect.md) for SNVs and [SomaticIndelDetector](../methods/gatk-somatic-indel-detector.md) for indels; [ANNOVAR](../methods/annovar.md) for functional annotation; PolyPhen-2 for missense impact prediction; 21 mutations across 9 genes validated by digital PCR or Sanger sequencing ([PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)).
- Mutational signatures by the [SomaticSignatures R package](../methods/somatic-signatures.md) using NMF, compared to 30 COSMIC signatures (cosine similarity) and to TCGA SCCs ([CESC](../cancer_types/CESC.md), [ESCA](../cancer_types/ESCA.md), [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md), [LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md)) plus published [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md) and oral SCC datasets ([PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)).
- Copy-number alterations called via the ngCGH module and RankSegmentation in [Nexus v7.5](../methods/nexus-copy-number.md); LOH events inferred with [Sequenza](../methods/sequenza.md), then manually curated using depth ratio and B-allele frequency ([PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)).

## Key findings

- **Etiology-stratified mutation burden:** HPV(−) vulvar SCCs carried more nonsilent mutations and more driver mutations than HPV(+) cases, while CNA burden and mutational signatures did not differ between the two groups ([PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)).
- **HPV(−) drivers recapitulate prior reports:** known recurrent mutations in [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), and [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) ([PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)).
- **Recurrent CNAs in HPV(−):** 7p and 8q gains and 2q loss ([PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)).
- **Novel HPV(+) mutations for vulvar SCC:** [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), and [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md) — none previously reported in this tumor type ([PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)).
- **High-frequency alterations across the cohort:** [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) altered in 40% (6/15) and [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md) altered in 40% (6/15) of vulvar SCCs irrespective of HPV status ([PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)).
- **Kataegis** (localized hypermutation with average inter-mutation distance ≤10 kb) detected in 2 HPV(+) tumors ([PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)).
- **Copy-neutral loss of heterozygosity** observed in 4 tumors (1 HPV(+), 3 HPV(−)) ([PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)).
- The authors compared their mutation signatures against the 30 COSMIC signatures and four TCGA SCC cohorts ([CESC](../cancer_types/CESC.md), [ESCA](../cancer_types/ESCA.md), [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md), [LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md)) plus skin and oral SCC datasets ([PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)).
- Prior targeted-panel work (cited by the authors) had reported that 83% of HPV(−) vulvar SCCs carried somatic mutations in [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [PPP2R1A](../genes/PPP2R1A.md), or [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), versus only 17% of HPV(+) cases having a [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation — context this study extends to whole-exome scope ([PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)).

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — somatic mutations in HPV(−) vulvar SCCs; consistent with prior literature naming TP53 the most commonly mutated gene in HPV(−) vulvar SCC ([PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)).
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — recurrent in HPV(−) tumors ([PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)).
- [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) — recurrent in HPV(−) tumors ([PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)).
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — first description in HPV(+) vulvar SCC; altered in 40% of the 15-tumor cohort overall ([PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)).
- [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) — novel mutation finding in HPV(+) vulvar SCC ([PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)).
- [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md) — novel mutation finding in HPV(+) vulvar SCC ([PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)).
- [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md) — altered in 40% of vulvar SCCs irrespective of HPV status ([PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)).
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [PPP2R1A](../genes/PPP2R1A.md) — referenced from prior targeted-panel literature on HPV(−) vulvar SCC; not specifically called out as new findings in this WES cohort ([PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)).

## Clinical implications

- The authors frame the work as foundational for "the development of clinical treatment strategies" rather than asserting any specific therapeutic recommendation ([PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)).
- The 40% prevalence of [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) alterations across both etiologic subgroups suggests PI3K-pathway inhibition may be a tractable hypothesis worth testing in vulvar SCC, though the paper itself does not name a specific drug or trial.
- ~33% of vulvar SCC patients recur with limited therapeutic options (cited by the authors as motivation), underscoring the unmet need this molecular characterization aims to inform ([PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Small cohort.** N=15 (6 HPV(+), 9 HPV(−)) limits statistical power to detect rarer drivers and to claim subgroup-specific signature differences as truly absent.
- **Single-institution, single-ethnicity cohort.** All patients were Korean and accrued through The Catholic University of Korea — generalizability to other populations is untested.
- **FFPE-dominant material.** 14 of 15 tumors were FFPE, which can introduce sequencing artifacts (notably C>T deamination) that may confound mutation-signature interpretation.
- **No survival or treatment-response data** linked to the molecular calls in the analyzed text.
- **Open question for cross-paper synthesis:** whether the high [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) and [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md) prevalence reported here replicates in larger vulvar SCC cohorts and aligns with rates seen in other gynecologic and head-and-neck SCCs ([CESC](../cancer_types/CESC.md), [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md)).
