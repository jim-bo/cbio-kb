# TNS1

## Overview

TNS1 (Tensin-1) is a focal adhesion protein involved in integrin signaling and actin cytoskeleton organization. In cancer genomics, TNS1 has emerged as a fusion partner in rare kinase rearrangements, most notably in sarcomas, where TNS1-ALK fusions have been identified as a novel oncogenic driver in leiomyosarcoma ([LMS](../cancer_types/LMS.md)).

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Novel TNS1-ALK fusion identified in 16 leiomyosarcoma ([LMS](../cancer_types/LMS.md)) cases in a pan-sarcoma genomic profiling study of 7,494 cases (FoundationOne CDx); kinase fusions overall present in 2.6% of sarcomas; TNS1-ALK fusions may identify ALK-inhibitor candidates [PMID:35705558](../papers/35705558.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[LMS](../cancer_types/LMS.md) (Leiomyosarcoma):** TNS1-ALK fusion detected in 16 cases; represents a novel actionable kinase rearrangement [PMID:35705558](../papers/35705558.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- TNS1-ALK fusion co-occurs with the broader spectrum of kinase fusions in sarcoma ([ALK](../genes/ALK.md), [ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md), [RET](../genes/RET.md), [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md), NTRK1/2/3, [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)), collectively present in 2.6% of sarcomas [PMID:35705558](../papers/35705558.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- TNS1-ALK fusions may identify LMS patients eligible for [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) inhibitor therapy (by analogy with ALK-rearranged [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md)); no clinical trial data specifically in TNS1-ALK LMS reported as of this cohort study [PMID:35705558](../papers/35705558.md).

## Open questions

- Clinical response to [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) inhibitors in TNS1-ALK-fused LMS has not been formally evaluated; prospective biomarker-driven trial enrollment is needed.
- ALK NTRK amplifications (vs. fusions) are not actionable per cited trial findings, underscoring the importance of distinguishing fusion from copy-number events [PMID:35705558](../papers/35705558.md).
