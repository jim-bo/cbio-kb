# RAD50

## Overview

RAD50 encodes a component of the MRN (MRE11-RAD50-NBN) complex, essential for DNA double-strand break recognition and repair, as well as telomere maintenance. In endometrial cancer, RAD50 protein expression differences across molecular subtypes suggest subtype-specific variation in DNA repair capacity.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- RAD50 shows elevated RPPA protein expression in the copy-number-low endometrial carcinoma subgroup, suggesting differences in DNA repair capacity across endometrial cancer subtypes [PMID:23636398](../papers/23636398.md)
- CDK12, FANCA, PALB2, and RAD50 are additional somatic HR pathway genes identified in advanced prostate cancer (locoregional through mCRPC); combined germline+somatic HR-deficiency rate was 27% across canonical DDR genes [PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [UCEC](../cancer_types/UCEC.md): RAD50 protein level (by RPPA) is elevated in copy-number-low endometrioid tumors relative to other endometrial cancer subgroups [PMID:23636398](../papers/23636398.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Copy-number-low endometrial subgroup (elevated RAD50) is enriched for [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) and [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) mutations and low [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation rate [PMID:23636398](../papers/23636398.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Elevated DNA repair capacity in copy-number-low endometrial cancers (inferred from RAD50 RPPA expression) may influence sensitivity to platinum-based chemotherapy or PARP inhibitors.

## Open questions

- Whether RAD50 elevation reflects increased protein expression, gene amplification, or post-translational regulation is not specified in this cohort.
