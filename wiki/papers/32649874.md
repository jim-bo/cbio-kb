**PMID:** 32649874 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.cell.2020.06.013 · **Journal:** Cell (2020)

## TL;DR

The CPTAC consortium performed comprehensive proteogenomic characterization of 110 treatment-naïve [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) tumors and 101 matched normal adjacent tissues (NATs), integrating whole-exome and whole-genome sequencing, RNA-seq, miRNA-seq, DNA methylation, and deep-scale proteomics, phosphoproteomics and acetylproteomics. The cohort intentionally balanced smokers with self-reported never-smokers and included patients from multiple countries (notably USA, Vietnam, China). Multi-omics NMF clustering yielded four subgroups (C1–C4) anchored on driver mutations, geography, and gender. Phosphoproteomics revealed therapeutic vulnerabilities downstream of [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), and [EML4](../genes/EML4.md)-[ALK](../genes/ALK.md) drivers — most notably outlier PTPN11/Shp2 phosphorylation in EGFR-mutant and ALK-fusion tumors, and [SOS1](../genes/SOS1.md) S1161 phosphorylation in KRAS-mutant tumors. [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) mutation was strongly associated with an immune-cold phenotype, with proteomic evidence pointing to neutrophil degranulation as a potential immunosuppressive mechanism. The dataset is released as the cBioPortal study [luad_cptac_2020](../datasets/luad_cptac_2020.md).

## Cohort & data

- 110 treatment-naïve [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) tumors and 101 paired normal adjacent tissues (NATs); complete multi-omics data for 101 tumors and 96 NATs.
- 111 participants (73 males, 38 females; age range 35–81); samples collected from 13 tissue source sites across 8 countries, with deliberate enrichment for never-smokers and non-Western populations relative to TCGA.
- Assays: whole-exome sequencing (~150× coverage), whole-genome sequencing (~15× coverage), RNA-seq, miRNA-seq, EPIC DNA methylation array, TMT-based isobaric labeling for deep-scale proteomics, phosphoproteomics and acetylproteomics.
- Released as cBioPortal study [luad_cptac_2020](../datasets/luad_cptac_2020.md); raw proteomics at the CPTAC data portal (S056), genomics via Genomic Data Commons (dbGaP phs001287.v5.p4).

## Key findings

- **Multi-omics NMF clustering identified four [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) subgroups (C1–C4)** that overlapped previously described mRNA subtypes (proximal-inflammatory, proximal-proliferative, terminal respiratory unit) but split proximal-proliferative into two distinct clusters:
  - C1 (proximal-inflammatory): enriched for [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutants, [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) wild-type, CIMP-high.
  - C2 (proximal-proliferative subset): enriched for Western (especially USA) patients, TP53/EGFR wild-type, intermediate CIMP.
  - C3 (dominant proximal-proliferative): enriched for Vietnamese patients and [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) mutation.
  - C4 (terminal respiratory unit): enriched for [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) mutations, female sex, Chinese nationality; essentially devoid of [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) or STK11 mutations. Most [EML4](../genes/EML4.md)-[ALK](../genes/ALK.md) fusion tumors assigned to C4.
- **Kinase fusion landscape:** rearrangements involving [ALK](../genes/ALK.md), [ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md), [RET](../genes/RET.md), and [PTK2](../genes/PTK2.md) were most recurrent; oncogenic kinases generally formed in-frame fusions while tumor-suppressor kinases (STK11, STK4, [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), FRK, EPHA1) showed disruptive out-of-frame events. Seven in-frame ALK fusions used 5′ partners [EML4](../genes/EML4.md) and novel HMBOX1 and ANKRD36B.
- **ALK phosphosite Y1507** was specifically and dramatically increased in ALK-fusion tumors (validated by IHC with phospho-Y1507 antibody); fusion-specific phosphorylation of partner-side residues EML4-Y226 and HMBOX-S141 was also seen only in fusion-positive samples.
- **Cis/trans CNA effects:** 6,043 RNA, 2,354 protein, and 244 phosphoprotein significant positive cis-correlations; only 156 cis-effects overlapped across all three layers. Twelve cancer-associated genes ([CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md), [KMT2B](../genes/KMT2B.md), PSIP1, [AKT2](../genes/AKT2.md), [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md), GMPS, [IL6ST](../genes/IL6ST.md), [IRF6](../genes/IRF6.md), NFKB2, [PHF6](../genes/PHF6.md), [YES1](../genes/YES1.md), ZBTB7B) showed overlapping cis-regulation.
- **Methylation cis-effects:** 120 methylation-driven cis-effects coordinated across RNA, protein, and phosphoprotein (85/120 supported by promoter-region probes), including LUAD-relevant [CLDN18](../genes/CLDN18.md), [ANK1](../genes/ANK1.md), and [PTPRCAP](../genes/PTPRCAP.md).
- **Driver-mutation effects on the proteome:** [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) mutations elevated cognate protein/phosphosite abundance, while STK11, [RBM10](../genes/RBM10.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), and [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md) mutations reduced them.
- **TP53 mutant tumors** showed proteomic upregulation of mismatch repair ([MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md), [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md), [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md)) and DNA-damage-response ([ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [ATR](../genes/ATR.md), [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md)) proteins, elevated [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) protein relative to RNA, and downregulation of Wnt-signaling proteins ([AXIN1](../genes/AXIN1.md), [TCF7L2](../genes/TCF7L2.md)).
- **KEAP1/NFE2L2 axis:** 12 tumors carried KEAP1 mutations that did not affect RNA but generally reduced KEAP1 protein and increased [NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md) phosphorylation at S215 and S433 (FDR <0.05). A BTB-domain missense KEAP1 G511V mutation preserved KEAP1 protein but produced amongst the highest NFE2L2 phosphorylation — proposed to disrupt KEAP1–NFE2L2 binding rather than KEAP1 stability.
- **KRAS-mutant tumors:** extreme outlier phosphorylation of SOS1 S1161 (a GEF that activates [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)), nominating SOS1 inhibition as a therapeutic strategy.
- **EGFR-mutant tumors:** highly consistent outlier tyrosine phosphorylation of PTPN11/Shp2 at Y62 (in the N-SH2/PTP interface) without changes in RNA or protein abundance — the first such report in primary treatment-naïve LUAD. ALK-fusion tumors showed [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) phosphorylation at C-terminal Y546/Y584. The authors nominate EGFR-mutant and ALK-fusion LUAD as promising target populations for PTPN11/Shp2 inhibitors.
- **Outlier kinase analysis** (FDR <0.20) identified driver-specific hyperphosphorylated kinases, often without corresponding CNA/RNA/protein changes: EGFR in EGFR-mutants, [PRKCD](../genes/PRKCD.md) in KRAS-mutants, [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) in TP53-mutants, [WEE1](../genes/WEE1.md) in EML4-ALK fusions. 27 additional putatively druggable kinases were nominated with known but non-FDA-approved inhibitors.
- **Immune landscape:** three immune clusters (Hot-tumor-enriched HTE, Cold-tumor-enriched CTE, NAT-enriched). HTE was enriched for multi-omics cluster C1 and CIMP-low status, with upregulated PD1, PD-L1, [CTLA4](../genes/CTLA4.md) (FDR <1E-10), [FOXP3](../genes/FOXP3.md) (FDR <0.0001), TGF-β, and IL-10. [IDO1](../genes/IDO1.md) protein was upregulated in HTE and IFNG-high clusters, supporting IDO1 inhibition as an opportunity in immune-hot LUAD.
- **STK11 immune-cold signature:** STK11-mutant tumors showed the most dramatic immune downregulation, with reductions in xCell-derived dendritic-cell, NK T-cell, and macrophage signatures (FDR <0.1); ESTIMATE immune scores were lowest in KRAS-wildtype STK11 mutants. A deep-learning model trained on histopathology predicted STK11 mutation status with 94% slide-level accuracy.
- **Neutrophil degranulation** emerged as the proteomic pathway most strongly associated with STK11 mutation; all 16 measured neutrophil-degranulation proteins (CAMP, [LTF](../genes/LTF.md), BPI, MMP8, MMP9, MPO, LCN2, ELANE, ARG1, others) were coherently overexpressed in STK11-mutant tumors despite no enrichment in neutrophil counts — proteins are stored in granules so the signal was undetectable at the RNA level.
- **Smoking signature:** ~50% of mutations were GG→TT/CC→AA dinucleotide changes; smoking-high samples correlated with polycyclic-aromatic-hydrocarbon environmental signatures (DBADE, DBA, 5-Methylchrysene). Tumors had higher RNA stemness than NATs, with HSS samples higher than [LSS](../genes/LSS.md) within both tumors and NATs (field cancerization effect).
- **Never-smoker biology:** [ARHGEF5](../genes/ARHGEF5.md) protein and its Y1370 phosphosite were elevated in non-smokers, highest in ALK-fusion patients; the negative Rho-GTPase regulator SRGAP1 was suppressed — hypothesized to drive tumorigenesis through Rho-GTPase hyperactivation in this subset.
- **Tumor–NAT biomarker candidates:** 289 proteins upregulated at the protein level using stringent thresholds (log2 FC >2, FDR <0.01, differential in ≥90% of pairs); 60 were also RNA-differential, including 5 (GFPT1, [BZW2](../genes/BZW2.md), PDIA4, P4HB, PMM2) up in all tumors vs paired NATs. [GREM1](../genes/GREM1.md) (log2 FC >5) and OCIAD2 (log2 FC >4) were highlighted as poor-prognosis markers.
- **Cancer-testis antigens / neoantigens:** 44 CT antigens recurrently overexpressed; 9 in ≥10% of samples. KIF2C was the most ubiquitous (63% of samples). 2,481 mRNA-validated and 49 peptide-validated somatic mutation–derived neoantigens identified across 104 patients. 97 samples showed evidence of CT antigens or neoantigens.

## Genes & alterations

- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) — activating mutations enriched in cluster C4 (terminal respiratory unit), associated with female sex and Chinese nationality. EGFR-mutant tumors showed elevated cognate protein/phosphosite abundance, outlier PTPN11/Shp2 Y62 phosphorylation, elevated [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) protein/phosphoprotein (despite reduced RNA), [MET](../genes/MET.md) overexpression, and ROCK1 pathway activation.
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — mutant tumors showed outlier SOS1 S1161 phosphorylation, hyperphosphorylated PRKCD, MAPK1/TAK1 pathway activation, upregulation of complement/coagulation cascades, and enrichment for chemokine CXCL8 and immune target THY1.
- [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) / [EML4](../genes/EML4.md) — seven in-frame ALK fusions (EML4-ALK, novel HMBOX1-ALK, ANKRD36B-ALK) clustered in C4 with miRNA cluster enriched for miR-494/495/496. Outlier ALK Y1507 phosphorylation specific to fusion-positive samples, validated by IHC. Hyperphosphorylated WEE1 and PTPN11 (Y546, Y584).
- [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) — predominantly disruptive out-of-frame fusion events plus point mutations; enriched in cluster C3 and in Vietnamese patients. Strongly associated with immune-cold phenotype, low ESTIMATE immune scores, neutrophil-degranulation protein signature, and amino-acid-metabolism/urea-cycle perturbation. STK11 mutation was associated with increased [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) S138 phosphorylation.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — mutant tumors enriched in C1; elevated cognate protein/phosphosite, upregulation of MMR (MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2) and DDR (ATM, ATR, BRCA1) proteins, elevated EZH2 protein, downregulated Wnt-signaling proteins (AXIN1, TCF7L2). High expression of CCNA2, [TOP2A](../genes/TOP2A.md), [PLOD2](../genes/PLOD2.md), ANLN, MMP12, [CDK1](../genes/CDK1.md), [CCNB1](../genes/CCNB1.md). Outlier BRAF hyperphosphorylation.
- [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md) / [NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md) — 12 KEAP1-mutant tumors; KEAP1 mutation generally reduced KEAP1 protein and increased NFE2L2 S215/S433 phosphorylation. KEAP1 G511V (BTB domain) preserved KEAP1 protein but produced high NFE2L2 phosphorylation, suggesting binding disruption rather than degradation.
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — mutations associated with increased [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) protein abundance, potentially conferring CDK4/6-inhibitor resistance.
- [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) — mutations led to increased [SMAD2](../genes/SMAD2.md) protein expression.
- [RBM10](../genes/RBM10.md) / [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) — mutations reduced cognate protein and phosphosite abundance.
- [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) — Y62 phosphorylation in EGFR-mutants, Y546/Y584 phosphorylation in ALK-fusions; nominated as priority therapeutic target.
- [SOS1](../genes/SOS1.md) — S1161 C-terminal phosphorylation in KRAS-mutant tumors.
- [IDO1](../genes/IDO1.md) — protein upregulated in immune-hot and IFNG-high clusters.
- [ARHGEF5](../genes/ARHGEF5.md) — protein and Y1370 phosphosite elevated in never-smokers, peaking in ALK-fusion patients; nominated as a therapeutic target via auto-inhibitory peptides.
- Methylation cis-effect targets with LUAD relevance: CLDN18, ANK1, PTPRCAP, [BCLAF1](../genes/BCLAF1.md), GSTP1, [MGA](../genes/MGA.md), [TBX3](../genes/TBX3.md).

## Clinical implications

- **PTPN11/Shp2 inhibition** is nominated for EGFR-mutant and EML4-ALK-fusion LUAD based on consistent outlier phosphorylation; multiple Shp2 inhibitors are already in clinical trials.
- **SOS1 inhibition** is proposed for KRAS-mutant LUAD downstream of the SOS1 S1161 phosphorylation signal.
- **IDO1 inhibition** is highlighted as a candidate in immune-hot LUAD where IDO1 protein is upregulated.
- **Anti-CTLA4 therapy** may benefit HTE tumors given high CTLA4/FOXP3 expression and T-reg infiltration.
- **CDK4/6 inhibitor resistance** in RB1-mutant LUAD is suggested by elevated CDK4 protein.
- **VTE management may be stratifiable by mutation type** — coagulation cascades were upregulated in KRAS- and downregulated in EGFR-mutant tumors, consistent with [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) literature showing lower VTE risk in EGFR-wildtype patients.
- **Driver-specific outlier kinases with FDA-approved drugs** include EGFR (EGFR-mutants), PRKCD (KRAS-mutants), BRAF (TP53-mutants), and WEE1 (EML4-ALK fusions).
- **Biomarker candidates for early detection or prognosis** include GFPT1, BZW2, PDIA4, P4HB, PMM2, GREM1, OCIAD2, [DHFR](../genes/DHFR.md), HYOU1, [LDHA](../genes/LDHA.md), CBX8, AKAP4, MORC1, NUF2.
- **Histopathology-based deep learning** predicted STK11 mutation with 94% slide-level accuracy, suggesting a low-cost screening route for immune-cold LUAD identification.

## Limitations & open questions

- Heavy interdependence between mutational status, ethnicity/geography, gender, and smoking status; cohort size (110 tumors) limited the effectiveness of linear-model adjustment for these confounders, frustrating clean attribution of gender- or smoking-specific effects.
- Bulk-tumor analyses lack spatial and cellular resolution needed to disambiguate tumor-epithelium versus microenvironment contributions; single-cell or spatial proteogenomic approaches were called out as future directions.
- The associations described are explicitly hypothesis-generating and require functional validation before being treated as biological conclusions.
- Strong correlations between cigarette-smoking PAH signatures and apparently unrelated environmental signatures (Figure S6C) caution against over-interpretation of mutational-signature attributions and motivate richer environmental/occupational annotation.
- The neutrophil-degranulation signal in STK11-mutant tumors was detected only at the proteome level (granule storage explains the RNA invisibility); the functional contribution of these proteins to immune-cold biology remains to be tested.
- Several novel kinase fusions (HMBOX1-ALK, ANKRD36B-ALK) and CT antigens (MORC1, NUF2 in LUAD) require validation of oncogenic / immunogenic roles.
