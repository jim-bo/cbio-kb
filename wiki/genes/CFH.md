# CFH

## Overview

CFH (Complement Factor H) encodes a major regulator of the alternative complement pathway. In cancer, CFH has been identified as a recurrently homozygously deleted gene, suggesting a potential tumor-suppressive role in hepatocellular carcinoma.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Recurrent homozygous deletion in hepatocellular carcinoma ([HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)) [PMID:25822088](../papers/25822088.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)**: Recurrent homozygous deletion identified in a comprehensive genomic landscape study [PMID:25822088](../papers/25822088.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-deleted with [IRF2](../genes/IRF2.md) and [PTPN3](../genes/PTPN3.md) as part of a set of recurrently homozygously deleted genes in [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) [PMID:25822088](../papers/25822088.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting reported in the corpus.

## Open questions

- Functional role of CFH loss in [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) tumorigenesis and whether it affects complement-mediated immune evasion remains to be established.
