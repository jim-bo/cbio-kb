# YWHAE

## Overview

YWHAE (14-3-3 epsilon) encodes a member of the 14-3-3 protein family, which acts as a regulatory scaffold for signal transduction pathways. In oncology, YWHAE is best known as the 5' fusion partner in YWHAE-NUTM2A/B rearrangements, which define a subset of high-grade endometrial stromal sarcomas (HGESSs) with distinctive histology and aggressive behavior. These fusions are used as diagnostic markers to classify uterine sarcomas that lack typical JAZF1-SUZ12 or other low-grade [ESS](../cancer_types/ESS.md) fusions.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- YWHAE/NUTM2A and YWHAE/NUTM2B rearrangements identified by MSK-Fusion in two high-grade, non-pleomorphic stromal sarcomas within a uterine sarcoma cohort sequenced by MSK-IMPACT, supporting their diagnostic utility for high-grade endometrial stromal sarcoma classification [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[LGESS](../cancer_types/LGESS.md) / High-grade endometrial stromal sarcoma:** YWHAE/NUTM2 rearrangements serve as diagnostic markers distinguishing high-grade ESS from other uterine sarcomas; detected in uterine sarcoma profiling cohort at MSK [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- YWHAE fusions are mutually exclusive with JAZF1-SUZ12 fusions (the canonical low-grade ESS fusion); presence of YWHAE/NUTM2 rearrangement indicates high-grade rather than low-grade ESS biology [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No targeted therapy directly approved for YWHAE-fusion sarcomas as of this corpus; fusion detection primarily has diagnostic/prognostic rather than therapeutic implications in current practice [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)

## Open questions

- The optimal treatment strategy for YWHAE-NUTM2A/B high-grade ESS remains undefined; clinical outcome data for fusion-positive cases in the MSK cohort were not separately reported [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)
