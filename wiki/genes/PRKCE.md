# PRKCE

## Overview

PRKCE encodes protein kinase C epsilon (PKC-epsilon), a calcium-independent member of the PKC family of serine/threonine kinases. PKC-epsilon is involved in cell growth, survival, and differentiation, and has been implicated in cancer through its role as a beta-catenin binding partner. In hepatocellular carcinoma, PRKCE was identified as one of the CTNNB1-mutant differentially-expressed proteins not also differential at the mRNA level, implicating post-transcriptional regulation.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Among the 10 CTNNB1-mutant differentially-expressed proteins NOT also differential at mRNA in a 122-patient [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) multi-omics cohort, implying post-transcriptional regulation; PRKCE functions as a beta-catenin binding partner [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)**: Post-transcriptionally upregulated protein in CTNNB1-mutant HCCs in a European multi-omics cohort [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Protein-level differential expression in [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md)-mutant HCCs; grouped with [TNRC6B](../genes/TNRC6B.md) and [PPIE](../genes/PPIE.md) as evidence of post-transcriptional regulation in the Wnt/beta-catenin axis [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- PRKCE as a beta-catenin binding partner suggests potential relevance to Wnt-pathway-directed therapies in CTNNB1-mutant HCC [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Open questions

- Whether PRKCE protein upregulation is driver or passenger in CTNNB1-mutant HCCs and whether it represents a druggable vulnerability is unresolved.
