# KNSTRN

## Overview

KNSTRN (kinetochore-localized astrin/SPAG5 binding protein) encodes a kinetochore protein involved in chromosome segregation and mitotic fidelity. Recurrent somatic mutations in KNSTRN have been described in cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma, marking it as a novel cSCC driver gene with a UV mutational signature.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- KNSTRN was cited as a recently described cSCC driver gene discussed in the context of metastatic cSCC genomic profiling (n=29 lymph-node metastases); it was not covered by the OncoPanelv2 (504-gene) capture reagent used in this study and thus could not be directly assessed. [PMID:25589618](../papers/25589618.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md)**: KNSTRN has been nominated as a driver gene in primary cSCC based on recurrent UV-pattern somatic mutations; its prevalence and significance in metastatic cSCC remains uncharacterized due to panel coverage limitations. [PMID:25589618](../papers/25589618.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Relationship to canonical cSCC drivers ([TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md)) not assessed in this cohort. [PMID:25589618](../papers/25589618.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No therapeutic associations reported. [PMID:25589618](../papers/25589618.md)

## Open questions

- Whether KNSTRN mutations are enriched in primary versus metastatic cSCC and whether they overlap with RAS/RTK/PI3K activation events is unresolved. [PMID:25589618](../papers/25589618.md)
