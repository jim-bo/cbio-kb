## TL;DR

The TCGA chromophobe renal cell carcinoma (ChRCC) project characterized 66 primary ChRCC tumors using whole-exome sequencing, whole-genome sequencing (n=50), mtDNA sequencing (n=61), RNA-seq, miRNA-seq, DNA methylation (HM450), and Affymetrix SNP6 arrays. ChRCC has a low exonic mutation rate (~0.4/Mb, ~3-fold lower than ccRCC), with only two MutSig-significant genes ([TP53](../genes/TP53.md) at 32% and [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) at 9%) and a characteristic loss of whole chromosomes 1, 2, 6, 10, 13, and 17 in 86% of cases. DNA methylation and gene-expression patterns place ChRCC origin in the distal nephron, distinct from ccRCC's proximal-tubule origin. Recurrent mtDNA mutations (notably MT-ND5 in 6/61 cases) and increased mitochondrial biogenesis indicate elevated oxidative phosphorylation — counter to the Warburg pattern of ccRCC. Most strikingly, WGS uncovered recurrent genomic rearrangement breakpoints within the [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter region in 6/50 cases, associated with massively elevated [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) expression and kataegis — a novel TERT-upregulation mechanism distinct from C228T/C250T point mutations or amplification ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).

## Cohort & data

- 66 primary ChRCC ([CHRCC](../cancer_types/CHRCC.md)) tumors with matched normal tissue/blood collected by TCGA; biospecimens from BWH, MSKCC, NCI, and MD Anderson ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- Histologic subtypes by expert consensus pathology review: classic (n=47), eosinophilic (n=19) ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- Dataset: [kich_tcga_pub](../datasets/kich_tcga_pub.md). Comparative cohort: 417 ccRCC tumors ([CCRCC](../cancer_types/CCRCC.md)) from TCGA.
- Platforms: [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) (66 cases, NimbleGen VCRome 2.1 42MB, Illumina HiSeq, 90% target coverage ≥20×), [whole-genome-seq](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) (50 cases, 60× tumor / 30× normal), [mtdna-long-range-pcr](../methods/mtdna-long-range-pcr.md) (61 cases), [rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md), [mirna-seq](../methods/mirna-seq.md), [hm450-methylation-array](../methods/hm450-methylation-array.md), [affymetrix-snp6](../methods/affymetrix-snp6.md) ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- Analytical pipelines: Mercury alignment, [gistic](../methods/gistic.md) for copy number, [mutsig](../methods/mutsig.md) for significantly mutated genes, [meerkat](../methods/meerkat.md) for structural rearrangements ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).

## Key findings

- **Aneuploidy signature.** 86% (57/66) of ChRCC showed loss of one copy of whole chromosomes 1, 2, 6, 10, 13, and 17; additional whole-chromosome losses of 3, 5, 8, 9, 11, 18, and 21 at 12–58% frequency. No focal copy-number events by GISTIC, indicating a simpler chromosomal landscape than ccRCC. All 47 classic ChRCC showed this pattern; only 10/19 eosinophilic cases did, with 4 eosinophilic cases having no CNAs at all ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- **Low mutation burden, two MutSig-significant genes.** Median exonic somatic mutation rate ~0.4/Mb (~3-fold below ccRCC). One outlier case had >10/Mb mutations with a mismatch-repair-deficiency signature. Only [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (q<0.1, mutated in 21/66 = 32%) and [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) (6/66 = 9% nonsilent, plus 2 homozygous deletions) reached MutSig significance ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- **mTOR-pathway involvement.** Beyond PTEN, [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md) (2 cases), [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md)/[TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) (4 cases combined), and one activating [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) mutation contributed; in total mTOR-pathway alterations spanned 15/66 (23%) of ChRCC ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- **Distinct DNA methylation and cell of origin.** Differential methylation at >64,000 of ~450,000 HM450 loci between ChRCC and ccRCC (p<0.001, beta diff >0.1). ChRCC showed more hypomethylation. [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)/p16 was epigenetically silenced in 4 ChRCC cases. Gene-expression correlation against a nephron atlas (Cheval et al., 2012) placed ChRCC's origin in the distal nephron and ccRCC's in the proximal tubule ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- **Mitochondrial activation.** Nuclear-encoded Krebs cycle and electron transport chain genes were broadly upregulated in ChRCC vs normal kidney, with elevated [PPARGC1A](../genes/PPARGC1A.md) (mitochondrial biogenesis regulator, p<1E-5) and ~4× mitochondrial genome copy number in ChRCC vs normal — opposite the transcriptional suppression of mitochondrial genes seen in ccRCC ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- **Recurrent mtDNA mutations, especially in Complex I.** Across 61 ChRCC cases, 142 somatic mtDNA events were called (75 in the D-Loop); 35 events in 27 cases at >50% heteroplasmy. Complex I gene mutations occurred in 11/61 (18%) cases. MT-ND5 was the most frequently altered mtDNA gene (6 cases, all >70% heteroplasmy); 5 of these 6 were histologically eosinophilic (p<0.01, Fisher's exact) and 3 had no CNAs (p<0.002). MT-ND5-mutated cases showed a distinct 719-gene transcriptional signature (FDR<0.05) enriched for mitochondrial genes including [SDHB](../genes/SDHB.md) and [NDUFS1](../genes/NDUFS1.md) ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- **Genomic rearrangements and kataegis.** WGS (Meerkat) detected an average of 16 rearrangements per case (range 0–207) without recurrent gene fusions. A subset of cases showed kataegis (highly localized C>T or C>G mutation clusters) co-occurring with rearrangements. APOBEC-pattern mutagenesis was significantly enriched in kataegis regions and across the whole genome in 6 cases; [APOBEC3B](../genes/APOBEC3B.md) mRNA was elevated in ChRCC vs normal kidney ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- **TERT-promoter structural rearrangements (novel mechanism).** Genomic rearrangement breakpoints fell within ~10 kb upstream of the [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) transcription start site in 6/50 ChRCC by WGS (confirmed by PCR + Sanger in 6 cases). These cases had the highest TERT expression (average >500 units; p<1E-20 by t-test), well above the 3 cases harboring TERT-promoter C228T point mutations (~1 unit average). 5 of 6 rearrangements were intrachromosomal (one involved [PDCD6](../genes/PDCD6.md)); the sixth involved [NEK5](../genes/NEK5.md) on chromosome 13. 3 of the 6 also showed the strongest kataegis (p=0.001, Fisher's exact). The variants were estimated to be present in nearly all tumor cells, suggesting they are early/driver events ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- **Survival correlates.** mRNA, miRNA, and DNA methylation correlates of patient survival in ChRCC overlapped with cell-cycle correlates previously identified in ccRCC, but did *not* include the Warburg-effect-like signature seen in aggressive ccRCC ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — somatic mutation in 21/66 (32%); MutSig q<0.1. Mutations correlated with decreased expression of p53 transcriptional targets ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — nonsilent somatic mutation in 6/66 (9%) plus 2 homozygous deletions; MutSig q<0.1. Germline PTEN mutations underlie Cowden syndrome (predisposes to ChRCC) ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) — recurrent genomic rearrangement breakpoints in 6/50 ChRCC within ~10 kb upstream of the TSS, associated with >500-unit TERT expression — a novel TERT up-regulation mechanism distinct from C228T/C250T promoter point mutations (also seen here in 3 cases at low expression) and from amplification ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md) — somatic mutation in 2/66 ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md), [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) — somatic mutations in 4/66 (combined). Germline TSC1/TSC2 (tuberous sclerosis complex) predisposes to ChRCC ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) — one activating somatic mutation ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- [FLCN](../genes/FLCN.md) — germline mutations cause Birt-Hogg-Dubé syndrome; 34% of BHD-associated kidney tumors are ChRCC. [FLCN](../genes/FLCN.md) is not somatically recurrent in this sporadic cohort but is invoked as a pathway-convergence point ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — epigenetically silenced in 4 ChRCC cases ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- [PPARGC1A](../genes/PPARGC1A.md) — significantly elevated mRNA in ChRCC vs normal kidney (p<1E-5), consistent with increased mitochondrial biogenesis ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- [APOBEC3B](../genes/APOBEC3B.md) — elevated mRNA in ChRCC vs normal; APOBEC mutational signature enriched in kataegis-bearing cases ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- [PDCD6](../genes/PDCD6.md) — partner in one intrachromosomal TERT-promoter rearrangement ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- [NEK5](../genes/NEK5.md) — partner in the one inter-chromosomal TERT rearrangement on chromosome 13 ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- [SDHB](../genes/SDHB.md), [NDUFS1](../genes/NDUFS1.md) — among 43 mitochondrial-GO genes elevated in MT-ND5-mutated cases ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- MT-ND5 (mitochondrially encoded NADH dehydrogenase 5; not a HUGO nuclear gene) — somatic mtDNA mutations in 6/61 cases at >70% heteroplasmy; most frequently altered Complex I gene ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md) — invoked as a precedent for discovering frequent kidney-cancer driver mutations from modest sample sizes (originally identified in ccRCC from 25 tumors); not somatically recurrent in this ChRCC cohort ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).

## Clinical implications

- **ChRCC is biologically distinct from ccRCC and warrants disease-specific therapeutic strategies** rather than adopting ccRCC regimens — driven by different cell of origin, opposite mitochondrial-metabolism phenotype (non-Warburg), and largely non-overlapping driver genetics ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- **mTOR-pathway alterations in 23% of ChRCC** (PTEN, [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md), TSC1/2, [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) combined) suggest a subset of ChRCC patients may be candidates for mTOR-pathway-directed therapy, though the paper does not test agents directly ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- **TERT-promoter structural rearrangements** define a previously unrecognized telomerase-activation mechanism; the authors suggest comparable structural events may operate at TERT or other drivers in other cancer types and should be sought by WGS analysis ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- **Histologic subtype tracks with genomics.** Eosinophilic ChRCC is enriched for MT-ND5 mutations and includes a subset with no CNAs, suggesting subtype-aware classification could be diagnostically useful ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).

## Limitations & open questions

- Single biopsy per tumor — intra-tumor and inter-biopsy heterogeneity not addressed; sub-clonal/temporal ordering of mtDNA, structural, and TERT-promoter events not resolved (the authors flag this explicitly) ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- N=66 limited statistical power for genes mutated below ~10% frequency; many cancer-relevant low-frequency mutations (e.g., MTOR n=2, NRAS n=1) cannot be called recurrent in this cohort ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- The precise mechanism by which TERT-promoter rearrangements upregulate TERT (cis-regulatory element relocation vs escape from condensed chromatin) remains unresolved ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- Whether MT-ND5/Complex I mutations cause compensatory mitochondrial biogenesis, or alternative metabolic adaptations, is open — the paper rules out a simple Warburg-like loss-of-OXPHOS interpretation but does not establish the causal alternative ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
- ~47% of cases lack alterations in either the p53 or PTEN pathways, suggesting additional driver mechanisms (possibly in non-exonic regions) remain to be discovered ([PMID:25155756](../papers/25155756.md)).
