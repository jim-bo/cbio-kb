**PMID:** 26544944 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.cell.2015.10.025 · **Journal:** Cell (2015)

## TL;DR

The TCGA Research Network performed integrated multi-platform molecular characterization of 333 primary prostate adenocarcinomas (cohort [`prad_tcga_pub`](../datasets/prad_tcga_pub.md), [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md)) using whole-exome and whole-genome sequencing, SNP arrays, DNA methylation arrays, mRNA/miRNA sequencing, and RPPA. 74% of tumors fell into one of seven mutually exclusive molecular subtypes defined by fusions of [ERG](../genes/ERG.md) (46%), [ETV1](../genes/ETV1.md) (8%), [ETV4](../genes/ETV4.md) (4%), or [FLI1](../genes/FLI1.md) (1%), or hotspot mutations in [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md) (11%), [FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md) (3%), or [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) (1%). 25% of tumors carried potentially actionable PI3K/MAPK pathway lesions, and 19% had DNA repair gene defects relevant to PARP inhibitor therapy.

## Cohort & data

- **N = 333 primary prostate adenocarcinomas** from radical prostatectomy specimens; final cohort drawn from 425 cases after pathology and quality-control review ([PMID:26544944](../papers/26544944.md)).
- Cancer type: [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) (prostate adenocarcinoma); dataset: [`prad_tcga_pub`](../datasets/prad_tcga_pub.md).
- Platforms: [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) (333 tumor/normal pairs), [whole-genome-seq](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) (low-pass on 100 pairs; high-pass on 19), [affymetrix-snp6](../methods/affymetrix-snp6.md) for copy number, [hm450-methylation-array](../methods/hm450-methylation-array.md), [rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md), [mirna-seq](../methods/mirna-seq.md) (330 samples), and [rppa](../methods/rppa.md) (152 samples). 19 matched non-malignant adjacent prostate samples were profiled for methylation and RNA/miRNA expression.
- Integrative clustering with [icluster](../methods/icluster.md); significantly mutated genes via [mutsig](../methods/mutsig.md) (MutSigCV); focal SCNAs via [gistic](../methods/gistic.md) (GISTIC 2.0); tumor purity estimated via [absolute](../methods/absolute.md) and other DNA/RNA methods.
- Average follow-up under two years; outcome analysis was not possible.

## Key findings

- **Seven mutually exclusive molecular subtypes** capture 74% of primary prostate cancers ([PMID:26544944](../papers/26544944.md)):
  - ETS-family fusions: [ERG](../genes/ERG.md) 46%, [ETV1](../genes/ETV1.md) 8%, [ETV4](../genes/ETV4.md) 4%, [FLI1](../genes/FLI1.md) 1% (53% total ETS-fusion-positive).
  - Recurrent mutations: [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md) 11%, [FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md) 3%, [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) 1%.
- **[TMPRSS2](../genes/TMPRSS2.md) is the dominant 5' fusion partner**; additional androgen-regulated partners include [SLC45A3](../genes/SLC45A3.md) and [NDRG1](../genes/NDRG1.md). Tumors with elevated full-length ETS transcripts but no detectable fusion (12 [ETV1](../genes/ETV1.md)-high, 6 [ETV4](../genes/ETV4.md), 2 [FLI1](../genes/FLI1.md)) were identified, potentially driven by cryptic translocations or epigenetic mechanisms.
- **Overall mutational burden** is 0.94 mutations/Mb (median; range 0.04–28), or ~19 non-synonymous mutations per tumor (median; IQR 13–25) — lower than most epithelial tumors without an exogenous mutagen.
- **13 significantly mutated genes** (q < 0.1): [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [MED12](../genes/MED12.md), [CDKN1B](../genes/CDKN1B.md), plus newly identified [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md), [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [ZMYM3](../genes/ZMYM3.md), and [NKX3-1](../genes/NKX3-1.md). Subthreshold but biologically meaningful: [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), [APC](../genes/APC.md), [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md).
- **Focal SCNAs** by GISTIC 2.0: 20 amplifications and 35 deletions (q < 0.25). Homozygous [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) deletion occurred in 15% — one of the highest rates of any TCGA tumor type. Other recurrent amplifications: [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) 8q24.21 (8%), [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md)/[WHSC1L1](../genes/WHSC1L1.md) 8p11.23 (8%), [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) 11q13.2 (2%). Heterozygous [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) loss (13q13.1) almost always co-occurred with [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) loss (13q14.2).
- **SCNA burden stratification** identified three groups (quiet, intermediate, high-burden); the high-burden group had significantly higher Gleason scores, PSA, and tumor cellularity.
- **Four epigenetic clusters** from methylation profiling. [ERG](../genes/ERG.md) fusion-positive tumors split between a low/moderate-methylation cluster (3) and a distinct hypermethylated cluster (1) with ~twice the number of hypermethylated loci.
- **[IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) R132 mutations define a CIMP-like subgroup** with even greater genome-wide hypermethylation than IDH1-mutant [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) or [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md), fewer SCNAs, ETS-fusion-negative, [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md) wild-type, and associated with younger age at diagnosis (Figure 3B).
- **Epigenetic silencing of 164 genes** identified by integrating methylation and mRNA expression. SHF, FAXDC2, [GSTP1](../genes/GSTP1.md), ZNF154, KLF8 silenced in >85% of tumors; [STAT6](../genes/STAT6.md) silenced predominantly in ETS-fusion-positive tumors; HEXA silenced in 86.5% of SPOP-mutant vs 14.5% of ERG-fusion tumors (p<5.4·10⁻¹⁵).
- **Androgen receptor ([AR](../genes/AR.md)) activity is variable** across all subtypes. Tumors with [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md) or [FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md) mutations had the highest [AR](../genes/AR.md) transcriptional activity (p = 1.1·10⁻⁶ and 0.04 respectively, t-test).
- **[AR](../genes/AR.md) splice variants (notably AR-V7)** detected at low levels in hormone-naive primary tumors and occasionally in adjacent benign tissue — previously assumed restricted to metastatic castration-resistant disease.
- **SPOP/FOXA1 co-mutation** observed in 4 tumors, both alterations clonal in all cases (i.e., same tumor cells).
- **DNA repair gene inactivation in 19% of tumors** (Figure 5A) — including [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) 3% (germline + somatic), [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) 1 case, [CDK12](../genes/CDK12.md) 1%, [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) 3 cases, [FANCD2](../genes/FANCD2.md) lesions (6% focal heterozygous loss + truncating/homozygous deletions), [RAD51C](../genes/RAD51C.md) 3%.
- **PI3K/MAPK pathway lesions in ~25%**: [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) deletion/mutation 17%, [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) hotspot mutations (6 tumors), [PIK3CB](../genes/PIK3CB.md) mutations in 2 PTEN-deleted tumors, [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) E17K and D323Y mutations (3 tumors total), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) Q61R hotspot (3/4 mutants), and 8 [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) mutations (none V600E; includes K601E, G469A, L597R, and a K601 in-frame 3-aa deletion).
- **Comparison with metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer** (Robinson et al. 2015 cohort, n=150): subtype distribution similar except no [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) mutants in mets; metastatic tumors had higher SCNA burden, higher mutational burden, more frequent [AR](../genes/AR.md) amplification/mutation (essentially absent in primary), more frequent DNA repair, [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) alterations; [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md) mutations slightly less frequent in mets (8% vs 11%).

## Genes & alterations

- **[ERG](../genes/ERG.md)** — Most common ETS fusion (46%); TMPRSS2-ERG dominant. ERG-fusion-positive tumors split between low-methylation and hypermethylated epigenetic subclusters. Co-occurs preferentially with [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) deletion.
- **[ETV1](../genes/ETV1.md), [ETV4](../genes/ETV4.md), [FLI1](../genes/FLI1.md)** — Define less common ETS-fusion subtypes (8%, 4%, 1%). Cryptic [ETV1](../genes/ETV1.md) rearrangement to chr14 near MIPOL1/FOXA1 identified in one high-expressing case lacking a canonical fusion. Three tumors had fusions involving more than one ETS gene, likely reflecting convergent clonal evolution.
- **[TMPRSS2](../genes/TMPRSS2.md)** — Dominant 5' androgen-regulated fusion partner.
- **[SLC45A3](../genes/SLC45A3.md), [NDRG1](../genes/NDRG1.md)** — Alternative androgen-regulated 5' fusion partners.
- **[SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md)** — Hotspot mutations in 11%; mutually exclusive with ETS fusions; defines a subtype with [CHD1](../genes/CHD1.md) deletion, elevated methylation, homogeneous expression, [SPINK1](../genes/SPINK1.md) overexpression, and highest AR transcriptional output.
- **[FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md)** — Mutated in 3–4%; missense mutations cluster in the winged-helix DNA-binding domain but not at DNA-binding residues; mutually exclusive with all other subtype-defining alterations except [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md) (4 co-mutated tumors, both clonal). [FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md) mutants also have elevated AR output.
- **[IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md)** — R132 hotspot mutations in ~1% define a CIMP-like, ETS-negative, SPOP-wt, SCNA-quiet subtype with younger age at diagnosis. Genome-wide hypermethylation exceeds that of IDH1-mut [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) and [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md).
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)** — Recurrently mutated; enriched in the 26% "unclassified" subset with high SCNA burden.
- **[PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md)** — Homozygous deletion in 15% (highest rates among TCGA tumor types); total alteration 17%; preferentially co-occurs with [ERG](../genes/ERG.md) fusion.
- **[CHD1](../genes/CHD1.md)** — Co-deletion with SPOP-mutant subtype (5q15-q21). [MAP3K7](../genes/MAP3K7.md) (6q12-22) and [CHD1](../genes/CHD1.md) co-deletion associated with aggressive ETS-negative disease.
- **[MED12](../genes/MED12.md), [CDKN1B](../genes/CDKN1B.md), [NKX3-1](../genes/NKX3-1.md), [ZMYM3](../genes/ZMYM3.md)** — Significantly mutated; [ZMYM3](../genes/ZMYM3.md) (2%) is novel in prostate cancer; [NKX3-1](../genes/NKX3-1.md) somatically mutated in 1% (also commonly deleted).
- **[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)** — 2.4% mutation rate (higher than previously reported); 8 mutations, none V600E. Includes K601E, G469A, L597R (MEK-inhibitor sensitive), a K601 in-frame deletion, F468C, and others.
- **[HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), [RAC1](../genes/RAC1.md), [RRAS2](../genes/RRAS2.md)** — [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) Q61R in 3/4 mutants; [RAC1](../genes/RAC1.md) Q61R and [RRAS2](../genes/RRAS2.md) Q72L each in one tumor (paralogous to RAS Q61).
- **[AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md)** — E17K hotspot in 2 tumors; D323Y in 1 tumor (likely activating, abuts E17K in 3D structure).
- **[PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)** — Hotspot mutations (E545K, Q546K, N345I, C420R, E542A) in 6 tumors; focal amplification with overexpression in ~1%.
- **[PIK3CB](../genes/PIK3CB.md)** — Mutated in 2 PTEN-homozygous-deleted tumors (E552K paralogous to [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) helical-domain hotspot) — suggests combined PI3K/AR inhibition strategy.
- **[ATM](../genes/ATM.md)** — Nonsense mutation in 1 case; kinase-dead N2875 hotspot in 2 cases.
- **[BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md)** — 1 germline frameshift (V923, ClinVar RCV000083190.3).
- **[BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md)** — Inactivation in 3% (germline + somatic). All six [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) germline mutations were K3326* (C-terminal truncation, debated impact). Two tumors had focal homozygous deletions with low transcript.
- **[CDK12](../genes/CDK12.md)** — Loss-of-function or homozygous deletion in 1% (4 tumors).
- **[FANCD2](../genes/FANCD2.md)** — Truncating mutation (1 tumor), homozygous deletion (2), focal heterozygous loss in 6%.
- **[RAD51C](../genes/RAD51C.md)** — Focal DNA losses in 3% (mostly heterozygous).
- **[CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md), [APC](../genes/APC.md)** — Beta-catenin pathway mutations ([APC](../genes/APC.md) truncating).
- **[KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md)** — Truncating chromatin-modifier mutations; enriched in the 26% unclassified subset.
- **[MYC](../genes/MYC.md), [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md)** — Recurrent focal amplifications (8% and 2%).
- **[FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md), [WHSC1L1](../genes/WHSC1L1.md)** — Co-amplified at 8p11.23 (8%).
- **[MAP3K1](../genes/MAP3K1.md), [MAP3K7](../genes/MAP3K7.md)** — Recurrent focal deletions; [MAP3K7](../genes/MAP3K7.md) co-deletion with [CHD1](../genes/CHD1.md).
- **[RB1](../genes/RB1.md)** — Heterozygous loss often co-incident with [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) loss (13q).
- **[SPINK1](../genes/SPINK1.md)** — Frequently overexpressed in SPOP-mutant/CHD1-deleted tumors.
- **[AR](../genes/AR.md)** — Mutation/amplification essentially absent in primary disease but frequent in mCRPC. AR-V7 and other splice variants detectable at low levels in primary tumors.
- **[FOXP1](../genes/FOXP1.md)/[RYBP](../genes/RYBP.md)/[SHQ1](../genes/SHQ1.md)** — Complex 3p13 deletion locus.
- **[SPOPL](../genes/SPOPL.md)** — Recurrent focal deletion (2q22.1).
- **[MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md)** — Rare activating mutations contributing to PI3K-pathway aberration.

## Clinical implications

- **PARP inhibitors (e.g., [olaparib](../drugs/olaparib.md))** — 19% of primary tumors have DNA-repair gene defects ([BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [CDK12](../genes/CDK12.md), [FANCD2](../genes/FANCD2.md), [RAD51C](../genes/RAD51C.md)). The authors explicitly link this to TOPARP-A trial results in mCRPC and propose that PARP-inhibitor strategies could be considered at earlier disease stages ([PMID:26544944](../papers/26544944.md)).
- **PI3K/MAPK pathway inhibitors** — ~25% of tumors carry actionable PI3K or MAPK pathway lesions. PTEN-deleted/PIK3CB-mutant cases may benefit from combined PI3K + androgen-signaling inhibition. Non-V600E [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) mutants (K601E, L597R) may be MEK-inhibitor sensitive.
- **IDH1-targeted therapy** — Rare [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) R132-mutant prostate cancers (~1%) may be candidates for [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) inhibitors, by analogy with [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) and [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md), though clinical follow-up was inadequate to assess prognosis directly.
- **AR-pathway considerations** — Variable AR transcriptional output across subtypes (highest in SPOP/FOXA1-mutant tumors) implies subtype-stratified responses to AR-directed therapies such as [enzalutamide](../drugs/enzalutamide.md) and [abiraterone](../drugs/abiraterone.md). AR-V7 expression — previously a marker of resistance to AR-targeted therapy in mCRPC — is detectable in some hormone-naive primary tumors.
- **Molecular subtyping for risk stratification** — The seven-class taxonomy and SCNA burden subgrouping (quiet/intermediate/high) provide a framework for prognostic and treatment-selection studies, although outcome analysis was precluded in this cohort.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Short clinical follow-up** (average <2 years post-prostatectomy) precluded formal outcome and prognostic analyses.
- **Single tumor focus per patient**, despite known multifocality and inter-focus molecular heterogeneity in primary prostate cancer (Cooper et al. 2015; Boutros et al. 2015; Lindberg et al. 2013) — biomarker panels must account for multifocal disease.
- **26% of tumors lack a taxonomy-defining driver**, even after WGS of 19 high-cellularity unclassified cases; no recurrent regulatory (e.g., TERT-promoter) mutations identified. This subset is clinically and genomically heterogeneous.
- **Lower tumor cellularity** in low-SCNA tumors may partially mask alteration detection (p = 0.0002 for SCNA-low vs others); however, ETS-fusion-positive vs negative cellularity not significantly different (p = 0.32).
- **AR splice variant expression in primary tumors** — functional and prognostic significance unclear since AR-V7 levels did not correlate with AR-target-gene expression or with the seven genomic subtypes.
- **Mechanism of [FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md) missense mutations** — these affect the winged-helix DNA-binding domain but not direct DNA-binding residues; impact may relate to chromatin cofactor interactions rather than DNA binding per se.
- **Drivers of ERG-fusion epigenetic heterogeneity** — why ERG-positive tumors split into two methylation subclusters remains unknown.
- **IDH1-mutant prostate cancer** — too rare here for outcome analysis; prognostic implications and IDH-inhibitor sensitivity remain to be tested in larger cohorts.
