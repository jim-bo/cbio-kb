# WNT5B

## Overview

WNT5B (Wnt family member 5B) encodes a non-canonical Wnt ligand involved in developmental signaling. In cholangiocarcinoma, WNT5B expression is upregulated in the Cluster 2 molecular subtype alongside [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) and [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md), pointing to activated Wnt/PI3K signaling as a feature of this subtype.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Upregulated expression in Cluster 2 cholangiocarcinoma (p < 0.05) alongside CTNNB1 and AKT1 [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Cholangiocarcinoma: WNT5B upregulation is a defining feature of Cluster 2 (Fluke-positive-enriched) molecular subtype in the 489-sample multi-omic CCA cohort [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-upregulated with CTNNB1 and AKT1 in CCA Cluster 2; Cluster 2 tumors are significantly enriched for [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) amplification [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Wnt pathway activation (WNT5B/CTNNB1) in Cluster 2 is a candidate therapeutic vulnerability; no specific Wnt-targeted agent tested in this cohort [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md)

## Open questions

- Whether WNT5B upregulation is a driver or passenger in CCA Cluster 2 remains unresolved; functional validation studies are lacking [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md)
