# GPC2

## Overview

GPC2 (Glypican-2) encodes a glycosylphosphatidylinositol-anchored heparan sulfate proteoglycan that functions as a cell-surface co-receptor involved in Wnt signaling and cell proliferation. In pediatric cancer, GPC2 is dramatically overexpressed on the surface of neuroblastoma cells and in additional pediatric histotypes, making it a high-priority target for antibody-drug conjugates (ADCs) and T-cell-engager immunotherapies.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- GPC2 dramatically overexpressed in neuroblastoma PDX models and in additional pediatric histotypes in the PPTC dataset; flagged as a high-expression immunotherapy target for antibody-drug-conjugate and T-cell-engager basket-trial design [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [NBL](../cancer_types/NBL.md) — high-level surface overexpression in neuroblastoma models; co-flagged with [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) as the top immunotherapy target candidates in this histology [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-overexpressed with [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) in neuroblastoma models; both flagged together as basket-trial immunotherapy targets across pediatric histotypes [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- GPC2 overexpression in neuroblastoma supports its use as an ADC and T-cell-engager target; RNA-seq-defined high expression across multiple pediatric histotypes enables biomarker-driven basket-trial selection in the PPTC resource [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md)

## Open questions

- Breadth of GPC2 overexpression across all 37 pediatric subtypes in the PPTC dataset needs further systematic characterization; functional role of GPC2 overexpression (driver vs. passenger) is not established.
