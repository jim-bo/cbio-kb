# NSD3

## Overview

NSD3 (Nuclear receptor SET domain-containing protein 3, also known as WHSC1L1) is a histone methyltransferase that catalyzes H3K36 methylation. It is located at the 8p11-p12 amplicon, a recurrently amplified region in lung squamous cell carcinoma. NSD3 amplification is thought to promote oncogenesis through epigenetic dysregulation of gene expression.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Amplification at the 8p11-p12 locus identified as a novel somatic copy-number alteration (SCNA) region in lung squamous cell carcinoma (TCGA, 178 tumors) [PMID:22960745](../papers/22960745.md)
- Recurrent 8p11.23 focal amplification (co-amplified with FGFR1) in [LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md); proteomic evidence nominates NSD3 as the primary oncogenic driver of this amplicon, independently validated as a key LSCC chromatin regulator; FGFR1-targeted therapy failure in 8p11.23-amplified LSCC supports NSD3 as the true driver target [PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md): Copy-number amplification at 8p11-p12 locus; identified as a novel SCNA driver region [PMID:22960745](../papers/22960745.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Located in the same 8p11-p12 amplicon as [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md); co-amplification with FGFR1 has been described in [LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No approved targeted therapy; the 8p11-p12 amplicon including FGFR1 is under investigation as a therapeutic target in LUSC.

## Open questions

- Whether NSD3 itself or FGFR1 is the primary oncogenic driver of the 8p11-p12 amplicon in LUSC requires functional characterization.
