# NES

## Overview

NES (Nestin) encodes an intermediate filament protein originally characterized as a neural stem/progenitor cell marker. In cancer, NES is expressed in a variety of tumor types and has been proposed as both a diagnostic biomarker and a prognostic marker in primary liver cancers (PLC). In combined hepatocellular and intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma (cHCC-ICC), Nestin IHC positivity in both hepatocellular and biliary components makes it a more sensitive diagnostic marker than EpCAM, which only marks the biliary component. A TP53-NES transcriptional repression axis (demonstrated in mouse models) may mechanistically link [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) loss to NES upregulation in liver cancer.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- NES (Nestin) protein is positively stained (IHC) in 81.3% of cHCC-ICC tumors; positive in both [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) and ICC components of combined-type cHCC-ICC, unlike EpCAM which is restricted to the ICC component. Proposed as a cHCC-ICC diagnostic and pan-PLC prognostic biomarker. [PMID:31130341](../papers/31130341.md)
- Elevated NES mRNA ([EPCAM](../genes/EPCAM.md), [KRT19](../genes/KRT19.md), [PRDM5](../genes/PRDM5.md), and NES) is enriched in the P1 (combined-type cHCC-ICC) transcriptional cluster, consistent with biliary/progenitor marker expression. [PMID:31130341](../papers/31130341.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- cHCC-ICC (combined hepatocellular and intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma): NES IHC positive in 81.3% of cases vs significantly lower rates in pure HCC and ICC; proposed as a specific diagnostic IHC marker. [PMID:31130341](../papers/31130341.md)
- Pan-PLC prognostic marker: NES-positive patients had a median overall survival of 18.7 months vs 46.6 months for NES-negative patients (p < 0.0001) in a Chinese liver cancer cohort. [PMID:31130341](../papers/31130341.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- TP53 loss may promote NES upregulation via a TP53-NES repression axis described in mouse models (Tschaharganeh et al., 2014); direct demonstration of this axis in cHCC-ICC patients is lacking but referenced in the context of cHCC-ICC biology. [PMID:31130341](../papers/31130341.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- NES is proposed as a diagnostic IHC biomarker for distinguishing cHCC-ICC from pure HCC or ICC, with potential clinical utility in pathology workflows for liver tumor subtyping. No direct therapeutic target role identified.

## Open questions

- The mechanism of NES upregulation in cHCC-ICC has not been directly demonstrated in human tumor samples; the TP53-NES repression axis is inferred from mouse models.
- Prospective validation of NES IHC as a diagnostic criterion for cHCC-ICC classification and as a prognostic marker in non-Asian liver cancer cohorts is needed.
