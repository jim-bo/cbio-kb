# HMGCR

## Overview

HMGCR (3-hydroxy-3-methylglutaryl-CoA reductase) is the rate-limiting enzyme in cholesterol biosynthesis and the primary target of statins. In [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md), HMGCR is implicated in cholesterol-driven tumor progression.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- HMGCR is a cholesterol biosynthesis enzyme implicated in HCC; targeting the cholesterol pathway (HMGCR, [SQLE](../genes/SQLE.md), [MVK](../genes/MVK.md)) with statins or pathway-specific inhibitors is proposed as a therapeutic strategy [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) — HMGCR-driven cholesterol biosynthesis is a metabolic vulnerability in HCC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Part of the HMGCR/SQLE/MVK cholesterol biosynthesis axis in HCC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Statins (HMGCR inhibitors) are proposed as low-cost, low-toxicity adjuncts to HCC therapy; retrospective data suggest reduced HCC incidence in statin users [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Open questions

- Prospective validation of statins as HCC-specific therapeutic agents is lacking; clinical benefit in established HCC remains unproven.
