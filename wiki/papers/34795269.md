**PMID:** 34795269 · **DOI:** 10.1038/s41467-021-27093-y · **Journal:** Nature Communications (2021)

## TL;DR

Smith et al. sequenced 733 [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md)-amplified primary and metastatic breast cancers ([BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md)) from Memorial Sloan Kettering ([brca_mapk_hp_msk_2021](../datasets/brca_mapk_hp_msk_2021.md)) using [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) and identified enrichment of somatic MAPK pathway alterations — most commonly biallelic [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) loss (8% of metastatic tumors) and activating [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) mutations (7% of metastatic tumors) — in tumors that progressed rapidly on anti-HER2 therapy. In 145 patients receiving first-line [trastuzumab](../drugs/trastuzumab.md)/[pertuzumab](../drugs/pertuzumab.md)-based therapy, MAPK-altered tumors had median PFS of 9.9 months vs 21 months for MAPK-WT (HR 2.03, 95% CI 1.18–3.51; p=0.011 multivariate). Functional studies in cell lines and a HER2+/NF1-null patient-derived xenograft show these mutations cause a switch in driver pathway from AKT to MEK/ERK with marked sensitization to [trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md) (MEK inhibitor), mediated by MEK-dependent activation of [CDK2](../genes/CDK2.md) kinase.

## Cohort & data

- **733 ERBB2-amplified breast tumors** from 664 patients with HER2+ metastatic breast cancer ([BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md)), prospectively sequenced April 2014–February 2021 at MSKCC under IRB protocol NCT01775072 ([PMID:34795269](../papers/34795269.md)).
- **Dataset**: [brca_mapk_hp_msk_2021](../datasets/brca_mapk_hp_msk_2021.md) (cBioPortal study, 385 metastatic + 348 primary samples).
- **Assay**: [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) hybridization capture NGS (341–505 cancer-associated genes; average coverage 663X). Copy number called with [FACETS](../methods/facets.md); mutations annotated with [OncoKB](../methods/oncokb-annotation.md).
- **Survival cohort**: 145 patients receiving first-line [docetaxel](../drugs/docetaxel.md) + [trastuzumab](../drugs/trastuzumab.md) + [pertuzumab](../drugs/pertuzumab.md) (THP) with pre-treatment tumor sequencing.
- **Preclinical models**: SKBR3, BT-474, MDA-MB-361, HCC1954 cell lines (shRNA, CRISPR/Cas9 [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) depletion); BT-474 [subcutaneous xenografts](../methods/subcutaneous-xenograft.md); a HER2+/NF1-null [patient-derived xenograft](../methods/patient-derived-xenograft.md) from a trastuzumab-refractory patient.

## Key findings

- **MAPK alterations enriched in metastatic HER2+ disease** ([PMID:34795269](../papers/34795269.md), Fig. 1a-b): [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) loss in 8% of metastatic vs 4% of primary tumors; [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) activating mutations in 7% vs 3%; [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) 0.9% vs 1%; [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md) each ≤0.3%. Enrichment significant (p=0.020, two-sided Fisher exact).
- **MAPK alterations predict poor PFS on first-line anti-HER2 therapy** (n=145): median PFS 9.9 months (95% CI 5.5–17, n=23 MAPK-altered) vs 21 months (95% CI 17–30, n=122 MAPK-WT); HR 2.03 (1.18–3.51), univariate log-rank p=0.023, multivariate p=0.011. After adjusting for [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)/[AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md)/[PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) alterations: HR 2.25 (1.29–3.93), p=0.0043 — independent of PI3K pathway lesions.
- **PI3K/AKT pathway co-alterations** in the cohort: [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) 30%, [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) 2.6%, [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) 0.3%; no mutual exclusivity with MAPK alterations (OR 1.20 [0.74–1.92], p=0.42).
- **Case example** (Fig. 1d-e): de novo metastatic HER2+ invasive ductal carcinoma ([IDC](../cancer_types/IDC.md)) patient acquired an [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) intragenic inversion (c.1527+970:NF1_chr17:g.57371683inv) at lymph-node progression after 31.5 months of THP/HP; the rearrangement bisects [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) across the RAS-GTPase domain.
- **NF1 loss confers anti-HER2 resistance in vitro**: shNF1 and CRISPR/Cas9 NF1-knockout SKBR3, BT-474, MDA-MB-361, HCC1954 cells are resistant to 500 nM [lapatinib](../drugs/lapatinib.md), 50 nM [neratinib](../drugs/neratinib.md), and 100 nM [tucatinib](../drugs/tucatinib.md). Re-expression of NF1 in HER2i-resistant SKBR3 restores HER2i sensitivity.
- **[ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) L755S, [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E, [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) G12V** expressed in SKBR3 each phenocopy NF1 loss in driving resistance to [lapatinib](../drugs/lapatinib.md) and [tucatinib](../drugs/tucatinib.md) (Fig. 2f).
- **Driver pathway switch from AKT to MEK/ERK**: in shNF1 HER2i-R SKBR3, [trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md) IC50 falls from 738 nM (control) to 13 nM (~57-fold sensitization); ERK inhibitor SCH772984 IC50 from 2,851 to 250 nM. AKT inhibitor [MK-2206](../drugs/mk-2206.md) IC50 rises from 197 to 4,475 nM (~23-fold resistance). KRAS G12V and HER2 L755S expressing cells similarly sensitized to MEK inhibition (IC50 16–24 nM).
- **In vivo MEK sensitivity**: BT-474 shNF1 HER2i-R xenografts show 37% growth inhibition with 1 mg/kg [trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md) (p=0.0059); shRenilla control xenografts unaffected. A HER2+/NF1-null PDX shows 70% growth inhibition with trametinib monotherapy vs no effect of [trastuzumab](../drugs/trastuzumab.md) (p<0.001).
- **Mechanism — MEK-dependent [CDK2](../genes/CDK2.md) activation**: in shNF1 HER2i-R cells, the G1/S checkpoint is regulated by MEK rather than AKT; MEK inhibition reduces pRb, cyclin E2/A2/D1, and induces p27. CDK2 immunoprecipitation kinase assays show MEK inhibition reduces CDK2 activity by ~80% in resistant cells with 6-fold induction of p27, while control cells are unaffected.
- **CDK2 not CDK4/6 mediates resistance**: shNF1 HER2i-R cells are resistant to CDK4/6 inhibitors ([palbociclib](../drugs/palbociclib.md) IC50 292→857 nM; [abemaciclib](../drugs/abemaciclib.md)/LY-2835219 2,816→2,981 nM) but sensitive to selective CDK2 inhibition (MK-8776 IC50 7,883→597 nM) and CDK2/4/6 inhibition (PF-06873600 IC50 45→15 nM). [CCNE2](../genes/CCNE2.md) shRNA knockdown phenocopies CDK2 inhibition in restoring HER2i sensitivity.

## Genes & alterations

- **[ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md)** — Amplification is the cohort entry criterion (all 733 tumors). Activating mutations (e.g., L755S) in 7% of metastatic vs 3% of primary samples drive anti-HER2 resistance via MAPK reactivation.
- **[NF1](../genes/NF1.md)** — Biallelic loss-of-function (truncating mutations, deep deletions, intragenic inversions) in 8% of metastatic vs 4% of primary tumors; most common MAPK alteration; sufficient to confer resistance to lapatinib/neratinib/tucatinib and MEK hypersensitivity.
- **[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)** — Activating mutations (e.g., G12V) in 0.3% of both groups; G12V phenocopies NF1 loss in driving HER2i resistance with MEK sensitization.
- **[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)** — V600E in 0.3% of both groups; expressed BRAF V600E drives HER2i resistance in SKBR3.
- **[EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md)** — Alterations in 0.9% of metastatic, 1% of primary.
- **[MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md)** — Activating mutations in 0.3% of metastatic samples (0% in primary).
- **[PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md)** — Co-occurring PI3K-pathway alterations (30%, 2.6%, 0.3%); MAPK resistance signal is independent of these.
- **[CDK2](../genes/CDK2.md)** — Effector of MEK-driven resistance; targetable in MAPK-activated HER2+ disease.
- **[CCNE2](../genes/CCNE2.md)** — Cyclin E2 knockdown rescues anti-HER2 sensitivity in shNF1 HER2i-R cells, confirming CDK2/cyclin E axis as the proximal cell-cycle driver.

## Clinical implications

- **Resistance biomarker**: pre-treatment MAPK-pathway alterations on [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) (NF1 LoF, ERBB2 activating, KRAS/BRAF/MAP2K1 activating) predict ~2-fold worse PFS on first-line anti-HER2 therapy; effect is independent of PI3K-pathway status.
- **Therapeutic vulnerability**: MAPK-altered HER2+ tumors are profoundly sensitized to MEK inhibition ([trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md)) in vitro and in vivo, suggesting MEK/ERK inhibitor addition to anti-HER2 regimens for patients with MAPK pathway alterations who have progressed on standard therapy.
- **CDK2 over CDK4/6**: HER2+ tumors with MAPK activation acquire CDK4/6-inhibitor resistance but become CDK2-dependent — the authors speculate CDK2 inhibition may offer greater benefit than [palbociclib](../drugs/palbociclib.md)/ribociclib/[abemaciclib](../drugs/abemaciclib.md) in treatment-refractory HER2+ disease.
- **Sequencing recommendation**: The data support pre- and post-progression genomic profiling to identify acquired MAPK lesions and stratify candidates for MEK or CDK2 inhibitor-based combinations.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Single-institution cohort**: 145-patient PFS analysis is restricted to MSKCC patients on first-line THP; external validation in independent cohorts pending.
- **Rare individual alterations**: BRAF, KRAS, [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md), [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) each ≤1%; aggregated MAPK signal is driven primarily by NF1 loss and ERBB2 mutations.
- **MEK/ERK inhibitor clinical efficacy unproven**: preclinical sensitization is striking but no prospective clinical trial of MEK inhibition in MAPK-altered HER2+ breast cancer is reported here.
- **Metastatic phenotype causation**: whether MAPK activation downstream of HER2 also promotes metastatic progression itself, independent of treatment resistance, is unresolved.
- **CDK2 inhibitor maturity**: selective CDK2 inhibitors (e.g., PF-06873600) are early-phase agents; clinical translation of the CDK2-dependence finding awaits durable, selective drugs.
- **Sample type adjustment**: multivariate model adjusts for sample type (metastatic vs primary) and hormone receptor status — residual confounding from treatment history before sequencing is possible.
