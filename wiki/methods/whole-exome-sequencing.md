# Whole-exome sequencing (WES)

## Overview

Whole-exome sequencing (WES) captures and sequences the protein-coding regions (exons) of the genome, typically at 100–200× depth, providing comprehensive single-nucleotide variant and small indel calls at lower cost than whole-genome sequencing. It is widely used for somatic mutation discovery in cancer and germline variant calling for cancer predisposition.

## Used by

- [PMID:36611031](../papers/36611031.md) — WES at 150× depth performed on 13 colitis-associated cancer (CAC) tumors and 20 dysplasia samples paired with adjacent normal mucosa from the [bowel_colitis_msk_2022](../datasets/bowel_colitis_msk_2022.md) cohort; identified that fraction genome altered and whole-genome doubling are significantly increased in CAC vs. dysplasia; showed that most genomic alterations in paired dysplasia–CAC samples were private (P=0.00022 for shared vs. private mutations) and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) alterations were absent in adjacent normal mucosa [PMID:36611031](../papers/36611031.md).

## Notes

- In the CAC study, WES was used to complement [IMPACT468](../methods/IMPACT468.md) targeted sequencing, providing broader mutational landscape coverage for a subset of patients.
