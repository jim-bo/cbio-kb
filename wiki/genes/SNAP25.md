# SNAP25

## Overview

SNAP25 (Synaptosomal-Associated Protein 25 kDa) is a neuronal SNARE protein involved in synaptic vesicle exocytosis. In cancer genomics, it has emerged as a neuronal marker relevant to glioma biology, where neuron-to-glioma synaptic signaling promotes tumor invasion and growth. SNAP25 expression in neoplastic glioma cells marks the stem-like recurrence phenotype at the invasive tumor rim.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- SNAP25 is a neuronal marker and component of the stem-like recurrence signature in recurrent diffuse glioma; detected by immunofluorescence on neoplastic cells in infiltrating-tumor regions of recurrent glioma samples from the GLASS cohort [PMID:35649412](../papers/35649412.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Diffuse glioma (IDHwt and IDHmut): SNAP25 expression marks stem-like neoplastic cells at the invasive rim of recurrent tumors; associated with neuronal-signaling upregulation in infiltrating cells [PMID:35649412](../papers/35649412.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-expressed with SOX2 and OLIG2 in stem-like neoplastic cell populations in recurrent glioma; component of the neuronal-signaling program upregulated at the invasive frontier [PMID:35649412](../papers/35649412.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Neuron-to-glioma synaptic signaling (involving SNAP25-mediated vesicle release) has been proposed as a candidate intervention target in recurrent IDHwt glioma; neuronal-circuitry-directed strategies are hypothesized to limit tumor invasion and growth [PMID:35649412](../papers/35649412.md)

## Open questions

- Whether the neuronal-signaling stem-like signature (including SNAP25) has independent prognostic value in recurrent glioma is unclear — no association with overall survival was observed (P > 0.05, Wald test) in the GLASS cohort [PMID:35649412](../papers/35649412.md)
- Directionality of neuron-to-glioma synaptic signaling and whether blocking SNAP25-mediated neurotransmitter release reduces glioma invasion in vivo requires further study.
