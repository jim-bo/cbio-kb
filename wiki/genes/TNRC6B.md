# TNRC6B

## Overview

TNRC6B (trinucleotide repeat containing 6B) encodes a GW182-family protein that functions in β-catenin-independent Wnt signaling and microRNA-mediated post-transcriptional gene silencing. It has been identified as a protein differentially expressed in CTNNB1-mutant hepatocellular carcinoma at the protein but not the mRNA level, implicating post-transcriptional regulation in the context of Wnt pathway activation.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- TNRC6B was among 10 proteins differentially expressed at the protein level but not at mRNA in CTNNB1-mutant HCC tumors (122-biopsy proteogenomics cohort, hcc_meric_2021), implicating post-transcriptional regulation downstream of Wnt/β-catenin signaling [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)** — Protein-level upregulation in CTNNB1-mutant HCC without concordant mRNA change, identified in an integrated proteogenomics study of 122 biopsies from 114 treatment-naive patients [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- TNRC6B protein expression is elevated in CTNNB1-mutant HCC; CTNNB1 and TP53 mutations are mutually exclusive in this cohort (Fisher p=0.012) [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting of TNRC6B was reported in the corpus. Its association with the CTNNB1-mutant (Wnt-active) HCC subtype (iCluster 2, improved OS) provides biological context for Wnt-pathway intervention strategies [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Open questions

- The functional consequence of TNRC6B protein upregulation in CTNNB1-mutant HCC — whether it promotes or opposes Wnt signaling — remains unresolved. The mRNA/protein discordance may reflect microRNA derepression or altered protein stability.
