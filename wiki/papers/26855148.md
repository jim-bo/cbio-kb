**PMID:** 26855148 · **DOI:** 10.1038/nm.4045 · **Journal:** Nature Medicine (2016)

## TL;DR

Beltran et al. profiled 114 metastatic biopsies from 81 men with castration resistant prostate cancer — 51 with adenocarcinoma histology (CRPC-Adeno) and 30 with neuroendocrine histology (CRPC-NE) — using whole-exome sequencing, RNA-seq, and genome-wide DNA methylation (eRRBS). CRPC-NE and CRPC-Adeno share a largely overlapping somatic copy-number landscape, but CRPC-NE is enriched for concurrent [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) loss + [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) alteration and is depleted of [AR](../genes/AR.md) genomic alterations. Serial samples support a divergent clonal evolution model: CRPC-NE arises from a CRPC-Adeno precursor under AR-targeted therapy pressure rather than from pre-existing neuroendocrine clones. Strong epigenetic segregation (including [SPDEF](../genes/SPDEF.md) hypermethylation and [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) over-expression) distinguishes the two states and yielded a 70-gene NEPC classifier with precision/recall >0.99 in the discovery cohort. ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md))

## Cohort & data

- **81 patients / 114 metastatic biopsies** of castration-resistant [prostate cancer](../cancer_types/PRAD.md): 51 [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md)-Adeno (70 samples) and 30 CRPC-NE (44 samples); matched normals from all patients; multi-region/serial biopsies in 17 patients ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- **Cohort identifier:** [nepc_wcm_2016](../datasets/nepc_wcm_2016.md) (Weill Cornell Medicine NEPC cohort); BAM files deposited at dbGaP `phs000909.v.p1` and made available on the cBioPortal for Cancer Genomics ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- **Assays:** [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) using SureSelect v2/v4 or HaloPlex Exome (Agilent), Illumina HiSeq, mean target coverage ~100×; [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) (TruSeq, HiSeq 2500, 2×75 bp paired-end) on 49 specimens; [eRRBS](../methods/rrbs.md) genome-wide single-cytosine methylation; custom NanoString assay for samples with limited material; [immunohistochemistry](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md) for AR, synaptophysin, chromogranin A, CD56, EZH2, MMR proteins; [FISH](../methods/fish.md) for [CYLD](../genes/CYLD.md) verification ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- **Computational tools:** [MuTect](../methods/mutect.md), [Oncotator](../methods/oncotator.md), [MutSig](../methods/mutsig.md), and [CLONET](../methods/clonet.md) for tumor purity/ploidy and allele-specific copy-number clonality ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- **External validation cohorts** queried for the NEPC classifier: TCGA prostate ([prad_tcga](../datasets/prad_tcga.md), n=460 treatment-naïve adenocarcinomas), Grasso et al. Michigan 2012 ([prad_mich](../datasets/prad_mich.md)), and Robinson et al. SU2C/PCF 2015 ([prad_su2c_2015](../datasets/prad_su2c_2015.md)) — totaling 683 prostate samples ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).

## Key findings

- **Concurrent RB1/TP53 loss is the genetic hallmark of CRPC-NE.** [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) deletion: 70% of CRPC-NE vs. 32% of CRPC-Adeno (P = 0.003, proportion test). [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation or deletion: 66.7% vs. 31.4% (P = 0.0043). Concurrent RB1 + TP53 loss: 53.3% vs. 13.7% (P < 0.0004) ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- **CRPC-NE lacks the AR alterations that dominate CRPC-Adeno** (P < 0.0001, Wilcoxon test). 29 cases showed [AR](../genes/AR.md) focal amplification or point mutation and 21 had alterations in AR co-activators ([FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md), [NCOR1](../genes/NCOR1.md)/[NCOR2](../genes/NCOR2.md), [ZBTB16](../genes/ZBTB16.md)); AR point mutations were notably absent in CRPC-NE and AR gains were low-level and explained by polyploidy. The ARv7-to-wild-type AR ratio was significantly decreased in CRPC-NE (P = 0.0025) ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- **No global differences** in non-silent SNV rate, polyploidy, or copy-number burden between subtypes; both have >30% of the genome aberrant on average; median 41 non-silent SNVs (range 2–729). 5 of 6 hyper-mutated samples (115–663 SNVs) carried mismatch-repair gene alterations ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- **CYLD focal deletion enriched in CRPC-NE.** [CYLD](../genes/CYLD.md) deleted in 51% of CRPC-NE samples (FDR<10% for both DNA and mRNA, FISH-verified), with concordant down-regulation of mRNA and modest decrease of AR-signaling genes — also confirmed in the SU2C/PCF cohort and cell lines ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- **Focal allelic imbalance of [DEK](../genes/DEK.md)** in CRPC-NE vs. CRPC-Adeno (P = 0.04, binomial test) ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- **Divergent clonal evolution from a CRPC-Adeno precursor.** Serial sampling in patient WCMC7520 (homozygous [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) deletion and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation in primary, lymph-node, and CRPC-NE metastases; divergent allelic states of [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md)) and patient WCMC161 (CRPC-Adeno LN, CRPC-Adeno bone, and CRPC-NE liver-on-abiraterone with divergent [SBDS](../genes/SBDS.md) allelic states) — together with multi-tumor phylogenetic trees — favor model V (divergent clonal evolution) over linear or independent parallel models ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- **Strong epigenetic segregation** of CRPC-NE from CRPC-Adeno on unsupervised eRRBS analysis, despite genomic similarity. 22% of top dysregulated CRPC-NE transcripts had concordant DNA-methylation changes (P < 0.0002). Methylation-based clustering re-classified 3 histologic adenocarcinomas with clinical AR-independence (radiographic progression with stable/low PSA) as CRPC-NE ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- **Hypermethylation and silencing of [SPDEF](../genes/SPDEF.md)** in CRPC-NE (P < 10⁻⁹, Wilcoxon test); confirmed in NCI-H660 (NE) vs. LNCaP (adeno) cell lines ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- **EZH2 over-expression and PRC2 target down-regulation in CRPC-NE.** [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) mRNA 2× higher than in CRPC-Adeno (P < 10⁻⁶); EZH2-repressed targets including WNT genes [DKK1](../genes/DKK1.md) (P = 0.0002), [NKD1](../genes/NKD1.md) (P = 0.0046), and HOX genes (P = 0.001) significantly down-regulated. EZH2 inhibitor GSK343 preferentially reduced viability of NCI-H660 vs. non-NE prostate cell lines and down-regulated CRPC-NE genes [NCAM1](../genes/NCAM1.md) (CD56), [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md), and [PEG10](../genes/PEG10.md) ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- **70-gene integrated NEPC classifier** (built from genes prioritized by genomic, transcriptomic, and epigenomic signal — including [AURKA](../genes/AURKA.md) at P < 10⁻⁵ and [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md) at P < 10⁻⁴) achieved precision and recall >0.99 in the discovery cohort, identified an elevated NEPC score in up to 8% of metastatic prostate tumors across [prad_tcga](../datasets/prad_tcga.md), [prad_mich](../datasets/prad_mich.md), and [prad_su2c_2015](../datasets/prad_su2c_2015.md) (n=191 metastatic) and 0% of treatment-naïve adenocarcinoma (n=460) or benign prostate (n=32). >80% of high-score cases on pathology re-review had CRPC-NE features ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- **In vitro plasticity:** LNCaP cells treated long-term with [enzalutamide](../drugs/enzalutamide.md) acquired CRPC-NE molecular features including SPDEF promoter methylation ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).

## Genes & alterations

- [AR](../genes/AR.md) — focal amplification, activating point mutations, and ARv7 splice variant common in CRPC-Adeno but largely absent / low-level in CRPC-NE; AR signaling attenuated in CRPC-NE ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — deletion in 70% CRPC-NE vs. 32% CRPC-Adeno (P = 0.003) ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — mutation/deletion in 66.7% CRPC-NE vs. 31.4% CRPC-Adeno (P = 0.0043); concurrent with RB1 loss in 53.3% of CRPC-NE (P < 0.0004) ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) — homozygous deletion shared across primary, lymph-node, and CRPC-NE metastases in patient WCMC7520, marking a common ancestor ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md) — divergent allelic states across metastatic sites in patient WCMC7520; over-expressed in CRPC-NE (P < 10⁻⁴); included in NEPC classifier; down-regulated by GSK343 in NCI-H660 ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- [CYLD](../genes/CYLD.md) — focal deletion in 51% of CRPC-NE with concordant mRNA loss (FISH-verified); deletion modestly suppresses AR signaling ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- [DEK](../genes/DEK.md) — focal allelic imbalance enriched in CRPC-NE (P = 0.04, binomial test) ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- [SBDS](../genes/SBDS.md) — divergent allelic state across CRPC-Adeno LN, CRPC-Adeno bone, and CRPC-NE liver metastases in patient WCMC161, illustrating clonal divergence ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- [FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md), [NCOR1](../genes/NCOR1.md), [NCOR2](../genes/NCOR2.md), [ZBTB16](../genes/ZBTB16.md) — AR co-activator alterations enriched in CRPC-Adeno (21 cases) ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- [TMPRSS2](../genes/TMPRSS2.md)–[ERG](../genes/ERG.md) — fusion concordance between adenocarcinoma and neuroendocrine foci of mixed tumors cited as supporting common cell of origin ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) — mRNA and protein up in CRPC-NE (P < 10⁻⁶); EZH2 inhibitor GSK343 preferentially kills NCI-H660 ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- [SPDEF](../genes/SPDEF.md) — promoter hypermethylation and mRNA loss in CRPC-NE (P < 10⁻⁹); also induced by long-term [enzalutamide](../drugs/enzalutamide.md) treatment of LNCaP ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- [AURKA](../genes/AURKA.md) — over-expressed in CRPC-NE (P < 10⁻⁵); part of NEPC classifier ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- [NCAM1](../genes/NCAM1.md) (CD56), [PEG10](../genes/PEG10.md) — CRPC-NE-associated transcripts down-regulated by EZH2 inhibition ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- [DKK1](../genes/DKK1.md), [NKD1](../genes/NKD1.md) — WNT-pathway EZH2 targets significantly down-regulated in CRPC-NE (P = 0.0002 and P = 0.0046 respectively) ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).

## Clinical implications

- **Diagnostic supplement to morphology.** The integrated 70-gene NEPC classifier outperformed conventional neuroendocrine markers ([CHGA](../genes/CHGA.md), [SYP](../genes/SYP.md), NSE, CD56 transcript ± PSA), AR mRNA, AR-signaling status, and individual differentially-expressed genes such as SPDEF in distinguishing CRPC-NE — and re-classified three histologic adenocarcinomas with clinical AR-independence (radiographic progression with stable/low PSA) as CRPC-NE ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- **Predictive biomarker hypothesis for AR-targeted therapy.** Castration-resistant tumors with moderate or rising NEPC scores may represent AR-independent transitional states unlikely to respond to next-line [abiraterone](../drugs/abiraterone.md) or [enzalutamide](../drugs/enzalutamide.md), and could be candidates for platinum chemotherapy or co-targeting strategies ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- **Therapeutic rationale for EZH2 inhibition** in CRPC-NE: EZH2 over-expression, PRC2-target silencing, and selective in vitro vulnerability of NCI-H660 to GSK343 nominate EZH2 inhibitors as candidate epigenetic modifiers to test for reversing or preventing the CRPC-NE state ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- **Liquid-biopsy applicability.** Because the classifier accepts partial input (DNA, RNA, or methylation), it is amenable to small metastatic biopsies and potentially circulating tumor DNA — relevant where multiple immunohistochemical assays are impractical ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).

## Limitations & open questions

- The authors cannot fully exclude **metastasis-to-metastasis seeding** as an alternative to divergent evolution within individual patients ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- An alternative model — **de-differentiation of adenocarcinoma to a progenitor-like state** with subsequent local acquisition of neuroendocrine features — cannot be fully excluded ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- Cases with **low AR signaling and low NEPC classifier score** exist; a less-common, distinct AR-low non-NE subset cannot be ruled out ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- **Validation cohorts contained relatively few CRPC-NE cases**; larger clinical studies are needed to confirm classifier superiority and prognostic/predictive utility ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- Effects of **prior therapy differences** between CRPC-Adeno and CRPC-NE patients on gene expression and the apparent absence of AR alterations in CRPC-NE cannot be fully disentangled ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
- The **reversibility of the CRPC-NE state** with early intervention or epigenetic modifiers (e.g. EZH2 inhibitors) is hypothesized but not yet tested in patients ([PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)).
