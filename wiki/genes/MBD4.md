# MBD4

## Overview

MBD4 (Methyl-CpG Binding Domain Protein 4) encodes a DNA glycosylase that excises T/U mismatches opposite methylated CpG sites, playing a role in repair of deaminated 5-methylcytosine. Germline protein-truncating variants (PTVs) in MBD4 are associated with a distinct somatic mutational signature characterized by elevated CpG > T substitutions pan-cancer.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Germline PTVs in MBD4 cause significantly elevated somatic CpG > T mutagenesis (P < 2.5×10⁻⁶ in the PCAWG cohort of 2,658 whole genomes; replicated P = 7.1×10⁻⁴ in TCGA n = 8,134); MBD4 germline PTVs are identified as candidate inherited modifiers of somatic mutation landscape [PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- Pan-cancer: germline MBD4 PTVs modulate the somatic CpG > T mutation burden across 38 tumor types analyzed in PCAWG [PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- MBD4 germline PTVs are grouped with APOBEC3 germline variants as inherited modifiers of somatic mutational processes that may inform predisposition screening and signature-based therapy stratification [PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting reported in the corpus. MBD4 PTVs are proposed as candidate biomarkers for predisposition screening given their reproducible effect on somatic CpG > T mutation burden [PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md).

## Open questions

- Whether the elevated CpG > T burden from germline MBD4 PTVs translates to increased neoantigen load or altered immune responses has not been tested in the corpus.
