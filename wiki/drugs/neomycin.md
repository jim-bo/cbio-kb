# Neomycin

## Overview

Neomycin is an aminoglycoside antibiotic used orally to reduce gut bacterial load. In the context of cholangiocarcinoma ([CHOL](../cancer_types/CHOL.md)), oral neomycin has been evaluated as a microbiota-modulating adjuvant, with proposed benefit through blocking the [CXCL1](../genes/CXCL1.md)–PMN-MDSC axis driven by [TLR4](../genes/TLR4.md) signaling.

## Evidence in the corpus

- Oral neomycin blocks the TLR4-driven [CXCL1](../genes/CXCL1.md)–PMN-MDSC axis in CCA preclinical models, reducing immunosuppressive myeloid-derived suppressor cell recruitment to the tumor microenvironment [PMID:25608663](../papers/25608663.md)
