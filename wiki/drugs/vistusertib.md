# vistusertib

## Overview

Vistusertib (AZD2014) is a selective dual mTORC1/2 inhibitor that blocks both the rapamycin-sensitive (mTORC1) and rapamycin-insensitive (mTORC2) complexes, thereby suppressing 4EBP1 phosphorylation and AKT feedback activation. It is distinct from first-generation rapalogs such as [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md). Vistusertib has been investigated in several solid tumor types under the AcSé-ESMART pediatric platform trial.

## Evidence in the corpus

- Vistusertib (mTOR inhibitor) was used in AcSé-ESMART platform arms E/F in the MAPPYACTS trial (NCT02613962) to target PIK3CA/PIK3R1 alterations (n=23) and [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) losses (n=25); mTOR inhibitors received 123 treatment recommendations across the 432 actionable-alteration patients in the MAPPYACTS cohort [PMID:35292802](../papers/35292802.md)
