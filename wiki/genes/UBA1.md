# UBA1

## Overview

UBA1 (Ubiquitin Like Modifier Activating Enzyme 1) encodes the primary E1 ubiquitin-activating enzyme, which is the initiating enzyme of the ubiquitin-proteasome system and is central to DNA damage response. UBA1 is a newly identified candidate driver gene in lung cancer in never smokers (LCINS), with mutations enriched in the piano subtype — a stem-cell-like, low-TMB subtype with long latency and distinct evolutionary biology from smoking-associated lung cancer.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Newly identified candidate driver in LCINS: 6 of 9 UBA1-mutated tumors are in the piano subtype; encodes the E1 ubiquitin enzyme central to DNA damage response; identified in a whole-genome sequencing study of 232 lung tumors in never smokers across three histologic subtypes ([LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md), [LNET](../cancer_types/LNET.md), [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md)) [PMID:34493867](../papers/34493867.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md): UBA1 mutations enriched in the piano (low-TMB, stem-cell-like) subtype of lung adenocarcinoma in never smokers; part of the mutually exclusive driver set in piano alongside [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [RET](../genes/RET.md), and [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) [PMID:34493867](../papers/34493867.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- UBA1 mutations in piano are part of a mutually exclusive driver set alongside KRAS, RET fusions, and ARID1A; piano tumors exhibit a low driver burden, making each subtype-enriched driver more prominent [PMID:34493867](../papers/34493867.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- UBA1 mutations in piano-subtype LCINS suggest potential vulnerabilities in ubiquitin-proteasome-mediated DNA damage response; targeting E1 ubiquitin enzyme activity or synthetic lethal combinations with DNA damage response pathways is hypothetically attractive but not yet clinically evaluated in this context [PMID:34493867](../papers/34493867.md)

## Open questions

- UBA1 is a newly identified candidate driver with limited functional validation in LCINS; the mechanistic role of UBA1 loss-of-function in the piano subtype's stem-cell-like phenotype and long latency requires dedicated experiments [PMID:34493867](../papers/34493867.md)
- The European ancestry skew of the LCINS cohort (97.4% European) limits generalizability to Asian LCINS populations where EGFR-mutant LUAD predominates [PMID:34493867](../papers/34493867.md)
