# EP300

## Overview

EP300 (E1A binding protein p300) is a transcriptional coactivator and histone acetyltransferase (HAT) that is the paralog of CREBBP/CBP. EP300 acetylates histones H3 and H4, as well as non-histone proteins including p53, and serves as a scaffold for transcription complexes. EP300 loss-of-function mutations occur in multiple cancer types. In glioblastoma, EP300 protein and acetylation levels are key drivers of H2B N-terminal hyperacetylation that marks a classical-like immune-low GBM subtype.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- EP300 protein and acetylation levels are strongly associated with H2B-K12/K13/K16/K17/K21 acetylation in GBM (gbm_cptac_2021, n=99) by Lasso regression; together with CREBBP, EP300 activity defines the classical-like, immune-low H2B-hyperacetylated GBM subset [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **Glioblastoma (GBM):** EP300 protein and acetylation activity co-drives H2B N-terminal hyperacetylation with CREBBP in the classical-like (nmf3) immune-low subtype; BRD1/BRD3/BRD4 correlate with additional H2B acetylation sites in this subset [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- EP300 activity co-occurs with CREBBP activity and BRD1/BRD3/BRD4 in the H2B-hyperacetylated, classical-like GBM subset [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- EP300 and CREBBP inhibitors are nominated as therapeutic candidates for the classical-like, H2B-hyperacetylated GBM subset; hypothesis-generating from proteogenomic data only, not clinically tested in this cohort [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Open questions

- Whether targeting EP300/CREBBP HAT activity is clinically feasible and specifically effective in H2B-hyperacetylated GBM requires preclinical and clinical validation [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).
