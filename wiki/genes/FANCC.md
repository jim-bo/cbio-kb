# FANCC

## Overview

FANCC encodes a component of the Fanconi anemia (FA) core complex, essential for DNA interstrand crosslink repair. Germline FANCC mutations cause Fanconi anemia, and somatic alterations have been observed in various cancers.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- FANCC (along with [FANCA](../genes/FANCA.md), [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md)) identified in the cluster of DNA repair gene alterations at relapse in fusion-negative rhabdomyosarcoma (FN-RMS) by ctDNA analysis [PMID:37730754](../papers/37730754.md) (referenced in the corpus via the CAC paper).
- Germline pathogenic FANCC alterations identified among 14% of consented CAC patients with germline alterations (colitis-associated colorectal cancer, n=73 sequenced); cancer susceptibility for germline FANCC in IBD-associated cancer remains uncertain [PMID:36611031](../papers/36611031.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- CAC (colitis-associated colorectal cancer) — germline FANCC among the DNA repair gene alterations in CAC patients [PMID:36611031](../papers/36611031.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with FANCA and other Fanconi pathway gene alterations in the germline landscape of CAC [PMID:36611031](../papers/36611031.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- PARP inhibitor sensitivity may be relevant for FANCC-deficient tumors; not directly tested in the corpus.

## Open questions

- Cancer susceptibility for germline FANCC in IBD-associated cancer remains uncertain; germline testing is recommended for CAC patients.
