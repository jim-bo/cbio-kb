# EPHA4

## Overview

EPHA4 (Ephrin type-A receptor 4) is a receptor tyrosine kinase involved in cell signaling, axon guidance, and angiogenesis. In cancer genomics, it has been identified as a low-frequency but potentially FDA-druggable alteration in hepatocellular carcinoma.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Low-frequency but FDA-druggable alteration in [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) (MAP kinase / RTK pathway) [PMID:25822088](../papers/25822088.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)**: Identified as a low-frequency alteration in the RTK/RAS/MAP kinase pathway in a comprehensive [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) genomic study [PMID:25822088](../papers/25822088.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Grouped with [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md), [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md), [KIT](../genes/KIT.md), [KDR](../genes/KDR.md), [TEK](../genes/TEK.md), and [JAK1](../genes/JAK1.md) as low-frequency druggable RTK/MAPK pathway alterations in [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) [PMID:25822088](../papers/25822088.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Potentially actionable in [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) given the availability of FDA-approved agents targeting this pathway [PMID:25822088](../papers/25822088.md)
