**PMID:** 28667006 · **DOI:** 10.1158/2159-8290.CD-17-0368 · **Journal:** Cancer Discovery (2017)

## TL;DR

Jusakul et al., on behalf of the International Cancer Genome Consortium, profiled 489 [cholangiocarcinomas](../cancer_types/CHOL.md) (CCAs) from 10 countries — combining whole-genome (71), exome (200), targeted (188), copy-number (175), DNA methylation (138), and gene-expression (118) platforms — to define four etiology-driven molecular subtypes. Fluke-Positive Clusters 1 and 2 are enriched in [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) amplifications and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations; Fluke-Negative Clusters 3 and 4 either exhibit high copy-number alterations with [PD-1](../genes/PDCD1.md)/[PD-L2](../genes/PDCD1LG2.md) immune-checkpoint expression (Cluster 3) or carry chromatin-modifier mutations ([IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md)/[IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md), [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md)) with [FGFR](../genes/FGFR2.md)/PRKA-related rearrangements (Cluster 4). The study nominates four new CCA driver genes ([RASA1](../genes/RASA1.md), [STK11](../genes/STK11.md), [MAP2K4](../genes/MAP2K4.md), [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md)), reports the first [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)-[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md) fusion in CCA, identifies [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) 3′UTR deletions as a new mechanism of [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) upregulation, and reveals two epigenetically distinct hypermethylation subtypes (CpG-island vs CpG-shore) reflecting carcinogen-driven vs genetically-driven oncogenesis [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).

## Cohort & data

- **Total cohort:** 489 [cholangiocarcinomas](../cancer_types/CHOL.md) (133 Fluke-Positive: 132 *Opisthorchis viverrini*, 1 *Clonorchis sinensis*; 356 Fluke-Negative; 39 HBV/HCV-positive; 5 PSC-positive) from 10 countries (Singapore, Romania, Thailand, Italy, France, South Korea, Brazil, Taiwan, China, Japan), all staged AJCC 7th edition, none pretreated [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **Anatomical breakdown:** intrahepatic ([IHCH](../cancer_types/IHCH.md)), perihilar ([PHCH](../cancer_types/PHCH.md)), and distal extrahepatic ([EHCH](../cancer_types/EHCH.md)) tumors, with anatomical and survival data on 459 samples [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **Genomic platforms:** [whole-genome sequencing](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) on 71 tumor/normal pairs at average 64.2× depth (Illumina HiSeq X10/2500/2000); [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) on 200 cases (previously published); [targeted DNA sequencing](../methods/targeted-dna-seq.md) of 404 genes via SureSelect XT2 capture on 188 cases (HiSeq 4000, 99.6% coding coverage); HumanOmniExpress SNP arrays on 175 cases ([Affymetrix-style SNP CN profiling](../methods/affymetrix-snp6.md)); [Illumina 450K methylation BeadChip](../methods/450k-methylation-array.md) on 138 cases; HumanHT-12 Expression BeadChip ([microarray gene expression](../methods/microarray-gene-expression.md), [Illumina platform](../methods/illumina-microarray.md)) on 118 cases [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **Integrative clustering:** [iCluster (iClusterPlus)](../methods/icluster.md) on the 94 samples with all four data types (sSNVs/indels, sCNAs, mRNA, methylation); validated by randomized subsampling and an expanded 121-sample reanalysis with 90% cluster-prediction accuracy [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **Validation cohort:** newly classified samples plus a published 38-sample US TCGA CCA series for survival reproducibility [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **Cell lines:** H69 (immortalized cholangiocyte), HEK293T, EGI-1 (DSMZ [ACC](../cancer_types/ACC.md) 385), HUCCT1 (JCRB0425), and M213 (JCRB1557, from the Liver Fluke and Cholangiocarcinoma Research Center) were used for luciferase reporter and shRNA functional assays [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **Dataset:** [chol_icgc_2017](../datasets/chol_icgc_2017.md). Comparison study: [chol_jhu_2013](../datasets/chol_jhu_2013.md).

## Key findings

- **Four molecular CCA clusters from integrative multi-omic clustering.** Cluster 1 (mostly Fluke-Pos): CpG-island promoter hypermethylation, enriched in [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) (p < 0.01) and [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md)/[BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) (p < 0.05) mutations, high non-synonymous mutation burden, H3K27me3-promoter mutation enrichment. Cluster 2 (mixed): upregulated [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md), [WNT5B](../genes/WNT5B.md), [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) expression. Clusters 1 and 2 are enriched in [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations (p < 0.001) and [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) amplifications (p < 0.01), Fisher's exact test. Cluster 3 (Fluke-Neg): highest sCNA burden, immune-checkpoint upregulation ([PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md), [PDCD1LG2](../genes/PDCD1LG2.md), [BTLA](../genes/BTLA.md)). Cluster 4 (Fluke-Neg): [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md), [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md)/[IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) mutations, [FGFR](../genes/FGFR2.md) alterations (all p < 0.01), CpG-shore hypermethylation [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **Cluster correlates with anatomy and prognosis.** Clusters 1 and 2 are enriched in extrahepatic tumors; Clusters 3 and 4 are almost entirely intrahepatic (p < 0.001). Patients in Clusters 3 and 4 have significantly better overall survival (log-rank p < 0.001), independent of fluke status, anatomical location, and stage (Cox p < 0.05); reproduced in an independent validation cohort [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **Mutation burden:** average 82 non-silent somatic mutations/tumor (median 47); 64 sSNVs (median 41) and 18 indels (median 6). Three CCAs were hypermutated (5.91 sSNVs/Mb and 24.17 indels/Mb) with MSI signatures and two carrying [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) mutations. Excluding hypermutators, Fluke-Pos CCAs carried significantly more somatic mutations than Fluke-Neg CCAs (median 4,700 vs 3,143/tumor, p < 0.05) [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **Four new CCA driver genes** (32 SMGs total by [MutSigCV](../methods/mutsig.md) and IntOGen, q < 0.1 by both): [RASA1](../genes/RASA1.md) (4.1%, mostly frame-shift/nonsense; shRNA knockdown enhances migration/invasion in CCA cell lines), [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) (5%, mostly inactivating), [MAP2K4](../genes/MAP2K4.md) (homozygous deletions in 2 Fluke-Pos cases plus 2.2% mutations, half inactivating), and [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md) (4.6%, hotspots at codons 625 and 700, previously seen in uveal melanoma and breast cancer) [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **[ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) amplification is enriched in Fluke-Pos CCAs** (10.4% vs 2.7% in Fluke-Neg, p < 0.01). [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md)-amplified samples averaged 14 copies (by [ASCAT](../methods/ascat.md) on SNP array or Quandico on sequencing data) and were independently validated by [FISH](../methods/fish.md). Activating [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) mutations (S310F/Y, G292R, T862A, D769H, L869R, V842I, G660D) were detected in 9 cases (2%) [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **Other recurrent CN events:** [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) amplification (n=12), [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) (n=9), [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) (n=11), [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) (n=7) amplifications; [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) (n=17), [UTY](../genes/UTY.md) (n=17), [KDM5D](../genes/KDM5D.md) (n=16) deletions [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **Structural variants:** [CREST](../methods/crest.md) called ~93 somatic SVs/tumor (median 69, range 0–395), 91% PCR-validated, mostly intra-chromosomal (65%), associated with [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [CDKN2A/B](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [TTC28](../genes/TTC28.md), and fragile site 1q21.3. SV burden varied across clusters (Kruskal–Wallis p < 0.05); [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md), and [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) mutation status was associated with increased SV burden (q < 0.1) [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **FGFR rearrangement landscape.** Five in-frame fusions with intact tyrosine-kinase domains: [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md)-STK26, [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md)-TBC1D1, [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md)-WAC, [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md)-[BICC1](../genes/BICC1.md), and [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)-[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md). The [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)-[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md) fusion is the first reported in CCA. All [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) rearrangements occurred exclusively in Cluster 4 (p < 0.001) [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **FGFR2 3′UTR loss as a new activation mechanism.** Recurrent truncating SVs translocated [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) without its 3′UTR to intergenic regions; FGFR2-truncated CCAs had significantly higher FGFR2 transcript levels (p < 0.01), and luciferase reporter assays in HEK293T and H69 cells confirmed that the intact FGFR2 3′UTR represses expression [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **[PRKACB](../genes/PRKACB.md) (PKA catalytic subunit B) rearrangements.** ATP1B1-[PRKACB](../genes/PRKACB.md) and LINC00261-[PRKACB](../genes/PRKACB.md) fusions retain the PKA pseudokinase domain and may activate downstream MAPK signaling [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **L1 (LINE-1) retrotransposition is recurrent and Fluke-Pos-associated.** 52 events in 20/71 (28.2%) of WGS tumors, 98% PCR-validated, predominantly originating from a [TTC28](../genes/TTC28.md) intron-1 L1 element. L1 retrotransposition was enriched in Fluke-Pos tumors (p < 0.01) and correlated with increased SV burden (p < 0.05) [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **[TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter mutations are rare in CCA.** Only 2/71 WGS cases (2.8%) carried [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter mutations (chr5:1295228); no other recurrent non-coding promoter point mutations were identified [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **FIREFLY (a new method) identifies non-coding regulatory dysregulation at the gene-set level.** Applied to 70 WGS samples and 6,639 mutated promoters, [FIREFLY](../methods/firefly.md) — which integrates [protein-binding microarray](../methods/protein-binding-microarray.md) data for 486 TFs — identified four gene sets enriched for promoter mutations that alter TF binding and produce concordant transcriptional dysregulation. Two of the four sets (MIKKELSEN_MCV6_HCP_WITH_H3K27ME3 and MIKKELSEN_MEF_ICP_WITH_H3K27ME3) are PRC2 targets bearing the H3K27me3 mark, and 3 of 4 sets were preferentially mutated in Cluster 1, supporting a role for PRC2/H3K27me3 dysregulation in that subtype [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **Two distinct DNA-methylation subgroups.** Unsupervised methylation clustering on 138 CCAs reproduced Cluster 1 (CpG-island hypermethylation, Fluke-Pos) and Cluster 4 (CpG-shore hypermethylation, Fluke-Neg) at 96.3% and 86.1% concordance with the integrative clusters. [GSEA](../methods/gsea.md) showed both target PRC2 pathways but at different genomic features. Promoter methylation was inversely correlated with transcript level in both clusters (q < 0.05) [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **Distinct mechanisms drive Cluster 1 vs Cluster 4 hypermethylation.** Cluster 1 shows downregulation of the demethylase [TET1](../genes/TET1.md) and upregulation of histone methyltransferase [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md). Cluster 4 is enriched in [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md)/[IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) mutations (31.6% vs 1.0% in other clusters, q < 0.001) and, among IDH-WT Cluster 4 tumors, enriched in [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md) inactivating point mutations and regional deletions (q < 0.001 and q < 0.05 respectively) [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **[Mutational signatures](../methods/mutational-signatures.md):** ten established signatures detected — COSMIC Signatures 1, 5, 8, 16, 17; APOBEC (Signatures 2, 13); MMR-deficient (Signatures 6, 20); aristolochic acid (Signature 22). Fluke-Pos CCAs were enriched for APOBEC mutation burden (p < 0.001). Signature 1 (CpG>TpG) was elevated in Cluster 1 even after age adjustment (p < 0.001), and CpG>TpG mutations were preferentially located near hypermethylated regions in Cluster 1 (p < 0.001) but not Cluster 4 — consistent with deamination of methylated cytosines as a Cluster 1 mutational driver [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **Cluster 1 is subclonally heterogeneous, Cluster 4 is clonal.** VAF-distribution analysis of point mutations (in copy-neutral regions, purity-adjusted) showed wide spread in Cluster 1 indicating heterogeneous subclones, vs tightly clonal structure in Cluster 4 [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **Driver-gene–anatomy associations:** [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md) and [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) were more frequently mutated in intrahepatic CCAs (q < 0.1, Fisher's exact test), persisting after fluke-status adjustment in multivariate regression [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **Immune signal in Cluster 3.** [ESTIMATE](../methods/estimate.md) showed immune infiltration in both Clusters 2 and 3, but only Cluster 3 specifically upregulated immune-checkpoint genes ([PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md), [PDCD1LG2](../genes/PDCD1LG2.md), [BTLA](../genes/BTLA.md)) and antigen cross-presentation, CD28 co-stimulation, and T-cell signaling pathways [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).

## Genes & alterations

- **[ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md)** — Amplification in 3.9–8.5% of CCAs, enriched in Fluke-Pos (10.4% vs 2.7%, p < 0.01); average 14 copies; FISH-validated. Activating point mutations (S310F/Y, G292R, T862A, D769H, L869R, V842I, G660D) in 2% of cases. Defining co-feature of Clusters 1 and 2; nominated as anti-HER2 therapeutic target [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)** — Significantly enriched in Clusters 1 and 2 (p < 0.001). [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation associated with increased SV burden (q < 0.1) [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **[ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md)** — Enriched in Cluster 1 (p < 0.01); also a recurrent SV target [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **[BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md)/[BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md)** — Enriched in Cluster 1 (p < 0.05) [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **[IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md)/[IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md)** — Enriched in Cluster 4 (31.6% vs 1.0% in other clusters, q < 0.001); proposed driver of CpG-shore DNA hypermethylation via 2-hydroxyglutarate oncometabolite production. Suggested as candidates for IDH inhibitors (e.g. [ivosidenib](../drugs/ivosidenib.md), then in trial NCT02073994) [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **[BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md)** — Enriched in IDH-WT Cluster 4 (q < 0.001 for inactivating point mutations, q < 0.05 for regional deletions); BAP1-mutant CCAs show increased CpG hypermethylation. More frequent in intrahepatic CCAs (q < 0.1) [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **[FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md)** — In-frame fusions ([FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md)-STK26, -TBC1D1, -WAC, -[BICC1](../genes/BICC1.md)) and a new class of recurrent truncating rearrangements removing the 3′UTR — both classes elevate FGFR2 expression. Indels (n=3), SNVs (n=10), and copy-gain (n=1) also observed. Rearrangements are exclusive to Cluster 4 (p < 0.001); aggregated FGFR2 alterations enriched in Cluster 4 (p < 0.01) [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **[FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)–[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md)** — First reported in CCA; previously characterized as oncogenic in bladder cancer, glioblastoma, and lung cancer [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **[PRKACB](../genes/PRKACB.md)** — ATP1B1-PRKACB and LINC00261-PRKACB fusions retain the pseudokinase domain; proposed to activate MAPK signaling [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **[RASA1](../genes/RASA1.md)** — Newly nominated CCA driver; inactivating mutations in 4.1% (10 frame-shift, 4 nonsense) plus focal CN losses, both correlated with reduced expression. shRNA knockdown in CCA cell lines (M213, HUCCT1) enhanced migration and invasion in Transwell assays — supporting tumor-suppressor function [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **[STK11](../genes/STK11.md)** — Newly nominated CCA driver; mutated in 5%, mostly inactivating (7 nonsense, 9 frame-shift) [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **[MAP2K4](../genes/MAP2K4.md)** — Newly nominated CCA driver; homozygous deletions in 2 Fluke-Pos cases plus 2.2% mutations (half inactivating) — consistent with tumor-suppressor role [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **[SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md)** — Newly nominated CCA driver; mutated in 4.6% at hotspots codon 625 (23%) and codon 700 (14%) — implicating splicing dysregulation in CCA, paralleling uveal melanoma and breast cancer hotspots [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)** — Significantly more frequent in intrahepatic CCAs (q < 0.1) [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **[TERT](../genes/TERT.md)** — Promoter mutations rare in CCA (2/71 WGS cases, 2.8%) at chr5:1295228 [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **[FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md), [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md)** — Mutations associated with elevated SV burden (q < 0.1) [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **[CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md), [WNT5B](../genes/WNT5B.md), [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md)** — Upregulated expression in Cluster 2 (p < 0.05) [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **[MYC](../genes/MYC.md), [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md), [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md), [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md)** — Recurrent oncogene amplifications (n=12, 9, 11, 7 respectively) [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **[CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [UTY](../genes/UTY.md), [KDM5D](../genes/KDM5D.md)** — Recurrent deletions (n=17, 17, 16 respectively) [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **[TTC28](../genes/TTC28.md)** — Source of recurrent somatic L1 retrotransposition in 28.2% of WGS tumors [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **[POLE](../genes/POLE.md)** — Mutated in 2 of the 3 hypermutator CCAs (alongside MSI mutational signatures) [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **[PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md) (PD-1), [PDCD1LG2](../genes/PDCD1LG2.md) (PD-L2), [BTLA](../genes/BTLA.md)** — Specifically upregulated in Cluster 3, motivating immune-checkpoint blockade as a candidate therapeutic strategy in that subtype [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **[TET1](../genes/TET1.md), [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md)** — Cluster 1 shows downregulated [TET1](../genes/TET1.md) and upregulated [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) expression, suggesting loss-of-demethylation plus gain-of-repressive-methylation as the epigenetic mechanism for Cluster 1 hypermethylation [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).

## Clinical implications

- **Molecular subtype provides prognostic information beyond anatomy.** Clusters 3 and 4 carry significantly better overall survival than Clusters 1 and 2 (log-rank p < 0.001), independent of fluke status, anatomical site, and stage on multivariate Cox regression (p < 0.05); reproduced in an independent validation cohort [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **Cluster-specific therapeutic hypotheses (require clinical validation):**
  - **Clusters 1 and 2 — anti-HER2.** [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md)-amplified CCAs (predominantly Fluke-Pos) are candidates for HER2-targeted agents; cell-line data referenced by the authors suggest high-ERBB2 CCAs are more sensitive to ERBB2 inhibition than low-ERBB2 cases [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
  - **Cluster 3 — immune checkpoint blockade.** Upregulation of [PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md) (PD-1), [PDCD1LG2](../genes/PDCD1LG2.md) (PD-L2), and [BTLA](../genes/BTLA.md), combined with antigen-presentation and T-cell signaling pathway upregulation, motivates immunotherapy trials in this subtype — though sample size is small [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
  - **Cluster 4 — IDH inhibitors and FGFR-targeted agents.** [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md)/[IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) mutations and [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md)/[FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) rearrangements suggest opportunities for IDH inhibitors (e.g. [ivosidenib](../drugs/ivosidenib.md), then in NCT02073994) and FGFR inhibitors. [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) 3′UTR-loss truncating rearrangements expand the FGFR-targetable population beyond canonical in-frame fusions and activating mutations [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **Anatomical classification alone is insufficient for treatment decisions.** Tumors in different anatomical sites can be molecularly similar, and tumors in the same anatomical site span all four molecular clusters; current oncology guidelines do not discriminate CCA treatment by anatomical site [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **Liver fluke status is not equivalent to molecular subtype.** Cluster-associated survival differences persist after fluke-status adjustment, and Cluster 2 contains both Fluke-Pos and Fluke-Neg tumors — cautioning against using fluke status as a proxy for molecular classification [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Platform heterogeneity.** Sample-resource constraints (DNA, RNA, FFPE) prevented all platforms from being applied to all samples; the integrative clustering rests on 94–121 samples with multi-platform data while the full driver/SV/CN analyses use larger but partially-overlapping subsets. Authors used statistical models and overlap concordance to argue platform bias is minimal, but cross-platform merging restricts analysis to common genomic regions [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **Cluster 3 is small.** Immunotherapy nominations for Cluster 3 rest on a small sample size; the mechanistic basis for immune-checkpoint upregulation in this subtype is unresolved (the authors note that aneuploidy-associated immunogenicity is one possible explanation) [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **Center-specific pre-processing differences** (collection, biopsy site, processing protocols) may introduce sequencing biases; partially mitigated by standardized AJCC 7th-edition histology review and tumor-cell-content estimation [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **Functional validation is restricted.** Only [RASA1](../genes/RASA1.md) (shRNA knockdown migration/invasion) and [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) 3′UTR (luciferase reporter) and 2/3 selected promoter mutations (FIREFLY-prediction validation) were experimentally tested; the other newly nominated drivers ([STK11](../genes/STK11.md), [MAP2K4](../genes/MAP2K4.md), [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md)) and the proposed therapeutic vulnerabilities require further functional and clinical validation [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **FIREFLY does not predict directionality of expression change** for individual TF binding-change mutations, since TFs can be activators or repressors; gene-set-level effects are inferred without per-mutation directionality [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **Cell-of-origin remains a competing explanation** for the Cluster 1 vs Cluster 4 hypermethylation differences. The biliary system contains multipotent stem/progenitor cells; liver fluke infection primarily affects large bile ducts while parenchymal liver diseases affect canals of Hering and bile ductules — distinct vulnerable populations could partly explain the molecular differences alongside the proposed extrinsic-vs-intrinsic carcinogenesis model [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **Comparison with the contemporary US TCGA CCA series** (38 samples, exclusively fluke-negative, mostly intrahepatic, North American — overlaps with [chol_jhu_2013](../datasets/chol_jhu_2013.md) lineage of CCA studies). The TCGA "IDH" and "METH3" groups map approximately to Cluster 4; "ECC" to Cluster 2; the TCGA "METH2" group ([CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) amplifications) and Cluster 3 are not obviously matched [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
- **Generalizability of FIREFLY.** Whether the gene-set-level promoter-mutation framework recovers analogous H3K27me3/PRC2 signals in other cancer types remains an open empirical question raised by the authors [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md).
