**PMID:** 25631445 · **DOI:** [10.1038/nature14129](https://doi.org/10.1038/nature14129) · **Journal:** Nature (2015)

## TL;DR

The Cancer Genome Atlas Network profiled 279 head and neck squamous cell carcinomas ([HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md)) with whole-exome sequencing, low-pass whole-genome sequencing, RNA-seq, miRNA-seq, DNA methylation, RPPA, and SNP6.0 arrays. The study defined two largely non-overlapping disease biologies: human-papillomavirus-positive (HPV+, n=36) tumours dominated by helical-domain [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) mutations, loss of [TRAF3](../genes/TRAF3.md), and amplification of [E2F1](../genes/E2F1.md); and smoking-related HPV-negative tumours with near-universal [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) loss-of-function, [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) inactivation, and recurrent amplifications at 3q26/28 and 11q13/22. A favourable-outcome subgroup of mostly oral-cavity tumours combined infrequent CNAs with activating [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) or [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) mutations and inactivating [CASP8](../genes/CASP8.md), [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md), and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations. Additional subgroups featured loss-of-function alterations of the chromatin modifier [NSD1](../genes/NSD1.md), WNT-pathway genes [AJUBA](../genes/AJUBA.md) and [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md), and activation of oxidative-stress factor [NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md) (mainly in laryngeal tumours). Candidate therapeutic alterations were identified in most HNSCCs.

## Cohort & data

- **n=279** head and neck squamous cell carcinomas with complete multi-platform data, drawn from a larger TCGA target of 500 tumours.
- Anatomic sites: oral cavity 172/279 (62%) — [OCSC](../cancer_types/OCSC.md); oropharynx 33/279 (12%) — [OPHSC](../cancer_types/OPHSC.md); larynx 72/279 (26%) — [LXSC](../cancer_types/LXSC.md); plus rarer [HPHSC](../cancer_types/HPHSC.md) (hypopharynx) cases.
- Patients predominantly male (203/279, 73%) and heavy smokers (mean pack-years = 51).
- HPV status by RNA-seq read mapping to viral genes E6/E7 (threshold >1,000 mapped reads): 36 HPV(+), 243 HPV(−). 64% of oropharyngeal tumours were HPV(+) vs 6% of non-oropharyngeal tumours.
- Cancer type: [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md).
- Dataset / cohort: [hnsc_tcga_pub](../datasets/hnsc_tcga_pub.md) (TCGA [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md) 2015 publication).
- Assays: [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) (mean coverage 95×; 82% of target bases ≥30×); high-coverage [whole-genome-seq](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) on n=29; [rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md); [mirna-seq](../methods/mirna-seq.md); [affymetrix-snp6](../methods/affymetrix-snp6.md) (CNAs); [hm450-methylation-array](../methods/hm450-methylation-array.md); [rppa](../methods/rppa.md).
- Analytic tools: [mutsig](../methods/mutsig.md) (MutSigCV) for significantly mutated genes; [gistic](../methods/gistic.md) for focal CNAs; targeted re-sequencing of 394 unique regions validated 99% of mutations.

## Key findings

- **Genomic instability.** Mean 141 CNAs (amplifications or deletions) per tumour from microarray data and 62 structural aberrations per tumour by WGS (n=29). 39 regions of recurrent copy-number loss and 23 of recurrent copy-number gain (q < 0.1).
- **Squamous-cancer chromosomal pattern.** Recurrent losses of 3p and 8p, gains of 3q, 5p, and 8q — resembling lung squamous cell carcinoma ([LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md)). Focal 3q26/28 amplifications (in both HPV(+) and HPV(−) tumours) span the squamous lineage transcription factors [TP63](../genes/TP63.md) and [SOX2](../genes/SOX2.md) and the oncogene [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md).
- **HPV(+)-specific alterations.** Novel recurrent deletions (5/36, 14%) and truncating mutations (3/36, 8%) of [TRAF3](../genes/TRAF3.md) — the first evidence linking [TRAF3](../genes/TRAF3.md) inactivation to HPV-associated carcinomas. Focal amplification of [E2F1](../genes/E2F1.md). Intact 9p21.3 ([CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) region) that is commonly deleted in HPV(−) tumours. HPV(+) tumours showed clear evidence of host-genome integration (usually a single locus per sample, almost always with amplification of the host genome); however, integration target genes were non-recurrent (no [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) integrations as previously reported in HPV(+) cell lines).
- **HPV(−)-specific alterations.** Co-amplifications of 11q13 ([CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md), [FADD](../genes/FADD.md), [CTTN](../genes/CTTN.md)) and 11q22 ([BIRC2](../genes/BIRC2.md), [YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md)) — implicating cell-death/NF-κB and Hippo pathways. Novel focal deletions in [NSD1](../genes/NSD1.md) and tumour-suppressors ([FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md), [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md), [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)). Recurrent focal RTK amplifications ([EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md), [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md), [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md)).
- **Mutation rates.** 12,159 synonymous and 37,061 non-synonymous somatic variants across 279 samples. Mutation rates did not differ by HPV status, but transversions at CpG sites were more frequent in HPV(−) tumours and a TpC-mutation predominance (APOBEC-like signature) was noted in HPV(+) cases.
- **Significantly mutated genes (MutSigCV, q < 0.1).** Eleven genes statistically significant: [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) 72%, [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) 22%, [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md) 23%, [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) 21%, [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) 19%, [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) (MLL2) 18%, [NSD1](../genes/NSD1.md) 10%, [CASP8](../genes/CASP8.md) 9%, [AJUBA](../genes/AJUBA.md) 6%, [NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md) 6%, [HLA-A](../genes/HLA-A.md) 3%. Trend-significant (q < 1): [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) 4%, [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md) 5%, [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) 3%, [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md) 1%, [TRAF3](../genes/TRAF3.md) 1%, [CUL3](../genes/CUL3.md) 4%, [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) 2%, [TGFBR2](../genes/TGFBR2.md) 4%.
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md) by HPV status.** TP53 mutated in 86% of HPV(−) samples (higher than previously reported); only 1/36 HPV(+) cases had a non-synonymous TP53 mutation.
- **[PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) hotspots.** 73% of PIK3CA mutations localised to E542K, E545K, and H1047R/L hotspots. ~25% of mutated PIK3CA cases had concurrent amplification; an additional 20% of tumours had focal amplification without mutation. PIK3CA mutation frequency was 56% in HPV(+) vs 34% in HPV(−) tumours, and HPV(+) PIK3CA mutations were enriched in the helical domain (exon 9).
- **[HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) hotspots.** Recurrent activating mutations at GTPase-domain residues 11–13 (q ≈ 0.2).
- **CNA cluster ("M class") subgroup.** Unsupervised clustering of CNAs identified a mutually exclusive subset of predominantly oral-cavity tumours with reduced CNAs (mutation-driven, "M class"). This subset contained a novel three-gene pattern of activating [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) mutations co-occurring with inactivating [CASP8](../genes/CASP8.md) mutations on a wild-type [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) background — confirming a previously reported favourable clinical outcome in HNSCCs with few CNAs.
- **Structural variants.** Known fusion oncogenes involving ALK/ROS/RET were not observed. Previously reported [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)–[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md) fusions detected in 2 HPV(+) tumours. EGFRvIII (type III isoform) in only 1/279 patients. Alternative [MET](../genes/MET.md) exon-14 skipping transcript in 2 HPV(−) tumours. Structural alterations (homozygous deletions, intra-/inter-chromosomal fusions) more commonly drove loss of function in tumour-suppressors ([CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md), [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md)) than protein-coding fusions.
- **[NSD1](../genes/NSD1.md) inactivation in 33 HNSCCs.** Inactivating mutations (n=29) and focal homozygous deletions (n=4). NSD1 is a histone H3K36 methyltransferase, similar to [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md) (mutated in ccRCC); germline NSD1 inactivation causes Sotos syndrome with predisposition to squamous carcinoma. NSD1 loss associated with DNA hypomethylation.
- **Four mRNA expression subtypes confirmed** (atypical 24%, mesenchymal 27%, basal 31%, classical 18%) — replicating prior HNSCC classifications. Co-occurring features per subtype:
  - **Classical** — [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation, [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) loss-of-function, chromosome 3q amplification, oxidative-stress gene alterations ([KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md), [NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md), [CUL3](../genes/CUL3.md)), heavy smoking, larynx sub-site — resembling [LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md).
  - **Basal** — [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) inactivation with intact oxidative-stress signalling and few 3q alterations; marked relative decrease of [SOX2](../genes/SOX2.md) expression; enriched for [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md)–[CASP8](../genes/CASP8.md) co-mutation and co-amplified 11q13/q22 tumours.
  - **Atypical** — lack of chromosome 7 amplifications; enriched in HPV(+) tumours with [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) helical-domain mutations; hypomethylation and LOF mutations of [NSD1](../genes/NSD1.md) (with wild-type [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md), shared with classical subtype).
  - **Mesenchymal** — high levels of innate-immunity gene alteration; high NK-cell marker CD56; low frequency of HLA class I mutations.
- **Anatomic-site differential mutations.** [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (P<0.001), [CASP8](../genes/CASP8.md) (P=0.01), [NSD1](../genes/NSD1.md) (P=0.01), and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) (P=0.06) were most differentially mutated across sites. 22/24 [CASP8](../genes/CASP8.md) mutations (92%) occurred in oral-cavity tumours; TP53, NSD1, and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) had reduced mutation rates in oropharyngeal tumours.
- **[NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md) oxidative-stress axis as a tobacco signature.** Alterations of [NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md), [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md), and [CUL3](../genes/CUL3.md), with heavy smoking history and larynx sub-site, co-occurred mainly in classical-subtype tumours — paralleling [LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md).
- **miRNA / copy-number networks.** let-7c-5p and miR-100-5p were decreased in tumours vs normal; their deletion strongly associated with increased expression of target oncogenes [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md), [E2F1](../genes/E2F1.md), [PLK1](../genes/PLK1.md), and [HMGA2](../genes/HMGA2.md).
- **Survival stratification.** HPV(+) tumours and HPV(−), TP53 wild-type tumours both demonstrated favourable outcomes compared with TP53-mutant and 11q13/[CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md)-amplified tumours.

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — mutated in 72% overall (86% in HPV(−), 1/36 in HPV(+)); predominantly loss-of-function. Distinguishes HPV(−) from HPV(+) biology; central to classical subtype.
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — inactivation in 22% (58% in HPV(−), 0% in HPV(+) on pathway analysis); co-occurs with TP53 loss in smoking-related tumours; falls in significant deletion peak at 9p21.
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — mutated in 21% (56% in HPV(+), 34% in HPV(−)); 73% of mutations at hotspots E542K/E545K/H1047R/L; helical-domain enriched in HPV(+); often co-amplified.
- [TRAF3](../genes/TRAF3.md) — recurrent deletions (14%) and truncating mutations (8%) in HPV(+); first link to HPV-associated cancer; loss promotes aberrant NF-κB signalling.
- [E2F1](../genes/E2F1.md) — focal amplification predominantly in HPV(+); also increased expression via let-7c-5p/miR-100-5p loss.
- [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) — activating mutations (4%) at GTPase residues 11–13; defines an M-class oral-cavity subset with [CASP8](../genes/CASP8.md) co-mutation and wild-type TP53.
- [CASP8](../genes/CASP8.md) — clustered missense and inactivating mutations (9% overall; 92% of mutations in oral cavity); anti-correlated with 11q13 amplification.
- [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) — inactivating mutations in 19%; [NOTCH2](../genes/NOTCH2.md) (9%) and [NOTCH3](../genes/NOTCH3.md) (5%) non-significant; convergent with FAT1/AJUBA on β-catenin signalling.
- [AJUBA](../genes/AJUBA.md) — previously unreported somatic mutations and deletions (6%); primarily 5′-inactivating events plus clustered missense in the LIM domain; regulates Hippo/Wnt/β-catenin.
- [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md) — mutated in 23%; novel SMG in HNSCC; aberrant Wnt activation.
- [NSD1](../genes/NSD1.md) — inactivating mutations (n=29) and focal homozygous deletions (n=4) in 33 tumours (10%); H3K36 methyltransferase; loss associated with DNA hypomethylation; defines atypical/classical mRNA-subtype enrichment.
- [NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md) — significantly mutated in 6%; activating; tobacco-associated; co-occurs with [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md) and [CUL3](../genes/CUL3.md) alterations in classical subtype.
- [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md), [CUL3](../genes/CUL3.md) — oxidative-stress complex partners of NFE2L2; tobacco signature.
- [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md), [FADD](../genes/FADD.md), [CTTN](../genes/CTTN.md) — 11q13 co-amplification cassette (31% in HPV(−); 3% in HPV(+)).
- [BIRC2](../genes/BIRC2.md), [YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md) — 11q22 co-amplification (rarely amplified without 11q13); selection inferred from [BIRC2](../genes/BIRC2.md)–[FADD](../genes/FADD.md)–caspase cascade interaction inhibiting cell death; majority of 11q13-amplified tumours had large telomeric 11q22 deletions including [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [CASP1](../genes/CASP1.md), CASP4, CASP5, CASP12.
- [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) (MLL2) — significantly mutated (18%); contributes to defective immunosurveillance.
- [HLA-A](../genes/HLA-A.md) — significantly mutated (3%); immune-evasion role.
- [TGFBR2](../genes/TGFBR2.md) — sporadic inactivating mutations and deletions; primarily oral-cavity tumours.
- [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md) — recurrent Arg505Gly/Leu substitutions (n=14); falls in 4q31.3 significant deletion peak; targets cyclin E and NOTCH.
- [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md) — mutated in 1%; one of multiple genes with at least one identical COSMIC mutation.
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — mutated/altered in 2% overall; component of RTK/RAS/PI(3)K alteration in 62% of HPV(−) and 61% of HPV(+) tumours.
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — mutations in 3%; structural alterations more often inactivating than coding fusions.
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) — focal amplification in 15% of HPV(−) vs 6% of HPV(+); rare EGFRvIII isoform (1/279).
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) — focal amplification in HPV(−) (3%).
- [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) — focal amplification in HPV(−) (10%) vs 0% HPV(+).
- [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) — amplification in 2% HPV(−) vs 11% HPV(+); [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)–TACC3 fusions in 2 HPV(+) tumours.
- [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md), [IGF1R](../genes/IGF1R.md), [DDR2](../genes/DDR2.md), [EPHA2](../genes/EPHA2.md), [MET](../genes/MET.md) — additional candidate RTK alterations across HPV groups (Fig. 3).
- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) — amplification in 14% HPV(−) and 3% HPV(+); not the target of HPV integration in this cohort.
- [SOX2](../genes/SOX2.md), [TP63](../genes/TP63.md) — 3q26/28 co-amplified squamous-lineage transcription factors; [TP63](../genes/TP63.md) RNA splice variants identified.
- [ZNF750](../genes/ZNF750.md) — TP63 target; mutation in 4% within significant 17q25.3 deletion peak.
- [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) — focal deletions in HPV(−) tumour-suppressor set.
- [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) — alteration in 3% HPV(−).
- [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) — terminal node of Wnt convergence implicated via FAT1/AJUBA/NOTCH inactivation.
- [B2M](../genes/B2M.md) — implicated in HLA-A/B antigen presentation pathway alterations (7% HPV(−), 11% HPV(+)).
- [PTCH1](../genes/PTCH1.md), [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md), [SCN9A](../genes/SCN9A.md) — genes with at least one identical previously COSMIC-reported mutation.

## Clinical implications

- **Therapeutic candidate alterations identified in most HNSCCs** (Fig. 3 and pathway analyses).
- **HPV(+) oropharyngeal cancers** — aberrant NF-κB activation via [TRAF3](../genes/TRAF3.md) loss, [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) activating mutations, and [E2F1](../genes/E2F1.md) amplification suggest opportunities for PI(3)K-pathway and cell-cycle-targeted therapy.
- **HPV(−) oncogenic axes** — mutually exclusive subsets defined by 11q13/[CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md)/[FADD](../genes/FADD.md)/[BIRC2](../genes/BIRC2.md)/[YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md) co-amplification or by [CASP8](../genes/CASP8.md)–[HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) co-mutation; both target cell cycle, cell death, NF-κB, and Hippo pathways and motivate BIRC-, PI(3)K-, Wnt/β-catenin-, and NOTCH-targeted strategies (under investigation).
- **Favourable outcomes** in HPV(+) and HPV(−)/TP53 wild-type tumours vs poorer outcomes in TP53-mutant and 11q13/CCND1-amplified tumours — supporting [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) status and 11q13 amplification as candidate prognostic biomarkers beyond HPV.
- **Oral-cavity M-class subset** ([HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) + [CASP8](../genes/CASP8.md) + wild-type [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), few CNAs) — favourable outcome subgroup that may warrant de-escalated therapy.
- **Wnt/β-catenin axis** — combined inactivation of [AJUBA](../genes/AJUBA.md), [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md), and [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) predicted to converge on unchecked β-catenin signalling (potential therapeutic target).
- **Tobacco-associated oxidative-stress axis** ([NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md), [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md), [CUL3](../genes/CUL3.md)) — highlights this complex as a therapeutic target in laryngeal/classical-subtype HNSCC, paralleling [LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md).
- **RTK targeting** — [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md)/[ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) or [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md)/[FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) alterations are the most frequent among RTKs; HPV(−) tumours enriched for [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) and [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) amplification; HPV(+) tumours enriched for [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) alterations (including FGFR3–TACC3 fusions).
- **Immune escape** — significantly mutated [HLA-A](../genes/HLA-A.md) and [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) plus [B2M](../genes/B2M.md)/HLA-B pathway alterations may contribute to defective immunosurveillance, with implications for immune-checkpoint strategies.

## Limitations & open questions

- Cohort reflects an early TCGA freeze (279 of an a-priori 500-tumour target); statistical power for rare alterations remains limited.
- HPV(+) subset is modest (n=36), limiting power for HPV(+)-specific significantly mutated genes and structural variants.
- High-coverage whole-genome sequencing was performed in only 29 tumours; structural-variant detection sensitivity in the remainder relies on RNA-seq and SNP-array signals.
- HPV-integration target genes were non-recurrent, suggesting no single driver mechanism related to HPV integration — biological consequences of host-locus amplification at integration sites remain to be defined.
- Mechanistic significance of co-amplification of [BIRC2](../genes/BIRC2.md)/[YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md) at 11q22 with 11q13 in the context of telomeric 11q22 deletions (which remove ATM, CASP1, CASP4, CASP5, CASP12) needs functional dissection.
- Whether candidate therapeutic alterations identified across most tumours translate to clinical benefit requires prospective trials targeting the specific HPV+/HPV− and CNA-class subsets defined here.
- Biological roles of agents targeting BIRCs, PI(3)K, Wnt/β-catenin, and NOTCH in HNSCC are described as "under investigation" by the authors.
