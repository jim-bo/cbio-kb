Colorectal Cancer in Nigerian Patients (ARGO/MSKCC, 2020)

Overview

Prospective cohort of 64 Nigerian colorectal cancer (COAD/READ) specimens collected through the African Research Group for Oncology (ARGO), spanning 12 Nigerian tertiary care facilities affiliated with Obafemi Awolowo University. Patients were enrolled April 2013–November 2018. Tumors underwent MSK-IMPACT paired tumor-normal targeted sequencing, with a subset profiled by genome-wide DNA methylation (RRBS) and MMR protein IHC. The dataset was deposited in cBioPortal to enable race-stratified molecular comparisons against MSKCC and TCGA colorectal cohorts. PMID:34819518

Composition

  • 64 Nigerian COADREAD specimens profiled by MSK-IMPACT paired tumor-normal sequencing. PMID:34819518
  • 94 Nigerian tumors with MMR protein IHC (MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2). PMID:34819518
  • 25 of the 64 MSK-IMPACT specimens also had genome-wide DNA methylation by RRBS. PMID:34819518
  • Median patient age 55.8 years; 50.8% rectal, 53.8% stage IV at presentation. PMID:34819518
  • 100% of specimens genetically determined to be of African ancestry by ADMIXTURE v1.3 against 1000 Genomes references. PMID:34819518
  • Clinical comparator: 1,145 MSKCC COADREAD MSK-IMPACT specimens (April 2014–September 2016). PMID:34819518

Assays / panels (linked)

  • MSK-IMPACT — paired tumor-normal targeted NGS on all 64 specimens.
  • Immunohistochemistry — MMR protein panel (MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2) on 94 specimens.
  • RRBS — Zymo Methyl-MiniSeq genome-wide DNA methylation on 25 specimens.
  • MSIsensor — microsatellite instability scoring.
  • ADMIXTURE — global ancestry estimation.

Papers using this cohort

  • PMID:34819518 — Alatise et al. (2021) — primary study describing race-stratified molecular profiling of Nigerian vs. MSKCC CRC.

Notable findings derived from this cohort

  • 28.1% (18/64) of Nigerian tumors were MSI-H by MSIsensor, vs. 14.2% TCGA and 8.5% MSKCC (P<0.001); among MSI-H cases, BRAF V600E was absent and KRAS mutations trended higher (61.1% vs. 41.1%) compared to MSKCC MSI-H. PMID:34819518
  • Only 28.6% of Nigerian MSI-H tumors showed MLH1 hypermethylation and only 8% were CIMP-H — substantially lower than predominant somatic MSI mechanism in high-income country cohorts; 16.7% (3/18) of Nigerian MSI-H patients carried germline Lynch syndrome MMR mutations. PMID:34819518
  • In microsatellite-stable tumors, APC was depleted in Nigeria (36.9% vs. 76.0%, P<0.01) and overall WNT-pathway alteration was 47.8% vs. 81.9% (P<0.001); RAS-pathway alteration was enriched (76.1% vs. 59.6%, P=0.03); PIK3R1 (8.7% vs. 2.2%, P=0.02) and PIK3CB (4.3% vs. 0.5%, P=0.04) were enriched. PMID:34819518
