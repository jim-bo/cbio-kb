# TFE3

## Overview

TFE3 (Transcription Factor Binding to IGHM Enhancer 3) is an MiT/TFE family transcription factor located on chromosome Xp11.23. In cancer, TFE3 is most prominently associated with recurrent chromosomal translocations that produce in-frame fusion proteins with a variety of 5' partners. These fusions preserve the TFE3 DNA-binding and transcriptional activation domains, resulting in constitutive nuclear localization and aberrant target gene activation. TFE3 fusions define a subset of Xp11 translocation renal cell carcinomas and are also reported in other tumor types including alveolar soft-part sarcoma.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- [SFPQ](../genes/SFPQ.md)–TFE3 recurrent in-frame fusion detected in 5 of 417 [CCRCC](../cancer_types/CCRCC.md) samples in the TCGA comprehensive molecular characterization; all fusion-bearing tumors were [VHL](../genes/VHL.md) wild-type and many carried Xp11 translocation by FISH — defining a molecularly distinct subset of clear cell renal cell carcinoma [PMID:23792563](../papers/23792563.md)
- ASPSCR1-TFE3 and PRCC-TFE3 fusions recovered as expected in translocation renal cell carcinomas (tRCC); TFE3 is part of the MiTF-high subtype including fusion, amplification, or overexpression [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [KIRC](../cancer_types/KIRC.md): SFPQ–TFE3 fusion identifies a VHL-wild-type subgroup within CCRCC; Xp11-translocation RCC is recognized as a distinct histological entity, but this corpus entry reflects its occurrence within a CCRCC TCGA cohort [PMID:23792563](../papers/23792563.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Mutual exclusivity with [VHL](../genes/VHL.md) mutation: all SFPQ–TFE3 fusion tumors were VHL wild-type, consistent with TFE3 fusion defining an alternative molecular pathway for CCRCC oncogenesis [PMID:23792563](../papers/23792563.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- mTORC1 inhibitors (e.g., [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md), [temsirolimus](../drugs/temsirolimus.md)) are active in advanced RCC broadly; TFE3 fusion tumors activate mTORC1 signaling, but targeted data specific to this fusion subgroup were not reported in the corpus.

## Open questions

- Whether SFPQ–TFE3 fusion CCRCC behaves clinically differently from VHL-mutant CCRCC (response to anti-VEGF vs. mTOR agents) remains unresolved from the TCGA dataset alone.
