# PCSK7

## Overview

PCSK7 (Proprotein Convertase Subtilisin/Kexin Type 7) encodes a serine endopeptidase of the subtilisin/kexin family involved in processing precursor proteins in the trans-Golgi network. In the context of cancer cell line proteomics, PCSK7 mutation has been linked to SUMO E1 heterodimer protein levels through an unknown mechanism, identifying it as a gene of interest in SUMOylation pathway biology.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- PCSK7 mutation is uniquely associated with SUMO E1 heterodimer (SAE1/UBA2) protein levels in CCLE cell lines profiled by TMT proteomics (375 lines, 22 lineages); the mechanistic basis of this association is unknown [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- No specific cancer type association reported in the corpus; the PCSK7-SUMO E1 correlation was identified pan-lineage across CCLE cell lines [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- PCSK7 mutation co-annotates with [WRN](../genes/WRN.md) synthetic lethality analysis — WRN knockdown sensitivity is also predicted by SUMO E1 (SAE1/UBA2) abundance — suggesting a potential shared pathway context [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic implications reported. The PCSK7-SUMO E1 association may represent a novel dependency relevant to SUMOylation-targeted therapy, but remains mechanistically uncharacterized [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md).

## Open questions

- The mechanistic basis of the PCSK7 mutation / SUMO E1 heterodimer protein-level correlation is unknown [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md).
- Whether PCSK7 mutation status is relevant in primary tumor biology (vs. cell lines only) has not been assessed.
