# xav-939

## Overview

XAV-939 is a small-molecule inhibitor of tankyrases (TNKS and TNKS2), which stabilizes AXIN and promotes beta-catenin degradation, thereby inhibiting WNT/beta-catenin signaling. It is an investigational tool compound used to probe WNT-pathway dependence in cancer cell models and ex vivo screens.

## Evidence in the corpus

- In the Beat [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md) program ex vivo sensitivity screen of 409 specimens from 363 AML patients against a 122-compound panel, [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md) mutation alone (without [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md) co-mutation) correlated with sensitivity to XAV-939 and the multi-kinase inhibitor [crizotinib](../drugs/crizotinib.md), distinguishing this subgroup from the BCOR/RUNX1 co-mutant subgroup which instead showed JAK-inhibitor sensitivity [PMID:30333627](../papers/30333627.md).
