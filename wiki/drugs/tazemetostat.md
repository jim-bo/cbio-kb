# tazemetostat

## Overview

Tazemetostat is an oral [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) inhibitor (histone methyltransferase inhibitor) that blocks H3K27 methylation. It is structurally related to the tool compound EPZ011989 used in preclinical [SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md) studies. Tazemetostat is FDA-approved for epithelioid sarcoma and relapsed/refractory follicular lymphoma.

## Evidence in the corpus

- Tazemetostat (structurally related to EPZ011989 used in [SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md) PDX experiments) is proposed as a clinical [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) inhibitor for combination with cisplatin/etoposide or [irinotecan](../drugs/irinotecan.md) in [SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md); tolerability data from 82 NHL patients showed grade ≥3 thrombocytopenia in 11% and grade ≥3 neutropenia in 6%, supporting feasibility of combination with cytotoxic chemotherapy; the paper argues against single-agent activity and recommends combination strategies evaluated early in clinical development [PMID:28196596](../papers/28196596.md).
- Durable partial response to tazemetostat was observed in a sarcoma NOS patient with [SMARCB1](../genes/SMARCB1.md) deletion in the MSK sarcoma sub-cohort anecdotal response series; across 7,494 sarcomas, [SMARCB1](../genes/SMARCB1.md) was inactivated in 56% of epithelioid sarcomas and 60% of malignant rhabdoid tumors [PMID:35705558](../papers/35705558.md).
- SMARCB1 deletion in 66% of epithelioid sarcoma ([EPIS](../cancer_types/EPIS.md); n=99) represents a Level 1 FDA biomarker–drug pairing for tazemetostat in the 2,138-patient MSK sarcoma IMPACT cohort; SWI/SNF alterations in 43% of uterine adenosarcoma also motivate tazemetostat exploration given SWI/SNF–[EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) synthetic lethality [PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md).
- EZH2 inhibitor; [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) truncating mutations in 7.9% [LIHC](../cancer_types/LIHC.md), 9.9% [IHCH](../cancer_types/IHCH.md), 7.2% [STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md) in Chinese pan-cancer cohort (n=10,194) are actionable with tazemetostat at OncoKB Level 4 [PMID:35871175](../papers/35871175.md)
