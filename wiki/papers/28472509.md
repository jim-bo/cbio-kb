**PMID:** 28472509 · **DOI:** 10.1093/neuonc/nox086 · **Journal:** Neuro-Oncology (2017)

## TL;DR

Thomas et al. report a multicenter phase II trial (NCT00588523) testing whether single-agent [temozolomide](../drugs/temozolomide.md) (TMZ) induction followed by myeloablative high-dose [thiotepa](../drugs/thiotepa.md)/[busulfan](../drugs/busulfan.md) chemotherapy with autologous stem-cell transplant (ASCT) can defer radiotherapy in newly diagnosed [anaplastic oligodendroglioma](../cancer_types/ODG3.md) (AO) and anaplastic oligoastrocytoma (AOA). Forty-one patients were enrolled (85% with 1p/19q codeletion); 21 received HDC-ASCT. After median follow-up of 66 months, the 2-year PFS for transplanted patients was 86%, 5-year PFS was 60%, and no transplanted patient had died (5-year [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) 100%). Among all 33 1p/19q-codeleted patients, 5-year PFS was 50% and 5-year OS was 93%. NGS using the [MSK-IMPACT 410-gene panel](../methods/IMPACT410.md) on 19 tumors confirmed the expected oligodendroglioma signature ([IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md), [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter, [CIC](../genes/CIC.md), [FUBP1](../genes/FUBP1.md)) in codeleted cases and glioblastoma-like signatures ([EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) amplification/mutation, [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md), wild-type IDH1/2) in 1p/19q-intact cases. The full clinical and genomic dataset is available as the [odg_msk_2017 cBioPortal study](../datasets/odg_msk_2017.md).

## Cohort & data

- **Trial:** Investigator-initiated multicenter phase II trial sponsored by Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center (NCT00588523), enrolling 41 patients with newly diagnosed [anaplastic oligodendroglioma](../cancer_types/ODG3.md) (AO; 36 patients, 88%) or anaplastic oligoastrocytoma (AOA; 5 patients, 12%) between August 2005 and November 2012 [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **Eligibility:** WHO 2000 diagnosis of AO or AOA (mixed tumors required ≥25% oligodendroglial elements), age ≥18, KPS ≥60. 1p/19q codeletion was assessed but was not an inclusion criterion [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **Patient characteristics:** 27 men / 14 women; median age 44 years (range 30–66); median KPS 90 (range 70–100); median baseline Mini-Mental State Exam 30 (range 23–30) [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **1p/19q status (FISH, [fish-1p19q](../methods/fish-1p19q.md)):** 33/39 (85%) codeleted, 6 non-codeleted, 2 unknown [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **Treatment regimen:** Induction with oral [temozolomide](../drugs/temozolomide.md) 200 mg/m² days 1–5 in 28-day cycles for 6 cycles; responding/SD patients received high-dose chemotherapy with intravenous [thiotepa](../drugs/thiotepa.md) 250 mg/m²/day × 3 (days −8 to −6), then intravenous [busulfan](../drugs/busulfan.md) 3.2 mg/kg/day × 3 (days −5 to −3), then PBSC reinfusion on day 0 [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **Genomic assay:** Of 41 enrolled patients, 19 had tissue with adequate DNA for NGS using the [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) assay — a hybridization-capture, Illumina HiSeq 2500 platform with full exon coverage of 410 cancer-related genes ([IMPACT410](../methods/IMPACT410.md)) — 14 with confirmed 1p/19q codeletion and 5 with 1p/19q intact tumors [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **Annotation:** Sequencing results were matched against the [OncoKB](../methods/oncokb-annotation.md) database (accessed 6 January 2017) to classify potentially oncogenic and actionable mutations by level of evidence [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **Cancer type:** [Oligodendroglioma, IDH-mutant, 1p/19q-codeleted, Grade 3 (ODG3)](../cancer_types/ODG3.md) — historically called anaplastic oligodendroglioma. Parent code: [ODG](../cancer_types/ODG.md).
- **Dataset:** [odg_msk_2017](../datasets/odg_msk_2017.md) — clinical and genomic data publicly available at http://cbioportal.org/study?id=[odg_msk_2017](../datasets/odg_msk_2017.md).
- **Median follow-up of survivors:** 65.7 months [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).

## Key findings

- **Treatment was well tolerated with no toxic deaths.** Of 41 enrolled patients, 38 received TMZ (198 cycles total); 29 (70.7%) completed all 6 cycles. Grade 3/4 TMZ toxicities included thrombocytopenia (n=5), lymphopenia (n=3), neutropenia (n=1), hyponatremia (n=1). Among 21 transplanted patients, no toxic deaths were observed. Median time to neutrophil engraftment was 9 days (range 8–12), to platelet engraftment 12 days (range 9–18) [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **2-year PFS in transplanted patients (primary endpoint) was 85.7% (95% CI: 62%–95.2%).** 5-year PFS in this group was 60.4% (95% CI: 36%–78%); 5-year OS was 100% — none of the 21 transplanted patients had died at the time of analysis [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **Intent-to-treat (N=41) survival:** Median PFS 49.5 months; 2-year PFS 66.7% (95% CI: 48.8%–79.5%); median OS not reached; 5-year OS 78.6% (95% CI: 61.6%–88.8%) [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **1p/19q-codeleted population (N=33):** Median PFS 62.8 months; 2-year PFS 75.9% (95% CI: 55.9%–87.7%); 5-year PFS 50.3% (95% CI: 30.7%–67%); median OS not reached; 5-year OS 93.4% (95% CI: 76.2%–98.3%). 1p/19q codeletion was a significant predictor of PFS (P = 0.03) and OS (P < 0.001) [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **Radiotherapy deferral was substantial.** Among 1p/19q-codeleted patients, median time to RT was not reached. At 2 years, 82% of codeleted patients (95% CI: 62%–92%) were alive without RT; at 5 years, 52% (95% CI: 30%–74%) were alive without RT [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **Transplant eligibility was tightly tied to 1p/19q codeletion.** All 21 transplanted patients lacked intact 1p/19q (20 codeleted, 1 unknown); patients with codeletion preferentially passed induction. 9/35 (25.7%) experienced disease progression during induction TMZ and were referred for RT [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **TMZ induction was non-inferior to PCV induction in an exploratory cross-trial comparison.** Versus the prior PCV-induction cohort (n=20) with the same HDC/ASCT backbone, the TMZ cohort had longer median OS in all patients (HR 3.38 favoring TMZ, P = 0.005) and in 1p/19q-codeleted patients (HR 5.68, P = 0.018). PFS comparisons trended in the same direction (HR 1.79 all patients, P = 0.086; HR 1.54 codeleted, P = 0.34) [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **NGS recapitulated the canonical oligodendroglioma signature.** In 19 sequenced tumors, [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter mutation was the most frequent abnormality (18/19), followed by [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) (13/19; all R132H), [CIC](../genes/CIC.md) (10/19), [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) (6/19), [NOTCH2](../genes/NOTCH2.md) (5/19), and [FUBP1](../genes/FUBP1.md) (5/19). None of these alterations were predictive of PFS or OS [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **1p/19q-intact tumors displayed glioblastoma-like signatures.** Wild-type [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) and [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) plus mutations/amplifications in [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), and [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) — consistent with de novo glioblastoma — were observed in 1p/19q-intact cases [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **NGS exposed a false-positive 1p/19q FISH call.** One patient classified as 1p/19q-codeleted by [FISH](../methods/fish-1p19q.md) had a glioblastoma-like NGS signature ([PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) loss, [CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md) and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)P16INK4A/P14ARF deletion, IDH1/2 wild type) with no NGS evidence of 1p/19q loss; this patient progressed on the first MRI and died at 13 months. The authors recommend NGS as a more reliable 1p/19q screening tool than FISH for trials deferring upfront RT [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **Actionable mutations were heterogeneous and individually rare.** [OncoKB](../methods/oncokb-annotation.md) classification of the 19 sequenced tumors identified Level 2B alterations ([CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) amplification in 2/19; [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md) total in 1/19), Level 3B alterations ([IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) R132H in 13/19, [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) R172K in 1/19, [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) Q61H in 1/19, [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) in 2/19), and hotspot mutations of unknown therapeutic implication in [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md), [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md), [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md), and [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md). Other potentially oncogenic mutations spanned [APC](../genes/APC.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md), [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md), [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md), [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) amplification, [ETV1](../genes/ETV1.md) amplification, [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md), [GLI1](../genes/GLI1.md) amplification, [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md), [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) amplification, [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) (4/19, 21%), [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [RASA1](../genes/RASA1.md), [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md), and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **Public data release.** Raw clinical and genomic data are available at http://cbioportal.org/study?id=odg_msk_2017 — see [odg_msk_2017](../datasets/odg_msk_2017.md) — for further meta-analyses [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).

## Clinical implications

- **Chemotherapy-only approach can defer radiotherapy in 1p/19q-codeleted [anaplastic oligodendroglioma](../cancer_types/ODG3.md).** With 5-year RT-free survival of 52% in codeleted patients and 5-year OS of 93%, induction TMZ + HDC-ASCT is a viable upfront strategy that may delay RT-related neurotoxicity (cognitive impairment, neuroendocrine disorders, cerebral necrosis) without compromising survival, especially relevant in long-term survivors of this slowly progressive disease [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **Single-agent [temozolomide](../drugs/temozolomide.md) is a reasonable substitute for [PCV](../drugs/procarbazine.md)/[lomustine](../drugs/lomustine.md)/[vincristine](../drugs/vincristine.md) induction.** In an exploratory cross-trial comparison, TMZ-induction patients fared at least as well as the prior PCV cohort with the same myeloablative backbone (HR 3.38 for OS, P = 0.005, all patients) — countering concerns that TMZ is inferior to PCV in this disease [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **Chemotherapy alone is not appropriate for 1p/19q-intact tumors.** Tumors lacking 1p/19q codeletion progressed early and often had glioblastoma-like NGS signatures; the authors support requiring 1p/19q assessment for trial eligibility in chemotherapy-deferral strategies [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **NGS should be considered the screening tool for 1p/19q status in chemotherapy-only trials.** A patient classified as 1p/19q-codeleted by [FISH](../methods/fish-1p19q.md) but with NGS-detected glioblastoma-like signature died at 13 months, illustrating the clinical cost of false-positive FISH calls when RT is to be deferred [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **Aside from [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md)/[IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md), actionable mutations are individually rare and patchy.** Each [OncoKB](../methods/oncokb-annotation.md)-classified actionable alteration ([CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) amplification, [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)) was found in only 1–2 of 19 patients, motivating basket-trial designs that pool oligodendroglioma patients with other tumor types sharing the same target [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Continuous follow-up is still required to characterize later endpoints.** Although follow-up exceeds 5 years, full assessment of OS in this slow-progressing disease remains incomplete; prospective collection of post-progression imaging and salvage-RT response patterns was not part of the original design [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **Neuropsychological outcomes were not formally measured.** Although no progression-free patient was reported with neurotoxicity, no formal neuropsychological evaluation was performed, leaving open the central rationale for deferring RT [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **No correlative immunology.** T-cell repertoire and post-transplant immune-reconstitution studies were not collected, although the dramatically favorable OS relative to PFS suggests an unmeasured immune component may contribute to disease control [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **Small sample, voluntary attrition, no randomization.** Of 41 enrolled patients, only 21 completed HDC-ASCT (5 refused after eligibility). Cross-trial comparisons with the prior PCV-induction cohort suffer from differing length of follow-up and lack of randomization — TMZ vs PCV non-inferiority within an HDC-ASCT backbone is suggestive, not definitive [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **Whether myeloablative HDC + ASCT is necessary versus standard-dose chemotherapy alone is unresolved.** The trial does not have an arm of TMZ alone (without transplant); results may not generalize to single-agent TMZ regimens [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **NGS analysis was limited to 19/41 patients with adequate tissue/DNA quality**, which is too small to test whether non-canonical mutations (e.g. [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)) modify response or prognosis within codeleted tumors. The authors explicitly state that none of TERT/IDH1/CIC/FUBP1/NOTCH1/NOTCH2 was predictive of PFS or OS in this small NGS subset [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
- **The CODEL trial (NCT00887146)** previously suggested TMZ-alone was inferior to RT-containing arms (12 vs 24 patients), prompting discontinuation of the TMZ-alone arm; the present authors argue that the CODEL TMZ-alone subset may have been an outlier compared with other prospective chemotherapy-alone studies, but the question remains formally open [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md).
