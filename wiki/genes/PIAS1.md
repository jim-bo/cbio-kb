# PIAS1

## Overview

PIAS1 (Protein Inhibitor of Activated STAT 1) is an E3 SUMO ligase and transcriptional co-regulator that negatively regulates JAK/STAT signaling by blocking [STAT1](../genes/STAT1.md) DNA binding. In cancer, amplification of PIAS1 can suppress interferon-gamma pathway signaling, thereby dampening anti-tumor immune responses. This makes PIAS1 amplification a candidate genomic mechanism of primary resistance to immune checkpoint blockade.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Focal amplification on the PIAS1 locus was enriched in progressive disease (PD) patients in a pooled ICB cohort (193 patients across melanoma, [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md), bladder, HNSCC, and other cancer types); amplifications of PIAS1 and [PIAS4](../genes/PIAS4.md) together with [SOCS1](../genes/SOCS1.md) and [SOCS3](../genes/SOCS3.md) amplifications and interferon-gene deletions constitute an interferon-gamma pathway CNA signature enriched in PD (19/123 PD vs 3/70 CR/PR; p=0.019, Fisher's exact) [PMID:30150660](../papers/30150660.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Pan-cancer (pooled melanoma, NSCLC, bladder, HNSCC, and others): PIAS1 amplification as a potential ICB resistance marker [PMID:30150660](../papers/30150660.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-amplified with PIAS4, SOCS1, and SOCS3 as part of an interferon-gamma pathway suppression CNA pattern in ICB non-responders [PMID:30150660](../papers/30150660.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- PIAS1 amplification is a candidate genomic predictor of primary resistance to anti-CTLA-4 and anti-PD-1/L1 checkpoint inhibitors; loss of intact interferon-gamma signaling may be a candidate exclusion criterion for ICB [PMID:30150660](../papers/30150660.md)

## Open questions

- Whether PIAS1 amplification is causally sufficient to suppress IFN-gamma signaling in vivo or is a passenger CNA co-amplified with a nearby driver has not been established in this cohort.
