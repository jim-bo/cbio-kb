# Oral Cavity Squamous Cell Carcinoma (OCSC)

## Overview

Oral Cavity Squamous Cell Carcinoma (OCSC) is a subtype of Head and Neck Squamous Cell Carcinoma ([HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md)) arising from the oral cavity epithelium. It is classified under the Head and Neck Cancer main type in OncoTree (parent node [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md)). OCSC is characterized by high genomic instability (74% of tumors with ≥20 copy-number alterations), four major driver pathways (mitogenic signaling, Notch, cell cycle, [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)), and a high proportion of targetable alterations.

## Cohorts in the corpus

- [hnsc_mdanderson_2013](../datasets/hnsc_mdanderson_2013.md) — 40 OSCC patients from MD Anderson Cancer Center; multi-platform analysis including Affymetrix SNP6 copy-number arrays (38 tumors), whole-exome sequencing, Illumina 450k methylation arrays, Affymetrix exon expression arrays, and Agilent miRNA arrays. [PMID:23619168](../papers/23619168.md)

## Recurrent alterations

- Multi-platform genomic analysis of 40 OSCC (an [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md) subtype) identified four major driver pathways (mitogenic signaling 63%, Notch 66%, cell cycle 94%, [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) 60%), novel [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md) inactivation in 46% and [CASP8](../genes/CASP8.md) mutations in 10% defining a CN-quiet subtype; 80% of tumors harbored at least one targetable alteration [PMID:23619168](../papers/23619168.md)
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutated in 60% (24/40); 13% splice-site mutations (markedly enriched vs. 2% baseline across tumor types); [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations associated with higher CNA burden (p=0.0051). [PMID:23619168](../papers/23619168.md)
- [FAT1](../genes/FAT1.md) mutated in 30% (12/40), the highest frequency reported for HNSCC at time of publication; two-thirds were inactivating; 46% combined inactivation including focal deletions. [PMID:23619168](../papers/23619168.md)
- [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) mutated in 9% of primary OSCC; 16/44 HNSCC cell lines had [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) alterations; functional [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) signaling (ICN1) inhibited proliferation in vitro and in orthotopic xenograft models via G1 arrest and p21 induction. [PMID:23619168](../papers/23619168.md)
- [CASP8](../genes/CASP8.md) mutations in 10% (4/40) define a low-CNA subtype co-occurring with [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) mutations (p=0.0016 in discovery cohort; validated in TCGA [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md) p<0.0001). [PMID:23619168](../papers/23619168.md)
- Arm-level CNAs in >50% of tumors: 3p loss (76%), 8q gain (63%), 3q gain (58%), 18q loss (58%), 8p loss (53%); 8p loss associated with reduced disease-free survival (p=0.047). [PMID:23619168](../papers/23619168.md)
- Focal high-level amplifications: [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) (22%), [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) (16%); cell-cycle pathway altered in 94% (33/35) via [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) amplification and/or [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) loss. [PMID:23619168](../papers/23619168.md)
- Oral cavity squamous cell carcinoma (OCSC) comprised 172/279 (62%) of the TCGA [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md) cohort; [CASP8](../genes/CASP8.md) inactivating mutations were highly enriched in OCSC (92% of all [CASP8](../genes/CASP8.md) mutations), and [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md)+[CASP8](../genes/CASP8.md)+wild-type [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) co-mutation defined a favourable-outcome M-class subset predominantly in this site. [PMID:25631445](../papers/25631445.md)

## Subtypes

- **CASP8-mutant subtype (~10%):** Low copy-number alteration burden; [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) co-mutation; more aggressive orthotopic xenograft phenotype with [CASP8](../genes/CASP8.md) loss-of-function. [PMID:23619168](../papers/23619168.md)
- **mRNA/miRNA expression cluster 1 (poorly differentiated):** Higher ploidy (3.33 vs. 2.62), fewer mutations (median 53 vs. 81, p=0.0073); all non-tobacco users in this cluster (p<0.007). [PMID:23619168](../papers/23619168.md)
- **CIMP-like methylation subgroup:** Higher methylation frequency, fewer CNAs; tobacco use associated with methylation cluster (p<0.009). [PMID:23619168](../papers/23619168.md)

## Therapeutic landscape

- 80% (28/35) of OSCC tumors harbored ≥1 targetable alteration; 54% (19/35) had ≥2; notable targets include PI3K signaling (PIK3CA/AKT1-3, 34%), SRC-family kinases (SRC/LYN/YES1, 29%), [TNK2](../genes/TNK2.md) (17%), PTK2/FAK (14%), Aurora kinases (AURKA/C, 14%). [PMID:23619168](../papers/23619168.md)
- [cetuximab](../drugs/cetuximab.md) (anti-EGFR) is an established HNSCC therapy; [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) amplified in 16% of OSCC, but downstream PI3K alterations ([HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md)) may explain limited EGFR-inhibitor benefit. [PMID:23619168](../papers/23619168.md)
- [cisplatin](../drugs/cisplatin.md) remains backbone therapy alongside surgery and radiation. [PMID:23619168](../papers/23619168.md)
