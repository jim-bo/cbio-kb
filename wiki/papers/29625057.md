## TL;DR

Lee et al. established a biobank of 22 patient-derived bladder cancer organoid lines from 18 patients undergoing transurethral resection (TUR) or nephroureterectomy at Columbia University Medical Center and Memorial Sloan Kettering. Targeted sequencing with the [MSK-IMPACT 468-gene panel](../methods/IMPACT468.md) plus whole-exome sequencing showed the organoids recapitulate the mutational spectrum of human bladder cancer — including recurrent alterations in [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md), epigenetic regulators ([ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md)), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), and a validated [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)–[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md) fusion. Organoids underwent linear and branched clonal evolution in culture and orthotopic xenografts, displayed APOBEC and smoking mutational signatures, and showed drug responses (to [trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md), [gemcitabine](../drugs/gemcitabine.md), [sirolimus](../drugs/sirolimus.md), the mTOR inhibitor [AZD8055](../drugs/azd8055.md), and the FGFR inhibitor JNJ-42756493/[erdafitinib](../drugs/erdafitinib.md)) that partially tracked with mutational profile and were validated in vivo. Sequencing data were deposited as cBioPortal study [bladder_columbia_msk_2018](../datasets/bladder_columbia_msk_2018.md).

## Cohort & data

- **Patients:** 18 total — 17 with urothelial carcinoma ([BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md)) and 1 with squamous cell carcinoma of the bladder ([BLSC](../cancer_types/BLSC.md)); 13 males / 5 females, ages 48–86 (median 72.7). 2 of the patients contributed patient-derived xenograft (PDX) lines established from nephroureterectomies (upper-tract urothelial carcinoma, [UTUC](../cancer_types/UTUC.md)) at Memorial Sloan Kettering.
- **Organoid lines:** 22 patient-derived bladder cancer organoid lines (SCBO-1 through SCBO-16, with metachronous lines designated SCBO-3.2, SCBO-11.2, SCBO-11.3, etc.). Recent establishment efficiency ≈70% (n=12/17 during the last nine months prior to publication); lines were serially passaged up to 26 passages and successfully cryopreserved. Two organoid lines (SMBO-1, SMBO-2) were generated from MSK PDXs.
- **Orthotopic xenografts:** 83% efficiency (n=15/18) of conversion from organoid to orthotopic xenograft via ultrasound-guided implantation into NOG mouse bladder wall.
- **Sequencing:** Targeted exome via the [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) 468-gene panel ([IMPACT468](../methods/IMPACT468.md)); ~500-fold coverage enabling subclonal mutation detection. [Whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) on 24 samples from 5 lines (Agilent SureSelect V4, HiSeq 2500, ~240× on-target). [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) on parental tumors and organoids (deposited GEO GSE103990). Targeted sequencing data deposited in cBioPortal as study [bladder_columbia_msk_2018](../datasets/bladder_columbia_msk_2018.md).
- **Reference genome:** hg19 (BWA alignment, MuTect for SNVs, Pindel for indels <50 bp, VEP/vcf2maf annotation).
- **Functional assays:** [Organoid drug screening](../methods/organoid-drug-screening.md) (CellTiter-Glo 3D, 7-point 5-fold dilution series, 6-day incubation), Western blot, immunofluorescence, BASE47 and MDACC luminal/basal classifiers, [phylogenetic tree reconstruction](../methods/phylogenetic-tree-reconstruction.md), [mutational signatures](../methods/mutational-signatures.md) analysis (29 COSMIC signatures per Alexandrov et al. 2013), [Sanger sequencing](../methods/sanger-sequencing.md) of the [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)–[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md) fusion.

## Key findings

- **Histopathologic concordance:** H&E review by a urological pathologist showed strong concordance in histopathologic features between organoids and parental tumors; benign tissue was never observed during serial passaging. The SCBO-11 line/parental tumor displayed squamous-cell-carcinoma features ([BLSC](../cancer_types/BLSC.md)).
- **Mutational concordance:** 11 of the lines analyzed showed >80% mutational concordance with their parental tumor; only 4 lines displayed <60% concordance.
- **Mutation-rate landscape** in non-recurrent organoid lines (n=15):
  - Epigenetic regulators ([ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md)) mutated in **73% (11/15)**.
  - Activating [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) mutations in **60% (9/15)**, consistent with the ~70%/~12% rates reported for non-muscle invasive vs muscle invasive bladder cancers.
  - [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutated in **33% (5/15)** — lower than the >50% rate in muscle-invasive disease, reflecting predominantly non-muscle-invasive origin.
  - Only one line had an oncogenic [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) mutation — lower than established bladder cancer cell lines.
  - Less common drivers ([STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md), [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md), [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md)) detected.
- **[FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)–[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md) fusion:** SCBO-10 line and parental tumor harbored a fusion between exon 17 of [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) and exon 11 of [TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md), validated by PCR amplification and [Sanger sequencing](../methods/sanger-sequencing.md). The exact junction matches one previously reported in glioblastoma line GBM-021 and in the RT112 bladder cancer cell line.
- **Clonal evolution in culture:** Truncal mutations were retained, but subclonal mutations could be gained or lost during serial passaging and xenograft conversion. Examples include the [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md)(S45F) activating hotspot mutation arising in SCBO-3.2 (not detected in parental tumor or early-passage organoids), and SCBO-5 losing [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md)(D227N) and [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md)(H538Y) while gaining [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md)(S831). Phylogenetic analysis of WES data showed both linear and branched evolutionary patterns.
- **Mutational signatures:** APOBEC signature detected in SCBO-4 and SCBO-5 (consistent with published bladder cancer signature literature); a smoking signature was identified in SCBO-6, established from a former smoker. Signatures were stable across passages and xenografts despite ongoing clonal evolution.
- **Tumor mutational burden:** SCBO-3 and SCBO-3.2 TMB resembled low-grade non-muscle-invasive disease; SCBO-4, SCBO-5, SCBO-6 TMB resembled high-grade non-muscle-invasive and muscle-invasive bladder tumors.
- **Luminal/basal phenotypic plasticity:** 36% (8/22) of lines were phenotypically stable; 64% (14/22) showed shifts. In the unstable group, 86% (12/14) of parental tumors that predominantly expressed luminal markers shifted to a basal phenotype in organoid culture; these reverted to the luminal phenotype in xenografts in 88% (7/8) of cases and recurred to basal in xenograft-derived organoids in 100% (5/5). BASE47 (Damrauer et al. 2014) and MDACC (Choi et al. 2014) classifiers placed most organoids into basal/luminal/mixed categories concordant with immunofluorescence.
- **Drug response:**
  - SCBO-1 and SCBO-5 (both [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)-mutant) responded to MEK inhibitor [trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md) and ERK inhibitor SCH772984; SCBO-2 and SCBO-6 (also [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)-mutant) did not respond, indicating mutation alone is insufficient to predict response.
  - **No** lines responded to three different FGFR inhibitors under the conditions tested.
  - SCBO-6 ([TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md) nonsense mutation, [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) activating mutation) was sensitive to mTOR inhibitor [AZD8055](../drugs/azd8055.md); SCBO-5 (no [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md) mutation) was resistant. Western blot confirmed mTOR pathway inhibition (p-S6 downregulation) in both sensitive and resistant lines.
  - SCBO-6 showed additive responses to the FGFR inhibitor JNJ-42756493 ([erdafitinib](../drugs/erdafitinib.md)) combined with either [AZD8055](../drugs/azd8055.md) or the mTOR inhibitor [sirolimus](../drugs/sirolimus.md).
  - Metachronous comparison: SCBO-3.2 (post-treatment with intravesical BCG and [mitomycin-C](../drugs/mitomycin-c.md), 420 days after SCBO-3) was markedly more resistant to a wide range of drugs than SCBO-3, whereas SCBO-11.2 (untreated interval) had drug responses very similar to SCBO-11.
- **In vivo validation:** Orthotopic xenograft drug studies (6 mice/arm SCBO-6, 4 mice/arm SCBO-3, 8 mice/arm SCBO-5) showed significant tumor-size reductions on ultrasound for [trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md) (0.5 mg/kg, 5-on/2-off oral gavage) and [gemcitabine](../drugs/gemcitabine.md) (100 mg/kg twice weekly IP), with decreased Ki67 ([trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md)) and increased cleaved caspase-3 ([gemcitabine](../drugs/gemcitabine.md)) on histology.

## Genes & alterations

- [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) — activating mutations in 60% (9/15) of non-recurrent lines; SCBO-10 carries an [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)–[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md) fusion (exon 17–exon 11 junction). Mutation alone did not predict trametinib/SCH772984 response.
- [TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md) — fusion partner with [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) in SCBO-10; same junction previously reported in glioblastoma GBM-021 and bladder cell line RT112.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — mutated in 33% (5/15) of lines; nuclear p53 immunostaining concordant with mutation calls.
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) — driver in subset of lines; example: SCBO-5 lost a subclonal [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md)(D227N) variant during culture.
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) — detected as a less-common driver alteration in organoid biobank.
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md) — epigenetic regulators mutated collectively in 73% (11/15) lines; SCBO-5 gained a [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md)(S831) variant during organoid culture.
- [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md) — detected as a recurrent driver in lines; consistent with published bladder cancer landscape.
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — oncogenic mutation in only one organoid line; lower frequency than reported in established bladder cancer cell lines.
- [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) — S45F activating hotspot mutation appeared in SCBO-3.2 (recurrent tumor after BCG + [mitomycin-C](../drugs/mitomycin-c.md) treatment); absent from parental tumor and early organoid passages — a documented clonal-evolution event.
- [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md) — subclonal H538Y variant present in early SCBO-5 organoids, lost during serial passaging.
- [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md) — nonsense mutation in SCBO-6 conferred sensitivity to mTOR inhibitor [AZD8055](../drugs/azd8055.md); additive with FGFR inhibitor JNJ-42756493.
- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) — promoter region covered by the [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) panel design (intronic/promoter capture in 17 recurrently rearranged genes plus [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter).

## Clinical implications

- **Co-clinical organoid models for precision oncology in bladder cancer:** authors propose that patient-derived bladder organoids can be established efficiently enough (≈70%) to serve as preclinical avatars for individual patients, particularly relevant because bladder cancer patients commonly undergo repeated TUR biopsies and intravesical therapy cycles. Drug responses identified in organoids could in principle be validated in organoid-derived xenografts and then used to guide intravesical therapy choices.
- **Combinatorial therapy hypotheses:** [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)-activated, [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md)-null tumors (modeled by SCBO-6) showed additive benefit from combining an FGFR inhibitor (JNJ-42756493 / [erdafitinib](../drugs/erdafitinib.md)) with mTOR inhibition ([AZD8055](../drugs/azd8055.md) or [sirolimus](../drugs/sirolimus.md)) — a mutation-guided combination strategy worth clinical evaluation.
- **Treatment-resistance modeling:** metachronous organoid lines (e.g., SCBO-3 vs SCBO-3.2) bracket pre- and post-treatment-failure disease, providing a model system to interrogate mechanisms of resistance to intravesical BCG plus [mitomycin-C](../drugs/mitomycin-c.md).
- **Biomarker caveat:** [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) mutation status alone was insufficient to predict MEK/ERK-inhibitor response across organoids (4 [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)-mutant lines tested; only 2 responded). The authors emphasize that "partial correlations with mutational profiles" should temper expectations of single-marker drug-matching schemes in bladder cancer.

## Limitations & open questions

- Authors note the organoid lines cluster together in PCA rather than with their parental tumors, indicating that organoid culture itself imposes a transcriptional signature (cell-cycle and ERBB-signaling enrichment from exogenous [EGF](../genes/EGF.md); loss of stromal/immune components), making subtype classifications that depend on stromal genes (e.g., Robertson et al. 2017) or cell-cycle genes (e.g., Hedegaard et al. 2016) unreliable for organoids.
- The luminal-to-basal phenotypic shift in 64% of lines is largely reversible in xenografts and is presumed epigenetic, but the mechanistic basis (epigenetic plasticity, stromal dependency, or recapitulation of squamous differentiation) remains unresolved.
- No lines responded to any of three tested FGFR inhibitors, despite the prevalence of [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) activation — authors flag this as either inadequate potency under their conditions or unexplained molecular complexity; the absence of an organoid-level [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)-inhibitor response benchmark is an open question given the clinical activity of [erdafitinib](../drugs/erdafitinib.md) in [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)-altered bladder cancer.
- Authors call for prospective co-clinical studies to determine whether organoid drug-response prediction prospectively matches patient response to the same treatments.
- The biobank is enriched for non-muscle-invasive disease (only 4 muscle-invasive lines from TUR samples) — under-sampling of high-grade muscle-invasive biology is acknowledged.
- The patient cohort is from a single institution (Columbia/NewYork-Presbyterian) with 31% female and 19% ethnic-minority representation — generalizability to broader populations is not formally assessed.
