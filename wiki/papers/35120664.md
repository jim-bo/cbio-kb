**PMID:** 35120664 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.cell.2022.01.003 · **Journal:** Cell (2022)

## TL;DR

Nguyen et al. assembled MSK-MET, a pan-cancer cohort of 25,775 patients (15,632 primary and 10,143 metastatic specimens) spanning 50 tumor types, all profiled with [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/IMPACT468.md) targeted sequencing and linked to 99,419 EHR-derived metastatic events across 21 anatomic sites. By comparing primary vs. metastatic tumors, primary tumors from metastatic vs. non-metastatic patients, and metastases by organ site, they showed that fraction of genome altered ([FGA](../genes/FGA.md), a proxy for chromosomal instability) correlates with metastatic burden in some lineages (notably [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md), [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md), HR+/HER2+ ductal breast) but not in others ([COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md), [HGSOC](../cancer_types/HGSOC.md)), and identified driver-level associations between specific somatic alterations and target-organ metastasis ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).

## Cohort & data

- 25,775 patients profiled by [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/IMPACT468.md) (341/410/468-gene panels: [IMPACT341](../methods/IMPACT341.md), [IMPACT410](../methods/IMPACT410.md), [IMPACT468](../methods/IMPACT468.md)) between 2013-11-18 and 2020-01-06; 15,632 (61%) primary and 10,143 (39%) metastatic specimens across 50 tumor types ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- Median sequencing coverage 653x (IQR 525–790); median tumor purity 40% (IQR 20–50%); median sample-to-sequencing interval 62 days ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- Most common metastatic biopsy sites: lymph nodes (n=2,305, 23%), liver (n=2,289, 23%), lung (n=982, 10%), bone (n=726, 7%) ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- 99,419 metastatic events from 21,546 metastatic patients extracted from electronic health records and mapped to 21 reference anatomic locations ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- Median age at sequencing 64y; median follow-up 30 months; five-year survival 40% (range: 90% testicular seminoma to 10% pancreatic adenocarcinoma) ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- Allele-specific copy-number analysis on a subset (n=17,224) used [FACETS](../methods/facets.md) to derive purity/ploidy-adjusted FGA, whole-genome doubling (WGD) status, and clonality estimates ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- Independent validation cohort: 9,215 MSK-IMPACT samples sequenced 2020-01-07 to 2021-08-18 ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- Dataset publicly available as [msk_met_2021](../datasets/msk_met_2021.md) on cBioPortal and Zenodo (DOI 10.5281/zenodo.5801902) ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).

## Key findings

- **Metastases are more chromosomally unstable** than primaries in 16 of 50 tumor types by FGA; differences confirmed in 11 tumor types using FACETS-adjusted FGA ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- **Whole-genome doubling is more frequent in metastases** in 7 tumor types; uterine endometrioid showed the most striking shift (WGD 4% primary vs 16% metastatic; CIN 0% vs 14%) ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- **TMB is higher in metastases** in 10 tumor types; TMB-high (>=10 mut/Mb) rates rose from 19% to 27% in [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md), 2% to 7% in HR+/HER2- ductal breast cancer, and 5% to 19% in lobular breast cancer (all q<0.001) ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- **Clonality**: metastases from 12 tumor types had a significantly higher fraction of clonal mutations, consistent with an evolutionary bottleneck ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- **67 statistically significant oncogenic-alteration frequency differences** between primaries and metastases across 17 tumor types; 53 enriched in metastases vs only 14 in primaries ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- **FGA correlates with metastatic burden** pan-cancer and in 11 individual tumor types; the strongest association was prostate adenocarcinoma (rho=0.33, q=7.0E-45). No association in MSS [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md) where CIN is already high at low metastatic burden ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- **TMB does not correlate with metastatic burden pan-cancer**; positive in 3 tumor types, negative in endometrioid and hypermutated uterine cancer ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- **24 oncogenic-alteration and 16 pathway associations with metastatic burden** identified across 8 and 7 tumor types respectively; most enriched in high-burden patients ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- **Organ-specific metastasis genomics**: 57 oncogenic alterations and 48 oncogenic-pathway associations linked to specific organotropisms across 10 and 12 tumor types ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- Higher metastatic burden was associated with shorter overall survival in 39 of 50 (78%) tumor types ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — mutation enriched in metastases in 7 tumor types ([LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md), [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md), HR+/HER2- ductal breast, MSS [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md), lobular breast, pancreatic neuroendocrine, endometrioid uterine); inverse in [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md) (62% primary vs 45% metastatic, q=0.01). Positively correlated with metastatic burden in [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md), [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md), and HR+/HER2- ductal breast. More frequent in [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) CNS/brain mets (55% vs 66%, q=0.002) and in [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md) liver mets (63% vs 84%, q<0.001) ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — deletion enriched in metastases in 5 tumor types; in [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) more frequent in skin (16% vs 60%, q=0.03) and liver mets (16% vs 27%, q=0.03) but less frequent in lymph node mets (19% vs 12%, q=0.002). Deletion frequency correlated with metastatic burden in bladder urothelial, [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md), and papillary thyroid cancer. [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) mutations less frequent in primary [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md) of metastatic patients (17% vs 7%, q=0.02) ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — mutation and deletion enriched in metastases in 4 tumor types. Mutation more frequent in [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md) brain metastases (7% vs 23%, q=0.01) and in [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md) patients with brain metastasis (7% vs 14%, q=0.04). [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) deletion more frequent in [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) bone mets (9% vs 19%, q<0.001); PTEN loss more frequent in [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) liver mets (11% vs 30%, q<0.001) ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) — amplification enriched in metastases in 4 tumor types; amplification frequency correlates with metastatic burden in [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) and [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md). More frequent in primaries of metastatic patients in [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) (1% vs 4%, q=0.03), MSS [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md) (1% vs 4%, q=0.03), and triple-negative ductal breast (3% vs 17%, q=0.03) ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [AR](../genes/AR.md) — amplification (1% vs 30%) and mutations (0% vs 6%) dramatically more frequent in [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) metastases (both q<0.001); [AR](../genes/AR.md) amplification positively associated with metastatic burden in metastatic [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md); enriched in bone (5% vs 21%, q<0.001) and brain (14% vs 34%, q<0.001) [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) metastases. Attributed to androgen-deprivation therapy selection pressure ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) — mutations enriched in HR+/HER2- ductal breast (2% vs 19%, q<0.001), lobular breast (2% vs 13%, q<0.001), and endometrioid uterine (3% vs 10%, q=0.002) metastases; correlate with metastatic burden in HR+/HER2- ductal and lobular breast; enriched in liver metastases of HR+/HER2- ductal breast (5% vs 16%, q<0.001 burden-associated; 79% vs 58% organ-specific, q=0.003). Attributed to aromatase-inhibitor selection pressure ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) — promoter mutations more frequent in papillary thyroid metastases (46% vs 69%, q=0.001) and [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md) metastases (70% vs 81%, q=0.02), but more frequent in primary [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md) (41% vs 25%, q=0.02). [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter mutations more frequent in primary tumors of metastatic papillary thyroid patients (20% vs 56%, q=0.004). TERT amplification associated with [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) CNS/brain metastasis ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) — fusions slightly more frequent in [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) metastases (3% vs 6%, q<0.001) ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — mutations more frequent in pancreatic neuroendocrine metastases (1% vs 10%, q=0.03); 12p gain (encompassing [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)) more frequent in [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md) metastases (17% vs 4%, q=0.002). MSS [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md) patients with lung metastasis had higher KRAS mutation frequency (39% vs 52%, q<0.001) ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) — mutations enriched in [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) patients with CNS/brain metastasis ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [RBM10](../genes/RBM10.md) — mutations less frequent in [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) CNS/brain metastasis ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md) — mutations negatively correlated with metastatic burden in [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md); enriched in lobular breast cancer liver metastases (3% vs 33%, q=0.02) ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [CBFB](../genes/CBFB.md) — mutations negatively correlated with metastatic burden in HR+/HER2- ductal breast; less frequent in bone metastases (5% vs 1%, q=0.009) ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [CDH1](../genes/CDH1.md) — mutations negatively correlated with metastatic burden in lobular breast cancer ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md) — mutations negatively correlated with metastatic burden in urothelial bladder cancer ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [RHOA](../genes/RHOA.md) — mutations enriched in lobular breast ovarian metastasis (3% vs 36%, q=0.02) ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [ERG](../genes/ERG.md) — fusions less frequent in [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) bone metastases (30% vs 15%, q=0.002; secondary analysis 29% vs 24%, q=0.04) ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) — mutations more frequent in [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md) lung metastases (20% vs 38%, q=0.003) ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) — mutations more frequent in bladder urothelial lung metastases (11% vs 39%, q=0.005) ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md) — mutations less frequent in primary [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) from metastatic patients (18% vs 12%, q=0.03); previously reported pattern of decreased metastatic potential ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — mutations less frequent in primaries of HR+/HER2- ductal breast metastatic patients (49% vs 38%, q=0.003); PI3K pathway alterations enriched in HR+/HER2- ductal breast bone metastases (44% vs 56%, q=0.003) ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — loss more frequent in [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) liver metastases (3% vs 10%, q<0.001) ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [APC](../genes/APC.md) — mutations more frequent in [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) liver (5% vs 11%, q=0.001) and lung (5% vs 12%, q<0.001) metastases ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) — mutations more frequent in [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) lung metastases (3% vs 8%, q=0.007); consistent with WNT pathway role in [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) lung tropism (WNT pathway alterations in 26% of lung-mets [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) vs 13%, q<0.001) ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — mutations less frequent in papillary thyroid bone metastasis patients (73% vs 53%, q=0.02) ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) — amplification more frequent in esophageal cancer patients with lung metastasis (16% vs 37%, q<0.001) ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [MAP3K1](../genes/MAP3K1.md) — mutations less frequent in HR+/HER2- ductal breast brain-metastasis patients (9% vs 3%, q=0.02); proposed surrogate for less-aggressive luminal A subtype ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [SRC](../genes/SRC.md) — amplification less frequent in MSS [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md) lung metastases (7% vs 3%, q<0.001) ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).

## Clinical implications

- **Hormone-therapy resistance signatures are written into metastatic genomes.** The strongest primary-vs-metastatic enrichments were [AR](../genes/AR.md) amplifications/mutations in [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) and [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) mutations in HR+/HER2- breast cancer — both consistent with positive selection under androgen-deprivation and aromatase-inhibitor therapy respectively ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- **Chromosomal instability is a candidate therapeutic vulnerability** in lineages where it tracks with metastatic burden ([PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md), [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md), HR+/HER2+ ductal breast). The authors propose drugs targeting CIN as an attractive strategy in these tumor types ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- **Organotropism-genotype associations generate biomarker hypotheses**: e.g., [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md)/[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)/TERT-amp in [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) brain mets; WNT pathway/[CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md)/[APC](../genes/APC.md) in [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) lung mets (with tankyrase-inhibition vulnerability cited from Leibold et al. 2020); [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) in bladder lung mets; [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) amp in esophageal lung mets ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- **Metastatic burden is itself a prognostic axis**: higher burden tracked with shorter overall survival in 78% of tumor types studied ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- **TMB-high frequency rises with metastatic progression** in [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) and breast cancer, potentially affecting checkpoint-inhibitor eligibility downstream ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) fusions slightly enriched in [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) metastases — implication noted for ALK-inhibitor biomarker testing on metastatic biopsies ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Sample-size imbalance across 50 tumor types** prevents robust conclusions in rarer cancers; non-significant differences may reflect lack of statistical power ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- **Tumor-type definitions are coarse** — e.g., [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) histologic subtypes are not resolved; refinement would further reduce per-group sample size ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- **ICD-billing-code-derived metastatic events** likely under-capture events and are subject to inter-physician variability; NLP/ML on EHR text is an obvious next step ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- **Targeted-panel limitation**: [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/IMPACT468.md) covers 341–468 genes; whole-exome or whole-genome sequencing might surface additional signals (e.g., structural variants, non-coding drivers) ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- **Single representative sample per patient** — longitudinal/multi-site sampling needed to investigate timing of genomic events and inter-metastasis heterogeneity ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- **No integration of treatment history, microenvironment, or systemic factors** at the per-event level; precludes causal claims about drug-driven vs lineage-intrinsic enrichment for many alterations ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
- **Open question on CIN directionality**: in tumor types where copy-number patterns appear set early (e.g., [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md), [HGSOC](../cancer_types/HGSOC.md)), CIN does not stratify metastatic burden — when and how this differs mechanistically from CIN-burden-coupled lineages remains unresolved ([PMID:35120664](../papers/35120664.md)).
