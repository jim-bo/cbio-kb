**PMID:** 31564440 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.cmet.2019.08.024 · **Journal:** Cell Metabolism (2020)

## TL;DR

Granlund, Tee, and colleagues at Memorial Sloan Kettering imaged 12 men with biopsy-proven prostate cancer using hyperpolarized [1-13C] pyruvate MRI before radical prostatectomy (17 total injections, including 5 patients re-imaged for test/re-test). The maximum lactate ratio (Lac_max) was significantly higher in tumor regions than in normal prostate (p=0.0001 for Gleason 3, p<0.0001 for Gleason ≥4) and rose with Gleason grade. Immunohistochemistry plus TCGA RNA-seq pinned the upregulated monocarboxylate transporter 1 (MCT1; HUGO [SLC16A1](../genes/SLC16A1.md)) as the rate-limiting driver of the lactate signal. Targeted [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) sequencing of the same patients revealed that homozygous [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) loss tracked with the highest Lac_max (p=0.059), suggesting HP-MRI could serve as a non-invasive readout of PI3K-pathway activation in [prostate adenocarcinoma](../cancer_types/PRAD.md).

## Cohort & data

- **12 patients** (mean age 62 ± 7 yrs, range 51–72) with histologically confirmed [prostate adenocarcinoma](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) (>1 cm) scheduled for radical prostatectomy at MSKCC; enrolled May 2015–July 2018 under IRB#14–205 (NCT02421380).
- **17 hyperpolarized [1-13C] pyruvate injections**: 5 patients were imaged twice (4 same-day, 1 hr apart; Patient 1 imaged 3 weeks apart). Patient 3 opted for brachytherapy and was excluded from imaging–tissue comparisons.
- **Imaging assay**: 3T wide-bore scanner (GE MR750w) with dual-tuned ¹H/¹³C endorectal coil + clamshell ¹³C transmit coil; 2D dynamic echo-planar spectroscopic imaging (EPSI) at 1×1×1.5-cm³ voxels, 4.9-s temporal resolution. Pyruvate prep: 14.2 M [1-13C] pyruvate with 15 mM trityl radical, dissolved, neutralized with Tris/NaOH, terminally sterilized; 0.43 mL/kg of 250 mM dose IV. See [hyperpolarized-13c-mri](../methods/hyperpolarized-13c-mri.md).
- **Pathology**: whole-mount step-section prostatectomies (H&E + IHC) read by a genitourinary pathologist; MCT1 IHC at 1:100 (Santa Cruz). See [immunohistochemistry](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md).
- **Genomics**: targeted DNA sequencing via the [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) prospective clinical platform (468-gene [IMPACT468](../methods/IMPACT468.md) hybridization-capture panel, Cheng et al. 2015). Allele-specific copy number via [FACETS](../methods/facets.md) run sequentially (purity at critical value 100, then sensitivity at 50). Raw IMPACT data deposited at EVA accession PRJEB33969 and at cBioPortal as [prad_msk_2019](../datasets/prad_msk_2019.md).
- **External RNA-seq**: TCGA [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) (`limma voom` differential expression on RSEM-normalized Firebrowse data) — [prad_tcga](../datasets/prad_tcga.md). See [rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md).

## Key findings

- **HP pyruvate delivery is reproducible across patients when corrected for arterial input.** Time-to-max pyruvate in the gland ranged 11.2–33.5 s (21.8±5.4 s) post-injection but collapsed to 6.2–12.4 s (10.1±2.4 s) when corrected to the internal pudendal artery input; corrected delivery showed a bimodal distribution (6.7±0.4 s and 11.6±0.6 s), separation matching the 4.9-s temporal resolution of the EPSI sequence ([PMID:31564440](../papers/31564440.md)).
- **Test/re-test is stable.** Across 5 patients imaged twice, neither time-to-max pyruvate nor time-to-max lactate differed significantly between injections after perfusion correction (p=0.24 and p=0.78); Lac_max also did not differ between paired injections (p=0.2) ([PMID:31564440](../papers/31564440.md)).
- **Lac_max rises with Gleason grade.** Lac_max was significantly lower in normal prostate than in cancer regions (p=0.0001 for primary Gleason grade 3, p<0.0001 for primary Gleason ≥4) ([PMID:31564440](../papers/31564440.md)).
- **Lactate signal correlates poorly with conventional clinical markers**, with PSA the strongest (R²=0.26) and spatial measurements (prostate volume, tumor volume, max tumor dimension) all R²<0.20 — arguing that metabolism, not bulk disease, drives the lactate signal ([PMID:31564440](../papers/31564440.md)).
- **MCT1 ([SLC16A1](../genes/SLC16A1.md)) explains the lactate signal.** In TCGA PRAD bulk RNA-seq, [SLC16A1](../genes/SLC16A1.md) and [LDHA](../genes/LDHA.md) were significantly upregulated in tumor vs. matched normal. By IHC, MCT1 stained strongly in cancer regions across all patients; ROIs with >30% positive MCT1 staining had Lac_max 60% higher than low-MCT1 ROIs (0.38 ± 0.3 vs. 0.23 ± 0.04, P=0.028) ([PMID:31564440](../papers/31564440.md)).
- **Homozygous [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) loss tracks with the highest Lac_max.** [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) sequencing identified n=2 ROIs with homozygous [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) loss and n=3 with heterozygous loss; the association between PTEN loss and Lac_max approached significance (P=0.059) ([PMID:31564440](../papers/31564440.md)).
- **[TMPRSS2](../genes/TMPRSS2.md)–[ERG](../genes/ERG.md) fusion did not segregate Lac_max.** A subset of patients carried TMPRSS2-ERG gene fusions; Lac_max in fusion-positive ROIs was not elevated relative to non-fusion ROIs ([PMID:31564440](../papers/31564440.md)).
- **HP-MRI can light up lesions missed by anatomic MRI.** Supplementary Figure S1F shows an anterior apical lesion not clearly visible on T2-weighted or diffusion-weighted images but co-localized with HP lactate signal at the pathology-confirmed tumor site ([PMID:31564440](../papers/31564440.md)).

## Genes & alterations

- **[PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md)** — copy-number loss (homozygous in 2 ROIs, heterozygous in 3 ROIs) called from [IMPACT468](../methods/IMPACT468.md) panel via [FACETS](../methods/facets.md); homozygous loss tracked with the highest HP lactate (P=0.059). Authors invoke PI3K-pathway de-repression as the mechanistic link to increased glycolytic flux.
- **[SLC16A1](../genes/SLC16A1.md) (MCT1)** — significantly upregulated at the RNA level in [prad_tcga](../datasets/prad_tcga.md) tumors vs. matched normals; IHC-positive in cancer regions across all 12 patients; identified as the rate-limiting transporter for HP pyruvate uptake and the proximate driver of the lactate signal in vivo.
- **[LDHA](../genes/LDHA.md)** — also significantly upregulated in [prad_tcga](../datasets/prad_tcga.md) tumor vs. normal; catalyzes the forward pyruvate→lactate reaction whose flux HP-MRI measures.
- **[SLC16A3](../genes/SLC16A3.md) (MCT4)** — discussed as the alternate monocarboxylate transporter candidate but not the primary driver in this cohort; MCT1 dominated by IHC.
- **[TMPRSS2](../genes/TMPRSS2.md)–[ERG](../genes/ERG.md) gene fusion** — present in a subset of patients (called from [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md)); did not correlate with HP lactate signal in this 12-patient cohort, though authors note recent lung-cancer work suggesting the fusion can regulate lactate export.

## Clinical implications

- **Imaging biomarker for tumor aggressiveness.** HP lactate ratio (Lac_max) tracks Gleason grade and is reproducible within patients, supporting its development as a non-invasive readout for active surveillance vs. definitive-treatment decisions in localized [prostate adenocarcinoma](../cancer_types/PRAD.md).
- **Companion biomarker for MCT1-targeted therapy.** Because MCT1 expression is the proximate driver of HP lactate signal, HP-MRI could pre-select patients likely to respond to MCT1 inhibitors and provide an early pharmacodynamic readout.
- **Functional readout of PI3K-pathway activation.** Homozygous [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) loss — a known driver of PI3K-pathway de-repression and aggressive disease — tracked with the highest HP lactate, suggesting HP-MRI could stratify patients for PI3K/AKT/mTOR-targeted regimens without an upfront biopsy.
- **Treatment-response monitoring.** Test/re-test stability (no significant change at 1 hr) and prior case-report evidence of HP lactate dropping after androgen deprivation therapy (Aggarwal et al., 2017) support using HP-MRI to monitor metabolic response on a sub-week timescale, faster than tumor-volume readouts.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Small cohort (n=12 patients, 17 injections).** Authors explicitly call out the patient population as limiting subtlety of detectable metabolic differences and call for large-cohort studies analogous to PET trials.
- **Spatial resolution (1×1×1.5-cm³ voxels) limits intratumoral heterogeneity analysis** and causes partial-volume contamination when matching to whole-mount pathology; authors expect the Lac_max–Gleason correlation to strengthen with higher-resolution acquisitions.
- **PTEN–Lac_max association is borderline (P=0.059).** Confirming the mechanistic link, and disentangling it from MCT1 expression, will require a larger cohort with paired [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) and HP-MRI data.
- **TMPRSS2-ERG vs. lactate result is underpowered** and contradicts emerging lung-cancer literature on [ERG](../genes/ERG.md) regulating lactate export; whether prostate ERG fusions modulate glycolytic flux remains open.
- **Temporal resolution (4.9 s) limits arterial-input correction**; the bimodal time-to-max distribution is an artifact of this sampling, motivating bolus-tracked or non-localized scout acquisitions.
- **Only one bicarbonate detection (1/12 patients)** — pyruvate dehydrogenase flux could not be evaluated across the cohort.
- **No comparator imaging modality** (e.g., FDG-PET, multi-parametric MRI quantitative metrics) was prospectively co-acquired to benchmark added value.
