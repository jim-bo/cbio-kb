# CCDC170

## Overview

CCDC170 (Coiled-Coil Domain Containing 170) is a gene involved in fusion events with [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) (Estrogen Receptor 1). The ESR1-CCDC170 fusion has been identified in cancer cell lines as a fusion-driven dependency that confers dependence on ESR1, making it a potential therapeutic target in hormone-receptor-driven cancers.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- ESR1-CCDC170 fusion identified in cancer cell lines as a fusion-driven dependency conferring dependence on ESR1; detected via integrated genomic and RNA analysis of CCLE/GDSC cell lines [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Breast cancer (inferred from ESR1-driven dependency context): ESR1-CCDC170 fusion creates ESR1 dependence relevant to estrogen-receptor-positive subtypes [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- ESR1-CCDC170 fusion links this gene to ESR1 dependency biology [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- ESR1-CCDC170 fusion-bearing lines show dependence on ESR1, suggesting potential vulnerability to estrogen receptor antagonists or degraders [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)

## Open questions

- Frequency of ESR1-CCDC170 fusions in primary tumors vs cancer cell lines remains to be established in large clinical cohorts.
