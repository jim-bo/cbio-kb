# NCOR2

## Overview

NCOR2 (Nuclear Receptor Corepressor 2, also known as SMRT) encodes a transcriptional corepressor that interacts with nuclear receptors and represses transcription. Recurrent alterations in NCOR2 have been identified in fusion-positive rhabdomyosarcoma (FP-RMS).

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Recurrent alterations in fusion-positive rhabdomyosarcoma (FP-RMS) identified in a sequential multi-platform genomic study of 35 tumor pairs from 27 patients [PMID:37730754](../papers/37730754.md).
- Recurrently deleted in prostate cancer identified in integrative genomic profiling of 218 tumors [PMID:20579941](../papers/20579941.md)
- Nonsense mutation identified in SHH-subgroup medulloblastoma in WES of 92 tumors (Broad cohort); component of the nuclear co-repressor complex [PMID:22820256](../papers/22820256.md)
- In mCRPC, NCOR2 was identified as an [AR](../genes/AR.md) pathway regulator recurrently altered in the 150-patient SU2C–PCF prospective cohort. [PMID:26000489](../papers/26000489.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Fusion-positive rhabdomyosarcoma (FP-RMS) — recurrent secondary alterations alongside [IGF2](../genes/IGF2.md) and [MED12](../genes/MED12.md) [PMID:37730754](../papers/37730754.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with [IGF2](../genes/IGF2.md) and [MED12](../genes/MED12.md) recurrent alterations in FP-RMS [PMID:37730754](../papers/37730754.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic link reported in the corpus.

## Open questions

- The functional significance of NCOR2 alterations in FP-RMS progression and their relationship to the PAX::[FOXO1](../genes/FOXO1.md) fusion-driven transcriptional program is not characterized.
