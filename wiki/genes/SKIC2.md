# SKIC2

## Overview

SKIC2 (formerly SKIV2L) encodes an RNA helicase subunit of the SKI cytoplasmic ribosome-associated RNA-surveillance (RRQC) complex. The SKI complex mediates the 3'-to-5' degradation of aberrant mRNAs through the exosome and is essential for ribosome-associated quality control (RQC). In cancer, SKIC2 has been identified as a protein whose abundance predicts synthetic lethal sensitivity in microsatellite-instable (MSI) tumors — specifically, [RPL22L1](../genes/RPL22L1.md) knockdown sensitivity and [WRN](../genes/WRN.md) helicase dependency are linked to SKIC2 protein levels.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- SKIC2 (SKIV2L) and [SKIC3](../genes/SKIC3.md) (TTC37) form the SKI cytoplasmic ribosome-associated RNA-surveillance complex; both proteins are downregulated in MSI CCLE cell lines and are associated with WRN knockdown sensitivity in MSI contexts; RPL22L1 knockdown sensitivity is best predicted by SKIC2 protein abundance [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Protein downregulation co-occurs with MSI phenotype, MLH1/PMS2 loss, and [RPL22](../genes/RPL22.md) mutation in MSI cell lines [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- SKIC2 protein abundance may serve as a biomarker for WRN-directed therapy eligibility in MSI tumors, complementing DNA- and RNA-based MSI testing [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Open questions

- The mechanistic basis by which SKI complex downregulation connects to MSI-associated ribosome dysfunction and WRN synthetic lethality remains unresolved [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
