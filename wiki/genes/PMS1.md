# PMS1

## Overview

PMS1 (PMS1 Homolog 1, Mismatch Repair System Component) is a DNA mismatch repair (MMR) gene encoding a subunit of the MutL heterodimer complex. Germline pathogenic variants in PMS1 are associated with Lynch syndrome and predisposition to mismatch repair-deficient (MMRd) cancers. Somatic MMR gene mutations drive therapy-induced hypermutation in tumors previously treated with alkylating agents.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- PMS1 is among MMR germline genes (with [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md), [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md)) where germline pathogenic/likely-pathogenic alleles were identified in a prospective glioma cohort; two cases with biallelic MMR loss exhibited Lynch-pattern MSI hypermutation; somatic MMR mutations were observed in 31/38 (82%) hypermutated recurrences [PMID:31263031](../papers/31263031.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Glioma: PMS1 germline alleles identified in adult glioma patients; biallelic MMR loss leads to Lynch-pattern MSI hypermutation at recurrence [PMID:31263031](../papers/31263031.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with MSH2, MSH6, PMS2 germline variants in MMR-deficient cancer contexts [PMID:31263031](../papers/31263031.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- MMR-deficient tumors with biallelic PMS1 loss may be candidates for immune checkpoint blockade given their hypermutated phenotype [PMID:31263031](../papers/31263031.md)

## Open questions

- Whether PMS1 germline variants are enriched in glioma relative to other tumor types requires larger prospective cohorts.
