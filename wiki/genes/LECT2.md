# LECT2

## Overview

LECT2 (Leukocyte Cell Derived Chemotaxin 2) is a secreted hepatokine with roles in neutrophil chemotaxis, immune regulation, and metabolism. It has been studied primarily in liver biology and liver cancer, where it can act as a tumor suppressor. Its upregulation in pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDAC) represents a newly reported observation, nominating it as a candidate early-detection biomarker.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Protein upregulated >2-fold in PDAC vs normal adjacent tissue and vs normal ductal tissue in the CPTAC proteogenomic cohort (n=140 treatment-naive tumors); elevated even in early-stage tumors. Authors report this as the first identification of elevated LECT2 in PDAC. Listed among 12 secreted proteins nominated as candidate serum/pancreatic-juice early-detection biomarkers. Validated by orthogonal DIA mass spectrometry. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md):** Protein consistently upregulated in treatment-naive PDAC including early-stage tumors; secreted nature supports liquid biopsy investigation. [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Upregulation co-occurs with other PDAC-enriched secreted proteins including [THBS2](../genes/THBS2.md), LAMC2, [LOXL2](../genes/LOXL2.md), and [GSDMB](../genes/GSDMB.md). [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting described in the corpus. Candidate for serum-based early-detection assay development.

## Open questions

- Source of LECT2 upregulation in PDAC (tumor cells vs stromal compartment) not established.
- Specificity of LECT2 elevation for PDAC vs other gastrointestinal cancers or liver disease not yet assessed.
