# CCN1

## Overview

CCN1 (Cellular Communication Network Factor 1; formerly CYR61, Cysteine-Rich Angiogenic Inducer 61) is a matricellular protein and canonical transcriptional target of the YAP1/WWTR1 (YAP/TAZ) co-activators acting through TEAD transcription factors. It mediates cell adhesion, migration, and angiogenesis. In prostate cancer, CCN1 expression marks YAP/TAZ-active states and is implicated in lineage plasticity.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Canonical YAP/TAZ transcriptional target; expression downregulated upon YAP1/WWTR1 double knockdown in CRPC-SCL cell lines (MSKPCa3 and DU145), supporting its role as a YAP/TAZ effector in lineage-plastic castration-resistant prostate cancer ([prad_organoids_msk_2022](../datasets/prad_organoids_msk_2022.md)) [PMID:35617398](../papers/35617398.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md): CCN1 is downregulated upon YAP/TAZ knockdown in CRPC-SCL models, implicating YAP/TAZ→CCN1 signaling in the stem-like lineage-plastic subtype of castration-resistant prostate cancer [PMID:35617398](../papers/35617398.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-expressed with [CCN2](../genes/CCN2.md), [AXL](../genes/AXL.md), [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md), [AJUBA](../genes/AJUBA.md), and [ANKRD1](../genes/ANKRD1.md) as part of the canonical YAP/TAZ target gene program in CRPC-SCL [PMID:35617398](../papers/35617398.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- YAP1/WWTR1 double knockdown selectively suppresses CRPC-SCL cell growth and downregulates CCN1 alongside other YAP/TAZ targets, suggesting that YAP/TAZ inhibition may have utility in CCN1-expressing lineage-plastic prostate cancer [PMID:35617398](../papers/35617398.md)

## Open questions

- Whether CCN1 expression level is a useful biomarker for identifying YAP/TAZ-active CRPC-SCL tumors amenable to YAP/TAZ-directed therapy has not been prospectively tested [PMID:35617398](../papers/35617398.md)
