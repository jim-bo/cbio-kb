# KCNQ5

## Overview

KCNQ5 encodes a voltage-gated potassium channel subunit. In cancer genomics, KCNQ5 has been identified as a cis-regulatory target of non-coding driver mutations at [CTCF](../genes/CTCF.md) binding sites (CBSs) in gastric and colorectal cancers, where CBS hotspot mutations at chr6:73.12 Mb are associated with reduced KCNQ5 expression and enrichment in the chromosomal instability (CIN) subtype.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- KCNQ5 identified as cis-target of a CTCF binding site (CBS) hotspot mutation at chr6:73.12 Mb in gastric adenocarcinoma (212 whole genomes across TCGA, ICGC, Hong Kong, and Singapore cohorts); downregulated in CBS-mutated tumors (P=0.0059, adj 0.047); downregulated in GC vs normal (Wilcoxon P=3.2×10⁻⁷); CTCF knockdown previously shown to downregulate KCNQ5. [PMID:29670109](../papers/29670109.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md): KCNQ5 expression downregulated in gastric adenocarcinoma vs normal tissue; linked to CBS hotspot mutations in CIN-subtype tumors (25% of gastric cancers harbor CBS mutations). [PMID:29670109](../papers/29670109.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- CBS hotspot mutations affecting the KCNQ5 regulatory region are enriched in CIN-subtype gastric tumors and co-occur with nearby somatic copy number alteration (SCNA) breakpoints. [PMID:29670109](../papers/29670109.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting of KCNQ5 described in current corpus. KCNQ5 downregulation via CBS disruption is proposed as a candidate non-coding driver mechanism.

## Open questions

- Functional validation of the CBS hotspot–KCNQ5 expression link is lacking (no paired RNA-seq for all mutated samples); the downstream consequences of KCNQ5 loss in gastric cancer biology remain unexplored. [PMID:29670109](../papers/29670109.md)
