# A Novel Approach to Quantify Heterogeneity of Intrahepatic Cholangiocarcinoma: the Hidden-Genome Classifier

**PMID:** 38864854 · **DOI:** 10.1158/1078-0432.CCR-24-0657 · **Journal:** Clinical Cancer Research (2024)

## TL;DR

Song et al. applied a supervised machine-learning "hidden-genome" classifier to 527 intrahepatic cholangiocarcinomas (IHC) sequenced by MSK-IMPACT, scoring each tumor's genetic similarity to extrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma/gallbladder cancer (EHC/GBC) versus hepatocellular carcinoma ([HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)). IHC genetics form a spectrum: "biliary-class" IHC (>90% EHC/GBC homology, 23%) is KRAS/SMAD4/CDKN2A-driven and has markedly worse overall survival than "HCC-class" IHC (>90% [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) homology, 5.7%, TERT-driven), and the classifier predicts [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) independently of [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) and [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) alterations and outperforms histologic subtyping [PMID:38864854](../papers/38864854.md).

## Cohort & data

- 1,370 patients with histologically confirmed biliary tract cancer, [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md), or biphenotypic IHC/HCC tumors at MSK, sequenced 2003–2022 on [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) (panels 341/410/468/505; 341 common genes used).
- 527 [IHCH](../cancer_types/IHCH.md) classified; trained against [EHCH](../cancer_types/EHCH.md), [GBC](../cancer_types/GBC.md), and [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) reference classes.
- Dataset: [hcc_msk_2024](../datasets/hcc_msk_2024.md).

## Key findings

- 410 IHC (78%) had >50% genetic homology with EHC/GBC; 122 (23%) exceeded >90% homology and were labeled "biliary-class" [PMID:38864854](../papers/38864854.md).
- 117 IHC (22%) had >50% genetic homology with HCC; 30 (5.7%) exceeded >90% homology and were labeled "HCC-class" [PMID:38864854](../papers/38864854.md).
- Biliary-class IHC was characterized by alterations in [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md), and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) loss; HCC-class was characterized by [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) alterations [PMID:38864854](../papers/38864854.md).
- Median [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md), unresectable disease: biliary- vs. non-biliary-class = 1.0 year (95% CI 0.77–1.5) vs. 1.8 years (95% CI 1.6–2.0) [PMID:38864854](../papers/38864854.md).
- Median OS, resectable disease: biliary- vs. non-biliary-class = 2.4 years (95% CI 2.1–NR) vs. 5.1 years (95% CI 4.8–6.9) [PMID:38864854](../papers/38864854.md).
- Large-duct IHC (n=28) was almost exclusively biliary-class (n=27); HCC-class tumors were mostly small-duct IHC (64%, p=0.02) [PMID:38864854](../papers/38864854.md).
- The hidden-genome classifier predicted OS independently of [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) and [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) alterations; histologic subtype did not predict OS [PMID:38864854](../papers/38864854.md).

## Genes & alterations

- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — enriched in biliary-class IHC; discriminative of EHC/GBC-like genetics.
- [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) — enriched in biliary-class IHC.
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — loss enriched in biliary-class IHC.
- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) — alterations characterize HCC-class IHC.
- [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) — actionable driver; classifier adds prognostic information independent of [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) status.
- [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) — actionable driver; classifier prognostic independent of [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) status.

## Clinical implications

- The hidden-genome classifier stratifies IHC into prognostic subgroups (biliary- vs. HCC-class) with large OS differences in both resectable and unresectable disease, and does so independently of the established FGFR2/IDH1 targetable alterations [PMID:38864854](../papers/38864854.md).
- Authors propose using the classifier to stratify patients in future IHC clinical trials and to help explain heterogeneous treatment responses in IHC [PMID:38864854](../papers/38864854.md).

## Limitations & open questions

- Single-institution retrospective MSK cohort; restricted to the 341 genes common across MSK-IMPACT panel versions.
- Classifier was trained on EHC/GBC vs. HCC reference classes only; intermediate/mixed IHCs (roughly half the cohort) are not cleanly captured by the >90% homology thresholds.
- Prospective validation and linkage to specific therapy responses (e.g., gemcitabine/cisplatin, FGFR/IDH1 inhibitors, immunotherapy) remain open.
