**PMID:** 39185963 · **DOI:** 10.1158/2159-8290.CD-24-0286 · **Journal:** Cancer Discovery (2025)

## TL;DR

Rekhtman and colleagues at MSKCC define "atypical [SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md)" (aSCLC) — a rare subset of small cell lung carcinoma in never/light smokers that lacks dual [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) inactivation. Using integrated MSK-IMPACT targeted NGS, WGS, RNA-seq, FISH, and IHC across 20 patients and 49 pathologic specimens, they show that 84% of non-MSI aSCLC harbor extensive chromothripsis — predominantly on chromosomes 11 and 12 — producing ecDNA amplification of [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) or co-amplification of [CCND2](../genes/CCND2.md)/[CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md)/[MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md). aSCLC shows histogenetic links to pulmonary carcinoids (carcinoid-to-SCLC plasticity), contrasting with [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md)/APOBEC-driven adenocarcinoma-to-SCLC plasticity in [RB1](../genes/RB1.md)−/[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)− never-smoker [SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md).

## Cohort & data

- **Discovery cohort:** 20 patients with aSCLC (3% of 600 consecutive de novo [SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md) patients sequenced by [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) at MSKCC). All never/light smokers (≤10 pack-years).
- **Comparator cohorts on the same platform:** smoking-associated SCLC (sSCLC, n = 206); never-smoker [RB1](../genes/RB1.md)−/[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)− SCLC (nsSCLC-RB1−/TP53−, n = 18); pulmonary carcinoids ([LNET](../cancer_types/LNET.md), n = 157); atypical carcinoids (n = 50).
- **Specimens:** 49 pathologic specimens (1–7 per patient); 31 profiled by [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) tNGS, 12 by [WGS](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) (11 patients), 7 by [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md).
- **Dataset:** [asclc_msk_2024](../datasets/asclc_msk_2024.md) on cBioPortal; raw WGS/RNA-seq in dbGAP phs003676.v1.p1.
- **Assays/methods:** [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) (341/468/505-gene versions); WGS on NovaSeq 6000 (~100× tumor / ~80× normal); FACETS for CN calling; [ShatterSeek](../methods/shatterseek.md) for chromothripsis; [MSIsensor](../methods/msisensor.md)/[MiMSI](../methods/mimsi.md) for MSI; [OncoKB](../methods/oncokb-annotation.md) for driver annotation; FISH (3-color CCND1/CCND2/CDK4/MDM2); MutationTimeR for clonal timing.

## Key findings

- **aSCLC is rare and demographically distinct.** 20 of 600 (3%) [SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md) cases were [RB1](../genes/RB1.md)+/TP53+; all were never or light (≤10 pack-year) smokers. Mean age 53 vs 67 years for other SCLC (P < 0.0001); youngest was 19 years old.
- **Lower-end Ki67 but classic SCLC morphology.** Mean Ki67 69% in aSCLC vs 87% in other SCLC (P < 0.0001). 5/20 patients had carcinoid histotype in primary/intrathoracic samples alongside SCLC histotype in metastases.
- **Low TMB, no smoking signature.** Most aSCLC had TMB < 2 Mut/Mb; outliers were temozolomide-treated case A07 and the single MSI-H case A20 (somatic [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md) homozygous deletion + dual MLH1/PMS2 IHC loss). Smoking signature absent; dominant signatures reflected systemic therapy (e.g., [temozolomide](../drugs/temozolomide.md)).
- **Carcinoid-like mutation spectrum.** Recurrent SNVs in [MEN1](../genes/MEN1.md) (15%), [EIF1AX](../genes/EIF1AX.md) (10%), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) (25%), and [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) (30%). [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) mutation rate enriched vs carcinoids (8%, P = 0.008) and conventional SCLC (3%, P = 0.0003).
- **Recurrent, mutually exclusive oncogene amplifications.** [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) amplified in 30%; [CCND2](../genes/CCND2.md)/[CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md)/±[MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) co-amplified in 15%; [MYCL](../genes/MYCL.md) amplified in 10%. CN often >10 copies, with case A02 harboring 125 copies of [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md). [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) amplification was found in 0% of conventional SCLC (P = 0.0006) and 1% of carcinoids (P = 0.01).
- **Chromothripsis is the hallmark.** 16/19 (84%) non-MSI aSCLC showed chromothripsis by combined WGS+tNGS. Chromosome 11 (35% of cases) was most commonly affected, always co-occurring with chromothripsis on chromosome 3; chromosome 12 (20%) and chromosome 1 (15%) followed. Average 565 SVs per case (range up to >1,900).
- **Chromothripsis localizes oncogene amplifications.** Chr 11 chromothripsis in 5/6 [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md)-amplified cases; chr 12 in 3/3 [CCND2](../genes/CCND2.md)/[CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md)/±[MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) cases; chr 1 in 2/2 [MYCL](../genes/MYCL.md)-amplified cases.
- **ecDNA mechanism confirmed by FISH.** All 5 FISH-tested cases showed extrachromosomal (double-minute) amplification of [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) or CCND2/CDK4/MDM2; one case had both extra- and intrachromosomal amplification.
- **Enhancer hijacking as alternative oncogene activation.** Case A17 lacked [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) amplification but harbored a chromothripsis-derived rearrangement juxtaposing the active chromatin of [SH3RF3](../genes/SH3RF3.md) (chr 2) upstream of [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) (chr 11), with marked cyclin D1 overexpression by IHC and RNA-seq.
- **Novel tNGS-based chromothripsis detection.** Manual detection from oscillating CNA patterns on FACETS hisens plots achieved 100% specificity and 77% sensitivity vs WGS gold standard (minimum 5 consecutive oscillating segments, CCF ≥50%).
- **Chromothripsis is early and clonally stable.** MutationTimeR placed all chromothripsis-associated amplifications early in tumorigenesis. Multisample analysis across 14 patients showed conservation of chromothriptic chromosomes and amplifications across primary and metastatic specimens, independent of intervening systemic therapy.
- **Cell-cycle pathway deregulation in 75% of samples.** pRb pathway deregulation at DNA, mRNA, or protein level in 75% of samples; aSCLC showed major upregulation of G2M-checkpoint and E2F-target signatures by RNA-seq exceeding carcinoids and approaching sSCLC.
- **Paradoxical p16 expression in chr 12 chromothripsis cases.** All chr 12 chromothripsis cases (A07, A08, A09) were p16-high despite wild-type pRb — likely reflecting posttranslational pRb inhibition by ecDNA-amplified [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) creating a pRb-null–like state.
- **Acquired subclonal [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) alterations in some metastases.** Two patients (A16, A20) acquired private [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) mutations in metastatic samples with concurrent pRb IHC loss; one primary (A12) showed a subclonal RB1 mutation (CCF 71%) with regional pRb loss.
- **Histogenetic link to carcinoids.** 55% of aSCLC had carcinoid features by histology, [OTP](../genes/OTP.md) IHC/RNA expression, or carcinoid-characteristic mutations ([MEN1](../genes/MEN1.md), [EIF1AX](../genes/EIF1AX.md)). aSCLC was uniformly [ASCL1](../genes/ASCL1.md)-dominant; [YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md) was absent in aSCLC but enriched in nsSCLC-RB1−/TP53−.
- **Two distinct pathways of plasticity-mediated SCLC pathogenesis in never-smokers.** (1) Carcinoid-to-SCLC via chromothripsis in RB1+/TP53+ background (aSCLC), and (2) adenocarcinoma-to-SCLC via [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) (39%) or [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) (6%) mutations plus APOBEC mutagenesis in RB1−/TP53− background.
- **Distinct survival.** Median overall survival in aSCLC was 58 months — intermediate between sSCLC (16 months), nsSCLC-RB1−/TP53− (22 months), and atypical carcinoids (184 months).
- **Reduced platinum sensitivity.** Among 15 evaluable patients, platinum-based therapy yielded 13% CR and 20% PR — well below the historical ~70% response rate for conventional SCLC.
- **Suggested benefit from immunotherapy and TMZ.** 3/5 patients treated with immune checkpoint inhibitors remained on therapy 2 to >5 years; 4/6 [temozolomide](../drugs/temozolomide.md)-treated patients had >10 months on treatment (up to 2 years). Long TMZ-responders (A01, A07) had lowest [MGMT](../genes/MGMT.md) expression by RNA-seq.

## Genes & alterations

- **[RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)** — proficient at baseline in all aSCLC by integrated genomic + IHC analysis; defines the subset. Acquired subclonal [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) mutations seen in some metastatic samples (A12, A16, A20) without overt proliferation escalation.
- **[CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) (11q13)** — amplified in 30% of aSCLC, typically as ecDNA double-minutes from chr 11 chromothripsis; up to 125 copies. Two cases (A11, A17) overexpressed [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) without amplification — A17 via SH3RF3-CCND1 enhancer hijacking.
- **[CCND2](../genes/CCND2.md) (12q13), [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) (12q14), [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) (12q15)** — co-amplified in 15% via chr 12 chromothripsis-derived ecDNA. May functionally phenocopy [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) + [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) inactivation.
- **[MYCL](../genes/MYCL.md) (1p34)** — amplified in 10% via chr 1 chromothripsis.
- **[HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), [ERBB3](../genes/ERBB3.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)** — additional amplifications co-localized to chromothriptic chromosomes (11p15, 12q13, 12q12 respectively).
- **[MEN1](../genes/MEN1.md), [EIF1AX](../genes/EIF1AX.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md)** — carcinoid-characteristic SNVs/deletions; [MEN1](../genes/MEN1.md) (11q13) and [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) (1p36) deletions co-localize with chromothriptic chromosomes 11 and 1.
- **[ATM](../genes/ATM.md)** — somatic mutations in 30% (enriched vs carcinoids and SCLC); no pathogenic germline [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) variants identified. Authors hypothesize [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) disabling could be permissive for chromothripsis via micronucleus formation.
- **[TGFBR2](../genes/TGFBR2.md) (3p24)** — deleted in 2/7 chr 3 chromothripsis cases; the only recurrent driver event on chromothriptic chromosome 3.
- **[CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)** — paradoxically p16-high (overexpression by IHC and RNA-seq) in all chr 12 chromothripsis cases despite wild-type pRb.
- **[MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md)/[PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md)** — somatic [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md) homozygous deletion with dual IHC loss in the single MSI-H case (A20), an alternative genomic context to chromothripsis.
- **[ASCL1](../genes/ASCL1.md), [NEUROD1](../genes/NEUROD1.md), [POU2F3](../genes/POU2F3.md), [YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md)** — all evaluated aSCLC were ASCL1-dominant; [YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md) entirely absent in aSCLC vs enriched in nsSCLC-RB1−/TP53−.
- **[DLL3](../genes/DLL3.md), [SEZ6](../genes/SEZ6.md)** — uniformly high expression in aSCLC (mean H scores 278 and 240 respectively across 9 evaluated samples); both are emerging immunotherapy and ADC targets in SCLC.
- **[OTP](../genes/OTP.md)** — carcinoid-specific marker expressed by IHC and RNA-seq in a subset of aSCLC, supporting histogenetic link to carcinoids.
- **[TERT](../genes/TERT.md)** — no [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) overexpression detected; alternative lengthening of telomeres [footprints](../methods/footprints.md) also absent.
- **[ALDH1L2](../genes/ALDH1L2.md)** — sole in-frame fusion involving a likely-oncogenic gene (folate regulator) by OncoKB across all RNA-seq cases; not recurrent. Authors conclude fusions are unlikely dominant drivers in aSCLC.
- **[SH3RF3](../genes/SH3RF3.md)** — donor of active chromatin in the case A17 SH3RF3-CCND1 enhancer-hijacking rearrangement.
- **[EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)** — canonical driver mutations found in 39% and 6% of nsSCLC-RB1−/TP53− respectively, anchoring the adenocarcinoma-to-SCLC pathway; absent in aSCLC.

## Clinical implications

- **RB1/TP53 IHC + smoking history as triage for aSCLC.** Routine IHC-based assessment of pRb and p53 can flag candidate aSCLC, with potential prognostic and therapeutic stratification implications.
- **Distinct prognosis.** aSCLC has substantially longer median [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) than smoking-related SCLC (58 vs 16 months), warranting separate clinical consideration.
- **Reduced platinum sensitivity.** Only 33% achieved CR/PR on platinum-based chemotherapy ([carboplatin](../drugs/carboplatin.md)/[cisplatin](../drugs/cisplatin.md) + [etoposide](../drugs/etoposide.md)) — well below the conventional SCLC benchmark of ~70%.
- **Potential benefit from immune checkpoint inhibitors.** 3/5 ICI-treated patients (including those receiving [atezolizumab](../drugs/atezolizumab.md)) remained on therapy 2 to >5 years.
- **[Temozolomide](../drugs/temozolomide.md) shows promise.** 4/6 TMZ-treated patients had >10 months on therapy (up to 2 years); long responders had lowest [MGMT](../genes/MGMT.md) expression. Relevant given dual histology link to SCLC and carcinoids — TMZ is active in both.
- **Emerging targets:** Uniformly high [DLL3](../genes/DLL3.md) and [SEZ6](../genes/SEZ6.md) expression suggests aSCLC patients may benefit from emerging DLL3-directed (e.g., tarlatamab class) and SEZ6-directed agents under clinical development.
- **[CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) and [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) inhibition as rationale-based options.** Recurrent [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) and [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) ecDNA amplification provides genomic rationale for [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) and [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) inhibitors, plus emerging ecDNA-targeting agents.
- **Method advance for clinical sequencing.** First demonstration that chromothripsis can be detected from panel NGS (MSK-IMPACT) using oscillating CNA pattern recognition with 100% specificity and 77% sensitivity vs WGS — enables wider clinical recognition.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Small cohort (n = 20).** The aSCLC cohort is too small for robust subgroup analyses of treatment outcomes by genomic feature.
- **tNGS sensitivity for chromothripsis is incomplete (77%).** Case A06 had [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) amplification (23 copies) without detectable chromothripsis on tNGS alone; false negatives cannot be excluded.
- **Trigger of chromothripsis remains unclear.** No pathogenic germline variants in 90 cancer-predisposition genes among 17 evaluable patients; no viral DNA detected; no [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) overexpression or alternative telomere lengthening [footprints](../methods/footprints.md).
- **Mechanism of carcinoid-to-SCLC transition.** Matched primary-metastatic samples (A05, A17) shared chromothripsis without acquiring additional drivers in the SCLC component; epigenetic reprogramming is hypothesized but not directly demonstrated.
- **Functional role of acquired subclonal [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) loss in aSCLC is unclear** — does not correlate with proliferation escalation, suggesting a noncanonical role outside E2F-mediated cell cycle control.
- **Specific target gene(s) of chr 3 chromothripsis are not established.** Only [TGFBR2](../genes/TGFBR2.md) deletion was recurrent (2/7); the broader 3p loss observed in >90% of SCLC remains mechanistically opaque.
- **Generalizability beyond never/light smokers.** A prior WGS report (George et al.) identified one chromothripsis-associated RB1+/TP53+ SCLC in a 30-pack-year smoker, suggesting tobacco exposure does not strictly preclude this pathway.
- **No prospective treatment data.** Therapeutic vulnerability claims (CDK4i, MDM2i, ecDNA-targeting agents, ICI, TMZ) are hypothesis-generating; prospective trials enriched for aSCLC are needed.
