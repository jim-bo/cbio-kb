# XDH

## Overview

XDH (Xanthine Dehydrogenase) encodes an enzyme involved in purine catabolism that catalyzes the oxidation of hypoxanthine to xanthine and xanthine to uric acid. In cancer, XDH has been implicated in metastatic progression in multiple tumor types. In adenoid cystic carcinoma, XDH carries subclonal mutations in divergent metastatic tumor subpopulations.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Subclonal XDH mutation identified in a divergent metastatic cluster (metastases 6D, 2B, 4A) during multiregion WES + ultra-deep targeted sequencing of one salivary adenoid cystic carcinoma; found co-occurring with [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md), [LTF](../genes/LTF.md), and [TMEM2](../genes/TMEM2.md) mutations in the same NMF-defined cluster — genes implicated in metastasis in other tumor types [PMID:31483290](../papers/31483290.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md)**: Subclonal XDH mutation observed in a divergent metastatic cluster of a multiregion salivary [ACC](../cancer_types/ACC.md) case; mutations were not detected in primary tumor subregions, consistent with late metastatic acquisition [PMID:31483290](../papers/31483290.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with SF3B1, LTF, and TMEM2 mutations in the same subclonal metastatic cluster of adenoid cystic carcinoma; these late subclonal events were absent from the 6 primary tumor subregions [PMID:31483290](../papers/31483290.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic relevance established from this corpus; XDH's role in purine catabolism and reactive oxygen species production may be relevant to metabolic reprogramming in metastatic ACC [PMID:31483290](../papers/31483290.md).

## Open questions

- XDH's specific functional contribution to ACC metastasis is unclear; observations come from a single multiregion case and the co-mutation pattern with SF3B1, LTF, and TMEM2 requires replication in larger cohorts [PMID:31483290](../papers/31483290.md).
