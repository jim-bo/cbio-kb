**PMID:** 38355797 · **DOI:** [10.1097/HC9.0000000000000837](https://doi.org/10.1097/HC9.0000000000000837) · **Journal:** Hepatology Communications (2025)

## TL;DR

Narrative review by Wong, Kong, and Ma summarizing how reprogrammed lipid metabolism in [hepatocellular carcinoma](../cancer_types/HCC.md) cells (enhanced lipid uptake, reactivation of de novo lipogenesis, increased fatty acid oxidation, suppression of lipid peroxidation, bioactive lipid signaling, and cholesterol biosynthesis) drives both disease progression and resistance to standard-of-care therapies — tyrosine kinase inhibitors ([sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md), [lenvatinib](../drugs/lenvatinib.md), [regorafenib](../drugs/regorafenib.md)), immune checkpoint inhibitors ([atezolizumab](../drugs/atezolizumab.md) plus [bevacizumab](../drugs/bevacizumab.md), [durvalumab](../drugs/durvalumab.md) plus [tremelimumab](../drugs/tremelimumab.md)), and radiotherapy. The authors argue that nearly 3 of 4 [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) patients fail to benefit from current SoC, and lay out the rationale and ongoing clinical trials for combining lipid-metabolism inhibitors ([FASN](../genes/FASN.md), ACC1/2, [SCD](../genes/SCD.md), CPT1, CD36, statins) and dietary interventions (calorie restriction, ketogenic, Mediterranean, ω3-PUFA) with TKIs and ICIs. Real-world response rates for current SoC are quoted at 26%–28% for [atezolizumab](../drugs/atezolizumab.md)+[bevacizumab](../drugs/bevacizumab.md) and 16%–23% for [lenvatinib](../drugs/lenvatinib.md), with median PFS <12 months; second-line ORR after [atezolizumab](../drugs/atezolizumab.md)+[bevacizumab](../drugs/bevacizumab.md) progression is ~7% [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Cohort & data

- **Article type:** Narrative review (no primary patient cohort, no new sequencing dataset).
- **Disease focus:** [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md), which accounts for 85–90% of liver cancer. Etiology emphasis on MAFLD/MASLD (49% of [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) patients carry MAFLD; ~12% have MAFLD as sole underlying liver disease), HBV, and chemically induced (DEN) mouse models [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- **Sources synthesized:** Preclinical mouse models (DEN-induced HCC, high-fat diet, transgenic Pten/Met/Akt models, Wnt/β-catenin–driven models), HCC patient transcriptomics/proteomics/lipidomics, real-world TKI and ICI response cohorts, and registered combination clinical trials (Tables 1–2 in the paper, NCT identifiers including NCT06911008 for the [SCD](../genes/SCD.md) inhibitor SSI-4/MTI-301, NCT05118776 for TVB-2640 + bevacizumab in glioblastoma, NCT06824974 for fasting-mimicking diet post locoregional therapy in HCC) [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- **Genetic context cited:** "Mixed genetic mutation landscape of HCC with very few dominant drivers (e.g., [TERT](../genes/TERT.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md))" [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Key findings

- **Six metabolic signatures of HCC lipid reprogramming.** The review classifies HCC lipid dysregulation into (1) enhanced lipid uptake, (2) reactivation of de novo lipogenesis (DNL), (3) increased fatty acid oxidation (FAO), (4) suppression of lipid peroxidation, (5) bioactive lipid biosynthesis, and (6) cholesterol biosynthesis — overexpression of multiple enzymes in each pathway is observed in HCC and linked to therapy resistance [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- **Lipid uptake and TKI/radiotherapy resistance.** [CD36](../genes/CD36.md) upregulation drives Src/AKT/mTOR and YAP signaling and is induced in irradiation-resistant HCC cells. The CD36-blocking humanized antibody PLT012 suppressed HCC in preclinical models, recruited cytotoxic progenitor-exhausted CD8 T cells, reduced [FOXP3](../genes/FOXP3.md)+ Treg, sensitized HCC to ICIs, and received FDA Orphan Drug Designation for liver/intrahepatic bile duct cancer. [SLC27A4](../genes/SLC27A4.md) (FATP4) selectively imports MUFA to protect against ferroptosis and confers [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md) resistance. [SLC27A5](../genes/SLC27A5.md) (FATP5) findings are contradictory — loss protects against high-fat-diet steatosis but also promotes HCC via KEAP1/NRF2 and AKT/mTOR. [FABP1](../genes/FABP1.md) lets HCC outcompete T cells for linoleic acid, and LINC01116/FABP1 inhibition plus anti-PD-1 markedly reduced HCC progression. [FABP5](../genes/FABP5.md) inhibitor SBFI-103 promoted lipid peroxidation, restored FAO, recruited [CD80](../genes/CD80.md)+/CD86+ macrophages and [CD44](../genes/CD44.md)+CD62L− effector T cells, and suppressed HCC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- **De novo lipogenesis and TKI resistance.** [SREBF1](../genes/SREBF1.md) is transcriptionally driven by [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) (frequently amplified in HCC) and post-transcriptionally activated by the PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway, upregulating [FASN](../genes/FASN.md), [SCD](../genes/SCD.md), and [ACACA](../genes/ACACA.md). Enhanced DNL and lipid droplets are observed in [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md)-resistant cells; phosphatidic acid generated by DNL activates RTK signaling and confers [lenvatinib](../drugs/lenvatinib.md) resistance. Cardiolipin synthesis maintains mitochondrial integrity and impairs cytochrome-c–mediated apoptosis to drive radiotherapy resistance [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- **DNL and ICI resistance — bidirectional.** [ACLY](../genes/ACLY.md) promotes DNL to lower the PUFA:SFA ratio, suppress cGAS–STING, and decrease [CD274](../genes/CD274.md) (PD-L1) expression, conferring anti-PD-L1 resistance. Conversely, [FASN](../genes/FASN.md) inhibition does not downregulate PD-L1 and instead synergizes with anti-PD-L1. Clinically, MAFLD-related HCC was reported unresponsive to ICIs in some reanalyses, while a small retrospective cohort reported intratumor steatosis as more responsive — the review flags this as an unresolved conflict [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- **Fatty acid oxidation is context-dependent.** [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md)-mutant / Wnt/β-catenin–driven HCC (>25% of patients) is addicted to FAO via PPARα and [CPT1A](../genes/CPT1A.md); etomoxir suppresses tumor development in this context. In TP53-mutant HCC and DEN+high-fat-diet models, FAO suppression is instead pro-tumor. A FAO-gene signature stratifies HCC patients: the FAO-high cluster is less responsive to anti-PD-1 (Liu et al. clustering analysis cited) [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- **Lipid peroxidation, ferroptosis, and TKI/radio resistance.** [ACSL4](../genes/ACSL4.md) ligates arachidonic acid into phospholipids and is both a ferroptosis driver and a predictive biomarker of [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md) sensitivity. HCC cells suppress ferroptosis by lowering PUFA, raising MUFA via [SCD](../genes/SCD.md), and inducing [GPX4](../genes/GPX4.md) and [SLC7A11](../genes/SLC7A11.md). FASN stabilizes HIF1α to upregulate [SLC7A11](../genes/SLC7A11.md) and resist radiotherapy and [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md). HCC cells also drive lipid peroxidation in CD8 T cells via CD36 uptake of oxidized LDL, inducing PD-1 and TIM-3 exhaustion markers; palmitate-induced [STAT3](../genes/STAT3.md) palmitoylation in CD8 T cells drives terminal exhaustion [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- **Cholesterol biosynthesis and TKI resistance.** [HMGCR](../genes/HMGCR.md), [SQLE](../genes/SQLE.md), and [MVK](../genes/MVK.md) are upregulated in HCC. [Lenvatinib](../drugs/lenvatinib.md)- and [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md)-resistant HCC cells accumulate cholesterol; remodeled lipid rafts upregulate the drug-efflux pump [ABCB1](../genes/ABCB1.md). 25-hydroxycholesterol activates Sonic Hedgehog signaling to drive [lenvatinib](../drugs/lenvatinib.md) resistance; 27-hydroxycholesterol upregulates [GPX4](../genes/GPX4.md) to resist sorafenib-induced lipid peroxidation. [LCAT](../genes/LCAT.md) is downregulated in HCC, and HDL treatment sensitized HCC to lenvatinib in preclinical models [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- **Statin epidemiology vs. trials.** Population data link statins to reduced cancer-related mortality, and lipophilic statins ([simvastatin](../drugs/simvastatin.md), [lovastatin](../drugs/lovastatin.md), [atorvastatin](../drugs/atorvastatin.md)) showed a more pronounced HCC-risk reduction than hydrophilic ([pravastatin](../drugs/pravastatin.md), rosuvastatin). However, multiple phase 2/3 trials combining [pravastatin](../drugs/pravastatin.md) with [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md) in advanced HCC failed to improve survival, attributed in part to use of hydrophilic statins in trials vs. lipophilic statins preclinically [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- **Clinical trial pipeline (Table 1).** Active combination trials targeting lipid metabolism summarized for the review: TVB-2640 (FASN inhibitor) with [enzalutamide](../drugs/enzalutamide.md) in pancreatic cancer (NCT05743621), with paclitaxel/trastuzumab/endocrine therapy in HER2+ breast (NCT03179904), with bevacizumab in glioblastoma (NCT05118776); MTI-301/SSI-4 (SCD inhibitor) in refractory solid tumors (NCT06911008); multiple statin combinations (e.g., [lovastatin](../drugs/lovastatin.md) + [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md) in HNSCC NCT06636734; pitavastatin + [venetoclax](../drugs/venetoclax.md) in CML/AML NCT04512105). No active HCC-specific trial of a DNL inhibitor combination was identified at the time of writing other than the SCD-inhibitor MTI-301 (broader solid-tumor phase I) [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- **Dietary intervention pipeline (Table 2).** Fasting-mimicking diet post-PTA/TACE/TARE in HCC (NCT06824974); ketogenic diet + ICIs in metastatic melanoma/RCC (NCT06391099); ω3-PUFA (DHA+EPA) post chemo/TKI/radiotherapy in ER/PR-negative breast (NCT02295059). Authors highlight a preclinical rationale that ω3-PUFA enhances [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md)-induced ferroptosis [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Genes & alterations

- **[FASN](../genes/FASN.md)** — Rate-limiting DNL enzyme. Required for c-MYC– and AKT-driven hepatocarcinogenesis. Inhibition ([orlistat](../drugs/orlistat.md), TVB-2640, TVB-3664) suppresses HCC and synergizes with [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md), anti-PD-L1, and [cabozantinib](../drugs/cabozantinib.md); however, FASN-loss tumors compensate by activating cholesterol biosynthesis and lipid uptake, and combined Fasn+Srebp2 loss is required to fully abolish tumor development.
- **[ACACA](../genes/ACACA.md)** (ACC1) — Catalyzes acetyl-CoA to malonyl-CoA in DNL. ND-654 (ACC1 inhibitor) synergizes with [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md). Phase IIa trial of ACC1/2 inhibitor + DGAT2 inhibitor (PF-05221304) reduced steatosis. Counter-intuitively, liver-specific ACC1 knockout suppressed DNL but promoted DEN-induced HCC by upregulating [CD36](../genes/CD36.md) and FATP5.
- **[ACLY](../genes/ACLY.md)** — ATP citrate synthase that generates cytosolic acetyl-CoA. ACLY-driven DNL lowers PUFA:SFA, suppresses cGAS–STING, and downregulates [CD274](../genes/CD274.md) (PD-L1) to confer anti-PD-L1 resistance.
- **[SCD](../genes/SCD.md)** — Stearoyl-CoA desaturase. Drives [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md)/[regorafenib](../drugs/regorafenib.md) resistance by lowering PUFA:MUFA ratio and suppressing ER-stress–induced apoptosis. Inhibitors aramchol, A939572, MF-438, and SSI-4 (MTI-301) re-sensitize HCC; MTI-301 entering phase I (NCT06911008).
- **[SREBF1](../genes/SREBF1.md)** (SREBP1) — Master transcription factor of DNL, regulated by [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) and PI3K/AKT/mTOR; deacetylated/activated by post-translational modifiers ([SIRT3](../genes/SIRT3.md), KAT8).
- **[SREBF2](../genes/SREBF2.md)** (SREBP2) — Master transcription factor of cholesterol biosynthesis. Stabilized by caspase-3 (Mok et al.) and unspliced XBP1; loss is required (with [FASN](../genes/FASN.md) loss) to fully abolish tumor growth.
- **[CD36](../genes/CD36.md)** — Long-chain fatty acid scavenger receptor; upregulated in HCC; drives Src/PI3K/AKT/mTOR and YAP signaling and radiotherapy resistance. Humanized neutralizing antibody PLT012 received FDA Orphan Drug Designation for liver/intrahepatic bile duct cancer; small-molecule SSO inhibits CD36 and sensitizes β-catenin–activated / Tp53-loss HCC to anti-PD-1.
- **[SLC27A4](../genes/SLC27A4.md)** (FATP4) — Selectively imports MUFA to protect against lipid peroxidation and ferroptosis, conferring [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md) resistance.
- **[SLC27A5](../genes/SLC27A5.md)** (FATP5) — Bidirectional role; deficiency activates KEAP1/NRF2 and AKT/mTOR; non-canonical regulation of PIP4K2A pre-mRNA splicing as a metastasis suppressor; can also promote sorafenib-induced ferroptosis via glutathione reductase regulation.
- **[FABP1](../genes/FABP1.md)** — HCC [FABP1](../genes/FABP1.md) outcompetes T cells for linoleic acid in the TME; LINC01116 inhibition + anti-PD-1 markedly reduces progression.
- **[FABP4](../genes/FABP4.md)** — Tumor suppressor role in HCC reported (suppresses proliferation/invasion); poor-prognosis marker.
- **[FABP5](../genes/FABP5.md)** — Protects against ferroptosis by suppressing FAO; promotes lipid-loaded macrophage immunosuppression ([FABP5](../genes/FABP5.md)+ macrophages express PD-L1 and galectin-1); SBFI-103 inhibitor suppresses HCC.
- **[ACSL4](../genes/ACSL4.md)** — Ligates arachidonic acid to CoA; both a ferroptosis driver and an HCC promoter via c-Myc/SREBP1; expression predicts [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md) sensitivity (Feng et al.).
- **[SLC7A11](../genes/SLC7A11.md)** — Cystine/glutamate antiporter providing cysteine for glutathione synthesis; ferroptosis suppressor stabilized by FASN-driven HIF1α and a [MYC](../genes/MYC.md)–ZNF706 circuit; SOCS2-mediated ubiquitination of [SLC7A11](../genes/SLC7A11.md) enhances radiosensitivity.
- **[GPX4](../genes/GPX4.md)** — Glutathione peroxidase 4; negative regulator of lipid peroxidation; upregulated by 27-hydroxycholesterol to resist [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md).
- **[CPT1A](../genes/CPT1A.md)** — Rate-limiting FAO enzyme; addiction in Wnt/β-catenin / APC-loss HCC; CPT1A loss disrupts BCAA metabolism in TP53-mutant HCC; inhibitors etomoxir (hepatotoxic — failed in heart-failure trial), perhexiline, teglicar (ST1326).
- **[HMGCR](../genes/HMGCR.md)** — Cholesterol biosynthesis rate-limiting enzyme; target of statins.
- **[SQLE](../genes/SQLE.md)** — Squalene epoxidase; targeting it restores anti-PD-1 efficacy in MASH-induced HCC (Wen et al.).
- **[MVK](../genes/MVK.md)** — Mevalonate kinase; overexpressed and prognostic in HCC.
- **[LDLR](../genes/LDLR.md)** — LDL receptor; inhibition raises intracellular cholesterol synthesis via MEK/ERK and promotes HCC.
- **[PCSK9](../genes/PCSK9.md)** — Regulator of [LDLR](../genes/LDLR.md) degradation; flagged as part of the cholesterol-uptake axis.
- **[LCAT](../genes/LCAT.md)** — Lecithin–cholesterol acyltransferase; downregulated in HCC; predicts [lenvatinib](../drugs/lenvatinib.md) response; loss promotes lenvatinib resistance by enhancing TG catabolism and FAO; HDL administration sensitizes HCC to lenvatinib.
- **[PPARA](../genes/PPARA.md)** / **[PPARG](../genes/PPARG.md)** — PPAR transcription factors; PPARα drives FAO in Wnt/β-catenin HCC; PPARγ drives DNL.
- **[MYC](../genes/MYC.md)** — Amplified in HCC; transcriptionally drives SREBP1 and DNL; defines FASN-dependent HCC subset.
- **[CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md)** — β-catenin activation defines a >25% subset of HCC patients with FAO addiction; [APC](../genes/APC.md) loss is the upstream alteration in some models.
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)** — TP53-mutant HCC depends on CPT1A/BCAA metabolism; FAO suppression is pro-tumor in this subset.
- **[TERT](../genes/TERT.md)** — Listed among the few dominant HCC drivers (review context only).
- **[PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) + [MET](../genes/MET.md)** — Pten-loss + cMet overexpression model activates AKT/mTOR and DNL; FASN ablation only delays (does not prevent) tumor development; loss of both [FASN](../genes/FASN.md) and [SREBF2](../genes/SREBF2.md) abolishes tumors.
- **[EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) / [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md) / [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md)** — RTK/PI3K/AKT/mTOR signaling integrates lipid metabolism with proliferation; cholesterol-remodeled lipid rafts activate [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md).
- **[ITGB4](../genes/ITGB4.md)** — Forms PD-L1/EGFR/ITGB4 complex that drives SREBP1c-dependent lipid accumulation (Zhao et al.).
- **[ABCB1](../genes/ABCB1.md)** — Drug-efflux transporter upregulated in cholesterol-rich lipid rafts of TKI-resistant HCC; potential mechanism for TKI export.
- **[RNF43](../genes/RNF43.md)** — Loss-of-function activates Wnt/β-catenin and DNL to drive HCC.
- **[ALOX5](../genes/ALOX5.md) / [PTGS2](../genes/PTGS2.md)** (COX-2) — Bioactive lipid synthesis; PGE2 (via COX-2 / [PTGS2](../genes/PTGS2.md)) promotes HCC and immunosuppression; NSAIDs ([aspirin](../drugs/aspirin.md), [celecoxib](../drugs/celecoxib.md)) sensitize HCC to sorafenib and IFN-α in preclinical models; LTB4 (via ALOX5/BLT1) instead suppresses HCC by reducing TGF-β and promoting T-cell infiltration to sensitize to anti-PD-1.

## Clinical implications

- **Combination strategy framework.** The review proposes pairing lipid-metabolism inhibitors with current SoC as a therapeutic strategy for the ~75% of HCC patients who do not benefit from monotherapy SoC due to primary or acquired resistance [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- **Predictive biomarkers proposed.** [ACSL4](../genes/ACSL4.md) expression as a sorafenib-sensitivity biomarker; [LCAT](../genes/LCAT.md) expression as a lenvatinib-response biomarker; a FAO-gene-degradation signature stratifies HCC into clusters with differential anti-PD-1 response; [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) activation marks the FAO-addicted subset (>25% of HCC) potentially targetable by CPT1 inhibitors [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- **Drug-repurposing candidates.** Statins ([HMGCR](../genes/HMGCR.md)), [orlistat](../drugs/orlistat.md) and TVB-2640 (FASN), NSAIDs ([aspirin](../drugs/aspirin.md), [celecoxib](../drugs/celecoxib.md); COX-2/PTGS2), with the caveat that hydrophilic-statin trials in HCC have been negative while lipophilic statins ([lovastatin](../drugs/lovastatin.md), [simvastatin](../drugs/simvastatin.md), [atorvastatin](../drugs/atorvastatin.md)) show stronger preclinical and epidemiologic signals [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- **Dietary intervention as low-cost adjunct.** Calorie-restricted, ketogenic, Mediterranean, and ω3-PUFA (DHA, EPA) diets are proposed as safe, cheap means to reprogram tumor lipid metabolism and counter cachexia, with phase I/II/II combination trials underway (notably NCT06824974: fasting-mimicking diet post locoregional therapy in HCC) [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- **Etiology stratification.** MAFLD-related HCC simultaneously activates DNL and FAO; HBV-driven HCC activates DNL via HBx/LXR; DEN-induced HCC exhibits different lipid-metabolism reliance depending on diet — argues for etiology-guided trial design [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Single-protein inhibition triggers compensatory rewiring.** Loss of [FASN](../genes/FASN.md) compensates via cholesterol biosynthesis and lipid uptake (only Fasn+Srebp2 double loss abolishes tumors); ACC1 knockout paradoxically promotes DEN-induced HCC via [CD36](../genes/CD36.md) upregulation. Authors call for combination targeting and systemic-effect studies.
- **Hepatotoxicity of FAO inhibitors.** Etomoxir failed a phase II heart-failure trial due to liver toxicity, limiting clinical translation despite preclinical efficacy. Teglicar (ST1326) is the proposed safer alternative but is unproven in HCC.
- **Statin trial failures vs. epidemiology.** Lipophilic vs. hydrophilic statin choice, HDL-C vs. LDL-C dynamics, and inter-patient cholesterol baseline variability are uncontrolled in past trials.
- **ICI response in MAFLD-HCC is conflicted.** Some real-world reanalyses say MAFLD-HCC is unresponsive to ICIs; one small retrospective cohort reports intratumor steatosis as more responsive. The review flags this as unresolved; relevant for cross-paper synthesis with [PMID:35140760](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35140760) (Pfister et al. NASH/HCC ICI surveillance — cited as ref [86]) and Murai et al. ref [82].
- **Lack of HCC-specific clinical trials.** Of the 17 active combination trials tabulated by the authors targeting lipid metabolism + cancer therapy (Table 1), only the MTI-301 phase I (NCT06911008) admits HCC as an eligible indication (broader refractory solid tumors). For dietary intervention (Table 2), only NCT06824974 is HCC-specific.
- **Interplay between etiology, genetic background, and lipid metabolism is underexplored.** Authors specifically ask: "Does targeting lipid metabolism result in different responses in MAFLD-HCC patients with or without β-catenin mutation?"
- **No cBioPortal-linked dataset.** This is a narrative review; no primary patient cohort, no sequencing dataset, no specific cBioPortal `studyId` is generated. Cross-references to large-scale HCC genomic studies (TCGA [LIHC](../cancer_types/LIHC.md), Chen et al. 2024 whole-genome analysis of 494 HCCs) are cited only for the dominant-driver landscape ([TERT](../genes/TERT.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md)).
