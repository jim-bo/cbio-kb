# PIK3CB

## Overview

PIK3CB encodes the p110β catalytic subunit of phosphoinositide 3-kinase (PI3K). Activating mutations at positions equivalent to canonical [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) hotspots were discovered in metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer (mCRPC), where they co-occur with [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) loss and may respond to PIK3CB-specific inhibitors.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- In mCRPC, PIK3CB mutations were observed for the first time at positions equivalent to canonical [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) hotspots; they co-occur in the context of [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) deficiency. PIK3CB also harbors focal amplifications and novel activating fusions resulting in PIK3CB overexpression. PIK3CB mutations/amplifications/fusions contribute to the PI3K pathway alteration in 49% (73/150) of the SU2C–PCF prospective cohort. [PMID:26000489](../papers/26000489.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) (mCRPC): activating mutations, amplifications, and gene fusions; frequently co-occurring with biallelic [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) loss. [PMID:26000489](../papers/26000489.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) biallelic loss in mCRPC. [PMID:26000489](../papers/26000489.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- PIK3CB-specific inhibitors may have utility in mCRPC patients with PIK3CB mutation, amplification, or fusion; authors cite durable (>1 year) responses to PIK3CB-specific inhibition in carriers of activating PIK3CB alterations (de Bono 2015). [PMID:26000489](../papers/26000489.md)

## Open questions

- The relative contribution of PIK3CB vs [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) alterations in PI3K-pathway-driven mCRPC, and optimal inhibitor strategy, requires prospective clinical validation.
