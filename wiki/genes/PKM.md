# PKM

## Overview

PKM (pyruvate kinase M) encodes the pyruvate kinase isoforms generated by mutually exclusive splicing: PKM1 (constitutively active) and PKM2 (allosterically regulated, promotes glycolysis and biosynthesis). PKM2 is the predominant isoform expressed in proliferating cells and tumors, where it supports the Warburg effect by diverting glycolytic intermediates toward biosynthetic pathways. Upregulation of PKM2 is a hallmark of aerobic glycolysis in cancer and a candidate immunotherapy resistance mechanism.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- PKM2 protein (not PKM1) is the predominant isoform in a colon cancer proteogenomic cohort (106 tumors); confirmed by SRM (selected reaction monitoring) validation with isoform-specific assays. PKM2 was elevated 1.4-fold in MSI tumors versus other unified molecular subtypes (p = 0.003, t-test). [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md) (colon adenocarcinoma):** PKM2 protein is significantly elevated in MSI-H colon tumors. Across the MSI subtype, glycolytic enzymes including PKM2 are universally upregulated at the protein level but not uniformly at the mRNA level — indicating a protein-level Warburg effect. PKM2 abundance negatively correlates with CD8 T-cell infiltration in MSI tumors (median glycolytic-enzyme vs CD8: Spearman rho = -0.61, p = 0.02), suggesting a glycolysis-driven immune evasion mechanism. [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- PKM2 elevation co-occurs with [SLC2A3](../genes/SLC2A3.md) (glucose transporter, 1.85-fold elevated) and other glycolytic enzyme upregulation in MSI-H tumors. Both were validated by SRM. The glycolytic enzyme set inversely correlates with CD8 infiltration, specifically within the MSI subtype. [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- PKM2-targeting agents are proposed in combination with immune checkpoint blockade for MSI-H colorectal cancer as a strategy to overcome glycolysis-driven immune evasion. The anti-correlation between glycolytic enzyme (including PKM2) abundance and CD8 infiltration specifically in MSI tumors provides the rationale. [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Open questions

- Why is the Warburg effect (protein-level glycolytic upregulation, TCA-cycle downregulation at protein but not mRNA) restricted to the MSI subtype of colon cancer? The mechanistic basis for the protein-level specificity is not resolved. No therapeutic validation data for PKM2-targeting agents in colon cancer are reported. [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)
