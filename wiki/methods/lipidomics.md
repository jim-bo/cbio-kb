# Lipidomics

## Overview

Lipidomics is the large-scale, mass-spectrometry-based profiling of the complete lipid complement (lipidome) of a biological system. Instrumentation typically involves liquid chromatography coupled to high-resolution tandem mass spectrometry (LC-MS/MS), enabling identification and quantification of hundreds to thousands of lipid species across major classes (glycerophospholipids, sphingolipids, glycerolipids, fatty acids, sterols, and others). In cancer research, lipidomics captures metabolic reprogramming events — including altered fatty acid synthesis, phospholipid remodeling, and sphingolipid signaling — that cannot be detected by genomic or transcriptomic assays.

## Used by

- Reviewed as one of the core omics modalities in the multi-omics precision health framework alongside WGS, WES, RNA-seq, proteomics, DNA methylation, ChIP-seq, ATAC-seq, [metabolomics](../methods/metabolomics.md), and wearable monitoring; the review argues that lipidomics captures lipid-metabolic states functionally relevant to disease that are invisible to nucleic-acid-based assays. [PMID:37119971](../papers/37119971.md)

## Notes

- Lipidomics is distinct from but complementary to [metabolomics](../methods/metabolomics.md): metabolomics typically covers small polar metabolites, amino acids, and organic acids; lipidomics focuses specifically on lipid species, which require different extraction and ionization protocols.

- In cancer biology, altered lipid metabolism is a recognized hallmark; lipidomics has been applied in contexts including hepatocellular carcinoma metabolic subtyping and ovarian cancer biomarker discovery, though specific cBioPortal studies in this corpus do not report lipidomic data directly.
