**PMID:** 30867592 · **DOI:** 10.1038/s41586-019-1012-y · **Journal:** Nature (2019)

## TL;DR

This single-arm phase 2 trial (NCT02649972) treated 18 adults with histiocytic neoplasms (12 Erdheim–Chester disease, 2 Langerhans cell histiocytosis, 2 Rosai–Dorfman disease, 2 mixed) with the oral MEK1/2 inhibitor [cobimetinib](../drugs/cobimetinib.md) at 60 mg daily, 21-of-28 days, regardless of tumor genotype. The PET-defined overall response rate was 89% (90% CI 73–100), with 72% complete responses; at one year, 100% of responses were ongoing and 94% of patients remained progression-free, with no acquired resistance observed. Responses occurred across the full spectrum of MAPK-pathway lesions tested ([BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600 and non-V600, [ARAF](../genes/ARAF.md), [RAF1](../genes/RAF1.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md), MEK1, MEK2), and three previously uncharacterized alterations — MEK2 Y134H, RAF1 K106N, and BRAF N486_T491delinsK — were shown in Ba/F3 cells to activate ERK and confer [cobimetinib](../drugs/cobimetinib.md) sensitivity ([PMID:30867592](../papers/30867592.md)).

## Cohort & data

- 18 adult patients enrolled at Memorial Sloan Kettering on a single-institution, single-arm phase 2 study (NCT02649972); patient-level clinical data and sequencing deposited in cBioPortal as [histiocytosis_cobi_msk_2019](../datasets/histiocytosis_cobi_msk_2019.md).
- Histologic mix: [ECD](../cancer_types/ECD.md) 12/18 (67%), [LCH](../cancer_types/LCH.md) 2/18 (11%), [RDD](../cancer_types/RDD.md) 2/18 (11%), [MIXED](../cancer_types/MIXED.md) histiocytosis 2/18 (11%).
- Median age 51.9 years (range 18.3–79.5); 72% male; 50% had CNS involvement; 89% had ≥1 prior systemic therapy and 56% ≥2; ECOG 2–3 in 28%.
- Primary endpoint: PET response (CR + PR) per FDG-PET response criteria assessed by a dual-boarded radiologist; secondary: RECIST v1.1 by CT/MRI, duration of response, PFS, safety.
- Genomic profiling per tissue availability: [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) targeted exon sequencing, [MSK Heme-PACT](../methods/msk-hemepact.md), [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) with fingernail germline control, and [Archer FusionPlex](../methods/archer-fusionplex.md) custom solid panel for targeted RNA fusion detection; cell-free DNA, PCR, and Sequenom were also used in subsets ([PMID:30867592](../papers/30867592.md)).

## Key findings

- PET-defined ORR: 89% (90% one-sided CI 73–100) in 18 patients — 13 CR (72%), 3 PR (17%), 1 SD (6%), 0 PD, 1 not evaluable (counted as non-responder). RECIST v1.1 ORR: 57% (90% CI 37–100) in 14 evaluable patients ([PMID:30867592](../papers/30867592.md)).
- Median time to best PET response: 3.2 months (range 1.6–15.9). At median follow-up of 11.9 months (range 4.6–26.4), neither median DOR nor median PFS had been reached; at 1 year, 100% of responses were ongoing and 94% of patients remained progression-free, with no responder having progressed. No acquired resistance was observed.
- Responses occurred at all disease sites, including CNS (a site historically associated with morbidity and mortality in [ECD](../cancer_types/ECD.md)).
- At least one MAPK-pathway mutation was identified in 83% (15/18) of patients; mutational landscape included [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E (n=4) and BRAF N486_T491delinsK (n=1), [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md) (MEK1; Q56P, P105_I107del, P124L, P124Q; n=4), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) (R149G, G12R, G13C; n=3), [ARAF](../genes/ARAF.md) (S225V, P216A; n=2), [RAF1](../genes/RAF1.md) K106N (n=1), [MAP2K2](../genes/MAP2K2.md) (MEK2) Y134H (n=1), and [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) G12D (n=1); two patients harbored compound MAPK mutations and three had unknown mutational status. Responses occurred across all genotypes and in 2 of 3 patients with unknown mutations.
- Functional characterization in Ba/F3 cells: MEK2 Y134H, RAF1 K106N, and BRAF N486_T491delinsK each conferred IL3-independent growth and activated ERK (wild-type counterparts did not), and each sensitized Ba/F3 cells to cobimetinib relative to parental controls (two-way ANOVA, p<0.0001 across growth assays; one-way ANOVA, p<0.01 for IC50 differences).
- Safety: 10/18 (56%) had a dose reduction; attribution-related grade ≥3 adverse events were uncommon and included diarrhea (11%), hyponatremia (11%), anemia (11%), hypokalemia (11%), CPK elevation (6%), serum amylase elevation (6%), lipase elevation (6%), and central retinal vein occlusion (6%, leading to permanent discontinuation in 1 patient). One patient with extensive baseline lung histiocytic involvement died of pneumonia (deemed unrelated) prior to first response assessment and was counted as a non-responder ([PMID:30867592](../papers/30867592.md)).

## Genes & alterations

- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — V600E (n=4 patients) and N486_T491delinsK (n=1, in [LCH](../cancer_types/LCH.md)); the in-frame indel N486_T491delinsK was shown to be activating and cobimetinib-sensitive in Ba/F3, expanding the actionable BRAF lesion catalogue beyond V600.
- [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md) (MEK1) — Q56P, P105_I107del, P124L, P124Q across 4 patients; all responded to cobimetinib despite prior preclinical reports that some MEK1 mutations confer MEK inhibitor resistance.
- [MAP2K2](../genes/MAP2K2.md) (MEK2) — novel Y134H in an ECD patient; functionally validated as ERK-activating and cobimetinib-sensitive in Ba/F3 (this is the first functional characterization of MEK2 Y134H in any setting).
- [ARAF](../genes/ARAF.md) — S225V and P216A; ARAF mutations are rare outside histiocytoses and were responsive to MEK1/2 inhibition.
- [RAF1](../genes/RAF1.md) — K106N in a patient with mixed histiocytosis; RAF1 mutations had not previously been described in histiocytoses and K106N had no prior functional characterization. Ba/F3 work confirmed activating, cobimetinib-sensitive phenotype.
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — R149G (single mutation, ECD) plus G12R and G13C (in compound-mutation patients). All KRAS-mutant patients responded, despite known KRAS allele-specific MEK dependence variability in solid tumors.
- [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) — G12D (in a compound-mutation LCH patient with BRAF V600E and KRAS G13C).

## Clinical implications

- Provides the first prospective evidence that MEK1/2 inhibition with [cobimetinib](../drugs/cobimetinib.md) is efficacious and durable in histiocytic neoplasms regardless of MAPK genotype, including non-V600 BRAF, RAF1, ARAF, MEK1, MEK2, KRAS, and NRAS alterations and in patients with unknown mutational status — extending molecularly targeted therapy to the ~50% of histiocytosis patients without BRAF V600 who previously had no standard targeted option (the prior standard, [vemurafenib](../drugs/vemurafenib.md), is approved only for BRAF V600-mutant Erdheim–Chester disease per the VE-BASKET data).
- Responses included CNS sites of disease, addressing a major source of morbidity and mortality in [ECD](../cancer_types/ECD.md).
- Suggests that histiocytic neoplasms are collectively defined by MAPK-pathway dependence and may lack the adaptive escape mechanisms that limit single-agent MEK inhibition in other MAPK-altered solid tumors; the absence of acquired resistance during the observation window contrasts with disappointing single-agent MEK inhibitor activity reported in melanoma and other lineages.
- Authors recommend that empiric cobimetinib be considered for histiocytosis patients regardless of genotype, particularly when sequencing is inconclusive or BRAF inhibition is unavailable or has failed.

## Limitations & open questions

- Small cohort (n=18) at a single institution; ECD comprised 67% of enrollment, so generalizability to Rosai–Dorfman, Langerhans cell, and mixed histiocytoses is limited despite responses being observed in all subtypes.
- Eligibility required refractory or multiorgan disease (BRAF V600-mutant patients were eligible only after BRAF inhibitor intolerance/inaccessibility), so the population is selected for treatment-resistant biology.
- Sequencing was performed on heterogeneous assays per tissue availability; histiocytic neoplasms have low tumor cellularity and VAFs, and 17% (3/18) of patients had no MAPK mutation identified (2 of whom responded), leaving open whether undetected MAPK lesions or alternative drivers underlie response in mutation-negative cases.
- Median follow-up was 11.9 months at the data cut; durability beyond 2 years and the eventual emergence of acquired resistance remain to be evaluated.
- Functional characterization of novel alleles relied on Ba/F3 IL3-independence and Western blot for pERK; biochemical kinase data and structural mechanism for MEK2 Y134H, RAF1 K106N, and BRAF N486_T491delinsK are not provided.
- Dose modifications were common (56% had ≥1 reduction, most often for ejection-fraction decline, rash, or diarrhea); long-term cardiac, dermatologic, and ocular toxicity in this generally indolent disease population will require continued surveillance.
