**PMID:** 33397444 · **DOI:** 10.1186/s13059-020-02230-w · **Journal:** Genome Biology (2021)

## TL;DR

Su et al. performed integrative genomic, transcriptomic, and DNA-methylation profiling of 40 upper tract urothelial carcinomas (UTUCs; 20 non-muscle-invasive, 20 muscle-invasive) from a single French biobank (Pitié-Salpêtrière) to dissect the molecular basis of muscle invasiveness. Whole-exome sequencing on 30 paired tumor–normal cases identified [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) (50%), [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md) (27%), and a novel [UTUC](../cancer_types/UTUC.md) tumor suppressor [ZFP36L1](../genes/ZFP36L1.md) (20%) as significantly mutated genes (MutSigCV FDR<0.05); mutations of the [ZFP36](../genes/ZFP36.md) family ([ZFP36](../genes/ZFP36.md), [ZFP36L1](../genes/ZFP36L1.md), [ZFP36L2](../genes/ZFP36L2.md)) collectively affected 26.7% of cases. Infinium EPIC methylation profiling of 35 tumors plus 8 adjacent normals identified two epi-clusters — **EpiC-low** (hypomethylated, immune-depleted, FGFR3-enriched, NMI-enriched) and **EpiC-high** (hypermethylated, immune-infiltrated, [SWI/SNF](../themes/swi-snf-complex.md)-mutated, MI-enriched, worse [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) HR=6.57) — that were also recapitulated when the UTUC methylation signature was applied to TCGA muscle-invasive bladder cancer (BLCA-C1 vs BLCA-C2).

## Cohort & data

- **40 UTUC patients** (20 NMI + 20 MI), fresh-frozen surgical resections from the Pitié-Salpêtrière Hospital biobank, all microsatellite-stable, all consented; cohort registered as cBioPortal study [utuc_igbmc_2021](../datasets/utuc_igbmc_2021.md). Median follow-up 53 months (range 2–98); 18 recurred, 12 died of disease.
- **Assays per case (matched germline available for 30/40):**
  - [Whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) on 30 paired tumor–normal (Agilent SureSelect 50 Mb, Illumina HiSeq 2000, 2×76 bp; tumor coverage 79–158×, germline 43–80×). 15 NMI + 15 MI cases.
  - [Targeted Sanger sequencing](../methods/sanger-sequencing.md) of [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) hotspots (S249C, Y373C) for 35 cases; Sanger validation of FGFR3/KDM6A/ZFP36L1 calls.
  - [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) on 20 cases (15 MI + 5 NMI, NuGen Ovation V2, HiSeq 2000 75 bp PE, aligned to GRCh37/hg19 with MOSAIK, GENCODE15).
  - [Infinium MethylationEPIC array](../methods/epic-methylation-array.md) on 35 UTUC + 8 normal adjacent bladder tissues; 836,691 probes after filtering sex chromosomes and NA-containing probes.
- **External cohorts used for cross-validation:** [blca_tcga_pub_2017](../datasets/blca_tcga_pub_2017.md) / [blca_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/blca_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md) (412 MI [BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md) samples for methylation + somatic mutation), Hurst NMI-BLCA cohort [blca_nmibc_2017](../datasets/blca_nmibc_2017.md) (n=24 TaG2), [blca_dfarber_mskcc_2014](../datasets/blca_dfarber_mskcc_2014.md) (DFCI), [blca_bgi](../datasets/blca_bgi.md), MSKCC UTUC cohort [utuc_mskcc_2015](../datasets/utuc_mskcc_2015.md) for KMT2C/FGFR3 co-occurrence replication, and [ccle_broad_2019](../datasets/ccle_broad_2019.md) for 20 bladder cell lines (DNA methylation + expression from GDSC).
- **Bioinformatic pipeline:** ANNOVAR + SIFT annotation; [MutSigCV](../methods/mutsigcv.md) v1.41 for SMGs; [deconstructSigs](../methods/deconstructsigs.md) and [NMF](../methods/nmf-clustering.md) for COSMIC v2 mutational signatures; [GISTIC 2.0](../methods/gistic.md) for focal SCNAs (q<0.2); [ChAMP](../methods/champ.md) for DMP/DMR with DMRcate; [iClusterBayes](../methods/icluster.md) for multi-omics integration; [consensusMIBC](../methods/cms-classifier.md) for MIBC transcriptomic subtype assignment plus the BASE47 47-gene classifier; [MCP-counter](../methods/mcp-counter.md) and [ESTIMATE](../methods/estimate.md) for immune/stromal deconvolution; MeTIL score for methylation-derived TIL inference.

## Key findings

- **Three SMGs in UTUC by MutSigCV (FDR<0.05):** [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) 50% (FDR=3.15×10⁻³), [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md) 27% (FDR=1.03×10⁻³), and **novel** [ZFP36L1](../genes/ZFP36L1.md) 20% (FDR=2.62×10⁻³). Average tumor mutation burden 2.9±3.5 mut/Mb; median 1.5 (range 0.3–15.7).
- **Additional recurrently mutated (>10%, P<0.05, FDR>0.05):** [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) (reported as "MLL2", P=0.037), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) (P=0.02), [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md) (P=8.3×10⁻⁷), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) (P=0.01), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (P=0.018), [CRIPAK](../genes/CRIPAK.md) (P=0.0001), [GANAB](../genes/GANAB.md) (P=0.04).
- **Co-occurrence patterns:** FGFR3↔[KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) co-occur (log odds ratio = 3.1, P=0.04); validated in [utuc_mskcc_2015](../datasets/utuc_mskcc_2015.md) (LOR=2.9, P<0.001, FDR<0.001). [ZFP36L1](../genes/ZFP36L1.md) co-occurs with both [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md) (LOR=3.1, P=0.03) and [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md) (LOR=3.29, P=0.04).
- **ZFP36L1 mutation spectrum:** 8 mutations in 6 cases; 6/8 frameshift indels, 2 deleterious missense (Polyphen2/SIFT). Adding [ZFP36L2](../genes/ZFP36L2.md) E249X (a known TCGA-BLCA hotspot) and [ZFP36](../genes/ZFP36.md) p.P253A mutations gives **26.7% (8/30)** UTUCs with ZFP36-family mutations. ZFP36L1 mutations across 10,967 TCGA tumors (32 histologies): 120 (1.09%), highest in bladder (30/410 = 7.3%); when extended to [ZFP36](../genes/ZFP36.md) family, frequency reaches ~3% pan-cancer and 9.1–10% across three bladder cohorts ([blca_tcga_pub_2017](../datasets/blca_tcga_pub_2017.md), [blca_dfarber_mskcc_2014](../datasets/blca_dfarber_mskcc_2014.md), [blca_bgi](../datasets/blca_bgi.md)).
- **ZFP36-family mutations associate with higher mutational load** in UTUC (P=0.008) and in TCGA BLCA (P=0.0009); pooled bladder cohorts (n=562) show better OS (P=0.01) and trend toward better PFS (P=0.08) for ZFP36-mutant tumors.
- **Functional ZFP36L1 knockdown** (siRNA in TCCSUP bladder cancer line) caused loss of E-cadherin, EMT-consistent spindle-shaped morphology, and significantly increased migration on [transwell assay](../methods/transwell-invasion-assay.md); no effect on proliferation or apoptosis. Knockdown confirmed by Western blot.
- **Recurrent focal SCNAs (GISTIC):** losses at 1p36.33, 9p21.3 (encompassing [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)/[CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md)), 11p15.1; gains at 1q23.3, 6p21.33, 8p11.23, 8q22.3 ([YWHAZ](../genes/YWHAZ.md)), 12q15 ([MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md)), 19q12 ([CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md)). 8q22.3 gain co-occurs with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations (P=0.02) and is mutually exclusive with FGFR3 mutations (P=0.03); 8q22.3 gain, 12q15 gain, and 11p15.11 loss enrich in MI cases.
- **NMI- vs MI-UTUC comparison with bladder cohorts:** vs Hurst NMI-BLCA ([blca_nmibc_2017](../datasets/blca_nmibc_2017.md)), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) trend higher in NMI-UTUC (P=0.08) while [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) higher in NMI-BLCA (P=0.0001). vs TCGA MI-BLCA (n=412), MI-UTUC has more [GANAB](../genes/GANAB.md) (P=0.021), [CRIPAK](../genes/CRIPAK.md) (P=0.022), and trend for [ZFP36L1](../genes/ZFP36L1.md) (P=0.1).
- **Mutational signatures:** signatures 1, 13, 16 dominate. NMF resolved three clusters — C1 (n=7, sig 16, unknown etiology), C2 (n=8, sig 1, age-related deamination), C3 (n=15, sigs 13/2, APOBEC). No association with MeTIL score.
- **FGFR3-mutant UTUC is immune-cold:** significantly lower MeTIL score than FGFR3-WT (P=7.8×10⁻⁵), validated in TCGA-BLCA (P=9.0×10⁻⁶); independent of muscle invasiveness (P=0.008 for MI; P=0.024 for NMI). FGFR3 mutation/fusion also linked to lower immune (P=0.016) and stromal (P=0.003) gene-expression scores.
- **Transcriptomic subtypes:** by [consensusMIBC](../methods/cms-classifier.md), 14/20 (70%) were luminal papillary (including all NMI cases), 2 luminal unstable, 2 stromal-rich, 2 basal/squamous; none of the stromal-rich/basal-squamous had FGFR3 mutations. BASE47 classified 14 luminal-like and 6 basal-like. One FGFR3-TACC3 fusion detected (UTUC-11).
- **DNA methylation defines two epi-clusters** (top 1% variable probes, n=836,691 retained): **EpiC-high** (n=23, formerly C1, hypermethylated: 14,209 hypermethylated probes vs only 34 in EpiC-low) and **EpiC-low** (n=12, formerly C2). DMR-enriched pathways: PRC2 / MLL targets, [SMARCA2](../genes/SMARCA2.md) target genes, [ZEB1](../genes/ZEB1.md) targets.
  - EpiC-high = hypermethylated, **immune-infiltrated** (higher MeTIL, P=0.003), **MI-enriched** (16/23 = 69.6%), enriched for [SWI/SNF](../themes/swi-snf-complex.md) gene mutations (50% vs 0%, P=0.01, FDR=0.054).
  - EpiC-low = hypomethylated, immune-depleted, **NMI-enriched** (11/12 = 91.7%, P=0.0009), enriched for [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) mutations (90% vs 28%, P=0.004, FDR=0.044).
  - **Prognostic:** EpiC-high → shorter OS (P=0.038; HR=6.57; 95% CI 1.87–23.04) and shorter distant-metastasis-free survival (P=0.036; HR=6.55; 95% CI 1.87–22.98). No other clinicopathological variable was independently prognostic.
- **FGFR3-mutant UTUC is hypomethylated relative to FGFR3-WT:** 84,717/836,691 probes differentially methylated (Δβ≥0.2, FDR<0.05); 82,991 (97.8%) hypomethylated in FGFR3-mut. Hypomethylated probes enriched in enhancers (3.8% vs 3.2%) and DNase [HS](../cancer_types/HS.md) sites (63.7% vs 57.4%); top DMRs overlap bladder genes including [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md), [GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md), [KRT15](../genes/KRT15.md), [KRT5](../genes/KRT5.md). Validated in 20 bladder cell lines ([ccle_broad_2019](../datasets/ccle_broad_2019.md)): cell lines clustered by FGFR3 alteration (mutations/fusions) vs [SWI/SNF](../themes/swi-snf-complex.md) gene mutations using the 14,209-probe signature; cluster C1 (SWI/SNF-mut) showed higher interferon-γ stimulation signature (P=0.022).
- **Translation to TCGA MI-BLCA:** projecting the EpiC signature onto [blca_tcga_pub_2017](../datasets/blca_tcga_pub_2017.md) recovered two analogous subgroups — BLCA-C1 (hypermethylated, higher MeTIL, immune-inflamed with fibroblasts/MDC/monocytes/B/T/CD8 by MCP-counter, high-grade, non-papillary, poor OS) vs BLCA-C2 (FGFR3-amp/mut/overexpressing, neutrophil-enriched P=1.4×10⁻⁸, papillary, low-grade). BLCA-C1 vs BLCA-C2 OS: HR=1.38 (95% CI 1.03–1.85), P=0.035; median OS 2.4 vs 5.4 years.
- **Promoter CpG-island methylation:** 905 frequently methylated genes (>10%) in UTUC tumors; [ACTL6B](../genes/ACTL6B.md) (a SWI/SNF subunit) hypermethylated in 20% of tumors vs 0% of normals. No promoter methylation of [ZFP36L1](../genes/ZFP36L1.md) detected.
- **iClusterBayes** on 28 UTUCs with paired WES+methylation yielded iCluster-high (n=17) vs iCluster-low (n=9), strongly congruent with EpiC clusters (P=4.9×10⁻⁵). Posterior-probability mutation drivers: FGFR3 (0.99), SWI/SNF (0.63), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) (0.63), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (0.56); FGFR3 (P=0.011, FDR=0.047) and KMT2D (P=0.028, FDR=0.057) enriched in iCluster-low; 8q22.3 amplification enriched in iCluster-high (P=0.023, FDR=0.093).
- **Among recurrently mutated genes, only [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) mutations independently associated with improved OS** (P=0.005) and PFS at distant sites (P=0.007); FGFR3 mutations were enriched in NMI vs MI (73% vs 27%, P=0.027, FDR=0.2); [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) mutations were enriched in MI (33% vs 0%, P=0.042, FDR=0.2).

## Genes & alterations

- **[FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)** — Mutations in 50% (15/30) of UTUC WES cases; defining driver of the EpiC-low (hypomethylated, immune-cold) subtype; mutually exclusive with 8q22.3 gain; FGFR3-mut UTUC has globally hypomethylated genome (97.8% of differential probes hypomethylated). One FGFR3-TACC3 fusion detected. Associates with better OS/PFS.
- **[KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md)** — Mutations in 27% of WES UTUC cases (FDR=1.03×10⁻³); co-occurs with [ZFP36L1](../genes/ZFP36L1.md) (LOR=3.1, P=0.03).
- **[ZFP36L1](../genes/ZFP36L1.md)** — Novel UTUC tumor suppressor: 20% mutation rate, 6/8 frameshift indels; siRNA knockdown in TCCSUP bladder line drives loss of E-cadherin, EMT-like morphology, and increased migration with no proliferation/apoptosis effect; 7.3% in TCGA bladder, highest among all 32 TCGA histologies profiled.
- **[ZFP36L2](../genes/ZFP36L2.md)** — Recurrent E249X stop-gain (TCGA-BLCA hotspot) identified in 1 UTUC; ZFP36L1 and [ZFP36L2](../genes/ZFP36L2.md) mutations are mutually co-occurring across TCGA (P<0.001, FDR=0.002).
- **[ZFP36](../genes/ZFP36.md)** — Deleterious p.P253A mutation in 1 UTUC (COSMIC-curated); contributes to 26.7% ZFP36-family mutation rate in UTUC.
- **[KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md)** — Mutated in >10% UTUC (P=0.02); co-occurs with FGFR3 (LOR=3.1, P=0.04); replicated in [utuc_mskcc_2015](../datasets/utuc_mskcc_2015.md) (LOR=2.9, P<0.001).
- **[KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md)** — Reported as "MLL2" in the paper (the older synonym for KMT2D); recurrent mutations (P=0.037) associated with higher mutational load (P=0.04). Posterior probability 0.63 in iClusterBayes; enriched in iCluster-low (FGFR3-rich subtype), P=0.028.
- **[STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md)** — Recurrent mutations (P=8.3×10⁻⁷); co-occur with ZFP36L1 (LOR=3.29, P=0.04).
- **[ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md)** — Recurrent (P=0.01); associated with higher mutational load (P=0.01); enriched in MI tumors (33% vs 0% in NMI, P=0.042, FDR=0.2); collapsed into the "SWI/SNF" composite mutation feature for iClusterBayes.
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)** — Recurrent (P=0.018); co-occurs with 8q22.3 gain (P=0.02); iCluster posterior 0.56.
- **[CRIPAK](../genes/CRIPAK.md)** — Recurrent in UTUC (P=0.0001); enriched in MI-UTUC vs TCGA MI-BLCA (P=0.022). Caveat: [CRIPAK](../genes/CRIPAK.md) has since been reported as a problematic gene model — the paper does not flag this.
- **[GANAB](../genes/GANAB.md)** — Recurrent (P=0.04); associated with higher mutational load (P=0.02); enriched in MI-UTUC vs TCGA MI-BLCA (P=0.021).
- **[CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) / [CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md)** — Lost at 9p21.3 (focal GISTIC peak); deletion contributes to iClusterBayes (posterior 0.54).
- **[MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) (12q15), [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md) (19q12), [YWHAZ](../genes/YWHAZ.md) (8q22.3)** — Recurrent focal SCNA gains; 8q22.3 ([YWHAZ](../genes/YWHAZ.md) region) gain enriches in MI and iCluster-high (P=0.023, FDR=0.093) and was recently linked to aggressive bladder cancer.
- **[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md)** — Partner in the single FGFR3-TACC3 oncogenic fusion identified by RNA-seq (case UTUC-11).
- **[SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md)** — Co-occurred with FGFR3 mutation in the 639V bladder cell line (where FGFR3 mutation is otherwise non-functional/non-expressing in J82).
- **[SMARCA2](../genes/SMARCA2.md)** — [SMARCA2](../genes/SMARCA2.md) target gene set enriched among DMRs distinguishing EpiC-high from EpiC-low (GSEA).
- **[ACTL6B](../genes/ACTL6B.md)** — SWI/SNF member; promoter CpG-island hypermethylation in 20% of UTUC tumors vs 0% of normals.
- **[GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md), [KRT5](../genes/KRT5.md), [KRT15](../genes/KRT15.md)** — Among the top DMRs distinguishing FGFR3-mutant from FGFR3-WT UTUC (genes known to be involved in bladder cancer luminal/basal identity).
- **[HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md)** — Cited as a prior UTUC-relevant gene whose mutation frequency differs from bladder carcinomas (no new findings in this paper).
- **[PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)** — Significantly enriched in NMI-BLCA ([blca_nmibc_2017](../datasets/blca_nmibc_2017.md)) vs NMI-UTUC (P=0.0001); shows tissue-of-origin difference between upper-tract and bladder NMI tumors.
- **[EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md)** — Therapeutic implication discussed: [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) inhibition ± [cisplatin](../drugs/cisplatin.md) proposed as strategy for the SWI/SNF-mutant EpiC-high subtype based on cited prior NK-cell-activating mechanism (no in-paper EZH2 mutation data).
- **[ZEB1](../genes/ZEB1.md)** — [ZEB1](../genes/ZEB1.md) target gene set enriched among EpiC-defining DMRs (GSEA).

## Clinical implications

- **EpiC classification as prognostic stratifier:** EpiC-high vs EpiC-low is the only independent predictor of OS (HR=6.57; 95% CI 1.87–23.04; P=0.038) and distant-metastasis-free survival (HR=6.55; 95% CI 1.87–22.98; P=0.036) in this UTUC cohort, beating all conventional clinicopathological features. Authors propose using it for post-resection stratification (e.g., who should receive preoperative chemotherapy or adjuvant FGFR3-targeted therapy).
- **EpiC-low / FGFR3-mutant subtype = candidate for FGFR inhibition + immune sensitization:** Authors note that [erdafitinib](../drugs/erdafitinib.md) received accelerated FDA approval for relapsed/refractory metastatic bladder cancer (40% response rate, n=90, in tumors with actionable FGFR alterations) and propose combination with PD-1/PD-L1 blockade to convert the T-cell-depleted FGFR3-mutant tumors into immune-responsive ones.
- **EpiC-high / SWI/SNF-mutant subtype = candidate for EZH2 inhibition:** Cite prior work showing EZH2 inhibition ± [cisplatin](../drugs/cisplatin.md) activates NK-cell-mediated immunity in bladder cancer xenografts; propose this as a strategy for FGFR3-WT, SWI/SNF-mutant, immune-inflamed MI UTUC.
- **FGFR3 mutation status as a biomarker of "cold" tumors:** FGFR3 mutation independently associates with low MeTIL score in both UTUC and TCGA-BLCA (P=7.8×10⁻⁵ and P=9.0×10⁻⁶), reinforcing the rationale to combine FGFR-directed agents with checkpoint inhibition in this lineage.
- **ZFP36-family mutations as a putative TMB / outcome marker:** ZFP36-family-mutant tumors have higher mutational load and better pooled-bladder-cohort OS (P=0.01, n=562); not formally a biomarker yet but suggested for follow-up.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Small monocentric cohort (n=40 UTUC; 30 WES; 20 RNA-seq; 35 methylation)** — authors explicitly call out the rarity-driven sample size; some sub-analyses (e.g., RNA clusters vs clinicopathology) were under-powered.
- **No matched primary/metastasis profiling** — stability of EpiC subtypes across disease progression is untested.
- **CRIPAK as a recurrently mutated gene** is reported uncritically; the broader community has since flagged CRIPAK as a likely false-positive gene model. The paper does not address this.
- **Mechanistic gap on FGFR3 → hypomethylation axis:** Whether the global hypomethylation seen in FGFR3-mutant UTUC is a direct FGFR3-signaling consequence or a passenger of the luminal-papillary lineage is left as an open question.
- **SWI/SNF mutation ↔ immune infiltration link is correlative and contradictory across cancer types** (authors cite PMID:30166959-style debate, not in corpus, without resolving for UTUC).
- **Functional validation of ZFP36L1 done in a single bladder cell line (TCCSUP)** with siRNA, not CRISPR; in vivo and UTUC-specific models are missing.
- **Therapeutic recommendations (FGFR+PD-1 combo; EZH2 inhibition) are hypotheses** — not tested prospectively in this paper.
- **External UTUC validation cohort for the EpiC classifier is absent;** the methylation classifier is only projected onto bladder ([blca_tcga_pub_2017](../datasets/blca_tcga_pub_2017.md), [blca_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/blca_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md)) and bladder cell lines ([ccle_broad_2019](../datasets/ccle_broad_2019.md)) — UTUC-specific reproduction in [utuc_mskcc_2015](../datasets/utuc_mskcc_2015.md) is limited to FGFR3/KMT2C co-occurrence.
