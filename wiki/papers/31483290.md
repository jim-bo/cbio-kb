**PMID:** 31483290 · **DOI:** [10.1172/JCI128227](https://doi.org/10.1172/JCI128227) · **Journal:** *J Clin Invest* (2019;129(10):4276–4289)

## TL;DR

Ho, Ochoa, and colleagues performed an integrated genomic analysis of 1,045 adenoid cystic carcinomas (177 primary, 868 recurrent/metastatic [R/M]) — by far the largest [ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md) cohort assembled to date — combining WES, WGS, and targeted NGS data from Sanger/MDACC, MSKCC ([MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md), n=94), and [Foundation Medicine](../methods/foundationone.md) (n=730). R/M tumors were significantly enriched for activating [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) mutations (26.3% vs. 8.5% in primary, OR 3.86, P<0.0001), other Notch-pathway genes, and chromatin-remodeling genes ([KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md), [ARID1B](../genes/ARID1B.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md), [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md)). [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter mutations (13.1% of R/M cases) were mutually exclusive with both [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) mutations (q = 3.3×10⁻⁴) and [MYB](../genes/MYB.md)/[MYBL1](../genes/MYBL1.md) fusions (q = 5.6×10⁻³), defining four mutually exclusive molecular subgroups ([MYB](../genes/MYB.md)⁺NOTCH1⁺, MYB⁺/other, MYBᵂᵀNOTCH1⁺, MYBᵂᵀTERT⁺) with distinct survival. The data are deposited as the cBioPortal study [acc_2019](../datasets/acc_2019.md).

## Cohort & data

- **Total cases:** 1,045 ACCs — 177 primary, 868 recurrent/metastatic (R/M) [PMID:31483290](../papers/31483290.md).
- **Cancer types:** Adenoid cystic carcinoma ([ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md)); 89.8% salivary head/neck origin, 6.8% lung, 3.4% breast.
- **Dataset:** [acc_2019](../datasets/acc_2019.md) on cBioPortal (study_id: `acc_2019`).
- **Assays / platforms:**
  - [Whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) and [whole-genome sequencing](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) (n=193 combined across cohorts; mean TMB 0.34 mut/Mb).
  - Targeted NGS on n=852, including [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) (94 MSKCC R/M cases; 410 or 468 genes; median exon coverage >×600) and [FoundationOne](../methods/foundationone.md) (730 R/M cases; median exon coverage >×500).
  - Multiregion validation sequencing via a custom Ion AmpliSeq panel (165 amplicons; mean coverage ×5,406) on one heavily sampled salivary [ACC](../cancer_types/ACC.md) ([targeted deep amplicon seq](../methods/targeted-deep-amplicon-seq.md)).
  - [FISH](../methods/fish.md) for MYB and [MYBL1](../genes/MYBL1.md) rearrangements; clonality of MYB-NFIB confirmed with a 3-color BAC probe mix.
  - Clonality and ITH inferred with [FACETS](../methods/facets.md) and [PyClone](../methods/pyclone.md).
  - Microsatellite status by [MSIsensor](../methods/msisensor.md) and orthogonal [MiMSI](../methods/mimsi.md).
  - Variant annotation by [OncoKB](../methods/oncokb-annotation.md).
- **Germline:** 90 R/M MSKCC cases analyzed against a 76-gene cancer-predisposition panel; pathogenic variants scored per ACMG.
- **Clinical follow-up:** 84 MSKCC R/M ACC patients with median follow-up 8.1 years.

## Key findings

- **R/M enrichment of Notch pathway alterations:**
  - [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) 26.3% (R/M) vs. 8.5% (primary), OR 3.86, P<0.0001; 18.3% of R/M had *activating* hot-spot mutations (HD or PEST domain) [PMID:31483290](../papers/31483290.md).
  - [NOTCH2](../genes/NOTCH2.md) 4.6% vs. 2.3% (OR 2.09, P=0.17); [NOTCH3](../genes/NOTCH3.md) 5.7% vs. 2.3% (OR 2.63, P=0.067); [NOTCH4](../genes/NOTCH4.md) 3.6% vs. 0.6% (OR 6.59, P=0.067).
  - In aggregate, 34.1% of R/M cases harbored a mutation in NOTCH1–4 and 39.6% in the broader Notch pathway (including [SPEN](../genes/SPEN.md) 6.7% and [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md) 3.8%).
- **R/M enrichment of chromatin-remodeling alterations** (all R/M vs. primary, BH FDR<0.10):
  - [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md) 15.2% vs. 3.4% (OR 5.12, P=0.0001).
  - [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) / MLL3 14.3% vs. 4.0% (OR 4.06, P=0.0005).
  - [ARID1B](../genes/ARID1B.md) 14.1% vs. 4.0% (OR 4.00, P=0.0006).
  - [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) 13.7% vs. 2.3% (OR 6.87, P=0.0002).
  - [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md) 13.3% vs. 1.7% (OR 8.92, P=0.0002).
  - [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) / MLL2 12.8% vs. 4.5% (OR 3.10, P=0.0027).
  - [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md) 11.1% vs. 4.5% (OR 2.63, P=0.011).
- **Other R/M-enriched genes:** [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) 6.8% vs. 1.7% (OR 4.22, P=0.016); [LRP1B](../genes/LRP1B.md) 6.8% vs. 1.1% (OR 6.43, P=0.010).
- **[TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter:** Mutated in 13.1% of R/M cases. Mutually exclusive with NOTCH1 mutation (OR 0.62, q=3.3×10⁻⁴) and with MYB/MYBL1 fusion (OR 3.71 in fusion-negative cases, q=5.6×10⁻³; OR 4.60 in FISH-confirmed subset).
- **Co-occurrence with NOTCH1:** [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md) (OR 1.20, q=3.2×10⁻¹⁰), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) (OR 1.15, q=3.1×10⁻⁵), [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md) (OR 1.35, q=3.2×10⁻¹⁰), [EP300](../genes/EP300.md) — suggesting cooperativity between NOTCH1 and chromatin-remodeling alterations.
- **Four molecular subgroups** (defined by mutually exclusive MYB / NOTCH1 / TERT status, accounting for 53.2% (305/573) of R/M cases with data): MYB⁺NOTCH1⁺, MYB⁺/other, MYBᵂᵀTERT⁺, MYBᵂᵀNOTCH1⁺ — significantly different [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) (P<0.0001). MYBᵂᵀNOTCH1⁺ and MYB⁺NOTCH1⁺ subgroups had the worst prognosis.
- **NOTCH1-mutant survival:** Median OS 55.1 vs. 204.5 months (NOTCH1-mutant vs. [WT](../cancer_types/WT.md), P=1.10×10⁻⁴). Among NOTCH1-mutant cases, activating mutations conferred worse OS than non-activating (median 31.1 vs. 73.8 months, P=0.042).
- **KDM6A-mutant survival:** Median OS 48.5 vs. 169.3 months (KDM6A-mutant vs. WT, P=1.32×10⁻³).
- **MYB and TERT alone not prognostic:** MYB-NFIB (P=0.87) and TERT promoter (P=0.12) status did not significantly affect OS.
- **MYB/MYBL1 rearrangements:** 22.2% (129/581) of R/M tumors with available data; primarily detected via MYB intron-14 capture or FISH, so MYBᵂᵀ may include cryptic fusions.
- **Triple-negative subgroup:** 60.1% (160/266) of MYBᵂᵀNOTCH1ᵂᵀTERTᵂᵀ cases nonetheless harbored at least one chromatin-remodeling alteration.
- **Sequencing depth not a confounder:** Downsampling [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) BAMs to ×100 changed mean VAF by 0.011; only 1/101 enriched mutations was lost.
- **Intratumor genetic heterogeneity (ITH):**
  - 34.5% of 58 bulk-sequenced cases had ≥2 subclonal populations (SCPs), mean 1.5 SCPs/tumor; 6.9% had ≥3 SCPs — distribution similar to HR⁺ breast cancer.
  - Multiregion WES + ultra-deep targeted sequencing (mean ×5,406 primary, ×1,556 metastases) of one salivary ACC with 6 primary subregions + 8 lung metastases identified 36 unique mutations, only 7/36 present in all regions; 70% of metastatic mutations were detected in only 1–3 of 6 primary subregions and 4 mutations not detected in any primary subregion.
  - NMF on PyClone CCFs defined 3 clusters; the most divergent cluster (metastases 6D, 2B, 4A) carried subclonal [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md), [XDH](../genes/XDH.md), [LTF](../genes/LTF.md), and [TMEM2](../genes/TMEM2.md) mutations — genes previously implicated in metastasis.
  - In 2 breast ACCs transforming to high-grade triple-negative disease, MYB-NFIB and (in one case) NOTCH1 were clonal, while distinct subclonal mutations emerged in high-grade regions.
- **Germline:** 33/90 (36.7%) of MSKCC R/M cases carried ≥1 pathogenic germline variant; 38 pathogenic variants total (mean 0.42 per sample). 22.2% involved DNA-repair pathways; 4/90 (4.4%) had splicing-region variants in [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) (c.5152+20T>A, c.5406+8T>C, c.5332+3A>G) or [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) (c.9257–16T>C), all monoallelic on FACETS. 15/90 (16.7%) carried [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md) or [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md) germline variants, but all cases were microsatellite-stable on [MSIsensor](../methods/msisensor.md).
- **Tyrosine kinase landscape:** 40.3% (346/868) of R/M tumors harbored mutations in genes potentially targetable with available kinase inhibitors, although only 10.6% of alterations had OncoKB-supported clinical evidence.

## Genes & alterations

- [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) — Activating hot-spot mutations in HD and PEST domains (18.3% of R/M cases); 26.3% of R/M had any NOTCH1 alteration vs. 8.5% of primaries (OR 3.86, P<0.0001). Strongly associated with poor OS (median 55.1 vs. 204.5 months, P=1.10×10⁻⁴). Mutually exclusive with TERT promoter mutations; co-occurs with chromatin-remodeling alterations. Authors call activating NOTCH1 mutations "targetable with Notch-directed therapies such as γ secretase inhibitors" [PMID:31483290](../papers/31483290.md).
- [NOTCH2](../genes/NOTCH2.md), [NOTCH3](../genes/NOTCH3.md), [NOTCH4](../genes/NOTCH4.md) — Lower-prevalence Notch-family mutations also numerically enriched in R/M ([NOTCH4](../genes/NOTCH4.md) OR 6.59, P=0.067).
- [SPEN](../genes/SPEN.md) (6.7%), [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md) (3.8%) — Broader Notch-pathway activators mutated in R/M ACC.
- [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md) — Most-enriched chromatin-remodeling gene (15.2% R/M vs. 3.4% primary, OR 5.12). KDM6A-mutant OS 48.5 vs. 169.3 months (P=1.32×10⁻³).
- [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) (MLL3) — 14.3% R/M vs. 4.0% primary, OR 4.06.
- [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) (MLL2) — 12.8% R/M vs. 4.5% primary.
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [ARID1B](../genes/ARID1B.md) — SWI/SNF complex; both significantly enriched in R/M (OR 6.87 and 4.00).
- [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md) — Largest fold-change among chromatin genes (OR 8.92, P=0.0002).
- [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md), [EP300](../genes/EP300.md) — MYB transcriptional coactivators; co-occur with NOTCH1.
- [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) — DNA damage repair gene enriched in R/M (OR 4.22, P=0.016).
- [LRP1B](../genes/LRP1B.md) — Enriched in R/M (OR 6.43, P=0.010).
- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) — Promoter mutations in 13.1% of R/M cases, defining a distinct subgroup that is mutually exclusive with both NOTCH1 mutation and MYB/MYBL1 fusion. TERT promoter status alone not prognostic (P=0.12).
- [MYB](../genes/MYB.md) – [NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) fusion — Canonical ACC driver; confirmed clonal by FISH across primary and metastatic regions in multiregion case. MYB intron-14 rearrangement present in 22.2% of R/M cases with data; not associated with prognosis.
- [MYBL1](../genes/MYBL1.md) — Alternative ACC fusion partner; rearrangements likely under-counted (NGS panels did not capture MYBL1).
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — Hot-spot mutations in 8 MSK-IMPACT cases; 6 were enrolled on PI3K-inhibitor basket trials.
- [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md), [XDH](../genes/XDH.md), [LTF](../genes/LTF.md), [TMEM2](../genes/TMEM2.md) — Subclonal mutations identified in divergent metastatic cluster of the multiregion case, implicated in metastasis in other tumor types.
- [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) — Splicing-region germline variants in 4/90 R/M MSKCC cases; all monoallelic (no LOH).
- [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md) — Germline variants in 15/90 (16.7%) of cases, but all tumors microsatellite-stable.

## Clinical implications

- **Notch-directed therapy candidates:** ~18% of R/M ACCs carry activating NOTCH1 hot-spot mutations and are anticipated to be candidates for γ-secretase inhibitors; authors reference ongoing trials NCT03691207, NCT03422679, NCT01695005 [PMID:31483290](../papers/31483290.md).
- **PI3K-inhibitor activity in PIK3CA-mutant ACC:** Of 6 MSK-IMPACT patients with [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) hot-spot mutations enrolled on phase I basket trials of [taselisib](../drugs/taselisib.md) (β-sparing PI3K inhibitor, NCT01296555) or [alpelisib](../drugs/alpelisib.md) (α-specific PI3K inhibitor, NCT01928459), 1/6 achieved partial response and 5/6 had stable disease after 2 months; 5/6 had any tumor-volume reduction (mean 18.8%, range 4.8%–30.6%); 4/6 had PR or SD lasting >6 months. No clear genomic differences distinguished responders.
- **Multikinase inhibitors:** Authors cite prior phase II trials of [axitinib](../drugs/axitinib.md) (potential benefit in 4q12-amplified ACC) and [lenvatinib](../drugs/lenvatinib.md) (ORR 16%–27% in R/M ACC) as evidence that tyrosine-kinase inhibition may benefit subsets of ACC patients, while emphasizing the need for predictive biomarkers.
- **Prognostic biomarkers:** Activating NOTCH1 mutations and [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md) mutations each independently identify R/M ACC patients with markedly worse OS; MYB-NFIB and TERT promoter status do not.
- **Germline counseling:** Nearly one-third of MSKCC R/M ACC patients carried at least one pathogenic germline variant — most would not have been detected by guideline-based testing. Authors caution that BRCA1/2 splice variants identified were all monoallelic and of unclear clinical relevance, and that wider validation is needed before recommending universal germline analysis in ACC.

## Limitations & open questions

- ACC is rare, so cases were aggregated from heterogeneous platforms (WES, WGS, multiple targeted NGS panels), limiting analyses such as tumor mutational burden and uniform germline testing.
- Clinical data (stage, histology, type of metastasis, survival) were missing for many cases.
- MYB/MYBL1 status was primarily inferred from MYB intron-14 capture or FISH; cryptic MYBL1 fusions and other MYB breakpoints likely went undetected, meaning "MYBᵂᵀ" likely overcounts true fusion-negative cases. MYB overexpression can occur independently of detectable fusion.
- Germline analysis was limited to 90 anonymized MSKCC R/M cases; penetrance of these variants in salivary tissues is unknown, and ACC is otherwise considered noninheritable (only one potentially familial case reported).
- Multiregion clonality analyses were performed on a small number of patients (1 salivary multiregion case + 2 published breast ACC cases); broader validation of branching-evolution patterns in ACC is needed.
- Authors note that cooccurrence between NOTCH1 and chromatin-remodeling alterations could reflect treatment-induced selective pressure in R/M tumors, not solely biological synergy.
- Activity of γ-secretase inhibitors in NOTCH1-mutant ACC remained under active clinical investigation at publication and was not directly assessed here.
