## TL;DR

The Clinical Proteomic Tumor Analysis Consortium (CPTAC) applied deep multi-omics
proteogenomics — whole-exome sequencing, RNA-seq, miRNA-seq, and TMT-based global
proteomics with phosphoproteomic and acetylproteomic profiling — to 122
treatment-naive primary breast tumors prospectively collected under a stringent
short-ischemia protocol. Non-negative matrix factorization on integrated multi-omics
data resolved four robust subtypes (NMF LumA-I, LumB-I, HER2-I, Basal-I) that
refined PAM50 classification, identified pseudo-ERBB2+ tumors lacking the protein
target of anti-HER2 therapy, exposed APOBEC mutagenesis and single-strand break
repair defects as candidate biomarkers for immune checkpoint therapy in luminal
breast cancer, and showed that Rb protein/phosphoprotein status — rather than [RB1](../genes/RB1.md)
genotype alone — tracks CDK4/6 inhibitor sensitivity in TNBC.

## Cohort & data

- **n = 122 prospectively collected, treatment-naive primary breast tumors**
  (134 participants enrolled; ages 30–95). Cryopulverized, with co-isolation of
  DNA, RNA, and protein from a single homogeneous sample.
- **Cancer type**: invasive [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) (predominantly
  [IDC](../cancer_types/IDC.md); some [ILC](../cancer_types/ILC.md)),
  stage IIA–IIIC. Hormone-receptor classification by central IHC: ER and/or
  PR positive = 83, HER2 positive = 13, triple-negative = 16, ER/PR negative
  with equivocal HER2 = 12.
- **PAM50 distribution**: 14 HER2-E, 29 Basal, 57 LumA, 17 LumB, 5 Normal-like.
- **Dataset**: cBioPortal study [brca_cptac_2020](../datasets/brca_cptac_2020.md);
  raw genomics released to GDC under dbGaP phs000892 (GRCh38); proteomics at
  CPTAC accession S060 / PDC000120.
- **Comparator cohorts**: [METABRIC](../datasets/brca_metabric.md) (Curtis
  et al., 2012) for outcome validation of NMF luminal reassignment; the
  earlier TCGA-residue CPTAC [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) cohort
  ([Mertins et al. 2016](../datasets/brca_tcga_pub2015.md) sample set) for
  retrospective replication.
- **Assays/methods**: [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md)
  (Nextera Rapid Capture, 150× on-target), [rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md)
  (TruSeq stranded, ≥30M PE150 reads),
  [mirna-seq](../methods/mirna-seq.md), TMT-10-plex
  [global proteomics](../methods/tmt-proteomics.md) and
  [phospho/acetyl-proteomics](../methods/tmt-phosphoproteomics.md) following
  Mertins et al. 2018 protocol;
  [NMF multi-omics clustering](../methods/nmf-clustering.md),
  [PAM50](../methods/pam50.md), [GISTIC](../methods/gistic.md) for SCNAs,
  [GSEA](../methods/gsea.md), centralized
  [IHC](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md) (HER2 SP3, ER 6F11, CD3
  polyclonal) and [FISH](../methods/fish.md) for [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) status.
- **Data yield**: 29,647 somatic mutations, 23,692 gene-level copy number
  events, 23,121 transcripts, 10,107 proteins, 38,968 phosphosites, 9,869
  acetylation sites passing QC.

## Key findings

- **Integrated multi-omics NMF resolves four BRCA subtypes** (LumA-I, LumB-I,
  HER2-I, Basal-I) that broadly mirror PAM50 but reassign biology. NMF LumB-I
  includes a subset of PAM50 LumA samples with fewer
  [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) mutations (binomial p = 1.50 × 10⁻³ vs.
  LumA-I) and lower stromal scores; in METABRIC, these reassigned samples had
  outcomes intermediate between PAM50 LumA and LumB.
- **NMF Basal-I** is strongly enriched for [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations,
  hormone-receptor negativity, chromosome-instability and stemness scores,
  and proliferation pathways (E2F, G2/M).
- **NMF HER2-I is heterogeneous in ERBB2 status**: although centered on PAM50
  HER2-E and clinically [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md)+ tumors, it contains samples
  from every other PAM50 subtype. HER2-unrelated drivers of HER2-I clustering
  enriched for endoplasmic-reticulum proteins and cholesterol/sterol
  biosynthesis (independent of ERBB2 amplification).
- **Pseudo-ERBB2+ tumors**: 2 of 17 ERBB2-amplified cases in the prospective
  cohort (and 1 of 16 in the retrospective Mertins 2016 cohort) had ERBB2
  protein levels within the distribution of ERBB2-negative tumors, despite
  exome-level amplification. One pseudo-ERBB2+ case in each cohort instead
  showed [TOP2A](../genes/TOP2A.md) amplification with [TOP2A](../genes/TOP2A.md) protein
  overexpression, supporting TOP2A as an alternative chromosome-17 amplicon
  driver. Only 7 of 15 proteogenomically ERBB2+ samples were PAM50 HER2-E;
  PAM50 HER2-E samples that were not ERBB2 PG+ had elevated phosphorylation
  of other ERBB family members and MAPK signaling.
- **Subtype-specific phospho-kinase outliers** recovered via the Black-Sheep
  package (FDR < 0.01): [PRKDC](../genes/PRKDC.md), [MAP4K4](../genes/MAP4K4.md),
  and [SPEG](../genes/SPEG.md) in NMF Basal-I; [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) and
  [CDK12](../genes/CDK12.md) in NMF HER2-I; [DCLK1](../genes/DCLK1.md) in NMF
  LumA-I.
- **Tumor-suppressor-loss to targetable-kinase associations** (novel):
  upregulated phospho-[TNIK](../genes/TNIK.md) (WNT pathway) in
  [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) mutants; upregulated phospho-[RIPK3](../genes/RIPK3.md)
  in [MAP3K1](../genes/MAP3K1.md) mutants; high phospho-[MAST4](../genes/MAST4.md)
  and phospho-[DCLK1](../genes/DCLK1.md) in [GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md) mutants;
  elevated phospho-SLK/LATS1 in [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md)-mutated tumors
  (mTOR / HIPPO cross-talk).
- **Metabolism/acetylproteomics**: NMF Basal-I tumors show upregulated
  glycolytic protein levels (HK3, PFKP, GAPDH, ENO1, [LDHB](../genes/LDHB.md)) but
  hypoacetylation of glycolytic enzymes (GPI, TPI1, GAPDH, PGK1, PGAM1, ENO1,
  [PKM](../genes/PKM.md), [LDHA](../genes/LDHA.md)), upregulated serine biosynthesis ([PHGDH](../genes/PHGDH.md),
  PSAT1), and broad hyperacetylation of mitochondrial TCA-cycle and β-oxidation
  proteins. Protein levels of the mitochondrial deacetylase
  [SIRT3](../genes/SIRT3.md) were significantly negatively correlated with
  mitochondrial protein acetylation, suggesting [SIRT3](../genes/SIRT3.md) depletion as a driver of
  Basal-I mitochondrial hyperacetylation. NMF HER2-I tumors uniquely show
  upregulated cholesterol biosynthesis and lipid metabolism *independent* of
  ERBB2 amplification status.
- **APOBEC mutagenesis correlates with active immune microenvironment in
  luminal BRCA**: 6 APOBEC-enriched cases (1 HER2-E, 3 LumA, 2 LumB) had
  higher mutation loads (Wilcoxon p = 0.008) and elevated
  [APOBEC3B](../genes/APOBEC3B.md) protein.
  [APOBEC3G](../genes/APOBEC3G.md) protein correlated with PD-L1
  ([CD274](../genes/CD274.md)) mRNA and CIBERSORT immune score in both
  PAM50 luminal and basal samples.
- **Single-strand break repair (SSBR) protein loss is anti-correlated with
  PD-L1 and immune infiltration specifically in luminal but not basal BRCA**
  (GO BP nucleotide excision repair, base excision repair, mismatch repair
  pathways). Low GO NER protein levels associated with high mutation load
  in luminal samples only.
- **Rb status refines CDK4/6 inhibitor prediction**: in HR+/ERBB2− tumors,
  Rb protein (Spearman ρ = 0.24, p = 0.035) and median Rb phosphosite
  (ρ = 0.53, p = 1.06 × 10⁻⁶) correlated *positively* with the multi-gene
  proliferation score; in TNBC they correlated *negatively* (ρ = −0.54,
  p = 0.003 protein; ρ = −0.46, p = 0.015 phospho). In TNBC, Rb-low vs.
  Rb-high phospho-protein groups (n = 16 vs. n = 12) tracked
  [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md)/[CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md) activity; 3 of 4
  TNBC tumors with [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) mutation/deletion were
  Rb-phospho low. In GDSC cell-line data, RB1-wild-type lines with low Rb
  protein were poorly responsive to [palbociclib](../drugs/palbociclib.md)
  (Rb protein ~ [palbociclib](../drugs/palbociclib.md) response: Spearman ρ = −0.61, p = 0.022),
  arguing that protein-level Rb assessment adds predictive value beyond RB1
  genotype.

## Genes & alterations

- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) — gene amplification confirmed by WES;
  IHC/FISH per ASCO-CAP. 15/122 tumors classified proteogenomically as ERBB2+;
  pseudo-ERBB2+ phenotype (amplified but protein-low) identified in 2/17
  amplified cases. ERBB2 phospho-kinase activity enriches NMF HER2-I.
- [TOP2A](../genes/TOP2A.md) — alternative chromosome-17q amplicon driver in
  pseudo-ERBB2+ cases.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — mutations strongly enriched in NMF Basal-I and
  TNBC; 9 of 10 BL1 TNBC samples carried [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations.
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — mutations/deletions in TNBC are associated with
  Rb-phospho-low state and palbociclib non-response; however, many TNBC
  tumors show Rb protein loss without RB1 genomic aberration.
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — less frequent in NMF LumB-I than NMF LumA-I
  (binomial p = 1.50 × 10⁻³); recurrent in LAR-subtype TNBC.
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) mutations associate with elevated
  phospho-[TNIK](../genes/TNIK.md) (WNT pathway).
- [MAP3K1](../genes/MAP3K1.md) loss-of-function in luminal BRCA associates
  with elevated phospho-[RIPK3](../genes/RIPK3.md).
- [GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md) mutations associate with elevated phospho-
  [MAST4](../genes/MAST4.md) and phospho-[DCLK1](../genes/DCLK1.md)
  (novel — requires validation).
- [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) mutations associate with elevated phospho-SLK and
  phospho-LATS1.
- [APOBEC3B](../genes/APOBEC3B.md) / [APOBEC3G](../genes/APOBEC3G.md) protein
  levels track APOBEC mutagenesis signatures (COSMIC SBS2/SBS13) and
  PD-L1 / immune-infiltration scores in luminal BRCA.
- [PHGDH](../genes/PHGDH.md), PSAT1 (serine synthesis) — upregulated at the
  protein level in NMF Basal-I; nominated as therapeutic vulnerability.
- [SIRT3](../genes/SIRT3.md) — depletion proposed as a driver of broad
  mitochondrial protein hyperacetylation in NMF Basal-I.
- [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md), [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md),
  [CDK2](../genes/CDK2.md), [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md),
  [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md) — Cyclin D1 higher in HR+/ERBB2−; Cyclin E /
  [CDK2](../genes/CDK2.md) higher in TNBC ([CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md) RNA p = 6.2 × 10⁻¹², phosphoprotein
  p = 7.3 × 10⁻¹⁰); CDK4/6 activity tracks proliferation in HR+ but not TNBC.
- [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md) — elevated activation-loop acetylation (K1591/K1592)
  in nuclear-Ac Basal-I cluster 1, accompanying broad histone-H2B
  N-terminal acetylation.

## Clinical implications

- **HER2 diagnostics**: integrated DNA + protein quantification of the
  chromosome-17 amplicon is proposed as a more quantitative alternative to
  IHC/FISH; pseudo-ERBB2+ status could explain
  [trastuzumab](../drugs/trastuzumab.md)-refractory amplified tumors.
- **Immune checkpoint therapy**: subsets of luminal A/B BRCA with APOBEC
  mutagenesis or SSBR (NER/BER/MMR) defects display active I-TMEs and high
  PD-L1, supporting immunotherapy trials in endocrine-resistant ER+ BRCA.
- **CDK4/6 inhibitors**: proteogenomic Rb status (protein + phospho) refines
  candidate selection for CDK4/6i, both in HR+/ERBB2− and in a subset of
  intact-Rb TNBC; cell-line evidence (GDSC) supports clinical evaluation of
  [palbociclib](../drugs/palbociclib.md) in RB1-wild-type, Rb-protein-high
  TNBC.
- **Targeting tumor-suppressor loss via downstream kinases**: [TNIK](../genes/TNIK.md) inhibition
  in ARID1A-mutant BRCA, [DCLK1](../genes/DCLK1.md) inhibition (e.g., LRRK2-IN-1) in GATA3-mutant
  or LumA tumors, and consideration of necroptosis-related signaling in
  MAP3K1-mutant luminal BRCA.
- **Metabolic vulnerabilities**: serine-synthesis ([PHGDH](../genes/PHGDH.md)) inhibition for
  basal tumors; SIRT3-modulated mitochondrial acetylation as a candidate
  axis (therapeutic direction ambiguous given dual oncogene/TS roles).

## Limitations & open questions

- Prospective accrual prioritized data quality over demographic representation;
  cohort may not be optimized for subgroup analyses.
- Bulk cryopulverized tissue sacrifices architectural and single-cell
  resolution; integration with imaging mass cytometry or microscaled
  workflows (Satpathy et al. 2020, DP1 cohort) is needed.
- Associations are hypothesis-generating; therapeutic claims (TNIK, DCLK1,
  [MAST4](../genes/MAST4.md), PHGDH, SIRT3, APOBEC-immune link, Rb-CDK4/6) need functional and
  clinical validation.
- N = 122 limits statistical power for rare-event subsets (e.g., n = 6
  APOBEC-enriched, n = 2 pseudo-ERBB2+ in the prospective cohort).
- Equivocal HER2 cases per ASCO-CAP could not all be resolved due to
  unavailable repeat FISH.
- SIRT3 has both tumor-suppressor and oncogenic roles in the literature;
  whether to inhibit or activate it for therapeutic benefit remains open.
