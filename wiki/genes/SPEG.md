# SPEG

## Overview

SPEG (SPEG Complex Locus) encodes a serine/threonine kinase involved in muscle development and calcium homeostasis. In cancer genomics, phosphoproteomic profiling has identified SPEG as a subtype-specific kinase outlier in breast cancer, with elevated phospho-activity in basal-like tumors.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Identified as a phospho-kinase outlier specifically in NMF Basal-I (TNBC) breast tumors (FDR < 0.01) via Black-Sheep analysis in the CPTAC breast proteogenomic cohort (n=122 primary breast tumors); co-identified with [PRKDC](../genes/PRKDC.md) and [MAP4K4](../genes/MAP4K4.md) as Basal-I-specific outlier kinases [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md)**: Elevated phospho-SPEG is a subtype-specific phosphoproteomic feature of NMF Basal-I/TNBC breast cancer; significance for tumor biology or therapy not yet established [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Phospho-SPEG outlier status co-occurs with phospho-PRKDC and phospho-MAP4K4 elevation in the NMF Basal-I breast cancer subtype [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic implications described; SPEG as a Basal-I kinase outlier is hypothesis-generating and requires functional validation [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md).

## Open questions

- Whether elevated SPEG phospho-activity is functionally relevant to TNBC biology or represents a passenger phosphorylation event is unknown [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md).
