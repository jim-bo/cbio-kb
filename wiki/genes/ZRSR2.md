# ZRSR2

## Overview

ZRSR2 (Zinc Finger CCCH-Type, RNA Binding Motif And Serine/Arginine Rich 2) encodes an essential splicing factor that participates in the recognition of 3' splice sites during pre-mRNA splicing. Somatic loss-of-function mutations in ZRSR2 are recurrently observed in myeloid malignancies, where they define part of the chromatin-spliceosome genomic subgroup in acute myeloid leukemia ([AML](../cancer_types/AML.md)) and are associated with adverse prognosis.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Splicing factor mutation in AML; part of the chromatin-spliceosome subgroup alongside [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md), [U2AF1](../genes/U2AF1.md), and [SRSF2](../genes/SRSF2.md); subgroup confers adverse prognosis with 84% of patients reclassified from ELN intermediate to adverse risk based on observed outcomes [PMID:27276561](../papers/27276561.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md) — recurrent mutation in the chromatin-spliceosome genomic subgroup; associated with adverse prognosis and ELN risk reclassification [PMID:27276561](../papers/27276561.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with SF3B1, U2AF1, SRSF2 (other splicing factors) and chromatin regulators [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md), [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md), [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md), [PHF6](../genes/PHF6.md), [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) PTD, [ASXL1](../genes/ASXL1.md), and [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md) in the AML chromatin-spliceosome subgroup [PMID:27276561](../papers/27276561.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No approved targeted therapies. The chromatin-spliceosome subgroup defined by ZRSR2 and co-occurring mutations represents a high-risk AML population for whom allogeneic transplantation may be preferred based on adverse outcome data [PMID:27276561](../papers/27276561.md)

## Open questions

- ZRSR2 is X-linked; the functional consequences of hemizygous versus homozygous loss and potential sex-specific effects in AML require further study.
