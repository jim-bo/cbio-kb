# Atypical Choroid Plexus Papilloma (ACPP)

## Overview

Atypical choroid plexus papilloma (ACPP) is a rare intracranial tumor of the choroid plexus with intermediate grade between choroid plexus papilloma and choroid plexus carcinoma. In the OncoTree hierarchy it sits under CPT (Choroid Plexus Tumor) in the CNS/Brain tissue. In pediatric brain tumor studies it is often grouped under the broader "craniopharyngioma" ([CP](../genes/CP.md)) umbrella, but OncoTree code ACPP specifically denotes the adamantinomatous craniopharyngioma variant per cBioPortal usage. It is distinct from papillary craniopharyngioma (PPCP).

## Cohorts in the corpus

- [brain_cptac_2020](../datasets/brain_cptac_2020.md) — 16 craniopharyngioma (CP) samples in a 218-sample pediatric brain tumor proteogenomic cohort from Children's Hospital of Philadelphia / CBTTC. [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md)

## Recurrent alterations

- [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) mutation elevates β-catenin abundance and trans-regulates [APC](../genes/APC.md), GSK3A/GSK3B, and [TCF25](../genes/TCF25.md) (RNA and protein); phosphosite APC Ser2812 elevated. [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) mutation status did not distinguish the two CP proteomic clusters. [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md)
- A subset of pediatric CP (C4 cluster) clustered proteomically with [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)V600E [LGG](../cancer_types/LGG.md) despite lacking BRAFV600E mutation, showing elevated MEK/ERK phosphorylation (ERK1/2) and AKT/mTOR activation — not detectable from RNA data. [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md)

## Subtypes

- **C4 (Cranio/LGG-BRAFV600E) proteomic cluster** — shares MEK/ERK activation signature with BRAFV600E LGG at protein/phosphoprotein level despite BRAFV600E-WT genotype; AKT/mTOR pathway upregulated. [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md)
- **C8 (LGG BRAFWT-rich) proteomic cluster** — remaining CP cases; distinct from C4 at phosphoproteomic level. [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md)
- **Epithelial microenvironment cluster** — entirely composed of CP tumors; upregulates [CTLA4](../genes/CTLA4.md) and PD-1, supporting prior reports of potential immune-checkpoint benefit. [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md)

## Therapeutic landscape

- MEK inhibitor therapy proposed for C4 CP subset based on proteomic MEK/ERK activation paralleling BRAFV600E LGG; combined MEK + mTOR inhibition also supported by AKT1/AKT2 upregulation data. [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md)
- Immune-checkpoint inhibition (anti-CTLA4, anti-PD-1) supported by the Epithelial microenvironment cluster upregulating CTLA4 and PD-1 in CP. [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md)
