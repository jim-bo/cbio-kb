# SLC27A5

## Overview

SLC27A5, also known as fatty acid transport protein 5 (FATP5), is a liver-specific membrane fatty acid transporter. In hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC), SLC27A5 has a contradictory, context-dependent role: loss protects against high-fat-diet steatosis, yet SLC27A5 deficiency can also promote HCC progression via KEAP1/NRF2 and AKT/mTOR pathway activation. SLC27A5 also serves as a non-canonical metastasis suppressor through regulation of PIP4K2A pre-mRNA splicing. Additionally, SLC27A5 can promote sorafenib-induced ferroptosis through glutathione reductase regulation, further illustrating its complex, context-dependent biology.

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Interacts with NRF2/KEAP1 and AKT/mTOR signaling networks; functionally distinct from [SLC27A4](../genes/SLC27A4.md) (FATP4) which selectively imports MUFA for ferroptosis protection [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Context-dependent roles of SLC27A5 complicate therapeutic targeting; its role in promoting sorafenib-induced ferroptosis may be exploitable but requires etiology stratification [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Open questions

- Contradictory findings on SLC27A5 in HCC — tumor-suppressive vs. oncogenic roles depending on context — remain unresolved; the review flags this explicitly as a conflict [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)
