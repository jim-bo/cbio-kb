## TL;DR

Tsui et al. profiled plasma cell-free DNA (cfDNA) from 118 patients with metastatic solid tumors at MSKCC using a tumor fraction-guided, multi-assay strategy. Each sample was analyzed in parallel by cf-IMPACT (a plasma version of the 410-gene MSK-IMPACT panel) and by shallow whole genome sequencing (sWGS) to estimate cfDNA tumor fraction via genome-wide z-scores. Samples negative on cf-IMPACT were triaged by tumor fraction: low z-score (<5) samples went to the more sensitive ~129-gene MSK-ACCESS assay, while high z-score (≥5) samples went to whole exome sequencing (WES). cf-IMPACT alone detected mutations in 71/118 (60%) patients; the three-assay strategy raised this to 90/118 (76%), with clinically actionable alterations identified in 25% of the cohort.

## Cohort & data

- **N = 118** patients with metastatic solid tumors enrolled at [Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center](../datasets/mixed_cfdna_msk_2020.md) under IRB protocol NCT01775072.
- Tumor types included [bladder cancer (BLCA)](../cancer_types/BLCA.md), [prostate cancer (PRAD)](../cancer_types/PRAD.md), [breast cancer (BRCA)](../cancer_types/BRCA.md), [testicular cancer (TGCT)](../cancer_types/TGCT.md), [melanoma (MEL)](../cancer_types/MEL.md), and [non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC)](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md).
- Matched tumor MSK-IMPACT data available for 76/118 patients; remaining 42 had no available tumor data or had failed tumor profiling.
- **Assays** (linked):
  - [cf-IMPACT (IMPACT410)](../methods/IMPACT410.md) — plasma version of MSK-IMPACT, 410 cancer-associated genes, ~631× depth, designed to detect known mutations at 1% VAF (genotyping) or de novo mutations at 2% (hotspot) / 5% (non-hotspot) VAF.
  - [MSK-ACCESS (ACCESS129)](../methods/ACCESS129.md) — ultra-deep (>12,000× target), UMI error-corrected, 129 cancer-associated genes (~13% of cf-IMPACT footprint), detection down to 0.1% VAF.
  - [Whole exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) of cfDNA (cf-WES) to mean depth 384× on the NimbleGen SeqCap Exome platform.
  - [Shallow whole genome sequencing](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) (sWGS, ~10M reads/sample), analyzed with Plasma-Seq z-score statistics and [ichorCNA](../methods/ichorcna.md).
  - [MSIsensor](../methods/msisensor.md) for MSI status, [FACETS](../methods/facets.md) for tumor clonality (CCF), [OncoKB](../methods/oncokb-annotation.md) for actionability annotation.
- Plasma data also publicly available on cBioPortal as study `mixed_cfdna_msk_2020`; raw WGS/WES deposited in dbGaP under accession **phs002290.v1.p1**.

## Key findings

- **Tumor fraction stratification works.** sWGS-based genome-wide z-scores were significantly higher in patients with mutations detected by cf-IMPACT (mean 7.91; range 0.106–34.2) than those without mutations detected (mean 2.14; range 0.0178–17.9; Mann-Whitney p = 5.6e−09), and both groups were much higher than healthy blood donors (mean 0.026; p = 4.7e−10 vs negative-mutation cancer patients).
- **z-score outperformed fragment size for tumor-fraction prediction.** Genome-wide z-score from sWGS achieved AUC = 0.925 for predicting high (≥10% mVAF) tumor fraction; the short/long fragment-size ratio (0–150 bp / 151–500 bp) alone achieved AUC = 0.828; combining both gave AUC = 0.928 (no meaningful gain over z-score alone).
- **cf-IMPACT detection rate scales with tumor fraction.** In the 76 patients with matched tumor data, cf-IMPACT detected somatic mutations in 55/76 (72%). 22/25 (88%) samples with mVAF ≥10% had z-score ≥5; 46/51 (90%) samples with mVAF <10% had z-score <5.
- **Mutational concordance is far higher in high-tumor-fraction plasma.** Tumor-plasma shared-mutation fraction was 207/283 (73%) in z-score ≥5 samples vs 160/320 (50%) in z-score <5 samples (Fisher exact p = 6.72e−09). The effect held for both clonal (96% vs 64%, p = 5.78e−08) and sub-clonal mutations (81% vs 30%, p = 1.03e−13).
- **TMB concordance also tracks tumor fraction.** Tumor-vs-plasma TMB correlation was 0.894 (p = 1.7e−09, slope 0.929) in z-score ≥5 samples vs 0.476 (p = 0.0079, slope 0.525) in z-score <5 samples. Of 15 patients with high tumor TMB (≥10 mut/Mb), 11 were also high-TMB on cf-IMPACT; the 4 discordant samples all had z-score <5.
- **De novo plasma profiling without tumor knowledge.** Among the 42 patients without informative tumor data, cf-IMPACT identified mutations in 16/42 (38%): 11/25 (44%) of patients with no tumor available or failed tumor profiling, and 5/17 (29%) of patients whose prior tumor MSK-IMPACT had been mutation-negative.
- **Overall yield.** Across the full cohort, cf-IMPACT identified somatic mutations in 71/118 (60%) patients and OncoKB level 1–4 actionable variants in 30/118 (25%).
- **MSK-ACCESS rescued low-tumor-fraction negatives.** Of the 47 cf-IMPACT-negative samples, 42 had z-score <5; 29 had sufficient material for [MSK-ACCESS](../methods/ACCESS129.md), which detected 19 high-confidence mutations in 14/29 (48%) patients (median VAF 0.49%, range 0.05–3.64%). 7/19 (34%) of these mutations were OncoKB-actionable.
- **cf-WES rescued high-tumor-fraction negatives.** Of the 47 cf-IMPACT-negative samples, 5 had z-score ≥5. cf-WES detected somatic mutations in all 5 (average 212, range 169–268 mutations; average mVAF 11%, range 8.1–13.4%); **99% of these mutations fell outside the cf-IMPACT panel footprint**, and 13.1% were in genes mutated in the matching TCGA cohort. Predominant tumor mutational signatures were recoverable in plasma.
- **Combined yield.** Tumor-fraction-guided triage across cf-IMPACT + MSK-ACCESS + cf-WES identified mutations in **90/118 (76%) patients** and oncogenic / likely-oncogenic mutations in 19/34 (43%) of cf-IMPACT-negative samples that were re-analyzed.

## Genes & alterations

- [AR](../genes/AR.md) — sub-clonal **AR p.H875Y** (hormonal-therapy resistance) detected in plasma of an mCRPC patient by cf-IMPACT; subsequent tumor biopsy 6 days later confirmed the mutation at 0.3% VAF in tissue, below the de novo calling threshold of tumor MSK-IMPACT.
- [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) — OncoKB level 1 alterations identified in plasma of prostate cancer patients, supporting [olaparib](../drugs/olaparib.md) (PARP inhibitor) eligibility.
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — OncoKB level 1 alterations identified in plasma of breast cancer patients, supporting [alpelisib](../drugs/alpelisib.md) eligibility.
- [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) — **ESR1 p.E380Q** (1.7% VAF) detected by MSK-ACCESS in a heavily pre-treated metastatic breast cancer patient whose tumor MSK-IMPACT and cf-IMPACT were both negative; previously associated with hormonal-therapy resistance. Evidence was present in the cf-IMPACT data below the assay's detection threshold.
- [IRF1](../genes/IRF1.md) — likely oncogenic frameshift deletion in the tumor suppressor IRF1 identified by cf-WES in a prostate cancer patient, in a region not covered by cf-IMPACT.
- [EP400](../genes/EP400.md) — likely oncogenic frameshift deletion identified by cf-WES in a urothelial cancer patient; encodes a NuA4 histone acetyltransferase complex component, not covered by cf-IMPACT.

## Clinical implications

- **Triage logic.** A negative cf-IMPACT (or comparable targeted panel) result should be interpreted in the context of cfDNA tumor fraction: if tumor fraction is high, the lesion is genuinely outside the panel footprint and broader sequencing (WES) is warranted; if tumor fraction is low, a deeper but narrower assay (MSK-ACCESS) can recover sub-threshold variants.
- **MSI-High discovery in plasma.** A metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer patient with two failed tumor biopsies had MSI-High and high TMB called from cf-IMPACT alone; a later bone biopsy confirmed MSI status, leading to [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md) therapy and a durable response (serum PSA dropped from 118 to <10, sustained >1 year).
- **Actionable yield.** OncoKB level 1–4 variants identified in 25% (30/118) of the cohort by cf-IMPACT alone, including therapeutic targets of FDA-approved drugs ([olaparib](../drugs/olaparib.md) for BRCA2-mutant mCRPC, [alpelisib](../drugs/alpelisib.md) for PIK3CA-mutant breast cancer, [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md) for MSI-High tumors).
- **Sub-clonal resistance detection.** cfDNA detected the AR p.H875Y resistance allele below the calling threshold of tumor MSK-IMPACT, illustrating that plasma can surface sub-clonal driver mutations missed by single-site biopsy.

## Limitations & open questions

- Plasma and tumor samples were collected **retrospectively**, with variable clinical status, treatment, and tumor-to-plasma collection time intervals.
- Predictive value of sWGS- or fragment-size-based tumor-fraction estimates may vary by **tumor type**; the paper calls for prospective, disease-specific studies.
- 30–40% of tumors in this cohort (and reported literature) had few copy number alterations, in which case the genome-wide z-score from sWGS may underestimate tumor fraction. Fragment-size profiles are independent of tumor copy-number state but were less predictive on their own.
- The triage workflow assumes sufficient residual cfDNA for follow-up assays; in this cohort only 29/47 cf-IMPACT-negative samples had enough leftover DNA for MSK-ACCESS.
- Generalization to commercial cfDNA panels (often <500 kb and even more focused than cf-IMPACT) is plausible but not directly tested.
