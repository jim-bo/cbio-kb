# sorafenib

## Overview

Sorafenib is an oral multi-kinase inhibitor with FDA approvals in hepatocellular carcinoma, differentiated thyroid carcinoma, and renal cell carcinoma. In osteosarcoma it is an NCCN-listed option for relapsed/refractory disease.

## Evidence in the corpus

- In the UCLA PDTO sarcoma screen, sorafenib was one of the recurrent NCCN-listed top-five regimens identified for [osteosarcoma](../cancer_types/OS.md) samples, appearing as part of the actionable backbone for this histology alongside [regorafenib](../drugs/regorafenib.md), [etoposide](../drugs/etoposide.md), [cisplatin](../drugs/cisplatin.md), [doxorubicin](../drugs/doxorubicin.md), [cabozantinib](../drugs/cabozantinib.md), [gemcitabine](../drugs/gemcitabine.md), [docetaxel](../drugs/docetaxel.md), and [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md). [PMID:39305899](../papers/39305899.md)
- PDTOs from patients with rapidly progressing tumors at follow-up showed increased sensitivity to sorafenib (p=0.042) compared to non-rapidly-progressing specimens. [PMID:39305899](../papers/39305899.md)
- A narrative review of lipid metabolism in [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) identifies sorafenib resistance as mechanistically linked to FATP4 ([SLC27A4](../genes/SLC27A4.md))-mediated MUFA import that protects cells from lipid peroxidation/ferroptosis, and to enhanced de novo lipogenesis with increased lipid-droplet formation in sorafenib-resistant cells. Targeting these axes (e.g., FATP4 inhibition, [SCD](../genes/SCD.md) inhibition, GPX4/SLC7A11 inhibition) is proposed as a strategy to re-sensitize resistant HCC. [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Resistance mechanisms

- FATP4 (SLC27A4)-mediated MUFA import protects HCC cells from sorafenib-induced lipid peroxidation/ferroptosis; enhanced de novo lipogenesis and lipid-droplet formation are observed in sorafenib-resistant HCC cells. [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md)
- [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) — hepatocellular carcinoma; lipid-metabolism axes (FATP4, SCD, GPX4/SLC7A11, DNL) drive resistance; real-world ORR ~16–23% ([lenvatinib](../drugs/lenvatinib.md) reference).
