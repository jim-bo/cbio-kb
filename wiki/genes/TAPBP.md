# TAPBP

## Overview

TAPBP (TAP Binding Protein), also known as Tapasin, encodes a chaperone protein that bridges the TAP transporter to MHC class I molecules during peptide loading in the endoplasmic reticulum. TAPBP is an essential component of the peptide-loading complex (PLC), and its loss impairs efficient MHC class I surface expression and antigen presentation.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Component of the MHC class I antigen-processing machinery (APM); enriched for somatic mutations in TIL-rich colorectal tumors, consistent with immune-escape selection pressure in tumors with high neoantigen load, in a cohort of 619 CRC cases [PMID:27149842](../papers/27149842.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md) / Colorectal cancer:** Somatic mutations in APM components including TAPBP are enriched in TIL-rich tumors, suggesting adaptive immune-escape selection [PMID:27149842](../papers/27149842.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-mutated alongside other APM components ([TAP1](../genes/TAP1.md), [TAP2](../genes/TAP2.md), [B2M](../genes/B2M.md), [CALR](../genes/CALR.md), [CANX](../genes/CANX.md), [HSPA5](../genes/HSPA5.md), [PDIA3](../genes/PDIA3.md)) in TIL-rich colorectal tumors [PMID:27149842](../papers/27149842.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- APM gene mutations including TAPBP are candidate adaptive resistance mechanisms to immune-checkpoint blockade; functional loss of tapasin reduces peptide-MHC-I stability and could reduce T-cell recognition of tumor antigens [PMID:27149842](../papers/27149842.md).

## Open questions

- Whether TAPBP mutations confer primary or acquired resistance to checkpoint inhibitors in colorectal cancer was not tested in the citing study [PMID:27149842](../papers/27149842.md).
