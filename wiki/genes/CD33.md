# CD33

## Overview

CD33 (also known as SIGLEC3) encodes a sialic acid-binding immunoglobulin-type lectin expressed on myeloid progenitor cells and acute myeloid leukemia ([AML](../cancer_types/AML.md)) blasts. CD33 is the antigen targeted by gemtuzumab ozogamicin (GO), the only approved antibody-drug conjugate for AML. In the context of AML cell-state biology, CD33 expression and gemtuzumab ozogamicin response are understood against the background of differentiation-state-driven drug sensitivity.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- CD33 is the antigen of gemtuzumab ozogamicin; noted in background context of the Beat AML Waves 3+4 study as part of the therapeutic landscape for AML (ex vivo drug sensitivity study, n=805 patients) [PMID:35868306](../papers/35868306.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[AML](../cancer_types/AML.md):** CD33 is uniformly expressed on AML blasts and is the clinical target of gemtuzumab ozogamicin; CD33-directed therapy is evaluated in the context of AML cell-state (differentiation state) biology [PMID:35868306](../papers/35868306.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- No specific co-mutation or exclusivity data reported in this corpus; CD33 surface expression is relevant across multiple AML mutation contexts [PMID:35868306](../papers/35868306.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Gemtuzumab ozogamicin (GO) targets CD33-positive AML blasts; approved by FDA for CD33-positive AML; the Beat AML resource ([aml_ohsu_2022](../datasets/aml_ohsu_2022.md)) provides cell-state context that may inform GO response heterogeneity [PMID:35868306](../papers/35868306.md)

## Open questions

- Whether AML maturation cell state (HSC-like, GMP-like, Monocyte-like, etc.) modulates gemtuzumab ozogamicin response beyond CD33 surface expression levels is not addressed in the corpus [PMID:35868306](../papers/35868306.md)
