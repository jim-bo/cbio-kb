# Lung Neuroendocrine Tumor (LNET)

## Overview

Lung Neuroendocrine Tumor (LNET) encompasses the spectrum of pulmonary neuroendocrine neoplasms, including typical and atypical carcinoids, which are low-to-intermediate-grade tumors arising from neuroendocrine cells of the lung. LNET is the parent OncoTree node for [SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md) (Small Cell Lung Cancer). Carcinoids express neuroendocrine markers (OTP, [DLL3](../genes/DLL3.md), [SEZ6](../genes/SEZ6.md)) and are characterized by genomic alterations in [MEN1](../genes/MEN1.md), [EIF1AX](../genes/EIF1AX.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), and [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) — a landscape distinct from high-grade [SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md).

## Cohorts in the corpus

- 157 pulmonary carcinoids (typical + atypical) profiled by [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) at MSKCC, used as a comparator cohort in the aSCLC genomic study; 50 atypical carcinoids included for survival analysis. [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md)

## Recurrent alterations

- [MEN1](../genes/MEN1.md) mutations in 15% of atypical SCLC (aSCLC) — a frequency enriched relative to typical SCLC, reflecting the carcinoid-like mutational background of aSCLC. [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md)
- [EIF1AX](../genes/EIF1AX.md) mutations in 10% of aSCLC — carcinoid-associated alteration. [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md)
- [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) mutations in 8% of carcinoids (vs. 30% in aSCLC, P=0.008, and 3% in smoking SCLC, P=0.0003); enrichment of ATM in aSCLC suggests carcinoid-like ancestry. [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md)
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) mutations enriched in aSCLC relative to conventional SCLC, consistent with carcinoid-associated genomic landscape. [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md)

## Subtypes

- Typical carcinoid (low-grade) and atypical carcinoid (intermediate-grade) are the canonical LNET subtypes.
- A subset of atypical SCLC (aSCLC, [RB1](../genes/RB1.md)+/[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)+) shows histologic overlap with LNET: 5/20 aSCLC patients had carcinoid histotype in their primary tumor and SCLC histotype in metastases; 55% showed at least one line of evidence for a carcinoid relationship (OTP expression or co-expressed carcinoid markers). This overlap suggests carcinoid-to-SCLC plasticity driven by chromothripsis in never/light smokers. [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md)

## Therapeutic landscape

- High [DLL3](../genes/DLL3.md) and [SEZ6](../genes/SEZ6.md) expression (mean H-scores 278 and 240) is shared between aSCLC and LNET, positioning carcinoid-lineage tumors as candidates for DLL3- and SEZ6-targeted therapies. [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md)
- Median overall survival for atypical carcinoids (LNET) is 184 months, markedly longer than aSCLC (58 months) or conventional SCLC (16 months). [PMID:39185963](../papers/39185963.md)
