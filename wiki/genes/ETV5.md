# ETV5

## Overview

ETV5 is an ETS family transcription factor and a known transcriptional target of CIC (capicua transcriptional repressor). In oligodendroglioma, where CIC loss-of-function mutations are recurrent, ETV5 derepression serves as a functional readout of CIC inactivation.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- ETV5 expression is upregulated in cells carrying a subclonal CIC loss-of-function mutation (~30% VAF) in the MGH53 oligodendroglioma; the increased ETV5 (and [ETV1](../genes/ETV1.md)) expression in CIC-mutant cells is consistent with derepression of known CIC target genes, and this mutation spans all three developmental compartments (stem/progenitor, oligodendrocyte-like, astrocyte-like) [PMID:27806376](../papers/27806376.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [ODG](../cancer_types/ODG.md) — ETV5 derepression in CIC-mutant oligodendroglioma cells; subclonal mutation present across all tumor compartments, suggesting CIC mutation is an early event [PMID:27806376](../papers/27806376.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- ETV5 derepression co-occurs with [ETV1](../genes/ETV1.md) derepression in CIC-mutant oligodendroglioma cells [PMID:27806376](../papers/27806376.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Not directly targeted in the corpus; ETV5 upregulation is a functional biomarker of CIC inactivation [PMID:27806376](../papers/27806376.md).

## Open questions

- Whether ETV5 derepression contributes to the developmental plasticity of oligodendroglioma or is merely a passenger consequence of CIC loss is unresolved [PMID:27806376](../papers/27806376.md).
