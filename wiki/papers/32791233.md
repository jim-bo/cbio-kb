**PMID:** 32791233 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.jtho.2020.08.005 · **Journal:** Journal of Thoracic Oncology (2020)

## TL;DR

Caso et al. analyzed 604 patients with stage I–III lung adenocarcinoma ([LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md)) who underwent complete surgical resection at Memorial Sloan Kettering and had targeted next-generation sequencing performed via the [MSK-IMPACT panel](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) (study [luad_mskcc_2020](../datasets/luad_mskcc_2020.md)). Tumors were stratified by predominant histologic subtype into three architectural-grade groups: lepidic (LEP), acinar/papillary (ACI/PAP), and micropapillary/solid (MIP/SOL). MIP/SOL tumors exhibited significantly higher tumor mutational burden, fraction of genome altered, copy-number amplifications, whole-genome doubling, and number of altered oncogenic pathways. APOBEC-associated mutational signatures SBS2 and SBS13 conferred the highest risk of postresection recurrence across the cohort, while [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) alterations were enriched in LEP and [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)-V600E in MIP/SOL.

## Cohort & data

- **Sample size:** 604 patients with stage I–III [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) after complete resection, sequenced between February 2010 and December 2018.
- **Histologic-subtype distribution:** LEP 88 (15%); ACI 368 (61%); PAP 43 (7%); MIP 37 (6%); SOL 68 (11%). Grouped by architectural grade: LEP 88 (15%), ACI/PAP 411 (68%), MIP/SOL 105 (17%).
- **Pathologic stage:** I 447 (74%); II 95 (16%); III 62 (10%).
- **Demographics:** median age 68 (IQR 62–74); 67% female; 77% ever-smokers (median 20 pack-years).
- **Adjuvant therapy:** 116 patients (19%) received adjuvant therapy (83% chemotherapy/immunotherapy, 15% chemoradiation, 2% radiation).
- **Dataset:** [luad_mskcc_2020](../datasets/luad_mskcc_2020.md) (cBioPortal).
- **Assay/panel:** Targeted NGS via the [MSK-IMPACT panel](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md); sequencing breadth: 8 patients on [IMPACT341](../methods/IMPACT341.md) (0.98 Mb), 190 on [IMPACT410](../methods/IMPACT410.md) (1.06 Mb), and 406 on [IMPACT468](../methods/IMPACT468.md) (1.22 Mb).
- **Median follow-up:** 2.51 years (IQR 2.08–3.14).
- **Exclusions:** mucinous [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md), induction therapy, residual disease, stage IV, low-quality NGS.

## Key findings

- **Genomic burden scales with subtype invasiveness:** Median TMB increased monotonically with subtype invasiveness (LEP 3.9, ACI/PAP 4.9, MIP/SOL 7.9 mutations/Mb; p<0.001). Median fraction of genome altered ([FGA](../genes/FGA.md)) followed the same pattern (LEP 0.174, ACI/PAP 0.222, MIP/SOL 0.304; p=0.001). The correlation between TMB and FGA strengthened with invasiveness (LEP ρ=−0.03; ACI/PAP ρ=0.11; MIP/SOL ρ=0.28).
- **Subtype-defining genes:** Three genes were significantly enriched in LEP tumors vs ACI/PAP vs MIP/SOL: [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) (42% vs 31% vs 19%; p=0.001), [RBM10](../genes/RBM10.md) (26% vs 12% vs 10%; p=0.001), and [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) (14% vs 5% vs 10%; p=0.012). Four genes were enriched in MIP/SOL vs ACI/PAP vs LEP: [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (62% vs 33% vs 16%; p<0.001), [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md) (8% vs 3% vs 1.1%; p=0.048), [MGA](../genes/MGA.md) (11% vs 2% vs 2.3%; p<0.001), and [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) (6% vs 1.5% vs 2.3%; p=0.035).
- **Chromosomal instability:** MIP/SOL tumors had the highest number of copy-number amplifications (p=0.021) and the highest rate of whole-genome doubling (WGD): 18% vs 10% vs 4.5% (p=0.008). Copy-number deletions did not differ between subtypes (p=0.208). Statistically significant arm-level differences spanned 2p, 2q, 4q, 5p, 6p, 6q, 7p, 7q, 11p, 11q, 12p, 17p (p=0.008), and 17q (p<0.001). Amplified oncogenes across the cohort included [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md), [TERT](../genes/TERT.md), [NKX2-1](../genes/NKX2-1.md), [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md), [MYC](../genes/MYC.md), [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md), and [MET](../genes/MET.md).
- **Mutational signatures and recurrence:** Mutational signatures were computed for 268 tumors (44%) with ≥13.8 mut/Mb. SBS3 (defective DNA repair) was more frequent in LEP. SBS2 and SBS13 (attributable to [APOBEC3A](../genes/APOBEC3A.md)/[APOBEC3B](../genes/APOBEC3B.md) cytidine deaminase activity) increased with subtype invasiveness. Across all tumors, SBS2 (SHR 2.07; 95% CI 1.11–3.84; p=0.021) and SBS13 (SHR 2.27; 95% CI 1.29–4.02; p=0.005) were associated with increased recurrence risk; in ACI/PAP tumors, SBS2 (SHR 2.56; p=0.02) and SBS13 (SHR 2.85; p=0.007) remained significant.
- **Oncogenic pathways:** The RTK/RAS pathway was most altered in LEP (92% vs 91% vs 81%; p=0.010). Three pathways were significantly enriched in MIP/SOL vs ACI/PAP vs LEP: p53 (67% vs 43% vs 28%; p<0.001), Wnt (7.6% vs 5% vs 0%; p=0.042), and Myc (13% vs 5.6% vs 9.1%; p=0.022). Mean number of pathways altered (NPA) increased with invasiveness (LEP 1.65; ACI/PAP 2.00; MIP/SOL 2.34; p<0.001).
- **Pathway co-occurrence:** Across the whole cohort, four pathway pairs co-occurred: p53–cell cycle (OR 4.47; p<0.001), PI3K–Nrf2 (OR 6.48; p<0.001), p53–Notch (OR 4.07; p=0.01), p53–Hippo (OR 6.93; p=0.03). In MIP/SOL, p53–cell cycle co-occurrence had OR 16.35 (p=0.02).
- **Cumulative incidence of recurrence:** Three-year CIR was 23% overall (8% LEP, 23% ACI/PAP, 37% MIP/SOL; p<0.001). In multivariable competing-risks regression: cell-cycle alterations (SHR 2.74; 95% CI 1.73–4.34; p<0.001) and PI3K alterations (SHR 2.11; 95% CI 1.32–3.37; p=0.002) were independently associated with recurrence in ACI/PAP tumors. In MIP/SOL tumors, PI3K alterations were independently associated with recurrence (SHR 1.97; 95% CI 1.00–3.88; p=0.049).
- **Therapeutic actionability:** [OncoKB annotation](../methods/oncokb-annotation.md) identified 735 actionable alterations across 30 genes. RTK/RAS harbored 73% (n=536) of actionable alterations; 37% of these (200/536) had level I evidence. LEP tumors had the highest fraction of level I targets (35% vs 27% vs 21%). MIP/SOL tumors with level I targets had significantly lower TMB than other-level MIP/SOL tumors (median 3.25 vs 10.5; p<0.001).

## Genes & alterations

- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) — Targetable [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) alterations (exon 19 deletion or L858R) more frequent in LEP (23%) than ACI/PAP (14%) or MIP/SOL (5%); p=0.001. Also amplified across the cohort. Overall mutation rate by subtype: 42% LEP, 31% ACI/PAP, 19% MIP/SOL.
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — Targetable BRAF-V600E mutations more frequent in MIP/SOL (5%) than ACI/PAP (1.2%) or LEP (1.1%); p=0.046. Among LEP tumors, BRAF-V600K, BRAF-K601E, and BRAF-K601N were level 3B; among ACI/PAP and MIP/SOL, BRAF-V600E was level 1.
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — No statistically significant difference in KRAS-G12C mutation frequency across subtypes.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — Strongly enriched in MIP/SOL tumors (62% vs 33% vs 16%; p<0.001). Discussed as a permissive driver preceding WGD.
- [RBM10](../genes/RBM10.md) — Enriched in LEP tumors (26% vs 12% vs 10%; p=0.001).
- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) — Enriched in LEP tumors (14% vs 5% vs 10%; p=0.012); also among recurrently amplified oncogenes.
- [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md) — Enriched in MIP/SOL tumors (8% vs 3% vs 1.1%; p=0.048).
- [MGA](../genes/MGA.md) — Enriched in MIP/SOL tumors (11% vs 2% vs 2.3%; p<0.001).
- [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) — Enriched in MIP/SOL tumors (6% vs 1.5% vs 2.3%; p=0.035); cited as a subclonal driver enriched in MIP/SOL.
- [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) — Cited as known LUAD subclonal genes with higher alteration frequencies in MIP/SOL tumors.
- [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md), [NKX2-1](../genes/NKX2-1.md), [MYC](../genes/MYC.md), [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md), [MET](../genes/MET.md) — Recurrently amplified oncogenes across the cohort, consistent with prior LUAD reports.
- [APOBEC3A](../genes/APOBEC3A.md) and [APOBEC3B](../genes/APOBEC3B.md) — Implicated as the enzymatic source of SBS2 and SBS13 mutational signatures, which were independently associated with postresection recurrence.

## Clinical implications

- **Subtype-stratified surveillance:** Cell-cycle and PI3K pathway alterations in ACI/PAP tumors, and PI3K alterations in MIP/SOL tumors, were independently associated with recurrence — supporting consideration of more intensive surveillance and/or adjuvant therapy for resected patients with these alterations.
- **Targeted therapy enrichment differs by subtype:** LEP-predominant tumors are enriched for targetable EGFR alterations and the highest fraction of level I actionable targets; MIP/SOL tumors are enriched for targetable BRAF-V600E. Authors suggest histologic subtype could help anticipate which targetable drivers to prioritize at diagnosis.
- **Immunotherapy considerations:** MIP/SOL tumors had the highest TMB (which has been associated with improved checkpoint-inhibitor benefit in [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md)) but also the highest FGA (which has been associated with reduced response). The authors cite this duality as a potential explanation for heterogeneous immunotherapy outcomes in this subtype.
- **APOBEC as a recurrence biomarker:** SBS2 and SBS13 conferred ~2-fold elevated recurrence risk and may serve as a postresection prognostic marker, particularly in ACI/PAP tumors.

## Limitations & open questions

- Single-region tumor sampling cannot capture intratumoral heterogeneity or clonal architecture, a known feature of LUAD.
- The study excluded mucinous LUAD (2%–10% of resected tumors), which has a distinct molecular profile and a more aggressive course.
- Predominant-subtype assignment ignores minor-subtype components, which may also influence prognosis.
- Mutational-signature analysis omitted doublet-base substitutions and indels, which contribute to overall mutational burden.
- The cohort is a single-institution (MSKCC) surgically resected series; generalizability to other practice settings and unresectable disease is unclear.
- Median follow-up of 2.51 years limits assessment of late recurrences.
