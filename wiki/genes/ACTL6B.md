# ACTL6B

## Overview

ACTL6B (Actin-Like 6B) is a subunit of the SWI/SNF chromatin remodeling complex. It is expressed predominantly in neural and germline tissues, and its promoter CpG-island hypermethylation has been reported in upper tract urothelial carcinoma ([UTUC](../cancer_types/UTUC.md)), implicating epigenetic silencing of SWI/SNF components in urothelial tumorigenesis.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- SWI/SNF member ACTL6B shows promoter CpG-island hypermethylation in 20% of UTUC tumors (8/40) vs 0% of matched normal tissues; classified in the EpiC-high (hypermethylated, immune-inflamed) methylation subtype of UTUC; epigenetic silencing contributes to the composite SWI/SNF mutation feature used in iClusterBayes subtyping [PMID:33397444](../papers/33397444.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [UTUC](../cancer_types/UTUC.md): promoter CpG hypermethylation in 20% of tumors; enriched in EpiC-high (SWI/SNF-mutant, immune-inflamed, muscle-invasive) subtype [PMID:33397444](../papers/33397444.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Collapsed alongside other SWI/SNF member mutations ([ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md)) into the composite "SWI/SNF" iClusterBayes feature in UTUC subtyping [PMID:33397444](../papers/33397444.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) inhibition proposed as a therapeutic strategy for the EpiC-high SWI/SNF-mutant UTUC subtype (which includes ACTL6B-methylated tumors), based on prior NK-cell-activating mechanism of EZH2 inhibition in bladder cancer [PMID:33397444](../papers/33397444.md)

## Open questions

- Whether ACTL6B promoter methylation functionally silences the gene in UTUC, and whether this epigenetic event has phenotypic consequences distinct from SWI/SNF subunit coding mutations, remains unresolved [PMID:33397444](../papers/33397444.md)
