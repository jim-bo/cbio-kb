# TOMM34

## Overview

TOMM34 (Translocase of Outer Mitochondrial Membrane 34) is a component of the translocase of the outer mitochondrial membrane (TOM) complex, involved in protein import into mitochondria. TOMM34 is located on chromosome 20q, a region frequently amplified in colorectal and other cancers.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Confirmed 20q amplification driver in colon cancer via integrated copy-number/mRNA/protein prioritization in the CPTAC proteogenomics study ([coad_cptac_2019](../datasets/coad_cptac_2019.md)); co-identified with [HNF4A](../genes/HNF4A.md) as a prioritized 20q driver [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Colorectal cancer ([COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md)) — TOMM34 identified as a 20q amplification driver by integrating copy-number, mRNA, and protein data in the CPTAC colon cancer proteogenomics cohort (96 tumor/NAT pairs); the integrated approach was used to distinguish true amplification-driven expression from bystander copy gains [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-amplified and co-prioritized with [HNF4A](../genes/HNF4A.md) as the two confirmed 20q amplification drivers in [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md); 20q is one of the most recurrent focal copy-number gains in colon cancer [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- TOMM34 amplification could be a candidate for therapeutic targeting, though no specific inhibitor is established; the proteogenomic prioritization approach used in CPTAC identified it as a likely functional driver rather than a passenger of the 20q amplicon [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Open questions

- The functional consequence of TOMM34 amplification on mitochondrial protein import and tumor biology is not characterized in the CPTAC study; mechanism linking 20q gain to colon tumorigenesis requires further investigation.
