# IL17RE

## Overview

IL17RE (Interleukin 17 Receptor E) encodes a receptor for IL-17C involved in epithelial innate immune responses. In the cancer immunotherapy context, IL17RE was identified among a pre-therapy gene expression signature associated with response to anti-PD-1 therapy in melanoma, nominated as an immune-environment modulator.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Highly expressed pre-therapy in CR/PR vs PD patients in the 189-DEG responder signature (q < 0.20) in advanced [melanoma](../cancer_types/SKCM.md) patients treated with [nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md) (CA209-038 trial, n=68); nominated as an immune-environment modulator. [PMID:29033130](../papers/29033130.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[Skin Cutaneous Melanoma (SKCM)](../cancer_types/SKCM.md):** Pre-therapy IL17RE expression was differentially elevated in responders vs non-responders to [nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md) in the CA209-038 trial (n=68 advanced melanoma, paired pre-/on-therapy biopsies). [PMID:29033130](../papers/29033130.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-expressed with [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) and [IL17RC](../genes/IL17RC.md) in the pre-therapy 189-DEG responder set, suggesting shared immune-regulatory context. [PMID:29033130](../papers/29033130.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct targeted therapy against IL17RE is reported in this corpus. High pre-therapy expression may serve as a predictive biomarker for anti-PD-1 response in melanoma, pending validation. [PMID:29033130](../papers/29033130.md)

## Open questions

- The 189-DEG analysis was limited by small sample size; IL17RE as a standalone predictive biomarker requires prospective validation.
- The mechanistic basis for IL17RE expression predicting anti-PD-1 response in melanoma is not established. [PMID:29033130](../papers/29033130.md)
