# H1-5

## Overview

H1-5 (H1.5 Linker Histone, Cluster Member) is a linker histone involved in chromatin compaction and gene regulation. Formerly designated HIST1H1B under an older HUGO nomenclature, the gene was renamed H1-5 in a 2021 HGNC update. It has been identified as a chromatin modification driver in chronic lymphocytic leukemia (CLL).

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Identified as a chromatin modification driver in CLL (HUGO symbol updated from HIST1H1B to H1-5); enriched in a comprehensive WES study of 538 CLL patients [PMID:26466571](../papers/26466571.md)
- H1-5 (HIST1H1B) linker histone with recurrent mutations co-defining DLBCL C4 GCB subtype; co-occurring with H1-2, H1-3, H1-4 and HIST1H2 core histone mutations; domain mutations reduce chromatin association. [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **CLL** — novel driver candidate in chronic lymphocytic leukemia, classified among chromatin modification drivers alongside [ASXL1](../genes/ASXL1.md), [BAZ2B](../genes/BAZ2B.md), and [IKZF3](../genes/IKZF3.md) [PMID:26466571](../papers/26466571.md)
