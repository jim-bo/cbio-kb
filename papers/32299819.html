Genomic Landscape of Uterine Sarcomas Defined through Prospective Clinical Sequencing

Authors

Martee L. Hensley

Shweta S. Chavan

David B. Solit

Rajmohan Murali

Robert A. Soslow

Sarah Chiang

Achim A. Jungbluth

PMID: 32299819 · DOI: 10.1158/1078-0432.CCR-19-3959 · Journal: Clinical Cancer Research (2020)

TL;DR

Hensley and colleagues prospectively sequenced tumors from 107 women with uterine sarcoma at Memorial Sloan Kettering using the MSK-IMPACT targeted DNA panel, with MSK-Fusion RNA sequencing in a subset. The cohort covered uterine leiomyosarcoma (uLMS, n=80), high-grade non-LMS (n=22), low-grade endometrial stromal sarcoma (LG-ESS, n=4), and STUMP (n=2). uLMS was dominated by loss-of-function alterations in TP53 (56%), RB1 (51%), and ATRX (31%), with recurrent homozygous deletions of BRCA2 (5%) and PDCD1 (the highest rate of PDCD1 homozygous deletion across all cancer types with ≥25 cases in the contemporary 15,816-patient MSK-IMPACT cohort). Potentially actionable alterations were identified in 45% (48/107) of patients; among uLMS patients with somatic BRCA2 alterations, PARP-inhibitor-containing therapy produced sustained partial responses, supporting BRCA2 as a therapeutically relevant target in uLMS.

Cohort & data

  • Cohort: 107 women with histologically confirmed uterine sarcoma profiled at Memorial Sloan Kettering between April 2014 and April 2017 under prospective IRB protocol NCT01775072 (PMID:32299819).
  • Histologic breakdown: ULMS n=80; high-grade non-LMS n=22 (further subclassified as HG-ESS n=7, undifferentiated n=5, high-grade adenosarcoma with sarcomatous overgrowth n=4, and other rare subtypes); LGESS n=4; STUMP n=2 (PMID:32299819).
  • Stage / status: 57% (n=61) FIGO stage I at diagnosis; 89% (n=95) recurrent or metastatic at time of sequencing; 94.3% (n=101) histologically high grade (PMID:32299819).
  • Samples: 39% (n=42) primary, 61% (n=66) metastatic (PMID:32299819).
  • Cancer type / OncoTree code: USARC.
  • Dataset: usarc_msk_2020 (cBioPortal).
  • Assays: MSK-IMPACT targeted DNA exon-capture (341-gene n=44, 410-gene n=44, 468-gene n=20 samples; ~646× average coverage); subset additionally profiled with MSK-Fusion RNA panel for diagnostic work-up.
  • Computational methods: MSIsensor v0.2 for MSI scoring (≥10 = MSI-H), FACETS v0.5.6 for allele-specific copy number / purity / ploidy, mutational signature decomposition on samples with ≥10 SNVs, and IHC confirmation of MMR proteins (MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2) and of PDCD1 loss in homozygous-deletion cases.
  • Annotation: OncoKB used to assign actionability levels (as of October 2019); germline variant annotation across 76 cancer-predisposition genes via the MSK-IMPACT germline pipeline.

Key findings

  • uLMS driver landscape: Loss-of-function alterations or homozygous deletions of TP53 (56%), RB1 (51%), and ATRX (31%) dominated uLMS. The same genes were significantly less commonly altered in high-grade non-LMS (14%, 14%, and 5% respectively; p<0.01 for all) (PMID:32299819).
  • BRCA2 homozygous deletions in uLMS: Homozygous deletions of BRCA2 occurred in ~5% of patients and were found exclusively in uLMS. Combined somatic BRCA2 alterations (deletions plus mutations) accounted for seven of the 107 patients (PMID:32299819).
  • PDCD1 deletions enriched in uLMS: uLMS had the highest rate of PDCD1 homozygous deletions among all cancer types with ≥25 cases in the 15,816-patient MSK-IMPACT cohort. IHC in two informative cases (of seven) confirmed loss of PD-1 protein expression (PMID:32299819).
  • Amplifications: MAP2K4 and RIT1 amplifications were most common in uterine sarcomas relative to the contemporary MSK-IMPACT pan-cancer cohort. RIT1 was amplified in 24% (5/21) of high-grade non-LMS vs. 2.5% (2/80) of uLMS (p=0.004) (PMID:32299819).
  • PTEN site-specific enrichment: PTEN alterations were more frequent in metastasis vs. primary samples (p=0.046; remained significant within uLMS, p=0.04) (PMID:32299819).
  • MED12 low in uLMS: Only 11% (9/80) of uLMS harbored MED12 mutations, vs. ~70% in benign uterine leiomyomas, arguing that uLMS rarely evolves from antecedent leiomyomas (PMID:32299819).
  • uLMS vs. non-uterine LMS: In a direct comparison with 68 non-uterine LMS sequenced at MSK, TP53 alterations were more common in non-uterine LMS (71% vs. 56%, p=0.01), MAP2K4 amplifications trended higher in non-uterine LMS (19% vs. 8%, p=0.06), and MED12 mutations were exclusively in uLMS (11% vs. 0%, p=0.01). Compared with a small TCGA uLMS cohort (n=27), RB1 alterations were significantly more frequent in the MSK cohort (51% vs. 22%, p=0.02) (PMID:32299819).
  • Whole-genome duplication: 50.5% (50/99) of high-grade uterine sarcomas displayed WGD; none of the low-grade tumors did (p=0.04). WGD was not associated with overall survival (PMID:32299819).
  • Two hypermutated MSI-H uLMS outliers: Two uLMS cases had outlier TMB with MSI-H by MSIsensor and MMR-D mutational signatures, with IHC loss of MSH2 and MSH6 respectively. Neither had a somatic driver in these genes (etiology likely epigenetic/germline). Both achieved complete responses with multimodality therapy and remain disease-free at 5.9 and 13 years; neither received immune checkpoint inhibitors. Excluding these two outliers, uLMS TMB exceeded high-grade non-LMS TMB (2.5 vs. 1.1 mutations/Mb; p=0.05) (PMID:32299819).
  • Germline TP53: Two patients carried pathogenic germline TP53 variants (one with somatic biallelic inactivation, one with known Li-Fraumeni syndrome). No other known/likely pathogenic germline variants identified after anonymized analysis (PMID:32299819).
  • Survival: Median survival from diagnosis was 5.6 years (range 0.2–23.1). High-grade non-LMS had significantly worse survival than uLMS (3.4 vs. 6.3 years; HR=0.48, 95% CI 0.24–0.96, p=0.03). No common genomic alteration nor WGD was prognostic for OS in univariate Cox regression (PMID:32299819).
  • Actionability: Potentially actionable alterations identified in 45% (48/107); 17% (8/48) received matched therapy, with two radiographic responses, one prolonged stable disease (ALK fusion case), and five progressors (PMID:32299819).

Genes & alterations

  • TP53 — loss-of-function mutations / homozygous deletions in 56% of uLMS; significantly more frequent in non-uterine LMS (71%, p=0.01). Two germline pathogenic carriers (one with Li-Fraumeni syndrome) (PMID:32299819).
  • RB1 — inactivated in 51% of uLMS vs. 22% in TCGA uLMS (p=0.02) and 14% in high-grade non-LMS (p<0.01) (PMID:32299819).
  • ATRX — altered in 31% of uLMS vs. 5% of high-grade non-LMS (p<0.01) (PMID:32299819).
  • BRCA2 — homozygous deletions in 5% of uLMS (exclusively in uLMS); seven total somatic BRCA2 alterations (four homozygous deletions + three somatic mutations); validation in five post-data-freeze uLMS patients with BRCA2 alterations treated with PARP inhibitors (including one complete radiographic response) (PMID:32299819).
  • BRCA1 — discussed in Discussion as a potential mechanism of HRD via promoter hypermethylation; not directly assayed by MSK-IMPACT for methylation in this study (PMID:32299819).
  • PTEN — enriched in metastasis vs. primary samples (p=0.046; uLMS subset p=0.04) (PMID:32299819).
  • PDCD1 — recurrent homozygous deletions in uLMS, the highest frequency across all cancer types in the 15,816-patient MSK-IMPACT cohort; IHC confirmed PD-1 protein loss in two informative cases (PMID:32299819).
  • MAP2K4 — amplifications most common in uterine sarcomas vs. pan-cancer; 8% (6/80) of uLMS vs. 19% (13/68) of non-uterine LMS (p=0.06) (PMID:32299819).
  • RIT1 — amplifications enriched in high-grade non-LMS (24%, 5/21) vs. uLMS (2.5%, 2/80; p=0.004) (PMID:32299819).
  • MED12 — mutated in 11% (9/80) of uLMS vs. ~70% in benign leiomyomas; absent in non-uterine LMS (p=0.01) (PMID:32299819).
  • BRAF — two activating mutations in uLMS (V600E and the class-2 dimer-dependent K601E); modest response to BRAF/RAF inhibitors (BRAF V600 patient progressed in 4 weeks on an oral BRAF inhibitor; K601E patient achieved 4 months stable disease on a pan-RAF inhibitor) (PMID:32299819).
  • ALK — two ALK fusions reclassified as inflammatory myofibroblastic tumor (IMT): one LBH-ALK fusion (initially missed by IMPACT DNA, then identified retrospectively; treated with crizotinib with 30+ months disease stabilization) and one post-data-freeze ALK fusion (brief crizotinib response, progression, then progression on ceritinib) (PMID:32299819).
  • ESR1 — Y537S ligand-binding domain mutation in a metastatic LGESS patient on long-term aromatase inhibitor therapy; switching to fulvestrant produced tumor regression (PMID:32299819).
  • BCOR / ZC3H7B — a BCOR-ZC3H7B fusion in one case initially classified as high-grade myxoid uLMS was reclassified as BCOR-mutated high-grade stromal sarcoma; patient survived 3.4 years (PMID:32299819).
  • JAZF1 / SUZ12 — one JAZF1-SUZ12 fusion identified by MSK-Fusion confirmed LGESS diagnosis (PMID:32299819).
  • YWHAE / NUTM2A / NUTM2B — YWHAE/NUTM2 rearrangements identified by MSK-Fusion in two high-grade, non-pleomorphic stromal sarcomas (PMID:32299819).
  • LBH — partner in the LBH-ALK fusion (PMID:32299819).
  • MSH2 / MSH6 — IHC loss of one each in the two MSI-H hypermutated uLMS cases; no somatic driver identified, suggesting epigenetic or germline etiology (PMID:32299819).

Clinical implications

  • PARP inhibitors for BRCA2-altered uLMS: Sustained partial responses observed with PARP-inhibitor-containing therapy in uLMS patients with somatic BRCA2 alterations. Validation in five additional post-data-freeze uLMS patients with BRCA2 alterations all showed at least some radiographic regression, including one complete radiographic response; treatment durations ranged from 6 to 28 months. The authors argue BRCA testing is justified in all uLMS patients (PMID:32299819).
  • ALK inhibitors for ALK-fusion sarcomas reclassified as IMT: Crizotinib yielded 30+ months of radiographic stabilization in one patient and a brief radiographic response in another (who later progressed on ceritinib), supporting that ALK fusion detection should prompt diagnostic reclassification to IMT and consideration of ALK-targeted therapy (PMID:32299819).
  • Selective estrogen receptor degraders for ESR1-mutant LG-ESS: Switching from aromatase inhibitor to fulvestrant produced tumor regression in an ESR1 Y537S–mutant LG-ESS patient previously on >20 years of anti-estrogen therapy (PMID:32299819).
  • BRAF inhibition has limited activity in BRAF-mutant uLMS: The BRAF V600E uLMS patient progressed in 4 weeks on a first-generation BRAF inhibitor; the K601E (class 2 dimer-dependent) patient achieved 4 months stable disease on a pan-RAF inhibitor — consistent with prior characterization of K601E as resistant to first-generation BRAF inhibitors (PMID:32299819).
  • Diagnostic reclassification by fusion detection: Genomic profiling redirected histologic diagnosis in three cases — two uLMS reclassified as IMT via ALK fusion and one uLMS reclassified as BCOR-mutated high-grade stromal sarcoma via BCOR-ZC3H7B fusion. JAZF1-SUZ12 and YWHAE/NUTM2 fusions were also used diagnostically (PMID:32299819).
  • MSI-H phenotype in uLMS: The two MSI-H hypermutated uLMS patients remain disease-free 5.9 and 13 years from diagnosis after multimodality management without immune checkpoint inhibitors, suggesting MSI-H may be prognostic for chemotherapy sensitivity and/or disease-free survival in uLMS (PMID:32299819).
  • PDCD1 deletion as a potential immunotherapy biomarker: Recurrent PDCD1 homozygous deletions in uLMS — confirmed by IHC loss — may help explain the paucity of objective responses to immune checkpoint inhibitors in published phase II uLMS trials (PMID:32299819).

Limitations & open questions

  • Survival bias: All patients had to be alive to enroll and consent for prospective sequencing, so the cohort likely over-represents patients with more favorable survival; patients dying very shortly after diagnosis may be under-represented with potentially distinct genomic profiles (PMID:32299819).
  • Single-center cohort: Sequenced at a single comprehensive cancer center (MSK) using one targeted DNA panel (MSK-IMPACT); generalizability to community settings and alternative assays is untested (PMID:32299819).
  • Modest matched-therapy uptake: Although 45% had potentially actionable alterations, only 17% of those (7% of the whole cohort) received matched therapy, limiting therapeutic claims (PMID:32299819).
  • No prognostic genomic biomarkers identified: Univariate Cox regression found no oncogenic alteration nor WGD significantly associated with OS, possibly reflecting cohort survival bias rather than true absence of effect (PMID:32299819).
  • MSI-H etiology unresolved: Neither MSI-H case had a somatic driver in MSH2 or MSH6; whether the cause is epigenetic silencing or germline mutation was not determined (PMID:32299819).
  • BRCA1 promoter hypermethylation not assayed: Authors hypothesize a subset of uLMS may be HRD-driven via BRCA1 promoter hypermethylation but did not directly test methylation status or its response to PARP inhibition (PMID:32299819).
  • DNA-based fusion detection blind spots: The first LBH-ALK fusion was initially missed by IMPACT DNA sequencing because the breakpoint was in an intron not commonly targeted for ALK fusions; orthogonal RNA-based assays like MSK-Fusion remain important for fusion detection in sarcomas (PMID:32299819).
  • Small low-grade subset: Only n=4 LG-ESS and n=2 STUMP, limiting statistical power for low-grade-specific conclusions (PMID:32299819).
