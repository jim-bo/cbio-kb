# [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations identify high-risk events for peripheral T-cell lymphoma treated with CHOP-based chemotherapy

**PMID:** 37078708 · **DOI:** 10.1182/bloodadvances.2023009953 · **Journal:** Blood Advances (2023)

## TL;DR

Retrospective single-institution (MSK) study of 132 adults with nodal peripheral T-cell lymphoma ([PTCL](../cancer_types/PTCL.md)) treated with curative-intent CHOP-based chemotherapy and profiled on MSK-IMPACT. [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations and *[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)*/17p deletions were the only somatic aberrancies correlating with inferior progression-free survival (PFS); median PFS was 4.5 vs 10.5 months for TP53-mutated vs TP53-wildtype cases. [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) deletions (n=9) correlated with inferior overall survival ([OS](../cancer_types/OS.md)) [[PMID:37078708](../papers/37078708.md)].

## Cohort & data

- 132 adults with nodal [PTCL](../cancer_types/PTCL.md) ([PTCL](../cancer_types/PTCL.md)-NOS, [AITL](../cancer_types/AITL.md), PTCL-TFH, [ALK](../genes/ALK.md)+/− [ALCL](../cancer_types/ALCL.md)) plus [EATL](../cancer_types/EATL.md) and [MEITL](../cancer_types/MEITL.md), treated at MSK 2015–2020 with curative-intent CHOP-based chemotherapy and profiled on MSK-IMPACT (up to 500 genes; [IMPACT341](../methods/IMPACT341.md) / [IMPACT410](../methods/IMPACT410.md) / [IMPACT468](../methods/IMPACT468.md) depending on era) [[PMID:37078708](../papers/37078708.md)].
- Three analysis cohorts: entire cohort (N=132), complete clinical data (CCD, n=87), prospective (n=72).
- Histology mix: [AITL](../cancer_types/AITL.md) 47%, PTCL-NOS 27%, [ALK](../genes/ALK.md)− [ALCL](../cancer_types/ALCL.md) 11%, PTCL-TFH 7%, [ALK](../genes/ALK.md)+ [ALCL](../cancer_types/ALCL.md) 5%, [MEITL](../cancer_types/MEITL.md) 3%.
- Regimens: CHOEP/EPOCH 45%, CHOP 30%, BV-CH(E)P 12%, CHOP + novel agent 13%.
- Genetic data deposited in cBioPortal as study [`mtnn_msk_2022`](../datasets/mtnn_msk_2022.md).

## Key findings

- Most frequently mutated genes across the cohort: [*TET2*](../genes/TET2.md) 52% (n=69), [*RHOA*](../genes/RHOA.md) 30% (n=40), [*DNMT3A*](../genes/DNMT3A.md) 19% (n=25), [*TP53*](../genes/TP53.md) 16% (n=21), [*IDH2*](../genes/IDH2.md) 11% (n=15) [[PMID:37078708](../papers/37078708.md)].
- Most common CNAs: *[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)* deletions 7% (n=9) and [*CDKN2A*](../genes/CDKN2A.md) deletions 7% (n=9).
- On multivariate analysis in the CCD cohort, clinical factors predicting inferior PFS were advanced-stage disease (HR 5.1) and bone marrow involvement. Somatic factors predicting inferior PFS were *[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)* mutations (HR 3.1; 95% CI 1.4–6.8; P=.005) and *[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)*/17p deletions (HR 4.1; 95% CI 1.1–15.0; P=.03).
- Median PFS: 4.5 months for TP53-mutated [PTCL](../cancer_types/PTCL.md) (n=21) vs 10.5 months for TP53-wildtype [PTCL](../cancer_types/PTCL.md) (n=111).
- Of 21 *[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)*-mutated cases, 15 (71%) had no concurrent *TP53*/17p deletion; 6 (29%) were co-deleted.
- No *TP53* aberrancy correlated with inferior OS.
- *[CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)* deletion correlated with inferior OS (HR 12.1; 95% CI 2.8–52.0; P<.001); median OS 17.6 months vs 56.7 months.
- In [AITL](../cancer_types/AITL.md), 88% of *[RHOA](../genes/RHOA.md)* mutations were the canonical G17V variant.
- In [MEITL](../cancer_types/MEITL.md) (n=4), 3/4 (75%) had *[CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)* aberrancies.

## Genes & alterations

- [*TP53*](../genes/TP53.md) — mutations in 16% (21/132); inferior PFS (HR 3.1, P=.005). *[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)*/17p deletions in a subset also inferior PFS (HR 4.1, P=.03). Neither correlated with OS.
- [*CDKN2A*](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — homozygous/heterozygous deletions in 7% (9/132); strong independent predictor of inferior OS (HR 12.1, P<.001). Enriched in [MEITL](../cancer_types/MEITL.md) (3/4 cases).
- [*TET2*](../genes/TET2.md) — most frequently mutated gene (52%), no independent prognostic effect reported.
- [*RHOA*](../genes/RHOA.md) — 30%; 88% of mutations in [AITL](../cancer_types/AITL.md) were G17V.
- [*DNMT3A*](../genes/DNMT3A.md) — 19%.
- [*IDH2*](../genes/IDH2.md) — 11%.
- [*STAT3*](../genes/STAT3.md), [*FAT1*](../genes/FAT1.md), [*TP63*](../genes/TP63.md) — additional recurrently altered genes profiled in subset analyses.

## Clinical implications

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation status identifies [PTCL](../cancer_types/PTCL.md) patients with markedly inferior PFS on CHOP-based induction and may warrant consideration of alternative induction or consolidation strategies and stratification in first-line randomized trials [[PMID:37078708](../papers/37078708.md)].
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) deletion flags a rare but very high-risk subset for OS, particularly relevant in [MEITL](../cancer_types/MEITL.md).
- Authors explicitly state these findings warrant prospective confirmation.

## Limitations & open questions

- Single-institution retrospective design at a referral center; selection bias risk (hence three nested cohorts).
- Small absolute numbers for some subsets (e.g., [MEITL](../cancer_types/MEITL.md) n=4, [ALK](../genes/ALK.md)+ [ALCL](../cancer_types/ALCL.md) n=6, [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)-deleted n=9); HR confidence intervals are wide.
- Targeted panel (MSK-IMPACT) rather than WES/WGS — non-panel drivers not assessed.
- Heterogeneous CHOP-based regimens (CHOP, CHOEP/EPOCH, BV-CH(E)P, + novel agents) complicate treatment-specific inference.
- Discordance between PFS and OS effect of [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) is unexplained — possibly salvage therapy confounding.
