# FLI1

## Overview

FLI1 (Friend leukemia integration 1 transcription factor) is an ETS-family transcription factor. It is most prominently known as the C-terminal fusion partner in the EWSR1::FLI1 fusion oncogene, which defines the majority of Ewing sarcomas. The fusion protein acts as an aberrant transcription factor that rewires gene expression programs in the cell of origin.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- EWSR1::FLI1 fusion expressed in heMSCs is sufficient to induce Ewing sarcoma–like transcriptome, BRCA1 dysregulation, DDR defect, and metastatic tumor formation; binds intronic CA microsatellites in stem cell context vs GGAA microsatellite enhancers in established Ewing lines [PMID:25186949](../papers/25186949.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Ewing sarcoma: defined by EWSR1::FLI1 (or variant EWSR1::ETS fusions) in ~85% of cases.
