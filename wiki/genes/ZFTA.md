# ZFTA

## Overview

ZFTA (Zinc Finger Translocation Associated; formerly C11orf95) is a transcription factor gene on chromosome 11q13.1 that acts as the recurrent fusion partner in supratentorial ependymoma. ZFTA–[RELA](../genes/RELA.md) fusions, generated by chromothripsis on chromosome 11q, are the dominant driver in supratentorial ependymoma (ST-EPN-RELA subtype) and constitute one of the most clinically significant structural variants in pediatric CNS tumors. The fusions result in nuclear localization of an aberrant NF-κB-activating complex that drives oncogenesis.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- ZFTA fusion — identified as a supratentorial ependymoma driver gene fusion partner; observed in approximately 6% of CNS-tumor oncomap alterations in the MAPPYACTS pediatric precision-oncology trial (n=624 recurrent/refractory pediatric patients) [PMID:35292802](../papers/35292802.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [EPM](../cancer_types/EPM.md) (ependymoma) — ZFTA fusions (most commonly ZFTA–[RELA](../genes/RELA.md)) are defining events in supratentorial ependymoma (ST-EPN-RELA subtype); detected in the MAPPYACTS cohort as part of CNS-tumor molecular profiling [PMID:35292802](../papers/35292802.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- ZFTA fusions are typically mutually exclusive with other ependymoma molecular subgroups (e.g., [YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md) fusion-driven posterior fossa or spinal subtypes); co-occurrence with additional actionable alterations has not been systematically reported in the corpus.

## Therapeutic relevance

- No targeted therapy with regulatory approval specific to ZFTA-fusion ependymoma exists as of the corpus date. In the MAPPYACTS trial ZFTA fusions were catalogued as "investigational" actionable alterations; the trial framework matched such patients to early-phase AcSé-ESMART platform arms [PMID:35292802](../papers/35292802.md).

## Open questions

- Whether ZFTA fusion-positive ependymomas respond to NF-κB pathway inhibitors or other targeted approaches tested on the AcSé-ESMART platform remains an open clinical question.
- The frequency [estimate](../methods/estimate.md) of ~6% of CNS-tumor alterations from MAPPYACTS reflects a recurrent/refractory pediatric cohort and may differ from at-diagnosis frequencies.
