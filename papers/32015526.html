A prognostic signature for lung adenocarcinoma in people who have never smoked

Authors

Wei Zhao

Tongwu Zhang

Xing Hua

Phuc H. Hoang

Jianxin Shi

Soo-Ryum Yang

Maria Teresa Landi

Doi

10.1101/2025.08.13.670169

PMID: 32015526 · DOI: 10.1101/2025.08.13.670169 · Journal: bioRxiv (2025)

TL;DR

This study analyzed RNA-seq from 684 treatment-naive lung adenocarcinomas in people who have never smoked (NS-LUAD), combining the Sherlock-Lung cohort (n=610) with TCGA-LUAD never-smoker cases (n=74). Non-negative matrix factorization (NMF) of the transcriptome identified three NS-LUAD subtypes — steady, proliferative, and chaotic — distinguished by proliferation, cell composition, driver gene alterations, and survival. A 60-gene signature recapitulates the classification and stratifies mortality risk even within stage I tumors and within TP53-wild-type subgroups, and was validated in the independent Genomic Institute of Singapore (GIS) NS-LUAD cohort (n=110; corresponds to cBioPortal study luad_oncosg_2020).

Cohort & data

  • Sherlock cohort: 684 NS-LUAD tumors (610 Sherlock-Lung + 74 TCGA-LUAD never-smokers), all treatment-naive, with paired adjacent-normal RNA-seq in 498 subjects (491 Sherlock-Lung + 7 TCGA-LUAD) PMID:32015526.
  • Ancestry: 295 European (EUR), 342 East Asian (EAS), 4 African (AFR), 32 American/Mixed, 11 unknown. Only 80 genes were differentially expressed between EUR and EAS, and no significant pathway differences, so groups were combined PMID:32015526.
  • Demographics: median age 64; 555 (81.1%) female and 129 (18.9%) male; 386 (56.4%) stage I tumors; 40 (5.8%) stage IV PMID:32015526.
  • Histologic grading: 301 cases reviewed using the IASLC system — 34 (11.3%) well, 69 (22.9%) moderate, 198 (65.8%) poorly differentiated PMID:32015526.
  • Integrated omics: 499 tumors had matched whole-genome sequencing (486 Sherlock-Lung + 13 TCGA-LUAD) used to integrate driver mutations, structural variants, whole-genome doubling, and kataegis PMID:32015526.
  • Validation: the Genomic Institute of Singapore (GIS) NS-LUAD cohort, 110 tumors of Asian ancestry (cBioPortal luad_oncosg_2020; EGA accession EGAD00001004421) PMID:32015526.
  • Assays and tools: Illumina NovaSeq6000 2x151bp paired-end RNA-seq; STAR v2.7.3 (star) alignment to GRCh38; HTSeq + GENCODE v35 quantification; ComBat-Seq batch correction; DESeq2 with TMM normalization; NMF (R package; Brunet’s algorithm); fusions called by SFyNCS v0.15 and STAR-Fusion; CAMTHC and Bisque deconvolution; immune response predicted by TIDE; tumor purity by ESTIMATE; 60-gene classifier built with ClaNC; survival analyzed with Cox proportional-hazards models PMID:32015526.

Key findings

  • Three NS-LUAD transcriptomic subtypes identified by NMF clustering at rank 3: steady (normal-like, low proliferation, alveolar-cell enriched), proliferative (high MYC-regulated and cell-cycle genes), and chaotic (high EMT and cell-adhesion disruption) PMID:32015526.
  • Stage distribution: steady tumors were 74% stage I (p=2.75x10⁻⁸ vs others); ~2/3 of chaotic tumors were advanced-stage (p=1.44x10⁻⁸ vs others) PMID:32015526.
  • Grade: 53%, 27% and 23% of steady, proliferative, and chaotic tumors were well/moderately differentiated (steady vs others p=5.52x10⁻⁶) PMID:32015526.
  • Lineage fidelity: steady had the highest LUAD lineage score (p=6.84x10⁻²²); chaotic had the highest mixed-lineage score (p=2.31x10⁻¹⁸) despite being histologically LUAD PMID:32015526.
  • Cell composition: proliferative tumors were enriched for epithelial cells (mean 38.33%, p=2.03x10⁻¹²); chaotic tumors enriched for fibroblasts (mean 21.55%, p=6.88x10⁻⁴⁰); steady tumors enriched for alveolar AT2 cells (mean 34.94%, p=5x10⁻²⁹) and depleted of basal cells (mean 3.40%, p=1.09x10⁻⁵³). Chaotic was specifically enriched for COL10A1+ and COL4A1+ cancer-associated fibroblasts (CAFs) and depleted of non-malignant fibroblasts PMID:32015526.
  • Driver genes by subtype (integrated WGS, n=499): TP53 mutations significantly enriched in proliferative (p=4.99x10⁻⁶, FDR=9.73x10⁻⁴) and depleted in steady (p=4.63x10⁻¹⁴, FDR=9.03x10⁻¹²); ALK fusions negatively associated with steady (p=1.73x10⁻⁴, FDR=0.0168); co-occurrence of ALK fusions and TP53 mutations seen in 7 proliferative tumors; EGFR mutations were more frequent in steady but not significant after FDR (p=3.65x10⁻³, FDR=0.315); no driver mutations or fusions (including MET exon 14 skipping) were significantly enriched in chaotic PMID:32015526.
  • Gene fusions: 11,947 fusions detected by RNA-seq in 638/684 tumors; 54.3% supported by WGS structural variants. Proliferative had the highest mean fusion count (22.1 vs 13.9 steady vs 10.7 chaotic, p=1.11x10⁻¹³). Most frequent in-frame fusions: EML4-ALK (33 tumors) and KIF5B-RET (8 tumors). PARG-BMS1 was a novel recurrent fusion in 5 tumors. Top fusion partners: ALK (35), EML4 (33), RET (12), ROS1 (9). In-frame ALK fusion frequency: 8.2% proliferative vs 2.3% steady vs 1.6% chaotic PMID:32015526.
  • Survival (multivariable Cox, age/sex/stage adjusted): Steady HR=0.43 (95% CI 0.30–0.63, p=1.3x10⁻⁵); chaotic HR=1.4 (95% CI 0.99–2.0, p=0.056 at 10y; HR=1.5, 95% CI 1.0–2.3, p=0.031 at 5y) PMID:32015526.
  • Stage I-only survival (n=374): steady HR=0.39 (95% CI 0.22–0.68, p=8.8x10⁻⁴); chaotic HR=2.5 (95% CI 1.3–4.7, p=0.0043 at 10y; HR=3.7, 95% CI 1.8–7.3, p=2.2x10⁻⁴ at 5y) — the subtype identifies aggressive stage I tumors not captured by stage alone PMID:32015526.
  • Beyond TP53: within 169 TP53-wild-type tumors, proliferative still had shorter survival than steady (HR=2.1, 95% CI 1.2–3.6, p=0.011) PMID:32015526.
  • Within S-LUAD TRU subset (n=303): prognostic separation persisted (steady HR=0.53, p=0.0025) PMID:32015526.
  • ICB response prediction: Steady had the lowest TIDE scores (p=6.21x10⁻¹⁴) and was enriched in the bottom-20% TIDE quintile (p=1.09x10⁻⁹), while chaotic was depleted (p=3.58x10⁻⁴). Steady had lower abundances of MDSCs (p=4.77x10⁻¹⁹) and CAFs (p=1.44x10⁻¹⁰). Adding subtype to CD274 (PD-L1)/PDCD1 (PD-1) expression raised the R² of TIDE prediction from 0.093 (PD-L1+PD-1 only) to 0.25 (subtype+PD-L1+PD-1, p=1.17x10⁻²²) PMID:32015526.
  • 60-gene signature (built with ClaNC): correctly classified 87.0% (595/684) of samples vs whole-transcriptome NMF; cross-validation error rates 13.2% (training) and 15.1% (testing). Includes ~30 lung-development genes and ~10 EMT/cell-adhesion genes PMID:32015526.
  • External validation in GIS cohort (luad_oncosg_2020, n=110): signature classified 35 steady / 64 proliferative / 11 chaotic; steady HR=0.32 (95% CI 0.12–0.87, p=0.026) overall, also significant within TP53-wt; mortality c-index=0.682 PMID:32015526.
  • Best combined prognostic model: expression subtype + stage achieved c-index=0.68 in the GIS testing set, outperforming stage alone, driver-mutation models (TP53, EGFR, KRAS mutations, EML4-ALK fusion), or histologic grade PMID:32015526.

Genes & alterations

  • TP53 — mutations enriched in proliferative subtype (p=4.99x10⁻⁶, FDR=9.73x10⁻⁴) and depleted in steady (p=4.63x10⁻¹⁴, FDR=9.03x10⁻¹²) NS-LUAD PMID:32015526.
  • EGFR — mutations more frequent in steady NS-LUAD (p=3.65x10⁻³, not FDR-significant); consistent with previously reported slow growth of EGFR-positive NS-LUAD tumors PMID:32015526.
  • KRAS — used together with TP53 and EGFR mutations + EML4-ALK fusion in the “Mutation model” Cox prognostic comparator PMID:32015526.
  • ALK — fusions negatively associated with steady (p=1.73x10⁻⁴, FDR=0.0168); 35 tumors carry ALK fusions; in-frame ALK fusions enriched in proliferative (8.2%) PMID:32015526.
  • EML4 — partner in the canonical EML4-ALK fusion (33 tumors, most frequent in-frame fusion event) PMID:32015526.
  • RET — second-most-frequent in-frame fusion partner (12 tumors); KIF5B-RET seen in 8 tumors PMID:32015526.
  • ROS1 — recurrent in-frame fusion gene (9 tumors) PMID:32015526.
  • KIF5B — partner in KIF5B-RET fusion (8 tumors) PMID:32015526.
  • MET — MET exon 14 skipping looked for but not enriched in chaotic PMID:32015526.
  • MYC — MYC-regulated proliferation signatures upregulated in proliferative subtype PMID:32015526.
  • MKI67 — low expression marker of steady subtype; high in proliferative PMID:32015526.
  • CDH2 — mesenchymal marker highly expressed in chaotic subtype PMID:32015526.
  • TWIST1 — EMT transcription factor highly expressed in chaotic PMID:32015526.
  • CD274 (PD-L1) — expression measured to anchor the TIDE-based ICB-response analysis; only modestly predictive (R²=0.081) of TIDE score PMID:32015526.
  • PDCD1 (PD-1) — expression measured alongside CD274; combined R²=0.093, much lower than the subtype-augmented R²=0.25 PMID:32015526.
  • PARG — partner in a novel recurrent PARG-BMS1 fusion (5 tumors); PARG protein involved in DNA damage repair PMID:32015526.
  • BMS1 — partner in the novel recurrent PARG-BMS1 fusion (5 tumors) PMID:32015526.

Clinical implications

  • The 60-gene signature provides a clinically usable prognostic tool for NS-LUAD that retains discriminative power within stage I and within TP53-wt subgroups, identifying early-stage tumors at unexpectedly high mortality risk that may warrant adjuvant therapy PMID:32015526.
  • Combining the expression subtype with tumor stage yielded the most accurate survival predictor (c-index=0.68 in the GIS testing set), outperforming stage, mutation, or grade alone — supporting integration of transcriptomic subtyping into clinical workup PMID:32015526.
  • Adding subtype to CD274/PDCD1 expression nearly tripled the R² for predicted ICB response (0.093 → 0.25), suggesting that NS-LUAD patients in the steady subtype may be a candidate population for immune checkpoint blockade despite NS-LUAD being typically considered less ICB-responsive than smoking-related LUAD; the authors explicitly call this a hypothesis needing validation in cohorts with both transcriptomes and ICB outcomes PMID:32015526.
  • ALK fusions co-occurring with TP53 mutations — a known poor-prognosis, treatment-resistant combination — were concentrated in the proliferative subtype (7 tumors) PMID:32015526.

Limitations & open questions

  • The validation cohort (GIS, luad_oncosg_2020) is exclusively Asian ancestry and n=110, including only 11 chaotic tumors — chaotic survival inference in the validation set is underpowered PMID:32015526.
  • ICB-response inference is computational (TIDE) rather than direct; the authors note that the predicted enrichment of steady tumors in ICB-responder space “warrants further validation” in cohorts with paired transcriptomic and ICB outcome data PMID:32015526.
  • The chaotic subtype lacks an enrichment for any known driver mutation or fusion, so its identification depends on having transcriptomic data — there is no equivalent DNA-only assay PMID:32015526.
  • The Sherlock cohort is skewed female (81.1%) and includes only 4 African-ancestry patients, leaving open whether the signature performs equally across ancestries beyond EUR/EAS PMID:32015526.
  • The manuscript is a bioRxiv preprint (posted Aug 2025) and had not been peer-reviewed at the time of compilation; the corresponding cBioPortal study luad_oncosg_2020 is from a separate prior publication (Chen et al.) that the authors use as a validation set, not a re-release of the Sherlock-Lung cohort.
