**PMID:** [23817572](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23817572) · **DOI:** [10.1038/ng.2682](https://doi.org/10.1038/ng.2682) · **Journal:** Nature Genetics (2013)

## TL;DR

The ICGC PedBrain Tumor Project performed whole-genome sequencing (with matched RNA-seq in 73 cases and long-insert mate-pair sequencing in 68 cases) of 96 pediatric pilocytic astrocytomas. They found an extremely low mutation rate (<0.1/Mb) and confirmed that pilocytic astrocytoma is a single-pathway disease: 100% of tumors harbored a MAPK pathway alteration. Beyond known [KIAA1549](../genes/KIAA1549.md):[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) fusions, they identified four novel [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) fusion partners ([RNF130](../genes/RNF130.md), [CLCN6](../genes/CLCN6.md), [MKRN1](../genes/MKRN1.md), [GNAI1](../genes/GNAI1.md)), a novel [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) p.R506_insVLR insertion, two new [NTRK2](../genes/NTRK2.md) fusions ([QKI](../genes/QKI.md):[NTRK2](../genes/NTRK2.md) and [NACC2](../genes/NACC2.md):NTRK2) in non-cerebellar tumors, recurrent hotspot mutations in [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) (N546 and K656; 5 tumors in the WGS cohort, 9 more in a screening cohort of 45 non-cerebellar tumors), and recurrent [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) mutations (E69K, E76A) in [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md)-mutant tumors. The same [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md)/[H3-3A](../genes/H3-3A.md) K27M/[NF1](../genes/NF1.md) triple-mutation constellation was identified in 3/48 pediatric glioblastomas, suggesting [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) signaling is a therapeutic target in both diseases.

## Cohort & data

- 96 pilocytic astrocytomas ([PAST](../cancer_types/PAST.md)) with matched blood DNA subjected to whole-genome sequencing on Illumina HiSeq2000.
- Matched RNA sequencing (n=73) and long-insert mate-pair sequencing (n=68) on the same samples for structural variant detection.
- Tumor locations spanned the CNS (~half cerebellar, ~half non-cerebellar/extra-cerebellar/midline); cohort identifier: [past_dkfz_heidelberg_2013](../datasets/past_dkfz_heidelberg_2013.md).
- Additional independent screening cohort of 45 non-cerebellar pilocytic astrocytomas (negative for [KIAA1549](../genes/KIAA1549.md):[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)) screened for [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) exons 12 & 14 and [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) exon 3.
- Comparison cohort: re-examination of previous pediatric [GB](../cancer_types/GB.md) exome sequencing data (48 samples).
- Gene expression array data from 118 pilocytic astrocytomas (Affymetrix U133 Plus2.0, including 66 from the present series) vs. 158 other astrocytic tumors.
- Methods: [whole-genome-seq](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md), [rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md), mate-pair sequencing, BWA + samtools + Picard alignment to hg19, [pindel](../methods/pindel.md), CREST, DELLY for SV detection, TopHat-Fusion and deFuse for fusion calling, [genome-music](../methods/genome-music.md) for significantly mutated genes, [annovar](../methods/annovar.md) + [oncotator](../methods/oncotator.md) for annotation, PCR + [sanger-sequencing](../methods/sanger-sequencing.md) for verification (>98% SNV verification, >70% InDel verification).
- Accession: European Genome-phenome Archive EGAS00001000381.

## Key findings

- 100% of 96 pilocytic astrocytomas harbored a MAPK pathway alteration; pilocytic astrocytoma is confirmed as a prototypic single-pathway disease.
- Mean somatic mutation rate <0.1/Mb; mean 1.6 non-synonymous SNVs per tumor (range 0–9); <1 coding InDel per case.
- Genome-wide mutation rate positively correlated with patient age (Pearson r = 0.42, P = 2.3 × 10⁻⁵), driven by C-to-T transitions at CpG sites.
- 70/96 cases harbored a [KIAA1549](../genes/KIAA1549.md):[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) fusion; one [FAM131B](../genes/FAM131B.md):[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) fusion; four [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E mutations; one [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) p.ins599T.
- Four novel [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) fusions identified: [RNF130](../genes/RNF130.md):[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) (n=2, from reciprocal t(5;7)(q35;q34)), [CLCN6](../genes/CLCN6.md):[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [MKRN1](../genes/MKRN1.md):[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), and [GNAI1](../genes/GNAI1.md):[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) (each n=1); all variants delete the BRAF amino-terminal regulatory region.
- Novel [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) p.R506_insVLR (3-aa insertion in the interdomain cleft) in ICGC_PA65 — stabilizes a dimeric form of BRAF, confirmed by co-immunoprecipitation, and increased ERK phosphorylation as effectively as BRAF V600E.
- Two cases (ICGC_PA117, ICGC_PA142) harbored compound [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) mutations on the same allele (E63K/R73M and L19F/Q22K respectively) — non-hotspot residues, distinct from classical codon 12/13/61 hotspots.
- All but one cerebellar tumor in the series harbored a [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) fusion (the exception had a [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) alteration); 9/48 (19%) non-cerebellar tumors lacked any classical MAPK alteration on initial screening.
- Two novel [NTRK2](../genes/NTRK2.md) fusions identified in 3 non-cerebellar samples: [QKI](../genes/QKI.md):[NTRK2](../genes/NTRK2.md) and [NACC2](../genes/NACC2.md):[NTRK2](../genes/NTRK2.md); both 5' partners contain dimerization domains, predicted to drive ligand-independent NTRK2 (TrkB) dimerization. Verified by PCR.
- Recurrent [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) kinase-domain hotspot mutations (N546, K656) in 5/96 WGS tumors plus 9/45 in the screening cohort (14 total).
- One additional [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) alteration (ICGC_PA89): a ~4.5 kb internal tandem duplication (ITD) of the portion encoding the kinase domain, reminiscent of [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md) ITDs in [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md).
- Strong, diffuse phospho-FGFR1 immunohistochemistry in 7/7 [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md)-mutant tumors vs. 0/11 [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md)-WT tumors; all samples were strongly phospho-ERK positive.
- Recurrent [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) (SHP-2) mutations E69K and E76A — both in [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md)-mutant tumors (ICGC_PA84 and ICGC_PA166); one additional E69K in the screening cohort, again in an [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md)-mutant case.
- In vitro: [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) mutant alone did not elevate pERK; [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) mutants alone or with mutant [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) upregulated pERK — supporting [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) as a modifier of [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md)-driven activation.
- [FGF2](../genes/FGF2.md) and [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) (SHP-2) expression were significantly elevated in pilocytic astrocytoma vs. 158 other astrocytomas or normal tissues — present in both [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md)-mutant and WT tumors, suggesting a broader ligand-mediated activation role.
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), and [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) were the only genes significantly mutated by Genome MuSiC (max-FDR 0.05); except for [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md)+[PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) co-occurrence, alterations were mutually exclusive (permutation test P < 0.0001).
- All [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md)-mutant pilocytic astrocytomas were extra-cerebellar, mostly midline — overlapping the anatomic distribution of [H3-3A](../genes/H3-3A.md) K27M-mutant gliomas.
- Cross-disease finding: 3/48 (6%) pediatric [GB](../cancer_types/GB.md) samples from a prior exome cohort harbored the same triple constellation of [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) activation + [H3-3A](../genes/H3-3A.md) K27M + somatic [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) alteration; all three were [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)-WT. One previously misclassified medulloblastoma case carried [H3-3A](../genes/H3-3A.md) K27M + [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) + [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) K659E (homologous to [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) K656E), totaling 5 triple-mutant cases.

## Genes & alterations

- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — activating fusions ([KIAA1549](../genes/KIAA1549.md):BRAF in 70/96; novel partners [RNF130](../genes/RNF130.md) n=2, [CLCN6](../genes/CLCN6.md), [MKRN1](../genes/MKRN1.md), [GNAI1](../genes/GNAI1.md); plus [FAM131B](../genes/FAM131B.md):BRAF), V600E in 4 tumors, p.ins599T in 1, and novel p.R506_insVLR (interdomain-cleft insertion that stabilizes dimerization and matches BRAF V600E for ERK phosphorylation).
- [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) — recurrent kinase-domain hotspot mutations at N546 and K656 (5/96 WGS + 9/45 screening cohort); plus a ~4.5 kb kinase-domain internal tandem duplication (ICGC_PA89). Mutants drive ERK phosphorylation in NIH3T3. Phospho-FGFR1 IHC positive in all FGFR1-mutant tumors examined. All FGFR1-mutant tumors were extra-cerebellar, mostly midline. Same [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) mutations recurred in 3/48 pediatric glioblastomas in a published exome cohort.
- [NTRK2](../genes/NTRK2.md) — two novel kinase-domain-retaining fusions in 3 non-cerebellar samples: [QKI](../genes/QKI.md):NTRK2 and [NACC2](../genes/NACC2.md):NTRK2; 5' partners contribute dimerization domains, predicted to confer ligand-independent activation.
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — non-hotspot compound mutations on the same allele in 2 cases (E63K/R73M; L19F/Q22K) — distinct from classical codon 12/13/61 hotspots; one cerebellar tumor lacking a BRAF fusion harbored a [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) alteration.
- [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) — E69K and E76A activating mutations (previously described in juvenile myelomonocytic leukemia), exclusively co-occurring with [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) mutations; PTPN11/SHP-2 expression also globally elevated in pilocytic astrocytoma.
- [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) — germline + somatic second hit in 3 NF1-associated cases (classical tumor-suppressor model); somatic [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) alterations in pediatric glioblastomas with FGFR1+[H3-3A](../genes/H3-3A.md) K27M.
- [H3-3A](../genes/H3-3A.md) (formerly H3F3A) — K27M mutation co-occurring with [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) activation and [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) alteration in one ambiguous pilocytic astrocytoma/[GB](../cancer_types/GB.md) case (ICGC_PA69) and in 3/48 pediatric [GB](../cancer_types/GB.md) samples.
- [FGF2](../genes/FGF2.md) — significantly increased expression in pilocytic astrocytoma vs. other astrocytomas/normal tissues; not restricted to FGFR1 status, suggesting ligand-mediated MAPK activation across the entity.
- [KIAA1549](../genes/KIAA1549.md) — most frequent 5' fusion partner for [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) in pilocytic astrocytoma (70/96 cases).
- [FAM131B](../genes/FAM131B.md) — alternative 5' fusion partner for [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) (1 case).
- [RNF130](../genes/RNF130.md), [CLCN6](../genes/CLCN6.md), [MKRN1](../genes/MKRN1.md), [GNAI1](../genes/GNAI1.md) — newly identified 5' fusion partners for [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md).
- [QKI](../genes/QKI.md), [NACC2](../genes/NACC2.md) — newly identified 5' fusion partners for [NTRK2](../genes/NTRK2.md); both contribute dimerization domains.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — wild-type in all triple-mutant [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md)/[H3-3A](../genes/H3-3A.md) K27M/[NF1](../genes/NF1.md) pediatric [GB](../cancer_types/GB.md) cases (distinct from most K27M-mutant GBM/DIPG, which typically carry [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations).
- [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) — K659E mutation (homologous to [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) K656E) observed in a triple-mutant case originally classified as medulloblastoma but reclassified by expression as glioblastoma.
- [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md), [TACC1](../genes/TACC1.md), [TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md), [RAF1](../genes/RAF1.md), [NTRK1](../genes/NTRK1.md), [NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md), [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md) — discussed as background context (homologous [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md):TACC3/FGFR1:[TACC1](../genes/TACC1.md) fusions in adult [GB](../cancer_types/GB.md); analogous NTRK1/NTRK3 fusions in other cancers; [RAF1](../genes/RAF1.md) fusions in pilocytic astrocytoma; [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md) ITDs in AML serve as the conceptual parallel for the FGFR1 kinase-domain ITD).

## Clinical implications

- Identifies [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md), [NTRK2](../genes/NTRK2.md), and MEK inhibition as rational targets for the ~5–20% of non-cerebellar pilocytic astrocytomas lacking [KIAA1549](../genes/KIAA1549.md):[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), where surgical resection is often impossible and tumors progress chronically.
- BRAF V600E-specific agents are a logical option for ~5% of pilocytic astrocytomas with V600E. The authors caution against non-V600E-selective BRAF inhibitors in [KIAA1549](../genes/KIAA1549.md):BRAF-fusion tumors (the majority) because of preclinical evidence of paradoxical MAPK activation in that context.
- Combination strategies (FGFR + NTRK2 + MEK inhibitors) may be warranted given that downstream NTRK2 signaling converges on the MAPK pathway.
- The discovery of the same [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) hotspot mutations in 3/48 pediatric [GB](../cancer_types/GB.md) with [H3-3A](../genes/H3-3A.md) K27M and [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) loss extends a potential FGFR-targeted therapeutic axis to a clinically devastating tumor subset.
- Diagnostic implication: phospho-FGFR1 IHC reliably stratifies FGFR1-mutant vs. wild-type tumors and may be deployable as a clinical assay.

## Limitations & open questions

- The downstream signaling and transforming potential of the two non-hotspot compound [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) mutations (E63K/R73M; L19F/Q22K) on the same allele remain to be functionally characterized.
- ICGC_PA69 carried the triple-mutation constellation ([FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) + [H3-3A](../genes/H3-3A.md) K27M + [NF1](../genes/NF1.md)) and was histologically read as pilocytic astrocytoma by three neuropathologists, but a [GB](../cancer_types/GB.md) diagnosis could not be conclusively excluded due to limited material — the biological identity of triple-mutant tumors is unresolved.
- Why the [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) + [H3-3A](../genes/H3-3A.md) K27M + [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) mutations occur in concert, and whether they together specify a fate of oncogene-induced senescence (pilocytic astrocytoma) vs. aggressive malignancy ([GB](../cancer_types/GB.md)), is unknown.
- The NF1-associated cohort (3/96) is smaller than the prospective 5–10% rate, reflecting limited tissue availability from optic-pathway tumors.
- The authors do not report functional dissection of every novel [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) fusion partner ([CLCN6](../genes/CLCN6.md), [MKRN1](../genes/MKRN1.md), [GNAI1](../genes/GNAI1.md)).
- Mate-pair coverage limited to 68/96 cases, so some structural variants may still be undetected.
- The model that NF1 loss may mimic elevated [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) activity (both blocking RasGAP recruitment) is proposed but not directly tested.
