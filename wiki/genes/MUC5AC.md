# MUC5AC

## Overview

MUC5AC is a secreted gel-forming mucin glycoprotein. In pancreatic cancer, it is among the most upregulated secreted proteins relative to normal tissue and is enriched even in early-stage tumors, making it a strong candidate for serum- or pancreatic-juice-based early detection.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Mucin glycoprotein upregulated in [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md) vs normal tissue; enriched even in early-stage tumors and among the proteins nominated as candidate serum/pancreatic-juice early-detection biomarkers in a proteogenomic landscape study of 140 treatment-naive pancreatic tumors [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md):** MUC5AC protein overexpression observed in pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma relative to normal adjacent tissue; proposed as a secreted early-detection biomarker [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-overexpressed with [MUC1](../genes/MUC1.md) and [MUC13](../genes/MUC13.md) in [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md) tumors vs normal tissue; all three mucin glycoproteins enriched in early-stage disease [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting in the corpus. As a secreted protein enriched in early-stage PAAD, MUC5AC is a candidate non-invasive detection biomarker pending prospective validation [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md).

## Open questions

- MUC5AC has not been prospectively validated as a standalone serum biomarker; cited findings are from a discovery-phase proteogenomic study [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md).
