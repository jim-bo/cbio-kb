**PMID:** 26091043 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.cell.2015.05.044 · **Journal:** Cell (2015)

## TL;DR

The TCGA Network performed integrated multi-platform genomic, transcriptomic, and proteomic characterization of 333 primary and/or metastatic cutaneous melanomas from 331 patients, establishing a framework for genomic classification into four subtypes based on the most prevalent significantly mutated genes: **[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)-mutant** (52%), **RAS-mutant** ([NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md)/[HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md)/[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) hot-spot, 28% [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) alone), **[NF1](../genes/NF1.md)-mutant** (14%), and **Triple-WT** (wild-type for all three, n = 46). The Triple-WT subtype was enriched for [KIT](../genes/KIT.md) mutations, focal amplifications of receptor tyrosine kinases ([KIT](../genes/KIT.md), [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md), [KDR](../genes/KDR.md)), and complex structural rearrangements, with notably few [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter mutations and UV signatures. Although genomic subtype did not correlate with post-accession survival, a transcriptomic "immune" subclass — characterized by high immune-gene expression, high lymphocyte score on pathology review, and elevated [LCK](../genes/LCK.md) protein — was associated with significantly improved survival in patients with regional metastatic disease.

## Cohort & data

- **333 cutaneous melanoma samples from 331 adult patients** across 14 tissue source sites: 67 (20%) primary cutaneous melanomas (all non-glabrous skin) and 266 (80%) metastases. Of the metastases, 160 were from regional lymph nodes, 52 from regional skin/soft tissue, and 35 from distant sites. Median primary thickness 2.7 mm (mean 4.9 mm).
- **Cancer type**: [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md) (Skin Cutaneous Melanoma, OncoTree).
- **Dataset**: [skcm_tcga_pub_2015](../datasets/skcm_tcga_pub_2015.md) (cBioPortal study id; raw data at GDAC Firehose `stddata__2013_11_14` and the TCGA [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md) Data Portal).
- **Assays / platforms**:
  - [[whole-exome-sequencing|WES]] on 320 samples (paired tumor + germline for 318 patients; Agilent SureSelect Human All Exon v2.0 44 Mb, Illumina HiSeq 2 × 76 bp PE, ~87× mean exon coverage).
  - DNA copy-number profiling on 333 samples ([Affymetrix SNP 6.0](../methods/affymetrix-snp6.md)).
  - [mRNA sequencing](../methods/rna-seq.md) on 331 samples (Illumina mRNA TruSeq, HiSeq 2000).
  - [microRNA sequencing](../methods/mirna-seq.md) on 323 samples.
  - DNA methylation profiling on 333 samples ([Illumina 450K](../methods/450k-methylation-array.md)).
  - [Reverse-phase protein array](../methods/rppa.md) (RPPA, 181 cancer-related proteins/phosphoproteins) on 202 samples.
  - [[whole-genome-sequencing|WGS]] (deep) on 38 samples; low-pass WGS on 119 samples.
  - PCR-Sanger [sequencing](../methods/sanger-sequencing.md) of [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter C228T/C250T in 115 samples.
- Complete six-platform data available on a core set of 199 samples.
- Significantly mutated genes called by **[MutSig](../methods/mutsig.md)** (Q < 0.1) and **[InVEx](../methods/invex.md)** (Bonferroni p < 0.05 or Q < 0.1).
- Copy-number purity/ploidy via [ABSOLUTE](../methods/absolute.md); integrative subtypes via [iCluster](../methods/icluster.md); transcriptomic clusters via [consensus-hierarchical-clustering](../methods/consensus-hierarchical-clustering.md); complex structural rearrangements via [ShatterSeek](../methods/shatterseek.md); reads aligned with [BWA](../methods/bwa.md) / Picard / Firehose at the Broad.

## Key findings

- **Mutation burden**: mean 16.8 mutations/Mb — the highest reported for any TCGA tumor type at the time. 228,987 total mutations (SNVs + indels) identified across 318 WES samples; 96% targeted-validation rate on 455 SMG SNVs.
- **UV signature**: 76% (44/58) of primaries and 84% (221/262) of metastases had a UV signature (C>T at dipyrimidines > 60% or CC>TT > 5%); median C>T at dipyrimidines 77.7%; median CC>TT 3.9%.
- **13 InVEx SMGs / 42 MutSig SMGs**: [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [RAC1](../genes/RAC1.md) (UV hot-spot, 6.9%), [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md), [PPP6C](../genes/PPP6C.md), [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) (R132, ~6%, UV hot-spot 6.2%), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), and novel candidate [DDX3X](../genes/DDX3X.md) (RNA helicase). MutSig also flagged [MRPS31](../genes/MRPS31.md) and [RPS27](../genes/RPS27.md) ribosomal-protein SMGs with UV-induced 5′ UTR hot-spots (~5% and ~9%).
- **Four genomic subtypes** (n = 318 WES cases):
  - **[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) subtype (52%, n = 166)**: 145 V600 alterations — V600E (124), V600K (18), V600R (3); 5 K601 mutations. [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600/K601 anti-correlated with [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) hot-spots (Fisher's p < 1e–15); non-hot-spot BRAF mutations co-occurred with [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md)/[HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md)/[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) and [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) mutations.
  - **RAS subtype (28% [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md), n = 88)**: NRAS hot-spots Q61R (35), Q61K (28), Q61L (11), Q61H (4), G12R/D/A (4), G13R/D (3); 4 [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) (G13D, G13S, Q61K×2) and 3 [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) (G12D, G12R, Q61R) — all mutually exclusive with BRAF V600/K601 and NRAS.
  - **[NF1](../genes/NF1.md) subtype (14%, n = 28)**: 65 mutations; >50% LoF (27 nonsense, 9 splice, 4 frameshift; InVEx LoF p = 1.8e–11, Q = 9.1e–12). Highest mutation prevalence (39 mut/Mb, ~2× other subtypes). NF1 mutated in 38.7% of non-hot-spot BRAF/NRAS samples (29/75) and ~70% (26/38) of those with UV signature. Anti-correlated with hot-spot BRAF (p = 1.93e–9).
  - **Triple-WT subtype (n = 46)**: no hot-spot BRAF/RAS/NF1. Recurrent COSMIC alterations included [KIT](../genes/KIT.md) (n = 6), [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) (n = 3), [GNA11](../genes/GNA11.md) Q209L (n = 2), [GNAQ](../genes/GNAQ.md) Q209P (n = 1), [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) (n = 1). Two [GNA11](../genes/GNA11.md) hot-spot tumors co-occurred with [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md) R625H but lacked [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md) mutations (contrast with uveal melanoma).
- **UV signature is depleted in Triple-WT**: only 30% (14/46) Triple-WT vs 90.7% BRAF / 93.5% RAS / 92.9% NF1 (Fisher's p = 1e–15).
- **Copy-number landscape (Triple-WT-enriched)**: significantly more CN segments; focal amplifications of [KIT](../genes/KIT.md) / [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md) / [KDR](../genes/KDR.md) (4q12), [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md), [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md), [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md), [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) (p < 0.01–0.05, FDR < 0.05). In the BRAF subtype: focal amplifications of [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [MITF](../genes/MITF.md) (melanocyte lineage oncogene; p < 0.01, FDR < 0.05), and [CD274](../genes/CD274.md) (PD-L1 ligand). [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) amplifications co-occurred with NRAS mutations.
- **Structural rearrangements**: 224 candidate fusion drivers identified by integrative CNA/RNA-seq/WGS analysis. Recurrent partner promiscuity around [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) (ATG7-BRAF, TAX1BP1-BRAF), [RAF1](../genes/RAF1.md) (TRAK1-RAF1, RAF1-AGGF1, CLCN6-RAF1), [AKT3](../genes/AKT3.md) (CEP170-AKT3, AKT3-PLD5, ZEB2-AKT3, ARHGAP30-AKT3), 3 [MITF](../genes/MITF.md) fusions (MITF-[FOXP1](../genes/FOXP1.md), CADM2-MITF, FRMD4B-MITF), and 3 [HMGA2](../genes/HMGA2.md) fusions. 8 fusions had a predicted intact kinase domain. Driver fusions and complex rearrangements (ShatterSeek) significantly enriched in Triple-WT (fusion enrichment p = 2e–04; complex rearrangement 11/16, Fisher's p = 0.00098); 7/7 Triple-WT samples with complex rearrangements lacked UV signature.
- **[TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter mutations**: C228T (23.5%) and C250T (40.9%) mutually exclusive (n = 115). Only C228T was associated with elevated TERT mRNA (rank-sum p = 0.001), contrasting with [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) (difg_glass) where both increase expression. Frequencies by subtype: BRAF 75% (39/52), RAS 71.9% (23/32), NF1 83.3% (10/12), Triple-WT only 6.7% (1/15) (p = 8e–5) — suggesting alternative TERT activation (amplification/rearrangement) in Triple-WT.
- **CIMP phenotype**: strongest associations with [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) (OR = 4.05, p = 0.005) and [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md) (OR = 3.5, p = 0.0003) — both chromatin-related. Higher NRAS hot-spot (OR = 2.3, p = 0.003) and lower BRAF hot-spot (OR = 0.4, p = 0.0008) in CIMP cluster.
- **RPPA signaling differences**: phospho-MEK1/2 (S217/S221) elevated in BRAF and RAS subtypes; highest phospho-ERK1/2 ([MAPK1](../genes/MAPK1.md)/[MAPK3](../genes/MAPK3.md) T202/Y204) in RAS subtype; highest [KIT](../genes/KIT.md) protein abundance in Triple-WT; highest CRAF expression in NF1 subtype; highest [BCL2](../genes/BCL2.md) in Triple-WT; elevated [IGFBP2](../genes/IGFBP2.md) in BRAF hot-spot mutants.
- **Pathway integration (n = 318)**: RAS-MAPK-AKT altered in 91%, [RB1](../genes/RB1.md)/[CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) cell-cycle in 69%, [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md)/[TP53](../genes/TP53.md) apoptosis in 19%. 46/49 (93.9%) [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations and all 12 [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) mutations occurred in UV-signature samples. [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) mutations/deletions enriched in BRAF-mutant; [AKT3](../genes/AKT3.md) amplification/overexpression enriched in RAS, NF1, and Triple-WT vs BRAF (p < 0.05).
- **Three transcriptomic subclasses (n = 329, top-1,500 variant genes)**:
  - **"Immune" (51%, n = 168)** — enriched for T-cell, B-cell, NK-cell, checkpoint, cytokine genes; favorable post-accession survival (log-rank p = 0.003 among regional metastases). Not driven by lymph-node tissue admixture (no signature difference between LN and non-LN samples).
  - **"Keratin" (31%, n = 102)** — high keratin/pigmentation/epithelial expression; 74% of primaries clustered here; worst regional-metastasis outcome (log-rank p = 0.0007 vs Immune/MITF-low).
  - **"[MITF](../genes/MITF.md)-low" (18%, n = 59)** — low [MITF](../genes/MITF.md) target / pigmentation / epithelial expression; enriched for nervous-system / neuronal-development genes; highest BRAF hot-spot frequency (66% vs 33%/45% for keratin/immune; Fisher's p = 0.0003).
- **iCluster integration** identified an Immune-mRNA + low-CN + normal-like methylation cluster, a hypomethylation + MITF-expression cluster, and a CIMP + keratin + miRNA-cluster-3 group.
- **Lymphocyte score (LScore)**: standardized pathology review by AWG dermatopathologists. Higher in LN-derived tumors (Wilcoxon p = 5.6e–8). Elevated LScore significantly associated with prolonged post-accession survival in regional metastases; strikingly concordant with assignment to the Immune transcriptomic subclass (Fisher's p < 1e–12).
- **Protein markers ([LCK](../genes/LCK.md), [SYK](../genes/SYK.md))**: both T/B-cell tyrosine kinases were highly expressed in tumors enriched for Immune subclass and high LScore. Only high **[LCK](../genes/LCK.md)** (not [SYK](../genes/SYK.md)) was associated with favorable post-accession survival.
- **Tri-feature prognostic model**: combining LScore + Immune subclass + [LCK](../genes/LCK.md) expression predicted regional-metastasis outcome better than any single feature (log-rank p = 8.0e–6).
- **Bivariate clinical-deployable model**: high LScore + high LCK vs low/low had log-rank p = 7.9e–5, HR = 5.5; both terms independently significant in multivariable Cox regression — proposed as a more practical replacement for unsupervised transcriptomic clustering.

## Genes & alterations

- **[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)** — 52% mutated; V600E (n = 124), V600K (18), V600R (3), K601 (5); recurrent fusion partners ATG7 and TAX1BP1 (kinase domain intact); focal amplifications in BRAF subtype. Anti-correlated with hot-spot NRAS and NF1.
- **[NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md)** — 28% mutated; Q61R/K/L/H dominant; mutually exclusive with hot-spot [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600/K601; amplifications co-occur with NRAS mutations.
- **[HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) / [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)** — rare hot-spot mutations (HRAS G13D/G13S/Q61K×2; KRAS G12D/G12R/Q61R); mutually exclusive with NRAS and BRAF V600/K601.
- **[NF1](../genes/NF1.md)** — 14% mutated; >50% LoF; defines third MAPK-activating subtype; ~70% of non-hot-spot BRAF/NRAS UV-signature tumors carry NF1 mutations.
- **[RAC1](../genes/RAC1.md)** — UV hot-spot in 6.9%; previously linked to BRAF-inhibitor resistance (Van Allen et al., 2014; Watson et al., 2014).
- **[IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md)** — R132 in ~6%; UV hot-spot; enriched in CIMP cluster (OR = 4.05, p = 0.005).
- **[ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md)** — chromatin-remodeling SMG strongly enriched in CIMP (OR = 3.5, p = 0.0003); frequently co-occurs with [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) — proposed synthetic-lethal vulnerability for chromatin-modifier targeting.
- **[PPP6C](../genes/PPP6C.md)** — hot-spot SMG frequently co-occurring with RAS mutations; proposed Aurora kinase inhibitor combination target.
- **[MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md)** — recurrent SMG in MAPK pathway.
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)** — 49 mutations; 93.9% (46/49) in UV-signature samples; enriched in BRAF/RAS/NF1 vs Triple-WT.
- **[RB1](../genes/RB1.md)** — 12 mutations, all in UV-signature samples; enriched in NF1 subtype.
- **[CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) / [CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md)** — alterations evenly distributed across subtypes.
- **[CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md), [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md)** — focal amplifications enriched in Triple-WT.
- **[PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md)** — mutations/deletions enriched in BRAF-mutant melanomas.
- **[AKT3](../genes/AKT3.md)** — amplification + overexpression and recurrent fusions (CEP170-AKT3, AKT3-PLD5, ZEB2-AKT3, ARHGAP30-AKT3) enriched in RAS/NF1/Triple-WT vs BRAF (p < 0.05).
- **[PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) / [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md)** — recurrent mutations ([PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) E545K, H1047L; AKT1/3 E17K) nominated as biomarkers for combination MEK + PI3K/AKT/mTOR therapy.
- **[KIT](../genes/KIT.md)** — mutations and 4q12 focal amplifications enriched in Triple-WT; highest KIT protein abundance in Triple-WT by RPPA; rationale for [imatinib](../drugs/imatinib.md)/[dasatinib](../drugs/dasatinib.md).
- **[PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md), [KDR](../genes/KDR.md)** — co-amplified with [KIT](../genes/KIT.md) at 4q12 in Triple-WT; rationale for [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md), [crenolanib](../drugs/crenolanib.md), [regorafenib](../drugs/regorafenib.md), [pazopanib](../drugs/pazopanib.md).
- **[MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md)** — focal amplifications enriched in Triple-WT; rationale for [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) inhibitors ([nutlin-3](../drugs/nutlin-3.md), [amg-232](../drugs/amg-232.md)).
- **[TERT](../genes/TERT.md)** — promoter C228T (23.5%) and C250T (40.9%) mutually exclusive; only C228T raises TERT mRNA; depleted in Triple-WT (6.7%, p = 8e–5) — Triple-WT activates TERT via amplification or rearrangement.
- **[MITF](../genes/MITF.md)** — focal amplifications in BRAF subtype; recurrent fusions (MITF-[FOXP1](../genes/FOXP1.md), CADM2-MITF, FRMD4B-MITF); defines low-expression transcriptomic subclass.
- **[CD274](../genes/CD274.md)** (PD-L1) — focal amplifications in BRAF subtype; relevant to [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md)/[nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md) biomarker hypothesis.
- **[PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md)** (PD-1) — expression elevated in Immune transcriptomic subclass.
- **[GNAQ](../genes/GNAQ.md) / [GNA11](../genes/GNA11.md)** — rare Q209 hot-spots in Triple-WT (typically uveal-melanoma driver); co-occur with [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md) R625H but not [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md) mutations in this cutaneous cohort.
- **[SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md)** — R625H hot-spot, co-occurring with [GNAQ](../genes/GNAQ.md)/[GNA11](../genes/GNA11.md) in Triple-WT.
- **[CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md)** — recurrent COSMIC mutations (n = 3) in Triple-WT.
- **[EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md)** — Y641 hot-spot in Triple-WT (n = 1).
- **[BCL2](../genes/BCL2.md)** — highest median protein expression in Triple-WT (RPPA); rationale for BH3 mimetics.
- **[LCK](../genes/LCK.md)** — high RPPA expression in Immune subclass tumors; independent favorable prognostic marker.
- **[SYK](../genes/SYK.md)** — high RPPA expression in Immune subclass but NOT prognostic — suggests T-cell (LCK) rather than B-cell ([SYK](../genes/SYK.md)) signaling drives the survival benefit.
- **[RAF1](../genes/RAF1.md)** — recurrent fusion partner (TRAK1-RAF1, RAF1-AGGF1, CLCN6-RAF1) preserving intact kinase domain.
- **[HMGA2](../genes/HMGA2.md)** — 3 recurrent fusions (PCBP2-HMGA2, TSFM-HMGA2, SENP1-HMGA2).
- **[MAPK1](../genes/MAPK1.md) / [MAPK3](../genes/MAPK3.md)** — phospho-ERK1/2 (T202/Y204) highest in RAS subtype by RPPA.
- **[IGFBP2](../genes/IGFBP2.md)** — elevated in BRAF hot-spot mutants by RPPA.
- **[DDX3X](../genes/DDX3X.md)** — novel candidate SMG (RNA helicase), previously unreported in melanoma.
- **[MRPS31](../genes/MRPS31.md), [RPS27](../genes/RPS27.md)** — ribosomal-protein SMGs with UV-induced 5′ UTR hot-spots (~5% and ~9%); [RPS27](../genes/RPS27.md) 5′ TOP element regulates translation downstream of PI(3)K/AKT/mTOR.

## Clinical implications

- **Therapy stratification by subtype**:
  - **BRAF**: targetable with FDA-approved [vemurafenib](../drugs/vemurafenib.md), [dabrafenib](../drugs/dabrafenib.md), [trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md).
  - **RAS**: combinatorial MAPK + [PPP6C](../genes/PPP6C.md)-context Aurora kinase inhibition (Gold et al., 2014).
  - **NF1**: MEK and/or ERK inhibitors (extending [trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md) to BRAF-wt NF1-mutant patients); synthetic lethal targeting of chromatin modifiers in co-occurring [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md)-mutant tumors.
  - **Triple-WT**: [imatinib](../drugs/imatinib.md) / [dasatinib](../drugs/dasatinib.md) for [KIT](../genes/KIT.md) mutation/amplification (Carvajal et al., 2011; Hodi et al., 2008); combination with [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md) / [crenolanib](../drugs/crenolanib.md) / [regorafenib](../drugs/regorafenib.md) / [pazopanib](../drugs/pazopanib.md) to target co-amplified [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md) / [KDR](../genes/KDR.md); [nutlin-3](../drugs/nutlin-3.md) / [amg-232](../drugs/amg-232.md) / BH3-mimetics for [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md)-amplified, [BCL2](../genes/BCL2.md)-high tumors.
- **PI3K/AKT/mTOR combination**: [AKT3](../genes/AKT3.md) amplification/overexpression in RAS/NF1/Triple-WT and [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) / [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) hot-spots in BRAF/RAS subtypes support combination MEK + PI3K/AKT/mTOR trials.
- **Immune checkpoint blockade biomarkers**: [CD274](../genes/CD274.md) (PD-L1) and [PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md) (PD-1) expression highest in the Immune transcriptomic subclass; [CD274](../genes/CD274.md) focal amplifications observed in the BRAF subtype. The authors caveat that the data do not prove the immune subtype is uniquely responsive to [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md)/[nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md)/[ipilimumab](../drugs/ipilimumab.md), but the parallels with Tumeh et al. (2014) and existing [aldesleukin](../drugs/aldesleukin.md)/[interferon-alpha](../drugs/interferon-alpha.md) data are emphasized.
- **Prognostic biomarker for stage III (regional metastasis)**: a bivariate model of pathology-derived **LScore** + RPPA **[LCK](../genes/LCK.md) expression** predicts survival (HR = 5.5 for both-high vs both-low; log-rank p = 7.9e–5). Both features are independent in multivariable Cox regression and the authors propose integration into future AJCC staging and adjuvant-therapy decision frameworks.
- **No genomic subtype × outcome correlation**: BRAF/RAS/NF1/Triple-WT assignment did not predict post-accession survival — consistent with the absence of preferential anti-tumor responses by BRAF status in checkpoint trials (Ascierto et al., 2014; Robert et al., 2014).
- **TERT activation in Triple-WT**: targeted-therapy programs for TERT-driven tumors should consider amplification/rearrangement-based (rather than promoter-mutation-based) TERT activation in this subtype.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Advanced-tumor bias**: specimens required sufficient mass and quality, so thick primaries and metastases dominate. Population thickness was higher than registry baselines; early-stage primaries are under-represented.
- **Only two matched primary + metastasis pairs** had complete multi-platform data — limits inference about clonal evolution and metastatic progression.
- **Genomic subtype did not correlate with survival** — the authors flag this as a key open question about what does drive outcome differences within and across subtypes.
- **Functional validation gap**: novel SMG [DDX3X](../genes/DDX3X.md) and the ribosomal SMGs [MRPS31](../genes/MRPS31.md) / [RPS27](../genes/RPS27.md) are nominated but not functionally characterized as melanoma drivers.
- **Immune subtype ≠ proven immunotherapy responders**: the cohort lacks paired checkpoint-blockade outcomes, so the predictive (rather than prognostic) value of the Immune signature for [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md) / [nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md) / [ipilimumab](../drugs/ipilimumab.md) response remains untested.
- **Fusion functional validation**: 224 candidate driver fusions are nominated; only kinase-domain intactness is reported. Transforming activity, kinase activation, and drug sensitivity of fusions like ATG7-[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) and TRAK1-[RAF1](../genes/RAF1.md) await follow-up.
- **Antigen / neoepitope hypothesis**: whether specific mutated melanoma antigens drive the lymphocyte infiltration phenotype is unresolved (cited Robbins et al., 2013; Snyder et al., 2014).
- **TERT C250T paradox**: C250T was as frequent as C228T but only C228T raised TERT mRNA — the mechanistic explanation differs from [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) (difg_glass) (where both raise expression) and remains unexplained.
- **Triple-WT heterogeneity**: this is explicitly a residual category; only ~30% had UV signatures and a substantial fraction lacked any of the recurrent COSMIC alterations interrogated.
- **Generalizability**: the "keratin" subclass had worse outcomes but is partially confounded with primary-tumor origin; whether keratin expression itself is prognostic vs reflecting site of origin is unsettled.
