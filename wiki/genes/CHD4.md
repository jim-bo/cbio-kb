# CHD4

## Overview

CHD4 (Chromodomain Helicase DNA Binding Protein 4) is the catalytic subunit of the NuRD (Nucleosome Remodeling and Deacetylase) complex. It plays roles in chromatin remodeling, transcriptional regulation, and DNA damage response. In cancer, CHD4 loss-of-function mutations have been described as invasion-associated events in breast cancer, and CHD4 alterations have been identified across multiple tumor types.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- CHD4 mutation identified as an IDC-NST–private (invasive-stage-restricted) alteration in the synchronous DCIS/IDC-NST breast cancer WES cohort (n=25 patients), suggesting CHD4 inactivation may be acquired during progression from [DCIS](../cancer_types/DCIS.md) to invasive disease [PMID:32220886](../papers/32220886.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **BRCA/IDC**: CHD4 mutations found among invasive-restricted alterations in synchronous DCIS/IDC-NST pairs, consistent with acquisition during invasive transition [PMID:32220886](../papers/32220886.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Observed co-occurring with other IDC-NST–private alterations including [GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md), [SOX2](../genes/SOX2.md), [SPEN](../genes/SPEN.md), [SMO](../genes/SMO.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [NCOA1](../genes/NCOA1.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [NBN](../genes/NBN.md), [GNAQ](../genes/GNAQ.md), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), and [TET2](../genes/TET2.md) in the invasive component of synchronous breast tumors [PMID:32220886](../papers/32220886.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic claims reported in this corpus.

## Open questions

- Whether CHD4 inactivation is a driver or passenger of DCIS-to-invasive progression requires functional validation; the breast cohort study was observational and did not test CHD4 as a causal progression event [PMID:32220886](../papers/32220886.md).
