**PMID:** 33106634 · **DOI:** [10.1038/s41588-020-00710-0](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41588-020-00710-0) · **Journal:** Nature Genetics (2020)

## TL;DR

Using prospective MSK-IMPACT targeted sequencing of paired blood and tumor from 24,146 solid-tumor cancer patients, Bolton et al. show that the fitness landscape of clonal hematopoiesis (CH) is shaped in a gene-specific way by environmental and therapeutic exposures: smoking selects for [ASXL1](../genes/ASXL1.md) mutations, while cancer therapy (especially platinum agents, topoisomerase II inhibitors, and external beam radiation) selects for clones with DNA damage response (DDR) mutations in [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [PPM1D](../genes/PPM1D.md), and [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md). Sequential blood sampling in 525 patients provided direct in-human evidence that DDR clones outcompete other CH clones under therapy but not in its absence. In 19/35 paired CH→tMN cases the leukemia-defining mutation was already present at the CH timepoint, and CH was a strong predictor of subsequent therapy-related myeloid neoplasm (tMN) risk (HR=6.9 for CH VAF>2%) in a 9,437-patient pooled analysis. Mutation calls are released as [msk_ch_2020](../datasets/msk_ch_2020.md) on cBioPortal. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)

## Cohort & data

- **Cross-sectional cohort:** 24,146 cancer patients with matched tumor + blood [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) sequencing on protocol NCT01775072, prior to July 1, 2018 — 56 primary tumor types represented. CH defined as a blood-only variant at VAF ≥2% with ≥10 supporting reads. Dataset released as [msk_ch_2020](../datasets/msk_ch_2020.md) on cBioPortal. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- **Treatment-annotated subcohort:** 10,138 patients with complete cancer-care histories at MSK; 5,978 (59%) exposed to cancer therapy prior to blood draw, 4,160 (41%) treatment-naive. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- **Serial sampling cohort:** 525 patients with solid tumors and ≥2 blood draws (median interval 23 months, range 6–53). 389 (74%) had CH at first draw; 590/620 (95%) of CH mutations were present at both time points. None developed secondary hematologic malignancies during follow-up. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- **CH→tMN cohort:** 35 paired pre-tMN and tMN samples (median interval 24 months, range 5–90). [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- **Pooled tMN risk cohort:** 9,437 cancer patients exposed to therapy (MSK + Gillis MOF n=68 + Takahashi MDA n=67 + Gibson DFC n=401), of whom 75 developed tMN. AML/MDS risk reference panel: Abelson PMC (n=969) + Young WSU (n=103) for healthy individuals. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- **Assays:** [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) — 341-gene ([IMPACT341](../methods/IMPACT341.md)), 410-gene ([IMPACT410](../methods/IMPACT410.md)), and 468-gene ([IMPACT468](../methods/IMPACT468.md)) versions. Serial-sampling repeat CH testing used a comparable 163-gene myeloid capture panel (median depth 665×, range 111–1987×). Median blood coverage on MSK-IMPACT 497×; tumor 790×. Reference genome hg19. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- **Calling stack:** [BWA](../methods/bwa.md) 0.7.5a → ABRA 0.92 → [GATK](../methods/gatk.md) 3.3-0 base-quality recalibration; SNVs by [MuTect](../methods/mutect.md) + [VarDict](../methods/vardict.md) (retained if called by both); indels by Somatic Indel Detector + VarDict; annotated with [VEP](../methods/vep.md) v86 and [OncoKB](../methods/oncokb.md). Filters: gnomAD frequency ≤0.005, blood/tumor VAF ratio ≥2× (≥1.5× for lymph-node biopsies), homopolymer/strand-bias filters. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- **Selection inference:** [dNdScv](../methods/dndscv.md) limited to IMPACT-410/468 with q<0.1 and ≥25 variants per gene. Absolute-risk modeling used the [iCARE](../methods/icare.md) R package combined with NCCN AML/MDS rates and SEER mortality. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)

## Key findings

- **Prevalence of CH.** 7,216/24,146 patients (30%) carried at least one CH mutation (VAF ≥2%); 11,076 unique CH mutations. Median VAF 5.0% (range 2–78%). 69% of CH carriers had one mutation, 31% had two or more. 52% of CH calls (n=5,810) met CH-PD (putative-driver) criteria; 91% of CH-PD (n=5,301) were recurrent in myeloid neoplasms (CH-myeloid-PD). [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- **Mutational landscape.** Most-frequent CH genes were [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md), [TET2](../genes/TET2.md), and [ASXL1](../genes/ASXL1.md). 48% of all CH mutations fell in myeloid driver genes, even though those genes make up only 20% of the MSK-IMPACT content — indicating a strong fitness advantage in HSPCs for myeloid drivers. Median VAF was higher for myeloid drivers (0.047 vs 0.038, P significant) and for CH-PD (0.050 vs 0.038). [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md) R882 hotspot mutations carried higher VAFs than other [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md) mutations even after adjusting for total mutation count. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- **Clinical determinants of CH.** Age (OR=1.9 per category, p<10⁻⁶), prior cancer therapy (OR=1.3, p=1×10⁻⁶), and smoking (OR=1.1, p=5×10⁻³) were independently associated with CH. CH was less common in Asian than Caucasian patients (OR=0.7, p=1×10⁻³). [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- **Gene × exposure specificity.**
  - Aging (DNMT3A weaker than other CH mutations): spliceosome [SRSF2](../genes/SRSF2.md) (OR=3.6, q=7×10⁻⁶) and [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md) (OR=5.0, q<10⁻⁶) showed the strongest age effects.
  - Smoking: [ASXL1](../genes/ASXL1.md) (OR=2.5, q=1×10⁻⁴; current-smoker OR=3.1, former-smoker OR=2.4).
  - DDR genes × therapy: [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (OR=2.8, q=2×10⁻⁴), [PPM1D](../genes/PPM1D.md) (OR=4.3, q<10⁻⁶), [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md) (OR=4.5, q=6×10⁻⁶).
  - Epigenetic-modifier and spliceosome CH was *not* enriched by therapy exposure. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- **Tumor-type heterogeneity.** [PPM1D](../genes/PPM1D.md) mutations were found in 13% of [ovarian](../cancer_types/OV.md) and 7% of [endometrial](../cancer_types/UCEC.md) cancer patients (vs <5% in other subgroups). Among untreated patients, [PPM1D](../genes/PPM1D.md) CH was seen in 8% of ovarian and 0% of endometrial cancer patients — confirming therapy/exposure context drives most tumor-type heterogeneity. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- **Therapy-class associations.** Strongest CH-PD associations: external-beam radiation (OR=1.4, p<10⁻⁶), cytotoxic chemotherapy (OR=1.2, p=2×10⁻³), radionuclide therapy (OR=1.6, p=0.01). Within cytotoxics: topoisomerase II inhibitors (OR=1.3, p=0.01) and platinum agents (OR=1.2, p=0.02). [Carboplatin](../drugs/carboplatin.md) was strongly associated (OR=1.4, p=0.001) while [cisplatin](../drugs/cisplatin.md) (OR=1.1, p=0.10) and [oxaliplatin](../drugs/oxaliplatin.md) (OR=0.98, p=0.88) were not. Targeted therapy and immunotherapy showed no significant CH association. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- **Gene-specific therapy associations.** [PPM1D](../genes/PPM1D.md) was most strongly linked to platinum (OR=3.2, q<10⁻⁶) and radionuclide therapy (OR=6.2, q=7×10⁻⁶) plus topoisomerase II inhibitors (OR=2.0, q=0.002), taxanes (OR=1.8, q=0.003), topoisomerase I inhibitors (OR=1.7, q=0.002), and external-beam radiation (OR=1.8, q=0.04). [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) was associated with platinum (OR=2.1, q=0.03), radiation (OR=1.8, q=0.04), and taxanes (OR=1.9, q=0.05). [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md) was associated with platinum (OR=2.4, q=0.02) and topoisomerase II inhibitors (OR=2.2, q=0.02). [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- **Dose-response.** Trend tests showed CH-PD risk rose with cumulative platinum exposure (p-trend=0.04), with the strongest effects for [carboplatin](../drugs/carboplatin.md) (p-trend=3×10⁻⁵) and [cisplatin](../drugs/cisplatin.md) (p-trend=0.04). [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- **Longitudinal clonal dynamics.** Of 590 CH mutations seen at both timepoints, 62% (n=367) were stable, 28% (n=164) grew, and 10% (n=59) shrank. Under cytotoxic chemo or external-beam radiation, DDR CH mutations in [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [PPM1D](../genes/PPM1D.md), and [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md) grew fastest. Patients with multiple CH mutations had faster growth (p=0.03). New CH mutations appeared in 4% (n=13) of patients receiving interval cytotoxic/radiation therapy vs 1% (n=2) of those who did not (p=0.06). [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- **In-patient competition.** Among 34 patients each carrying one DDR-CH and one non-DDR-CH mutation, DDR clones outgrew non-DDR clones under cytotoxic or radiation therapy, but were outcompeted by non-DDR clones (e.g. [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md)) in untreated patients — providing direct, gene-controlled evidence of treatment-driven selection. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- **CH→tMN transitions.** In 19/32 (59%) paired CH→tMN cases, ≥1 leukemia-defining mutation was present at the CH timepoint with ≥1 supporting read; 13 of these (41%) harbored two or more. In all such cases the CH mutation was present at tMN diagnosis. 91% of transformations also acquired additional events — chromosomal aneuploidies or mutations in late-progression genes such as [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md). 14/35 (40%) tMN cases carried [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations; 10/14 of these were detectable at the CH stage. At transformation, 12/13 [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)-mutant tMN cases had isolated chromosomal aneuploidies or complex karyotype. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- **tMN risk modeling (n=9,437; 75 events).** CH VAF >2% increased tMN risk with HR=6.9 (p<10⁻⁶), with effect rising with mutation number and clone size. Strongest gene-specific HRs were for [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and spliceosome genes ([SRSF2](../genes/SRSF2.md), [U2AF1](../genes/U2AF1.md), [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md)). Effect sizes for tMN in cancer patients were similar to those for [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md) risk in healthy individuals, suggesting CH-mediated risk is largely independent of cancer/therapy context (multiplicative model). [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- **Absolute-risk illustration.** In a synthetic iCARE model of women aged 50–75 with breast cancer in the U.S., 96% had 10-year [absolute](../methods/absolute.md) MN risk <1%; for the top 1% of risk (by CH and blood-count features), adjuvant chemotherapy increased absolute MN risk by ≈9%, plausibly exceeding the survival benefit of chemo in some early-stage [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) patients. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)

## Genes & alterations

- [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md) — most frequent CH gene; weaker age effect than other CH mutations; R882 hotspot has higher VAFs than non-hotspot variants even after adjustment. Outcompetes DDR clones in untreated patients. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- [TET2](../genes/TET2.md) — second most common CH gene; an epigenetic modifier whose CH was not strongly modulated by therapy exposure. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- [ASXL1](../genes/ASXL1.md) — significantly enriched in current/former smokers (OR=2.5, q=1×10⁻⁴), with stronger signal in current smokers (OR=3.1) than former (OR=2.4). [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — DDR; therapy-associated CH (OR=2.8, q=2×10⁻⁴). Detected at CH stage in 10/14 tMN cases that harbored [TP53](../genes/TP53.md). Co-occurred with complex karyotype/aneuploidy in 12/13 evaluable TP53-mutant tMNs. Strong predictor of tMN risk. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- [PPM1D](../genes/PPM1D.md) — DDR; strongest therapy effect (OR=4.3, q<10⁻⁶); highest enrichment in [ovarian](../cancer_types/OV.md) (13%) and [endometrial](../cancer_types/UCEC.md) (7%) cancers, almost entirely explained by therapy exposure. Truncating C-terminal variants are the canonical CH form ([PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md) corroborates from cfDNA). [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md) — DDR; therapy-associated CH (OR=4.5, q=6×10⁻⁶); platinum (OR=2.4) and topoisomerase II inhibitor (OR=2.2) class effects. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- [SRSF2](../genes/SRSF2.md), [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md), [U2AF1](../genes/U2AF1.md) — spliceosome CH; strongly age-associated; not enriched by therapy; collectively among the strongest tMN risk predictors. [SRSF2](../genes/SRSF2.md) and [PPM1D](../genes/PPM1D.md) were not on the IMPACT-341 panel and so were under-ascertained in earlier sub-cohorts. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) — late-progression drivers acquired between CH and tMN diagnosis in many transformations. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) — included in CH-myeloid-PD criteria when truncating; partially under-ascertained in the serial-sampling panel (missing from the 163-gene myeloid panel). [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md), [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md), [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md), [CALR](../genes/CALR.md), [TERT](../genes/TERT.md), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md), [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md), [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md) — additional CH-myeloid-PD genes whose truncating, hotspot, or in-frame variants were classed as putative drivers per the paper's annotation rules. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)

## Clinical implications

- **Biomarker.** CH at VAF >2% identifies cancer patients at materially elevated risk of subsequent tMN (HR=6.9), with risk scaling by mutation count, clone size, gene context, and peripheral-blood indices (RDW, hemoglobin). Pre-treatment screening for CH is presented as a feasible, scalable risk-stratification tool because MSK-IMPACT-style panels are already in clinical use. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- **Therapy choice.** [Carboplatin](../drugs/carboplatin.md), topoisomerase II inhibitors, and external-beam radiation drive the strongest gene-specific (DDR) clonal selection. The authors raise the possibility of substituting or deferring such agents in patients with high-risk CH when the absolute survival benefit is marginal — illustrated by a synthetic iCARE model in early-stage [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md). [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- **Mechanistic prediction.** DDR-CH clones (particularly [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)) have *lower* competitive fitness than DNMT3A-class clones in the absence of therapy, but become dominant under genotoxic stress — implying that the same patient's CH risk profile depends on prospective treatment plan, not just baseline genotype. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- **No evidence for targeted-therapy or immunotherapy selection** of CH at this resolution, suggesting non-cytotoxic regimens may be CH-safer where clinically interchangeable. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)

## Limitations & open questions

- The MSK-IMPACT panel surveys cancer-driver genes; it misses CH drivers such as SRCAP, [BRCC3](../genes/BRCC3.md), ZNF318, and (in earlier panel versions) [PPM1D](../genes/PPM1D.md) and [SRSF2](../genes/SRSF2.md). [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) was absent from the 163-gene serial-sampling panel, restricting sub-cohort analyses. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- VAF cutoff of 2% means small CH clones — known to be relevant in healthy aging cohorts — were excluded. Error-corrected sequencing is needed to extend findings to sub-2% VAF. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- The serial cohort did not include patients who went on to develop hematologic malignancy, so growth-rate estimates are a lower bound for tMN-bound clones. Sampling did not bracket therapy temporally (no pre-/intra-/post-therapy triplets), so kinetics of clone growth during versus after therapy could not be resolved. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- The pooled tMN risk cohort (n=9,437) restricted to therapy-exposed patients, so gene-by-treatment interactions on tMN risk could not be formally estimated; the absolute-risk model assumes multiplicative effects. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- TP53-driven tMN is one mechanism — the authors flag that processes leading to MLL fusions, complex karyotype, and other tMN-associated alterations may follow distinct selection rules not captured by this design. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- The synthetic breast-cancer risk model is exploratory, depends on external HRs (NCCN/SEER) and assumes the MSK untreated solid-tumor population is representative of the general untreated breast-cancer population. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
- Bulk sequencing cannot determine whether multiple CH mutations within a patient sit on the same clone — single-cell sequencing is needed to confirm the competitive interpretation of multi-mutation patients. [PMID:33106634](../papers/33106634.md)
