**PMID:** 35121966 · **DOI:** [10.1038/s42003-025-08727-z](https://doi.org/10.1038/s42003-025-08727-z) · **Journal:** Communications Biology (2025)

## TL;DR

Priyadarshi et al. present **OncoMark**, a neural multi-task learning (N-MTL) framework that simultaneously quantifies the activity of ten cancer hallmarks from bulk-tumor transcriptomes. Because no labeled "hallmark-annotated" bulk biopsies exist, the authors generate synthetic pseudo-bulk profiles from 3.1 million single cells (941 patients, 14 tissue sites from the Weizmann 3CA repository), annotate each cell with curated hallmark gene sets via UCell, and aggregate same-class cells into training profiles. The shared-backbone / hallmark-specific-head architecture achieves >99% accuracy / F1 in 5-fold cross-validation and ≥96.6% on five held-out external scRNA-seq cohorts. Predictions on six bulk cancer datasets (TCGA, MET500, [CCLE](../datasets/ccle_broad_2019.md), TARGET, [PCAWG](../datasets/pancan_pcawg_2020.md), [POG570](../datasets/pog570_bcgsc_2020.md)) and two normal datasets (GTEx, ENCODE) cleanly separate malignant from normal tissue (Kolmogorov–Smirnov statistics >0.69, p ≈ 0 for all 10 hallmarks). Hallmark activity rises monotonically with AJCC/TNM stage and is modulated by specific drugs in TCGA survival analyses.

## Cohort & data

- **Training corpus:** 3.1 M single cells from 941 patients across 56 studies (Weizmann 3CA), spanning 14 tumor sites — head & neck, lung, liver/biliary, kidney, prostate, sarcoma, brain, breast, pancreas, neuroendocrine, colorectal, ovarian, skin, hematologic ([MIXED](../cancer_types/MIXED.md)).
- **Training/validation split:** 90% of studies (846 patients, 51 studies) for training + 5-fold CV repeated twice; 10% of studies (95 patients, 5 studies) held out for external testing.
- **Bulk benchmarking datasets:** TCGA (n=6,679), MET500 (n=868), [CCLE](../datasets/ccle_broad_2019.md) (n=1,019), TARGET (n=734), [PCAWG](../datasets/pancan_pcawg_2020.md) (n=1,210), [POG570](../datasets/pog570_bcgsc_2020.md) (n=570), plus normal references GTEx (n=8,228) and ENCODE (n=329).
- **Assay:** bulk and single-cell RNA-seq (no targeted DNA panel).
- **Model:** multi-task neural network — shared backbone learning pan-hallmark features + ten hallmark-specific output heads, trained on 9,326 high-variance genes.

## Key findings

- Internal 5-fold CV (repeated twice) on the synthetic pseudo-bulk training set: accuracy / F1 / balanced accuracy all > 98.7% for every hallmark; SPS (sustaining proliferative signaling) reached 100% on all metrics across 695 patients (3,305 positive / 3,796 negative samples).
- External validation across five held-out scRNA-seq studies (95 patients, 627 positive / 600 negative samples): accuracy ≥ 96.6%, F1 ≥ 93.5%, with two studies (Gao et al., Nam et al.) yielding perfect scores; AUROC ranged 0.97–1.00 across the five datasets.
- Standard baselines (logistic regression, SVC, decision tree, [random forest](../methods/random-forest-classifier.md), [XGBoost](../methods/xgboost.md), MLP) collapsed to near-zero hallmark probabilities on cancer samples — i.e., they trained to majority-class inactivity — while OncoMark preserved discrimination.
- Kolmogorov–Smirnov tests of predicted hallmark-probability distributions between normal (GTEx, ENCODE) and cancer (TCGA, [CCLE](../datasets/ccle_broad_2019.md), [POG570](../datasets/pog570_bcgsc_2020.md), [PCAWG](../datasets/pancan_pcawg_2020.md), TARGET, MET500) cohorts: K–S statistic 0.690–0.774 with p-values effectively zero for all ten hallmarks.
- In TCGA matched primary/metastatic data from Vareslija et al. and Cosgrove et al., primary tumors of patients with confirmed metastasis already showed elevated Activating Invasion and Metastasis (AIM) hallmark scores — consistent with early metastatic competence.
- Hallmark activity rises monotonically with AJCC stage (I→IV), T-stage (t1→t4), N-stage (n0→n2/3), and M-stage (M0→M1) in TCGA, with strongest co-association at advanced stages.
- Drug–hallmark survival impact (logistic regression on TCGA, using OncoMark hallmark scores as features): for disease-free survival, [anastrozole](../drugs/anastrozole.md) ↔ ERI, [cyclophosphamide](../drugs/cyclophosphamide.md) ↔ AIM, radiation ↔ TPI, [trastuzumab](../drugs/trastuzumab.md) + [vinorelbine](../drugs/vinorelbine.md) ↔ RCD; for overall survival, [anastrozole](../drugs/anastrozole.md) + [leuprolide](../drugs/leuprolide.md) ↔ ERI, [cyclophosphamide](../drugs/cyclophosphamide.md) + [trastuzumab](../drugs/trastuzumab.md) ↔ RCD, [irinotecan](../drugs/irinotecan.md) ↔ GIM + IA, radiation ↔ SPS.

## Genes & alterations

- OncoMark is a **transcriptomic hallmark classifier**, not a mutation caller — the paper reports no individual gene-level alteration findings. The model consumes 9,326 high-variance genes selected from pseudo-bulk profiles; the hallmark gene-set membership is curated from multiple databases and pruned to genes with TCGA Cox HR > 1.05 (Supplementary Data 1). No per-gene or per-variant findings are claimed.

## Clinical implications

- Hallmark scores stratify tumors by molecular activity independent of stage/grade, potentially flagging biologically aggressive tumors that look indolent on conventional pathology.
- Predicted hallmark activity tracks AJCC and TNM staging, suggesting use as a biomarker for disease staging and as a candidate intermediate phenotype for therapy response monitoring.
- Drug-impact scores from the survival analysis nominate hallmark-targeting therapies: e.g., [anastrozole](../drugs/anastrozole.md) and [leuprolide](../drugs/leuprolide.md) for ERI-high tumors; anti-angiogenic agents for tumors with elevated angiogenesis hallmark; DNA-damage-repair inhibitors for tumors with high genomic-instability hallmark.
- A web tool (https://oncomark-ai.hf.space/) is provided so clinicians/researchers can score new bulk transcriptomic profiles without local infrastructure.

## Limitations & open questions

- Training labels are synthetic — derived from binarized UCell scores on single cells aggregated into pseudo-bulks. The authors note that no ground-truth hallmark-annotated bulk biopsies exist, so real-world calibration is structurally unverifiable with current data.
- Cells with intermediate UCell scores were excluded from training, which the authors argue reduces label noise but may discard biologically informative ambiguous states.
- Training data, by construction, contained only non-overlapping hallmark labels; the MTL framework is invoked to bridge to the real-world co-activation regime, but this generalization is asserted rather than directly measured against labeled co-active samples.
- Requires bulk transcriptomic profiling, which the authors acknowledge limits adoption in resource-constrained clinical settings.
- Drug-impact analysis uses logistic regression on hallmark scores as features in TCGA; it is an association, not causal evidence of drug mechanism, and is confounded by indication selection.
- No external survival validation outside TCGA is reported, so the drug-hallmark-survival rankings are single-cohort observations.
