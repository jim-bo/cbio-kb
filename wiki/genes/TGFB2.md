# TGFB2

## Overview

TGFB2 (Transforming Growth Factor Beta 2) encodes a secreted cytokine of the TGF-beta family with potent immunosuppressive properties. In the context of immune checkpoint blockade, elevated TGFB2 expression has been associated with resistance to anti-PD1 therapy in melanoma, particularly in patients without prior [ipilimumab](../drugs/ipilimumab.md) exposure, suggesting a role in creating an immunosuppressive tumor microenvironment that limits T-cell-mediated killing.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- TGFB2 expression higher in progressors versus responders in ipilimumab-naive melanoma patients receiving anti-PD1 therapy (MWW P=0.002); no significant difference in ipilimumab-pretreated patients (P=0.43), indicating an ipilimumab-context-dependent effect [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [MEL](../cancer_types/MEL.md): elevated TGFB2 expression associated with primary resistance to anti-PD1 ICB in ipilimumab-naive melanoma [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- High TGFB2 expression in progressors correlates with low MHC-II HLA gene expression, consistent with an immunosuppressed tumor microenvironment [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- TGFB2 overexpression may identify ipilimumab-naive melanoma patients unlikely to respond to anti-PD1 monotherapy; these patients may benefit from TGF-beta pathway inhibition or combination ICB strategies [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)

## Open questions

- The differential predictive value of TGFB2 expression in ipilimumab-naive vs ipilimumab-pretreated settings suggests prior [CTLA4](../genes/CTLA4.md) blockade may alter the immunosuppressive landscape; mechanistic basis unclear.
- Whether protein-level TGFB2 (as opposed to mRNA expression) recapitulates the predictive signal requires validation.
