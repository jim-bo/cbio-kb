# ZNF730

## Overview

ZNF730 encodes a zinc finger protein of the C2H2-type family with poorly characterized function. In cancer genomics, ZNF730 was flagged as an OncodriveFML coding driver in metastatic cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma ([CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md)) but was not corroborated by all three driver tools applied in that study, placing its driver status as uncertain.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- OncodriveFML coding driver (Q-value <0.1) in metastatic CSCC (n=25 WGS); not corroborated by MutSigCV or dNdScv — classified as a single-tool call requiring further validation [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **CSCC (cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma):** Single-tool driver call in a metastatic WGS cohort with high UV-signature TMB; biological significance unconfirmed [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Identified in the same metastatic CSCC WGS cohort as [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [SLC22A6](../genes/SLC22A6.md), [ZNF442](../genes/ZNF442.md) and other candidate drivers; individual co-occurrence data not reported [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No established therapeutic relevance.

## Open questions

- Driver status is uncertain: single-tool (OncodriveFML) nomination without corroboration by MutSigCV or dNdScv; may reflect background UV mutational noise rather than positive selection [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)
