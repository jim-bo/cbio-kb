**PMID:** [22810696](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22810696) · **DOI:** 10.1038/nature11252 · **Journal:** Nature (2012)

## TL;DR

The Cancer Genome Atlas performed genome-scale analysis of 276 colorectal carcinomas using exome sequencing, DNA copy number arrays, promoter methylation profiling, and mRNA/miRNA expression. The study identified 16% of CRCs as hypermutated (75% MSI-H with [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md) silencing, 25% with mismatch-repair gene or [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) mutations). Excluding hypermutated tumours, colon and rectal cancers showed indistinguishable genomic alteration patterns. Twenty-four genes were significantly mutated, with novel frequent mutations in [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [SOX9](../genes/SOX9.md), and [AMER1](../genes/AMER1.md) (FAM123B). The study identified drug-targetable [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) amplifications and novel [IGF2](../genes/IGF2.md) amplifications, a recurrent NAV2-TCF7L1 fusion, and demonstrated near-ubiquitous WNT pathway activation and MYC-directed transcriptional programs.

## Cohort & data

- 276 colorectal carcinoma tumour/normal pairs from the TCGA [coadread_tcga_pub](../datasets/coadread_tcga_pub.md) cohort
- 224 pairs analysed by whole-exome sequencing; 97 by low-pass whole-genome sequencing
- 257 tumours profiled for somatic copy-number alterations with Affymetrix SNP 6.0 arrays
- 236 tumours profiled for promoter DNA methylation (Illumina HumanMethylation27)
- mRNA expression (Agilent microarrays + RNA-Seq), miRNA expression
- Cancer type: [COADREAD](../cancer_types/COADREAD.md) (colorectal adenocarcinoma)

## Key findings

- 16% of CRCs are hypermutated: 77% of these are MSI-H (usually with MLH1 methylation/CIMP), 23% have somatic mismatch-repair or [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) mutations without MSI-H.
- Among non-hypermutated tumours, the eight most frequently mutated genes are [APC](../genes/APC.md) (81%), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (60%), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) (43%), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) (18%), [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md) (11%), [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) (10%), [TCF7L2](../genes/TCF7L2.md) (9%), and [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) (9%).
- Non-hypermutated colon and rectal cancers are genomically indistinguishable (copy number, methylation, mRNA, miRNA patterns).
- WNT signalling pathway altered in 93% of all tumours (biallelic [APC](../genes/APC.md) inactivation or activating [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) mutations in ~80%).
- 55% of non-hypermutated tumours have alterations in [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md), or [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), with mutual exclusivity.
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) amplification at 17q21.1 found in 4% of tumours; potentially targetable with [trastuzumab](../drugs/trastuzumab.md).
- Focal amplification of 11p15.5 (7% of tumours) targets [IGF2](../genes/IGF2.md) and miR-483; IGF2 overexpression is mutually exclusive with PI3K pathway mutations (P < 0.01).
- Recurrent NAV2-TCF7L1 fusion found in 3 cases, joining [NAV2](../genes/NAV2.md) exons 1-2 with the 3' portion of [TCF7L1](../genes/TCF7L1.md), lacking the beta-catenin binding domain.
- Co-occurrence of RAS and PI3K pathway alterations in one-third of tumours (P = 0.039).
- PARADIGM integrative analysis revealed near-universal changes in [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) transcriptional targets across all tumours.

## Genes & alterations

- [APC](../genes/APC.md): Inactivating mutations in 81% non-hypermutated, 51% hypermutated; biallelic inactivation common.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md): Mutations in 60% non-hypermutated, 20% hypermutated (P < 0.0001).
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md): Oncogenic codon 12/13 mutations in 43% non-hypermutated; mutually exclusive with [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) and [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md).
- [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md): Codon 61 mutations in 9% non-hypermutated.
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md): V600E mutations dominant in hypermutated tumours.
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md): Mutations in 18% non-hypermutated; mutually exclusive with [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md) and [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) loss.
- [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md): Mutations in 2% non-hypermutated.
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md): Deletions/mutations in 4% non-hypermutated.
- [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md): Mutations and deletions; chromosome 18 lost in 66% of tumours.
- [SMAD2](../genes/SMAD2.md): Mutated in non-hypermutated tumours.
- [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md): Activating mutations in tumours lacking APC mutations.
- [SOX9](../genes/SOX9.md): Novel — all 9 mutated alleles in non-hypermutated CRCs were frameshift or nonsense; not previously associated with human cancer.
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md): Frameshift/nonsense mutations; chromatin remodelling gene.
- [AMER1](../genes/AMER1.md) (FAM123B/WTX): X-linked negative WNT regulator; virtually all mutations were loss-of-function.
- [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md): Inactivating mutations in 11% non-hypermutated; never co-occurred with distant metastasis (P = 0.0019).
- [TCF7L2](../genes/TCF7L2.md): Deleted or mutated in 12% non-hypermutated; focal deletion at 10p25.2.
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md): Focal amplification at 17q21.1 in 4%; recurrent V842I mutation.
- [ERBB3](../genes/ERBB3.md): Recurrent V104M mutation in 2 non-hypermutated cases.
- [IGF2](../genes/IGF2.md): Focal amplification at 11p15.5 in 7%; overexpression mutually exclusive with PI3K pathway events.
- [IRS2](../genes/IRS2.md): Overexpression on chromosome 13; mutually exclusive with IGF2 overexpression and PI3K mutations.
- [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md): Epigenetic silencing via methylation in 19 of 23 MSI-H hypermutated tumours.
- [TGFBR2](../genes/TGFBR2.md): Recurrently mutated in hypermutated cancers (60%).
- [ACVR2A](../genes/ACVR2A.md): Most frequently mutated gene in hypermutated tumours (63%).
- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md): Amplification at 8q24; transcriptional targets universally altered per PARADIGM.
- [NAV2](../genes/NAV2.md)-[TCF7L1](../genes/TCF7L1.md): Recurrent fusion in 3 cases; TCF7L1 loses beta-catenin binding domain.
- [POLE](../genes/POLE.md): Somatic mutations in ultramutated subset lacking MSI-H.
- [MSH3](../genes/MSH3.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md), [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md): Mismatch-repair gene mutations in hypermutated tumours.

## Clinical implications

- ERBB2 amplifications (4%) represent a targetable alteration; breast and gastric cancers with ERBB2 amplification respond to [trastuzumab](../drugs/trastuzumab.md).
- IGF2-IGF1R-IRS2 axis signals to PI3K, suggesting therapeutic targeting could block PI3K activity in a subset of patients.
- Simultaneous inhibition of RAS and PI3K pathways may be required given their co-occurrence in one-third of tumours.
- WNT signalling inhibitors and small-molecule beta-catenin inhibitors are potential therapeutic avenues (93% WNT activation).
- Proteins in the RTK-RAS and PI3K pathways (IGF2, [IGF1R](../genes/IGF1R.md), ERBB2, [ERBB3](../genes/ERBB3.md), MEK, AKT, [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md)) are potential targets for inhibition.
- Mutation rate (hypermutated vs non-hypermutated) may be a better prognostic indicator than MSI status alone, given survival differences.
- Non-hypermutated colon and rectal cancers are genomically equivalent, supporting unified treatment strategies.

## Limitations & open questions

- Survival data were not available for most tumours (prospective collection); aggressiveness was inferred from tumour stage, lymph node status, and vascular invasion.
- Low-pass whole-genome sequencing (~3-4x coverage) limits sensitivity for detecting structural variants.
- FAM123B (AMER1) is X-linked, complicating interpretation of loss-of-function in males vs females.
- The functional significance of the NAV2-TCF7L1 fusion and its therapeutic implications remain unclear.
- Why hypermutated tumours predominantly arise from the right colon is unexplained.
- The role of FZD10 overexpression (~17% of samples, up to 100x normal) in WNT-driven CRC warrants further investigation.
