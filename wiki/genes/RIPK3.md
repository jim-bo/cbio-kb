# RIPK3

## Overview

RIPK3 (Receptor Interacting Serine/Threonine Kinase 3) is a key mediator of programmed necrotic cell death (necroptosis). In cancer, RIPK3 has been associated with tumor suppression through necroptotic pathway activation. Phosphoproteomic studies have linked elevated RIPK3 phosphorylation to upstream tumor suppressor loss events.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Elevated phospho-RIPK3 identified as a kinase outlier specifically in MAP3K1-loss-of-function breast tumors in the CPTAC breast proteogenomic cohort (n=122 primary breast tumors); this tumor-suppressor-loss to targetable-kinase association was detected by the Black-Sheep phospho-outlier analysis [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md)**: Phospho-RIPK3 elevation in MAP3K1-mutant luminal breast cancer suggests necroptosis-related signaling as a downstream consequence of [MAP3K1](../genes/MAP3K1.md) loss-of-function [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Elevated phospho-RIPK3 is associated with MAP3K1 loss-of-function mutations in luminal breast cancer; association is at the phosphoprotein level (not genomic co-mutation) [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Necroptosis-related signaling via RIPK3 is flagged as a candidate therapeutic axis in MAP3K1-mutant luminal breast cancer; however, the therapeutic direction (inhibit or activate RIPK3) remains unclear and requires functional validation [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md).

## Open questions

- Whether elevated phospho-RIPK3 in MAP3K1-mutant [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) represents a targetable vulnerability or a necroptosis-resistance mechanism is unresolved [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md).
- Functional and clinical validation of RIPK3 as a therapeutic target in MAP3K1-mutant luminal breast cancer is needed [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md).
