# NFE2L2

## Overview

NFE2L2 (NRF2) is a master transcription factor of the antioxidant response, regulated by [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md). In [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md), NRF2 activation (often via KEAP1 loss or [SLC27A5](../genes/SLC27A5.md) downregulation) promotes tumor progression and drug resistance.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- NFE2L2 (NRF2) activated upon FATP5 (SLC27A5) loss in HCC, promoting tumor progression; KEAP1/NRF2 pathway activation is a resistance mechanism to [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md) [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) — NRF2 activation through KEAP1 inactivation or FATP5 loss drives HCC progression and sorafenib resistance [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- NRF2 activation co-occurs with KEAP1 inactivation; both are linked to SLC27A5 loss in HCC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Targeting the KEAP1/NRF2 axis to suppress antioxidant defense is proposed as a strategy to resensitize HCC to sorafenib [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Open questions

- Clinical NRF2 inhibitors are not yet approved; NRF2 activation is a double-edged target (cytoprotection in normal liver vs. tumor promotion).
