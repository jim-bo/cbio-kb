# SPAG17

## Overview

SPAG17 (sperm associated antigen 17) encodes a protein involved in axonemal structure. In cancer genomics, SPAG17 was identified as a fusion partner of [NOTCH2](../genes/NOTCH2.md) in metastatic solid tumors, defining a gamma-secretase-inhibitor-sensitive NOTCH fusion class distinct from PARS2-NOTCH2 fusions.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Fusion partner in NOTCH2-SPAG17 rearrangement in metastatic solid tumors; this class is predicted gamma-secretase-inhibitor-sensitive (unlike PARS2-NOTCH2) [PMID:28783718](../papers/28783718.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Metastatic solid tumors: NOTCH2-SPAG17 fusion identified in the MET500 cohort (500 metastatic solid tumors sequenced by WGS/RNA-seq) [PMID:28783718](../papers/28783718.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- NOTCH2-SPAG17 and PARS2-NOTCH2 represent two functionally distinct NOTCH fusion classes with differential drug-sensitivity predictions: NOTCH2-SPAG17 is gamma-secretase-inhibitor-sensitive, PARS2-NOTCH2 is not [PMID:28783718](../papers/28783718.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- NOTCH2-SPAG17 fusion subclass predicted to be gamma-secretase-inhibitor-sensitive, guiding therapy selection over the PARS2-NOTCH2 class [PMID:28783718](../papers/28783718.md)

## Open questions

- Functional validation of gamma-secretase inhibitor sensitivity for NOTCH2-SPAG17 fusions in vivo is pending; the prediction is based on fusion structure and not prospectively tested [PMID:28783718](../papers/28783718.md)
