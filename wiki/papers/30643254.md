**PMID:** 30643254 · **DOI:** 10.1038/s41588-018-0312-8 · **Journal:** Nature Genetics (2019)

## TL;DR

Samstein et al. analyzed clinical and targeted-NGS genomic data ([msk-impact-panel](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md)) for 1,662 advanced cancer patients treated with immune checkpoint inhibitors (ICI) and 5,371 non-ICI–treated patients sequenced at MSKCC, in order to test whether tumor mutational burden (TMB) predicts overall survival ([OS](../cancer_types/OS.md)) after ICI across cancer types. Higher somatic TMB (top 20% within each histology) was associated with better OS (HR 0.52; p = 1.6 × 10⁻⁶), an effect present in most histologies but not in glioma. The TMB cutpoint defining "high" varied markedly across cancer types, arguing against a single universal threshold. The cohort is the cBioPortal study [tmb_mskcc_2018](../datasets/tmb_mskcc_2018.md).

## Cohort & data

- **N = 1,662 ICI-treated patients** with advanced cancer profiled by MSK-IMPACT, plus **N = 5,371 non-ICI–treated** patients as a control for general prognostic effects of TMB.
- Histologies represented include [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) (n = 350), melanoma ([MEL](../cancer_types/MEL.md) / [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md); n = 321), renal cell carcinoma ([RCC](../cancer_types/RCC.md); n = 151), bladder cancer ([BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md); n = 214), head & neck squamous cell carcinoma ([HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md); n = 138), esophagogastric cancer ([EGC](../cancer_types/EGC.md)), colorectal cancer ([COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md)), and glioma ([GB](../cancer_types/GB.md) / [DIFG](../cancer_types/DIFG.md)).
- 94% of non-glioma tumors were stage IV / metastatic; 10 patients had locally recurrent disease, and a subset of melanoma patients had regionally advanced unresectable (stage III) disease.
- ICI regimens: 146 received anti-CTLA4 ([ipilimumab](../drugs/ipilimumab.md), [tremelimumab](../drugs/tremelimumab.md)); 1,447 received anti–PD-1/PD-L1 ([nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md), [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md), [atezolizumab](../drugs/atezolizumab.md), [avelumab](../drugs/avelumab.md), [durvalumab](../drugs/durvalumab.md)); 189 received both.
- **Assay:** MSK-IMPACT targeted NGS using 341-, 410-, or 468-gene versions ([IMPACT341](../methods/IMPACT341.md), [IMPACT410](../methods/IMPACT410.md), [IMPACT468](../methods/IMPACT468.md)), with matched germline normal sequencing to identify true somatic mutations.
- **TMB definition:** non-synonymous somatic mutations normalized by exonic megabases sequenced. TMB-high defined within each histology (top 20th percentile, with sensitivity at 10–50%).
- **Outcome:** OS from first ICI dose to death or last follow-up; median follow-up 19 months (range 0–80), 830 patients (50%) alive and censored at last follow-up.
- **Data availability:** the cBioPortal study [tmb_mskcc_2018](../datasets/tmb_mskcc_2018.md) ([http://www.cbioportal.org/study?id=tmb_mskcc_2018](http://www.cbioportal.org/study?id=tmb_mskcc_2018)).

## Key findings

- **Pan-cancer survival benefit with high TMB.** Across the entire ICI cohort, the top-20%-by-histology TMB group had univariate HR 0.52 (95% CI 0.42–0.64) for OS compared to the bottom 80% (two-sided log-rank). The 10–20% decile group had HR 0.76 (95% CI 0.62–0.94) [Fig. 1A].
- **Cutpoint-robust.** The trend of decreasing HR with increasing TMB cutoff was observed across cutpoints ranging from the top 10–50% [Fig. 1B; Supp. Figs. 3–4].
- **Multivariable confirmation.** Cox regression ([cox-proportional-hazards](../methods/cox-proportional-hazards.md)) adjusting for cancer type, age, ICI drug class, and year of ICI start showed TMB was significantly associated with OS as a continuous variable (HR = 0.985 per unit, p = 3.4 × 10⁻⁷) and as a binary top-20%-of-each-histology variable (HR 0.61, 95% CI 0.508–0.733, p = 1.3 × 10⁻⁷) [Table 1].
- **Effect not driven by [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) or melanoma alone.** Removing these two histologies preserved the significant association of higher TMB with longer OS [Supp. Table 2].
- **Histology-stratified analysis.** Selecting the top 20% TMB within each cancer type gave HR < 1 in nearly all histologies, with [GB](../cancer_types/GB.md) the clearest exception [Fig. 2].
- **No prognostic benefit absent ICI.** In the 5,371-patient non-ICI cohort, higher TMB was *not* associated with better OS (HR 1.12, p = 0.11), consistent across histologies — arguing the OS benefit is ICI-specific rather than a general prognostic effect of mutation load.
- **Histology-specific cutpoints.** The TMB value defining the top 20% varied widely across cancer types (e.g., 52.2 mut/Mb for [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md), reflecting MSI-high enrichment), undermining the idea of a single universal TMB-high threshold [Fig. 2].
- **PFS and response concordance.** Similar associations between higher TMB and clinical benefit / progression-free survival were observed in histologies where response data was available — [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md), melanoma, esophagogastric, head & neck, and renal cell cancer [Supp. Figs. 7–8].
- **Cohort enrichment ruled out.** Recomputing top-20% TMB across both ICI- and non-ICI–treated patients did not materially change cutpoints or the OS associations [Supp. Figs. 10–11], arguing clinicians were not systematically triaging high-TMB patients to ICI.

## Genes & alterations

This study does not focus on specific gene alterations as predictors. The biomarker analyzed is aggregate non-synonymous somatic [tumor mutational burden](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) across the MSK-IMPACT 341/410/468-gene panels; mutations in driver oncogenes were not excluded from the TMB count. No per-gene survival associations are reported in the main text.

## Clinical implications

- **TMB is a pan-cancer predictive biomarker for ICI**, supporting its use as a candidate selection biomarker across diverse histologies treated with anti-CTLA4 or anti-PD-1/PD-L1 agents.
- **No universal numerical cutoff** — TMB-high thresholds vary by histology, so a single pan-cancer cutpoint is unlikely to be clinically appropriate. The authors explicitly note that the top-20% thresholds shown "may not represent the ideal values for use clinically."
- **Targeted panels are sufficient.** Sampling ~3% of the coding exome via MSK-IMPACT provides enough information for TMB to retain predictive value for OS after ICI, supporting use of clinical-grade targeted NGS rather than WES for this biomarker.
- **Mismatch-repair / MSI alignment.** The findings are consistent with the FDA's tissue-agnostic approval of [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md) for MSI-high / dMMR tumors; high-TMB [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md) in this cohort likely reflects MSI-high biology.
- **Glioma is the outlier.** No OS benefit was observed in glioma, possibly because TMB in glioma often reflects post-temozolomide subclonal expansion of less immunogenic mutations or CNS-specific immune biology.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Heterogeneous, retrospective cohort.** Patients varied in line of therapy, prior treatment, combination regimens, and timing of MSK-IMPACT testing relative to ICI start; non-ICI prior/concurrent treatments were not recorded for the analysis.
- **No response data for some histologies.** PFS / clinical-benefit analyses were limited to NSCLC, melanoma, esophagogastric, head & neck, and renal cell carcinoma; for other histologies only OS is reported.
- **Top-20% cutoff is methodological, not clinical.** The authors caution that the dichotomization was for analytic convenience and does not define a clinically actionable threshold.
- **Platform portability.** TMB measured by targeted panels correlates with WES, but [absolute](../methods/absolute.md) cutpoints are not directly transferable across platforms.
- **Mechanistic factors unaddressed.** Other determinants of ICI response (clonality, neoantigen quality, HLA genotype, immune-cell infiltration, expression of checkpoint molecules) were not jointly modeled here.
- **Glioma signal.** Whether the lack of TMB–OS benefit in [GB](../cancer_types/GB.md) reflects temozolomide-induced subclonal mutations, CNS-specific immunology, or insufficient sample size remains open.
