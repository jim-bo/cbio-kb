# SCD

## Overview

SCD (stearoyl-CoA desaturase) is the rate-limiting enzyme for conversion of saturated fatty acids (SFAs) to monounsaturated fatty acids (MUFAs), principally oleoyl-CoA (18:1) from stearoyl-CoA (18:0). In hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC), SCD is upregulated downstream of SREBF1 as part of de novo lipogenesis (DNL) reprogramming. By raising the MUFA:PUFA ratio, SCD suppresses lipid peroxidation and ferroptosis, which confers resistance to sorafenib and regorafenib. SCD inhibitors are under active clinical investigation for solid tumors including HCC.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Drives sorafenib and regorafenib resistance in HCC by lowering the PUFA:MUFA ratio, suppressing ER-stress-induced apoptosis; SCD inhibitors aramchol, A939572, MF-438, and SSI-4 (MTI-301) re-sensitize HCC to these agents; MTI-301 entered phase I clinical trial (NCT06911008) for refractory solid tumors [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md):** SCD upregulated downstream of SREBF1/MYC; elevated MUFA:PUFA ratio from SCD activity suppresses ferroptosis and confers TKI resistance [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- SCD is transcriptionally driven by [SREBF1](../genes/SREBF1.md) and [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) amplification; acts in concert with [SLC7A11](../genes/SLC7A11.md) and [GPX4](../genes/GPX4.md) to suppress ferroptosis in HCC cells [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- SCD inhibitors aramchol, A939572, MF-438, and MTI-301 (SSI-4) re-sensitize HCC cells to sorafenib and regorafenib in preclinical models; MTI-301 in phase I for refractory solid tumors (NCT06911008) [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Open questions

- Clinical benefit of SCD inhibitors in HCC-specific trials remains unproven; the MTI-301 phase I admits broader solid tumors, not HCC specifically [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)
