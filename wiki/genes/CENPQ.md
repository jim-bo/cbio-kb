# CENPQ

## Overview

CENPQ encodes a subunit of the CENP-OPQR-U centromere complex required for accurate kinetochore assembly and mitotic progression. In the context of cancer genomics, CENPQ has been nominated as a cis-regulatory target of recurrent non-coding [CTCF](../genes/CTCF.md) binding site (CBS) hotspot mutations in gastric adenocarcinoma, with upregulation of CENPQ expression potentially linking chromatin organizational disruption to chromosomal instability (CIN).

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- CENPQ expression is upregulated in gastric tumors mutated at the chr6:50.57 Mb CTCF binding site hotspot (P=0.007, adj 0.026), and also upregulated in GC tumors vs. normal gastric tissue (P=0.0028); as a centromere subunit linked to mitotic progression, CENPQ upregulation may provide a mechanistic bridge between CBS disruption and chromosomal instability [PMID:29670109](../papers/29670109.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md): nominated cis-target of the chr6:50.57 Mb CBS hotspot in gastric adenocarcinoma; CENPQ upregulation enriched in CIN-subtype tumors [PMID:29670109](../papers/29670109.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- CBS hotspot mutations at chr6:50.57 Mb associate with CENPQ upregulation and are enriched in CIN-subtype gastric tumors (71% of CBS hotspot mutations, Fisher P=0.012) [PMID:29670109](../papers/29670109.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No targeted therapeutic agents are currently linked to CENPQ in this corpus.

## Open questions

- Whether CENPQ upregulation is a direct consequence of CBS disruption (loss of CTCF-mediated insulation) or a bystander of CIN remains unresolved; allele-specific expression could not be confirmed due to the >5 kb distance between hotspot and gene [PMID:29670109](../papers/29670109.md)
