# NACC2

## Overview

NACC2 (Nucleus Accumbens Associated 2) encodes a transcriptional repressor containing a BEN domain. In cancer genomics, NACC2 has been identified as a 5' fusion partner for [NTRK2](../genes/NTRK2.md) in pediatric brain tumors, contributing a dimerization domain predicted to confer ligand-independent kinase activation.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Identified as a novel 5' fusion partner for [NTRK2](../genes/NTRK2.md) in pilocytic astrocytoma; the NACC2:NTRK2 fusion retains the NTRK2 kinase domain and the NACC2 BEN domain contributes dimerization predicted to drive ligand-independent activation (3 non-cerebellar samples total across [QKI](../genes/QKI.md):NTRK2 and NACC2:NTRK2 fusions) [PMID:23817572](../papers/23817572.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Pilocytic astrocytoma: fusion partner in NTRK2-activating gene fusions [PMID:23817572](../papers/23817572.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- NACC2:NTRK2 fusion occurs in non-cerebellar pilocytic astrocytomas lacking [KIAA1549](../genes/KIAA1549.md):[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) fusions; QKI is the other novel 5' partner for NTRK2 in the same series [PMID:23817572](../papers/23817572.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- The NACC2:NTRK2 fusion nominates NTRK2 (TrkB) inhibition as a therapeutic strategy in non-cerebellar pilocytic astrocytomas where surgical resection is limited [PMID:23817572](../papers/23817572.md)

## Open questions

- Whether NACC2:NTRK2 fusions respond to TRK inhibitors (e.g., [larotrectinib](../drugs/larotrectinib.md), [entrectinib](../drugs/entrectinib.md)) has not been directly tested in this tumor context.
