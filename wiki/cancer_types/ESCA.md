# Esophageal Adenocarcinoma (ESCA)

## Overview

Esophageal adenocarcinoma (ESCA) is a subtype of esophagogastric adenocarcinoma ([EGC](../cancer_types/EGC.md)) originating in the esophagus, frequently arising from Barrett's esophagus. On OncoTree it is a child of [EGC](../cancer_types/EGC.md), alongside [GEJ](../cancer_types/GEJ.md) and [STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md).

## Cohorts in the corpus

- Part of the combined 1,123-patient EGC cohort (219 early-onset, 904 average-onset) at MSK, 2005--2018. Esophageal primary site was less common in early-onset (16% vs. 28%, P<0.001). MSK-IMPACT somatic profiling in 902 patients; data deposited as [egc_msk_2023](../datasets/egc_msk_2023.md). [PMID:37699004](../papers/37699004.md)
- Part of the 37-patient phase II trial cohort of HER2-positive metastatic esophageal, gastroesophageal junction, or gastric adenocarcinoma. Dataset: [egc_trap_ccr_msk_2023](../datasets/egc_trap_ccr_msk_2023.md). [PMID:37406106](../papers/37406106.md)
- OE19 (HER2+, ESCA cell line): preclinical ADC radiosensitization model — a single 0.25 nmol T-DM1 dose + 2.5 Gy × 3 fractions increased tumor doubling time from 7 to 66 days in xenograft. [PMID:27698471](../papers/27698471.md)

## Recurrent alterations

- [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md) — amplification enriched in early-onset EGC (16% vs. 7%), driven by chromosomal instability in the esophageal/GEJ subgroup. [PMID:37699004](../papers/37699004.md)
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) — amplification present; trend toward higher frequency in average-onset tumors. Associated with improved survival in multivariable analysis (HR=0.65, P=0.01). [PMID:37699004](../papers/37699004.md)
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — enriched in average-onset EGC (22% vs. 11%, P<0.001); associated with worse survival. [PMID:37699004](../papers/37699004.md)
- See also [EGC](../cancer_types/EGC.md) for shared alterations across the esophagogastric spectrum.
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) (HER2) amplification/overexpression: OE19 ESCA cells are HER2+ with T-DM1 IC50 <1 nM vs. >100 nM in HER2− controls; T-DM1 more potent than paclitaxel, cisplatin, trastuzumab, lapatinib, or erlotinib in OE19. [PMID:27698471](../papers/27698471.md)

## Subtypes

- Early-onset esophageal cancer is less prevalent than gastric in the early-onset setting (16% vs. 64% gastric site). [PMID:37699004](../papers/37699004.md)
- [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md) amplification enrichment in early-onset tumors is driven by the chromosomal instability (CIN) esophageal/GEJ subgroup. [PMID:37699004](../papers/37699004.md)

## Therapeutic landscape

- HER2-directed therapy ([trastuzumab](../drugs/trastuzumab.md)) combined with PD-1 blockade ([pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md)) and chemotherapy showed median PFS 13 months, [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) 27 months, ORR 89% in HER2-positive metastatic esophagogastric cancer including ESCA. [PMID:37406106](../papers/37406106.md)
- CCNE1 amplification represents a potential therapeutic target (CDK2 inhibitors) in early-onset ESCA. [PMID:37699004](../papers/37699004.md)
- [trastuzumab-emtansine](../drugs/trastuzumab-emtansine.md) (T-DM1) combined with IR produced long-term tumor control in HER2+ OE19 (ESCA) xenografts; T-MMAE was equally efficacious at 1 nmol. Authors propose T-DM1 + chemoradiotherapy as a strategy warranting clinical evaluation in HER2+ locally advanced esophageal cancer. [PMID:27698471](../papers/27698471.md)
