# HERC2

## Overview

HERC2 is an E3 ubiquitin ligase with established roles in DNA damage response and pigmentation (regulating OCA2/MC1R activity). In the context of normal skin biology, its expression marks a high-mutation-burden melanocyte subpopulation with UV-damage signatures.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- HERC2 is a transcriptomic marker of the HighMut melanocyte subpopulation (high UV mutation burden, enriched in interfollicular epidermis) in a single-cell multi-omic atlas of 297 melanocytes from 31 donors; co-expressed with pigmentation markers [MC1R](../genes/MC1R.md), [HMOX1](../genes/HMOX1.md), [ABCC2](../genes/ABCC2.md), [LIPA](../genes/LIPA.md), and [HLA-DPA1](../genes/HLA-DPA1.md) [PMID:39975212](../papers/39975212.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- No cancer-type associations reported in the corpus. Findings are from non-lesional skin of a melanoma patient; no melanoma driver claim is made [PMID:39975212](../papers/39975212.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- HERC2 expression co-occurs with [HMOX1](../genes/HMOX1.md), [HLA-DPA1](../genes/HLA-DPA1.md), [MC1R](../genes/MC1R.md), and [ABCC2](../genes/ABCC2.md) in the HighMut program; anti-correlates with the LowMut neural-crest program ([HACD1](../genes/HACD1.md), [VCAN](../genes/VCAN.md), [TAGLN](../genes/TAGLN.md)) [PMID:39975212](../papers/39975212.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Not reported in the corpus.

## Open questions

- Whether HERC2-expressing HighMut melanocytes represent an intermediate step toward melanoma initiation or are an endpoint of UV-induced field cancerization is not established [PMID:39975212](../papers/39975212.md).
