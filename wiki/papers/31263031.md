## TL;DR

Jonsson et al. integrated prospective clinical sequencing of 1,004 tumors (733 primary, 271 recurrent) from 923 adult glioma patients at Memorial Sloan Kettering with curated treatment and outcome data ([MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) n=837; [FoundationOne](../methods/foundationone.md) n=167) and deposited the cohort as [glioma_mskcc_2019](../datasets/glioma_mskcc_2019.md). They report (i) a 13% germline pathogenic-variant rate enriched for DNA-repair genes, (ii) cell-cycle alterations (chiefly [CDKN2A/B](../genes/CDKN2A.md) loss) as IDH-status-independent drivers of malignant progression and contrast enhancement in 1p19q-intact astrocytomas, (iii) alkylator-induced hypermutation more common in IDH-mutant and [MGMT](../genes/MGMT.md)-methylated tumors with cell-cycle defects likely preceding [temozolomide](../drugs/temozolomide.md) exposure, and (iv) [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600 responses to RAF/MEK inhibition conditioned on clonality, allele class, and co-mutational context.

## Cohort & data

- **N = 923 patients / 1,004 tumors** prospectively sequenced July 2013–July 2017 at MSKCC; 733 primary and 271 recurrent samples; 57 patients had matched pre/post-treatment tumors.
- Cancer types: WHO 2016 diffuse glioma ([DIFG](../cancer_types/DIFG.md)) spanning IDH-mutant [astrocytoma](../cancer_types/ASTR.md) (grades II–IV), IDH-mutant 1p/19q co-deleted [oligodendroglioma](../cancer_types/ODG.md), IDH-WT and IDH-mutant [glioblastoma](../cancer_types/GBM.md), and rare/[HGGNOS](../cancer_types/HGGNOS.md) subtypes. 355 IDH-mutant vs. 649 IDH-WT tumors.
- Assays: [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) paired tumor/normal (n=837) and [FoundationOne](../methods/foundationone.md) tumor-only (n=167); both detect SNVs, indels, CNVs, fusions across cancer-associated coding exons.
- Allele-specific copy number via [FACETS](../methods/facets.md) v0.3.9 (75% threshold for 1p/19q codeletion, 90% threshold for chr7 gain / chr10 loss); [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) clonality estimated from MSK-IMPACT purity-corrected CCF.
- Mutational signatures inferred for samples with ≥5 mutations; alkylator-induced hypermutation called when [mutational signatures](../methods/mutational-signatures.md) signature 11 ≥50% and TMB exceeded 1.5 MAD above median (per platform).
- [MGMT](../genes/MGMT.md) promoter methylation available for 636 patients (pyrosequencing or methylation-specific real-time PCR after bisulfite conversion).
- External reference cohorts: AACR GENIE (498 samples), TCGA (894), Foundation Medicine (739); re-annotated with VEP v88 via [vcf2maf](../methods/vcf2maf.md).
- Data deposited in cBioPortal as [glioma_mskcc_2019](../datasets/glioma_mskcc_2019.md).

## Key findings

- **Germline burden.** 13% (103/764) of evaluable patients harbored a pathogenic or likely pathogenic germline variant; 28% (29/103) targeted DNA-repair effectors. High-penetrance syndromes were observed in [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), and [RB1](../genes/RB1.md). Three patients carried [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) P/LP alleles. Six patients carried germline MMR defects (two [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), two [PMS1](../genes/PMS1.md), one [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md), one [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md)); two had biallelic somatic loss + MSI-pattern hypermutation consistent with Lynch syndrome. No association with age at diagnosis, pathological grade (P=0.3), or IDH status (P=0.7).
- **Novel hotspots.** Combining the MSK cohort with retrospectively sequenced gliomas (n=3,130 total) identified 34 previously uncharacterized hotspots in [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) and [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md), and candidate driver mutations in [MAP3K1](../genes/MAP3K1.md) and [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md) — two genes not previously linked to glioma.
- **Cell-cycle alterations and contrast enhancement.** In IDH-mutant tumors, cell-cycle pathway alterations were strongly enriched in MRI-enhancing 1p19q-intact tumors (44% enhancing vs. 8% non-enhancing, Bonferroni-corrected P<0.001) but absent in 1p19q-codeleted tumors (3% vs. 4%). Longitudinal sequencing of 22 IDH-mutant patients showed cell-cycle aberrations co-acquired with contrast enhancement only in astrocytomas (6/11) and not codeleted tumors (0/11, P=0.01).
- **Cell-cycle and progression.** Among recurrent IDH-mutant 1p19q-intact grade II/III tumors with enhancement (n=65), cell-cycle alterations were independently associated with shorter progression-free survival (HR=2.6, 95% CI 1.6–5.7, log-rank P=0.02). Spatial sequencing of a multi-region IDH-mutant [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) showed an [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) deletion confined to the enhancing component.
- **Alkylator-induced hypermutation.** 38 hypermutated tumors (signature 11–driven) were identified; therapy-induced hypermutation was more common in IDH-mutant than IDH-WT alkylator-exposed tumors (29% vs. 12%, P=0.004) and in MGMT-methylated patients (30% vs. 10%, P=0.006). 31/38 (82%) harbored mutations in MMR effectors. In 1p19q-intact tumors, all astrocytic relapses with therapy-induced hypermutation (n=22) carried a cell-cycle alteration — 15/26 were focal [CDKN2A/B](../genes/CDKN2A.md) deletions. In three patients with pre-treatment matched specimens, cell-cycle alterations preceded TMZ exposure and remained clonal through hypermutation.
- **Therapeutic actionability.** 32% of patients (295/923) harbored a potentially actionable lesion (OncoKB levels 1–3); 20% of IDH-WT and 39% of IDH-mutant tumors carried an investigationally actionable alteration (P<0.001). 32 patients received approved/standard-of-care targeted therapy and 32 additional patients received a targeted agent on a preclinical basis; 60% of targeted-therapy recipients were IDH-WT GBMs.
- **BRAF biology by glioma subtype.** Oncogenic BRAF mutations occurred in 29 samples (2% of cohort). BRAF V600 mutations were clonal and nearly exclusive to epithelioid IDH-WT GBMs or high-grade tumors arising from lower-grade lesions (17/17). Conventional IDH-WT GBMs had a mix of subclonal V600 and non-V600 class-3 alleles (G466, G469, L597; 5/10), preferentially associated with +7/–10 (4/6 vs. 2/13, P=0.05).
- **BRAF V600 response.** 7 BRAF V600E gliomas received RAF/MEK/ERK or RET-directed therapy: 4 partial/near-complete responses (ongoing at 9–41 months), 2 stable disease. The one patient who progressed immediately had a subclonal V600E (only treated tumor with subclonal BRAF). One responder with 78% overall regression had a spinal metastasis that failed RAF inhibition and carried a [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) homozygous deletion absent from responding lesions — echoing [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)'s role in BRAF-inhibitor resistance in melanoma.

## Genes & alterations

- [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) R132 and [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) R140/R172 — define the IDH-mutant subgroup (355 tumors); not directly targeted in this study but anchor all subtype analyses. After the data freeze, OncoKB upgraded to level 2B for glioma; held at level 4 here for analytical consistency.
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) / [CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md) — focal deletions are the dominant cell-cycle alteration driving malignant progression and contrast enhancement in 1p19q-intact gliomas; also tied to BRAF-inhibitor resistance in a treated patient. CDKN2A/B alterations were enriched in higher-grade IDH-WT disease.
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) — alternative cell-cycle alterations; one patient with primary [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) mutation and one with [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) amplification later acquired CDKN2A/B loss at recurrence and died within 11–15 months.
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — V600 (clonal, epithelioid/secondary GBMs) vs. non-V600 class-3 (G466, G469, L597; subclonal in conventional GBMs). Allele class and clonality predict MAPK-inhibitor response.
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md), [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md) — 34 newly identified hotspots from a 3,130-sample meta-analysis; rare [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) kinase-domain-retaining fusions identified.
- [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)–[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md) — fusions in 16 patients.
- [MGMT](../genes/MGMT.md) — promoter methylation associated with higher rate of alkylator-induced hypermutation (30% vs. 10%, P=0.006).
- [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md), [PMS1](../genes/PMS1.md), [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md) — germline MMR alleles; two cases had biallelic loss and Lynch-pattern MSI hypermutation. Somatic MMR mutations were observed in 31/38 (82%) hypermutated recurrences.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — germline P/LP alleles tied to known heritable syndromes.
- [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) — preferentially altered in recurrent lower-grade IDH-WT tumors that started as grade II/III (P<0.05).
- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter, [CIC](../genes/CIC.md), [FUBP1](../genes/FUBP1.md), [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) — canonical glioma subtype-defining lesions; frequencies stable across primary vs. recurrent specimens.
- [MAP3K1](../genes/MAP3K1.md), [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md) — candidate novel glioma drivers from the 3,130-sample meta-analysis.
- [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md), [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md), [SRSF2](../genes/SRSF2.md), [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md), [TET2](../genes/TET2.md) — clonal-hematopoiesis filter genes used to exclude CH-attributable variants from germline calls; [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md) V617F removed wherever observed.
- [ATR](../genes/ATR.md), [ERCC2](../genes/ERCC2.md), [MUTYH](../genes/MUTYH.md) — among "Other" DNA-repair germline P/LP alleles annotated in Fig. 1G.

## Clinical implications

- **Cell-cycle alterations as a prognostic biomarker.** CDKN2A/B (and broader cell-cycle pathway) loss in 1p19q-intact lower-grade gliomas is an IDH-status-independent marker of impending malignant progression and shorter PFS at recurrence (HR=2.6), supporting routine assessment in molecular pathology workflow alongside IDH/1p19q status.
- **Predictive biomarker for therapy-induced hypermutation.** Pre-existing cell-cycle defects in 1p19q-intact tumors may flag patients at elevated risk of [temozolomide](../drugs/temozolomide.md)-driven hypermutation upon recurrence — relevant to TMZ-versus-radiation sequencing decisions and to immunotherapy candidacy (hypermutated tumors are MMR-mutant).
- **Germline screening rationale.** A 13% pathogenic germline rate (DNA-repair-enriched) argues for broader germline assessment in glioma, including Lynch-related MMR genes, [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), and high-penetrance NF1/TP53/PTEN/RB1.
- **MAPK-pathway therapy in glioma.** Clinical responses to RAF/MEK/ERK inhibitors ([vemurafenib](../drugs/vemurafenib.md), [dabrafenib](../drugs/dabrafenib.md), [trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md), [cobimetinib](../drugs/cobimetinib.md)) are conditioned by BRAF allele class (V600 vs. non-V600 class-3), mutation clonality, and co-mutational context (CDKN2A loss as a candidate resistance modifier). Subclonal V600E may not be a useful predictive biomarker.
- **IDH-directed therapy.** [Ivosidenib](../drugs/ivosidenib.md) (approved in [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md)) is the canonical IDH inhibitor under glioma investigation; this cohort provides the molecular substrate (355 IDH-mutant tumors with longitudinal context) for assessing IDH-inhibitor response.

## Limitations & open questions

- Tumor-only [FoundationOne](../methods/foundationone.md) samples (n=167) cannot be used for germline analysis, biasing the germline-prevalence denominator to 764 MSK-IMPACT patients.
- Targeted-therapy responses are exploratory (n=7 BRAF V600 treated); the case for CDKN2A as a BRAF-inhibitor resistance modifier in glioma is hypothesis-generating and rests on one mixed-response patient.
- Only three matched pre-/post-TMZ pairs were available to establish that cell-cycle alterations precede alkylator-induced hypermutation; the "biomarker of TMZ-susceptibility" interpretation needs validation in a larger longitudinal series.
- OncoKB level-4 alterations (including EGFR amplification) were excluded from actionability counts, conservatively under-estimating molecular eligibility for trials.
- Authors did not report tumor-infiltrating lymphocyte, neoantigen, or immune-checkpoint response data on hypermutated tumors despite the obvious immunotherapy implication.
- IDH-WT lower-grade astrocytomas were a rare subset; cell-cycle association was demonstrated but underpowered.
- Whether MGMT methylation interacts with cell-cycle status to gate alkylator-induced hypermutation (versus being marginally additive) is not formally tested.
