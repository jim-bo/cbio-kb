# IKZF2

## Overview

IKZF2 (IKAROS Family Zinc Finger 2, also known as Helios) is a zinc-finger transcription factor and member of the Ikaros family involved in lymphoid differentiation and Ras/PI3K pathway modulation. Deletions of IKZF2 are highly recurrent in low hypodiploid ALL and modulate RAS and PI3K signaling activity.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Deletions in 52.9% of low hypodiploid ALL and 36.4% of adult low hypodiploid ALL (St. Jude WGS/WES, 44 pediatric ALL tumors); shRNA knockdown of IKZF2 increases pERK and pS6 levels, indicating a role as a Ras/PI3K pathway modulator [PMID:23334668](../papers/23334668.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[BLL](../cancer_types/BLL.md)** (B-lymphoblastic leukemia, low hypodiploid): Deletions in ~53% of pediatric cases and ~36% of adult cases; functional data supports IKZF2 as a negative modulator of Ras/PI3K signaling in ALL [PMID:23334668](../papers/23334668.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-deleted with [RB1](RB1.md) alterations in low hypodiploid ALL; found alongside [TP53](TP53.md) mutations (which define this subtype) and [IKZF3](IKZF3.md) deletions [PMID:23334668](../papers/23334668.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- IKZF2 loss upregulates PI3K/mTOR signaling; dual PI3K/mTOR inhibitors (BEZ235, GDC-0941) show activity in low hypodiploid ALL xenografts, suggesting IKZF2 loss contributes to therapeutic vulnerability [PMID:23334668](../papers/23334668.md)

## Open questions

- Functional consequences of IKZF2 loss are based on shRNA knockdown in cell lines; in vivo validation in patient-derived models is needed [PMID:23334668](../papers/23334668.md)
