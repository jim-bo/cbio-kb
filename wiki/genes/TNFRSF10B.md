# TNFRSF10B

## Overview

TNFRSF10B (Tumor Necrosis Factor Receptor Superfamily Member 10B), also known as TRAIL-R2 or DR5, is a pro-apoptotic death receptor and a transcriptional target of p53. Its expression is regulated by the p53 pathway and it serves as a readout of p53 activity in cancer cells. In endometrial carcinoma, its protein levels are anticorrelated with candidate oncogenic amplicon genes on chromosome 1q.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Protein levels anticorrelated with 1q-encoded histone modifiers ([SETDB1](../genes/SETDB1.md), SDE2, [PARP1](../genes/PARP1.md), [GATAD2B](../genes/GATAD2B.md)) in endometrial carcinoma; acts as a p53-pathway target protein whose expression is suppressed by candidate 1q amplicon genes [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Endometrial carcinoma: downregulated by 1q amplicon-encoded histone modifiers suppressing p53 pathway output [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Anticorrelated with SETDB1, SDE2, PARP1, and GATAD2B protein levels in endometrial carcinoma [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- As a p53 pathway effector, TNFRSF10B expression may serve as a biomarker of p53 pathway competency in tumors with 1q amplification [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)

## Open questions

- Whether pharmacologic inhibition of 1q amplicon genes (e.g., PARP1 inhibitors) can restore TNFRSF10B expression and sensitize tumors to apoptosis [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)
