# PPIE

## Overview

PPIE (peptidylprolyl isomerase E) is a cyclophilin-family member involved in spliceosome function. It has been implicated in the FAST lncRNA/beta-catenin regulatory axis, placing it at the intersection of post-transcriptional regulation and Wnt signaling.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- PPIE protein differentially expressed in CTNNB1-mutant HCCs without corresponding mRNA-level change — among 10 CTNNB1-mutant differentially-expressed proteins NOT also differential at mRNA — implicating post-transcriptional regulation; roles in spliceosome function, FAST lncRNA pathway, and beta-catenin axis noted; identified in a European [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) proteogenomics cohort (n=51 with proteome data) [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md): Protein-level differential expression in CTNNB1-mutant tumors without mRNA concordance [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-dysregulated at protein (but not mRNA) level with [TNRC6B](../genes/TNRC6B.md) and [PRKCE](../genes/PRKCE.md) in CTNNB1-mutant HCCs, suggesting shared post-transcriptional regulatory mechanism [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic target data from the corpus; involvement in the beta-catenin/Wnt axis suggests potential relevance to Wnt-targeted approaches.

## Open questions

- The mechanistic basis for PPIE protein-level regulation in CTNNB1-mutant HCCs independent of mRNA changes remains to be characterized.
