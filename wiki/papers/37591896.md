# Genomic analysis and clinical correlations of non-small cell lung cancer brain metastasis

**PMID:** 37591896 · **DOI:** 10.1038/s41467-023-40793-x · **Journal:** Nature Communications (2023)

## TL;DR

Skakodub et al. profiled 233 resected [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) brain metastases (BM) with MSK-IMPACT plus matched primary tumors (n=47) and extracranial metastases (n=42), with detailed clinical annotation. BM specimens showed higher TMB, FGA, and chromosomal instability than non-BM sites, with [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)/B deletions and cell-cycle pathway alterations enriched in BM. [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) alterations — often uncommon/non-canonical drivers — were enriched in patients who developed leptomeningeal disease (LMD), and [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) amplifications were associated with multifocal regional intracranial progression [PMID:37591896](../papers/37591896.md).

## Cohort & data

- 233 patients with resected [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) BM sequenced by MSK-IMPACT; 180 (77%) [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md), 23 (10%) [LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md), 30 (13%) other [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md). Median age 67; 57% female; 80% current/former smokers.
- Matched samples: 47 primary tumors (PT) and 42 extracranial metastases (EM); downstream [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md)-restricted analysis used 179 BM, 37 PT, 34 EM.
- External comparator [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) PT cohorts: BM+ (N=32), BM−/EM+ (N=1549), BM−/EM− (N=582).
- Dataset: [bm_nsclc_mskcc_2023](../datasets/bm_nsclc_mskcc_2023.md).
- Assay: MSK-IMPACT targeted panel ([IMPACT341](../methods/IMPACT341.md) / [IMPACT410](../methods/IMPACT410.md) / [IMPACT468](../methods/IMPACT468.md) / [IMPACT505](../methods/IMPACT505.md)) [PMID:37591896](../papers/37591896.md).

## Key findings

- TMB was higher in BM vs extracranial metastases (median 8.8 vs 5.8; p=0.00766) and FGA was higher in BM vs EM (p=2.77e-06) and vs PT (p=2.27e-07) [PMID:37591896](../papers/37591896.md).
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)/[CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md) alterations were more frequent in BM (34%) than PT (13%) (p=0.003, q=0.04); cell-cycle pathway alterations 56% BM vs 32% PT (p=0.004, q=0.041), driven by [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)/B.
- In the [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) sub-cohort, CDKN2A/B enrichment persisted (31% BM vs 18% PT; p=0.004) as did cell-cycle pathway enrichment (52% vs 27%; p=0.007).
- Comparing [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) PT BM+ vs BM−/EM−, [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [MYC](../genes/MYC.md), [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), and [FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md) alterations were enriched in PTs from patients who later developed BM; [NKX2-1](../genes/NKX2-1.md) alterations were enriched in both BM and EM cohorts.
- Among 179 [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) BM patients, 101 (56%) had intracranial progression after craniotomy + RT: regional (30%), local (14%), LMD (11%). Median [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) 2.7 years (95% CI 2.3–4.0); median iPFS 1.2 years (95% CI 1.0–1.5).
- LMD patients had more [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) alterations than non-progressors (45% vs 21%; p=0.044). Local progressors had more [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) loss (24% vs 6%; p=0.022) and [NKX3-1](../genes/NKX3-1.md) alterations (16% vs 3%; p=0.044). Multifocal regional progressors had [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) amplifications in 22% vs 0% in local progressors (p=0.023). [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) pathway alterations were enriched in LMD (p=0.013, q=0.14) and regional progression groups.
- [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) alterations were more frequent in LMD patients (15%) than other groups.
- Paired BM–BM samples from multi-lesion patients showed high genomic concordance, contrasting with lower concordance seen in synchronous BM/PT pairs.

## Genes & alterations

- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) / [CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md): deep deletions enriched in BM; cell-cycle pathway driver in BM development.
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md): enriched in LMD; uncommon drivers (L861Q, G719A/S, A755G, N771_H773dup) in 45% of LMD patients; persistence of these drivers despite TKI therapy; one vignette showed emergence then clearance of T790M under [osimertinib](../drugs/osimertinib.md) with ongoing L861Q/G719S.
- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md): amplifications associated with multifocal regional intracranial progression; [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) pathway alterations enriched in LMD and regional progression.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md): commonly shared between BM and PT/EM; often truncal.
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md): loss associated with local intracranial progression.
- [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md), [NKX2-1](../genes/NKX2-1.md): enriched in PTs of BM+ patients vs non-metastatic.
- [STK11](../genes/STK11.md): 22% vs 0% in [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) BM vs SCC BM (p=0.01).
- [NF1](../genes/NF1.md): enriched in LMD patients.
- [HLA-B](../genes/HLA-B.md): subset of private BM mutations [PMID:37591896](../papers/37591896.md).

## Clinical implications

- Non-canonical [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) mutations in BM may identify [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) patients at elevated risk for LMD and partial resistance to [osimertinib](../drugs/osimertinib.md), with persistence across serial tissue/CSF samples despite maximal EGFR-directed and local therapy [PMID:37591896](../papers/37591896.md).
- Cell-cycle / [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)-B loss appears to be a near-obligate event for BM development, suggesting a rationale for [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md)/6-directed strategies in CNS-tropic [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) (not tested here).
- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) amplification may flag patients at risk for multifocal regional intracranial progression.
- Prior [erlotinib](../drugs/erlotinib.md) exposure features in the EGFR-mutant LMD trajectories described.

## Limitations & open questions

- Retrospective single-institution cohort, reliant on MSK-IMPACT targeted panel (not WES/WGS).
- Matched PT and EM sample counts are modest (47 and 42 respectively); synchronous BM/PT analysis N=2.
- Some q-values were non-significant after multiple-testing correction (e.g., [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) local-progression association q=0.573), warranting validation.
- Causal mechanism linking non-canonical [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) drivers to leptomeningeal tropism vs [osimertinib](../drugs/osimertinib.md) partial resistance remains unresolved.
