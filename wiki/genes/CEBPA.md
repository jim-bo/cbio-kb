# CEBPA

## Overview

CEBPA (CCAAT Enhancer Binding Protein Alpha) encodes a transcription factor critical for myeloid differentiation. Recurrent mutations in CEBPA define a distinct favorable-risk subgroup in acute myeloid leukemia ([AML](../cancer_types/AML.md)) and are mutually exclusive with several other major AML drivers.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Recurrently mutated in AML; part of the mutual-exclusivity set with transcription-factor fusions, [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md), [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md), and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) [PMID:23634996](../papers/23634996.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- AML: recurrent CEBPA mutations define a favorable-risk category mutually exclusive of NPM1 and [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md) mutations [PMID:23634996](../papers/23634996.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Mutually exclusive with NPM1, FLT3, RUNX1, TP53, and transcription-factor fusions (PML-RARA, RUNX1-RUNX1T1, MYH11-CBFB) in AML [PMID:23634996](../papers/23634996.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- CEBPA-mutant AML is associated with favorable prognosis and may influence treatment stratification.

## Open questions

- Role of co-occurring epigenetic mutations ([DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md), [TET2](../genes/TET2.md)) in CEBPA-mutant AML.
