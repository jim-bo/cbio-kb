# HRAS

## Overview

HRAS encodes a GTPase in the RAS/MAPK signaling pathway. In radiation-associated angiosarcoma, HRAS harbors hotspot missense mutations in the Ras GTPase domain (e.g., A59T, Q61R/L) at significantly higher frequency than sporadic angiosarcoma.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Missense and in-frame insertion/deletion mutations in 14% of RT-[ANGS](../cancer_types/ANGS.md) vs 2% of sporadic AS; all hotspot mutations at the Ras GTPase domain (A59T, Q61R/L) [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).
- Co-occurs with [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) amplification, [FLT4](../genes/FLT4.md), [CRKL](../genes/CRKL.md), and [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) alterations in breast/chest wall RT-AS [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [ANGS](../cancer_types/ANGS.md) — HRAS hotspot mutations enriched in RT-AS (14%) vs sporadic AS (2%); implicated in both the stress-activated MAPK cascade and VEGF/VEGFR signaling pathways [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) amplification, [FLT4](../genes/FLT4.md) amplification, [CRKL](../genes/CRKL.md) amplification, and [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) alterations in RT-AS [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- HRAS is part of both the stress-activated MAPK cascade and VEGF/VEGFR signaling pathways identified as potential molecular targets in RT-AS [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).

## Open questions

- Whether RAS-directed therapies or downstream MEK/ERK inhibitors are effective in HRAS-mutant RT-AS is not addressed in the corpus [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).
- The functional significance of the specific HRAS hotspot variants (A59T, Q61R/L) in angiosarcoma biology has not been characterized [PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md).
