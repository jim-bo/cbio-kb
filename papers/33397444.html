Comprehensive integrative profiling of upper tract urothelial carcinomas

Authors

Xiaofan Lu

Sehrish Khan Bazai

Eva Compérat

Roger Mouawad

Hui Yao

Morgan Rouprêt

Jean-Philippe Spano

David Khayat

Irwin Davidson

Nizar N. Tannir

Fangrong Yan

Doi

PMID: 33397444 · DOI: 10.1186/s13059-020-02230-w · Journal: Genome Biology (2021)

TL;DR

Su et al. performed integrative genomic, transcriptomic, and DNA-methylation profiling of 40 upper tract urothelial carcinomas (UTUCs; 20 non-muscle-invasive, 20 muscle-invasive) from a single French biobank (Pitié-Salpêtrière) to dissect the molecular basis of muscle invasiveness. Whole-exome sequencing on 30 paired tumor–normal cases identified FGFR3 (50%), KDM6A (27%), and a novel UTUC tumor suppressor ZFP36L1 (20%) as significantly mutated genes (MutSigCV FDR<0.05); mutations of the ZFP36 family (ZFP36, ZFP36L1, ZFP36L2) collectively affected 26.7% of cases. Infinium EPIC methylation profiling of 35 tumors plus 8 adjacent normals identified two epi-clusters — EpiC-low (hypomethylated, immune-depleted, FGFR3-enriched, NMI-enriched) and EpiC-high (hypermethylated, immune-infiltrated, SWI/SNF-mutated, MI-enriched, worse OS HR=6.57) — that were also recapitulated when the UTUC methylation signature was applied to TCGA muscle-invasive bladder cancer (BLCA-C1 vs BLCA-C2).

Cohort & data

  • 40 UTUC patients (20 NMI + 20 MI), fresh-frozen surgical resections from the Pitié-Salpêtrière Hospital biobank, all microsatellite-stable, all consented; cohort registered as cBioPortal study utuc_igbmc_2021. Median follow-up 53 months (range 2–98); 18 recurred, 12 died of disease.
  • Assays per case (matched germline available for 30/40):
    • Whole-exome sequencing on 30 paired tumor–normal (Agilent SureSelect 50 Mb, Illumina HiSeq 2000, 2×76 bp; tumor coverage 79–158×, germline 43–80×). 15 NMI + 15 MI cases.
    • Targeted Sanger sequencing of FGFR3 hotspots (S249C, Y373C) for 35 cases; Sanger validation of FGFR3/KDM6A/ZFP36L1 calls.
    • RNA-seq on 20 cases (15 MI + 5 NMI, NuGen Ovation V2, HiSeq 2000 75 bp PE, aligned to GRCh37/hg19 with MOSAIK, GENCODE15).
    • Infinium MethylationEPIC array on 35 UTUC + 8 normal adjacent bladder tissues; 836,691 probes after filtering sex chromosomes and NA-containing probes.
  • External cohorts used for cross-validation: blca_tcga_pub_2017 / blca_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018 (412 MI BLCA samples for methylation + somatic mutation), Hurst NMI-BLCA cohort blca_nmibc_2017 (n=24 TaG2), blca_dfarber_mskcc_2014 (DFCI), blca_bgi, MSKCC UTUC cohort utuc_mskcc_2015 for KMT2C/FGFR3 co-occurrence replication, and ccle_broad_2019 for 20 bladder cell lines (DNA methylation + expression from GDSC).
  • Bioinformatic pipeline: ANNOVAR + SIFT annotation; MutSigCV v1.41 for SMGs; deconstructSigs and NMF for COSMIC v2 mutational signatures; GISTIC 2.0 for focal SCNAs (q<0.2); ChAMP for DMP/DMR with DMRcate; iClusterBayes for multi-omics integration; consensusMIBC for MIBC transcriptomic subtype assignment plus the BASE47 47-gene classifier; MCP-counter and ESTIMATE for immune/stromal deconvolution; MeTIL score for methylation-derived TIL inference.

Key findings

  • Three SMGs in UTUC by MutSigCV (FDR<0.05): FGFR3 50% (FDR=3.15×10⁻³), KDM6A 27% (FDR=1.03×10⁻³), and novel ZFP36L1 20% (FDR=2.62×10⁻³). Average tumor mutation burden 2.9±3.5 mut/Mb; median 1.5 (range 0.3–15.7).
  • Additional recurrently mutated (>10%, P<0.05, FDR>0.05): KMT2D (reported as “MLL2”, P=0.037), KMT2C (P=0.02), STAG2 (P=8.3×10⁻⁷), ARID1A (P=0.01), TP53 (P=0.018), CRIPAK (P=0.0001), GANAB (P=0.04).
  • Co-occurrence patterns: FGFR3↔︎KMT2C co-occur (log odds ratio = 3.1, P=0.04); validated in utuc_mskcc_2015 (LOR=2.9, P<0.001, FDR<0.001). ZFP36L1 co-occurs with both KDM6A (LOR=3.1, P=0.03) and STAG2 (LOR=3.29, P=0.04).
  • ZFP36L1 mutation spectrum: 8 mutations in 6 cases; 6/8 frameshift indels, 2 deleterious missense (Polyphen2/SIFT). Adding ZFP36L2 E249X (a known TCGA-BLCA hotspot) and ZFP36 p.P253A mutations gives 26.7% (8/30) UTUCs with ZFP36-family mutations. ZFP36L1 mutations across 10,967 TCGA tumors (32 histologies): 120 (1.09%), highest in bladder (30/410 = 7.3%); when extended to ZFP36 family, frequency reaches ~3% pan-cancer and 9.1–10% across three bladder cohorts (blca_tcga_pub_2017, blca_dfarber_mskcc_2014, blca_bgi).
  • ZFP36-family mutations associate with higher mutational load in UTUC (P=0.008) and in TCGA BLCA (P=0.0009); pooled bladder cohorts (n=562) show better OS (P=0.01) and trend toward better PFS (P=0.08) for ZFP36-mutant tumors.
  • Functional ZFP36L1 knockdown (siRNA in TCCSUP bladder cancer line) caused loss of E-cadherin, EMT-consistent spindle-shaped morphology, and significantly increased migration on transwell assay; no effect on proliferation or apoptosis. Knockdown confirmed by Western blot.
  • Recurrent focal SCNAs (GISTIC): losses at 1p36.33, 9p21.3 (encompassing CDKN2A/CDKN2B), 11p15.1; gains at 1q23.3, 6p21.33, 8p11.23, 8q22.3 (YWHAZ), 12q15 (MDM2), 19q12 (CCNE1). 8q22.3 gain co-occurs with TP53 mutations (P=0.02) and is mutually exclusive with FGFR3 mutations (P=0.03); 8q22.3 gain, 12q15 gain, and 11p15.11 loss enrich in MI cases.
  • NMI- vs MI-UTUC comparison with bladder cohorts: vs Hurst NMI-BLCA (blca_nmibc_2017), KMT2C trend higher in NMI-UTUC (P=0.08) while PIK3CA higher in NMI-BLCA (P=0.0001). vs TCGA MI-BLCA (n=412), MI-UTUC has more GANAB (P=0.021), CRIPAK (P=0.022), and trend for ZFP36L1 (P=0.1).
  • Mutational signatures: signatures 1, 13, 16 dominate. NMF resolved three clusters — C1 (n=7, sig 16, unknown etiology), C2 (n=8, sig 1, age-related deamination), C3 (n=15, sigs 13/2, APOBEC). No association with MeTIL score.
  • FGFR3-mutant UTUC is immune-cold: significantly lower MeTIL score than FGFR3-WT (P=7.8×10⁻⁵), validated in TCGA-BLCA (P=9.0×10⁻⁶); independent of muscle invasiveness (P=0.008 for MI; P=0.024 for NMI). FGFR3 mutation/fusion also linked to lower immune (P=0.016) and stromal (P=0.003) gene-expression scores.
  • Transcriptomic subtypes: by consensusMIBC, 14/20 (70%) were luminal papillary (including all NMI cases), 2 luminal unstable, 2 stromal-rich, 2 basal/squamous; none of the stromal-rich/basal-squamous had FGFR3 mutations. BASE47 classified 14 luminal-like and 6 basal-like. One FGFR3-TACC3 fusion detected (UTUC-11).
  • DNA methylation defines two epi-clusters (top 1% variable probes, n=836,691 retained): EpiC-high (n=23, formerly C1, hypermethylated: 14,209 hypermethylated probes vs only 34 in EpiC-low) and EpiC-low (n=12, formerly C2). DMR-enriched pathways: PRC2 / MLL targets, SMARCA2 target genes, ZEB1 targets.
    • EpiC-high = hypermethylated, immune-infiltrated (higher MeTIL, P=0.003), MI-enriched (16/23 = 69.6%), enriched for SWI/SNF gene mutations (50% vs 0%, P=0.01, FDR=0.054).
    • EpiC-low = hypomethylated, immune-depleted, NMI-enriched (11/12 = 91.7%, P=0.0009), enriched for FGFR3 mutations (90% vs 28%, P=0.004, FDR=0.044).
    • Prognostic: EpiC-high → shorter OS (P=0.038; HR=6.57; 95% CI 1.87–23.04) and shorter distant-metastasis-free survival (P=0.036; HR=6.55; 95% CI 1.87–22.98). No other clinicopathological variable was independently prognostic.
  • FGFR3-mutant UTUC is hypomethylated relative to FGFR3-WT: 84,717/836,691 probes differentially methylated (Δβ≥0.2, FDR<0.05); 82,991 (97.8%) hypomethylated in FGFR3-mut. Hypomethylated probes enriched in enhancers (3.8% vs 3.2%) and DNase HS sites (63.7% vs 57.4%); top DMRs overlap bladder genes including FGFR3, GATA3, KRT15, KRT5. Validated in 20 bladder cell lines (ccle_broad_2019): cell lines clustered by FGFR3 alteration (mutations/fusions) vs SWI/SNF gene mutations using the 14,209-probe signature; cluster C1 (SWI/SNF-mut) showed higher interferon-γ stimulation signature (P=0.022).
  • Translation to TCGA MI-BLCA: projecting the EpiC signature onto blca_tcga_pub_2017 recovered two analogous subgroups — BLCA-C1 (hypermethylated, higher MeTIL, immune-inflamed with fibroblasts/MDC/monocytes/B/T/CD8 by MCP-counter, high-grade, non-papillary, poor OS) vs BLCA-C2 (FGFR3-amp/mut/overexpressing, neutrophil-enriched P=1.4×10⁻⁸, papillary, low-grade). BLCA-C1 vs BLCA-C2 OS: HR=1.38 (95% CI 1.03–1.85), P=0.035; median OS 2.4 vs 5.4 years.
  • Promoter CpG-island methylation: 905 frequently methylated genes (>10%) in UTUC tumors; ACTL6B (a SWI/SNF subunit) hypermethylated in 20% of tumors vs 0% of normals. No promoter methylation of ZFP36L1 detected.
  • iClusterBayes on 28 UTUCs with paired WES+methylation yielded iCluster-high (n=17) vs iCluster-low (n=9), strongly congruent with EpiC clusters (P=4.9×10⁻⁵). Posterior-probability mutation drivers: FGFR3 (0.99), SWI/SNF (0.63), KMT2D (0.63), TP53 (0.56); FGFR3 (P=0.011, FDR=0.047) and KMT2D (P=0.028, FDR=0.057) enriched in iCluster-low; 8q22.3 amplification enriched in iCluster-high (P=0.023, FDR=0.093).
  • Among recurrently mutated genes, only FGFR3 mutations independently associated with improved OS (P=0.005) and PFS at distant sites (P=0.007); FGFR3 mutations were enriched in NMI vs MI (73% vs 27%, P=0.027, FDR=0.2); ARID1A mutations were enriched in MI (33% vs 0%, P=0.042, FDR=0.2).

Genes & alterations

  • FGFR3 — Mutations in 50% (15/30) of UTUC WES cases; defining driver of the EpiC-low (hypomethylated, immune-cold) subtype; mutually exclusive with 8q22.3 gain; FGFR3-mut UTUC has globally hypomethylated genome (97.8% of differential probes hypomethylated). One FGFR3-TACC3 fusion detected. Associates with better OS/PFS.
  • KDM6A — Mutations in 27% of WES UTUC cases (FDR=1.03×10⁻³); co-occurs with ZFP36L1 (LOR=3.1, P=0.03).
  • ZFP36L1 — Novel UTUC tumor suppressor: 20% mutation rate, 6/8 frameshift indels; siRNA knockdown in TCCSUP bladder line drives loss of E-cadherin, EMT-like morphology, and increased migration with no proliferation/apoptosis effect; 7.3% in TCGA bladder, highest among all 32 TCGA histologies profiled.
  • ZFP36L2 — Recurrent E249X stop-gain (TCGA-BLCA hotspot) identified in 1 UTUC; ZFP36L1 and ZFP36L2 mutations are mutually co-occurring across TCGA (P<0.001, FDR=0.002).
  • ZFP36 — Deleterious p.P253A mutation in 1 UTUC (COSMIC-curated); contributes to 26.7% ZFP36-family mutation rate in UTUC.
  • KMT2C — Mutated in >10% UTUC (P=0.02); co-occurs with FGFR3 (LOR=3.1, P=0.04); replicated in utuc_mskcc_2015 (LOR=2.9, P<0.001).
  • KMT2D — Reported as “MLL2” in the paper (the older synonym for KMT2D); recurrent mutations (P=0.037) associated with higher mutational load (P=0.04). Posterior probability 0.63 in iClusterBayes; enriched in iCluster-low (FGFR3-rich subtype), P=0.028.
  • STAG2 — Recurrent mutations (P=8.3×10⁻⁷); co-occur with ZFP36L1 (LOR=3.29, P=0.04).
  • ARID1A — Recurrent (P=0.01); associated with higher mutational load (P=0.01); enriched in MI tumors (33% vs 0% in NMI, P=0.042, FDR=0.2); collapsed into the “SWI/SNF” composite mutation feature for iClusterBayes.
  • TP53 — Recurrent (P=0.018); co-occurs with 8q22.3 gain (P=0.02); iCluster posterior 0.56.
  • CRIPAK — Recurrent in UTUC (P=0.0001); enriched in MI-UTUC vs TCGA MI-BLCA (P=0.022). Caveat: CRIPAK has since been reported as a problematic gene model — the paper does not flag this.
  • GANAB — Recurrent (P=0.04); associated with higher mutational load (P=0.02); enriched in MI-UTUC vs TCGA MI-BLCA (P=0.021).
  • CDKN2A / CDKN2B — Lost at 9p21.3 (focal GISTIC peak); deletion contributes to iClusterBayes (posterior 0.54).
  • MDM2 (12q15), CCNE1 (19q12), YWHAZ (8q22.3) — Recurrent focal SCNA gains; 8q22.3 (YWHAZ region) gain enriches in MI and iCluster-high (P=0.023, FDR=0.093) and was recently linked to aggressive bladder cancer.
  • TACC3 — Partner in the single FGFR3-TACC3 oncogenic fusion identified by RNA-seq (case UTUC-11).
  • SMARCA4 — Co-occurred with FGFR3 mutation in the 639V bladder cell line (where FGFR3 mutation is otherwise non-functional/non-expressing in J82).
  • SMARCA2 — SMARCA2 target gene set enriched among DMRs distinguishing EpiC-high from EpiC-low (GSEA).
  • ACTL6B — SWI/SNF member; promoter CpG-island hypermethylation in 20% of UTUC tumors vs 0% of normals.
  • GATA3, KRT5, KRT15 — Among the top DMRs distinguishing FGFR3-mutant from FGFR3-WT UTUC (genes known to be involved in bladder cancer luminal/basal identity).
  • HRAS — Cited as a prior UTUC-relevant gene whose mutation frequency differs from bladder carcinomas (no new findings in this paper).
  • PIK3CA — Significantly enriched in NMI-BLCA (blca_nmibc_2017) vs NMI-UTUC (P=0.0001); shows tissue-of-origin difference between upper-tract and bladder NMI tumors.
  • EZH2 — Therapeutic implication discussed: EZH2 inhibition ± cisplatin proposed as strategy for the SWI/SNF-mutant EpiC-high subtype based on cited prior NK-cell-activating mechanism (no in-paper EZH2 mutation data).
  • ZEB1 — ZEB1 target gene set enriched among EpiC-defining DMRs (GSEA).

Clinical implications

  • EpiC classification as prognostic stratifier: EpiC-high vs EpiC-low is the only independent predictor of OS (HR=6.57; 95% CI 1.87–23.04; P=0.038) and distant-metastasis-free survival (HR=6.55; 95% CI 1.87–22.98; P=0.036) in this UTUC cohort, beating all conventional clinicopathological features. Authors propose using it for post-resection stratification (e.g., who should receive preoperative chemotherapy or adjuvant FGFR3-targeted therapy).
  • EpiC-low / FGFR3-mutant subtype = candidate for FGFR inhibition + immune sensitization: Authors note that erdafitinib received accelerated FDA approval for relapsed/refractory metastatic bladder cancer (40% response rate, n=90, in tumors with actionable FGFR alterations) and propose combination with PD-1/PD-L1 blockade to convert the T-cell-depleted FGFR3-mutant tumors into immune-responsive ones.
  • EpiC-high / SWI/SNF-mutant subtype = candidate for EZH2 inhibition: Cite prior work showing EZH2 inhibition ± cisplatin activates NK-cell-mediated immunity in bladder cancer xenografts; propose this as a strategy for FGFR3-WT, SWI/SNF-mutant, immune-inflamed MI UTUC.
  • FGFR3 mutation status as a biomarker of “cold” tumors: FGFR3 mutation independently associates with low MeTIL score in both UTUC and TCGA-BLCA (P=7.8×10⁻⁵ and P=9.0×10⁻⁶), reinforcing the rationale to combine FGFR-directed agents with checkpoint inhibition in this lineage.
  • ZFP36-family mutations as a putative TMB / outcome marker: ZFP36-family-mutant tumors have higher mutational load and better pooled-bladder-cohort OS (P=0.01, n=562); not formally a biomarker yet but suggested for follow-up.

Limitations & open questions

  • Small monocentric cohort (n=40 UTUC; 30 WES; 20 RNA-seq; 35 methylation) — authors explicitly call out the rarity-driven sample size; some sub-analyses (e.g., RNA clusters vs clinicopathology) were under-powered.
  • No matched primary/metastasis profiling — stability of EpiC subtypes across disease progression is untested.
  • CRIPAK as a recurrently mutated gene is reported uncritically; the broader community has since flagged CRIPAK as a likely false-positive gene model. The paper does not address this.
  • Mechanistic gap on FGFR3 → hypomethylation axis: Whether the global hypomethylation seen in FGFR3-mutant UTUC is a direct FGFR3-signaling consequence or a passenger of the luminal-papillary lineage is left as an open question.
  • SWI/SNF mutation ↔︎ immune infiltration link is correlative and contradictory across cancer types (authors cite PMID:30166959-style debate, not in corpus, without resolving for UTUC).
  • Functional validation of ZFP36L1 done in a single bladder cell line (TCCSUP) with siRNA, not CRISPR; in vivo and UTUC-specific models are missing.
  • Therapeutic recommendations (FGFR+PD-1 combo; EZH2 inhibition) are hypotheses — not tested prospectively in this paper.
  • External UTUC validation cohort for the EpiC classifier is absent; the methylation classifier is only projected onto bladder (blca_tcga_pub_2017, blca_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018) and bladder cell lines (ccle_broad_2019) — UTUC-specific reproduction in utuc_mskcc_2015 is limited to FGFR3/KMT2C co-occurrence.
