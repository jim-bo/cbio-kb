# SLC27A5

## Overview

SLC27A5 (FATP5) is a bile acid CoA ligase and fatty acid transporter. In [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md), loss of SLC27A5 promotes tumor progression through activation of the KEAP1/NRF2 axis, AKT/mTOR signaling, and epithelial-mesenchymal transition.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- SLC27A5 (FATP5) loss promotes HCC via KEAP1/NRF2, AKT/mTOR, and EMT pathways; FATP5 loss is a tumor-suppressor mechanism distinct from FATP4 upregulation [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) — FATP5 loss is a tumor-promoting mechanism activating KEAP1/NRF2 and AKT/mTOR [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- FATP5 loss and FATP4 upregulation represent distinct lipid-metabolic resistance mechanisms in HCC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Restoring FATP5 expression or blocking downstream KEAP1/NRF2 activation is proposed as a therapeutic strategy [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Open questions

- Whether FATP5 loss is a mutation-driven or epigenetic event in clinical HCC is not detailed in the corpus.
