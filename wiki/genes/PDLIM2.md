# PDLIM2

## Overview

PDLIM2 (PDZ and LIM domain 2) is an actin-cytoskeleton scaffolding protein that regulates cell polarity, adhesion, and NF-kB signaling. Its differential phosphorylation in CTNNB1-mutant [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) links it to Wnt-driven cytoskeletal remodeling and loss of epithelial polarity.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- PDLIM2 phosphorylation altered in CTNNB1-mutant HCCs (actin-organization phosphoprotein), consistent with loss of epithelial polarity and Wnt-driven cytoskeletal remodeling; identified in a European HCC proteogenomics cohort (n=122 WES, n=51 with phosphoproteome) alongside [PARD3](../genes/PARD3.md), [MAP1B](../genes/MAP1B.md), and [MACF1](../genes/MACF1.md) [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md): Differential phosphorylation in CTNNB1-mutant tumors [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Phosphorylation co-altered with PARD3, MAP1B, and MACF1 in CTNNB1-mutant HCCs, forming a coordinated actin-cytoskeleton remodeling signature [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic target data from the corpus; downstream of Wnt/CTNNB1 signaling which is a candidate therapeutic axis in HCC cluster 2 [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Open questions

- Whether the phosphorylation changes observed in CTNNB1-mutant HCCs are causally driven by [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) mutation or are correlative requires functional mutagenesis experiments.
