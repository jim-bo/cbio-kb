# CASP5

## Overview

CASP5 (Caspase 5) is an inflammatory caspase involved in innate immune signaling and apoptosis. In colon cancer, CASP5 is one of four newly identified significantly mutated genes (SMGs) restricted to the hypermutated (MSI-H) subgroup in a prospective proteogenomic cohort, with mutation frequency >50% of hypermutated samples — a discovery not previously reported in the TCGA colorectal study.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- CASP5 identified as a newly discovered SMG in the hypermutated (MSI-H) subgroup of the CPTAC proteogenomic colon cancer cohort (n=110); mutated in >50% of hypermutated samples; not reported as an SMG in the prior TCGA colorectal study [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md):** Hypermutated-group SMG in the CPTAC colon cohort; frameshift/truncating mutations are expected in the MSI-H context [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with other newly identified hypermutated SMGs [BMPR2](../genes/BMPR2.md), [RNF43](../genes/RNF43.md), and [LTN1](../genes/LTN1.md) in MSI-H colon tumors; all four were mutated in >50% of hypermutated samples in the CPTAC cohort [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting described in the corpus; CASP5 mutations may be MSI-H-context bystander events or functionally relevant to immunogenic cell death pathways [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Open questions

- Whether CASP5 mutations are drivers or passengers in MSI-H colon cancer remains unresolved; functional validation and mechanistic studies are explicitly outside the scope of the CPTAC proteogenomic study [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)
