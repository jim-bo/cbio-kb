# LINC01003

## Overview

LINC01003 is a long non-coding RNA (lncRNA) with tumor-suppressor activity. It has been shown to act via the miR-33a-5p / [PIM1](../genes/PIM1.md) axis in multiple myeloma. In the context of cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma ([CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md)), it was identified as one of the most recurrently mutated genes in metastatic disease, occurring in the non-coding space invisible to standard whole-exome sequencing or targeted gene panels.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Recurrently mutated tumor-suppressor lncRNA in 64% (16/25) of metastatic cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma (mCSCC) samples profiled by WGS; identified by all three driver-calling tools (OncodriveFML, dNdScv, MutSig); proposed as a candidate non-coding biomarker of metastatic CSCC [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Metastatic CSCC ([CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md)) — 64% of WGS-profiled mCSCC samples (n=25) carry LINC01003 SNVs; recurrence nominates it as a functional non-coding driver in the metastatic setting [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- None reported.

## Therapeutic relevance

- Proposed as a candidate non-coding biomarker for metastatic CSCC; alterations reside in genomic regions inaccessible to standard WES or targeted-panel platforms, supporting WGS for comprehensive biomarker ascertainment in mCSCC [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Open questions

- Functional mechanism in CSCC is inferred by analogy to the miR-33a-5p / PIM1 axis described in multiple myeloma; direct mechanistic validation in CSCC is lacking.
- Cohort is small (n=25) and predominantly male (24/25); independent replication required.
