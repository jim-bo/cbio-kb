# IFNGR1

## Overview

IFNGR1 encodes the ligand-binding chain of the interferon-gamma receptor (IFN-γR1), a key component of innate and adaptive anti-tumor immune signaling. Loss-of-function mutations in IFNGR1 can impair IFN-γ pathway activity, while gain-of-function or other mutations may influence tumor immunogenicity and response to immune checkpoint blockade.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- IFNGR1 mutations (n=3) found exclusively in responders to combination PD-1+CTLA-4 blockade ([nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md)+[ipilimumab](../drugs/ipilimumab.md)) in [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) cohort (n=75); potential role in interferon-gamma signaling and anti-tumor immune response. [PMID:29657128](../papers/29657128.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md): IFNGR1 mutations observed exclusively in immunotherapy responders in a 75-patient NSCLC cohort treated with combination PD-1+CTLA-4 blockade. [PMID:29657128](../papers/29657128.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- IFNGR1 mutations found exclusively among responders to nivolumab + ipilimumab in NSCLC; relevance to IFN-γ signaling in immunotherapy response warrants further study. [PMID:29657128](../papers/29657128.md)

## Open questions

- Whether IFNGR1 mutations are predictive of immunotherapy benefit or merely co-occur with other responder features (e.g., high TMB) is unresolved given the small number of cases (n=3). [PMID:29657128](../papers/29657128.md)
