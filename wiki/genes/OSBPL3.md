# OSBPL3

## Overview

OSBPL3 (Oxysterol Binding Protein Like 3) is a lipid-transfer protein involved in intracellular sterol transport and membrane trafficking. In cancer genomics, OSBPL3 has been identified as part of a metastasis-associated mutational signature in hormone receptor-positive, HER2-negative breast cancer.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Member of an eight-gene metastasis-enrichment signature ([ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md), [FSIP2](../genes/FSIP2.md), AGRN, [FRAS1](../genes/FRAS1.md), [IGFN1](../genes/IGFN1.md), [EDC4](../genes/EDC4.md), OSBPL3, [PALB2](../genes/PALB2.md)) that carries a 2-fold higher death hazard in HR+/HER2− metastatic breast cancer vs early breast cancer; authors note the finding requires validation independent of mutational load [PMID:28027327](../papers/28027327.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **Breast cancer ([BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md))**: Implicated via metastasis-enrichment signature in HR+/HER2− metastatic breast cancer (n=216 mBC cohort) [PMID:28027327](../papers/28027327.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with ESR1, PALB2, and six other genes in an 8-gene metastatic-enrichment signature in HR+/HER2− breast cancer [PMID:28027327](../papers/28027327.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting reported in this corpus.

## Open questions

- The survival association with the 8-gene metastatic signature (including OSBPL3) was not corrected for mutational load or signature contribution, limiting causal inference [PMID:28027327](../papers/28027327.md)
