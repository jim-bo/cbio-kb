**PMID:** 34358469 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.cell.2021.07.016 · **Journal:** Cell (2021)

## TL;DR

The CPTAC consortium performed a deep proteogenomic characterization of 108 treatment-naive primary lung squamous cell carcinomas (LSCC) and 99 paired normal adjacent tissues (NATs), integrating WES, WGS, RNA-seq, miRNA-seq, methylation arrays, and TMT-based global proteomics with phospho-, acetyl-, and (for the first time in LSCC) ubiquitylproteomics ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)). NMF-based multi-omic clustering identified five subtypes — Basal-Inclusive (B-I), EMT-Enriched (EMT-E), Classical, Inflamed-Secretory (I-S), and Proliferative-Primitive (P-P) — and prioritized [NSD3](../genes/NSD3.md) rather than [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) as the likely 8p11.23 amplicon driver, nominated [BIRC5](../genes/BIRC5.md) (survivin) as a target in ΔNp63-low tumors, supported chromatin modifiers ([KDM1A](../genes/KDM1A.md)/LSD1, [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md)) for targeting [SOX2](../genes/SOX2.md)-overexpressing tumors, and argued for RB1-protein-based stratification before declaring CDK4/6 inhibition unsuccessful in LSCC ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).

## Cohort & data

- **108 treatment-naive primary [LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md) tumors + 99 paired NATs**, collected prospectively by 13 tissue source sites in 7 countries between May 2016 and August 2018 (median follow-up 2.5 years; age range 40–88; 90 male / 23 female before exclusions; 5 of 113 originally enrolled samples excluded after pathology review) ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- Dataset is released as cBioPortal study [lusc_cptac_2021](../datasets/lusc_cptac_2021.md); processed data at LinkedOmics; raw at PDC (PDC000232/233/234/237), GDC (dbGaP phs001287.v10.p5), and TCIA.
- Nine data modalities: [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md), [whole-genome-seq](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md), [rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md), [mirna-seq](../methods/mirna-seq.md), [epic-methylation-array](../methods/epic-methylation-array.md), TMT11-based [tmt-proteomics](../methods/tmt-proteomics.md) and [tmt-phosphoproteomics](../methods/tmt-phosphoproteomics.md), acetylproteomics, and ubiquitylproteomics; reference genome GRCh38/hg38.
- Companion analytic methods include [nmf-clustering](../methods/nmf-clustering.md) (multi-omic subtyping), [gistic](../methods/gistic.md) (focal CNAs), [xcell](../methods/xcell.md) and [estimate](../methods/estimate.md) (immune deconvolution), [clumps](../methods/clumps.md) (PTM 3D clustering), and [immunohistochemistry](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md) (validation).

## Key findings

- **[NSD3](../genes/NSD3.md) ([WHSC1L1](../genes/WHSC1L1.md)), not [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md), is the likely driver of 8p11.23 amplifications in LSCC.** Of 5,523 significant (FDR<0.01) cis CNA-mRNA events, 2,154 also correlated with protein; six amplified and 29 deleted "cancer-associated genes" (CAGs) were identified in significant focal events (Q<0.25). Within the recurrent 8p11.23 amplicon, proteomic data implicated [NSD3](../genes/NSD3.md) rather than [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) as the critical oncogene, offering a potential mechanistic explanation for the historical failure of FGFR1-targeted therapy in this population ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- **Five multi-omic NMF subtypes.** Basal-Inclusive (B-I; basaloid histology, upregulated metabolic/immune/ER signaling), EMT-Enriched (EMT-E; myxoid features, fibroblast infiltration, Wnt regulators DVL3/FN1/FHL2), Classical (KEAP1/CUL3/NFE2L2 mutations, SOX2/TP63 high-level amplification, CIMP-high, OXPHOS-up, immune-down), Inflamed-Secretory (I-S), and Proliferative-Primitive (P-P) ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- **"Mixed" cluster-membership samples had significantly worse survival** and were enriched for [SOX2](../genes/SOX2.md) amplifications (p=0.0038); other tested variables (grade, mutation burden, stemness) showed no association ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- **EMT-E tumors show phosphorylation-driven [PDGFRB](../genes/PDGFRB.md) and [ROR2](../genes/ROR2.md) signaling.** Of 9 RTKs with high correlation-based phosphosite enrichment (CBPE) scores, 7 associated with NMF subtype (Kruskal-Wallis p<0.01); [PDGFRB](../genes/PDGFRB.md) and [ROR2](../genes/ROR2.md) scores were markedly elevated in EMT-E, correlated with loss of epithelial junction markers and gain of mesenchymal/ECM-remodeling proteins, and implicated non-canonical Wnt branches as targetable pathways ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- **Universal CDK4/6-pathway dysregulation.** Across 108 tumors, every sample showed loss of at least one of [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) or [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) function (homozygous deletion of CDKN2A → loss of both p16/p14; 28 CDKN2A-WT tumors had p16-promoter hypermethylation; the eight CDKN2A-WT tumors retaining high p16 expression all had RB1 mutations/deletions or low Rb protein), consistent with the mutual exclusivity reported pan-cancer (Knudsen et al., 2020). [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) amplification correlated with higher mean Rb protein and phospho-Rb but with broad overlap, helping explain heterogeneous response to [abemaciclib](../drugs/abemaciclib.md) and [palbociclib](../drugs/palbociclib.md) ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- **NRF2 pathway activation extends beyond mutation.** 33 tumors carried NFE2L2/KEAP1/CUL3 hits (28 one-hit, 5 two-hit) versus 68 NRF2-WT; an ssGSEA NRF2 pathway score was elevated in mutated tumors, with the five two-hit cases showing dose-dependent up-/down-regulation of NRF2-pathway genes. The score was also elevated in many NRF2-WT Classical-subtype tumors with [NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md) phosphorylation, and [CDK5](../genes/CDK5.md) protein and PTM-SEA-derived activity were significantly upregulated in the Classical subtype (FDR<0.05), suggesting CDK5-mediated NFE2L2 S433 phosphorylation as an alternative NRF2 activation mechanism ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- **ΔNp63-low LSCC subset overexpresses survivin ([BIRC5](../genes/BIRC5.md)).** 10 histologically-confirmed LSCC samples lacked [TP63](../genes/TP63.md) RNA/protein elevation; survivin was among the most overexpressed proteins in this group, and TP63-low LSCC cell lines were significantly more vulnerable to the survivin inhibitor YM-155, nominating TP63 status as a biomarker for survivin-directed therapy ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- **Chromatin regulators as a path to "undruggable" [SOX2](../genes/SOX2.md).** [KDM1A](../genes/KDM1A.md) (LSD1) protein correlated significantly with SOX2 protein in unbiased correlation analysis; [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) dependency was observed in SOX2-amplified LSCC cell lines; KDM3A also surfaced as a known SOX2 regulator ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- **TP63 / [TP73](../genes/TP73.md) / miR-205 axis.** miR-205 correlated tightly with TP63 expression, with ΔNp63α a transcriptional regulator of the miRNA; [TP73](../genes/TP73.md) correlated with miR-205 at transcript and (more strongly) at protein level; miR-205 negatively correlated with EMT activity and positively with DNA replication ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- **Two ubiquitylproteome clusters, with HERC5/ISG15 ISGylation linking immune signaling to glycolysis.** Consensus clustering on K-GG abundances (cognate-protein-corrected) yielded two clusters associated with country of origin and ethnicity (Fisher p<0.01); 474 Ub/UbL sites correlated positively with [HERC5](../genes/HERC5.md) (FDR<0.01) and were enriched for glycolysis / TCA cycle, including K-GG sites on [PKM](../genes/PKM.md), PGK1 and ENO1; [HERC5](../genes/HERC5.md) was elevated in the IFN-gamma dominant immune subtype ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- **PTM 3D clustering ([clumps](../methods/clumps.md)).** Significant ubiquitylation clusters in 33 proteins and acetylation clusters in 17 proteins (p<0.05); top hits enriched for glycolysis, fatty acid metabolism, and OXPHOS. PGK1 and PKM showed reduced acetylation at known inhibitory sites in tumors versus NATs ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- **Three immune microenvironment classes (Hot / Warm / Cold) plus an NAT-enriched cluster** from [xcell](../methods/xcell.md) signatures. NMF I-S subtype strongly associated with the Hot cluster (Fisher p=6.371×10⁻⁹). Hot tumors upregulated [CTLA4](../genes/CTLA4.md), PD-1 ([PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md)), PD-L1 ([CD274](../genes/CD274.md)), [IDO1](../genes/IDO1.md) and [FOXP3](../genes/FOXP3.md), plus Rho GTPase signaling (with K135 acetylation of RhoGDIb/ARHGDIB as a hypothesized regulator of immune-cell function), and [CSF1R](../genes/CSF1R.md) phosphosite activity ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- **[EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) biology differs between squamous and adenocarcinoma lung tumors.** [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) protein was significantly upregulated in squamous cancers but not [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md); EGFR amplification did not correlate with PROGENy-based EGFR pathway activity, which instead tracked mRNA abundance of five EGFR ligands. The authors argue ligand abundance — rather than EGFR amplification — may better predict response to ligand-blocking inhibitors like [cetuximab](../drugs/cetuximab.md) in squamous tumors ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- **Biomarker mining vs. TCGA LSCC.** Of 502 tumor-vs-NAT differential proteins, 4 highly differential genes associated significantly with poor overall survival and 15 more with poor disease-free survival in the TCGA LSCC cohort; knockdown of these biomarker proteins reduced fitness across 16 LSCC cell lines (DepMap) ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).

## Genes & alterations

- [NSD3](../genes/NSD3.md) (WHSC1L1) — recurrent 8p11.23 focal amplification (co-amplified with [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md)); proteomic evidence nominates NSD3 as the driver oncogene; independently validated as a key LSCC oncogenic chromatin regulator (Yuan et al., 2021 — cited in this paper) ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) — co-amplified in the 8p11.23 amplicon but protein-level data argues against it as the primary oncogene, consistent with failure of FGFR1-targeted therapy in LSCC ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- [SOX2](../genes/SOX2.md) — recurrent 3q amplification, co-amplified with [TP63](../genes/TP63.md); considered undruggable but anti-correlated with JAK-STAT signaling and [JAK1](../genes/JAK1.md) protein, and proposed as targetable via chromatin modifiers [KDM1A](../genes/KDM1A.md)/LSD1 and [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- [TP63](../genes/TP63.md) — 3q amplification; ΔNp63α dominant isoform; high TP63 expression correlated with improved survival in TCGA LSCC; 10 "Np63-low" LSCC tumors lacked TP63 elevation and overexpressed [BIRC5](../genes/BIRC5.md) ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- [BIRC5](../genes/BIRC5.md) (survivin) — overexpressed specifically in Np63-low LSCC; TP63-low cell lines sensitive to survivin inhibitor YM-155 ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- [KDM1A](../genes/KDM1A.md) (LSD1) — strongest protein-protein correlation with SOX2 in unbiased screen; clinical trial NCT04350463 (LSD1 + immunotherapy) cited ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) — dependency observed in SOX2-amplified LSCC cell lines ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — homozygous deletion abolishes p16/p14; mutations paradoxically yielded increased RNA; promoter hypermethylation of p16 (not p14) in 28 [WT](../cancer_types/WT.md) tumors; loss of CDKN2A or RB1 is universal in LSCC ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — mutated tumors had upregulated cell-cycle proteins; loss of Rb protein in CDKN2A-WT samples with preserved p16 expression; mutually exclusive with CDKN2A inactivation ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) — amplification associated with higher mean Rb protein and phospho-Rb (but overlapping distributions); no significant association of CCND2/CCND3/[CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md)/[CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md) amplification with CDKN2A/p16 or Rb protein levels ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- [CDK5](../genes/CDK5.md) — protein and PTM-SEA-derived kinase activity significantly upregulated in the Classical subtype (FDR<0.05); proposed CDK5-mediated phosphorylation of NFE2L2 S433 as a non-mutational driver of NRF2 activation ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- [NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md), [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md), [CUL3](../genes/CUL3.md) — NRF2 pathway mutations in 33/101 evaluable tumors; concordant differential mRNA/protein/phosphoprotein of NRF2 pathway genes; dosage effect at [SQSTM1](../genes/SQSTM1.md), [NR0B1](../genes/NR0B1.md), AKR1B10, [CARD11](../genes/CARD11.md), FFAR2 in two-hit cases. [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md) mutations did NOT reduce KEAP1 protein in LSCC (unlike LUAD), suggesting NRF2-pathway dysregulation differs between [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) subtypes ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md), contrasted with [PMID:32649874](../papers/32649874.md)).
- [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) — mutations associated with elevated [GSK3B](../genes/GSK3B.md) protein and T433 phosphorylation, decreased inhibitory GSK3A S21, and upregulated NLK; both NLK and GSK3B inhibit [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md), consistent with its tumor-suppressor role in LSCC ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) — mutations associated with higher TMB and downregulated [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) protein; ARID1A loss linked to worse [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) in NSCLC ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- [PDGFRB](../genes/PDGFRB.md), [ROR2](../genes/ROR2.md) — high CBPE scores in EMT-E subtype; targetable RTKs implicated in non-canonical Wnt signaling and EMT progression ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) — significantly upregulated in squamous lung and HNSCC tumors but not LUAD; EGFR amplification did NOT correlate with PROGENy EGFR pathway activity, which instead tracked ligand mRNA abundance ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- [CSF1R](../genes/CSF1R.md) — highest CBPE score among nine RTKs profiled; phospho-activity positively associated with the immune-Hot cluster and macrophage signatures ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- [HERC5](../genes/HERC5.md), [ISG15](../genes/ISG15.md) — [HERC5](../genes/HERC5.md) ([ISG15](../genes/ISG15.md) E3 ligase) correlated with 474 K-GG sites enriched for glycolysis/TCA; proposed bridge between IFN signaling and glycolytic regulation ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- [TP73](../genes/TP73.md) — positive correlation with miR-205 and TP63 protein; supports a TP63-TP73 stoichiometric model driving miR-205 overexpression ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- [PTGES3](../genes/PTGES3.md) — among the 20 genes whose promoter-methylation cis-effects cascaded to RNA, protein, and PTM levels, and that were differentially expressed between tumors and NATs ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).

## Clinical implications

- **CDK4/6 inhibition stratification.** Despite limited overall efficacy of [abemaciclib](../drugs/abemaciclib.md) and [palbociclib](../drugs/palbociclib.md) in LSCC trials, heterogeneous Rb protein and phospho-Rb in CCND1-amplified tumors offers a possible explanation for outlier responses. The authors argue that downstream functional assessment (RB1 protein expression + phospho-Rb) — not CDKN2A status alone — should guide CDK4/6-inhibitor patient selection ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- **NSD3 as an FGFR1-amplicon alternative.** Failed FGFR1-targeted therapy in 8p11.23-amplified LSCC may reflect that NSD3 is the true driver; NSD3-directed strategies (e.g., chromatin-targeting agents) are nominated for further study ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- **Survivin ([BIRC5](../genes/BIRC5.md)) inhibition in ΔNp63-low LSCC.** TP63-status (specifically Np63-low histologically-confirmed LSCC) is proposed as a candidate biomarker for survivin-inhibitor response, despite prior survivin trials in unselected NSCLC showing modest efficacy ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- **Chromatin-modifier strategy for SOX2-amplified LSCC.** [KDM1A](../genes/KDM1A.md)/LSD1 (clinical trial NCT04350463, LSD1 + immunotherapy) and [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) are nominated as actionable upstream regulators of "undruggable" [SOX2](../genes/SOX2.md) ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- **EGFR ligand-abundance as a biomarker for [cetuximab](../drugs/cetuximab.md)-class therapy in squamous lung.** Because EGFR amplification fails to predict EGFR pathway activity in LSCC and ligand mRNA abundance does, the authors suggest ligand abundance may better stratify response to ligand-blocking EGFR inhibitors than EGFR amplification ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- **Targetable EMT-E RTKs.** [PDGFRB](../genes/PDGFRB.md) and [ROR2](../genes/ROR2.md) phospho-activity in the EMT-E subtype is proposed as a therapeutic vulnerability; pharmacologic TGFβ inhibition was predicted to reverse the EMT-E signature by LINCS query ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- **Immune-Hot LSCC as candidates for checkpoint therapy.** Upregulation of CTLA4, PD-1 (PDCD1), PD-L1 (CD274), IDO1, and FOXP3 in the Hot cluster, plus the strong I-S/Hot association, supports immune-checkpoint targeting in this subset; [CSF1R](../genes/CSF1R.md) and AKAP13 are noted as additional anti-cancer targets in immune-Hot tumors ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- **Cross-cancer triangulation.** Comparison with LUAD and HNSCC delineated unique LSCC features (e.g., 4p14/5q deletions of TLR/interleukin loci) and shared squamous features (3q amplification, [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md) deletion, EGFR protein elevation); informs trial design that leverages or avoids tissue-of-origin assumptions ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Hypothesis-generating, not validated.** Authors emphasize that landscape-style proteogenomic surveys must be followed by targeted functional validation by the broader community before clinical action ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- **Bulk-tumor analysis cannot resolve intratumor heterogeneity.** Authors call out the need for microdissection-MS, mass cytometry, and single-cell approaches as adjuncts ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- **Primary tumors only.** No matched metastatic samples; LSCC mortality is driven by metastatic disease, and metastasis-focused proteogenomics is required to understand lethal biology ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- **Long-term clinical follow-up was incomplete** at time of analysis; survival biomarker validation relied on the external TCGA LSCC cohort ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- **Ubiquitylproteomics coverage incomplete** — not all 108 tumors had K-GG data; ubiquitylprotein data was excluded from the multi-omic NMF clustering for this reason ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- **Ubiquitylproteome clusters confounded by country of origin / ethnicity** (Fisher p<0.01) — the biological vs. technical / population-stratification interpretation is unresolved ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- **NRF2 kinase identity unclear.** [CDK5](../genes/CDK5.md) is nominated as a candidate kinase for NFE2L2 S433 phosphorylation in LSCC based on prior astrocyte work, but this remains hypothetical ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
- **Site-level functional roles of most acetyl/ubiquitin marks are unknown**, and crosstalk-analysis methodology is nascent — interpretations stated as "suitably cautious" ([PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)).
