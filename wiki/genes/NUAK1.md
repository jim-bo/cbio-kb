# NUAK1

## Overview

NUAK1 (NUAK Family Kinase 1), also known as ARK5, is a serine/threonine kinase belonging to the AMPK-related kinase family. NUAK1 has been implicated in cancer cell survival, anoikis resistance, and invasion. In esophageal adenocarcinoma (EAC), NUAK1 was nominated as a significantly mutated gene candidate by whole-exome sequencing.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Mutated in 3% of 145 esophageal adenocarcinoma cases; nominated as a significantly mutated gene candidate (MutSig FDR q<0.1) in the Broad WES cohort (149 tumor/normal pairs); classified with [AKAP6](../genes/AKAP6.md) (8%), [HECW1](../genes/HECW1.md) (8%), and [AJAP1](../genes/AJAP1.md) (6%) as additional significant candidates beyond the core set [PMID:23525077](../papers/23525077.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[ESCA](../cancer_types/ESCA.md) (Esophageal Adenocarcinoma):** Mutated in 3% of cases; one of 26 significantly mutated genes identified (MutSig q<0.1) in the 149-tumor Broad WES cohort [PMID:23525077](../papers/23525077.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Specific co-mutation patterns with other EAC drivers not reported in the paper [PMID:23525077](../papers/23525077.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- NUAK1 is a kinase and potentially targetable; no direct therapeutic data in EAC are reported in this study [PMID:23525077](../papers/23525077.md)

## Open questions

- Functional validation of NUAK1 mutations in EAC has not been performed; whether these mutations are gain-of-function, loss-of-function, or passenger events is unknown [PMID:23525077](../papers/23525077.md)
