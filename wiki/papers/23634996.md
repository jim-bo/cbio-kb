**PMID:** 23634996 · **DOI:** 10.1056/NEJMoa1301689 · **Journal:** N Engl J Med (2013)

## TL;DR

The Cancer Genome Atlas Research Network sequenced 200 clinically annotated adult de novo [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md) cases — 50 by whole-genome sequencing and 150 by whole-exome sequencing, complemented by RNA-seq, microRNA-seq, DNA-methylation profiling, and SNP arrays. Adult AML genomes are mutation-sparse (mean 13 coding mutations per sample, ~5 in recurrently mutated genes), yet nearly every sample (199/200) harbored at least one driver in one of nine functional categories: transcription-factor fusions (18%), [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md) (27%), tumor suppressors (16%), DNA-methylation genes (44%), activated signaling (59%), chromatin modifiers (30%), myeloid transcription factors (22%), cohesin-complex (13%), and spliceosome (14%). A novel co-occurring triplet of [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md) + [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md) + [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md) mutations defined a distinct epigenetic AML subtype.

## Cohort & data

- 200 adult de novo AML cases ([AML](../cancer_types/AML.md)) drawn from a single-institution Washington University tissue-banking protocol (samples banked Nov 2001–Mar 2010), selected to reflect a real-world distribution of FAB and cytogenetic subtypes.
- Dataset: [laml_tcga_pub](../datasets/laml_tcga_pub.md) (TCGA AML, NEJM 2013).
- Assays:
  - [whole-genome-seq](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) on 50 tumor/normal-skin pairs (mean coverage 30.54×).
  - [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) on the remaining 150 tumor/normal-skin pairs (mean coverage 167.50×).
  - [rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) on 179 samples; Affymetrix U133 Plus 2 expression on 197 samples.
  - microRNA sequencing on 194 samples.
  - [450k-methylation-array](../methods/450k-methylation-array.md) (Illumina Infinium HumanMethylation450 BeadChip) on 192 samples.
  - [affymetrix-snp6](../methods/affymetrix-snp6.md) on tumor and matched normal skin for all 200 patients.
- All candidate somatic variants validated with hybridization-capture deep digital sequencing.

## Key findings

- **Mutation burden.** 2,315 somatic SNVs and 270 small indels in coding regions across 200 samples (mean 13 tier-1 mutations/sample, range 0–51); 1,539 SNVs (66%) missense and 191 of 270 indels (71%) frameshift [PMID:23634996](../papers/23634996.md).
- **Significantly mutated genes.** 23 genes reached FDR <0.05 in the MuSiC SMG test, including [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md), [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md), [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md), [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md), [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md), [CEBPA](../genes/CEBPA.md), plus newly implicated [U2AF1](../genes/U2AF1.md), [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md), [SMC1A](../genes/SMC1A.md), and [SMC3](../genes/SMC3.md); an additional 237 genes were mutated in ≥2 samples.
- **Cohort stratification by fusion / risk.** Of the 200 samples, 11 had [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) (MLL) fusions with the fewest recurrent tier-1 mutations (mean 2.09 vs 5.24 overall, P=0.002); 20 [PML](../genes/PML.md)–[RARA](../genes/RARA.md) samples had mean 3.25 (P=0.001); 7 [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md)–[RUNX1T1](../genes/RUNX1T1.md) samples had mean 7.85 (P=0.04); 13 unfavorable-risk samples with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations had mean 7.00 (P=0.049).
- **Co-mutation.** [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md) co-occurred with [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md) (P<6.3×10⁻⁷) and with [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md) (P<1.9×10⁻⁶). Concurrent [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md) + [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md) + [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md) samples clustered together in mRNA, miRNA, and DNA-methylation space, defining a putative novel AML subtype.
- **Mutual exclusivity (Dendrix++).** Three sets emerged: (A) transcription-factor fusions, [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md), [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CEBPA](../genes/CEBPA.md); (B) [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md) vs other tyrosine kinases, serine–threonine kinases, phosphatases, and RAS-family proteins; (C) [ASXL1](../genes/ASXL1.md) vs cohesin-complex genes, other myeloid transcription factors, and other epigenetic modifiers. [PML](../genes/PML.md)–[RARA](../genes/RARA.md), [MYH11](../genes/MYH11.md)–[CBFB](../genes/CBFB.md), and MLL-fusion genes were mutually exclusive of [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md) and [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md) (P<0.007, P<0.04, P<0.04). [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md) and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations were mutually exclusive of [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md) and [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md).
- **Signaling.** [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md) mutated in 56/200 samples; 62 additional samples had mutations in other kinases, phosphatases, or RAS-family proteins ([KIT](../genes/KIT.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md), [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) dominated). 59% of samples had a signaling-pathway mutation.
- **Gene fusions.** De novo RNA-seq assembly across 179 samples identified 118 fusions in 80 samples (mean 1.5; range 0–8; 71 distinct events). 74 in-frame fusions included [PML](../genes/PML.md)–[RARA](../genes/RARA.md), [MYH11](../genes/MYH11.md)–[CBFB](../genes/CBFB.md), [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md)–[RUNX1T1](../genes/RUNX1T1.md), [BCR](../genes/BCR.md)–[ABL1](../genes/ABL1.md), [PICALM](../genes/PICALM.md)–[MLLT10](../genes/MLLT10.md)/AF10, [NUP98](../genes/NUP98.md)–[NSD1](../genes/NSD1.md), and multiple [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) (MLL) partners; 15 new in-frame events were identified. One out-of-frame fusion (GAS6–FAM70B) recurred in 3 samples.
- **miRNA variants.** Somatic SNVs in miRNA genes in 7/200 (4%); 4 of these targeted the seed region of mature miR-142-3p, expected to alter mRNA target specificity.
- **Allelic expression bias.** RNA-seq showed increased or exclusive expression of mutant [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md), [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md), [PHF6](../genes/PHF6.md), and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) alleles in several cases, frequently explained by LOH or partial uniparental disomy.
- **Clonal architecture.** Deep digital sequencing of WGS samples enabled VAF-based clustering; >50% of tumors had both a founding clone and ≥1 subclone, with up to three independent subclones detectable in a single tumor.
- **DNA methylation.** [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md)/[IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md)-mutant samples showed extensive methylation gains versus normal CD34+CD38− cells, while [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) fusions and concurrent [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md)+[DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md)+[FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md) samples showed extensive methylation loss. Across the cohort, 160,519 CpG loci (42% of those tested) were significantly differentially methylated (67% gain, 33% loss). Strongest signatures occurred in CpG-sparse regions rather than CpG islands. Triple-mutant samples lost methylation at 328/382 differentially methylated regions >1 kb (86%).
- **Expression clusters.** NMF consensus clustering identified 7 RNA-seq groups and 5 miRNA-seq groups; groups mapped onto FAB subtypes (group 4↔M1, group 3↔M3, group 5↔M4, group 7↔M5) and miRNA group 3 was strongly associated with [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md), [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md), [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md), and cohesin-complex mutations with high miR-10a and low miR-424.
- **Genome stability.** Median 1 somatic copy-number variant and <1 fusion event per genome; no chromothripsis observed; strong correlation (Pearson r=0.78) between coding and noncoding mutation counts, consistent with most mutations being pre-leukemic background events in hematopoietic stem cells.

## Genes & alterations

- [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md) — mutated in 27% of cases; central node co-occurring with [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md) and [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md) and mutually exclusive of transcription-factor fusions.
- [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md) — mutated in 56/200 (28%); part of the activated-signaling category; mutually exclusive of mutations in other tyrosine kinases / RAS-family genes.
- [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md) — among the most significantly mutated DNA-methylation genes; allelic expression bias observed; co-occurs with [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md) and [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md).
- [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md), [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) — recurrent; mutant samples show distinctive CpG methylation gains versus normal CD34+CD38− cells.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — strongly associated with unfavorable / complex cytogenetics; mutually exclusive of [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md) and [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md); allelic expression bias observed.
- [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md) — recurrent driver; mutually exclusive of [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md) and [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md); allelic expression bias observed.
- [CEBPA](../genes/CEBPA.md) — recurrent; partner in the mutual-exclusivity set with transcription-factor fusions, [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md), [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md).
- [TET2](../genes/TET2.md) — DNA-methylation-related driver category.
- [KIT](../genes/KIT.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md), [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md) — non-FLT3 activated signaling genes contributing to the 59% signaling-pathway prevalence.
- [ASXL1](../genes/ASXL1.md) — anchor of the third mutual-exclusivity set (vs cohesin and other epigenetic modifiers).
- [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md), [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md), [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) — chromatin-modifying-gene category.
- [U2AF1](../genes/U2AF1.md), [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md), [SRSF2](../genes/SRSF2.md) — spliceosome-complex genes (14% prevalence).
- [SMC1A](../genes/SMC1A.md), [SMC3](../genes/SMC3.md), [RAD21](../genes/RAD21.md), [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md) — cohesin-complex genes (13% prevalence).
- [PHF6](../genes/PHF6.md), [WT1](../genes/WT1.md) — additional recurrent drivers; [PHF6](../genes/PHF6.md) showed allelic expression bias.
- [PML](../genes/PML.md)–[RARA](../genes/RARA.md), [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md)–[RUNX1T1](../genes/RUNX1T1.md), [MYH11](../genes/MYH11.md)–[CBFB](../genes/CBFB.md) — favorable-risk transcription-factor fusions; mutually exclusive with [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md) and [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md).
- [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) (MLL) fusion partners observed: [MLLT3](../genes/MLLT3.md) (AF9), [MLLT10](../genes/MLLT10.md) (AF10), and others; MLL-fused samples carried the fewest cooperating mutations.
- [NUP98](../genes/NUP98.md)–[NSD1](../genes/NSD1.md), [PICALM](../genes/PICALM.md)–[MLLT10](../genes/MLLT10.md), [BCR](../genes/BCR.md)–[ABL1](../genes/ABL1.md) — additional recurrent in-frame fusions detected by RNA-seq.

## Clinical implications

- The concurrent [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md) + [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md) + [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md) genotype, combined with distinct mRNA, miRNA, and CpG-sparse methylation signatures, may define a novel intermediate-risk AML subtype warranting separate classification [PMID:23634996](../papers/23634996.md).
- The authors argue current classification schemes (which already incorporate [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md), [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md), [CEBPA](../genes/CEBPA.md), and [KIT](../genes/KIT.md)) are insufficient and that newer markers such as [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md), [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md)/[IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md), and [TET2](../genes/TET2.md) add prognostic information for intermediate-risk patients.
- The 23 significantly mutated genes and the nine functional categories provide a foundation for refined molecular classification of AML.
- The full dataset was released through the TCGA data portal as a public resource for risk stratification and pathogenesis studies ([laml_tcga_pub](../datasets/laml_tcga_pub.md)).

## Limitations & open questions

- Whole-genome sequencing mean coverage was 30.54×, limiting detection of subclones with VAF <10%; exome at 167.50× modestly improved sub-clonal detection but did not significantly increase tier-1 mutation yield (14.5 WGS vs 12.7 exome per sample, P=0.17).
- Many of the 260 genes mutated in ≥2 samples have unclear pathophysiologic relevance and require functional validation, especially fusion partners observed in only one sample.
- The biological significance of strong methylation differences in CpG-sparse genomic regions (rather than CpG islands) is not yet understood.
- Cohort-size limits some subgroup comparisons (e.g., MLL-fusion vs PML-RARA mutation-count differences) that the authors note will need confirmation in larger series.
- Only 4% of samples had miRNA mutations, but the pathogenic impact of mutated miR-142 seed sequences remains to be functionally proven.
- Tier 2/3 non-coding and mitochondrial variants were catalogued but their pathogenic relevance is unclear.
