**PMID:** 34534465 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.cell.2021.08.023 · **Journal:** Cell (2021)

## TL;DR

The CPTAC consortium performed integrated proteogenomic characterization of 140 treatment-naive pancreatic cancers (135 PDACs + 5 adenosquamous), 67 paired normal adjacent tissues (NATs), and 9 macrodissected normal pancreatic ductal tissues using WES, WGS, RNA-seq, miRNA-seq, DNA methylation, TMT proteomics, phosphoproteomics, and glycoproteomics. [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) alterations were detected in 97% of tumors. After filtering to 105 tumors with sufficient neoplastic cellularity (KRAS VAF ≥ 0.075), the authors mapped genomic alterations to functional protein/phospho/glyco effects, identified candidate early-detection biomarkers and glycoprotein/kinase therapeutic targets, characterized an "immune-cold" subtype driven by endothelial remodeling and glycolysis, and defined two proteogenomic subtypes (C1 classical, C2 basal-like) with stronger prognostic separation than the Moffitt RNA-only subtypes (CoxPH HR 3.4 vs 2.3).

## Cohort & data

- **140 treatment-naive pancreatic tumors** (135 PDAC + 5 pancreatic adenosquamous carcinomas; [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md), [PAASC](../cancer_types/PAASC.md)), 67 paired NATs, and 9 normal macrodissected pancreatic ductal tissues; cohort = [paad_cptac_2021](../datasets/paad_cptac_2021.md).
- Prospectively collected from multiple international source sites with controlled ischemia time; 75% of resected tumors from pancreatic head; 59% Stage I–II, 9 Stage IV; 42% alive at data freeze. Risk-factor prevalence: smoking 37%, chronic pancreatitis 22%, obesity 11%, type II diabetes 28%.
- **8 omics layers** on the same tissue portion: [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md), [whole-genome-seq](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md), [rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md), [mirna-seq](../methods/mirna-seq.md), [dna-methylation-array](../methods/dna-methylation-array.md), [tmt-proteomics](../methods/tmt-proteomics.md), [tmt-phosphoproteomics](../methods/tmt-phosphoproteomics.md), and N/O-linked glycoproteomics (via [lc-ms-ms-proteomics](../methods/lc-ms-ms-proteomics.md)).
- Quantification yields: 28,057 genes (RNA), 2,416 miRNAs, 850,000 CpG sites, 11,662 proteins (8,781/sample), 51,469 phosphosites (25,764/sample), 34,024 glycopeptides (30,660 N-linked + 3,364 O-linked).
- Subtyping/deconvolution used [xcell](../methods/xcell.md), DNA-methylation deconvolution, [nmf-clustering](../methods/nmf-clustering.md), and [gsea](../methods/gsea.md).

## Key findings

- **KRAS landscape.** [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) alterations in 97% of 140 tumors (96% hotspot mutations + 1 amplification); G12D, G12V, and G12R were the dominant hotspots. [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) altered in 83%, [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) in 48% (CNV-driven; more focal deletions than intragenic mutations), [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) in 29% of the 105 high-cellularity tumors.
- **Additional SMGs (>5% of tumors):** [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [RNF43](../genes/RNF43.md), [GNAS](../genes/GNAS.md), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), [TGFBR2](../genes/TGFBR2.md), [RBM10](../genes/RBM10.md).
- **Cellularity gating.** 105/140 samples passed neoplastic-purity QC (KRAS VAF ≥ 0.075, equivalent to ≥15% neoplastic cellularity, or significant mutation burden + CNVs); 4 KRAS-WT tumors were retained based on other SMG alterations. KRAS VAF correlated with DNA-methylation deconvolution at Spearman r = 0.81.
- **RNA-protein correlation** median 0.35 across tumors (0.36 tumor-only, 0.26 NAT-only), consistent with prior CPTAC tissue types.
- **Genomic-to-proteomic trans-effects.** [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations had the largest trans-effect footprint, with elevated phosphorylation of DNA-damage-repair proteins ([MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md), TP53, [TP53BP1](../genes/TP53BP1.md)) and the proliferation marker MKI67-S1376. [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) mutations down-regulated [SERPINE1](../genes/SERPINE1.md) RNA/protein and up-regulated [MAPK3](../genes/MAPK3.md) + E2F4 phosphorylation. [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) deletions raised phospho-HDAC1 (S406/S410/S421), phospho-[SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) S613, and phospho-CREBBP S274; [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) deletions raised phospho-[NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md) S70/S214. [RBM10](../genes/RBM10.md) mutations showed a cis-effect increasing cognate mRNA/protein/phospho levels.
- **Epigenetics.** 86 epigenetically silenced genes identified (22 also reported by TCGA); two (ZNF544, THNSL2) silenced in >10% of tumors and associated with patient survival. Two methylation clusters (M1, M2) identified.
- **Early-detection candidates.** 27 proteins remained > 2-fold up-regulated in PDAC vs NAT after stromal/immune adjustment; 21 also > 2-fold up vs normal ductal tissue and similarly elevated in early-stage tumors. 12 are secreted (serum/pancreatic-juice candidates). Examples include [THBS2](../genes/THBS2.md), LAMC2, [LOXL2](../genes/LOXL2.md) (associated with shorter overall survival), [HK2](../genes/HK2.md), COL12A1, C19orf33, TSPAN1, MDK, SDR16C5, ANKRD22; the authors are first to report elevated [GSDMB](../genes/GSDMB.md) and [LECT2](../genes/LECT2.md) in PDAC. 14/21 validated by orthogonal DIA mass spectrometry.
- **Glycoproteomics.** 75 N-linked glycoproteins upregulated > 2-fold in tumors (48/75 DIA-validated); 18 were newly identified. Mucin-type O-linked glycoproteins [MUC1](../genes/MUC1.md), [MUC3A](../genes/MUC3A.md), [MUC5AC](../genes/MUC5AC.md), MUC5B, [MUC13](../genes/MUC13.md), [MUC16](../genes/MUC16.md) were significantly up-regulated, including in early-stage tumors. [CEACAM5](../genes/CEACAM5.md) and [CEACAM6](../genes/CEACAM6.md) were upregulated in tumors with KRAS G12D, G12V, and Q61H — but not G12R — mutations.
- **Glycosylation enzyme dysregulation.** [ST6GAL1](../genes/ST6GAL1.md), [ST3GAL1](../genes/ST3GAL1.md), [FUT3](../genes/FUT3.md), [FUT11](../genes/FUT11.md), [B4GALT1](../genes/B4GALT1.md), [B4GALT4](../genes/B4GALT4.md), B3GALT5, ST6GALNAC1 were elevated in PDAC vs NAT; FUT3/FUT11/B4GALT1/B4GALT4 validated by DIA. ST6GAL1/ST3GAL1/B4GALT1 elevation was protein-specific (not seen at the transcript level), supporting sialyl/fucosyltransferase inhibition as a therapeutic hypothesis.
- **Kinase-substrate signaling.** Five kinase-substrate axes with FDA-approved or investigational inhibitors were identified across 41 tumor/NAT pairs: [CDK7](../genes/CDK7.md)–[MCM2](../genes/MCM2.md), [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md)–[FLNA](../genes/FLNA.md), [PAK1](../genes/PAK1.md)–[BAD](../genes/BAD.md) (S134), [PAK2](../genes/PAK2.md)–[MAPK6](../genes/MAPK6.md) (S189), [SRC](../genes/SRC.md)–[STAT3](../genes/STAT3.md). [PAK1](../genes/PAK1.md) elevated in >70% and [PAK2](../genes/PAK2.md) in ~90% of tumors; concordant up-regulation of [MET](../genes/MET.md) with [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [RAC1](../genes/RAC1.md), PAK1, PAK2 was observed. 19 kinases stratified by KRAS hotspot, including 7 FDA-approved drug targets.
- **Immune subtyping (all 140 samples).** xCell-based deconvolution into 4 clusters (A–D). Cluster D = "immune hot" (high CD8+ T-cell infiltration, cytotoxic enzymes, immune checkpoint molecules; only ~6% of tumors, with one case explained by lymph-node contamination). Clusters B and C are "immune-cold" tumors (cluster A enriched with non-neoplastic acinar/islet cells). Immune-hot tumors were also enriched for endothelial cells; immune-cold tumors had reduced endothelial-adhesion proteins, elevated VEGF and hypoxia pathway activity, increased glycolysis (GAPDH, PGAM1, ENO1 phosphorylation), and elevated phosphorylation of cell-junction proteins.
- **Proteogenomic subtyping (105 high-purity tumors).** NMF on CNV + mRNA + protein + phosphosite + glycosite revealed two clusters: C1 (proteogenomic classical; pancreas beta-cell, bile-acid/fatty-acid metabolism, KRAS-signaling suppression) and C2 (proteogenomic basal-like; EMT, DNA repair, glycolysis, hypoxia, apoptosis, ROS, proliferation/signaling). Subtypes overlap Moffitt classical/basal-like but reclassify 22/97 tumors. **Proteogenomic-dichotomized subtypes had stronger prognostic separation than Moffitt RNA-only (CoxPH HR 3.4 vs 2.3).** C1 enriched for SMAD4 mutations; C2 enriched for higher MTOR/MAP3K2/MAP4K4/MET expression and TGF-β signaling. DSigDB GSEA associated chemotherapeutic-drug signatures ([docetaxel](../drugs/docetaxel.md), [vinblastine](../drugs/vinblastine.md), cabazitaxel) with C1 and kinase-inhibitor signatures (PP-242/mTOR, CP466722/ATM-ATR, sunitinib/PDGFR-VEGFR) with C2.
- **Survival associations.** CD8+ T-cell infiltration was favorable; elevated VEGF and hypoxia pathway signaling both associated with decreased survival. C1 correlated with longer survival and tumor-free status; C2 correlated with tumor necrosis and Stage IV status (persisted after excluding Stage IV tumors). 392 proteins and 258 phosphosites carried significant prognostic value (CoxPH adj. p < 0.15).

## Genes & alterations

- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — 97% prevalence in this PDAC cohort; G12D/G12V/G12R hotspot dominance; G12C present in <1% of PDACs (relevant to [adagrasib](../drugs/adagrasib.md)/MRTX849 and [sotorasib](../drugs/sotorasib.md) targeting). Drives [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) and PAK1/PAK2 signaling axes.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — altered in 83% of high-purity tumors; missense mutations show cis-effect on TP53 protein and TP53-S315 phosphosite; both missense and truncating alterations show trans-effects on MKI67-S1376, MSH6-S219, TP53BP1-S321.
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — altered in 48% (CNV-driven focal deletions > intragenic mutations); deletions raise phospho-NPM1 S70/S214.
- [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) — altered in 29% (more frequent in C1 subtype); downregulates [SERPINE1](../genes/SERPINE1.md) mRNA/protein; deletions raise phospho-HDAC1 (S406/S410/S421), phospho-SMARCA4 S613, phospho-CREBBP S274.
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [RNF43](../genes/RNF43.md), [GNAS](../genes/GNAS.md), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), [TGFBR2](../genes/TGFBR2.md), [RBM10](../genes/RBM10.md) — each altered in ≥5% of tumors.
- [GATA6](../genes/GATA6.md) — focal amplification identified among arm-level/focal CNVs (9p, 11q, 18q, 22q amps; [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) del).
- [PAK1](../genes/PAK1.md) / [PAK2](../genes/PAK2.md) — elevated in >70% and ~90% of tumors respectively; downstream effectors of KRAS via [RAC1](../genes/RAC1.md); combined PAK1/2 + MAPK-ERK/PI3K-AKT-mTOR inhibition proposed.
- [MET](../genes/MET.md) — concordantly upregulated with KRAS/RAC1/PAK1/PAK2 at RNA and protein levels.
- [CEACAM5](../genes/CEACAM5.md), [CEACAM6](../genes/CEACAM6.md) — upregulated specifically in KRAS G12D/G12V/Q61H tumors (not G12R); [CEACAM6](../genes/CEACAM6.md) overexpression linked to [gemcitabine](../drugs/gemcitabine.md) resistance and low cytolytic T-cell activity.
- [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) — referenced as germline-mutation context for PARP-inhibitor therapy in PDAC.

## Clinical implications

- **Early detection biomarkers.** 12 secreted proteins (subset of the 21 PDAC-vs-normal up-regulated set) are candidate serum/pancreatic-juice early-detection markers, including [THBS2](../genes/THBS2.md), LAMC2, [LOXL2](../genes/LOXL2.md), [GSDMB](../genes/GSDMB.md), [LECT2](../genes/LECT2.md). 14/21 validated by DIA mass spectrometry. Mucin glycoproteins ([MUC1](../genes/MUC1.md), [MUC5AC](../genes/MUC5AC.md), [MUC13](../genes/MUC13.md)) and [LGALS3BP](../genes/LGALS3BP.md) enriched even in early-stage tumors.
- **KRAS hotspot-stratified therapy.** Anti-CEACAM5/6 monoclonal antibodies (e.g., preclinical MN-15, MN-3) coupled with first-line chemotherapy proposed for KRAS G12D/G12V/Q61H tumors based on selective CEACAM5/6 upregulation in these hotspots.
- **KRAS-downstream targeting.** [adagrasib](../drugs/adagrasib.md) (MRTX849) and analogous KRAS-G12C inhibitors apply to <1% of PDACs; for the broader cohort the authors propose combined PAK1/PAK2 inhibition with MAPK-ERK and PI3K-AKT-mTOR inhibitors as a strategy to target KRAS-driven signaling.
- **Glycosylation-enzyme inhibition.** Sialyl-/fucosyltransferase inhibition (targeting [ST6GAL1](../genes/ST6GAL1.md), [ST3GAL1](../genes/ST3GAL1.md), [FUT3](../genes/FUT3.md), [FUT11](../genes/FUT11.md), [B4GALT1](../genes/B4GALT1.md)) proposed as therapeutic strategy; FUT3 upregulation in C1 subtype suggests subtype-tailored glycan-capping blockade. Authors caution that systemic sialylation blockade (e.g., 3F-NeuAc) caused liver/kidney toxicity in mouse models.
- **Immune-cold biology.** Antiangiogenic therapy ([sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md), NGR-TNF) and HIF-1 inhibition proposed to normalize endothelial cells and promote immune infiltration in immune-cold PDACs. Immune checkpoint blockade remains effective only for MSI-H tumors (<2% of PDACs).
- **Subtype-directed treatment.** [sunitinib](../drugs/sunitinib.md) (PDGFR/VEGFR) and PP-242/mTOR-axis inhibitors enriched in C2 (basal-like) drug signatures; chemotherapeutics ([docetaxel](../drugs/docetaxel.md), [vinblastine](../drugs/vinblastine.md), cabazitaxel) enriched in C1 (classical) drug signatures.
- **Prognosis.** Proteogenomic subtyping refines Moffitt classical/basal-like assignments and yields stronger prognostic separation (HR 3.4 vs 2.3). [LOXL2](../genes/LOXL2.md) protein abundance associated with shorter [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md); an APOD N-linked glycosite, two PIGR phosphosites, and an [ERRFI1](../genes/ERRFI1.md) phosphosite carry prognostic signal independent of protein abundance.

## Limitations & open questions

- All tissues are **treatment-naive and surgically resected** (CPTAC protocol), limiting extrapolation to metastatic disease treated with systemic therapy; adjuvant regimens were non-standardized across institutions.
- Findings are **correlative** — causal effects cannot be inferred without cell-line, PDX, or clinical-trial validation; the tumors profiled were not used to generate PDX models.
- **Bulk-tissue** measurements; heterogeneity in cellularity and microenvironment partially addressed by KRAS-VAF/methylation/RNA-based purity filtering and by including 9 normal ductal macrodissections, but LCM or single-cell follow-up would be beneficial.
- One immune-hot case (C3N-00303) was driven by inadvertent lymph-node inclusion, underscoring the necessity of histologic review.
- Therapeutic hypotheses for sialyl-/fucosyltransferase inhibition face on-target toxicity concerns based on prior mouse work; selective inhibitors not yet developed.
- Clinical-trial data linking proteogenomic subtypes to treatment outcomes remain limited because most trials generate only transcriptomic data.
