**PMID:** 35292802 · **DOI:** 10.1158/2159-8290.CD-21-1136 · **Journal:** Cancer Discovery (2022)

## TL;DR

MAPPYACTS ([NCT02613962](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT02613962)) is a European prospective precision-medicine trial that performed on-purpose tumor sampling plus WES and RNA-seq in 787 pediatric and young-adult patients with recurrent/refractory malignancies enrolled across France, Italy, Ireland, and Spain (Feb 2016–Jul 2020). Among 624 patients with successful tumor sequencing, 69% (432/624) had at least one "potentially actionable" alteration; 10% (44/432) carried an alteration deemed "ready for routine use." Of 356 patients with ≥12 months of follow-up, 107 (30%) received ≥1 matched targeted therapy — 56% within phase I/II trials, mostly the AcSé-ESMART platform ([NCT02813135](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT02813135)). Overall ORR for matched therapy was 17%, rising to 38% for ready-for-routine-use alterations. In an ancillary cfDNA arm, plasma WES captured 76% of actionable alterations seen in matched extracerebral solid-tumor tissue. Data are deposited at EGA under accession `EGAS00001005935` and the cBioPortal study is [pancan_mappyacts_2022](../datasets/pancan_mappyacts_2022.md).

## Cohort & data

- **Enrollment:** 791 patients consented, 787 included, 774 underwent ≥1 procedure (833 procedures total) — median age 11.6 years (range 0.5–38.5), 59% male, median time since initial diagnosis 1.8 years, median first relapse (range 1–10) — [PMID:35292802](../papers/35292802.md).
- **Tumor distribution (n=774):** sarcomas 290 (37%), CNS tumors 216 (28%), other solid tumors 181 (23%), leukemia 54 (7%), lymphoma 33 (4%) — covering [RMS](../cancer_types/RMS.md) (alveolar [ARMS](../cancer_types/ARMS.md) / embryonal [ERMS](../cancer_types/ERMS.md)), osteosarcoma [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md), Ewing sarcoma [ES](../cancer_types/ES.md), [DSRCT](../cancer_types/DSRCT.md), [MPNST](../cancer_types/MPNST.md), synovial sarcoma [SS](../cancer_types/SS.md), rhabdoid tumor [MRT](../cancer_types/MRT.md), neuroblastoma [NBL](../cancer_types/NBL.md), [Wilms tumor](../cancer_types/WT.md), hepatoblastoma [LIHB](../cancer_types/LIHB.md), high-grade glioma [HGGNOS](../cancer_types/HGGNOS.md), low-grade glioma [LGGNOS](../cancer_types/LGGNOS.md), medulloblastoma [MBL](../cancer_types/MBL.md), ependymoma [EPM](../cancer_types/EPM.md), [ATRT](../cancer_types/ATRT.md), [ALCL](../cancer_types/ALCL.md), B-ALL [BLLNOS](../cancer_types/BLLNOS.md), T-ALL [TLL](../cancer_types/TLL.md), and [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md).
- **Sample acquisition:** biopsy in 55% of patients; on-purpose for MAPPYACTS in 62% of solid-tumor/lymphoma procedures; 53% from metastatic sites. 67 procedure-related AEs across 55 procedures (6% of on-purpose, 9% of therapeutic/diagnostic); grade 3/4 in 19/833 cases.
- **Sequencing pipeline:** paired tumor/germline [WES](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) (Agilent SureSelect V5 50 Mb, Clinical Research Exome 54 Mb, SureSelect XT All Exon CRE v1/v2, or Twist Human Core Exome; 100× mean depth, hg19/GRCh37) plus [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) (TruSeq Stranded mRNA, fusion calling via in-house metacaller); panel sequencing in 3 cases of low cellularity. SNVs/indels called with GATK3 + samtools + Varscan; CNAs and structural variants from in-house pipelines; ANNOVAR annotation; dbNSFP30a functional prediction.
- **Sample yield:** 695/833 samples (84%) had sequencing performed (138 excluded mostly for <30% tumor cellularity); 632 samples / 624 patients (91%) successfully sequenced — 547 WES+RNA-seq, 81 WES only, 1 RNA-seq only, 2 RNA-seq + panel, 1 panel only.
- **cfDNA ancillary arm:** plasma WES at 100× attempted in 234 extracerebral solid-tumor patients (of first 500 enrolled); 225 analyzable, 128/190 (67%) with paired-tumor WES success — see [cfdna-wes](../methods/cfdna-wes.md).
- **Tumor board:** weekly molecular tumor board (MTB) → clinical molecular tumor board ([CMTB](../methods/mappyacts-cmtb.md)) with treating physician using an ESCAT-adapted pediatric actionability scale ("ready for routine use" / "investigational" / "hypothetical" / "resistance").
- **Data sharing:** EGA accession `EGAS00001005935`; cBioPortal study [pancan_mappyacts_2022](../datasets/pancan_mappyacts_2022.md).

## Key findings

- **Actionable yield:** 1,144 "potentially actionable" findings across 436/632 samples (69%) in 432/624 patients (69%) — 533 SNVs (484 somatic, 49 germline), 527 focal CNAs (212 amplifications/high-level gains, 315 deletions), 59 targetable gene fusions, 25 elevated TMB.
- **Ready-for-routine-use alterations (10% of actionable patients, 4% of all detected alterations; n=44 patients):** gene fusions in [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) (n=10), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) (n=14 — combining fusions and [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E mutations), [NTRK1/2/3](../genes/NTRK1.md) (n=9), [ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md) (n=2), [COL1A1](../genes/COL1A1.md)/[PDGFB](../genes/PDGFB.md) (n=1), and [RET](../genes/RET.md) (n=1); pathogenic mutations in [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) (n=4), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) (n=2), and [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) (n=1); mutation/deletion in [PTCH1](../genes/PTCH1.md) (n=4) in selected tumor types. 19/44 (42%) had been previously unknown to the patient's clinical workup.
- **Treatment recommendations (median 2/patient, range 1–7):** most-recommended drug classes were [WEE1](../genes/WEE1.md) inhibitors (n=150), [mTOR](../genes/MTOR.md) (n=123), [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md)/[CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md) (n=105), MEK (n=95), PARP (n=64), BET (n=59), [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) (n=38), FGFR (n=31), and PD1/PD-L1 (n=31).
- **Evidence-level distribution of 1,144 actionable events:** "ready for routine use" 4%, "investigational" 80%, "hypothetical" 16%.
- **Recurrent investigational alterations:** [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md)/[MYC](../genes/MYC.md) amplification, fusion, or activating mutation (n=43); RAS family ([KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)/[NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md)/[HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md)) n=42; [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md)/[FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md)/[FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)/[FGFR4](../genes/FGFR4.md) n=27; [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)/[PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md) n=23; [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) n=15; deletions/deleterious mutations in [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) n=215, [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)/[CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md) n=157, [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) n=33, [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md)/[SMARCB1](../genes/SMARCB1.md) n=27, [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) n=25, [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) n=11.
- **Resistance mutations:** 10 patients with putative resistance mutations in [NTRK1](../genes/NTRK1.md)/[NTRK2](../genes/NTRK2.md)/[NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md) (n=3), [SMO](../genes/SMO.md) (n=3), [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) (n=3), [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) (n=1) — 9/10 had previously received the specific targeted agent.
- **Diagnostic impact:** initial diagnosis revised in 12/624 patients (1.9%); 8 of those revisions driven by specific gene fusion detection. Genetic counseling recommended for 51/674 patients (7.6%) with a potentially relevant germline finding.
- **Follow-up & treatment delivery:** 356/432 patients with ≥12 months follow-up; 107 (30%) received 122 matched targeted therapies (range 1–4 per patient). 14/122 (11%) therapies were "ready for routine use," 97 (80%) "investigational," 11 (9%) "hypothetical." 64/122 (52%) given as single agent, 45 (37%) combined with chemotherapy, 13 (11%) with another targeted therapy. 68/122 (56%) administered within phase I/II trials; 49/68 (72%) within AcSé-ESMART.
- **Reasons for not receiving matched therapy (n=249 patients):** death prior to CMTB (n=24, 10%) or within 3 months after (n=44, 18%), other non-matched therapy (n=92, 37%), effective ongoing therapy (n=35, 14%).
- **Response outcomes (n=109 evaluable patients on matched therapy):** ORR 17% (18 PR; 95% CI 10–25%), SD 25% (n=27), DCR 41% (95% CI 32–51%). Median treatment duration for the 45 patients with disease control was 129 days (range 58–697). ORR by evidence tier: ready-for-routine-use 38% (5/13; 95% CI 18–65%, all single-agent); investigational 14% (12/86); hypothetical 10% (1/10). By combination: matched-targeted + chemotherapy ORR 18% (8/44), single-agent matched 13% (5/39), targeted-targeted combinations 0% (0/13). When patients who had received their high-evidence match prior to MAPPYACTS inclusion are included (12 evaluable, 7 PR + 2 CR), global ready-for-routine-use ORR was 56% (95% CI 37–73%).
- **Tumor mutational burden:** median 0.6 mut/Mb (range 0–195.3) across the first 451 enrolled solid-tumor patients (452 WES samples). 7 patients with TMB >10 mut/Mb (3 high-grade glioma — one >100, 3 neuroblastoma, 1 medulloblastoma); 3 of those linked to probable constitutional mismatch repair deficiency involving [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md), and [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md); 5 tumors showed microsatellite instability.
- **cfDNA / ctDNA findings:** cfDNA quantity differed significantly across cancer types (ANOVA P=1.4e−03), highest in neuroblastoma vs. other tumors (Wilcoxon P=3e−04). ctDNA fraction by Facets also highest in neuroblastoma — median 35% (range 0–95%) vs. 16% (0–86%) in other diseases (Wilcoxon P=1.4e−08). cfDNA WES succeeded in 128/190 (67%) of patients with successful paired tumor WES; success was higher in metastatic vs. localized disease (66% vs. 47%, χ² P=0.04). 71/94 (76%) potentially actionable SNVs in tumor were also detected in plasma; 14 patients had 35 additional actionable SNVs detected by cfDNA WES that were not seen in tumor (median 1 per patient, range 1–13), including an [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) p.Arg1275Gln seen only in plasma. Across all SNVs in successful pairs: 57% shared, 31% tumor-only, 11% plasma-only.
- **Salvage cfDNA in failed tumor WES:** cfDNA WES succeeded in 11/35 patients (34%) where tumor WES was not done or failed, identifying 12 SNVs in actionable genes in 5 patients.
- **Germline / cancer predisposition:** 7%–12% of pediatric patients harbored germline cancer-predisposition findings consistent with prior reports (matching Zhang et al. 2015 N Engl J Med predisposition gene list).
- **PDX resource:** 9 participating laboratories established 131 patient-derived xenografts over the 4-year trial; shared with the IMI2 ITCC-P4 project.

## Genes & alterations

- **[ALK](../genes/ALK.md)** — fusions (n=10) ready-for-routine-use across [ALCL](../cancer_types/ALCL.md) ([ALK](../genes/ALK.md)–NPM1/TFG/TRAF1) and other tumors; activating mutations (n=15) investigational in [NBL](../cancer_types/NBL.md); a p.Arg1275Gln seen only in plasma by cfDNA WES; 3 resistance mutations after prior ALK inhibitor exposure.
- **[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)** — V600E mutations (n=4 in non-midline HGG) and [KIAA1549](../genes/KIAA1549.md)–BRAF fusions (n=10 in low-grade glioma) ready-for-routine-use; targeted by [dabrafenib](../drugs/dabrafenib.md).
- **[NTRK1](../genes/NTRK1.md)/[NTRK2](../genes/NTRK2.md)/[NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md)** — fusions (n=9) ready-for-routine-use across multiple solid tumors (e.g., NTRK1-LMNA, NTRK3-ETV6, NTRK2-TLE4, NTRK3-SPECC1L); 3 resistance mutations after prior TRK inhibitor.
- **[ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md)** — ROS1-GOPC fusions (n=2) ready-for-routine-use in non-RMS soft-tissue sarcoma and low-grade glioma.
- **[RET](../genes/RET.md)** — RET-CCDC6 fusion (n=1) ready-for-routine-use in carcinoma.
- **[COL1A1](../genes/COL1A1.md) / [PDGFB](../genes/PDGFB.md)** — [COL1A1](../genes/COL1A1.md)–[PDGFB](../genes/PDGFB.md) fusion (n=1) ready-for-routine-use, targetable by [imatinib](../drugs/imatinib.md).
- **[IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md)** — R132L mutation (n=1) ready-for-routine-use in [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md), targetable by [ivosidenib](../drugs/ivosidenib.md).
- **[PTCH1](../genes/PTCH1.md) / [SMO](../genes/SMO.md)** — [PTCH1](../genes/PTCH1.md) mutation/deletion (n=4) ready-for-routine-use in selected tumor types (SHH-pathway medulloblastoma); [SMO](../genes/SMO.md) investigational; 3 SMO resistance mutations after prior hedgehog inhibitor.
- **[NF1](../genes/NF1.md)** — pathogenic mutations (n=2) ready-for-routine-use; broader investigational role in [MPNST](../cancer_types/MPNST.md) and low-grade glioma (n=33 deleterious events).
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)** — most frequent driver — 215 deleterious mutations/deletions; recommended for [WEE1](../genes/WEE1.md) inhibitor matching based on ovarian-cancer data (Leijen et al. 2016) and explored in AcSé-ESMART arms in combination with chemotherapy. Three CNS subgroups (HGG, [MBL](../cancer_types/MBL.md), EPN) carried [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations as a top recurrent lesion.
- **[CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) / [CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md)** — 157 deletions/inactivating events across sarcomas, CNS tumors, and leukemia; matched to CDK4/6 inhibitors.
- **[CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) / [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md)** — amplifications particularly in [RMS](../cancer_types/RMS.md) and other sarcomas — 105 CDK4/6 inhibitor recommendations.
- **[MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md) / [MYC](../genes/MYC.md)** — amplifications/fusions/activating mutations (n=43) recurrent in neuroblastoma (with 11q deletion and ALK) and medulloblastoma; matched to BET inhibitors.
- **RAS family — [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md)** — 42 events; matched to MEK inhibitors (n=95 recommendations) such as [selumetinib](../drugs/selumetinib.md). Authors deliberately did **not** classify HRAS/KRAS mutations as ready-for-routine-use given limited clinical activity of MEK inhibitors in pediatric mutant tumors — differing from the INFORM registry classification.
- **[FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md)/[FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md)/[FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)/[FGFR4](../genes/FGFR4.md)** — 27 events across HGG, rhabdomyosarcoma, and other sarcomas.
- **[PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) / [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md) / [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) / [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md)** — 23 PIK3CA/PIK3R1 events plus 25 [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) losses; matched to mTOR inhibitors ([vistusertib](../drugs/vistusertib.md), [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md)) explored in AcSé-ESMART arms E/F.
- **[SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) / [SMARCB1](../genes/SMARCB1.md)** — 27 inactivating events recurrent in rhabdoid tumor (MRT/ATRT) and other CNS tumors; matched to [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) inhibitors.
- **[EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md), [BRD4](../genes/BRD4.md), [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md)** — investigational matches; 38/59/(low frequency) recommendations respectively.
- **[ZFTA](../genes/ZFTA.md) (formerly C11orf95)** — supratentorial ependymoma driver gene fusion partner — 6% of CNS-tumor oncomap alterations.
- **[EWSR1](../genes/EWSR1.md)** — Ewing sarcoma-defining fusions; covered in the sarcoma oncomap.
- **[EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) / [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) / [ERBB3](../genes/ERBB3.md) / [ERBB4](../genes/ERBB4.md)** — 5% of CNS-tumor oncomap alterations; investigational ERBB-inhibitor matches.
- **Germline / DNA repair — [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md)** — 49 germline SNVs across cohort; CMMRD identified in 3 hypermutated patients. Heterozygous chr11q deletions encompassing [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), CHK1, MRE11A, H2AFX considered actionable for PARP inhibitors.
- **Other recurrent (CNS oncomap):** [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md), [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md)/[TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md), [MN1](../genes/MN1.md), [KDR](../genes/KDR.md), [KIT](../genes/KIT.md), [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md).

## Clinical implications

- **Feasibility:** comprehensive paired tumor + germline WES + RNA-seq is feasible at relapse in a multicenter pediatric setting: 91% success on WES, 90% on RNA-seq, manageable procedure safety profile (19/833 procedures with grade 3–4 AEs).
- **Actionability ceiling at relapse:** only ~10% of patients with actionable alterations have a high-evidence ("ready for routine use") match, but those matches yield meaningful clinical benefit — 38% single-agent ORR (and 56% global ORR including pre-MAPPYACTS matches). Authors advocate moving high-evidence alteration testing — ALK/BRAF/NTRK/ROS1/RET fusions, [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) mutations, PTCH1 alterations — into front-line pediatric diagnostics.
- **Half of high-evidence alterations were missed by prior routine diagnostics**, underscoring the need for comprehensive profiling beyond targeted panels.
- **Investigational/hypothetical-tier matches still beat historical chemotherapy** in pediatric phase I/II trials (14% vs. ~4% historical ORR), supporting structured platform trials such as AcSé-ESMART for low-evidence matches.
- **Targeted-targeted combinations underperformed** (0/13 ORR) vs. matched-single-agent (13%) or matched + chemotherapy (18%), suggesting careful prioritization of combinations.
- **Liquid biopsy / cfDNA WES is clinically informative** in extracerebral pediatric solid tumors — recovering 76% of tumor actionable SNVs and capturing additional plasma-only actionable events including a clinically relevant [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) Arg1275Gln mutation. Particularly useful in neuroblastoma (high ctDNA fraction) and when re-biopsy is not feasible (e.g., bone-marrow-only relapses). Deserves prospective validation, addressed in planned MAPPYACTS 2 and integrated into France's PFMG2025.
- **Germline findings in 7–12%** justify integrated genetic counseling pathways for relapsed pediatric patients.
- **Resistance profiling at progression** (10 cases here) supports re-biopsy and re-sequencing at targeted-therapy failure to inform second-generation drug choice.
- **TP53 + [WEE1](../genes/WEE1.md) inhibition** rationale carried into AcSé-ESMART platform combination arms, alongside CDK, PI3K/mTOR, homologous-recombination, and immune-checkpoint targeting arms.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Evolving evidence levels:** classifications were made at the time of each CMTB and will shift with new data; the trial's "investigational" and "hypothetical" tiers carry inherent subjectivity (96% of matches fell into these tiers).
- **Treatment-recommendation lag:** most patients began first-line salvage chemotherapy while awaiting molecular results, so matched treatment was typically administered at a later relapse — temporal tumor evolution may have altered the actionable landscape.
- **cfDNA WES caveats:** 38% sequencing failure rate (often in low-burden disease); restricted largely to SNV detection (CNA threshold 20% vs. SNV 5%); different capture kits between tumor and plasma complicate CNA calling; results were not used to drive treatment decisions in this iteration.
- **Disagreement with INFORM** on ALK and HRAS/KRAS classification — MAPPYACTS withheld these from "ready for routine use" given limited MEK and ALK inhibitor activity in pediatric mutant settings; harmonization across European programs remains an open question.
- **Tumor heterogeneity** between spatial sites and primary vs. metastatic is widely observed but not systematically resolved; paired primary–relapse sequencing comparisons remain to be done.
- **No unique drivers in most cancers** — multiple oncogenic events per hallmark argue for combination strategies and innovative trial designs that current pediatric platforms are not yet built for.
- **Predisposition-gene list dependency:** germline yield (7–12%) varies with the predefined gene panel.
- **Functional gaps:** variants of unknown significance, post-translational and immunologic context remain underexplored.
- **Generalizability:** all patients received salvage tumor sampling at recurrence, so findings may differ from at-diagnosis cohorts (e.g., INFORM, Zero Childhood Cancer, NCI-COG Pediatric MATCH).
- **MAPPYACTS 2** is positioned to address liquid-biopsy validation, health-economic outcomes, and psychosocial impact alongside the French PFMG2025 program.
