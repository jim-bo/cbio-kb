# CDKN1B

## Overview

CDKN1B encodes p27 (KIP1), a cyclin-dependent kinase inhibitor that restrains cell cycle progression at G1 by binding and inhibiting cyclin E–[CDK2](../genes/CDK2.md) and cyclin A–CDK2 complexes. Loss of p27 function promotes unchecked proliferation and is a recognized mechanism of tumor progression, particularly in prostate cancer where it correlates with worse prognosis.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Identified as somatically altered in integrative genomic profiling of prostate cancer (MSKCC cohort) [PMID:20579941](../papers/20579941.md)
- Somatic substitutions in 3/112 samples and deletions in 16; first report of somatic point mutations in this cell cycle regulator in prostate cancer; activated by FOXA1 [PMID:22610119](../papers/22610119.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md)**: Somatic alteration detected in the MSKCC prostate cancer cohort as part of comprehensive genomic characterization [PMID:20579941](../papers/20579941.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- In prostate cancer, CDKN1B loss may co-occur with [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) deletion and [AR](../genes/AR.md) pathway alterations; specific co-mutation patterns in the corpus not yet characterized.

## Therapeutic relevance

- CDK4/6 inhibitors may partially compensate for CDKN1B loss; direct therapeutic targeting of p27 restoration remains investigational.

## Open questions

- Frequency and specific alteration type (deletion vs mutation) of CDKN1B in the MSKCC prostate cohort requires further detail from the source paper.
