# g007-lk

## Overview

G007-LK is a selective tankyrase (TNKS/TNKS2) inhibitor that stabilizes Axin (AXIN1/AXIN2) in the β-catenin destruction complex, thereby suppressing Wnt/β-catenin signaling downstream of receptors. It acts independently of Wnt ligand availability.

## Evidence in the corpus

- In a study of colitis-associated cancers (CAC, n=174 patients), tankyrase inhibition with G007-LK in three CAC patient-derived xenografts (PDXs) did not suppress tumor growth, providing functional confirmation that CAC — including APC-mutant tumors — has undergone transcriptional rewiring away from Wnt/β-catenin pathway dependence. This contrasts with sporadic colorectal cancer, where tankyrase inhibitors such as G007-LK are expected to be active in APC-mutant disease. [PMID:36611031](../papers/36611031.md)

## Resistance mechanisms

- Wnt-pathway–independent growth in CAC: both APC-mutant and Wnt-wild-type CAC organoids and PDXs grow without Wnt pathway activation and are insensitive to tankyrase inhibition, consistent with an alternative, Wnt-low/regenerative transcriptional program. [PMID:36611031](../papers/36611031.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md) — colitis-associated colorectal cancer; G007-LK did not suppress CAC PDX growth; Wnt/β-catenin independence functionally demonstrated.
