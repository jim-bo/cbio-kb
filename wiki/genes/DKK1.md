# DKK1

## Overview

DKK1 (Dickkopf WNT Signaling Pathway Inhibitor 1) encodes a secreted inhibitor of the canonical WNT/β-catenin signaling pathway. In cancer, DKK1 acts as a context-dependent tumor suppressor (inhibiting WNT-driven growth) or a promoter of invasion and immune evasion. In the context of neuroendocrine prostate cancer (CRPC-NE), DKK1 was identified as an EZH2-repressed WNT-pathway target that is significantly downregulated relative to castration-resistant adenocarcinoma.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Transcriptional downregulation of DKK1 in CRPC-NE vs. CRPC-Adeno (P = 0.0002, Wilcoxon test) as part of the PRC2/EZH2 target gene repression program. DKK1 was among the most significantly down-regulated WNT-pathway antagonists in CRPC-NE. [PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) (castration-resistant, neuroendocrine subtype):** DKK1 mRNA significantly lower in CRPC-NE than CRPC-Adeno (P = 0.0002, Wilcoxon); repression linked to [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) overexpression and PRC2 activity in CRPC-NE. Cohort: 114 metastatic biopsies, 81 patients ([nepc_wcm_2016](../datasets/nepc_wcm_2016.md)). [PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- DKK1 downregulation co-occurs with EZH2 overexpression (mRNA 2× higher in CRPC-NE, P < 10⁻⁶) and [NKD1](../genes/NKD1.md) downregulation in the CRPC-NE epigenetic program. EZH2 inhibitor GSK343 preferentially reduced viability of NCI-H660 (NE) cells and partially reversed CRPC-NE gene expression. [PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- DKK1 repression in CRPC-NE is mechanistically downstream of EZH2 overactivity. EZH2 inhibition (GSK343) preferentially killed CRPC-NE cells in vitro and down-regulated CRPC-NE-associated transcripts, suggesting EZH2 inhibitors as a candidate strategy to reverse the epigenetic repression of DKK1 and other WNT antagonists in CRPC-NE. [PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)

## Open questions

- Whether DKK1 repression in CRPC-NE is a causally relevant event promoting Wnt-dependent survival/invasion or a passenger consequence of global EZH2 hyperactivation is not resolved. Functional rescue experiments are needed. [PMID:26855148](../papers/26855148.md)
