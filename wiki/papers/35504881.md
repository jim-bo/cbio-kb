**PMID:** 35504881 · **DOI:** 10.1038/s41467-022-30003-5 · **Journal:** Nature Communications (2022)

## TL;DR

Rodriguez-Calero et al. profiled prostate cancer brain metastases (PCBM) from 51 Swiss patients by multi-region whole-exome sequencing and targeted RNA-seq (168 samples: 105 metastases + 63 primaries, with non-synchronous matched primaries in 20/51). Compared with mCRPC samples from the SU2C/PCF CRPC500 cohort and primary PCa from [prad_tcga](../datasets/prad_tcga.md), PCBM showed significantly elevated mutation burden, enrichment of the COSMIC SBS3 homologous-recombination-deficiency (HRD) signature, and a high prevalence of pathogenic alterations in the 15 [PROfound](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) homologous recombination repair (HRR) genes — 10/51 (19.6%) patients would have met PROfound inclusion criteria and 5/51 (9.8%) carried pathogenic [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md)/[BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) alterations. The authors argue PCBM patients, who were excluded from the [PROfound](../drugs/olaparib.md) trial, may benefit from PARP inhibitors.

## Cohort & data

- 51 patients with [prostate adenocarcinoma](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) brain metastases (PCBM); average age at PCBM diagnosis 71 years.
- 168 tumor regions sequenced: 105 from PCBM (41% parenchymal, 35% dural, 24% mixed/unclear), 63 from matched primary PCa available in 20/51 patients.
- Histology: pure acinar adenocarcinoma in 48/51 (94%) PCBM and 20/20 (100%) primaries; remaining 3/51 had neuroendocrine differentiation (focal NE in 2; small-cell NE areas in 1; intermediate NE/acinar features in 2 patients).
- Clinical exposure: 42/51 (82%) received androgen deprivation or orchiectomy; 11/42 (26%) received next-generation ARSi ([abiraterone](../drugs/abiraterone.md) and/or [enzalutamide](../drugs/enzalutamide.md)).
- Comparator cohorts: [TCGA primary PCa](../datasets/prad_tcga.md) (n=495), CRPC500 non-brain mCRPC ([prad_su2c_2019](../datasets/prad_su2c_2019.md), n=411 metastases).
- Cohort dataset: [prostate_pcbm_swiss_2019](../datasets/prostate_pcbm_swiss_2019.md).
- Assays: [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) via Agilent SureSelectXT V7 on Illumina NovaSeq6000 (2×100 bp; ~258× target); [targeted RNA-seq](../methods/multiregion-exome-seq.md) using a custom Ion AmpliSeq prostate-specific RNA panel for fusion detection; [IHC](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md) for [ERG](../genes/ERG.md), p53, [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), plus Chromogranin-A, Synaptophysin, PSA, CK5/6, p63 in NE-suspected cases.

## Key findings

- **Elevated mutation burden in PCBM.** Mean coding alteration rate 4.43 mut/Mb across 168 samples (range 0.28–50.45); mean 2.98 SNVs/Mb, 0.49 deletions/Mb, 0.15 insertions/Mb. PCBM had significantly more SNVs (q=6.75×10⁻⁶), insertions (q=3.06×10⁻⁵) and deletions (q=9.30×10⁻³) than matched primaries (Wilcoxon).
- **PCBM > non-brain metastases.** PCBM enriched for somatic alterations versus [CRPC500](../datasets/prad_su2c_2019.md): SNVs q=2.38×10⁻¹⁹, insertions q=2.82×10⁻¹⁵, deletions q=8.44×10⁻¹⁴ (Wilcoxon). Differences persisted under stringent VAF/read thresholds and downsampling to match TCGA/CRPC500 depth.
- **HRD signature enrichment.** SBS3 (HRD COSMIC signature) was high in 4 patients (>10% of mutations: P7, P9, P19, P30). By SigMA, HRD signature was significantly more represented in PCBM vs CRPC500 (q=0.041) and in PCBM primaries vs TCGA (q=0.0003, also significant vs high-grade TCGA subset q=0.0034).
- **PROfound criteria fulfilment.** 10/51 (19.6%) patients met PROfound inclusion criteria with pathogenic alterations in one of 15 HRR genes ([BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [BRIP1](../genes/BRIP1.md), [BARD1](../genes/BARD1.md), [CDK12](../genes/CDK12.md), [CHEK1](../genes/CHEK1.md), [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md), [FANCL](../genes/FANCL.md), [PALB2](../genes/PALB2.md), [PPP2R2A](../genes/PPP2R2A.md), [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md), [RAD51C](../genes/RAD51C.md), [RAD51D](../genes/RAD51D.md), [RAD54L](../genes/RAD54L.md)). 8/51 (15.7%) showed biallelic loss; 5/51 (9.8%) had pathogenic [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md)/[BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) specifically. All 51 patients (100%) had at least some alteration (mutation or CNA loss/deletion) in ≥1 PROfound gene.
- **HRR alteration prevalence.** Defining HRR alteration as SigMA-detected HRD signature and/or LOH/deletion/mutation in a PROfound gene, PCBM had 100% vs CRPC500 87.26% affected (P=0.0025, Fisher's exact); trend toward higher HRR alteration in PCBM primaries vs high-grade TCGA (P=0.052).
- **Most-mutated genes in PCBM metastases.** [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) 13/51 (25%), [APC](../genes/APC.md) 8/51 (16%), [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md) 8/51 (16%); [AR](../genes/AR.md) coding alterations 7/51 (14%) and [AR](../genes/AR.md) amplifications 32/51 (63%); [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) loss/deletion 33/51 (64.71%); [TMPRSS2](../genes/TMPRSS2.md)–[ERG](../genes/ERG.md) fusions in 13/51 (25%), correlated with [ERG](../genes/ERG.md) IHC overexpression (Pearson r=0.70, P<2.2×10⁻¹⁶).
- **Hypermutators.** Four patients (P39, P58, P48, P55) exceeded ~15 mut/Mb. P39 and P48 carried [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md) missense/frameshift respectively and were MSI-H by clinical assay (high SBS44 signature). P58 had a [POLD1](../genes/POLD1.md) frameshift plus [RFC4](../genes/RFC4.md) missense; P55 had [RNMT](../genes/RNMT.md) and POL3RC missense without MMR/HRR alterations but high SBS10b.
- **Aging signatures.** SBS1 in 19/51 (37%) and SBS5 in 22/51 (43%) — consistent with median age 71. SBS16 (strand bias of DNA damage / nucleotide excision repair) in 19/51 (37%).
- **Clonal evolution.** In 18/20 matched-primary cases, subclonal primary clones expanded to clonal level (CCF>0.9) in metastases. Phylogenetic trees recovered in 14/20 patients. Detailed examples: patient P4 (dural; truncal mutations in [ZFHX3](../genes/ZFHX3.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), POU2F); P8 (parenchymal; truncal [CDK12](../genes/CDK12.md), [EPHA2](../genes/EPHA2.md), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md)); patient P5 metastases carried an [ASXL2](../genes/ASXL2.md) variant in expanding clones.
- **Site-specific pathway enrichment.** Metastatic-clonal genes were enriched for calcium signaling (including [EP300](../genes/EP300.md) paralog [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md)) and FAT10 signaling ([TCF7L2](../genes/TCF7L2.md), [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md)). Parenchymal metastases additionally enriched for calcium signaling (q=0.013) and EMT regulation in development (q=0.020); dural metastases enriched for aldosterone signaling (q=0.047). Trend toward [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md)-regulated targets in dural and mir17HG/[SIRT1](../genes/SIRT1.md)-regulated targets in parenchymal metastases (both q=0.07).

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — mutated in 13/51 (25%) PCBM metastases; truncal in patient P4.
- [APC](../genes/APC.md) — mutated in 8/51 (16%) of PCBM.
- [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md) — mutated in 8/51 (16%) of PCBM.
- [AR](../genes/AR.md) — coding alterations 7/51 (14%); amplifications 32/51 (63%) — consistent with high prior ADT/ARSi exposure.
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — loss/deletion in 33/51 (64.71%).
- [ERG](../genes/ERG.md) / [TMPRSS2](../genes/TMPRSS2.md) — [TMPRSS2](../genes/TMPRSS2.md)–ERG fusions detected by targeted RNA-seq in 13/51 (25%), highly correlated with ERG IHC (r=0.70, P<2.2×10⁻¹⁶).
- [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) — pathogenic alterations meeting PROfound criteria in 5/51 (9.8%).
- [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [CDK12](../genes/CDK12.md), [BRIP1](../genes/BRIP1.md), [BARD1](../genes/BARD1.md), [CHEK1](../genes/CHEK1.md), [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md), [FANCL](../genes/FANCL.md), [PALB2](../genes/PALB2.md), [PPP2R2A](../genes/PPP2R2A.md), [RAD51B](../genes/RAD51B.md), [RAD51C](../genes/RAD51C.md), [RAD51D](../genes/RAD51D.md), [RAD54L](../genes/RAD54L.md) — together comprise the 15 PROfound HRR genes; 10/51 (19.6%) patients qualified by alteration in any; 8/51 (15.7%) had biallelic loss in at least one.
- [CDK12](../genes/CDK12.md) — truncal in patient P8 (parenchymal metastases).
- [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md) — missense (P39) and frameshift (P48) variants in two MSI-H hypermutator patients with SBS44 enrichment.
- [POLD1](../genes/POLD1.md), [RFC4](../genes/RFC4.md) — patient P58 hypermutator ([POLD1](../genes/POLD1.md) frameshift + [RFC4](../genes/RFC4.md) missense).
- [RNMT](../genes/RNMT.md) — missense in patient P55 (hypermutator, high SBS10b, also POL3RC missense).
- [EPHA2](../genes/EPHA2.md), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md), [ZFHX3](../genes/ZFHX3.md) — truncal in matched primary/metastasis lineage of patients P4/P8 illustrating clonal evolution.
- [ASXL2](../genes/ASXL2.md) — variant in expanding metastatic clones of patient P5.
- [EP300](../genes/EP300.md) — paralog [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md) altered among calcium-signaling enriched metastatic-clonal genes.
- [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) — trend toward dural-metastasis enrichment of EZH2-regulated targets (q=0.07).
- [TCF7L2](../genes/TCF7L2.md), [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) — in FAT10 signaling enrichment among metastatic-clonal genes.
- [SIRT1](../genes/SIRT1.md) — implicated together with mir17HG in dsDNA break repair link to parenchymal HRD enrichment.
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — cited as known mCRPC outcome driver in the background; not the primary focus.

## Clinical implications

- **Biomarker for brain metastasis risk.** HRD signature (SBS3) was significantly higher in PCBM primaries than in TCGA primaries (q=0.0003), even after restricting to high-grade TCGA (q=0.0034). The authors propose the HRD signature may serve as a risk-stratification biomarker for progression of metastatic PCa to brain.
- **PARP inhibitor opportunity.** 10/51 (19.6%) PCBM patients would have met PROfound inclusion criteria; 5/51 (9.8%) had pathogenic [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md)/[BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md). Because PROfound excluded known brain-metastasis patients, this cohort suggests a sizable population that could benefit from [olaparib](../drugs/olaparib.md) but was not eligible. The authors note that PARPi including [olaparib](../drugs/olaparib.md) and [niraparib](../drugs/niraparib.md) have shown activity against brain metastases in HRD breast cancer and in murine models, supporting CNS-permeable PARPi efficacy.
- **Treatment line context.** 11/42 (26%) of ADT-treated patients had received [abiraterone](../drugs/abiraterone.md) and/or [enzalutamide](../drugs/enzalutamide.md) — matching PROfound's post-ARSi setting.
- **MMR testing.** Two MSI-H hypermutator PCBM patients with [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md) alterations were identified, supporting MMR/MSI testing in the PCBM setting (potential ICI eligibility, not explicitly trialed here).

## Limitations & open questions

- **No matched normal in 24/51 patients.** Mutations in unmatched cases were called against a pooled normal panel; the authors note conservative filters and report that enrichment results held when restricted to matched-normal cases.
- **Limited site-comparison power.** The dural vs parenchymal pathway enrichment was based on small subsets (9 dural, 10 parenchymal) and is described as exploratory.
- **Retrospective/archival material.** All samples are FFPE; biopsies vs autopsy material vary across patients, contributing tumor purity/depth variability.
- **No direct PARPi efficacy data in PCBM.** The clinical implication is by analogy to the PROfound trial; the paper does not present prospective treatment outcomes on this cohort.
- **Mechanism of brain tropism unclear.** Calcium signaling, FAT10, EMT, [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md), and mir17HG/SIRT1 are flagged but not functionally validated; epigenetic and microenvironmental factors were not assessed.
- **POU2F annotation.** The paper writes "POU2F" as a truncal mutation for patient P4 without specifying the family member (POU2F1/POU2F2/POU2F3); we did not include POU2F in the validated gene list.
