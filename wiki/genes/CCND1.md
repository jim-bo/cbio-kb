# CCND1

## Overview

CCND1 (Cyclin D1) is a key cell-cycle regulator that activates CDK4 and CDK6 to phosphorylate and inactivate the RB1 tumor suppressor, driving G1-to-S phase progression. Amplification of CCND1 is a recurrent event in several cancers including esophageal adenocarcinoma (EAC), where it contributes to cell cycle pathway dysregulation.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- CCND1 amplification is a major contributor to Cell Cycle pathway enrichment in the perioperative intent to treat (PIT) cohort of esophageal/gastroesophageal junction adenocarcinomas (egc_mskcc_2020, n=487) [PMID:33795256](../papers/33795256.md).
- Focal amplification detected in 6.0% of CSCC tumors; nominated as a driver gene candidate in the CSCC meta-analysis of 88 QC-passed WES/WGS tumors [PMID:34272401](../papers/34272401.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **Esophageal/gastroesophageal junction adenocarcinoma (EAC):** CCND1 amplification contributes to the Cell Cycle pathway signal enriched in advanced-stage PIT patients; no independent OS association reported for CCND1 alone in this cohort [PMID:33795256](../papers/33795256.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with CCNE1 and CDKN2B alterations as part of the Cell Cycle pathway enrichment in EAC [PMID:33795256](../papers/33795256.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Cell Cycle pathway enrichment (including CCND1 amplification) tracks with advanced/perioperatively treated EAC, suggesting potential relevance for CDK4/6 inhibitor strategies, though not directly tested in this cohort [PMID:33795256](../papers/33795256.md).

## Open questions

- Whether CCND1 amplification alone confers independent prognostic value in EAC, separate from the broader Cell Cycle pathway signal, is not established in this dataset [PMID:33795256](../papers/33795256.md).
