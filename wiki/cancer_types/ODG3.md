# Oligodendroglioma, IDH-mutant, and 1p/19q-Codeleted, Grade 3 (ODG3)

## Overview

ODG3 (historically called anaplastic oligodendroglioma, WHO grade 3) is a diffuse glioma defined by [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) or [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) mutation together with 1p/19q codeletion. Under the 2021 WHO Classification, this combination is required for diagnosis; the grade 3 designation indicates mitotic activity or microvascular proliferation without necrosis. Parent OncoTree code: [ODG](ODG.md). It is associated with a relatively favorable prognosis compared to IDH-mutant astrocytomas of equivalent grade, particularly in the 1p/19q-codeleted subset.

## Cohorts in the corpus

- [odg_msk_2017](../datasets/odg_msk_2017.md) — 41-patient multicenter phase II trial cohort (NCT00588523); 19 tumors subjected to NGS with MSK-IMPACT 410-gene panel.

## Recurrent alterations

- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter mutations in 18/19 (95%) sequenced tumors — the most frequent alteration in ODG3, characteristic of the 1p/19q-codeleted lineage. [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md)
- IDH1 R132H in 13/19 (68%); IDH2 R172K in 1/19; defining mutations absent in 1p/19q-intact glioblastoma-like cases. [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md)
- [CIC](../genes/CIC.md) mutations in 10/19 (53%); [FUBP1](../genes/FUBP1.md) in 5/19 (26%) — canonical 19q and 1p drivers respectively. [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md)
- [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) mutated in 6/19 (32%); [NOTCH2](../genes/NOTCH2.md) in 5/19 (26%). [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md)
- [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) amplification in 2/19 (10.5%; OncoKB Level 2B); [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) mutations (E365K, H1047R) in 2/19 (10.5%; OncoKB Level 3B). [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md)
- 1p/19q-intact tumors (glioblastoma-like): [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) amplification/mutation, [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) mutation, [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) amplification, IDH1/2 wild-type — these represent diagnostic misclassification; NGS-confirmed 1p/19q status recommended over FISH alone. [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md)
- In a phase II multicenter trial (NCT00588523, n=41), induction [temozolomide](../drugs/temozolomide.md) followed by high-dose thiotepa/busulfan plus autologous stem-cell transplant achieved 2-year PFS of 86% and 5-year [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) of 100% in transplanted anaplastic oligodendroglioma patients (85% 1p/19q codeleted); NGS (MSK-IMPACT 410-gene panel) in 19 tumors confirmed TERT promoter (95%), IDH1 R132H (68%), CIC (53%), NOTCH1 (32%), FUBP1 (26%) as the dominant alteration landscape. [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md)

## Subtypes

- 1p/19q-codeleted (true ODG3): IDH-mutant, TERT-promoter-mutant, CIC/FUBP1-mutant; favorable prognosis; chemotherapy-alone strategies viable.
- 1p/19q-intact (glioblastoma-like): IDH wild-type, EGFR/NF1/MDM2-driven; progresses early; chemotherapy alone is not appropriate.

## Therapeutic landscape

- Induction [temozolomide](../drugs/temozolomide.md) (6 cycles, 200 mg/m²) followed by myeloablative [thiotepa](../drugs/thiotepa.md)/[busulfan](../drugs/busulfan.md) with autologous stem-cell transplant deferred radiotherapy in 52% of 1p/19q-codeleted patients at 5 years (5-year OS 93.4%); TMZ was non-inferior to prior PCV-induction regimens in cross-trial comparison (HR 3.38 for OS, P=0.005). [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md)
- IDH1/IDH2 inhibitors (OncoKB Level 3B for IDH1 R132H / IDH2 R172K) represent a potential targeted approach. [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md)
