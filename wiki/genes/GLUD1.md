# GLUD1

## Overview

GLUD1 (glutamate dehydrogenase 1) encodes the mitochondrial enzyme that catalyzes the reversible oxidative deamination of glutamate to alpha-ketoglutarate (alpha-KG). GLUD1 sits at the intersection of amino acid catabolism and the TCA cycle and is relevant in IDH-mutant tumors where 2-HG production and disrupted glutamate metabolism interact.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- GLUD1 protein upregulated in IDH1-mutant [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) tumors (Wilcoxon p<0.05; validation p=0.07) in a CPTAC proteogenomic GBM cohort (n=99 tumors); anti-correlates with glutamate levels (Pearson r=-0.29, p<0.01) [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **Glioblastoma (GBM):** GLUD1 protein elevation accompanies [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) R132H mutation; glutamate depletion in IDH1-mutant tumors is consistent with GLUD1 upregulation consuming glutamate for alpha-KG production; 2-HG is the highest-abundance metabolite in these tumors (median log2 FC=3.62, FDR<0.05) [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- GLUD1 upregulation co-occurs with [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) R132H mutation and correlates inversely with glutamate abundance in GBM [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- GLUD1 upregulation in IDH1-mutant GBM reflects altered glutamate metabolism and may contribute to the 2-HG metabolic phenotype; targeting GLUD1 or the IDH1/glutamate axis has not been directly evaluated in this cohort [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Open questions

- Whether GLUD1 upregulation is a direct transcriptional consequence of IDH1 mutation or a compensatory metabolic response to 2-HG accumulation is unresolved; the borderline validation p-value (0.07) warrants confirmation in larger IDH1-mutant GBM cohorts [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).
