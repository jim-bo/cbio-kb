# ACSL4

## Overview

ACSL4 (acyl-CoA synthetase long-chain family member 4) encodes a long-chain fatty acid-CoA ligase that plays a key role in lipid metabolism, particularly the esterification of polyunsaturated fatty acids (PUFAs) into membrane phospholipids. ACSL4 is a critical mediator of ferroptosis, a form of iron-dependent oxidative cell death driven by lipid peroxide accumulation. Its upregulation in cancer subtypes with high oxidative lipid content is a marker of ferroptosis pathway activation.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- In a proteogenomic analysis of 99 treatment-naive GBMs, ACSL4 protein was upregulated exclusively in the mesenchymal-like (nmf2) subtype, suggesting ferroptosis-pathway activation and accumulation of oxidized PUFA-containing phosphatidylethanolamines (PEs) in this [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) subset. [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md):** ACSL4 protein upregulated in mesenchymal-like tumors; associated with ferroptosis pathway activation alongside [ALOX5](../genes/ALOX5.md). [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- ACSL4 upregulation co-occurs with ALOX5 upregulation specifically in the mesenchymal-like GBM subtype (nmf2), marking a ferroptosis-susceptible lipid profile. [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Upregulation of ACSL4 and ALOX5 in mesenchymal-like GBM, together with enrichment of triacylglycerols and depletion of phosphatidylcholines, suggests ferroptosis induction and lipid-metabolism targeting as hypothesis-generating therapeutic strategies for this subtype. [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Open questions

- Whether ACSL4 upregulation in mesenchymal GBM reflects a vulnerability (ferroptosis inducibility) or a resistance mechanism requires preclinical validation. The association is based on proteogenomic profiling without direct functional testing. [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)
