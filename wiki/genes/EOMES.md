# EOMES

## Overview

EOMES (Eomesodermin) encodes a T-box transcription factor involved in brain development and neuronal differentiation. In medulloblastoma, EOMES shows methylation-dependent differential expression across molecular subgroups and is inversely co-expressed with [TBR1](../genes/TBR1.md) in Group 4 tumors, implicating it in subgroup-specific developmental programs.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Inversely expressed with [TBR1](../genes/TBR1.md) in Group 4 medulloblastoma; methylation-dependent differential expression across subgroups identified by WGS/WES (ICGC, 76 tumors) [PMID:22832583](../papers/22832583.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **MB (medulloblastoma):** Subgroup-specific expression pattern — inversely correlated with TBR1 in Group 4; epigenetic (methylation) regulation implicated [PMID:22832583](../papers/22832583.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Inversely expressed with TBR1 in Group 4 medulloblastoma; both are T-box transcription factors with complementary subgroup expression patterns [PMID:22832583](../papers/22832583.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting reported in the corpus; subgroup-specific epigenetic regulation suggests potential relevance for epigenetic therapy strategies in Group 4 medulloblastoma.

## Open questions

- Whether EOMES expression differences are driver or passenger events in Group 4 medulloblastoma remains unresolved.
- Functional consequences of methylation-mediated silencing/activation of EOMES across subgroups not yet characterized.
