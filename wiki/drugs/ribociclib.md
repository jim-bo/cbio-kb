# ribociclib

## Overview

Ribociclib is a CDK4/6 inhibitor that blocks G1-to-S cell cycle progression. It was evaluated in hepatocellular carcinoma ([HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)) in the context of [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) loss and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) deletion, which occur in 4–5% and 2–5% of HCCs respectively.

## Evidence in the corpus

- Mentioned in comprehensive [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) molecular landscape review as a CDK4/6 inhibitor under evaluation in the context of [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) LOF (4%) and homozygous deletion (5%) and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) deletions in HCC [PMID:24798001](../papers/24798001.md).
- CDK4/6 inhibitor; included among the CDK4/6 inhibitor class (with [palbociclib](../drugs/palbociclib.md) and [abemaciclib](../drugs/abemaciclib.md)) studied for resistance mechanisms in HR+/HER2- metastatic breast cancer by Wander et al.; 84.5% of the 58-patient cohort received palbociclib, with ribociclib and abemaciclib as alternatives [PMID:32404308](../papers/32404308.md).
- In CDK6-overexpressing and PTEN-loss ER+ breast cancer xenografts, ribociclib (25 mg/kg PO) shows only transient tumor suppression followed by outgrowth, whereas CDK4/6 bifunctional PROTACs (BSJ-05-017, BSJ-03-096) suppress tumor volume by 47–69%; this highlights ribociclib failure as an in vivo model of INK4-mediated CDK4/6i resistance. [PMID:34544752](../papers/34544752.md)
- Ribociclib (CDK4/6 inhibitor) was among CDK4/6 inhibitor therapies recommended (n=105 recommendations) in MAPPYACTS for CDK4/CDK6 amplifications (particularly in [RMS](../cancer_types/RMS.md) and other sarcomas) and CDKN2A/CDKN2B deletions across pediatric recurrent malignancies [PMID:35292802](../papers/35292802.md)
