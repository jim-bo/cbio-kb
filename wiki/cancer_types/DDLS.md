# Dedifferentiated Liposarcoma (DDLS)

## Overview

Dedifferentiated liposarcoma (DDLS) is a high-grade soft tissue sarcoma characterized by a well-differentiated liposarcoma component alongside an abrupt transition to non-lipogenic, high-grade sarcoma. On OncoTree it is a child of [LIPO](../cancer_types/LIPO.md) (Liposarcoma) under Soft Tissue. DDLS is defined by near-universal chromosome 12q amplification encompassing [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md), [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md), and [YEATS4](../genes/YEATS4.md).

## Cohorts in the corpus

- [sarc_mskcc](../datasets/sarc_mskcc.md): 50 DDLS patients (24.2% of 207 high-grade STS), profiled by targeted resequencing (722 genes), 250K SNP-array SCNA, LOH, and expression arrays. Three DDLS cell lines (LPS141, DDLS8817, FU-DDLS-1) used in functional shRNA screen. [PMID:20601955](../papers/20601955.md)

## Recurrent alterations

- Chromosome 12q amplification in ~90% of DDLS — the highest prevalence SCNA in any subtype across the seven-subtype cohort. [PMID:20601955](../papers/20601955.md)
- [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) — amplified on 12q; functionally validated as a DDLS dependency by shRNA screen (anti-proliferative in ≥2 of 3 cell lines); most overexpressed amplified hit vs. normal fat. Pharmacologic inhibition by [palbociclib](../drugs/palbociclib.md) (PD0332991) induces G1 arrest. [PMID:20601955](../papers/20601955.md)
- [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) — amplified on 12q; sustained shRNA knockdown (>1 week) impairs proliferation; supports cooperative p53-network repression with [YEATS4](../genes/YEATS4.md). [PMID:20601955](../papers/20601955.md)
- [YEATS4](../genes/YEATS4.md) — co-amplified with [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) on 12q; upregulated in amplified vs. copy-neutral tumors; knockdown anti-proliferative; proposed p53-network repressor. [PMID:20601955](../papers/20601955.md)
- Complex karyotype: copy-neutral LOH confined to DDLS and other complex subtypes ([PLLS](../cancer_types/PLLS.md), [LMS](../cancer_types/LMS.md), [MFS](../cancer_types/MFS.md)). [PMID:20601955](../papers/20601955.md)
- Of 385 genes screened by shRNA in three DDLS lines, 99 showed anti-proliferative effects (nominal p<0.05) in ≥1 line; 91/99 confirmed by ≥2 independent shRNAs; 27/99 were amplified in ≥1 line, supporting 12q amplification as a source of oncogene dependencies. [PMID:20601955](../papers/20601955.md)
- DDLPS (n=50) had the highest SCNA frequency of any TCGA tumor type, driven by near-universal 12q13~15 amplification (MDM2 100%, [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) 92%, [HMGA2](../genes/HMGA2.md) 76%); SCNA + methylation sub-clustering defined three DSS-stratified groups (K1 JUN-amplified, K2 TERT-amplified, K3 6q25.1-amplified) with worst outcome in hypermethylated K1+K2 (Meth2 HR=4.4, p=0.002) [PMID:29100075](../papers/29100075.md)
- In a 7,494-sarcoma cohort, MDM2 amplification was present in >94% of well/dedifferentiated liposarcomas and was pathognomonic for this histology; MDM2 overall rate 10.7% across all sarcomas [PMID:35705558](../papers/35705558.md).
- In the 2,138-sarcoma MSK-IMPACT cohort (n=167 DDLS), MDM2/CDK4 co-amplification was present in >97%; beyond this, [GLI1](../genes/GLI1.md) (25.3%), [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) (14.4%), and [JUN](../genes/JUN.md) (13.8%) amplifications were enriched vs [WDLS](../cancer_types/WDLS.md); JUN and GLI1 were mutually exclusive; DDLS had 17 genomic clusters with highest intra-subtype entropy [PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md).

## Subtypes

- Dedifferentiated component may display variable histology (storiform pleomorphic, myxofibrosarcoma-like, or rhabdomyoblastic); prognosis driven by high-grade dedifferentiated component.

## Therapeutic landscape

- [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md)/CDK6 inhibition with [palbociclib](../drugs/palbociclib.md) (PD0332991): pharmacologic G1 arrest in LPS141 and DDLS8817 cell lines; functional validation supports clinical evaluation. [PMID:20601955](../papers/20601955.md)
- [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) antagonists ([nutlin-3a](../drugs/nutlin-3a.md)): co-amplification of MDM2 + YEATS4 provides rationale for p53–MDM2 interaction antagonists in DDLS. [PMID:20601955](../papers/20601955.md)
