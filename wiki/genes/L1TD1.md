# L1TD1

## Overview

L1TD1 (LINE-1 Type Transposase Domain Containing 1) is encoded by an endogenous retrovirus-derived element and is involved in RNA binding and stem cell maintenance. In cancer genomics, L1TD1 has been noted for markedly elevated expression in contexts of global DNA hypomethylation, suggesting reactivation of transposable-element-associated genes.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- L1TD1 — upregulated 5.17-fold (q = 0.039) in SWNTS-SWNs vs NS-SWNs; LINE-1-encoded gene; upregulation coincides with global hypomethylation (TET1/TET2 upregulation, DNMT1/DNMT3A unchanged), implicating passive demethylation as the mechanism of reactivation [PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Schwannomatosis (SWNTS-SWN): L1TD1 transcriptionally upregulated 5.17-fold in SWNTS-SWNs compared to sporadic schwannomas [PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- L1TD1 upregulation co-occurs with TET1/TET2 upregulation and global hypomethylation in SWNTS-SWNs; [DNMT1](../genes/DNMT1.md) and [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md) are unchanged, pointing to passive demethylation as the driver of L1TD1 reactivation [PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic relevance established; L1TD1 upregulation is proposed as a biomarker of the epigenetic landscape in SWNTS-SWNs rather than a therapeutic target [PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)

## Open questions

- Whether L1TD1 reactivation contributes functionally to schwannomatosis pathogenesis or is a passenger consequence of global hypomethylation remains unresolved [PMID:33025139](../papers/33025139.md)
