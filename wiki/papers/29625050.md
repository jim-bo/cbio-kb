**PMID:** 29625050 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.cell.2018.03.035 · **Journal:** Cell (2018)

## TL;DR

Sanchez-Vega et al. integrated somatic mutations, copy-number alterations, mRNA expression, gene fusions and DNA methylation across 9,125 TCGA PanCanAtlas tumors from 33 cancer types (64 molecular subtypes) to map alterations in 10 canonical oncogenic signaling pathways: cell cycle, Hippo, Myc, Notch, NRF2/oxidative stress, PI3K/Akt, RTK-RAS, TGFβ, p53, and WNT/β-catenin. They report that 89% of tumors carry at least one driver alteration in these pathways, 57% have at least one alteration potentially targetable by an existing drug, and 30% have multiple targetable alterations — establishing a quantitative pan-cancer rationale for both pathway-level precision oncology and combination therapy. All alteration calls and curated pathway templates are accessible via cBioPortal (`http://cbioportal.org/pancanatlas`) and PathwayMapper.

## Cohort & data

- **9,125 tumors** drawn from the TCGA PanCanAtlas whitelist (started from 11,276 patients; required all four platforms — mutations, copy number, methylation, mRNA — with QC/pathology filtering).
- **33 cancer types**, stratified into **64 genomically distinct subtypes** per individual TCGA analysis working groups.
- Sample composition: 8,602/9,125 (94%) primary solid tumors; 162/9,125 (2%) blood tumors from the [LAML](../cancer_types/LAML.md) subset; 361/9,125 (4%) metastatic samples from melanoma ([SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md)) patients.
- Cancer types covered include (non-exhaustive): [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md), [LGG](../cancer_types/LGG.md), [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md), [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md), [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md), [READ](../cancer_types/READ.md), [KIRC](../cancer_types/KIRC.md), [KIRP](../cancer_types/KIRP.md), [KICH](../cancer_types/KICH.md), [LIHC](../cancer_types/LIHC.md), [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md), [LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md), [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md), [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md), [STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md), [ESCA](../cancer_types/ESCA.md), [CESC](../cancer_types/CESC.md), [UCEC](../cancer_types/UCEC.md), [UCS](../cancer_types/UCS.md), [OV](../cancer_types/OV.md), [THCA](../cancer_types/THCA.md), [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md), [BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md), [ACC](../cancer_types/ACC.md), [CHOL](../cancer_types/CHOL.md), [MESO](../cancer_types/MESO.md), [LAML](../cancer_types/LAML.md), [PCPG](../cancer_types/PCPG.md), [SARC](../cancer_types/SARC.md), [TGCT](../cancer_types/TGCT.md), [THYM](../cancer_types/THYM.md), [UVM](../cancer_types/UVM.md).
- Corresponding cBioPortal studies (subset present in the wiki): [gbm_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/gbm_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [brca_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/brca_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [coadread_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/coadread_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [luad_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/luad_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [blca_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/blca_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [cesc_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/cesc_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [chol_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/chol_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [laml_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/laml_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [prad_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/prad_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [thca_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/thca_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md).
- Assays: somatic mutations from [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) using the [MC3 MAF v2.8](../methods/mc3.md) call set; copy-number from [Affymetrix SNP6](../methods/affymetrix-snp6.md) arrays; gene expression from [RNA-Seq](../methods/rna-seq.md); DNA methylation from Illumina Infinium HM450 ([hm450-methylation-array](../methods/hm450-methylation-array.md)) arrays.
- Driver-discovery and annotation methods: [MutSigCV](../methods/mutsig.md) for mutation recurrence, [GISTIC 2.0](../methods/gistic.md) for copy-number recurrence (confidence 0.95; thresholds +2 and −2 for amplification/deep deletion), `cancer-hotspots` (2D linear and 3D protein-structure mutational hotspots), [OncoKB](../methods/oncokb.md) for oncogenicity/actionability annotation, and `RESET` for [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) promoter methylation calling (probe cg13601799). Fusions from RNA-Seq via [STAR-Fusion](../methods/star-fusion.md), [EricScript](../methods/ericscript.md) and [BreakFast](../methods/breakfast.md). Mutual-exclusivity / co-occurrence testing via `SELECT`. Pathway templates visualized via `PathwayMapper`.

## Key findings

- **Pan-cancer driver burden:** 89% of 9,125 tumors carry at least one driver alteration in one of the 10 canonical pathways; 57% have at least one *clinically actionable* alteration (including BRCA1/2 and IDH1/2 outside the 10 pathways); 30% carry two or more potentially targetable alterations [PMID:29625050](../papers/29625050.md).
- **RTK-RAS is the most-altered pathway pan-cancer**, with a median frequency of 46%. Tumor subtypes with the highest RTK-RAS alteration rates: [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md) 95%, colorectal GS 88%, [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) Her2-enriched 82%, [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md) 78%, [LGG](../cancer_types/LGG.md) IDHwt 82%, [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) 74%, [THCA](../cancer_types/THCA.md) 84%.
- **PI3K pathway** is dominant in squamous and uterine cancers: [LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md) 68%, EBV+ esophagogastric 80%, non-hypermutated [UCEC](../cancer_types/UCEC.md) CN-high 86% / CN-low 95%.
- **NRF2/oxidative-stress pathway** had the lowest overall alteration rate (4%), peaking in [LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md) (25%) and esophagogastric squamous ([ESCC](../cancer_types/ESCC.md)) (23%).
- **Cell-cycle pathway** is broadly altered but rarely in [UVM](../cancer_types/UVM.md), [THYM](../cancer_types/THYM.md), [TGCT](../cancer_types/TGCT.md), or [LAML](../cancer_types/LAML.md).
- **MSI-H and POLE-mutant subtypes** (GI and uterine) carry both the highest mutation burden and the highest overall pathway alteration frequency, dominated by inactivating mutations.
- **416 oncogenic alterations were tested for co-occurrence/exclusivity via SELECT**, yielding 152 mutually exclusive pairs and 116 co-occurring pairs (Table S5).
- **Within-pathway mutual exclusivity** is strongest in p53, cell cycle, RAS, and PI3K — consistent with one alteration being functionally sufficient.
- **Cross-pathway interactions:** RTK activation is mutually exclusive with downstream RAS or PI3K alterations (because RTKs alone activate both); notable exception is significant co-occurrence between [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md)/[FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) alterations and PI3K-pathway alterations.
- **p53 and cell-cycle pathways are frequently co-altered** — including co-occurrence of [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation with [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md) amplification, [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) mutation, [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) loss, and [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md) or [E2F3](../genes/E2F3.md) amplification. [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation is *mutually exclusive* with [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) deletion (because the CDKN2A locus encodes both p16 and ARF/p53-regulating). [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) amplification is mutually exclusive with [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) loss, but [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) and [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) are almost always co-amplified due to genomic proximity.
- **PI3K–NRF2 axis:** the strongest cross-pathway co-occurrence. [NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md) GoF/amplification co-occurs with [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) amplification and tends to co-occur with [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) mutation and [PIK3CB](../genes/PIK3CB.md) amplification. [NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md) amplification is mutually exclusive with [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) loss, but [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) loss co-occurs with loss-of-function [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md) mutation. Co-occurring NRF2–PI3K alterations are concentrated in lung (squamous and adenocarcinoma), esophagogastric, head and neck, and uterine tumors.
- **[EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) alteration biology:** [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) is involved in more significant mutual-exclusivity pairs than any other gene. EGFR amplification is mutually exclusive with [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) activation and with [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)/[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) mutation and [NF1](../genes/NF1.md)/[RASA1](../genes/RASA1.md) loss. In [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) and IDHwt [LGG](../cancer_types/LGG.md), however, EGFR amplification co-occurs with EGFR mutation/fusion and with focal 4q12 amplification of [KIT](../genes/KIT.md)/[PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md).
- **Top-altered RTK-RAS genes pan-cancer:** [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) 9%, [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) 7%, [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) 4%. [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) hotspots concentrated in [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md) (72%), colorectal GS (69%), [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) (33%). [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) hotspots in [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md) (51%) and [THCA](../cancer_types/THCA.md) (62%). EGFR alterations in [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) (50%), [LGG](../cancer_types/LGG.md) IDHwt (52%), HPV− [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md) (13%), [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) (13%), esophagogastric squamous (14%). [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) alterations cluster in [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md), chromosomally-unstable esophagogastric ([STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md) CIN 26%), and [CESC](../cancer_types/CESC.md) (23%).
- **Novel cancer gene flagged:** recurrent hotspot ([SOS1](../genes/SOS1.md) A90V/T, N233Y/S) and other activating mutations (M269I/V, G434R, R552S/K/G/M, E846K) in `SOS1` — present in ~1% of [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md), ~1% of uterine carcinomas independent of subtype, and at lower rates in several other cancer types. Demonstrates that rare but functional drivers emerge at PanCanAtlas sample sizes.
- **Standard-of-care actionability:** apart from HER2+ breast cancer (most have standard-care HER2-targeted therapy), [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md) leads with 46% of samples carrying an OncoKB Level 1 or 2A alteration (driven by [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) hotspots), followed by esophagogastric ([ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) amplifications). Luminal-A breast cancer leads in OncoKB Level 3A frequency (driven by [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md), [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) mutations).
- **Lowest actionability:** [UVM](../cancer_types/UVM.md) 2.5% and testicular non-seminoma 8.5%; [THYM](../cancer_types/THYM.md), [MESO](../cancer_types/MESO.md), and [KIRC](../cancer_types/KIRC.md) also have low actionable-alteration rates.
- **Combination-therapy opportunities** (frequency of co-occurring actionable pairs): [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) + MDM2 inhibitors in 78% of dedifferentiated liposarcomas; HER2 + PI3K inhibitors in 17% of HER2-enriched [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md), 7% of [UCS](../cancer_types/UCS.md), 7% of [UCEC](../cancer_types/UCEC.md) CN-high, 7% of cervical adenocarcinoma; PI3K + MEK in 10% of EBV+ stomach; CDK4 + PI3K in 7% of [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md); HER2 + MEK in 7% of [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md); PI3K + RAF in 12% of [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md); IDH + PI3K in 14% of IDH-mutant [LGG](../cancer_types/LGG.md).

## Genes & alterations

- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — most-altered RTK-RAS gene pan-cancer (9%); hotspot mutations dominate; concentrated in [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md) (72%), colorectal genomically-stable (69%), [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) (33%).
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — 7% pan-cancer; hotspot mutations in [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md) 51%, [THCA](../cancer_types/THCA.md) 62%; primary Level-1/2A driver in melanoma actionability.
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) — 4% pan-cancer; involved in the most significant SELECT pairs; mutated/amplified/fused in [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) 50% and IDHwt [LGG](../cancer_types/LGG.md) 52%; amplification co-occurs with EGFR mutation/fusion and with 4q12 ([KIT](../genes/KIT.md)/[PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md)) amplification in glioma, but is mutually exclusive with [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) activation and with [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)/[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) mutation or [NF1](../genes/NF1.md)/[RASA1](../genes/RASA1.md) loss elsewhere.
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) — alterations concentrated in [BRCA](../genes/BRCA.md)/HER2-enriched and esophagogastric CIN (26%) and [CESC](../cancer_types/CESC.md) (23%); mutually exclusive with [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) amplification.
- [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), [RASA1](../genes/RASA1.md) — RAS-pathway negative regulators; loss is mutually exclusive with [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) amplification, suggesting an antagonistic interaction.
- [SOS1](../genes/SOS1.md) — newly nominated RTK-RAS driver: recurrent hotspots A90V/T and N233Y/S plus activating M269I/V, G434R, R552S/K/G/M, E846K; ~1% of [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) and uterine carcinoma.
- [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) / [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) — exceptional co-occurrence with PI3K-pathway alterations (versus the typical mutual exclusivity between RTK and downstream RAS/PI3K).
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — co-occurs with cell-cycle alterations ([CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md) amp, [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) mut, [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) loss, [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md)/[E2F3](../genes/E2F3.md) amp); mutually exclusive with [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) deletion (because CDKN2A deletion removes both p16 and ARF).
- [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) — mutually exclusive with [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) loss but co-amplified with [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) (genomic proximity); 78% of dedifferentiated liposarcomas have CDK4+MDM2 co-amplification.
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md), [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md), [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md), [E2F3](../genes/E2F3.md) — frequent cell-cycle drivers across the cohort; many co-altered with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md).
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [PIK3CB](../genes/PIK3CB.md), [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) — core PI3K pathway drivers; PIK3CA activation + [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) inactivation dominate in head-and-neck, breast, GI and gynecologic tumors.
- [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) — loss co-occurs with [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md) loss in lung cancer; mutually exclusive with [NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md) amplification.
- [NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md), [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md), [CUL3](../genes/CUL3.md) — NRF2-pathway drivers ([NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md) GoF + KEAP1/CUL3 LoF); concentrated in squamous lung/esophagus, head and neck, and uterine cancers.
- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) — pathway driven mainly by 8q amplification; most common in [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md), [OV](../cancer_types/OV.md), and others.
- [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md), [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) — outside the 10 pathways but enumerated as actionable in [LGG](../cancer_types/LGG.md) (IDH + PI3K inhibitor combination indicated in 14% of IDH-mutant [LGG](../cancer_types/LGG.md)).
- [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) — outside the 10 pathways; included in the 57% pan-cancer actionability figure (vs 52% restricted to the 10 pathways).
- [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) — POLE-mutant tumor subtype (with MSI-H) carries the highest mutation burden and the highest pathway alteration frequency.

## Clinical implications

- The paper claims a quantitative pan-cancer rationale for **targeted therapy planning by pathway, not just by gene**: 57% of TCGA tumors carry at least one OncoKB-actionable alteration once BRCA1/2 and IDH1/2 are included; 30% carry multiple.
- **Standard-of-care biomarkers remain concentrated in a few tumor types** — [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) in [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md) (46% Level 1/2A), [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) in HER2-enriched [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) and esophagogastric. Most other actionable hits are still investigational (OncoKB Level 3A/3B).
- **Combination-therapy hypotheses** that the data directly nominate:
  - CDK4 + MDM2 inhibitors — supported by 78% co-amplification in dedifferentiated liposarcoma.
  - HER2 + PI3K inhibitors — 17% co-actionable in HER2-enriched [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md), 7% in [UCS](../cancer_types/UCS.md)/[UCEC](../cancer_types/UCEC.md) CN-high/cervical adeno.
  - PI3K + MEK in EBV+ [STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md) (10%); CDK4 + PI3K in [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) (7%); HER2 + MEK in [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md) (7%); PI3K + RAF in [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md) (12%); IDH + PI3K in IDH-mutant [LGG](../cancer_types/LGG.md) (14%).
- **NRF2 inclusion criteria for trials:** alterations in [NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md) and [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md) are noted as enrollment criteria for a phase-2 TORC1/2 inhibitor trial — direct example of pathway-level biomarker stratification.
- **Resistance reasoning:** mutual-exclusivity patterns (e.g. [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) amp ⟂ [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)/[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) mut ⟂ [NF1](../genes/NF1.md)/[RASA1](../genes/RASA1.md) loss) double as resistance hypotheses — secondary acquisition of any of these could bypass single-agent EGFR targeting (cf. [Sun et al. 2014, Unni et al. 2015] cited in the paper).
- **Not all mutations are targets:** the authors caution that MSI-H and POLE-mutant tumors have inflated apparent actionability counts because most of their alterations are passengers; clinical interpretation requires functional filtering (OncoKB-style).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Tumor-type scope:** most hematologic cancers (other than [LAML](../cancer_types/LAML.md)) are not included; metastatic and post-treatment disease is largely absent (only 4% metastatic, all melanoma).
- **Power for rare drivers:** 9,125 samples still underpowers tumor-type-specific drivers below ~1% frequency; the [SOS1](../genes/SOS1.md) finding illustrates both the opportunity and the limitation. Larger tumor-type-specific cohorts are needed.
- **Pathway scope:** restricted to 10 canonical mitogenic-signaling pathways; DNA repair, spliceosome, ubiquitination, metabolic, and epigenetic-modifier pathways are deferred to other PanCanAtlas papers. Cross-pathway interactions with these processes are not assessed here.
- **Filtering bias:** filtering by OncoKB "oncogenic / likely / predicted oncogenic" status — alterations not yet annotated as oncogenic are removed, which can underestimate alteration frequency for under-studied genes.
- **Pathway membership is curated, not derived:** the 10 pathway templates are union sets from prior TCGA marker papers plus expert curation. Membership disagreements between curators are not quantified.
- **Combination-therapy claims are genomic, not pharmacologic:** the paper identifies *co-occurring actionable alterations*; whether the corresponding drug combinations are tolerable or synergistic is not tested.
- **No germline analysis:** germline contributions to the 10-pathway landscape (and their interaction with somatic alterations) are deferred to other work.
