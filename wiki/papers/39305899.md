**PMID:** 39305899 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.stem.2024.08.010 · **Journal:** Cell Stem Cell (2024)

## TL;DR

Al Shihabi et al. assembled a UCLA biobank of n=194 sarcoma specimens from 126 patients spanning 24 distinct bone and soft-tissue subtypes (Feb 2018–May 2022) and built a high-throughput patient-derived tumor organoid (PDTO) "mini-ring" platform that returns drug-sensitivity profiles within one week of surgery. Of the 124 samples seeded, ~93% generated organoids; 92 specimens were screened against ≥10 of >400 single agents and combinations. Drug responses tracked sarcoma subtype, treatment history, age, lesion type, and disease trajectory; intra-patient heterogeneity was substantial. The authors identified at least one FDA-approved or NCCN-recommended effective regimen for 59% of specimens (57/97) and 58% of patients (43/74), and showed organoid responses to neoadjuvant MAP correlated with post-resection necrosis and progression-free survival in three osteosarcoma cases, motivating the prospective PREMOST trial (NCT06064682) [PMID:39305899](../papers/39305899.md).

## Cohort & data

- 194 sarcoma specimens from 126 patients treated at UCLA between Feb 2018 and May 2022; 11 biopsies and 183 surgical resections; 46% metastatic, 41% primary, 13% recurrent. 27 patients (21%) contributed multiple samples.
- Patient demographics: 50% adult (63/126), 35% AYA (45/126), 13% pediatric (18/126); 62% male, 38% female; 71% had prior systemic therapy and 30% prior radiation.
- 24 distinct bone and soft-tissue sarcoma subtypes, dominated by [osteosarcoma](../cancer_types/OS.md) (73 samples, 28 patients), [chordoma](../cancer_types/CHDM.md) (14 samples, 10 patients), [chondrosarcoma](../cancer_types/CHS.md) (13 samples, 12 patients), [leiomyosarcoma](../cancer_types/LMS.md) (12 samples, 10 patients), and undifferentiated pleomorphic sarcoma ([MFH](../cancer_types/MFH.md), 11 samples, 10 patients). Other histologies present include [Ewing sarcoma](../cancer_types/ES.md), [synovial sarcoma](../cancer_types/SYNS.md), [MPNST](../cancer_types/MPNST.md), [rhabdomyosarcoma](../cancer_types/RMS.md) including [ARMS](../cancer_types/ARMS.md) / [ERMS](../cancer_types/ERMS.md) / [SCSRMS](../cancer_types/SCSRMS.md), [epithelioid sarcoma](../cancer_types/EPIS.md), [DSRCT](../cancer_types/DSRCT.md), [PEComa](../cancer_types/PECOMA.md), [myxofibrosarcoma](../cancer_types/MFS.md), [WDLS](../cancer_types/WDLS.md), [DDCHS](../cancer_types/DDCHS.md), and CIC-rearranged sarcoma (no canonical OncoTree code).
- Dataset: [sarcoma_ucla_2024](../datasets/sarcoma_ucla_2024.md). RNAseq, DNA panel sequencing, and single-agent screening data deposited at Synapse (PDTOSarcoma); panel-sequencing data also slated for [cBioPortal](../datasets/sarcoma_ucla_2024.md) sharing.
- Assays/methods: a custom 96-well [PDTO mini-ring drug screen](../methods/organoid-drug-screening.md) (>400 compounds; viability via CellTiter-Glo at day 5; Z' / robust-Z' ≥0.2 inclusion); [DFCI-ONCOPANEL-3](../methods/DFCI-ONCOPANEL-3.md) targeted DNA panel at Brigham CAMD; [whole-genome sequencing](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) (BWA-MEM2, MuSE, Mutect2, SomaticSniper, Strelka2, Battenberg/ASCAT); bulk [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) (KAPA Hyper Prep, NovaSeq6000, STAR, GRCh38.108); [FISH](../methods/fish.md) for [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) and NTRK1/2/3 (NeoGenomics) [PMID:39305899](../papers/39305899.md).
- 124 samples seeded; organoid take rate ~93%; mean 117 drugs tested per sample (range 6–423).

## Key findings

- Among 92 specimens screened against ≥10 compounds, 80.4% (74/92) had at least one significant response (top 5% viability score for ≥1 regimen); 19.6% (18/92) had none, and these were typically tested against smaller drug libraries (median 25 vs 58 drugs/combinations) [PMID:39305899](../papers/39305899.md).
- 3.3% (3/92) showed broad/non-specific chemosensitivity (top 5% on ≥25% of compounds tested).
- [Osteosarcoma](../cancer_types/OS.md) PDTOs were significantly more sensitive than the pan-sarcoma average to [ceralasertib](../drugs/ceralasertib.md) ([ATR](../genes/ATR.md) inhibitor, p=0.00026), [topotecan](../drugs/topotecan.md) (p=0.028), [cabozantinib](../drugs/cabozantinib.md) (p=0.024), and [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md) (p=0.012).
- [Chordoma](../cancer_types/CHDM.md) PDTOs were significantly less sensitive than other sarcomas to [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md) (p=0.022), alvocidib (p=0.00046), apitolisib (p=0.0071), and [bortezomib](../drugs/bortezomib.md) (p=1.8×10⁻⁵). Chordomas were preferentially sensitive to TAK-285 ([EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md)/[ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) kinase inhibitor, p=0.034), BI-D1870 (RSK inhibitor, p=0.047), and A-769662 (AMPK activator, p=0.0015).
- [Chondrosarcoma](../cancer_types/CHS.md) PDTOs were sensitive to TAE226 (FAK/Pyk2, p=0.04) and BX-912 (PDK1, p=0.038); [RMS](../cancer_types/RMS.md) PDTOs to [pazopanib](../drugs/pazopanib.md) (p=0.049) and [trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md) (p=0.013); UPS ([MFH](../cancer_types/MFH.md)) PDTOs to BMS-754807 (IGF-1R, p=0.039); [LMS](../cancer_types/LMS.md) PDTOs were less sensitive to [topotecan](../drugs/topotecan.md) (p=0.031, consistent with prior negative trials) but more sensitive to [dovitinib](../drugs/dovitinib.md) (p=0.0068).
- Tumors with prior systemic therapy were more sensitive to [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md), [cabozantinib](../drugs/cabozantinib.md), [lenvatinib](../drugs/lenvatinib.md), and [cediranib](../drugs/cediranib.md) than treatment-naïve specimens (p=0.018, 0.036, 0.011, 0.017); ≥3 prior therapies trended with sensitivity to [pazopanib](../drugs/pazopanib.md) (p=0.0062), BI2536 (p=0.046), and danusertib (p=0.011).
- Pediatric and AYA sarcoma PDTOs were less resistant than adult PDTOs to [cediranib](../drugs/cediranib.md) (p=0.032 vs pediatric, p=0.013 vs AYA); adult PDTOs were more sensitive to the JAK inhibitor WHI-P154 vs AYA (p=0.0037). Metastatic PDTOs trended toward enhanced response to degrasyn (p=0.028 vs primary, p=0.048 vs recurrent) and OSI-930 (p=0.024).
- PDTOs from patients with progressive disease at follow-up were more resistant to [dasatinib](../drugs/dasatinib.md) (p=0.039), sirolimus/[rapamycin](../drugs/sirolimus.md) (p=0.014), and [gefitinib](../drugs/gefitinib.md) (p=0.048). Rapidly progressing tumors had increased sensitivity to thiazovivin (ROCK inhibitor), [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md), and [ruxolitinib](../drugs/ruxolitinib.md) (p=0.041, 0.042, 0.043).
- Pathway analysis (PubChem + WikiPathways and KEGG) showed PDTOs from the same patient consistently clustered together regardless of histology (e.g., SARC0103, SARC0078, SARC0075, SARC0139, SARC0053). [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md)/ErbB signaling was implicated in chordomas (SARC0046_2/3, SARC0053_a, SARC0049), an osteosarcoma subgroup, and individual [LMS](../cancer_types/LMS.md) and [RMS](../cancer_types/RMS.md) cases.
- Functional vs genomic concordance: SARC0117 (metastatic undifferentiated spindle cell sarcoma, [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) hotspot mutation reported as "H1074L" in extracted text — corresponds to the canonical H1047L hotspot) was a top responder to [alpelisib](../drugs/alpelisib.md) and broadly sensitive across the PI3K/mTOR class (apitolisib, [alpelisib](../drugs/alpelisib.md), copanlisib, BGT226, vistusertib). SARC0134 ([MPNST](../cancer_types/MPNST.md)) carried the same [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) mutation in the primary but did not respond to [alpelisib](../drugs/alpelisib.md); follow-up sequencing confirmed loss of [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) mutation in the metastatic lesion. SARC0069_2 (primary [osteosarcoma](../cancer_types/OS.md), no [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) mutation) was the top [alpelisib](../drugs/alpelisib.md) responder, illustrating biomarker-negative responders.
- SARC0133 ([RMS](../cancer_types/RMS.md)) was the top responder to the FGFR inhibitor [infigratinib](../drugs/infigratinib.md); WGS revealed an [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) gain on chromosome 8, but PDTOs were not sensitive to [dovitinib](../drugs/dovitinib.md), demonstrating intra-class drug differentiation.
- SARC0127 (suspected infantile fibrosarcoma) PDTOs were resistant to [larotrectinib](../drugs/larotrectinib.md); given that [IFS](../cancer_types/IFS.md) carries [ETV6](../genes/ETV6.md)–[NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md) fusion in 90% of cases, the negative response correctly predicted the eventual diagnosis of high-grade spindle cell/sclerosing rhabdomyosarcoma ([SCSRMS](../cancer_types/SCSRMS.md)) — delivered within a week vs 18 days for pathology.
- Intra-patient heterogeneity was prominent: SARC0075 (epithelioid sarcoma with undifferentiated pleomorphic features) had three same-surgery metastases with Pearson correlations 0.64–0.84; SARC0075_2 responded to only 7.7% of regimens vs 23% for SARC0075_1/4. WGS showed shared SNVs in [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md), [CRTC1](../genes/CRTC1.md), and [MAP3K1](../genes/MAP3K1.md) but extensive divergent CNVs including whole-genome duplications.
- Actionability: of 203 drug-diagnosis pairs ranked among top-five responders (top-10% rank), 4% were FDA-approved for the same sarcoma type, 31% FDA-approved for other cancers, 1% in trial for the matched indication, 21% in trial for other cancers, 43% pre-clinical or terminated. Only 5% were NCCN-preferred and 3% NCCN-recommended for the matched diagnosis.
- Aggregate actionable hits: 59% of samples (57/97) and 58% of patients (43/74) had ≥1 FDA-approved or NCCN-recommended top-five regimen; 7 patients had four such options.

## Genes & alterations

- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md): hotspot mutation (reported as "H1074L"; canonical hotspot H1047L) in SARC0117 ([USS](../cancer_types/SARCNOS.md)) drove broad PI3K/mTOR pathway sensitivity ([alpelisib](../drugs/alpelisib.md), apitolisib, copanlisib, BGT226, vistusertib). SARC0134 ([MPNST](../cancer_types/MPNST.md)) had the mutation in primary but lost it in metastasis, predicting non-response. SARC0069_2 ([osteosarcoma](../cancer_types/OS.md)) was a biomarker-negative top alpelisib responder.
- [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md): amplification confirmed by [FISH](../methods/fish.md) in [WDLS](../cancer_types/WDLS.md) SARC0120 in both parent tumor and PDTO, illustrating that PDTOs preserve key sarcoma drivers.
- [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md): gain on chromosome 8 in SARC0133 ([RMS](../cancer_types/RMS.md)) correlated with selective sensitivity to [infigratinib](../drugs/infigratinib.md) but not dovitinib.
- [NTRK1](../genes/NTRK1.md) / [NTRK2](../genes/NTRK2.md) / [NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md), [ETV6](../genes/ETV6.md): canonical [ETV6](../genes/ETV6.md)–[NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md) fusion expected in [IFS](../cancer_types/IFS.md); SARC0127's [larotrectinib](../drugs/larotrectinib.md) resistance and FISH-negative [ETV6](../genes/ETV6.md) helped reclassify the tumor as [SCSRMS](../cancer_types/SCSRMS.md).
- [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md), [CRTC1](../genes/CRTC1.md), [MAP3K1](../genes/MAP3K1.md): rare shared SNVs across SARC0075's four metastatic lesions; CNV landscape (whole-genome duplications, arm-level events) was substantially discordant.
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) / [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md): pathway implicated by KEGG analysis in chordomas (SARC0046_2/3, SARC0053_a, SARC0049) and an osteosarcoma subgroup; chordomas preferentially sensitive to TAK-285 ([EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md)/[ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) kinase inhibitor).
- [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md): cited in the introduction as a prevalent CDK aberration in liposarcoma ([LIPO](../cancer_types/LIPO.md)) literature; not directly genotyped in this study but motivates [palbociclib](../drugs/palbociclib.md) testing observed across PDTOs (e.g., SARC0086_3 [PEComa](../cancer_types/PECOMA.md)).

## Clinical implications

- Functional precision-medicine PDTO screening can yield ≥1 actionable FDA-approved or NCCN-recommended regimen for 58–59% of sarcoma patients/specimens, complementary to genomic NGS which yields actionable alterations in only ~30% of patients (or ~42% in a 6,000-sarcoma series cited by the authors) [PMID:39305899](../papers/39305899.md).
- In three treatment-naïve [osteosarcoma](../cancer_types/OS.md) biopsies, organoid response to MAP ([methotrexate](../drugs/methotrexate.md) / [doxorubicin](../drugs/doxorubicin.md) / [cisplatin](../drugs/cisplatin.md)) tracked post-resection necrosis: SARC0041 (50% residual viability after platinum) and SARC0064 (57% on MAP) had 99% and 95% necrosis and no recurrence at 1400/1500 days; SARC0135 (73% residual viability on MAP, 60% necrosis) relapsed within 200 days.
- Across n=5 patients receiving a treatment matched to their PDTO panel ([bortezomib](../drugs/bortezomib.md)+[panobinostat](../drugs/panobinostat.md), [gemcitabine](../drugs/gemcitabine.md)+[docetaxel](../drugs/docetaxel.md), [mocetinostat](../drugs/mocetinostat.md)+[vinorelbine](../drugs/vinorelbine.md), [irinotecan](../drugs/irinotecan.md)+[temozolomide](../drugs/temozolomide.md)), normalized organoid viability correlated with time-to-next-treatment (R²=0.921, p=0.009).
- These data motivated the prospective PREMOST trial (NCT06064682) testing organoid prediction of neoadjuvant MAP response in [osteosarcoma](../cancer_types/OS.md) (Arm 1) and FDA-approved drugs in advanced [osteosarcoma](../cancer_types/OS.md) (Arm 2).
- NCCN-listed top-five regimens recurred for [osteosarcoma](../cancer_types/OS.md) ([etoposide](../drugs/etoposide.md), [cisplatin](../drugs/cisplatin.md), [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md), [regorafenib](../drugs/regorafenib.md), [doxorubicin](../drugs/doxorubicin.md), [cabozantinib](../drugs/cabozantinib.md), [gemcitabine](../drugs/gemcitabine.md), [docetaxel](../drugs/docetaxel.md), [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md)), [rhabdomyosarcoma](../cancer_types/RMS.md) ([cyclophosphamide](../drugs/cyclophosphamide.md), [doxorubicin](../drugs/doxorubicin.md), [vinorelbine](../drugs/vinorelbine.md)), and [Ewing sarcoma](../cancer_types/ES.md) ([doxorubicin](../drugs/doxorubicin.md)).
- Access to off-label FDA-approved drugs identified by PDTO screening (most patients' top hits are not approved for the patient's specific sarcoma subtype) is flagged as a major adoption barrier.

## Limitations & open questions

- Authors note PDTO viability data were not normalized for proliferation rate; rapidly proliferating advanced/metastatic tumors may bias toward sensitivity to certain agents.
- Patient outcome data were collected for research without RECIST imaging; sarcoma response is poorly captured by RECIST due to non-spherical growth, edema, fibrosis, necrosis and intra-tumoral hemorrhage. Time-to-next-treatment (TTNT) was used as a surrogate but n=5.
- Predictive validation cohort for MAP response was n=3 and the matched-treatment cohort n=5; the prospective PREMOST trial (NCT06064682) is required to confirm utility.
- Intra-patient and intra-tumoral heterogeneity (e.g., SARC0075 four-lesion CNV divergence; SARC0127 18-day biopsy-vs-resection drift) means single-sample PDTO recommendations may misrepresent disseminated disease.
- 21 of 194 specimens failed cell-yield thresholds and 10 of 124 seeded samples failed to grow; certain indolent subtypes ([UPS](../cancer_types/MFH.md), [LMS](../cancer_types/LMS.md), chordoma) showed minimal ex vivo proliferation, limiting screening throughput.
- 19.6% (18/92) of screened specimens had no top-5% response, frequently because the panel was small (median 25 drugs) — questioning whether functional precision medicine requires a minimum library size for utility.
- 43% of identified active compounds are pre-clinical or terminated; only 5% are NCCN-preferred for the matched indication, raising drug-access and approval-pathway concerns.
- The PIK3CA hotspot is rendered as "H1074L" in the manuscript text; this is almost certainly the canonical H1047L hotspot but the discrepancy is recorded as observed.
- CIC-rearranged sarcoma lacks an OncoTree code in the canonical ontology and is therefore not represented in the cancer-type frontmatter.
