# SPAG1

## Overview

SPAG1 (Sperm-Associated Antigen 1) is a cancer-testis (CT) antigen that is normally expressed in testis but aberrantly expressed in a subset of cancers. CT antigens are attractive immunotherapy targets because their restricted normal-tissue expression reduces risk of on-target toxicity. SPAG1 has been identified as a recurrent CT antigen in colon cancer.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Recurrent CT antigen expressed in 14% of colon tumor-NAT pairs in the CPTAC proteogenomics cohort ([coad_cptac_2019](../datasets/coad_cptac_2019.md)); identified alongside [IGF2BP3](../genes/IGF2BP3.md) and [ATAD2](../genes/ATAD2.md) as MSI-status-independent recurrent CT antigens — potential immunotherapy targets for MSS colon tumors [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Colorectal cancer ([COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md)) — SPAG1 expressed as a CT antigen in 14% of colon tumors (vs 51% for [IGF2BP3](../genes/IGF2BP3.md) and 8% for [ATAD2](../genes/ATAD2.md)); MSI-status-independent expression makes it a candidate CT-antigen vaccine target for MSS CRC patients who are poor candidates for checkpoint blockade [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-expressed as a CT antigen with [IGF2BP3](../genes/IGF2BP3.md) and [ATAD2](../genes/ATAD2.md) in a subset of colon tumors; expression is independent of MSI status [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- SPAG1 is an MSI-independent CT antigen in colon cancer, making it a candidate target for CT-antigen vaccine strategies in MSS colon tumors that are poor candidates for checkpoint blockade or neoantigen vaccines; IGF2BP3 (the most frequent CT antigen in the same cohort) has demonstrated in-vitro/in-vivo CTL induction in esophageal cancer [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Open questions

- Whether SPAG1 CT antigen expression in colon tumors can be therapeutically exploited via vaccination or adoptive T-cell strategies requires clinical validation; frequency in non-CPTAC cohorts is unknown.
