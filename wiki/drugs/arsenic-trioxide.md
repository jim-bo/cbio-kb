# arsenic-trioxide

## Overview

Arsenic trioxide (ATO) is a small inorganic compound that induces differentiation and apoptosis in acute promyelocytic leukemia ([APL](../cancer_types/AML.md)) cells through degradation of PML-RARA fusion protein. Combined with [tretinoin](../drugs/tretinoin.md) (ATRA), it is first-line therapy for APL. It is also included in broader [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md) drug panels for ex vivo profiling purposes.

## Evidence in the corpus

- Arsenic trioxide was included in the 122-compound ex vivo drug sensitivity panel screened against 409 specimens from 363 AML patients in the Beat AML program; results informed mutation–drug sensitivity associations in the broader AML landscape [PMID:30333627](../papers/30333627.md).
