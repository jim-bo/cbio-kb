**PMID:** 30427539 · **DOI:** 10.1002/cncr.31850 · **Journal:** Cancer (2019)

## TL;DR

Narayan et al. profiled 81 primary gallbladder cancer (GBCA) tumors from three centers — Memorial Sloan Kettering (USA, n=49), Arturo Lopez Perez Foundation Cancer Institute (Chile, n=21), and Yokohama City University (Japan, n=11) — using the [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) targeted DNA-sequencing assay. They report regional differences in clinicopathology and mutation pattern: Japanese tumors lacked [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) and [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) mutations, Chilean tumors lacked [ERBB3](../genes/ERBB3.md) and [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md) mutations, and Chilean tumors carried the highest mutation burden (median 7 vs. 6 in Japan vs. 4 in the United States; P-adj = .006). [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) was mutated in roughly one-third of patients across all three sites and was independently associated with worse overall survival (HR 2.01, 95% CI 1.02–3.94; P = .043). Eighty percent of tumors carried at least one mutation in a potentially actionable gene target.

## Cohort & data

- **N = 81** patients with primary [gallbladder cancer (GBC)](../cancer_types/GBC.md) treated between September 1999 and March 2016.
- Distribution by site: 49 from MSK (United States), 21 from Arturo Lopez Perez (Chile), 11 from Yokohama City University (Japan).
- Only adenocarcinoma and adenosquamous histologies; 14 patients with metastatic-only tissue and 6 with other histologies (neuroendocrine, small cell, sarcomatoid, cholangiocarcinoma) were excluded.
- Paired tumor + normal DNA sequenced with the [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) hybridization-capture targeted NGS assay (341–410 cancer-associated genes; [IMPACT341](../methods/IMPACT341.md) / [IMPACT410](../methods/IMPACT410.md) panel versions). Mutation burden was normalized by panel size (1.2 Mb for IMPACT-341; 1.38 Mb for IMPACT-410).
- Data deposited as [gbc_msk_2018](../datasets/gbc_msk_2018.md) on cBioPortal.
- Survival analysis used [Kaplan–Meier](../methods/kaplan-meier.md) and [Cox proportional-hazards](../methods/cox-proportional-hazards.md) modeling; actionable targets were defined via [OncoKB](../methods/oncokb-annotation.md).

## Key findings

- **Mutation burden differed by region** (P-adj = .006): Chile median 7 (range 3–20), Japan median 6 (range 1–23), United States median 4 (range 0–27).
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)** was the most commonly mutated gene cohort-wide (47/81; 58%). Chilean cohort 76% (16/21), Japanese 55% (6/11), American 51% (25/49); regional differences not statistically significant (P-adj = .45).
- **[SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) mutated in 25/81 (31%)** — 38% Chile, 36% Japan, 27% US (P-adj = .68 across sites). Univariately associated with worse overall survival (median 10.4 mo vs. 24.8 mo; P-adj = .039). After controlling for stage, gallstone status, and sex in a multivariable Cox model, [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) remained independently associated with worse [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) (hazard ratio 2.01, 95% CI 1.02–3.94; P = .043).
- **[ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) mutated in 20/81 (25%)** but **absent in all 11 Japanese patients** (0% vs. 38% Chile, 25% US; P-adj = .35).
- **[PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) mutations** were exclusively observed in Chilean (3/21; 14%) and American (7/49; 14%) patients; **none in Japanese patients**.
- **[ERBB3](../genes/ERBB3.md) and [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md) mutations were absent in Chilean patients** but present at 9% in Japanese (1/11 each) and 12%/10% in American patients.
- Pathway-level recurrence: [p53](../genes/TP53.md) pathway (73%), [RTK-RAS](../themes/rtk-ras-pathway.md) (44%) — the two most commonly altered pathways; no pathway showed regional differences (P-adj = .62 to >.95).
- **80% of patients carried at least one mutation in a potentially actionable gene target** (per OncoKB or prior literature).
- **No mutation or pathway alteration was significantly linked to gallstone-associated GBCA** (P = .34 to .92 for individual genes; P-adj = .25 to >.95 for pathways).
- Median overall survival after complete resection (n=46) was 19.6 months (95% CI 14.5–28.8); 5-year survival 18% (95% CI 8–31%).
- Exploratory trends toward worse survival (not significant after adjustment) for [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) (median 9.9 mo vs. 24.5 mo; P-adj = .09) and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) (median 8.5 mo vs. 23.8 mo; P-adj = .09).
- Mutual exclusivity / co-occurrence: [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) appeared mutually exclusive (log-OR 1.92; P-adj = .13, NS); co-occurrence trends among [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md)/[TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md)/[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md)/[CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md)/[CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)/[CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md)/[AR](../genes/AR.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md)/[KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), and [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md)/[TERT](../genes/TERT.md) — none reached adjusted significance.

## Genes & alterations

- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)** — Most commonly mutated gene (58%); trend toward enrichment in Chilean tumors (76%, NS after adjustment).
- **[SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md)** — Mutated in 31% of tumors; independently associated with worse overall survival (HR 2.01; multivariable Cox).
- **[ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md)** — Mutated in 25% of all tumors; entirely absent in Japanese cohort.
- **[PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)** — Mutated in 12% of cohort; entirely absent in Japanese cohort; trend toward worse OS (P-adj = .09).
- **[ATM](../genes/ATM.md)** — Mutated in 19% of cohort; trend toward mutual exclusivity with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md).
- **[CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)** — Mutated in 15%; trend toward worse OS (P-adj = .09).
- **[TERT](../genes/TERT.md)** — Mutated in 12%; promoter region; co-occurrence trend with [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md).
- **[BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md)** — Mutated in 10%; co-occurrence trends with [AR](../genes/AR.md) and [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md).
- **[KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md)** — Mutated in 10%.
- **[ERBB3](../genes/ERBB3.md)** — Mutated in 9% of cohort but absent in Chilean patients.
- **[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)** — Mutated in 9%; previously associated with anomalous pancreatobiliary duct junction (a Japanese-predominant risk factor).
- **[NF1](../genes/NF1.md)** — Mutated in 9%.
- **[ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md)** — Mutated in 7% of cohort but absent in Chilean patients.
- **[AR](../genes/AR.md)** — Mutated in 7%.
- **[CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md)** — Mutated in 7%; co-occurrence trend with [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) (chromosome 9 adjacency).
- **[MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md)** — Mutated in 6%; same pathway as TP53 and [ATM](../genes/ATM.md).

## Clinical implications

- **[SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) as a converging prognostic biomarker.** Although region-specific drivers ([PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), ARID2, [ERBB3](../genes/ERBB3.md)) varied across cohorts, SMAD4 was mutated at comparable frequency (~one-third) in all three populations and predicted worse OS independently of stage, sex, and gallstone status. The authors propose SMAD4 mutation as a candidate convergent indicator of poor prognosis in GBCA across regions.
- **High frequency of actionable targets supports precision-oncology trial design.** 80% of patients carried at least one mutation in a gene flagged as actionable by [OncoKB](../methods/oncokb-annotation.md) or prior literature, providing rationale for prospective biomarker-driven trials in GBCA.
- **Regional differences argue for stratified clinical-trial enrollment.** The absence of ARID1A and PIK3CA mutations in Japanese tumors, and of ERBB3 and ARID2 in Chilean tumors, suggests that single-arm trials enrolling internationally may obscure region-specific response signals.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Small sample sizes per region** (Japan n=11; Chile n=21) limit power for site-specific subgroup conclusions and may explain why several regional differences did not reach adjusted significance.
- **Retrospective design** with potential selection bias; only 21% of cases were incidental, despite ~70% of GBCA being incidentally diagnosed in the general population (cases with adequate tumor for sequencing are over-represented).
- **Primary tumor-only sampling** — 14 patients with metastatic-only tissue were excluded; the metastatic landscape may differ.
- **DNA sequencing alone may underestimate pathway activity.** Authors note that expression assays might better capture compensatory pathway effects (e.g., TGF-β signaling cannot be fully resolved from somatic alterations alone).
- **National representativeness is uncertain.** It is unclear whether the three single-institution cohorts reflect their broader national GBCA populations or center-specific referral biases.
- **Conflict with prior reports on [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) in Asian patients.** This study found no ARID1A mutations in Japanese patients, contrasting with one Japanese report of inactivating ARID1A mutations (Sasaki et al., 2016) but consistent with other Asian-cohort reports that also failed to find ARID1A mutations.
