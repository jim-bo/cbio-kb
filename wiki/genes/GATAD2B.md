# GATAD2B

## Overview

GATAD2B (GATA zinc finger domain containing 2B) encodes a component of the NuRD (nucleosome remodeling and deacetylase) chromatin-remodeling complex. As a 1q-encoded histone modifier, GATAD2B is of interest in cancers with 1q amplification, where copy number gains may alter chromatin state and suppress tumor suppressor pathways.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- GATAD2B is a 1q-encoded histone modifier that anticorrelates with p53-pathway target proteins ([CDKN1A](../genes/CDKN1A.md), [TNFRSF10B](../genes/TNFRSF10B.md)) in endometrial cancer; part of the SETDB1/SDE2/PARP1/GATAD2B 1q-amp cluster identified by proteogenomics [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[UCEC](../cancer_types/UCEC.md)** (Endometrial carcinoma): observed in the context of 1q amplification in MSI and serous EC subtypes [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-amplified on 1q with [SETDB1](../genes/SETDB1.md), SDE2, and [PARP1](../genes/PARP1.md); this cluster anticorrelates with p53-pathway targets [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- 1q-amplification of GATAD2B and co-encoded histone modifiers may suppress p53-pathway activity in EC tumors, potentially contributing to resistance to p53-pathway-dependent therapies.

## Open questions

- Whether GATAD2B itself or neighboring 1q-encoded genes (SETDB1, PARP1) is the primary functional driver of p53-pathway suppression in 1q-amplified EC remains unresolved.
