# WRN

## Overview

WRN (Werner Syndrome RecQ Like Helicase) is a RecQ-family DNA helicase/exonuclease involved in DNA repair, replication, and telomere maintenance. Germline loss-of-function mutations in WRN cause Werner syndrome (premature aging). In cancer biology, WRN has emerged as a synthetic lethal target in microsatellite-instable (MSI) tumors: MSI cancers depend on WRN to resolve toxic DNA secondary structures that accumulate at microsatellite loci, making WRN inhibition selectively lethal in MSI cells. The proteome-level predictors of WRN knockdown sensitivity include mismatch-repair complex proteins, SKI/RQC complex components, and SUMO E1 (SAE1/UBA2) abundance.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Synthetic lethal vulnerability in MSI cancer cell lines (CCLE); sensitivity predicted by mismatch-repair protein levels ([MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md), [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md), [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md)), SKI-complex components ([SKIC2](../genes/SKIC2.md), [SKIC3](../genes/SKIC3.md)), and SUMO E1 heterodimer (SAE1/UBA2) abundance [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- MSI tumors (pan-cancer): WRN is a clinically actionable synthetic lethal target; proteomic biomarkers from CCLE identify predictors of sensitivity [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Sensitivity to WRN knockdown co-varies with MSH2/MSH6 (MutS) and MLH1/PMS2 (MutL) protein abundance, as well as SKI-complex (SKIC2/SKIC3) and SUMO E1 (SAE1/UBA2) levels [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- WRN is a validated synthetic lethal target in MSI tumors; protein-complex biomarkers (MMR, SKI, SUMO E1) may complement DNA/RNA-based MSI testing for patient selection [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Open questions

- Whether SUMO E1 (UBA2/SAE1) dependency on WRN reflects a direct functional interaction or is mediated through shared MSI-associated proteome remodeling [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
