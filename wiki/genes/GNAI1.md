# GNAI1

## Overview

GNAI1 encodes a G protein alpha subunit (Gi1) involved in signal transduction. In pilocytic astrocytoma, GNAI1 was identified as a novel 5' fusion partner for [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), contributing to the growing catalogue of BRAF fusion partners that activate the MAPK pathway in this tumor type.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Identified as a novel 5' fusion partner for [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) in pilocytic astrocytoma by whole-genome sequencing (n=96); all BRAF fusions disrupt the leucine-rich negative regulatory domain and produce constitutive BRAF kinase activation [PMID:23817572](../papers/23817572.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **Pilocytic astrocytoma** — GNAI1:BRAF fusion identified as one of several novel BRAF fusion partners in tumors lacking the canonical [KIAA1549](../genes/KIAA1549.md):BRAF fusion [PMID:23817572](../papers/23817572.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Alternative 5' fusion partner for [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), alongside [KIAA1549](../genes/KIAA1549.md) (70/96 cases), [FAM131B](../genes/FAM131B.md), [RNF130](../genes/RNF130.md), [CLCN6](../genes/CLCN6.md), and [MKRN1](../genes/MKRN1.md) [PMID:23817572](../papers/23817572.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- BRAF fusions activate the MAPK pathway; MEK inhibition is a rational strategy for fusion-positive pilocytic astrocytomas; caution advised against non-V600E-selective BRAF inhibitors due to paradoxical MAPK activation risk [PMID:23817572](../papers/23817572.md)

## Open questions

- Functional validation of GNAI1:BRAF specifically has not been reported; frequency of this individual fusion is not specified beyond being one of several rare events [PMID:23817572](../papers/23817572.md)
