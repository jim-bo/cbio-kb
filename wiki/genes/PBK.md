# PBK

## Overview

PBK (PDZ Binding Kinase, also known as TOPK) is a serine/threonine kinase expressed during mitosis and overexpressed in multiple cancer types. It is classified as a cancer-testis (CT) antigen — normally restricted to testis but aberrantly expressed in tumors — making it a candidate immunotherapy target and potential vaccine antigen.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- PBK is a CT antigen observed in more than 10% of endometrial carcinoma (EC) tumors in the CPTAC proteogenomic cohort (n=95); it is among CT antigens with immunogenic peptide potential in EC, supporting proteogenomics as a vaccine target-discovery tool [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [UCEC](../cancer_types/UCEC.md): PBK CT antigen expression detected in >10% of EC tumors (CPTAC cohort, n=95) across [POLE](../genes/POLE.md), MSI, CNV-low, and CNV-high subtypes [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- PBK overexpression clusters with [ATAD2](../genes/ATAD2.md) and [IGF2BP3](../genes/IGF2BP3.md) as CT antigens in EC; IGF2BP3 is highly upregulated in serous EC and is a known immunogenic peptide source [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- 78% of EC tumors harbor either a neoantigen or a high-expression CT antigen including PBK; the authors propose CT antigens as targets for cancer vaccine development in EC [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md).

## Open questions

- Immunogenicity of PBK-derived peptides in EC has not been directly validated in the corpus.
- Whether PBK expression correlates with specific EC genomic subtypes (POLE, MSI, CNV-high) is not reported.
