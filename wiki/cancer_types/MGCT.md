# Mixed Germ Cell Tumor (MGCT)

## Overview

Mixed Germ Cell Tumor is a Non-Seminomatous Germ Cell Tumor (parent NSGCT).

## Cohorts in the corpus

- [makeanimpact_ccr_2023](../datasets/makeanimpact_ccr_2023.md): 54 male germ cell tumor patients were profiled as part of the Make-an-IMPACT direct-to-patient outreach cohort on MSK-IMPACT [PMID:36862133](../papers/36862133.md).

## Recurrent alterations

- Not specifically characterized in this corpus beyond cohort enrollment counts [PMID:36862133](../papers/36862133.md).
- 49 mixed GCTs (teratoma-containing) in the MSK-IMPACT cohort (Bagrodia et al., N=180); TP53/MDM2 alterations independently predicted shorter PFS (HR 1.83, P=.016) after adjusting for IGCCCG risk; 55% of cisplatin-resistant GCTs harbored actionable alterations [PMID:27646943](../papers/27646943.md).
- In the 15-patient PM NSGCT cohort, mixed GCT (MGCT) histology was common; 73% had teratoma components (predominant in 60%) and 53% had yolk sac tumor components; phylogenetic reconstruction showed every GCT was clonally related to the concomitant hematologic malignancy. [PMID:32897884](../papers/32897884.md)
