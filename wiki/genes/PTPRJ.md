# PTPRJ

## Overview

PTPRJ (Protein Tyrosine Phosphatase Receptor Type J) encodes a receptor-type phosphatase with established tumor-suppressor functions. In adenoid cystic carcinoma (ACC), PTPRJ harbors missense mutations.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Missense mutation observed in ACC; classified as a tyrosine phosphatase tumor suppressor [PMID:23685749](../papers/23685749.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Adenoid cystic carcinoma (ACC): missense mutation [PMID:23685749](../papers/23685749.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with other receptor tyrosine phosphatase tumor suppressors ([PTPRG](../genes/PTPRG.md), [PTPRH](../genes/PTPRH.md), [PTPRK](../genes/PTPRK.md)) recurrently mutated in ACC [PMID:23685749](../papers/23685749.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic nominations in the corpus.

## Open questions

- Whether PTPRJ missense mutations are loss-of-function and contribute to PI3K/AKT pathway activation in ACC is unknown.
