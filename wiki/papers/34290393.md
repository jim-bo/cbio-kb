**PMID:** 34290393 · **DOI:** 10.1038/s41698-021-00210-2 · **Journal:** npj Precision Oncology (2021)

## TL;DR

In 426 treatment-naive patients with clinical N2-negative stage I/II lung adenocarcinoma ([LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md)) sequenced with [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md), the authors built a multivariable logistic regression model combining preoperative clinical features (solid CT morphology, tumor SUVmax, clinical stage) with tumor genomic features ([SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) and [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) alterations) to predict pathologic lymph node (LN) metastasis. Both gene alterations were independently associated with pN-positive disease, alongside higher fraction of genome altered ([FGA](../genes/FGA.md)), copy number amplifications, whole-genome doubling, and enrichment of APOBEC mutational signatures. The dataset is released as the cBioPortal study [luad_msk_npjpo_2021](../datasets/luad_msk_npjpo_2021.md).

## Cohort & data

- **N = 426** treatment-naive [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) patients who underwent complete anatomic resection (lobectomy, segmentectomy, or pneumonectomy) with LN dissection at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center between 2010 and 2018.
- Inclusion required clinical N2-negative (cN0 or cN1) disease and successful [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) sequencing on the primary tumor. Exclusions: induction therapy, wedge resection, R1/R2 residual disease, low-quality NGS.
- Composition: 60% (255) male; 74% (316) ever-smokers; 78% (334) clinical stage I, 22% (92) clinical stage II; 85% (362) cN0 and 15% (64) cN1.
- Pathologic outcomes: pN0 80% (n=341), pN1 11% (n=48), pN2 9% (n=37); pN1 and pN2 were grouped as pN-positive.
- Sequencing performed across MSK-IMPACT 341-, 410-, and 468-gene panels (0.98, 1.06, 1.22 Mb coding region respectively). Copy number and WGD called from FACETS ([facets](../methods/facets.md)); focal copy number from [GISTIC 2.0](../methods/gistic.md); mutational signatures computed against COSMIC SBS reference for samples with >=13.8 Mut/Mb.
- Dataset published as [luad_msk_npjpo_2021](../datasets/luad_msk_npjpo_2021.md) on cBioPortal.

## Key findings

- **Clinical false-negative rate of preoperative LN staging was high.** Of 362 cN0 patients, 15% (n=54) had pathologic LN metastasis at surgery; of 64 cN1 patients, 48% (31/64) had confirmed pN+ disease. False-negative rate for preoperative clinical staging was 64% [PMID:34290393](../papers/34290393.md).
- **Three genes were altered at significantly higher frequency in pN-positive tumors** (Fisher's exact, alterations in >=2% of cohort): [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) 22% vs. 12% (p=0.024), [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) 8% vs. 1.8% (p=0.006), [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) 7% vs. 1.5% (p=0.011). [RBM10](../genes/RBM10.md) was significantly enriched in pN-negative tumors (17% vs. 8%; p=0.044).
- **Most frequently altered genes overall:** [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) (35% pN-neg / 34% pN-pos), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (34% / 45%), [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) (33% / 31%).
- **No significant difference in targetable [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) alterations** (n=9 genes) between pN groups (Supplementary Fig. 2).
- **TMB did not differ** between groups (median 4.9 vs. 6.1 mut/Mb; p=0.058), but **fraction of genome altered (FGA) was higher** in pN-positive tumors (median 0.343 vs. 0.269; p=0.037).
- **Copy number amplifications were significantly higher in pN-positive tumors** (median 0.152 vs. 0.085; p=0.001); deletions did not differ (p=0.299). Arm-level changes on 2p and 2q were enriched in pN+ tumors (FDR p=0.122 for both, threshold FDR p<0.2).
- **Gene-level CNAs (in >=1% of cohort):** [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) amplifications were more frequent in pN-negative tumors (8% vs. 1.2%; p=0.029); [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) alterations were more frequent in pN-positive tumors (9% vs. 4%; p=0.039). [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) was the most frequently CNA-altered gene in pN-negative tumors (10%).
- **Whole-genome doubling (WGD) was more frequent in pN-positive tumors** (22% vs. 13%; p=0.028).
- **APOBEC mutational signatures (SBS2 and SBS13) were enriched in pN-positive tumors:** SBS2 27.3% vs. 12.8% (p=0.03); SBS13 31.8% vs. 14.9% (p=0.02). Smoking signature (SBS4) did not differ.
- **Multivariable model** (Fig. 4) independently associated with pN metastasis: solid vs. nonsolid CT morphology (OR 4.25, 95% CI 1.81-9.93, p=0.001); tumor SUVmax (OR 1.09, 95% CI 1.03-1.15, p=0.002); clinical stage II vs. I (OR 3.31, 95% CI 1.87-5.88, p<0.001); [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) alteration (OR 3.67, 95% CI 1.02-13.16, p=0.046); [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) alteration (OR 5.01, 95% CI 1.29-19.45, p=0.02).
- **[STK11](../genes/STK11.md) enrichment in pN+ disease did not survive the final multivariable model**, although it was significant in stage-adjusted univariable analysis (p=0.026) and entered model selection.
- **Smoking subgroup analysis:** STK11/SMARCA4/SMAD4/RBM10 enrichment was driven by ever-smokers; no significant pN+ vs. pN- difference among never-smokers (likely small-sample / lower TMB; Supplementary Fig. 1).

## Genes & alterations

- [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) — alterations independently associated with pathologic LN metastasis (multivariable OR 5.01, p=0.02); 7% in pN+ vs. 1.5% in pN- (p=0.011). Authors note prior literature linking reduced [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) expression in [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) to increased DNA damage, reduced DNA repair, and topoisomerase inhibitor sensitivity [PMID:34290393](../papers/34290393.md).
- [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) — alterations independently associated with pN metastasis (multivariable OR 3.67, p=0.046); 8% in pN+ vs. 1.8% in pN- (p=0.006). SWI/SNF subunit; loss linked to poor oncologic outcomes and to co-occurrence with [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)/[STK11](../genes/STK11.md)/[KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md) in prior work.
- [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) — enriched in pN+ tumors (22% vs. 12%, p=0.024) and entered the multivariable selection but did not retain independence in the final model.
- [RBM10](../genes/RBM10.md) — significantly more altered in pN-negative tumors (17% vs. 8%, p=0.044).
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) — most prevalent overall; no significant pN-status difference for [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) or [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md); [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) nominally higher in pN+ (45% vs. 34%) but not flagged significant in the >=2% Fisher analysis.
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — copy number alterations enriched in pN-positive tumors (9% vs. 4%, p=0.039).
- [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) — amplifications enriched in pN-negative tumors (8% vs. 1.2%, p=0.029).
- [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) — most frequently copy-number altered gene in pN-negative tumors (10%).
- [MGA](../genes/MGA.md) — altered 2.1% pN- vs. 6% pN+ (asterisked as significant in OncoPrint).
- Other genes assessed at >=2% frequency without significant pN association: [TERT](../genes/TERT.md), [MET](../genes/MET.md), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md), [NKX2-1](../genes/NKX2-1.md), [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md), [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [PTPRT](../genes/PTPRT.md), [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [MED12](../genes/MED12.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [APC](../genes/APC.md).

## Clinical implications

- **Risk-stratification before surgery.** The authors propose that combining preoperative clinical features (CT morphology, SUVmax, clinical stage) with targetable preoperative-biopsy genomic data ([SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) and [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) alteration status) could identify clinical stage I/II [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) patients at high risk of occult LN metastasis who might benefit from neoadjuvant therapy rather than upfront resection [PMID:34290393](../papers/34290393.md).
- **APOBEC enrichment as immunotherapy biomarker.** APOBEC signature enrichment in pN+ tumors is discussed as a potential predictor of immunotherapy response in NSCLC.
- **No actionable target shift.** Frequency of canonical targetable LUAD drivers did not differ by pN status, so the model is for surgical-planning risk stratification rather than redirecting first-line targeted therapy.

## Limitations & open questions

- NGS performed on **single-region sampling of the primary tumor**; intratumoral heterogeneity in LUAD may cause this to underrepresent clonal complexity.
- Genomic analyses were on **surgical specimens, not preoperative biopsies**. The model's clinical utility presumes biopsy NGS is feasible, which the authors argue is now realistic at 80-90% success rates but is not demonstrated here.
- **Modest [absolute](../methods/absolute.md) frequency** of [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) and [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) alterations (8% and 7% in pN+) limits population impact, especially in never-smokers where these genes are rarely altered.
- **External validation required** — the model has not been tested in an independent cohort.
- Never-smoker subgroup is **underpowered** to test the same gene-level associations; small sample (n=110 never-smokers total, of whom only 19 were pN+) plus lower TMB obscures any signal.
- pN1 and pN2 patients were pooled as "pN-positive"; the model does not stratify the magnitude of nodal disease.
