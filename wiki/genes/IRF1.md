# IRF1

## Overview

IRF1 is an interferon regulatory transcription factor central to Th1/IFNγ signaling. In the corpus it is a component of the 20-gene Immunologic Constant of Rejection (ICR) immune signature.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- No somatic alteration claims; IRF1 is used as an expression component of the ICR immune-classification signature [PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md) — IRF1 expression contributes to the ICR signature that outperforms CMS and MSI classifications for prognosis in primary colon cancer (n=348) [PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-expressed as part of the ICR Th1 module with [IFNG](../genes/IFNG.md), [TBX21](../genes/TBX21.md), [CD8A](../genes/CD8A.md), [CD8B](../genes/CD8B.md), [IL12B](../genes/IL12B.md), and [STAT1](../genes/STAT1.md) [PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- ICR-high status (including IRF1) correlates with immunotherapy response in other tumor types referenced by the authors; not directly tested in colon cancer [PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md).

## Open questions

- Whether IRF1 expression alone adds prognostic value beyond the composite ICR signature is not addressed [PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md).
