# TYK2

## Overview

TYK2 (Tyrosine Kinase 2) is a member of the JAK family of non-receptor tyrosine kinases involved in cytokine signaling, particularly type I interferon and IL-12 pathways. In invasive lobular carcinoma ([ILC](../cancer_types/ILC.md)), TYK2 expression was identified as a marker of the immune-related mRNA subtype in a TCGA/METABRIC integrated genomic analysis.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Identified as a marker of the immune-related ILC mRNA subtype in a TCGA/METABRIC integrated analysis of invasive lobular carcinoma [PMID:26451490](../papers/26451490.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-expressed with JAK2, IDO1, and IFNG as markers of the immune-related ILC mRNA subtype [PMID:26451490](../papers/26451490.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- TYK2 expression in the immune-related ILC subtype may indicate relevance of cytokine/JAK-STAT pathway modulation; no specific drug claims reported [PMID:26451490](../papers/26451490.md)

## Open questions

- Whether TYK2 expression in immune-related ILC predicts benefit from immunotherapy or JAK inhibitors requires prospective investigation [PMID:26451490](../papers/26451490.md)
