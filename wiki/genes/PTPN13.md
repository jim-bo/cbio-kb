# PTPN13

## Overview

PTPN13 (Protein Tyrosine Phosphatase Non-Receptor Type 13, also known as FAP-1 or PTPL1) is a large non-receptor tyrosine phosphatase with PDZ domains. It has tumor suppressor functions and is implicated in apoptosis regulation. In cancer genomics it has been identified as a structural deletion in metastatic cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Structural deletion (DEL) detected by LINX/PURPLE in metastatic [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md) WGS cohort (n=25) [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **CSCC (cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma):** structural deletion in metastatic nodal disease WGS cohort [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Deleted alongside [PTPRD](../genes/PTPRD.md), [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [NEGR1](../genes/NEGR1.md), [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md), and [APC](../genes/APC.md) in the LINX/PURPLE structural variant calls in metastatic CSCC [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting reported in corpus.

## Open questions

- Whether PTPN13 deletion is a driver or passenger in CSCC metastasis is unresolved; functional validation is lacking [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)
