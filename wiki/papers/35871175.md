**PMID:** 35871175 · **DOI:** [10.1038/s41467-022-31780-9](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-022-31780-9) · **Journal:** Nature Communications (2022)

## TL;DR

Wu et al. report the largest pan-cancer somatic-mutation landscape of an Asian (predominantly Chinese, 92% Han) cohort to date: 10,194 solid-tumor patients across 25 principal tumor types profiled with the OrigiMed [CSYS 450-gene NGS panel](../methods/csys-450-gene-panel.md) under CLIA/CAP. They compare allele frequencies, TMB, MSI, gene fusions, and OncoKB-defined actionable variants against the [MSK-IMPACT 2017 cohort](../datasets/msk_impact_2017.md) (n=2,820 per arm after propensity-score matching) and TCGA, finding broadly similar mutation spectra but disease-specific differences (notably enrichment of [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) in [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) in [IHCH](../cancer_types/IHCH.md), and depletion of [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md) in [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) in the Chinese cohort). 64% of patients harbored at least one OncoKB Level 1–4 actionable variant. Data are deposited as the [pan_origimed_2020](../datasets/pan_origimed_2020.md) cBioPortal study.

## Cohort & data

- **Patients screened:** 11,553 across 25 principal tumor types and >100 subtypes from China; 31 ethnicities represented (92% Han) [PMID:35871175](../papers/35871175.md).
- **Final analyzed cohort:** 10,194 (88%) tumor + matched-blood pairs after excluding insufficient tumor content, low DNA yield, or sequencing failure.
- **Geography:** 41% East China, 29% South China.
- **Stage:** 55% advanced (stage III/IV), 35% early (pre-cancer or stage I/II).
- **Treatment status:** 76% treatment-naive, 16% pretreated, 8% unknown.
- **Principal tumor types (% of cohort):** [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) 20%, [COADREAD](../cancer_types/COADREAD.md) 12%, [LIHC](../cancer_types/LIHC.md) 11%, [STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md) 8%, [ESCA](../cancer_types/ESCA.md) 6%, [STS](../cancer_types/STS.md) 6%, [IHCH](../cancer_types/IHCH.md) 5%, [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md) 5%, [EHCH](../cancer_types/EHCH.md) 3%, [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) 3%.
- **Assay:** OrigiMed [CSYS 450-gene NGS panel](../methods/csys-450-gene-panel.md) targeting ~2.6 Mb of coding exons of 450 genes plus the [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) promoter and [select](../methods/select.md) introns of 39 rearrangement-prone genes; CLIA-certified, CAP-accredited.
- **Sequencing:** Median tumor unique coverage 1,202×; matched normal blood mean unique coverage 300×; Illumina NextSeq 500 / NovaSeq 6000.
- **Variant calling:** SNVs via [MuTect](../methods/mutect.md), InDels via [Pindel](../methods/pindel.md), CNVs via [EXCAVATOR](../methods/excavator-cnv.md); fusions via in-house algorithm; all variants manually reviewed in IGV.
- **PD-L1:** Immunohistochemistry with anti-PD-L1 clone 28-8 (Abcam ab205921, 1:300); TPS ≥1% positive (analog to but distinct from [22C3](../methods/pd-l1-ihc-22c3.md)).
- **Comparator cohorts:** [MSK-IMPACT 2017](../datasets/msk_impact_2017.md) (n=10,366; 266 common genes; aOM vs MSK = 2,820 per arm after PSM on primary/metastatic, sampling, gender, smoke) and [TCGA PanCancer Atlas + ovarian cancer](../datasets/ov_tcga_pub.md) (aOM vs aTCGA = 1,008 per arm; SNV/InDel/truncation only).
- **Data availability:** Deposited on cBioPortal as study [pan_origimed_2020](../datasets/pan_origimed_2020.md).

## Key findings

- **Variant inventory in the analyzed cohort:** 80,703 SNVs/InDels; 19,192 truncations; 17,779 gene amplifications; 1,688 homozygous deletions; 3,111 gene fusions/rearrangements [PMID:35871175](../papers/35871175.md).
- **Top recurrently altered genes (pan-cohort frequency):** [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) 58%, [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) 18%, [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) 14%, [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) 13%, [APC](../genes/APC.md) 13%, [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) 12%, [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) 11%. Most common point hotspots: [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) G12, [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) L858, [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) R273.
- **TP53 hotspots:** R273, R175, R248 are the top three. R175H/L most common in [COADREAD](../cancer_types/COADREAD.md) (8%) and [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md) (6%); R249S in [LIHC](../cancer_types/LIHC.md) (10%) and [IHCH](../cancer_types/IHCH.md) (3%).
- **Significant pairwise co-occurrence:** [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) and [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) at the SNV/InDel and CNV level — an unusual pattern relative to the canonical mutual exclusivity reported in Western LUAD.
- **CNV peaks:** High-frequency [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)/CDKN2B deletion; [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) amplification; [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) amplification; [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) amplification; chr 11q13.3 ([CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md)/[FGF3](../genes/FGF3.md)/[FGF4](../genes/FGF4.md)/[FGF19](../genes/FGF19.md)) amplification.
- **Tumor-type CNV enrichment vs other types:** [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) amplification in [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) (24 vs 2%, FDR=7.645×10⁻¹⁰⁵); 11q13.3 amplification in [ESCA](../cancer_types/ESCA.md) (43 vs 4%, FDR=3.553×10⁻³⁰¹).
- **Fusions detected:** 513 fusion events spanning 31 driver genes. [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) (n=139), [ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md) (n=51), [RET](../genes/RET.md) (n=50), [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md)/[FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) (n=50), [NTRK1](../genes/NTRK1.md)/[NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md) (n=30), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) (n=12) were broadly distributed; [EWSR1](../genes/EWSR1.md) restricted to sarcomas; [TFE3](../genes/TFE3.md) restricted to [KIRC](../cancer_types/KIRC.md). Novel partners reported include GRIK2-ROS1, PARP12-BRAF, KIF13B-[MET](../genes/MET.md), LRRC28-NTRK3, and new exonic boundaries in KIF5B-ALK and EML4-ALK relative to the Quiver database.
- **Clinical-feature associations (FDR<0.05):** In [COADREAD](../cancer_types/COADREAD.md), 270 / 100 differentially mutated genes between younger/early-stage vs older/advanced (consistent with MSI-H and [POLE](../genes/POLE.md)-associated hypermutation in young early-stage CRC). In [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md), female nonsmokers with early-stage disease enriched for [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) mutations; male smokers with advanced disease enriched for [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md). Younger [STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md) patients had more [CDH1](../genes/CDH1.md); older more [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)/[NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md)/[FAT4](../genes/FAT4.md). Younger [LIHC](../cancer_types/LIHC.md) patients had more [TP53](../genes/TP53.md); younger [KIRC](../cancer_types/KIRC.md) more [TFE3](../genes/TFE3.md); younger bone sarcoma more [VEGFA](../genes/VEGFA.md); older counterparts had [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md), [TERT](../genes/TERT.md), and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) respectively.
- **aOM vs MSK (PSM-matched, n=2,820 each, 15 tumor types, 266 shared genes):** Only 12 tumor-type:gene pairs were significantly different (FDR<0.05). Most differences localized to lung adenocarcinoma and hepatobiliary tumors — notably [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md): [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) (higher in aOM), [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md): [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md) (lower in aOM), and [IHCH](../cancer_types/IHCH.md): [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md). [COADREAD](../cancer_types/COADREAD.md): [APC](../genes/APC.md) was virtually identical (71.9% vs 72.7%, FDR=1); [SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md): [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) 84.2 vs 71.1%, FDR=1.
- **aOM vs aTCGA (PSM-matched, n=1,008 each, 9 tumor types):** 6 tumor-type:gene pairs differed (FDR<0.05); 3 trended consistently with the aOM-vs-MSK comparison (higher CRC:TP53, higher LUAD:EGFR, lower LUAD:[KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md) in aOM).
- **TMB:** TMB-H defined as ≥10 Muts/Mb per [KEYNOTE-158](../themes/keynote-158.md). Overall density similar between aOM and MSK; a long tail of 119 samples with TMB≥40 in aOM. Significant tumor-type differences vs MSK for 7+ tumor types (p<0.05).
- **MSI:** 186 MSI-H samples (~2% of cohort); 55% (102/186) in [COADREAD](../cancer_types/COADREAD.md), 26% in [STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md), 7% in [IHCH](../cancer_types/IHCH.md).
- **Combined IO biomarker positivity (MSI-H OR TMB-H OR PD-L1 TPS≥1%) in 2,723 evaluable tumors:** 30.3% (824/2,723); highest in [SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md) (48%, 24/50), [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) (46%, 298/648), [ESCA](../cancer_types/ESCA.md) (34%, 80/235).
- **[CD274](../genes/CD274.md) amplification:** 85 (1%) tumors had [CD274](../genes/CD274.md) amplification (copy ≥6); co-occurred with [PDCD1LG2](../genes/PDCD1LG2.md) (89%) and [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md) (82%) amplifications on 9p24.3-9p22.2; 30 evaluable cases had a 70% PD-L1 IHC positive rate; 78% harbored concurrent [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations.
- **OncoKB actionability:** 64% of patients (n=6,498) harbored at least one Level 1–4 actionable variant when TMB-H was included (Level 1: 32%; 2: 1%; 3A: 1%; 3B: 13%; 4: 16%). Excluding TMB-H, 58% (Level 1: 17.9%; 2: 1.5%; 3A: 1.5%; 3B: 17.8%; 4: 19.2%). Removing TMB-H drops Level 1 to 18%. Of 3,696 patients without Level 1–4 variants, 4% were PD-L1 IHC positive — candidates for checkpoint blockade outside the OncoKB framework.
- **TMB-H × fusion in [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md):** Negative association — 3% fusion frequency in TMB-H vs 13% in TMB-L (P=1.31×10⁻¹¹), driven mostly by [ALK](../genes/ALK.md). MSI-H × fusion: positive association (6% vs 1%, P=0.04), driven mostly by [NTRK1](../genes/NTRK1.md)/[NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md).

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — 58% pan-cohort SNV/InDel; hotspots R273, R175, R248; R249S enriched in [LIHC](../cancer_types/LIHC.md) (10%) and [IHCH](../cancer_types/IHCH.md) (3%) consistent with aflatoxin signature.
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — 18% pan-cohort; G12 hotspot; significantly elevated in [IHCH](../cancer_types/IHCH.md) vs MSK; G12C in 2.7% of [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) ([sotorasib](../drugs/sotorasib.md) Level 1).
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) — 13% pan-cohort; elevated in [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) vs MSK and TCGA; L858R 20% and exon 19 deletion 19% within NSCLC; G719 mutations in 3% of NSCLC; pairwise co-occurrence with [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) noted (atypical for Western cohorts).
- [APC](../genes/APC.md) — 13% pan-cohort; 72% of [COADREAD](../cancer_types/COADREAD.md), indistinguishable from MSK.
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — 12%; deep deletion peak in CNV analysis; LoF in 20% of [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md) (CDK4/6 inhibitor candidate per OncoKB).
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — 11%; canonical hotspots (C420R, E542K, E545A/D/G/K, H1047L/R/Y, Q546E/R) actionable to [alpelisib](../drugs/alpelisib.md) in 31% of [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) and 7.4% of CRC.
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) — amplification in 24% of [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) (vs 2% in other types); HER2-amp BRCA actionable with [lapatinib](../drugs/lapatinib.md), [trastuzumab](../drugs/trastuzumab.md)+[pertuzumab](../drugs/pertuzumab.md), [neratinib](../drugs/neratinib.md), margetuximab, and [trastuzumab](../drugs/trastuzumab.md)+[tucatinib](../drugs/tucatinib.md). [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) GoF mutations in 3% of NSCLC actionable with T-DM1 ([ado-trastuzumab-emtansine](../drugs/ado-trastuzumab-emtansine.md)) and [trastuzumab-deruxtecan](../drugs/trastuzumab-deruxtecan.md).
- [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) fusions — 6.7% of NSCLC; actionable with [crizotinib](../drugs/crizotinib.md), [ceritinib](../drugs/ceritinib.md), [alectinib](../drugs/alectinib.md).
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) truncating mutations — 7.9% [LIHC](../cancer_types/LIHC.md), 9.9% [IHCH](../cancer_types/IHCH.md), 7.2% [STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md); PLX2853 / [tazemetostat](../drugs/tazemetostat.md) (Level 4).
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E — 4.4% of [COADREAD](../cancer_types/COADREAD.md); [encorafenib](../drugs/encorafenib.md)+[cetuximab](../drugs/cetuximab.md) Level 1.
- [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) LoF — 4.9% of LIHC; [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md) Level 4.
- [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md) amplification — 10.9% of [STS](../cancer_types/STS.md); [abemaciclib](../drugs/abemaciclib.md)/[palbociclib](../drugs/palbociclib.md) Level 4.
- [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md) — significantly *lower* in aOM [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) vs MSK and TCGA.
- [CD274](../genes/CD274.md)/[PDCD1LG2](../genes/PDCD1LG2.md)/[JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md) — co-amplified 9p24.3-9p22.2 cluster; CD274 amplification in 1% of tumors, 70% PD-L1 IHC positive.
- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) — promoter mutations captured by the panel; 14% pan-cohort.
- [TFE3](../genes/TFE3.md) — fusion-restricted to [KIRC](../cancer_types/KIRC.md) (Xp11 translocation [RCC](../cancer_types/RCC.md)).
- [EWSR1](../genes/EWSR1.md) — fusion-restricted to sarcomas.
- [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md), [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md), [NTRK1](../genes/NTRK1.md), [NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md), [RET](../genes/RET.md), [ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md), [MET](../genes/MET.md) — pan-cancer fusion drivers with multiple novel partner genes catalogued.

## Clinical implications

- **Actionability framework for Asian patients:** ~64% of Chinese solid-tumor patients harbor an OncoKB Level 1–4 variant including TMB-H — a baseline against which institutional precision-oncology programs in China can benchmark.
- **NSCLC:** [erlotinib](../drugs/erlotinib.md) ± [ramucirumab](../drugs/ramucirumab.md) for EGFR L858R / exon 19 del; [afatinib](../drugs/afatinib.md) for G719; [sotorasib](../drugs/sotorasib.md) for KRAS G12C; ALK-fusion TKIs; [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md) for TMB-H. TMB-H × fusion mutual exclusivity argues against combining IO with ALK-TKIs in many TMB-H tumors.
- **CRC:** [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md) for MSI-H (8.3% of cases); [nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md)±[ipilimumab](../drugs/ipilimumab.md) for MSI-H; [encorafenib](../drugs/encorafenib.md)+[cetuximab](../drugs/cetuximab.md) for [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E; MEK inhibitors ([binimetinib](../drugs/binimetinib.md), [cobimetinib](../drugs/cobimetinib.md), [trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md)) for KRAS GoF; [alpelisib](../drugs/alpelisib.md) for [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) GoF.
- **HER2-amp BRCA (24%):** dual HER2 blockade ([trastuzumab](../drugs/trastuzumab.md)+[pertuzumab](../drugs/pertuzumab.md)), [lapatinib](../drugs/lapatinib.md), [neratinib](../drugs/neratinib.md), [tucatinib](../drugs/tucatinib.md).
- **[PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md):** [abemaciclib](../drugs/abemaciclib.md)/[palbociclib](../drugs/palbociclib.md)/[ribociclib](../drugs/ribociclib.md) for [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) LoF (20%); MEK inhibitors for KRAS (74% of PAAD have KRAS GoF actionable to MEKi at Level 3B/4).
- **LIHC:** [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md) for [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) LoF; [tazemetostat](../drugs/tazemetostat.md) for [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) truncation; [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md) for TMB-H (15.8% of LIHC are TMB-H Level-1 candidates).
- **Triple-biomarker IO triage (MSI-H ∨ TMB-H ∨ PD-L1+):** captures 48% of [SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md), 46% of NSCLC, 34% of [ESCA](../cancer_types/ESCA.md) — argues for broad biomarker-panel testing rather than single-marker IHC.
- **CD274 amplification as an orthogonal IO biomarker:** ~1% of tumors qualify even when MSI/TMB/PD-L1 IHC are negative; consider when first-line biomarkers fail.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Confounders only partially controlled.** PSM balanced primary/metastatic status, sampling method, gender, smoke, age, stage and grade but the authors acknowledge unmeasured confounders persist (sequencing depth, tumor purity, subtype heterogeneity, ancestry beyond Han).
- **Methodological heterogeneity vs TCGA.** Only SNVs/InDels/truncations were comparable to TCGA (different platforms, callers, and variant criteria); CNVs and fusions could not be benchmarked against TCGA in this study.
- **TMB algorithm.** No consensus pipeline for clinical TMB; authors used in-house calculation against [KEYNOTE-158](../themes/keynote-158.md) thresholds — TMB-H values are panel-specific and not portable between assays.
- **MSI method.** In-house bioinformatic MSI caller (>15% unstable loci) rather than the canonical Bethesda panel or MSIsensor — cross-study MSI-H rate comparisons require caution.
- **PD-L1 antibody clone.** Clone 28-8 (used here) is not the regulatory companion diagnostic ([22C3 / Dako](../methods/pd-l1-ihc-22c3.md) is); cross-trial PD-L1 numbers will differ.
- **Coverage of the panel.** Targets 450 cancer-related genes (~2.6 Mb), so signature analysis (mutational signatures, HRD, large-scale rearrangements) is limited compared to WGS/WES studies.
- **No outcome data.** The cohort is cross-sectional somatic landscape; survival, response to therapy, and longitudinal resistance trajectories are not reported.
- **Open question:** How much of the EGFR–KRAS co-occurrence in this cohort is biological (Asian-specific Han subset selection on EGFR-driven LUAD) versus tumor-heterogeneity / subclonal [admixture](../methods/admixture.md) artifact requiring single-cell or multiregion sequencing to resolve.
- **Open question:** Whether the lower [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md) frequency in Chinese [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) translates to differential sensitivity to checkpoint blockade or KEAP1-pathway therapeutics relative to North American patients.
