# CBFA2T3

## Overview

CBFA2T3 (Core-Binding Factor Subunit Alpha-2 Translocated To 3, also known as ETO2 or MTG16) is a transcriptional corepressor. In acute megakaryoblastic leukemia ([AMKL](../cancer_types/AMKL.md)), the CBFA2T3-GLIS2 fusion gene — arising from an inv(16)(p13.3q24.3) — defines a biologically and clinically distinct disease subtype associated with poor prognosis.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- CBFA2T3-GLIS2 fusion in acute megakaryoblastic leukemia (AMKL); identified as a diagnostic marker and poor-prognosis indicator; supported recommendation for bone-marrow transplant in the PIPseq pediatric precision-oncology cohort (Table 3) [PMID:28007021](../papers/28007021.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[AMKL](../cancer_types/AMKL.md)**: CBFA2T3-GLIS2 fusion is diagnostic of a specific AMKL subtype and confers poor prognosis; finding prompted bone-marrow transplant recommendation [PMID:28007021](../papers/28007021.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- No co-mutation data beyond AMKL subtype assignment in this corpus.

## Therapeutic relevance

- CBFA2T3-GLIS2-positive AMKL has poor outcomes with standard therapy; bone-marrow transplant recommended based on poor-prognosis designation [PMID:28007021](../papers/28007021.md)

## Open questions

- Targeted therapies specifically addressing the CBFA2T3-GLIS2 oncoprotein remain under investigation.
