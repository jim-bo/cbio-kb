# futibatinib

## Overview

Futibatinib is a covalent, irreversible pan-FGFR inhibitor targeting [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md), [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md), [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md), and [FGFR4](../genes/FGFR4.md). Unlike reversible FGFR inhibitors ([pemigatinib](../drugs/pemigatinib.md), [infigratinib](../drugs/infigratinib.md)), its covalent mechanism of action retains activity against secondary kinase-domain resistance mutations that commonly emerge on reversible agents. Approved for [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) fusion/rearrangement-positive intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma (iCCA) based on the FOENIX-CCA2 phase II trial.

## Evidence in the corpus

- [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) fusion/rearrangement-positive iCCA: FOENIX-CCA2 phase II ORR 42.0%, mPFS 9.0 mo, mOS 21.7 mo; as a covalent irreversible FGFR inhibitor it retains activity against on-target secondary FGFR2 kinase-domain resistance mutations (N550, V565) that emerge under reversible inhibitors [PMID:25526346](../papers/25526346.md)
- Cited as an FGFR2-targeted agent that synergizes with [cisplatin](../drugs/cisplatin.md) in FGFR2-rearranged iCCA, proposed as a backbone for combination strategies in cholangiocarcinoma treatment [PMID:25608663](../papers/25608663.md)

## Resistance mechanisms

- Secondary [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) kinase-domain mutations (N550, V565) that confer resistance to reversible inhibitors ([pemigatinib](../drugs/pemigatinib.md), [infigratinib](../drugs/infigratinib.md)) may remain sensitive to futibatinib due to its covalent mechanism [PMID:25526346](../papers/25526346.md)
