# MIR21

## Overview

MIR21 (MicroRNA 21) is one of the most widely overexpressed microRNAs in human cancer. It targets multiple tumor suppressor genes including [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), PDCD4, and [SPRY2](../genes/SPRY2.md), promoting cell survival, proliferation, and invasion. In lung cancer, MIR21 is located within a novel focal amplification region identified by TCGA pan-lung analysis.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- MIR21 resides within a novel focal amplification region (together with [TUBD1](../genes/TUBD1.md)) in lung adenocarcinoma identified in pan-lung cancer TCGA analysis (n=1144) [PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Lung adenocarcinoma: focal amplification of MIR21/TUBD1 locus [PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Focal amplification co-occurs with TUBD1 at the same genomic locus in lung ADC [PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- MIR21 amplification in lung ADC may represent an oncogenic driver with potential as a therapeutic target; specific therapeutic strategies not yet established in this cohort [PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)

## Open questions

- Whether MIR21 or TUBD1 is the primary target of the focal amplification at this locus in lung ADC has not been resolved [PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)
