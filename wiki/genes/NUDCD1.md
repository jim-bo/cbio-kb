# NUDCD1

## Overview

NUDCD1 (NudC domain containing 1), also known as OVA66, is located at chromosome 8q22.2. It promotes epithelial-to-mesenchymal transition (EMT) and apoptosis evasion via PI3K/AKT, ERK1/2-MAPK, and IGF-1R-MAPK pathways. In [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) it was nominated as a cis-driver candidate based on copy-number amplification and multi-omic evidence.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- NUDCD1 (OVA66, 8q22.2) nominated as a cis-driver candidate in [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) based on copy-number amplification co-occurring with mRNA/protein upregulation; promotes EMT and apoptosis evasion via PI3K/AKT, ERK1/2-MAPK, and IGF-1R-MAPK pathways; identified in a European [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) proteogenomics cohort (n=122 WES) [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-located at 8q22.2 amplification peak with [YWHAZ](../genes/YWHAZ.md) (14-3-3 zeta); both are cis-driver candidates in [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- PI3K/AKT and MAPK pathway dependence suggested by EMT-promoting function; no direct therapeutic trial data available from the corpus.

## Open questions

- Functional studies directly attributing the 8q22.2 amplification phenotype specifically to NUDCD1 (versus co-amplified [YWHAZ](../genes/YWHAZ.md) or other genes) are lacking.
