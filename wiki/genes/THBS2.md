# THBS2

## Overview

THBS2 (Thrombospondin 2) encodes a secreted extracellular matrix glycoprotein that mediates cell-matrix interactions, angiogenesis inhibition, and cell proliferation. In cancer, THBS2 is upregulated and shed into circulation, making it a candidate serum biomarker for early detection. In pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDAC), THBS2 has been nominated as one of the top candidate early-detection biomarkers based on multi-omic proteogenomic profiling.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- THBS2 protein identified as one of 12 secreted proteins (from a set of 21 PDAC-vs-normal upregulated proteins) that are candidate serum/pancreatic-juice early-detection markers; 14/21 in the full set validated by DIA mass spectrometry in the CPTAC 2021 PDAC cohort (n=140 treatment-naive resected tumors) [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md): THBS2 protein upregulated in PDAC vs normal pancreatic tissue; classified as a secreted protein with early-detection potential based on proteogenomic profiling and DIA mass spectrometry validation [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- THBS2 upregulation co-occurs with upregulation of LAMC2, [LOXL2](../genes/LOXL2.md), [GSDMB](../genes/GSDMB.md), and [LECT2](../genes/LECT2.md) as part of the 12-secreted-protein early-detection candidate set in PDAC [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- THBS2 is nominated as a candidate serum biomarker for early PDAC detection rather than a direct therapeutic target; its utility in blood-based detection panels requires prospective clinical validation [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Open questions

- Prospective clinical validation of THBS2 as a serum early-detection marker for PDAC is required; findings are correlative from treatment-naive resected tumors and cannot be generalized to early-stage or metastatic disease without further study [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)
