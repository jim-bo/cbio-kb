# GAS7

## Overview

GAS7 (growth arrest specific 7) is a member of the pombe Cdc15 homology (PCH) family of proteins involved in cytoskeletal organization and differentiation. It has been described as a [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) (MLL) translocation partner in therapy-related acute myeloid leukemia, particularly following topoisomerase II inhibitor exposure in pediatric cancer patients.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Identified as a KMT2A translocation partner in pediatric neuroblastoma patients who developed therapy-related AML/MDS; KMT2A-GAS7 rearrangement detected by RNA capture in pre-transformation bone marrow samples [PMID:35078859](../papers/35078859.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Therapy-related AML/MDS in pediatric neuroblastoma survivors: KMT2A-GAS7 fusion detected as a pre-transformation event, associated with topoisomerase-II inhibitor exposure [PMID:35078859](../papers/35078859.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- KMT2A rearrangements (including GAS7 fusions) cluster with topoisomerase-II exposure history; observed alongside KMT2A-ELL, KMT2A-AFF1, KMT2A-FRYL, and novel KMT2A-PRDM10/DDX6 fusions [PMID:35078859](../papers/35078859.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- KMT2A-GAS7 fusions detected a median of 15 months before clinical leukemia diagnosis by RNA capture-based bone marrow surveillance; early detection may enable risk-adapted intervention in pediatric neuroblastoma survivors [PMID:35078859](../papers/35078859.md)

## Open questions

- Relative leukemogenic potential and clinical prognosis of KMT2A-GAS7 compared with more common KMT2A partners (e.g., KMT2A-AFF1, KMT2A-ELL) in therapy-related [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md) remain to be characterized in larger series [PMID:35078859](../papers/35078859.md)
