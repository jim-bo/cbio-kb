# SKIC3

## Overview

SKIC3 (formerly TTC37) encodes a tetratricopeptide repeat-containing subunit of the SKI cytoplasmic ribosome-associated RNA-surveillance (RRQC) complex. Together with [SKIC2](../genes/SKIC2.md), SKIC3 forms the catalytic core of the SKI complex responsible for 3'-to-5' degradation of aberrant mRNAs via the exosome. Germline SKIC3 mutations cause Trichohepatoenteric Syndrome (THES), a rare congenital diarrhea disorder. In cancer, SKIC3 is downregulated in MSI cell lines and is associated with [WRN](../genes/WRN.md) synthetic lethality in the MSI context.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- SKIC2 (SKIV2L) and SKIC3 (TTC37) form the SKI cytoplasmic ribosome-associated RNA-surveillance complex; both proteins are downregulated in MSI CCLE cell lines and are associated with WRN knockdown sensitivity; their downregulation is part of a broader MSI proteomic signature that includes mismatch repair and H3K4 methyltransferase complexes [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Protein downregulation co-occurs with MSI phenotype and with downregulation of SKIC2, MLH1/PMS2 (MMR), and H3K4 methyltransferases ([KMT2B](../genes/KMT2B.md), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), [SETD1B](../genes/SETD1B.md)) in MSI cell lines [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- SKIC3 protein downregulation, as part of the broader MSI proteomic signature, may complement DNA-based MSI testing for selecting patients for WRN-directed therapy [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Open questions

- The mechanistic link between SKIC3 downregulation, [RPL22](../genes/RPL22.md) mutation, and MSI-associated ribosome quality control defects is not fully resolved [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
