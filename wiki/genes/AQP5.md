# AQP5

## Overview

AQP5 (Aquaporin 5) is a water channel protein expressed in secretory epithelia. In the colorectal pre-cancer context, AQP5 is part of a gastric/endodermal gene-expression program activated in serrated sessile colon lesions (SSLs), contributing to the metaplastic phenotype that distinguishes the serrated pathway from conventional adenomas.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Co-expressed as part of the gastric/endodermal program in sessile serrated colonocytes (SSCs) in the COLON MAP / HTAN pre-cancer atlas ([crc_hta11_htan_2021](../datasets/crc_hta11_htan_2021.md)); co-expressed with [TACSTD2](../genes/TACSTD2.md) (TROP2), [TFF2](../genes/TFF2.md), and [MSLN](../genes/MSLN.md) in the metaplastic serrated lineage [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [COADREAD](../cancer_types/COADREAD.md): AQP5 upregulation marks the BRAF-V600E-driven gastric-type metaplasia state in serrated polyps (HPs, SSLs) that can progress to MSI-H CRC [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-expressed with [MUC5AC](../genes/MUC5AC.md), TACSTD2, TFF2, MSLN in SSCs; this gastric metaplasia program is mutually exclusive with the WNT/LGR5 stem-cell program characteristic of conventional adenomas [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- AQP5 expression is a feature of the serrated/metaplastic lineage driven by BRAF-V600E; the broader gastric-program may represent a diagnostic marker for SSL classification [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Open questions

- Whether AQP5 itself is functionally required for serrated progression or is a passive marker of the metaplastic state is not established.
