# APC2

## Overview

APC2 ([APC](../genes/APC.md) Regulator Of WNT Signaling Pathway 2) is a paralog of APC that functions as a negative regulator of the WNT signaling pathway. In colorectal cancer, APC2 mutations have been identified as a non-APC mechanism for WNT pathway reactivation, particularly in MSI-H tumors where biallelic APC loss is less common.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Activating WNT-pathway mutations in 27% of MSI-H CRCs in the COLON MAP / HTAN pre-cancer atlas ([crc_hta11_htan_2021](../datasets/crc_hta11_htan_2021.md)), representing a non-APC mechanism of WNT reactivation in microsatellite-unstable tumors [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [COADREAD](../cancer_types/COADREAD.md): APC2 mutations observed in MSI-H colorectal carcinomas as an alternative WNT-pathway activation mechanism to APC loss [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- APC2 mutations occur in the context of WNT pathway activation in MSI-H CRCs where canonical APC truncation is less frequent (33% vs 100% in MSS CRCs) [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- WNT pathway activation via APC2 mutation may indicate susceptibility to WNT-targeted therapies; context within MSI-H tumors may also confer checkpoint-immunotherapy sensitivity [PMID:34910928](../papers/34910928.md)

## Open questions

- Whether APC2 mutations are driver events or passenger alterations in MSI-H CRCs with high background mutation rates remains to be determined.
