**PMID:** 32299819 · **DOI:** 10.1158/1078-0432.CCR-19-3959 · **Journal:** Clinical Cancer Research (2020)

## TL;DR

Hensley and colleagues prospectively sequenced tumors from 107 women with [uterine sarcoma](../cancer_types/USARC.md) at Memorial Sloan Kettering using the [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) targeted DNA panel, with [MSK-Fusion](../methods/msk-fusion.md) RNA sequencing in a subset. The cohort covered [uterine leiomyosarcoma](../cancer_types/ULMS.md) (uLMS, n=80), high-grade non-LMS (n=22), [low-grade endometrial stromal sarcoma](../cancer_types/LGESS.md) (LG-ESS, n=4), and STUMP (n=2). uLMS was dominated by loss-of-function alterations in [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (56%), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) (51%), and [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) (31%), with recurrent homozygous deletions of [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) (5%) and [PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md) (the highest rate of [PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md) homozygous deletion across all cancer types with ≥25 cases in the contemporary 15,816-patient MSK-IMPACT cohort). Potentially actionable alterations were identified in 45% (48/107) of patients; among uLMS patients with somatic [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) alterations, [PARP-inhibitor](../drugs/olaparib.md)-containing therapy produced sustained partial responses, supporting [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) as a therapeutically relevant target in uLMS.

## Cohort & data

- **Cohort:** 107 women with histologically confirmed uterine sarcoma profiled at Memorial Sloan Kettering between April 2014 and April 2017 under prospective IRB protocol NCT01775072 ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **Histologic breakdown:** [ULMS](../cancer_types/ULMS.md) n=80; high-grade non-LMS n=22 (further subclassified as [HG-ESS](../cancer_types/HGESS.md) n=7, undifferentiated n=5, high-grade adenosarcoma with sarcomatous overgrowth n=4, and other rare subtypes); [LGESS](../cancer_types/LGESS.md) n=4; STUMP n=2 ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **Stage / status:** 57% (n=61) FIGO stage I at diagnosis; 89% (n=95) recurrent or metastatic at time of sequencing; 94.3% (n=101) histologically high grade ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **Samples:** 39% (n=42) primary, 61% (n=66) metastatic ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **Cancer type / OncoTree code:** [USARC](../cancer_types/USARC.md).
- **Dataset:** [usarc_msk_2020](../datasets/usarc_msk_2020.md) (cBioPortal).
- **Assays:** [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) targeted DNA exon-capture (341-gene n=44, 410-gene n=44, 468-gene n=20 samples; ~646× average coverage); subset additionally profiled with [MSK-Fusion](../methods/msk-fusion.md) RNA panel for diagnostic work-up.
- **Computational methods:** [MSIsensor](../methods/msisensor.md) v0.2 for MSI scoring (≥10 = MSI-H), [FACETS](../methods/facets.md) v0.5.6 for allele-specific copy number / purity / ploidy, [mutational signature](../methods/mutational-signatures.md) decomposition on samples with ≥10 SNVs, and [IHC](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md) confirmation of MMR proteins ([MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md), [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md), [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md)) and of [PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md) loss in homozygous-deletion cases.
- **Annotation:** OncoKB used to assign actionability levels (as of October 2019); germline variant annotation across 76 cancer-predisposition genes via the MSK-IMPACT germline pipeline.

## Key findings

- **uLMS driver landscape:** Loss-of-function alterations or homozygous deletions of [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (56%), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) (51%), and [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) (31%) dominated [uLMS](../cancer_types/ULMS.md). The same genes were significantly less commonly altered in high-grade non-LMS (14%, 14%, and 5% respectively; p<0.01 for all) ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **BRCA2 homozygous deletions in uLMS:** Homozygous deletions of [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) occurred in ~5% of patients and were found exclusively in uLMS. Combined somatic BRCA2 alterations (deletions plus mutations) accounted for seven of the 107 patients ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **PDCD1 deletions enriched in uLMS:** uLMS had the highest rate of [PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md) homozygous deletions among all cancer types with ≥25 cases in the 15,816-patient MSK-IMPACT cohort. IHC in two informative cases (of seven) confirmed loss of PD-1 protein expression ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **Amplifications:** [MAP2K4](../genes/MAP2K4.md) and [RIT1](../genes/RIT1.md) amplifications were most common in uterine sarcomas relative to the contemporary MSK-IMPACT pan-cancer cohort. [RIT1](../genes/RIT1.md) was amplified in 24% (5/21) of high-grade non-LMS vs. 2.5% (2/80) of uLMS (p=0.004) ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **[PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) site-specific enrichment:** [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) alterations were more frequent in metastasis vs. primary samples (p=0.046; remained significant within uLMS, p=0.04) ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **[MED12](../genes/MED12.md) low in uLMS:** Only 11% (9/80) of uLMS harbored [MED12](../genes/MED12.md) mutations, vs. ~70% in benign uterine leiomyomas, arguing that uLMS rarely evolves from antecedent leiomyomas ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **uLMS vs. non-uterine [LMS](../cancer_types/LMS.md):** In a direct comparison with 68 non-uterine [LMS](../cancer_types/LMS.md) sequenced at MSK, [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) alterations were more common in non-uterine LMS (71% vs. 56%, p=0.01), [MAP2K4](../genes/MAP2K4.md) amplifications trended higher in non-uterine LMS (19% vs. 8%, p=0.06), and [MED12](../genes/MED12.md) mutations were exclusively in uLMS (11% vs. 0%, p=0.01). Compared with a small TCGA uLMS cohort (n=27), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) alterations were significantly more frequent in the MSK cohort (51% vs. 22%, p=0.02) ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **Whole-genome duplication:** 50.5% (50/99) of high-grade uterine sarcomas displayed WGD; none of the low-grade tumors did (p=0.04). WGD was not associated with overall survival ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **Two hypermutated MSI-H uLMS outliers:** Two uLMS cases had outlier TMB with MSI-H by MSIsensor and MMR-D mutational signatures, with IHC loss of [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md) and [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md) respectively. Neither had a somatic driver in these genes (etiology likely epigenetic/germline). Both achieved complete responses with multimodality therapy and remain disease-free at 5.9 and 13 years; neither received immune checkpoint inhibitors. Excluding these two outliers, uLMS TMB exceeded high-grade non-LMS TMB (2.5 vs. 1.1 mutations/Mb; p=0.05) ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **Germline [TP53](../genes/TP53.md):** Two patients carried pathogenic germline [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) variants (one with somatic biallelic inactivation, one with known Li-Fraumeni syndrome). No other known/likely pathogenic germline variants identified after anonymized analysis ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **Survival:** Median survival from diagnosis was 5.6 years (range 0.2–23.1). High-grade non-LMS had significantly worse survival than uLMS (3.4 vs. 6.3 years; HR=0.48, 95% CI 0.24–0.96, p=0.03). No common genomic alteration nor WGD was prognostic for [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) in univariate Cox regression ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **Actionability:** Potentially actionable alterations identified in 45% (48/107); 17% (8/48) received matched therapy, with two radiographic responses, one prolonged stable disease ([ALK](../genes/ALK.md) fusion case), and five progressors ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — loss-of-function mutations / homozygous deletions in 56% of uLMS; significantly more frequent in non-uterine LMS (71%, p=0.01). Two germline pathogenic carriers (one with Li-Fraumeni syndrome) ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — inactivated in 51% of uLMS vs. 22% in TCGA uLMS (p=0.02) and 14% in high-grade non-LMS (p<0.01) ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) — altered in 31% of uLMS vs. 5% of high-grade non-LMS (p<0.01) ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) — homozygous deletions in 5% of uLMS (exclusively in uLMS); seven total somatic BRCA2 alterations (four homozygous deletions + three somatic mutations); validation in five post-data-freeze uLMS patients with BRCA2 alterations treated with [PARP inhibitors](../drugs/olaparib.md) (including one complete radiographic response) ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) — discussed in Discussion as a potential mechanism of HRD via promoter hypermethylation; not directly assayed by MSK-IMPACT for methylation in this study ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — enriched in metastasis vs. primary samples (p=0.046; uLMS subset p=0.04) ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- [PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md) — recurrent homozygous deletions in uLMS, the highest frequency across all cancer types in the 15,816-patient MSK-IMPACT cohort; IHC confirmed PD-1 protein loss in two informative cases ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- [MAP2K4](../genes/MAP2K4.md) — amplifications most common in uterine sarcomas vs. pan-cancer; 8% (6/80) of uLMS vs. 19% (13/68) of non-uterine LMS (p=0.06) ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- [RIT1](../genes/RIT1.md) — amplifications enriched in high-grade non-LMS (24%, 5/21) vs. uLMS (2.5%, 2/80; p=0.004) ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- [MED12](../genes/MED12.md) — mutated in 11% (9/80) of uLMS vs. ~70% in benign leiomyomas; absent in non-uterine LMS (p=0.01) ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — two activating mutations in uLMS (V600E and the class-2 dimer-dependent K601E); modest response to BRAF/RAF inhibitors ([BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600 patient progressed in 4 weeks on an oral BRAF inhibitor; K601E patient achieved 4 months stable disease on a pan-RAF inhibitor) ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) — two [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) fusions reclassified as [inflammatory myofibroblastic tumor (IMT)](../cancer_types/IMT.md): one LBH-ALK fusion (initially missed by IMPACT DNA, then identified retrospectively; treated with [crizotinib](../drugs/crizotinib.md) with 30+ months disease stabilization) and one post-data-freeze ALK fusion (brief [crizotinib](../drugs/crizotinib.md) response, progression, then progression on [ceritinib](../drugs/ceritinib.md)) ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) — Y537S ligand-binding domain mutation in a metastatic [LGESS](../cancer_types/LGESS.md) patient on long-term aromatase inhibitor therapy; switching to [fulvestrant](../drugs/fulvestrant.md) produced tumor regression ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md) / [ZC3H7B](../genes/ZC3H7B.md) — a BCOR-ZC3H7B fusion in one case initially classified as high-grade myxoid uLMS was reclassified as BCOR-mutated high-grade stromal sarcoma; patient survived 3.4 years ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- [JAZF1](../genes/JAZF1.md) / [SUZ12](../genes/SUZ12.md) — one JAZF1-SUZ12 fusion identified by MSK-Fusion confirmed [LGESS](../cancer_types/LGESS.md) diagnosis ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- [YWHAE](../genes/YWHAE.md) / [NUTM2A](../genes/NUTM2A.md) / [NUTM2B](../genes/NUTM2B.md) — YWHAE/NUTM2 rearrangements identified by MSK-Fusion in two high-grade, non-pleomorphic stromal sarcomas ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- [LBH](../genes/LBH.md) — partner in the LBH-ALK fusion ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md) / [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md) — IHC loss of one each in the two MSI-H hypermutated uLMS cases; no somatic driver identified, suggesting epigenetic or germline etiology ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).

## Clinical implications

- **PARP inhibitors for BRCA2-altered uLMS:** Sustained partial responses observed with PARP-inhibitor-containing therapy in uLMS patients with somatic [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) alterations. Validation in five additional post-data-freeze uLMS patients with BRCA2 alterations all showed at least some radiographic regression, including one complete radiographic response; treatment durations ranged from 6 to 28 months. The authors argue [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) testing is justified in all uLMS patients ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **ALK inhibitors for ALK-fusion sarcomas reclassified as [IMT](../cancer_types/IMT.md):** [Crizotinib](../drugs/crizotinib.md) yielded 30+ months of radiographic stabilization in one patient and a brief radiographic response in another (who later progressed on [ceritinib](../drugs/ceritinib.md)), supporting that ALK fusion detection should prompt diagnostic reclassification to [IMT](../cancer_types/IMT.md) and consideration of ALK-targeted therapy ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **Selective estrogen receptor degraders for ESR1-mutant LG-ESS:** Switching from aromatase inhibitor to [fulvestrant](../drugs/fulvestrant.md) produced tumor regression in an [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) Y537S–mutant LG-ESS patient previously on >20 years of anti-estrogen therapy ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **BRAF inhibition has limited activity in BRAF-mutant uLMS:** The BRAF V600E uLMS patient progressed in 4 weeks on a first-generation BRAF inhibitor; the K601E (class 2 dimer-dependent) patient achieved 4 months stable disease on a pan-RAF inhibitor — consistent with prior characterization of K601E as resistant to first-generation BRAF inhibitors ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **Diagnostic reclassification by fusion detection:** Genomic profiling redirected histologic diagnosis in three cases — two uLMS reclassified as [IMT](../cancer_types/IMT.md) via ALK fusion and one uLMS reclassified as BCOR-mutated high-grade stromal sarcoma via BCOR-ZC3H7B fusion. JAZF1-SUZ12 and YWHAE/NUTM2 fusions were also used diagnostically ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **MSI-H phenotype in uLMS:** The two MSI-H hypermutated uLMS patients remain disease-free 5.9 and 13 years from diagnosis after multimodality management without immune checkpoint inhibitors, suggesting MSI-H may be prognostic for chemotherapy sensitivity and/or disease-free survival in uLMS ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **PDCD1 deletion as a potential immunotherapy biomarker:** Recurrent PDCD1 homozygous deletions in uLMS — confirmed by IHC loss — may help explain the paucity of objective responses to immune checkpoint inhibitors in published phase II uLMS trials ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Survival bias:** All patients had to be alive to enroll and consent for prospective sequencing, so the cohort likely over-represents patients with more favorable survival; patients dying very shortly after diagnosis may be under-represented with potentially distinct genomic profiles ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **Single-center cohort:** Sequenced at a single comprehensive cancer center (MSK) using one targeted DNA panel ([MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md)); generalizability to community settings and alternative assays is untested ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **Modest matched-therapy uptake:** Although 45% had potentially actionable alterations, only 17% of those (7% of the whole cohort) received matched therapy, limiting therapeutic claims ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **No prognostic genomic biomarkers identified:** Univariate Cox regression found no oncogenic alteration nor WGD significantly associated with OS, possibly reflecting cohort survival bias rather than true absence of effect ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **MSI-H etiology unresolved:** Neither MSI-H case had a somatic driver in [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md) or [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md); whether the cause is epigenetic silencing or germline mutation was not determined ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **[BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) promoter hypermethylation not assayed:** Authors hypothesize a subset of uLMS may be HRD-driven via BRCA1 promoter hypermethylation but did not directly test methylation status or its response to PARP inhibition ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **DNA-based fusion detection blind spots:** The first LBH-ALK fusion was initially missed by IMPACT DNA sequencing because the breakpoint was in an intron not commonly targeted for ALK fusions; orthogonal RNA-based assays like [MSK-Fusion](../methods/msk-fusion.md) remain important for fusion detection in sarcomas ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
- **Small low-grade subset:** Only n=4 LG-ESS and n=2 STUMP, limiting statistical power for low-grade-specific conclusions ([PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)).
