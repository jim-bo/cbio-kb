TL;DR

Liu et al. assembled a clinically annotated cohort of 144 patients with advanced melanoma treated with anti-PD1 immune-checkpoint blockade (ICB) and performed whole-exome and bulk transcriptome sequencing on pre-treatment tumors (mel_dfci_2019). They show that tumor mutational burden (TMB) as a response predictor is confounded by melanoma subtype, identify MHC-II-related antigen-presentation features (transcriptomic MHC-II HLA expression, TAP2 and MHC-I HLA locus amplifications) as novel predictors of response, and demonstrate that prior anti-CTLA4 (ipilimumab) exposure changes which features predict response. They then build two parsimonious logistic-regression models of intrinsic resistance — one for ipilimumab-pretreated tumors (MHC-II HLA + LDH + lymph-node metastasis; AUC 0.90) and one for ipilimumab-naive tumors (heterogeneity + ploidy + purity; AUC 0.77).

Cohort & data

  • n = 144 patients with advanced melanoma (after QC; 121 with RNA-seq) treated with anti-PD1 ICB (nivolumab n=59, 41.0%; pembrolizumab n=85, 59.0%). PMID:31792460
  • 206 patients initially identified; 144 passed WES QC, 121 passed RNA-seq QC (Extended Data Fig. 1). PMID:31792460
  • Cancer types: 72.9% cutaneous (SKCM, n=105), 13.2% occult (n=19), 6.9% acral (n=10), 6.9% mucosal (n=10) — collectively MEL. PMID:31792460
  • Prior ipilimumab: 41.7% (n=60); ipilimumab-naive: 58.3% (n=84). PMID:31792460
  • Stage: 75.0% M1c (n=108), 12.5% M1b, 5.6% M1a, 6.9% unresectable III. Elevated LDH in 49.3%. ECOG 0 in 68.8%. Median follow-up 29.9 months. PMID:31792460
  • Assays: pre-treatment tumor whole-exome-seq (matched normal) and bulk rna-seq; purity/ploidy via absolute; mutational-signatures; pathway analysis with gsea; immune deconvolution with cibersortx (CIBERSORT, LM22 signature matrix). PMID:31792460
  • Best objective response (RECIST v1.1): 45% PD, 14% SD, 3% MR, 26% PR, 12% CR; overall response rate 38%. PMID:31792460
  • Independent validation cohort for prior-ipilimumab MHC-II analysis: mel_iatlas_riaz_nivolumab_2017 (n=32; 15 ipilimumab-treated, 17 ipilimumab-naive). PMID:31792460

Key findings

  • Median nonsynonymous TMB was 6.5 mutations/Mb (IQR 2.0–14.4; 250.5 mutations/exome, IQR 77.75–578.5). PMID:31792460
  • Mutational frequencies in driver genes: BRAF 39%, NRAS 30%, NF1 17%. PMID:31792460
  • TMB was higher in responders (CR/PR, n=55) than progressors (PD, n=65): two-sided MWW P = 0.026. But TMB was not significantly different between responders and SD/MR (n=24): P = 0.14. PMID:31792460
  • TMB is confounded by melanoma subtype. Cutaneous + occult (n=124) had median 297.5 nonsynonymous mutations versus 58 for acral + mucosal (n=20); MWW P = 1.1×10⁻⁶. Stratified within subtype, TMB no longer separated responders from progressors (e.g. skin n=42/43, P=0.27; acral n=7/2, P=0.19). In multivariate logistic regression adjusting for subtype, TMB was not significant (P = 0.24). PMID:31792460
  • After multiple-hypothesis correction, no single gene mutation was significantly associated with response. PMID:31792460
  • TAP2 amplification (n=6) was exclusively in responders: Fisher’s exact P = 0.008. Amplification of MHC-I HLA-A/HLA-B/HLA-C (n=6) also exclusively in responders: Fisher’s P = 0.008. Combined TAP2/MHC-I HLA amplification (n=8): Fisher’s P = 0.001. PMID:31792460
  • LOH of JAK1 was associated with resistance: OR = 0.33, Fisher’s P = 0.02. PMID:31792460
  • LOH in B2M (16% responders vs 25% progressors, OR=0.6) and biallelic CDKN2A alteration (27% vs 38%, OR=0.6) were not significant. LOH in HLA-A/HLA-B/HLA-C and SERPINB3/SERPINB4 mutations were not associated with response. PMID:31792460
  • Expression of all 13 MHC-II HLA genes was higher in responders (collective two-sided binomial P = 0.0002); HLA-DMA, HLA-DMB, HLA-DOA, HLA-DOB individually passed MWW P<0.05. MHC-I HLA genes trended higher in responders (binomial P = 0.02) but no individual gene passed significance. PMID:31792460
  • gsea (Hallmark gene sets): 24 pathways enriched in responders at FDR q<0.1; 5 of top 6 were immune-related (IFN-γ response, allograft rejection, complement, inflammatory response, IL-6–JAK–STAT3). No pathways enriched in progressors. PMID:31792460
  • Tumor heterogeneity (proportion subclonal mutations) higher in progressors (MWW P = 0.02); ploidy lower in progressors (P = 0.04); purity higher in progressors (P = 0.04). PMID:31792460
  • Prior ipilimumab changes the predictor landscape. MHC-II HLA ssGSEA score was higher in responders versus progressors overall (P=0.03) and in the ipilimumab-treated subgroup (P=0.03) but not in the ipilimumab-naive subgroup (P=0.31). Higher heterogeneity and lower ploidy distinguished progressors only in ipilimumab-naive tumors (P=0.06 and P=0.004). PMID:31792460
  • IFN-γ and IFN-α response pathways were enriched in responders overall (FDR q<0.001) and in ipilimumab-treated subgroup (q<0.001) but not in ipilimumab-naive subgroup (q=0.13 and q=0.997). Replicated in validation cohort mel_iatlas_riaz_nivolumab_2017. PMID:31792460
  • Low MHC-II expression (median split) was associated with primary resistance in the ipilimumab-treated subgroup (Fisher’s P=0.02; OR = 9.9, 95% CI 1.5–63.7) but not the ipilimumab-naive subgroup (P=0.32; OR=1.9). PMID:31792460
  • Low immune infiltrate was strongly associated with intrinsic resistance in the ipilimumab-treated subgroup (Fisher’s P=0.02; OR = 12.9, 95% CI 1.3–125.8) but not in the ipilimumab-naive subgroup or overall cohort. PMID:31792460
  • Predictive model — ipilimumab-treated (n=34): MHC-II HLA + LDH + lymph-node metastasis. AUC = 0.90 (5-fold CV mean 0.83; empiric P<0.001). TMB did not significantly improve fit (P=0.10). High-versus-low model score stratified PFS (log-rank P=0.0001) and OS (log-rank P=0.001). PMID:31792460
  • Predictive model — ipilimumab-naive (n=84): heterogeneity + ploidy + purity. AUC = 0.77 (10-fold CV mean 0.73; empiric P=0.036). TMB did not improve fit (P=0.63). High-versus-low score stratified PFS (P=0.005) and OS (P<0.0001). PMID:31792460
  • Each model performed poorly on the opposite subgroup (AUC 0.49 and 0.54, respectively); interaction tests P=0.004 and P=0.018 — i.e. prior-ipilimumab status stratifies which model applies. PMID:31792460

Genes & alterations

  • BRAF, NRAS, NF1 — recurrent melanoma drivers; mutated in 39% / 30% / 17% of the cohort but not differentially associated with anti-PD1 response. PMID:31792460
  • TAP2 — focal amplification (chr 6p21, MHC region) found exclusively in responders (n=6/120; Fisher’s P=0.008). Four of six co-amplified with MHC-I HLA-A/B/C; two were more focal. TAP2 expression was higher in responders in the ipilimumab-treated subgroup (MWW P=0.02). PMID:31792460
  • HLA-A, HLA-B, HLA-C — focal amplification in the ~1.5 Mb MHC-I region (n=6) exclusively in responders (Fisher’s P=0.008). LOH in these genes was not associated with response (P=0.52, 0.57, 0.84). PMID:31792460
  • B2M — LOH in 9/55 (16%) responders versus 16/65 (25%) progressors (OR=0.6), not significant (Fisher’s P=0.37); only rare point mutations observed. PMID:31792460
  • TAP1 — only rare mutations in MHC-I antigen-presentation genes; no significant association with response. PMID:31792460
  • JAK1 — LOH significantly associated with resistance (OR=0.33; Fisher’s P=0.02), confirming prior IFN-γ-pathway resistance findings. PMID:31792460
  • CDKN2A — biallelic alteration in 27% responders versus 38% progressors (OR=0.6), not significant (P=0.24). PMID:31792460
  • SERPINB3, SERPINB4 — mutations not associated with response (Fisher’s P=0.51 and P=1.0); does not replicate previously proposed positive association. PMID:31792460
  • MHC-II HLA gene set (13 genes including HLA-DRA, HLA-DRB1, HLA-DRB5, HLA-DPA1, HLA-DPB1, HLA-DQA1, HLA-DQA2, HLA-DQB1, HLA-DQB2, HLA-DMA, HLA-DMB, HLA-DOA, HLA-DOB) — collective transcriptomic expression higher in responders (binomial P=0.0002); ssGSEA composite was the strongest single predictor of response in ipilimumab-treated tumors. PMID:31792460
  • CXCL9, CXCL10, CXCR3 — higher expression in responders in the ipilimumab-treated subgroup (MWW P=0.05, 0.08, 0.02) but not in ipilimumab-naive tumors. PMID:31792460
  • CTLA4 — higher expression in responders in the ipilimumab-treated subgroup (P=0.008). PMID:31792460
  • TGFB2 — higher expression in progressors in the ipilimumab-naive subgroup (MWW P=0.002), but not in the ipilimumab-treated subgroup (P=0.43). PMID:31792460

Clinical implications

  • TMB as a single-marker biomarker is unreliable in melanoma. Subtype (especially mucosal/acral vs cutaneous/occult) confounds the TMB–response association; authors argue TMB “cannot be applied generically across melanoma subtypes as a predictive biomarker for anti-PD1 ICB.” PMID:31792460
  • MHC-II HLA expression is a candidate biomarker for response to anti-PD1 ICB, particularly in ipilimumab-pretreated patients. Combined with LDH and lymph-node metastasis status, it yields a parsimonious model with AUC 0.90 in discovery (CV 0.83). PMID:31792460
  • In ipilimumab-naive patients, tumor heterogeneity, ploidy and purity (all WES-derivable) constitute a parsimonious predictor (AUC 0.77, CV 0.73) of primary resistance. PMID:31792460
  • Prior anti-CTLA4 exposure (ipilimumab) stratifies predictors of response to subsequent anti-PD1 ICB. Patients with low immune infiltrate and low MHC-II expression at post-ipilimumab biopsy are very likely intrinsically resistant to anti-PD1 (ipilimumab → nivolumab/pembrolizumab sequence). These patients may benefit from alternative regimens (e.g., combination ICB) rather than further anti-PD1 monotherapy. PMID:31792460
  • High predictive-model score (low MHC-II / LDH / LN met OR high heterogeneity-low ploidy-high purity) was associated with substantially worse PFS and OS: in the ipilimumab-treated subgroup, median PFS 2.8 vs 38.1 months and median OS 9.0 months versus not reached. PMID:31792460
  • B-cell infiltrate markers (CD19, MS4A1/CD20) were enriched in ipilimumab-experienced responders — consistent with emerging evidence for B-cell roles in neoadjuvant immunotherapy response. PMID:31792460
  • Authors note BRAF/NRAS mutation status was not predictive of anti-PD1 response, so targeted-therapy decisions (e.g., dacarbazine historical context) are separate from these ICB biomarker considerations. PMID:31792460

Limitations & open questions

  • Bulk transcriptome cannot disambiguate cell-of-origin of MHC-II expression. Whether the predictive signal arises from tumor-cell MHC-II (as suggested by prior IHC studies the authors cite) or from antigen-presenting cells in the microenvironment is unresolved. PMID:31792460
  • Limited independent validation. The largest available external cohort with both prior-ipilimumab metadata and matched WES/RNA-seq was n=32, underpowered for formal validation of the ipilimumab-stratified models. Predictor directions were concordant but most were not significant in validation (e.g., low MHC-II in ipilimumab-treated empiric P=0.21; heterogeneity in ipilimumab-naive empiric P=0.066). PMID:31792460
  • Mucosal/acral subtypes are underpowered (n=20 total). The TMB-confounding finding rests on a relatively small mucosal/acral group; responder counts there are tiny (4). PMID:31792460
  • Mechanism of anti-CTLA4–induced predictor shift is unknown. Whether prior ipilimumab induces an immune-resistant state versus simply reveals a preexisting one is explicitly flagged as needing further investigation. Cross-resistance to sequential ICB may also predict resistance to combination ICB — testable hypothesis. PMID:31792460
  • Biological basis of ploidy and purity associations is unclear. Authors hypothesize purity is a proxy for immune infiltrate; ploidy association is not mechanistically explained (and runs counter to prior aneuploidy findings). PMID:31792460
  • Models were trained and discovery-tested in the same cohort; the AUC numbers reported (0.90, 0.77) are cross-validated within the discovery cohort, not on a fully held-out external set. PMID:31792460
  • No formal correction for the per-subgroup gene-expression p-values (P=0.02, 0.03, 0.04 for individual immune genes); authors note these are unadjusted. PMID:31792460
