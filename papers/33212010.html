Proteogenomic Landscape of Breast Cancer Tumorigenesis and Targeted Therapy

Authors

Karsten Krug

Eric J. Jaehnig

Shankha Satpathy

Lili Blumenberg

Alla Karpova

Meenakshi Anurag

Philipp Mertins

D.R. Mani

Steven A. Carr

Matthew J. Ellis

Michael A. Gillette

Clinical Proteomic Tumor Analysis Consortium

Doi

10.1016/j.cell.2020.10.036

PMID: 33212010 · DOI: 10.1016/j.cell.2020.10.036 · Journal: Cell (2020)

TL;DR

The Clinical Proteomic Tumor Analysis Consortium (CPTAC) applied deep multi-omics proteogenomics — whole-exome sequencing, RNA-seq, miRNA-seq, and TMT-based global proteomics with phosphoproteomic and acetylproteomic profiling — to 122 treatment-naive primary breast tumors prospectively collected under a stringent short-ischemia protocol. Non-negative matrix factorization on integrated multi-omics data resolved four robust subtypes (NMF LumA-I, LumB-I, HER2-I, Basal-I) that refined PAM50 classification, identified pseudo-ERBB2+ tumors lacking the protein target of anti-HER2 therapy, exposed APOBEC mutagenesis and single-strand break repair defects as candidate biomarkers for immune checkpoint therapy in luminal breast cancer, and showed that Rb protein/phosphoprotein status — rather than RB1 genotype alone — tracks CDK4/6 inhibitor sensitivity in TNBC.

Cohort & data

  • n = 122 prospectively collected, treatment-naive primary breast tumors (134 participants enrolled; ages 30–95). Cryopulverized, with co-isolation of DNA, RNA, and protein from a single homogeneous sample.
  • Cancer type: invasive BRCA (predominantly IDC; some ILC), stage IIA–IIIC. Hormone-receptor classification by central IHC: ER and/or PR positive = 83, HER2 positive = 13, triple-negative = 16, ER/PR negative with equivocal HER2 = 12.
  • PAM50 distribution: 14 HER2-E, 29 Basal, 57 LumA, 17 LumB, 5 Normal-like.
  • Dataset: cBioPortal study brca_cptac_2020; raw genomics released to GDC under dbGaP phs000892 (GRCh38); proteomics at CPTAC accession S060 / PDC000120.
  • Comparator cohorts: METABRIC (Curtis et al., 2012) for outcome validation of NMF luminal reassignment; the earlier TCGA-residue CPTAC BRCA cohort (Mertins et al. 2016 sample set) for retrospective replication.
  • Assays/methods: whole-exome-seq (Nextera Rapid Capture, 150× on-target), rna-seq (TruSeq stranded, ≥30M PE150 reads), mirna-seq, TMT-10-plex global proteomics and phospho/acetyl-proteomics following Mertins et al. 2018 protocol; NMF multi-omics clustering, PAM50, GISTIC for SCNAs, GSEA, centralized IHC (HER2 SP3, ER 6F11, CD3 polyclonal) and FISH for ERBB2 status.
  • Data yield: 29,647 somatic mutations, 23,692 gene-level copy number events, 23,121 transcripts, 10,107 proteins, 38,968 phosphosites, 9,869 acetylation sites passing QC.

Key findings

  • Integrated multi-omics NMF resolves four BRCA subtypes (LumA-I, LumB-I, HER2-I, Basal-I) that broadly mirror PAM50 but reassign biology. NMF LumB-I includes a subset of PAM50 LumA samples with fewer PIK3CA mutations (binomial p = 1.50 × 10⁻³ vs. LumA-I) and lower stromal scores; in METABRIC, these reassigned samples had outcomes intermediate between PAM50 LumA and LumB.
  • NMF Basal-I is strongly enriched for TP53 mutations, hormone-receptor negativity, chromosome-instability and stemness scores, and proliferation pathways (E2F, G2/M).
  • NMF HER2-I is heterogeneous in ERBB2 status: although centered on PAM50 HER2-E and clinically ERBB2+ tumors, it contains samples from every other PAM50 subtype. HER2-unrelated drivers of HER2-I clustering enriched for endoplasmic-reticulum proteins and cholesterol/sterol biosynthesis (independent of ERBB2 amplification).
  • Pseudo-ERBB2+ tumors: 2 of 17 ERBB2-amplified cases in the prospective cohort (and 1 of 16 in the retrospective Mertins 2016 cohort) had ERBB2 protein levels within the distribution of ERBB2-negative tumors, despite exome-level amplification. One pseudo-ERBB2+ case in each cohort instead showed TOP2A amplification with TOP2A protein overexpression, supporting TOP2A as an alternative chromosome-17 amplicon driver. Only 7 of 15 proteogenomically ERBB2+ samples were PAM50 HER2-E; PAM50 HER2-E samples that were not ERBB2 PG+ had elevated phosphorylation of other ERBB family members and MAPK signaling.
  • Subtype-specific phospho-kinase outliers recovered via the Black-Sheep package (FDR < 0.01): PRKDC, MAP4K4, and SPEG in NMF Basal-I; ERBB2 and CDK12 in NMF HER2-I; DCLK1 in NMF LumA-I.
  • Tumor-suppressor-loss to targetable-kinase associations (novel): upregulated phospho-TNIK (WNT pathway) in ARID1A mutants; upregulated phospho-RIPK3 in MAP3K1 mutants; high phospho-MAST4 and phospho-DCLK1 in GATA3 mutants; elevated phospho-SLK/LATS1 in AKT1-mutated tumors (mTOR / HIPPO cross-talk).
  • Metabolism/acetylproteomics: NMF Basal-I tumors show upregulated glycolytic protein levels (HK3, PFKP, GAPDH, ENO1, LDHB) but hypoacetylation of glycolytic enzymes (GPI, TPI1, GAPDH, PGK1, PGAM1, ENO1, PKM, LDHA), upregulated serine biosynthesis (PHGDH, PSAT1), and broad hyperacetylation of mitochondrial TCA-cycle and β-oxidation proteins. Protein levels of the mitochondrial deacetylase SIRT3 were significantly negatively correlated with mitochondrial protein acetylation, suggesting SIRT3 depletion as a driver of Basal-I mitochondrial hyperacetylation. NMF HER2-I tumors uniquely show upregulated cholesterol biosynthesis and lipid metabolism independent of ERBB2 amplification status.
  • APOBEC mutagenesis correlates with active immune microenvironment in luminal BRCA: 6 APOBEC-enriched cases (1 HER2-E, 3 LumA, 2 LumB) had higher mutation loads (Wilcoxon p = 0.008) and elevated APOBEC3B protein. APOBEC3G protein correlated with PD-L1 (CD274) mRNA and CIBERSORT immune score in both PAM50 luminal and basal samples.
  • Single-strand break repair (SSBR) protein loss is anti-correlated with PD-L1 and immune infiltration specifically in luminal but not basal BRCA (GO BP nucleotide excision repair, base excision repair, mismatch repair pathways). Low GO NER protein levels associated with high mutation load in luminal samples only.
  • Rb status refines CDK4/6 inhibitor prediction: in HR+/ERBB2− tumors, Rb protein (Spearman ρ = 0.24, p = 0.035) and median Rb phosphosite (ρ = 0.53, p = 1.06 × 10⁻⁶) correlated positively with the multi-gene proliferation score; in TNBC they correlated negatively (ρ = −0.54, p = 0.003 protein; ρ = −0.46, p = 0.015 phospho). In TNBC, Rb-low vs. Rb-high phospho-protein groups (n = 16 vs. n = 12) tracked CDK4/CDK6 activity; 3 of 4 TNBC tumors with RB1 mutation/deletion were Rb-phospho low. In GDSC cell-line data, RB1-wild-type lines with low Rb protein were poorly responsive to palbociclib (Rb protein ~ palbociclib response: Spearman ρ = −0.61, p = 0.022), arguing that protein-level Rb assessment adds predictive value beyond RB1 genotype.

Genes & alterations

  • ERBB2 — gene amplification confirmed by WES; IHC/FISH per ASCO-CAP. 15/122 tumors classified proteogenomically as ERBB2+; pseudo-ERBB2+ phenotype (amplified but protein-low) identified in 2/17 amplified cases. ERBB2 phospho-kinase activity enriches NMF HER2-I.
  • TOP2A — alternative chromosome-17q amplicon driver in pseudo-ERBB2+ cases.
  • TP53 — mutations strongly enriched in NMF Basal-I and TNBC; 9 of 10 BL1 TNBC samples carried TP53 mutations.
  • RB1 — mutations/deletions in TNBC are associated with Rb-phospho-low state and palbociclib non-response; however, many TNBC tumors show Rb protein loss without RB1 genomic aberration.
  • PIK3CA — less frequent in NMF LumB-I than NMF LumA-I (binomial p = 1.50 × 10⁻³); recurrent in LAR-subtype TNBC.
  • ARID1A mutations associate with elevated phospho-TNIK (WNT pathway).
  • MAP3K1 loss-of-function in luminal BRCA associates with elevated phospho-RIPK3.
  • GATA3 mutations associate with elevated phospho- MAST4 and phospho-DCLK1 (novel — requires validation).
  • AKT1 mutations associate with elevated phospho-SLK and phospho-LATS1.
  • APOBEC3B / APOBEC3G protein levels track APOBEC mutagenesis signatures (COSMIC SBS2/SBS13) and PD-L1 / immune-infiltration scores in luminal BRCA.
  • PHGDH, PSAT1 (serine synthesis) — upregulated at the protein level in NMF Basal-I; nominated as therapeutic vulnerability.
  • SIRT3 — depletion proposed as a driver of broad mitochondrial protein hyperacetylation in NMF Basal-I.
  • CCND1, CCNE1, CDK2, CDK4, CDK6 — Cyclin D1 higher in HR+/ERBB2−; Cyclin E / CDK2 higher in TNBC (CCNE1 RNA p = 6.2 × 10⁻¹², phosphoprotein p = 7.3 × 10⁻¹⁰); CDK4/6 activity tracks proliferation in HR+ but not TNBC.
  • CREBBP — elevated activation-loop acetylation (K1591/K1592) in nuclear-Ac Basal-I cluster 1, accompanying broad histone-H2B N-terminal acetylation.

Clinical implications

  • HER2 diagnostics: integrated DNA + protein quantification of the chromosome-17 amplicon is proposed as a more quantitative alternative to IHC/FISH; pseudo-ERBB2+ status could explain trastuzumab-refractory amplified tumors.
  • Immune checkpoint therapy: subsets of luminal A/B BRCA with APOBEC mutagenesis or SSBR (NER/BER/MMR) defects display active I-TMEs and high PD-L1, supporting immunotherapy trials in endocrine-resistant ER+ BRCA.
  • CDK4/6 inhibitors: proteogenomic Rb status (protein + phospho) refines candidate selection for CDK4/6i, both in HR+/ERBB2− and in a subset of intact-Rb TNBC; cell-line evidence (GDSC) supports clinical evaluation of palbociclib in RB1-wild-type, Rb-protein-high TNBC.
  • Targeting tumor-suppressor loss via downstream kinases: TNIK inhibition in ARID1A-mutant BRCA, DCLK1 inhibition (e.g., LRRK2-IN-1) in GATA3-mutant or LumA tumors, and consideration of necroptosis-related signaling in MAP3K1-mutant luminal BRCA.
  • Metabolic vulnerabilities: serine-synthesis (PHGDH) inhibition for basal tumors; SIRT3-modulated mitochondrial acetylation as a candidate axis (therapeutic direction ambiguous given dual oncogene/TS roles).

Limitations & open questions

  • Prospective accrual prioritized data quality over demographic representation; cohort may not be optimized for subgroup analyses.
  • Bulk cryopulverized tissue sacrifices architectural and single-cell resolution; integration with imaging mass cytometry or microscaled workflows (Satpathy et al. 2020, DP1 cohort) is needed.
  • Associations are hypothesis-generating; therapeutic claims (TNIK, DCLK1, MAST4, PHGDH, SIRT3, APOBEC-immune link, Rb-CDK4/6) need functional and clinical validation.
  • N = 122 limits statistical power for rare-event subsets (e.g., n = 6 APOBEC-enriched, n = 2 pseudo-ERBB2+ in the prospective cohort).
  • Equivocal HER2 cases per ASCO-CAP could not all be resolved due to unavailable repeat FISH.
  • SIRT3 has both tumor-suppressor and oncogenic roles in the literature; whether to inhibit or activate it for therapeutic benefit remains open.
