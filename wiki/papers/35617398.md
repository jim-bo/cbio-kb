**PMID:** 35617398 · **DOI:** 10.1126/science.abe1505 · **Journal:** Science (2022)

## TL;DR

Tang et al. integrated ATAC-seq, RNA-seq, and DNA sequencing across a biobank of 22 patient-derived organoids, 6 PDXs, and 12 cell lines of metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer ([CRPC](../cancer_types/PRAD.md)) and identified four chromatin-defined molecular subtypes: CRPC-AR (androgen receptor–dependent), CRPC-NE (neuroendocrine; see [PRSCC](../cancer_types/PRSCC.md)), CRPC-WNT (Wnt-driven), and a newly described CRPC-SCL (stem cell–like) subtype driven by AP-1 family transcription factors (notably [FOSL1](../genes/FOSL1.md)) cooperating with [YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md)/[WWTR1](../genes/WWTR1.md) (TAZ) and TEAD. Applying transcriptomic signatures to 366 CRPC patients across the [SU2C](../datasets/prad_su2c_2019.md) and WCM cohorts, CRPC-SCL was the second-most-common subtype (~28%) and was associated with shorter time on next-generation ARSI ([enzalutamide](../drugs/enzalutamide.md)/[abiraterone](../drugs/abiraterone.md)). Verteporfin and the c-Fos/AP-1 inhibitor T-5224 selectively reduced growth of CRPC-SCL models, nominating the YAP/TAZ/AP-1 axis as a therapeutic vulnerability.

## Cohort & data

- **40 metastatic prostate cancer models** profiled by ATAC-seq and RNA-seq: 22 patient-derived organoids (15 newly derived MSKPCa8–MSKPCa24 + 7 prior), 6 patient-derived xenografts ([PDX](../methods/patient-derived-xenograft.md)), and 12 cell lines (7 with new ATAC-seq + 5 NEPC models from Park et al.) ([PMID:35617398](../papers/35617398.md)).
- **Mutational/CNV profiling** of organoids and 10/15 matched tumor biopsies with [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md); copy-number landscape compared against the [SU2C metastatic CRPC cohort](../datasets/prad_su2c_2019.md).
- **Patient classification cohort**: 366 CRPC patients with polyA-enriched RNA-seq — 266 from the published [SU2C](../datasets/prad_su2c_2019.md) cohort (Abida et al. 2019) and 100 from a Weill Cornell Medicine (WCM) cohort.
- **Dataset of record**: organoid biobank deposited as [prad_organoids_msk_2022](../datasets/prad_organoids_msk_2022.md) on cBioPortal.
- **Functional validation**: ChIP-seq for FOSL1/TEAD/YAP/TAZ in MSKPCa3 and DU145; CRISPR sgRNA cell-competition assays; [siRNA knockdown](../methods/sirna-knockdown.md) of YAP/TAZ; exogenous [FOSL1](../genes/FOSL1.md) overexpression in LNCaP.

## Key findings

- **Four chromatin-defined CRPC subtypes** identified by consensus k-means clustering of ATAC-seq peaks (validated by hierarchical clustering and UMAP): CRPC-AR, CRPC-NE, CRPC-WNT, and a novel CRPC-SCL (stem cell–like). Signature peaks were enriched at enhancers vs. promoters (one-tailed Fisher's exact P = 4.18e-126).
- **861,195 reproducible ATAC-seq peaks** across 35 newly profiled models + 5 published NEPC models, mostly at distal intergenic/intronic regions.
- **Tumor-suppressor loss burden**: [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) was the most frequently altered gene with putative driver mutations or deep deletions in 23/35 (66%) samples; [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) bi-allelic loss in 20% and [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) bi-allelic loss in 43%. Including transcript/protein-level loss, [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) loss was observed in additional 11/35 (31.4%) and [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) loss in 10/35 (28.5%) samples.
- **[RB1](../genes/RB1.md) loss enriched in AR-negative/low samples** (14/24) vs CRPC-AR (2/12) (one-tailed Fisher's exact P = 0.0200); no difference for [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) or [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — consistent with prior data linking RB1 (but not TP53/PTEN) to shorter CRPC survival.
- **CRPC-AR** enriched for [AR](../genes/AR.md) amplification and/or [AR](../genes/AR.md) mutation (one-tailed Fisher's exact P = 7.01e-06); top key TFs were [AR](../genes/AR.md) and [FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md).
- **CRPC-WNT** (4/35 models): all carry Wnt pathway alterations; 3 with hotspot [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) mutations, 1 with shallow [APC](../genes/APC.md) deletion + [RSPO2](../genes/RSPO2.md) gain; top TF [TCF7L2](../genes/TCF7L2.md) (TCF-4), with LEF1/TCF7/TCF7L1 also high-ranked.
- **CRPC-NE**: top TFs [NEUROD1](../genes/NEUROD1.md) and [ASCL1](../genes/ASCL1.md) (the same master TFs in [SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md)); high SYP/CHGA/DLL3, enriched for RB1 deep deletion.
- **CRPC-SCL** (newly identified): enriched for mammary stem cell signature, cancer stem cell markers [CD44](../genes/CD44.md) and [TACSTD2](../genes/TACSTD2.md) (TROP2A), basal signature, FGFR signaling, and TGF-β/EMT/TNF-α/IFN response pathways. Top key TFs were AP-1 family members, with [FOSL1](../genes/FOSL1.md) the highest-ranked.
- **AP-1 + YAP/TAZ/TEAD co-operate in CRPC-SCL**: TEAD motifs were the second-most-enriched (after AP-1) in CRPC-SCL accessible peaks. ChIP-seq in MSKPCa3 and DU145 showed significant overlap of FOSL1/TEAD/YAP/TAZ peaks (Fisher's exact P < 0.001), all enriched at CRPC-SCL ATAC-seq peaks but not CRPC-AR peaks (P < 0.0001).
- **Functional dependency**: sgRNA depletion of [FOSL1](../genes/FOSL1.md) in MSKPCa3 and DU145 reduced GFP+ fraction (positive-control-like) but not in CRPC-AR organoid MSKPCa2. siRNA double-knockdown of YAP/TAZ significantly decreased growth of MSKPCa3 and DU145 (two-tailed unpaired t-test P < 0.0001) but not of MSKPCa2 or 22Rv1.
- **FOSL1 acts as a pioneer factor**: stable FOSL1 overexpression in [AR](../genes/AR.md)-high LNCaP cells (alone or with YAP/TAZ) increased chromatin accessibility at CRPC-SCL-specific sites and decreased CRPC-AR-specific accessibility (permutation P < 0.001), with concordant upregulation of CRPC-SCL signature genes (FDR < 1e-5) — direct evidence of lineage transformation.
- **Positive feedback loop**: YAP/TAZ double KD depleted FOSL1 at RNA and protein levels in MSKPCa3 and DU145; FOSL1 itself was a target of FOS/JUN and TEAD in the inferred regulatory network, with a CRPC-SCL-specific FOSL1 enhancer co-bound by TEAD/YAP/TAZ/FOSL1.
- **Patient subtype distribution** (Nearest Template Prediction on RNA-seq from 366 CRPC patients): 206/266 SU2C and 82/100 WCM patients assignable to one of four subtypes; CRPC-AR largest, CRPC-SCL the second-most-common (~28% across cohorts), then CRPC-NE, then CRPC-WNT. NTP and SVM classifiers agreed on 92% (WCM) and 85% (SU2C) of samples.
- **CRPC-SCL is ARSI-refractory**: among 56 SU2C patients with time-on-ARSI data ([enzalutamide](../drugs/enzalutamide.md)/[abiraterone](../drugs/abiraterone.md)), CRPC-SCL had significantly shorter time on treatment (Cox log-rank).
- **Therapeutic vulnerability**: [verteporfin](../drugs/verteporfin.md) (YAP/TAZ inhibitor; FDA-approved photosensitizer) and T-5224 (c-Fos/AP-1 DNA-binding inhibitor) preferentially inhibited proliferation of CRPC-SCL models MSKPCa3 and DU145 vs. CRPC-AR models MSKPCa2 and 22Rv1.

## Genes & alterations

- [AR](../genes/AR.md) — amplification and/or mutation enriched in CRPC-AR; AR signaling lost or attenuated in the three AR-negative/low subtypes.
- [FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md) — co-top TF with AR in CRPC-AR.
- [FOSL1](../genes/FOSL1.md) — highest-ranked key TF for CRPC-SCL; acts as pioneer factor whose overexpression in AR-high LNCaP cells reprograms chromatin toward CRPC-SCL.
- [YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md) and [WWTR1](../genes/WWTR1.md) (TAZ) — transcriptional coactivators upregulated in CRPC-SCL; double knockdown selectively suppresses CRPC-SCL cell growth; co-bind with FOSL1/TEAD at CRPC-SCL enhancers.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — putative driver mutations or deep deletions in 23/35 (66%) of CRPC models, no subtype-specific enrichment.
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) — bi-allelic loss in 20% of models genomically; combined with RNA/protein loss, present in 14/24 (58%) AR-negative/low samples vs. 2/12 CRPC-AR (P = 0.0200); deep deletion enriched in CRPC-NE patients in the SU2C/WCM cohorts.
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — bi-allelic loss in 43% of models; not subtype-specific.
- [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) — hotspot mutations in 3/4 CRPC-WNT models.
- [APC](../genes/APC.md), [RSPO2](../genes/RSPO2.md) — combined shallow deletion + gain in the fourth CRPC-WNT model.
- [TCF7L2](../genes/TCF7L2.md), [LEF1](../genes/LEF1.md), [TCF7](../genes/TCF7.md), [TCF7L1](../genes/TCF7L1.md) — top TFs in CRPC-WNT.
- [ASCL1](../genes/ASCL1.md), [NEUROD1](../genes/NEUROD1.md) — top TFs in CRPC-NE, mirroring the master regulators of [SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md).
- [SYP](../genes/SYP.md), [CHGA](../genes/CHGA.md), [DLL3](../genes/DLL3.md) — high NE markers in CRPC-NE.
- [CD44](../genes/CD44.md), [TACSTD2](../genes/TACSTD2.md) (TROP2A) — stem cell markers elevated in CRPC-SCL (with [CD44](../genes/CD44.md) also elevated in CRPC-SCL patients).
- [CCN1](../genes/CCN1.md) (CYR61), [AXL](../genes/AXL.md), [CCN2](../genes/CCN2.md) (CTGF), [AJUBA](../genes/AJUBA.md), [ANKRD1](../genes/ANKRD1.md), [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md) — canonical YAP/TAZ targets downregulated upon YAP/TAZ double knockdown in MSKPCa3 and DU145.
- [AXIN2](../genes/AXIN2.md) — elevated in CRPC-WNT patients (Wnt target).

## Clinical implications

- **Subtype-directed therapy stratification**: the authors propose that transcriptomic NTP/SVM classifiers using their derived signatures can assign CRPC patients to one of four subtypes from bulk RNA-seq, with potential to inform treatment selection.
- **ARSI resistance biomarker**: CRPC-SCL patients have significantly shorter time on next-generation ARSIs ([enzalutamide](../drugs/enzalutamide.md), [abiraterone](../drugs/abiraterone.md)), suggesting subtype identification could deprioritize ARSI escalation in these patients.
- **YAP/TAZ/AP-1 pathway as a CRPC-SCL vulnerability**: [verteporfin](../drugs/verteporfin.md) and the AP-1/c-Fos inhibitor T-5224 selectively reduce CRPC-SCL cell growth in preclinical models, nominating the pathway for clinical development in this subtype. (Authors caution that [verteporfin](../drugs/verteporfin.md)'s clinical translation may be limited by YAP-independent effects.)
- **Lineage plasticity as a unifying mechanism**: CRPC-SCL tumors lack neuroendocrine features but share basal/stem-like reprogramming with NEPC, suggesting a broader model in which loss of AR dependence proceeds via distinct master-TF-driven trajectories (FOSL1/AP-1 for SCL, ASCL1/NEUROD1 for NE).
- [CD44](../genes/CD44.md) (and to a lesser extent [AXIN2](../genes/AXIN2.md)) are proposed as candidate IHC/RNA-level biomarkers for CRPC-SCL and CRPC-WNT respectively.

## Limitations & open questions

- CRPC-SCL identification leans heavily on in vitro/PDX models — the four subtypes are defined epigenetically in 40 models and then projected onto bulk RNA-seq from patients, which may miss intra-tumoral heterogeneity or sample composition effects.
- Authors note 60/266 SU2C and 18/100 WCM patients remained unassigned by NTP, with unassigned samples showing more heterogeneity and a tendency toward CRPC-SCL — possibly representing transitional states.
- [Verteporfin](../drugs/verteporfin.md)'s clinical potential is explicitly hedged due to known YAP-independent off-target effects; T-5224 has not yet been tested in prostate cancer trials.
- ARSI-treatment analysis was limited to 56 SU2C patients with time-on-treatment data; CRPC-WNT and CRPC-NE arms had <5 patients each, so direct comparison across all four subtypes was not possible.
- In vivo durability and specificity of YAP/TAZ/AP-1 inhibition in CRPC-SCL await longer-term PDX/xenograft experiments.
- Mechanistic basis for lineage choice (why some AR-loss tumors become CRPC-NE vs. CRPC-SCL despite shared TP53/RB1 co-loss) remains unresolved.
