**PMID:** 32015526 · **DOI:** 10.1101/2025.08.13.670169 · **Journal:** bioRxiv (2025)

## TL;DR

This study analyzed RNA-seq from 684 treatment-naive lung adenocarcinomas in people who have never smoked (NS-LUAD), combining the Sherlock-Lung cohort (n=610) with [TCGA-LUAD](../datasets/luad_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md) never-smoker cases (n=74). Non-negative matrix factorization (NMF) of the transcriptome identified three NS-LUAD subtypes — **steady**, **proliferative**, and **chaotic** — distinguished by proliferation, cell composition, driver gene alterations, and survival. A 60-gene signature recapitulates the classification and stratifies mortality risk even within stage I tumors and within TP53-wild-type subgroups, and was validated in the independent Genomic Institute of Singapore (GIS) NS-LUAD cohort (n=110; corresponds to cBioPortal study [luad_oncosg_2020](../datasets/luad_oncosg_2020.md)).

## Cohort & data

- **Sherlock cohort:** 684 NS-LUAD tumors (610 Sherlock-Lung + 74 [TCGA-LUAD](../datasets/luad_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md) never-smokers), all treatment-naive, with paired adjacent-normal [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) in 498 subjects (491 Sherlock-Lung + 7 TCGA-LUAD) [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- **Ancestry:** 295 European (EUR), 342 East Asian (EAS), 4 African (AFR), 32 American/Mixed, 11 unknown. Only 80 genes were differentially expressed between EUR and EAS, and no significant pathway differences, so groups were combined [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- **Demographics:** median age 64; 555 (81.1%) female and 129 (18.9%) male; 386 (56.4%) stage I tumors; 40 (5.8%) stage IV [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- **Histologic grading:** 301 cases reviewed using the IASLC system — 34 (11.3%) well, 69 (22.9%) moderate, 198 (65.8%) poorly differentiated [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- **Integrated omics:** 499 tumors had matched [whole-genome sequencing](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) (486 Sherlock-Lung + 13 TCGA-LUAD) used to integrate driver mutations, structural variants, whole-genome doubling, and kataegis [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- **Validation:** the Genomic Institute of Singapore (GIS) NS-LUAD cohort, 110 tumors of Asian ancestry (cBioPortal [luad_oncosg_2020](../datasets/luad_oncosg_2020.md); EGA accession EGAD00001004421) [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- **Assays and tools:** Illumina NovaSeq6000 2x151bp paired-end RNA-seq; [STAR](../genes/STAR.md) v2.7.3 ([star](../methods/star.md)) alignment to GRCh38; HTSeq + GENCODE v35 quantification; ComBat-Seq batch correction; DESeq2 with TMM normalization; NMF (R package; Brunet's algorithm); fusions called by SFyNCS v0.15 and [STAR-Fusion](../methods/star-fusion.md); CAMTHC and Bisque deconvolution; immune response predicted by [TIDE](../methods/tide.md); tumor purity by [ESTIMATE](../methods/estimate.md); 60-gene classifier built with [ClaNC](../methods/clanc.md); survival analyzed with [Cox proportional-hazards models](../methods/cox-proportional-hazards.md) [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).

## Key findings

- **Three NS-LUAD transcriptomic subtypes** identified by [NMF clustering](../methods/nmf-clustering.md) at rank 3: *steady* (normal-like, low proliferation, alveolar-cell enriched), *proliferative* (high MYC-regulated and cell-cycle genes), and *chaotic* (high EMT and cell-adhesion disruption) [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- **Stage distribution:** steady tumors were 74% stage I (p=2.75x10⁻⁸ vs others); ~2/3 of chaotic tumors were advanced-stage (p=1.44x10⁻⁸ vs others) [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- **Grade:** 53%, 27% and 23% of steady, proliferative, and chaotic tumors were well/moderately differentiated (steady vs others p=5.52x10⁻⁶) [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- **Lineage fidelity:** steady had the highest [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) lineage score (p=6.84x10⁻²²); chaotic had the highest mixed-lineage score (p=2.31x10⁻¹⁸) despite being histologically LUAD [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- **Cell composition:** proliferative tumors were enriched for epithelial cells (mean 38.33%, p=2.03x10⁻¹²); chaotic tumors enriched for fibroblasts (mean 21.55%, p=6.88x10⁻⁴⁰); steady tumors enriched for alveolar AT2 cells (mean 34.94%, p=5x10⁻²⁹) and depleted of basal cells (mean 3.40%, p=1.09x10⁻⁵³). Chaotic was specifically enriched for COL10A1+ and [COL4A1](../genes/COL4A1.md)+ cancer-associated fibroblasts (CAFs) and depleted of non-malignant fibroblasts [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- **Driver genes by subtype (integrated WGS, n=499):** [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations significantly enriched in proliferative (p=4.99x10⁻⁶, FDR=9.73x10⁻⁴) and depleted in steady (p=4.63x10⁻¹⁴, FDR=9.03x10⁻¹²); [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) fusions negatively associated with steady (p=1.73x10⁻⁴, FDR=0.0168); co-occurrence of [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) fusions and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations seen in 7 proliferative tumors; [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) mutations were more frequent in steady but not significant after FDR (p=3.65x10⁻³, FDR=0.315); no driver mutations or fusions (including [MET](../genes/MET.md) exon 14 skipping) were significantly enriched in chaotic [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- **Gene fusions:** 11,947 fusions detected by RNA-seq in 638/684 tumors; 54.3% supported by WGS structural variants. Proliferative had the highest mean fusion count (22.1 vs 13.9 steady vs 10.7 chaotic, p=1.11x10⁻¹³). Most frequent in-frame fusions: [EML4](../genes/EML4.md)-[ALK](../genes/ALK.md) (33 tumors) and [KIF5B](../genes/KIF5B.md)-[RET](../genes/RET.md) (8 tumors). [PARG](../genes/PARG.md)-[BMS1](../genes/BMS1.md) was a novel recurrent fusion in 5 tumors. Top fusion partners: [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) (35), [EML4](../genes/EML4.md) (33), [RET](../genes/RET.md) (12), [ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md) (9). In-frame ALK fusion frequency: 8.2% proliferative vs 2.3% steady vs 1.6% chaotic [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- **Survival (multivariable Cox, age/sex/stage adjusted):** Steady HR=0.43 (95% CI 0.30–0.63, p=1.3x10⁻⁵); chaotic HR=1.4 (95% CI 0.99–2.0, p=0.056 at 10y; HR=1.5, 95% CI 1.0–2.3, p=0.031 at 5y) [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- **Stage I-only survival (n=374):** steady HR=0.39 (95% CI 0.22–0.68, p=8.8x10⁻⁴); chaotic HR=2.5 (95% CI 1.3–4.7, p=0.0043 at 10y; HR=3.7, 95% CI 1.8–7.3, p=2.2x10⁻⁴ at 5y) — the subtype identifies aggressive stage I tumors not captured by stage alone [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- **Beyond TP53:** within 169 TP53-wild-type tumors, proliferative still had shorter survival than steady (HR=2.1, 95% CI 1.2–3.6, p=0.011) [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- **Within S-LUAD TRU subset (n=303):** prognostic separation persisted (steady HR=0.53, p=0.0025) [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- **ICB response prediction:** Steady had the lowest [TIDE](../methods/tide.md) scores (p=6.21x10⁻¹⁴) and was enriched in the bottom-20% TIDE quintile (p=1.09x10⁻⁹), while chaotic was depleted (p=3.58x10⁻⁴). Steady had lower abundances of MDSCs (p=4.77x10⁻¹⁹) and CAFs (p=1.44x10⁻¹⁰). Adding subtype to [CD274](../genes/CD274.md) (PD-L1)/[PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md) (PD-1) expression raised the R² of TIDE prediction from 0.093 (PD-L1+PD-1 only) to 0.25 (subtype+PD-L1+PD-1, p=1.17x10⁻²²) [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- **60-gene signature (built with [ClaNC](../methods/clanc.md)):** correctly classified 87.0% (595/684) of samples vs whole-transcriptome NMF; cross-validation error rates 13.2% (training) and 15.1% (testing). Includes ~30 lung-development genes and ~10 EMT/cell-adhesion genes [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- **External validation in GIS cohort ([luad_oncosg_2020](../datasets/luad_oncosg_2020.md), n=110):** signature classified 35 steady / 64 proliferative / 11 chaotic; steady HR=0.32 (95% CI 0.12–0.87, p=0.026) overall, also significant within TP53-wt; mortality c-index=0.682 [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- **Best combined prognostic model:** expression subtype + stage achieved c-index=0.68 in the GIS testing set, outperforming stage alone, driver-mutation models ([TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) mutations, [EML4](../genes/EML4.md)-[ALK](../genes/ALK.md) fusion), or histologic grade [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — mutations enriched in proliferative subtype (p=4.99x10⁻⁶, FDR=9.73x10⁻⁴) and depleted in steady (p=4.63x10⁻¹⁴, FDR=9.03x10⁻¹²) NS-LUAD [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) — mutations more frequent in steady NS-LUAD (p=3.65x10⁻³, not FDR-significant); consistent with previously reported slow growth of EGFR-positive NS-LUAD tumors [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — used together with TP53 and [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) mutations + EML4-ALK fusion in the "Mutation model" Cox prognostic comparator [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) — fusions negatively associated with steady (p=1.73x10⁻⁴, FDR=0.0168); 35 tumors carry ALK fusions; in-frame ALK fusions enriched in proliferative (8.2%) [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- [EML4](../genes/EML4.md) — partner in the canonical EML4-ALK fusion (33 tumors, most frequent in-frame fusion event) [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- [RET](../genes/RET.md) — second-most-frequent in-frame fusion partner (12 tumors); [KIF5B](../genes/KIF5B.md)-RET seen in 8 tumors [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- [ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md) — recurrent in-frame fusion gene (9 tumors) [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- [KIF5B](../genes/KIF5B.md) — partner in KIF5B-RET fusion (8 tumors) [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- [MET](../genes/MET.md) — MET exon 14 skipping looked for but not enriched in chaotic [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) — MYC-regulated proliferation signatures upregulated in proliferative subtype [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- [MKI67](../genes/MKI67.md) — low expression marker of steady subtype; high in proliferative [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- [CDH2](../genes/CDH2.md) — mesenchymal marker highly expressed in chaotic subtype [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- [TWIST1](../genes/TWIST1.md) — EMT transcription factor highly expressed in chaotic [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- [CD274](../genes/CD274.md) (PD-L1) — expression measured to anchor the TIDE-based ICB-response analysis; only modestly predictive (R²=0.081) of TIDE score [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- [PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md) (PD-1) — expression measured alongside [CD274](../genes/CD274.md); combined R²=0.093, much lower than the subtype-augmented R²=0.25 [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- [PARG](../genes/PARG.md) — partner in a novel recurrent PARG-BMS1 fusion (5 tumors); [PARG](../genes/PARG.md) protein involved in DNA damage repair [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- [BMS1](../genes/BMS1.md) — partner in the novel recurrent PARG-BMS1 fusion (5 tumors) [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).

## Clinical implications

- The 60-gene signature provides a clinically usable prognostic tool for NS-LUAD that retains discriminative power within stage I and within TP53-wt subgroups, identifying early-stage tumors at unexpectedly high mortality risk that may warrant adjuvant therapy [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- Combining the expression subtype with tumor stage yielded the most accurate survival predictor (c-index=0.68 in the GIS testing set), outperforming stage, mutation, or grade alone — supporting integration of transcriptomic subtyping into clinical workup [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- Adding subtype to [CD274](../genes/CD274.md)/[PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md) expression nearly tripled the R² for predicted ICB response (0.093 → 0.25), suggesting that NS-LUAD patients in the steady subtype may be a candidate population for immune checkpoint blockade despite NS-LUAD being typically considered less ICB-responsive than smoking-related LUAD; the authors explicitly call this a hypothesis needing validation in cohorts with both transcriptomes and ICB outcomes [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) fusions co-occurring with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations — a known poor-prognosis, treatment-resistant combination — were concentrated in the proliferative subtype (7 tumors) [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).

## Limitations & open questions

- The validation cohort (GIS, [luad_oncosg_2020](../datasets/luad_oncosg_2020.md)) is exclusively Asian ancestry and n=110, including only 11 chaotic tumors — chaotic survival inference in the validation set is underpowered [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- ICB-response inference is computational ([TIDE](../methods/tide.md)) rather than direct; the authors note that the predicted enrichment of steady tumors in ICB-responder space "warrants further validation" in cohorts with paired transcriptomic and ICB outcome data [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- The chaotic subtype lacks an enrichment for any known driver mutation or fusion, so its identification depends on having transcriptomic data — there is no equivalent DNA-only assay [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- The Sherlock cohort is skewed female (81.1%) and includes only 4 African-ancestry patients, leaving open whether the signature performs equally across ancestries beyond EUR/EAS [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md).
- The manuscript is a bioRxiv preprint (posted Aug 2025) and had not been peer-reviewed at the time of compilation; the corresponding cBioPortal study [luad_oncosg_2020](../datasets/luad_oncosg_2020.md) is from a separate prior publication (Chen et al.) that the authors use as a validation set, not a re-release of the Sherlock-Lung cohort.
