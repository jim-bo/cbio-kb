# H1-3

## Overview

H1-3 (formerly HIST1H1D) encodes a linker histone H1 variant involved in higher-order chromatin compaction. In DLBCL, H1-3 mutations co-define the C4 GCB subtype alongside other H1 family members ([H1-2](../genes/H1-2.md), [H1-4](../genes/H1-4.md), [H1-5](../genes/H1-5.md)); domain mutations are proposed to reduce chromatin association and create a locally hypermutable chromatin environment.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- H1-3 (HIST1H1D) linker histone mutations co-define DLBCL C4 GCB subtype in a 304-patient WES cohort; globular/C-terminal domain mutations likely reduce chromatin association and raise local mutation rate; co-occurring with H1-2, H1-4, H1-5, and HIST1H2 core histone mutations. [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [DLBCLNOS](../cancer_types/DLBCLNOS.md): H1-3 mutations are part of the linker histone mutation cluster defining the C4 GCB-DLBCL genetic subtype, which carries a more favorable prognosis than C3 or C5. [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with [H1-2](../genes/H1-2.md), [H1-4](../genes/H1-4.md), [H1-5](../genes/H1-5.md), and HIST1H2 core histone mutations in the DLBCL C4 subtype. [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No specific targeted therapy for H1-3-mutant DLBCL described in current corpus; C4 subtype carries relatively favorable prognosis.

## Open questions

- Functional consequences of H1-3 domain mutations on chromatin accessibility, mutation rate, and lymphomagenesis remain to be experimentally determined. [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)
