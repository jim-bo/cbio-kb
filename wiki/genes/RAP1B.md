# RAP1B

## Overview

RAP1B (Ras-related protein Rap-1b) is a small GTPase of the Ras superfamily involved in integrin-mediated cell adhesion, cell migration, and signal transduction. Activating mutations analogous to known Ras-activating residues have been observed in [cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma](../cancer_types/CSCC.md).

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Mutated in 2.4% of CSCC tumors; one amplified tumor also carried an activating point mutation analogous to known Ras-activating residues [PMID:34272401](../papers/34272401.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **CSCC** — mutated at 2.4% frequency; co-amplification with activating point mutation observed [PMID:34272401](../papers/34272401.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with other Ras/PI3K pathway alterations in CSCC [PMID:34272401](../papers/34272401.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct targeted therapy reported in this corpus; pathway context (Ras signaling) may be relevant to MEK/ERK inhibitor strategies.

## Open questions

- Functional validation of the activating point mutation analogous to Ras-activating residues in CSCC is pending.
