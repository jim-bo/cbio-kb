# enasidenib

## Overview

Enasidenib is a selective inhibitor of mutant [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) (isocitrate dehydrogenase 2). It is FDA-approved for relapsed/refractory [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md) harboring [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) mutations, blocking the neomorphic production of 2-hydroxyglutarate and promoting differentiation of malignant blasts. It is structurally distinct from the [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) inhibitor [ivosidenib](../drugs/ivosidenib.md).

## Evidence in the corpus

- In the Beat [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md) cohort (n=562 [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md) patients), [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md)-mutant specimens showed broad ex vivo drug sensitivity across the 122-compound panel, contrasting with the broad resistance pattern seen in IDH1-mutant cases; enasidenib was part of the AML targeted-therapy landscape context for the study [PMID:30333627](../papers/30333627.md).
- [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md) inhibitor; IDH2 mutations discussed as targets in AML context in Beat AML Waves 3+4 (n=805 patients) ex vivo functional-genomic study [PMID:35868306](../papers/35868306.md)

## Resistance mechanisms

- [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) mutation — IDH1-mutant AML showed a distinct broad-resistance signature in ex vivo profiling, suggesting [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) vs IDH2 confer divergent pharmacological vulnerabilities [PMID:30333627](../papers/30333627.md).
