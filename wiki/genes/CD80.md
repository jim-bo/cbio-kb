# CD80

## Overview

CD80 (also known as B7-1) is a co-stimulatory ligand expressed on antigen-presenting cells that binds [CD28](../genes/CD28.md) on T cells to provide the co-stimulatory second signal for T-cell activation, and also binds CTLA-4 with higher affinity to deliver an inhibitory signal. In the tumor microenvironment, CD80 expression on tumor cells or infiltrating immune cells can modulate T-cell responses. Under immune checkpoint blockade, CD80 expression is induced as part of the pharmacological on-therapy immune activation program.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- CD80 was upregulated on-therapy in melanoma patients receiving [nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md) (anti-PD-1), as part of a 475-gene pharmacologic on-therapy response set (q < 0.20) regardless of clinical response category. CD80 was specifically named among immune checkpoint genes upregulated on-therapy: [PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md), [CD274](../genes/CD274.md), [CTLA4](../genes/CTLA4.md), CD80, [ICOS](../genes/ICOS.md), [LAG3](../genes/LAG3.md), and [TNFRSF9](../genes/TNFRSF9.md). [PMID:29033130](../papers/29033130.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md):** CD80 was among the immune checkpoint and co-stimulatory genes consistently upregulated on-therapy under nivolumab across ipilimumab-naive and ipilimumab-progressed melanoma patients, independent of clinical response. CD80 upregulation was also enriched in the genomic-contraction-phenotype DEG set. [PMID:29033130](../papers/29033130.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- CD80 co-upregulates on-therapy with its TCR co-stimulation axis partners [CD86](../genes/CD86.md) and [CD28](../genes/CD28.md), and with immune checkpoints [CTLA4](../genes/CTLA4.md), [PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md), [CD274](../genes/CD274.md), [ICOS](../genes/ICOS.md), and [LAG3](../genes/LAG3.md). [PMID:29033130](../papers/29033130.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- CD80 induction on-therapy under nivolumab reflects the broader immune activation pharmacodynamic response. The dual role of CD80 as a CD28 agonist and CTLA-4 ligand makes it a mechanistic link between anti-PD-1 and anti-CTLA-4 combination strategies. [PMID:29033130](../papers/29033130.md)

## Open questions

- Whether on-therapy CD80 levels differentiate patients who will experience durable responses vs early progression was not specifically reported. [PMID:29033130](../papers/29033130.md)
