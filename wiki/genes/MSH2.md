# MSH2

## Overview

MSH2 is a core component of the mismatch repair complex (MutSalpha with [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md); MutSbeta with [MSH3](../genes/MSH3.md)). Germline mutations in MSH2 cause Lynch syndrome, predisposing to colorectal, endometrial, and other cancers. Somatic MSH2 inactivation produces MSI-H tumors sensitive to immune checkpoint blockade.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Somatic and germline MSH2 mutations detected in dMMR/MSI-H gynecologic cancers; 30% of cases harbored pathogenic somatic MMR gene mutations including MSH2 [PMID:38653864](../papers/38653864.md)
- In glioblastoma treated with [temozolomide](../drugs/temozolomide.md), alterations in mismatch repair genes including MSH2 are associated with a hypermutator phenotype when [MGMT](../genes/MGMT.md) is methylated [PMID:18772890](../papers/18772890.md).
- MSH2 mutations with loss of protein expression by IHC in treatment-refractory corticotroph pituitary neuroendocrine tumors [PMID:38758238](../papers/38758238.md)
- Deleterious MSH2 alterations in 75% of MSI-H/dMMR prostate cancers; MSH2 altered in 6% of TMB-H/MSS tumors (P<0.001 difference) [PMID:38949888](../papers/38949888.md)
- MSH2 protein loss detected in 166/842 (20%) MMR-D tumors in a prospective pan-cancer cohort; significantly higher TMB than MLH1-loss tumors (median 46.5 vs 37.7 mut/Mb, P=0.0013). One discordant MSI-H false-positive case (Sample_54409, colon adenocarcinoma) carried an MSH2 E580* nonsense variant alongside a dominant [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) exonuclease-domain signature. [PMID:39746944](../papers/39746944.md)
- MMR gene alterations ([MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md), MSH2, [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md), [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md)) identified as Lynch syndrome in 17/2,336 PDAC patients (0.7%); 6/17 were MSI-H. [PMID:39753968](../papers/39753968.md)
- Mismatch-repair gene mutated in hypermutated colorectal tumors in the 276-tumor TCGA CRC cohort [PMID:22810696](../papers/22810696.md)
- Biallelic somatic mutation in ACINAR01 (MSI-H pancreatic acinar cell carcinoma), with >90% of sequenced tags mutated; clear causal driver of microsatellite instability in this tumor [PMID:24293293](../papers/24293293.md)
- Germline loss-of-function confers Lynch syndrome gastric cancer risk; standard multigene panel for familial gastric cancer includes MSH2 alongside MLH1, MSH6, PMS2, and EPCAM [PMID:24816255](../papers/24816255.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Gynecologic cancers: MSH2 somatic/germline mutations contribute to dMMR; [nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md) achieves durable responses in MSH2-deficient tumors [PMID:38653864](../papers/38653864.md)
- [Glioblastoma (GB)](../cancer_types/GB.md) — MSH2 is part of the mismatch repair pathway whose compromise, in the presence of [MGMT](../genes/MGMT.md) methylation and [temozolomide](../drugs/temozolomide.md) treatment, leads to hypermutation [PMID:18772890](../papers/18772890.md).
- Prostate cancer: MSH2 alterations enriched in MSI-H/dMMR subset; associated with ICB response [PMID:38949888](../papers/38949888.md)
- Pituitary neuroendocrine tumors: MSH2 mutations in aggressive, treatment-refractory corticotroph PitNETs [PMID:38758238](../papers/38758238.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-mutated with [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md), [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md) in MMR-deficient tumors [PMID:38653864](../papers/38653864.md) [PMID:38949888](../papers/38949888.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- MSH2-deficient dMMR/MSI-H tumors respond to [nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md) and other anti-PD-1 agents [PMID:38653864](../papers/38653864.md)
- MSH2 alterations support [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md) use in treatment-refractory PitNETs with MMR deficiency [PMID:38758238](../papers/38758238.md)
- Prostate cancer with MSH2-associated dMMR status shows durable ICB responses [PMID:38949888](../papers/38949888.md)

## Open questions

- The relative contributions of individual MMR gene defects (MSH2 vs. [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md) vs. [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md)) to differential ICB outcomes within dMMR populations are not well characterized.
