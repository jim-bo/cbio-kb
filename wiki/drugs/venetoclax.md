# venetoclax

## Overview

Selective small-molecule [BCL2](../genes/BCL2.md) inhibitor.

## Evidence in the corpus

- In the 1,148-patient CLL map, [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation in the absence of 17p deletion was not associated with adverse outcomes in U-CLL, consistent with the contemporary ibrutinib/venetoclax treatment era reducing the isolated TP53-mutation prognostic impact [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- [BCL2](../genes/BCL2.md) — the venetoclax target — is recurrently involved through Ig-locus translocations predominantly in M-CLL (5/88 WGS cases, 5.7%) via aberrant V(D)J recombination [PMID:35927489](../papers/35927489.md).
- Venetoclax (BCL-2 inhibitor) was discussed as a known therapeutic-targeting agent for [BCL2](../genes/BCL2.md)-expressing AML in the Beat AML program genomic landscape, alongside hypomethylating agents and DOT1L inhibitors as the emerging targeted-therapy armamentarium in AML [PMID:30333627](../papers/30333627.md).

## Resistance mechanisms
