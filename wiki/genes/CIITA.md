# CIITA

## Overview

CIITA (Class II Major Histocompatibility Complex Transactivator) is the master transcriptional regulator of MHC class II gene expression and is required for antigen presentation by professional antigen-presenting cells. In diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL), recurrent structural variants involving CIITA have been identified as a mechanism of immune escape, reducing MHC class II surface expression on tumor cells.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- CIITA is rearranged by structural variant in 3% of DLBCLs (n=304 cohort); SVs are listed among the recurrently rearranged immune-escape genes alongside [B2M](../genes/B2M.md), [CD70](../genes/CD70.md), [CD58](../genes/CD58.md), CD274/PD-L1, and PDCD1LG2/PD-L2; collectively, genetic immune-escape mechanisms are present in 74% of DLBCLs [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [DLBCLNOS](../cancer_types/DLBCLNOS.md): CIITA SVs in 3% of primary DLBCLs; enriched in immune-escape gene alteration landscape [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- CIITA SVs co-occur with other immune-escape alterations (B2M, CD58, CD70, CD274/PD-L1) as part of the 74% immune-escape frequency in DLBCL; enriched in C1 ABC-DLBCL subtype context [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- CIITA SVs reducing MHC class II expression are proposed as a rationale for immune checkpoint blockade in affected DLBCL patients, particularly in C1 tumors with frequent CD274/PDCD1LG2 SVs [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Open questions

- The specific CIITA SV partners and their functional consequences on MHC class II expression in each DLBCL subtype (C1 vs. others) have not been dissected in detail in this cohort.
