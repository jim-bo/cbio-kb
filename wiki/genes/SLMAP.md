# SLMAP

## Overview

SLMAP (Sarcolemmal Membrane Associated Protein) encodes a tail-anchored membrane protein involved in cardiac and skeletal muscle development. In cancer, SLMAP is primarily noted as a fusion partner in the NTRK3-SLMAP rearrangement, which produces an activating kinase fusion relevant as a therapeutic target for TRK inhibitors (e.g., larotrectinib, entrectinib).

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- NTRK3-SLMAP activating fusion identified in a neuroblastoma patient via cWGTS; this rearrangement constitutes a therapeutic target for TRK inhibitors and was detected in a pediatric oncology cohort evaluating comprehensive sequencing over clinical panels [PMID:35585047](../papers/35585047.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **Neuroblastoma (NBL):** NTRK3-SLMAP fusion identified as an actionable alteration; TRK inhibitor eligibility dependent on fusion detection [PMID:35585047](../papers/35585047.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- No co-occurrence or mutual exclusivity data yet in this corpus.

## Therapeutic relevance

- NTRK3-SLMAP fusion confers potential eligibility for TRK inhibitor therapy (e.g., larotrectinib, entrectinib); detected in neuroblastoma [PMID:35585047](../papers/35585047.md)

## Open questions
