# XPO5

## Overview

XPO5 (exportin 5) encodes a nuclear export receptor responsible for transporting pre-microRNAs (pre-miRNAs) from the nucleus to the cytoplasm, a critical step in the canonical miRNA biogenesis pathway. In cancer, XPO5 can be subject to copy-number gains and has been implicated as a cis-regulated candidate driver gene in hepatocellular carcinoma.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- XPO5 was identified as a cis-copy-number-driven candidate driver gene in HCC, dysregulated at both mRNA and protein levels co-regulated with copy number in a 122-biopsy proteogenomics cohort (hcc_meric_2021); among 9 genes dysregulated at both omics layers across five recurrent GISTIC2 focal CNA peaks [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)** — Cis-regulated amplification candidate across recurrent GISTIC2 peaks; protein and mRNA co-dysregulation confirmed in an integrated proteogenomics study of 114 treatment-naive patients [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- XPO5 dysregulation co-occurs with LRRC47 in the same set of 9 cis-driver candidates dysregulated at both mRNA and protein levels in the HCC proteogenomics cohort [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting of XPO5 was reported in the corpus. Given XPO5's role in miRNA transport, dysregulation of the miRNA biogenesis pathway in XPO5-amplified HCC may have downstream implications for oncogenic miRNA processing.

## Open questions

- Whether XPO5 amplification drives altered global miRNA expression patterns in HCC and whether this produces a distinct transcriptomic subtype remains to be characterized.
