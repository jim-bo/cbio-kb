# WDR87

## Overview

WDR87 (WD Repeat Domain 87) is a poorly characterised WD-repeat family protein. It was identified as a significantly mutated gene preferentially targeted in African American (AA) colorectal cancers in a whole-exome and targeted sequencing study, suggesting a potential ethnicity-associated role in CRC biology.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Identified as one of 20 significantly mutated genes preferentially targeted in African American CRCs (n=103 AA, n=129 Caucasian); part of a 15-gene set with significantly higher mutation frequency in AA than Caucasian CRCs [PMID:25583493](../papers/25583493.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [COADREAD](../cancer_types/COADREAD.md) — significantly mutated in African American CRCs; part of the 15-gene AA-preferential panel accounting for 41% of AA CRCs vs 15% of Caucasian CRCs [PMID:25583493](../papers/25583493.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-identified in the same AA-specific significantly mutated gene panel alongside [WASHC1](../genes/WASHC1.md), [ZNF717](../genes/ZNF717.md), [ZNF862](../genes/ZNF862.md), [EPHA6](../genes/EPHA6.md), [FLCN](../genes/FLCN.md), and others [PMID:25583493](../papers/25583493.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No drug targeting reported; biological function in CRC remains to be characterised [PMID:25583493](../papers/25583493.md)

## Open questions

- Functional consequences of WDR87 mutations in CRC have not been experimentally validated in this study; mechanism of action unknown [PMID:25583493](../papers/25583493.md)
