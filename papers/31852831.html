Genomic and phenotypic characterization of a broad panel of patient derived xenografts reflects the diversity of glioblastoma

TL;DR

Vaubel et al. report the comprehensive genomic and phenotypic characterization of the Mayo Clinic patient-derived xenograft (PDX) panel — 96 glioblastoma PDX lines established from 261 glioma patients surgically resected at Mayo Clinic between 2000 and 2017. Whole-exome sequencing (n=83), RNA-seq (n=68) and genome-wide methylation profiling (n=76) show that the panel recapitulates the canonical genetic drivers of GBM (TERT promoter mutation, EGFR, PTEN, TP53, CDKN2A, BRAF, IDH1) at frequencies broadly comparable to TCGA, with one striking exception: PDGFRA amplification is absent. WES of 24 matched patient–PDX pairs shows preservation of most driver alterations (including EGFR amplification in 100% of pairs), although four pairs gained or lost drivers consistent with clonal selection. MGMT promoter methylation correlated with response to temozolomide in the PDX panel, mirroring the human clinical phenotype.

Cohort & data

  • Panel size: 96 PDX lines from 261 glioma patients (overall engraftment rate 36%; WHO grade IV engraftment 47%; newly diagnosed 50%, recurrent 42%) (PMID:31852831).
  • Histology of viable PDX: GBM, IDH-wildtype (n=93); GBM, IDH-mutant (n=2); diffuse midline glioma, H3 K27M-mutant (DMG, n=1). Primary tumors n=60; recurrent n=34 (PMID:31852831).
  • Dataset: gbm_mayo_pdx_sarkaria_2019 — Mayo Clinic Brain Tumor PDX National Resource (PMID:31852831).
  • Assays: whole-exome-seq n=83 (SureSelect / TGen Strexome V2; mouse reads removed with Xenome); rna-seq n=68 (TruSeq libraries); epic-methylation-array n=76 (Illumina MethylationEPIC at MD Anderson); sanger-sequencing for IDH1/2 and TERT promoter; methylation-specific PCR (qmsp) for MGMT promoter (n=78); fish for EGFR/MDM2/N-MYC/PDGFRA validation; immunohistochemistry for GFAP/Ki67/EGFR/PDGFRA; str-profiling for tissue provenance (PMID:31852831).
  • Reference comparison set: TCGA glioblastoma cohort (gbm_tcga) used as the frequency benchmark for driver alterations (PMID:31852831).
  • Patient–PDX pairs: matched WES on 24 patient tumors with derivative PDX; germline WES on 55 patients (PMID:31852831).

Key findings

  • Engraftment is restricted to grade IV astrocytomas. No viable PDX were established from any of 59 WHO grade II–III glioma samples. Within grade IV, engraftment did not correlate with patient age, sex, or overall survival (newly diagnosed p=0.76; recurrent p=0.11) (PMID:31852831).
  • Driver landscape mirrors TCGA glioblastoma. TERT promoter mutation 86%, homozygous deletion of CDKN2A 70% (n=59), TP53 mutation 36% (n=30), PTEN alteration 48% (n=40), NF1 alteration 17% (n=14), RB1 loss/mutation 16% (n=13), BRAF alteration 4% (n=3) (PMID:31852831).
  • EGFR is the dominant RTK alteration. 45% of PDX (n=37/83) carried at least one EGFR alteration; 35 had high-level amplification, 14 SNVs were observed across 12 lines (11 of those with concurrent amplification), and 16 PDX harbored exon-deletion variants including EGFRvIII (n=11, Δexon2–7), EGFRvII (n=2, Δexon14–15), c-terminal deletions (n=2, Δexon25–27), and a previously uncharacterized Δexon6–7 (n=1). EGFR protein expression by IHC was strongly correlated with copy number (PMID:31852831).
  • PDGFRA amplification is absent from the panel. No PDX showed PDGFRA amplification despite a ~15% expected frequency in IDH-wildtype glioblastoma; three PDX carried PDGFRA SNVs (including oncogenic D842V), and 29% of PDX still showed strong PDGFRA protein expression (PMID:31852831).
  • Other RTK pathway alterations are sparser. MET amplification n=2; FGFR3 amplification n=1; p53 pathway also includes MDM2 amplification (n=8, 10%) and MDM4 amplification (n=2, 2%); Rb pathway also inactivated by CDK4 amplification (n=7, 8%) or CDK6 amplification (n=1, 1%). MYC (n=3) and MYCN (n=2) amplifications are also captured (PMID:31852831).
  • Expression and methylation subtypes are broadly represented. Of 68 PDX with RNA-seq: classical 46% (n=31), mesenchymal 31% (n=21), proneural 23% (n=16); classical subgroup enriched for EGFR amplification (78% vs 29% mesenchymal / 7% proneural). Methylation classes (n=76): three LGm1 (IDH-mutant), LGm4 46%, LGm5 44%, LGm6 10% (PMID:31852831).
  • Recurrent-glioma-associated alterations are captured. Four recurrence-derived PDX carry multiple MSH6 mutations with high mutation burden (hypermutation phenotype); variants of LTBP4 (14%), IGF1R (7%) and PRDM2 (7%) were identified in recurrent PDX at frequencies similar to prior reports. GBM154 (recurrence of a low-grade oligodendroglioma 10 years post-diagnosis) lost IDH1 p.R132H but gained a novel IDH1 p.F397L while retaining 1p/19q co-deletion, CHEK2, and TERT promoter mutations (PMID:31852831).
  • Recurrent and novel fusions. FGFR3–TACC3 fusions in three lines (GBM14, GBM148, GBM232); two non-canonical FGFR3 fusions (FGFR3–TRIM54, FGFR3–CALCOCO1); previously reported PTPRZ1–MET and CHTOP–NTRK1 fusions in three PDX, both representing targetable alterations (PMID:31852831).
  • Therapy response mirrors patient response. Across 37 PDX treated orthotopically, median survival ratio relative to placebo was 1.6 for RT, 2.5 for temozolomide monotherapy, and 2.8 for concurrent RT/TMZ. MGMT promoter methylation was strongly associated with TMZ and RT/TMZ benefit but not RT alone. Single-agent bevacizumab gave a survival ratio >1.3 in 23% of PDX (7/30). Patient and PDX survival under standard therapy were positively correlated (Spearman ρ=0.53) (PMID:31852831).
  • Patient–PDX concordance is high but not perfect. Across 24 matched patient–PDX pairs, EGFR amplification was preserved in 100% (also confirmed by FISH in four pairs). Four pairs (GBM168, GBM110, GBM159, GBM181) showed clinically meaningful divergence consistent with clonal selection: e.g., GBM110 acquired MYCN amplification in PDX (FISH: subclonal 0–27% in patient, 100% in PDX); GBM159 lost PDGFRA amplification (45–75% of patient tumor cells with two amplified subclones distinguishable by centromeric probe co-amplification) and gained CDK6 amplification in PDX; GBM168 patient and PDX shared EGFR/CDK4/MDM2 amplifications but had different EGFR exon-deletion variants, plus a private PTEN alteration in the patient and a private TP53 variant in the PDX; GBM181 showed many private SNVs in both directions consistent with MSH6-driven MMR-deficient hypermutation (PMID:31852831).
  • Blood–brain barrier integrity varies between models. GBM6 intact; GBM108 partial disruption; GBM39 more complete disruption — relevant to drug-delivery design choices (PMID:31852831).

Genes & alterations

  • EGFR: amplification (n=35, 42%), 14 SNVs across 12 PDX, exon-deletion variants in 16 PDX (EGFRvIII n=11, EGFRvII n=2, C-terminal deletion n=2, Δexon6–7 n=1). Amplification preserved in 100% of 24 matched patient–PDX pairs; different exon-deletion variants in GBM168 between patient and PDX (PMID:31852831).
  • PDGFRA: no amplification observed in any PDX despite ~15% expected frequency in IDH-wildtype GBM; three PDX with SNVs including D842V; PDX-engraftment-associated loss demonstrated in GBM159 (FISH: amplified in 45–75% of patient tumor cells, not in PDX) (PMID:31852831).
  • TERT: promoter mutations in 86% of PDX, the most frequent alteration in the panel (PMID:31852831).
  • CDKN2A: homozygous deletion in 70% (n=59); GBM164 and GBM187 showed homozygous loss in PDX vs single-copy loss in matched patient — clonal-selection candidate (PMID:31852831).
  • TP53: mutation in 36% (n=30); GBM168 PDX carried a private TP53 variant absent from the patient tumor (PMID:31852831).
  • PTEN: alteration in 48% (n=40); GBM168 patient carried a private PTEN alteration not detected in the PDX (PMID:31852831).
  • MDM2 / MDM4: amplification in 10% and 2% respectively; MDM2 amplification preserved in 100% of GBM108 patient and PDX cells by FISH (PMID:31852831).
  • RB1 / CDK4 / CDK6: Rb pathway inactivated by RB1 loss/mutation (16%), CDK4 amplification (8%), or CDK6 amplification (1%) (PMID:31852831).
  • NF1 / BRAF: MAPK pathway alterations in 17% and 4% of PDX, respectively (PMID:31852831).
  • MYC / MYCN: amplifications in 3 and 2 PDX, respectively; GBM110 PDX acquired N-MYC amplification (subclonal 0–27% in patient by FISH) (PMID:31852831).
  • MET: amplification in 2 PDX; copy number gains in both IDH-mutant lines (GBM164, GBM196), consistent with the secondary-glioblastoma literature (PMID:31852831).
  • FGFR3: amplification n=1; canonical FGFR3–TACC3 fusion in three PDX; two novel fusions FGFR3–TRIM54 and FGFR3–CALCOCO1 (PMID:31852831).
  • MSH6: multiple mutations in four recurrent-tumor-derived PDX, driving MMR-deficient hypermutation phenotype; GBM181 patient–PDX pair shows the pattern of many private SNVs in both directions (PMID:31852831).
  • IDH1 / ATRX / H3-3A: IDH1-mutant PDX (GBM164, GBM196) carried TP53 mutations and homozygous CDKN2A deletion; GBM36 (cerebellar diffuse midline glioma) carried H3F3A (H3-3A) p.K27M, ATRX and TP53 mutations; GBM154 lost IDH1 p.R132H but acquired IDH1 p.F397L on recurrence (PMID:31852831).
  • PTPRZ1 / NTRK1 / CHTOP: PTPRZ1–MET and CHTOP–NTRK1 fusions in three PDX — flagged as potentially targetable (PMID:31852831).
  • LTBP4 / IGF1R / PRDM2: variants identified in recurrent PDX at frequencies similar to prior reports (14%, 7%, 7% of 28 recurrent PDX), though some also seen in primary PDX (possible germline) (PMID:31852831).
  • CIC / FUBP1 / CHEK2: present in the original oligodendroglioma that gave rise to GBM154; only CHEK2 (and TERT promoter mutation, plus 1p/19q co-deletion) was retained in the high-grade recurrent PDX (PMID:31852831).

Clinical implications

  • MGMT methylation predicts TMZ benefit, not RT benefit. In the PDX panel, MGMT promoter methylation was strongly associated with response to temozolomide and concurrent RT/TMZ, but not response to radiation alone — directly mirroring the human clinical phenotype and supporting the use of MGMT status as a stratification biomarker in glioblastoma trials (PMID:31852831).
  • PDX panel suitable for 1×1×1 preclinical trial designs. The authors explicitly position the Mayo PDX collection as the substrate for population-level “preclinical phase II” trials; they cite the veliparib + temozolomide work in 28 of these PDX (efficacy limited to MGMT-methylated tumors) as the biomarker-enrichment rationale for the Alliance veliparib trial (PMID:31852831).
  • Targetable fusions present. FGFR3–TACC3, PTPRZ1–MET, and CHTOP–NTRK1 fusions are present in the panel, providing models for testing FGFR, MET, and TRK inhibitors in glioblastoma (PMID:31852831).
  • bevacizumab sensitivity is rare and unstratified. Single-agent bevacizumab gave a survival ratio >1.3 in only 23% of PDX (7/30); the authors do not identify a predictive biomarker, consistent with the modest benefit observed clinically (PMID:31852831).
  • Practical caution on PDGFRA-amplified disease. Because no PDGFRA-amplified PDX engrafted (and GBM5 lost PDGFRA amplification by passage eight), the Mayo panel is not suitable for studying the ~15% of glioblastoma patients with PDGFRA amplification — investigators studying this subset must look elsewhere (e.g., BTIC or GEMM models) (PMID:31852831).
  • Open-resource availability. All models and accompanying genomic data are openly available to the neuro-oncology research community via the Mayo PDX National Resource (PMID:31852831).

Limitations & open questions

  • Selection bias against PDGFRA-amplified tumors. The complete absence of PDGFRA amplification in 83 sequenced PDX (vs ~15% expected) is unexplained; authors raise both engraftment failure and serial-passage loss as candidates but cannot adjudicate (PMID:31852831).
  • Drift over serial passage is not directly assessed. WES was performed at varying passage; the manuscript does not characterize evolution across multiple passages of the same line and only recommends restoring from early-passage frozen stock and re-verifying alterations of interest before downstream studies (PMID:31852831).
  • Orthotopic PDX lack microvascular proliferation and necrosis. Fewer than 20% of orthotopic PDX showed these diagnostic features of glioblastoma, whereas >80% of flank PDX show palisading necrosis — implying microenvironmental contribution to these phenotypes and potentially limiting fidelity for studies of vascular biology or pseudopalisading necrosis (PMID:31852831).
  • Methylation classification is noisy in PDX. Principal component analysis did not cleanly separate PDX by methylation class, attributed by the authors to predominance of IDH-wildtype tumors, contaminating mouse stroma, and effects of higher passage number (PMID:31852831).
  • No intact immune system. As athymic-nude-mouse xenografts, the panel is unsuitable for immunotherapy studies — a limitation the authors flag as motivating complementary GEMM resources (PMID:31852831).
  • Engraftment limited to grade IV. Zero of 59 grade II–III gliomas engrafted, restricting the panel’s reach to the most aggressive end of the glioma spectrum (PMID:31852831).
  • Clinical correlation of patient–PDX response is moderate. Spearman ρ=0.53 between patient and matched-PDX survival is supportive but leaves substantial unexplained variance; the authors do not break this down by treatment arm or MGMT status (PMID:31852831).
