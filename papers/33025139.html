TL;DR

Mansouri et al. assembled the largest multi-platform molecular profile of schwannomatosis-associated schwannomas (SWNTS-SWNs) to date — 165 tumors from 72 patients — and compared them to non-syndromic sporadic schwannomas (NS-SWNs). DNA methylation, whole-exome, whole-genome and RNA sequencing reveal that SWNTS-SWNs split into four methylation subgroups tied to anatomic location, carry higher copy-number burden (especially in LZTR1-mutant cases), and harbor the SH3PXD2A–HTRA1 fusion in ~14% of cases — predominantly in LZTR1-mutant tumors. Painful tumors show coordinated upregulation of RAS/MAPK, PIGF/VEGF, mTOR, and ERBB2 pathways, nominating MEK inhibitors (e.g., selumetinib) and anti-angiogenics (e.g., bevacizumab) as therapeutic candidates for tumor and pain management.

Cohort & data

  • 165 SWNs from 72 schwannomatosis patients (F/M: 31/41), assembled through the Children’s Tumor Foundation Synodos for Schwannomatosis Consortium and now released as cBioPortal study schw_ctf_synodos_2025 (PMID:33025139).
  • Comparator: non-syndromic sporadic schwannomas (NS-SWNs) and 90 neurofibromas (56 plexiform, 34 cutaneous) for DNA methylation profiling (PMID:33025139).
  • Histology: schwannoma (peripheral nerve sheath tumor); related context for MPNST excluded.
  • Assays per tumor: Illumina Infinium HumanMethylationEPIC (epic-methylation-array); whole-exome sequencing on 29 SWNTS-SWNs + 25 NS-SWNs (Agilent SureSelect All Exon V5+UTRs, ≥250× tumor / 50× normal); whole-genome sequencing on 22 SWNTS-SWNs (Illumina HiSeqX, 60× tumor / 30× normal); RNA-seq on 24 SWNTS-SWNs + 41 NS-SWNs (TruSeq Stranded Total RNA / Ribo-Zero Gold).
  • Variant calling: MuTect, Strelka, HaplotypeCaller; SVs by DELLY v0.8.1 with MAVIS validation; CN/LOH by Sequenza; fusion discovery by FusionCatcher with RT-PCR confirmation; focal CN peaks by GISTIC; pathway analysis by GSEA; immune deconvolution by CIBERSORTx; NF2 coding-region screen by Sanger sequencing (ABI 3100/310); methylation clustering by ConsensusClusterPlus + Ward linkage + t-SNE on top 10,000 variably methylated CpGs (PMID:33025139).
  • Reference genome: hg19 (GRCh37) for DNA; hg38 for RNA-seq alignment.

Key findings

  • DNA methylation separates SWNs from neurofibromas, not from NS-SWNs. Unsupervised clustering of the top 2,000 most variably methylated CpGs cleanly partitioned SWNs (SWNTS + NS) from neurofibromas, but SWNTS-SWNs and NS-SWNs intermixed — supporting a shared Schwann-cell origin (PMID:33025139, Fig. 1a–b).
  • Global hypomethylation in SWNTS-SWNs. 75% of differentially methylated CpGs were hypomethylated in SWNTS-SWNs relative to NS-SWNs, concentrated in CpG islands and promoter regions. LINE-1 repetitive elements were significantly hypomethylated (Student’s t, p = 0.0005), with corresponding 5.17-fold upregulation of L1TD1 (q = 0.039) and higher TET1/TET2 expression (PMID:33025139).
  • Four methylation subgroups within SWNTS-SWNs, stable on consensus clustering of N = 88, segregated by anatomic location (lower extremity, upper extremity, spinal, truncal, head & neck). Clusters 1–4 showed dominant upregulation of cAMP, KRAS, MEK/PIGF, and PIGF/VEGF programs respectively; tumors from the same patient at different anatomic sites resolved into different clusters, indicating origin-of-cell heterogeneity (Fig. 1d–f, PMID:33025139).
  • Tumor mutation burden is low and similar across SWNTS-SWNs and NS-SWNs. SWNTS-SWN: WES 0.17/Mb (5.92/sample), WGS 0.23/Mb (8.81/sample); NS-SWN: WES 0.22/Mb (6.08/sample). C>T transitions dominate; COSMIC signatures present in ≥2 SWNTS-SWNs include 1A (MUTYH, 26/29), 6 (MMR, 7/29), 15 (MMR, 4/29), and 2 (APOBEC, 3/29). Signature 2 was absent from NS-SWNs; signature 20 appeared in 3/24 NS-SWNs (PMID:33025139).
  • NF2 is the only recurrent driver SNV but is more frequently hit in syndromic tumors. SWNTS-SWNs vs. NS-SWNs harboring NF2 mutation + copy-number loss: 83% vs. 58% (Chi-square, p < 0.0001). All germline LZTR1-mutant SWNTS-SWNs had either an NF2 mutation or deletion (PMID:33025139, Fig. 2a).
  • 22q deletions are far more common in SWNTS-SWNs: 80% vs. 30% in NS-SWNs (Chi-square, p < 0.01), with 76% vs. 35% of SWNTS-SWNs vs. NS-SWNs showing deep deletions across LZTR1, SMARCB1, and NF2. Other genes on 22q11.21–q11.23 — MAPK1, BCR, EWSR1, PATZ1, ZNRF3, MYH9 — were deleted in 77% and their expression significantly reduced (PMID:33025139).
  • Three-hit / four-hit progression confirmed, with 9/29 (31%) of SWNTS-SWN samples showing two hits (22q deletion) without somatic NF2 mutation — a pattern absent from NS-SWNs (PMID:33025139).
  • Recurrent non-22q arm-level deletions in chromosomes 4, 16, 19, and 21 were specific to LZTR1-mutant tumors. LZTR1-mutant SWNTS-SWNs carried ~9-fold higher CNV than LZTR1-wild-type (Student’s t, p = 0.0072). Overall CNV in SWNTS-SWNs vs. NS-SWNs: WES 5.97% vs. 0.86%, WGS 2.40% (Student’s t, p = 0.005) (PMID:33025139, Fig. 2c–d).
  • Structural variants: 1 recurrent inversion, 4 inter-chromosomal translocations, and 4 inter-chromosomal inverted translocations seen in ≥2/22 WGS tumors. Most frequent (3/22): inverted translocation between ZNF708 (Chr 19p) and ZNF138 (Chr 7q); significance unknown (PMID:33025139).
  • SH3PXD2A–HTRA1 fusion confirmed in SWNTS-SWNs. RT-PCR detected the fusion in 14% (≈3/22) of SWNTS-SWNs, enriched in males (14/22, 63%), and strongly associated with germline LZTR1 mutation (15/22, 68%; Chi-square, p = 0.025) (PMID:33025139). The fusion was originally described in ~10% of sporadic schwannomas (Agnihotri et al. 2016, Nat Genet — not yet in this wiki corpus).
  • Other fusions: NAIP–OCLN on Chr 5 was significantly higher in SWNTS-SWNs (67%) vs. NS-SWNs (29%) (Chi-square, p < 0.01). The KANSL1–ARL17A KANSARL predisposition fusion was present at similar rates in both groups (PMID:33025139).
  • Transcriptomic upregulation in SWNTS-SWNs vs. NS-SWNs of PIGF, VEGF, MEK, ERBB2, and SHH pathways; RB is the top downregulated pathway. Cell cycle, cell division, and DNA repair programs are coordinately upregulated; MMR/DNA-repair genes MSH3, MSH6, PMS2, and MLH3 are significantly higher (Student’s t, q < 0.01), with MSH6 and MLH1 showing concordant promoter hypomethylation. LZTR1 and SMARCB1 transcripts are significantly reduced in SWNTS-SWNs (q < 0.001) (PMID:33025139, Fig. 4).
  • Immune contexture differs from NS-SWNs. SWNTS-SWNs have higher naïve B cells, plasma cells, and activated NK cells but lower total macrophages and CD8+ T cells (Student’s t, p < 0.05). NF2-mutant SWNTS-SWNs are enriched for M2 macrophages (p < 0.01). HLA-A/-B/-C/-DPB1 expression is reduced; CD28 and CD80 are upregulated (PMID:33025139, Fig. 5a–h, j).
  • Genomic correlates of pain. Painful SWNTS-SWNs are enriched in: lower extremity location (58%, Chi-square p = 0.001), female sex (64%, z-score p < 0.001), germline LZTR1 mutation (57%, Chi-square p = 0.002), and SH3PXD2A-HTRA1 fusion (16%, z-score p = 0.039). They carry 4.4-fold higher CNV (p = 0.042); TMB is not significantly different. Upregulated pathways in painful tumors: RAS/MAPK, PIGF/VEGF, ERBB2/HER2, RB/RAF, mTOR, MEK. Painful tumors also carry higher total mast-cell infiltrates (Student’s t, p < 0.01) (PMID:33025139).

Genes & alterations

  • LZTR1 — germline LZTR1 mutation defines a SWNTS-SWN subclass with markedly higher CNV (~9-fold), recurrent non-22q arm-level deletions (Chr 4/16/19/21), enrichment for SH3PXD2A-HTRA1 fusion, NF2 co-loss, painful clinical course, and upregulated RAS/MAPK + ERBB2/VEGF programs (PMID:33025139).
  • NF2 — sole recurrent driver SNV across all SWNs; SWNTS-SWNs harbor NF2 mutation/loss in 83% vs. 58% in NS-SWNs (p < 0.0001). NF2-mutant tumors show RAS/MAPK upregulation and M2 macrophage enrichment (PMID:33025139).
  • SMARCB1 — co-deleted on 22q in 76% of SWNTS-SWNs; significantly lower transcript level than in NS-SWNs (q < 0.001) (PMID:33025139).
  • SH3PXD2A–HTRA1 — recurrent gene fusion in 14% of SWNTS-SWNs (3/22 WGS samples by RT-PCR), enriched in LZTR1-mutant and painful tumors; therapeutic rationale for MEK inhibition by analogy with sporadic schwannomas (PMID:33025139).
  • 22q neighbors of NF2/SMARCB1 — MAPK1, BCR, EWSR1, PATZ1, ZNRF3, MYH9: deleted in 77% of cases on 22q11.21–q11.23 with coordinated expression loss; proposed as additional contributors to schwannomatosis pathogenesis (PMID:33025139).
  • MMR / DNA-repair genes: MSH3, MSH6, PMS2, MLH3 upregulated transcriptionally; MLH1 and MSH6 promoter-hypomethylated in SWNTS-SWNs; BRCA1/BRCA2 also up. COSMIC signatures 6/15 are present but no 22q microsatellite instability is detected (PMID:33025139).
  • Demethylation machinery: TET1, TET2 upregulated; DNMT1, DNMT3A unchanged. L1TD1 (LINE-1-encoded) upregulated 5.17-fold (q = 0.039) (PMID:33025139).
  • Novel deleterious SNVs validated by Sanger in single cases: MYPN, CAV3, SMOX — non-recurrent (PMID:33025139).
  • Other recurrent fusions: NAIP–OCLN on Chr 5 (67% SWNTS-SWN vs. 29% NS-SWN, p < 0.01); KANSARL (KANSL1–ARL17A) at similar rates in both groups (PMID:33025139).

Clinical implications

  • MEK inhibition (e.g., selumetinib) is nominated as a therapeutic strategy in SWNTS-SWNs, particularly tumors carrying germline LZTR1 mutation, somatic NF2 mutation, the SH3PXD2A-HTRA1 fusion, or anatomic location in extremities — all of which show coordinated RAS/MAPK upregulation. The authors note FDA approval of MEK inhibitors for plexiform neurofibromas in NF1 (Gross et al. 2020, NEJM) as supporting precedent (PMID:33025139).
  • Anti-angiogenic therapy (bevacizumab / Avastin and PIGF-targeting compounds) is proposed for management of painful SWNTS-SWNs given PIGF/VEGF/RAF pathway upregulation in painful tumors. The authors specifically call for clinical trials with pain alleviation and quality-of-life as primary endpoints (PMID:33025139).
  • mTOR inhibition is suggested for chronic pain management, citing established mTOR involvement in pain initiation/maintenance and observed mTOR pathway upregulation in painful SWNTS-SWNs and tumors from extremities (PMID:33025139).
  • Methylation-based subclassification (4 clusters by anatomic origin) may guide management. Each subgroup has a distinct dominant pathway (cAMP / KRAS / MEK-PIGF / PIGF-VEGF) that could inform targeted therapy selection — even when tumors are histologically identical (PMID:33025139).
  • Clinical risk stratification: painful SWNTS-SWNs are statistically enriched in lower-extremity tumors from female patients with germline LZTR1 mutations and SH3PXD2A-HTRA1 fusion — a clinically tractable signature for prioritizing aggressive intervention (PMID:33025139).

Limitations & open questions

  • Modest sequencing cohort sizes despite being the largest schwannomatosis series to date: 29 WES + 22 WGS + 24 RNA-seq SWNTS-SWN samples — statistical power for rare-variant discovery is limited (e.g., MYPN/CAV3/SMOX SNVs were non-recurrent) (PMID:33025139).
  • Inferred mechanism for MMR-gene upregulation (promoter hypomethylation) without direct demonstration; the source of low-level COSMIC signatures 6/15 in MSI-negative tumors is not resolved (PMID:33025139).
  • Significance of the recurrent ZNF708–ZNF138 inverted translocation is unknown; no functional follow-up (PMID:33025139).
  • Therapeutic claims are correlative, not validated in preclinical models or trials within this paper; the MEK / VEGF / mTOR proposals require prospective evaluation (PMID:33025139).
  • No NF2-mutant germline cases were enrolled (intentional, to focus on SWNTS), so the molecular boundary between SWNTS and mosaic NF2 disease remains incompletely separated (PMID:33025139).
  • Reference-genome split (DNA aligned to hg19, RNA aligned to hg38) is documented but introduces potential coordinate-conversion artifacts in multi-omic integration (PMID:33025139).
  • The patient/tumor identifier table is in Online Resource Table 1; this wiki page does not attempt to enumerate per-sample annotations.
