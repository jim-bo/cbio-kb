## TL;DR

Roelands et al. built AC-ICAM (Sidra-LUMC Atlas and Compass of Immune-Cancer-Microbiome interactions), a multi-omics resource profiling 348 treatment-naive primary [colon cancer](../cancer_types/COAD.md) patients with [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md), whole-exome sequencing, TCR sequencing, 16S rRNA gene sequencing, and tumor whole-genome sequencing on paired tumor/normal colon tissue. They show that the 20-gene Immunologic Constant of Rejection (ICR) signature outperforms consensus molecular subtype (CMS) and MSI classification for prognosis, that quantifying genetic immunoediting further refines its prognostic value, and that a *Ruminococcus bromii*-driven microbiome signature combined with ICR (mICRoScore) identifies a subgroup with excellent survival ([PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md)).

## Cohort & data

- 348 treatment-naive patients with primary [colon adenocarcinoma](../cancer_types/COAD.md) from the Sidra-LUMC [AC-ICAM cohort](../datasets/coad_silu_2022.md); fresh-frozen tumor and matched healthy colon tissue ([PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md)).
- Platforms: [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md), [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md), [deep TCRβ sequencing](../methods/tcr-seq.md) (immunoSEQ), [16S rRNA gene sequencing](../methods/16s-rrna-seq.md), and tumor [whole-genome sequencing](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) for microbiome characterization ([PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md)).
- An additional 42 tumor-only samples (ICAM42) were profiled with 16S rRNA sequencing ([PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md)).
- Median follow-up 4.6 years ([PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md)).
- [TCGA-COAD (coadread_tcga)](../datasets/coadread_tcga.md) used as a comparator; limited survival follow-up and tumor-purity selection criteria motivated the new cohort ([PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md)).

## Key findings

- Consensus clustering on the 20-gene [ICR signature](../methods/icr-signature.md) segregated AC-ICAM into ICR-high (hot), ICR-medium, and ICR-low (cold) subtypes ([PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md)).
- ICR was prognostic for overall survival: ICR-high vs ICR-low HR = 0.54 (95% CI 0.34–0.86, P = 0.0095); ICR-medium vs ICR-low HR = 0.63 (0.43–0.91, P = 0.014) ([PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md)).
- ICR outperformed [CMS](../methods/cms-classifier.md) and MSI classifications for prognosis and retained prognostic value within the otherwise heterogeneous CMS4/mesenchymal subtype ([PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md)).
- NK and T cell subset abundances ([ConsensusTME](../methods/consensus-tme.md)) were highest in ICR-high tumors across all CMS subtypes ([PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md)).
- Quantifying [genetic immunoediting](../methods/immunoediting-quantification.md) (fewer observed neoantigens than expected) refined ICR's prognostic value ([PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md)).
- A microbiome signature driven by *Ruminococcus bromii* was associated with favorable outcome ([PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md)).
- The composite mICRoScore (ICR + microbiome signature) identified a subgroup with excellent survival probability and was validated within the resource ([PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md)).

## Genes & alterations

- The 20-gene ICR panel includes T-helper-1 signaling genes ([IFNG](../genes/IFNG.md), [TBX21](../genes/TBX21.md), [CD8A](../genes/CD8A.md), [CD8B](../genes/CD8B.md), [IL12B](../genes/IL12B.md), [STAT1](../genes/STAT1.md), [IRF1](../genes/IRF1.md)), CXCR3/CCR5 chemokine ligands ([CCL5](../genes/CCL5.md), [CXCL9](../genes/CXCL9.md), [CXCL10](../genes/CXCL10.md)), effector cytotoxic genes ([GNLY](../genes/GNLY.md), [PRF1](../genes/PRF1.md), [GZMA](../genes/GZMA.md), [GZMB](../genes/GZMB.md), [GZMH](../genes/GZMH.md)), and immunoregulatory counter-activation genes ([CD274](../genes/CD274.md), [CTLA4](../genes/CTLA4.md), [FOXP3](../genes/FOXP3.md), [IDO1](../genes/IDO1.md), [PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md)) ([PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md)).
- No single driver gene is proposed as a biomarker; the paper's gene-level claims are about the ICR module as a whole ([PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md)).

## Clinical implications

- ICR-based immune classification is a stronger prognostic biomarker than CMS or MSI status in primary colon cancer and could be incorporated into risk stratification ([PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md)).
- Combining tumor immune status with gut microbiome features (mICRoScore) can identify very-good-prognosis patients, suggesting a rationale for microbiome-aware prognostic models ([PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md)).
- ICR also correlates with immunotherapy response in other tumor types referenced by the authors, suggesting potential predictive utility that this paper does not directly test in colon cancer ([PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md)).

## Limitations & open questions

- AC-ICAM is a single multi-center European cohort of treatment-naive primary colon cancers; generalization to rectal cancer, metastatic disease, or diverse populations is not established ([PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md)).
- Microbiome composition is sensitive to sample handling, diet, and geography; the *R. bromii* signal requires external validation across cohorts ([PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md)).
- The paper validates mICRoScore internally; prospective validation and standardized assays for a fresh-frozen, multi-omics workflow would be required for clinical deployment ([PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md)).
- Immunoediting quantification depends on neoantigen prediction pipelines, which have known noise ([PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md)).
