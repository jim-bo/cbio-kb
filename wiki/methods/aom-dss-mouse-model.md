# AOM/DSS mouse model of colitis-associated cancer

## Overview

The AOM (azoxymethane)/DSS (dextran sulfate sodium) mouse model induces colitis-associated colorectal cancer by combining a chemical carcinogen (AOM) with a colitis-inducing agent (DSS). It recapitulates the inflammatory microenvironment of IBD-associated cancer and is widely used to study the genomic and molecular features of colitis-associated carcinogenesis.

## Used by

- [PMID:36611031](../papers/36611031.md) — used to model independent genesis of multiple neoplastic lesions in the same host; two close-proximity neoplastic lesions per mouse showed few shared genetic events (one mouse shared a [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) mutation; the other had no shared alterations), corroborating findings of genetically independent multifocal tumors in the human CAC cohort [PMID:36611031](../papers/36611031.md).

## Notes

- Supports the absence of a shared genomic field defect across spatially proximal IBD-associated lesions.
