# Anal Squamous Cell Carcinoma (ANSC)

## Overview

Anal squamous cell carcinoma (ANSC) is a squamous cell carcinoma of the anal canal and perianal skin. In OncoTree it sits under BOWEL (large intestine / anal tract) with main type Anal Cancer. It is strongly associated with HPV infection (predominantly HPV-16/18) and is treated with concurrent chemoradiation (the Nigro protocol). Immune checkpoint blockade has been evaluated in advanced/metastatic ANSC.

## Cohorts in the corpus

- N=1 anal cancer sample included in the pan-tumor ICB genomics cohort of [PMID:30150660](../papers/30150660.md) (Miao et al. 2018; pooled WES of 249 MSS tumors from seven ICB cohorts).

## Recurrent alterations

- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — clonal hotspot mutation observed in the responder (CR/PR) subset across the pooled ICB cohort that included ANSC; response association is cancer-type context-dependent [PMID:30150660](../papers/30150660.md).

## Subtypes

- Squamous cell carcinoma is the predominant histology (~85% of anal cancers); adenocarcinoma and other types are rarer and classified separately in OncoTree.

## Therapeutic landscape

- Anti-PD-1/PD-L1 immune checkpoint blockade ([pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md), [nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md)) — early evidence of activity in MSS anal cancer evaluated in ICB cohorts [PMID:30150660](../papers/30150660.md).

## Sources

- [PMID:30150660](../papers/30150660.md) — Miao et al. "Genomic correlates of response to immune checkpoint blockade in microsatellite-stable solid tumors." *Nature Genetics* 2018.
