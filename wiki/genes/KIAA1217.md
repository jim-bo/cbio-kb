# KIAA1217

## Overview

KIAA1217 is a gene encoding a protein of poorly characterized function. In cholangiocarcinoma genomics, KIAA1217 has been identified as a recurrent [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) fusion partner in intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma (IHC), where FGFR2–KIAA1217 fusions are among the most frequent FGFR2 fusion events and represent a potentially actionable therapeutic target.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- KIAA1217 identified as one of the most frequent FGFR2 fusion partners in intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma (IHC) in a prospective MSK-IMPACT cohort (n=195); FGFR2 gene fusions present in 14% of IHC with wide breakpoint variation across multiple partners including [BICC1](../genes/BICC1.md) and KIAA1217. [PMID:29848569](../papers/29848569.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [CHOL](../cancer_types/CHOL.md): KIAA1217 acts as a recurrent FGFR2 fusion partner in intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma; FGFR2 fusions including FGFR2–KIAA1217 are targetable by pan-FGFR inhibitors such as [infigratinib](../drugs/infigratinib.md). [PMID:29848569](../papers/29848569.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- FGFR2 fusions (including FGFR2–KIAA1217) are exclusive to the intrahepatic subtype and mutually exclusive with [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), and other EHC-enriched alterations. [PMID:29848569](../papers/29848569.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- FGFR2–KIAA1217 fusion is a potentially actionable alteration in IHC; targeted by pan-FGFR inhibitors (e.g., [infigratinib](../drugs/infigratinib.md)); patients with FGFR2 fusions were enrolled in FGFR inhibitor trials. [PMID:29848569](../papers/29848569.md)

## Open questions

- The precise molecular consequence of the FGFR2–KIAA1217 fusion (kinase activation mechanism, dimerization properties) has not been fully characterized in this cohort. [PMID:29848569](../papers/29848569.md)
