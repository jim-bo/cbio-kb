Multi-omics profiling of younger Asian breast cancers reveals distinctive molecular signatures

TL;DR

Kan et al. performed whole-exome and transcriptome profiling of 187 primary breast tumors from a Korean cohort (SMC) enriched in younger, pre-menopausal patients (88.2% pre-menopausal; n=125 ≤40 years) and systematically compared them with the predominantly Caucasian, post-menopausal TCGA BRCA cohort (n=1116). Compared with TCGA, SMC tumors had higher proportions of HER2+ and Luminal B subtypes, lower Luminal A proportion with reduced ESR1 expression, significantly higher prevalence of germline BRCA1/BRCA2 pathogenic mutations and somatic TP53/ERBB2 alterations, and a striking enrichment of the homologous-recombination-deficiency (HRD) mutational signature (S3) in TNBC. Virtual microdissection by NMF further showed that, after adjusting for age and subtype, Korean cohort status was independently associated with a higher tumor-infiltrating-leukocyte (TIL) factor and lower TGF-β signaling — suggesting younger Asian breast cancers harbor a more immune-active microenvironment than Western breast cancers (PMID:29713003).

Cohort & data

  • SMC (Korean) cohort: n=187 primary breast tumors (study brca_smc_2018); WES on 186 tumor/normal pairs; RNA-Seq on 168 tumors + 10 adjacent normals; 95% (179/187) treatment-naive; all tumors ≥60% tumor cells; 100% Asian. 88.2% pre-menopausal (n=165); 10.2% post-menopausal. Age groups: YBC (≤40, n=125) and IBC (>40, n=62) (PMID:29713003).
  • TCGA BRCA benchmark cohort: n=1116 subjects, primarily Caucasian (66.8% White, 14.2% Black, 5.1% Asian); 72.3% post-menopausal; mean age 58.3±13.2 yrs. WES (n=1001) and RNA-Seq matched to Omicsoft 2016 Q1 release. Age groups: YBC (≤40, n=181), IBC (40–60, n=562), OBC (>60, n=535) (dataset brca_tcga_pub2015; PMID:29713003).
  • Cancer types & histology: invasive breast carcinoma (BRCA), predominantly invasive ductal carcinoma (IDC, 92.0% in SMC vs. 74.4% in TCGA), with under-representation of invasive lobular carcinoma (ILC, 3.7% SMC vs. 17.3% TCGA, p=2.9e-08). Clinical subtype distribution: SMC ER+ 55.1%, ER+/HER2+ 14.4%, HER2+ 8%, TNBC (MBC) 19.8% (PMID:29713003).
  • Assays & reference: Agilent SureSelect XT Human All Exon V5 capture; Illumina HiSeq 2500 PE100 (whole-exome-seq); RSEM gene expression on hg19 (rna-seq); reference genome hg19. WES/RNA-Seq deposited at EGA EGAS00001002621 and GEO GSE113184 (PMID:29713003).
  • Analytics: Varscan 2.4.1 for somatic mutation calling, MutSigCV v1.2 for significantly mutated genes, deconstructSigs over the 30 COSMIC signatures (mutational-signatures), Genefu PAM50 + in-house naive molecular classifier (NMC) on ERBB2/ESR1/PGR for subtyping, iC10/IntClust for integrative clustering, NMF (brunet algorithm) for virtual microdissection of bulk-tumor expression, GSVA over MSigDB v5.1 (gsea) hallmark/C2/C5/C6 genesets for pathway scores, and Elastic Net + multiple regression for multivariate cohort-vs.-feature associations (PMID:29713003).

Key findings

  • Molecular subtype distribution shifts. Compared with TCGA, SMC had a lower proportion of ER+ tumors (53.5% vs. 75.4%, log-reg p=3.6e-75) and a higher proportion of HER2+ (28.3% vs. 4.3%, LR: p=7.5e-4). The Luminal A subtype was depleted in SMC (vs. TCGA) while Luminal B was enriched (39.2% vs. 33.2%, p=0.136); among Luminal cases, 58% were Luminal B in SMC vs. 43% in TCGA. SMC was also enriched for IntClust 5 (Fisher’s exact p=0.02), a HER2-amplified-intermediate-genomic-instability subtype (PMID:29713003).
  • Germline BRCA1/BRCA2 burden. 18.8% (35/186) of SMC patients carried germline pathogenic mutations in at least one of 13 BC predisposition genes; BRCA1 or BRCA2 germline pathogenic mutations were significantly enriched in SMC vs. TCGA (10.8% vs. 4.7%, LR: p=0.0027) and within SMC were enriched in YBC vs. IBC (13.7% vs. 4.8%, p=0.08) and vs. TCGA OBC (3.4%, p=6.85e-05). SMC patients with a positive family history of breast cancer clustered in YBC (24% vs. 8% IBC, FE p=0.02) (PMID:29713003).
  • Significantly mutated genes in SMC (MutSigCV, FDR<0.1). Six genes: TP53 (47.9%), PIK3CA (28.5%), GATA3 (12.4%), CBFB (2.7%), PTEN (3.2%), and CDH1 (2.2%). All six were previously called as significantly mutated by the TCGA BRCA study (PMID:29713003).
  • TP53 and ERBB2 are over-represented in SMC. TP53 somatic mutation prevalence: SMC 47.9% vs. TCGA 32.0% (LR p=5.0e-5; lobular-excluded 49.4% vs. 37.3%, p=0.003). TP53 mutation rates in SMC by subtype: TNBC 87.5%, HER2+ 75%, ER+/HER2+ 64.3%, ER+ 23.5%. ERBB2 somatic alteration prevalence: 20% (SMC) vs. 9.1% (TCGA) (PMID:29713003).
  • GATA3 vs. CDH1 cohort asymmetry. GATA3 mutations were more common in SMC (12.4%) than TCGA (9.1%); CDH1 mutations were more common in TCGA (11.5%/10.1%) than SMC (2.2%/2.4%) — consistent with the lower ILC fraction in SMC (PMID:29713003).
  • Pathway-level alterations. Cell-cycle, PI3K/AKT, and RTK signaling pathways were altered in 56.5%, 36.0%, and 28.0% of SMC vs. 46.7%, 42.7%, and 21.7% of TCGA; cell-cycle-checkpoint alteration enrichment in SMC was significant (LR p=0.019). Epigenetic regulators altered in ~10% (SMC) vs. ~15% (TCGA) (PMID:29713003).
  • Mutational signatures. 10 signatures were detected; APOBEC signatures S2/S13 were enriched in HER2+/ER+HER2+ and in ERBB2-amplified tumors in both cohorts. The HRD-related signature S3 was the dominant signature in TNBC and was significantly enriched in SMC TNBC vs. TCGA TNBC — 85% of SMC TNBC were HRD-positive (S3 score >0.2) vs. only 52% of TCGA TNBC (FE p=7e-4). S3 correlated negatively with patient age (Pearson r=-0.18, p=1.0e-9) (PMID:29713003).
  • Virtual microdissection (NMF). NMF on a 1,678-sample expression compendium (SMC + TCGA + CCLE + GTEx) yielded 14 factors mapped to tumor (F2/F4/F7/F13), stroma (F1), TIL (F9), normal (F3/F8/F14), and cohort-specific compartments. F9 (TIL) was strongly correlated with the CYT score (geometric mean of GZMA/PRF1; Spearman R=0.86, p=7.2e-54) and with CD8A expression (PMID:29713003).
  • TIL is elevated and TGF-β is suppressed in SMC. TIL factor F9 was significantly higher in SMC than TCGA (Student’s t p=1.55e-06); CD274 (PD-L1) and CD8A were among the F9-associated genes over-expressed in SMC. Conversely, the TGF-β hallmark signature and TGFB1 expression were higher in TCGA across multiple subtypes (PMID:29713003).
  • Multivariate independence of cohort effects. Elastic Net (500 bootstraps) and multiple regression after adjusting for age, tumor purity, molecular subtype, histology subtype, and menopausal status showed that TGF-β signature and TIL factor F9 had 100% variable usage and remained independently associated with SMC-vs.-TCGA cohort status. ERBB2 amplification, Luminal A/B status, and TP53 mutation were also independently associated with cohort. BRCA1/BRCA2 mutation and ESR1 expression instead tracked with patient age, not cohort (PMID:29713003).
  • HRD–TNBC interaction. When restricting to TNBC, the variable-usage frequency of S3 jumped from 19.8% (all subtypes) to 86.4%, with independent associations to cohort (MLR p=0.084) and age (MLR p=0.036) (PMID:29713003).

Genes & alterations

  • TP53 — most frequently mutated gene in SMC (47.9%, 89/186) and significantly more mutated than in TCGA (32.0%, LR p=5.0e-5). Subtype prevalence in SMC: TNBC 87.5%, HER2+ 75%, ER+/HER2+ 64.3%, ER+ 23.5%. Mutations are independently associated with SMC cohort status after subtype/lobular adjustment (MLR p=0.035) (PMID:29713003).
  • PIK3CA — second most frequently mutated (28.5%); rate not significantly different from TCGA (32.0%, p=0.81) (PMID:29713003).
  • GATA3 — mutated in 12.4% of SMC vs. 9.1% of TCGA; restricted to hormone-receptor-positive subtypes (PMID:29713003).
  • CBFB — significantly mutated (2.7%, q=0); previously reported in TCGA (PMID:29713003).
  • PTEN — significantly mutated (3.2%, q=0.015) (PMID:29713003).
  • CDH1 — significantly mutated in SMC (2.2%, q=0.076) but at far lower prevalence than TCGA (11.5%), reflecting the lower lobular fraction in SMC (p=2.46e-04) (PMID:29713003).
  • ERBB2 — somatic alteration (mostly amplification, CN ≥6) in 20% of SMC vs. 9.1% of TCGA; HER2 amplification drives APOBEC S2/S13 signature enrichment in HER2+ tumors and underlies the HER2+/Luminal-B enrichment in SMC (PMID:29713003).
  • BRCA1 / BRCA2 — germline pathogenic mutations affect 10.8% of SMC vs. 4.7% of TCGA (LR p=0.0027); strongly associated with the HRD mutational signature S3 (MLR p=2.0e-08) and with TNBC (MLR p=5.1e-39); associated with patient age, not cohort, in multivariate analysis (PMID:29713003).
  • ESR1 — expression significantly lower in SMC vs. TCGA within ER+ tumors (ST p=1.49e-04); positively correlated with patient age and menopausal status. ESR1 expression-based features (F7) remain associated with histology subtype (lobular) and older age, not with SMC cohort status (PMID:29713003).
  • PGR — used (with ERBB2 and ESR1) for naive molecular classifier (NMC) subtyping (PMID:29713003).
  • CD8A, CD274 (PD-L1) — over-expressed in SMC vs. TCGA, marking elevated cytotoxic-T-cell content and immune checkpoint expression in younger Asian breast cancers (PMID:29713003).
  • TGFB1 — expression and TGF-β hallmark signature significantly higher in TCGA than in SMC across multiple subtypes; independently associated with cohort status after adjusting for clinical covariates (PMID:29713003).
  • GZMA, PRF1 — used to define the CYT cytolytic score that correlates with the TIL NMF factor F9 (Spearman R=0.86) (PMID:29713003).
  • Pathway-level recurrence: RTK signaling, MAPK signaling, PI3K/AKT signaling, cell-cycle checkpoints, and epigenetic regulators were the five oncogenic pathway groupings examined; cell-cycle alterations were significantly enriched in SMC (LR p=0.019) (PMID:29713003).

Clinical implications

  • PARP-inhibitor opportunity in younger Asian TNBC. The combination of (i) significantly higher BRCA1/BRCA2 germline mutation prevalence in younger SMC patients and (ii) HRD signature S3 enrichment in 85% of SMC TNBC (vs. 52% of TCGA TNBC) is explicitly highlighted by the authors as a rationale for evaluating PARP inhibitors (e.g., olaparib) in younger Asian TNBC patients (PMID:29713003).
  • Endocrine-therapy resistance hypothesis. Lower ESR1 expression and weaker ER signaling in younger SMC ER+ tumors, combined with co-occurring TP53/HER2 alterations, are offered as a partial molecular explanation for the worse outcomes of younger ER+ patients on tamoxifen reported in prior Korean Breast Cancer Society cohorts (PMID:29713003).
  • Immuno-oncology rationale in Asian breast cancer. Independent enrichment of TIL factor F9, CD8A, and PD-L1 (CD274) expression — together with lower TGF-β signaling — in SMC suggests that immune-checkpoint blockade may be more relevant in younger Asian breast cancers than the predominantly post-menopausal Caucasian TCGA cohort would suggest. The authors explicitly call for IO trials, single-agent and combination, in Asian BC patients (PMID:29713003).

Limitations & open questions

  • Single-institution Korean cohort. All SMC patients were diagnosed and treated at Samsung Medical Center; broader Asian generalization requires multi-institution / multi-country cohorts (PMID:29713003).
  • Age vs. ethnicity entanglement. The SMC cohort is enriched for younger patients (mean 39.3±8.5 yrs) while TCGA skews older (58.3±13.2 yrs). Despite multivariate adjustment, the authors acknowledge that some “cohort” effects may reflect age or environmental exposures rather than genetic ancestry per se (PMID:29713003).
  • TIL difference not biologically dissected. Although TIL factor F9 is independently associated with SMC cohort status, the upstream cause (hereditary, environmental, viral, microbial) remains unidentified (PMID:29713003).
  • Bulk-tumor NMF assumptions. Virtual microdissection assumes additive linear mixing of compartments and may underestimate spatially heterogeneous immune infiltrates; cohort-specific NMF factors (F5, F6, F11, F12) were excluded but the authors note they may absorb real biology (PMID:29713003).
  • No survival endpoint analysis. The paper reports no overall-survival or disease-free-survival comparisons stratified by the molecular features it identifies; clinical translation of the PARP/IO hypotheses is left to future studies (PMID:29713003).
  • Treatment-naive only. 95% of SMC tumors were treatment-naive; resistance mechanisms cannot be studied from this cohort (PMID:29713003).
