# SCD

## Overview

SCD (stearoyl-CoA desaturase) converts saturated fatty acids (SFAs) to monounsaturated fatty acids (MUFAs), contributing to cell membrane fluidity and ferroptosis resistance. In [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md), SCD upregulation desaturates SFA to MUFA, enabling ferroptosis evasion.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- SCD upregulated in HCC; desaturates SFA to MUFA to evade ferroptosis; part of the DNL effector axis with [FASN](../genes/FASN.md) and [ACACA](../genes/ACACA.md) [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) — SCD upregulation is a ferroptosis-evasion mechanism and DNL effector [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Part of the FASN/SCD/ACACA DNL axis in HCC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- SCD inhibition is proposed as a strategy to restore ferroptosis sensitivity and resensitize HCC to sorafenib/regorafenib [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Open questions

- SCD inhibitors' selectivity and toxicity profiles in cirrhotic livers are not established.
