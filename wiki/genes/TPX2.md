# TPX2

## Overview

TPX2 (Targeting Protein for Xklp2) is a microtubule-associated protein essential for spindle pole formation and Aurora A kinase activation during mitosis. It is a downstream effector in pathways regulated by [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations and is frequently upregulated in chromosomally unstable tumors. In endometrial carcinoma proteomics, TPX2 is identified among proteins dysregulated downstream of TP53, [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md), and [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) mutations in trans.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Identified as a downstream protein regulated in trans by TP53, CTNNB1, and ARID1A mutations in endometrial carcinoma proteomics; part of the PLK1/CDK1/CHEK1/XPO1 mitotic regulatory cluster [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Endometrial carcinoma: protein abundance regulated downstream of major driver mutations (TP53, CTNNB1, ARID1A) [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-regulated with [PLK1](../genes/PLK1.md), [CDK1](../genes/CDK1.md), [CHEK1](../genes/CHEK1.md), [XPO1](../genes/XPO1.md), and [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) downstream of TP53/CTNNB1/ARID1A mutations in endometrial carcinoma [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- As a mitotic regulator co-dysregulated with PLK1 in endometrial carcinoma, TPX2 may contribute to sensitivity to Aurora A or PLK1 inhibitors [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)

## Open questions

- Direct driver vs. passenger role of TPX2 dysregulation in endometrial carcinoma subtypes [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)
