TL;DR

Zhao et al. longitudinally profiled 66 adult GBM patients (Columbia n=46, Northwestern n=20) treated with PD-1 inhibitors (nivolumab or pembrolizumab) upon recurrence, including 17 long-term responders. Using whole-exome sequencing, RNA-seq, single-cell transcriptomics, multiplex immunofluorescence, and TCR/Ig repertoire profiling, they identified that PTEN mutations are enriched in non-responders and associated with an immunosuppressive tumor microenvironment, while MAPK pathway alterations (BRAF, PTPN11) are enriched in responders. Responsive tumors followed a branched evolutionary pattern with elimination of predicted neoepitopes, while non-responders followed linear evolution. The work supports a molecular, personalized approach for selecting GBM patients for anti-PD-1 therapy.

Cohort & data

  • 66 adult GBM patients treated with PD-1 inhibitors (nivolumab or pembrolizumab) upon recurrence, from two institutions: Columbia University (n=46) and Northwestern University (n=20). All received standard temozolomide + radiation prior to PD-1 inhibitor PMID:30742119.
  • 17 long-term responders classified as responsive if tissue showed only inflammatory response with few tumor cells (pseudo-progression) OR if MRI showed stable/shrinking disease over at least six months PMID:30742119.
  • Data modalities: 58 whole exomes (matched tumor/blood), 38 transcriptomes (longitudinal), cancer gene panel for 39 patients, single-cell RNA-seq from 9,000 cells across 3 GBMs, quantitative multiplex immunofluorescence on FFPE specimens from 17 patients with paired pre/post anti-PD-1 samples PMID:30742119.
  • Sequencing achieved ~100× tumor and ~60× normal exome coverage, reads mapped by BWA to hg19; somatic mutations called by SAVI2; copy-number by CNVkit; tumor purity by ABSOLUTE; HLA typed by POLYSOLVER; RNA aligned by STAR; pathway scoring by ssGSEA / GSEA; TCGA tumor purity from ESTIMATE PMID:30742119.
  • Cohort registered as cBioPortal study gbm_columbia_2019. TCGA IDH1-wild-type gbm_tcga (n=458 / n=503) used as background reference for mutation-frequency comparisons.
  • Tumor microenvironment profiled by multiplexed immunofluorescence (Opal/Vectra) staining for CD3, CD8, CD68, HLA-DR, PD-L1, and SOX2 PMID:30742119.

Key findings

  • Response correlates with improved post-treatment survival. Excluding patients with <6 months survival, responders had median post-treatment survival of 14.3 months vs 10.1 months for non-responders (p=0.0081, log-rank). Including the full cohort, the gap widened to 15.5 vs 5.7 months (p=2.2e-5, log-rank, n=42; 13 responders, 29 non-responders) PMID:30742119.
  • No association between mutation burden and response. Median 47 non-synonymous somatic mutations (range 14–83) across 33 sequenced tumors. Non-responders trended higher (median 40 nsSNV) than responders (median 26) — opposite to the expected direction (p=0.11, Wilcoxon). HLA-I neoepitope load also not associated (45 vs 35, p=0.41). HLA zygosity, aneuploidy (p=0.88), and tumor purity (p=0.19) likewise not predictive PMID:30742119.
  • PTEN mutations enriched in non-responders. Among 45 IDH1-wild-type tumors: 23/32 non-responders vs 3/13 responders had PTEN mutations (Fisher p=0.0063, OR=8.5, FDR-corrected p<0.05). Versus TCGA IDH1-wild-type GBM background of 33% (154/458), PTEN was still over-represented in non-responders (Fisher p=0.0018, OR=3.3) PMID:30742119.
  • MAPK pathway alterations enriched in responders. 4 mutations in MAPK components (BRAF and PTPN11) among 13 responders vs 1 in 32 non-responders. Versus TCGA background MAPK mutation rate of 7.8% (36/458), enriched in responders (Fisher p=0.018, OR=5.1). Within-cohort enrichment: p=0.019, OR=12.8 PMID:30742119.
  • Distinct evolutionary patterns. Of 5 patients with paired pre/post anti-PD-1 samples, the 2 non-responders (patients 20 and 53) followed a linear evolution pattern; the 3 responders (patients 55, 71, 101) showed branched evolution with elimination of clones harboring predicted-expressed neoepitopes (e.g., CYP27B1 G194E in patient 55; FNIP1 T409M in patient 71; TCF12 A605S in patient 101; RPKM>5 each) PMID:30742119.
  • Differential T- and B-cell clonal dynamics. Across 7 patients with pre/post RNA-seq, non-responders had a greater increase in T-cell clonal diversity (Shannon entropy, p=0.024, Mann-Whitney) and a similar effect in immunoglobulin/B-cell clonality (p=0.048, Mann-Whitney) — suggesting failure of selective lymphocyte recruitment in non-responders PMID:30742119.
  • Transcriptomic correlates. Of 9,292 gene sets from MSigDB tested by ssGSEA, regulatory T-cell (FOXP3) signatures were enriched in non-responders pre-treatment (p=0.037). After treatment, immunosuppressive signatures including FOXP3, STAT3, and a renal-cell-carcinoma immune-evasion signature were more active in responders — consistent with acquired resistance. Proneural/mesenchymal/classical expression subtype was not associated with response PMID:30742119.
  • Source of the FOXP3 signature is tumor cells, not Tregs. IHC of 5 post-treatment tumors did not identify CD4+FOXP3+ Tregs. Single-cell RNA-seq of 9,000 cells from 3 GBMs (including one PTEN-mutated) localized the immunosuppressive signature to a CD44+ tumor subpopulation in the PTEN-mutated case (p<1e-16, t-test) PMID:30742119.
  • PTEN mutations shape tumor microenvironment in TCGA. In 172 TCGA samples, PTEN mutations correlated with FOXP3-related transcriptional signature, lower tumor purity (p=0.028), and higher macrophage, microglial, and neutrophil infiltration (FDR-corrected p<0.1) PMID:30742119.
  • Spatial microenvironment in qmIF (17 patients). PTEN-mutated tumors had significantly more CD68+HLA-DR- macrophages (p=0.011) — a subset linked to poor melanoma survival. After immunotherapy, CD3+ T-cell density increased only in PTEN-wild-type cases (p=0.0095), not in PTEN-mutants. Tumor cells clustered more tightly in PTEN-mutants pre-treatment (pair correlation, p=2.4e-4) PMID:30742119.

Genes & alterations

  • PTEN — mutated in 23/32 IDH1-wild-type non-responders vs 3/13 responders (OR=8.5, Fisher p=0.0063). Mechanistically tied to PI3K-AKT pathway activation (ssGSEA, t-test p=0.049 in non-responders), an immunosuppressive CD44+ tumor cell signature (single-cell), lower tumor purity, and higher infiltration by macrophages/microglia/neutrophils. PTEN loss reduces T-cell infiltration and increases immunoresistance, consistent with reports in melanoma and uterine leiomyosarcoma PMID:30742119.
  • BRAF — part of MAPK-pathway enrichment in responders (combined with PTPN11; 4/13 responders vs 1/32 non-responders) PMID:30742119.
  • PTPN11 — MAPK pathway alterations in responders; authors highlight a previously proposed PTEN/PTPN11 axis governing immunosuppression in prostate cancer PMID:30742119.
  • IDH1 — R132G/H mutations in 11/56 cohort tumors (4 responders, 7 non-responders); IDH1-mutant cases excluded from primary mutation-enrichment analysis PMID:30742119.
  • CD274 (PD-L1) — RNA expression not different between responders and non-responders (t-test p=0.374) PMID:30742119.
  • FOXP3 — pre-treatment regulatory-T-cell signature enriched in non-responders (p=0.037); post-treatment FOXP3 signature active in responders. IHC ruled out CD4+FOXP3+ Tregs as the source — signature traced to CD44+ tumor cells PMID:30742119.
  • STAT3 — STAT3-related immunosuppressive signature elevated in post-treatment responder tumors, interpreted as acquired-resistance signal PMID:30742119.
  • CD44 — marks the tumor subpopulation harboring the FOXP3-like immunosuppressive signature in a PTEN-mutant GBM (single-cell topological analysis) PMID:30742119.
  • CD68 — macrophage/microglia marker used in qmIF; CD68+HLA-DR- subpopulation enriched in PTEN-mutant tumors (p=0.011) PMID:30742119.
  • SOX2 — tumor marker used in the Opal multiplex IF panel (with DAPI, CD3, CD8, CD68, HLA-DR, PD-L1) PMID:30742119.
  • Patient-specific eliminated neoantigen-generating mutations from responders’ post-treatment tumors: CYP27B1 G194E and MYPN R409H, UBQLN3 R159W (patient 55); FNIP1 T409M (patient 71); TCF12 A605S (patient 101) — predicted immunogenic and highly expressed (RPKM>5) PMID:30742119.

Clinical implications

  • Biomarker selection. PTEN status (and possibly MAPK-pathway status) may help stratify recurrent GBM patients for anti-PD-1 therapy. The authors propose this as the basis for a “molecular, personalized approach for refining patient selection for immunotherapy” — though they explicitly note further validation is required PMID:30742119.
  • Combination therapy rationale. Enrichment of BRAF/PTPN11 MAPK alterations in responders, together with preclinical evidence that MAPK inhibition potentiates checkpoint blockade, supports combining MAPK-targeted therapy with checkpoint inhibitors PMID:30742119.
  • TMB does not predict response in GBM. Unlike melanoma or NSCLC, mutation/neoepitope burden is not predictive in this cohort — implying that tumor-intrinsic microenvironmental signals (e.g., PTEN-driven immunosuppression) dominate over neoantigen load in GBM PMID:30742119.
  • PD-L1 (CD274) RNA is not predictive in this GBM cohort, in contrast to other indications PMID:30742119.

Limitations & open questions

  • Retrospective, two-institution cohort (n=66) with heterogeneous data modalities (only 17 of 66 patients had both pre- and post-anti-PD-1 tumor tissue and matched WES; only 7 had paired pre/post RNA-seq) PMID:30742119.
  • Evolutionary inference rests on just 5 patients (2 non-responders, 3 responders); branched vs linear assignment was based on shared/exclusive mutation fractions, not full clonal deconvolution PMID:30742119.
  • The authors excluded 3 intragenic exon deletions in PTEN from the TCGA comparison “for the sake of egalitarianism” because TCGA does not report them — leaving open whether the true PTEN-alteration enrichment is even larger PMID:30742119.
  • Response was defined by a composite of pathology (predominantly inflammatory tissue) and radiology (≥6 months stable/shrinking on MRI); the temporal component required excluding short-survival patients in the headline univariate survival analysis, which the authors flag as a confounding consideration PMID:30742119.
  • Single-cell RNA analysis of the CD44+ immunosuppressive signature is drawn from one PTEN-mutant tumor; broader sampling needed PMID:30742119.
  • HLA zygosity was not associated with response or survival here, but the sample (n=17 patients with HLA typing) is small relative to the prior studies in melanoma/NSCLC that reported the effect PMID:30742119.
  • Validation in a prospective trial and mechanistic dissection of how PTEN loss specifically remodels the GBM tumor microenvironment (vs the well-described melanoma mechanism) remain open PMID:30742119.
