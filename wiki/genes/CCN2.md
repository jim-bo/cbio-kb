# CCN2

## Overview

CCN2 (cellular communication network factor 2, formerly CTGF) is a matricellular protein and marker of activated fibroblasts/CAFs. It is induced by TGF-β and mechanical stiffness and promotes desmoplastic stroma formation in multiple cancers.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Upregulated as a CAF activation marker (CTGF/CCN2) under high-stiffness matrix in GBC-associated gallbladder fibroblasts; part of the stiffness-induced fibroblast activation signature driven by YAP1/WWTR1/TEAD1 [PMID:24997986](../papers/24997986.md)
- Canonical YAP/TAZ target (also known as CTGF); downregulated upon YAP1/WWTR1 double knockdown in CRPC-SCL cell lines, supporting YAP/TAZ-driven transcriptional programs in lineage-plastic prostate cancer ([prad_organoids_msk_2022](../datasets/prad_organoids_msk_2022.md)) [PMID:35617398](../papers/35617398.md)
