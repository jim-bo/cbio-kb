# PLOD2

## Overview

PLOD2 (procollagen-lysine, 2-oxoglutarate 5-dioxygenase 2) is a collagen-modifying enzyme that catalyzes hydroxylation of lysine residues in collagen, facilitating cross-linking and stabilization of the extracellular matrix. PLOD2 is implicated in tumor invasion, metastasis, and stromal remodeling. It has been identified as a hypoxia-inducible gene and is associated with poor prognosis in multiple cancers.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- PLOD2 protein is elevated >2-fold in 96% of tumor-NAT (normal adjacent tissue) pairs in a 106-tumor colon cancer proteogenomic cohort, placing it among the most uniformly upregulated proteins across the cohort. Only 7 of the 31 cancer-associated proteins identified (>2-fold in tumors) are in the Cancer Gene Census; PLOD2 is detectable in plasma, making it a candidate blood biomarker. [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

It is among eight proteins elevated in >95% of tumor-NAT pairs (alongside [DDX21](../genes/DDX21.md), [S100A11](../genes/S100A11.md), RSL1D1, [S100P](../genes/S100P.md), RPL36A, [SERPINH1](../genes/SERPINH1.md), [GPRC5A](../genes/GPRC5A.md)). ## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-elevated with DDX21, S100A11, RSL1D1, S100P, RPL36A, SERPINH1, and GPRC5A in >95% of colon tumor-NAT pairs — a cluster of highly penetrant cancer-associated proteins largely invisible to mutation-based genomic discovery. [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- PLOD2 was flagged as a candidate for biomarker or therapeutic follow-up given its near-universal elevation in colon cancer tumors and plasma detectability. Its extracellular matrix remodeling function may offer accessibility as a therapeutic target. [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Open questions

- PLOD2 is not in the Cancer Gene Census; its driver vs passenger status in colon cancer is unclear. Prospective validation as a plasma diagnostic biomarker is needed. [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)
