# ERCC5

## Overview

ERCC5 (excision repair cross-complementation group 5), also known as XPG, encodes a structure-specific DNA endonuclease involved in nucleotide excision repair (NER). Germline variants cause xeroderma pigmentosum; somatic alterations have been catalogued across multiple cancer types including prostate cancer. ERCC5 is classified as a DNA-repair gene and is monitored on targeted sequencing panels as a marker of repair deficiency.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- ERCC5 (DNA-repair gene) showed no significant alteration-prevalence difference by self-reported race (Black, White, or Asian) in an MSK-IMPACT prostate cancer cohort of 2,069 tumors, contrasting with a prior Project GENIE-based analysis by Mahal et al. (NEJM 2020); MSI prevalence and TMB also did not differ by race. [PMID:34667026](../papers/34667026.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting of ERCC5 reported in the citing paper. Absence of race-associated differences suggests that ERCC5-targeting strategies (e.g., PARP inhibitor or platinum eligibility) would not be expected to differ by race in this cohort. [PMID:34667026](../papers/34667026.md)

## Open questions

- The null finding (no racial difference) contradicts a prior Project GENIE-based report; methodological differences (single-institution MSK-IMPACT vs multi-institutional GENIE panel) may account for the discrepancy. [PMID:34667026](../papers/34667026.md)
