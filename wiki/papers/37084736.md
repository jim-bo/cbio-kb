# Genomic Mapping of Metastatic Organotropism in Lung Adenocarcinoma

**PMID:** 37084736 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.ccell.2023.03.018 · **Journal:** Cancer Cell (2023)

## TL;DR

Lengel et al. analyzed 2,532 lung adenocarcinoma ([LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md)) specimens from 2,309 MSK patients with matched clinicopathologic and targeted-panel genomic data to identify features associated with metastasis, metastatic burden, organotropism, and metastasis-free survival. Patients developing metastasis were younger and more often male, with primaries enriched for micropapillary/solid histology, higher TMB, chromosomal instability, and fraction of genome doubled. Inactivation of [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md), and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) correlated with shorter site-specific time to metastasis; APOBEC signatures were enriched in metastases, especially liver lesions; and only ~4% of metastases harbored actionable alterations not already present in the matched primary [PMID:37084736](../papers/37084736.md).

## Cohort & data

- 2,532 primary and metastatic [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) specimens from 2,309 patients profiled at Memorial Sloan Kettering [PMID:37084736](../papers/37084736.md).
- Sites profiled include adrenal gland, bone, CNS, liver/biliary, lung, lymph node, and pleura [PMID:37084736](../papers/37084736.md).
- Targeted sequencing via the [MSK-IMPACT panel](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) ([luad_mskcc_2023_met_organotropism](../datasets/luad_mskcc_2023_met_organotropism.md)), with an additional [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) subcohort for mutational signature analysis [PMID:37084736](../papers/37084736.md).

## Key findings

- Ever-metastatic primaries had higher TMB, chromosomal instability, and fraction of genome doubled than never-metastatic primaries [PMID:37084736](../papers/37084736.md).
- APOBEC signatures SBS2/SBS13 were more prevalent in metastases than primaries (18% vs 8%, p=0.012), and were particularly enriched in liver metastases in the WES cohort [PMID:37084736](../papers/37084736.md).
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) were among ten genes differentially altered between ever- and never-metastatic primary tumors [PMID:37084736](../papers/37084736.md).
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)/[EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) co-alteration with [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md) (q<0.001) and [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) (q<0.001) was significant only in ever-metastatic primaries [PMID:37084736](../papers/37084736.md).
- [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md), [STK11](../genes/STK11.md), [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md), and [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) alterations were mutually exclusive with [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) alterations [PMID:37084736](../papers/37084736.md).
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) (p=0.002) and [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) (p<0.001) alterations were associated with worse metastasis-free survival [PMID:37084736](../papers/37084736.md).
- [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) alterations were strongly enriched in patients with bone metastasis (OR=6.47) and shortened time to bone metastasis (OR=2.79, 95% CI 1.71–4.56, p<0.001) [PMID:37084736](../papers/37084736.md).
- Adrenal gland metastases had the highest proportion of [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) activating mutations (133/202, 66%) [PMID:37084736](../papers/37084736.md).
- In primary tumors that metastasized to liver, [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) G12C was elevated (21%) vs the matched liver metastases (6%, p<0.001) [PMID:37084736](../papers/37084736.md).
- Only ~4% of metastases carried therapeutically actionable alterations undetected in their matched primaries [PMID:37084736](../papers/37084736.md).

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — inactivation correlated with site-specific shorter time to metastasis; co-altered with [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) in ever-metastatic context [PMID:37084736](../papers/37084736.md).
- [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) — strong enrichment in bone metastases; shortened time to bone metastasis [PMID:37084736](../papers/37084736.md).
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — inactivation more frequent in CNS and liver metastases than in matched primaries [PMID:37084736](../papers/37084736.md).
- [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md), [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) — co-alteration with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)/[EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) defines higher-risk metastatic context [PMID:37084736](../papers/37084736.md).
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) — activating mutations enriched at adrenal gland metastases; associated with worse MFS [PMID:37084736](../papers/37084736.md).
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — G12C preferentially seen in primaries that metastasized to liver vs the liver lesions themselves [PMID:37084736](../papers/37084736.md).
- [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) — alterations associated with worse MFS [PMID:37084736](../papers/37084736.md).
- Additional genes altered more often in metastases: [NKX2-1](../genes/NKX2-1.md), [FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md), [MET](../genes/MET.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [MGA](../genes/MGA.md); altered less in metastases: [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) [PMID:37084736](../papers/37084736.md).

## Clinical implications

- Because only ~4% of metastases carry new actionable alterations not present in the primary, routine re-biopsy of metastases for new targetable drivers yields limited incremental benefit in [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) [PMID:37084736](../papers/37084736.md).
- Co-alteration patterns (e.g., [TP53](../genes/TP53.md)+[EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md)+[KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md)/[STK11](../genes/STK11.md)) and [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) status may inform site-specific metastatic risk, notably for bone [PMID:37084736](../papers/37084736.md).

## Limitations & open questions

- Targeted panel ([MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md)) limits mutational signature resolution; authors partially address this with a WES subcohort [PMID:37084736](../papers/37084736.md).
- Single-institution (MSK) cohort; external validation was performed but organotropism findings remain cancer-type-specific and may not generalize beyond [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) [PMID:37084736](../papers/37084736.md).
- Some site-specific analyses (e.g., liver for [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md)) were underpowered [PMID:37084736](../papers/37084736.md).
