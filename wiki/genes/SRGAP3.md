# SRGAP3

## Overview

SRGAP3 (Slit-Robo Rho GTPase-Activating Protein 3) is a Rho GAP that couples SLIT-ROBO receptor signaling to actin cytoskeletal reorganization, influencing cell polarity and migration. Like [SRGAP2](../genes/SRGAP2.md), it acts in the SLIT-ROBO-SRGAP pathway controlling neural and epithelial cell movement. In adenoid cystic carcinoma (ACC), SRGAP3 mutation is part of a recurrently disrupted axon-guidance pathway hypothesized to facilitate perineural invasion.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- SRGAP3 single-tumor somatic mutation detected in adenoid cystic carcinoma (ACC) of the salivary gland as part of a recurrently disrupted axon-guidance pathway (56% of tumors in the cohort) [PMID:26862087](../papers/26862087.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Adenoid cystic carcinoma (ACC): SRGAP3 mutation contributes to axon-guidance pathway disruption present in 56% of tumors [PMID:26862087](../papers/26862087.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- SRGAP3 mutation co-occurs with other axon-guidance pathway alterations ([SLIT1](../genes/SLIT1.md), SRGAP2, [SEMA3G](../genes/SEMA3G.md), [SEMA5A](../genes/SEMA5A.md), [PLXNA1](../genes/PLXNA1.md), [PLXNB1](../genes/PLXNB1.md), [PRKD1](../genes/PRKD1.md)) in ACC [PMID:26862087](../papers/26862087.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Rho-pathway and axon-guidance pathway inhibition flagged as candidate therapeutic strategies for ACC; no functional or clinical data specific to SRGAP3 [PMID:26862087](../papers/26862087.md)

## Open questions

- Functional consequence of SRGAP3 mutation in ACC is uncharacterized [PMID:26862087](../papers/26862087.md)
