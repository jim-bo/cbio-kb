# RACK1

## Overview

RACK1 (also known as GNB2L1) encodes the Receptor for Activated C-Kinase 1, a scaffolding protein that anchors activated protein kinase C to cellular membranes and participates in PI3K/AKT signaling. In clear cell renal cell carcinoma (ccRCC), RACK1 resides in the 5q35.3 amplicon, and its copy number gain is correlated with elevated mRNA expression and PI3K pathway activation.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Copy number gain at the 5q35.3 amplicon; copy number correlated with mRNA expression and PI3K pathway alteration status in ccRCC. [PMID:23792563](../papers/23792563.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[CCRCC](../cancer_types/CCRCC.md)**: RACK1 is located within the recurrently amplified 5q35.3 region; co-amplification with [SQSTM1](../genes/SQSTM1.md) links both genes to PI3K activation in this tumor type. [PMID:23792563](../papers/23792563.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-amplified with [SQSTM1](../genes/SQSTM1.md) in the 5q35.3 amplicon; both genes are linked to PI3K pathway activation in ccRCC. [PMID:23792563](../papers/23792563.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway is nominated as a therapeutic target in ccRCC; RACK1 amplification contributes to pathway activation, supporting use of [mTOR](../genes/MTOR.md) inhibitors such as [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md) and [temsirolimus](../drugs/temsirolimus.md). [PMID:23792563](../papers/23792563.md)

## Open questions

- Whether RACK1 copy number gain independently drives PI3K pathway activity or is a passenger co-amplification with other 5q35.3 genes remains to be determined. [PMID:23792563](../papers/23792563.md)
