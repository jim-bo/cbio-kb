# midostaurin

## Overview

Midostaurin is an oral multi-kinase inhibitor with activity against [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md) (including ITD and TKD mutations), [KIT](../genes/KIT.md), VEGFR, PDGFR, and protein kinase C. It was the first FDA-approved targeted therapy for FLT3-mutant [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md) (in combination with standard induction chemotherapy, based on the RATIFY trial). It is also approved for advanced systemic mastocytosis.

## Evidence in the corpus

- In the Beat [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md) program, [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md)-ITD was one of the key driver mutations examined for ex vivo drug-sensitivity associations across a 122-compound panel including FLT3-pathway inhibitors; midostaurin was part of the AML targeted-therapy landscape characterised in the study [PMID:30333627](../papers/30333627.md).

## Resistance mechanisms

- Secondary [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md) kinase-domain mutations can reduce midostaurin binding. [DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md) mutation alone (without FLT3-ITD or [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md)) was insufficient to confer sensitivity to BTK/SYK-pathway inhibitors in ex vivo Beat AML profiling, illustrating the co-mutation context-dependence of targeted therapy response [PMID:30333627](../papers/30333627.md).
