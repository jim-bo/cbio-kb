# LDHB

## Overview

LDHB (Lactate Dehydrogenase B) encodes the B subunit of lactate dehydrogenase, which forms tetramers with [LDHA](../genes/LDHA.md) subunits to catalyze pyruvate/lactate interconversion. In cancer genomics, LDHB is notable for its paralog relationship with LDHA: promoter hypermethylation of one paralog creates a dependency on the other, exemplifying methylation-gated synthetic lethality. LDHB expression is frequently suppressed in some tumor types while upregulated in others, and its essentiality is context-dependent based on the epigenetic state of LDHA.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- LDHB and LDHA exhibit reciprocal paralog synthetic lethality gated by promoter methylation in cancer cell lines; when LDHB promoter is hypermethylated, LDHA becomes essential, and vice versa; this relationship was replicated in TCGA primary tumors. [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Synthetic lethality between LDHB and LDHA was observed across multiple cancer cell line lineages in the Cancer Cell Line Encyclopedia (CCLE), with validation in TCGA primary tumor methylation data. [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- LDHB and LDHA show reciprocal promoter methylation patterns — hypermethylation of one paralogue predicts dependence on the other; identified in the next-generation CCLE characterization (1,403 cell lines) as a canonical paralog synthetic lethality. [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Tumors with LDHB promoter hypermethylation may be selectively vulnerable to LDHA inhibition; conversely, LDHB inhibition is a candidate therapeutic strategy in LDHA-methylated tumors. Methylation state is the proposed patient-selection biomarker. [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)

## Open questions

- Whether LDHB/LDHA methylation status can serve as a reliable clinical biomarker for LDH-inhibitor sensitivity in vivo has not been prospectively tested.
