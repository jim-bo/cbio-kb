# POP7

## Overview

POP7 (Ribonuclease P/MRP Subunit POP7) encodes a subunit of the RNase P/MRP RNA processing complex. In the context of cancer cell line dependencies, POP7 exhibits paralogue synthetic lethality with [RPP25](../genes/RPP25.md): when RPP25 is silenced by promoter methylation, cells become dependent on POP7. This relationship was identified in the Cancer Cell Line Encyclopedia (CCLE) multi-omic profiling integrated with Project Achilles and DRIVE dependency screens.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- POP7 shows paralogue synthetic lethality with RPP25; methylation/silencing of RPP25 drives POP7 dependence in cancer cell lines; this relationship was discovered via integration of CCLE methylation data with CRISPR/shRNA dependency screens across 1,072 lines [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Pan-cancer (cancer cell lines): POP7 dependence is gated by RPP25 promoter methylation status, identified across multiple lineages in the CCLE resource [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Synthetic lethal pair with RPP25 (paralogue); also linked to [RPP25L](../genes/RPP25L.md) in the same RNase P/MRP complex dependency network [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- POP7 may represent a synthetic lethal therapeutic target in tumors where RPP25 is silenced by promoter methylation [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)

## Open questions

- The frequency of RPP25 methylation in primary tumor types (vs. cell lines) that would make POP7 a clinically relevant target has not been systematically established.
