# FLNA

## Overview

FLNA (Filamin A) encodes a large actin-binding scaffold protein that crosslinks actin filaments and anchors membrane proteins to the actin cytoskeleton. In cancer signaling, FLNA is a downstream phospho-substrate of [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md), and its phosphorylation status has been identified as a kinase-substrate axis in pancreatic adenocarcinoma (PDAC) with therapeutic implications.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Identified as a phospho-substrate of AKT1 (FLNA site) in a kinase-substrate signaling axis detected across PDAC tumors vs NAT pairs; one of five phospho-substrates with FDA-approved or investigational inhibitors [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md)**: FLNA phosphorylation by AKT1 is part of the kinase-substrate network elevated in pancreatic tumors relative to normal adjacent tissue [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- The AKT1-FLNA phospho-substrate axis co-occurs with upregulated CDK7-MCM2, PAK1-BAD, PAK2-MAPK6, and SRC-STAT3 axes in the PDAC kinase-substrate signaling map [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- AKT inhibitors (FDA-approved or investigational) proposed to target the AKT1-FLNA phospho-axis in PDAC [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Open questions

- Functional consequences of FLNA phosphorylation by AKT1 in PDAC invasion and metastasis require experimental validation.
