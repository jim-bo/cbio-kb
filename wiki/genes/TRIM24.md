# TRIM24

## Overview

TRIM24 (TIF1A) is a transcriptional co-regulator with bromodomain and PHD finger domains. In the corpus, TRIM24 is identified as a 5' fusion partner for [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) in the tumor-agnostic BRAF fusion landscape.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- TRIM24 was identified as a 5' fusion partner for BRAF among 82 unique partners across 52 histologies in a tumor-agnostic BRAF fusion cohort (MSK-IMPACT/MSK-Fusion) [PMID:38922339](../papers/38922339.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- Not specifically linked to a single cancer type in the corpus; BRAF fusions involving TRIM24 were observed across diverse histologies [PMID:38922339](../papers/38922339.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- TRIM24-BRAF fusions co-occur with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations (22% across all histologies) and other frequent co-alterations in BRAF fusion-positive tumors [PMID:38922339](../papers/38922339.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- BRAF fusions are emerging actionable targets; MEK inhibitors ([trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md), [selumetinib](../drugs/selumetinib.md)) and BRAF dimer blockers are under investigation [PMID:38922339](../papers/38922339.md).

## Open questions

- Clinical characteristics and response rates specific to TRIM24-BRAF fusions are not separately reported in the corpus [PMID:38922339](../papers/38922339.md).
