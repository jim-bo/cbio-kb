# PCDH18

## Overview

PCDH18 (Protocadherin 18) encodes a member of the protocadherin subfamily of the cadherin superfamily of cell-adhesion molecules. It plays roles in cell-adhesion, migration, and signalling pathways including WNT signalling. In cancer genomics, PCDH18 has been identified as over-expressed in specific molecular subtypes of malignant rhabdoid tumours ([MRT](../cancer_types/MRT.md)).

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- PCDH18 among the most over-expressed genes in mRNA sub-group 2 (cell adhesion/migration, WNT, differentiation) in extra-cranial malignant rhabdoid tumors (MRT). [PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **MRT (Malignant Rhabdoid Tumor):** Over-expressed in mRNA sub-group 2 of extra-cranial MRT, a subgroup characterised by cell adhesion/migration and WNT-pathway activity. [PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-expressed with [WNT5A](../genes/WNT5A.md), [TCF21](../genes/TCF21.md), and [MEIS1](../genes/MEIS1.md) in mRNA sub-group 2 of extra-cranial MRT, defining a cell-adhesion/migration/WNT/differentiation expression signature. [PMID:26977886](../papers/26977886.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting reported in the corpus.

## Open questions

- Whether PCDH18 over-expression is a driver or passenger event in MRT mRNA sub-group 2 is not established. The sub-group association is derived from an unsupervised clustering analysis and mechanistic causality has not been demonstrated.
