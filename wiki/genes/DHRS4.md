# DHRS4

## Overview

DHRS4 (Dehydrogenase/Reductase 4) encodes a member of the short-chain dehydrogenase/reductase (SDR) superfamily involved in carbonyl metabolism and retinol oxidation. It has been identified as a candidate coding driver in metastatic cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma ([CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md)) via multi-tool consensus driver analysis in a whole-genome sequencing study, though functional validation is lacking.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Candidate coding driver in metastatic CSCC: called by ≥2 of three driver-detection tools (OncodriveFML, MutSigCV, dNdScv) in a WGS study of 25 lymph node metastases; listed among 12-gene consensus driver set alongside [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md): Multi-tool candidate driver in 25-sample metastatic CSCC WGS cohort; altered in [KHDRBS2](../genes/KHDRBS2.md), [COLEC12](../genes/COLEC12.md), and other co-nominated candidate drivers [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-nominated with KHDRBS2, COLEC12, [SLC22A6](../genes/SLC22A6.md), [LINGO2](../genes/LINGO2.md), [CDHR5](../genes/CDHR5.md), [ZNF442](../genes/ZNF442.md), and [PRLR](../genes/PRLR.md) in the 12-gene consensus driver set from metastatic CSCC [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic relevance established; DHRS4's role in retinol metabolism could in principle intersect with retinoic acid signaling but no clinical evidence in CSCC [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Open questions

- Functional validation of DHRS4 as a bona fide CSCC driver is outstanding; biological mechanism by which DHRS4 mutation drives UV-associated skin cancer is unknown [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)
