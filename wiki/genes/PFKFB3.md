# PFKFB3

## Overview

PFKFB3 (6-phosphofructo-2-kinase/fructose-2,6-bisphosphatase 3) is a bifunctional enzyme that controls the cellular concentration of fructose-2,6-bisphosphate, a potent allosteric activator of glycolysis. Its kinase activity promotes glycolytic flux by generating fructose-2,6-bisphosphate, which stimulates phosphofructokinase-1. PFKFB3 is frequently overexpressed in cancers and has emerged as a druggable therapeutic target, particularly in settings where aerobic glycolysis (Warburg effect) drives tumor growth and immune evasion.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Elevated kinase activity detected by phosphoproteomics in colon cancer tumors versus matched normal adjacent tissue; activity inference based on activating-site phosphorylation enrichment and target-site patterns. PFKFB3 was among seven kinases flagged as therapeutic candidates elevated in colon tumors, with inhibitors currently in clinical development. [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md) (colon adenocarcinoma):** PFKFB3 kinase activity is elevated in colon tumors. The glycolytic phenotype in MSI-H tumors — where glycolytic enzymes are universally upregulated at the protein level and anti-correlate with CD8 infiltration — further supports PFKFB3 as a target in MSI-H colorectal cancer. Combining PFKFB3 inhibitors with immune checkpoint blockade is proposed as a strategy for MSI-H tumors failing checkpoint monotherapy. [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- PFKFB3 elevated kinase activity co-occurs with elevated [CDK1](../genes/CDK1.md), [CDK2](../genes/CDK2.md), [CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md), [CDK7](../genes/CDK7.md), [MELK](../genes/MELK.md), and [PI4KB](../genes/PI4KB.md) activity in the same colon tumor cohort — all representing potential co-targeting opportunities. [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- PFKFB3 inhibitors are in clinical development. The proteogenomic analysis of colon cancer specifically proposes combining PFKFB3 inhibition with immune checkpoint blockade in MSI-H colorectal tumors, given the negative correlation between glycolytic activity and CD8 T-cell infiltration in MSI tumors (Spearman rho = -0.61, p = 0.02). [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)

## Open questions

- Therapeutic validation is computational only; no in vivo or clinical evidence for PFKFB3 inhibition in colon cancer has been reported from this dataset. [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)
