**PMID:** 31792460 · **DOI:** 10.1038/s41591-019-0654-5 · **Journal:** Nature Medicine (2019)

## TL;DR

Liu et al. assembled a clinically annotated cohort of 144 patients with advanced melanoma treated with anti-PD1 immune-checkpoint blockade (ICB) and performed whole-exome and bulk transcriptome sequencing on pre-treatment tumors ([mel_dfci_2019](../datasets/mel_dfci_2019.md)). They show that tumor mutational burden (TMB) as a response predictor is confounded by melanoma subtype, identify [MHC-II](../genes/HLA-A.md)-related antigen-presentation features (transcriptomic MHC-II HLA expression, [TAP2](../genes/TAP2.md) and MHC-I HLA locus amplifications) as novel predictors of response, and demonstrate that prior anti-CTLA4 ([ipilimumab](../drugs/ipilimumab.md)) exposure changes which features predict response. They then build two parsimonious logistic-regression models of intrinsic resistance — one for ipilimumab-pretreated tumors (MHC-II HLA + LDH + lymph-node metastasis; AUC 0.90) and one for ipilimumab-naive tumors (heterogeneity + ploidy + purity; AUC 0.77).

## Cohort & data

- **n = 144 patients** with advanced melanoma (after QC; 121 with RNA-seq) treated with anti-PD1 ICB ([nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md) n=59, 41.0%; [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md) n=85, 59.0%). [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- 206 patients initially identified; 144 passed WES QC, 121 passed RNA-seq QC (Extended Data Fig. 1). [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- Cancer types: 72.9% cutaneous ([SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md), n=105), 13.2% occult (n=19), 6.9% acral (n=10), 6.9% mucosal (n=10) — collectively [MEL](../cancer_types/MEL.md). [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- Prior ipilimumab: 41.7% (n=60); ipilimumab-naive: 58.3% (n=84). [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- Stage: 75.0% M1c (n=108), 12.5% M1b, 5.6% M1a, 6.9% unresectable III. Elevated LDH in 49.3%. ECOG 0 in 68.8%. Median follow-up 29.9 months. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- Assays: pre-treatment tumor [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) (matched normal) and bulk [rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md); purity/ploidy via [absolute](../methods/absolute.md); [mutational-signatures](../methods/mutational-signatures.md); pathway analysis with [gsea](../methods/gsea.md); immune deconvolution with [cibersortx](../methods/cibersortx.md) (CIBERSORT, LM22 signature matrix). [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- Best objective response (RECIST v1.1): 45% PD, 14% SD, 3% MR, 26% PR, 12% CR; overall response rate 38%. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- Independent validation cohort for prior-ipilimumab MHC-II analysis: [mel_iatlas_riaz_nivolumab_2017](../datasets/mel_iatlas_riaz_nivolumab_2017.md) (n=32; 15 ipilimumab-treated, 17 ipilimumab-naive). [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)

## Key findings

- Median nonsynonymous TMB was **6.5 mutations/Mb** (IQR 2.0–14.4; 250.5 mutations/exome, IQR 77.75–578.5). [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- Mutational frequencies in driver genes: [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) 39%, [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) 30%, [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) 17%. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- TMB was higher in responders (CR/PR, n=55) than progressors (PD, n=65): two-sided MWW **P = 0.026**. But TMB was *not* significantly different between responders and SD/MR (n=24): P = 0.14. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- **TMB is confounded by melanoma subtype.** Cutaneous + occult (n=124) had median 297.5 nonsynonymous mutations versus 58 for acral + mucosal (n=20); MWW P = 1.1×10⁻⁶. Stratified within subtype, TMB no longer separated responders from progressors (e.g. skin n=42/43, P=0.27; acral n=7/2, P=0.19). In multivariate logistic regression adjusting for subtype, TMB was not significant (P = 0.24). [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- After multiple-hypothesis correction, **no single gene mutation** was significantly associated with response. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- [TAP2](../genes/TAP2.md) amplification (n=6) was exclusively in responders: Fisher's exact **P = 0.008**. Amplification of MHC-I [HLA-A](../genes/HLA-A.md)/[HLA-B](../genes/HLA-B.md)/[HLA-C](../genes/HLA-C.md) (n=6) also exclusively in responders: Fisher's P = 0.008. Combined TAP2/MHC-I HLA amplification (n=8): Fisher's P = 0.001. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- LOH of [JAK1](../genes/JAK1.md) was associated with resistance: OR = 0.33, Fisher's **P = 0.02**. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- LOH in [B2M](../genes/B2M.md) (16% responders vs 25% progressors, OR=0.6) and biallelic [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) alteration (27% vs 38%, OR=0.6) were not significant. LOH in [HLA-A](../genes/HLA-A.md)/[HLA-B](../genes/HLA-B.md)/[HLA-C](../genes/HLA-C.md) and [SERPINB3](../genes/SERPINB3.md)/[SERPINB4](../genes/SERPINB4.md) mutations were not associated with response. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- Expression of all 13 MHC-II HLA genes was higher in responders (collective two-sided binomial **P = 0.0002**); HLA-DMA, HLA-DMB, HLA-DOA, HLA-DOB individually passed MWW P<0.05. MHC-I HLA genes trended higher in responders (binomial P = 0.02) but no individual gene passed significance. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- [gsea](../methods/gsea.md) (Hallmark gene sets): 24 pathways enriched in responders at FDR q<0.1; 5 of top 6 were immune-related (IFN-γ response, allograft rejection, complement, inflammatory response, IL-6–JAK–[STAT3](../genes/STAT3.md)). No pathways enriched in progressors. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- Tumor heterogeneity (proportion subclonal mutations) higher in progressors (MWW P = 0.02); ploidy lower in progressors (P = 0.04); purity higher in progressors (P = 0.04). [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- **Prior ipilimumab changes the predictor landscape.** MHC-II HLA ssGSEA score was higher in responders versus progressors overall (P=0.03) and in the ipilimumab-treated subgroup (P=0.03) but not in the ipilimumab-naive subgroup (P=0.31). Higher heterogeneity and lower ploidy distinguished progressors only in ipilimumab-naive tumors (P=0.06 and P=0.004). [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- IFN-γ and IFN-α response pathways were enriched in responders overall (FDR q<0.001) and in ipilimumab-treated subgroup (q<0.001) but not in ipilimumab-naive subgroup (q=0.13 and q=0.997). Replicated in validation cohort [mel_iatlas_riaz_nivolumab_2017](../datasets/mel_iatlas_riaz_nivolumab_2017.md). [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- Low MHC-II expression (median split) was associated with primary resistance in the ipilimumab-treated subgroup (Fisher's P=0.02; **OR = 9.9**, 95% CI 1.5–63.7) but not the ipilimumab-naive subgroup (P=0.32; OR=1.9). [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- Low immune infiltrate was strongly associated with intrinsic resistance in the ipilimumab-treated subgroup (Fisher's P=0.02; **OR = 12.9**, 95% CI 1.3–125.8) but not in the ipilimumab-naive subgroup or overall cohort. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- **Predictive model — ipilimumab-treated (n=34):** MHC-II HLA + LDH + lymph-node metastasis. AUC = **0.90** (5-fold CV mean 0.83; empiric P<0.001). TMB did not significantly improve fit (P=0.10). High-versus-low model score stratified PFS (log-rank P=0.0001) and [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) (log-rank P=0.001). [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- **Predictive model — ipilimumab-naive (n=84):** heterogeneity + ploidy + purity. AUC = **0.77** (10-fold CV mean 0.73; empiric P=0.036). TMB did not improve fit (P=0.63). High-versus-low score stratified PFS (P=0.005) and OS (P<0.0001). [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- Each model performed poorly on the opposite subgroup (AUC 0.49 and 0.54, respectively); interaction tests P=0.004 and P=0.018 — i.e. prior-ipilimumab status stratifies which model applies. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)

## Genes & alterations

- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) — recurrent melanoma drivers; mutated in 39% / 30% / 17% of the cohort but not differentially associated with anti-PD1 response. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- [TAP2](../genes/TAP2.md) — focal amplification (chr 6p21, MHC region) found exclusively in responders (n=6/120; Fisher's P=0.008). Four of six co-amplified with MHC-I HLA-A/B/C; two were more focal. [TAP2](../genes/TAP2.md) expression was higher in responders in the ipilimumab-treated subgroup (MWW P=0.02). [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- [HLA-A](../genes/HLA-A.md), [HLA-B](../genes/HLA-B.md), [HLA-C](../genes/HLA-C.md) — focal amplification in the ~1.5 Mb MHC-I region (n=6) exclusively in responders (Fisher's P=0.008). LOH in these genes was *not* associated with response (P=0.52, 0.57, 0.84). [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- [B2M](../genes/B2M.md) — LOH in 9/55 (16%) responders versus 16/65 (25%) progressors (OR=0.6), not significant (Fisher's P=0.37); only rare point mutations observed. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- [TAP1](../genes/TAP1.md) — only rare mutations in MHC-I antigen-presentation genes; no significant association with response. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- [JAK1](../genes/JAK1.md) — LOH significantly associated with resistance (OR=0.33; Fisher's P=0.02), confirming prior IFN-γ-pathway resistance findings. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — biallelic alteration in 27% responders versus 38% progressors (OR=0.6), not significant (P=0.24). [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- [SERPINB3](../genes/SERPINB3.md), [SERPINB4](../genes/SERPINB4.md) — mutations not associated with response (Fisher's P=0.51 and P=1.0); does not replicate previously proposed positive association. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- MHC-II HLA gene set (13 genes including HLA-DRA, HLA-DRB1, HLA-DRB5, [HLA-DPA1](../genes/HLA-DPA1.md), HLA-DPB1, HLA-DQA1, HLA-DQA2, HLA-DQB1, HLA-DQB2, HLA-DMA, HLA-DMB, HLA-DOA, HLA-DOB) — collective transcriptomic expression higher in responders (binomial P=0.0002); ssGSEA composite was the strongest single predictor of response in ipilimumab-treated tumors. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- [CXCL9](../genes/CXCL9.md), [CXCL10](../genes/CXCL10.md), [CXCR3](../genes/CXCR3.md) — higher expression in responders in the ipilimumab-treated subgroup (MWW P=0.05, 0.08, 0.02) but not in ipilimumab-naive tumors. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- [CTLA4](../genes/CTLA4.md) — higher expression in responders in the ipilimumab-treated subgroup (P=0.008). [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- [TGFB2](../genes/TGFB2.md) — higher expression in progressors in the ipilimumab-naive subgroup (MWW P=0.002), but not in the ipilimumab-treated subgroup (P=0.43). [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)

## Clinical implications

- **TMB as a single-marker biomarker is unreliable in melanoma.** Subtype (especially mucosal/acral vs cutaneous/occult) confounds the TMB–response association; authors argue TMB "cannot be applied generically across melanoma subtypes as a predictive biomarker for anti-PD1 ICB." [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- **MHC-II HLA expression is a candidate biomarker** for response to anti-PD1 ICB, particularly in ipilimumab-pretreated patients. Combined with LDH and lymph-node metastasis status, it yields a parsimonious model with AUC 0.90 in discovery (CV 0.83). [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- **In ipilimumab-naive patients**, tumor heterogeneity, ploidy and purity (all WES-derivable) constitute a parsimonious predictor (AUC 0.77, CV 0.73) of primary resistance. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- **Prior anti-CTLA4 exposure (ipilimumab) stratifies predictors of response to subsequent anti-PD1 ICB.** Patients with low immune infiltrate and low MHC-II expression at post-ipilimumab biopsy are very likely intrinsically resistant to anti-PD1 ([ipilimumab](../drugs/ipilimumab.md) → [nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md)/[pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md) sequence). These patients may benefit from alternative regimens (e.g., combination ICB) rather than further anti-PD1 monotherapy. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- High predictive-model score (low MHC-II / LDH / LN met OR high heterogeneity-low ploidy-high purity) was associated with substantially worse PFS and OS: in the ipilimumab-treated subgroup, median PFS 2.8 vs 38.1 months and median OS 9.0 months versus not reached. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- B-cell infiltrate markers (CD19, MS4A1/CD20) were enriched in ipilimumab-experienced responders — consistent with emerging evidence for B-cell roles in neoadjuvant immunotherapy response. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- Authors note BRAF/NRAS mutation status was not predictive of anti-PD1 response, so targeted-therapy decisions (e.g., [dacarbazine](../drugs/dacarbazine.md) historical context) are separate from these ICB biomarker considerations. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)

## Limitations & open questions

- **Bulk transcriptome cannot disambiguate cell-of-origin** of MHC-II expression. Whether the predictive signal arises from tumor-cell MHC-II (as suggested by prior IHC studies the authors cite) or from antigen-presenting cells in the microenvironment is unresolved. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- **Limited independent validation.** The largest available external cohort with both prior-ipilimumab metadata and matched WES/RNA-seq was n=32, underpowered for formal validation of the ipilimumab-stratified models. Predictor directions were concordant but most were not significant in validation (e.g., low MHC-II in ipilimumab-treated empiric P=0.21; heterogeneity in ipilimumab-naive empiric P=0.066). [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- **Mucosal/acral subtypes are underpowered** (n=20 total). The TMB-confounding finding rests on a relatively small mucosal/acral group; responder counts there are tiny (4). [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- **Mechanism of anti-CTLA4–induced predictor shift is unknown.** Whether prior [ipilimumab](../drugs/ipilimumab.md) *induces* an immune-resistant state versus simply *reveals* a preexisting one is explicitly flagged as needing further investigation. Cross-resistance to sequential ICB may also predict resistance to combination ICB — testable hypothesis. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- **Biological basis of ploidy and purity associations is unclear.** Authors hypothesize purity is a proxy for immune infiltrate; ploidy association is not mechanistically explained (and runs counter to prior aneuploidy findings). [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- Models were trained and discovery-tested in the same cohort; the AUC numbers reported (0.90, 0.77) are cross-validated within the discovery cohort, not on a fully held-out external set. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
- No formal correction for the per-subgroup gene-expression p-values (P=0.02, 0.03, 0.04 for individual immune genes); authors note these are unadjusted. [PMID:31792460](../papers/31792460.md)
