**PMID:** 36007103 · **DOI:** 10.1158/1078-0432.CCR-22-1134 · **Journal:** Clinical Cancer Research (2023)

## TL;DR

This prospective proof-of-principle study from Memorial Sloan Kettering tested whether high-sensitivity, UMI-based cell-free DNA (cfDNA) sequencing can capture the mutational repertoire of primary endometrial cancer (EC) and enable longitudinal disease monitoring. In 44 newly diagnosed EC patients with serial pre- and post-surgical plasma collections over 24 months, primary tumors were profiled by [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/IMPACT468.md) (468 genes) and matched cfDNA by [MSK-ACCESS](../methods/ACCESS129.md) (129-gene high-depth, molecular-barcoded assay). Baseline cfDNA mutations were detected in 22% (8/36) of patients — almost all with advanced stage or aggressive histology — and 35/38 (92%) of primary-tumor mutations covered by MSK-ACCESS were detected in baseline plasma. Changes in ctDNA fraction during follow-up tracked treatment response and disease progression, with a lead time over imaging in two cases. Detectable ctDNA at baseline (p<0.001, HR 11.14) and immediately post-surgery (p=0.014, HR 15.56) were significantly associated with reduced progression-free survival.

## Cohort & data

- **N = 44** prospectively accrued, biopsy-confirmed newly diagnosed [endometrial cancer (UCEC)](../cancer_types/UCEC.md) patients consented at [MSK](../datasets/ucec_ccr_cfdna_msk_2022.md) between 09/2017 and 07/2018 ([PMID:36007103](../papers/36007103.md)).
- **Stage distribution:** FIGO stage I in 34/44 (77%); advanced stage III/IV in 10/44 (23%).
- **Histology:** [endometrioid (UEC)](../cancer_types/UEC.md) 24/44 (55%); [serous carcinoma (USC)](../cancer_types/USC.md) 7/44 (16%); [carcinosarcoma (UCS)](../cancer_types/UCS.md) 6/44 (14%); remaining cases included high-grade carcinomas of no specific type and an adenosarcoma (CD37).
- **Tissue NGS:** 42 primary ECs profiled with [MSK-IMPACT 468-gene panel](../methods/IMPACT468.md) (median tumor depth 618x, range 101–1030x; normal 504x). Six stage III/IV cases also had microdissected extra-uterine sites (sentinel lymph nodes, peritoneal implants, fallopian tube) sequenced.
- **cfDNA NGS:** 36 patients with adequate plasma input (10–20 ng cfDNA) were sequenced on [MSK-ACCESS](../methods/ACCESS129.md), a 129-gene UMI-based liquid-biopsy assay (median raw coverage 25,611x; median duplex coverage 1,340x post-collapsing). Variant calling was tumor-informed ("genotyping") via Waltz, requiring ≥2 duplex consensus reads.
- **Longitudinal:** Blood drawn at baseline (pre-op), post-op (median 22 days, range 9–57), and every 6 months to 24 months. 229/258 (89%) intended draws were collected despite COVID-19 disruption. Longitudinal serial samples from 25 patients were sequenced.
- **Dataset:** Targeted sequencing data deposited at [cBioPortal as ucec_ccr_cfdna_msk_2022](../datasets/ucec_ccr_cfdna_msk_2022.md) ("Endometrial Carcinoma cfDNA (MSK, Clin Cancer Res 2022)").
- **Follow-up:** Median 33 months (range 0.3–44); 11 patients had recurrent or persistent disease.

## Key findings

- **cfDNA concentration correlates with stage.** Median pre-operative cfDNA concentration was 2.71 ng/mL (range 0.66–20.62). Patients with stage III/IV disease had significantly higher cfDNA than stage I patients (Wilcoxon rank sum, p=0.028). cfDNA was numerically higher in [carcinosarcoma (UCS)](../cancer_types/UCS.md) and high-grade carcinomas of no specific type but did not reach significance (p>0.1) ([PMID:36007103](../papers/36007103.md)).
- **Primary-tumor mutational landscape recapitulates TCGA.** Median 7.5 non-synonymous mutations per primary tumor (range 1–92). Most frequently mutated genes: [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) 52%, [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) 50%, [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) 50%, [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) 36%, [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md) 29%. TCGA molecular subtype distribution: 10/42 (24%) MSI, 12/42 (29%) copy-number low, 20/42 (48%) copy-number high.
- **Baseline ctDNA detection enriched in advanced/high-risk disease.** Tumor-derived mutations were detected in baseline cfDNA in 8/36 (22%) patients sequenced; 6/8 had stage III/IV disease, and the remaining two were a stage IA high-grade adenosarcoma and a stage IB high-grade mixed endometrioid/serous carcinoma with isolated tumor cells and lymphovascular space invasion. **No** low-grade stage I [endometrioid (UEC)](../cancer_types/UEC.md) patients had detectable baseline ctDNA.
- **High concordance between primary tumor and baseline cfDNA.** Of 38 somatic mutations in primary tumors covered by [MSK-ACCESS](../methods/ACCESS129.md), 35 (92%) were detected at baseline and 38 (100%) at some follow-up timepoint. All mutations clonal in the primary tumor and within the panel were detected in pre-operative cfDNA, with the exception of patient [CD28](../genes/CD28.md) (subclonal [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) detected, but clonal [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) E61Pfs*54 and [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) C228T promoter mutation only detected at later timepoints).
- **Baseline ctDNA fractions varied.** Most ctDNA-positive cases had baseline ctDNA fraction 0.7–2.0%; outliers were CD22 (stage IVB serous EC, 5.8%) and CD23 (stage IVB carcinosarcoma with 2,608 cm³ tumor burden, 74.6%). Both patients died of disease 30–31 months post-baseline.
- **Tumor–lymph node heterogeneity.** In stage III/IV cases with sequenced extra-uterine sites, mutations could be private to the primary or to specific sentinel lymph nodes. Notable: in CD20, [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) truncating, [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) and [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) hotspot mutations subclonal in the primary became clonal in sentinel lymph nodes; a clonal [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) mutation plus [RNF43](../genes/RNF43.md), [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md), [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) frameshifts were shared with only one of two SLNs; [AGO2](../genes/AGO2.md) and [FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md) missense mutations were SLN-restricted. cfDNA at baseline captured the primary repertoire more faithfully than the lymph node repertoire.
- **Serial cfDNA mirrors clinical course.** In 6/25 patients with serial sampling, changes in MAFs and ctDNA fraction tracked imaging-confirmed response or progression. Examples: CD20 (stage IIIC2 dedifferentiated EC) — MAFs rose with progression at 8–9 months, fell during salvage chemotherapy, rose again at 18–24 months with retroperitoneal adenopathy; CD26 (stage IIIC1 carcinosarcoma) — ctDNA cleared on therapy then rose with bony/nodal recurrence; CD22 (stage IVB serous) — [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md)/[PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)/[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)/[PPP2R1A](../genes/PPP2R1A.md) MAFs fell post-treatment then rose with lung and perirectal metastases.
- **Lead time over imaging in selected cases.** In CD28 and CD36, rising ctDNA preceded imaging-detected recurrence by 6 months. In CD22 and CD28, ctDNA MAF rises preceded elevated serum CA125 by 5.8 and 17 months, respectively, suggesting ctDNA may be more sensitive than CA125 for surveillance in EC.
- **Baseline ctDNA is prognostic.** Detectable baseline ctDNA was significantly associated with worse progression-free survival (PFS): **p<0.001, HR 11.14 (95% CI 2.72–45.59)**. 7/8 patients with baseline ctDNA recurred within follow-up (the exception was CD37 adenosarcoma).
- **Post-surgical ctDNA is prognostic.** Landmark analysis (landmark = 51 days post-surgery) showed detectable post-surgery ctDNA was associated with disease progression: **p=0.014, HR 15.56 (95% CI 2.16–112.16)**. All 4 patients with detectable post-surgery ctDNA had recurrent or persistent disease.
- **Uterine sarcoma feasibility.** The CD37 adenosarcoma harbored 12 somatic mutations including pathogenic [DICER1](../genes/DICER1.md), [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), and [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) mutations; the 3 covered by [MSK-ACCESS](../methods/ACCESS129.md) were detected in baseline cfDNA, suggesting liquid biopsy may extend to uterine sarcomas.

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — mutated in 52% of primary ECs; the most common driver in this cohort. Subclonal [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) detected in CD28 baseline cfDNA; clonal [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) tracked in CD20, CD22, CD26, CD28 longitudinal series.
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — mutated in 50%. Clonal [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) shared between primary EC and one of two SLNs in CD20 (heterogeneity); pathogenic [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) detected in CD37 adenosarcoma cfDNA.
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — mutated in 50%. Hotspot [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) subclonal in CD20 primary became clonal in sentinel lymph nodes; serial monitoring of [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) MAFs tracked progression in CD22, CD26, CD28.
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) — mutated in 36%; truncating [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) mutations recurrent in cfDNA-positive cases (CD20, CD22, CD37).
- [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md) — mutated in 29%. Tracked in CD36 longitudinal series (stage IIIA grade 1 endometrioid EC).
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — hotspot mutations subclonal in CD20 primary, clonal in SLN.
- [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) — listed among genes interrogated in hotspot cfDNA EC literature reviewed by the authors.
- [PPP2R1A](../genes/PPP2R1A.md) — shared mutation in CD22 (primary, mesenteric implant, three lymph nodes, cfDNA).
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — E61Pfs*54 clonal in CD28 primary; not detected at baseline but detected at 6-month follow-up.
- [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) — C228T promoter mutation clonal in CD28 primary; detected later in cfDNA, not at baseline.
- [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md), [APC](../genes/APC.md), [ERBB3](../genes/ERBB3.md) — among the 5 primary-tumor mutations in CD36 (stage IIIA grade 1 endometrioid), all detected in baseline cfDNA.
- [RNF43](../genes/RNF43.md), [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md), [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) — frameshift mutations co-occurring with [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) in CD20, present in primary + one of two SLNs.
- [AGO2](../genes/AGO2.md), [FOXA1](../genes/FOXA1.md) — missense mutations restricted to sentinel lymph nodes in CD20 (private to nodal compartment).
- [DICER1](../genes/DICER1.md), [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md), [NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md) — pathogenic mutations in CD37 adenosarcoma ([DICER1](../genes/DICER1.md), [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md)) and CD26 carcinosarcoma ([NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md)).

## Clinical implications

- **Prognostic biomarker at diagnosis.** A single pre-operative blood draw analyzed by [MSK-ACCESS](../methods/ACCESS129.md) yields a strong prognostic signal in newly diagnosed EC (HR 11.14 for PFS), supporting baseline ctDNA as a candidate risk stratifier beyond stage and histology — particularly in high-risk histologies ([USC](../cancer_types/USC.md), [UCS](../cancer_types/UCS.md)).
- **MRD detection post-surgery.** Detectable ctDNA within ~51 days post-surgery is associated with very poor PFS (HR 15.56), identifying patients who may benefit from intensified adjuvant therapy. Conversely, ctDNA-negative low-grade stage I [endometrioid (UEC)](../cancer_types/UEC.md) patients had no detectable cfDNA signal, consistent with their favorable prognosis and potentially supporting de-escalation studies.
- **Treatment response monitoring.** Serial ctDNA tracking on adjuvant or salvage [paclitaxel](../drugs/paclitaxel.md)/[carboplatin](../drugs/carboplatin.md) and radiation showed real-time correlation with imaging response and progression, with ctDNA rises preceding clinically detected recurrence by up to 6 months and elevations in serum CA125 by 5.8–17 months.
- **Targetable alteration capture.** Because EC has a high number of recurrently mutated cancer genes ([TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md)), gene-panel-based tumor-informed cfDNA assays are well suited for longitudinal monitoring without requiring personalized tumor-bespoke panels.
- **Sarcoma extension.** Detection of [DICER1](../genes/DICER1.md), [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), and [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) mutations in cfDNA of the CD37 adenosarcoma motivates larger cfDNA studies in uterine sarcomas.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Small sample size.** N=44 with stratification across stage and histology yields very small subgroup numbers (some histology categories have only 1–2 patients), and PFS analyses are based on limited events; multivariate analysis was not feasible.
- **Non-consecutive enrollment.** Cases were selected to represent histologic and FIGO stage diversity, not a consecutive series, so prevalence estimates of baseline ctDNA positivity may not generalize to an unselected diagnostic cohort.
- **Limited sensitivity in low-stage, low-grade disease.** [MSK-ACCESS](../methods/ACCESS129.md) at 0.1% (tumor-informed) / 0.5% (de novo) detection threshold could not detect ctDNA in any low-grade stage I [endometrioid (UEC)](../cancer_types/UEC.md) patient. Whether more sensitive personalized MRD assays would identify clinically meaningful ctDNA in this subgroup remains open.
- **Panel coverage gaps.** [MSK-ACCESS](../methods/ACCESS129.md) covers a 129-gene subset of [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/IMPACT468.md); the 38 primary-tumor mutations interrogated represent only the panel-overlapping subset, so true tumor-to-plasma concordance for the full mutational repertoire is not measured.
- **COVID-19 disruption.** 11% of intended follow-up draws were missed, particularly at the 18- and 24-month timepoints.
- **CA125 comparison is exploratory.** Only two patients had serial CA125 measurements; broader comparison of ctDNA vs. CA125 sensitivity in EC requires prospective studies.
- **Therapeutic decision impact unproven.** The study demonstrates prognostic and monitoring value but does not test whether ctDNA-guided treatment intensification improves outcomes — larger interventional trials are warranted.
- **Heterogeneity between primary and lymph node sites** suggests baseline cfDNA may miss mutations that emerge in nodal metastases, complicating any future use of cfDNA as a tumor-genotyping surrogate for therapy selection.
