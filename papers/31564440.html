Hyperpolarized MRI of human prostate cancer reveals increased lactate with tumor grade driven by monocarboxylate transporter 1

TL;DR

Granlund, Tee, and colleagues at Memorial Sloan Kettering imaged 12 men with biopsy-proven prostate cancer using hyperpolarized [1-13C] pyruvate MRI before radical prostatectomy (17 total injections, including 5 patients re-imaged for test/re-test). The maximum lactate ratio (Lac_max) was significantly higher in tumor regions than in normal prostate (p=0.0001 for Gleason 3, p<0.0001 for Gleason ≥4) and rose with Gleason grade. Immunohistochemistry plus TCGA RNA-seq pinned the upregulated monocarboxylate transporter 1 (MCT1; HUGO SLC16A1) as the rate-limiting driver of the lactate signal. Targeted MSK-IMPACT sequencing of the same patients revealed that homozygous PTEN loss tracked with the highest Lac_max (p=0.059), suggesting HP-MRI could serve as a non-invasive readout of PI3K-pathway activation in prostate adenocarcinoma.

Cohort & data

  • 12 patients (mean age 62 ± 7 yrs, range 51–72) with histologically confirmed prostate adenocarcinoma (>1 cm) scheduled for radical prostatectomy at MSKCC; enrolled May 2015–July 2018 under IRB#14–205 (NCT02421380).
  • 17 hyperpolarized [1-13C] pyruvate injections: 5 patients were imaged twice (4 same-day, 1 hr apart; Patient 1 imaged 3 weeks apart). Patient 3 opted for brachytherapy and was excluded from imaging–tissue comparisons.
  • Imaging assay: 3T wide-bore scanner (GE MR750w) with dual-tuned ¹H/¹³C endorectal coil + clamshell ¹³C transmit coil; 2D dynamic echo-planar spectroscopic imaging (EPSI) at 1×1×1.5-cm³ voxels, 4.9-s temporal resolution. Pyruvate prep: 14.2 M [1-13C] pyruvate with 15 mM trityl radical, dissolved, neutralized with Tris/NaOH, terminally sterilized; 0.43 mL/kg of 250 mM dose IV. See hyperpolarized-13c-mri.
  • Pathology: whole-mount step-section prostatectomies (H&E + IHC) read by a genitourinary pathologist; MCT1 IHC at 1:100 (Santa Cruz). See immunohistochemistry.
  • Genomics: targeted DNA sequencing via the MSK-IMPACT prospective clinical platform (468-gene IMPACT468 hybridization-capture panel, Cheng et al. 2015). Allele-specific copy number via FACETS run sequentially (purity at critical value 100, then sensitivity at 50). Raw IMPACT data deposited at EVA accession PRJEB33969 and at cBioPortal as prad_msk_2019.
  • External RNA-seq: TCGA PRAD (limma voom differential expression on RSEM-normalized Firebrowse data) — prad_tcga. See rna-seq.

Key findings

  • HP pyruvate delivery is reproducible across patients when corrected for arterial input. Time-to-max pyruvate in the gland ranged 11.2–33.5 s (21.8±5.4 s) post-injection but collapsed to 6.2–12.4 s (10.1±2.4 s) when corrected to the internal pudendal artery input; corrected delivery showed a bimodal distribution (6.7±0.4 s and 11.6±0.6 s), separation matching the 4.9-s temporal resolution of the EPSI sequence (PMID:31564440).
  • Test/re-test is stable. Across 5 patients imaged twice, neither time-to-max pyruvate nor time-to-max lactate differed significantly between injections after perfusion correction (p=0.24 and p=0.78); Lac_max also did not differ between paired injections (p=0.2) (PMID:31564440).
  • Lac_max rises with Gleason grade. Lac_max was significantly lower in normal prostate than in cancer regions (p=0.0001 for primary Gleason grade 3, p<0.0001 for primary Gleason ≥4) (PMID:31564440).
  • Lactate signal correlates poorly with conventional clinical markers, with PSA the strongest (R²=0.26) and spatial measurements (prostate volume, tumor volume, max tumor dimension) all R²<0.20 — arguing that metabolism, not bulk disease, drives the lactate signal (PMID:31564440).
  • MCT1 (SLC16A1) explains the lactate signal. In TCGA PRAD bulk RNA-seq, SLC16A1 and LDHA were significantly upregulated in tumor vs. matched normal. By IHC, MCT1 stained strongly in cancer regions across all patients; ROIs with >30% positive MCT1 staining had Lac_max 60% higher than low-MCT1 ROIs (0.38 ± 0.3 vs. 0.23 ± 0.04, P=0.028) (PMID:31564440).
  • Homozygous PTEN loss tracks with the highest Lac_max. MSK-IMPACT sequencing identified n=2 ROIs with homozygous PTEN loss and n=3 with heterozygous loss; the association between PTEN loss and Lac_max approached significance (P=0.059) (PMID:31564440).
  • TMPRSS2–ERG fusion did not segregate Lac_max. A subset of patients carried TMPRSS2-ERG gene fusions; Lac_max in fusion-positive ROIs was not elevated relative to non-fusion ROIs (PMID:31564440).
  • HP-MRI can light up lesions missed by anatomic MRI. Supplementary Figure S1F shows an anterior apical lesion not clearly visible on T2-weighted or diffusion-weighted images but co-localized with HP lactate signal at the pathology-confirmed tumor site (PMID:31564440).

Genes & alterations

  • PTEN — copy-number loss (homozygous in 2 ROIs, heterozygous in 3 ROIs) called from IMPACT468 panel via FACETS; homozygous loss tracked with the highest HP lactate (P=0.059). Authors invoke PI3K-pathway de-repression as the mechanistic link to increased glycolytic flux.
  • SLC16A1 (MCT1) — significantly upregulated at the RNA level in prad_tcga tumors vs. matched normals; IHC-positive in cancer regions across all 12 patients; identified as the rate-limiting transporter for HP pyruvate uptake and the proximate driver of the lactate signal in vivo.
  • LDHA — also significantly upregulated in prad_tcga tumor vs. normal; catalyzes the forward pyruvate→lactate reaction whose flux HP-MRI measures.
  • SLC16A3 (MCT4) — discussed as the alternate monocarboxylate transporter candidate but not the primary driver in this cohort; MCT1 dominated by IHC.
  • TMPRSS2–ERG gene fusion — present in a subset of patients (called from MSK-IMPACT); did not correlate with HP lactate signal in this 12-patient cohort, though authors note recent lung-cancer work suggesting the fusion can regulate lactate export.

Clinical implications

  • Imaging biomarker for tumor aggressiveness. HP lactate ratio (Lac_max) tracks Gleason grade and is reproducible within patients, supporting its development as a non-invasive readout for active surveillance vs. definitive-treatment decisions in localized prostate adenocarcinoma.
  • Companion biomarker for MCT1-targeted therapy. Because MCT1 expression is the proximate driver of HP lactate signal, HP-MRI could pre-select patients likely to respond to MCT1 inhibitors and provide an early pharmacodynamic readout.
  • Functional readout of PI3K-pathway activation. Homozygous PTEN loss — a known driver of PI3K-pathway de-repression and aggressive disease — tracked with the highest HP lactate, suggesting HP-MRI could stratify patients for PI3K/AKT/mTOR-targeted regimens without an upfront biopsy.
  • Treatment-response monitoring. Test/re-test stability (no significant change at 1 hr) and prior case-report evidence of HP lactate dropping after androgen deprivation therapy (Aggarwal et al., 2017) support using HP-MRI to monitor metabolic response on a sub-week timescale, faster than tumor-volume readouts.

Limitations & open questions

  • Small cohort (n=12 patients, 17 injections). Authors explicitly call out the patient population as limiting subtlety of detectable metabolic differences and call for large-cohort studies analogous to PET trials.
  • Spatial resolution (1×1×1.5-cm³ voxels) limits intratumoral heterogeneity analysis and causes partial-volume contamination when matching to whole-mount pathology; authors expect the Lac_max–Gleason correlation to strengthen with higher-resolution acquisitions.
  • PTEN–Lac_max association is borderline (P=0.059). Confirming the mechanistic link, and disentangling it from MCT1 expression, will require a larger cohort with paired MSK-IMPACT and HP-MRI data.
  • TMPRSS2-ERG vs. lactate result is underpowered and contradicts emerging lung-cancer literature on ERG regulating lactate export; whether prostate ERG fusions modulate glycolytic flux remains open.
  • Temporal resolution (4.9 s) limits arterial-input correction; the bimodal time-to-max distribution is an artifact of this sampling, motivating bolus-tracked or non-localized scout acquisitions.
  • Only one bicarbonate detection (1/12 patients) — pyruvate dehydrogenase flux could not be evaluated across the cohort.
  • No comparator imaging modality (e.g., FDG-PET, multi-parametric MRI quantitative metrics) was prospectively co-acquired to benchmark added value.
