# azathioprine

## Overview

Azathioprine is a thiopurine immunosuppressant used in organ transplantation, autoimmune diseases, and inflammatory bowel disease. It is a prodrug converted to 6-thioguanine nucleotides that incorporate into DNA, causing mutagenesis. Long-term exposure is associated with a characteristic mutational signature (COSMIC SBS32) and increased risk of skin cancers, particularly cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma ([CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md)).

## Evidence in the corpus

- One patient in metastatic [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md) WGS cohort (n=25) was on long-term azathioprine for immunosuppression; their tumor showed SBS32 (azathioprine-associated mutational signature), confirming iatrogenic mutagenesis as a contributor to CSCC in transplant recipients [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Resistance mechanisms

- Not applicable (immunosuppressant, not oncology targeted therapy).
