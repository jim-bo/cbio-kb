# MAST4

## Overview

MAST4 (Microtubule-Associated Serine/Threonine Kinase Family Member 4) is a serine/threonine kinase with a PDZ domain involved in signaling scaffolding. It has been linked to neurological development and has emerging roles in cancer signaling. In breast cancer proteogenomics, elevated MAST4 phosphorylation has been identified as a downstream consequence of [GATA3](../genes/GATA3.md) loss-of-function mutations, nominating it as a potential therapeutic target in luminal breast cancer.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Elevated phospho-MAST4 was identified as a novel association with GATA3 mutations in luminal breast cancer ([BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md)) proteogenomic analysis; GATA3 mutants show high phospho-MAST4 and phospho-DCLK1, representing a tumor-suppressor-loss → targetable-kinase link (requires validation). [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **BRCA (luminal)**: Phospho-MAST4 elevation is specifically associated with GATA3-mutant luminal breast cancer subtypes. [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-elevated with phospho-[DCLK1](DCLK1.md) in [GATA3](GATA3.md)-mutated luminal breast tumors. [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- MAST4 inhibition is a hypothesis-generating therapeutic target in GATA3-mutant luminal breast cancer, based solely on phosphoproteomic association; no clinical data or in vitro functional validation reported in this study. [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md)

## Open questions

- The mechanistic link between GATA3 loss-of-function and elevated MAST4 phosphorylation is undefined; whether it is direct or via an intermediate pathway is unknown. [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md)
