# ZNF350

## Overview

ZNF350 (also known as ZBRK1) encodes a KRAB-domain zinc finger protein that interacts with [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) and functions as a transcriptional repressor. It has been implicated in DNA damage response pathways through its BRCA1 interaction. Recurrent expressed mutations in rhabdomyosarcoma suggest a candidate role in pediatric sarcoma pathogenesis.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Recurrently expressed mutations (2 cases) in a comprehensive rhabdomyosarcoma ([RMS](../cancer_types/RMS.md)) genomic analysis; interacts with BRCA1; flagged as a candidate target requiring functional validation [PMID:24436047](../papers/24436047.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

## Therapeutic relevance

- BRCA1-interacting partner; loss of ZNF350 function may affect homologous recombination DNA repair, with potential relevance to PARP inhibitor sensitivity (not directly established in this corpus).

## Open questions

- Functional role of ZNF350 mutations in RMS is unvalidated; relationship to BRCA1-mediated DNA repair in this tumor type is hypothetical.
