# LIN28A

## Overview

LIN28A encodes an RNA-binding protein that regulates developmental timing and cellular reprogramming. It is a key oncogenic driver in embryonal tumors with multilayered rosettes (ETMR), a rare and highly aggressive pediatric brain tumor subtype, where LIN28A overexpression is a hallmark molecular feature often associated with C19MC amplification.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Overexpressed (along with C19MC amplification and/or TTYH1 fusions) in ETMR pediatric brain tumor PDX models (ICb-S1129MB, ICb-1343ENB, IBs-2373PNET) in the PPTC panel of 261 PDX models spanning 37 pediatric cancer types [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **ETMR** — LIN28A overexpression is a defining molecular feature of ETMR; co-occurs with C19MC amplification and TTYH1 fusions in PPTC PDX models [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with C19MC amplification and TTYH1 fusions in ETMR models [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- LIN28A overexpression in ETMR PDX models represents a candidate biomarker for experimental therapies; the PPTC resource enables rational preclinical trial design for this histotype [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md).

## Open questions

- LIN28A-overexpressing ETMRs remain among the most lethal pediatric brain tumors with no established targeted therapy; prospective validation of preclinical models for drug sensitivity is needed.
