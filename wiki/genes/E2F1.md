# E2F1

## Overview

E2F1 encodes a transcription factor that is a key regulator of cell-cycle entry and DNA synthesis. As a downstream effector of the Rb pathway, E2F1 activity is often unleashed by CDKN2A/RB1 loss or cyclin D/E amplification. In bladder TCC, E2F1 has been identified as a focal amplification target.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Focal amplification in bladder TCC [PMID:24121792](../papers/24121792.md)
- Focal amplification predominantly in HPV(+) HNSCC; also shows increased expression via let-7c-5p/miR-100-5p loss [PMID:25631445](../papers/25631445.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md): focal amplification alongside [E2F3](../genes/E2F3.md), [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md), and [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md) in a cell-cycle deregulation context [PMID:24121792](../papers/24121792.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Amplified alongside [E2F3](../genes/E2F3.md), [CCND1](../genes/CCND1.md), [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md), and [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) in bladder TCC [PMID:24121792](../papers/24121792.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct drug data reported in the current corpus; E2F pathway dysregulation is broadly linked to CDK inhibitor sensitivity.

## Open questions

- Relative contribution of E2F1 vs E2F3 amplification to bladder cancer pathogenesis is not resolved in the current corpus.
