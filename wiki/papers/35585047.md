**PMID:** 35585047 · **DOI:** 10.1038/s41467-022-30233-7 · **Journal:** Nature Communications (2022)

## TL;DR

Shukla, Levine, Gundem and colleagues at Memorial Sloan Kettering (MSK) prospectively
applied clinical whole genome and transcriptome sequencing (cWGTS) to 114 pediatric,
adolescent, and young adult patients with solid tumors and rare cancers. They built an
end-to-end workflow with a 9-day sample-to-report turnaround, benchmarked optimal WGS
depth at ≥80× with ≥20% tumor purity, and showed that cWGTS recovered all clinically
reported variants from matched [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) and
[MSK-Fusion](../methods/msk-fusion.md) panels while adding at least one cancer-associated
oncogenic finding in 54% of patients (n=62). The principal value added by WGS over WES +
RNA-seq came from structural variants (SVs) — including enhancer-hijacking events,
[TP53](../genes/TP53.md) tumor-suppressor SVs in osteosarcoma, and [TERT](../genes/TERT.md)
and [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) rearrangements in neuroblastoma — together with genome-wide
mutation signatures, novel fusion genes, and integrated germline assessment. A
proof-of-concept companion analysis showed that comprehensive WGS profiling can also be
derived from cell-free DNA in patients with sufficient ctDNA content.

## Cohort & data

- **Patients (n=114)** with solid tumors enrolled prospectively at MSK on protocol
  NCT01775072; median age 12.6 years (range 4.5 months to 43.8 years). Disease classes
  included [sarcoma](../cancer_types/SARC.md) (n=54), [neuroblastoma](../cancer_types/NBL.md)
  (n=29), [osteosarcoma](../cancer_types/OS.md) (n=29), CNS tumors,
  [carcinoma](../cancer_types/CCRCC.md) (n=7), [Wilms tumor](../cancer_types/WT.md),
  [germ cell tumor](../cancer_types/GCT.md), hepatoblastoma, and other rare entities.
  Of 201 nominated tumors, 58 were excluded on pathology review and 29 failed the >20%
  tumor-purity threshold.
- **Dataset:** [mixed_kunga_msk_2022](../datasets/mixed_kunga_msk_2022.md). Sequencing
  was performed on fresh frozen (FF) or OCT tumor biopsies with matched normal buffy coat.
- **Assays:** [Whole-genome sequencing](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) (Illumina NovaSeq 6000,
  PE150) at a median tumor depth of 95× (range 67–181) and normal at 50× (range 32–159);
  whole-transcriptome [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) on 101 patients; orthogonal panel
  testing by [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) (468-gene DNA panel) and
  [MSK-Fusion](../methods/msk-fusion.md) (RNA fusion capture); germline analysis on a
  subset of 88 cancer-predisposition genes.
- **Companion exploratory cohort:** 7 paired FF tumor + plasma cfDNA samples for
  [cfDNA WGS](../methods/cfdna-wes.md) feasibility analysis (coverage 94–102×).

## Key findings

- **Turnaround:** End-to-end sample-to-report time of 9 days (n=16 post-optimization
  samples), compared with 17 days during the developmental phase (range 11–29, n=59) and
  3–8 weeks reported in prior WGS literature. This is comparable to the Hartwig Medical
  Foundation centralized infrastructure.
- **Yield over standard of care:** cWGTS identified at least one additional oncogenic
  cancer-associated variant in **54% of patients (n=62)** beyond [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md),
  [MSK-Fusion](../methods/msk-fusion.md), and germline 88-gene panel testing combined.
  Of these 62, 33 had directly clinically relevant additional findings: 7 diagnostic
  (21%), 15 prognostic (45%), 5 therapy-informing (15%), 5 previously undescribed
  oncofusions (15%), and 6 germline (18%).
- **Cancer-associated alterations:** cWGTS captured alterations in 99% of patients
  (n=114/114 in supplementary tables; 113 reported in main text), including CNAs in 105,
  cancer-associated SNVs/indels in 77, fusions in 75, germline predisposition in 17,
  translocations or chromothripsis in additional patients, and MSI signal in 7.
- **Concordance with MSK-IMPACT:** Of 221 somatic mutations called by
  [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md), 174 (79%) were independently called by
  WGS, with high VAF concordance (Pearson R²=0.75) across 5–97% VAF. The 47 discordant
  calls were predominantly subclonal (46/47 with <90% cancer cell fraction by
  MSK-IMPACT, median VAF 8.5%) and were attributed to intratumor heterogeneity (ITH)
  rather than WGS sensitivity loss. Targeted re-sequencing of WGS libraries by
  MSK-IMPACT (median local depth 469×) failed to recover any of 44 tested discordant
  calls, supporting ITH as the basis. Same-aliquot concordance reached **at least 94%
  (43/47)** and up to 100% for evaluable samples.
- **Germline and fusions:** cWGTS captured all 13 germline-predisposition variants and
  all 18 oncogenic fusions identified by panel assays, with WGS + RNA-seq providing
  orthogonal validation of every fusion within a single workflow.
- **Optimal WGS depth:** Subsampling 97 tumors with ≥60× coverage (298 derivative BAMs
  at 100×, 80×, 60×, 30–40×) showed >91% of clinically relevant oncogenic events
  recalled at 30–40× and >98% at 60–100×. Genome-wide variant sensitivity across SNVs,
  indels, and SVs plateaued at **≥80× effective coverage** — the threshold the authors
  recommend for clinical WGS. SV sensitivity was slightly lower than SNV sensitivity at
  matched depth.
- **Most additional findings come from SVs and signatures:** Of the incremental
  findings, only 27% would have been captured by WES + RNA-seq in combination (16% by
  RNA-seq alone, 13% by WES alone). The remainder were driven by SVs and genome-wide
  mutation signatures inaccessible to panel/exome assays.
- **Immune-checkpoint case studies:** Two patients (H135022, metastatic adrenocortical
  carcinoma; H135462, relapsed clear-cell carcinoma) lacking therapy-informing
  biomarkers by clinical testing had the highest fusion and SV burden in the cohort.
  H135022 achieved complete response after 3 cycles of
  [nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md) + [ipilimumab](../drugs/ipilimumab.md), remaining
  disease-free 26 months after therapy cessation; H135462 achieved complete response
  after 6 cycles of [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md) and was disease-free at
  10 months.
- **Chromothripsis and whole-genome duplication:** Chromothripsis was detected in 40%
  of patients, most often in sarcomas (35/58), germ cell tumors (2/4), and
  neuroblastoma (6/29). WGD was seen in 42/114 patients, enriched in sarcoma (24/54),
  carcinoma (3/7), and neuroblastoma (11/30).
- **cfDNA proof-of-concept:** WGS from matched FF + plasma cfDNA in 7 patients yielded
  cfDNA genome-wide coverage of 94–102× with tumor content ranging ~10–83%. For
  patient IH158182 (high ctDNA content), the cfDNA WGS profile recapitulated the FF
  tumor's genome-wide mutation landscape; for H135967 (ctDNA ~20%), all known oncogenic
  events including [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) substitution, [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) and
  [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md) amplifications, and SVs targeting
  [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) and [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) were detected.

## Genes & alterations

- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)** — Among 29 osteosarcoma patients, [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) SNV/indel
  mutations were identified in 12 and noncoding SVs targeting the [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) locus in 13
  (only 3 of those SVs were called by MSK-IMPACT). RNA-seq integration confirmed that
  TP53 SVs correlated with loss of TP53 expression (two-sided Mann–Whitney U,
  p=1.645e-03). Without cWGTS, these patients would be erroneously classified as TP53
  wild type, with implications for p53-pathway-modulating drug trials.
- **[TERT](../genes/TERT.md)** and **[ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md)** — SVs detected in 8
  (28%) and 5 (17%) of neuroblastoma patients, respectively, with the [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) SVs
  identifiable only by cWGTS and only ¼ of [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) deletions reported by MSK-IMPACT. Both
  are therapy-defining risk-stratification biomarkers for neuroblastoma. [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) was also
  observed in 8 osteosarcomas, with associations to telomere length.
- **[DLG2](../genes/DLG2.md)** — Recurrent SVs in 15/29 osteosarcoma patients and 3/29
  neuroblastoma patients, including 6 homozygous deletions. The authors propose [DLG2](../genes/DLG2.md)
  SV recurrence in neuroblastoma as a novel observation warranting follow-up.
- **[MYB](../genes/MYB.md)–[NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) enhancer hijacking** — A
  chromoplexy event involving chromosomes 6, 9, and 18 localized an [NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) enhancer to
  the [MYB](../genes/MYB.md) locus in an [adenoid cystic carcinoma](../cancer_types/ACYC.md) patient
  (H133676) without informative clinical sequencing findings; RNA-seq confirmed [MYB](../genes/MYB.md)
  overexpression. MYB fusions are detected by conventional assays in only 30% of [ACC](../cancer_types/ACC.md)
  cases.
- **[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)** — Concomitant amplification and overexpression in two
  patients (H135462 and H195916) without clinical biomarkers by panel testing; the
  amplification was not detected by MSK-IMPACT, pointing to lower copy-number
  sensitivity of panel assays.
- **[KBTBD4](../genes/KBTBD4.md) p.R313_M314insPRR** — Disease-defining mutation in a
  pineal parenchymal tumor of intermediate differentiation ([PPTID](../cancer_types/PPTID.md)).
- **[SETBP1](../genes/SETBP1.md) p.D868N** — Somatic mutation identified in a germ cell
  tumor.
- **[SIX1](../genes/SIX1.md) p.Q177R** — Mutation in a Wilms tumor.
- **[SBDS](../genes/SBDS.md)** splice-site c.258+2T>C — Germline finding in a
  rhabdomyosarcoma.
- **[BARD1](../genes/BARD1.md) p.E652fs*69** — Germline in a neuroblastoma.
- **[EP300](../genes/EP300.md) p.A2259fs*20** and **[EXT2](../genes/EXT2.md) p.W414\*** —
  Germline variants in two osteosarcoma patients.
- **[MUTYH](../genes/MUTYH.md)** — Pathogenic germline variant in neuroblastoma patient
  H135421 (c.924+3A>C) with somatic LOH; 42% of the tumor's mutations were attributed
  to the SBS36 [MUTYH](../genes/MUTYH.md) signature.
- **[PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md)** — Pathogenic germline c.538-1G>C in immature teratoma
  patient H135466 with LOH; tumor was MSI-high (TMB=11.23, 90,246 indels, 17,840 SNVs,
  44 SVs) with PMS2-associated repeat-mediated indels and SBS26 enrichment. A separate
  osteosarcoma patient (H201472) with a likely-pathogenic [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md) p.D699H was identified
  through cWGTS-driven recognition of an MSI-high hypermutated genome (TMB=16.7,
  31,520 SNVs, 89,588 indels, 568 SVs), prompting germline testing — MSK-IMPACT had
  reported only an indeterminate MSI score (7.5).
- **Fusion genes (8 novel/incremental, RNA + WGS support):**
  - **[PAX3](../genes/PAX3.md)–[FOXO3](../genes/FOXO3.md)** — t(2;6) translocation
    reclassifying an [ERMS](../cancer_types/ERMS.md) patient as
    [ARMS](../cancer_types/ARMS.md) (H134768); methylation tSNE clustered the patient
    with [ARMS](../cancer_types/ARMS.md) samples.
  - **[UACA](../genes/UACA.md)–[LTK](../genes/LTK.md)** — Metastatic papillary thyroid
    carcinoma ([THPA](../cancer_types/THPA.md)).
  - **[SH3PXD2A](../genes/SH3PXD2A.md)–[HTRA1](../genes/HTRA1.md)** — Pathognomonic
    fusion establishing a schwannoma ([SCHW](../cancer_types/SCHW.md)) diagnosis in a
    patient evaluated for relapsed stage-IV neuroblastoma.
  - **[NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md)–[SLMAP](../genes/SLMAP.md)** — Activating [NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md) fusion
    in a neuroblastoma patient, a therapeutic target for TRK inhibitors.
  - **[EPC2](../genes/EPC2.md)–[AFF3](../genes/AFF3.md)** and
    **[MAN1A2](../genes/MAN1A2.md)–[ACBD6](../genes/ACBD6.md)** — Undescribed fusions
    in two undifferentiated sarcoma patients.
  - **[CITED2](../genes/CITED2.md)–[MGA](../genes/MGA.md)** — Undescribed fusion in
    a round-cell sarcoma NOS.
- **[SS18](../genes/SS18.md)–[SSX1](../genes/SSX1.md)/[SSX2](../genes/SSX2.md)** —
  cWGTS reclassified an MSK-Fusion call of SS18-SSX1 as SS18-SSX2 (supported by
  spanning reads in WGS) in a synovial sarcoma case.
- **Other amplifications driven by chromothripsis:** Recurrent
  [MYC](../genes/MYC.md), [VEGFA](../genes/VEGFA.md), and [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md)
  amplifications co-occurring with chromothripsis-mediated TP53 loss (10/29).

## Clinical implications

- **One integrated test can replace multiple targeted assays** in pediatric / rare
  cancer care: cWGTS captures the same clinically reported variants as
  [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md), [MSK-Fusion](../methods/msk-fusion.md),
  and 88-gene germline panels with comparable sensitivity at 5–10× lower depth.
- **Operational benchmarks for clinical adoption:** ≥80× WGS depth, ≥20% tumor purity,
  and 9-day turnaround are achievable in a comprehensive cancer center with dedicated
  laboratory, computational, and tumor-board infrastructure.
- **Diagnostic reclassifications** — cWGTS-detected fusions (PAX3-FOXO3, SH3PXD2A-HTRA1)
  changed final histopathologic diagnosis with direct treatment implications.
- **Therapy-informing additions** — [NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md) fusions enabling TRK inhibitor consideration;
  high fusion/SV burden identifying immunotherapy-responsive carcinoma patients (CR on
  [nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md)+[ipilimumab](../drugs/ipilimumab.md) and on
  [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md)); [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) amplification missed by panel
  testing.
- **Prognostic risk stratification** — [TERT](../genes/TERT.md) and ATRX SV detection in neuroblastoma,
  detectable only by WGS, refines risk groups currently estimated from panel data.
- **Germline reclassification via signatures** — integrating somatic mutation
  signatures (SBS36 for [MUTYH](../genes/MUTYH.md), SBS26 for
  [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md)) with germline variant calls disambiguates VUS from
  pathogenic variants and triggers cascade family screening.
- **TP53 SVs as a missed inclusion criterion** — Wild-type TP53 is an enrollment
  criterion for p53-modulating drug trials; osteosarcoma patients with TP53 SVs would
  be erroneously classified as TP53 wild type by panel sequencing, with implications
  for trial eligibility and treatment selection.
- **Treatment-related signatures** — TMB was higher in therapy-exposed than
  treatment-naive samples (Mann–Whitney p=1.892e-04) and correlated with platinum and
  [temozolomide](../drugs/temozolomide.md) signatures in 45/114 patients, evidence of
  persistent clones surviving prior therapy.
- **Liquid biopsy potential** — cfDNA WGS offers a tumor-agnostic alternative for
  patients without adequate FF biopsy, recapitulating the full cancer genomic
  landscape when ctDNA content is sufficient.

## Limitations & open questions

- **FF biopsy requirement** — The validated workflow depends on fresh-frozen tumor
  with ≥20% purity, restricting access for patients with only FFPE or insufficient
  biopsy material; WGS in FFPE retains a high genome-wide error rate.
- **Cohort size and disease mix** — 114 patients across many disease entities limits
  per-disease statistical power; reported per-tumor-type frequencies (e.g., [DLG2](../genes/DLG2.md) SVs
  in neuroblastoma) are hypothesis-generating and warrant validation in larger
  pediatric/rare-cancer cohorts.
- **SV / fusion burden as an immunotherapy biomarker** — Two responding carcinoma
  patients are anecdotal; the authors explicitly call out the need for systematic
  evaluation of SV and fusion burden as biomarkers of immune-checkpoint blockade
  response.
- **Expression biomarkers** — Only 8% of aberrant expression biomarkers were
  associated with an acquired SV or fusion, leaving the clinical utility of
  expression-only biomarkers largely unvalidated in this design.
- **cfDNA WGS validation** — n=7 proof-of-concept paired samples; analytical
  validation, ctDNA-content thresholds, and tumor-agnostic variant prioritization
  pipelines for cfDNA WGS remain to be established at scale.
- **Cost and infrastructure** — Sub-2-week turnaround and ≥80× WGS depth at scale
  require dedicated bioinformatics infrastructure (Isabl platform, reserved HPC
  nodes, biweekly molecular tumor board), which may not be transferable to community
  practice without further automation.
