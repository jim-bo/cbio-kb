# NEK2

## Overview

NEK2 (NIMA-related kinase 2) is a serine/threonine kinase involved in centrosome separation, mitotic spindle assembly, and cell-cycle progression. Its kinase activity is elevated in several cancer types and has been proposed as a therapeutic target, particularly in contexts of aberrant mitotic checkpoint signaling.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Elevated NEK2 kinase activity (by KSEA — kinase-substrate enrichment analysis) in TP53-mutant HCCs compared to non-TP53-mutant tumors; identified as part of a broader pro-mitotic kinase signature (alongside CDK1/2/5, [AURKA](../genes/AURKA.md), [TTK](../genes/TTK.md), [EIF2AK2](../genes/EIF2AK2.md), [PRKD2](../genes/PRKD2.md), [MAPK13](../genes/MAPK13.md)) in a European [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) proteogenomics cohort (n=122 WES, n=51 with phosphoproteome) [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md): Elevated NEK2 kinase activity in TP53-mutant tumors [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- NEK2 activity co-elevated with [CDK1](../genes/CDK1.md), [CDK2](../genes/CDK2.md), [CDK5](../genes/CDK5.md), [AURKA](../genes/AURKA.md), [TTK](../genes/TTK.md), [EIF2AK2](../genes/EIF2AK2.md), [PRKD2](../genes/PRKD2.md), and [MAPK13](../genes/MAPK13.md) in TP53-mutant HCCs by KSEA [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Flagged alongside [AURKA](../genes/AURKA.md) and CDK1/2/5 as a targetable kinase in [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md); preclinical evidence of synergy with sorafenib/regorafenib and chemotherapeutic agents noted [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Open questions

- Whether NEK2 kinase-activity elevation is a consequence of [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) loss-of-function or a parallel oncogenic event remains to be mechanistically dissected.
