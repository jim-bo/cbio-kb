# etomoxir

## Overview

Etomoxir is an irreversible inhibitor of carnitine palmitoyl transferase 1 (CPT1, encoded by [CPT1A](../genes/CPT1A.md)), the rate-limiting enzyme for mitochondrial long-chain fatty acid import and fatty acid oxidation (FAO). It is an investigational compound used primarily in preclinical models; clinical use in cancer has not been established.

## Evidence in the corpus

- A narrative review of lipid metabolism in [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) identifies etomoxir (CPT1 inhibitor) as a tool compound to investigate FAO dependence in HCC: in Wnt/β-catenin–driven (APC-loss or CTNNB1-mutant) HCC, FAO is required via PPARα/CPT1 activation, and etomoxir impairs tumor development in this context. Conversely, p53-mutation–driven and DEN+high-fat-diet HCC require FAO suppression — etiology and genetic background dictate FAO dependence and thus sensitivity to etomoxir. [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Resistance mechanisms

- Etiology-dependent: CTNNB1-mutant / APC-loss HCC is FAO-addicted and sensitive; p53-mutant or DEN+HFD HCC may require FAO suppression, predicting intrinsic resistance (or paradoxical harm). Predictive biomarkers for FAO-targeting agents are lacking. [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) — hepatocellular carcinoma; FAO dependence is subtype-specific; etomoxir impairs Wnt/β-catenin–driven HCC growth preclinically.
