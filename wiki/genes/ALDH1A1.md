# ALDH1A1

## Overview

ALDH1A1 (aldehyde dehydrogenase 1 family member A1) is a widely used marker of cancer stem-like cells (CSCs). Its expression is associated with EMT, tumor initiating capacity, and poor prognosis in multiple cancer types.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- EMT/stemness marker upregulated in [GBC](../cancer_types/GBC.md) cells (GBC-SD, NOZ) in response to paracrine [SEMA7A](../genes/SEMA7A.md) from stiff-matrix-activated cancer-associated fibroblasts; downregulated by [SEMA7A](../genes/SEMA7A.md) knockdown in co-injection xenografts [PMID:24997986](../papers/24997986.md)
- Stemness marker derepressed upon [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) loss in intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma; [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) represses ALDH1A1 transcription by recruiting HDAC1 and reducing H3K27 acetylation at its promoter [PMID:25526346](../papers/25526346.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [GBC](../cancer_types/GBC.md): ALDH1A1 upregulation downstream of SEMA7A/ITGB1/AKT1/EP300 signaling axis reflects acquisition of stem-like traits in [GBC](../cancer_types/GBC.md) cells exposed to CAF-conditioned medium [PMID:24997986](../papers/24997986.md)
