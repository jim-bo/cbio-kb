**PMID:** [32864625](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32864625/) · **DOI:** 10.1038/s43018-020-0047-1 · **Journal:** Nature Cancer (2020)

## TL;DR

A single-institution phase I/II dose-escalation trial (NCT01870505) of the PI3Kα-selective inhibitor [alpelisib](../drugs/alpelisib.md) combined with an aromatase inhibitor ([letrozole](../drugs/letrozole.md) or [exemestane](../drugs/exemestane.md)) enrolled 51 patients with hormone-receptor-positive (HR+) metastatic breast cancer ([MBC](../cancer_types/MBC.md)). Among 44 evaluable patients the clinical benefit rate was 52% and maculopapular rash was the dominant grade 3 toxicity (33%). Through paired pre/post-treatment tumor and ctDNA sequencing on the [breast_alpelisib_2020](../datasets/breast_alpelisib_2020.md) cohort, the authors identified loss-of-function [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) alterations in 25% of patients with resistance and showed that activating [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) mutations expand in allele fraction under therapy, establishing [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) loss and [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) mutations as recurrent mechanisms of resistance to [alpelisib](../drugs/alpelisib.md) + AI.

## Cohort & data

- 51 patients enrolled June 2013 – September 2015 across four arms (Arm A: n=7 continuous alpelisib + [letrozole](../drugs/letrozole.md); Arm B: n=7 continuous alpelisib + [exemestane](../drugs/exemestane.md); Arm C: n=12 intermittent 7-on/7-off alpelisib + letrozole; Arm D: n=25 intermittent 5-on/2-off alpelisib + exemestane). Data lock April 30, 2018.
- Cohort: median age 58 (range 28–83); all HR+, HER2 non-amplified [MBC](../cancer_types/MBC.md) ([BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md)); 69% visceral disease, 31% bone-only; 88% with ≥1 prior line endocrine therapy for metastatic disease, 51% with ≥1 prior line chemotherapy.
- Dataset: [breast_alpelisib_2020](../datasets/breast_alpelisib_2020.md) — assembled prospective somatic mutational data from ctDNA and tumors deposited in cBioPortal.
- Tissue sequencing: [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) NGS on n=39 baseline tumors; Foundation Medicine NGS on n=2; [Sequenom](../methods/sequenom-genotyping.md) mass-spectrometry hotspot panel ([AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md), [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md), [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)) on n=9 with insufficient tissue.
- ctDNA: 73-gene targeted [Guardant360](../methods/guardant360.md) cfDNA panel (CLIA/CAP) on 90 pre-/on-/post-treatment plasma samples from 47 patients, including 32 paired pre/post pairs.
- Orthogonal validation: [ddPCR](../methods/droplet-digital-pcr.md) on PIK3CA hotspots (H1047R/L/Y, E545K, E542K, C420R, N345K) in all plasma specimens.
- Analysis tooling: [FACETS](../methods/facets.md) for allele-specific copy number and CCF; [deconstructSigs](../methods/deconstructsigs.md) for APOBEC and other mutational signatures.
- Clinical trial: NCT01870505 (single-institution, investigator-initiated, MSKCC); reference genome hg19 for ctDNA.

## Key findings

- Among 44 evaluable patients, clinical benefit rate (CR + PR + SD ≥16 weeks) = **52% (23/44, 95% CI 37–68%)**; overall response rate among 31 with measurable disease = **19% (6/31, 95% CI 7–37%)**, with 5 partial responses (Arm A: 1; Arm D: 4) and 1 complete response (Arm D).
- Median time to treatment failure across all 51 patients = **14 weeks** (range 2–169); by arm A/B/C/D = 21/8/12/17 weeks.
- Clinical benefit was confined to [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)-mutant tumors: **CBR 57.5% (23/40) in PIK3CA-mutant vs 0/6 in PIK3CA-WT** (p = 0.025). PIK3CA mutation distribution followed the canonical HR+ pattern with hotspots in kinase (H1047R/L/Y) and helical (E545K/A, E542K) domains; 70% (31/39) of PIK3CA mutations in 32 evaluable pre-treatment tumors were clonal.
- Baseline activating [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) mutations (D538G n=3; Y537S n=3) were present in 6 patients — **all of whom had no clinical benefit** — and absent in benefit-derivers (p = 0.0067).
- Two patients with concurrent clonal activating PIK3CA mutations and [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) loss (#032, #060) both had progressive disease at first radiographic evaluation (week 8), despite the canonical mutual exclusivity of PIK3CA/PTEN in primary breast cancer.
- ctDNA analysis of paired pre/post samples (n=32) identified **PTEN alterations in 25% (8/32)** of patients — 3 with pre-treatment loss-of-function alterations (A126S, R130*, and a homozygous deletion also confirmed in tumor) who progressed rapidly, plus 5 with post-treatment-emergent PTEN LOF (two truncating, four LOF missense, one homozygous deletion) absent from baseline ctDNA/tumor.
- Significant post-treatment enrichment of hotspot activating ESR1 mutation VAFs across the cohort; combined PTEN loss + ESR1 mutations were present in **15/32 (~47%)** of paired-ctDNA patients and associated with progression.
- Normalized ΔVAF analysis (using clonal PIK3CA VAF as a disease-burden proxy) revealed disproportionate expansion under therapy of pathogenic alterations in the PI3K-downstream pathway ([AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md), [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md)) and the MAPK pathway ([ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md)).
- 3/4 patients with acquired PTEN mutations had a hypermutator phenotype, with two showing an APOBEC mutational signature (signatures 2 and 13 via deconstructSigs).
- Engineered ER+ breast cancer xenografts with CRISPR/Cas9 knock-in [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) Y537S progressed more rapidly than ESR1-WT counterparts under alpelisib + estrogen deprivation, providing functional evidence that AI-resistant ESR1 mutations contribute to combination-therapy failure.

## Genes & alterations

- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — activating hotspot mutations (H1047R/L/Y kinase domain; E545K/A and E542K helical domain) in 88% (44/50) of baseline-genotyped patients; a required-in-practice (though not protocol-mandated) eligibility feature. PIK3CA-WT tumors derived no clinical benefit (0/6).
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — loss-of-function alterations in 25% (8/32) of paired-ctDNA patients, including A126S, R130*, multiple LOF missense, two truncating, and homozygous deletions. Three were pre-existing (intrinsic resistance); five emerged on therapy (acquired resistance). Concurrent PIK3CA + PTEN loss disrupted the canonical mutual exclusivity seen in primary breast cancer and was associated with intrinsic resistance.
- [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) — activating mutations D538G and Y537S enriched at baseline in non-responders (6/6 baseline ESR1-mutant patients had no clinical benefit; p = 0.0067); VAFs expanded significantly under therapy; CRISPR knock-in Y537S xenografts validated a functional role in driving combination resistance.
- [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md), [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md) — pathogenic alterations downstream of PI3K showed disproportionate VAF expansion under therapy.
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md) — MAPK-pathway alterations selectively expanded in post-treatment ctDNA, suggesting parallel/bypass resistance pathways.

## Clinical implications

- Establishes that resistance to [alpelisib](../drugs/alpelisib.md) + AI in HR+ [MBC](../cancer_types/MBC.md) is a **convergent phenotype** arising from alterations in either component's target pathway (PI3K — PTEN, AKT1, [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md); ER — ESR1).
- Argues for **comprehensive baseline genomic profiling beyond PIK3CA status** prior to alpelisib-based therapy, because pre-existing PTEN loss or ESR1 mutations may identify patients unlikely to benefit.
- Suggests that patients with baseline ESR1 mutations be considered for trials of oral SERDs, and patients with baseline PTEN loss for AKT inhibitor trials, rather than alpelisib + AI.
- Provides genomic context for the SOLAR-1 phase III trial of alpelisib + [fulvestrant](../drugs/fulvestrant.md), where pervasive resistance was observed despite PIK3CA selection.
- Intermittent dosing schedules (Arms C/D) had numerically lower all-grade and grade-3 rash than continuous dosing (Arms A/B), supporting MTD = alpelisib 350 mg 5-on/2-off + daily exemestane in this study; no grade 4 toxicity or drug-attributable deaths.
- [ctDNA](../methods/guardant360.md) profiling captured resistance alleles invisible to single-site needle biopsy, including post-treatment-emergent PTEN LOF, supporting longitudinal liquid biopsy as a biomarker strategy in PI3Kα-inhibitor trials.

## Limitations & open questions

- Small phase I/II study population (n=51 enrolled, 44 evaluable, 32 with paired ctDNA); no aromatase-inhibitor monotherapy comparator arm.
- Genomic mechanisms of resistance were not exhaustively characterized — gene expression changes were not analyzed; the assays employed could not fully resolve mutational signatures at the per-patient level.
- Paired pre/post tumor biopsies were limited, so most resistance-genomics inference relies on ctDNA — leaving open whether some "acquired" alterations were undetectable subclonal events at baseline rather than truly new events.
- The relative contribution of APOBEC-driven hypermutation to PTEN-loss acquisition needs validation in larger cohorts.
- Whether oral SERDs combined with alpelisib will overcome ESR1-mutation-mediated resistance, and whether AKT inhibitors are effective in the PTEN-loss subset, remain open clinical questions raised but not answered by this trial.
- Several toxicity-arm comparisons (continuous vs intermittent rash rates) were post hoc and not powered for formal comparison.
