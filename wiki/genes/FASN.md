# FASN

## Overview

FASN (fatty acid synthase) is the rate-limiting enzyme in de novo lipogenesis (DNL), catalyzing synthesis of palmitate from malonyl-CoA. In [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) and many other cancers, FASN is upregulated and its inhibition blocks tumor formation in animal models.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- FASN upregulated in HCC as a DNL effector; FASN loss blocks HCC formation in animal models; part of the DNL axis transcriptionally driven by SREBP1 and [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) — FASN upregulation drives DNL and palmitate synthesis; FASN inhibition blocks HCC formation in models [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Part of the FASN/SCD/ACACA DNL effector axis downstream of SREBF1/MYC in HCC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- FASN inhibitors are proposed as a strategy to suppress DNL and resensitize HCC to TKIs and ICIs [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Open questions

- Clinical FASN inhibitors (e.g., TVB-2640) have been in clinical trials; HCC-specific data from the corpus are preclinical.
