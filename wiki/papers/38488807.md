# Developing Novel Genomic Risk Stratification Models in Soft Tissue and Uterine Leiomyosarcoma

**PMID:** [38488807](../papers/38488807.md) · **DOI:** 10.1158/1078-0432.CCR-24-0148 · **Journal:** Clinical Cancer Research (2024)

## TL;DR

This study developed three-tier genomic risk stratification models for soft tissue leiomyosarcoma (STLMS) and uterine leiomyosarcoma ([ULMS](../cancer_types/ULMS.md)) using MSK-IMPACT targeted sequencing data from 195 STLMS and 238 ULMS cases at MSKCC. In STLMS, co-occurrence of [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) mutation with chr12q deletion or [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) mutation defined a high-risk group with significantly worse disease-specific survival (DSS), outperforming traditional FNCLCC grading. In ULMS, concurrent [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation with chr20q amplification or ATRX mutation defined high risk. The STLMS model was externally validated in the AACR GENIE cohort (317 STLMS cases). Longitudinal sequencing confirmed that most driver alterations are early clonal events persisting through disease progression.

## Cohort & data

- **STLMS cohort:** 195 cases from MSKCC (151 primary at presentation, 44 metastatic), profiled by [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) (341-505 genes), with at least one-year follow-up. Median age 58 years; 64% female; 74% retroperitoneal/intraabdominal/pelvic ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- **ULMS cohort:** 238 cases from MSKCC (177 primary at presentation, 61 metastatic), profiled by [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md), with at least one-year follow-up. Median age 54 years ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- **External validation:** 317 STLMS cases from AACR GENIE consortium (128 metastatic at presentation, 40%), using multiple tumor-only targeted panels from different institutions ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- **Longitudinal subset:** 18 STLMS and 15 ULMS patients with sequential tumors sequenced ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- **Dataset:** [lms_msk_2024](../datasets/lms_msk_2024.md) (cBioPortal public)

## Key findings

- **STLMS genomic landscape:** [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations (56%), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) deletions (12%), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) mutations (26%), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) deletions (24%), [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) mutations (10%), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) deletions (12%), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) deletions (2.6%) ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- **ULMS genomic landscape:** [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations (55%), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) deletions (16%), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) mutations (13%), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) deletions (39%), [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) mutations (32%), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) deletions (18%), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) deletions (10%), [MED12](../genes/MED12.md) mutations (15%) ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- **STLMS 3-tier genomic risk for DSS (MSKCC, primary, n=151):** high risk (co-occurrence of [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) mutation + chr12q deletion or [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) mutation, n=10, median DSS 75 months); intermediate risk ([RB1](../genes/RB1.md) mutation, [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) mutation, or del12q, n=60, median DSS 101 months); low risk (none, n=81, median DSS 158 months; global log-rank P=0.04) ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- **STLMS entire cohort (n=195):** genomic risk stratification remained significant for DSS (global log-rank P=0.00057); high risk: 5-year DSS 43%; intermediate: 64%; low: 79% ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- **STLMS external validation (AACR GENIE, n=317):** simplified model using [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) alterations + [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) mutation was significant for [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) (global log-rank P=0.00013); high risk (n=36, median OS 23 months, 5-year OS 10%); intermediate (n=114, median OS 29 months, 5-year OS 21%); low (n=167, median OS 44 months, 5-year OS 43%) ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- **ULMS 3-tier genomic risk for DSS (primary, n=177):** high risk (concurrent [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation + [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) mutation or chr20q amplification, n=50, median DSS 52 months); intermediate ([TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation, [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) mutation, or amp20q, n=66, median DSS 92 months); low (none, n=61, median DSS 157 months; global log-rank P=0.00012) ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- **ULMS entire cohort (n=238):** genomic risk for DSS remained significant (global log-rank P=0.00011) ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- **Traditional vs genomic risk in STLMS:** FNCLCC grading weakly predicted DSS (P=0.047) but tumor size did not (P=0.47). On multivariate Cox regression, only genomic high-risk group remained significant for DSS ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- **Traditional vs genomic risk in ULMS:** tumor size, mitotic rate, and necrosis were all associated with worse DSS and PFS. On multivariate analysis, only tumor size and genomic high-risk group remained significant for DSS ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- **Mutual exclusivity:** [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) mutations and [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) deletions were mutually exclusive (P<0.01) in both STLMS and ULMS, as were [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) deletions (P<0.01) ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- **Co-occurrence patterns:** In STLMS, [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations co-occurred (P<0.01). In ULMS, [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) mutations co-occurred (P<0.05), and [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) deletions with [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) deletions co-occurred (P<0.01) ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- **TMB:** Median TMB was 2 mt/Mb (STLMS) and 3 mt/Mb (ULMS), with 99% and 98% of cases <10 mt/Mb respectively ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- **Longitudinal sequencing:** Most molecular alterations were early clonal events that persisted during progression; a subset of cases acquired [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) loss or [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) mutations later ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md): Mutations (56% STLMS, 55% ULMS) and deletions (12% STLMS, 16% ULMS); point mutations (not deletions) were prognostically significant in ULMS; truncal clonal event ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- [RB1](../genes/RB1.md): Mutations (26% STLMS, 13% ULMS) and deletions (24% STLMS, 39% ULMS); inactivating mutations (not deletions) predicted worse DSS in STLMS; key component of STLMS genomic risk model ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md): Mutations (10% STLMS, 32% ULMS); prognostically significant in both STLMS and ULMS risk models; contributes to high-risk classification when co-occurring with RB1 mutations (STLMS) or TP53 mutations (ULMS) ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md): Deletions/mutations (12% STLMS, 18% ULMS); [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) deletions co-occurred with RB1 deletions in ULMS; associated with worse PFS in STLMS ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md): Deletions more common in ULMS (10%) than STLMS (2.6%) ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- [MED12](../genes/MED12.md): Recurrent mutations in ULMS (15%), infrequent in STLMS (1%) ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).

## Clinical implications

- Genomic risk stratification using clinically available targeted NGS data (RB1, ATRX, chr12q status for STLMS; TP53, ATRX, chr20q status for ULMS) provides superior or comparable prognostic information relative to traditional FNCLCC grading and tumor size ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- IHC for TP53, RB1, and ATRX may serve as surrogate biomarkers for genomic alterations, potentially enabling risk stratification in settings without NGS access ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- The simplified model (RB1 + ATRX alterations only) validated in the AACR GENIE cohort suggests feasibility across different NGS platforms and institutions ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- Risk stratification could inform decisions on neoadjuvant or adjuvant therapy for [LMS](../cancer_types/LMS.md) patients ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).

## Limitations & open questions

- The MSKCC STLMS cohort was biased toward higher-grade tumors referred for NGS, which may explain why tumor size was not prognostic in this cohort ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- The AACR GENIE validation cohort used tumor-only panels from multiple institutions with variable probe coverage, and lacked chromosomal arm-level CNV data, limiting direct comparison ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- OS in the AACR GENIE cohort was calculated from time of sequencing rather than diagnosis, introducing potential lead-time bias ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- Insufficient ULMS cases with follow-up in AACR GENIE precluded external validation of the ULMS risk model ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- Why RB1 point mutations but not deletions are prognostically significant requires further functional investigation; gain-of-function/dominant-negative effects of mutant TP53 may similarly explain why mutations but not deletions predict outcome in ULMS ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
- No therapeutic interventions were evaluated; future work needed to determine if risk-stratified patients benefit from different treatment approaches ([PMID:38488807](../papers/38488807.md)).
