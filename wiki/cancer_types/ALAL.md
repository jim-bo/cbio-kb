# Acute Leukemias of Ambiguous Lineage (ALAL)

## Overview

Acute leukemias of ambiguous lineage (ALAL) are a heterogeneous group of leukemias that lack clear myeloid or lymphoid lineage commitment. In OncoTree, ALAL sits under the Mixed/Not Otherwise Specified (MNM) grouping. This category encompasses mixed-phenotype acute leukemia (MPAL) and other ambiguous-lineage presentations occurring in both pediatric and adult patients.

## Cohorts in the corpus

- [pptc_2019](../datasets/pptc_2019.md) — Pediatric Preclinical Testing Consortium PDX cohort (261 models, 37 pediatric cancer subtypes); ALAL models included as a rare histology group [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md).

## Recurrent alterations

- ALAL PDX models included among rare histology groups in the PPTC cohort (n=261 total across 37 pediatric cancer molecular subtypes); specific ALAL genomic landscape not detailed in this study beyond cohort inclusion [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md).

## Subtypes

- Mixed-phenotype acute leukemia (MPAL) — co-expression of myeloid and lymphoid markers.
- Undifferentiated acute leukemia — no lineage-specific markers detectable.
- Acute leukemia with ambiguous lineage by other criteria.

## Therapeutic landscape

- No targeted therapies specifically for ALAL are documented in the current corpus. Regimens often depend on dominant lineage features at presentation.
