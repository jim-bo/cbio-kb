# HSPA4L

## Overview

HSPA4L encodes a member of the HSP110 (heat shock protein 110) family, a subset of large heat-shock proteins involved in protein folding and disaggregation. It is downregulated in microsatellite-instable (MSI) cancer cell lines, placing it within the broader proteostasis network disrupted by mismatch-repair deficiency.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- HSPA4L is a heat-shock protein downregulated in MSI cancer cell lines (CCLE pan-cancer proteomics, n=375 lines); grouped with [DNAJB14](../genes/DNAJB14.md) and [HSPH1](../genes/HSPH1.md) as HSP-family members whose expression is reduced under MSI conditions [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- No specific cancer type associations beyond MSI context; observed across multiple solid-organ MSI cell lines in the CCLE.

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-downregulated with HSPH1 and DNAJB14 in MSI cell lines; HSPH1 (HSP110) has an established disease role in MSI cancers [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Potential involvement in the proteostasis vulnerabilities of MSI tumors; relationship to [WRN](../genes/WRN.md) synthetic lethality in MSI not directly established.

## Open questions

- Whether HSPA4L downregulation in MSI is a cause or consequence of mismatch-repair deficiency is not established. Functional role relative to HSPH1 in MSI disease biology requires further study.
