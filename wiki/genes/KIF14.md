# KIF14

## Overview

KIF14 encodes a kinesin family member involved in mitotic spindle assembly and cytokinesis. It maps to chromosome 1q31, a region frequently gained in retinoblastoma. KIF14 has been discussed as a candidate driver gene in retinoblastoma, but its role remains unconfirmed.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- KIF14 discussed as a prior-literature candidate driver gene for retinoblastoma (maps to 1q31, a region of frequent 1q gain); not recurrently mutated at the coding level in an MSK-IMPACT retinoblastoma cohort (n=83 enucleated specimens) — classified as a driver candidate discussed but not supported by this dataset [PMID:33466343](../papers/33466343.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **Retinoblastoma ([RBL](../cancer_types/RBL.md)):** KIF14 was evaluated as a candidate gene in this MSK-IMPACT cohort; no coding mutations were identified; its potential driver role rests on 1q gain patterns rather than sequence-level evidence from this study [PMID:33466343](../papers/33466343.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- 1q gain (the chromosomal region containing KIF14) independently associates with vitreous seeding in retinoblastoma (the principal indication for enucleation) and does not statistically co-occur with 16q LOH (p=0.37) [PMID:33466343](../papers/33466343.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic implications reported for KIF14 in retinoblastoma [PMID:33466343](../papers/33466343.md).

## Open questions

- Whether KIF14 is a true driver at the 1q gain locus or a passenger amplified by chromosomal instability remains unresolved; no coding KIF14 mutations were identified in this cohort [PMID:33466343](../papers/33466343.md).
