# RFC4

## Overview

RFC4 encodes replication factor C subunit 4, a component of the RFC clamp-loader complex essential for DNA replication and repair. Mutations in RFC4 have been associated with hypermutation phenotypes, as it participates in the DNA replication machinery whose dysfunction can drive mutational signatures associated with defective proofreading.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- RFC4 missense variant co-occurring with a [POLD1](../genes/POLD1.md) frameshift in patient P58, a hypermutator prostate cancer brain metastasis (PCBM) with high SBS10b signature; together implicated in replication-error-driven hypermutation [PMID:35504881](../papers/35504881.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md)**: RFC4 missense in a hypermutator PCBM sample co-altered with [POLD1](../genes/POLD1.md) frameshift; SBS10b signature enriched, consistent with replication proofreading defect [PMID:35504881](../papers/35504881.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-altered with [POLD1](../genes/POLD1.md) frameshift in a hypermutator prostate cancer brain metastasis (patient P58) [PMID:35504881](../papers/35504881.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Hypermutation due to replication fidelity defects (POLD1/RFC4) may confer immune checkpoint inhibitor eligibility; no direct therapy trial data in this cohort [PMID:35504881](../papers/35504881.md)

## Open questions

- Whether RFC4 missense alone is sufficient to drive the hypermutation phenotype or acts as a modifier to the [POLD1](../genes/POLD1.md) frameshift is not established.
