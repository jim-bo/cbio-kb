# RPL22L1

## Overview

RPL22L1 (Ribosomal Protein L22 Like 1) is a paralogue of [RPL22](../genes/RPL22.md), encoding a ribosomal large subunit protein. In MSI cancer cell lines, frameshift mutations in RPL22 (K15fs, present in ~68% of MSI lines) elevate RPL22L1 expression; elevated RPL22L1 in turn promotes [MDM4](../genes/MDM4.md) exon-6 inclusion (MDM4-FL), increasing MDM4 protein levels and sensitizing [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) wild-type cells to [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) inhibitors. RPL22L1 over-expression itself marks RPL22L1 dependency.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- RPL22 K15fs frameshift (68% of MSI cancer cell lines) leads to elevated RPL22L1 expression, which promotes MDM4 exon-6 inclusion and MDM4 dependence; RPL22L1 over-expression marks RPL22L1 dependency; identified in CCLE multi-omic profiling integrated with Project Achilles and DRIVE dependency screens [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)
- Derepressed upon RPL22 loss; downregulated in MSI CCLE lines; sensitivity to RPL22L1 knockdown is best predicted by SKIC2 protein abundance, suggesting an association with the SKI complex/ribosome rather than MSI causation per se [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- RPL22L1 protein upregulated in MSI endometrial tumors, especially in RPL22-mutant tumors (p = 0.002), supporting a synthetic-lethal relationship with RPL22 [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- MSI tumors (pan-cancer cell lines): RPL22L1 elevation is a downstream consequence of RPL22 frameshift in MSI lines; marks MDM4-FL status and MDM2-inhibitor sensitivity [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Elevated RPL22L1 is mechanistically downstream of RPL22 K15fs frameshift mutation; correlates with MDM4 exon-6 inclusion (MDM4-FL) and sensitivity to [nutlin-3a](../drugs/nutlin-3a.md) class MDM2 inhibitors in TP53 wild-type backgrounds [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- RPL22L1 over-expression is proposed as a biomarker for MDM2 inhibitor sensitivity (nutlin-3a class) in TP53 wild-type cancer cell lines, beyond MDM4 splice variant status alone [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)

## Open questions

- Whether RPL22L1 elevation in primary tumors translates to the same MDM4-FL / MDM2-inhibitor sensitivity axis seen in cell lines requires clinical validation.
