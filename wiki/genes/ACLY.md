# ACLY

## Overview

ACLY encodes ATP citrate lyase, the enzyme that generates cytosolic acetyl-CoA from citrate, linking the TCA cycle to de novo lipogenesis (DNL) and cholesterol synthesis. In hepatocellular carcinoma ([HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)), ACLY-driven DNL has been shown to suppress immune responses by altering the fatty acid composition of tumor cells.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- ACLY-driven de novo lipogenesis in HCC lowers PUFA:SFA ratio, suppresses cGAS-STING, and downregulates PD-L1 ([CD274](../genes/CD274.md)), conferring anti-PD-L1 resistance [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **HCC**: ACLY activity sustains DNL and, via PUFA:SFA modulation, suppresses innate immune sensing (cGAS-STING) and adaptive immune checkpoint expression; implicated in resistance to anti-PD-L1 therapy [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Functionally upstream of [FASN](../genes/FASN.md), [SREBF1](../genes/SREBF1.md), and [SCD](../genes/SCD.md) in the DNL network; the ACLY-cGAS-STING-CD274 axis connects lipid metabolism to immune evasion [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- ACLY inhibition is a candidate strategy to restore cGAS-STING signaling and PD-L1 expression, potentially re-sensitizing HCC to anti-PD-L1 immunotherapy [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Open questions

- Whether ACLY inhibitor combinations with immune checkpoint blockade translate to clinical benefit in HCC requires prospective evaluation [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)
