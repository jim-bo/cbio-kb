# HECW1

## Overview

HECW1 (HECT, C2 and WW domain containing E3 ubiquitin protein ligase 1, also known as NEDL1) encodes a HECT-domain E3 ubiquitin ligase. In cancer, it has been identified as a statistically significant candidate driver gene in esophageal adenocarcinoma (EAC) based on whole-genome and whole-exome sequencing, though its precise oncogenic mechanism in this context remains to be experimentally validated.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Recurrently mutated in 8% of esophageal adenocarcinomas (EAC) in a WGS/WES study of 145 tumors; nominated as a statistically significant candidate driver but not yet experimentally validated [PMID:23525077](../papers/23525077.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **EAC (Esophageal Adenocarcinoma):** mutated in ~8% of tumors in a large WGS/WES cohort; statistical significance established but functional role not confirmed in this study [PMID:23525077](../papers/23525077.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-nominated alongside [AKAP6](../genes/AKAP6.md), [AJAP1](../genes/AJAP1.md), and [NUAK1](../genes/NUAK1.md) as additional significant candidate drivers in EAC [PMID:23525077](../papers/23525077.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No approved targeted therapies; nominated as a candidate driver warranting further validation [PMID:23525077](../papers/23525077.md)

## Open questions

- Functional validation is lacking; HECW1 is nominated statistically in EAC but not experimentally confirmed in this study [PMID:23525077](../papers/23525077.md)
