# High-Grade Endometrial Stromal Sarcoma (HGESS)

## Overview

High-grade endometrial stromal sarcoma (HGESS) is an aggressive uterine mesenchymal malignancy. In OncoTree it is a child of [Endometrial Stromal Sarcoma (ESS)](../cancer_types/ESS.md), which sits under [Uterine Sarcoma (USARC)](../cancer_types/USARC.md). HGESS is histologically and molecularly distinct from [LGESS](../cancer_types/LGESS.md): it lacks the hormone receptor positivity and JAZF1-SUZ12 fusions characteristic of low-grade disease, and instead is defined by YWHAE/NUTM2 rearrangements and a more clinically aggressive behavior. It must be distinguished from undifferentiated uterine sarcoma, which lacks any retained stromal morphology.

## Cohorts in the corpus

- [usarc_msk_2020](../datasets/usarc_msk_2020.md) — prospective MSK-IMPACT sequencing of 107 uterine sarcoma patients at MSKCC; HGESS comprised n=7 of the high-grade non-LMS subgroup (n=22 total). [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)

## Recurrent alterations

- YWHAE/NUTM2A and YWHAE/NUTM2B rearrangements detected by MSK-Fusion RNA sequencing in two high-grade non-pleomorphic uterine stromal sarcomas (classified as HGESS), underscoring RNA-based fusion detection as a diagnostic tool [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)
- High-grade non-LMS uterine sarcomas (including HGESS) showed significantly worse [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) than uLMS (median 3.4 vs 6.3 years; HR 0.48, 95% CI 0.24–0.96, p=0.03); [RIT1](../genes/RIT1.md) amplification enriched in high-grade non-LMS (24%, 5/21) vs uLMS (2.5%, 2/80; p=0.004) [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)
- In the 2,138-sarcoma MSK-IMPACT cohort, high-grade endometrial stromal sarcoma was among the 45 OncoTree-mapped pathological entities profiled; MSI was rare across all sarcoma subtypes (5/1,893 MSI-high total) [PMID:35705560](../papers/35705560.md).

## Subtypes

- No further OncoTree subtypes defined below HGESS. Molecular distinction from undifferentiated uterine sarcoma relies on retained stromal morphology and YWHAE/NUTM2 fusion confirmation.

## Therapeutic landscape

- No targeted therapy with established efficacy specific to HGESS in the corpus. Actionable alterations were identified in 45% of the broader uterine sarcoma cohort; matched therapy was administered in 17% of those patients. [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)

## Sources

- [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md) — Hensley et al., prospective MSK-IMPACT sequencing of 107 uterine sarcomas; HGESS characterized as part of the high-grade non-LMS subgroup.
