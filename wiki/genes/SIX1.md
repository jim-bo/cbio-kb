# SIX1

## Overview

SIX1 (SIX Homeobox 1) encodes a homeodomain transcription factor that plays key roles in embryonic development and organogenesis. Activating mutations in SIX1 are well-established drivers in Wilms tumor (nephroblastoma), where they impair differentiation of the developing kidney. SIX1 mutations are among the most recurrently mutated transcription factor genes in pediatric kidney cancers.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- SIX1 p.Q177R mutation identified in a Wilms tumor patient via comprehensive whole-genome/transcriptome sequencing (cWGTS); the mutation was detected as part of a pediatric oncology sequencing study demonstrating the added diagnostic value of cWGTS over standard clinical panels [PMID:35585047](../papers/35585047.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **Wilms tumor (WT):** SIX1 p.Q177R hotspot mutations are recurrent in Wilms tumor and considered disease-defining drivers; reported in this pediatric oncology cWGTS cohort [PMID:35585047](../papers/35585047.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- No co-occurrence or mutual exclusivity data yet in this corpus.

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting reported in this corpus. SIX1 mutations in Wilms tumor may inform risk stratification.

## Open questions

- Functional impact of p.Q177R vs. other SIX1 hotspot mutations (e.g., p.Q177L) in terms of transcriptional dysregulation and clinical outcome.
