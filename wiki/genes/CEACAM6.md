# CEACAM6

## Overview

CEACAM6 (Carcinoembryonic Antigen-Related Cell Adhesion Molecule 6) encodes a GPI-anchored cell-surface glycoprotein in the CEA family. In cancer, CEACAM6 overexpression has been linked to [gemcitabine](../drugs/gemcitabine.md) resistance in pancreatic adenocarcinoma (PDAC) and reduced cytolytic T-cell activity, making it a candidate therapeutic target in specific [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) mutant contexts.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Upregulated in PDAC tumors with KRAS G12D, G12V, and Q61H mutations but not G12R; CEACAM6 overexpression linked to gemcitabine resistance and low cytolytic T-cell activity [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md)**: Glycoprotein upregulation is KRAS-hotspot-specific (G12D/G12V/Q61H), supporting anti-CEACAM6 monoclonal antibody strategies in hotspot-stratified PDAC [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-upregulated with [CEACAM5](../genes/CEACAM5.md) in KRAS G12D/G12V/Q61H PDAC tumors; both absent in KRAS G12R tumors [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Anti-CEACAM5/6 monoclonal antibodies (e.g., preclinical MN-15, MN-3) combined with first-line chemotherapy proposed for KRAS G12D/G12V/Q61H PDAC based on selective upregulation [PMID:34534465](../papers/34534465.md)

## Open questions

- Mechanism linking KRAS G12R (but not G12D/G12V/Q61H) to absent CEACAM6 upregulation is unresolved; KRAS effector bias (G12R preferentially signals through PI3K/RAL vs. RAF) is a plausible hypothesis requiring validation.
