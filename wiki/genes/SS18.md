# SS18

## Overview

SS18 (SS18 Subunit Of BAF Chromatin Remodeling Complex) is the defining fusion partner in synovial sarcoma ([SS](../cancer_types/SS.md)), participating in the t(X;18)(p11;q11) translocation that produces in-frame SS18-SSX1 or SS18-SSX2 fusion oncoproteins. These fusions disrupt the BAF (SWI/SNF) chromatin remodeling complex and are present in virtually 100% of synovial sarcomas, making them the pathognomonic genetic event of this tumor type. SS18-SSX fusions act as aberrant transcriptional regulators that remodel chromatin to silence differentiation programs.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- t(X;18) SS18-[SSX1](../genes/SSX1.md) or SS18-[SSX2](../genes/SSX2.md) fusions present in 100% of synovial sarcoma ([SS](../cancer_types/SS.md)) cases in the TCGA pan-sarcoma cohort [PMID:29100075](../papers/29100075.md).
- SS18–SSX1/SSX2 fusion is disease-defining in synovial sarcoma; identified by the Fusion AWG in the PanCanAtlas pan-cancer integration as a canonical example of a fusion driver confined to a single histologic entity [PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)
- cWGTS reclassified an MSK-Fusion call of SS18-SSX1 as SS18-SSX2 (supported by WGS spanning reads) in a synovial sarcoma, demonstrating superior fusion partner discrimination of comprehensive sequencing over clinical panels [PMID:35585047](../papers/35585047.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **SS (Synovial Sarcoma)** — SS18-SSX fusion is the defining pathognomonic event in 100% of synovial sarcomas; fusion partner is either [SSX1](../genes/SSX1.md) or [SSX2](../genes/SSX2.md) [PMID:29100075](../papers/29100075.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- SS18-SSX1 and SS18-SSX2 are mutually exclusive by definition (each represents a distinct fusion isoform); virtually no other recurrent drivers are co-present given the t(X;18) defines the entire entity [PMID:29100075](../papers/29100075.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- SS18-SSX fusion disrupts BAF complex function; BET bromodomain inhibitors and [EZH2](../genes/EZH2.md) inhibitors have preclinical rationale in SS18-SSX-driven synovial sarcoma [PMID:29100075](../papers/29100075.md).

## Open questions

- Whether SS18-SSX1 vs SS18-SSX2 fusions have different clinical outcomes or therapeutic vulnerabilities remains an open question [PMID:29100075](../papers/29100075.md).
