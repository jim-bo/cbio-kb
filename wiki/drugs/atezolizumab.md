# atezolizumab

## Overview

Atezolizumab is an IgG1 monoclonal antibody targeting PD-L1 ([CD274](../genes/CD274.md)), preventing binding to PD-1 and B7-1. In [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md), it is approved in combination with [bevacizumab](../drugs/bevacizumab.md) (IMbrave150 regimen) as first-line therapy.

## Evidence in the corpus

- A narrative review of lipid metabolism in HCC cites atezolizumab + bevacizumab (IMbrave150) as the current first-line standard of care for HCC, with real-world response rates of 26%–28% and median PFS <12 months. The review identifies multiple lipid-metabolic resistance mechanisms: [CD36](../genes/CD36.md) upregulation recruits [FOXP3](../genes/FOXP3.md)+ Tregs and suppresses cytotoxic T cells; [FABP1](../genes/FABP1.md) competes with T cells for long-chain fatty acids; ACLY-driven DNL lowers PUFA:SFA ratio, suppressing cGAS–STING and PD-L1 (paradoxically conferring anti-PD-L1 resistance); MAFLD-related HCC may be less responsive to ICIs. [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Resistance mechanisms

- CD36-mediated lipid uptake drives FOXP3+ Treg recruitment and suppresses cytotoxic CD8 T-cell progenitors, attenuating atezolizumab efficacy; PLT012 (anti-CD36) sensitized HCC to ICIs preclinically. [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)
- ACLY-driven DNL suppresses cGAS–STING signaling and reduces PD-L1 expression, conferring resistance to anti-PD-L1 therapy in a subset of HCC. [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)
- FABP1-mediated competition for linoleic acid impairs T-cell activation, undermining the immune response restored by checkpoint blockade. [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) — hepatocellular carcinoma; first-line standard of care with bevacizumab (IMbrave150); real-world ORR 26%–28%; lipid-metabolic axes drive resistance.
