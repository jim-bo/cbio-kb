# HNSCC Broad (Stransky 2011)

## Overview

Whole-exome sequencing study of 74 head and neck squamous cell carcinoma (HNSCC) tumors generated by the Broad Institute and Dana-Farber Cancer Institute. Initially 92 patients were sequenced; 18 were excluded for stromal admixture. Two tumors also underwent whole-genome sequencing. The dataset characterized the mutational landscape of HNSCC including HPV-associated tumors across oral cavity, oropharynx, hypopharynx, larynx, and sinonasal cavity subsites.

## Composition

- Cancer type: [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md), n = 74 tumors with matched normals.
- Subsites: oral cavity, oropharynx, hypopharynx, larynx, sinonasal cavity.
- 89% reported tobacco use; 79% alcohol use; 14% HPV-16 positive by PCR/ISH.
- Assay: [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) at 150x mean coverage (87% of loci >20x); two tumors also underwent [whole-genome-seq](../methods/whole-genome-seq.md) at 31x mean coverage.
- Copy number profiled by SNP arrays.

## Notable findings derived from this cohort

- Mean non-synonymous mutation rate of 3.3 mutations/Mb; HPV-positive tumors had lower rates (2.28 vs 4.83 mutations/Mb; p = 0.004) [PMID:21798893](../papers/21798893.md).
- MutSig identified 39 significantly mutated genes (FDR q < 0.1), with [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) mutated in 11% (predominantly loss-of-function) and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) nearly universally inactivated [PMID:21798893](../papers/21798893.md).
- A squamous differentiation gene set ([NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md), [IRF6](../genes/IRF6.md), [TP63](../genes/TP63.md)) was the top enriched functional category among significantly mutated genes [PMID:21798893](../papers/21798893.md).
- G-to-T transversion frequency (mean 12%) characteristic of tobacco exposure; HPV-negative laryngeal cancers had highest rates [PMID:21798893](../papers/21798893.md).
