# ATP6V1C1

## Overview

ATP6V1C1 (ATPase H+ Transporting V1 Subunit C1) encodes a subunit of the vacuolar-type H+-ATPase (V-ATPase), a proton pump that regulates intracellular pH and endosomal trafficking. It lies in the 8q22.3 amplification peak and has been nominated as a cis-driver candidate in hepatocellular carcinoma with implicated roles in Wnt/beta-catenin, Notch, mTOR signaling, and cell migration.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- ATP6V1C1 (V-ATPase subunit, 8q22.3) nominated as a cis-driver candidate in HCC with roles in Wnt/β-catenin, Notch, and mTOR signaling and migration [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC): ATP6V1C1 in the 8q22.3 amplification peak; nominated cis-driver candidate in Chinese HCC cohort (n=159) [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-localizes in 8q22.3 amplicon with RRM2B as a cis-driver candidate [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- V-ATPase inhibition is a potential therapeutic strategy given roles in Wnt/Notch/mTOR signaling; specific agents not studied in this cohort [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Open questions

- Direct role of ATP6V1C1 in HCC oncogenesis is unclear; cis-driver status requires functional validation [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)
