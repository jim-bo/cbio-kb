# celecoxib

## Overview

Celecoxib is a selective cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2; [PTGS2](../genes/PTGS2.md)) inhibitor classified as a non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID). It suppresses prostaglandin E2 (PGE2) biosynthesis from arachidonic acid. In the oncology context it is explored as an adjunct to sensitize tumors to standard-of-care therapy by reducing PGE2-mediated immunosuppression in the tumor microenvironment. It is not approved by the FDA as an anti-cancer therapy.

## Evidence in the corpus

- NSAIDs celecoxib and aspirin (COX-2/[PTGS2](../genes/PTGS2.md) inhibitors) sensitize [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) cells to [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md) and IFN-α in preclinical models via reduction of PGE2-mediated immunosuppression; the review flags celecoxib as a drug-repurposing candidate for combination with TKIs or ICIs in HCC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Resistance mechanisms

- Not applicable (celecoxib itself is a sensitizing agent in HCC preclinical models rather than a primary therapeutic).
