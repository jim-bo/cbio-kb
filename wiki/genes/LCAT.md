# LCAT

## Overview

LCAT (lecithin–cholesterol acyltransferase) encodes an enzyme that esterifies cholesterol in HDL particles, playing a central role in reverse cholesterol transport. In hepatocellular carcinoma ([HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)), LCAT is downregulated and has been identified as a predictive biomarker for response to the TKI [lenvatinib](../drugs/lenvatinib.md). Loss of LCAT promotes lenvatinib resistance by enhancing triglyceride catabolism and fatty acid oxidation, while exogenous HDL administration can resensitize HCC cells to lenvatinib in preclinical models.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Downregulated in HCC relative to normal liver tissue; lower expression correlates with poorer lenvatinib response [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).
- Loss-of-function promotes lenvatinib resistance by activating TG catabolism and FAO pathways [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md):** LCAT is downregulated and functions as a predictive biomarker for lenvatinib sensitivity; HDL treatment sensitized lenvatinib-resistant HCC to the drug in preclinical models [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- LCAT downregulation occurs within the broader context of cholesterol biosynthesis pathway upregulation ([HMGCR](../genes/HMGCR.md), [SQLE](../genes/SQLE.md), [MVK](../genes/MVK.md) overexpression) in HCC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- **[Lenvatinib](../drugs/lenvatinib.md):** LCAT expression predicts lenvatinib response in HCC; loss confers resistance. Exogenous HDL sensitizes LCAT-low HCC cells to lenvatinib in preclinical models [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Open questions

- Whether LCAT expression can serve as a prospective biomarker in clinical trials of lenvatinib in HCC has not been formally tested.
