# SEMA3E

## Overview

SEMA3E (semaphorin 3E) encodes a secreted class-3 semaphorin involved in axon guidance, acting through plexin receptors. In pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma, SEMA3E is co-amplified with [SEMA3A](../genes/SEMA3A.md), suggesting coordinate dysregulation of semaphorin signaling contributes to PDAC pathogenesis.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Co-amplified with SEMA3A in pancreatic cancer WES of 142 ICGC tumors; part of the broader axon guidance pathway dysregulation in PDAC [PMID:23103869](../papers/23103869.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma ([PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md)): co-amplified with SEMA3A in a subset of tumors [PMID:23103869](../papers/23103869.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-amplified with SEMA3A; both are class-3 semaphorins signaling through [PLXNA1](../genes/PLXNA1.md) in pancreatic cancer [PMID:23103869](../papers/23103869.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Therapeutics targeting semaphorin-plexin signaling represent unexplored candidates for PDAC treatment [PMID:23103869](../papers/23103869.md)

## Open questions

- Functional role of SEMA3E amplification in PDAC as driver versus passenger requires experimental validation [PMID:23103869](../papers/23103869.md)
