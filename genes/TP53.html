TP53

Overview

TP53 (Tumor Protein P53) encodes the p53 transcription factor, the most frequently mutated gene across all human cancers. p53 acts as a master regulator of the DNA damage response, cell cycle arrest, apoptosis, and senescence. TP53 is inactivated by missense mutation (often with dominant-negative gain-of-function effects), deletion, or MDM2-mediated degradation. Its mutation frequency varies substantially by cancer type, and in the metastatic setting TP53 is often enriched relative to primary tumors. Germline TP53 mutations define Li-Fraumeni syndrome.

Alterations observed in the corpus

  • Somatic mutation identified in the TCGA PCC/PGL cohort (pcpg_tcga_pub, n=173) as part of a cancer-relevant gene scan alongside SETD2 and ARNT. PMID:28162975
  • Germline TP53 S215N missense identified in a pediatric AML patient in the PIPseq program (n=101, Columbia University); explained lack of response to conventional therapy and increased other-cancer risk. PMID:28007021
  • Significantly mutated in both EAC and ESCC in the TCGA esophageal carcinoma cohort (stes_tcga_pub, n=164 esophageal); notably mutated in only 1 of 4 ESCC3 tumors. PMID:28052061
  • Mutated in 27% of HR+/HER2− metastatic breast cancer (mBC) vs 20% of HR+/HER2− early breast cancer (eBC) in 216 mBC patients (Fisher p=0.13, not statistically significant); TP53 mutation is not enriched in the metastatic setting for HR+/HER2− subtype. PMID:28027327
  • TP53 recurrently altered at baseline in SCLC PDX models; alterations preserved through acquired cisplatin/etoposide resistance with no evidence of additional acquired TP53 mutations under chemotherapy selection PMID:28196596.
  • TP53 significantly enriched in the unknown mitogenic driver (UMD) subset of 860 metastatic LUAD patients profiled by MSK-IMPACT (p<0.05 vs level 1–4 cohort); elevated in heavy-smoker UMD samples PMID:28336552.
  • Single TP53 R248W missense event observed in an acral lentiginous melanoma (ACRM) cohort of 34 patients; MDM2/TP53 (apoptosis/senescence) pathway altered in 17% of ALM patients PMID:28373299.
  • Driver SNV in lung adenocarcinoma (LUAD); not associated with pre-operative ctDNA detection within LUADs in the TRACERx circulating tumor DNA study PMID:28445469
  • E258K mutation in 1/19 sequenced oligodendroglioma tumors PMID:28472509
  • Most frequently altered gene in pan-cancer MSK-IMPACT analysis (41% of 10,336 samples across 62 tumor types); largely truncating/splice-disrupting inactivating mutations; significantly enriched vs TCGA in prostate (PRAD), chromophobe renal cell carcinoma (CHRCC), glioblastoma, and gastric cancer PMID:28481359
  • Mutated in 39.7% of CCEC; enriched in serous-like CCECs; co-enriched with PPP2R1A mutations in serous-like molecular profiles PMID:28485815
  • Alterations rise stepwise with stage/grade in NMIBC (p<0.001); co-listed with MDM2 as part of the TP53/MDM2 pathway enriched at higher tumor stage and grade PMID:28583311
  • Significantly enriched in CCA Clusters 1 and 2 (p < 0.001); mutation associated with increased structural variant burden (q < 0.1) PMID:28667006
  • Germline variants predominantly restricted to SHH medulloblastoma in the 491-sample ICGC cohort PMID:28726821
  • Most frequently altered tumor suppressor across 500 metastatic solid tumors (266/500, 53.2%); also recurrent in fusions (n=11) as a tumor-suppressor breakage event PMID:28783718
  • Present at 27% locoregional / 30% metastatic noncastrate / 48% mCRPC prostate cancer; clonal early events in matched primaries of patients who progress to metastasis, consistent with aggressive behavior PMID:28825054
  • Recurrent driver in DLBCL; mutually exclusive with KLHL6; CRISPR-enriched (loss of TP53 increases fitness) PMID:28985567
  • Mutated in 48% of MIBC; enriched in basal-squamous and neuronal subtypes; co-occurs with RB1 and E2F3 amplification; mutually exclusive with MDM2 amplification and CDKN2A loss; enriched in genome-doubled tumors (p < 10⁻⁷) PMID:28988769
  • Pan-sarcoma SMG; mutated in 50% of LMS (40/80); deep deletions in 9% LMS, 16% UPS, 12% MFS PMID:29100075
  • Most frequently mutated gene (73%) in metastatic gastric/gastroesophageal junction cancer (HER2-positive cohort); enriched relative to TCGA EGC non-MSI-H (62%) PMID:29122777
  • Mutated in 78% of mCRC cases; the only gene significantly enriched in metastatic vs early-stage colorectal primaries. PMID:29316426
  • Altered in 67% of durable clinical benefit (DCB) and 54% of no-durable-benefit (NDB) NSCLC patients on anti-PD-(L)1 therapy; no significant enrichment for response or non-response. PMID:29337640
  • Co-mutation with HER2 (ERBB2) enriched in patients without clinical benefit to neratinib (nominal p=0.018), associated with cell-cycle pathway co-alterations in pan-tumor HER2-mutant basket trial. PMID:29420467
  • Somatic mutations present in HPV(−) vulvar squamous cell carcinomas; named the most commonly mutated gene in HPV(−) vulvar SCC consistent with prior literature. PMID:29422544
  • Six LP/PV carriers (21.4% of LP/PV carriers) in a pediatric cancer cohort; strongest single-gene burden-test association (OR=32.8, p=7.83x10^-8). Recurrent c.586C>T p.(Arg196*) detected twice. Three of six carriers (50%) developed secondary malignant neoplasms; carriers presented with rhabdomyosarcoma, osteosarcoma, or BCP-ALL PMID:29489754
  • Called as a KIRC SMG by both MutSig2CV and MuSiC2 on the TCGA MC3 open-access MAF; one of the canonical pan-cancer drivers consistently recovered across callers PMID:29596782
  • Confirmed established prostate cancer driver in the SU2C/PCF 1,013-sample cohort; enriched in metastatic vs. primary tumors; part of the metastasis-enrichment genomic marker set proposed for prospective risk stratification PMID:29610475
  • Predominantly mutated (not fused) across cancer types; an exception is SARC where both TP53 fusion and mutation events were observed in the TCGA pan-cancer fusion landscape PMID:29617662
  • The only somatic mutation positively associated with high aneuploidy in the pan-cancer linear model (coefficient +0.13); even with p53 inhibited via SV40 large-T in AALE cells, chr_3p deletion still slowed proliferation initially, suggesting non-TP53 mechanisms also restrain aneuploidy PMID:29622463
  • TP53 is the largest hub of somatic–somatic interactions pan-cancer: co-occurs with IDH1, ATRX, PPP2R1A, RB1, CDKN2A; mutually exclusive with PIK3CA, HRAS, CTNNB1, ARID1A, FGFR3; context-dependent (mutually exclusive with KRAS in COAD/READ/LUAD but significantly co-occurring in PAAD); enriched in ≥10 multiplatform iClusters PMID:29625049
  • TP53 mutation co-occurs with cell-cycle alterations (CCNE1 amp, CDKN2A mut, RB1 loss, CDK6/E2F3 amp) and is mutually exclusive with CDKN2A deletion pan-cancer; TP53 is among the three genes enriched in ≥10 multiplatform iClusters (with KRAS and PIK3CA) PMID:29625050
  • TP53 is mutated in 33% (5/15) of bladder cancer organoid lines in a biobank of 15 patient-derived lines; nuclear p53 immunostaining was concordant with mutation calls PMID:29625057
  • Only MutSigCV-significant recurrently mutated gene enriched in responders to combination PD-1 + CTLA-4 in NSCLC (odds ratio 2.9, Fisher’s exact p=0.048); TP53 mutations associated with increased TMB in both CheckMate-012 and TCGA NSCLCs, consistent with smoking-associated mutagenesis PMID:29657128
  • Cited as the only functional region more frequently mutated than CTCF-binding-site hotspots in gastric cancer, occurring in ~50% of tumors; used as benchmark to contextualize the significance of recurrent non-coding CBS hotspot mutations PMID:29670109
  • Most frequently mutated gene in SMC Korean breast cancer cohort (47.9%, 89/186), significantly higher than TCGA (32.0%, LR p=5.0e-5); subtype prevalence: TNBC 87.5%, HER2+ 75%, ER+/HER2+ 64.3%, ER+ 23.5%; independently associated with SMC cohort status after subtype adjustment (MLR p=0.035) PMID:29713003
  • Biallelic inactivation by mutation plus 17p copy loss defines DLBCL genetic cluster C2; CLUMPS structural analysis localizes mutations to the DNA-binding site and Zn2+-coordinating residues; associated with independently inferior PFS and OS in multivariable Cox analysis PMID:29713087
  • Enriched in extrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma (EHC) vs intrahepatic; mutually exclusive with IDH1, BAP1, and FGFR2; co-occurs with CDKN2A/CDKN2B; in a 189-patient prospective NGS cohort PMID:29848569
  • Significantly more common in metastases of all receptor subtypes except HR-/HER2+; biomarker of poor prognosis in endocrine-resistant metastatic breast cancer (n=2,732-tumor combined cohort) PMID:30205045
  • TP53 pathway alterations in 61% of secondary MIBC (progression from prior non-muscle-invasive bladder cancer); this group had significantly worse cancer-specific survival after cisplatin-based NAC vs cystectomy alone (p=0.002) PMID:30290956
  • TP53 mutations associated with broad drug resistance and trend toward elesclomol sensitivity in Beat AML ex vivo drug-sensitivity profiling (n=672 AML specimens) PMID:30333627
  • Mutated in 32.5% of advanced HCC (MSK-IMPACT cohort); mutually exclusive with WNT/CTNNB1 pathway alterations (P=0.003); enriched in virally mediated HCC (45% vs. 21%, P=0.001) PMID:30373752
  • Most commonly mutated gene (58%) in gallbladder carcinoma (GBCA) WES cohort (n=57); trend toward enrichment in Chilean tumors (76%, NS after adjustment); mutually exclusive with ATM mutations (trend) PMID:30427539
  • TP53 is the most frequent nonsynonymous SNV target in EOPC prostate cancer (6% of cases); 8 biallelic inactivation events were observed; parallel biallelic inactivation of TP53 and PTEN was seen in subclones of the multi-region tumour PCA041, illustrating co-operative late events PMID:30537516.
  • TP53 protein-coding mutations are common in IDH-mutant non-codeleted gliomas and were among the most frequently detected alterations in CSF ctDNA; 100% concordance between CSF and matched tumour tissue was observed for truncal TP53 mutations in non-hypermutated cases (10/10 LGG pairs, 20/20 GBM pairs) PMID:30675060.
  • R273 hotspot missense mutations associated with >10-fold elevation of TP53-S315 phosphorylation (paired t-test p=0.001) in colon cancer; finding confirmed in CPTAC breast and ovarian cohorts (coad_cptac_2019) PMID:31031003
  • Altered in 13% (10/80) of metastatic panNET patients; all calls classified as likely oncogenic; no independent OS effect detected in the panet_msk_2018 cohort PMID:30687805
  • Mutated in 49.2% of cHCC-ICC (significantly higher than HCC 31% or ICC 22–38%); R249S AFB1-signature hotspot in 25.8% of TP53-mutant cases (all HBV+); focal deletion/loss in 46%; trunk driver in phylogenetic trees of cHCC-ICC PMID:31130341
  • Among the most frequently altered genes in mCRPC (444 tumors, n=429 patients); co-occurs with RB1; associated with shorter time on first-line ARSI (P=0.046, CPE=0.609); not significantly prognostic for overall survival in this cohort (P=0.072) PMID:31061129
  • Promoter hypomethylation-driven MDM4 exon-6 inclusion (MDM4-FL) marks TP53 wild-type lines sensitive to MDM2 inhibitor nutlin-3a in the CCLE dataset PMID:31068700
  • Germline P/LP alleles tied to Li-Fraumeni syndrome; identified among pathogenic germline variants (13% prevalence) in a 923-patient glioma genomic profiling cohort PMID:31263031
  • Recurrently mutated in upper tract urothelial carcinoma (UTUC, n=37 high-grade tumors) at frequencies not significantly different from TCGA urothelial bladder carcinoma PMID:31278255
  • TP53 was the top co-occurring driver mutation in rectal cancer tumoroids and the 287-patient MSK rectal cancer cohort (rectal_msk_2019) PMID:31591597.
  • TP53 altered in 82% of osteosarcoma PDX models and 70% of Ewing sarcoma PDX models; intron-1 fusion breakpoints identified (TP53-FXR2 in Wilms KT-9, TP53-TNR as diagnostic-only); TP53 inactivation classifier (AUROC 0.89) identifies pathway-inactive tumors even among TP53-wild-type models; altered in 11% of neuroblastoma models PMID:31693904.
  • TP53 is an early IDHmut-noncodel glioma driver; cancer cell fraction (CCF) increases from initial to recurrence (P=0.037); CCF higher than IDH1 in recurrent IDHmut-noncodels (P=0.0018) PMID:31748746
  • TP53 variants detected in WBC-matched cfDNA sequencing; can be misinterpreted as tumor-derived without matched WBC sequencing, confounding TMB and mutational-signature calls PMID:31768066
  • TP53 mutation in 36% of GBM PDX (n=30/84); one PDX (GBM168) carried a private TP53 variant absent from the matched patient tumor, indicating engraftment-associated clonal selection PMID:31852831
  • TP53 mutation among the most common co-mutations in TRK fusion-positive cancers across tumor types; IDH1-mutant GBM PDX with NTRK1-TPM3 fusion also carried TP53 mutations PMID:31871300
  • Mutation associated with a small set of p53-pathway and DNA-damage-response proteins in CCLE proteomics; TP53/MDM2/CDKN1A knockdown sensitivities co-vary with cell-division-related complexes PMID:31978347
  • Mutations enriched in the proliferative NS-LUAD subtype (p=4.99e-6, FDR=9.73e-4) and depleted in the steady subtype (p=4.63e-14, FDR=9.03e-12); ALK fusions co-occurring with TP53 mutations concentrated in the proliferative subtype (7 tumors), a known poor-prognosis combination PMID:32015526
  • Driver in 954 pan-cancer tumors; 736 (77%) biallelic, 96% somatic-SNV + somatic-deletion. Pan-cancer associated with chromothripsis (OR=3.22, P=8.3e-35) across PCAWG PMID:32025007
  • Mutations in 23 endometrial carcinoma tumors including all serous carcinomas; missense DNA-binding-domain hotspots (R248, R273) elevate p53 protein and dysregulate AURKA, XRN2; truncating mutations increase PLK1-T210 phosphorylation and mitotic-marker proteins; downstream trans-effects include PLK1, CDK1, CHEK1, XPO1, TPX2, KMT2D, MAP3K4 PMID:32059776
  • TP53 mutated in 52% of synchronous DCIS and 54% of synchronous IDC-NSTs (P>0.05); 13/14 DCIS and 12/14 IDC-NST mutations were inactivating with LOH; 75% frequency in grade-3 vs 0% in grade-2 DCIS (P<0.001); 14% in pure DCIS PMID:32220886
  • TP53 alterations associated with higher castration-resistance rates (1.5- to 5-fold range across pathway alterations) in metastatic castration-sensitive prostate cancer; OS association attenuated after multivariable adjustment PMID:32220891
  • TP53 loss-of-function mutations or homozygous deletions in 56% of uterine leiomyosarcoma (uLMS); significantly more frequent in non-uterine LMS (71%, p=0.01); two germline pathogenic carriers including one with Li-Fraumeni syndrome PMID:32299819
  • TP53 mutated less frequently in CDK12-biallelic inactivation (CDK12-Bi) prostate cancer compared to CDK12 wild-type PMID:32317181
  • TP53 tumor-suppressor loss observed in UTUC PDX models alongside RB1 and CDKN2A/CDKN2B PMID:32332851
  • Mutant tumors enriched in multi-omics cluster C1 (proximal-inflammatory) in LUAD (CPTAC cohort, n=110); elevated cognate protein/phosphosite, upregulation of MMR (MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2) and DDR proteins (ATM, ATR, BRCA1), elevated EZH2 protein, downregulated Wnt-signaling proteins (AXIN1, TCF7L2), and outlier BRAF hyperphosphorylation PMID:32649874
  • Mutated in 24/41 (58.5%) CDK4/6i-resistant vs 0/18 sensitive HR+/HER2- metastatic breast cancer biopsies; enriched in resistance but not sufficient in vitro (CRISPR knockout does not confer CDK4/6i resistance in MCF7 cells) PMID:32404308
  • Among commonly mutated genes profiled in the JHCNC NF1 biorepository across plexiform neurofibroma and MPNST (23 patients, 55 samples); mutational profile preserved between tumor, derived cell line, and PDX PMID:32561749
  • More frequent in N-terminal APC mutant MSS mCRC tumors (vs C-terminal APC mutant tumors) in the MSK retrospective cohort. PMID:32730818
  • Strongly enriched in MIP/SOL-predominant LUAD (62% vs 33% ACI/PAP vs 16% LEP; p<0.001); discussed as a permissive driver preceding whole-genome doubling in aggressive LUAD subtypes. PMID:32791233
  • Shared truncal TP53 mutations (including hotspots R248L, Q144Rfs26, Q144Rfs28) with CNLOH defined the ancestral GCT-blood-cancer clone; shared in 91% of GCT/hematologic-malignancy pairs vs. 61% of GCT-only and 9% of de novo AML; independently acquired secondary hits in each lineage PMID:32897884
  • DDR CH gene; therapy-associated CH (OR=2.8, q=2×10⁻⁴); detected at CH stage in 10/14 tMN cases that harbored TP53; co-occurred with complex karyotype/aneuploidy in 12/13 evaluable TP53-mutant tMNs; strong predictor of tMN risk PMID:33106634
  • TP53 alterations in 8/51 BRAFi-monotherapy melanoma patients (25% harbored functional TP53 or MDM2 events); no association with BRAFi response grade or survival PMID:32913971
  • Mutations strongly enriched in NMF Basal-I (TNBC) breast cancer; 9 of 10 BL1 TNBC samples in the CPTAC breast cohort (n=122) carried TP53 mutations PMID:33212010
  • All three recurrent medulloblastomas in the CPTAC pediatric brain tumor cohort (n=218) acquired new TP53 mutations absent in matched primary tumors, suggesting clonal evolution at recurrence PMID:33242424
  • Recurrent mutations (MutSigCV P=0.018) in upper tract urothelial carcinoma (UTUC, n=40); co-occurs with 8q22.3 gain (P=0.02); iClusterBayes posterior 0.56; enriched in iCluster-high (muscle-invasive) subtype PMID:33397444
  • Largely missense mutations in DNA-binding domain in GBM (CPTAC, n=99); protein elevated without RNA change in mutant tumors; recurrent X126_splice in-frame exon-5 truncation; DNA-repair phosphosites upregulated without protein-level upregulation in TP53-mutant tumors; ATR, CDK2, CDK9 elevated; MDM2 decreased; TP53 S315 and TP53BP1 S1099/S1106/S1109 phosphorylation correlate with TP53 abundance (Pearson r=0.89/0.53). PMID:33577785
  • Mutated in 17% of iCCA (N=412); independent predictor of shorter OS in resected (HR 1.82, 95% CI 1.08–3.06, P=0.03) and unresected disease on multivariate analysis; associated with LVI (76% vs 47%, Q=0.02), nodal disease (43% vs 15%), large bile-duct type (33% vs 7%); co-occurs with TERT (τ=0.19, Q=0.002) and PI3K/TGF-β/cell cycle/RTK-RAS pathway alterations; enriched in PSC-associated iCCA (83%). PMID:33765338
  • Mutated in ~80% of esophageal/esophagogastric junction adenocarcinomas (EAC, N=487 MSK-IMPACT); TP53 alteration status not associated with OS; mutually exclusive with MDM2 amplification (p<0.001, q<0.05) — MDM2 amplification was present in 24% of TP53-wild-type tumors. PMID:33795256
  • Mutations in 89% of TERT-WT vs 50% of TERT-altered metaplastic breast carcinomas; mutually exclusive with TERT alterations (CoMEt p<0.001) PMID:33863915
  • Component of the high-risk composite alteration set (TP53 mut + KRAS mut + CDKN2A/B deletion) associated with worse OS in lymph-node-positive intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma (N1 disease); median OS 12.1 months (95% CI 5.7–21.5) vs 30.9 months for wild-type (P=0.002) PMID:33963001
  • Most commonly altered gene across MSK-ACCESS cfDNA cohort; mutated in 144/248 (58%) of NSCLC samples with detectable alterations and recurrently mutated/deleted in biliary, bladder, breast, prostate, and pancreatic cancers; two TP53 p.R253H calls (0.17%/0.24% VAF) were borderline false positives on the orthogonal-NGS specificity panel PMID:34145282
  • Mutated in 66.3% of CSCC tumors (88 QC-passed tumors); defining p53-pathway lesion in cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma; mutually exclusive with TERT alterations is not evaluated in this exome dataset PMID:34272401
  • Most prevalent gene in LUAD (overall); nominally higher in pN+ (45% vs 34%) but not statistically significant in the >=2% Fisher analysis for lymph node metastasis association PMID:34290393
  • Altered in 83% of high-purity PDAC tumors; missense mutations show cis-effect on TP53 protein and TP53-S315 phosphosite; both missense and truncating alterations show trans-effects on MKI67-S1376, MSH6-S219, TP53BP1-S321 PMID:34534465
  • Subtype gradient in LCINS: piano 7.0%, mezzo-forte 18.6%, forte 31.9%; mutually exclusive with MDM2 amplification; HR=2.9 jointly with MDM2; part of five-feature genomic risk score for LCINS mortality PMID:34493867
  • Altered in 82% of MSS EO-CRC vs 73% in AO-CRC; initial enrichment in 36-49 age group vs AO lost statistical significance after adjustment for tumor sidedness; likely reflects left-sided predominance of EO-CRC PMID:34405229
  • Biallelic somatic TP53 variants observed in 5/6 biallelic RAD51B tumors in a cohort of 3,422 breast/ovarian cancer patients, consistent with selection in BRCA-associated HRD cancers. PMID:34635660
  • Fewer alterations in Black prostate cancer patients (19% vs. 30% White), specifically fewer missense mutations; R273 hotspot prevalence is only 2% in White men in MSK-IMPACT cohort (n=1,123). PMID:34667026
  • Loss-of-function mutations and CNAs are the defining HGSOC alteration; TP53 ranked #1 by positive Δκrisk (0.209) in network curvature analysis of 45 ICI-treated HGSOC patients, validating the methodology against known HGSOC biology. PMID:34819508
  • Similar overall mutation frequency in MSS colorectal tumors (71.7% Nigeria vs 77.5% MSKCC), but skewed away from right-sided tumors in Nigerian CRC (25% right-sided vs 81.6% left-sided) PMID:34819518
  • Mutated in 71% of MSS CRCs vs 7% of MSI-H CRCs in the COLON MAP/HTAN colorectal polyp atlas, confirming TP53 as a major driver of the conventional adenoma-to-MSS CRC pathway PMID:34910928
  • Somatic hotspot mutations (C135S, V172F, R175H, R213P, R249S, L257Q, G266R) in 5/17 pediatric neuroblastoma-to-leukemia transformation patients; acquired post-therapy (distinct from adult CH); co-occurs with PPM1D mutations in 2 patients, consistent with parallel DDR-pathway clonal evolution PMID:35078859
  • Mutation enriched in metastases in 7 tumor types (LUAD, PRAD, HR+/HER2- ductal breast, MSS COAD, lobular breast, pancreatic neuroendocrine, endometrioid uterine); inverse pattern in HNSC (62% primary vs 45% metastatic, q=0.01); positively correlated with metastatic burden in PRAD, LUAD, and HR+/HER2- ductal breast PMID:35120664
  • TP53 co-mutated in CRC-DD (dual MAPK driver colorectal cancer); TP53 C275Y identified as a truncal event in patient P-0021518’s evolutionary tree, preceding both KRAS mutations in the clonal hierarchy PMID:35235413.
  • TP53 most frequent driver in the AcSé-MAPPYACTS pediatric cohort with 215 deleterious mutations/deletions across 632 relapsed/refractory samples; recommended for WEE1 inhibitor matching based on ovarian-cancer data; recurrent in CNS subgroups (HGG, MBL, EPN) PMID:35292802.
  • Concurrent TP53 mutations in 8 RET fusion-positive lung cancer cases linked to significantly shorter PFS (HR=3.5) on selpercatinib therapy in the MSK clinical cohort PMID:35304457.
  • TP53 altered in 79% of MSI-stable esophageal adenocarcinoma (EAC) patients in the MSK neoadjuvant CRT cohort (n=237); severity of TP53 dysfunction (TruncBi > Other > WT) tracks with worse pathologic treatment response and shorter PFS; TP53 TruncBi mutations (n=37) had 24-month cumulative progression incidence 67% PMID:35377946.
  • TP53 missense, truncating, splice, and in-frame mutations in 92% (24/26) clinical SCLC samples and 90% of PDX samples; retained in 16/18 (89%) matched clinical-PDX pairs; TP53 loss is SCLC-specific in MSK773 admixed SCLC/LUAD tumor; 2 PDXs (MSK599, MSK871) lost the TP53 alterations seen in matched clinical samples PMID:35440124.
  • TP53 mutations are rare in low-grade serous ovarian carcinoma (LGSOC) and are characterized in the WHO classification as distinguishing LGSC from HGSC; no recurrent TP53 driver mutations were found in 119 LGSOC cases on MSK-IMPACT PMID:35443055
  • TP53 was mutated in 13/51 (25%) prostate cancer brain metastasis (PCBM) cases and served as a truncal alteration in patient P4, illustrating early clonal origin of PCBM PMID:35504881
  • TP53 mutations in HCC (122-biopsy proteogenomics cohort) were associated with higher grade (p=0.001), HBV etiology (p=0.010), and poor prognosis (multivariate Cox p=0.034 independent of BCLC); TP53 and CTNNB1 mutations were mutually exclusive (Fisher p=0.012); TP53-mutant HCCs showed increased CDK1/CDK2/CDK5, AURKA, TTK, NEK2, EIF2AK2, PRKD2, and p38δ kinase activity by KSEA PMID:35508466
  • TP53 locus was altered by noncoding structural variants (SVs) in 13/29 osteosarcoma patients and by SNV/indels in 12/29; only 3 of the SV-driven alterations were detected by MSK-IMPACT, demonstrating that panel sequencing misclassifies TP53 SV-altered tumors as wild type — with implications for p53-pathway clinical trial eligibility PMID:35585047
  • TP53 was the most frequently altered gene in 40 metastatic CRPC models (organoids, PDXs, cell lines), with putative driver mutations or deep deletions in 23/35 (66%) samples across all four chromatin-defined subtypes; no subtype-specific TP53 enrichment was observed, distinguishing it from RB1 which was enriched in AR-negative/low samples PMID:35617398
  • TP53 shows a trend toward co-occurrence with PIK3R1 alterations (not statistically significant) in prostate cancer, in an MSK-IMPACT cohort of 1,417 prostate cancers PMID:35670774
  • Altered in 37.1% of sarcomas overall (44.9% complex genomic vs. 11.3% translocation-associated); 79.3% short variants; drives p53-pathway co-occurrence with MDM2/MDM4; confers resistance to MDM2 inhibitors; carries prognostic weight in Ewing sarcoma, in FoundationOne CDx pan-sarcoma cohort of 7,494 cases PMID:35705558
  • Altered across multiple sarcoma subtypes; near-universal pathway hit in DDLS/WDLS via CDK4/MDM2 co-amplification (214/215, 99%); co-occurs with STAG2 in Ewing sarcoma (10% ES); cell cycle and TP53 pathways altered in >=50% of samples in 8 of 22 core subtypes, in 2,138-sample MSK-IMPACT sarcoma cohort PMID:35705560
  • In AML cell-state analysis (n=68), TP53 mutations confirmed as adverse-risk variable; enriched in HSC-like cell state; TP53-mutant cases show elevated PEAR1 expression and greater ex vivo sensitivity to PIK-family inhibitors PMID:35868306
  • Most common SNV/InDel driver in Asian pan-cancer (aOM) cohort at 58% pan-cohort frequency; hotspots R273, R175, R248; R249S enriched in LIHC (10%) and IHCH (3%) consistent with aflatoxin mutational signature PMID:35871175
  • Altered in 81% of pMMR/MSS rectal tumors and only 38% of dMMR/MSI tumors (p<0.001) in LARC cohort (n=692); biallelic inactivation correlated with higher FGA; truncating mutations correlate with FGA more than missense; less frequent in immune-hot IG3/IG4 tumors PMID:35970919
  • Loss-of-function in 24/25 metastatic CSCC WGS samples (only CSCC_0009 wildtype); driver by all three tools (MutSigCV, OncodriveFML, dNdScv); cohort frequency consistent with high-TMB UV-driven phenotype PMID:35982973
  • Mutated in 52% of primary endometrial carcinomas in cfDNA pilot (n=28 cases); subclonal TP53 detected at diagnosis in cfDNA; clonally tracked across longitudinal timepoints including sentinel lymph nodes PMID:36007103
  • TP53-mutant HCC subset depends on CPT1A/BCAA metabolism; fatty acid oxidation suppression is pro-tumor in this context; TP53 listed among few dominant HCC drivers alongside TERT and CTNNB1 PMID:38355797
  • Altered in 90% of CAC (colitis-associated colorectal cancer) and ~50% of CAC dysplasia; clonal in the majority of CAC, subclonal in 14%, absent in adjacent non-neoplastic mucosa (n=13 WES); convergent independent TP53 acquisition demonstrated in multifocal lesions PMID:36611031
  • RB1+/TP53+ (proficient) status defines the aSCLC subset (20/600 SCLC cases, 3%); all aSCLC were never/light smokers; acquired subclonal RB1 mutations in metastatic samples of 2 patients without proliferation escalation PMID:39185963

Cancer types (linked)

  • PHC / PGNG: Somatic TP53 mutation identified as a cancer-relevant gene scan finding; not a primary driver in this low-mutation-burden tumor type. PMID:28162975
  • AML: Germline TP53 S215N — a functionally relevant mutation explaining conventional-therapy resistance and conferring elevated risk for secondary cancers; family counseling implications. PMID:28007021
  • ESCC / ESCA: TP53 is a significantly mutated gene in both ESCC and EAC; low frequency in ESCC3 distinguishes this subtype. PMID:28052061
  • BRCA: TP53 mutation rate (27%) in HR+/HER2− mBC is slightly above eBC (20%) but not statistically enriched (p=0.13); TP53 is not a metastasis-specific driver in HR+/HER2− breast cancer. PMID:28027327
  • GBM: Largely missense mutations in DNA-binding domain; protein elevated without RNA change; DNA-repair phosphosites upregulated without protein-level upregulation in TP53-mutant tumors; nominates ATR/CDK inhibitors. PMID:33577785
  • IHCH: Mutated in 17% of iCCA; independent predictor of shorter OS (HR 1.82, P=0.03 resected; HR 2.05, P<0.001 unresected on multivariate); associated with LVI, nodal disease, large bile-duct subtype, and PSC. PMID:33765338
  • EGC / ESCA: Mutated in ~80% of EAC; not associated with OS; mutually exclusive with MDM2 amplification. PMID:33795256

Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

  • In PCC/PGL, somatic TP53 mutation occurred in a context of otherwise mutually exclusive driver events; driver gene mutual exclusivity was significant (p < 1e-4) across the 21-gene set. PMID:28162975
  • In ESCC, TP53 is nearly universally mutated and does not show the same strong mutual exclusivity pattern as in PCC/PGL. PMID:28052061
  • In GBM, TP53 mutations co-occur with ATRX loss and IDH1 R132H; mutually exclusive with TERT promoter hotspot mutations. PMID:33577785
  • In iCCA, TP53 co-occurs with TERT promoter alterations (τ=0.19, Q=0.002) and PI3K/TGF-β/cell cycle/RTK-RAS pathway alterations; mutually exclusive with IDH1/2 mutations (τ=−0.15, Q=0.02). PMID:33765338
  • In EAC, TP53 mutations are mutually exclusive with MDM2 amplification (p<0.001, q<0.05); MDM2 amplification present in 24% of TP53-wild-type tumors. PMID:33795256

Therapeutic relevance

  • Germline TP53 S215N in pediatric AML explained poor response to conventional therapy; this finding has implications for altered treatment strategies and second-cancer surveillance. PMID:28007021
  • In GBM, TP53-mutant tumors show upregulated DNA-repair phosphosites with elevated ATR, CDK2, and CDK9, nominating DNA-damage-response inhibitors (ATR inhibitors, CDK inhibitors) as a hypothesis-generating therapeutic strategy for TP53-mutant GBM. PMID:33577785
  • In iCCA, TP53 mutation is an independent predictor of shorter OS (HR 1.82 resected, P=0.03); proposed as part of a pretreatment prognostic genomic panel alongside KRAS and CDKN2A to inform surgical vs. systemic therapy decisions. PMID:33765338

Open questions

  • The functional significance of somatic TP53 mutation in PCC/PGL (a tumor type with low overall mutation burden) and its relationship to tumor aggressiveness requires further characterization. PMID:28162975
  • Whether TP53 mutations in metastatic HR+/HER2− breast cancer influence response to CDK4/6 inhibitors or endocrine therapy requires prospective biomarker studies. PMID:28027327
