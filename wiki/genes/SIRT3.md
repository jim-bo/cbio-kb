# SIRT3

## Overview

SIRT3 is a mitochondrially-localized NAD+-dependent deacetylase (sirtuin family) that regulates mitochondrial metabolism and oxidative stress responses. In cancer contexts, SIRT3 has been implicated in histone deacetylation and chromatin organization. Multi-omic profiling of endometrial carcinoma identified SIRT3 as part of a histone acetylation regulatory network, negatively correlated with H3K27 and H3K36 acetylation marks.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- SIRT3 protein levels correlate negatively with H3K27 and H3K36 acetylation in CPTAC endometrial carcinoma (alongside [SIRT1](../genes/SIRT1.md) and [BRD4](../genes/BRD4.md)); this deacetylase activity axis is part of the broader histone modification landscape altered by [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md) hotspot mutations [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)
- Protein levels significantly negatively correlated with mitochondrial protein acetylation in NMF Basal-I breast tumors (CPTAC cohort, n=122); SIRT3 depletion proposed as a driver of broad mitochondrial hyperacetylation in Basal-I; therapeutic direction ambiguous given dual oncogene/tumor-suppressor roles in the literature [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md)
