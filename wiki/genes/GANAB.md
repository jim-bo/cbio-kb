# GANAB

## Overview

GANAB (Glucosidase II Alpha Subunit) encodes the alpha subunit of glucosidase II, an enzyme involved in N-linked glycoprotein processing in the endoplasmic reticulum. It plays roles in protein folding quality control. In upper tract urothelial carcinoma ([UTUC](../cancer_types/UTUC.md)), GANAB has been identified as a recurrently mutated gene associated with higher mutational load and enrichment in muscle-invasive disease.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- GANAB recurrently mutated in UTUC (P=0.04); associated with higher mutational load (P=0.02); enriched in muscle-invasive (MI)-UTUC vs TCGA MI-BLCA (P=0.021) [PMID:33397444](../papers/33397444.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[UTUC](../cancer_types/UTUC.md)**: Recurrent GANAB mutations (P=0.04) associated with elevated TMB (P=0.02) and enriched in MI-UTUC relative to TCGA muscle-invasive bladder cancer (P=0.021) [PMID:33397444](../papers/33397444.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

No co-occurrence or mutual exclusivity data reported for GANAB in this corpus.

## Therapeutic relevance

No direct therapeutic implications reported for GANAB in this corpus. The association with higher mutational load may have indirect relevance for immunotherapy eligibility.

## Open questions

- The mechanism by which GANAB mutations contribute to elevated TMB in UTUC is unclear.
- Replication of GANAB recurrence in independent UTUC cohorts is needed.
