# ZC3H7B

## Overview

ZC3H7B encodes a zinc finger CCCH-type containing 7B protein involved in RNA processing. In cancer, ZC3H7B is known as the 3' fusion partner in BCOR-ZC3H7B fusions, which characterize a subset of high-grade uterine stromal sarcomas. Detection of BCOR-ZC3H7B fusion by genomic profiling prompts reclassification away from leiomyosarcoma toward BCOR-mutated high-grade stromal sarcoma, which has distinct clinical and pathological features.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- BCOR-ZC3H7B fusion detected by MSK-Fusion in one case initially classified as high-grade myxoid uterine leiomyosarcoma (uLMS); genomic profiling prompted reclassification as BCOR-mutated high-grade stromal sarcoma; the patient survived 3.4 years from diagnosis [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **Uterine undifferentiated/high-grade stromal sarcoma:** BCOR-ZC3H7B fusion is a defining alteration for a subset of BCOR-rearranged high-grade uterine stromal sarcomas; detection via RNA-based fusion profiling enables diagnostic reclassification from histologically overlapping entities such as uLMS [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- BCOR-ZC3H7B fusions define cases that lack the hallmark genomic features of true uterine leiomyosarcoma (e.g., [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) loss, [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) inactivation, [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) alteration); fusion positivity is therefore in practice mutually exclusive with the uLMS genomic signature [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No targeted therapy directly approved for BCOR-ZC3H7B fusion sarcomas as of this corpus; the primary clinical impact is diagnostic reclassification [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)

## Open questions

- The natural history and optimal treatment of BCOR-ZC3H7B high-grade stromal sarcoma remain incompletely characterized; larger series are needed to define prognosis and potential therapeutic vulnerabilities [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)
