# PRLR

## Overview

PRLR (Prolactin Receptor) encodes the transmembrane receptor for prolactin. In cancer genomics, PRLR has been identified as a candidate coding driver in metastatic cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma via whole-genome sequencing.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Candidate coding driver called by ≥2 driver-detection tools in metastatic [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md) WGS cohort (n=25) [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md) (cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma):** candidate driver in metastatic nodal disease WGS cohort [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Called alongside [KHDRBS2](../genes/KHDRBS2.md), [COLEC12](../genes/COLEC12.md), [SLC22A6](../genes/SLC22A6.md), [LINGO2](../genes/LINGO2.md), [CDHR5](../genes/CDHR5.md), and [ZNF442](../genes/ZNF442.md) as candidate coding drivers by ≥2 tools in the same CSCC cohort [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting reported in corpus; PRLR is a surface receptor and a candidate therapeutic target in principle.

## Open questions

- Functional validation of PRLR as a driver in CSCC is lacking; mechanism of oncogenic activity is not established [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)
