# ACVR1C

## Overview

ACVR1C (Activin A Receptor Type 1C; also known as ALK7) encodes a type I serine/threonine kinase receptor in the TGF-β superfamily. It transduces activin/nodal signals through SMAD2/3 phosphorylation. In cancer genomics, ACVR1C emerges as a recurrently mutated TGF-β-axis component in pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma, augmenting the pathway disruption driven by [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) loss.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- TGF-β-axis alteration in pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDA), co-occurring with [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) loss and augmenting TGF-β pathway disruption [PMID:25855536](../papers/25855536.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md)** — recurrently mutated TGF-β receptor component alongside [TGFBR1](../genes/TGFBR1.md), [TGFBR2](../genes/TGFBR2.md), [ACVR1B](../genes/ACVR1B.md), [SMAD3](../genes/SMAD3.md), and [SMAD6](../genes/SMAD6.md) [PMID:25855536](../papers/25855536.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) loss and other TGF-β pathway lesions ([TGFBR1](../genes/TGFBR1.md), [TGFBR2](../genes/TGFBR2.md), [ACVR1B](../genes/ACVR1B.md), [SMAD3](../genes/SMAD3.md), [SMAD6](../genes/SMAD6.md)) in PDA [PMID:25855536](../papers/25855536.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- TGF-β pathway alterations including ACVR1C nominate pathway inhibitors as potential therapeutic candidates in PDA [PMID:25855536](../papers/25855536.md)

## Open questions
