## TL;DR

Puscz et al. retrospectively analyzed 39 patients with histologically confirmed [angiosarcoma](../cancer_types/ANGS.md) (AS) treated at a single German sarcoma center (BG University Hospital Bergmannsheil, Bochum) between 2001 and 2019. Secondary AS predominated (25/39, 64%) and were overwhelmingly radiation-associated cutaneous breast AS in women previously treated for [breast cancer](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) (24/25 secondary cases were cutaneous AS of the breast after radiotherapy). One-year and 5-year overall survival were 87.2% and 51.3%, respectively. On univariate logistic regression, nicotine use (OR 0.043, p=0.010) and prior carcinoma history (OR 0.065, p=0.012) were significantly associated with worse 1-year survival; breast cancer history was significantly associated with worse 5-year survival (OR 5.143, p=0.02). In multivariate analysis for 5-year survival, diabetes mellitus approached significance (OR 0.178, 95% CI 0.028–1.13, p=0.067). The authors frame the study as hypothesis-generating given the small sample.

## Cohort & data

- **Cohort:** 39 patients ≥18y with histologically confirmed AS (WHO 2020 soft tissue sarcoma definition), drawn from the clinic's internal sarcoma database, 2001–2019. Of 49 identified, 10 were excluded for incomplete data.
- **Setting:** Retrospective, single-center cohort study at BG University Hospital Bergmannsheil (Ruhr-University Bochum), Plastic Surgery / Sarcoma Centre, Germany. Ethics approval 20-7067_1-bio. STROBE-compliant.
- **Cancer types:** [angiosarcoma](../cancer_types/ANGS.md) ([ANGS](../cancer_types/ANGS.md)) — 14 primary (35.9%), 25 secondary (64%); 24/25 secondary AS were cutaneous breast AS after radiotherapy for [breast cancer](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) ([BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md)). The single male with secondary AS developed it after radiotherapy for upper-extremity liposarcoma.
- **Demographics:** Women 76.9% (30/39), men 23.1% (9/39); mean age at diagnosis 66 years (SD 14). Among secondary AS patients, 96% (24/25) were women; 88% (22/25) had prior breast cancer; 100% (25/25) had prior radiotherapy.
- **Endpoint:** 5-year overall survival (primary); 1-year overall survival (secondary).
- **Assays / genomics:** None. This is a clinical/epidemiological study; no sequencing, panel testing, or molecular profiling was performed.
- **Statistical methods:** Univariate binary logistic regression for candidate variable screening (threshold p<0.1 for multivariate inclusion); multivariate binary logistic regression for 5-year survival; Fisher's exact test, Shapiro–Wilk, t-test / Mann–Whitney U for descriptives. Tools: Microsoft Excel 16.84, IBM SPSS 25, GraphPad Prism 10.3.1. Significance α=0.05.

## Key findings

- **Cohort composition:** 39 AS patients; 14 (35.9%) primary, 25 (64%) secondary [PMID:32042194](../papers/32042194.md).
- **Sex skew driven by secondary AS:** Overall 76.9% female vs 23.1% male (p=0.0004 for sex distribution between primary and secondary subgroups). Among secondary AS, 96% (24/25) were female vs 42.9% (6/14) among primary AS [PMID:32042194](../papers/32042194.md).
- **Survival:** 1-year [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) 87.2%, 5-year OS 51.3% for the full cohort; primary AS 5-year OS 35.7% vs secondary AS 60% (p=0.19, NS) [PMID:32042194](../papers/32042194.md).
- **Univariate predictors of 1-year survival (significant):**
  - Nicotine use: OR 0.043, 95% CI 0.004–0.469, p=0.010.
  - Other carcinoma history: OR 0.065, 95% CI 0.008–0.552, p=0.012.
  - Only 5 deaths at 1 year — too few for multivariate analysis [PMID:32042194](../papers/32042194.md).
- **Univariate predictors of 5-year survival:**
  - Breast cancer history: OR 5.143, 95% CI 1.299–20.360, p=0.02 (significant).
  - Female sex: OR 0.19, 95% CI 0.034–1.078, p=0.061 (trend).
  - Diabetes mellitus: OR 0.19, 95% CI 0.034–1.078, p=0.061 (trend).
  - History of radiation: OR 2.7, p=0.151 (NS) [PMID:32042194](../papers/32042194.md).
- **Multivariate 5-year survival (breast cancer + female sex + diabetes):**
  - Diabetes mellitus: OR 0.178, 95% CI 0.028–1.13, p=0.067 (approaches significance; decreased odds of 5-year survival).
  - Breast cancer: OR 2.99, 95% CI 0.493–18.121, p=0.233 (NS after adjustment).
  - Female sex: OR 0.356, 95% CI 0.038–3.339, p=0.366 (NS) [PMID:32042194](../papers/32042194.md).
- **Treatment:** Complete (R0) resection achieved in 66.7% (26/39); adjuvant radiotherapy in 28.2% (11/39); adjuvant chemotherapy in 12.8% (5/39). Recurrence occurred in 56.4% (22/39), driven primarily by secondary AS (72% recurrence rate, p=0.017) [PMID:32042194](../papers/32042194.md).
- **Obesity not prognostic:** No association of obesity with 1-year or 5-year survival, consistent with the Barnes et al. meta-analysis cited as reference [25] [PMID:32042194](../papers/32042194.md).

## Genes & alterations

This paper reports no gene-level or alteration-level findings — it is a clinical epidemiology study without sequencing or molecular profiling. The authors note in their Discussion (section 4.3 Limitations) that future work should include "molecular and genetic profiling [to] help identify subgroups with distinct risk profiles and therapeutic vulnerabilities."

## Clinical implications

- **Long-term post-radiation surveillance:** Reinforces existing guidance for extended (>20y) follow-up after breast-conserving radiotherapy. Secondary cutaneous AS of the breast occurred in 22/25 secondary cases, all in women with prior radiotherapy.
- **Radical resection of the irradiated field:** Authors endorse resecting the entire former radiation field (not just visible tumor) for radiation-associated AS, citing Li et al. 2017 (ref [6]) for lower recurrence and improved 5-year survival.
- **Metabolic risk-factor management as a candidate intervention:** Diabetes mellitus showed a multivariate trend (p=0.067) toward worse 5-year survival in AS. The authors propose — but do not establish — that glycemic control may be relevant in AS management, and recommend prospective studies incorporating HbA1c.
- **Biomarker / molecular profiling:** Not addressed in this study; flagged as a future direction.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Small sample (n=39):** Authors explicitly call the study hypothesis-generating; multivariate findings are exploratory.
- **Quasi-complete separation:** Several univariate models (adjuvant chemotherapy, metastasis for 1-year survival; other carcinoma for 5-year survival) returned infinite or near-infinite ORs due to zero-event subgroups — authors flag these as statistical artifacts, not clinical signal.
- **Retrospective single-center design:** Risk of incomplete documentation, misclassification of comorbidities, and selection bias.
- **No diabetes subtyping:** Unable to distinguish type 1 vs type 2; no HbA1c data — precludes any glycemic-control inference.
- **No genomic data:** No HUGO-level alteration findings; authors call for molecular/genetic profiling in future cohorts.
- **Sex distribution differs from prior literature:** Albores-Saavedra et al. (ref [15]) report near-even sex distribution in AS; the female predominance here (76.9%) reflects the secondary-AS-after-breast-cancer enrichment specific to this referral cohort.
