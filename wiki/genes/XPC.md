# XPC

## Overview

XPC (Xeroderma Pigmentosum Complementation Group C) encodes a DNA damage recognition protein essential for global-genome nucleotide excision repair (GG-NER). Germline loss-of-function mutations cause xeroderma pigmentosum (XP), a rare autosomal recessive disorder characterized by extreme UV sensitivity and dramatically elevated risk of skin cancers including cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma ([CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md)). XPC is not a somatic driver of CSCC but the germline context modifies the mutational landscape of UV-induced tumors.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Germline loss in two xeroderma pigmentosum patients in a CSCC meta-analysis; XPC deficiency modifies the trinucleotide context of UV-induced somatic mutations in CSCC tumors [PMID:34272401](../papers/34272401.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **CSCC** — germline loss-of-function predisposes to CSCC via impaired UV-damage repair; modifies mutation spectrum (trinucleotide context) in resultant tumors [PMID:34272401](../papers/34272401.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Germline XPC loss creates a distinct UV-mutagenesis profile in CSCC tumors compared to sporadic XPC-WT cases [PMID:34272401](../papers/34272401.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No targeted therapy directly linked to XPC status in this corpus; immunotherapy (immune-checkpoint blockade) is the current standard of care for advanced CSCC [PMID:34272401](../papers/34272401.md)

## Open questions

- Whether XPC-deficient CSCC tumors have a distinct somatic driver gene profile beyond the UV mutational signature shift remains to be characterized.
