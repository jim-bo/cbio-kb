**PMID:** 35868306 · **DOI:** [10.1016/j.ccell.2022.07.002](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ccell.2022.07.002) · **Journal:** Cancer Cell (2022)

## TL;DR

Bottomly et al. expand the Beat [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md) functional-genomic resource ([aml_ohsu_2018](../datasets/aml_ohsu_2018.md) Waves 1+2) with two additional accrual waves (Waves 3+4), producing a harmonized cohort of **942 specimens from 805 [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md) patients** with paired ex vivo drug sensitivity, DNA sequencing, RNA-Seq, and curated clinical annotations ([PMID:35868306](../papers/35868306.md)). By deconvolving bulk RNA-Seq into six single-cell-defined maturation-state signatures and intersecting them with mutational status and ex vivo response across a broad drug panel, they show that AML cell differentiation state broadly governs drug sensitivity and frequently modifies known mutation–drug associations. Conditional random-forest modeling of overall survival nominates a single gene — **[PEAR1](../genes/PEAR1.md)** (a.k.a. JEDI/MEGF12) — as among the strongest predictors of outcome, performing equivalently to the LSC17 leukemic stem-cell signature, with independent validation in the Malani 2022 cohort and TCGA [LAML](../cancer_types/LAML.md) ([laml_tcga_pub](../datasets/laml_tcga_pub.md)).

## Cohort & data

- **Cumulative harmonized cohort:** 942 specimens from 805 patients (real-world AML, inclusive of de novo, transformed, and therapy-related cases; 70% collected at initial diagnosis). The dataset is published as cBioPortal study [aml_ohsu_2022](../datasets/aml_ohsu_2022.md) and accessible at Vizome (www.vizome.org/aml2) ([PMID:35868306](../papers/35868306.md)).
- **Waves 3+4 (new in this paper):** 293 specimens from 279 patients (243 unique to Waves 3+4), collected over 2.5 years; combined with the Tyner et al. 2018 Waves 1+2 ([aml_ohsu_2018](../datasets/aml_ohsu_2018.md); [PMID:30333627](../papers/30333627.md)).
- **Cohort-level analyses** used only the first-timepoint specimen per patient with remission samples excluded ([PMID:35868306](../papers/35868306.md)).
- **Assays:**
  - [Ex vivo drug sensitivity screen](../methods/ex-vivo-drug-sensitivity-screen.md) with probit dose-response curve fits.
  - [RNA-Seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) with conditional quantile normalization (CQN) parameters carried over from Waves 1+2.
  - DNA sequencing via an 11.9 Mb custom capture library covering all variants previously reported in AML (extending the [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) used in Waves 1+2).
  - FLT3-ITD and [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md) 4-bp insertion calls via internal PCR amplicon size-based methodology.
- **Clinical:** ELN 2017 risk categories assigned at initial-diagnosis specimens only, prioritizing internal deep-sequencing calls for [ASXL1](../genes/ASXL1.md), [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) over EMR-curated clinical results. NLP pipeline (Linguamatics I2E + Python) extracted structured clinical elements from EHR notes with accuracy 79–93%, precision 85–96%.
- **Data availability:** dbGaP study ID 30641 (accession phs001657.v2.p1); Genomic Data Commons; code at https://github.com/biodev/beataml2_manuscript.

## Key findings

- **Strong cross-cohort concordance** between Waves 1+2 and Waves 3+4: 89% concordance for mutation–drug-sensitivity associations and 98% concordance for the 13 WGCNA module eigengene–drug response associations across the two cohorts ([PMID:35868306](../papers/35868306.md)).
- **Added statistical power:** Harmonized analysis yielded **65 additional mutation–drug associations** that did not reach significance in Waves 1+2 alone (a 27% increase).
- **AML cell-maturation state deconvolution:** Using the top 30 genes from each of six Van Galen et al. single-cell AML signatures (HSC-like, Progenitor-like, GMP-like, Promonocyte-like, Monocyte-like, cDC-like), the authors summarized each as a PC1 score. HSC-like scores were higher in FAB M0–M2; Monocyte-like scores were higher in M4–M5.
- **Cell-state–mutation correlations:** [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md), and [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md) enriched for HSC-like; [NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md) for Promonocyte-like; RAS ([KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)/[NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md)) for Monocyte-like; [RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md)-mutant tumors showed dual correlation with both HSC-like and cDC-like states.
- **Cell-state–drug correlations:** 73 drugs showed response significantly correlated with at least one of the six cell-types; hierarchical clustering produced three drug groups (HSC/Progenitor-biased, cDC/Monocyte-biased, and a third class with mixed sensitivity).
- **[Venetoclax](../drugs/venetoclax.md) and the HDAC inhibitor [panobinostat](../drugs/panobinostat.md)** were extreme outliers exhibiting inverse cell-state response patterns relative to one another.
- **Conditional cell-state interactions:** [Sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md) (a [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md) inhibitor) shows expected greater efficacy in [FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md)-ITD samples, but a high Monocyte-like signal *confers resistance* in FLT3-ITD-positive cases, while a high Progenitor-like score predicts the strongest [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md) sensitivity in FLT3-ITD AML.
- **Drug-family analysis:** 25 drug families (using Cancer Targetome + KINOMEscan with IUPHAR family classifications) revealed that mutations in treatment-refractory subtypes (TP53, [STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md), RUNX1) confer **greater sensitivity to PIK-family inhibitors**, and N/KRAS mutation confers greater sensitivity to CDK inhibitors. PIK-family inhibitors were active against NPM1-mutated cases with high cDC-like scores but resistant in NPM1-mutated cases with high HSC-like scores.
- **Survival modeling (n=435 RNA-Seq + initial-diagnosis samples):** WGCNA modules 3, 9, and 12 plus the HSC-like cell-type score clustered with shorter overall survival; modules 3 and 9 had the strongest survival associations.
- **[PEAR1](../genes/PEAR1.md) as the dominant module-3 hub:** Within module 3 (n=203 genes), [PEAR1](../genes/PEAR1.md) had a far higher kME (hub correlation with module eigengene) than any other module member. No analogous singular hub was identified for module 9.
- **Cforest variable importance (de novo AML, n=298):** age, mutated [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), and WGCNA module 3 were the strongest predictors of overall survival; in young (<45 y) patients and in TP53-wild-type middle-age (45–60 y) patients, **module 3 expression was the strongest single determinant of outcome**.
- **PEAR1 vs ELN risk (single-gene expression):** Adverse vs Favorable ELN PEAR1 mean difference = 3.765 (p = 3.276e-25, N=172) in ≥60 y patients; 1.497 (p = 0.006, N=72) in middle-age; **not significant** in young (p = 0.170, N=79) despite PEAR1 being a strong outcome predictor in this group — i.e., PEAR1 carries prognostic signal *independent of ELN* in young AML.
- **PEAR1 vs LSC17:** Single-gene PEAR1 expression discriminated outcome in young AML patients comparably to the 17-gene LSC17 leukemic stem-cell signature; hazard ratios were similar across age strata and across bone-marrow vs peripheral-blood specimen types.
- **Independent validation:** elevated PEAR1 expression conferred significantly shortened [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) in both the Malani et al. 2022 cohort and TCGA LAML ([laml_tcga_pub](../datasets/laml_tcga_pub.md); [Cancer Genome Atlas Research et al., 2013]).
- **Surface expression confirmed:** PEAR1 surface protein on AML patient samples (anti-PEAR1 brilliant violet 650 flow cytometry) correlates with PEAR1 transcript level, supporting feasibility of antibody-based targeting.

## Genes & alterations

- **[PEAR1](../genes/PEAR1.md)** (a.k.a. JEDI, MEGF12) — single-gene expression hub of WGCNA module 3; positively correlated with HSC-like cell state; predicts shortened OS in AML across age groups; independent of ELN risk in young (<45 y) patients; equivalent to LSC17 in hazard-ratio analyses; surface-expressed on AML blasts ([PMID:35868306](../papers/35868306.md)).
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)** — confirmed strong adverse-risk variable in cforest survival modeling; enriched in HSC-like cell state; mutations confer increased sensitivity to PIK-family inhibitors; PEAR1 elevation observed in TP53-mutant cases.
- **[FLT3](../genes/FLT3.md)** — FLT3-ITD detected via PCR amplicon sizing; conferred sensitivity to sorafenib, but sensitivity is conditional on a high Progenitor-like score (resistant when Monocyte-like dominates).
- **[NPM1](../genes/NPM1.md)** — 4-bp insertion detected via PCR; enriched for Promonocyte-like state; lower PEAR1 expression in NPM1-mutant cases; PIK-inhibitor response is conditional on cell-state context.
- **[RUNX1](../genes/RUNX1.md)** — dual correlation with HSC-like and cDC-like states ("co-opting" disparate maturation states); confers PIK-family-inhibitor sensitivity; elevated PEAR1.
- **[ASXL1](../genes/ASXL1.md)** — elevated PEAR1 expression in ASXL1-mutant AML, paralleling ASXL1's role in clonal hematopoiesis and possibly linking PEAR1 biology to cardiovascular comorbidity in aging hematopoiesis.
- **[BCOR](../genes/BCOR.md), [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md)** — enriched in HSC-like state.
- **[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md)** ("RAS") — correlated with Monocyte-like state; conferred greater sensitivity to STE7/MEK inhibitors (expected) and to CDK-family inhibitors (unexpected).
- **[STAG2](../genes/STAG2.md)** — confers greater sensitivity to PIK-family inhibitors.
- **[SRSF2](../genes/SRSF2.md), [GATA2](../genes/GATA2.md), [MECOM](../genes/MECOM.md)** (incl. GATA2-MECOM fusion / inv(3)/t(3;3)) — elevated PEAR1 expression in these poor-prognosis genetic subsets.
- **[CEBPA](../genes/CEBPA.md)** — lower PEAR1 expression in cases with this favorable-prognosis marker; bi-allelic [CEBPA](../genes/CEBPA.md) manually adjudicated for ELN risk calling.
- **[BCL2](../genes/BCL2.md)** — pathway target of [venetoclax](../drugs/venetoclax.md), one of two extreme outlier drugs in cell-state response analysis (the other being [panobinostat](../drugs/panobinostat.md)).
- **[IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) / [IDH2](../genes/IDH2.md)** — discussed as targets of [ivosidenib](../drugs/ivosidenib.md) and [enasidenib](../drugs/enasidenib.md) in the AML therapeutic landscape (background, not a primary finding here).
- **[CD33](../genes/CD33.md)** — antigen of gemtuzumab ozogamicin (background context).
- **[PYCARD](../genes/PYCARD.md), [MAPK8](../genes/MAPK8.md)** — [PYCARD](../genes/PYCARD.md) was co-nominated with PEAR1 in a prior TCGA-LAML immune prognostic signature (Dao et al., 2021); [MAPK8](../genes/MAPK8.md) (JNK) is part of the PEAR1-downstream phagocytic signaling axis with [SYK](../genes/SYK.md) and SRC-family kinases (mechanistic background).

## Clinical implications

- **Prognostic biomarker:** PEAR1 expression is proposed as a single-gene prognostic biomarker for OS in AML, performing equivalently to the LSC17 signature and carrying prognostic value *independent of ELN risk* in young (<45 y) patients ([PMID:35868306](../papers/35868306.md)).
- **Therapeutic target:** PEAR1 is a type-I transmembrane signaling receptor with confirmed surface expression on AML blasts, raising the possibility of small-molecule and antibody-based targeting. PEAR1-null mice retain platelet function, suggesting a potential therapeutic window.
- **Cell-state as a treatment-stratification axis:** Because most drugs and 18/25 drug families show cell-state-biased response, AML cell-maturation state should be assessed as a confounder/modifier of drug response in clinical evaluation — the authors argue cell-type adjustment should be "as routine as population-substructure adjustment."
- **Refined precision-medicine guidance:** Cell-state context refines existing mutation–drug rules — e.g., FLT3-ITD predicts sorafenib sensitivity *only* when the tumor is Progenitor-like-biased; Monocyte-like FLT3-ITD AML resists sorafenib.
- **Unexpected pharmacogenomic associations:** TP53/STAG2/RUNX1-mutant AML — typically chemotherapy-refractory — shows greater ex vivo sensitivity to PIK-family inhibitors; N/KRAS-mutant AML shows greater sensitivity to CDK-family inhibitors.

## Limitations & open questions

- The prognostic significance of PEAR1 was established in cohorts treated predominantly with **anthracycline-based regimens**; with the modern shift to venetoclax + hypomethylating-agent combinations and targeted therapies, PEAR1's prognostic value under non-anthracycline therapy remains to be demonstrated ([PMID:35868306](../papers/35868306.md)).
- Apparent **ethnic differences in PEAR1 expression** (Non-Hispanic White mean 1.395, N=359; African Heritage mean 2.205, N=13) warrant validation in larger non-Caucasian cohorts, especially given known racially-skewed PEAR1 polymorphism frequencies and reported outcome disparities for Black AML patients.
- Forced PEAR1 expression in bone-marrow or fibroblast-stromal cells was shown (Krivtsov et al., 2007) to *reduce* clonogenic myeloid colony formation — apparently counterintuitive to the association of elevated PEAR1 with worse AML outcome; the underlying biology of why elevated PEAR1 is adverse in AML is unresolved.
- The cforest survival model was restricted to **de novo AML (n=298)** because of confounding between AML type and other variables; generalization to transformed/therapy-related AML is shown for individual analyses but not for the full model.
- Whether and how PEAR1 signaling can be therapeutically interrupted without impairing platelet aggregation in patients (despite the mouse-knockout reassurance) remains to be tested clinically.
- The Waves 3+4 expansion enabled discovery of 65 additional mutation–drug associations, suggesting many additional pharmacogenomic signals in AML remain underpowered even at n=805 patients.
