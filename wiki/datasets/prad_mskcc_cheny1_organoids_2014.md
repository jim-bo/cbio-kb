# MSKCC Prostate Cancer Organoids (Gao/Chen 2014)

## Overview

Seven patient-derived 3D organoid lines (MSK-PCa1–7) established from metastatic biopsies (n=6) and one circulating-tumor-cell sample from men with advanced prostate cancer ([PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md)) at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center (IRB 90-040/06-107; Dec 2012–Nov 2013). The lines were profiled by whole-exome sequencing, RNA-seq, and 1M array-CGH. Establishment efficiency was ~15–20% from metastatic biopsies.

## Composition

- 7 organoid lines from advanced [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md); 6 from metastatic biopsies (bone, lymph node, soft tissue, pleural effusion), 1 from CTCs.
- Source biopsy pool: 32 metastasis samples (18 bone, 9 lymph node, 2 liver, 1 brain, 1 bladder, 1 pleural effusion) and 17 blood draws.
- Assays: [whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) (Agilent SureSelectXT2, ~142× mean), [rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md), [array-cgh-agilent-1m](../methods/array-cgh-agilent-1m.md).
- Reference genome: hg19.

## Notable findings derived from this cohort

- 6/7 organoid lines carry focal homozygous deletion of [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md); [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) is mutated with complete loss of function in 4/7 lines; [CHD1](../genes/CHD1.md) is homozygously deleted in 3/7 lines (MSK-PCa2, PCa4, PCa7) [PMID:25201530](../papers/25201530.md).
- MSK-PCa7 carries the first in vitro model of a [SPOP](../genes/SPOP.md) F133L substrate-recognition-domain hotspot mutation; MSK-PCa1 and MSK-PCa3 carry [TMPRSS2](../genes/TMPRSS2.md)-[ERG](../genes/ERG.md) interstitial deletion [PMID:25201530](../papers/25201530.md).
- AR-amplified MSK-PCa2 was sensitive to [enzalutamide](../drugs/enzalutamide.md) (IC50 ~50 nM) and to [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md); combined enzalutamide + everolimus significantly enhanced xenograft response over enzalutamide alone [PMID:25201530](../papers/25201530.md).
