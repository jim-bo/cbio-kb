**PMID:** 31978347 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.cell.2019.12.023 · **Journal:** Cell (2020)

## TL;DR

Nusinow and colleagues add a deep, quantitative proteomics layer to the [Cancer Cell Line Encyclopedia](../datasets/ccle_broad_2019.md) by profiling 375 cell lines across 22 lineages with TMT 10-plex tandem-mass-tag LC-MS/MS, quantifying >12,000 proteins at 1% protein-level FDR. They find that the primary axis of proteome variation is organized by coordinated expression *across* biological pathways and protein complexes, not by tissue lineage or mutation burden, and that this pattern is largely absent from matched RNA-seq. An MSI-focused analysis uncovers only ~50 differentially expressed proteins (vs. >1,000 mRNAs), enriched for surveillance complexes (mismatch repair, MRN, SKI/SKIC2-SKIC3, RQC/LTN1, H3K4 methyltransferases) and identifies [RPL22](../genes/RPL22.md) mutation as uniquely associated with these complex-level changes and SKI complex downregulation. Complex-level proteome features further nominate associations between protein-complex abundance and sensitivity to gene knockdown (e.g., MCM complex with [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md), integrins with [TEAD1](../genes/TEAD1.md), SUMO E1 with [WRN](../genes/WRN.md) and [PCSK7](../genes/PCSK7.md) mutation). [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Cohort & data

- 375 CCLE cell lines selected across 22 tissue lineages, dominated by solid-organ lines; cell-line provenance and annotations follow the CCLE release ([cellline_ccle_broad](../datasets/cellline_ccle_broad.md); [ccle_broad_2019](../datasets/ccle_broad_2019.md)). NCI60 lines were largely excluded in favor of less-characterized lines, and lines overlapping with DRIVE and Achilles screens were emphasized. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- Lines authenticated by SNP-based DNA fingerprinting prior to mass spectrometry. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- Assay: [TMT 10-plex tandem-mass-tag proteomics](../methods/tmt-proteomics.md) with MS3/SPS quantification on Thermo Orbitrap Fusion / Fusion Lumos instruments; 42 ten-plexes × 12 fractions = 504 LC-MS runs (~4 hr per cell line, [LC-MS/MS proteomics](../methods/lc-ms-ms-proteomics.md)). A bridge channel built from 11 pooled lines (NCIH446, DMS79, NCIH460, DMS53, NCIH69, HCC1954, CAMA1, KYSE180, NMCG1, UACC257, AU565) was always labeled with the 131 reagent for cross-batch normalization. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- Database search: Sequest against UniProt human (SwissProt + TrEMBL, Feb 2014) with target-decoy LDA filtering to 1% peptide FDR and Picked-FDR-based 1% protein-level FDR across the 504-run dataset. >12,000 proteins quantified across all samples; >9,000 in a majority. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- Companion data integrated from the broader CCLE: RNA-seq, histone modification profiling, mutation calls, MSI status (as inferred by Ghandi et al., 2019), CRISPR sensitivity from Project Achilles/Avana (depmap.org 18Q3), [shRNA sensitivity from Project DRIVE](../methods/shrna-rnai-screen.md), and CTD2 drug sensitivity. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- Analyses run primarily in R, with [GSEA (fgsea)](../methods/gsea.md), PCA, ICA, hierarchical clustering (Ward / Euclidean), [elastic-net](../methods/elastic-net.md) feature selection, and FDR estimation via fdrtool. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Key findings

- **Replicate quality.** Mean replicate-replicate correlation 0.8 vs. mean cell-line/cell-line correlation −0.05 (p < 2e-16), median CV of 60% between biological replicates within a line. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- **Tissue lineage explains only part of proteome structure.** Hierarchical clustering by protein has mean Gini purity 0.46 (1.0 = perfect tissue-based clustering); RNA clustering 0.6. Skin and haematopoetic/lymphoid lineages cluster most tightly. Haematopoetic/lymphoid lines were removed before downstream PCA. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- **Pathway co-expression — not tissue or mutation — dominates PC1.** 75% of proteins are significantly correlated with PC1 at 1% FDR. >200 MSigDB pathways are enriched along PC1 of the protein data versus far fewer along PC1 of the matched RNA-seq. The two anticorrelated PC1 poles align with (i) growth-factor signaling (MAPK, insulin receptor), glycolysis, nucleotide metabolism, cell division and (ii) cell-cell/cell-matrix adhesion, [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) and p53 signaling, and oxidative phosphorylation — the latter recapitulating the Warburg switch. Total mutational burden does *not* explain PC1. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- **RNA-protein correlation per gene.** Per-gene Pearson correlation between mRNA and protein averages just under 0.5, with extremes such as [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) (good RNA proxy) and [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) (poor RNA proxy). [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- **EMT-like axis is proteome-wide.** ~50% of proteins are positively or negatively correlated with [EPCAM](../genes/EPCAM.md) (top PC1 loading), ~33% with [VIM](../genes/VIM.md); roughly half of these correlations are not mirrored in mRNA. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- **Expression correlation network.** Solid-organ network: 3,777 proteins, 41,600 edges at 1% FDR (~40,000 positive). ~5,500 edges shared with an RNA-seq correlation network (~195,000 edges, ~9,400 genes); >6,300 edges annotated by protein-protein interaction / shared complex membership (BioPlex 2.0, BioGRID, CORUM); ~3,500 by shared subcellular localization; >7,900 by shared pathway membership — the largest annotated category. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- **MSI proteome buffering.** mRNA shows >1,000 up/downregulated genes in MSI cells; protein shows only **50** differentially expressed proteins (30 shared with mRNA), reflecting strong posttranscriptional buffering. **24 of those 50 proteins** are members of surveillance complexes for DNA, RNA, and nascent-protein quality control. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- **MSI-associated complex downregulation.** Mismatch repair (MutL: [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md), [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md); MutS: [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md)), MRN, the SKI complex helicase [SKIC2](../genes/SKIC2.md) (SKIV2L) and partner [SKIC3](../genes/SKIC3.md) (TTC37), the RQC E3 ligase [LTN1](../genes/LTN1.md), ribosomal accessory proteins [RPL22](../genes/RPL22.md) / [RPL22L1](../genes/RPL22L1.md), heat-shock proteins [HSPA4L](../genes/HSPA4L.md) / [DNAJB14](../genes/DNAJB14.md) / [HSPH1](../genes/HSPH1.md) (HSP110), and H3K4 methyltransferases [KMT2B](../genes/KMT2B.md) / [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) / [SETD1B](../genes/SETD1B.md). The RQC co-factor [TCF25](../genes/TCF25.md) was upregulated. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- **MutL- vs. MutS-correlated arms.** In MSI lines, MutL components ([MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md), [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md)) and HMT complex members are best fit by an MSI-status-only linear model (independent of total mutation burden); the MutS-correlated cluster — including [SKIC2](../genes/SKIC2.md), [SKIC3](../genes/SKIC3.md), [LTN1](../genes/LTN1.md), [RPL22](../genes/RPL22.md), [RPL22L1](../genes/RPL22L1.md) — improves under a model that combines MSI status with mutation burden. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- **H3K4 methylation drop in MSI.** Bulk H3K4me1 and H3K4me2 are significantly reduced in MSI cell lines and the effect is MSI-specific (no effect from total mutation burden alone), consistent with downregulation of the H3K4 methyltransferases [KMT2B](../genes/KMT2B.md), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), [SETD1B](../genes/SETD1B.md). [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- **[RPL22](../genes/RPL22.md) mutation uniquely tracks the MSI complex signature.** Among per-gene mutation regressions across solid-organ lines, [RPL22](../genes/RPL22.md) mutation is uniquely associated with altered protein levels in MSI-associated complexes (mismatch repair, SKI complex, HMTs). RPL22 expression in MSI cells clusters with the MutS arm. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- **[WRN](../genes/WRN.md) synthetic lethality predictors.** [WRN](../genes/WRN.md)-knockdown sensitivity in DRIVE is associated with protein abundance of mismatch repair components *and* the SKI proteins [SKIC2](../genes/SKIC2.md) / [SKIC3](../genes/SKIC3.md), strengthening the link between MSI and SKI downregulation. [SKIC2](../genes/SKIC2.md) was the only protein commonly predictive of both WRN and [RPL22L1](../genes/RPL22L1.md) knockdown sensitivity. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- **Complex-level associations with knockdown sensitivity.** Filtering DRIVE knockdowns with strong, variable effects, complexes co-vary with sensitivities to multiple genes: [ATR](../genes/ATR.md) with the ATR/ATRIP complex ([ATRIP](../genes/ATRIP.md)) and the RPA complex ([RPA1](../genes/RPA1.md)); [TEAD1](../genes/TEAD1.md) with integrin complexes containing [ITGB1](../genes/ITGB1.md) (consistent with the [YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md)/Hippo–integrin axis); [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md), and [CDKN1A](../genes/CDKN1A.md) with the Chromosomal Passenger Complex and CDK-containing complexes; [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) uniquely with MCM-complex abundance; [WRN](../genes/WRN.md) with the SUMO E1 heterodimer ([SAE1](../genes/SAE1.md) / [UBA2](../genes/UBA2.md)). [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- **Mutation-to-complex map.** [PCSK7](../genes/PCSK7.md) mutation is uniquely associated with SUMO E1 heterodimer protein levels; [RPL22](../genes/RPL22.md) mutation again recovers the mismatch repair and SKI complexes (plus [KDR](../genes/KDR.md)). [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Genes & alterations

- [RPL22](../genes/RPL22.md) — mutation hotspot of MSI endometrial cancers (Novetsky et al., 2013); in CCLE, RPL22 mutation is uniquely associated with proteome-level changes in MSI-associated complexes and its expression clusters with the MutS arm (combined MSI + mutation-burden model). [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- [RPL22L1](../genes/RPL22L1.md) — derepressed upon RPL22 loss; downregulated in MSI; sensitivity to RPL22L1 knockdown in MSI cells is best predicted by [SKIC2](../genes/SKIC2.md) protein abundance, suggesting an association with the SKI complex / ribosome rather than with MSI causation per se. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- [SKIC2](../genes/SKIC2.md) (SKIV2L) and [SKIC3](../genes/SKIC3.md) (TTC37) — SKI cytoplasmic ribosome-associated RNA-surveillance complex; both proteins are downregulated in MSI cell lines and are associated with [WRN](../genes/WRN.md)-knockdown sensitivity. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- [LTN1](../genes/LTN1.md) — RQC E3 ubiquitin ligase that targets nascent dysfunctional proteins; downregulated in MSI lines; its co-complex partner [TCF25](../genes/TCF25.md) is upregulated. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md), [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md) (MutL) and [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md) (MutS) — mismatch repair components downregulated in MSI; their protein abundance predicts [WRN](../genes/WRN.md) knockdown sensitivity. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- [HSPA4L](../genes/HSPA4L.md), [DNAJB14](../genes/DNAJB14.md), [HSPH1](../genes/HSPH1.md) — heat-shock proteins downregulated in MSI lines; [HSPH1](../genes/HSPH1.md) (HSP110) has an established role in MSI disease. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- [KMT2B](../genes/KMT2B.md), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md), [SETD1B](../genes/SETD1B.md) — H3K4 methyltransferases (SET-domain) downregulated in MSI cells; H3K4me1 and H3K4me2 marks correspondingly reduced. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — mutation associated with broad changes in cell-motility and adhesion-protein levels across solid-organ lines. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — mutation associated with a small set of p53-pathway and DNA-damage-response proteins; TP53/[MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md)/[CDKN1A](../genes/CDKN1A.md) knockdown sensitivities co-vary with cell-division-related complexes. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) — knockdown sensitivity uniquely associated with MCM-complex protein levels, hinting at a p53-independent function. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- [MDM4](../genes/MDM4.md) — splicing tied to [RPL22L1](../genes/RPL22L1.md) levels (cross-reference to Ghandi et al., 2019). [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- [WRN](../genes/WRN.md) — synthetic lethal vulnerability in MSI; sensitivity predicted by mismatch-repair and SKI-complex protein levels and by SUMO E1 ([SAE1](../genes/SAE1.md) / [UBA2](../genes/UBA2.md)) abundance. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- [PCSK7](../genes/PCSK7.md) — serine endopeptidase whose mutation is uniquely associated with SUMO E1 heterodimer protein levels; mechanism unknown. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- [TEAD1](../genes/TEAD1.md) — knockdown sensitivity associated with integrin complexes containing [ITGB1](../genes/ITGB1.md); consistent with the [YAP1](../genes/YAP1.md)/Hippo–mechanosensing pathway. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- [ATR](../genes/ATR.md) — knockdown sensitivity tracks the ATR/[ATRIP](../genes/ATRIP.md) and [RPA1](../genes/RPA1.md)-containing RPA complexes. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- [EPCAM](../genes/EPCAM.md), [VIM](../genes/VIM.md) — canonical epithelial / mesenchymal markers anchoring a proteome-wide EMT-like axis on PC1. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) (HER2) — upregulated in breast-derived lines; protein abundance largely predicted by [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) copy number. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — contrasting RNA-protein correlations (high for [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md), low for [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)). EGFR-targeting drugs constituted the largest subset of compounds whose sensitivity correlated with cell-line PC1 projection. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- [PIK3CB](../genes/PIK3CB.md), [ZEB2](../genes/ZEB2.md) — among CRISPR knockdowns whose sensitivities correlate with PC1; [PIK3CB](../genes/PIK3CB.md)-targeting drugs also tracked PC1. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Clinical implications

- The MSI proteome signature (mismatch repair + SKI/RQC + H3K4 HMTs) and its overlap with [WRN](../genes/WRN.md) synthetic lethality offer protein-level biomarkers that may complement mRNA- and DNA-based MSI calls for selecting patients amenable to WRN-directed therapy or other MSI-targeted strategies. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- Tight buffering of transcriptomic changes by the proteome implies that disrupting posttranscriptional buffering of mutation-driven transcriptomic perturbations may itself be a therapeutic vulnerability — explicitly proposed by the authors as a future direction. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- Protein-complex abundance is a better cross-line predictor of gene-knockdown sensitivity than any single transcript or mutation in several cases (e.g., MCM complex with [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md), integrins with [TEAD1](../genes/TEAD1.md)), supporting the use of proteomic biomarkers in target prioritization beyond mutation calls. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- The data set itself is positioned as a community resource hosted at gygi.med.harvard.edu/publications/ccle and depmap.org for direct use in target discovery and biomarker work. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)

## Limitations & open questions

- **Cell lines, not tumors.** Findings derive entirely from cultured CCLE cell lines; how proteome-level pathway coordination and MSI buffering generalize to primary tumors is not directly tested here. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- **Missing values from multiplexing.** Bridge-normalization across 42 TMT 10-plexes leaves missing values; authors deliberately avoided imputation, which limits some downstream analyses. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- **Modest MSI sample size.** Only the dominant form of hypermutation (MSI) is well-powered in CCLE; other hypermutation modes (UV, smoking, APOBEC) are noted but not deeply dissected. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- **Mechanism gaps.** The mechanistic link between [RPL22](../genes/RPL22.md) mutation, SKI complex downregulation, and MSI is not resolved; the [PCSK7](../genes/PCSK7.md)–SUMO E1 association lacks known biology. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- **Annotation approach.** The authors note their correlation-network annotation uses simple methods and that Bayes nets / partial correlations could yield stronger mechanistic inference. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- **Replicates concentrated in early batches.** Only the first two TMT 10-plexes (18 lines) were run in biological triplicate, so per-line technical reproducibility is best characterized for a subset of the cohort. [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
