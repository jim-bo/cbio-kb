# ITGB4

## Overview

ITGB4 (integrin beta-4) is a cell adhesion receptor involved in hemidesmosomes and various signaling pathways. In [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md), ITGB4 interacts with [CD274](../genes/CD274.md) (PD-L1) to activate AKT/mTOR signaling and induce lipid accumulation.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- ITGB4 binds CD274 (PD-L1) to activate AKT/mTOR and induce lipid accumulation in HCC; part of the PD-L1/EGFR/ITGB4 signaling axis linking immune checkpoint to lipid metabolic reprogramming [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) — ITGB4 is part of the CD274/EGFR/ITGB4 axis driving lipid accumulation and AKT/mTOR activation [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-signals with CD274 (PD-L1) and [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) in HCC lipid reprogramming [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- ITGB4 is implicated in the PD-L1 signaling axis; disrupting this axis may resensitize HCC to ICIs [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Open questions

- Whether ITGB4 expression levels predict ICI response in HCC is not established.
