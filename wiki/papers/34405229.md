**PMID:** 34405229 · **DOI:** [10.1093/jnci/djab124](https://doi.org/10.1093/jnci/djab124) · **Journal:** JNCI: Journal of the National Cancer Institute (2021)

## TL;DR

Cercek et al. assembled the largest single-institution cohort comparing early-onset colorectal cancer (EO-CRC, n=759: 151 patients aged 35 and younger, 608 aged 36-49) with average-onset CRC (AO-CRC, n=687, aged 50+) at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center (MSK). After excluding hereditary syndromes, inflammatory bowel disease, and mismatch-repair–deficient tumors, they profiled tumors using [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) (341- to 468-gene panel) and performed prospective secondary germline analysis using a 76- to 88-gene panel. EO-CRC was more frequently left-sided (>80%) and presented more often with rectal bleeding and abdominal pain, but after adjusting for tumor sidedness, somatic gene/pathway alteration frequencies, response to first-line chemotherapy, and overall survival in metastatic microsatellite-stable disease were statistically indistinguishable from AO-CRC. Pathogenic/likely pathogenic germline variants were enriched in the youngest cohort (23.3% at age ≤35 vs 14.1% in AO; P=.01), driven largely by high-penetrance CRC predisposition genes including Lynch syndrome mismatch-repair genes and [APC](../genes/APC.md). The authors conclude that aggressive treatment regimens based solely on age at CRC diagnosis are not warranted.

## Cohort & data

- **EO-CRC cohort:** 759 patients aged <50 years diagnosed with CRC at MSK between January 2014 and June 2019, stratified into ≤35 years (n=151) and 36-49 years (n=608).
- **AO-CRC comparator:** 687 patients aged ≥50 years treated at MSK during a similar timeframe with identically annotated clinical and genomic data ([PMID:34405229](../papers/34405229.md)).
- **Tumor types:** Colon adenocarcinoma ([COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md)) and rectal adenocarcinoma ([READ](../cancer_types/READ.md)).
- **Dataset:** [crc_eo_2020](../datasets/crc_eo_2020.md) (cBioPortal study summary).
- **Somatic profiling:** [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) 341- to 468-gene next-generation sequencing assay ([IMPACT341](../methods/IMPACT341.md) / [IMPACT468](../methods/IMPACT468.md)) on formalin-fixed paraffin-embedded tumor with matched normal blood. Detects mutations, small indels, copy-number alterations, [select](../methods/select.md) structural rearrangements, and MSI. Oncogenic variants filtered using [OncoKB](../methods/oncokb-annotation.md).
- **Germline profiling:** Prospective secondary analysis on blood-derived DNA using a 76-gene (n=351) or 88-gene (n=730) MSK-IMPACT panel, including all cancer-predisposing genes per ACMG guidelines ([acmg-variant-classification](../methods/acmg-variant-classification.md)).
- **Exclusions for somatic/clinical comparison:** Known CRC predisposition syndromes, inflammatory bowel disease, and mismatch-repair–deficient tumors (these patients were retained in germline analysis).
- **Statistics:** 2-sided Fisher exact test (1-sided for germline comparisons), Wilcoxon test for continuous variables, [Kaplan-Meier](../methods/kaplan-meier.md) survival with log-rank test, multivariate [Cox proportional hazards](../methods/cox-proportional-hazards.md) models, Benjamini-Hochberg multiple-testing correction (q<0.1).

## Key findings

- **Tumor sidedness:** Left-sided tumors were markedly more common in EO-CRC than AO-CRC (≤35 years = 80.8%; 36-49 years = 83.7%; AO = 63.9%; P<.001 for both comparisons). Rectal cancer alone accounted for 33.7% of both EO cohorts vs 22.6% of AO (P=.007 and P<.001).
- **Presenting symptoms:** Rectal bleeding (≤35 = 41.1%; 36-49 = 41.0%; AO = 25.9%; P=.001 and P<.001) and abdominal pain (≤35 = 37.1%; 36-49 = 34.0%; AO = 26.8%; P=.01 and P=.005) were enriched in EO-CRC; anemia was more common in AO-CRC (AO = 14.6%; ≤35 = 2.0%; 36-49 = 3.6%; P<.001 for both).
- **Comorbidity / BMI:** Hypertension, diabetes, smoking history, and obesity all progressively increased with age cohort (P<.001 for both EO vs AO comparisons). Median BMI was 24.3 in ≤35 vs 27.0 in AO (P<.001).
- **Stage at diagnosis:** Not statistically different between cohorts; most patients in all groups presented with stage IV disease (reflecting the next-generation-sequencing referral population).
- **Somatic alteration frequencies (MSS tumors only; ≤35 n=142, 36-49 n=588, AO n=626):** Most common alterations in EO-CRC were [APC](../genes/APC.md) (78.7%), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (82.1%), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) (42.5%), [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) (15.5%), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) (14.9%), [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md) (8.9%), [SOX9](../genes/SOX9.md) (7.7%), [TCF7L2](../genes/TCF7L2.md) (7.2%), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) (5.5%).
- **Initial age-stratified differences disappear after sidedness adjustment:** [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) alterations were initially enriched in 36-49 vs AO (82.1% vs 73.5%; q=0.1), and RTK-RAS pathway alterations were less frequent in 36-49 vs AO (64.6% vs 71.7%; q=0.04). After adjustment for tumor location, no statistically significant gene-level or pathway-level differences remained between any cohorts.
- **No difference in genome-wide measures:** Tumor mutational burden, fraction of genome altered, whole-genome duplication, and loss of heterozygosity (LOH) did not differ between cohorts.
- **Metastatic chemotherapy response and survival equivalent:** Among metastatic MSS patients (≤35 n=110; 36-49 n=455; AO n=574), most received fluoropyrimidine + [oxaliplatin](../drugs/oxaliplatin.md) ± [bevacizumab](../drugs/bevacizumab.md) as first-line therapy ([fluorouracil](../drugs/fluorouracil.md), [oxaliplatin](../drugs/oxaliplatin.md), [bevacizumab](../drugs/bevacizumab.md)). Radiographic response (≤35 = 71.9%; 36-49 = 61.8%; AO = 66.5%; P=.36 and P=.70) and median overall survival (≤35 = 46.9 months; 36-49 = 56.4 months; AO = 54.5 months; ≤35 vs AO P=.90; 36-49 vs AO P=.17) did not differ.
- **Multivariate survival analysis:** After adjustment for metastasectomy, tumor grade, [APC](../genes/APC.md)/20q amplification, [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), and [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) alterations, age was not statistically significantly associated with outcome. A non-significant trend toward worse outcomes in age ≤35 was noted (HR=1.43, 95% CI 0.99-2.07; P=.06).
- **Germline P/LP variant prevalence:** 17.5% overall in EO-CRC. Highest in the ≤35 cohort at 23.3% vs 14.1% in AO (P=.01), driven by enrichment of high-penetrance gene variants (12.1% vs 5.9%; P=.02). 92.8% (13/14) of high-penetrance carriers harbored variants in CRC-associated predisposition genes: mismatch-repair genes (n=9), [APC](../genes/APC.md) (n=3), and [POLD1](../genes/POLD1.md) (n=1).
- **Lynch syndrome:** Most common cancer predisposition syndrome, accounting for 5.5% of all EO-CRCs.
- **Biallelic inactivation:** Overall 36% of P/LP germline mutations exhibited biallelic inactivation (somatic mutation or LOH). The ≤35 cohort had the highest biallelic inactivation rate (51.7% vs 32.6% in AO; P=.04), supporting these germline events as drivers of carcinogenesis in the youngest patients.

## Genes & alterations

- [APC](../genes/APC.md) — Truncating mutations in 78.7% of EO-CRC MSS tumors; no statistically significant age-cohort difference after sidedness adjustment. Germline P/LP variants in 3 of the 14 high-penetrance carriers in EO-CRC (familial adenomatous polyposis). [APC](../genes/APC.md) alteration or 20q amplification associated with improved survival on univariate analysis (HR=0.64, 95% CI 0.44-0.93; P=.02).
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — Altered in 82% of EO MSS tumors vs 73% in AO; initial enrichment in 36-49 vs AO (q=0.1) lost statistical significance after adjustment for tumor sidedness. Likely reflects the left-sided predominance of EO-CRC.
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — Mutated in 42.5% of EO-CRC; frequencies similar across age cohorts (≤35 = 46%; 36-49 = 42%; AO = 48%). RTK-RAS pathway alterations initially appeared less frequent in 36-49 vs AO (64.6% vs 71.7%; q=0.04) but were not statistically different after sidedness adjustment.
- [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) — Altered in ~15-19% across cohorts; [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) alteration statistically significantly associated with survival on univariate analysis (HR=1.46, 95% CI 1.08-1.94; P=.01).
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — Altered in ~13-17% across cohorts; no age-cohort difference.
- [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md), [SOX9](../genes/SOX9.md), [TCF7L2](../genes/TCF7L2.md) — Frequently altered (7-10%); no statistically significant age-cohort differences.
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — Mutated in 4-8% of MSS EO/AO tumors. Contrary to a prior report of lower [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E in EO-CRC, no statistically significant difference remained after adjustment for MSI status and tumor sidedness. BRAF alteration trended toward worse survival on multivariate analysis (HR=1.41, 95% CI 0.96-2.07; P=.08).
- [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) — Defined a small ultramutated subgroup (≤35: 2.6%; 36-49: 0.7%; AO: 0.7%; not statistically significantly different).
- [POLD1](../genes/POLD1.md) — Germline P/LP variant identified in 1 EO-CRC high-penetrance carrier.
- [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md), [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md), [PMS2](../genes/PMS2.md) — Germline Lynch syndrome mismatch-repair gene variants accounted for 5.5% of all EO-CRCs and 9 of 14 high-penetrance carriers in EO-CRC.
- [MUTYH](../genes/MUTYH.md), [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md), [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md), [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [PALB2](../genes/PALB2.md) — Among the moderate- and low-penetrance germline genes interrogated; specific frequencies shown in Figure 4 of the paper. Rates of moderate/low-penetrance gene mutations were similar between EO and AO after adjustment for the number of genes tested.

## Clinical implications

- **No routine treatment intensification by age alone:** Equivalent first-line chemotherapy response and overall survival in metastatic MSS disease across EO and AO cohorts argue against escalating chemotherapy intensity solely on the basis of young age at CRC diagnosis.
- **Symptom-based clinical suspicion:** Rectal bleeding and abdominal pain in young adults should prompt early colonoscopic evaluation given the >80% left-sided distribution of EO-CRC and the average diagnostic delays previously reported in this population.
- **Germline testing in very early-onset disease:** 23.3% pathogenic/likely pathogenic germline variant prevalence in patients aged ≤35 (vs 14.1% in AO) — with 12.1% in high-penetrance genes — supports a low threshold for multigene panel germline testing in this very early-onset population, beyond Lynch syndrome alone. APC, mismatch-repair genes, and [POLD1](../genes/POLD1.md) dominated the high-penetrance findings.
- **Biological framing:** The high biallelic-inactivation rate (51.7%) of germline P/LP variants in the ≤35 cohort suggests these germline events are bona fide carcinogenic drivers in very-early-onset disease rather than incidental findings.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Retrospective, single-institution design** at a tertiary cancer center, with attendant referral and demographic biases. The AO comparator was matched only on institution and timeframe, not on age-/population-controlled exposures.
- **Stage-IV–enriched cohort** reflects the population undergoing next-generation sequencing at MSK; conclusions about earlier-stage EO-CRC biology are necessarily indirect.
- **Hereditary cases excluded from somatic/clinical analyses** by design, so the comparison addresses sporadic EO-CRC vs sporadic AO-CRC — different conclusions might apply to syndromic patients.
- **MSS-only somatic genomic analysis** removes the well-known Lynch-driven MSI enrichment in EO-CRC but also removes a biologically distinct subset; the paper explicitly limits its somatic claims to MSS disease.
- **Etiology unresolved:** The authors did not compare with age- and population-matched controls and therefore cannot adjudicate environmental, microbial, or lifestyle drivers of the rising EO-CRC incidence; they call for further research on diverse populations and modifiable risk factors.
- **Trend toward worse outcomes in age ≤35** (HR=1.43, 95% CI 0.99-2.07; P=.06) is not statistically significant but may be hypothesis-generating in larger cohorts.
- **Germline panel composition changed (76- vs 88-gene)** during the study window; the authors adjust for number of genes tested but residual gene-content heterogeneity remains.
