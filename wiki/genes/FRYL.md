# FRYL

## Overview

FRYL (FRY like transcription coactivator) is a large scaffold protein implicated in chromatin regulation. It is known as a rare [KMT2A](../genes/KMT2A.md) (MLL) translocation partner in acute myeloid leukemia. KMT2A-FRYL fusions have been observed in therapy-related myeloid neoplasms and represent a novel fusion partner identified through RNA capture-based surveillance of pediatric cancer survivors.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Identified as a novel KMT2A translocation partner in pediatric neuroblastoma patients who developed therapy-related AML/MDS; KMT2A-FRYL fusions detected by RNA capture in pre-transformation bone marrow surveillance [PMID:35078859](../papers/35078859.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Therapy-related AML/MDS in pediatric neuroblastoma survivors: KMT2A-FRYL fusion detected as a pre-transformation event, associated with topoisomerase-II inhibitor exposure [PMID:35078859](../papers/35078859.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- KMT2A rearrangements (including FRYL fusions) cluster with topoisomerase-II exposure history; co-occur in patient populations with KMT2A-ELL, KMT2A-AFF1, KMT2A-GAS7, and novel KMT2A-PRDM10/DDX6 fusions [PMID:35078859](../papers/35078859.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- KMT2A-FRYL fusions detected a median of 15 months before clinical leukemia diagnosis by RNA capture-based bone marrow surveillance in pediatric neuroblastoma patients [PMID:35078859](../papers/35078859.md)

## Open questions

- FRYL is a novel KMT2A partner whose leukemogenic mechanism and prognosis relative to established partners ([ELL](../genes/ELL.md), [AFF1](../genes/AFF1.md)) remain poorly characterized [PMID:35078859](../papers/35078859.md)
