**PMID:** 23778141 · **DOI:** 10.1172/JCI67201 · **Journal:** J Clin Invest (2013)

## TL;DR

Stephens et al. performed whole-exome sequencing on 24 adenoid cystic carcinoma (ACC) tumors with matched normals, plus extension sequencing of [SPEN](../genes/SPEN.md) (42 cases) and [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) (25 cases). Beyond the known [MYB](../genes/MYB.md)–[NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) fusion that defines most ACC, they identified mutations in known cancer genes ([PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md), [SUFU](../genes/SUFU.md), [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md), [CYLD](../genes/CYLD.md)), implicated chromatin-remodeling deregulation in 12/24 cases, found [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md)/[NOTCH2](../genes/NOTCH2.md) mutations in 3 cases, nominated [SPEN](../genes/SPEN.md) as a novel ACC cancer gene (truncating mutations in 5 cases, enriched in solid histology), and discovered 3 likely activating [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) mutations that nominate FGFR inhibition (e.g. [dovitinib](../drugs/dovitinib.md)) as a therapeutic avenue.

## Cohort & data

- 24 [ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md) cases — 23 pretreatment primary tumors plus 1 local-regional lymph-node metastasis — with matched normal salivary-gland parenchyma [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).
- Histology: roughly equal mixture of cribriform and predominantly solid forms; 19/24 scored positive for [MYB](../genes/MYB.md) activation [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).
- Solution-phase exome capture + next-generation sequencing of the coding exome ([whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md)) with somatic-mutation validation; SNP-array copy-number profiling on the Affymetrix SNP6.0 platform ([affymetrix-snp6](../methods/affymetrix-snp6.md)) analyzed with ASCAT v2.1 [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).
- MYB-NFIB fusion status determined by fusion-transcript sequencing, qRT-PCR, [FISH](../methods/fish.md), and 3′ RACE [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).
- Extension cohorts: targeted [sanger-sequencing](../methods/sanger-sequencing.md) of [SPEN](../genes/SPEN.md) across an additional 42 ACC cases and of [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) across an additional 25 ACC cases [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).
- Dataset deposited as [acyc_sanger_2013](../datasets/acyc_sanger_2013.md); sequence data in EGA (EGA00001064049); SNP6 data in ArrayExpress E-MTAB-1141; expression data in ArrayExpress E-MTAB-1397 [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).

## Key findings

- 312 somatic mutations across 24 exomes (range 2–35 per tumor; mean 13/exome) — lower than most adult solid tumors. Breakdown: 182 missense, 59 silent, 14 nonsense, 45 frameshift, 6 in-frame indels, 6 essential splice site [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).
- Neither histology subtype nor [MYB](../genes/MYB.md) status was a significant predictor of mutation burden (generalized linear model, negative binomial; P = 0.34 for histology, P = 0.28 for [MYB](../genes/MYB.md)) [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).
- Recurrent copy-number losses observed at 1p36, 6q, 9p, and 12q, consistent with prior cytogenetic work; many MYB-positive cases also showed breakpoints/copy-number changes at the MYB and [NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) loci that likely underlie reported 6/9 involvement [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).
- Genes involved in chromatin biology were mutated in 12/24 (50%) cases, including [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [ARID1B](../genes/ARID1B.md), [ARID5B](../genes/ARID5B.md), [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md), [EP300](../genes/EP300.md), [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md), [KDM5A](../genes/KDM5A.md), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) (MLL3), [SMARCA2](../genes/SMARCA2.md), [CHD2](../genes/CHD2.md), and [BRD2](../genes/BRD2.md) [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).
- [SPEN](../genes/SPEN.md) nominated as a new cancer gene in ACC: 6 truncating mutations in 5 of 24 discovery cases (all solid histology), plus 2 additional truncating mutations (p.R1403*, p.Q3355*) in solid-histology cases on extension of 42 cases. All truncations fall before the SPOC domain, but no consistent LOH was observed — arguing against a simple loss-of-function mechanism [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).
- [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) missense (p.F1702S) and frameshift (p.Y550fs*81) in 2 cases; [NOTCH2](../genes/NOTCH2.md) compound truncating mutations (p.Q2308fs*5, p.E2420*) in a single case (PD3189), which also carried 2 [SPEN](../genes/SPEN.md) truncations — together implicating NOTCH-pathway deregulation [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).
- Three likely activating [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) mutations identified across discovery+extension: p.Y376C (identical to Beare-Stevenson cutis gyrata syndrome germline allele and to recurrent endometrial/ovarian carcinoma somatic mutations), an in-frame deletion p.I389_V393>M in the transmembrane domain, and p.K642R in the kinase domain (identical to the Pfeiffer syndrome germline allele) [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).

## Genes & alterations

- [MYB](../genes/MYB.md) — activation via t(6;9) MYB-NFIB fusion in 19/24 cases; copy-number breakpoints at MYB locus on SNP6 in many cases [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).
- [NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) — translocation partner of MYB; breakpoints at NFIB locus in MYB-activated cases [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).
- [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) — canonical activating hotspot p.H1047L [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).
- [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) — missense p.R337C in the kinase domain; same residue recurrently mutated (R337S/H/C) in colorectal cancer and B-CLL, supporting oncogenicity [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — truncating frameshift mutation; 3 additional cases had LOH encompassing [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) without nearby NFIB involvement, suggesting a CDKN2A role independent of the fusion event [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).
- [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md), [SUFU](../genes/SUFU.md), [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md), [CYLD](../genes/CYLD.md) — truncating mutations in known cancer/tumor-suppressor genes [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).
- [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md) — missense p.F1702S (in the region targeted by T-ALL activating mutations) and frameshift p.Y550fs*81 (upstream of reported truncating activations); functional direction (activating vs tumor-suppressor like HNSCC) not resolvable from mutation pattern alone [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).
- [NOTCH2](../genes/NOTCH2.md) — compound heterozygous truncations p.Q2308fs*5 and p.E2420* (PD3189), reminiscent of activating mutations seen in Hajdu-Cheney syndrome [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).
- [SPEN](../genes/SPEN.md) — 8 total truncating mutations (6 in discovery, 2 in extension), all premature stops before the SPOC domain; no clear LOH; co-occurring with [NOTCH2](../genes/NOTCH2.md) truncations in PD3189 — implicates NOTCH-pathway abrogation [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).
- [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) — p.Y376C, p.I389_V393>M (transmembrane in-frame deletion), and p.K642R (kinase domain); all paralogous to known activating germline craniosynostosis alleles or recurrent somatic activating alleles in endometrial/ovarian cancer [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).
- Chromatin-remodeling genes — [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [ARID1B](../genes/ARID1B.md), [ARID5B](../genes/ARID5B.md), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md), [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md), [KDM5A](../genes/KDM5A.md), [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md) (including missense p.R1446C in the KAT11 domain), [EP300](../genes/EP300.md), [SMARCA2](../genes/SMARCA2.md) (3 somatic missense), [CHD2](../genes/CHD2.md) (truncating), [BRD2](../genes/BRD2.md) (in-frame deletion) — collectively mutated in 12/24 cases [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).

## Clinical implications

- The presence of activating [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) mutations in a subset of ACC nominates small-molecule FGFR inhibitors as a therapeutic strategy; the authors specifically highlight the then-open clinical trial of [dovitinib](../drugs/dovitinib.md) (TKI258) in ACC (NCT01524692) and suggest evaluating [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) mutation status in trial participants [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).
- Identification of [SPEN](../genes/SPEN.md) and [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md)/[NOTCH2](../genes/NOTCH2.md) mutations supports exploration of NOTCH-pathway targeting as a therapeutic angle in ACC [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).
- Solid-histology ACC, which carries worse prognosis (higher rates of local-regional relapse and distant metastasis), is enriched for [SPEN](../genes/SPEN.md) truncations — a possible prognostic and biological subgroup [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).

## Limitations & open questions

- Small discovery cohort (24 cases) limits power to detect lower-frequency drivers and to test associations with histology or MYB status [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).
- Functional direction of [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md)/[NOTCH2](../genes/NOTCH2.md) mutations in ACC is not resolvable from mutation pattern alone — they could be activating (per T-ALL/Hajdu-Cheney parallels) or loss-of-function (per HNSCC parallels), and may depend on cooperating mutations [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).
- [SPEN](../genes/SPEN.md) truncations are not accompanied by consistent LOH or by loss of transcript expression, so a simple loss-of-function mechanism is unlikely; mechanism remains unresolved [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).
- Predicted therapeutic benefit of FGFR inhibition is inferred from analogous mutations in other tumor types; direct evidence in ACC tumors with these alleles awaits trial readouts [PMID:23778141](../papers/23778141.md).
