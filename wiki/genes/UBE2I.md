# UBE2I

## Overview

UBE2I (Ubiquitin Conjugating Enzyme E2 I), also known as UBC9, encodes the sole E2 SUMO-conjugating enzyme in mammals. It is essential for all SUMOylation events and regulates a broad set of nuclear processes including chromatin organization, DNA damage response, and cell cycle control. In glioblastoma, UBE2I protein upregulation is associated with high H2B N-terminal acetylation and co-expression with [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md), a known SUMOylation target.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Protein upregulated in [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) tumors with high H2B N-terminal acetylation (classical-like nmf3 subtype); UBE2I protein levels track [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md) protein levels (Pearson r=0.413), consistent with CDK6 being a known SUMOylation substrate; observed in a CPTAC proteogenomic cohort of 99 treatment-naive GBMs. [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md): UBE2I protein upregulation in H2B-hyperacetylated classical-like tumors; co-regulated with [SUMO1](../genes/SUMO1.md) and [CDK6](../genes/CDK6.md) in the acetylome-defined classical-like subtype. [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- UBE2I upregulation co-occurs with [SUMO1](../genes/SUMO1.md) in high-H2B-acetylation GBM tumors; UBE2I–CDK6 protein correlation (Pearson r=0.413) is among the notable co-expression relationships in the classical-like GBM subtype. [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting of UBE2I in GBM is described in the current corpus; UBE2I's role in the H2B-acetylation–SUMOylation axis in classical-like, immune-low GBM suggests potential adjacency to BET inhibitor and CBP/p300 inhibitor strategies that target this subtype. [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)

## Open questions

- Whether UBE2I–CDK6 co-expression reflects a functional SUMOylation-dependent cell-cycle regulatory circuit in classical-like GBM, and whether it predicts response to CDK or SUMO-pathway inhibition, has not been tested. [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md)
