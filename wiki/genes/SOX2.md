# SOX2

## Overview

SOX2 (SRY-Box Transcription Factor 2) is a transcription factor essential for maintaining pluripotency and self-renewal in embryonic stem cells. In squamous cell carcinomas, SOX2 functions as an oncogene through genomic amplification at 3q26.33, a locus co-amplified with TP63. SOX2 amplification is a hallmark of squamous-lineage cancers of the lung, cervix, esophagus, and head/neck.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- SOX2 and [TP63](../genes/TP63.md) are co-amplified at 3q in 48% of ESCCs ([ESCC](../cancer_types/ESCC.md)) in the TCGA esophageal carcinoma cohort (stes_tcga_pub, n=90 ESCC); combined amplification rate is higher in the ESCC1 molecular subtype (classical squamous / NRF2-pathway subtype). [PMID:28052061](../papers/28052061.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [ESCC](../cancer_types/ESCC.md): SOX2 amplification at 3q is one of the most prevalent copy-number events in ESCC; co-amplification with TP63 defines a squamous lineage identity marker enriched in the ESCC1 subtype. Subtype-directed Hippo/YAP1-targeted approaches are proposed for SOX2/TP63-amplified ESCC1 tumors. [PMID:28052061](../papers/28052061.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-amplified with [TP63](../genes/TP63.md) at the 3q locus in ESCC; this amplicon defines squamous lineage and is enriched in ESCC1. [PMID:28052061](../papers/28052061.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- SOX2/TP63-amplified ESCC1 tumors with Hippo pathway activation (YAP1 amplification) are proposed candidates for Hippo/YAP1-targeted therapy. [PMID:28052061](../papers/28052061.md)

## Open questions

- Prospective clinical evidence for SOX2-amplification as a predictive biomarker for any targeted therapy in ESCC is lacking in the corpus. [PMID:28052061](../papers/28052061.md)
