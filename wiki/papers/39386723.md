**PMID:** [39386723](../papers/39386723.md) · **DOI:** 10.1101/2024.09.25.615007 · **Journal:** bioRxiv (2024)

## TL;DR

This study integrates cyclic immunofluorescence (CyCIF) and GeoMx spatial transcriptomics to map the tumor-immune microenvironment across the full progression spectrum of high-grade serous ovarian carcinoma ([HGSOC](../cancer_types/HGSOC.md)), from p53 signatures and serous tubal intraepithelial carcinomas (STIC) in fallopian tubes through to invasive cancer, using 44 specimens from 43 individuals. The authors demonstrate that localized interferon (IFN) signaling and chromosomal instability (CIN) are unexpectedly early events, that the cGAS-STING pathway is activated via micronuclear rupture in STIC precursors, and that the immune microenvironment transitions from early NK-cDC1-mediated surveillance to progressive immune suppression characterized by CD8+ T cell exhaustion, Treg accumulation, and HLA-E-mediated NK cell evasion.

## Cohort & data

- 44 FFPE fallopian tube specimens from 43 individuals collected from a multi-center collaboration (University of Pennsylvania, Swedish Cancer Institute, Seattle) ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).
- Cancer type: HGSOC.
- Group 1 (n=24): invasive cancer with co-occurring STIC (STIC.C); 15/24 [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) WT, 7/9 germline [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md), 6/7 germline [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md).
- Group 2 (n=19): no invasive cancer; incidental p53 signatures (p53.I, n=10) and incidental STICs (STIC.I, n=9) identified during risk-reducing surgery.
- 35/44 specimens analyzed with NanoString GeoMx whole transcriptome analysis (WTA) across >600 regions of interest (ROI).
- All 44 specimens analyzed with CyCIF (31-antibody panel), yielding 4.22 x 10^7 single cells classified into 21 cell neighborhood topics via latent Dirichlet allocation (LDA).
- Dataset: [ovary_geomx_gray_foundation_2024](../datasets/ovary_geomx_gray_foundation_2024.md), integrated into cBioPortal.

## Key findings

- **Early IFN activation**: IFN-alpha and IFN-gamma pathway genes ([STAT1](../genes/STAT1.md), [IFITM1](../genes/IFITM1.md), [IRF9](../genes/IRF9.md), [IRF7](../genes/IRF7.md), [ISG15](../genes/ISG15.md), TAP1, [HLA-A](../genes/HLA-A.md)) are significantly upregulated beginning at the p53 signature stage and persist through STIC and invasive cancer ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).
- **[HLA-E](../genes/HLA-E.md) as immune evasion mechanism**: Non-classical MHC-I molecule HLA-E is progressively upregulated (p53.I median 4% positive epithelial cells to STIC.C 26%), with HLA-E-positive regions showing strong association with IFN and IRDS gene signatures. HLA-E overexpression may inhibit NK cell cytotoxicity via NKG2A engagement ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).
- **cGAS-STING pathway activation via micronuclear rupture**: BANF1-positive ruptured micronuclei with cGAS co-localization were observed as early as STIC.I lesions, with increasing frequency in invasive cancer. 3D CyCIF confirmed co-localization of [BANF1](../genes/BANF1.md), [CGAS](../genes/CGAS.md), and gamma-H2AX ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).
- **NK-cDC1 axis decline**: HLA-DR+ cDC1 cells were 10-fold higher in p53.I epithelium and 15-fold higher in STIC.I epithelium vs. normal, but stromal cDC1 decreased 12-fold from STIC.I to STIC.C. NK cells became nearly undetectable in STIC.I, STIC.C, and cancer (median 0.02% vs. 0.1% in normal/p53.I) ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).
- **CD8+ T cell dynamics**: CD8+ T cells increased 2-fold in STIC.I epithelium and 10-fold in stroma vs. normal, then declined in STIC.C and cancer. Exhaustion markers (LAG3, PD1) increased 3- to 7-fold in later stages. Tissue-resident memory T cells (TRM) peaked in p53.I and declined progressively ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).
- **Macrophage shift**: CD163+ M2-like and CD68+ M1-like macrophages increased progressively with disease stage; >50% of CD68+ cells co-expressed CD11c, functioning as macrophage-derived APCs ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).
- **IRDS emergence**: IFN-related DNA damage resistance signature genes (STAT1, [MX1](../genes/MX1.md), [MCL1](../genes/MCL1.md)) were upregulated in STIC.C and cancer, suggesting chronic IFN activation may promote treatment resistance ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).
- **EMT and TGF-beta pathways**: TGF-beta, EMT, hypoxia, and TNF-alpha/NFkB pathways emerged primarily at the STIC.C and cancer stages, independent of HLA-E status ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md): Mutations define p53 signatures, the earliest precursor lesions; clonally shared across p53 signatures, STICs, and concurrent HGSOC ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).
- [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) / [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md): Germline mutations in subset of cohort (BRCA1 n=12, BRCA2 n=12); nearly half of HGSOCs exhibit homologous recombination deficiency via somatic or germline BRCA1/2 alterations or epigenetic silencing ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).
- [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md): Amplification noted as early CNA event in STICs, linked to breakage-fusion-bridge cycles ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).
- [HLA-A](../genes/HLA-A.md): Upregulated in precursor epithelium as part of classical MHC-I antigen presentation; co-expressed with MX1 in STIC.C and cancer epithelial cells ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).
- [HLA-E](../genes/HLA-E.md): Progressive upregulation from p53 signatures through cancer; proposed mechanism of NK cell immune evasion via NKG2A receptor engagement ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).
- [STAT1](../genes/STAT1.md): Key upstream IFN signaling activator; upregulated from p53.I stage onward; also part of IRDS signature ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).
- [CGAS](../genes/CGAS.md) / [STING1](../genes/STING1.md): cGAS-STING pathway activated by ruptured micronuclei; cGAS recruited to BANF1+ micronuclei as early as STIC.I ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).
- [ISG15](../genes/ISG15.md), [IFITM1](../genes/IFITM1.md), [IRF7](../genes/IRF7.md), [IRF9](../genes/IRF9.md): IFN-stimulated genes upregulated early in HGSOC development; IFITM1 induced even at fimbriae stage ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).
- [MX1](../genes/MX1.md): IFN-inducible protein forming discrete puncta in STIC.C and cancer; also part of IRDS signature ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).
- [MCL1](../genes/MCL1.md): Anti-apoptotic BCL-2 family member upregulated as part of IRDS in STIC.C and cancer ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).
- [BANF1](../genes/BANF1.md): Used as marker for cytosolic DNA from ruptured micronuclei; BANF1+ micronuclei observed from STIC.I onward ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).
- [KLRK1](../genes/KLRK1.md): Encodes NKG2D NK cell activation receptor; expression declined in STIC.C and cancer, indicating progressive NK cell dysfunction ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).

## Clinical implications

- **Early detection biomarkers**: IFN pathway activation (STAT1, HLA-A, HLA-E) and cGAS-STING signaling in p53 signatures and STICs could serve as biomarkers for risk stratification of precursor lesions ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).
- **NK cell reactivation therapy**: HLA-E upregulation in precursor lesions suggests that anti-NKG2A antibodies (e.g., [monalizumab](../drugs/monalizumab.md)) could be a promising early interception strategy, particularly in high-risk patients with incidental STICs ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).
- **IRDS and chemoresistance**: Chronic IFN activation and emergence of the IRDS signature in STIC.C and cancer may contribute to the chemoresistance observed in >80% of stage III/IV HGSOC patients ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).
- **Immune checkpoint targets**: Progressive CD8+ T cell exhaustion with upregulation of PD1, LAG3, TIM3, and [CTLA4](../genes/CTLA4.md) supports investigation of immune checkpoint inhibitors in the HGSOC precancer setting ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).

## Limitations & open questions

- Sample size (44 specimens from 43 patients) limits statistical power, particularly for subgroup analyses by BRCA status and disease stage ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).
- Approximately 25% of cancer-associated STICs may represent disseminated cancer cells rather than true precursors, complicating interpretation of STIC.C findings ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).
- The study is a preprint (bioRxiv) and has not undergone peer review at the time of data extraction.
- Functional validation of the proposed HLA-E/NKG2A immune evasion axis and cGAS-STING activation in HGSOC precursors is needed.
- Expanding the cohort to include more incidental STIC lesions would improve identification of markers linked to clinical outcomes ([PMID:39386723](../papers/39386723.md)).
- The relationship between IRDS emergence and clinical chemoresistance remains correlative; prospective studies are needed.
