# TP53BP1

## Overview

TP53BP1 ([TP53](../genes/TP53.md) Binding Protein 1), commonly known as 53BP1, is a key mediator of the DNA damage response (DDR), particularly in non-homologous end joining (NHEJ) of DNA double-strand breaks. It is a substrate of [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) kinase and plays a central role in the DNA damage checkpoint. In endometrial serous carcinoma, phosphorylation of 53BP1 at S1763 is elevated and has been linked to PLK1-driven DDR inactivation and G2M checkpoint bypass.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- S1763 phosphosite elevated in serous endometrial tumors; PLK1-driven inactivation of the DDR and G2M-checkpoint progression [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Endometrial serous carcinoma: elevated TP53BP1 S1763 phosphorylation consistent with [PLK1](../genes/PLK1.md) hyperactivation and DDR inactivation [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-elevated phosphorylation with [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md) S163 in serous endometrial tumors, downstream of PLK1 hyperactivation [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- PLK1-driven phospho-inactivation of TP53BP1 in serous EC provides rationale for PLK1 inhibitor evaluation in this subtype [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)

## Open questions

- Whether TP53BP1 S1763 phosphorylation is a functional driver of DDR suppression or a passenger of PLK1 hyperactivation in serous EC [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)
