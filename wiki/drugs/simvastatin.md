# simvastatin

## Overview

Simvastatin is a lipophilic statin that inhibits [HMGCR](../genes/HMGCR.md) (HMG-CoA reductase), the rate-limiting enzyme of cholesterol biosynthesis. Its lipophilicity enables greater passive diffusion into tumor cells compared to hydrophilic statins. Population-based studies associate simvastatin and other lipophilic statins with reduced hepatocellular carcinoma incidence and cancer-related mortality. It is not FDA-approved as an anti-cancer agent.

## Evidence in the corpus

- Population data link lipophilic statins (simvastatin, [lovastatin](../drugs/lovastatin.md), [atorvastatin](../drugs/atorvastatin.md)) to a more pronounced [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)-risk reduction than hydrophilic statins ([pravastatin](../drugs/pravastatin.md), rosuvastatin); multiple phase 2/3 trials of hydrophilic statins combined with [sorafenib](../drugs/sorafenib.md) in advanced HCC failed to improve survival, underlining the importance of lipophilicity for tumor penetration and HMGCR inhibition in HCC [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md).

## Resistance mechanisms

- Not applicable (simvastatin is evaluated as a sensitizing/preventive agent).
