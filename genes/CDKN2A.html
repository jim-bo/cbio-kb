CDKN2A

Overview

CDKN2A encodes two distinct tumor suppressors via alternative reading frames: p16/INK4A (inhibits CDK4/6-Cyclin D complexes, blocking RB1 phosphorylation) and p14/ARF (stabilizes p53 by inhibiting MDM2). Homozygous deletion of CDKN2A is one of the most common alterations across human cancers and is a major driver of cell-cycle dysregulation. CDKN2A loss is independently prognostic in multiple cancer types including intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma and esophageal adenocarcinoma.

Alterations observed in the corpus

  • CDKN2A alterations associated with worse OS in esophageal/GEJ adenocarcinoma: KM p<0.001; adjusted HR 1.65; multivariable HR 1.50 (95% CI 1.14–1.97, p=0.004) in the egc_mskcc_2020 cohort (n=487). CDKN2A deletions alone were not prognostic on univariable analysis PMID:33795256.
  • CDKN2A focal alterations in 15% of intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma (iCCA); homozygous deletions in 13%; strongest independent prognostic factor (HR 3.40 in resected cohort multivariate; HR 2.5 in whole cohort). Only deep (homozygous) deletions are prognostic; monoallelic alterations are not. Mutually exclusive with IDH1/2 mutations and FGFR2 fusions PMID:33765338.
  • CDKN2A/CDKN2B show differential alteration patterns between IDH-WT and IDH-mutant GBM tumors in the CPTAC proteogenomic cohort (gbm_cptac_2021, n=99) PMID:33577785.
  • Detected at varying frequencies across TERT-altered and TERT-WT metaplastic breast cancer groups in a 60-case MSKCC cohort PMID:33863915
  • Deletion (HR 3.3, 95% CI 1.1–9.5, P=0.030) independently predicts worse OS in node-positive (N1) intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma; combined CDKN2A/B deletion (HR 5.0) part of the poor-prognosis composite (TP53 mut / KRAS mut / CDKN2A/B del): median OS 12.1 vs 30.9 months (P=0.002) PMID:33963001
  • Mutated/deleted in 34.9% of CSCC tumors including focal homozygous deletions; drives cell-cycle-checkpoint (Rb pathway) loss present in 38.6% of tumors PMID:34272401
  • Copy number alterations enriched in pN-positive LUAD tumors (9% vs 4%, p=0.039) in a 426-patient MSK-IMPACT cohort; CDKN2A was among gene-level CNAs assessed at ≥1% frequency PMID:34290393
  • Homozygous deletion universally present in LSCC (along with RB1 loss); 28 CDKN2A-WT tumors had p16-promoter hypermethylation; mutations paradoxically yielded increased CDKN2A RNA; loss of CDKN2A or RB1 was found in every one of 108 LSCC samples PMID:34358469
  • Among the long-latency driver groups in LCINS (MRCA > a decade pre-diagnosis); identified among 25 recurrently mutated IntOGen driver genes in the 232-patient Sherlock-Lung WGS cohort of never smokers PMID:34493867
  • Altered in 48% of high-cellularity PDAC tumors (CNV-driven focal deletions > intragenic mutations); deletions raise phospho-NPM1 S70/S214 as a trans-proteomic effect PMID:34534465
  • CDKN2A (p16INK4A) is absent endogenously in MCF7 parental cells; ectopic p16 overexpression in T47D reduces abemaciclib and palbociclib potency; high CDKN2A mRNA correlates with poor palbociclib response in PRISM screen, consistent with the CDK6-INK4 resistance mechanism PMID:34544752
  • Deletion enriched in metastases in 5 tumor types in the MSK-MET cohort; in LUAD more frequent in skin (16% vs 60%, q=0.03) and liver (16% vs 27%, q=0.03) metastases but less frequent in lymph node mets (19% vs 12%, q=0.002); deletion frequency correlated with metastatic burden in bladder urothelial, LUAD, and papillary thyroid; mutations less frequent in primary PAAD of metastatic patients (17% vs 7%, q=0.02) PMID:35120664
  • CDKN2A — 157 deletions/inactivating events across sarcomas, CNS tumors, and leukemia in MAPPYACTS pediatric cohort (n=624); matched to CDK4/6 inhibitors (105 recommendations) PMID:35292802
  • CDKN2A altered in 22% of MSI-stable locally advanced esophageal/GEJ adenocarcinoma (n=237); high prevalence but not significantly associated with pathologic response to neoadjuvant chemoradiotherapy PMID:35377946
  • CDKN2A among the genes listed in the genomic landscape of SCLC PDX/CDX cohort (n=46 models from 33 MSK patients); canonical SCLC genomic landscape dominated by TP53 and RB1 loss PMID:35440124
  • Homozygous deletion in 8% (9/119) of low-grade serous ovarian carcinomas (LGSC) sequenced by MSK-IMPACT; acquired CDKN2A/B deletion observed at recurrence in 1/5 patients initially without detectable driver alteration PMID:35443055
  • Non-SMG cancer gene mutated in ≥3 HCCs in a European multi-omic cohort (n=122); associated with poor OS independent of BCLC staging in multivariate Cox analysis PMID:35508466
  • CDKN2A deleted in 16.7% of sarcomas (84.5% homozygous deletions); enriched in MPNST (47.3%) and chordoma (36.8%); co-occurs with CDK4/CDK6 amplification and CCND1/2/3 in unusual within-pathway co-occurrence PMID:35705558
  • Acquired focal CDKN2A deletion at recurrence drives expansion of proliferating stem-like neoplastic cells in IDHmut glioma (P=3e-3, n=6); already prevalent at initial diagnosis in IDHwt tumors and not enriched at recurrence there PMID:35649412
  • CDKN2A deletions in up to 48% of MPNST; broadly distributed actionable hits across sarcoma subtypes; included in Level 3B highlights with up to 48% deletion frequency in MPNST PMID:35705560
  • Deep deletion peak in CNV analysis; LoF in 20% of PAAD in a pan-Asian solid-tumor cohort (CDK4/6 inhibitor candidate per OncoKB); 12% pan-cohort frequency PMID:35871175
  • Recurrently mutated (LOF impacting p16INK4a and ARF) and structurally deleted (DEL) in 25 metastatic CSCC WGS samples; driver confirmed by all three tools; deep deletion in 1/25 samples; proposed rationale for CDK4/6 inhibition with palbociclib in metastatic CSCC PMID:35982973
  • E61Pfs*54 frameshift clonal in one endometrial carcinoma primary (CD28); not detected in baseline cfDNA but detected at 6-month follow-up by MSK-ACCESS panel PMID:36007103
  • Paradoxically p16-high (overexpression by IHC and RNA-seq) in all chromosome 12 chromothripsis atypical SCLC cases (A07, A08, A09) despite wild-type pRb, reflecting posttranslational pRb inhibition by ecDNA-amplified CDK4 creating a pRb-null-like state PMID:39185963

Cancer types (linked)

  • Esophageal/gastroesophageal junction adenocarcinoma (EAC): CDKN2A alterations independently prognostic of worse OS (multivariable HR 1.50, p=0.004); deletions alone not significant on univariable; Cell Cycle pathway enrichment PMID:33795256.
  • Intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma (IHCH): homozygous deletion in 13%; strongest independent prognostic factor (HR 3.40 resected, HR 2.5 whole cohort); defines a subgroup with no benefit from resection in clinical high-risk patients; mutually exclusive with IDH1/2 and FGFR2 PMID:33765338.
  • Glioblastoma (GBM): differential CDKN2A/CDKN2B alteration patterns between IDH-WT and IDH-mutant tumors PMID:33577785.

Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

  • Mutually exclusive with IDH1/2 mutations and FGFR2 fusions in intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma PMID:33765338.
  • CDKN2A alterations associated with multifocal disease in iCCA PMID:33765338.
  • Co-occurs with TP53 and KRAS alterations as part of the high-risk genomic profile in iCCA PMID:33765338.

Therapeutic relevance

  • In iCCA, CDKN2A homozygous deletion (with TP53 and KRAS) should be routinely reported as part of pretreatment targeted sequencing to stratify OS independently of stage and treatment; patients with CDKN2A deep deletion + clinical high-risk features may be better served by upfront systemic therapy ± HAIC than resection PMID:33765338.

Open questions

  • In EAC, why CDKN2A deletions alone are not prognostic on univariable analysis while the broader alteration category is, requires further investigation; may reflect reduced sensitivity of panel-based sequencing for deletions at low tumor purity PMID:33795256.
  • Panel-based sequencing has reduced sensitivity for deletions at low tumor purity, potentially dampening the apparent prognostic effect of CDKN2A deletions PMID:33795256.
