# BACH2

## Overview

BACH2 (BTB and CNC Homology 2, Basic Leucine Zipper Transcription Factor 2) encodes a transcriptional repressor that plays a central role in B-cell differentiation, germinal center formation, and immune homeostasis. It acts as a brake on plasma cell differentiation and is recurrently mutated or deleted in B-cell malignancies including diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL). Loss-of-function alterations in BACH2 can deregulate terminal B-cell differentiation, contributing to lymphomagenesis.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Identified as one of 98 candidate cancer genes (CCGs) by MutSig2CV (q < 0.1) in a comprehensive whole-exome sequencing study of 304 primary DLBCLs ([dlbcl_dfci_2018](../datasets/dlbcl_dfci_2018.md)); 40 of the 98 CCGs were previously undescribed drivers in DLBCL [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)
- BACH2 — highly expressed in non-NE SCLC-P (POU2F3-driven) subtype PDXs; acts as a transcriptional repressor contributing to the non-neuroendocrine gene signature in SCLC-P models ([SCLC](../cancer_types/SCLC.md) PDX/CDX multi-omic cohort, n=46 models from 33 MSK patients) [PMID:35440124](../papers/35440124.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [DLBCLNOS](../cancer_types/DLBCLNOS.md) — recurrently mutated candidate cancer gene identified in DLBCL genomic landscape study [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Part of a 98-gene CCG set distributed across five genetic DLBCL subtypes (C1–C5); specific co-occurrence or mutual exclusivity with BACH2 not separately detailed [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct targeted therapy reported in the corpus; BACH2 mutations in DLBCL may influence response to R-CHOP-based immunochemotherapy as part of the broader genetic subtype framework [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)

## Open questions

- Subtype specificity of BACH2 mutations across C1–C5 DLBCL clusters was not separately detailed; functional consequences of observed mutations remain to be characterized [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)
