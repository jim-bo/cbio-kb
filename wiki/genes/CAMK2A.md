# CAMK2A

## Overview

CAMK2A (Calcium/Calmodulin-Dependent Protein Kinase II Alpha) is a serine/threonine kinase involved in synaptic signaling and neuronal function. In pediatric brain tumors, CAMK2A shows highest protein abundance in ganglioglioma but highest kinase activity in high-grade glioma (HGG), where it phosphorylates substrates involved in gap-junction assembly and synaptic transmission, linking it to glioma-neuron synapse-mediated invasion.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- CAMK2A kinase activity is highest in HGG among pediatric brain tumor types (CPTAC proteogenomics, n=218 samples); in HGG, CAMK2A phosphorylates GJA1/connexin-43 (Ser325/Ser314, involved in gap-junction assembly and glioma invasion) and SYN1 (Ser568/Ser605, synaptic transmission), supporting a role in glioma invasion via neuron-glioma synapses [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md)
- CAMK2A protein abundance is most abundant in ganglioglioma; CDK1/2 and CAMK2A protein abundance are mutually exclusive within HGG, implying two distinct HGG signaling programs; both observations were confirmed in an independent 23-patient HGG validation cohort [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HGGNOS](../cancer_types/HGGNOS.md): highest CAMK2A kinase activity among all pediatric brain tumor types; GJA1 and SYN1 phosphorylation substrates linked to invasion [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md)
- [GNG](../cancer_types/GNG.md) (ganglioglioma): highest CAMK2A protein abundance, co-elevated with CDK5/GSK3B signaling [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- CAMK2A protein abundance is mutually exclusive with CDK1/CDK2 protein abundance within HGG, suggesting two distinct signaling programs in pediatric high-grade glioma [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Elevated CAMK2A kinase activity and its substrates (GJA1, SYN1) in HGG nominate CAMK2A as a candidate therapeutic target for disrupting glioma-neuron synapse-mediated invasion; drug connectivity analysis (LINCS L1000) predicts CDK inhibitors as reversing the HGG-aggressiveness signature [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md)

## Open questions

- Whether CAMK2A-driven phosphorylation of GJA1 and SYN1 directly mediates glioma invasion in vivo, and whether CAMK2A inhibitors can achieve sufficient CNS penetration for therapeutic effect, are unresolved [PMID:33242424](../papers/33242424.md)
