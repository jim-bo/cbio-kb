# NUTM2A

## Overview

NUTM2A (NUT midline carcinoma family member 2A) encodes a member of the NUT family of chromatin-associated proteins. In uterine sarcomas, NUTM2A is most recognized as a fusion partner with [YWHAE](../genes/YWHAE.md) (tyrosine 3-monooxygenase/tryptophan 5-monooxygenase activation protein epsilon), producing the YWHAE-NUTM2 rearrangement characteristic of high-grade endometrial stromal sarcoma and related uterine mesenchymal tumors. Detection of this fusion by molecular profiling has diagnostic and reclassification implications in uterine sarcoma.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- YWHAE/NUTM2A rearrangement identified by MSK-Fusion in two high-grade, non-pleomorphic uterine stromal sarcomas among 107 uterine sarcoma patients profiled by MSK-IMPACT; used diagnostically to reclassify cases initially categorized as uterine leiomyosarcoma [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[ULMS](../cancer_types/ULMS.md) / high-grade uterine stromal sarcoma:** YWHAE-NUTM2A/B fusions are a defining molecular event for a subset of high-grade stromal sarcomas; detected by MSK-Fusion panel in prospective MSK uterine sarcoma sequencing cohort [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- YWHAE-NUTM2A fusions co-occur with YWHAE-NUTM2B fusions as alternative fusion partners within the same diagnostic category (high-grade uterine stromal sarcoma with YWHAE rearrangement) [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No targeted therapy directly addressing NUTM2A fusions reported in this corpus; fusion detection is primarily used for diagnostic reclassification and to separate YWHAE-NUTM2–rearranged sarcomas from uLMS [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)

## Open questions

- Whether YWHAE-NUTM2A vs. YWHAE-NUTM2B fusions have distinct clinical behavior is unresolved in this cohort due to small case numbers [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)
