# COL7A1

## Overview

COL7A1 encodes the alpha-1 chain of type VII collagen, the primary structural component of anchoring fibrils at the dermal-epidermal junction. Germline loss-of-function mutations in COL7A1 cause recessive dystrophic epidermolysis bullosa (RDEB), a rare blistering skin disease associated with markedly elevated [cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma](../cancer_types/CSCC.md) ([CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md)) risk (~100-fold over lifetime). COL7A1 is not a somatic CSCC driver gene; rather, the RDEB context of chronic skin inflammation and blistering creates a permissive tumor-promoting microenvironment that influences the somatic mutational landscape of CSCC arising in these patients.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Germline cause of RDEB (recessive dystrophic epidermolysis bullosa); not a somatic CSCC driver. In the 88-tumor CSCC meta-analysis, the RDEB etiologic subtype is identified by COL7A1 germline status and is associated with distinctive somatic driver enrichment — specifically, CASP8 mutation is significantly enriched in RDEB-CSCC [PMID:34272401](../papers/34272401.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md): germline COL7A1 loss defines the RDEB-CSCC etiologic subtype; RDEB patients carry ~100-fold elevated CSCC risk; RDEB tumors show relatively low TMB driven primarily by APOBEC mutagenesis and are enriched for [CASP8](../genes/CASP8.md) somatic mutations [PMID:34272401](../papers/34272401.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- RDEB context (COL7A1 germline loss) associated with enrichment of somatic [CASP8](../genes/CASP8.md) mutations in CSCC [PMID:34272401](../papers/34272401.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Germline COL7A1 status has no direct drug target; the RDEB-CSCC patient population is typically ineligible for immune-checkpoint blockade (the standard care for sporadic advanced CSCC) due to immunosuppression concerns, representing an unmet clinical need [PMID:34272401](../papers/34272401.md)

## Open questions

- Mechanism linking chronic blistering/inflammation driven by COL7A1 loss to selective pressure for CASP8 mutations in somatic tumorigenesis is not fully elucidated [PMID:34272401](../papers/34272401.md)
