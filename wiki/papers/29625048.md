**PMID:** 29625048 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.cell.2018.03.022 · **Journal:** Cell (2018)

## TL;DR

Hoadley and colleagues performed an integrative multi-platform molecular analysis of ~10,000 tumors from 33 cancer types in The Cancer Genome Atlas (TCGA) PanCancer Atlas, combining chromosome-arm aneuploidy, DNA methylation, mRNA, miRNA, and reverse-phase protein array (RPPA) data. Using iCluster across the four most complete platforms (copy number, DNA methylation, mRNA, miRNA) on 9,759 samples, they identified 28 integrated molecular subtypes. Cell-of-origin and tissue/organ-system patterns dominated the classification, but the analysis also surfaced cross-tissue groupings (pan-GI, pan-gyn, pan-squamous, pan-kidney) that motivate organ-system and stemness-based pan-cancer companion analyses.

## Cohort & data

- **Samples:** 11,286 tumors across 33 cancer types with data on at least one of five assay platforms; 9,759 with complete data across four platforms (aneuploidy, DNA methylation, mRNA, miRNA); RPPA available for 7,858 samples across 32 tumor types ([LAML](../cancer_types/LAML.md) lacked protein data).
- **Patients:** 11,188 TCGA patients with at least one molecular platform — 5,769 females, 5,282 males, 137 unknown; median age at diagnosis 60 (range 10–90).
- **Cancer types covered:** [LAML](../cancer_types/LAML.md), [DLBCLNOS](../cancer_types/DLBCLNOS.md), [THYM](../cancer_types/THYM.md), [OV](../cancer_types/OV.md), [UCEC](../cancer_types/UCEC.md), [CESC](../cancer_types/CESC.md), [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md), [BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md), [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md), [TGCT](../cancer_types/TGCT.md), [KIRC](../cancer_types/KIRC.md), [KICH](../cancer_types/KICH.md), [KIRP](../cancer_types/KIRP.md), [THCA](../cancer_types/THCA.md), [ACC](../cancer_types/ACC.md), [ESCA](../cancer_types/ESCA.md), [STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md), [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md), [READ](../cancer_types/READ.md), [LIHC](../cancer_types/LIHC.md), [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md), [CHOL](../cancer_types/CHOL.md), [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md), [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md), [LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md), [MESO](../cancer_types/MESO.md), [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md), [LGG](../cancer_types/LGG.md), [SARC](../cancer_types/SARC.md), [UCS](../cancer_types/UCS.md), [PCPG](../cancer_types/PCPG.md), [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md), [UVM](../cancer_types/UVM.md).
- **Datasets (cBioPortal study mirror):** the 33 TCGA PanCancer Atlas studies, e.g. [gbm_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/gbm_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [brca_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/brca_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [luad_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/luad_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), [coadread_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/coadread_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md), and the other PanCancer Atlas study IDs listed in frontmatter.
- **Assays / platforms:** Affymetrix SNP 6.0 arrays for somatic copy number ([affymetrix-snp6](../methods/affymetrix-snp6.md)) processed with [GISTIC 2.0](../methods/gistic.md) and [ABSOLUTE](../methods/absolute.md); Illumina Infinium [HM27](../methods/hm27-methylation-array.md) and [HM450](../methods/hm450-methylation-array.md) DNA methylation arrays merged across 22,601 shared probes; mRNA sequencing ([rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md)) on Illumina GAII/HiSeq; miRNA sequencing ([mirna-seq](../methods/mirna-seq.md)); reverse-phase protein arrays ([RPPA](../methods/rppa.md)).
- **Integrative analytics:** [iCluster](../methods/icluster.md) on copy number + DNA methylation + mRNA + miRNA over 9,759 tumors; [COCA](../methods/coca.md) (Cluster-Of-Cluster-Assignments) to compare platform-specific cluster memberships; [PARADIGM](../methods/paradigm.md) to infer activity of ~19,000 pathway features; [TumorMap](../methods/tumormap.md) for 2D visualization of iCluster latent-variable distances.

## Key findings

- **28 integrative iClusters** were identified from joint clustering of 9,759 tumors across four platforms. Copy-number alterations contributed 47% of the integrated signal, mRNA + miRNA 42%, and DNA methylation 11%.
- **Single-platform clustering recapitulated cell-of-origin:** aneuploidy yielded 10 groups, DNA methylation 25 groups (from 3,139 CpGs hypermethylated in ≥1 tumor type), mRNA 25 groups (≥40 samples each), miRNA 15 groups, and RPPA 10 groups — with squamous (LUSC/HNSC/CESC/ESCA), pan-GI (COAD/READ/STAD/non-squamous [ESCA](../cancer_types/ESCA.md)), and pan-kidney (KIRC/KIRP) tumors repeatedly co-clustering.
- **16 of 33 tumor types had >80% of samples in a single iCluster;** 8 iClusters were dominated by one tumor type (C24: [LAML](../cancer_types/LAML.md), C11: [LGG](../cancer_types/LGG.md) [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) mut], C6: [OV](../cancer_types/OV.md), C8: [UCEC](../cancer_types/UCEC.md), C12: [THCA](../cancer_types/THCA.md), C16: [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md), C26: [LIHC](../cancer_types/LIHC.md), C14: [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md)).
- **Cross-tissue iClusters:** pan-kidney mapped almost entirely to C28 ([KIRC](../cancer_types/KIRC.md)+[KIRP](../cancer_types/KIRP.md)); [KICH](../cancer_types/KICH.md) co-clustered with [ACC](../cancer_types/ACC.md) in C9 (both hypodiploid). Pan-GI separated into C1: [STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md) (EBV-CIMP, hypermethylated EBV-associated tumors), C4: pan-GI (CRC, COAD/READ with CIN), and C18: pan-GI (MSI, mostly STAD/COAD). Pan-squamous formed C10, C25 (chr11 amp), and C27: pan-SCC (HPV+, including most [CESC](../cancer_types/CESC.md), HPV+ [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md), and some [BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md)).
- **C2: [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) (HER2 amp)** comprised mostly [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md)-amplified tumors across BRCA, BLCA, and STAD, demonstrating an actionable cross-tissue amplification subtype.
- **Mutation burden by iCluster:** C15:SKCM/UVM (UVB signature) and C14:[LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) (smoking signature) showed the highest mutation rates; [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) signatures appeared in hypermutated C8: [UCEC](../cancer_types/UCEC.md) and C4: pan-GI (CRC); C18: pan-GI (MSI) was enriched for mismatch-repair-deficient mutational signatures.
- **Stromal and immune content:** C20 had the highest median stromal fraction (relabeled C20: mixed [stromal/immune]); C3 had the highest leukocyte-to-stromal ratio and was relabeled C3: mesenchymal (immune). Both shared high [PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md) (PD-1), [CTLA4](../genes/CTLA4.md), and T-cell/B-cell activation gene programs (GP2).
- **Pathway features:** PARADIGM and gene-program analyses showed C28: pan-kidney enriched for hypoxia, retinoid metabolism, PPAR-RXR, and immune checkpoints PD-1/CTLA4; pan-squamous iClusters (C10, C25, C27) shared dNp63/TAp63 squamous signaling, proliferation, hypoxia, and basal signaling; C3 and C20 shared upregulated JAK2/STAT1,3,6 signaling with C14:LUAD and C10:pan-SCC.
- **Stemness:** [TGCT](../cancer_types/TGCT.md) showed strong enrichment of both mRNA- and DNA-methylation-based stemness signatures (Malta et al., 2018 companion); LAML was strongly enriched only for the mRNA-based stemness signature.
- **[CUP](../cancer_types/CUP.md) relevance:** authors note that this iCluster classification could improve management of the 1%–3% of cancer patients newly diagnosed with cancer of unknown primary, where RNA- or DNA-methylation-based profiling has already improved outcomes.

## Genes & alterations

- [IDH1](../genes/IDH1.md) — defines C11: [LGG](../cancer_types/LGG.md) (IDH1 mut), one of the most homogeneous and high-silhouette iClusters; IDH1-wildtype LGG co-clusters with [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) in C23: GBM/LGG (IDH1wt).
- [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) — focal amplification defines C2: BRCA (HER2 amp), which spans BRCA, BLCA, and STAD — a clear cross-tissue alteration subtype.
- [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) — POLE-mutation signature found in hypermutated subsets of C8: [UCEC](../cancer_types/UCEC.md) and C4: pan-GI (CRC).
- [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) (ER-α), [AR](../genes/AR.md), [IGFBP2](../genes/IGFBP2.md) — high in P3/P4 RPPA groups containing luminal BRCA, UCEC, [OV](../cancer_types/OV.md), and some [LIHC](../cancer_types/LIHC.md); estrogen-signaling gene program (GP7) high in C2: BRCA (HER2 amp) and C19: BRCA (luminal).
- [PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md) (PD-1), [CTLA4](../genes/CTLA4.md) — immune checkpoint programs enriched in C3: mesenchymal (immune), C20: mixed (stromal/immune), and C28: pan-kidney; basis for the authors' suggestion of immunotherapy susceptibility.
- [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md), [STAT1](../genes/STAT1.md), [STAT3](../genes/STAT3.md), [STAT6](../genes/STAT6.md) — JAK-STAT pathway upregulated in C3, C20, C14:LUAD, and C10:pan-SCC, raising hypothesis of repurposing JAK-STAT inhibitors approved in non-malignant settings (rheumatoid arthritis, myelofibrosis, polycythemia vera).
- [ALK](../genes/ALK.md), [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [KIT](../genes/KIT.md), [ABL1](../genes/ABL1.md) — cited as cancer-driving targets that motivate tissue-agnostic basket/umbrella trials (e.g. NCI-MATCH); the authors caution that the same [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) mutation yields unpredictable clinical responses across diverse malignancies, illustrating why tissue-of-origin context still matters.

## Clinical implications

- The 28-iCluster classification has potential utility for **cancer of unknown primary (CUP)** management, where DNA-methylation- and RNA-based profiling have already improved patient outcomes.
- **Cross-tissue ERBB2-amplified C2** (BRCA + BLCA + STAD) supports use of HER2-directed agents beyond breast cancer.
- **Immune-enriched iClusters (C3 and C20)** with high PD-1/CTLA4 and T-cell/B-cell activation gene programs are nominated as candidates for immune-checkpoint blockade across diverse histologies.
- **Shared JAK-STAT activation** across C3, C20, C14:LUAD, and C10:pan-SCC suggests repurposing of JAK inhibitors currently approved for non-malignant inflammatory and myeloproliferative diseases.
- Findings support **basket/umbrella trial design** (e.g. NCI-MATCH) but argue that integrated molecular tumor profiling should consider both targetable mutations and oncogenic-pathway context plus tissue of origin — illustrated by the heterogeneous response of BRAF-mutant tumors across cancer types.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Mutation data were excluded from iCluster** due to sparsity of mutations across the platform-aligned cohort; mutation burden and signatures were used only for downstream characterization, so mutation-driven subtypes may be under-represented.
- **Aneuploidy was weakly concordant** with other platforms because more than a third of samples (AN7) had few arm-level events, limiting COCA's resolution.
- **Heterogeneous iClusters remain hard to interpret** — C20: mixed (stromal/immune) contains 25 of 33 tumor types and 6 tumor types had <50% of samples in any single iCluster (BLCA, [UCS](../cancer_types/UCS.md), HNSC, ESCA, STAD, [CHOL](../cancer_types/CHOL.md)), indicating substantial residual molecular heterogeneity.
- **TCGA cohort biases:** adult tumors only (median age 60), predominantly treatment-naive primary specimens with high RIN/DNA-quality thresholds — results may not generalize to pediatric, metastatic, or treatment-resistant cancers.
- **Translation to clinical actionability is hypothesis-generating:** PARADIGM/gene-program activations identify candidate vulnerabilities (PD-1/CTLA4, JAK-STAT) but trials are needed; the authors' own [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) example shows that pathway activation does not guarantee response.
