**PMID:** 34819508 · **DOI:** 10.1038/s41525-021-00259-9 · **Journal:** npj Genomic Medicine (2021)

## TL;DR

Elkin et al. apply a novel geometric measure — Ollivier–Ricci (OR) network curvature — to copy-number alteration (CNA) profiles of 45 high-grade serous ovarian carcinoma ([HGSOC](../cancer_types/HGSOC.md)) patients treated with immune checkpoint inhibitors (ICIs) at Memorial Sloan Kettering. CNAs were extracted from targeted (MSK-IMPACT) and whole-exome sequencing, mapped onto a fixed protein–protein interaction (PPI) topology from HPRD, and a patient-specific "total curvature" κ<sub>G</sub> was computed. Stratifying patients at the 25th percentile of κ<sub>G</sub> separated high- vs low-risk groups for overall survival ([OS](../cancer_types/OS.md)) with log-rank p = 0.00047 — almost two orders of magnitude better than TMB (p = 0.032), LST (p = 0.43), or [FGA](../genes/FGA.md) (p = 0.20). Curvature was not prognostic in an HGSOC cohort that did *not* receive ICI, suggesting it is an ICI-specific biomarker. Top curvature-ranked genes recapitulated known HGSOC biology, with [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) dominating the positive-Δκ list.

## Cohort & data

- **n = 45** patients with recurrent HGSOC treated with ICIs, drawn as a subset from the 69-patient series in [PMID:32616680](https://doi.org/10.1200/JCO.19.02059) (Zamarin et al., reanalyzed here). [PMID:34819508](../papers/34819508.md)
- Cancer type: [high-grade serous ovarian carcinoma (HGSOC)](../cancer_types/HGSOC.md); broader bucket [ovarian cancer (OV)](../cancer_types/OV.md).
- Sample composition: 32 metastases, 13 primary (adnexal) tumors; 32 deaths at analysis. Mean age 58 y; mean TMB 5.9 mut/Mb.
- Dataset: [hgsoc_msk_2021](../datasets/hgsoc_msk_2021.md) — publicly available at cBioPortal.
- Assay: [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) targeted panel sequencing for both tumor and normal samples; CNA segments called by [FACETS](../methods/facets.md) and mapped to GRCh37 genes. Whole-exome data also contributed to CNA calls.
- Network topology: HPRD-derived PPI restricted to the largest connected component intersecting the data — 3,489 genes and 9,710 edges (mean degree 5.57).
- Validation cohort: [METABRIC](../datasets/brca_metabric.md) breast cancer (n = 1903), Supplementary Fig. 14.
- ICI regimen breakdown: 25 PD-1/PD-L1 monotherapy, 15 PD-1/PD-L1 + CTLA-4 combo, 4 PD-1/PD-L1 + other, 1 CTLA-4 monotherapy. Specific agents included [nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md), [ipilimumab](../drugs/ipilimumab.md), and [avelumab](../drugs/avelumab.md) (drawn from the parent Zamarin cohort and cited combination-ICI studies with [olaparib](../drugs/olaparib.md) and [durvalumab](../drugs/durvalumab.md)).

## Key findings

- **Total curvature κ<sub>G</sub> stratifies OS after ICI**: low-curvature (n = 12) vs high-curvature (n = 33) Kaplan–Meier comparison yielded **log-rank p = 0.00047**. Median OS was 7.4 mo (low) vs 20.3 mo (high curvature) — Table 2. [PMID:34819508](../papers/34819508.md)
- **Curvature beats standard genomic biomarkers**: in the same cohort, TMB p = 0.03153, LST p = 0.42865, FGA p = 0.19568. Curvature outperformed TMB by roughly two orders of magnitude in log-rank p-value.
- **Curvature is ICI-specific, not prognostic at baseline**: when the curvature pipeline was applied to MSK-IMPACT data from HGSOC patients who did *not* receive ICI, total curvature was not significantly associated with OS (Supplementary Fig. 8). The authors interpret this as evidence the biomarker is predictive of ICI response rather than generally prognostic.
- **Top curvature-ranked genes recapitulate HGSOC biology**: [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) had the largest positive Δκ<sub>risk</sub> (0.209) across every sub-analysis (risk, OS, primary vs metastasis, topology reference), reflecting its functional centrality. Other top positive-Δκ genes included [ATXN1](../genes/ATXN1.md), [EP300](../genes/EP300.md), [SMAD2](../genes/SMAD2.md), [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md), [SRC](../genes/SRC.md), [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md), and [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md); top negative-Δκ genes included [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md), SHC1, [PTK2](../genes/PTK2.md), [AR](../genes/AR.md), [MYC](../genes/MYC.md), and [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) (Table 1).
- **Total curvature correlates with FGA but not TMB**: Pearson r p-values were TMB p = 0.9674, FGA p = 0.0059, LST p = 0.0867. FGA alone is *not* prognostic for OS in this cohort despite the correlation, supporting that curvature captures network-level information beyond CNA burden.
- **Cohort-level clinical features that differ by curvature group**: age at ICI start (p = 0.023), OS (p = 0.007), sample type (all 13 primaries in high-curvature group, p = 0.010), FGA (p = 0.005), LST (p = 0.024). TMB, platinum status, BRCA1/2 mutation status, and ICI target distribution did not differ between groups.
- **Generalizability**: the curvature method was validated on the [METABRIC](../datasets/brca_metabric.md) breast cancer cohort (n = 1,903) in Supplementary Fig. 14.

## Genes & alterations

- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — loss-of-function mutations and CNAs are the defining HGSOC alteration; TP53 ranked #1 by positive Δκ<sub>risk</sub> (0.209) and topped every sub-analysis, validating the methodology against known HGSOC biology.
- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), [MYC](../genes/MYC.md), [CCNE1](../genes/CCNE1.md), [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) — cited as canonical HGSOC oncogenes commonly overexpressed via CNA-driven aneuploidy; together they drive G1→S progression and centrosome dysregulation downstream of TP53 loss. [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) and [AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md) appear in the curvature-ranked gene lists.
- [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) — appears in the negative Δκ<sub>risk</sub> list; mechanistically linked to AKT-driven p53 degradation in the paper's pathway model (Fig. 3).
- [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) / [BRCA2](../genes/BRCA2.md) — captured as clinical covariates (10/45 patients mutated); no association with curvature group (p = 1.000). [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) also appears in the top-50 positive Δκ<sub>risk</sub> list (rank 43, Δκ = 0.003).
- [EP300](../genes/EP300.md), [SMAD2](../genes/SMAD2.md), [PIK3R1](../genes/PIK3R1.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md), [ATXN1](../genes/ATXN1.md), [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) — high-positive Δκ<sub>risk</sub> genes flagged as putative network drivers of ICI-modulated robustness.
- [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md) — top-ranked negative-Δκ<sub>risk</sub> gene (−0.064), the largest decrease in curvature between low- and high-risk groups.

The paper studies copy-number alteration (CNA) signal at the gene level, not point mutations; alterations are integer copy numbers per gene (range [0, 38]) mapped from FACETS segments to GRCh37 gene coordinates.

## Clinical implications

- Total network curvature κ<sub>G</sub>, derived from gene-level CNAs over a fixed PPI topology, is a candidate **predictive biomarker for OS** in HGSOC patients treated with ICIs — distinct from prognostic biomarkers because it had no significant association with OS in HGSOC patients who did not receive ICI.
- Curvature outperformed TMB, FGA, and LST in this cohort. The authors note that traditional HGSOC ICI biomarkers — TMB (low in HGSOC), PD-L1 IHC, TILs — have been inconsistent or unhelpful in HGSOC and propose curvature as a complement.
- The method requires only DNA copy-number data plus a PPI scaffold, making it applicable to any MSK-IMPACT-style targeted CNA assay; it does not depend on RNA, IHC, or immune-cell quantification.
- The authors explicitly position curvature as a tool to be combined with FGA in larger validation cohorts.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Small cohort**: n = 45 ICI-treated patients, with a 12/33 low/high split — adequate for a proof-of-concept Kaplan–Meier signal but underpowered for multivariate modeling. The authors call for larger datasets to validate.
- **Single time point**: only one CNA snapshot per patient. The "change in curvature" is computed by comparing risk groups (or topology references), not longitudinal samples.
- **Cutoff selection**: the 25th-percentile split was chosen by inspection of the sorted curvature curve; a maximally-selected log-rank cutoff was reported as comparable (Supplementary Fig. 7) but the curve-shape choice is admitted to be partly arbitrary.
- **PFS not significant**: no significant PFS difference in this cohort; only OS differs by curvature group. The authors cite a broader pattern of ICI affecting OS without PFS in HGSOC.
- **No PD-L1 / TIL comparison**: insufficient tissue prevented head-to-head comparison with established biomarkers in the same patients.
- **PPI scaffold dependence**: the curvature pipeline assumes HPRD-derived interactions are an adequate proxy for functional connectivity; alternative interactomes were not evaluated here.
- **No mechanistic validation in patients**: the top-ranked genes were validated only against known HGSOC biology and a referenced mouse-model study; functional perturbation experiments in patient material were not performed.
