**PMID:** 29657128 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.ccell.2018.03.018 · **Journal:** Cancer Cell (2018)

## TL;DR

Hellmann et al. performed whole-exome sequencing on tumor and paired germline DNA from 75 patients with advanced non-small-cell lung cancer ([NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md)) treated with combined PD-1 plus CTLA-4 blockade ([nivolumab](../drugs/nivolumab.md) + [ipilimumab](../drugs/ipilimumab.md)) on the CheckMate-012 trial. High tumor mutation burden (TMB; >median 158 nonsynonymous mutations) was strongly and independently associated with improved objective response (51% vs 13%), durable clinical benefit (65% vs 34%), and progression-free survival (median 17.1 vs 3.7 months, HR 0.41). TMB was independent of PD-L1 expression, and in multivariable analysis incorporating PD-L1, histology, smoking status, performance status, and tumor burden, TMB was the strongest feature associated with efficacy ([PMID:29657128](../papers/29657128.md)).

## Cohort & data

- **n = 75** patients with advanced [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) (79% non-squamous, 21% squamous; 88% stage IV) treated with nivolumab + ipilimumab as part of the CheckMate-012 study.
- Tumor and paired blood underwent [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md); mean target coverage 148X (IQR 116–182X) in tumors, 81X (IQR 70–98X) in normal; 94% of targets at ≥20X depth in tumors.
- Dataset: [nsclc_mskcc_2018](../datasets/nsclc_mskcc_2018.md).
- 60/75 (80%) were current/former smokers; PD-L1 expression was known in 70/75 (93%).
- Predictive fidelity of TMB was re-evaluated when restricting to genes covered by the [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) 468-gene panel and the FoundationOne 315-gene panel; both maintained similar predictive value to WES-derived TMB ([PMID:29657128](../papers/29657128.md)).

## Key findings

- **TMB higher in responders**: median TMB 273 mutations in patients with complete/partial response (n = 24) vs 114 in stable/progressive disease (n = 51), Mann-Whitney p = 0.0004. Median TMB 210 vs 113 for durable clinical benefit (DCB, n = 37) vs no durable benefit (NDB, n = 38), p = 0.0071 ([PMID:29657128](../papers/29657128.md)).
- **High TMB (>median 158 mutations) vs low TMB**:
  - ORR 51% vs 13% (odds ratio 6.97, 95% CI 2.19–19.0, Fisher's exact p = 0.0005)
  - DCB rate 65% vs 34% (OR 3.55, 95% CI 1.3–8.64, p = 0.011)
  - PFS median 17.1 vs 3.7 months (Mantel-Haenszel HR 0.41, 95% CI 0.23–0.73, log-rank p = 0.0024).
- **ROC for TMB**: AUC 0.75 (95% CI 0.62–0.88, p = 0.0006) for objective response; AUC 0.68 (95% CI 0.56–0.8, p = 0.0076) for DCB.
- **TMB and PD-L1 are independent**: no correlation between TMB and PD-L1 expression (Spearman r = −0.087, p = 0.48). Median TMB was 162 in PD-L1-positive vs 135 in PD-L1-negative tumors (p = 0.89).
- **Multivariable analysis**: incorporating TMB, PD-L1, histology, smoking status, performance status, and tumor burden — TMB was independently associated with ORR (p = 0.001) and PFS (p = 0.002). Multivariable ROC AUC for ORR = 0.869; for PFS at 12 months = 0.831.
- **Composite TMB + PD-L1**: combined high TMB (>median) AND PD-L1 ≥1% had significantly higher ORR than either alone or neither (chi-square for trend p < 0.0001; PFS log rank for trend p = 0.0072). 4 of 5 PD-L1-negative responders had high TMB ([absolute](../methods/absolute.md) range 307–1175 mutations, TCGA percentile rank 72nd–98th).
- **Nonsynonymous TMB outperforms total TMB**: stronger association with ORR and PFS than total (including silent) variants, supporting the neoantigen hypothesis.
- **Clonal neoantigen burden** more predictive of PFS than total predicted neoantigen burden (log rank p = 0.04 for clonal; p = 0.07 for total). TMB and predicted neoantigen burden were highly correlated (Spearman r = 0.92, p < 0.0001).
- **Generalizability**: TMB distribution and transition/transversion ratio in this cohort were similar to [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) tumors profiled by TCGA. Clinical features mirrored the broader CheckMate-012 enrollment ([PMID:29657128](../papers/29657128.md)).

## Genes & alterations

- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)** — only MutSigCV-significant recurrent gene enriched in responders (odds ratio 2.9, Fisher's exact p = 0.048). [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutations were also associated with increased mutation burden in both the combination immunotherapy cohort and TCGA NSCLCs, consistent with TP53's known link to smoking-associated mutagenesis ([PMID:29657128](../papers/29657128.md)).
- **[STK11](../genes/STK11.md)** — zero responses among 7 patients with [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) mutations; exclusively associated with resistance but did not reach statistical significance (small numbers). Consistent with prior reports of a T-cell-excluded phenotype in STK11-mutant tumors ([PMID:29657128](../papers/29657128.md)).
- **[PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md)** — zero responses among 4 patients with [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) mutations; resistance association did not reach significance ([PMID:29657128](../papers/29657128.md)).
- **[IFNGR1](../genes/IFNGR1.md)** — mutations (n = 3) found exclusively in responders ([PMID:29657128](../papers/29657128.md)).
- **[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)** — discussed in context of prior reports of TP53/KRAS mutation status and PD-1 blockade response in lung adenocarcinoma; this paper did not find a distinct [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) association beyond what is reported in PD-1 monotherapy literature ([PMID:29657128](../papers/29657128.md)).

## Clinical implications

- **TMB as a predictive biomarker for combination PD-1 + CTLA-4 immunotherapy**: the authors argue TMB should be incorporated into trials examining PD-(L)1 with CTLA-4 blockade in NSCLC. Based in part on these data, TMB assessment was incorporated into the Phase III CheckMate-227 trial ([PMID:29657128](../papers/29657128.md)).
- **Targeted NGS panels are clinically practical TMB surrogates**: TMB estimated from [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) (468 genes) and FoundationOne (315 genes) was similarly predictive of efficacy as WES-derived TMB, supporting use of routine clinical panels for prospective TMB measurement.
- **TMB + PD-L1 as a composite biomarker**: combining TMB and PD-L1 explains "curious exceptions" (PD-L1-negative responders with high TMB) and improves predictive accuracy over either marker alone.
- **Low TMB / PD-L1-negative patients remain a refractory population**: combination immunotherapy does NOT overcome the negative predictive impact of low TMB. These patients warrant alternative strategies ([PMID:29657128](../papers/29657128.md)).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Retrospective design**: tissue was not available for all CheckMate-012 patients; insufficient tissue precluded additional expression analysis (e.g., bulk/single-cell RNA-seq, TCR-seq, multiplex IHC).
- **Small numbers limit individual-gene associations**: STK11 (n = 7) and PTEN (n = 4) resistance signals did not reach statistical significance despite zero responses; larger cohorts needed to define "immunologic driver" landscape.
- **Neoantigen prediction is incomplete**: predicted neoantigen burden was not more predictive than TMB; HLA-shuffling experiments in prior work suggest patient-specific HLA-peptide affinity alone is inadequate. Future work needs to incorporate clonality, fitness, and TCR-binding nuances.
- **Mechanism of TMB benefit unresolved**: the authors note it remains unclear whether combination PD-1 + CTLA-4 simply broadens responses among TMB-high patients or modestly steepens the TMB-benefit slope vs PD-1 monotherapy.
- **WES is not routinely clinically deployable**: turnaround and scale limit prospective use; targeted panels and cell-free DNA TMB (bTMB) are emerging alternatives but still being validated.
- **Cohort restricted to first-line advanced NSCLC**: acquired resistance landscape, post-immunotherapy genomics, and TMB dynamics over treatment were not assessed.
