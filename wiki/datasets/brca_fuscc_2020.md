# FUSCC FUTURE Trial — Refractory Metastatic TNBC

## Overview

The brca_fuscc_2020 cohort was generated by the FUTURE trial (NCT03805399), a phase Ib/II single-center open-label umbrella trial conducted at Fudan University Shanghai Cancer Center. It enrolled 69 refractory metastatic triple-negative breast cancer ([BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md)) patients out of 87 screened between October 18, 2018 and March 25, 2020. The trial combined immunohistochemistry-based TNBC molecular subtyping (LAR / IM / BLIS / MES) with targeted NGS profiling to assign patients to one of seven biomarker-matched treatment arms. [PMID:32719455](../papers/32719455.md)

## Composition

- **n = 69** enrolled patients (median age not specified in corpus); all with refractory metastatic TNBC.
- Heavily pretreated: median 3 prior antitumor regimens in the metastatic setting (range 1–8); ≥99% had received taxanes, 86% anthracyclines, 88% platinums.
- 81% had ≥2 metastatic organs; 67% progressed within 6 months of first-line chemotherapy.
- Tumor biopsies taken at recurrence/metastasis; IHC for [AR](../genes/AR.md), CD8, and [FOXC1](../genes/FOXC1.md) used as surrogate subtype markers.
- Data cutoff: April 7, 2020; median follow-up 9.2 months (IQR 5.3–12.2). [PMID:32719455](../papers/32719455.md)

## Assays / panels (linked)

- [FUSCC 484-gene NGS panel](../methods/fuscc-ngs-484-panel.md) — somatic and germline variant calling on metastatic tumor biopsies.
- [Immunohistochemistry](../methods/immunohistochemistry.md) — IHC staining for AR, CD8, and FOXC1 for molecular subtype assignment.

## Papers using this cohort

- [PMID:32719455](../papers/32719455.md) — Jiang et al. (2021) FUTURE trial: molecular subtyping and genomic profiling in refractory metastatic TNBC; ITT ORR 29.0% (20/69); anti-PD-1 arm ORR 52.6% in IM-subtype patients.

## Notable findings derived from this cohort

- Intention-to-treat ORR was 29.0% (20/69; 95% CI 18.7%–41.2%) across all seven biomarker-matched arms in heavily pretreated metastatic TNBC. [PMID:32719455](../papers/32719455.md)
- Anti-PD-1 ([camrelizumab](../drugs/camrelizumab.md)) plus [nab-paclitaxel](../drugs/nab-paclitaxel.md) achieved an ORR of 52.6% (10/19; 95% CI 28.9%–75.6%) in the immunomodulatory (IM) subtype. [PMID:32719455](../papers/32719455.md)
- Most frequently mutated genes in the cohort: [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (72%), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) (18%), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) (10%), [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) (9%), [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) (9%); [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation frequency was significantly lower than the MSKCC metastatic TNBC cohort. [PMID:32719455](../papers/32719455.md)
- Metastasis-enriched low-frequency alterations compared to primary TNBC: [PTPRD](../genes/PTPRD.md), [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md), [PLCG1](../genes/PLCG1.md), [ARID1B](../genes/ARID1B.md), [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md), and [FAM47C](../genes/FAM47C.md). [PMID:32719455](../papers/32719455.md)
- [TOP2A](../genes/TOP2A.md) somatic mutations detected in 2/6 non-PR patients (0/10 PR patients) on the anti-PD-1 arm, nominated as a candidate negative predictor of ICI response. [PMID:32719455](../papers/32719455.md)
