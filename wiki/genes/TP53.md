# TP53

## Overview

TP53 (Tumor Protein P53) encodes the p53 transcription factor, the most frequently mutated gene across all human cancers. p53 acts as a master regulator of the DNA damage response, cell cycle arrest, apoptosis, and senescence. TP53 is inactivated by missense mutation (often with dominant-negative gain-of-function effects), deletion, or MDM2-mediated degradation. Its mutation frequency varies substantially by cancer type, and in the metastatic setting TP53 is often enriched relative to primary tumors. Germline TP53 mutations define Li-Fraumeni syndrome.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Somatic mutation identified in the TCGA PCC/PGL cohort (pcpg_tcga_pub, n=173) as part of a cancer-relevant gene scan alongside [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md) and [ARNT](../genes/ARNT.md). [PMID:28162975](../papers/28162975.md)
- Germline TP53 S215N missense identified in a pediatric AML patient in the PIPseq program (n=101, Columbia University); explained lack of response to conventional therapy and increased other-cancer risk. [PMID:28007021](../papers/28007021.md)
- Significantly mutated in both EAC and ESCC in the TCGA esophageal carcinoma cohort (stes_tcga_pub, n=164 esophageal); notably mutated in only 1 of 4 ESCC3 tumors. [PMID:28052061](../papers/28052061.md)
- Mutated in 27% of HR+/HER2− metastatic breast cancer (mBC) vs 20% of HR+/HER2− early breast cancer (eBC) in 216 mBC patients (Fisher p=0.13, not statistically significant); TP53 mutation is not enriched in the metastatic setting for HR+/HER2− subtype. [PMID:28027327](../papers/28027327.md)
- TP53 recurrently altered at baseline in SCLC PDX models; alterations preserved through acquired cisplatin/etoposide resistance with no evidence of additional acquired TP53 mutations under chemotherapy selection [PMID:28196596](../papers/28196596.md).
- TP53 significantly enriched in the unknown mitogenic driver (UMD) subset of 860 metastatic [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) patients profiled by MSK-IMPACT (p<0.05 vs level 1–4 cohort); elevated in heavy-smoker UMD samples [PMID:28336552](../papers/28336552.md).
- Single TP53 R248W missense event observed in an acral lentiginous melanoma ([ACRM](../cancer_types/ACRM.md)) cohort of 34 patients; MDM2/TP53 (apoptosis/senescence) pathway altered in 17% of ALM patients [PMID:28373299](../papers/28373299.md).
- Driver SNV in lung adenocarcinoma (LUAD); not associated with pre-operative ctDNA detection within LUADs in the TRACERx circulating tumor DNA study [PMID:28445469](../papers/28445469.md)
- E258K mutation in 1/19 sequenced oligodendroglioma tumors [PMID:28472509](../papers/28472509.md)
- Most frequently altered gene in pan-cancer MSK-IMPACT analysis (41% of 10,336 samples across 62 tumor types); largely truncating/splice-disrupting inactivating mutations; significantly enriched vs TCGA in prostate ([PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md)), chromophobe renal cell carcinoma ([CHRCC](../cancer_types/CHRCC.md)), glioblastoma, and gastric cancer [PMID:28481359](../papers/28481359.md)
- Mutated in 39.7% of CCEC; enriched in serous-like CCECs; co-enriched with PPP2R1A mutations in serous-like molecular profiles [PMID:28485815](../papers/28485815.md)
- Alterations rise stepwise with stage/grade in NMIBC (p<0.001); co-listed with MDM2 as part of the TP53/MDM2 pathway enriched at higher tumor stage and grade [PMID:28583311](../papers/28583311.md)
- Significantly enriched in CCA Clusters 1 and 2 (p < 0.001); mutation associated with increased structural variant burden (q < 0.1) [PMID:28667006](../papers/28667006.md)
- Germline variants predominantly restricted to SHH medulloblastoma in the 491-sample ICGC cohort [PMID:28726821](../papers/28726821.md)
- Most frequently altered tumor suppressor across 500 metastatic solid tumors (266/500, 53.2%); also recurrent in fusions (n=11) as a tumor-suppressor breakage event [PMID:28783718](../papers/28783718.md)
- Present at 27% locoregional / 30% metastatic noncastrate / 48% mCRPC prostate cancer; clonal early events in matched primaries of patients who progress to metastasis, consistent with aggressive behavior [PMID:28825054](../papers/28825054.md)
- Recurrent driver in DLBCL; mutually exclusive with [KLHL6](../genes/KLHL6.md); CRISPR-enriched (loss of TP53 increases fitness) [PMID:28985567](../papers/28985567.md)
- Mutated in 48% of MIBC; enriched in basal-squamous and neuronal subtypes; co-occurs with [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) and [E2F3](../genes/E2F3.md) amplification; mutually exclusive with [MDM2](../genes/MDM2.md) amplification and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) loss; enriched in genome-doubled tumors (p < 10⁻⁷) [PMID:28988769](../papers/28988769.md)
- Pan-sarcoma SMG; mutated in 50% of LMS (40/80); deep deletions in 9% LMS, 16% UPS, 12% MFS [PMID:29100075](../papers/29100075.md)
- Most frequently mutated gene (73%) in metastatic gastric/gastroesophageal junction cancer (HER2-positive cohort); enriched relative to TCGA EGC non-MSI-H (62%) [PMID:29122777](../papers/29122777.md)
- Mutated in 78% of mCRC cases; the only gene significantly enriched in metastatic vs early-stage colorectal primaries. [PMID:29316426](../papers/29316426.md)
- Altered in 67% of durable clinical benefit (DCB) and 54% of no-durable-benefit (NDB) NSCLC patients on anti-PD-(L)1 therapy; no significant enrichment for response or non-response. [PMID:29337640](../papers/29337640.md)
- Co-mutation with HER2 (ERBB2) enriched in patients without clinical benefit to neratinib (nominal p=0.018), associated with cell-cycle pathway co-alterations in pan-tumor HER2-mutant basket trial. [PMID:29420467](../papers/29420467.md)
- Somatic mutations present in HPV(−) vulvar squamous cell carcinomas; named the most commonly mutated gene in HPV(−) vulvar SCC consistent with prior literature. [PMID:29422544](../papers/29422544.md)
- Six LP/PV carriers (21.4% of LP/PV carriers) in a pediatric cancer cohort; strongest single-gene burden-test association (OR=32.8, p=7.83x10^-8). Recurrent c.586C>T p.(Arg196*) detected twice. Three of six carriers (50%) developed secondary malignant neoplasms; carriers presented with rhabdomyosarcoma, osteosarcoma, or BCP-ALL [PMID:29489754](../papers/29489754.md)
- Called as a KIRC SMG by both MutSig2CV and MuSiC2 on the TCGA MC3 open-access MAF; one of the canonical pan-cancer drivers consistently recovered across callers [PMID:29596782](../papers/29596782.md)
- Confirmed established prostate cancer driver in the SU2C/PCF 1,013-sample cohort; enriched in metastatic vs. primary tumors; part of the metastasis-enrichment genomic marker set proposed for prospective risk stratification [PMID:29610475](../papers/29610475.md)
- Predominantly mutated (not fused) across cancer types; an exception is SARC where both TP53 fusion and mutation events were observed in the TCGA pan-cancer fusion landscape [PMID:29617662](../papers/29617662.md)
- The only somatic mutation positively associated with high aneuploidy in the pan-cancer linear model (coefficient +0.13); even with p53 inhibited via SV40 large-T in AALE cells, chr_3p deletion still slowed proliferation initially, suggesting non-TP53 mechanisms also restrain aneuploidy [PMID:29622463](../papers/29622463.md)
- TP53 is the largest hub of somatic–somatic interactions pan-cancer: co-occurs with IDH1, ATRX, PPP2R1A, RB1, CDKN2A; mutually exclusive with PIK3CA, HRAS, CTNNB1, ARID1A, FGFR3; context-dependent (mutually exclusive with KRAS in COAD/READ/LUAD but significantly co-occurring in PAAD); enriched in ≥10 multiplatform iClusters [PMID:29625049](../papers/29625049.md)
- TP53 mutation co-occurs with cell-cycle alterations (CCNE1 amp, CDKN2A mut, RB1 loss, CDK6/E2F3 amp) and is mutually exclusive with CDKN2A deletion pan-cancer; TP53 is among the three genes enriched in ≥10 multiplatform iClusters (with KRAS and PIK3CA) [PMID:29625050](../papers/29625050.md)
- TP53 is mutated in 33% (5/15) of bladder cancer organoid lines in a biobank of 15 patient-derived lines; nuclear p53 immunostaining was concordant with mutation calls [PMID:29625057](../papers/29625057.md)
- Only MutSigCV-significant recurrently mutated gene enriched in responders to combination PD-1 + CTLA-4 in NSCLC (odds ratio 2.9, Fisher's exact p=0.048); TP53 mutations associated with increased TMB in both CheckMate-012 and TCGA NSCLCs, consistent with smoking-associated mutagenesis [PMID:29657128](../papers/29657128.md)
- Cited as the only functional region more frequently mutated than CTCF-binding-site hotspots in gastric cancer, occurring in ~50% of tumors; used as benchmark to contextualize the significance of recurrent non-coding CBS hotspot mutations [PMID:29670109](../papers/29670109.md)
- Most frequently mutated gene in SMC Korean breast cancer cohort (47.9%, 89/186), significantly higher than TCGA (32.0%, LR p=5.0e-5); subtype prevalence: TNBC 87.5%, HER2+ 75%, ER+/HER2+ 64.3%, ER+ 23.5%; independently associated with SMC cohort status after subtype adjustment (MLR p=0.035) [PMID:29713003](../papers/29713003.md)
- Biallelic inactivation by mutation plus 17p copy loss defines DLBCL genetic cluster C2; CLUMPS structural analysis localizes mutations to the DNA-binding site and Zn2+-coordinating residues; associated with independently inferior PFS and OS in multivariable Cox analysis [PMID:29713087](../papers/29713087.md)
- Enriched in extrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma (EHC) vs intrahepatic; mutually exclusive with IDH1, BAP1, and FGFR2; co-occurs with CDKN2A/CDKN2B; in a 189-patient prospective NGS cohort [PMID:29848569](../papers/29848569.md)
- Significantly more common in metastases of all receptor subtypes except HR-/HER2+; biomarker of poor prognosis in endocrine-resistant metastatic breast cancer (n=2,732-tumor combined cohort) [PMID:30205045](../papers/30205045.md)
- TP53 pathway alterations in 61% of secondary MIBC (progression from prior non-muscle-invasive bladder cancer); this group had significantly worse cancer-specific survival after cisplatin-based NAC vs cystectomy alone (p=0.002) [PMID:30290956](../papers/30290956.md)
- TP53 mutations associated with broad drug resistance and trend toward elesclomol sensitivity in Beat AML ex vivo drug-sensitivity profiling (n=672 AML specimens) [PMID:30333627](../papers/30333627.md)
- Mutated in 32.5% of advanced HCC (MSK-IMPACT cohort); mutually exclusive with WNT/CTNNB1 pathway alterations (P=0.003); enriched in virally mediated HCC (45% vs. 21%, P=0.001) [PMID:30373752](../papers/30373752.md)
- Most commonly mutated gene (58%) in gallbladder carcinoma (GBCA) WES cohort (n=57); trend toward enrichment in Chilean tumors (76%, NS after adjustment); mutually exclusive with ATM mutations (trend) [PMID:30427539](../papers/30427539.md)
- TP53 is the most frequent nonsynonymous SNV target in EOPC prostate cancer (6% of cases); 8 biallelic inactivation events were observed; parallel biallelic inactivation of TP53 and PTEN was seen in subclones of the multi-region tumour PCA041, illustrating co-operative late events [PMID:30537516](../papers/30537516.md).
- TP53 protein-coding mutations are common in IDH-mutant non-codeleted gliomas and were among the most frequently detected alterations in CSF ctDNA; 100% concordance between CSF and matched tumour tissue was observed for truncal TP53 mutations in non-hypermutated cases (10/10 LGG pairs, 20/20 GBM pairs) [PMID:30675060](../papers/30675060.md).
- R273 hotspot missense mutations associated with >10-fold elevation of TP53-S315 phosphorylation (paired t-test p=0.001) in colon cancer; finding confirmed in CPTAC breast and ovarian cohorts (coad_cptac_2019) [PMID:31031003](../papers/31031003.md)
- Altered in 13% (10/80) of metastatic panNET patients; all calls classified as likely oncogenic; no independent OS effect detected in the panet_msk_2018 cohort [PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)
- Mutated in 49.2% of cHCC-ICC (significantly higher than HCC 31% or ICC 22–38%); R249S AFB1-signature hotspot in 25.8% of TP53-mutant cases (all HBV+); focal deletion/loss in 46%; trunk driver in phylogenetic trees of cHCC-ICC [PMID:31130341](../papers/31130341.md)
- Among the most frequently altered genes in mCRPC (444 tumors, n=429 patients); co-occurs with RB1; associated with shorter time on first-line ARSI (P=0.046, CPE=0.609); not significantly prognostic for overall survival in this cohort (P=0.072) [PMID:31061129](../papers/31061129.md)
- Promoter hypomethylation-driven MDM4 exon-6 inclusion (MDM4-FL) marks TP53 wild-type lines sensitive to MDM2 inhibitor nutlin-3a in the CCLE dataset [PMID:31068700](../papers/31068700.md)
- Germline P/LP alleles tied to Li-Fraumeni syndrome; identified among pathogenic germline variants (13% prevalence) in a 923-patient glioma genomic profiling cohort [PMID:31263031](../papers/31263031.md)
- Recurrently mutated in upper tract urothelial carcinoma (UTUC, n=37 high-grade tumors) at frequencies not significantly different from TCGA urothelial bladder carcinoma [PMID:31278255](../papers/31278255.md)
- TP53 was the top co-occurring driver mutation in rectal cancer tumoroids and the 287-patient MSK rectal cancer cohort (rectal_msk_2019) [PMID:31591597](../papers/31591597.md).
- TP53 altered in 82% of osteosarcoma PDX models and 70% of Ewing sarcoma PDX models; intron-1 fusion breakpoints identified (TP53-FXR2 in Wilms KT-9, TP53-TNR as diagnostic-only); TP53 inactivation classifier (AUROC 0.89) identifies pathway-inactive tumors even among TP53-wild-type models; altered in 11% of neuroblastoma models [PMID:31693904](../papers/31693904.md).
- TP53 is an early IDHmut-noncodel glioma driver; cancer cell fraction (CCF) increases from initial to recurrence (P=0.037); CCF higher than IDH1 in recurrent IDHmut-noncodels (P=0.0018) [PMID:31748746](../papers/31748746.md)
- TP53 variants detected in WBC-matched cfDNA sequencing; can be misinterpreted as tumor-derived without matched WBC sequencing, confounding TMB and mutational-signature calls [PMID:31768066](../papers/31768066.md)
- TP53 mutation in 36% of GBM PDX (n=30/84); one PDX (GBM168) carried a private TP53 variant absent from the matched patient tumor, indicating engraftment-associated clonal selection [PMID:31852831](../papers/31852831.md)
- TP53 mutation among the most common co-mutations in TRK fusion-positive cancers across tumor types; IDH1-mutant GBM PDX with NTRK1-TPM3 fusion also carried TP53 mutations [PMID:31871300](../papers/31871300.md)
- Mutation associated with a small set of p53-pathway and DNA-damage-response proteins in CCLE proteomics; TP53/MDM2/CDKN1A knockdown sensitivities co-vary with cell-division-related complexes [PMID:31978347](../papers/31978347.md)
- Mutations enriched in the proliferative NS-LUAD subtype (p=4.99e-6, FDR=9.73e-4) and depleted in the steady subtype (p=4.63e-14, FDR=9.03e-12); ALK fusions co-occurring with TP53 mutations concentrated in the proliferative subtype (7 tumors), a known poor-prognosis combination [PMID:32015526](../papers/32015526.md)
- Driver in 954 pan-cancer tumors; 736 (77%) biallelic, 96% somatic-SNV + somatic-deletion. Pan-cancer associated with chromothripsis (OR=3.22, P=8.3e-35) across PCAWG [PMID:32025007](../papers/32025007.md)
- Mutations in 23 endometrial carcinoma tumors including all serous carcinomas; missense DNA-binding-domain hotspots (R248, R273) elevate p53 protein and dysregulate AURKA, XRN2; truncating mutations increase PLK1-T210 phosphorylation and mitotic-marker proteins; downstream trans-effects include PLK1, CDK1, CHEK1, XPO1, TPX2, KMT2D, MAP3K4 [PMID:32059776](../papers/32059776.md)
- TP53 mutated in 52% of synchronous DCIS and 54% of synchronous IDC-NSTs (P>0.05); 13/14 DCIS and 12/14 IDC-NST mutations were inactivating with LOH; 75% frequency in grade-3 vs 0% in grade-2 DCIS (P<0.001); 14% in pure DCIS [PMID:32220886](../papers/32220886.md)
- TP53 alterations associated with higher castration-resistance rates (1.5- to 5-fold range across pathway alterations) in metastatic castration-sensitive prostate cancer; OS association attenuated after multivariable adjustment [PMID:32220891](../papers/32220891.md)
- TP53 loss-of-function mutations or homozygous deletions in 56% of uterine leiomyosarcoma (uLMS); significantly more frequent in non-uterine LMS (71%, p=0.01); two germline pathogenic carriers including one with Li-Fraumeni syndrome [PMID:32299819](../papers/32299819.md)
- TP53 mutated less frequently in CDK12-biallelic inactivation (CDK12-Bi) prostate cancer compared to CDK12 wild-type [PMID:32317181](../papers/32317181.md)
- TP53 tumor-suppressor loss observed in UTUC PDX models alongside RB1 and CDKN2A/CDKN2B [PMID:32332851](../papers/32332851.md)
- Mutant tumors enriched in multi-omics cluster C1 (proximal-inflammatory) in [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) (CPTAC cohort, n=110); elevated cognate protein/phosphosite, upregulation of MMR (MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2) and DDR proteins (ATM, ATR, BRCA1), elevated EZH2 protein, downregulated Wnt-signaling proteins (AXIN1, TCF7L2), and outlier BRAF hyperphosphorylation [PMID:32649874](../papers/32649874.md)
- Mutated in 24/41 (58.5%) CDK4/6i-resistant vs 0/18 sensitive HR+/HER2- metastatic breast cancer biopsies; enriched in resistance but not sufficient in vitro (CRISPR knockout does not confer CDK4/6i resistance in MCF7 cells) [PMID:32404308](../papers/32404308.md)
- Among commonly mutated genes profiled in the JHCNC NF1 biorepository across plexiform neurofibroma and [MPNST](../cancer_types/MPNST.md) (23 patients, 55 samples); mutational profile preserved between tumor, derived cell line, and PDX [PMID:32561749](../papers/32561749.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [PHC](../cancer_types/PHC.md) / [PGNG](../cancer_types/PGNG.md): Somatic TP53 mutation identified as a cancer-relevant gene scan finding; not a primary driver in this low-mutation-burden tumor type. [PMID:28162975](../papers/28162975.md)
- [AML](../cancer_types/AML.md): Germline TP53 S215N — a functionally relevant mutation explaining conventional-therapy resistance and conferring elevated risk for secondary cancers; family counseling implications. [PMID:28007021](../papers/28007021.md)
- [ESCC](../cancer_types/ESCC.md) / [ESCA](../cancer_types/ESCA.md): TP53 is a significantly mutated gene in both ESCC and EAC; low frequency in ESCC3 distinguishes this subtype. [PMID:28052061](../papers/28052061.md)
- [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md): TP53 mutation rate (27%) in HR+/HER2− mBC is slightly above eBC (20%) but not statistically enriched (p=0.13); TP53 is not a metastasis-specific driver in HR+/HER2− breast cancer. [PMID:28027327](../papers/28027327.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- In PCC/PGL, somatic TP53 mutation occurred in a context of otherwise mutually exclusive driver events; driver gene mutual exclusivity was significant (p < 1e-4) across the 21-gene set. [PMID:28162975](../papers/28162975.md)
- In ESCC, TP53 is nearly universally mutated and does not show the same strong mutual exclusivity pattern as in PCC/PGL. [PMID:28052061](../papers/28052061.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Germline TP53 S215N in pediatric AML explained poor response to conventional therapy; this finding has implications for altered treatment strategies and second-cancer surveillance. [PMID:28007021](../papers/28007021.md)

## Open questions

- The functional significance of somatic TP53 mutation in PCC/PGL (a tumor type with low overall mutation burden) and its relationship to tumor aggressiveness requires further characterization. [PMID:28162975](../papers/28162975.md)
- Whether TP53 mutations in metastatic HR+/HER2− breast cancer influence response to CDK4/6 inhibitors or endocrine therapy requires prospective biomarker studies. [PMID:28027327](../papers/28027327.md)
