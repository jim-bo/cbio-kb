# MYO6

## Overview

MYO6 encodes an unconventional myosin motor protein that moves toward the minus (pointed) end of actin filaments — unique among myosins. It participates in endocytosis, vesicle trafficking, and Golgi complex organization. In cancer, MYO6 has been identified as a rare recurrent fusion partner with [NFIB](../genes/NFIB.md) on chromosome 6q in adenoid cystic carcinoma (ACC).

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Single-tumor NFIB fusion partner on chromosome 6q in ACC [PMID:26862087](../papers/26862087.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **ACC (adenoid cystic carcinoma)**: single-tumor MYO6–NFIB intrachromosomal fusion (chromosome 6q) [PMID:26862087](../papers/26862087.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- MYO6–NFIB fusion occurs alongside other NFIB rearrangements ([MYB](../genes/MYB.md), [MAP3K5](../genes/MAP3K5.md), [MYBL1](../genes/MYBL1.md), [RIMS1](../genes/RIMS1.md), [RPS6KA2](../genes/RPS6KA2.md)) in ACC cohort [PMID:26862087](../papers/26862087.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting reported in this corpus.

## Open questions

- The functional consequence of MYO6–NFIB fusion on tumor biology is unknown.
