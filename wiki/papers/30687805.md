**PMID:** 30687805 · **DOI:** 10.1200/PO.17.00267 · **Journal:** JCO Precision Oncology (2018)

## TL;DR

Raj et al. prospectively sequenced 96 tumor samples from 80 patients with metastatic well-differentiated pancreatic neuroendocrine tumors (panNETs) using the [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) matched tumor-normal NGS platform between May 2014 and March 2017. Somatic alterations were identified in 95% of patients, dominated by chromatin-remodeling factors ([DAXX](../genes/DAXX.md), [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md)), histone methyltransferases ([MEN1](../genes/MEN1.md), [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md)), and mTOR-pathway genes ([TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md)). The authors document grade progression, mTOR-inhibitor resistance mutations on serial biopsy, alkylator-induced hypermutation, and a 16% rate of clinically actionable germline pathogenic variants — motivating reconsideration of universal germline testing for panNET patients ([PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)).

## Cohort & data

- **80 patients / 96 samples** with metastatic well-differentiated [PANET](../cancer_types/PANET.md); 8 patients contributed 2 samples and 4 patients contributed 3 samples for serial sequencing ([PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)).
- **Cohort identifier:** [panet_msk_2018](../datasets/panet_msk_2018.md) (ClinicalTrials.gov NCT01775072); patients accrued between May 2014 and March 2017 at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center.
- **Baseline demographics:** median age 54 (range 10–79); 56% female; all patients had metastatic disease (liver 81%, lymph nodes 33%); WHO grade distribution 30% G1, 46% G2, 24% G3; median Ki-67 10% (range 1–80%) ([PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)).
- **Assay:** [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) matched tumor-normal hybridization-capture NGS. Panel versions used: pilot 275-gene (2 samples / 2%), version 1/[IMPACT341](../methods/IMPACT341.md) (not used in this cohort breakdown), [IMPACT410](../methods/IMPACT410.md) (16 samples / 17%), [IMPACT468](../methods/IMPACT468.md) (57 samples / 59%), and version 4 (21 samples / 22%). Median sequencing depth 665.5× (range 75×–1255×) ([PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)).
- **Germline panel:** 76 cancer-susceptibility genes called from the matched normal `.bam` with MuTect + GATK HaplotypeCaller; ExAC <1% population-frequency cutoff; ACMG variant interpretation ([PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)).
- **Loss of heterozygosity:** quantified via [FACETS](../methods/facets.md) allele-specific copy-number analysis on 95 of 96 samples.
- **Actionability:** annotated with the [OncoKB](../methods/oncokb-annotation.md) tier scale (levels 1–4).

## Key findings

- **Overall somatic detection rate:** 76 of 80 patients (95%) harbored at least one somatic alteration ([PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)).
- **Top recurrently altered genes (first sample per patient):** [MEN1](../genes/MEN1.md) 45/80 (56%), [DAXX](../genes/DAXX.md) 32/80 (40%), [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) 20/80 (25%), [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) 20/80 (25%), [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md) 14/80 (18%), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) 10/80 (13%), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) 10/80 (13%), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md) 9/80 (11%), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) 6/80 (8%), [KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) 7/80 (9%) ([PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)).
- **Pathway frequencies:** chromatin remodeling factors 52/80 (65%), histone methyltransferases 53/80 (66%), mTOR pathway 34/80 (43%), cell-cycle signaling 31/80 (39%). All 10 [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) alterations were classified as likely oncogenic ([PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)).
- **Tumor mutational burden tracks with grade:** average mutations/Mb were 2.74 (G1), 4.20 (G2), and 23.64 (G3). Median TMB across the full cohort was 2.95 mut/Mb (range 0–201.76) ([PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)).
- **Loss of heterozygosity is dominant:** LOH affecting ≥50% of the genome was identified in 55/95 sequenced samples (58%). LOH was significantly enriched among samples carrying [MEN1](../genes/MEN1.md), [DAXX](../genes/DAXX.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), or [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) alterations (q < 0.05). Recurrent LOH was observed on chromosomes 1, 2, 3, 6, 8, 10, 11, 15, 16, 21, and 22; presence of LOH trended toward improved overall survival (P = .06) ([PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)).
- **Survival (median follow-up 27.6 months; 14 deaths; median [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) 118.9 months, 95% CI 99.6–NR):**
  - 1-year OS in [MEN1](../genes/MEN1.md)-altered vs [WT](../cancer_types/WT.md): 98% (95% CI 84–99%) vs 82% (63–92%), P = .01.
  - 1-year OS in [DAXX](../genes/DAXX.md)- or [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md)-altered vs WT: 97% (83–99%) vs 83% (64–93%), P < .01.
  - 1-year OS in [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)-altered vs WT: 75% (13–96%) vs 95% (87–98%), P < .01.
  - No significant OS difference by mTOR-pathway or [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) status ([PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)).
- **Novel recurrently altered genes vs prior panNET series:** [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md) (18% — predominantly truncating loss-of-function; 17 mutated samples from 14 patients, 71% G2 and 24% G3) and [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) (8% — 10 mutated samples from 6 patients including V600E ×2 and non-V600 variants K601E, T599K, T310I, plus a compound G596D + E451K tumor). 50% of [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)-mutated samples were G3 ([PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)).
- **Serial-sample clonal evolution (12 patients sequenced at multiple time points):**
  - Patient 1 ([everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md) + [alpelisib](../drugs/alpelisib.md)/BYL719): acquired oncogenic [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) Q298* at progression; clone was present at low VAF in the pretreatment pancreas; tumor grade progressed G1→G3.
  - Patient 2 (everolimus 8 weeks): acquired [RHEB](../genes/RHEB.md) hotspot Y35S in a progressing liver lesion; allele fraction rose on continued observation; G1→G3.
  - Patient 3 (everolimus >9 months): post-progression liver biopsy lost an [IGF2](../genes/IGF2.md) G226S mutation and acquired [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) splice (X161_splice) and [AKT2](../genes/AKT2.md) G16D — no mutations shared with the pretreatment pancreas (clinically consistent with single primary; possible unrecognized clonal sweep).
  - Patient 4 ([vemurafenib](../drugs/vemurafenib.md) for [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E): acquired [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) Q61R at progression ([PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)).
- **Alkylator-induced hypermutation:** three patients re-sequenced after cytotoxic chemotherapy acquired >100 new mutations (117, 165, and 267). Two had received alkylators ([dacarbazine](../drugs/dacarbazine.md), [temozolomide](../drugs/temozolomide.md)) and showed a predominant C>T mutational signature and acquired mismatch-repair gene mutations; all three sampled tumors progressed G1→G3 ([PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)).
- **Germline pathogenic / likely pathogenic variants in 14 / 88 patients (16%):** high-penetrance — [MEN1](../genes/MEN1.md) (1), [VHL](../genes/VHL.md) (1), [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) (1); moderate-penetrance [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md) in 4 patients (5%); low-penetrance monoallelic [MUTYH](../genes/MUTYH.md) (4) and [APC](../genes/APC.md) I1307K (3); plus recessive [FANCC](../genes/FANCC.md) and [RECQL4](../genes/RECQL4.md) alleles and a [RAD50](../genes/RAD50.md) R1093* of uncertain actionability ([PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)).
- **Notable germline finding:** a 32-year-old with a germline [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) R1200W did not meet clinical criteria for tuberous sclerosis but had brain MRI evidence of tubers and radial bands — discovery was incidental to tumor-normal NGS ([PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)).
- **OncoKB-actionable somatic alterations:** Level 1/2a 0%; Level 2b 19/80 (24%) — [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) ×16, [TSC1](../genes/TSC1.md) ×1, [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E ×2; Level 3b 4 patients (5%) — [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) ×2, [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) K601E, [PTCH1](../genes/PTCH1.md) ×2; Level 4 14 patients (18%) — [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) ×8, [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) ×3, [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md) ×3, [MSH6](../genes/MSH6.md) ×2, [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md), [MSH2](../genes/MSH2.md), [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md) (each ×1) ([PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)).
- **Treatment response to [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md) was not predicted by mTOR-pathway mutation status:** among 12 patients with mTOR alterations who received everolimus, 17% regression / 50% stable / 8% progression; among 17 mTOR-WT patients on everolimus, 29% regression / 41% stable / 24% progression ([PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)).

## Genes & alterations

- [MEN1](../genes/MEN1.md) — somatic alteration in 56% (45/80); also recurrent germline driver (1 high-penetrance carrier). Associated with significantly improved 1-year OS (98% vs 82%, P = .01).
- [DAXX](../genes/DAXX.md) / [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) — somatic alterations in 40% and 25% respectively; together define the chromatin-remodeling / ALT axis; both associated with improved 1-year OS (97% vs 83%, P < .01) in this metastatic cohort, contrasting with prior reports in resected disease.
- [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) — somatic alterations in 25% (20/80); dominant mTOR-pathway driver; one high-penetrance germline R1200W carrier presenting incidentally (no clinical TSC phenotype); acquired splice mutation in one patient at [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md) progression.
- [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) — somatic in 13%; acquired Q298* in one patient at [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md) + [alpelisib](../drugs/alpelisib.md) progression.
- [RHEB](../genes/RHEB.md) — Y35S hotspot acquired at [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md) progression in one patient; allele fraction rose on continued observation.
- [AKT2](../genes/AKT2.md) — G16D acquired post-everolimus.
- [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md) — 18% of patients; predominantly truncating loss-of-function; enriched in G2/G3 tumors and in post-treatment biopsies; positioned as a panNET tumor suppressor.
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — 8% of patients; V600E plus several non-V600 alterations (K601E, T599K, T310I, compound G596D + E451K); enriched in post-treatment samples; significantly worse 1-year OS (75% vs 95%, P < .01); paired patient on [vemurafenib](../drugs/vemurafenib.md) acquired [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) Q61R at progression.
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — 13%; all calls classified as likely oncogenic; no independent OS effect.
- [IGF2](../genes/IGF2.md) — G226S lost in one patient on [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md), suggesting clonal replacement rather than direct resistance.
- [VHL](../genes/VHL.md), [CHEK2](../genes/CHEK2.md), [MUTYH](../genes/MUTYH.md), [APC](../genes/APC.md), [FANCC](../genes/FANCC.md), [RECQL4](../genes/RECQL4.md), [RAD50](../genes/RAD50.md) — germline pathogenic / likely pathogenic carriers (high-, moderate-, low-penetrance, recessive, or uncertain classes; see Table 2 of the paper).

## Clinical implications

- **Prognostic biomarkers in metastatic panNET:** [MEN1](../genes/MEN1.md), [DAXX](../genes/DAXX.md), and [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) alterations were associated with improved 1-year OS — opposite to reports in resected disease where DAXX/ATRX loss correlates with poor outcome ([PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)). The authors interpret this as a metastatic-stage–specific signal that needs confirmation.
- **[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) alterations mark aggressive, post-treatment disease** with significantly worse 1-year OS (P < .01) and warrant closer surveillance.
- **Universal germline testing for panNET is reasonable:** 16% pathogenic/likely-pathogenic carriers across high-, moderate-, and low-penetrance genes — including a [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) carrier missed by phenotype-based testing criteria — argues for genetic-risk assessment in all panNET patients ([PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)).
- **Targeted-therapy resistance mechanisms are detectable in real time:** acquired [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) Q298*, [RHEB](../genes/RHEB.md) Y35S, [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) splice, and [AKT2](../genes/AKT2.md) G16D after [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md); [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) Q61R after [vemurafenib](../drugs/vemurafenib.md) — supporting serial NGS on disease progression.
- **mTOR mutation status did not predict [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md) benefit** (response rates 17% vs 29%); the authors caution against using mTOR-pathway mutations as a single-marker selection criterion.
- **Alkylator chemotherapy ([temozolomide](../drugs/temozolomide.md), [dacarbazine](../drugs/dacarbazine.md)) induces marked hypermutation and MMR-gene mutations in panNET** — a previously unreported pattern in this disease that could be relevant to subsequent immunotherapy decisions.
- **OncoKB-actionable alterations affected 47% of patients** when including Levels 2b/3b/4, though no Level 1/2a evidence was available at the time of the report ([PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Sample size remains too small** to clarify genetic predictors of response or resistance to commonly used panNET therapies, the prognostic effect of individual alterations, or the typical patterns of clonal evolution; only 12 patients underwent multi-timepoint sequencing ([PMID:30687805](../papers/30687805.md)).
- **The MSK-IMPACT panel evolved across the accrual window** (preclinical pilot 275 → 341 → 410 → 468 genes), so some early-cohort samples were not interrogated for genes (e.g., [RHEB](../genes/RHEB.md)) that later became standard — affecting allele-frequency comparisons over time.
- **DAXX/ATRX/MEN1 survival benefit conflicts with prior resected-cohort data** showing the opposite direction; needs prospective replication in metastatic disease.
- **mTOR-pathway alteration prevalence (43%) is substantially higher than prior series**, but did not translate into a predictive biomarker for [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md) benefit in this cohort.
- **Single-institution, primarily tertiary-referred metastatic cohort** — generalizability to earlier-stage or community-treated panNETs is unclear.
- **Future direction:** the authors propose noninvasive (presumably ctDNA-based) serial NGS to enable real-time clonal-evolution profiling without repeat biopsy.
