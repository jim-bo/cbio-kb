# HEBP2

## Overview

HEBP2 (Heme Binding Protein 2) is a member of the SOUL/HBP family of heme-binding proteins. In cancer genomics, HEBP2 was identified as the 5′ partner in a novel gene fusion with NTRK2 detected by whole-genome sequencing in a regional metastatic cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma sample. This fusion may activate NTRK2 kinase signaling.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- HEBP2–NTRK2 fusion detected in sample CSCC_0011 from a WGS cohort of 25 regional metastatic [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md) patients; arises from an unbalanced translocation; HEBP2 is the 5′ donor and [NTRK2](NTRK2.md) the 3′ acceptor [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md):** Single-case HEBP2–NTRK2 fusion observed in regional metastatic head-and-neck CSCC by WGS; one of only two coding–coding fusions detected in the entire 25-sample cohort [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- HEBP2–NTRK2 fusion occurred in the [cscc_ranson_2022](../datasets/cscc_ranson_2022.md) sample that also harbored the TP53-mutant background; no co-occurrence data beyond this single case available [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- NTRK2 fusions are generally pan-cancer targets for TRK inhibitors ([larotrectinib](../drugs/larotrectinib.md), [entrectinib](../drugs/entrectinib.md)), although the specific HEBP2–NTRK2 fusion was not evaluated for kinase activity or drug sensitivity in this study [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md).

## Open questions

- Whether the HEBP2–NTRK2 fusion retains an intact NTRK2 kinase domain and is oncogenic has not been validated functionally.
