# CHUK

## Overview

CHUK (conserved helix-loop-helix ubiquitous kinase), also known as IKKα, encodes the alpha catalytic subunit of the IκB kinase (IKK) complex, a master regulator of NFκB signaling. In skin biology, CHUK is required for keratinocyte terminal differentiation — knockout mice and humans with defective CHUK fail to differentiate keratinocytes. A Sleeping-Beauty transposon screen previously implicated CHUK in keratinocyte tumorigenesis on a Pten-sensitized background. In cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma ([CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md)), recurrent truncating mutations with biallelic inactivation point to a tumor-suppressor role for CHUK in the skin epithelium.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Mutated in 10.8% of CSCC tumors (88 QC-passed WES/WGS tumors from a 10-study meta-analysis); high truncating-mutation frequency with biallelic alterations in most mutated tumors. Nominated as a novel CSCC driver gene (>10% frequency, not previously implicated in CSCC) [PMID:34272401](../papers/34272401.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md): 10.8% mutation rate in 88-tumor meta-analysis cohort; grouped with stress-response genes (CASP8, NFE2L2) mutated collectively in 32.5% of CSCC tumors; biallelic inactivation in most CHUK-mutated cases supports a tumor-suppressor function [PMID:34272401](../papers/34272401.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs within the stress-response pathway in CSCC alongside [CASP8](../genes/CASP8.md) and [NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md) [PMID:34272401](../papers/34272401.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting reported in this corpus. CHUK loss-of-function implicates NFκB pathway dysregulation in CSCC, which may influence response to immune-checkpoint blockade (the current standard of care for advanced CSCC) [PMID:34272401](../papers/34272401.md)

## Open questions

- No functional/mechanistic in vitro or in vivo validation was performed in the nominating study; CHUK's role as a CSCC driver rests on statistical nomination [PMID:34272401](../papers/34272401.md)
