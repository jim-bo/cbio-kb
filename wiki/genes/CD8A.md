# CD8A

## Overview

CD8A encodes the alpha chain of the CD8 co-receptor that defines cytotoxic T lymphocytes and is a canonical marker of adaptive anti-tumor immune infiltration.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Included in the 20-gene Immunologic Constant of Rejection (ICR) signature as a Th1/CD8 effector component, applied to the 348-patient AC-ICAM colon cancer cohort [PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- Colon adenocarcinoma — CD8A contributes to ICR-based stratification; NK and T cell subset abundances by ConsensusTME were highest in ICR-high tumors across all CMS subtypes [PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-expressed with [CD8B](../genes/CD8B.md), [IFNG](../genes/IFNG.md), [TBX21](../genes/TBX21.md), and the ICR cytotoxic effector module in ICR-high colon cancers [PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct CD8A-targeted therapy is evaluated; CD8A expression contributes to the prognostic ICR score [PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md).

## Open questions

- Independent contribution of CD8A vs the full ICR module is not dissected [PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md).
