# SEMA3C

## Overview

SEMA3C (semaphorin 3C) encodes a secreted member of the semaphorin family involved in axon guidance, angiogenesis, and neural-crest cell migration. Expression is prominent in neural-crest-derived lineages and has been associated with tumor invasion and therapy resistance in multiple cancer types.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- **LowMut melanocyte subpopulation marker (neural-crest lineage):** SEMA3C transcript expression characterizes the LowMut melanocyte subset in a normal human skin single-cell atlas — these cells carry fewer UV-attributable (SBS7) mutations and show clock-like (SBS1/SBS5) mutational patterns. No somatic driver mutations in SEMA3C are reported. [PMID:39975212](../papers/39975212.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[MEL](../cancer_types/MEL.md) / [SKIN](../cancer_types/SKIN.md):** SEMA3C expression aligns with neural-crest-lineage melanocyte states (WIMMS "AXL/Neuronal/Invasive", Belote et al. "MSC") that have been linked to melanoma invasiveness and drug resistance in external literature. The current paper establishes the normal skin context. [PMID:39975212](../papers/39975212.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-expressed with [VCAN](../genes/VCAN.md), [FBN1](../genes/FBN1.md), [PALLD](../genes/PALLD.md), [ITM2A](../genes/ITM2A.md), [TAGLN](../genes/TAGLN.md), [MYL9](../genes/MYL9.md), [MYLK](../genes/MYLK.md), [SGCE](../genes/SGCE.md), [HACD1](../genes/HACD1.md), [RGMB](../genes/RGMB.md), [TCF4](../genes/TCF4.md), [DAAM2](../genes/DAAM2.md), [NTNG1](../genes/NTNG1.md) as part of the LowMut neural-crest melanocyte gene signature. [PMID:39975212](../papers/39975212.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic finding reported in the current corpus. SEMA3C's role in cancer invasion biology makes it a candidate of interest in melanoma progression studies.

## Open questions

- Whether SEMA3C expression distinguishes melanoma subtypes (e.g., invasive/neural-crest-like vs. proliferative) and predicts therapy response remains an open question not addressed by the current corpus. [PMID:39975212](../papers/39975212.md)
