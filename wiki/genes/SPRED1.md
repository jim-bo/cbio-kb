# SPRED1

## Overview

SPRED1 (Sprouty Related EVH1 Domain Containing 1) encodes a negative regulator of the RAS/MAPK pathway that inhibits RAF activation. Germline loss-of-function mutations cause Legius syndrome (a RASopathy). In cancer, SPRED1 loss-of-function is functionally analogous to [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) inactivation and contributes to aberrant MAPK signaling. In melanoma, it appears as a secondary co-alteration in the context of NF1-altered or [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) Class 3 tumors.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Secondary RTK-RAS regulator contributing to NF1/BRAF Class 3 co-alteration patterns in melanoma; identified as a backseat co-alteration in driver-stratified immunotherapy outcomes study [PMID:33509808](../papers/33509808.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[MEL](../cancer_types/MEL.md)**: Co-alteration with NF1-loss and BRAF Class 3 mutations; contributes to MAPK pathway dysregulation in the context of co-altered tumors [PMID:33509808](../papers/33509808.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with NF1 loss, BRAF Class 3 mutations, and other RTK-RAS regulators ([CBL](../genes/CBL.md), [PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md), [RASA1](../genes/RASA1.md), [RAC1](../genes/RAC1.md), [SOS1](../genes/SOS1.md), [RAF1](../genes/RAF1.md)) in melanoma [PMID:33509808](../papers/33509808.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Tumors with NF1/SPRED1 co-alterations show high TMB and favorable outcomes on PD-1 monotherapy [PMID:33509808](../papers/33509808.md)

## Open questions

- Whether SPRED1 alone can act as a sole driver of melanoma (analogous to NF1 loss) or only acts as a co-alteration modifier is not defined in this cohort.
