**PMID:** 35235413 · **DOI:** 10.1200/PO.21.00365 · **Journal:** JCO Precision Oncology (2022)

## TL;DR

Gularte-Mérida et al. analyzed [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) clinical sequencing data from 4,561 targeted-therapy-naive colorectal cancer ([CRC](../cancer_types/COADREAD.md)) patients and identified 47 cases (1.03%) harboring dual RAS hotspot / [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E driver mutations (CRC-DD) — a phenotype that contradicts the textbook view of MAPK-driver mutual exclusivity. Using a novel [single-cell genotyping](../methods/single-cell-genotyping.md) method (96.3% detection rate, 92.1% genotype accuracy) applied to four cases, they demonstrated same-cell co-occurrence of dual driver mutations in 14%–95% of tumor cells. Compared with single-driver CRC (CRC-SD), CRC-DD showed enrichment for MSI-H status (27% v 8.7%, P=.0004), lower allelic imbalance favoring the oncogenic allele (32.5% v 51.3%, P=.0026), and lower fraction of genome altered (P=7.99×10⁻⁹). Findings have implications for understanding MAPK dosage biology and targeted-therapy selection.

## Cohort & data

- **Discovery cohort:** 4,561 CRC patients sequenced with [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) at Memorial Sloan Kettering; 47 (1.03%) had dual RAS hotspot/BRAF V600E driver mutations (CRC-DD). The CRC-DD subset is publicly available as cBioPortal study [crc_dd_2022](../datasets/crc_dd_2022.md).
- **Comparison cohort:** 271 CRC cases with a single MAPK pathway driver mutation (CRC-SD) from Yaeger et al. 2018 (`Cancer Cell` 33:125-136), and a wild-type RAS/RAF CRC group from the same cohort.
- **Cancer type:** [colorectal adenocarcinoma](../cancer_types/COAD.md) ([COADREAD](../cancer_types/COADREAD.md)); CRC-DD samples were predominantly primary tumors (89.3% v 59.4% in CRC-SD, P=.0001).
- **Assays:**
  - [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) — 468-gene clinical NGS panel for somatic mutations, CNAs, [select](../methods/select.md) fusions, and MSI.
  - Multiregional sequencing in 7 CRC-DD using an MSKCC custom AmpliSeq panel of 98 cancer-associated genes (2–4 sites per case).
  - [Single-cell genotyping (SCG)](../methods/single-cell-genotyping.md) on 2,094 cells across 4 CRC-DD cases (P-0021518, P-0061563, C-XPMDEU, P-0008729); FACS-isolated nuclei, multiplexed PCR with combinatorial barcode indexing, β-binomial mixture model genotype calling.
  - [FACETS](../methods/facets.md) for clonality and allelic imbalance (AI); [ABSOLUTE](../methods/absolute.md) for re-estimation of CCF on SCG-genotyped mutations; infSCITE for tumor evolution inference.
  - [Sanger sequencing](../methods/sanger-sequencing.md) for technical and biological validation of SCG calls.

## Key findings

- **Prevalence:** 47/4,561 (1.03%) of CRC cases harbored dual RAS hotspot/BRAF V600E driver mutations. Composition: 32 dual [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)/KRAS, 11 [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)/[NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md), 2 KRAS/BRAF V600E, 1 NRAS/BRAF V600E, 1 dual NRAS/NRAS.
- **[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E frequency lower in CRC-DD vs CRC-SD:** 3% v 13.3% (P=.0058). Concurrent BRAF V600E + canonical RAS hotspot was extremely rare — only 3/4,561 (0.06%) of CRC.
- **Same-cell co-occurrence by SCG (four cases):**
  - P-0021518: [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) G13D / [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) G12C in 95% of tumor cells.
  - C-XPMDEU: [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) G12D / [NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md) G12V in 48% of tumor cells.
  - P-0008729 (MSI-H): [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) V600E / KRAS G12D in 44% of tumor cells.
  - P-0061563: KRAS G12D / NRAS G13V in 14% of tumor cells (all NRAS G13V cells also harbored KRAS G12D).
- **SCG performance:** 96% mutation detection rate, 92% genotype prediction accuracy; false-negative rate 0.9%–3.7%, false-positive rate 2%–14%.
- **Clonality patterns (43 CRC-DD with FACETS):** 21% both clonal, 40% one clonal/one subclonal, 19% both subclonal, 21% indeterminate. CRC-DD had higher rates of subclonal RAS hotspot mutations vs CRC-SD (P=8.1×10⁻⁹).
- **Allelic imbalance (AI):** AI favoring the oncogenic allele occurred in 26/80 CRC-DD mutations vs 119/232 CRC-SD mutations (32.5% v 51.3%, P=.0026). AI was enriched in KRAS in CRC-DD (P=.038). **No BRAF V600E mutation in CRC-DD showed AI.** Both CRC-DD with clonal KRAS + clonal BRAF V600E were MSI-H without mutant AI.
- **MSI-H enrichment:** 27% of CRC-DD were MSI-H v 8.7% across CRC-SD (P=.0004). All BRAF V600E mutants in CRC-DD were MSI-H. No CRC-DD harbored [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) exonuclease domain hotspot mutations.
- **C→T / G→A transitions enriched in MAPK drivers of MSI-H CRC-DD** (P=.043, one-tailed) — consistent with MSI-H/MMR-deficiency signature preceding the MAPK mutation chronologically.
- **Lower genomic instability in CRC-DD:** mean fraction of genome altered ([FGA](../genes/FGA.md)) 0.325±0.224 v 0.511±0.244 in CRC-SD (P=7.99×10⁻⁹); difference persisted in MSS-only subset (P=2.67×10⁻⁷).
- **Multiregional sequencing (7 CRC-DD):** 5/7 retained both driver mutations across all sampled sites. The 2 discordant cases were both MSI-H — P-0001237 (BRAF V600E + KRAS G13D in primary; only BRAF V600E in abdominal wall and liver mets) and P-0039163 (KRAS G12D + K117N in primary; only KRAS G12D in liver met; primary was MSI-H, liver met was MSS).
- **Tumor evolution (P-0021518 infSCITE):** [APC](../genes/APC.md) S1505Afs, [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) I135V, and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) C275Y were the earliest events, followed by NRAS G13D, a second [APC](../genes/APC.md) mutation (I1008Yfs), and finally KRAS G12C.
- **Survival:** No significant difference in overall survival between CRC-DD and CRC-SD (P=.07) or wild-type RAS/RAF CRC (P=.80). Median [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md): CRC-DD 63.5 months (95% CI 56.1–NR) v CRC-SD 42.9 months (95% CI 38.9–53.1). Multivariate Cox: single-mutant status HR=1.74 (95% CI 1.34–2.30, P<.001) vs RAS/RAF wild-type reference.
- **Atypical KRAS pairing:** 4 cases (9%) showed atypical KRAS V14I in combination with a canonical KRAS mutation.
- **No significant difference between CRC-DD and CRC-SD in:** [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), APC, or [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) mutation prevalence (P>.05); median age at diagnosis (56 v 54 years, P=.23); proximal tumor location prevalence (53% v 47%, P=.43).

## Genes & alterations

- **[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)** — most frequent driver in CRC-DD: G12D (43%), G13D (36%), G12V (23%), G12C (13%), A146T (11%), other (36%). Subclonal KRAS mutations enriched in CRC-DD vs CRC-SD. AI most prevalent in KRAS (21/65 instances).
- **[NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md)** — present in 28% of CRC-DD (any mutation); hotspots include G12V, G13V, G13D, G12C, Q61K. AI in 5/13 NRAS instances.
- **[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)** — V600E in 6% of CRC-DD (3/4,561 = 0.06% concurrent with canonical RAS). All BRAF V600E + RAS hotspot dual cases were MSI-H. No BRAF V600E showed allelic imbalance in CRC-DD.
- **[APC](../genes/APC.md)** — high prevalence in CRC-DD (no significant difference v CRC-SD); SCG of P-0021518 placed APC S1505Afs as an early/truncal event followed later by APC I1008Yfs.
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)** — co-mutated, with TP53 C275Y identified as a truncal event in P-0021518.
- **[PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md)** — [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) I135V identified as an early event in P-0021518's evolutionary tree.
- **[PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)** — surveyed alongside APC and TP53; no significant difference in prevalence between CRC-DD and CRC-SD.
- **[POLE](../genes/POLE.md)** — explicitly negative: no CRC-DD harbored [POLE](../genes/POLE.md) exonuclease domain hotspot mutations (ruling out polymerase-driven hypermutator as the explanation for dual MAPK drivers).

## Clinical implications

- **Anti-EGFR therapy ([cetuximab](../drugs/cetuximab.md), [panitumumab](../drugs/panitumumab.md)):** standard CRC management restricts these to RAS wild-type tumors; the existence of 1% dual-driver CRC means that pre-treatment, subclonal RAS hotspot mutations may coexist with BRAF V600E and complicate biomarker interpretation.
- **BRAF inhibitor therapy ([encorafenib](../drugs/encorafenib.md) + [cetuximab](../drugs/cetuximab.md), with or without [binimetinib](../drugs/binimetinib.md)):** authors highlight that microsatellite-stable BRAF V600E–mutant CRC did *not* harbor a second MAPK driver before therapy, which "is important for BRAF inhibitor therapy selection." Dual MSI-H BRAF V600E + RAS cases are extremely rare; clinicians encountering one should consider that this 1.1% subset may not benefit from canonical targeted therapy as suggested by prior studies of [acquired resistance via mutant allele amplification](../genes/BRAF.md).
- **Resistance biology:** authors propose that a small reservoir of dual-driver cells may be selected under targeted therapy, paralleling EGFR/BRAF/HER2/TRK inhibitor resistance mechanisms that emerge within ~6 months. Awareness of pretreatment dual drivers is relevant for [liquid biopsy](../themes/ctdna-liquid-biopsy.md)-based monitoring strategies.
- **MSI-H + BRAF V600E + RAS triad:** discordant multiregional findings (2/2 discordant cases were MSI-H) suggest subclonal MAPK mutations may be lost in metastases of MSI-H tumors. Liquid biopsy at metastatic progression may capture clinically relevant heterogeneity that tissue biopsy misses.
- **Mechanistic interpretation:** authors argue dual drivers, rather than allelic imbalance, may be an alternative mechanism to boost MAPK output in MSI-H tumors that are intolerant of copy number alterations (CIN/MSI inverse relationship).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Rarity limits statistical power:** with only 47 CRC-DD cases, survival analyses were underpowered (CRC-DD v CRC-SD OS P=.07, not significant).
- **SCG was targeted to MAPK loci only,** limiting reconstruction of evolutionary trajectories beyond RAS/RAF/BRAF and a small set of co-occurring mutations.
- **SCG technical noise:** false-positive rate ranges 2%–14% depending on training set; discriminating mutant allele dropout from wild-type calls remains imperfect.
- **Only 4 of 47 CRC-DD underwent SCG** — same-cell co-occurrence in the remaining 43 is inferred indirectly from clonality patterns (FACETS) rather than directly demonstrated.
- **Treatment outcome data are limited** — the study cohort is targeted-therapy-naive by design; the question of whether dual-driver CRC responds differently to [encorafenib](../drugs/encorafenib.md) + [cetuximab](../drugs/cetuximab.md) or anti-EGFR therapy was not directly tested.
- **Mechanism for dual-driver emergence remains unknown** — the authors propose a dose-dependent MAPK signaling model but do not test it experimentally; the inverse correlation with allelic imbalance is suggestive but not mechanistic.
- **MSI-H subset with apparent loss of subclonal driver in metastasis** (e.g., P-0039163 MSS liver met) raises questions about how MMR status changes during metastatic dissemination — relevant for [microsatellite instability theme](../themes/microsatellite-instability.md) cross-paper synthesis.
