# cemiplimab

## Overview

Cemiplimab is a fully human anti-PD-1 monoclonal antibody ([PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md) target) approved for advanced cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma ([CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md)), basal cell carcinoma ([BCC](../cancer_types/BCC.md)), and non-small cell lung cancer ([NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md)). It was the first FDA-approved systemic therapy for advanced [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md) (Migden et al., 2018, NEJMoa1805131).

## Evidence in the corpus

- Extreme TMB (mean 238.7 mutations/Mb), recurrent [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) loss-of-function in metastatic CSCC WGS cohort (n=25) provide genomic rationale for high response rates observed with cemiplimab in advanced CSCC [PMID:35982973](../papers/35982973.md)

## Resistance mechanisms

- Resistance mechanisms in CSCC include [PTPRD](../genes/PTPRD.md) loss ([STAT3](../genes/STAT3.md) hyperactivation), loss of antigen presentation; largely unknown in the WGS cohort.

## Cancer types (linked)

- [CSCC](../cancer_types/CSCC.md) — extreme TMB and TP53/CDKN2A loss support anti-PD-1 response
