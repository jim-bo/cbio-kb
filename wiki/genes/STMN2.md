# STMN2

## Overview

STMN2 (Stathmin-2, also known as SCG10) encodes a microtubule-destabilizing protein involved in axonal growth and cellular differentiation. In cancer, STMN2 has been reported as a prognostic marker and plays roles in cell migration and proliferation through its modulation of microtubule dynamics. In TP53-mutant HCC, STMN2 shows a notable discordance between mRNA and protein levels, suggesting post-transcriptional regulation.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- STMN2 protein levels were elevated while mRNA expression was decreased in TP53-mutant HCCs in a Chinese proteogenomic HCC cohort (n=159); this discordance between mRNA and protein suggests post-transcriptional regulation specific to TP53-mutant tumor biology [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **Hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC):** STMN2 shows mRNA/protein discordance in TP53-mutant tumors; co-dysregulated with NCOR1 in this context [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- STMN2 protein upregulation co-occurs with NCOR1 protein upregulation specifically in TP53-mutant HCCs [PMID:35508466](../papers/35508466.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No direct therapeutic targeting reported in this corpus.

## Open questions

- Mechanism underlying post-transcriptional STMN2 upregulation in TP53-mutant HCC; whether STMN2 protein level is a prognostic biomarker in this context.
