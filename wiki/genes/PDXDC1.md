# PDXDC1

## Overview

PDXDC1 (Pyridoxal Dependent Decarboxylase Domain Containing 1) is a gene significantly mutated in chromophobe renal cell carcinoma (chRCC) identified in a comprehensive multisubtype non-clear-cell RCC genomic study. Its functional role in cancer is uncharacterized.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Significantly mutated in chRCC in a multisubtype nccRCC WES study; oncogenic vs passenger status unestablished [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- chRCC: significant mutation frequency in discovery cohort; co-nominated with PTEN, FAAH2, PDHB, and ZNF765 as chRCC-enriched SMGs [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-nominated with PTEN, FAAH2, PDHB, and ZNF765 as significantly mutated genes in chRCC [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- No targeted therapies established; functional characterization lacking [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)

## Open questions

- PDXDC1 lacks functional characterization in cancer — oncogenic vs passenger status is unestablished [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)
