# TWIST2

## Overview

TWIST2 encodes a basic helix-loop-helix (bHLH) transcription factor that drives epithelial-to-mesenchymal transition (EMT). TWIST2 promotes invasion and metastasis by repressing E-cadherin and activating mesenchymal gene expression programs. In the context of cancer immunotherapy, TWIST2 upregulation is part of a transcriptional program associated with innate resistance to anti-PD-1 therapy.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Mesenchymal-transition transcript up-regulated in pretreatment melanoma tumors that failed to respond to anti-PD-1 therapy; co-enriched within the IPRES (innate anti-PD-1 resistance) transcriptional signature in a 28-sample RNA-seq cohort [PMID:26997480](../papers/26997480.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md) / Melanoma:** Overexpressed in innately anti-PD-1-resistant pretreatment tumors as part of the IPRES signature [PMID:26997480](../papers/26997480.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-expressed with [AXL](../genes/AXL.md), [ROR2](../genes/ROR2.md), [WNT5A](../genes/WNT5A.md), [LOXL2](../genes/LOXL2.md), [TAGLN](../genes/TAGLN.md), and [FAP](../genes/FAP.md) as part of the IPRES mesenchymal-transition gene program in anti-PD-1 non-responding melanomas [PMID:26997480](../papers/26997480.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- TWIST2 and the IPRES signature are proposed as candidate transcriptomic biomarkers of innate anti-PD-1 resistance; the IPRES program is enriched across multiple cancer types, suggesting that EMT modulators or anti-angiogenics in combination with checkpoint blockade could overcome this resistance mechanism [PMID:26997480](../papers/26997480.md).

## Open questions

- The IPRES signature (including TWIST2) is correlative, not causal; whether pharmacologically suppressing TWIST2/IPRES restores anti-PD-1 sensitivity has not been demonstrated [PMID:26997480](../papers/26997480.md).
