Integrative Analysis of Drug Response and Clinical Outcome in Acute Myeloid Leukemia

Authors

Bottomly D

Long N

Schultz AR

Kurtz SE

Tognon CE

Johnson K

Druker BJ

McWeeney SK

Tyner JW

Doi

10.1016/j.ccell.2022.07.002

PMID: 35868306 · DOI: 10.1016/j.ccell.2022.07.002 · Journal: Cancer Cell (2022)

TL;DR

Bottomly et al. expand the Beat AML functional-genomic resource (aml_ohsu_2018 Waves 1+2) with two additional accrual waves (Waves 3+4), producing a harmonized cohort of 942 specimens from 805 AML patients with paired ex vivo drug sensitivity, DNA sequencing, RNA-Seq, and curated clinical annotations (PMID:35868306). By deconvolving bulk RNA-Seq into six single-cell-defined maturation-state signatures and intersecting them with mutational status and ex vivo response across a broad drug panel, they show that AML cell differentiation state broadly governs drug sensitivity and frequently modifies known mutation–drug associations. Conditional random-forest modeling of overall survival nominates a single gene — PEAR1 (a.k.a. JEDI/MEGF12) — as among the strongest predictors of outcome, performing equivalently to the LSC17 leukemic stem-cell signature, with independent validation in the Malani 2022 cohort and TCGA LAML (laml_tcga_pub).

Cohort & data

  • Cumulative harmonized cohort: 942 specimens from 805 patients (real-world AML, inclusive of de novo, transformed, and therapy-related cases; 70% collected at initial diagnosis). The dataset is published as cBioPortal study aml_ohsu_2022 and accessible at Vizome (www.vizome.org/aml2) (PMID:35868306).
  • Waves 3+4 (new in this paper): 293 specimens from 279 patients (243 unique to Waves 3+4), collected over 2.5 years; combined with the Tyner et al. 2018 Waves 1+2 (aml_ohsu_2018; PMID:30333627).
  • Cohort-level analyses used only the first-timepoint specimen per patient with remission samples excluded (PMID:35868306).
  • Assays:
    • Ex vivo drug sensitivity screen with probit dose-response curve fits.
    • RNA-Seq with conditional quantile normalization (CQN) parameters carried over from Waves 1+2.
    • DNA sequencing via an 11.9 Mb custom capture library covering all variants previously reported in AML (extending the whole-exome-seq used in Waves 1+2).
    • FLT3-ITD and NPM1 4-bp insertion calls via internal PCR amplicon size-based methodology.
  • Clinical: ELN 2017 risk categories assigned at initial-diagnosis specimens only, prioritizing internal deep-sequencing calls for ASXL1, RUNX1, TP53 over EMR-curated clinical results. NLP pipeline (Linguamatics I2E + Python) extracted structured clinical elements from EHR notes with accuracy 79–93%, precision 85–96%.
  • Data availability: dbGaP study ID 30641 (accession phs001657.v2.p1); Genomic Data Commons; code at https://github.com/biodev/beataml2_manuscript.

Key findings

  • Strong cross-cohort concordance between Waves 1+2 and Waves 3+4: 89% concordance for mutation–drug-sensitivity associations and 98% concordance for the 13 WGCNA module eigengene–drug response associations across the two cohorts (PMID:35868306).
  • Added statistical power: Harmonized analysis yielded 65 additional mutation–drug associations that did not reach significance in Waves 1+2 alone (a 27% increase).
  • AML cell-maturation state deconvolution: Using the top 30 genes from each of six Van Galen et al. single-cell AML signatures (HSC-like, Progenitor-like, GMP-like, Promonocyte-like, Monocyte-like, cDC-like), the authors summarized each as a PC1 score. HSC-like scores were higher in FAB M0–M2; Monocyte-like scores were higher in M4–M5.
  • Cell-state–mutation correlations: TP53, BCOR, and SF3B1 enriched for HSC-like; NPM1 for Promonocyte-like; RAS (KRAS/NRAS) for Monocyte-like; RUNX1-mutant tumors showed dual correlation with both HSC-like and cDC-like states.
  • Cell-state–drug correlations: 73 drugs showed response significantly correlated with at least one of the six cell-types; hierarchical clustering produced three drug groups (HSC/Progenitor-biased, cDC/Monocyte-biased, and a third class with mixed sensitivity).
  • Venetoclax and the HDAC inhibitor panobinostat were extreme outliers exhibiting inverse cell-state response patterns relative to one another.
  • Conditional cell-state interactions: Sorafenib (a FLT3 inhibitor) shows expected greater efficacy in FLT3-ITD samples, but a high Monocyte-like signal confers resistance in FLT3-ITD-positive cases, while a high Progenitor-like score predicts the strongest sorafenib sensitivity in FLT3-ITD AML.
  • Drug-family analysis: 25 drug families (using Cancer Targetome + KINOMEscan with IUPHAR family classifications) revealed that mutations in treatment-refractory subtypes (TP53, STAG2, RUNX1) confer greater sensitivity to PIK-family inhibitors, and N/KRAS mutation confers greater sensitivity to CDK inhibitors. PIK-family inhibitors were active against NPM1-mutated cases with high cDC-like scores but resistant in NPM1-mutated cases with high HSC-like scores.
  • Survival modeling (n=435 RNA-Seq + initial-diagnosis samples): WGCNA modules 3, 9, and 12 plus the HSC-like cell-type score clustered with shorter overall survival; modules 3 and 9 had the strongest survival associations.
  • PEAR1 as the dominant module-3 hub: Within module 3 (n=203 genes), PEAR1 had a far higher kME (hub correlation with module eigengene) than any other module member. No analogous singular hub was identified for module 9.
  • Cforest variable importance (de novo AML, n=298): age, mutated TP53, and WGCNA module 3 were the strongest predictors of overall survival; in young (<45 y) patients and in TP53-wild-type middle-age (45–60 y) patients, module 3 expression was the strongest single determinant of outcome.
  • PEAR1 vs ELN risk (single-gene expression): Adverse vs Favorable ELN PEAR1 mean difference = 3.765 (p = 3.276e-25, N=172) in ≥60 y patients; 1.497 (p = 0.006, N=72) in middle-age; not significant in young (p = 0.170, N=79) despite PEAR1 being a strong outcome predictor in this group — i.e., PEAR1 carries prognostic signal independent of ELN in young AML.
  • PEAR1 vs LSC17: Single-gene PEAR1 expression discriminated outcome in young AML patients comparably to the 17-gene LSC17 leukemic stem-cell signature; hazard ratios were similar across age strata and across bone-marrow vs peripheral-blood specimen types.
  • Independent validation: elevated PEAR1 expression conferred significantly shortened OS in both the Malani et al. 2022 cohort and TCGA LAML (laml_tcga_pub; [Cancer Genome Atlas Research et al., 2013]).
  • Surface expression confirmed: PEAR1 surface protein on AML patient samples (anti-PEAR1 brilliant violet 650 flow cytometry) correlates with PEAR1 transcript level, supporting feasibility of antibody-based targeting.

Genes & alterations

  • PEAR1 (a.k.a. JEDI, MEGF12) — single-gene expression hub of WGCNA module 3; positively correlated with HSC-like cell state; predicts shortened OS in AML across age groups; independent of ELN risk in young (<45 y) patients; equivalent to LSC17 in hazard-ratio analyses; surface-expressed on AML blasts (PMID:35868306).
  • TP53 — confirmed strong adverse-risk variable in cforest survival modeling; enriched in HSC-like cell state; mutations confer increased sensitivity to PIK-family inhibitors; PEAR1 elevation observed in TP53-mutant cases.
  • FLT3 — FLT3-ITD detected via PCR amplicon sizing; conferred sensitivity to sorafenib, but sensitivity is conditional on a high Progenitor-like score (resistant when Monocyte-like dominates).
  • NPM1 — 4-bp insertion detected via PCR; enriched for Promonocyte-like state; lower PEAR1 expression in NPM1-mutant cases; PIK-inhibitor response is conditional on cell-state context.
  • RUNX1 — dual correlation with HSC-like and cDC-like states (“co-opting” disparate maturation states); confers PIK-family-inhibitor sensitivity; elevated PEAR1.
  • ASXL1 — elevated PEAR1 expression in ASXL1-mutant AML, paralleling ASXL1’s role in clonal hematopoiesis and possibly linking PEAR1 biology to cardiovascular comorbidity in aging hematopoiesis.
  • BCOR, SF3B1 — enriched in HSC-like state.
  • KRAS, NRAS (“RAS”) — correlated with Monocyte-like state; conferred greater sensitivity to STE7/MEK inhibitors (expected) and to CDK-family inhibitors (unexpected).
  • STAG2 — confers greater sensitivity to PIK-family inhibitors.
  • SRSF2, GATA2, MECOM (incl. GATA2-MECOM fusion / inv(3)/t(3;3)) — elevated PEAR1 expression in these poor-prognosis genetic subsets.
  • CEBPA — lower PEAR1 expression in cases with this favorable-prognosis marker; bi-allelic CEBPA manually adjudicated for ELN risk calling.
  • BCL2 — pathway target of venetoclax, one of two extreme outlier drugs in cell-state response analysis (the other being panobinostat).
  • IDH1 / IDH2 — discussed as targets of ivosidenib and enasidenib in the AML therapeutic landscape (background, not a primary finding here).
  • CD33 — antigen of gemtuzumab ozogamicin (background context).
  • PYCARD, MAPK8 — PYCARD was co-nominated with PEAR1 in a prior TCGA-LAML immune prognostic signature (Dao et al., 2021); MAPK8 (JNK) is part of the PEAR1-downstream phagocytic signaling axis with SYK and SRC-family kinases (mechanistic background).

Clinical implications

  • Prognostic biomarker: PEAR1 expression is proposed as a single-gene prognostic biomarker for OS in AML, performing equivalently to the LSC17 signature and carrying prognostic value independent of ELN risk in young (<45 y) patients (PMID:35868306).
  • Therapeutic target: PEAR1 is a type-I transmembrane signaling receptor with confirmed surface expression on AML blasts, raising the possibility of small-molecule and antibody-based targeting. PEAR1-null mice retain platelet function, suggesting a potential therapeutic window.
  • Cell-state as a treatment-stratification axis: Because most drugs and 18/25 drug families show cell-state-biased response, AML cell-maturation state should be assessed as a confounder/modifier of drug response in clinical evaluation — the authors argue cell-type adjustment should be “as routine as population-substructure adjustment.”
  • Refined precision-medicine guidance: Cell-state context refines existing mutation–drug rules — e.g., FLT3-ITD predicts sorafenib sensitivity only when the tumor is Progenitor-like-biased; Monocyte-like FLT3-ITD AML resists sorafenib.
  • Unexpected pharmacogenomic associations: TP53/STAG2/RUNX1-mutant AML — typically chemotherapy-refractory — shows greater ex vivo sensitivity to PIK-family inhibitors; N/KRAS-mutant AML shows greater sensitivity to CDK-family inhibitors.

Limitations & open questions

  • The prognostic significance of PEAR1 was established in cohorts treated predominantly with anthracycline-based regimens; with the modern shift to venetoclax + hypomethylating-agent combinations and targeted therapies, PEAR1’s prognostic value under non-anthracycline therapy remains to be demonstrated (PMID:35868306).
  • Apparent ethnic differences in PEAR1 expression (Non-Hispanic White mean 1.395, N=359; African Heritage mean 2.205, N=13) warrant validation in larger non-Caucasian cohorts, especially given known racially-skewed PEAR1 polymorphism frequencies and reported outcome disparities for Black AML patients.
  • Forced PEAR1 expression in bone-marrow or fibroblast-stromal cells was shown (Krivtsov et al., 2007) to reduce clonogenic myeloid colony formation — apparently counterintuitive to the association of elevated PEAR1 with worse AML outcome; the underlying biology of why elevated PEAR1 is adverse in AML is unresolved.
  • The cforest survival model was restricted to de novo AML (n=298) because of confounding between AML type and other variables; generalization to transformed/therapy-related AML is shown for individual analyses but not for the full model.
  • Whether and how PEAR1 signaling can be therapeutically interrupted without impairing platelet aggregation in patients (despite the mouse-knockout reassurance) remains to be tested clinically.
  • The Waves 3+4 expansion enabled discovery of 65 additional mutation–drug associations, suggesting many additional pharmacogenomic signals in AML remain underpowered even at n=805 patients.
