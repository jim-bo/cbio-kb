# BIRC5

## Overview

BIRC5 (baculoviral IAP repeat containing 5), also known as survivin, encodes a member of the inhibitor of apoptosis (IAP) protein family. It is overexpressed in many cancers and acts to suppress caspase-dependent apoptosis. In lung squamous cell carcinoma (LSCC), BIRC5 overexpression is specifically associated with tumors lacking [TP63](../genes/TP63.md) (Np63-low subtype), defining a candidate patient selection biomarker for survivin-inhibitor therapy.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Overexpressed specifically in Np63-low LSCC tumors (10 tumors lacking TP63 elevation) in CPTAC LSCC proteogenomics (108 treatment-naive tumors); TP63-low cell lines showed sensitivity to the survivin inhibitor YM-155 in vitro. [PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **LSCC ([LSCC](../cancer_types/LSCC.md)):** BIRC5 overexpression defines a Np63-low LSCC subgroup (10/108 tumors); these tumors lack the characteristic TP63 3q amplification seen in the majority of LSCC. Survivin inhibitor YM-155 proposed as a targeted therapy for this subgroup. [PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Mutually exclusive with high TP63 expression in LSCC: BIRC5 overexpression occurs in the Np63-low subset that lacks TP63 protein elevation, the dominant feature of most LSCC tumors. [PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Survivin inhibitor YM-155 showed selective activity in TP63-low (Np63-low) LSCC cell lines in vitro; TP63 status (specifically Np63-low) proposed as a candidate biomarker for survivin-inhibitor response. Prior survivin trials in unselected [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) showed modest efficacy, consistent with the hypothesis that subgroup selection is required. [PMID:34358469](../papers/34358469.md)

## Open questions

- YM-155 efficacy in Np63-low LSCC has not been validated in clinical trials; the biomarker hypothesis requires prospective confirmation.
- Mechanism linking TP63 loss to BIRC5 overexpression in LSCC is not fully characterized.
