PMID: 31978347 · DOI: 10.1016/j.cell.2019.12.023 · Journal: Cell (2020)

TL;DR

Nusinow and colleagues add a deep, quantitative proteomics layer to the Cancer Cell Line Encyclopedia by profiling 375 cell lines across 22 lineages with TMT 10-plex tandem-mass-tag LC-MS/MS, quantifying >12,000 proteins at 1% protein-level FDR. They find that the primary axis of proteome variation is organized by coordinated expression across biological pathways and protein complexes, not by tissue lineage or mutation burden, and that this pattern is largely absent from matched RNA-seq. An MSI-focused analysis uncovers only ~50 differentially expressed proteins (vs. >1,000 mRNAs), enriched for surveillance complexes (mismatch repair, MRN, SKI/SKIC2-SKIC3, RQC/LTN1, H3K4 methyltransferases) and identifies RPL22 mutation as uniquely associated with these complex-level changes and SKI complex downregulation. Complex-level proteome features further nominate associations between protein-complex abundance and sensitivity to gene knockdown (e.g., MCM complex with MDM2, integrins with TEAD1, SUMO E1 with WRN and PCSK7 mutation). PMID:31978347

Cohort & data

  • 375 CCLE cell lines selected across 22 tissue lineages, dominated by solid-organ lines; cell-line provenance and annotations follow the CCLE release (cellline_ccle_broad; ccle_broad_2019). NCI60 lines were largely excluded in favor of less-characterized lines, and lines overlapping with DRIVE and Achilles screens were emphasized. PMID:31978347
  • Lines authenticated by SNP-based DNA fingerprinting prior to mass spectrometry. PMID:31978347
  • Assay: TMT 10-plex tandem-mass-tag proteomics with MS3/SPS quantification on Thermo Orbitrap Fusion / Fusion Lumos instruments; 42 ten-plexes × 12 fractions = 504 LC-MS runs (~4 hr per cell line, LC-MS/MS proteomics). A bridge channel built from 11 pooled lines (NCIH446, DMS79, NCIH460, DMS53, NCIH69, HCC1954, CAMA1, KYSE180, NMCG1, UACC257, AU565) was always labeled with the 131 reagent for cross-batch normalization. PMID:31978347
  • Database search: Sequest against UniProt human (SwissProt + TrEMBL, Feb 2014) with target-decoy LDA filtering to 1% peptide FDR and Picked-FDR-based 1% protein-level FDR across the 504-run dataset. >12,000 proteins quantified across all samples; >9,000 in a majority. PMID:31978347
  • Companion data integrated from the broader CCLE: RNA-seq, histone modification profiling, mutation calls, MSI status (as inferred by Ghandi et al., 2019), CRISPR sensitivity from Project Achilles/Avana (depmap.org 18Q3), shRNA sensitivity from Project DRIVE, and CTD2 drug sensitivity. PMID:31978347
  • Analyses run primarily in R, with GSEA (fgsea), PCA, ICA, hierarchical clustering (Ward / Euclidean), elastic-net feature selection, and FDR estimation via fdrtool. PMID:31978347

Key findings

  • Replicate quality. Mean replicate-replicate correlation 0.8 vs. mean cell-line/cell-line correlation −0.05 (p < 2e-16), median CV of 60% between biological replicates within a line. PMID:31978347
  • Tissue lineage explains only part of proteome structure. Hierarchical clustering by protein has mean Gini purity 0.46 (1.0 = perfect tissue-based clustering); RNA clustering 0.6. Skin and haematopoetic/lymphoid lineages cluster most tightly. Haematopoetic/lymphoid lines were removed before downstream PCA. PMID:31978347
  • Pathway co-expression — not tissue or mutation — dominates PC1. 75% of proteins are significantly correlated with PC1 at 1% FDR. >200 MSigDB pathways are enriched along PC1 of the protein data versus far fewer along PC1 of the matched RNA-seq. The two anticorrelated PC1 poles align with (i) growth-factor signaling (MAPK, insulin receptor), glycolysis, nucleotide metabolism, cell division and (ii) cell-cell/cell-matrix adhesion, KRAS and p53 signaling, and oxidative phosphorylation — the latter recapitulating the Warburg switch. Total mutational burden does not explain PC1. PMID:31978347
  • RNA-protein correlation per gene. Per-gene Pearson correlation between mRNA and protein averages just under 0.5, with extremes such as EGFR (good RNA proxy) and BRAF (poor RNA proxy). PMID:31978347
  • EMT-like axis is proteome-wide. ~50% of proteins are positively or negatively correlated with EPCAM (top PC1 loading), ~33% with VIM; roughly half of these correlations are not mirrored in mRNA. PMID:31978347
  • Expression correlation network. Solid-organ network: 3,777 proteins, 41,600 edges at 1% FDR (~40,000 positive). ~5,500 edges shared with an RNA-seq correlation network (~195,000 edges, ~9,400 genes); >6,300 edges annotated by protein-protein interaction / shared complex membership (BioPlex 2.0, BioGRID, CORUM); ~3,500 by shared subcellular localization; >7,900 by shared pathway membership — the largest annotated category. PMID:31978347
  • MSI proteome buffering. mRNA shows >1,000 up/downregulated genes in MSI cells; protein shows only 50 differentially expressed proteins (30 shared with mRNA), reflecting strong posttranscriptional buffering. 24 of those 50 proteins are members of surveillance complexes for DNA, RNA, and nascent-protein quality control. PMID:31978347
  • MSI-associated complex downregulation. Mismatch repair (MutL: MLH1, PMS2; MutS: MSH2, MSH6), MRN, the SKI complex helicase SKIC2 (SKIV2L) and partner SKIC3 (TTC37), the RQC E3 ligase LTN1, ribosomal accessory proteins RPL22 / RPL22L1, heat-shock proteins HSPA4L / DNAJB14 / HSPH1 (HSP110), and H3K4 methyltransferases KMT2B / KMT2D / SETD1B. The RQC co-factor TCF25 was upregulated. PMID:31978347
  • MutL- vs. MutS-correlated arms. In MSI lines, MutL components (MLH1, PMS2) and HMT complex members are best fit by an MSI-status-only linear model (independent of total mutation burden); the MutS-correlated cluster — including SKIC2, SKIC3, LTN1, RPL22, RPL22L1 — improves under a model that combines MSI status with mutation burden. PMID:31978347
  • H3K4 methylation drop in MSI. Bulk H3K4me1 and H3K4me2 are significantly reduced in MSI cell lines and the effect is MSI-specific (no effect from total mutation burden alone), consistent with downregulation of the H3K4 methyltransferases KMT2B, KMT2D, SETD1B. PMID:31978347
  • RPL22 mutation uniquely tracks the MSI complex signature. Among per-gene mutation regressions across solid-organ lines, RPL22 mutation is uniquely associated with altered protein levels in MSI-associated complexes (mismatch repair, SKI complex, HMTs). RPL22 expression in MSI cells clusters with the MutS arm. PMID:31978347
  • WRN synthetic lethality predictors. WRN-knockdown sensitivity in DRIVE is associated with protein abundance of mismatch repair components and the SKI proteins SKIC2 / SKIC3, strengthening the link between MSI and SKI downregulation. SKIC2 was the only protein commonly predictive of both WRN and RPL22L1 knockdown sensitivity. PMID:31978347
  • Complex-level associations with knockdown sensitivity. Filtering DRIVE knockdowns with strong, variable effects, complexes co-vary with sensitivities to multiple genes: ATR with the ATR/ATRIP complex (ATRIP) and the RPA complex (RPA1); TEAD1 with integrin complexes containing ITGB1 (consistent with the YAP1/Hippo–integrin axis); TP53, MDM2, and CDKN1A with the Chromosomal Passenger Complex and CDK-containing complexes; MDM2 uniquely with MCM-complex abundance; WRN with the SUMO E1 heterodimer (SAE1 / UBA2). PMID:31978347
  • Mutation-to-complex map. PCSK7 mutation is uniquely associated with SUMO E1 heterodimer protein levels; RPL22 mutation again recovers the mismatch repair and SKI complexes (plus KDR). PMID:31978347

Genes & alterations

  • RPL22 — mutation hotspot of MSI endometrial cancers (Novetsky et al., 2013); in CCLE, RPL22 mutation is uniquely associated with proteome-level changes in MSI-associated complexes and its expression clusters with the MutS arm (combined MSI + mutation-burden model). PMID:31978347
  • RPL22L1 — derepressed upon RPL22 loss; downregulated in MSI; sensitivity to RPL22L1 knockdown in MSI cells is best predicted by SKIC2 protein abundance, suggesting an association with the SKI complex / ribosome rather than with MSI causation per se. PMID:31978347
  • SKIC2 (SKIV2L) and SKIC3 (TTC37) — SKI cytoplasmic ribosome-associated RNA-surveillance complex; both proteins are downregulated in MSI cell lines and are associated with WRN-knockdown sensitivity. PMID:31978347
  • LTN1 — RQC E3 ubiquitin ligase that targets nascent dysfunctional proteins; downregulated in MSI lines; its co-complex partner TCF25 is upregulated. PMID:31978347
  • MLH1, PMS2 (MutL) and MSH2, MSH6 (MutS) — mismatch repair components downregulated in MSI; their protein abundance predicts WRN knockdown sensitivity. PMID:31978347
  • HSPA4L, DNAJB14, HSPH1 — heat-shock proteins downregulated in MSI lines; HSPH1 (HSP110) has an established role in MSI disease. PMID:31978347
  • KMT2B, KMT2D, SETD1B — H3K4 methyltransferases (SET-domain) downregulated in MSI cells; H3K4me1 and H3K4me2 marks correspondingly reduced. PMID:31978347
  • KRAS — mutation associated with broad changes in cell-motility and adhesion-protein levels across solid-organ lines. PMID:31978347
  • TP53 — mutation associated with a small set of p53-pathway and DNA-damage-response proteins; TP53/MDM2/CDKN1A knockdown sensitivities co-vary with cell-division-related complexes. PMID:31978347
  • MDM2 — knockdown sensitivity uniquely associated with MCM-complex protein levels, hinting at a p53-independent function. PMID:31978347
  • MDM4 — splicing tied to RPL22L1 levels (cross-reference to Ghandi et al., 2019). PMID:31978347
  • WRN — synthetic lethal vulnerability in MSI; sensitivity predicted by mismatch-repair and SKI-complex protein levels and by SUMO E1 (SAE1 / UBA2) abundance. PMID:31978347
  • PCSK7 — serine endopeptidase whose mutation is uniquely associated with SUMO E1 heterodimer protein levels; mechanism unknown. PMID:31978347
  • TEAD1 — knockdown sensitivity associated with integrin complexes containing ITGB1; consistent with the YAP1/Hippo–mechanosensing pathway. PMID:31978347
  • ATR — knockdown sensitivity tracks the ATR/ATRIP and RPA1-containing RPA complexes. PMID:31978347
  • EPCAM, VIM — canonical epithelial / mesenchymal markers anchoring a proteome-wide EMT-like axis on PC1. PMID:31978347
  • ERBB2 (HER2) — upregulated in breast-derived lines; protein abundance largely predicted by ERBB2 copy number. PMID:31978347
  • EGFR, BRAF — contrasting RNA-protein correlations (high for EGFR, low for BRAF). EGFR-targeting drugs constituted the largest subset of compounds whose sensitivity correlated with cell-line PC1 projection. PMID:31978347
  • PIK3CB, ZEB2 — among CRISPR knockdowns whose sensitivities correlate with PC1; PIK3CB-targeting drugs also tracked PC1. PMID:31978347

Clinical implications

  • The MSI proteome signature (mismatch repair + SKI/RQC + H3K4 HMTs) and its overlap with WRN synthetic lethality offer protein-level biomarkers that may complement mRNA- and DNA-based MSI calls for selecting patients amenable to WRN-directed therapy or other MSI-targeted strategies. PMID:31978347
  • Tight buffering of transcriptomic changes by the proteome implies that disrupting posttranscriptional buffering of mutation-driven transcriptomic perturbations may itself be a therapeutic vulnerability — explicitly proposed by the authors as a future direction. PMID:31978347
  • Protein-complex abundance is a better cross-line predictor of gene-knockdown sensitivity than any single transcript or mutation in several cases (e.g., MCM complex with MDM2, integrins with TEAD1), supporting the use of proteomic biomarkers in target prioritization beyond mutation calls. PMID:31978347
  • The data set itself is positioned as a community resource hosted at gygi.med.harvard.edu/publications/ccle and depmap.org for direct use in target discovery and biomarker work. PMID:31978347

Limitations & open questions

  • Cell lines, not tumors. Findings derive entirely from cultured CCLE cell lines; how proteome-level pathway coordination and MSI buffering generalize to primary tumors is not directly tested here. PMID:31978347
  • Missing values from multiplexing. Bridge-normalization across 42 TMT 10-plexes leaves missing values; authors deliberately avoided imputation, which limits some downstream analyses. PMID:31978347
  • Modest MSI sample size. Only the dominant form of hypermutation (MSI) is well-powered in CCLE; other hypermutation modes (UV, smoking, APOBEC) are noted but not deeply dissected. PMID:31978347
  • Mechanism gaps. The mechanistic link between RPL22 mutation, SKI complex downregulation, and MSI is not resolved; the PCSK7–SUMO E1 association lacks known biology. PMID:31978347
  • Annotation approach. The authors note their correlation-network annotation uses simple methods and that Bayes nets / partial correlations could yield stronger mechanistic inference. PMID:31978347
  • Replicates concentrated in early batches. Only the first two TMT 10-plexes (18 lines) were run in biological triplicate, so per-line technical reproducibility is best characterized for a subset of the cohort. PMID:31978347
