**PMID:** 27158780 · **DOI:** 10.1038/ng.3564 · **Journal:** Nature Genetics (2016)

## TL;DR

Campbell et al. compared exome sequences and SNP-array copy-number profiles from 660 lung adenocarcinoma (ADC) and 484 lung squamous cell carcinoma (SqCC) tumor/normal pairs (1,144 NSCLCs total) to identify novel drivers and to contrast molecular landscapes between the two histologies. Recurrently altered genes in lung SqCC more closely resembled those of head & neck squamous and bladder carcinomas than they did lung ADC. The authors nominated novel significantly mutated genes ([PPP3CA](../genes/PPP3CA.md), [DOT1L](../genes/DOT1L.md), CMTR2/FTSJD1 in ADC; [RASA1](../genes/RASA1.md) in SqCC; [KLF5](../genes/KLF5.md), [EP300](../genes/EP300.md), [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md) in both); novel amplification peaks ([MIR21](../genes/MIR21.md) in ADC, [MIR205](../genes/MIR205.md) and [YES1](../genes/YES1.md) in SqCC, [MAPK1](../genes/MAPK1.md) in both); and additional Ras/Raf/RTK pathway candidates ([SOS1](../genes/SOS1.md), [VAV1](../genes/VAV1.md), RASA1, [ARHGAP35](../genes/ARHGAP35.md)) in oncogene-negative lung ADCs. 47% of lung ADC and 53% of lung SqCC tumors had ≥5 predicted neoepitopes, supporting broad immunotherapy applicability across both subtypes ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).

## Cohort & data

- **Lung ADC:** 660 tumor/normal exome pairs — 274 previously unpublished, 227 from prior TCGA ([luad_tcga_pub](../datasets/luad_tcga_pub.md)), 159 from the Imielinski et al cohort ([luad_broad](../datasets/luad_broad.md)) ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **Lung SqCC:** 484 tumor/normal exome pairs — 308 previously unpublished, 176 previously described in TCGA ([lusc_tcga_pub](../datasets/lusc_tcga_pub.md)) ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **Cancer types:** [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) and [LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md) (combined cohort referred to as "Pan-Lung" / [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md)).
- **Cohort/study:** [nsclc_tcga_broad_2016](../datasets/nsclc_tcga_broad_2016.md).
- **Assays:** Exome capture with Agilent SureSelect Human All Exon 50MB followed by Illumina paired-end sequencing ([whole-exome-seq](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md)); SNP-array copy number on Affymetrix SNP 6.0 ([affymetrix-snp6](../methods/affymetrix-snp6.md)); RNA-seq for 495 ADCs and 476 SqCCs ([rna-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md)); mutation calling with [mutect](../methods/mutect.md) and [indelocator](../methods/indelocator.md); driver discovery with MutSig2CV ([mutsig](../methods/mutsig.md)); recurrent SCNAs with [gistic](../methods/gistic.md) (GISTIC2.0); purity/ploidy with [absolute](../methods/absolute.md); mutational signatures via non-negative matrix factorization ([bayesian-nmf](../methods/bayesian-nmf.md), [mutational-signatures](../methods/mutational-signatures.md)); fusion calls via [prada](../methods/prada.md) ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).

## Key findings

- **Median somatic mutation rate** was 8.7/Mb in lung ADC and 9.7/Mb in lung SqCC; MutSig2CV identified **38 significantly mutated genes in ADC** and **20 in SqCC** (q < 0.1) ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- Only six genes — [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [RB1](../genes/RB1.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) — were significantly mutated in both histologies; [TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), and [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) were significantly more frequently mutated in SqCC than ADC (p < 0.01, Fisher's exact) ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- Lung SqCC most closely resembled [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md) and [BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md) (>25% overlap of significantly mutated genes), while lung ADC most resembled [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md) and CRC; the two [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) subtypes overlapped only ~12% with each other (p = 0.105) ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **Six mutational signatures** identified by NMF, mapping to COSMIC SI4 (smoking, C>A transversions), SI7 (UV), SI13/SI2 (APOBEC), SI15/SI6 (MMR), and SI5 (clock-like). Smoking-signature SI4 separated never- vs ever-smokers far better in ADC (AUC = 0.87) than in SqCC (AUC = 0.62) — implying inaccurate smoking annotation in some SqCC cases ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- 87% of lung ADCs from never-smokers were transversion-low (TV-L; ≤0.696 SI4 mutations/Mb; p = 8.5 × 10⁻³⁷), but only 45% of TV-L lung ADCs came from never-smokers ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- Three lung SqCCs (~1%) showed UV-pattern mutations (SI7) and one (TCGA-18-3409) had a documented prior basal-cell carcinoma — likely cutaneous metastases misclassified as lung primaries ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- Seven tumors (4 ADC, 3 SqCC) had MMR-like signature (SI15/SI6) with elevated SSNVs and indels (p < 0.001) and reduced [MLH1](../genes/MLH1.md) expression (p = 0.011) ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **Pan-Lung joint analysis** revealed 14 additional significantly mutated genes including [KLF5](../genes/KLF5.md) (zinc-finger hotspot), the [EP300](../genes/EP300.md)/[CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md) HAT-domain hotspot region, [FBXW7](../genes/FBXW7.md), and [B2M](../genes/B2M.md) (FDR q = 0.006) ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **Novel focal amplifications:** [CCND3](../genes/CCND3.md) and [MIR21](../genes/MIR21.md)/[TUBD1](../genes/TUBD1.md) in ADC; [YES1](../genes/YES1.md) and [MIR205](../genes/MIR205.md) in SqCC; [MAPK1](../genes/MAPK1.md) in the Pan-Lung analysis ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **Novel focal deletions:** [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) and [ARID2](../genes/ARID2.md) in ADC; [ZMYND11](../genes/ZMYND11.md), [CREBBP](../genes/CREBBP.md), [ROBO1](../genes/ROBO1.md), [USP22](../genes/USP22.md), [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md) in SqCC; [B2M](../genes/B2M.md) and [TRAF3](../genes/TRAF3.md) recurrently lost in both ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **Sex-specific enrichment:** [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) mutations in females and [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) mutations in males in ADC; [RB1](../genes/RB1.md) mutations in females and [PASK](../genes/PASK.md) mutations exclusively in males in SqCC (FDR q < 0.1) ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **Oncogene-negative lung ADC:** 242/660 ADCs lacked a known activating RTK/Ras/Raf alteration. Among them, [SOS1](../genes/SOS1.md) (recurrent autoinhibitory-domain p.N233Y, n=4), [VAV1](../genes/VAV1.md) (p.S67Y near CH/Ac/PH interface), [RASA1](../genes/RASA1.md), and [ARHGAP35](../genes/ARHGAP35.md) were significantly enriched (q < 0.1), as were amplifications around [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md)/[WHSC1L1](../genes/WHSC1L1.md), [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md)/[KIT](../genes/KIT.md)/[KDR](../genes/KDR.md), and [MAPK1](../genes/MAPK1.md) (q < 0.25) ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- After incorporating these new candidates, **499/660 (76%) lung ADCs** harbored an alteration in a known or putative RTK/Ras/Raf driver; in the 227-tumor expert-pathology-reviewed subset with RNA-seq, the rate rose to **193/227 (85%)** ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **Co-occurrence:** [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) mutations significantly co-occurred with activating [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) (p = 1.1 × 10⁻⁶), and 28 oncogene-negative ADCs additionally carried [STK11](../genes/STK11.md) mutations — suggesting an unrecognized KRAS-related event complementary to STK11 loss ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- High [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) amplification overlapped with activating [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) mutations (p = 1.9 × 10⁻⁸); [MET](../genes/MET.md) amplifications co-occurred with [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) mutations (p = 0.019) ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **Neoantigens:** Recurrent predicted neoepitopes included [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) p.E542K, [NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md) p.E79Q, [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) p.G466V, [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) p.G719A, [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) p.V157F/p.G154V/p.R175G/p.P278A, and a previously unappreciated recurrent [MB21D2](../genes/MB21D2.md) (C3orf59) p.Q311E. **47% of lung ADC and 53% of lung SqCC** tumors had ≥5 predicted neoepitopes ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- Neoepitope and nonsynonymous mutation counts were significantly **lower in never-smoker ADCs vs ever-smoker ADCs** (p < 0.001, Wilcoxon rank-sum), but did not differ between ever-smoker ADCs and SqCCs ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).

## Genes & alterations

- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md), [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)** — significantly more frequently mutated in lung SqCC than ADC (p < 0.01) ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **[STK11](../genes/STK11.md), [RBM10](../genes/RBM10.md), [KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md), [RAF1](../genes/RAF1.md), [RIT1](../genes/RIT1.md), [MET](../genes/MET.md)** — significantly mutated exclusively in lung ADC vs other TCGA tumor types (q < 0.1) ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **[NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md), [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md), [RASA1](../genes/RASA1.md), [NOTCH1](../genes/NOTCH1.md), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md)** — significantly mutated in lung SqCC but not other cancer types (excluding HNSC, BLCA) ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **[AKT1](../genes/AKT1.md)** — recurrent p.E17K mutation in lung ADC; **[CDK4](../genes/CDK4.md)** — recurrent p.R24L; **[DNMT3A](../genes/DNMT3A.md)** — modest q-value in ADC ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **[PPP3CA](../genes/PPP3CA.md)** (calcineurin catalytic subunit) — novel SMG in lung ADC; missense mutations cluster in the autoinhibitory C-terminal domain (suggesting gain of function) and co-occur with activating [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) (p = 0.033) ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **[DOT1L](../genes/DOT1L.md)** (H3K79 methyltransferase) — novel SMG in 3% of lung ADCs, enriched for truncating mutations ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **[KMT2C](../genes/KMT2C.md) (MLL3) and [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md)** — significantly mutated methyltransferases in lung ADC ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **[CMTR2](../genes/CMTR2.md) / FTSJD1** (cap methyltransferase) — novel SMG in lung ADC enriched for frameshifts; also recurrent in [SF3B1](../genes/SF3B1.md) and [SNRPD3](../genes/SNRPD3.md) ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **[RASA1](../genes/RASA1.md)** (p120GAP) and **[CUL3](../genes/CUL3.md)** ([KEAP1](../genes/KEAP1.md) partner) — novel SMGs in lung SqCC enriched for frameshift mutations (p < 0.001 for RASA1 frameshift enrichment) ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **[EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md)** — kinase-domain duplication in lung ADC sample TCGA-49-4512; complex indels in EGFR or [MET](../genes/MET.md) in 11 tumors ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **[MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md)** — recurrent in-frame insertion in lung ADC ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **MET fusions:** novel **[MET](../genes/MET.md)–[CAPZA2](../genes/CAPZA2.md)** fusion (with neighboring gene) and previously reported **[KIF5B](../genes/KIF5B.md)–MET** ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **[NTRK2](../genes/NTRK2.md) fusions:** **[TRIM24](../genes/TRIM24.md)–[NTRK2](../genes/NTRK2.md)** in ADC and a novel **NTRK2–[TP63](../genes/TP63.md)** fusion in lung SqCC ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **[MET](../genes/MET.md) and [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) high-level amplifications** — enriched in tumors lacking other RTK/Ras/Raf activating events (p < 0.01) ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **Novel amplification target genes:** [KAT6A](../genes/KAT6A.md), [ZNF217](../genes/ZNF217.md), [MYCL](../genes/MYCL.md) (ADC); [IGF1R](../genes/IGF1R.md), [KDM5A](../genes/KDM5A.md), [PTP4A1](../genes/PTP4A1.md)/[PHF3](../genes/PHF3.md), [MYCL](../genes/MYCL.md) (SqCC) ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **[B2M](../genes/B2M.md)** — focally deleted in both ADC and SqCC, enriched for loss-of-function mutations (p < 0.01), Pan-Lung FDR q = 0.006 — implicated in MHC-I antigen presentation loss ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **[MB21D2](../genes/MB21D2.md) (C3orf59)** — recurrent p.Q311E with predicted neoepitope properties; not previously implicated in lung cancer ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).

## Clinical implications

- **Targeted therapy is largely subtype-specific.** The subtype-distinct landscapes of recurrently mutated drivers and SCNAs imply that approved RTK-directed therapies (e.g., EGFR-TKI for [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) mutant ADC, [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) and [ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md) inhibitors for [ALK](../genes/ALK.md)/[ROS1](../genes/ROS1.md) fusion ADC) are expected to apply almost exclusively to lung ADC, while lung SqCC has few directly actionable kinase drivers ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **Expanded druggable pool in oncogene-negative lung ADC.** Adding [SOS1](../genes/SOS1.md), [VAV1](../genes/VAV1.md), [RASA1](../genes/RASA1.md), [ARHGAP35](../genes/ARHGAP35.md), and amplifications at [FGFR1](../genes/FGFR1.md)/[WHSC1L1](../genes/WHSC1L1.md), [PDGFRA](../genes/PDGFRA.md)/[KIT](../genes/KIT.md)/[KDR](../genes/KDR.md), and [MAPK1](../genes/MAPK1.md) raises the proportion of lung ADCs with a candidate Ras/Raf/RTK pathway driver to 76% (overall) and 85% (in expert-reviewed subset) — narrowing the unexplained fraction ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **Predicted [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) kinase-domain duplication (KDD)** in TCGA-49-4512 — separately reported as afatinib-responsive in lung cancer (citation 31 in the paper) ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **Immunotherapy applies broadly across NSCLC histologies.** With ≥5 predicted neoepitopes in 47% of ADCs and 53% of SqCCs, the authors argue that checkpoint immunotherapy is expected to benefit both subtypes (in contrast to histology-restricted targeted therapies) ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **Smoking history is the dominant correlate of neoantigen load in ADC.** Never-smoker ADCs have significantly fewer neoepitopes than ever-smoker ADCs (p < 0.001) — relevant for biomarker stratification of immunotherapy candidates ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **Recurrent neoepitope hotspots** — [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md) p.E542K, [NFE2L2](../genes/NFE2L2.md) p.E79Q, [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) p.G466V, [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) p.G719A, multiple [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) hotspots, and [MB21D2](../genes/MB21D2.md) p.Q311E — are candidate shared neoantigens for off-the-shelf vaccine design ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- No mutation-status association with patient survival or tumor stage survived multiple-hypothesis correction (with or without controlling for stage) ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).

## Limitations & open questions

- **Whole-exome only.** The study could not detect mutations in non-coding regions or regulatory elements; the authors flag this as a target for future whole-genome lung cancer studies ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **Single sample per patient.** Intra-tumoral heterogeneity could not be assessed (in contrast to multi-region sequencing studies) ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **Underpowered for rare RTK/Ras/Raf events.** With 15–25% of lung ADCs still lacking a detectable activating alteration, additional rare recurrent drivers in known and novel pathway members likely remain undiscovered ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **Incomplete RNA-seq coverage** — fusion calling and MET exon-14 skipping may be underestimated because matched RNA-seq was not available for every tumor ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **Smoking annotation inaccuracy** — SI4-based classification predicted ever-smoker status well in ADC (AUC 0.87) but poorly in SqCC (AUC 0.62), suggesting some clinically annotated SqCC "never-smokers" are misclassified ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
- **Possible cutaneous metastases** — three lung SqCCs with UV signature SI7 may represent skin SCC metastatic to the lung, not true lung primaries ([PMID:27158780](../papers/27158780.md)).
