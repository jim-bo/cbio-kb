# Distinct clinical outcomes and biological features of specific KRAS mutants in human pancreatic cancer

**PMID:** 39214094 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.ccell.2024.08.002 · **Journal:** Cancer Cell (2024)

## TL;DR

In a consecutive MSK cohort of 1,360 resected PDAC patients, KRAS^G12R^ is enriched in stage I disease, associated with increased node-negativity, decreased distant recurrence, and improved overall survival relative to KRAS^G12D^. Spatial profiling (NanoString CosMx SMI, n=20) and bulk RNA-seq (n=100 tumors) reveal enhanced oncogenic KRAS signaling and EMT in KRAS^G12D^ versus increased TNF/NF-κB signaling in KRAS^G12R^ tumors. Isogenic Kras^mut^;Trp53^KO^ mouse organoids recapitulate the reduced migratory potential and improved orthotopic survival phenotype, validating the clinical findings.

## Cohort & data

- **Primary cohort:** 1,360 consecutive PDAC patients undergoing surgical resection at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center (MSKCC) between April 2004 and April 2019; 397 (29%) stage I, 963 (71%) stage II–III (AJCC 8th edition) [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md). Median follow-up of survivors: 84.9 months (95% CI 76.5–94.7 months).
- **Sequenced subset:** 397 patients with targeted tumor sequencing on MSK-[IMPACT341](../methods/IMPACT341.md)/[IMPACT410](../methods/IMPACT410.md)/[IMPACT468](../methods/IMPACT468.md) (103 stage I, 294 stage II–III); tumor DNA from FFPE resection specimens matched to germline blood controls [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- **Transcriptomic sub-cohort:** Bulk RNA-seq on 100 resected tumors (63 KRAS^G12D^, 37 KRAS^G12R^) from the Canadian COMPASS trial (NCT02750657) plus resected patients from the same consortium [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- **Spatial profiling:** High-plex in situ RNA profiling via [CosMx SMI](../methods/cosmx-smi.md) on tissue microarrays of 20 patients (7 KRAS^G12R^ PDAC, 7 KRAS^G12D^ PDAC, 6 normal pancreas); ~400 0.5×0.5 mm fields-of-view interrogated [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- **Mouse models:** Isogenic ex vivo-transformed pancreatic organoids from LSL-KrasG12D and LSL-KrasG12R mice, both carrying Trp53 loss via CRISPR; orthotopic transplantation into immunocompetent B6 mice [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- **Genomic data:** Deposited at cBioPortal as study `pancreas_msk_2024` (reference genome: hg19). RNA-seq deposited in the European Genome-Phenome Archive (EGA) [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- **External validation:** Pooled 950-patient multi-dataset cohort comprising [TCGA-Firehose Legacy](../datasets/paad_tcga.md) (n=148), [ICGC-PACA-AU/CA](../datasets/paad_icgc.md) (n=375 + n=232), and the Sausen resectable cohort (n=100); survival analyses used the 855 patients with matched genomic and survival data [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).

## Key findings

- KRAS altered in 361/397 (90%) of sequenced patients; [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) in 71%, [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) in 24%, [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) in 17%; no significant difference in driver-gene frequencies between early- and late-stage disease after multiple-testing correction [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- KRAS allele distribution: G12D 36.5% (n=145), G12V 32.5% (n=129), G12R 13.9% (n=55), other 8.1% (n=32), WT 9.1% (n=36) [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- KRAS^G12R^ enriched in stage I vs stage II–III PDAC (23% vs 11%; p=0.022); 43.6% of KRAS^G12R^ patients were stage I vs 23.6% for KRAS^G12D^ (p=0.022) [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- Stage migration for KRAS^G12R^ driven by node-negativity, not primary tumor size: KRAS^G12R^ node-negative in 47% vs KRAS^G12D^ 26% (p=0.019); T-stage was not different between alleles [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- KRAS^G12R^ patients more likely to have a family history of pancreatic cancer (16.4% vs 4.2% for KRAS^G12D^, p=0.003) and tended to be non-smokers (not significant) [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- KRAS^G12R^ tumors carried more [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) mutations than KRAS^G12D^ (40% vs 22.1%, p=0.046); two or more tumor suppressor co-mutations (TP53/SMAD4/CDKN2A) more common in KRAS^G12R^ (51% vs 30%, p=0.029); TP53 and SMAD4 frequencies not allele-dependent [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- Time-to-recurrence: distant recurrence less frequent in KRAS^G12R^ (2-year CIF 24.2% vs 43.2% for KRAS^G12D^; p=0.017); local recurrence elevated in KRAS^G12R^ (2-year CIF 20.6% vs 10.6%; p=0.045) [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- Overall survival (Kaplan-Meier): other KRAS 47 months, KRAS^WT^ 46, KRAS^G12R^ 39, KRAS^G12V^ 39, KRAS^G12D^ 30 (p=0.026); TP53 mutation associated with worse OS (33.7 vs 43.6 months, p=0.014) [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- External validation (n=855 pooled): KRAS^G12D^ median OS 15.6 months vs KRAS^G12R^ 20.8, KRAS^G12V^ 22.4, KRAS^WT^ 27 months (p=0.006); multivariate: KRAS^G12R^ (HR 0.77 [0.60–0.99], p=0.038) and KRAS^G12V^ (HR 0.71 [0.58–0.88], p=0.02) independently predictive of better OS [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- [CosMx SMI](../methods/cosmx-smi.md) spatial profiling and bulk [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md): KRAS^G12D^ enriched for EMT (vimentin, fibronectin), mTORC1, E2F/G2M Hallmarks; KRAS^G12R^ enriched for cytokeratin, NF-κB (p65), CD31, TNFα/NF-κB signaling [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- Mouse organoid orthotopic transplantation: KrasG12R;p53KO median OS 90 days vs KrasG12D;p53KO 44 days (p<0.001); KrasG12R organoids show reduced migration without growth/proliferation differences [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).

## Genes & alterations

- [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) — PDAC allele-specific biology; G12R is prognostically favorable (early-stage, node-negative, longer OS, less distant recurrence but increased local recurrence) while G12D drives canonical oncogenic KRAS/EMT transcriptional programs. G12V confers improved OS without distinct clinicopathologic features. Allele frequencies: G12D 36.5%, G12V 32.5%, G12R 13.9% in 397 sequenced patients [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — mutated in 71% of sequenced PDAC; frequency not different by KRAS allele; TP53 mutation associated with shorter OS (33.7 vs 43.6 months, p=0.014) [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — mutated in 24% overall; enriched in KRAS^G12R^ tumors (40% vs 22.1% for KRAS^G12D^, p=0.046); copy number deletion frequency low and not incorporated into allele comparisons [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- [SMAD4](../genes/SMAD4.md) — mutated in 17% of sequenced PDAC; frequency not different by KRAS allele [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) — putative oncogenic mutations enriched in early-stage vs late-stage disease (3.9% vs 0.7%, p=0.042) but not significant after multiple comparisons [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- [TGFBR2](../genes/TGFBR2.md) — enriched in early-stage vs late-stage disease (8.7% vs 2.4%, p=0.008) but not significant after multiple comparisons [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) — KrasG12R organoids show loss of Myc activation relative to KrasG12D; MYC is a key downstream effector of KRAS in PDAC [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).

## Clinical implications

- Routine KRAS allele-specific mutation profiling is warranted in resected PDAC: KRAS^G12R^ identifies a subset with better intrinsic prognosis marked by reduced nodal spread and distant recurrence, suggesting greater net benefit from local control via surgery and reduced need for intensive neoadjuvant chemotherapy [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- KRAS^G12R^ tumors may respond more favorably to neoadjuvant [fluorouracil](../drugs/fluorouracil.md)-based chemotherapy (most frequently FOLFIRINOX: [fluorouracil](../drugs/fluorouracil.md) + [irinotecan](../drugs/irinotecan.md) + [oxaliplatin](../drugs/oxaliplatin.md)): enrichment of KRAS^G12R^ among NAC-followed-by-resection patients suggests higher chemosensitivity rather than borderline-resectable selection (trend; p=0.094) [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- Allele-specific transcriptional programs (NF-κB in G12R vs KRAS/EMT in G12D) suggest divergent actionable vulnerabilities across KRAS-mutant PDAC, rather than a uniform pan-KRAS therapeutic strategy; novel KRAS inhibitors in preclinical development should be tested in allele-specific contexts [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- Tumor suppressor co-mutation burden (number of co-mutated TP53/SMAD4/CDKN2A) is an independent prognostic factor in external multi-cohort validation; absence of tumor suppressor co-mutations: HR 0.72 (0.56–0.92), p=0.009 for improved OS [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).

## Limitations & open questions

- Sequenced cohort (n=397) is a subset of the full 1,360 resected patients; MSK-IMPACT panel-based sequencing incompletely captures CDKN2A/B deletions (noted by authors) [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- Spatial profiling (n=14 tumors) and bulk RNA-seq (n=100) sample sizes are modest; allele-specific transcriptional differences are internally consistent but require larger external validation [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- Mechanistic basis for reduced metastatic propensity of KRAS^G12R^ beyond NF-κB/EMT axis and deficient PI3K/macropinocytosis engagement remains unresolved [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- Single-institution, surgically-resectable cohort — findings may not generalize to unresectable stage III or metastatic stage IV disease, which was not studied [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- How KRAS allele status modifies biological responses to conventional chemotherapy or novel targeted therapy was not addressed [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
- KRAS^G12V^ shows improved OS similar to KRAS^G12R^ but without distinctive clinicopathologic or transcriptional features; underlying mechanism is an open question [PMID:39214094](../papers/39214094.md).
