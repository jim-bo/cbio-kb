# CDKN2B

## Overview

CDKN2B encodes p15^INK4b, a cyclin-dependent kinase inhibitor co-located with [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) on 9p21 and typically co-deleted, contributing to cell-cycle dysregulation.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- CDKN2A/CDKN2B alterations were more frequent in [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) brain metastases (34%) than in primary tumors (13%; p=0.003, q=0.04); cell-cycle pathway alterations were 56% in BM vs 32% in PT (p=0.004, q=0.041), with the pathway enrichment driven by CDKN2A/B [PMID:37591896](../papers/37591896.md).
- In the [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) sub-cohort the CDKN2A/B enrichment persisted (31% BM vs 18% PT; p=0.004) [PMID:37591896](../papers/37591896.md).
- Frequently co-altered with [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) in urothelial carcinoma (alongside [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) and [KDM6A](../genes/KDM6A.md)) in the MSK [bladder_msk_2023](../datasets/bladder_msk_2023.md) cohort [PMID:37682528](../papers/37682528.md).
- Homozygous CDKN2A/B deletion defined "molecular grade-high" in IDH-mutant low-grade glioma and drove ~2x faster tumor volume growth rate (19.17% vs 9.54% per 6 months) during active surveillance [PMID:37910594](../papers/37910594.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) brain metastasis — CDKN2B deep deletion, co-occurring with [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md), marks cell-cycle pathway dysregulation as a near-obligate event for BM development [PMID:37591896](../papers/37591896.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- CDKN2B is co-deleted with [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) as a 9p21 unit in [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) brain metastases [PMID:37591896](../papers/37591896.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- CDKN2A/B loss is proposed as a rationale for CDK4/6-directed strategies in CNS-tropic [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md), though not tested in the corpus [PMID:37591896](../papers/37591896.md).

## Open questions

- Whether CDK4/6 inhibition modifies BM risk or outcome in CDKN2A/B-deleted [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md) remains untested [PMID:37591896](../papers/37591896.md).
