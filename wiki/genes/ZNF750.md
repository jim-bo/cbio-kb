# ZNF750

## Overview

ZNF750 (Zinc Finger Protein 750) is a transcription factor that regulates squamous-cell differentiation. It has been identified as a novel tumor suppressor in esophageal squamous cell carcinoma ([ESCC](../cancer_types/ESCC.md)), with recurrent truncating mutations and focal deletions.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- ZNF750 is significantly mutated (q = 1.24E-06) in [ESCC](../cancer_types/ESCC.md), predominantly truncating mutations; focal deletion in 3.4% of [ESCC](../cancer_types/ESCC.md); mRNA and protein down-regulated; functional novel tumor suppressor regulating squamous-cell differentiation [PMID:24686850](../papers/24686850.md)
- [TP63](../genes/TP63.md) transcriptional target; mutated in 4% of HNSCC within the significant 17q25.3 deletion peak (TCGA, n=279) [PMID:25631445](../papers/25631445.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- Esophageal squamous cell carcinoma ([ESCC](../cancer_types/ESCC.md)): significantly mutated tumor suppressor with focal deletion in 3.4% of cases [PMID:24686850](../papers/24686850.md)
