MSK HCC Prospective IMPACT Cohort (2018)

Overview

A prospective single-institution cohort of 127 unique patients with advanced hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) sequenced at MSKCC under protocol NCT01775072 (August 2014 – August 2017) using the MSK-IMPACT targeted NGS assay. The study linked genotype to clinical outcomes on systemic therapy — specifically sorafenib (n=81) and immune checkpoint inhibitors (ICI; n=31). Key findings were a PI3K-mTOR pathway predictive biomarker for sorafenib resistance and a WNT/β-catenin pathway predictive biomarker for ICI innate resistance. All data are available at cBioPortal (study hcc_mskimpact_2018) and cBioPortal DataHub. PMID:30373752

Composition

  • 127 unique patients (134 specimens, including 7 serial biopsies); enrolled August 2014 – August 2017 at MSKCC. PMID:30373752
  • Cancer type: conventional HCC; fibrolamellar and biphenotypic HCC-cholangiocarcinoma excluded. PMID:30373752
  • Demographics: 79% male, 76% non-Asian; etiology HBV 17%, HCV 35%, nonviral 49%; BCLC stage A 29%, B 40%, C 30%. PMID:30373752
  • MSK-IMPACT panels: IMPACT341 (21 cases), IMPACT410 (70 cases), IMPACT468 (36 cases); Illumina HiSeq 2500; mean coverage 690-fold; median tumor purity 60%. PMID:30373752
  • TMB and MSI: TMB calculated per sample; MSI via MSIsensor (MSI-H threshold ≥10 repeats). PMID:30373752
  • Treatment subsets: sorafenib n=81 (75 first-line, 6 second-line); ICI n=31 (1 anti-CTLA-4 monotherapy, 25 anti-PD-1/PD-L1 monotherapy, 5 combination). PMID:30373752
  • Reference comparator: TCGA HCC cohort (lihc_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018), N=196. PMID:30373752
  • Reference genome: GRCh37

Notable findings derived from this cohort

  • Mutational landscape (134 specimens): alterations detected in 95%; most frequent genes: TERT 55.6%, CTNNB1 35.7%, TP53 32.5%, ARID1A 12.7%, TSC2 10.6%. PMID:30373752
  • Mutual exclusivity: TP53 mutations vs. WNT pathway alterations (p=0.003) defines at least two major HCC genomic subsets. PMID:30373752
  • Sorafenib (n=81, 79 evaluable): median PFS 4.8 months, median OS 16.4 months. Oncogenic PI3K-mTOR pathway alterations (n=12) associated with markedly shorter PFS (1.9 vs. 5.3 months; HR 3.8, 95% CI 2.0–7.5, p<0.0001) and OS (10.4 vs. 17.9 months; HR 2.5, p=0.01). PMID:30373752
  • Immune checkpoint inhibitors (n=31, 27 evaluable): ORR 11.1%; activating WNT/β-catenin alterations (CTNNB1 gain-of-function or AXIN1 loss) in 10 patients yielded 0/10 clinical benefit vs. 9/17 (53%) for non-WNT patients (p=0.009); WNT PFS 2.0 vs. 7.4 months (HR 9.2, 95% CI 2.9–28.8, p<0.0001). PMID:30373752
  • TMB not predictive for ICI: median TMB 4.08 overall; no MSI-H cases; TMB did not stratify ICI response (median 3.8 vs. 3.9 mutations/Mb, p=0.98). PMID:30373752
  • Actionability: 24% of patients harbored ≥1 actionable alteration via OncoKB; no level 1/2A alterations. Actionable hits include TSC2 7%, FGF19 amplification 6.3%, PTEN 3.9%, MET amplification 1.5%. PMID:30373752
  • Genome-matched therapy: 5/87 (5.7%) systemically-treated patients matched to genome-directed therapy; 4 received everolimus for TSC1/2-altered HCC; 3/4 achieved ≥5 months clinical benefit. PMID:30373752
  • Etiology: virally mediated HCC enriched for TP53 mutations vs. nonviral (45% vs. 21%, p=0.001). PMID:30373752
