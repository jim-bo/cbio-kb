# ITGB4

## Overview

ITGB4 (Integrin Beta 4) is a transmembrane receptor that typically forms heterodimers with ITGA6 (alpha-6 integrin) to mediate cell-matrix adhesion. In hepatocellular carcinoma ([HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md)), ITGB4 participates in a PD-L1/EGFR/ITGB4 trimolecular complex that drives SREBP1c-dependent lipid accumulation, linking immune checkpoint signaling to metabolic reprogramming in the tumor microenvironment.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- ITGB4 forms a PD-L1/EGFR/ITGB4 complex in HCC that drives SREBP1c-dependent lipid accumulation (Zhao et al.); this complex mechanistically links PD-L1 signaling to de novo lipogenesis in HCC. [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md): ITGB4 participates in PD-L1/EGFR/ITGB4 complex–mediated activation of SREBP1c and lipid accumulation — connecting immune checkpoint biology to metabolic reprogramming in the HCC tumor microenvironment. [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- ITGB4 function in HCC is linked to [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) (co-complex partner) and SREBF1/SREBP1c (downstream transcriptional effector), placing it at the intersection of lipid metabolism and immune checkpoint pathways. [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- The PD-L1/EGFR/ITGB4 complex represents a potential mechanism by which PD-L1 drives metabolic reprogramming beyond its canonical immune checkpoint role; disruption of this complex is a hypothesis-generating therapeutic concept in HCC.

## Open questions

- Whether ITGB4 expression or complex formation is actionable as a biomarker or therapeutic target in HCC has not been prospectively tested.
- The precise structural and signaling mechanism of the PD-L1/EGFR/ITGB4 complex driving SREBP1c activation in HCC requires further characterization.
