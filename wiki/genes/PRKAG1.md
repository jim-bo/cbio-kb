# PRKAG1

## Overview

PRKAG1 (Protein Kinase AMP-Activated Non-Catalytic Subunit Gamma 1) encodes a regulatory gamma subunit of AMP-activated protein kinase (AMPK). It harbors a mutation analogous to known activating substitutions in PRKAG2 in an independent chromophobe renal cell carcinoma (chRCC) cohort, implicating the AMPK metabolic axis in chRCC pathogenesis.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- p.Arg299Gln mutation identified in an independent chRCC validation cohort; analogous to PRKAG2 p.Arg531Gln (CBS4 AMP-binding domain substitution), predicted activating by analogy to glycogen storage cardiomyopathy mutants [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- chRCC: recurrent AMPK regulatory subunit mutation suggesting a metabolic axis as a driver [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- PRKAG1 p.Arg299Gln parallels PRKAG2 CBS4 mutations in chRCC, suggesting convergent AMPK activation via either gamma subunit [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- Combined PRKAG1/PRKAG2 and PDHB mutations in chRCC define a metabolically deregulated subtype, potentially actionable via AMPK or PDH modulators; no clinical data available [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)

## Open questions

- Whether PRKAG1 p.Arg299Gln is truly activating in the tumor context requires direct functional validation; the analogy to PRKAG2 cardiomyopathy mutants is circumstantial [PMID:25401301](../papers/25401301.md)
