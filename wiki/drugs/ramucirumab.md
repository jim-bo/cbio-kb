# ramucirumab

## Overview

Ramucirumab is a fully human IgG1 monoclonal antibody targeting VEGFR2 ([KDR](../genes/KDR.md)), blocking VEGF ligand binding and downstream angiogenic signaling. It is FDA-approved for several indications including [HCC](../cancer_types/HCC.md) (second-line, AFP ≥400 ng/mL, REACH-2 trial).

## Evidence in the corpus

- A narrative review of lipid metabolism in HCC cites ramucirumab among the subsequent TKIs/ICIs available for HCC after progression on first-line therapy; it represents one of the anti-angiogenic options in the HCC treatment landscape. Lipid-metabolic reprogramming (enhanced lipid uptake, de novo lipogenesis, suppression of ferroptosis) is proposed as a cross-resistance mechanism against multiple TKIs including ramucirumab. [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Resistance mechanisms

- Not specifically characterized in the corpus; cross-resistance with other VEGFR-targeting agents in HCC is proposed via lipid-metabolic reprogramming (FATP4, [SCD](../genes/SCD.md), DNL, GPX4/SLC7A11 axes). [PMID:38355797](../papers/38355797.md)

## Cancer types (linked)
