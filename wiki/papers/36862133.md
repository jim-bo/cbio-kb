# Overcoming barriers to tumor genomic profiling through direct-to-patient outreach

**PMID:** 36862133 · **DOI:** 10.1158/1078-0432.CCR-22-3247 · **Journal:** Clinical Cancer Research (2023)

## TL;DR

The Make-an-IMPACT program used direct-to-patient outreach via social media and disease-specific advocacy groups to enroll 333 patients with rare cancers (histiocytosis, female germ cell tumors, pediatric cancers) worldwide for free clinical tumor genomic profiling via MSK-IMPACT, returning results to patients and their local physicians. Matched targeted therapy produced clinical benefit in 17/18 histiocytosis patients (mean duration 21.7 months), and whole-exome sequencing of 59 female germ cell tumors uncovered a near-haploid genomic subtype in 17% of cases [PMID:36862133](../papers/36862133.md).

## Cohort & data

- 333 consented patients enrolled between March 2016 and October 2020; 288 (86.4%) provided tumor tissue, 250 (86.8% of those) had DNA of sufficient quality for MSK-IMPACT sequencing [PMID:36862133](../papers/36862133.md).
- 63% enrolled from US sites; 124 patients (37%) from 17 countries outside the US.
- Most common cancer types successfully profiled: histiocytosis (n=84; [LCH](../cancer_types/LCH.md), [ECD](../cancer_types/ECD.md)), female germ cell tumor (n=54; [OGCT](../cancer_types/OGCT.md)), male germ cell tumor (n=54; [MGCT](../cancer_types/MGCT.md)), adenoid cystic carcinoma (n=19; [ACYC](../cancer_types/ACYC.md)).
- Assay: [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/IMPACT505.md) (up to 505 genes) with select targeted RNA sequencing; whole-exome recapture performed for 62 ovarian GCT tumor/normal pairs from 59 patients.
- Data deposited in cBioPortal (study [`makeanimpact_ccr_2023`](../datasets/makeanimpact_ccr_2023.md)) and AACR Project GENIE; WES BAMs in dbGaP phs001783.

## Key findings

- Histiocytosis (n=84 profiled): most commonly mutated actionable genes were [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) (33%), [MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md) (13%), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) (7%), and [CSF1R](../genes/CSF1R.md) (2.4%); actionable fusions included MS4A6A-[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), DOCK8-BRAF, HLA-A-BRAF, TFG-[ALK](../genes/ALK.md), and a PRDX1-[NTRK1](../genes/NTRK1.md) fusion detected by [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md) [PMID:36862133](../papers/36862133.md).
- 47/84 (56%) histiocytosis patients had a potentially actionable alteration; 18 received matched targeted therapy; 17/18 (94%) had clinical benefit with a mean treatment duration of 21.7 months (range 6–40+) [PMID:36862133](../papers/36862133.md).
- Female GCT (n=83 with MSK-IMPACT; 67 ovarian primaries): oncogenic mutations in [KIT](../genes/KIT.md), [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md), and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) were seen in a minority; mean TMB was low (2.86; range 0–42.1); two ovarian GCTs with squamous transformation were TMB-H (28.1 and 42.1 mut/Mb) [PMID:36862133](../papers/36862133.md).
- One TMB-H ovarian GCT with squamous transformation (42.1 mut/Mb) achieved a complete response to [pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md) ongoing at 34 months.
- WES of 62 female GCT pairs: whole genome doubling in ≥13 patients; 17% (n=10) of female GCTs had a near-haploid genomic profile, mutually exclusive with 12p gain — a phenotype rarely seen in other cancer types [PMID:36862133](../papers/36862133.md).
- 51% of arm-level deletions in female GCTs contained a reciprocal amplification (RLOH), versus 4% in TCGA-OV.
- Central pathology review led to reclassification in several cases (e.g., yolk sac carcinoma misdiagnosed as GCT; a pediatric "rhabdomyosarcoma" reclassified as undifferentiated sarcoma with an EWSR1-PATZ1 fusion, changing the chemotherapy regimen).

## Genes & alterations

- **[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md)** — V600E in ~33% of histiocytosis; 8 patients treated with [vemurafenib](../drugs/vemurafenib.md) or [dabrafenib](../drugs/dabrafenib.md). [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) fusions (MS4A6A, DOCK8, HLA-A) also identified. One case had co-occurring [KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md) G12D and [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) D594H.
- **[MAP2K1](../genes/MAP2K1.md)** — mutated in 13% of histiocytosis; treated with MEK inhibitors ([trametinib](../drugs/trametinib.md), [cobimetinib](../drugs/cobimetinib.md)); one patient received an ERK inhibitor on trial.
- **[KRAS](../genes/KRAS.md)** — mutated in 7% of histiocytosis; also among oncogenic mutations in female GCT.
- **[NRAS](../genes/NRAS.md)** — A59_E76 mutation in one histiocytosis patient treated with MEK inhibitor; mostly subclonal in female GCT.
- **[CSF1R](../genes/CSF1R.md)** — mutated in 2.4% of histiocytosis.
- **[ALK](../genes/ALK.md)** — TFG-[ALK](../genes/ALK.md) fusion in one histiocytosis patient.
- **[NTRK1](../genes/NTRK1.md)** — PRDX1-[NTRK1](../genes/NTRK1.md) fusion in one histiocytosis patient, detected only by targeted [RNA-seq](../methods/rna-seq.md).
- **[PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)** — one histiocytosis patient had a durable response to [alpelisib](../drugs/alpelisib.md); two ovarian GCT [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)-mutant patients had no durable response.
- **[PTPN11](../genes/PTPN11.md)** — mutated in one histiocytosis patient treated with a MEK inhibitor.
- **[KIT](../genes/KIT.md)**, **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md)** — oncogenic mutations observed in a minority of female GCTs; mostly clonal by WES.
- **[ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md)** — amplification in one female GCT patient treated with [trastuzumab](../drugs/trastuzumab.md) (no durable response).

## Clinical implications

- Direct-to-patient outreach is feasible for rare-cancer cohort assembly and can deliver actionable genomic results returned to local physicians for treatment selection [PMID:36862133](../papers/36862133.md).
- MAPK-pathway matched therapy (RAF/MEK/ERK inhibitors) produced high rates of clinical benefit in histiocytosis.
- Tumor-agnostic immunotherapy ([pembrolizumab](../drugs/pembrolizumab.md)) may benefit rare TMB-H GCTs with squamous transformation.
- Lineage context matters: [PIK3CA](../genes/PIK3CA.md)-mutant histiocytosis responded to [alpelisib](../drugs/alpelisib.md) while PIK3CA-mutant ovarian GCT did not, suggesting lineage-specific conditioning of targeted therapy response.
- Central pathology review changed diagnosis/treatment in multiple cases, arguing for centralized review for rare cancers.

## Limitations & open questions

- Response assessments were made by local physicians without central radiology review; restaging intervals were not protocolized.
- Access to matched targeted or immunotherapies was often limited by cost or local insurance, especially internationally.
- Selection bias: a subset of histiocytosis patients were enrolled after prior local profiling was uninformative, potentially inflating the no-mutation-detected fraction.
- The functional and therapeutic implications of the near-haploid ovarian GCT phenotype remain to be explored.
- Germline analysis was not clinically returned in this program.
