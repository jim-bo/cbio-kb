**PMID:** 37646491 · **DOI:** 10.1002/mp.16704 · **Journal:** Medical Physics (2024)

## TL;DR

Peng et al. propose a conditional denoising diffusion probabilistic model (DDPM) that translates cone-beam CT (CBCT) images into synthetic CT (sCT) images suitable for online adaptive radiotherapy (ART). Trained on paired CBCT and deformed planning-CT slices from 41 brain and 47 head-and-neck (H&N) patients, the conditional DDPM concatenates the CBCT as static guidance into each reverse-diffusion step. On held-out test slices the method reduced mean absolute error (MAE) of Hounsfield units versus deformed planning CT from 40.63 HU to 25.99 HU (brain) and from 38.99 HU to 32.56 HU (H&N), and outperformed four other diffusion-based schemes and a supervised Cycle GAN in both quantitative metrics and visual artifact suppression [PMID:37646491](../papers/37646491.md).

## Cohort & data

- **Patients:** 41 brain patients (30 train / 11 test) and 47 H&N patients (37 train / 10 test); cancer histology not specified — patients were selected as IGRT recipients with paired imaging.
- **Imaging:** planning CT acquired on Siemens SOMATOM Definition AS at 120 kVp; CBCT acquired on Varian TrueBeam at 100 kVp. Voxel size 1.0 × 1.0 × 1.0 mm³ for both modalities.
- **Training data:** 4682 brain slices and 4314 H&N slices of paired CBCT and deformed planning CT (dpCT). Deformable registration performed in Velocity AI 3.2.1; body contours merged across modalities; slices cropped to 256 × 256 and intensity-normalized to [−1, 1].
- **Test data:** 500 randomly selected slices per anatomical site.
- **No molecular/genomic data.** No cBioPortal study, no gene panel, no drug — this is an imaging-physics study.

## Key findings

- **Brain study quantitative results (sCT vs CBCT vs dpCT ground truth):** MAE 25.99 HU vs 40.63 HU; PSNR 30.49 dB vs 27.87 dB; NCC 0.99 vs 0.98 [PMID:37646491](../papers/37646491.md).
- **H&N study quantitative results:** MAE 32.56 HU vs 38.99 HU for CBCT; PSNR 27.65 dB vs 27.00 dB; NCC ~0.98 in both [PMID:37646491](../papers/37646491.md).
- **Averaging over 10 stochastic samples** further improved MAE and PSNR (e.g. brain MAE → 22.31 HU, PSNR → 31.71 dB) due to reduced posterior-sample variance.
- **Comparison to other generative schemes:** the proposed conditional DDPM beat Uncond-1 (ILVR), Uncond-2, Adap-Cond-1, Adap-Cond-2, and a supervised Cycle GAN on MAE / PSNR / NCC for both anatomical sites; competing methods showed structural distortion, residual artifacts, or hallucinated low-contrast features.
- **Robust to severe input artifacts:** in H&N cases corrupted by metal-implant streaking, the sCT was less artifact-laden than even the dpCT training target, suggesting the model learned an artifact-free CT manifold rather than memorizing the imperfect supervisory signal.
- **HU-histogram fidelity:** sCT histograms across all 500 test slices closely tracked the dpCT histograms while CBCT histograms were systematically displaced.
- **Architecture:** time-embedded U-Net with residual and attention blocks; T = 1000 diffusion steps; β linearly scheduled from 1e-4 to 0.02; Adam optimizer (lr 1e-4); batch size 2; trained 1 × 10⁶ iterations (~50 h on a single 24 [GB](../cancer_types/GB.md) Nvidia RTX 3090).
- **Throughput:** ~2 minutes per slice at inference — explicitly flagged by authors as too slow for online ART without sampling-acceleration techniques.

## Genes & alterations

None. This is a medical-physics imaging paper; no genomic alterations are studied.

## Clinical implications

- **Adaptive radiotherapy enablement:** the authors argue accurate HU values in sCT make CBCT-derived images directly usable for dose calculation and organ segmentation, a prerequisite for online CBCT-based ART that avoids per-fraction replanning CT.
- **Artifact robustness:** demonstrated suppression of beam-hardening, streaking, and metal artifacts could expand the patient population eligible for image-guided dose adaptation, particularly H&N patients with dental implants.
- No treatment, biomarker, or prognostic claim is made.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Reference standard imperfection:** dpCT ground truth contains residual mismatch with CBCT due to inter-fraction anatomy change, so MAE/PSNR underestimate the model's true fidelity to a hypothetical artifact-free CT.
- **Inference speed:** ~2 min/slice and ~1000 forward passes per sample is incompatible with online ART; authors defer acceleration (knowledge distillation, DDIM, DPM-Solver) to future work.
- **2D slice-wise:** no 3D context or patch-based modeling, which the authors expect would improve through-plane structural preservation.
- **Hallucination versus residual artifact:** the authors note these are visually indistinguishable in heavily artifact-corrupted regions; a physician-in-the-loop study has not yet been conducted.
- **No multi-channel / dual-energy CT extension:** the stochastic noise prediction has no mechanism to enforce inter-channel consistency for DECT synthesis.
- **No cancer-type stratification:** patient indications, tumor histology, and clinical outcomes are not reported, so downstream dosimetric or clinical impact is unmeasured.
