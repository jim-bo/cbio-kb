# TRPC4AP

## Overview

TRPC4AP encodes transient receptor potential cation channel subfamily C member 4-associated protein, which interacts with [MYCN](../genes/MYCN.md) and has been implicated in ubiquitin-mediated protein regulation. Its role in cancer is not well established, but recurrent expressed mutations in rhabdomyosarcoma suggest it may be a candidate driver in pediatric sarcomas.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Recurrently expressed mutations (2 cases) in a comprehensive rhabdomyosarcoma ([RMS](../cancer_types/RMS.md)) genomic analysis; interacts with MYCN; flagged as a candidate target requiring functional validation [PMID:24436047](../papers/24436047.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- [RMS](../cancer_types/RMS.md): recurrent expressed mutations in pediatric RMS genomic characterization [PMID:24436047](../papers/24436047.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

## Therapeutic relevance

- MYCN interaction raises potential relevance to MYCN-driven oncogenesis in RMS; no direct therapeutic targeting reported in this corpus.

## Open questions
