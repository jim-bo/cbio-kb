**PMID:** 23792563 · **DOI:** 10.1038/nature12222 · **Journal:** Nature (2013)

## TL;DR

The Cancer Genome Atlas Research Network performed multi-platform molecular characterization of clear cell renal cell carcinoma ([CCRCC](../cancer_types/CCRCC.md)) across more than 400 primary nephrectomy tumors, integrating whole-exome sequencing, DNA copy number arrays, mRNA and miRNA sequencing, DNA methylation arrays, and reverse phase protein arrays. They identified 19 significantly mutated genes — dominated by [VHL](../genes/VHL.md), [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md), [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md), [KDM5C](../genes/KDM5C.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md), [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md), and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — and showed that mutations in the SWI/SNF chromatin remodeling complex ([PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md)) and the H3K36 methyltransferase [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md) drive widespread DNA hypomethylation and pleiotropic transcriptional changes. The PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway was altered in ~28% of tumors. Aggressive cancers showed a metabolic shift toward the Warburg phenotype — decreased AMPK and Krebs cycle activity, increased pentose phosphate pathway and fatty acid synthesis — that correlated with worse survival.

## Cohort & data

- 446 primary nephrectomy specimens with histologically confirmed clear cell renal cell carcinoma ([CCRCC](../cancer_types/CCRCC.md)), ≥60% tumor nuclei by pathology review (median 85%); 372 samples had data across all platforms ("Core" set), 446 across at least one ("Extended" set).
- Dataset: [kirc_tcga_pub](../datasets/kirc_tcga_pub.md) — TCGA Kidney Renal Clear Cell Carcinoma publication cohort.
- Platforms: [whole-exome sequencing](../methods/whole-exome-seq.md) (n=417 tumors yielding 36,353 putative somatic mutations), [Affymetrix SNP arrays](../methods/affymetrix-snp6.md) for copy number, [RNA sequencing](../methods/rna-seq.md), miRNA sequencing, DNA methylation arrays ([450K Human Methylation](../methods/450k-methylation-array.md), n=224 for [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md) analysis), and reverse phase protein arrays. Whole-genome sequencing on 22 patients calibrated and confirmed 83% of WES mutation calls.

## Key findings

- **19 significantly mutated genes (q<0.1)**: top 8 (q<0.00001) are [VHL](../genes/VHL.md), [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md), [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md), [KDM5C](../genes/KDM5C.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md), [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md), and [TP53](../genes/TP53.md). Only [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md) mutation correlated with worse survival (Figure S27). Approximately 20% of cases had none of the 19 SMGs.
- **Chromosome 3p loss in 91% of samples**, encompassing the four most commonly mutated genes ([VHL](../genes/VHL.md), [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md), [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md), [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md)). Arm-level 14q loss (with [HIF1A](../genes/HIF1A.md)) occurred in 45% of samples; 5q gain in 67%.
- **Focal amplifications** implicated [PRKCI](../genes/PRKCI.md) and [MECOM](../genes/MECOM.md) (3q26), [MDM4](../genes/MDM4.md) (1q32), [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) (8q24), and [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md) (9p24); focal deletions affected [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) (9p21), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) (10q23), [NEGR1](../genes/NEGR1.md), [QKI](../genes/QKI.md), [CADM2](../genes/CADM2.md), [PTPRD](../genes/PTPRD.md), and [NRXN3](../genes/NRXN3.md).
- **Recurrent [SFPQ](../genes/SFPQ.md)–[TFE3](../genes/TFE3.md) fusion** in 5 samples (84 putative RNA fusions total, 11/13 validated → 85% true positive rate); all five fusion-positive tumors were [VHL](../genes/VHL.md) wildtype with X(p11) rearrangements visible by FISH in 3/3 evaluable cases.
- **Epigenetic silencing of [VHL](../genes/VHL.md) in ~7% of tumors**, mutually exclusive with [VHL](../genes/VHL.md) mutation. [UQCRH](../genes/UQCRH.md) hypermethylated in 36% of tumors. Promoter hypermethylation frequency increased with stage and grade.
- **[SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md) mutation associated with regional loss of DNA methylation** at non-promoter regions (FDR=0.001, |Δβ|>0.1, n=2,557 CpG loci differentially methylated between mutant and wild-type), consistent with H3K36me3 guiding DNMT3A-mediated methylation.
- **Four mRNA subtypes (m1–m4) and four miRNA subtypes (mi1–mi4)** by unsupervised clustering. m1 corresponds to prior ccA classification with survival advantage; m4 captures previously unclassified tumors. m1 was enriched for [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md) mutations (39% vs 27%, p=0.027); m3 enriched for [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) deletion (53% vs 26%, p<0.0001) and [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) mutation (11% vs 1%, p<0.0001); m4 enriched for [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md) (17% vs 7%, p=0.002) and [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md) (12% vs 4%, p=0.01) mutations.
- **PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway altered in ~28% of tumors** by MEMo mutual-exclusivity analysis; the module includes [RACK1](../genes/RACK1.md) (GNB2L1) and [SQSTM1](../genes/SQSTM1.md)/p62 from the 5q35.3 amplicon, and frequent over-expression of [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) correlated with receptor phosphorylation.
- **Metabolic shift correlates with worse survival**: down-regulation of AMPK complex and Krebs cycle genes; up-regulation of pentose phosphate pathway ([G6PD](../genes/G6PD.md), PGLS, TALDO, TKT) and fatty acid synthesis ([FASN](../genes/FASN.md), ACC); increased glutamine transporter expression. miR-21 promoter hypomethylation (higher miR-21 expression) and [GRB10](../genes/GRB10.md) promoter methylation patterns were prognostic, with both regulating PI3K signaling.
- **HotNet identified 25 subnetworks**; the largest centered on [VHL](../genes/VHL.md) and partners, the second on the PBAF SWI/SNF complex ([PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md), [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md)). TieDIE linked chromatin-modifier mutations to transcriptional hubs including HIF/ARNT, MYC/Max, SP1, [FOXM1](../genes/FOXM1.md), JUN, and FOS, and to downstream effectors including [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md), NFκB1, IL6, [HIF1A](../genes/HIF1A.md), [BRCA1](../genes/BRCA1.md) (via [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md)), [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md), and [TGFBR2](../genes/TGFBR2.md).
- **Prognostic signatures** built on discovery (n=193) and validated on n=253: mRNA, miRNA, and protein signatures all added prognostic power beyond clinical variables in multivariate Cox analysis. Top protein correlates of worse survival were reduced AMPK and increased acetyl-CoA carboxylase (ACC).

## Genes & alterations

- [VHL](../genes/VHL.md) — top significantly mutated gene; mutually exclusive with promoter methylation (~7% of tumors silenced epigenetically); central to HIF stabilization in [CCRCC](../cancer_types/CCRCC.md).
- [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md) — significantly mutated (q<0.00001); SWI/SNF (PBAF) subunit; enriched in m1 subtype (39% vs 27%).
- [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md) — H3K36 methyltransferase; mutation associated with regional DNA hypomethylation at non-promoter CpGs (n=2,557 differentially methylated loci).
- [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md) — only SMG correlated with worse survival; enriched in m4 mRNA subtype (17% vs 7%, p=0.002).
- [KDM5C](../genes/KDM5C.md), [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md), [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md), [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) — among the top eight most significant SMGs (q<0.00001). [PTEN](../genes/PTEN.md) mutation enriched in m3 (11% vs 1%, p<0.0001); [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md) mutation enriched in m4 (12% vs 4%, p=0.01).
- [ARID1A](../genes/ARID1A.md), [SMARCA4](../genes/SMARCA4.md) — SWI/SNF complex members co-occurring in the HotNet PBAF subnetwork with [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md).
- [HIF1A](../genes/HIF1A.md) — frequently lost via 14q deletion (45% of samples), predicted to drive aggressive disease.
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md) — focal 9p21 deletion; enriched in m3 (53% vs 26%, p<0.0001).
- [MYC](../genes/MYC.md), [MDM4](../genes/MDM4.md), [JAK2](../genes/JAK2.md), [PRKCI](../genes/PRKCI.md), [MECOM](../genes/MECOM.md) — focal amplifications.
- [SFPQ](../genes/SFPQ.md)–[TFE3](../genes/TFE3.md) — recurrent in-frame fusion in 5 samples, all VHL wildtype, often with X(p11) translocation by FISH.
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) — over-expressed in the PI3K/AKT/mTOR alteration module with corresponding phospho-receptor increase; prior link to [lapatinib](../drugs/lapatinib.md) response cited.
- [RACK1](../genes/RACK1.md) (GNB2L1), [SQSTM1](../genes/SQSTM1.md) — both in the 5q35.3 amplicon, both linked to PI3K activation; copy number gain correlated with mRNA expression and pathway alteration status.
- [GRB10](../genes/GRB10.md) — promoter methylation pattern prognostic; tumor suppressor that negatively regulates PI3K/insulin signaling.
- [TSC2](../genes/TSC2.md) — PI3K pathway inhibitor whose higher expression correlated with better survival.
- [UQCRH](../genes/UQCRH.md) — hypermethylated in 36% of tumors, top inverse expression-vs-methylation correlate; not previously linked to CCRCC.
- [CTNNB1](../genes/CTNNB1.md), [TGFBR2](../genes/TGFBR2.md), [FASN](../genes/FASN.md), [G6PD](../genes/G6PD.md), [NEGR1](../genes/NEGR1.md), [QKI](../genes/QKI.md), [CADM2](../genes/CADM2.md), [PTPRD](../genes/PTPRD.md), [NRXN3](../genes/NRXN3.md) — downstream pathway and SCNA features highlighted in TieDIE / focal-deletion analyses.

## Clinical implications

- The authors explicitly nominate the **PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway as a therapeutic target** in CCRCC, supporting use of [MTOR](../genes/MTOR.md) and related inhibitors (citing prior trials of [everolimus](../drugs/everolimus.md) and [temsirolimus](../drugs/temsirolimus.md) in advanced RCC).
- [EGFR](../genes/EGFR.md) over-expression and phosphorylation in the PI3K-alteration module is linked by the authors to prior [lapatinib](../drugs/lapatinib.md) response data in CCRCC (citing Ravaud et al. 2008 phase III trial).
- [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md) mutation is the only SMG independently prognostic for worse survival.
- mRNA, miRNA, and protein signatures provide prognostic information beyond standard clinical variables (validated on n=253).
- Metabolic biomarkers — AMPK, ACC, pentose phosphate pathway, miR-21 / [GRB10](../genes/GRB10.md) methylation — are proposed as both prognostic markers and candidate therapeutic-vulnerability indicators given the Warburg-like shift in aggressive tumors.

## Limitations & open questions

- **Single-site sampling**: tumors were sampled from a single region of the primary, but CCRCC is known to be regionally heterogeneous (citing Gerlinger 2012). Extent to which convergent evolution captures critical events at single sites remains open.
- **Tumor cellularity** was lower and more variable than pathology review suggested (median 54% ± 14% vs 85% by review), reflecting stromal/endothelial contribution or heterogeneity; the authors argue mutation frequencies were nonetheless consistent with prior reports.
- **Primary-only cohort**: samples reflect patients fit for nephrectomy; the metastatic landscape was not characterized and is flagged for future work.
- **Mechanistic uncertainty for chromatin modifiers**: how [PBRM1](../genes/PBRM1.md), [SETD2](../genes/SETD2.md), and [BAP1](../genes/BAP1.md) mutations mechanistically drive transformation and the metabolic shift remains to be defined.
- **Therapeutic translation**: PI3K/AKT/mTOR targeting is supported by the genomics but specific predictive biomarkers tying alteration class to response were not tested in this study.
