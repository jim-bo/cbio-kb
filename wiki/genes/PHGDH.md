# PHGDH

## Overview

PHGDH (Phosphoglycerate Dehydrogenase) catalyzes the first committed step of the serine biosynthesis pathway, converting 3-phosphoglycerate to 3-phosphopyruvate. Upregulation of PHGDH supports one-carbon metabolism, nucleotide synthesis, and redox homeostasis in proliferating cancer cells. PHGDH amplification and overexpression have been reported in breast cancer, particularly in ER-negative/basal-like subtypes, making it a candidate metabolic therapeutic target.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Upregulated at the protein level (alongside PSAT1) in NMF Basal-I breast cancer in CPTAC proteogenomic analysis of 122 treatment-naive primary tumors; nominated as a therapeutic vulnerability in the basal/TNBC subtype. [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **[BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md) (NMF Basal-I / TNBC)**: PHGDH protein overexpression in Basal-I BRCA is part of a broader serine-synthesis upregulation alongside broad glycolytic protein elevation and mitochondrial hyperacetylation. [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md)

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-upregulated with PSAT1 (serine synthesis pathway) in NMF Basal-I alongside glycolytic enzymes (HK3, PFKP, GAPDH, ENO1, [LDHB](../genes/LDHB.md)). [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md)

## Therapeutic relevance

- PHGDH inhibition nominated as a metabolic therapeutic strategy for basal/TNBC breast cancer based on proteogenomic upregulation; clinical validation and preclinical functional studies are needed. [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md)

## Open questions

- Whether PHGDH protein overexpression in NMF Basal-I reflects gene amplification, transcriptional upregulation, or post-transcriptional regulation was not dissected in this study. [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md)
- The therapeutic window for PHGDH inhibition in TNBC vs normal tissue serine synthesis remains to be characterized. [PMID:33212010](../papers/33212010.md)
