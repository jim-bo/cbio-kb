# Large Cell/Anaplastic Medulloblastoma (AMBL)

## Overview

Note: OncoTree code AMBL maps to "Large Cell/Anaplastic Medulloblastoma" under the Embryonal Tumor branch. In the corpus (PMID:27442865), the label "ameloblastic carcinoma" was the original diagnostic classification of a jaw tumor later reclassified by MSK-IMPACT as Ewing sarcoma ([ES](../cancer_types/ES.md)) via EWSR1-FLI1 fusion detection. This page reflects the cBioPortal AMBL code as used in the study's cancer type labeling; see [ES](../cancer_types/ES.md) for the reclassified diagnosis.

## Cohorts in the corpus

- [hnc_mskcc_2016](../datasets/hnc_mskcc_2016.md) — 1 odontogenic case initially classified as ameloblastic carcinoma, included among 2 odontogenic carcinomas in 151 recurrent/metastatic head and neck tumors profiled by MSK-IMPACT 410-gene panel [PMID:27442865](../papers/27442865.md).

## Recurrent alterations

- **[EWSR1](../genes/EWSR1.md)-[FLI1](../genes/FLI1.md) fusion** — identified by MSK-IMPACT in the tumor originally diagnosed as ameloblastic carcinoma; reclassified the case as Ewing sarcoma with epithelial differentiation ([ES](../cancer_types/ES.md)) [PMID:27442865](../papers/27442865.md).

## Subtypes

- The identification of EWSR1-FLI1 fusion in this odontogenic-site tumor illustrates that [EWSR1](../genes/EWSR1.md) fusions can drive tumors at unusual anatomic locations; diagnosis reclassification has direct therapeutic implications [PMID:27442865](../papers/27442865.md).

## Therapeutic landscape

- **Ewing sarcoma-directed therapy** — the reclassification from ameloblastic carcinoma to [ES](../cancer_types/ES.md) via EWSR1-FLI1 enabled use of Ewing sarcoma-directed cytotoxic regimens [PMID:27442865](../papers/27442865.md).

## Sources

- [PMID:27442865](../papers/27442865.md) — Morris et al. 2017 (JAMA Oncol). Odontogenic EWSR1-FLI1-positive tumor reclassified from ameloblastic carcinoma to Ewing sarcoma by MSK-IMPACT.
