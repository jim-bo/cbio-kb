# Distinct genomic landscapes in radiation-associated angiosarcoma compared with other radiation-associated sarcoma histologies

**PMID:** [37350195](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37350195/) · **DOI:** [10.1002/path.6137](https://doi.org/10.1002/path.6137) · **Journal:** J Pathol (2023)

## TL;DR

This MSK study performed a comprehensive comparative genomic analysis of 82 radiation-associated (RT) sarcomas across four histotypes -- angiosarcoma (AS), malignant peripheral nerve sheath tumor ([MPNST](../cancer_types/MPNST.md)), undifferentiated pleomorphic sarcoma (UPS), and osteosarcoma ([OS](../cancer_types/OS.md)) -- using [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md). RT-AS showed a distinct genomic landscape dominated by [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) amplification (75%) with co-occurring [FLT4](../genes/FLT4.md), [CRKL](../genes/CRKL.md), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), and [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) alterations, while other RT-sarcomas were driven by [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) and [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)/[CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md) loss-of-function events. Each RT-sarcoma histotype harbored distinct mutations and copy number alterations, supporting histotype-specific therapeutic approaches rather than treating RT-sarcomas as a homogeneous category.

## Cohort & data

- **82 patients** with radiation-associated sarcomas: 44 RT-[ANGS](../cancer_types/ANGS.md) (38 from breast/chest wall), 12 RT-[MPNST](../cancer_types/MPNST.md), 14 RT-[MFH](../cancer_types/MFH.md) (UPS), 12 RT-[OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) ([PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md)).
- **Control groups** of sporadic sarcomas with matched histologies: 135 sporadic AS, 64 sporadic [MPNST](../cancer_types/MPNST.md), 273 sporadic UPS, 76 sporadic [OS](../cancer_types/OS.md) ([PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md)).
- Demographics: 68 (83%) females, 14 (17%) males, aged 37-88 years (mean 64) ([PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md)).
- Sequenced on [MSK-IMPACT](../methods/msk-impact-panel.md) targeted DNA-NGS panel; reference genome GRCh37 (hg19) ([PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md)).
- Dataset: [sarcoma_msk_2023](../datasets/sarcoma_msk_2023.md) ([PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md)).

## Key findings

- **[MYC](../genes/MYC.md) amplification** was present in 75% of RT-AS, exclusively in breast/chest wall cases (87% of the 38 breast cases). No RT-AS from other sites were MYC-amplified ([PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md)).
- RT-AS had more frequent [FLT4](../genes/FLT4.md) (16% vs 8%), [CRKL](../genes/CRKL.md) (14% vs 9%), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) (14% vs 2%), and [KMT2D](../genes/KMT2D.md) (11% vs 1%) alterations compared to sporadic AS. [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md) mutations were all hotspot mutations at the Ras GTPase domain (e.g., A59T, Q61R/L) ([PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md)).
- Sporadic AS was enriched for [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) (23% vs 9%), [KDR](../genes/KDR.md) (24% vs 11%), [ATM](../genes/ATM.md) (10% vs 2%), and [ATRX](../genes/ATRX.md) (7% vs 2%) alterations relative to RT-AS ([PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md)).
- [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)/[CDKN2B](../genes/CDKN2B.md) deletions were almost non-existent in RT-AS (2%) but present in 92% of RT-MPNST, 29% of RT-UPS, and 33% of RT-OS ([PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md)).
- RT-MPNST had the most frequent [CDKN2A](../genes/CDKN2A.md)/B deletions (92% vs 44% sporadic), [NF1](../genes/NF1.md) inactivation (67% vs 36%), [TEK](../genes/TEK.md) deletions (42% vs 6%), and [SUZ12](../genes/SUZ12.md) inactivation (33% vs 17%) compared to sporadic MPNST ([PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md)).
- RT-AS harbored the lowest fraction of genome altered (FGA): 9% vs 51% in RT-MPNST, 25% in RT-UPS, 32% in RT-OS (ANOVA P < 0.0001). RT-MPNST had significantly higher FGA than sporadic MPNST (51% vs 31%, P = 0.014) ([PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md)).
- RT-AS had the lowest insertion:SNV and deletion:SNV ratios among RT-sarcomas, while RT-UPS had the highest (adjusted P < 0.05) ([PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md)).
- Translocations were more frequent among RT-MPNST and RT-OS but non-existent in RT-AS (mixed linear effect model P = 0.04) ([PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md)).
- Predominant mutational signatures across all four RT-sarcoma histotypes were associated with errors in DNA repair and replication (defective DNA mismatch repair, slippage during DNA replication) ([PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md)).
- Median time from radiation to RT-sarcoma diagnosis: RT-AS 8.0 years, RT-MPNST 12.5 years, RT-UPS 18.5 years, RT-OS 9.0 years (global log-rank P = 0.0047). MYC-amplified RT-AS had significantly shorter latency than non-MYC-amplified cases (P = 0.0083) ([PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md)).
- No statistically significant differences in overall survival or progression-free survival among the four RT-sarcoma categories, though the cohort was small. Median OS: RT-AS 5.1 years, RT-MPNST 2.1 years, RT-UPS 4.8 years, RT-OS 2.8 years ([PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md)).

## Clinical implications

- RT-sarcomas demonstrate histology-specific genomic profiles, supporting the current approach of treating RT-sarcomas based on histology rather than as a homogeneous category ([PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md)).
- Both primary and RT-AS are treated with taxane-based chemotherapeutic regimens ([PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md)).
- Investigations on small molecular inhibitors of DNA methyltransferase (DNMT1) in sporadic/NF1-related and RT-MPNSTs harboring PRC2-inactivating mutations (e.g., [SUZ12](../genes/SUZ12.md)) are ongoing ([PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md)).
- Potential molecular targets for precision medicine may be histotype-dependent; the stress-activated MAPK cascade ([CRKL](../genes/CRKL.md), [FLT4](../genes/FLT4.md), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), [MYC](../genes/MYC.md)) and VEGF/VEGFR signaling ([FLT4](../genes/FLT4.md), [HRAS](../genes/HRAS.md), [KDR](../genes/KDR.md)) pathways are activated in RT-AS ([PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md)).

## Limitations & open questions

- Survival analysis did not reach statistical significance across RT-sarcoma histotypes, likely due to the small cohort size (n = 82 total, n = 12-44 per histotype) ([PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md)).
- Survival analysis did not account for stage at diagnosis or treatment (e.g., chemotherapy) ([PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md)).
- Allele-specific inactivation status of [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) was not addressed ([PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md)).
- Targeted NGS panel (MSK-IMPACT) cannot capture all genomic alterations, particularly those involving noncoding regions; whole-genome sequencing would be needed to fully examine the spectrum of genomic alterations ([PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md)).
- The mechanism underlying the exclusive association of [MYC](../genes/MYC.md) amplification with breast/chest wall RT-AS remains unclear ([PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md)).
- Whether the distinct mutational profiles of RT-AS vs sporadic AS translate to different treatment responses is not established ([PMID:37350195](../papers/37350195.md)).
