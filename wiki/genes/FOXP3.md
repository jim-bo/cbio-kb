# FOXP3

## Overview

FOXP3 is the master transcription factor of regulatory T cells (Tregs), and is used as a marker of immunoregulatory/counter-activation activity in tumor immune signatures.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- Component of the 20-gene Immunologic Constant of Rejection (ICR) signature as an immunoregulatory counter-activation marker in colon cancer [PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md).

## Cancer types (linked)

- [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md) — expression measured as part of the ICR signature in the AC-ICAM cohort, where ICR-high tumors carried better OS [PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-expressed with [CD274](../genes/CD274.md), [CTLA4](../genes/CTLA4.md), [IDO1](../genes/IDO1.md), [PDCD1](../genes/PDCD1.md) in the immunoregulatory arm of ICR [PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Not reported as a direct target in the corpus; relevant as an ICR component for prognostic stratification [PMID:37202560](../papers/37202560.md).
