# plx8394

## Overview

PLX8394 (plixorafenib) is a next-generation, paradox-breaking [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md) inhibitor designed to inhibit BRAF Class 2 alterations — including oncogenic BRAF fusions — without paradoxical activation of ERK signaling that limits conventional BRAF inhibitors ([vemurafenib](../drugs/vemurafenib.md), [dabrafenib](../drugs/dabrafenib.md)) in RAS-mutant contexts. It binds BRAF as a monomer, selectively disrupting Class 2 and fusion-driven BRAF signaling. PLX8394 is not FDA-approved as of the knowledge cutoff but has demonstrated clinical activity in early-phase trials, including a durable complete metabolic response in a case of BRAF-AGK fusion-driven melanoma.

## Evidence in the corpus

- Produced a durable complete metabolic response in a BRAF V600 wild-type cutaneous melanoma patient harboring a BRAF-[AGK](../genes/AGK.md) Class 2 fusion, after progression through PD-1 and CTLA-4 checkpoint blockade; one of 8 patients (30% of 27 treated) achieving ≥6 months of benefit from genomically matched targeted therapy [PMID:33509808](../papers/33509808.md)
