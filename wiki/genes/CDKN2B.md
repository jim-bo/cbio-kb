# CDKN2B

## Overview

CDKN2B (p15/INK4B) is a CDK4/6 inhibitor and tumor suppressor located at chromosome 9p21, adjacent to CDKN2A. CDKN2B is frequently co-deleted with CDKN2A and functions to suppress cell cycle progression. In esophageal adenocarcinoma, CDKN2B contributes to Cell Cycle pathway enrichment, while in glioblastoma it shows differential alteration patterns between IDH-WT and IDH-mutant subtypes.

## Alterations observed in the corpus

- CDKN2B alteration is a major contributor to Cell Cycle pathway enrichment in esophageal/GEJ adenocarcinoma (egc_mskcc_2020, n=487); grouped with CCNE1 and CCND1 [PMID:33795256](../papers/33795256.md).
- CDKN2A/CDKN2B show differential alteration patterns between IDH-WT and IDH-mutant GBM tumors (gbm_cptac_2021, n=99) [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).
- Deletion (HR 6.0, 95% CI 2.5–14.6, P<0.001) is the strongest independent predictor of worse OS among individual alterations in node-positive (N1) intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma; median OS for CDKN2A/B-deleted N1 patients ~7 vs ~27 months for wild-type [PMID:33963001](../papers/33963001.md)
- CDKN2B (p15INK4B) is the dominant INK4 binder to CDK6 in abemaciclib-resistant FAT1CR MCF7 cells (6 unique peptides in IP-MS); CRISPR knockout of CDKN2B + CDKN2C (double KO) almost entirely restores RB-phosphorylation suppression and re-sensitizes cells to 100 nmol/L abemaciclib over 21 days (P<0.0001) [PMID:34544752](../papers/34544752.md)

## Cancer types (linked)

- **Esophageal/gastroesophageal junction adenocarcinoma (EAC):** CDKN2B is among the top Cell Cycle pathway contributors; no independent OS association for CDKN2B alone reported [PMID:33795256](../papers/33795256.md).
- **Glioblastoma (GBM):** CDKN2B alteration pattern differs between IDH-WT and IDH-mutant subtypes [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Co-occurrence and mutual exclusivity

- Co-occurs with CCNE1 and CCND1 as part of the Cell Cycle pathway signal in EAC [PMID:33795256](../papers/33795256.md).
- Co-alteration with CDKN2A is expected given chromosomal co-location at 9p21 [PMID:33577785](../papers/33577785.md).

## Therapeutic relevance

- Cell Cycle pathway disruption (including CDKN2B loss) may be relevant for CDK4/6 inhibitor sensitivity; not directly tested in these cohorts [PMID:33795256](../papers/33795256.md).

## Open questions

- Independent prognostic significance of CDKN2B loss separate from CDKN2A co-deletion is not assessed in these datasets [PMID:33795256](../papers/33795256.md).
