**PMID:** 29617662 · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.celrep.2018.03.050 · **Journal:** Cell Reports (2018)

## TL;DR

Gao et al. systematically called gene fusions across 9,624 tumor samples spanning 33 TCGA cancer types using a multi-tool RNA-seq pipeline ([star-fusion](../methods/star-fusion.md), [ericscript](../methods/ericscript.md), and [breakfast](../methods/breakfast.md)) followed by stringent panel-of-normals filtering. They identified **25,664 fusions** with a 63.3% WGS validation rate (on the 18.2% of fusions with available [wgs](../methods/wgs.md) data) and reproduced 95.5% of fusions reported in TCGA marker papers. Integrating expression, copy number, and driver-gene annotations, they argue that fusions drive **~16.5% of cancer cases** and act as the *sole* driver in **>1%**, while **6.0% of samples** harbor at least one druggable fusion. The work catalogs 1,275 kinase fusions with intact catalytic domains, highlights cancer-type-specific kinase-fusion enrichments (e.g., 35.6% of [THCA](../cancer_types/THCA.md) samples), shows mutual exclusivity between fusions and mutations in the same driver gene, and predicts neoantigens from fusion junctions for exploratory immunotherapy purposes [PMID:29617662](../papers/29617662.md).

## Cohort & data

- **9,624 tumor samples + 713 normal samples** from TCGA spanning 33 cancer types: [ACC](../cancer_types/ACC.md), [BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md), [LGG](../cancer_types/LGG.md), [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md), [CESC](../cancer_types/CESC.md), [CHOL](../cancer_types/CHOL.md), [COAD](../cancer_types/COAD.md), [ESCA](../cancer_types/ESCA.md), [GBM](../cancer_types/GBM.md), [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md), [KICH](../cancer_types/KICH.md), [KIRC](../cancer_types/KIRC.md), [KIRP](../cancer_types/KIRP.md), [LAML](../cancer_types/LAML.md), [LIHC](../cancer_types/LIHC.md), [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md), [LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md), DLBCL, [MESO](../cancer_types/MESO.md), [OV](../cancer_types/OV.md), [PAAD](../cancer_types/PAAD.md), [PCPG](../cancer_types/PCPG.md), [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md), [READ](../cancer_types/READ.md), [SARC](../cancer_types/SARC.md), [SKCM](../cancer_types/SKCM.md), [STAD](../cancer_types/STAD.md), [TGCT](../cancer_types/TGCT.md), [THYM](../cancer_types/THYM.md), [THCA](../cancer_types/THCA.md), [UCS](../cancer_types/UCS.md), [UCEC](../cancer_types/UCEC.md), and [UVM](../cancer_types/UVM.md) ([PANCAN](../cancer_types/PANCAN.md) scope).
- **Datasets:** TCGA RNA-seq corpus from CGHub processed on the ISB Cancer Genomics Cloud; mutation calls from MC3 (Public MAF; Ellrott et al., 2018); Level-3 RSEM expression and segment-based copy number from Broad GDAC Firehose (2016_01_28). Frontmatter-anchored to [gbm_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018](../datasets/gbm_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018.md); the analysis spans the equivalent pan-can_atlas studies for all 33 disease types.
- **Assays / pipelines:** RNA-seq fusion calling with [star-fusion](../methods/star-fusion.md), [ericscript](../methods/ericscript.md), and [breakfast](../methods/breakfast.md) (5 kb and 100 kb min-distance cutoffs); kinase-domain integrity inference via [agfusion](../methods/agfusion.md) against UniProt/PFAM; WGS-based fusion validation; HLA-typed neoantigen prediction with [netmhc4](../methods/netmhc4.md); driver-gene mutation overlay from [mc3](../methods/mc3.md); druggability annotation from the curated [depo](../methods/depo.md) (Database of Evidence for Precision Oncology).
- **Reference genome:** GRCh38 (build 38).

## Key findings

- **Fusion landscape (n = 25,664):** Filtering across multiple panels-of-normals (TCGA normals, GTEx tissues, non-cancer cells from Babiceanu et al., 2016) yielded 25,664 fusions; the pipeline recovered 405 of 424 (95.5%) curated TCGA marker-paper fusions, and 63.3% of testable predictions (4,675 of 25,664; 18.2%) were WGS-validated by ≥3 discordant read pairs [PMID:29617662](../papers/29617662.md).
- **Breakpoint distribution:** Most breakpoints fall in CDS of both partners; significantly more breakpoints occur in 5′UTRs than 3′UTRs (Mann-Whitney U, p < 2.2e-16) for both partners — consistent with more open 5′ chromatin and a larger 5′-UTR exon count [PMID:29617662](../papers/29617662.md).
- **Per-sample fusion burden:** Median 1 fusion/sample overall (range 0–60); cancer types with median 0 are KICH, KIRC, KIRP, [LGG](../cancer_types/LGG.md), PCPG, TGCT, [THCA](../cancer_types/THCA.md), [THYM](../cancer_types/THYM.md), and UVM [PMID:29617662](../papers/29617662.md).
- **Top per-cancer recurrent fusions:** [TMPRSS2](../genes/TMPRSS2.md)–[ERG](../genes/ERG.md) in [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) (38.2%), [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)–[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md) in [BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md) (2.0%), [CESC](../cancer_types/CESC.md) (1.7%), and [LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md) (1.2%); [EML4](../genes/EML4.md)–[ALK](../genes/ALK.md) in [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) (1.0%); [CCDC6](../genes/CCDC6.md)–[RET](../genes/RET.md) in [THCA](../cancer_types/THCA.md) (4.2%); [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md)–[BICC1](../genes/BICC1.md) in [CHOL](../cancer_types/CHOL.md) (5.6%) [PMID:29617662](../papers/29617662.md).
- **Expression follows oncogene/TSG identity:** 6% (MESO) to 28% (KIRP) of kinase-partner fusions were over-expression outliers; oncogene partners over-express, TSG partners under-express. [TSG](../genes/CBFB.md) under-expression ranged from 3% ([BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md)) to 38% (PCPG) of TSG-partner fusions [PMID:29617662](../papers/29617662.md).
- **Kinase fusions (n = 2,892):** 1,172 with kinase at 3′, 1,603 with kinase at 5′, 117 both-kinase. **1,275 (44.1%) retained an intact kinase domain.** [THCA](../cancer_types/THCA.md) is dramatically enriched (35.6% of samples; Fisher p < 2.2e-16), with 94.0% being 3′-kinase fusions. Top 3′-kinase recurrent partners are tyrosine kinases enriched in [THCA](../cancer_types/THCA.md): [RET](../genes/RET.md), [BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [NTRK1](../genes/NTRK1.md), [NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md), [ALK](../genes/ALK.md). [FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md) and [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) dominate the 5′-kinase recurrent set [PMID:29617662](../papers/29617662.md).
- **Promoter-swap mechanism:** 70.5% of 3′-kinase fusions show higher 5′-partner expression vs. ~66.7% of 5′-kinase fusions show lower partner expression — i.e., 3′ kinases borrow their partner's stronger promoter. Concrete example: a [TRABD](../genes/TRABD.md)–[DDR2](../genes/DDR2.md) fusion in one [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md) sample drives [DDR2](../genes/DDR2.md) overexpression via the [TRABD](../genes/TRABD.md) promoter [PMID:29617662](../papers/29617662.md).
- **WNK-family fusions:** 23 fusions involving [WNK1](../genes/WNK1.md) or [WNK2](../genes/WNK2.md) detected across cancer types, most associated with elevated WNK mRNA *without* matching copy-number amplification (e.g., neither [WNK1](../genes/WNK1.md) nor [WNK2](../genes/WNK2.md) amplified in [ESCA](../cancer_types/ESCA.md) or [LIHC](../cancer_types/LIHC.md)); [ERC1](../genes/ERC1.md)–[WNK1](../genes/WNK1.md) was independently reported in a Chinese [ESCC](../cancer_types/ESCC.md) cohort (Chang et al., 2017) [PMID:29617662](../papers/29617662.md).
- **Fusion-only drivers:** Across 8,955 patients with both MC3 mutations and fusion calls, 8.3% had both driver mutations and driver fusions, 6.4% had both mutations and fusions in driver genes, and **1.8% had driver fusions only** (mean 1.1 fusions, no driver mutations). The "driver fusion only" group has mutational burden comparable to the no-driver-alteration group (Mann-Whitney U, p < 2.2e-16) [PMID:29617662](../papers/29617662.md).
- **Mutual exclusivity:** When fusion events are present in a driver gene, point mutations in that same gene are rarely observed — strict in [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) (0 overlapping samples). [TP53](../genes/TP53.md) is mutation-dominated except in [SARC](../cancer_types/SARC.md), where both fusions and mutations occur. In [LAML](../cancer_types/LAML.md), [CBFB](../genes/CBFB.md) is fused but rarely mutated [PMID:29617662](../papers/29617662.md).
- **[LAML](../cancer_types/LAML.md) "fusion-only" cases:** 14.0% of [LAML](../cancer_types/LAML.md) tumors had fusions but no driver-gene mutations; recovered fusions include [CBFB](../genes/CBFB.md)–[MYH11](../genes/MYH11.md) (n=3), [BCR](../genes/BCR.md)–[ABL1](../genes/ABL1.md) (n=2), [PML](../genes/PML.md)–[RARA](../genes/RARA.md) (n=2), and the leukemia-initiating [NUP98](../genes/NUP98.md)–[NSD1](../genes/NSD1.md) (n=2) [PMID:29617662](../papers/29617662.md).
- **Druggable fusions cover 6.0% of samples (574/9,624)** by DEPO annotation across 29 cancer types. Major recurrent druggable targets: [TMPRSS2](../genes/TMPRSS2.md) in [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md) (205 samples), [RET](../genes/RET.md) in [THCA](../cancer_types/THCA.md) (33 samples), [PML](../genes/PML.md)–[RARA](../genes/RARA.md) in [LAML](../cancer_types/LAML.md) (16 samples). [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) is a potential target in 15 cancer types [PMID:29617662](../papers/29617662.md).
- **Smoking-stratified [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) druggability:** Of 500 [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) with known smoking status, never-smokers had a significantly higher rate of druggable fusions (15/75, 20%) than smokers (9/425, 2.1%) (chi-square p < 1e-6) [PMID:29617662](../papers/29617662.md).
- **Neoantigen prediction:** Mean 1.5 predicted neoantigens per fusion across cancer types (range 0.33 in KICH to 2.88 in [THYM](../cancer_types/THYM.md)). Frameshift fusions yielded more epitopes than inframe fusions (mean 2.2 vs. 1.0). [TMPRSS2](../genes/TMPRSS2.md)–[ERG](../genes/ERG.md), [CCDC6](../genes/CCDC6.md)–[RET](../genes/RET.md), and [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)–[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md) had the most samples with at least one predicted neoantigen [PMID:29617662](../papers/29617662.md).

## Genes & alterations

- **[TMPRSS2](../genes/TMPRSS2.md)–[ERG](../genes/ERG.md):** Most-recurrent intra-cancer fusion overall (38.2% of [PRAD](../cancer_types/PRAD.md), 205 samples flagged druggable). Modest neoantigen yield in only a small subset.
- **[FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)–[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md):** Recurrent across [BLCA](../cancer_types/BLCA.md) (2.0%), [CESC](../cancer_types/CESC.md) (1.7%), [LUSC](../cancer_types/LUSC.md) (1.2%); inframe activating kinase fusion. [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md) named druggable target in 15 cancer types.
- **[EML4](../genes/EML4.md)–[ALK](../genes/ALK.md) and other [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) fusions:** [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) fusions in 20 samples across 8 cancer types (5 in [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md)); [EML4](../genes/EML4.md) is the most frequent 5′ partner (7/17). Fusion status corresponds to copy-number-neutral [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) overexpression — the rationale for [crizotinib](../drugs/crizotinib.md) and other approved [ALK](../genes/ALK.md) inhibitors.
- **[CCDC6](../genes/CCDC6.md)–[RET](../genes/RET.md) and other [RET](../genes/RET.md) fusions:** Recurrent in [THCA](../cancer_types/THCA.md) (4.2%); 33 [THCA](../cancer_types/THCA.md) samples flagged as druggable on [RET](../genes/RET.md). Inframe protein kinase fusions with overexpression of the 3′ [RET](../genes/RET.md) oncogene; also seen in [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md).
- **[FGFR2](../genes/FGFR2.md)–[BICC1](../genes/BICC1.md):** Most recurrent fusion in [CHOL](../cancer_types/CHOL.md) (5.6%).
- **[ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) fusions:** 4 samples; 3 of 4 had HPV integration within 1 Mb of [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md). Partner genes [PPP1R1B](../genes/PPP1R1B.md) and [IKZF3](../genes/IKZF3.md) are genomic neighbors of [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md), suggesting fusions arise from local instability potentially induced by viral integration. Compares with [trastuzumab](../drugs/trastuzumab.md)-targetable HER2+ amplification in BRCA.
- **[CBFB](../genes/CBFB.md)–[MYH11](../genes/MYH11.md):** Recurrent in [LAML](../cancer_types/LAML.md); strongly associated with decreased [CBFB](../genes/CBFB.md) (TSG/transcriptional regulator) expression. [CBFB](../genes/CBFB.md) is fused but rarely mutated in LAML — alternative inactivation mechanism.
- **[DNAJB1](../genes/DNAJB1.md)–[PRKACA](../genes/PRKACA.md):** Liver-only; specific to fibrolamellar carcinoma ([FLC](../cancer_types/FLC.md)) subtype of [LIHC](../cancer_types/LIHC.md), corroborating Dinh et al. 2017.
- **[ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) fusions:** 16 samples across 5 cancer types (9 in [BRCA](../cancer_types/BRCA.md), 8 of which are luminal A/B). Strict mutual exclusivity with [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) point mutations. When [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) is the 5′ partner, the AF1 transactivation domain is preserved; when 3′, the AF2 domain is preserved. [ESR1](../genes/ESR1.md) expression is elevated in fusion-positive samples, especially in the 9 BRCA cases.
- **[BCR](../genes/BCR.md)–[ABL1](../genes/ABL1.md), [PML](../genes/PML.md)–[RARA](../genes/RARA.md), [NUP98](../genes/NUP98.md)–[NSD1](../genes/NSD1.md):** Classic leukemic fusions recovered in [LAML](../cancer_types/LAML.md) "fusion-only" tumors.
- **[WNK1](../genes/WNK1.md)/[WNK2](../genes/WNK2.md):** 23 WNK-family fusions; mostly higher WNK mRNA without copy-number amplification. [ERC1](../genes/ERC1.md)–[WNK1](../genes/WNK1.md) independently reported in a Chinese ESCC cohort.
- **[TRABD](../genes/TRABD.md)–[DDR2](../genes/DDR2.md):** [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md) sample with promoter-swap-driven [DDR2](../genes/DDR2.md) overexpression — proposed as candidate for [dasatinib](../drugs/dasatinib.md). [DDR2](../genes/DDR2.md) fusions seen in nine additional samples across five cancer types.
- **[BRAF](../genes/BRAF.md), [NTRK1](../genes/NTRK1.md), [NTRK3](../genes/NTRK3.md):** Among the top recurrent 3′-kinase tyrosine kinases enriched in [THCA](../cancer_types/THCA.md).
- **[TP53](../genes/TP53.md):** Predominantly mutated, not fused, across cancer types — except in [SARC](../cancer_types/SARC.md), where both fusion and mutation events were observed.
- **[MERTK](../genes/MERTK.md) ([TMEM87B](../genes/TMEM87B.md) partner) and [FGR](../genes/FGR.md)–[WASF2](../genes/WASF2.md):** Recurrent singleton-pattern kinase fusions; FGR–WASF2 in seven samples uses the WASF2 5′UTR promoter to drive FGR overexpression in five of seven. (Wiki pages do not yet exist for MERTK, TMEM87B, FGR, or WASF2.)

## Clinical implications

- **Druggable fusions in 6.0% of pan-can samples** suggests substantial untapped opportunity for fusion-directed targeted therapy; [DEPO](../methods/depo.md) was used as the druggability oracle (off-label allowed).
- **[crizotinib](../drugs/crizotinib.md) and other FDA-approved ALK inhibitors** are flagged for the 20 [ALK](../genes/ALK.md)-fusion samples spanning 8 cancer types, with [EML4](../genes/EML4.md) being the dominant 5′ partner and ALK overexpression copy-number neutral.
- **[dasatinib](../drugs/dasatinib.md)** is proposed for [DDR2](../genes/DDR2.md)-overexpressed tumors with promoter-swap fusions (e.g., [TRABD](../genes/TRABD.md)–[DDR2](../genes/DDR2.md) in [HNSC](../cancer_types/HNSC.md)) following von Massenhausen et al., 2016.
- **[trastuzumab](../drugs/trastuzumab.md)** is contextualized as the canonical [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md)-amplification therapy; the four [ERBB2](../genes/ERBB2.md) fusions identified here (with [PPP1R1B](../genes/PPP1R1B.md) and [IKZF3](../genes/IKZF3.md) partners) point to a different but potentially actionable mechanism of HER2 dysregulation.
- **Never-smoker [LUAD](../cancer_types/LUAD.md) cohort enrichment for druggable fusions** (20% vs. 2.1% in smokers, p < 1e-6) implies fusion screening should be prioritized in non-smoker [NSCLC](../cancer_types/NSCLC.md).
- **Immunotherapy caution:** Patients with driver-fusion-only tumors have low overall mutational burden and may be poor candidates for checkpoint immunotherapy despite fusion peptides themselves being potentially immunogenic. Predicted fusion-derived neoantigens were highest for [TMPRSS2](../genes/TMPRSS2.md)–[ERG](../genes/ERG.md), [CCDC6](../genes/CCDC6.md)–[RET](../genes/RET.md), and [FGFR3](../genes/FGFR3.md)–[TACC3](../genes/TACC3.md), but only in a small subset of the carriers.
- **Diagnostic implication:** Robust pan-cancer fusion diagnostics (RNA-seq based) are necessary to identify the 1.8% of patients whose tumor is driven *only* by fusion events and who would otherwise look "driver-less" on mutation panels.

## Limitations & open questions

- **WGS validation only on 18.2% of fusions (1,725/9,624 samples).** The 63.3% validation rate is therefore a partial [estimate](../methods/estimate.md); the unvalidated 81.8% retains some false-positive load even after stringent filtering.
- **RNA-seq-based fusion calling cannot directly measure fusion allele frequency or clonality**, limiting interpretation of clonal evolution and intratumor heterogeneity for fusions.
- **Neoantigen prediction is "exploratory and speculative":** no immune-cell-infiltration or response data are integrated. Frameshift fusions may be down-regulated by nonsense-mediated decay, dampening the apparent advantage over inframe fusions in epitope yield.
- **Druggability annotation depends on [DEPO](../methods/depo.md)**, which permits off-label inclusion — overstates true clinical benefit and does not consider co-occurring resistance alterations.
- **STAR-Fusion is the dominant caller**; results may inherit STAR-Fusion biases despite multi-tool integration. Disagreements among callers were filtered using FFPM > 0.1 thresholds for STAR-Fusion-only calls — borderline real fusions could be lost.
- **Cancer-type-specific fusion biology** remains under-explained: e.g., the dramatic 3′-kinase enrichment in [THCA](../cancer_types/THCA.md) (94% of THCA kinase fusions) lacks a mechanistic explanation here.
- **Several reported fusion partners are not yet wiki entities** (MERTK, TMEM87B, FGR, WASF2, REF1) and need either confirmation against canonical HUGO symbols (REF1 may be an alias) or new gene pages.
